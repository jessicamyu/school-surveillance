AD1 - 2016 Sales Tax Project List
Meeting
Jul 27, 2016 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the proposed Project List for the Local Government Infrastructure Sales Surtax referendum included on the November 8, 2016 general election ballot.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the proposed Project List for the Local Government Infrastructure Surtax.

  • On May 10, 2016, the School Board approved an Interlocal Agreement to participate in a one-cent Local Government Infrastructure Sales Surtax.  Per this agreement, the School District will receive 50 percent of the proceeds.
  • The District completed a detailed Facility Condition Assessment (FCA), which was presented to the School Board on June 1, 2016.
  • The results of the FCA were used to develop a project list, which estimates the amount to be spent at each facility.  It is important to note the amounts listed by school are estimates based upon the best information available at this time and subject to change as the projects are completed over the course of the ten-year sales tax.
  • The one-cent sales surtax shall "sunset" in the event that total aggregate distributions of surtax proceeds equal or exceed $2.7 billion on or before September 1 of any year during the 10 year term of the ordinance.
  • Any changes to the project list will be reviewed by the Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee (ISSOC) and brought to the School Board for approval.

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Financial Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

If approved by voters in November 2016, the one-cent sales surtax is projected to generate $1.345 billion in revenue to fund School District projects on the proposed Referendum Project List.  An additional $325 million in other local capital funding sources will be used to fund the remainder of the $1.67 billion Referendum Project List.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Affiliation Agreement for Education Services with Non-Violent Project USA, Inc., effective July 1, 2016 through June 30, 2019; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The Non-Violence Project USA, Inc. d/b/a MCUSA Healthcare is a private, not-for-profit national healthcare company dedicated to excellence with a focus on a full circle of behavioral health and wellness for our community's youngest citizens.  MCUSA Healthcare will coordinate the "Journey to Care" service modality which, includes the following best practices:

  • IMPACT:  Gender-specific program that is a strength-based group approach to promote male adolescents' safe, strong and healthy passage into adulthood.
  • POWER:  A one-on-one mentoring model rated a "Best Practice" substance abuse program.  This program utilizes 40-Developmental Assets to promote, support and improve positive youth development.

The School District will provide MCUSA Healthcare, Inc. with an on-campus teaching facility to provide effective lesson planning and availability to students and faculty involved with the MCUSA Heathcare's efforts on campus.  The Center will also provide a site liaison that will serve as the main contact between administration at participating schools and MCUSA Healthcare.

 

Participating schools include:  39 middle schools, 7 alternative schools, and 4 piloted programs at Palm Beach Lakes High School for the duration of this agreement, as indicated in Exhibit A, will also provide a site liaison that will serve as the main contact between administration at designated schools and MCUSA Healthcare.

Subject
SFS1 House Bill - Improving Child Nutrition and Education Act of 2016 (H.R. 5003) - Block Grant
Meeting
Jul 27, 2016 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
12. New Business
Type
Action
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve opposition to the Block Grant pilot contained in the proposed U.S. House of Representatives Bill 5003; take action in the future to oppose legislation that negatively impacts the child nutrition program; and authorize the Superintendent to sign related documents, including signing and forwarding the Food Research and Action Center's (FRAC) opposition statement attached.

DESCRIPTION:

The United States House of Representatives proposal H.R 5003 - Improving Child Nutrition and Education Act of 2016 includes a block grant pilot in three states which could severely negatively impact the nutrition services provided to children in Palm Beach County and nationwide.

 

The Block Grant would:

  • Provide states with a fixed amount of money to operate the school meals program, eliminating per meal reimbursement, as currently provided in the regulations. The money would be capped at the amount received in 2016 and would not fluctuate with participation increases.  This would prevent districts from receiving additional funds for increased cost of food or if the economy creates increased need for subsidized school meals.
  • Reduce funds by approximately $.35 per meal ($.06 for menu compliance and $.29 for paid meals).  This equates to approximately a $2.4 million decrease in funds for Palm Beach County.
  • Eliminate national consistency for the Free and Reduced eligibility guidelines, as eligibility would be determined by each state.  This creates the potential to adversely affect families in need of support as well as the potential to change Title 1 allocations to schools.
  • Eliminate national consistency for Nutrition Guidelines which would be determined by each state.  With the reduced funding, the nutritional integrity of the program would be compromised.

Both the School Nutrition Association (SNA) and the Food Research and Action Center (FRAC) have urged the public to take action against the block grant. 

 

FRAC's opposition statement and call to action web page are attached.

 

The block grant goes against the core purpose of our school nutrition program: to decrease childhood hunger and childhood obesity and to positively affect the ability of children to achieve academically.  As participation increases, so do the expenses, and without additional funding for additional meals, participation will not be able to rise and fewer children will benefit, leading to many hungry children in our schools.  Additionally, if participation rises without reimbursement, the general operating fund may be impacted.

 

Staff has reached out to our legislative representatives to voice our concerns.  Response letters (attached) have been received from Patrick Murphy, Congressman, 18th District and Theodore Deutch, Congressman, 21st District. Both share our concern.

 

Additional information regarding the block grant can be found on the following websites: 

Center on Budget and Policy Priorities:  http://www.cbpp.org/research/food-assistance/house-proposal-to-block-grant-school-meal-programs-would-put-childrens

SNA – Take Action Site:  http://cqrcengage.com/schoolnutrition/home

FRAC Legislative Action Center – Call to Action:  http://frac.org/leg-act-center/

SNA Stop the Block site: https://schoolnutrition.org/LegislationPolicy/ActionCenter/StopTheBlock/

Senator Look Up Site: https://www.flsenate.gov/Senators/Find
