Palm Beach County came into existence on July 1, 1909. The county was created from Dade County, which at the time, stretched from the St. Lucie River south to the tip of the Florida Peninsula.  The School District of Palm Beach County was then formed and had its first meeting on July 6, 1909. The first School Board was composed of Herbert A. Pennock, Chairman, Henry T. Grant and J.J. Schabinger. The first Superintendent, James C. Harris was appointed July 13, 1909.  In honor of the School District's Centennial Celebration, Superintendent Harris' grandson, James C. Harris III, is being recognized along with our Centennial premier partner Bank of America.  80 Little Red Schoolhouses are being used as centerpieces for multiple Centennial Celebrations throughout the District this year.  The dedicated employees, retired educators and volunteers who built the Schoolhouse centerpieces are being recognized.

 The District purchases Commercial Crime Insurance to protect itself from the theft of cash and securities etc. (not personal property) by employees or individuals with whom we conduct business.  The policy also satisfies the requirement of Florida Statute 1010.07, to ensure that each employee with responsibility for handling/expending funds be appropriately bonded or insured.



This policy protects the District from employee thefts of cash and securities with a limit up to $5,000,000 per occurrence with a $100,000 deductible.  It also provides policy limits of $100,000 with a $1,000 deductible for thefts by individuals with whom we do business.



Additionally, the policy covers the School District for Faithful Performance of Duty for $1,000,000 with a $100,000 deductible.



This insurance also covers losses from Forgery/Alteration ($1,000,000 limit/$10,000 deductible) and Disappearance/Destruction ($100,000 limit/$1,000 deductible) caused by individuals with whom we do business.



Our Broker, The Beacon Group, contacted several insurance carriers and could not find any who could compete with The Travelers program with comparable premiums.

In August, 2004 The District implemented an interscholastic athletic accident insurance participation fee as allowed by School Board Policy 5.60 to help defray approximately half of the $913,500 insurance premium.    Although the premium has escalated since then, there has been no corresponding increase in the student fees.   However, with the new substantial premium increase this year, the student participation fee is being increased from $50 to $75 per athlete in order to keep pace with the escalating premium.

P2 Approve the negotiated agreements for the Transportation of Special Needs Students for the contract period July 31, 2009 through July 30, 2010.



On July 23, 2008, the Board approved negotiated contracts with Med Aide Services, Palm Beach Metro Transportation and Imperial Transportation to support the transportation needs of our Special Needs / Disabled Students. 



In 2001 Congress passed the McKinney-Vento Act to strengthen existing laws which gave children and youth in homeless situations the right to go to school, no matter where they lived or how long they had lived there.  This law gives children and youth in homeless situations, shelters and temporary foster care, the right to stay in their school of origin if they move.  It also gives them the right to receive transportation services to school for a determined period of time.  In some situations our current vendors provide transportation primarily by taxi service.

P8 Approve the Microsoft Volume Licensing, Campus and School Agreement



The Campus and School Agreement allows the District to license Microsoft products on a subscription basis, granting a non-perpetual right to use the software during the Licensed Period. The District would participate in the program by first submitting Enrollment requests for a one or three-year Licensed Period and then submitting Purchase Orders for the corresponding subscriptions. Choosing the three-year period guarantees the same subscription price for the three years. The one-year subscription guarantees that annual extensions will not be priced at more than a 10% increase year over year. Subscriptions are paid one year at a time, regardless of the one or three-year period election.



An Enrollment may contain multiple products but must be for the same user or device (computer) count. Multiple enrollments must be used if the counts differ.



Only one ‘Campus and School Agreement’ is needed. The actual purchase of the license happens at a later time when an Enrollment is paid for via a Purchase Order.



Benefits





  • Price guarantees as explained above (no increases for the 3-year enrollment, no more than 10% for one-year agreements; still paid on a yearly basis regardless and the District can opt to not renew after each year)

  • Software Assurance is built in; as new versions and upgrades to the licensed products are released during the subscription period, the District will have the right to install them and upgrade applications at no extra cost.

  • Cost: The price the District will pay per year will be a fraction of the cost to purchase the perpetual license to use the software, typically 18-20%.






    1. This will help the District with cash flow, not having to make one lump-sum payment up-front.

    2. Furthermore, if the licensed products are near an upgrade cycle (such as the next 3-5 years), the cost savings in not having to pay for a full upgrade to the newer version can be significant; the total cost of ownership is reduced this way also.

    3. Under our current licensing practices, which do not include purchasing Software Assurance, the District pays for a full version every time a new release comes out as Microsoft does not offer upgrade discounts.




  • During the Licensed Period, staff Users who are licensed to use a product at work, have the right to run one copy of such product on a home PC and to access the server products for work-related purposes.


Challenges & Caveats





  • Annual licensing will require budgeting for these licenses on a yearly basis as opposed to one time capital outlays

  • Capital has been used in most cases for these types of purchases and moving to annual licensing will likely move these purchases to the Operating budget thus requiring proper budget planning

  • Under certain circumstances, systems have had the same software version for a duration longer than 5 years, sometimes due to hardware insufficiency to run the upgraded product; on such cases, a perpetual license may be more cost effective.

  • Budgeting, IT and Purchasing processes will need to change to support forecasting, software & licensing inventory management and timely upgrades

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve the Campus and School Agreement. No purchases or other expenditures are attached to this agreement just yet. The Agreement would allow staff this additional purchasing option for Microsoft Software.

P4 Approve the Credit Card Implementation Project





  • The Board approved the negotiated agreement with KEV Group on July 30, 2008 for software and credit card services that allow the district to accept online credit card payments for school fees which would lessen cash handling at schools and ease the payment process for parents and employees.

  • The initial phase was rolled out during the 2008-2009 school year and within two (2) months, parents made payments online for school meals in over 86% of the schools and student activity fee payments have exceeded over $900,000 since implementation. 

  • The cost for maintenance of the software and training for new users is $71,400.

  • The cost for the use of the credit card facility is estimated at $776,433.  Final cost will depend on the actual usage by parents


 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval for the estimated expenditure for access to the online credit card facility in the amount of $776,433 in addition to the $71,400 for annual maintenance and training for the period of July 31, 2009 through July 30, 2010.

For the purpose of:


(1) clarifying the appropriate uses of purchasing cards and preventing
unintentional misuse of the privilege
(2) restoring the public trust in the financial management of the
school district
I ask that the Board request
A board workshop on purchasing cards including but not limited to
(a)-who is authorized to have a purchasing card?
(b)-the plans to review and clarify policies and procedures associated
with the purchasing card (It is my expectation that these
policies/procedures are reviewed by the audit committee prior to board
approval and implementation)
(c)-the mandatory training planned for anyone that will use a purchasing
card for any period of time (I think that it is critically important
that this training be internet based.)
(d)-the training planned for those who monitor purchasing card use
(e)-the various accounts accessible by principals, the Superintendent's
Leadership Team, the Superintendent and the School Board (including
administrative courtesy accounts)-their funding sources and appropriate usage


 


For the purpose of restoring the public trust and providing the Board with an opportunity to determine if more procedures may be needed for guidance in this area, I ask that the Board request a review by the Internal Auditor of expenditures by the School Board, the Superintendent , and the Superintendent’s Leadership Team which did not require specific approval by the Board for FY 2008 and 2009. Further I request a review of associated policies and procedures.
