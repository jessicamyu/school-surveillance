FM3 Five Year Capital Plan and District Facility Work Program
Meeting
Sep 14, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Financial Management
Type
Action (Consent)

·         The District Facility Work Program is a planning tool that includes all capital projects for the next five years as well as  long-range planning for facilities needs over 10 and 20-year periods.  The first five years is in agreement with the Five Year Capital Plan but in the format required by the Florida Department of Education in accordance with Fl. St. 1013.35.  The document is updated annually to ensure that sufficient capacity exists to support the current and projected student enrollment throughout the Five Year Planning period and that the Plan is financially feasible.



 



·         Fl St. 1013.61 requires the School District approve the District Facility Work Program prior to the adoption of the capital budget. 



 



·         The Five Year Plan is a planning tool that is in agreement with the District Facility Work Program but does not include any long-range planning beyond five years.  The sum of the columns titled “Balance from FY 2011 for Ongoing Projects” and “New Appropriation of FY 2012” equal the FY 2012 Capital Budget. 



 



·         The Five-Year Capital Plan demonstrates the ability to fund planned projects from existing or forecasted revenue sources.  The Plan includes capital projects needed to serve the current and projected student and staff populations and other projects to meet the needs of the students and employees that learn and work in our facilities.



 



·         The FY 2012 Capital Plan includes the FY 2012 budget of $593,369,195.  $383.6 million is beginning fund balance and $209.7 million of new revenues, primarily property taxes.  $66.3 million is reserved for future years to provide funds for the continued maintenance of district facilities and equipment.



 



·         Since the last capital budget workshop, funding for the North Palm Beach Elem Modernization project has been added to the budget.  The project was included on the sales tax referendum list and will be funded with sales tax proceeds made available as other sales tax funded projects were closed.



 



·         The Ten and Twenty Year Projections serve mainly as a guiding tool for general planning purposes.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Five Year Capital Plan and the District Facility Work Program.

Joseph Sanches (joseph.sanches@palmbeachschools.org)

Financial Impact:

The Five Year Capital Plan totals $1.5 billion including the FY 2012 Capital Budget of $593 million.

AC1 - Afterschool Literacy Enrichment Initiative (ALEI) Prime Time, PBC Grant
Meeting
Sep 14, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type

The Afterschool Literacy Enrichment Initiative (ALEI) is funded by a grant from Prime Time, PBC, and through the  cost recovery budgets of District Afterschool programs. ALEI is a comprehensive multi-curricula academic enrichment component for implementation in District and community-based afterschool programs.



ALEI combines literacy best practices, student opportunities for voice and choice, opportunities for physical activity and project-based learning.
ALEI serves up to 2400 children from kindergarten through fifth grade in afterschool settings from school based sites and community based organizations
ALEI is implemented for one hour per day, 3days per week and 33 weeks per year
ALEI  provides children a comprehensive academic enrichment component incorporating a hands on project-based curriculum
ALEI is infused with opportunities for students to share their ideas and opinions through voice and choice
ALEI incorporates physical activities into daily literacy lessons.
ALEI integrates opportunities for discussion of some pressing issues for children today
ALEI utilizes the regular site-based child care counselors as the instructors for the initiative
ALEI is funded by Prime Time, Palm Beach County which serves as the afterschool intermediary for the entire county



This grant provides funding for staff in Department of Afterschool Programming to develop, revise, and implement the ALEI initiative and to monitor fidelity to grant protocols.


Recommendation:


I recommend that the School Board approve the Afterschool Literacy Enrichment Initiative (ALEI) Prime Time, PBC Grant effective October 1, 2011 through September 30, 2012 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all required documents and contracts related to the agreement.

Meeting

P2 Approve the Award of RFP 12C-001H Research Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Students in Palm Beach County.





  • Federal Statute requires supplemental educational services are provided to eligible Title I and IDEA students in non-public, private schools. For the past five years, the services have been delivered by third party provider, Catapult Learning.


  • More than 700 Title I students were served in FY11 at 37 private schools with 35 part-time teachers and 2 full-time teachers. The cost for the instructional services amounted to $1,219,559 and the cost paid to Catapult for administering the program was $187,102. For FY12, a partial contract will be used to support our Palm Beach District Provided program that includes hand-held technology for eligible students in grades 1-8. Thirteen full time teachers will be hired to support the delivery of instruction and progress monitoring. The partial contract will provide instructional services for approximately 60 eligible kindergarten (and some first grade) students at 18 schools and 150 high school students at 11 schools. The cost estimate for these services is not more than $327,040.


  • Approximately 135 ESE students were served in FY11 at 44 private schools with 35 part-time teachers and 2 full-time teachers.  The cost paid to Catapult for these services was $470,000.  Services will be provided for the majority of eligible private school students through the district approved program.  Services may be utilized to address supplementary tutorial needs of public school students with disabilities.  This contract will only be used for a limited number of students with disabilities on an as needed basis.


  • Section 1120 of  the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) requires participating Local Education Agencies (LEA) to provide eligible private school students, their teachers and families with Title I services or other benefits that are equitable to those provided to eligible Title I public school children, their teachers, and their families.  




    • To qualify for assistance under Title I, a student must reside within the attendance area of a participating public school located in a low-income area and be failing, or at risk of failing, to meet student academic achievement standards.


    • Services will be provided for the majority of eligible private school students through the district provided program.  This contract will only be used for limited number of students on an as needed basis. 


  • Prior to discussing and evaluating each of the responder's proposals, the Committee agreed that a recommendation of award would  be made to Proposers receiving 85 points or more.  After discussing each proposal, two proposers received a score of 85 points or more.  The two are Catapult Learning with a score of 97.4 and Nonpublic Educational Services, Inc (NESI) with a score of 86.5.  Both companies received the maximum points for Minority Participation resulting from their commitment to use M/WBE firms as subcontractors for auxiliary services during the life of this contract.


  • Services to be provided include but are not limited to:




    • Research based direct supplemental academic instruction in reading and/or math


    • Instructional services, as needs are identified, to students at various private and/or public schools throughout the Palm Beach County School District


    • All materials for evaluation and instructional services


    • Comprehensive program review at the end of the school year which is to include disaggregated data, parent, staff and administrative surveys, achievement pre and post data and a summary of services


    • Florida-certified teachers with the responsibility of assessing, planning, implementing and evaluating a directed research based supplemental instruction program in reading and/or math

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the contract for Research Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Students in Palm Beach County for the period of September 15, 2011 through September 14, 2014 with Catapult Learning and Nonpublic Educational Services, Inc. (NESI), the two highest scored responsive and responsible proposers, based on the criteria outlined in the RFP and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

Sep 14, 2011 - Special Meeting

On or about June 16, 2011, Ms. Perry became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of Failure to Report to Duty on Time; Failure to Protect Students from Conditions Harmful to Learning; Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment; Ethical Misconduct; Gross Insubordination; and Failure to Follow Policy/Rule or Directive.  Over a period of two school years, Ms. Perry has repeatedly reported to work late and failed to notify the school of her late arrival.  She has received multiple written directives and site level discipline for this behavior; however, the behavior has not improved.  The Principal requested that Employee Relations assist them in proceeding with the next step of progressive discipline.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.



Therefore, the actions violated the following:  Rolling Green Faculty Handbook, Responsibilities, Page 5; School Board Policy 0.01 (2)(c), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; Rule 6B-1.006 (3) (a), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 3.02 (4)(a), and (4)(j), Code of Ethics; Rule 6B-1.001 (2) & (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment; School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; and Rule 6B-4.009 (3) and (4), F.A.C., Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal.  Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Perry completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02 (Code of Ethics) and acknowledged such on June 24, 2010.



There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Robin Perry.



On August 30, 2011, the "Notice of Five (5) Day Suspension" was hand-delivered to Ms. Perry from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Robin Perry's five (5) day suspension without pay, effective September 30, 2011, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.
