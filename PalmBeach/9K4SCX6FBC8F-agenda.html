AC2 - Consultant Contract with Fish and Richardson for EBS - Related Services
Meeting
Jul 14, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent

Action (Consent)

Legal Advisor was hired by the School Board in March, 2005 to assist and advise the School Board and Chief Counsel with various Federal Communications Commission (FCC) matters.  Under this contract, Legal Advisor provides legal advice and expertise related to the FCC licenses held by the School Board for Educational Broadband Spectrum (EBS).  A portion of the EBS-related work represents a recurring effort that is necessary and will continue for as long as the School Board holds the EBS licenses.  The remainder of the EBS-related work is made of various initiatives that are finite and will ultimately be completed.




EBS matters that represent recurring efforts include, but are not limited to the following:





  • Periodic renewal of the School Board's eight EBS licenses.

  • Miscellaneous unanticipated issues that arise as a result of FCC activities that Legal Advisor monitors and other unanticipated circumstances.  For example, there is legislation pending in Congress requiring the FCC to undertake a spectrum utilization analysis, with a likely outcome being that licensees who are not making effective use of their licensed spectrum would not be entitled to a license renewal.


EBS matters that are finite include, but are not limited to the following:





  • Complete work on EBS band plan transition including:  finalization of transition documentation; closing on payment of $1.75M to School Board; FCC related filings, and; mandatory conversion of the School Board's FCC licenses to past-transition licenses.

  • Preparation of legal memorandum and follow up meetings and conferences to assist the School Board with satisfaction of the FCC's new substantial service requirements for the School Board's eight EBS licenses.  Failure to meet the FCC's requirements could lead to the loss of the School Board's licenses and their value.

  • Advise and assist the School Board with ensuring satisfaction of the FCC's new substantial service requirements by May 1, 2011 including:  preparation of written compliance plans for each of the School Board's eight EBS licenses; revisions to operational and license status memorandums to reflect changes in operations, and; preparation and filing of substantial service showings with the FCC.

  • Unwinding of the Sprint (now Clearwire) lease agreement which allows the School Board to use pre-transition channels E1, E2 and E3 pending completion of the EBS band plan transition in the Palm Beach market.

  • Resolution of outstanding legal issues concerning coverage of new EBS licenses issued to School Board (FCC licensing data does not accurately reflect the School Board's agreement with FAU).   This issue must be resolved to ensure that the School Board maintains all of the population and area coverage that it is entitled to keep under the FAU agreement, but which is not currently reflected in the FCC's database.


The Consultant shall perform the following services: Legal Advisor shall provide FCC regulatory and transactional advice to the School Board with respect to Educational Broadband Service Spectrum.
This contract is for a not to exceed amount of $85,000.00 and the term of the contract through June 30, 2011.
The District needs an attorney with expertise in FCC regulatory matters, spectrum use and valuation issues to assist staff in the protection of the EBS licenses and their value, and evaluation of the alternatives available to the School Board.



The firm of Fish & Richardson and Attorney Ed Lavergne have provided excellent service since being hired in March 2005. Approval of this contract will allow Mr. Lavergne to continue to provide the Chief Counsel with sound advice that can be used as the basis for making future decisions regarding the EBS licenses held by the School Board.

Recommendation:

I recommend award of the Consultant Contract with Fish & Richardson for an amount not to exceed $85,000, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all needed documents.

PS 1 The consultant shall perform the following services:



Lane Carlee has worked with students with traumatic brain injury (TBI) for the past 15 years.  She works with the Nurse Case Manager from the Brain and Spinal Cord Injury Program (through the Department of Health) a minimum of three times per week.  The Nurse Case Manager works with students who have a moderate to severe head injury for two years post injury.  She works with the Nurse Case Manager to reintegrate students who have suffered a traumatic brain injury successfully back  in to school.  Ms. Carlee provides staff development to district staff so their work with TBI students has a good outcome.  While doing this, Ms. Carlee is cognizant of following Federal and State laws.  During the FY09 -10 school year she had 203 contacts with the Brain and Spinal Cord Injury Nurse Case Manager, 183 consults with parents whose child had suffered a traumatic brain injury, 636 school district contacts, has attended 86 meetings at schools, has completed 11 home visits, has had 95 student contacts and has provided 88 trainings.



For the FY10 -11 school year she shall perform the following services:  Provide case management services with area hospitals, Brain and Spinal Cord Injury nurse case manager, TBI students, their parents and various TBI vendors (tutors, behavior specialists).  Provider shall attend IEP and other school meetings, work one-on-one with individual students to reintegrate them back in to school, provide staff development to school district personnel and work with the ESE Department to remain in compliance with Federal and State laws.



The expertise in TBI is rare and there is nobody in the School District currently with this person's level of knowledge.  However, during the FY10 -11 school year we will develop our own staff to be able to carry on these tasks in the future.

The Florida Educational Equity Act, Florida Statutes, Section 1000.05, became law in June 1984 and the State Board of Education adopted the implementing rules in February 1985.



Each year the Commissioner of Education designates the areas of concentration under the requirements of the Educational Equity Act.



The 2009-2010 Plan Update will continue to evaluate the success of methods and strategies to reduce gaps in student performance and to increase participation of under represented groups by race/ethnicity and gender in upper level courses.



 



The review also reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Advanced Placement courses for White, Black and Hispanic male students in grades 9-12. However, the data does not reveal progress in decreasing the enrollment gaps as the White/Black male enrollment gap increased while the White/Hispanic male gap remained the same.



The review also reveals that the dual enrollment rates at grades 9-12 for White, Black and Hispanic male students remained stable for the past three years. However, the data does not reveal progress in decreasing the enrollment gaps as the White/Black and White/Hispanic male enrollment rate gap remained stable.



The review also reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Honors courses for White, Black and Hispanic male students in grades 9-12. While the data does reveal progress in decreasing the White/Hispanic male student enrollment gap, the White/Black male gap remained about the same.



The review also reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Level 3 courses for White, Black and Hispanic male students in grades 9-12. Although the review reveals some progress in decreasing the White/Hispanic male enrollment , no such progress was noted for the White/Black male enrollment gap.



The requirements of the report are included in the executive summary section.



 A copy of the complete report is attached.

I recommend that the School Board approve the District's Florida Educational Equity Act 2009-2010 Annual Update for submission to the Florida Department of Education.
