I recommend the Board approve the FY18 Social Studies Grades 6-12 Instructional Materials listed in attachment A for the District adoption periods indicated and authorize the Board Chairman and Superintendent to sign all required documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to School Board Policy 8.122 (3), district instructional materials adoption committees were convened during 2016-2017 to evaluate and recommend new materials for adoption in the area of social studies, grades K-12.  Committee membership included: subject area classroom teachers and coaches, district level social studies curriculum administrators, and lay citizens.  Committees reviewed all instructional materials programs submitted for state adoption and some additional submissions that were considered appropriate for committee review.  Titles recommended for district adoption are contained in Attachment A.

 

Members were given the opportunity to examine and investigate supplemental materials such as student instructional workbooks, intervention materials, assessments, and online/technological resources. The process included reviewing materials that would address the varying needs or our students, including Students with Disabilities, English Language Learners, Dual Language, Gifted, and diverse students that often do not see their culture represented. 

 

During the review process, the following components were discussed:

 

●     Presentation of the Big Ideas and supporting benchmarks

●     Flow and logical sequencing of ideas and activities

●     Cognitive and complexity levels of materials, student work, and assessments

●     Social Studies process standards

●     Instructional development from concrete to abstract

●     Scaffolding and differentiation of instruction for all levels and all types of learners

●     Incorporation of interactive content and hands-on activities

●     Response to intervention support and materials

●     Alignment of publisher assessments to NGSSS standards and benchmarks and high-stakes test preparation

●     Real world and cross-curricular connections

●     Critical thinking skills

●     Connection to access points and support for Students with Disabilities

●     Multicultural/Cultural representation and English Language Learner support

 

The committee also took into consideration the importance of the new series aligning with the district’s technology requirements in building our learning portals for the use of teachers and students.  

 

After all feedback from district teachers was considered and presentations were viewed, each committee unanimously chose McGraw-Hill as their number one choice.  McGraw-Hill’s support to ESE and ELL was a deciding factor in the decision, as well as their superior online platform. The district will save an additional 1.5 million dollars to the IM budget by going with McGraw Hill for grades 6-12.

 

The recommended instructional materials programs will be provided in both print and/or online digital formats.  Student editions in the core courses will be available to students via their Edline accounts.  Teacher materials and resources will also be available on Blender.  The elementary and middle school recommended instructional materials are available in Spanish for dual language schools.

 

Staff development for instructional materials will be provided at no cost to the District by publishers and will be focused on the appropriate use of materials to implement the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards.
