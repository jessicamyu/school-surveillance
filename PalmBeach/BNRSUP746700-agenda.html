DESCRIPTION:

1. On May 12, 2015, the School Board approved an increase in the leasing rates for use of School Facilities and Ancillary Facilities for SY 2015-2016. (See Board Report)

2. On May 10, 2017, due to countywide economic stability and no changes in rates, the School Board approved a continuation of the same rates for School and Ancillary facilities until the end of FY 2020. (See Board Report)

3. Policy 7.18, Community Use of School Facilities, requires that the Superintendent or designee present to the Board for its approval a District-wide rate schedule for the use of school facilities on a non-profit and commercial basis. The policy also requires that any changes to the rate schedule be approved by the Board.

4. To save the District funds, the proposed rates for Commercial and Non-Profit leases are based on an in-house Facilities Rent Study.

5. Based on this in-house study and the current economy, staff is recommending the following minor procedural and rate changes to be effective on July 1, 2020:
 

● To bring School District facility leasing rates in line with other comparable facilities within Palm Beach County, we are recommending slight increases to our rate schedule, $6 for Commercial and $3 for Nonprofit per leased space.

● A two-hour minimum for all spaces leased during weekends and holidays, when there is no residual air conditioning from the school day.

● All facilities listed on the lease must be leased for the same number of hours. For example, if the Cafeteria and 2 classrooms are leased for an event, all three spaces must be leased for the same number of hours, for maintenance and security reasons.
 

6. The Labor Relations and Budget departments requested that the rate for School Police Officers be increased from $55 to $65 to properly reflect the actual average overtime rate plus benefits.

7. If the staff sees a need for changes in the rate schedule, based on the economy and the market conditions, utility costs, or other changes in conditions, staff will bring back any proposed changes for Board approval.

COM3 Petition for Variance from Waiver of a portion of the School Board Policy 2.54 -- One-Stop Film Permitting
Meeting

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Waiver Petition request filed by Jupiter Entertainment, Inc. only as to the modified film on Pahokee High School and Glades Central High School, that has been shown to the District on May 6, 2020, and as subsequently modified as per Jupiter Entertainment's agreement with the District on May 8, 2020. I further recommend that the Board authorize the Superintendent or designee and Board Chair to sign the attached Order and all documents which are required for its implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

On March 5, 2020, Jupiter Entertainment filed the attached Petition for Variance from or Waiver of a Portion of School Board Policy 2.54--One-Stop Film Permitting with the Board Clerk. The Petition seeks to ask the School Board to permanently waive Section (2)(a) of the Policy that states that films do not use School Board facilities or properties for scenes that "use the school name." The Petition also seeks relief from the permits from the Film Commission that state as a condition of filming: that they "may not film any signs, logos or verbiage containing the name of the school.” Petitioner seeks to be allowed to film and utilize the names, logos, and signage of the schools featured in the documentary film project that mentions four high schools.
 
The film here is extensively concerning Pahokee High School and Glades Central High School, shows their facilities and frequently refers to the schools by name.Palm Beach Lakes High School and William T. Dwyer High School are also mentioned in this film as the other schools in some filmed sporting events.
 
The Petition was filed pursuant to Florida Statutes § 120.542(5), Florida Administrative Code § 28-104.002(1), and School Board Policy 1.120, which follows the Statute and rule. The Board timely provided Notice of the Petition and the notice which was posted on the District website remains at: https://go.boarddocs.com/fl/palmbeach/Board.nsf/Public#tab-policies . No written comments to the Petition were received within the 14 day time limit after publication of the Notice or thereafter..
 

 

Within 90 days after receipt of the original petition, the Board must finish processing the petition. Waiver of a School Board Policy shall be granted when these two conditions are met:

(1) a person subject to a policy demonstrates that the purpose of the underlying statute will be or has been achieved by other means by the person, and

(2) when application of the Policy would create a substantial hardship or would violate principles of fairness.
 
Note, the statutes upon which this policy is based (as stated at the bottom of the policy) are general statutes concerning the authority and powers of the school board and superintendent, This includes controlling property,managing facilities, rulemaking authority to determine programs and policies to improve the school system, and providing for the proper attention to health, safety, and other matters which will best promote the welfare of children, These statutes do not address the specific issue of naming a school in a film.
 
A “substantial hardship” means a demonstrated economic, technological, legal, or other type of hardship to the person requesting the variance or waiver, while “principles of fairness” are violated when the literal application of a rule affects a particular person in a manner significantly different from the way it affects other similarly situated persons who are subject to the rule. The Board may limit the duration of any grant of a variance or waiver or otherwise impose conditions on the grant only to the extent necessary for the purpose of the underlying statute to be achieved.
 
The principals of Pahokee High School and Glades Central High School, their Regional Superintendent , and the Communications office, had a chance to view all the videos and comment. On May 8, 2020, more edit requests were submitted to Jupiter Entertainment. Jupiter Entertainment verbally agreed to make more revisions. The Deputy superintendent will review the updated documentary, when it is available, to see if the revisions are satisfactory.
 
The Board is required to grant or deny the petition by order. The Board’s failure to grant or deny the petition within 90 days results in the petition being deemed approved. A copy of the order shall contain a statement of the relevant facts and reasons supporting the Board’s action and shall be filed with the Administrative Procedures Committee.
 
A proposed Order granting the Petition is attached hereto.

The Palm Beach County Youth Services Department provides these funds to our schools based on the number of students enrolled to attend up to 100 students in the summer programs per school. 

 

Due to COVID-19, the Palm Beach County Youth Services Department agreed to let our schools receive the grants for this summer, and are allowing us to continue working in a Virtual setting. Teachers hired will work daily and Fridays will be used for Virtual Field Trips and Virtual PE activities for 4 hours. This grant is used for students in grades K-8. Our parents look forward to this program each summer, as it is no cost to parents to participate in this program.
