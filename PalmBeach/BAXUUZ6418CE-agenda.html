Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with the Achievement Center for Children and Families to provide summer camp for children of Delray Full Service Center.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement will provide summer camp for children of Delray Full Service Center (DFSC) students and will assist in retaining our students during the summer. Traditionally there has been a drop in attendance when the K-12 school years ends. Parents of K-12 students cannot afford daycare or pay for summer camp and therefore, must stay home to care for their children.

 

Providing summer camp for children of DFSC students will increase the outreach to Delray Beach community and support the mission of both Delray Full Service Center and the School District of Palm Beach County. The academic focus of this camp will reduce or eliminate "summer slide" for the children of DFSC students.

 

Enrollment in the camp will have a positive academic impact on our families' children and the elementary and middle schools in Delray Beach.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with Faro Motta, M.D., P.A., effective May 2, 2019 through June 30, 2024; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement. This agreement originated with Jupiter High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

DESCRIPTION:

The purpose of this MOU is to establish cooperative workforce training, employment, and economic development efforts between Eckerd Connects Workforce Development Program and the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act Youth program with the Department of Adult and Community Education, School District of Palm Beach County. 

This agreement is intended to coordinate resources and ensure the effective delivery of workforce services such as Adult Education job training and human services while targeting disengaged youth between the ages of 17 to 24. 

Eckerd Connects will provide funding for tuition for students in the Boynton/Delray areas that participate in industry recognized credential training through Adult Education.  They assist with soft skills instruction, job placement, college assistance and paid internships.  Youth will be provided transportation and clothing assistance as well as earning up to $750 during training and up to $2,000 for paid internships.  In order to qualify, students can be high school drop- outs, or graduates that have barriers such as low income, ESE students, DJJ re-entry students or teen parents.
