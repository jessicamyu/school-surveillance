This Project Modification contains the following item:







  • Architect Additional Services in the amount of $2,700.00.




  • Description: Prepare and submit civil, plumbing and electrical plans, and provide bidding and contract administration for a lift station for the covered play area.




  • Cause: School District Requested.




  • Justification: The covered play area was relocated to the south side of the site to make room for the modular addition scheduled to start in 2011.  The south side of the site is at a lower elevation which resulted in the need for a lift station for the sanitary sewer system.

  • This three-year successor contract has a duration of July 1, 2009 through June 30, 2012 with reopeners for the second and third school years on salary, the salary schedule (Appendix A), two additional appendices and four additional Sections selected by each of the parties.




  • For the 2009-2010 school year, the teachers’ salary schedule remains unchanged, the value of the step is zero and no step increment is provided.  All full-time and half-time instructional employees who were in an active pay status for at least 99 days during the 2009-2010 school year and who remain employees of the District as of today’s date will be paid a one-time, non-reoccurring bonus of $500, minus standard deductions.




  • High Schools and other secondary Vocational, Alternative or Special Schools that are classified as being an Intervene, a Correct II, a Turn-Around or a Transition School may be required by the District to implement an eight period instructional schedule or a 4 X 4 block or a modified block schedule (or a seven period schedule at a Middle School that is so classified).

  • Effective upon School Board adoption, each teacher’s duty-free lunch period will be at least 30 minutes in duration.  In the event a school cannot provide a 30-minute duty-free lunch period for each instructional employee at that school, the issue will be brought to the attention of the appropriate Area Superintendent for final resolution.

IN1 - ADOPTION OF HEALTH AND OTHER INSURANCE PROVISIONS OF THE COLLECTIVE BARGAINING AGREEMENTS
Meeting
Oct 13, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

The above listed Unions/Associations come together as a coalition to negotiate with the District over changes to the Health, Dental, Life And Vision Insurances offered by the District to its employees.  Coalition bargaining on insurances has existed for approximately 16 years.  The coalition members and District negotiators reached a tentative agreement on these changes in the health insurance benefits to be effective January 1, 2011.  In addition, the Principals and the Staff Associations have been informed of these changes and given the opportunity to provide feedback.



 



In compliance with insurance cost trends, the District has shared it expectation of an 8 to 10% increase in the cost of health insurance in 2011 and again in 2012.  With that in mind, these coalition negotiations have resulted in a unified recognition that employees must become more aware of their health conditions and take positive steps to prevent further health problems in the future.  Thus, the adoption of the Biometric & Health Assessment program as a part of this tentative agreement.



 



Summary of Changes:



-Continues no premium increases to employees through calendar 2011



-Requires new employees to enroll in the EPO plan (referred to as Catastrophic HMO in the Contract) for at least 12 months.  The EPO is similar to the regular HMO but with higher office visit co-pays, a $500 annual deductible and 20% co–insurance for tests and hospitalizations.



-To comply with Health Reform Laws, removes co-pays for preventative care and the plan maximums in the EPO (Catastrophic HMO), regular HMO and PPO Plans 



-To comply with Health Reform Laws, dependents to age 26 may be enrolled as dependents of employees, (removes student status and residency requirements)



- Eliminates the POS Plan, but continues the PPO, regular HMO and EPO (Catastrophic HMO) Plans



-Reduces monthly 401(a) District contributions for those who waive medical coverage from $120 to $100 for full-time employees and from $90 to $50 for part-time employees.



-Eliminates the monthly 401(a) Board contributions of $20 for part-time employees who enroll in the “employee only coverage” under the EPO (Catastrophic HMO) Plan.



-Defines the “default plan” as “employee only coverage” under the EPO (Catastrophic HMO) Plan



-Establishes a minimum monthly increase in employee contributions to their medical premiums of $50 effective January 1, 2012



-Establishes Biometrics and Health Assessment requirements that if completed by August 1, 2011 will result in a $50 per month premium savings effective January 1, 2012



-Establishes a monthly employee paid premium surcharge of $50 for tobacco users effective January 1, 2012



- To comply with Health Reform Laws, in order to receive reimbursement from one’s Flexible Medical Spending Account, over-the-counter drugs must have a doctor’s prescription



- Employees on unpaid, non-Family Medical Leave Act (FMLA) leave may continue benefits, but only in keeping with COBRA rules & regulations and limitations

Recommendation:

I recommend that the Board approve the Contract amendments on Health and other Insurances that were ratified by the Association of Educational Secretaries and Office Professionals on 09/28/10, the Classroom Teachers Association on 09/30/10, the Police Benevolent Association on 10/04/10 and pending ratification by the Service Employees International Union/Florida Public Services Union, to be effective January 1, 2011; and to adopt these same amended provisions for all eligible District non-bargaining employees effective January 1, 2011.

PROCLAMATION



Walk to School Month



           



Whereas, the School Board of Palm Beach County is proclaiming the month of October as Walk to School month to coincide with the International Walk to School program; and



 



Whereas, the International Walk to School program is in its 13th year; and is in effect in more than 40 countries to celebrate the benefits of safely walking to school; and



 



Whereas, Walk to School Month is intended to encourage more families to get out of the car and onto their feet and walk their children to school; and



 



Whereas, the School District is working with the schools in helping students learn to stay safe whenwalking  to and from school; and



 



Whereas, the lack of physical activity plays a leading role in rising rates of obesity in children; and



 



Whereas, children who walk to school will increase their physical activity and improve their health; and



 



Whereas, the School Board has received from the Florida Department of Transportation grant funds for Safe Routes to School (SRTS) program, which is a sustained effort that involves parents, schools, community leaders and local, state, and federal governments in enabling and encouraging children to walk and bicycle to school; and



 



Whereas, SRTS activities include constructing sidewalks, raising awareness of school zones and encouraging students to take active trips to school; and



 



Whereas, the School District staff works closely with the County and local governments in providing safe routes to school;



 



NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Superintendent and the School Board of Palm Beach County do hereby declare the month of October as Walk to School Month and encourage parents and students in Palm Beach County to walk to school.

PL-2 Approval of Joint Participation Agreement with FDOT and Resolution to assist students to walk or bicycle safely to school.
Meeting
Oct 13, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)


  • The School District Planning Department submitted a grant application to Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT) for non-infrastructure funds to assist students attending 12 elementary schools and 3 middle schools in Palm Beach County to walk and bicycle safely to school. 


 





  • This grant will provide assistance to these schools which all have attendance boundaries.  These monies are to be used to involve and educate the community and students to walk and bicycle safely to school.


 





  • On May 21, 2010, the District received a letter from FDOT informing us that a grant in the amount of $129,200 has been awarded to fund non-infrastructure projects and activities that would assist students in walking and bicycling safely to school.  Activities planned include:  teaching the Florida Traffic and Bicycle Safety Education Program, providing training for parents, teachers and staff in ways to assist students in walking or bicycling to school safely, collecting pertinent data on students and providing students with rewards such as helmets, bicycle bells, reflectors etc. 


 





  • On September 16, 2010, the Joint Participation Agreement was received from FDOT which requires School Board approval.


 





  • School District staff works closely with the County in planning for safe routes to school.  Some of our accomplishments include obtaining 2.3 million dollars in infrastructure funds for sidewalks and flashers, prioritizing sidewalk projects annually which are funded by the Board of County Commissioners and working with developers to ensure sidewalks are in place for students to walk or bicycle safely to school.


 





  • The District plans to identify infrastructure needs and apply to FDOT for infrastructure funds to address the needs.


 





  • School District Planning Staff represents the School District on the Metropolitan Technical Advisory Committee and Bicycle, Pathways and Pedestrian Committee.


 





  • School District staff has formed a Safe Routes to School coalition which includes a representative from the County’s MPO, the Palm Beach County Health Department, Community Partnership - Safe Schools; Commit to be Fit, the School District’s Transportation, Wellness Program, Robert Wood Johnson, and Curriculum.


 





  • The coalition will host a Safety Fair in early 2011.  The first Safety Fair held in April 2010 was successful and hosted by the School District’s Planning and Transportation staff.  The parents and students learned about walking and bicycling safely to school and received helmets, backpacks and pedometers donated from the Community Partnership and FDOT.


 





  • FDOT also gave to the School District a bicycle trailer and 32 new bicycles which staff plans to use in the physical education programs at our schools.


 





  • FDOT is focusing on providing safe routes to all elementary and middle schools.  There are various funding sources.   The County received monies from FDOT to work with the University of Miami in also addressing safe routes to school.


 





  • The School District staff plans to prepare a Comprehensive Plan to include all of the elementary and middle schools.  The Plan will include maps showing safe routes to school, it will identify infrastructure needs, establish programs to encourage students to walk or bicycle to school, show data on the number of students walking to schools and contain accidents reports.


 





  •  School District planning staff will coordinate closely with the Transportation Department, Palm Beach County Traffic Department and the Cities in which these schools are located in implementing the activities included in the grant application.


 





  • The School District has until December 2011 to expend these funds that will be awarded to us on a reimbursement basis

Recommendation:

 I recommend the School Board approve the Joint Participation Agreement and Resolution accepting the grant from the Florida Department of Transportation for non-infrastructure funds to encourage students to walk and bicycle safely to school and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

  • Atlantic Community High School has a Career Academy Program, known as the Eagle Nest Program, that provides training for those students interested in a career in the construction industry.  The program provides the opportunity for participants to obtain practical experience by designing and building houses. 


 





  • This endeavor is supported by the City of Delray Beach and the Delray Beach CRA.  The City will provide the land upon which the house will be built and the CRA will provide $130,000.00 to the project.  This money will be granted to the City and the City will disburse the funds to the School District for the construction effort.  The completed house will then be available for purchase through the City’s First Time Homebuyer Program.  Upon the sale of the house, the CRA will be reimbursed for its initial investment of $130,000.00 plus 10% of the profit and the School and Program will get the remaining 90% of the profit.


 





  • This program is important to all three participants in that it helps to provide a useful skill to District students and will help the City and the CRA to fill the need for affordable housing in the area.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement (ILA) and Multiple Party  Agreement between the City of Delray Beach, the Community Redevelopment Agency (CRA) of Delray Beach and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the Atlantic Community High School Career Academy Program, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.
