Subject
FMT1 Ratification of ISDAfix Antitrust Litigation Settlement
Meeting
Aug 1, 2018 - 6. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board ratify the ISDAfix Antitrust Litigation Settlement.

DESCRIPTION:

Over the past decade, many groups including the State Attorney Generals for roughly half of the States have been working to investigate and pursue remedies for any inappropriate actions in the financial markets during the financial crisis. Many settlements have been reached and the School Board has participated three settlements in 2012, 2016 and 2017. As additional settlements are reached and the School Board is notified they are a possible party, staff reviews the information to determine if it is appropriate to join the suit.
 

The School Board received notice of another settlement, which includes all parties that participated in an ISDAfix Instrument between January 1, 2006 and January 31, 2014. During that window of time, the School Board entered into a constant maturity swap with UBS. The transaction allowed the District to swap a short term borrowing rate with a long term borrowing rate and resulted in more than $12 million of savings due to the unusual interest rate yield curve observed during the recession. The lawsuit claims that the defendants engaged in anticompetitive acts that affected the market.  A settlement has been reached with the ten defendants, including UBS.
 

During discussions with the District’s Financial Advisor, staff learned the following:

  • When the transaction was executed in 2006 and subsequently transferred to another entity in 2011, the pricing was compared to market indices and were determined to be at a fair market price.
     
  • At the time of the transactions, the District had no reason to believe any inappropriate activity occurred. Documents were signed by the parties specifically stating that the transactions were completed based on all applicable rules and regulations.

The District was notified of the settlement agreement by the District’s Financial Advisor on Friday, July 13, 2018. The deadline to submit a claim form was Monday, July 16, 2018.   The Financial Management Team and Office of General Counsel worked together to review the information and determine how best to proceed.  Staff agreed it was in the best interest of the District to submit the claim timely and qualify to receive a portion of the predetermined settlement.  Due to the time constraints, a memo was sent to the Board and the claim filed on July 16, 2018.  The agreement is presented to the School Board for ratification on August 1, 2018.

 

The Finance Committee was apprised of the settlement agreement at their meeting on July 26, 2018.

CS1 Eagle Arts Academy, Inc. – Proposed Immediate Termination of the Charter Contract
Meeting

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Immediate Termination of the Charter with Eagle Arts Academy, Inc., and approve the recommendation to not assume operation of Eagle Arts Academy during the pendency of any hearing, based upon the reasons stated in this report and its attachments; and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents, take all appropriate action, and provide Eagle Arts Academy, Inc. with an Immediate Termination letter.

DESCRIPTION:

 

On July 30, 2014, the School Board granted Eagle Arts Academy, Inc. a five (5) year charter agreement with the School Board.  The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.    

 

Eagle Arts Academy currently serves 273 students in grades K-8.

 

The School Board voted on March 14, 2018, to provide a 90-Day Notice of Termination of Eagle Arts Academy, Inc. (EAA) for good cause.

 

The Superintendent is now recommending an Immediate Termination of EAA based on the reasons stated in the attached July 26, 2018 letter. (See Exhibit A.) Additionally, for the reasons stated in the July 26, 2018 letter, the Superintendent recommends that the School Board not assume operation of Eagle Arts Academy during the pendency of any hearing because the continued operation of the School would materially threaten the health, safety, and welfare of the students. See § 1002.33(8)(c), Fla. Stat. (2018).

 

Alternatively, the Governing Board of EAA may voluntarily terminate the Charter Contract in accordance with the Contract’s provisions prior to this Board meeting.  To voluntarily terminate the Charter, EAA is requested to submit a written notice of voluntary termination and a copy of the pertinent Governing Board minutes to the Charter Schools Department by noon before the School Board meeting as noted in S.B. Policy 2.57.

 

By letter dated July 26, 2018 (Exhibit A) hereto, EAA was notified of this Board meeting.

 

Furthermore, the attached proposed Immediate Termination Notice (Exhibit A) details those reasons for the recommended termination of Eagle Arts Academy, Inc.

 

Also attached is a copy of the Charter Contract.
