The School District of Palm Beach County has 1,371 students with Autism Spectrum Disorder.  The School District is proud to partner with the Autism Speaks organization in its dedication to increasing awareness of autism spectrum disorders.  Autism Speaks was founded in 2005 with a goal of funding research into the causes, prevention, treatments, and cure for autism.  Autism is a lifelong neurological disorder that impairs a person's ability to communicate and relate to others.  It is the fastest-growing serious developing disorder in the United States.  Today, a shocking 1 in 110 children are diagnosed with autism and 1 in 70 boys are diagnosed with autism.  Sunday, March 7, Autism Speaks will hold its annual Walk for Autism on Flagler Drive in West Palm Beach.  Many of our schools have already formed teams.  In addition, for the 2nd year, the School District will have its own Team, headed by ESE Director, Laura Pincus.  Please join the ESE Department as we support our families impacted by Autism.

The 2009-2010 Palm Beach County Outstanding Student, Adult and Senior School Volunteers are: Brandon Corsentino a senior at Palm Beach Central High School - Student Volunteer of the Year, Debbie Ellman from Spanish River High School - Adult Volunteer of the Year and Arnold Rich from Park Vista High School - Senior Volunteer of the Year. 



Many students are involved in a myriad of extra-curricular activities.  However, very few have the ability, or sense of responsibility, to balance their time as Brandon Corsentino of Palm Beach Central High School does.  The Wellington resident is ranked 11th in his class of 644 students, succeeding in both Advanced Placement and Honors classes, while actively participating in varsity football, coaching of flag football, Key Club, BETA Club, and National Honor Society.  Through these clubs, he has coordinated and garnered school participation in countless school-related community service activities, such as St. Baldrick’s Day, Relay for Life, Hispanic Human Resources Council, Quantum House, Gold Coast Down Syndrome’s Buddy Walk and aiding students in nearby Title I elementary schools. 



Debbie Ellman has volunteered her time and talents at three different Palm Beach County schools during the past thirteen years. The Boca Raton resident is currently Spanish River High School’s PTSA President, and has also served at Omni Middle School and Del Prado Elementary.  She has created several innovative community involvement programs at Spanish River High School. When Debbie’s oldest child started at Spanish River, she experienced how the transition may be difficult for middle school parents.  Debbie took the initiative and developed a program entitled “To the River” where parents of middle school students are invited to Spanish River High for tours and an open house.  Debbie has tirelessly served in fundraising, mentoring, and other active roles in our schools as well as serving as a board member of the Children’s Place at Home Safe. 



Arnold Rich is a dedicated volunteer and school volunteer coordinator for Park Vista Community High School.  Since the opening of the school in August, 2004, Mr. Rich has recruited over 500 school volunteers, half of which are seniors from the surrounding communities. The Boynton Beach resident has personally contributed in excess of 5,400 hours volunteering at the school since 2004.  A retired businessman, Mr. Rich, utilizes his business skills and expertise as a member of the Academies Board of Directors. In addition, he is a guest speaker in the Medical Academy with presentations on end of life experiences and Hospice.  Mr. Rich also volunteers for Hospice of Palm Beach County.  He has organized substantial fund-raisers, gives presentations, mentors, and tutors English Language Learners (ELL) students.  He also serves as an assistant coach with the school’s golf team.

This Board Agenda Item identifies a Cooperative Agreement between Nova Southeastern University and the School District of Palm Beach County that will allow the School District to be a joint partner in a Parent Resource Center (PRC) that will be housed at the Hispanic Human Resources Council, Inc.   Nova Southeastern University will provide two full time parent liaisons to the PRC who will be funded by the State of Florida DOE's Voluntary Public School Choice (VPSC) Grant and administered by the University.  The University will also provide all needed materials, equipment and office furniture for the PRC liaisons.

The Department of Charter Schools received an application, on August 3, 2009, from Florida High School for Accelerated Learning Palm Beach County ("FL HS for Accel. Learn. PB County") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2010.



The application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A"). 



The instrument and the application process were explained to the applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop that was held on May 28, 2009 (see Exhibit "B").



The District Reviewers were trained on the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Application Process on May 15, 2009 (see Exhibit "C").



The Reviewers evaluated the 21 sections of the written application.  Four of the 21 sections were rated "Partially Meets the Standard or Did Not Meet the Standard," meaning the responses addressed most of the criteria, but lacked meaningful detail and required important additional information (see Exhibit "D").



Partially Meets the Standard:
Section 5 - Assessment
Section 10 - Governance
Section 12 - ESP
Section 19- Budget



The responses to each section that did not meet the stated criteria served as the basis of inquiry for the interview.  Please refer to Step 5:  Applicant Interview (see Exhibit "E").



Step 6, second paragraph in the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process states, "The sponsoring board will only approve applicants that demonstrate quality in all components of the application process. An application cannot have significant weaknesses in some components of the application and still be judged to have met the standard for approval," (see Exhibit "F").  Each applicant received communication that outlined the proposed recommendation, the evaluator's second review following the interview, summary of the interview, and was granted the option to voluntarily withdraw the application or allow the application to be placed on the Board agenda for action (see Exhibit "G").



Pursuant to F.S.1002.33 (5)(c):
"An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than 30 calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ."



A copy of FL HS for Accel. Learn. PB County's application is in the Board Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of Florida High School for Accelerated Learning Palm Beach County and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.
