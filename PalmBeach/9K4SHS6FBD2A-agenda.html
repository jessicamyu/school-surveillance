On or about August 24, 2011, Ms. O'Donnell became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation when on August 16, 2011 Ms. O'Donnell reported to work late for her scheduled duty day, which marked the first day for teachers to report to work.  On September 22, 2011, Ms. O'Donnell again reported to work late.  Ms. O'Donnell's disciplinary history includes several directives regarding reporting to work on time and both a Verbal Reprimand (Written Notation) and a Written Reprimand for failing to report to work on time and repeated failure to follow directives.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.



Therefore, the actions violated the following:  Sandpiper Shores Elementary Employee Handbook, Page 13; School Board Policy 0.01 (2)(c), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; Rule 6B-1.006 (3) (a), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 3.02(4)(a), and (4)(j), Code of Ethics; Rule 6B-1.001(2) & (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment; School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; and Rule 6B-4.009 (4), F.A.C., Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal.  Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. O'Donnel completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02 (Code of Ethics) and acknowledged such on January 18, 2011.



There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Shannon O'Donnell.



On December 19, 2011, the "Notice of Five (5) Day Suspension" was hand-delivered to Ms. O'Donnell from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

 I.            Overview



On August 1, 2011, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Florida International Language Academy (FILA) to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.  Per the listing of applicants filed with the State of Florida, FILA has not submitted an application with any other county, nor do they have a charter school presently operating in the State of Florida.



The mission of Florida International Language Academy is to deliver a first-class academic program that offers a unique bilingual, bi-literate and bi-cultural curriculum.  FILA will prepare students in foreign language acquisition in grades K-8.  Students will be prepared for advanced language study at the secondary and postsecondary levels, as well as develop into culturally-literate, responsible citizens in a more globalized future.



The District’s charter application process was explained to applicants during the New Applicants’ Workshop held on May 27, 2011.  The applicant was invited and did not attend.



A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.



II.            Projected Enrollment



First Year         K-8      364 Projected Enrollment



Second Year    K-8      546 Projected Enrollment



Third Year       K-8      728 Projected Enrollment



Fourth Year     K-8      728 Projected Enrollment



Fifth Year        K-8      728 Projected Enrollment



 



III.            Education Program Design



The Educational Program Design at Florida International Language Academy is Traditional (same core subject areas as other District schools) and Bilingual – (English and Spanish).



IV.            Location



The applicant has not chosen a desired location at this time.  Based on a minimum requirement of 60 sq. ft. per student (50 sq. ft. office space and other) and a projected initial enrollment of 364 students, the minimum initial square footage will be around 22,000 sq. ft.



V.            Applicant’s Founding Governing Board



The Founding Board members are local.



VI.           District’s Reviewers Evaluation Ratings of  Florida International Language Academy’s Application



Designated District staff reviewed the 19 sections of Florida International Language Academy’s application. There were two evaluations of this application.  After the first evaluation, Florida International Language Academy had 6 of 19 sections that received a rating of (Partially Meets the Standard), 3 sections that received a rating of (Does Not Meet the Standard), and one section that received a rating of (Not Applicable).

On or about October 6, 2011, Ms. Hess became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of Failure to Fulfill the Responsibilities of a Teacher; Failure to Protect the Student from Conditions Harmful to Learning or to Health and Safety; Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment; Ethical Misconduct and Failure to follow Policy/Directive or Rule.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.



On October 6, 2011, the Assistant Principal found a male Kindergarten student wandering by himself in the hallways at Wynnebrook Elementary School.  The student told the Assistant Principal that he had been sent by Ms. Hess to find two other missing students.  Ms. Hess did not notify the office that any students were missing, nor request the assistance of any adults to locate the missing students.  Ms. Hess' disciplinary history contains a Written Reprimand dated September 19, 2011 for failing to provide adequate supervision of Kindergarten students.



Therefore, the actions violated the following:  Wynnebrook Elementary School 2011-2012 Employee Handbook, Section F, Teacher Responsibilities; School Board policy 0.01 (2)(c), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; Rule 6B-1.006 (3)(a), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 3.02 (4)(a), (4)(b), (4)(h) and (4)(j), The Code of Ethics; 6B-1.001 (2) & (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida and School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff.  Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Hess completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02 (Code of Ethics) and acknowledged such on May 12, 2010.



There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Laura Hess.



On December 19, 2011, the "Notice of Ten (10) Day Suspension" was hand-delivered to Ms. Hess from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Laura Hess' ten (10) day suspension without pay, effective January 27, 2012 subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

On October 3, 2011, Mr. Romero became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation when on September 7, 2011, the Principal visited Mr. Romero's classroom to observe a lesson.  During his visit, he noticed that activities and directives given by Mr. Romero were not consistent with the objective stated on the board.  Mr. Romero was unable to provide the Principal with strategies, materials, or activities supporting the objective listed on the board, or reflected on the lesson plans.  Mr. Romero has had prior incidents of the same or similar behavior that have resulted in a Verbal Reprimand with Written Notation and two Written Reprimands for insubordination, poorly composed lesson plans and failure to exercise best professional judgment.  Additionally, the Principal provided copies of two administrative directives regarding lesson plans, which Mr. Romero has failed to follow.



Therefore, the actions violated the following:  School Board Policy 1.013(4), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; The Collective Bargaining Agreement with CTA, Article II, Section U, Lesson Plans; Rule 6B-1.006(3)(a) & (d), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for Educators; School Board Policy 0.01(2)(b) & (c), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; Rule 6B-1.001(2) & (3), F.A.C., The Code of Ethics of the Education Profession; School Board Policy 0.00(1), Preamble to the Code; School Board Policy 3.02(4)(a),(4)(b),(4)(h), & (4)(j); The Code of Ethics; School Board Policy 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment; and School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel.  Furthermore, it is noted that Mr. Romero completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02 (Code of Ethics) and acknowledged such on May 5, 2010.



There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Michael Romero.



On December 19, 2011, the "Notice of Five (5) Day Suspension" was hand-delivered to Mr. Romero from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Michael Romero's five (5) day suspension without pay, effective January 27, 2012, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.
