  1. To identify that Renaissance Charter School at East Palm Beach is now to be known as Renaissance Charter School at Wellington; and
  2. That a significant amount of time has lapsed since the original application was submitted in 2011.  The new approval will provide the School the full five (5) year Charter extended to 2019, rather than the previous approval period ending in 2017.

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

PS1 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Lamarcus Thomas
Meeting
Jan 8, 2014 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
11. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Lamarcus Thomas' three-day suspension without pay, effective January 24, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about September 10, 2013, Mr. Thomas became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Violation – On Duty, Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Violation – Second Offense, Ethical Misconduct and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

On September 10, 2013, a trained administrator at Lake Shore Middle contacted Professional Standards to report that he had reason to suspect that employee Lamarcus Thomas was under the influence of drugs or alcohol.  The administrator documented observable criteria including: leaving early without permission, failing to complete assignments, red eyes, asking for food, and declining performance.  A lab technician was dispatched to the site to collect blood and urine for analysis.  Mr. Thomas was subsequently, assigned to his home.  On September 17, 2013, Professional Standards received a copy of the drug test results from NMS Laboratories.  The test results indicated that Mr. Thomas violated the School District’s Drug Free Workplace Policy 3.96 (4)(c).  Mr. Thomas completed a treatment program through EAP and was cleared to return to work.  Mr. Thomas’ disciplinary history includes a Written Reprimand issued on March 8, 2013, for an off-duty conviction for charges of possession of Marijuana and Operating a Vehicle while Driver’s License is Suspended/Canceled or Revoked.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.96(4)(c) and (2)(v), Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy;  3.02(4)(a), (4)(j) and (5)(b)(i), Code of Ethics, and 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel.  Furthermore, it is noted that Mr. Thomas completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics and acknowledged such on April 7, 2010.   

 

There is “just cause” to support the suspension of Mr. Lamarcus Thomas. 

 

 

On December 6, 2013, the “Notice of Recommendation for Three Day Suspension Without Pay” was hand-delivered to Mr. Thomas from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend the Board approve Derrick Campbell's termination from employment, effective January 24, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about July 11, 2013, Mr. Campbell became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Inappropriate Comments to Students (Sexual Harassment); Inappropriate Comments to Co-Workers (Sexual Harassment); Ethical Misconduct; and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

On July 11, 2013, Professional Standards received a School Police report that indicated Derrick Campbell had made inappropriate comments to two female students at Village Academy.  The School Police Report also chronicled several accounts from three female employees from the cafeteria at the school who allege that Mr. Campbell made inappropriate comments of a sexual nature.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies  5.81(2),Protecting Students from Sexual Harassment & Discrimination, 3.02(4)(a), (4)(d), (4)(e) and (4)(g), (5)(a)(v), (5)(a)(vii), (5)(i), Code of Ethics;  3.19(3), Policy Prohibiting Discrimination and Harassment, and 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Employees.  Note, Mr. Campbell has no prior discipline since he commenced employment with the School District in April 2012.

 

There is “just cause” to support the termination of employment of Mr. Derrick Campbell.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about April 16, 2013, Ms. Sandhoff became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Excessive Tardiness and Abuse of Leave, Insubordination/Failure to Follow Policy Rule or Directive and Unethical Conduct.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

Beginning January 2012, Ms. Sandhoff has repeatedly been late for work without following the procedures established by the school.  Ms. Sandhoff has received two memorandums regarding this behavior in addition to a Written Notation of Verbal Reprimand and a Written Reprimand for Failure to Report to Work on Time and Repeated Failure to Follow Directives and Gross Insubordination.  After receiving the Written Reprimand, Ms. Sandhoff was late for work eleven (11) more times.  Additionally, Ms. Sandhoff utilized fifty-seven (57) hours of sick leave prior to the time that it was earned or accumulated.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.80(1), Leave of Absence; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment; 3.02(4)(a), (4)(f), and (4)(j), Code of Ethics and 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel, as well as the Collective Bargaining Agreement with the Classroom Teachers Association, Article III, Section B(1)(a) and B(1)(b), Employee Hours and Conditions; Article III, Section (E)(1), Responsibility of Absent Employees, Article V, Section B(1)(b), Leaves (Specific Paid Leaves).  This conduct violates sec. 1012.67, F.S., Absence without Leave.  Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Sandhoff completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on June 7, 2010.  Note:  Ms. Sandhoff’s disciplinary history includes a Specific Incident Memorandum issued on January 26, 2012 for Tardiness and School and Policy(ies) Violations; Verbal Reprimand (Written Notation) issued on March 13, 2012 for Failure to Use Acceptable Professional Judgment; Written Reprimand issued on June 5, 2012 for Tardiness and Gross Insubordination; Memorandum issued on December 6, 2012 for Tardiness; Specific Incident Memorandum issued on April 15, 2013 for Absenteeism. 

 

There is “just cause” to support the suspension of Ms. Cynthia Sandhoff.

I recommend the Board approve Mary Maloney's termination from employment, effective January 24, 2014 subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about August 16, 2013, Ms. Maloney became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Violation – Off Duty; Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Violation – Second Offense; Off Duty Criminal Conduct; Ethical Misconduct; and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

On January 13, 2013, Ms. Maloney was arrested for several criminal charges including Driving Under the Influence and Bribery of a Public Official.  Due to the nature of the incident, Ms. Maloney’s arrest resulted in a great deal of local media attention.  Ms. Maloney was placed into a non-student contact position while the charges were pending.   On July 22 2013, Ms. Maloney was adjudicated guilty of Driving Under the Influence – Causing Injury to Person or Property; Leaving the Scene of a Crash involving Damage and Resisting an Officer Without Violence.  All other charges, including felony bribery charge, were dismissed.   Ms. Maloney’s disciplinary history includes a Written Reprimand issued on October 12, 2009 for violating School Board Policy 3.96, The Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy.  This reprimand was issued as a result of a conviction for a violation of 316.193 F.S., Driving Under the Influence.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policy 3.96(4)(b) and (2)(v), Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy,  3.02(4)(b), (5)(h)(iv) Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment and School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel as well as 6A-10.080(3) F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida (formerly 6B-1.001).  Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Maloney completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on April 22, 2010.

 

There is “just cause” to support the termination of employment of Ms. Mary Maloney. 

 

On December 6, 2013, the “Notice of Recommendation for Termination from Employment” was hand-delivered to Ms. Maloney from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.
