Palm Beach County's future prosperity depends on nurturing the healthy development of the more than 298,000 children currently living, growing and learning in our many diverse communities.  The abuse and neglect of children can cause severe, costly and lifelong problems, affecting all of society, including physical and mental health illnesses, school failure and criminal behavior.  The Florida Department of Children and Families joins public and private sector agencies, childcare and early learning professionals, advocates for children and young adults, and residents during the month of April and year-round in increasing understanding of child development, reducing parental isolation and helping to prevent abuse.



The Department of Children and Families (DCF), Child and Family Connections, and The Department of Afterschool Programming students in District elementary afterschool programs have joined together for the statewide “Hanging of the Hands” advocate project.  During Children’s Week, April  3-9, 2011, student artwork will proudly be displayed at the Capitol Rotunda in Tallahassee.

Back To Nature Day

Established in 1998, the Arthur R. Marshall Foundation is based on the principles of visionary environmental advocate, Arthur R. Marshall, Jr. (1919-1985). For more than a decade, the Arthur R. Marshall Foundation has championed the restoration and preservation of the greater Everglades ecosystem through science-based outreach programs and hands-on activities to return original plant life and water flow patterns to the historic “River of Grass.”



The Foundation’s efforts to develop future stewards of the environment, encourage appreciation for Florida’s natural resources, and ensure safe drinking water for generations to come have only been possible thanks to the personal dedication and support of the community. During the past decade, Marshall Foundation volunteers have planted almost 100,000 native Florida trees in wetlands areas and helped harvest close to 3,000 pounds of cypress seeds.



More than $350,000 in scholarships and internships has been awarded to students pursuing careers in environmental sciences, and, in 2008 alone, more than 7,500 school-aged children participated in Marshall Foundation education programs such as Ambassadors of the Everglades, Everglades2U, and Wetlands: Weird, Wild and Wonderful.

Barbara Borello, Media Clerk, is the Lighthouse Elementary School nominee to the 2011 Florida School-Related Employee of the Year.  Ms. Borello has worked in various areas of responsibility at Lighthouse Elementary, with the last two years in the Media Center.  As the Media Clerk, she arrives early and stays late to help with the increasing demands of the Media Center.  Some of her innovations include; organizing a check-in procedure for aftercare bus students to ensure their safety, initiating production room training for our junior volunteers, and overseeing the center volunteers.  Ms. Borello attends available trainings to bring new ideas back to the Media Center, and she actively participates in Book Fair Family Nights, monthly Media Nights and the Barnes and Noble Family Read-Aloud twice a year.  To generate extra excitement for the students on Book Fair Family Nights, she dresses in costume to go with the theme.  Ms. Borello is credited with being instrumental in helping Lighthouse Elementary School win the 2010 Florida Power-Library Award.  Ms. Borello will soon celebrate six years with Lighthouse Elementary.  



Deena Miller Zoppoth is a Kindergarten Teacher at Lighthouse Elementary.  In the words of Leslie Bolte, Principal, Deena Zoppoth brings to life the words, "All I ever needed to know I learned in Kindergarten."  Ms. Zoppoth believes that public education provides the foundation that students need to have the best opportunities in life, and that her role is to set the foundation.  To accomplish this goal, she nurtures the whole child.  Each morning, Ms. Zoppoth meets each individual student, eye to eye, to say hello and set the tone for the day.  She builds a community in her classroom where children work to learn together, enhancing their social skills, group skills, and communication skills while she adds the academic flair.  She values center based instruction, which provides opportunities for experimentation and exploration.  One of her goals is to instill a lifelong love of learning, and she finds that thematic units spark the interest of the children and help them progress academically.  She finds flexibility a valuable asset.  Just ask her about teaching her students about President Theodore Roosevelt, and his good character and sportsmanship while hunting.  The students decided they wanted to learn more about different types of bears, and so they enthusiastically helped to plan a unit on bears.



Ms. Zoppoth's impact as an educator is demonstrated by the student's academic growth.  By the end of the second trimester this year, her students have excellent reading percentages.  Ms. Zoppoth models problem solving so that children learn to solve their own problems and learn to have discussions before they escalate.  Major character building skills help children throughout their lives.  Of upmost importance to Ms. Zoppoth is involving the parents of her students in the children's education whenever possible.  She values parents as a great resource, but for parents to demonstrate the value of education to their children is "priceless". 



Ms. Zoppoth was awarded a grant from the Children's Services Council, she developed multicultural presentations, is a supervising teacher for college students and a peer teacher for beginning teachers.  She has served as a grade chair, mentor teacher, math and science tutor, and she serves actively on the technology committee.

IN1 Approval to Purchase Flood Insurance
Meeting
Mar 23, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

In order to qualify for reimbursement in a federally declared flood disaster, the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) requires that the School District purchase insurance from the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) for all buildings in Flood Zones A and V.  New flood zone maps were recently redrawn resulting in 10 new locations in the flood-prone zones A and V, and three (3) locations eliminated from these zones.  The new locations and buildings required new elevation certificates at a cost of approximately $5,000.



The net effect is that the School District now has 32 locations with 121 buildings valued in excess of $986 million in Flood Zones A  and V.  FEMA requires the District to purchase one policy per buidling, up to an insured limit of $500,000. We have 121 buildings in these zones, for total building coverage of $59.7 million and contents coverage of $40.2 million. We will have a $50,000 deductible for each building and another $50,000 deductible for contents in each building prior to any FEMA reimbursement. 

The total premium for the flood insurance is not to exceed $320,000, barring any federal rate increase prior to April 28, 2011.  Our broker is placing this flood insurance with the Travelers' Insurance Company.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the purchase of flood insurance and elevation certificates effective April 28, 2011 to April 28, 2012 at a cost not to exceed $325,000 through our Broker, the Plastridge Insurance Agency, Inc.

G1 - Teacher Induction Program Effectiveness Grants (MetLife)
Meeting
Mar 23, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion

The MetLife Foundation has awarded the School District of Palm Beach County $15,000 to study the impact that the implementation of the New Teacher Program has had on new teacher effectiveness and retention. The goals of this study include:



·      Goal #1:  To compare the student achievement levels of students in second year teachers’ classrooms who are supported by NTC Mentors to the achievement level of second year teachers’ students in “like” high-needs schools that are not supported by NTC Mentors.





  • Goal #2: To determine the impact of the PBC New Teacher Program on influencing the retention of new teachers in high-needs schools as compared to their counterparts who are not supported by the NTC Program in “like” schools.

  • Goal #3: To determine if support from the PBC New Teacher Program is an independent/influencing variable that overrides other variables in impacting teacher retention.


Completing the proposed study of inquiry will provide valuable information on program impact and effectiveness which can be used to build buy-in for the program. If the three goals are met with success, the study will provide evidence that the program can be successfully replicated in high needs schools within Palm Beach County; has a direct impact on teacher retention, performance, and student achievement, and justifies that human and capital resources used to implement and maintain the program. The funds from this grant will be provided to the district's Department of Assessment. Employees will complete the research required and create the quantitative reports to measure the impact of the New Teacher Mentoring support program. There is no district match required. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board accept the grant for the Teacher Induction Program Effectiveness (MetLife) award from the New Teacher Center.

CLS1 - English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) Add-On Endorsement Plan 2011-2016
Meeting
Mar 23, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion
The ESOL Endorsement courses are offered by the District online through a contracted vendor, CaseNex, in order to comply with state requirements. The ESOL Endorsement is primarily used to certify teachers who need to add ESOL to their Florida teaching certificate. Participants must complete five courses: Culture (Cross-cultural Communications), Language and Literacy (Applied Linguistics), Methods of Teaching English to Speakers of Other Language (ESOL), ESOL Curriculum and Materials Development, and Assessment (ESOL Testing and Evaluation). Participants must successfully complete all specific coursework in order to apply for the endorsement. In order to receive the endorsement, participants will be required to verify by transcript or by district inservice records that they have met all of the requirements. The following program is designed to provide specific training to those participants who are not certified in ESOL. This training is intended to increase their knowledge of teaching techniques and accommodations for ESOL students. Florida school districts, including two of the largest in the nation, educate over 240,000 English Language Learners (ELLs), with over 300 different languages spoken among them. These demographics reflect the growth of ELLs throughout Florida. The Florida Department of Education’s Bureau of Student Achievement through Language Acquisition (SALA) assists schools and districts, while ensuring ELLs receive comprehensible instruction. In addition, the Bureau monitors schools and districts for compliance with state and federal rules, regulations, the 1990 League of United Latin American Citizens (LULAC) et al. v. the State Board of Education (SBE) Consent Decree, and the 2003 Modification of the Consent Decree. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that this plan be approved for renewal by the School Board of Palm Beach County.

The Athletic Coaching Endorsement courses are offered by American Sports Education Program (ASEP) with follow-up implementation support from the school-based Athletic Director. The Athletic Coaching Endorsement is primarily used to certify individuals that do not currently hold a teaching certificate and for teachers who are interested in obtaining the five (5) year coaching certificate. If this plan is not approved, the School District of Palm Beach County will have no method by which to offer endorsement certification in Athletic Coaching to the teachers and non-instructional staff of Palm Beach County and will be out of compliance with state regulations as of July 1, 2011.

P2 RFP Renewal for District-Wide HVAC Filter Replacement Services/Preventive Maintenance Air-Handler Services 09C-006T
Meeting
Mar 23, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Discussion

P2 Approve the renewal for RFP 09C-006T for District-Wide HVAC Filter Replacement Services / Preventive Maintenance Air-Handler Services for the contract period of May 20, 2011 through February 18, 2012 with awarded vendor, Precision Air System, Inc. 



Services to be provided include District wide filter replacement and preventive maintenance that:





  • Maximizes life cycle efficiency of filters and operating efficiency of equipment

  • Improves indoor air quality

  • Maximizes energy conservation


In addition, Precision Air System, Inc.





  • Employs a dedicated staff of technicians to service the District

  • Coordinates with schools to establish the need for after school hours service calls at no extra charge to the District.

  • Maintains a dedicated storage facility to handle bulk capacity of filters.

  • Disposes of used filters following all Federal, State and Local Codes for such disposal.


Original contract was awarded by the Board on January 21, 2009 for the period of February 19, 2009 through February 18, 2011 with the option to renew for three additional one-year periods.  A ninety day extension with no additional funding needed  was approved by the Board on February 16, 2011 for the period of February 19, 2011 through May 19, 2011 to allow  for price negotiations with the awarded vendor.



Negotiations between District personnel and Precision Air System, Inc resulted in a reduced pricing schedule  with an estimated cost savings of $51,000 for this renewal period and a guarantee of additional price reductions for renewals two and three which will equate to an additional $187,000 if the Board chooses to approve those renewals at the required renewal dates. 



The average cost per filter is $13.56 which includes the cost of the filter, labor to install the new filter and removal and disposal of the old filter.  All work is performed after normal school hours so as not to conflict with the on-going activities and includes a basic inspection to ensure that the units are operating efficiently. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the contract for the District-Wide HVAC Filter Replacement Services / Preventive Maintenance Air-Handler Services be renewed for the period May 20, 2011 through February 18, 2012 in the amount of $1,600,000.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore, Chief Operating Officer (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Martin Mets, Director of Facility Management (metsm@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

Financial Impact:

The estimated annual financial impact to the Capital Maintenance Transfer budget is $1,600,000. The source of funds is the Facilities Service budget.
