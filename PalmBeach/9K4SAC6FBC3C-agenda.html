PM-1 Project Modifications for Pre-K Classroom Additions
Meeting
Aug 4, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action

This project modification contains the following items:



Item #1







  • Additional Architectural Services in the amount of $4,180.00.




  • Description:  Provide testing services and revised design for the Pre-K parking lot sub-base at Benoist Farms ES.




  • Cause:  Unforeseen Condition




  • Justification:  Unsuitable soils were encountered in a portion of the Pre-K parking lot.  The civil engineer provided testing services to identify the limits of the unsuitable materials and revised the sub-base design in this area of the parking lot.


Item #2







  • Additional Architectural Services in the amount of $10,505.00.




  • Description:  Revised routing of sanitary sewer for the Cholee Lake ES Pre-K classrooms.




  • Cause:  Unforeseen Condition




  • Justification:  The sanitary sewer line was rerouted to avoid the portable/concreteable classrooms that were built after the design was complete, but prior to the Pre-K Classroom construction.


Item #3







  • Additional Architectural Services in the amount of $7,150.00.




  • Description:  Revised routing of storm water line and exfiltration trench for the Indian Pines Pre-K classrooms.




  • Cause:  Unforeseen Condition




  • Justification:  The storm water line and exfiltration trench were in conflict with an FPL duct bank.  The duct bank was at a different elevation than shown on the as-built drawings.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board ratify Project Modifications in the amount of $21,835.00 to Brown & Brown Architects, Inc., for the Pre-K Classroom Addition Projects (Benoist Farms ES, Cholee Lake ES, and Indian Pines ES) and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.

Contact:

Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is an increase of $21,835.00 to the Brown & Brown Architects, Inc. Agreement.



The source of funds is the Benoist Farms ES, Cholee Lake ES, and Indian Pines ES Pre-K project budgets as approved by the Board in the FY 2010-2014 5-Year Plan.

PL-1 Resolution of Support for the Florida Department of Transportation’s South Florida East Coast Corridor Project
Meeting
Aug 4, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)


  • The School District Planning Department has participated in the planning for the use of the SFEC to enhance mobility to the Southeast Florida region from Jupiter to Miami. 


 





  • There are 715 School District employees who live in Broward and Dade counties who could benefit from this project to get to and from work.


 





  • There are approximately 70 schools that offer “Choice” programs in the District.  Allowing passenger service on the SFEC rail would benefit “Choice” students who commute daily to school.


 





  • The State’s policies encourage redevelopment of the east coast, which would be stimulated by the enhance mobility on the SFEC  


 





  • School District planning staff will continue to work closely with FDOT and the Treasure Coast Regional Planning Council in planning for the South Florida East Coast (SFEC) Corridor project.


 

Recommendation:

 I recommend the School Board approve resolution PL08042010-1 supporting the Florida Department of Transportation’s South Florida East Coast (SFEC) Corridor project to enhance mobility to the Southeast Florida Region and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

PL-2 Revised Financial Assistance Agreement (FAA) between Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County
Meeting
Aug 4, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)




  •  Palm Beach County Commissioner Burt Aaronson provided the $100,000.00 reimbursable funding to enhance the landscape for the West Boca Raton Community High School and along Glades Road.  The project was to be completed by September 30, 2006.




  •  Work on the project was delayed, which caused the deadline for completion to be extended to September 30, 2007. 




  •  The deadline had to be extended once more to September 30, 2009.  The FAA requires that the work be completed and the invoices be submitted to the County by that date.  The landscaping work was completed by the deadline date, but the invoices were not submitted.




  •  Planning Department staff contacted the Commissioner’s office and was able to obtain another extension of the deadline date to assure that the District will receive reimbursement for the expended funds. 




  • The revised FAA stipulates that the landscape work must be completed and the invoices submitted to the County no later than September 30, 2011.  The landscaping has been completed since 2009.  Once the invoices are submitted, the County will reimburse the School District.   

Recommendation:

 



I recommend the School Board approve the Revised Financial Assistance Agreement (FAA) between Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County for landscape improvements not to exceed $100,000.00 for the West Boca Raton Community High School and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph Moore - moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us;



 



Joseph Sanches - sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



 

Financial Impact:

No more than $100,000.00 from the School District’s Capital Budget to be reimbursed by Palm Beach County.

  • The Sustainable Communities Initiative involves the development of a "Regional Plan for Sustainable Development" that will identify critical projects and infrastructure to improve the Region’s sustainability and to be used as a guide by federal funding agencies to prioritize and focus future federal investment.

I further recommend that the Board authorize the Superintendent to sign a letter of support and other associated documents.
