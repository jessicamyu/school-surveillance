CLS1 - Alternative Unlimited, Inc. Drop Back In Academy Program
Meeting
Jul 22, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Wkshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)




  • Drop Back In Academy (DBIA) is a dropout retrieval program where students who already dropped out of school are given an opportunity to return and attend a program leading to graduation.




  • DBIA will be entering its third year of operation in Palm Beach County.




  • The DBIA Program is housed in leased space within Palm Beach County.




  • Students who are 16-21 years old and have dropped out of our district high schools are eligible to attend.




  • The DBIA Program recruits students in the community and at workplaces employing students without a diploma.




  • The DBIA Program offers flexible schedules, on-line and small group teacher directed classes with certified teachers.




  • The DBIA Program is presently in 7 states, including Florida.  Their programs are in a number of other counties in Florida.




  • The DBIA Program bears the entire expense of setting up their sites.  They pay teachers, administrators, on-line subscription costs and for materials.  As students enroll, the district obtains FTE for each student.  The district gives the DBIA Program nine-tenths of the FTE for each student enrolled and one-tenth is kept for oversight costs to the district and to provide textbooks.  This is the only cost to the district other than the time of district for oversight of the program.




  • Students who meet all requirements of a diploma in the state of Florida, including passing FCAT, receive a high school diploma.




  • The program has started with 4 sites and presently has 8 sites.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and Alternatives Unlimited, Inc., to provide Drop Back In Academy (DBIA) Program in Palm Beach County, effective from the date of execution through June 30, 2010, and authorize the superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

AC1 - Statement of Work Between The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida and CORE K12 Education (CORE Projects and Technologies, Ltd)
Meeting
Jul 22, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Wkshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

In the previous three school years, the School Board approved Statements of Work that have authorized CORE K12 Education, formerly known as The Princeton Review (TPR), to provide access to a complete online formative assessment system for all students in grades 3-10.  This system is designed to provide teachers and students with information about what should occur next in the teaching and learning process.  Specifically, the Statements of Work allowed CORE K12 Education to:



 







  • enable all schools to administer 262 common assessments and 326 mini assessments through the web, by scanners, or by clickers;




  • ensure that the technical infrastructure was in place to deliver and score the assessments;




  • create a customized Pareto-like Return on Investment (ROI) report linking test items to Pre-requisite and Necessary Skills that directs educators to specific skills on which students performed the lowest and link to online resources supporting supplemental instruction of those skills; and


  • provide immediate reporting of the assessment results.


Researchers, including Marzano (2000), Borman and Hewes (2000), and Black and Wiliam (2008), have found that the school-level factor of “Opportunity to Learn” which includes articulating clear curriculum targets, having assessments based on the curriculum targets, and monitoring the extent that teachers cover the curriculum, have twice the impact on student achievement than any other school-level factor (e.g., time on task, pressure to achieve, school climate and leadership).



During SY09, even though they were not mandated to do so, schools administered over 400,000 tests using this platform.  The District’s Department of Research and Evaluation found that in SY09, elementary school students with the highest usage of the TPR Assessment Center had gains in reading and math that were statistically higher than the district mean.  High school students with the highest usage had gains in reading that were also statistically higher than the district mean.



Use of this formative assessment tool is mandated for the 2009-10 school year.  It is a critical component of the Instructional Frameworks project.  In this project, embedded assessments have been aligned to the current and/or Next Generation Sunshine State Standards, as applicable, and to the District’s scope and sequence of instruction.  We anticipate that schools will administer approximately 5,000,000 embedded and teacher-created assessments.  The Assessment Center, provided by this Statement of Work, is the vehicle through which these embedded assessments will be administered and how results will be collected and reported.  It is a key tool that teachers and administrators will use to monitor student and group progress in mastering the Standards and to adjust instruction.



This Statement of Work allows CORE K12 Education to produce the following deliverables:







  • provide a customized and secure bank of approximately 10,000 assessment items to be used by staff to create the embedded assessments




  • enable all schools to administer embedded assessments through the web, by scanners, or by clickers, and ensure that the technical infrastructure is in place to deliver and score the assessments




  • provide immediate reporting of the assessment results




  • provide a redesigned ROI report which captures the results of the embedded assessments




  • provide data transfer to and from the School District of Palm Beach County




  • provide project management for problem resolution, staff development, on-site follow-up support, technical assistance, data management, and ad hoc reporting


This Statement of Work continues a successful partnership between The School District of Palm Beach County and CORE K12 Education (formally TPR).  Working together, we have developed a highly regarded assessment system.  In fact, the Board previously approved a Royalty Agreement that allows the district to share in some profits derived from the marketing of mutually developed products and ideas.  As a result, we are anticipating a $15,000 disbursement by mid-September 2009.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Statement of Work in the amount of $1,358,000, and a project contingency fund in the amount of $138,000 for a total of $1,496,000.
