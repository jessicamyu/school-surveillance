DESCRIPTION:

Award of RFP 19C-043K for Provider fo Research-Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County from Believe to Achieve Educational Services, LLC, Learn-It Systems, LLC, and One on One Learning Corp.  The eligible Private Schools will have an opportunity to interview the vendors recommended for award and select the vendor that best meets their needs to provide services as described below.

 

Section 1120 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) requires eligible Title I students in non-public, not-for-profit private schools to receive supplemental educational services.  These services also extend to the teachers an families of the eligible students and must be equitable to the services provided to eligible Title I public school children, teachers, and families.  To qualify for assistance under Title I, students must meet the following requirements:

  • Reside within the attendance area of a participating public school located in a low-income area
  • Are failing (or at risk of failing) to meet student academic achievements standards.

The services provide include but are not limited to:

  • Research-based direct supplemental academic instruction in reading and/or math.
  • Needs-based instructional services at various private schools throughout the Palm Beach County School District
  • Aligned materials for evaluation and instructional services.
  • Detailed and comprehensive program review at the end of the school year to include disaggregated data; parent, staff and administrative surveys; achievement pre- and post-data; and a summary of services.
  • Florida-certified teachers with the responsibility of assessing, planning, implementing, and evaluating a directed, research-based supplemental instructional program in reading and/or math.
  • Planned parental Involvement activities for parents of participating students.

COM3 District Human Resources Division Wins Best Practices Award
Meeting
Jul 24, 2019 - 3. BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category

Type
Presentation, Recognition

DESCRIPTION:

As a first for the School District of Palm Beach County, the District's Human Resources Division received a Sterling Best Practices Award in the area of Customer Focused Excellence from the world-renowned, process-excellence focused Florida Sterling Council. The Sterling Council uses the Sterling/Baldridge criteria to assess the level of specialization used by organizations.

 

This criteria is the international standard for organizational performance excellence and is evaluated and improved every two years based on ever-evolving evidence-based best practices for leadership. This award recognizes the multi-year and continuous process excellence journey of the District's HR Division, and staff was honored to accept the award at the Governor’s Sterling Award Banquet back in May in Orlando.

 

Established in 1992, the Florida Sterling Council is a public/private not-for-profit corporation supported by the Executive Office of the Governor. The Council oversees the Governor’s Sterling Award for Performance Excellence and administers the Georgia Oglethorpe Award. The Awards are presented annually by the Governor to high performing, role-model organizations, both private and public, who demonstrate superior management approaches and role model results.

DESCRIPTION:

Back on May 10, the Budget Department was notified that the School District of Palm Beach County was awarded the Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA) Best Practices in School Budgeting for the FY18 budget year. The District is one of only eight Districts nationwide and the first in the state of Florida to receive this prestigious award.

 

Prior to the GFOA Award for Best Practices in School Budgeting, GFOA focused solely on the final adopted budget document. The new award for Best Practices in School Budgeting is a rigorous assessment that encompasses the entire budget process from the initial plan through sustainability, while also demonstrating how resources are aligned to student achievement. More than 800 pages of supporting documentation was submitted including the final adopted budget document.

DESCRIPTION:

The Adult Education and Family Literacy- Adult General Education Grant is a grant in the amount of $1,972,807. The program performance dates are July 1, 2019 to June 30, 2020.

 

The priorities of this grant are to assist adults to become literate, to obtain knowledge and skills necessary to become full partners in the educational development of their children, assist adults in attaining a secondary school diploma or equivalent, and assist immigrants to improve their reading, writing, and speaking skills.

 

Adult Education programs are provided at 26 adult and community schools, one full time adult education center, one virtual academy and numerous satellite programs located throughout Palm Beach County, These programs are offered at various times throughout day and evening in order to accommodate students needs and schedules. 

 

Students progress will be measured by tracking student movement from one Educational Functioning Level (EFL) to the next in a given content area during the projected year base on the results from assessments that has been approved by the USDOE and FLDOE and administered in a pre and post-test. An educational gain may also include students in ABE Level 5 who are awarded academic credit. It also includes students who attend a high school diploma or equivalent.

I recommend the Board approve the renewal of the Interlocal Agreement with The Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County, effective October 1, 2019 through September 30, 2022.

DESCRIPTION:

This agreement, extends for three years the existing Interlocal Agreement between the Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida.  The term of this Agreement between the School Board and the Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County will be in effect from October 1, 2019 through September 30, 2022.  The attached agreement will allow the School Board to share data with the Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County, for the following purposes:

  • Evaluation:  To acquire data that will allow the Agency to assess the effectiveness of individual programs.
  • Research:  To acquire data on students served by the Agency funded programs and comparison groups to analyze the impact of educational programs.
  • Planning:  To acquire District, targeted geographic or school specific data to be aggregated and analyzed to facilitate planning for the students’ health, social and educational needs.

The use or disclosure of confidential information will be limited to that information necessary to benefit the students.

 

On June 5, 2009, the Board approved an Interlocal Agreement with Children’s Services Council (CSC) concerning sharing student information.  The Agreement was in effect from July 1, 2009 through June 30, 2014 with an option to extend the agreement for up to three additional one-year terms.

  • On September 20, 2012, the Board approved a revised Interlocal Agreement with CSC.  This Agreement was in effect from September 20, 2012 through September 30, 2014 with an option to extend the agreement for up to three additional one-year terms.
  • On September 4, 2014, the Board approved an extended Interlocal Agreement with CSC.  This Agreement was in effect from October 1, 2014 through September 30, 2016 with an option to extend the agreement for up to three additional one-year terms.
  • On June 15, 2016, the Board approved an extended Interlocal Agreement with CSC.  This Agreement was in effect from October 1, 2016 through September 30, 2017 with an option to extend the agreement for up to two additional one-year terms.
  • On July 19, 2017, the Board approved an extended Interlocal Agreement with CSC.  This Agreement was in effect from October 1, 2017 through September 30, 2018 with an option to extend the agreement for one additional year.
  • On July 18, 2018,  the Board approved an extended Interlocal Agreement with CSC.  This Agreement is in effect from October 1, 2018 through September 30, 2019 and expires.

  4. Since the acquired portion of Right-of-Way along Boynton Beach Boulevard is maintained by the Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT), FDOT is now requesting the School Board convey ownership of the +/- 0.142 acre strip of land. 
     
  5. Conveyance of the strip of land will eliminate the School Board's liability and will not negatively impact the school campus.

  2. School District and Village staff both agree that there is a public need in the Wellington area for athletic facilities for the use and enjoyment of the Wellington Community High School (WCHS) students, the residents of the Village and the Board’s public education programs. Village staff proposed locating these facilities on a portion of the WCHS campus. School District and Village staff both acknowledged the need for a new ILA specifically constructed to deal with the WCHS Sport Complex.
     
  3. As part of this agreement, Village shall be responsible at its sole expense to fund, plan, design, construct, and maintain the new improvements consisting of four (4) synthetic turf athletic fields, eight (8) tennis courts, three (3) basketball courts; in addition to relocating the field event/equipment area and the parent drop-off loop, and the resurfacing of the existing stadium field with synthetic turf, in full compliance with the State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF), including capital and renewal/replacement funding, on the WCHS campus.
     
  4. Since the Village will be maintaining the new improvements, the District will no longer be responsible for maintaining the school's tennis and basketball courts, stadium field, and practice fields.
     
  5. WCHS has sufficient land area for the installation of the new improvements and, if approved by Village Council, for a 50-meter-long sanctioned swimming pool. (See Exhibit "A")
     
  6. The Term of this Agreement (Term) shall be for a period of thirty (30) years from the issuance of the Certificate of Completion or Certificate of Occupancy for the Sports Complex Facilities, whichever occurs first. However, if the Swimming Pool is not constructed on the WCHS campus by July 1, 2027, either Party shall have the option to terminate or renegotiate this Agreement twenty (20) years from the start date of the Term. 
     
  7. At such time that the Board schedules modernization/replacement of WCHS, the Village agrees that the four new artificial turf fields at the northwest corner of the campus may be used to locate temporary school facilities and that all recreational/athletic utilization of these fields for the duration of the modernization/replacement project will be suspended.
     
  8. Priority of use of various facilities of the Sport Complex are detailed in Section 5 of the Agreement.
     
  9. The Village will install its own metering systems for water, power and sewage for the four (4) synthetic turf athletic fields and, if approved by Village Council, for the swimming pool, and will pay for all incurred costs associated with the use of the Facilities.
     
  10. The Village will be the Authority Having Jurisdiction for building permits for the project.
     
  11. The School Board’s Chief Operating Officer has the final authority to approve final plans, security fencing, and related construction documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the User Agreement and Addendum for ArbiterPay.

 

ArbiterPay is an online service to pay athletic officials. The company was created by the NCAA to provide this service for college sports and have since been spun off as a private company. The company continues to handle the payment of athletic officials for the NCAA as well as 80% of high schools throughout the country.
 

Currently, in the Palm Beach County School District, each school is writing hundreds of checks annually to pay officials. The athletic director completes a form requesting payment, which is then processed by the school treasurer and signed by the principal. School staff is also burdened with obtaining personal information needed for tax documents of each athletic official.

 

With ArbiterPay, the payment process will be centralized at the District level with funds transferred as needed (approximately every two weeks) to fund payments by Arbiter to athletic officials. While each school athletic director will still be required to request payment after work is completed, the athletic directors will have access to an app or website to approve payments and access information regarding the officials scheduled for their games. ArbiterPay, rather than the School District, will issue the Form 1099-MISC tax document to athletic officials.

 

The annual estimated payments made through Arbiter for high school sports is $700,000 and transaction costs associated with the payments is estimated at $25,000.  The payments, which have been paid by schools in the past, will continue to be allocated to each schools’ internal account and fees associated with the service will be funded by the District to eliminate any negative impact on the schools.

 

Implementation of ArbiterPay will coincide with the implementation of Arbiter by the local sports associations. Each association (football, baseball, etc) will assign officials to games eliminating the current process managed at the District level. A contract will be established with each association and the estimated cost for those agreements are estimated at $50,000. These new contracts will be funded by the District and the Officials will have the option to decide individually what form they prefer to receive their payments.

 

The District shall have the right to terminate this User Agreement with or without cause by closing the Account, and ArbiterPay shall within three (3) business days return to the District all unrestricted Funds held in the Trust Account.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve Master agreement with Kleo, Inc. d/b/a ClassWallet to provide ClassWallet, an online platform that allows teachers to receive and spend monies easily while providing District-level reporting. In addition, the platform will be used to facilitate the distribution of Florida Teachers Classroom Supply Assistance Program “Supply Funds”. The implementation of ClassWallet has been coordinated with representatives from the Classroom Teachers Association.

 

ClassWallet is extremely easy to use and online instructions as well as video tutorials will be available for easy reference. Currently, ClassWallet is used throughout the country and most recently was implemented in Miami-Dade and Hillsborough School Districts.

 

Currently each teacher is required to upload all receipts into PeopleSoft annually. Using ClassWallet, each teacher receiving “Supply Funds” will have online access to a virtual wallet. Funds can then be used to purchase items through the online portal. Teachers can also purchase items from other sources, including local stores, and request reimbursement by scanning the receipt into the website or app. Funds will be reimbursed via direct-deposit in two (2) to five (5) business days. Teachers purchasing items through the ClassWallet portal will not be required to upload receipts to the District.

 

ClassWallet also offers the ability to set up crowdfunding through Go Fund Me. Teachers will be able to log into their ClassWallet account and set up a Go Fund Me project. DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County is in the process of implementing the mandates of SB 7030, Implementation of Legislative Recommendations of the Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School Public Safety Commission, specifically that all public schools have a Safe School Officer on campus during all hours that school is in session, otherwise referenced as “bell to bell”.

 

Due to the fact that SB 7030 was not signed by Governor DeSantis until May 8, 2019, and requires action by the first day school, August 12, 2019, it has become apparent that some charter schools in Palm Beach County may not be capable of full compliance by this deadline without assistance from the School District of Palm Beach County.

 

Due to this emergency situation, and to ensure that every public school in Palm Beach County is in compliance with SB 7030, the School District of Palm Beach County shall for the 2019-2020 school year assign a School Resource Officer pursuant to F.S. 1006.12(1) or School Security Guard pursuant to F.S. 1006.12(4) to any charter school which has not complied with the requirements of SB 7030 as it pertains to Safe School Officers.

 

This assignment shall be for the 2019-2020 School Year. The District, during the 2019-2020 school year, shall collaborate with charter schools in Palm Beach County to continue to facilitate access to the four Safe School Officer options set forth in F.S. 1006.12, for the 2020-2021 school year and all subsequent years. The additional time allows charter schools to select the best option for their school facilities and financial conditions.

 

For the charter schools which necessitate the assignment of a School Resource Officer or School Security Guard, the School District of Palm Beach County shall retain that school’s Safe School Allocation for the 2019-2020 School year, and does not waive its rights in the future to seek reimbursement for the difference between the actual cost of the assigned School Resource Officer or School Security Guard and the school’s Safe School Allocation.
