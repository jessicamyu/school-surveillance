PL-1 Revised Agreement for Landscaping at West Boca Raton CHS
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)


  • Palm Beach County Commissioner Burt Aaronson provided the $100,000.00 reimbursable funding to enhance the landscape for the West Boca Raton Community High School and along Glades Road.  The project was to be completed by September 30, 2006. 


  • Work on the project was delayed, which caused the deadline for completion to be extended to September 30, 2007.  


  • The deadline had to be extended once more to September 30, 2009.  The FAA requires that the work be completed and the invoices be submitted to the County by that date.  The landscaping work was completed by the deadline date, but the invoices were not submitted. 


  • Planning Department staff contacted the Commissioner’s office and was able to obtain another extension of the deadline date to assure that the District will receive reimbursement for the expended funds. 


  • The revised FAA stipulates that the landscape work must be completed and the invoices submitted to the County no later than September 30, 2011.  The landscaping has been completed since 2009.  Once the invoices are submitted, the County will reimburse the School District.


  • The revision was approved by the School Board at the August 4, 2010 Workshop and was scheduled for the October 4, 2010 Board of County Commissioners agenda, however, the item was pulled to further revise language in the document.


  • The latest revision to the FAA that states a payment by the County of no more than $100,000.00 for landscape improvements at West Boca Raton Community High School along Glades Road.  This revision extends the deadline of the project as well as adds new language (non-discrimination clause, etc.) and reformats the FAA document to letter size.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Revised Financial Assistance Agreement (FAA) between Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County for landscape improvements not to exceed $100,000.00 for the West Boca Raton Community High School and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us; sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

No more than $100,000.00 from the School District’s Capital Budget to be reimbursed by Palm Beach County.

Approval of an Agreement between Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County for a Traffic Signal at Sunset Palms Elementary School
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action

·         Sunset Palms Elementary School (03Z) was opened in 2008.  It was designed for a FISH capacity of 978 and based on the October 2010 data it has a K-5 student enrollment of 996.



 



·         The main access to the school is from Acme Dairy Road.  At the time of school construction a signal at Acme Dairy Road and the entrance to the school was not warranted.



 



·         Based on the traffic volume on Acme Dairy Road at the entrance of the school, the County Traffic Division has determined that the construction of a northbound left turn lane into the site and the installation of a traffic signal at the intersection of Acme Dairy Road and the entrance to Sunset Palms Elementary School will improve traffic flow.



 



·           The County agreed to pay upfront for the installation of the signal with the School Board reimbursing at the time of completion of the Middle School.



 



·         The Middle School is not planned and therefore is not included in the Five Year Capital Plan.



 



·         In order to install the signal, the County requested an easement from us which the Real estate Department is handling (Please see Real Estate Board Item).



 



·         The attached Agreement outlines the commitment from the County to pay up front for the signal and to assume all responsibility for design, contract administration and maintenance of the improvements.  The School Board is required to reimburse the County for the full cost of the traffic signal estimated at $110,000 and a not to exceed cost of $130,000 at the time the Middle School opens.



 

Recommendation:

 I recommend the School Board approve the Agreement between Palm Beach County Board of County Commissioners and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the installation of a traffic signal at the intersection of Acme Dairy Road and Sunset Palm Elementary School.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore moore@palmbeach.k12.fl.us;



Joseph M. Sanches sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

The School District will reimburse Palm Beach County for the cost of the traffic signal in an amount not to exceed $130,000 once the Middle School adjoining Sunset Palms Elementary has been built.  The reimbursement will be funded from the construction project for the Middle School.

FM1 - Resolution to Terminate Swaps (EMERGENCY ITEM)
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

·         In 2002, The School Board issued Certificates of Participation, Series 2002D for the construction of eight schools.



·         Later in 2002, a fixed margin basis swap was negotiated with the counterparty of Citibank to reduce the debt service.  As of June 2010, the transaction has generated approximately $3.7 million which offset interest payments on the 2002D COP issue.



·         In 2006, a constant maturity swap was negotiated with UBS as counterparty to further reduce the debt service.  As of June 2010, the transaction has generated approximately $4.4 million which offset interest payments on the 2002D COP issue.



·         The Finance Committee reviewed the transactions at a meeting in February and again in September 2010.  The Committee recommended termination of both transactions when appropriate with the proceeds set aside to deal with some upcoming transactions in 2011 and 2012.  Minutes from the meetings as well as the detailed presentation given at the February 2010 meeting are available online at: http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/2010FinanceCommitteeMeetings.asp



·         When the Finance Committee recommended the termination of the transaction in February, the estimated proceeds from the termination of the Constant Maturity Swap was approximately $4 million.  In September, the estimated proceeds for the same transaction was approximately $6 million.



·         Due to recent activity in the bond market, the estimated proceeds from the termination of both transactions is now approximately $8 million.



·         On November 9, 2010, the District’s Financial Advisor strongly recommended the District move forward with the termination of the Constant Maturity Swap as soon as possible.  The termination of the Fixed Margin Basis Swap will take place when the District can be assured of a positive outcome.



·         The actual purchase price would be negotiated between the District, Citibank and UBS with the assistance of the District’s Financial Advisor.



·         The termination of the swaps will also provide greater flexibility in managing the remaining swap during these turbulent financial times.



Recommendation:

On November 9th (yesterday), a recommendation was received from the District’s Financial Advisor regarding the termination of two swaps associated with the 2002D COP issue.  Due to the volatility in the bond markets, they recommended the transactions be completed as quickly as possible.  The transactions are expected to produce revenue of at least $6 million and possibly up to $8 million depending on the market conditions.  Taking advantage of the market conditions to generate revenue for the School District is in the public interest.  I believe this is good cause and justifies the consideration of the resolution as an emergency item.



 



I recommend the School Board adopt a resolution authorizing the termination of the Fixed Margin Basis Swap and the Constant Maturity Swap and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign necessary documents.

Financial Impact:

The financial impact will be revenues of at least $6 million.  Fees will be paid to the District’s bond counsel and financial advisor in accordance with their previously approved contracts.
