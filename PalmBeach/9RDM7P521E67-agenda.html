DESCRIPTION:

On August 4, 2014, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Palm Beach Collegiate to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2015. 

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

Palm Beach Collegiate “Does Not Meet the Standard” in the required 19 sections.

 

Two sections received a rating of “Partially Meets the Standard:”

  • Section 04 – Curriculum Plan
  • Section 15 – Transportation

One section received a rating of “Does Not Meet the Standard:”

  • Section 17 - Budget

Per the Overall Assessment Summary/Checklist, the ratings indicated that the application did not meet the statutory requirements set forth in the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instruments and their referenced statutes, including F.S. 1002.33(6).  (See Overall Assessment/Checklist)  It was determined that substantive changes to the application would have been needed to cure these deficiencies and the School District does not accept substantive changes.

 

“If an application is denied, the sponsor shall, within 10 calendar days after such denial, articulate in writing the specific reasons, based upon good cause, supporting its denial of the charter application and shall provide the letter of denial and supporting documentation to the applicant and to the Department of Education.”

 

Attached are copies of the Application, Budget, Interview Letter, Evaluation Instruments with Reviewers’ Notes, Overall Assessment, Board Notification, and Matrix for December 10th Agenda Items.

Subject
LD4 Educational Broadband Service Long-Term Lease Agreement with Sprint Spectrum LP
Meeting
Dec 10, 2014 - 5. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
3. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County holds five Federal Communications Commission (FCC) issued licenses, covering 13 different channels for use of spectrum within the Educational Broadband Service (EBS) band. These licenses cover various parts of Palm Beach County and several include areas outside of Palm Beach County. Currently the District is utilizing only three of these channels. The School District can hold on to the unused channels or lease the channels as is customary by school districts. 

 

On July 17, 2013, the Board contracted with Select Spectrum LLC to market the Board’s EBS Licenses to a telecommunications entity under an EBS Long-Term De Facto Transfer Lease Agreement (Lease).

 

Select Spectrum released a public solicitation in the spring of 2014 and Sprint Spectrum LP (Sprint) offered the best terms.

 

Below are the essential financial/business elements of the negotiated lease:

 

•              The Lease will included all 13 channels, but will continue to have the right to use the 3 video channels currently being used by TEN;

•              30 year lease term, with a Sprint-option to cancel after 21 years;

•              $4,500,000 upfront payment;

•              Monthly lease fees in the amount of $115,000 ($1,380,000 per year);

•             A $625,000 payment after Sprint gives notice that it is ready to begin using the channels currently being used to broadcast TEN’s video content to schools;

•             $40,000 in reimbursement of the School Board’s outside counsel legal fees associated with negotiating the Lease; and

•             Monthly service credits in the amount of $5,000 to be used to purchase Sprint’s products and services.

 

The total cash paid over the life of the lease will total $46,565,000 and the value of the service credits will be an additional $1,800,000.  The net present value of the entire deal, discounted at the industry standard of 10% per year, is approximately $19.3 million.

 

The upfront payment and monthly royalty payments, along with the $625,000 payment (once it is received) will be maintained in a unique budget program and used to defray current operating costs associated with The Education Network and make necessary improvements to the TV station's infrastructure. This new revenue stream will free up general fund operating dollars for re-appropriation through the School Board's annual budget development and approval process. Consideration will be given to using a portion of these funds to enhance the district’s communication, marketing and engagement efforts. A portion of the revenue received in the first three years of the Lease will also be used to pay the agreed-upon fee to Select Spectrum in the total approximate amount of $260,000 and maintenance costs of the Video Over IP system that replaces the current microwave delivery system of signals to schools.

 

The School District will work with Sprint to participate in their ConnectED initiative to bring free high-speed wireless broadband service to students and families in Palm Beach County who meet the qualifications for the program.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the First Amendment to the Board’s Charter with Everglades Preparatory Academy and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related correspondence and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 20, 2012, the School Board granted Everglades Preparatory Academy, Inc., on behalf of Everglades Preparatory Academy a five (5) year Renewal Charter Contract with the School Board of Palm Beach County.  The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2012, and ending June 30, 2017, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.

 

Section I(B)(5)(a) of the Charter Contract permits an amendment of that Contract during its term through mutual agreement of the parties, provided such modifications are agreed to in writing and executed by both parties.

 

Everglades Preparatory Academy provides dropout prevention and academic intervention services pursuant to Fla. Stat. Section 1003.53.

 

Everglades Preparatory Academy wishes to amend the Contract to reflect Everglades Preparatory Academy’s services to a high at-risk population and requests that Everglades Preparatory Academy be recognized as an alternative school.

 

Fla. Stat. Section 1008.34(3)(a)(1) states:  “An alternative school may choose to receive a school grade under this section or a school improvement rating under s. 1008.341.  For charter schools that meet the definition of an alternative school pursuant to State Board of Education rule, the decision to receive a school grade is the decision of the charter school governing board.”

 

Pursuant to Fla. Stat. Section 1008.34(3)(a)(1), the Governing Board of Everglades Preparatory Academy currently wishes to receive a School Improvement Rating under Fla. Stat. Section 1008.341.  Any mention in the Contract to Everglades Preparatory Academy receiving a school grade shall, upon approval of the Florida Department of Education (DOE), and concurrence of the School’s Governing Board, be replaced with the standards set forth under Fla. Stat. Section 1008.341.

 

School District staff reviewed information submitted by Everglades Preparatory Academy as required by School Board Policy 2.57, paragraph 6, to justify the School’s request to change its School Improvement Rating to those listed in Fla. Stat. Section 1008.341.

 

The School Board seeks to update the Contract to cover provisions that were required by the State’s charter school contract template.

 

The School Board is now required through a Resolution Agreement with the U.S. Department of Justice to include provisions in charter contracts relating to the agreement and additionally seeks to include provisions in the Contract relating to the Inspector General.

 

The Governing Board of the school has signed the First Amendment to the Charter Contract between the School Board of Palm Beach County and Everglades Preparatory Academy, Inc., on behalf of Everglades Preparatory Academy.

 

Attached are copies of the Charter Contract, First Amendment to Charter Contract, Legal Checklist, Data Panel, Board Notification, and Matrix for December 10, 2014, Agenda Items.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve a resolution authorizing the issuance of certificates of participation series 2015C to refund all or a portion of COP series 2011A.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The School District, in conjunction with the Finance Committee, actively manages the debt of the District, always seeking to reduce capital costs and manage risk.
  • Due to continued low interest rates in the current market, the District has the opportunity to refund, or refinance, COP Series 2011A and obtain savings for the District which will reduce future debt service payments.
  • The 2011A is scheduled to revert to a variable rate in August 2016 if no action is taken.
  • Based on the recommendation of the Finance Committee, the District would refund the certificates, locking in fixed rates until maturity, when the net present value savings is at least 5%, included all associated fees. 
  • A summary of the maximum fees, or costs of issuance, is attached.
  • This transaction would result in net present value savings of more than $3 million and eliminate future interest rate risk.
  • On November 14, 2014, the Finance Committee unanimously recommended the District move forward with the transaction as soon as possible.
