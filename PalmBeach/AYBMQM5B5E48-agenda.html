I recommend the Board approve the 2018 Comprehensive Safety Inspection Report.

 

DESCRIPTION:

Annual Comprehensive Safety Inspections were performed by the Building Code Services Fire and Safety Inspectors as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). All educational, auxiliary and ancillary facilities were included in these inspections, using the State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) for casualty and sanitation, and Florida Administrative Code Chapter 69A-58 for fire safety.

 

The number of citations for Public Schools, Alternative Schools and Ancillary Facilities rose from 1,233 in 2017 to 1,453 in 2018. This represents an 18% increase over one year and a 97% reduction since 2000. All conditions considered by the inspector to be hazardous or posing an imminent danger were reported to the Facilities Services Department at the time of discovery for immediate action.

 

A summary report by facility, detailed individual facility reports and descriptive charts are attached.

 

According to SREF, Chapter 5, the inspection reports shall be approved by the Board, and the Board shall adopt a plan and a schedule for the correction of each deficiency. The corrective action plans for each citation were developed in conjunction with the local fire authorities and are included on each report.

 

Upon the board approving the inspection reports, copies of the reports will be submitted to the State Fire Marshal as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). The Board shall certify the completion of the inspection report to the State Fire Marshal by June 30, 2018.

AVID is designed to increase the number of students who enroll and succeed in higher education and in their lives beyond high school. The AVID College Readiness System is a catalyst for developing a school culture that closes the expectation and opportunity gaps many students face and prepares all students for success in a global society. 

 

AVID Excel is a middle school program intended to accelerate the academic language acquisition of English Language Learners (ELLs) and puts them on a pathway to high school AVID and college readiness. The School District of Palm Beach County’s AVID participation has increased from six high schools in 2009 to 54 sites in 2018 at the elementary, middle, and high school levels. Approximately 879 AVID students have successfully graduated since the first graduating class in 2012. Of the 225 AVID seniors in 2016-17:

 

● 97.8% graduated on time

● 68% plan to attend a four-year college

● 14% plan to attend a two-year college

● 84% graduated “college-ready”

● 73% took both the SAT and ACT

● 64% took at least one AP/IB/AICE exam

 

Approximately 919 middle school students were enrolled in the AVID elective in 2016-17. Of those students:

 

● 63% completed Algebra 1 or higher math with a “C” or better

● 441 have taken an honors or high school credit-bearing course other than math 

 

Over 1,000 teachers and 100 administrators and District staff have been trained in AVID’s strategies and methodologies at Summer Institutes, Path trainings, AVID Professional Learning Workshops, and AVID District Leader Trainings. A sustainable support system has been established, as the District now has seven AVID Center Staff Developers.

 

These agreements will allow the School District of Palm Beach County to implement AVID’s curriculum and use of the AVID logo in all 54 sites. The agreements include District Director training, AVID curriculum materials, and implementation support.

I recommend the Board approve the Real Estate Sale and Purchase contract for acquisition of an improved parcel of land containing 0.23 acres, more or less, located adjacent to Addison Mizner Elementary as herein described; approve a resolution declaring structures located on the property to be acquired by the School Board to be unnecessary for Educational purposes, surplus, and without commercial value; and authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  1. The FY 2017-2021 Five Year Plan includes the modernization of Addison Mizner Elementary.
     
  2. The modernization of the campus requires the acquisition of three residential parcels contiguous to the school.  One of the three parcels was purchased November, 2017.
     
  3. The subject property is located at 201 SW 12th Avenue, Boca Raton, Florida, containing +/- 0.23 acres. (See Map)
     
  4. The contract price for the improved property is $468,000. The appraised value of the property is $390,000. 
     
  5. The contract offer price was based on several factors including staff’s belief that acquisition of the property is essential to the school modernization plan and that the need for the property would have to be satisfied through eminent domain if an agreement in lieu of eminent domain could not be reached with the owner.
     
  6. Given that eminent domain would be required in the event of unsuccessful negotiations, staff believes that the amount of the offer in excess of appraised value is justified, based on the time and expenses that would have to be expended to acquire the property through eminent domain.
     
  7. The demolition cost for the existing home is estimated to be $25,000.
     
  8. A Phase I Environmental Audit has been ordered to confirm that there are no environmental concerns on this property.
     
  9. Because the recommended purchase price exceeds the appraised value of the property, a supermajority of the Board is statutorily required for approval.

I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with the Health Care District for the provision of basic school health services from July 1, 2018 through June 30, 2019.

DESCRIPTION:

The Health Care District of Palm Beach County (HCD) funds nursing coverage in all regular education schools within our district. The HCD agrees to place one nurse in every regular school within our district schools and one full time nurse split between four (4) alternative schools. Currently, they provide the placement and administrative support of a registered nurse in 162 schools and one full time nurse split between four (4) alternative schools.
 

The original Agreement with the HCD in 2001 initiated our support to the school nurse program by funding nursing services in schools built after 2001 and contributing to the cost of basic health supplies for all schools.

 

The Agreement addresses the following funding items:

  • To provide $75,000 per school for a school nurse in 34 schools built after 2001.
  • To provide $150,000 for two (2) additional nurses to provide equity in all regular schools and a full time nurse coverage of 5 alternative schools.
  • To pay $83,000 per annum to help offset the cost of student medical supplies.
  • The Health Care District is responsible to pay for background checks for all of the employees participating in the school nurse program.

I recommend the School Board approve the District's Mental Health Assistance Allocation Plan.

DESCRIPTION:

Senate Bill 7026 (Chapter 2018-3, Laws of Florida) comprehensively addresses school safety, in part, through a Mental Health Assistance Allocation and district implementation plan.  Florida Statutes 1011.62 requires a Mental Health Assistance Allocation to provide funding to school districts to help establish or expand school-based mental health care.  Before receiving funds, school districts are required to annually develop and submit a plan outlining the local program and planned expenditures to district school boards for approval.  The plan, once approved by the board, is due to the Commissioner of Education by August 1 of each fiscal year.
