Consider the First Amendment to the Interagency Agreement in order to include Computer Aid, Inc. (CAI) on the list of approved contractors on Exhibit "B" and Exhibit "C".  CAI will follow all requirements and provisions included in the original agreement, signed September 20, 2007 and expiring September 20, 2012.



This amendment is being implemented in order to assist CSC in updating their current database of District students served in their family counseling programs.  CSC received limited access to student records through an Interagency Agreement signed September 20, 2007.  This amendment adds Computer Aid, Inc. as an approved contractor to complete an update to their database.  The result of the update will enable CSC to produce improved reports which will be shared with the school district so the students in the CSC to produce improved reports which will be shared with the school district so the students in the CSC programs can be better served.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the amendment to the existing Interagency Agreement with the Children Service Council and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the agreement which are required for implementation of the agreement activities.

Subject
G1 - Approval of Arts Education Model Development and Dissemination Grant
Meeting
Aug 11, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

The Arts Integration Dissemination Project will target two diverse Title I elementary schools largely populated with economically disadvantaged and minority group students who are in need of non-linguistic strategies that will provide:
-  arts integrated strategies infused throughout the core curriculum
- standards based arts education and ELL education embedded in reading/language arts, math, and science curriculum
- teachers with the skills to engage all students in the learning process through integrated activities that create, perform, and respond to the arts and the world around them.



This project will promote a continuous flow of information, analyzing, creating, performing, responding, and experiencing arts forms.  Academic data will be collected on student achievement and monitored for continuous improvement.  The goal of this project is to provide significant data on effective techniques and learning strategies that can be replicated.



This is a four year grant award for $1.2 million.  Out of 200 applications across the country, 30 were awarded.  The School District of Palm Beach County's application was ranked 12th.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board accept the U.S. Department of Education Arts In Education Model Development and Dissemination Grant in the amount of $1.2 million over a four-year period.
