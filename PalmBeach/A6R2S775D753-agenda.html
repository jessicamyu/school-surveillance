Subject
Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) Grant
Meeting
Feb 10, 2016 - 2. WORKSHOP immediately following A/C Session in the Board Room
Category
2. Workshop Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County plans to apply for the Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) Grant from the United States Department of Education (USDOE), in response to the “Notice” to be published in the Federal Register in early 2016.  In this year’s competition, the USDOE will award grants to an estimated 25-30 school districts across the nation.  Each grant will award up to $4 million per year for three years, or up to $12 million over the course of the MSAP project period from October 1, 2016 through September 30, 2019.

 

Pending receipt of the MSAP grant, the following five new magnet schools are proposed for development and implementation beginning in school year 2016-17:

 

MSAP Magnet Schools - Magnet Themes (School-Wide)
Palmetto Elementary - IB Primary Years Programme (IB PYP)
Grove Park Elementary - IB Primary Years Programme (IB PYP)
Howell L. Watkins Middle  - IB Middle Years Programme (IB MYP) Infused with STEM
Carver Middle  - IB Middle Years Programme (IB MYP) Infused with STEM   
Congress Middle  - Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM)
 
Students who live in the attendance boundaries of these schools will participate in, and benefit from, the school-wide magnet program, along with students who are recruited to attend the magnet schools from outside each school’s attendance area.  The identified schools all have adequate space available to recruit additional students to attend the magnet schools.

 

MSAP grants are awarded to school districts on a competitive basis to establish and operate magnet schools that meet the following MSAP statutory purposes:
• To  eliminate, reduce, and prevent minority group isolation in schools with substantial numbers of minority group students;
• To promote diversity through a specialized magnet curriculum capable of attracting and bringing together students from different racial, ethnic, and socio-economic backgrounds;
• To provide all students with the opportunity to meet challenging academic content standards;
• To ensure that all students in the magnet school have equitable access to the high quality education that will enable them to succeed academically and continue with post-secondary education or productive careers; and
• To support capacity-building activities to enable the continued operation of the magnet schools at a high performance level after the funding ends.

 

To be eligible for the MSAP grant, the School Board will be required to approve the MSAP Voluntary Plan to transform eligible public schools into innovative, evidence-based, school-wide thematic programs that promote diversity through magnet school choice.
