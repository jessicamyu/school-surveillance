PM-1 Educational Specification for Pahokee Middle/High School
Meeting
Nov 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action


1. The remodeling/renovation of Pahokee Middle/High School is scheduled to commence 2010 (FY 2011) and be completed by 2012 according to the Board-adopted FY 2011-15 Five-Year Plan.



2. The Educational Specification includes the remodeling of seven (7) existing science laboratories at the High School.  The seven (7) science laboratories will be remodeled into five (5) science laboratories, one (1) computer skills laboratory and one (1) distance learning laboratory. 



3. The Educational Specification includes the remodeling of three (3) existing Physical Education rooms at the High School.  The three (3) Physical Education rooms will be remodeled into an Activity Room/Fitness Room.



4. The Educational Specification includes the remodeling of three (3) existing classrooms at the High School.  The classrooms will be remodeled into the JROTC classrooms, storage and other related spaces.



5. The Educational Specification includes the remodeling of two (2) existing restrooms at the High School.  The restrooms will be remodeled into ESE Changing Room/Restroom.



6. The Educational Specification includes the auditorium renovations including a new floor for the entry/lobby, new HVAC for the group restrooms and a projector and screen for the stage.



7. The Educational Specification includes the media center renovations including upgrades to the CCTV studio and control room.



8. The Educational Specification for Pahokee Middle/High School is consistent with the Class Size Reduction (CSR) Facility Lists, and CSR Generic Educational Specification approved by the Board on September 16, 2009.



9. The FISH Capacity of the school will be 1,081 for grades 9-12 and the "Program Capacity" of this school will be for 919 students.  The use of the "Program Capacity" for the construction at the school is consistent with the approval of the Educational Plant Survey by the School District of Palm Beach County, April 21, 2010 and the Department of Education (DOE), June 30, 2010.  With the adoption of the Educational Plant Survey, the "Program Capacity" is 85% of the FISH Capacity.



10. A "Spot" Survey or amendment to the Educational Plant Survey will be prepared and submitted to DOE for approval of the remodeling/renovation at Pahokee Middle/Senior High School.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Educational Specification and the amendment to the Educational Plant Survey for the remodeling/renovation of Pahokee Middle/High School.

Financial Impact:

Financial impact will be determined when construction contract is negotiated.

1. The School District requested Palm Beach County install a span wire traffic signal at the intersection of Summit and Davis Road; adjacent to the Central Bus Compound to allow school buses to safely exit and return to the bus compound.
2. Palm Beach County is requesting approximately +/- 0.027 acres to allow for the installation of the signal.  The School Board will be responsible for installation and construction costs for the traffic signal. (See Exhibit "A")
3. The estimated value of the 0.027 acres is less than $1,000.00.
4. The cost of the span wire traffic signal is not to exceed $150,000.00. (See Exhibit "B")
5. Palm Beach County will be responsible for a 100% of the maintenance and repair cost in exchange for the 0.027 acres.
6. The traffic signal will improve safety for the School District and the community.

RE-2 Palm Beach Public West Campus Sale and Purchase Agreement
Meeting
Nov 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)
1. On September 22, 2010, the School Board declared the Palm Beach Public West Campus surplus and directed staff to advertise to divest the 0.69 acre improved property. (See Exhibit "A")
2. The School District did not receive any proposals in response to the advertised solicitation however The Society of the Four Arts, pursuant to their right of first refusal offered to purchase the property in accordance with their letter to the Board dated February 22, 2010. The letter included an immediate purchase offer of Five million, four hundred thousand dollars ($5,400,000.00) and a partnership that includes the following:
      A.  A commitment to provide a perpetual parking easement for up to 39 teacher parking spaces for Palm Beach Public's staff.
      B.  Expand the Society's Fortin Endowment for Children's Activities to not less than one million dollar ($1,000,000.00) by October 31, 2015 for the arts-enrichment outreach program for the students of Palm Beach County.
      C.  Allow Palm Beach Public to use the Society's remodeled auditorium located on the former Palm Beach Public West Campus.
      D. The Society will rearrange their lecture schedule to begin their programs at 3:00 PM to accommodate Palm Beach Public's school hours.
3. The current Lease provides that the School Board may not terminate the lease during the first fifteen (15) years unless the School Board decides to sell the West Campus.  If the Lease is terminated (after the first fifteen (15) years) or the West Campus is sold during the Lease term, the School Board must refund, or credit, the Society of the Four Arts with an amortized amount of the prepaid rent.  (See Exhibit "C" to the Lease Agreement)  At this time, the unamortized rent is equal to $320,000.00.
4. The Society believes that this partnership can create an arts-education relationship that could serve as a model for other public-private efforts in many educational areas.
5. The 2010 averaged appraised value is $5,100,000.00. The contract price is $5,400,000.00 and the Society waived their right to a $320,000.00 credit for unamortized rent under the Lease.
6. Seller and Buyer shall equally divide costs for Title Insurance Commitment, copies of title exemptions, title examination fees, closing fees, courier fees, owner's title insurance premium, documentary stamps and recording costs estimated at $58,000.00.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Real Estate Sale and Purchase Agreement, the Parking Easement Agreement, and the Cooperative Agreement between the Society of the Four Arts and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the Palm Beach Public West Campus located at 240 Cocoanut Row, Palm Beach; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore (moorej@palmbeachschools.org)
Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeachschools.org)

Financial Impact:

A sale of the West Campus would result in a positive financial impact of $5,400,000.00, less not to exceed $29,000.00 in closing costs.
