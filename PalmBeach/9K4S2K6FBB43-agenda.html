CP-1 Termination of the Architectural Services Agreement for Palm Beach Gardens Community High School Modernization
Meeting
Aug 27, 2008 - Capital Budget Tentative Approval and Construction Projects
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

Termination of the Owner/Architect Agreement as allowed by Article 8, Paragraph 8.1 (Termination, Suspension or Abandonment) of the Agreement, the Owner may terminate for failure to substantially perform.



The following is a brief history on the events leading to the recommendation of Sylla, Inc.’s termination:





  • On March 14, 2005 the Palm Beach Gardens High School Project was advertised.

  • Subsequent to a competitive selection process, on May 16, 2005 Sylla, Inc. was selected.

  • On July 21, 2005, the School Board entered into a Standard Form of Agreement with Sylla, Inc. to provide Architectural/Engineering design services for the Palm Beach Gardens High School, Project No. 1371-7506.

  • On or about May 29, 2008, School District personnel received a Notice of Levy from the IRS regarding Sylla, Inc.

  • The Notice of Levy required the School Board to turn over to the IRS all property and rights to property (such as money, credits, and bank deposits) that the School Board has that are obligated to Sylla, Inc.

  • To date, the School Board is up to date with payment of all payment applications submitted by Sylla, Inc. and is in compliance with the Agreement.  The School Board’s last payment to Sylla, Inc. was prior to receipt of the Notice of Levy on May 9, 2008.

  • Subsequently, due to receipt of a Notice of Levy by Sylla, Inc.’s bank, it withheld all funds that Sylla, Inc. deposited including the payment that it received from the School District on May 9, 2008.  As a result Sylla, Inc. was unable to pay its subconsultants in violation of the Prompt Payment Act and in violation of its Agreement with the School Board.

  • The Prompt Payment Act requires that upon receiving payment from the School District, it will pay all of its obligations for labor, services, and materials on the Project in accordance with contract terms.  The contract terms of each and every subconsultant contract that Sylla, Inc. has entered into on this Project requires monthly payment for services rendered.

  • The Agreement requires that Sylla take no action that would adversely affect the Project and also requires that Sylla, Inc. ensure that its portion of the work is done in an expeditious manner.  The Agreement further requires that Sylla comply with all applicable laws that affect the Project.

  • Sylla, Inc.’s failure to pay its subconsultants has jeopardized the project as Sylla Inc.’s subconsultants have indicated that they will cease work on the project due to Sylla, Inc.’s failure to pay for work performed as required under their respective subconsultant contracts.  Clearly, without its subconsultants, Sylla, Inc. cannot ensure that the work on the Project is done in an expeditious manner.

  • On August 12, 2008, Sylla, Inc. was sent a letter demanding that it immediately comply with its contractual and statutory obligations and make payment on all outstanding bills due and owning to its subconsultants, and provide the School District with a written statement of action that it will take to address and prevent the problems outlined above.  The August 12th letter further states that Sylla’s failure to comply with the School District’s demand will result in its termination in accordance with Article 8.1 of the Agreement.

  • To date, Sylla, Inc. has not paid its subconsultants in violation of the Prompt Payment Act and the Agreement.  As a result, the subconsultants would like to cease work on the Project thereby ceasing all Architectural and Engineering support on the Project.  A ceasing of all Architectural and Engineering support on the Project represents a substantial failure to perform on the Project and will prove fatal to the Project.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the termination of the Owner/Architect Agreement dated July 21, 2005 with Sylla, Inc., for Palm Beach Gardens High School, Project No. 1371-7506.

There is no Financial Impact. All payment applications submitted to date by Sylla, Inc. have been paid and The School District is in compliance with the Agreement.

CP-2 Architectural Design Services for Palm Beach Gardens High School
Meeting
Aug 27, 2008 - Capital Budget Tentative Approval and Construction Projects

Discussion

Architectural/Engineering required for Construction Administration services during the construction of the new 2,774 student station facility and demolition of the existing facility.



 



Architectural/ Engineering for the Palm Beach Gardens High School, Project No. 1371-7506 was advertised on March 14, 2005.



Subsequent to a competitive selection process, on May 16, 2005 Sylla, Inc. was selected.



 



On July 21, 2005, the School Board entered into a Standard Form of Agreement with Sylla, Inc. to provide Architectural/Engineering design services for the Palm Beach Gardens High School, Project No. 1371-7506.



 



On or about May 29, 2008, School District personnel received a Notice of Levy from the IRS regarding Sylla, Inc.



 



The Notice of Levy required the School Board to turn over to the IRS all property and rights to property (such as money, credits, and bank deposits) that the School Board has that are obligated to Sylla, Inc.



 



To date, the School Board is up to date with payment of all payment applications submitted by Sylla, Inc. and is in compliance with the Agreement.  The School Board’s last payment to Sylla, Inc. was prior to receipt of the Notice of Levy on May 9, 2008.



 



Subsequently, due to receipt of a Notice of Levy by Sylla, Inc.’s bank, it withheld all funds that Sylla, Inc. deposited including the payment that it received from the School District on May 9, 2008.  As a result Sylla, Inc. was unable to pay its subconsultants in violation of the Prompt Payment Act and in violation of its Agreement with the School Board.



 



The Prompt Payment Act requires that upon receiving payment from the School District, it will pay all of its obligations for labor, services, and materials on the Project in accordance with contract terms.  The contract terms of each and every subconsultant contract that Sylla, Inc. has entered into on this Project requires monthly payment for services rendered.



 



The Agreement requires that Sylla take no action that would adversely affect the Project and also requires that Sylla, Inc. ensure that its portion of the work is done in an expeditious manner.  The Agreement further requires that Sylla comply with all applicable laws that affect the Project.



 



Sylla, Inc.’s failure to pay its subconsultants has jeopardized the project as Sylla Inc.’s subconsultants have indicated that they will cease work on the project due to Sylla, Inc.’s failure to pay for work performed as required under their respective subconsultant contracts.  Clearly, without its subconsultants, Sylla, Inc. cannot ensure that the work on the Project is done in an expeditious manner.



On August 12, 2008, Sylla, Inc. was sent a letter demanding that it immediately comply with its contractual and statutory obligations and make payment on all outstanding bills due and owning to its subconsultants, and provide the School District with a written statement of action that it will take to address and prevent the problems outlined above.  The August 12th letter further states that Sylla’s failure to comply with the School District’s demand will result in its termination in accordance with Article 8.1 of the Agreement.



 



To date, Sylla, Inc. has not paid its subconsultants in violation of the Prompt Payment Act and the Agreement.  As a result, the subconsultants would like to cease work on the Project thereby ceasing all Architectural and Engineering support on the Project.  A ceasing of all Architectural and Engineering support on the Project represents a substantial failure to perform on the Project and will prove fatal to the Project.



 



As a result of the above mentioned facts, on August 27, 2008, there exists an agenda item – CP1 - which recommends the termination of Sylla, Inc.



 



Should the School Board terminate Sylla, Inc. at the August 27, 2008 Board meeting, the public welfare requires that the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida take immediate action to award the contract for Architectural/ Engineering for Palm Beach Gardens High School, Project No. 1371-7506 for reasons outlined above; and hence, a valid public emergency exists.



Tercilla, Courtemanche Architects, Inc. has been a subconsultant of Sylla, Inc. since the same date that the School Board awarded the Architectural/ Engineering for Palm Beach Gardens High School Project to Sylla, Inc. – July 21, 2005.  Hence, Tercilla, Courtemanche Architects, Inc. will not have to expend time to familiarize itself with the Project.  Additionally, Tercilla, Courtemanche Architects, Inc. has indicated that it would utilize all of the other subconsultants that Sylla, Inc. utilized on the Project so as to minimize the time for Tercilla, Courtemanche Architects, Inc.’s subconsultants to familiarize themselves with the Project.
