Recommended Action
I recommend the Board consider 2017 Preliminary State Legislative Recommendations as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

The attached list of critical issues was discussed at the July 27 Workshop on 2017 Legislative issues. Consensus, suggestions, and requests by Board members have been incorporated and are presented for approval.  These recommendations may be modified or augmented at any future time as desired by the School Board up to and during the 2016 regular legislative session which begins March 7, 2017, and runs 60 calendar days through May 5, 2017. These issues can also be continued or modified in the event of any subsequent special session.  The adopted recommendations will be submitted to appropriate state and local organizations and agencies, including Palm Beach County business and community organizations, Palm Beach County Legislative Delegation, state associations, Treasure Coast workgroup, State Board of Education and Commissioner Pam Stewart, and Governor Rick Scott.

 

Because of developing changes, especially concerning state assessment and implementation of federal ESSA (Every Student Succeeds Act) legislation, it is anticipated these proposals will be brought back for Board review and modification in December or early January, prior to a joint meeting with the Palm Beach County Legislative Delegation in Late January.  Adopted proposals will serve as the basis for presentation and discussion at a joint meeting with the Palm Beach County Legislative Delegation scheduled for September 30, 2015 (2-4:30pm, Forest Hill Community High School auditorium).

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Settlement Agreement in the case of S.S. v. Palm Beach County School Board, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent/designee to finalize and execute all necessary and related Settlement documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This Settlement Agreement is recommended to avoid further risk exposure to the School Board in terms of additional attorney fees and possible sanctions relating to DOAH Case No.:16-1860E.

 

CONTACT:

JulieAnn Rico, Esq., General Counsel (julieann.rico@palmbeachschools.org)

Laura Pincus, Esq., Assistant General Counsel (laura.pincus@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The settlement provides the student’s parents with $10,000 to be used toward compensatory tutoring and $50,000 for attorney fees, and shall resolve all claims in DOAH Case No.:16-1860E.
