FMPO1 Award of Bid 17C-817A for Purchase of Unleaded Gasoline and Diesel Fuel

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the award of Bid 17C-817A for the purchase of unleaded gasoline and diesel fuel for the period April 1, 2017 through March 14, 2020; not to exceed $11,000,000 annually.

DESCRIPTION:

Award of Bid 17C-817A, an alternate source contract off of the Southeast Florida Governmental Purchasing Cooperative Group RFP E-13-17, for the purchase of unleaded gasoline and diesel fuel from Port Consolidated, the awarded vendor.

 

The City of Pompano Beach, acting as the lead agency for the purchase of unleaded gasoline and diesel fuel for the Southeast Florida Governmental Purchasing Cooperative Group of which the School District of Palm Beach County is a member, solicited proposals under RFP E-13-17, combining the usage of over 25 agencies that chose to participate in this procurement.  A representative from the School District of Palm Beach County served on the evaluation committee and also participated in negotiations which resulted in a significant cost avoidance for the Cooperative.  The contract resulting from this RFP was executed on March 15, 2017.  

 

In addition to the competitive cost offered by Port Consolidated, when evaluating this company against other responders, it was noted that Port Consolidated has the largest petroleum storage capacity, which includes a major facility in Palm Beach County. They also own and operate a number of commercial fueling facilities (similar to gas stations) throughout their primary service area (Miami-Dade, Broward, Palm Beach, Martin & St. Lucie Counties) including a fueling facility on Garden Road in Riviera Beach just in front of North Transportation which is critical to the District during hurricanes.  It was also determined that Port Consolidated fuel contracts with suppliers enables them to be better positioned for access to fuel during emergencies including hurricanes.

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The annual financial impact to the District budget is not to exceed $11,000,000. The source of funds is the Transportation Department Operating Budget and various school and department budgets.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve a budget increase in the amount of $150,000, for a total of $800,000, and approve the Owner – Contractor Agreement with Ibis Building Corporation to renovate the existing cafeteria at Palm Beach Central High School, Invitation to Bid 17-40R, Project No. 2631-1605 for $667,916.72; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all required documents as necessary.

DESCRIPTION:

Approval of the contract with Ibis Building Corporation is resulting from Invitation to Bid 17-40R, for the Cafeteria Renovation at Palm Beach Central High School, Project No. 2631-1605, to include remodeling the cafeteria and serving lines, to enhance customer service and ultimately increase participation. Improvements to the serving area will include new menu boards, decorative lighting, and additional support equipment. Improvements to the multipurpose room will include Bahama shutters, faux columns, signage, new flooring and televisions.

 

The budget increase is to cover the actual construction cost plus contingency, as well as design fees, required data cabling and other related expenses.

 

JUSTIFICATION:

School Food Service (SFS) developed a plan of action with the cooperation of the State (Office of Food, Nutrition and Wellness) to reinvent the way students perceive and receive their school meals.  This plan took into consideration the meal time provided, physical facility, customer service, nutritious food offerings and the overall dining experience.  Since its inception, the High School Reengineering Program (HSRP) has not only met, but exceeded the expectations of SFS.  With these modifications, students will enjoy a customer driven dining experience with the opportunity to nutritionally recharge for improved physical and mental well-being, supporting the District’s goal of improving academic performance.

 

The Board approved the budget of $650,000 on Board Item PM2 on November 17, 2015.  The budget increase will cover all expenses, excluding equipment and startup purchases best managed outside of the construction contract to avoid unnecessary cost markups.

 

A minimum of 13% M/WBE African American subcontractor participation is included in this contract.
