Subject
Cell Towers on School Campuses
Meeting
Feb 11, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Wkshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action




  •  School District has cell tower agreements at nine (9) schools which were negotiated between the years of 1994-96




  •   School Board was involved in a lawsuit in the 90’s over cell towers on school campuses and the radiation issue




  • School Board imposed a “moratorium” on any new cell towers subsequent to the lawsuit




  • In 2005 the western community requested consideration for a new cell tower to be located at Western Pines MS to improve cell phone reception 




  •  School Board made an exception due to the community request, approved an RFP resulting in a new cell tower agreement for Western Pines MS in August 2005




  • Hillsborough County School District recently issued an RFP for a District wide analysis of all school sites and awarded an agreement to vendor who is responsible for identifying areas that need additional cellular service, coordination with all stakeholders (schools, impacted communities, municipalities, regulatory agencies, cellular providers, etc.) and developing cellular towers on district properties under a revenue sharing scenario.  




  • Discussion is requested regarding the School Board’s interest in revisiting the placement of cell towers on school property under an agreement similar to that which is being done by the Hillsborough County School District.

Energy Conservation and Recycling Workshop
Meeting
Feb 11, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Wkshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action

1.      On March 5, 2008 the School Board made a verbal commitment to build all future buildings as Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) Silver Certified.



2.      While Pine Jog Elementary School is getting much deserved attention including recently winning first place at the Florida Educational Facilities Planners Association (FEFPA) Winter Conference, it is just the culmination of a number of efforts the District has and continues to undertake to “green” our schools.



3.      Staff will present information on a sampling of the many things the District is doing in the areas of cost control and energy and resource conservation.  Staff will also make recommendations for the District’s next bold steps to realize the quintuple benefits of:



o   Cost Savings



o   Better Academic Performance



o   Increased Work Productivity



o   Improved Health for Occupants



o   Environmental Stewardship



4.      Issues to be covered during this workshop include, but are not limited to:



o   Auditing of Utility Bills



o   Energy Management System



o   Waste Reduction and Recycling



o   Built in Measures vs. Behavior Modification



o   Related Policies



o   Performance Contracting



o   School Success Stories



o   Next Steps
