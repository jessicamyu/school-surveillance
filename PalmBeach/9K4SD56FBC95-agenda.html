G1 - Carol M. White Physical Education Program (PEP) Grant

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded the competitive Carol M. White Physical Education Program (PEP) Grant from the United States Department of Education for up to $2,089,421 for a three-year period from October 1, 2011, through September 30, 2014.



The "Kids Getting Fit!" grant program will be implemented district-wide to provide a research-based, sequential K-5  physical education curriculum, aligned with Florida's Next Generation Sunshine State Standards for Physical Education (PE).  Over the course of the three-year grant project, "Kids Getting Fit!" will be implemented through the planned phase-in of the new curriculum in 36-37 elementary schools per year.



To address the physical activity component, the project will implement the Sports Play and Active Recreation for Kids (SPARK) curriculum. To address the need for improved nutrition instruction, the project will implement the Healthy Kids Challenge (HKC) curriculum.  Elementary physical education (PE) teachers throughout the District will be supported as they learn to use a consistent delivery model to provide K-5 students with a blended fitness, and nutrition program to meet the needs of all students, regardless of ability, and to use a standard protocol for student assessment. The project's overall goal is to improve the number of students K-5 who meet Florida Next Generation Standards in Physical Education. This will be accomplished through (1) implementation of the sequential PE curriculum, (2) instruction in cognitive concepts related to fitness, health, and nutrition, (3) a program that focuses on cooperative behavior and group play, (4) professional development for elementary PE teachers, (5) purchase of curriculum-supporting equipment for schools, and (6) thorough, and frequent student assessment.  All equipment will be centrally purchased for the schools at a discounted price.  The equipment for the grant is aligned to the training provided in the grant and will be developmentally and grade level appropriate. Upon approval, the grant will fund a Project Coordinator to manage and monitor program delivery, equipment, and day to day tasks associated with the project; and the grant will fund a curriculum/assessment specialist to support teachers with curriculum implementation and data collection using standardized protocol.



The "Kids Getting Fit!" evaluation component includes the objective evaluation of student proficiency levels of various motor skills and fitness assessments with combined in-school and out-of-school activities. This grant will fund an external evaluator to assess quantitative and qualitative data, determine the project's progress, and report on its impact on meeting project goals and state standards.



The district received official notification of this award on September 30, 2011. Programmatic, personnel and purchase actions will begin, following board approval, on November 3, 2011. Although the grant is effective October 1, 2011, this does not prejudice the School Board.  The total budget request is for $2,089,421 for three years.  The grant award for the first year will be $651,865. A copy of the grant application is available for review in the Board Office.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Carol M. White Physical Education Program (PEP) Grant from the United States Department of Education for up to $2,089,421 effective October 1, 2011, through September 30, 2014, and any no cost extension as applicable; and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded The JPB Foundation (formerly known as The Picower Foundation grant).  The goals of the JPB Foundation grant are consistent with the goals of the original School Board Picower Foundation grant approved on June 4, 2008.  Barbara Picower, President and Chair of the JPB Foundation, is fulfilling a previous commitment to the School District of Palm Beach County to support the Single School Culture © for Academics project (Learning Team Meeting Initiative).





Learning Team Facilitators are highly trained instructional teacher-leaders who provide just-in-time, on-site professional development to the teachers on the campus through Professional Learning Communities called Learning Team Meetings.  Professional Learning Communities (PLCs) are required by the State in the Differentiated Accountability Plan.  The research of Saunders et al., 2009 very clearly spells out the need for structure in PLCs if the meetings are going to be effective.  The Learning Team Meetings in our District deliberately utilize research-based protocols that provide direction and structure to the meetings and through participation, build teacher capacity.





The grant will support training stipends for participating teachers from schools that do not have a Learning Team Facilitator but are still accountable for the requirements of the State of Florida's Differentiated Accountability plan.  Some of the training components include:






  • data analysis/instructional strategies and action planning;

  • unpacking Next Generation Sunshine State Standards (NGSSS) and articulation of academic targets;

  • development of rigorous standards-based assignments;

  • lesson study; and,

  • writing Learning Goals and Scales for NGSSS.


One additional component agreed upon with the JPB Foundation is a partnership with the Handel Group to provide professional development for principal growth.  The Handel Group is an executive consulting and coaching company which has worked with many major corporations including AOL, Citibank, the New York Times, Vogue, and Sony Records.  The Handel Group Education Division's mission is to enhance traditional models of education by offering instruction on how to lead powerfully and effectively.  The Handel Group has expertise in creating high functioning leaders able to utilize their natural ability and incorporate proven techniques to transform the culture in a school to support the development of teachers and students alike.





In order for Learning Team Meetings to be effective in supporting the development of teachers, a supportive environment must be in place.  The Handel Group prides itself on the ability to provide leaders with the knowledge and skills to develop a true learning environment.  Research on PLCs indicates that effective change and continuous improvement are more likely to endure when an appropriate adult learning environment is created.





A copy of the grant is available in the Board Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the JPB Foundation grant (formerly The Picower Foundation Grant) for $534,311 effective October 15, 2011 through October 14, 2013 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

In order to address the need for new and well-trained teachers, FAU and its regional partners, which includes the School District of Palm Beach County, have created a three-prong, innovative approach to prepare College of Education majors to be well-trained and highly-qualified teachers upon graduation and entry into the teaching profession.



Good FIT increases the number of education majors by providing freshmen, sophomores and others, interested in teaching, the opportunity to provide instructional support with model classroom teachers. This will increase the number and quality of students entering and remaining in the field of education and teacher preparation.  Undergraduate students will be receiving extensive classroom experiences and intensive multi-year support with retired educators and mentors, as well as a minimal stipend.  The students in the school classrooms will be provided with additional support from qualified and caring undergraduates to directly support mutual learning in concert with the classroom teachers.



The Substituting with Mentors and Realistic Teaching (SMaRT) program provides classroom experience to college juniors and seniors to work as substitute teachers with mentors.  A SMaRT site (elementary, middle or high school), where public school and university personnel work together to facilitate high levels of learning by all children at the school, utilizes Florida Atlantic University students as substitute teachers. These students work under the direction of a FAU mentor. This program will be dedicated to improving the quality of pre-service and in-service training. The School District of Palm Beach County and Florida Atlantic University will combine resources and provide leadership for the development of the SMaRT sites.



The purpose of this relationship is to enable the parties to:



·         improve the performance of students respective of each institution;



·         extend mutual research and grant capacity;



·         apply and model promising teaching practices;



·         inform and improve school and university instruction; and \test innovative means to develop new education professionals.



The Accelerated Induction into Teaching (AIT) Program fills high-need teacher vacancies with selected university seniors who are ready to student teach as the actual classroom teacher with daily support from a mentor. The District's Academic Business Plan calls for intensive and targeted recruitment strategies to insure the availability of highly-qualified teachers for high needs schools. The AIT Program is an initiative that provides for the identification and placement of high-quality teacher candidates in high needs schools.



 



·         AIT candidates will be selected from Florida Atlantic University's Teacher Training Program;



·         All AIT candidates will have successfully passed the CLAST and the Florida Teacher Training Program;



·         The AIT will be supervised by an experienced Master Mentor hired by FAU and reviewed by the School District.  The Master Mentor will provide direct supervision/observation.  (see agreement);



·         The AIT Program management model incorporates three levels of supervision: Master Mentor, University Supervision, and Cooperating Teacher; and  the AIT Program will reduce the need to employ alternative certification applicants and substitute teachers.

Recommendation:

Consider approval of the Master Affiliation Agreement FIAT Programs between the School District of Palm Beach County and Florida Atlantic University effective FY12.
