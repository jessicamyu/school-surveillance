Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Construction Change Order #2 for an increase of $1,324,561 to State Contracting & Engineering Corp. for the Okeeheelee Middle School Sales Tax Renovations, Project 2151-7100.

DESCRIPTION:

Project Modifications include the following:

  • Change Order Proposal Request (COPR) #2: an increase in the amount of $1,324,561.
     
  • Description: The proposed change order includes the remaining work recommended in the Facility Condition Assessment. This includes:
    • Milling, paving and restriping existing parking lots
    • Provide coating on the existing metal roof to extend its useful life
    • Provide coating on existing metal fascia panels to extend their useful life
    • Upgrade HVAC units
    • Repair damaged casework and wall finishes
       
  • Additional funds totaling $1,324,561 are needed to complete the additional work.
     
  • Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee, ISSOC, recommended the increase of $1,324,561 to the project budget at the meeting on June 6, 2019.
     
  • Cause: District Request
     
  • Justification: The GMP brought to the School Board on May 8, 2019, maximized the work that could be completed during the summer of 2019. With the recommendation of additional funds by ISSOC and approval by the School Board, the remainder of the scope will be completed.

CONTACT:

David G. Dolan, Deputy Chief of Facilities Management (david.dolan@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The financial impact is an increase in the amount of $1,324,561 to State Contracting & Engineering Corp. contract (CM @ Risk Agreement). The source of funds is the Sales Tax Revenue Contingency Account.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the School Improvement Plan for Glades Academy and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related correspondence and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 22, 2011, the School Board granted Glades Academy a renewal Charter Contract with the School Board of Palm Beach County. The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of ten (10) years commencing July 1, 2011, and ending June 30, 2021, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.

 

Glades Academy received a grade of “D” for the fiscal year 2019. F.S. 1002.33, Section 9(n)(1) states:

 

“The principal and a representative of the governing board of a charter school that has earned a grade of “D” or “F” pursuant to s. 1008.34(2) shall appear before the sponsor to present information concerning each contract component having noted deficiencies. The principal and a representative of the governing board shall submit to the sponsor for approval a school improvement plan to raise student achievement. Upon approval by the sponsor, the charter school shall begin implementation of the school improvement plan. The department shall offer technical assistance for developing, submitting, and approving such plans.” (emphasis added)

 

The Department of Charter Schools staff provided technical assistance to Glades Academy in the drafting of the School Improvement Plan attached.
