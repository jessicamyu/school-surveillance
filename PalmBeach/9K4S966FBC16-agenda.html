PS 1 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Paula Prudente

Nov 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics

Consent

In April of 2010, Paula Prudente became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation following a request from the Principal of her school to proceed to the next step of progressive discipline.  The Employee Relations was based upon allegations of unprofessional conduct, gross insubordination, ethical misconduct, misuse of District technology, and violations of School Board Policies 0.00, Preamble to the Code; 0.04, Commitment to Professional Employment Practices; 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff; 3.29, Employee Use of Technology and Devices; 3.31, Grievance Procedures for Employees; 5.002, Prohibition of Bullying and Harrassment; and Florida Administrative Code 6B-1.006, Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida.



The investigation revealed that Ms. Prudente has continuously used District email to send inappropriate emails regarding District and school administrators and School Board members.  The investigation further revealed that Ms. Prudente has received multiple directives regarding the misuse of District email and has been disciplined regarding her continued behavior, to include suspension without pay, recommended on December 2, 2009.  Her continued disregard for directives and School Board Policy constitutes gross insubordination.  All forms of progressive discipline have failed.



There is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations and to support the termination. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Paula Prudente's suspension without pay, effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Paula Prudente's employment effective November 29, 2010 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

CLS1 - JFK Middle School - International Baccalaureate Training in Montreal, Quebec, Canada
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

Principal Hamlet and three faculty members request permission to attend the International Baccalaureate training conference in Montreal, QC, Canada November 12-15, 2010.  This conference will allow faculty to acquire new strategies and resources regarding increasing leadership capacity in schools to improve student learning through an extended 12-month teacher contract that allows for distributed leadership. 



The IB Training is a requirement in order for JFK Middle School to maintain its accreditation.  Being an accredited IB MYP school will open up opportunities for students to embrace a global perspective of the world in their daily studies.  The workshops also review a model that created time for professional learning, monitoring of new teachers, vertical K-12 alignment, student transition, and school improvement by reinventing the approach to school leadership.  The training will also enable the administrators to understand the philosophy of IB and how to implement a successful IB MYP program which will prepare students for their high school IB years.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve a trip for the principal and three faculty members, to travel to Montreal QC, Canada , November 12-15, 2010, for the purpose of attending the International Baccalaureate Training Conference.

PS 2 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Ernest Brown
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action

A special audit of Suncoast High School's Band Account was conducted for fiscal year 2008 at the request of the former and current Principals to audit the internal funds.  Based upon the audit, the school's band account incurred a total deficit of $107,895 in two (2) overseas trips.  The Band Account had a total deficit of $62,495 at the end of School Year 2008 resulting from the Band's field trip to Paris, France and a total deficit of $45,400 resulting from the Band's field trip to London, England in 2007.  There were a total of one hundred (100) people that participated in the trip, which included fifty-four (54) Suncoast students, thirteen (13) parents, the band director, the school's former Principal, four (4) Dreyfoos students, eight (8) college students and nineteen (19) non-Suncoast related adults.  Eighteen (18) participants went on the trip free of charge and sixty-six (66) of the participants received partial waivers, ranging from $5 to $2,520.  Mr. Brown and his wife, as well as his son, two daughters, nephew and niece went on the Paris trip either free or at little cost via fee waivers.  The audit concluded that Mr. Brown's relatives received total waivers of $14,710 for the trip and that other non-school related persons received fee waivers amounting to $45,292.  Thus, the total of fee waivers for the band trip to Paris was $60,002 or 96% of the total deficit.



Following the Special Audit, Ernest Brown became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation regarding allegations of failure to properly control District funds, failure to exercise best professional judgment, conversion of School District funds and/or property, Violation of School Board 6.07, Internal Accounts, and School Board Policy 1.013, Paragraph 1, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff.  In addition, the allegations include violations of Florida Administrative Code 6B-1.006(4) (c) & (d), the Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida, as well as 6B-1.001(3) the code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.



Based upon substantial information gathered through the audit and subsequent investigation, there is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve a seven (7) day suspension without pay for Ernest Brown effective November 29, 2010, subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

AC1 - Cooperative Agreement between T.E.N. and WXEL to Expand T.E.N.'s Service
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

In the continued effort to maximize accessibility to T.E.N.'s programming to residents of Palm Beach County, T.E.N. and WXEL have worked out a collaborative agreement that will mutually benefit both organizations.  This agreement will be based on shared productions and in-kind services.



Presently, T.E.N.'s maximum possible outreach on Comcast is 465,000 subscribers, many of whom are unable to receive our channel assignment on Comcast.  WXEL can reach over 5 million viewers located throughout all of Palm Beach County and several additional counties, including Broward, North Dade, Martin and others, (see attached map).  WXEL is an "over-the-air" station.  Anyone, even those who do not subscribe to cable, Dish or Uverse, can receive WXEL with Rabbit Ears.



Benefits to T.E.N:
     *T.E.N.'s programming (selected by WXEL) will be carried over the primary channel;
     *T.E.N. will have access to WXEL Channel 42.4 from 6:00 pm until 6:00 am, and can possibly include
       weekends and be expanded at a future date;
     *Access would be for 2 years;
     *Agreement can be dissolved by either party with 30 days notice;
     *This agreement would enable everyone in Palm Beach County and beyond to access our programming
       featuring student performances, distance learning, school news, Board Meetings/Workshops, and
       documentary programming;
      *Co-branding our productions and transmission with WXEL;
      *WXEL will be airing our productions on their main channel, and we will be accredited;
      *Collaboration to produce and market programming;
      *Cross promotion of programming and activities.



T.E.N. is responsible for equipment and connections to WXEL to receive T.E.N.'s programming.



Benefits to WXEL:
      *Access to T.E.N.'s programming;
      *Access to T.E.N.'s facilities;
      *Production support;
      *Access to remote truck facilities;
      *Engineering consultation.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Cooperative Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and WXEL that will allow T.E.N. to transmit programming on WXEL in exchange for in-kind services from T.E.N. in the form of shared facilities, and  engineering and production support.
