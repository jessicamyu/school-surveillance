Subject
2018-19 Boundary Cycle Workshop - Odyssey Boundary Study
Meeting
Dec 6, 2017 - 6. WORKSHOP immediately following Risk Mgmt Session in the Board Room
Category
2. Workshop Items
Type
Presentation

DESCRIPTION:

Commencing the 2018-2019 school year: Changes in student attendance boundaries for five (5) Middle Schools – Carver, Christa McAuliffe, Congress, Lantana, and Woodlands-- and repeal of the current student attendance boundary for Odyssey.

 

Pursuant to Policy 5.01, Student Assignment, the purpose of the Workshop is to provide a summary of the Advisory Boundary Committee’s (ABC) and Superintendent’s recommendations for the 2018-19 Boundary Cycle to shift the current Odyssey Middle attendance boundary to several area middle schools, due to the repurposing of Odyssey Middle.

 

On November 9, 2017, the ABC voted 8-4 to present the following Boundary Change Proposal to the Superintendent:

 

“Odyssey Boundary Study Updated 10-26-17” (Odyssey to Carver, Christa McAuliffe, Congress, Lantana, Woodlands).

 

The study recommended by the ABC specified that students currently residing in the Odyssey Middle attendance boundary will be shifted to five (5) area Middle Schools commencing the 2018-19 school year- Carver, Christa McAuliffe, Congress, Lantana, and Woodlands Middle Schools. The recommended boundary study provides additional enrollment at other schools and moves the following SAC’s:

 

  • SACs 261, 262A, 262B, 270, 271, 274C, 275, 278, 279, 280A, 280B, 281, 283, and 372 from Odyssey Middle to Carver Middle
  • SACs 242B, 252, 253, and 260 from Odyssey Middle to Christa McAuliffe Middle
  • SACs 250B, 250D, 250E, 266B, 267, 268, 269, 272, 273, 274A, 274B, 370, 371, 406A, and 406B from Odyssey Middle to Congress Middle
  • SACs 251, 258, 350, 368, and 369 from Odyssey Middle to Lantana Middle
  • SACs 242A, 259A, and 259B from Odyssey Middle to Woodlands Middle

 

The four dissenting ABC votes were due to concerns that the study would put Christa McAuliffe Middle School over capacity and that the study does not fully utilize the capacity available at Carver Middle School.


The Superintendent reviewed and considered the recommendation of the ABC, as well as public comments received at the ABC meetings and in written correspondence, and made a minor adjustment to the ABC’s recommendation. The Superintendent’s recommendation shifts SAC 261, which the ABC study assigned to Carver, to Christa McAuliffe. By including the entire SAC, the boundary study no longer has the partial SAC “finger” configuration that concerned some ABC members when reviewing the Superintendent’s original study. SAC 261 currently has 67 middle school students that reside in the Odyssey attendance boundary, and 18 students that are attending Odyssey Middle. SAC 261 contains 15 residential developments, seven of which are 55+ adult communities.

 

The study makes note that new Middle School “17-PP,” located adjacent to Sunset Palms Elementary, was added to this year’s Capital Plan and will open as soon as possible based on demographics, procurement procedures, and construction timeline. Staff has determined that the leasing of temporary portable classrooms at Christa McAuliffe Middle is feasible and will not impact DOE approval of the Educational Plant Survey, if the classrooms are temporary.

 

On 11/30/17, the study recommended by the Superintendent was discussed by the Diversity and Equity Committee. The following are comments and concerns raised by the Diversity and Equity Committee: 

 

  • Concerns that boundary changes may have the potential to affect students with disabilities. Many students with disabilities face difficulties with alterations in their normal routine and a change in school assignments is a major upheaval that could disrupt their learning. Additionally, special education services and resources vary across different schools.
  • A committee member asked about the resulting racial composition of the schools in the boundary study.
  • A committee member expressed concern that charter schools are lacking when it comes to serving students with disabilities, and that the District should take a stronger approach to ensure charter schools are sufficiently serving students with disabilities.
  • Committee had some concerns that South Tech Academy will compete directly against traditional District schools.
  • If the new middle school "17-PP" will offer any choice programs.

The boundary study proposal and Supporting Documentation, including the study, maps and the SAC descriptions, are provided as attachments for both the ABC and the Superintendent’s recommendations.
