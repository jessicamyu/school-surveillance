Aug 31, 2011 - Special Meeting

This is the annual agreement with the Palm Beach County Education Commission, Inc. and the School District of Palm Beach County.






  • The Education Commission is a value-added organization.  No one institution can afford to staff a position to assist and support all of the collaborative initiatives.  The Children's Services Council, Florida Atlantic University, Palm Beach State College, Workforce Alliance, the Palm Beach County School District and private funders all contribute financially to the Education Commission.  In turn the Education Commission facilitates, link resources, people, partnerships, and identifies barriers to projects.  The Education commission never directly initiates or oversees projects.




  • The most important function of the Education Commission is through the work of its Board.  Its Board is private sector driven and was developed to create a venue for discussion of education issues and solutions.  Public sector CEOs and community leaders meet face-to-face on a regular basis.  The process has created a high level of inter-agency trust and collaboration as well as community support for educational issues.


Deliverables/Projects continuing in 2012 include the following:







  • Grants Consortium - including representation from all funding partners and the Palm Beach County Health Care District, Palm Beach County Government, Hispanic Human Resources, Prime Time PBC, Urban League of Palm Beach County, ASPIRA, and other interested parties to provide networking for grant writing opportunities.




  • Readers/Writers Workshop - expanding the School District's initiative




  • Supporting th digital Divide Project




  • Ethics Initiative - linking the county wide ethics initiative and Palm Beach State College's Ethics Institute with existing School District projects




  • Six Pillars Framework - established by the Florida Chamber Foundation to serve as a statewide strategic visioning process, the education commission facilitates:  Talent Supply and, Innovation and Economic Development, Infrastructure and Growth Leadership, Business Climate and Competitiveness, Civics and Governance Systems, Quality of Life and Quality Places




  • Digital Domain - a provate partnership between Digital Domain and Florida State University will offer a dual-enrollment degree program with FSU and the Digital Domain Institute for students seeking a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree in animation and digital arts.  Digital Domain will work closely with the school district to align curriculum with existing programs and provide hands-on learning experiences for students.


In an effort to create a seamless education system, the Palm Beach County Education Commission will promote services for the care, support, and education of children from birth and continuing through Pre-K until a child enters the formal K-20 education system.  The Palm Beach County Education Commission seeks to improve the knowledge and skills of Palm Beach county's residents based on a supportive learning environment.



Although it is not our practice to back-date agreements, doing so in this case does not prejudice the rights of the Board.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the invoice to support the Palm Beach County Education Commission, Inc., effective from July 1, 2011 to June 30, 2012.

G1 - 2011-2012 Federal Entitlement Grants

The following nine (9) items are all Federal Entitlement Grants from the Department of Education, offered to the School District for specific services for certain groups of students, schools, and staff for academic achievement and learning services.  Each grant specifies to whom the services are delivered and how funding must be expended.  Federal Grant dollars change from year to year, as well as specifications within the grants.  If the grant application is not written, submitted and accepted, the funds are forfeited.



We would like to make the Board aware of the goals, services, and deliverables of the following nine grants:



1. Title I:   Improving the Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged





  • Amount awarded based on State formula which includes number of economically disadvantaged students.

  • Purpose is to increase the academic achievement of students who are economically disadvantaged and provide additional (above regular allocation) programming for academics including additional tutorial, family involvement, Pre-K, and academic capacity coaching for targeted schools.

  • Required to "set aside" a portion of funding of the total Title I grant to particular areas:  highly qualified staff development, family involvement, choice and Supplemental Educational Services, Homeless, Neglected, and Non-Public School Students (34% of Title I total).


2. Title I (C): Migrant Education





  • Amount awarded based on the number of identified migrant students that enter the county.

  • Purpose is to identify, recruit, and provide academic and social services for eligible Migrant families to increase proficiency and increase family involvement for migrant students


3. Title I (D):  Neglected and Delinquent Youth Services





  • Amount awarded based on the number of programs and identified students at at-risk sites.

  • Purpose is to increase academic achievement of students enrolled that are considered neglected, delinquent, and at risk.  Services include transition programs, tutorials, and additional technological support for these students.


4. Title II: Teacher and Principal Training and Recruitment





  • Amount awarded based on State discretionary funds.

  • Purpose is to promote recruitment and retention activities; provide capacity building in academic areas to schools (through area offices); provide curriculum and data support (through Learning Team Facilitators, EDW professional development and support staff)

  • Required to "set aside" a portion of funding to private school equitable services (6%)


5. Title III:  Student Achievement of English Language Learners (ELL)





  • Amount awarded based on the number of ELL's served by the District

  • Purpose is to increase ELL language proficiency and academic achievement (meet Federal Annual Measurable Achievement Objectives - AMAO's) as well as increase family involvement, guidance services and ELL teacher capacity.


6. IDEA:  Individual  with Disabilities Education Act: Entitlement and IDEA PK Entitlement





  • Amount awarded based on a Base State Allocation with additional funding given for county-wide-size and population, number of free-reduced lunch students (survey 2), and number of private school students.

  • Purpose is to identify students with disabilities and provide for individualized academic and support services such as: specified learning plans, additional teacher support, additional hardware/software, paraprofessionals, interpreters, materials, supplies, substitute teachers, evaluations, counseling, occupational and physical therapy, psychological services, audiology and other medically related services.

  • Required to 'set-aside' a portion of funding to non-public school students (3.71%) $ 1,318,005; pre-school students (1.23 %) $11,410, and charter school students (formula) $2,300,000.


7. SEDNET:  Students with Emotional/Behavior Disabilities Network





  • Amount awarded based on State discretionary funding within the IDEA Act.

  • Purpose is to promote a high quality system of care for children who have severe emotional behavioral disturbance, access to mental health treatment and residential services when needed; link community services to families, provide training;  coordinates implementation of school-based mental health services for students.  This includes the staffing of a Project Coordinator, substitute teachers, extra duty days, and travel for personnel.


8. FIN:   Florida Inclusion Network





  • Amount awarded based upon State discretionary funding within the IDEA Act.

  • Purpose is to train teachers in effective instructional inclusive practices for ESE students and increase the number of schools that utilize the model.  This includes the staffing of a project coordinator and resource teacher.


9. FDLRS:  Florida Diagnostic and Learning Resources System





  • Amount awarded based upon State discretionary funding within the IDEA Act.

  • Purpose is to provide Child Find Services (identification and evaluation of youth) Human Resource Development and effective implementation of IDEA as well as parent services and technology for students.


Complete copies of each of the above grants will be available in the Board Office for your viewing.  These grants were written and submitted to FDOE due to a short timeline.  They can be amended throughout the year due to need.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the acceptance of each of the Federal Entitlement Grants listed and authorize the superintendent to sign all documents related to the grant which are required for implementation for the FY12 school year.

P2 Approve the award of Request for Proposal, RFP 11C-002T District Wide Filter Replacement Services/Preventive Maintenance Air Handler Services as revised. The recommendation was originally presented to the School Board for award on June 15, 2011. The School Board rejected the recommendation and requested that staff work to reduce costs. As a result of the School Board's request, a reduction of $250,000 over the five (5) year term has been achieved.  





  • The Evaluation Committee consisted of District personnel including a representative from the Office of Diversity in Business Practices.  The Committee reviewed the proposals and evaluated them based on the criteria outlined in the RFP.  The criteria included: Experience and Qualification of the firm, Qualification of proposer's staff, Approach/Methodology, Time of Completion, Cost of Services, and Minority/Women Business Participation.

  • After discussing each of the responder's proposals, the Committee ranked Precision Air Systems, Inc highest with 85 points.  Precision Air Systems, Inc is a minority owned business; however, they have exceeded the School District of Palm Beach County guidelines and have graduated from the program.

  • Services to be provided include but are not limited to



    • District Wide Filter Replacement Services (estimated at 117,825 filters per year)

    • Preventive Maintenance Air Handler (AHU) Services with each visit

    • Quarterly inspections of damper blades and damper actuator motors

    • Semi-annual cleaning of inlet guide vanes, lubrication of mechanism on guide vanes and inspection of variable frequency drive for air moving equipment.

    • Annual cleaning of AHU coils according to manufacturer's specifications and recommendations

    • A dedicated staff of  technicians to service the District

    • Coordination with schools to establish after school hours service calls

    • A dedicated storage facility to handle bulk capacity of filters committed to the District

    • Disposal of used filters following all Federal, State and Local Codes for such disposal

    • Monthly  reports regarding equipment deficiencies


  • An evaluation was conducted to identify a cost comparison of an in-house model versus an outsourcing model for this service. The evaluation concluded that bulk purchase of filters by the District, using state contract pricing, was $728,408 and district labor rates, including overhead, for job code 83660 HVAC Repair Technician and job code 84310 Trades Helper was $1,648,360 $1,319,560 for a total annual cost of $2,375,768 $2,042,968.54 compared to Precision's annual cost of $1,392,094.

  • This revised action will be posted for 72 hours after Board Award.

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the contract for District Wide Filter Replacement Services / Preventive Maintenance Air Handler Services for the period September 8, 2011 through September 7, 2014 with the option to renew for one additional two (2) year period with Precision Air Systems, Inc., the highest scored, responsive, responsible proposer, based on the criteria outlined in the RFP, at the reduced rate offered by the vendor.
