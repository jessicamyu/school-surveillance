DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified.  Such assignment typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field. Those new hires who are offered jobs as an Out-of-Field, and are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements/tests prior to the end of May.

 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate.  Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary or ESE.  Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements. This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification. It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track.  Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014 include the following statement, “Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment.”

The Department of Professional Development receives thousands of requests for placement of Pre-Service students annually from varying colleges and universities.
