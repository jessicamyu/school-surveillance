The Smaller Learning Communities (SLC) grant is a five-year federal grant project for six of our largest and neediest high schools.  The project will reorganize each of the participating large public high schools into a set of smaller, student-centered learning communities of teachers and students, organized around rigorous academics and relevant career themes, and built upon quality personal relationships among students, teachers, and families.  The Smaller Learning Communities initiative will create a college-bound culture in each school to ensure that all students achieve at high levels and graduate with the knowledge and skills necessary to succeed in postsecondary education and careers. The grant supports the full-time position of a College Bound Coach to be assigned to each of the participating schools.



 



Participating Schools:



 



Atlantic High School



Glades Central High School



Lake Worth High School



Palm Beach Gardens High School



Palm Beach Lakes High School



Santaluces High School



 



The project’s measurable outcomes for all students in each participating high school focus on 1) improving academic achievement, 2) increasing the rigor and relevance in the curriculum, 3) personalizing the learning environment, and 4) creating a college-bound culture in each school to prepare all students for college and careers.  



 



An external evaluator, as mandated by the Grant, will work with the project manager to assess the project’s effectiveness with respect to the identified measures and implemented changes for school improvement.  A consultant contract will be developed to evaluate the grant as part of the SLC grant implementation requirement.  DKH Consulting Services, Inc. will be contracted to conduct the required evaluation process in the amount of $75,000 each year for the life of the grant.



 



A copy of the SLC grant application is available in the Board Minutes Office.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Smaller Learning Communities competitive grant awarded by the United States Department of Education in the amount of $7,627,073.00  effective July 10, 2008 through July 09, 2013, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of grant activities.

Financial Impact:

The financial impact of the Special Revenue-Other Funds Budget is an increase of $7,627,073.00 over the next five years, including FY2009, FY2010, FY2011, FY2012, and FY2013.  All grant related expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue.
