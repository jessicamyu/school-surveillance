G1-Professional Development Grant for Boynton Beach High School
Meeting
Jan 21, 2009 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

This grant is designed to increase student achievement in Math by increasing the content knowledge and instructional strategies of  the school's math teachers. The goal is to build capacity of the math department at Boynton Beach High School and to provide a solid foundation on which the school can continue to build in the future.



The professional development provided will facilitate immediate results as well as long term improvement.   The goals of the grant will be achieved by using professional development service providers from the Australian United States Services in Education, Inc. (AUSSIE).  AUSSIE will provide on site professional development for all the Math teachers at Boynton Beach High School. This training consists of customized, job-embedded, research-based professional development. The monies provided by this grant will be used pay AUSSIE for their services.  The funding for the professional services will be provided from two grant sources: the Smaller Learning Community (SLC) Grant (previously approved by the Board) and a grant from the Mary and Robert Pew Educational Fund.



AUSSIE will provide 18 days of on-site professional development at the school.  The AUSSIE Team will visit Boynton Beach High School approximately 5 times during the year and spend a minimum of 3 days during each visit. The first visit will provide the opportunity for the team to observe and then, working with teachers and administrators, to develop and begin the implementation of a plan for improving the Math instruction at Boynton Beach High School.



The plan will support instructional practices that provide a balance between teacher direction and student input, that blend a variety of instructional approaches, and that recognize that students are active learners. The plan will also:





  • target student achievement by improving instructional practices

  • use data to both customize and monitor the professional development plan

  • be embedded in the everyday practice of the participants

  • build sustainability by providing ongoing, professional learning opportunities for all members of the school community

  • reflect current research


Boynton Beach High School must furnish the funders a final report due by July 30, 2009. Information to be provided includes the financial statement, a narrative account of what was accomplished by the expenditure of funds and a report on the retention of  principals and teachers.



The use of ongoing assessments to measure what students know and to track their progress over time, and the use of the data to differentiate instruction accordingly, will be the evaluative piece of the project

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Professional Development Grant funded by the Pew Foundation in the amount of $20,000.00 for Boynton Beach High School effective January 22, 2009-June 30,2009 and $16,000 from the previously approved Smaller Learning Community Grant and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

This student has been residentially placed by the Agency for Persons with Disabilities (APD).  The district pays only educational costs.  APD places students in residential facilities when they determine the student's behaviors at home and in the community are so severe as to render the student, the family, and the community at risk.



Due to the severity of the student's needs, the IEP team agreed that the student could  be educated in the facility chosen by APD.  This student requires extensive educational and behavioral services. 



The student has been appropriately identified as a student with disabilities by the School District of Palm Beach County in compliance with state and local policies and regulations.  Prior to entering the residential facility the student  had been attending Jupiter Middle School in a self-contained Special Education classroom.



The district will pay the educational costs for those days the student is physically present and in attendance at the residential facility.  The individual cost for educational services is not more than $11,548.60.  The total cost to the school District is $11,548.60 if the student remains in the facility through the end of the school year.  The total FTE to be earned by this student during the FY08 school year is $20,458.



The student will remain in the facility until such time that an APD team, with school district participation, determines that the student is stabilized to the point where the student is no longer a danger to self or others.



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in this particular case due to the fact that a bed had become available.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.



This confirming cooperative agreement is in compliance with State Board Rule 6A.6-0361, "Special Programs Contractual Agreements with Non-Public Schools."

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the confirming cooperative agreement with Carlton Palms Educational Center for not more than $11,548.60 for the educational costs of one student with disabilities from December 15, 2008 through June 30, 2009 and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to these agreements.

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Mavericks High, D. Wades School of South Palm Beach County ("Mavericks of Central PBC") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County on August 1, 2009.



The application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A"). 



The instrument and the application process were explained to the applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop that was held on May 30, 2008 (see Exhibit "B").



The District Reviewers were trained on the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Application Process on May 16, 2008 (see Exhibit "C").



The Reviewers evaluated the 21 sections of the written application and identified deficiencies.  Six of the 21 sections were rated "Partially Meets the Standard," meaning the responses addressed most of the criteria, but lacked meaningful detail and required important additional information (see Exhibit "D").



The responses to each section that did not meet the stated criteria served as the basis of inquiry for the interview.  Please refer to Step 5:  Applicant Interview (see Exhibit "E").



Based on the written application and the interview, this application has significant weaknesses in six sections.  Step 6, second paragraph in the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process states, "The sponsoring board will only approve applicants that demonstrate quality in all components of the application process. An application cannot have significant weaknesses in some components of the application and still be judged to have met the standard for approval," (see Exhibit "F").  Each applicant received communication that outlined the proposed recommendation, the evaluator's second review following the interview, summary of the interview, and was granted the option to voluntarily withdraw the application or allow the application to be denied by the School Board (see Exhibit "G").



Pursuant to F.S.1002.33 (5)(c):
"An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than 30 calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ."



A copy of Mavericks of South PBC's application is in the Board Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of Mavericks High, D. Wade's School of South Palm Beach County and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Mavericks High, D. Wades School of Central Palm Beach County ("Mavericks of Central PBC") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County on August 1, 2009.



The application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A"). 



The instrument and the application process were explained to the applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop that was held on May 30, 2008 (see Exhibit "B").



The District Reviewers were trained on the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Application Process on May 16, 2008 (see Exhibit "C").



The Reviewers evaluated the 21 sections of the written application and identified deficiencies.  Six of the 21 sections were rated "Partially Meets the Standard," meaning the responses addressed most of the criteria, but lacked meaningful detail and required important additional information (see Exhibit "D").



The responses to each section that did not meet the stated criteria served as the basis of inquiry for the interview.  Please refer to Step 5:  Applicant Interview (see Exhibit "E").



Based on the written application and the interview, this application has significant weaknesses in six sections.  Step 6, second paragraph in the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process states, "The sponsoring board will only approve applicants that demonstrate quality in all components of the application process. An application cannot have significant weaknesses in some components of the application and still be judged to have met the standard for approval," (see Exhibit "F").  Each applicant received communication that outlined the proposed recommendation, the evaluator's second review following the interview, summary of the interview, and was granted the option to voluntarily withdraw the application or allow the application to be denied by the School Board (see Exhibit "G").



Pursuant to F.S.1002.33 (5)(c):
"An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than 30 calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ."



A copy of Mavericks of Central PBC's application is in the Board Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of Mavericks High, D. Wade's School of Central Palm Beach County and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

An agreement between the School District of Palm Beach County and Alexa Lee was approved in the amount of $9,802.  This is a request to amend the current agreement to increase the scope of Ms. Lee's job responsibilities and therefore increase her hours from 368 hours provided to 1,240 hours provided (an increase of 872 hours).  This increase in the number of hours equates to an increase of $24,479.60, bring the total of her services to $34,282.00. Additionally, an increase of $2,200 for travel is included in this amendment. (As a part of this grant, the funder requires attendance at out-of-county, grant-related conferences.)



The contract is funded entirely by a U.S. Department of Education Grant to Reduce Alcohol Abuse, approved by the School Board on February 20, 2008. Per the direction of the USDOE, two Program Coordinators are funded to provide direct services to the schools as they implement a multitude of alcohol prevention activities. This provider will serve as one of these Program Coordinators.



The provider's responsibilities include:





  • coordinate staff training and program delivery of the grant-required, science-based curriculum in conjunction with Hanley Center

  • assist in the development of the schools' Youth Leadership Councils and their anti-alcohol social marketing campaigns

  • assist in the development of the Strategic Planning Committees and their strategic plans

  • develop implementation guides and related documents

  • ensure compliance with grant requirements, including the use of School Based Teams and FACE IT

  • coordinate the implementation of community/school activities

  • ensure fulfillment of all grant-required evaluation activities. 


Alexa Lee will spearhead the schools' efforts as they coordinate a youth-led anti-alcohol social marketing campaign and collaborate with the community to change attitudes and behaviors around teen alcohol use. These activities will ensure success of the grant-defined goals, which are:
       *  Increase the number of youth who believe that alcohol is harmful to their health;
       *  Increase the number of youth who disapprove in alcohol use; and
       *  Decrease the number of youth who binge drink.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the amendment to the agreement with Alexa Lee for an additional  $24,479.60 effective June 22, 2009 through June 30, 2009 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all needed documents.
