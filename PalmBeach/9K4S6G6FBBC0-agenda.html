Council For Exceptional Children Educator Awards
Meeting
Oct 21, 2009 - School Board Meeting
Category

Action (Consent)


The Council for Exceptional Children is the largest international professional organization dedicated to improving the educational success of individuals with disabilities and/or gifts and talents. Local Chapter #200 is proud to honor several outstanding Palm Beach School District educators and administrators for their contributions to the education of exceptional students.



Revia Lee has served as Principal of Royal Palm School for 19 years and in the Palm Beach School District for 35 years.  Royal Palm School is one of Palm Beach School District's two special day schools serving ESE students.   At Royal Palm School there are over 600 ESE students from Pre-K through 22 years of age.  Mrs. Lee is a master at managing the teachers, therapists, specialists, and assistants required  to care for the specialized needs of the student population while continuing to promote a welcoming environment where parents, staff, business partners and students are a part of the decision making team.   Mrs. Lee will dedicate her newly rebuilt school this winter and then retire for some well deserved rest and relaxation.  Council for Exceptional Children is proud to present the Principal of the Year Award to Revia Lee.



Janet Stanton has served as a general education teacher for the past 24 years.   Each year she has educated ESE students within her general education setting.  Her dedication to ESE students is evident through her actions and words.  In Janet's words, "I encourage all my students to self advocate. I offer a setting where an ESE student can blend into the general population. I try to be flexible in all aspects of my teaching style and structure and to make accommodations as needed."  Council for Exceptional Children is proud to present the General Educator of the Year Award to Janet Stanton.



Donna Szwed has served as a paraprofessional in Palm Beach School District for the past 14 years servicing children from PreK through the 12th grade. She has committed herself to working with students including those who have Intellectual Disabilities, Emotional/ Behavioral Disabilities, Autism Spectrum Disorder and Physical Disabilities.  In Donna's words, "I truly love working with our ESE students. To me, they are angels. I always see the best in students and do not judge, no matter who they are or how severe their disability may be. I strongly believe that students should feel valued and have a sense of self worth, despite any deficits they may have." Council for Exceptional Children is proud to present the Paraprofessional of the Year Award to Donna Szwed.



Donna Marie Ott currently serves as the Technology Instructor at Royal Palm School.  Donna Ott supports the teachers with technology related needs and the 600 ESE students housed at Royal Palm School. Donna is totally committed to raising the academic bar through technology for the student's of Royal Palm School.  In Donna's words, "I am most proud of the work I have done with the Intellectually Disabled and the Physically Disabled students. Watching one of our graduates use an adaptive joystick and switch to create his first picture when he was in high school, finding the right technology so that a child can joyfully make his own choices independently and correctly, or watching a kindergarten student color the Statue of Liberty on her computer while the rest of the class colors using both limbs is truly phenomenal." Council for Exceptional Children is proud to present the ESE Teacher of the Year Award to Donna Ott.

RE-1 Seminole Ridge Community High School's Lake Deed and Access Easement
Meeting
Oct 21, 2009 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

1.  On May 19, 2004 the School Board approved the Interlocal Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and the Seminole Improvement  District for dedication and perpetual maintenance of the surface management lake located on Seminole Ridge Community High School's campus.(See Exhibit "A")  The essential elements of the Agreement were:





  • In order to meet Surface Water Management standards for Seminole Ridge Community High School, the School District was required to construct a lake to be maintained exclusively for adequate drainage.   

  • Seminole Improvement District agreed to accept ownership and perpetual maintenance of the surface water management lake and associated land. Seminole Improvement District's maintenance of the lake will result in a recurring cost savings to the School District.

  • The School District agreed to provide Seminole Improvement District with an access easement over School Board property to allow for access to the lake for maintenance purposes.(See Exhibit "B") 

  • Seminole Improvement District also desired to make changes to the off-site waste water facilities and certain on-site potable water facilities.  The changes did not impact the service or schedule of water and sewer to serve the School.

  • The School District made the changes and Seminole Improvement District agreed to reimburse the School District for the total cost of $90,532.00.


2. Staff is seeking approval of this item due to the five year gap between the approval of the Interlocal Agreement and fulfillment of the School District's obligation to convey the land interests to the Seminole Improvement District.  The Seminole Improvement District will reimburse the School District for the $90,532.00 associated with water and sewer improvements when the School District delivers the deed and easement.       



                



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Quit Claim Deed and Access Easement from the School Board of Palm Beach County to Seminole Improvement District for the dedicaton and perpetual maintenance of the surface management lake; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

PM-4 Pahokee High School Stadium Project Modifications
Meeting
Oct 21, 2009 - School Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

This Project Modification contains the following items:



Item #1







  • CCD #1 in the amount of $87,997.00.




  • Description:  Upgrade track surface from latex to polyurethane.




  • Cause:  School District Requested




  • Justification:  The latex surface installed on high school tracks has had high maintenance costs.  The polyurethane surface has a higher initial cost, but lower maintenance costs.  The overall life cycle costs are slightly higher, but the performance characteristics of the polyurethane surface are better.  Considering both cost and performance, the polyurethane was determined to be the best surface. 


Item #2







  • CCD #2 in the amount of $36,254.00




  • Description:  Provide a dedicated 8" line water line for fire protection.




  • Cause:  Outside Agency




  • Justification:  The design at GMP had one line coming off the water main feeding both domestic water and fire protection.  The Palm Beach County Fire District requested a dedicated 8" line for fire protection.


Item #3







  • CCD #3 in the amount of $78,122.00




  • Description:  Add middle aisle to home side bleachers.  Other changes included deleting powder coating of steel under the bleachers and press box modifications. 




  • Cause:  School District Requested.




  • Justification:  The Building Department approved a middle aisle variance for the Boca Raton HS stadium.  The Architect incorrectly assumed the same variance would be approved for the Pahokee stadium.


Item #4







  • CCD #4 in the amount of $98,605.00




  • Description:  Revise location and depth of irrigation well and add a second well.




  • Cause:  Unforeseen Condition




  • Justification:  The test well indicated that a second well was required to meet the volume requirement.  In addition, the water quality was not acceptable for Bermuda grass.  Two additional test wells were drilled to find a location that had acceptable water for Bermuda grass.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board ratify Project Modifications in the amount of $300,978.00 to W.G. Mills, Inc. for the Pahokee High School Stadium Project #1771-8275, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.
