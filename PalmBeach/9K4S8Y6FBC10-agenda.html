FM1 - Modernization of Gove Elementary

Action (Consent)

1)      QSCB Financing - The construction of Galaxy Elem and Gove Elem will be funded from the Qualified School Construction Bonds (QSCBs).



2)      QSCB Deadlines - The QSCBs must be sold prior to October 31, 2010 to avoid the market complications that will take place in November and December. Prior to the sale, the project list must be finalized, bond ratings obtained, offering statements prepared, and 7-10 days of marketing/advertising must take place to ensure a successful sale.



3)      District Charter Status - As the District no longer enjoys a charter district status, the projects must be approved by the Department of Education (DOE).  The School Board must approve each of the following actions prior to submittal to the DOE.



a)      School Size – The Educational Plant Survey revision must be approved by the DOE indicating that the school will be renovated based on student enrollment.  The number of classrooms allowed by the DOE is largely determined based on class size reduction.  The DOE will look at the number of k-3 students divided by 18 and students in grades 4-5 divided by 22 to calculate the maximum number of classrooms.  In the case of Gove, there will be 8 less classrooms than would have been allowed with our charter district status.



b)      Renovate vs Replace - A Castaldi analysis must be prepared and then approved by the DOE.  The Castaldi compares the costs of replacement versus renovation.  The DOE will only approve the most cost effective method which may be renovating the newer buildings and replacing the older buildings.



c)       Final DOE Approval – Another revision to the Educational Plant Survey will be prepared to indicate what portion of the school will be replaced and what will be renovated.  This must be completed prior to the sale of the QSCBs.



4)      K-5 vs K-6 Center - A k-5 center is recommended as there is additional space at Lake Shore Middle to accommodate the 6th graders.  If a k-6 center is submitted to DOE, staff anticipates it could be denied based on the capacity available at Lake Shore Middle. 



5)      Maximum Cost per Student Station - The planned construction will have to comply with the maximum cost per student station.



6)      Remaining Glades Area Projects - As the other project(s) in the Glades area will be funded with residual COP (Certificates of Participation) revenues, the same DOE approvals will be required.  Since QSCBs will not be used for the remaining project(s), the timing, from a financial standpoint, is not as critical, therefore a decision regarding Glade View and Rosenwald can postponed to a future meeting.



Attachments: The first proposed revision to the Ed Plant Survey and a comparison of the current and proposed survey is attached.  A sample Castaldi analysis for Gove is available online at: http://www.palmbeachschools.org/planning/SchoolConcurrency/GladesMP.asp



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to sign and approve all necessary documents to gain approval from the Department of Education for the modernization of Gove Elementary School.
