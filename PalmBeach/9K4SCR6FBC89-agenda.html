RE1 New Suncoast High School Road Improvements License Agreement
Meeting
Mar 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

1.  The FY 2009-2013 Five Year Plan includes the construction of a New Suncoast High School, scheduled for opening in August, 2010.



2.   Due to the construction of the New Suncoast High School certain road improvements on Congress Avenue, 13th Street and Jake Lane are required to meet Palm Beach County's and the City of Riviera Beach's Traffic Standards.  (See Exhibit "A")



3.  Acquisition of the Temporary Use Parcels is needed to successfully complete the required road improvements. 



4.  Four owners have already agreed to allow the School District to utilize a portion of their property to facilitate the required road improvements. (See Exhibit "B")



5.  The fifth owner has agreed to allow the School District to utilize a portion of his property. (See Exhibit "B")



6.  The appraised value of the fifth Temporary Use Parcel is $250.00



7.  The agreed upon License Agreement cost for the fifth Temporary Use Parcel is $250.00

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the License Agreement for the Temporary Use Parcel needed for construction of certain road improvements for the New Suncoast High School; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore (moorej@palmbeachschools.org)
Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeachschools.org)

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the Capital Project's Fund Budget is $250.00

Subject
PM1 Carver Middle School Project Modification
Meeting
Mar 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

This Project Modification contains the following item:







  • Change Order Proposal Request (COPR) #1 credit in the amount of ($1,056,003.41)




  • Description: Final Contract reconciliation.






    • Sales Tax is excess of guaranteed amount  ($1,702.28)




    • Direct Material Purchase (DMP) Deduct  ($931,066.49)




    • Master Control Insurance Program (MCIP) Deduct  ($123,234.64)




  • Cause: School District Requested




  • Justification:  Funds are deducted from the Construction Manager (CM) Agreement that the School District uses for purchasing materials and equipment directly from suppliers.  These purchases are tax free.  The CM Agreement is also adjusted to return sales tax savings in excess of the guaranteed amount to the School District.  Insurance payments to the MCIP by the CM and its subcontractors are also deducted from the CM Agreement amount.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve Project Modifications credit in the amount of ($1,056,003.41) from Horus Construction Services, Inc. for the Carver Middle School Classroom Addition Project #2041-8307 and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.

Contact:

Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is a credit of ($1,056,003.41) from Horus Construction Services Agreement and a credit of ($1,702.28) to the Project Budget.  After this credit the Project Budget needs an additional $74,800.00 to pay a portion of the MCIP premium that was not paid through CM and Subcontractor deducts.



The source of funds will be Capital Contingency.
