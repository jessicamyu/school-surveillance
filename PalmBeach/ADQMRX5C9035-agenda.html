PIR1 Interlocal Agreement with the City of Delray Beach for Antennas and Equipment on Existing Communication Towers
Meeting
Oct 19, 2016 - 1. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

  1. The proposed Agreement will allow the City of Delray Beach access to District T.E.N. communication towers located at Banyan Creek Elementary School, Orchard View Elementary School and Carver Community Middle School for the placement of water metering equipment. Additional school locations may be approved by school board staff, on a case by case basis. This action will allow the City to perform its electronic water metering project.  This enhancement will allow the City a more immediate and accurate readings of water usage for School Board facilities as well as for other City customers.  
  2. The installation of the equipment will be at no cost to the School Board and will be inspected twice each year, with an inspection report to be sent to the Station Manager of TEN.  Prior to any equipment installation, the City will submit plans to be reviewed and approved by TEN Station Manager as well as the School Board’s Building Code Services Department.  No installation of equipment can occur until a permit is issued for the work.
  3. Equipment installation, maintenance and repair shall take place with the consent of TEN Station Manager and the School Board Building Official.  Antenna and associated equipment shall comply with all Federal Communications Commission requirements.  If City equipment causes interference of other performance issues with School Board equipment, the City shall cease using the equipment until a solution is found.
  4. The City will be responsible for the reimbursement costs of electrical service to its equipment at each location at a rate of $10.00 per month per location. 
  5. The Agreement has an initial term of five (5) years and, with mutual consent, may be renewed for four (4) five-year terms.

PM1 Business Partnership Agreement - Perry J. Cohen Foundation - Created Wetlands Habitat and Outdoor Classroom Project
Meeting
Oct 19, 2016 - 1. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

Jupiter Community High School, the Jupiter Environmental Research & Field Studies Academy and the Partnership for Environmental Education have been working for a number of years to transform the existing storm water retention pond at the front of Jupiter High School into a created wetlands habitat and outdoor classroom (the “Project”).  The Project envisions native plant materials, educational information and a boardwalk meeting Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) standards, providing an opportunity for hands-on education in an attractive and ecologically productive landscape.

 

The Perry J. Cohen Foundation is a registered not for profit 501(c)(3) organization devoted to the advancement of Boating Safety Education, Environmental, Marine, & Wildlife Education & Preservation, Teenage Entrepreneurship, and The Arts. The Foundation creates awareness and education for all individuals centered on the safe enjoyment of recreational boating, fishing, and water sports, facilitating a deeper connection between America’s youth and proper boating safety standards, along with an annual scholarship program that recognizes deserving students who plan to further their education in the Marine Science and Marine Wildlife fields.  In support of these programs and the preservation of our coastlines, the Foundation has committed to contribute and raise funds to complete the Project.

 

In keeping with School Board Policy 7.03 “Naming of School District Facilities and Dedicating Areas of School District Facilities or Grounds”, upon completion the Project is to be named the “Perry J. Cohen Wetlands Laboratory at Jupiter High School.”

 

The project will comply with Board Policy 7.26 “Facilities or Grounds Modifications Funded by Internal Accounts or Donations”.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the Member Service Agreement with Envision Payment Solutions, Inc. for the collection of funds from returned checks.  This Agreement authorizes Envision Payment Solutions, Inc. to manage the collection process for any check that is returned for non-payment on any District or School bank account.

 

The use of a collection service was recommended by the Audit Committee to remove the process from schools and encourage acceptance of checks.  This should reduce the amount of cash handling in schools. 

 

Upon implementation, Wells Fargo will deliver all returned checks to Envision Payment Solutions, Inc. and Envision Payment Solutions, Inc. will follow procedures as outlined in the attached Collection Procedures. This service will allow the District to recoup the losses previously encountered from returned checks and will allow Parents/Guardians and others the opportunity to discreetly pay for any inadvertently returned item.  To ease that process, Envision has partnered with a local bank with many locations in Palm Beach County which will accept those payments.

 

Schools will receive automatic, guaranteed reimbursement for all eligible returned checks on a bi-monthly basis via ACH payment processing. Eligible returned checks are $500 or less, drawn on a US financial institution, and received no less than 25 days of the date the item was written.  Envision will attempt collection for all other items, but payment will be sent to the school or department when the collection is successful.

 

Envision Payment Solutions, Inc. offers a robust reporting system that will allow schools to easily monitor the collection process.  In addition, District staff can easily monitor the collection process at a district-wide level to ensure contract compliance.

 

This contract has been fully vetted by our Risk Management Department and our Office of General Counsel.  Based on their recommendation, substantial insurance requirements have been included in the Agreement, including the following:

 

Errors and Omissions Insurance – minimum of $1,000,000 coverage

Cyber Liability Insurance - $1,000,000 per occurrence

Fidelity/Dishonesty Insurance for employer and third party coverage - $250,000

 

All referenced policies have been received and reviewed by our Risk Management department.  This level of insurance coverage for collection services is well beyond the industry norm so the School District will pay an annual fee of $2,814 for the contract.

 

This purchase does not require competition since the amount of the contract is less than the amount required by Board Policy 6.14, Rule 6A-1.012 Florida Administrative Code and section 1010.04 Florida Statutes.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District has had a partnership with Publix to provide a work experience (Community Based Vocational Education) site for ESE students in Palm Beach County Schools.  The program has allowed students in our secondary CBVE programs the opportunity to work in job training experiences at Publix grocery stores throughout the county in order to develop skills for post-secondary readiness according to their Transition IEP goals. Publix enters into partnerships with schools to help students with emotional, mental or physical challenges that have compromised their readiness for employment.  This vocational training is designed to benefit participating students and not Publix's operations, therefore, students are not paid for participating in the training.  The School District of Palm Beach County provides adequate supervision including having a job coach accompany the students to the workplace daily for the first week and at least once a week thereafter.  The program has no costs attached.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board receive and file the Internal Audit Report-Special Review of Money Collections Records at Rolling Green Elementary (Report No. 2016-03).

DESCRIPTION:

During the 2015 Internal Funds Audit of Rolling Green Elementary, the Office of Inspector General (OIG) noted irregularities related to the school’s money collections. Subsequently, the OIG expanded the scope to review the money collection process at the school.  Specifically, the OIG obtained and analyzed all the available money collections records and documentation for the FY 2015 which included: (1) original Monies Collected Records (MCR) filed with the official accounting records maintained by the school treasurer, (2) bank deposit records, (3) Drop-safe Log, (4) Internal Funds General Ledger, and (5) the yellow copies of MCRs and official receipts retained by activity sponsors.  Additionally, the OIG interviewed some of the school staff.  The special review covered the period of July 1, 2014 through June 30, 2015.

 

The following conclusions resulted from the special review.

  1. Funds in the amount of $340.06 were not accounted for, due to the funds not being deposited into the bank or recorded in the Internal Funds General Ledger, or an unexplained cash shortage of funds. 
  2.  Noncompliance with internal control procedures due to:

a) The failure to record money collections totaling $16,005 on Drop-safe logs or to place in the drop-safe (though the monies were recorded in the General Ledger);

b) the school treasurer's failure to have another school employee accompany and verify the treasurer's removal of monies from the drop-safe, resulting in only the treasurer's initials on the Drop-safe Log: and,

c) instructing staff to turn-in all yellow copies of Monies Collected Reports (MCR) to the school treasurer in the school's 2014-15 Checkout Procedures, contrary to the District Procedures requiring the documents to be submitted to the school secretary for filing and future references at year end.
