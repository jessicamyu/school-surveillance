AC1 - Berkshire Elementary Walking Trail - Interlocal agreement
Meeting
Jul 18, 2012 - School Board Meeting
Category

Discussion
In December of 2009, The School District of Palm Beach County was awarded a $360,000 Healthy Kids, Healthy Communities (HKHC) grant from the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation (RWJF) to make policy and environmental changes to combat obesity in the Lake Worth, Greenacres, and Palm Springs communities. To meet this directive, the HKHC coalition, which is comprised of thirty community stakeholders and organizations, developed goals to increase opportunities for physical activity and to improve access to affordable healthy foods.   





In November, 2011, The Palm Beach County Office of Community Revitalization granted an additional $20,000 to the Governor's Council for Community Health Partnership (GCCHP) to assist with funding to install a walking trail on the campus of Berkshire Elementary School. The walking trail is an obesity prevention amenity to support HKHC's second goal of developing interlocal agreements to open a portion of school grounds to the public on weekends and holidays. The walking trail at Berkshire Elementary School will provide community residents an opportunity to become physically active in an area that is currently without a park, and within walking distance of the school. The Palm Beach County Parks and Recreation Department has agreed to partner with The School Board to support the creation of the walking trail, and serve as a co-sponsor on future grants to support the park space. The Office of Community Revitalization has requested that the $20,000 for the walking trail go directly to the School Board rather than going through the GCCHP, as was originally intended. Funding has been secured from the Quantum Foundation to fence off the open school area, including the walking trail from the rest of Berkshire's campus.





As a community we receive many benefits of recreation and parks.  Physical activity reduces risk for obesity, diabetes, many cancers, osteoporosis, chronic diseases, and high blood pressure. It boosts your immune system, increases life expectancy, decreases mental health disorders and depression.  Physical activity reduces stress and improves quality of life. Recreation has been shown to strengthen communities, reduce crime, encourages volunteerism, promotes stewardship and social bonds.  Recreation has also been shown to build cultural diversity and harmony, supports individuals with disabilities, supports seniors and youth.  It builds self esteem and deters negative behaviors.  (California State Parks, 2005)





A 2009 Harris poll of parents commissioned by KaBOOM! found that nearly half reported there was no play space or facility within walking distance in their community, yet eight in 10 parents feel it is important that such facilities be within walking distance. (Harris Interactive Assessment on Play Study, 2009) Community design affects access. Neighborhoods without sidewalks, bike paths, and safe walking and biking routes put up barriers to play. Where children used to walk or ride to school, many children now go to school by car. In 1969, 90% of children living within one to five miles from school walked there, while today only 15% from the same group walk to school. The Adults with Disabilities (AWD) Grant is a continuation grant in the amount of $760,481.  South Tech Academy, through the School District of Palm Beach County as Local Education Agency (LEA), has historically been the source of continued support for this grant.  The School District's Department of Adult and Community Education serves as the coordinating department and works with the proper district departments to obtain School Board approval.



The AWD program has been implemented through South Tech Academy since 1983.  This program is intended to improve the quality of life of adults with disabilities.  The program uses a realistic work environment with skilled and certified teachers who provide the training and support needed to make the student successful.  The AWD program is designed to make each student as self-sufficient as possible and for those that qualify, to become employable.



The AWD program combines job skill training with special education instructional methodology which enables the students to gain both employability skills and real world job skills.  The goal is to create self-sufficiency for the disabled by providing training and income.  The funding is used to train and enhance the skills and lives of approximately 600 students.  Each student's Adult Individual Education Plan (AIEP) is closely monitored to assure that AIEP goals are being met, as well as tracking the number of students who obtain competitive employment and non-competitive employment.  Competitive employment is where students interview for positions and non-competitive employment is where students do not interview for positions but are placed in jobs according to their abilities.



The target population focuses on adult individuals who were born with a disability, acquired a disability later in life, and individuals who are 55 years of age or older and have disabilities, who are referred to as senior citizens with disabilities.



There are five liaison agencies: ARC of Palm Beach County, Inc., Habilitation Center for the Handicapped, Jeff Industries, Inc., Palm Beach Habilitation Center, Seagull Industries for the Disabled who partner to provide training.  Each agency promotes the philosophy of challenging students to attain the greatest degree of self-sufficiency in both their work skills and personal life skills.  This funding enables students that are disabled to become employable in the community.

The newest technologies can significantly advance the delivery of education and information to/from students, parents, teachers, administrators, and citizens.  The School District of Palm Beach County (SDPBC) intends to employ these progressive technologies, known as Social Networking, to expand and enhance its educational processes and public engagement.  These platforms, such as Facebook and Twitter, engage stakeholders by "pushing" relevant information to "friends", who wish to engage with the organization. Conversely, web sites are "pull" platforms that require stakeholders to seek out specific websites and information from the plethora of those available.



As an initial step toward District Social Networking, a Pilot Project consisting of a District Facebook site and Twitter account administered by the Public Affairs Department only will be created, maintained and monitored by Public Affairs staff (four Specialists who now write/produce the press releases for schools and departments on the Main Page and Community Page, Chief Public Information Officer, Department Executive Assistant and a member of the Information Technology staff).



What information will be posted on the District's Public Affairs administered Facebook/Twitter/Social Media accounts?  Essentially, the information that currently is the subject of Public Affairs press releases.
·         Student, teachers, administrators and other stakeholders national, regional and state awards and recognitions
·         District accomplishments, such as the Broad Prize, recognition in national publications
·          School/program accomplishments, such as  national Career Academy recognition
·         Upcoming district-wide educational events, such as student involvement in Presidential Debate and academic competitions
·         Hurricane information
·         Information about scholarships, colleges and universities
·         School and district event photos and videos
·         Links to schools' newspapers/newsletters
·         District-wide information, such as Back to School
·         Links to school web pages
·         Graduation information
·         Activities, events, updates, or any information that is pertinent to the entire district



What content will be considered inappropriate and deleted?
·         No graphic, obscene, explicit or racial comments or submissions are permitted, nor do we allow comments that are abusive, hateful or intended to defame anyone or any organization.
·         No solicitations or advertisements. This includes promotion or endorsement of any financial, commercial or non-governmental agency. Similarly, we do not allow attempts to defame or defraud any private, public or   
          non-governmental agency.
·         Details or comments about an ongoing investigation or legal or administrative proceeding that could prejudice the processes or could interfere with an individual's rights or the proceeding will be deleted from this page.
·         Apparent spamming or trolling will be removed and may cause the author(s) to be blocked from the page without notice.
·         No copyrighted or trademarked images or graphics. Imagery posted on  Social Media Sites should be owned by the user.
·         No comments, photos or videos that suggest or encourage illegal activity.
·         No documents of any kind should be posted on District social media sites .
·         Users participate at their own risk, taking personal responsibility for their comments, usernames and any information provided, with an understanding that all information is subject to the Public Records Law. 



The following guidelines have been established and will be posted prominently on the Facebook and Twitter:



This page is intended to provide information to our students, teachers, staff, parents, community members and other stakeholders. Please note that the School District of Palm Beach County does NOT endorse or attest to the accuracy of information, opinions, claims, or advice shared on this page by our fans. Should you require a response from the School District or wish to request services you must visit www.palmbeachschools.org or call (561) 434-8000.
Furthermore, fans agree that postings on the School District of Palm Beach County Facebook page or Twitter account are their own and do not necessarily represent the School Board of Palm Beach County's opinion or position.
Open conversation - not personal issues or complaints about students and/or school personnel. This page is not intended to circumvent regular communication channels for  sharing personal issues and concerns, including personnel matters and student concerns. Comments regarding a personal issue with the District, a school, student or a staff member will be removed.



Be respectful. Personal attacks or comments that are deemed offensive to any member of our community will not be tolerated. Inappropriate remarks and profanity will be removed.
Product sales and political campaign messages are for individual personal Facebook pages or Twitter accounts.   The District Facebook page and Twitter account is for sharing news and information regarding district and school activities, events, and information.



Stay on topic. This is a page dedicated to the education of students of the SDPBC.  Let's keep the conversation related to our postings regarding district and school activities, events and information. Comments are welcome so long as they directly relate to our postings regarding district and school activities, events and information.



Have a question for a specific employee? Contact them directly via their email, phone or written correspondence. Fans should not expect responses to questions or comments posted on this page.



Be aware of the mission. The mission of SDPBC is education. Please be certain that your comments are appropriate for an educational environment and fans of all ages.  This site is a limited or designated public forum reserved for limited communicative purposes.



This is a government owned property and the School District of Palm Beach County's determination is final.  Be factual. Blatantly inaccurate information will not be allowed. False information will be removed.



The District reserves the right to remove postings that are:
>Offensive, hateful, graphic, explicit or racial comments or comments that defame anyone or any organization.
>abusive, obscene or contain profanity
>in violation of any laws or regulations
>fraudulent or misleading



The School District of Palm Beach County reserves the right to remove any fan who posts material that falls into any of the above categories and they will not be allowed to participate .



Under Florida law, the entire content con the District's Facebook page and Twitter account is subject to the public records law. By becoming a fan of the School District of Palm Beach County, your information will be a matter of public records. The District is required to retain this information in accordance with the State of Florida General Records Schedules and the SDPBC Records Retention Schedule. This may include information on your own Facebook page. All comments will be maintained for a minimum of 30 days.



Social networking technologies allow participants to connect and collaborate in a media rich fashion, in near real-time, with individuals and groups; thereby SDPBC can more easily interact with students, parents, educators, and community members.  This Social Networking Pilot, a District Facebook page and Twitter account administered by Public Affairs, supports Policy 1.015 (Parental/Family Involvement) that calls for a collaborative environment in which the parents and families of our students are invited and encouraged to be involved. 



Social Networking is designed for exactly that task. The overarching purpose is to advance student academic performance, and as stated in Policy 1.015, "Student achievement can reach higher levels when parents, schools, and the community work together in a collaborative endeavor to support student learning."  Therefore, the  Pilot will provide notices of SDPBC activities and events to constituents and provide information regarding SDPBC stakeholders as required in Policy 2.04 Public Information.



The information gathered during this pilot will be integrated into the forth coming Social Networking Policy.
