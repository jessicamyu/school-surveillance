  • Lake Worth Drainage District (LWDD) is requesting the School Board grant an exclusive easement for +/- .043 acres, a portion of West Palm Beach Area Elementary School (O6-D), for the purpose of accessing and maintaining Canal E-3 (See Exhibit "A"), (1).






  • LWDD is also requesting the School Board grant an exclusive easement for +/  .28 acres, a portion of West Palm Beach Area Elementary School (O6-D), for the purpose of accessing and maintaining Canal L-2 (See Exhibit "A"), (2).






  • LWDD will issue a permit to connect the 06-D surface water management system to the E-3 and L-2 canals upon receipt of easement.






  • Granting of an exclusive easement and confirmation of ownership requires Board approval (See Exhibit "B").

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Easement Deed between Lake Worth Drainage District and the School Board of Palm Beach County located on Stacy Street for the West Palm Beach Area ES (O6-D), and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

LA1 - Tentative Agreement between the School District and AESOP
Meeting
May 6, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Tentative Agreement provides for:





  • a new three-year successor contract effective January 1, 2009, through December 31, 2011, with limited reopener provisions for salary and a stipulated number of additional items for year two (2010), and year three (2011);

  • continuing the January 1, 2008 Salary Schedules through December 31, 2009, and the base salaries of bargaining unit employees in effect December 31, 2008, through December 31, 2009;

  • clarifying the definition of a grievance and transferring from the Superintendent of Schools to the Area Superintendent or respective department head, the responsibility for responding to grievances at level two of the Grievance procedure;

  • compensating an employee assigned full-time to a higher job classification after ten (10) consecutive workdays in the assignment (currently fifteen (15) workdays);

  • increasing the timeframe for releasing an employee who is approved for a transfer from twenty (20) workdays to thirty (30) workdays whenever a District hiring freeze is in effect;

  • developing procedures for an employee, a group of employees or management to request a job reclassification;

  • increasing the recall period following a reduction-in-force from twelve (12) months to eighteen (18) months;

  • increasing the promotional probationary period from ninety (90) calendar days to ninety (90) work days;

  • granting the District the right to require the electronic deposit of pay subject to a ninety (90) day notice of intent to the Association; and

  • granting the Association the right to use a school's public address system during regular announcement periods before and after the instructional day.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Tentative Agreement for a new three-year successor contract  (January 1, 2009 - December 31, 2011) between the Association of Educational Secretaries and Office Professionals (AESOP) and the School District of Palm Beach County.
