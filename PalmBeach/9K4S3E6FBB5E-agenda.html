  • Drop Back In (DBI) is a dropout retrieval program where students who already dropped out of school are given an opportunity to return and attend a program leading to graduation.


 





  • The DBI Program will be housed in leased space within Palm Beach County.


 





  • Students who are 16-21 years old and have dropped out of our districtâ€™s high schools are eligible to attend.


 





  • The DBI Program recruits students in the community and at work places employing students without a diploma.


 





  • The DBI Program offers flexible schedules, online and small group teacher directed classes with certified teachers.


 





  • The DBI Program is presently in 7 states, including Florida.  Their programs are in Duvall, Broward and other counties in Florida.


 





  • The DBI Program bears the entire expense of setting up their sites.  They pay teachers, administrators, online subscription costs and for materials.  As students enroll, the district obtains FTE for each student.  The district gives the DBI Program 9/10th of the FTE for each student enrolled and 1/10th is kept for oversight costs to the district and to provide textbooks.  This is the only cost to the district other than the time of district employees in the Department of Supplemental Educational Services to oversight the program..


 





  • Students who meet all requirements of a diploma in the state of Florida, including passing FCAT, receive a high school diploma.


 





  • The program is presently at four (4) sites in Palm Beach County.  The program plan is to expand to additional sites.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the contract with Alternatives Unlimited to provide a Drop Back In (DBI) Program in Palm Beach County.
