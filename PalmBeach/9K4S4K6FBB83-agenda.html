The proposed FY08-09 school calendar was developed through the efforts of a committee that consisted of parents, principals, representatives from the Classroom Teachers' Association, The Association of Educational Secretaries and Office Professionals, and representation from the Principals' Association and Staff Association, as well as various School District Departments, including Human Resources, Payroll and Labor Relations.  Input was also obtained from citizens through phone calls and written correspondence.



The proposed calendar meets the state requirement that school begin no earlier than at least two weeks prior to Labor Day [1001.42(4)(f), F.S.] and includes 180 days of instruction.



In developing the calendar, the committee considered many factors, including:



. honoring the current bargaining agreements
. historic days of high absenteeism
. requests to consider Veterans' Day and/or Election Day as either teacher work days or holidays
. maintaining balanced semesters
. dates of FCAT administration



The proposed calendar:



. Starts school for students on Tuesday, August 19, 2008
. Ends the first semester on January 15, 2009
. Ends the second semester on June 5, 2008
. Contains 89 days of instruction during the first semester and  91 days of instruction during the second semester
. Contains balanced trimesters and quarterly periods
. Provides Election Day (November 4, 2008) as a holiday for students and a staff development day for employees.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the FY08-09 School Calendar.
