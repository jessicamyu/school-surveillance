COM6 Holocaust Education Award Recipient
Meeting
Jan 23, 2019 - 7. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
9. Presentations
Type
Presentation, Recognition

DESCRIPTION:

Maureen Carter is the District's K-5 Social Studies/Holocaust Program Planner in the Division of Teaching and Learning. She is the recipient of the 2018 Robert I. Goldman Award for Excellence in Holocaust Education, awarded by The Jewish Foundation for the Righteous (JFR). This award recognizes an educator who has demonstrated excellence in teaching about the Holocaust. Maureen was chosen specifically because of her strong work in classrooms throughout the District, and her commitment to furthering her own knowledge of the Holocaust.


The award is named for Robert I. Goldman, a founding member of the JFR, who served as Vice Chair of the Foundation and Chairman of its Education Committee until his death in August 1998. As a tribute to his commitment to Holocaust education, the Foundation established this award in his name. As the recipient of this award, Ms. Carter received a cash prize of $1,000 and she was honored at a dinner in New York City in December 2018.

Keep Palm Beach County Beautiful (KPBCB) partnered with the District's Environmental and Conservation Services department to provide more than $15,000 worth of recycling bins and sustainability resources to more than 600 teachers across the District. This generous donation has increased District-wide access to green resources.

Under this agreement, facilities throughout the District have their centralized computerized automation systems monitored and, if needed, updated to achieve additional energy and related cost savings.

 

This centralized computer system enables the District to:

  • Monitor, schedule, troubleshoot and analyze data at school center facilities
  • Save energy and reduce energy cost
  • Control and monitor HVAC equipment, temperatures, humidity, outside lighting and consequently improve the overall environment of the facilities
  • Reduce maintenance costs through efficient operations and increase the life cycle of equipment, thus reducing capital expenditure for replacement equipment
  • Monitor District mechanical equipment and School Food Service cooling equipment and provide an automatic staff notification in the event of system failures
  • Control and monitor equipment required for hurricane shelters

Per District policy 6.14, this Agreement resulted from a direct negotiation with Wisch and Jackson Co. of Florida, Inc.
