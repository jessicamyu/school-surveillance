Outstanding Student, Adult and Senior Volunteers of the Year
Meeting
Jan 18, 2012 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action

The 2011-2012 Palm Beach County Outstanding Student, Adult and Senior School Volunteers are: Student Volunteer of the Year Keli Hodges, sophomore at Alexander W. Dreyfoos, Jr. School of the Arts; Adult Volunteer of the Year Kendra Palumbo from Del Prado Elementary and Eagles Landing Middle; Senior Volunteer of the Year Marilyn Schneider from S. D. Spady Elementary.  Over 37,000 volunteers work with our students and teachers supporting our goal of academic excellence.  These three outstanding volunteers directly impact student achievement and are shining examples of the thousands of dedicated volunteers working with our students each and every day.



Keli Hodges began her volunteer service while she was at Bak Middle School of the Arts when she and her older brother founded Students Supporting Other Students (SSOS).   SSOS, now a service club at Dreyfoos, provides students in need with uniforms, summer reading books, school supplies, clothes, and lunch money as well as support with their mathematics, science and language classes.  Keli is also a member of the American Sign Language Club, Speech and Debate Club and the International Thespian Society.  After Keli’s mother, other family members, and a family friend battled cancer, Keli decided to work on behalf of those impacted by the disease.  She volunteers with a group called “A Prom to Remember” which provides the ultimate prom experience for teens that have been affected by cancer.  Keli has a 4.6 HPA and is ranked first in her sophomore class out of 335 students.  She has earned the respect and admiration of her peers and faculty alike for her leadership abilities, giving nature, and sincere compassion for others. 



Kendra Palumbo serves on both school SAC committees, and plans and implements Teacher Appreciation Week for both.   She also assists in the media centers at each school and successfully writes grants to increase funding within the classrooms at both schools.   Since the beginning of this school year, she has logged in close to 1,680 hours for her work at both schools.  In addition, Kendra volunteers as a support coach for Del Prado’s Math Academic games team, Linguistiks Academic games team, Social Studies Academic games team, and for the Brain Bowl for SECME.  At Eagles Landing, Ms. Palumbo is the President of the PTSA.  But don’t think that she doesn’t do the “every day” volunteer jobs.  She is part of the copy/laminating team four hours per week.  She works on fundraising, even chairing an auction at both schools this year.  She volunteers on all Professional Development and teacher work days to provide staff with food and volunteers for all diagnostic and FCAT testing days.  In addition, this outstanding volunteer lends her support to several community outreach programs such as Food for Families.  



This school year marks Marilyn Schneider’s 17th year of volunteering at S. D. Spady Elementary, as well as her 86th birthday.  She has faithfully assisted the school’s lowest performing students on an average of 20 hours per week, working with students individually and in small groups.  Ms. Schneider came to S. D. Spady through the Listen to Children program, sponsored by the Mental Health Association. Over the years she has mentored hundreds of boys and girls. Currently she mentors 19 students, twice a week in reading and math and is a good listener for any problems they share.    In 2002, Governor Bush awarded her the prestigious Points of Light Award for her volunteer service. This year, Ms. Schneider created a “Book of the Month Club” for all students at the school.   Each member of the club picks a “buddy” in the class and inspires him or her to read also.  At the end of the year, the students that read the most books will be invited to a local restaurant for lunch.   In addition she has set up the “Holly Schneider Book Fund” in memory of her daughter who was an avid reader.  The fund is used to raise money to buy exciting books for the students to read.  Ms. Schneider stated, “In my lifetime, I want boys and girls to enjoy reading.  It would give me great satisfaction to leave a legacy to be proud of.”

This Project Modification contains the following items:



Item #1







  • Change Proposal Request (COPR) #1 in the amount of $48,119.00




  • Description:  Furnish and install a new main fire alarm control panel.




  • Cause:  Regulatory Agency Required.




  • Justification:  Prior to starting work in existing buildings, the School District along with the construction manager (CM), electrical contractor and fire alarm subcontractor, checked the existing fire alarm system to ensure there are no existing faults.  During this testing, it was determined that the panel had a fault condition.  The Facilities Services fire alarm group, Building Department, electrical contractor and fire alarm subcontractor all recommended that the best solution for correcting the fault condition is to replace the main fire alarm panel. 


Item #2







  • COPR #2 in the not-to-exceed amount of $20,007.00




  • Description:  Paint the interior of all rooms in Building 2.




  • Cause:  School District Requested




  • Justification:  The Building 2 scope of work included in the Guaranteed Maximum Price was to remodel the seven existing science classrooms into five science classrooms per the latest School District high school prototype design, a computer lab, and a distance learning lab.  It also included new HVAC for the entire building.  The original painting scope of work was limited to the seven science classrooms being remodeled.  It became apparent as the HVAC modifications were progressing, which included work in every classroom, that the interior of the other 17 rooms in Building 2 should be painted.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve Project Modifications in the not-to-exceed amount of $75,169.00 to Gilbane Building Company d/b/a Mills Gilbane for Pahokee Educational Complex, High School Remodel Project #1771-8427, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.

PS 1 The Consultant will provide the following services:



Professional Crisis Management Association, Inc. (PCMA) provides technical assistance to the Department of Exceptional Student Education for students who exhibit very challenging behaviors.  Many of these students are at risk for residential placements.  After PCMA observes and interacts with the student, they provide staff with a comprehensive report outlining additional behavioral strategies which staff should implement, in order for the student to become more successful. PCMA reviews Functional Behavior Assessments (FBA's) and individualized Behavior Intervention Plans (BIP's) which district staff have completed. PCMA will role model and coach staff on the recommended strategies as stated in the student's Behavior Intervention Plan or the new recommendations.   If necessary, PCMA will testify at Mediations, Due Process Hearings and IEP meetings.



Per the Board's direction, a committee was formed to review other vendors who provide this service.  The committee has met and is in the process of gathering information, viewing presentations, and reviewing the cost of other vendors.  This contract is being written for six months in order to continue services for our ESE students until the end of the year, at which time the committee will recommend a vendor to the Board.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend award of the Professional Services Agreement with Professional Crisis Management Association, Inc. for $13,642.50 from January 19, 2012 through June 30, 2012 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all documents related to this agreement.

AC2 - Second Amendment (extension) of the Family Central Agreements with the School District of Palm Beach County

Category
New Business

The District is currently involved in (3) three similar agreements with Family Central Inc., which provide funding for programs through subsidies for both Pre-K and Afterschool Services.  The Board approved these (3) agreements in June 2011.



Agreements:
1.  Early Childhood (Pre-K)
2.  School Readiness Subsidies (Afterschool)
3.  After School Program to Accept Scholarships - "Continue to Care"
 
This second amendment amends the following terms in each of the (3) agreements:
--An extension of these terms/agreement until June 30, 2012
--A revision of the provider responsibilities - including the dispute and resolution policy and procedures
--A modification of the termination provisions
--A new phone number to report at-risk child absences (Pre-K)
--A modification of method of payment (additional dates to cover extension period)



This extension will enable the District to continue to be a provider for families who qualify for subsidies for Pre-K and Afterschool services.  This new agreement language does not impact the funding or the programming the District receives for Pre-K or Afterschool Programs.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the Board approve the Second Amendment of the Family Central Agreement, which extends the terms of the contract until June, 30th, 2012 for the following (3) District Agreements:



1.  Early Childhood (Pre-K) Services
2.  School Readiness Subsidies (Afterschool)
3.  After School Program to Accept Scholarships - "Continue to Care"



and and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents and contracts pertaining to these agreements.

Contact:

Judith Klinek



Keith Oswald



Janis Andrews

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the District Operational Budget for these agreements.



Earnings from Family Central/Early Learning Coalition (subsidies) for students in need of Pre-K services may total up to $150,000.  These funds are allocated to the District's Early Childhood (Pre-K) Program for the operation of Pre-K Programs.



Earnings from Family Central/Early Learning Coalition (subsidies) for students in need of afterschool care may total up to $4,500,000.  These funds are allocated to the District's Afterschool Program site budgets for the operation of Afterschool Programs.
