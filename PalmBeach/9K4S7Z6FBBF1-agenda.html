Subject
PM-1 Suncoast High School Masonry Wall Project Modification
Meeting
May 12, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

This Project Modification contains the following item:







  • Change Order Proposal Request (COPR) #6 in the not-to-exceed amount of $209,385.00




  • Description: Design and install a ten-foot-high masonry wall along the west property line between the athletic facilities and residential homes of Congress Lakes.  Several designs were considered and based on an acoustical study a 10-foot high masonry wall would provide the best physical, visual and acoustical barrier.




  • Cause: Outside Agency 




  • Justification: Because of site constraints, the baseball field, football stadium, and tennis courts are located very close to the west property line.  The close proximity of the playfields to the west boundary line was brought up by the Congress Lakes Homeowner Association at public meetings early in the project.  The School District requested, and the Homeowner Association agreed, that a decision regarding the need for a wall be postponed until later in the project when actual conditions could be evaluated.  The construction of the fields is nearly complete and the Homeowner Association has requested that a boundary wall that provides physical, visual and acoustic benefits be added along the boundary between the school and their neighborhood.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Project Modifications in the not-to-exceed amount of $209,385.00 to Suffolk Construction, Inc. for the Suncoast High School Modernization Project #0151 7511, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.

Contact:

Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is an increase of $209,385.00 to the Suffolk Construction Agreement.

Subject
PM2 - Agreement between the owners in the Congress Lakes Subdivision and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the installation of a Wall.
Meeting
May 12, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)


  • The owners of Congress Lakes Subdivision have requested that the School Board install a wall along the west boundary of the new school where it is adjacent to the east boundary of Congress Lakes subdivision.


 





  • The School Board is willing to install the Wall provided it receives certain assurances from the owners, on behalf of all current and future owners in Congress Lakes.


 





  • The School Board shall construct the Wall at its sole cost and expense as part of the new school construction project.  The Wall will be ten feet (10’) high and run south to north along the west property line of the new school.  It will be made of split face concrete masonry block and run approximately 980 feet along Congress Lakes. 


 





  • Following the completion of construction of the Wall, the School Board shall maintain its structural integrity and during the period of any manufacturer’s warranty applicable to the Wall, will take commercially reasonable efforts to enforce the manufacturer’s warranty, as it pertains to aesthetic defects on both sides of the wall.


 





  • After  the completion of construction of the Wall, the owners shall be responsible, at their sole cost and expense, for maintaining, landscaping and taking all other necessary actions (except maintaining structural integrity and matters covered under any manufacturer’s warranty agreement) with respect to the west side of the Wall.


 





  • Installation of the Wall is contingent upon all home owners in the Congress Lakes subdivision signing the agreement.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Agreement between the owners of Congress Lakes Subdivision and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the installation of a ten (10) foot high wall along the west boundary of the new Suncoast High School where it is adjacent to the east boundary of Congress Lakes subdivision.
