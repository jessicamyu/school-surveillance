Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the attached list of Out-of-Field/Out-of-Compliance Teachers - Survey 3 for the FY21 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject-matter that is outside the field in which they are certified.  Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject-matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field in the following areas: Gifted, Reading, and ASD. Those new hires who are offered jobs as an Interim Out-of-Field, and are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements prior to the end of May. If a teacher does not satisfy the coursework requirement their Interim position ends.   Once a teacher completes their Out-of-Field requirements the school is notified and teachers can be moved from Interim Out-of-Field to Regular.  Teachers who are placed Out-of-Field in core content areas are eligible to apply for reimbursement on the Subject Area Exam registration fee after they add the subject area to their certificate.

 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate.  Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary, or ESE.  Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements. This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification. It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track.  Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014, include the following statement, “Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment.”

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Amendment 1 to the Collaboration Agreement with The Harvard Graduate School of Education and The Center for Education Policy Research for the Proving Ground initiative extending our initial agreement through August 31, 2021; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to this agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This is a revision of the agreement initially presented and approved by the Board on September 29, 2020. The revisions have been reviewed by District legal counsel and approved.  Proving Ground, housed at the Center for Education Policy Research (CEPR) at Harvard University, is a network of education agencies focused on iterative improvement. Proving Ground provides timely, actionable data and analysis, peer networking, and change management support. Proving Ground’s goal is to make evidence-gathering and evidence-use an intuitive part of how education agencies conduct their daily work. Proving Ground’s intuitive continuous improvement approach guides districts in addressing barriers to student achievement by understanding their challenges, identifying possible solutions, testing those solutions, and examining their impact.

This collaboration is between Proving Ground, ImpactFL and The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida. Through this collaboration, Proving Ground and Impact FL will work with partners, using Proving Ground’s approach to help The School Board of Palm Beach County select, design, plan, assess impact, and improve the implementation of interventions to inform the School Board’s decision-making.
