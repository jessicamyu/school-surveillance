UnitedHealthcare Grant Awards

Pediatric obesity is real and itâ€™s rising.  More than 30 percent of young people are obese, and more than 60 percent have at least one avoidable risk factor for heart disease, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention.  As a company committed to helping people live healthier lives, UnitedHealthcare has collaborated with Youth Service American (YSA) to launch this program that offers grants to teachers, school-based service-learning coordinators and students in the health professions to implement semester-long service-learning projects that help combat pediatric obesity.   



As a valued partner, UnitedHealthcare invited The School District of Palm Beach County students and teachers to participate in the application process for the grant opportunities available.    YSA developed an on-line review and scoring process to ensure a fair and consistent process.  The final review and selections were completed between February 1 and 15, and UnitedHealthcare is pleased to present six District schools and individuals with grant awards that will promote healthy eating and physical activity in schools and community organizations.



Dan Rosenthal, CEO of UnitedHealthcare will present the award winner checks.



$500 Award Recipients





  • Christine Black of Allamanda Elementary


Project:  Fifteen students will grow a garden to learn more about healthy habits and lifestyles.  When the garden has produced vegetables the students will cook their products and share with their families their newfound knowledge about fitness and nutrition.  It is an interactive program to encourage students to become aware of their own personal health and the well-being of those around them.  Youth volunteers plan to reach out to 100 people in the community to demonstrate the benefits of growing one's own garden and using products from these gardens to implement their own healthy standards.





  • Sharon Patelsky of Frontier Elementary


Project:  There is a unique type of after school program occurring at Frontier Elementary.  Twenty students will work their teachers to reduce their body mass index.  They will learn about food, nutrition, and how they can apply this knowledge to everyday tasks like shopping and cooking.  When the project has been completed at the end of the semester the students will be well-versed on the subject and present the data to 815 other students to persuade their peers to pursue a healthier lifestyle.





  • Ellen Smith of Gove Elementary


Project:  One hundred students at Gove Elementary will host two health fairs this semester to address pediatric obesity.  The fairs will be held in the cafeteria during the lunch periods so that students can collect data at various booths.  Teachers will incorporate nutrition into the curriculum, making for a well-rounded exposure to a proper balanced diet.  Twenty-five students will play a role in researching the data and producing the literature.  They will reach 1500 students at the fairs.





  • Paula Triana, School Food Service Dept


Project:  Students will have the opportunity to help set up and participate in two health fairs.  The fairs will provide a good opportunity to distribute helpful resources on healthy living while introducing students and families to samples of nutritious fruits and vegetables.  Project organizers see this as a great opportunity to involve everyone in the community in finding answers to a problem that requires a whole village to confront.



 





  • Eric Stern, Physical Education Dept


Project:  Type II diabetes has been on the rise among students in the Palm Beach County school district.  Youth volunteers are looking forward to developing a program that will address the issue through an interactive program of tracking footsteps not only among students but among their family members as well.  Students will be provided with pedometers as part of a series of health and wellness projects throughout the school year.  Physical education teachers will track the data campus wide and send home regular reports to maintain everyone involved abreast of their exercise progress.  Approximately 900 volunteers are expected to initiate this program, though by the end of the program it is expected that at least 1,500 individuals will have benefited from the interaction.



 



$1,000 Award





  • Linda Zaskey of Cypress Trails Elementary 


Project:  Fifty members of the student council at Cypress Trails Elementary will design and build three walking trails throughout the campus for students and staff of 1500 people to use during the school day.  The trails will include a variety of fitness stations to encourage stretching, balance, and strengthening activities while walking.  The stations will be colored coded with a lion's paw stencil every 20 feet and marked every tenth of a mile.  The trails will be maintained by the entire student body in their P.E. classes as well as occasional student council work meetings.

A Memorandum of Agreement has been made between the School District of Palm Beach County and Palm Beach Community College:





  • In accordance with Senate Bill 1908, The School District of Palm Beach County and Palm Beach Community College must implement a district-wide College Placement Testing program for all high school juniors.  An agreement is necessary for both participants to share student information and CPT test scores.

  • The agreement is necessary because NCLB allows for the sharing of student identifiable information.  It does not allow for the sharing of test scores,

  • Students will be tested at School Distict of Palm Beach County high schools, in April , 2009. 

  • The CPT scores will be accessible to Palm Beach Community College and given to the School District of Palm Beach County

  • The School District will inform students of their scores

  • For students who do not obtain a passing score on any section of the CPT, the School District will offer remediation during the student's senior year of high school

  • In years past, this test and any resulting remediation were the purview of the community colleges and universities.  Testing in the high school junior year allows high schools to provide remediation, when needed, so that students graduate high school, ready to begin college level coursework.

Florida Statute 1011.62(6)(b) allows district school boards to consider and approve an amendment to the school district operating budget transferring Instructional Materials  categorical funds to the appropriate account for expenditure, provided all instructional material purchases have been completed for the fiscal year. 



 



Additionally, Senate Bill 6-A, Education Conforming Bill, passed during the 2009A Special Session and signed into law by Governor Crist on January 27, 2009, waives the requirement in Florida Statute 1006.40(2)(a) that instructional materials purchases must be made within the first 2 years of the effective date of the adoption cycle, for the 2008-2009 budget year. 



 



The district needs to exercise the option to redirect the remaining $3,040,550 Instructional Materials appropriation, after all purchases have been completed for the 2008-2009 year, to offset the $24.3 million mid-year revenue reduction, which is the result of the state revenue shortfall.  The reduction in Instructional Materials appropriations is in lieu of an equivalent reduction of 46 instructional positions within the General Fund.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board adopt the attached budget resolution to amend the 2008-2009 general fund budget in order to use $3,040,550 of Instructional Materials categorical appropriations to offset the mid-year revenue shortfall.

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1012.42, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach a subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The District provides support for out-of-field teachers though in-service opportunities to develop instructional competencies required for the assigned duty.  Partnerships have been established with colleges and universities for required coursework and exam preparation. Teachers teaching in subject areas not covered by their current certification are required to sign Out-of-Field Agreements which permit continued employment on the condition that they obtain appropriate in-field certification within a specified time frame.

Â§         The Curriculum Team will create Character Education/Ethical Extension Resources to be integrated into the existing Math, Science, Social Studies, Language Arts, Fine Arts, and Physical Education high school curriculum and benchmarks using Learning Village.

Â§         Use of the standards-aligned curriculum, Building Decision Skills, created by the Institute for Global Ethics and recognized by What Works in Character Education Clearinghouse.



Â§         Support for a school-based contact at each school to help promote, and coordinate project delivery.



Â§         School commitment to participate in existing character education efforts including Character Education Conferences, District Character Education Committee Meetings, training, recognition events, etc.



Â§         The program can be sustained and expanded to all high schools after the grant period.



Â§         The project will be evaluated, and the randomization process conducted, as required by the grantor, by an independent evaluator.

  1. On July 26, 2006, the School Board approved an Interlocal Agreement between the School Board and the City of Delray Beach, permitting the purchase and construction of a soccer complex and joint use of the land and facilities at the Seacrest Campus.  (See Exhibit "A")

  2. Section 5.01 of the Interlocal Agreement also allows the City use of the Football Stadium Field.

  3. The City has requested section 5.01 of the Interlocal Agreement be defined further by a Lease Agreement between the City and the School Board.

  4. The specific facilities to be leased are the football stadium complex, including the press box building, concession stand building and the restroom building all located north of the field and the south parking lot. (See Exhibits "B" and "C")  The football stadium will not be needed by Plumosa Elementary School.

  5. The term of this Lease shall be for twenty (20) years beginning on the date of execution. Either party may terminate the lease upon thirty (30) days written notice.

  6. All proposed alterations by the City shall be subject to the review and approval of the School Board's Chief of Facilities Management.

  7. The City shall be responsible for 100% of the maintenance costs.  If groups other than the Lessee utilize the lease area more than ten (10) times per year, the City and School Board will meet to appropriately proportion the maintenance costs.

  8. The City shall assume 100% of costs of water for irrigation, restrooms and concession stands, 100% of electricity for field lighting, press box, restrooms and concessions stand and 100% of cost of installation of telephone lines.

  9. The School Board shall assume 100% of the estimated costs of removal of all overhead power lines leading to the property (approximately $3,870.00).

  10. The School Board and the City shall each assume 50% of the estimated costs of installing new underground power lines ($40,000.00), temporary power to the property ($20,000.00) and the estimated engineering costs ($5,350.00).

  11. The City will reimburse the School Board's 50% share upon receipt of a thirty (30) day request from School District staff. The School District will save approximately $50,000-$75,000 per year in maintenance and utilities for the field.

  12. Annual rent for the term of the Lease shall be $1.00 per year.


 

Recommendation:

  • The School Board has an existing Cell Tower Lease Agreement with Verizon Wireless for ground space at Santaluces High School.

  • FPL FiberNet has been contracted by Verizon Wireless to provide the necessary maintenance for the Cell Tower site.

  • In order to provide the necessary maintenance work, FPL FiberNet is requesting the School Board grant a License Agreement for +/-0.114 acres for the installation of a fiber optic cable.

  • The proposed License area is approximately 10' wide immediately east of the school's bus loop.  (See Exhibit "A")

  • The License Agreement is for the sole benefit of Verizon Wireless and shall be coterminous with the current Cell Tower Lease Agreement between the School Board and Verizon Wireless.

  • The appraised value of the License area is $7,300.00.  FPL FiberNet has agreed to pay the School Board $7,300.00.

  • FPL FiberNet will be responsible for all repair and maintenance costs of the License area.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the License Agreement between FPL FiberNet, LLC., and the School Board of Palm Beach County for 0.114 acres located at Santaluces High School; and authorize the Chairman and the Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

-Florida Statutes 1012.585 and 1012.985 and State Board of Education Rule 6A.5071 require the development and maintenance of an inservice education and training program for all employees. 



 



- The Master Inservice Plan, coordinated through the Department of Professional Development, must contain all training components leading to certification.  It is based on documentation of the assessment process utilized to determine inservice training needs.  Each component shall remain in the plan for a period of at least five (5) years.



 



-The current listing consists of active components and new components which cover a variety of topic areas, including instructional strategies, non-instructional strategies, safe schools, technology, social studies, assessment, fine arts, language arts, learning styles, mathematics, reading, and science. 



 



A current listing of inservice components and copies of the new components are on file in the Board Minutes Office for your review. The current list of inservice components can also be viewed on the Professional Development web site (http://www.palmbeach.k12.fl.us/StaffDev/MIP/index.htm).

On May 21, 2008, Board Agenda Item P2052108 was approved for site-licenses and miscellaneous equipment for the School Food Service Department.  This request is for the purchase of Biometric Key Pads.  This new technology utilizes an automated touch pad to recognize a person based on finger image.  This system does not store fingerprints or images of the finger but is based on selected points of the finger. 



This purchase will provide for:



- Greater ease and increased cafeteria line speed over card or pin  numbers
- Increased security as it reduces misuse of accounts by other
- Reduces costs and administrative time



This system is currently in use at Disney World and Sea World.



The estimated costs per the attached quote are listed below:



 511 Pad+ Biometric Device (Includes Shipping)  $231,944.00
 Less:  Volume Discounts and Trade-ins              ($40,800.00)
