Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Amendment 1 to the Collaboration Agreement with The Harvard Graduate School of Education and The Center for Education Policy Research for the Proving Ground initiative extending our initial agreement through August 31, 2021; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to this agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION: 
Proving Ground, housed at the Center for Education Policy Research (CEPR) at Harvard University, is a network of education agencies focused on iterative improvement. Proving Ground provides timely, actionable data and analysis, peer networking, and change management support. This collaboration is between Proving Ground, Impact FL, and The School Board of Palm Beach County and is the result of a Gates grant designed to improve students' achievement in 9th grade Algebra with a focus on learning gaps resulting from COVID school closures. Through this collaboration, Proving Ground and Impact FL will work with four school districts, using Proving Ground’s approach to help each district select, design, plan, assess impact, and improve the implementation of a selected intervention.

BD1 Board Discussion - ROBINSON - More Comprehensive COVID-19 Dashboard
Meeting
Sep 30, 2020 - 6. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
13. Discussion Items
Type
Discussion, Procedural

School Board Member Dr. Debra Robinson, would like the Board to discuss creating a more comprehensive COVID-19 Dashboard for monitoring and decision-making, and should contain the following metrics:

 

1. Hospital capacity for ICU's for Palm Beach County;

2. Hospital capacity for general medical for Palm Beach County;

3. Total positive test percentage for Palm Beach County;

4. Influenza-like Illness (ILI) downward trend for Palm Beach County;

5. COVID19-like Illness (CLI) downward trend for Palm Beach County;

6. Daily lab positivity (total positive tests reported/total tests done) for Palm Beach County;

7. Student absenteeism - school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high);

8. Staff absenteeism - school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high);

9. Reported positive cases - school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high);

(If we can get this weekly testing done, we can have real data that included reported positive tests done on campus/ total tests done on campus.)

10. Number of students that can be held at 6 feet distancing (static number). Use all available space instead of only traditional classroom space, school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high);

11. Number enrolled for face-to-face, school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high);

12. Percent utilization at 6 feet distancing, school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high)

13. Number of students that can be held at 3 feet distancing (static number), school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high); and

14. Percent utilization at 3 feet distancing-school-by-school, as well as by level (elementary, middle, high).
