Subject
G1 - Race to the Top - District (RttT-D) Grant
Meeting
Oct 10, 2012 - Special Meeting 10-10-12
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

The School District of Palm Beach County has filed a Notice of Intent to Apply for the new Race to the Top - District (RttT-D) Grant competition from the U. S. Department of Education, with a grant deadline of October 31, 2012.



The District intends to submit a four-year grant proposal, with a project budget of up to $40,000,000, to include teachers and students in all K-12 District schools.  The Classroom Teachers Association, as the District's primary partner, has collaborated to prepare this application. The application requires the signature of the Superintendent, the School Board Chair, and the President of the Classroom Teachers Association.



The Race to the Top proposal is centered on four core educational reform areas:
(a) Adopting standards and assessments that prepare students to succeed in college and the workplace and to compete in the global economy;
(b) building data systems that measure student growth and success and inform teachers and principals how they can improve instruction;
(c) recruiting, developing, rewarding, and retaining effective teachers and principals, especially where they are needed most; and
(d) turning around the Nation's lowest achieving schools.



The purpose of the RttT-D Grant competition is to build on these four core assurance areas to support bold, locally directed reforms and improvements in learning and teaching that will directly improve student achievement and educator effectiveness. 



The RttT Grant focuses on the following goals:







  • Create personalized  learning environments for all students in all of schools;




  • provide students and teachers with personalized  strategies, tools, and supports aligned with college and career ready standards;




  • accelerate student achievement and deepen student learning by meeting the academic needs of each students;




  • make equity and access a priority and provide each student the opportunity to pursue a rigorous course of study;




  • prepare each student to master content and skills required for college-and-career-readiness;




  • increase educator effectiveness and expand student access to the most effective educators;




  • decrease achievement gaps across all student groups; and




  • increase rates at which students graduate from high school prepared for college and careers.


The RttT-D Grant aligns with the District's Strategic Plan and specifically supports: Goal 1) Student First, Goal 2) Family Matters, and Goal 3) Highly Effective Workforce.



A copy of the grant proposal will be available for review in the Board Office.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve submission of the Race to the Top - District Grant to the United States Department of Education for up to $40,000,000, effective from January 1, 2013 through December 31, 2016, and approve any no cost extension as applicable; and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

Contact:

Janis Andrews (Janis.Andrews@palmbeachschools.org)
Keith Oswald

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the Special Revenue - Other Funds Budget is an increase of up to $40,000,000, if funded. All grant related expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue. There is no financial impact to the Operating Budget.

Oct 10, 2012 - Special Meeting 10-10-12

The Florida Legislature placed Amendment 4 on the November 6, 2012 ballot as a proposed constitutional amendment to reduce local government (county and municipal) revenues without any replacement sources.  While this amendment does not directly impact public schools, any reduction of local government revenues may well force counties and municipalities to cut or reduce services provided to schools.  At a School Board workshop on October 3, 2012, School Board members indicated support for Vice-chair Debra Robinson's request for consideration of a position to oppose Amendment 4.



Link to the official state summary of the proposed amendment.

School Board Member Karen Brill represented Palm Beach County at the September 28-29, 2012 meeting of the Greater Florida Consortium of School Boards in Miami. 



Attached is the cover letter explaining the adoption process and transmitting of the draft legislative program agreed to by Ms. Brill and representative school board members from the other 10 school districts.  The attached "First Draft" version shows the changes from 2012.  Most of Palm Beach county's 2013 legislative program are included in this document or were agreed to for submission by the Consortium to the Florida School Boards Association whose legislative committee meets on October 18, 2012 in Tampa and where Palm Beach County will be represented by Board Member Marcia Andrews. 



Also attached for information purposes only are two additional documents.  The attachment entitled FSBA Proposals will be forwarded to the Florida School Board Assosciation for consideration.  These are issues submitted by districts that Consortium attendees felt were more of a statewide concern.  The other attachment, FHSAA Proposals, is a student health issue recommendation that will be sent to the Florida High School Athletic Association.  Again, neither of these two attachments require Board action.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve the Greater Florida Consortium of School Boards' 2013 Legislative Proposals ("Final_Draft" version).
