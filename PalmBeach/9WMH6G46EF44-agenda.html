Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Cooperative Agreement with Community Child Care Center of Delray Beach, Inc. and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The purpose of this Agreement is to provide for use of the facilities for a Summer Camp Program at Orchard View Elementary School beginning June 8, 2015 and ending on July 31, 2015.  This Agreement may be renewed and extended for additional periods of time by agreement of the parties.

 

Achievement Centers for Children & Families (ACCF) will provide a Summer Camp Program to students at Orchard View Elementary.  The Summer Camp Program includes, but is not limited to the following services and activities:

  • Children's Summer Camp beginning after the end of the school year in June and operating throughout the summer from 7:30 a.m. to 5:30 p.m., Monday through Friday
  • Creative arts programming, provided through Prime Time, Educational enhancement programs/curriculum, such as "Journeys" and character development programs such as "Character Counts"
  • Daily snacks and meals
  • A variety of field trips of interest including:  educational experiences; activities to enhance socialization; leadership opportunities; community connectedness; and, fun engagement opportunities.

ACCF will, at its expense, provide the Summer Camp Program, including but not limited to: 

  • A certified teacher
  • books
  • arts and crafts or other appropriate expenses necessary to operate the Summer Camp Program

The School Board and ACCF shall be authorized to seek grants, funding gifts, and other funds in order to fund the Summer Camp Program from other sources.  There shall be no requirement for additional funding or continued funding after the dates designated for the services of programs designated.

Subject
FMT1 Resolution to Issue COP Series 2015E to Refinance COP 2012B and Terminate Associated Interest Rate Swap
Meeting
May 27, 2015 - 3. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
11. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board authorize a resolution to issue COP series 2015E, to refinance or restructure the 2012B COP and terminate the associated interest rate swap.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The District, with the School Board's Finance Committee, actively manages all outstanding debt to reduce borrowing costs and limit risks whenever possible.  This strategy has resulted in savings of over $100 million since 2001.
  • In 2005, the District entered into an agreement to refund the 2002D COP issue with variable rate debt and an interest rate swap. The District received an upfront payment of $4.24 million at that time, which was placed into the Board's Contingency Reserve.  
  • The variable rate debt was issued in 2012, as the COP 2012B.  At that time, the interest rate was locked to an index for a period of three years, which will end August 1, 2015.
  • The School Board's Finance Committee has requested that staff and the District's Financial Advisor look for options to convert the debt to a fixed rate and terminate the interest rate swap.  While the transaction has worked as designed, the financial markets are more turbulent and the risks associated with the transaction have increased.  Converting the transaction to fixed rate and terminating the interest rate swap will reduce the risks associated with the existing variable rate/swap structure.
  • The market conditions may, in the near future, be appropriate for the transaction to be refinanced with fixed rate debt and the swap to be terminated with little financial impact to the School District.  The District successfully completed a similar transaction in July of 2014.  This resolution will allow the District to act quickly when the market conditions support an economically feasible transaction.
  • This resolution authorizes fixed rate debt to be issued and the interest rate swap to be terminated.  The new par amount will not exceed $152 million and the net debt service will increase by no more than $3,219,300, which will fit within the existing debt service budget due to the other refundings that were completed this year.
  • The cost of issuance associated with the transaction cannot exceed $1,105,545 and will be included in the debt issue.  A listing of firms involved and their maximum fees is attached.  The actual fees should be less than these amounts depending on the final structure of the transaction.
  • If the market conditions change so that the refinance and termination of the swap is no longer a viable option, the District will renegotiate the current structure and not terminate the swap.  Under this scenario, the cost of issuance will be significantly reduced to less than $200,000.  The resolution contemplates several possible financial structures that could be used based on the market conditions.
  • The Finance Committee reviewed this transaction at a meeting on April 23, 2015 and unanimously recommended the item move forward to the School Board for consideration.  The minutes from the meeting, as well as the presentation will be available on BoardDocs and the Finance Committee website http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/FinComm2015.asp

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Operating Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

Leanne Evans, Treasurer (leanne.evans@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The net financial impact of this transaction in a net present value increase in the debt service for this transaction not to exceed $3,219,300 or approximately $270,000 annually to be funded from the debt service budget.

Meeting
May 27, 2015 - 3. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
11. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the salary increases and revised salary schedules for School-Based Administrators as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

The proposed salary increases for School-Based Administrators reflect utilization of an overall 2 percent increase budget. To help mitigate a market equity issue with starting Principal and Assistant Principal salaries, the majority of funds available are being utilized to raise the entry-level base pay for both positions. The proposed FY15 increase falls well short of eliminating the market disparity and represents an initial adjustment towards improving equity within the limited funds available. Additional adjustments should be considered a priority until salary levels reach par with the market.

 

Effective March 2, 2015, consider approval of salary increases and revised salary schedules for School-Based Administrators as follows: *

 

A. Principals

1. Raise salary range minimum to $90,000. Maintain salary range maximum and complexity dollar values at current levels.

2. Provide $1,500 base market equity increase. (For those at the salary range maximum, provide this payment in the form of cash lump sum.)

3. Provide additional base increase of: $500 for Highly Effective Principals; $375 for Effective Principals (treat New / Not Rated as Effective).

 

B. Assistant Principals

1. Raise salary range minimums commensurate with Principals' minimum (i.e., for 216 duty day positions, raise salary range minimum to $67,500). Maintain salary range maximums and complexity dollar values at current levels.

I recommend the School Board approve the final reading of the proposed boundary change to offer relief for Sunset Palms Elementary School.

DESCRIPTION:

  • On December 4, 2014, the Advisory Boundary Committee (ABC) voted 9-2 to forward "Study S-4-Revised" to the Superintendent.
  • The proposed boundary change moves the future Standard Pacific residential development named "Palm Meadows" (288 single family homes), located in SAC 242, from Sunset Palms Elementary to Crystal Lakes Elementary.
  • There are currently no homes constructed nor any students residing in the proposed change area; the first homes are anticipated to be constructed and occupied during the first quarter of 2016, with 100 percent build-out estimated to occur within three to four years.
  • The boundary change proposal relieves future crowding at Sunset Palms Elementary, optimizes capacity by using available space at Crystal Lakes Elementary, and will not impact any current students, as the proposed change area consists of future development.
  • A community input meeting was held on December 2, 2014 at Christa McAuliffe Middle School. Notification was provided to Standard Pacific via email. Additional public input was gathered at ABC meetings and through emails and phone calls. Copies of public input correspondence are available for review in the School Board Office.
  • The School Board approved the first reading (7-0) on April 22, 2015.
