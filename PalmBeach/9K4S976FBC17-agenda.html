Special Meeting re: Florida Elections Commission
Meeting
Dec 8, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Discussion

On November 1, 2010, a complaint was filed with the Commission alleging violations of Florida’s election laws.



On November 2, 2010, the Commission’s Executive Director notified the Respondents that staff would investigate the following section of law:



Section 106.113(2), Florida Statutes:  Superintendent of Schools, Arthur C. Johnson, and the School District of Palm Beach County, expended or authorized the expenditure of public funds for a political advertisement or electioneering communication concerning an issue, referendum, or amendment that is subject to a vote of the electors as alleged in the complaint.



 



The Respondents expressed a desire to enter into negotiations directed toward reaching a consent agreement.



Ron Meyer, Esq., outside counsel for the District, who has had previous extensive experience with representing persons before the Florida Elections Commission, entered into negotiations with the Commission to determine if a settlement could be achieved. The result of these negotiations is the attached proposed Consent Final Order and Mr. Meyer would recommend entry into this consent decree. This is a business decision for the Board to end this case and save substantial costs and attorney’s fees if the Board decided to continue to defend the District in this issue.

If approved by the Board, the Order would still be subject to a vote of approval by the Elections Commission.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve entry into the proposed consent final order with the Florida Elections Commission and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to sign all related documents.

Contact:

Joseph Moore; Chief Operating Officer



Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Chief Counsel

Financial Impact:

The cost will be $750.
