AC3 - Waiver Request and MOU between the School District and CTA - U.B. Kinsey School of the Arts Elementary
Meeting
Jun 1, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

UB Kinsey/Palmview School of the Arts Elementary has a long-standing choice program that creates opportunities for students to learn concentrations in band, vocal, digital media, drama, dance, movement, visual arts, strings, piano, TV productions, and percussion.  Students, parents, and community members value this choice program as one of the only elementary Arts schools in the northern part of the District.  In order to provide the extended learning opportunities in the Arts, a waiver and contract with CTA is attached to extend the school day by one-half hour for students and teachers in order to provide students with these core concentration courses.



The waiver supports: 100% of School Improvement goals, 100% of School Advisory Council, 100% approval of the instructional staff, and FCAT Test results for 2010 (see data panel).



The waiver was voted on by the School Advisory Council, and by the school faculty in a silent vote.  The waiver was submitted and approved by the District's School Waiver Committee for recommendation.  The School District Waiver Committee has considered and determined that the following factors exists, as set forth within School Board Policy 2.09, Paragraph 7:  1) The waiver is possible without incurring inconsistency with State Statute or State Board of Education rules;  2) the waiver is focused on maximizing student outcomes; and 3) the waiver is based on research and best practices.



A key outcome expected to continue from the extended-day schedule is an increase in the performance of students on the Florida Comprehensive Assessment Test.

Recommendation:

I recommend that through SB Policy 8.01, the School Board approve a waiver request from U.B. Kinsey Elementary School to waive SB Policy 2.36 (Length of School Days); and further, that through the process contained in SB Policy 2.09 (Paragraph 8) and through SB Policy 3.01, the School Board approve the continuation of the Memorandum of Understanding between CTA and the School District of Palm Beach County to waive Article III, Section B (1) of the Collective Bargaining Agreement to allow the continuation of the 30 minute extended school day at U.B. Kinsey Elementary School for school years 2011-12 and 2012-13.

The Adult Education and Family Literacy grant is a grant in the amount of $1,672,244. The program performance dates of this grant are July 1, 2011 to June 30, 2012.



The purpose of this grant is to facilitate the improvement and expansion of adult basic education programs including family literacy and workplace literacy. Programs for adults are provided to foster literacy development, lifelong learning and community involvement and development. The elements of this grant relate to the administration of adult education and family literacy programs for the development of basic and functional literacy skills, workplace literacy, and family literacy services.



In addition, this grant will provide adult education and literacy services in order to:



1. Assist adults in becoming literate and obtaining the knowledge and skills necessary for employment and self-sufficiency.



2. Assist adults who are parents in obtaining the education skills necessary to become full partners in the educational development of their children.



3. Assist adults in completing high school or the equivalent.



4. Assist adults to enter postsecondary education without the need for developmental courses.



Grant funds will also be used for personnel, staff development, instructional hardware, software, videos, and distance learning tools and materials to support the goals of this grant.



Adult Education programs are provided at 25 adult and community schools, one full-time adult education center, and numerous satellite programs located throughout Palm Beach County. These programs are offered at various times throughout the day and evening in order to accommodate student needs and schedules.



The term "adult education" means services or instruction below the postsecondary level for individuals who have attained 16 years of age; who are not enrolled or required to be enrolled in secondary school under State law; who lack sufficient mastery of basic educational skills to enable the individuals to function effectively in society; do not have a secondary school diploma or its recognized equivalent and have not achieved an equivalent level of education; or are unable to speak, read, or write the English language.



To ensure continuous improvement of this adult education grant, student progress will be measured by progression through Literacy Completion Points (LCPs). The Adult General Education program or course numbers and associated LCPs are used to document improvements in literacy performance standards. LCPs represent a student's attainment of an Education Function Level gain in adult federal reportable programs. These tracing measures must be used by all adult education programs for all enrolled students to ensure compliance in reporting.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Adult Education and Family Literacy - Adult General Education Grant from the Department of Education in the amount of $1,672,244 effective July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2012 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

Florida Senate Bill 736 "Student Success Act" revises provisions in several State Statutes relating to the evaluation of instructional personnel and school administrators. The Student Success Act amended F.S. 1012.34 to require the Department of Education to approve each school district's instructional and school administrator  evaluation systems.  Florida school districts are to establish a teacher observation and evaluation system that meets the following requirements:





  1. Alignment with the newly adopted Florida Educator Accomplished Practices.

  2. Practices strongly linked to increased student achievement.

  3. Based on contemporary research of effective practices.

  4. The principal, direct supervisor and any other individual performing observations will use, at a minimum, the same core of effective practices.

  5. Meet criteria listed in s.1012.37, F.S.


The Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) has selected the leading edge model for teacher evaluation that embodies contemporary research for effective practices. Dr. Robert Marzano's Art and Science of Teaching Causal Teaching Evaluation have been adopted as the State's model.  By adopting the State model, the School District of Palm Beach County will have met the state requirements as well as provide a teacher observation and evaluation system that is research based to improve teacher practices focused on raising student achievement.



The program from Learning Sciences International includes:





  • Evaluation Tool for Teacher Accomplished Practices

  • Face to face training for principals - (two separate) 3-day training institutes in three different locations (all 
    principals and assistant principals) - Including a train the-trainer model cadre for future trainings.                                

  • On-line professional training and support for teacher development - consists of 350 videos, articles,  
    interviews, and multimedia presentations

  • The iObservation Data Collection and Management System that reports data from classroom walkthroughs, teacher observation, self-assessments, and evaluations

Recommendation:

I recommend the School board approve the contract with Learning Sciences International for the new teacher evaluation tool, training, and resources, in the amount of $514,000 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all required documents.

Florida Senate Bill 736 “Student Success Act” revises provisions in several State Statutes relating to the evaluation of instructional personnel and school administrators. The Student Success Act amended F.S. 1012.34 to require the Department of Education to approve each school district's instructional and school administrator evaluation systems. Florida school districts are to establish a school administrator evaluation system that meets the following amended F.S. 1012.34 requirements:





  • Contemporary Research Based 

  • Assesses the Florida Principal Leadership Standards 

  • Staff development component (iObservation) 

  • Focus on student learning growth and effective instruction 

  • Provides for continuous growth by identifying professional development needs 

  • Four levels of performance ratings


The Florida Multidimensional Leadership Assessment, (FMLA) is based on three decades of international research. It is supported by a web based data collection system through iObservation.



This tool can be used to assess: principals; assistant principals, and instructional administrative support personnel. All district principals, assistant principals and identified district personnel must be trained on the evaluation system prior to it being used to evaluate their performance (F.S. 1012.34). The school leadership evaluation system must be submitted to State for approval no later than July 1, 2011. The new evaluation system will begin in part in FY12 and will be fully implemented by FY15 as required in the newly enacted Student Success Act.



The program contract from the Learning Sciences International (2) includes:



• Evaluation Tool for Administrators - The Reeves Leadership Performance Matrix provides the tools to support a new vision for leadership assessment and coaching that is formative, constructive, and unambiguous.





  • Consistent, clear, and fair definitions of leadership performance

  • Common language

  • Rubrics developed for each dimension and sub-dimension

  • Contemporary research


• The iObservation Data Collection and Management System from Learning Sciences International stores and reports data from classroom walkthroughs, teacher observation, self-assessments, and evaluations.





  • Documents judgments about leadership performance through supporting evidences

  • Offers continuous and effective feedback for veteran, new, and aspiring leaders

  • Supports collaborative processes and collegial conversations essential for Professional Learning Communities

  • Promotes observations and evaluations as part of a holistic learning system that develops leadership effectiveness


The program from the Leadership and Learning Center includes:



• Training for leadership includes various professional development components including:





  • Strategic Planning Session

  • A face to face 2-day training institute in four different locations for principals, assistant principals, and other administrative instructional support personnel

  • Leadership Seminar Series (8) – On-site visits

  • Leadership Seminar Series (4) – Webinar format

During a Special Meeting on May 11 2011 (item FM1), the School Board approved the issuance of the 2011B COPs.  The transaction will replace the 2001B COP issue.  The adopted resolution contemplated either a public sale or a private sale to an individual bank through a competitive process.  The resolution as approved as well as the executive summary of the transaction attached.  



 



Due to market conditions, the private sale was selected as the most



cost effective option at this time.  Proposals were received from 7



banks and the proposal with the best price was selected.  The winning



proposal was a syndicate of two banks with one bank acting as the



lead.  During the document review process, the second bank in the



syndicate indicated it would not be able to close the transaction in time



to meet our deadline and further was not agreeable to the terms



included in the documents.



 



Based on the advice of the District’s Financial Advisor and Bond



Counsel, it is in the best interest of the District to split the transaction



into subseries so that two or more banks can participate in the



transaction.  Simply put, no single bank is willing to purchase the



entire transaction with terms agreeable to the School District.



 



This amendment to the resolution will allow the District to negotiate



with more than one bank in order to complete the transaction.  The



amendment is within the guidelines approved by the Finance



Committee on May 4, 2011.



 



In order for the transaction to close prior to June 29, 2011, which is



the deadline for proper notice to our investors, this amendment must be



approved as soon as possible.  This constitutes good cause for the item



to be considered as an emergency item. 

Recommendation:

CHAIRMAN READS:



 



The School District has been notified that no single bank is willing to purchase the 2011B COPs with acceptable terms as stated in a resolution approved by the School Board on May 11, 2011.  An amendment to the resolution is needed to allow the debt issue to be split into subseries so two or more banks can purchase pieces of the debt issue.  The amendment should be approved before staff begins formal negotiations with another bank.  This transaction is a reissue of the 2001B COP issue that must be closed by June 29, 2011 to provide proper notice to the investors.  The negotiations and document preparation will take several weeks.  I believe this is a good cause and justifies the consideration of the amendment as an emergency item.



 



SUPERINTENDENT READS: 



 



I recommend the School Board adopt an amendment to the Resolution authorizing issuance of 2011B COPs to refund 2001B COPS in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $188 million.

Plumosa Elementary School of the Arts was a part of the Magnet Schools of America Program Grant for the past four years.  This grant program assisted the school in planning, beginning, and supporting their Arts program.  This program creates opportunities for students to learn concentrations in band, vocal, digital media, drama, dance, movement, visual arts, strings, piano, TV productions, and percussion. 



The students, parents, and community members value this choice program as one of the only elementary Arts schools in the southern part of the District.  In order to provide the extended learning opportunities in the Arts, a waiver and contract with CTA was created to extend the school day by one-half hour for students and teachers in order to provide students with these core concentration courses.



The waiver supports: 100% of School Improvement goals, 100% of School Advisory Council, 92% approval of the instructional staff, and FCAT Test results for 2010 (see data panel).



The waiver was voted on by the School Advisory Council, and by the school faculty in a silent vote.  The waiver was submitted and approved by the District's School Waiver Committee for recommendation.  The School District Waiver Committee has considered and determined that the following factors exists, as set forth within School Board Policy 2.09, Paragraph 7:  1) The waiver is possible without incurring inconsistency with State Statute or State Board of Education rules;  2) the waiver is focused on maximizing student outcomes; and 3) the waiver is based on research and best practices.



A key outcome expected to continue from the extended-day schedule is an increase in the performance of students on the Florida Comprehensive Assessment Test.

Recommendation:

I recommend that through SB Policy 8.01, the School Board approve a waiver request from Plumosa Elementary School of the Arts to waive SB Policy 2.36 (Length of School Days); and further, that through the process contained in SB Policy 2.09 (paragraph 8) and through SB Policy 3.01, the School Board approve the continuation of the Memorandum of Understanding between CTA and the School District of Palm Beach County to waive Article III, Section B (1) of the Collective Bargaining Agreement to allow the continuation of the 30 minute extended school day at Plumosa Elementary School of the Arts for school year 2011-12 and 2012-13.
