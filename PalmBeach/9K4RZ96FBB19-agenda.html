1. The US House of Representatives convened January 15th and the Senate will convene on January 22nd to begin the 2nd session of the 110th Congress. in 2007, Congress reauthorized Head Start and increased somewhat the funding for the Elementary and Secondary Education Act. Congress did not reauthorize ESEA as scheduled. Additrionally, Palm Beach County Schools will receive a $560,000 (less 2%) appropriation to increase data gathering capabilities among local law enforcement concerning street violence and gang activities.


 


2. The proposed Federal Legislative Priorities for 2008 largely remain the same as last year. ESEA/NCLB reauthorization and funding remains top priority. Continuation of Medicaid claiming, which is set to expire June 30th of this year, is a $3.5 million issue to Palm Beach County. Funding of the Perkins career education and IDEA special education programs, likewise, remain significant issues.


 


The proposals reflect input from staff, a review of action taken in 2007, and a review of issues among school districts. They are consistent with concerns represented by the Council of Great City Schools, National School Boards Association and American Association of School Administrators. The Board may modify or add to these proposals at any time while Congress is in session which is expected to last through October.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board adopt the list of federal legislative proposals for the 2008 Session of the 110th Congress and authorize School Board Members and designated district staff to work with other school districts, representatives of appropriate state and national organizations, and members of the Florida Congressional Delegation to secure legislative approval.

Contact:

Vern Crawford/Joseph Moore

Financial Impact:

To be determined by the outcome of the 110th Session of Congress.
