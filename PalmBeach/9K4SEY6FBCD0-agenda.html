G1 - Statewide Online Tobacco Prevention and Intervention Teacher Training Project
Meeting
Jun 15, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Statewide Online Tobacco Prevention and Intervention Teacher Training Project was created last year based on the findings and recommendations from The Needs, Barriers, and Challenges in Tobacco Prevention and Intervention Teacher Training Program among School Districts in Florida Report by the University of Florida.  The report recommended that the Florida Department of Education consider using an online program for teacher training and grant reporting. Student Intervention Services in the Department of Safe Schools conducted tobacco prevention teacher training for nine years under grants from the Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) Office of Healthy Schools.   For three years, this training was online.  The FL DOE stated that, "as a leader in Florida with establishing online tobacco prevention teacher training, Palm Beach County has laid the foundation to create a statewide online component in support of the funding priorities." Therefore, in July 2010, the FL DOE issued a Non-Competitive Discretionary Request for Application (RFA) to the School District of Palm Beach County to provide the training statewide, to all districts.  The district expects the grant to be reissued for SY 2012 to begin July 1, 2012 and intends to apply. 



The Midyear Report for 2012 by W. William Chen, Ph.D., CHES, Department of Health Education & Behavior, University of Florida, and his colleagues, concludes that teachers who took the course and their students demonstrate statistically significant improvements.  Teachers'  pre post surveys measure knowledge. The student assessments measure the impact the teacher's online training has on their students regarding changes in knowledge about tobacco hazards, media influences over tobacco use, and refusal skills.



The impact of this project will be measured in the numbers of teachers who complete the course, teachers' increased knowledge of tobacco prevention education and related effective teaching strategies, and increased student knowledge. Tobacco use by students interferes with their academic achievement and is associated with other drug use; school based prevention efforts have demonstrated success in reducing tobacco use among youth.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Statewide Online Tobacco Prevention and Intervention Teacher Training Project to the Florida Department of Education for up to $300,000 from July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2012, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.  The reason this item is coming to the Board at this time is because the RFA is expected to be received and due before July 1, 2011, and online instructional and support positions are paid for in the budget.

Project Transition provides adult education and vocational training and access to work readiness programs for refugees of all nationalities. The eligible targeted populations are refugee adults with Federal I-94 forms or other documentation proving their legal status in the United States. This grant will assist refugees with the knowledge and skills necessary for successful employment, leading to economic self-sufficiency and effective citizenship. 



These programs consist of the following Limited English Proficient (LEP) programs aimed at improving the language skills of non-English speakers; English Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL), Post-Secondary Adult Vocational (PSAV), Adult Basic Education (ABE), General Educational Development (GED), Citizenship, Vocational English for Speakers of Other Languages (VESOL), English Language for Career and Technical Education (ELCATE), English for Academic Skills (ESOLAS), Workforce Readiness Skills (WRS), and Vocational Preparatory Instruction (VPI).  For compliance purposes, student enrollment and student completion rates must be monitored in each literacy level within a program.  In addition, the grant requires that the country of origin for clients served be tracked and reported as well.



An English Literacy Program means a program of instruction designed to help individuals of limited English proficiency achieve competence in the English language.  In this grant, limited English proficiency means an adult refugee who has limited ability in speaking, reading, writing, or understanding the English language and whose native language is a language other than English; or lives in a family or community environment where a language other than English is the dominant language.



To ensure continuous improvement of this grant, student progress will be measured by progression through Literacy Completion Points (LCPs).  The Adult General Education program or course numbers and associated LCPs are used to document improvements in literacy performance standards.  LCPs represent a student's attainment of and Educational Functioning Level gain in adult federal reportable programs.  These tracking measures must be used by all adult education programs for all enrolled students to ensure compliance in reporting.

The purpose of this grant is to build the capacity of eligible providers responsible for adult education and literacy services in the State of Florida to increase the number and percentage of adult education students who enter postsecondary education and earn a degree, certificate and/or industry credential.



The program is designed to serve adult students in Palm Beach County who are enrolled in Adult Basic Education (ABE), English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL), General Education Development (GED), and Adult High School (AHS).  The grant will allow adult education programs to develop a systematic framework that connects adult education programs, career options and postsecondary education.  Adult education programs will assist in directing students to postsecondary institutions that offer career pathways services where students will be presented with career focused education courses and various levels of technical training, leading to a postsecondary certificate or higher education.  Educational opportunities will include multiple exit points for postsecondary education, employment, career exploration, transitioning assistance and support services.

The Department of Adult Education will establish a 5 year strategic plan.  The plan will focus on the goals of increasing the number and percentage of adult education students entering postsecondary education, as well as, the program design, curriculum, professional development, support services, assessment, partnerships, marketing and accountability.



Regular analysis of program goals and student data is included in the project monitoring so that accurate reporting of learning gains, employment and postsecondary referrals can be provided to the Department of Education.

This is the annual contract agreement with the Arc of Palm Beach County that supports the provision of services specified in Section 1003.54, F.S. Teenage Parent Programs to provide Teenage Parent Child Care Services.



The Arc of Palm Beach County will provide a high-quality, developmentally appropriate infant/toddler child care program at Gold Coast Community School.  Child care is offered to the babies and toddlers of teenage parents who attend Gold Coast.  The services will be provided in an existing classroom located on the school's campus, with a total number of 16 seats for the babies/toddlers. 



Because the children are in child care, the teen parents are able to attend school.  We are serving parenting students and their young children by paying for their child care while the teenage parent goes to school.



In addition to child care, the Arc of Palm Beach County Child Care Program provides opportunities for teenage parents to work with their children in a parent education program on-site.  The Arc assesses each child using the Ages and Stages Assessment tool.  In addition, teenage parents receive referrals to health and social services and transportation. The services allow the teenage parents to attend school while their babies/toddlers receive high quality child care.



The School District will evaluate the program by assessing the weekly attendance of the children served in the Arc infant/toddler program, examining the results of the infant/toddler Ages and Stages Assessment tool, and assessing the quality of the program by using the Environmental Rating Scale Assessment tool.



The School District will provide funds for this program from FTE generated by the children of teenage parents who are enrolled in the Teenage Parent Program and the cost will be subsidized with State funding (SAI-Supplemental Academic Instruction).

PLEASE NOTE: As part of NCLB EETT Title II Part D, private and charter schools must be included in grant development and participation based on compliance with Office of Civil Rights Approved Criteria which is required by the Federal Law.



The School District of Palm Beach County (SDPBC) plans to implement a Comprehensive Science Investigation (CSI) Palm Beach project. The purpose of this project is to focus on utilizing mobile technology, digital tools, and curriculum integration. Students will draw on 21st century resources using reasoning and deduction skills, developing a hypothesis, and gathering data to enhance cognitive analysis skills. Through the use of digital storytelling tools, such as podcasts and videos, students will document their learning to produce dynamic accounts of their investigations.
CSI Palm Beach has three major goals:



• Students will create science content through the use of tools such as digital microscopes, probeware, and mobile computing devices. This equipment will allow students to collect, organize, and analyze data focused on real-world learning scenarios.
• Technology Specialists will provide professional development opportunities to support teachers and administrators to transform the classroom environment into a place that supports student-centered learning, teacher guided instruction, and modernized assessments through technology.
• Participants will develop best practices in how to support mobile learning devices in the classroom to provide for continuous learning and modernized assessments.



With the assistance of district technology specialists, each participating school will identify teachers who specialize in science curriculum in grades 9-12. These teachers will integrate the mobile technology to teach science concepts through a hands-on, project-based approach, stressing real-world exploration. In addition to the mobile technology, teachers will integrate probeware and digital microscopes with students to gain practical experience in collecting, analyzing, and synthesizing information.



High Quality Professional Development for teachers and administrators will be conducted through the SDPBC No Child Left Behind (NCLB) Initiatives in partnership with the Florida Digital Educator Program and the Master Digital Educator Program. This partnership will align district efforts with state initiatives.  In addition, all teachers integrating this equipment will develop a curriculum for the effective use of digital tools in the classroom. A wiki will be created to monitor challenges and successes in using technology in education as well as develop strategies for classroom management and logistics. The wiki can be shared with other teachers on a local and national level.



Project participants will mentor other teachers at their school site to help build capacity in technology skills. Participants will also be able to share their knowledge at a district level through training days and conferences. Through this process, technology will be used in the entire instructional cycle from instruction to assessment in order to determine success.



Project Schools include:  Santaluces Community High School, Palm Beach Central High School, Palm Beach Gardens High School, and South Tech Charter School. Two private schools are collaborating at the requirements of NCLB which are Saint Luke Catholic School and All Saints Catholic School.
