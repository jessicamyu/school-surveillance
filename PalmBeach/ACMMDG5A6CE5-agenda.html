This is the only expense to the District for Driver Education on-the-road training.

 

2. In 2002 the State passed the Dori Slosberg Driver Education Safety Act which assessed a $3 fee to certain moving violations to help provide for an expanded driving program for a greater number of students. Through an inter-local agreement, the School District acts as a pass-through for the funds generated in Palm Beach County, receiving funds from the County quarterly and passing them on to the Safety Council quarterly. In April 2003 it was agreed that the School District would pay the Safety Council up to $150,000 per quarter.

 

3. In 2007 an amendment to the Dori Slosberg Driver Education Safety Act was passed, increasing the violation fee assessed from $3 to $5. Because of this increased revenue, the fee structure would change from the original maximum of $150,000 per quarter to the following:

The School District would pass to the Safety Council 70% of the funds received from the County Commission. The remaining 30% would be used by the School District as designated in the Dori Slosberg Driver Education Act for other Driver Education necessities. Since recent quarterly collections are approaching $250,000, pass-through funding to the Safety Council up to one million dollars would be allowed.

DESCRIPTION:

State Board of Education Rule 6A-5.071 states that:

  • Each District School Board shall develop and maintain a Master Inservice Plan for all district employees based on state adopted standards for high quality Professional Development as required under Section 1012.98, F.S.
  • The Master Inservice Plan shall be updated annually and approved each year by the District School Board.  By October 1 of each year, a letter verifying that the District School Board has approved the Master Inservice Plan and that the plan meets the criteria pursuant to this rule shall be sent to the Commissioner.
  • Each component shall remain in the plan for a period of at least five years.
