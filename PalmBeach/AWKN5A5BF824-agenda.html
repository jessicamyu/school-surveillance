I recommend the Board approve the Amendment to the Agreement with Palm Beach Gardens Community Hospital, Inc., effective April 19, 2018 through June 30, 2021; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Amendment is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.
 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Palm Beach Gardens High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.
 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 6000 high school students.
 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with the Achievement Center for Children and Families.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement will provide summer camp for children of Delray Full Service Center (DFSC) students and will assist in retaining our students during the summer. Traditionally there has been a drop in attendance when the K-12 school year ends.  Parents of K-12 students cannot afford daycare or to pay for summer camp and therefore, must stay home to care for their children.

 

Providing summer camp for the children of DFSC students will increase the outreach to the Delray Beach community and support the mission of both Delray Full Service Center and the School District of Palm Beach County. The academic focus of this camp will reduce or eliminate "summer slide" for the children of DFSC students.

 

Enrollment in the camp will have a positive academic impact on our families' children and the elementary and middle schools in Delray Beach.

I recommend the Board approve a lease agreement with South Tech Charter Academy, Inc. for the Odyssey Middle School Campus, excluding portable units, to house South Tech Academy, a conversion charter school, beginning school year 2019-2020; and authorize the Board Chairman and Superintendent to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The proposed lease of Odyssey Middle School campus to South Tech Charter Academy, Inc. is to operate South Tech Academy.  As a conversion charter school, South Tech Academy is currently housed in School District property on Woolbright Road in Boynton Beach. 

 

Terms of the lease:

  • Under the terms of the conversion charter school contract, the District will not charge a rental fee; and the premises shall be utilized solely for operation of South Tech Academy Conversion Charter School and Adult Workforce education.  
     
  • The term will commence upon the School's occupancy and will expire on the same date as the Conversion Charter School Contract.
     
  • The 14 portables currently located on site are not included in the lease.
     
  • The District will expend an estimated $15.2 million to renovate existing buildings to accommodate existing South Tech programs.
     
  • South Tech will cover the cost of utilities and the move from Woolbright Road to the Odyssey Middle School campus.
     
  • The District will be responsible for maintenance of base building systems, including roof, structural, electrical (power only), HVAC, plumbing, and irrigation systems.
