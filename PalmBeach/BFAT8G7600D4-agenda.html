Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Renewal Charter of ten (10) years for Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On July 1, 2014, Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School was granted a five (5) year Charter Contract to serve students in grades 9-12.

 

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)1, Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School meets the criteria for renewal and does not fall under any grounds for termination.  The School is in compliance with its current charter. The Renewal Charter was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff.

 

The renewal charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of ten (10) years, commencing on July 1, 2019, and ending on June 30, 2029, except as provided in the charter.

 

The mission of the school is “to provide a community of support and guidance for students in grades 9-12 by engaging students in competitive career academies that create innovative learning opportunities for all students to be successful in careers and college, through partnership with families and community resources.”

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve and adopt the FY 2020 through 2029 Capital Plan as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

FY 2020 Capital Plan

•  Each year, the School Board is required to adopt a capital plan prior to the adoption of the budget.
 
•  The first year of the plan is the FY 2020 capital budget that will be approved with the budget later today.
 
•  As required by law, the documents submitted for adoption are balanced.  
 
•  The School Board last reviewed the capital plan on August 21, 2019.  No changes have been made since that time.
 
•  The projects planned for FY 2021 through FY 2029 (years 2 through 10) can and will be amended as we work through the budget process for those years.  Any future changes that impact the Sales Tax Project List will be reviewed by the Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee (ISSOC) and presented to the School Board for approval.
 
•  The Tentative District Education Facilities Plan, which is the state version of the capital plan, will be brought to the School Board at a future meeting after DOE makes the online forms available.
