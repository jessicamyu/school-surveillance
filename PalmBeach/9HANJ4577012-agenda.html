Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the non-renewal of the Charter Contract between Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba, Inc., on behalf of Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba Charter School and the School Board of Palm Beach County; and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents and send the required notification letter to JLNS.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 30, 2004, Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba, Inc., on behalf of Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba Charter School (“JLNS”) was granted a ten (10) year renewal contract to serve ages 5-15, grades K-8.  The Charter Contract expires June 30, 2014.

 

The mission of the school is the Core Philosophy of the Nguzo Saba Charter School (NSCS). “Children who reside in public housing – most of whom are African-American – are as capable of learning and using what they learn as any in our society.  That these children generally do not demonstrate their innate capability reflects a severe lack of self-esteem.  Their lack of self-esteem, in turn, results from a lack of cultural awareness… not knowing who they are or from whence they came.  Logically then, re-claiming and re-affirming their cultural heritage and history is a prerequisite to applying their natural talents and abilities to building a better quality of life for themselves and those who love and care for them.  When imbued with a more positive self-concept, these young people can potentially become change-agents for their respective families, doing for them what many black professional athletes and entertainers have historically done for their families and loved-ones: break the cycle of poverty.  Each and every family has among its members at least one who has the potential to become a professional in one or another field of endeavor necessary to the betterment of American life; NSCS will identify these individuals, determine their aptitudes and interests, place them on a ‘track’ leading to their desired profession, provide the necessary support systems needed to maximize potential for success, and monitor their progress towards achieving their goals.”

 

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7) (b) 1, "A charter may be renewed provided a program review demonstrates that the criteria in paragraph (a) have been successfully accomplished and that none of the grounds for non-renewal established by paragraph (8) (a) has been documented."  In February, 2014, the district staff conducted a Program Review.  The results of reviewing the criteria outlined in the Charter Renewal Program Review of JLNS, show that deficiencies exist regarding Academics, Finance and Operations, ESE, and Transportation.  A significant statutory violation also exists relating to the school’s grade and the school’s failure to implement an appropriate corrective action. At the March 26, 2014, Special School Board meeting, the Superintendent will recommend to non-renew the Charter between the Palm Beach County School Board and JLNS.

 

F.S. 1002.33(8) (a) - The sponsor may choose not to renew or may terminate the charter for any of the following grounds:

 

1.  Failure to participate in the state's education accountability system created in Fla. Stat. 1008.31, as required in this section, or failure to meet the requirements for student performance stated in the charter;

2.  Failure to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management;

3.  Violation of law;

4.  Other good cause shown.

 

 

Specific details of the grounds underlying the proposed non-renewal are listed below:

 

1.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33(2) (b) (1) & (2).

  1. School Year 10-11      Grade of F
  2. School Year 11-12      Grade of D
  3. School Year 12-13      Grade of F

2.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (9) (k) 4 (n) 2. a & b.

  1. No evidence of F-D-F corrective action implemented as defined in Statute even though the District communicated with the school, including through a letter, to choose one of the four statutorily required actions.
  2. No response in communication to the Sponsor of intent to implement corrective action as defined in Statute. 

3.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with its Charter Contract Part I, Section 3.0, A & F (Curriculum) and Fla. Stat. 1003.03, as modified by Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (16) (b) 3(Maximum Class Size for charter schools).

  1. No evidence of specialized instruction scheduled on a daily basis for students reading below grade level;
  2. No evidence of a research-based instructional program for students reading below grade level;
  3. Evidence of operating in conflict with maximum number for students as provided in the Charter Contract in the core-curricula courses assigned to teachers.

4.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with its Charter Contract Part I, Section 5.0, A (Instructional Methods to be Used).

     No evidence of an innovative educational program that includes a variety of instructional methods designed to encourage student achievement.

 

5.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (16) (a) (3) and its Charter Contract Part II, Section 13.0, I, J, & K.

  1. No evidence of ESE students receiving services documented on their IEP.
  2. No evidence of ESE procedures being followed for eligibilities and evaluations.
  3. No evidence the ESE students’ IEPs are being written to meet student’s needs.

 

6.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with its Charter Contract Part III, Section 19.0, B & C  (Funding and Financial Responsibilities) and Section 21.0, E (Internal Financial Controls and Audit Process).

  1. Failure of school to satisfy past due rent to former landlord.
  2. No evidence that the school’s governing board approved or is monitoring the budget.
  3. Evidence exists that expenditures exceed available resources in each fund.
  4. June, July, November and December 2013 monthly financial statements were not filed timely with the District.
  5. Submitted inaccurate fixed asset inventory forms and did not resubmit corrected forms when requested.
  6. Evidence exists that accrued liabilities had been removed from financial statements (refer to 6a). The past due rent has been added back to the monthly financial statements and the audited annual financial report was recently restated to include this liability at the request of the District.
  7. No evidence of compliance with class size reduction as applicable to charter schools; K-3 classrooms were over resulting in a $15,467 penalty.
  8. Financial ratios indicate the school continues to be at risk for being in deteriorating financial condition, such as consistently negative fund balance over the past five years and unstable student enrollment.

 

7.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with its Charter Contract Part III, Section 20.0, C (FTE Enrollment Verification Process)

  1. Survey 2 FTE for middle schools was not reported accurately requiring District staff to re-enter this data.
  2. Survey 2 FTE for Transportation was not reported by the School requiring District staff to enter the data.8.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with its Charter Contract Part IV, Section 24.0.  The school provided transportation services as the school year began but did not provide evidence of the services including contract records, policies for student conduct, bus emergency evacuation drills, or confirm supervision for students in the bus loading zones at the school.

             8.  Failure by Joseph Littles-Nguzo Saba to comply with its Charter Contract Part IV, Section 24.0.  The school provided transportation services as the school year began but did not provide evidence of the services including contract

          records, policies for student conduct, bus emergency evacuation drills, or confirmation of supervision for students in the bus loading zones at the school.

 

The District conducted a mid-year review of the school, found numerous deficiencies, and provided the school an opportunity to cure and offered assistance.  The above items had not been corrected at the time of the program review.

Additional grounds for non-renewal are found in the Charter. Joseph Little-Nguzo Saba's contract states that grounds for non-renewal of its Charter in General provisions (G) include:

  1. A failure by the Charter School to meet the requirements for student performance stated in this Charter; 
  2. A failure by the Charter School to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management; 
  3. A violation of the law by the Charter School; or
  4. Other good cause shown.

By letter dated March 14, 2014, including attachments, JLNS was notified of this Board Meeting.

 

Copies of the Charter Renewal Program Review, Charter Contract, Data Panel and Board Notification Letter are attached.

DESCRIPTION:

On September 17, 2008, Renaissance Learning Academy was granted a five (5) year renewal contract to serve Exceptional Student Education/Autism ages 14-21.  The Charter Contract expires on June 30, 2014.

 

The mission of the school is to, “provide students with an autism spectrum disorder (ASD) with an intense, comprehensive educational program based on the scientifically validated principles of Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA), and evidenced based instructional techniques and therapy services.”

 

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)1, Renaissance Learning Academy meets the criteria for renewal and does not fall under any grounds for termination.

 

Renaissance Learning Academy does not meet the requirements for non-renewal as stated in F.S. 1002.33(8)(a). 

 

The School Board’s approval of the Charter Renewal Program indicates an agreement to enter into charter contract negotiations, and if successfully negotiated by Legal and approved by the School Board, will result in entering into a Charter Contract.  Once approved, the School Board will enter into a Charter Contract.  The Charter will be a binding agreement, which shall detail the rights and responsibilities of the School Board and the Charter School.  If the Charter Renewal Program is not approved by the School Board, the School Board is required to send a 90 day notice letter indicating the reasons for non-renewal and the right to appeal.  If the School does not appeal, or the School appeals and the School Board prevails, then the School is closed after the 90 days.

I recommend the School Board approve the Charter Renewal Program Review for Boca Raton Charter School.

DESCRIPTION:

On August 19, 2009 Boca Raton Charter School was granted a five (5) year renewal contract to serve students in grades K-6.  The charter contract expires on June 30, 2014.

 

The mission of the school is to, “provide an elementary International Baccalaureate Candidate program to children in the east Boca Raton area through public education dollars".  As defined by The International Baccalaureate Organization, its aim is to develop inquiring, knowledgeable and caring young people who help to create a better and more peaceful world through intercultural understanding and respect.  The program encourages students across the world to become active, compassionate and lifelong learners who understand that other people, with their differences, can also be right.

 

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)1, Boca Raton Charter School meets the criteria for renewal and does not fall under any grounds for termination. 

 

Boca Raton Charter School does not meet the requirements for non-renewal as stated in F.S. 1002.33(8)(a). 

 

The School Board’s approval of the Charter Renewal Program indicates agreement to enter into charter contract negotiations, and if successfully negotiated by the Legal Department and approved by the School Board, will result in entering into a Charter Contract.  Once approved, the School Board will enter into a Charter Contract.  The Charter will be a binding agreement which shall detail the rights and responsibilities of the School Board and the Charter School.  If the Charter Renewal Program is not approved by the School Board, the School Board is required to send a 90-day notice letter indicating the reasons for non-renewal and the right to appeal.   If the School does not appeal, or the School appeals and the School Board prevails, then the School is closed after the 90 days.

 

Copies of the Charter Renewal Program Review, Charter Contract, Data Panel and Board Notification Letter for Boca Raton Charter School are attached.
