I recommend the Board approve the First Extension Agreement to Charter Negotiations for Florida High School for Accelerated Learning-West Palm Beach Campus 2, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The application for Florida High School for Accelerated Learning-West Palm Beach Campus 2 was approved on October 7, 2015, by the School Board of Palm Beach County.  Per Section 1002.33(6)(h), F.S., the School Board, as the Sponsor, sent out the proposed Charter Agreement on November 6, 2015, within thirty (30) days of the School Board approval.

 

Also, per Section 1002.33(6)(h), F.S., the Parties “have 40 days to negotiate and notice the charter contract for final approval by the sponsor unless both parties agree to an extension.”  Since the forty (40) days would expire on or before December 16, 2015, the Parties jointly agree to extend the time for negotiations and notice to the School Board of the charter school contract for ninety (90) days, from December 16, 2015, to March 15, 2016.

 

Copies of the Application, First Extension Agreement to Charter Negotiations for Florida High School for Accelerated Learning-West Palm Beach Campus 2, Legal Checklist, Board Notification, and Matrix for December 9, 2015 Agenda Items are attached.

Dec 9, 2015 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop at The Education Network, 505 South Congress Avenue, Boynton Beach, FL

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board grant the Petition seeking a waiver of School Board Policy 1.09 Advisory Committees to the Board, Section 5(b), pursuant to the Petition for Waiver filed by Construction Oversight Committee Chair David Porter and enter the Final Order consistent therewith and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Board Policy 1.09 Advisory Committees to the Board, Section 5(b) provides as follows:

 

Committee Chairs Subject to Term Limitation. An advisory committee member selected by committee members to serve as the committee's chair shall be limited to four (4) consecutive years as committee chair. The term limitation for an advisory committee chair as provided for herein shall become effective commencing with the reorganization meeting occurring in January immediately following the adoption of this policy.

 

David Porter is completing his fourth consecutive year as Chair of the Board’s Construction Oversight and Review Committee (CORC).  At its November 12, 2015 meeting, a majority of CORC members voted in favor of (with two [2] members dissenting) requesting that the Board amend or waive the chair term limit provision in Board Policy 1.09 so that Mr. Porter could be permitted to continue to serve as the Chair, if so nominated and elected, by the CORC members.  On November 23, 2015, Mr. Porter filed a Petition for Waiver of School Board Policy (“Petition”).  The rationale for the waiver request is set forth in the Petition and the Petition meets the statutory requirements and is legally sufficient.

 

In order to bring this matter to the Board prior to the next scheduled election of CORC Chair and Vice-Chair in January 2016, Mr. Porter filed a Petition with the School Board Clerk on November 23, 2015 seeking a waiver of this provision.  This waiver process is in accordance with Board Policy 1.120 Waiver of a School Board Policy as well as Florida law.  A notice of the Petition was published in the Palm Beach Post on November 25, 2015 and on the District’s website.

 

In order to provide sufficient time for staff to present proposed revisions to Board Policy 1.09 to the Board and for the Board to consider and approve those revisions, it is recommended that the Board grant the Petition seeking a waiver of the four consecutive year chair term limit provision contained in Section 5(b) for a period of one year for the CORC Committee.  Unless modified by an amendment to Board Policy 1.09 or the CORC Policy 1.093 or extended by the grant of a future waiver, the grant of Petition and approval of this waiver would automatically expire immediately prior to the annual election of the Chair and Vice-Chair of the CORC Committee in January 2017.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve adoption of the proposed revised Policy 6.14, entitled “Purchasing Department.”

DESCRIPTION:

By Board approval May 13, 2013, major revisions to Policy 6.14 became effective on July 1, 2013, with the understanding that additional revisions might be recommended to the Board after the Policy had been in effect for some time.  Now, after the second year with the revised policy, the following revisions are being recommended to Policy 6.14: 

 

The proposed changes include:

 

o   Revise the Board’s delegation of authority to principals for commodities or contractual services that are to be paid from the internal funds of the school.  In the current Policy, principals have authority up to $15,000 per vendor per fiscal year (Section 4(b)(ii)(C)).  It is being proposed that the Board delegates up to $50,000 for principals to purchase commodities and contractual services from vendors who have already been awarded a contract with the School Board in accordance with Section 5 of this Policy for the particular commodities and contractual services sought by the principal.  (Beginning on Line 156)

 

o   Add award and/or recognition banquets to the principal’s authority. (Line 179)

 

o   Revise the Board’s delegation of authority to the Superintendent to approve purchases for Professional Services up to $25,000 and up to $250,000 for any competitive solicitation based on the annual value of each competitive solicitation for the term of award, rather than fiscal year.  Non-competed projects, other than professional services, will remain at $250,000 per vendor per fiscal year (Section 4(d)(i)(A)(B)).  (Beginning on Line 239 plus Lines 315 and 353)

 

o   The recommended changes will streamline the process for principals to make purchases from internal funds and for overall monitoring of contract expenditures.
