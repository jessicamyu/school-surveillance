I recommend the Board approve the remodeling of Palm Beach Central High School Cafeteria Renovation Project in an amount not to exceed $650,000, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.

DESCRIPTION

Remodel the cafeteria and serving lines at Palm Beach Central High School.  To enhance customer service, improvements to the serving area will include new menu boards, decorative lighting, and additional support equipment. Improvements to the multipurpose room may include Bahama shutters, faux columns, signage, new flooring and televisions.  Construction will begin in May 2017 for completion prior to the students returning in August 2017.

 

JUSTIFICATION:

  • School Food Service (SFS) developed a plan of action with the cooperation of the State (Office of Food, Nutrition and Wellness) to reinvent the way students perceive and receive their school meals.  This plan took into consideration the meal time provided, physical facility, customer service, nutritious food offerings, and the overall dining experience.  Since its inception, the High School Reengineering Program (HSRP) has not only met, but exceeded the expectations of SFS.
  • Increased lunch participation is one key identifier of the program's success. 
    • As the pilot program, Atlantic High School has sustained a 22 percent increase in lunch participation since FY 2011. This represents an additional 200 students being fed lunch every school day as a direct result of the cafeteria modification.
    • Forest Hill High School has seen a 35 percent increase in lunch participation since the cafeteria was modified in the summer of 2013. An additional 489 students are being fed lunch every school day as a direct result of the cafeteria modification.
    • Santaluces High School opened this year and within the first 20 days served an additional 10,639 meals compared to last year.
    • Royal Palm Beach High School is in the design phase with an anticipated opening date of August 2016.
  • Based on the success of the HSRP, SFS would like to expand the program to an additional High School. Palm Beach Central High School was selected due to the potential for growth in the lunch program and the support of the Principal, Mr. Darren Edgecomb.  
  • With these modifications, students will enjoy a customer driven dining experience with the opportunity to nutritionally recharge for improved physical and mental well-being, supporting the District's goal of improving academic performance.

I recommend the Board approve the Lease Agreement with Palm Beach County Sports Commission for use of the football/soccer field at Plumosa School of the Arts Elementary School, authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents and delegate approval authority to the Superintendent or his designee for future requests.

DESCRIPTION:

1. Palm Beach County Sports Commission (PBCSC) has requested use of the football/soccer stadium field at Plumosa School of the Arts Elementary campus for the 2015 National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics (NAIA) Men’s Soccer National Championship tournament;  November 30 through December 5, 2015.  (See Lease Agreement)

 

2. Since 1991, PBCSC has been a private, not-for-profit organization contracted by Palm Beach County to promote and market the County as a sports tourism destination.  PBCSC has enhanced the economic impact of Palm Beach County by attracting visitors for various sporting events and activities.

 

3. On November 18, 2014, the School Board approved a 5 day lease Agreement with the PBCSC to host the 2014 NAIA Men’s Soccer National Championship.

 

4. This Lease is governed by the provisions within School Board policy 7.18, which includes no smoking and no consumption of intoxicating beverages on the premises. The Lease requires Board approval due to a portion of the event times taking place during school hours. (See Lease Terms and Conditions)

 

5. The Principal of Plumosa School of the Arts supports PBCSC's use of the facilities.  The City of Delray Beach Police Department will provide security at each check point to ensure the continued safety of the school's students. 

 

6. Keiser University in conjunction with the NAIA will also be conducting a Champions of Character program which provides training for the School’s students to instill 5 core values: Integrity, Responsibility, Servant Leadership, Respect and Sportsmanship.

 

7. As it is anticipated that PBCSC will wish to continue to use the football/soccer stadium field at Plumosa in the future, this item also includes a recommendation that the Board delegate approval authority to the Superintendent for anticipated future years of 2016 through 2019.  Future lease approvals within this time frame will not be brought back to the Board.

 

8. The Lease fee for this event is $3,825. 

 

9.  This item is being recommended to be added to the November 17, 2015 Board Agenda for "good cause" as the NAIA tournament is scheduled to begin on November 30, 2015 and there is no other Board meeting scheduled prior to the start of the tournament at which the Board could approve the lease.
