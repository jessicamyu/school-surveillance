Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the five (5) year Charter with Early Career Academy, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Early Career Academy, Inc. to open Early Career Academy (ECA) in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on November 6, 2013, as Item CS5 during a Special Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, except as provided in the charter.

 

Early Career Academy will serve students in grades 11-12.

 

Early Career Academy’s mission is, “A career-focused charter high school for grades 11 and 12 in which students earn a high school diploma while simultaneously earning an associate degree in a career-oriented field.  It will provide students with an opportunity to acquire an in-demand body of knowledge; obtain secondary and post-secondary credentials, saving them a material portion of post-secondary tuition costs and additional study time; and, if desired, obtain employment in a career-oriented field upon attainment of a post-secondary credential.”

 

Attached are copies of the full contract, budget, application, legal checklist and contract matrix for Early Career Academy.

I recommend the School Board approve the one-year Charter Renewal Program Review and the one-year Charter Renewal Contract for Leadership Academy West; and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Last year, following a renewal program evaluation and the Superintendent’s recommendation to deny renewal, the School Board voted against not renewing the School’s Charter at its meeting of March 6, 2013, and discussed placing the School on a corrective action plan.

 

The Corrective Action Plan and First Amendment to the Charter were presented to and approved by the School Board on May 15, 2013.  Later, on July 24, 2013, the Board voted and Leadership Academy West was granted a one (1) year renewal contract to serve students ages 14-19, in grades 9-12.  This Renewal Charter Contract expires on June 30, 2014.  Staff has determined that the School complied with the requirements in this plan.

 

The mission of the School is to, “Provide opportunities for enrolled students to acquire an education focused on rigorous academics, technology, and technical skills to produce graduates who are prepared for success in a global community.  Through a science-based and technology driven curriculum, students will gain knowledge and skills to prepare them for college and the workforce upon graduation.  Along with a high level of achievement in core academic areas, students will create ownership of challenging goals in their individual areas of interest.  Immersion in the community and local businesses will encourage students to take advantage of local resources.”

 

Based on this year’s renewal program evaluation and pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)(1), Leadership Academy West meets the criteria for renewal and does not warrant through documentation non-renewal as stated in F.S. 1002.33(8)(a).  As noted in the attached matrix, staff has determined that concerns regarding governance and ESE services have been cured.  Operational concerns are being addressed.  Pursuant to F.S. 1002.345 (1) (c), the parties have agreed upon a financial corrective action plan to address all financial concerns and the plan is being submitted to the Department of Education for approval.

 

The School Board’s approval of the Charter Renewal Program indicates an agreement to enter into charter contract negotiations, and if successfully negotiated by Legal and approved by the School Board, will result in entering into a Renewal Charter Contract for one year.  The parties have successfully negotiated the terms of a new one year Renewal Charter.  The language in this one year Charter is almost identical to the language within last year's one year Charter with a new commencement date of July 1, 2014 and ending June 30, 2015. 

 

Note, that the School received a grade of “D” in 2013; and a grade of “F” in 2012.  This is a reason the recommendation is for a one year renewal only.  As stated in F.S. 1002.33(9)(n)2, if the School this year earns a grade of “F”, absent a statutory exception:

 

a…the charter school governing board shall choose one of the following corrective actions:

 

  1. Contract for educational services to be provided directly to students, instructional personnel, and school administrators, as prescribed in state board rule;
  2. Contract with an outside entity that has a demonstrated record of effectiveness to operate the school;
  3. Reorganize the school under a new director or principal who is authorized to hire new staff; or
  4. Voluntarily close the charter school.

 

b  The charter school must implement the corrective action in the school year following receipt … a second nonconsecutive grade of “F” within a 3-year period.

 

Copies of Leadership Academy West’s Corrective Action Plan and First Amendment, Renewal Charter Contract, this year’s Charter Renewal Program Review for a proposed One Year Charter Contract Renewal, Proposed 2014.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board authorize the resolution to issue COP series 2014B, to refinance the 2011B COP and terminate the associated interest rate swap.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The District, with the School Board's Finance Committee, actively manages all outstanding debt to reduce borrowing costs and limit risks whenever possible.  This strategy has resulted in savings of over $80 million since 2001.
  • In 2005, the District entered into an agreement to refund the 2001B COP issue with variable rate debt and an interest rate swap. The District received an upfront payment of $6.2 million at that time, which was placed into the Board's Contingency Reserve.  
  • The variable rate debt was issued in 2011 as the 2011B COP issue.  At that time, the interest rate was locked to an index for a period of three years, which will end August 1, 2014.
  • The School Board's Finance Committee has requested that staff and the District's Financial Advisor look for options to convert the debt to a fixed rate and terminate the interest rate swap.  While the transaction has worked as designed, the financial markets are more turbulent and the risks associated with the transaction have increased.
  • The market conditions appear to be appropriate for the transaction to be refinanced with fixed rate debt and the swap to be terminated with little financial impact to the School District.
  • This resolution authorizes fixed rate debt to be issued.  The new interest rate will be less but the par amount will be more, not to exceed $200 million, in order to make the required termination payment for the swap, which cannot exceed $35 million.
  • The cost of issuance associated with the transaction cannot exceed $532,000 and will be included in the debt issue.  A listing of firms involved and their maximum fees is attached.  The fees should be less than these amounts depending on the final structure of the transaction.
  • The net present value of the debt service for the transaction will increase by approximately $1.4 million or $150,000 annually.  This is within the existing debt service budget.
  • The transaction will reduce market and other types of financial risk for the School District.
  • Once completed, the District will have saved over $4.8 million from this transaction since 2005.
  • If the market conditions change so that the refinance and termination of the swap is no longer a viable option, the District will renegotiate the current structure and not terminate the swap.  The resolution contemplates several possible financial structures that could be used based on the market conditions.
  • The Finance Committee will review this transaction at a meeting on May 30, 2014.  The minutes from the meeting, as well as the presentation will be available on BoardDocs and the Finance Committee website http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/FinanceCommitteeFinal1.asp
