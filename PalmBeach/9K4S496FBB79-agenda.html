Interlocal Agreement between the Greater Boca Raton Beach and Park District and the School Board.
Meeting
Feb 4, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action

·         On January 14, 2009, the School Board reviewed this agenda item and requested additional information and clarification.



 



·         The Board requested the following language:



 



o       clarification of the definition of Athletic Field Facilities in the body of the Agreement,



o       allow the Boca Raton Beach and Park District to have first priority use instead of exclusive use after 6:00 PM, and



o      add language regarding early termination of the Agreement and reimbursement provisions. 



 



·         The new language is shown in red.



 





  • The Greater Boca Raton Beach and Park District (District) has agreed to reimburse the School Board for the actual costs of construction of the lighting of the Athletic Field Facilities estimated at $250,000.


 





  • The District will reimburse the School Board for the actual costs of maintenance of the Athletic Field Facilities lighting, including normal repairs.


 





  • The District will pay all power bills associated with the Athletic Field Facilities’ lighting.


 





  • The School Board will plan, design and construct the Athletic Field Facilities improvements.


 





  • The School Board agrees that the District, its residents and the District’s approved uses groups shall have first priority use of the Athletic Fields after 6:00 P.M. on weekdays.


 





  • During the holidays, the School and the District shall mutually agree on a schedule of use, with the general intent that the District shall have first priority use during weekends and holidays and the School shall have use of the Athletic Field Facilities for special School activities and programs. 


 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement between the Greater Boca Raton Beach and Park District and the School Board of Palm Beach County for funding of the Boca Raton Middle School Athletic Field Lighting Facilities.

FM1 - Consider Resolution Authorizing Revenue Anticipation Notes, Series 2009
Meeting
Feb 4, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)


·        The School Board approved the issuance of Certificates of Participation (COPs) in September 2008 for projects in the Five Year Capital Plan.



·         Due to the instability in the credit markets, the District has not been able to issue the COPS.  Additionally, the current borrowing rate for long term debt is close to 6%, which was the maximum rate allowed in the Board Resolution.



·        Two of the construction projects are scheduled to begin in February to comply with the current schedules and meet the requirements of concurrency.



·        The District can issue Revenue Anticipation Notes (RANs) as an interim financing tool.  The Notes would be very similar to the Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs) issued every fall.  Rather  than pledging the first receipts from general fund tax revenues for operations as is the case with TANS, a RAN pledges all legally available revenues on the capital side, primarily the 1.75 capital outlay millage. However, it may be paid from, and in this case is expected to be paid from a future COP issue.



·        The RAN would mature in one year and the anticipated interest rate is less than 1%.  The notes can be re-issued up to 4 times, allowing up to five years for the needed revenues to become available.



·        The District still plans to sell the COP issue when the markets stabilize.



·        The projects to be funded are on the Sales Tax Referendum Project list.  If the District is not able to sell the COP issue any excess sales tax collected over the $560 million would also be used to fund the projects and retire the RAN.



·        The Finance Committee reviewed and approved the transaction at its meetings on December 8, 2008 and January 30, 2009.  The minutes of the meetings are available online at http://www.palmbeach.k12.fl.us/finman/financialdivisions/treasury/ntreasury%20fincom.htm..



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Resolution authorizing the issuance of Revenue Anticipation Notes, Series 2009 in an amount not to exceed $75 million and other necessary documents; authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign necessary documents; and authorize necessary budget amendments.

FM2 - Consider Purchase of 2002B Swap Termination Option
Meeting
Feb 4, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action

·         In 2002, The School Board entered into an Interest Rate Swap on the 2002B COP issue with Citibank.  The transaction included a termination option which allows Citibank to cancel the swap at any time after five (5) years at no cost.



·         Citibank paid the School District $6,142,000 for the termination option.  The funds were placed in the Board Contingency.



·         The District’s Financial Advisor has recommended that we purchase the option from Citibank now as the price is at a historic low.



·         The District has monitored this transaction carefully since inception, hoping for an opportunity to purchase the option back.  The expected price at this time is approximately $2.6 million which means the District will have made $3.5 million plus interest on the transaction.



·         The actual purchase price would be negotiated between the District and Citibank with the assistance of the District’s Financial Advisor.



·         The purchase of the option will provide greater flexibility in managing the remaining swap during these turbulent financial times.



·         The purchase of the option would be funded from the District’s Debt Service Budget.

The Finance Committee reviewed and approved the transaction at their meeting on January 30, 2009.  The minutes of the meetings are available online at http://www.palmbeach.k12.fl.us/finman/financialdivisions/treasury/ntreasury%20fincom.htm

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the purchase of the 2002B Swap Termination Option in an amount not to exceed $3 million; authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign necessary documents; and authorize necessary budget amendments.
