I recommend the Board approve a Resolution authorizing the issuance of the 2018 Tax Anticipation Note, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to approve all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The resolution authorizes the issuance of the 2018 Tax Anticipation Note.
  • The Tax Anticipation Note, or TAN, is a short term financing instrument used to alleviate cash shortfalls in the general fund each year. Current estimates show that additional cash will be needed in November 2018.
  • Local property taxes provide the majority of the revenues for the School District. As the first receipt of tax revenues is expected in late November, it is necessary to borrow money for cash flow purposes until the tax revenues are received.
  • Based on projected cash needs, the District plans to issue a $115 million TAN this year. It will be structured for the same amount and term as the 2017 TAN.
  • A summary of the transaction is attached. Projected interest expense is $2.1 million and the costs of issuance are not to exceed $91,405. The projected costs are detailed in the attachment titled 2018 TAN Cost of Issuance. Interest earnings are expected to offset the costs of the transaction.
  • The sale is tentatively planned for September 25, 2018 at 11:00 am. The transaction should close on or about October 02, 2018 and mature no later than August 30, 2019.
  • Students from the Finance Academies have been invited to attend a presentation beginning at 9:30 on September 25th, followed by a mock sale which will allow them to participate in the note sale. Financial professionals have also been invited to help the students understand the career opportunities in public finance.
  • The Finance Committee reviewed the proposed tax anticipation note during their April 27th meeting and unanimously recommended the transaction move forward to the School Board for consideration. The presentation provided to the committee is also attached for your reference.

I recommend the Board approve the Resolution to hold a referendum to seek voter approval to levy 1.00 mills for operational purposes in non-charter District schools in FY2020, FY2021, FY2022, and FY2023; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

 DESCRIPTION:

In 2010, and again in 2014, the voters approved the continuation of a 0.25 mill levy through FY2019 for school operational purposes.  As stipulated in the ballot language, all proceeds from this levy have been used to fund over 650 teachers within arts, music, physical education, choice and career education programs.

 

The Independent Referendum Oversight Committee (IROC) comprised of independent citizens has been overseeing expenditures of the millage levy to ensure all proceeds are expended for teachers and programs as specified in the ballot language approved by voters.  This requirement will remain in place under the proposed resolution.

 

This request to levy 1.00 mill would be included on the ballot for the upcoming November 6, 2018 general election.

 

This levy will generate approximately $200 million in revenue for the FY2020 general fund operating budget and be used to address critical school operating needs.  This includes the continuation and expansion of art, music, physical education, and career education teachers.  In addition, the school operations millage revenue would be dedicated to school safety enhancements and increasing teacher salaries to help retain experienced teachers.

 

School safety enhancements includes increasing the number school police officers and mental health professionals and purchasing all related safety equipment.

 

Florida Statutes Sections 1011.71(9) and 101.73(2) authorize the School Board to levy additional millage for school operational purposes by a local referendum or in a general election for a period not in excess of four years.

 

The School Board, by adopting this resolution at a regular meeting, shall direct the County Commissioners to call an election at which the voters may approve an ad valorem tax millage.

 

Subsequent to Board discussion on June 20, 2018, the Office of General Counsel obtained a detailed legal opinion Greenberg Traurig, the law firm of Bob Gang, the District’s bond counsel.  The attached opinion analyzes whether, under Florida law, the School Board is obligated to share proceeds from the millage levy with charter schools. 
 

The conclusion is as follows:

 

(1) The plain language of Section 1 002.33(17)(b ), Florida Statutes, obligates the Board to share with District charter schools "funds from the school district's current operating discretionary millage levy."
 

(2) The "additional" voter-approved millage levy authorized by Section 1011.71(9), Florida Statutes, to generate funds for "school operational purposes" is a different category of millage levy from the "current operating discretionary millage levy" from which charter schools are funded pursuant to Section 1 002.33(17)(b).
 

(3) Because the "additional" voter-approved millage levy described in Section 1011.79(9) is not listed as a source of funds for charter schools in Section 1002.33(17)(b), the Board does not have an obligation to share with District charter schools any proceeds from such a millage levy.
 

Elliot H. Scherker, who signed the Opinion, is the co-chair of Greenberg Traurig’s National Appellate Practice Group, and he has 40-plus years of experience as an appellate attorney and has handled more than 2000 appeals in state and federal courts.

I recommend the Board approve the proposed Security Agreement with Local Law Enforcement Agencies; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to execute the Security Agreements, substantially in the form attached.

DESCRIPTION:

Under the provisions of the Marjory Stoneman Douglas Act, the School District is adding 75 new School Police positions to ensure each District-operated school has at least one police officer staffed daily beginning with the first day of school on August 13, 2018. Hiring and filling the new officer positions is underway, however, until the positions are fully staffed, the District will utilize officers from local law enforcement agencies to ensure full coverage when school starts.

 

Normally, District staff brings contracts that are approved and executed by the city or village councils to the School Board for approval. With time very short until the start of school and in the interest of expediency, the proposed contract is being brought to the School Board prior to approval by city and village councils, and because of that, some minor changes to the agreement may be necessary in order for local councils to approve them. With the School Board’s approval, the Superintendent and staff will be authorized to modify and finalize the Security Agreements, which will be substantially in the form attached to this item and the Superintendent and Board Chairman will be authorized to executed the finalized Security Agreements.

 

As the District increases its own police force, staffing requested from local law enforcement agencies will be reduced. The estimated financial impact of $5,400,000 assumes full utilization for the entire school year. Actual cost is anticipated to be less than the full amount.
