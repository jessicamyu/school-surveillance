4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Subject
ELR1 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Raymond Berry
Meeting
Jun 23, 2021 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

On or about November 20, 2019, Raymond Berry became the subject of an Employee and Labor Relations investigation based upon allegations of policy violations related to Unsafe Operation of a District School Bus, Inappropriate Interaction with Students, and Insubordination: Continued Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive.

 

The allegation is that Raymond Berry on multiple occasions put students at risk while operating the school bus has been substantiated.  He instructed students several times to move from the front of the bus to the back of the bus and vice versa while the bus was in motion. Students were standing, sitting with their feet in the aisle, kneeling on the seats while the bus is in motion, and he even had conversations with students standing at the front of the bus while the bus in motion. Mr. Berry’s bus lacked proper student management. In addition, Mr. Berry had inappropriate conversations with the students.  In addition, when a student missed his stop and was crying on the bus, Mr. Berry spoke inappropriately, embarrassing the student. This occurred in front of other students on the bus.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following: School Board Policies 1.013(1), Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff; 3.21(3)(a), (3)(f), and (3)(l), Safe Operation of District School Buses; 3.02 (2), (4)(a), (4)(b), (4)(d), (4)(e), (4)(j), (5)(a)(ii), and (5)(a)(viii), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment with the District; School Bus Drivers and Bus Attendants Handbook, Chapter One – Operations, Section 4(E), and Chapter Two – Students, Section 8(A); Rule 6A-3.0171 (3)(f), (3)(h), (3)(l), and (3)(m), F.A.C., Responsibilities of School Districts for Student Transportation.

 

There is “just cause” to support Raymond Berry’s termination of employment.

 

On June 2, 2021, the Superintendent's "Notice of Recommendation for Termination of Employment" was sent via Email and sent via Regular US and Certified Mail to Raymond Berry, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend the Board approve the Florida Virtual School 3 Year Agreement effective July 1, 2021 through June 30, 2024; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

In response to the growth in online education, Florida Virtual School developed the FLVS Franchise program. Through the Franchise, the District takes advantage of the courses and learning management system developed by Florida Virtual School and implements an online program. The Franchise is implemented through the District's virtual school, Palm Beach Virtual School. 

 

Palm Beach Virtual School operates like a traditional full-time school for students in grades K-12 seeking a standard Palm Beach County high school diploma. Additionally, Palm Beach Virtual School is used for part-time students who need flexibility in their schedule, to take courses for graduation, acceleration, grade forgiveness, or to raise their GPA. The Florida Virtual School Franchise Agreement for FY2021-2024 will allow the District to continue to provide virtual services for schools with particular emphasis on meeting high school graduation requirements.

AVID Excel is a middle school program intended to break the detrimental cycle that turns many English Language Learners (ELLs) into long-term ELLs. AVID Excel accelerates students’ academic language acquisition and puts them on a pathway to high school AVID and college readiness.

Approximately 1,379 middle school students were enrolled in the AVID elective in 2019-2020 Of those students: 42% completed Algebra 1 or higher math with a “C” or better 392 have taken an honors or high school credit-bearing course other than math.

 

On average, 20% of each AVID school's faculty have been trained in AVID’s strategies and methodologies at Summer Institutes, Path trainings, AVID Professional Learning Workshops, and AVID District Leader Trainings. A sustainable support system has been established, as the District now has five AVID Center Staff Developers.

Annual Comprehensive Safety Inspections were performed by Building Code Services Fire and Safety Inspectors, as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). All educational, auxiliary and ancillary facilities were included in these inspections, using the State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) for casualty and sanitation, and Florida Administrative Code Chapter 69A-58 for fire safety.

 

The number of citations for Public Schools, Alternative Schools and Ancillary Facilities decreased from 1,848 in 2020 to 1,097 in 2021. This represents a 41% decrease over one year.

 

The citations are identified by “type”

  • Type O = Operational. This is the responsibility of the school staff to correct.
  • Type M = Maintenance. This is the responsibility of the Maintenance Department to correct.
  • Type C = Capital. This is the responsibility of Facilities Construction to correct.
  • Serious Citations are identified with an added “S” to the Type

The number of citations is the lowest in over 20 years. The total number in each category (Capital, Operational and Maintenance) went down. The total number of Maintenance citations is also the lowest in over 20 years. Additional highlights include a reduction in the number of serious citations in each category. All conditions considered by the inspector to be hazardous or posing an imminent danger were reported to the Maintenance and Plant Operations Department at the time of discovery for immediate action.

 

A summary report by facility, detailed individual facility reports, and descriptive charts are attached. According to SREF, Chapter 5, the inspection reports shall be approved by the Board, and the Board shall adopt a plan and a schedule for the correction of each deficiency.

 

Upon the Board approving the inspection reports, copies of the reports will be submitted to the local fire protection agencies, as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(2). The Board hereby certifies the completion of the inspection report to the State Fire Marshal.
