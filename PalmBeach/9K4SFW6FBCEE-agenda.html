Definition – Students are college ready when they have the knowledge, skills, and behaviors to successfully complete a college course of study without remediation.



 



Why it is important to build a college readiness culture



 



Skills students need in order to be considered college ready – predictors of college success



 



Monitoring and measuring college readiness



·         EDW Reports for College Readiness; Potential Students for Postsecondary Readiness Report; Accelerated Participation in AP; List of Potential AP Students; AP District and School Summary; AP Current Year Scores by School



 



Strategies implemented to support college readiness



 



·         School Counselor Initiatives – College readiness professional development for all counselors K – 12; Evidenced Based School Counseling, book study; Effective school transitions at all educational levels; David Conley's Framework for College and Career Readiness; College Board's National Office for School Counselor Advocacy (NOSCA).



 



·         Community, State and National Initiatives – Own the Turf Campaign; FACTS.org; Dual Enrollment; ePEP; Realizing the College Dream; Believing the College Dream; Student Success Skills; National Office for School Counselor Advocacy (NOSCA)



 



·         AVID – accelerates student learning in rigorous courses, uses research based methods of effective instruction, provides meaningful and motivational professional development, acts as a catalyst for systemic reform



 



·         SpringBoard and Advanced Placement overview



 



·         General Suggestions for Expanding and Supporting Advanced Placement Programs – AP Potential; Instructional Planning Reports; Integrated Summary Reports; Increase students taking PSAT in 9th-10th grades; Expand AP courses offered ; College Board administrator training



 



·         Teacher Training and Support - AP, Pre-AP and Vertical Team Training; One-day AP seminars; AP Summer Institutes; AP Teacher Roundtables / Learning Communities; data chats; peer mentors; team teaching; AP Roundtables



 



·         Strategies for Student and Family Support – College Bound, AmeriCorps and Graduation Coaches; promote school-wide strategic college-preparatory activities to advance rigorous curricula for all students; College and Career Fair;  AP Parent Nights; Financial Aid workshops, College and Career Exploration workshops; expand AP course offerings; early identification of students needing support; AP tutoring program; summer enrichment programs and transition program; rigorous instruction in all grade levels and all classes; strong vertical articulation.



 



·         Partnerships for College Readiness – Palm Beach State College; Florida Atlantic University, The College Board; Take Stock in Children; Johnson Scholarship Foundation; ACT; Workforce Alliance; Parent Child Center, Inc.; Literacy Coalition; AmeriCorps
