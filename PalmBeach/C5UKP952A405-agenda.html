Item Added:

COS1 Memorandum of Understanding with Florida Department of Health

 

Good Cause Statement:

Good cause exists for adding this item as it will benefit the health, safety, and welfare of students, their families, and staff by providing two convenient locations and much needed COVID-19 testing availability for students, District staff, and their families.  On August 27, 2021, the Florida Department of Health contacted the District and offered to set up two drive-through COVID-19 rapid antigen testing sites on District property so that up to 1,000 COVID-19 rapid antigen tests could be conducted. The Florida Department of Health is prepared to set up COVID 19 testing beginning September 2, 2021, if Board approval is obtained. It is believed this will help alleviate some of the delay in getting students, staff and family members tested.

I recommend the Board approve the proposed Greater Florida Consortium of School Boards' 2022 Legislative Program as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

School Board Member Erica Whitfield represented Palm Beach County at the August 19-20, 2021 meeting of the Greater Florida Consortium of Schools Boards.

 

Attached is the cover letter explaining the adoption process and transmittal of the draft legislative program agreed to by representative school board members from the 11 school districts that make up the Consortium.  Palm Beach County's current concerns are reflected.

 

As noted in the attached Memorandum from Consortium President Bob Segur, the final version will contain only those proposals that receive 100 percent concurrence by the 11 district school boards.  A school board may vote to delete an item, but may not make any change to it.  The Consortium recommendations will be submitted to the Florida School Boards Association.
