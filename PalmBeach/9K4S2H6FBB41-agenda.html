The Storm: 1928 Remembered
Meeting
Sep 17, 2008 - Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action (Consent)

The Richard and Pat Johnson Palm Beach County History Museum is featuring the exhibit "The Storm: 1928 Remembered."  On the afternoon of September 16, 1928 a Great Hurricane (equal to a Category 4) struck Palm Beach County. The storm's strong winds damaged almost every building in its path and destroyed many of them.
 
Along the coast from Boca Raton to Jupiter, residents huddled in houses, schools, and stores in an effort to survive the storm as it raged throughout the afternoon and night. When the storm passed, coastal residents ventured outside to access the damage.



The seemingly endless piles of debris from buildings, trees, and boats were but a small part of the catastrophic damage to be found in the wake of the storm. Within days, the scope of the horror became clear as survivors from the Glades reached the coastal areas with reports of great flooding and horrendous loss of life.



The exhibition which runs from September 16, 2008 through January 16, 2009, features a post-storm diorama of Lake Okeechobee, numerous photographs, diaries and artifacts. A teacher's guide and lesson plan to accompany the exhibition is also available.

History/Situation





  •  Palm Beach Lakes High School is on a relatively small site (41 acres) with limited space.  (See Exhibit "C")


  • The FY 2008-2012 Five Year Plan includes the Palm Beach Lakes High School Addition and Academy scheduled to open 2009-2010.  This addition will be constructed on the school's only practice field leaving the school with no space for its various teams, band and others to practice.


  • The acquisition of the subject vacant 1.61± acre parcel is the only viable option the School District has to expand the campus.


Options






  • District staff has evaluated several campus design alternatives to provide the school with a practice field.  (See Exhibit "C")


  • Based on staff's analysis it was determined that the best option would be to acquire the subject property and construct a practice field for the school.  (See Exhibit "B")


Property Description





  • The property located south of 45th Street, West Palm Beach contains 1.61 ± acres and is adjacent to the Palm Beach Lakes High School campus. (See Exhibit "A")  This option involves two acquisitions and a Transfer of Title.


  • Between the 1.61 acre parcel needed for the practice field and the Palm Beach Lakes High School campus there are two strips of land.


  • Staff has a signed contract for the strip containing 0.83 acres at appraised value.  (See Board Agenda Item RE-3, September 17, 2008)


  • The other strip owned by Palm Beach County is located on the north side and is currently utilized as part of Palm Beach Lakes High School's Campus stadium facility.  (See Exhibit "A")


  • Staff is requesting Palm Beach County to transfer title of the strip of land to the School Board of Palm Beach County.


  • The acquisition of both strips eliminates the hiatus between Palm Beach Lakes High School Campus and the 1.61 acres.


  • The vacant 1.61 ± acre parcel is part of a larger improved parcel totaling 3.85± acres.  Located on the 3.85± acres is an established business with 45th Street frontage.


  • The owner has agreed to sell the vacant portion of this property to the School District.  However he feels strongly that by selling a portion of his property it diminishes the value of the whole parcel and therefore has only agreed to sell for a premium.


  • The averaged appraised value of the vacant 1.61 acre parcel is $822,500.00.  The contract price is $1,325,000.00.


  • The Real Estate Sale and Purchase Contract for the 1.61 acre is contingent upon the acquisition of the 0.83 acre strip and the Transfer of Title from Palm Beach County.


  • A Phase I and Phase II Environmental Audit have been conducted.  The Phase II concluded that no additional assessment is warranted.  Due to a small borrow pit on site a geotechnical suitability study is recommended prior to acquisition and construction.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Real Estate Sale and Purchase Contract for acquisition of a vacant property containing 1.61 acres, more or less, located on 45th Street; approve the acceptance of vacant property containing 1 acre, more or less, located south of 45th Street, West Palm Beach, which are needed for the Palm Beach Lakes High School Addition and Academy; and authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to sign all necessary documents.
