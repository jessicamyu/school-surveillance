DESCRIPTION:

Over 50,000 students nationwide have competed in the annual Academic Games National Tournament, since 1966.  This year, sixteen (16) Palm Beach County students representing seven different schools brought home National Championship awards from the National Tournament in Knoxville, Tennessee. The team awards included National Championships in Middle Division (grades 7-8) World Events; Junior Division (grades 9-10) Equations, LinguSHTIK, and Propaganda; and, Senior Division (grades 11-12) LinguiSHTIK.

 

Palm Beach County also has the Individual National Champion in Middle Division World Events, Robert Zipper from Eagles Landing Middle, and the Individual National Champion in Junior Division in Linguishtik, Ryan Childress from Suncoast High School.  In addition to the five teams and two individual National Championships, the Palm Beach County Academic Games All-Stars received numerous other awards.  Jessica Stone, Suncoast High School, was honored as an Outstanding Senior for her contributions to Academic Games during her many years of participation in the program.  Only seven high school seniors nationwide were honored this year at the National Tournament.

 

Academic Games tournaments challenge students to use higher order thinking skills in the subjects of Mathematics, Language Arts, and Social Studies.  Competition to qualify for the National Tournament began in September.  After a year of local tournaments and the state tournament in March, thirty-five students and eight coaches representing nineteen different schools qualified to represent Palm Beach County at the tournament.

COM9 Prevent Teen Pregnancy Month

DESCRIPTION:

In Palm Beach County during 2013, there were 684 births to teen mothers ages 15-19, and during that same year, one-sixth (114) of those births were to teen mothers who already had at least one child.  Less than half of mothers who have a child before they turn 18 ever graduate from high school, and less than two percent of mothers who have children before 18 have a college degree by age 30. Teen pregnancy is closely linked to a number of critical social issues such as poverty, educational attainment, involvement in criminal justice and child welfare systems, and more.  While the teen pregnancy rate in Palm Beach County has dropped 25 percent between 2011 and 2013 and the repeat pregnancy percentage has fallen nearly 4 percentage points in the same period, the vast majority of teens and adults agree that schools, medical providers, faith based and community organizations should be doing more to prevent teen pregnancy.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the renewal of contract resulting from the RFP 11C-002T for the purchase of Filter Replacement Services and Preventive Maintenance Air Handler Services from Precision Air Systems, Inc.  The renewal term of this contract is September 8, 2014 through September 7, 2016.

 

By Board action on August 31, 2012, the contract with Precision Air Systems, Inc. was approved to purchase Filter Replacement Services and Preventive Maintenance Air Handler Services. .

 

Services provided by Precision Air Systems, Inc. include but are not limited to:

  • District Wide Filter Replacement Services (estimated at 132,000 filters per year)
  • Preventive Maintenance Air Handler (AHU) Services with each visit
  • Quarterly inspections of damper blades and damper actuator motors
  • Semi-annual cleaning of inlet guide vanes, lubrication of mechanism on guide vanes
  • Inspection of variable frequency drive for air moving equipment
  • Annual cleaning of AHU coils according to manufacturer’s specifications and recommendations
  • A dedicated staff of  technicians to service the District
  • Coordination with schools to establish after school hours service calls
  • A dedicated storage facility to handle bulk capacity of filters committed to the District
  • Disposal of used filters following all Federal, State and Local Codes for such disposal
  • Monthly  reports regarding equipment deficiencies

 

Per Purchasing Department procedures, an industry standard review comparing similar bids from other governmental entities was conducted.  The review indicated that three agencies out-source this service while two of them perform in-house service. Research also showed that there is varying change out cycles among the agencies ranging from four times per year to six times per year, different requirements on air filter thicknesses and several of the agencies purchase only filter replacement services and forego the Preventive Maintenance Monthly Service.

 

The District’s Maintenance and Plant Operations Department conducted a study to identify cost comparison of an in-house model versus outsourcing for this service.  The evaluation concluded that bulk purchase of filters, storage of the filters and additional District staff required to replace the filters would cost approximately $1,770,000 annually without the added benefit of the Preventive Maintenance service to resolve issue before they get out of hand.

 

Additional negotiations with Precision Air Systems resulted in a 4 percent reduction in price per air filter and a $5 per hour reduction in labor rates for emergency services calls. It was also agreed that the previously negotiated $50,000 annual credit would remain for the term of this contract. This results in an annual spend of $1,540,000.

 

The Office of Diversity in Business Practices was notified and identified eight M/WBE vendors registered with the District.  All eight vendors were invited to participate in the RFP.  Only one chose to do so, as a sub-contractor to one of the primary vendors. Precision Air Systems, Inc. is an M/WBE vendor who has graduated the District’s program.

 

Precision Air Systems, Inc. is also awarded Term Contract 10C-92T, HVAC Mechanical Contractor Services and the spend for this vendor is defined as follows:

  • Spend with Precision Air Systems, Inc will not exceed $1,540,000 for FY15 under RFP 11C-002T
  • Spend with Precision Air Systems, Inc will not exceed $175,000 for FY15 under Term Contract 10C-92T, HVAC Mechanical Contractor Services
