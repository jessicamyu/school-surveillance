B2 Principal Autonomy Pilot Program Initiative
Meeting
Dec 14, 2016 - 4. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category

Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Principal Autonomy Pilot Program Initiative application for submission to the Florida Department of Education.

DESCRIPTION:

The Principal Autonomy Pilot Program Initiative (PAPPI) was established by the 2016 Legislature's passage of HB287.  The implementation statute (1011.6202 F.S.) provides an opportunity for highly effective principals of participating schools to operate their schools with increased autonomy and authority "in a way that produces significant improvements in student achievement and school management while complying with constitutional requirements."

 

Palm Beach is one of seven school districts eligible to participate in the pilot program for three years (FY18-FY20).  To participate in the pilot program, our District must identify three schools that received at least two school grades of "D" or "F" during the previous three school years along with three principals who have earned a highly effective rating on their prior year performance evaluation.  Based on this criteria, the District is proposing Orchard View Elementary, West Riviera Elementary, and Lake Shore Middle School participate in the pilot.

 

Participating schools are required to form a three-member leadership team and work with District personnel to complete a nationally recognized school turnaround program which focuses on improving leadership, instructional infrastructure, talent management, and differentiated support and accountability.  The District shall receive $100,000 from FDOE to cover the cost of the recognized school turnaround program and each participating school will receive an additional $10,000 to be used as a principal salary supplement or discretionary fund, or a combination thereof.

I recommend the Board approve the Stipulated Settlement Agreement with The Florida Department of Transportation regarding removal of a School Board-owned outdoor sign at approximately 3010 South Congress Avenue, Boynton Beach; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent/designee to finalize and execute all necessary and related settlement documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • Presently, the School Board owns an outdoor sign (“Sign”) indicating the location of South Tech Charter Academy, Inc. (“South Tech”), a conversion charter school, which is located on the northeast corner of Congress and SW 30th Avenue in Boynton Beach.  Palm Beach County has entered into a maintenance agreement with Florida Department of Transportation (“FDOT”) for Congress Avenue.  The Sign is in Palm Beach County's right of way for Congress Avenue and in the sight/visibility triangle.
  • District staff is unable to determine when this Sign was erected, however, staff believes that the Sign has been in place since the 1980s.  Likewise, staff does not have any records to establish that the Sign ever was permitted by either the City of Boynton Beach or Palm Beach County.
  • On March 20, 2013, FDOT issued a Notice of Violation – Illegally Erected Sign to South Tech Charter Academy, Inc.
  • In June of 2016, FDOT resumed its enforcement action and contacted the Office of General Counsel.  Thereafter, on September 8, 2016, FDOT referred the Notice of Violation to the Division of Administrative Hearings (“DOAH”), where it was assigned DOAH Case No. 16-5158. 

​The following are essential elements of the settlement:

  • The School Board will remove the Sign on or before May 2, 2017. The scope of the removal will consist of removal and disposal of concrete foundation, add compacted fill, disassemble and dispose of sign (preserving panels), removal of electric meter and power drop from FPL, repair sidewalk and sod, maintenance of traffic costs during crane time, permitting with City and FDOT.
  • FDOT will voluntarily withdraw its enforcement action against School Board and South Tech related to the Sign and the Parties will waive their rights to appeal any decisions regarding this matter.
  • The Settlement Agreement document resolves all outstanding claims and all underlying issues between all Parties.  Upon completion of the work by the School Board, FDOT will voluntarily dismiss its enforcement action against South Tech.
  • Internal staff and existing contractors with continuing contracts will be used to complete the work which is estimated to cost between $13,000 - $15,000.
  • South Tech does not object the proposed removal of the sign, and its Board will formerly consider approval of the Stipulation at its December 8, 2016 meeting.
  • The Office of General Counsel recommends the approval of this Settlement to avoid the costs of the DOAH Hearing and the potential that at the conclusion of the DOAH Hearing the Board would be ordered to remove the Sign.

DESCRIPTION:

  • Serving lines and kitchen will include partition walls, digital menu boards, decorative lighting, additional support equipment, security gates, and layout changes.

After reviewing the selection criteria and data for the reengineering program, School Food Service reached out to the Principal Ed Tierney of John I. Leonard High School. JILHS is currently the highest populated school in the District. Increased lunch participation is one key element of the program’s success.  Current student participation in the lunch program at JILHS is 44% overall with only 49% of their free and reduced students participating. This significant deficit in participation, coupled with the school’s large population, provides an excellent opportunity to positively impact student participation in the meal program and improve the school’s overall appeal.

 

With these modifications, students will enter a more relaxing, inviting, social, safe, and customer-driven dining experience, creating an opportunity for increased student participation.  Therefore, they will look forward to this time to nutritionally recharge for improved physical and mental well-being; supporting the District’s goal of improving academic performance.

DESCRIPTION:

Section 218.391, F.S., establishes procedures for the School Board's selection of an external auditing firm to perform the annual financial audits of the District required by Sec. 218.39, F.S.  This statute establishes duties for the governing bodies of the entities and their audit committees.

 

Subsection (4) of Section 218.391, F.S., establishes the duties of the School Board to:

  • Inquire with qualified firms as to the basis of compensation.  See Subsec. (4).
  • Select one of the firms recommended by the Audit Committee.  See Subsec. (4).
  • Negotiate a contract using one method if compensation is one of the factors used to evaluate the firms, or another method if compensation is not a factor used to evaluate the firms.  See Subsec. (4)(a) and (b)
  • Appoint a designee to conduct negotiations on its behalf.  See Subsec. (4)(d).

The duties of the Audit Committee relative to the auditor selection process include:

  • Establishment of factors to be used for the evaluation of audit services to be provided by the audit firm.
  • Public announcement of an RFP.
  • Provision of interested firms with the RFP.
  • Evaluation of proposals provided by qualified firms.
  • Ranking and recommendation in order of preference of no fewer than three firms deemed to be the most highly qualified to perform the required services.  If fewer than three firms respond to the RFP, the committee shall recommend such firms as it deems to be the most highly qualified. See Sec. 218.391(3)(a)-(e), F.S.

At its November 18, 2016 meeting, the Audit Committee reviewed and approved a request for proposals, RFP No. 17C-009L (Request for Proposals for External Independent Auditing Services).  The request indicates the District will attempt to use the following time schedule for selection of the audit firm.

December 8, 2016        Deadline for submission of questions at 5:00 PM.

December 21, 2016      Proposals due no later than 2:00 PM EST.

January 19, 2017         Evaluation Committee Meeting

January 23, 2017         Oral Presentation (If required)

February 3, 2017         Posting of Recommendation(s)

March 15, 2017           Recommend proposers to School Board for approval
