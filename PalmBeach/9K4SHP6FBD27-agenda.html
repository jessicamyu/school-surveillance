B1 - Education Jobs Fund Budget Amendment
Meeting
Apr 27, 2011 - Special Meetings
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

The School District of Palm Beach County is eligible to receive $38,091,758 in Federal Education Jobs Funds (EJF).  Of this amount, $1,976,621 must be made available to eligible charter schools.  That leaves $36,115,137 for district operated schools.  The EJF program requires that Local Education Authorities (LEA) or school districts use the funds to pay the salaries and benefits of teachers, school administrators, and other essential school-based staff.  These grant funds may only be used for school based salary and fringe benefit costs within FY2011 or FY2012.  The Florida Department of Education (FDOE) has recommended school districts utilize these funds in FY2011 to avoid any possible loss of funds resulting from budget actions by the Federal government.  Further, FLDOE advised staff to expend the EJF dollars in FY2011 and reserve a like amount in local general fund dollars if it is the district's intent to utilize these funds for the FY2012 budget.



To execute this transaction, $36,115,137 in existing FY2011 expenditures for teacher salaries and benefits will be transferred out of the general fund operating into the special revenue EJF budget.  The general fund budget made available through this transfer will be placed in a reserve and carried forward to FY2012 for the School Board's budget deliberations.



The Federal supplement/not supplant provisions do not apply to EJF at the LEA level, but the state is required to document maintenance of effort (MOE).  This budget amendment is fully compliant with all State and Federal grant requirements.  The proposed budget amendment will be completed upon Board approval and reported to the School Board as part of the April Budget Amendment agenda item scheduled for the regular meeting in June.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the transfer of $36,115,137 in FY2011 teacher salaries and benefit expenditures from the general fund operating budget to the Federal Education Jobs Fund special revenue budget, and place the general fund dollars freed up by this transfer in a reserve for FY2012.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



Michael J. Burke burkem@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



 

Financial Impact:

This expenditure transfer will increase year-end fund balance in the general fund operating budget by $36,115,137.  An equal expenditure will occur in the FY2011 special revenue other budget.  This transfer will fully expend the District's Education Jobs Fund allocation.

A Memorandum of Understanding has been negotiated with and approved by the Palm Beach County Classroom Teachers Association (CTA) that amends, by addition, Article VIII, Section B 8 of the parties’ Collective Bargaining Agreement effective April 12, 2011.  This amendment establishes a special provision for placement of retired teachers on the Teachers’ Salary Schedule who are employed by the District on or after April 12, 2011.



 



"Instructional personnel employed pursuant to Florida Statutes Section 121.091 (9) (b) and (c) (i.e. employed or re-employed retired personnel from Florida or elsewhere)….will be placed on Step 7 of the Teachers’ Salary Schedule on the effective date of their employment and will advance a step(s) when all other instructional employees advance a step(s) on such salary schedule."



 



In addition, the District agrees to post on its compensation website information that reflects that retired instructional employees who are employed by the District on or after April 12, 2011 will be placed on Step 7 of the Teachers’ Salary Schedule.

P1 - RFP 11C-008D for Medical Claims Adjudication

Apr 27, 2011 - Special Meetings
Category
Consent Agenda

Action (Consent)

P1 Approve the Request for Proposal, RFP 11C-008D for Medical Claims Adjudication that was solicited and returned with five proposals. 





  • The Evaluation Committee consisted of District personnel including M/WBE and Union representatives.  The Committee reviewed the proposals and evaluated them based on the criteria outlined in the RFP.  The criteria included: Wellness Program Support, Disease Management, Provider Disruption, Cost of Services, Scope of Services, Provider Discounts, Minority/Women Business Participation and Performance Guarantees.

  • After discussing the criteria, the Committee ranked United Healthcare highest with 90.6 points and Cigna second with 84.78 points.   The committee requested that negotiation be opened with United Healthcare and if negotiations were not successful, move to the next highest ranked proposer.

  • United Healthcare initially proposed a $29.85 fixed cost per employee per month.  This proposal term was for the first 3 years of the contract with a 3% increase for years four and five.  After negotiations this five year administrative agreement was reduced to a fixed cost of $27 per employee per month for the first 3 years with a $1 per month per employee packaged saving discount for utilizing United Healthcare's Dental services.  The costs for years four and five would increase by 3%.  Including the Dental discount the costs will be $26.00 per employee per month for the first three years.

  • The contract continues with two onsite United Healthcare representatives as well as United Healthcare funding a district wellness position and support of our Diabetes prevention program.  The contract adds new programs as well as an annual $150,000 allowance for the various wellness initiatives and an onsite nurse to work with employees on disease management programs.

  • 100% pharmacy rebates will continue to be returned to the district.

  • Increased performance guarantees are included


 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the five year Administrative Services Agreement with United Healthcare for Health Care Administration and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore, Chief Operating Officer (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Dianne Howard, Director of Risk and Benefits Management



 

Financial Impact:

The estimated financial impact to the operating budget is $7,128,000 per year for each of the first three years, $7,570,567 for year four and $7,806,155 for year five.  The term of the Agreement is from January 1, 2012 through December 31, 2016.  The source of funds is the Internal Services Fund.

Apr 27, 2011 - Special Meetings

A Memorandum of Understanding has been negotiated with CTA and approved on April 25, 2011 by the Palm Beach County Classroom Teachers Association’s Board of Directors that amends Article II, Section G of the parties’ Collective Bargaining Agreement effective April 27, 2011.  This amendment establishes a special Negotiations Committee comprised of no less than six appointees from the District and no less than six appointees from CTA to immediately start negotiations on a new teacher evaluation instrument/procedures to replace the current CTAS Evaluation Handbook as well as the provisions of Article II, Section G of the CBA for all T-bargaining unit employees in keeping with all applicable State Statutes and/or Florida Department of Education’s directives, policies, rules and/or regulations for required implementation on July 1, 2011, and as may be further required thereafter.



 



In addition, the MOU provides for:



 



1)     An ad hoc advisory committee to provide recommendations and feedback to the special Negotiations Committee.  Such ad hoc committee to be comprised of representatives of the educational stakeholders in the County such as the County Council of PTAs, the Principals’ Association, the Education Foundation, the Economic Council, past Dwyer Award winners, etc. (This list is not all inclusive.)



2)      A deadline of June 15, 2011 at which time the parties agree to declare a negotiations impasse should they fail to reach a tentative agreement by that date.  The parties agree that if impasse is declared, both parties will waive the appointment of a Special Magistrate so that the School Board, acting as the Legislative body, may resolve the impasse per FS 447.403.



3)    The District agrees to provide TDEs for all members of the special Negotiations Committee when negotiation sessions are scheduled and conducted during employee duty time.



4)      Either party may invite consultants who, because of their expertise, can assist the parties negotiate a new Teacher Evaluation Instrument/procedures and Article II, Section G of the CBA.



           

Recommendation:

Good cause to bring this MOU forward for adoption at this time is because of the recent change in legislative requirements, upcoming DOE deadlines for approval of evaluation provisions and the recent CTA agreement.



 



I recommend approval of the attached Memorandum of Understanding that amends the Collective Bargaining Agreement between the District and CTA as stated therein.
