Special Meeting RE: New Suncoast – T/A Riviera Limited Partnership Stipulated Final Judgment
Meeting
Oct 6, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action

The FY 2009-2013 Five Year Plan includes the construction of a New Suncoast High School, which opened in August, 2010.



 



Due to the construction of the New Suncoast High School, certain road improvements on Congress Avenue, 13th Street and Jake Lane are required to meet Palm Beach County's and the City of Riviera Beach's Traffic Standards.  Acquisition of the Temporary Use Parcels or Temporary Construction Easements is required to successfully complete the required road improvements.



 



On February 3, 2010 the School Board approved Resolution No. 02032010RE2 authorizing the acquisition of Temporary Construction Easements through eminent domain proceedings.  On March 15, 2010, the School Board’s attorney filed an eminent domain petition to acquire the necessary interests.



 



Through negotiations with one of the owners, T/A Riviera Limited Partnership, an agreement on the amount of compensation to be paid to T/A Riviera Limited Partnership for the Temporary Construction Easement was reached.



 



The School District's latest appraisal of the parcel is $5,202.00.  This amount was deposited into the registry of the Court following the issuance of the Order of Taking.  T/A Riviera Limited Partnership has agreed to settle for the amount of the good faith deposit that is held in the registry of the Court.



 

Settlement is recommended as being in the best financial interest of the School District.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Stipulated Final Judgment between T/A Riviera Limited Partnership and the School Board of Palm Beach County in the case of the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida v.  Marsh Harbour Associates Ltd., Case No. 502010CA007232XXXXMBAO, regarding the acquisition of a Temporary Construction Easement over 4,729 square feet, more or less; and authorize the Chief Counsel to execute all related documents.

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Interim Chief Counsel



Blair R. LittleJohn, Esq.; Senior Counsel

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is $5,202.00 from the Capital Project's Budget Fund.

Special Meeting re: Authorization to File Suit – 1325 Gateway L.L.C.
Meeting
Oct 6, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action (Consent)

Survivors Charter School, Inc. (“Survivors”) entered into an Lease Agreement dated January 21, 2003 (“Lease”) with 1325 Gateway L.L.C. (“1325 Gateway”), in which 1325 Gateway leased the building located at 1325 Gateway Boulevard, Boynton Beach, Florida ("Premises") to Survivors.



 



At the inception of the Lease, Survivors paid 1325 Gateway a $50,000.00 security deposit and a last month’s rent payment in the amount of $55,675.00.



 



Following the School Board’s termination of Survivors’ Charter School Agreement in January 2006, 1325 Gateway requested that the School Board provide assurance as to the School Board’s willingness to assume the Lease during the pendency of the Survivors’ appeal and, in the event the School Board prevailed (which it did), for the remaining term of the Lease.



 



The School Board and 1325 Gateway entered into a Conditional Acceptance and Assumption of Lease in February 2006 and the School Board fulfilled all of its obligations under the Lease until it expired on July 31, 2010.



 



Even though the last month’s rent was paid in advance by Survivors at the inception of the Lease, 1325 Gateway still billed the School Board for rent in July 2010, and through an oversight, the invoice was processed and paid.



 



Upon discovery of the double payment of July 2010 rent, the staff requested that the second payment be returned and that the security deposit, less any mutually agreed upon deductions be returned to the School Board.  1325 Gateway refused to return the overpayment and any portion of the security deposit and has taken the position that these funds were forfeited when the School Board assumed the Lease from Survivors.



 



The suit will seek to force 1325 Gateway to return the second payment of July 2010 rent and whatever portion of the security deposit is determined due.



 



If outside counsel is deemed necessary by the Chief Counsel, the contract to handle the matter will be brought to the Board.



 

The Lease has a “prevailing party” provision, so the staff intends to seek recovery for its costs and attorney’s fees against 1325 Gateway.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board authorize the Chief Counsel to file suit and take all appropriate action, including hiring experts, and the authority to settle the matter subject to the approval of the Superintendent, against 1325 Gateway L.L.C. in the 15th Judicial Circuit in and for Palm Beach County, Florida; and authorize the Chairman, Superintendent and Chief Counsel to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Interim Chief Counsel



Blair R. LittleJohn, Esq; Senior Counsel



Barry Present

Financial Impact:

A judgment in favor of the School Board, if successful should exceed the litigation expenses.  The exact amount cannot be determined at this time.
