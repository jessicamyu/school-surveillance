I recommend the Board approve the Adult Education and Family Literacy Integrated English Literacy/Civics Grant from The Florida Department of Education.

DESCRIPTION:

The Adult Education and Family Literacy Integrated English Literacy/ Civics Grant is a grant in the amount of $481,188. The program performance dates are July 1, 2018 to June 30,2019.

 

The priorities of this grant are to prepare adults who are English Language Learners (ELL) for employment and places such as adults in unsubsidized employment, within high demand industries and occupations that lead to economic self-sufficiency; and Integrate our adult education program with local workforce development system and it's functions to carry out the activities of the program.

 

Adult Education programs are provided at 26 adult and community schools, one full time adult education center, one adult virtual academy and numerous satellite programs located throughout Palm Beach County. These programs are offered at various times throughout the day and evening in order to accommodate student needs and schedules.

 

Student progress will be measured by tracking student movement from one Educational Functioning Level (EFL) to the next in a given content area during the project year based on the results from an assessment that has been approved by the USDOE and FLDOE and administered in a pre- and post-test. It also includes students who attained a high school diploma or equivalent.

The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

I recommend the Board authorize the General Counsel to file an appeal of the State Board of Education’s Final Order with the Fourth District Court of Appeal, pursuant to section 1002.33(6)(d), Florida Statutes.

DESCRIPTION:

On December 10, 2014, the School Board met and voted to deny the application of Florida Charter Educational Foundation, Inc. (“FCEF”), for South Palm Beach Charter School. FCEF filed its appeal with the State Board of Education in January of 2015. The State Board ultimately entered an order on April 23, 2015, overturning the School Board’s decision. The School Board appealed that order to the Fourth District Court of Appeal, and the Fourth District Court reversed the State Board’s decision and remanded for a rehearing of the cases by the Charter School Appeal Commission and the State Board of Education. Sch. Bd. of Palm Beach Cty. v. Fla. Charter Educ. Found., Inc., 213 So. 3d 356 (Fla. 4th DCA 2017).

 

The Charter School Appeal Commission met on June 13, 2018 and heard the appeal. The Commission voted to recommend that the State Board of Education grant FCEF’s appeal. The State Board of Education Appeal hearing regarding the School Board’s denial of FCEF’s application was then held on July 18, 2018.
