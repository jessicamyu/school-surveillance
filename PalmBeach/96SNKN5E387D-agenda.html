11.1 CS1 South Tech Preparatory Academy - New Five Year Contract
Meeting
Apr 24, 2013 - 4) Special Meeting - following Workshop
Category
11. Consent Agenda - Academics
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
1. I recommend that the School Board approve the five (5) year Charter Contract with South Tech Preparatory Academy and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.
2. I further recommend that the School Board approve the Joinder and Consent to the Sublease Agreement between South Tech Academy and South Tech Preparatory Academy (the "Sublease") and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The application filed by South Tech Preparatory Academy, Inc. stated that the name of the school would be South Tech Preparatory Academy to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2013.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on December 12, 2012, as Item CS1 during a Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel's office.

 

South Tech Preparatory Academy will serve students in grades 6-8.

 

The mission of South Tech Preparatory Academy is, "To graduate students prepared to matriculate into a career academy program of study which will prepare them for work, higher education, and productive citizenship.

 

The School Board of Palm Beach County ("School Board") is the owner of the property at 1300 S.W. 30th Ave., Boynton Beach, Florida, currently leased to and occupied by South Tech Academy.  The School Board joins in and consents to the Sublease Agreement between South Tech Academy and South Tech Preparatory Academy (the "Sublease"), and further agrees that the execution of the Sublease shall not be considered an event of default, notwithstanding any provision prohibiting subleases, or other uses of the Real Property that may exist in the Charter School Agreement between the School Board and South Tech Academy.

 

Attached are copies of the application, full Charter Contract, budget, legal checklist and Joinder and Consent for South Tech Preparatory Academy.

 

CONTACT:

Cheryl Alligood, Chief Academic Officer - cheryl.alligood@palmbeachschools.org

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.

Faith-Ann Cheek, Ed.D.

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

South Tech Preparatory Academy is projecting to serve about 264 students in grade 8 for FY 2014, which represents:

  1. A loss of revenue to the District of approximately $1,384,827 (net of the five (5%) percent administrative fee).
  2. A loss of District teacher positions of 12 (based on the projected enrollment divided by the average class size requirements.

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board of Palm Beach County approve the proposed Corrective Action Plan and First Amendment to the My Choice Academy Charter Contract between My Choice Academy, Inc. and the School District of Palm Beach County and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

At the Special Board Meeting held on March 6, 2013, the Superintendent recommended to terminate My Choice Academy's Charter Contract.

 

The Board did not vote to terminate the charter and it was stated that the school be allowed to enter into a CAP with the District to remedy the issues presented by staff.

 

Staff has developed the attached proposed CAP and First Amendment to the My Choice Academy Charter.

 

Staff has communicated through the school's attorney to review the proposed CAP and First Amendment.

 

The school has agreed to all of the provisions proposed in the CAP and First Amendment.

 

Copies of My Choice Academy, Inc.'s Application, Charter Contract, proposed CAP and First Amendment, and Board Notification Letter are attached.

 

MY CONTACT:

Cheryl Alligood (cheryl.alligood@palmbeachschools.org)

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.

Faith-Ann Cheek, Ed.D.

 

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

My Choice Academy, Inc. currently owes the District $59,583,* based on the actual FTE compared to the revenue paid through January, 2013.  Since the school longer has a facility, students have enrolled in either their District home school or another charter school, and the school had no students for the February survey period.

 

*  Please note the $59,583 overpaid amount may change and be adjusted slightly as the District updates this figure to reflect subsequent calculations of the FY2013 FEFP by the Florida Department of Education.
