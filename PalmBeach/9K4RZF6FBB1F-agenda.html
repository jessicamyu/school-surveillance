Meeting

§      The Johnson Scholars Program is a scholarship program that targets underserved populations in five urban Palm Beach County high schools and provides targeted interventions to place the students on the pathway to graduation and college success.



§      Students will be selected from the following high schools:  Lake Worth, Palm Beach Lakes, Palm Beach Gardens, Santaluces, and William T. Dwyer.



§      The Johnson Scholars Program will work in conjunction with the Picower College Success Program that is funded by the Picower Foundation.



§      A new cohort of students will be identified annually at each of the five schools, creating a cycle of preparation and awards that will reach full capacity within four years.

AC5-Renewal of Florida Virtual School Franchise Agreement
Meeting
Jun 4, 2008 - Special Meeting re Curriculum
Category
New Business
Type
Discussion

Florida Virtual School was founded in 1997 as the State's first internet-based public high school. Today, FLVS serves both middle and high school students, providing over 90 courses based upon the Sunshine State Standards and 10 Advanced Placement courses. It is an established leader in developing and providing virtual K-12 education to students within Florida, using a nationally recognized e-Learning model.



As the need and demand for online learning grew, Florida Virtual School established the FLVS franchise program. Establishing a franchise program meant that Districts did not need to start from the beginning to develop an online program. As a franchise, the Districts could take advantage of the courses and learning management system already developed by Florida Virtual School and implement the successful instructional model already in use, using local educators.



In May, 2007, the Board approved the District's participation in the FLVS franchise program for FY08. The franchise was implemented through the District's virtual school, Palm Beach Virtual Community School. Using the data provided by Florida Virtual School for District students, PBVCS offered the top six courses District high school students were taking through FLVS.



At the present time, the District is serving its own high school students and is used for students who need flexibility in their scheduling, to take additional courses for graduation, for credit recovery, acceleration, grade forgiveness or to raise their GPA. They are enrolled in the virtual program after consultation with and approval by their school guidance counselor and their parent/guardian.



The renewal of the FLVS franchise for FY09 will allow the District to continue the franchise and broaden the opportunities for students taking virtual education courses through Palm Beach Virtual Community School.
