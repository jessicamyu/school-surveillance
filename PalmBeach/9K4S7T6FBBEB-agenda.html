The FY 2009-2013 Five Year Plan includes the construction of a New Suncoast High School, scheduled for opening in August, 2010.



 



Due to the construction of the New Suncoast High School, certain road improvements on Congress Avenue, 13th Street and Jake Lane are required to meet Palm Beach County's and the City of Riviera Beach's Traffic Standards.  Acquisition of the Temporary Use Parcels or Temporary Construction Easements is required to successfully complete the required road improvements.



 



On February 3, 2010 the School Board approved Resolution No. 02032010RE2 authorizing the acquisition of Temporary Construction Easements through eminent domain proceedings.  On March 15, 2010, the School Boardâ€™s attorney filed an eminent domain petition to acquire the necessary interests.



 



Through negotiations with one of the owners, FF Landlord #3, and their tenant, Flowers Baking and the mortgage holder, Wells Fargo, an agreement to acquire a License Agreement instead acquire a Temporary Construction Easement was reached.



 



The School District's latest appraisal of the parcel is $1,355.  The owner, tenant and mortgage holder have agreed to settle for $1,355 plus actual attorney's fees incurred in the amount of $7,787 in negotiating the settlement.



 



Settlement is recommended as being in the best financial interest of the School District due to the anticipated costs of the litigation and litigation risk inherent in such an eminent domain proceeding.



 

In the event that the School Board rejects the proposed settlement, the School Board and owner and tenant will prepare for the Order of Taking hearing scheduled for May 7, 2010 and the owner and tenant will likely contest the value of the interest.  The School Board's attorney's fees and the expert witness costs of both sides will ultimately be borne by the School Board.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Stipulated Order of Taking and Final Judgment between FF Landlord #3 LLC, Wells Fargo Bank Northwest, N.A., as Trustee and Flowers Baking Co. of Miami, LLC and the School Board of Palm Beach County in the case of the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida v.  Marsh Harbour Associates LTD. regarding the acquisition of a License Agreement over 968 square feet, more or less; and authorize the School Board attorney to execute all related documents.
