Samantha Kerker, a senior at Atlantic High School, was named one of America's top 10 youth volunteers of the year by The Prudential Spirit of Community Awards. Selected from a field of more than 26,000 youth volunteers across the country, she received a $5,000 award, an engraved gold medallion, and a crystal trophy for her school. She also selected a nonprofit charitable organization to receive a $5,000 grant from The Prudential Foundation.



Samantha founded a student club with chapters in all Palm Beach County public high schools to promote monthly service projects benefiting poor people. Inspired by local university students and faculty who lost their lives in Haiti's devastating earthquake while aiding the poor, Samantha used $7,000 from her own nonprofit tie-dye business to build a home for a homeless family in Nicaragua. When she returned home, Samantha told her friends about her experience, and realized how little they knew about the lives of the poor.



Samantha proposed starting a "Students for the Poor" club in every high school in the county. She developed a mission statement and met with School District officials and high school principals.  More than 450 students signed up at Atlantic High, making it the largest club in school history. There are also more than 1,000 students currently involved throughout the county. Each month, club members partner with local community service organizations to sponsor an event or undertake another project to make a difference for people living in poverty.

  4.   6. The buyer will make an $80,500 deposit to an escrow account on behalf of the District.

  7. The buyer has 90 days from the effective date of the agreement to conduct the typical due diligence inspections of the property. The buyer may terminate the agreement if it timely notifies the District prior to the end of the inspection period. If the buyer does not timely terminate the agreement the $80,500 becomes non-refundable.

  8. Closing is scheduled for April 1, 2013.

  9. In addition, the buyer will be responsible for all due diligence costs and all closing costs.

  10. The School District saved an estimated $200,000.00 in commission by staff marketing and negotiating in house.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve a Resolution declaring the +/- 153 acre Former Burt Reynolds Ranch property surplus and approve the Real Estate Sale and Purchase Agreement between the Jupiter Land Preservation Alliance LLC and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the +/- 153 acres, more or less, located at 16133 Jupiter Farms Road, Jupiter, Florida; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Florida Statute 1012.98 requires that districts develop and submit a professional development system for approval by the Department of Education.

The Professional Development System is created in cooperation with District stakeholders such as administrative personnel, instructional personnel, and support personnel.

This renewal Agreement with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. provides $2,696,452 to the School District for the period effective October 1, 2012, through September 30, 2013, for the continuation of the Quality Counts (QC) Early Learning Coaches program.  The coaching program is one of a number of components that work together to create a comprehensive, voluntary early care and education quality rating improvement system for Palm Beach County.  The system partners work together to provide services to enhance the quality of early education programs so that children will enter school eager and ready to learn.  The Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County and a number of stakeholders initiated the Quality Counts System.  System partners include Children’s Services Council, Palm Beach State College, Palm Beach County Early Learning Coalition, Family Central, the Children's Forum and the School District.



This grant funds 31 Early Learning Coaches (certified teachers) and related support/administrative staff to serve child care centers and school-based sites that voluntarily participate.  To be eligible to participate, child care centers must have been licensed for at least one year and must hold a rate agreement with Family Central.  School District classrooms offering the Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) program are participants.   



The Quality Counts coaches assist with the implementation of improvement plans and strategies developed though collaborations between classroom teachers, child care directors, and/or other administrative supports. Plans are based on data from formal and informal assessments.  Highly trained outside assessors administer the assessments.  Multiple assessment measures are used, including but not limited to, the Early Childhood Environment Rating Scale (ECERS-R) and the Infant/Toddler Environment Rating Scale (ITERS), both nationally recognized, research-based assessment tools.  The assessments provide for the award of a “Star Rating,” ranging from 1 to 5 stars.  The lowest rating, a 1-Star rating, equates to the attainment of current state and local child care licensing standards.



Coaching supports are provided using a three-level, consultation approach, based on an evidence-based coaching model developed by Pat Wesley, a research scientist at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill.



The Quality Counts System currently has 124 participating private child care centers.  The District is both a partner and a voluntary participant and most participating school-based sites have earned the highest possible star rating.

Florida Atlantic University (FAU) was awarded a National Center for Education Research Grant to study the impact of the Student Success Skills (SSS) Program on academic achievement.  Originally awarded to FAU in August 2011, continued management of the four-year project will be through Florida State University (FSU).  The School District's participation in this project began with Board approval of a one-year Agreement on December 14, 2011.  The grant period is from December 15, 2011 through July 31, 2015. 



The SSS Program was developed to systematically teach students fundamental learning, social, and self-management skills that have a demonstrated a link to improved academic achievement outcomes.  It is based on a strong body of theoretical and empirical research and uses developmentally appropriate student lessons, activities, and teaching strategies.  The purpose of the project is to allow for rigorous research to evaluate both the proximal and distal outcomes resulting from large group participation in SSS.







  • Year 1: The year one planning stage is complete with 100% of the 13 performance indicators being achieved.  Grant funding provided for the hiring of a School Counseling IES Grant Specialist.   Elementary school principals were informed about the project and given the opportunity to participate.  The grant goal of including thirty schools in the IES-SSS project was met. 




  • Year 2: In the implementation stage 15 intervention schools, approximately 20 school counselors, and 150 teachers will receive staff development, curriculum materials, and guidance on using the SSS Program, with an expected benefit to 1,800 grade five students.  School counselors will implement the SSS curriculum and grade five teachers will encourage students to apply SSS strategies during core instruction. 




  • Year 3: In year three of the project, the remaining 15 control schools, school counselors, and grade five teachers will be invited to receive staff development, SSS materials and continued specialist support, with an expected benefit to additional students.




  • Year 3 and 4: In years three and four of the project, schools, school counselors and grade five teachers will receive continued support to implement SSS.  Final study results will be reported to local and regional stakeholders in year four.

The Florida Educational Equity Act, Florida Statutes, Section 1000.05, became law in June 1984 and the State Board of Education adopted the implementing rules in February 1985.



Each year the Commissioner of Education designates the areas of concentration under the requirements of the Educational Equity Act.



The 2011-2012 Plan Update is used to evaluate the success of methods and strategies to reduce gaps in student performance and to increase participation of underrepresented groups by race/ethnicity and gender in upper level courses.



Grade 8, Algebra I  The review reveals a decrease in student enrollment spanning the 5 year period reported in the 2011-2012 Equity update.  In 2006-2007, the District encouraged the enrollment of 100% of Grade 8 students in Algebra I, an initiative that proved to be unsuccessful.  Subsequently, the District has enrolled Grade 8 students in Algebra I based on student/skill readiness, thus the percentage of Grade 8 students enrolled in Algebra I has declined over the past five years.  The evaluation, however, reveals progress in closing the gap between student groups.  In addition, with the implementation of the more rigorous Next Generation Sunshine State Standards, in 2011-2012 fewer students were identified as meeting readiness for Algebra I and were enrolled in pre-algebra/algebra readiness curriculum in Grade 8 to prepare students for Algebra I in Grade 9.  A new EDW report has been developed to assist school staff in identifying students demonstrating readiness that are not currently enrolled in Grade 8 Algebra I.  This new report, combined with a continuation of strategies designed to increase the number of students demonstrating the necessary readiness skills for Grade 8 Algebra I will be utilized during the 2012-2013 school year.



Advanced Placement  The review reveals an increase in student enrollment spanning the 5 year period reported in the 2011-2012 Equity update.  The review reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Advanced Placement courses for White, Black and Hispanic male students in grades 9-12 over this same period.  A continuation of previous strategies and the use of the AP Potential report designed to identify students (particularly targeting underrepresented students) for enrollment in AP courses will be in place for the 2012-2013 school year.  Additionally, a new district report was created to identify whether students in AP Potential were registered for the appropriate AP courses.



Dual Enrollment  The review reveals an increase in student enrollment spanning the 5 year period reported in the 2011-2012 Equity update.  A continuation of previous strategies as well as the following additions will be in place for the 2012-2013 school year:





  • All 11th Grade students will be provided with a school-day SAT test administration in the spring of 2013 .

  • Students will be provided with free access to an online college course through CollegeBoard to prepare for the SAT.

  • Expand the Historically Black Colleges and Universities Recruitment Tour from 2 to 3 days, to include high schools with a high percentage of Hispanic students.  Three of the major Historically Black Colleges and Universities in Florida now have Hispanic recruiters and requested this opportunity with our Hispanic students.


Honors Courses  The review reveals an increase in student enrollment spanning the 5 year period reported in the 2011-2012 Equity update.  A continuation of previous strategies will be in place for the 2012-2013 school year.



Level 3 Courses  The review reveals an increase in student enrollment spanning the 5 year period reported in the 2011-2012 Equity update.  A continuation of previous strategies will be in place as well as the following:





  • identify middle school and high school students particularly underrepresented populations demonstrating the readiness skills to be successful in level 3 courses and monitor their enrollment.

  • stress the importance of enrolling in level 3 courses in high school to parents and students in order to be college ready.


Gender Equity in Athletics  The review reveals that the School District of Palm Beach County is in compliance with the requirements of Gender Equity in Athletics.  A continuation of previous strategies will be in place for the 2012-2013 school year.



Employment Equity  The review reveals that there has been an increase in the percentage of Black and Hispanic candidates hired.  A continuation of previous strategies and the recent creation of a District Diversity and Equity Committee which includes participation from community stakeholders will be in place for the 2012-2013 school year.



Progress monitoring of the above listed sections will occur monthly in the Academic Leadership Meetings.



The requirements of the report are included in the executive summary section.



A copy of the complete report has been placed in the Board Office for your review.

I recommend that the School Board approve the District's Florida Educational Equity Act 2011-2012 Annual Update for submission to the Florida Department of Education.
