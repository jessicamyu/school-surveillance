DA 3 - Christine Michelcic - Personnel Disciplinary Action
Meeting
May 2, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action
On or about January 24, 2012, Ms. Michelcic became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of Failure to Protect the Student from Conditions Harmful to Learning; Ethical Misconduct; Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment; and Failure to Follow Policy/Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

Therefore, the actions violated the following:  sec. 1012.53(2), F.S. Duties of Instructional Personnel; Rule 6B-1.006(3)(a), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 0.01(2)(c), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; Rule 6B-1.001(2) and (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 3.02(4)(a),(4)(f), (4)(j), Code of Ethics; and School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel.  Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Michelcic completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on May 22, 2010.

There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Christine Michelcic.

On April 16, 2012, the "Notice of Five (5) Day Suspension Without Pay" was hand-delivered to Ms. Michelcic from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Christine Michelcic's five (5) day suspension without pay, effective May 18, 2012 subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

PS 1 Amendment to Professional Services Agreement for Randee Gabriel
Meeting
May 2, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda

This amendment will enable the district to continue providing PAL services to students with disabilities.

Changes made to the agreement are as follows:

  • The original contract was approved in the amount of $9,950 with an effective date of July 1, 2011, through June 30 , 2012.  Due to an increase in needs across the district it is necessary to amend this contract at this time increasing it by $3,990 which will provide an additional 266 hours of time in support of parents of students with disabilities.

Parents As Liaisons (PALS) are parents of students with disabilities who are enrolled in the School District of Palm Beach County. The PALS have received special training in IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act), State Law, Individual Education Plans/504 Plans, Mediation, Classroom Strategies/Inclusion, Advocacy, Disability and Network Information.  As parents of children with special needs, the PALS are uniquely able to assist parents and educators in a variety of ways.  PALS encourage home-school relationships by facilitating positive pro-active communication between families and educators.  Parents as Liaisons are available to help teachers, administrators and all support staff build more effective relationships with families.



The PALS mission is to promote the positive educational experience of exceptional students by providing families with information and guidance on how to access educational and community services, emphasizing and strengthening family roles and responsibilities in the education process, providing parents and school staff with up-to-date resources and support.  They ensure that families have access to exceptional student training and information, provide information to families and professionals about local resources and family support groups, and promote partnerships between schools and parents to support student achievement.



Parent Services are a required part of the IDEA grant and the PALS are one method the department uses to provide information, training, and support to families in order to promote effective parent participation in the educations of children who are exceptional and/or have special needs.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve the Amendment to the Professional Services Contract with Randee Gabriel for an additional $3,990 through June 30, 2012, and authorize the Superintendent  and Board Chairman sign all needed documents.

AC1 - Elementary Science Fair and South Florida Fairgrounds License Agreement
Meeting

Category

The Elementary Mathematics and Science Fair is an opportunity for all kindergarten through fifth grade students to showcase their critical thinking and scientific problem-solving abilities with other students and the general public.  The projects provide a foundation for the students' education because they incorporate the skills and disciplines necessary for future success in higher-level math and science courses.  





In 2011, seventy-six public, private, and home school organizations participated. Schools entered 1,552 project boards and approximately 2,500 Palm Beach County School District children attended the fair in field trips.  Over seventy community members assisted with judging to recognize student achievement.  The student recognition awards included first, second, and third place ribbons in thirty categories (90 ribbons), 130 Honorable Mentions, three Aviation (Flight Safety) awards, three Physical Science (Up & Running Machinery, Inc.) awards, and three Green awards.





Sponsors included: Houghton Mifflin Harcourt Publishing, Palm Beach County Science Educators Association (Green Earth awards), Palm Beach County Council of Teachers of Math, ETA Cuisenaire (hands-on sponsor), Up & Running Machinery, Inc. (Physical Science awards), and Flight Safety International (Aviation awards).

CS1 - LaMensa Academy - Proposed 90-day Termination of Charter Contract
Meeting
May 2, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

On June 22, 2011, the School Board granted La Mensa Academy - New Generation School, Inc., on behalf of LaMensa Academy New Generation School (LaMensa), a five year Charter Contract  with the School Board.  The charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2011, and ending June 30, 2016, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.



LaMensa currently serves students in Grades K-3, and was approved to serve students in Grades K-5 in subsequent years. 



The Superintendent's recommendation to terminate LaMensa's Charter Contract is based on the following reasons that establish the legal grounds for the proposed 90-day termination of the Charter:







  1. Failure by LaMensa to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management pursuant to Florida Statute Section 1002.33(8)(a)(2);




  2. Failure by LaMensa to meet governance requirements pursuant to Florida Statute Sections 1002.33(9)(h) & (i);




  3. Failure by LaMensa to comply with its Charter, Section 4:  Financial Accountability and Section 8:  Governance; and/or




  4. Good cause shown for the reasons stated above and as set forth in Florida Statute Section 1002.33(8)(a)(4).


Alternatively, LaMensa's Governing Board may voluntarily terminate the Charter Contract in accordance with Section 1:  General Provisions, subsection (G) of the Charter Contract prior to or after this Board Meeting of May 2, 2012.  To voluntarily terminate the Charter, LaMensa has been requested to submit a written notice of voluntary termination and a copy of the pertinent Governing Board minutes to the Charter School Department before June 7, 2012, and to continue operating the school until the end of this school year, which ends in June, 2012. 



There will be no negative impact to the students.  The school district will ensure the transition of each of the students to an appropriate school for the 2013 school year.



By letter dated April 24, 2012, (Exhibit A) hereto, LaMensa was notified of this Board Meeting.



Furthermore, the attached proposed 90-day Termination Notice (Exhibit B) details those reasons for the recommended 90-day termination of the LaMensa Charter.  This Notice also includes the supporting exhibits (Exhibits 1 through 20) for each of the termination reasons.



 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board authorize the Superintendent to notify LaMensa's Governing Board of the proposed 90-day Termination of the Charter Contract.  I authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign and provide the attached 90-day Termination Letter with supporting documentation and all related documents.

The financial impact for SY 2012 is an over payment of FEFP (Operating) funds in the amount of $117,662.

On June 22, 2011, the Board granted la Mensa Academy - New Generation School, Inc. (La Mensa), a five year charter Contract with the School Board, ending June 30, 2016, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.   La Mensa currently serves students in Grades K-3.




The Superintendent planned to recommend termination of La Mensa's Charter Contract based on reasons that establish legal grounds for the proposed 90-day termination of the Charter (see attached letter of Proposed 90-day Termination Notice).




La Mensa was notified of the proposed termination on April 24, 2012. 




The School District provided the charter school an option to voluntarily terminate its charter with the School Board rather than the Board's voting on a recommendation to terminate the charter in 90 days.  La Mensa exercised that option and its governing board on Monday, April 30, 2012 at a public meeting voted to voluntarily terminate the charter effective June 7, 2012, and notified the Board on May 1, 2012, of its action by letter and provided minutes of its governing board meeting.

Recommendation:

Chair Reads: Good cause exists for adding this item and its recommendation on the agenda. The School District provided this charter school an option to voluntarily terminate its charter with the School Board rather than the Board's voting on a recommendation to terminate the charter in 90 days, and they exercised that option on Monday, April 30, 2012.
