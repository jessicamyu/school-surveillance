I recommend the School Board approve the five (5) year Charter with Eagle Arts Academy, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Eagle Arts Academy, Inc. to open Eagle Arts Academy in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on October 2, 2013, as Item CS2 during a Special Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this Charter School was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, except as provided in the charter.

 

Eagle Arts Academy will serve students in grades 6-8.  The Application projected a first year of enrollment to be 894 students.  However, due to the response the School received from prospective students, the first year enrollment is projected to be 948 students in the Charter Contract.

 

Eagle Arts Academy’s mission is, “To educate the whole child using a challenging 21st century curriculum that focuses on the arts.  An arts-infused curriculum provides opportunity for intellectual, emotional, aesthetic, and social growth while cultivating essential skills and while enhancing a child’s creativity and imagination.  Our innovative facilities will provide practical knowledge and hands on experience in the performing and production arts in a caring and supportive academic environment.  A broader, integrated curriculum will provide students with multiple experiences so that they can grow into accomplished, successful young adults.”

DESCRIPTION:

Project Transition provides adult education and vocational training and access to work readiness programs for refugees of all nationalities.  The eligible targeted populations are refugee adults with Federal I-94 forms or other documentation proving their legal status in the United States.  This grant will assist adult Limited English Proficient (LEP) refugees at all Community Schools to master English and be able to access skill-based vocational training.  It will assist refugees with the knowledge and skills necessary for successful employment, leading to economic self-sufficiency and effective citizenship.

 

Project Transition assists the eligible targeted population by providing tuition for vocational programs, as well as the purchase of supplies for use in the adult ESOL programs.

Jul 30, 2014 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING following WORKSHOP in the Board Room.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the continuation of the Adults with Disabilities Entitlement Grant in the amount of $760,481, effective August 1, 2014 to June 30, 2015; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION:

The Adults with Disabilities (AWD) Grant is a continuation grant in the amount of $760,481.  South Tech Academy, through the School District of Palm Beach County as Local Education Agency (LEA), has historically been the source of continued support for this grant.  The School District's Department of Adult and Community Education serves as the coordinating department and works with the proper District departments to obtain School Board approval.

 

The AWD program has been implemented through South Tech Academy since 1983.  This program is intended to improve the quality of the lives of adults with disabilities.  The program uses a realistic work environment with skilled and certified teachers who provide the training and support needed to make the student successful.  The AWD program is designed to make each student as self-sufficient as possible and for those that qualify, to become employable.

 

The AWD program combines job skill training with special education instructional methodology which enables the students to gain both employability skills and real world job skills.  The goal is self-sufficiency for the disabled by providing training and income.  The funding is used to train and enhance the skills and lives of approximately 600 students.  Each student's Adult Individual Education Plan (AIEP) is closely monitored to assure that AIEP goals are being met as well as tracking the number of students who obtain competitive employment and non-competitive employment.  Competitive employment is where students interview for job positions and non-competitive employment is where students do not interview for job positions and are placed in jobs according to their abilities.

 

The target population focuses on adult individuals who were born with a disability, acquired a disability later in life, and individuals who are 55 years of age or older and have disabilities, who are referred to as senior citizens with disabilities.

 

The following are the five liaison agencies who partner to provide training: The Arc of Palm Beach County, Inc., Habilitation Center for the Handicapped, Inc., Jeff Industries, Palm Beach Habilitation Center, and Seagull Industries for the Disabled, Inc.  Each agency promotes the philosophy of challenging students to attain the greatest degree of self-sufficiency in both their work skills and personal life skills.  This funding enables students that are exceptional to become employable in the community.

I recommend the Board approve the Tax Anticipation Notes as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The resolution authorizes the issuance of the 2014 TAN.
  • The Tax Anticipation Note, or TAN, is a short term financing instrument used to alleviate cash shortfalls in the general fund each year.  Current estimates show that additional cash will be required for the Operating Fund in November 2014.
  • The Ad Valorem millage levy provides the majority of the revenues for the School District.  As the first receipt of property taxes is expected in late November, it is necessary to borrow money for cash flow purposes until the tax revenues are received. 
  • Based on the District's cash needs, the District plans to issue $115 million in Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs).
  • The TAN issuance in 2013 was also for $115 million.
  • The net cost of the transaction is expected to be approximately $58,450.  This includes expected interest cost of $61,333, interest earnings of $95,833 and costs of issuance not to exceed $92,950.  A breakdown of the costs, by vendor, is attached in the document titled 2013 TAN Cost of Issuance.  The actual interest earnings and interest expense will be determined by the market.
  • The sale is tentatively planned for September 10, 2014 at 11 am.  Students from the Finance Academies have been invited to attend a presentation beginning at 9:30 am.  The presentation will include a mock sale so the students will be able to participate in the process.  Financial professionals have also been invited to help the students understand the career opportunities available related to public finance.
  • The transaction is planned to close on or about September 26, 2014 and will mature no later than February 1, 2015.
  • The Finance Committee reviewed and approved the transaction at their meeting on May 30, 2014.  A draft of the minutes as well as the video recording are available on the Finance Committee website:  http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/FinanceCommittee.asp

The proceeds of this financing transaction will be used to meet the cash flow needs of the general fund.  The net costs associated with the issuance of this debt instrument are estimated to be $58,450 which includes estimated interest expense of $61,333, estimated interest earnings of $95,833 and costs of issuance not to exceed $92,950.

On or about April 22, 2014, Willie Richardson became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Ethical Misconduct-Falsification of Employment Qualifications and Failure to Fulfill the Essential Functions of a Human Resources Technician II.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

There is “just cause” to support Willie Richardson’s termination of employment.
