Kristen Rulison-Palm Beach County Teacher of the Year
Meeting
Apr 20, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action

Kristen Rulison, from Elbridge Gale Elementary School was recently named the 2011-2012 Palm Beach County Teacher of the Year.  Mrs. Rulison currently wears two hats at Elbridge Gale.  In the morning, she is the Supplemental Academic Instruction (SAI) teacher for twenty third grade students, for whom she provides remediation strategies and extra reading support.  In the afternoon, she teaches reading for a class of gifted/advanced third grade students by providing differentiation in reading instruction through guided reading groups, collaborative learning projects and literature circles.  Kristen Rulison wrote the following:



I am charged with providing a world-class education to children at a critical stage in their development as learners.   I must teach children to be authentic learners and independent thinkers.  Teaching is not just creating lessons for my students to memorize the skills and concepts that they are studying, but rather to help them make connections to the importance of what they are learning and how this knowledge can be applied and useful in their own lives.  Another role in educating the next generation of citizens is the critical transmission of the core ethical values that we celebrate in our country:  caring for others, being honest, being fair, being responsible, and how to respect others.



Kristen Rulison's classroom is an inviting environment for learning, where you see children enjoying reading and writing experiences.  Her methods inspire every one of her students to the highest levels of achievement, and for these reasons she was twice selected as a "Model Teacher" with a "Model Classroom".  Through her work with the Literacy in Action Center, Mrs. Rulison demonstrated lessons for more than 150 west area elementary teachers and administrators.  Teachers who attended these year-long professional development sessions reported that the experience "was the best in-service ever provided because we learned how to teach young children to read and write from an outstanding teacher." 



Mrs. Rulison is involved in many school-wide and district activities such as serving as treasurer of the PTO, assisting to plan and to implement a Parent Literacy Program, serving as Chairperson of the CHAMPS/Positive Behavior Support Team, and a member of the Professional Development Team.  As an active member of the District Report Card Committee, she has presented many forms that she developed.  In addition to mentoring student teachers and new teachers, Kristen offers an after school dance club for students twice a year.  As a dance instructor, she choreographs the dances and provides costumes for all the students.  According to Princpal Gail Pasterczyk, "Parents are amazed at the children's accomplishments".



Following her selection as the District's "Florida Department of Education/Macy's Teacher of the Year", Mrs. Rulison will advance in the selection process held by FLDOE in Tallahassee.  In July 2011, the FLDOE//Macy's Teacher of the Year 2012 will be announced at Macy's Gala Awards Ceremony.

Victoria Smith, School Bus Driver at the West Transportation Facility, was recently named the 2010-2011 Palm Beach County School-Related Employee of the Year.  Ms. Smith is known for her positive and professional manner.  While on an activity run at night a few years ago, she encountered a vehicle whose driver lost control and crashed into a canal on State Road 80.  She quickly called 911, and because it was dark, she stayed on the phone to help the 911 dispatcher direct the emergency responders to the exact location of the accident   The Sheriff credited her responsible and compassionate manner with helping to save the woman's life.  Later the same year, she was the first responder to a school bus fire on State Road 80, where she assisted the other driver in evacuating the students and safely transported them home.

The departments of Risk and Benefits Management, Facilities Services and Purchasing work together toward the goal of providing safe play areas for our students.  The Certified Playground Safety Inspector (CPSI) program is offered by the National Certification Board in coordination with the National Park and Recreation Association and the National Playground Safety Institute. CPSIs are certified to inspect playgrounds for safety hazards and to ensure compliance with national standards set by the American Society for Testing and Materials (ASTM) and Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC).

Risk and Benefits Management conducts annual playground inspections for 303 playgrounds at 107 schools. This annual process takes approximately 396 hours or 50 days per year. Safety Technicians also provide playground accident investigations, playground supervisory training for staff and they educate Facilities Services staff on current playground issues.



Facilities Services maintains 303 playgrounds and completes work orders generated by the schools and work needed after the annual inspections are done by Risk Management. The Facility Administrator manages all aspects of projects for installation of new playgrounds, surfacing repairs and renovations performed by outside vendors. The Warranty Senior Tech handles all warranty issues and maintains condition assessment information on all District playgrounds. He also assists in getting replacement parts and repairs covered as warranty issues thereby saving the District funds for these repairs. Approximately $20,000 to $30,000 dollars has been saved this year.



Purchasing works closely with Risk Management and Facilities to prepare and update the District's playground specifications to ensure our playgrounds are not only cost effective but also ensure they meet the highest standards for safety and ADA compliance, often exceeding industry standards. The purchasing department's bid specifications and procedures for playground equipment and surfacing are recognized state-wide as a leader in safety and ADA compliance and the bid is utilized (piggybacked) by many school districts and government municipalities throughout Florida.  Our procedures allow open competition on all projects and require review by Risk Management of all playground proposals for safety and ADA compliance prior to any purchase. Risk Management conducts a final inspection of new playgrounds and surfacing prior to being released for use.

P1 Approve the request for renewal of RFP 07C-007B for Sign Language/Oral/Cued Speech Interpreters/Transliterators awarded to Deaf Communication Specialists, Inc., Nationwide Interpreter Resource, Inc. and *Signs Of Excellence, LLC. (* represents a certified M/WBE vendor) The option to renew is now being exercised for the period July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2012. Deaf Communication Specialists, Inc., Nationwide Interpreter Resource, Inc. and Signs Of Excellence, LLC have agreed to honor terms, conditions and pricing of the existing contract for the forthcoming contract period.



The original contract was awarded by the Board on June 27, 2007 for the period July 1, 2007 through June 30, 2011 with the option to renew for one additional one-year period.



By Board action on August 20, 2008, the estimated expenditure was increased by $3,500,000



Services to be provided include sign language interpreters/Transliterators for students, parents, District employees and other requests for accommodations under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). Fees for these services are billed at a firm hourly rate.
Renewal of RFP 07C-007B for Sign Language/Oral/Cued Speech Interpreters/Transliterators is recommended for one additional year for multiple reasons including: cost, critical shortage of qualified staff and growth in population.





  • The costs of services in the current contract are reasonable and customary per industry standards.  The current contract includes pay rates based on level of credential versus a flat rate for any interpreter with varying skills.

The workshop model is the structure that New York City schools have implemented to successfully turn around high-needs schools.  New York City Department of Education research shows that from 2002 to 2008, the achievement gap for proficiency on the state-wide English Language Arts test between black and white fourth grade students closed 6.3 points.  The achievement gap between Hispanic and white fourth grade students closed 6.2 points.  Additional research shows that from 2006 to 2008, 13 out of 15 schools, specifically implementing the Lucy Calkins Units of Study writing curriculum, had a 3%-20% increase in student performance on this same assessment. 



During this institute, teachers will learn about the structures, methods and expectations for the workshop model. Additionally, they will learn about effective strategy-based instruction as well as utilizing assessment as a means of differentiation and curriculum development.  The academic needs of students from these schools make this professional development critical.  The overall goal is for teachers to sharpen their ability to provide high quality literacy instruction for their students now and in the future.



It is cost effective to bring the staff developers to Palm Beach County.  If the district were to provide funds to send these teachers and administrators to an institute in New York City, the cost would be over $750,000.  This is a net savings of over $670,000.



The staff developers from Columbia University's Teachers College are leaders in the field of literacy.
