In conjunction with Black History Month,  the Martin Luther King, Jr. Coordinating Committee contest winners  will be recognized.  The 2008 Celebration Theme is "Accentuate the Positive, Eliminate the Negative: Fulfill the Dream."



In 1971, the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Coordinating Committee, Inc. (MLKCC), was organized initially as the Black Educators Caucus, sponsoring remedial tutorial programs in cooperation with the Minority Mental Health Association.  In 1975, the program became an after school project and in 1979 expanded in partnership with the School District of Palm Beach County to (1) sponsor oratory, poster and drama contest commemorating Dr. King's January birth date; (2) to provide Afro-Arts Annual presentation and (3) to participate in Palm Beach Community College's Annual Multicultural Festival.  In addition to several other activities the competitive events include art exhibits, performing arts, oratorical, poster and essay contests.  The executive director is retired Palm Beach County teacher, Edith C. Bush.

The Grants to Reduce Alcohol Abuse proposes to continue to carry out the mission of the Prevention Center in the Department of Safe Schools to improve academic and social and emotional learning by reducing the risk factors for alcohol use and changing attitudes towards alcohol use. The  grant  proposes to implement a comprehensive, customized approach to the problems of underage drinking, which is associated with poor academic performance, a reduced ability to learn, increased dropout rates, lower GPAs, higher truancy rates, and  lack of college planning, as well as violence, fatalities, injuries and future alcohol dependence.



 



The grant will provide prevention services to three selected high schools and one middle school. The design and implementation will be replicated for use by other schools. Program elements include:





  • Development of a Youth Council and Strategic Planning Team;

  • Implementation of Project Northland/Class Action, a science-based model alcohol prevention program including curriculum, training, materials, payment for substitutes; partnering with Hanley Center;

  • Social norms marketing;

  • Staff development;

  • Underage Drinking Town Hall meetings; and

  • Continuation, support, and training for the Alternative to Suspension program FACE IT, a family educational substance abuse program for students and their parents.


The impact of this project will be measured in the reduction of binge drinking, increased number of students who believe alcohol abuse is harmful, increased numbers of students who disapprove of alcohol abuse, decreased alcohol use incidents, increased use of Alternative to Suspension program for alcohol use incidents, and numbers of teachers and staff participating in the on-line course.

CC 1 Consultant shall perform the following services:  Students will learn the latest dance moves choreographed to fun, lively, up beat music that they enjoy.  Participants will learn proper body alignment and body isolation techniques while having fun learning street-style dances.  They will also learn proper vocabulary and terminology. 



The pass-through funding for this consultant is provided by the Palm Beach County Commissioners, via the Palm Beach County Department of Parks and Recreation.  Parks and Recreation pre-approves all recreation modules, including lesson plans.  Because of this partnership, over 120 different recreation modules are available to students.  The variety of recreation activities offered is a motivator for students to attend the Middle School Afterschool Programs.  Two recent studies have again reinforced the importance of providing a safe place for students to go after school.  The findings are listed below.

Durlak, J.A., & Weissgerg, R.P. (2007).  The impact of after-school programs that promote personal and social skills.  Chicago, IL:  Collaborative for Academic, Social, and Emotional Learning.

Subject
LC1 Leasing Corp - Conversion of interest rate modes for Certificates of Participation, Series 2003B.
Meeting
Feb 20, 2008 - Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)




  • This resolution authorizes the conversion of the 2003B COP issue from7-day VRDO (Variable Rate Demand Obligation) mode to another mode, such as an Index Rate Mode.




  • The resolution also delegates authority to the Superintendent to negotiate the terms of the transaction with the underwriters or COP purchasers within the parameters set forth in the resolution.  The resolution also authorizes the execution and delivery of all instruments and documents necessary to complete the transaction.




  • The 2003B COP issue was originally completed in June 2003 as a variable rate transaction, specifically in a 7-day VRDO (Variable Rate Demand Obligation) mode insured by Ambac Assurance Corporation.  The rating of the insurer has been lowered from AAA to AA by Fitch Ratings and also put on negative watch by S&P and Moody’s, causing the cost of borrowing to increase. The downgrade has also increased the risk associated with the transaction.  The original documents contemplated and provided for conversion among interest rate modes.




  • The costs to date for FY 08 are still within the amount budgeted, but all indications are that interest costs will continue to be higher than expected.  This action will transition the 2003B COP issue to a more efficient market and reduce the risk associated with the transaction.




  • The Finance Committee recommended the transition at the meeting on January 30, 2008.  A drafts of the meeting minutes are available online at: http://www.palmbeach.k12.fl.us/FinMan/FINANCIALDIVISIONS/Treasury/nTreasury%20FinCom.htm




  • Some of the attachments to the authorizing resolution are forthcoming from bond and underwriter's counsel and will be available in the Board Minutes Office prior to this meeting.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp adopt a resolution authorizing the conversion of interest rates modes with respect to the $124,295,000 aggregate principal amount of Certificates of Participation, Series 2003B and authorize execution of related documents.

Subject
CLS1-2008-2009 Instructional Materials Adoption and Program Budget
Meeting
Feb 20, 2008 - Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Discussion

District instructional materials adoption committees were convened during 2007-2008, pursuant to School Board policy and district adoption procedures, to recommend new materials for the following subject areas:  Comprehensive Core Reading K-5, Comprehensive Intervention Reading K-5, Comprehensive Intervention Reading 6-8, Comprehensive Intervention Reading 9-12.  Committees reviewed all instructional materials programs submitted for state adoption.  Titles recommended for district adoption are contained in Attachment A.



The district adoption committee for Comprehensive Core Reading K-5 reviewed the content of all publishers' programs for alignment with the revised Sunshine State Standards for Reading and Language Arts and with the District's specifications for a program that is directed toward student competency in phonological awareness, phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension as well as oral language, writing and shared reading.  The committee also considered online input from teachers across the district who reviewed the two finalists' materials.  The district adoption committee was in agreement that the Macmillan McGraw-Hill Treasures program was the best program to meet the needs of Palm Beach District students and teachers.  Online student and teacher editions and leveled readers accompany the print version of the Treasures program.  The program is available in Spanish for Dual Language schools.



The Elementary Comprehensive Intervention Reading committee recommended materials from three publishers for further review by a team of district reading specialists and administrators.  The Elementary District Intervention Team recommended adoption of the following programs for intensive remediation of students who read two or more years below grade level:  Wright Group's Breakthrough to Literacy for third grade students, and Wright Group's Fast Track for fourth and fifth grade students.  Since the program selected for comprehensive core reading provides an intervention component for all classroom teachers in grades K-5, the District Intervention Team did not feel that an additional intervention program was necessary for grades K-2.



The district adoption committees for Secondary Comprehensive Intervention Reading reviewed materials submitted for state adoption and recommended two finalists for each level, grades 6-8 and 9-12 for further consideration by a Secondary Intervention Reading Team and staff from the Educational Technology (ET) Department.  The following programs were recommended for district adoption for students reading at FCAT Level 1:  Scholastic READ 180 for grades 6-8, and National Geographic Hampton-Brown Edge for grades 9-12.  Both programs contain print and technology components to support instruction in the five essential components of reading instruction using the district's recommended rotational model.  Scholastic READ 180 was purchased during 2007-2008 by the ET Department for Level 1 students in AAA schools.  ET has agreed to fund the purchase of READ 180 for Level 1 students in non-AAA schools for 2008-2009.



The 2008-2009 Instructional Materials Cost Analysis (Attachment B) represents estimated costs for implementation of new adoptions, maintenance of existing instructional materials programs, opening three new elementary schools, purchasing Dual Enrollment materials and providing allocations for charter schools.  The estimated costs are based on projected student enrollments and anticipated program needs.  Funding is provided from the projected 2008-2009 State Categorical Allocation for Instructional Materials and Science Lab Materials and projected collections for lost/damaged materials from FY2008.



Staff development for instructional materials will be provided at no cost to the District by publishers and will be focused on the appropriate use of materials to implement the Sunshine State Standards.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the list of instructional materials recommended for District adoption for the period indicated, and the 2008-2009 Instructional Materials Program budget of $15,472,713.
