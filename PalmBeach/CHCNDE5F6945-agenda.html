Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Technical Assistance Specialists Program Renewal Agreement, effective October 1, 2022, through September 30, 2023, with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents and contracts.

DESCRIPTION:

This renewal Agreement provides $1,268,242 to the Department of Early Childhood Education for the continuation of the Strong Minds Network Technical Assistance Specialists program.  This program is a component of the Palm Beach County Strong Minds Network, a countywide voluntary quality improvement system funded by the Children’s Services Council.  The mission of the Strong Minds Network is to use researched-based and data-driven strategies to increase the quality of early care and education services for children from birth to kindergarten entry served in participating private childcare centers and select public schools.  

 

The District's Technical Assistance Specialists provide on-site coaching and supports in the areas of teacher-child interactions (Classroom Assessment Scoring System); assessment and documentation (Teaching Strategies GOLD); curriculum and instruction; behavior management; accreditation; and foundational practices.  The Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County is the founder of the Strong Minds Network, and its partners include Palm Beach State College, the Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, and the School District.

I recommend the Board approve the AVID Agreement, effective July 1, 2022, until June 30, 2023, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID) is a systemic instructional approach for students in kindergarten through higher education (K-16).  The AVID College Readiness System is a schoolwide transformational effort focused on instruction, leadership, systems, and culture.  AVID is designed to increase the number of students who enroll and succeed in higher education and in their lives beyond high school.  The AVID College Readiness System is a catalyst for developing a school culture that closes the expectation and opportunity gaps many students face and prepares all students for success in a global society.

 

AVID Excel is a middle school program intended to break the detrimental cycle that turns many English Language Learners (ELLs) into long-term ELLs.  AVID Excel accelerates students’ academic language acquisition and puts them on a pathway to high school AVID and college readiness.

 

The School District of Palm Beach County’s AVID participation has increased from 6 high schools in 2009 to 64 implementing schools including four middle schools with AVID Excel. Of the 304 AVID seniors in 2020-2021:
       78% took at least one AP/IB/AICE exam
       98.4% graduated on time
       90% completed 4-year college entrance requirements 

 

According to the National Student Clearinghouse, 
67% of  Palm Beach County AVID females enrolled in the Fall semester at a four-year university compared to the national average of 45% 
52% of Palm Beach County AVID males enrolled in the Fall semester at a four-year university compared to the national average of 40% 
Of those enrolled:
        57% are Black or African American compared to 36% nationally
        61% are Hispanic or Latino compared to 26% nationally 
        69% are White (not Hispanic) compared to 49% nationally 
        55% are Other compared to 68% nationally 

 

Approximately 1,423 middle school students were enrolled in the AVID elective in 2020-2021.  Of those students:
39% completed Algebra 1 or higher math with a “C” or better
484 have taken an honor or high school credit-bearing course other than math.

 

On average 22% of each AVID school's faculty have been trained in AVID’s strategies and methodologies impacting almost 60,000 students at 27 elementary schools, 18 middle schools, 18 high schools, and 1 K-12 school.  

 

These Agreements will allow the School District of Palm Beach County to implement AVID’s curriculum and use of the AVID logo in all 64 sites. The Agreements include AVID membership, curriculum materials, and implementation support.

I recommend the Board approve the Interlocal and Transfer of Development Rights (TDRs) Agreement with the City of West Palm Beach for the Dreyfoos School of the Arts TDRs and authorize the Chair and Superintendent to sign all related documents including associated deeds, covenants, leases, unity of title, right-of-way dedication, and other documents required by the City.

DESCRIPTION:

On February 23, 2022, the School Board authorized staff to pursue Historic Designation for four buildings on the A.W. Dreyfoos School of the Arts campus, along with negotiation of an Interlocal Agreement with the City of West Palm Beach for purchase of the Transfer of Development Rights generated by the historic property.

 

Accordingly, District staff has applied for Historic Designation for Buildings 1,2,3, & 9 on the Dreyfoos Campus under the City of West Palm Beach's Historic Preservation Ordinance, as well as the City’s Transfer of Development Rights (TDR) Program. (Please see attached map for building locations.) The program requires a perpetual covenant to retain the historic structures.

 

The TDR program encourages the preservation of valuable Historic properties by allowing owners of eligible Historic properties to sell the rights to the additional square footage that could be hypothetically be constructed. The square footage of the existing buildings is subtracted from the highest existing development potential of the property and the difference in square footage is eligible for sale through the TDR program.

 

Recent sales of Historic TDRs have been in the $11 to $12 per square foot range. Due to the scarcity of additional TDRs, negotiations with City staff resulted in a $17 per eligible square purchase price, as indicated in the February 23, 2022, agenda item. The value calculations are as follows:

 

The resulting site is:  221,720 sf

Allowable Floor Area Ratio (FAR) = 2.75

Maximum development capacity =  221,720 x 2.75 = 609,731 sf 

Existing buildings on site = 120,554 sf

Building #1 - 37,374 sf   

Building #2 - 28,521 sf 

Building #3 - 31,151 sf  

Building #9 - 23,508 sf

TDRs available for sale = 609,731 - 120,554 = 489,176 sf

489,176x $17/sf = $8,315,992 (survey validated)

 

 

In addition to the revenue generated by the sale of the TDR’s, the transaction includes the City’s long term lease to the District (with future conveyance option) of a portion of the former Golf Course parking lot adjacent to Forest Hill Community High School, with an estimated value of $175,000 according to the Palm Beach County Property Appraiser’s Market Valuation.

 

During discussion of the February 23, 2022, agenda item, the Board asked that District staff work with the City of West Palm Beach to make every effort to ensure that at least 25 percent of the housing units built as a result of the TDRs be workforce and affordable housing. A synopsis of those efforts is attached.
