§  a communications device

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded a competitive cooperative agreement (grant) from the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) Division of Adolescent School Health (DASH) for $2,075,000 over a 5-year period, from August 1, 2013, through July 31, 2018.  The District was awarded similar five-year CDC grants in 1997, 2003, and 2008.
 
The goals of this grant are to:
• increase the number of schools that implement the Human Growth and Development program 
• establish linkages through school nurses to organizations that have expertise in adolescent health services 
• expand program activities designed to increase school connectedness and parent engagement and to prevent bullying and sexual harassment 
• track policy implementation and educate decision makers on policy solutions and increase the number of schools using the School Health Index
• conduct the Youth Risk Behavior Survey and School Health Profiles  
 
The CDC Grant requires and funds an external evaluator who will assess quantitative and qualitative data to determine the project’s impact on these goals. 
 
Program activities to meet the above goals for the Human Growth and Development curriculum and tracking policy will target all elementary and secondary school students within the District. This grant aligns with the District's strategic plan and specifically supports: Goal 1: Student-First Philosophy. 
 
A copy of the grant application is available for review in the Board Office.

I recommend that the School Board approve the State of Florida Voluntary Prekindergarten Education Program Statewide Provider Agreement with the Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, Inc., effective August 19, 2013, through August 30, 2014, and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents and contracts.

DESCRIPTION:

The Florida Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) Education Program Statewide Provider Agreement allows the District to continue offering a high quality, school year and summer VPK program in select schools.  In FY 2014, the District will offer the VPK program in 86 classes in 48 schools, and a matrix of the program models and schools offering the program is attached.

 

Florida VPK is a legislatively mandated, free program that allows students to participate in either a school-year 540-hour free VPK program or a summer 300-hour free VPK program, but not both.  The District offers school year VPK primarily as a VPK/Enrichment program, as either a VPK/Title I Enrichment model or a VPK/Parent Fee Enrichment model.  In these two models, VPK supports three free instructional hours per day.  All enrolled VPK students are eligible, but not required per state law, to attend the enrichment portion of the day.  The enrichment portion of the day is funded by either Title I in select schools or by parent fees or School Readiness (subsidy) funding in other schools.  In addition to the VPK/Title I and Parent Fee Enrichment models, the VPK program is also braided with other programs to create partnership models, including VPK/ESE Inclusion, VPK/Migrant, VPK/Head Start, and VPK/Career Academy/ESE Inclusion.  The District has participated in the VPK program since its inception in FY 2006.

On June 12, 2013 an Evaluation Committee consisting of District personnel, including a representative from the Office of Diversity in Business Practices and a representative from each bargaining unit, reviewed five proposals received and evaluated each of them based on the criteria outlined in the RFP.  After discussing and scoring each proposal, United Healthcare Insurance Company offered the District the lowest cost per point.  The evaluation committee recommended the District enter into negotiations with United Healthcare Insurance Company to secure an agreement for the contracted Dental Insurance services.

 

The representative from the Office of Diversity in Business Practices awarded United Healthcare Insurance Company four points out of a possible 10 points for M/WBE subcontractor participation.

 

The services to be provided include Dental Insurance for all eligible employees, retirees and dependents.  A summary of the services offered are:

 

  • While the plans offered are similar to the current plans the District has with United Healthcare Insurance Company, there are two new enhancement items of coverage: composite resin fillings (white) will be covered at the same cost to the member as amalgam (silver) fillings and coverage for dental implants has been added.
  • Prepaid dental plans (managed care) has an open-access provision which means the member can be treated by any approved dentist, rather than having to be assigned to one.
  • The PPO plans contain a provision called max multiplier which allows any unused amount of the yearly maximum annual benefit of $1000 to roll over to the following year increasing the $1000 annual benefit by the unused amount from the previous year.  
  • Premiums are guaranteed for two years.
  • A packaged savings program which saves the District $1.00 per employee per month off our medical administration fee.  This is a savings of approximately $240,000 per year.
  • Final negotiations resulted in a slight reduction from the current managed care plan premiums offered by United Healthcare Insurance Company and a 9.8% increase to the PPO plan premiums.

Tamarind Avenue, West Palm Beach; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The School Board declared a portion of Roosevelt Full Service Center as "Interim" and available for Lease.  The facility was made available to Charter Schools through a Solicitation.  Joseph Littles-Nguzo-Saba's (JLNS) lease proposal was selected and approved on May 18, 2011.  (See Lease Agreement).
  • The School Board approved a First Amendment on September 21, 2011 permitting JLNS Charter School to occupy additional space at Roosevelt Full Service Center. (See First Amendment) 
  • The lease agreement provided two one-year renewal options.
  • The School Board approved a Second Amendment on May 2, 2012 allowing JLNS Charter School to extend their lease for one (1) year from July 1, 2012 through June 30, 2013. (See Second Amendment)
  • JLNS is requesting to exercise their right to renew for a second year from July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2014.
  • The Lease Agreement does not permit subleasing by the tenant and all facility leasing activity is handled by the School District. (See Lease Agreement) 
  • JLNS Charter School is responsible for all utility and maintenance fees and costs related to the operation of the leased premises based on tenant's occupancy of 44% of the air conditioned space.
  • The tenant is also responsible for basic maintenance of the leased premises. The lease is being amended to include provisions that make the Tenant responsible for the costs of any required "Fire Watch " in the event of a fire alarm failure. Should major repairs be required for the base building systems (including, but not limited to, the roof, structural, fire alarm, electrical, and plumbing systems), the District shall have the option to make the repairs or terminate the Lease Agreement pursuant to Article XIII (early termination) and the tenant shall have the responsibility of making the School occupiable while any repairs are being made.
  • This Third Amendment states that both parties expressly agree that this extension shall be the final extension granted.
