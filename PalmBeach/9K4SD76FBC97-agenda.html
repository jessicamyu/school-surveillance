I. Overview



On August 1, 2011, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Mavericks High of Palm Beach County West to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.



Special Note:  This is one of three applications for Mavericks High.  Mavericks High of Palm Beach County, Mavericks High of Palm Beach County West and Mavericks High of Metro Palm Beach County applications are identical.



History:



Mavericks submitted two applications in Dade County and withdrew both applications.  Mavericks submitted two applications in Orange County.  Eventually, one of those applications was approved and one application was withdrawn.



The following is an overview of the eight charter schools that Mavericks operates in five Districts in the State of Florida:







  1. Osceola County - Mavericks High of Osceola, opened SY2009-2010.  This is the only Mavericks charter school that Mavericks listed in its application submitted to Palm Beach County.  In 2009-10 they received an "I" as a school grade (incomplete).  In 2010-11, they received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete).   (See Exhibit "A")




  2. Broward County - Mavericks High of Central Broward, opened SY2010-2011 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete).  (See Exhibit "B")




  3. Broward County - Mavericks High of North Broward, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.




  4. Miami-Dade County - Mavericks High of North Miami-Dade, opened SY2009-2010 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete). (See Exhibit "B")




  5. Miami-Dade County - Mavericks High of South Miami-Dade, opened SY2009-2010 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete).  (See Exhibit "B")




  6. Palm Beach County - Mavericks of Palm Springs, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.




  7. Pinellas County - Mavericks in Education opened in SY2009-10 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete). (See Exhibit "B")




  8. Pinellas County - Mavericks of North Pinellas, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.




  9. Pinellas County - Mavericks of South Pinellas, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.


According to Mavericks' application submitted to Palm Beach County, the mission of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County is, "To provide students who have dropped out or are in danger of dropping out an innovative education solution to earn a high school diploma.  Our school is committed to providing an individualized, rigorous, self-paced, technology-enhanced curriculum that prepares students to earn a high school diploma.  Our school is dedicated to helping students successfully transition from high school to post secondary opportunities such as employment, college, military or vocational/technical school."



II.  Projected Enrollment



      First Year         9-12      400 Students



      Second Year    9-12      500 Students



      Third Year       9-12      550 Students



      Fourth Year     9-12      550 Students



      Fifth Year        9-12      550 Students



III.  Education Program Design



The Educational Program Design for Mavericks High of Palm Beach County is Blended Learning.  Blended Learning combines traditional classroom instruction with online instruction.  This Educational Program Design is similar to two other charter schools in Palm Beach County.



IV.  Location



The applicant has not secured a site for the proposed charter school at this time.  However, Mavericks' plan for its proposed school site is in Appendix P of its application.



V.  Applicant's Founding Governing Board



The three Founding Governing Board Members are local residents.  The qualifications of these three Board Members include real estate, marketing and finance. 



VI.  Ratings of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County's Application



A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.



Designated District staff reviewed the 19 sections of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County's application that are uploaded for the Board Meeting for February 1, 2012.  Each section of the Evaluation Instrument received one of the following ratings:  "Meets the Standard," "Partially Meets the Standard," or "Does Not Meet the Standard."



The District completed two evaluations of this application.  Mavericks High of Palm Beach County's received the following ratings:  16 sections received a rating of "Meets the Standard," 3 sections received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard."



Listed below are the three sections that received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard" and the reason for each:







  • Section 5 - Assessment:  The criteria for promotion from one level to the next and for graduation were unclear.




  • Section 7 - English Language Learners:  The meeting for the Parent Leadership Council and the ELL student plan that needs to be addressed twice a year were not addressed.




  • Section 9 - Governance:  There is a concern regarding who would own the furniture, fixtures and equipment purchased with school funds.


After Evaluation 1 the District provided Mavericks with an opportunity to cure the sections that received "Partially Meets the Standard" pursuant to F.S.1002.33(6) that states:  "Before approving or denying any application, the sponsor shall allow the applicant, upon receipt of written notification, at last 7 calendar days to make technical or non-substantive corrections and clarifications, including, but not limited to, corrections of grammatical, typographical, and like errors or missing signatures, if such errors are identified by the sponsor as cause to deny the application."  The pertinent sections of the Mavericks' application were emailed to the applicant group on September 29, 2011, and sent certified mail on September, 2011.  (See Exhibit "C")



On October 7, 2011, Mavericks submitted additional information that cured the "Partially Meets the Standard" ratings outlined in Exhibit "E".  (See Exhibit "D")



In order to "Meet the Standard," Section 5, Student Performance, Assessment and Evaluation, was revised and included the evaluation criteria that defined what students attending the school should know and be able to do and reflect how the academic progress of individual students, cohorts over time, and the school as a whole will be measured.



In order to "Meet the Standard," Section 7, English Language Learners, was revised and included the evaluation criteria that demonstrates an understanding of the requirements of the school to serve English Language Learners and provide a concrete plan for meeting the broad spectrum of educational needs and providing all students with a quality education.



In order to "Meet the Standard," Section 9, Governance, was revised and included the evaluation criteria that describes how the policy-making and oversight function of the school will be structured and operate.



Mavericks Application Evaluation 2 - After the second evaluation, all 19 sections of Mavericks' application received a rating of "Meets the Standards".  (See Exhibit "E")  Accordingly, Mavericks' Overall Assessment Checklist indicated that its application met the statutory requirement set forth in F.S.1002.33(6).



Special Note - Additional Information Requested:  Although the District conducted a 1st and 2nd evaluation, a constituency letter and media postings  about the effectiveness of Mavericks Charter Schools prompted the District to verify the data that Mavericks attached to their application. (Copies of the letter and media postings available in the board office.)



Mavericks suggests that: "Mavericks High of Osceola County completed its second year of operation with enrollment over 500 students" and that the school's "FCAT results, overall attendance and number of graduates support the effectiveness of the school design."  (See Exhibit "F" - Mavericks' Data)



The District verified Mavericks' data by obtaining data from the State FCAT website.  After analysis of Mavericks' data and the State data, the District found that some of Mavericks' data could not be substantiated with the State reported data, such as: number of graduates, percentage of 9th grade reading at proficiency, and percentage of 10th grade reading at proficiency.  There was also an error found in Mavericks' data which included their labeling of a FCAT 10th grade exam as an FCAT 11th grade exam. (See Exhibit "G")



Thus, some of the the FCAT information Mavericks submitted for SY 10-11 for its charter school in Osceola County is unsubstantiated and inconsistent with State documents. (See Exhibits "F" and "G")



The District sent a letter to Mavericks granting an opportunity to provide additional data that "the existing design has been effective and successful in raising student achievement."  (See Exhibit "H", Dr.  Cover's letter)



Mavericks' response to the letter did not provide any verified evidence to substantiate the academic performance data stated in its application for its Osceola County Charter School.   (See Exhibit I)



Mavericks was granted several opportunities to provide performance data as required in Section 3 of the application.







  • The New Applicants' Workshop held on May 27, 2011




  • Three evaluations of its application




  • An opportunity to respond to the letter from the Area Superintendent of Quality Assurance requesting information to substantiate and/or explain its academic performance data and accomplishments for charter schools in Florida.


Based on the forgoing facts, the District changed its application rating for Section 3 to "Partially Meets the Standard." (See Exhibit "J")



In many cases, Charter School Assessments with one or more "Partially Meets the Standard" ratings could be approved if those sections could be cured via contract negotiations.  However, Section 3 of this application contains data that is only partially substantiated, and thus cannot be cured during contract negotiations.  Therefore, the District's recommendation to the Board is to deny this application.

The charter school applicant did not provide the District an extension of time to vote upon its application. (See Exhibit "K")



The communication advising the charter school applicant of this Board meeting is attached as Exhibit "L".



A copy of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County West's Application is in the Board Office for review.

I.   Overview



On August 1, 2011, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Mavericks High of Palm Beach County to open a charter School in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.



Special Note:  This is one of three applications for Mavericks High.  Mavericks High of Palm Beach County, Mavericks High of Palm Beach County West and Mavericks High of Metro Palm Beach County applications are identical.



History:



Mavericks submitted two applications in Dade County and withdrew both applications.  Mavericks submitted two applications in Orange County.  Eventually, one of those applications was approved and one application was withdrawn.



The following is an overview of the eight charter schools that Mavericks operates in five Districts in the State of Florida:







  1. Osceola County - Mavericks High of Osceola, opened SY2009-2010.  This is the only Mavericks charter school that Mavericks listed in its application submitted to Palm Beach County.  In 2009-10 they received an "I" as a school grade (incomplete).  In 2010-11, they received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete).   (See Exhibit "A")




  2. Broward County - Mavericks High of Central Broward, opened SY2010-2011 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete).  (See Exhibit "B")




  3. Broward County - Mavericks High of North Broward, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.




  4. Miami-Dade County - Mavericks High of North Miami-Dade, opened SY2009-2010 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete). (See Exhibit "B")




  5. Miami-Dade County - Mavericks High of South Miami-Dade, opened SY2009-2010 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete).  (See Exhibit "B")




  6. Palm Beach County - Mavericks of Palm Springs, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.




  7. Pinellas County - Mavericks in Education opened in SY2009-10 and received a School Improvement Rating of "I" (incomplete). (See Exhibit "B")




  8. Pinellas County - Mavericks of North Pinellas, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.




  9. Pinellas County - Mavericks of South Pinellas, opened SY2011-2012.  No academic performance data is available on the FLDOE website, as it is new.


According to Mavericks' application submitted to Palm Beach County, the mission of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County is, "To provide students who have dropped out or are in danger of dropping out an innovative education solution to earn a high school diploma.  Our school is committed to providing an individualized, rigorous, self-paced, technology-enhanced curriculum that prepares students to earn a high school diploma.  Our school is dedicated to helping students successfully transition from high school to post secondary opportunities such as employment, college, military or vocational/technical school."



II.  Projected Enrollment

      Fourth Year     9-12      550 Students

III.  Education Program Design



The Educational Program Design for Mavericks High of Palm Beach County is Blended Learning.  Blended Learning combines traditional classroom instruction with online instruction.  This Educational Program Design is similar to two other charter schools in Palm Beach County.



IV.  Location



The applicant has not secured a site for the proposed charter school at this time.  However, Mavericks' plan for its proposed school site is in Appendix P of its application.



V.  Applicant's Founding Governing Board



The three Founding Governing Board Members are local residents.  The qualifications of these three Board Members include real estate, marketing and finance. 



VI.  Ratings of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County's Application



A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.



Designated District staff reviewed the 19 sections of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County's application that are uploaded for the Board Meeting for February 1, 2012.  Each section of the Evaluation Instrument received one of the following ratings:  "Meets the Standard," "Partially Meets the Standard," or "Does Not Meet the Standard."



The District completed two evaluations of this application.  Mavericks High of Palm Beach County's received the following ratings:  16 sections received a rating of "Meets the Standard," 3 sections received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard."



Listed below are the three sections that received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard" and the reason for each:







  • Section 5 - Assessment:  The criteria for promotion from one level to the next and for graduation were unclear.




  • Section 7 - English Language Learners:  The meeting for the Parent Leadership Council and the ELL student plan that needs to be addressed twice a year were not addressed.




  • Section 9 - Governance:  There is a concern regarding who would own the furniture, fixtures and equipment purchased with school funds.


After Evaluation 1 the District provided Mavericks with an opportunity to cure the sections that received "Partially Meets the Standard" pursuant to F.S.1002.33(6) that states:  "Before approving or denying any application, the sponsor shall allow the applicant, upon receipt of written notification, at last 7 calendar days to make technical or non-substantive corrections and clarifications, including, but not limited to, corrections of grammatical, typographical, and like errors or missing signatures, if such errors are identified by the sponsor as cause to deny the application."  The pertinent sections of the Mavericks' application were emailed to the applicant group on September 29, 2011, and sent certified mail on September, 2011.  (See Exhibit "C")



On October 7, 2011, Mavericks submitted additional information that cured the "Partially Meets the Standard" ratings outlined in Exhibit "E".  (See Exhibit "D")



In order to "Meet the Standard," Section 5, Student Performance, Assessment and Evaluation, was revised and included the evaluation criteria that defined what students attending the school should know and be able to do and reflect how the academic progress of individual students, cohorts over time, and the school as a whole will be measured.



In order to "Meet the Standard," Section 7, English Language Learners, was revised and included the evaluation criteria that demonstrates an understanding of the requirements of the school to serve English Language Learners and provide a concrete plan for meeting the broad spectrum of educational needs and providing all students with a quality education.



In order to "Meet the Standard," Section 9, Governance, was revised and included the evaluation criteria that describes how the policy-making and oversight function of the school will be structured and operate.



Mavericks Application Evaluation 2 - After the second evaluation, all 19 sections of Mavericks' application received a rating of "Meets the Standards".  (See Exhibit "E")  Accordingly, Mavericks' Overall Assessment Checklist indicated that its application met the statutory requirement set forth in F.S.1002.33(6).

Special Note - Additional Information Requested:  Although the District conducted a 1st and 2nd evaluation, a constituency letter and media postings  about the effectiveness of Mavericks Charter Schools prompted the District to verify the data that Mavericks attached to their application. (Copies of the letter and media postings available in the board office.)

Mavericks suggests that: "Mavericks High of Osceola County completed its second year of operation with enrollment over 500 students" and that the school's "FCAT results, overall attendance and number of graduates support the effectiveness of the school design."  (See Exhibit "F" - Mavericks' Data)

The District verified Mavericks' data by obtaining data from the State FCAT website.  After analysis of Mavericks' data and the State data, the District found that some of Mavericks' data could not be substantiated with the State reported data, such as: number of graduates, percentage of 9th grade reading at proficiency, and percentage of 10th grade reading at proficiency.  There was also an error found in Mavericks' data which included their labeling of a FCAT 10th grade exam as an FCAT 11th grade exam. (See Exhibit "G")

Thus, some of the the FCAT information Mavericks submitted for SY 10-11 for its charter school in Osceola County is unsubstantiated and inconsistent with State documents. (See Exhibits "F" and "G")

The District sent a letter to Mavericks granting an opportunity to provide additional data that "the existing design has been effective and successful in raising student achievement."  (See Exhibit "H", Dr.  Cover's letter)

Mavericks' response to the letter did not provide any verified evidence to substantiate the academic performance data stated in its application for its Osceola County Charter School.   (See Exhibit I)

Mavericks was granted several opportunities to provide performance data as required in Section 3 of the application.

  • The New Applicants' Workshop held on May 27, 2011
  • Three evaluations of its application
  • An opportunity to respond to the letter from the Area Superintendent of Quality Assurance requesting information to substantiate and/or explain its academic performance data and accomplishments for charter schools in Florida.

Based on the forgoing facts, the District changed its application rating for Section 3 to "Partially Meets the Standard." (See Exhibit "J")

In many cases, Charter School Assessments with one or more "Partially Meets the Standard" ratings could be approved if those sections could be cured via contract negotiations.  However, Section 3 of this application contains data that is only partially substantiated, and thus cannot be cured during contract negotiations.  Therefore, the District's recommendation to the Board is to deny this application.

The charter school applicant did not provide the District an extension of time to vote upon its application.  (See Exhibit "K")



The communication advising the charter school applicant of this Board meeting is attached.  (See Exhibit "L")



A copy of Mavericks High of Palm Beach County's revised Application is in the Board Office for review.
