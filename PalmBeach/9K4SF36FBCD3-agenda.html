AC1 - Standard Agreement for Supplemental Educational Services (SES) Providers
Meeting
Jun 22, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)


  • In accordance with Title I Part A of the ESEA Act as reauthorized by the NCLB Act (P.L. 107-110), Section 1116 (e) (2) and (3), Supplemental Educational Services (SES) MUST be offered to eligible students in schools that have not made Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP). The School District of Palm Beach County offers SES tutorial services in accordance with Florida’s federally approved plan for Differentiated Accountability (DA). Under this plan, SES must be offered to eligible students in schools that have not made AYP for two or more consecutive years. The item to be voted on tonight is the standard contract providers will sign.

  • Students are eligible if they receive free or reduced-price meals and they attend an eligible school. Parents are encouraged to apply for tutoring prior to electing "choice with transportation" as an option.

  • The State of Florida provides each school district with a list of approved private providers. Each provider must sign the contract with the district. Parents may then choose a provider from the list of approved providers who signed a contract.

  • Using private tutorial providers to serve eligible students is a federal mandate. Only those approved providers may serve students.

  • The services are paid from the mandated Title I 20% set aside funds.

  • For FY 2012, the state has set the per pupil amount at $1,251.00

  • If parental requests for SES tutorial services exceed the amount of available funding, priority will be given to the lowest-achieving eligible students in grades 3-11.

  • The total amount that The School District of Palm Beach County must set aside is $6,739,135. This amount will provide SES tutorial services to 5,387 students.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve a standard agreement for providers of Supplemental Educational Services (SES) to meet the ESEA mandate for Title I schools and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

Contact:

Judith Klinek (judith.klinek@palmbeachschools.org)



Alison Adler



Marcia Baldanza

Financial Impact:

Each district must use an amount equal to 20 percent of its Title I, Part A allocations (the “20-percent set-aside”) received from the Florida Department of Education to cover choice-related transportation costs for students who exercise a public school choice option (PSC) and to pay for Supplemental Educational Services for eligible students whose parents request such services.



The financial impact for Supplemental Educational Services is estimated at $6,739,135 to be paid from the required 20% set-aside from the Title I allocation.

• Execute and deliver of all instruments and documents necessary to complete the transaction.

The District purchases Commercial Crime Insurance to protect itself from theft of cash and securities, etc. (not personal property) by employees or individuals with whom we conduct business.  The policy also satisfies the requirements of Florida Statute 1010.07, to ensure that each employee with responsibility for handling/expending funds be appropriately bonded or insured.  The policy is also endorsed for the Superintendent and Board members.



This policy protects the district from employee thefts of cash and securities with a limit of up to $5,000,000 per occurrence with a $100,000 deductible.  It also provides policy limits of $100,000 with a $1,000 deductible for thefts by individuals with whom we do business.



Additionally, the policy covers the School District for Faithful Performance of Duty for $1,000,000 with a $100,000 deductible.



This insurance also covers losses from Forgery/Alteration ($1,000,000 limit/$10,000 deductible) and Disappearance/Destruction ($100,000 limit/$1,000 deductible) caused by individuals with whom we do business.



The Broker, The Beacon Group contacted several insurance carriers and could not find any who could compete with The Travelers program with comparable premiums. There is a limited market for this coverage because of the size of our budget and the fact that we are public entity.  The premium increased approximately $200 this year to $27,500.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board purchase the commercial Crime Insurance policy effective August 7, 2011 through August 7, 2012 from The Travelers Insurance Company, for an annual premium of $27,500.  The 20% commission on this policy was negotiated as part of the overall annual contract with the Beacon Group as approved by the School Board  on May 18, 2011.  This commission is included in premium of $27,500.

The Entitlement Grant from the Florida Department of Children and Families, Office of Refugee Resettlement for Project Transition is a continuation grant in the amount of $1,403,404 effective August 1, 2011 - July 31, 2013.



Project Transition provides adult education and vocational training and access to work readiness programs for refugees of all nationalities.  The eligible targeted populations are refugee adults with Federal I-94 forms or other documentation proving their legal status in the United States.  This grant will assist refugees with the knowledge and skills necessary for successful employment, leading to economic self-sufficiency and effective citizenship.



These programs consist of the following: Limited English Proficient (LEP) programs aimed at improving the language skills of non-English speakers;  English Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL), Post-Secondary Adult Vocational (PSAV), Adult Basic Education (ABE), General Educational Development (GED), Citizenship, Vocational English for Speakers of Other Languages (VESOL), English Language for Career and Technical Education (ELCATE), English for Academic Skills (ESOLAS), Workforce Readiness Skills (WRS), and Vocational Preparatory Instruction (VPI).  For compliance purposes, student enrollement and student completion rates must be monitored in each literacy level within a program.  In addition, the grant requires that the country of origin for clients served be tracked and reported as well.



An English Literacy Program means a program of instruction designed to help individuals of limited English proficiency achieve competence in the English language.  In this grant, limited English proficiency means an adult refugee who has limited ability in speaking, reading, writing, or understanding the English language and whose native language is a language other than English;  or lives in a family or community environment where a language other than English is the dominant language.



To ensure continuous improvement of this grant, student progress will be measured by progression through Literacy Completion Points (LCPs).  The Adult General Education program or course numbers and associated LCPs are used to document improvements in literacy performance standards.  LCPs represent a student's attainment of and Educational Functioning Level gain in adult federal reportable programs.  These tracking measures must be used by all adult education programs for all enrolled students to ensure compliance in reporting.

This Project Modification contains the following item:







  • Change Order Proposal Request (COPR) #4 for a credit in the amount of $5,058,553.47


 







  • Description: Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) Reconciliation.
       o Deduct for Direct Material Purchases ($4,706,827.56) (credit)
       o Additional Sales Tax Savings ($13,025.35) (credit)
       o Master Controlled Insurance Program (MCIP) Adjustment ( $882,124.71) (add)
       o Unused Contingency, General Requirements, etc. ($1,220,825.27) (credit)


 







  • Cause: School District Requested.


 







  • Justification: The Construction Manager (CM) Agreement is a cost plus fixed fee with a Guaranteed Maximum Price contract. The fixed fees are General Conditions, Construction Phase Fee (CM staff) and Construction Manager Fee (OH&P). All other costs are billed at actual cost as long as the GMP is not exceeded. The difference between the GMP and the actual cost is returned to the School District prior to or at project completion. These unused funds include contingency, general requirements, bonds, and insurance. In addition, the Direct Material Purchase cost and the associated Sales Tax Savings above the Guaranteed Sales Tax Savings amount are deducted from the CM Agreement. The Guaranteed Sales Tax Savings were deducted from the CM Agreement when the GMP was approved. An estimated MCIP payment was deducted from the CM agreement when the GMP was approved. The actual MCIP cost was less than what was estimated, therefore the difference is being returned to the CM.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve a Project Modification for a credit in the amount of $5,058,553.47 from C.R. Klewin SE, Inc. for the Royal Palm School, Project #1801-8357, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.

Background: 



 



On June 9, 2011, the School District and the above-named parties were served with a summons and complaints for the above lawsuits.  Upon receipt of the lawsuits, the Office of Chief Counsel and Department of Risk Management and Benefits discussed with the designated outside counsels, Scott Krevans, Esq. and Dale Friedman, Esq., of Conroy Simberg, et al., the representation for the cases.   Counsel has recommended that the School Board also provide representation to the above designated parties.  Should the Board approve such representation; the attached letter will be provided to the employees or former employees.



 



At the time of the facts alleged in the complaints, all of the parties were employed with the School Board and worked at H.L. Only Ann Wark and Nicole Williams are presently District employees.



 



Pursuant to Section 10112.26, Florida Statutes, the School Board may provide "legal services for officers and employees who are charged with civil or criminal actions arising out of and in the course of the performance of assigned duties and responsibilities."  Under the statute, the provision of such legal services is “declared to be a district school purpose for which district school funds may be expended.”    Should an employee be found liable as a result of the lawsuit, the statute further provides the employee “shall reimburse the district school board for any legal services which the school board may have rendered pursuant to this section.”



 



Prior to the commencement of the representation, Section 1012.26, Florida Statutes, requires “Each expenditure by a district school board for legal defense of an officer or employee, or for reimbursement pursuant to this section, shall be made at a public meeting with notice pursuant to s. 120.525(1).”

• Negotiate the terms of the transaction with a bank or underwriting firm based on the best terms and conditions included in competitive proposals received via the District’s Financial Advisor.  Negotiate the terms with the swap counterparty as needed to implement the most cost effective way to replace the liquidity facility, including the possibility of amending the swap.



• Execute and deliver of all instruments and documents necessary to complete the transaction.



• This transaction has been discussed with the Finance Committee on numerous occasions and was reviewed at a special meeting on June 20, 2011.
