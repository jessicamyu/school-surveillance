I recommend the Board approve the five-year Initial Charter with Renaissance Charter High School, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Renaissance Charter High School in August 2015.  The application for Renaissance Charter High School was approved on April 10, 2019, as Item CS1 during a Special Board Meeting. 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing on July 1, 2020, and ending on June 30, 2025, except as provided in the charter. 

 

The development of the Education Model was based on Robert J. Marzano’s 35 years of research. It is implemented within a framework of continuous improvement to ensure its validity and the development of supporting tools to meet the needs of each individual student. Its structure enables administrators and teachers to better understand, explain, and predict the elements needed to facilitate each student’s mastery of the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards and Florida Standards. Educational best practices, technology, communication, and documentation tools are created and revised to help implement the model and ensure that teachers have the support necessary to meet the needs of each student. The Education Model described throughout this application is implemented at all Renaissance Charter Schools in Palm Beach County. The School’s connection to other Renaissance schools will aid in implementation of the plan with fidelity and ensure effectiveness.

Guaranteed and Viable Curriculum

The core of the Education Model is the Guaranteed and Viable Curriculum (GVC), which ensures academic excellence in every classroom and encourages steady academic progress as students build their knowledge and skills from one year to the next in order to master the Florida Standards and NGSSS. The GVC is primarily a combination of the factors “opportunity to learn” and “time.” Both have strong correlations with academic achievement. The concept of opportunity to learn is a simple but powerful one: If students do not have the opportunity to learn the content expected of them, there is little chance that they will. Opportunity to learn addresses the extent to which the curriculum in a school is “guaranteed.” It also means that individual teachers do not have the option to disregard or replace assigned content (Marzano). After clear guidance is given to teachers regarding the content to be addressed in specific courses and at specific grade levels, the ESP (which provides curriculum and educational strategy support to the School) and school administration monitor learning so that the academic content necessary for achievement within each grade is not disregarded or replaced.

I recommend the Board approve the five-year Initial Charter with NorthStar Academy of Palm Beach County, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from NorthStar Academy of Palm Beach County in February 2020.  The application for NorthStar Academy of Palm Beach County was approved on March 4, 2020, as Item CS2 during a Special Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing on July 1, 2020 and ending on June 30, 2025, except as provided in the charter. 

 

NorthStar Academy is dedicated to providing a comprehensive educational alternative to middle school students who are over-age for grade and may have failed one or more grade levels, and high school students who have dropped out of the traditional school setting, or who are not on track to graduate with their cohort and are at risk of dropping out, or who are seeking to accelerate their high school learning experience. Many of these students have not experienced academic success at the traditional school. NorthStar Academy is founded on the belief that, given the option to participate in an innovative, student-centered learning environment that combines teacher-directed and technology-based instruction, every student has the potential to succeed in school, earn a standard high school diploma, and be prepared for post-secondary studies or careers.  The School is committed to helping students realize their dreams of becoming self-sufficient, contributing members of society so that they can lead productive, fulfilling lives. NorthStar Academy will offer credit accrual and acceleration in an academic environment that features both life-skills mentoring and teacher-directed instruction, which will be carried out by qualified and highly-committed teachers in one-to-one, small group, or whole group settings.  Each student will be assessed, assigned to one of the “Learning Labs” (online instruction) and scheduled for “Direct Instruction” (DI) with state-certified teachers. Teacher-directed lessons (in DI classrooms) will address the specific learning needs of each student, based on real-time student performance data, through the use of research-based programs and strategies that are designed to promote student mastery of state standards.

NorthStar Academy will implement the EdisonLearning Achievement Framework™, a comprehensive, research-based framework for sustained school improvement – a framework that can be flexibly applied to specific school priorities in order to identify the key levers for improving the quality of learning and teaching and managing change. The Framework is structured around planning and assessment processes, pedagogy, the culture and climate of learning, and the foundations of quality teaching. Teachers will participate in professional development throughout the year on the Achievement Framework. NorthStar Academy will apply the comprehensive framework to ensure excellence in education and effective implementation of the EdisonLearning Bridgescape Model. This educational model comprises five key tenets that the School believes are essential in meeting the needs of its target student population:

  1. A Blended Learning Model that combines a technology-based, self-paced curriculum and face-to-face, teacher-directed instruction;
  1. A Student Mentoring Program that provides individualized mentoring for each student;
  1. Social-Emotional Support which includes Social Emotional Learning (SEL), Mental Health Services, and the integration of Core Values;
  1. Individualized Instructional Plans that personalize each student’s instructional program which includes academic and career goals;

A College and Career Focus which includes college and career guidance, and the implementation of Career Academies that will allow students to earn industry certification in various high-demand fields.

I recommend the Board approve the Unilateral Transition Plan for South Tech Charter Academy, Inc.'s Adult and Community Education Programs back to the School District effective July 1, 2020; and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Based on the Inspector General’s audit finding of Inlet Grove back in September 2019, the School District decided to transition all of the Adult and Community Education programs being operated by the two conversion charter schools Inlet Grove Community High School and South Tech Charter Academy, Inc., back to the District.  Inlet Grove transitioned all of their Adult and Community Education programs effective as of December 14, 2019.

 

Between January 2020 and June 2020 the District and South Tech Charter Academy, Inc. held several meetings to address the transition of South Tech’s Adult and Community Education programs back to the District effective July 1, 2020.  The District offered to hire South Tech’s Adult and Community Education's Administrator and to share use of the new facility that was recently renovated by the District with over $16 million dollars public funds for South Tech’s use primarily.  However, South Tech rejected the District’s offer. Therefore, the District will be utilizing the fourteen portables on the new campus exclusively and prepared the Unilateral Transition Plan for review and approval by the Board, solely.
