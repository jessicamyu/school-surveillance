Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the one (1) year Renewal Charter Contract for Leadership Academy West Charter School and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

 

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(6)(g), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the district and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

Following a renewal program evaluation and the Superintendent’s recommendation to deny renewal, the School Board voted against not renewing the School’s Charter at its meeting of March 6, 2013, and discussed placing the School on a corrective action plan.  The Corrective Action Plan was presented to and approved by the School Board on May 15, 2013. 

 

The Renewal Charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of one (1) year, commencing July 1, 2013, and ending June 30, 2014, except as provided in the charter. The proposed Charter Contract was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff.  If any provision with this Charter Contract is inconsistent with the School’s Renewal Program Review and Application, the provisions of this Charter Contract shall prevail.  If any provision with this Charter Contract is inconsistent with the School’s Corrective Action Plan, the School’s Corrective Action Plan shall prevail.

 

The mission is to, “Provide opportunities for enrolled students to acquire an education focused on rigorous academics, technology, and technical skills to produce graduates who are prepared for success in a global community.  Through a science-based and technology driven curriculum, students will gain knowledge and skills to prepare them for college and the workforce upon graduation.  Along with a high level of achievement in core academic areas, students will create ownership of challenging goals in their individual areas of interest.  Immersion in the community and local businesses will encourage students to take advantage of local resources.”

 

The Data panel for Leadership Academy West is attached.

 

Copies of Leadership Academy West’s Corrective Action Plan, Renewal Program Review and Application are attached for review.

 

The One Year Renewal Charter and legal checklist are attached.

 

CONTACT:

 

Cheryl C. Alligood (cheryl.alligood@palmbeachschools.org)

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.

Faith-Ann Cheek, Ed.D.

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

 

Leadership Academy West is projecting to serve about 146 students in grades 9-12, for FY 2014, which represents:

  1. A loss of revenue to the District of approximately $802,139 (net of the five (5%) percent administrative fee).
  2. A loss of District teacher positions of 6 (based on the projected enrollment divided by the average class size requirements).

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the five (5) year Amended Charter Contract with Palm Beach School for Autism and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

 

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

Palm Beach School for Autism is in compliance with its current charter.

 

Under the current 2008 Charter Contract, the school served students from Pre-K to Grade 5 for up to 85 students this upcoming year and 90 students for the following four years.  At the school's request, the Amended Charter Contract would allow the school to serve students from Pre-K to Grade 12 for up to 260 students this upcoming year and 310 students for the following four years.  In addition, the Amended Charter  Contract complies with the State mandated model template.  The Amended Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel's office.

 

The charter will become effective upon signed by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2013, and ending June 30, 2018.

 

The mission of Palm Beach School for Autism is, "To provide students with autism spectrum disorder and other related disabilities, a developmentally appropriate education that builds on the strength and needs of each individual student."

 

Copies of the full Amended Charter Contract, budget and legal checklist are attached.

Cheryl C. Alligood (cheryl.alligood@palmbeachschools.org)

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.

Faith-Ann Cheek, Ed.D.

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

 

Palm Beach School for Autism is projecting to serve about 260 students in Pre-K to Grade 12 for FY 2014, which represents:

 

1.  A loss of revenue to the District of approximately $5,800,000 (net of the five (5) percent administrative fee).

 

2.  A loss of District teacher and paraprofessional positions of 60 (based on the projected enrollment divided by the average class size requirements).

DESCRIPTION:

 

The K-12 Online Educational Products and Services Order provides that K12, LLC (Contractor) will deliver part time and full-time, 180 day instructional programs for eligible students in kindergarten through grade 12 in the virtual programs.
