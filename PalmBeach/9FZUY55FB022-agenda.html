Recommended Action
I recommend the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp authorize the issuance of COP 2015A in an amount not to exceed $107 million to refund of a portion of the 2005A COP, and authorize the Secretary and President to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The School Board has an opportunity to refund, or refinance, the 2005A COP.
  • The bonds are not callable until May 2015, but the District received a proposal from Bank of America to execute the documents and lock in the rate now, in advance of the call date.
  • The transaction is a bank loan. The bank has the option to cancel the transaction between now and the execution date of May 2015 under the following circumstances:
    • The State of Florida disallows the issuance of COPS
    • The Federal Government disallows the tax-exempt status of our bonds
    • The School District's credit rating is reduced below investment grade (BBB)
  • These risks are present for any transaction we do, but usually for a much shorter period of time. Staff and the Finance Committee are very comfortable with the risks associated with the transaction.
  • Should the School District decide to cancel the transaction, there would be a breakage fee based on the market rates. The transaction should be approved with the intent to close in May 2015. The only reason the District would ever consider cancelling the transaction would be if interest rates declined significantly during the next year. That possibility is highly unlikely.
  • Fees associated with the transaction are approximately $182,000 and are detailed in the attached document. The bank loan structure is less expensive than a public offering, enhancing the savings generated by the transaction.
  • The transaction currently provides over 6% present value savings or roughly $6 million over the next 8 years . The District normally requires a 3 percent savings threshold for current transactions. Given the forward nature of this financing, staff recommends a minimum savings threshold of 5 percent.
  • The Finance Committee reviewed this transaction in detail at their meeting on January 17, 2014. The documents and minutes are available online at http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/2014FinanceCommittee.asp.
  • The transaction must also be approved by the School Board of Palm Beach County.  It is item FMT3 on the February 19th School Board agenda.

 

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Operating Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

Leanne Evans, Treasurer (leanne.evans@palmbeachschool.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

There is no financial impact to the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp.  The financial impact for the School Board will be at least $5 million or 5 percent present value savings, thus reducing the debt service payments. Fees associated with the transaction are estimated at $182,000.
