B1 - CONSIDER FEDERAL LEGISLATIVE PROPOSALS FOR 2010-2011
Meeting
Feb 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)


·         The attached proposals are based on actions taken or not taken since the 2008 Session of the 110th Congress.



 



·         Funding emphasis is placed on maintaining and, if possible, increasing entitlement funding for Title I, educationally disadvantaged, of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) and for Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) as well as essential programs such as Perkins Career/Vocational Education.



 



·         Program emphasis is placed on reauthorization of the 1965 Elementary and Secondary Education Act so that standards and assessment provisions are more consistent nationwide; student performance is based on same year, multiple measurements; and accountability and operational conflicts between federal and state requirements are eliminated.



 



·         These recommendations are consistent with the positions expressed by the Florida School Boards Association, the National School Boards Association, American Association of School Administrators and the Council of Great City Schools.  District staff will work collaboratively with these organizations on these recommendations.



 



·         These recommendations address issues that are of paramount importance for Palm Beach County. The School Board and staff may identify additional issues for which support is needed, when that occurs a recommendation will be brought to the School Board for formal action while Congress is still in session.



 



·         District staff will work with the School Board’s contracted legislative liaisons that include Schoolhouse Consulting Group, Inc. (Vernon Pickup-Crawford) and Alcalde and Fay (former PB County Congressman Skip Bafalis and staff) for federal approval of local priorities.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board adopt the list of federal legislative proposals for the 2010 Session of the 111th Congress and authorize School Board Members and designated district staff to work with other school districts, representatives of appropriate state and national organizations, and members of the Florida Congressional Delegation to secure legislative approval.

FM1 - Consider Resolution Authorizing Revenue Anticipation Notes, Series 2010
Meeting
Feb 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)




  • Due to the instability in the credit markets last year, the District was not been able to issue COPS as planned for the construction of two referendum projects.




  • On February 4, 2009, the School Board approved the issuance of a Revenue Anticipation Note in lieu of a COP issue to make sure the schools opened on time.




  • The District planned to sell the COP issue once the markets stabilized.  However, if the District could not sell the COP issue any excess sales tax collected over the $560 million could also be used to fund the projects.




  • Due to the decline in capital millage revenues, the issuance of a COP is not advisable at this time.  The Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee and the Finance Committee have recommended that the excess sales tax revenues be used to pay off the RAN in 2011.




  • Revenue Anticipation Notes (RANs) are an interim financing tool.  The Notes are very similar to the Tax Anticipation Notes issued every fall.  Rather than anticipating tax revenues, a RAN is anticipating other revenues, in this case a future COP issue.




  • The RAN issued in 2009 was for $72 million.  When it matures in March 2010, $16 million will be paid off and a new issue for $55,826,022 will be issued.  The new RAN would mature in one year and the anticipated interest rate is approximately 0.50%.




  • The Finance Committee reviewed and approved the transaction at their meeting on January 29, 2010. The minutes of the meetings are available online at http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/FinanceCommittee.asp.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Resolution authorizing the issuance of Revenue Anticipation Notes, Series 2010 in an amount not to exceed $60 million and other necessary documents; authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign necessary documents; and authorize necessary budget amendments.

RE-1 Galaxy Elementary School/Interlocal Agreement with the City of Boynton Beach
Meeting
Feb 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Discussion

1.  The FY 2009-2013 Five Year Plan includes the modernization of Galaxy Elementary School schedule to be completed by 2012.
2.  The City of Boynton Beach owns a 3.96 acre park adjacent to Galaxy Elementary School.  (See Exhibit "A")
3.  The School Board needs to acquire the 3.96 acre parcel for the modernization of Galaxy Elementary School.
4.  The School District will convey a 4.01 acre portion of the existing Galaxy Elementary School site to the City of Boynton Beach as part of the replacement park.
5.  The appraisals for each exchange parcel were based on a land value of $6.50 per sq. ft. which equates to a land for land exchange with no exchange of dollars.
6.  The acquisition of the City's park permits the school to be located further away from I-95 which presumably has the added benefit of reducing the noise and carbon dioxide levels for the school.
7.  Building the new school on the park property also allows the District to avoid the approximately $150,000 cost of transporting the students to another campus while the school gets rebuilt as there is not enough space on the existing campus without the park land to rebuild the school while the existing buildings remain in operation.
8.  In exchange the School District will construct a replacement park on a portion of the School District's property not required for the replacement school and convey that property and improvements to the City after the replacement school has been completed. (See Exhibit "B")
9.  The City property is subject to a reversionary interest in favor of the County.  The County is willing to release the reversionary right on the City property and accept in substitution a new reversionary right on the replacement park property.
10. All of the deeds involved in this transaction will be submitted and held in escrow pursuant to the Escrow Agreement.
11. The deed from the City for the park property and the County's reverter will be released from the escrow when the School Board is ready to commence construction.
12. The deed from the School Board to the City (in fee) and County (reverter) will be released from the escrow when the replacement park is completed and accepted by the City, however, not later than March 1, 2013.
13. The City shall pay for the Commitment and Title Policy for the City property, one-half cost of the survey, the cost of recording the City's property deed and the cost of satisfying and correcting title and survey matters.
14.  The School Board shall pay for the Commitment and Title Policy for the replacement park property, one-half cost of survey, the cost of recording the replacement park deed and the cost of satisfying and correcting title and survey matters.
15.  The City and the School Board shall have the non-exclusive right to use each other's parking facilities during specified times at the Replacement Park and new school.
16.  The City will work with the School District on the abandonment of the 15,607 sq. ft. Galaxy Way Right-of-Way currently being used by both the School and the City for the entrance to the existing school and park that will become part of the new Galaxy Elementary School Site.(See Exhibit "B")



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement and associated Escrow Agreement for the exchange of land between the City of Boynton Beach, Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the modernization of Galaxy Elementary School; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DISCUSSION ITEM REGARDING: BUDGET



 



I would like the Board to discuss the following:



As we enter into more and more difficult budgetary times, it is important that we deliberate and plan with a focus on the needs of children.



I ask that the Board direct the Superintendent and Staff to create a transparent budget process that is open for constructive input.



I think that this should be a true zero-based budget process.



This would include but not be limited to:



1. A line item budget by department with the funding stream and comparisons for each line item over the past 3 years.



2. A notation as to how each department/program/initiative assists us in meeting the mission of the school district.



3. A process for input with separate input from (a) students; (b) parents; (c) teachers; (d) assistant principals and principals; and (e) other employees.



I suspect that this input would need to be provided electronically after a public solicitation for input to the various groups (Student Government, PTA’s, SAC’s, CTA, FPSU, Staff Association, etc) and the public at large.



This input would not need to address specific line items, but could provide comment as to the effectiveness, or lack thereof, of a particular program or funded strategy.
