CS1 - Champs Charter School at Royal Palm Beach – New Five Year Contract
Meeting
Apr 3, 2013 - Special Meeting 04-03-13 Following Workshop
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.



The application filed by Champs Charter Schools, Inc. stated that the name of the school would be Champs Charter School at Royal Palm Beach to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on August 22, 2012, as Item CS1 during a Board Meeting.



The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel's office.



The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2013, and ending June 30, 2018. 



Champs Charter School at Royal Palm Beach will serve students in grades K-5.



The mission of Champs Charter School at Royal Palm Beach is, “To provide the highest standards of academic excellence incorporating a guided self-directed learning philosophy.  We are committed to providing a challenging and supportive learning environment that fosters high expectations in moral values, character development, leadership skills, social growth, and intellectual development that help form a catalyst for the success of every student.  Infusing technology into every aspect of learning where appropriate, will be a significant part of enhancing the learning experience.  Utilizing a strong partnership between home and school, we strive to have our parents, teachers and community members actively involved in our students’ learning and development to achieve their greatest potential and ultimately become lifelong learners.



Attached are copies of the full contract, budget, and legal checklist for Champs Charter School at Royal Palm Beach.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the five (5) year Charter Contract with Champs Charter Schools, Inc. – Champs Charter School at Royal Palm Beach and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.



 



 

Contact:

Janis Andrews, Ed.D./ ( janis.andrews@palmbeachschools.org )



Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.



Faith-Ann Cheek, Ed.D.

Financial Impact:

Champs Charter School at Royal Palm Beach is projecting to serve about 250 students in grades K-5, for FY 2014, which represents:





 





  1. A loss of revenue to the District of approximately $1,519,184 (net of the five (5%) percent administrative fee).


 





  1. A loss of District teacher positions of 13 (based on the projected enrollment divided by the average class size requirements).

PD1 Disciplinary Action - Jeffrey Burg
Meeting
Apr 3, 2013 - Special Meeting 04-03-13 Following Workshop
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)
On or about January 14, 2013, Mr. Burg became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon the allegations of Possession of an Alcoholic Beverage on a School Campus; Failure to Make a Reasonable Effort to Protect Students from Conditions Harmful to Learning; Health or Safety: Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment; Ethical Misconduct; and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated. 




On December 19, 2012, a male student was found to be in possession of an alcoholic beverage on the campus of Odyssey Middle School. The male student reported getting the alcoholic beverage from a female student on campus.  The female student reported getting the alcoholic beverage from the storage closet in Mr. Burg's classroom.  The Principal and Assistant Principal responded to Mr. Burg's classroom where they found a cooler containing the alcoholic beverage.  Mr. Burg stated that he brought the cooler to work to replenish the water supply in the refrigerator.  He stated he saw the alcohol in the cooler at that time and that he meant to take it home.





Therefore, the actions violated the following:  School Board Policy 3.96(4)(a), Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy; School Board Policy 3.02(4)(a), (5)(a)(vii), & (5)(b)(i), Code of Ethics; School board Policy 0.01(2)(c), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff; Rule 6B-1.001(2) & (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics for the Education Profession in Florida; and 6B-1.006(3)(a), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for Educators in Florida.  Furthermore, it is noted that Mr. Burg completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on June 28, 2010.





There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Jeff Burg





On March 21, 2013, the "Notice of Three (3) Day Suspension Without Pay" was hand-delivered to Mr. Burg from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Jeffrey Burg's three (3) day suspension without pay, effective April 19, 2013 subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.
