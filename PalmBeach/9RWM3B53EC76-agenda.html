4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Subject
FMPO5 Approval of Master Software and Services Contract for the Purchase of Student Information System
Meeting
Jan 21, 2015 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the Software and Services Contract with Focus School Software, LLC for the Purchase of Student Information System and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the Contract for the term of January 22, 2015 through January 21, 2020 for the purchase of Student Information System (SIS) software from Focus School Software, Inc. and the installation, project management, planning, integration, and training services necessary to successfully implement the system. A Letter of Interest was issued by the District Purchasing Department and five vendors responded.  A committee evaluated all responses and ranked Focus School Software, LLC first. 

 

According to Gartner, a Student Information System (SIS) is a software application for the collection, organization and management of student data that includes, but is not limited to, student schedules, enrollment, course history, achievement profile, grades, attendance and demographic information.  In Palm Beach County, the SIS system houses the student FTE and teacher data which generates over $1 billion dollars of Florida Education Finance Program operating revenue each year.

 

Additionally, the District’s SIS is the system of record for required student data and is the source for many ancillary systems.

 

Since 1991, the District has been using an SIS called TERMS.  TERMS is a mainframe-based legacy system that met the District’s SIS requirements for many years.  However, those requirements have changed and have increased dramatically in recent years.  It has become extremely difficult to hire and retain staff with adequate Cobol programming skills to support TERMS.  In addition, due to TERMS’ limited functionality, several costly and labor-intensive ancillary software applications have been purchased or built over the years to handle functions not available with TERMS.

 

The new SIS will include the following software modules and functions: Florida state and federal reporting; student demographic information; emergency contact information; online grade book and teacher portal; discipline/suspension information; student grades; attendance; absence tracking and reporting; grade transcripts/GPA/class rank; standards alignment; national, state and local test scores; online course requests; data field validation; ad-hoc reporting; automated imports and exports; fees and fines; online student registration/enrollment/withdrawal; admissions; health and immunization records; parent and student portal; exceptional student education; individual education plans; adult education; career and technical education (vocational); response to intervention tracking; learning management system (LMS); athletics and activities; assignments, tests, and homework; graduation requirements/planning; student schedules; and locker assignment and management. 

 

Once implemented, the SIS will positively impact many stakeholder groups including students, teachers, parents, school administrators, and District support staff.  The new system will make FTE revenue collection more efficient, and student information more accurate, secure, effective, convenient and accessible, which will then be used to drive academic decisions for each student.

 

Focus School Software's response to the District’s letter of interest included a fee proposal of $7,150,000.  Through negotiations, the District contract for the period FY16 through FY20 with Focus School Software shall not exceed $5,900,400, a savings of $1,249,600, plus a reduction of $54,000 in the annual fee until such time as we implement the LMS and/or Special Student Services (SSS) modules.  In addition, the contract includes a cap on future cost increases of the annual fee of 2% for any year in the 5 year period following the initial Contract term and each additional 5 year period thereafter. All payments will be made upon completion and acceptance of milestones.  

 

The SIS project was approved by the Technology Advisory Committee (TAC) on May 7, 2014 and was an ongoing agenda item at each subsequent 2014 TAC meeting.  All student data will be housed inside the District.

Subject
B2 Class Size Reduction Compliance Plan
Meeting
Jan 21, 2015 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
14. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the District's 2014-15 Class Size Compliance Plan under Section 1003.03(4)(e),F.S., authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all associated documents, and authorize the submission of the CSR Plan to the Commissioner of Education to be received no later than February 1, 2015.

DESCRIPTION:

On December 3, 2014, the Florida Department of Education, (FDOE), notified the School District of Palm Beach County, (SDPBC), of its class size non-compliance based on the District's October 2014 student FTE Survey.  The results consisted of 126.34 FTE over the class size cap in District schools that were measured on a class-by-class basis, as required by Section 1003.03(1), Florida Statutes, (F.S.).

 

Section 1003.03(4)(a), F.S., requires a reduction in class size categorical funding for districts and charter schools that are out of compliance with class size requirements.  Up to 25 percent of the reduction is reallocated to districts and charter schools that are in compliance with these requirements.  The remaining balance is restored to districts and charter schools that are not in compliance, but have submitted a plan to the Commissioner by February 1, 2015, identifying actions the district or charter school will take to ensure compliance by October 2015.  Section 1003.03(4)(e), F.S., requires the compliance plan for District-operated schools be certified by the School Board.

 

In order to meet class size requirements in October 2015, SDPBC will utilize class size reduction strategies as listed in Section 1003.03(3) and (5), F.S., as well as strategies not listed in statute.  The proposed compliance plan is a working document and is subject to change as the District implements the plan in the upcoming school year.

 

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

P.J. D'Aoust  (pj.d'aoust@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

Board approval and submission of the CSR Compliance Plan reduces the potential non-compliance categorical reduction from an approximate penalty of $708,894 to $177,224.  This represents a 75 percent reduction to the potential penalty.

  5. The County lease expires October 30, 2015 and has the option to renew for another five (5) year period.
  6. The County has invested approximately one million, five hundred thousand dollars ($1,500,000) since 2000 for improvements to the gym.  These improvements have kept the facility usable and saved the School District money that would have otherwise been needed for demolition.  In addition, the conveyance relieves the District of future liability and future maintenance costs.
  7. The County intends to continue the current uses of the facility for the benefit of the Belle Glade residents and has requested that the School Board convey the improved 1.564 acres instead of renewing the lease.
  8. The Florida Department of Environmental Protection (FDEP) has notified the County that no action is required by the Board of Trustees and the conveyance will be subject to all restrictions on the deed and the 2002 modifications. (See 2002 Modifications and Deed 18599)
