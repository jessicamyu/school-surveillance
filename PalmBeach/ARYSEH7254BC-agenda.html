COM2 Principal of the Year for 2017-2018
Meeting
Nov 15, 2017 - 5. BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
9. Presentations
Type
Presentation, Recognition

DESCRIPTION:

Sandra Edwards is the Principal at Washington Elementary School in Riviera Beach. She received the 2017-2018 Principal of the Year award at a reception on October 25. When she took over as principal at Washington Elementary, a Title I school with 96 percent of the student population receiving free or reduced lunch benefits, the school had been placed on a list from the state as being one of the lowest performing schools in Florida.

 

By the end of the 2016-2017 school year the school raised its grade from an “F” to a “B” in the state accountability system, and 3rd graders improved their English Language Arts (ELA) proficiency by 29 percent over the previous school year, the highest increase of any school in Palm Beach County. Washington Elementary students had exceeded expectations, ranking in the 99th percentile in the state for ELA learning gains.

Teachers must exemplify the very best in providing specialized instruction and differentiated practices that meet the needs of all students.

I recommend the Board approve the amended Agreement with The Harvard Graduate School of Education and The Center for Education Policy Research for the Proving Ground initiative from November 15, 2017 through June 30, 2020; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to this agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District has identified attendance as a core component of students being high school ready.  Based on our FY17 data, more than 10 percent of our students (across all demographic sub-groups) have 11 or more absences. Despite local efforts, chronic absenteeism continues to be a challenge for our system. To get more students high school ready, we must address the issue of student attendance. The Harvard School of Education and the Center for Education Policy Research Proving Ground initiative is designed to assist school districts and other organizations on issues such as chronic absenteeism.  

 

The Proving Ground initiative is committed to helping education agencies meet their practical needs, by making evidence cheaper, faster, and easier to use. Proving Ground’s goal is to make evidence-gathering and evidence-use an intuitive part of how education agencies conduct their daily work.
 

The Proving Ground approach offers a more responsive, pragmatic, and agile analytic cycle that empowers education policymakers and administrators to:

  1. quickly and reliably assess the impact of investments,
  2. continuously improve implementation by testing new strategies, and
  3. incorporate new and better evidence into decision-making.
     

Proving Ground model includes three components:

 

1.  A peer network that provide district leaders with opportunities to compare notes, benchmark outcomes, brainstorm about and pilot solutions with their peers.  By pooling data and sharing the cost of piloting interventions, districts can test more ideas together than alone. The specific peer network activities include: (a) an annual convening in which the leadership team from the partner districts come together to review each other’s findings and collaborate on joint pilots; (b) five to six webinars per year to learn about new results, each other’s projects, successes, and challenges whereby Proving Ground would facilitate joint problem solving; (c) a collaboration hub where documents are shared between Proving Ground and its’ partners and partners with each other; and (d) conference calls to coordinate and plan the implementation of joint strategies

2.  On-site change management support to help district leadership teams interpret their data, assess the alignment of their strategies with evidence, plan interventions and evaluate results. Proving Ground will conduct three site visits with Palm Beach County Public Schools each year of the engagement.

3.  A data and analytic infrastructure that will provide district leaders with timely evidence that they can use to optimize their decisions. The data and analytic infrastructure include: (a) standardized process for data collection and cleaning; (b) a shared data warehouse for data storage; (c) programming code that would allow us to standardized reports on historical district performance, program implementation and program impact; (d) a data visualization tool that our partners can access to see relevant results; and (e) a standardized set of process and rules (that will be adjusted) to for the piloting and testing of new interventions.

Through our engagement with Proving Ground we will address a historical/predictive analysis that will lead to strategy implementation and its impact. The attached documents outline the scope of work to be included.
