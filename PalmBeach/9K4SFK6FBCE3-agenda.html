AC1 - Amendment to Cooperative Agreement with the Devereux Foundation, Inc.
Meeting
Apr 28, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

This amendment is necessary to change the ending date of the original cooperative agreement from June 30, 2010 to May 14, 2010.   The student was placed in residential treatment on March 13, 2008.  The Devereux Foundation, Inc. does not offer residential services for students beyond their 18th birthday.  The student turns 18 years old on May 14, 2010.  After that date, the student will continue to attend school at the Devereux School but will not remain in their residential program.  A separate contract for the educational program will be presented to the School board. 



The original amount of the cooperative agreement was $150,015.  The new amount is $130,698 which will be a savings of $19,317 to the district.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve this amendment to the cooperative agreement with Devereux Foundation, Inc. in the amount of $130,698 for the educational and residential costs for a student with a disability from July 1, 2009 through May 14, 2010, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.

CLS1 - Proposed Board Report re: Termination of Contract with Alternatives Unlimited, Inc. (Drop Back In Academy)
Meeting
Apr 28, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion

In July 2009 the School Board approved and entered into an agreement with Alternatives Unlimited, Inc., which has operated the Drop Back In Academy.  A copy of the agreement is attached to this Board report.



Drop Back In Academy (DBIA) is a dropout retrieval program where students who already dropped out of school are given an opportunity to return and attend a program leading to graduation.



The agreement provides within paragraph 3 of its termination clause that either party may terminate the agreement with or without cause with 30 days’ written notice.



The school year ends June 2, 2010 and staff recommends it would be appropriate to terminate this agreement effective close of business on Friday, June 4, 2010, and that the term  of the agreement not be extended.



All School Board furnished supplies, equipment, furniture, materials and/or textbooks, and any other property of the School Board, must be returned by that date. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board terminate without cause, effective close of business on Friday, June 4, 2010, its agreement with Alternatives Unlimited, Inc., pursuant to paragraph 3 of the agreement’s termination clause, that the term of the agreement not be extended, that a demand be made that all School Board furnished supplies, equipment, furniture, materials and/or textbooks, and any other property of the School Board, be returned to the School Board by that date, and that the Superintendent be authorized to sign all necessary documents relating to this matter and provide notice to Alternatives Unlimited, Inc. of these actions .

AC2 - Cooperative Agreement with the Devereux Foundation, Inc.
Meeting
Apr 28, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The purpose of this cooperative agreement is to continue to provide a highly restrictive educational setting to a student with a disability whose severe developmental, behavioral and mental health needs cause the student to be both self injurious to himself and a danger to others.  The student was placed in residential treatment on March 13, 2008.  When the student turns 18 year old, the residential program will no longer continue.  This student's mother has temporarily moved to the area so that the student can continue to remain in the school program.



Prior to the residential placement  the student was receiving ESE services as a student with a disability through the Hospital/Homebound Program.  The student has been identified as a student with disabilities in the areas of Intellectual Disabled, Autism Spectrum Disorder, Occupational Therapy, and Speech and Language Impaired by the School District of Palm Beach County in compliance with state and local statutes and regulations.



The cooperative agreement is in compliance with State Board Rule 6A.6-0361, "Special Programs Contractual Agreements with Non-Public Schools".

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve this confirming cooperative agreement with Devereux Foundation, Inc. in the amount of $11,784 for the educational costs for a student with a disability from May 15, 2010 through June 30, 2011 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.
