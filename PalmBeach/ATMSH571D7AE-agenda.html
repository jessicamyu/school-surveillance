Mentors meet with their mentees at least three times a month during the students’ lunch periods.  Take Stock mentors give emotional and social support to their mentees by listening, encouraging, guiding, and helping them navigate their educational career through high school and into college. Take Stock's college coaches also provide guidance for students. Upon high school graduation, all Take Stock students receive a full-tuition college scholarship. Take Stock in Children and their mentors truly make the difference in students’ lives and give them hope for the future.

Meeting
Jan 17, 2018 - 3. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with Infants and Children, P.A., effective January 18, 2018 through June 30, 2022; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.
 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement. This agreement originated with Jupiter High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.
 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy.   • SACs 251, 258, 350, 368, and 369 from Odyssey Middle to Lantana Middle
  • SACs 242A, 259A, and 259B from Odyssey Middle to Woodlands Middle

The four dissenting ABC votes were due to concerns that the study would put Christa McAuliffe Middle School over capacity and does not fully utilize the capacity available at Carver Middle School.

 

The study makes note that new Middle School “17-PP,” located adjacent to Sunset Palms Elementary, was added to this year’s Capital Plan and will open as soon as possible based on demographics, procurement procedures, and construction timeline.   • A committee member expressed concern that charter schools are lacking when it comes to serving students with disabilities, and that the District should take a stronger approach to ensure charter schools are sufficiently serving students with disabilities.

  • If the new middle school "17-PP" will offer any choice programs.
