Subject
FM1 - Class Size Reduction Compliance Plan
Meeting
Feb 9, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

On November 30, 2010, FLDOE notified the School District of Palm Beach County (SDPBC) of its class size non-compliance based on the district's October 2010 FTE Survey.  The results of the non-compliance consisted of 21,246 classes (all grades) over the class size cap on 103,238 total classes or 20.58%.  Compliance with fiscal year 2010-11 class size reduction maximums is calculated at the classroom level for traditional schools as required F.S. 1003.3(1),F.S.



On January 13, 2011, FLDOE notified the district of a potential class size reduction categorical reduction of $15,810,246.  Section 1003.03(4)(e),F.S., requires each district and charter school not in compliance to submit to the Commissioner by February 15, a plan that describes the specific actions that will be taken to fully comply with class size reduction requirements by October 2011.  A compliance plan for each traditional public school is attached and must be approved by the school board prior to submission.  By submitting a compliance plan, the CSR reduction will be reduced by at least seventy-five percent (75%) and lower the potential penalty to a maximum of $3,952,562.



The penalty may be further reduced provided funds remain after compliant districts receive CSR bonuses.  For traditional public schools, the balance of the reduction remaining after the reallocation to districts that are in compliance will be added back to the district's class size categorical allocation for those districts that are not in compliance but have submitted a certified plan by the required deadline.  The reallocation is based on each qualifying district's proportion of the total reduction for all qualifying districts for which a reduction was calculated.  No district or charter school shall have an amount added back that is greater than the amount reduced.  Districts or charter schools that submit a compliance plan after the February 15, 2011 deadline will not be eligible to participate in the reallocation calculation.



In order to meet class size requirements in October, 2011, SDPBC will utilize select class size reduction strategies as listed in Section 1003.03(3) and (5),F.S., as well as strategies not listed in statute.  As provided in the FLDOE CSR Compliance Plan template, the plan addresses each school on an individual basis.  This document represents a working plan and is subject to change as the district implements the plan over the next several months.  Components of the ultimate implementation plan may require new or revised School Board policies and be subject to collective bargaining. 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the District's 2010-11 Class Size Reduction Compliance Plan and adopt a Resolution certifying the plan under Section 1003.03(4)(e), Florida Statutes and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all associated documents and authorize the submission of the Resolution and the CSR Plan to the Commissioner of Education to be received no later than February 15, 2011.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

Board approval and submission of the CSR Compliance Plan reduces the potential non-compliance categorical reduction from $15,810,246 to $3,952,562 or $11,857,684.  This represents a 75% reduction to the potential penalty.
