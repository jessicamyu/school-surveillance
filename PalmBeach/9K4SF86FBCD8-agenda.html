Subject
School Concurrency
Meeting
Aug 31, 2011 - Workshops
Category
Workshop Item(s)
Type
Discussion

In 2001, the School District entered into an Interlocal Agreement (ILA) with the County and 26 participating municipalities to become the first County to implement School Concurrency.  At the time, School Concurrency was a local option.



In 2005, under SB 360, School Concurrency became mandatory.  However, since Palm Beach County had already implemented School Concurrency under the 2002 optional requirement, the State informed the District that the new mandatory requirements would not require implementation until 2011, when the County conducts its Evaluation and Appraisal Report for its Comprehensive Plan.



The original School Concurrency ILA expired on January 25, 2011, ten years after its effective date.  Just as we were preparing to implement the mandatory School Concurrency provisions, House Bill 7207, was adopted by the Florida Legislature during its 2011 session.  This new Growth Management Legislation entitled "Community Planning Act" again makes School Concurrency a local option.



The provisions for School Concurrency under HB 7207 are similar to those in effect at the time that School Concurrency was first implemented in Palm Beach County on June 27, 2002.



While the new legislation makes School Concurrency optional, it continues to require and emphasize coordinated planning with local governments through an Interlocal Agreement.



During the workshop, District staff will be seeking Board direction regarding whether School Concurrency should continue to be implemented in Palm Beach County and whether other options should be explored.
