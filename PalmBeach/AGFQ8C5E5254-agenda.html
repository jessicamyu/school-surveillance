DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County has made significant investments over the years in writing and developing instructional content for teachers that is aligned to Standards.  This content is organized and housed in Blender, a product from SRG Technology and a vendor to the District.  Blender ensures that every teacher at every school has the ability to utilize high quality, standards based teaching content in providing first class instruction to our students.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Interlocal Agreement with Palm Beach County from January 18, 2017 through January 18, 2018; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The purpose of this Interlocal Agreement is to enable the Board and county to utilize each other's personnel, expertise and services, and provide mutually agreed upon Art Education to the School District of Palm Beach County Students with Disabilities.

 

Art Education relates to performing and visual art skills. Board facilities are facilities on real property owned or operated by the Board, excluding facilities that are leased, licensed or under the contractual control of others.

 

The School Board will allow facilities to be used by the County for Art Education at no cost or expense to the county and are available to students ages 4-22 during the school day by working in conjunction with the office of Exceptional Student Education.

 

The County will coordinate, with the Department of Exceptional Student Education and individual principals,the provision of trained visual and performing art instructors for students with disabilities. The County will also provide all costs associated with the program including staff, supplies and equipment, curriculum and contractual instructors.

FMA2 Tangible Personal Property Disposal Report – June 30, 2016
Meeting
Jan 18, 2017 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
14. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board receive and accept the Disposal of Tangible Property Report for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2016.

DESCRIPTION:

Florida Statute, Section 274.07, requires the District record the disposal of tangible personal property in its minutes.  Generally Accepted Accounting Principles require assets which are considered to be nonfunctional or damaged beyond economic repair be removed from property records.

 

During fiscal year 2016, the District disposed of approximately 2,740 assets with a net book value of approximately $240,000, and an original acquisition cost of $20.1 million.  Approximately $135,000 was collected from scrap and auction proceeds which partially offsets the remaining net book value.  A breakdown of disposed assets by age and the percentage of items that met or exceeded their life expectancy is provided on page 2 of the attached report.

 

Notable disposals (with original acquisition cost) include:

  • $6.3 million of obsolete software ranging from 5 to 20 years old (see listing provided on page 3).
  • 78 buses with an original cost of $6.0 million, which ranged in age from 8 to 20 years old (the District purchased 135 buses during 2015 and 2016).
  • 66 other vehicles with original cost of $1.1 million, which ranged from 12 to 37 years old.
  • $2.2 million of technology related items that were obsolete and /or scrapped.  Technology changes very quickly and 98% of these assets ranged in age from 5 to 32 years old.
  • $1.8 million of electronic related items that were obsolete and / or scrapped.  This type of equipment, like technology changes very quickly and 99% of these assets ranged in age from 5 to 21 years old.
  • $1.3 million of classroom assets that were mainly disposed of as obsolete and / or scrapped, 99% of these assets ranged in age from 7 to 38 years old.
  • $1.1 million of cafeteria equipment that were obsolete and / or scrapped, 93% of these assets ranged in age from 7 - 35 years old.

The second, or Aviation Maintenance Science (AMS) Track, will provide students with the General portion of the FAA Airframe and Power plant Mechanics Certificate curriculum.  This program has been in existence since 2007.
