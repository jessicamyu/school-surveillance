Clifford E. Ripley was born and raised in Massachusetts, moving with his family to Lake Worth in 1947. He received his B. S. Degree in Engineering and his Masters Degree in Education from The University of Miami. His Educational Specialist Degree is from the University of Florida.  During World War II he served in the Pacific arena with the U. S. Navy Sea Bees.



Mr. Ripley was Principal at Palm Beach Public School and Seacrest High School from 1965-1970. He served as Assistant Superintendent  in Administrative Services from 1970 to his retirement in 1980.  He was founder and chairperson of the Palm Beach County Math Council, President of the Palm Beach County Classroom Teachers Association, President of Elementary Principals, President of Junior High Principals, and President of Senior High Principals.



His civic affiliations are numerous. He served 15 years on the Bethesda Hospital Foundation Board founding the employees' childhood center, named "The Clifford and Madeline Ripley Early Childhood Center". He was the first president of the Quail Ridge Property Owners Association in 1980 and former President of the Palm Beach County Mental Health Association. For 47 years, he has been a member of Rotary International with membership primarily in the Rotary Club of Delray Beach.



In 1977, Mr. Ripley founded and is Chairman of the Scholastic Achievement Foundation of Palm Beach County. He envisioned honoring students for "scholastic athletes".  Every year, the Foundation holds a banquet at the Breakers Hotel with distinguished keynote speakers. The program includes outstanding students from every local public, private, and parochial high school in Palm Beach County. Over 2,800 students have been honored in the banquet's 29 year history with more than 140 scholarships being awarded through the Foundation.

Florida Statute 1012.42 gives each district school board the authority to allow teachers to teach out-of-field.  Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction. This contract is written to provide students recommended for out of school suspension the option to attend school at an Alternative to Suspension site.  There they receive interim educational services in a safe, structured environment that reinforces traditional academic concepts as well as character education and career exploration skills.



Program benefits include:



*   The opportunity for students to continue their academic momentum  without being penalized.



*   Offers social and emotional skill building opportunities in addition to job seeking and interviewing skills.



*   Allows students to receive full credit for attendance.



* Provides an academic curriculum that includes FCAT preparation for strategic reading, mathematics and writing.



*    Parents are required to attend an Orientation Session for registration including program rules and requirements.



* The Lake Worth/Osborne Alternative to Suspension site will be a receiving facility for truant students who are accompanied to the site by law enforcement officers.  If such students have been suspended from the sending school and assigned to the program, said student may receive the benefits of the program offerings.
