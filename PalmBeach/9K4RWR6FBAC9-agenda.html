On or about December 6, 2007, Michael Rice became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of inappropriate language and/or behavior with or to students, failure to exercise best professional judgment, and unnecessary physical force on a student.  Furthermore, the allegations included potential violations of School Board Policy 0.01, Commitment to the Student (2)(c), and/or Policy 0.01, Commitment to the Student (2)(d), and/or Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and/or staff, and/or Florida Administrative Code, Department of Education 6B-1.001 (3), Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and/or Florida Administrative Code, Department of Education 6B-1.001 (2), Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and/or 6B-1.006 (3) (a) Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida, and/or 6B-1.006 (3) (e) Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida.



On or about October 18, 2007, an incident occurred at Coral Sunset  Elementary School involving Michael Rice and a first grade student.  Seven witnesses observed Mr. Rice grab the student by the backpack, and be verbally aggressive toward the student.  During the pre-disciplinary meeting, Mr. Rice stated that during the incident, he did use an elevated voice, and that he did not recall but may have touched the backpack of the student.  Based on the foregoing facts the allegations against Mr. Rice include inappropriate language and/or behavior with or to students, failure to exercise best professional judgment, and unnecessary physical force on a student.

Recommendation:

Michael Rice offered to resign on March 12, 2008.  I recommend that the School Board does not approve his resignation.



I recommend that the School Board approve Michael Rice's suspension without pay, effective March 27, 2008 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the March 26, 2008, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Darron Davis; Kenyetta Haywood

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District.
