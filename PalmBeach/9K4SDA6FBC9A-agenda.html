P1 RFP 12C-009C Banking Services
Meeting
Jun 6, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

Approve the request for School Banking Services with Bank of America, N.A.



On April 5, 2012 Request for Proposal, RFP 12C-009C for Banking Services was solicited.  The RFP was approved by the Finance Committee prior to release.



The RFP for Banking Services consisted of three (3) sections:



General Banking Services - which includes processing of deposit checks, direct deposit of payroll, fraud prevention, and reconcilement services.  The District also requested free banking general services for employees and the ability for our employees to cash District checks at no cost.  Additionally, the District requested a free electronic alternative to direct deposit for employees that do not have a bank relationship.  This will save employees the time of going to the bank and the cost of having checks cashed at a check cashing store.



School Banking Services - includes general banking services for the school's internal accounts.  By centralizing the accounts to a single bank, the District could gain better volume discounts.  Additionally, the billing statements were centralized and will be managed by the District's Treasury department.  The District will have oversight on these bank accounts to ensure the funds are safe and coordinate any banking issues which will save time for the school treasurer.



Custody Services - includes holding the District's investments, providing market valuations and necessary reports.



The Evaluation Committee, consisting of District staff and a member of the Finance Committee, convened and reviewed all responsive submissions.  It was the recommendation of the Evaluation Committee to award the School Banking Services to Bank of America, N.A.



The Finance Committee reviewed the recommendation during their meeting on May 22, 2012 and recommended that staff should present the contract for School Banking Services to the School Board for consideration.



The Treasury Department and the Finance Committee recommended proceeding with the School Banking Services portion of the RFP immediately so that it can be in place before the end of the school year for a smooth transition prior to the new school year.  The Treasury Department will oversee the conversion by ordering required supplies for the schools. 



Upon completion of negotiations a recommendation regarding the General Banking Services and Custody Services portions of the RFP will be brought to the Board as a separate item for approval.



It was the recommendation of Diversity in Business Practices that minority points be awarded based on the responders' ability to provide the percentages of their budget allocated to loans, grants, business development and Community Reinvestment Act (CRA) activities that assist SBE/MWBE firms.  Eight (8) out of a maximum of ten (10) points were awarded to only Branch Banking and Trust Company and Bank of America, N.A.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the School Banking Services with Bank of America, N.A. for the period of June 7, 2012 through June 6, 2015 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Operating Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)
Leanne Evans, Treasurer (leanne.evans@palmbeachschools.org)

Financial Impact:

There will be no financial impact.  All fees will be offset by balances maintained in the School and District bank accounts.  No bank fees will be assessed to any school.

On May, 20, 2002, JFK Medical Center Charter School was granted a five (5) year contract to serve students grades K-5, which ended on June 30, 2007.  On July 18, 2007, JFK Medical Center Charter School was granted a renewed Charter Contract for ten (10) years to serve grades K-8 and that Charter Contract will end on June 30, 2017.



 JFK Medical Center Charter School was granted the status of a high-performing charter school evidenced by the attached letter from the Florida Department of Education, pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(1), and the Governing Board is requesting that the current Charter Contract be extended for an additional five (5) years, ending June 30, 2022.



"A high-performing charter school is authorized to:  (e):  Receive a modification of its charter to a term of 15 years or a 15-year charter renewal.  The charter may be modified or renewed for a shorter term at the option of the high-performing charter school.  The charter must be consistent with s. 1002.33(7)(a)19, and (10)(h) and (i), is subject to annual review by the sponsor and may be terminated during its term pursuant to s. 1002.33(8)."



The mission of JFK is to, "Provide a work place charter public school of choice for the employees of JFK Medical Center as a natural evolution of their Work/Life balance initiatives."
