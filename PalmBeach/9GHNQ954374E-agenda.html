We have been implementing School-wide Positive Behavior Intervention Support (SwPBS) in our district for a few years. Golden Grove is the first school in our district that has applied for model school status.

 

SwPBS is guided by Essential Components which include:

Teach students what is expected of them and pro social skills

Utilize data based decision making to address and solve school-wide behavior concerns

Develop a continuum of behavior and academic interventions and supports

Monitor student performance and progress continuously

 

Bronze Model School Status entails implementation of a comprehensive system of support at the Core level that ultimately improves student outcomes. Golden Grove’s Universal Guidelines for all students is that Gators ROCK (R= Respect & Responsibility, O= Ownership, C=Citizenship, K=Kindness).

 

The rock theme is infused throughout the school by expectations taught and posted around campus at every location for all staff members.  Teachers implement classroom management systems directly tied to their Universal Guidelines that promote “Rockin’ behavior”.

I1 Approval to Purchase Masters Builders' Risk Insurance
Meeting
Mar 12, 2014 - REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board aprove the purchase of the Masters Builders' Risk Insurance Policy with Lexington Insurance through the broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., effective March 31, 2014 to March 31, 2015, at a premium cost of up to .7655 per $100 of construction costs.

DESCRIPTION:

The policy will cover damage to the property being built as well as theft of materials from the site during the construction phase for the new and replacement schools/buildings.  This policy covers all facilities started during the policy period and those currently still under construction.  While our major construction is winding down, we still have sufficient values to purchase this product.

 

The Masters Builders' Risk Policy eliminates the need to go directly to the contractor for a project-specific, stand-alone builders' risk policy.  Over the past several years, the program saved 70 percent of contractor-purchased builders' risk coverage.  The District purchases the insurance and premiums are charged back to the individual construction projects.

 

The policy will have annual aggregate limits of $25 million, subject to a sub-limit of $10 million for any one other wind loss occurrence and $10 million aggregate in one policy year for Named Windstorm.  Named Windstorm also has a five (5) percent deductible (Self-Insurance Retention), subject to a $250,000 minimum.  However, because the District owns the policy rather than the contractor, the District will be eligible for FEMA reimbursements, for which a contractor would not be eligible.

 

The deposit premium is based on anticipated construction values of approximately $62,767,264 in major construction to be completed in the policy year.  Therefore, the premium will be approximately $491,538.11 (inclusive of assessments and fees), of which we pay 50 percent now or $245,771.57, subject to annual audit adjustments.

 

The broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc. will receive a commission of six (6) percent on this policy.  In addition to replacing the insurance, they will be responsible for tracking all payments, any changes relevant to this coverage, and all reconciliations at year end.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the attached Tentative Agreement containing the 2014 negotiated modifications to the January 1, 2012-December 31, 2014 SEIU/FPSU Collective Bargaining Agreements (Regular, Supervisory and Paraprofessional IIs/ECPs).

DESCRIPTION:

A Tentative Agreement has been reached on modifications to the 2012-2014 Collective Bargaining Agreement between The School District of Palm Beach County and The Service Employees International Union-Florida Public Services Union (SEIU-FPSU).  This Tentative Agreement reflects the following negotiated changes to the Agreement:

  1. A 4 percent salary increase, effective January 1, 2014, is awarded to each SEIU-FPSU represented employee who was an employee of the District on the date the School Board approves these modifications.
  2. Effective January 1, 2014 the minimum salaries of all salary levels are increased by 3 percent and the maximum salaries of all salary levels are increased by 4 percent.

I recommend the School Board grant permission for staff to pursue an 8-unit modular relocation and parking lot expansion project at the Adult Education Center in West Palm Beach to better serve the community for GED Testing.

  1. The replacement of wooden portables with concrete units will make more efficient use of the space, enabling a parking area expansion because the units can be constructed side-by-side.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the remodeling of Santaluces High School Cafeteria project in an amount not to exceed $550,000.

DESCRIPTION:

Remodel the cafeteria and serving lines at Santaluces High School.  To enhance customer service, improvements to the serving area will include new menu boards, decorative lighting, and additional support equipment. Improvements to the multipurpose room will include Bahama shutters, faux columns, signage, new flooring and televisions.  Construction will begin in May 2015 for completion prior to the students returning in August 2015.

 

JUSTIFICATION:

  • School Food Service (SFS) developed a plan of action with the cooperation of the State (Office of Food, Nutrition and Wellness) to reinvent the way students perceive and receive their school meals.  This plan took into consideration the meal time provided, physical facility, customer service, nutritious food offerings, and the overall dining experience.  Since its inception, the High School Reengineering Program (HSRP) has not only met, but exceeded the expectations of SFS.
  • Increased lunch participation is one key identifier of the program's success. 
    • As the pilot program, Atlantic High School has sustained a 22% increase in lunch participation since FY 2011. This represents an additional 200 students being fed lunch every school day as a direct result of the cafeteria modification.
    • While JFK Middle School’s lunch program had high participation to start with, the effect of the cafeteria modification was a rise in lunch participation to over 88% of students.
    • Forest Hill High School has seen an 11% increase in lunch participation since the cafeteria was modified this past summer. An additional 130 students are being fed lunch every school day as a direct result of the cafeteria modification.
  • Based on the success of the HSRP, SFS would like to expand the program to an additional High School. Santaluces High School was selected due to the potential for growth in the lunch program and the support of the Principal, Dr. Kathleen Weigel.  
  • With these modifications, students will enjoy a customer driven dining experience with the opportunity to nutritionally recharge for improved physical and mental well-being; supporting the District's goal of improving academic performance.
  • The reuse of architectural drawings will be taken into consideration for future site selection.
