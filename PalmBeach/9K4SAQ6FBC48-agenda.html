AVID, Advancement Via Individual Determination, is a college readiness system that is designed to increase school wide learning and performance. The AVID system accelerates student learning, uses research based methods of effective instruction, provides meaningful and motivational professional development, and acts as a catalyst for systemic reform and change.  Five high schools have received certification as AVID sites by the AVID Center: Atlantic, Glades Central, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, and Santaluces. 



Although AVID serves all students, it focuses on the least served students in the academic middle.  The formula is simple - raise expectations of students and, with the AVID support system in place, they will rise to the challenge.
 
What differentiates AVID from other educational reform programs is its astounding success rate. Since 1990, more than 85,500 AVID students have graduated from high school and planned to attend college. Of the 22,210 AVID 2010 seniors who reported their plans, 91.3% intended to attend a postsecondary institution; 58.3% in four-year institutions and 33.0% in two-year institutions.

Meeting

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded the 21st Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) competitive grant from the Florida Department of Education for up to $3,080,000, for a five-year period from August 1, 2011 through July 31, 2016.  The Florida Department of Education did not notify the District of the grant award until August 31, 2011.  The Florida Department of Education will not be releasing the funds until the middle of October after all awardees attend a project meeting and therefore, the program will not begin until the end of October. 



The 21st CCLC Grant supports community learning centers for academic enrichment opportunities during afterschool and summer.  The programs implemented will  assist students in meeting  standards in core academic subjects, such as reading and math; offer students a broad array of enrichment activities to complement their regular academic programs; and offer literacy and other educational services to the families of participating children.



The 21st CCLC Grant targets five elementary schools with a high percentage of low income students:
*  Lantana Elementary
*  Palmetto Elementary
*  South Grade Elementary
*  Starlight Cove Elementary
*  West Gate Elementary



These schools were selected based on the grant requirements, they are Title I schools with large English Language Learners (ELL) populations; former Beacon sites, and communities/neighborhoods that have been designated as high risk by the Childrens' Services Council.  These schools matched the criteria.



The 21st CCLC grant budget will support 55 at-risk students in grades K-5 at each school and will be selected to participate based on the following criteria:
*  below grade level on recent school and/or state assessments
*  students who have been retained or in jeopardy of being retained
*  ELL and/or ESE students
*  vulnerable students:  foster children or children displaced from the earthquakes or natural disasters



The 21st CCLC Grant goals established for all participating students and their families will:
1)  improve academic performance
2)  increase social competence
3)  increase parental involvement and family literacy
4)  improve physical fitness and lifestyle choices



The 21st CCLC Grant requires and funds an external evaluator who will assess quantitative and qualitative data to determine the project's impact on these goals.



Services will be provided free to students and their families, including afterschool and summer programs.  Student activities will include:
*  Literacy enrichment
*  Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) education
*  Arts and music
*  Technology
*  Tutoring and mentoring
*  Service Learning
*  Physical Education, recreation, and wellness
*  Dropout prevention, character education, and drug/violence prevention



Parent/Caregiver activities focus of family literacy, including parent education in literacy and computer skills; parenting skills to support their child's learning; and inter-generational literacy:  Parent and Child Together Time (PACT) to promote literacy and language development.



The total budget request for the 21st CCLC Grant is $3,080,000 for five years.  The maximum award is $700,000 per award for years 1 and 2; and $560,000 for years 3, 4, & 5.  A copy of the grant is available for review in the Board Minutes Office.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the 21st Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) Grant from the Florida Department of Education for up to $3,080,000, effective August 1, 2011 through July 31, 2016, and approve any no cost extension as applicable; and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

On August 1, 2011, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Somerset Academy Canyons – High School ("Somerset Canyons HS") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.  Somerset Canyons HS is a high-performing charter school evidenced by the attached letter from the Florida Department of Education verifying a high-performing charter school operating in Dade County (see Exhibit "A").  Per F.S. 1002.331(1) High-performing charter schools - A charter school is a high-performing charter school if it:



(a)    Received at least two school grades of "A" and no school grade below "B," pursuant to s. 1008.34, during each of the previous 3 school years.



(b)   Received an unqualified opinion on each annual financial audit required under s. 218.39 in the most recent 3 fiscal years for which such audits are available.



(c)    Did not receive a financial audit that revealed one or more of the financial emergency conditions set forth in s. 218.503(1) in the most recent 3 fiscal years for which such audits are available.  However, this requirement is deemed met for a charter school-in-the-workplace if there is a finding in an audit that the school has the monetary resources available to cover any reported deficiency or that the deficiency does not result in a deteriorating financial condition pursuant to s. 1002.345(1)(a)3.



(d)   F.S. 1002.331(3)(a)   . . . If the sponsor fails to act on the application within 60 days after receipt, the application is deemed approved and the procedure in s. 1002.33(6)(h) applies.  However, the applicant agreed to an extension (see attached Extension Letter) beyond the statutory time line.



The mission of Somerset Canyons HS is, "To provide a college preparatory educational environment that furthers the philosophy of respect and high expectations for all, enabling students to become confident, self-directed, and responsible life-long learners." 



Its application was written utilizing the Model Florida Charter School Application (see Exhibit "B").  Thereafter, designated School District of Palm Beach County ("District") reviewers evaluated their application using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "C"). 



The District’s staff reviewers of charter school applications were trained to evaluate the Model Florida Charter School Application utilizing the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument on May 16, 2011 (see Exhibit "D").



The District’s charter application process was explained to applicants during the New Applicants’ Workshop held on May 27, 2011 (see Exhibit "E").



A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

A.     Section 7 – English Language Learners



The English Language Learners section should demonstrate an understanding of the requirements of the school to serve English Language Learner students and provide a concrete plan for meeting the broad spectrum of educational needs and providing all students with a quality education.



 



Statutory Reference(s):



 



F.S. 1002.33(16)(a)(3)



Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument Criteria:



 



A response that meets the standard will present:





  • Demonstrated understanding of state and federal requirements regarding the education of English language learner students.

  • Demonstrated a commitment to serving the full range of needs of English language learner students.

  • Sound plans for educating English language learner students that reflect the full range of programs and services required to provide all students with a high quality education.

On June 22, 2011, the Board approved the recommendation of the Superintendent to suspend without pay for fifteen (15) days and terminate the employment of Priscilla Bowers-Maloney.  The termination was to be effective July 7, 2011; Ms. Bowers-Maloney requested an appeal from the State of Florida Division of Administrative Hearings (DOAH).
