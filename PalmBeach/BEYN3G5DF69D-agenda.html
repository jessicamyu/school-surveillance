The Recycle Florida Today, Environmental Sustainability Award recognizes programs that display an increased awareness of the importance of sustainability and ability to motivate others to maximize their recycling, composting, grass-roots efforts, waste reduction, sustainability efforts and improve their recycling rates through education, outreach and by example. Environmental & Conservation Services Department leads the School District of Palm Beach County’s Sustainable Schools Initiative, which develops innovative programs to support schools to reduce waste, recycle, conserve water and energy, and promote environmentally-friendly practices.

 

Environmental initiatives in departments such as School Food Service, Facilities Construction, and Maintenance and Plant Operations also helped the District earn the Environmental Sustainability award including: reducing food waste through sharing tables, wellness promotion, managing energy usage with scheduled air conditioning and lighting systems, and green construction for new schools.

Their goal is to provide supplies to all of the Title I schools in Delray Beach to support continued arts education in public schools.

DESCRIPTION:

In an effort to recruit and develop the best teachers for our highest needs schools, the NYU Teacher Residency Program began in August 2019 at two District schools (JFK Middle and Palm Beach Lakes High). Seven residents went through a rigorous screening process by both NYU and the District to be selected for a year-long internship at the schools while they completed a master’s degree in Secondary Education at New York University.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Park Vista High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.
