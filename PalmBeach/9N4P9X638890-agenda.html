I recommend the School Board approve the Second Amendment to the Charter Agreement for Boca Raton Charter School, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Boca Raton Charter School’s Charter Agreement expired June 30, 2014.  The Renewal Program Review was approved by the School Board on March 26, 2014, and the School Board and Boca Raton Charter School have been and continue to be in the process of negotiating terms and conditions of a renewal Charter Agreement.  On June 4, 2014, at the Special Board Meeting, the School Board approved the School’s First Amendment to Extend Charter Agreement for two months, until September 2, 2014. 

  

In order to complete meaningful negotiations to secure a mutually beneficial and legally binding Charter Agreement, the parties agree that it is necessary for their mutual benefit and protection that the term of the current Charter Agreement must be extended.

 

Therefore, through this Extension Agreement, the parties would agree that the term for existence of the current Charter Agreement is hereby extended for one month from its expiration, until October 1, 2014.

 

All other terms of the Charter Agreement shall remain in full force and effect, subject to further extensions of time, as mutually deemed necessary by the parties.  This Second Amendment, together with the Charter and previous amendments, constitutes and represents the entire agreement between the parties hereto and supersedes any prior understandings or agreements, written or verbal, respecting the subject matter herein.

 

Copies of the Second Amendment to Extend,  2009 Contract, First Amendment to Extend, Renewal Program Review, Legal Checklist, Board Notification, Data Panels, and Matrix for August 27th Agenda Items are attached.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the cooperative agreement with Florida Atlantic University from August 13, 2014 through June 30,2017, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA) requires school districts to provide Free and Appropriate Education through the year of the 22nd birthday for students with disabilities who have not graduated with a standard diploma.  Currently there are 352 students with disabilities identified in the district between the ages of 18-22 returning after receiving a special diploma.  

 

This agreement will enable up to 12 students with disabilities, ages 18 to 22, an educational environment to develop transitional life skills and increase the students' opportunity to practice age appropriate life skills outside the traditional classroom setting on a high school campus.
 
The ultimate goal of this transitional life skills education program is to teach students the skills needed for independent living.  Transitional life skills education will consist of money management classes, use of public transportation, accessing community services and vocational training consisting of on-the-job training in a variety of department settings at Florida Atlantic University, such as but not limited to the bookstore, cafeteria, library, and maintenance departments. 
 
The students will receive daily living and vocational education from a certified School Board teacher.  Job training will be provided by a School Board job trainer.  Additional supports will be provided by the School Board ESE Department Transition Services.
 
The Agency will facilitate participants access to Florida Atlantic University's facilities and student activities, including the computer lab, fitness center, career center, library, recreation and athletic facilities.  Additionally, the Agency will facilitate and encourage University departments to provide non-paid career experience for the participating students.
 
 
The purpose of this Agreement is to set forth the Board and FAU's duties and responsibilities for providing personnel, expertise, and facilities relating to the provision of Transitional Life Skills and educational services to School District of Palm Beach County Students with Disabilities between the ages of 18 to 22.
