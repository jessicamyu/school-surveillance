PA3 National Mentoring Month
Meeting
Jan 16, 2013 - School Board Meeting 1-16-13
Category
Presentations
Type
Action (Consent)

Mentoring provides an exemplary opportunity for youth in Palm Beach County to have more positive role models in their lives.  Research shows that mentored youth are 52% less likely to skip a day of school, 46% less likely to start using drugs, and 27% less likely to start drinking.  Mentoring is a true partnership, requiring businesses, volunteers, administrators, teachers, students, parents, and private and public community members to work together to provide a successful program.  National Mentoring Month provides an opportunity to recognize and commend the efforts of these programs and raise community awareness of the importance of mentoring.



The School District partners with a number of organizations to provide mentors for our students.  Take Stock in Children is one of those organizations.  The mission of Take Stock in Children is to passionately promote personal worth, self-responsibility, and academic success for deserving children from low-income families by providing a unique set of resources including mentors, scholarships, long-term support, student advocacy, and a guaranteed educational opportunity.
 
Take Stock in Children is a comprehensive program that helps children succeed by providing college and vocational scholarships, volunteer mentors; student advocates/case managers, tutoring, early intervention and long-term support. High standards, parental involvement, and community support are crucial to the program's success.



Take Stock's Mentors are a vital part or "key" to the success of the program.  They meet with their student once a week for 30 minutes. These men and women in our community give their time, support, encouragement and guidance to help their student achieve their goals in acquiring a college scholarship and becoming a responsible and motivated adult with a passion to succeed.
 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County proclaims the month of January 2013 as National Mentoring Month.

The Office of General Counsel is responsible for providing legal advice to the School Board and School District staff.  Lawyers heavily rely on and utilize an online service to conduct legal research.  Access to an online service is substantially cheaper than purchasing and maintaining hard copies of books, which must be updated regularly.  In addition to the cost savings, the search features and functionality of an online service increases attorneys' and paralegals' productivity while decreasing the time necessary to research, evaluate, and resolve novel and significant legal issues and/or disputes.




This Subscriber License Agreement gives the Office of the General Counsel a non-exclusive license to access the Westlaw law libraries and databases.  The office has used Westlaw for over eleven years.





Westlaw is one of the world's most comprehensive legal research engines.  The service caters to the needs and interests of practitioners in the legal profession.  Information resources on Westlaw include more than 40,000 databases of case law, state and federal statutes, administrative codes, newspaper and magazine articles, public records, law journals, law reviews, American law reports, legislation, treatises, legal forms, and other information resources.


 


The Westlaw online legal research service is the service most commonly used by governmental entities in Florida.  We were able to negotiate a cost-effective plan and also allow us to upgrade our current plan for same price.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board authorize the Office of the General Counsel to enter into an agreement with Westlaw to use its online research tool effective December 1, 2012 through November 30, 2013, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all related documents.

IN1 Diabetes Prevention and Control Program Grant
Meeting
Jan 16, 2013 - School Board Meeting 1-16-13
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Diabetes Prevention and Control Program is sponsored by the Florida Department of Health.  It is funded in the amount of $34,300, paid throughout the year, based upon performance and completion of tasks and deliverables.  This grant was written by our Wellness Coordinator, Kimberly Sandmaier, who will be coordinating the events required.



The purpose of this program is to ensure that adults with or at high risk for chronic diseases in the School District of Palm Beach County have access to information and programs to best manage their conditions.  The National Center for Chronic Disease Prevention and Health Promotion  works to address chronic diseases throughout the community. This program refers patients to evidence-based programs that increase the likelihood that people with heart disease, diabetes or prediabetes and arthritis will be able to "follow doctors' orders" and take charge of their health - improving their quality of life, averting or delaying onset or progression of disease, avoiding complication and reducing the need for additional health care. We will be focusing on diabetes and prediabetes.



The following are the requirements of this grant:





  1. Provide baseline information from The District's previous Health Risk Assessment (HRA).

  2. Complete three (3) screening events.

  3. Collaborate with UnitedHealthcare (UHC) to identify those with prediabetes or suspected diabetes and carry out targeted marketing to recruit these participants into a 16-week diabetes prevention program that is recognized by the Center for Disease Control and Prevention.

  4. Implement at least one 16-week diabetes prevention program in collaboration with The Palm Beach County Health Department.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve the receipt of this Grant in the amount of $34,300.

The School District of Palm Beach County is applying for two (2) competitive STEM Grant Projects from the Florida Department of Education to support science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education in eligible elementary schools. The purpose of the program is to permit school districts to use funds under the 21st Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) Program to support high-quality, enrichment learning activities during the school day in eligible schools.



The two grant applications target a total of eight (8) elementary schools, four schools in each grant.



The STEM Inquiry Lab (iLab) Project includes:







  • Grove Park Elementary




  • Galaxy Elementary




  • Barton Elementary




  • Northmore Elementary


The STEM Investigation Lab (iLab) Project includes:







  • Dr. Mary McLeod Bethune Elementary




  • Belle Glade Elementary




  • Pioneer Park Elementary




  • West Riviera Elementary


The participating schools were selected based requirements as defined by the grant program. Each is a Title I school that received a grade of "D" within the last two years, and each school has been mandated to provide expanded learning time. 



This program will establish the STEM iLab in each school, staffed with a certified teacher to serve K-5 students on a rotational schedule throughout the year.



The Stem iLab activities will focus on the Engineering is Elementary (EIE) curriculum, a research-based, standards-driven program, to provide hands-on, authentic, project-based learning experiences to foster an understanding of engineering concepts within a meaningful context for learning elementary science and mathematics. 



The goals in both grants are for students to improve academic performance and to increase social competence and teamwork. The grant program requires and funds an external evaluator who will assess quantitative and qualitative data to determine the project's impact on these goals.



If funded, each grant project will receive up to $500,000 per year, or $1.5 million over the three-year project period. If both grants are funded, the total for two awards will be up to $3 million over the three-year project period. Copies of the STEM grant applications are available for review in the Board Office. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board accept the STEM grants from the Florida Department of Education if awarded in the amount of up to $3,000,000, effective January 1, 2013 through December 31, 2015  and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities including any no cost extension as applicable.

PR1 Antennae Placement Interlocal Agreement - Palm Beach County
Meeting
Jan 16, 2013 - School Board Meeting 1-16-13
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)
1.  Since 2007, the Palm Beach County School District and Palm Beach County have collaborated, as a part of the Palm Beach Broadband Consortium for technology projects. Included in the collaboration is the Digital Divide project, which targets and supports communities with a high population of public school students receiving free and reduced lunches.
2.  The goal of the Digital Divide project is to provide access to digital resources housed on the School District's Learning Tools website and prepare students with technological skill sets for the Digital Age Society.
3.  The School District provides recycled and or refurbished computers and Palm Beach County provides free wireless internet service to the families of the students that participate in the Digital Divide program.
4.  In order to provide wireless internet access, the County requires access to School District Instructional Television (ITV) towers to install a small radio antenna on the tower.  After the radio antenna(s) are installed, the County installs additional wireless radio antennas on Florida Power and Light poles located throughout the targeted neighborhoods.
5.  This Interlocal Agreement will institute a formal process whereby the School District can review on a case by case basis the County's request to place antenna(s) on specific District ITV towers.   The antennae may also be used for broadband connectivity for the School District or broadband connectivity for County projects.
6.  Each antennae placement request will require the review and or approval by School District staff for building and code compliance, technical and real estate requirements.
7.  The term of this Agreement shall be for five years and may be renewed with mutual consent by both parties for four 5 year renewal terms.
8.  The County will be responsible for all costs associated with the installation and maintenance of the antennae and related equipment.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement between Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County to allow access to School District Instructional Television and cellular communication towers for the placement of antennae(s) for broadband connectivity and the Digital Divide project.
