Gerald A. Williams Memorial Dedication
Meeting
Sep 15, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action (Consent)

Tuesday, May 25, 2010, we lost a Florida icon, an accomplished attorney, a caring mentor, a role model and friend. Gerald A. Williams, Chief Counsel to The School District of Palm Beach County, was 59 years old.  Mr. Williams served honorably as Chief Counsel since November 2005. Before that, he handled a range of District issues as Chief Negotiator and Chief Officer of Administration. In 2009, Florida Trend magazine honored Mr. Williams by selecting him for its Legal Elite.



He served his community in many ways, including as general counsel for the Virgil Hawkins Florida Chapter of the National Bar Association, as treasurer and executive board member for the Urban League of Palm Beach County, and as co-founder and president of the Suncoast Chamber of Commerce in West Palm Beach.



Born Oct. 9, 1950 in Pensacola, Mr. Williams was among the first African-American graduates of the University of Florida College of Law in 1975. He then helped grow Haygood & Williams into the largest African-American law firm in the state. In 1997, he left private practice to serve as Chief Labor Counsel, and Chief Officer of Labor and Legislative Relations for Dade County.



During his life, Mr. Williams cherished his commitment to educating youth. A Memorial Scholarship which will provide ongoing support and assistance to deserving African-American law students throughout the State of Florida has been established in Gerald A. Williams' name.  At the School District, the Legal Department will be named in honor of Mr. Williams and the Pre-Law Academy at Palm Beach Lakes Community High School will be named in his honor.



 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County proclaims the location of the Office of Chief Counsel, as “The Gerald A. Williams, Esq., Office of Chief Counsel,” effective Wednesday, September 15, 2010.



 



I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County proclaims the Law Academy at Palm Beach Lakes Community High School, as “The Gerald A. Williams, Esquire, Law Academy,” effective Wednesday, September 15, 2010.

Hagen Road Elementary Honored By Alliance For A Healthier Generation For Efforts To Combat Childhood Obesity
Meeting
Sep 15, 2010 - School Board Meeting

Every day in the United States, 53 million people go to a school to work or to learn, making America's schools one of the most important environments in the struggle to reduce childhood obesity. Schools are powerful places to not only teach young people the academic skills they need to succeed, but also healthy life skills. Healthy students learn better, are better able to concentrate on their work, attend school on a regular basis and perform better in class. Because of this, the Alliance for a Healthier Generation launched its Healthy Schools Program in February of 2006 with funding from the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation. The Healthy Schools Program promotes a comprehensive approach by helping schools improve access to healthier foods, increase physical activity opportunities before, during, and after the school day, enhance nutrition education, and establish staff wellness programs.



The Alliance for a Healthier Generation was founded by the American Heart Association and the William J. Clinton Foundation.  Hagen Road Elementary School is the first school in Palm Beach County to receive the Alliance for a Healthier Generation's Healthy Schools Program National award for making changes that promote healthier eating and physical activity for students and staff.  As a Bronze National Recognition Award winner, Hagen Road joins 178 other schools in the country, and 33 schools in Florida that are receiving this honor for their healthy achievements. Kim Sandmaier, Employee Wellness Coordinator who helps coordinate the program attended the annual Healthy Schools Program Forum in New York City to accept the award on behalf of the school.  The award demonstrates Hagen Road Elementary School's commitment to preventing childhood obesity and to fostering the health of the school's students and staff.

AC1 - Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc., Quality Counts Coaches Agreement
Meeting
Sep 15, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

This Agreement with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. provides $3,071,000 to the School District for the period effective October 1, 2010, through September 30, 2011, for the continuation of the Quality Counts (QC) Early Learning Coaches program.  The coaching program is one of a number of components that work together to create a comprehensive, voluntary early care and education quality rating improvement system for Palm Beach County.  The system partners work together to provide services to enhance the quality of early education programs so that children will enter school eager and ready to learn.  The Quality Counts System was initiated by the Children's Services Council of Palm Beach County and a number of stakeholders.  System partners include: Children’s Services Council, Palm Beach State College, Palm Beach County Early Learning Coalition, Family Central and the School District.



 



This grant funds 36 Early Learning Coaches (certified teachers) and related support/administrative staff to serve child care centers and school- based sites that voluntarily participate.  The coaches demonstrate behaviors and practices that can help improve learning environments and activities, as well as enhance interactions between practitioners and children. To be eligible to participate, child care centers must have been licensed for at least one year and must hold a rate agreement with Family Central.  School District classrooms offering the Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) program are participants as are those classrooms in the Delray Full Service Center Head Start program and the Migrant Education program.   



 



The Quality Counts coaches assist with the implementation of improvement plans and strategies developed collaboratively with classroom teachers and child care directors based on data from formal and informal assessments.  The assessments are administered by Family Central assessors, who have been trained to “reliability” and are monitored for proficiency.  Multiple assessment measures are used, including but not limited to, the Early Childhood Environment Rating Scale (ECERS-R) and the Infant/Toddler Environment Rating Scale (ITERS), both nationally recognized, research-based assessment tools. 



 



The Quality Counts System has defined “Pathways to Quality” assessment components, which are proven indicators of quality.  The Pathways to Quality each have achievable benchmarks/standards that are assessed and reported using a weighted formula (in parentheses below) that gives a provider its “Star Rating.”     Star ratings range from 1 to 5 stars.  A 1-Star rating equates to the attainment of current state and local child care licensing standards.  The assessment results are used to develop classroom improvement plans. 



(1) Environment (40%)



(2) Staff Qualifications/Professional Development (20%)



(3) Curriculum (15%)



(4) Program – Family Engagement & Administration (15%)



(5) Staff to Child Ratios and Group Size (10%)



 



Coaching supports are provided using a three-level, consultation approach, based on a coaching model developed by Pat Wesley, a research scientist at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Coaching levels include:



 



Level 1- Intensive



·         Intensive coaching services are based on the number of classrooms and the results of the informal baseline assessment.



·       Onsite intensive services range from 1 day/week up to 2.5 days/week.



Level 2 – Maintenance



·        Maintenance coaching services begin after sites have a formal assessment.  The formal assessment scores are used to award a star rating.



·        Onsite maintenance services range from 2 to 3 days per month for year 1 and from 1 to 2 days per month for year 2 participants.



Level 3 – Model



·        Quarterly visits are scheduled with a child care center director and/or administrative leadership for sites that consistently maintain quality improvements.



·        Follow-up supports may be provided via phone and e-mail.



The Quality Counts System currently has 100 participating child care centers.  The District is both a partner and a voluntary participant and most participating school-based sites have earned the highest possible star rating. Participating public schools include: Barton Elementary, Belle Glade Elementary, Benoist Farms Elementary, Berkshire Elementary, Cholee Lake Elementary, Delray Full Service Center, D.D. Eisenhower Elementary, Elbridge Gale Elementary, Galaxy Elementary, Heritage Elementary, Highland Elementary, Hope-Centennial Elementary, Indian Pines Elementary, K.E. Cunningham /Canal Point Elementary, Lincoln Elementary, Pahokee Elementary, Palm Beach Gardens Elementary, Pine Grove Elementary, Pine Jog Elementary, Roosevelt Elementary, Royal Palm School, Sandpiper Shores Elementary, Starlight Cove Elementary, U.B. Kinsey Elementary, West Riviera Elementary, Westward Elementary, and Wynnebrook Elementary.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve a contract with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. in the amount of $3,071,000 effective from October 1, 2010, until September 30, 2011, and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant that are required for implementation of grant activities.

The objectives of these classes are to: improve the kindergarten readiness of the students; identify children with developmental delays or other health issues; engage parents in their child’s learning; maintain a high quality star rating as measured by the Quality Counts system; and expand the classroom teaching teams’ knowledge of child development and the implementation of best practices. This is the annual contract agreement with the Palm Beach County Education Commission, Inc. and the School District of Palm Beach County.



The Education Commission is a value-added organization.  No one institution can afford to staff a position to assist and support all of the collaborative initiatives.  The Children's Services Council, Florida Atlantic University, Palm Beach State College, Workforce Alliance, the Palm Beach County School District and private funders all contribute financially to the Education Commission.  In turn the Education Commission facilitates, links resources, people, partnerships, and identifies barriers to projects.  The Education Commission never directly initiates or oversees projects.



The most important function of the Education Commission is through the work of its Board.  Its Board is private sector driven and was developed to create a venue for discussion of education issues and solutions.  Public sector CEOs and community leaders meet face-to-face on a regular basis.  The process has created a high level of inter-agency trust and collaboration as well as community support for educational issues.



Projects continuing in 2011 include the following:







  • Grants Consortium - including representation from all funding partners and the Palm Beach County Health Care District, Palm Beach County government, Hispanic Human Resources, PrimeTime PBC, Urban League of Palm Beach County, ASPIRA, and other interested parties to provide networking for grant writing opportunities.




  • Readers/Writers Workshop - expanding the School District's initative by linking to the City of Delray Beach's Get Caught Reading.




  • Wellness Campaign - The campaign was moved from the Education Foundation to the Education Commission because the scope of work was broadened beyond just K-12 programs.




  • Promise Network Initiative - Linking the existing Digital Divide projects to the Promise Neighborhood Initiative for the City of West Palm Beach.




  • Ethics Initiative - Linking the countywide Ethics initiative and Palm Beach State College's Ethics Institute with existing School District projects.




  • Everglades Youth Camp - Partnership with the School District, FAU, county government, and the South Florida Water Management District to provide connectivity at the Everglades Youth Camp.  FAU will expand their teacher training programs, telemetric monitoring and youth opportunities.


In an effort to create a seamless educational system, the Palm Beach County Education Commission will promote services for the care, support, and education of children from birth and continuing through pre-kindergarten until a child enters the formal K-20 education system.



The Palm Beach County Education Commission seeks to improve the knowledge and skills of Palm Beach County's residents based on a supportive learning environment.



Although it is not our practice to back-date agreements, doing so in this case does not prejudice the rights of the Board.  In 2011, the request is made to bring the deliverables to the School Board in June.
