Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Amendment with Walgreens, effective April 22, 2021 through June 30, 2026; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc. In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences, we are adding the above-referenced agreement. This agreement originated with Santaluces High School but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy, and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry-level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

I recommend the Board approve the Consultant Agreement with Creative People Consulting, Inc. for $77,625 for the period of April 27, 2021 through June 30, 2021; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.
DESCRIPTION:
Approve the Consultant Agreement with Creative People Consulting, Inc. to meet the Professional Development training needs for teachers seeking to increase their knowledge within the Computer Science curriculum, computer-programming languages, and to become industry certified in computer science certifications. The purpose of this training is to enhance teacher capacity at various schools throughout our District in the area of computer science education.
 
A total of 69 teachers will complete Microsoft Technology Associate Certification Training. Microsoft offers a number of industry certifications called Microsoft Technology Associate (MTA) exams, which address fundamental technical concepts and assess core technical knowledge. This MTA training will prepare teachers to teach computer science skills in one of two tracks (i.e. Programming and Network or Cybersecurity). Teachers who enroll in the programming courses will acquire skills in the following programming languages: Java, Python, JavaScript, and/or HTML & CC. Teachers who complete the training will be familiar with the features and capabilities of programming languages; and, they will be able to write, debug, and maintain well-formed, well-documented code in the respective programming languages. Teachers who enroll in the networking and cybersecurity courses will be trained in security, networking, and windows operating systems fundamentals. Teachers who complete the training will acquire fundamental security knowledge and skills in Windows Server, Windows-based networking, active directory, anti-malware products, firewalls, network topologies and devices, and network management tools. Teachers who complete the courses will also be prepared to sit for the related MTA exams in the aforementioned.
 
Creative People Consulting, Inc. is a self-reporting M/WBE and SBE vendor.
 
This purchase is considered a Professional Service as defined in Section 1010.04(4)(a)FS and Board Policy 6.14 and therefore is exempt from competition requirements based on the specialized qualifications required to perform these services.

I recommend the Board approve a Third Amendment to the Engagement Letter with Boies Schiller & Flexner LLP and authorize the Superintendent, General Counsel, or Board Chair to sign all necessary documents.
DESCRIPTION:
Third Amendment to Engagement Letter with Boies Schiller & Flexner.
 
Boies Schiller & Flexner LLP (the “Firm”) was engaged in February 2019 to defend the Board in the case styled Academy for Positive Learning, Inc., et al. v. School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida, and later consolidated with the case styled G-Star School of the Arts, Inc. v. School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida.
 
The original contract amount, which was based on the Firm’s best estimate at that time, was $250,000. The not-to-exceed amount was increased by the Board on December 11, 2019 to $408,500 to cover an overage on the original scope of work and to allow for the Firm to handle the appeal (See attached Board Report). However, the actual engagement letter amendment only increased the not-to-exceed amount to $383,500 (See attached December 4, 2019 letter from Stuart Singer). The contract amount was increased a second time on October 21, 2020, when the not-to-exceed amount was increased by $73,500 to $483,500.
 
On April 22, 2020, the 4th District Court of Appeal issued a 2-1 decision in favor of the Board, upholding the trial court’s decision to grant summary judgement. On September 15, 2020 the 4th DCA issued a notice that the Court intended to reconsider their decision en banc and on February 24, 2021 the 4th DCA issued a 7-4 opinion reversing the trial court and original panel’s decision. The Firm filed a Motion to Reconsider with the 4th DCA and that motion is currently pending.
 
The Firm has agreed to handle the additional work associated with the Court’s reconsideration of the en banc decision, and if the request is not granted, to handle an appeal to the Florida Supreme Court, if so authorized by the Board. This would include potentially filing additional briefs, with assistance from attorneys in the Office of General Counsel, and appearing at oral argument, if either of the respective Courts decide to hear them. It has been estimated that the attorney’s fees and costs through this process should not exceed $683,500, an additional $200,000 of spending authority.
 
This purchase is considered a Professional Service as defined in Section 1010.04(4)(a) F.S. and Board Policy 6.14; therefore, the purchase is exempt from competition requirements based on the specialized qualifications required to perform these services.
 
Staff estimates that an unfavorable outcome in this case would result in the Board being required to share between $20 million and $24 million of the Referendum proceeds per year over the course of the four-year term.
