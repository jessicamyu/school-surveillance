CS1 Transitions Elementary - Voluntary Termination
Meeting
Feb 4, 2015 - 2. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING following A/C Session in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board accept the voluntary termination of Transitions Elementary Charter School effective December 19, 2014.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County, (“Sponsor”), entered into a charter agreement with Transitions Elementary commencing July 1, 2012, and the term of the Agreement was for five (5) years, ending on June 30, 2017.  The School deferred opening until August, 2013.  Transitions then requested, and the School Board voted and allowed on June 19, 2013, a second deferral.  Transitions Elementary opened in the third year of its Charter in August, 2014.

 

On December 11, 2014, the Board Chair of Transitions Elementary met with Sponsor and stated that she was taking a request to consider termination of their Charter to their Board.  December 15, 2014, Sponsor met with the Board Chair on campus and was informed that the Governing Board of Transitions Elementary Charter School unanimously elected to terminate the charter on December 19, 2014, due to financial challenges as a result of insufficient enrollment. 

 

Transitions Elementary served students in grades K-5.  The school did not reopen in January, 2015.  There is no negative impact to any student.  There were 74 students enrolled at the school.  The parents were notified by the School and the District that Transitions Elementary would not reopen in January, 2015.

 

Attached are copies of the Contract, Board Notification, and Matrix for February 4, 2015, Agenda Items.

Subject
CS2 Hope Learning Community of Riviera Beach, Inc., d/b/a Noah's Ark - Proposed 90-Day Termination Notice
Meeting
Feb 4, 2015 - 2. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING following A/C Session in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Charter Contract with Hope Learning Community of Riviera Beach, Inc. d/b/a Noah's Ark International Charter School be terminated 90 days after notice to Noah's Ark International Charter School's Governing Board of this proposed termination, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign and provide to Noah's Ark International Charter School a 90-day termination letter with supporting documentation and all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On May 11, 2011, the School Board of Palm Beach County granted Hope Learning Community of Riviera Beach, Inc., d/b/a Noah’s Ark International Charter School a five (5) year Charter Renewal Contract with the School Board.  The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of five (5) years commencing August 1, 2011, and ending August 1, 2016, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.

 

Hope Learning/Noah’s Ark International Charter School served students in grades K-5.

 

The Superintendent’s recommendation to terminate Hope Learning/Noah’s Ark International Charter School’s Charter Contract is warranted for good cause shown for the reasons stated below and as set forth in Fla. Stat. 1002.33(8)(a)(4) and Section 1, D), 4 and 1, D) 3 of the Charter:  Sections 1 B) 3 and 4; Section 5 A) 1, 2, and 4; and Section 5 C) 3 in the Charter Contract. Although some of the items have been rectified, they were untimely under the Charter causing students to have no school to enroll in on the first day of school.  The Superintendent’s recommendation to terminate Hope Learning/Noah’s Ark Charter Contract is based on any of the following reasons that establish the legal grounds for the proposed 90 day termination of the Charter:

  1. The school year commenced for students on August 18, 2014 and the School did not have an appropriate facility to house students and conduct an educational program that had been approved by the District.  Partial documentation for the Charter School Opening Checklist was not submitted until August 27, 2014 and some documentation had not yet been submitted.
  1. Missing items required by the Charter, (which were due July 31, 2014):

The School was required to provide the following at least 15 days before the opening of school:

 

          **Facility Certification:  Certificate of Occupancy (for new schools and/or schools in new locations) or a letter from the local government stating, “The operation of the Charter School and its facility are in compliance with all applicable regulations including Building, Fire, and Zoning Codes” is on file.

 

Significantly, there had been no proof of a lease, no required inspections and no certificates of occupancy submitted for the permanent facilities.  The School District attempted to notify the School on several occasions relating to the need for the CO and fire protection inspections for its facility and the need to comply with the Opening of School Checklist.  Hope Learning/Noah’s Ark did not have and/or had not provided the requisite critical documentation to the District:

 

a.     Fire Inspection

b.     Health Inspection, and

c.     Certificate of Occupancy

  1. Through a meeting conducted with Ms. Ericka Hadden on August 25, 2014, the District notified the School of various contractual violations and provided the School an opportunity to provide the requisite documents for the students to attend school on campus.  This had not occurred timely
  1. The School had not opened for students this School year and did not submit any plan as to when it would open and how the required instructional hours would be met.  Accordingly, the District had approved no delayed opening and this constitutes another violation of the Charter.

Alternatively, Hope Learning/Noah’s Ark International Charter School’s Governing Board may vote, if it has not already done so, in public to voluntarily terminate the Charter Contract in accordance with Section I G) School Election to terminate or non-renew of the Charter Contract prior to or after the aforementioned School Board meeting.  To voluntarily terminate the Charter, Hope Learning/Noah’s Ark has been requested to submit a written notice of voluntary termination and a copy of the pertinent Governing Board minutes to the Charter School Department before February 4, 2015, or before the effective date of termination. 

 

By letter dated January 28, 2015, attached hereto, Hope Learning/Noah’s Ark was notified of this Board Meeting.

 

Attached are copies of the Charter Contract, Data Panel, Board Notification, and the Matrix for February 4, 2015, Agenda Items.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the attached list of out-of-field/out-of-compliance teachers for Survey 3 of the FY15 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.45, F.S., teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach a subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction. Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The District provides support for out-of-field/out-of-compliance teachers through in-service opportunities to develop instructional competencies required for the assigned duty.  Partnerships have been established with colleges and universities for required coursework and exam preparation.  Teachers teaching in subject areas not covered by their current certification are required to obtain an appropriate in-field certification within a specified time frame.

I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County approve the School Improvement Plan for Leadership Academy West and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related correspondence and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 4, 2014, the School Board granted Leadership Academy West a one (1) year Charter Renewal Contract with the School Board of Palm Beach County.  The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of one (1) year commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2015, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.

 

Leadership Academy West received a grade of “F” for the fiscal year 2014.  F.S. 1002.33, Section 9(n)(1) states:  “The director and a representative of the governing board of a charter school that has earned a grade of “D” or “F” pursuant to s. 1008.34(2) shall appear before the sponsor to present information concerning each contract component having noted deficiencies.  The director and a representative of the governing board shall submit to the sponsor for approval a school improvement plan to raise student achievement.  Upon approval by the sponsor, the charter school shall begin implementation of the school improvement plan.  The department shall offer technical assistance for developing, submitting, and approving such plans.” (emphasis added)

 

The Sponsor’s staff provided technical assistance to Leadership Academy West in the drafting of the School Improvement Plan attached.
