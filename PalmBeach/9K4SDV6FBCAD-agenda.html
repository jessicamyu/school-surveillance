Special Meeting re: Request for Attorney General Opinion
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action




  • To promote wellness and set good examples to students, several members of District staff and health care partners would want the School Board to consider adopting a policy related to tobacco free campuses.




  • Pursuant to Florida and federal law, as well as current School Board policy 7.19, smoking is prohibited inside all School Board facilities and all School Board vehicles.




  • Florida’s Clean Indoor Air Act contains a provision within Florida Statute Section 386.209, entitled Regulation of smoking preempted to state, which reads: “This part expressly preempts regulation of smoking to the state and supersedes any municipal or county ordinance on the subject.”




  • In 2005 the Florida Attorney General issued an opinion which stated that the “plain language of the preemptory provision makes it clear that the Legislature has directed that the state, not local governments, regulates smoking wherever it may occur.”




  • The opinion further stated that a city’s attempt to regulate smoking in a public park was preempted to the state and of no effect.




  • Florida Statute Section 1001.32 (2) provides home rule powers to a school board for school purposes.  The provision reads: “In accordance with the provisions of s. 4(b) of Art. IX of the State Constitution, district school boards shall operate, control, and supervise all free public schools in their respective districts and may exercise any power except as expressly prohibited by the State Constitution or general law.”




  • The Interim Chief Counsel recommends that the School Board seek a determination by the Attorney General as to whether the preemption provision applies to a school board.


  • The Attorney General requires that questions for an Attorney General opinion relating “to the powers and duties of a public board…should be requested by a majority of the members of that body.”

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board authorize its Interim Chief Counsel to request a formal Attorney General’s opinion on the question of whether the Board may adopt a policy related to public schools within the School District being tobacco free campuses and authorize the attorney to sign the related documents.

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Interim Chief Counsel

Special Meeting re: New Suncoast – Mary Baker Stipulated Order of Taking and Final Judgment
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings

The FY 2009-2013 Five Year Plan includes the construction of a New Suncoast High School, which opened in August, 2010.



 



Due to the construction of the New Suncoast High School, certain road improvements on Congress Avenue, 13th Street and Jake Lane are required to meet Palm Beach County's and the City of Riviera Beach's Traffic Standards.  Acquisition of the Temporary Use Parcels or Temporary Construction Easements is required to successfully complete the required road improvements.



 



 On February 3, 2010 the School Board approved Resolution No. 02032010RE2 authorizing the acquisition of Temporary Construction Easements through eminent domain proceedings.  On March 15, 2010, the School Board’s attorney filed an eminent domain petition to acquire the necessary interests.



 



Through negotiations with one of the owners, Mary Baker, a/k/a Mary Baker Baldwin, an agreement on the taking of the Temporary Construction Easement interest and amount of compensation to be paid to Ms. Baker for the Temporary Construction Easement was reached.



 



The School District's latest appraisal of the parcel is $250.00.  This amount was previously deposited into the registry of the Court.  Ms. Baker has agreed to settle for the amount of the good faith deposit that is held in the registry of the Court.



 

Settlement is recommended as being in the best financial interest of the School District.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Stipulated Order of Taking and Final Judgment between Mary Baker and the School Board of Palm Beach County in the case of the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida v.  Marsh Harbour Associates Ltd., Case No. 502010CA007232XXXXMBAO, regarding the acquisition of a Temporary Construction Easement over 269 square feet, more or less; and authorize the Chief Counsel to execute all related documents.

Contact:

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Interim Chief Counsel



 

Blair R. LittleJohn, Esq.; Senior Counsel

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is $250.00 from the Capital Project's Budget Fund.

Special Meeting re: Survivors Charter School v. SBPBC Settlement
Meeting
Nov 10, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action

The case of Survivors Charter School vs. School Board of Palm Beach County has been a source of litigation since 2006.  This case revolved around the immediate termination of the Survivors Charter School’s Charters after an audit determined that public monies were being utilized for inappropriate purposes, including the purchase of Miami Dolphins season tickets and the lease of a BMW.  Survivors challenged the Board’s immediate closure and the case proceeded through the court system up to the Supreme Court of Florida with the School Board prevailing.



 



However, a separate Palm Beach County Circuit Court case remained open with respect to fifty-thousand dollars ($50,000.00) which was placed in the trust fund of Dennis Koehler, Esq., Survivors’ previous attorney.  The Court had ordered that those funds be frozen pending the outcome of the other actions.  All other actions have been resolved with the School Board prevailing, yet potential litigation remains in the Palm Beach County case.  The parties have agreed to settle that case and split the monies in the trust account (pending court approval) with thirty thousand dollars ($30,000.00) going to Survivors and twenty thousand dollars (20,000.00) going to the School Board.  This settlement will conclude several years of litigation and bring the issues surrounding Survivors Charter Schools to a final conclusion.



 

Settlement is recommended as being in the best financial interest of the School District.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Settlement Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and Survivors Charter School, Palm Beach County Circuit Court case number 502006CA000833XXXMB_AJ and authorize the Chief Counsel, or his designee, to execute all related documents.

Contact:

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Chief Counsel to the Board



 

Corey M. Smith, Esq.; Associate Counsel

Financial Impact:

The School Board will receive twenty thousand dollars ($20,000.00) as part of the settlement.
