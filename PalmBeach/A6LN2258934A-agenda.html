9. Consent Agenda
Subject
LD1 Q.B. Settlement
Meeting
Feb 3, 2016 - 1. SPECIAL MEETING at 2 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
9. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the agreement not to oppose a Legislative Claims Bill Settlement.

DESCRIPTION:

This is an agreement not to oppose a claims bill.  The initial claim could not be settled.  The claim went to a jury trial in 2013 and the claimant was awarded $1.7 million.  The District paid its statutory cap of $100,000 and the plaintiff is seeking the remaining $1.6 million, plus costs, through the Florida legislative claims bill process. The District would agree not to oppose a claims bill in the amount of $600,000, payable, upon enactment by the Legislature, in two annual installments of $300,000 each. After fees and costs are paid, the remaining funds are to be placed in a special needs trust for the benefit of the child.

 

In addition, upon enactment of the claims bill, the School District agrees to purchase an annuity and to provide additional services to benefit the claimant, with an expected value that meets the amount of the jury verdict.

Accident:   This is a personal injury claim brought on by a student who was injured on a school bus on January 6, 2007.

 

Injuries:    The claimant's damages are alleged to be largely psychological.

 

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Operating Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

Dianne L. Howard, Director of Risk & Benefits Management (dianne.howard@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The financial impact to the District for this settlement is approximately $1,020,000.  The source of funds is the Workers' Compensation and Liability Claims Internal Service Fund.
