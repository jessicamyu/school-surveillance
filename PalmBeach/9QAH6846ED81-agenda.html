Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Proposed Amended Renewal Charter Contract with Learning Excellence Foundation of South Palm Beach, Inc., d/b/a Imagine Schools – Chancellor Campus, f/k/a Chancellor Charter School of Lantana; and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter. 

 

The Amended Renewal Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel's office with input from staff.

 

Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus was approved by the Sponsor to provide educational services in accordance with the terms of a charter school agreement; and was originally authorized to begin operations in 2001, and is currently operating pursuant to a Renewal Charter School Agreement, dated July 1, 2009, and approved by the School Board on August 26, 2009, for a term of ten (10) years. 

 

The School and Sponsor have agreed to propose to the School Board to amend the existing Renewal Charter School Agreement to expand its term to fifteen (15) years from its inception in 2009, to facilitate access to long-term financial resources for construction purposes.

 

The Amended Charter, in addition to expanding its current term, updates the current Charter to comply with the State model template and recent changes in the laws, includes references to the DOJ Resolution Agreement and Inspector General provisions, and includes language from the District’s current standard charter as well as some changes requested by the school.

 

Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus serves students in grades K-8. 

 

Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus’ mission is, “To provide each child with an opportunity to realize his or her full potential in a setting that is safe, orderly and small enough so that students feel recognized and cared for.”

 

Attached are copies of the fully Amended Renewal Charter Contract, Revised Budget, Data Panels, Legal Checklist, Board Notification, and Matrix for November 5th Agenda Items.

FMPA1 Alternative Communication Services LLC Professional Services Agreement
Meeting
Nov 5, 2014 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
9. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the professional services agreement with Alternative Communication Services LLC in the amount of $23,062.50 for the period November 6, 2014 through June 30, 2015; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The consultant shall perform the following services:

 

Educational Communication Access Realtime Translation (CART) and/or Text Interpreting (C-Print/Type Well) services for select students who are Deaf or Hard of Hearing. CART is a verbatim transcription of speech. Text Interpreting is meaning for meaning transcription. The services may be provided on-site or remotely. The District will provide on-site hardware (laptop or iPad) and microphone in order to access this service.

 

These services are not provided under our current contract with Signs of Excellence, LLC or Nationwide Interpreter Resource, Inc.

 

This purchase is considered a Professional Service as defined in Section 1010.04(4)(a)FS and Board Policy 6.14 and therefore is exempt from competition requirements based on the specialized training required to perform these services.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board authorize a resolution to refund all or portions of the COP Series 2006A, 2007A and 2007E in one or more series of certificates of participation.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The School District, in conjunction with the Finance Committee, actively manages the debt of the District, always seeking to reduce capital costs and manage risk.
  • Due to historically low interest rates in the current market, three outstanding series of certificates of participation (COPs) are at or near a point when they can be refunded, or refinanced, and obtain savings for the District which will reduce future debt service payments.
  • The certificates under consideration are series 2006A, 2007A and 2007E.
  • Based on the recommendation of the Finance Committee, the District would refund the certificates when the net present value savings is at least 5%, which incorporates the costs of issuance.
  • It is anticipated that the refundings will result in one or more new series of COPs.  
  • Based on current market conditions at the time of selling the Certificates, the District will combine the refunded series into as few transactions as possible which will materially reduce the Costs of Issuance.
  • A summary of the maximum costs of issuance is attached.
  • On May 30, 2014, the Finance Committee unanimously recommended the District move forward with the transactions as soon as possible.
