DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement will provide summer camp for children of Delray Full Service Center (DFSC) students and assist in retaining our students during the summer. Traditionally there has been drop in attendance when K-12 school year ends. Parents of K-12 students cannot afford daycare or pay summer camp and therefore, must stay home to care for their children.

 

Providing summer camp for children of DFSC students will increase the outreach to Delray Beach community and support the mission of both Delray Full Service Center and the School District of Palm Beach County. The academic focus of this camp will reduce or eliminate "summer slide" for the children of DFSC students. 

 

Enrollment in the camp wil have a positive academic impact on our families' children and elementary and middle schools in Delray Beach.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with John I Leonard High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

Purpose and Overview of the Project The purpose of this MOU is to document the terms under which Education Authority will provide access to information, which may include personally-identifiable student information (the “Covered Data”), for use in connection with a research project defined in Researcher’s agreement with the U.S. Department of Education, Institute of Education Sciences, National Center for Education Evaluation and Regional Assistance (Contract # 91990018C0044) titled An Impact Evaluation to Inform the Teacher and School Leader (“TSL”).
 

Through its contract with the Researcher, the U.S. Department of Education’s Institute of Education Sciences (“IES”) will (1) collect implementation information about the TSL Incentive Fund program FY2017 grants and (2) evaluate the impact of the coaching model that is common among the funded TSL FY2017 grants (collectively, the “Evaluation”). More specifically, the Evaluation will use a random assignment design to examine the impact of coaching provided by non-TSL math or ELA teacher leaders within grades 3-6 at districts that are similar to TSL districts. The Evaluation will focus on the impact of the teacher leader role on student and teacher outcomes and the extent to which funding teacher leader roles is a more or less effective use of resources than other strategies for improving student achievement. The Evaluation will target a sample size of 100 non-TSL elementary and middle schools across 10 districts as well as include information from fourteen TSL FY2017 grantees. Researcher and its subcontractors and consultants who are part of the Evaluation team (the “Study Team”) will conduct the Evaluation.  
 

The Researcher will conduct the Evaluation on behalf of the Education Authority to, among other things, to (A) develop, validate, or administer predictive tests; (B) administer student aid programs; or (C) improve instruction. Even though Researcher is conducting the Evaluation for or on behalf of the Education Authority, nothing in this MOU shall be construed to imply any official endorsement or warranting of the work or services of Researcher by the Education Authority.

The purchase of the installation of Fiber and Fiber Related Products under this bid is contingent upon approval of the Federal Communications Commission E-Rate program. No District funds is spent until E-rate approval is received.
 
The Telecommunications Act of 1996 established a fund by which Schools and Libraries across the Country could access discounts on eligible telecommunications products and services. The program is commonly known as the E-rate Program. The eligibility for discounts on internet access, telecommunications products and services, internal connection products, services and maintenance is determined by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC). Funding is made available upon application approval by the Schools and Libraries Division (SLD) of the Universal Service Administrative Company (USAC), which was established by the Act. The discount amount is based upon the numbers of students receiving free and reduced price meals. Our District qualifies for a discount of 80%.
 
As part of the E-Rate program, the District is responsible for any portion not covered by E-rate. Some schools have already exhausted some or all of their E-Rate funding and therefore will receive partial or no E-Rate funding from this bid, so the School District of Palm Beach County will be responsible for the balance at these locations.
 
As the District moves further into the five-year project, we are seeing more sites that have exhausted most or all of their eligible funding. This coupled with additional fiber requirements due to renovations and addition of portables, the District is responsible for a larger portion of the project up to and including the balance at some of these locations.
 
The additional funds will be used to provide for the fiber installation services at the remaining locations for this upgrade project.
