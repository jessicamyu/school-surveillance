Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Interlocal Agreement with the Loxahatchee River Environmental Control District to supply Beacon Cove Elementary and Independence Middle with reclaimed water for irrigation purposes, and authorize the Board Chair and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • In 2001, the Board approved two "Standard Irrigation Quality Water Agreements - Retail"; one agreement was for Beacon Cove ES (signed on May 24, 2001) and the other was for Independence MS (signed on August 16, 2001).
     
  • The agreements are between the Loxahatchee River Environmental Control District (LRD) and the School Board of Palm Beach County (SBPBC).  Under these agreements, LRD agrees to provide non-potable, irrigation-quality water (AKA reclaimed or recycled water) to each school.
     
  • The term of each of the original agreements is 20 years; the current agreements are set to expire on May 21, 2021 and August 16, 2021, respectively.
     
  • The current item extends the agreement between LRD and SBPBC another 20 years.  The agreements for both schools have been merged into a single document.
     
  • The water provided is not metered, but rather, it is supplied based on a predetermined allocation amount (17,000 gallons per day at Beacon Cove and 39,500 gallons per day at Independence) and a flat per-gallon cost.
     
  • The use of reclaimed water for irrigation purposes is beneficial for SBPBC, as it is a more cost-effective option than potable water. In addition, it is a more environmentally-preferable option.

AD1 Authorization to Request a Formal Attorney General Opinion regarding Board and District Advisory Committee Meetings
Meeting
Nov 17, 2020 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
17. New Business
Type
Action
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board authorize it's General Counsel to request a formal Attorney General’s opinion on the question of whether the School Board may adopt, under its home rule authority for a school purpose, policies allowing Sunshine meetings for Board and District Advisory Committees and other Sunshine meetings where a quorum is required to be physically present, to be conducted by means of communications media technology during a public health emergency, including a pandemic, and authorize the Board Chair, Superintendent, and General Counsel to sign the related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • Several Sunshine meetings occur within the School District including Board Advisory Committees, certain District Committees, such as the ABC, and School Advisory Councils.
  • Per Florida Attorney General Opinions, if a quorum is required to conduct official business, a quorum of the members must be physically present at the meeting absent a statute or if this requirement was lawfully suspended.
  • During the pandemic, these meetings were allowed to be conducted virtually without members physically present because of Executive Orders issued by the Governor, but allowing these virtual meetings through Executive Orders expired on October 31, 2020.
  • Some Board Advisory Committees have expressed a desire to continue to meet virtually during the pandemic as the Governor’s Order allowing virtual meetings expired. The Chair of CORC emailed the Governor requesting an extension for advisory committees.
  • The public health emergency continues as the emergency declaration in the Governor’s Executive Order issued on November 3, 2020 continues for 60 days and the State Surgeon General’s Order on October 23 continues for 60 days.
  • Florida Statute Section 1001.32 (2) provides home rule powers to a school board for school purposes. The provision reads: “In accordance with the provisions of s. 4(b) of Art. IX of the State Constitution, district school boards shall operate, control, and supervise all free public schools in their respective districts and may exercise any power except as expressly prohibited by the State Constitution or general law.”
  • Although Attorney General Opinions exist, it is the opinion of the General Counsel that a good faith argument can be made that there is no provision in the State Constitution or general law that requires that a quorum be physically present for these Committee Sunshine meetings, As a result, through home rule, the School Board should be able to lawfully suspend the requirement that a quorum be physically present for these meetings during the state of emergency.
  • The General Counsel recommends that the School Board seek a determination by the Attorney General as to whether the Board’s home rule authority would allow the Board to adopt policies allowing Sunshine meetings for Board and District advisory Committees and other Sunshine meetings, where a quorum is required to be physically present, to be conducted by means of communications media technology during a public health emergency including a pandemic.
  • The Attorney General requires that questions for an Attorney General opinion relating “to the powers and duties of officials who sit on a public board…should be requested by a majority of the members of that body.” Adopting policies relates to powers and duties of the School Board. Therefore, the School Board would need to vote to approve requesting this Attorney General Opinion.
