Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the purchase of the Masters Builders Risk Insurance Policy with Lexington Insurance through the broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., effective March 31, 2015 through August 31, 2015, at a premium cost of up to .7655 per $100 of construction costs.

DESCRIPTION:

The policy will cover damage to the property being built, as well as theft of materials from the site during the construction phase for new and replacement schools/buildings.  This policy covers all facilities started during the policy period and those currently still under construction. While our major construction will be completed by August, we still have sufficient values remaining to be constructed to warrant the purchase of this insurance for Glade View Elementary and Rosenwald Elementary.

 

The Master Builders Risk Policy eliminates the need to go directly to the contractor for a project-specific, stand-alone builders risk policy.  Over the last several years the program saved 70 percent of contractor-purchased builders risk coverage.  The District purchases the insurance and premiums are charged back to the individual construction projects.

 

The policy will have annual aggregate limits of $25 million subject to a sub-limit of $10 million for any one other than wind loss occurrence and $10 million aggregate in one policy year for Named Windstorm.  Name Windstorm also has a five (5) percent deductible (Self-Insured Retention) subject to a $250,000 minimum.  However, because the District owns the policy rather than the contractor, the District will be eligible for FEMA reimbursements, for which a contractor would not be eligible.

 

The deposit premium is based on anticipated construction values of approximately $21,078,644 in major construction to be completed in the policy year.  Therefore, the premium will be approximately $135,817  (inclusive of assessments and fees), of which we pay 50 percent now, or $67,908.32, subject to annual audit adjustments.

 

The broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., will receive a commission of six (6) percent on this policy.  In addition to placing the insurance, they will be responsible for tracking all payments, any changes relevant to this coverage and all reconciliations at year-end.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the retention of Wyland & Tadros, LLP for the handling of the claim against Midwest Employers Casualty Company for the worker’s compensation excess claim, and include the approval to file suit against Midwest if the case is not resolved, and authorize the execution of all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Risk Management has advised of the following:

  • Midwest Employers Casualty Company provided Excess Workers Compensation Coverage under policy number 4518-SO-FL, for the period of 8/15/1994 through 8/15/1995.  Midwest has refused to reimburse the District on one (1) excess claim, that of Ambrosio Arango, (LI# 127435). To date, the School District’s excess claim totals over $52,000.  As medical bills and lost wages continue to be paid on this claim, this number will continue to increase.
  • Multiple written requests have been made, along with submission of required information in accordance with the policy requirements by the District’s Third Party Administrator.
  • Board permission is sought to retain the law firm of Wyland & Tadros, LLP, a District-approved vendor, on a contingency fee basis, to prepare a breach of contract law suit in the event that Midwest continues to avoid its obligations under the Policy.
  • In matters of this nature, it is customary to agree to a 20 percent to 30 percent contingency fee based on what is recovered from Midwest.  David Tadros, Esq., has agreed to a 20 oercebt contingency fee, plus costs, to handle this case on behalf of the School Board.  A contingency fee provides that the attorney will not be paid unless the client (the School Board) obtains a recovery from the other party to the case (Midwest).  Because the benefits received include future claims, the attorney has agreed to limit his recovery to the monies collected at the conclusion of the case and not against any future benefits received.  Should suit be filed and a judgment entered by the Court, the judgment may include a separate award for attorney’s fees and costs; in that case, the 20 percent contingency fee may not apply, but the amount awarded by the court, if larger than 20 percent, would apply.  That amount, however, would only be recovered from the monies collected by the School Board from Midwest.  Per the insurance policy at issue and Florida law, venue will be in Palm Beach County.
  • The work associated with pursuing this claim will be quite extensive.  In addition, it would be beneficial to utilize an attorney who is knowledgeable and has expertise in this unique area of law.  Risk Management has requested retention of this attorney and law firm.

I recommend the School Board approve the Strong Minds Network Master Agreement with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents and contracts.

DESCRIPTION:

The Strong Minds Network, formerly the Quality Counts System, is a quality rating improvement system, funded by the Children’s Services Council and implemented by community partners, that promotes high quality in early care and education programs in Palm Beach County.  The goal of Strong Minds is to ensure that all children arrive at kindergarten ready to learn.  Strong Minds focuses on:

  • Improving children’s learning and development
  • Improving teacher-child interactions
  • Increasing family engagement in children’s learning and development

Strong Minds is designed to encourage higher quality programs in their continuous quality improvement efforts by making a variety of supports available to them, including:

  • Reliable and valid program assessments, including the CLASS (Classroom Assessment Scoring System) and the Environment Rating Scale (ERS) assessment tools, administered by highly trained qualified assessors
  • Child assessments, including the Teaching Strategies GOLD system, a research-based online child observation and documentation system that shows what children know and can do and what adjustments can be made to instruction
  • Navigation services to help early education and care participants access services
  • Targeted technical assistance provided onsite by specialists (District’s Early Childhood Education Department provides this service as a system partner through a Board approved contract)
  • Tiered reimbursement supplements to help fund quality programming
  • Career advising, SEEK scholarships, formal and informal training, registry membership and salary supplements (based on salary level) for early education practitioners
  • Screening and assessment that links parents with their children’s development, individualized instruction based on child assessments and parent input, and increased parent access to quality early education and care through CSC child scholarships

Participation in the Strong Minds Network requires the attainment of select quality benchmarks and the execution of a Master Agreement, which includes an electronic agreement for each participating school site. The ECE Department will submit and manage applications for District school sites offering the Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) program, with an expected assessment completion date of May 2015.
