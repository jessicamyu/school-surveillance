Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Affiliation Agreement with CVS Pharmacy, Inc., effective February 19, 2015 through June 30, 2019; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Board Agenda item identifies an Affiliation Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and  CVS Pharmacy Inc.

 

This Affiliation Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education Program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education curriculum frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.

 

Full-time Health Science Education Programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic High, Boca Raton High, and Glades Central High, John I. Leonard High, Inlet Grove High, Jupiter High, Lake Worth High, Leadership Academy West, Pahokee High, Palm Beach Gardens High, Palm Beach Lakes High, Park Vista High, Royal Palm Beach High, Somerset Academy, South Tech Academy, and West Boca Raton High School. These licenses cover various parts of Palm Beach County and several include areas outside of Palm Beach County. Currently the District is utilizing only three of these channels. The School District can hold on to the unused channels or lease the channels as is customary by school districts. 

 

On July 17, 2013, the Board contracted with Select Spectrum LLC to market the Board’s EBS Licenses to a telecommunications entity under an EBS Long-Term De Facto Transfer Lease Agreement (Lease).

 

Select Spectrum released a public solicitation in the spring of 2014 and Sprint Spectrum LP (Sprint) offered the best terms.

 

Below are the essential financial/business elements of the negotiated lease:

 

•              The Lease will included all 13 channels, but will continue to have the right to use the 3 video channels currently being used by TEN;

•              30 year lease term, with a Sprint-option to cancel after 21 years;

•              $4,500,000 upfront payment;

•              Monthly lease fees in the amount of $115,000 ($1,380,000 per year);

•             A $625,000 payment after Sprint gives notice that it is ready to begin using the channels currently being used to broadcast TEN’s video content to schools;

•             $40,000 in reimbursement of the School Board’s outside counsel legal fees associated with negotiating the Lease; and

•             Monthly service credits in the amount of $5,000 to be used to purchase Sprint’s products and services.

 

The total cash paid over the life of the lease will total $46,565,000 and the value of the service credits will be an additional $1,800,000.  The net present value of the entire deal, discounted at the industry standard of 10% per year, is approximately $19.3 million.

 

The upfront payment and monthly royalty payments, along with the $625,000 payment (once it is received) will be maintained in a unique budget program and used to defray current operating costs associated with The Education Network and make necessary improvements to the TV station's infrastructure. This new revenue stream will free up general fund operating dollars for re-appropriation through the School Board's annual budget development and approval process. Consideration will be given to using a portion of these funds to enhance the district’s communication, marketing and engagement efforts. A portion of the revenue received in the first three years of the Lease will also be used to pay the agreed-upon fee to Select Spectrum in the total approximate amount of $260,000 and maintenance costs of the Video Over IP system that replaces the current microwave delivery system of signals to schools.

 

The School District will work with Sprint to participate in their ConnectED initiative to bring free high-speed wireless broadband service to students and families in Palm Beach County who meet the qualifications for the program.

If the pilot program is successful, District wide implementation with CheckCare for 100% reimbursement, up to $1,500 per check, will begin for the 2015-16 School Year.

 

In FY14, schools handled 2,320 returned checks totaling approximately $255,000 and school staff, teachers, bookkeepers and cafeteria managers have been responsible for pursuing collection of returned checks.  Some schools have stopped accepting checks to avoid the collection process which is counterproductive to the goal of staff and the Audit Committee to reduce the amount of cash handled at the schools.  At the close of the 2013-14 school year, approximately $75,000 remained uncollected by the schools for student activities. 

 

Upon implementation, Bank of America will deliver all returned checks to CheckCare, and CheckCare will follow procedures as outlined in the attached Collection Procedures. This service will allow the District to recoup the losses previously encountered from a returned checks and allows Parents/Guardians the opportunities to discreetly pay for any inadvertent returned item.

 

Schools will receive automatic, guaranteed reimbursement for all eligible returned checks on a bi-monthly basis. This service is currently being utilized successfully at the Hillsborough, Orange and Miami-Dade School Districts.

 

This purchase does not require competition since the amount of the contract is less than the amount required by Board Policy 6.14, Rule 6A-1.012 Florida Administrative Code and section 1010.04 Florida Statutes.
