3. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Subject
BCS1 Educational Plant Survey
Meeting
Jun 6, 2018 - 7. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
3. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

  1. A formal and complete Educational Plant Survey (Survey) is required by the Florida Department of Education (DOE) to be conducted every five (5) years. The last Survey was approved on May 12, 2015 (Agenda Item PIR-7). 
     
  2. An out-of-cycle Survey was prepared and submitted for review to DOE to reflect the FY 2018-2027 Capital Plan that the Board approved on September 6, 2017.  DOE has reviewed and commented on the draft Survey and has released staff to obtain Board approval.
     
  3. Authorization for the Survey is specified in Article IX and Article XII of the Florida Constitution; Chapters 1001, 1011, and 1013, Florida Statutes (F.S.); State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF); and the Florida Building Code.
     
  4. The purpose of the Survey is to assist District staff with recommendations for housing the educational activities of the students and staff of the School District in the future. It is a needs assessment which compares what exists at a facility, as listed on the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH), to what an educational or ancillary plant is allowed to have based on the latest Facilities Lists (DOE Facilities Lists for generic elementary, K-8, middle and high schools are attached).
     
  5. The Survey is a planning tool to assist in identifying future projects in the Capital Plan based on District needs.
     
  6. DOE Table II (attached) is the summary of the individual evaluations of each facility with respect to student capacity, projected student capacity and a recommendation of the final student capacity at each site within the next five years.
     
  7. DOE Table III (attached) is the financial data of all the facility recommendations within the first five years of the Survey based on State formulas and requirements.
     
  8. The Ten Year Capacity, Infrastructure and Maintenance tables and the Twenty Year Capacity, Infrastructure and Maintenance tables are also included as part of the long range planning of the Survey and are based on the draft District Capital Facilities Plan.
     
  9. The Survey can be amended at any time based on need.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the five-year initial Charter Contract with Olympus International Academy, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Olympus International Academy in March 2017.  The application for Olympus International Academy was approved on June 7, 2017, as Item CS10 during a Special Board Meeting. 

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing August 1, 2018, and ending July 31, 2023, except as provided in the charter. 

 

The Olympus International Academy curriculum will be performance-based in correlation with the Florida standards and will use innovative learning programs such as STEAM (science, technology, engineering, arts, and math), and focus on global competences, world languages and cultures in all grade levels for the entire school year.  The global classroom fosters enthusiasm for learning creativity, collaboration, new and effective means of communication through diverse channels, inclusive social environments, and meaningful connections with the world.

 

The students will connect with world cultures through language studies.  The looping structure (K-2, 3-5) has a team of specialist teachers, one who focuses on Math, another on English language arts, and the third with a science specialty in order to develop tinker labs and makerspaces for all grade levels starting with kindergarten to strengthen our STEAM program.

 

Olympus International Academy will focus on geography, 21st Century Skills, and STEAM beginning in kindergarten.  It will utilize looping, specialist grade level clusters, work stations, differentiation, and interdisciplinary teaching.
