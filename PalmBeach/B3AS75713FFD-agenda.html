On July 1, 2013, South Tech Preparatory Academy (STP) was granted a five (5) year Charter Contract to serve students in grades 6-8. The contract expired June 30, 2018. On June 6, 2018, a First Amendment to the charter was granted to extend the charter three (3) months to September 30, 2018.

Therefore, the parties agree that the term for the existence of the current Charter Agreement (as amended) is hereby extended up through and including December 31, 2018.

 

All other terms of the Charter Agreement shall remain in full force and effect, subject to further extensions of time, as mutually deemed necessary by the parties.  This Second Amendment, together with the Charter, constitutes and represents the entire agreement between the parties hereto and supersedes any prior understandings or agreements, written or verbal, respecting the subject matter herein.

 

The mission of the school is “to graduate students prepared to matriculate into a career academy program of study, which will ready them for work, higher education, and productive citizenship. As a feeder program of South Tech Academy High School, STP will provide a pre-career academy program that includes a strong academic curriculum and the career exploration needed to matriculate into professional pathways. The goals of STP are to point students toward a successful livelihood, instill a desire to pursue continuing education, and encourage the tenets of productive citizenship.”

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County has applied for four (4) competitive grants from the Florida Department of Education for the 21st Century Community Learning Centers (21st CCLC) Program. The maximum annual award is $700,000 per grant, or up to $3,500,000 per grant over a five-year period. If all four grants are awarded, the total award to the District will be up to $14,000,000 for the period from September 1, 2018 through August 31, 2023.

 

The 21st CCLC grants propose to provide afterschool and summer programs for the following elementary schools: 1) 21st CCLC Shimmering Starz – Seminole Trails Elem, Rolling Green Elem, Pine Grove Elem; 2) 21st CCLC Wonder Kidz – Dr. Mary M. Bethune Elem, West Riviera Elem, Lake Park Elem; 3) 21st CCLC Clever Kidz – Pleasant City Elem, Roosevelt Elem, Northmore Elem; and 4) 21st CCLC Rising Starz – Highland Elem, Indian Pines Elem, C.O. Taylor/Kirklane Elem. These schools were selected based upon grant requirements related to ELA achievement scores and percentage on free or reduced lunch.

 

The grants propose to serve 60 students per school, 180 per grant, or a total of 720 in kindergarten through 5th grade across all four grants. In each school, students will be selected to participate based on one or more of the following needs criteria:

  • below grade level on recent school and/or state assessments;
  • students who have been retained or in jeopardy of being retained;
  • ELL and/or ESE students; and/or
  • vulnerable students: homeless or foster children.

The grant goals are to: 1) improve academic performance; 2) increase social competence; and 3) increase parental involvement and family literacy. Services will be provided free to participating students and their families.

I recommend the Board receive and file the Special Review of Community School Fee Collections At Palm Beach Lakes High School (Report# 2018-06).

DESCRIPTION: 
The primary objective of this special review was to determine if the school's Community School Program fees were properly accounted for during Fiscal Year 2017.  The review produced the following major conclusions:

 

(1) $2,486 in Collections Unaccounted For. Money missing included $1,326 in cash and $1,160 in 16 checks.

 

(2) $4,197 ActiveNet Payments Missing, But Recouped by the School.  As confirmed by the District's outside vendor, ActiveNet, that collects Adult Ed program fees, six checks totaling $5,324.55 were mailed to the school during Fiscal Year 2017.  However, only two checks totaling $1,127.49 were deposited into the school's Internal Funds; and $4,197.06 ($5,324.55 - $1,127.49) in four checks were not cashed by the school. 

 

In response to OIG's request, ActiveNet sent the school a $4,197.06 replacement check. After OIG's coordination with the Accounting Department, all future payments by ActiveNet will be deposited directly into the School District's account effective January 2018.

 

(3) Attendance Rosters Not Maintained. The Community School did not maintain student attendance rosters. Without complete records, there is no assurance that all program fees were properly collected and accounted for.

 

At the July 19, 2018 Audit committee Meeting, the Committee reviewed and recommended approval of Audit Report # 2018-06 Special Review of Community School Fee Collections At Palm Beach Lakes High School.
