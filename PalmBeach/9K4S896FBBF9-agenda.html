Suncoast High School Foundation and PTSA
Meeting
Aug 25, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action (Consent)

The Suncoast Foundation was formed in 2001 to provide financial support for students and faculty. During the 2009-2010 school year, the Foundation raised over $100,000 in donations, and made grants to the school in excess of $37,000. The bulk of that money was spent on advanced teacher training for IB and AP courses, and funds were also donated to the Chemistry, Photography and Math departments for equipment needs. An additional grant to the Spanish department for upgraded textbooks is pending.



When the Foundation was informed that state funding for the summer program at Florida Atlantic University for students in Suncoast's Math, Science & Engineering (MSE) Program was in jeopardy, one board member, Meredith Trim, spearheaded a successful drive to raise private donations from MSE parents to support this program. When the state funding came through at the 11th hour, most of those donors agreed to allow the funds to be used for other needs at the school. With some of the donated funds, the Foundation is purchasing concrete tables and benches for the courtyard of the new campus, to accommodate overflow from the cafeteria.



The Foundation is actively selling naming rights to components of the new school facility, in an effort to raise funds to create an endowment for future needs.



The Suncoast High School PTSA has also made generous and major contributions to the school.  Last year they raised $67,000 with the bulk of the money coming from the PTSA concession stand ($30,000), annual fund-raising event ($12,000) and Adopt-A-Class ($17,000).  The funds were used in part for Spanish IB books, Debate Club, Robotic Club, Project Graduation, scholarships, Appreciation Breakfasts and Luncheons for teacher, year-end banquets and padding for the new gym floor.

G1 - Grant Application for Continuation of the Florida Inclusion Network Project Grant
Meeting
Aug 25, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

We have applied and received the Florida Inclusion Network (FIN) Grant for 10 years.  The FIN Grant Funds are used to:





  • Train teachers in research-based, effective instructional practices, which will benefit students, parents, and school staff;

  • Provide training as designated by the Florida Department of Education/Bureau of Exceptional Education and Student Services to assist the school district in the transition from traditional service delivery models to expanded models of student support;

  • Maintain a working knowledge of the ongoing status of inclusion within the school district; and

  • Increase the inclusion of students with disabilities in the state and district assessments in collaboration with other district programs and projects.


The project staff will assess the outcomes of the aforementioned goals by:



(1) Gathering data on learning opportunities provided via multiple delivery methods (e.g., face-to-face; web-based, etc.) to families, teachers, administrators, and district personnel to facilitate inclusion of students with disabilities.
(2) Gathering data as measured by time with non-disabled peer by looking at the overall inclusion rate at the district.
(3) Gathering data on Reading and Math proficiency gains of students with disabilities as measured by FCAT scores.



The grant application will be submitted to the Florida Department of Education.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the continuation of the Florida Inclusion Network Project Grant in the amount of  $212,000, effective September 1, 2010 through August 31, 2011, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

FM2 - Tax Anticipation Notes, Series 2010
Meeting
Aug 25, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)


  • The Tax Anticipation Note, or TAN, is a short term financing instrument used to alleviate cash shortfalls in the general fund each year.  Current estimates show that additional cash will be required for the Operating Fund in November 2010.

  • The Ad Valorem millage levy provides the majority of the revenues for the School District, especially in the general fund.  As the first receipt of property taxes is expected in late November, it is necessary to borrow money for cash flow purposes until the tax revenues are received.

  • Based on the District's cash needs, the District plans to issue $115 million in Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs).  

  • The TAN issuance in 2009 was for $85 million.  The amount has been increased due to a change in the way the State of Florida is funding education.  In prior years, the State provided all FEFP revenues during the first quarter of the fiscal year.  Beginning in FY11, the State will disburse the funds over 12 months which means we will have even less cash during the first quarter than in prior years.

  • The sale is planned for September 9, 2010 from 9am to 11am in the Board Room.  Students from the Finance Academies will be invited to participate.

  • The transaction is planned to close on September 23rd and will mature no later than February 1, 2011.

  • The Finance Committee reviewed and approved the transaction at their meeting on August 11, 2010.  A draft of the minutes as well as the audio recording is available on the Finance Committee website: http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/FinanceCommittee.asp.

  • The attachments to the authorizing resolution are forthcoming from bond counsel and will be available in the Board Minutes Office.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Resolution authorizing the issuance of  Tax Anticipation Notes, Series 2010 and other necessary documents; authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign necessary documents; and authorize necessary budget amendments.

AC8 - Standard Agreement to Provide Services to Parentally Placed Private School Students
Meeting
Aug 25, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA) of 2004 requires that the School District provide services to parentally placed nonprofit private school students with disabilities.  Specifically, IDEA requires that the District:





  • divide the total IDEA allocation for the fiscal year by the total number of eligible students attending public and private schools located in Palm Beach County, including McKay Scholarships students.  This amount is referenced as the proportionate share.  This amount is to be "set aside" to provide services to parentally placed students in nonprofit private schools.

  • spend the proportionate share of IDEA funds for provision of services to parentally placed students with disabilities in nonprofit private schools Prek-12. 

  • participate in  meaningful consultation between representatives of all nonprofit private schools located in Palm Beach County, private school parents, and the school District.  Meaningful consultation was held on January 28, 2010. 

  • provide services as outlined in the attached Cooperative Agreement pursuant to annual meaningful consultation. Services provided to students based on the Services Plans written by the District.  Two hundred and ninety-seven (297) students accessed services during the 2009-2010 school year.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Standard Cooperative Agreement format and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all cooperative agreements with nonprofit private schools that conform to the Standard Cooperative Agreement.  These agreements shall be in effect from August 18, 2010 through June 30, 2013.  The agreements are renewed yearly for the three year period, if both parties agree.

G2 - Acceptance of Teaching American History Grant (MARCH through Time)
Meeting
Aug 25, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category

The Teaching American History grant award will fund professional development projects to raise student achievement by improving teachers’ knowledge, understanding, and appreciation of traditional American History. This competitive grant award will provide a district-wide professional development model for each of three years for three groups of thirty Palm Beach County elementary and middle school teachers of American History, or a total of ninety teachers over a three year period. The Teaching American History grant will fund one (1.0) FTE as project manager for the three years of the grant.



To implement this federal grant award, activities will be carried out in partnership with the following non-profit organizations: Gilder Lehrman Institute of American History, eLearning Systems International,  Florida Atlantic University, the Organization of American Historians, the Historical Society of Palm Beach County, the Norton Museum, the Spady Cultural Heritage Museum, and the College Board. 



For each of the three funded grant years, American History teacher professional development will focus on central themes in American History through a series of seminars, workshops, field experiences, and week-long historical summer immersion institutes in St. Augustine, Colonial Williamsburg, and Boston for the  teachers and project managers.



The Teaching American History Grant project will be reviewed by DKH Consulting using the following guidelines: 1) to improve teachers’ knowledge, understanding, and appreciation of traditional American history; 2) to improve teachers’ content delivery of traditional American history in the classroom; 3) to increase students’ interest, performance, and achievement in American history; and 4) to build long-term capacity to support and sustain improved teaching and learning in American history.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board accept the Teaching American History competitive grant award, MARCH through Time, from the United States Department of Education for the period 9/1/2010 through 8/31/2013, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are implementation of the grant activities, which includes but is not limited to partnerships with Gilder Lehrman Institute of American History, eLearning Systems International, and DKH Consulting Services, Inc.
