Subject
ELR1 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Emily Morales
Meeting
May 5, 2021 - 5. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

On or about March 29, 2021, Emily Morales became the subject of an Employee and Labor Relations investigation based upon allegations of policy violations related to Gross Insubordination, Failure to Adhere to Work Hours/Schedule, and Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgement.

The allegation that Ms. Morales has been absent (without pay and without leave) on multiple occasions have been substantiated due to Ms. Morales failure to submit the required TDEs to document such absences. According to the findings (investigative report and supplemental report), Ms. Morales has been absent (without pay) on forty (40) different occasions. Hence, Ms. Morales was absent (without pay) 47% of the time in September, 46% of the time in October and 70% in November. In addition, Ms. Morales had not submitted PBSD 0032 leave forms for these absences. Ms. Morales failed to submit the required TDEs on 37 occasions, or 92.5% of the time.

Therefore, these actions violate the following: School Board Policies 3.02 (4)(a), (4)(b), (4)(h) and (4)(j), Code of Ethics; 3.80(1) and (3), Leaves; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment with the District; AESOP CBA Article 3 Employee Rights and Responsibilities, Section B Personnel Procedures, #6(C) Job Descriptions and Assignments; AESOP CBA Article 4 Leaves, Section B Sick Leave, #2(b) Leaves. There is “just cause” to support Emily Morales’ termination of employment. 

 

On April 20, 2021, the Superintendent’s “Notice of Recommendation for Termination of Employment” was sent Via Email and sent Via Regular US and Certified Mail to Emily Morales, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend the Board approve the Consultant Agreement with M FERTAKIS Consulting LLC for the period of May 6, 2021 through June 30, 2021; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This contract will allow for a continuation of services with National School Boards Association (NSBA) Equity Consultant Mary Fertakis. Work first began with Ms. Fertakis in November 2020 and primarily focused on foundational work with the School Board around equity-related topics (Root Cause Analysis, Creation of Equity Statement/Definition, and Revisions to Mission and Vision). The Board identified this foundational work was needed prior to engaging in the development of the District's next Strategic Plan. Given the sense of urgency, the Superintendent's Authority was used to approve an initial contract with Ms. Fertakis in the amount of $25,000 to complete a series of equity workshops with the Board.

 

This second contract, in the amount of $16,666.67, will allow Ms. Fertakis to begin building internal capacity around equity, which is needed in order to ensure equity is embedded into the District's next strategic plan. Ms. Fertakis will also review drafts of proposed goals and associated measures to provide guidance on inclusion of equity as well as develop a structure, process, and/or protocol for internal staff to use when examining current policies and practices for equity.
