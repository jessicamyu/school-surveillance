Personnel Disciplinary Action - Michael Rice
Meeting
Mar 12, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Disciplinary Actions
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

On or about December 6, 2007, Michael Rice became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of inappropriate language and/or behavior with or to students, failure to exercise best professional judgment, and unnecessary physical force on a student.  Furthermore, the allegations included potential violations of School Board Policy 0.01, Commitment to the Student (2)(c), and/or Policy 0.01, Commitment to the Student (2)(d), and/or Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and/or staff, and/or Florida Administrative Code, Department of Education 6B-1.001 (3), Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and/or Florida Administrative Code, Department of Education 6B-1.001 (2), Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and/or 6B-1.006 (3) (a) Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida, and/or 6B-1.006 (3) (e) Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida.



On or about October 18, 2007, an incident occurred at Coral Sunset Elementary School involving Michael Rice and a first grade student.  Seven witnesses observed Mr. Rice grab the student by the backpack, and be verbally aggressive toward the student.  During the pre-disciplinary meeting, Mr. Rice stated that during the incident, he did use an elevated voice, and that he did not recall but may have touched the backpack of the student.  Based on the foregoing facts the allegations against Mr. Rice include inappropriate language and/or behavior with or to students, failure to exercise best professional judgment, and unnecessary physical force on a student.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Michael Rice's suspension without pay, effective March 13, 2008 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the March 12, 2008, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Darron Davis; Kenyetta V. Haywood

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District

On or about May 16, 2007, Bettina Young became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of insubordination.



Sufficient just cause exists for Ms. Young to be disciplined pursuant to Sections 1012.22(1)(f) and 1012.27(5) Florida Statutes; 6B-4.009 Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal; Palm Beach County School District Policies 1.013 and 3.27, as well as Administrative Directive 3.27, and Article II, Section M(6) of the Collective Bargaining Agreement between the Palm Beach County Classroom Teachers Association and the School Board of Palm Beach County, for violations of the foregoing.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Bettina Young's suspension without pay, effective March 13, 2008 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the March 12, 2008, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.
