Oct 15, 2014 - 1. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

DESCRIPTION:

The purpose of this Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) is to set forth the terms and conditions that will apply to the research study project that is to be performed to evaluate the District's K-5 Literacy program.  The Literacy Program utilizes a District-created curriculum and instructional materials from various publishers. This study will take place over multiple school years, for the sole purpose of realigning the curriculum and improving instruction.

 

Phase 1 took place during the 2013-2014 School Year.  During Phase 1 the District collected and shared de-identified student data, which does not identify the data by student name, student number, parent name, birth date, address, age, or social security number.  It only identifies data by race, ethnicity, Title I status, gender, FRL, ESE, and ELL with scores on the FAIR, FLKRS, Running Records/Benchmark Assessments, by school number and name, for the purpose of review and analysis of the Literacy Program.

 

Phase 2 (2014-2015) of the study will replicate Phase 1 to continue to monitor the success of the Literacy Program and to continue to provide information for the realignment of the curriculum to ensure the success of all students.

 

Specific research activities, such as the development of research questions learned from Phase 1, will be conducted during the 2014-2015 and the 2015-2016 school year (Phase 3) and will include a research report. Through this research the District will gain a better understanding of the Program's impact on student achievement, as well as the implementation and fidelity of the Literacy Program.  It will provide us with feedback in order to make recommendations on how to strengthen the curriculum to meet the needs of all students.

EAI-2 Twenty-first Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) Grant
Meeting
Oct 15, 2014 - 1. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the 21st Century Community Learning Centers Grant from the Florida Department of Education for up to $3,080,000, effective October 1, 2014 through September 30, 2019, pending the receipt of the award letter, and approve any no-cost extension as applicable; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION:

The 21st Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) Grants support academic enrichment opportunities during afterschool and summer.  The grant is a formal joint applicant agreement with Prime Time Palm Beach County and includes many local partners. Since fall 2011, the  School District has been awarded five (5) grants to serve 25 elementary schools with a high percentage of low income students. This application, 21st CCLC Dazzling Starz, targets four (4) elementary schools:  Hammock Pointe, Egret Lake, Loxahatchee Groves, and The Conservatory of North Palm Beach.

 

These programs will assist students in meeting standards in core academic subjects; offer students a broad array of enrichment activities to complement their regular academic programs; and offer literacy and other educational services to the families of participating children. Each school site will support at least 60 low income students in grades K-5. The students will be selected to participate based on one or more of the following criteria:

  • below grade level on recent school and/or state assessments;
  • students who have been retained or in jeopardy of being retained;

College Board Readiness System Products Agreement (SAT Agreement with the College Board)

The SAT Agreement with the College Board provides the School District of Palm Beach County with the ability to administer the SAT to all 11th grade students at all district high schools during the school day as a means to provide these students with an opportunity to meet state college readiness requirements. The school day testing removes the most common barriers (location, transportation, inability to attend on a Saturday due to work or need to care for siblings) for students of poverty who might not otherwise participate in college readiness testing. In addition, it provides eligibility for one makeup test to participants who are absent from the designated test administration site.

 

The products provided by this Agreement include the following:

·        The SAT test for approximately 12,262  juniors at a discounted rate of $35 per exam

·        Free access to the Official SAT Online Course to all students participating in the school day testing (normal price is $69.95 per student)

·        Access to training for administrative personnel

·        Bulk registration

·        A Data License Agreement 

 

District Key Result #6 states that all schools will increase participation and performance of students of each racial/ethnic group taking the SAT, with a particular emphasis on underrepresented populations.  In 2010, Palm Beach County was the first district in the nation to offer the SAT during the school day, starting a nationwide trend in school day SAT testing. Data from the 2014 school day SAT results indicate a significant (3%) increase in participation.  Additionally, as compared to the 2013 school day SAT results, students are showing an increase in performance level as indicated by the increase in students achieving among the higher score bands. Arc Flash Electrical Safety NFPA 70E Training will provide our employees with electrical safety certification and fulfill requirements relative to OSHA 29 CFR Part 1910, Subpart S Electrical, and NFPA 70E®.  OSHA 1910.399 specifically defines a qualified person as “One who has received training in and has demonstrated skills and knowledge in the construction and operation of electrical equipment and installations and the hazards involved.” 

 

Three quotes were obtained and American Trainco LLC offered the lowest price per hour, and they have designed their course to help our employees “receive training” and “demonstrate knowledge” as required by the qualified person definition.
