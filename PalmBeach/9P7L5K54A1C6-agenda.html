I recommend the School Board approve the fifteen (15) year Renewal Charter Contract for Boca Raton Charter School and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(6)(g), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the district and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

Boca Raton Charter School is in compliance with its current charter.

 

The Charter Contract was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff.

 

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of fifteen (15) years, commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2029, except as provided in the charter.

 

The mission of Boca Raton Charter School is, “To provide an elementary International Baccalaureate Candidate program to children in the east Boca Raton area through public education dollars.  As defined by The International Baccalaureate Organization, its aim is to develop inquiring, knowledgeable and caring young people who help to create a better and more peaceful world through intercultural understanding and respect.  The program encourages students across the world to ‘become active, compassionate and lifelong learners who understand that other people, with their differences, can also be right.’”

 

Attached are copies of the full Contract, Program Review, Budget, Legal Checklist, Data Panels, Board Notification and Matrix for October 1st Agenda Items.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Diane Baumann's ten-day suspension without pay, effective Thursday, October 23, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about March 3, 2014, Ms. Baumann became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Inappropriate Interaction with Co-Workers/Threats of Violence, Ethical Misconduct, and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated. 

 

On February 19, 2014, it was reported to School Police that teacher Diane Baumann made specific threats against five co-workers at South Grade Elementary School.  School Police took a sworn written statement from Ms. Baumann in which she admitted to saying that she was “under so much pressure that she was going to go up into a tower, in a clown suit with a rifle” also adding, “but, I only have guns” to the statement. 

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.02(4)(a),(4)(d,(4)(e) and (5)(i), Code of Ethics; 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 6A-10.080(3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; 6A-10.081(5)(d), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida. Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Baumann completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on January 6, 2011. 

 

There is “just cause” to support the suspension of Ms. Diane Baumann. 

 

On September 15, 2014, the “Notice of Recommendation for Ten-Day Suspension Without Pay” was hand-delivered to Ms. Baumann from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

There is “just cause” to support the suspension of Ms. Selena Evans.

I recommend the Board approve Erika Latson's ten-day suspension without pay, effective Thursday, October 23, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about December 12, 2013, Ms. Latson became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Failure to Properly Account for School District Funds, Failure to Fulfill the Responsibilities of the Afterschool Site Director, Ethical Misconduct, and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated. 

 

On October 14, 2013, the After School Assistant Site Director received a payment of $1,100.00 in cash from a parent.  The Assistant Site Director then turned the funds over to Ms. Latson, who was the Site Director.  Ms. Latson first stated that she dropped the funds into the drop safe.  She later stated that she returned the funds to the Assistant Site Director.  During the course of the investigation, Ms. Latson voluntarily submitted to a Computer Voice Stress Analysis (CVSA) test regarding the missing funds.  The CVSA test indicated that Ms. Latson was being deceptive when she stated the she did not take the money. 

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.02(4)(a),  4(g), and (5)(c), (5)(f)(ii), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment; 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff. Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Latson completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on     August 19, 2010. 

 

There is “just cause” to support the suspension of Ms. Erika Latson. 

 

On September 15, 2014, the “Notice of Recommendation for Ten-Day Suspension Without Pay” was hand-delivered to Ms. Latson from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend the Board approve James Byrd's five-day suspension without pay, effective Thursday, October 23, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about March 7, 2014, James Byrd became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Inappropriate Comments to Students, Failure to Protect Student from Conditions Harmful to Learning, Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment, Ethical Misconduct, and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated. 

 

On February 10, 2014, it was reported that teacher James Byrd made several inappropriate comments to students at Emerald Cove Middle School.  These comments included things like “I am going to cut off your tongue if you don’t stop talking” or “I will rip off your arms” to a student for touching another student.  It was also reported that Mr. Byrd spoke to his students about having guns and carrying guns.  Mr. Byrd admitted to investigators that he had made these statements, but said that he was only joking.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 0.01(2)(c)(d), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; 1.013(1), Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff; 3.02(4)(a), (4)(d), (4)(e), (4)(g), and (5)(a)(i)(vii), Code of Ethics; 6A-10.080(2) and (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; 6A-10.081(3)(a)(e), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida.  Furthermore, it is noted that     Mr. Byrd completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on April 28, 2010.   

 

There is “just cause” to support the suspension of Mr. James Byrd. 

 

On September 15, 2014, the “Notice of Five-Day Suspension Without Pay” was hand-delivered to Mr. Byrd from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.
