COM1 Down Syndrome Awareness Month
Meeting
Oct 16, 2013 - 2. SCHOOL BOARD MEETING AT 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category

Type
Presentation

DESCRIPTION:

Approximately one in every 800 children are born with Down syndrome, representing approximately 5,000 births per year in the United States.  While research and early prevention have resulted in dramatic improvements in the lifespan and potential of those who are affected, more investigation is needed in the causes and treatment of Down syndrome.  People with Down syndrome possess a wide range of abilities, and are active participants in educational, occupational, social and recreational circles of the community.  Developed by the National Down Syndrome Society in 1995, the Buddy Walk is an annual event in cities across Florida and the nation celebrating the accomplishments of children and adults with Down syndrome. National Buddy Walks are promoted to increase understanding and acceptance of people with Down syndrome, while raising funds for scientific research into the causes and treatments.

The Council for Exceptional Children (CEC) is the largest international professional organization dedicated to improving the educational success of individuals with disabilities and/or gifts and talents. CEC advocates for appropriate governmental policies, sets professional standards, provides professional development, advocates for individuals with exceptionalities, and helps professionals obtain conditions and resources necessary for effective professional practice.

 

2013 CEC Chapter 200 Distinguished Award Winners:

 

Blake Bennett, CEC Chapter 200 Principal of the Year (Wellington Landings Middle)

Ms. Bennett has embraced the importance of an inclusive educational community for ALL learners. She facilities an inclusive climate through the implementation of the “Peer Partner Program”, where 7th grade students apply at the end of the school year to be Partners as a course in 8th grade. Students are trained to work in the classrooms, assist with Community Based Instruction, assist with small learning groups and centers, and participate in all activities. The bonds that are developed through this program are amazing. Last year they had the unfortunate loss of a student with a congenital heart condition. The Peer Partners rallied the school and created a memorial for her on our campus. This program and more of the great things at her school were presented to the State Board of Education last October.

 

Suzanne Pollard, CEC Chapter 200 Special Education Teacher of the Year (Wellington High School) This year marks Ms. Pollard’s 40th year in education. She began as a Spanish Teacher in Wilmington, Delaware. For the past 20 years she has been instrumental in supporting, advocating, and serving special education students at Wellington High School, most notably as ESE Contact and Department Chair. She believes that the opportunity to provide guidance, self-advocacy skills, support, and encouragement to students in her department has been her greatest contribution to education. Ms. Pollard will represent Chapter 200 as their 2013 Marjorie Crick Teacher of the Year nominee, aspiring to become the 2014 Florida CEC Teacher of the Year and represent the State of Florida at the National CEC conference in April 2015.

 

Joe Ametrano, CEC Chapter 200 General Education Teacher of the Year (Atlantic High School) In relation to how he plans for the social, emotional, and academic needs of his ESE students in his drama class, he states: “Over the years, I’ve always made an effort to encourage all of my students, no matter what their challenges or obstacles are, to get up there and perform. I always strive to create an atmosphere in the classroom that is a safe environment for all to be free and express themselves without the fear of being judged by their peers. I call it the “judgment free zone”. Often, I find that some of my ESE students are the least inhibited and most enthusiastic in the group, frequently, leading the way for other students to free themselves and get up there.

 

Omega Graham, CEC Chapter 200 Paraprofessional of the Year (Wellington High). When asked why she should be considered for “Paraprofessional of the Year”, Ms. Graham responded “It takes a special individual to be able to carry the worries, doubts, anxieties, and fears of not only themselves, but of others. I take a strong stand for equal opportunities, fairness, righteousness, and for those without a voice. I am a spiritually grounded individual that gives selflessly. I am always thinking of the best interest of the student. This helps me to be patient and compassionate when students require an extra dose of love, attention, or supervision. I feel I demonstrate the flexibility it takes to play the role of caretaker in the lives of our students.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the attached list of out-of-field/out-of-compliance teachers for Survey 2 for the FY2014 school year to service one or more classes out of their fields of certification.

DESCRIPTIION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified.  Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The District provides support for out-of-field/out-of-compliance teachers through in-service opportunities to develop instructional competencies required for the assigned duty.  Partnerships have been established with colleges and universities for required coursework and exam preparation.  Teachers teaching in subject areas not covered by their current certification are required to obtain an appropriate in-field certification within a specified time frame.
