I recommend the School Board approve the Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and Gaetz Aerospace Institute, Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University Agreement, effective March 24, 2022 through June 30, 2022 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

The Aerospace Science Academy (BASA) course taught by ERAU faculty at Boynton Beach High School and the Aeronautics courses taught by credentialed JROTC Instructors at Boca Raton High School and Lake Worth High School, will be transferable to appropriate ERAU academic programs, in accordance with the guidelines in force in the ERAU Undergraduate Catalog at the time the individual students matriculate into the ERAU system.  All individuals teaching BASA courses shall be qualified to teach under the criteria established by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS) and the State of Florida.  ERAU and the District will give first and serious preference to allowing qualified individuals employed already by the District to teach these courses.

 

ERAU will offer two curriculum tracks at BASA. The first, or Academic Track, will be a curriculum of introductory courses in a range of aviation career areas, including flight operations, weather, air traffic management, safety, business, unmanned aviation, and others, depending on student demand and the availability of qualified faculty.  The second, or Aviation Maintenance Science (AMS) Track, will provide students with the General portion of the FAA Airframe and Power Plant Mechanics Certificate curriculum.  The program has been in existence since 2007.

Our business education program will include a student computer lab and portable lab, internet-based educational learning software, teacher operated network management, and printing stations. The labs and associated equipment will be used to teach youth prerequisite skills in the business management and information technology career clusters. Students will be offered the opportunity to earn industry certifications in

  • Digital Information Technology

Approve Contract with Green Dot Corporation for Paycard Services. 

 

Rapid PayCard will provide the District a legally compliant 100% ePayroll program that enables significant cost savings over paper checks and wage statements, while providing a benefit for cardholders, and creating a more sustainable environmental model for payroll delivery. 

 

This program will provide PayCard Services at no charge to the District.  Vendors pay a fee when they accept credit and debit cards and a portion of that fee goes to Rapid Pay.  District employees do not pay any fees to use the debit card to make a purchase and the PayCard will benefit District employees who do not have a bank account or prefer to have their paycheck on a debit card. Approximately 500 of our 23,000 employees use this method to receive their paycheck.

 

Employees who do not sign up for direct deposit within a month of working with the District are issued a PayCard. Each pay period, the PayCard is loaded with the employee’s paycheck amount and can be used to pay for items with any vendor that accepts credit or debit cards.  Employees can also use an ATM to get cash.  

 

Our current provider, Bank of America (BOA), like all other banks, is planning to exit the PayCard market.  Due to the urgency to have a program in place prior to BOA cancelling this service and with the recommendation of Green Dot Corporation customers such as Pinellas, Hillsborough and Lake Counties, it was determined that in accordance with the District’s Purchasing Policy 6.14, the authority to engage in a Direct Negotiation was made to move forward with Green Dot Corporation.

The Vendor collects the fees for services and then deducts fees for the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) and Florida Department of Law Enforcement (FDLE).

 

The Office of Diversity and Business Practices has one District certified SBE vendor registered for this commodity and that vendor is awarded on the District’s Mobile Fingerprinting Bid.
