Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(6)(g), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the district and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.



Palm Beach Maritime Charter School is a high-performing charter school evidenced by the attached letter from the Florida Department of Education, pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(1).



The Charter Contract was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff.



The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of fifteen (15) years commencing July 1, 2012 and ending June 30, 2027, except as provided in the charter. Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(12)  ". . . In order to facilitate access to long-term financial resources for charter school construction, charter schools that are operated by a municipality or other public entity as provided by law are eligible for up to a fifteen-year charter, subject to approval by the district school board."



At the request of Palm Beach Maritime, the school does not acknowledge that School Board Policy 1.092, “Inspector General,” will govern.  However, many provisions exist within the Charter that would allow the Inspector General go monitor the school and inspect records such as Section VIII (D), sub-paragraphs 2-5 on pages 54-55 and one of the good cause grounds to terminate the contract is, “(g) failure to allow the Sponsor reasonable access to facilities and records to review data sources, including collection and recording procedures” on page 18.



Palm Beach Maritime Charter School will serve students grades K-8.



The mission of Palm Beach Maritime is to, "Actively engage and challenge students in a nurturing environment, while providing a rigorous, experienced-based instruction, utilizing the unique location and facilities of the Palm Beach Maritime Museum.  The Charter School curriculum will focus on bringing young people to the sea so that they might better understand its impact on all aspects of humanity."



The Data Panel for Palm Beach Maritime Charter School is attached.



The FLDOE High Performing Letter is attached.



A copy of the Charter Contract and annual budget for Palm Beach Maritime Charter School is attached for your review.

I recommend that the School Board approve the fifteen (15) year Renewal Charter Contract for Palm Beach Maritime Charter School and authorize the Superintendent or his/her designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

Approve Amendment 3 of the Teacher Evaluation Systems with Learning Sciences International LLC.



By Board Action on June 1, 2011, a one year contract with Learning Sciences International, LLC was approved to meet the requirements in Florida Senate Bill 736 "Student Success Act" which revised provisions in several State Statutes relating to the evaluation of instructional personnel and school administrators. The Student Success Act amended F.S. 1012.34 to require the Department of Education to approve each school district's instructional and school administrator evaluation systems.  Florida school districts are required to establish a teacher observation and evaluation system that meets the following requirements:



1. Alignment with the adopted Florida Educator Accomplished Practices.
2. Practices strongly linked to increased student achievement.
3. Based on contemporary research of effective practices.
4. The principal, direct supervisor and any other individual performing observations will use, at a minimum, the same core of effective practices.
5. Meet criteria listed in s.1012.37, F.S.



The Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) selected Dr. Robert Marzano's Art and Science of Teaching Causal Teaching Evaluation as the State's model. 



On June 20, 2012 the Board approved Amendment 1 of The Learning Sciences International, LLC, agreement which extended the term of the agreement through 6/30/13 and included a new Service Summary at a cost of $209,800.



Amendment 2 to the Agreement established a revised Service Summary which re-purposes some of the training included in the Original Service Summary for this fiscal year to better align with training needs and added additional services at a cost of $206,000.



Amendment 3 to the Agreement augments the Service Summary to include Attachment A, Teacher Mentor Program at a cost of $45,500.  The Teacher Mentor Program allows for one designated teacher per school to be trained in Domain 1 and Deliberate Practice which will be implemented next year.  The liaison will assist administration with the professional development of the staff on Marzano and be available to staff to answer questions and be a peer coach.  Professional Development Staff will also participate in the training so as to provide continual support to the liaisons and the schools.




Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the Learning Science International Amendment 3 to the Agreement for Teacher Mentor Program in the amount of $45,500 effective through 6/30/12 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

Nov 7, 2012 - Special Meeting 11-07-12
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

The Governing Board of Florida International Language Academy ("FILA") elected to terminate the Charter Contract on October 17, 2012, due to extremely low enrollment and financial challenges.  (See attached FILA’s Notification Letter)



The School District of Palm Beach County entered into a Charter Contract with FILA to operate a charter school in Palm Beach County on July 18, 2012, and the term of Contract was for five (5) years, ending on June 30, 2017, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.



FILA served students in Grades K-8.  There is no negative impact to any student.  There were 68 students enrolled at the school.  The parents have been notified that FILA voluntarily closed as of October 17, 2012. 





Moreover, none of the teachers are on leave from the School District of Palm Beach County.





There is no Data Panel, as the school just opened in 2012.





A copy of FILA’s Contract is attached for your review.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board Accept the voluntary termination of Florida International Language Academy (FILA) effective November 7, 2012.

On August 1, 2012, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Florida Futures Academy, Riviera Beach Campus to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2013. 



Per the listing of applicants filed with the Department of Education, Florida Futures Academy, Riviera Beach Campus did not submit an application in any other District in Florida and there are no other charter schools currently operating.



According to the Florida Futures Academy, Riviera Beach Campus' application submitted to Palm Beach County, the mission is, "To provide the community with a viable alternative for adolescents and young adults to earn a high school diploma.  Through a blended, self-paced instructional model, delivery of individualized instruction and support services, relationship building in a nurturing, learning environment, students formerly at-risk will transform to students at-promise.  The mission is to enable those who cannot currently envision a positive future into young adults who become productive and contributing members of the community."
