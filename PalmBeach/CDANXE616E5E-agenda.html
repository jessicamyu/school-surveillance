I recommend that the board approves the Agreement with Communities in Schools of Palm Beach County, Inc., with the Support Services Department effective September 1, 2022; and authorized the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

Communities In Schools of Palm Beach County (CIS), Inc. provides services through its Students Achieving Program as integrated student services program. The Communities in Schools goal is to link needed community services and resources by developing both business and health and human service partnerships to targeted school sites.  The focus is on students in 6th – 12th grade so they are motivated through incentive and enrichment activities to improve their academic achievement, stay in school, succeed, advance to the next grade level and build their personal strengths.  Communities In Schools is focused on building self-sufficiency by providing teacher inspired and intangible methodologies that concentrate on an assets based approach delivery of services.

 

Targeted populations area are identified as at-risk students by school personnel who take into account their attendance, academic behavior, attitudes or other social emotional needs.  These students are serviced by CISPBC staff because of poor test scores, multiple retentions, poor attendance, or low grades-all characteristics of students who are at risk of dropping out of school.  Research has proven that school dropouts pose an economic and social risk to the community.  Communities In Schools is an evidence-based program.

 

The expected outcomes for the FY23 school year include:

  • Increased attendance
  • Improved academic performance
  • Improved behavior

 

Schools Serviced

  • Pahokee High School
  • Glades Central High School
  • Lakeshore Middle School
  • Riviera Beach Preparatory

I recommend the Board approve the renewal of the Agreement with the School Board of Seminole County effective on the last date of approval by a party through June 30, 2023.

DESCRIPTION:

The East Coast Technical Assistance Center (ECTAC) provides technical assistance to a collaborative network of school districts regarding selective programs contained in the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA). ECTAC has provided services for the District since 2005. Services include: 

 

1. Quarterly meetings for federal programs administrators at which key state and federal Department of Education staff present on relevant and timely topics impacting federal programs practitioners. 

 

2. Ongoing technical assistance for federal programs to ensure the District remains in compliance with the requirements of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) and Florida's School Improvement grants.

 

3. Networking with other member districts across the state for the purpose of sharing effective programs and practices. 

 

4. Advocacy on behalf of the District on program administration, program review, and implementation of Florida Department of Education guidelines. 

 

5. Capacity development for District staff in the areas of the effective engagement of families, implementation of effective instructional strategies and best practices identified in scientifically based research, school improvement reform, and the maximization of resources to improve student outcomes. 

 

The School Board of Seminole County is the fiscal agent for ECTAC.

DESCRIPTION:

On January 22, 2020, the Board approved the purchase of a Master Builders Risk policy, which covers damage to property under construction, as well as theft of materials from the site during the construction phase for new and replacement schools/buildings.  The policy covers all facilities construction projects started and scheduled during the policy period.  The policy period is March 1, 2020 through March 1, 2023.

 

The Master Builders Risk Policy eliminates the need to pay contractors directly for a project-specific, stand-alone, builders risk policy.  This reduces expenditures because the contractors' rates for this coverage are estimated to be much higher per school than what the School Board can obtain on its own by insuring all the projects under one policy.

 

The Master Builders Risk policy has one year remaining in its three-year term.  It has a per-project maximum value of $75,000,000 (which is sufficient to cover every proposed project), with a non-windstorm deductible ranging from $5000 - $25,000 per loss (the deductible varies based on the project size).  Named windstorm losses are subject to a three (3) percent deductible ($100,000 minimum).  Since the District owns the policy, rather than the contractor, the District is eligible for FEMA reimbursements for deductible payments.  Contractors, on the contrary, are not eligible for FEMA assistance. 

 

The policy was underwritten based on anticipated construction values of approximately $340 million over three years, which was provided by Facilities Management, however the estimate was not sufficient to account for the sharp increase in construction costs caused by a surge in demand and supply chain delays.   New projects have also been added to the policy, with a total additional estimated construction cost of $410 million over the original estimate at policy inception.  It is still anticipated the District will reduce its Builders Risk insurance expenditure over the policy period, as compared to what it would be paying contractors for similar coverage.
