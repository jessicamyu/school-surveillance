COM3 National Mentoring Month

DESCRIPTION:

Mentoring provides an exemplary opportunity for youth in Palm Beach County to have more positive role models in their lives.  Research shows that mentored youth are 52 percent less likely to skip a day of school, 46 percent less likely to start using drugs, and 27 percent less likely to start drinking.  Mentoring is a true partnership, requiring businesses, volunteers, administrators, teachers, students, parents, and private and public community members to work together to provide a successful program.  National Mentoring Month provides an opportunity to recognize and commend the efforts of these programs and raise community awareness of the importance of mentoring.

 

The School District partners with a number of organizations to provide mentors for our students.  Take Stock in Children is one of those organizations.  The mission of Take Stock in Children is to passionately promote personal worth, self-responsibility, and academic success for deserving children from low-income families by providing a unique set of resources including mentors, scholarships, long-term support, student advocacy, and a guaranteed educational opportunity.

 

Take Stock in Children is a comprehensive program that helps children succeed by providing college and vocational scholarships, volunteer mentors; student advocates/case managers, tutoring, early intervention and long-term support. High standards, parental involvement, and community support are crucial to the program's success.

 

Take Stock's Mentors are a vital part or "key" to the success of the program.  They meet with their student once a week for 30 minutes. These men and women in our community give their time, support, encouragement and guidance to help their student achieve their goals in acquiring a college scholarship and becoming a responsible and motivated adult with a passion to succeed.

Subject
COM4 Becky Watson, Belle Glade Citizen of the Year
Meeting
Jan 15, 2014 - REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
9. Presentations
Type
Presentation

DESCRIPTION:

Becky Watson has been a dedicated educator with the School District of Palm Beach County for 32 years. A Music teacher at Rosenwald Elementary, Ms. Watson was recently named Citizen of the Year by the Belle Glade Chamber of Commerce.  When she began with the School District in 1982 she recognized the need for enrichment programs for the area’s youth and started a Cub Scout Pack at her school. She served as Cubmaster for many years, earning several awards from the Boy Scouts of America’s Gulf Stream Council.  When the Kravis Center for the Performing Arts and the Palm Beach County School District began their Spotlight on Young Musicians program, Ms. Watson was quick to make sure her students were involved; even driving them back and forth to the Kravis Center for rehearsals. One year she had the honor of being the Director of the program. She has also been a finalist for the Dwyer Award and was awarded a grant from VH-1’s Save the Music Foundation to purchase band instruments for her students.

Ms. Watson is also involved in her community.  As one of Belle Glade’s few piano teachers, she has taught countless numbers of children to play the piano. When the Living Christmas Tree committee decided to add a children’s choir to the program, she joined the committee and went to work as the children’s choir director, something she has continued to do for over 20 years.  At her church she is the Chair of the Leadership Council and spear-headed the church’s first Law-Enforcement Appreciation dinner, something that has become an annual event. She is the director of the hand bell choir, a member of United Methodist Women and the Chancel Choir.

She is also a member of the Black Gold Jubilee Committee where she has co-chaired the Gospel Sing for many years. In recognition for her years of service to education and the community, she was honored as a “Woman of Accomplishment” by Palm Beach State College.

DESCRIPTION:

AdvancED is the world’s largest education community, serving more than 30,000 public and private schools and districts across the United States and in more than 70 countries.   AdvancED brings together more than 100 years of experience and the expertise of three US-based accreditation agencies including the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Council on Accreditation and School Improvement (SACS CASI). 

 

Being an accredited district ensure that students continue to have enhanced access to federal loans and scholarships, as well as to postsecondary education and military opportunities. Having a fully accredited school system helps the community compete for businesses that bring more jobs to our area.

The self-study was completed and a 36 member team from AdvancED conducted a three day external review in January 2013.  The team conducted interviews at District’s schools with school leaders, teachers, students, parents, and members of the community. The team also talked with all School Board members, district-level leadership, school principals, union and advisory committee representatives, area superintendents, and community and business members.

As a result of the self-study and external visit, AdvancED congratulates The School District of Palm Beach County on the achievement of accreditation for the whole institution.

This achievement recognizes the quality of education afforded the students in the School District of Palm Beach County through the leadership of the superintendent and governing authority, the dedication and service of the professional staff, and the support of community stakeholders.

 

The AdvancED Accreditation Commission provides international recognition to The School District of Palm Beach County, the students, and community for its achievement of accreditation of a quality school system.

DESCRIPTION:

A joint committee of District and CTA representatives have negotiated amendments to Article II, Section G of the Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) and a new teacher evaluation instrument/procedure to replace the Classroom Teacher Assessment System (CTAS) Handbook and in doing so to comply with all applicable State Statutes including the “Student Success Act.”   After 30 months of negotiations, including the summers of 2011, 2012 and 2013, the Joint Evaluation Negotiations Committee (JENC) reached a tentative Agreement on December 16, 2013 to replace the current contract language in Article II, Section G with new contract language.  Also on December 16, 2013, JENC reached a tentative agreement on a new Classroom Teachers Evaluation System (CTES) Handbook to replace the current CTAS Handbook.  Together these two documents comprise a tentative new agreement on the negotiated Evaluations policies, procedures and forms to be used when evaluating employees in the T-bargaining unit, i.e. District employees represented by CTA.

 

A teacher’s annual evaluation will be based on two components:  An Instructional Practices component and a Student Learning Growth component.  These two components are then combined and the teacher is rated that school year as “Highly Effective”, “Effective”, “Needs Improvement” (or for an employee in his/her first three years of employment as “Developing”) or “Unsatisfactory”.  Most of the content of the new CTES Handbook directly relates to identifying a teacher’s strengths and weaknesses and to provide professional development to improve the teacher’s skills.
