I recommend the School Board approve the Charter Renewal for Seagull Academy for Independent Living and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

Please Note:  Charter School personnel will have five minutes to make comments to the Board.

 

DESCRIPTION:

On December 8, 2010, Seagull Academy of Independent Living was granted a five (5) year Renewal Contract to serve Exceptional Student Education ages 11-22 in grades 6-12.  This Contract expires on June 30, 2015.

 

The mission of the school is to, “Assist people to live in dignity and realize their life potential through education, training, employment, residential services, transportation and support services. People are assisted throughout the greater Palm Beach County area.”

 

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)1, Seagull Academy of Independent Living meets the criteria for renewal and does not fall under any grounds for termination.

 

The School Board’s approval of the Charter Renewal indicates an agreement to enter into charter contract negotiations, and if successfully negotiated by Legal and approved by the School Board, will result in entering into a Charter Contract.  The Charter will be a binding agreement, which shall detail the rights and responsibilities of the School Board and the Charter School.  If the Charter Renewal is not approved by the School Board, the School Board is required to send a 90 day notice letter indicating the reasons for non-renewal and the right to appeal.  If the Charter School does not appeal, or the School appeals and the School Board prevails, then the school is closed after the 90 days.

 

Attached are copies of the Charter Contract, Mid-Year Review, Program Review, Board Notification, and Matrix for April 1, 2015 Board Agenda Items.

DESCRIPTION:

On December 8, 2010, the School Board granted Day Star Academy of Excellence a five (5) year Renewal Charter Contract with the School Board of Palm Beach County.  The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2010, and ending June 30, 2015, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.

 

The mission of the school, “Incorporates a belief in the philosophy that 'every child can learn' given the appropriate tools, skills, and opportunity needed to foster a love for reading and to promote self-discipline, motivation, and excellence in learning.  All students will learn – given a set of standards (academic and behavioral), enough time to learn attitudinal and academic readiness, quality instruction (aligned instructional objectives, strategies, activities, assessments, and parental and community involvement that support learning), and instructional delivery that (1) sets a stage – what is to be learned, how it is to be learned, why it is important to be taught and learned; (2) varies the teaching techniques of the same lesson for students of varying learning styles within that same classroom, and (3) actively involves students in the preparation and delivery of instruction.”

 

Day Star Academy of Excellence received a grade of “F” for the fiscal year 2014.  The Sponsor’s staff provided technical assistance to Day Star Academy of Excellence in the drafting of the School Improvement Plan that was approved by the School Board of Palm Beach County on February 4, 2015.

 

Due to noted financial, facilities, and academic problems, on February 25, 2015, during the Program Review, the Sponsor recommended, and has developed, the attached proposed Corrective Action Plan (CAP) and Second Amendment to Day Star Academy’s Charter Contract.

 

The Sponsor has communicated through the school’s attorney to review the proposed CAP and Second Amendment. 

 

The School has agreed to all of the provisions in the CAP and Second Amendment.

CS3 - Leadership Academy West - Proposed Non-Renewal of Charter Contract
Meeting
Apr 1, 2015 - 3. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County approve the proposed Non-Renewal of the Charter Contract between the School Board of Palm Beach County and Leadership Academy West and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related correspondence and documents.

Please Note:  Charter School personnel will have five minutes to make comments to the Board.

 

DESCRIPTION:

On June 4, 2014, the School Board granted Leadership Academy West a one (1) year Charter Renewal Contract with the School Board of Palm Beach County.  The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of one (1) year commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2015, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.

 

Leadership Academy West received a grade of “F” for the fiscal year 2014.  The Sponsor’s staff provided technical assistance to Leadership Academy West in the drafting of the School Improvement Plan that was approved by the School Board of Palm Beach County on February 4, 2015.

 

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)1, "A charter may be renewed provided a program review demonstrates that the criteria in paragraph (a) have been successfully accomplished and that none of the grounds for non-renewal established by paragraph (8)(a) has been documented."  In February, 2015, the District staff conducted a Program Review.  Based on the results of the criteria outlined in the Charter Renewal Program Review, Leadership Academy West shows there are deficiencies found regarding Academics and Assessment.  

 

F.S. 1002.33(8)(a)  The Sponsor may choose not to renew or may terminate the charter for any of the following grounds:

  1. Failure to participate in the state's education accountability system created in Fla. Stat. 1008.31, as required in this section, or failure to meet the requirements for student performance stated in the charter.
  2. Failure to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management.
  3. Violation of law.
  4. Other good cause shown.

 

The Superintendent’s recommendation of the proposed non-renewal of Leadership Academy West’s Charter is based on:

​

I.  Statutory and/or Contractual Grounds for Non-Renewal

 

1.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33(2)(b)1 & 2.

2.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33(7) & (9)(c).

3.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section I.D.3.c.

4.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section I.D.1a.(2)(c).

5.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section I.C.2.

6.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section III.F.1.c.

7.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section X.

8.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33(7) & (9)(c) and its Charter, Section I-General Provisions, Sub-Section D, 1.a.(1)(b) & (2)(m), Section IV, and Section XI.

 

II.  Reasonable details of the grounds underlying the proposed non-renewal along with supporting documentation.   

 

1.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33(2)(b) (1) & (2).

 

a)  School Year 11-12     Grade of F

b)  School Year 12-13     Grade of D

c)  School Year 13-14     Grade of F

 

The Charter also states in Section I, D, 1.a.(2)(c) that, "two (2) nonconsecutive grades of "F" within a three (3) year period" is good cause for non-renewal.

 

2.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (7) & (9)(c).

Insufficient evidence documented in the Program Renewal Review of the school's successful performance academically and financially (Exhibit 1).

 

3.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract, Section I, D, 3, (c).

Lack of evidence of specialized instruction scheduled with a certified instructor on a daily basis for secondary school students reading below grade level.

 

4.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract, Section I, D, 1, a.(2)(c).

Three consecutive years of school grades "F-D-F" demonstrate lack of academic success for the student population.

 

District specialists' review of formative assessments for current school year indicate little or no academic improvement for the student population which would predict a fourth year of poor academic performance.

 

5.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section I, C, 2.

The school has not been successful in recruiting and retaining certified skilled staff for instruction for core courses especially for students reading below grade level.

 

6.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section III, F, 1, (c). (Exhibit 1)

a)  Lack of evidence of ESE students receiving services documented on their IEP.

b)  Lack of evidence of ESE procedures being followed for eligibilities and evaluations.

c)  Lack of evidence the ESE student IEPs are being written to meet student's needs.

 

7.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with its Charter Contract Section X.

The school has not been successful in recruiting and retaining certified skilled staff for instruction for core courses especially for students reading below grade level.

 

8.  Failure by Leadership Academy West to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (7) & (9)(c), and its Charter, Section I-General Provisions, Sub-Section D, 1, a, (1)(b) & (2)(m), Section IV, and Section XI.

Numerous budgetary, financial and operational issues documented in the Program Renewal Review, including the failure to provide the District reliable financial data since the June 30, 2013, audited financial report to enable a determination of financial viability. (Exhibit 1)

 

The District conducted a Mid-Year Review of the School and found numerous deficiencies.  The Director of the Department of Charter Schools and staff provided technical assistance via meeting to analyze the Review and provide the School another opportunity to cure.  The above items had not been corrected.

 

Please also note that the Sponsor just discovered that the third governing board member is the principal.  This violates Florida Statute Section 1002.33 (26)(c) which states:

 

(26)  STANDARDS OF CONDUCT

 

(c)  An employee of the charter school, or his or her spouse, or an employee of a charter management organization, or his or her spouse, may not be a member of the governing board of the charter school.

 

Based on the foregoing, statutory and contractual reasons and good cause exist to non-renew the Charter.
