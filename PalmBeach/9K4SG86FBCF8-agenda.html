Oct 20, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category

Action
Approximately one in every 800 children are born with Down syndrome, representing approximately 5,000 births per year in the United States.  While research and early prevention have resulted in dramatic improvements in the lifespan and potential of those who are affected, more investigation is needed in the causes and treatment of Down syndrome.  People with Down syndrome possess a wide range of abilities, and are active participants in educational, occupational, social and recreational circles of the community.  Developed by the National Down Syndrome Society in 1995, the Buddy Walk is an annual event in cities across Florida and the nation celebrating the accomplishments of children and adults with Down syndrome. National Buddy Walks are promoted to increase understanding and acceptance of people with Down syndrome, while raising funds for scientific research into the causes and treatments.

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1012.42, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. G1 - 2010-2011 Grant Application for the Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention (“OJJDP”)
Meeting
Oct 20, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The purpose of this project is to supplement and expand the mentoring role of the Career/Graduation coach to provide a comprehensive, multi-component approach for academic success and career transition for Palm Beach County’s at risk and adjudicated youth utilizing the services of the Family Youth Counselor.  The project’s activities will focus on providing mentoring and coaching opportunities for at risk and adjudicated youths 16 - 18 years of age in Palm Beach County Alternative Education schools with the primary goal of reducing the dropout rate of this population.



 



 The Family Youth Counselor will work directly with students, their parents/guardians and with support staff (guidance counselors, career/graduation coach and academic coaches) at the Alternative Education schools to develop individualized Support Plans. Services will include individual and group mentoring on 1) normative education, 2) information about the consequences of drugs and violence, and 3) social skills training, including social influences training, goal setting training and parent education skills.



 



Through collaboration with students, parents/guardians, teachers and coaches, community control officers, business partners, community resources, career centers, and postsecondary/vocational schools, the Family Youth Counselor will promote supportive learning environments through graduation and career counseling, postsecondary/vocational awareness activities with guest speakers, mentoring programs; professional development to PBC teachers and guidance counselors, and monitoring student progress. The Family Youth Counselor will model successful mentoring school programs to build capacity within the fourteen (14) Alternative Education Schools.  An estimated student population of 945 will be targeted to increase attendance, reduce discipline referrals which will improve academic gains and lead to more graduations. 



 



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in this particular case due to official award notification September 14, 2010.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.



A copy of the Grant application will be on file in the Board Minutes office. 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Grant application for  the Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention in the estimated amount of $200,000 effective August 1, 2010 to July 31, 2012, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

AC1 - Standard Agreement to Provide Services to Parentally Placed Private School Students
Meeting
Oct 20, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA) of 2004 requires that the School District provide services to parentally placed nonprofit private school students with disabilities.  Specifically, IDEA requires that the District:





  • divide the total IDEA allocation for the fiscal year by the total number of eligible students attending public and private schools located in Palm Beach County, including McKay Scholarships students.  This amount is referenced as the proportionate share.  This amount is to be "set aside" to provide services to parentally placed students in nonprofit private schools.

  • spend the proportionate share of IDEA funds for provision of services to parentally placed students with disabilities in nonprofit private schools Prek-12. 

  • participate in annual meaningful consultation between representatives of all nonprofit private schools located in Palm Beach County, private school parents, and the School District.  Meaningful consultation was held on January 28, 2010. 

  • provide services as outlined in the attached Cooperative Agreement pursuant to annual meaningful consultation. Services provided to students based on the Services Plans written by the School District.  Two hundred and ninety-seven (297) students accessed services during the 2009-2010 school year.

  • In August, 2010, twenty-seven contracts were approved by the School Board.  These additional five contracts were returned to the School District in time for the October board meeting.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the standard cooperative agreement format and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.  These agreements shall be in effect from October 22, 2010 through June 30, 2013.  The agreements are renewed yearly for the three year period, if both parties agree.

G2 - IBM Reading Companion Grant for Afterschool
Meeting
Oct 20, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

The Department of Afterschool Programming has been awarded IBM’s Reading Companion Grant, valued at $10,000 per school site to include: Reading Companion program software, technical support, and training.  This grant will target 33 Title I schools, providing additional support to struggling readers or low performing students enrolled in the Afterschool Literacy Enrichment Initiative (ALEI).  The Afterschool Reading Companion Project is an extension of the ALEI program.  In addition to participation in the ALEI program 4 hours per week, select Reading Companion identified students will be provided two (2) additional hours per week of computer-assisted, teacher directed guidance and support within the afterschool setting.



IBM Reading Companion Program general information:
• An interactive web-based, free technology that listens, guides, and teaches children and adults how to read and improve their English literacy skills.
• Uses innovative speech recognition technology that “listens” and provides individualized feedback to the user, enabling emerging readers to practice pronunciation as they acquire fundamental reading skills. 
• Software is designed to supplement, not replace, existing literacy curriculum in the school setting or within an adult literacy program.  Reading Companion is not designed to replace teachers, but meant to enhance reading instruction.
• Upon enrollment in the program, participants will receive a personal ID and password, allowing access during the school day and afterschool from any internet-accessible computer. 



Preliminary selection criteria for afterschool programs participating in this project include:
• Participation in Afterschool Literacy Enrichment Initiative (ALEI)
• AYP status
• Minority %
• ELL %
• Title I designation

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the IBM Reading Companion Afterschool Grant to the Department of Afterschool Programming in the amount of $330,000.00 In-Kind donation effective October 1, 2010 through September 30, 2011, and authorize the Superintendent of designee to sign all documents related to the grant which are required for implementation of grant activities.

Alison Adler          aadler@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the District.  This award is an In-Kind donation of $330,000.00 in software, training, and support, which will be allocated to to 33 afterschool school program sites operating under the Department of Afterschool Programming.
