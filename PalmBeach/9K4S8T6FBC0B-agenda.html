P1 Approve renewal of RFP 07C-002B for Advertising District-Wide Adult & Community Education Program Schedules with Florida Pennysaver/Palm Beach Newspapers



The original contract was awarded by the Board on January 24, 2007 for the period of February 2, 2007 through October 1, 2009 with the option to renew for two additional one-year periods. The first option to renew was exercised for the period of October 2, 2009 through October 1, 2010. This is the second and final renewal.



The Palm Beach Community Educator has been advertising adult and community education classes for the past ten years.  The Educator is mailed three times a year to over 600,000 homes throughout Palm Beach County. After the inception of the Community Educator in 2000, Adult and Community Education experienced a significant increase in student enrollment.



The following is a list of benefits as a direct result of advertising with the Palm Beach Community Educator:



1) Adult and Community Education is categorically funded by the Department of Education and/or student fees.  The entire program, which includes the funding of the Educator, has no impact on the K-12 budget. The Palm Beach County School District receives approximately $1.4 million for supporting Adult and Community Education programs.



2) The funds generated from the classes advertised in the Community Educator are the only source of revenue used to fund the K-12 tutoring support program. With the revenue generated last year, 110 schools were provided tutoring assistance and awarded 6,600 hours of tutoring instruction (60 hours per school) at a cost of $165,000. 



3) High School Credit Lab - Last year 3,515 high school students enrolled in credit recovery programs in order to get back on track to graduate. The credit labs are open in the summer for seniors who did not graduate with their class. This past summer, accessibility of credit labs enabled 91 seniors to graduate and 1,292 credits were earned by participating students.  The Credit Labs are advertised in the Educator.



4) 1,585 residents earned a State of Florida High School Diploma by passing the GED last year.  This program is advertised in the Educator.



5) The Educator advertises leasing opportunities to outside agencies and organizations.  Leasing generates money for the district as well as the school centers. In 2008-2009 leasing generated $1,459,214 in revenue.



6) In 2009-2010, Community Schools provided ongoing How to be a Better Substitute classes for 1,060 new daytime substitute teacher applicants. These classes are advertised in the Educator.



7) Over 56,000 tax payers participated in Adult and Community Education programs last year.



8) The Educator advertises English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESL) programs and citizenship classes for the growing non-English speaking population.  Last year 16,328 students participated in the ESL programs.



9) The Educator is used as a vehicle for disseminating school district information, such as the half cent sales tax referendum. In addition, the upcoming distribution of the educator will include information regarding the proposed property tax changes.



10) The Educator is distributed to all citizens of Palm Beach County, regardless if they have school-aged children.  Some programs are provided free of charge and others at very affordable prices.



In summary, the Palm Beach Community Educator's benefits far outweigh the cost.  The main goals are to provide life-long learning opportunities to our citizens, assist the K-12 program, and make the community a better place to live. The Palm Beach Community Educator is our life line and we are requesting the continued support of the school district.



The total cost of the Community Educator is funded through Adult and Community Education. The approximate total cost ranges from $407,580.00 up to $475,000.00.  Adult Education is categorically funded by Workforce Development funds through the Department of Education.  The cost of the Winter 2010 Community Educator is approximately $135,502.00 and will be funded through Workforce Development funds.  The cost of the Spring 2011 Community Educator is approximately $135,851.00 and the cost of the Fall 2011 Community Educator is approximately $136,227.00.  Both issues will be funded through fees generated from fee supported classes and GED fees.  Community Education funds include a $10 registration fee. 10% of the class fees plus $4 of the registration fee are used for funding the K-12 Tutoring Support grant.  $6 dollars of the registration fee is allocated for the printing and distribution of the Palm Beach Community Educator. 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the renewal of RFP 07C-002B for Advertising District-Wide Adult & Community Education Program Schedules for the period of October 2, 2010 through October 1, 2011.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore, Chief Operating Officer (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Judith Klinek, Assistant Superintendent, Grants Admin. & School Reform Accountability

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the K-12 operating budget. The funding comes from fees generated from Community Education classes, GED fees and Workforce Education funding.

RE-2 Palm Beach Public West Campus
Meeting
Sep 22, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)




  1.  In late 2003, the School Board entertained offers to purchase and lease the Palm Beach Public West Campus.  For various reasons, the School Board decided that it did not want to sell the West Campus but it was willing to enter into a 25 year lease.  (See Attached Lease Agreement Attachment "I")


  2. On January 21, 2004, the School Board directed staff to finalize negotiations with the Society of the Four Arts for lease (the “Lease”) of the Palm Beach Public West Campus.


  3. The 25 year Lease term began March 1, 2006 and expires on February 28, 2031.


  4. The Society of the Four Arts made an upfront lump-sum payment of $500,000.00 and in-kind contributions which includes School use of 35 parking spaces, educational programs and special events for the educational benefit of the students of Palm Beach County.


  5. At the initiation of the Lease, the Society of the Four Arts planned to spend an estimated $2,000,000.00 for repairs and renovations to the three (3) School Buildings on the West Campus, including needed upgrades to make the buildings ADA compliant, modernization of the heating, lighting and ventilation systems, and improvements to restore and preserve the character of the buildings.  However, the Society has since determined that would be more successful raising funds for the renovation of the facility if it were to purchase the West Campus.


  6. The Lease provides that the School Board may not terminate the lease during the first fifteen (15) years unless the School Board decides to sell the West Campus.  If the Lease is terminated (after the first fifteen (15) years) or the West Campus is sold during the Lease term, the School Board must refund, or credit, the Society of the Four Arts with an amortized amount of the prepaid rent.  (See Exhibit "C" to the Lease Agreement)


  7. The Lease also provides that the Society of the Four Arts will have a Right of First Refusal in the event the School Board decides to sell the West Campus during the Lease term.


  8. The newly modernized Palm Beach Public functions successfully without the West Campus.


  9. Considering that the three (3) school buildings on the west campus are no longer needed and that the age and condition of the buildings requires costly renovations, it is recommended that 0.69 acres be declared surplus.  This recommendation is in conformance with the educational plant survey performed on June 30, 2010.


  10. Staff will utilize an expedited Solicitation process to determine the most advantageous offer for the disposal of the West Campus.


  11. The 2004 average appraised value was $4,200,000.00.  The 2007 averaged appraised value was $6,625,000.00.  The 2010 averaged appraised value is $5,100,000.00.  The valuation was based on the Fee Simple interest without considertion of the lease.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board adopt a resolution declaring the former Palm Beach Public West Campus located at 240 Cocoanut Row, Palm Beach, to be surplus; direct staff to advertise to divest the 0.69 acre site; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore (moorej@palmbeachschools.org)
Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeachschools.org)

Financial Impact:

A sale of the West Campus would result in the receipt of revenue by the School Board.
