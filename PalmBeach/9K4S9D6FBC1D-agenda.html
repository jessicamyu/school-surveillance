In conjunction with Black History Month,  the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Coordinating Committee Oratorical contest winners  will be recognized.  In 1971, the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Coordinating Committee, Inc. (MLKCC), was organized initially as the Black Educators Caucus, sponsoring remedial tutorial programs in cooperation with the Minority Mental Health Association.  In 1975, the program became an after school project and in 1979 expanded in partnership with the School District of Palm Beach County to (1) sponsor oratory, poster and drama contest commemorating Dr. King's January birth date; (2) to provide Afro-Arts Annual presentation and (3) to participate in Palm Beach Community College's Annual Multicultural Festival.  In addition to several other activities the competitive events include art exhibits, performing arts, oratorical, poster and essay contests.  The executive director is retired Palm Beach County teacher, Edith C. Bush.

State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) §6.1(7)(c) requires that, "Prior to April 1 of each year, each district shall review the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) and shall certify to the Office of Educational Facilities that the inventory is current and accurate." 



The Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) is available to all school districts for reporting facilities information. District FISH reports are available on-line for all district staff who have access to the electronic data base.



The facilities inventory may be corrected by submitting on-line transactions through the Educational Facilities Information System (EFIS). A district's facilities inventory may be corrected at any time when new additions or remodeling occurs, during a validation study conducted by the district or the Office of Educational Facilities (OEF), or as the result of an approved educational plant survey. New construction may be added to the inventory when a construction contract is issued. Large areas that are scheduled to undergo major remodeling should be updated in the inventory as soon as a contract agreement has been made. If a major remodeling project is conducted by district staff, the inventory should be updated when the project is substantially completed. Each district should conduct at least an annual update of FISH.

I.    Overview



On August 2, 2010, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Transitions Elementary Charter School ("Transitions") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2011.



The mission of Transitions is "To provide an innovative, loving, caring and supportive education for students with a deep interest in the arts to strive for excellence in academics and performance through the use of a fine arts and technology integrated curriculum."  Its intention is to provide an environment that sparks curiosity and inspires all students to develop their intellectual, creative and artistic talents in a manner that will enrich their own lives and the lives of those in their respective communities.



Its application was written utilizing the Model Florida Charter School Application (see Exhitit "A-1").  Thereafter, designated School District of Palm Beach County ("District") reviewers evaluated their application using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A-2").



The District's staff reviewers of charter school applications were trained how to evaluate the Model Florida Charter School Application utilizing the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument on May 13, 2010 (see Exhibit "B").



The District's charter application process was explained to applicants including Transitions during the New Applicants' Workshop held on May 27, 2010 (see Exhibit "C").



Transitions participated in a due process opportunity to meet and clarify their application.  Two reviews of the application were conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument ("Evaluation").  The second review included an Applicant Interview as outlined in Step 5 of the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process (see Exhibit "D").



II.   Applicant's Founding Governing Board



The members of the Founding Board are:  Mr. Jorge L. Perez, Ms. Marcia Griffin and Ms. Alicia Rodriguez Bower.



III.   Applicant Attending the Charter School Application Training



Transitions' representatives attended the District's charter school orientation conducted on May 27, 2010.  The meeting is not mandatory but is supplementary to the Florida Department of Education's mandatory applicant training to make applicants aware of the District's procedures.



IV.   District's Application Evaluation using Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Due Process Meetings with the District's Reviewers





  • September 8, 2010 - Application reviewed 1st time by District reviewers - Step #5 of the District's Application Process

  • Janaury 4, 2011 - Application reviewed 2nd time by District reviewers - Step #5 of the District's Application Process


V.   District's Reviewers Evaluation Ratings of Transitions' Application



The applicant received a copy of the Interview Summary (see Exhibit "E").  Designated District staff reviewed the 20 sections (see Exhibit "F") of Transitions' application that were uploaded for the Board Workshop, tentatively scheduled for February 2, 2011.  Each section of the Evaluation Instrument received one of the following ratings: Meets the Standard, Partially Meets the Standard, or Does Not Meet the Standard.  The following ratings indicated that their application did not meet the statutory requirement set forth in F.S. 1002.33(6).



VI.   Details Concerning Each of the Evaluation Instrument Ratings for Transitions



The evaluators for the two sections listed below were in attendance.





  • Section 6 - Exceptional Students rating - Partially Meets the Standard

  • Section 18 - Budget rating - Partially Meets the Standard


District's Reviewer's Responses



   A.    Section 6 - Exceptional Students



The Exceptional Students section should demonstrate an understanding of the requirements of the school to serve all students and provide a concrete plan for meeting the board spectrum of educational needs and providing all students with a quality education.



Statutory Reference(s):



F.S. 1002.33(16)(a)(3)



Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument Criteria:



A response that meets the standard will present:





  • Describe how the school will comply with the state and federal requirements for serving students with disabilities, including the procedures that will be utilized for identifying students with special needs, developing Individualized Education Plans and 504 Plans, and providing a full range of services.

  • Identify the staffing plan for the school's special education program, including the number and qualifications of staff.

  • Describe how the school will serve gifted and talented students.

  • Describe how the school's effectiveness in serving exceptional education students will be evaluated.


District's Reviewer's Questions After the Second and Final Review:





  • Elaborate on assessment of ESE students.

  • Please elaborate on what is meant by will be segerated only if the nature and severity of the disability is such that education in regular classes will be the use of supplementary aids and services cannot be achieved satisfactory.


The applicant will address the reviewer's concerns via contract negotiations.



   B. Section 18 - Budget



The Budget section should provide financial projections for the school over the term of its charter.



Statutory Reference(s):



Per Florida Statute, an application is subject to the following requirements:



Contains an annual financial plan for each year requested by the charter for operation of the school for up to 5 years.  This plan must contain anticipated fund balances based on revenue projections, a spending plan based on projected revenues and expenses, and a description of controls that will safeguard finances and projected enrollment trends. 1002.33(6)(a)(5)F.S.



In order to ensure fiscal responsibility, an application for a charter school shall include a full accounting of expected assets, a projection of expected sources and amounts of income, including income derived from projected student enrollments and from community support, and an expense projection that includes full accounting of the costs of operation, including start-up costs. 1002.33(6)(b)(2)F.S.



Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument Criteria:



A response that meets the standard will present:





  • Budgetary projection which are consistent with all parts of the application, including the school's mission, educational program, staff plan and facility.

  • A realistic assessment of the projected sources of revenue and expenses that ensure the financial viability of the school.


The applicant included all the five (5) year budgets as required in the application.  The budget presented was not consistent in the areas of ESE and Employment sections.  In addition, the transportation revenue was overstated for the ESE students.



Based on the criteria outlined in the Application Evaluation Instrument, the application includes most of the information but requires additional information that can be cured at the time of contract negotiations. 



VII.   District's Reviewer's Response:



The application addresses most of the criteria, but the responses lack meaningful detail.





  • Paraprofessionals listed in the staffing plan, Attachment E, were not allocated properly in the budget.

  • Tutoring expense to enhance student learning for those students reading below level is not included in the budget.

  • Library expense for reading enrichment is not included in the budget.


The assessment of the projected sources of revenue and expenses that ensure the financial viability of the school is not realistic.





  • Revenues - The applicant overstated the transportation revenue using the highest funding to transport ESE students but the school will service 251 matrices.

  • As a result, the revenue shortfall is $8K for each year of operation.

  • Expenditures - The applicant understated the expenditures as listed above.


The District prepared an analysis to reflect the employees not included in the budget (i.e. paraprofessionals, guidance counselor, and security personnel) as well as expenditures excluded from the budget (i.e. tutoring, library books, etc.).  The applicant will be strongly dependent on obtaining Implementation Grant funds to avoid a loss/deficit.  If the grant is not received, it could result in the charter school being at risk of financial emergency.



A copy of Transitions' application is attached for your review.

The SAT Agreement with the College Board provides the School District of Palm Beach County with the ability to administer the SAT to:





  • Students on April 27, 2011, during the school day, as a means to assist these students in preparing and planning for success in college. 


  • All 11th grade students


  • All 12th grade students who have not met graduation or college readiness requirements as an alternate means of fulfilling those requirements.


The products provided by this Agreement include the following:





  • The SAT for approximately 7,324 students


  • The Official SAT Online Course which includes 18 interactive lessons, six official practice tests, personalized score reports, and instant scoring and feedback, including automated scoring of the essay portion.  College Board is waiving the customary fee of $5.50 per student, resulting is a cost-savings of over $40,000.


  • SAT Skills Insight a comprehensive online tool showing students and educators the specific academic skills that are tested on the exam, using real SAT test questions. The program offers individualized support and practice for students as they prepare to take the SAT.


  • Professional development on SAT Skills Insight, the Official SAT Online Course and school-based test supervisor and registration coordinator roles and responsibilities.


  • A tailored communications package to support the District, schools, students and parents.


A Data License Agreement that will provide for SAT student data sharing between College Board and the District to:





  • Enable the District to incorporate College Board Data into its analysis and Educational Data Warehouse systems to improve college readiness.


  • Demonstrate to the District the impact of student preparation (measured by the difficulty and rigor of students' high school curriculum) on SAT scores and overall college readiness.


  • Assist the College Board in building new Palm Beach County District/State reporting services for future initiatives; such as the development and validation of a scale that measures the difficulty and rigor of students' high school curriculum. The College Board and the District will work collaboratively to determine how the research will be used for future initiatives in Palm Beach County.


  • Provide professional development to District teachers receiving and reviewing student SAT score data.


A Data License Amendment allowing the same SAT student data sharing as described above from the FY2010 SAT.

BACKGROUND INFORMATION



 



The motion before the Board is permitted under Florida Statutes to grant voluntary recognition to a Union to be the exclusive bargaining representative for a group of employees without the necessity of conducting a formal representational election to determine the wishes of this group of employees. 



 



The SEIU-FPSU has indicated it has proof that a majority of



the District’s Food Service Managers have shown they want



SEIU-FPSU as their exclusive bargaining representative;



however the District has no verifiable evidence that this is



accurate.



 



SEIU-FPSU contracted with True Ballot, Inc, to conduct a



referendum among the District’s Food Service Managers in



December of 2010 with respect to the formation of a union.



True Ballot, Inc’s reported results of this referendum show



that of the 183 identified Food Service Managers, 103



responded:  98 saying “Yes, I want to form a Union” and 5



saying “No, I do not want to form a Union”. 



 



This referendum did not ask Food Service Managers if they



wanted SEIU-FPSU as their exclusive bargaining



representative.  It asked only if there was interest in forming



a union.    



 



Based on discussions and verbal agreements between Staff and



Union leadership on January 19, 2011, followed by subsequent



commitments to the School Board that an MOU would be drafted



as soon as possible, an MOU was drafted for approval by the



Board and the Union that would allow for a Florida Public



Employees Relations Commission (PERC) conducted



representational election of the District’s Food Service Managers



to determine their desire to be or not to be represented by SEIU-



FPSU for the purposes of collective bargaining.



 



Such MOU was approved by District Counsel for legal sufficiency



and hand-delivered to the SEIU-FPSU President on the afternoon



of Monday, February 7, 2011 for SEIU-FPSU’s consideration and



approval.



 



Should the Board adopt the motion to voluntarily recognize



SEIU-FPSU as the representative union for all Food Service



Managers, the District’s Food Service Managers will be



denied the right to make the decision if they want or do not



want SEIU-FPSU to be their exclusive bargaining



representative.  A PERC conducted election, such as the one



called for in the MOU, will guarantee that a fair and impartial



vote will be conducted and that the wishes of our Food



Service Managers will be honored.    



 



As set forth in the MOU, if PERC certifies that a majority of



Food Service Managers voting in this PERC conducted election



selects SEIU-FPSU, the District will not oppose PERC from



certifying SEIU-FPSU as the exclusive  collective



bargaining representative of all Food Service Managers and



once so certified the District will commence bargaining to



amend the January 1, 2009 –December 31, 2011 Supervisory



Collective Bargaining Agreement to include Food Service



Managers as a part of the supervisory bargaining unit.   



 



A possible alternative to adopting this Motion is to substitute



the Motion with the MOU and to adopt the MOU pending



approval of SEIU-FPSU.   

Recommendation:

Having good cause to entertain this motion as it relates to the Board’s earlier direction on January 19, 2011 to bring forth  a motion to recognize SEIU-FPSU as the exclusive bargaining representative for the District’s Food Service Mangers, the following motion is presented to the Board for its consideration:



 



I recommend that the School Board under  Florida Statutes Section 447.307 voluntarily acknowledge SEIU-FPSU as the representative union for all Food Service Managers employed by the School District and authorize the Chair and Superintendent to execute all related documents.  In granting recognition, the Board is also waiving its rights, obligations and responsibilities under this statute in relation to this action.

CLS1 - 2011-2012 INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS ADOPTIONS AND PROGRAM BUDGET
Meeting
Feb 16, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category

The District Instructional Materials Adoption Committee was convened during 2010-2011, pursuant to School Board Policy 8.122 (3) to evaluate and recommend new materials for District adoption in the area of Science, grades K-12.  Committee membership included: subject area classroom teachers and coaches, District level science curriculum administrators, and lay citizens.  Committees reviewed all instructional materials programs submitted for state adoption and some additional submissions that were considered appropriate for committee review.  Titles recommended for District adoption are contained in Attachment A.



In August 2011, we complete our transition to the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards in Science at all grade levels.  The standards in grades K-8 identify four important topics (Bodies of Knowledge) for each grade level which are to be the instructional emphasis for that grade.  This curriculum organization is a major paradigm shift in science education.  The curriculum will develop deeply at each grade level around Big Ideas rather than continually spiraling and keeping science instruction on the surface.  To ensure that the texts selected for District adoption will assist teachers to effectively deliver the depth and richness in science demanded by the new standards, all Adoption Committee members received instruction on the new standards and expectations for teachers and students.  The instructional materials programs recommended for District adoption were carefully reviewed by committees to assure that they are aligned with specifications of the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards in Science.  The purchase of these new adoptions is essential so teachers have the tools they need to teach the new standards and provide a solid science foundation for high school and college level science courses.



The recommended instructional materials programs will be provided in both print and/or online digital formats.  Student editions in the core courses will be available to students via their Edline accounts.  Teacher materials and resources will be also available on Learning Village.  The elementary and middle school recommended instructional materials are available in Spanish for Dual Language Schools.



The 2011-2012 Instructional Materials Cost Analysis (Attachment B) represents estimated costs for implementation of new adoptions, maintenance of existing instructional materials programs, purchasing Dual Enrollment materials and providing allocations for charter schools.  The estimated costs are based on projected student enrollments and anticipated program needs.  Funding is provided from carryover balance from the 2010-2011 State Categorical Allocation for Instructional Materials, the projected 2011-2012 State Categorical Allocation, and projected collections for lost/damaged materials from FY2011.



Staff development for instructional materials will be provided at no cost to the District by publishers and will be focused on the appropriate use of materials to implement the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards. 



Test items are provided through the Exam View Program for each of the adoptions.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the 2011-2012 Instructional Materials Program budget of $14,560,000 and the list of instructional materials for the District adoption periods indicated.
