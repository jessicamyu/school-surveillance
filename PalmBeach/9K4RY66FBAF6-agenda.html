RE-1 New Suncoast High School-Thomas, Virginia & Mary Poindexter Eminent Domain Settlements
Meeting
Mar 12, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Broadmoor and Real Estate Items
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action


  • The eminent domain suit was filed to acquire a number of parcels necessary for the New Suncoast High School in Riviera Beach.


  • The 0.34 acre parcel and the 0.17 acre parcels are each improved with a single-family home (See Location Map attached as Exhibit "A" and "A-1").


  • The School Board has already taken title to the parcels via the "Quick Take" process provided for under the Florida Statutes.


  • The District's latest appraisals of the parcels is $590,000.00.  The owners originally demanded $755,000.00 but have agreed to settle for $714,000.00 plus attorney's fees and expert witness costs ($7,000.00).


  • Settlement is recommended as being in the best financial interest of the District due to the anticipated costs of the litigation and litigation risk inherent in such an eminent domain proceeding.


  • In the event that the Board rejects the proposed settlement, the Board and owner will be forced to prepare this matter for a jury trail, incurring additional attorney's fees and costs on both sides that the Board will ultimately be responsible for.


  • Title costs: Not to exceed $4,000.00.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Stipulated Final Judgment between Thomas, Virginia and Mary Poindexter and the School Board of Palm Beach County in the case of School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida v. Parson Enterprises, Inc. et al. regarding the acquisition of certain real properties of 0.51 acres, more or less; and authorize the School Board attorney to execute the related documents.

RE-2 Broadmoor Relocation Assistance Program's Consultant Agreement
Meeting
Mar 12, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Broadmoor and Real Estate Items
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action


  • On April 11, 2007 the School Board approved the acquisition of the 182 unit apartment complex located at 1805 West Blue Heron Boulevard., Riviera Beach, containing 8.37 acres.


  • In order to meet the construction timeline the 182 units must be vacated by March 31, 2008.


  • On January 14, 2008 the Board and the "consultant" entered into an Agreement under which the consultant agreed to provide certain services to facilitate the relocation and identification of replacement housing for tenants residing at the Broadmoor Apartments.


  • The agreement expires on March 24, 2008 and needs to be extended to April 30, 2008 in order to assure the replacement of all tenants.


  • One hundred and ten tenants out of the 182 units have relocated and thirty more units should be vacated within the next two weeks.


  • The consultant is working with the remaining 42 tenants in order to identify replacement housing and facilitate their relocation by March 31, 2008 either to a permanent or temporary housing facility.


  • The School Board shall pay the consultant the maximum sum of $15,000.00 for a maximum of 300 hours.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the First Amendment to the Consultant Agreement between Pamela R. Williams ("Consultant") and the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida ("Board"); and authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to sign all necessary documents.
