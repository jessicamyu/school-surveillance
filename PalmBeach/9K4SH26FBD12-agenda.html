The Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA) of 2004, a mandated Federal Law, requires that the School District provide services to private school students with disabilities via a set-aside of a proportionate share of the IDEA grant.  The proportionate share amount is determined by the Florida Department of Education.



In order to comply with this requirement the School District provides services as outlined in the attached Standard Cooperative Agreement.  Services are provided to students with disabilities based on the Services Plans written by the District.  357 students accessed services during the 2010-2011 school year.



To provide these services at the private school site, a cooperative agreement must be entered into between the school and the District.



The two attached agreements are in addition to the  44 agreements previously approved by the School Board on August 25, 2010 and August 17, 2011.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the attached Standard Cooperative Agreements and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to these agreements.  These agreements shall be in effect from August 22, 2011 through June 30, 2013.  The agreements are renewed yearly for the two year period, if both parties agree.

There is no additional financial impact.  The funding to support the services at these two schools is embedded in the existing mandated set-aside.

Requirements




Florida Statute 1001.452 and School Board Policy 2.09 require each school to annually:






  • Establish the membership of their School Advisory Council (SAC)

  • Have their SAC review and adopt SAC Bylaws

  • Complete a School Improvement Plan (SIP), with their SAC's assistance.


The link below contains the documents associated with these requirements for each of our schools:





http://www.palmbeachschools.org/SchoolImprovement





In addition, School Board Policy 2.09 requires the Superintendent to submit the membership list of each school's SAC to the School Board for review.





Compliance





Area Superintendents have submitted School Improvement Plans and School Advisory Council Bylaws Confirmation Receipt (PBSD 2250) for their schools.  By submitting this Receipt, Area Superintendents have confirmed that each of their schools has:






  • completed a School Improvement Plan Checklist (PBSD 2248),  which includes an affirmation that SAC membership is in compliance with Florida Statute 1001.452 and School Board Policy 2.09;

  • adopted SAC Bylaws; and

  • developed a School Improvement Plan.



Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board accept and approve the School Improvement Plans as developed by the School Advisory Council at each school and accept the submission of each school's SAC Membership list.

On August 1, 2011, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from iGeneration Empowerment Academy – Riviera Beach to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.



The mission of iGeneration Empowerment Academy – Riviera Beach is, “to provide a standards-based, rigorous online curriculum to students, coupled with site-based instruction in a unique, campus-based, learning studio.  The School will be distinct from other full-time online programs available in Florida by offering a blend of both online and face-to-face, or campus-based, learning.  The School’s mission is also to empower students to develop a customized road map of their personal, educational, career and lifelong goals, and to help them achieve a foundation of success for the rest of their journey.”



Its application was written utilizing the Model Florida Charter School Application (see Exhibit “A”).  Thereafter, designated School District of Palm Beach County (“District”) reviewers evaluated their application using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "B"). 



The District’s staff reviewers of charter school applications were trained how to evaluate the Model Florida Charter School Application utilizing the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument on May 16, 2011 (see Exhibit “C”).



The District’s charter application process was explained to applicants during the New Applicants’ Workshop held on May 27, 2011 (see Exhibit “D”).  The applicant was invited but did not attend.



A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

The Educational Program Design at iGeneration Empowerment Academy - Riviera Beach is Traditional (Blended – using online and face to face services), similar to that offered at other high schools in Palm Beach County.

IV.            Applicant’s Founding Governing Board



This school is not a replication of any existing schools and, therefore, there is no data to share about the school or founders.  The Founding Board members are not local.  The School’s governing board will appoint a representative to facilitate parental involvement, provide access to information, assist parents and others with questions and concerns, and resolve disputes.  The representative will reside in the school district in which the charter school is located and may be a governing board member, charter school employee, or individual contracted to represent the governing board.



V.           District’s Reviewers Evaluation Ratings of  iGeneration Empowerment Academy – Riviera Beach’s Application



Designated District staff reviewed the 19 sections of iGeneration Empowerment Academy – Riviera Beach’s application that were uploaded for the Board Meeting for December 7, 2011.  Each section of the Evaluation Instrument received one of the following ratings:  Meets the Standard, Partially Meets the Standard, or Does Not Meet the Standard. 



There were two evaluations of this application.  After the first evaluation, iGeneration Empowerment Academy - Riviera Beach had 8 of 19 sections that received a rating of (Meets the Standard).



Eight sections received a rating of (Partially Meets the Standard):





  • Section 3 -       Educational Program Design;

  • Section 5 -       Assessment;

  • Section 7 -       English Language Learners;

  • Section 8 -       School Climate and Discipline;

  • Section 15 -     Transportation;

  • Section 16 -     Food Service;

  • Section 17 -     Budget;

  • Section 19 -     Action Plan.


 Three sections received a rating of (Does Not Meet the Standard):



 





  • Section 9 –       Governance;

  • Section 11 –     Education Service Providers;

  • Section 18 –     Financial Management and Oversight.  


 Pursuant to F.S.1002.33(6),  “Before approving or denying any application, the sponsor shall allow the applicant, upon receipt of written notification, at least 7 calendar days to make technical or non-substantive corrections and clarifications, including, but not limited to, corrections of grammatical, typographical, and like errors or missing signatures, if such errors are identified by the sponsor as cause to deny the application”.  The letter and 19 sections were emailed to the applicant group on September 29, 2011 and sent certified mail on September 29, 2011. (See Exhibit “E”) 



 



iGeneration Empowerment Academy - Riviera Beach then submitted additional information that cured the concerns that were outlined in Exhibit E for Section 3 – Educational Program Design; Section 5 – Assessment; Section 7 – English Language Learners; Section 8 – School Climate and Discipline; Section 9 – Governance Section 11 – Education Service Providers; Section 15 – Transportation; Section 16 – Food Service;  Section 18 – Financial Management and Oversight; and Section 19 – Action Plan. 



After the second evaluation, one section received a rating of “Partially Meets the Standard”:





  • Section 17 – Budget.  (See Exhibit “F”)


This one section can be cured during contract negotiations.

Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument



 Each section presents criteria for a response that meets the standard and these criteria should guide the overall rating for the section.  The Strengths and Weaknesses boxes provide space to identify data and other evidence that supports the rating.  The rationale for each rating is important, especially if some of the data or evidence does not fit neatly into the criteria provided.



The following definitions should guide the ratings:



Meets the Standard:                                        The response reflects a thorough understanding of key issues and demonstrates capacity to open and operate a quality charter school.  It addresses the topic with specific and accurate information that shows thorough preparation and presents a clear, realistic picture of how the school expects to operate.



Partially Meets the Standard:                         The response addresses most of the criteria, but the responses lack meaningful detail and require important additional information.



Does Not Meet the Standard:                          The response lacks meaningful detail; demonstrates lack of preparation; or otherwise raises substantial concerns about the applicant’s understanding of the issue in concept and/or ability to meet the requirement in practice.
