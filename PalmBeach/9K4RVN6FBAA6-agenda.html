Subject
Cooperative Agreement with The Urban League of Palm Beach County, Weekday Warriors Program
Meeting
Dec 5, 2007 - SM re Supplemental Services
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

The focus of the Weekday Warriors Program (WWP) is to raise FCAT scores of grade 3 students through tutoring activities. 



The program employs three (3) certified teachers who are employed by the School District.  Students are grouped at a 12:1 ratio and the curriculum used is the Comprehensive After School Program (CAP).



Tutorial services are offered at three (3) locations:  Gaines park, Coleman Park, and Orthodox Zion Primitive Baptist Church on Monday through Wednesday from 3:30 to 5:30 p.m.  Currently, there are 36 grade 3 students enrolled in the program.  Students are recruited from community schools.



The School District will provide $24,000 to the Urban League to support implementation of the WWP.  Funding will be used for the salaries of the teachers and site coordinators as well as to purchase supplies.



Success of the WWP will be evaluated based on student performance on district-wide and state-wide assessments.



A copy of the Agreement is attached.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the agreement with the Urban League of Palm Beach County, Inc. in the amount of $24,000 for implementation of the Weekday Warriors Program effective December 6, 2007 through May 31, 2008.

Contact:

Ann Killets (akillets@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Janice S. Cover

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the General Fund Budget is $24,000.  The source of the funds is the Quality Assurance budget.

The focus of the C.L.A.S.S. program is to improve academic skills and raise FCAT scores through tutoring activities.



The eight (8) teachers who are employed by C.L.A.S.S. provide services Monday through Friday from 3:30 to 7:45 p.m.



The School District will provide $63,000 to C.L.A.S.S. to support implementation of the program.  Funding will be used for the salaries of the program teachers.



Success of the C.L.A.S.S. program will be evaluated based on student performance on district-wide and state-wide assessments.



A copy of the Agreement is attached.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Agreement with Class of Palm Beach County, Inc.,in the amount of $63,000 for implementation of the tutorial program effective December 6, 2007 through May 31, 2008.
