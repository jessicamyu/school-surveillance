Read-n-Run Marathon Mile
Meeting
Dec 17, 2008 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action
The Read-n-Run Marathon Mile is an exciting and challenging school-based activity in conjunction with the Marathon of the Palm Beaches.  Three thousand (3,000) elementary and middle school students ran the first 25 miles of the marathon from the start of school to December 5th. They also read 15 minutes extra every day. Students ran the final 1.2 miles at the actual site of the Marathon of  the Palm Beaches on December 6th.  Seventy (70) schools took part in the Read-n-Run Marathon with the top two schools with the largest participation receiving a $1,000 and $500.00 check for their Physical Education department.  South Olive Elementary with 175 students running was awarded the first prize money and Royal Palm Beach Elementary won the second prize money.
The students of Palm Beach have made a commitment to Read-n-Run.  Our students have read an additional 15,000 books, totaling 4,500,00 minutes and have ran over 80,000 miles.  This means the students of Palm Beach County ran around the world three and one quarter times.  This year the Scholastic book company handed out a book to every student that crossed the finish line.

Dolores Santiago, a  teacher at Okeeheelee Middle School, has been named the “Hispanic Teacher of the Year.”  Ms. Santiago is of Cuban descent and was born and raised in Belle Glade.  She  has been teaching in Palm Beach County public school since 1995 and considers herself to be a catalyst for innovative thinking. The Hispanic Teacher of the Year Awards event is sponsored by the Hispanic Human Resources Council, (HHRC) Inc. a non-profit community based organization, established in 1977 to address the social needs of the community and to improve cultural awareness and understanding among the residents of Palm Beach County. Each school year, HHRC and the School District of Palm Beach County collaborate to recognize contributions made by public school teachers of Hispanic descent.
