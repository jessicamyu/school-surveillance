CS1 Pivot Charter School - Extension of Deferred Opening
Meeting
Apr 29, 2015 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the additional one (1) year deferral of opening of Pivot Charter School, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Pivot Charter School, Inc. to open Pivot Charter School in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on October 2, 2013, as Item CS3 during a Special Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The Charter Contract was approved by the Palm Beach County School Board on September 10, 2014.

 

The Charter Contract covers a term of five (5) years, commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, except as provided in the charter.  Pivot Charter School decided to use the first year of the contract as a planning year; and projected opening in August, 2015.

 

Pivot Charter School has requested an additional year of deferred opening in order to accommodate facility needs.

 

Pivot Charter School will serve students in grades K-8.

 

Pivot Charter School’s mission is, “To graduate middle and high school students through a unique, technology rich learning environment.  Through our blended learning education model, Pivot students will evolve into autonomous learners and excel in their goals to achieve post-secondary options.”

 

Attached are copies of the full Contract, Budget, Legal Checklist, Board Notification and Matrix for April 29, 2015, Agenda Items.

 

CONTACT:

Keith Oswald, Interim Chief Academic Officer (keith.oswald@palmbeachschools.org) 

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.

James T. Pegg

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

If the Board approves the additional year of deferral, there may be a loss of revenue to the General Fund Operating Budget of approximately $2.0 million up to $2.5 million per year in years three through five.

I recommend the School Board approve the First Amendment to the Charter Contract between the School Board of Palm Beach County and Somerset Academy Canyons High School, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On October 3, 2012, Somerset Academy Canyons High School was granted a five (5) year Charter Contract to serve students in grades 9-12, for up to 800 students over its five (5) years of operation.  This Contract expires on June 30, 2017.

 

Somerset Canyons High School wishes to expand to increase its enrollment (see attached Governing Board letter) over the remaining contract years from 800 students to 1200 students as follows:

 

Years 3 through 5:  Grades 9 through 12 – up to 1200 students.  (Note, this is for years 3-5 in the current Charter and does not extend the term of the Charter.)

 

The School District staff has reviewed information submitted by Somerset Canyons High School as required by School Board Policy 2.57, paragraph 6, to justify the increase in school enrollment, and are satisfied that the requirements have been met.

 

The School Board seeks to revise the enrollment capacity provision and some other provisions now found in the State’s standard charter school contract or consistent with changes in the law.

 

The School Board is now required through a Resolution Agreement with the U.S. Department of Justice to include provisions in charter contracts relating to the agreement.

 

Attached are copies of the First Amendment to Charter Contract, Charter Contract, Legal Checklist, Board Notification, and Matrix for April 29, 2015 Board Agenda Items.
