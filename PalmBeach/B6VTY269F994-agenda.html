I recommend the Board approve the First Amendment to the Charter Contract with Everglades Preparatory Academy, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 12, 2017, Everglades Preparatory Academy, Inc. was granted a ten (10) year Charter Contract to serve At-Risk high school students in grades 9-12, commencing July 1, 2017 and ending on June 30, 2027.

 

As an exception to grades 9-12, the Everglades Preparatory Academy wishes to amend their contract to serve as an 8.5 Placement Option (as defined in the District’s Student Progression Plan), when available and appropriate.

 

The School District of Palm Beach County has investigated Everglades Preparatory Academy’s request to expand the enrollment, reviewed its proposed budget and District staff is satisfied that Everglades Preparatory Academy would be capable to meet the needs of the increased enrollment due to the addition of 8th grade students.

 

All other terms of the Amended Charter shall remain in full force and effect.

 

The mission of the high school is “to provide a meaningful alternative education high school to promote opportunities in an alternative setting for preparation for future career or college experiences.  This school will assist every student enrolled to reach high levels of achievement and competence in all subjects and skills essential to success in school, life, and work.”

DESCRIPTION:

  1. The FY 2019-2023 Five Year Plan includes the modernization of Verde Elementary and Addison Mizner Elementary. In order to facilitate the modernization projects, the School District will need to construct a temporary holding school facility to accommodate each school during construction at the existing elementary campuses.
     
  2. The City of Boca Raton owns approximately 44 acres near Don Estridge High Tech Middle School commonly referred to as “Countess de Hoernle Park Property”. The City has made +/- 11.92 acres of vacant land available to allow for the construction of temporary elementary school facilities including portable classrooms, parking lot and other facilities related to the construction and operation for the holding school. (See Exhibits “A” and “B”)
     
  3. The term of this lease shall be for four (4) years and 364 days and rent shall be in the amount of $1.00 per year.
     
  4. School Board’s obligation under this lease shall be contingent upon obtaining an Environmental Audit 30 days following the commencement date of the Lease.
     
  5. Access to the premises shall occur from Military Trail via Banyan Trail or another mutually agreeable route. The School Board shall be responsible for the construction of any road improvements deemed necessary to access the premises.
     
  6. The School Board will provide City access to existing City wells located adjacent to the leased premises, access to areas utilized by the City for existing pipelines located on the leased property as well as to the driveway between Military Trail and the proposed temporary school parking area.
     
  7. The School Board shall be the permitting authority for all improvements associated with construction of the facilities on the leased premises. The School Board is responsible for removing any improvements that the City indicates are not to remain on the premises upon expiration of the Lease.
     
  8. The City of Boca Raton approved this Lease Agreement on November 27, 2018.

Education Program Design

Rigor/Relevance + Relationships Framework— SLAM’s educational program design is grounded on the philosophy of the three R’s: Rigor, Relevance, and Relationships. The three R’s represent the essential components that must be in place to effectively prepare students for the demands of the 21st Century. Attributed to the work of Dr. Bill Daggett[1], the three R’s model has been linked by research to higher attendance and graduation rates, stronger test scores, and safer schools.

 

SLAM Academy combines the three R’s with an innovative approach to engage students.

 

SLAM Academy integrates sports themes into the Florida Standards-based curriculum to keep students engaged. This supports interdisciplinary teaching by providing a vehicle for integrating content areas in a way that makes sense to children. It helps them make connections in order to transfer knowledge they acquire and apply it meaningful ways

 

Career Academies—The secondary program (9-12) offers a career academy model that produces college-bound students. Students will choose one of the following career academies that will prepare students for majors in sports-related careers:

  • Sports Medicine
  • Sports Broadcasting and Journalism
  • Sports Marketing, Entertainment Arts and Management

SLAM’s use of career academies is based research validating that “career academies are the most durable and best-tested component of reform strategy to prepare students for college and careers.”[2]

Education Program Design

The proposed school’s educational program intends to fulfill the school’s mission of maximizing student academic achievement through an arts-integrated curriculum designed to foster the development of accountable, 21stcentury learners in a safe and enriching environment. Through a rigorous and well-rounded educational program, in accordance with the Palm Beach County Student Progression Plan and following a Florida standards aligned scope and sequence (see attached sample), students will be better prepared for success in middle and high school, and subsequently, college.

 

Accordingly, the proposed school will combine the best practices developed by this extensive network of innovative Somerset board members, parents, and educators working together for a common purpose, and those principles driving essential school reform nation-wide. The school will meet high standards of student achievement and provide a solid academic foundation that will enable students to be successful at subsequent levels. The curriculum will prioritize mastery of all applicable Florida state standards in all core subject areas, while an emphasis on arts-education will endeavor to support both the academic and socioemotional wellbeing of all students. Additionally, the school will offer a rich array of specials/electives in the arts, health, physical education, and world languages.
