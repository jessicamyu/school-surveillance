Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement Amendment with Florida Atlantic University Board of Trustees (FAU Outreach), effective January 21, 2020 through June 30, 2025; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Lake Worth High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7,000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

Payment of Instructors/Supervision for Dual Enrollment courses taught at high school sites:
-PBSC shall be reimbursed $2100 per course taught by PBSC faculty;
-FAU shall be reimbursed for the costs associated with for the costs associated with the proportion of salary and benefits of the instructor for courses taught by an FAU instructor.  For dual enrollment courses on the high school campus taught by a high school teacher, the school district is responsible for the actual costs associated with dual enrollment.  
 
The Dual Enrollment Articulation Agreement with PBSC and FAU is being submitted in accordance with Florida Statute 1007.271.
 
The format of the Agreement aligns with Florida Department of Education requirements including:
-courses and programs that are available to students eligible to participate in dual enrollment
-process by which students and parents/guardians are notified of the option to participate in dual enrollment
-eligibility criteria for student participation in dual enrollment courses and programs
-institutional responsibilities for student screening prior to enrollment and monitoring enrolled students
-criteria by which the quality of dual enrollment courses and programs are judged.
