AC3 - Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. Early Learning Coaches Program Agreement
Meeting
Oct 5, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

This Agreement with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. provides $3,021,225 to the School District for the period effective October 1, 2011, through September 30, 2012, for the continuation of the Quality Counts (QC) Early Learning Coaches program.  The coaching program is one of a number of components that work together to create a comprehensive, voluntary early care and education quality rating improvement system for Palm Beach County.  The system partners work together to provide services to enhance the quality of early education programs so that children will enter school eager and ready to learn.  The Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County and a number of stakeholders initiated the Quality Counts System.  System partners include Children’s Services Council, Palm Beach State College, Palm Beach County Early Learning Coalition, Family Central and the School District.



 



This grant funds 34 Early Learning Coaches (certified teachers) and related support/administrative staff to serve child care centers and school-based sites that voluntarily participate.  To be eligible to participate, child care centers must have been licensed for at least one year and must hold a rate agreement with Family Central.  School District classrooms offering the Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) program are participants as are those classrooms in the Village Academy Head Start program and the VPK/PreK Migrant Education partnership program.  



 



The Quality Counts coaches assist with the implementation of improvement plans and strategies developed collaboratively with classroom teachers and child care directors based on data from formal and informal assessments.  Highly trained outside assessors administer the assessments.  Multiple assessment measures are used, including but not limited to, the Early Childhood Environment Rating Scale (ECERS-R) and the Infant/Toddler Environment Rating Scale (ITERS), both nationally recognized, research-based assessment tools.  The assessments provide for the award of a “Star Rating,” ranging from 1 to 5 stars.  The lowest rating, a 1-Star rating, equates to the attainment of current state and local child care licensing standards.



 



Coaching supports are provided using a three-level, consultation approach, based on an evidence-based coaching model developed by Pat Wesley, a research scientist at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Coaching levels include:



 



Level 1- Intensive



·      Intensive coaching services are based on the number of classrooms and the results of the informal baseline assessment.



·      Onsite intensive services range from 1 day/week up to 2.5 days/week.



Level 2 – Maintenance



·      Maintenance coaching services begin after sites have a formal assessment.  The formal assessment scores are used to award a star rating.



·      Onsite maintenance services range from 2 to 3 days per month for year 1 and from 1 to 2 days per month for year 2 participants.



Level 3 – Model



·      Quarterly visits are scheduled with a child care center director and/or administrative leadership for sites that consistently maintain quality improvements.



 



·       Follow-up supports may be provided via phone and e-mail.



 



The Quality Counts System has served 113 participating private child care centers and 32 school sties.  As of October 1, 2011, additional private centers and school sites will be added to the program.  The District is both a partner and a voluntary participant and most participating school-based sites have earned the highest possible star rating.



 



Participating public schools include: Acreage Pines Elementary, Belle Glade Elementary, Benoist Farms Elementary, Berkshire Elementary, Cholee Lake Elementary, D.D. Eisenhower Elementary, Elbridge Gale Elementary, Equestrian Trails Elementary, Galaxy Elementary, Grassy Waters Elementary, Greenacres Elementary, Heritage Elementary, Highland Elementary, Hope-Centennial Elementary, Indian Pines Elementary, K.E.C./Canal Point Elementary, Lincoln Elementary, Pahokee Elementary, Palm Beach Gardens Elementary, Panther Run Elementary, Pine Grove Elementary, Roosevelt Elementary, Royal Palm School, Sandpiper Shores Elementary, Starlight Cove Elementary, U.B. Kinsey Elementary, Village Academy, West Riviera Elementary, Westward Elementary, and Wynnebrook Elementary.   Schools entering the program in October 1, 2011 include: Boynton Beach High School Early Learning Academy, Loxahatchee Groves Elementary, Manatee Elementary, North Palm Beach Elementary, Pleasant City Elementary, and Pioneer Park Elementary.



 



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in this particular case due to the late receipt of the final grant agreement, which was delayed due to protracted contract negotiations and related legal reviews.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.

I recommend that the School Board approve a contract with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. in the amount of $3,021,225 effective from October 1, 2011, until September 30, 2011, and authorize the superintendent or his designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant that are required for implementation of grant activities.

This Agreement with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. provides $245,888 to the School District for the period effective October 1, 2011 through September 30, 2012 for the continuation of the school-year and summer early childhood education component of the Bridges Beacon Program at Highland Elementary. The early education portion of the program was started in January 2009 and is contingent upon the allocation of funds from a grant award from the U.S. Department of Education’s Full Service Community School’s program (award # U215J080431 A), which was secured by the Children’s Services Council of Palm Beach County (CSC) in partnership with the Highland principal.



 



The grant funds are braided with other funding streams, such as Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) reimbursements and school readiness earnings, to operate four preschool classes and related services. The teaching teams in the preschool classes uniquely integrate dual-language (Spanish-English) practices and the use the High/Scope curricular approach.  The project includes a VPK/Title I enrichment class, a VPK/grant funded enrichment class, a preschool class for 3-year-olds, and a toddler class for 2-year-olds that opened in January 2011.



 



Objectives set for this program include:







  • Maintain a high quality rating as measured by the Quality Counts System.




  • Improve the Kindergarten readiness of the students as measured by components of the Florida Kindergarten Readiness Screener (FLKRS), reported as an annual VPK Readiness Rate score.   


The Bridges Beacon Center project was initiated by the Children’s Services Council, which administers this grant and provides “pass through funding” to the collaborative partners for the implementation of the various strategies.  Highland Elementary provides space for the early childhood classrooms, for the managers employed by the Housing Partnership for the parent engagement and mentoring programs, and for a parent resource room with computers (provided by another partner). 



 



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in this particular case due to the late receipt of the final grant agreement, which was delayed due to protracted contract negotiations and related legal reviews.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.

This Agreement with the Palm Beach County Board of County Commissioners provides $1,052,115 to the School District for the period effective October 1, 2011 through September 30, 2012 for the continuation of full-day Head Start services for 185 three- and four-year-old students and their families.  Village Academy will host the Head Start program, creating a preschool to high school continuum of aligned services for the community.  The Village Academy Principal and Department of Early Childhood Education Director will be the academic and grant program leaders and will secure parent, community and District participation.  Currently, a diverse community group under the leadership of the Children’s Services Council is engaged in an enriched community collaborative process to support this program.   



 



Child development and family support services will be provided during the school year, Monday through Friday, between the hours of 7:30 a.m. and 5:30 p.m. Families will be provided with a full complement of Head Start services, including medical, dental and family social services supports.  The four-year-old classes will also participate in the Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) program provided under the auspice of the Department of Early Childhood Education, which will provide enhanced supports and oversight.  These supports include, but are not limited to, on-site coaching, along with financial assistance and management services.  The Village Academy Head Start program will also participate in and receive supports from the Palm Beach County Quality Counts System, a quality rating and improvement system.  The program will be subject to audits from local and federal Head Start agents, VPK auditors and Quality Counts assessors.



 



Over the past four years, a majority of the Head Start graduates have participated in the District’s Summer VPK program held at Delray Full Service Center.  These students were screened annually for Kindergarten readiness using the Florida Kindergarten Readiness Screener (FLKRS) upon entry into Kindergarten.  The State Board of Education disaggregated the FLKRS scores back to the Delray Full Service Center VPK site and awarded an annual Kindergarten Readiness Rate based on the FLKRS scores.  For the past two years, the site has earned a score above the low performing provider threshold.



 



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in this particular case due to the lateness of the receipt of the contract.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.

AC2 - Transition To Teaching (TTT) Grant Program
Meeting
Oct 5, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category

Action (Consent)

On September 22, 2011, the School District of Palm Beach County was notified that we have been awarded the competitive Transition To Teaching (TTT) grant from the United States Department of Education for up to $2,027,803.00, for a five-year period from October 1, 2011, through September 30, 2016.  The fiscal year begins on October 1, 2011, but money will not be accessed until after the Board approves.This grant was written by staff in the Professional Growth Division.



The mission of the "I-TEACH" grant is to create and maintain an Alternative Certification Program for the district. All grant funds will be targeted to create a recruitment, placement, induction, and retention model that will target high-quality, career-changing professionals and recent college graduates with non-education college degrees to teach in Title 1 6-12 schools for a minimum of three years.  Special emphasis will be placed on the recruitment of individuals from groups traditionally underrepresented in Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) subjects to teach math and science, including minorities and women. Over the course of the five-year grant project, I-TEACH will provide a structured alternative certification program, consisting of high-quality professional development and intensive mentoring support to produce a minimum of 100 highly-qualified teachers who will earn their professional teaching certificate in core academic subjects within 18 months.



Through I-TEACH, the project staff will collaborate with three project partners (the Palm Beach County Workforce Alliance, the New Teacher Center, and the Scripps Research Institute of Florida) to accomplish the project's three primary goals:  1) recruit and place high-quality, career-changing professionals and recent college graduates as teachers in Title 1 secondary schools; 2) develop and expand the I-TEACH accelerated teacher preparation program for participants to earn Florida professional certification through the Alternative Certification Program (ACP) within 18 months; 3) retain and support highly-qualified project participants as teachers of record in high-needs schools for at least three years.  The TTT grant requires and funds an external evaluator who will assess quantitative and qualitative data to determine the I-TEACH project's impact on these goals.



Although the grant is effective October 1, 2011, this does not prejudice the School Board.  The total budget request is for $2,027,803.00 for five years.  The maximum award per year will be $450,000.00.  A copy of the grant application is available for review in the Board Office.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Transition To Teaching (TTT) Grant from the United States Department of Education for up to $2,027,803.00 through September 30, 2016 as well as any no cost extension as applicable.  I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

CLS1 - Waiver for Chairperson of the District Academic Advisory Committee (AAC)
Meeting

Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)
During the September 12th AAC meeting, Ms. Carla Donaldson was elected by the Academic Advisory Committee to act as Chairperson.





She would like to serve as Chariperson, but is disclosing, through this waiver, 4A Disclosure Form, that she also holds a contract with the school district as a "PAL", Parent As Liaison, for up to $6,000 for the 2011-2012 school year.





Her responsibilities as a "PAL" are to Bridge the gap between families of children with special educational needs and available supports. PALS encourage home-school relationships by facilitating positive pro-active communication between families and educators. The PALS are contracted consultants with the Department of Exceptional Student Education (ESE) and the Florida Diagnostic and Learning Resources System (FDLRS).





PAL's do not make any curricular decisions, sell products, or influence the sale of products for the district or schools, nor do they make IEP decisions for individual students.





We do not believe this contractual relationship with the District would interfere with the discharge of her duties an Academic Advisory Committee member.



Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Waiver 4A Disclosure Form of Business Transaction from Carla Donaldson for her service as a member of the Academic Advisory Committee.

Judith Klinek  Judith.klinek@palmbeachschools.org

There is no financial impact to the district.
