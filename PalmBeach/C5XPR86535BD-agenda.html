3. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Subject
ELR1 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Diane Baumann
Meeting
Sep 14, 2021 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
3. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

On or about February 2, 2021, Diane Baumann became the subject of an Employee and Labor Relations investigation based upon allegations of policy violations related to Failure to Fulfill the Responsibilities of a Teacher timely and/or with fidelity; Unprofessional communication/unethical conduct/retaliatory language with coworkers, including about a student, and/or in the presence of students;, Failure to follow established rules, procedures, and Gross Insubordination: Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive(s).

 

The allegation that Ms. Baumann’s failure to fulfill job responsibilities of a Classroom Teacher was evidenced by her unwillingness and/or inability to meet deadlines. These issues were also witnessed by an Instructional Superintendent during a joint walkthrough of Ms. Baumann’s classroom on December 7, 2020. Concerns were also expressed regarding inability to communicate professionally with multiple coworkers. Ms. Baumann received a written reprimand for insubordination and failing to complete multiple items on a year-end checklist in a timely manner with fidelity on September 19, 2020. Ms. Baumann failed to appropriately handle student records, and also failed to adhere to all directives integral and with basic requirements of teachers and received a written reprimand on January 30, 2020.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following: School Board Policies 2.34, Records and Reports; 3.02(2) (4)(a),(b),(f),(h), and (j), Code of Ethics; 1.013, (1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; Florida Administrative Code, Rule 6A-10.081(1), and (2)(a)(1), Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida; Florida Statute 1008.22(1), Student Assessment.

 

There is “just cause” to support Diane Baumann’s termination of employment.

 

On August 30, 2021, the Superintendent’s “Notice of Recommendation for Termination of Employment” was sent Via Email and sent Via Regular US and Certified Mail to Diane Baumann, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve and adopt the FY 2022 through 2031 Capital Plan.

DESCRIPTION:

FY 2022 Capital Plan

  • Each year, the School Board is required to adopt a five-year capital plan prior to the adoption of the budget.  The District has expanded the plan to cover ten years so that all referendum projects can be included.
     
  • The first year of the plan is the FY 2022 capital budget which is included the budget that will be presented for approval later today.
     
  • As required by law, the capital plan is balanced.
     
  • The School Board last reviewed the capital plan on August 4, 2021.  The only change are related to final adjustments for FY 2021 including accrual of sales tax and impact fee revenues.  
     
  • The projects planned for FY 2023 through FY 2031 (years 2 through 10) can and will be amended as needed during the budget process each year.  Any future changes that impact projects on the referendum project list will be reviewed by ISSOC.
     
  • The capital plan was reviewed by the Construction Oversight and Review Committee on August 11, 2021 the Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee on September 3, 2021 and the Finance Committee on September 10, 2021.
     
  • The Tentative District Education Facilities Plan, which is the state version of the capital plan, will be brought to the School Board at a future meeting.
