Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the receipt of the Wallace Foundation Partnerships for Social and Emotional Learning Initiative Grant in partnership with Prime Time Palm Beach County, effective September 7, 2017 through August 31, 2021; approve any no-cost extension as applicable; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all Grant Agreements and Appendices, documents, Data Sharing Agreements, and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION: 

The School District and Prime Time Palm Beach County, have been awarded a Wallace Foundation Partnerships for Social and Emotional Learning Initiatives grant. The purpose of this grant is to foster children's social and emotional learning (SEL) through partnerships between school districts and out-of-school time intermediaries (OSTIs), Wallace also seeks to generate credible, useful knowledge and has commissioned independent research to develop practical knowledge about how these efforts can be carried out, the role of adult skills development, and the benefits to children.

Seven pilot sites have been selected to conduct the initiative at the elementary school and in their out of school programs: Crosspointe, Diamond View, Egret Lakes, Forest Hill, Lantana, Pahokee, and West Gate Elementary Schools. Comparison schools have also been selected which will receive compensation throughout the project and programming at the end of the four year grant period. The Action Plan developed during the Planning grant period will be implemented and will include:

  • Professional Development on SEL for pilot schools as well as other schools and district leadership and staff.
  • Extensive support and coaching for program sites.
  • Participation in Wallace research with RAND Corporation and Mathematica.
  • Implementation of SEL practices, including Morning Meetings, and approved SEL curriculum.
  • Continued advisory Core Team and partnership development that includes: the District, Prime Time, Palm Beach County Birth to 22, and other community groups and representatives.
  • Professional Learning Communities between the schools and between other grant awardees across the country.
  • A coordinated Communications Plan.
