Special Meeting re: Request for Authorization to File Suit
Meeting
Jul 8, 2009 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action
The District Auditor issued a report, a copy of which was previously provided to the Board, which concluded that School Board Policies were not followed.  As a result revenues from leasing fees or fund raising activities were not received by the School Board or properly documented in accordance with School Board Policies.  Mr. Bloom and his companies were allowed to use school facilities without paying a leasing fee, or operated a personal business on school property in violation of School Board Policy 2.32.  Florida Statute 1001.41(4) provides that the School Board has the power to sue.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board authorize the Chief Counsel to file suit against those parties who failed to pay leasing fees as required by School Board Policy 7.18 or failed to properly account for fund raising proceeds in accordance with School Board Policies.

Contact:

Gerald A. Williams, Esq.; Chief Counsel williamsg@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



Robert A. Glassman, Esq.; Senior Counsel

Financial Impact:

A judgment in favor of the School Board, if successful should exceed the litigation expenses.  The exact amount cannot be determined at this time.

I recommend that the School Board authorize the Chief Counsel to file suit against those parties who failed to pay leasing fees as required by School Board Policy 7.18 or failed to properly account for fund raising proceeds in accordance with School Board Policies.

Special Meeting re: Waiver of Conflict - Representing Attorney
Meeting
Jul 8, 2009 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action

This claim (#936985) involves allegations of diverse wrong doing on the part of a District employee which has not been substantiated in any investigation to date and appears groundless. 



 



Eugene Pettis, of the Law Firm Haliczer Pettis, has been hired to defend the School Board and the employee in this matter.



 

Absent approval of this recommendation, the District would be obligated to pay for or reimburse the employee for legal defense, which would be expensive.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board waive the potential conflict of interest of the attorney representing both the School Board and the employee in the litigated case of Jane Doe vs. The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida.

Contact:

Gerald A. Williams, Esq.; Chief Counsel williamsg@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



Dianne Howard - EBRM Director



Robert A. Glassman, Esq.; Senior Counsel

Financial Impact:

There is a potenial for cost savings as additional counsel will not need to be retained.
