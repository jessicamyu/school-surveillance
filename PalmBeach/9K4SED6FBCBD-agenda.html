PA4 Seminole Ridge Construction Academy Habitat House
Meeting
Dec 12, 2012 - School Board Meeting 12-12-12
Category
Presentations
Type
Discussion
The proud students of the Seminole Ridge Construction Academy have completed their first modular house for Habitat for Humanity - Palm Beach County, and it now rests on the property in the Westgate neighborhood of West Palm Beach.  This unique teaching program has trained 170 or so Construction Academy students in the skills of carpentry, insulation, electrical, plumbing, cabinetry, drywall, painting, windows and doors, weatherproofing, building structure, building codes, and construction job safety.  





The students began construction of the three bedroom, two bath, 1206 SF one-story house in August of 2011. The program had budgeted 18 months for construction of this first house, knowing there would be a learning curve for everyone involved.  The students beat that schedule and the house rolled out of the shop 15 months after it began.





The project was a joint venture between the School District and Habitat for Humanity of Palm Beach County. Habitat provided the property, all site-built elements, and all materials that the students would need for the modular components and roof sections.  The students were trained by their teacher, Rick Terkovich, and a team of volunteer subcontractors and tradespersons who came into the shop to show the students how to frame the floors, walls, and ceilings, how to install electrical wiring and plumbing, and how to install and finish drywall.  The students, under supervision of the project's mentors led by architect David Porter, then did all of the work on the house, all the way through the final electrical and plumbing connections and painting. Habitat will install the exterior siding, roof shingles, finished flooring, and tie the electrical and plumbing together from the separate modules.





Two other schools were also brought into the project.  The Royal Palm Beach High School Air Conditioning Academy students will be installing the AC ductwork and equipment. William T. Dwyer High School Construction Academy students have been at the property building the attached shed and the front porch.


On November 13th, the house and roof sections were pushed out of the shop by the students and were craned onto seven awaiting tractor-trailers.  Not a single part moved out of place.  On November 14th, the tractor-trailers hauled the components to Westgate and one-by-one, they were lifted onto the awaiting foundation.  Four house modules and six roof sections later, as the sun started to set, the individual house components built by Seminole Ridge had become a home, completely dried-in and ready for Habitat's, Royal Palms, and Dwyers completion.





The proud owner, Tina Cromatie and her family, will get to move into this one-of-a-kind home right after the first of the year.

IN2 Purchase of Property Insurance

The District currently has  property insurance covering $5.72 billion in  Total Insured Values (TIV) with a maximum of $60 million in catastrophic property insurance at a cost of approximately $7.47 million.



 



The District also has a $10 million first layer deductible for all perils other than wind storm.  In addition, the District also had a named windstorm deductible of 5% of values with a $500,000 minimum at each location with an additional $10 million deductible.  This additional $10 million deductible applies only  once a year.



 



Market capacity continues to be an issue for our estimated loss limit and premium cost.  The District’s property is concentrated in one centralized area (the County) which is considered mostly coastal.  Initial review of wind modeling results reflects similar loss scenarios when compared to last year.  This modeling places the District’s probable maximum loss (PML) at $480 million for a 100-year storm event.  Wind mitigation factors were gathered from 28 campuses (248 buildings) that modeled poorly last year to see if gathering additional construction details will improve loss projections.  At the time of submittal of this item, the report with those details had just been received, with re-modeling of our properties with these details to occur in the next few weeks.



 



The District brokers have contacted numerous insurers/reinsurers and are assembling a consortium of carriers in order to cover our property insurance renewal.  Similar to last year, the team has had to bring in new insurers to replace current insurers who will no longer participate in or who have reduced their participation in the current program.  The actual layer program and pricing is being finalized. Unfortunately, we are receiving slower commitments due to Hurricane Sandy which shut down NYC carrier offices.



 



The goal this year is to reduce premium slightly (5% or less) in hopes that higher loss limits can be purchased.  In 2010/2011 policy year, the District had $100 million in limits, and had sought similar coverage limits this past year, with no success due to pricing.  Compliance with the provisions of our COPS (Certificates of Participation) and FEMA eligibility (The Stafford Act) both mandate that we purchase insurance coverage to the extent it is reasonably and affordably available. 



 



Our recommendation is to allow us to approve coverage with premiums and fees not to exceed $8 million.  We anticipate this will provide enough premium dollars to purchase between $ 65 and $75 million in coverage limits.  The District would rely on FEMA for damages in excess of this amount.  The total package will be finalized before the Winter Break holidays. 



 



The District has previously requested and received confirmation from the State Office of Insurance Regulation and State Office of Emergency Management through the Reasonableness Letter that our property insurance program complies with the Stafford Act requirements from FEMA.  The Risk Management Department will again request this confirmation from the state once the property insurance program is finalized.



 



Beacon Group will be compensated at a 4% commission rate to a maximum cap of $290,000 for their services, including  the services of any wholesaler/intermediary used to place the District’s property insurance program.



Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the purchase of property insurance through our broker, The Beacon Group, Inc., with probable catastrophic limits of between $65 and $75 million effective December 31, 2012 at a premium not to exceed $8 million.

Dianne L. Howard, Director, Risk and Benefits Management (dianne.howard@palmbeachschools.org)

The financial impact to the District is not to exceed $8,000,000.  The source of the funds is a transfer from the Capital Maintenance Funds budget to the General Funds budget.

·         Emergency Make-Up Days (EMDs) will be added to this Calendar as determined by the Superintendent and his leadership team.



·         Elementary and Secondary Report Card distribution dates will be added to this Calendar after receiving input from the FTE And Student Reporting Office.
