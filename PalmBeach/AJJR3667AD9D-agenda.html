Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the amended Principal Autonomy Pilot Program Initiative application.

DESCRIPTION:

The Principal Autonomy Pilot Program Initiative (PAPPI) criteria requires the District to identify three schools that received at least two school grades of "D" or "F" during the previous three school years along with three principals who have earned a highly effective rating on their prior year performance evaluation.  This amendment replaces West Riviera Elementary with Lincoln Elementary.  Lincoln will join Orchard View Elementary and Lake Shore Middle as the three District schools participating in this pilot program.  This is the only change to the original application appproved by the School Board on December 14, 2016 (agenda item B2).

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the attached Tentative Agreement with the Association of Educational Secretaries and Office Professionals (AESOP) to be effective January 1, 2017 through December 31, 2019, pending ratification by AESOP.

DESCRIPTION:

The Tentative Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and the Association of Educational Secretaries and Office Professionals (AESOP) provides the following:

  1. Granting all eligible bargaining unit members a three percent (3%) salary increase, effective January 1, 2017.
  2. Also effective January 1, 2017, the minimum salaries of all salary levels are increased by two percent (2%) and maximum salaries of all salary levels are increased by two percent (2%).
  3. Authorizing the upgrade of the Instructional Technical Support Assistant position from Pay Level 10 to Pay Level 11 with a minimum increase of 5% in lieu of the 3% increase.
  4. Agreeing to review the job descriptions for Treasurer I and Treasurer II to ensure alignment with the job functions performed by a majority of the employees currently serving in those positions.

Subject
PIR1 Boundary Study C-1 Hybrid, as Amended January 18, 2017, for Calusa Elementary Relief and Establish a Choice Priority SAC Area for Morikami Park Elementary
Meeting
Feb 22, 2017 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
9. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Commencing the 2017-2018 School Year:
I recommend the Board approve adoption of Boundary Study C-1-Hybrid, as Amended on January 18, 2017, Calusa Elementary Relief, with current 4th grade students attending the sending elementary schools, having the option to stay for 5th grade, and currently enrolled K-3 siblings of 4th grade students having the option to stay for the remainder of their elementary years. Siblings will be reviewed and processed through the Choice Department (reassignments). For students whose attendance zones are impacted by this boundary study, but who are allowed and opt to stay at their current school, transportation will be the responsibility of the parent. I further recommend the Board approve adoption of adding SAC 296C as a Choice Priority SAC area for Morikami Park Elementary School.

DESCRIPTION:

Changes in students attendance boundaries for six (6) Elementary Schools - Calusa, Addison Mizner, Boca Raton, J.C. Mitchell, Sunrise Park and Whispering Pines and adding SAC 296C as a Choice Priority SAC area for Morikami Park.

 

On January 18, 2017, the School Board and the Advisory Boundary Committee held a public workshop relating to Boundary Study C-1 Hybrid Amended Calusa Relief.

 

On January 18, 2017, the School Board voted to amend Study C-1 Hybrid by removing SAC 296C (Casa Bella & Addison Reserve communities) from the Boundary Study C-1 Hybrid Calusa Relief recommendation. Study C-1 Hybrid Amended Calusa Relief recommendation was then approved by the Board for development and accepted by the Superintendent.

 

The boundary study proposal C-1 Hybrid as Amended January 18, 2017, and Supporting Documentation, including the study, maps and legal descriptions, are provided as attachments.

 

Previously, the Advisory Boundary Committee (ABC) had recommended Boundary Study C-1 Hybrid by a 7-4 vote for the 2017 - 2018 Boundary Cycle for Capacity Relief of Calusa Elementary, pursuant to Policy 5.01 on Student Assignment.

 

The ABC Committee voted to present the following Boundary Change Proposal to the Superintendent:

 

1. PIR-1 - Boundary Study C-1 Hybrid (Calusa to Whispering Pines Elementary, Whispering Pines to Sunrise Park Elementary, Calusa to J.C. Mitchell Elementary, J.C. Mitchell to Boca Raton Elementary) was voted on December 13, 2016 by the ABC to be recommended to the Superintendent.

 

The study approved by the ABC specified that current 4th grade students and their current K-3 grade siblings would be permitted to opt-in to remain at their current SY16-17 schools. ABC's proposed change provided relief to Calusa Elementary, provided additional enrollment at other schools, and moved the following SAC's.

  • SACs 296C and 317(Portion) from Calusa Elementary to Whispering Pines Elementary
  • SAC 311D from Whispering Pines Elementary to Sunrise Park Elementary
  • SACs 315 and 316A from Calusa Elementary to J.C. Mitchell Elementary
  • SAC 335 from J.C. Mitchell Elementary to Addison Mizner Elementary
  • SAC 336A from J.C. Mitchell Elementary to Boca Raton Elementary

The four dissenting ABC votes were due to concerns that the study does not provide substantial enough enrollment relief to Calusa Elementary.

 

On December 16, 2016, the Hybrid study and other studies in this boundary cycle were shared with the Diversity and Equity Committee. The Committee had no specific comments regarding the proposed studies. However, there was general discussion about concerns that for Calusa to Whispering Pines Elementary, Whispering Pines to Sunrise Park Elementary, parents in disadvantaged socioeconomic groups may not have the time and means to participate in the ABC and Community Input Meetings.

 

At the January 18, 2017, School Board Development Meeting, the Superintendent had recommended to the Board Boundary Study C-1 Hybrid as well as adding SAC 296C as a Choice Priority SAC area for Morikami Park. Within SAC 296C are the Casa Bella & Addison Reserve communities, which did not exist when nearby SACs were created as Choice Priority SAC areas for Morikami Park. SACs 296B, 306A & 306B are already priority SACs for Morikami Park. These are SACs surrounding SAC 296C. At the time these other SACs were deemed priority for Morikami Park there was no development in SAC 296C which is why that SAC was not included in the priority designation at that time.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Second Amendment to the Superintendent's Employment Contract as submitted and authorize the Chairman to execute on behalf of the Board.

DESCRIPTION:

The following changes are recommended to the Superintendent's Employment Contract:

 

The term of the Superintendent's Employment Agreement is being extended until June 30, 2021 and in future years will be automatically extended on a five-year rolling basis provided all of the following conditions are met: (i) Superintendent receives at least an “Effective” rating on his annual review by BOARD; (ii) neither party gives notice that the automatic one-year extension for that year will not occur; and (iii) the Employment Agreement has not been terminated.  A decision by the Board to not extend the Agreement pursuant to condition (ii) requires a super-majority vote of a minimum of five Board members.

 

Effective July 1, 2017, the Superintendent's salary is increased as follows: (i) if Superintendent is rated as “Highly Effective” Superintendent shall receive the same percentage salary increase as administrative employees, retroactive to July 1; (ii) if Superintendent is rated as “Effective”, Superintendent shall receive a salary increase equal to one percentage point less than provided to administrative employees, retroactive to July 1; and (iii) if Superintendent does not achieve a rating of “Highly Effective” or “Effective” on his annual review by BOARD for that year, or if BOARD does not provide a salary increase to 12-month administrative employees of the District during a particular year or years, Superintendent shall receive no salary increase.
