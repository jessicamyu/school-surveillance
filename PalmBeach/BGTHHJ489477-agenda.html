Meeting
Nov 20, 2019 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Type
Presentation, Recognition

DESCRIPTION:

Emily Cody is a Food Service Production Assistant at L.C. Swain Middle School. On September 20, 2019, she was covering in the manager's absence when she noticed that one of her co-workers Silvana Messina began to collapse. Emily ran over to her and realized that Silvana was choking.

 

Emily proceeded to do the Heimlich Maneuver and her efforts paid off, as doing the Maneuver dislodged the piece of gum Silvana was choking on. Emily maintained her composure throughout the entire ordeal, and was able to stabilize the work atmosphere while they were short-staffed. Emily was previously trained in CPR and the Heimlich.

I recommend the Board approve the Real Estate and Purchase Contract for acquisition of an improved parcel of land containing +/- 1.17 acres, located at 520 Toney Penna Drive in Jupiter, Florida, adjacent to Jupiter Community High School; and authorize the Board Chair and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

1.  The improved property located at 520 Toney Penna Dr., Jupiter +/- 1.17 acres is adjacent to Jupiter Community High School. (See Map)            

 

2.  The availability of this property provides the School District with the opportunity to accommodate growth and serve schools in the area. 

 

3.  School District staff plans to use the building, with minor retrofitting, as a regional police sub-station.  The location is ideal given its proximity to multiple Jupiter schools as well as the Town of Jupiter's new Emergency Operations Center. 

 

4.  School Police has significantly increased staff from approximately 150 to over 300 officers to ensure school security remains a top priority.  The additional hires require additional command and facility capacity.

 

5.  School Police Leadership has recommended a regional substation approach, given the geographic and population size of the District. Funding for Police Substations is reflected in the current Capital plan approved by the Board on September 11, 2019.

 

6.  Consistent with F.S. 1013.14, two appraisals were conducted and the average appraised value is $1,842,000.  Initially, the improved property was listed for sale at $2,800,000.  Staff negotiated the contracted price for the improved property at $2,500,000.  The Seller was entertaining offers from a National retailer, which influenced the contract price.

 

7.  Environmental & Conservation Services staff has reviewed the Seller's 2018 Phase I Environmental Assessment and advised an Environmental Audit is not needed.  As part of the Due Diligence phase of the contract, staff will order a thorough Property Condition Assessment.

 

8.   Because the recommended purchase price exceeds the appraised value of the property, a supermajority of the Board is statutorily required for approval and this item is being placed on the New Business Agenda solely for this purpose.
