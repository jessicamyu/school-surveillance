Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board proclaims April as a month of recognition of the presence and contributions of our local farm workers and acknowledge the barriers their children must face to become successful students. Further I recommend that the School Board proclaims today, April 15, as a Day of Awareness of the migrant farmworker and their family.

DESCRIPTION:

Palm Beach County leads the Nation and the state in the production of many agricultural products and the workers who plant, cultivate, harvest and package those products are mostly migrant farm workers. In order for these workers' children to qualify for services through the Title I Part C Migrant Education Program, the children must travel with the workers and often must overcome multiple barriers to achieve success in school. This proclamation acknowledges these workers' contribution to the quality of life in Palm Beach County and the hardships their children encounter in their efforts to achieve an education.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the MOU with AAA Medical Compliance Testing, Inc., effective April 16, 2015 through December 31, 2020; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Board Agenda item identifies the  MOU between the School Board of Palm Beach County and AAA Medical Compliance Testing, Inc.

 

This MOU is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education Program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education curriculum frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced MOU.

 

Full-time Health Science Education Programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic High, Boca Raton High, Glades Central High, John I. Leonard High, Inlet Grove High, Jupiter High, Lake Worth High, Leadership Academy West, Pahokee High, Palm Beach Gardens High, Palm Beach Lakes High, Park Vista High, Royal Palm Beach High, Somerset Academy, South Tech Academy, and West Boca Raton High School. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 5,607 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level jobs skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

The Individual with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004 requires the School District to provide special education services to eligible children ages three through five.  These services are provided through cooperative agreements with prekindergarten programs in the community as well as in the District schools.

 
Prekindergarten exceptional students at this site will receive services for 180 days according to their Individual Educational Plans (IEP).  Area staff maintains responsibility as the Local Education Agent (LEA) and will make on-site quarterly visits to ensure compliance of federal and state guidelines.  Student progress will be documented annually on each students'  IEP.

The district collects FTE for the ESE student enrolled at the Rehabilitation Center program.  
 
This cooperative agreement is in compliance with the State Board Rule 6A.6-03026, "Special Programs for Prekindergarten Children with Disabilities."
