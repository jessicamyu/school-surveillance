DESCRIPTION:

On August 1, 2016, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Olympus International University Foundation, Inc. to open Olympus International Academy, a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2017. 

 

Per the listing of applicants filed in 2016, with the Florida Department of Education, Olympus International Academy submitted no applications to other counties.

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

 

Olympus International Academy “Does Not Meet the Standard” in the required 22 sections.

 

Thirteen sections received a rating of “Meets the Standard.”

  • Section 1 – Mission, Guiding Principles & Purpose
  • Section 2 – Target Population & Student Body
  • Section 3 – Educational Program Design
  • Section 5 – Student Performance, Assessment & Evaluation
  • Section 6 – Exceptional Students (ESE)
  • Section 7 – English Language Learners
  • Section 8 – School Culture & Discipline
  • Section 10 – Governance
  • Section 14 – Student Recruitment & Enrollment
  • Section 17 – Transportation
  • Section 18 – Food Service
  • Section 19 – School Safety & Security
  • Section 22 – Start-Up Plan

Four sections received a rating of “Partially Meets the Standard:”

  • Section 4 – Curriculum & Instructional Design
  • Section 11 – Management & Staffing
  • Section 12 – Human Resources & Employment
  • Section 16 - Facilities

Two sections received a rating of “Does Not Meet the Standard:”

  • Section 20 – Budget
  • Section 21 – Financial Management & Oversight

Three sections received a rating of “Provided,” “Not Provided,” “Completed,” or “Not Completed:”

  • Section 9 – Supplemental Programming
  • Section 13 – Professional Development
  • Section 15 – Parent & Community Involvement

Per the Overall Assessment Summary/Checklist, the ratings indicated that the application did not meet the statutory requirements set forth in the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instruments and their referenced statutes, including F.S. 1002.33(6).  (See Overall Assessment/Checklist)  It was determined that substantive changes to the application would have been needed to cure these deficiencies and the School District does not accept substantive changes.

 

“If an application is denied, the sponsor shall, within 10 calendar days after such denial, articulate in writing the specific reasons, based upon good cause, supporting its denial of the charter application and shall provide the letter of denial and supporting documentation to the applicant and to the Department of Education.”

I recommend the Board approve the salary increases and revised salary schedules for School-Based Administrators as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

The proposed salary increases for School-Based Administrators reflect utilization of an overall 3% increase budget. Effective July 1, 2016, consider approval of salary increases and revised salary schedules for School-Based Administrators as follows: *

 

A. Principals (Attachment #1):

  1. Raise salary range minimum to $95,100 and maximum to $122,500. Maintain complexity dollar values at current levels.
  2. Provide $2,600 base market equity increase.
  3. Provide additional base increase of:  $525 for Highly Effective Principals; $395 for Effective Principals (treat New/Not Rated as Effective).

 

B. Assistant Principals (Attachment #2):

  1. Raise salary range minimums and maximums commensurate with Principals' minimum and maximum (i.e., for 216 duty day positions, raise salary range minimum to $71,400 and maximum to $91,900). Maintain complexity dollar values at current levels.
  2. Provide $2,000 base market equity increase for 216 duty day positions and proportional amount for other duty day Assistant Principal positions.
  3. Provide additional base increase of:  $395 for Highly Effective Assistant Principals; $295 for Effective Assistant Principals, both based on 216 duty days (treat New/Not Rated as Effective).

 

* Employee must be in active employment status with the District as of the date of Board approval in order to be eligible for a salary increase.

DESCRIPTION:

The Title X, Part C - Education for Homeless Children and Youth 2015-2018 funds help to provide services that comply with the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act. The services facilitate the enrollment, attendance, and success in school of children and youth experiencing homelessness, including foster care children awaiting placement. Services to be provided include:

  • Identification of homeless children and youth through communication with schools, shelters, community agencies, and the Department of Children and Families/ChildNet;
  • Removal of barriers to school enrollment for homeless children, including the coordination of transportation to maintain educational stability;
  • Referral to appropriate social service agencies in order to receive additional services, including summer camp scholarships, child care, medical, food, and housing assistance;
  • Homelessness awareness training for school-based and community service personnel;
  • Referral to School Based Teams;
  • Provision of school supplies and uniforms  to homeless students; and
  • Academic tutoring for homeless students who reside in homeless shelters.
