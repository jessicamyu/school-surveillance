I recommend the Board approve the Sixth Amendment to the Charter Contract with Seagull Academy for Independent Living, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Seagull Academy’s Charter Agreement expires June 30, 2015.  The Renewal of Seagull Academy’s Charter Agreement was approved by the School Board on April 1, 2015, and the School Board and Seagull Academy for Independent Living have been and continue to be in the process of negotiating terms and conditions of a renewal Charter Agreement.  On June 17, 2015, the School Board approved Seagull Academy’s Sixth Amendment to extend the current Charter Agreement until September 30, 2015.

 

In order to complete meaningful negotiations to secure a mutually beneficial and legally binding Charter Agreement, the parties agree that it is necessary for their mutual benefit and protection that the term of the current Charter Agreement must be extended.

 

Therefore, the parties agree that the term for existence of the current Charter Agreement (as amended) is hereby extended up through and including December 29, 2015.

 

All other terms of the Charter Agreement shall remain in full force and effect, subject to further extensions of time, as mutually deemed necessary by the parties.  This Sixth Amendment, together with the Charter and previous amendments, constitutes and represents the entire agreement between the parties hereto and supersedes any prior understandings or agreements, written or verbal, respecting the subject matter herein.

 

Copies of the 2010 Contract, Mid-Year Review 14-15; Fifth Amendment to Charter Contract, Sixth Amendment to Charter Contract, Legal Checklist, Board Notification, and Matrix for October 7, 2015 Agenda Items are attached.

I-1 Adoption of Health, Life, Dental, and Vision Insurance

Oct 7, 2015 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category

Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve amending the contract on health and other insurances, pending ratification by the four recognized bargaining units, as well as for all eligible District non-bargaining employees effective January 1, 2016.

DESCRIPTION:

The Union/Associations listed in the recommendation came together as a coalition to negotiate with the District over changes to the Health, Dental, Life, and Vision Insurances offered by the District to its employees.  Coalition bargaining on insurances has existed for approximately 20 years.  The coalition members and District negotiated this contract, pending ratification.

 

The District has shared its experience of a successful wellness program which resulted in zero premium increases for the medical plan in 2014, 2015 and now 2016.  The coalition negotiations have resulted in united recognition that employees must continue the culture of wellness with adoption of the continuation and expansion of the Health Rewards with Outcomes Program.

A summary of negotiated changes is listed:

 

·         Reduced co-pays in the Low Option HMO if members seek treatment with a Tier 1 provider.

·         Increased co-pays in the High Option HMO for members who seek treatment with providers who are not listed as Tier 1.

·         Added Virtual office visits in all plans.

·         Extension of one month to August 31 for employees to earn required points for Health Rewards with Outcomes Program. (For full year discount)

·         Addition of two Lifestyle and Weight Loss Programs (Real Appeal & Diabetes Prevention Program) as choices for points in the Health Rewards with Outcomes Program.

·         Decrease in the annual deductible for the Consumer Driven Health Plan from $4,500 to $3,500.

·         Medical plans will now be administered with prior authorizations and medical necessity programs that the carrier currently has in place for fully insured plans.

.      The District and Coalition Bargaining groups agreed to meet throughout the year to discuss various medical management programs including, but not limited to, transparency, pharmacy benefit programs and health plan utilization issues.

 

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Operating Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org

Dianne L. Howard, Director, Risk and Benefits Management (dianne.howard@palmbeachschools.org)

Van Ludy, Director of Labor Relations (van.ludy@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

This MOU does not change any financial costs for the District.  Annual spend is approximately $157.6 million per year for District-sponsored medical, life and EAP benefits as well as federal PPACA fees. The source of funds is the District's Health Internal Service Fund.

P2 Out-of-Field/Out-of-Compliance Teachers for 2016, Survey 2
Meeting
Oct 7, 2015 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the attached list of out-of-field/out-of-compliance teachers for Survey 2 for the FY16 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified.  Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field in the following areas: Gifted, Reading, and ASD. Those new hires who are offered jobs as an Interim Out-of-Field, and are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements prior to the end of May.  If a teacher does not satisfy the coursework requirement, their Interim position ends.   Once a teacher completes their Out-of-Field requirements, the school is notified and teachers can be moved from Interim Out-of-Field to Regular.  Teachers who are placed Out-of-Field in core content areas are eligible to apply for reimbursement on the Subject Area Exam registration fee, after they add the subject area to their certificate. 

 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate.  Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary, or ESE.  Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements. This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification. It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track.  Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014 include the following statement, “Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment.”

I recommend the Board deny the application of Bridge Prep Academy Palm Beach County for any and/or all of the reasons stated herein, the evaluation instruments, and the overall assessment; and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related correspondence and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On August 3, 2015, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from BridgePrep Academy, Inc. (“BridgePrep”) to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2016.  BridgePrep is a high-performing charter school evidenced by the attached letter from the Florida Department of Education verifying a high-performing charter school operating in Dade County (see High-Performing Letter).  Per F.S. 1002.331(1) High-performing charter schools - A charter school is a high-performing charter school if it:

 

(a)  Received at least two school grades of “A” and no school grade below “B,” pursuant to s. 1008.34, during each of the previous 3 school years.

 

(b)  Received an unqualified opinion on each annual financial audit required under s. 218.39 in the most recent 3 fiscal years for which such audits are available.

 

(c)  Did not receive a financial audit that revealed one or more of the financial emergency conditions set forth in s. 218.503(1) in the most recent 3 fiscal years for which such audits are available.  However, this requirement is deemed met for a charter school-in-the-workplace if there is a finding in an audit that the school has the monetary resources available to cover any reported deficiency or that the deficiency does not result in a deteriorating financial condition pursuant to s. 1002.345(1)(a)3.

 

(d)  F.S. 1002.331(3)(a)   . . . If the sponsor fails to act on the application within 60 days after receipt, the application is deemed approved and the procedure in s. 1002.33(6)(h) applies.  However, the applicant agreed to an extension (see attached Extension Letter) beyond the statutory time line.

 

Per the listing of applicants filed in 2015, with the Florida Department of Education, BridgePrep Academy, Inc. submitted one application to Collier County (BridgePrep Academy Collier County), one application to Dade County (BridgePrep Academy of Advanced Studies), one application to Duval County (BridgePrep Academy Duval County), one application to Hillsborough County (BridgePrep Academy Hillsborough County), one application to Orange County (BridgePrep Academy Orange County), one application to Osceola County (BridgePrep Osceola County) and one application to Polk County (BridgePrep Academy of Polk County).

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

BridgePrep Academy Palm Beach County “Does Not Meet the Standard” in the required 19 sections.

  • Section 17 – Budget

Per the Overall Assessment Summary/Checklist, the ratings indicated that the application did not meet the statutory requirements set forth in the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instruments and their referenced statutes, including F.S. 1002.33(6) and School Board Policy 2.57.  (See Overall Assessment/Checklist)  It was determined that substantive changes to the application would have been needed to cure these deficiencies and the School District does not accept substantive changes.

 

“If an application is denied, the sponsor shall, within 10 calendar days after such denial, articulate in writing the specific reasons, based upon good cause, supporting its denial of the charter application and shall provide the letter of denial and supporting documentation to the applicant and to the Department of Education.”
