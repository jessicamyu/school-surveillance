FMPA1 Award of Bid 14C-41K Term Contract to Provide Research-based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County
Meeting
Aug 6, 2014 - 4. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
9. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the award of Bid 14C-41K for Research Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County with Learn-It Systems, LLC for the period August 11, 2014 through August 10, 2017.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the award of Bid 14C-41K Term Contract to Provide Research Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County from Learn-It Systems, LLC.

 

Federal Statute requires supplemental educational services are provided to eligible Title I students in non-public, not for profit private schools. In FY14 more than 564 Title I students were provided reading and/or math services at 32 private schools with 32 part-time teachers. Section 1120 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) requires participating Local Education Agencies (LEA) to provide eligible private school students, their teachers and families with Title I services or other benefits that are equitable to those provided to eligible Title I public school children, their teachers, and their families.   To qualify for assistance under Title I, a student must reside within the attendance area of a participating public school located in a low-income area and be failing, or at risk of failing, to meet student academic achievement standards.

 

Services to be provided include but are not limited to:

  • Research based direct supplemental academic instruction in reading and/or math
  • Instructional services, as needs are identified, to students at various private schools throughout the Palm Beach County School District
  • All materials for evaluation and instructional services
  • Comprehensive program review at the end of the school year which is to include disaggregated data, parent, staff and administrative surveys, achievement pre and post data and a summary of services
  • Florida-certified teachers with the responsibility of assessing, planning, implementing and evaluating a directed research based supplemental instruction program in reading and/or math
  • Parental Involvement activities for parents of participating students.

Pricing submitted in response to this bid was based on a hypothetical scenario with an estimated number of schools, students and allocation of funds which are all subject to change. As a result of this solicitation process, the cost avoidance for the term of the initial contract is estimated as $320,000. 

 

Learn-It Systems, LLC has committed to use the services of a SDPBC M/WBE vendor as a sub-contractor for 5% of the contract value throughout the term of this Bid.

 

The Spend with Learn-It Systems, LLC will not exceed $1,750,000 in fiscal year 2015. 

 

A formal protest has been filed on this Invitation to Bid and a DOAH hearing is scheduled for August 5, 2014.   Florida Statutes, Chapter 120.57 (3) (c), requires that contract award process stop until final agency action has occurred unless delay would cause an immediate and serious danger to the public health, safety or welfare. We must provide the non-public school students who qualify and whose schools elect to participate in this program with Supplemental Academic Instruction as required by Federal Laws.  These include some of the most at-risk students for whom a delay in service would likely be detrimental to their educational progress.  The current contract expires on September 14, 2014.  This contract was originally scheduled to be presented to the School Board for award on June 18, 2014 in order for the vendor to hire teachers, meet with schools, set schedules, meet with parents and be ready to provide needed services by September 8, 2014.  With the delay, it is now scheduled to begin August 11,2014.  Learn-It Systems, Inc. has assured us that with Board award at this time they will be able to provide services as needed but that a further delay would jeopardize their ability to perform timely. Therefore, it is recommended that the award be made as presented.

LD1 Authorization to File Lawsuits regarding the Prematurely Decaying Fire Sprinkler Infrastructure at School Facilities

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board authorize the Office of General Counsel to file a lawsuit against any and all parties responsible for the prematurely decaying fire sprinkler infrastructure at any and all school facilities, hire experts and/or consultants, and finalize and sign all required documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The fire sprinkler systems installed by Construction Managers as part of new, replacement, and modernizations at several schools installed between 2004 and 2008 are experiencing premature decay and rust.  These latent defects were recently discovered and District staff has actively investigated the cause of the problem.

 

Staff and the Office of General Counsel have made numerous attempts to resolve this issue with the responsible parties in order to cause the fire sprinklers to be repaired; however, the parties responsible have not agreed to make the necessary repairs.  Authorization to file a lawsuit by the School Board against any and all responsible parties is requested.  The Office of General Counsel will seek pre-suit mediation wherever possible and practicable to reach an amicable resolution.

I recommend the School Board approve the five (5) year Charter with Florida Futures Academy - North Campus, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Florida Futures Academy Charter Schools, Inc. to open Florida Futures Academy – North Campus in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on October 2, 2013, as Item CS5 during a Special Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, except as provided in the charter.  The School is deferring opening for the first year in this charter.

 

Florida Futures Academy - North Campus will serve students in grades 9-12.

 

Florida Futures Academy - North Campus’ mission is, “To provide the community with a viable alternative for adolescents and young adults to earn a high school diploma.  Through a blended, self-paced instructional model, delivery of individualized instruction and support services, relationship building in a nurturing, learning environment, students formerly at-risk will transform to students at-promise.  The mission is to enable those who cannot currently envision a positive future into young adults who become productive contributing members of their community."

 

Attached are copies of the full contract, budget, legal checklist, Board notification, and matrix for August 6th board items.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Willie Richardson's termination from employment effective Thursday, August 28, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

I recommend the School Board approve Willie Richardson’s termination from employment effective Thursday, August 28, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

 

On or about April 22, 2014, Willie Richardson became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Ethical Misconduct-Falsification of Employment Qualifications and Failure to Fulfill the Essential Functions of a Human Resources Technician II.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated. 

 

On April 22, 2014, it was reported that a School District employee had taken and passed a typing test on behalf of an applicant for employment with the School District.  It was further alleged that HR Technician Willie Richardson allowed the falsified results to be submitted on behalf of the applicant. During the investigation, Mr. Richardson admitted his role in submitting the falsified test results. 

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 3.02 (4)(a), (4)(g) and (5)(c), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment with the District. Furthermore, it is noted that Mr. Richardson completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on May 28, 2010.   

 

There is “just cause” to support Willie Richardson’s termination of employment. 

 

The “Recommendation for Termination from Employment” was hand-delivered to  Mr. Richardson from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.
