I recommend the School Board approve the Fifth Amendment to the Charter Contract with Seagull Academy for Independent Living and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Seagull Academy’s Charter Agreement expires June 30, 2015.  The Renewal of Seagull Academy’s Charter Agreement was approved by the School Board on April 1, 2015, and the School Board and Seagull Academy for Independent Living have been and continue to be in the process of negotiating terms and conditions of a renewal Charter Agreement.

 

In order to complete meaningful negotiations to secure a mutually beneficial and legally binding Charter Agreement, the parties agree that it is necessary for their mutual benefit and protection that the term of the current Charter Agreement must be extended.

 

Therefore, through this Extension Agreement, the parties would agree that the term for existence of the current Charter Agreement is hereby extended for ninety (90) days from its expiration, or until September 30, 2015.

 

All other terms of the Charter Agreement shall remain in full force and effect, subject to further extensions of time, as mutually deemed necessary by the parties.  This Fifth Amendment, together with the Charter and previous amendments, constitutes and represents the entire agreement between the parties hereto and supersedes any prior understandings or agreements, written or verbal, respecting the subject matter herein.

I recommend the School Board approve the First Amendment to the Renewal Charter Contract with Renaissance Learning Center and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 20, 2012, the School Board of Palm Beach County (“School Board” or “Sponsor”) granted Renaissance Learning Center (“RLC” or “School”) a ten (10) year Renewal Charter Contract to serve Exceptional Student Education/Autism students, ages Pre-K – 14.

 

Section I, B, 5 of the Charter permits the modification of the Charter by mutual agreement in writing executed by the School Board and the School.

 

Renaissance Learning Center is currently located at 5800 Corporate Way and 5841 Corporate Way, West Palm Beach, Florida 33407.  The School wishes to relocate to a newly constructed facility located at 18370 Limestone Creek Road, Jupiter, Florida 33458 immediately prior to the commencement of the 2015-16 school year. 

 

Renaissance Learning Center also wishes to change the d/b/a of the School to “The Learning Center at The Els Center of Excellence,” which is intended to be used as a fictitious name and not a change in the School’s legal corporate name.

 

The School Board seeks to update the Charter to cover provisions now required by the State’s charter school contract template and is now also required, through a Resolution Agreement with the U.S. Department of Justice, to include provisions in charter contracts relating to the agreement.

 

The School Board additionally seeks to include provisions in the Charter relating to the Inspector General.

 

The School Board and RLC hereby agree to paragraphs 1. through 21. as stated in the attached First Amendment.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the University of Wisconsin-Madison for research for the My Classroom Economy (MCE) program.

DESCRIPTION:

This project, funded by the U.S. Department of Treasury’s Financial Empowerment Innovation Fund,

will evaluate an innovative approach to increasing the financial capability of elementary school

students--My Classroom Economy (MCE).  My Classroom Economy (www.myclassroomeconomy.org)

is a robust classroom management system that uses a simulated economy to teach students financial

responsibility. The beauty of the program is that students learn responsible behaviors through their

choices and experiences in the classroom economy, rather than through traditional curriculum.

The program requires a small investment of time for planning, but it does not supplant time spent

teaching and in fact, is likely to enhance classroom efficiency.

 

A study, conducted by the University of Wisconsin-Madison, will test the effects of MCE on elementary

students’ financial knowledge, attitudes, and behavior. Fourth and fifth grade teachers in participating schools

will implement the MCE program during the first trimester of 2016.  Data points collected will include teacher

surveys, parent surveys, de-identified student performance data collected by the District, and MCE transaction

logs maintained in the normal course of running the program. The results of this study will inform future efforts

to improve financial literacy and other outcomes for K-12 students within the School District of Palm Beach County and

across the nation.

DESCRIPTION:

Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID) is a systemic instructional approach for students in Kindergarten through  higher education (K-16). The AVID College Readiness System is a school-wide transformational effort focused on leadership, systems, instruction and culture.  AVID is designed to increase the number of students who enroll and succeed in higher education and in their lives beyond high school. The AVID College Readiness System is a catalyst for developing a school culture that closes the expectation and opportunity gaps many students face, and prepares all students for success in a global society.  
 
AVID Excel is a middle school program intended to break the detrimental cycle that turns many English Language Learners (ELLs) into long-term ELLs.  AVID Excel accelerates students' academic language acquisition and puts them on a pathway to high school AVID and college readiness.
     
The School District of Palm Beach County's AVID participation has increased from 6 high schools sites in 2009 to 26 sites at the elementary, middle and high school levels. Five of AVID sites in the District have been designated by the AVID Center as "Highly Certified". (The participating schools are listed in the attached addendum).
     
Approximately 200 AVID seniors have successfully graduated since the first graduating class in 2012.  Of the 2013-2014 AVID seniors, 100 percent graduated on time with 80 percent accepted to a four-year college and 17 percent accepted into a two-year college.  Eighty-three percent of the 2013-2014 graduating seniors graduated “college-ready," 95 percent took either the SAT or ACT, and 69 percent took at least one AP/IB exam. Of the 2013-2014 middle school students, 64 percent have taken an honors or a high school credit-bearing course. 
       
This Agreement will also allow the School District of Palm Beach County to implement AVID's curriculum and use the AVID logo in all twenty AVID sites.

Meeting
Jun 17, 2015 - 3. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the 2015 Comprehensive Safety Inspection Report.

DESCRIPTION:

 

The annual Comprehensive Safety Inspections were performed by the Building Code Services Fire and Safety Inspectors as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1).  All educational, auxiliary and ancillary facilities were included in these inspections, using the State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) for casualty and sanitation, and Florida Administrative Code Chapter 69A-58 for fire safety.

 

This year, the Division of Support Operations initiated a collaborative effort among Maintenance and Plant Operations, Program Management, Building Code Services and individual school centers to reduce the number of outstanding safety citations.  The strategies included raising the awareness of safety issues at schools, conducting critical code analysis to identify mitigation opportunities and channeling greater resources toward correcting safety citations in the field.  The results have been very successful and the strategy will be continued.

 

The number of citations for Public Schools, Alternative Schools and Ancillary Facilities fell from 2,898 in 2014 to 1,395 on 2015.  This represents a 52% decrease over one year and a 97% reduction since 2000.  All conditions considered by the inspector to be hazardous or posing an imminent danger were reported to the Maintenance and Plant Operation Department at the time of discovery for immediate action.

 

The success of the collaborative effort justified adjusting the corrective action due dates from the 2014 report.  The Building Code Services Department will work closely with the responsible parties to promptly correct these deficiencies.  None of the past due citations are considered immediate life threatening. A summary report by facility, detailed individual facility reports and descriptive charts are attached.

 

According to SREF, Chapter 5, the inspection reports shall be approved by the Board, and the Board shall adopt a plan and a schedule for the correction of each deficiency.   The corrective action plans for each citation were developed in conjunction with the local fire authorities and are included on each report.

 

Upon the Board approving the inspection reports, copies of the reports will be submitted to the State Fire Marshal as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). The Board shall certify the completion of the inspection report to the State Fire Marshal by June 30, 2015.

I recommend the Board approve the out-of-country travel request to Spain for two International Spanish Academy teachers, who are scholarship recipients, to attend the summer program at the University of Salamanca.

DESCRIPTION:

John I. Leonard Community High School is an International Spanish Academy (ISA).  The Embassy of Spain has awarded scholarships for their summer program at the University of Salamanca, Spain, to Ana Viton and Angel Valdes, ISA teachers at John I. Leonard High School.  These teachers will attend a university-level course on Spanish Language and Culture geared specifically toward teachers and administrators from the United States and Canada.  Travel dates are June 21 through July 4, 2015.

 

The scholarships include the cost of tuition, room, and board.  Transportation costs will be provided by the Multicultural Education Department and will not exceed $1,676.
