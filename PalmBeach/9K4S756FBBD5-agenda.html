P1 Sole Source Purchase and Installation of water holding tank at Jupiter Farms Elem.
Meeting
May 28, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Purchasing
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

Water holding tank at Jupiter Farms Elementary is leaking and in need of an replacement.  The tank will not pass the 5-year annual recertification which is due in August of 2008.  In addition, the new tank will be updated to meet current specifications/codes.  Below is sole source justification.





  • The proposed tank is from the original supplier and will be compatible with the "in -the-slab" existing structural support ring cast into the existing concrete slab.


  • The proposed tank is of an equal/identical capacity to the existing tank.


  • The attachment for the proposed tank is compatible with the supports for the existing tank.


  • The proposed tank materials are interchangeable with the existing tank materials/supports.


  • The replacement of the existing tank with the proposed tank by the Florida Aquastore utilizes the same design parameters as currently permitted by the Health Department and replacing this tank "like-for-like" will not require a permit from the Health Department.


  • Utilizing the proposed vendor will result in the following:




    • Minimal permitting through our Building Department


    • Elimination of the need to hire a design consultant


    • Eliminate the demolition of the existing concrete slab thereby reducing installation time


    • Less impact on the students/facility because of the faster turn around time in project completion.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval for the Sole Source purchase and installation of the water holding tank at Jupiter Farms Elementary in the not-to-exceed amount of $100,000.



   

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Warren Haan, Director of Program Management            

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the District Budget is estimated at the not-to-exceed amount of $100,000.  The source of the funds is Minor Capitol Budget.

P3 Certified Athletic Trainers for Fiscal Year 2009
Meeting
May 28, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Purchasing
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

Individual high schools through the Athletic Director and/or Principal will select an Athletic Trainer for their school to cover sports (boys and girls).



Athletic Trainers must be certified by the National Athletic Trainers Association (N.A.T.A.) and licensed in Florida.



The District will compensate each Athletic Trainer up to $22,500 ($15,000 for those with fewer sports) per high school based upon 1,000 hours of service of which a minimum of 250 hours will be allocated to each of the three sports season (fall, winter and spring); schools having fewer sports and teams will compensate the trainers based upon the hours needed.



The Athletic Trainers will work with the athletes and coaches based upon individual needs at the school.  Practice, game and event coverage will be coordinated and schedules agreed upon by both parties. Special attention will be given to those sports with the most potential for injuries (football, soccer, basketball and wrestling) but not to the exclusion of any other sport.



Professional liability and comprehensive general liability insurance are required from all athletic trainers/providers as indicated in the contract.



A sample standard contract between the School Board of Palm Beach County and a medical provider/athletic trainer is attached that includes a Hold Harmless clause.



The contract period is August 1, 2008 through July 31, 2009



 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the standard contract for Certified Athletic Trainers for the Fiscal Year 2009 in the not-to-exceed amount of $502,500.
