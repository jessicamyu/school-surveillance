Subject
CLS1 - West Boca Raton Community High School Advanced Placement World History Teacher to travel to Kazakhstan, Russia
Meeting
Mar 2, 2011 - Special Meetings
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

Barbara Johnson was selected to participate in the Teaching Excellence and Achievement Program (TEA) & International Leaders in Education Program (ILEP) U.S. Teacher Exchange.



The exchange opportunity is funded by the U.S. Department of State Bureau of Educational & Cultural Affairs and administered by IREX (International Research & Exchanges Board).  It will provde a unique opportunity to build global competence in the U.S. classroom through teacher professional development.



Ms. Johnson is one of six teachers selected from Florida by the State Department with a total of 110 teachers selected for this professional development grant opportunity.



Ms. Johnson will travel to the international host school for two weeks.  She will have the opporutnity to:





  • Collaborate with host school teachers on best practices in teaching and classroom leadership, as well as related curriculum and educational issues in their subject of Advanced Placement World History.

  • Interact with the local community through short home stays and meetings with local press, government officials, and school or parent groups.

  • Visit local schools and team teach with the TEA/ILEP international partner at their host school.

  • Meet with representatives of the Fulbright Commission, U.S. Public Affairs Section (PAS), and/or the Regional English Language Officer (RELO) in the host country.

  • Visit cultural sites.

  • During the eight work days the teacher is in Russia, a substitute will cover her classroom. The substitute will be paid from the school's professional development substitute funding budget.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the West Boca Raton Community Advanced Placement World History Teacher to travel to Kazakhstan, Russia.

Contact:

Judith Klinek, Chief Academic Officer



Nora Rosensweig, South Area Superintendent



Mark Stenner, Principal, West Boca Raton Community High School

Financial Impact:

The cost to the District will amount to $728.00 for the 8 days needed for the substitute to cover the teacher's class.  The funds will be deducted from the West Boca Raton High School/Professional Development substitute funding strip.
