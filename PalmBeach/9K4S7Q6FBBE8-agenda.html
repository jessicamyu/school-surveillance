Florida's natural beauty, crystal blue waters and white sand beaches attract residents and visitors from around the world.  Clean and sustainable water resources are vital to Florida's environment, economy and quality of life.  More than 90 percent of Florida's drinking water is supplied by underlying aquifers, and our quality of life and the economy depend upon a clean and healthy environment. Floridians consume more than 7.9 billion gallons per day of fresh water, and the future of Florida depends greatly upon the availability of water.  South Florida residents consume 179 gallons per person, per day - the highest water usage in the state.  The South Florida Water Management District, is implementing a Comprehensive Water Conservation Program to instill a lasting culture of conservation in our communities.  The Governor and Cabinet of the State of Florida are designating April as Florida's Water Conservation Month to encourage Floridians to conserve the state's precious water resources.

PM-1 Educational Plant Survey
Meeting
Apr 21, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

·         A formal and complete Educational Plant Survey is required to be conducted every five (5) year.



 



·         Authorization for the survey is specified in Article IX and Article XII of the Florida Constitution; Chapters 1001, 1011, and 1013, Florida Statutes (F.S.); State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF); and the Florida Building Code (FBC).



 



·         The survey report must describe the current educational plants of the district and the estimated capital outlay needs through a five-year period. The recommendations made in the survey must result from a systematic study of present educational and ancillary plants and the determination of future needs, including long range planning, to provide an appropriate educational program and services for each student based on projected COFTE approved or authorized for use by the Department of Education.



 



·         The purpose of the Educational Plant Survey is to aid in formulating recommendations for housing the educational activities of the students and staff of the School District for the next several years.  It is a needs assessment which compares what exists at a facility as listed on the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) to what an educational or ancillary plant is eligible to have based on the latest Board approved Facilities Lists.



 



·         The DOE Facility Lists are based upon new State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) requirements. 



 



·         Table II is the summary of all the Facility Recommendations with respect to student station adjustments.  It is a summary of the individual evaluations of each facility with respect to present student capacity, projected student capacity, the affect of Classroom Size Reduction (CSR) legislation, Program Capacity, and a recommendation of the final student capacity at each site. Under current formulas, spaces used for essential special programs are counted by the DOE as regular instructional spaces, thereby limiting the District's ability to construct adequate school facilities to meet program needs.  The District is asking DOE to approve the Educational Plant Survey based upon F.S.1013.31.1.(b) 5 allowing the District to include space needs that deviate from approved standards for determining space needs. Program Capacity has previously been approved by the Board and DOE in the Charter District Waiver #2 and is summarized in Table II. This is the basis upon which the School Board will determine the space needs at the local level.



 



·         Pursuant to Florida Statutes (F.S.) 1013.32, "an exception to the recommendation in the educational plant survey may be allowed if a board considers that it will be advantageous to the welfare of the educational system or that it will make possible a substantial saving of funds."  Under current formulas, spaces used for essential special programs are counted by the DOE as regular instructional spaces, thereby limiting the District's ability to construct adequate school facilities to meet program needs.  With the use of Program Capacity in Table II, the School Board will determine the space needs at the local level.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Educational Plant Survey with Program Capacity, as represented in Table II, the summary of the where students will be housed, and the DOE (Department of Education) Facility Lists.

The Mary and Robert Pew Public Education Fund has awarded the District a grant which will provide one-year funding in the amount of $21,800 to support on-going professional development for principals and assistant principals of schools implementing Reader's and Writer's Workshop. The goal of this cohort is for administrators to sharpen their ability to provide high quality literacy support for their teachers and students for years to come. Administrators will visit each others' schools, work alongside each other in study groups, share tools for analyzing the teaching that occurs within and across grade levels, develop and refine tactics for rallying their communities, share strategies, and improve the quality of teaching and student learning.

They will also learn how reading and writing are connected through conversing, thinking, and writing about literature related experiences. Through in-depth professional development, the administrators will be able to better support the full implementation of Reader's and Writer's Workshop in their schools.
