Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the award of ITN 22C-002V for Medical and Pharmacy Claims Administration for the period of January 1, 2022 through December 31, 2026 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Award of the Contract resulting from ITN 22C-002V for Medical and Pharmacy Claims Administration for the period of January 1, 2022 through December 31, 2026, with the option to renew for one (1) additional five-year period with United HealthCare Services, Inc.

 

An Invitation to Negotiate (ITN) was solicited and on August 9, 2021 and an Evaluation Committee consisting of District personnel, Union Members from the AESOP and CTA Unions with input from the District’s consultant, Gallagher Benefits Services, evaluated the three proposals received based on the criteria outlined in the ITN. The criteria that was scored included Experience and Qualifications, Services Requested, Scope of Service, Medical and Pharmacy Financials, Performance Guarantees and Small Business Enterprise (SBE) Participations. After careful consideration the evaluation committee recommended entering into negotiations with United HealthCare Services, Inc., the highest ranked vendor. These negotiations resulted in additional financial savings of over $1 million in pharmacy rebate guarantees.

 

The administrative service fee is reduced by 2.9% from the current per employee per month (PEPM) rate of $30.79 down to $29.89 for the HMO plans and to $32.66 from $33.64 for the CDHP plans. These fees are for the first 3 years of this contract-a savings of $216,000 per year, with expected annual cost of $7,200,000 based on 20,000 covered employees and retirees. The fee increases for years 4 and 5 to $30.76 HMO Plans and to $33.61 CDHP which equates to $7,410,000 in each year.

 

We are adding a PEPM fee of $11.25 in year 1, $11.70 in year 2 and $12.16 in year 3 to cover the Naviguard program, which is a discount for non-contracted services such as ambulance, radiology and anesthesia. This expected annual fee of $2.7 million replaces the shared savings programs which have been costing approximately $4 million per year.

 

In addition to providing two onsite service personnel and one registered nurse, UHC is providing $575,000 annually toward staffing, wellness and plan audits.

 

The Office of Diversity in Business Practices provided the names of five District certified SBE vendors that registered for this commodity. The Purchasing Department notified the vendors of the upcoming opportunity however none of those vendors responded to this solicitation. United HealthCare Services, Inc. has committed to utilizing one of the Districts certified SBE vendors, Benefits Outsource, Inc., for Cobra Administration, Health Equity Inclusion Program and Biometric Screening Program for At Risk Members.

 

CONTACTS:

Heather Frederick, CPA, Chief Financial Officer (heather.frederick@palmbeachschools.org)

Dianne Howard, Director of Risk and Benefits Management (dianne.howard@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The annual financial impact to the District is not to exceed $9,910,000 in administrative servicing fees for the first three years and $10.1 million for the last two years. Additionally, approximately $200 million annually in claim payments will be processed through this vendor. The source of funds is Health Internal Service Fund budget.
