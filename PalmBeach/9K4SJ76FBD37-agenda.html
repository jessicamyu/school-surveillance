LAC1 Amendments to the Collective Bargaining Agreement Between SDPBC and The Palm Beach County Classroom Teachers Association (CTA) re: adoption of the negotiated 2012-2013 amendments to the July 1, 2011 - June 30, 2014 Collective Bargaining Agreement
Meeting
Jan 9, 2013 - Special Meeting 1-09-13
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
1. Effective July 1, 2012, the Teachers' Salary Schedule is amended and represents an overall increase of 3.41%.  Only teachers who are currently employed by the District on the date the School Board adopts these amendments are eligible to receive a retroactive salary increase back to July 1, 2012.

 


2. The Contract will be reopened on Salary should the District incur a liability prior to June 30, 2013, as a result of the final judicial or legislative resolution of the Florida Supreme Court case Scott vs Williams.


 


3. The parties agree collaboratively to create a Performance-Based Salary Schedule having the same beginning salary and top salary as does the Teachers' Salary Schedule mentioned in #1 above.  For the time being, no employee will be paid based on this Performance-Based Salary Schedule.


 


4. The Insurance Benefit provisions negotiated earlier in coalition bargaining are a part of these negotiated amendments to the Collective Bargaining Agreement effective January 1, 2013.


 


5. Full-time T-bargaining employees who enroll their children in any of the District's After School Child Care Programs will be granted a 25% discount on the hourly rate charged by these programs to the general public.  These provisions will be effective no later than 45-days after these amendments are approved by the School Board.


 


6. The Unit Adjustment Transfer (UAT) provisions of the Contract are updated primarily to reflect an earlier grievance arbitration award and to bring them into compliance with State law.


 


7. Two separate joint committees are formed:  One to review Lesson Plan requirements and planning formats; and one to study the issues surrounding Team Planning meetings and to offer ways to make such meetings more meaningful to the participants.


 


8. Two outdated Appendices of the Contract and one outdated Section of the Contract were eliminated, and six Appendixes were added: 1) The SIG I MOU, 2) The Extended Day at 16 of our Elementary Schools MOU, 3) MOUs allowing for two High School Athletic Supplements for FY13 & FY14, 4)  The SIG II MOU, 5) Provisions for the issuance of employment contracts per State law, and 6)  The Virtual Education School MOU.


 


9. In addition, five Sections of the Contract were amended to bring them into compliance with changes in State law.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the 2012-2013 negotiated amendments to the July 1, 2011- June 30, 2014 Collective Bargaining Agreement as attached.

Michael J. Burke mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org



Van V. Ludy van.ludy@palmbeachschools.org



 

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the FY2013 General Fund Budget is $22.4 million.

LAC2 Collective Bargaining Agreement Between The SDPBC and The Police Benevolent Association of Palm Beach County (PBA) re: A successor two-year Collective Bargaining Agreement with PBA (January 1, 2012 through December 31, 2013)
Meeting
Jan 9, 2013 - Special Meeting 1-09-13
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

1.   Effective July 1, 2012, the current hourly rate pay schedule is amended and becomes a Min-Max Hourly Rate Schedule.                 



2.   Effective July 2, 2012, the Min-Max Hourly Rate Schedule is increased across-the-board by 3.5%.



3.   The parties have agreed to suspend the Performance Based Pay Plan to assist in funding the 3.5% increase stated above which means the net increase to the District to fund this 3.5% increase is 2%.



4.   All half-time through full-time employees represented by PBA who were on active status on October 22, 2012, and who remain a half-time through full-time employee will be paid a one-time bonus of $500, minus standard deductions.



5.   The Contract can be reopened on Salary should State funding for FY14 prove to be inadequate to fund the economic provisions of the new Contract; OR should the District incur a liability prior to June 30, 2013, as a result of the final judicial or legislative resolution of the Florida Supreme Court case Scott vs. Williams; however, the District pledges not to ask any Officer to return any wages already paid and further commits not to seek any reduction in the negotiated hourly rates between July 1, 2013 and December 31, 2013, unless the District faces a dire and catastrophic financial crisis that requires other employee groups, including administrators, to make financial accommodations and sacrifices to assist the District balance its FY14 budget.



6.   A joint 8-person committee is established to study and recommend revisions to the current Performance Based Pay Plan to make it more stringent.  Implementation of these recommended revisions is subject to future negotiations.  If no revisions are agreed to, the original Performance Based Pay Plan will be reinstated in January of 2014, unless otherwise agreed to by the parties.



7.   The Insurance Benefit provisions negotiated earlier in coalition bargaining are included in this new PBA Agreement effective January 1, 2013.



8.   As a one calendar year pilot, provisions for a new sick leave donation plan, as allowed by FS 1012.61, are included in the PBA Contract that permit Officers to donate sick leave to a fellow Officer whose sick leave is depleted within the guidelines established by this Florida Statute.



9.   Revised the Attendance Incentive Program to reward an Officer $250, minus standard deductions, who has perfect attendance (with certain limited exceptions) for approximately each nine-weeks grading period as set forth in the adopted School Calendar.



 

Recommendation:

Having received notice and verification that the PBA bargaining unit has voted to ratify this Agreement, I recommend that the School Board approve the successor 2012-2013 two-year SDPBC-PBA Collective Bargaining Agreement.

Contact:

Michael J. Burke mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org



Van. V. Ludy van.ludy@palmbeachschools.org



 

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the FY13 General Fund Budget is $147,196, plus an additional $69,165 one-time payment, including fringe benefit costs.

LAC3 Collective Bargaining Agreement Between SDPBC and The Association of Educational Secretaries And Office Professionals (AESOP) re: Successor 2012-2013 Collective Bargaining Agreement with AESOP
Meeting
Jan 9, 2013 - Special Meeting 1-09-13
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

1.   Effective July 1, 2012, the AESOP salary schedules are increased by $.72 per hour. 



2.   AESOP agreed to eliminate its Attendance Incentive Plan to assist in funding the $.72 per hour increase stated above.  Given the uniform $0.72 increase to the hourly rate, the effective salary percentage increase will vary based on the employees' current salary level.  The average salary increase across the employee group is 5%.  After considering the elimination of the attendance incentive, the net increase to base salary is 2%.



3.   The Contract can be reopened on the 2012-2013 salary settlement should State funding for FY14 prove to be inadequate to fund the economic provisions of the new Contract; OR should the District incur a liability prior to June 30, 2013, as a result of the final judicial or legislative resolution of the Florida Supreme Court case Scott vs. Williams.



4.   All half-time through full-time employees represented by AESOP who were on active status on October 22, 2012, and who remain a half-time through full-time employees of the District on the date this negotiated Contract is approved/adopted by the School Board will be paid a one-time bonus of $500, minus standard deductions.



5.   Effective January 1, 2013, Paraprofessionals represented by AESOP who are responsible for medically complex students and those assigned to EB/D units will receive an increase of $.50 per hour to the additional hourly rate they already receive for working with these special needs children.



6.   The Insurance Benefit provisions negotiated earlier in coalition bargaining are included in this new AESOP Agreement effective January 1, 2013.



7.   The Grievance Procedure in the new agreement has been updated and amended.



8.   Full-time AESOP represented employees who enroll their children in any of the District's After School Child Care Programs will be granted a 25% discount on the hourly rate charges by these programs to the general public.



9.   AESOP retains the right to demand bargaining over the impact of revised job descriptions on wages, hours and terms and conditions of employment.



10.   The parties agree there will be no discrimination against employees based on their membership or non-membership in the Association; and the District will abide by its policies relating to bullying and the harassment of employees represented by AESOP.



 



 



 



 



 



 



 



 

Recommendation:

Having received notice and verification that the AESOP bargaining unit has voted to ratify this Agreement, I recommend that the School Board approve the successor 2012-2013 two-year SDPBC - AESOP Collective Bargaining Agreement.

Contact:

Michael J. Burke mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org



Van V. Ludy van.ludy@palmbeachschools.org



 

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the School District, including associated fringe benefit costs, is $1,210,620 in recurring funds and $1,076,500 in non-recurring, one-time funds.
