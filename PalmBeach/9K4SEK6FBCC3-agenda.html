PA2-Days of Remembrance
Meeting
Mar 13, 2013 - School Board Meeting 03-13-13 at 5pm
Category

Type

The Holocaust was the state-sponsored, systematic persecution and annihilation of European Jewry by Nazi Germany and its collaborators between 1933 and 1945 - six million were murdered.  The history of the Holocaust offers an opportunity to reflect on the moral responsibilities of individuals, societies, and governments.  The Days of Remembrance have been set aside for the people of the School District of Palm Beach County to remember the victims of the Holocaust as well as to reflect on the need for respect of all peoples.





InSIGHT Through Education is a not-for-profit organization whose purpose is to inspire and educate using lessons learned from the Holocaust and Genocides to eliminate prejudice while encouraging respect and acceptance in our schools, in our communities and in our world.  InSIGHT has funded all of the School District's workshops and programs that require funding.  The organization recently funded an educational trip to Washington DC for 20 students, five chaperones and one Holocaust survivor.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County proclaims Sunday, April 7 through Sunday, April 13, 2013 as Days of Remembrance in memory of the victims of the Holocaust and in honor of the survivors as well as the rescuers and liberators, and further proclaim that we should work to promote human dignity and confront hate whenever and wherever it occurs.

The FAU/Pine Jog Environmental Education Center has partnered with the School District of Palm Beach County and The Education Network to create a series of virtual field trips that support the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards for science and engage elementary students in explorations of the natural world.  The goals of the virtual field trips are to enhance students' environmental awareness and to improve student achievement on environmental and life science concepts.  Each field trip, lasting approximately 10 minutes, consists of a virtual nature walk through the Pine Jog natural area and an in-depth study of a particular science concept such as life cycles of Florida plants and animals.

AC 1 - StudentTracker Research Agreement for Educational Organizations and Agencies
Meeting
Mar 13, 2013 - School Board Meeting 03-13-13 at 5pm
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

On May 9, 2012, the School Board approved The National Student Clearinghouse, which provides a nationwide, central repository of information on the enrollment status and educational achievements of postsecondary students.  Palm Beach County would retain the National Student Clearinghouse to obtain data to conduct research on the postsecondary enrollment statuses and educational achievements at postsecondary educational institutions of students who have graduated from or attended a high school in Palm Beach County.  This is a continuation of the current Agreement.



By participating in an Agreement with the National Student Clearinghouse, Palm Beach County will continue to:







  • submit and receive data enabling the District to track postsecondary student data for research and reporting purposes




  • gain information from the research to use in developing a college readiness pathway




  • document effectiveness of college readiness strategies used by schools and programs within the District


With the data obtained from the National Student Clearinghouse, the Department of Research and Evaluation has been able to conduct analysis of the data to determine what set of student experiences best predicts successful postsecondary outcomes. This information is currently incorporated into Educational Data Warehouse (EDW) reporting and has been used in the development of college readiness strategies.



This Agreement is in effect from May 10, 2013 through May 10, 2014.  However, this Agreement may be modified by written, mutual agreement of the parties and remains in effect until terminated by either party by providing 30 days written notice to the other party.



 




Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the StudentTracker Research Agreement for Educational Organizations and Agencies from May 10, 2013 through May 10, 2014, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and release or request data related to the Agreement.

PR1 Pine Grove El-City of Delray/ACCF Summer Camp ILA
Meeting
Mar 13, 2013 - School Board Meeting 03-13-13 at 5pm
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion


  1. The City of Delray Beach has partnered with the National Civic League's campaign to ensure grade level reading.  The City along with their community partners created a Community Solutions Action Plan (CSAP) which identifies three crucial areas that may hinder adolescent children from reading at grade level: school readiness, chronic absenteeism and summer learning loss.

  2. Pine Grove Elementary has been selected to implement the CSAP by creating an 8-week pilot summer program with reading enhancements.  The summer program will focus on improving reading scores and preventing summer learning loss, which will enable the students to perform better when the academic year begins.

  3. Pine Grove Elementary is a Title 1 school and was selected by the city to pilot the summer academic program.

  4. The City of Delray Beach and Achievement Centers for Children and Families (ACCF) is requesting the utilization of 7 classrooms, cafeteria, athletic fields and playgrounds in order to implement the program at Pine Grove.

  5. Approval of this Agreement will support the collaborative and Intergovernmental initiatives between the School Board, City and ACCF.

  6. Implementation of this program is dependent upon the School District being responsible for all associated facility, labor and maintenance costs.  The lease cost for the use of the facilities that would be waived as part of this agreement is $63,600.00. (See Exhibit "A").

  7. The District shall be responsible for custodial labor costs in the amount of $13,200.00.  The school has limited summer custodial staff that is responsible for daily assignments and specific summer projects. An additional custodian is required in order to meet the needs of the summer camp program that is targeted for 100 students. 

  8. The City and ACCF will share costs for a certified teacher, books, art and crafts and transportation.

  9. The term of this Agreement shall begin June 10, 2013 and terminate August 2, 2013.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Tripartite and Interlocal Agreement between the City of Delray Beach, Florida, Community Child Care Center of Delray Beach, Inc. d/b/a Achievement Centers for Children and Families, and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the use of a portion of Pine Grove Elementary campus for a summer camp; and authorize the Chairman and the Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

PR3 Florida Inventory of School Houses

State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) 6.1(7) (c) requires that, "Prior to April 1 of each year, each district shall review the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) and shall certify to the Office of Educational Facilities that the inventory is current and accurate."



The Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) is available to all school districts for reporting facilities information.  District FISH reports are available on-line for all district staff who has access to the electronic data base.



The facilities inventory may be corrected by submitting on-line transactions through the Educational Facilities Information System (EFIS). A district's facilities inventory may be corrected at any time when new additions or remodeling occurs, during a validation study conducted by the district or the Office of Educational (OEF), or as the result of an approved educational plant survey.  New construction may be added to the inventory when a construction contract is issued. Large areas that are scheduled to undergo major remodeling should be updated in the inventory as soon as a contract agreement has been made.  If a major remodeling project is conducted by district staff, the inventory should be updated when the project is substantially completed.  Each district should conduct at least an annual update of FISH.



Correct facilities data is essential in the District's and State's analyses for reporting necessities such as class size reduction, determination of PECO maintenance requirements, which results in legislative funding.  FISH errors can significantly influence funds the District receives and distort analyses made from FISH data.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the annual Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) Certification of Facilities Data.

IN4 Approval to Purchase Master Builders' Risk Insurance
Meeting
Mar 13, 2013 - School Board Meeting 03-13-13 at 5pm
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The policy will cover damage to the property being built as well as theft of materials from the site during the construction phase for new and replacement schools/buildings.  This policy covers all facilities started during the policy period and those currently still under construction.



The Master Builders' Risk Policy eliminates the need to go directly to the contractor for a project-specific, stand-alone builders' risk policy.  Over the last several years, the program has saved 70 percent of contractor-purchase builders' risk coverage.  The District purchases the insurance and premiums are charged back to the individual construction projects.



The policy will have an annual aggregate limit of $25 million subject to a sub-limit of $10 million for any one, other than wind loss occurrence, and $10 million aggregate in one policy year for Named Windstorm.  Named Windstorm also has a five (5) percent deductible (self-insured retention) subject to a $250,000 minimum.  However, because the District owns the policy rather than the contractor, the District will be eligible for FEMA reimbursements, whereas a contractor would not be eligible.



The renewal premium of .778 per $100 of construction costs is a not-to exceed figure and reflects an increase of 20% over last year, primarily due to the continued hardening of the property insurance market.  It is possible that the final rate from the insurance carrier may result in a 3 - 5% reduction.



The deposit premium is based on anticipated construction values of approximately $75.5 million in major construction and $1.3 million in covered walkway construction to be completed in the policy year.  Therefore, the full premium will be approximately $598,183, of which we pay 50 percent now or $299,092, with the balance subject to annual audit adjustment.  This premium has an additional State assessment of 1.3% of the billable premium for a total deposit due of  $303,000.



The broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., will receive a commission of six (6) percent on this policy.  In addition to placing the insurance, they will be responsible for tracking all payments, any changes relevant to this coverage and all reconciliations at the year-end.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the Board approve the purchase of the Master Builders' Risk Insurance Policy with Lexington Insurance through the broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., effective March 31, 2013 to March 31, 2014 at a premium cost of up to .778 per $100 of construction costs, for an expected premium of $598,183. The inital deposit premium will be  approximately $303,000.
