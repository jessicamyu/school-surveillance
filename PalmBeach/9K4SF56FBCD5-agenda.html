PR-1 Galaxy ES Master Naming Program
Meeting
Aug 10, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion


  • School Board Policy 7.03 allows assignment of naming rights for portions of school facilities to recognize private or corporate entities that make a significant contribution, financial or otherwise, to the District.


 





  • Individual building components may be named with the approval of the Superintendent, however naming of a school or creation of a master naming program requires School Board approval.


 





  • The Galaxy ES modernization project is scheduled in the adopted Five-Year Capital Plan to open in August 2013.


 





  • As indicated during the April 6, 2011, School Board workshop, the District aspires for Galaxy ES to be the first Platinum LEED Certified School in the State of Florida.


 





  • The naming program is part of a $2.3 million capital fundraising campaign.  The proceeds of the fundraising program will be used to cover the additional costs associated with the LEED Platinum certification along with educational enhancements to the school, such as the science-based "Wonderment Center."  As with most LEED certified schools, the additional upfront costs more than pay for themselves through enhanced energy efficiency, improved educational environment and teaching opportunites.


 





  • The Education Foundation will serve as the 501(3)(c) fiscal agent for the capital campaign.  To learn more about the Foundation, please visit www.EducationFoundationPBC.org.


 





  • As announced at the June 3, 2011 Galaxy ES Groundbreaking, the Quantum Foundation has generously agreed to contribute a matching grant of $750,000, contingent upon the District meeting its $2.3 milion capital fundraising goal.  The contribution is intended to be used for the Wonderment Center which would be named after the Foundation, in recognition of its contribution.


 





  • Since this is one of the first uses of the master naming program, the School Board may wish to direct staff regarding any changes to the process or to Policy 7.03.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve a Master School Naming program for Galaxy Elementary pursuant to policy 7.03.

Contact:

Joseph M. Sanches (joseph.sanches@palmbeachschools.org)



Kristin K. Garrison (kristin.garrison@palmbeachschools.org)



 

Financial Impact:

The fundraising goal for this project is $2.3 million to supplement the construction budget for Galaxy Elementary modernization.

Professional Crisis Management Association, Inc. (PCMA) provides technical assistance to the Department of Exceptional Student Education for students who exhibit very challenging behaviors.  PCMA reviews Functional Behavior Assessments (FBA's) and individualized Behavior Intervention Plans (BIP's) which district staff have completed.  Many of these students are at risk for residential placements.  If necessary, PCMA will role model and coach staff on the recommended strategies as stated in the student's Behavior Intervention Plan.  After observing the student, PCMA writes a comprehensive report outlining additional behavioral strategies which staff should implement, in order for the student to become more successful.  PCMA will also provide staff development to ESE teachers as requested by the ESE Department.  If necessary, PCMA will testify at Mediations, Due Process Hearings and IEP meetings.



PCMA will also recertify 15 PCMA Instructors

CLS1 - Boynton Beach High School IT Academy Agreement
Meeting
Aug 10, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category

Discussion

The School District of Palm Beach County is entering into an agreement with Moore Solutions, Inc. (MSI), a for-profit corporation, for the purpose of providing Information Technology Certification training to students attending Boynton Beach Community High School. Moore Solutions Incorporated (MSI) offers more services than a consultant/teacher. The relationship is more of a partnership with a team of IT training professionals that are dedicated to the success of the academy. Services and materials for the program include:



- The Instructor
- Student recruitment
- Program web site
- Brochures
- Curriculum
- Lesson Plans
- Rubrics
- Text books
- Custom Test preparation materials
- Student test remediation when needed
- A student internship program in third year

MSI also works as liaison between the school and the business community to give students much needed real world experience. MSI has IT Academy programs in high schools in Martin County, Palm Beach County (Riviera Beach Maritime Academy), Okeechobee County, and Saint Lucie County. In FY 2010 and FY 2011, Boynton Beach High School had no students pass the Advanced Technology Certification exams. MSI will provide the means by which student capacity can be increased in the area of advanced technology certification exams. MSI has proprietary curriculum criteria, including Advanced Technology Certification assessments for students. Students will benefit by earning college credits by passing Advanced Technology Certification exams. Through Tech Connections, MSI partners with the Workforce Development Board, Chambers of Commerce, economic developers, and local businesses to provide internships for secondary students. The school will benefit by having an instructor certified in Advanced Technology, an on-site testing center, and an advisory committee.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Moore Solutions, Inc. Agreement for Boynton Beach High School effective August 16, 2011 through July 30, 2012, and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all documents and contract related to the agreement.
