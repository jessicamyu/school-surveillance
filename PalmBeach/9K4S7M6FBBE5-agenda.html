PL-1 First Amendment to ILA Between PBC and the School Board regarding Drainage of Lawrence Road fronting the Royal Palm School Facility
Meeting
Mar 24, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)




  • The School District of Palm Beach County has committed to modernize the existing Royal Palm School in order to provide a more up-to-date and comprehensive facility to meet the special needs of its students.


 







  • The modernization of the school facility requires that improvements be made to the adjacent Lawrence Road as well as modifications to a portion of the school’s existing drainage system.  This modification to the drainage system will provide legal positive outfall for surface water from a segment of Lawrence Road.


 







  • The drainage pipe crosses School District property and discharges into an existing retention area.  In the absence of a drainage easement, the County wants to be assured that the School District will allow the uninterrupted flow of storm water discharge from the road through the District’s drainage system.  Additionally, the County wants assurance that the School District will not make any changes or modifications to the portion of the drainage system used for road drainage without prior written approval from the County.


 







  • The ILA to satisfy the County's concerns was approved by the School Board on November 11, 2009 and by the Palm Beach County Board of County Commissioners on January 12, 2010.


 







  • After the ILA was approved by both Boards, review of the document by the Palm Beach County Human Rights Council revealed that it did not contain the latest non-discrimination language.  This amendment to the approved ILA corrects that oversight.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the First Amendment to the Interlocal Agreement (ILA) between Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County pertaining to the drainage of a portion of Lawrence Road adjacent to the Royal Palm School facility and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

  • The School District Planning Department submitted a grant application to Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT) for non-infrastructure funds to assist students attending Dr. Mary McLeod Bethune and John F. Kennedy Middle School to walk and bicycle safely to school. 


 





  • There are approximately 900 students who reside within the 2 mile walks zone of these schools, with some students having to cross major roadways.


 



 





  • On January 26, 2010, the District received a letter from FDOT informing us that a grant in the amount of $45,000 has been awarded to fund non-infrastructure projects and activities that would assist students in walking and bicycling safely to school.  Activities planned include a Safety Fair, teaching the Florida Traffic and Bicycle Safety Education Program, providing training for parents, teachers and staff in ways to assist students in walking or bicycling to school safely and providing students with rewards such as helmets, bicycle bells, reflectors etc.  


 





  • School District planning staff will coordinate closely with the Transportation Department, Palm Beach County Traffic Department and the City of Riviera Beach Police Department in implementing the activities included in the grant application.


 





  • The School District has one year to expend these funds that will be awarded to us on a reimbursement basis.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Joint Participation Agreement and Resolution accepting the grant from the Florida Department of Transportation for non-infrastructure funds to encourage students to walk and bicycle safely to school and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

1.  The FY 2007-2011 Five Year Plan included the modernization of Royal Palm School, which was completed in 2010.



2.  Palm Beach County's Traffic Division required the construction of a right turn lane along Lawrence Road as a condition of approval for the site plan.



3.  As a result of the construction of the right turn lane, the existing sidewalk and fire hydrant along Lawrence Road had to be relocated.



4.  During construction over the 2009 winter holiday break the School District needed to relocate the fire hydrant 15 feet east on to School District property.



5.  The relocation of the fire hydrant during construction saved the School District time and money.
(See Exhibit "A")



6.  The fire hydrant and underground piping connecting to the hydrant is owned and maintained by the Town of Lake Clarke Shores, which necessitates the ratification of a twenty (20) foot utility easement. (See Exhibit "B")



7.  The easement area is fenced and is accessible from outside of the school fencing; therefore, Lake Clarke Shores' staff will not require access to the school.



8.  Lake Clarke Shores will continue to be responsible for all maintenance and repair of the fire hydrant.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board ratify a 20 foot utility easement along Lawrence Road at the Royal Palm School campus between the Town of Lake Clarke Shores and the School Board of Palm Beach County; and authorize the Chairman and the Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

PL-3 SunSmart E-Shelter Grant
Meeting
Mar 24, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)




  • The Florida Energy and Climate Commission (FECC) is the entity responsible for administering the state’s American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) funding related to energy. The State Energy Program includes two competitive programs that schools are eligible for: The SunSmart E-Shelter Grant and Small Matching Clean Energy Grant programs.


 





  • The SunSmart Grant Application process opened on March 1st and closes on April 12th. The Clean Energy Grant Program is not yet open for applications. As part of the new SunSmart Schools Emergency Shelter Program, each shelter school will be competitively selected to receive a minimum of 10 kW Photovoltaic (solar electric) emergency shelter power system. This system will feature a battery back-up that provides power to critical loads in the emergency shelter in the event of an electrical power outage.


 



·         In order to be considered for this program, schools must be an official Enhanced Hurricane Protection Area (EHPA) shelter. Palm Beach County has 24 official EHPA shelters. 



 



·        The Planning Department is working with 8 schools on an application for this grant: Atlantic Community High, Boca Raton Community High, Forest Hill Community High, Palm Beach Central Community High, Palm Beach Gardens Community High, Park Vista Community High, West Boca Raton Community High, and West Gate Elementary.



 



·         Approximately $10 million is allocated for the program and the state originally expected to fund approximately 90 schools statewide.



 



·         Palm Beach County represents 6.9% of the state's population and is located in a Coastal High Hazard Area. We are hopeful that the funds will be allocated proportionate to population, making us eligible for 6 plus grants if our applications are competitive.



 





  • The overall educational goal of this program is to increase the number of educators who teach the science and technology of renewable energy and therefore increase the number of students who are exposed to these concepts.


 





  • We will keep the Board informed regarding any of these grants that are received.


 





  • For more information on the E-Shelter Program visit the following link: http://www.fsec.ucf.edu/en/education/sunsmart/e-shelters/schools/index.htm


 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve applications for the SunSmart E-Shelter Program and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents, including the grant documents if awarded.
