LAC 1 - Successor Contract Agreements with the Service Employees International Union/Florida Public Services Union
Meeting
Dec 5, 2012 - Special Meeting 12-05-12
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

Consider ratifying (approving) the new three (3) year successor contracts with the three bargaining units (Regular, Supervisory, and Paraprofessional II's/Early Childhood Professionals) represented by the exclusive bargaining agent, Service Employees International Union/Florida Service Union (SEIU/FPSU).



These agreements:





  • establish new three (3) year successor contracts (January 1, 2012 - December 31, 2014) with two (2) year (2012 and 2013) wage agreements;

  • grant eligible bargaining unit members base salary increases for 2012 and 2013 ranging from 2.1% to 6% for School Bus Drivers, Bus Attendants and Food Service Workers; and from 1.4% to 4.8% for all other employees in the three bargaining units;

  • grant employees one-time bonuses ranging from $200 to $1000 payable in 2012 and/or 2013;

  • convert all salary schedules from a step structured configuration to a minimum-maximum configuration;

  • allow the District to reopen the wage agreements if 2014 educational appropriations are insufficient to fund the agreements or if the District incurs a financial liability as part of any final resolution of the Florida Retirement System (FRS) contribution litigation;

  • allow the School Board to issue contracting-out Requests for Proposal with prior notice to the Union and impacted employees;

  • prohibit employees from being subjected to unnecessary, spiteful or negative criticism in the presence of other employees, students, or the public;

  • allow extension of the new hire probationary period thirty (30) workdays, subject to prior notice to the employee;

  • establish an Alternative Grievance Procedure administered by an Alternative Grievance Committee composed of an equal number of management and employee representatives;

  • incorporate by reference the Safe Driver Standards and provisions of the School Board Policy 3.21;

  • establish a joint labor/management committee to review and make recommendations for revising the performance evaluation system and linking pay to individual/departmental performance;

  • limit an employee's right to appeal disciplinary action to DOAH to recommendations to suspend or terminate.


 



      

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the three-year (2012-2014) bargaining agreements with SEIU/FPSU Regular, Supervisory, and Paraprofessional Units and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all needed documents.

Van V. Ludy van.ludy@palmbeachschools.org

The total financial impact associated with this agreement, including associated fringe benefit costs, is estimated  at $3.8 million.  Of this amount, $2 million represents the recurring cost of base salary increases and associated benefits.  Non-recurring, one-time bonus payments and associated benefits make up the remaining $1.8 million.

PA1 - Adoption of 2013-2014 School Calendar
Meeting
Dec 5, 2012 - Special Meeting 12-05-12
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Submission of the School Calendar Committee's recommended 2013-2014 School Calendar to the Superintendent and the School Board for consideration and approval.



Background information leading to this recommendation:







  • The School Calendar Committee, comprised of parents, community leaders, District staff, principals and representatives of the employee Unions/Associations, met on November 2, 2012,to draft and make recommendations to the Superintendent for the 2013-2014 School Calendar.

  • The Committee recommends a School Calendar that sets Wednesday, August 14, 2013, as the first student attendance day.  This mid-week start date allows for a more gradual acclimation for both students and staff (especially for those students attending school for the first time or attending a new school) as the new school year begins.  It also allows for new employees to be scheduled to work at least fifteen (15) days in August so they will be eligible for insurance benefits on September 3 instead of having to wait until October 1 to be eligible for these benefits.

  • The recommended School Calendar establishes an Elementary Teacher Work Day at the end of each elementary trimester, a Secondary Teacher Work Day at the end of each secondary grading period, completes the first semester on Friday, December 20, 2013,  which is before winter break, complies with applicable provisions of all negotiated labor contracts relating to the School Calendar, and establishes Tuesday, June 3, 2014, as the last student attendance day of the school year.

  • The Calendar closes schools for students on the Veterans Day (Tuesday, November 11, 2013), provides for a three-day school holiday for Thanksgiving, a ten-day winter break, a six-day spring break and closes school for students and teachers on M.L. King's Birthday and Presidents Day.

  • The Calendar continues to provide 12-month employees with one "floating holiday" that can be used on any non-student attendance day after adequate notice is given and approval is granted.

  • Learning Team Meetings (High School late start days) and Professional Development Days (Middle and Elementary Schools early release days) will be added to this Calendar after input has been received from the Chief Academic Officer's staff concerning the dates on which these professional activities should be scheduled.

  • Emergency Make-up Days (EMDs) will be added to this Calendar as determined by the Superintendent and his leadership team.

  • Elementary and Secondary Report Card distribution dates will be added to this Calendar after receiving input from the FTE and Student Reporting Office.

  • The  "BEGIN" and "END" dates of the Employee Contract Periods are a function of the student start date and these dates will be added to this Calendar in recognition that the first student attendance day is August 14, 2013. 


Recommendation:

I recommend adoption of the 2013-2014 School Calendar.  This recommendation is important to the District so a timely setting of the District calendar can be available to stakeholders.

The Budget Control Act of 2011 includes a provision, known as sequestration, that would impose a $1.2 trillion (8.2%) across-the-board cut to almost all federal programs, including a cut of more than $4 billion to federal education appropriations.  These across-the-board cuts would become effective January 2, 2013, and would begin to impact school districts July 1, 2013 for the 2013-14 school year.



The potential sequestration funding cuts to Palm Beach County education programs include, but no limited to, more than $4.1 million cut from Title I, more than $3.2 million cut from Special Education, more than $600,000 cut from improving Teacher Quality, more than $400,000 cut from School Improvement, and more than $300,000 cut from English Language Acquisition.  Sequestration would also impact school construction by cutting federal payments by 7.6% for $67.7 million of Qualified School Construction Bonds issued by Palm Beach County Schools during the economic stimulus.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve this resolution urging Congress and the Administration to amend the Budget Control Act to mitigate drastic cuts to education funding.
