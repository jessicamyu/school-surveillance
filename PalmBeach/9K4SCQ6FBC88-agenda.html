  • The School District and the City of Lake Worth had a prior ILA for use of the City’s baseball field, known then as Dick Brown Colt Field, in 1992. 

  • The previous ILA was in effect for three (3) years.  It lapsed in 1995 with no update or replacement agreement. 

  • Certain items at the facility were in a state in disrepair and there was disagreement between the two parties as to which entity would be responsible for certain repairs. 

  • The new ILA clearly states the maintenance responsibilities of both parties and assures that the Lake Worth Community High School baseball team will be able to use the field for its athletic events.  The ILA will be effective for five (5) years after it has been approved by both entities. 

  • There are specific maintenance items that will be addressed in two (2) phases, ending in December of 2010.  The approximate cost for both phases of work by the School District is $200,000.00.  The ILA specifies a commitment by the School District of no more than $250,000.00 for maintenance during the two (2) phases.  The additional $50,000.00 is to cover any cost increases for materials or other contingencies.

  • The ILA went to the Board on April 8, 2009 and was approved.  Changes in Lake Worth administrative staff  delayed City approval.

  • In addition to minor text changes made for clarity, it was agreed that the School District would reimburse the City for its maintenance efforts the sum of $18,000 per year.  This change is reflected in Paragraph 3 of the ILA. 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the amended Interlocal Agreement (ILA) between the City of Lake Worth and the School Board of Palm Beach County to clarify maintenance issues and secure use of the field for Lake Worth Community High School during baseball season and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

NEW SUNCOAST HS ILA WITH RIVIERA BEACH FOR ROADWAY IMPROVMENTS



 



On January 27, 2010, the School Board approved an Interlocal Agreement between the City of Riviera Beach and the School Board for Roadway, Traffic, and Public Safety Improvements as it relates to the new Suncoast High School.



 



Also, on January 27, 2010 at a City of Riviera Beach Commission meeting that began at approximately 6:30 p.m., the City Staff presented same Interlocal Agreement to its Commission for approval.  Although the City of Riviera Beach Commission approved the language of the Interlocal Agreement approved by the School Board in its entirety, the Commission requested an additional paragraph be added that reads as follows:



 



The BOARD agrees to approve, within fourteen (14) days of the effective date of this Agreement, a resolution authorizing the immediate initiation of eminent domain proceedings pursuant to Chapters 73 and 74, Florida Statutes, including the acquisition by quick take of the real property interests required to fulfill the BOARD's obligations set forth in Paragraphs 3, 4 and 5 of this Agreement (the Real Estate Interests).  Both parties recognize that compliance with Section 73.015, Florida Statutes, is a prerequisite to the filing of an eminent domain petition to acquire the Real Estate Interests.



 



The Interlocal Agreement being presented includes the same language contained in the Agreement approved by the School Board on January 27, 2010, with the addition of two (2) paragraphs.  The first paragraph - paragraph 6 – pertains to the above referenced negotiated paragraph requested by the City of Riviera Beach.  District Staff has negotiated this paragraph and is in agreement with the wording.  The second paragraph – paragraph 14 – pertains to the Board required nondiscrimination provision.  The attached Interlocal Agreement has been negotiated with the City to memorialize the School Board’s agreement to construct the roadway and traffic improvements in order to get the City to issue the necessary water and sewer permits and perform the inspections.



 



The approval of this Interlocal Agreement by both parties resolves the dispute between the School Board and the City except with regard to the issue of the City’s liability for delay damages to be incurred by the School Board resulting from the City’s withholding of the water and sewer connection permits.  Since the permits have not yet been issued and it cannot be determined when the Construction Manager will complete the work on the water and sewer improvements, the parties mutually agreed to postpone the Chapter 164, F.S. Conflict Assessment meeting that had been scheduled for January 27, 2010.  Once the water and sewer connection permits are issued by the City and the work has been completed, the School Board will provide the City with the additional information regarding the delay damages suffered by the School Board.
