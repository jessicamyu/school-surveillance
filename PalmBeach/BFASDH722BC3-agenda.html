Subject
FMPF1 District Emergency Termination of Contract with Invictus, Inc.
Meeting
Aug 28, 2019 - 1. SPECIAL MEETING at 3:00 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
8. New Business
Type
Action
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the termination of the Contract with Invictus, Inc., based on the findings of the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office August 20, 2019 Memorandum.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County is in the process of implementing the mandates of SB 7030, Implementation of Legislative Recommendations of the Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School Public Safety Commission, specifically that all public schools have a Safe School Officer on campus during all hours that school is in session, otherwise referenced as “bell to bell."

 

As part of this Implementation, the School District of Palm Beach County entered into a contract with Invictus, Inc., to provide training to security guards hired by the School District of Palm Beach County to act as safe school officers at public schools in Palm Beach County.  Said training was contractually required to comply with the statutory requirements set forth in SB 7030, specifically Florida Statute 1006.12(4). 

 

On August 21, 2019, the School District of Palm Beach County was informed by the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office that the training program being provided by Invictus, Inc. to the security guard candidates was deficient in many areas under Florida Law.  These deficiencies included non CJSTC certified instructors or inactive certified trainers providing the training, passing candidates for firearms qualifications with 80 percent accuracy instead of the statutorily required 85 percent, inadequately documented curriculum and possibly passing candidates who had failed the statutorily required psychological testing.

 

Due to these significant deficiencies, the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office recommended that the Invictus security guard training be discontinued and declined to certify these security guard candidates to be deployed to Palm Beach County public schools as safe school officers. 

 

Whereas the training provided from Invictus, Inc. was contractually required to be statutorily compliant, the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office has found it deficient and recommended it discontinued, and therefore the School District of Palm Beach County must now utilize alternative training for the security guard candidates prior to their assignment at Palm Beach County public schools, Invictus, Inc. has breached the contract for security guard training, and it should be terminated immediately.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County is in the process of implementing the mandates of SB 7030, Implementation of Legislative Recommendations of the Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School Public Safety Commission, specifically that all public schools have a Safe School Officer on campus during all hours that school is in session, otherwise referenced as “bell to bell."

Due to these significant deficiencies, the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office recommended that the Invictus security guard training be discontinued and declined to certify these security guard candidates to be deployed to Palm Beach County public schools as safe school officers.  As an alternative to the Invictus, Inc. security guard training, the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office offered on August 23, 2019 to provide training to the School District of Palm Beach County security guard candidates.  Upon successful completion of the Palm Beach Sheriff Office’s security guard training, the candidates shall be certified by the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office to be assigned as safe school officers at Palm Beach County public schools, as required by the Florida Department of Education for compliance with the mandates of SB 7030.

 

The District shall enter into a memorandum of understanding with the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office to clarify the details of the training and the responsibilities of the Palm Beach County School District and the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office.
