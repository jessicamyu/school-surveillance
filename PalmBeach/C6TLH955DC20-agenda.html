Subject
School Board Member Residence Area Redistricting Workshop
Meeting
Oct 6, 2021 - 3. WORKSHOP immediately following Closed Session in the Board Room
Category
3. Workshop Items
Type
Discussion

DESCRIPTION:

  • Redistricting data released by the US Census Bureau on August 16, 2021, indicates that redistricting in 2021 is required due to disproportionate growth within the School Board Member Districts, a 12.31 percent difference between the largest and smallest Districts. (See Census 2020 Population Summary by Existing (2011) Districts, below)
     
  • School Board Member Redistricting may only be adopted in odd-numbered years (F.S.§1001.36(2)) and District School Board Member Residence Areas “shall be equal in population as nearly as practicable.”
     
  • Board Member Redistricting does not impact school attendance zones in any way.
     
  • On May 5, 2021, the School Board held a Workshop to discuss School Board Member Residence Area Redistricting. The primary purpose of the workshop was to discuss the severely compressed timeline due to late release of the 2020 Census Redistricting data, five months later than normal. The delays were caused by data collection consequences of COVID-19.
     
  • On August 18, 2021, the Board adopted seven criteria to be used for the redistricting, as follows:

  1. Achieve equal population among the districts

  2. Adhere to Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act of 1965, as amended and interpreted through case law

  3. Preserve communities of interest, including municipal boundaries where reasonable

  4. Follow natural and man-made boundaries

  5. Geographic compactness

  6. Contiguous districts, unless geographic exception required 

  7. Preserve the core of existing districts

  • Staff has developed a Scenario that best balances the adopted criteria and guidance from our Outside Counsel, Jerry Wilson Esq., and his associates.
     
  • All Redistricting information is being posted on the pbcredistricting.info, which also provides a vehicle for emailing public input to Redistricting@palmbeachschools.org. The Communications Department has developed a leveraged Communications and Public Input Plan.
