I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with Youthful Balance Medical Center, LLC, effective June 18, 2020 through June 30, 2025; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Palm Beach Gardens High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

  • Student records be timely updated into SIS.
  • Edgenuity Final Grade Sheet (PBSD 2361) be reviewed and approved accordingly prior to updating the final grades into the SIS System.  A periodic summary report should be generated for school principals' monitoring to ensure that all the required parties have signed off for the grade changes.

3. Policy 7.18, Community Use of School Facilities, requires that the Superintendent or designee present to the Board for its approval a District-wide rate schedule for the use of school facilities on a non-profit and commercial basis. The policy also requires that any changes to the rate schedule be approved by the Board.

4. To save the District funds, the proposed rates for Commercial and Non-Profit leases are based on an in-house Facilities Rent Study.

5. Based on this in-house study and the current economy, staff is recommending the following minor procedural and rate changes to be effective on July 1, 2020:

 

● To bring School District facility leasing rates in line with other comparable facilities within Palm Beach County, we are recommending slight increases to our rate schedule, $6 for Commercial and $3 for Nonprofit per leased space.

● A two-hour minimum for all spaces leased during weekends and holidays, when there is no residual air conditioning from the school day.

● All facilities listed on the lease must be leased for the same number of hours. For example, if the Cafeteria and 2 classrooms are leased for an event, all three spaces must be leased for the same number of hours, for maintenance and security reasons.

 

6. If the staff sees a need for changes in the rate schedule, based on the economy and the market conditions, utility costs, or other changes in conditions, staff will bring back any proposed changes for Board approval.

The child care programs are for the babies and toddlers of teen parents who are students of the School District of Palm Beach County. The major goal is to provide a comprehensive Teenage Parent Program consisting of educational and ancillary service components.

The School District will document that all children referred by the Teenage Parent Program staff are placed in child care in accordance with the terms of the contract and will review documentation of payments to child care providers.

 

The School District will provide funds for this program from FTE generated by the children of teenage parents who are enrolled in the Teenage Parent Program.

The AVID College Readiness System is a catalyst for developing a school culture that closes the expectation and opportunity gaps many students face and prepares all students for success in a global society.

AVID Excel is a middle school program that is intended to accelerate ELL students’ academic language acquisition and put them on a pathway to high school AVID and college readiness.

The School District of Palm Beach County’s AVID participation has increased from six high schools in 2009 to 66 sites in 2019-2020 at the elementary, middle, and high school levels and 11 schools in the planning phase.

Approximately 879 AVID students have successfully graduated since the first graduating class in 2012. Of the 339 AVID seniors in 2018-2019:

● 100% graduated on time

● 66% plan to attend a four-year college

● 14% plan to attend a two-year college

● 2% plan to attend a technical/trade school

● 4% plan to join the military

● 98% graduated “college-ready”

● 99% took both the SAT and ACT

● 67% took at least one AP/IB/AICE exam

Over 1,000 teachers and 100 administrators and District staff have been trained in AVID’s strategies and methodologies at Summer Institutes, 2-day Path Training, AVID Professional Learning Workshops, and AVID District Leader Training. A sustainable support system has been established, as the District now has five AVID Center Staff Developers.

These agreements will allow the School District of Palm Beach County to implement AVID’s curriculum and use the AVID logo in all 66 sites. The Agreements include AVID membership, curriculum materials, and implementation support.
