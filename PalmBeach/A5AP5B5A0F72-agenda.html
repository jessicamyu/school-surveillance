Subject
A4-1 Jupiter High School - Enviromental Research and Field Studies Academy - Trip to Panama
Meeting
Jan 20, 2016 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
14. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Out-of-Country Travel for an educational field trip for Jupiter High School, Environmental Research and Field Studies Academy, to travel to Panama on March 18-24, 2016 (Spring Break).

DESCRIPTION:

Twelve Jupiter Environmental Research and Field Studies Academy (JERFSA) students (11th and 12th graders) will be travel to Panama in support of their studies.  The trip will take place during Spring Break 2016 when school is not in session.  Only Juniors and Seniors who have met their testing requirements are eligible to participate in this trip.  The trip takes place in the rainforest of Panama. This experience will allow students to explore, learn and document wildlife, ecosystems, and conservation efforts in Panama.  Instructors from the Asocion para la Conservacion de Naturaliza (ANCON) will guide the students' travel and studies. Students will reside in facilities managed by ANCON.  Included in this trip will be: visits to various natural areas, cultural sites and archeological locations.

The benefits to the school and District include:

  • Ecological, geological, and cultural instruction for students;
  • Broadening of personal experience for students;
  • First-hand experience in a variety of natural ecosystems, geological and cultural settings that will augment instruction in Biology, Ecology, AP Environmental Science, and Environmental Science classes.

CONTACT:

David Christiansen, Ed.D, Deputy Superintendent/Chief of Schools (david.christiansen@palmbeachschools.org)

Camille Coleman, Area 4 Superintendent

Dan Frank, Principal, Jupiter High School

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The cost of the seven-day trip is $1,785.50 per student.  This covers airfare, ground transportation, lodging and the majority of the meals.  ANCON covers all in-country costs and fees for two chaperones for a group of this size. Chaperones airfare costs will be covered by donation for the District employee and personal funds for the spouse of the District employee. No student will be deprived of the opportunity to participate due to financial hardship.  The Academy Parent Group works to acquire financial assistance for students in need.  Additionally, fund-raising activities are held throughout the year to assist in cost reduction for students.
