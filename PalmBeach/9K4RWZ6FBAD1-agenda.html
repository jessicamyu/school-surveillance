LA1 - Amendment to the 2011-2012 School Calendar
Meeting
Feb 23, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

The 2011-2012 School Calendar adopted on December 15, 2010 contains an inequity that can be easily corrected.  The School Calendar for 2011-2012 has 47 student days during the first nine-weeks grading period, but only 35 student days during the second nine-weeks grading period.  This amendment will make these two grading periods more equal in length by moving up the date for the end of the first nine-weeks grading period to Thursday, October 20, 2011, by moving up the “Teacher Work Day/In-Service Day” to Friday, October 21, 2011 and by moving up the start date of the second nine-weeks grading period to Monday, October 24, 2011.  When adopted, this amendment will result in a more equitable 42 student day first nine-weeks grading period and a 40 student day second nine-weeks grading period.



 



Originally, Friday, October 28, 2011 was to be a “Teacher Work Day/In-Service Day” in deference to CTA whose state affiliate, the Florida Education Association (FEA), has its State Convention on Friday, October 28 and teachers who may be attending as delegates would not need a substitute that day.  However, when the calendar proposal was changed to complete the first semester prior to winter break, the first two secondary school grading quarters became unbalanced thereby giving rise to the calendar amendment before the Board for its consideration.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the attached amendment to the 2011-2012 School Calendar.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore (Joseph Moore); Van V. Ludy (Van Ludy)

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the FY2011 General Fund Budget is -0-.

WestlawNext is one of the primary online legal research services for lawyers and legal professionals in the United States. AC1 - SAT Agreement and Data License Amendment with the College Board
Meeting
Feb 23, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

·         The SAT Agreement with the College Board provides the School District of Palm Beach County with the ability to administer the SAT to:



o   Students on April 27, 2011, during the school day, as a means to assist these students in preparing and planning for success in college. 



o   All 11th grade students



o   All 12th grade students who have not met graduation or college readiness requirements as an alternate means of fulfilling those requirements.



·         The products provided by this Agreement include the following:



o   The SAT for approximately 8,633 students



o   The Official SAT Online Course which includes 18 interactive lessons, six official practice tests, personalized score reports, and instant scoring and feedback, including automated scoring of the essay portion.  College Board is waiving the customary fee of $5.50 per student, resulting is a cost-savings of over $47,000.



o   SAT Skills Insight a comprehensive online tool showing students and educators the specific academic skills that are tested on the exam, using real SAT test questions. The program offers individualized support and practice for students as they prepare to take the SAT. 



o   Professional development on SAT Skills Insight, the Official SAT Online Course and school-based test supervisor and registration coordinator roles and responsibilities.



o   A tailored communications package to support the District, schools, students and parents.



o   A Data License Agreement that will provide for SAT student data sharing between College Board and the District to:



§  Enable the District to incorporate College Board Data into its analysis and Educational Data Warehouse systems to improve college readiness.



§  Demonstrate to the District the impact of student preparation (measured by the difficulty and rigor of students’ high school curriculum) on SAT scores and overall college readiness.



§  Assist the College Board in building new Palm Beach County District/State reporting services for future initiatives; such as the development and validation of a scale that measures the difficulty and rigor of students’ high school curriculum. The College Board and the District will work collaboratively to determine how the research will be used for future initiatives in Palm Beach County.



§  Provide professional development to District teachers receiving and reviewing student SAT score data.



·         A Data License Amendment allowing the same SAT student data sharing as described above from the FY2010 SAT.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the SAT Letter of Agreement and the FY2010 Data License Amendment with the College Board and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to each.
