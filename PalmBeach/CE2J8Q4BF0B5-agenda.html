I recommend approval of the 2022 Comprehensive Safety Inspection Report

DESCRIPTION:

Annual Comprehensive Safety Inspections were performed by the Building Code Services Fire and Safety Inspectors as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). All educational, auxiliary and ancillary facilities were included in these inspections, using the State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) for casualty and sanitation, and Florida Administrative Code Chapter 69A-58 for fire safety.

 

The number of citations for Public Schools, Alternative Schools and Ancillary Facilities increased from 1,097 in 2021 to 1,289 in 2022. This represents a 17% increase over one year.

   

The citations are identified by “type”

  • Type O = Operational.  This is the responsibility of the school staff to correct
  • Type M = Maintenance.  This is the responsibility of the Maintenance Department to correct
  • Type C = Capital.  This is the responsibility of Facilities Construction to correct.
  • Serious Citations are identified with an added “S” to the Type  

 

The number of Capital citations went down while the number of Maintenance and Operational citations went up.   All conditions considered by the inspector to be hazardous or posing an imminent danger were reported to the Maintenance and Plant Operations Department at the time of discovery for immediate action.

 

A summary report by facility, detailed individual facility reports and descriptive charts are attached. According to SREF, Chapter 5, the inspection reports shall be approved by the Board, and the Board shall adopt a plan and a schedule for the correction of each deficiency.

 

Upon the Board approving the inspection reports, copies of the reports will be submitted to the local fire protection agencies as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(2). The Board hereby certifies the completion of the inspection report to the State Fire Marshal.

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the revised Interagency Agreement effective June 16, 2022 through January 31, 2027, and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the agreement which are required for implementation of the Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The multi-year interagency agreement has been signed to better meet the needs of children in foster care. Every Florida school district received a directive from the state agency to develop an Interagency Agreement based around a model to coordinate services for children in the Florida child welfare system.

The Interagency Agreement includes:

  • The School Board of Palm Beach County;
  • Agency for Persons with Disabilities, State of Florida, Southeast Region;
  • CareerSource Palm Beach County, Inc.;
  • ChildNet, Inc.;
  • Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, Inc.;
  • Florida Department of Children and Families, Circuit 15;
  • Florida Department of Juvenile Justice, Circuit 15; and
  • Southeast Florida Behavioral Health Network.

This Agreement coordinates the parties’ respective responsibilities for furnishing educational and vocational services and supports for children known to the Department of Children and Families “who are being served through ChildNet while remaining in their own homes, and those who have been placed by ChildNet or by order of the court in a foster family, boarding home, group home, agency boarding home, child care institution, or any combination thereof, and who must receive education and related services before, during and after the ages of compulsory school attendance.”

The “purposes” of the Agreement are to promote collaboration “to 1) ensure educational access and related care, including post-secondary pursuits, promote job training and employability skills and facilitate the delivery of services or programs to children known to the department; 2) avoid duplication of services or programs; and 3) combine resources to maximize availability or delivery of services or programs.”
