This is a renewal of the Cooperative Agreement between Youth Services International (YSI) and the School Board of Palm Beach County. This program is a high risk residential secure facility, located in West Palm Beach; FL. Palm Beach Juvenile Correctional Facility is a 98-bed residential treatment facility for male offenders between the ages of 13-18. Youth Services International (“YSI”) operates the program for the Department of Juvenile Justice. This facility is considered a Level 8 (high risk) residential facility.   • Students receive career counseling and career skills training which improves their chance for employment

May 12, 2015 - 3. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Elementary and Secondary School Counseling Grant from the U.S. Department of Education in the amount of $1,194,744, effective for three years from May 1, 2015 through April 30, 2018; approve any no-cost extension as applicable; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded the Elementary and Secondary School Counseling Grant from the U.S. Department of Education in the amount of $1,194,744 for three years from May 1, 2015 through April 30, 2018. This grant project will fund development, implementation, dissemination, and evaluation of a Middle School Counseling Project at the following schools:  Bear Lakes, Conniston, Roosevelt, and Palm Springs Community Middle Schools.

 

Within the framework of the Multi- tiered Systems of Support, the project will build infrastructure and increase capacity for a sustainable counseling delivery system by:

  • adding a certified school counselor to each school  
  • changing  counseling practice to align with recommendations of the American School Counselor Association (ASCA)
  • increased integration of counseling services with School-wide Positive Behavior Support efforts

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Educational Plant Survey.

DESCRIPTION:

  1. A formal and complete Educational Plant Survey (Survey) is required by the Florida Department of Education (DOE) to be conducted every five (5) years.  The last Survey was approved in 2010.
  2. Authorization for the Survey is specified in Article IX and Article XII of the Florida Constitution; Chapters 1001, 1011, and 1013, Florida Statutes (F.S.); State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF); and the Florida Building Code.
  3. The purpose of the Survey is to assist District staff with recommendations for housing the educational activities of the students and staff of the School District in the future.  It is a needs assessment which compares what exists at a facility, as listed on the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH), to what an educational or ancillary plant is allowed to have based on the latest Facilities Lists (DOE Facilities Lists for generic elementary, middle and high schools are attached).
  4. The Survey is a planning tool to assist in identifying future projects in the Capital Plan based on District needs.
  5. As part of the Survey process, DOE conducted a FISH validation of approximately 10% of District owned facilities.  The facilities reviewed by DOE staff showed that the sample facilities were within 98% accuracy with the FISH data. (See attached DOE letter)
  6. DOE Table II (attached) is the summary of the individual evaluations of each facility with respect to student capacity, projected student capacity and a recommendation of the final student capacity at each site within the next five years.
  7. DOE Table III (attached) is the financial data of all the facility recommendations within the first five years of the Survey based on State formulas and requirements. 
  8. The Ten Year Capacity, Infrastructure and Maintenance tables and the Twenty Year Capacity, Infrastructure and Maintenance tables are also included as part of the long range planning of the Survey and are based on the draft District Capital Facilities Plan.
  9. Modernizations of facilities are not included in the Survey since this will be completed through individual Surveys after a scope of work is approved for each facility to be modernized.
  10. The Survey can be amended at any time based on need.
