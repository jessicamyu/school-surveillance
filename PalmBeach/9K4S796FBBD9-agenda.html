This agreement provides authority for the Prevention Partnership for Children to fund the Beacon Center Initiative afterschool Scholarships at Northmore Elementary and West Gate Elementary from October 1, 2009 through December 31, 2009. Funds will be received at the District level by the Department of Afterschool Programming and will then be appropriated to the school center Afterschool (SACC) programs to scholarship students as follows:



Northmore - 80 students



West Gate - 50 students

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Prevention Partnership for Children School Age Child Care (SACC) Agreement for the Department of Afterschool Programming in the amount of $74,484.80, effective October 1, 2009 through December 31, 2009, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to the grant which are required for implementation of grant activities.

AC2 - College Board Advanced Placement Achievement Institute
Meeting
Sep 30, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion

§        The purpose of the Advanced Placement (AP) Achievement Institute, October 2-5, 2009, is to assist educators in meeting the increasing demands that academically diverse Advanced Placement students pose for teachers and administrators.



 



§        It is designed to support schools committed to preserving open access to AP by identifying strategies (for both administrators and teachers) to overcome potential barriers to the success of students who may have been under-prepared for the rigorous expectations of an AP course.



 



§        Participating schools will send a complete team to the training, since the focus will be on planning and developing the college readiness culture of the school, to include:



o        Principal



o        College Readiness Coordinator/Assistant Principal



o        All Advanced Placement English Language Arts and Social Studies teachers teaching (or being groomed to teach) AP classes.



 



§        Teachers will learn strategies for differentiating instruction without sacrificing rigorous expectations. 



 



§        Administrators will learn: strategies for strengthening an AP program by building departmental ownership of a pipeline of courses built on rigorous expectations and vertically aligned curriculum; and how to staff and create Advanced Placement gateway courses that are intentionally designed to introduce students to the AP experience. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the  Advanced Placement Achievement Institute Agreement with the College Board in the amount of $80,000, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation of the Agreement activities.

Contact:

Jeffrey Hernandez       Jeffrey.Hernandez@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



Alison Adler

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the Special Revenue-Other Funds budget is $80,000.  This funding will be provided by the Advanced Placement Incentive Program Federal Grant.  There is no impact to the Operating budget.
