DESCRIPTION:

  1. The School District owns approximately 800 buses and recently leased 110 new buses.
  2. The Transportation Department's intent is to wash the buses it owns semi-annually and the 110 leased buses will be washed quarterly, as required by the Lease Agreement.
  3. The School District does not own adequate bus washing facilities to efficiently and as required by the lease agreement.
  4. Palm  Beach County (Palm Tran) owns and operates three bus washing facilities: one in West Palm Beach, one in the Delray area and one in the Glades area (please see exhibit A) and has capacity available to wash School District buses.
  5. The County's bus washing facilities will be available for use by the School District five days per week, Monday through Friday from 8 a.m. to 3 p.m. After 3 p.m., the bus washing facilities are being used by Palm Beach County for its own buses.
  6. The County's fee for washing a bus shall be eighteen dollars and fifty six cents ($18.56) and shall be paid quarterly to the County.  This initial fee includes cost for new cameras to be installed at the facilities for accountability.  Upon renewal of the Agreement, the bus wash fee shall be updated to reflect the County's actual cost for washing the buses at that time.
  7. The Agreement will be for an initial three years with an option for an additional three-year extension.
  8. The Agreement shall become effective after the Palm Beach County Board of County Commissioner's approves the Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

This Board Agenda item provides for Amendment 6 to the Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and the Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University.

 

The Objective of the Boynton Aerospace Science Academy (BASA) is to introduce selected high school students to collegiate and professional trade-level study with a focus on professional aeronautical topics leading to both high school and college credits for courses completed.

 

BASA course taught by ERAU faculty will be transferable to appropriate ERAU academic programs, in accordance with the guidelines in force in the ERAU Undergraduate Catalog at the time the individual students matriculate into the ERAU system.

 

ERAU will offer two curriculum tracks at BASA. The first, or Academic Track, will be a curriculum of introductory courses in a range of aviation career areas, including flight operations, weather, air traffic management, safety, business, unmanned aviation, and others, depending on student demand and the availability of qualified faculty. The second, or Aviation Maintenance Science (AMS) Track, will provide students with the General portion of the FAA Airframe and Power plant Mechanics Certificate curriculum.

The goals of the program include increasing knowledge of character traits. Gulf Stream Council will also provide its supplemental career awareness program and leadership and life skills development workshops to students in the Academy. I recommend the School Board approve the Agreement with Habitat for Humanity of Palm Beach County, Inc., effective September 18, 2014 through June 30, 2015; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign any and all required documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This is an agreement between Habitat for Humanity of Palm Beach County, Inc. (HABITAT) and the SCHOOL BOARD whereby HABITAT and the Construction Academy at Seminole Ridge Community High School will work together to provide HABITAT with a pre-fabricated, modular house unit to be delivered to and installed onto a foundation to be provided by HABITAT.

 

The pertinent provisions of the Agreement are as follows:

  • SCHOOL BOARD AND HABITAT will jointly develop architectural drawings (no cost to SCHOOL BOARD) for the house(s) that will be completed with the understanding that the house(s) will be constructed in 4 parts: complete, rectangular modular units consisting of 4 walls each, a structural wood framed floor and ceiling joists so that the interior of the units can be completely finished and painted prior to shipping the modular units to the property.  The interiors will be fully plumbed and wired, inspected, drywalled, and fully painted.  Cabinets will be put in place, plumbing fixtures installed, doors, trim, and hardware installed, air conditioning, heating unit (AHU) in place (no ductwork), toilet accessories installed, windows and doors installed and trimmed on the inside.  Each modular unit will be fully wired and electrical covers installed on switches and receptacles.  All will be inspected during the rough-in stage during construction at the school site.  The electrical systems for the separate modular units will be connected together by HABITAT at the Property.  The roof will be built in sections of pre-fabricated wood trusses with plywood sheathing and then dried in, ready for shipping and assembly as whole roof sections onto the house boxes at the property.
  • HABITAT will provide all necessary materials and disposable tools to the school for the proper construction of the modular house units in a timely manner.
  • HABITAT will pay for the on-site services of the project electrical contractor, plumbing contractor, and air conditioning contractor for their respective work at the property for making all final connections of their systems at the assembled House at the Property.
  • HABITAT will provide the SCHOOL BOARD with a payment to reimburse for the electrical tools, equipment, and supplies consumed by the SCHOOL BOARD’s process of constructing the modular units (i.e. saw blades, broken drills, drill bits, damaged nail guns, etc.)  Said payment shall be paid by HABITAT upon the SCHOOL BOARD’s presentation of invoices for replacement tools, parts, service, and disposable tools and to not exceed $1,000 per house for the term of this contract.
  • Each student working on this Project is covered by the SCHOOL BOARD’s insurance policies, unless HABITAT allows the student to work on the Project on HABITAT property.  If that were to occur, HABITAT shall either provide insurance to cover the students or obtain waivers of insurance from the parents of the students involved with construction on HABITAT property.
  • The start of construction of the modular house shall commence one day after the issuance of a single family home construction permit.

To meet the School Community Professional Development Act, Florida Statute 1012.98, the Department of Professional Development has established a standards-based process of quality expectations which is derived from Learning Forward (NSDC) standards.  These guidelines are provided by the Florida Professional Development System Evaluation Protocol, and SBE Rule 6A-5.071.  The Professional Development System is aligned with Florida’s Professional Development Evaluation System Protocol for high quality professional learning.

 

Florida’s Professional Development System Protocol outlines the critical relationship between professional learning and the school improvement process. The following are the district guidelines for high quality professional learning: 

  • Professional learning is organized in a cohesive plan and sustained over time.
  • Student achievement is the focal point of professional learning.
  • Utilize multiple data sources to identify professional learning needs.
  • Professional learning is directed toward transformation versus information.
  • It is intended to impact job performance and student achievement.
  • All four areas of planning, learning, implementing, and evaluating must be addressed when designing high quality professional learning.
  • Professional learning requires participants to engage in sustained job-embedded learning that is centered around the issues and challenges they encounter on a daily basis.
  • In-service points toward recertification are only offered for opportunities that incorporate the high quality professional learning practices in the areas of planning, learning, implementing, and evaluating.

I recommend the School Board approve the Technical Assistance Specialists Program Agreement with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. for the period October 1, 2014, through September 30, 2015, and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents and contracts.

DESCRIPTION:

This renewal Agreement with Prevention Partnerships for Children, Inc. provides $1,523,398 to the School District for the period effective October 1, 2014 – September 30, 2015, for the continuation of the Quality Counts Technical Assistance Specialists program.  The technical assistance program is one of a number of components that work together to create a comprehensive, voluntary, early care and education quality rating improvement system for Palm Beach County.  The system partners work together to provide services to enhance the quality of early education programs so that children will enter school eager and ready to learn.  System partners include Children’s Services Council, Palm Beach State College, Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, Family Central, the Children’s Forum and the School District.

 

This grant funds 14 technical assistance specialists and related support/administrative staff to serve child care centers and school-based sites that voluntarily participate.  To be eligible to participate, child care centers must have been licensed for at least one year and must hold a rate agreement with the Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County.  All School District classrooms offering the Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) program are eligible to participate.

 

The technical assistance specialists assist with the implementation of improvement plans and strategies developed through collaborations between classroom teachers, child care directors, and/or other administrative supports.  Plans are based on formal and informal assessments administered by highly trained outside assessors.  The current assessments provide for the award of a “Star Rating,” ranging from 1 to 5 stars.  The lowest rating, a 1-Star rating, equates to the attainment of current state and local child care licensing standards. 

 

The Quality Counts System has approximately 165 participating private child care centers and public school sites.  The District is both a partner and a voluntary participant; most participating school-based sites have earned the highest possible star rating.
