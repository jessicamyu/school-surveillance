PS1 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Jamila Smith-Lee

Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Jamila Smith-Lee's termination from employment, effective Friday, March 27, 2015, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about November 19, 2014, Jamila Smith-Lee became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Failure to Provide for the Health, Safety, or Welfare of Students, Failure to Abide by the School Bus Drivers and Attendants Handbook, Failure to Abide by Florida DOE Rules Regarding the Safe Operation of Buses, Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment, and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.  

 

These actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.02(4)(a),(4)(b), (4)(j), and 3.02(5)(a)(vii), Code of Ethics; 3.21(1), (3)(f), (3)(l) and (4), Safe Operation of District School Buses; 5.186(4)(a), Student Transportation Conduct; 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff;  Chapter 1, Section (2)(a)( Bullet 1) and ( Bullet 6), Section (3)(a), Section 4(c) and Section (4)(d), School Bus Driver and Bus Attendants Handbook.

 

There is “just cause” to support Jamila Smith-Lee’s termination of employment. 

 

On February 10, 2015, the “Notice of Recommendation for Termination From Employment” was hand-delivered to Jamila Smith-Lee from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Keith Oswald, Interim Chief Academic Officer

Sandra L. Gero, Chief of Human Resources

Vincent L. Caracciolo, Director, Professional Standards

There is no financial impact to the School District.

Mar 4, 2015 - 3. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room

Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Libby Stroud's termination of employment, effective Friday, March 27, 2015, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about November 3, 2014, Libby Stroud became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Failure to Protect the Learning Environment, Failure to Fulfill the Responsibilities of a Teacher, Ethical Misconduct, Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment, and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive. At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.  

 

These actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 0.01(2)(c), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; 2.34, Records and Reports; 3.02(4)(a), (4)(c), (4)(f), (4)(h), and (4)(j), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment; 1.013(1) and (4), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 1008.22(1)(a-e), F.S., Student Assessment Program for Public Schools; 6A-10.080(2) and (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; 6A-10.081(3)(a), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession; The Collective Bargaining Agreement with CTA, Article II, Section U, Lesson Plans. 

                        

There is “just cause” to support Libby Stroud’s termination of employment. 

 

On February 11, 2015, the “Notice of Recommendation for Termination From Employment” was hand-delivered to Libby Stroud from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.
