DESCRIPTION:

Overview

On May 27, 2017, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from South Tech Success Center, Inc. to open South Tech Success Center charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August 2018.

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument. South Tech Success Center, Inc. either “Meets the Standard” or “Partially Meets the Standard” in all 20 sections of the application.

 

Projected Enrollment

First Year 9-12 125 Projected Enrollment

Second Year 9-12 192 Projected Enrollment

Third Year 9-12 253 Projected Enrollment

Fourth Year 9-12 308 Projected Enrollment

Fifth Year 9-12 500 Projected Enrollment

 

Education Program Design

South Tech Success Center (STSC) is committed to creating a school environment which enables students to succeed in spite of previous academic, social, and emotional struggles. STSC recognizes the need for secondary schools to provide these students with the motivation and support needed to earn a standard high school diploma. Moreover, schools need to facilitate the transition for students from graduation to viable careers and high-paying jobs. The educational program at STSC is specifically designed to meet the needs of a diverse population of students who have a record of low performance, may have special educational needs, and are clearly at-risk of not meeting their graduation requirements. Instructional personnel will be highly qualified and committed to working with these students to achieve the school's core mission: to maximize each student's individual capacity by graduating students prepared for work, higher education, and productive citizenship.

 

Location

The projected charter school site is in Boynton Beach, FL.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Memorandum of Agreement with Florida Department of Health, Palm Beach County, effective August 1, 2018 - July 31, 2019. The Parties may renew the Agreement annually, if agreed upon by both parties in writing, but such renewals shall not extend beyond July 31, 2023. This Agreement is contingent upon authorized appropriation of the funds under a final fiscal budget approved by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

DESCRIPTION:

A Memorandum of Agreement/Understanding is a requirement of the School District’s application to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention for Promoting Adolescent Health Through School-Based HIV Prevention ("CDC Grant"). The District has been a recipient of this collaborative grant for 20 years-four 5-year cycles. During this time, teen pregnancy and other high risk behaviors have decreased. The new application is due April 13, 2018. Award notification will be provided in July, 2018.

 

The Agreement outlines a Plan to continue to implement the grant’s prevention education, services, and activities to students in middle and high school.  The Parties may renew the Agreement annually, if agreed upon by both parties in writing, but such renewals shall not extend beyond July 31, 2023. This Agreement is contingent upon authorized appropriation of the funds under a final fiscal budget approved by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
