PS 1 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Sandra Rhategan
Meeting
Aug 4, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

A special Audit of Del Prado Elementary School was conducted by the District Auditor's Office in November 2008 and August 2009, which concluded that the PTA had been sponsoring fee based clubs between 2:15 pm and 3:30 pm and paying stipends to the school employees who proctored the clubs during this time.  Essentially, non-instructional employees who participated in these clubs were receiving stipends in addition to their hourly wage from the School District for work conducted during the regular business hours.  Following the Special Audit, Sandra Rhategan became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation regarding allegations of failure to exercise best professional judgment and violation of school Board Policies.  The specific allegations include potential violations of School Board Policy 1.014, Responsibilities of Principals, School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff, School Board Policy 2.121, Student Activities in the School, School Board Policy 2.62, Audit Recommendations, School Board Policy 7.18, Community Use of School Facilities, and School Board Policy 3.29(2), General Standards of Appropriateness.  Additionally, there were potential violations of 6B-1.001, (3), of the Florida Administrative Code, The Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida.  As Principal, Mrs. Rhategan is responsible for the leadership of the entire school staff and managing the financial affairs of the school.  Based on the investigation, Ms. Rhategan seriously neglected her duties and abrogated her responsibilities as Principal of Del Prado Elementary School.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.  These acts are grounds for the termination of her multi-year contract of employment for instructional supervisors and administrators and demotion to the position of Assistant Principal or the equivalent level thereof.



Based upon substantial information gathered throughout the investigation, there is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations.

Recommendation:

I recommend to the Board that Sandra Rhategan, be demoted from her current position as Principal of Del Prado Elementary School to an Assistant Principal or the equivalent level thereof.

Subject
AC1 - Memorandum of Understanding with the Community Partnership Group (d/b/a Parent Child Center, Inc. & Housing Partnership, Inc.)
Meeting
Aug 4, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Substantial research literature has emerged documenting the fact that the transition into high school is marked by increased disengagements and declining motivation, particularly for low-performing youth (National Research Council, 2004).



Community Partnership Group (d/b/a Housing Partnership, Inc. and the Parent-Child Center, Inc.), in conjunction with the Department of Safe Schools, are combining resources to provide targeted mentorship and mental health counseling services to approximately 21 high-risk youth at JFK Middle School who have just completed 8th grade and will be entering Palm Beach Lakes High School.



The proposed pilot program is designed to deepen the identified students' knowledge and skills, increase their social and emotional probelm-solving capabilities, and enhance their feelings of competence, self-determination, and connectedness with supportive others. The goal of the program will be 100% successful transitions, as indicated by students who feel a sense of connectedness and belonging, who are engaged in classroom learning, and who are able to cope with daily stressors.  In addition to providing social and emotional support for students and families, staff will closely monitor students throughtout 9th grade to track progress and provide continual individual support.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Memorandum of Understanding for the Transition to High School Program and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Memorandum of Understanding which are required for implementation of activities.
