Read Together Program-The Adventures of Charlie Pierce: The Last Egret-A Collaborative Endeavor
Meeting
Aug 17, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Discussion
As part of a countywide “Read Together” program for children and families, the Historical Society of Palm Beach County distributed 14,000 copies of the Adventures of Charlie Pierce: The Last Egret to the School District for use in fourth-grade classrooms.  The Program was made possible through the financial support and partnership with the Wachovia Wells Fargo Foundation.  The book chronicles the childhood adventures of Charlie Pierce, who ultimately became one of the barefoot mailmen.  It also describes the environmental disaster that occurred in South Florida when plume hunting led many pioneers to slaughter birds for their exotic feathers, which were then used to decorate ladies’ hats.  The “Read Together” program, which began November 1, 2010, is a collaboration between the Wachovia Wells Fargo Foundation, School District of Palm Beach County, The Education Network (T.E.N.), the Arthur R. Marshall Foundation, author Harvey E. Oyer III, and the Historical Society of Palm Beach County.  The Department of Elementary Curriculum has written more than 20 lessons to support teachers in providing instruction as their students read The Last Egret.  All public elementary schools as well as charter and alternative elementary schools received copies of the book.

The National Association of Biology Teachers (NABT), in conjunction with Prentice Hall Publishing, has honored Stephen M. Anand from the Dreyfoos School of the Arts with the 2011 Outstanding Biology Teacher Award. This honor, given annually since 1961, identifies a teacher from each of the United States, Puerto Rico, and Canada who has made valuable contributions to the profession and to students. Criteria for the award include teaching ability, experience, creativity, initiative, inherent teaching strengths, and cooperation in the school and the community. He has also been named the Outstanding Biology Teacher in the State of Florida. Mr. Anand currently teaches Advanced Biology and Honors Biology as well as serving as varsity soccer coach.



Mr. Anand is a member of the National Association of Biology Teachers, Florida Association of Science Teachers, NEA, FEA, CTA, JAMA, and several organizations through MIT. The popular teacher coordinates a yearly Marine Research Development Trip to Key Largo for his students and has also taken groups of students to Europe.

Subject
School Food Service-USDA Best Practice Award For Increasing Meal Participation At Lunch Through Program Entitled "Nutritious Dishes"
Meeting
Aug 17, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Discussion
The School Food Service Department has been recognized at the State and the Southeast Regional levels with United States Department of Agriculture Best Practice Awards in the Increasing Participation in School Lunch or Breakfast category.  The receipt of these awards acknowledges the district’s on-going efforts to provide high-quality nutrition services to the children of Palm Beach County.
 
In 2008, the District School Food Service initially partnered with HealthCorps to create a marketing plan that educates and empowers teens to make healthy choices. Using this creative marketing plan and a “nutritious dishes” serving line, students were involved in weekly taste tests and meetings to determine what nutritious foods they would like to have on their menu. A dedicated reimbursable line was created to serve regular menu items in such a way that a healthier nutritional value is offered to students each day.
 
The marketing program and serving line has been successful in getting students excited about eating healthier meals.  Included in the offerings are a variety of entrée salads, fresh fruit bowls, wraps, Yogurt Parfaits, milk and juice, and healthy snacks.  Schools currently participating in the program are Atlantic High, Boynton Beach High, Forest Hill High, John I Leonard High, Santaluces High, Wellington High, and Independence Middle.

Sue Alex, Department of Exceptional Student Education-Audiology and Speech-Language Pathology State Award
Meeting
Aug 17, 2011 - School Board Meeting

Sue Alex, Program Planner in the Department of Exceptional Student Education was recently honored at the 2011 Florida Speech-Language-Hearing Association Convention, held on Marco Island, FL.  Ms. Alex was the recipient of the esteemed Rhonda Work School Clinician of the Year Award in recognition of her exemplary commitment and contribution to the delivery of Audiology and Speech-Language Pathology services in Florida's Public Schools.  Earning this award qualifies Ms. Alex to be Florida's nominee for the American Speech Language Hearing Association's Van Hattum Award, which was established to advance the work of school-based professionals in serving persons with communication disorders.



Ms. Alex is the epitome of clinical excellence in her current administrative role for Speech, Language, Deaf, Hard-of-Hearing, Dual Sensory Impaired, Audiology, and Pre-Kindergarten Programs for Students with Disabilities.  She oversees all aspects of services for more than 15,000 students who meet eligibility within the above mentioned categories.  She provides clinical support to more than 300 Speech-Language Pathologists (SLPs) within the School District.



In addition to her School District responsibilities, she has served as Chairperson for the Gold Coast Consortium which represents SLPs in Palm Beach, Broward, Miami-Dade, and Monroe Counties. Ms. Alex was recently honored at the Edinboro University 21st Annual Alumni Conference as "Alumni on the Move".  She earned both her Bachelor and Master degrees in Speech-Language Pathology from Edinboro University in Pennsylvania and has been employed in her chosen profession for over 20 years.

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA) of 2004, a mandated Federal Law, requires that the School District provide services to private school students with disabilities via a set-aside of a proportionate share of the IDEA grant.  The proportionate share amount is determined by the Florida Department of Education.



In order to comply with this requirement the School District provides services as outlined in the attached Standard Cooperative Agreement.  Services are provided to students with disabilities based on the Services Plans written by the District.  357 students accessed services during the 2010-2011 school year.



To provide these services at the private school site, a cooperative agreement must be entered into between the school and the District.



The two attached agreements are in addition to the  42 agreements previously approved by the School Board on August 25, 2010. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Standard Cooperative Agreements and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to these agreements.  These agreements shall be in effect from August 22, 2011 through June 30, 2013.  The agreements are renewed yearly for the two year period, if both parties agree.

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(6)(g), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the district and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.



The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Florida High School for Accelerated Learning - Palm Beach Campus d/b/a Seacrest High School to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2011.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on February 2, 2011 during a Special Board Meeting. 



The Charter Contract for Florida High School for Accelerated Learning - Palm Beach Campus d/b/a Seacrest High School was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff.



The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2011 and ending June 30, 2016, except as provided in the charter.  Florida High School for Accelerated Learning - Palm Beach Campus d/b/a Seacrest High School has elected deferred opening.  Accordingly, the School's opening shall commence with the 2012 school year.  The first year of the Contract Term shall serve as a planning year.



Florida High School for Accelerated Learning - Palm Beach Campus d/b/a Seacrest High School will serve grades 9-12, ages 16-21.



Florida High School for Accelerated Learning - Palm Beach Campus d/b/a Seacrest High School's mission is dedicated to changing and saving lives and "creating success stories, one student at a time" by developing productive citizens and life-long learners through an integrated, technology-enhanced, rigorous, and relevant curriculum that is customized to the individual needs of students who will drop out of high school or have already dropped out, and need an alternative approach to earn a high school diploma.



A copy of the Charter Contract and Budget of Florida High School for Accelerated Learning - Palm Beach Campus d/b/a Seacrest High School is attached.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the five (5) year Charter for Florida High School for Accelerated Learning - Palm Beach Campus d/b/a Seacrest High School and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

Judith Klinek/(klinek@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)
