I recommend the Board approve the ratification of purchases exceeding the previously approved expenditure with Sage Corporation.

DESCRIPTION:

The ratification of purchases exceeding the previously approved expenditure for the Sage Corporation Contract and revised financial impact for the remaining term of the Contract. The SAGE Corporation operates Class A Truck Driving courses at West Tech. This program provides students with the opportunity to explore careers in the CDL Class A trucking industry and be job-ready to begin a career in driving Class A Tractor Trailers after successful completion of the course and related one-on-one driving experiences on the road with a SAGE instructor. The SAGE Corporation provides all trucks, instructors, curriculum, classroom instruction, one-on-one driving experiences, and assists with marketing the program and provides job placement services.

 

On November 20, 2019 (CAO-2) the Board approved an expenditure of $145,080 for the services provided by Sage Corporation. However, the agenda item did not reflect the appropriate total cost of the three-year contract which is $435,240. 

 

Services/scholarships provided this year exceeded the previously approved financial impact of $145,080 by $34,470. To comply with FDOE requirements that all invoices be paid by August 31 and avoid the $34,470 being disallowed by the grant, a purchase order was issued on August 26, 2020, which exceeded the previously approved amount of $145,080.

I recommend the Board to approve Adult Education and Family Literacy Adult General Education Grant from the Florida Department of Education, effective July 1,2020 through June 30, 2021.

DESCRIPTION:

The Adult Education and Family Literacy-Adult General Education Grant is a grant in the amount of $1,972,807. The program performance dates are July 1, 2020 to June 30, 2021.

 

The priorities of this grant are to assist adults to become literate, to obtain knowledge and skills necessary to become full partners in the educational development of their children, assist adults attending a secondary school diploma or equivalent, and assist immigrants to improve their reading, writing, and speaking skills.

 

Adult Education programs are provided at 26 adult and community schools, one full time adult education center, one adult virtual academy and numerous satellite programs located throughout Palm Beach County. These programs are offered at various times throughout day and evening in order to accommodate student needs and schedules.

 

Students progress will be measured by tracking student movement from one Educational Functioning Level (EFL) to the next in a given content area during the projected year based on the results from assessments that have been approved by the USDOE and FLDOE and administered in a pre and post-test. An educational gain may also include students in ABE Level 5 who are awarded academic credit. It also includes students who attained a high school diploma or equivalent.
