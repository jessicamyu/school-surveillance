Subject
AC1 - School Health Services Plan 2010-2012
Meeting
Apr 13, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion




  • The School Health Services Plan is prepared and reviewed by the Department of Health (DOH) and the Department of Education (DOE) to clearly identify each of the statutory requirements that relate to mandated basic student health services in all public schools.




  • "Column 1 - Statutory Requirements" and "Column 2 - Program Standards" are mandatory to this Plan and established by DOH and DOE to establish the primary requirements and mandates for school health.  Chapter 64F-6.002 F.A.C. requires the Plan to be completed biennially.  Therefore, you are receiving the 2010-2012 Plan at this time.




  • During this biennial time frame, the School Health Services Plan is submitted to local school health agencies to provide strategies that will be used to implement the services mandated by law in "Column 3 - Local Agency Responsible" and "Column 4 - Local Implementation Strategy & Activities". 




  • Working together, the County Health Department (CHD), School District (LEA), Health Care District (HCD), and School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) identify and determine the responsibilities for providing the school health services described by DOH and DOE.  This is a collaborative process to define the activities and services provided based upon each agency staffing models, funding sources, and community partnerships.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the School Health Services Plan 2010-2012.

FM1 - Approve the Negotiated Agreements for Underwriter Services
Meeting
Apr 13, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

On February 3, 2011 Invitation to Negotiate, ITN 11C-014C for Underwriter Services was solicited.  Twenty responses were received.





  • The Evaluation Committee, consisting of District staff and a member of the Finance Committee, convened and reviewed all responsive submissions.  It was the recommendation of the Evaluation Committee to award the contract to two (2) Sr. Managers and four (4) Co. Managers

  • J.P. Morgan Securities, LLC and Morgan Stanley & Co. are being recommended as Sr. Managers.

  • Bank of America Merrill Lynch, Citigroup Global Markets, LLC, RBC Capital Markets, LLC and Wells Fargo Bank, N.A. are being recommended as Co Managers.

  • Debt issuance dollars is unknown at this time.  Unlike the last two contract periods, no "new money" transactions are anticipated over the term of the contract.  At this time, only three complex restructuring transactions are anticipated over the term of the contract.  Other transactions may be recommended as needed.  Each transaction will be individually reviewed by the Finance Committee and subsequently presented to the School Board for approval. 

  • The Finance Committee reviewed the ITN before it was released.  The Finance Committee also reviewed the recommendation of the selection committee during their meeting on April 5, 2011.  The Finance Committee recommended that staff should present the agreements to the School Board for consideration with a vote of 4 - 0 with one member abstaining due to a conflict and two members absent.

  • The contract period will be from April 21, 2011 through April 20, 2014.

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the Negotiated Agreements for Underwriter Services and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

Gulf Oils Spill Multi-District Litigation
Meeting
Apr 13, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Discussion

At the April 6, 2011 special meeting, the Board voted to postpone any action on this issue for one week.  Chief Counsel and special counsel will report any new information.



 

For an anticipated 5 to 10 minutes, the Board will further consider, discuss and possibly vote on whether to join in potential multi-district litigation and to become a claimant due to a reduction in sales tax revenue and other losses as a result of the Gulf oil spill.  This would involve the retention of special counsel, including the law firm of Morgan & Morgan, as stated on the attached proposal, to file a claim on the Board’s behalf in the pending suit before April 20, 2011.  Special counsel may also file any other claims as outlined in the proposal.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve action stemming from the Board discussion.

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Chief Counsel
