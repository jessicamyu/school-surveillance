I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County approve the application of Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On August 1, 2013, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014.  This academy will be an extension of the current Palm Beach Maritime Academy Charter School.

 

The mission of Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School is, “To provide a community of support and guidance for students in grades 9-12 by engaging students in competitive career academies that create innovative learning opportunities for all students to be successful in careers and college, through partnership with families and community resources.”

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.  Below is a summary of the key components of the application.

 

Projected Enrollment

 

            First Year                 9-10                  808 Projected Enrollment

            Second Year             9-11                1350 Projected Enrollment

            Third Year                9-12                1850 Projected Enrollment

            Fourth Year              9-12                2125 Projected Enrollment

            Fifth Year                 9-12                2000 Projected Enrollment

 

Education Program Design

Their educatonal program will include AICE/AP, Global Business and Entrepreneurship, Maritime Studies and Environmental Science Studies.

 

Location

Their site has not been acquired.

 

Applicant’s Founding Governing Board

Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School will be governed by a board with at least one person having a background in business/finance, one person with background in education, one parent of current student and two to four persons from the community who can contribute to the areas of Leadership, Communications, Fundraising, and Governance.

 

Ratings of Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School’s Application 

Palm Beach Maritime Academy High School “Meets the Standard” in the required 19 sections.

Projected Enrollment

FMT2 Qualified School Construction Bonds
Meeting
Oct 2, 2013 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
11. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board adopt a Resolution authorizing the addition of Pioneer Park Roof Replacement Project and Belle Glade Elementary Roof Replacement Project to the Schedule 2010A to the Master Lease and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and execute all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • This Resolution authorizes the addition of the roofing projects at Belle Glade Elementary and Pioneer Park Elementary to the Qualified School Construction Bonds.
  • In November 2010, the School Board issued Qualified School Construction Bonds (COPS Series 2010A) to finance the construction of Galaxy Elementary School and Gove Elementary School. 
  • Qualified School Construction Bonds are a financing instrument created by the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act.  Through the program, the District was able to borrow over $66 million at a very low interest rate.  At the same time, the District locked in the maximum investment rate for the escrow accounts.  If all terms are met and there is no further negative impact from sequestration, the District will need to repay only $62 million rather than the $66 million that was borrowed.
  • A key term that must be met is to spend all monies prior to November 15, 2013.  Staff has worked diligently to ensure the projects are completed.  It appears the final payments will be made after the deadline and the projects are coming in under budget.  After much analysis, it appears that $4.6 million will not be spent on time.
  • If the monies are not spent, the District would be required to re-issue the bonds.  There would be significant costs associated with that process as well as damage to the District’s credibility in the marketplace.  No other District in the country has been required to take such an action.  Instead, School Districts have added other projects to the QSCB Program to ensure the monies are spent.
  • It is important to note that remaining funds can only be spent on construction, renovation or remodeling projects that are bid in compliance with the Davis-Bacon act.  This act requires all wages associated with the project be in compliance with federal wage rates.  Furniture and equipment can also be purchased for the facilities with construction projects that are funded with the Qualified School Bonds.
  • In anticipation of possible delays, the District bid, already funded, two roofing projects in compliance with the Davis Bacon Act.  The two roofing projects are at Belle Glade Elementary and Pioneer Park Elementary. 
  • The two roof replacements were already funded, so $2 million of the $4.6 million to be spent will be swapped from another funding source. 
  • The remaining $2.6 million from the Gove and Galaxy projects will be spent for furniture and equipment at the four schools based on need and number of students at each school.
  • Belle Glade Elem                             $565,235                             
  • Pioneer Park Elem                           $671,314
  • Galaxy Elem                                    $568,532
  • Gove Elem                                       $879,919

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The financial impact is a transfer of $2,685,001 from restricted reserve to the four construction projects, as shown above, to ensure all funds within the Qualified School Construction Bond fund are spent prior to the deadline of November 15, 2013.
