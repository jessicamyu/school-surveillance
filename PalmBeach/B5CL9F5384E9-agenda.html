DESCRIPTION:

Maureen Carter is the District's K-5 Social Studies/Holocaust Program Planner in he Division of Teaching and Learning. She is the recipient of the 2018 Robert I. Goldman Award for Excellence in Holocaust Education, awarded by The Jewish Foundation for the Righteous (JFR). This award recognizes an educator who has demonstrated excellence in teaching the Holocaust. Maureen was chosen specifically because of her strong work in classrooms throughout the District, and her commitment to furthering her own knowledge of the Holocaust.

 

The award is named for Robert I. Goldman, a founding member of the JFR, who served as Vice Chair of the Foundation and Chairman of its Education Committee until his death in August 1998.  In tribute to his commitment to Holocaust education, the Foundation established this award in his name. As the recipient of this award, Maureen will receive a cash prize of $1,000 and she will be honored at a dinner on Monday, December 3, 2018, in New York City.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Data Sharing Agreement with Best Foot Forward effective November 14, 2018 through October 30, 2020; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The purpose of the agreement is to provide participant data to Best Foot Forward. The Data Sharing Agreement supports Best Foot Forward who in turn supports foster care youths' goals to graduate from high school.  Their mission is to empower foster care youth to maximize their potential by providing essential academic support and guidance.  Best Foot Forward pairs students with an Academic or Graduation Coach from kindergarten to college.  Through one-on-one support, each Coach provides a range of educational planning and monitoring to ensure academic success.  By working together as a team, Coaches provide students with the necessary resources to graduate from high school with a plan for their future.  Without dedicated guidance and support, children in foster care face unimaginable academic adversity due to a lack of basic skills, frequent changes to home and school placement, and emotional upheaval.  In fact, only 46 percent of youth in foster care graduate high school and less than 3 percent graduate college.  Best Foot Forward firmly believes that educational achievement is the greatest counterweight to these challenges.
