On or about March 6, 2009, Jill Shadoff became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon allegations of ethical misconduct and failure to exercise best professional judgment.  Furthermore, the allegations include potential violations of School District Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff and 3.96 (4)(b) Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy and violations of 6B-1.001, (3) Florida Administrative Code, The Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and 6B-4.009(2) Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal and the CTA Collective Bargaining Agreement, Article II, Section M (6) - Rights and Responsibilities, Discipline of Employees (Progressive Discipline).



On March 5, 2009, Jill Shadoff was arrested by the Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office and was charged with Drug Trafficking.  On August 28, 2009, Jill Shadoff entered a plea agreement in which she plead guilty to one (1) count of attempted trafficking in Oxycodone, which is a second degree felony.  Adjudication was withheld and Ms. Shadoff agreed to undergo a substance abuse evaluation; submit to random drug testing; complete 100 hours of community service and serve three (3) years of probation.



Ms. Shadoff's conduct is inconsistent with the standards of public conscience and good morals.  It is conduct that is sufficiently notorious to bring the individual concerned and the education profession into public disgrace.  Her crimes are those of immorality and moral turpitude.



There is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Jill Shadoff's suspension without pay, effective December 3, 2009.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Jill Shadoff's employment effective December 18, 2009 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

G1 - Robert Wood Johnson Foundation Healthy Kids, Healthy Communities Grant - EMERGENCY ITEM
Meeting
Dec 2, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Wkshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Healthy Kids, Health Communities Grant is for four years and a total of $360,000 focusing on obesity among the Hispanic community.  A steering committee representing a broad spectrum of stakeholders selected the Lake Worth-Palm Springs-Greenacres "corridor", a locally known section of Palm Beach County.





  • The deciding factor for selecting the Lake Worth-Palm Springs-Greenacres area was the readily available resources, the stakeholder commitment to address the identified needs, and articulated priorities of these communities which are as follows: (1) improving land and safer communities that foster physical activity; and (2) increased access to affordable, culturally acceptable nutritious foods to encourage healthy eating.

  • The PBC Healthy Kids, Healthy Communities partnership currently has 15 partners.  Others have been part of the planning process and will be part of the implementation process.

  • One of the overarching goals for this initiative is to build on the success of the District's wellness promotion efforts which implement policy, system and environment changes within the context of the school day.  With that as a foundation, there is a strong public and private sector committed to creating a community of wellness that complements the school-based initiatives by engaging families and diverse community sectors in fostering healthy eating and active living opportunities for our youth.

  • The long-term goals for this initiative are to maintain an effective public/private partnership, to serve as a catalyst for action, and to increase access to healthy food and active living opportunities for low-income families.

  • Proposed strategies are to: (1) Support county and city comprehensive plans, (policies and goals that promote recreation facilities, bike lanes/paths and pedestrian safety). (2) Establish inter-local agreements to allow after-hours use of schools' recreation facilities throughout the target area. (3) Promote community safety that encourages children and families to participate in outdoor physical activity and recreation. (4) Establish community gardens, farmer's markets, and other retail initiatives, including partnerships among food retailers, local farmers and community garden programs to increase access to fresh fruits/vegetables for vulnerable children and their families.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board accepts the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation Healthy Kids, Healthy Communities Grant for four years effective December 3, 2009 in the amount of $360,000 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are implementation of the grant activities, focusing on obesity among the Hispanic community.



Staff advises that this item was added to the agenda after the first publication because:





  • On November 20, 2009 the School District of Palm Beach County was asked by the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation to sign their grant Letter of Agreement.


  • In order to start the grant on December 15, 2009, the individuals listed on the Letter of Agreement must sign it and return one original signed copy to the address below no later than December 4, 2009.


  • If the Letter of Agreement is received by the Foundation after the deadline, then the start date would be be delayed.


  • The School District's Legal Services department has reviewed and approved the Letter of Agreement received by the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation.


  • Many partners that collaborate with the School District's Wellness Promotional Policy have pledged good cause financial contributions to help promote healthy eating habits and physical activity.

The purpose of this confirming cooperative agreement is to provide a highly restrictive, therapeutic educational/residential facility for a student with a disability whose aggressive and impulsive behaviors have escalated to the point that the student requires crisis stabilization and a diagnostic evaluation in order to determine the appropriate educational services and placement.



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in this particular case due to the time it takes to secure a bed in a residential facility.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.



This contract is presented to the Board to provide placement in the recommended facility.



Prior to placement in the residential facility the student was receiving ESE services at Crestwood Middle School.



The student has been identified as Emotionally/Behaviorally Disabled and Speech Impaired by the School District of Palm Beach County in compliance with state and local statutes and regulations.



The cooperative agreement is in compliance with State Board Rule 6A.6-0361, "Special Programs Contractual Agreements with Non-Public Schools".

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the confirming cooperative agreement with SP Behavioral, LLC, DBA Sandy Pines Hospital in the amount of $18,200 for the educational and residential costs for a student with a disability from October 28, 2009 through December 18, 2009, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.

There is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations.
