Subject
CLS1 - 2010-2011 School Improvement Plans
Meeting
Jan 12, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Florida Statute 1001.452 and School Board Policy 2.09 describe the requirements of the school advisory council (SAC). In accordance with Florida Statute 1001.452 and School Board Policy 2.09, the 2010-2011 school improvement plans were developed, reviewed, and approved by the SAC members at each school. The plans are based on needs assessments that reflect current student achievement data from multiple data sources. Each plan includes objectives with strategies that are focused on reading, writing, mathematics, and science achievement.



The 2010-2011 School Improvement Plans, saved to the State's online template system, as well as the School Advisory Council bylaws, can be viewed at:



http://www.palmbeachschools.org/SchoolImprovement





  • Plans are developed by school staff and business and community members and include a review of the data for school grade (A+ System), Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP), and trend data.

  • The school improvement plans include professional development aligned with objectives, contain training and instructional materials, technology, staffing, student support services, school safety, and other matters of resource allocation.  The plans also address Single School Culture and an appreciation of multicultural diversity.  SACs determine the use of school improvement funds.

  • Title I plan requirements are addressed through the inclusion of the federally required Components and Elements (attachment).

  • Each school improvement plan meets or exceeds the indicators of District Accreditation High Quality Standards.

  • To ensure compliance with federal, state and local policy and law, an analysis of the (a) Area Superintendent's confirmation receipts    (PBSD 2250) recognizing the respective school improvement plans and bylaws; (b) Schools 2010-2011 school improvement plan checklists (PBSD 2248); (c) School improvement plan of each school; and (d) SACs bylaws of each school indicate 100% of the schools in Palm Beach County continuously work toward exceeding the requirements of Florida statute and School Board Policy.

I recommend that the School Board accept and approve the School Improvement Plans as developed by the School Advisory Council at each school.
