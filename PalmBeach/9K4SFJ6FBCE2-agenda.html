Special Meeting re: C.M. v. PBCSB Settlement
Meeting
Mar 3, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action

In 2009, the parents of C.M., a minor child with disabilities, filed an administrative Due Process Complaint with the Florida Department of Administrative Hearings, under the provisions of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act and Florida law.



 



By School Board agreement, C.M. was residentially placed at the Devereux School, Viera, Florida, in March 2008, which is still C.M.’s educational placement.



 



The Due Process Complaint alleged that certain IEPs at Devereux School denied C.M. an appropriate education, and that the District failed to properly evaluate C.M. and make him eligible in all areas of disability.



 

After completion of the Due Process Hearing and subsequent to School Board approval of settlement authority, the parties engaged in mediation. Counsels’ subsequent discussions led to the Settlement Agreement.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Settlement Agreement in the case of C.M. v. The School Board of Palm Beach County (DOAH Case No. 09-1799E). I recommend the School Board authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all related documents.

Contact:

Gerald A. Williams, Esq.; Chief Counsel



Steven E. Lake, Esq; Senior Counsel

Financial Impact:

The financial impact of this settlement is Thirty Seven Thousand Dollars and No Cents ($37,000.00), to be paid from the District Legal Settlement Fund.

Special Meeting re: 05-C Fees and Costs
Meeting
Mar 3, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action

In September 2007, School Board authorized eminent domain on a 20 acre ± parcel in unincorporated western Boca Raton.  This parcel was identified as the site for the new 05-C Elementary School. 



 



The eminent domain suit was filed in June, 2008. 



 



On April 8, 2009, the Board directed the Chief Counsel’s office to dismiss the eminent domain suit.



 



School Board staff was able to negotiate compensation with the landowner’s law firm and each of the landowner’s expert witnesses except for one.  This partial settlement was approved by the Board in November 2009.  Following an evidentiary hearing on January 14, 2010, the Board was ordered by the Circuit Court Judge to pay the landowner’s surveying and engineering company $61,527.50, which the Board approved on January 27, 2010, resolving all expert witness fees associated with this case.



 



The final remaining item in this case is the attorney’s fees and taxable costs payable, pursuant to Section 73.092(2) Florida Statutes, to the landowner’s law firm, Forman, Hanratty & Montgomery, for their handling of the expert witness fee negotiations and evidentiary hearing.  Through negotiations, the parties have reached an agreement to pay the landowner’s attorney a lump sum of $21,000 for attorney’s fees and taxable costs.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the payment pursuant to Section 73.092(2) Florida Statutes to Forman, Hanratty & Montgomery in the amount of $21,000 related to the lawsuit styled School Board of Palm Beach County v. E.F. Johns, Ltd., et al, Case Number 502008CA019824XXXXMBAA and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all necessary documents.

Gerald A. Williams, Esq.; Chief Counsel
