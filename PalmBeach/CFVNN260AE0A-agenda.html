I recommend the Board approve the renewal of the Master Affiliation Agreement Programs FIAT with Florida Atlantic University effective for FY2022 through FY2023.

DESCRIPTION:

Florida Atlantic University and its regional partners, including the School District of Palm Beach County, have created an innovative approach to prepare College of Education majors to be well-trained and highly qualified teachers upon graduation and entry into the teaching profession by renewing the contract starting July 1, 2022 – June 30, 2023.

 

Good FIT (First Introduction to Teaching) increases the number of education majors by providing freshmen, sophomores, and others interested in teaching the opportunity to provide instructional support with model classroom teachers. This program increases the number and quality of students entering and remaining in the field of education and teacher preparation. Undergraduate students receive extensive classroom experience and intensive multi-year support from retired educators and mentors as well as a minimal stipend.

 

The Accelerated Induction into Teaching (AIT) Program fills high-need teacher vacancies with selected university seniors who are ready to student teach as the actual classroom teacher with daily support from a mentor. The District’s priorities call for intensive and targeted recruitment strategies to ensure the availability of highly qualified teachers for high-needs schools. The AIT Program is an initiative that provides such teachers

Subject
PD2 Memorandum of Understanding - Florida State University Educational Leadership Program
Meeting
Aug 3, 2022 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the FHESC Boardroom
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve this Memorandum of Understanding between The School Board of Palm Beach County and the Educational Leadership Program, College of Education, Florida State University, to outline components within the Partnership Alliance that the two parties mutually support and further develop a cooperative relationship that will increase the quality of applicants in FSU’s Educational Leadership program and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to sign all needed documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Alongside a growing body of research that outlines program features in quality leadership preparation programs is the need for collaboration among universities like FSU that house leadership preparation programs and the school districts where aspiring leadership candidates will eventually be employed. The accountability expectations are: for districts to hire highly effective school leaders that can improve student achievement and for leadership preparation programs to scale up their commitment to the districts they serve as they prepare qualified candidates to enter district leadership pools. This is a clear recognition of the impact that university/district partnerships can have on preparing high-quality leaders that will increase student achievement and advance school improvement.   

 

The elements agreed to in this Alliance provide for the institutionalization of the partnership with the goal being to promote leadership preparation opportunities at FSU that prepare aspiring school leaders to be employed as high-performing entry-level school leaders and to work collaboratively to enhance leadership development initiatives within the district.
