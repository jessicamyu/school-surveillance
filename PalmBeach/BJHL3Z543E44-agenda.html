Dianne Rivelli-Schreiber has created a truly school-wide green culture at Calusa Elementary and inspires her team every day.

Subject
COM2 Second Place Top Green Schools Award
Meeting
Jan 22, 2020 - 4. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
9. Presentations
Type
Presentation, Recognition

DESCRIPTION:

H.L. Johnson Elementary was the 2nd place award winner out of 129 schools in 2019 in the Green Schools Recognition Program. The Program encourages schools to develop cultures of sustainability and environmental stewardship in their classrooms, on their school grounds, and throughout their communities. It is coordinated by the Pine Jog Environmental Education Center of Florida Atlantic University in partnership with the School District of Palm Beach County and the Martin County School District, and is now in its 12th year of growing green schools in the area. Participating schools develop and document their green initiatives each year and apply to earn Green Schools recognition and become eligible for special awards.

 

Over the past seven years, H.L. Johnson Elementary has worked to enhance all aspects of school sustainability. This past year, they put a special emphasis on solid waste reduction. They also made providing outdoor learning a top priority, using Google Forms to gather data and strategize about how to optimize use of their outdoor learning spaces, including their Lady Bug Lounge, Garden Club Garden and Chickee Hut. Sixty percent of teachers reported using these areas on a regular basis.

Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the purchase of the Master Builders Risk Insurance Policy with Everest Insurance Company through the broker, Beacon Group, Inc., effective March 1, 2020 to March 1, 2023

DESCRIPTION:

The Master Builders Risk policy will cover damage to the property under construction, as well as theft of materials from the site during the construction phase for new and replacement schools/buildings.  This policy covers all facilities started and scheduled during the policy period.  The policy period is proposed to be March 1, 2020-March 1, 2023.

 

The Master Builders Risk Policy eliminates the need to pay contractors for a project-specific, stand-alone, builders risk policy.  In years past, a similar policy was purchased by the District, which reduced expenditures because the contractors' rates for this coverage are much higher per school than what the School Board can obtain on its own by insuring all the projects under one policy.  The premium for each project is based on many variables, such as construction type and what construction takes place during hurricane season.

 

The proposed Master Builders Risk policy will have a 3-year term.  It will have a per project maximum value of $75,000,000 (which is sufficient to cover the planned new construction projects), with a non-windstorm deductible ranging from $5,000-$25,000 per loss (deductible varies based on project size).  Named windstorm losses will be subject to a three (3) percent deductible ($100,000 minimum).  Since the District will now own the policy, rather than the contractor, the District will be eligible for FEMA reimbursements for deductible payments.  Contractors, on the contrary, are not eligible for FEMA assistance. 

 

There is a deposit premium of $10,000 due in order to bind coverage, and then all future payments will be due at the time the individual projects commence.  The policy was underwritten based on anticipated construction values of approximately $340 million over the next three years, which was provided by Facilities Management.  A comparison was done between current contractor provided builders risk policies and the proposed master builders risk policy.  It is anticipated that the School Board will reduce its Builders Risk insurance expenditure by as much as $1.7 million over 3 years, as compared to what it would be paying contractors for similar coverage.

 

The broker, Beacon Group, Inc., will receive a commission of ten percent of the policy premium.  In addition to placing the insurance, they will be responsible for tracking all payments, any changes relevant to this coverage, and all reconciliations at year-end.

DESCRIPTION:

The School Board of Palm Beach County Florida will develop, publish, and host an online tobacco education course for youth, ages 11-18 years. The course will be available for use by school districts throughout the State of Florida. The course will include, but is not limited to, information about the health harms of all tobacco consumption, including cigarettes, cigars, cigarillos, smokeless, hookah, pipe, and electronic cigarette devises. The School District will also maintain existing online tobacco education courses for teachers and school nurses, including a new 10-hour module focused on electronic cigarettes use (vaping).

 

The impact of this project will be measured in the numbers of students, teachers, and school nurses across the state who complete the courses; increased knowledge of tobacco prevention education; increased knowledge of related effective teaching strategies; increases in student knowledge; changes in students' attitudes and behavior; and numbers of students who receive tobacco prevention lessons in lieu of suspension days. Approval of the Contract does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.
