RE 1- Approve Interlocal Agreement for Roadway Improvements/Forest Hill High School
Meeting
Jul 8, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action

  1.  The School Board and the City of West Palm Beach have prepared an Interlocal Agreement for Traffic and Roadway improvements on Parker Avenue in conjunction with the operation of Forest Hill High School.  



  2.  The traffic circulation on Parker Avenue south of Forest Hill Blvd. does not allow for an orderly flow of traffic in and out of Forest Hill High School and the adjacent neighborhood.



 3.  This situation creates a traffic safety issue for students, parents and other  pedestrians.



 4.  As a component of the Interlocal Agreement the School Board shall dedicate the 0.10+/- acre property  (See Exhibit "A") along the west side of Parker Avenue to the City in order to correct the problem.    School Board shall dedicate the 0.10 acre of property by deed substantially in the form 
attached as Exhibit "B".



 5.  The value of 0.10+/- acre parcel is $10,000.00 +/-.



 6.  The School Board shall pay for the preparation of final civil design plans,  including construction plans; the plans shall in all material respects be in accordance with the design set forth in Exhibit "C" not to exceed $15,000.00.



 7.  The School Board grants to the City the right to enter upon District property for the construction of Improvements, as defined in the ILA.



 8.  The City of West Palm Beach shall construct the Improvements at its cost.



 9.  The installation of the Improvements shall help correct the traffic circulation and safety issues.



10. The City shall be responsible for ordinary maintenance of the Right-of-Way.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement (ILA) between the City of West Palm Beach and the School Board of Palm Beach County; approve the Right-of-Way deed containing 0.10+/- acres located on Parker Avenue; and authorize the Chairman and the Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Joseph M. Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

The Financial impact to the Capital Project's Fund Budget is not to exceed $25,000.00
