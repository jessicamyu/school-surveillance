Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the third Amendment for extension to the Charter Contract with Franklin Academy, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Franklin Academy’s Charter Agreement expired June 30, 2017, but via previous Charter Amendments, the expiration of the charter agreement expires December 30, 2017.

 

In order to complete meaningful negotiations to secure a mutually beneficial and legally binding Charter Agreement, the parties agree that it is necessary for their mutual benefit and protection that the term of the current Charter Agreement be extended.

Therefore, through this Extension Agreement, the parties would agree that the term for existence of the current Charter Agreement is hereby extended until June 30, 2018.

 

All other terms of the Charter Agreement shall remain in full force and effect, subject to further extensions of time, as mutually deemed necessary by the parties.  This Amendment, together with the Charter constitutes and represents the entire agreement between the parties hereto and supersedes any prior understandings or agreements, written or verbal, respecting the subject matter herein.

Subject
PIR1 Odyssey Boundary Study - Development
Meeting
Dec 6, 2017 - 7. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Commencing the 2018-2019 School Year: I recommend, with the repurposing of Odyssey Middle School, the Board approve the development of “Odyssey Boundary Study - Superintendent’s Recommendation” to shift student attendance boundaries for five (5) Middle Schools - Carver, Christa McAuliffe, Congress, Lantana, and Woodlands – and to repeal the current Odyssey Attendance Boundary.

DESCRIPTION:

Commencing for the 2018-2019 school year: Changes in student attendance boundaries for five (5) Middle Schools – Carver, Christa McAuliffe, Congress, Lantana, and Woodlands-- and repeal of the current student attendance boundary for Odyssey.

 

On November 9, 2017, the ABC voted 8-4 to present “Odyssey Boundary Study Updated 10-26-17” (Odyssey to Carver, Christa McAuliffe, Congress, Lantana, Woodlands) the to the Superintendent.

 

The Superintendent reviewed and considered the recommendation of the ABC, as well as public comments received at the ABC meetings and in written correspondence, and made a minor adjustment to the ABC’s recommendation. The Superintendent’s recommendation shifts SAC 261, which the ABC study assigned to Carver, to Christa McAuliffe. By including the entire SAC, the boundary study no longer has the partial SAC “finger” configuration that concerned some ABC members when reviewing the Superintendent’s original study. SAC 261 currently has 67 middle school students that reside in the Odyssey attendance boundary, and 18 students that are attending Odyssey Middle.

 

The revised study recommended by the Superintendent shifts students currently residing in the Odyssey Middle attendance boundary to five (5) area Middle Schools commencing the 2018-19 school year- Carver, Christa McAuliffe, Congress, Lantana, and Woodlands Middle Schools. The recommended boundary study provides additional enrollment at other schools and moves the following SAC’s:

 

  • SACs 262A, 262B, 270, 271, 274C, 275, 278, 279, 280A, 280B, 281, 283, and 372 from Odyssey Middle to Carver Middle

 

  • SACs 242B, 252, 253, 260, and 261 from Odyssey Middle to Christa McAuliffe Middle

 

  • SACs 250B, 250D, 250E, 266B, 267, 268, 269, 272, 273, 274A, 274B, 370, 371, 406A, and 406B from Odyssey Middle to Congress Middle

 

  • SACs 251, 258, 350, 368, and 369 from Odyssey Middle to Lantana Middle

 

  • SACs 242A, 259A, and 259B from Odyssey Middle to Woodlands Middle

 

The four dissenting ABC votes were due to concerns that the study would put Christa McAuliffe Middle School over capacity and does not fully utilize the capacity available at Carver Middle School.

 

The study makes note that new Middle School “17-PP,” located adjacent to Sunset Palms Elementary, was added to this year’s Capital Plan and will open as soon as possible based on demographics, procurement procedures, and construction timeline. Staff has determined that the leasing of temporary portable classrooms at Christa McAuliffe Middle is feasible and will not impact DOE approval of the Educational Plant Survey, if the classrooms are temporary.

 

On 11/30/17, the study recommended by the Superintendent was discussed by the Diversity and Equity Committee. The following are comments and concerns raised by the Diversity and Equity Committee: 

 

  • Concerns that boundary changes may have the potential to affect students with disabilities. Many students with disabilities face difficulties with alterations in their normal routine and a change in school assignments is a major upheaval that could disrupt their learning. Additionally, special education services and resources vary across different schools.
  • A committee member asked about the resulting racial composition of the schools in the boundary study.
  • A committee member expressed concern that charter schools are lacking when it comes to serving students with disabilities, and that the District should take a stronger approach to ensure charter schools are sufficiently serving students with disabilities.
  • Committee had some concerns that South Tech Academy will compete directly against traditional District schools.
  • If the new middle school "17-PP" will offer any choice programs.

The boundary study proposal and Supporting Documentation, including the study, maps and the SAC descriptions, are provided as attachments for both the ABC and the Superintendent’s recommendations. For additional information regarding this cycle's Advisory Boundary Committee meetings and associated materials, please visit the District's BoardDocs LT site:

 

  • 10/3/2017 ABC Meeting
  • 10/26/17 ABC Meeting
  • 11/9/17 ABC Meeting
