I recommend the Board approve the Poinciana STEM Elementary School Robotics Team request to travel and compete in the First Lego League Open European championship in Tenerife, Canary Islands, Spain. The trip will take place May 1 to May 9, 2016.

DESCRIPTION:

Poinciana STEM Elementary School has three competitive robotic teams, called the TAZBOTS, registered with First Lego League, an international organization with 29,000 teams around the world. Poinciana STEM is requesting a TAZBOT team of seven students, (four 4th graders and three 5th graders) to travel to Tenerif, Canary Islands, Spain to accept the invitational bid to compete in the World European Open Invitational.  They were  selected for this honor by the FLL South Florida (Miami) Region as Core Values Champions.  The competition includes a presentation of the team's real world solution to a world problem, as well as three "runs" of their self-designed, and programmed Lego Mindstorm robot.  This once-in-a-lifetime opportunity also includes one and a half days of guided tours around the island including learning opportunities in language, culture, wind-energy and earth science.  The trip is May 1 - May 9, 2016 and all students will have finished all FSA testing for the year.

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA) requires school districts to provide Free and Appropriate Education through the year of the 22nd birthday for students with disabilities who have not graduated with a standard diploma.  Currently there are 305 students with disabilities identified in the District between the ages of 18-22 returning after receiving a special diploma.  

 

This agreement will enable up to 12 students with disabilities, ages 18 to 22, to participate in an educational environment to develop transitional life skills and increase the students' opportunity to practice age-appropriate life skills outside the traditional classroom setting on a high school campus.

 

The ultimate goal of this transitional life skills education program is to teach students the skills needed for independent living.  Transitional life skills education will consist of money management classes, use of public transportation, accessing community services and vocational training consisting of on-the-job training in a variety of department settings at Florida Atlantic University, such as but not limited to the bookstore, cafeteria, library, and maintenance departments. 

 

The students will receive daily living and vocational education from a certified School Board teacher.  Job training will be provided by a School Board job trainer.  Additional supports will be provided by the School Board ESE Department Transition Services.

 

The Agency will facilitate participants’ access to Florida Atlantic University's facilities and student activities, including the computer lab, fitness center, career center, library, recreation and athletic facilities.  Additionally, the Agency will facilitate and encourage University departments to provide non-paid career experience for the participating students.

 

The purpose of the Agreement is to set forth the Board and FAU's duties and responsibilities for providing personnel, expertise, and facilities relating to the provision of Transitional Life Skills and educational services to School District of Palm Beach County Students with Disabilities between the ages of 18 to 22.

 

The purpose of this Amendment is to clarify the financial impact.   The Department of Vocational Rehabilitation, under the Florida Department of Education, will fund the tuition for the students who qualify for the program. Students will be allowed to audit up to two college classes per year.  The District will assist the student and family in applying for Vocational Rehabilitation services which may be able to pay for audited classes.
