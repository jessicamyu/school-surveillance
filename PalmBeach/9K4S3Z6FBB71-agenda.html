Special Meeting re: Corebridge
Meeting
Dec 3, 2008 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action


  • In 2004, Corebridge operated a charter school under the auspices of the Palm Beach County School District and operated said school on properties leased from Boca Fed, L.L.C.  Subsequent thereto, Boca Fed brought suit against Corebridge in order to obtain the termination of Corebridge’s lease agreement in the Circuit Court of the Fifteenth Judicial Circuit in and for Palm Beach County, Florida, in an action styled BOCA FED, L.L.C. V. COREBRIDGE EDUCATIONAL ACADEMY SCHOOL, INC., Case  502004CA011762XXXXAF (the Trial Court Action). Corebridge ceased its operations.


  • Boca Fed and Corebridge entered a settlement.  Once Boca Fed tendered the settlement monies to the Court, an Order of Dismissal with prejudice was entered by the Court, and Boca Fed was, in effect, no longer a party to the trial court action.


  • The School Board intervened in the above-styled action for, among other matters, delivery of financial documents to complete an audit, payment to the IRS on behalf of Corebridge, and determining whether the School Board was entitled to any unencumbered public funds from the settlement proceeds.  Over $100,000 was paid from the Boca Fed settlement funds to the US Department of Treasury for IRS liens.  It was also noted that the Corebridge teachers were owed salary from Corebridge.


  • Verne and Constance Tetreault were creditors of Corebridge and sought compensation in the Trial Court Action for monies loaned to the school. The circuit court awarded the remaining settlement funds of $28, 761.72 to the School Board.


  • Corebridge filed an interlocutory appeal of the Circuit Court’s award to the School Board to the Fourth District Court of Appeal in COREBRIDGE EDUCATIONAL ACADEMY CHARTER SCHOOL, INC., ETC. V. SCHOOL BOARD OF PALM BEACH COUNTY, FLOIRDA, Case 4D08-270 (the Appellate Court Action).


  • On July 30, 2008 the School Board approved a Settlement Agreement between Corebridge and the School Board of these two actions.  Of the remaining $28,761.72, Corebridge was paid $16,000.00 in a cashier’s check made payable to Verne & Constance Tetreault, as creditors of Corebridge, and the School Board was paid the remaining $12,761.72


  • Pursuant to the terms of the Settlement Agreement, the Fourth District Court of Appeal dismissed the Appellate Court Action and the Fifteenth Judicial Circuit Court dismissed the Trial Court Action. The parties exchanged releases.


  • Subsequently, on November 4, 2008, Tracy Stone, a former Corebridge teacher, filed a motion with the Circuit Court to intervene and for the School District to disburse $12,506.80 to her for unpaid salary and Corebridge’s failure to pay insurance premium contributions.  On November 24, 2008, the Court entered the attached Order granting the Motion and ordering disbursement to Ms. Stone of $12,506.80.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board decide whether it agrees with the Court’s November

24, 2008 Order granting former Corebridge teacher Tracy Stone’s Motion to receive compensation for salary and insurance payments, or if it seeks to oppose and/or modify this Order.
