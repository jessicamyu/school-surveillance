PL1 - 2010 Census - Partnership Opportunities
Meeting
Sep 23, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)






  • The next Decennial Census will occur in 2010 with the official count day being April 1st.




  • Questionnaires will be mailed to all households in the Country during the month of March 2010.






  • Approximately $400 Billion in Federal funds is distributed based on the US Census, much of that going to education via Federal school lunch program, IDEA, Title 1, and Migrant Education.




  • The US Census Bureau is looking to school districts to play a leadership role in the 2010 census, especially in their efforts to reach the "Hard to Count" populations such as  linguistically isolated populations.




  • The Census is a teachable opportunity. The Census Bureau has provided lesson plans that educate our students on the importance of the Census.  These are available online (www.census.gov/schools) and can be incorporated via Frameworks.

RE-1 Western Communities Area ES (03-W)- Patane Eminent Domain Settlement
Meeting
Sep 23, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

1.  The School Board approved a Resolution authorizing the acquisition of an ingress, egress and utility eassement over a 0.317 acre strip of land on March 4, 2009 and two Temporary Construction Easements consisting of 0.012 acres, more or less, on September 16, 2009 through eminent domain proceedings.



2.  The eminent domain authorization was necessary to acquire an easement over the strip of land required for the bus access road and pedestrian sidewalk from Lyons Road for the Western Communities Area ES (03W).  However, through negotiations with the owner, the Board will instead acquire a fee simple interest in the driveway parcel and receive a license instead of temporary construction easement.



3.  The School District's latest appraisal of the parcel which includes the value of 0.317 strip of land, improvements, the two temporary construction easements (0.012 acres), landscaping (nursery stock inventory) damages to the remainder parcel and cost to cure is $219,700.00.  The owner originally demanded $315,250.00, but has agreed to settle for $250,000.00 plus statutory attorney's fees and expert witness costs.  In addition to the cash component of the settlement, the School District will construct or install on the owner's property driveway aprons, chain link gates and electric gate openers. The License Agreement between Buyer and Seller permits the Buyer, at no additional cost to the School District, to have access to that portion of the Seller's property needed to construct the driveway aprons, and install the gates and gate openers.



4.  Settlement is recommended as being in the best financial interest of the School District due to the anticipated costs of the litigation and litigation risk inherent in such a eminent domain proceeding.



5.  In the event that the School Board rejects the proposed settlement, the School Board and owner will prepare this matter for a jury trial to determine full compensation due to the owner.  The School Board's attorney's fees and the expert witness costs of both sides will ultimately be borne by the School Board.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Stipulated Order of Taking and Final Judgement between Robert J. and Stephanie Patane and the School Board of Palm Beach County in the case of the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida v. Robert and Stephanie Patane regarding the acquisition of certain real property consisting of 0.317 acres and two Temporary Construction Easements consisting of 0.012 acres, more or less; and authorize the School Board attorney to execute all related documents.
