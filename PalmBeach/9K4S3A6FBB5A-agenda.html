Action (Consent)

·     The District moved to a self insurance platform for employee medical coverage as of 1-1-2007. United HealthCare is under contract with the District to administer the District’s employee medical plan through December 31, 2009.



·     For 2007 we purchased individual stop loss insurance for any individual whose claims exceed $500,000 in any one year at a per subscriber per month premium of $4.95 ($1.24 million premium based on 21,000 covered employees and retirees).



·     United HealthCare is requesting a 21% increase on this individual stop loss to $ 6.00 per subscriber per month ($1.517 million based on 21,000 covered employees).



 



Past claims do not seem to justify this increase, however lower premiums are available with higher deductibles and $5 million of per claimant lifetime coverage.



 



Available Plans:





  1. $500,000 deductible = $6.00 PSPM (per subscriber, per month) = 1.517 million annual premium

  2. $750,000 deductible  = $2.15 PSPM = $541,800 annual premium

  3. $1 million deductible = $1.29 PSPM = $325,080 annual premium

·     If we figured premium at $1.2 million for each of the past 3 years to equal $3.6 million and claim reimbursements  for these 3 years at $2.212 million, we are paying more in premium than claims by $1.39 million, or an average of $450,000 per year.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board amend the contract with United HealthCare to purchase the individual Stop Loss Coverage of $5 million per subscriber with a $1 million dollar deductible retroactive to 1-1-2008 at a premium of $1.29 per subscriber per month (PSPM).
