DUE TO ACTION TAKEN IN ITEM POL-GC4, THIS ITEM IS NOW MOOT AND WAS WITHDRAWN.

 

On November 6, 2019, Policy 1.09 Advisory Committees to the Board was presented to the Board during a workshop. During the discussion Dr. Debra Robinson suggested changes to Policy 1.09 and other Board advisory committee policies (except those for IROC and ISSOC) relating to the residency requirement for membership. Because at a workshop no voting is allowed and these other policies were not then items before the Board as per the agenda (although a chart was attached to the item and some were discussed), the following motions are now being presented to the Board for action.

 

A Board member would be making the motion, and not the Superintendent. A separate motion exists for each advisory committee policy. Board members can vote yes or no on each of these motions, take other action on the motions, or decide to take no action.

 

The attached chart reflects the type of residency requirement, if any, stated in the current advisory committee policies.

 

If the Board votes that the particular policy should be amended, the intent is to present this change to the particular Committee for its input before presenting that Committee’s policy to the Board.

I make a motion that: The residency requirement in Policy 1.091—“Audit Committee” should be amended to read as follows:

 

All committee members shall be: a resident of Palm Beach County; a person with a business office in Palm Beach County; or a person with another demonstrated interest in the School District of Palm Beach County. By voting to approve an appointee to this committee, the Board is confirming that the appointee meets at least one of these qualifications.

DESCRIPTION:

The Ventus Charitable Foundation and the Education Foundation of Palm Beach County donated $97,000 in funding to support the Multi-sensory Strategies Program. The goal of the program is to improve the reading proficiency of students in primary grades who have language-based learning disabilities, including students with characteristics of dyslexia.

 

This is accomplished by increasing the strategies and approaches used by teachers to address the needs of their students and by developing the capacity of teachers in the area of research-based, multi-sensory strategies that can be utilized throughout the school day. This donation is to support expenses associated with this program that benefits district kindergarten and first grade students.

 

Individual teachers will receive professional development, a toolkit of multi-sensory materials, and ongoing job-embedded coaching support for strategy implementation. Training is to be facilitated by a collaboration of Exceptional Student Education Reading, Multicultural / Dual Language Program, and Elementary Literacy team members.

Re-engineer the cafeteria and serving lines at Palm Beach Gardens Community High School. To enhance customer service, improvements to the serving area will include new menu boards, decorative lighting, and additional support equipment. Improvements to the multipurpose room may include Bahama shutters, signage, and televisions. Construction will begin in May 2021, for completion prior to students returning in August 2021.

School Food Service, with the cooperation of the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services (FDACS) - Office of Food, Nutrition and Wellness, as reinvented the way students perceive and receive their school meals. The High School Reengineering Program (HSRP) transforms the physical facility, provides adequate time for students to eat, improves customer service, increases the number of nutritious food offerings, and enhances the overall dining experience. Based on the success of the HSRP, School Food Service would like to expand the program to one additional High School. 

 

After reviewing the selection criteria and data for the Reengineering program, School Food Service reached out to the Principal of Palm Beach Gardens High School, Dr. Donald Hoffman. Palm Beach Gardens currently has approximately 2,700 students enrolled.  Increased lunch participation is a key element of the program’s success. Current student participation in the lunch program at Palm Beach Gardens High School is 46 percent overall, with only 56 percent of their free and reduced students participating. This deficit in participation provides an excellent opportunity to positively impact student participation in the meal program and improve the school’s overall appeal.


With these modifications, students will enter a more relaxing, inviting, social, safe, and customer-driven dining experience, creating an opportunity for increased student participation.  Therefore, they will look forward to this time to nutritionally recharge for improved physical and mental well-being, supporting the District’s goal of improving academic performance.

I recommend the Board amend the Data Sharing Agreement with the University of Minnesota (UMN) to develop, implement, and evaluate technology supports and resources effective October 16, 2018 through November 30, 2022; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this Agreement

DESCRIPTION:

At the December 12, 2018, meeting, the School Board approved a Data Sharing Agreement with the University of Minnesota last December to be implemented at Woodlands Middle School.  We are amending the agreement to include two additional schools, Polo Park Middle School and Forrest Hill Community High School.

 

The project’s objective is to develop, implement, and evaluate technology supports and resources (i.e., staff readiness assessment tool, web-based applications of on-boarding modules, and professional development modules) that can facilitate the implementation of the Check & Connect (C & C) Student Engagement Intervention (SEI) and use of the C & C web-based application. Check & Connect is a comprehensive intervention designed to enhance student engagement at school and with learning for marginalized, disengaged students in grades K-12, through relationship building, problem-solving and capacity building, and persistence. The goal of Check & Connect is to foster school completion with academic and social competence.

 

It is comprised of four components:

  • a mentor who works with students and families for a minimum of two years;
  • regular checks, utilizing data schools already collect on students’ school adjustment, behavior, and educational progress;
  • timely interventions, driven by data, to reestablish and maintain the student’s connection to school and learning and to enhance the student’s social and academic competencies; and
  • engagement with families.

Schedule B amends the original agreement to now include Forest Hill High School and Polo Park Middle School, in addition to Woodlands Middle School. More schools may be included in the future.  For the schools' staff, the C & C intervention and supporting APP is designed to add efficiencies in reporting and managing student data. This would, in turn, allow for more time to be devoted to directly working with students and less time on entering and managing student data. Prior to the development of the C & C APP, data has been collected using a pencil-paper format which has proven to be inefficient.

 

Original Scope of the Project:

  • Establish a school-based implementation team
  • Train staff on the C & C intervention and APP and the SEI APP
  • Develop and implement the site readiness tool
  • Develop the technology support system (professional development modules)
  • Collect information from the mentors and staff on their experience in using the C & C and SEI APPS

Approval of this Amendment (Schedule B) does not prejudice the rights of the Board.
