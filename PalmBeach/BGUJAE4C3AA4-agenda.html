9. Consent Agenda
Subject
LD1 Proposed Litigation Against E-Cigarette Maker JUUL
Meeting
Oct 30, 2019 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING at 2:30 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
9. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve an agreement to be negotiated with the law firms of Wagstaff & Cartmell, LLP and The Maher Law Firm for legal services and representation, including filing suit on behalf of the Board against the manufacturers and/or distributors and/or sellers of electronic cigarettes and vaping products for damages arising out of the marketing, distribution, and sale of electronic cigarettes and vaping products to students; and authorize the Board Chair, Superintendent, and General Counsel to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This item approves the retention of the law firms of Wagstaff & Cartmell, LLP and The Maher Law Firm (“Law Firms”), to evaluate the viability of and potential prosecution of a civil action against the manufacturers and/or distributors and/or sellers of electronic cigarettes and vaping products (“Potential Defendants”), including filing suit on the Board’s behalf seeking recovery for damages arising out of the marketing, distribution and sale of electronic cigarettes and vaping products to students.

 

Payment of all attorney’s fees and costs associated with this Agreement is contingent upon and limited to the Board actually receiving a recovery through a verdict or settlement in the case against the Potential Defendants.  Costs and expenses associated with the case will be deducted from the gross recovery and the remaining amount, if any, will be divided between the Board and Law Firms with the Board receiving 75 percent of the net recovery and the Law Firms receiving 25 percent of the net recovery.  In the event the Board does not receive a recovery, the Board will have no liability to the Law Firms for costs, expenses or attorney’s fees.

 

CONTACT:

JulieAnn Rico, Esq., General Counsel (julieann.rico@palmbeachschools.org) 

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

If successful, the Board will receive funds for damages.  The Board’s liability for payment of attorney’s fees and costs are contingent upon and limited to any recovery by the Board.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve William Latson’s termination of employment effective Thursday, November 21, 2019 subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S., sec. 120.57, F.S..

DESCRIPTION:

On or about July 10, 2019, William Latson became the subject of an Employee and Labor Relations investigation based upon allegations of policy violations related to Ethical Misconduct and Failure to Carry out Job Responsibilities (Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive). At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

Therefore, these actions violated the following: School Board Policies 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 1.014(1), Responsibilities of Principals; 3.02(1), Code of Ethics; 3.02(4)(a), (4)(e), (4)(f),(4)(h), and (4)(j), Code of Ethics;3.02(5)(I), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment with District.

 

There is “just cause” to support William Latson termination of employment.

 

On October 11, 2019, the Superintendent’s “Notice of Recommendation for Termination of Employment” was hand-delivered to William Latson, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend the Board accept the state appropriation awarded during the 2019 Legislative Session in the amount of $250,000, approve the proposed scope of work, and authorize the Board Chair and Superintendent to sign all needed documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County submitted a local funding initiative request for West Technical Education Center (West Tech) during the 2019 Legislative Session, and subsequently received a partial award of $250,000 from the state. A scope of work was developed in conjunction with community stakeholders, West Tech staff, and District leadership in order to ensure alignment with the original local funding request application, address current programming needs, and guarantee full utilization of funds by the June 30, 2020 state deadline.
