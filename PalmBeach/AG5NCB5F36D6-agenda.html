Subject
CS3 South Tech Success Center - New Application
Meeting
Dec 7, 2016 - 3. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
3. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

On August 1, 2016, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from South Tech Success Center, Inc. to open South Tech Success Center, a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2017. 

 

Per the listing of applicants filed in 2016, with the Florida Department of Education, South Tech Success Center submitted no applications to other counties.

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

 

South Tech Success Center “Does Not Meet the Standard” in the required 22 sections.

 

Sixteen sections received a rating of “Meets the Standard:”

  • Section 1 – Mission, Guiding Principles & Purpose
  • Section 2 – Target Population & Student Body
  • Section 3 – Educational Program Design
  • Section 4 – Curriculum & Instructional Design
  • Section 5 – Student Performance
  • Section 7 – English Language Learners
  • Section 8 – School Culture & Discipline
  • Section 10 – Governance Section 18 – Food Service
  • Section 11 – Management & Staffing
  • Section 12 – Employment
  • Section 14 – Student Recruitment & Enrollment
  • Section 17 – Transportation
  • Section 18 – Food Service
  • Section 19 – School Safety & Security
  • Section 21 – Financial Management & Oversight
  • Section 22 – Start-Up Plan

One section received a rating of “Partially Meets the Standard:”

  • Section 6 – Exceptional Students

Two sections received a rating of “Does Not Meet the Standard:”

  • Section 16 – Facilities
  • Section 20 - Budget

Three sections received a rating of “Provided” or “Completed:”

  • Section 9 – Supplemental Programming
  • Section 13 – Professional Development
  • Section 15 – Parent & Community Involvement

 

Per the Overall Assessment Summary/Checklist, the ratings indicated that the application did not meet the statutory requirements set forth in the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instruments and their referenced statutes, including F.S. 1002.33(6).  (See Overall Assessment/Checklist)  It was determined that substantive changes to the application would have been needed to cure these deficiencies and the School District does not accept substantive changes.

 

“If an application is denied, the sponsor shall, within 10 calendar days after such denial, articulate in writing the specific reasons, based upon good cause, supporting its denial of the charter application and shall provide the letter of denial and supporting documentation to the applicant and to the Department of Education.”

FMT1 Remarketing of COP Series 2014A
Meeting
Dec 7, 2016 - 3. REGULAR SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
9. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Resolution authorizing the remarketing Certificates of Participation Series 2014A with an aggregate principal amount of $115,560,000 and authorize execution of related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • In 2002, the School Board approved the issuance of the 2002B COP issue to fund the construction of several schools.  The transaction was issued in a variable rate mode with weekly resets.  To reduce interest rate risk, an interest rate hedge was layered on top of the transaction.  When compared to a traditional fixed rate debt issue, this structure has saved approximately $7.5 million since 2002.
  • In 2011, the School Board approved a change to the interest rate mode.  Rather than a weekly reset with interest rates that could be impacted by the credit of the bank managing the transaction, the bond insurer, both of which were negatively impacted during the financial crisis, the rate was set based on an index plus a spread of 0.7% to be in place until February 2014.
  • In 2013, prior to the rate expiration scheduled for February 2014, the School Board approved a refunding of the transaction eliminating the 2002B COP issue and creating the 2014A COP issue.  The interest rate was again based on an index but with a reduced spread of 0.46% to be in place until January 30, 2017.
  • This resolution authorizes the remarketing of the 2014A COP issue prior to the January 2017 rate expiration.  The principal amount will remain unchanged and the rate will again be set to an index plus 0.42%.  Rather than waiting until the expiration in February, the bank (Bank of America) is willing to waive the remaining period and allow the District to use the lower rate effective December 15th, 2016.  This rate will be in place until January 30, 2020.  
  • The cost of issuance associated with the transaction will be funded from the existing debt service budget and should not exceed $100,500.  A detail list of the costs is attached.
  • The transaction was reviewed by the Finance Committee during their meeting on November 4, 2016 and was approved unanimously.  A draft of the meeting minutes is available online at https://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/FinComm2016/

Michael J. Burke, CFO (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

The Unions/Associations listed in the recommendation came together as a coalition to negotiate with the District over changes to the Health, Dental, Life, and Vision Insurances offered by the District to its employees.  Coalition bargaining on insurances has existed for over 20 years.  The coalition members and District negotiators reached a tentative agreement on the changes to the health insurance benefits, to be effective January 1, 2017.

 

The District has shared its experience of a successful wellness program which resulted in zero premium increases for the medical plan in 2017.  With that in mind, the coalition negotiations have resulted in a unified recognition that employees must continue the culture of wellness with adoption of the continuation and expansion of the Wellness Rewards Program, to include outcomes.

No medical premium increases.
