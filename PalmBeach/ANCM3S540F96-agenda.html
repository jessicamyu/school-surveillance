BRD2 Possible Challenge to House Bill 7069

 

Good Cause Statement:

House Bill 7069 was recently enacted by the Florida legislature, signed by the Governor, and became effective July 1, 2017.  This is the first Board meeting since that time to discuss this legislation which has a major impact on the School District and local control of public education in the County.  Attorneys for various school districts have recently been discussing possible legal challenges to portions of the legislation, several school boards have started to discuss or will very soon discuss possible challenges, and the Broward County School Board recently took action.  Outside attorneys are being considered.  Accordingly, it is very timely and good cause exists for this matter to be added to the agenda at this time.

I recommend the Board approve the agreement with Florida Virtual School effective July 1, 2017 through June 30, 2018; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

In response to the growth in online education, Florida Virtual School developed the FLVS Franchise program.  Through the franchise, the District takes advantage of the courses and learning management system developed by Florida Virtual School and implements an online program.

 

The Franchise is implemented through the District's virtual school, Palm Beach Virtual School.  Palm Beach Virtual School operates as a traditional full-time school for students in grades K-12 seeking a standard Palm Beach County high school diploma.  Additionally, Palm Beach Virtual School is used for part-time students who need flexibility in their scheduling to take courses for graduation, acceleration, grade forgiveness, or to raise their GPA.

 

The renewal of the Florida Virtual School Franchise Agreement for SY18 will allow the District to continue to provide virtual services for schools with particular emphasis on meeting high school graduation requirements.
