Statement of Work 5 Between The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida and the Princeton Review
Meeting
Jul 30, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Princeton Review
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

On August 2, 2007, the School Board approved Statement of Work 4 that authorized The Princeton Review (TPR) to provide access to a complete online assessment system for all students in grades 3-12. This program authorized TPR to:







  • provide access to their web platform - (Assessment Center)




  • enable all schools to administer 262 common assessments and 326 mini assessments through the web, by scanners, or by clickers




  • provide extensive content development and online instructional resources




  • provide professional development and related support materials




  • ensure that the technical infrastructure was in place to deliver and score the assessments by computers via the web, scanners, or clickers




  • provide immediate reporting of the assessment results




  • provide data transfers to the School District of Palm Beach County




  • evaluate the implementation and effectiveness of the online assessment program




  • create two grade 2 diagnostic assessments in reading and math  (one per subject)




  • import four SDPBC created grade 10 diagnostic assessments in reading and math for the "FCAT-retake" students into Assessment Center




  • create eight science cluster exams (two per cluster) at grades 5, 8, and 11


  



Subsequently, TPR and the Department of Assessment conducted a Mid-Year Evaluation of the online assessment program in January of 2008. The Evaluation found the following at all schools:







  • ability to access and administer the common assessments




  • ability to preprint, scan and score all student answer documents using Reviewscan software




  • at least one trained staff member




  • operational clicker technology at designated schools


 



It is now necessary to ensure that the currently participating schools in the district continue to have access to this complete online assessment system for students in grades 3 - 12. In addition, TPR will produce the following deliverables:







  • create a customized Pareto-like report linking test items to Pre-requisite and Necessary Skills that will direct educators to focus on skills, for which students performed the lowest, and link to supplemental resources for those skills




  •  align all reading and math common assessments to the district identified Pre-Requisite and Necessary Skills




  • make customized Pareto-like report available to teachers, school and district personnel

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Statement of Work 5 in the amount of $1,331,000.00.

Contact:

Ann Killets, akillets@palmbeach.k12.fl.us
Marc Baron, baron@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the FY 2009 Capital budget is $1,331,000.00.  The source of funds is the online assessment project as identified in the Five Year Capital Plan.
