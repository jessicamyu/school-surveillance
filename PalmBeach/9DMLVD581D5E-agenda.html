DESCRIPTION:

With a history of community outreach, Palm Beach Atlantic University along with public and private partners formed the Parker Avenue Consortium to bring additional resources into the Parker Ridge neighborhood of West Palm Beach. The Parker Avenue Consortium is a means to bring resources together to benefit kindergarten through university students and their families, and ultimately bringing a renewed vitality to the neighborhood. Through these and other relationships, Palm Beach Atlantic has a number of recent and ongoing initiatives taking place in the neighborhood, such as:

  • Science Education - In 2012 the University was awarded a STEM grant from the Quantum Foundation for $175,000. This grant will allow, over a three year period, PBA professors and teachers at Conniston Middle to develop innovative laboratory modules to be used in teaching specific BIG IDEA science concepts to sixth, seventh and eighth graders. This project also integrates the arts into STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math). Dr. Mireille Aleman is the PBAU Project Director
  • AT & T Algebra Project with Conniston Middle School - This two-year pilot project with Conniston Community Middle School helped students learn algebra bringing new instructional methods and technology to the classroom. Dr. Marsha Guntharp directed the project from August 2011-May 2013.
  • Palm Beach County Cultural Council Arts Integration - Sixth-graders at Conniston Community Middle School received arts instruction from faculty of the University's School of Music and Fine Arts Preparatory Department through a grant from the Palm Beach County Cultural Council. 
  • Summer Science Camp - Each summer since 2010, PBA has offered a summer science camp for children in kindergarten through eighth grade. The university hosts students on its main campus and gives children the opportunity to enjoy hands-on activities, guided by science-savvy camp counselors and PBA science professors. With grant funding, full scholarships were offered to 15 children from Belvedere Elementary and Conniston Middle in the summer of 2013.http://www.pba.edu/science-camp-2013
  • Book Boxes Project with Belvedere Elementary-A literacy development program for low-income youth and their parents funded by the Staples Foundation. PBA and Belvedere Elementary partnered to implement a "book boxes project," which brought interactive reading materials to their media center for parents and children to borrow.  Drs. Timothy Ladd and Chelly Templeton are the project directors
  • The consortium is expanding to include Forest Hill High School and Palmetto Elementary.

COM2 Outstanding Youth, Adult and Senior Volunteers of the Year
Meeting

DESCRIPTION:

The 2013-2014 Outstanding School Volunteers have been named by the School District in recognition of their commitment and dedication to students and schools.  Winners are selected from nominations based on years of service, number of volunteer hours, and characteristic or contribution of services.

 

The Outstanding Student Volunteer of the Year is Harrison Harward, a senior at Suncoast Community High School.  Ms. Harward, who was nominated by H. L. Johnson Elementary, began volunteering at an early age.  Through the years she has created a number of programs that set her apart.  She established a “Green Day” event at several schools which included the creation of a Green Team organizing school-wide recycling efforts, and a Green Fair to promote green businesses.  She also created a letter writing program with fellow classmates and Emotional Behavior Disorder students at neighboring schools.  These letters helped students communicate with role models and promoted positive behavior.  Her ingenuity didn’t stop there either.  She founded Communiteenz, a website of a close-knit group of nearly 300 teenagers with one common goal: to volunteer.  The program is now in six Palm Beach County high schools and is growing strong.  Communiteenz allows volunteers to sign up as a member, sign up for events, log hours, and suggest new events for volunteering.  This website allows schools, organizations and businesses to add events that need volunteers.

 

Lenore Hodges is the Outstanding Adult Volunteer of the Year, nominated by Alexander W. Dreyfoos, Jr. School of the Arts.  Ms. Hodges has volunteered in schools for over 32 years and in Palm Beach County schools for 17 years at 8 different schools, some multiple times.  She has donated over 10,000 hours of volunteer time and has brought in more than $40,000 through fundraising efforts.  Her numerous volunteer duties have included: fundraising, School Advisory Councils Chair, PTA leadership roles, School Volunteer Coordinator, School Business Partner Coordinator, Five Star School Award coordinator and Exceptional Student Education parent contact.  She has organized and run College and Career fairs and sold school lockers and Tri-rail passes.  Ms. Hodges has served as media consultant for the Dreyfoos Theater parent association and for other schools’ volunteer and business partnership programs. Additionally, throughout the years she has arranged for water and snacks for volunteers, helped clean and organize classrooms and served as a judge for numerous competitions.

 

Seminole Trails Elementary nominated Steve Berman as its Outstanding Senior Volunteer of the Year.  Mr. Berman began volunteering there by tutoring two students who were significantly below grade level in reading and at risk for retention. Instead of taking a vacation that summer and every summer since, Mr. Berman attended the Summer Reader Academy with students.  Now eight years later, Mr. Berman volunteers approximately 30 hours weekly.  During the reading process he listens to the child’s reading, encourages and models reading strategies for decoding, comprehension and fluency, and helps identify the student’s strengths and weaknesses. Not only do these students become literate but they develop a love of learning and sense of self-efficacy under his tutelage.  Now Mr. Berman has begun volunteering in one of the classrooms with autistic students.  He gives each child one-on-one attention to help them thrive.  Additionally, Mr. Berman has undertaken the role of Volunteer Coordinator at the school and gets involved in many school events such as Career Day, Spring Fling, and more.  Mr. Berman has become one of Seminole Trails’ greatest treasures, providing support for both students and staff alike.  His dedication and devotion has touched the lives of many people there.

The Sunshine State Scholar Program was founded in 1997 through the efforts of the Florida Department of Education, the Florida Education Foundation, and Florida universities. The goals of this program are to recognize student achievement and to increase the number of students with outstanding mathematics and science credentials who choose to attend Florida colleges and universities.

Each district high school was invited to select one junior, distinguished in both mathematics and science.  Using the academic and extracurricular history of the School Scholars, as well as the results of mathematics and science assessments administered on October 31, 2013, two District Scholars were selected.  The District Scholars are:

Akaash Mohan, Boca Raton Community High School, son of MJ Mohan and Simi Marwaha.

Meera Radhakrishnan, Atlantic Community High School, daughter of Meena and Ramesh Radhakrishnan.

Akaash and Meera have both excelled academically by participating in Mu Alpha Theta, the mathematics honor society, and completing multiple Advanced Placement courses in mathematics and science.

The District Scholars, together with their parents and a science or mathematics teacher of each student’s choice, will be invited to a recognition ceremony in Orlando, Florida on February 13-14, 2014. Students will be recognized for their academic achievements and have the opportunity to meet with recruiters from Florida colleges and universities.

I recommend the School Board adopt a resolution authorizing the conversion of interest rates modes with respect to the $115,350,000 aggregate principal amount of Certificates of Participation, Series 2002B and authorize execution of related documents.

  • This resolution authorizes the restructure of the 2002B COP issue from an Index Rate mode that will expire on 2/1/14 to another Index Rate mode or other mode such as a 7-day  VRDO (Variable Rate Demand Obligation).
  • The resolution also delegates authority to the Superintendent to negotiate the terms of the transaction with the underwriters or COP Purchasers within the parameters set forth in the resolution.  The resolution also authorizes the execution and delivery of all instruments and documents necessary to complete the transaction.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the School Calendar Committee's proposed 2014-2015 School Calendar; and when the State assessment dates are released, the calendar will be amended to reflect those dates.

DESCRIPTION:

The 23-member School Calendar Committee met on November 7, 2013 and having a quorum, took action to propose the attached 2014-2015 School Calendar to the Superintendent who is recommending this calendar to the School Board for adoption.

 

Summary of the Recommended Calendar's Provisions:

  • Starts school for students on Monday, August 18, 2014, which is 14 calendar days (2 weeks) before Labor Day.
  • Teachers return on Tuesday, August 12, 2014 which means a salary gap for teachers is avoided and teachers will continue to be paid every other Friday without an interruption to that every-other-week pay cycle.
  • Provides for alternating Teacher Work Days/In-Service Days at the end of each secondary and elementary grading period.
  • Completes the first semester prior to winter break.
  • Students are in attendance for 180 days (Secondary:  82 days the first semester and 98 days the second semester; and Elementary:  53 days the first trimester, 57 days the second trimester and 70 the third trimester.)
  • The traditional 3-day Thanksgiving break, the two-week Winter break and a 5-day Spring break are included in this calendar.
  • The traditional holidays are also a part of this calendar with the District closed on Independence Day, Labor Day, Martin Luther King's Birthday and Memorial Day.  In addition, schools are closed for students and teachers on a September 25 Fall Holiday, on Veterans Day (November 11) and on an April 3 Spring Holiday.
  • The calendar continues to provide 12-month employees with one "floating holiday" that can be used on any non-student attendance day after adequate notice is given and prior approval is granted.
  • Learning Team Meetings (High School late start days) and Professional Development Days (Middle and Elementary Schools early release days) will be added to this calendar after input has been received from the Chief Academic Officer concerning the dates on which these professional activities should be scheduled.
  • Emergency Make-Up Days (EMDs) will be added to this calendar as determined by the Superintendent and his Leadership Team.

DESCRIPTION:

This item has been added to the agenda for good cause in order to expedite the salary settlement with CTA.

 

The current Collective Bargaining Agreement between SDPBC and CTA is being amended to reflect the following negotiated modifications:

 

1.     Effective July 1, 2013, the Teachers' Salary Schedule is amended as negotiated in Appendix A.  This settlement reflects an average salary increase of 4.65% or $2,213.  Only teachers who are currently employed by the District on the date the School Board adopts this agreement are eligible to receive a retroactive salary increase back to July 1, 2013.

2.     This agreement includes an amended step-based schedule with an entry salary of $39,000 and a maximum salary of $73,750 for teachers reaching the longevity step.  Eligible teachers as stipulated in the agreement (Article VIII-Section A) will advance two (2) steps on the new 2013-14 step based schedule.  This schedule will become the District’s “Grandfathered” salary schedule in future years as prescribed in Florida Statute (FS 1012.22).

3.     A new Performance Salary Schedule is also established as required by Florida Statute (FS 1012.22) that will co-exist with the “Grandfathered Schedule.”  This new Performance Schedule is an “Open Range” (minimum-maximum) salary schedule that has a beginning base salary of $39,000 and a top annual base salary of $73,750.

4.     This agreement includes an increase to the Glades Supplement and adds two new seasonal supplements (High School Head Lacrosse Coach and High School Assistant Basketball Coach) previously addressed through an MOU.

5.     The Insurance Benefit provisions negotiated earlier in coalition bargaining are part of these negotiated amendments to the Collective Bargaining Agreement effective January 1, 2014.  As previously approved by the School Board, there is no increase to employee premiums for 2014.

6.     Contract language has been amended within several articles of the contract as indicated in the attached Tentative Agreement.
