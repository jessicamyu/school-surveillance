The 550 dedicated trades and support personnel of the Facilities Services Department execute the work program for the general operation, maintenance and repair of 186+ existing district facilities throughout the county in support of the educational mission.  This talented group of people is also responsible for completing hundreds of minor capital projects that add to the quality and functionality of the educational environment for the educational benefit of the Palm Beach County students.  They accomplish this vast scope of work utilizing a prioritized work order system.



To achieve the District's goals for a functional school center, the Department is aligned into nine service areas encompassing 39 trades.  These include the:



(1) Building Envelope Maintenance Program (BEMP) Section
The BEMP section addresses issues pertaining to the roof, windows and doors of a building to ensure the facility is weather tight and without water intrusion.  School exteriors are waterproofed on a periodic schedule to maximize their life and functionality.



(2) Zone Team Section
The Zone Team section provides dedicated work teams rotating throughout schools on a scheduled basis to correct code violations, Comprehensive Safety Inspection Report (CSIR) citations and general repair and maintenance activities.  In addition, Zone Teams respond to school requests for lock and window, plastering, and carpenter support.



(3) Custodial Section
The Custodial section provides recommendations to Principals regarding the school based and managed custodial functions inside school facilities.  This includes the development of cleaning plans for schools, ancillary facilities and the standardization of cleaning products for the District. This section also provides team cleaning supervisory guidance to school centers with dedicated Custodial Coordinators.  The Custodial section also recommends interior painting to Facility Management Coordinators in coordination with school-based custodial responsibilities and activities. Utilizing green cleaning practices and materials into the school centers has resulted in budget savings of over half a million dollars while maintaining the health and sanitation levels in the school centers.



(4) Central Services Section (Fire Alarm/ Intercom/ Sports Lighting/ Roofing/ Sheet Metal and Generators)
The Central Services section responds to school requests for all electrical issues and power requirements. This section provides fire alarm, intercom, roofing, sheet metal, welding, generators and boilers support as per code requirement.



(5) Grounds Section
The Grounds section coordinates with the school athletic coaches the recommended Turf Management schedule for all playfields, the industry recommended maintenance and capital work schedules for athletic fields, tracks and tennis courts.  They also provide tree trimming and debris removal including bulk trash pickups of old furniture for transport to the landfill.  In addition, they provide landscape support and irrigation repairs, playground repair, the environmentally friendly Integrated Pest Management Program and manages the District vendors with landscape maintenance contracts.



(6) Logistics Section (Material Inventory)
The Logistics section inventories and manages all materials needed for Facilities Services work orders and provides the appropriate fiscal audit information.



(7) Mechanical Section (HVAC)
The Mechanical section responds to school requests for support services for malfunctioning mechanical systems pertaining to Air Conditioning and plumbing.



(8) Commissioning/Quality Assurance Section
On-going commissioning activities and LEED for existing buildings, ensures mechanical systems are operating according to School District Master Specifications as well as the manufacturers' specifications. This work supports the district's standards for indoor air quality in the school centers.  They also conduct school warranty site visits.



(9) Professional Services Section (Design and Portables)
The Professional Services section executes the Planning Department's schedule of relocation and placement of new and existing portable classrooms throughout the District.



It is through this alignment and on-going communications with the single point of contact that the schools receive the requested and scheduled support services. Skilled trades are on call 24/7 for emergency repair work after hours and they are also the first responders to the District facilities after a hurricane event.   They are essential to the functioning of our educational facilities.

Over 1,000 frontline transportation staff must be fully qualified for the considerable demands of the job.  A stable, experienced corps of mechanics, drivers and attendants provides a higher degree of student safety when transporting student to and from school and other school related activities.

Employees in the Maintenance Section of the Transportation Department provide maintenance for 1700 district-owned vehicles, and complete over 9,500 vehicle inspections and 6,500 vehicle repairs annually.  Our transportation maintenance employees operate from 6 different Transportation Facilities and are on-call 24 hours a day, 7 days a week working two scheduled shifts from 5:00am - 8:00pm in support of student transportation and other district personnel throughout Palm Beach County.

1.  School Bus Driver

2. School Bus Attendant



School Bus Attendants provide supervision and care for approximately 5,000 special needs students.  Bus attendants are required to have 24 hours of classroom instruction and training.  They must pass a physical performance test designed to evaluate their physical ability to escort students on and off the bus, secure wheelchairs, and to evacuate students in an emergency.

IN2 High School Interscholastic Athletic Insurance and Student Catastrophic Accident Insurance
Meeting
Jul 21, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent

Action (Consent)

These are two different policies. The first policy is the Student Catastrophic Insurance Policy and provides coverage of up to $5 million with a $25,000 per student deductible. This coverage is required by the Florida High School Activities Association in order for the District to participate in the sanctioned sports programs. The policy covers medical expenses resulting from accidental injuries to any student while participating in school activities (including daytime activities).  The premium for this policy is remaining the same. 



 



The second policy is the High School Interscholastic Athletic plan and it covers up to $25,000 in medical expenses for accidental injuries to high school interscholastic athletes and cheerleaders.  This coverage is purchased to assist with medical claims related to high school sports injuries.  The premium for the current benefit option (Plan 1 on the attached summary) was proposed to increase from $1,304,000 to $1,535,795. 



 



An analysis of the experience of our plan from Monumental Life Insurance Company included the District’s most recent completed claim year (2008-2009) with a loss ratio of 136.8% when comparing claims paid to premiums collected.  This is the highest loss ratio the District has experienced in the past five (5) years. This high loss ratio was a significant reason for the high renewal.  The high loss experience also made it difficult to change carriers.  Our broker, The Beacon Group, explored pricing from two other carriers and the cost was higher than the renewal from the current carrier, Monumental Life.  The broker then obtained quotes for other benefit designs (attached) to allow for greater savings.



 



Student insurance fees as allowed by School Board policy 5.60 cover approximately half of the premium. The current fee is set at $75.00 per student.  If the District renewed the same benefits, the fee would have to be raised to $87.50 per student to support the increase. 



 



In an effort to hold down costs for the District and students, staff is recommending a change to (lower) plan benefits (Plan 4) for a savings of $209,944.  The plan benefits would reduce claim payments to 80% coinsurance or cost sharing on claim payments.   This benefit change would also include capping coverage for physical therapy to $80 per visit with a maximum of 20 visits and imaging services would be capped at $1,600 maximum benefit.  With the change to a lower benefit level, the student insurance fee would be able to stay the same. This would result in a new total premium for both policies of $1,567,151.  This is an increase of $21,851 (1.4%) over last year’s combined expiring premiums of $1,545,300. 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board purchase High School Interscholastic Athletic Insurance Plan 4 ($1,289,656) and Student Catastrophic Accident Insurance (($277,495) effective August 1, 2010 to August 1, 2011 from the Monumental Life Insurance Company (General Agent: Bollinger Insurance) for a combined total premium of $1,567,151.

The Prevention Center in the Department of Safe Schools has conducted tobacco prevention teacher training for nine years under grants from the Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) Office of Healthy Schools.   For the past three years, this training has been online.  Tobacco use by students interferes with their academic achievement and is associated with other drug use; prevention efforts have demonstrated success in reducing tobacco use among youth.



The Statewide Online Tobacco Prevention and Intervention Teacher Training Project is being created based on the findings and recommendations from The Needs, Barriers, and Challenges in Tobacco Prevention and Intervention Teacher Training Program among School Districts in Florida Report by the University of Florida.  The report recommended that the Florida Department of Education consider using an online program for teacher training and grant reporting in the future and allow for flexibility in scheduling training sessions.

The impact of this project will be measured in the numbers of teachers who complete the course, teachers'  increased knowledge of tobacco prevention education and related effective teaching strategies, and student receipt of evidence-based tobacco prevention lessons.

I would like the board to consider naming and a memorial designation in honor of Gerald A. Williams, Esq. within the School District of Palm Beach County.  Gerald was a multifaceted and multitalented individual and his professional life in law had two main areas of focus and accomplishment.  First, Gerald’s career spanned 30 plus years where he served the needs of the public school children throughout the State of Florida and public school interests.  Secondly, through his passionate advocacy of public education and public school students, and his unrelenting pursuit of effective public policy, Gerald inspired numerous individuals to pursue successful careers in the legal profession.



 



Our memoriam to Gerald Williams needs to include both aspects of Gerald’s career focus.  For this reason I think it appropriate that the board ask the superintendent to instruct staff to prepare an appropriate proclamation with plaques and certificates both for the school district and the Williams family and invite the Williams family to take part in a presentation at the next board meeting or the one thereafter designating: 1) the legal department at district headquarters as The Gerald A. Williams, Esq. Legal Department; and 2) the law academy at Palm Beach Lakes Community High School as The Gerald A. Williams, Esq. Law Academy.  Through both of these designations, we do justice to each of the aspects of Gerald’s career, a career that was cut painfully short and a career that he fully intended to return to.
