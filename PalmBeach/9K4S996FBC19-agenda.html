IN1 Approval to Purchase Property Insurance
Meeting
Dec 8, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The District conducted a Broker Selection in 2009 and approved The Beacon Group, Inc. as it’s broker for Property and Boiler and Machinery policies, among others.  The School Board approved the contract with The Beacon Group, Inc. on July 21, 2010 for their services.    This item is approving the actual placement of property insurance.  Any commissions are paid only when the policies are placed.



 



Last year the District purchased $100 million in property insurance against total insured values (TIV) of $5.31 billion, at a cost of $7.618 million. This year we are proposing to maintain the $100 million in catastrophic limits to a TIV of $5.46 billion at a cost not to exceed $8 million. Although property replacement costs remained constant per square foot, the overall total property inventory increased by approximately $146 million (3%), including the addition of a new elementary school and a replacement high school.



 



The recommended option is as follows:



 



Loss Limit:                                $100,000,000



 



Deductible:                               All perils other than named wind - $10 million



 



Named Windstorm – 5% of values with $500,000 minimum at each location plus a $10 million corridor.  This $10 million corridor is applied only one time per policy year.



 



Estimated Premium:                   Not to exceed $8 million



 



The District has previously requested and received a Reasonableness Letter from the State Office of Insurance Regulation and State Office of Emergency Management confirming that our property insurance program complies with the Stafford Act requirements from FEMA. The District will again request this confirmation from the state upon placement of this insurance.



 



Our broker has contacted forty-five (45) insurers/reinsurers and assembled a consortium of carriers in order to cover our property insurance renewal.   Due to the timing of Board Meetings and a renewal date of December 31, 2010, pricing and placement have not yet been finalized.  Once the actual layer program and pricing have been finalized, we will have full-layer pricing available.



  



The Beacon Group, Inc. will be compensated at a 4% commission rate to a maximum cap of $280,000, which includes their services, and the services of any wholesaler/intermediary used to place the District’s property insurance program.



 



Market capacity continues to be an issue for our estimated loss limit and premium cost. The District’s property is mostly concentrated in one centralized area within a few miles of the coast. Our probable maximum loss (PML) is estimated in excess of $327 million for a 100-year storm event.



 



Due to multiple changes in National Flood Insurance Program flood zone mapping, the flood insurance will be brought to the Board at a future meeting before hurricane season.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the purchase of property insurance through our broker The Beacon Group, Inc., with limits of $100 million effective December 31, 2010 to December 31, 2011 at a premium cost not to exceed $8 million.

Contact:

Joseph Moore, Chief Operating Officer (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



 Dianne L. Howard, Director Risk and Benefits Management (dhoward@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



 

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the District for this coverage is not to exceed $8 million. The source of funds is a transfer from the Capital Maintenance Funds budget to the General Funds budget.

IN2 Renewal of Boiler and Machinery Insurance Policy

Dec 8, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations

Action (Consent)

Hartford Steam Boiler provides property damage protection in the event of explosions or unexpected mechanical malfunctions of our boilers or machinery. Additionally, each year they provide inspections and certificates for approximately half of the 400 objects at over 202 locations as required by Florida Statute 554.108.



 



The premium remained the same as the expiring policy even with a slight increase (3%) in Total Insured Values and the number of boilers/locations.



 



The District is able to renew the existing $50 million policy limit, which excludes windstorm and lightning coverage (excluded by all Boiler and Machinery carriers).



 



The deductible is $10,000 for all perils.



 



A few years ago, the District recovered $63,899 from Hartford Steam Boiler for two Boiler & Machinery claims. In addition, the District is required, at a minimum, to purchase Boiler & Machinery Insurance for schools funded through COPS programs at a cost of approximately $27,000.



 



The District’s broker, Beacon Group, Inc., recommends this Boiler and Machinery Insurance program and receives no commission on this policy.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board renew its Boiler and Machinery Insurance policy with Hartford Steam Boiler through our broker, The Beacon Group, Inc. This policy would become effective December 31, 2010 - December 31, 2011, for a premium of $42,647.25.

Summary of Advisory Boundary Committee (ABC)
2011-2012 Cycle
Boundary Change Proposals
Presentation to Superintendent (Joseph Moore-designee)
November 4, 2010 

1.  PL-1 Boundary Study S-3 amended (Sunset Palms Elementary to Manatee Elementary) was voted on by the ABC to be recommended to the Superintendent on October 21, 2010 with an 11-0 vote.



   The proposed changes provide relief to Sunset Palms Elementary and moves part of SAC 227A to Manatee Elementary.



   Current 4th grade students would have the option to remain at Sunset Palms Elementary as 5th graders without transportation.

   2.  PL-2 Boundary Study WW-2 amended  (Woodlands Middle to Christa McAuliffe Middle) was voted on by the ABC to be recommended to the Superintendent on October 21, 2010 with an 11-0 vote.

   The proposed changes provide alignment of feeder patterns with Study S-3 amended in item 1.  This will align school feeder patterns by moving students in part of SAC 227A from Woodlands Middle to Christa McAuliffe Middle.

   Current 7th grade students would have the option to remain at Woodlands Middle as 8th graders without transportation.

3.  PL-3 Boundary Study LH/BC-1 (Lighthouse Elementary/Beacon Cove Intermediate to Jerry Thomas Elementary) was voted on by the ABC to be recommended to the Superintendent on October 21, 2010 with an 11-0 vote.

   The proposed changes provide relief to Lighthouse Elementary/Beacon Cove Intermediate and moves a currently undeveloped part of SAC 028 to Jerry Thomas Elementary.  No students would be affected at this time as there are no units in the portion of SAC 028 proposed to move.

LA1 - CONSIDER APPROVAL OF THE SCHOOL CALENDAR COMMITTEE'S RECOMMENDED 2011-2012 SCHOOL CALENDAR
Meeting
Dec 8, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Background information leading to this recommendation:







  • The School Calendar Committee, comprised of parents, community leaders district staff, principals and representatives of the employee Unions/Associations, met on November 22, 2010 to draft and make recommendations to the Superintendent for the 2011-2012School Calendar.




  •  The Committee recommends a School Calendar that establishes Monday, August 22, 2011 as the first student attendance day, establishes an elementary teacher work day at the end of each elementary trimester, a secondary teacher work day at the end of each secondary grading period, completes the first semester on January 13, 2012, complies with applicable provisions of all negotiated labor contracts relating to the School Calendar, and establishes Wednesday, June 6, 2012 as the last student attendance day of the school year.




  • The Calendar closes schools for students on Veterans Day (Friday, November 11, 2011), continues a three-day school holiday for Thanksgiving, a ten day winter break and a five day spring break.




  •  In lieu of the option of taking either the fall holiday or the spring holiday, the calendar provides 12 month employees with one "floating holiday"  that can be used on any non-student attendance day after adequate notice is given and approval is granted.




  • Learning Team Meetings (High School late start days) and Professional Development Days (Middle and Elementary Schools early release days) will be added to this Calendar after input has been received from Principals and others concerning the dates on which these professional activities should be scheduled.




  •  Emergency Make-up Days will be added to this Calendar as determined by the Superintendent and his leadership team.




  • Elementary and Secondary Report Card distribution dates will be added to this Calendar after receiving input from the FTE And Student Reporting Office.




  • The "BEGIN" and "END" dates of the Employee Contract Periods are a function of the student start date and these dates will be added to this Calendar in recognition that the first student attendance day is August 22, 2011. 


 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the recommendation of the District's School Calendar Committee for the 2011-2012 School Year as attached.
