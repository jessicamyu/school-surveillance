Subject
The School Board as a Public Partner on the CWHIP grant applications for The Hammons in Lake Worth and the Village at Delray.
Meeting
Jan 9, 2008 - Spec Mtg re CWHIP Application
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

 





  • The 2007 Legislature approved House Bill 1375 includes monies for an affordable housing pilot program called Community Workforce Housing Innovation Pilot Program (CWHIP).


 





  • Last Year $50 million dollars were allotted under this Program.  The School partnered with MerryPlace development in West Palm Beach, FL on the CWHIP application.  MerryPlace development was successful in receiving one of the grants.


 





  • This year the total amount in grants has increased from $50 million to $62.4 million which will be distributed amongst 10 projects in high cost and high growth areas of the State.


 





  • New Urban/RFC Lake Worth, LLC/Caster Development and Auburn Development, LLC/Delray Beach Housing Authority approached School District staff requesting that the School District be their public partner on the application for CWHIP funds. 


 





  • The developers presented their projects to School District staff in October and November 2007. 


 





  • The Hammons development will consist of 48 town homes and 82 condos.  The Village at Delray will provide 264 rental units and 750 town homes/condominiums totaling over 1,000 units, however the CWHIP application will apply to portions of phases I and II of the development totaling 374 units.


 





  • Staffâ€™s recommendation is that $250,000 be set aside for the Hammons for subsidy assistance for homeownership units for eligible teachers at Title I schools and $500,000 to The Village at Delray.  


 





  • The monies will come from the general fund and will only be expended if the eligible teachers apply and qualify for the subsidy. 


 





  • The difference in level of subsidy from the School Board is based on the size of the project; the Hammons is 190 units and the Village is 1014 units. 


 





  • These monies will be available even if the developments are not successful with the CWHIP grant.


 





  • A Cooperative Agreement between the developers and the School Board specifying the terms of the partnership will be presented to the School Board for approval.  The School Board will also have to approve employee application requirements, eligibility criteria and any limitations prior to any funds being distributed.


 





  • The deadline for submitting the grant application is January 29, 2008.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the public / private partnership with New Urban/RFC Lake Worth, LLC/Caster Development for Hammon Park in Lake Worth, FL and Auburn Development, LLC/Delray Beach Housing Authority for The Village at Delray with the School Board as a public partner on the CWHIP grant applications to Florida Housing Finance Corporation and authorize the Chairman and the Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us; sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

$250,000 in subsidy assistance towards home ownership for the Hammon Development and $500,000 in subsidy for the Village with the funds coming from the general fund.
