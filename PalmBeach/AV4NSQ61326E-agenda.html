Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the attached list of Out-of-Field/Out-of-Compliance Teachers for Survey 3 for FY18 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. Such assignment typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction. Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience. The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field. Those new hires who are offered jobs as an Interim Out-of-Field, and are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements/tests prior to the end of May. If a teacher does not satisfy the coursework/testing requirement their Interim position ends. Once a teacher completes their Out-of-Field requirements the school is notified and teachers can be moved from Interim Out-of-Field to Regular.
 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate. Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary or ESE. Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements. This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification. It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track. Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014 include the following statement, “Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment.”
