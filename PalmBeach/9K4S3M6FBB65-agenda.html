The Alternative Education Peer Mediation Program will provide a student-to-student counseling process based on Restorative Justice principles and values aimed at anger management and conflict resolution. Peer Mediation is a process in which students in a school help other students to resolve conflicts. When there is a dispute, whether student/student or teacher/student, the mediator becomes a neutral third party and works with the disputants through a procedure of conflict resolution.



 



The Alternative Education Peer Mediation Program will target youth ages 12 to 17, who have been assigned to an alternative school placement in the Palm Beach County School District. Students eligible for such a placement are at-risk due to low academic performance and/ disruptive behaviors.



 



Parents will be encouraged to partner with the school as there is a necessity for them to complete necessary paperwork for all volunteer student mediators to participate in the program. Furthermore, the parents will be informed of this alternative method such that they can encourage their child to participate and learn through peer interaction to resolve some low-level behavior issues through mediation.



Objectives:



 



To provide a peer mediation-restorative justice program for at-risk youth enrolled in Alternative Education.



 



Reduce the number of school referrals for level 1 and 2 offenses by 75% during the 2nd semester of the 2008-2009 and the 2009-2010 school year.
