I recommend the Board approve the award of Bid 22C-825L for Athletic Surfacing with Related Materials, Supplies, Installation, and Services through August 7, 2023, not to exceed $1,200,000; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Award of Bid 22C-825L an alternate source contract (piggyback) off the Sourcewell RFP No. 060518-AST for the purchase of Athletic Surfacing with Related Materials, Supplies, Installation and services from AstroTurf Corporation, the bid awarded vendor.

 

Under this Contract, Astroturf Corporation will install Synthetic Turf at Boca Raton High School in Boca Raton, Florida. The turf field for Boca Raton High School is beyond its life span and can no longer be maintained safely for students and student-athletes to participate on. Installation of this new turf field will assist the District in providing Boca Raton High School with a safe playing surface for its students and student-athletes in time for FY23 season.  This will also allow for outdoor sports, physical education classes and other activities to function safely with minimal, if any disruption at the start of the school year and sports calendar. The cost for this service at Boca Raton High School is not to exceed $675,512 and the balance of the Board approved spend may be used at other locations throughout the District.

 

The Office of Diversity was contacted to determine if there were any District certified SBE vendors registered for this commodity and there are none at this time.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve: A) Salary actions and B) Bereavement Leave as proposed for the NBU Staff, Miscellaneous, and Confidential employees as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

A. Salary Actions: Consider approval for NBU Staff, Miscellaneous, and Confidential (SMC) as follows: *

  1. Employees to be granted a 3.5% base salary increase effective July 1, 2021.
  2. Employees to receive a one-time $1,500 payment funded from Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief Fund (ESSER) award.
  3. Salary schedules to be increased by 3.5% to range minimums and maximums. (See Staff Schedule Attachment #1, Miscellaneous Schedule Attachment #2, Confidential Schedule Attachment #3.)


*  Employee must be in an employed status with the District as of the date of Board approval to be eligible for approved pay provisions.

 

B.  Bereavement Leave: Consider approval for employees in Active status, per the following parameters:

 

     An employee required to be absent from work due to death in his/her immediate family shall be paid bereavement leave for up to three (3) days each fiscal year. ‘Immediate’ family is limited to parents, siblings, spouse, children, mother-  and father-in-law, grandparents and grandparents-in-law, domestic partner, or member of the employee’s own household. Use of bereavement leave shall not count against an employee’s accrued (or accrual of) sick, personal, or compensatory time. 


     Upon returning from a leave, when such leave shall be designated as ‘bereavement’, an employee provides to his/her supervisor the name of the deceased, date of death, city of death, and relationship to the employee. Failure to provide requested information may result in denial of a leave’s bereavement designation, whereby such leave time shall be designated as an otherwise paid or unpaid leave. If additional time beyond the approved bereavement leave is required, then use of an employee’s accrued sick and/or personal leave may apply. (Bereavement leave may not be used if such need occurs during an interval whereby an employee is already on a Leave status.)
