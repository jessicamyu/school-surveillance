  • Prism Response, Inc.  and Project Development Group have requested that the School Board consent to the assignment of the Contract, including all documents incorporated therein, to Prism Response, Inc;

  • Prism Response, Inc. has the experience, qualifications, and capability to perform the services set forth in the Contract, in accordance with each and every term and condition set forth in the Contract.

A.  I recommend approval for the additional estimated expenditures in the amount of $300,000 for the period beginning September 2, 2010.

This project modification contains the following item:







  • Second Amendment to Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and the State of Florida, Division of Emergency Management (DEM).  The agreement is for FEMA grant funding of $2,149,356 under the Hazard Mitigation Grants Program to replace the windows in the Fulton Holland Education Services Center.




  • Description:  Extend the expiration date of the agreement from April 20, 2010 to October 20, 2010.




  • Cause:  The School District completed the work by April 20, 2010.  However, the DEM was not able to schedule their final inspection prior to the April 20, 2010 agreement expiration date.




  • Justification:  The time extension provided time for the DEM to complete their final inspection and for the School District to complete all punch list items.  We have received $1,899,198 to date and will receive a final payment of $250,158.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Second Amendment to the Federally Funded Sub-grant Agreement between the State of Florida, Division of Emergency Management and the School Board of Palm Beach County, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.

Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

The financial impact of the Sub-grant Agreement is $2,149,356 to the SDPBC.

CP-1 Architect Agreement for Galaxy Elementary School Modernization
Meeting
Sep 1, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Architectural design services required for the development of permitted design documents and construction documents for the modernization/construction of a +/- 108,674 GSF (including 95,294 GSF of finished Work and 13,380 GSF of shell space for future build out), 605 student station elementary school, including replacement of an adjacent city park facility. The project will be designed and constructed to a minimum of LEED Silver Certification. As required by a Castaldi Analysis, two existing classroom buildings of +/- 10,000 GSF will be reused as ancillary space and a covered play area may be reused if it fits the facility site plan. All three structures will be analyzed by the project architect to determine appropriate usage of the structures and site and renovations required to meet SREF size standards, ADA and Code requirements. A +/- 4 acres parcel of contiguous property currently established as a city park facility will be available for use in the overall facility site design.     



 



The Design shall incorporate principals of LEED design and all requirements set forth in the permitted Construction Documents, Educational Specifications, Florida Building Code, Florida Accessibility Codes, District Master Specifications, Design Criteria and all other applicable specifications. The permitted design shall be in accordance with the Educational Specifications approved by the Board on December 16, 2009, and revised February 17, 2010.
