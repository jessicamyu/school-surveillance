PR-1 Gumbo Limbo Nature Center Interlocal Agreement
Meeting
Dec 14, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)


  1. The purpose of this Interlocal Agreement is for the School Board ("Board") to receive funds from the Greater Boca Raton Beach and Park District ("GBRBPD") for services provided during the 2010-2011 school year.

  2. In December 1989, the Board and the City of Boca Raton ("City") entered into a long-term Lease Agreement for the Gumbo Limbo Nature Center. (See Exhibit "A")

  3. In the Lease, the Board and the City agreed that there existed a public need for park, recreation and environmental programs for the use and enjoyment of their residents.

  4. The Board administered interpretive guides and developed trails for the Nature Center.

  5. In fulfillment of its obligations, the Board employed a full time Curriculum Specialist, at the Nature Center to train and supervise GBRBPD and City personnel and provided training for teachers to build content area capacity and assisted teachers in developing the skills needed to maximize student's experiences at the Nature Center.

  6. As a result of budget cuts prior to the start of the 2010-2011 school year, the Board was no longer able to fund the Curriculum Specialist position at the Nature Center and the year began with the Nature Center not being staffed by a Curriculum Specialist.

  7. In order to provide a transition until other staffing arrangements could be made, GBRBPD agreed to reimburse the Board for the salary and benefits of providing the Curriculum Specialist for approximately 9 months of the school year.

  8. The Board also provided the GBRBPD with a written Curriculum Manual based on the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards.

  9. In consideration of the above services, the Board provided to the GBRBPD, the GBRBPD agreed to provide a grant to the Board in the amount of $73,648.00.

  10. The Board has fulfilled all its obligations to the GBRBPD and the ratification of this agreement is only required so that GBRBPD can reimburse the Board for the salary and benefits incurred in providing the Curriculum Specialist.



Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement between the Greater Boca Raton Beach and Park District and the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida for previously provided Environmental Programs at Gumbo Limbo Nature Center; and authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph Sanches (joseph.sanches@palmbeachschools.org)

A group of (20) students (juniors and seniors) in the Jupiter High School Environmental Research and Field Studies Academy (JERFSA), will travel to Panama to engage in study of ecological and cultural and conversation issues of the rain forest and marine ecosystems of the country.  The trip will occur over Spring Break, with students departing on Friday, March 16, 2012 and returning on Wednesday, March 27, 2012.



This trip will allow students to experience first hand the wildlife and ecological functioning of both rain forest and tropical marine ecosystems, with careful analysis of the influences of economic and development pressures, tourism, and other natural and human pressures upon the continued health of tropical ecosystems.  Instruction, discussions, and tours will be provided by ANCON, the National Association for the Conservation of Nature, one of the region's most respected conservation organizations, dedicated to protecting the natural heritage of Panama.



Benefits to the students, school and District include:



*  Ecological, geological, and environmental learning for students and teachers;
*  Broadening of personal experience for students and teachers;
*  First-hand experience in a variety of natural ecosystems and geological settings that will improve instruction in Biology, Ecology, and AP Environmental Science classes.

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of an educational trip for students in the Jupiter Community High School Environmental Research and Field Studies Academy to travel to Panama, during Spring Break, March 16-27, 2012.  The trip will provide an opportunity for students to learn about the environmental, ecological and cultural aspects of the rain forests and tropical marine ecosystems of Panama.

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded two competitive grants under the Florida Teacher Quality Grants Program for a total of up to $3,000,000 for a three-year period from December 15, 2011 through August 31, 2014, from the Florida Department of Education. 





In partnership with the College of Education and the Harriett L. Wilkes Honors College at Florida Atlantic University (FAU), the District will implement the iTeach Geometry Partnership Project and the iTeach U.S. History Partnership Project to provide focused, content-rich comprehensive professional development to high school teachers of Geometry and U.S. History, with a particular emphasis on teachers from Title I high schools.  The annual instructional format for participating teachers includes graduate-level university coursework on Saturdays and during the summer to  reinforce content knowledge and strengthen pedagogical skills related to the delivery of high quality lessons  to support student learning and achievement.  The teacher education provided through this partnership will be research-based, rigorous, and specific to the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards (NGSSS) and in preparation for the implementation of the Common Core State Standards (CCSS).  Participating teachers will earn up to nine graduate-level credit hours in the content area.  





The grants awarded under the Florida Teacher Quality Program will each fund a project coordinator (2.0 FTE) to recruit and support participating teachers, to work with the FAU college partners to plan and facilitate the delivery of professional development through university coursework, to manage the grant budget, and to monitor progress toward achievement of goals and objectives on time and within budget. 


 




The goals established for these partnership projects are to: 1) improve and deepen content knowledge for Geometry and U.S. History high school teachers;  2) improve teachers’ use of research-based, NGSSS-focused instructional practices and pedagogy;  and 3) improve student achievement in Geometry and U.S. History, particularly among the District’s low-income student populations. The ultimate purpose and anticipated long-term outcome is to increase student achievement on Florida End-of-Course Assessments in Geometry and U.S. History.  The Florida Teacher Quality Grants Program requires and funds an external evaluator who will assess quantitative and qualitative data to determine each project’s impact and progress toward meeting its goals and objectives. 





On November 21, 2011, the District received official notification of these two grant awards under the Florida Teacher Quality Program from the Florida Department of Education. Although the grants are  effective September 1, 2011, this does not prejudice the School Board.  Upon Board approval, programmatic and personnel actions will begin December 15, 2011. Under the Florida Teacher Quality Program, the total grant award is $1.5 million per grant, for a total of $3 million for both grants over three years.  Copies of the grant applications for the iTeach Geometry Partnership Project and the iTeach U.S. History Partnership Project are available for review in the Board Office.



Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Florida Teacher Quality Grants Program awards from the Florida Department of Education for up to $3,000,000, effective December 15, 2011 through August 31, 2014, and any no-cost extension as applicable; and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for the implementation of grant activities.

Description:  The District was recently awarded the five-year, $2,027,803 Transition to Teaching Grant by the United States  Department of Education to implement the I-Teach Project, as approved by the Board on October 19, 2011.  The purpose of this grant project is to  recruit, train, mentor and retain high quality mid-career changing professionals and recent college graduates with  non-education college degrees to teach in critical need subject areas in our secondary schools. The grant project will provide a structured, accelerated certification program, consisting of high quality professional development and intensive mentoring support, to prepare the project participants to attain the Florida  Professional Teaching Certificate in their subject  area and to retain such teachers in our District.  To this end, a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between the District and the Classroom Teachers Association (CTA) was negotiated, setting forth the terms and conditions of employment of these new I-Teach Project participants.  The negotiated MOU is attached and provides the following:



 



·         Working collaboratively with the Palm Beach County Workforce Alliance, the District will select potential participants from a pool of college graduates of accredited colleges and  universities who have earned at least a non-education bachelors degree  and qualify for Florida Temporary Certification to teach in   the District’s secondary schools.



·         Before being approved as an I-Teach Project participant, the applicant must agree in writing to do his/her utmost to attain a valid State Professional Teaching Certificate in his/her subject area within eighteen (18) months of the date of his/her active employment with the District; to attend all in-service trainings scheduled by the District and/or its partners in this Project; to accept and work positively with an assigned mentor(s); to participate in the I-Teach Accelerated Certification Program; to  agree to observe and do their best to learn from District teachers who are identified by the District as  master teachers; to commit to remain in the  secondary school to which he/she was initially assigned for a period of not less than four (4) full school years, unless involuntarily reassigned by the District during this four (4) full school year period of time or unless the participant is authorized by his/her Principal to seek a voluntary transfer to another school after completing his/her third year; and to agree to repay in full any incentives and awards that may be paid in keeping with this MOU should the participant fail to live up to his/her commitments made to become a participant in the I-Teach Project.  



·         With I-Teach Project grant funds, the District pledges to pay a monetary incentive to each accepted participant of $1,500, minus standard deductions, within sixty (60) calendar days of the date of the participant’s active employment with the District.  In addition, the District also pledges to pay $500, minus standard deductions, to award each participant who successfully attains a valid State Professional Teaching Certificate within eighteen (18) months of his/her initial date of active employment with the District.   



·         In addition, the District pledges to provide the participant with a mentor(s); in-service training; pay for mandatory in-service training scheduled during after-duty hours at the hourly rate set forth in the parties’ Collective Bargaining Agreement; release-time during the duty day to attend scheduled in-service trainings and observations of master teachers; and the I-Teach Accelerated Certification Program.



·         The parties agree that any provisions of the CBA that are in conflict with the MOU are waived for the duration of the MOU, but only for those instructional employees participating in the I-Teach Project.

Last year the District purchased $100 million in property insurance for Total Insured Values (TIV) of $5.46 billion at a cost of approximately $7.845 million.



The District also had a $10 million first layer deductible for all perils other than wind storm. In addition, the District also had a named windstorm deductible of 5% of values with a $500,000 minimum at each location with an additional $10 million deductible. This additional $10 million deductible would only apply once in a year.



Property replacement costs increased per square foot this year resulting in the overall TIV increase of approximately $360 million (6.6%) to a new TIV of approximately $5.72 billion. Due to this increased TIV coupled with the difficult economic times and a severe hardening of the insurance market, the brokers are struggling to purchase $50 million worth of coverage and maintain a premium under $8 million. In addition, the state of uncertainty in financial markets has contributed to the changes in the property insurance market. Premiums have increased from 70 to 100% in the lower (most vulnerable $25 million of coverage) layer. The District may have to assume different deductibles in order to maintain the premium at under $8 million and be able to purchase $50 million in coverage. The actual insurance premium has to remain at approximately $7,750,000 in order to allow for the 2-1/2 to 3% Citizens and Florida Catastrophic Fund assessments and maintain a total cost under $8 million.



Market capacity continues to be an issue for our estimated loss limit and premium cost. The District’s property is  concentrated in one centralized area (the County) which is considered mostly coastal. New wind modeling programs have increased the District’s estimated loss for a 100 year storm event from $327 million to $480 million.



The brokers have contacted numerous insurers/reinsurers and are assembling a consortium of carriers in order to cover our property insurance renewal. In addition, the brokers have had to bring in new insurers to replace current insurers who will no longer participate in or who have reduced their participation in the current program. The actual layer program and pricing is being finalized.

Our recommendation is to allow us to approve coverage with premiums and fees not to exceed $8 million. We anticipate this will provide enough premium dollars to purchase $50 million in coverage limits. The district would rely on FEMA for damages in excess of this amount. The total package will not likely be finalized before Dec 30, 2011.



The District has previously requested and received confirmation from the State Office of Insurance Regulation and State Office of Emergency Management through the Reasonableness Letter that our property insurance program complies with the Stafford Act requirements from FEMA. The Risk Management Department will again request this confirmation from the state once the property insurance program is finalized.



Beacon Group will be compensated at a 4% commission rate to a maximum cap of $280,000 for their services, and the services of any wholesaler/intermediary used to place the District’s property insurance program.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the purchase of property insurance through our broker, The Beacon Group, Inc., with probable limits of $50 million effective December 31, 2011 to December 31, 2012 at a premium not to exceed $8 million.
