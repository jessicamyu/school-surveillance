The Pine Jog Environmental Education Center through the FAU College of Education will provide a comprehensive science professional development program to fifty K-5 teachers who apply during an open enrollment period. 



The yearly program supports rigorous professional development for teachers in science content and inquiry skills correlated to the Sunshine State Standards Science Benchmarks.  It also emphasizes development and implementation of science literacy [interdisciplinary] instruction for a standards-based curriculum and other instructional strategies that reflect focus areas of the School District Key Results, as well as the preparation of teachers and students for FCAT Science.  The program reflects current research, compliments the adopted science textbook series, links to content and student performance standards, and provides for on-going follow-up activities. 



The benefit of the district is that all teachers will have access to a selection of model lessons and science units produced by the teachers involved in the Pine Jog professional development program through the Pine Jog Environmental Education Center's website (www.pinejog.org).  The potential exists for these lessons to be shared with all Palm Beach County elementary teachers on Learning Village.  Additionally, participants will have the opportunity to share their learned experiences with peers around the district.



Program evaluation will be completed by the Division of K-12 Curriculum staff, Pine Jog staff and teacher participants.  The evaluation consists of teacher feedback forms on program efficacy, documentation of teacher implementation of instruction in the classroom via follow-up implementation surveys, and teacher-generated FCAT-like student assessments.



In 2007-2008, forty (40) elementary schools participated in the Science Summer Institute professional development program as a result of the open enrollment process.  Over the past five years of the current contractual agreement, 257 teachers have received over 5,636 hours of professional development; 5,230 students across the district have participated in interactive, standards-based field experiences in conjunction with integrated science instruction.  Pine Jog Environmental Education Center conducted Schoolyard Inquiry Programs where they provided outreach science inquiry programs to the schools.  The center served 3,400 students K-5 in 2007-2008 with those programs.

District Afterschool Program Tuition Rates were previously adjusted by Board action in April 2005.  Adjustment to the base rate is necessary to :







  • Align student hourly fees to current program costs to facilitate all programs remaining in cost recovery status.




  • Provide access to the same quality of programming for all afterschool programs regardless of program size or socioeconomic levels.




  • Provide base hourly rates for serving pre-kindergarten students in afterschool.




  • Provide base hourly rates for summer camp programming.


Recommended Tuition Rates:



The kindergarten – grade five student tuition hourly fee rate of $2.50 an hour for the regular school year District Afterschool Programs for the 2009-2010 school year with the option for future increases of up to five percent annually by recommendation of the District Afterschool Advisory Committee.



 



The prekindergarten student tuition hourly fee rate of $3.50 an hour for the regular school year District Afterschool Programs for the 2009-2010 school year with the option for future increases of up to five percent annually by recommendation of the District Afterschool Advisory Committee.



 



The summer camp student tuition rate be based in an hourly rate of no more than $3.50 beginning with summer camp beginning June, 2010, with the option for future increases of up to five percent annually by recommendation of the District Afterschool Advisory Committee.
