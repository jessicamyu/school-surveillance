CS1 - Consider Denial of Redemptive Life School of Business Leadership & Global Economics
Meeting
Jan 6, 2010 - Special Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Department of Charter Schools received an application, on August 3, 2009, from Redemptive Life School of Business Leadership & Global Economics ("Redemptive Life") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2010.



The application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A"). 



The instrument and the application process were explained to the applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop that was held on May 28, 2009 (see Exhibit "B").



The District Reviewers were trained on the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Application Process on May 15, 2009 (see Exhibit "C").



The Reviewers evaluated the 21 sections of the written application.  Five of the 21 sections were rated "Partially Meets the Standard or Did Not Meet the Standard," meaning the responses addressed most of the criteria, but lacked meaningful detail and required important additional information (see Exhibit "D").



Partially Meets the Standard:
Section 6 - ESE
Section 16 - Facilities
Section 20 - Finance



Did Not Meet the Standard:
Section 13 - Employment
Section 19 - Budget



The responses to each section that did not meet the stated criteria served as the basis of inquiry for the interview.  Please refer to Step 5:  Applicant Interview (see Exhibit "E").



Step 6, second paragraph in the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process states, "The sponsoring board will only approve applicants that demonstrate quality in all components of the application process. An application cannot have significant weaknesses in some components of the application and still be judged to have met the standard for approval," (see Exhibit "F").  Each applicant received communication that outlined the proposed recommendation, the evaluator's second review following the interview, summary of the interview, and was granted the option to voluntarily withdraw the application or allow the application to be placed on the Board agenda for action (see Exhibit "G").



Pursuant to F.S.1002.33 (5)(c):
"An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than 30 calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ."



A copy of Redemptive Life's application is in the Board Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of Redemptive Life School of Business Leadership & Global Economics and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

THE 2009-2010 SCHOOL IMPROVEMENT PLANS, SAVED TO THE STATE'S ONLINE TEMPLATE SYSTEM, AS WELL AS THE SCHOOL ADVISORY COUNCIL BYLAWS, CAN BE VIEWED AT:



http://www.palmbeach.k12.fl.us/SchoolImprovement



Florida Statute 1001.452  and School Board Policy 2.09 describe the requirements of the school advisory council (SAC).



In accordance with Florida Statutes and School Board Policy, the 2009-2010 school improvement plans have been developed, reviewed, and approved by SAC members based on needs assessments that reflect current student achievement data from multiple data sources, and include objectives with strategies that are focused on reading, writing, mathematics, and science achievement.



The plans include professional development aligned with objectives, contain training and instructional materials, technology, staffing, student support services, school safety, and other matters of resource allocation. The plans also address single school culture as well as appreciation of multicultural diversity. School improvement funds are used primarily for professional development and procurement of instructional materials.



Title I School-Wide plan requirements are addressed in the school improvement plans of Title I schools.



Each school improvement plan meets or exceeds the indicators of District Accreditation High-Quality Standards.



An analysis of the (a) Area Superintendent's confirmation receipts (PBSD 2250) recognizing the respective school improvement plans and bylaws; (b) School's 2009-2010 school improvement plan checklists (PBSD 2248); (c) school improvement plan of each school; and (d) SAC bylaws of each school indicates that 100% of the schools in Palm Beach County continuously work towards exceeding the requirements of Florida Statute and School Board Policy.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the 2009-2010 School Improvement Plans and School Advisory Council Bylaws.

Action (Consent)

The Governing Board of Guided Path Academy ("Guided Path") elected to terminate the charter on December 2, 2009 due to financial challenges.  Guided Path requested the termination of its "Charter" on December 10, 2009 (see  Exhibit "A" attached, the Charter School's Notification Letter).



The School District of Palm Beach County entered into a charter agreement with Guided Path Academy ("Guided Path") to operate a charter school in Palm Beach County on April 24, 2004 and the term of the Agreement was for five (5) years, ending on June 30, 2009.  An amendment to extend the charter was granted by the School Board of Palm Beach County on May 27, 2009, to cover the period of July 1, 2009 to August 30, 2009.  The renewal process was incomplete and additional time was needed to complete a thorough evaluation.  Therefore, the School Board of Palm Beach County approved the Third Amendment for Guided Path extending the charter agreement for ten (10) months from the extended date of August 30, 2009 to June 30, 2010 (see Exhibit "B" attached).



Guided Path served students K-5.  The school will not reopen in January 2010 (see Notice of Termination attached). There is no negative impact to any student.  There are 47 students that are currently enrolled at the school.  The parents have been notified that Guided Path will not reopen in January 2010.



Moreover, none of the teachers are on leave from the School District of Palm Beach County.



Data Panels are attached.



A copy of Guided Path Academy's Contract is on file in the Board office for your review.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board accept the voluntary termination of Guided Path Academy, effective December 31, 2009.

On or about January 15, 2009, Rosa Morris was advised of performance deficiencies and placed on a thirty (30) work day assistance plan for improvement. Ms. Morris was given timely written feedback, specific recommendations for improvement and assistance in areas cited as deficient.  These areas were: Job Knowledge; Maintaining an Effective Working Relationship with Associates/Students; Following Directions Easily and Effectively and Completing Assignments on Time and In Compliance with Directions Given.  On March 4, 2009, Rosa Morris was rated overall unsatisfactory on her annual performance evaluation showing continuing deficiencies in all of the areas previously cited.



 



Rosa Morris’ continued failure to correct her deficiencies constitutes just cause for a recommendation of suspension and termination of employment in accordance with the requirements of the Palm Beach County School District’s Evaluation Process for Levels 16 and Below, Article 16, Section 2,  Agreement between the School District of Palm Beach County, SEIU/ Florida Public Services Union (formerly known as the National Conference of Fireman and Oilers) and Sections 1012.27(5), 1012.22(1)(f) and 1012.40 of the Florida Statutes.



 



 



 



 



 



 



 



 



 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Rosa Morris’ suspension without pay, effective January 7, 2010.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Rosa Morris’ employment effective January 21, 2010 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.
