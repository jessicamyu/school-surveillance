DESCRIPTION:

As a follow-up to the School Board's February 1, 2017 workshop request, District staff have coordinated an informational presentation on Public-Private Partnerships (P3s).  Mr. David Moore of the PFM Group will lead the presentation and provide the Board an overview of P3s and considerations for Florida school districts.  The PFM Group serves as the District's external financial adviser.  District staff and Mr. Robert Gang of Greenberg Traurig will also be on hand to address any questions.  Greenberg Traurig serves as the District's bond counsel and Mr. Gang is well versed in P3 legal considerations.  The workshop is scheduled for one hour and will allow time for Board questions and discussion.

ABC Boundary Workshop

Feb 22, 2017 - 1. WORKSHOP at 3 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
2. Workshop Items
Type
Presentation

DESCRIPTION:

Changes in student attendance boundaries for two (2) High Schools – Forest Hill and Palm Beach Lakes and four (4) Middle Schools – Roosevelt and Conniston, Western Pines and Osceola Creek.

 

On January 26, 2017 the ABC Committee voted to present the following Boundary Change Proposals to the Superintendent:

 

1. Boundary Study FH-1 (Forest Hill to Palm Beach Lakes High).

  • The study provides relief to Forest Hill High, and moves the following SAC's: 113, 114, 115A, 121A, 121B, 121C, 121D, 121E, 121F, 121G, 389A, 390.
  • The study specified that current 11th grade students and their current 9th and 10th grade siblings would be permitted to opt-in to remain at their current SY16-17 schools.
  • Further, each SAC considered in this study is closer to Palm Beach Lakes than Forest Hill High; the average distance of moving the proposed SAC's from Forest Hill to Palm Beach Lakes High is a decrease of 2.6 mi.
  • A community input meeting was held on January 17, 2017 at Palm Beach Lakes High, there were no members of the community who provided comments on this study.
  • The ABC vote was 6-1 with the one dissenting ABC vote due to concerns that the study does not provide substantial enough enrollment relief to Forest Hill High. 

2. Boundary Study R-1 (Conniston to Roosevelt Middle).

  • This companion study establishes a clean feeder pattern from elementary through high school by moving SAC 121E from Conniston to Roosevelt Middle.
  • The study specified that 6th and 7th graders attending Connistion may remain at Conniston.
  • Further, the study moves SAC 121E into the walk zone for Roosevelt Middle; it is currently outside of the walk zone for Conniston (3.5 mi. away).
  • A community input meeting was held on January 17, 2017 at Palm Beach Lakes High, there were no members of the community who provided comments on this study.
  • The ABC vote was 7-0.

3. Boundary Study OC-1 (Western Pines to Osceola Creek Middle)

  • The study provides for balancing future utilization capacity anticipated by the future Westlake and Avenir Developments, by moving the following SAC’s: 421E (partial section of this SAC to include future Westlake Development Residential Pods O-U), 068F and 068H (partial section of this SAC to include future Avenir Development).
  • The study notes that there are currently no existing students in the proposed change areas.
  • A community input meeting was held on December 5, 2016 at Osceola Creek Middle, 2 members of the community attended and both spoke in favor of this study.
  • The ABC vote was 11-0.

 

On 12/16/16, studies in this boundary cycle were shared with the Diversity and Equity Committee. The Committee had no specific comments regarding the proposed studies. However, there was general discussion about concerns that parents in disadvantaged socioeconomic groups may not have the time and means to participate in the ABC and Community Input Meetings.


The Superintendent would recommend to the Board studies R-1 and OC-1, and has study FH - 1 under review. Study FH–1 provides relief to Forest Hill High and in association, Study R-1 will provide a consistent feeder pattern and study OC-1 provides relief to Western Pines by balancing future utilization capacity. For students whose attendance zones are impacted by these boundary studies, but who are allowed and opt to stay at their current school, transportation will be the responsibility of the parent.

 

The boundary study proposals and supporting documentation, including the study, maps and the legal descriptions, are provided as attachments. For additional information regarding this cycle's Advisory Boundary Committee meetings and associated materials, please visit the District's BoardDocs LT site:
