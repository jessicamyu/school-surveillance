On or about June 18, 2007, Charles Ryan became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of Misuse of District Time and/or Property and/or Misconduct.  Furthermore, the allegations included violation of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and/or staff. 



 



On or about June 13, 2007, Charles Ryan, was observed by a School District of Palm Beach County Police Officer engaged in an altercation with a female non-employee while he was driving a District vehicle.  Both parties returned to the vehicle and the Police Officer followed the vehicle to the Central Transportation location.  At that location, Mr. Ryan was observed outside of the vehicle on the drivers’ side, pushing the female on her head.  Based on the investigation by the Police Officer, Mr. Ryan was arrested for Domestic Battery at the scene.  During the Pre-Disciplinary Meeting, Mr. Ryan admitted to using a District vehicle to pick up his girlfriend during work hours and without permission from his supervisor.  According to court documents filed on July 2, 2007, the charge of Domestic Battery was declined by the State’s Attorney’s Office. 



 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Charles Ryan’s suspension without pay, effective October 25, 2007 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the October 24, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Darron Davis; Kenyetta Haywood

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District.

On or about August 16, 2007 , Kimberly Manuel became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of  misconduct and willful disregard for policy and/or directive and/or rule, and intentional falsification of District documents.  Furthermore, the allegations included violations of School Board Policy, 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and/or staff.



 



On or about August 1, 2007, Kimberly Manuel, presented Mr. Falk, assistant principal, with papers she prepared to sign.  The papers included documentation to rehire the daughter of Ms. Manuel, who was released from a temporary assignment by the former Assistant Principal in January 2007.  The paperwork prepared by Ms. Manuel included the statement “Terminated in Error”.   When questioned about the paperwork, Ms. Manuel admitted to completing the paperwork with the intent of having her daughter rehired, she further admitted that she thought since Mr. Falk was new it would be a good time to get her daughter “back on” within the Community School.  

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Kimberly Manuel’s ten (10) suspension without pay, mandatory ethics training, and reassignment to another position, effective October 25, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Darron Davis; Kenyetta Haywood

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District

On or about January 1, 2007 , Cynthia Robinson, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of insubordination and/or failure to follow directive related to reporting leave.  Furthermore, the allegations included violations of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and/or staff. 



 



During the course of the FY06 and FY07, Cynthia Robinson was excessively absent.  The absenteeism was predominantly unsupported by medical documentation and although FMLA was offered by the District, the employee took action on only one instance (February 2 – 27, 2006) to apply for FMLA.  Furthermore, the absences included instances where the employee neither called nor showed for work for several days leading to the submission of six (6) job abandonment letters from the school.  Between July 1, 2006 and June 4, 2007, Ms. Robinson was absent 100 days and 2.5 hours.  The excessive absenteeism was documented and addressed through the progressive discipline process.  Several opportunities for improvement were provided through directive communication to Ms. Robinson. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Cynthia Robinson’s suspension without pay, effective October 25, 2007 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the October 24, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Darron Davis; Kenyetta Haywood

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District.

On or about April 23, 2007, Kim Washington became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of unnecessary physical force on a student and/or threats to students and/or inappropriate language with or to students.  Furthermore, the allegations included violations of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and/or staff. 



 



On or about March 26, 2007, Kim Washington, Bus Driver, was engaged in inappropriate physical force on a 15-year-old male student on a School District bus.  Mr. Washington stopped the bus, pulling over to the shoulder of I-95, to address an altercation between a male and female student.  School bus video shows Mr. Washington stopping the bus, walking back to the male student and yelling at the student to get off the bus.  The video continues to show Mr. Washington grabbing the student and pulling him from his seat, pushing him down into the seat and back up again.  Mr. Washington then grabs the student by the shirt, throws him toward the side of the bus and pins him up against the window on the bus continuing to yell at him to get off the bus.  Furthermore, the video shows that the student made no aggressive movements toward Mr. Washington.

Recommendation:

 



I recommend that the School Board approve Kim Washington’s suspension without pay, effective October 25, 2007 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the October 24, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Darron Davis; Kenyetta Haywood

There is no financial impact to the School District.

On or about December 22, 2006, Daniel Presmy became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of inappropriate physical contact with a student.  Furthermore, the allegations included potential violations of School Board Policy 0.01, Commitment to the Student (2)(c), and/or Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and/or staff, and/or 3.12, Criminal Background Checks, and/or Florida Administrative Code, Department of Education 6B-1.001, Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and/or 6B-1.006 Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida.



 



On or about August 2, 2007, Daniel Presmy, pled guilty and was adjudged guilty of Battery because of inappropriate conduct with a student that occurred on or about December 11, 2006.  The incident involved two students entering Mr. Presmy’s classroom wanting to get an eraser from one of the students in his class. He asked them to leave and they refused.  They stated that a student in the class had a pencil eraser that belonged to them.  Mr. Presmy obtained the item, walked to the door, and returned the pencil eraser to the students.  As he turned away, he was then struck on the side of the head with the eraser but he did not see who threw the eraser.  Mr. Presmy further stated that he went to the doorway and pushed one of the students in the head and told him to leave.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Daniel Presmy’s suspension without pay, effective October 25, 2007 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the October 24, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

There is no financial impact to the School District.
