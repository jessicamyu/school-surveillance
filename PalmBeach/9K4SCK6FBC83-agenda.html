PS 1 - Jonathan Todd
Meeting
Oct 14, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent

Action

Jonathan Todd, a middle school teacher, has been employed by the Palm Beach County School District since August 28, 1991.  On or about May 31, 2007, Jonathan Todd became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the following allegations:  inappropriate language and/or behavior with or to student(s); inappropriate contact with student(s); harassment and/or sexual harassment; and unprofessional conduct by exploiting his position as a teacher for personal gain or advantage.  Some of the allegations against Mr. Todd specifically consist of:  permitting a female, middle school student to sit in his lap on more than one occasion; calling a female, middle school student at 2:00 AM on her cellular phone; encouraging a female, middle school student to apply suntan lotion on his arms and legs on a trip to Key West and conducting himself with the student in a manner that a parent complained that they were acting as a couple; grabbing the buttocks of a female, middle school student; and inviting a female, middle school student to dinner.



The above allegations support a violation of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and/or Staff; State Board of Education Rules 6B-1.001, Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and/or 6B-1.006, Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida; and Section 1012.33(1)(a), Florida Statutes.



On April 2, 2008, the employee relations matter related to the above allegations and violations were removed from the Board agenda as a recommendation for termination to be replaced by a Last Chance Agreement, an agreement for disposition of the above allegations.  Mr. Todd failed to execute the agreement.  Therefore, this issue is returned to the Board for action.



On or about March 18, 2009, Jonathan Todd became the subject of another Employee Relations investigation based upon the following allegations:  insubordination, unethical conduct, failure to exercise best professional judgment and misconduct.  Furthermore, the allegations include potential violations of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; State Board of Education Rule 6B-1.001, Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, and Rule 6B-4.009(2), Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal, Florida Administrative Code, and Section 812.015, Florida Statutes, Felony Retail Theft.



On February 28, 2009, Jonathan Todd was arrested by the Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office for felony larceny retail theft of purchase goods, switch box contents.  Mr. Todd was observed inside the Costco store in Delray Beach by store security concealing computer hard drives inside a box of Tupperware containers.  On checkout, he paid for the containers, but did not disclose to store clerks or cashiers that he had other merchandise inside the containers.  Mr. Todd became a subject of interest to the store security because he had purchased and returned Tupperware on several occasions prior to February 28, 2009.  As Mr. Todd left the store, the store security and a Palm Beach County Sheriff deputy apprehended him with approximately $1200.00 worth of unpaid merchandise in his possession.  Deputy Navarro reported that at the time of his apprehension, Mr. Todd admitted to coming into Costco with the intention of taking the hard drives.



On May 1, 2009, Mr. Todd was advised of Garrity Rights compelling him to  give a statement in regards to his arrest.  Mr. Todd refused to answer any questions which constitute insubordination.



On March 4, 2009, Jonathan Todd was issued a Written Reprimand for violation of the Code of Ethics for the Education Profession in Florida and for failure to exercise best professional judgment.  Specifically, on March 2, 2009 Mr. Todd demonstrated poor judgment when he chose to open and read a personal email while at work that contained a pornographic image that was incidentally viewed by a co-worker.



Based upon Jonathan Todd's continuous misconduct in office, there is clear and convincing evidence to support the termination of Jonathan Todd.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Jonathan Todd's suspension without pay, effective October 15, 2009.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Jonathan Todd's employment effective October 29, 2009 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes

On March 1, 2009, Cheryl McMaster became the subject of an Employee Relations Investigation based on an allegation of violation of School District Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy 3.96 after she was arrested for Driving Under the Influence of Alcohol or Drugs; Hit and Run; Leaving the Scene of a Crash with Property Damage; Reckless Driving and Resisting and Arrest Without Violence.  The investigation resulted in a written reprimand for violation of the aforementioned policy.



On March 22, 2009, Ms. McMaster was arrested for a second similar incident.  The charges for the second arrest include violations of Driving Under the Influence of Alcohol or Drugs; Hit and Run; Leaving the Scene of a Crash with Property Damage; Reckless Driving and Resisting an Officer by Fleeing and Eluding with Lights and Sirens Active and Uninsured Driver which constitutes a second offense as defined in 3.96 Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy.  Furthermore, she also engaged in ethical misconduct, which violated 6B-1.001(3), The Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida.  In addition, she failed to exercise best professional judgment and violated School Board Policy 1.013 (1), Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff.  These violations meet the Criteria as defined in Florida Administrative Code 6B-4.009(3) Misconduct and 5(a) Drunkenness, for Suspension and Dismissal.



Ms. McMaster pled guilty to her arrest of March 1, 2009 and March 22, 2009 for Driving under the Influence of Alcohol or Drugs, Resisting an Officer without Violence, Reckless Driving and Driving with License Suspended or Revoked.



There is just cause to support the termination of Ms. Cheryl McMaster.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Cheryl McMaster's suspension without pay effective October 15, 2009.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Cheryl McMaster's employment effective October 29, 2009 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statute.

On or about May 20, 2009, Ms. Aikeea Howell, School Bus Attendant, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based on allegations of gross insubordination, failure to supervise students, misconduct - willful disregard for policy/directive/rule.  Furthermore, the allegations include potential violation of School Board Policies 1.013, Paragraph 1, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff, 3.27, Suspension and Dismissal of Employees, violation of School Bus Drivers and Bus Attendants Handbook Section X, (D) (8), Duties of Bus Attendant, Section XV, Grounds for Discharge of School Bus Drivers and Bus Attendants and the Collective Bargaining Agreement with Service Employees International Union, Florida Public Union, Article 17 (6), Discipline of Employees (Progressive Discipline).



On January 22, 2009, Ms. Aikeea Howell was found to have been negligent while performing her duties and failed to ensure the safety and the well-being of the students under her care.  Ms. Howell was found, once again, to have been sleeping during her bus route when she should have been properly supervising the students.  This misconduct was also captured by the bus video surveillance camera.



Ms. Howell has received both verbal and written reprimands for previous incidents of this same behavior.  Ms. Howell's willful misconduct and disregard for policy/directive/rule constitutes Gross Insubordination as her constant and continuous intentional refusal to obey verbal and written directives have been ignored.  The direct orders have been reasonable in nature and have been given by the proper authority.  The transportation department has further attempted corrective behavior through the form of progressive discipline which has failed.  Ms. Howell continues to grossly and willfully disregard policy, directives and rules.



There is probable cause to support the termination of Ms. Aikeea Howell.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Aikeea Howell's suspension without pay, effective October 15, 2009.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Aikeea Howell's employment effective October 29, 2009 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.
