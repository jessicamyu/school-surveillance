DESCRIPTION:

Adopt-A-Family of the Palm Beaches, Inc. (AAF) is providing affordable housing to families participating in the Julian Place Program with the intent to bridge the gap between housing stability and educational outcomes in the children in these families.  The purpose of the original Data Sharing Agreement is to share data between the District, AAF, and Geo Education & Research.  Through the Julian Place Program, AAF has been able to provide housing and supportive services to some families whose children attend Highland Elementary School. AAF will be using the data to evaluate the success of the Program and the extent to which participating students show progress in measures of school success compared to their peers and their own history.

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the renewal cooperative agreement with the Sequel Youth and Family Services, LLC to provide alternative educational services from Aug 18, 2022 through June 30, 2023.

DESCRIPTION:

This is the renewal cooperative agreement between Sequel Youth and Family Services, LLC and the School District. Sequel Youth is the new agency that is now contracted by Florida Department of Juvenile Justice to operate the Palm Beach Juvenile Correctional Facility located at the Fairgrounds.

This program is a high risk male, residential secure facility, located in West Palm Beach; FL. Palm Beach Juvenile Correctional Facility is a 98-bed residential treatment facility for male offenders between the ages of 13-18. This facility is considered a Level 8 (high risk) residential facility. The average length of stay for the youth is 9-12 months. Sequel Youth and Family Services will offer many services to our residential youth, including case management, mental health and substance abuse services, medical and psychiatric services. While in the program, youth attend public school, which is provided by the Palm Beach County Public School District.

  1. The goal of the program is the improvement of students’ behavior management, social skills and academic proficiency to ensure a successful transition to their home school or community after completion of the program.
  2. Students receive career counseling and career skills training which improves their chance for employment
  3. Students have the opportunity to prepare and successfully take and pass the GED exam
  4. The school district is responsible for the educational staff and materials needed to provide the educational program.

  • Each district school board shall develop and maintain a master plan of inservice activities for all district employees based on state-adopted standards for high-quality professional development.
     
  • The Master Plan of Inservice Activities shall be reviewed annually by the Department of Professional Development, in collaboration with other District, school, and community stakeholders.
     
  • A letter verifying that the district school board has approved the master plan of inservice activities, and that the plan meets the criteria set forth above, shall be sent to the Florida Department of Education by October 1 of each year.
     
  • Each component shall remain in the master plan for a period of at leave five years.
     
  • Inservice points awarded for successful completion of a component shall be assigned as follows: one inservice point shall be equivalent to one clock hour of participation, or as specified by the master inservice plan based on the competencies demonstrated.

 

The Professional Learning Catalog is the vehicle through which instructional personnel earn inservice points toward recertification of their Florida teaching certificate.  This master plan of inservice activities contains all training components leading to certification.  Instructional personnel must obtain 120 inservice points during the five-year validity period.  Non-Instructional personnel, affiliated with certain employee groups, use inservice points toward incentive programs and verify completion of required training(s).

 

The inservice activities outlined within the Professional Learning Catalog are designed to improve employee effectiveness, teacher practice, and ultimately student performance.  Quality professional learning provides continuous support and development for all staff seeking growth in knowledge, skills, and performance.  Through the implementation of this continuous improvement process, Palm Beach County will continue to raise the knowledge and skill level of all employees while making certain that highly qualified teachers are available to meet the identified instructional needs of our students.

 

The current listing of all active inservice components can be viewed in PeopleSoft's eLearning Management Course Catalog.
