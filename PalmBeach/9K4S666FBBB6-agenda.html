AC1 - Consultant for ESE Settlement
Meeting
Feb 4, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

In September 2008, a Legal Aid Society, Southern Legal Counsel and Southern Poverty Law Center filed a State Complaint on behalf of students with emotional/behavioral disabilities or who manifest behavioral issues, and have been subjected to repeated disciplinary removals totaling more than ten school days and the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People.



The parties agreed to enter into a resolution period during which time a settlement was reached.  The settlement requires the School District to hire a consultant who will:





  • Assist with the implementation of a District and school-wide positive behavior intervention and support program.

  • Develop a training protocol which shall include all schools in positive behavior interventions supports.

  • Assist with the development of functional behavior assessments and effective behavior intervention plans.

  • Make recommendations regarding the appropriate level of related services per week for ESE students who are currently receiving less than one hour of related services. 

  • Assist with the development of strategies, objectives and timelines for significantly reducing the number of suspensions of students who have been subjected to repeated disciplinary removals totaling more than ten school days.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve a consultant as a result of the attached Settlement Agreement, not to exceed $100,000. and authorize the Superintendent to sign all related documents.

Contact:

Ann Killets (akillets@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)
Judith Klinek
Laura E. Pincus

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the School District's special revenue fund (IDEA) is not to exceed $100,000.  The source of funds is the Exceptional Student Education Department IDEA budget.
