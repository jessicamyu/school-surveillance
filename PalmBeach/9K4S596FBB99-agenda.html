Agreement with NMS Management Services, Inc.
Meeting
Jan 14, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Wkshps re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action

NMS Management Services is a licensed, highly experienced drug testing agency that received a U.S. Department of Education Grant for School-Based Student Drug Testing Programs in the School District of Palm Beach County. (Note- NMS is currently an approved vendor; they provide drug testing for the district’s bus drivers). This drug testing program shall begin as a voluntary pilot interscholastic drug testing project in spring 2009 with the boys baseball and the girls softball teams at Spanish River, West Boca, Boynton Beach, Palm Beach Lakes and Seminole Ridge Community High Schools and Pahokee Senior High School.  Based upon evaluation and feedback from all stakeholders, the drug testing protocol will be expanded in SY10 and SY11. A cooperative agreement has been prepared outlining:



                     







    • Program purpose;


    • Background check and insurance requirements;


    • Protocols for the agency representatives to follow at the schools;


    • Documentation, confidentiality, and follow up requirements;


    • Collaboration with the School Based Team at the school site; and


    • Access to student information.


 



Drug testing will begin in the above pilot high schools.  Parents must sign a permission form for their students to participate. NMS Management Services must meet program implementation guidelines.  NMS shall survey all team members anonymously at the beginning of the season and at the conclusion of the season on their attitudes toward and use of alcohol, tobacco and other drugs.  NMS shall follow the standard test protocol for chain of custody and shall test for alcohol and illegal drugs.



 



Parents shall be advised of non-negative test results.  If a student tests non-negative (positive), the student will be referred to the District’s Alternative to Suspension Program for Alcohol, Tobacco and Other Drugs and/or an approved licensed substance abuse treatment provider, as recommended by the Chief of Safety and Learning Environment. 



 



The impact of this project will be measured by the changes in attitudes towards and use of alcohol, tobacco, and other drugs, as measured by the grant evaluators, Pacific Institute for Research and Evaluation.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approves the Agreement with NMS Management Services  for the provision of drug testing services for students athletes  effective January 7, 2009 through June 30, 2011, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the agreement which are required for implementation of the approved activities.

Amendment to the Online Assessment Services Agreement Between The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida and The Princeton Review
Meeting
Jan 14, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Wkshps re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)


The K-12 Division of The Princeton Review has been acquired by CORE Projects and Technologies, Ltd.  Section IV of the Agreement with The Princeton Review stipulates that the Agreement may not be reassigned.  Without an amendment our Agreement will be voided.



 



Since August 2, 2007, the School Board has authorized The Princeton Review (TPR) to provide access to a complete online assessment system for all students in grades 3 through 12.  This program authorized TPR to:





·   provide access to their web platform – (Assessment Center)



·   enable all schools to administer 262 common assessments and 326 mini assessments through the web, by scanners, or by clickers



·   provide extensive content development and online instructional resources



·   provide professional development and related support materials



·   ensure that the technical infrastructure was in place to deliver and score the assessments by computers via the web, scanners, or clickers



·   provide immediate reporting of the assessment results



·   provide data transfers to the School District of Palm Beach County



·   evaluate the implementation and effectiveness of the online assessment program



·   create two grade 2 diagnostic assessments in reading and math (one per subject)



·   import four SDPBC created grade 10 diagnostic assessments in reading and math for the “FCAT-retake” students into Assessment Center



·   create eight science cluster exams (two per cluster) at grades 5, 8, and 11



 



On July 30, 2008, the School Board authorized to provide additional deliverables:





·   create a customized Pareto-like report linking test items to Pre-requisite and Necessary Skills that will direct educators to focus on skills, for which students performed the lowest, and link to supplemental resources for those skills



·   align all reading and math common assessments to the district identified Pre-requisite and Necessary Skills



·   make customized Pareto-like report available to teachers, school and district personnel



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Amendment, which allows reassignment of the Online Assessment Services Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and The Princeton Review dated July 27, 2005.

The Amendment carries no additional financial impact to the district.  The financial impact to the FY 2009 Capital budget remains $1,331,000.00.  The source of funds is the online assessment project as identified in the Five Year Capital Plan.

The K-12 Division of The Princeton Review has been acquired by CORE Projects & Technologies, Ltd.  CORE currently has assets of $55,754,578 and a net profit of $1,766,472 during the first quarter and $2,374,709 profit during the second quarter of this fiscal year.



 



CORE Projects & Technologies Ltd. is a leading provider of best-of-breed end-to-end solutions in the education domain worldwide. The Company employs 850 people and is headquartered in India.  CORE has two additional offices in India, four offices in the United States, one office in the United Kingdom and one office in the United Arab Emirates. CORE utilizes best practices in its operations and technology and is registered as an ISO 9001:2000 and SEI CMM Level 3 Company. In 2007, the Company achieved revenue of $113 million, representing over 100% growth year over year, and ranked 2nd in the Deloitte Technology Fast 50 India.



 



CORE generates approximately one quarter of its revenue from its education offerings and expects to see the largest growth from this segment.  The Company serves education clients in the United States, United Kingdom, Caribbean, India, Sri Lanka and Africa.  In the United States, the Company serves customers in 13 states, representing more than a third of its global client-base.



 



CORE’s offerings in the education domain include products and solutions in the areas of School Management Systems including Assessment Systems, Accountability Systems, Grants Management, Examination Management, Performance Management, Decision Support Systems and many more. CORE has recently expanded into the United States and United Kingdom in an effort to establish itself as a large player in the global education marketplace. CORE has greatly broadened its offering of educational products and solutions and positioned itself as an end-to-end educational solutions provider, giving school administrators the tools they need to provide quality education to students around the world.
