The Pahokee School built in 1924, later named the East Lake High School, was the first institution in the City of Pahokee for the education of African-Americans in a time when African-Americans were only able to receive up to an 8th grade education.  From the 1950's to the 1970's, The East Lake High School served as a staple of educational excellence and community pride and allowed students to excel in academics and athletics in a highly stimulating atmosphere of excellence and perseverance.

This is the annual contract agreement with the Palm Beach Marine Institute (PBMI). This Juvenile Justice Day Treatment Program will serve up to 90 male or female youths who are under probation supervision or community control from adjudication with the department of Juvenile Justice.  The goal of the program is to provide students between the ages of 14-18 with behavior management, social skills and academic knowledge in a therapeutic environment with the intent to exit the student successfully to the home school at the end of the treatment period.  Length of the program is approximately four (4) months, is non-residential and requires an on-site interview.  There is a three-year student follow up with the PBMI case manager for students who successfully complete the program.



The PBMI will evaluate students through the SRI reading, STAR Math and FCAT tests.  PBMI provides the instructional personnel, counseling, classroom facilities, insurance, program evaluation, equipment, supplies and transportation.  The School District will provide free and reduced lunch for eligible students, textbooks and technical assistance.  The PBMI will be reimbursed 94% of the earned FTE for up to a maximum of ninety (90) students for provision of the instructional program and transportation.

The Pine Jog Environmental Education Center through the FAU College of Education will provide a comprehensive science professional development program to fifty K-5 teachers who apply during an open enrollment period. 



The yearly program supports rigorous professional development for teachers in science content and inquiry skills correlated to the Sunshine State Standards Science Benchmarks.  It also emphasizes development and implementation of science literacy [interdisciplinary] instruction for a standards-based curriculum and other instructional strategies that reflect focus areas of the School District Key Results, as well as the preparation of teachers and students for FCAT Science.  The program reflects current research, compliments the adopted science textbook series, links to content and student performance standards, and provides for on-going follow-up activities. 



The benefit of the district is that all teachers will have access to a selection of model lessons and science units produced by the teachers involved in the Pine Jog professional development program through the Pine Jog Environmental Education Center's website (www.pinejog.org).  The potential exists for these lessons to be shared with all Palm Beach County elementary teachers on Learning Village.  Additionally, participants will have the opportunity to share their learned experiences with peers around the district.



Program evaluation will be completed by the Division of K-12 Curriculum staff, Pine Jog staff and teacher participants.  The evaluation consists of teacher feedback forms on program efficacy, documentation of teacher implementation of instruction in the classroom via follow-up implementation surveys, and teacher-generated FCAT-like student assessments.



In 2006-2007, thirty (30) elementary schools participated in the Science Summer Institute professional development program as a result of the open enrollment process.  Over the past four years of the current contractual agreement, 193 teachers have received over 4,100 hours of professional development; 5,230 students across the district have participated in interactive, standards-based field experiences in conjunction with integrated science instruction.
