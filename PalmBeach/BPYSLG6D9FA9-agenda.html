BRD1 Proclamation 20-03 Statement by Michael Casserly, Executive Director, Council of the Great City Schools on the Killing of George Floyd

 

Good Cause Statement:

Good cause exists for adding this item to the agenda now.  With the killings of George Floyd, Ahmaud Aubery, and Breonna Taylor, it is timely for the Board to demonstrate its collective position in condemning these killings and so many others that are steeped in racism, by aligning itself with the statement recently published by the Council of Great City Schools.

Item Added:

BRD2 Inspector General Search

 

Good Cause Statement:

Good cause exists to add this item as one of the three shortlisted candidates has withdrawn his name from consideration.  In order to stay within the approved Timeline, a decision is needed whether the Board wishes to add another candidate, or go forward with the two remaining candidates.

 

- - - - - - - - - -

 

Item Added:

BRD3 Mutual Separation Agreement and Mutual Release

 

Good Cause Statement:

Good cause exists for adding this item at this time.  Due to decreasing State revenue caused by the economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic and the anticipated cuts to State K-12 education appropriates, departments are currently determining budget reductions for SY 20-21.  This separation agreement proposal was very recently considered.  The need to act now is for the Office of General Counsel to determine its current and future budget cuts, and for its planning purposes.  Approval of this item now would be consistent with those goals.

 

- - - - - - - - - -

 

Item Added:

POL-HR1 Policy 3.82 Interim Employee Protocols Due to COVID-19 - Emergency Rule Adoption

 

Good Cause Statement:

Good cause exists for adding this item to the agenda now.  As employees continue to enter District facilities, Board action is needed at this time for employee protocols for safety reasons during this pandemic and to be in accordance with CDC guidelines and governmental orders.  These measures would be temporary with the intent that a full plan would be brought to the Board for adoption as a policy for SY 20/21 within 90 days.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Amendment to the Master Agreement with PowerSchool Group, LLC; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the Amendment to the Master Agreement with PowerSchool Group, LLC to extend the term through June 11, 2021.

 

PowerSchool Group, LLC shall continue to provide Authoring Platform, Test Element Bank, and Assessment Delivery and Reporting System Software License, Maintenance, Support and Professional Services as specified in the Master School District of Palm Beach County Contract and The School Board of Orange County, Florida Contract No. ITX1711358.

 

The PowerSchool Group, LLC platform supports assessment and data analysis as key components of the Instructional process. The platform provides a robust tool for the development, administration (both online and paper), scoring, and reporting of formative assessment results. Using the platform, the district has developed and is administering over 400 quizzes and unit tests aligned to the instruction in the classroom as well as the comprehensive district diagnostic. This tool also helps schools and teachers use the results of these assessments in "real time" as the results are available in the platform within 24 hours. Were the district to discontinue the adoption of this tool we would severely limit the schools' access to formative assessment and timely data. Further, the results of these assessments are integrated within the instructional resources available in Blender.

I recommend the Board approve the attached Mutual Separation Agreement and Mutual Release between the Palm Beach County School Board and Ms. JulieAnn Rico, Esq., General Counsel to the Board; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to sign this Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The School Board of Palm Beach County and Ms. JulieAnn Rico, Esq., mutually agree to an amicable termination of their employment relationship, effective September 18, 2020.  Per Florida Statute, section 215.425, Ms. JulieAnn Rico, Esq., shall receive her regular salary through the date of termination and severance pay for 20 weeks.

 

This mutual separation will provide a significant cost savings to the Board as the Board addresses current and future budget crises caused by the economic impact of COVID-19.

 

It is proposed than in effort to ensure budget savings, the current General Counsel salary and benefits be frozen through FY 2022 and that Deputy General Counsel, Shawntoyia Bernard, Esq., be appointed as "Acting General Counsel" for this period.  The "Acting General Counsel" will fulfill all the duties of the General Counsel based on a contract to be considered by the School Board at the June 17, 2020 Virtual Board meeting.
