Nancy Kinard, Manager of K-12 Mathematics for the School District has been honored with the Kenneth P. Kidd Mathematics Educator of the Year Award by the Florida Council of Teachers of Mathematics. 
 
A product of the Palm Beach County School System, Ms. Kinard began her career in education in Palm Beach County in 1982 where she taught mathematics at H.L. Watkins Middle School.  She has also taught at Jupiter Middle School, Suncoast High School and Palm Beach Gardens High School.  At the district level, she has been a mathematics resource teacher, Title 1 Specialist and the Secondary Mathematics Program Planner.  She has been involved in mathematics education throughout the state having served as the President of the Florida Council of Teachers of Mathematics; participated on numerous Florida Comprehensive Assessment Test (FCAT) committees; served on the Florida Postsecondary Education Readiness Test committee as a reviewer; and was a Frameworks developer for the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards for mathematics. 



Ms. Kinard recently returned to the district from a leave of absence to work in Tallahassee for two years at the Test Development Center where she was in charge of the development of the Algebra 1 and Geometry End-of-Course Assessments for Florida.
 
The Kenneth P. Kidd Mathematics Educator of the Year Award was established in 1976 by the Florida Council of Teachers of Mathematics.  The Award is designed to recognize an individual's outstanding contributions to mathematics education. As the 2011 recipient, Ms. Kinard is honored as an outstanding mathematics educator who has made a significant impact on mathematics education across the state of Florida.

Responding to the increasing number of homeless families with children, President Ronald Reagan in 1987 signed into law the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act, guaranteeing equal access to a free, appropriate public education to children and youth experiencing homelessness.  Every school district in the state has a designated local Homeless Education Liaison who is responsible for ensuring that homeless students' educational rights are observed.  In the 2010-2011 school year, the School District of Palm Beach County identified 1,443 students experiencing homelessness in Palm Beach County.  Student Intervention Services in the Department of Safe Schools, School District of Palm Beach County, has partnered with The Homeless Coalition of Palm Beach County, Inc. to increase awareness of the issues facing homeless youth.

MOU with CTA To Implement a State Improvement Grant for four District Elementary Schools
Meeting
Nov 22, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category

Type
Action
The attached Memorandum of Understanding between the District and CTA was negotiated to implement a competitive State Improvement Grant the District was successful in obtaining that provides approximately $10.4 million over FYs 12, 13 and 14 for four of the District's elementary schools:  Belle Glade ES, Pioneer Park ES, Pleasant City ES and West Riviera ES.  The MOU establishes the terms and conditions of employment of the instructional employees at these four schools for the duration of this three year Grant.  The MOU provides the following:
  • A two-part Pay For Performance Plan and a Complexity Pay Program for the instructional staff assigned to these four pilot schools.
  • The two-part Pay For Performance Plan consists of an individual plan where teachers who are assigned to instruct FCAT tested subject areas may  earn up to an additional $1,500 per school year depending on the Proportionate Yearly Growth (PYG) that is attained by their students.  There is also a Team Pay For Performance Plan where all instructional employees at the school work as a team to improve the PYG of students where each team member can earn up to an additional $5,000 each school year.  
  • A Complexity Pay Program consisting of a location rating, a shortage/critical needs rating and a free/reduced lunch rating is also part of this MOU.  When these ratings were applied to these four schools, each school was rated as a 3 which is the highest rating and means each instructional employee assigned to these four pilot schools is paid an additional $4,000 each school year. 
  • During the second and third years of the Grant, up to 400 additional hours of instructional time with students will be implemented with teachers being required to work these additional instructional hours; and they will be paid their individual hourly rate of pay for working these additional hours.  Any teacher not wishing to remain at a pilot school may exercise his/her right to notify their Principal in early March of the first or second years of the Grant that they wish to leave the pilot school at the end of that school year.  Their wishes will be granted via Unit Adjust Transfers to other District schools that are not pilot schools.
  • Additional professional support staff are added to each school.
  • Each school was granted a Pre-K Program and effective with the second year of the Grant each school will implement a Parent/Grandparent University Project and a Community In Schools Partnership in which the instructional staff will participate.
  • Funding is available to pay for technology in the form of I-Pads for most students and almost all instructional staff along with educational technology training for staff, students and parents to enhance the teaching/learning process at each of these pilot schools.
  • While not a part of the MOU, school-based administrators at each school will be eligible to earn a team-based Pay For Performance stipend each year as well as Complexity Pay.              

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve the Memorandum of Understanding between the District and CTA and its Exhibit 1 in order to implement the State Improvement Grant for Belle Glade ES, Pioneer Park ES, Pleasant City ES and West Riviera ES for FYs 12, 13 and 14.

The changes to School Board Member residence areas come as a result of the 2010 Census and a desire to better balance population distribution among the 7 Board Member Districts.  A priority during this process was to keep communities of interest intact.  Pursuant to Florida Statute §§1001.36(2), "any district school board may make any change that it deems necessary in the boundaries of any district school board member residence area at any meeting of the district school board, provided that such changes shall be made only in odd-numbered years…"

 

 

After reviewing five scenarios, the Board directed that Scenario V be taken forward for public input.  Scenario V included the following changes:

 

 

 

 




     
  • The Glades communities will be represented by one Board Member in District 6 rather than two Board Members.  (Representation by one Board Member was endorsed by the Glades Tri-City Coalition.)
     
  • Likewise, Wellington, the Acreage and Royal Palm Beach are all represented by one Board Member District, rather than being divided by two Districts.
     
  • Western Boca Raton communities are being incorporated into District 5, as is the northeastern part of Boca Raton.  Boca Raton is now represented by one Board Member.
     
  • Western Boynton Beach communities are incorporated into District 3 rather than being split between two Districts, in an effort to reduce voter confusion.
     
  • The communities of Parker Ridge and Prospect Heights have been incorporated into District 2.  (This change was supported by the Hispanic Education Coalition.)
     
  • Singer Island has been incorporated into District 1 due to its geographic proximity to the northernmost District.

 

 

 

 

Several opportunities for public input were provided as follows:

 

 

 

 

1. Visiting the Redistricting website at www.PBCRedistricting.info to view the map and submit public comment by email.
2. Faxing comments to (561) 434-7380
3. Attending one of four Community Input meetings held throughout the county:

 

 

 

 

November 1, 2011 - 6:00 pm at Pahokee High School - Media Center
November 2, 2011 - 6:00 pm at Conniston Community Middle School - Media Center
November 3, 2011 - 6:00 pm at Park Vista Community High School - Cafeteria
November 8, 2011 - 6:00 pm at Palm Beach Lakes Community High School - Media Center

 

 

 

 

Input from these meetings is provided in the public input report.

 

 

 

 

For more information, please visit the website at www.PBCRedistricting.info.

Therefore, the move does not affect the population demographics as there are no people living in this area.

Pioneer Park Elementary School, Pleasant City Elementary School and West Riviera Elementary School.



 



The MOU establishes the terms and conditions of employment of the instructional employees at these four schools for the duration of this three-year grant.  The MOU provides the following:



 



§  A two-part Pay-for-Performance Plan and Complexity Pay Program for the instructional staff assigned to these four pilot schools.  The two-part Pay for Performance Plan consists of an individual plan where teachers who are assigned to instruct FCAT tested subject areas may earn up to an additional $1,500 per school year depending on the Proportionate Yearly Growth (PYG) that is attained by their students.



 



§  There is also a Team Pay-for-Performance Plan where all instructional employees at the school work as a team to improve the PYG of students where each team member can earn up to an additional $5,000 each school year. 



 



§  A Complexity Pay Program consisting of a location rating, a shortage/critical needs rating and free/reduced lunch rating is also part of this MOU.  When these ratings were applied to these four schools, each school was rated as a 3 which is the highest rating and means each instructional employee assigned to these four pilot schools is paid an additional $4,000 each school year. 



 



§  During the second and third years of the Grant, up to 400 additional hours of instructional time with students will be implemented with teachers being required to work these additional instructional hours.  Teachers will be paid their individual hourly rate of pay for working these additional hours.



 



§  Any teacher not wishing to remain at a pilot school may exercise his/her right to notify their Principal, in early March of the first or second year of the Grant, that they wish to leave the pilot school at the end of that school year.  Their wishes will be granted via Unit Adjustment Transfers to other District schools that are not pilot schools. 



 



§  Additional professional support staff are added to each school.  Each school was granted a Pre-K Program and, effective with the second year of the Grant, each school will implement Parent/Grandparent University Project and a Community in Schools Partnership in which the instructional staff will participate.



 



§  Funding is available to pay for technology in the form of iPads for most students and almost all instructional staff along with educational technology training for staff, students and parents to enhance the teaching/learning process at each of these pilot schools.



 



§  While not a part of the MOU, school-based administrators at each will be eligible to earn a team-based Pay for Performance stipend each year as well as complexity pay.
