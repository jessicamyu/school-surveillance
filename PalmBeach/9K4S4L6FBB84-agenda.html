Boca Raton High School students and teacher Sharona Kay have created a project called The Triangles of Truth.  This program honors Holocaust victims by giving charity, in their names, to help stop the genocide in Darfur today.   Schools, organizations, and individuals are selling triangles that read “In honor of Holocaust victim (Name).  I choose to stop genocide in Darfur today.  I was not able to help then.  I am willing and able to help now.”  Students have filled in the name of a different Holocaust victim on each triangle and sell them for $1.00 each.  The triangles people buy are respectfully displayed and all the money raised, over $7,200 thus far, is donated to the Save Darfur Coalition and the Genocide Intervention Network.  The students will present a triangle to each person on the Board.

G1 - Safe Schools - Healthy Students Initiative 2008
Meeting
Apr 16, 2008 - Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion

This grant proposes to continue to carry out the mission of the Prevention Center in the Department of Safe Schools to improve academic and social and emotional learning by implementing an integrated, comprehensive community-wide plan designed to create safe, respectful, and drug-free school environments and to promote prosocial skills and healthy childhood development. This grant, which requires a preliminary Memorandum of Agreement between the district, local juvenile justice and law enforcement agencies, and the local public mental health authority, will provide a continuum of prevention and intervention services to students in secondary Alternative Education. A final Memorandum of Agreement will be submitted within six months of grant receipt. Services will include:



 





  • Training for Alternative Education site staff  in Emotional Literacy, strategies to reduce violence in youth, and strategies to address alcohol and other drug use among youth

  • Comprehensive intervention services for youth with mental health and substance abuse issues through school and community resources (School Based Teams, FACE IT, school based social services staff, Oakwood Center of the Palm Beaches, Drug Abuse Foundation, Drug Abuse Treatment Association, and other providers)

  • Referrals for youth and families experiencing challenges that impact successful school attendance and completion

  • Training for school staff and administration in cultural competency to reduce the numbers of minority youth referred to Alternative Education programs

  • Training and follow-up to ensure that district discipline consequences are imposed equitably

  • Extensive training at Alternative Education sites in writing effective, individualized intervention plans and measuring their success

  • Intensive transition assistance and services for youth who are returning to comprehensive middle and high schools, including but not limited to: social services staff, attendance staff, vocational counselors, staff, etc.

  • Extensive support services to youth to enhance school success including career education, mentoring, and job shadowing

  • An outreach campaign and parenting education program for shelter/foster care children ages 0-5.


 



The impact of this project will be measured in: a decrease in the percentage of students who did not go to school because they felt unsafe; a decrease in fighting incidents; a decrease in students who report past 30 day marijuana and alcohol use; and an increase in the number of students receiving mental health services. An outcome of the grant will be a reduction of referrals of youth, especially minority youth, to secondary Alternative Education schools.



 



A copy of the grant is available in the Board Minutes Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the the submission of the Safe Schools/ Healthy Students Initiative for up to $8,700,000, effective July 1, 2008 through June 30, 2012 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.
