I recommend the Board approve the renewal cooperative agreement with the Palm Beach Sherriff’s Office to provide educational services (ESP) at West and East Detention Facility from February 17, 2016 through June 30, 2017 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This is the renewal of a Cooperative Agreement between the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office Educational Services Program (ESP) and the School District of Palm Beach County.   The ESP program serves the following categories of eligible youths incarcerated in the PBSO Department of Corrections:

 

·         Youths identified as non-ESE up to age 18 and 20.

·         Youths who have been previously determined to be eligible for ESE services up to age 22.

 

The School District reserves the right to determine if a youth is eligible to participate in the ESP until the age of 20, provided if he or she was enrolled in a k-12 school prior to being incarcerated in PBSO’s Detention, then the student is presumed to be eligible.  This eligibility flexibility serves many educational goals that are beneficial to eligible students such as:

 

1.      Prevent a disruption of eligible student’s current educational services

2.      Reduce or prevent high school dropouts

 

The School District is responsible for the instructional staff and materials needed to provide the educational services.

The Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) is available to all school districts for reporting facilities information. District FISH reports are available on-line for all district staff who have access to the electronic data base.

Facilities changes are reported in FISH by District staff as they occur to assure that current, accurate information is always available in EFIS.

I recommend the Board approve award of Bid 16C-33J for the Purchase of Enterprise Wireless Local Area Network Equipment for District-operated schools, in the amount of $4,975,267 (FCC E-rate will fund 80%) for the period of July 1, 2016 through June 30, 2020 with United Data Technologies.

DESCRIPTION:

Purchases under this bid are contingent upon approval of the Federal Communications Commission E-Rate program.  No District funds will be spent until E-rate approval of their 80 percent share.  Once approved, purchases will be made during the four-year project.   

 
The contract resulting from Bid 16C-33J with United Data Technologies for the purchase of Enterprise Wireless Local Area Network equipment is to supplement the existing wireless products with future oriented technology which will be compatible, interchangeable, and interoperable with the existing School infrastructure.
 
The equipment in this bid upgrades older access points, increases availability where necessary, adds density to testing centers, and enhances outdoor wireless access at schools.  The District is preparing for significant wireless needs at schools centers in the upcoming four years.
 
 
The Telecommunications Act of 1996 established a fund by which Schools and Libraries across the Country could access discounts on eligible telecommunications products and services.  The program is commonly known as the E-rate Program.  The eligibility for discounts on internet access, telecommunications products and services, internal connection products, services and maintenance is determined by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC). Funding is made available upon application approval by the Schools and Libraries Division (SLD) of the Universal Service Administrative Company (USAC), which was established by the Act. The discount amount is based upon the numbers of students receiving free and reduced price meals and our District qualifies for a discount of 80 percent.
 
As part of the E-Rate program, the District is responsible for approximately 20 percent of the total cost of products and the remainder is funded directly by the FCC for all E-ratable purchases.  Equipment for non-E-ratable sites may also be purchased off this contract at full cost.
 
United Data Technologies is a State certified MWBE vendor. 
 
Through negotiations, United Data Technologies agreed to reduce their initial bid by $417,088.70.
