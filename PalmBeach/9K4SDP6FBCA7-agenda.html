The purpose of this project is to provide career transition guidance for Palm Beach County’s at risk and adjudicated youth.  The project’s goals and activities will focus on providing opportunities for at risk and adjudicated youths 13 to 17 years of age in the nine (9) DJJ and Youth Services educational facilities to support the skills needed for success in the workplace and the world. 



 



The Career/Graduation Counselor will work directly with students to develop individualized Career Support Plans that will focus on career readiness assessments which will measure abilities to apply skills on the job; curriculum focuses on applied math, reading for information, and locating information that is aligned to FCAT, TABE, ACT and GED. In addition, individualized counseling based on students' personal career and postsecondary goals will be provided. 



 



Through collaboration with students, parents, teachers, business partners, community resources, career centers, and postsecondary/vocational schools, the Career/Graduation Counselor will promote supportive learning environments through graduation and career counseling, postsecondary/vocational awareness activities with guest speakers, mentoring programs; professional development to PBC teachers and guidance counselors, and monitoring student progress.



 



A copy of the Grant application will be on file in the Board Minutes office.

P9 Approve the request for Renewal of the Banking Services Contract





  • The current negotiated banking services contract is for one year with a one year renewal and was approved by Board action on November 12, 2008.  The contracts initial term expires on November 4, 2009.


  • The renewal period begins on November 5, 2009 and expires on November 4, 2010. 


  • Due to the unusual conditions in the banking industry, it is recommended that the District renew the negotiated contracts with our current providers for one additional year. 


  • The District's current banks, as well as other competing banks, have agreed it is in the best interest of the District to extend the current banking contracts.  The District's Investment Advisor has also agreed that an RFP would not be an effective tool in this financial environment. 


  • The Finance Committee approved this recommendation with a unanimous vote at their meeting on August 28, 2009. 


  • Once the financial markets have stabilized, an RFP will be issued to ensure the District continues to receive the required services at the most competitive prices possible. 


  • There are two portions of the banking services contracts:




1. The general banking services (checks, deposits, etc) are handled by Bank of America.  Lee County Schools issued an RFP and received responses in April 2008.  Bank of America has agreed to honor all the terms of our existing contract and reduce pricing when necessary to match the prices offered to Lee County Schools.  This will reduce the annual fees for general banking services by approximately $19,000 per year.



2. The custody services (holding the District's investments) were originally to First Union/Wachovia Bank.  Wachovia subsequently sold that portion of their business to US Bank.  US Bank has agreed to honor the original terms of the contract as awarded in 2002.





  • The contract with Bank of America also includes reduced pricing for our schools' internal accounts.  Additionally, the contract includes special programs available to all District employees such as free or discounted banking, teacher loan programs and special mortgage programs.


  • Due to the number of bank failures and the increased level of FDIC insurance on all bank accounts, the cost of FDIC insurance has increased dramatically over the last year.  Bank of America agreed to increase the interest rate on all District accounts to 0.70%, which will more than offset the FDIC fees.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Banking Contracts for General Banking Services with Bank of America and Custody Services with US Bank.
