Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the five (5) year Initial Charter with SLAM Academy High School Palm Beach, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from SLAM Academy High School Palm Beach in February 2018.  The application for SLAM Academy High School Palm Beach was approved on December 5, 2018, as Item CS3 during a Special Board Meeting. 

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing on July 1, 2019 and ending on June 30, 2024, except as provided in the charter.  

 

SLAM’s educational program design is grounded on the philosophy of the three R’s: Rigor, Relevance, and Relationships. The three R’s represent the essential components that must be in place to effectively prepare students for the demands of the 21st Century. Attributed to the work of Dr. Bill Daggett, the three R’s model has been linked by research to higher attendance and graduation rates, stronger test scores, and safer schools. 

 

SLAM Academy combines the three R’s with an innovative approach to engage students.

 

SLAM Academy integrates sports themes into the Florida Standards-based curriculum to keep students engaged.  This supports interdisciplinary teaching by providing a vehicle for integrating content areas in a way that makes sense to children.  It helps them make connections in order to transfer knowledge they acquire and apply it meaningful ways

 

Career Academies—The secondary program (9-12) offers a career academy model that produces college-bound students.  Students will choose one of the following career academies that will prepare students for majors in sports-related careers:

•           Sports Medicine

•           Sports Broadcasting and Journalism

•           Sports Marketing, Entertainment Arts and Management

 

SLAM’s use of career academies is research based validating that “career academies are the most durable and best-tested component of reform strategy to prepare students for college and careers.”

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing on July 1, 2019 and ending on June 30, 2024, except as provided in the charter. 

 

The proposed school’s educational program intends to fulfill the school’s mission of maximizing student academic achievement through an arts-integrated curriculum designed to foster the development of accountable, 21st century learners in a safe and enriching environment. Through a rigorous and well-rounded educational program, in accordance with the Palm Beach County Student Progression Plan and following a Florida standards aligned scope and sequence (see attached sample), students will be better prepared for success in middle and high school, and subsequently, college.

 

Accordingly, the proposed school will combine the best practices developed by this extensive network of innovative Somerset board members, parents, and educators working together for a common purpose, and those principles driving essential school reform nation-wide. The school will meet high standards of student achievement and provide a solid academic foundation that will enable students to be successful at subsequent levels. The curriculum will prioritize mastery of all applicable Florida state standards in all core subject areas, while an emphasis on arts-education will endeavor to support both the academic and socio-emotional well-being of all students. Additionally, the school will offer a rich array of specials/electives in the arts, health, physical education, and world languages.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the award of RFP 19C-043K for Provider of Research-Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County for the period of July 25, 2019 through July 24, 2022, not to exceed $1,500,000 annually and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION: 

Award of RFP 19C-043K for Provider of Research-Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County from Believe to Achieve Educational Services, LLC, Learn-It Systems, LLC and One on One Learning Corp. The eligible Private Schools will have an opportunity to interview the vendors recommended for award and select the vendor that best meets their needs to provide services as described below.

Section 1120 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) requires eligible Title I students in non-public, not-for-profit private schools to receive supplemental educational services. These services also extend to the teachers and families of the eligible students and must be equitable to the services provided to eligible Title I public school children, teachers, and families. To qualify for assistance under Title I, students must meet the following requirements.

  • Reside within the attendance area of a participating public school located in a low-income area
  • Are failing (or at risk of failing) to meet student academic achievement standards

The services provided include but are not limited to:

  • Research-based direct supplemental academic instruction in reading and/or math
  • Needs-based instructional services at various private schools throughout the Palm Beach County School District
  • Aligned materials for evaluation and instructional services
  • Detailed and comprehensive program review at the end of the school year to include disaggregated data; parent, staff and administrative surveys; achievement pre- and post-data; and a summary of services
  • Florida-certified teachers with the responsibility of assessing, planning, implementing and evaluating a directed, research-based supplemental instructional program in reading and/or math
  • Planned parental Involvement activities for parents of participating students.
