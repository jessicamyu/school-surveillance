The Department of Charter Schools received an application, on August 2, 1010, from The Learning Place Academy ("The Academy") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.



The application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application" (see Exhibit "A").



The instrument and application process were explained to applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop that was held on May 27, 2010 (see Exhibit "B").  The Academy's representatives didn't attend the District's charter school orientation.  The meeting is not mandatory but is supplementary to the Florida Department of Education's mandatory applicant training to make applicants aware of the District's procedures.



The District Reviewers were trained on the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Application Process on May 13, 2010 (see Exhibit "C").



The Reviewers evaluated the 20 sections of the written application.  Twelve of the 20 sections were rated "partially Meets the Standard," meaning the responses addressed most of the criteria, but lacked meaningful detail; demonstrated lack of preparation; or "Did Not Meet the Standard," meaning the responses lacked meaningful detail; demonstrated lack of preparation; or otherwise raises substantial concerns about the applicant's understanding of the issue in concept and/or ability to meet the requirement in practice (see Exhibit "D").







  • Section 3 - Educational Program Design - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 5 - Student Performance, Assessment - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 6 - Exceptional Students - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 7 - English Language Learners - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 9 - Governance - Does Not Meet the Standard




  • Section 10 - Management - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 11 - Education Service Providers - Does Not Meet the Standard




  • Section 15 - Facilities - Did Not Meet the Standard




  • Section 16 - Transportation - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 18 - Budget - Does Not Meet the Standard




  • Section 19 - Financial Management and Oversight - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 20 - Action Plan - Partially Meets the Standard


The responses to each section that did not meet the stated criteria served as the basis of inquiry for the interview.  Please refer to Step 5:  Applicant Interview (see Exhibit "E").



The applicant attended the Applicant Interview on December 7, 2010, at which time applicant orally withdrew the application for The Academy.  On January 10, 2011, an email was sent to applicant requesting a signed copy of the withdrawal letter that was given to her on December 7, 2010.  On February 14, 2011, a certified letter was sent to applicant enclosing an additional withdrawal letter and requesting a response within 10 calendar days (see Exhibit "F") or advising that we would move forward to the Board with our recommendation for a denial.  Several dated attempts have been made to communicate with the applicant.  No response was received by the District.



Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(5)(c):
"An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than 30 calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ."



A copy of The Learning Place Academy's application is attached.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of The Learning Place Academy and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

Special Meeting re: SBPBC vs. Paula Prudente Settlement

Category

Discussion

These actions were brought by Paula Prudente, a teacher who has been suspended without pay since November 10, 2010.  The first DOAH case involved a suspension of the employee for ten (10) days without pay and a final hearing was held on November 2-3, 2010.  The Administrative Law Judge filed a Recommended Order on January 24, 2011, rescinding the suspension and the School Board file Exceptions on February 8, 2011. 



The second DOAH case involves the termination of this employee, which was based upon the suspension case previously stated.  The School Board approved the termination on November 10, 2010.  However, since the employee appealed this termination, it was stayed and the file was closed without prejudice on February 17, 2011, pending the approval of this Settlement Agreement. 

Settlement is recommended as being in the best financial interest of the District based upon the terms of the Agreement whereby Prudente agrees to a suspension without pay for ten (10) days and releases the District from any and all claims resulting from the disciplinary actions in this matter.  Prudente will be reinstated as instructional personnel effective April 7, 2011 and she shall strictly comply with any and all District policies and directives.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the settlement agreement between the School Board Palm Beach County, Florida and Paula Prudente and authorize the Chairman and Acting Superintendent to execute all related documents.

The total amount of the settlement is $37,847.65.  This payment will come out of the general fund.

CS1 - LaMensa Academy New Generation School New Application
Meeting
Apr 6, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

I.   Overview



On August 2, 2010, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from LaMensa Academy New Generation School ("LaMensa") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.



LaMensa ("Applicant") proposes to serve a maximum of 100 for grades Pre-K through grade three for first year commencing the fall of 2012; a maximum of 125 for grades Pre-K through grade three for the second year commencing the fall of 2013; a maximum of 144 for grades Pre-K through grade four for the third year commencing the fall of 2014; a maximum of 216 for grades Pre-K through grade five for the fourth and fifth years commencing the fall of 2015.



The mission of LaMensa is committed to the development of the whole child, providing a rich and stimulating classroom learning environment in which certified, trained teachers guide children to reach their full potential as self-reliant, confident, self-disciplined, life-long learners.  Each child is allowed freedom of choice, is encouraged personal responsibility both socially and academically, and is provided guidance in setting individual goals.  Also, to inspire academic excellence; to nurture curiosity, creativity, and imagination; and to do so with an environment rich with warmth, kindness and respect.



Its application was written utilizing the Model Florida Charter School Application (see Exhibit "A-1").  Thereafter, designated School District of Palm Beach County ("District") reviewers evaluated their application using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A-2").



The District's staff reviewers of charter school application have been trained how to evaluate the Model Florida Charter School Application utilizing the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument on May 13, 2010 (see Exhibit "B").



The District's charter application process was explained to applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop held on May 27, 2010 (see Exhibit "C").



LaMensa had two due process opportunities to meet and clarify their application.  Two reviews of the application were conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument ("Evaluation").  The second review included an Applicant Interview as outlined in Step 5 of the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process (see Exhibit "D").



In conclusion, their application is being recommended for denial because the District's reviewers evaluated their application twice with the following final determinations:







  • Section 10 - Management  - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 18 - Budget  - Does Not Meet the Standard


Based on the foregoing facts, LaMensa failed to meet the requisite statutory requirements as stated in F.S. 1002.33(6).



II.   Applicant's Founding Governing Board



The members of the Founding Board are:  Delores Oliver Calloway, Elizabeth Taylor, Julia Bullard, Betty Lawson and Pamela Patterson.



III.   Applicant Attended the Charter School Application Training



LaMensa representatives didn't attend the District's charter school orientation conducted on May 27, 2010.  The meeting is not mandatory but is supplementary to the Florida Department of Education's mandatory applicant training to make applicants aware of the District's procedures.



IV.   District's Application Evaluation using Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Due Process Meetings with the District's Reviewers







  • December 3, 2010 - Application reviewed 1st time by District reviewers - Step #5 of the District's Application Process




  • January 21, 2011 - Application reviewed 2nd time by District reviewers - Step #5 of the District's Application Process


V.   District's Reviewers Evaluation Ratings of LaMensa's Application



The applicant received the Interview Summary (see Exhibit "E") on January 28, 2011.  Designated District staff reviewed the 20 sections (see Exhibit "F") of LaMensa's application that were uploaded for the Board Meeting scheduled for April 6, 2011.  Each section of the Evaluation Instrument received one of the following ratings:  Meets the Standard, Partially Meets the Standard, or Does Not Meet the Standard.  The following rating indicated that their application did not meet the statutory requirement set forth in F.S. 1002.33(6):



VI.   Details Concerning Each of the Evaluation Instrument Ratings for LaMensa







  • Section 10 - Management rating - Partially Meets the Standard




  • Section 18 - Budget rating - Does Not Meet the Standard


The Founding Board members of LaMensa failed to meet the following statutory requirements as outlined in the Model Florida Charter Application.



   A.   Section 10 - Management



The Management Section should describe how the day-to-day administration of the school's operations will be structured and fulfilled.



Statutory Reference(s):



F.S. 1002.33(7)(a)(9); F.S. 1002.33(7)(a)(14)



Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument Criteria:



A response that meets the standard will present:







  • A management structure that includes clear delineation of the roles and responsibilities for administering the day-to-day activities of the school.




  • A sound plan for the recruitment, selection and evaluation of the school leader.




  • A viable and adequate staffing plan.




  • A sound plan for recruiting and retaining qualified and capable staff.


District's Reviewer's Questions:







  • The applicant clarified the recruiting plan via the Applicant Interview.  However, the "Partially Meets the Standard" rating does not change because there was not a staffing plan for each year of the charter term outlined in the Application.




  • The lack of information did not provide for the Budget to include a realistic assessment of projected sources of revenue and expenses that ensure the financial viability of the school, including a realistic FTE projection, as outlined in F.S. 1002.33(6)(b)(2), "In order to ensure fiscal responsibility, an application shall include a full accounting of expected assets, a projection of sources and amounts of income, including income derived from projected enrollment . . ." 




  • Please show in the Application the viable and adequate staffing plan.




  • Please show in the Application a sound plan for recruiting and retaining qualified and capable staff.


   B.   Section 18 - Budget



The Budget section should provide financial projections for the school over the term of its charter.



Statutory Reference(s):



F.S. 1002.33(6)(a)(5); F.S. 1002.33(6)(b)(2)



Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument Criteria:



A response that meets the standard will present:







  • Budgetary projections which are consistent with all parts of the application, including the school's mission, educational program, staffing plan and facility.




  • A realistic assessment of the projected sources of revenue and expenses that ensure the financial viability of the school.


VII.   District's Reviewer's Response:



The application lacks meaningful detail; demonstrates lack of preparation; or raises substantial concerns about the applicant's understanding of the application in concept and/or ability to meet the requirements in practice.



The budgetary projections are not consistent within the application, specifically the instruction and administrative staff, employment and facilities sections.  The assessment of the projected sources of revenue and expenses that ensure the financial viability of the school is not realistic.







  • The budget does not include enough teachers' positions to meet class size reduction.




  • The school states that they will be public employers but the budget does not allocate FRS contribution expenses.




  • The staffing plan is not included in the application.  Budget does not include the following personnel:  additional instructional staff to meet class size, PE, Art, Music, paraprofessionals, and a full time Principal.


The assessment of the projected sources of revenue and expenses that ensure the financial viability of the school is not realistic.







  • Revenues - The applicant included Fiscal Stablilization Funds which end September 30, 2011.  The applicant overstated the transportation revenue using the highest funding to transport ESE students but the school will be serving 251 matrices.  As a result, the revenue shortfall in the first year is $43K increasing to $90K in the fifth year of operation.  The application does not contain sufficient reserves to cover the revenue shortfalls.




  • Expenditures - The applicant understated the expenditures as listed above.


The District prepared an analysis to reflect missing personnel and operational cost.  The net result is a loss putting the charter at risk for being in financial emergency.



Consequently, the Founding Board members of LaMensa failed to meet the following statutory requirements as outlined in the Model Florida Charter School Application.



A copy of LaMensa's application is attached for your review.



VIII.   Appeal Rights of LaMensa



Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(5)(c):  "An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than thirty (30) calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ."

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of LaMensa Academy and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.
