P1 - Biometric Pads - School Link Technologies

Apr 22, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

In May, 2008, Board Agenda Item P2052108 was approved for site-licenses and miscellaneous equipment for the School Food Service Department.  This request is for the purchase of Biometric Key Pads.  This new technology utilizes an automated touchpad to recognize a person based on finger image.  This system does not store fingerprints or images of the finger but is based on selected points of the finger.  It is not possible to generate a fingerprint from the biometric keypad.    This purchase will provide for:



 





  • Greater ease and increased cafeteria line speed over card or pin numbers

  • Increased security as it reduces misuse of accounts by others

  • Reduces administrative time


 



Biometrics is being utilized in other districts across the country such as:  Seminole County, Florida; Lake County, Florida; Griffin County, Georgia; and Denver, Colorado.  Biometrics, in a similar form, is also in use at Disney World and Sea World.



 



The estimated costs to utilize biometrics at all schools per the attached quote are listed below:



 



            Pad+ Biometric Device (Includes Shipping)        $231,994.00



            Less:  Volume Discounts and Trade-ins              ($40,800.00)



            Total                                                                $191,194.00

FM1 - Replacement of Remarketing Agent
Meeting
Apr 22, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

In 2002, The School Board issued Certificates of Participation Series 2002B in a variable rate mode.  Citi (at the time Salomon Smith Barney) was named as the senior underwriter and remarketing agent.  The transaction was backed by FSA (the bond insurer) and Dexia (the liquidity provider).



As the remarketing agent, Citi sets the weekly interest rate based on the market conditions and price required to find or keep buyers for the Certificates. 



From 2002 and throughout most of the instability that occurred throughout 2008, this transaction performed as expected. 



In October 2008, the bond insurer (FSA) was downgraded which meant the transaction was no longer rated AAA.  Since then, the cost of borrowing has increased and Citi has had increased difficulty remarketing the transaction.



Staff has worked consistently with the Finance Committee, Financial Advisor and underwriting team to determine steps to be taken to reduce the costs associated with the transaction.



After much review, it has been determined that the ongoing remarketing of the transaction could be handled more efficiently by JP Morgan.  JP Morgan currently serves as the other senior underwriter in the District’s underwriting team.



When comparing the rates achieved for similar transactions (bonds or COPs insured backed by FSA and Dexia), JP Morgan has priced transactions an average of 90 to 120 bps better than Citi since October 2008.



It should be noted that Citi continues to do an outstanding job remarketing the commercial paper associated with the District’s Sales Tax program. 



The cost of the remarketing would increase from 8 basis points (.08%) to 10 bps (.10%) each year.  The only upfront cost associated with the change is for work done by the District’s bond counsel and financial advisor, which is included in their contracts.  The fees will be paid from the debt service budget as approved in the Five Year Capital Plan.

The Finance Committee reviewed and approved the transaction at their meeting on April 10, 2009.  The minutes of the meetings are available online at http://www.palmbeach.k12.fl.us/finman/financialdivisions/treasury/ntreasury%20fincom.htm

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the replacement of the remarketing agent for the 2002B COP issue; authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign necessary documents.

Michael J. Burke burkem@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

The financial impact to the debt service budget will be a $23,070 increase in the annual fee for remarketing of the certificates and savings of up to $1 million in interest expense.
