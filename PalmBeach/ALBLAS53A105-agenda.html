4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Subject
PIR4 Interlocal Agreement with the Loxahatchee River Environmental Control District for Sanitary Sewer Connection at Jupiter Farms Elementary
Meeting
May 10, 2017 - 3. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

  1. The School Board of Palm Beach County and the Loxahatchee River Environmental Control District (LRECD) entered into an Interlocal Agreement for the operation and maintenance of an on-site Package Treatment Plant when the School was originally constructed in 1990.
     
  2. The Package Treatment Plant is owned and operated by the School Board of Palm Beach County.
     
  3. The Package Treatment Plant is no longer performing well and fails to meet the Public Health Department’s Code for nitrate limit.
     
  4. The School District supports the removal of septic fields (Package Treatment Plants) and has connected numerous schools to the regional systems in the past due to environmental considerations and to promote public health.
     
  5. The Public Health Department cited the School District for not addressing the deteriorating condition of the Package Treatment Plant and has recommended that Jupiter Farms Elementary School connect directly to LRECD Wastewater Treatment facility through the regional sewer service.
     
  6. The existing Package Treatment Plant must be either replaced with a new one or the School connect directly to the available sanitary sewer service provided by LRECD.
     
  7. LRECD is willing and has the capacity to undertake the design and construction of the project for the School.
     
  8. Replacing the Package Treatment Plant is costly and the new plant will eventually fail again.
     
  9. The proposed connection to the sewer service will serve the interest of the School and improve the ground water quality for the community.
     
  10. LRECD proposes to remove the failing plant from the school property and to connect the School directly to the sanitary sewer system.
     
  11. The School Board of Palm Beach County authorizes staff to grant to LRECD a temporary construction easement to allow access to the Jupiter Farms Elementary School property proximate to the proposed wastewater infrastructure and a permanent easement for maintenance.
     
  12. LRECD will invoice the costs of the project as they are incurred without a markup.
     
  13. The expected time to complete the work is one year from the date of commencement.  The total cost is estimated at $1,043,000.  Should the costs exceed $1,043,000 School Board approval will be required for the difference in cost above $1,043,000.
     
  14. LRECD agrees to own, operate maintain the rehabilitated Jupiter Farms Elementary School’s wastewater pumping station and off-site force main in perpetuity.
     
  15. LRECD, as owner and operator of the force main, will not declare the wastewater force main “available” for residential properties for a minimum period of 10 years.

The Commit 2B Fit School Program is a comprehensive educational program that teaches children the basics of sound nutrition and the importance of regular physical activity. Commit 2B Fit is a non-profit organization that raises funds to produce and implement programs designed to help children and families adopt habits for a lifetime of wellness.

 

Commit 2B Fit’s core program is a school-based initiative that targets elementary-school children who are just beginning to develop attitudes and habits regarding nutrition, exercise and goal setting. Participating students use the Commit 2B Fit program daily during the entire academic year. Kids receive additional reinforcement through community collaborations and ongoing messaging to promote parent awareness and involvement.

 

The District has 61 elementary schools participating in the Commit 2B Fit program. The following five schools have gone above and beyond to demonstrate that they are model schools:

Recommended Action
Pursuant to School Board Policy 2.591, I recommend the Board approve a Call to Action urging Governor Scott to veto PreK-12 Education Conforming Bill HB 7069 and FY18 State Budget.

DESCRIPTION:

The FY18 State Budget and Prek-12 Education Conforming Bill (HB 7069) approved by the Legislature on May 8 fails to meet the needs of students served by Florida’s traditional public schools.  The PreK-12 Education budget reflects the House of Representatives position; reducing the State Base Student Allocation (BSA) by $27.07 and providing only a 0.34 percent overall funding increase per student.  This is far below the 3 percent increase in student funding included in the Governor’s Budget Recommendation and 2.91 percent originally proposed by the Senate.

 

Rather than adequately fund PreK-12 education through the established Florida Education Finance Program (FEFP), the Legislature has appropriated over $400 million to create and expand new programs through the conforming bill, including “Schools of Hope” and the Best and Brightest Teacher and Principal Scholarship Program.  Through HB 7069, school boards are required to distribute local capital outlay revenue generated by the 1.50 mill property tax levy to eligible charter schools.  In addition, HB 7069 mandates 20 minutes of recess per day for K-5 students attending traditional public schools.  This recess requirement does not apply to charter schools.  HB 7069 places additional restrictions on the school board’s use of Federal Title I funds and impacts more areas than can be listed within this agenda item description.  HB 7069 includes policy proposals from a large number of bills that failed to pass in the regular session.  The lack of transparency and limited 72-hour period for the public to review this legislation prior to the Legislature’s approval on Monday is cause for concern. A copy of the complete 278-page conforming bill (HB 7069) and FEFP budget run is attached as a reference.

 

A Call to Action is warranted to help inform the community and afford them an opportunity to express their views on the budget, particularly in the area of PreK-12 education, to Governor Scott as he deliberates exercising his veto authority.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the merger of Gold Coast Community School with Riviera Beach Preparatory Academy commencing the 2017-2018 school year so all students currently enrolled and eligible to attend Gold Coast would then be transitioned to Riviera Beach Preparatory Academy or another more appropriate school. I further recommend the Board approve later converting the Gold Coast Community School facility to a new adult education center, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to take all necessary actions to implement and to sign all documents related to these recommendations.

DESCRIPTION:

At the May 3, 2017 Strategic School Facilities Workshop, it was recommended to merge Gold Coast Community School with Riviera Beach Preparatory Academy and to later use the Gold Coast facility for a new adult education center.

 

Both of these sites are alternative education sites for secondary students for academic reasons.  This is a measure to optimize resources and therefore, it is imperative to be implemented for the next school year starting July 1.

 

The District needs to provide timely notice to all students, families, and staff relating to this merger.
