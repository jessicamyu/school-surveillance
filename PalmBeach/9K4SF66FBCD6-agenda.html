Subject
PR1 Redistricting - Board Member Districts
Meeting
Aug 10, 2011 - Workshops
Category
Workshop Items
Type
Discussion

DESCRIPTION:

  • The 2010 US Census Data became available in April 2011.  The release of decennial census data is used to evaluate whether districts remain reasonably equal in population.  Planning staff has conducted an evaluation using Geographic Information System (GIS) analysis to determine the population in each Board Member district.
  • This analysis indicates that Districts 1 through 3 have become disproportionately larger than Districts 4 through 7.  The largest discrepancy is between the population in Districts 3 and 7, approximately a 37.5% difference.  Please refer to attachment, "Population by Current District."  A commonly accepted rule of thumb for meeting the statutory standard of "equal in population as nearly as practicable," is no more than a 10% variation between the population of the largest district and the smallest district.  However, given the recent availability of sophisticated technology such as Geographic Information Systems (GIS), redistricting efforts should strive for a smaller deviation from the optimum equal distribution of population.
     
  • Therefore, staff recommends that the School Board should redistrict before the end of 2011.  This is consistent with the County's plan to redistrict the County Commission districts this year.
  • F.S. 1001.36, "District school board member residence areas," does not mandate a deadline for review and adjustment of Board Member district boundaries. However, it does provide that "Any district school board may make any change that it deems necessary in the boundaries of any district school board, provided that such changes shall be made only in odd-numbered years and that no change that would affect the residence qualifications of any incumbent member shall disqualify such incumbent member during the term for which he or she is elected."
  • The traditional factors to be considered in redistricting include:
     
    • contiguity;
       
    • compactness of districts, and
       
    • respect for political subdivisions and communities of interest.
  • The School District retained Miguel De Grandy in May of 1999 to assist with redistricting following the 2000 Census.  The following Criteria were used:
     
    • Equalizing population - minimal deviation
       
    • Maintaining communities of interest
       
    • Geographic compactness - "sensible looking"
       
    • Equity among facilities
       
    • Dispersion of student population
       
    • East-West districts
       
    • Socio-economic diversity
  • At the 5/3/11 County Commission meeting, the County Commission approved the following criteria for use in their redistricting; these criteria were used in the County's 1991 and 2001 redistricting exercises:
     
    • Achieve population equity (10% or less variance among districts population) among Commission Districts
       
    • Adhere to Section 2 of the Voting Rights Acts of 1965, as amended and interpreted through case law
       
    • Follow major natural and man-made boundaries
       
    • Maintain integrity of communities of interest, including municipal boundaries
       
    • Insure compactness
       
    • Contiguity of Districts
       
    • Preserve the core of the existing voter's districts
  • Florida's 2010 Constitutional Amendments 5 and 6 (full text provided as an attachment) only pertain to redistricting of State Legislative and Federal Congressional boundaries; they do not legally apply to School Board redistricting.  However, the Board could direct staff to use the provisions as a guideline when crafting redistricting scenario(s).
  • The District's GIS Coordinator has evaluated redistricting tools available to local governments and recommends the use of the ESRI Redistricting tool, which is free to software license holders.  She has become proficient in this tool; therefore staff recommends the mapping be conducted in-house as a cost saving measure.
  • During the workshop the staff will be seeking Board direction on redistricting criteria and priorities.
