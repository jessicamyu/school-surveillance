Consent

Action (Consent)

Champs Charter School of Wellington filed an Application with the School District of Palm Beach County on August 1, 2011.  The Application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument." 



The District Reviewers evaluated the 19 Sections of the Application.  After a second review, Section 6 still received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard," due to the fact that the concern relating to the pre-referral process for students with disabilities may be viewed as discriminatory.  This can be cured during contract negotiations.  (See Revised Application attached.)



The School District of Palm Beach County denied the Application on February 1, 2012, at a Special Board Meeting.  Champs Charter School of Wellington appealed the Board’s decision to deny the Application and appealed to the State Board of Education.



The Hearing before the Charter School Appeal Commission was held in Tallahassee on June 28, 2012.  After hearing oral arguments by the parties, the Commission issued its recommendation that the Charter School’s appeal be granted.



The appeal came before the State Board of Education on July 17, 2012.  Upon review of the evidence presented to the School Board, the Commission recommendation and hearing transcripts, the State Board of Education granted the appeal of the Charter Applicant and issued a Final Order on July 25, 2012.



Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(6)(c), "The sponsor shall implement the decision of the State Board of Education."  (See Final Order DOE Case No. 2012-2448 FOI attached.)



Data Panel is not applicable.



 

Recommendation:

Per the mandate of the Florida Board of Education, I recommend that the School Board approve the new Application for Champs Charter School of Wellington and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all related documents.

G1: Patterson Middle School Book Club Grant
Meeting
Aug 22, 2012 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)
Author James Patterson, through his ongoing partnership with Public Affairs, offered to fund a program at the middle school level that would promote a love of reading in students by offering them the opportunity to read books of their choice in a safe and nurturing environment.

The grant for the Patterson Middle School Book Club will provide specific funding for professional development, instructional personnel to facilitate the book club, and books for (3) schools:  Pahokee Middle, John F. Kennedy Middle, and H.L. Watkins Middle School.

The Book Club will include an area that will house books specifically purchased or donated for this program, and a consistent time during the week when the area will be available for students to visit, choose books, sit and read quietly, and receive assistance from the facilitator to support the reading needs and goals of the students.  The instructional staff will facilitate the Book Club and provide support to the students who visit throughout the 2012-2013 school year. The goals for this grant are to create a nurturing literary environment, instill positive habits in reading, encourage students to read for the pleasure of reading, increase students' reading proficiency, and provide support to students with reading deficiencies and/or needs. 

The district will monitor the implementation of the book club through a steering committee made up of the school-based administration and district personnel.  The steering committee will meet quarterly to discuss the progress of the program and troubleshoot any areas in need. Outcomes will be evaluated through a pre and post survey given to the students.  

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Patterson Middle School Book Club Grant in the amount of $48,500.00 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents, contracts and extensions related to the program activities through June 30, 2013.

The existing Interagency Agreement with the Boys and Girls Club of Palm Beach County, Inc.  expired on June 30, 2012.  The revised Agreement will be effective from August 16, 2012 through June 30, 2015.



The Boys and Girls Clubs of Palm Beach County CARES (Children Achieving and Reaching Educational Success) Program,  through 21st Century Community Learning Centers grants provides more than twelve hundred students from thirty Title I schools a variety of fun and creative academic and personal enrichment activities. The program's number one goal is to improve the academic achievements of participating youth in Reading/Language Arts, Science and Math.



The 21st CCLC Program operates before school, after school, and during the summer.  This free program offers educational and recreational, social, leadership experiences to all children, with priority provided to low-performing, at-risk students.  Monthly educational workshops are also provided for Adult family members.  The Program employs Palm Beach County certified teachers to facilitate the academic activities.  



Boys and Girls Club has successfully provided 21st CCLC programs for nine years.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement with the Boys and Girls Club of Palm Beach County, Inc., effective August 23, 2012 through June 30, 2015, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and amendments related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

Professional Crisis Management Association, Inc. (PCMA) provides technical assistance to the Department of Exceptional Student Education for students who exhibit very challenging behaviors.  Many of these students are at risk for residential placements.  After PCMA observes and interacts with the student, they provide staff with a comprehensive report outlining additional behavioral strategies which staff should implement, in order for the student to become more successful.  PCMA reviews Functional Behavior Assessments (FBA's) and individualized Behavior Intervention Plans (BIP's) which district staff has completed.  PCMA will act as a role model and coach staff on the recommended strategies as stated in the student's Behavior Intervention Plan or the new recommendations.  If necessary, PCMA will testify at Due Process Hearings, attend Mediations and IEP meetings.  PCMA will provide recertification training to 12 PCMA Instructors.
