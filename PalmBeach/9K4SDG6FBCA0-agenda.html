CS1 - Palm Beach Science, Inc. d/b/a Palm Beach Science Academy - First Amendment

Oct 3, 2012 - Special Meeting 10-03-12
Category

Type
Discussion

July 20, 2011, Palm Beach Science Academy was granted a five (5) year Charter Contract to serve students in grades 6-12, commencing July 1, 2011 and ending June 30, 2016.  The Charter Contract permitted Palm Beach Science Academy to defer opening until August of 2012.  Palm Beach Science Academy has requested, and the Sponsor has agreed, to allow the Charter School to defer opening until August of 2013.  (See Exhibit A)



Section 1(B)(2) on page 1 of 56 of the Charter Contract currently reads:  “Term:  The term shall cover five (5) years commencing July 1, 2011, and ending June 30, 2016, unless amended by the parties hereto or otherwise terminated in accordance with the provisions of this Charter.



Section 1(B)(2) on page 1 of 56 is hereby stricken in its entirety and hereby amended and replaced with the following:  "Term:  The term shall cover five (5) years commencing July 1, 2011, and ending June 30, 2016, unless amended by the parties hereto or otherwise terminated in accordance with the provisions of this Charter.  The Charter School has deferred opening until year three of this Charter in August of 2013.



Section 3(D)(2) on page 13 of 56 currently provides the student enrollment for years 1 through 5 and reads:  "*The School has deferred opening until Year 2."



Section 3(D)(2) on page 13 of 56 is hereby stricken in its entirety and amended and replaced with the following:  "*The School has deferred opening until Year 3, no further deferrals will be granted by the Sponsor, except as provided by Sections (A)(1) and (2) of this Charter, and, if the Charter School does not open by that time, it voluntarily elects to terminate the Charter Contract, without further notice or any hearing.  The student enrollment breakdown in paragraph 1 above will be adjusted to reflect the cancellation of the first two years and Year 5 will reflect the maximum capacity permitted.  The last year of the term of the Contract does not change."



The third sentence of Section 5(A)(1) relating to Deadline to Secure Facility on page 32 of 56 currently reads:  "In the event School is unable to meet this deadline School shall at its option either delay opening for the current year (provided the School will meet the required hours of instruction under Florida law) or defer opening by one year."



The third sentence of Section 5(A)(1) relating to Deadline to Secure Facility on page 32 of 56 is hereby stricken in its entirety and amended and replaced with the following:  "In the event School is unable to meet this deadline, School shall delay opening for the current year, but only provided that the School will meet the required hours of instruction under Florida law."



The second sentence of Section 5(A)(2) relating to Deadline to submit zoning approvals and Certificate of Occupancy on page 32 of 56 currently reads:  "If the Charter School does not have the appropriate certifications by August 1st prior to the first year of operation or the initial opening of classes in a different facility, the Charter School may defer opening by one year or delay opening for the current year, provided the Charter School will meet the required hours of instruction in accordance with Florida law."



The second sentence of Section 5(A)(2) to Deadline to submit zoning approvals and Certificate of Occupancy on page 32 of 56 is hereby stricken in its entirety and amended and replaced with the following:  "If the Charter School does not have the appropriate certifications by August 1, 2013 or by August 1st prior to the initial opening day of classes in a different facility, the Charter School may delay opening for the current year, but only provided that the Charter School will meet the required hours of instruction in accordance with Florida law."



All other terms of the Charter Contract shall remain in full force and effect subject to further extensions of time, as mutually deemed necessary by the parties. 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the First Amendment for Palm Beach Science Academy and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all related documents.

Approve Amendment 2 of the Teacher Evaluation Systems with Learning Sciences International LLC.



By Board Action on June 1, 2011 a one year contract with Learning Sciences International, LLC was approved to meet the requirements in Florida Senate Bill 736 "Student Success Act" which revised provisions in several State Statutes relating to the evaluation of instructional personnel and school administrators. The Student Success Act amended F.S. 1012.34 to require the Department of Education to approve each school district's instructional and school administrator evaluation systems.  Florida school districts are required to establish a teacher observation and evaluation system that meets the following requirements:



1. Alignment with the adopted Florida Educator Accomplished Practices.
2. Practices strongly linked to increased student achievement.
3. Based on contemporary research of effective practices.
4. The principal, direct supervisor and any other individual performing observations will use, at a minimum, the same core of effective practices.
5. Meet criteria listed in s.1012.37, F.S.



The Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) selected Dr. Robert Marzano's Art and Science of Teaching Causal Teaching Evaluation as the State's model. 



On June 20, 2012 the Board approved Amendment 1 of The Learning Sciences International, LLC, agreement which extended the term of the agreement through 6/30/13 and included a new Service Summary at a cost of $209,800.



Amendment 2 to the Agreement establishes a revised Service Summary which re-purposes some of the training included in the Original Service Summary for this fiscal year to better align with training needs and adds the following to the Amended Agreement:





  • Marzano Custom Session for Teacher Training webinars/videos on each of the 9 Design Questions in Domain 1

  • Additional Inter Rater-Reliability Training for Principals

  • Inter-Rater Reliability Training for Asst. Principal/District Office


Definition: Inter-Rater reliability is the extent to which individual raters agree when they observe the same individual, use the same instrument, and apply it the same way they give the same rating.  It is a process, training so that we can ensure better reliability in our use of the instrument this year. 



By approving the revised Service Summary the Teacher Evaluation System with Learning Sciences International LLC., the School District of Palm Beach County will continue to meet the state requirements as well as provide a teacher observation and evaluation system that is research based to improve teacher practices focused on raising student achievement.



Learning Sciences International LLC. has included at no charge supplies and training for this fiscal year totaling $104,200. This is in addition to the negotiated price reduction of $97,000 and the credit of $67,200 from last fiscal year. The total price of this contract with the additional training included in Amendment 2 for this fiscal year is $415,800. 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of Amendment 2 to the Teacher Evaluation System Agreement with Learning Sciences International, LLC and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

IN1 ADOPTION OF HEALTH AND OTHER INSURANCE PROVISIONS OF THE COLLECTIVE BARGAINING AGREEMENTS
Meeting
Oct 3, 2012 - Special Meeting 10-03-12
Category
Consent Agenda

Action (Consent)

The Unions/Associations listed in the recommendation came together as a coalition to negotiate with the District over changes to the Health, Dental, Life and Vision Insurances offered by the District to its employees.  Coalition bargaining on insurances has existed for approximately 18 years.  The coalition members and District negotiators reached a tentative agreement on the changes in the health insurance benefits to be effective January 1, 2013.



The District has shared its expectation of an 8% increase in the cost of health insurance in 2013.  With that in mind, these coalition negotiations have resulted in a unified recognition that employees must continue to increase their awareness of their health conditions and take positive steps to prevent further health problems in the future.  Thus, the adoption of the continuation and expansion of the Biometric and Health Assessment Program is a part of this tentative agreement.



Summary of Changes:





  • Continues and enhances the Healthy Rewards Program to continue the $50 per month premium discount for those employees who comply with the Healthy Rewards Program by published deadlines. 

  • Provides partial year premium discounts for those employees who miss the July 31 deadline, but complete their wellness Rewards requirements by December 31. 

  • Allows employees and their covered spouses to earn points towards the Wellness Rewards Program by substituting certain preventative services with coaching programs for the annual physical.  Also requires the covered spouses/domestic partners, as well as the employee to complete the requirements of the wellness physical, biometric screening and on-line health assessment in 2012 for the discount in 2013.

  • Requires a premium increase of between $6.00 to $60.00 per month on the part of the employees for the High Option HMO and PPO plans. 

  • Provides for no premium increases in the Low Option HMO and continues a zero premium in the Low Option HMO as long as the employee completes the Health Rewards requirments.

Recommendation:

I recommend that pending ratification by the Association of Secretaries and Office Professionals, the Classroom Teachers' Association, the Police Benevolent Association, and the Service employees International Union/Florida Public Services Union, the Board approve the Contract amendments on Health and other insurances to be effective January 1, 2013 and to adopt these same amended provisions for all eligible District non-bargaining employees effective January 1, 2013.

The Title X, Part C - Education for Homeless Children and Youth 2012-13 helps to fund services that comply with the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act. The services facilitate the enrollment, attendance, and success in school of children and youth experiencing homelessness, including foster children awaiting placement. Services to be provided include:







  • Identification of homeless children and youth through communication with schools, shelters, community agencies, and the Department of Children and Families/Child and Family Connections;




  • Removal of barriers to school enrollment for homeless children, including the coordination of
    transportation with the Transportation Department to maintain educational stability;




  • Referral to the appropriate social services agencies in order to receive additional recommended
    services, including summer camp scholarships to homeless children;




  • Homelessness awareness training for school-based and community service personnel;




  • Assistance with aftercare, summer camp and day care services;




  • Referral to School Based Teams;




  • Provision of school supplies and uniforms  to homeless students; and




  • Academic tutoring for homeless students who reside in homeless shelters.


A copy of the grant application is available for review in the Board Minutes Office due to the competitive nature of the grant.

Approve the request for an Integrated Supply Agreement Between Genuine Parts Company (dba NAPA Auto Parts) and the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida



The National Joint Powers Alliance®  (NJPA)  is a public agency serving as a national municipal contracting agency established under the Service Cooperative statute by Minnesota Legislative Statute §123A.21 with the authority to develop and offer cooperative procurement services to its membership and provide volume purchasing discounts to its members. Eligible membership includes cities, counties, governmental agencies, both public and non-public educational agencies, colleges, universities and non-profit organizations and the School District of Palm Beach County is a member in good standing with this agency.



This Agreement is made in accordance with Board Policy 6.14 which authorizes the Board to purchase supplies from a competitively bid proposal or contract secured by another governmental entity or government-related association, in this case the National Joint Powers Alliance (NJPA) Contract No. 010511-GPC. This contract was chosen to piggyback due to the favorable prices and terms offered.



Currently, the Transportation Department operates and manages its automotive parts function in-house from its primary maintenance facility in West Palm Beach and six (6) outlying facilities located throughout the county. As part of the FY13 budget process the Transportation Department's staff was tasked with finding ways to increase effectiveness of its operation, gain efficiencies and reduce the operating budget.   The transportation department has determined that outsourcing a portion of the transportation department's automotive parts function would accomplish all goals.  This Agreement provides for NAPA to provide a comprehensive in-house store operation to include all materials, equipment, parts and supplies required for the operation and maintenance of the School Board's fleet with no less than eighty percent (80%) in-stock availability for all parts requested.  The inventory will include NAPA Products as well as Non-NAPA Products, based on the requirements of the School Board's fleet. NAPA will provide on-going review and adjustment of inventory; access to automotive and heavy-duty replacement parts; and delivery of non-stocked parts. The Agreement provides that the School Board will pay NAPA 10% above Product Costs and for operational costs that were included within the annual budget approved by the Board during its normal budget process.



Over the past years the practice of outsourcing automotive parts functions by both the public and private sector has become more and more the accepted best management practice. A complete turnkey vendor managed parts system, operated within an existing fleet facility, is readily available and comes with a proven track record of success and increased efficiency. NAPA has approximately 250 similar contracts nationally with both private and governmental entities. Several counties and cities within the State of Florida have already converted to this method including: Hillsborough, Sarasota, St. Johns and Polk as well as cities like Lakeland, Miramar, Miami, Coral Gables and Ocala. Palm Beach County approved a contract with NAPA for a similar Vehicle and Equipment Parts Management and Supply Agreement on September 11, 2012.   Nationwide, the trend is also seen in cities including New York City, Boston, Chicago, Detroit and Houston.



By entering into this Integrated Supply Agreement, not only will the Transportation Department eliminate responsibility for excess inventory and parts obsolescence, the Transportation Department will increase the quality of service/performance and realize cost savings by dramatically reducing procurement and transaction management activities.  Quality of service/performance and staff productivity is expected to significantly increase while equipment downtime will decrease.



NAPA Auto Parts will work with the Office of Diversity to identify and utilize School District Certified Minority/Women Owned, Small and/or Local Vendors as part of this contract and will include in the monthly reports submitted to the Transportation Department details of minority participation. 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the integrated Supply Agreement with Genuine Parts Company (dba NAPA Auto Parts) for a period of three years beginning October 4, 2012 through October 3, 2015 with an option to renew for an additional two (2) year period with Board approval and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

This is the fourth year that the School District has operated as a fiscal agent between the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) and K12, LLC.  Several years ago, when full-time virtual school was in a pilot stage, Palm Beach County was one of the pilot districts.  Some districts did not choose to provide virtual at that time from K12.  The FDOE inquired if Palm Beach County would be the fiscal agent for a "Safety Net" program that allowed the students from those districts to access virtual education.  Palm Beach County agreed.  The students are in the accountability of the districts where they reside.  They are not within Palm Beach County School District's accountability.  We report the students for FTE (Full Time Equivalent) and pay the contract price to K12.  The contract makes money for the District.  This year we renegotiated the contract price so that we would continue to be in the black financially.



This contract has been approved in the same format for three previous years. 



We do not execute data panels as there is no student accountability since that resides with the students' home districts.

FM-1 Muni Bond Settlement Agreements from Attorneys General
Meeting
Oct 3, 2012 - Special Meeting 10-03-12
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)




  • State Attorney Generals for roughly half of the States have been working together since 2008 to investigate and pursue remedies for any inappropriate actions in the financial markets.  To date, settlements have been reached with GE Funding Capital Markets Corp, Bank of America, JP Morgan, Wells Fargo and UBS.




  • The Florida Attorney General has the right to sue and negotiate settlements on behalf of governmental entities within the State of Florida.  Generally, they do the investigation, negotiate a settlement and send the affected governmental entities a check, without any consultation to the governmental entity or even an opt-out opportunity.  Since this is a multi-state effort, governmental issuers have been given the opportunity to “opt in” if they wanted to participate in the settlement agreement.  A similar process has been completed for the settlement with Bank of America and all 151 participants eligible to share in the settlement chose to opt in to the program.




  • The School District received two Notice Letters pertaining to these settlements in September:






    • GE Funding Capital Markets Corp (Trinity Plus Funding Company) - Guaranteed Investment Contracts – alleged violations of state and federal laws






      • Investment of proceeds of 2000A COPS – Payment offer of $110,109.31




      • Transaction was competitively bid.




    • UBS – Derivatives – alleged violations of state and federal antitrust and other laws






      • 2003B COPS – Knock Out Option on interest rate swap - Payment of $801,956.11




      • Transaction was negotiated.




  • The District’s Treasury and Legal Departments have conducted conference calls with the District’s Investment Advisor, Financial Advisor and the Florida Attorney General.   We learned the following:




  • The District was included in the settlements based on data received from Trinity Plus Funding Company and UBS.  The settlement amounts were calculated by financial experts hired by the Attorneys General and were not based on an issue by issue analysis.  Models were created to simulate market conditions if the alleged violations had not occurred. 




  • When the District’s transactions were completed, the pricing was compared to market indices and were determined to be at a fair market price.




  • Public Financial Management, the District’s Financial Advisor and PFM Asset Management, the District’s Investment Advisor have reviewed their records and confirmed that the District’s transactions were priced appropriately based on the market conditions at the time.




  • At the time of the transactions, the District had no reason to believe any inappropriate activity occurred.  Documents were signed by the parties specifically stating that the transactions were completed based on all applicable rules and regulations.




  • To “opt in”, the District must sign a release that might prevent the District from participating in class action suits that are in currently underway.  These cases have an expanded timeline and are still in the discovery phase.  During the discussion with the Attorney General’s office, the attorney indicated there is no guarantee the District would be eligible to receive any award from the class action suit even if the District did not participate in these settlements or that the District was even considered to be  a member of the class.  At this point, the District has not received any notice that it is being considered a member of the class in these other suits.




  • The Finance Committee reviewed this information at their meeting on September 26, 2012 and unanimously recommended this item to the School Board for consideration

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the settlement agreements proposed by the Attorneys General and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

Leanne Evans (leanne.evans@palmbeachschools.org)
