The International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF) was recently held in Los Angeles, California and was attended by a record 1,700+ finalists from over 70 countries.  Eight Palm Beach County students qualified for this competition, the most of any region in Florida.   ISEF, which is sponsored by Intel, is the largest high school science research competition in the world.

 

Joshua Hellerstein from Suncoast High School received a FIRST Place Grand Award in the category of Electrical Engineering and $3,000 for the overall competition.  Joshua had already received Third Place at the State Science and Engineering Fair.  He also won Second Place at the Palm Beach Regional Science and Engineering Fair earlier this year.

 

Andrew O’Neill from Suncoast High School received a THIRD Place Grand Award in the category of Energy and Transportation, a FIRST Patent and Trademark Office Society Award and $1,500 for the overall competition.  Andrew had already received First Place and a Grand Award in the Physical Sciences at the State Science and Engineering Fair.  He also won First Place at the Palm Beach Regional Science and Engineering Fair earlier this year.

 

Maria Elena Grimmett from Oxbridge Academy of the Palm Beaches received a THIRD Place Grand Award in the category of Environmental Sciences and $1,000 for the overall competition.  Maria Elena had already received First Place at the State Science and Engineering Fair.  She also won First Place at the Palm Beach Regional Science and Engineering Fair earlier this year.

In early 2014, the Economic Council of Palm Beach County agreed to work cooperatively with the Palm Beach County School District to examine areas where efficiencies in operation might be identified and implemented in order to reduce pressure on the District’s annual budget.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the continuation of the agreement with Florida Virtual School effective July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2014 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents and contracts.

DESCRIPTION:

This Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) is between Florida Virtual School (FLVS) and the School Board of Palm Beach County.

 

Florida Statute 1003.428 requires a completed virtual course for graduation.  This MOU covers the enrollments of students in FLVS courses in blended classrooms in our district.

 

In past years FLVS reported these students for revenue.  New legislation requires that districts report for the revenue and pay FLVS for the cost of the student enrollments. Currently the District reports Blended Learning Classes (BLCs) enrollments for FTE, collects resulting revenue, then pays the FLVS invoice at $267 per semester course.

 

The cost of these enrollments is $878,964.

I recommend the School Board approve the Dual Enrollment Articulation Agreements with Palm Beach State College and Florida Atlantic University.

DESCRIPTION:

The 2013 legislative session passed significant changes in funding dual enrollment. Senate Bill 1514 requires school districts to pay the standard tuition rate per credit hour to public postsecondary institutions delivering dual enrollment instruction on its campuses or at high school sites.

 

For 2014-2016, the standard tuition rate at a Florida College System institution is $71.98 per credit hour (Palm Beach State College). For programs leading to a career certificate or an applied technology diploma, the standard tuition shall be $2.33 per contact hour.  Florida Atlantic University (FAU) will charge its standard tuition rate which is $103.32.

  • For dual enrollment courses offered on the high school campus by college faculty, the school district must reimburse the college for costs associated with the proportion of salary and benefits of the instructor.
  • For dual enrollment courses offered on the high school campus by a high school teacher, the school district is responsible for the college’s actual costs associated with dual enrollment.

The Dual Enrollment Articulation Agreement with Palm Beach State College and Florida Atlantic University is being submitted in accordance with Florida Statute 1007.235.

 

The format of the Agreement aligns with Florida Department of Education requirements, including:

  • Courses and programs that are available to students eligible to participate in dual enrollment
  • Process by which students and parents/guardians are notified of the option to participate in dual enrollment
  • Eligibility criteria for student participation in dual enrollment courses and programs
  • Institutional responsibilities for student screening prior to enrollment and monitoring enrolled students
  • Criteria by which the quality of dual enrollment courses and programs is to be judged and maintained
  • Institutional responsibilities for the cost of dual enrollment courses and programs
  • Mechanisms and strategies for:
  • Reducing the incidence of postsecondary remediation in math, reading, and writing for the first-time enrolled recent high school graduates
  • Promoting career and technical programs of study
  • Improving the preparation of elementary, middle, and high school teachers

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the revised resolution to hold a referendum to seek voter approval to continue to levy 0.25 mills for operational purposes in non-charter District schools in FY2016, FY2017, FY2018, and FY2019; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION: 

  • Florida Statutes Sections 1011.71(9) and 101.73(2) authorize the School Board to levy additional millage for school operational purposes by a local referendum or in a general election for a period not in excess of 4 years.
  • Florida Statutes Section 1011.73 states: "The district school board, pursuant to resolution adopted at a regular meeting, shall direct the county commissioners to call an election at which the electors within the school district may approve an ad valorem tax millage as authorized under 1011.71(9)."
  • On May 13, 2014, the School Board approved a resolution directing the county commissioners to call an election seeking voter approval to continue the existing 0.25 mill levy for school operational purposes for four additional fiscal years (FY2016-FY2019).
  • On June 3, 2014, the Board of County Commissioners considered an agenda item to place the question on the 2014 general election ballot. A majority of Commissioners suggested the Board consider an amendment to the ballot language clarifying the levy will be directed entirely to district-operated schools if that remains the School Board's intent.
  • The attached resolution and ballot language has been amended to clearly indicate the millage will be dedicated "for operational needs of District-operated schools".   
  • State statute provides charter school funding through the Florida Education Finance Program formula and charter school capital outlay (PECO). There is no requirement that local tax revenue generated through a voter approved levy be distributed to charter schools.
  • The School Board has assured voters the funds would be used in accordance with the ballot language and created an Independent Oversight Committee to ensure this occurred. The referendum funds approximately 530 fine art, PE and Choice/Magnet teachers in district-operated schools. The School Board is not responsible for the governance of charter schools and would not be able to control their expenditures or guarantee accountability.
  • This referendum allowed the School District of Palm Beach County to maintain arts programs throughout the recession while other school districts suffered budget reductions in this area. Any dilution of this funding source would require a reduction to the District's operating budget.
