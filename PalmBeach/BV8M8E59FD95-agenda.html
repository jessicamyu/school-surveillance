Recommended Action
I recommend the Board receive and accept the Disposal of Tangible Property Report for the year ended June 30, 2020.

DESCRIPTION:

Florida Statute, Section 274.07, requires the District record the disposal of tangible property in its minutes.  Generally Accepted Accounting Principles require assets which are considered to be nonfunctional or damaged beyond economical repair be removed from the property records.

 

  • 82 buses with an original cost of $8.1 million, which ranged in age from 8 to 21 years old were auctioned or scrapped (the District purchased 102 buses in the past year), 11 other vehicles with original cost of $265K, which ranged from 8 to 27 years old were also auctioned. Disposal of all auctioned vehicles were previously approved by the Board.
     
  • $5.3 Million of IT Equipment (Servers and other storage components) were disposed as traded in for another asset in order to upgrade existing technology in the District. These assets ranged in age from 3 to 13 years old.
     
  • $9.7 million of switches were obsoleted as the IT department is replacing the entire Network with new Switches and Fiber Optics through the E-rate program. Technology changes very quickly and 99 percent of these assets ranged in age from 6 to 10 years old.
     
  • $3.3 million of electronics, Classroom, Furniture, Cafeteria and Maintenance Equipment related items that were obsolete and / or scrapped. 98% of these assets ranged in age from 5 to 38 years old.
     
  • $285K of obsolete software was removed with 100 percent of the assets ranging from 6 to 18 years old.

The program's number one goal is to improve the academic achievements of participating youth in Reading/Language Arts, Science and Math.

 

The 21st CCLC Program operates before school, after school, and during the summer. This free program offers educational and recreational, social, leadership experiences to all children, with priority provided to low-performing, at-risk students. Monthly educational workshops are also provided for adult family members. The Program employs Palm Beach County certified teachers to facilitate the academic activities.

 

Boys and Girls Club has successfully provided 21st CCLC programs for thirteen years.
