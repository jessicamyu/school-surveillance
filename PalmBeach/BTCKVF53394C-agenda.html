Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Amendment with District Hospital Holdings, Inc., d/b/a Lakeside Medical Center, effective October 22, 2020 through June 30, 2025; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Glades Central High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7,000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry-level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

I recommend the Board approve and accept the Education Foundation of Palm Beach County's grant through a cooperative partnership with The Frederick A. DeLuca Foundation in support of a Cybersecurity Academy expansion at Santaluces Community High School in the amount of $750,000. This grant is for a three-year period effective October 22, 2020, through July 31, 2023.

DESCRIPTION:

This 3-year grant in partnership with the Education Foundation of Palm Beach County and The Frederick A. DeLuca Foundation provides $750,000 to expand the existing Cybersecurity CTE Program into a full Career Academy at Santaluces Community High School. This academy is designed to provide an innovative learning environment that meets the needs of students who wish to specialize in the areas of Cybersecurity. Students in this academy will take specialized state approved cybersecurity courses while exploring Cybersecurity and IT-related careers. Participating students will be able to graduate from high school with their diploma, as well as industry certification(s) that will provide opportunities for post-graduate success.

To begin this partnership, Aurora's Voice (now Soil & Soul) worked directly with the Principal and administration at North Grade Elementary under an eBusiness Partnership Agreement.  That year they produced 1,000 pounds of food.

 

Working collaboratively with the District, Soil & Soul intends to provide additional developmental, social, and cultural opportunities for children and the community.  They will be offering classes to teach students how to grow their own food, plant crops, and harvest produce to take home. To ensure the community and students are protected from the sun while they learn, Soil & Soul intends to build a Chickee Hut which will allow the garden to be a year round venue for the neighborhood and North Grade Elementary. Their long term goal is to partner with farmers out west to supplement the produce generated and create youth-led farmers markets to feed more neighbors. Produce that will become available at the TTEG include bananas, papayas, yucca, and moringa which are the biggest crops right now.

 

The produce generated by this endeavor will be shared with the volunteers and neighbors that make it all possible.  Should the garden yield the overflow they are hoping for, they intend to work with the administration at North Grade Elementary to bring more produce into the community through the parents of students at the school and perhaps even at a local food bank.  The expected yield for the year is somewhere between 1,000-2,500 pounds, which may vary depending on various factors, including weather.
