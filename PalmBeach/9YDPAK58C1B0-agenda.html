Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the additional one (1) year deferral of opening of Sports Leadership and Management (SLAM) Middle School, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Sports Leadership and Management Foundation, Inc. to open Sports Leadership and Management (SLAM) Middle School in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on September 18, 2013, as Item CS1 during a Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The Charter Contract was approved by the Palm Beach County School Board on November 5, 2014.

 

The Charter Contract covers a term of five (5) years, commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, except as provided in the charter.  Sports Leadership and Management (SLAM) decided to use the first year of the contract as a planning year; and projected opening in August, 2015.

 

Sports Leadership and Management (SLAM) has requested an additional year of deferred opening in order to accommodate facility needs and projected opening in August, 2016.

 

Sport Leadership and Management (SLAM) will serve students in grades 6-8.

 

Sports Leadership and Management’s (SLAM) mission is, “To prepare middle school students for high school and careers beyond through an innovative and engaging educational program emphasizing sports leadership career exploration.”

 

Attached are copies of the full Contract, Budget, Letter Requesting Deferment, Board Notification and Matrix for July 22, 2015, Agenda Items.

 

CONTACT:

Keith Oswald, Chief Academic Officer, (keith.oswald@palmbeachschools.org)

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D., Assistant Superintendent

James T. Pegg, Director

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The deferred opening will have no financial impact on the General Fund Operating Budget for FY15-16.  However, for years three through five, there may be a loss of revenue to the General Fund Operating Budget of approximately $4.6 million up to $4.8 million.

I recommend the Board approve the amendment to extend the current service agreements with community agencies for a period of 60 days to provide time for negotiation and final preparation of renewal agreements for the 2015-2016 school year.

DESCRIPTION: 

The service agreements with six (6) agencies and city of Delray Beach provide child care and/or camp experiences at thirteen (13) elementary school centers that do not currently have a District afterschool program in operation. The current agreements Board approved on December 17, 2014, (item EAI-8) expire July 31, 2015. The District is finalizing a master template and cost recovery fee assessment to address the financial impact the programs have on District facilities. The extension of the current agreements will provide time to work with school centers and agencies in transitioning to a uniform agreement.
