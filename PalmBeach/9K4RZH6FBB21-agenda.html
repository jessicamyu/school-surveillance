Poinciana Elementary Odyssey of the Mind vehicle team, called “Odyssey Road Rally” came in 1st place out of 57 teams at the World Competition.  Students from around the world competed in the 29th Odyssey of the Mind World Finals.  This competition emphasizes creativity and teamwork. It has grown into the largest international creative problem-solving competition because Odyssey of the Mind makes learning fun while giving students the chance to explore their imaginations and express their creativity.  The team of seven Poinciana students presented their solutions to their problems along with students from all over the world.

Each year, five new competitive problems are presented for the teams to solve. These long-term problems are solved over weeks and months.  Poinciana  team members:  Angelo Sierra, Claire Collins, Giselle Vergara, Mariah Joy, Austin Pomianowski, Craig Carrier, and Bryce Connolly were coached by Gloria Gibson, Poinciana’s SACC Director, and Scott Pomianowski, a supportive parent. CC 2  The School District has had a partnership agreement with the Safety Council of Palm Beach County, Inc., a not-for-profit organization, to provide an on-the-road driving experience to students in Palm Beach County schools. The current program consists of the following:



1. The School District provides $148,500 each year to the Safety Council to pay for the administration of the on-the-road driving program. These funds assist the Safety Council for the on-the-road driving experience to provide professionally trained instructors, sites for weekend classes, advertisements for the program, scheduling of students into behind-the-wheel driving sessions, and maintenance, fuel, management of the office, phone, lights and storage of the vehicles used to teach the courses.



2. In 2002 the State passed the Dori Slosberg Driver Education Safety Act. The program will use a Comprehensive, Multi-Component Approach, including (1) normative education provided by Palm Beach County School District Employee; (2) Information dissemination about the consequences of drugs and violence much of it provided by Life Skills training and (3) conflict resolution training, including social influences training by the Family Counselor.  Parent/Guardian Involvement will be ensured through the implementation of Family Support Contracts, and parent “ownership” of the program, which will be enriched by monthly parent meetings/workshops.



 



The base of the program will lay with Life Skills Training, a state of the art drug abuse and violence prevention program. LST is a school-based drug and violence abuse prevention program targeting youth in grades 6-8. The effectiveness of the LST approach has been well established with over 20 years of rigorous empirical research, including a randomized trail with six-year long-term follow-up data. 



 



The Family Counselor will meet with the students during the designated non-FTE generated class period, Monday through Friday for implementation of the Life Skills Training curriculum. The Family Counselor will also conduct Life Skills Training Parenting Curriculum with the families once per month to assist the parents and/or guardians with parenting skills. This will also serve to assist the parent and/or guardians with creating a partnership with the school setting, which assists the students in enhancing positive attitudes regarding schooling. 



 

The targeted youth population consists of at-risk youth, grades 6 to 8 from high risk zip code areas

including: 33407, 33415, 33404, 33430, 33411, 33401, 33417, 33409 and 33414. They are students who have been suspended or expelled for adverse behaviors.



 



Objectives:





  • 85% of participating youth will remain crime free during the program and for six months thereafter

  • Positive supervision and involvement by parents/guardians will increase by 25%

  • At least 75% of participating youth will demonstrate a decrease in suspensions and behavior referrals as evidenced by school district behavior records

  • At least 75% of participating youth will demonstrate a decrease in truancy as evidenced by school district attendance records


 



Evaluation for the grant program will be evidenced through individual participating student’s school records, as well as reports from the school on the number of referrals/absences a student had (on a monthly basis) prior to the program and during the last months of the students program. Furthermore, a pre/post test will be implemented with students, which will be evaluated on an individual basis.
