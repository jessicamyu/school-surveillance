Meeting
Jun 15, 2016 - 3. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Type

DESCRIPTION:

Approve renewal of the Contract with Edline, LLC dba Blackboard Engage, a bid exempt vendor.

 

Blackboard Engage has been the web publishing solution for schools and classrooms in Palm Beach County since 2004, providing teachers access to web publishing tools that allow them to post news, calendar events, assignments, and links to their school and classroom pages.

 

All parents, students, and teachers are provided with Blackboard Engage accounts to access the resources posted and to see progress reports, diagnostic testing reports, and online textbooks. Schools and teachers may send e-mails to parents and students using the Blackboard Engage system and parents have an easy method for e-mailing teachers from each classroom web page.

 

Blackboard Engage has become an integral part of the communication between schools, parents, and students and is continuously updated with new features using the latest technology to ensure ease of use for parents and students. Visitors who access the SDPBC Edline Portal are in excess of 700,000 annually and over five million pages are displayed in an average month.

 

GradeQuick, an electronic grade book for secondary schools also purchased from Blackboard Engage, has been used by the District for over 10 years. The current desktop application has been updated to an online version with cloud storage. Updates to the GradeQuick Web will significantly improve the ability of teachers to work on their grade books on any device from school or home and provides the District with improved capacity for integrating TERMS information with teacher grade books and school Blackboard Engage sites.

DESCRIPTION:

1.  Northboro Elementary School, located in the City of West Palm Beach, was modernized in 2009.

       

2.  As part of the City’s approval of the site plan, the Historic Preservation Board imposed a condition to rehabilitate building 4 (formerly building 7, prior to the modernization) on the exterior since the School and property are contributing structures in a Historic District.

 

3.  The Educational Plant Survey was amended to declare building 4 as ancillary use since it was no longer needed for the School's use.

 

4.  As part of the modernization budget for the School, funds were included to rehabilitate building 4 on the exterior.  However, the cost to accomplish this exceeded what was budgeted.

 

5. The money budgeted was returned to the capital budget and the building remains boarded up.  The City continues to remind the District of its obligation to restore the building on the exterior.

 

6. The City staff informed the Florida Fishing Academy, Inc., a not for profit Florida corporation, of the vacant building.

 

7. The Florida Fishing Academy submitted a proposal to improve and lease the building for the Florida Fishing academy and would like the opportunity to investigate the feasibility of developing an advanced marine lab and science center on the Property.

 

8.  Through this Agreement, the Board grants exclusive right to negotiate an advanced marine lab and science center lease for the Property in accordance with the terms in the Agreement.

 

9.  The rights granted in this Agreement begin on the effective date of the Agreement and shall end no later than December 31, 2016 or on the date the Board and Academy execute a lease agreement.

 

10.  Prior to the expiration of this Agreement, Florida Fishing Academy shall provide the District with a report from a Florida architect or engineer detailing its proposed renovations and estimated costs.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with West Boca Medical Center, effective June 16, 2016 through June 16, 2019; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

DESCRIPTION:

Autism Spectrum Disorders (ASD) are among the most rapidly growing classifications in special education. The numbers of students diagnosed with ASD increases annually and creates critical need for teachers trained and endorsed to deliver instruction appropriately to these students.

 

The School District of Palm Beach County currently has 42,038 students under the Exceptional Student Education umbrella.  Of those, 3,318 have the eligibility of ASD, with 2,243 on general education standards. Currently, 233 teachers serve these students. There are numerous teachers in Palm Beach County Public Schools who desire further training, as many of these students are in inclusive settings.  Teachers also desire to further their professional development by adding an endorsement in autism to their certificates. Additionally, an endorsement program in autism will facilitate teachers’ acquisition of skills and competencies needed to identify students with ASD, provide the required interventions, and assist in the improvement of student learning and performance. 

 

Specialization requirements for Endorsement in Autism Spectrum Disorder are mandated by the State Board of Education Rule 6A-4.10796. Instructors of students in kindergarten through grade 12 with 100 percent of their students identified as an eligible student with ASD, either as primary or secondary exceptionality, will need to be certified in an exceptional student education area and have an endorsement in ASD. This district add-on endorsement is designed for teachers who currently hold a bachelor’s or higher degree with certification in any exceptional student education area.

·         Forty-nine percent (49%) reduction in Operational type citations.  Schools and facility managers are responsible for correcting Operational citations.

·         Serious citations were reduced by 38 percent.

·         Only ten (10) citations were noted as not meeting the specified corrective action date.  Staff will assign extra attention to these facilities to make corrections immediately.

 

A summary report by facility, detailed individual facility reports and descriptive charts are attached.
