I recommend the Board approve the First Amendment to the Charter and Corrective Action Plan with Belle Glade Excel, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 20, 2012, Belle Glade Excel, Inc. was granted a five (5) year Charter Contract to serve K-5 students.  The Charter Contract expires June 30, 2017.  On March 1, 2017, Belle Glade Excel, Inc. was granted a 1 year renewal contingent on the charter school receiving an FY17 school grade of “D” or better and agreeing to implement a Corrective Action Plan (CAP) to address identified deficiencies in the Educational Plan, Organizational Plan, and Financial and Business Plan.

 

The mission of the school is “to incorporate active parental involvement, community resources, and to utilize the best curriculum to prepare students to be successful in their continuing education. Students will hold themselves to the highest standards of achievement and academic excellence as they have an equal opportunity to receive a quality education and gain and educational foundation on which they can learn and grow.”

I recommend the Board approve the award of Bid 17C-818J for the purchase of Debt Counsel Services through February 28, 2020, not to exceed $80,000 annually.

DESCRIPTION:

Award of Bid 17C-818J for the purchase of Debt Counsel Services.

 

Bid 17C-818J, an alternate source contract (piggyback) off the Miami-Dade County Public Schools, Florida RFP No. 16-029-MT was awarded to Greenberg Traurig, P.A. and Nabors, Giblin & Nickerson, P.A.

 

Services to be provided include, but are not limited to, legal advice and all necessary legal services related to debt issuance including bond/special tax counsel services and/or disclosure counsel services, and other financial issues under the supervision and direction of the Board’s General Counsel. Greenberg Traurig, P.A. will provide services for Special Tax/Bond Counsel and Nabors, Giblin & Nickerson, P.A., will represent the District in the role of Disclosure Counsel.

 

This item was presented at the February 17, 2017, Finance Committee meeting and the committee recommended this action be presented to the Board for consideration.

 

Additional funds may be needed if the District completes any other debt issues over the course of the year but those additional debt issues will be approved by the Board through an agenda item with the anticipated costs to issue the debt included.

 

This purchase is considered a Professional Service as defined in Section 1010.04(4) (a)FS and therefore is being submitted to the Board for consideration in accordance with Board Policy 6.14.

I recommend the Board approve the Tentative Agreement with the Palm Beach County Police Benevolent Association (PBA) effective January 1, 2017, pending ratification by PBA.

DESCRIPTION:

A Tentative Agreement has been reached between the Palm Beach County Police Benevolent Association (PBA) and The School District of Palm Beach County.  The following are the highlights of this Tentative Agreement:

 

Effective January 1, 2017

  1. A 3% increase is awarded to all officers.
  2. A 2% increase to the maximum salary in the Minimum-Maximum Salary Schedule.
  3. No increase to the minimum salary in the Minimum-Maximum Salary Schedule.
  4. Article 3 expanded the definition to include gender expression and/or gender identify.
  5. Article 17.1 (b) changed to allow overtime compensation to be paid on the second (2nd) pay date following the pay period the overtime was earned.
  6. Allow officers to participate in BENCOR.
