Subject
Reconsideration and Appeal Hearing of School Board July 20, 2011 Action
Meeting
Aug 31, 2011 - Administrative Hearing re Built Right

Special Meetings
Type
Discussion

On July 20, 2011, the School Board approved the non-renewal of the vendor, Built Right Construction Inc. (BRC), as it no longer met the uniform prequalification criteria established in Section 4.1, State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) as follows:



-          failure to provide adequate management of projects, and



-          failure to satisfactorily resolve claims by a sub-contractor



 



Such failures are considered contrary to the criteria for the prequalification of contractors pursuant to School Board Policy 7.08, as well as Paragraphs 4.1(1)(a)(3), and (4), SREF.  Back up information to support documentation of the above referenced failures was attached to the July 20, 2011 Board Report.  A link to the July 20, 2011, School Board Report with back up information is as follows:



 



http://www.palmbeach.k12.fl.us/agenda/Wednesday,%20July%2020,%202011%20School%20Board%20Meeting/F7AF65C5-5496-48FA-B263-76E1A7AE04EB-4936A910-CDF2-4FE3-BBB8-0AD2AAF09932.HTM



 



Pursuant to Paragraph 4.1 (h), SREF, BRC shall be given the benefit of reconsideration and appeal by submitting a written request for reconsideration, along with any additional information, within 10 days after receiving notification of the action by the School Board.  On July 21, 2011, District Staff provided BRC with notification of the School Board’s action.  BRC received such notification on July 22, 2011.  On August 1, 2011, BRC timely appealed the School Board’s July 20th action by submitting its written request for reconsideration and attached additional information.



 



On July 20, 2011 the Board ratified the Superintendent’s decision to find BRC delinquent pursuant to Paragraph 4.1 (1)(f), SREF.  See link above which includes back up documentation supporting the School Board ratification.  A contractor becoming delinquent, pursuant to Paragraph 4.1 (1)(f), SREF, and/or no longer meeting the uniform prequalification criteria established in Section 4.1, SREF, represents good cause to revoke that contractor’s prequalification certificate.  Since the School Board both ratified BRC as delinquent, and determined that BRC does not meet the uniform prequalification criteria established in 4.1, SREF, then BRC’s prequalification certificate should be revoked.



 



Paragraph 4.1 (h), SREF states that the School Board shall act upon the BRC’s written request for reconsideration and attached additional information within thirty (30) calendar days after its filing and shall notify BRC of the School Board’s action to adhere to, modify, or reverse the School Board’s original action.  In compliance with Paragraph 4.1 (h), SFREF, on August 31, 2010, the School Board has scheduled a Reconsideration and Appeal Hearing to review BRC’s written request for reconsideration and additional information for the purpose of acting upon BRC’s written request and attached additional information.  Further, BRC will be informed at the hearing as to the School Board’s decision to adhere to, modify, or reverse its original action.



 



Finally, Paragraph 4.1 (h), SFREF, states that the School Board can require additional information to justify the reconsideration.

Recommendation:



I recommend the School Board modify its original July 20, 2011 action by revoking Built Right Construction Inc.’s prequalification certificate for the reasons stated in the description, and authorize the Superintendent and the Board Chairman to finalize and sign all required documents.
