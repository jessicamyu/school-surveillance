The School Board approved the AEMDD grant from the USDOE on October 15, 2014.

DESCRIPTION:

This is a one-year extension to the STEM Partnership with the University of Florida approved by the School Board of Palm Beach County on May 15, 2013.

 

Recognizing the critical importance of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) education in the lives of their students, the School Board of Palm Beach County and its philanthropic partners, including the Community Foundation of Palm Beach and Martin Counties, the Mary and Robert Pew Public Education Fund, and the Quantum Foundation invited the Lastinger Center at the University of Florida to join them to help conceptualize, design, implement, and evaluate a far-reaching multi-partner effort to position the school system as a national leader in the recruitment, development and retention of master teachers in STEM subject areas - teachers who will lead their students to the highest levels of academic success.  Out of this collective effort was born the Palm Beach County STEM Initiative.

 

The Palm Beach County STEM Initiative's funding and resources are targeted at the improvement of teacher quality and student achievement in STEM subject areas across grade levels in Palm Beach County's Title I schools.

DESCRIPTION:  

East Coast Technical Assistance Center (ECTAC) has provided services for the District since 2005.  ECTAC provides the District with the following services:

  1. As a member district, Palm Beach County Federal and State Programs staff are involved in the quarterly Technical Assistance meetings for Title I, Part A and C, and Title I Parental Involvement programs where key United States and Florida Department of Education staff present on topics by request and receive other electronic communication regarding updates on Title I policies.
  2. Technical assistance in the development and implementation of Title I programs consistent with the requirements of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) and Differentiated Accountability
  3. Networking with high performing/high poverty schools across the state for the purpose of sharing effective practices and with 30 or more districts on common issues at the District level.
  4. Advocates for the needs of the District with the Florida Department of Education, especially when audit findings are in question
  5. Capacity development for District staff in the areas of the effective engagement of families in the education of their children, implementation of effective instructional strategies and educational best practices identified in scientifically based research, and effectively utilizing Title I resources and other school improvement reform processes
  6. The School Board of Seminole County is the fiscal agent for ECTAC.

I recommend the Board receive and file the audit of the PeopleSoft Security System (Report No. 2016-10).

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to the Office of Inspector General's (OIG) 2015-16 Work Plan, the OIG performed the above subject audit.  The primary objective of the audit was to assess the adequacy and extent of compliance with the internal controls for accessing the PeopleSoft System at the District.  The School District uses the PeopleSoft System to manage and process the District's human resource and financial transactions and databases through various modules related to financial (e.g., asset management, eProcurement and purchasing, billing, contracts, expenses, general ledger, inventory, project costing, receivables and budget commitment controls) and human capital management (e.g., HR, payroll, benefits administration, time and labor, absences, employee self-service).  This audit produced the following major conclusions which are subject to disclosure:

 

1. General Procedural Controls Appeared Adequate.  OIG reviewed certain procedural controls in managing PeopleSoft access security and the status of corrective actions to address the audit findings identified in the 2014 Audits by the Florida Auditor General and the District's external auditor.  Our review concluded that related corrective actions have been implemented.  Also, the procedural controls reviewed during this audit appeared adequate.

 

2. Untimely Removal of Access for Terminated Employees.  A total of 901 District and charter school employees terminated their employment during February through April 2015.  These 901 employees had a total of 1,021 job records due to the fact that some of them had more than one job assignment.  The review of these 1,021 job records found the 607 (59%) were not timely updated, with delay ranging from one to 118 days.      

 

* For the District, Human Resources enters the termination information into PeopleSoft HR Database upon receipt of the required paper forms.  However, 549 (59%) of the 933 terminated job records were not updated in PeopleSoft until one to 98 days after the jobs were terminated due to delays of submission of forms by department and schools.

 

* Charter Schools are responsible for entering their employee termination information into the PeopleSoft HR Database.  However, 60 (68%) of the 80 terminated job records were not updated in PeopleSoft until one to 118 days after the jobs were terminated.

 

3. A Dedicated Person Is Needed to be Responsible for the Information Security.  For the District,  IT security related duties are assigned to individuals in combination with other job responsibilities, making it less of a  priority and limiting accountability.  IT experts emphasize the importance of securing computer data from unauthorized access.  School Districts similar to ours (Miami Dade and Broward) have a dedicated person to oversee the security program across the District's enterprises.

 

Several findings and recommendations have been redacted from the report in accordance with Florida Statute 282.318(4)(g).

I recommend the Board approve the receipt of the Wallace Foundation Grant in partnership with Prime Time Palm Beach County, effective November 16, 2016 through December 30, 2017; approve any no-cost extension as applicable; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District, in partner with Prime Time Palm Beach County, is in receipt of a Wallace Foundation Planning Grant. The purpose of this grant is to foster children’s social and emotional learning (SEL) through partnerships between school districts and out-of-school time intermediaries (OSTIs). These partnerships are intended to enable school and OSTI leadership and staff to effectively teach and develop these skills in school and out-of-school settings. During the planning period, the following activities will occur: 

  • Establish a leadership and decision-making structure for the partnership;
  • Identify joint goals and measures of success;
  • Collect baseline data; gather input from families, school, OSTI staff, and students to inform plans;
  • Select approximately five elementary school-OSTI pairings that could work together during implementation;
  • Identify evidence-based SEL programming or practices to put in place during implementation; and
  • Develop a detailed, multi-year SEL plan for implementation. 

Upon completion of the planning grant, the District, in collaboration with Prime Time Palm Beach County, will be eligible to receive a multi-year Wallace Foundation Grant to implement the plan.
