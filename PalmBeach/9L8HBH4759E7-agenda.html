I recommend approval of the award of Bid 14C-41K for Research Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County with Learn-It Systems, LLC for the period September 15, 2014 through September 14, 2017.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the award of Bid 14C-41K Term Contract to Provide Research Based Supplemental Academic Instruction to Private School Students in Palm Beach County from Learn-It Systems, LLC.

 

Federal Statute requires supplemental educational services are provided to eligible Title I students in non-public, not for profit private schools. In FY14 more than 564 Title I students were provided reading and/or math services at 32 private schools with 32 part-time teachers. Section 1120 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) requires participating Local Education Agencies (LEA) to provide eligible private school students, their teachers and families with Title I services or other benefits that are equitable to those provided to eligible Title I public school children, their teachers, and their families.   To qualify for assistance under Title I, a student must reside within the attendance area of a participating public school located in a low-income area and be failing, or at risk of failing, to meet student academic achievement standards.

Services to be provided include but are not limited to:

  • Research based direct supplemental academic instruction in reading and/or math
  • Instructional services, as needs are identified, to students at various private schools throughout the Palm Beach County School District
  • All materials for evaluation and instructional services
  • Comprehensive program review at the end of the school year which is to include disaggregated data, parent, staff and administrative surveys, achievement pre and post data and a summary of services
  • Florida-certified teachers with the responsibility of assessing, planning, implementing and evaluating a directed research based supplemental instruction program in reading and/or math
  • Parental Involvement activities for parents of participating students.

Pricing submitted in response to this bid was based on a hypothetical scenario with an estimated number of schools, students and allocation of funds which are all subject to change. As a result of this solicitation process, the cost avoidance for the term of the initial contract is estimated as $320,000. 

 

Learn-It Systems, LLC has committed to use the services of a SDPBC M/WBE vendor as a sub-contractor for 5% of the contract value throughout the term of this Bid.

 

The Spend with Learn-It Systems, LLC will not exceed $1,750,000 in fiscal year 2015. 

 

A formal protest has been filed on this Invitation to Bid and a DOAH hearing has been requested.   Florida Statutes, Chapter 120.57 (3) (c), requires that contract award process stop until final agency action has occurred unless delay would cause an immediate and serious danger to the public health, safety or welfare.  We must provide the non-public school students who qualify and whose schools elect to participate in this program with Supplemental Academic Instruction as required by Federal Laws.  The current contract expires on September 14, 2014.  Therefore, in order to provide services as required by Federal Laws, it is recommended that the award be made as presented.

I recommend the School Board approve the school name change request from North Palm Beach Elementay School to The Conservatory School @ North Palm Beach.

DESCRIPTION:

 

North Palm Beach Elementary is transitioning from an elementary school to a K-8 program and with the addition of a 6-8 instrumental music choice program, a new name is needed to represent the new school structure. The school is essentially comprised of two choice programs, the Kindergarten through fifth grade Choice program and the 6-8 grade Choice program.

 

A 34 member Naming Committee was assembled by the school principal with the opportunity to select, rank and vote for a preferred choice for school name. Notably in attendance and serving on this committee were the Honorable Mayor Aubrey of the Village of North Palm Beach, the NPBE school historian; a school foster grandparent volunteer who has lived in the Village of North Palm Beach since 1979, two teachers who live in the Village, a teacher who attended North Palm Beach Elementary as a student in 1958, a student representative from the incoming 6th grade class, a business partner who lives and does business in the Village, the SAC Co-Chair; also a North Palm resident, and several other North Palm families, families of Choice students, teachers, and staff. 

 

 

The Top 3 Committee Choices:

1.       The Conservatory School @ North Palm Beach  **

2.       The Conservatory School of the Palm Beaches

3.       The Conservatory School

 

** the term ‘conservatory’ reflects the arts education offerings the school provides: music, dance, and theater. Conservatory is defined as ‘a school giving instruction in one or more of the fine or dramatic arts, specifically a school of music.’

 

 

The results are as follows:

Votes of Naming Committee Members

    Choice #1: 32 votes

   Choice #2:  2 votes

   Choice #3 : 0 votes

 

Final re-name recommendation:

 

The Conservatory School @ North Palm Beach

I recommend approval of the Interlocal Agreement with Pinellas County Clerk of Circuit Court & Comptroller for investigative services to be performed by its Inspector General, in accordance with Board Policy 1.092; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

At the request of the Board, the Office of Inspector General sought the services of another Office of Inspector General to address any investigations related to School Board members, the Superintendent and the Office of Inspector General as provided for in Section 5 of School Board Policy 1.092 (Inspector General).  The Pinellas County Clerk of Circuit Court and Comptroller's Inspector General/Chief Audit Executive (Clerk's IG), Mr. Hector Collazo, Jr., indicated an interest in performing such services on behalf of the School Board.  The offices of General Counsel and Inspector General worked with Mr. Collazo and the Pinellas County Attorney's Office to complete the interlocal agreement.

 

The interlocal agreement provides for the Clerk's IG to investigate ethical misconduct complaints, in violation of the Board Policy 3.02 (Code of Ethics); whistleblower complaints, in violation of Board Policy 3.28 (Whistleblower Protection Policy); and, other complaints of wrongdoing not addressed in Section 5 of Board Policy 1.092.  Please see Sections 3.03 and 3.04 of the Interlocal Agreement.

 

Sections 3.05 through 3.09 of the Interlocal Agreement designate a point of contact for investigations.  The point of contact may vary based upon the party being investigated.  Please see the attached chart outlining the point of contacts related to investigations for Sections 3.05 through 3.09.

 

Prior to commencing an investigation, the Clerk's IG is to submit to the point of contact the following:  the projected amount of time needed to complete the investigation, the clerk's staff member(s) assigned to conduct the investigation, and the hourly rate of the staff member(s).  Please see Section 3.10.   The District will reimburse the Clerk's IG for the hourly salary rate, inclusive of benefits, of the assigned staff member(s) ("personnel expenses"); expenses and costs which may include out-of-pocket expenses for express mail, postage, photocopying, and rental which may include out-of-pocket expenses for express mail, postage, photocopying, and rental cars, mileage, meal, lodging and associated travel, in accordance with Board policy and state law ("reimbursable expenses"); and the Clerk's IG in-house legal counsel expenses.  Please see Section 4.02.  Legal counsel expenses are to be invoiced in accordance with the interlocal agreement, and the Clerk's IG must obtain prior approval of the point of contact if legal expenses exceed $5,000.  Please see Section 4.04.

 

The interlocal agreement has been reviewed by the Audit Committee, with the last presentation being at the committee's June 17, 2014 meeting.
