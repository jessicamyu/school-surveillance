I recommend that the School Board approve the renewal of the Contract with the Practical Academic Cultural Education, PACE Center for Girls, Inc. in order to provide Alternative Educational services from July 1, 2022, through June 30, 2023.

DESCRIPTION:

This is the renewal of a contract agreement between the PACE Center for Girls (PACE) and the School District. This Department of Juvenile Justice Day Treatment Prevention Program will serve up to 65 female youths who are placed through the School District Alternative Education Choice Program process. 

The goal of the program is to provide girls between the ages of 12-18 with behavior management, social skills and academic knowledge in a therapeutic environment with the intent to exit the student successfully to the home school at the end of the treatment period.  The length of the program is a maximum of two years, is non-residential and requires an on-site interview. 

  1. PACE will evaluate students through the diagnostic reading and math exams.   PACE provides the instructional personnel, counseling, classroom facilities, insurance, program evaluation, equipment, supplies and continuation of 3 months of counseling and family services while attending their home school. 

 

The School District will provide free and reduced lunch for eligible students, textbooks, transportation and technical assistance.

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the renewal cooperative agreement with the Palm Beach Sheriff’s Office to provide educational services (ESP) at West and East Detention Facility from July 1,2022 through June 30, 2023.

DESCRIPTION:

This is the renewal of a Cooperative Agreement between the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office Educational Services Program (ESP) and the School District of Palm Beach County.   

The ESP program serves the following categories of eligible youths incarcerated in the PBSO Department of Corrections:

  • Youths identified as non-ESE up to age 18 and 20.
  • Youths who have been previously determined to be eligible for ESE services up to age 22.

The School District reserves the right to determine if a youth is eligible to participate in the ESP until the age of 20, provided if he or she was enrolled in a k-12 school prior to being incarcerated in PBSO’s Detention, then the student is presumed to be eligible.  This eligibility flexibility serves many educational goals that are beneficial to eligible students such as;

  1. Prevent a disruption of eligible student’s current educational services
  2. Reduce or prevent high school dropouts
  3. The school district is responsible for the instructional staff and materials to provide the educational services.

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the Interlocal Agreement with Palm Beach County for Direct Connect Radio Interoperability and delegate authority to the Superintendent and General Counsel to finalize the Agreement and delegate authority to the Board Chair and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This Interlocal Agreement with Palm Beach County will provide the District access to the County's Public Safety Radio System and will improve radio communication for the South Region schools to enhance public safety.

 

A few of the services provided are:   

  • Provide access with the County Communications System for 30 handheld mobile radios and control stations that operate in the 800MHz spectrum that have the ability to be programmed and used on the County's System. 
  • Provide access to Common Talk Groups established on the County's System that are made available to the County agencies, municipalities and other non-County agencies for interoperable communications with other municipalities and other non-County agencies for the purpose of providing mutual assistance and planning and execution of on-scene operations.
  • Provide access to County Talk Groups established on the County's System that are made available to County agencies providing for inter-departmental communication, which are reserved for particular departments/agencies and are only available to outside departments by separate agreement.

 

On the date of agenda publication, this agreement was still being negotiated with the County and it is subject to minor changes that are approved by the General Counsel and Superintendent.
