DESCRIPTION

This license agreement with the Colorado Council on Economic Education will help offer the online course, Teaching Personal Financial Literacy in the Elementary Grades, to K-5 teachers throughout the School District of Palm Beach County. It is designed to teach personal financial literacy content and pedagogy. The district will offer a facilitated course for interested teachers, with the possibility of offering the option of two graduate credits for participating teachers. This course will provide elementary teachers with the financial literacy and economics background to provide them with the tools to integrate financial literacy in their classrooms. The district already has an employee trained to be a facilitator, and the licensing fee for using the course will be covered by the financial literacy grant, so there will be no cost to the district, schools, or teachers.

 

The School District of Palm Beach County has generously received a grant to expand personal financial literacy education throughout the District, and this course helps meet those goals. Community donors to the financial literacy grant include: Mary and Robert Pew Public Education Fund, Community Foundation of Palm Beach & Martin Counties, The Celia Lipton Farris and Victor W. Farris Foundation, Wells Fargo, and the Education Foundation of Palm Beach County.

I recommend the Board approve the issuance of the 2016 Tax Anticipation Note and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to approve all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The resolution authorizes the issuance of the 2016 Tax Anticipation Note.
  • The Tax Anticipation Note, or TAN, is a short term financing instrument used to alleviate cash shortfalls in the general fund each year.  Current estimates show that additional cash will be needed in November 2016. 
  • Local property taxes provide the majority of the revenues for the School District.  As the first receipt of tax revenues is expected in late November, it is necessary to borrow money for cash flow purposes until the tax revenues are received.
  • Based on projected cash needs, the District plans to issue a $115 million TAN this year.  The 2015 TAN was also issued for $115 million.
