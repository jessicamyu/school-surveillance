Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board adopt the FY2015 Five Year Capital Plan and the FY2015 Tentative District Educational Facilities Plan.

DESCRIPTION:

  • Each year, the School Board is required to adopt the Tentative District Educational Facilities Plan (Work Plan) prior to the adoption of the budget.
  • The Work Plan contains the same financial data as the Five Year Capital Plan, last presented to the School Board on August 27, 2014, but in a format created by the Florida Department of Education.
  • The first year of each plan is the FY 2015 Capital Budget that will be approved with the FY 2015 budget later today.
  • As required by law, the documents submitted for adoption are balanced.  Staff has also developed a version of the Five Year Capital Plan based on the basic needs of the School District.  This plan, also attached, is not balanced and indicates a shortfall of $837 million for the five years.  A summary of the reductions required to balance the documents is also attached.
  • The projects planned for FY 2016 through FY 2019 (years 2 through 5) can and will be amended as we work through the budget process for those years.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the five (5) year Charter with Pivot Charter School, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(7), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the School District and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.

 

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Pivot Charter School, Inc. to open Pivot Charter School in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014.  The application was approved by the School District of Palm Beach County on October 2, 2013, as Item CS3 during a Special Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.

 

The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, except as provided in the charter.  Pivot Charter School has decided to use the first year of the contract as a planning year; and will open in August, 2015.

 

Pivot Charter School will serve students in grades K-8.

 

Pivot Charter School’s mission is, “To graduate middle and high school students through a unique, technology rich learning environment.  Through our blended learning education model, Pivot students will evolve into autonomous learners and excel in their goals to achieve post-secondary options.”

 

Attached are copies of the full Contract, Budget, Application, Legal Checklist, Board Notification and Matrix for September 10 Agenda Items.

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

There may be a loss of revenue to the General Fund Operating Budget of approximately $1.6 million up to $2.4 million in years two through five.
