PA3-100th Anniversary Delta Sigma Theta
Meeting
Feb 20, 2013 - School Board Meeting 02-20-13 at 5 pm
Category
Presentations
Type
Action

Delta Sigma Theta Sorority is a non-profit organization whose purpose is to provide assistance and support through established programs in local communities throughout the world. A sisterhood of more than 200,000 predominately Black college educated women, the Sorority currently has over 900 chapters located in the United States, England, Japan, Germany, the Virgin Islands, Bermuda, the Bahamas and the Republic of Korea. The three chapters in Palm Beach County are Glades Alumnae Chapter, South Palm Beach County Alumnae Chapter and West Palm Beach Alumnae Chapter.



The major programs of the sorority are based upon the organization's Five Point Programmatic Thrust:



•Economic Development
•Educational Development
•International Awareness and Involvement
•Physical and Mental Health
•Political Awareness and Involvement



The Sorority was founded in 1913 by 22 students at Howard University. These young women wanted to use their collective strength to promote academic excellence; to provide scholarships; to provide support to the underserved; educate and stimulate participation in the establishment of positive public policy; and to highlight issues and provide solutions for problems in their communities.

Approve the contract for the KEV Group, Inc. to continue providing the School District of Palm Beach County the School Cash Suite; an integrated package of online services that accepts online payments from parents and the public and provides the accounting system used by the schools to manage school internal accounts.



Five years ago, the District conducted a Competitive Solicitation for a way to accept credit card payments and for new accounting software to be used by the schools to manage their internal accounts. 



After evaluating the options, the District entered into two contracts with the KEV Group.  The first was a contract for an online accounting program to manage the school internal accounts.  The second contract was for the online payment solution for parents.  Since that time, the programs have been enhanced and fully integrated and are now offered only as a suite of services.  They cannot be purchased separately.



The software suite provides an accounting solution for schools to manage internal accounts and accept payments in cash, check or credit card.  It makes it possible for parents and employees to pay for lunches, student activities and other items with a credit card or debit card, view their payment history, including meal details, online.  Payments can be made from any location, 24 hours a day, seven days a week during the school year, using Master Card, VISA, or debit card.



The credit card processing, which averages 1.5% of purchases, is handled by Bank of America through a separate contract with the State of Florida.



The software is used by all our schools and eight departments at this time, including Teacher Certification, The Office of Diversity in Business Practices, and Afterschool Programming and the activity continues to increase.



The District pays a portion of the software costs and a portion is assessed to anyone using the system.  Parents and staff are charged $1.00 per month, per student or employee, for each month when an online payment is made.  No fee is assessed if a parent uses the system only to monitor their child's school food service account.  This results in approximately $140,000 that is collected from parents and other users that is forwarded to the vendor.



The Audit Committee has repeatedly recognized the advantages of using credit cards with School Cash Online system.  1) Payment via credit cards means children aren't carrying cash; teachers and bookkeepers aren't counting as much cash; and parents don't have to worry about finding lunch money every day - they simply load money on their child's account.  2) The interface with the accounting system automatically updates the child's account, with no action required by the teacher or bookkeeper once the payment is set up.  3) Finally, parents can check their child's account to ensure payment was recorded completely and see how money was spent, including what their child chose to eat at lunch each day.



The District considered conducting another competitive selection process.  However, if a new firm were selected, all the school bookkeepers would be required to convert to a new accounting system.  This would require significant training over the summer and would negatively impact school staff.  District staff has reviewed other software packages that are currently available in the marketplace.  There are other packages that may be acceptable, but the negative impact on our schools far outweighed any possible benefits that may be obtained by using a different package.  Further, upon the review, it was determined that the fees included in the other packages were similar or greater than the fees proposed in this contract.



As with other critical software packages, this contract has an evergreen clause and the District will evaluate the effectiveness of the software annually and the contract will automatically renew unless the District chooses to cancel the contract.

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the contract with KEV Group, Inc. for the School Cash Suite and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.
