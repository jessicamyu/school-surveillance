These are two separate policies, although they must be purchased together. The first policy is the Student Catastrophic Accident Insurance policy which provides coverage of up to $5 million with a $25,000 per student deductible. This is required coverage for the District's participation in the Florida High School Activities Association (FHSAA) for sanctioned sports and covers medical expenses resulting from accidental injuries to any student while participating in school activities (including daytime activities).  The terms and conditions have been reduced slightly.  The benefit period for injuries covered under the catastrophic policy will be reduced from lifetime to 10 years while keeping the disability benefit at 20 years.  This benefit change is due to the overall insurance market's desire to eliminate the lifetime benefit option from all similar policies due to the increased cost of reinsurance.  The District has not had a claim requiring medical treatment beyond three (3) years.  This benefit change and a reduction in claim experience will result in an overall premium reduction of five (5) percent (over $75,600). The premium for this policy will be reduced to $165,685.




The second policy is the High School Interscholastic Athletic plan. This policy covers up to $25,000 in medical expenses for accidental injuries to high school interscholastic athletes and cheerleaders. As of last year, the FHSAA also requires some type of student accident insurance in order to participate in FHSAA sanctioned events.  The premium will remain at $1,271,000.





Student insurance fees, as allowed by School Board Policy 5.60 cover approximately half of the premium.  The current fee is set at $75.00 per student.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board renew the purchase of the High School Interscholastic Athletic Insurance and Student Catastrophic Accident Insurance, effective August 1, 2012 to August 1, 2013 from the Monumental Life Insurance Company (General Agent:  Bollinger Insurance) for a combined total premium of $1,436,685.

The Adult Education and Family Literacy - Adult General Education Grant is a continuation grant in the amount of $1,672,244.  The program performance dates of this grant are July 1, 2012 to June 30, 2013.



To ensure continuous improvement of this adult education grant, student progress will be measured by progression through Literacy Completion Points (LCPs) which correlates to the number of students that completed a level and the percentage completing a level.  The Adult General Education program or course numbers and associated LCPs are used to document improvements in literacy performance standards.  LCPs represent a student's attainment of an Education Function Level gain in adult federal reportable programs.  The tracking measures must be used by all adult education programs for all enrolled students to ensure compliance in reporting.



The elements of this grant relate to the administration of adult education and family literacy programs for the development of basic and functional literacy skills, workplace literacy, and family literacy services.  Adult Education programs are provided at 26 adult and community schools, one full-time adult education center, and numerous satellite programs located throughout Palm Beach County.  These programs are offered at various times throughout the day and evening in order to accommodate students needs and schedules.



The term "adult education" means services or instruction below the postsecondary level for individuals who have attained 16 years of age; who are not enrolled or required to be enrolled in secondary school under State law; who lack sufficient mastery of basic educational skills to enable the individuals to function effectively in society; do not have a secondary school diploma or its recognized equivalent level of education; or are unable to speak, read, or write the English language.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Adult Education and Family Literacy - Adult General Education Continuation Grant from the Department of Education in the amount of $1,672,244 effective July 1, 2012 through June 30, 2013 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

An English Literacy Program means a program of instruction designed to help individuals of limited English proficiency achieve competence in the English language.  In this grant, limited English proficiency means an adult or out-of-school youth who has limited ability in speaking, reading, writing, or understanding the English language and whose native language is a language other than English or lives in a family or community environment where a language other than English is the dominant language.

  • Description:  Provide additional architectural and engineering services for the renovation of Building #4.  The scope of work includes structural improvements of the foundation, floor, wall, window, door and roof.




  • Cause: Unforeseen Condition




  • Justification:  Building #4 was built in the 1920's. As part of the modernization project approval process, the City of West Palm Beach Historical Preservation Board required the School District to rehabilitate the exterior of Building #4. The Phase 3 Guaranteed Maximum Price included funds to rehabilitate the exterior and renovate the interior of Building #4. During the demolition of the interior of Building #4, numerous unforeseen structural deficiencies were identified and the work was put on hold. CORC was provided an update and they recommended that staff explore other options with the City including demolition. Staff met with the City on multiple occasions and the outcome was that the Historical Preservation Board still requires the exterior of the building to be rehabilitated.




  • Estimated Construction Cost: The total estimated construction cost for Building #4 is $1,800,000.  The Modernization project has $1,100,000 available for building 4.  The proposed FY 2013-2017 Five-Year Plan includes an additional $700,000.  Facilities Services plans to request two bid prices from each bidder for Building #4.  One for interior/exterior renovation and the structural repairs.  The other for just exterior renovation and structural repairs.  If the bids for interior/exterior renovation and structural renovations are within the $1,800,000 budget, a contract will be issued based on this scope of work.  If the bids for the interior/exterior renovation and structural repairs are over the budget, a contract will be issued only for the exterior renovation and structural repairs.   

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve a Project Modification in the amount of $63,410.00 to BRPH Architect-Engineers, Inc. for the Northboro Elementary School Modernization Project #0291-8504, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to finalize and execute all related contract documents.
