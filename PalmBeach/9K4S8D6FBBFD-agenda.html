FM1 - CONSIDER PRELIMINARY 2011 LEGISLATIVE PROPOSALS
Meeting
Aug 11, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action




  • Proposed legislative priorities are being submitted at this time in order to provide input to  various organizations such as the Florida School Boards Association (FSBA), Greater Florida School Boards Consortium (GFSBC) and Treasure Coast Workgroup.


 







  • The preliminary list of recommended priority issues is based on a review of the 2010 Legislative session. There are five proposed “Top Priority Issues.” Because 2010 is an election year that will determine a new governor, Cabinet and many members of the state Legislature, these recommendations are preliminary and serve the primary purpose of providing input to the Florida School Boards Association, Greater Florida Consortium of School Boards, and other organizations preparing legislative agendas for 2011. These priorities and other positions may be modified or augmented at any future time as desired by the School Board up to and during the 2011 regular legislative session which begins March 8, 2011, and lasts 60 calendar days. 


 







  • Proposals adopted at this School Board meeting will be submitted formally to the Florida School Boards Association, Greater Florida School Boards Consortium, Treasure Coast school districts, and Florida Association of District School Superintendents for consideration. They will also be submitted to all other School Boards and appropriate state organizations for consideration of endorsement and support.


 







  • Further, they will serve as the basis for initial review as part of the joint School Board-Palm Beach County Legislative Delegation meeting to be scheduled after the November general election and the 2010-2012 School Board is seated. 


 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board adopt preliminary Legislative Proposals for the regular 2011 Florida Legislative session and for presentation to the Palm Beach County Legislative Delegation, other members of the Legislature, and to statewide education organizations for support.

Contact:

Joseph Moore



Vernon A. Pickup-Crawford

Financial Impact:

Financial impact will be determined after completion of the 2011 Legislature.

FM2 - Revise Resolution to Hold Referendum to Seek Voter Approval to Continue to Levy 0.25 Mill for School Operational Purposes in FY2012, FY2013, FY2014, and FY2015
Meeting
Aug 11, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Discussion

The School Board adopted a resolution at their July 21, 2010 meeting (Agenda Item B-3) to place a referendum on the ballot for the upcoming November 2, 2010, general election.  This referendum  seeks voter approval to continue the 0.25 mill levy for school operational purposes for the four subsequent fiscal years (FY2012-FY2015). 



 



The Board of County Commissioners is scheduled to adopt the School Board’s resolution at their August 17, 2010, meeting.  The Superintendent and staff are recommending revisions to the ballot language contained in the resolution prior to the County Commission’s consideration.  A revised resolution with the recommended amendments is attached.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the revised resolution to hold a referendum to seek voter approval to continue to levy 0.25 mills for school operational purposes in FY2012, FY2013, FY2014, and FY2015 and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents. 



 



This item was added to the August 11, 2010 Board Agenda in order to revise the Resolution and ballot language prior to the August 17, 2010, Board of County Commissioners meeting.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore



Michael J. Burke

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the district operating budget is approximately $32 million in local tax revenues for FY2012, FY2013, FY2014, and FY2015.
