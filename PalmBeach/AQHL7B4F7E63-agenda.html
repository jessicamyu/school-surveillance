Recommended Action
I recommend the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp adopt a Resolution authorizing the issuance of COP 2017B to refinance 2011A and 2012B COP issues.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The 2011A and 2012A COP issues are eligible to be refinanced with net present value savings of approximately $2.2 million or 5%.   The savings have already been accounted for in the debt service budget for FY 2018 and the capital plan.
     
  • We plan to combine the refinancings into one transaction, to reduce the borrowing costs, but the resolution allows for the transactions to be completed separately if necessary based on market conditions.  The total par amount will not exceed $52 million and the maturity will be the same as the original debt issues.
     
  • The costs of issuance for the transaction cannot exceed $848,840 and will be included in the debt issue.  This is a maximum assuming market conditions dictate two separate transactions.  It is more likely they will be combined which will significantly reduce the costs.  A list of the firms to be included in the transaction as well as the maximum fees expected for each is attached.  
     
  • The Finance Committee reviewed the transaction at their meeting on August 18th and recommended the transaction be presented to the School Board for approval.  The minutes from the meeting as well as the presentation are available on Board Docs and the Finance Committee website: https://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/financecommitteefinal1/

Recommended Action
I recommend the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp authorize a Resolution to amend the COP Master Lease Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The Master Lease Agreement was adopted by the School Board in 1994, prior to the issuance of the first COP issue.
     
  • The document governs all COP issues.
     
  • The amendment addresses provisions regarding insurance on property included in the master lease (all schools that were financed with COPs).  The language has been updated to reflect current practices, specifically self-insurance and the use of a consultant to annually verify that coverage is sufficient.
     
  • Secondly, the amendment addresses the ability to make changes to the Master Lease.  Currently, changes must be approved by the School Board, the Leasing Corp, the Trustee (Bank of New York Mellon), AND the Bond Insurer.  Since many of the Bond Insurers have been significantly downgraded, most of our outstanding COPs are no longer insured.  The amendment allows for the majority of investors to approve changes in those instances.  The amendment also allows facility substitutions to take place without consent of investors under certain circumstances.
     
  • The School Board will also consider this item on September 6, 2017 as agenda item FMT7.
