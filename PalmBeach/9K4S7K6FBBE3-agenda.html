CLS1 - 2010-2011 INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS ADOPTIONS AND PROGRAM BUDGET
Meeting
Mar 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

District instructional materials adoption committees were convened during 2009-2010, pursuant to School Board Policy 8.122 (3) to evaluate and recommend new materials for district adoption in the area of Mathematics, grades K-12. Committee membership included: subject area classroom teachers and coaches, District level math curriculum administrators, principals, and lay citizens. Committees reviewed all instructional materials programs submitted for state adoption and some additional submissions that were considered appropriate for committee review. Titles recommended for district adoption are contained in Attachment A.



In August 2010, we complete our transition to the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards in Mathematics at all grade levels.  The standards in grades K-8 identify three important mathematical topics (Big Ideas) for each grade level which are to be the instructional emphasis for that grade.  This curriculum organization is a major paradigm shift in mathematics education.  The curriculum will develop deeply at each grade level around the Big Ideas rather than continually spiraling and keeping mathematics instruction on the surface.  To ensure that the texts selected for district adoption will assist teachers to effectively deliver the depth and richness in mathematics demanded by the new standards, all adoption committee members received instruction on the new standards and expectations for teachers and students.  The textbook programs recommended for district adoption were carefully reviewed by the committees to assure that they are aligned with the specifications of the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards in Mathematics.  The purchase of these new adoptions is essential so teachers have the tools they need to teach the new standards and provide a solid mathematical foundation for high school and college level mathematics courses.



The recommended textbook programs will be provided in both print and online digital formats.  Student editions in the core courses will be available to students via their Edline accounts.  Teacher materials and resources will be also available on Learning Village.  The elementary and middle school recommended texts are available in Spanish for the Dual Language schools.



The 2010-2011 Instructional Materials Cost Analysis (Attachment B) represents estimated costs for implementation of new adoptions, maintenance of existing instructional materials programs, opening a new elementary school, purchasing Dual Enrollment materials and providing allocations for charter schools.  The estimated costs are based on projected student enrollments and anticipated program needs.  Funding is provided from the carry over balance from the 2009-2010 State Categorical Allocation for Instructional Materials, the projected 2010-2011 State Categorical Allocation, and projected collections for lost/damaged materials from FY2010.



Staff development for instructional materials will be provided at no cost to the District by publishers and will be focused on the appropriate use of materials to implement the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards in Mathematics.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the list of instructional materials recommended for District adoption for the period indicated, and the 2010-2011 Instructional Materials Program budget of $16,795,255.

·         The purpose of the College Board District Diagnostic process is to assess the current state of the District in relation to several key areas supporting college readiness as well as to assess key cultural and functional capabilities to support change management.  



·         The College Board will provide the following services and deliverables as a part of the District Diagnostic Agreement:



o   Site visits to include a review of sample documents such as, course selection options, strategic plan, school improvement plans, master schedules, professional development calendars, teacher, counselor and administrative performance evaluation instruments, and the district report card



o   Interviews with selected central office staff, selected members of the Superintendent’s cabinet and District stakeholders



o   Visits to a representative sample of middle and high schools to observe classroom instruction in core curricular areas  



o   A presentation to the Superintendent and cabinet of the written report of the findings related to the District’s readiness for large scale school reform.  The report will include recommendations designed to inform a plan for a three to five year collaboration between District and the College Board intended to improve rates of college and career readiness of District students



 



·         Key Areas to be addressed in the Diagnostic include:



o   Curriculum Coherence



o   Assessments that Inform



o   Student Academic Support



o   Student Family Support



o   Staff Professional Development



o   Ongoing Improvement



o   District Infrastructure/Central Office Governance



o   District/Student Performance



·         The term of the Agreement is March 4, 2010 – December 31, 2010

Recommendation to Rescind Expulsion of Student 09/10-X-029

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board rescind the expulsion of student 09/10-X-029 from the regular public school program of The School District of Palm Beach County.  On February 3, 2010, student 09/10-X-029 was expelled from the Palm Beach County School District until October 27, 2010.  The expulsion was based on the information provided by the school at that time.  The student has been attending Turning Points Academy since November 13, 2009.  At Turning Points, the student exhibited severe, maladaptive behaviors and was referred for an evaluation for EBD eligibility.  It was determined that this student meets the criteria for an EBD program and needs to be placed appropriately.  Therefore, I recommend that the expulsion of February 3, 2010 be rescinded by the School Board.
