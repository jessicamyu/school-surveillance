ITEM ADDED:

Emergency Special Meeting and Item LD1 Settlement Agreement with Built Right Construction, Inc.

 

 

GOOD CAUSE STATEMENT:

This Special Meeting qualifies as an Emergency Special Meeting because this very complex litigation matter is set for Jury trial on November 5, 2014, and court-ordered mediation has resulted in a proposed settlement between the parties as of October 21, 2014. An immediate approval of the settlement by the Board is required in order to avoid immediate danger to the public welfare, by way of expending significant public funds for costs and expenses in preparation for trial as well as risk of liability as a result of trial verdict. The procedures for notice of this meeting are fair in that 1). the Settlement Agreement has been reached between the parties as a result of court-ordered mediation on October 21, 2014; 2). there will be a publication of newspaper; 3). notice appears on the School District website on October 21; and 4). the stakeholders have been notified in advance of this meeting and will have an opportunity to be heard.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Settlement Agreement in the case of Built Right Construction Inc. v. The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent/designee to finalize and execute all necessary and related settlement documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The  Office of General Counsel is recommending the Board approve the settlement of the cases arising out of the default and delinquency of Built Right Construction Inc. The settlement agreement incorporates specific terms of settlement, including the resolution of claims for damages by Built Right against the School Board in excess of 2 million dollars, as well as resolution of any claims the Board had or may have against Built Right and/or its sureties in connection with construction Aluminum walkway covers projects.

 

This settlement item is being recommended for good cause as it is in the best financial interest of the District due to anticipated costs of litigation, the risk associated with an adverse jury verdict, as well as costs of continued protracted litigation and/or appeal of this and related matters.
