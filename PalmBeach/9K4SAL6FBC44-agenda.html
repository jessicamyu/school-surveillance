The Florida Educational Equity Act, Florida Statutes, Section 1000.05, became law in June 1984 and the State Board of Education adopted the implementing rules in February 1985.

The 2010-2011 Plan Update will continue to evaluate the success of methods and strategies to reduce gaps in student performance and to increase participation of under represented groups by race/ethnicity and gender in upper level courses.



 



Grade 8, Algebra 1 The review reveals an inconsistent pattern of enrollment.  The reason being, the focus for Algebra 1 has been shifted to Grade 9.



 



Advanced Placement The review reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Advanced Placement courses for White, Black and Hispanic male students in grades 9-12.



Dual Enrollment The review reveals that the dual enrollment rates at grades 9-12 for White, Black and Hispanic male students remained stable for the past three years.



Honors Courses The review reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Honors courses for White, Black and Hispanic male students in grades 9-12.



Level 3 Courses The review also reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Level 3 courses for White, Black and Hispanic male students in grades 9-12.

AC1 - FY 2012 Florida Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) Statewide Provider Agreement
Meeting
Jul 27, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category

Action (Consent)

The Florida Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) Education Program Statewide Provider Agreement allows the District to continue to offer a high quality, school year and summer VPK program in select schools.  VPK is offered as an intervention strategy to help attain the State of Florida goal of all children performing on grade level by 3rd grade.  The District has participated in the VPK program since its inception in SY 2005-2006.



 



Florida VPK is a legislatively mandated, free program that allows students to participate in either a school-year 540-hour free VPK program or a summer 300-hour free VPK program, but not both.  The District offers school year VPK primarily as a VPK/Enrichment program, as either a VPK/Title I Enrichment model or a VPK/Parent Fee Enrichment model.  In these two models, VPK supports three free instructional hours per day.  All enrolled VPK students are eligible, but not required per state law, to attend the enrichment portion of the day.  The enrichment portion of the day is funded by either Title I in select schools or by parent fees or School Readiness (subsidy) funding in other schools.  The District’s VPK program is also braided with other programs to create partnership models, including VPK/ESE Inclusion, VPK/Migrant, VPK/Head Start, and VPK/Career Academy.



 



In FY 2012, the District will offer VPK classes, including partnership models, in 65 classrooms in 36 schools.  Program models and schools offering the school year VPK program include:

The District earns approximately $2,480 per student on a pro rata basis for each enrolled school-year VPK student, with the final earnings determined by each student’s enrollment date and attendance.  Projected VPK revenues for the 2011-12 school year programs are estimated at approximately $2.3 million for approximately 950 students.  The Department places VPK and other earned revenues in participating school budgets to fund teacher and classroom costs.  Summer VPK is funded at a lower per student pro rata basis and the VPK summer revenues cover all related teacher and classroom costs.

This is an item from the 7/20/11 being brought back with modifications.



The agreement is submitted in accordance with Florida Statute 1007.235, which requires approval by all parties prior to the start of high school registration for the fall term. It was approved by the Board of Trustees of Palm Beach State College on April 12, 2011, and Florida Atlantic University is scheduled to bring the agreement to the Board of Trustees after approval by the School Board of Palm Beach County.



- The agreement format aligns with what is required by the Florida Department of Education.



- Participation in the dual enrollment program has increased. (see attached data panel)



- The revised table in the document details the committee response to reducing the number of post-secondary students who require remediation course work. (see Appendix B of the agreement)



- Revisions to previous agreement include the following items.



   * Effective fall 2011, the grade point average (gpa) eligibility requirement for seniors to enroll in Strategies for College Success will increase from a 2.5 gpa to a 3.0 gpa, the same gpa required for all other students in all other dual enrollment courses, except vocational programs.



The School District of Palm Beach County will develop a course with the same standards as Strategies for College Success and offer it to students who are unable to take it to PBSC. 



   * Effective fall 2011, the eligibility requirements for enrollment in Post Secondary Adult Vocational (PSAV) programs will include the Test of Adult Basic Education (TABE) scores required by the specific program.



   * Effective January 2012, students must meet college-ready scores on either ACT (English, reading and math), SAT (critical reading and math) or all sections of the CPT or PERT to be eligible for dual enrollment at Palm Beach State College. (Up until this time, a college-ready score must be achieved only in the subject associated with the course taken.)



The School District of Palm Beach County is committed to work with PBSC to see if an exception can be made to this rule.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Interinstitutional Articulation Agreement with Palm Beach State College and Florida Atlantic University for the 2011-2012 school year.
