Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with the Health Care District of Palm Beach County, effective August 1, 2021 through June 1, 2022; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to the agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

Under this program, the Health Care District employs approximately 200 school nurses consisting of a full-time nurse assigned to a majority of public schools in Palm Beach County.  This program maximizes the health and well-being of Palm Beach County residents by providing comprehensive planning, funding, and coordination of health care service delivery to students, their families, and staff.  The School Health Program prevents and addresses physical, social, and emotional health problems; achieves learning readiness, and enhances the quality of life for students, their families, and staff.  Services provided under this program include, by way of example but not limitation, nursing assessment, first aid, medication administration, referrals, and consultations with parents and school staff.

Subject
SP1 Security Agreements with Municipal Local Enforcement Agencies
Meeting
Aug 4, 2021 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the proposed Security Agreement with Local Law Enforcement Agencies; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to execute the agreement, substantially in the form attached.

DESCRIPTION:

Under the provisions of the Marjory Stoneman Douglas Act, the School District has added and continues to fill any vacant School Police positions to ensure each District-operated school has at least one police officer staffed daily beginning with the first day of school on August 10, 2021. Hiring and filling the vacant officer positions is underway, however, until the positions are fully staffed, the District will utilize School Police Officers on every campus, and will utilize municipal and law enforcement officers to supplement coverage when schools starts.

 

Normally, District staff brings contracts that are approved and executed by the city or village councils to the School Board for approval.  In the interest of expediency, due to the short time the proposed contract is being brought to the School Board prior to approval by city and village councils, and because of that, some minor changes to the agreement may be necessary in order for local councils to approve them. With the School Board's approval, the Superintendent and staff will be authorized to modify and finalize the Security Agreements, which will be substantially in the form attached to this item and Superintendent and Board Chairman will be authorized to execute the final Security Agreements. 

 

As the District increases its own police force, staffing requested from local law enforcement agencies will be reduced. The estimated financial impact of 1,000,000 assumes full utilization for the entire school year.  Actual cost is anticipated to be less than the full amount.

Wanda F. Paul, Chief Operating Officer (wanda.paul@palmbeachschools.org)

The estimated financial impact to the general fund budget is $1,000,000. The source of funds is the School Police operating budget.
