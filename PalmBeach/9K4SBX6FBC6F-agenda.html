In 1971, the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Coordinating Committee, Inc. (MLKCC), was organized initially as the Black Educators Caucus, sponsoring remedial tutorial programs in cooperation with the Minority Mental Health Association.  In 1975, the program became an after school project and in 1979 expanded in partnership with the School District of Palm Beach County to (1) sponsor oratory, poster and drama contest commemorating Dr. King's January birth date; (2) to provide Afro-Arts Annual presentation and (3) to participate in Palm Beach Community College's Annual Multicultural Festival.  In addition to several other activities the competitive events include art exhibits, performing arts, oratorical, poster and essay contests.  The executive director is retired Palm Beach County teacher, Edith C. Bush.

Pursuant to s.1012.42, Florida Statute, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The District provides support for out-of-field teachers through in-service opportunities to develop instructional competencies required for the assigned duty.  Partnerships have been established with colleges and universities for required coursework and exam preparation. Teachers teaching in subject areas not covered by their current certification are required to obtain appropriate in-field certification within a specified time frame.

Therefore, we will expand IB at Suncoast so that all qualified students will be able to add the IB Diploma Program by 11th grade.  With this community wide thrust, it is critically important that the entire leadership team be fully educated regarding the impact of IB in our daily work.  Every member of the leadership team has responsibilities supervising and observing IB teachers as well as participating in our relationships with our sister schools, JFK Middle School and Dr. Mary McCleod Bethune Elementary School.  The conference has specific sessions on the IB K-12 Continuum that will enhance our ability to fully implement this vision for the Riviera Beach community.

FS-1 North County Service Center Remodel Project
Meeting
Feb 15, 2012 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

Description:  Remodel the first floor of the North County Service Center to create additional warehouse space, a combination workspace/training room, and office space for the Food and Nutrition Services Department's Equipment and Facilities Group.



Justification: 







  • The Equipment and Facilities Group saves the Food and Nutrition Services National School Lunch Grant budget approximately $520,000 per year through restoring and refurbishing of equipment, and through the sale of surplus equipment.  This project will provide the space needed to continue these cost saving programs.  Presently, equipment is being stored in a warehouse at the Burt Reynolds Ranch.






  • Remodeling the first floor of the North County Service Center (NCSC) is preferred over renovating the building at Burt Reynolds Ranch.






  • The building where the equipment is presently being stored needs a new roof (estimated cost of $300,000) and theft has become a significant issue. 






  • The NCSC building is more secure and the location is more centralized to all schools in the District.  Relocating the warehouse to the NCSC will increase productivity by at least eight (8) hours per week and also reduce wear and tear on District vehicles by eliminating at least three trips per week back and forth between Burt Reynolds Ranch and NCSC.






  • The District is considering selling the Burt Reynolds Ranch property.  This sale, if approved, would require the relocation of the materials stored at this location.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Remodeling of North County Service Center project in an amount not to exceed $450,000.00.

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the Special Revenue Fund - Food Service budget is $450,000.  The source of funds is the fund balance.  This is a one-time, non-recurring expenditure.

P1 Approve the request to renew the contract with Northwest Regional Data Center Services - Memorandum of Understanding (MOU).



A three year contract with the Northwest Regional Data Center (NWRDC) was approved by Board action on April 15, 2009 for Disaster Recovery.  Florida State University created the NWRDC to provide centralized computing support to public education entities with a non-commercial pricing structure.  NWRDC is governed by a policy board comprised of customer representatives which includes the SDPBC.



The NWRDC in Tallahassee FL serves a dual purpose. Its primary purpose will be the District's Disaster Recovery site for distributive systems. In the event of a disaster it will allow the recovery of critical systems: ERP, EDW, CAFM and future systems. During non-disaster times the site and equipment will be used for testing and development.  



Services to be provided include a secure and fully connected data center in Tallahassee, Florida and as needed technical support assuring core business continuity. 



The attached Memo of Understanding (MOU) is for the contract period of April 15, 2012 through June 30, 2013. 



The renewal and new MOU specifies the same facilities, services and support as our current MOU but reflects NWRDC reduced costs resulting in an annual decrease of $10,000 (see attached financial analysis).



The Technology Advisory Committee (TAC) unanimously approved this item at its February meeting.

Kennedy Middle School including improvements to the serving lines and the cafeteria.  Improvements to the serving lines will include new menu boards, decorative lighting, additional support equipment, and layout changes that will improve service to students.  Improvements to the cafeteria will include Bahama shutters, shades, faux columns, signage, and televisions.  Construction would begin in May 2012 and be complete prior to the students returning in August.



Justification:







  • School Food Services (SFS) developed a plan of action with the cooperation of the Florida Department of Education Food and Atlantic Community High School (ACHS) to reinvent the way students perceive and receive their school meals.  This plan took into consideration the meal time provided, physical facility, customer service, nutritious food offerings, and the overall dining experience.  Since opening day the High School Cafeteria Pilot Program (HSCPP) at ACHS has not only met, but exceeded the expectations of SFS. 






  • Increase lunch participation is one key identifier of the program's success.  Another key indicator, and perhaps the most significant, is the behavior modification of the students. 






  • Based on the success of the HSCPP, SFS would like to conduct a Middle School Cafeteria Pilot Program.  After reviewing possible school sites, John F. Kennedy was selected. 
     




  • SFS believes with these modifications, students will enter a more relaxing, inviting, social, safe and student driven dining experience which in return will create an opportunity for student retention and modify student behavior both during and after the school day.  Therefore, they will look forward to this time to nutritionally recharge for improved physical and mental well-being thus supporting the District goal of improving academic performance.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Remodeling of John F. Kennedy Middle School Cafeteria project in an amount not to exceed $175,000.00.

The IB Conference of the Americas is a unique professional development experience.  It is the only IB event in the region that brings together the entire IB community: heads of schools, coordinators, teachers, district officials and university and government representatives for four days of learning and dialog about what it means to be an IB educator. 



William T. Dwyer’s leadership will spend time with other educators from the Americas to share best practices and listen to amazing key note speakers such as Tony Wagner, Linda Darling-Hammond, Khaled Hoseini, and Wade Davis for inspiration to tackle another year of challenges.



Every year there are exciting changes in IB; new structures, new program options and new visions.  All designed to allow more students to access IB programs around the world.  As a new IB authorized Diploma Programme (DP), Dwyer’s vision of IB is to develop a DP with open access to the IB curriculum for students.  The conference has specific sessions on the IB Career Certification (IBCC) program.  One of Dwyer’s goals for SY 13 is to apply for authorization to offer the IBCC curriculum to our students in our Career Academies.  This offering will increase a diverse student population enrolled in IB courses and more opportunity for students to earn college credits.  Due to the success of our well established Academy of Finance (AOF), and the continued community requests for high level courses offered in AOF, the IBCC will be an outstanding addition to Dwyer’s course options.



As a developing program, it is important that our leadership team be fully educated regarding the demands and requirements of the IB in our daily schedule and goals.
