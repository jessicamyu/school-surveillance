Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the District ELL Plan to be submitted to the Florida Department of
Education, and authorize the Board Chair and the Superintendent to sign all documents related to
the plan.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County's English Language Learner (ELL) Plan describes the District's procedures and methodologies for serving ELL students. The plan contains descriptions of instructional, categorical, and student services to ensure ELLs are provided equitable access to an appropriate education that is tailored to their educational needs and equips them with the skills for academic success. Included in the plan are instructional models intended to personalize rigorous standards-based instruction designed to meet the language needs of the District's ELLs. It outlines criteria for entry, exit, and post-reclassification monitoring of students participating in the ESOL program. In addition, information related to the District's procedures for employing qualified staff are detailed in the plan. Each Florida school district is required to have a district ELL Plan approved by the School Board, Superintendent, and the Commissioner of Education prior to program implementation.

Award of the Contract resulting from ITN 22C-031J for An Alternative Dropout Prevention Program and Attendance Recovery Program for the period February 24, 2022 through May 26, 2022 with the option to renew for the FY22-23 School year and four additional one-year periods with Graduation Alliance, Inc.

 

An Evaluation Committee consisting of District personnel convened and evaluated the proposals received based on the criteria outlined in ITN 22C-031J.  Graduation Alliance, Inc. was ranked first and is recommended for award. The purpose of the Dropout & Attendance Recovery Program is to provide support and educational opportunities for eligible students.  Services to be provided by Graduation Alliance, Inc. are:

  • Provide outreach and enrollment services for students referred to Graduation Alliance by District Outreach Counselors by locating and engaging students who have not reconnected with District’s educational offerings or who are not consistently attending or progressing in their education offerings.
  • Provide coaching to those students who choose to return to District campuses and, using a proprietary algorithm, data from the School Board and self-reported data from the student, provide the student with the appropriate support to ensure continued participation and success of the student.
  • Provide dropout recovery services through the Florida Student Reengagement Program. This program will provide educational opportunities for eligible students who are working toward course credits which can be converted to high school credits and enable these students to obtain a graduation certificate from the School District of Palm Beach County.

The Contract resulting from Bid 22C-24J with PC Solutions & Integration, Inc. for the purchase of Enterprise Local Area Network (LAN) equipment will supplement the existing ethernet switch products with future oriented technology which will be compatible, interchangeable, and interoperable with the existing School infrastructure.

 

The equipment in this bid upgrades routers, cores, and switches. The School District's current LAN configurations consist of 10Gb, 1Gb, and 250Mg. This bid will improve the switches speed to 25Gb, for all schools including Alternative Schools. These new switches will be fed with dual fiber, meaning that the data switch will remain operational if a fiber or core transceiver fails. This option is unavailable today; therefore, we have more school down network incidents due to damaged fiber and core transceivers.

 

The new LAN upgrade will supplement the new wireless upgrade currently taking place. The new wireless system will take advantage of the newer technology and enhance performance.  This will also enhance external Wi-Fi expansion which requires more watts to operate at its full potential. 

 

Another critical technology that benefits from the LAN upgrade is the Security Camera project. The current Security Camera System has several older generation switches that will be upgraded in this process to facilitate the additional camera requirements at schools.   

 

The Telecommunications Act of 1996 established a fund by which Schools and Libraries across the Country could access discounts on eligible telecommunications products and services.  The program is commonly known as the E-rate Program. The eligibility for discounts on internet access, telecommunications products and services, internal connection products, services and maintenance is determined by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC). Funding is made available upon application approval by the Schools and Libraries Division (SLD) of the Universal Service Administrative Company (USAC), which was established by the Act. The discount amount is based upon the numbers of students receiving free and reduced price meals and our District qualifies for a discount of 80 percent.

 

As part of the E-Rate program, the District is responsible for approximately 20 percent of the total cost of products and the remainder is funded directly by the FCC for all E-ratable purchases.  Equipment for non-E-ratable sites may also be purchased off this contract at full cost.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve pursuit of Historic Designation for four buildings on the A.W. Dreyfoos School of the Arts campus, along with negotiation of an Interlocal Agreement with the City of West Palm Beach for purchase of the Transfer of Development Rights generated by the historic property, and authorize the Chair and Superintendent to sign all related documents and applications.

DESCRIPTION:

  • West Palm Beach’s Historic Preservation staff have indicated that Buildings 1,2,3, & 9 on the A.W. Dreyfoos School of the Arts campus are eligible for Historic Designation under the City of West Palm Beach's Historic Preservation Ordinance, as well as the City’s Transfer of Development Rights (TDR) Program.(Please see attached map for building locations.)
  • City staff has indicated that the Historic Dreyfoos Campus, formerly “Central Schools,” represents one of the most important historic sites in the City.
  • The TDR program encourages the preservation of valuable Historic properties by allowing owners of eligible Historic properties to sell the rights they would be permitted to build if the Historic structures were removed. The square footage of the existing buildings is subtracted from the highest existing development potential of the property and the difference in square footage is eligible for sale through the TDR program.
  • In order to be eligible for TDRs, the District must make minor improvements to the four buildings to protect the building envelope, such as painting and roof repairs, which will be included in the upcoming Facility Condition Assessment improvements for the Dreyfoos campus.
  • Recent sales of Historic TDRs have been in the $11 to $12 per square foot range. Due to the scarcity of additional TDRs, discussions with City staff have indicated that $17 per eligible square foot would be an agreeable purchase price. 
  • In addition to the revenue generated by the sale of the TDR’s, the transaction would include the City’s donation of a portion of the former Golf Course parking lot adjacent to Forest Hill Community High School, with an estimated value of $175,000, according to the Palm Beach County Property Appraiser’s Market Valuation.
  • The estimated calculations provided by City staff are as follows:
    • The resulting site is: 217,435 sf
    • Allowable Floor Area Ratio (FAR) - 2.75
    • Maximum development capacity = 217,435*2.75 = 597,946 sf 
    • Existing buildings on site = 120,554 sf

Building #1 - 37,374 sf   

Building #2 - 28,521 sf 

Building #3 - 31,151 sf  

Building #9 - 23,508 sf

  • TDRs available for sale = 597,946-120,554 = 477,392 sf
  • 477,392 x $17/sf = approx  $8.1 million *subject to survey validation

 

  • District staff favors an agreement with the City over a general market solicitation as it allows for one global transaction, enables the City to conduct better planning, and includes the donation of land needed by Forest Hill Community High School.
  • The transaction will also require that the City, in the event that it ultimately sells any of the TDR’s for more than $17 per TDR, to pay to the School Board 50% of the portion of the City’s actual sales price in excess of the $17 per TDR. The City has expressed an interest in acquiring the TDRs to fairly distribute them among several developers and have indicated they do not intend to sell them above the $17 per square foot cost.
  • This transaction is consistent with the School Board's Historic Designation Policy 7.25 insofar as the School Board is making the application for designation and TDR eligibility, and historic designation will generate the ability to raise revenue through its eligibility for the TDR program, thereby compensating for direct costs incurred by preserving the four buildings. Staff will bring forward a resolution for the School Board to designate the four buildings as Historic at the appropriate point in the process.
  • Links to Historical Information: 
    • Historical Society of PBC - Palm Beach History Online
    • HMdb.org- Palm Beach High School/ Central Schools
  • A portion of the TDR proceeds, a minimum of 10%, will be set aside to maintain the four historic buildings on the Dreyfoos campus, as required by the City's TDR Ordinance.
