Subject
FMT1 Proposed 2023 A and B COP Issues
Meeting
Nov 2, 2022 - 5. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Boardroom
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board authorize the resolution to issue Certificates of Participation Series 2023 A and B with an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $225 million.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The FY 2023 Capital Plan includes the issuance of debt to fund the construction of several projects.  Based on the construction schedules, six projects will be financed this year.
    • North Tech/Riviera Beach Prep Modernization
    • Pine Grove ES Modernization
    • Roosevelt Full Service Modernization
    • Timber Trace Core Expansion
    • West Riviera ES Modernization
    • Wynnebrook ES Modernization
  • The District traditionally issues tax-exempt debt, which allows for construction of public schools. However, tax-exempt debt is not appropriate for funding projects that will generate revenues so taxable debt will be used to finance the construction at Roosevelt Full Service.  The taxable transaction will mature first to reduce the additional interest cost.
    • Tax-exempt COP series 2023A will finance the North Tech/Riviera Beach Prep, Pine Grove, Timber Trace, West Riviera, and Wynnebrook projects with a par amount not to exceed $200 million.
    • Taxable COP series 2023B will finance the Roosevelt Full Service project with a par amount not to exceed $25 million.
  • Both transactions will mature no later than 8/1/2048.
  • The estimated costs associated with the issuance of the debt is not to exceed $1.94 million.  A detailed list of the estimated costs is attached.
  • B of A Securities, Inc will serve as the Senior Underwriter and J.P. Morgan will serve as the Co-Senior Underwriter.  RBC Capital Markets, LLC, Siebert Williams Shank & Co. LLC and Stifel, Nicolaus & Company, Inc will serve as Co-Underwriters on the transactions.  The underwriter team is based on the contract approved by the School Board in January 2018.
  • The Finance Committee will review the transactions at their meeting on October 28, 2022.  The meeting will be recorded and available online shortly after the meeting via this link:
    https://www.palmbeachschools.org/school_board/board_advisory_committees/board_advisory_committee_meetings_on_you_tube/ 
  • The Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp will also consider this item on November 2, 2022 (agenda item LC1)

 

CONTACT:

Heather Frederick, CFO (Heather.Frederick@palmbeachschools.org)

Leanne Evans, Treasurer (Leanne.Evans@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The issuance of COP series 2023A and B will provide the funding needed to finance a portion of the FY 2023 capital budget.  Revenues of approximately $202 million will be used to finance the construction projects listed above and costs of issuance not to exceed $1.94 million.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the naming of the new High School "OOO" from the list of recommendations submitted in the order of preference as follows: Dr. Joaquín García High School, Western Wave High School, and Park Ridge High School.

DESCRIPTION:

In accordance with School Board Policy 7.03, three names are being submitted for the naming of High School "OOO." A committee comprised of parents, students, and community members met to review names for the school. Committee members represented the geographical area of the community and met the Policy requirements.

 

  • Dr. Joaquín García High School - Dr. Joaquín García was a founding member of the Hispanic Education Coalition and served as chairman of the organization for more than 12 years. He believed education was a lever to improve the social and economic health of the community. 

 

  • Western Wave High School - Less than 10 miles from the ocean and just down the road from Coral Reef Elementary, this name reflects the connection to the environment and lifestyle of South Florida.

 

  • Park Ridge High School - This name is part of the neighborhood identity. The school is being built adjacent to the ridge area, where a nearby golf course is of the same name. 

 

The selection committee was comprised of the following members: Superintendent Designee/Principal: Oscar Otero, Superintendent Designee/Central Region Instructional Superintendent: Karen Whetsell, Lay Persons: Carlton Smith, Cathie Bannister, Amy Fowler, and Mabel Datena, PBC Historical Society Representative: Rose Guerrero; and two students likely to attend the school.

University Prep received a grade of "D" for the school year 2022.  F.S. 1002.33, Section 9(n)(1) states:

 

The principal and a representative of the governing board of a charter school that has earned a grade of “D” or “F” pursuant to s. 1008.34(2) shall appear before the sponsor to present information concerning each contract component having noted deficiencies. The principal and a representative of the governing board shall submit to the sponsor for approval a school improvement plan to raise student achievement. Upon approval by the sponsor, the charter school shall begin implementation of the school improvement plan. The department shall offer technical assistance for developing, submitting, and approving such plans.” (emphasis added)

 

The Department of Charter Schools staff provided technical assistance to University Prep in the drafting of the School Improvement Plan attached.
