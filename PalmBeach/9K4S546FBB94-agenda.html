Personnel Disciplinary Action - Ricky Smith

Jul 23, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Disciplinary Actions
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

On or about March 26, 2008, Ricky Smith, a Teacher, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegation of gross insubordination.  Furthermore, the allegation included potential violations of Florida Administrative Code 6B-4.009, (4), Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal, and/or School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff.



On or about November 16, 2007, Ricky Smith received a Written Directive that included specific directives related to attendance procedures and expectations of behavior.  Subsequently, Mr. Smith failed to follow the November 16, 2007 directives and was issued two Verbal Reprimands with Written Notation, dated February 8, 2008 and February 19, 2008, for insubordination and failure to follow administrative directives.  A written Reprimand was issued to Mr. Smith on March 11, 2008 for continued insubordination and failure to follow the administrative directives issued on November 16, 2007.  Mr. Smith attended a pre-disciplinary meeting with Employee Relations on April 21, 2008 for the allegation of gross insubordination based on continued insubordination behavior and failure to follow the November 16, 2007 administrative directives as described in documentation received from E. Wayne Gent, Principal, on April 14, 2008.  Based on the foregoing facts, the allegation against Mr. Smith includes gross insubordination. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Ricky Smith's ten (10) day suspension without pay, effective August 11, 2008 at the July 23, 2008, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Chief James P. Kelly; Angelette Green

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District
