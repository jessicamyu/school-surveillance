Subject
Instruction That Meets the Needs of All Learners
Meeting
Mar 2, 2011 - Workshop
Category
Workshop Items
Type
Action (Consent)

In a continued effort to provide consistent district recognition of best practices in reading instruction, the area directors, Departments of Elementary and Secondary Curriculum, Department of Safe Schools, and T.E.N. have developed a series of vodcasts highlighting the components of balanced literacy instruction. The vodcasts are entitled Instruction That Meets the Needs of All Learners.



Each presentation serves the following roles.



- Depicts best practices demonstrated by some of Palm Beach County’s most proficient teachers



- Reinforces professional development provided for teachers and administrators



- Demonstrates features that research has shown to be most effective in reading instruction



- Takes place in Palm Beach County elementary, middle, and high schools



- Includes clips of high quality instructional practices



- Includes comments from teachers and students about the featured component of instruction



 



Additional information concerning the implementation of the initiative, Instruction That Meets the Needs of All Learners, is below.



 



- To date, three six minute vodcasts have aired at monthly Administrators and Supervisors meetings for principals.



-  Principals are encouraged to view the vodcasts with teachers.



- The vodcasts are posted on the web pages of the Departments of Elementary and Secondary Curriculum.



- The following vodcasts have been shown: Overview, Appropriate Classroom Environment, and Teacher’s Role in Whole Group Explicit Instruction.



- The following vodcasts will be aired over the next few months: Student’s Role in Whole Group Instruction, Differentiated Small Group Instruction, and Independent Reading Practices.



 



A brief overview vodcast has been prepared for the Palm Beach County School Board to provide an understanding of this project.

The purpose of this workshop is to provide information about

Differentiated Accountability.  Differentiated Accountability (DA) is the

State of Florida’s required accountability system which includes the State A+

system and the Federal accountability system (AYP) (State Board Rule

6A-1.099811).  It began three years ago, and it provides a range of

support to increase school improvement for schools that do not make AYP and/or

are struggling with their letter grade.  The supports and requirements are

funded by Title I.









DA regulations apply to all schools and affect 157 schools

in our district at this time.  During this workshop you will learn:

















  •       

    What is Differentiated Accountability?
  •        

    What is Adequate Yearly Progress?
  •        

    What does our monitoring process look like?
  •        

    What does Differentiated Accountability look like

    in our schools?




  •      

    What are our next steps?
