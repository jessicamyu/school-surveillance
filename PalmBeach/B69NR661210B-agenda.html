The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County will expand the existing partnership and contract with the Asia Society’s Center for Global Education International Studies Schools Network (ISSN) at Jupiter Middle School of Technology to include Melaleuca and Jupiter Elementary Schools. The program combines professional learning and coaching for whole school transformation to build students’ and teachers’ global competence based on best practices in curriculum, assessment and instruction aligned to state standards. Asia Society is the leading educational organization dedicated to promoting mutual understanding and strengthening partnerships among peoples, leaders and institutions of Asia and the United States in a global context.

While Melaleuca will concentrate on building exposure and ultimately increasing language offerings, with a focus on international diplomacy, Jupiter Elementary is proposing global education with a cyber security curriculum from WOZ U Education.

I recommend the Board enter into a Data Sharing Agreement with the University of Minnesota (UMN) to develop, implement, and evaluate technology supports and resources effective October 16, 2018 through November 30, 2022; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to this Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The project’s objective is to develop, implement, and evaluate technology supports and resources (i.e., staff readiness assessment tool, web-based applications of onboarding modules, and professional development modules) that can facilitate the implementation of the Check & Connect (C & C) Student Engagement Intervention (SEI) and use of the C & C web-based application.  Check & Connect is a comprehensive intervention designed to enhance student engagement at school and with learning for marginalized, disengaged students in grades K-12, through relationship building, problem solving and capacity building, and persistence. A goal of of Check & Connect is to foster school completion with academic and social competence. It is comprised of four components:

  • A mentor who works with students and families for a minimum of two years;

  • regular checks, utilizing data schools already collect on students’ school adjustment, behavior, and educational progress;

  • timely interventions, driven by data, to reestablish and maintain the student’s connection to school and learning and to enhance the student’s social and academic competencies; and

  • engagement with families.

For Woodlands’ staff the C & C intervention and supporting APP is designed to add efficiencies in reporting and managing student data.  This would, in turn, allow for more time to be devoted directly working with students and less time on entering and managing student data.  Prior to the development of the C & C APP, data has been collected using a pencil-paper format which has proven to be inefficient.

Scope of the Project:

  • Establish a school-based implementation team

  • Train staff on the C & C intervention and APP and the SEI APP

  • Develop and implement the site readiness tool

  • Develop the technology support system (professional development modules with Woodlands’ staff participation

  • Collect information from the mentors and staff on their experience in using the C & C  and SEI APPS

 

The UMN will also develop a Memorandum of Understanding between Woodlands Middle School and the UMN project team that specifically details the responsibilities of each party.  In addition, the UMN project will provide Woodlands Middle School with a $12,000 stipend to support staff involvement beyond current contract hours. Approval of this Agreement does not prejudice the rights of the Board.
