Subject
Special Meeting re: Expert Witness Contract with Matson Driscoll & Damico LLP
Meeting
May 4, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action

Thomas Wilson, the Managing Partner of the Miami and Orlando offices of Matson Driscoll & Damico LLP will provide forensic accounting services associated with the alleged breach of a eminent domain settlement agreement involving the acquisition of a property required for the New Suncoast High School, as assigned him by the Chief Legal Counsel from time to time, including, but not limited to review and analysis of financial documents obtained through the discovery process, preparation of a written expert report, attendance at a deposition and testimony at trial as an expert witness. 



 



This Contract runs through December 31, 2011.  This Contract is for the not to exceed maximum amount $30,000.



 



This case is set for trial in the spring of 2011 and is being vigorously defended.  The former landowner is claiming that the alleged breach of the settlement agreement by the School District resulted in damages in excess of $600,000.  Notwithstanding the staff’s position that the District did not breach the settlement agreement and therefore, has no liability, the Court refused to bifurcate the trial on liability from the trial on damages so the case must be prepared on the assumption that it is possible that some liability for breach exists and therefore the defense as to the amount of damages sustained by the former landowner must be defended.



 

Mr. Wilson has practiced in the areas of forensic and investigative accounting for over 25 years, is qualified and has served as an expert witness in both federal and state court cases and comes highly recommended to the Board’s outside counsel in this matter.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the Board approve the Contract with Matson Driscoll & Damico LLP and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Chief Counsel



Blair R. LittleJohn, Esq.; Senior Counsel

Financial Impact:

$30,000.00 from the Capital Project funds.
