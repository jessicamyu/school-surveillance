Pursuant to Florida Statute 1012.42, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The District provides support for out-of-field teachers though in-service opportunities to develop instructional competencies required for the assigned duty.  Partnerships have been established with colleges and universities for required coursework and exam preparation. Teachers teaching in subject areas not covered by their current certification are required to sign Out-of-Field Agreements which permit continued employment on the condition that they obtain appropriate in-field certification within a specified time frame.

    • Positive supervision and involvement by parents/guardians will increase as evidenced in pre-post surveys by 40%

• On or about March 17, 2009, application for a water connection permit from the City of Riviera Beach was submitted for the Suncoast High School Replacement Project, Project No. 0151-7511.



• Initially, the City of Riviera Beach refused to issue the School Board the water connection permit. (See Attachment 2) The City of Riviera Beach, Florida claimed that its sewage system would not be able to handle the sewage from the school and indicated that it would not issue the water connection permit until the School Board upgraded the sewer system.



• The City of Riviera Beach, Florida was provided with $96,926.00 to replace the lining of 8” sewer lines and work associated with the replacement of the lining, as well as two (2) new lift station pumps.



• Subsequent to payment for the improvements to the sewage system, on March 31, 2009 the water connection permit was issued. However, the City of Riviera Beach, Florida then refused to inspect the work and placed a hold on the permit due to unrelated traffic matters. (See Attachment 3) Note that the School Board cannot tie into the City of Riviera Beach, Florida’s water system without the City’s approval to inspect at which time a City water meter is provided.



• On July 31, 2009, application for a sewer connection permit from the City of Riviera Beach was submitted for the Suncoast High School Replacement Project, Project No. 0151-7511.



• The City of Riviera Beach, Florida has verbally stated that the sewer connection permit is on hold due to unrelated traffic matters.



• The City of Riviera Beach, Florida stated that placing the School Board’s permits on hold was the only leverage that it had to cause the School Board to negotiate the traffic matters.



• The City of Riviera Beach, Florida has not articulated any other reason to place the School Board’s water and sewer connection permits for the Suncoast High School Replacement Project, Project No. 0151-7511 on hold other than the unrelated traffic matters.



• The City of Riviera Beach, Florida’s hold on the School Board’s water and sewer connection permits on the Suncoast High School Replacement Project is improper; and the School Board is entitled to its water connection permit and sewer connection permit.



• Furthermore, the City of Riviera Beach, Florida’s improper hold on the School Board’s water connection permit and sewer connection permit on the Suncoast High School Replacement Project may be exposing the School Board to delay damages of up to $8,570.47 per day.



• The Florida Governmental Conflict Resolution Act (Chapter 164, Florida Statutes) requires that prior to litigation between two governmental entities, those entities must participate in a procedural process outlined in the Act, in an attempt to resolve all conflicts.



• The Act allows for a streamlined process that will cause a Conflict Assessment meeting to occur within 30 days of receipt of the letter noticing the initiating the conflict resolution procedure. This meeting is the first step in the conflict resolution procedural process.



• In order to initiate in the conflict resolution procedural process outlined in Chapter 164, Florida Statutes, the School Board must approve a Resolution initiating the conflict resolution procedures.



• Should the conflict resolution procedures in Chapter 164, Florida Statute, fail to resolve the conflict, litigation against the City of Riviera Beach is necessary.



• In conclusion, initiation of the conflict resolution procedures in Chapter 164, Florida Statute, and if necessary, litigation against the City of Riviera Beach, Florida is necessary to avoid exposure of delay damages in the Suncoast High School Replacement Project, or if exposure is imminent, to seek damages against the City of Riviera Beach, Florida.

Recommendation:



I recommend the School Board approve a Resolution, pursuant to Section 164.1052(1), Florida Statutes, to elect to initiate, as a primary conflicting governmental entity, the conflict resolution process with the City of Riviera Beach, Florida, as to the City of Riviera Beach, Florida’s failure to approve, appropriately and fully, a water connection permit, and a sewer connection permit for the Suncoast High School Replacement Project, Project No. 0151-7511. I also recommend that the School Board authorize the Office of the Chief Counsel and all supporting District Staff to abide by all required statutory proceedings, to pursue all options available to resolve the conflict, and to join any party(ies) deemed necessary (none known at this time). Should the conflict not be resolved during the Chapter 164, Florida Statutes, conflict resolution process, I further recommend the School Board approve a Resolution authorizing the office of the Chief Counsel to file suit for equitable and legal relief, and take all appropriate action, including hiring experts, hiring outside counsel, if necessary, and the authority to settle the matter subject to the approval of the Superintendent and School Board, against the City of Riviera Beach, Florida, in the 15th Judicial Circuit in and for Palm Beach County or other appropriate forum, and against any other necessary party(ies) in the appropriate Court.
