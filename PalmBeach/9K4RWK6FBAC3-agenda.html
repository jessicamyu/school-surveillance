The Agenda Item AC7 - "FY 2008 Graduation Agreements at Two Sites" (FAU and the Kravis Center) included in the January 20, 2008 meeting was approved for $129,056.30. An additional $15,000 is needed to cover the cost estimate not included in the Agenda Item approved on Jan. 20, 2008.  This cost estimate covers other costs not included in the estimated invoice submitted along with the Graduation Agreement by the Kravis Center. Those costs are invoiced after the event has concluded.



In addition the Palm Beach County Convention Center (a third site) has been reserved for graduation ceremonies for the Adult Education Center, Palm Beach County Summer School, and 10 high schools which will be served by the Convention Center for Spring Graduations as follows:



° John I. Leonard High
° Jupiter Community High
° Lake Worth Community  High
° Palm Beach Central High
° Palm Beach Gardens High
° Royal Palm Beach Community High
° Seminole Ridge Community High
° Suncoast High
° Wellington Community High
° William T. Dwyer High



The graduation cost includes rehearsals, room rental fees and deposits, custodial services, and security.
Funds are included in the District operating budget for the Department of K-12 Curriculum.



The agreement is for annual renewal and provides for multi-year use of facilities, pending availability with annual cost, dates and specific needs to be determined.



The projected cost for the Palm Beach County Convention Center is $230,529.95 including rental and applicable parking fees. The total, including the additional cost estimate of $15,000 for the Kravis Center, is $245,529.95.

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the graduation agreement with The Palm Beach County Convention Center  for the   FY 2008 high school graduation ceremonies, effective March 17, 2008, in the amount of $245,529.95, which includes the additional $15,000 cost estimate for the Kravis Center, and authorize the Superintendent to sign all related documents and contract.

Contact:

Ann Killets (Killetsa@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Dr. Constance Tuman-Rugg, Ed.D.



Elizabeth Perlman

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is $245,529.95.  The source of  funds is the operating budget from the Department of K-12 Curriculum.
