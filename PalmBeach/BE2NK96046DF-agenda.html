I recommend the Board adopt the Resolution affirming support of and partnership with regards to the 2020 Census.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The decennial Census occurs every ten years to enumerate every person residing in the United States, and is fundamental to ensuring that all residents of our diverse County have access to fair representation, essential services, and economic development.

  • An accurate count of persons living in Palm Beach County in the 2020 Census is vital to determine the representation of individuals, families, and communities in our local, state, and federal governments.

  • The 2020 Census count of children living in the School District of Palm Beach County will be the basis of federal education funding allocated to our schools over the next ten years, specifically the federal programs serving our students living in poverty and students with disabilities.

  • The Superintendent has established an internal District Complete Count Committee (CCC), a cross-functional team of department representatives. The CCC will develop and execute a plan of action to help facilitate a complete count of all students and their families residing within Palm Beach County on the 2020 Census. The internal CCC will also act as a liaison with the County's CCC to facilitate extensive and coordinated efforts.

  • The Resolution affirms that School Board of Palm Beach County is committed to working in partnership with other local civic, business, and community leaders, the State of Florida, and others to engage, educate, and count every resident in Palm Beach County in the 2020 Census.

I recommend the Board approve the Amendment to Entitlement Grant with Florida Department of Children & Families, Office of Refugee Resettlement for Project Transition in the amount of $230,000 effective August 1, 2019 though December 31, 2019.

DESCRIPTION:

Consider approval of the Amendment to Entitlement Grant from the Florida Department of Children and Families, Office of Refugee Resettlement for Project Transition in the amount of $230,000 effective August 1, 2019 though December 31, 2019 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of grant activities. 

 

Project Transition provides adult education for all refugees of all nationalities. The eligible targeted populations are refugee adults with Federal I-94 forms or other documentation proving their legal status in the United States. This grant will assist Limited English Proficient (LEP) refugees to master English and be able to access College and Career Readiness classes. It will assist refugees with the knowledge and skills necessary for successful employment, leading to economic self-sufficiency and effective citizenship. 

 

Since the Office of Refugee Resettlement (ORR) has limited the Refugee Service funding nationwide, the Florida Department of Children and Families will implement a different Comprehensive Refugee Service Program under a different  agency in Palm Beach County, effective on January 1, 2020. 

 

Project Transition will continue serving our local refugee population funded by the Florida Department of Children and Families until December 31, 2019.

 

The Director of Adult Education and Community Education will monitor this program internally to ensure compliance with all state and federal requirements. In addition, a representative from the Department of Children and Families, Office of Refugee Resettlement will conduct an ongoing evaluation/ monitoring process. The evaluation will include a review of student records, including student performance and completion rates.

I recommend the Board approve a Resolution authorizing the issuance of the 2019 Tax Anticipation Note, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to approve all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The resolution authorizes the issuance of the 2019 Tax Anticipation Note.
     
  • The Tax Anticipation Note, or TAN, is a short term financing instrument used to alleviate cash shortfalls in the general fund each year. Current estimates show that additional cash will be needed in November 2019.
     
  • Local property taxes provide the majority of the revenues for the School District. As the first receipt of tax revenues is expected in late November, it is necessary to borrow money for cash flow purposes until the tax revenues are received.
     
  • Based on projected cash needs, the District plans to issue a $115 million TAN this year. It will be structured for the same amount and term as the 2018 TAN.
     
  • A summary of the transaction is attached. Projected interest expense is $1.2 million and the costs of issuance are not to exceed $90,405. The projected costs are detailed in the attachment titled 2019 TAN Cost of Issuance. Interest earnings are expected to offset the costs of the transaction.
     
  • The sale is tentatively planned for September 24, 2019, at 11:00 am. The transaction should close on or about October 02, 2019, and mature no later than August 30, 2020.
     
  • Students from the Finance Academies have been invited to attend a presentation beginning at 9:30 a.m. on September 24, followed by a mock sale which will allow them to participate in the note sale. Financial professionals have also been invited to help the students understand the career opportunities in public finance.
     
  • The Finance Committee reviewed the proposed tax anticipation note during their April 26 meeting and unanimously recommended the transaction move forward to the School Board for consideration.
