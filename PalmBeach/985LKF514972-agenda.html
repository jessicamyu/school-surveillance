Recommended Action
Approval of Homegrown Summer Literacy Institute

DESCRIPTION:

 

The purpose of this contract is to obtain staff developers from Columbia University's Teachers College who will deliver professional development at the Homegrown Summer Literacy Institute for teachers implementing the workshop model.  The Institute for approximately 300 teachers in 70 elementary and middle schools will focus on improving the quality of teaching and student learning.

 

This institute will focus on the structures, methods, and expectations for the Workshop Model, incorporating the Common Core State Standards in reading and writing curriculum, effective strategy-based instruction as well as utilizing assessments as a means of differentiating instruction.  The overall goal is for teachers to sharpen their ability to provide, monitor and assess high quality literacy instruction for their students.

 

The staff developers from Columbia University's Teachers College are leaders in the field of literacy.  They provide professional development to schools throughout New York City and across the world.  Many of them are authors of high-quality literacy books and resources.  During the four days, the staff developers will deliver professional development that includes discussion groups and practical hands-on application.

 

The District will monitor implementation of best practices by making frequent visits to the schools participating in this institute.  Outcomes will be evaluated through data collected from the Fountas and Pinnel Benchmark Assessment System and pre and post writing assessments administered by participating schools.  These data sources will assist in tracking student data to determine an increase in students performing at or above grade level and a decrease in students performing below grade level.

 

The contract will provide professional development to accompany the professional development currently being led at school sites by staff developers from Columbia University's Teachers College and district staff developers.  This professional development will be funded by FDOE Title I and Title II dollars.

The financial impact to the district is $102,800 provided from FDOE Title I and Title II dollars.  This amount will fund staff developers from Columbia University's Teachers College who will provide professional development during the Homegrown Summer Literacy Institute for teachers of schools implementing the workshop model.  All expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue.
