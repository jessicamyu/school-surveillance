National Board Certified Teachers Summer Program Highland Elementary
Meeting
Aug 19, 2009 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action

The Department of Student Intervention Services provides tutorial services throughout the year for students identified as homeless, particularly those living in shelters.   Highland Elementary School was identified for the past two years as having the highest number of homeless students in our school district.  Through our grant, stipends were put aside to provide an academically focused tutorial program as a component of the Beacon Bridges summer project at Highland, utilizing the skills of some of our most highly qualified teachers.



In an effort to provide an exemplary tutorial staff for this very vulnerable population of students, outreach was made to National Board Certified Teachers in Palm Beach County, through their President, Sheri Predmore.  Discussions were held with Brian Killeen, Principal of Highland Elementary, Kathy Wall and Jamie Bradshaw, of Adopt-A-Family and the Beacon Bridges project to determine the needs of the student and focus of the summer tutorial project. 



For the last two summers, a cadre of National Board Certified Teachers, under the direction of Ms. Predmore, designed, planned, and implemented a science themed, cross curriculum, standards based tutorial program that would rival science summer programs that parents would normally pay a high tuition to enroll their children. 



Last summer, the theme was “Rocket and Rollin’ and this year the “Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Repair” Math and Science Summer camp had an environmental focus.  The students were engaged in many hands-on activities, that included parental involvement, with additional collaboration with the business community, as well.



Each year, the highlight of the camp is an assembly program of presentations by the students to demonstrate their understanding of the concepts covered, highlighted by visits from special guests. 



We recognize these National Board Certified Teachers, Sheri Predmore, Anita Ferron, Susan Swain, JoAnn Parizek, Michelle Cates, Tonya Hodge, Angela Jacques, Donna Bruney, Cindy Twombly, Cindy Okun, Lisa Fitzpatrick, Irene Aunger, Cory Littles, Cara Tatonetti and two student volunteers Madelynn Camp and Kenya Reynolds.

PS 1 The Provider shall perform the following services:
 Legal Advisor was hired by the School Board in March, 2005 to assist and advise the School Board and Chief Counsel with various Federal Communications Commission (FCC) matters.  Since the fall of 2008, Legal Advisor has provided legal services in two main areas.  The first area encompasses legal advice and expertise related to the FCC licenses held by the School Board for Educational Broadband Spectrum (EBS).  A portion of the EBS-related work represents a recurring effort that is necessary and will continue for as long as the School Board holds the EBS licenses.  The remainder of the EBS-related work is made of various initiatives that are finite and will ultimately be completed.
EBS matters that represent recurring efforts include, but are not limited to the following:
Periodic renewal of the School Board's eight EBS licenses.  At the present time, the renewal for EBS station WHR-994 is pending with the FCC. 
Miscellaneous unanticipated issues that arise as a result of FCC activities that Legal Advisor monitors and other unanticipated circumstances.  For example, there is legislation pending in Congress requiring the FCC to undertake a spectrum utilization analysis, with a likely outcome being that licensees who are not making effective use of their licensed spectrum would not be entitled to a license renewal.
EBS matters that are finite, include but are not limited to the following:
Preparation of legal memorandum and follow up meetings and conferences to assist the School Board with satisfaction of the FCC's new substantial service requirements for the School Board's eight EBS licenses.  Failure to meet the FCC's requirements could lead to the loss of the School Board's licenses and their value.
 
Completion of negotiations and drafting of an agreement in connection with EBS band plan transition in the Palm Beach area (the School Board has requested approximately $2 million in reimbursement from Clearwire for EBS transition-related expenses).   Because Clearwire is the "proponent" for the Palm Beach market, the FCC mandates that the School Board transition its licenses in cooperation with Clearwire.



Unwinding of the Sprint (now Clearwire) lease agreement which allows the School Board to use pre-transition channels E1, E2 and E3 pending completion of the EBS band plan transition in the Palm Beach market.    The School Board currently uses these channels as part of its EBS operations.



Resolution of outstanding legal issues concerning coverage of new EBS licenses issued to FAU (FCC licensing data does not accurately reflect the School Board's agreement with FAU).   This issue must be resolved to ensure that the School Board maintains all of the population and area coverage that it is entitled to keep under the FAU agreement, but which is not currently reflected in the FCC's database.
The second main area that Legal Advisor has provided legal assistance and expertise entails legal services regarding the negotiation and drafting of agreements related to the potential purchase of the assets of WXEL TV and FM radio. 
Prior to this contract, all legal fees, whether they were related to EBS matters or WXEL matters, were paid from a common fund.  In order to allow the Board to more fully understand the effort being expended on these two distinct matters, staff has broken the work out into two separate contracts, each of which will have a separate budget.
This contract is for EBS related work generally described above. 
The Consultant shall perform the following services: Legal Advisor shall provide FCC regulatory and transactional advice to the School District with respect to Educational Broadband Service Spectrum.
This contract is for a not to exceed amount of $90,000.00 and the term of the contract through June 30, 2010.
The District needs an attorney with expertise in FCC regulatory matters, spectrum use and valuation issues to assist staff in the protection of the EBS licenses and their value, evaluation of the alternatives available to the School Board.
The firm of Fish & Richardson and Attorney Ed Lavergne have provided excellent service since being hired in March 2005. Approval of this contract will allow Mr. Lavergne to continue to provide the Chief Counsel with sound advice that can be used as the basis for making future decisions regarding the EBS licenses held by the School Board.

Policy 8.16



Homework Policy





  1. Homework is to be defined as subject-related assignments given by a teacher which will require time and effort outside the regular classroom for successful completion.


  2. The School Board believes homework will promote the educational process and can form an educational partnership between the school, students, and parents.


  3. The School Board and the School District have adopted the following procedures to carry out the homework policy:




    1. Principals are to serve as primary advocates for school-wide policies regarding homework assignments.


    2. Each school will establish a method of communicating the school's homework policy to students and parents.


    3. Each school will develop its own standards for assignment of homework and completion of homework. The following should be included in these standards:




      1. A purpose for homework assignments should be established.


      2. A portion of the student's grade (according to the Pupil Progression Plan) should be based upon the completion of homework assignments.


      3. Homework should not be used as a form of punishment.


      4. Homework should not require the use of reference materials unavailable to the student.
