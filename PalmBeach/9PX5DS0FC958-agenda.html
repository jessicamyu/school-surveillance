Meeting
Nov 18, 2014 - 2. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve receipt of the 'Developing Knowledge About What Works to Make Schools Safe' Grant from the National Institute of Justice in the amount of $3,909,116, effective January 1, 2015 through December 31, 2017; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded the Developing Knowledge About What Works to Make Schools Safe Grant from the National Institute of Justice (NIJ) for $3,909,116 for three years, from January 1, 2015 through December 31, 2017.  

 

The purpose of the District’s School/Justice Partnership Project is to research the expansion of a school-based intervention to increase school safety, while reducing delinquency referrals and improving student outcomes.   This grant project will serve students in Palm Beach Gardens High School, Royal Palm Beach High School, Glades Central High School, and Lake Worth High School.

 

The research partner is the College of Criminology and Criminal Justice at Florida State University.  Other partners include the Drug Abuse Treatment Association, local law enforcement agencies, Community Partners, and the Department of Juvenile Justice.

 

The program will build upon and expand the pilot School Justice Partnership developed in 2012 for Palm Beach Lakes High School through the Department of Educational Alternatives.  Funds will be used for school-based, Multi-Tiered System of Support (MTSS) personnel, program management, the required external program evaluation, contracted mental health and substance abuse services, and contracts with the  Department of Juvenile Justice for school-based probation officers.

 

CONTACT:

Keith Oswald, Interim Chief Academic Officer (keith.oswald@palmbeachschools.org)

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The financial impact to the FY15 Special Revenue-Other Funds Budget will be a total increase of up to $3,909,116.  These funds will be allocated to the Department of Educational Alternatives (9304).  All grant related expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue.

Meeting

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to execute and transmit all necessary documents to participate in the Municipalities Continuing Disclosure Cooperation Initiative including, without limitation, completion of the questionnaire and, if appropriate, a settlement agreement with the Securities Exchange Commission under favorable terms and conditions.

DESCRIPTION:

·         In March 2014, the Securities Exchange Commission (SEC) launched the MCDC Initiative or  “Municipalities Continuing Disclosure Cooperation Initiative” with a goal of improving compliance with SEC Rule 15c2-12 or the “Rule”.  The Rule requires underwriters of municipal securities to review disclosure documents prior to selling municipal debt to investors.   This new initiative is directed at issuers who made inaccurate statements in a final Official Statement regarding their prior compliance with their continuing disclosure filings within the past 5 years, and at underwriters who underwrote municipal securities within the past 5 years without having a reasonable basis to believe in the accuracy of an issuer’s representations in the Official Statement about its prior continuing disclosure compliance.  

·         To avoid an SEC enforcement action, the MCDC Initiative allows issuers and underwriters to “self-report” any material misstatements in offering statements.  This initiative further offers favorable settlement terms to anyone that participates in the initiative.  The deadline to “self-report” was September 10, 2014 for underwriters and is December 1, 2014 for issuers.

·         The District has been diligent in filing all items required by the Rule.  However, in 2012, unrelated to this initiative, District staff found some items that did not fully comply with the Rule.  The District has been advised that while we believe these items are not material and all the information was readily available to the market, mitigating any negative impact to investors, it is in the best interest of the District to “self-report” the information and take advantage of the safe harbor offered through the MCDC Initiative.

o   The District filed the required annual financial information with the “Nationally Recognized Municipal Securities Information Repository (the “NRMSIRs”) as required in our offering statements.   In 2009, EMMA or “Electronic Municipal Market Access” became the exclusive site for all disclosure however District staff was not aware of that change until 2011.   The District continued to make filings with the NRMSIRs in 2009 and 2010.  The District was not notified that the filing was done incorrectly.  Even the annual audit and the review from the Auditor General continued to verify that filings were made with the NRMSIRs.  The documents were posted on our website and sent to all interested parties such as underwriters, rating agencies, and bond insurers. 

o   Two defeasance notices were not filed by the District.  Other notices were filed by the Trustee so investors were not materially impacted.

o   The District did not file notices regarding the downgrade of certain bond insurers.  District staff were notified that was no longer required by the SEC but were not notified when the SEC reversed that decision.  These downgrades were widely published so again, investors were not materially impacted.

o   The District immediately remedied all deficiencies and has disclosed the errors in all offering statements beginning in 2012. 

·         Underwriters throughout the country have participated in the MCDC Initiative to avoid additional sanctions.  The District has been advised that two underwriters have “self-reported” for three of the District’s debt issues.

·         The “self-report” process includes the completion of a questionnaire and an agreement to enter into a standardized settlement agreement in which the District would neither admit nor deny any findings nor pay any fines, but would consent to the entry of a cease and desist order prohibiting any future violations of the securities laws. 

·         The settlement will stipulate the following:

o   Compliance with all existing continuing disclosure undertakings, including updating past delinquent or omitted filings (already complete)

o   Adoption of continuing disclosure policies and procedures (in process)

o   Cooperation with any subsequent investigation by the SEC

o   Disclosure of the settlement terms in future Official Statements during the next five years

o   A written certificate of compliance to the SEC within one year of the date of commencement of the proceedings

·         To ensure the District remains in compliance, several actions have been taken.

o   Digital Assurance Certification, L.L.C. (DAC) was hired as a dissemination agent in June 2013 to ensure disclosures are filed correctly.

o   Staff has recommended a contract with Nabors, Giblin and Nickerson as disclosure counsel.  This contract will be considered by the School Board on November 18, 2014 to ensure future offering statements are created in the best interest of the School Board and in full compliance with the Rule.

o   District staff is working with bond counsel, financial advisor and the Finance Committee to modify the debt policy, debt guidelines, and procedures to ensure ongoing and future compliance with the District’s continuing disclosure undertakings pursuant to the Rule.

·         The Finance Committee discussed the MCDC Initiative and this agenda item at their meeting on November 14, 2014.

I recommend approval of the renewal of Term Contract 12C-36A for Temporary Employment Services for the period of January 1, 2015 through December 31, 2015; not to exceed $4,000,000 and the 2015 fiscal year spend with A & Associates, and Estime & Irvin Associates, Inc.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the renewal of Term Contract 12C-36A for temporary employment services with A & Associates, a District certified M/WBE vendor and Estime & Irvin Associates, Inc., both bid awarded vendors.  The renewal term of this contract is January 1, 2015 through December 31, 2015.

 

This bid is utilized mainly by the School Food Service Department to hire temporary employees to efficiently manage the volume of workers needed to support this program.  Schools and Departments also utilized temporary employees as needed.

 

Per Purchasing Department Procedures, an industry standard review comparing similar bids from other governmental entities was conducted and it was found that those bids were consistent with this bid with regards to the hourly rates. It is a requirement that hourly rates utilized for this bid are in line with the Florida Agency for Workforce Development, Labor Market Statistics for the West Palm Beach – Boca Raton MSA.

 

A1A Employment of Miami was originally awarded on this bid but is no longer in business.

 

Spend with A & Associates will not exceed $1,000,000 for the term of this contract and spend with Estime & Irvin Associates, Inc. will not exceed $3,000,000.
