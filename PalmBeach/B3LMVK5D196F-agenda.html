I recommend the Board approve the Consultant Agreement with Asia Society's Center for Global Education effective August 30, 2018 through September 30, 2019; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to finalize and sign all necessary documents

DESCRIPTION:

This is a consultant agreement with Asia Society's Center for Global Education to implement an innovative learning system based on best practices in curriculum, assessment and instruction aligned to state standards.  Asia Society is the leading educational organization dedicated to promoting mutual understanding and strengthening partnerships among peoples, leaders and institutions of Asia and the United States in a global context.  

The mission of the Asia Society is to prepare all students for work and citizenship in an interconnected world by across the fields of arts, business, culture, education and policy.  The Society provides insight, generates ideas, and promotes collaboration to address present challenges and create a shared future.  They also work to ensure that youth, regardless of their background, have the competencies to prosper within a global economy, thrive amid diversity and take on the world's intractable problems.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve and adopt the FY 2019 through 2028 Capital Plan as submitted.

DESCRIPTION:

FY 2019 Capital Plan.

  • Each year, the School Board is required to adopt a capital plan prior to the adoption of the budget.
     
  • The first year of the plan is the FY 2019 Capital Budget that will be approved with the FY 2019 budget later today.
     
  • As required by law, the documents submitted for adoption are balanced.  
     
  • Since the School Board last viewed the capital plan on June 6th, the plan has been updated to include balances remaining from FY 2018.  Revenue projections have been updated based on the latest information available and minor adjustments were made to reflect the FY 2019 budget.  
     
  • The projects planned for FY 2020 through FY 2028 (years 2 through 10) can and will be amended as we work through the budget process for those years.  Any future changes that impact the Sales Tax Project List will be reviewed by the Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee (ISSOC) and presented to the School Board for approval.
     
  • The Tentative District Education Facilities Plan, which is the state version of the capital plan, will be brought to the School Board at a future meeting after DOE makes the online forms available.

PIR1 City of West Palm Beach and Board of Supervisors of the WPB Golf Club Parking Lot - Interlocal Agreement
Meeting
Sep 5, 2018 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Interlocal Agreement with the City of West Palm Beach and the West Palm Beach Golf Club to utilize a portion of the existing golf course parking lot, and authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  1. Historically, Forest Hill Community High School has not had sufficient parking for school faculty, students and staff.  Because of continued student body growth, the School District is again requesting use of that portion of the golf course parking lot utilized by the school previously.
     
  2. Approval of the Agreement would allow teachers and staff to park in the lower parking area of the adjacent City-owned Golf Course. The lower lots currently contain approximately 55 parking spaces that will be available for School Board use. (See map)
     
  3. The School Board will create a parking permit system to identify staff members who will park in the lower parking area offered by the City.
     
  4. The School Board will provide general security for the premises while under Board use.
     
  5. The School Board will must return the lot to the condition it was in at the beginning of this term of agreement. 
     
  6. The City of West Palm Beach approved this Interlocal Agreement on August 14, 2018.
     
  7. This Agreement may be terminated by either party with thirty (30) days notice to the other party.
     
  8. The School Board will be responsible for any maintenance, repairs and improvements associated with the lower bowl parking. 

 

CONTACT:

Wanda F. Paul, Chief Operating Officer (wanda.paul@palmbeachschools.org)

Kristin Garrison, Director of Planning and Intergovernmental Relations (kristin.garrison@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The financial impact to the budget will be the cost of maintenance and repair of the parking lot, performed as needed during the term of the agreement, as well as any improvements deemed necessary by the District to utilize and secure the area.

Subject
DS2 Use of State Seal in School District Buildings
Meeting
Sep 5, 2018 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
12. New Business
Type
Action
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board ratify and approve the use of the State Seal of Florida to be displayed in each District building to comply with Florida Statute 1003.44 (4), find that this use of the Great Seal by the School District meets the conditions of Fla. Admin. Code Rule 1-2.0021(9), and authorize the Superintendent or designee to take all necessary actions to implement the posting and the Board Chairman or designee to sign the authorization related to these recommendations.

DESCRIPTION:

Section 22 of the recently approved HB7055, approved during the last legislative session, amends Florida Statute 1003.44 and now requires the following:

 

(4) Each district school board shall adopt rules to require, in all of the schools of the district and in each building used by the district school board, the display of the state motto, “In God We Trust,” designated under s. 15.0301, in a conspicuous place.

 

As such, the District needed to display in a conspicuous place in each of District building the State’s motto.  These words appear on the Seal of the State of Florida and Staff recommends the use of the Seal.

 

Note, the required policy is currently being developed for future Board approval.

 

The Florida Department of State has issued Fla. Admin. Code Rule 1-2.0021-- Use of Seal of the State of Florida-- which states requirements for a governmental entity to use the seal:

 

(9) State and Local Government Agencies. Subject to the requirements of subsection 1-2.0021(6), F.A.C., state and local governmental agencies as defined in paragraphs 1-2.0021(5)(d) and (e), F.A.C., may use and display the Great Seal in connection with official business without application to the Department, when specific written approval for use of the Great Seal has been granted by the head of the governmental entity.

 

Accordingly, Board approval is needed for the use of this Seal.

 

The conditions set forth in subsection 1-2.0021(6) of that Rule are as follows:

(a) The specific item to be manufactured; (b) The manner in which the Great Seal is to be displayed on the item to be manufactured; (c) The nature of the proposed use, including manner, purpose and place of use; (d) Whether the public would tend to be misled by the appearance of the Great Seal on the product to believe that the product carries official State sanction or approval; (e) Whether the use of the Great Seal would tend to mislead the public into believing that a person, meeting, project or event carries official State sanction or approval;(f) Whether the dignity of the Great Seal will be preserved if approval is granted; and  (g) Whether the requested use of the Great Seal will promote a stated governmental goal

 

The nature of the use of the Seal and the other standards are met.  The reason for the display is to meet the new statutory requirement to display these words, there is no misleading of the public, and the dignity of the Seal will be preserved.
