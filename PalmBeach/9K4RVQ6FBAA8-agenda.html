On July 11, 2007, the Fourth District Court of Appeal reversed the decision of the State Board of Education to uphold this School Board’s termination of the two charter agreements with Survivors Charter Schools, Inc. In addition, the Court remanded the case to the School Board “so that the termination of Survivors’ charters based on a determination of good cause shown can be considered following proper notice and subject to the due process protections of the APA.”  The School Board filed several motions with the Court, including a motion for rehearing, and the Court by Order dated December 4, 2007, denied the motions. 



 



Prior to the issuance of the mandate from the Court, and due to time deadlines, the Superintendent and School Board have several options to consider at this Board meeting. Among the options are the following that may be recommended and voted upon by the Board :



 



1.   Whether to accept or reject a settlement with Survivors.  [and/or]



 



2.     a. Whether to hold an evidentiary hearing as to termination of the charters and, if so, the procedures and if the hearing will be held before the full School Board, a Committee of School Board members, or one Board member as allowed by the APA. [and/or]



b. The hiring of outside counsel and a decision as to which particular counsel should be retained. [and/or]



 



3.     Whether to pursue further judicial remedies, including whether to seek review by the Florida Supreme Court.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board consider the options as listed in the above description approve those it deems appropriate and authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to execute any necessary documents.  Further, Chief Counsel is authorized to take all necessary action to effectuate any chosen option.
