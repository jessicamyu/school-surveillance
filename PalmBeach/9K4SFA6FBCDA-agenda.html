PR-1 Galaxy Elementary School/Interlocal Agreement with the City of Boynton Beach
Meeting
Mar 28, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)


  1. The FY 2009-2013 Five Year Plan includes the modernization of Galaxy Elementary School.

  2. On February 10, 2010 the School Board approved the Interlocal Agreement with the City of Boynton Beach for the exchange of land for the construction of the new Galaxy Elementary School and a Replacement Park for the City. (See Exhibit "A")

  3. The School Board will convey a 5.48 Preservation Property to the City of Boynton Beach that is contiguous to the City's new Replacement Park. (See Exhibit "B")

  4. The City shall construct a greenway, nature trail, and pathway markers within the Preservation Property and a sidewalk providing connectivity for the neighborhoods to the North, to the Replacement Park and the new Galaxy Elementary School.

  5. The City shall be responsible for all construction costs, fees, permits and all maintenance costs and care of the Preservation Property.

  6. The City shall pay for the Commitment, Title Policy, survey, the costs of recording the property deed and the cost of satisfying and correcting title and survey matters.

  7. The Deed will include a reverter clause that if the City does not use the Preservation Property as intended the City must offer the 5.48 acre property back to the School Board at no cost. 

  8. The City may not divest the property to a third party.

  9. The School Board shall have the exclusive right to use the Preservation Property during school hours inclusive of normal after-school operational hours for educational needs.

  10. Both the School Board and the City share the mission to utilize the Preservation Property and improvements to further environmental education, environmental stewardship, environmental research and environmental curricula serving all ages and educational levels.


 

Recommendation:









I recommend the Board approve the Interlocal Agreement for the conveyance of a 5.48 acre Preservation Property to the City of Boynton Beach, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

IN1 Approval to Purchase Master Builders Risk Insurance
Meeting
Mar 28, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

The policy will cover damage to the property being built as well as theft of materials from the site during the construction phase for new and replacement schools/buildings.  This policy covers all facilities started during the policy period and those currently still under construction.



The Master Builders Risk Policy eliminates the need to go directly to the contractor for a project-specific, stand-alone builders risk policy.  Over the last several years the program saved 70 percent of contractor-purchased builders risk coverage.



The policy will have annual aggregate limits of $25 million subject to a sub-limit of $10 million for any one loss occurrence and $10 million aggregate in one policy year for Named Windstorm.  Name Windstorm also has a five (5) percent deductible (Self-Insured Retention) subject to a $250,000 minimum.  However, because the District owns the policy rather than the contractor, the District will be eligible for FEMA reimbursements, for which a contractor would not be eligible.



The deposit premium is based on anticipated construction values of approximately $63.2 million to be completed in the policy year.  Therefore, the premium will be approximately $421,000 of which we pay 50 percent now or $210,500, subject to annual audit adjustments.  In addition, last year's anticipated construction values were not met, resulting in a return premium of approximately $122,500.  The net result is a premium expenditure for March 31, 2012 to March 31, 2013 of $88,000.



The broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., will receive a commission of six (6) percent on this policy.  In addition to replacing the insurance, they will be responsible for tracking all payments, any changes relevant to this coverage and all reconciliations at year-end.

Recommendation:

I recomment that the Board approve the purchase of the Master Builders Risk Insurance Policy with Lexington Insurance through the broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., effective March 31, 2012 to March 31, 2013 at a premium cost of up to .7208 per $100 of construction costs, for a one-year net deposit premium of $88,000 including assessments and taxes.

Contact:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Operations Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)



Dianne L. Howard, Director, Risk and Benefits Management (dianne.howard@palmbeachschools.org)



 

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the District for this coverage is approximately $88,000 for the policy year.  Additional coverage will be purchased as needed based on construction costs.  The source of the funds is a transfer from the District's Capital Funds budget to the General Fund.

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)(1), "A charter may be renewed provided by a program review demonstrates that the criteria in paragraph (a) have been successfully accomplished and that none of the grounds for non renewal established by paragraph (8)(a) has been documented." F.S. 1002.33 (8)(a) The sponsor may choose not to renew or may terminate the charter for any of the following grounds:







  1. Failure to participate in the state's education accountability system created in s. 1008.31, as required in this section, or failure to meet the requirements for student performance stated in the charter.




  2. Failure to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management.




  3. Violation of law.




  4. Other good cause shown.


The School Board's approval of the Charter Renewal Program indicates an agreement to enter into charter contract negotiations, and if successfully negotiated by Legal and approved by the School Board, will result in entering into a Charter Contract. Once approved, the School Board will enter into a Charter Contact. The Charter will be a binding agreement, which shall detail the rights and responsibilities of the School Board and the Charter School. If the Charter Renewal Program is not approved by the School Board, the School Board will have to send a 90 day notice letter indicating the reasons for non renewal and the right to appeal. The charter school then has the right to file an appeal. If the school does not appeal or the school appeals and the School Board prevails, then the school is closed after the 90 days.

CLS 1 2012-2013 INSTRUCTIONAL MATERIALS ADOPTIONS
Meeting

Pursuant to School Board Policy 8.122 (3), district instructional materials adoption committees were convened during 2011-2012 to evaluate and recommend new materials for adoption in the area of social studies, grades K-12.  Committee membership included: subject area classroom teachers and coaches, district level social studies curriculum administrators, and lay citizens.  Committees reviewed all instructional materials programs submitted for state adoption and some additional submissions that were considered appropriate for committee review.  Titles recommended for district adoption are contained in Attachment A.



The current-adopted texts are seven years old and do not comply with the new standards (NGSSS adopted 2008), nor do they address the increasing mandates of social studies instruction such as the Common Core State Standard Initiative (CCSSI), state end-of-course examinations, or the Sandra Day O'Connor Civics Education Act.  Additionally, Florida Statute 1003.42 mandates instruction in certain areas of history and social studies such as African and African-American Studies, Holocaust Studies, Hispanic, Gender, and Women's Studies; current texts do not support these areas. 



Publishers have realized the major impact of social studies on the 21st century learner and have provided clear guidelines for the teacher.  New texts include the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards, Common Core Standards, and civics instruction guidelines.  These new texts will also combine English Language Learner and Exceptional Student Education strategies.  The School District of Palm Beach County has an increasing number of dual language schools, and the next instructional materials adoption series will address the strategies necessary to educate students within these programs.



The recommended instructional materials programs will be provided in both print and/or online digital formats.  Student editions in the core courses will be available to students via their Edline accounts.  Teacher materials and resources will also be available on Learning Village.  The elementary and middle school recommended instructional materials are available in Spanish for dual language schools.



The 2012-2013 Instructional Materials Cost Analysis (Attachment B) represents estimated costs for implementation of new adoptions.  Funding is provided from carry-over balance from the 2011-2012 State Categorical Allocation for Instructional Materials, the projected 2012-2013 State Categorical Allocation, and projected collections for lost/damaged materials from FY2012.  The projected 2012-2013 State Categorical Allocation figure is an estimation and actual funding is subject to legislative appropriation.



The Instructional Materials Department has worked closely with the Purchasing Department to obtain bids from the various publishers.  This will secure the best possible price to help ensure a complete K-12 social studies implementation.



Staff development for instructional materials will be provided at no cost to the District by publishers and will be focused on the appropriate use of materials to implement the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards. 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the 2012-2013 Instructional Materials Program budget of $10,246,000 and the list of instructional materials for the District adoption periods indicated.
