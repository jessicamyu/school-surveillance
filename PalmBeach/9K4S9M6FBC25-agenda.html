Frances Carter-Educator and Community Advocate
Meeting
May 18, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Discussion

Frances Carter is a former educator with the School District, a pillar of the community, an advocate for youth and an activist for civil rights.  She attended school at S.D. Spady Elementary, Carver High School and graduated with a Bachelor of Science Degree with a Major in Science from Barber-Scotia College in Concord, North Carolina, she also attended Florida Atlantic University.  Mrs. Carter began her teaching career during a volatile time in 1965 at S.D. Spady Elementary; after her first year, she was one of two blacks reassigned to Addison-Mizner Elementary in Boca Raton to integrate staff in 1967.  She taught at Addison-Mizner Elementary for 21 years, during which time Mrs. Carter served on the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools for the accreditation and review of schools in St. Lucie County.  She transferred to Carver Middle School for two years where she started a drop-out prevention program; then, Omni Middle where she worked until she retired in 1999. 



In 1974, Mrs. Carter started tutoring neighborhood children in her home. She eventually moved to her uncle's garage as well as a large room at the police substation on 12th Avenue to support the growing group as the program gained more publicity, hence inaugurating SMART (Spelling, Math, And Reading Tutoring).  Mrs. Carter applied for and received a small grant from Children Services Council of Palm Beach County in 1993 and relocated her growing program to the Masonic Building where it was divided into two groups, Elementary and Middle/High School.  In 1997, SMART joined MAD DADS (Men Against Destruction, Defending Against Drugs and Social Disorder) of Greater Delray Beach and with the assistance of their staff, was incorporated and became Children Learning After School Session (C.L.A.S.S.) of Palm Beach County, Inc., which was made up of 12 people including retired and current teachers, high school students and retired employees of the school system.  Financial assistance from the School Board of Palm Beach County permitted C.L.A.S.S. to expand, benefiting students that attended the program with significant improvements in reading and math test scores, grades, students achieving honor roll and an increase in students going to college and graduating as a testament to its success.

This agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County and the National Research Center for Career and Technical Education expresses the mutual intent to conduct Rigorous Tests of Student Outcomes in Career and Technical Education Programs of Study. The research proposes to measure Career and Technical Education‚Äôs impact on student academic and technical achievement. The measures of effectiveness of the Programs of Study being captured include: (a) academic achievement (b) technical skills achievement (c) high school completion (d) placement in postsecondary education, work, or the military (e) program participation and completion by nontraditional students and (f) program participation and completion by students from special populations as defined by Perkins IV.



The scientifically based study of student outcomes in schools implementing CTE Programs of Study will identify successful methods for addressing the education and training needs of high school students, including nontraditional and special populations. The results of this study may be used to increase the effectiveness and improve the implementation of Programs of Study at other high schools so that they implement coherent and rigorous CTE content aligned with challenging academic standards. Per the MOU presented, the SDPBC will provide the student choice lottery file information to include current school enrollment and the ability to interview students and teachers enrolled in CTE Programs of Study. The National Research Center for Career and Technical Education will provide fees to be paid to the SDPBC for data received and results of the research in a written report.



Per this MOU, the School District will receive payment for data provided to the National Research Center.

RE-4 Joseph Littles-Nguzo-Saba Charter School Lease
Meeting
May 18, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)




  1. Roosevelt Full Service Center will be relocated to North Technical High School (North Tech) formerly known as Inlet Grove Community High School, 7071 Gardens Road, Riviera Beach, during the summer of 2011.




  2. Since a portion of the Roosevelt Full Service Center Campus will be available JLNS will relocate from North Tech., where they have been temporarily housed, to the Roosevelt Full Service Center Campus for one year (July 1, 2011 - June 30, 2012) with two one year renewal options.




  3. The lease permits JLNS to occupy Buildings 5 & 6, and in Building 1, a portion of the office facilities and Room 203 (adult restroom facilities). The cafeteria, gym and basketball courts will be available for their use during school hours. (See Exhibit A)




  4. JLNS Charter School will be responsible for all costs associated with the move.




  5. JLNS Charter School is responsible for all utilities and maintenance fees related to the operation of the Leased Premises.




  6. A "Spot" Survey or amendment to the Educational Plant Survey will be prepared and submitted to Florida Department of Education for approval of the relocation of JLNS Charter School to Roosevelt Full Service Center and the remodeling/renovation of the current North Tech (aka Old Inlet Grove) to house Roosevelt Full Service and other District departments.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve a Lease Agreement between Joseph Littles-Nguzo-Saba, Inc. Charter School (JLNS) and the School Board of Palm Beach County for a portion of the Roosevelt Full Service Center Campus located at 1601 N. Tamarind Avenue, West Palm Beach; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.
