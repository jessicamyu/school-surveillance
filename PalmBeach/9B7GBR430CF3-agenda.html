I recommend that the School Board approve the Third Amendment to the Charter Agreement for Montessori Academy of Early Enrichment, Inc., and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The School Board of Palm Beach County ("School Board") and Montessori Academy of Early Enrichment, Inc. ("Montessori") entered into an Agreement on July 1, 2008, and ended June 30, 2013.  On July 17, 2013, the School Board approved Montessori's First Amendment to extend the current Charter Agreement until August 31, 2013.

 

Montessori Academy of Early Enrichment, Inc.'s Charter Agreement expired August 31, 2013.   The Renewal Program Review was approved by the School Board on March 6, 2013, and the School Board and Montessori Academy have been and continue to be in the process of negotiating terms and conditions of a renewal Charter Agreement.

 

In order to complete meaningful negotiations to secure a mutually beneficial and legally binding Charter Agreement, the parties agree that it is necessary for their mutual benefit and protection that the term of the current Charter Agreement must be extended.

 

Therefore, the parties agree that the term for existence of the current Charter Agreement (as amended) is hereby extended up through and including October 31, 2013.

 

All other terms of the Charter Agreement shall remain in full force and effect, subject to further extensions of time, as mutually deemed necessary by the parties.  This Third Amendment, together with the Charter and previous amendments, constitutes and represents the entire agreement between the parties hereto and supersedes any prior understandings or agreements, written or verbal, respecting the subject matter herein.

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the Second Amendment to the Charter Contract (hereinafter Secon Corrective Action Plan-CAP) between the School Board of Palm Beach County and My Choice Academy, Inc. and authorize the Superintendent or his designe to sign all related contracts or documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On June 20, 2012, the School Board granted My Choice Academy, Inc. (My Choice) a five (5) year Charter Contract with the School Board.  The Charter became effective upon signing by both parties, and covers a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2012, and ending June 30, 2017, unless amended or terminated as provided in the Charter.

 

Due to  noted financial, governance, facilities, and academic problems, on April 24, 2013, the School Board voted to approve a Corrective Action Plan and First Amendment to My Choice's current Charter to resolve the issues that caused a Superintendent recommendation to terminate the Charter.

 

My Choice later became out of compliance with many of the matters in the Corrective Action Plan and did not have an approved facility, but opened school on August 19, 2013. The school was located in a temporary location not approve by the District and without providing the District with a certificate of occupancy, appropriate insurance coverage certificates, and certification of its bus driver.

 

The Superintendent initially recommended an immediate and 90 day termination of the Schools' Charter for danger to the health and safety of the students, failure to provide items in the Opening School Checklist, and violation of the Corrective Action Plan.

 

The parties reached an agreement in principle and the Superintendent revised his recommendation that the School Board approve a settlement with these terms unless the governing board of My Choice did not approve the agreement.

 

On August 21, 2013, the School Board voted to approve the Superintendent's revised recommendation and that the General Counsel's office draft the Second Corrective Action Plan to be brought to the School Board for approval at its next meeting, which is September 11, 2013.

 

The Second Amendment to the My Choice June 21, 2012 Charter (Second Corrective Action Plan-CAP) and Executive Summary of this Amendment are attached for your review.  Also attached are the Charter, First Amendment and legal checklist.

 

By letter dated September 5, 2013, attached hereto, My Choice Academy was notified of this Board Meeting.

James T. Pegg

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

According to the WPBMCS application submitted to Palm Beach County, the mission is, “To provide a quality traditional elementary education to the students residing in the municipality of West Palm Beach in a high quality facility and environment with high quality faculty and staff that are efficient and effective.  Emphasis will be placed on comprehensive literacy improvement for all grades and may include other areas of focus.  By engaging the community and generating a strong partnership between home and school, the City of West Palm Beach’s Elementary Charter School will strive for active participation and involvement of parents, teachers, business leaders, and community members in the students’ learning and development to enable them to achieve their greatest potential and ultimately become lifelong learners.”

 

The District’s charter application process was explained to applicants during the New Applicants’ Workshop held on May 8, 2013. 

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

 

Projected Enrollment

 

First Year                     K-5                        600 Projected Enrollment

Second Year                K-5                        600 Projected Enrollment

Third Year                   K-5                        600 Projected  Enrollment

Fourth Year                 K-5                        600 Projected Enrollment

Fifth Year                    K-5                        600 Projected Enrollment

 

Education Program Design

 

WPBMCS's core subjects of Language Arts, Reading, Mathematics, Social Studies, and Science will serve as the foundation around which individualized, guided, self-directed learning plans will be developed.  Through integration of WPBMCS's ethics and values with the guided self-directed learning model, the students will receive a world-class education and an excellent preparation for an advanced curriculum in subsequent grades.  WPBMCS's mission is rooted in the belief that it is essential to create a safe, stimulating, and supportive environment that fosters the personal, social, and intellectual growth of each student.

 

Location

 

Final desired location, design, size and layout of WPBMCS has not been completed, pending application approval.

 

Applicant's Founding Governing Board

 

The governing body of WPBMCS will be a seven member Board of Directors.  The Executive Officers of the school's Board of Directors will consist of a President, Vice President, Secretary, and  Financial Specialist.  The Board of Directors will also include three members at large with duties to be assigned as needed.  The Board will operate as one body.

 

Ratings of West Palm Beach Municipal Charter School's Application

 

Designated District staff reviewed the 19 sections of WPBMCS's application.  After the evaluation, WPBMCS received a rating of "Meets the Standard" in all 19 sections.

 

Per the Overall Assessment Summary/Checklist, the ratings indicated that their application did meet the statutory requirement set forth in F.S. 1002.33(6).  (See Overall Assessment/Checklist)
