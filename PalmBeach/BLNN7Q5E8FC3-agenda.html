Education Program Design

NorthStar Academy is dedicated to providing a comprehensive educational alternative to middle school students who are over-age for grade and may have failed one or more grade levels, and high school students who have dropped out of the traditional school setting, or who are not on track to graduate with their cohort and are at risk of dropping out, or who are seeking to accelerate their high school learning experience. Many of these students have not experienced academic success at the traditional school. NorthStar Academy is founded on the belief that, given the option to participate in an innovative, student-centered learning environment that combines teacher-directed and technology-based instruction, every student has the potential to succeed in school, earn a standard high school diploma, and be prepared for post-secondary studies or careers.  The School is committed to helping students realize their dreams of becoming self-sufficient, contributing members of society so that they can lead productive, fulfilling lives. NorthStar Academy will offer credit accrual and acceleration in an academic environment that features both life-skills mentoring and teacher-directed instruction, which will be carried out by qualified and highly-committed teachers in one-to-one, small group, or whole group settings.  Each student will be assessed, assigned to one of the “Learning Labs” (online instruction) and scheduled for “Direct Instruction” (DI) with state-certified teachers. Teacher-directed lessons (in DI classrooms) will address the specific learning needs of each student, based on real-time student performance data, through the use of research-based programs and strategies that are designed to promote student mastery of state standards.

NorthStar Academy will implement the EdisonLearning Achievement Framework™, a comprehensive, research-based framework for sustained school improvement – a framework that can be flexibly applied to specific school priorities in order to identify the key levers for improving the quality of learning and teaching and managing change. The Framework is structured around planning and assessment processes, pedagogy, the culture and climate of learning, and the foundations of quality teaching. Teachers will participate in professional development throughout the year on the Achievement Framework. NorthStar Academy will apply the comprehensive framework to ensure excellence in education and effective implementation of the EdisonLearning Bridgescape Model. This educational model is comprised of five key tenets that the School believes are essential in meeting the needs of its target student population:

  1. A Blended Learning Model that combines a technology-based, self-paced curriculum and face-to-face, teacher-directed instruction;
  1. A Student Mentoring Program that provides individualized mentoring for each student;
  1. Social-Emotional Support which includes Social Emotional Learning (SEL), Mental Health Services, and the integration of Core Values;
  1. Individualized Instructional Plans that personalize each student’s instructional program which includes academic and career goals;
  1. A College and Career Focus which includes college and career guidance, and the implementation of Career Academies that will allow students to earn industry certification in various high-demand fields.

These components, when implemented and promoted effectively by NorthStar Academy, and with the active participation and involvement of all stakeholders - students, parents, staff, and the community - will create a positive, optimal learning environment for all students. The overarching goal is to graduate well-educated, highly disciplined, goal-oriented, and independent young adults. A detailed summary of each of the components of NorthStar’s educational program is provided below.

1. BLENDED LEARNING MODEL (Online Learning combined with Direct and Teacher-

Directed Instruction)

Online Learning

Learning Labs – NorthStar Academy’s educational facility will contain at least six large, spacious rooms (referred to as “learning Labs”) that will be fully equipped with a minimum of 50 individual computer stations. Upon enrollment, each student will be assigned to a specific Learning Lab, based on his/her grade level and the number of courses completed or credits earned by the student to date. Each student will be assigned his/her own computer station in the Lab. Students in grades 6-12 will complete required core courses and electives through a research-based online curriculum – EdisonLearning eCourses. Certified teachers will support students as they work online to complete courses at their own pace. Teachers will provide academic support to individual students (at their work station) or to small groups of students (at a small table located in the Learning Lab). Teachers will also assist students with online technical issues.

Curriculum – NorthStar will utilize EdisonLearning eCourses to provide students with online instruction. EdisonLearning's eCourses are based on highly researched methodology and designed to personalize the learning experience. The courses are self-guided, enabling students to complete coursework at their own pace, while fully monitored and supported with daily assessments and interventions. Core eCourses include Diagnostic and Progress Test options upon enrollment to further personalize a student’s learning path within a given course. Based on a modular design with lessons limited to one or two standards based on objectives that are clear and attainable, the structure helps students maintain focus and motivation by accomplishing achievable goals in each lesson. Each lesson is also accompanied by an assessment that tests mastery of lesson content. An online PDF textbook is also included with each course that serves as a companion guide to the content presented in each lesson.

Direct Instruction (Teacher-Directed Instruction)

Direct Instruction (DI) / DI Classrooms –NorthStar Academy’s educational facility will contain at least eight regular-sized classrooms (referred to as “DI Classrooms”) that will be set up as traditional classrooms in order to provide students with teacher-directed instruction in core subject areas. Each student will be assigned to specific DI Classrooms based on his/her grade level and “Learning Level” (explained below). Direct instruction (DI) will be provided by certified teachers as part of the School’s Blended Learning Model. Teachers will utilize research-based materials and instructional techniques to motivate and engage students in their learning and to give them the personalized attention they need in order to succeed. The School’s main focus will be on increasing students’ reading proficiency. As such, every core area teacher will be required to be reading endorsed.

Learning Levels Model – The School’s “Learning Level Model” features distinctive Essentials and Foundation Learning Levels for students in grades 6-12 who may need intensive intervention or enhanced instruction. Students will participate in assessments that will serve as a baseline to determine which Learning Level will be most appropriate for him/her. The diagnostic assessments include Reading Plus® and Study Island®, which are research-based assessments that are used both locally and nationally to measure levels of aptitude. Student data collected is used to place students at the appropriate Learning Level in reading and math, and to assign them to specific DI Classrooms. The curriculum in Learning Levels 1 and 2 features remedial coursework in Mathematics and Reading for students who are performing at lower grade levels, have poor study skills, low attendance, and a wide range of learning challenges. Learning Level 3 (Completion) and Learning Level 4 (Advanced) will apply to students nearing high school completion. The Completion and Advanced Learning Levels curriculum focuses on core area subjects and Career Paths.

Programs and Resources Used in DI Classrooms/Intensive Courses/Interventions – Teachers in DI Classrooms will use research-based programs and other instructional resources and materials to provide teacher-directed instruction. DI Classrooms will be equipped with an interactive whiteboard (or projector and screen) to allow teachers to use technology to enhance instruction. All students in grades 9-12 who have not met the ELA requirement for graduation, and students in grades 6-8 who have not met proficiency in ELA, will be required to complete daily lessons in Reading Plus, a research-based, supplementary online reading program. Students in grades 6-12 who require additional support in mathematics will use online supplementary programs such as Accelerated Math (Renaissance) and/or Study Island (Edmentum) which are designed to improve students’ conceptual knowledge and skills in order to master state standards. Online resources will be utilized by teachers during direct instruction that may include ReadWorks, Math Nation/Algebra Nation, Khan Academy, ReadTheory, and other resources related to science and social studies. Students taking Intensive Reading will use research-based core materials during teacher-directed instruction. The core curriculum for Intensive Reading will be National Geographic Learning’s Hampton-Brown Edge (for grades 9-12) and National Geographic Learning’s Inside (for grades 6-8). Students requiring more intensive instruction will also utilize Voyager Sopris Learning’s REWARDS-Secondary. The School may adopt different research-based supplementary and intervention programs in the future based on district recommendations and program success rates.

Instructional Methods and Strategies Used in DI Classrooms – Research-based instructional methods and strategies will be used in DI Classrooms to ensure that all students are actively engaged in the lessons and all students' needs are met. The School’s goal is less emphasis on lecture/discussion/worksheet and more facilitation, more collaboration with other students and teachers, and a shift in attitude as teachers help and encourage students to take more responsibility for their own learning. The learning environment at NorthStar will include the following:

  • Standards-based instruction to ensure student mastery of state standards
  • Data-driven, small group or one-on-one instruction to meet individual students’ needs based on assessment data
  • Whole group, direct instruction to introduce new skills to entire group
  • Problem solving and project-based learning to actively engage students in lessons and increase depth of understanding
  • Higher order questioning techniques to ensure appropriate rigor and elicit critical thinking

 

Gradual Release of Responsibility Model to provide necessary scaffolding of the material presented and to guide students toward using different skills, strategies and procedures independently

  • Rotational Instructional Model to provide meaningful and effective instruction through the implementation of five key components (described in Section 4)
  • Reciprocal Teaching to ensure conceptual understanding of material read
  • Graphic Organizers (Story Maps, Venn Diagrams, Webbing) to increase comprehension of text
  • Writing experiences in a variety of contexts to increase literacy skills and solidify understanding of the material
  • Instructional strategies and techniques such as modeling, repetition, paraphrasing, modified language, increased practice, visuals, language dictionaries, group and peer learning, to support the needs of ELL, ESE, and struggling students.

 

Differentiated Instruction – The School’s digital curriculum, EdisonLearning eCourses, makes it possible to individualize instruction to address diverse student needs. Students can progress at their own pace to master the material. The online courses incorporate multimedia — in the form of images, audio, video, animations, and interactive elements — along with instructional text to provide students with multiple representations of concepts as well as address their different learning styles. Formative assessments help students to gauge their understanding and improve performance, while summative assessments chart progress and skill development.

In Direct Instruction (DI) classrooms, teachers will use a variety of assessments to determine gaps in understanding or areas for learning extension. Teachers will provide small group and individual instruction focused on specific foundational and conceptual learning needs. By differentiating instruction, teachers will meet students’ individual learning needs and increase their capacity for success.

MTSS / Interventions –The School will implement a problem-solving framework to provide instruction that meets the needs of the student. Through a multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS), the School will address the needs of students who require additional academic or behavioral support to succeed. NorthStar Academy is committed to providing high-quality instruction and appropriate interventions that effectively meet students’ needs. The MTSS process involves the systematic use of assessment data to inform instruction and provide appropriate resources and interventions to improve student learning.

Test Preparation “Boot Camps” (Direct Instruction) – NorthStar Academy will implement reading and math “bootcamps” at specified times throughout the year to target each of the Reporting Categories and accompanying standards (according to school-wide data reports that indicate areas where students need the most support). Lessons will be taught by teachers certified in these areas.

Tutoring Sessions (Direct Instruction) – NorthStar Academy will offer tutoring sessions for students (at no cost) which will be provided by certified teachers. Tutoring sessions may be offered during the school day, on Saturdays, and/or during the summer.

 

2. STUDENT MENTORING

Student Mentoring Program – Each student in grades 6-12 will be assigned an adult mentor who is a member of the staff. Mentors will develop impactful relationships with assigned student “mentees” and their families. Mentors will focus on the personal development of each student, encouraging and affirming individual achievement. Mentors will also focus on building college and career readiness skills and assisting with academic and personal goal setting. Mentors will meet with their mentees on a weekly basis during a scheduled mentorship session.

3. SOCIAL-EMOTIONAL SERVICES

Social Emotional Learning (SEL) and Student Support – The School will help students develop key cognitive, social and self-management skills. The evidence-based approaches contained within SEL are designed to improve the cognitive engagement, behavioral skills and self-efficacy capabilities of youths which will lead to enhanced academic performance, and college and career readiness. As part of this development, students will be required to take the Life Skills eCourse, which explores skills including conflict resolution, time management, team building, health and wellness, budgeting, speaking and listening, and other soft skills. Additionally, the School will use the Scholar Centric Success Hiqhways student resiliency survey and curriculum (Grades 6-10). The Success Highways Resiliency Assessment identifies the social emotional needs of students. Individual and school reports are provided. After the Success Highways curriculum is completed, the resiliency assessment is administered again to see how students’ resiliency profiles have changed.

Mental Health Counseling – The School’s educational program will address the needs of the “whole child” not just the student’s academic needs. As such, the School will provide behavioral health support through an onsite social worker, and make appropriate referrals for students demonstrating the need for mental health counseling. The School will explore the availability of community resources and organizations that may be able to provide services for students at the School or at the student’s home

Values – NorthStar Academy will foster a school culture that emphasizes a clear set of core values in order to establish and maintain a positive school climate. The School will integrate the EdisonLearning 9 Core Values into the curriculum and culture of the School: Wisdom, Hope, Justice, Respect, Courage, Responsibility, Compassion, Integrity and Resiliency. The nine core values will inform all decision making and interactions in the School. To this end, the core values will be an integral part of the learning environment with posters describing the core values and exemplars of the core values, and core values instruction will also be a key component of the direct instruction provided by members of the instructional staff.

4. INDIVIDUALIZED INSTRUCTIONAL PLANS

Individualized Academic Acceleration Plan (Middle School Students) – Upon enrollment, over-age for grade students in grades 6-8 will meet with the Guidance Counselor/Academic Advisor to review his/her transcript and discuss student’s short-term and long-term goals. Students will be assigned to appropriate Learning Labs (online learning classrooms) based on number of middle school courses passed; enrolled in any required intensive courses based on state assessment scores (reading and/or math); tested in reading and math to obtain baseline levels and determine student’s Learning Level, and scheduled for direct instruction (DI) based on their Learning Levels in reading and math. An Individualized Academic Acceleration Plan (IAAP) will be developed for each over-age for grade middle school student that includes an accelerated course recovery plan, an academic strategy that will best facilitate the student’s success, an assigned staff member to serve as the student’s mentor, a tutoring plan, a high school readiness plan, and a post-secondary success plan.

Individualized Graduation Plan (High School Students) – The Individualized Graduation Plan (“Grad Plan”) will be initiated by the School’s Guidance Counselor/Academic Advisor who will conduct a thorough transcript evaluation followed by discussions with the student about both short-term and long-term goals. The student will be required to explore his/her post-secondary goals, identifying a goal of employment, enlistment, or enrollment. All this information will be used to create a personalized schedule that includes completion of all required courses through EdisonLearning’s eCourses with an expected graduation date; direct instruction (DI) assignments, including Intensive Reading and/or Intensive Math courses; test prep (based on passing rates for all state-required assessments); a tutoring plan, an assigned mentor, and a post-secondary plan (enrollment in college, immediate employment in an identified career, or enlistment in the military). Members of the Leadership Team, the Guidance Counselor/Academic Advisor, Student Services Support Staff, and the student’s mentor will monitor the implementation of each student’s Grad Plan throughout the year to ensure each student is on track to graduate according to the established goals.

5.  COLLEGE AND CAREER PREPARATION

College and Career Guidance – The Guidance Counselor/Academic Advisor (with support from the student’s mentor) will provide assistance with college exploration, enrollment requirements, and scholarship opportunities. The advisor will discuss career goals and interests with students and will provide information on careers and college alternatives (i.e., vocational school, military, etc.). Special events or activities such as Discovery Days, College and Career Fairs, college visits, and career-related guest speakers will be scheduled throughout the year. The Guidance Counselor/Academic Advisor will work with each student using the services and support provided through My Career Shines. Over-age for grade middle school students will be required to complete the Career Exploration course offered through the eCourses curriculum, and they will engage in activities that will help them explore career options under the direction of the Career Academy Director. The School’s goal is to transition middle school students into high school as quickly as possible so that they can participate in one of the available career tracks offered through the School’s Career Academies.

Career Academies – NorthStar Academy will provide students in grades 9-12 with opportunities to enroll in a Career Academy of their choice (among the available career tracks offered by the School). Career and Technical Education (CTE) courses that lead to industry certification will be a major focus of the School’s educational program. Students will be able to choose from among several career options (described in Section 4). An on-site Career Academy Director will oversee the School’s Career Academy programs and provide students with support in achieving their career goals.

B. Describe the basic learning environment (e.g., classroom-based, independent study, blended learning), including rationale for class size and structure and how the learning environment supports and is consistent with the mission.

NorthStar Academy’s education model provides an alternative option for a unique population of students. The School’s instructional model design is based on research of at-risk youth and aligns with the school’s mission. The blended instructional approach is both technology‐based and teacher‐directed. The curriculum design is mastery-based and serves over‐age, credit deficient and retained students who learn at different rates and have diverse learning styles. The School’s robust curriculum will result in increased engagement, improved performance, and preparation for college, work and life.

In keeping with the School’s mission and vision, the learning environment at NorthStar Academy is designed to allow students in grades 6-12 to accelerate completion of courses and credit accrual through a learning environment inclusive of technology-driven instruction, teacher-directed instruction, and individualized student support and mentoring. Student learning will be supported by highly-dedicated, certified teachers and additional support staff. Upon enrollment, each student will be assessed and then assigned to one of the Learning Labs. Teacher instruction will reflect the learning needs of the students assigned to each Learning Level and DI Classroom. Students will have an opportunity to work with subject area teachers and interact with their peers in a supportive, student-centered, and collaborative learning environment. NorthStar recognizes that the academic learning environment is strongly correlated with student achievement. The School will create learning opportunities for students that are conducive to success. An individualized and differentiated curriculum and research-based instructional strategies will be provided with the intention of inspiring students to take interest in the learning process and master the state standards. Learning style and rate differs for each student, and teachers will utilize an individualized approach to meet the needs of each student. Instructional formats and methods that will be utilized in DI classrooms to fulfill NorthStar Academy’s educational mission and philosophy include:

  • Small group, teacher-directed instruction for students requiring remediation in various areas of core content.
  • Individual, one‐to‐one teacher instruction for students requiring additional academic support in various core content areas.
  • Whole group direct instruction will cover various objectives from topics that address academic skills, work-study skills, social‐emotional well-being, and self-sufficiency skills. Leaders within the community will be invited to assist with presentations.
  • Research-based strategies and best practices that promote active student engagement in all lessons will be of utmost importance. Students must be actively involved in their own learning. Teachers will plan and deliver lessons that incorporate research-based strategies and best practices (described in Section 4) that promote student engagement, motivation, and in-depth understanding of skills and concepts taught. Teachers will function as “facilitators” of learning, rather than delivering lessons through lectures or scripted lesson plans.
  • Mastery-based projects will be based upon direct contact and/or manipulation with objects, materials and resources that support both teacher-directed and technology-driven instruction. Each Mastery Project will challenge the student to apply the concept not only to the world around them through an External Investigation, but also to create a product of their understanding through the Action Project. In each Mastery Project, students explore an open-ended guiding question through an internal and external investigation. Students are then called to action to demonstrate their learning through the final Action Project. Each project requires that students complete work outside of their eCourse and go into their communities to encourage soft skill development and career readiness. Each resulting artifact created during the Action Project could be shared through a digital portfolio to assist with college entrance or job acquisition.

In addition to daily teacher-directed instruction and hands-on learning opportunities provided in DI Classrooms, NorthStar Academy will provide small group, teacher-directed interventions for students in need of intensive reading and math instruction/remediation. Students whose state assessment levels do not show proficiency will be scheduled for teacher-directed Intensive Reading and Intensive Math classes. As well, students who need assistance in preparing for End-of Course (EOC) exams will receive test preparation or tutoring in DI Classrooms.

C. Describe the research base used to design the educational program.

NorthStar Academy’s educational program and instructional model are based largely on the findings of the National Dropout Prevention Center (NDPC). Since 1986, the National Dropout Prevention Center (NDPC) has conducted and analyzed research; sponsored workshops and national conferences; and collaborated with researchers, policymakers, and practitioners to further the mission of reducing America’s dropout rate by meeting the needs of youth in at-risk situations, including students with disabilities. NDPC has identified 15 Effective Strategies that have the most positive impact on reducing school dropout. NorthStar Academy has incorporated much of this research to develop a comprehensive educational program designed to meet the needs of a targeted group of students.

There is extensive research on the effectiveness of using a blended instructional model to meet the needs of over-age for grade middle school students and high school students who are in danger of dropping out of school, or have already dropped out and returned to school in an attempt to earn a standard high school diploma. NorthStar Academy will individualize instruction to ensure that each student receives the services and support needed based on his/her academic history and current grade level/credit status. The School will create an individualized instructional plan for each student upon enrollment. NorthStar Academy’s educational program will also incorporate non-academic components that have been shown to have a positive impact on student learning for student populations such as the one that will be served by the School. NorthStar Academy will provide wraparound services that include individualized student mentoring; support services to address the social, emotional, and mental well-being of each student; and college/career guidance and preparation. All of the components of NorthStar Academy’s educational program are firmly grounded on research that supports their effectiveness and success in addressing the needs of at-risk middle and high school students.

Research on Blended Learning Approach for At Risk Students

NorthStar Academy believes that learning style and rate differ for each student, and teachers will utilize a blended approach to meet the individual needs of each student. Individual tutoring, small group and whole group teacher-directed instruction, as well as technology‐driven, mastery-based, and self-paced instruction, will be used to promote academic success. Online Learning for Credit Recovery (2015) identified three key conclusions as best practices in the field of online programs:

  • Many online programs serving credit recovery and at-risk students have a significant face-to-face component for student supports. The blended approach provides expanded counseling, tutoring, and support services including face-to-face contact with teachers, who provide not only subject-area support, but also guidance on effective study skills.
  • Online courses can motivate students who have failed in the traditional classroom settings, as they can now learn at their own pace with the one-on-one help of an instructor. Students’ individual learning preferences and interests can be integrated and curriculum can be adapted to meet the students’ needs in online courses, which may make them more engaging to some students than traditional face-to-face classes.
  • Online and blended learning are particularly well suited for students recovering credit because they allow for personalized instruction, both by the teacher and through the use of adaptive software and course management technology. Diagnostic testing allows students to proceed once mastery is demonstrated. Personalized content selections and activities can offer multiple support options for those who need specific help and to keep students engaged.

NorthStar Academy’s educational program is based heavily on iNACOL’s standards, specifically their research around using online learning options for credit recovery, published in September 2015, which emphasizes the importance of delivering the credit recovery curriculum through a blended learning model. iNACOL is a nonprofit organization that provides policy advocacy, publishes research, develops quality standards, and drives the transformation to personalized, competency-based education forward. iNacol has found that virtual content, enhanced by significant face-to-face interaction with teachers and advisors, results in increased student performance.

Blended learning programs can motivate students who have failed in traditional classrooms, because now they are empowered to engage in the curriculum at their own pace. Students are able to take as much time as they need on a concept until they are able to demonstrate mastery. Students can revisit learning objects as many times as they need to become successful.

Research on Student Mentoring for At Risk Students

Mentoring is a one-to-one caring, supportive relationship between a mentor and a mentee that is based on trust. Multiple research studies document the social and emotional benefits that middle school students receive through mentoring programs, especially those students at risk of not completing high school (Komosa-Hawkins, 2012). Research findings about mentoring programs suggest that mentoring programs focusing on building strong and meaningful relationships between mentor and mentee may have the greatest benefit to adolescents (Larose et al., 2010). Every student at NorthStar Academy in grades 6-12 will be assigned an adult mentor who will work with them on a weekly basis to support their academic, social, and emotional growth. Mentors will establish a close relationship with student mentees in an effort to provide them with the academic and social-emotional guidance needed to meet individualized short-term and long-term goals.

Research on Meeting the Social-Emotional Needs of At Risk Students

As shown through research studies, students in the middle and high school grades who fit the profile of the “student at risk of dropping out” benefit greatly when schools place an emphasis on meeting their social-emotional needs. Research indicates that social and emotional learning (SEL) programs assist students not only in dealing with daily struggles, but also increase students' academic skills. In a research study on closing the achievement gap for African American and Latino students, Miranda et al (2007) cite several studies that concluded that the implementation of an SEL program among minority students significantly increased posttest scores in both reading and mathematics. Another research study (Bradley, 2013) cited evidence that these types of programs can “help level the playing field for historically underperforming students, raising their achievement and academic success levels to a degree that makes postsecondary education and advanced technical training a viable option with substantially increased probabilities of success.” NorthStar Academy recognizes that in order to help the School’s at-risk students to succeed; it must address not only the students’ academic deficits, but also provide them with social and emotional support. Goleman (2004) states that scientific data has demonstrated that SEL programs are effective in helping young people face problems and address life’s challenges. According to Goleman, SEL programs not only help students deal with difficult life issues but they also facilitate academic learning. Goleman cites research studies that provide evidence of the connection between SEL programs and academic success. NorthStar Academy will require all students to take the Life Skills eCourse which explores skills including conflict resolution, time management, team building, health and wellness, budgeting, speaking and listening, and other soft skills. Mentors will receive training on working with students to address their social-emotional needs. The School’s social worker will work with staff to identify students who may have a need for mental health services. The School will implement a Social-Emotional Learning (SEL) curriculum to provide students at all grade levels with information and coping skills that will help them deal with the struggles and challenges they face on a daily basis. NorthStar Academy will use the Scholar Centric resiliency survey and curriculum, Success Highways, for students in grades 6-10 to identify students with social emotional needs and provide appropriate interventions before students experience the negative impact of academic failure.

Research on College and Career Preparation for At Risk Students

NorthStar Academy seeks to integrate academic and career-based skills and, thus, raise academic standards for all students. This model helps make the transition from school to work easier for young people. Stone (2004) has identified several program techniques that keep students in school: career guidance, work-based learning, career pathways, and tech prep. Career guidance is an important element in keeping students in school. Career pathways often take the form of Career Academies. These are often schools-within-schools that are focused around a specific career area. Coursework includes academic and vocational classes. Kemple and Snipes (2000) found that enrollment in a career academy significantly decreased the dropout rate of at-risk students. Tech prep is similar to career pathways, but it is directly connected to postsecondary education. In its efforts to assist students in achieving their post-secondary career goals, NorthStar Academy will strive to enhance its Career Academies throughout the term of the charter by partnering with local colleges, universities, and technical/vocational schools.

School hours for students in grades 6-8 will be from 8:55 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. Breakfast will be provided for middle school students from 8:20 – 8:50 a.m. and lunch will be provided during the school day for 30 minutes during one of three lunch periods scheduled each day.

E. Explain how the services the school will provide to the target population support attainment of the state-adopted standards, as required by section 1002.33, F.S.

NorthStar Academy will provide a mastery-based educational program for the School’s target population that is geared toward ensuring that these students meet state-adopted standards. The School will implement an educational program that is designed to support attainment of the Florida Standards and the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards through the following features:

  • The core online curriculum that will be utilized—EdisonLearning eCourses— is aligned to state-adopted standards in all core areas. EdisonLearning eCourses provide rigorous and robust instruction in a variety of formats that address students’ specific academic levels and learning styles. eCourses also require that students demonstrate mastery (i.e., receive a score of 70% or better) of the current lesson's assessment in order to advance to the next lesson.
  • Assessments, both formal and informal, will be conducted throughout the year to ensure students are making adequate progress toward mastering state standards. Results will be used to identify areas of weakness and strength in order to provide targeted, personalized instruction. Both struggling students and those who are working at or above level will be provided appropriate instruction (remediation, reinforcement, or enrichment or extension).
  • Instructional materials that align with Florida Standards for all subject areas will be coupled with a vigorous program of professional development focused on implementing NorthStar Academy’s blended learning model that includes technology-based, teacher-led, and mastery-based instruction to ensure progress toward meeting standards.
  • The School’s Student Mentoring Program will provide a high level of support and individual monitoring to ensure that over-age for grade middle school students are making progress and that high school students are on track to graduate. Mentors, in collaboration with teachers, school leaders, the Guidance Counselor/Academic Advisor, and the Social Worker, will work with students to ensure that their individual social-emotional-mental needs are being met.
  • The multi-tiered systems of support (MTSS) process will be implemented to address the needs of students who are struggling academically or who are exhibiting behavioral/emotional problems through tiered levels of interventions and support.
  • NorthStar Academy will incorporate an online student information system. This will enable the school’s administration, teachers, students, and parents to access information quickly and efficiently. Information pertaining to students’ grades, homework assignments, school events, and overall progress will foster communication, family involvement, support, and assist all students in reaching their academic goals.
  • Administrator-Teacher Data Review Meetings will be scheduled throughout the year to review student progress monitoring assessment data and student academic progress in courses to ensure students are mastering state standards and to make academic decisions based on data review.

Student data chats will be conducted three times per year to review each student’s current level of mastery on achieving state standards. Core area teachers will meet with each student to review the student’s performance on progress monitoring assessments to measure progress in mastering Language Arts Florida Standards (LAFS), Mathematics Florida Standards (MAFS), and Next Generation Sunshine State Standards, and to set target goals for achievement.
