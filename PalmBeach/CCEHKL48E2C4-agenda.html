PIR1 Boundary Study 05C-3 - Blue Lake Elementary - Adoption
Meeting
Apr 13, 2022 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Commencing the 2022-2023 School Year: I recommend the Board approve the adoption of the attached Boundary Study 05C-3, establishing the attendance zone for Blue Lake Elementary School and shifting the student attendance boundaries for two (2) Elementary Schools – Calusa and J.C. Mitchell and two (2) K-8 Schools – Addison Mizner School and Verde K-8, with current 4th grade students attending the sending schools having the option to stay for 5th grade, and currently enrolled kindergarten through 3rd grade siblings of 4th grade students having the option to stay for the remainder of their elementary years. In addition, incoming SY 2022/23 kindergarten siblings of current 4th grade students will have the option to stay for the remainder of their elementary years. Siblings will be reviewed and processed through the Choice and Career Options Department (reassignments). For students whose attendance zones are impacted by this boundary study, but who are allowed and opt to stay at their current school, transportation will be the responsibility of the parent.

DESCRIPTION:

(This Policy was presented to the Board at a workshop on 3/9/2022 and approved for development on 3/9/2022)

 

Commencing for the 2022-2023 school year: Study 05C-3 establishes the attendance zone for Blue Lake Elementary School and changes the student attendance boundaries for two (2) Elementary Schools – Calusa and J.C. Mitchell – and the elementary attendance boundaries for two (2) K-8 schools - Addison Mizner and Verde.

Boundary Study 05C-3 – (Calusa Elementary to Blue Lake Elementary, Verde K-8 to Blue Lake Elementary, Verde K-8 to Addison Mizner School, Addison Mizner School to J.C. Mitchell Elementary, and J.C. Mitchell Elementary to Blue Lake Elementary)

  • This study creates a compact attendance zone for Blue Lake Elementary School.
  • Provides immediate enrollment relief for Calusa Elementary, Addison Mizner School, and Verde K-8, and provides some additional enrollment for J.C. Mitchell Elementary 
  • The study better balances enrollment at the five schools; decreasing enrollment at Calusa Elementary, Addison Mizner School, and Verde K-8 while providing enrollment for new Blue Lake Elementary and increasing enrollment at J.C. Mitchell Elementary.
  • The study specified that current 4th grade students and their current K-3 grade siblings would be permitted to opt-in to remain at their current SY2021-22 schools.
  • The criteria in Policy 5.01 allowing for siblings to remain were considered.
  • Per School Board Policy 5.01, current 4th graders who opt to remain are not automatically entitled to District transportation; for other students in the change area who are provided the option to remain, transportation is not provided. Based on past history, transportation is not typically provided for any students who opt to remain.
  • A community input meeting was held on February 10, 2022, about sixty members of the community addressed the ABC with over half speaking in favor of the study.
  • On February 10, 2022, the ABC voted 8-1 to recommend “Boundary Study 05C-3” to the Superintendent.
  • The ABC also voted 8-1 for consideration of a full phase-in (grades KG-4th) for Verde SACs 333A & 333B and Addison Mizner SAC 335, as these would be the students proposed to move from a K-8 to a K-5 school.

 

On March 9, 2022, the School Board and the Advisory Boundary Committee held a public workshop relating to Boundary Study 05C3 – Blue Lake Elementary.

 

At the March 9, 2022 School Board Special Meeting, Boundary Study 05C-3 – Blue Lake Elementary was approved for development by the School Board.

 

 

The boundary study proposal and Supporting Documentation, including the study, maps and data, are provided as attachments. For additional information regarding this cycle's Advisory Boundary Committee meetings and associated materials, including video recordings of the meetings, please visit the District's BoardDocs LT site:

  • 12/09/2021 ABC Meeting
  • 01/13/2022 Community Input & ABC Meeting
  • 01/27/2022 ABC Meeting
  • 02/10/2022 Community Input & ABC Meeting

Meeting
Apr 13, 2022 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in Board Room
Category

Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the Florida Department of Law Enforcement (FDLE) grant contribution for the implementation of the FIBRS new incident-based crime data reporting system; and authorize the Superintendent and/or designee to sign all grant documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District Police Department has been awarded $59,208.88 by the Florida Department of Law Enforcement (FDLE) as a result of a grant contribution.  This contribution will provide partial funding for overtime coasts for Department employees to train on the Florida Incident Based Reporting System (FIBRS) Implementation for transitioning to incident-based crime data reporting.

 

The Federal Bureau of Investigations (FBI) launched a new crime reporting system called National Incident Based 'Reporting System (NIBRS). At the same time, FDLE launched FIBRS. NIBRS/FIBRS reporting includes more detailed crime reporting than the previous reporting system.  Transition to NIBRS/FIBRS is a top priority because implementation will improve the reliability, accuracy, and accountability of national and state crime statistics.  Federal grants will now require this more detailed data.
