POL-A1 Policy 5.3261 Emergency Protocols and Prevention Strategies for Students Due to COVID-19

 

Good Cause Statement:

This action is necessary due to the imminent danger to the public health, safety and welfare posed by the spread of COVID-19 and to implement protocols outlined in the Florida Department of Health’s Emergency Rule 64DER21-15 “Protocols for Controlling COVID-19 in School Settings” which was promulgated by the Florida Department of Health on September 22, 2021. The Board is taking only the action necessary to protect the public interest due to the imminent risk to the public health, safety and/or welfare posed by the spread of the Covid-19 pandemic. This immediate danger requires emergency action and the procedure for adoption for this emergency rule is fair under the circumstances for the reasons stated herein.

DS3 Integrated Literacy/Civic and Adult General Education Consolidated Grant

Category

Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Consolidated Adult Education Grant from the Florida Department of Education, effective July 1, 2021 through June 30, 2022.

DESCRIPTION:

The Consolidated Adult Education Grant FY22 includes two grants;

 

1. The Adult Education and Family Literacy Integrated English Literacy/Civic Education Grant (IELCE) is a grant in the amount of $661,731. The program performance dates are July 1, 2021 to June 30, 2022.

 

  • The priorities of this grant are to prepare adults who are English Language Learners (ELL) for employment and places adults in unsubsidized employment, within high demand industries and occupations that lead to economic self-sufficiency; and to integrate our adult education program with the local workforce development system and its’ functions to carry out the activities of the program.

 

2. The Adult Education and Family Literacy-Adult General Education Grant (AGE) is a grant in the amount of $2,397,447. The program performance dates are July 1, 2021 to June 30, 2022.

 

  • The priorities of this grant are to assist adults to become literate, to obtain knowledge and skills necessary to become full partners in the educational development of their children, assist adults attending a secondary school diploma or equivalent, and assist immigrants to improve their reading, writing, and speaking skills.

 

Adult Education programs are provided at 30 adult and community schools, four adult education centers, one adult virtual academy, and eight satellite programs located throughout Palm Beach County. These programs are offered at various times throughout the day and evening in order to accommodate student needs and schedules.

I recommend the Board approve the attached list of Out-of-Field/Out-of-Compliance Teachers for Survey 2 for the FY22 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject-matter that is outside the field in which they are certified.  Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject-matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field in the following areas: Gifted, Reading, and ASD. Those new hires who are offered jobs as Out-of-Field, and are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements prior to the end of May.  If a teacher does not satisfy the coursework requirement then they may be terminated for not meeting the hiring requirements. 

 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate.  Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary, or ESE.  Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements. This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification. It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track.  Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014, include the following statement, “Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment.”
