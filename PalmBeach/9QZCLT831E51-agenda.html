I recommend the School Board approve the contract with the Palm Beach County Literacy Coalition effective October 1, 2014 through June 30, 2015; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and amendments related to the agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

The objective of the Literacy Coalition Contract is to provide the Adult Education students in Palm Beach County an opportunity to get involved in a county-wide event that emphasizes writing skills, student collaboration, and recognition of student achievements. Winners in this annual event are given an opportunity to read their essay aloud to an audience of their peers as well as become qualified for consideration to represent the County at state-wide events. The Adult and Community Education sites work with students on their writing to prepare for the event and to help them fine-tune their submission. Student work is published and distributed locally. This is an inspirational activity for our Adult Education students as it provides an opportunity for them to tell their stories.

I recommend the School Board approve the School Calendar Committee's proposed 2015-2016 School Calendar and also approve the attached "Provisional" 2015-2016 School Calendar to replace the Committee's proposed Calendar should State law be amended during the upcoming Florida Legislative Session in such a way that would allow Districts to begin school as early as August 17.

DESCRIPTION

The 25-member School Calendar Committee met on October 30, 2014 and again on November 13, 2014, and having a quorum, took unanimous action to recommend the School Calendar Committee's 2015-2016 School Calendar, but not before investigating every reasonable way of completing the first semester prior to winter break.

 

With the current law that prohibits schools from opening for students any earlier than two weeks before Labor Day, and with Labor Day in 2015 falling on Monday, September 7, it is clear that unless the law is amended the District cannot begin school for students next year any earlier than Monday, August 24, 2015.  This late start does not allow the first semester to be completed prior to winter break because a semester course must have a minimum of 67.5 clock hours of instruction and this means there must be a minimum of 81 student attendance days in the first semester.

 

As a possible alternative, the attached "Provisional" 2015-2016 School Calendar is being recommended to replace the Committee's Calendar should the Florida Legislature, during its upcoming session, amend the law to allow schools to start as early as August 17.

 

Here is a comparison of these calendars for 2015-2016:

 

Calendar Committee's Recommendation:                                                     Provisional School Calendar

-Has an 86 day first semester ending Jan 15, 2016 (After Winter Break)         -Has an 82 day first semester ending Dec 18, 2015 (Before Winter Break)

-Has a 94 day second semester ending June 9, 2016                                    -Has a 98 day second semester ending June 2, 2016

-Schools are closed as a holiday on Veterans Day                                        -Schools are closed for students, but teachers have a Teacher Work Day on

                                                                                                                Veterans Day

 

Both Calendars:

 

-Have teachers returning four week days before students and both Calendars avoid a "pay gap" for teachers.

-Provide for alternating Teacher Work Days/In-Service Days at the end of each secondary and elementary grading period.

-Have the traditional three-day Thanksgiving break, a two week Winter Break and a five-day Spring Break.

-Contain the traditional holidays with the District closed on Independence Day, Labor Day, Dr. Martin Luther King's Birthday, and Memorial Day.  In addition, schools are  closed for students and teachers on two Fall Holidays (September 14 and 23).

-Provide for the continuation of a "floating holiday" for 12-month employees that can be used on any non-student attendance day after adequate notice is given and approval is  granted.

I recommend the School Board approve the District's Florida Educational Equity Act 2013-2014 Annual Update for submission to the Florida Department of Education.

DESCRIPTION:

The Florida Educational Equity Act, Florida Statutes, Section 1000.05, became law in June 1984 and the State Board of Education adopted the implementing rules in February 1985.

 

Each year the Commissioner of Education designates the areas of concentration under the requirements of the Educational Equity Act.

 

The 2013-2014 Plan Update is the submission of the annual compliance report used by the State to review the success of methods and strategies to reduce gaps in student performance and to increase participation of underrepresented groups by race/ethnicity and gender in upper level courses.

 

The attached data addendum (AP and AICE enrollment by student count FY12-FY15) is being provided to demonstrate the increase in overall student participation in accelerated coursework opportunities.   The District has significantly increased participation in Advanced International Certificate of Education (AICE) courses, which provides students with access to an academically rigorous, internationally used curriculum.  AICE not only prepares students to get into a university with up to 30 hours of college credit, but it also provides them with the skills required to be successful once there.
