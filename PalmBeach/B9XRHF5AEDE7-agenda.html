CI-1 Cooperative Agreement with The Drug Abuse Treatment Association, Inc. Kelly Center
Meeting
Apr 17, 2019 - 2. BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

This is a renewal of a cooperative agreement between the (DATA) Kelly Center and the School District. This program provides educational services for up to (24) twenty-four substance abuse, disciplinary and truant male and female youths between the ages of 13 and 17 whose families have voluntarily enrolled them in the program.

  1. The goal of the program is to develop the social skills and academic knowledge to ensure a successful transition to their home school after completion of the program. Length of the program is ninety (90) days and is residential. Students are evaluated through diagnostic reading and math assessments.
     
  2. The (DATA) Kelly Center provides intensive counseling and behavior management in a therapeutic setting with student and family counseling and social services after the student has completed the program.
     
  3. The (DATA) Kelly Center provides room and board and all other operational expenses incurred by the program. The school district is responsible for the instructional staff and materials needed to provide the educational program.

I recommend the Board approve the new course entitled Introduction to Personal Financial Literacy at the middle school level, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This would be a new elective course to be offered at the middle school level in Florida.  Shortly after passage of the CAPE Act in 2013, the FLDOE created a new Financial Literacy Strand to be added to existing Next Generation Sunshine State Standards for Social Studies. These standards were written for the elementary (4th grade standards), middle (8th grade standards), and high school (9th-12th) levels. There is both a core course (Economics with Financial Literacy – 2102335 / 2102345) and an elective course (Personal Financial Literacy – 2102372 / 2102374) at the high school level that has been developed to cover the Financial Literacy standards for grades 9-12. At the middle school level, the Financial Literacy standards have never been attached to any course, and, because of this, are not covered.

 

The Financial Literacy Strand of the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards for Social Studies are written with the expectation that the knowledge and skills that are expected to be included in any personal financial literacy curriculum are scaffolded. Thus, it is integral that students are exposed to personal financial literacy concepts early in their schooling. It is expected that students have a certain baseline of knowledge before they enter the required Economics with Financial Literacy core course in high school or the Personal Financial Literacy elective course in high school. Currently, there is no way for them to learn these skills, as the middle school version of these standards has not been placed in a course yet. The introduction of this course at the middle school level will solve that problem.
