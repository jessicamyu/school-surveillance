I recommend approval of the Contract resulting from RFP 1304078, an Alternate Source (Piggyback) for Authoring Platform, Test Element Bank, and Assessment Delivery and Reporting System in the amount of $6,277,500 for the period of April 21, 2014 through June 30, 2019 with Performance Matters, LLC; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the award of the Contract resulting from RFP 1304078 for  Authoring Platform, Test Element Bank, and Assessment Delivery and Reporting System, an alternate source contract, with Performance Matters, LLC, for the term April 21, 2014 through June 30, 2019.

 

This purchase was reviewed by the Technology Advisory Committee on April 2, 2014 and received unanimous approval.  It was also reviewed and approved by the Technology Clearing House Committee on April 1, 2014 and the Superintendents Technology Committee on March 31, 2014. 

 

This contract will provide a “Hosted” Test Authoring Platform for Collaborative Online Assessment Development (COAD), a Comprehensive Test Element Bank, and an Assessment Delivery and Reporting Platform. The vendor will implement, and host a dynamic Collaborative Online Assessment Development (COAD) Authoring Platform, Comprehensive Test Element Bank, and Assessment Delivery and Reporting Platform to be used by the District.

 

Beginning in FY2015, Florida will transition to new standards and assessments for reading and mathematics and maintain statewide assessments in science and social studies.  In addition, Florida Senate Bill 736 requires high-stakes end of course assessments for non-FCAT/EOC courses.  This will require a platform capable of creating and administering a wide range of assessment types from a variety of sources to a variety of students as well as managing and displaying the resulting data.  This system will integrate and manage the following:

  • District developed assessments
  • Teacher created assessments
  • 3rd party item bank(s) aligned to Florida Standards
  • State, district and consortia developed items and assessments
  • State, district and consortia developed high-stakes exams for non-FCAT/EOC courses

The goal of Research, Evaluation and Assessment is to acquire and implement an assessment and reporting system that can support creating, assembling, managing, administering, scoring, and reporting the resulting data to support the following goals:

  • Consolidate assessment and reporting solutions for efficiency and cost reduction
  • Provide access to a single assessment and reporting system to continuously monitor and inform teachers and students of what should occur next in the teaching and learning process
  • Provide access to assessments and student performance data using mobile devices across multiple browsers to allow greater convenience and flexibility for teachers and administrators
  • Provide access to intuitive color coded reports to reveal individual and group trends
  • Provide access to a high-quality item bank for District and teacher created assessments
  • Provide a collaborative online assessment development platform for the development of local assessments to comply with statutory requirements
  • Provide a single consolidated assessment content, delivery and reporting platform that will host secure and non-secure items and assessments from a variety of sources

 

Real-time, Web-based Online Assessment System

  • Create and administer assessments
  • Administer assessments online or via plain paper scanning

Integrated Item Bank

  • State, district, consortia developed content
  • Consortia developed content
  • 3rd party content 

COAD (collaborative online assessment development)

  • Item Workflow (authoring, reviewing, revising, approving)
  • Blueprint Construction

Palm Beach County School District is a recognized leader and prides itself as an organization with a data-driven culture, which highly values using data to drive performance.  The Educational Data Warehouse has brought about a cultural transformation, whereby educators now make fact-based decisions to guide educational programming and set measurable performance objectives.  This platform will provide an opportunity for the District to build on this foundation and utilize the next generation of web-based reporting and analytics tools to further empower users and provide an opportunity for systems integration and cost savings.

 

Online Reporting and Analytics

  • Performance Dashboards and Reports
  • Updated reporting aligned to Florida statewide assessments and accountability
  • Leverage economy of scale to realize cost savings
  • Provide immediate, real-time reporting of assessment results
  • Provide greater flexibility to users
  • Enable custom report development
  • Transactional system for data input/output
  • 30 performance dashboards
  • Leading and lagging indicator reports    
  • Filter on the fly for greater flexibility and efficiency
  • Ability to create and share custom reports

COM9 Parkinson’s Awareness Month
Meeting
Apr 16, 2014 - 2. Regular Board Meeting at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
9. Presentations
Type
Presentation

DESCRIPTION:

Parkinson’s disease is the second most common neurodegenerative condition after Alzheimer’s, with which it shares a target population over age 60.  As many as one million Americans live with Parkinson’s disease and it is estimated that another four to six million worldwide suffer from the condition. In the United States, 60,000 new cases are diagnosed each year.

 

Parkinson’s disease causes the deterioration of neurons in the brain that produce dopamine, an essential neurotransmitter that controls smooth, coordinated muscle function. The typical symptoms of Parkinson’s disease – tremor, rigidity, postural instability, and speech and swallowing dysfunction – appear when approximately 80% of the dopamine-producing cells are damaged. Diagnosis can be difficult, because there is no standard confirming test other than clinical examination.  While there have been considerable advances in treatment and care, the cause is still unknown, and there is no cure.

DESCRIPTION:

The Florida Department of Education requires that the Gifted Endorsement Program be renewed on a five year cycle. This program was renewed in 1994, 1999, 2004, and 2009.  The evaluation process includes participant performance, component effectiveness and program effectiveness.  All three of these components are used to determine the program’s direction and refinement. Evaluation measures include participant mastery of content, participant feedback, participant implementation of assignments, and a summative evaluation of each of the five courses that compose the Gifted Endorsement.

 

The Gifted Endorsement Program serves a large number of teachers by providing the opportunity to add Gifted Endorsement to their teaching certificates without the cost incurred by taking college graduate level course at universities or online. It also creates the opportunity for Palm Beach teachers to collaborate and exchange lesson plans and ideas that are focused on the District and its goals, vision and mission.

 

There are 850 teachers in the Palm Beach School District schools who have added Gifted Endorsement to their teaching certificates through the Gifted Endorsement program. Presently, 69 teachers are teaching gifted students out-of-field, participating in the program and a total of over 350 teachers who are working on obtaining their Gifted Endorsement through the District program. The Gifted Endorsement Program benefits both the District and the participants, while providing outstanding practices that benefit gifted students. The strategies and skills necessary for teaching gifted students are discussed, presented and explored in the five Gifted Endorsement courses.

 

The value of the Gifted Endorsement Program is well recognized and received by Principals and teachers throughout the District. Many of the teachers depend on the courses to fulfill their out-of-field agreements and certificate renewals. Evaluations for the courses consistently rank the classes 95%-100% in value compared to other district offered courses. Although the focus of the classes are strategies that address the characteristics and needs of Gifted students, these courses have proven themselves to be valuable for all teachers, especially with the movement to the Common Core State Standards.

DESCRIPTION:

In order to qualify for reimbursement in a federally declared flood disaster, the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA), requires that the School District purchase insurance from the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP) for all buildings in Flood Zones A and V.

 

The School District now has 30 locations with 118 buildings valued in excess of $769 million  in Florida Zones A and V.  FEMA requires the District to purchase one policy per building, up to an insured limited of $500,000.  We will have a $50,000 deductible for each building and another $50,000 deductible for contents.  The total building coverage will be $61.2 million and contents coverage of $42.7 million.  If a federal declaration of disaster applies, FEMA reimbursement would apply after policy limits are exhausted.

 

Premiums are increasing an average of 10% this year.  Part of the 10% overall increase, are increases on 32 buildings on 6 campuses, due to the expiration of previously grandfathered rates by the Biggert Waters Flood Insurance Reform Act of 2012.  We have acquired elevation certificates for these buildings in order to provide the best available rates at this time.

 

The total premium for the flood insurance is not-to-exceed $270,000, barring any federal rate increase prior to April 30, 2014.  This is a potential increase of $30,000 over last year's premium.  Premium and commission amounts are set by the federal government.  The broker, Plastridge Insurance Agency,  is placing this flood insurance with the Travelers' Insurance Company.

  2. The School Board also has interest in utilizing certain athletic and non-athletic facilities owned by the Town of Jupiter.
  3. The parties are interested in making improvements to the JCHS Stadium Field, including but not limited to, the addition of synthetic turf.  This will allow for the expanded use of the field.
  4. The preliminary project budget amount of $800,000.00 will be paid by the Town of Jupiter and JCHS Athletic Boosters (50% by each party).
  5. The JCHS Stadium Field improvements are contingent upon the JCHS Athletic Boosters raising $400,000.00 prior to the commencement of the project.

  8. Use agreements by third parties not covered by the Agreement will require that the user pay the standard lease rate, all personnel costs plus either an upfront capital contribution that will be used to fund the School's share of the turf project or a renewal/replacement payment that will be used to fund the future replacement of the playing surface.
  9. The standard 65% school/35%District split of the standard lease rate will be used for non-profit users and the 80% school/20% District split will be used for for-profit users.
  10. The Agreement requires JCHS to set aside the renewal/replacement revenue generated through use agreements and sponsorships associated with the JCHS  Stadium for repairs and replacement of the synthetic turf.
  11. The Superintendent is required to approve all agreements with a term of one year or more.
  12. The term of this Agreement shall be for a period of one (1) year and shall be automatically renewed up to nine (9) additional consecutive one (1) year terms, unless either party provides a written notice of intent to terminate the agreement to the other party ninety (90) days in advance.
  13. Contract(s) to construct the JCHS Stadium Field improvements will be approved consistent with applicable Board Policy.
  14. The Town Council of Jupiter approved the Interlocal Agreement at its March 18, 2014 meeting.
