Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7,000 high school students.

The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

I recommend the Board approve Mary Bradley’s three-day suspension without pay effective Tuesday, April 14, 2020, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S., sec. 120.57, F.S. and the SEIU/FPSU Collective Bargaining Agreement (Regular), Article 17, Section 8.

DESCRIPTION:

On October 21, 2019, Transportation Staff at the North Facility notified the Department of Employee and Labor Relations regarding School Bus Driver, Mary Bradley utilizing a cellular or other wireless communication device (WCD) while operating a District school bus. A review of the bus surveillance video depicts Ms. Bradley utilizing a cellular while operating a District School Bus (with and without students on board), and while the bus was being fueled. Additionally, Ms. Bradley is seen dropping off a student at Riviera Beach Preparatory Academy, an unauthorized stop on her route.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following: School Board Policies 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 3.02(4), (4)(a), (4)(b), (4)(j) and (5)(a)(viii), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment with the District; 3.21(3)(a), (3)(f) and (3)(l), Safe Operation of District School Buses; 3.29(10)(g)(vi), Acceptable Use of Technology by Employees; 6A-3.0171(3)(f) and (3)(v), F.A.C., Responsibilities of School Districts for Student Transportation; School Bus Drivers and Bus Attendants Handbook, Chapter One-Operations Section 2(A), 2(D), 3(A) and (4)(D).

 

There is “just cause” to support Mary Bradley’s three-day suspension without pay.

 

On February 25, 2020, the Superintendent’s “Notice of Three-Day Suspension Without Pay” was hand-delivered to Mary Bradley, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend the Board approve the attached Tentative Agreements with SEIU/FPSU (Regular, Supervisory and Paraprofessional II/ECPSs) effective January 1, 2020.

DESCRIPTION:

Tentative Agreements have been reached on the 2020 Modifications to the 2019-2021 Successor Collective Bargaining Agreements (Regular, Supervisory and Paraprofessional II/ECPSs) between the School Board of Palm Beach County and the Service Employees International Union/Florida Public Services Union (SEIU/FPSU). These Tentative Agreement reflect the following negotiated changes to the Collective Bargaining Agreements:

● A three percent (3%) salary increase effective January 1, 2020, is awarded to each SEIU/FPSU bargaining unit employee who is an employee of the District on the date the School Board approves these Agreements;
 

● Effective January 1, 2020, the minimums of all salary levels are increased by one percent (1%) and the maximum salaries are increased by two percent (2%).
