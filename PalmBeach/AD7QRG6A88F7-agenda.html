Recommended Action
I recommend the Board adopt the FY2017 Five Year Capital Plan and the FY2017 Tentative District Educational Facilities Plan.

DESCRIPTION:

Adopt the FY2017 Capital Plan and the FY2017 Tentative District Educational Facilities Plan.

  • Each year, the School Board is required to adopt the Tentative District Educational Facilities Plan (Work Plan) prior to the adoption of the budget.
  • The Tentative District Education Facilities Plan, or "Work Plan" contains the same financial data as the Five Year Capital Plan, but in a format created by the Florida Department of Education.
  • The Five Year Capital Plan, or "Capital Plan" has been expanded from five years to eleven years to include the proposed sales tax that is on the November 8, 2016 ballot.  The first year of the plan is the FY2017 Capital Budget that will be approved with the FY 2017 budget later today.
  • As required by law, the documents submitted for adoption are balanced.  
  • A second version of the Capital Plan, without the sales tax and long term financing, as well as a summary of the changes that will take place if the sales tax is not approved is also attached.
  • Since the Board last viewed the capital plan on August 17th, the plan has been updated to include balances remaining from FY 2016.  Revenue projections have been updated based on the latest information available and minor adjustments were made to reflect the FY2017 budget.  Other significant changes are
    • The new elementary school, originally referenced Minto West/Acreage Area ES (15-A) is now called as Western Communities ES (15-A).
    • A new high school, Western Communities HS (16-AAA) has been added to the plan.  Over the next 10 to 25 years, more than 13,000 new residential units are expected to be built in our western communities. Based on phasing information that staff recently received from developers, the new high school should be included in the latter years of the Capital Plan and the Sales Tax Project List.  The District already owns the high school site which is north of the Minto property. 
    • COP revenues have been increased to fund school construction projects.
    • COP lease payments have been increased to based on the increased borrowing for school construction projects.
    • $1 million was added for hurricane preparation.
  • The projects planned for FY2017 through FY2027 (years 2 through 11) can and will be amended as we work through the budget process for those years.  Any future changes that impact the Sales Tax Project List will be reviewed by the Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee (ISSOC) and presented to the School Board for approval.

 

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Financial Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

Leanne Evans, Treasurer (leanne.evans@palmbeachschools.org)

The financial impact for FY 2017 is $430,289,781.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board authorize the General Counsel to file suit and take all appropriate action, including hiring experts, and the authority to settle the matter subject to the approval of the Superintendent, against TJEM, Inc. d/b/a/ CheckCare in the 15th Judicial Circuit in and for Palm Beach County, Florida; and authorize the Chairman, Superintendent and General Counsel to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On February 18, 2015 the Board approved a Membership Agreement with TJEM, Inc. d/b/a/ CheckCare for the collection of funds from returned checks.  Pursuant to this Agreement, all returned checks were sent directly to CheckCare for collection and it agreed to reimburse the District 100% of the face value for all returned checks up to $1,500, regardless of whether CheckCare collected on the returned checks. The Agreement further provided that CheckCare would reimburse the District for checks in excess of $1,500 based on actual collection.

 

In late Fall 2015, Treasury Department staff became aware that CheckCare had been slow in sending its payments and had failed to submit its current reimbursement payment to the District.  As a result, staff contacted CheckCare requesting immediate payment for the delinquent reimbursement. CheckCare promised to rectify the situation and schools did receive some payments, but as time went on, CheckCare became less and less responsive to staff and payments were received less often.  Since CheckCare did not respond to staffs’ calls and emails seeking payment, on March 29, 2016 the District provided CheckCare with a notice of default with a demand for payment.  Following receipt of the March 29, 2016 default letter, CheckCare again began making regular payments however in June, payments began to become more sporadic and some of the checks were returned for insufficient funds. A final demand letter was sent to CheckCare on August 26, 2016. To date, CheckCare has failed to respond or make the demanded payment.  The District believes that CheckCare owes approximately $196,000.

 

The suit will seek to force TJEM, Inc. d/b/a/ CheckCare to comply with the terms and conditions of its Agreement by paying the District approximately $196,000 and allowable fees and costs.
