PS1 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Jonathan Prince
Meeting
Jul 8, 2009 - Spec Mtg and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

On or about June 11, 2009, Dr. Jonathan Prince, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation regarding allegations of misuse of District funds, failure to properly control District funds, misconduct and failure to exercise best professional judgment.  Specifically, these allegations included the misuse of Palm Beach Gardens' High School purchasing card.  Dr. Jonathan Prince used the school's purchasing card to make unauthorized purchases.  He has reimbursed $3867.29.  These allegations include potential violations of School Board Policies, Procedures, Directives and the Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida.  These potential violations include:  6B-1.001, (1), 6B-1.006, 4(c), 6B-1.006, 5(a) and 6B-4.009, Florida Administrative Code, The Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida.  Furthermore, the allegations include violations of School Board Policies 1.014, Responsibilities of School Principals, 0.02, Commitment to the Public, Principle II and 0.03, Commitment to the Profession, Principle III, 6.01, Authorized Travel Expense Reimbursement and 6.14, Purchasing and Purchasing Card Procedures.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.



Based upon substantial information gathered through out the investigation, there is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations.

Recommendation:

I recommend that  the School Board approve Dr. Jonathan Prince Twenty (20) Day suspension without pay for twenty (20) workdays effective July 27, 2009.  In addition to the Twenty (20) Day suspension, I recommend to the Board that Dr. Jonathan Prince be demoted from his current position as principal to that of assistant principal, only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

Jeffrey J. Hernandez; James P. Kelly; Angelette Green

There is not financial impact to the School District
