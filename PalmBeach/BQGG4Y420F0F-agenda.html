Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with Comcast Cable Communications Management, LLC, not to exceed $900,000 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.
DESCRIPTION:
 
Per Board Policy 6.14.5c, a Direct Negotiation was entered into with Comcast Cable Communications Management, LLC to provide home Internet access to District enrolled students using Comcast Internet Essentials Sponsored Services. The sponsored services agreement will allow the District to pay the cost of qualifying students, identified by the District, for a term of six months, which may be renewed if needed and with Board approval. Each eligible student/family must complete the application process to qualify for the Internet Essentials program and meet the income requirement. The Internet Essentials program provides 25Mbps. The Internet Essentials program is available to all qualified low-income households in Comcast’s service area for $9.95 per month. Any families who have not previously been enrolled in the Internet Essentials program will qualify for two months of free service upon confirmation of eligibility, thus reducing the amount billed to the District for those two months.
 
The term of the Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date of the Agreement and continue for a period of six months.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Addendum to the Master Software Agreement with the KEV Group, Inc. and authorize the Superintendent, Board Chair, and Treasurer to finalize and sign all necessary documents.
DESCRIPTION:
Approve the Addendum to the Master Software Agreement with KEV Group, Inc. for the purchase of School Cash Suite Software for the period of August 1, 2020 through July 31, 2025.
 
This web-based system provides the accounting solution for schools to manage internal accounts and provides the online payment portal used to collect credit card payments for lunches, student activities and other items. During FY19, more than $34 million of credit card payments were processed through the system.
 
Parents also have access to view their payment history, including meal details, online and payments can be made from any location, 24 hours a day, seven days a week during the school year, using Master Card, VISA, or debit card.
 
Additional functionality, such as fraud prevention, automation of monies collected forms, single sign on, teacher access for recording receipt of payments have been added. The cost of the software is $750 per school or $129,750 for FY 21. The cost will increase to $132,000 as the three new schools open over the course of this contract.
 
During FY19, more than $34 million of credit card payments were processed through the system while $66 million of payments were paid via cash or check. Enhancements added with this contract allow the District to recommend all payments be made through the online portal. This will reduce cash processing, which increases transparency and accountability. Reducing cash handling is also important as we work to lessen exposure to COVID-19.
 
The contract consolidates the Mastercard/Visa processing, currently provided by Bank of America Merchant Services, into this system. This will allow every school to utilize the system for online payments.
 
Transaction processing fees for the first year will be 3.5% plus $0.10 per transaction for the first year. Beginning in year two, processing fees will be 3.75% plus $0.10 per transaction. A rebate will be generated annually, based on the amount of credit card payments, and applied towards the software cost in the following year.
 
Transaction fees will be included in the price of each item available for sale from schools and departments. If all payments made in FY 2019 ($85 million) had been paid via credit card, the estimated fee assessed to parents/guardians would have been $3.4 million.
 
Transaction fees associated with meal payments will be billed monthly and paid by School Food Service. If all payments made in FY 2019 ($14.6 million) had been paid via credit card, the estimated fee paid by School Food Service would have been $0.6 million

FINANCIAL IMPACT:
The annual financial impact to the District operating budget will not exceed $140,000 which includes the software cost of $132,000 and $8,000 for training, if needed. Transaction fees associated with meal payments will not exceed $600,000. Transaction fees associated with items sold by schools and departments will be imbedded in the cost of the items sold and are estimated not to exceed $3.4 million.
