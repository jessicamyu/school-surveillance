Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(6)(g), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the district and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.



Montessori of Early Enrichment is in compliance with the current charter.



The Model Charter for Montessori of Early Enrichment was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff.



The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years commencing July 1, 2008 and ending June 30, 2013, except as provided in the charter.  An amendment to extend the charter was granted by the School Board of Palm Beach on June 24, 2008 to cover the period of July 1, 2008 to September 30, 2008.



Montessori of Early Enrichment serves students ages 3-7.  Sixty Percent of the students served are students  with disabilities, 20% English Language Learners (ELL) and 20% Regular Education Students.



The Data Panels attached are the K-2 Reading Running Records Assessment.



A copy of the Model Charter  for Montessori Academy of Early Enrichment is on file in the Board Office for review.

The Governing Board for Bright Futures International and Academy for International Studies submitted the "Articles of Merger" and "Articles of Amendment" to merge both schools to become Bright Futures Academy.  (see Exhibit "A")

The merger will be effective July 1, 2008.  The Data Panels are Adequate Yearly Progress and the School Accountability Report.



Academy for International Studies and Bright Futures International are in compliance with their  current charter.



Bright Futures International serves students grades K-5. Academy for International Studies serves students grades 6-8.  Hence, Bright Futures Academy will serve students K-8.



Data Panels are attached for both schools.

Florida Virtual School Contract for K-8 Virtual School Services for 2008-2009 school year (Revised)
Meeting
Sep 24, 2008 - SM re Charter Schls and K through 8 Virtual Contract
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

The contract provides that Florida Virtual School will deliver a full-time, 180-day instructional program for eligible students in kindergarten through grade 8.  The contractor is to provide instructional and administrative staff and all necessary instructional materials as defined by the Department of Education for a full-time K-8 virtual school. 



The 2008 Legislature adopted HB7067, (the “Statute”) an act relating to virtual instruction programs that authorizes school districts to implement virtual instruction programs through district-operated programs or programs provided by contracted providers approved by the Florida Department of Education.  School districts are required to offer specified virtual instruction programs in the 2009-2010 school year by operating one or more schools but are permitted to offer specified virtual instruction programs in the 2008-2009 school year.



This contract will allow the District to have involvement in a small full-time K-8 project, supporting the development of a district-wide project mandated by the new law in 2009-2010.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the Florida Virtual School contract in the not-to-exceed amount of $59,400 for the FY09 school year and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore, moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



Debra Johnson, Principal of K-12 Virtual School

Financial Impact:

The cost of the contract would not exceed $59,400 to the District.  FTE will be collected by the District on the students enrolled in the virtual school.
