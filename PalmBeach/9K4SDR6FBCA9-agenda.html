This contract is in response to a DOE request that Palm Beach serve as a fiscal agent for the reporting of these students for revenue and subsequent payment to the vendor. This is a one year process since the appropriations/program sunsets at the end of this academic year     (June 2010).



The state-level K-8 Virtual School Program was established as a pilot program by the 2003 Florida Legislature.  The purpose of this virtual school program is to make academic instruction accessible to full-time students in grades K-8 using online and distance learning technology.  Funding for the program has been provided through a line item appropriation in the General Appropriations Act since the time of inception through the 2008/09 fiscal year. The 2009 legislature eliminated the line item funding, and in Line Item 76 of SB2600 (2009 General Appropriations Act) authorized school district to enter into contracts with the two K-8 providers operating under section 1002.415, F.S. to ensure continuity of the educational program for students currently enrolled in the state-level program for the 2009-10 school year.

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1002.33(6)(g), the terms and conditions for the operation of the charter school shall be set forth by the district and the applicant in a written contractual agreement called a charter.



The Model Charter for Learning Excellence Foundation of South Palm Beach, Inc. d/b/a Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus f/k/a Chancellor Charter School at Lantana ("Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus") was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff.  Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus applied for a fifteen (15) year renewal.  Pursuant  to F.S. 1002.33(17)(b)(2), "The 15-year charter renewal that may be granted pursuant to subparagraph 1, shall be granted to a charter school that has received a school grade of "A" or "B" pursuant to s. 1008.34 in 3 of the past 4 years and is not in a state of financial emergency or deficit position as defined by this section."



Staff recommends a ten (10) year renewal contract.  Chancellor has earned school grades of two "A's" and two "B's" in the last four years and meets the academic criteria; however, does not meet the financial criteria for a fifteen (15) year renewal.  Chancellor has met one of the conditions described in Section 218.503(1), Florida Statute- "Determination of Financial Emergency".  The school accumulated deficits during prior years as a result of a start-up and operating cost that were pre-paid by their management company.  A comprehensive repayment of the debt to their management company has been implemented.  The school reported an accumulated net deficit of $208,758 on its statement of net assets and an unrestricted deficit of $595,967 as of June 30, 2008.  Although the school met one of the conditions under Section 218.503(1), the school continues to improve its financial conditions with an increase in net assets of $446,406 for the year ended June 30, 2008, therefore, the financial deficit is not a result of deteriorating financial condition.  The Sponsor and the administration of Imagine School-Chancellor Campus continue to monitor closely the deficit for future operations.



The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of ten (10) years commencing July 1, 2009 and ending June 30, 2019, except as provided in the charter.  An amendment to extend the charter was granted by the School Board of Palm Beach on May 27, 2009, to cover the period of July 1, 2009 to August 30, 2009.



Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus Charter School serves students grades K-8. 



The Data Panel is attached for Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus Charter School.



A copy of the Renewal Charter for Imagine Schools - Chancellor Campus Charter School is on file in the Board Office for review.
