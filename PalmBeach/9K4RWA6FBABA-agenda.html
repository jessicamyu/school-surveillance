I recommend that the School Board authorize the Florida School Boards Association, Inc., and its contracted attorneys, Meyer & Brooks, P.A., or other counsel approved by the District’s Chief Counsel, to include the School Board of Palm Beach County as a plaintiff in litigation and/or a petitioner in a rule challenge and to appeal the State Board of Education’s decision to deny this School District’s application for exclusive authority to sponsor charter schools within this County and challenging Florida Statutes Section 1002.335, which created the Florida Schools of Excellence Commission, State Board of Education Rule 6A-6.0783, and the process for determining exclusive authority; and authorize the Superintendent to appropriate funds for the payment of legal fees and litigation costs associated with such challenges.

Recommendation:



  • During the 2006 Session of the Florida Legislature, House Bill 135 was enacted (now codified as Florida Statutes Section 1002.335), creating the Florida Schools of Excellence Commission, which is an independent, state-level charter school authorizing entity under the supervision of the State Board of Education.



  •   




  • The creation of the Commission raised several constitutional questions as it relates to the power of elected school boards throughout the State of Florida to approve the establishment of, and regulate, charter schools within a school district.


 





  • Accordingly, FSBA contracted with the law firm of Meyer and Brooks, P.A. to bring a constitutional challenge to the establishment of the Florida Schools of Excellence Commission.


 



§         On September 27, 2006 the School Board voted to approve a Resolution authorizing the hiring of this law firm and instituting a lawsuit to challenge the Statute.



 



§         The lawsuit was filed but was voluntarily withdrawn due to a potential ripeness issue.



 



§         On September 27, 2006, as well as February 14, 2007, the School Board voted to approve Resolutions that this School District retain exclusive authority within this County to authorize charter schools.



 



§         Subsequently, the State Board of Education engaged in rulemaking and adopted State Board of Education Rule 6A-6.0783 relating to the process for the State Board of Education to determine if a district school board would retain exclusive authority to sponsor charter schools within its geographical boundaries.



 



§         The school districts provided input as to the content of this rule and this District voiced and submitted its opposition.



 



§         Hearings were held before the State Board of Education on September 18, 2007 and October 16, 2007 to consider the applications of the various school districts to retain exclusivity to authorize charter schools.  The District argued that its application for exclusive authority be approved.



 



§         At the October 16, 2007 meeting the State Board of Education denied this District’s application and also denied the application of almost all of the other school districts seeking exclusive authority, except for three.



 



§         To our knowledge, the State Board of Education has not yet issued a written order denying the application.



 



§         Pursuant to Florida Statutes Section 1002.335(5)(f), the decision of the State Board of education is not subject to the provisions of the Florida Administrative Procedures Act but is considered a final action subject to judicial review by the district court of appeal.

Motion & Voting

During the 2006 Session of the Florida Legislature, House Bill 135 was enacted (now codified as Florida Statutes Section 1002.335), creating the Florida Schools of Excellence Commission, which is an independent, state-level charter school authorizing entity under the supervision of the State Board of Education.



The creation of the Commission raised several constitutional questions as it relates to the power of elected school boards throughout the State of Florida to approve the establishment of, and regulate, charter schools within a school district.


Accordingly, FSBA contracted with the law firm of Meyer and Brooks, P.A. to bring a constitutional challenge to the establishment of the Florida Schools of Excellence Commission.

§ On September 27, 2006 the School Board voted to approve a Resolution authorizing the hiring of this law firm and instituting a lawsuit to challenge the Statute.

§ The lawsuit was filed but was voluntarily withdrawn due to a potential ripeness issue.

§ On September 27, 2006, as well as February 14, 2007, the School Board voted to approve Resolutions that this School District retain exclusive authority within this County to authorize charter schools.

§ Subsequently, the State Board of Education engaged in rulemaking and adopted State Board of Education Rule 6A-6.0783 relating to the process for the State Board of Education to determine if a district school board would retain exclusive authority to sponsor charter schools within its geographical boundaries.

§ The school districts provided input as to the content of this rule and this District voiced and submitted its opposition.

§ Hearings were held before the State Board of Education on September 18, 2007 and October 16, 2007 to consider the applications of the various school districts to retain exclusivity to authorize charter schools. The District argued that its application for exclusive authority be approved.

§ At the October 16, 2007 meeting the State Board of Education denied this District’s application and also denied the application of almost all of the other school districts seeking exclusive authority, except for three.

§ To our knowledge, the State Board of Education has not yet issued a written order denying the application.

§ Pursuant to Florida Statutes Section 1002.335(5)(f), the decision of the State Board of education is not subject to the provisions of the Florida Administrative Procedures Act but is considered a final action subject to judicial review by the district court of appeal.
