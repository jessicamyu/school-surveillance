This grant funds 14 technical assistance specialists and related support/administrative staff who provide on-site technical assistance to child care centers and school-based sites that voluntarily participate in the Network.  School District classrooms offering the Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) program participate in the Network, pending Network capacity.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Master Inservice Plan for FY18.

DESCRIPTION:

State Board of Education Rule 6A-5.071 states that:

  • Each district school board shall develop and maintain a Master Inservice Plan for all district employees based on state adopted standards for high quality professional development as required under Section 1012.98, F.S.
     
  • The Master Inservice Plan shall be updated annually and approved each year by the district school board. By October 1 of each year, a letter verifying that the district school board  has approved the master inservice plan and that the plan meets the criteria pursuant to this rule shall be sent to the Commissioner.
     
  • Each component shall remain in the plan for a period of at least five (5) years.
     
  • Inservice points awarded for successful completion of a component shall be assigned as follows: One (1) inservice point shall be equivalent to one (1) clock hour of participation, or as specified by the master inservice plan based on competency(ies) demonstrated.

The Master Inservice Plan is the vehicle through which instructional personnel earn inservice points toward recertification of their Florida teaching certificate. The Plan must contain all training components leading to certification. Instructional personnel must obtain 120 inservice points during their 5-year validity period. Non-Instructional personnel, affiliated with certain employee groups, use inservice points toward incentive programs and to verify completion of required training(s).

 

The current listing of all active inservice components can be viewed in PeopleSoft eLearning Management Course Catalog.

I recommend the Board approve the attached list of Out-of-Field/Out-of-Compliance Teachers for Survey 2 for FY18 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to Sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. Such assignment typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction. Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience. The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field in the following areas: Gifted, Reading and ASD. Those new hires who are offered jobs as an Interim Out-of-Field, and are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements prior to the end of May. If a teacher does not satisfy the coursework requirement their Interim position ends. Once a teacher completes their Out-of-Field requirements the school is notified and teachers can be moved from Interim Out-of-Field to Regular. Teachers who are placed Out-of-Field in core content areas are eligible to apply for reimbursement on the Subject Area Exam registration fee, after they add the subject area to their certificate.

 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate. Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary or ESE. Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements. This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification. It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track. Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014 include the following statement, “Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment.”
