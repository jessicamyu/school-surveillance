Subject
Personnel Disiciplinary Action - Pamela Dudeck
Meeting
Dec 5, 2007 - SM re Disciplinary Actions
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

On or about August 30, 2007, Pamela Dudeck became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of Conversation of School District funds and/or violation of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff and/or the "Financial and Program Cost Accounting and Reporting for Florida Schools, 2001", incorporated by rule 6A-1.001, District Financial Records of the Florida Administrative Code.



On or about September 5, 2007, Ms. Dudeck, a Treasurer at Atlantic High School , was involved in an audit conducted by a School District Auditor.  It was discovered that checks from a beverage vending service were not documented in the general ledger and monies collected reports but were deposited.  When cash was received the checks were substituted for the cash in deposits.  A Police Department investigation was conducted whereby the treasurer admitted to the theft of $2,286.10.  Criminal charges filed.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Pamela Dudeck's suspension without pay, effective December 6, 2007 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the December 5, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Darron Davis; Kenyetta Haywood

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District

On or about March 14, 2006, Jacqueline Daniels became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of arrest for felony offense and/or violation of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff and/or Section XV, Grounds for Discharge of School Bus Drivers and Bus Attendants of the School Bus Drivers and Bus Attendants Handbook.



On or about May 6, 2006, Ms. Daniels who is a bus driver was arrested by the Riviera Beach Police Department for Aggravated Battery with a Firearm.  She shot her husband at their residence during a domestic dispute.  In May of 2007 she plead guilty to Felony Battery, served a total of 30 days in jail and is on probation for 5 years.

I recommend that the School Board approve Jacqueline Daniel's suspension without pay, effective December 6, 2007 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the December 5, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

On or about September 26, 2007, Rodolfo Morales became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of possession of firearm on school property and/or School Board Policies 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff, and/or 3.26, Possession of Firearm by a School District Employee/Contractor/Visitor on School District Property, in addition to 6B-4.009, Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal of the Florida Administrative Code.



On or about September 26, 2007, Mr. Morales is a custodian at Limestone Creek Elementary School.  He notified Police Department Sergeant William Lammie of his concern of another custodian who possessed a knife which she displayed in a non threatening manner while giving her a ride home after work one night.  Morales advised Sgt. Lammie that he brought his firearm to work and had it in a holster in a back pack located in a vehicle driven by another custodian who drove him to work.  The firearm was taken by Sgt. Lammie.  Morales has a concealed firearms permit.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Rodolfo Morales' suspension without pay, effective December 6, 2007 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the December 5, 2007, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.
