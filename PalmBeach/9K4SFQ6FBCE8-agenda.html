West Boca Raton Community High School Teacher to Kazakhstan.
Meeting
Feb 23, 2011 - Workshop
Category
Workshop Items
Type
Action

Barbara Johnson was selected to participate in the Teaching Excellence & Achievement Program (TEA) & International Leaders in Education Program (ILEP) U.S. Teacher Exchange.



The exchange opportunity is funded by the U.S. Department of State Bureau of Educational & Cultural Affairs & administered by IREX (International Research & Exhanges Board).  It will provide a unique opportunity to build global competence in the U.S. classroom through teacher professional development.



Ms. Johnson is one of six teachers selected from Florida, with a total of 110 teachers selected for this professional development grant opportunity.



Ms. Johnson will travel to the International host school for two weeks.  She will have the opportunity to:





  • Collaborate with host school teachers on best practices in teaching & classroom leadership as well as related curriculum & educational issues in their subject.


  • Interact with the local community through short home stays & meetings with the local press, government officials, & school or parent groups.


  • Visit local school and team teach with the TEA/ILEP International partner at their host school.


  • Meet with representatives of the Fulbright Commission, U.S. Public Affairs Section (PAS), and/or the Regional English Language Officer (RELO) in the host country.


  • Visit cultural sites.

Recommendation:

I reommend that the School Board approve the West Boca Raton Community High School Social Studies to travel to Kazakhstan.

Contact:

Judith Klinek, Chief Academic Officer



Nora Rosensweig, South Area Superintendent



Mark Stenner, Principal, West Boca Raton Community High School          

Financial Impact:

There is no cost to the district.
