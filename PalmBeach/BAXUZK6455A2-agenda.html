Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Consultant Agreement with National Council for Behavioral Health for the period of May 15, 2019, through June 30, 2019; and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the consultant agreement with National Council for Behavioral Health to provide Train-the-trainer in Youth Mental Health First Aid services. Youth Mental Health First Aid is designed to teach how to help an adolescent (age 12-18) who is experiencing a mental health or addictions challenge or is in crisis. The course introduces common mental health challenges for youth, reviews typical adolescent development, and teaches a 5-step action plan for how to help young people in both crisis and non-crisis situations. All current and new instructors will also be trained in a new child supplement, inclusive of signs and symptoms for ages 5-12.

 

The School District of Palm Beach County began providing Youth Mental Health First Aid through a Project Aware grant in 2015, and has continued expanding upon trained staff through Youth Mental Health Awareness Training Grant. This school year the District has increased the number of staff certified instructors through a previous YMHAT training with National Council for Behavioral Health and because of its success would like to increase by up to fifteen additional certified instructors.

 

The School Board previously approved the Youth Mental Health Awareness Training Grant on June 20, 2018 (TL15). The Bureau of Exceptional Education and Student Services from DOE awarded the Youth Mental Health Awareness Training Discretionary Project Grant to SEDNET/ Exceptional Student Education Department. Grant funds are to facilitate training in mental health awareness, including training certified Youth Mental Health First Aid instructors.

 

This purchase is considered a Professional Service as defined in Section 1010.04(4)(a)FS and Board Policy 6.14 and therefore is exempt from competition requirements based on the specialized qualifications required to perform these services.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the contracts with the providers of Certified Athletic Trainers.

 

The Athletic Director and/or Principal in individual high schools have selected a company to provide Certified Athletic Trainers, certified by the National Athletic Trainers Association (N.A.T.A.) and licensed by the State of Florida, to consult and/or develop a program for sport specific fitness and conditioning for boys’ and girls’ sports, as requested by the principal of the public high school. The providers of the Certified Athletic Trainers are:

 

  • Action Physical Therapy, LLC
  • Gold Coast Physical Therapy Associates, LLC DBA Fyzical Therapy & Balance Centers

 

The District may compensate the provider of each Certified Athletic Trainer up to $22,500 per high school based upon 1,000 hours of service of which a minimum of 250 hours will be allocated to each of the three sports seasons (fall, winter and spring); schools having fewer sports and teams will compensate their provider of Certified Athletic Trainers based upon the hours needed at a rate of $22.50 per hour, not to exceed $15,000 during the contract period.

 

The Certified Athletic Trainers will work with athletes and coaches based upon individual needs at each school. Practice, game and event coverage will be coordinated and schedules agreed upon by both parties. Professional liability and comprehensive general liability insurance are required from each of the providers of Certified Athletic Trainers as indicated in the contract.

 

Due to the number of schools that have selected Gold Coast Physical Therapy Associates, LLC DBA Fyzical Therapy & Balance Centers to provide Certified Athletic Trainers for their schools, Gold Coast Physical Therapy Associates, LLC DBA Fyzical Therapy & Balance Centers will exceed $250,000 in fiscal year 19-20.

FMT1 Sales Tax Financing

May 8, 2019 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room

Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve a Resolution for a revolving line of credit with PNC Bank with a not to exceed par of $200 million for the sales tax program, and authorize the Board Chair and Superintendent to execute all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • To expedite the sales tax funded projects, a short-term financing will be needed.  While there is no need for additional cash at this time, state law requires the District have cash available to support any purchase order issued. 
     
  • Bids were solicited from all members of the District's underwriting team as well as all the large banks that participate in these types of transactions.  The winning proposal was from PNC for a revolving line of credit.  The facility will allow us to draw the cash whenever needed, which will satisfy the state requirement to have cash available.
     
  • For the first year, the line of credit will be set at $80 million.  The line will be increased annually to a maximum of $200 million and will be in place for three years.
     
  • Based on current cash flow projections, there will be no need to draw on the line of credit.  However, should the expenditures exceed forecasts or revenues fall below projections, the District will draw on the line of credit as needed. In that instance interest will be charged at a rate of 79% of 1-Month LIBOR plus 41 bps.  At this time, that is estimated at 2.445%.  If cash were drawn to match the proposed project budgets, the estimated interest cost is $9.1 million over three years.
     
  • If no cash is drawn on the line of credit, as currently expected, an unused fee of 10 basis point or 0.10% will be assessed.  Based on current estimates, the fee will be approximately $500,000 over the three-year period.  This fee will be funded from interest earnings to date on the sales tax proceeds.
     
  • The costs of issuance, or payments to the professionals involved in the transaction, will not exceed $135,750 for the initial transaction.  Each annual increase will cost an additional $22,500 for a maximum of $203,250.  The details of the cost of issuance are attached.  These costs will be funded from interest earnings to date on the sales tax proceeds.
     
  • The interest earnings from the sales tax proceeds through March 2019 were approximately $3.9 million.
     
  • The status of the line of credit will be reported to the Independent Sales Surtax Oversight Committee and the Finance Committee each quarter.
     
  • The concept of a short term financing for the sales tax was reviewed by ISSOC at their meeting on March 8, 2019.  The committee was advised the Finance Committee would review the transaction in April.
     
  • The Finance Committee reviewed the transaction in detail at their meeting on April 26, 2019.  The committee unanimously recommended the transaction to the School Board for approval.
