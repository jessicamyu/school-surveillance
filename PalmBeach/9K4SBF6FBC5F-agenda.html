PS 2 Changes made to the Agreement are as follows:



This is a request to amend the current agreement between the School District of Palm Beach County and Lauren Steele from $7,499.56 to $54,037.76, and increase of $46,538.20 and to extend the date of the contract to June 30, 2011.  The reason for this request is to increase the number of hours from 278 to 2,077 hours, an increase of 1,799 hours.  Lauren Steele will provide additional hours to Project SMART (Students Mobilizing for Alcohol Reduction Together) funded by the U.S. Department of Education GRAA (Grant to Reduce Alcohol Abuse) and to FACE IT Alternative to Suspension. Project Smart responsibilities are  to coordinate staff training and Project SMART teams; collaborate with adults and students to develop and implement schools' underage drinking prevention social marketing campaigns; alcohol prevention strategic planning; coordinate all grant-required evaluation activities within the schools; and kickoff the year end meeting. FACE IT responsibilities are instruction for Alternative to Suspension programs, FACE IT, which allows students to reduce out-of-school suspension days, and a required year-end meeting. The grant, which was approved by the board on February 20, 2008, allows for a position of a Coordinator to fulfill these duties. 

Recommendation:

I recommend award of the Amendment to the Professional Services Agreement with Lauren Steele for and additional $46,538.20 effective through June 30, 2011 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all needed documents.

FM1 - Approve Florida Education Investment Trust Fund Trust Agreement
Meeting
Sep 29, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)


  • The Florida Association of District School Superintendents and the Florida School Boards Associations are sponsoring agencies of the new Florida Education Investment Trust Fund.

  • The Trust was created to offer a much needed investment alternative to School Districts to replace the local government investment pool administered by the State of Florida.

  • The Board of Directors of the Trust is composed of Florida School Board members and Florida School Superintendents.

  • An investment advisory committee was created by the Board of Directors.  The committee is comprised of School Finance Officers.

  • The goal of the FEITF is to provide a safe and liquid investment vehicle for Florida School Districts.

  • By approving the Trust Agreement, the School District will be able but not obligated to invest funds in the Trust.

  • The Finance Committee has discussed this investment alternative on numerous occasions and approved it during their meeting on September 21, 2010.  

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board the approve the interlocal agreement to become a Signatory Public Agency of the Florida Education Investment Trust Fund and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to sign all necessary documents.

PS3 - Professional Services Agreement with Fish and Richardson for EBS - Related Services
Meeting
Sep 29, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Legal Advisor was hired by the School Board in March, 2005 to assist and advise the School Board and Chief Counsel with various Federal Communications Commission (FCC) matters.  Under this contract, Legal Advisor provides legal advice and expertise related to the FCC licenses held by the School Board for Educational Broadband Spectrum (EBS).  A portion of the EBS-related work represents a recurring effort that is necessary and will continue for as long as the School Board holds the EBS licenses.  The remainder of the EBS-related work is made of various initiatives that are finite and will ultimately be completed.



EBS matters that represent recurring efforts include, but are not limited to the following:





  • Periodic renewal of the School Board's seven EBS licenses.

  • Miscellaneous unanticipated issues that arise as a result of FCC activities that Legal Advisor monitors and other unanticipated circumstances.  For example, there is legislation pending in Congress requiring the FCC to undertake a spectrum utilization analysis, with a likely outcome being that licensees who are not making effective use of their licensed spectrum would not be entitled to a license renewal.


EBS matters that are finite, include but are not limited to the following:





  • Complete work on EBS band plan transition including:  finalization of transition documentation; closing on payment of $1.75M to School Board; FCC related filings, and; mandatory conversion of the School Board's FCC licenses to past-transition licenses.

  • Preparation of legal memorandum and follow up meetings and conferences to assist the School Board with satisfaction of the FCC's new substantial service requirements for the School Board's seven EBS licenses.  Failure to meet the FCC's requirements could lead to the loss of the School Board's licenses and their value.

  • Advise and assist the School Board with ensuring satisfaction of the FCC's new substantial service requirements by May 1, 2011 including:  preparation of written compliance plans for each of the School Board's seven EBS licenses; revisions to operational and license status memorandums to reflect changes in operations, and; preparation and filing of substantial service showings with the FCC.

  • Unwinding of the Sprint (now Clearwire) lease agreement which allows the School Board to use pre-transition channels E1, E2 and E3 pending completion of the EBS band plan transition in the Palm Beach market.

  • Resolution of outstanding legal issues concerning coverage of new EBS licenses issued to School Board (FCC licensing data does not accurately reflect the School Board's agreement with FAU).   This issue must be resolved to ensure that the School Board maintains all of the population and area coverage that it is entitled to keep under the FAU agreement, but which is not currently reflected in the FCC's database.



The Consultant shall perform the following services: Legal Advisor shall provide FCC regulatory and transactional advice to the School Board with respect to Educational Broadband Service Spectrum.



On July 1, 2010, a contract for FY '11 in the not to exceed amount of $10,000.00 was approved by the Superintendent pursuant to the delegation in School Board Policy 6.14.  This amendment is for an additional not to exceed amount of $75,000.00.



The District needs an attorney with expertise in FCC regulatory matters, spectrum use and valuation issues to assist staff in the protection of the EBS licenses and their value, and evaluation of the alternatives available to the School Board.



The firm of Fish & Richardson and Attorney Ed Lavergne have provided excellent service since being hired in March 2005. Approval of this contract will allow Mr. Lavergne to continue to provide the Chief Counsel with sound advice that can be used as the basis for making future decisions regarding the EBS licenses held by the School Board.

Recommendation:

I recommend award of Amendment No. 1 to the Consultant Contract with Fish & Richardson for an additional not to exceed amount of $75,000, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all needed documents.
