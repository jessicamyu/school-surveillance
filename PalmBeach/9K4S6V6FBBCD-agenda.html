Subject
PL1 - Resolution requesting that the Florida Legislature approve a dedicated funding source to support Tri-Rail
Meeting
Dec 2, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)


  • The South Florida Regional Transportation Authority (SFRTA) has tried unsuccessfully to obtain a dedicated funding source for Tri-Rail.


 





  • Approximately 750 Palm Beach County students use Tri-Rail on a daily basis to get to and from school. 


 





  • The operation of Tri-Rail is an important transportation and economic service to the residents and visitors of the South Florida region. 


 





  • At a joint meeting with the School Board, Board of County Commissioners and League of Cities held on October 28, 2009 there was discussion on adopting resolutions supporting a dedicated funding source for Regional Transportation Authorities, specifically South Florida Regional Transportation Authority for the funding of Tri-Rail.  


 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve Resolution PL 09-01 requesting that the Florida Legislature pass legislation providing for a dedicated funding source for Regional Transportation Authorities, specifically the South Florida Regional Transportation Authority to support Tri-Rail.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore moore@palmbeach.k12.fl.us;



Joseph M. Sanches sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

There will be no financial impact to the School District.

Motion & Voting

I recommend the School Board approve Resolution PL 09-01 requesting that the Florida Legislature pass legislation providing for a dedicated funding source for Regional Transportation Authorities, specifically the South Florida Regional Transportation Authority to support Tri-Rail.

Motion by Sandra Richmond, second by Carrie Hill.

1.  On December 8, 2004 the School Board approved the Interlocal Agreement with the City of Palm Beach Gardens for the Mutual Use of Recreational Facilities.  (See Exhibit "A")



2.  The existing Interlocal Agreement will expire December 8, 2009.



3.  The City is requesting to amend the existing Agreement to allow for a one (1) year extension with four (4) one (1) year renewals.
