Subject
Special Meeting re: PBCSB v. Elizabeth Stuglik Final Order
Meeting
Oct 13, 2010 - Legal Sessions
Category
Special Meetings
Type
Action (Consent)




  • Elizabeth Stuglik was employed by the School District of Palm Beach County as a teacher at Howard L. Watkins Middle School.




  • On March 3, 2010, the Superintendent made a recommendation that Ms. Stuglik be terminated based upon her engaging in sexual misconduct with a fellow teacher on the school campus.




  • Ms. Stuglik timely asserted her right to an appeal and a hearing was held before the Division of Administrative Hearings on May 19, 2010.  A Recommended Order was entered by the Administrative Law Judge on August 2, 2010, recommending the School Board award Ms. Stuglik back pay for the period she was under suspension without pay (March 4, 2010 through June 4, 2010).  The Recommended Order further recognizes that Ms. Stuglik was not entitled to reinstatement due to the expiration of her annual contract and non-reappointment.


  • This Final Order is in the best interest of the School Board.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Final order in the matter of Palm Beach County School Board v. Elizabeth Stuglik, DOAH Case No. 10-1526, a copy of which is attached hereto, and authorize the execution of all related documents.

Contact:

Bruce A. Harris, Esq.; Interim Chief Counsel



Elizabeth T. McBride, Esq.; Counsel

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the District is $5,778.08 from the general fund.

  • Amber Schmeider was employed by the School District of Palm Beach County as a teacher at Howard L. Watkins Middle School.




  • On March 3, 2010, the Superintendent made a recommendation that Ms. Schmeider be terminated based upon her engaging in sexual misconduct with a fellow teacher on the school campus.




  • Ms. Schmeider timely asserted her right to an appeal and a hearing was held before the Division of Administrative Hearings on June 11, 2010.  A Recommended Order was entered by the Administrative Law Judge on August 23, 2010, recommending the School Board terminate Ms. Schmeider’s employment.   




  • Neither party filed exceptions to the Recommended Order.




  • This Final Order is in the best interest of the School Board.
