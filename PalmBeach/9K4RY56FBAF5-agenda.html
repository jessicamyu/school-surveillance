Special Meeting re: Emergency Rule re: Alternative to Out of School Suspension Policy
Meeting
Mar 12, 2008 - Spec Mtg Emergency Rule Alt to Suspension
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

The alternative to Out-of-School Suspension (ATOSS) program is a voluntary program that will start as a pilot program for certain specified schools and offers an option to eligible students who have received an out of school suspension for violating any policy relating to student conduct in Chapter 5 of the School Board Policies.  The goal of ATOSS is to help students who have received an out of school suspension maintain academic momentum by providing an opportunity to reflect, adjust, and interact positively with their peers, educational environment, and community in a temporary placement in another educational setting.  ATOSS is specifically intended to serve as an alternative to an out-of-school suspension and thereby eliminate or reduce a student’s suspension days.  ATOSS is not a method to unilaterally remove “disruptive” students, nor is ATOSS a replacement or substitute for assignment to an Alternative School.



 



This recommendation for adoption of an emergency rule is being made upon a finding as required by law for the welfare of students because recent data has demonstrated significant increases in the failure of students who cannot recover academically after serving an out-of-school suspension.  This rule would allow students to maintain academic momentum while simultaneously serving suspension days in an alternative setting.  The rule requires immediate adoption to ensure consistency and fairness in the application of the ATOSS program.  Moreover, notice of this action is fair where the notice of the special meeting concerning this emergency rule is being posed on the District’s website, accessible to the public, on or about March 8, 2008, along with the agenda, the proposed emergency policy/rule, and the required notice of the Board’s special meeting will be timely published in the newspaper.



 



The proposed emergency policy/rule is attached as an appendix to this Board Report.



 



Regular development is also being recommended as soon as possible (anticipated to begin on April 2, 2008), and that process is likely to be completed prior to the start of the 2008-2009 school year in August of 2008.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the Board adopt the emergency rule, Policy 5.18155 entitled, “Alternative to Out-of-School Suspension of Students,” and make the findings as to an emergency as set forth in the Board report.
