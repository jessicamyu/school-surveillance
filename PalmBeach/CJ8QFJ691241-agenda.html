I recommend the Board approve the Amendment with District Hospital Holdings, Inc., d/b/a Lakeside Medical Center, effective September 7, 2022 through June 29, 2026; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Glades Central High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7,000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Behavioral Health Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry-level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Subscription with ArbiterPay and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the subscription with ArbiterPay. 

 

ArbiterPay is an online service to pay athletic officials.  The company was created by the NCAA to provide this service for college sports and have since been spun off as a standalone company.  The company continues to handle the payment of athletic officials for the NCAA as well as 80% of high schools throughout the country.

 

Board approval was given on July 24, 2019 (FMAP3) to enter into the User Agreement and Addendum with ArbiterPay so that the payment process for athletic officials would be centralized at the District level with funds transferred, as needed, to fund payments by Arbiter to high school athletic officials.  Each school athletic director is required to request payment after work is complete and use an app or website to approve payments as well as access information regarding the officials scheduled for their games.  

 

The use of the service has increased to include middle schools in FY 2023, otherwise the fees are comparable to the 2019 contract.  The estimated payments made through Arbiter annually for middle and high school sports is $1,750,000. The transaction fees associated with the payments under the original User Agreement are deleted under the License Subscription Order Form and Arbiter will credit the transaction fees back to the Board against the Year 1 Fees. The payments, which have been paid by schools in the past will continue to be allocated to each schools’ internal account. The fees associated with the service are funded by the District.  The fees cover the implementation for middle schools, annual refresher training, subscription for all middle and high schools as well as user licenses.  The total annual fees range from $29,300 for FY 2023 to $31,695 for FY 2027 and include new schools planned to open over the five-year window.  

 

Implementation of ArbiterPay coincided with the implementation of Arbiter by the local sports associations. Each association (football, baseball, etc.) assigns officials to games. A contract was established with each association and the cost for those annual agreements is estimated at $75,000. These contracts are funded by the District.

 

The License Subscription Order Form is retroactive to July 1, 2022, which is financially advantageous to the Board because the transaction fees have been deleted.

I recommend that the School Board approve the naming of the new Middle School "17-PP" from the list of recommendations submitted in order of preference as follows: Agnes Ballard Middle School, Boynton West Middle School, Summit West Middle School.

DESCRIPTION:

In accordance with School Board Policy 7.03, three names are being submitted for the naming of Middle School "17-PP". A committee of parents, students, and community members met to review names for the school. Committee members represented the geographical area of the community.

 

  • Agnes Ballard Middle School - Agnes Ballard was the first woman to serve as School Superintendent for Palm Beach County. She was elected in 1920 after the 19th Amendment was passed. She was also the first woman architect in Florida, and helped to build and ready many early Palm Beach County Schools.

 

  • Boynton West Middle School - The school is located in West Boynton Beach. The community around this school identifies as West Boynton and are part of Coalition of West Boynton Residential Association (COWBRA)

 

  • Summit West Middle School - The school is surrounded by the Canyons, Greystone, Cobblestone, etc. at the top, the peak, we will be the Summit amongst all of those.

 

 

The selection committee was comprised of the following members: Principal/Superintendent Designee -Joe Peccia, Superintendent Designee - Dionne Breaux, Lay Persons, Carly Abelson, Caroline Verlinde and President of COBWRA - Steve Wallace; Future Students: Emma Oden, Gavin Moses and PBC Historical Society - Rose Guerrero.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the adoption of the list of instructional materials for the periods indicated, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents to the FY23 Instructional Materials Program.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to School Board Policy 8.122 (3), an instructional materials committee was convened to evaluate and recommend new materials in the area of kindergarten Haitian Creole Language Arts for Dual Language (Trimester 3). Committee membership included: subject area classroom teachers and lay citizens. The instructional materials recommended for district adoption are contained in Attachment A.

 

District processes as provided by Florida Statutes and policy have been followed, including providing timely public access to the materials, and the appropriate notices for the meeting have been published. A timely public notice was published for the open public hearing which was held before the Board at a meeting on September 21, 2022. At the public hearing, no members of the public signed up to speak and thus no public comments were offered. The notice of the hearing stated that public comment would be accepted.

 

The recommended instructional materials programs will be provided in both print and/or online digital formats. Student editions in the core courses will be available to students in print and via their student portal accounts. Teacher materials and resources will also be available on Blender. 

 

The FY23 Trimester 3 Kindergarten Haitian Creole Language Instructional Materials Cost Analysis (Attachment B) represents estimated costs for the implementation of new instructional materials. The estimated costs are based on projected student enrollments and anticipated program needs. Funding is provided from Title III grant funds. 

 

Staff development for instructional materials will be provided by the Department of Multicultural Education and will be focused on the appropriate use of materials to implement the B.E.S.T. Standards.
