Throughout this school year a select group of students at H. L. Watkins Middle School have had unprecedented access and exposure to healthcare careers and patient care. Last year, the leadership of the T. Leroy Jefferson Medical Society, Good Samaritan Medical Center, Wells Fargo Bank, and H.L. Watkins Middle School met to formulate an innovative and unique Externship Program for a group of Health Career Academy students.

These 8th grade students had in-depth exposure to and visited many of the departments within Good Samaritan Medical Center. In the Human Resources Department, the students learned the importance of and underwent patient confidentiality training. In the Operating Room, they learned about sterile techniques; participated in the simulation of a patient being put to sleep and intubated; and performed robotic surgery on an artificial knee joint. In the Emergency Room and Cardiovascular Catheterization Lab, they learned about a variety of medical procedures from cardiac stress tests, to endovascular abdominal aortic aneurysm repair. On the Obstetrical Unit, the students had an in-depth review of the birth process; participated in mock deliveries (with full instrumentation) and newborn resuscitation; participated in in-depth discussions about maternal-fetal health and pathophysiology; and toured the newborn nursery. Through these experiences, the students gained perspectives to careers in medicine unrivaled to anywhere in our county.

DESCRIPTION:

The Department of Educational Technology’s eMobilize program has been recognized as an Apple Distinguished Program for the 2012-13 school year. The eMobilize program joins 103 nationwide programs that Apple has recognized as exemplary learning environments and centers of innovation, leadership, and educational excellence.

 

eMobilize has been selected as an Apple Distinguished Program because it demonstrates the Apple vision which supports the ways students live, and how they want to learn. This professional development program embraces the use of technology as a tool to enhance instruction in the classroom.
 

The Apple Distinguished Program prestigious designation is reserved for districts and schools that integrate Apple technology in education, and meet criteria for the five best practices:

  1. Visionary leadership,
  2. Innovative learning and teaching,
  3. Ongoing professional learning,
  4. Compelling evidence of success, and
  5. A flexible learning environment.
     

eMobilize was developed with funds from the No Child Left Behind: Enhancing Education Through Technology Title II D Entitlement Grant. The focus has been to create a project that explored the use of hybrid labs (containing laptops and iPads) in the classrooms.

 

The teachers involved have been working on developing curriculum and lesson plans, as well as discovering new management techniques that are unique to using these devices. They also spend a great deal of time conducting professional development.

 

During the first year, teachers learned how to use the devices in the classroom. The second year was focused on developing new projects and implementing new curriculum aligned with district standards, as well as meeting new learning outcomes using Digital Learning strategies that align to the Common Core State Standards.

 

This past year focused on the development of creating professional development experiences for teachers to not only learn how to use the devices, but also to integrate them into their curriculum.

 

This eMobilize project has evolved to include 13 teachers at 12 different schools. Even with no more funding, these teachers have dedicated themselves to the vision of the project and think little of donating their free time during their evenings, weekends, and summers. They continue to work tirelessly with their students to develop new dynamic learning environments that can be replicated for others to use in their classrooms.

 

Educators from within Palm Beach County, as well as across the globe, continue to use the award-winning eMobilize Wiki website as a valuable resource.

Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the cooperative agreement with the School Board of Seminole County, Florida to continue participation in a consortium for the Medicaid Administrative Claiming process and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all documents related to this agreement. The effective dates of this cooperative agreement are July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2014.

DESCRIPTION:

This is a renewal with the School Board of Seminole County will act as the lead district in a consortium of school districts and will serve as data manager and processor for the School District of Palm Beach County for the Medicaid Administrative Claiming Services.  Services provided by Seminole County include: 

 

  • the selection of random sample of participants to document services from staff lists provided by the district
  • the provision and monitoring of the documentation required in order to submit the random sample data
  • the provision and monitoring of the documentation required in order to submit the Medicaid Administrative Claim
  • the development of an online system of data entry to meet the requirement of random sampling reporting for Medicaid

The School District of Palm Beach County, using the Seminole County School District data report, will determine and submit the Administrative Claim to Medicaid for reimbursement.

 

The School District of Palm Beach County has been participating in Medicaid Administrative Claiming Services since 1998 when the legislation was passed.

    • National item bank (312,259 items)
    • Florida item bank (36,894 items)
    • FCAT aligned item bank (30,000 items focused on NGSSS benchmarks, and adhering to FCAT 2.0 item specifications)
    • 354 interim benchmark assessments focused on NGSSS benchmarks, adhering to FCAT 2.0 item specifications and aligned with District’s scope and sequence for reading, mathematics, science, Algebra 1, Biology, and Geometry
    • 92 multiple benchmark assessments focused on NGSSS benchmarks, adhering to FCAT 2.0 item specifications and aligned with District’s scope and sequence for reading, mathematics, science, Algebra 1, and Geometry
    • 27 district-created fall and winter diagnostic assessments aligned with Statewide assessments at the tested grade levels and subjects
      • Grade 6, 7, 8, 9 & 10 Reading
      • Grade 5, 6 Math
      • Algebra I, Geometry, Biology, U.S. History, and Civics
      • Grade 10 Reading and Mathematics Sunshine State Standards Retakes
      • Grade 10 Reading NGSSS Retakes
    • Student, teacher, and parent instructional resources and community outreach through CORE K12
    • Data transfers from CORE K12 to the District’s Educational Data Warehouse (EDW).

The AWD program has been implemented through South Tech Academy since 1983.  This program is intended to improve the quality of life of adults with disabilities.  The program uses a realistic work environment with skilled and certified teachers who provide the training and support needed to make the student successful.  The AWD program is designed to make each student as self-sufficient as possible and for those that qualify, to become employable.

 

The AED program combines job skill training with special education instructional methodology which enables the students to gain both employability skills and real world job skills.  The goal is self-sufficiency for the disabled by providing training and income.  The funding is used to train and enhance the skills and lives of approximately 600 students.  Each student's Adult Individual Education Plan (AIEP) is closely monitored to assure that AIEP goals are being met as well as tracking the number of students who obtain competitive employment and non-competitive employment.  Competitive employment is where students interview for job positions and non-competitive employment is where students do not interview for job positions and are placed in jobs according to their abilities.

 

The target population focuses on adult individuals who were born with a disability, acquired a disability later in life, and individuals who are 55 years of age or older and have disabilities, who are referred to as senior citizens with disabilities.

 

The following are the five liaison agencies who partner to provide training: The Arc of Palm Beach County, Inc., Habilitation Center for the Handicapped, Inc., Jeff Industries, Palm Beach Habilitation Center, and Seagull Industries for the Disabled, Inc.  Each agency promotes the philosophy of challenging students to attain the greatest degree of self-sufficiency in both their work skills and personal life skills.  This funding enables students that are exceptional to become employable in the community.

Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the 2013 Comprehensive Safety Inspection Report

DESCRIPTION:

 

The annual Comprehensive Safety Inspections were performed by the Building Code Services  Fire and Safety Inspectors as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). All educational, auxiliary and ancillary facilities were included in these inspections, using the State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) for casualty and sanitation, and Florida Administrative Code Chapter 69A-58 for fire safety.

 

The number of citations for Public Schools, Alternative Schools and Ancillary Facilities fell from 3,335 in 2012 to 3,279 in 2013. This represents a 2% decrease over one year and a 93% reduction since 2000.  Highlights this year also include the average citation age dropped to 1.89 years from 2.81 years in 2012 and 3.71 years in 2011.  

 

A summary of the number of citations for each school and ancillary facility is included with this report. Individual school reports are on file with the Building Code Services Department. All conditions considered by the inspector to be hazardous or posing an imminent danger were reported to Facilities Services at the time of discovery for immediate action.

 

According to SREF, Chapter 5, the inspection reports shall be approved by the Board, and the Board shall adopt a plan and a schedule for the correction of each deficiency.   The corrective action plans for each citation were developed in conjunction with the local fire authorities and are included on each report.

 

Upon the board approving the inspection reports, copies of the reports will be submitted to the State Fire Marshal as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). The Board shall certify the completion of the inspection report to the State Fire Marshal by June 30, 2013.

Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the Legal Services contract with Fish & Richardson, P.C. in the amount of $54,250, for the period of July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2014 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Legal Advisor was hired by the School Board in March, 2005 to assist and advise the School Board and Chief Counsel with various Federal Communications Commission (FCC) matters. Under this contract, Legal Advisor provides legal advice and expertise related to the FCC licenses held by the School Board for the Educational Broadband Service spectrum (EBS). A portion of the EBS-related work represents a recurring effort that is necessary and will continue for as long as the School Board holds the EBS licenses. The remainder of the EBS-related work is made of various initiatives that are finite and will ultimately be completed.

 

Under a recommended flat fee arrangement, the Consultant shall perform the following services:

 

1. Keep the School Board informed of relevant legal, policy, and regulatory developments affecting its EBS licenses, including developments concerning licensing of EBS white space;

 

2. Assist the School Board with routine EBS regulatory compliance matters at the FCC;

 

3. Provide advice and counsel on day-to-day EBS licensing and operational issues;

 

4. Update master licensing and operational memorandum as necessary to reflect licensing, technical and operational changes;

 

5. Assist School Board in evaluating third party brokerage and similar arrangements involving EBS spectrum leasing;

 

6. Monitor developments with Clearwire and other commercial entities that may have an interest in leasing the School Board’s EBS spectrum. Identify potential leasing opportunities and discuss with the School Board. (This does not include preparation of requests for proposals or solicitations, term sheets, lease negotiations, contract drafting, etc.).

 

Work that extends beyond the scope of the flat fee arrangement, such as the potential leasing of the Board’s licensed spectrum to a commercial operator, will be performed at discounted governmental rates or as a separate flat fee arrangement.

 

The Board needs an attorney with expertise in FCC regulatory matters, spectrum use and valuation issues to assist staff in the protection of the EBS licenses and their value, and evaluation of the alternatives available to the Board.

 

The firm of Fish & Richardson and Attorney Ed Lavergne have provided excellent service since being hired in March 2005. Approval of this contract will allow Mr. Lavergne and his team to continue to provide the Office of General Counsel and District staff with sound advice that can be used as the basis for making decisions regarding the EBS licenses held by the School Board.
