Subject
EAI-1 Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) Grant
Meeting
Feb 10, 2016 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room.
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the MSAP Grant, if awarded, and MSAP Voluntary Plan in an amount up to $12,000,000, effective October 1, 2016 through September 30, 2019, and approve any no cost extension as applicable.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County plans to apply for the Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) Grant from the United States Department of Education (USDOE), in response to the “Notice” to be published in the Federal Register in early 2016.  In this year’s competition, the USDOE will award grants to an estimated 25-30 school districts across the nation.  Each grant will award up to $4 million per year for three years, or up to $12 million over the course of the MSAP project period from October 1, 2016 through September 30, 2019.

 

Pending receipt of the MSAP grant, the following five new magnet schools are proposed for development and implementation beginning in school year 2016-17:  

MSAP Magnet Schools - Magnet Themes (School-Wide)

Palmetto Elementary - IB Primary Years Programme (IB PYP)

Grove Park Elementary - IB Primary Years Programme (IB PYP)

Howell L. Watkins Middle - IB Middle Years Programme (IB MYP) Infused with STEM

Carver Middle - IB Middle Years Programme (IB MYP) Infused with STEM   

Congress Middle - Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM)
 
Students who live in the attendance boundaries of these schools will participate in and benefit from the school-wide magnet program, along with students who are recruited to attend the magnet schools from outside each school’s attendance area.  The identified schools all have adequate space available to recruit additional students to attend the magnet schools.

 

MSAP grants are awarded to school districts on a competitive basis to establish and operate magnet schools that meet the following MSAP statutory purposes:

• To  eliminate, reduce, and prevent minority group isolation in schools with substantial numbers of minority group students;

• To promote diversity through a specialized magnet curriculum capable of attracting and bringing together students from different racial, ethnic, and socio-economic backgrounds;

• To provide all students with the opportunity to meet challenging academic content standards;

• To ensure that all students in the magnet school have equitable access to the high quality education that will enable them to succeed academically and continue with post-secondary education or productive careers;

• To support capacity-building activities to enable the continued operation of the magnet schools at a high performance level after the funding ends.

 

To be eligible for the MSAP grant, the School Board will be required to approve the MSAP Voluntary Plan to transform eligible public schools into innovative, evidence-based, school-wide thematic programs that promote diversity through magnet school choice.

PS1 Out-of-Field/Out-of-Compliance Teachers for 2016, Survey 3
Meeting
Feb 10, 2016 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room.
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the attached list of out-of-field/out-of-compliance teachers for Survey 3 for the FY16 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.42, F.S., teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified.  Such assignments are typically made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field in the following areas: Gifted, Reading, and ASD.  Those new hires who are offered jobs as an Interim Out-of-Field are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements prior to the end of May or pass a Subject Area Exam and add it to their certificate by the beginning of April.  If a teacher does not satisfy the coursework and/or testing requirements, their Interim position ends.  Once a teacher completes their Out-of-Field requirements, the school is notified and teachers can be moved from Interim Out-of-Field to Regular.  Teachers who are placed Out-of-Field in core content areas are eligible to apply for reimbursement on the Subject Area Exam registration fee, after they add the subject area to their certificate. 

 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate.  Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary, or ESE.  Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements.  This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification.  It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track.  Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014 include the following statement, " Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you  must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment."
