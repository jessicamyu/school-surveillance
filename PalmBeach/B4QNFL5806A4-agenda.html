DESCRIPTION:

 

Overview

On February 1, 2018, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Somerset Academy Gardens to open Somerset Academy Gardens charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August 2019.

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument. 

 

Projected Enrollment

            First Year                   K-8                  424 Projected Enrollment

            Second Year             K-8                  628 Projected Enrollment

            Third Year                 K-8                  800 Projected Enrollment

            Fourth Year               K-8                  884 Projected Enrollment

            Fifth Year                   K-8                 906 Projected Enrollment

 

Education Program Design

The proposed school’s educational program intends to fulfill the school’s mission of maximizing student academic achievement through an arts-integrated curriculum designed to foster the development of accountable, 21stcentury learners in a safe and enriching environment. Through a rigorous and well-rounded educational program, in accordance with the Palm Beach County Student Progression Plan and following a Florida standards aligned scope and sequence (see attached sample), students will be better prepared for success in middle and high school, and subsequently, college.

 

All Somerset programs implement the “neighborhood” school paradigm in their management structures to consistently maintain the feeling of a small school environment.

 

Accordingly, the proposed school will combine the best practices developed by this extensive network of innovative Somerset board members, parents, and educators working together for a common purpose, and those principles driving essential school reform nation-wide. The school will meet high standards of student achievement and provide a solid academic foundation that will enable students to be successful at subsequent levels. The curriculum will prioritize mastery of all applicable Florida state standards in all core subject areas, while an emphasis on arts-education will endeavor to support both the academic and socioemotional wellbeing of all students. Additionally, the school will offer a rich array of specials/electives in the arts, health, physical education, and world languages.

 

Location

Palm Beach Gardens

 

Applicant’s Founding Governing Board

David Concepcion, Board Director; Lourdes Isla Marerro, Board Chairperson; Todd Germab, Board Vice Chairperson; Ana Diaz, Board Secretary; Bernard Kimmel, Board Member; Louis Martin, Board Member; George B Ozuna, Board Member;Jenny Esquijarosa, Parent Representative

 

Ratings of Somerset Academy Gardens

Per the Overall Assessment the ratings indicated that their application did not meet the statutory requirement set forth in F.S. 1002.33(6) in all 20 sections.  (See Overall Assessment)

 

“If an application is denied, the sponsor shall, within 10 calendar days after such denial, articulate in writing the specific reasons, based upon good cause, supporting its denial of the charter application and shall provide the letter of denial and supporting documentation to the applicant and to the Department of Education.”

Education Program Design

The proposed educational program by Academic Solutions Academy (ASA) provides computer-based instructions for High-Needs student that fit their schedules and academic needs. ASA will establish a comprehensive opportunity for students seeking to obtain a high school diploma and pursue post-secondary education career skills development.

ASA combines computer assisted lessons through the Pearson Learning System (Gradpoint) in combination with one-on-one, whole group instruction in English, math, science, and electives that allow students to pick up wherever they left off in their traditional high school.
 

Students attend classes anywhere from five to eight hours a day. They may choose morning, afternoon, evening classes. They receive credits for completed course work. At the time of enrollment/orientation, students receive a Prescribe Instructional Plan (PIP) based on their transcripts provided by the district, indicating the courses needed to be completed to satisfy district’s graduation requirements.

Education Program Design

Rigor/Relevance + Relationships Framework— SLAM’s educational program design is grounded on the philosophy of the three R’s: Rigor, Relevance, and Relationships. The three R’s represent the essential components that must be in place to effectively prepare students for the demands of the 21st Century. Attributed to the work of Dr. Bill Daggett, the three R’s model has been linked by research to higher attendance and graduation rates, stronger test scores, and safer schools.

SLAM Academy combines the three R’s with an innovative approach to engage students.

 

SLAM Academy integrates sports themes into the Florida Standards-based curriculum to keep students engaged. This supports interdisciplinary teaching by providing a vehicle for integrating content areas in a way that makes sense to children. It helps them make connections in order to transfer knowledge they acquire and apply it meaningful ways Career Academies—The secondary program (9-12) offers a career academy model that produces college-bound students. Students will choose one of the following career academies that will prepare students for majors in sports-related careers:

  • Sports Medicine
  • Sports Broadcasting and Journalism
  • Sports Marketing, Entertainment Arts and Management

SLAM’s use of career academies is based research validating that “career academies are the most durable and best-tested component of reform strategy to prepare students for college and careers.”

I recommend the Board approve the attached list of Out-of-Field/Out-of-Compliance Teachers for Survey 2 for FY19 school year.

DESCRIPTION:

Pursuant to sec. 1012.42, F.S, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified.  Such assignment typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The School District will allow schools to hire new hires Out-of-Field in the following areas: Gifted, Reading and ASD. Those new hires who are offered jobs as an Interim Out-of-Field, and are required to complete appropriate coursework requirements prior to the end of May.  If a teacher does not satisfy the coursework requirement their Interim position ends.   Once a teacher completes their Out-of-Field requirements the school is notified and teachers can be moved from Interim Out-of-Field to Regular.  Teachers who are placed Out-of-Field in core content areas are eligible to apply for reimbursement on the Subject Area Exam registration fee, after they add the subject area to their certificate. 

 

Teachers identified as Out-of-Compliance are Category 1 teachers who have not added the ESOL endorsement to their certificate.  Category 1 teachers are teaching Language Arts, English, Reading, Elementary or ESE.  Category 1 teachers have six (6) years to meet their ESOL requirements. This timeline begins with the first ESOL Out-of-Field Notification. It is recommended that teachers take at least one ESOL Endorsement class each year to remain on track.  Job Offer letters for Instructional personnel hired after July 1, 2014 include the following statement, “Acceptance of this position means that as a condition of continued employment, you must complete the appropriate ESOL coursework required by this assignment.”
