  • The next Decennial Census will occur in 2010 with the official count day being April 1st

  • More than $400 billion per year in federal and state funding is allocated to communities based, in part, on census data, including health care, community development, housing, education, transportation, social services, employment and much more. Education funding includes Federal Lunch Program, IDEA, Title 1 and Migrant Education.

  • The fact that the official counts are only conducted every 10 years makes it imperative that we facilitate a complete and accurate count.

  • This District has an opportunity to make a meaningful impact in the Census for this area, as the County's largest employer and as a public entity that has daily contact with parents and community.

  • The Census is a teachable opportunity.  The Census Bureau has developed lesson plans that are available online (www.census.gov/schools).

I recommend the School Board approve Resolution PL 10-01 affirming support of and partnership with the 2010 Census.

FM2 - Refund of Impact Fees
Meeting
Jan 27, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)




  • Palm Beach County assesses impact fees for roads, parks, public buildings and schools each time that a building permit for a new residential unit is issued.  The school impact fees collected by the County are periodically remitted to the School Board, on a reimbursement basis, upon request of the School Board’s Treasurer.






  • On March 7, 2006, Waterbrook Peninsula, LLC (“Waterbrook”) paid to Palm Beach County a total of $57,874.00 for school impact fees for 20 residential units.  After deducting its 2% administrative collection fee, the County remitted $56,716.52 as part of a periodic reimbursement payment in the amount of $26,063,000.00 on June 23, 2006.






  • These 20 residential units were never constructed by Waterbrook and on June 25, 2008, Waterbrook filed for bankruptcy under Chapter 11 of the Bankruptcy Code.






  • As part of the bankruptcy proceeding, Waterbrook demanded from the County a refund of the impact fees it paid for the 20 unbuilt units.  The County settled this matter with regard to the roads, parks and public buildings by refunding to Waterbrook approximately $77,000.  The County did not refund the portion of the $56,716.52 associated with the school impact fees because these funds had already been remitted to the School Board.  Counsel for Waterbrook then contacted the Chief Counsel’s office and requested that a refund of the school impact fees be negotiated.  Following negotiations between the parties, it was agreed that Waterbrook would accept a refund in the amount of $36,000.00, less the County’s 3.4% administrative refund fee in full satisfaction of its claim.






  • Since the $56,716.52 had already been spent following receipt in 2006, the Chief Counsel’s office and Treasurer worked with County staff to have the $36,000.00 refund paid by the County out of funds currently being held by the County on behalf of the School Board.  An adjustment to the capital budget may occur once funds are received from the County towards the end of the fiscal year.  The County has agreed to deduct the $36,000.00 from the funds being held for the School Board and after subtracting the County’s 3.4% administrative refund fee remit the balance to Waterbrook.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve a Resolution authorizing Palm Beach County to deduct $36,000.00 from the school impact fees being held by the County as herein described; and authorize the Superintendent and Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

PL3 - NEW SUNCOAST HS ILA WITH RIVIERA BEACH FOR ROADWAY IMPROVMENTS
Meeting
Jan 27, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

The new Suncoast High School, located between John F. Kennedy Middle School and Mary McLeod Bethune Elementary School in Riviera Beach, is scheduled to open in August 2010.



 



As a part of the construction project of new Suncoast High School, the City of Riviera Beach (“City”) has required that the School Board undertake certain roadway and traffic improvements which the School Board staff has agreed to do.  In order to insure that the School Board constructed the roadway and traffic improvements, the City has refused to issue permits for and/or perform inspections of the potable water and sanitary sewer connections that need to be made to the City’s water and sewer systems for the new high school.  On December 16, 2009, the Board authorized staff to move forward with the conflict resolution process under Chapter 164, F.S.  The Interlocal Agreement being presented has been negotiated with the City to memorialize the School Board’s agreement to construct the roadway and traffic improvements in order to get the City to issue the necessary water and sewer permits and perform the inspections.



 



The essential elements of the Interlocal Agreement are:



·         The Board agrees to construct two (2) south bound left turn-lanes on Congress Avenue onto 13th Street.



·         The Board agrees to construct a north bound right turn-lane along Congress Avenue onto 13th Street.



·         The Board agrees to widen 13th Street to 4 (four) lanes with 5 feet sidewalk on both sides of 13th Street between Jake Lane and Congress Avenue.



·         The Board agrees to install traffic signals at the intersections of 13th Street and Congress Avenue and 13th Street and Jake Lane.



·         In the event the Board cannot meet the timeframes to 1) construct the right turn-lane along Congress Avenue onto 13th Street, 2) widen 13th Street to 4 (four) lanes with 5 feet sidewalk on both sides of 13th Street between Jake Lane and Congress Avenue, and 3) install traffic signals at the intersections of 13th Street and Congress Avenue and 13th Street and Jake Lane, it shall request, from the City, an amendment of the Interlocal Agreement to extend those completion deadlines.  The City’s approval of the amendment shall not be unreasonably withheld.



·         Within one (1) business day of the effective date of the Interlocal Agreement, the City agrees to approve and issue the permit for water and sewer connection for the new Suncoast High School.



·         By February 1, 2011, the Board shall conduct and submit to the City a traffic study to determine whether traffic entering Suncoast High School from westbound West 13th Street justifies construction of a right turn lane between Jake Lane and Forrest Lane on 13th Street for westbound traffic.  In the event that the traffic study determines that a westbound West 13th Street right hand turn lane is needed at Jake Lane, the Board shall, prior to February 1, 2012, construct the right hand turn lane.



·         By February 1, 2011, the Board shall conduct and submit to the City a traffic study to determine whether there is the need for traffic safety modifications to the intersection of West 13th Street and Avenue U.  In the event that the traffic study determines that traffic safety modifications should be made to the intersection of West 13th Street and Avenue U, the Board and City shall negotiate in good faith to allocate the responsibility for making and funding such modifications and the agreement of the parties shall be memorialized in a new Interlocal agreement between the parties. 



·         The Board shall, prior to August 1, 2010, re-evaluate the bus routes for Suncoast High School with the objective being to eliminate or reduce the number of buses turning right from eastbound Blue Heron Boulevard onto southbound Avenue S.  The Board shall notify the City in writing as to the conclusions of this re-evaluation.



·         The Board shall, prior to February 1, 2011, conduct and provide to the City a traffic safety study evaluating the need for school related traffic signage on the City public roads adjacent to the School Property.  The City shall be financially responsible for implementing, at the City’s option, the recommendations of this traffic safety study.



·         If any legal action is brought to enforce the terms and conditions of the Interlocal Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney’s fees, court costs and expenses.  This provision does not include fees, costs and expenses associated with any Chapter 164, F.S. proceeding.



 



The approval of this Interlocal Agreement by both parties resolves the dispute between the School Board and the City except with regard to the issue of the City’s liability for delay damages to be incurred by the School Board resulting from the City’s withholding of the water and sewer connection permits.  Since the permits have not yet been issued and it cannot be determined when the Construction Manager will complete the work on the water and sewer improvements, the parties have mutually agreed to postpone the Chapter 164, F.S. Conflict Assessment meeting that had been scheduled for January 27, 2010.  Once the water and sewer connection permits are issued by the City and the work completion date can be determined, the School Board will provide the City with the additional information regarding the delay damages suffered by the School Board.



 



 

Recommendation:

 



I recommend approval of the Interlocal Agreement between the City of Riviera Beach and the School Board for Roadway, Traffic, and Public Safety Improvements as it relates to the new Suncoast High School and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.
