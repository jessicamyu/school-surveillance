Subject
BRD2 Inspector General Employment Agreement and DROP Date

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board discuss and take action, as necessary, to extend the Inspector General’s exit date of March 30, 2020 to May 30, 2020 including, but not limited to, amending the Inspector General’s Employment Agreement and approving forms to effectuate the DROP period extension, and authorize the Board Chair and Superintendent to sign any necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On December 11, 2019, the School Board initiated a search for a new Inspector General. The Inspector General’s Employment Agreement requires the Inspector General to provide 150 days notice if he intends to resign prior to the June 30, 2020 expiration date. Additionally, the current Inspector General’s DROP period ends on March 30, 2020. In effort to create a seamless process, it is recommended that the current Inspector General remain in the position until a new Inspector General is hired and is able to transition into the position.
