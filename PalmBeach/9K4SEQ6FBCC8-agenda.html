PL1 - South Florida East Coast Corridor (SFECC) Transit Analysis Study Workshop
Meeting
Aug 26, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT) District 4 is leading a regional partnership that is conducting the South Florida East Coast Corridor (SFECC) Transit Analysis Study. The study iincludes developing and analyzing alternatives that potentially integrate passenger and freight transport along the SFECC, which is centered along the existing FEC Railway. Phase 2 of the study will consider various alignments, station locations, and transit technologies.



The School District of Palm Beach County is an important section of the South Florida East Coast Corridor. With FEC tracks running through the County along its densest areas, the impacts of an integrated passenger and freight transport next to public schools will need to be discussed.  



The Treasure Coast Regional Planning Council (TCRPC) has been assisting FDOT by working with municipalities to alter their comprehensive plans to allow for higher densities next to potential FEC station area locations.  



This workshop is to present this item to the School Board for information and input.

P1 Medical Claims Adjudication With & Without Prescription Drug Coverage, RFP 09C-007K

Aug 26, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations

Discussion

P1 Approve the Request for Proposal (RFP) 09C-007K for Medical Claims Adjudication with and without Prescription Drug Coverage was conducted and evaluated by an evaluation committee consisting of District Personnel as well as union representatives.





  • Detailed re-pricing exercises were conducted to validate which carrier's contracts with providers would translate to the lowest claims costs for the School District.   The results indicate that Blue Cross Blue Shield of Florida will provide the lowest claims costs; in the range of $4.5 to $6 million per year. 

  • The committee compared administration costs, access to providers, disruption of current providers as well as discounts that the carrier has with these providers.

  • Disruption will be minimized as Blue Cross Blue Shield of Florida has agreed to contract with providers currently utilized by our employees, assuming the providers are willing to accept their reimbursement levels, meet their credentialing criteria and are in good standing from a quality and outcome perspective. BCBS agrees to performance guarantees in the amount of $500,000 for the outreach and provider enrollment process.

  • BCBS offers short term away from home care utilizing the National Blue Card Program for non-emergencies (emergencies are covered Nationwide).  This access is newly negotiated due to employee concerns.

  • Administration fees are based on a percentage of savings model with an annual maximum (for an enrollment of 21,648) not to exceed $7.143 million for the first two (2) years. The fees increase to a maximum of $7.4 million in years three (3) and four (4) and to a maximum of $7.66 million for year five (5). Performance guarantees on servicing issues have been negotiated.  BCBS will fund $200,000 annually to support wellness and other enrollment costs. Additionally 100% of all pharmacy rebates will be returned to the School District.

  • Blue Cross Blue Shield of Florida will administer COBRA for the district and will also include Dental & Vision COBRA services. Fees for ad hock, necessary reports and services will not exceed $100,000.

  • We have outsourced our disease management services to Health Dialogue since last year. BCBS also uses Health Dialogue for their disease management program, which is included in the admin fee. Therefore the district will be able to cancel our contract with Health Dialogue; an annual savings of $870,000.

  • BCBS has contracted with Health Designs, Inc., a certified MWBE vendor for its Biometric screenings at an estimated spend of $250,000-$300,000 per year.

  • Our consultant, Edify has assisted in every step of this RFP process and fully supports this proposal.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve the contract with Blue Cross Blue Shield of Florida to act as a servicing agent to administer the District's employee medical plan for a five year period from January 1, 2010 through December 31, 2014.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore, Chief Operating Officer (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Dianne Howard, Director Risk and Benefits Management

Financial Impact:

The total annual expenditure for the medical plan is approximately $150 million, with $120 million paid by the School District and the remaining amount funded by employees and retirees. The source of funds is the internal services fund.
