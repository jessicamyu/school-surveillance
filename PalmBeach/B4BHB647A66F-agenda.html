Subject
FMPF2 Award of Bid 19C-803T for Data Communications Products and Services
Meeting
Oct 17, 2018 - 7. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the award of Bid 19C-803T, an alternate source contract off State of Florida 43220000-WSCA-14-ACS, for the purchase of Data Communications Products and Services, with Avaya, Inc., an awarded contractor. Avaya, Inc. utilizes a network of authorized resellers, which includes AT&T Corp, to source products and services on their behalf. 

 

The current Avaya/AT&T system is a Private Branch System (PBX) that was installed in 2004.  At that time, the PBX was state of the art, with an expected ten-year life expectancy.  Technology has significantly evolved over the past fourteen years.  The upgrade project will transition the District to the new Session Initiation Protocol (SIP). The upgrade will cause minimal disruption to the District and provide a cost avoidance by upgrading the current system rather than installing a completely new system. In addition, by switching to SIP trunking from the current Primary Rate Interface (PRI) trunking the District will realize an annual cost savings of approximately $504,000 and provide a cost avoidance on long distance billing of approximately $66,000 per year.

 

Most of the current phones, equipment and software used within the District have been deemed End of life and this poses a greater risk of catastrophic failure which could cost hundreds of thousands of dollars to recover from and a major phone system outage would disrupt communications throughout the District and impact our ability to contact first responders in an emergency situation.

 

The Contract associated with this bid will be used for the upgrade project of the District’s telephone system, including voice mail and Message Manager, which provides for large conference calls for the district as well as classes for homebound students. 

 

This upgrade project will take place over the next three years.  It will be done in multiple phases, as outlined below.  The timing of each phase varies; however, some phases may overlap one another. 
 

Phase I:       Upgrade the software and backbone infrastructure

Phase II:      Run parallel systems and testing

Phase III:     Site-by-Site evaluation and upgrade of peripheral equipment

Phase IV:     Training

Phase V:      District migration to new system

DESCRIPTION:

Lisa Harris is a 2nd grade teacher at North Grade Elementary School in Lake Worth. She recently received a grant from the Social, Emotional Learning (SEL) Innovation Fund. The Innovation Fund launched in 2016, and over the past three years it has given more than $2 million to teachers and districts. Ms. Harris submitted an application to the SEL Innovation Fund for a program she developed in her classroom called Improv to Improve. The grant is to help Ms. Harris expand her program to reach more students in her school and the District. Improv to Improve (i2i) seeks to bring improvisational exercises into classrooms as a means to teach social and emotional competencies to students.

 

The implementation of these games in the classroom enables teachers to explicitly teach children an emotional vocabulary, how to appropriately respond to one’s own emotional condition and be empathetic towards the feelings of others, and to hone speaking and listening skills. The games foster a positive community environment of learning and fun. The grant has enabled Mrs. Harris to teach the program to the teachers at North Grade Elementary, and she hopes to be able to bring Improv to Improve to more schools.

The Everglades Champion Schools Recognition Program (ECSRP) recognizes schools as critical partners in Everglades’ conservation. The goal of the program is to support and celebrate schools that make Everglades literacy a core part of their learning culture and that engage and empower students as the future stewards of the River of Grass. The ECSRP encourages schools to develop Everglades initiatives in five key areas:

 

• Professional development

• Curriculum integration

• Inquiry and project-based learning

• School leadership

• Innovation

 

Participating schools document their activities in each area and submit an application at the end of the school year to earn points towards recognition as a Bronze, Silver, or Gold-level Everglades Champion School. Galaxy Elementary and Freedom Shores Elementary each achieved Silver Level in the ECSRP. Meeting
Oct 17, 2018 - 7. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve receipt of the 2018-2021 competitive Title IX, Part A - Education of Homeless Children and Youth Project funds in the cumulative amount of $374,064 ($124,688 per year/three years) effective immediately, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION:

The Title IX, Part A - Education for Homeless Children and Youth Project 2018-2021 funds help to provide services that comply with the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act. The services facilitate the enrollment, attendance, and success in school of children and youth experiencing homelessness. Services to be provided include:

  • Identification of homeless children and youth through communication with schools, shelters, community agencies, and larger community;
     
  • Removal of barriers to school enrollment for homeless children, including the coordination of transportation to maintain educational stability;
     
  • Referral to appropriate social service agencies in order to receive additional services, including summer camp scholarships, child care, medical, food, and housing assistance;
     
  • Homelessness awareness training for school-based and community service personnel;
     
  • Referral to School Based Teams;
     
  • Provision of school supplies and uniforms to homeless students; and
     
  • Academic tutoring for students residing in local homeless and domestic violence shelters.

State Board of Education Rule 6A-5.071 states that:

  • Each district school board shall develop and maintain a Master Inservice Plan for all district employees based on state adopted standards for high quality professional development as required under Section 1012.98, F.S.
     
  • The Master Inservice Plan shall be updated annually and approved each year by the district school board. By October 1 of each year, a letter verifying that the district school board has approved the master inservice plan and that the plan meets the criteria pursuant to this rule shall be sent to the Commissioner.
     
  • Each component shall remain in the plan for a period of at least five (5) years.
     
  • Inservice points awarded for successful completion of a component shall be assigned as follows: One (1) inservice point shall be equivalent to one (1) clock hour of participation, or as specified by the master inservice plan based on competency(ies) demonstrated.

The Master Inservice Plan is the vehicle through which instructional personnel earn inservice points toward recertification of their Florida teaching certificate. The Plan must contain all training components leading to certification. Instructional personnel must obtain 120 inservice points during their 5-year validity period. Non-Instructional personnel, affiliated with certain employee groups, use inservice points toward incentive programs and to verify completion of required training(s).

IG4 Audit of the Competitive Solicitations Process (For Projects Funded by the One-Penny Sales Tax [of which half goes to District])
Meeting
Oct 17, 2018 - 7. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
17. New Business
Type
Action
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board receive and file the Audit of the Competitive Solicitations Process (For Projects Funded by the One-Penny Sales Tax [of which half goes to District]) Report# 2018-10.

DESCRIPTION:
Pursuant to the Office of Inspector General 2017-18 Work Plan, the OIG has audited the competitive solicitation and award process for six selected projects funded by the one-percent local sales tax. The audit objective was to evaluate District compliance with State Statutes, State Board of Education Rules, and School Board Policies, procedures, and guidelines that promote competition, transparency, and accountability in solicitation, selection, and award of contracts. The audit produced the following major conclusions:

 

1. Solicitation and Award Process Compliant with 12 Key Attributes Required by Chapter 287.055 F.S.
Section 119,0701 F.S. Florida Administrative Code 6A-1.012, SREF (Sect 4), School Board Policy 6.14, and the Purchasing Manual. The OIG reviewed six selected contract awards funded with local sales tax proceeds, and evaluated the competitive solicitation and award processes. The OIG concluded that the solicitation and award processes for the six selected contracts were substantially in compliance with the 12 key attributes required. These 12 attributes included:

  • Adequate public notice/advertisement of solicitation;
  • Proposals requested from three or more sources;
  • Bid tabulations supported the contract award; and
  • Participation of Minority Women Business Enterprises (M/WBE) or Small Business Enterprises (SBE), etc.

2. Unbalanced-Bid Noted in One Invitation to Bid (ITB). 
Review of the awarded contract for chain link fencing (Bid# 17C-50W) revealed unusually low prices for many bid items.  The vendor's proposal includes 152 different types of fencing/hardware.  OIG noted 43 (or 28%) of the 152 items were significantly under-quoted at $1.00 per unit. When the unusually under-quoted items are not ordered by the School District, the overall total price of the vendor's proposal may not be as low as presented. This practice undermines the competitive solicitation process and will not result in the lowest overall cost to the District. This practice would also discourage other legitimate vendors from submitting competing bids. This was confirmed by three vendors in the area who did not submit their bids because of the under-quoted prices by the one vendor.  

 

Also, OIG noted the ITB stated the need for a primary and a secondary vendor; but Purchasing awarded the contract to the one vendor rather than re-advertising the bid to ensure a secondary vendor was available to meet the fencing needs of the District.

 

Unusually low prices (i.e. $1.00 per unit quoted for fencing) is an unbalanced bid and should be re-evaluated further in depth by staff before contract is awarded. 

 

3. Evaluation Committee Meeting Not Properly Noticed. 
On November 1 and 2, 2017, the Evaluation Committee Meetings for ITN 18C-010J-Underwriting Services were held without providing reasonable public notice as required by Florida Statue 286.011, the Sunshine Law. The November 1, 2017 Evaluation Committee meeting was not noticed; and approximately a one-hour notice was provided in advance of the November 2 meeting.  The Sunshine Law requires the public to receive "reasonable notice of all such meetings."

 

At the August 27, 2018 Audit Committee Meeting, the Committee reviewed and recommended approval of Audit Report# 2018-10 Audit of the Competitive Solicitations Process (For Projects Funded by the One-Penny Sales Tax [of which half goes to District]).
