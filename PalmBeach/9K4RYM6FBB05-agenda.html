Personnel Disciplinary Action - Diana Castillo
Meeting
May 28, 2008 - Spec Mtg re Disciplinary Actions
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Discussion

On or about January 16, 2008, Diana Castillo, Bus Driver, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of misuse of District time, technologies, facilities, and/or property, failure to exercise best professional judgment, failure to maintain and/or provide for the safety of students, and misconduct to include a careless disregard for policy, directive and/or rule.  Furthermore, the allegations included potential violations of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff, and/or School Board Policy 3.21, Safe Operation of District School Buses, and/or Section VI of the School Bus Drivers and Bus Attendants Handbook entitled Procedures for School Buses and School Bus Drivers at All Railroad Crossings, and/or Chapter 316.1575 Florida Statutes, Obedience to traffic control devices at railroad-highway grade crossing, and/or Chapter 316.159 Florida Statutes, Certain vehicles to stop at all railroad grade crossings, and/or Section 10.4.3 of the Florida Department of Transportation CDL Handbook entitled School Bus, Recommended Procedures.



On or about November 27, 2007, Ms. Castillo was involved in an incident during her bus route.  While she was supervising students and driving her bus, she failed to adhere to required procedures and properly stop at a railroad crossing.  The incident resulted in the railroad-crossing gate striking the rear roof of the bus as Ms. Castillo drove over the railroad tracks.  Through video evidence, the investigation revealed audible confirmation of the crossing arm striking the bus followed by the train whistle 23 seconds later.  Passengers on the bus included three wheelchair bound students, two bus attendants and a Transportation Supervisor.  Another District bus driver while driving behind Ms. Castillo's bus additionally witnessed the incident.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Diana Castillo's suspension without pay, effective May 29, 2008 and Termination, effective fifteen (15) days after the May 28, 2008, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Chief James P. Kelly; Angelette Green

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District

On or about January 22, 2008, Trecia North became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegations of gross insubordination.  Furthermore, the allegations included potential violations of the Collective Bargaining Agreement, CTA Article II, Sec M (6), Rights and Responsibilities, Discipline of Employees (Progressive Discipline), and Florida Statute Title XLVIII, K-20 Education Code 1012.33, (1) (a), Contracts with instructional staff, supervisors and school principals.



Ms. North has been given Written Directives on 9/27/06, 10/5/07 and Specific Memorandums of Incident on 8/22/06, 11/21/06, 3/16/07, 3/29/07, 4/27/07, 11/9/07 and 1/26/07 by school-based administrators directing her to follow policies and procedures to ensure an efficient operation of the school center.  Specifically, Ms. North was directed to meet all deadlines for submitting grades, report cards, lesson plans, split coverage plans and emergency lesson plans, to follow proper procedures as outlined in the CTA Agreement and Faculty Handbook for reporting absences, and to follow proper procedures for School-Based Team Meetings.



Ms. North failed to follow the administrative directives, which is considered an act of insubordination.  Ms. North received Verbal Reprimands with Written Notation on 1/4/07 and 3/22/07 for failure to follow procedures in reporting absences and failure to submit report cards and honor roll on time; the first step of Progressive Discipline.



On 6/4/07, disciplinary action was taken for insubordination:  failure to follow procedures to report absences, failure to submit report cards and honor roll by the given deadlines.  Ms. North received a Written Reprimand; step two of Progressive Discipline.  Ms. North continued with this patter of behavior.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Trecia North's ten (10) day suspension without pay, effective May 29, 2008 at the May 28, 2008, Board Meeting, subject only to appeal under Section 120.68, Florida Statutes, unless a timely request for an administrative hearing is made within the fifteen (15) days stated herein pursuant to payment of salary and benefits until final School Board disposition is entered to the administrative hearing proceedings.

Contact:

Ann Killets; Chief James P. Kelly; Angelette Green

Financial Impact:

There is no financial impact to the School District.
