I recommend the School Board approve the Literacy AmeriCorps Palm Beach County Cooperative Agreement and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents and contracts.

DESCRIPTION:

This is a continuation of the Literacy AmeriCorps Palm Beach County Cooperative Agreement.

 

Each AmeriCorps Graduation Coach will serve at-risk 9th-12th grade students to: build sustained relationships through mentoring; assist in removing barriers to their academic success through data monitoring, tutoring, consulting with teachers, school counselors and administrators; present career and postsecondary options; and assist them in completing the FAFSA, college applications and scholarship applications.  

 

 

Each AmeriCorps Reading Coach will tutor (and assess) eight students daily in one-on-one 30 minute sessions.  They will also meet regularly with classroom teachers to identify areas of specific weakness and to communicate regarding students' academic status and progress.

 

This Agreement provides for five (5) AmeriCorps members who will serve as graduation coaches at the following high schools:

o   Boynton Beach Community High School

o   Forest Hill Community High School

o   John I. Leonard High School

o   Lake Worth Community High School

o   Village Academy

 

The Agreement also provides for seven (7) full-time and two (2) part-time AmeriCorps members who will serve as reading tutors at the following elementary schools:

o   Forest Park Elementary

o   Galaxy Elementary

o   Pine Grove Elementary

o   Rolling Green Elementary

 

Please note, elementary schools were chosen based on the number of students reading at level 1 and proximity to AmeriCorps housing.

 

Through the Johnson Scholars Foundation, the Agreement provides for one (1) AmeriCorps member who will serve as a mentor/coach at the following high schools:

o   Lake Worth Community High School

o   Palm Beach Gardens High School

o   Palm Beach Lakes High School

o   Santaluces High School

o   William T. Dwyer High School

 

The required funding match for graduation coaches is paid through a grant with The Education Foundation in the amount of $28,809.  The funding match for the reading tutors is paid through the K-12 Research-Based Comprehensive Reading Plan (CRRP) in the amount of $46,398. The funding match for the Johnson Scholars coach is paid through the Johnson Scholarship Foundation in the amount of $5,799.

I recommend the School Board approve the District's Florida Educational Equity Act 2012-2013 Annual Update for submission to the Florida Department of Education.

DESCRIPTION:

The Florida Educational Equity Act, Florida Statutes, Section 1000.05, became law in June 1984 and the State Board of Education adopted the implementing rules in February 1985.

 

Each year the Commissioner of Education designates the areas of concentration under the requirements of the Educational Equity Act.

 

The 2012-2013 Plan Update is used to evaluate the success of methods and strategies to reduce gaps in student performance and to increase participation of underrepresented groups by race/ethnicity and gender in upper level courses.

 

Advanced Placement  The evaluation reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Advanced Placement courses for Black and Hispanic students in grades 9-12th.  Black students increased by 7 percentage points, from 9% in 2008-09 to 16% in 2012-13; Hispanic students also increased by 9 percentage points, from 18% to 27% for the same time period.  The evaluation further reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Advanced Placement courses for Black and Hispanic Males in grades 9-12th.  Black Males increased by 7 percentage points, from 6% in 2008-09 to 13% in 2012-13; Hispanic Males also increased by 8 percentage points, from 15% to 23% for the same time period.

 

Dual Enrollment  The evaluation reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Dual Enrollment courses for Black Males, Hispanic Males, and overall Hispanic students in grades 9-12.  There was a 0% change in overall Black student enrollment from 1% in 2008-09 to 1% in 2012-13; Hispanic students increased by 1 percentage points from 1% to 2% for the same time period.  In addition the evaluation reveals that Black Males and Hispanic Males met the target by increasing enrollment in Dual Enrollment courses 1 percentage point and 2 percentage points respectively from 2008-2009 to 2012-2013.  To align with State Statute, beginning in January 2012, students had to be “College Ready” in all subject areas in order to be eligible for Dual Enrollment.  Prior to this change, a student only needed to be “college ready” in the area in which they were dual enrolling.

 

Honors Courses  The evaluation reveals progress in increasing enrollment in Honors courses for Black and Hispanic students in grades 9-12th.  Black students increased by 11 percentage points, from 29% in 2008-09 to 40% in 2012-13; Hispanic students also increased by 12 percentage points from 36% to 48% for the same time period.  In addition, Black Males (11 percentage point increase) and Hispanic Males (10 percentage point increase) both increased enrollment in Honors courses from 2008-2009 to 2012-2013.

 

Level 3 Courses  The evaluation reveals that the data provided for the 2012-2013 enrollment in Level 3 courses is not the same as in prior years.  The reporting requirements have changed for the 2012-2013 report.  The prior report consisted of enrollment data on “all Level 3 Courses” for a 5 year period.  This year's report is only asking for data on the “other Level 3 Courses.”  Therefore, the data provided does not allow for a 5 year comparison or a 1 year comparison.

 

Gender Equity in Athletics  The review reveals that the School District of Palm Beach County is in compliance with the requirements of Gender Equity in Athletics.  A continuation of previous strategies will be in place for the 2013-2014 school year.

 

Employment Equity  The review reveals that as a result of this intense focus and creative new strategies we have increased our hiring of diverse  candidates as follows:   Diverse teacher hires have increased 35% from a total of 411 in FY2011 to 553 thus far in FY2013.  Percentage of diverse District Administrators hired (Principals, AP’s, and District Staff) has increased from 37% in FY2011 to 43% year to date in FY2013.  These hiring efforts result in the  District currently having a population of 28% of teachers and 36% of District Administrators (Principals, AP’s and District Staff) of diverse race/ethnicity.

 

Progress monitoring of the above listed sections will occur monthly in the Academic Leadership Meetings.
