PS 2 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Levelt Julien
Meeting
Sep 29, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

In January 2010, Levelt Julien, custodian at Atlantic High School, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based on allegations of misconduct-willful disregard for policy/directive/rule and gross insubordination.  Levelt Julien's allegations also include potential violation of School Board Policy 1.013(1) Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff.



Mr. Julien has been grossly insubordinate and refused to follow the directives of his direct supervisor and the Assistant Principal of the school.  Mr. Julien's acts of insubordination include the following:





  • November 2009, he refused to disinfect every desk as requested by his supervisor, the head custodian.  Instead, he argued that he was doing his job and did not have to disinfect every desk.

  • December 18, 2009, Mr. Julien refused a reasonable request of the head custodian to remove the trash from a school building.  Rather, he left without approval and without completing his assigned duties.

  • March 10, 2010, Mr. Julien became irrate and insubordinate when he demanded that the custodian receiving door be opened during break and used excessive profanity in referring to his supervisor as witnessed by other employees.

  • March 11, 2010, Mr. Julien refused to attend a daily five minute staff meeting despite being advised by the Assistant Principal the day before that he must attend all future staff meetings.

  • March 12, 2010, despite being told by his supervisor and the Assistant Principal to clean the restrooms outside of the cafeteria, Mr. Julien was defiant and stated someone else should clean the restrooms.


Mr. Julien has been previously disciplined for insubordination.  On October 6, 2009, Mr. Julien received a Verbal Reprimand with Written Notation for acts of insubordination - to include reading a book in the classroom instead of completing assigned tasks and refusing the head custodian's directive to remove trash from a building.  He also received a Written Reprimand on November 12, 2009 for refusing to follow his supervisor's directive to more thoroughly clean a classroom.



There is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations and to support the termination.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Levelt Julien's suspension without pay, effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Levelt Julien's employment effective October 15, 2010 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

AC2 - FY2011 SpringBoard Subscription Agreement
Meeting
Sep 29, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

SpringBoard is the College Board's official Pre-Advanced Placement curriculum.



When implemented with fidelity, the SpringBoard curriculum provides scaffolded and recursive instructional units enabling students to build upon what they know, grow in confidence, and successfully complete the instructional materials that are based on the rigorous College Board Standards for College Success.



The teaching methods used in SpringBoard® ensure that research-based best practices are guiding the use of rigorous, high-interest instructional materials in the classroom. Student-centered classrooms focus on the needs, abilities and active participation of students.
By using the learning strategies in the SpringBoard® curriculum, teachers provide differentiated instruction for students of varied learning abilities and learning styles, giving them access to a challenging curriculum.



The SpringBoard curriculum is back-mapped from grades twelve to six to address the skills and competencies students need to become proficient.  These components support moving lower performing students toward proficiency and providing acceleration for others. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the FY2011 SpringBoard Subscription Agreement with the College Board in the amount of $213,019.27 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation of the subscription activities.

PS 4 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Sherron Brown
Meeting
Sep 29, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action

In April of 2010, Sherron Brown, Assistant Food Service I of Palm Beach Gardens Community High School, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based on allegations of misuse of District funds, failure to exercise best professional judgment and misconduct-willful disregard for policy/directive/rule.  Sherron Brown's allegations also include potential violations of School Board Policy 1.013(1) Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff.



A two (2) day surveillance of Ms. Brown's cash register transactions was initiated based upon a report of suspected misappropriation of funds.  This computerized surveillance allowed for the real time tracking of transactions identifying the student making the purchase, the amount of the purchase (when not voided) and the time of the purchase.  Based upon the surveillance records, Ms. Brown had a high number of purchases in which the student pin number was entered but the transaction was "cleared" or "voided", as if the purchase never occurred.  Some of the students, who were identified through this surveillance process, were interviewed and stated that they made a cash purchase of items from the cafeteria on the noted dates and times where "voids" or "cleared" transactions appeared.  A review of Ms. Brown's daily activity shows an extraordinary amount of these no sale transactions.  A prior incident in 2005 by Ms. Brown was reported to School Police and resulted in conferencing from her Principal regarding the proper handling of money at the register.



Based upon substantial information gathered during the investigation, there is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations and to support the suspension.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Sherron Brown's suspension without pay, effective October 15, 2010 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.
