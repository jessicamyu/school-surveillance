G1 - Title I Migrant, Part C - Improving Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged
Meeting
Dec 9, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The Migrant Education Program is designed to identify and recruit all migratory children, age 3 - 21, and provide supplementary educational and support services to meet their educational needs in a coordinated and efficient manner.



Specifically, the grant supports schools with high concentrations of migrant students to ensure that migratory children receive full and appropriate opportunities to meet challenging State academic content and student achievement standards that all children are expected to meet.  Additionally, the program is designed to help migratory students overcome educational disruptions, cultural and language barriers, social isolation, various health-related problems, and other factors that inhibit the ability of such children to do well in school.



Evaluation of the program indicated that 3616 students were identified and served in FY09.  Data indicates they lag significantly behind same-age peers on FCAT and in graduating high school.  Therefore, the program focus has been changed to provide educational tutoring and support.  Guidance Counselors have been hired to assist with graduation readiness and post-graduation goals.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the Board approve the application for the 2009-2010 Title I Migrant, Part C - Improving Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged grant to the Florida Department of Education in the amount of $2,673,246, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

Jeffrey J. Hernandez, (jffrey.hernandez@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the FY2010 Special Revenue - Other Funds Budget is an increase of $2,673,246.  These funds will be placed in the Department of Federal and State Programs.  All grant related expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of the revenue.
