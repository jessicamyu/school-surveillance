PS 1 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Rovielle Elmond
Meeting
Nov 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action

In September of 2009, Rovielle Elmond became the subject of a School Police investigation following after a special audit was conducted by the District's Auditors Office of Morikami Park Elementary School records covering December 19, 2007 through June 30, 2009.  The special audit indicated that there was falsification of revenue records, and removal of cash which was substituted by unrecorded checks and alteration of deposit tickets.  On May 4, 2010, Mr. Elmond became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon allegations of ethical misconduct, unprofessional conduct, misuse of District time, property and resources, falsification of documents, violation of internal accounts and funds, as well as violation of School Board Policy 1.013, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff.



During the investigation, Mr. Elmond admitted, among other things, that he wrote a check for $4,599.90 to the school to obtain cash to purchase an automobile and that he wrote personal checks to replace missing money and make School Aged Child Care payments for his children.  Mr. Elmond indicated that no one ever advised him that he could not substitute his personal checks for cash from the District funds.  In essence, Mr. Elmond utilized the school funds as an ATM.



There is sufficient evidence to substantiate the above allegations and to support the termination.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Rovielle Elmond's suspension without pay effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Rovielle Elmond's employment effective November 19, 2010 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

AC1 - 2010-2011 Agreement with PBC Commissioners for Head Start Delegate Agency
Meeting
Nov 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

This Agreement will provide funding for Pre-Kindergarten services for 185 children, 3 and 4 years of age, from low-income families.  The Head Start Delegate Agency agreement provides funding for: personnel, curricular materials, supplies, equipment, technology, student field trips, staff development (including travel/registration), parent involvement activities, and food for eligible students.  In addition, it provides funding for medical, dental and mental health care for children and families in need of services.



Palm Beach County Commissioners Office, the grantor, audits program performance four times during the contract year based on the Head Start Program Performance Standards.  The Head Start Program Principal and Director continuously monitor attendance, medical, dental, family social services, and student progression.  The Federal Head Start Program monitors the program through a Federal Audit every three years.



Summer VPK 4-year-olds are screened for Kindergarten readiness and reported yearly on the FLDOE Kindergarten Readiness Rate Report.  Over the last three years, on average, more than 90% of the Summer 4-year-old VPK students have been ready for Kindergarten as shown on the ECHOS Assessment of the Readiness Rate Report.



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in the particular case, due to a delay in the receipt of the agreement from Head Start.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.



 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the renewal of the Agreement with the Palm Beach County Board of County Commissioners for continuation of the Head Start Delegate Agency at the Delray Full Service Center in the amount of $1,157,791, effective October 1, 2010 to September 30, 2011, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

CS1 - My Choice Academy New Application
Meeting
Nov 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)
The Department of Charter Schools received an application, on August 2, 2010, from My Choice Academy ("My Choice") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2011. The application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A"). The instrument and the application process were explained to the applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop that was held on May 27, 2010 (see Exhibit "B"). The District Reviewers were trained on the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Application Process on May 13, 2010 (see Exhibit "C"). The Reviewers evaluated the 20 sections of the written application. Three of the 20 sections were rated "Partially Meets the Standard or Did Not Meet the Standard," meaning the responses addressed most of the criteria, but lacked meaningful detail and required important additional information (see Exhibit "D"). Partially Meets the Standard: Section 7 - English Language Learners Did Not Meet the Standard: Section 9 - Governance Section 18 - Budget The responses to each section that did not meet the stated criteria served as the basis of inquiry for the interview. Please refer to Step 5: Applicant Interview (see Exhibit "E"). Step 6, second paragraph in the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process states, "The sponsoring board will only approve applicants that demonstrate quality in all components of the application process. An application cannot have significant weaknesses in some components of the application and still be judged to have met the standard for approval," (see Exhibit "F"). Each applicant received communication that outlined the proposed recommendation, the evaluators' second review following the interview, summary of the interview, and was granted the option to voluntarily withdraw the application or allow the application to be placed on the Board agenda for action (see Exhibit "G"). Pursuant to F.S.1002.33 (5)(c): "An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than 30 calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ." A copy of My Choice's application is in the Board Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of My Choice Academy and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

PS 2 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Lesley Reed

Nov 3, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics

On or about January 2010, Ms. Reed became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based upon the allegation of gross insubordination.  Furthermore, allegations include potential violations of School Board Policy 1.013(1) & (4), Responsibilities of School District Personnel, Florida Administrative Code, Department of Education 6B-1.001(2), Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida, Florida Administrative Code, Department of Education 6B-4.009(4), Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal and the Collective Bargaining Agreement CTA Article III, Sec. E(3), Working Conditions.



On March 13, 2009, Ms. Reed received a Verbal Reprimand with Written Notation for insubordination for failure to comply with directives to complete lesson plans.  On May 1, 2009, Ms. Reed received a Written Reprimand for insubordination for failure to complete and submit lesson plans as directed.  On September 23, 2009, Ms. Reed received a Written Reprimand for gross insubordination for not completing and submitting lesson plans in the 2009/2010 school year, to include February, March and April 2010.  Ms. Reed's failure to submit lesson plans posed a negative impact on the academic performance of students as lesson plans help guide the teacher, providing purpose, structure and organization within the classroom.



Lesley Reed's constant and continuous intentional refusal to obey verbal and written directives constitutes gross insubordination.  The direct orders have been reasonable in nature and have been given by the proper authority.  The school administration has done due diligence in documenting Ms. Reed's consistent disregard for policy/directive and rules.  The school administration has further attempted to correct the behavior through progressive discipline for insubordination.  Progressive discipline, implemented to date, has failed.  Ms. Reed continues her gross and willful disregard of policy, directives and rules, which constitute Gross Insubordination.



There is clear and convincing evidence to substantiate the above allegations and to support the five (5) day suspension.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Lesley Reed's five (5) day suspension without pay, effective November 29, 2010, subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.
