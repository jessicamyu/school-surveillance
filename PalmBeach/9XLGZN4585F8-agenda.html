Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the financial award of additional grant funds from Lost Tree Village to four Riviera Beach Elementary Schools.

DESCRIPTION:

The "Transforming Teachers to Grow Great Readers" grant focuses on literacy instruction and student learning in the area of literacy.  The grant was initiated with the kindergarten classes at Washington and West Riviera Elementary Schools in FY14. The grant expanded in FY15 (Year 2) to include both kindergarten and first grade classes at Washington and West Riviera Elementary as well as kindergarten classes at Lincoln and Dr. Mary McLeod Bethune Elementary. The grant now serves all of the elementary schools in Riviera Beach. In FY16 (Year 3), the grant will include kindergarten through second grade classes at Washington at West Riviera Elementary and kindergarten and first grade classes at Lincoln and Dr. Mary McLeod Bethune Elementary schools.

 

The grant is for a total of $3 million (including this increase) and will provide additional services to Lincoln Elementary, Washington Elementary, West Riviera Beach Elementary, and Dr. Mary McLeod Bethune Elementary Schools in Riviera Beach.

I recommend the Board approve the renewal of the Memorandum of Understanding with the City of Riviera Beach, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This is a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between the City of Riviera Beach and the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida for the City to run a satellite site for North Tech Education Center.  This is a renewal of the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the City of Riviera Beach that the School Board approved on January 21, 2015.

 

The City of Rivera Beach Youth Empowerment Center serves as a Satellite Site for North Tech Education Center in Riviera Beach, Florida.  The Youth Empowerment Center reaches out to the community to serve the educational needs of adults.  This is a daytime program that helps accommodate the needs of adult learners in Riviera Beach.  The City of Riviera Beach will provide classroom space and city staff to assist in the operation of the site. Riviera Youth Empowerment Center will sponsor up to 30 students per term during school year 2015-2016.  The School District will provide textbooks, equipment, supplies, and instructors for eligible GED students as well as support and staff training and other assistance as delineated by the MOU.

DESCRIPTION:

This Board Item contains the annual agreement with the Health Care District of Palm Beach County (HCD) to fund nursing coverage in all regular education schools within our District.  The HCD agrees to place one nurse in every regular school within our District schools. Currently, they provide the placement and administrative support of a registered nurse in 162 schools.

 

The original Agreement with the HCD in 2001 initiated our support to the school nurse program by funding nursing services in 34 schools built after 2001 and contributing to the cost of basic health supplies for all schools.
