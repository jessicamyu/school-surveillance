G1 - High School Graduation Initiative Project
Meeting
Oct 13, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

The High School Graduation Initiative provides funding to reduce dropout rates in schools that exceeded the state average dropout rates from 2007-2008 according to the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES). The grant requires effective, research-based programs for youth at risk of dropping out and for those who have already dropped out. Four high schools with dropout rates that exceeded the state average of 3.3% are targeted to receive the My Grad Plan. The schools are: Forest Hill, John I. Leonard, Palm Beach Lakes, and Boynton Beach Community High Schools. These four schools were the only schools in our District that met the narrow eligibility requirements in the grant application. The purpose of the project is to graduate students college and/or career ready. The goals of the project are 1) to increase the graduation rates at each of the four project high schools and 2) to reduce the dropout rate at these schools.



The project's effective early identification, prevention, and intervention research-based strategies include use of data for evaluation/redirection; individualized personal connection with adult advocates; targeted academic support; career and workplace instruction; and behavioral, social and emotional supports.



The School District of Palm Beach County is the lead agent for this grant.  My Grad Plan is a collaboration between the School District, Palm Beach State College, Workforce Alliance, Take Stock in Children, Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID), and the Community Partnership Group.  The partners provide workforce instruction, college preparation, mentoring, summer transition, and other services.



The impact of the project will be measured in increased graduation rates at the schools and decreased dropout rates.



A copy of the grant is in the Board Office for review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the receipt of  the High School Graduation Initiative Project from  the U.S. Department of Education for up to $9,700,000 from October 1, 2010 through September 30, 2015, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the Special Revenue-Other Funds Budget is an increase of up to $9,700,000 over five years.  All project related expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue. There is no financial impact to the Operating Budget.

AC1 - Cooperative Agreement with Carlton Palms Educational Center
Meeting
Oct 13, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Pursuant Commissioner's Order No. DOE-2007-1384-FOI/Corrective Action, the School District is required to provide educational services for one (1) student parentally placed in a private residential facility. For the past three years, that parental placement has been Threshold Center for Autism and the school district has been funding the educational placement at a cost of $55,688 and the Agency for Persons with Disabilities has been funding the residential placement.  The parent has agreed to place their child in a different facility, Carlton Palms Educational Center and the district will pay for days the student is physically present and in attendance at the facility through the earned FTE of $18,609.32.  The total cost of educational services will be $14,632.80.



Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice,  it was necessary in this particular case, due to the parent agreement to change facilities and a spot becoming available.  It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.



This cooperative agreement is in compliance with State Board Rule 6A.6-0361, "Special Programs Contractual Agreements with Non-Public Schools."

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the cooperative agreement with Carlton Palms Educational Center in the amount of $14,632.80 for the educational cost of one student with disabilities from September 16, 2010 through June 30, 2011 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to these agreements.

The financial impact to the School District's general operating fund is $14,632.80.  The source of funds is the Exceptional Student Education Department budget.
