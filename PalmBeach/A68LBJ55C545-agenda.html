Subject
PS1 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Debra Turnbull
Meeting
Jan 27, 2016 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Debra Turnbull's five-day suspension without pay effective Thursday, February 18, 2016, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S., sec. 120.57, F.S., and the CTA Collective Bargaining Agreement, Article VII.

DESCRIPTION:

On May 4, 2015, Debra Turnbull became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of policy violations related to Bullying of Students, Failure to Protect Student from Conditions Harmful to Learning, Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment, Ethical Misconduct, and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.  

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies  0.01(2)(c) and (2)(d), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; 3.02(4)(a), (4)(d), (4)(e), (4)(f), (4)(h), and (4)(j), Code of Ethics; 5.002(3)(a), Anti-Bullying and Harassment, Expectations; 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 6A-5.056(4), F.A.C., Criteria for Suspension and Dismissal of Employees – Gross Insubordination; 6A-10.080(1),(2), and (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; 6A-10.081(3)(a), and (3)(e), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida.

 

There is “just cause” to support the suspension of Debra Turnbull. 

 

On January 6, 2016, the Superintendent’s “Notice of Five-Day Suspension Without Pay” was hand-delivered to Debra Turnbull, a copy of which is attached hereto.

PS2 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Louis Webber
Meeting
Jan 27, 2016 - 4. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
10. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Louis Webber's termination effective, Thursday, February 18, 2016, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S., sec. 120.57, F.S., and the SEIU/FPSU Collective Bargaining Agreement (Regular), Article 17, Section 8.

DESCRIPTION:

On September 9, 2015, Louis Webber became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of policy violations related to Unsafe Operation of a School Bus, Ethical Misconduct, Insubordination: Letter of Written Reprimand, March 11, 2011, and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule, Directive, or Statute.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.  

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.02(4)(a), (4)(b), (4)(d), (4)(f), (4)(h), and (4)(j), Code of Ethics; 3.02(5)(a)(vii), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment with the District; 3.21(3)(6) and (3)(9), and 3.21(4), Safe Operation of District School Buses; 3.29(8)(g)(a) and (8)(g)(f), Acceptable Use of Technology by Employees; 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 6A-3.0171(2)(3)(f), (2)(3)(I), and (2)(3)(r), F.A.C., Responsibilities of School Districts for Student Transportation – School Bus Operator; School Bus Driver and Bus Attendant’s Handbook, Chapter 1, Sec. 4(b), 4(c), 4(d), Operations;  School Bus Driver and Bus Attendant’s Handbook, Chapter 2, Sec. 11(b), Students; School Bus Driver and Bus Attendant’s Handbook, Chapter 3, Sec. 13(b), Performance and Best Practices (Carrying out Assigned Duties and Interference of Work Activities and Acts of Insubordination and Violations of other School Board Policies).

 

There is “just cause” to support Louis Webber’s termination of employment. 

 

On January 6, 2016, the Superintendent’s “Notice of Recommendation for Termination of Employment” was hand-delivered to Louis Webber, a copy of which is attached hereto.
