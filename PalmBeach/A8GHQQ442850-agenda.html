Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Julian Harrell's termination of employment effective, Thursday, April 28, 2016, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S., sec. 120.57, F.S., and the CTA Collective Bargaining Agreement, Article VII.

DESCRIPTION:

On September 23, 2015, Julian Harrell became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of policy violations related to Inappropriate Interaction with Student(s), Ethical Misconduct, Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment, and Gross Insubordination - Failure to Follow, Policy, Rule, or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 0.01(2)(3) and (2)(4), Commitment to the Student, Principle I; 3.02(4)(a), (4)(b), (4)(d), (4)(e), Code of Ethics; 3.02(5)(a)(i), (5)(a)(vi), (5)(a)(vii), and (5)(i), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment with the District; 5.001(11)(a), Protecting Students from Harassment and Discrimination; 5.002(c)(c), Anti-Bullying and Harassment; 1.013(1) and (4), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; 6A-5.056(2), F.A.C., Misconduct in the Office; 6A-10.080(2) and (3), F.A.C., Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; 6A-10.081(3)(a), (3)(e), and (3(g), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for the Education Profession in Florida.

 

There is "just cause" to support Julian Harrell's termination of employment.

 

On March 16, 2016, the Superintendent's "Notice of Recommendation for Termination of Employment" was hand-delivered to Julian Harrell, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend the Board approve the First Amendment to the Charter Contract with Palm Beach School for Autism, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

July 24, 2013, Palm Beach School for Autism was granted a five (5) year renewal Charter Contract to serve students in grades Pre-K-9, commencing July 1, 2013, and ending June 30, 2018. 

 

Palm Beach School for Autism has requested to expand the total student enrollment from up to 310 students to 400 students, beginning FY 16-17.  Section III, subsection (D), sub-subsection (1), sub-subsections (d-e) of the Amended Charter are replaced with the following:

 

          d.       Year 4:  (2016-2017)         Grades Pre-K up to 12 up to 400 students

          e.       Year 5:  (2017-2018)         Grades Pre-K up to 12 up to 400 students

 

The School District of Palm Beach County has investigated Palm Beach School for Autism’s request to expand the enrollment, reviewed its proposed budget and District staff is satisfied that Palm Beach School for Autism would be capable to meet the needs of the increased enrollment.  All other terms of the Amended Charter shall remain in full force and effect.

The Department of Charter Schools received an application from Florida High School for Accelerated Learning-West Palm Beach Campus 2 in August, 2015.  The application for Florida High School for Accelerated Learning-West Palm Beach Campus 2 was approved on October 7, 2015, as Item CS5 during a Special Board Meeting.

 

The Charter Contract for this charter school was reviewed and agreed upon by the appropriate District staff and negotiated by the General Counsel’s office.  The charter will become effective upon signing by both parties, and shall cover a term of five (5) years, commencing July 1, 2016, and ending June 30, 2021, except as provided in the charter. 

 

Florida High School for Accelerated Learning West Palm Beach Campus 2 will serve students in grades 9-12, ages 16-21 at risk. Their mission is, “To help at-risk students earn a standard high school diploma and prepare for postsecondary success."

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Interlocal Agreement among Palm Beach County, The School Board, and the Signatory Municipalities pertaining to the Shared Distribution and Use of the One Cent Local Government Infrastructure Surtax substantially in the form attached; delegate to the Superintendent the authority to finalize the Agreement; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On March 22, 2016, the Palm Beach County Board of County Commissioners voted to proceed with an Interlocal Agreement between Palm Beach County, The School Board and Signatory Municipalities to share distribution and use of a one cent local government infrastructure sales surtax.  The Interlocal Agreement specifies the proceeds will be split with 48% to the School Board, 33.5% to the County and 18.5% to municipalities.  With concurrence of the School Board and signatory municipalities, the County Commission is expected to take action on the Interlocal Agreement and related ordinance on April 19, 2016.
