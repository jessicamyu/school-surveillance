Subject
SDPBC Technology Plan 2010-2013
Meeting
Jun 9, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

A Technology Plan for the years 2010-2013 was developed to replace the current plan which will expire on June 30, 2010.  The new plan will be submitted to the Florida Department of Education (FLDOE) by June 30, 2010.   A FLDOE approved Technology Plan is essential for receiving Title 1 grants and E-Rate funds Universal Service Administrative Company.



The Technology Plan was developed with input from District Stakeholders including teachers, students, parents, community, technology specialists, and other end users.  A comprehensive requirements gathering process was used to collect information on the current state of technology, technology requirements for successful instruction, training, support, and efficient business processes.  Focus group sessions were held in the Districts four areas, providing all Stakeholders an opportunity to contribute to the technology vision.



The Districts technology objectives and priorities were derived from these findings, and the Technology Plan was developed to address the objectives and priorities.  The Technology Plan outlines a three year strategy for instructional technologies, the seamless integration of technology into the classroom, and the development of a robust infrastructure  that is required to support the growing needs.  The Plan defines training and support process improvements that will provide and timely support to schools and end users. The Plan also examines the strategy for replacing obsolete systems that are cost and resource intensive to maintain, as well as the integration of systems and applications to a common platform.  The District has adopted governance and best practices that will be further infused into all aspects of technology including planning, procurement, project execution, and change management.



The Technology Plan lays the foundation for the Districts technology initiatives for the next three years.  Technology projects and initiatives will be selected and implemented to address the priorities and objectives. Furthermore, criteria will be developed to ensure that initiatives are meeting the defined targets.



The three year initiatives listed on the last page are estimates as required by E-Rate for Federal reimbursements. In FY10, the District has received $2.5 million in Federal reimbursements and is projected to receive additional dollars for a total of $2.8 million for the year.  In  FY11 we expect to receive approximately $3.9 million in E-Rate reimbursements.



Attached are the draft PDF and the web link for the Technology Plan.



http://www.palmbeachschools.org/atr/ 



Please note the Technology Plan is pending Board and FLDOE approval. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Technology Plan for the years 2010-2013, to be submitted to FLDOE by June 30, 2010.
