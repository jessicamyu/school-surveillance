IN1 Approval to Purchase Master Builders Risk
Meeting
Mar 14, 2012 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action

The policy will cover damage to the property being built as well as theft of materials from the site during the construction phase for new and replacement schools/buildings. This policy covers all facilities started during the policy period and those currently still under construction.



 



The Master Builders Risk Policy eliminates the need to go directly to the contractor for a project-specific, stand-alone builders risk policy. Over the last three (3) years, the program saved 70 percent of contractor-purchased builders risk coverage.



 



The policy will have annual aggregate limits of $25 million subject to a sub-limit of $10 million for any one loss occurrence and $10 million aggregate in one policy year for a Named Windstorm. Named Windstorm also has a five (5) percent deductible (Self-Insured Retention) subject to a $250,000 minimum. However, because the District owns the policy rather than the contractor, the District will be eligible for FEMA reimbursements, for which a contractor would not be eligible.   



 



Due to an overall tightening property insurance market, we are experiencing a rate increase of 25% to the existing rate of .5766 per $100 of construction costs.



 



The deposit premium is based on anticipated new construction values of approximately $29 million to start in the policy year and approximately $90 million to be completed. Therefore, the premium will be approximately $472,000 for the current policy year of April 2012 to April 2013 subject to annual audit adjustments. In addition, there are fees and assessments including The State of Florida Assessment for the Florida Hurricane and Catastrophe Fund of approximately $6,000 for combined total premium of approximately $480,000.



 



The broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., will receive a commission of six (6) percent on this policy. In addition to replacing the insurance, they will be responsible for tracking all payments, any changes relevant to this coverage and all reconciliations at year-end.



 



 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the Board approve the purchase of the Master Builders Risk Insurance Policy with Lexington Insurance through the broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc., effective April 1, 2012 to April 1, 2013 at a premium cost of up to .7208 per $100 of construction costs, for a one-year deposit premium of $480,000 including assessments and taxes.

PR1-Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) Certification of Facilities Data
Meeting
Mar 14, 2012 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) 6.1(7) (c) requires that, "Prior to April 1 of each year, each district shall review the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) and shall certify to the Office of Educational Facilities that the inventory is current and accurate."




The Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) is available to all school districts for reporting facilities information.  District FISH reports are available on-line for all district staff who have access to the electronic data base. 



 



The facilities inventory may be corrected by submitting on-line transactions through the Educational Facilities Information System (EFIS).  A district's facilities inventory may be corrected at any time when new additions or remodeling occurs, during a validation study conducted by the district or the Office of Educational Facilities (OEF), or as the result of an approved educational plant survey. New construction may be added to the inventory when a construction contract is issued.  Large areas that are scheduled to undergo major remodeling should be updated in the inventory as soon as a contract agreement has been made.  If a major remodeling project is conducted by district staff, the inventory should be updated when the project is substantially completed.  Each district should conduct at least an annual update of FISH.



 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the annual Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) Certification of Facilities Data.
