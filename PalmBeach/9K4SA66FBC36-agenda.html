 This School Board Workshop will provide a Power Point Presentation by Mary Vreeland to the Board regarding the IB Access Project that the following high schools were selected by IB to participate: Atlantic, Forest Hill and Pahokee.



The  overall goals of the project are as follows:
1) Increase student participation and performance in the Diploma Programme among under-represented students.
2) Increase student preparation for the Diploma Programme by improving the articulation between MYP and the Diploma Programme, particularly in 9th and 10th grades in Mathematics, Science, Language A (English) and Language B (foreign language).



The specific goal of this Project is:
To pilot and evaluate an array of new curriculum and professional development resources developed by the IB in IB World Schools or IB candidate schools identified and supported by selected school districts with the explicit intention of increasing participation of under-represented IB MYP students in the IB Diploma Programme.  



Funds that were allocated to this Project will be available from the District to cover additional school staff costs for participation in the Project associated with teacher professional development and planning time related to this IB Project.

AC1 - Memorandum of Understanding Between IB Fund US Inc and the School Board of PBC
Meeting
Mar 10, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

 This MOU will allow the School District of Palm Beach County to participate in the IB Access Project which is a three year effort to increase student participation and success in the IB Diploma Programme by developing a set of curriculum and assessment resources aimed at improving the preparation of 9th and 10th grade IB Middle Years Programme (MYP)students, particularly low-income and minority students.  This Project seeks to change the landscape for low-income and minority students by providing a more rigorous curriculum prior to entrance in the IB Diploma Programme.  The following high schools that currently have IB MYP Programmes have been selected by IB for this Project: Atlantic, Forest Hill and Pahokee High Schools. 



The  overall goals of the project are as follows:
1) Increase student participation and performance in the Diploma Programme among under-represented students.
2) Increase student preparation for the Diploma Programme by improving the articulation between MYP and the Diploma Programme, particularly in 9th and 10th grades in Mathematics, Science, Language A (English) and Language B (foreign language).



The specific goal of this Project is:
To pilot and evaluate an array of new curriculum and professional development resources developed by the IB in IB World Schools or IB candidate schools identified and supported by selected school districts with the explicit intention of increasing participation of under-represented IB MYP students in the IB Diploma Programme.  



Funds that were allocated to this Project will be available from the District to cover additional school staff costs for participation in the Project associated with teacher professional development and planning time related to this IB Project.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Memorandum of Understanding between IB Fund US Inc and the School Board of Palm Beach County effective March11, 2010 through June 30, 2012, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and amendments related to the agreement which are required for implementation.

Contact:

Judith Klinek (klinek@palmbeachschools.org)
Mary R. Vreeland

Financial Impact:

The financial impact of the Special Revenue-Other Funds Budget is an increase of $65,000 over the next two years, including FY 2011 and FY2012.  All grant related expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue.
