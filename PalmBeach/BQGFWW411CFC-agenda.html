I recommend the Board approve the renewal of the Agreement with the School Board of Seminole County effective on the last date of approval by a party through June 30, 2021.

DESCRIPTION:

East Coast Technical Assistance Center (ECTAC) has provided services for the District since 2005. ECTAC provides the District with the following services: 

 

1.  As a member district, Palm Beach County Federal and State Programs staff are involved in the quarterly Technical Assistance meetings for Title I, Parts A, C, and D, Title II, Title IV, and Parent and Family Engagement programs where key United States and Department of Education staff present on topics by request. 

 

2.  ECTAC provides technical assistance to develop and implement federal programs consistent with the requirements of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) and School Improvement. 

 

3.  ECTAC facilitates networking with high performing/high poverty schools across the state for the purpose of sharing effective practices and with 30 or more districts on common issues encountered at the District level. 

 

4.  ECTAC advocates for the needs of the District with the Florida Department of Education, especially when monitoring findings are in question. 

 

5.  ECTAC provides capacity development for District staff in the areas of the effective engagement of families in the education of their children, implementation of effective instructional strategies and educational best practices identified in scientifically based research, and effective utilization of federal resources and other school improvement reform processes. 

 

6. The School Board of Seminole County is the fiscal agent for ECTAC.

I recommend the Board approve the Amendment to the Boys & Girls Clubs of Palm Beach County, Inc., at Gove Elementary School through September 30, 2020; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and amendments related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

  1. On September 25, 2019, the School Board approved a Services Agreement with the Boys and Girls Club to operate an Out-of-School program at "Old" Gove, 900 S.E. Avenue G. (Link to Board Report)
     
  2. On April 29, 2020, the School Board approved conveyance of the "Old" Gove property to the City of Belle Glade, prompting the need for a new location for the Club's Out-of-School program. (Link to Board Report)
     
  3. When District schools reopen, the Club will begin operating its program at the new Gove facility at 1000 S.E. Avenue G.
     
  4. In approving this Amendment, the Board is consenting to the Boys and Girls Clubs' continued use of the old Gove Elementary facility while the new Gove Elementary School facility is closed as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic emergency and this use will not be considered a violation of the reverter provision in the deed granted by the School Board conveying the old Gove Elementary facility to the City.
