Recommended Action
I recommend approval of the contract with Life Account, LLC, dba Compass Professional Health Services for innovations for controlling health care costs for SDPBC employees for the period November 11, 2013 through December 31, 2016, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the contract resulting from RFI 13C-012H, Innovative Ideas to Control the Healthcare Costs for the School District of Palm Beach County with Life Account, LLC, dba Compass Professional Health Services.

 

 On March 15, 2013, RFI 13C-012H, Innovative Ideas to Control the Healthcare Costs for the School District of Palm Beach County was solicited and 17 responses were received. A committee consisting of members from Risk and Benefits Management as well as Purchasing and the District's outside benefits consultant Wells Fargo reviewed the responses and identified seven proposers whose responses might meet the District’s  needs.  Interviews were conducted with those seven proposers, as provided in Board Policy (6.14.5.c.ii).  This contract was then developed by direct negotiations with the vendor determined to best meet the needs of the District,  Life Account, LLC, dba Compass Professional Health Services (Compass).

 

The Compass Care team will analyze the School District’s claims and biometric data to identify the top medical conditions impacting overall costs.  From this analysis, Compass will develop targeted interventions to improve the covered population’s health risks and resulting costs.  Compass will identify Center of Value (COV) providers to work more closely with the School District on their health improvement and cost, and Compass will monitor the COVs’ performance on behalf of the School District.  These COVs will operate as community Near-Site Clinics for our employees and their families. We expect to establish five or six COVs throughout the county. Performance standards will be established to attain defined health improvement, wellness and financial goals. The Compass Health Pro will promote the benefits of the COVs with the enrolled population.

 

Compass estimates annual health plan savings at $16 million.  The first year fee is $5 per employee, per month, beginning when these programs have been established (expected to be February 2014).  The fee increases to $6 per employee, per month in year two and to $7 per employee, per month in year three. There are additional pass through fees not to exceed $100,000 per year for incentives and postage. 

 

 

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Operating Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

Dianne Howard, Director of Risk and Benefits Management (Dianne.howard@palmbeachshools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The annual financial impact to the District for these services is not to exceed $1,270,000 through December 31, 2014; $1,504,000 through December 31, 2015 and $1,738,000 through December 31, 2016.  The source of funds is the Health Program Internal Service Fund.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board of Palm Beach County approve the application of the Early Career Academy, and authorize the Superintendent and/or his designee to sign all related contracts and documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On August 1, 2013, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from the Early Career Academy (ECA) to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2014. 

 

The Early Career Academy’s mission is, “A career-focused charter high school for grades 11 and 12 in which students earn a high school diploma while simultaneously earning an associate degree in a career-oriented field.  It will provide students with an opportunity to acquire an in-demand body of knowledge; obtain secondary and post-secondary credentials, saving them a material portion of post-secondary tuition costs and additional study time; and, if desired, obtain employment in a career-oriented field upon attainment of a post-secondary credential.”

 

A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.

 

Projected Enrollment

 

            First Year                 11                    120 Projected Enrollment

            Second Year            11-12               240 Projected Enrollment

            Third Year                11-12               240 Projected Enrollment

            Fourth Year              11-12               240 Projected Enrollment

            Fifth Year                 11-12               240 Projected Enrollment

 

Education Program Design

 

The curriculum for the ECA will be the ITT Tech Associate’s degree program, modified to meet the high school standards for Florida.  The focus of the dual-credit curriculum is on career preparation, atop a strong foundation of core concepts in general education.  ECA will meet all high school curriculum requirements under Florida state law.  Students will take a mix of core general education courses and technical courses in each of six trimesters.

 

Location

 

The facility is located at 1756 N. Congress Avenue, West Palm Beach, FL 33409.  The 20,000 square-foot building has been the location of the ITT Tech – West Palm Beach campus since 2011 and will house the Early Career Academy.

 

Applicant’s Founding Governing Board

 

The Founding Group believes they will be able to negotiate a favorable EMO contract and facility lease with ITT Educational Services, Inc., the owner of ITT Tech.  They believe that the agreement will include the use of the curriculum and learning materials of ITT Tech.  As such, the Founding Group believes that community members with knowledge of the ITT Tech curriculum, especially employers of ITT Tech graduates would be valuable board members.  Because of this the Founding Group has identified employers of ITT Tech graduates that are interested and qualified to become board members for ECA.  Additionally, the board will recruit new members from the community including ITT Tech Graduates and previous faculty.

 

Ratings of The Early Career Academy’s Application

 

The Early Career Academy “Meets the Standard” in the required 19 sections. 

 

A copy of Early Career Academy’s Application is attached.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board authorize the Board Chairman, Superintendent, and Treasurer to complete the process to ensure compliance with the Dodd-Frank legislation as it pertains to the School District’s existing swap agreements.

DESCRIPTION:

Between 2002 and 2005, the School District entered into swap agreements related to four COP issues.  The agreements were reviewed by the Finance Committee prior to execution and approved by the Board.  As of June 30, 2013, the agreements have resulted in savings in excess of $40 million.  The transactions are monitored closely by staff and reviewed at least once a year by the Finance Committee as part of the Annual Debt Report.  The FY 2013 Annual Debt Report is available online at http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/documents/2013AnnualDebtReportfinal.pdf

 

During the financial crisis, it became apparent that swap dealers were largely unregulated.  The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (“Dodd-Frank”) and the rules, regulations, orders and interpretations that are published or issued in connection therewith has implemented regulations for swaps and swap dealers.  As part of the transition from unregulated to regulated entities, swap dealers are now prohibited from terminating, amending, novating, modifying or even discussing anything pertaining to a pre-regulation swap unless that swap is modified to comply with the applicable Dodd-Frank rules and regulations implemented to date (the "Dodd-Frank Rules").  This includes the District's Swap Agreements.  Therefore, the District’s current Swap Agreements must be amended to bring them into conformity with the Dodd-Frank Rules. Adherence to the Dodd-Frank Rules and protocols is required by federal law.

 

The amendment is made via an online process that was developed in response to the Dodd-Frank legislation.  The District must indicate they have adhered to the protocol, completed a protocol questionnaire and chose the banks that we work with currently or in the future. 

 

The Protocols contain many provisions including the following:

  1. Representations of the School District that it is an "Eligible Contract Participant" and also a "Special Entity" under the Dodd-Frank Rules.
  2. An election by the School District that it is the "End-User" of the Swap Agreements, therefore exempting the Swap Agreements from the mandatory clearing requirements of the Dodd-Frank Rules and an agreement to provide the dealer whatever information necessary for such exception.
  3. Representations of the School District with respect to its hiring a "Designated Qualified Representative" as required under the Dodd-Frank Rules.  The District currently has a contract with Public Financial Management, Inc. to serve in this capacity. 
  4. Consent to receiving pre-trade mid-level quotes from the dealer orally instead of in writing.
  5. Agreement that daily marks will be calculated as of the close of business on the prior business day.
  6. Agreement that scenario analysis will be provided by the dealer only upon request.
  7. Consent to disclosure of trade information to regulatory authorities.
  8. Agreement as applicability of risk valuation procedures and any portfolio reconciliation.
  9. Agreement to notify the dealer of certain material events as to the School District such as merger or insolvency.

These protocols have been reviewed by the General Counsel’s office as well as Greenberg Traurig, the District’s Bond Counsel.  Staff has spent significant time reviewing the information with Public Financial Management, the District’s Financial Advisor.  All recommend we proceed with adherence with Dodd-Frank as soon as possible.  The District has a pending transaction that must be completed in January and the parties are precluded from discussing the transaction until the District is in compliance with Dodd-Frank.

 

This information will be reviewed by the School Board’s Finance Committee on November 1st.  A draft of the minutes from that meeting, as well as the vodcast of the meeting, will be available online at http://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/2013FinanceCommittee.asp by November 4th.

There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Mr. Julian Harrell.
