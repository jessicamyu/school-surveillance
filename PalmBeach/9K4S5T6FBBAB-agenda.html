Consider approval of the continuation of the Adults with Disabilities Grant in the amount of $1,186,525 effective July 1, 2009 to June 30, 2010.  This will be the seventh year of this grant.



The goal of this grant is to provide funding for a vocational training program for disabled adult students in Palm Beach County.  The population to be served will be limited to no less than 600 disabled adult students served through a combination of 14 full-time vocational instructors and 33 full-time paraprofessionals, employed by liaison agencies.



This program is administered by South Tech Charter Academy, Inc.



Two individualized employability benchmarks are established for each student on a yearly basis.  This grant-funded program helps the students work toward achieving their two benchmarks.



Evaluation of the program includes:



• creation of an IEP with at least two vocational or academic goals;



• formulation of a checklist outlining benchmarks for goals; and



• monthly evaluations by agency/teacher/student using checklist.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the continuation of the Adults with Disabilities competitive grant in the amount of $1,186,525 effective September 17, 2009 to June 30, 2010 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of grant activities.

Below you will find the title and a brief description for each of the Federal grants that are recommended for approval.



Title I, Part A, Improving the Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged and Title I, Part A: Improving the Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged - American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA):
The purpose of these grants is to provide additional services to ensure that students who live in poverty and attend a high-risk school, are provided additional support. Funds are allocated to work with teachers to become highly qualified. Additional personnel are provided to coach teachers and provide the services and support to promote effective teaching. Funds are also provided to private school students who meet the criteria and would have attended a Title I school. Additionally, Pre-K classes have been set up so students can come to school one year prior to kindergarten to ensure that they are given a quality Pre-K year which will boost them to grade level.



Title I, Part A, School Choice with Transportation (CWT) & SES and Title I, Part A, School Choice with Transportation (CWT) & SES - American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA):
The purpose of this grant is to utilize the 20% set-aside to provide required programs for students who are enrolled in a Title I SINI schools. Eligible students are provided opportunities to attend a neighboring school that is not a SINI school. District transportation is also provided. A parent who does not choose CWT may choose free tutoring at their home school. Students are provided tutoring by private providers until they reach the maximum funded amount per child.



Title I, Part D, Subparts I (State) and II (local) Programs for Neglected and Delinquent and American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA):
Though each grant functions separately at the program level, the purpose of this grant is to address and provide support and services regarding school improvement initiatives, Choice, supplemental services, and services for neglected and delinquent students, as well as professional development for stakeholders.  This year, concentrated focus will be on improving reading, mathematics, and career skills.



Title II, Part A, Teacher and Principal Training and Recruiting:
The purpose of this grant is to target and enhance highly qualified administrators and teachers through professional development, recruitment, and retention initiatives.  Title II supports Title I initiatives district-wide (non-Title I schools) in providing professional development in the core subject areas, best practice teaching strategies, and classroom management methods, as well as recruitment incentives for the newly hired. NCLB requires that eligible private schools receive equitable, but not necessarily the same, services in professional development (based on a per pupil-public/private-ratio).



Title II, Part D, Enhancing Education Through Technology and Title II, Part D, Enhancing Education Through Technology American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA): 
The purpose of this grant, using both regular and stimulus (ARRA) entitlement funds, is to provide quality staff development that equips participants with both curriculum and technical skills.  The provided professional development will help schools attain the highest levels of technology integration as outlined in the matrix developed by the Florida Department of Education.  This program will support both state and district goals for student achievement.



Title III, Part A, Supplementary Instructional Support for English Language Learners:
The purpose of this grant is to provide intensive academic support and professional development to school staffs in priority schools to accelerate achievement of ELLs in English language proficiency, reading, mathematics, writing and science.  Priority schools are determined based on ELL subgroup progress toward making AYP (Adequate Yearly Progress) and meeting proficiency targets.



Title IV, Part A, Safe and Drug-Free Schools:
The purpose of this grant is to support programs that prevent violence in and around schools; that prevent the illegal use of alcohol, tobacco, and drugs; that involve parents and communities; and that are coordinated with related federal, state, school, and community efforts and resources to foster a safe and drug-free learning environment that supports student academic achievement.  WITHDRAWN



Title X, Part C, Education of Homeless Children and Youth Project American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA):
The purpose of this grant is to provide additional and expanded services to identified students in the district in compliance with the McKinney-Vento requirements.  These funds will be used to facilitate the identification, enrollment, attendance, and tutoring to ensure the success in school of homeless children and youth.



IDEA, Individual with Disabilities Education Act Grants, Part B, Part B Kindergarten, and IDEA Part B - American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA):
The purpose of these grants is to implement statewide initiatives relative to the education of students with disabilities, in compliance with the mandates of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), Part B.  Funds allocated through the IDEA grant provide related and support services to students with disabilities.  The IDEA also requires a set-aside for parentally placed private school students who attend private schools located in the School District. The estimated amount for FY10 is approximately $1,972,465.
