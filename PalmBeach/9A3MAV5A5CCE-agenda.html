4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Subject
WITHDRAWN - PS2 Personnel Disciplinary Action-Arshley Emile
Meeting
Aug 7, 2013 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve Arshley Emile's three (3) day suspension without pay, effective August 23, 2013 subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:  On or about January 14, 2013, Mr. Emile became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon the allegations of Inappropriate Interaction with Student(s); Ethical Misconduct; Failure to Exercise Best Professional Judgment; and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

On February 11, 2013, the Office of Professional Standards received a School Police report that indicated Mr. Emile engaged in inappropriate interaction with students by "popping" them with a leather belt.  No seious injuries were reported.  Mr. Emile stated that although the incident was done in a playful manner and not intended as bullying or child abuse, he erred in judgment and it was wrong to use the belt.  School Police filed "child abuse" charges against Mr. Emile with the State Attorney's Office, who subsequently decided not to file criminal charges.

 

Therefore, the actions violated the following:  School Board Policy 0.01(2)(c) & (2)(d), Commitment to the Student, Principal I; School Board Policy 3.02(4)(a), (4)(d) & (5)(a)(vii), Code of Ethics; School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff; Rule 6B-1.006(3)(a) & (3)(e), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for Educators in Florida; and Rule 6B-1.001(2) & (3), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for Educators in Florida.  Furthermore, it is noted that Mr. Emile completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on September 21, 2010.  Note, that Mr. Emile has no prior discipline since he commenced employment with the School District in August 2010.

 

There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Mr. Arshley Emile.

 

On July 22, 2013, the "Notice of Three (3) Day Suspension Without Pay" was hand-delivered to Mr. Emile from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

I recommend that the School Board approve the Last Chance Agreement between the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida and Tonya Potter and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is the result of negotiations between the School District and Tonya Potter, an Instructional Technical Support Assistant (ITSA) who was alleged to have committed misconduct. A recommendation for disciplinary action on these specific issues was not made to the Board.

 

Settlement is recommended as being in the best interest of the District based upon the terms of the Agreement whereby Ms. Potter agrees to a suspension without pay for five (5) days and releases the District from any and all claims resulting from the disciplinary actions in this matter. Ms. Potter shall strictly comply with any and all District policies and directives. Should Ms. Potter engage in conduct similar to that which resulted in this Agreement between August 1, 2013 and June 30, 2014, a signed letter of resignation, which is part of the Agreement, will be submitted with with no right to appeal.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve Carla Holmes' suspension without pay effective August 8, 2013. I further recommend the School Board approve the termination of Carla Holmes' employment effective August 23, 2013, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S. and sec. 120.57, F.S. In the event Ms. Holmes timely requests an appeal, the suspension without pay will remain in effect until the conclusion of the administrative process.

DESCRIPTION:   On or about January 7, 2013, Ms. Holmes became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon the allegations of Refusal to Comply with a Reasonable Suspicion Drug Test; Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy(Second Violation); Ethical Misconduct; and Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

On January 7,2013, the Assistant Principal of Palm Springs Elementary contacted the Office of Professional Standards regarding Ms. Holmes, whom he believed was under the influence of a prohibited substance.  After identifying sufficient behaviors, the Assistant Principal requested a Reasonable Suspicion Drug Test.  Ms. Holmes refused to comply with the test, at which time she was notified that refusal to comply would be treated as a positive test.  Ms. Holmes received a prior Written Reprimand on September 17, 2008 for violating the Drug Free Workplace Policy while on duty, which would make this incident a "Second Offense".

 

Therefore, these actions violated the following:  School Board Policy 3.96(2)(v) & (4)(f), Drug and Alcohol Free Workplace Policy; School Board Policy 3.02(4)(a),(4)(f) & (4)(h), Code of Ethics; School Board Policy 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment; and School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff.  Furthermore, it is noted that Ms. Holmes completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics acknowledged such on April 24, 2010.

 

There is "just cause" to support the suspension and termination of Carla Holmes.

 

On July 23, 2013, the "Notice of Suspension & Termination" was hand delivered to Ms. Holmes from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.
