FM1 - Consider Adoption of a Resolution Authorizing Issuance of 2011A COPs
Meeting
May 11, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

·         In 2007, the School District issued the 2007B COP issue in a variable rate mode.



·         Due to instability in the credit markets, in 2008 the District reissued the debt in a fixed rate mode for three (3) years.  The three year period ends on August 1, 2011 so the debt must be reissued in the near future.



·         In October 2010, the School District issued Qualified School Construction Bonds to take advantage of 0% financing.  That allowed the District to finance the construction of Gove Elementary and Galaxy Elementary with no interest costs.To make room for the principal payments for the QSCBs, the District planned to move some of the principal payments for the 2007 B to 2030-2032 as part of this transaction. 



·         Based on the current interest rate environment, the District’s Financial Advisor and the Finance Committee have agreed the most cost effective structure for this transaction is a combination of traditional fixed rate debt and medium term fixed rate notes that will need to be reissued in 3-5 years. 



·         This transaction will replace the 2007B COP issue.



·         This resolution delegates authority to the Superintendent to, with the District’s Financial Advisor,  determine the best interest rate mode and negotiate the terms of sale of 2011A COPS with Morgan Stanley.  The Resolution also authorizes the execution and delivery of all instruments and documents necessary to complete the transaction.



·         The Finance Committee reviewed the transaction at two meetings, April 5, 2011 and May 4, 2011 and unanimously approved it at their meeting on May 4, 2011. 

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board adopt a Resolution authorizing issuance of 2011A COPs to restructure the 2007B COPS in an aggregate principal amount not to exceed $120 million.

Trecia North Last Chance Agreement
Meeting
May 11, 2011 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

This Agreement is the result of negotiations between the School District and Trecia North, a teacher who was alleged to have committed gross insubordination for failing to follow policies and procedures of the District and failing to follow directives given to her by her Principal during the 2008-2009 and 2009-2010 school years. A recommendation for disciplinary action on these specific issues was not made to the Board.



 

Settlement is recommended as being in the best interest of the District based upon the terms of the Agreement whereby Ms. North agrees to a suspension without pay for five (5) days and releases the District from any and all claims resulting from the disciplinary actions in this matter.  Ms. North will be treated as an involuntary transfer for placement as a classroom teacher for the 2011-2012 school year and she shall strictly comply with any and all District policies and directives. Should Ms. North engage in conduct to similar to that which resulted in this Agreement between July 1, 2011 and June 30, 2012, a signed letter of resignation, which is a part of the Agreement, will be submitted with no right to appeal.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Last Chance Agreement between the School Board Palm Beach County, Florida and Trecia North and authorize the Chairman and Acting Superintendent to execute all related documents.

·         In 2005, the School District entered into 2 forward swaptions (options to enter into swaps).  The transactions simulated an advance refunding (refinancing) of the two COP issues and allowed the District to receive a payment equal to the estimated present value savings of the refunding.  For the two transactions, the District received $10,490,000.  These funds were placed in the Board Contingency within the General Fund.



·         The transactions included an option that, if exercised, would require the District to enter into floating-to-fixed interest rate swaps.  In the event the option was exercised, it was contemplated the District would issue variable rate COPs in 2011 and 2012, which, when combined with the interest rate swap, would result in payments approximately matching the current lease payment schedule. 



·         This transaction is associated with one of the forward starting swaptions. 



·         The new debt issue, which will replace the 2001B COP issue.



·         This Resolution delegates authority to the Superintendent to negotiate the terms with the swap counterparty and to waive the exercise date requirement of the swap. 



·         This resolution also delegates authority to the Superintendent to negotiate the terms of sale of 2011B COPS with a bank or underwriting firm based on the best terms and conditions included in bids received via the District’s Financial Advisor.  The Resolution also authorizes the execution and delivery of all instruments and documents necessary to complete the transaction.



·         The Finance Committee reviewed the transaction at two meetings, April 5, 2011 and May 4, 2011 and unanimously approved it at their meeting on May 4, 2011. 

Recommendation:

  • Advanced Placement (AP) courses offer students the opportunity to earn college credit while they are still in high school and are effective in preparing students for the rigor of postsecondary study.

  • Research consistently shows that students taking AP courses and exams are much more likely than their peers to complete a college degree on time in four years. www.Collegeboard.com/research.

  • The following College Board Advanced Placement Workshops will be provided by these Agreements:
    o French Language
    o English Literature and Composition
    o English Language and Composition
    o Microeconomics
    o Physics B
    o Chemistry
    o Biology
    o Calculus AB
    o World History
    o Government and Politics - U.S.

  • Funding for this Agreement is provided by Title II, professional development funding. 

  • The workshops will be provided on June 13 - 17 and June 20 - 24, 2011.

  • The workshops will be advertised to teachers through a bulletin, and AP Roundtable Facilitators will be asked to recruit subject specific teachers to attend. 

  • The target audience will be current AP teachers, teachers who plan to teach AP in the future, as well as, other high school teachers interested in learning strategies to increase rigor in all instructional areas.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the College Board Professional Development Agreements, effective June 13, 2011 through June 24, 2011.

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the Special Revenue-Other Funds budgets $110,500 ($55,250 for each of the two agreements).    The source of funds is Title II, categorical funding for professional development only.  This money has been set aside for this purpose and must be spent by June 30, 2011.
