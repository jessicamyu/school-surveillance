Outstanding Student, Adult and Senior School Volunteers

The 2010-2011 Palm Beach County Outstanding Student, Adult and Senior School Volunteers have been named. Dhruv Tank a senior at Suncoast High School is the Student Volunteer of the Year, Maryann Polizzi from Alexander W. Dreyfoos Jr. School of the Arts is the Adult Volunteer of the Year and Ivorine Martin from Benoist Farms Elementary School is the Senior Volunteer of the Year.  Over 37,000 volunteers work with our students and teachers supporting our goal of academic excellence.  These three outstanding volunteers directly impact student achievement and are shining examples of the thousands of dedicated volunteers working with our students each and every day.



Dhruv Tank is an excellent student as well as being an extremely dedicated volunteer. He co-founded and manages a Mentor Club for freshman at Suncoast Community High School.  This program began in 2008 and has grown to such importance that it is incorporated into the School Improvement Plan.  The club's goal is to give each freshman a mentor who can assist in providing the new student a smooth transition from middle school to high school. Additionally, Dhruv is president of the Digital Divide Club, an organization that teaches students how to not only repair computers, but also how they work.  He also works tirelessly providing computer and technological assistance to both students and teachers throughout the year.  Dhruv Tank is a dedicated student who is devoted to the Suncoast High family.



For nine years every day, Monday thru Friday (and some weekends), Maryann Polizzi has been volunteering in Palm Beach County. The staff at Alexander W. Dreyfoos Jr. School of the Arts has been astonished watching her work her magic in developing school programs in ways they never thought possible.  Ms. Polizzi has attended every School Advisory Council (SAC) meeting since August 2007 and currently serves as the Chair.  She initiated a program where a group of volunteers help teachers with Scholastics and the National Foundation for Advancement in the Arts.  She also volunteers for and sits on the board of the School of the Arts Foundation. In addition, Ms. Polizzi has served as the co-chair and chair of the Visual Arts Parents Association since 2007.  This energetic volunteer also sells pizza during lunch period daily.  For the past three years in the spring Ms. Polizzi has organized and prepared a staff recognition luncheon. 



Ivorine L. Martin loves to volunteer, not just for the satisfaction of doing a good deed, but to see a child learn and grow.  The students in Ms. York's kindergarten class at Benoist Farms Elementary School look forward to seeing Ms. Martin, to whom they refer as "Grandma," walk through the door each morning.  At the beginning of the school year, she works with groups of students, until individual needs are assessed.  After the needs are identified, she works with each child, one on one, every day.  Her greatest joy is seeing a child learn to master a difficult task, such as writing his name for the first time.  Ms. Martin, a Foster Grandparent through the Area Agency on Aging, also provides extras for the students who go without.  Whether it's school supplies, or a hug, Ivorine Martin is willing to help out in any way she can because of her kind heart and generous spirit.

AC3 - Service to Science Initiative: Building Evaluation Capacity for Evidence-Based Interventions Mini-Subcontract Application
Meeting
Jan 19, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

As a winner of the a National Exemplary Innovative Substance Abuse Program, the FACE ITSM program was asked to apply for a Service to Science Initiative Mini-Subcontract.  Those eligible to apply include 51 programs that participated in a Regional Service to Science Academy in 2010. The initiative provides direct assistance to program developers, implementers, and evaluators to help assess their programs' effectiveness through more rigorous evaluations. FACE ITSM was created with federal grant funding by the Prevention Center in the Department of Safe Schools and has been implemented District-wide for the past eight years. It is a family-based, educational, life-skills, substance abuse prevention and early intervention program for youth ages 12 to 18 who are in trouble for Alcohol, Tobacco, or Other Drug (ATOD) offenses. They are referred from schools as an alternative to ATOD -related school suspension; from Youth Court in response to alcohol or other drug arrests; and from Teen Drug Court for more severe alcohol or other drug problems. Youth attend weekly FACE ITSM classes with their parents/caregivers. Siblings ages 12 to 18 are also invited to attend. In addition to the national award, FACE ITSM received Florida's 2007 Most Innovative Best Practices Substance Abuse Prevention Program award.



The Service to Science Mini-Subcontract will significantly impact FACE ITSM's evaluation capacity to ensure program effectiveness. The expected outcomes of the program are to:



1. Reduce teen ATOD use
2. Increase the number of youth who perceive ATOD is harmful to their health
3. Enhance academic achievement by decreasing the number of days students miss for out-of-school ATOD suspension
4. Improve family communication and family management strategies



Using existing resources, the data that has been collected and analyzed on FACE ITSM thus far includes: resuspensions; parent participation; suspension days saved, and program completion.



The impact of the project will be the development and implementation of a comprehensive evaluation plan and process that will demonstrate the effectiveness of FACE ITSM  and its long-term impact on youth and their families.  Such evidence is expected to help sustain the program through grants and other opportunities.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Service to Science Initiative: Building Evaluation Capacity for Evidence-Based Interventions for up to $30,000, effective January 20, 2011 through January 19, 2012, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities.

LA1 - Adoption of the MOU and attachment between the District and CTA relating to a School Improvement Grant from the FL DOE
Meeting
Jan 19, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action

The Florida Department of Education (DOE) offered the District a $2.3 million  grant to fund a three-year School Improvement Plan for three District schools identified by DOE  during the 2009-2010 school year as being in the lowest 5% of schools in the State based on their FCAT Scores from the 2008-2009 school year.  On December 9, 2011, the District and CTA agreed to a MOU and attachment (Exhibit 1) containing relevant pay for performance matrices.  This MOU and attachment provide:







  • A three school-year Pilot Project for instructional employees assigned to Glades Central High School, Lake Worth High School and Rosenwald Elementary School.




  • The staffs of the two high schools have agreed to an eight (8) period modified schedule for all three years of the Pilot and pursuant to the CTA Contract, those teachers assigned to a sixth teaching period are being paid the additional period supplement of $23.50 per additional period.




  • Teachers assigned to tutor students before school hours, after school hours or on non-student days will be paid at the rate of $20.44 per hour, per the CTA Contract.




  • The Pilot provides for a two-part Pay For Performance Plan and a Collegial Team Planning Component.




  • The two-part Pay For Performance Plan consists of an individual teacher Pay For Performance Plan where a teacher who instructs students in an FACT tested subject can be rewarded addition pay based on his/her students Proportion of Year’s Growth (PYG) up to $1,500 per school year.  The second part of the Pay For Performance Plan consists of a School-Wide Team of teachers working together to increase student PYG where each team member can be rewarded up to $5,500 per school year.




  • The Collegial Team Planning component consists of teams of Reading, Math and Science teachers at each school led by their respective academic coach that meets  two times each regular school week outside of their regular duty day to review the previous week’s common assessments, review best practices, plan common lessons, and prepare common assessments for the following week. 




  • Those teachers serving on these Collegial Team Planning committees will be paid an annual supplement totaling $1,500 and their respective academic coaches who are providing leadership for these Collegial Team Planning committees will be paid an annual supplement of $3,000.




  • Although not a part of the MOU, school based administrators at the three schools in the School Improvement Grant are also eligible for the School-Wide Team component of Performance Pay up to the annual maximum total award available for teachers ($7,000) based on their school’s level of performance.

1. The modernization of Gove Elementary School was approved in the 2011-2015 Five Year Plan.

      C. Relocate the school to another location within Belle Glade.  We considered other sites such as the old hospital and West Tech, however there were problems with those sites such as proximity to other schools and the prison.

4. Two buildings and the pavilion will remain on the existing school campus and will be converted into Ancillary Facilities.
5. The 18.80 acre agricultural parcel of land has a five year lease due to expire on December 31, 2012.
6. The cancellation of the lease will be handled by the current owner. The owner's loss of income for canceling the lease 25 months prior to expiration is $16,000.00.
7. The averaged appraised value is $517, 000.00.
8. The Purchase Price of $533,000.00 includes the averaged appraised value and the loss of income.
9. The Phase I and Phase II Environmental Audit has been completed.  The Phase II showed the muck was contaminated but not the groundwater. 
10. Program Management's modernization project includes a Soil Management Plan that involves excavating the retention area and placing the soil in designated areas on-site where it will be capped with a non-permeable surface to eliminate potential exposure to humans and prevent the impacted muck soils from leaching contaminates into the ground water.
