DESCRIPTION:

Florida Statute, Section 274.07, requires the District record the disposal of tangible property in its minutes.  Generally Accepted Accounting Principles require assets which are considered to be nonfunctional or damaged beyond economic repair be removed from the property records.  

 

During fiscal year 2015, the District disposed of approximately 7,850 assets with a net book value of approximately $309,200, and an original acquisition cost of $29.2 million.  Approximately $243,000 was collected from scrap and auction proceeds which offsets the remaining net book value.  A breakdown of disposed assets by age and the percentage of items that met or exceeded their life expectancy is provided on page 2 of the attached report.

 

Notable disposals include:

  • 50 buses with an original cost of $3.4 million, which ranged from 11 to 21 years old.  (The District purchased 130 new buses during 2014 and 2015.)
  • $8.9 million of obsolete software ranging from 5 to 20 years old. (Listing provided on page 3.)
  • $8.9 million of technology-related items that were obsolete and/or scrapped.  Technology changes very quickly and 99% of these assets ranged in age from 5 to 20+ years old.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board receive and file the Internal Audit Report - Adult Education Expenditures and Grant Compliance (Report No. 2015-14).

DESCRIPTION:

The Office of Inspector General audited Adult and Education and Family Literacy Grants for Fiscal Year (FY) 2013-14, as part of its approved  FY 2014-15 Work Plan.  The literacy grants were awarded by Florida Department. The primary objective of the audit was to determine whether the expenditures of the Adult Education Grant Funds and Adult Education Workforce Development Funds complied with related rules, regulations and laws.  During FY 2013 and FY 2014, a total of $19.3 million and $19.5 million of Adult Education Grant Funds were received by the District.

 

The audit of the above funds produced the following conclusions.

 

1.     $17 Million in Accumulated Adult Education Fund Balance.  

 

The Adult Education General Funds are earmarked solely for adult education and related workforce development programs.
