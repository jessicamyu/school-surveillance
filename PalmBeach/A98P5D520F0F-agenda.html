Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Resolution calling for a referendum to levy a half-cent discretionary sales surtax per dollar on all sales transactions.

DESCRIPTION:

If the Palm Beach County Board of County Commissioners approves the Interlocal agreement as approved by the Commissioners on May 3, 2016 and amended by the School Board on May 10, 2016, this item will be withdrawn from consideration.  In the absence of approval by the County Commission on May 10, 2016, the Resolution calls for a referendum as follows:

 

·         Per Florida Statute 212.055(6), the School Board may call for a referendum to be placed on the November 8, 2016 ballot to determine whether a half-cent sales surtax shall be levied.

 

·         The Resolution specifies the ballot language that shall be used.

 

·         If approved by voters, the sales surtax will be levied for 10 years, beginning on January 1, 2017 and automatically ending not later than December 31, 2026.  The sales surtax may end early if the target amount is distributed by September 1 of any year.

 

·         Proceeds of the sales surtax will be used to enhance education and safety by improving and upgrading district-owned school buildings, technology, security, equipment, air conditioning, fire and life safety, school support facilities and other school modernization projects.

 

·         Expenditure of referendum proceeds will the reviewed by an independent oversight citizen committee.

 

CONTACT:

Michael J. Burke, Chief Financial Officer (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

If approved by voters in November 2016, the estimated revenue generated for the School Board capital projects fund is $1,345,743,101, for the calendar years 2017 through 2026, which encompass fiscal years 2017 through 2027.

EAI-1 Teenage Parent Program Contract with Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, Inc.
Meeting
May 11, 2016 - 1. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room

14. Consent Agenda
Type

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the renewal contract with Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, Inc., for the delivery of Teenage Parent Child Care Services from July 1, 2016 through June 30, 2017; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to the Contract.

DESCRIPTION:

This is a renewal of the annual Teenage Parent Program Services Contract with the Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, Inc. (ELC) that supports the provision of services specified in Section 1003.54, F.S. Teenage Parent Programs to provide Teenage Parent Child Care Services.

 

For the children of teen parents the ELC provides childcare resource and referral services for enrollment in child care programs.  In addition, the ELC provides payment for these child care services.  The child care programs are for the babies and toddlers of teen parents who are students of the School District of Palm Beach County.  The services provided allow the teenage parents to attend school while their children are in child care.  The major goal is to provide a comprehensive Teenage Parent Program consisting of educational and ancillary service components.

 

ELC will provide, through its authorized vendors, child care resource and referral services and enrollment into child care programs for students in the Teenage Parent Program. ELC will also provide, through its authorized vendors, payment to child care providers for child care services.  The School District will document that all children referred by the Teenage Parent Program staff are placed in child care in accordance with the terms of the contract and will review documentation of payments to child care providers.

I recommend thel Board approve the AVID and AVID Excel Implementation Agreements with the AVID Center effective July, 1, 2016, through June 30, 2017; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to the Agreements which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID) is a systemic instructional approach for students in Kindergarten through higher education (K-16). The AVID College Readiness System is a school-wide transformational effort focused on leadership, systems, instruction, and culture. AVID is designed to increase the number of students who enroll and succeed in higher education and in their lives beyond high school. The AVID College Readiness System is a catalyst for developing a school culture that closes the expectation and opportunity gaps many students face, and prepares all students for success in a global society.
 
AVID Excel is a middle school program intended to break the detrimental cycle that turns many English Language Learners (ELLs) into long-term ELLs. AVID Excel accelerates students' academic language acquisition and puts them on a pathway to high school AVID and college readiness.
 
The School District of Palm Beach County's AVID participation has increased from six high school sites in 2009 to 43 sites in 2016 at the elementary, middle and high school levels. Five AVID sites in the District have been designated by the AVID center as "Highly Certified." (The participating schools are listed in the attached addendum.)
 
Approximately 300 AVID students have successfully graduated since the first graduating class in 2012. Of the 2014-15 AVID seniors: 
 - 99% graduated on time 
 - 70% accepted to a four-year college 
 - 12% accepted into a two-year college
 - 83% graduated "college-ready"
 - 70% took both the SAT and ACT
 - 69% took at least one AP/IB exam
 
Approximately 570 middle students were enrolled in the AVID elective in 2015-16. Of the 2015-16 middle school students:
 - 69% completed Algebra 1 or a higher math with a "C' of better
 - 54% have taken an honors or high school credit-bearing course other than math
 
Approximately 300 teachers and 100 administrators and District staff have been trained in AVID's strategies and methodologies at Summer Institutes, Path trainings, AVID Professional Learning Workshops, and AVID District Leader Trainings. A sustainable support system has been established in which the District has 7 certified AVID Trainer-of-Trainers serving the following contents: Critical Reading, History/Social Science Mathematics, and Science.
 
These Agreements will allow the School District of Palm Beach County to implement AVID's curriculum and use the AVID logo in all forty-three (43) schools.
