PS1 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Saglenda Johnson
Meeting
Mar 5, 2014 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Saglenda Johnson's termination from employment, effective March 20, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about May 22, 2012, Ms. Johnson became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Off Duty Conduct; Ethical Misconduct; and Insubordination for Failure to Follow Policy, Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

On May 20, 2012, Ms. Johnson was arrested by the West Palm Beach Police Department and charged with aggravated battery for assaulting a female with a weapon while off-duty. The assault resulted in the victim being transported by ambulance to the hospital.  On December 9, 2013, Ms. Johnson pled and was adjudicated guilty to misdemeanor battery regarding this incident. 

 

Ms. Johnson’s disciplinary history includes a Written Reprimand issued on May 31, 2011 for a prior incident in which she was also charged with aggravated battery, and convicted of the lesser charge of misdemeanor battery.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.02(4)(a)(b), (5)(h)(iv), Code of Ethics; 3.10(6), Conditions of Employment, and 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff. 

 

There is “just cause” to support the termination of employment of Ms. Saglenda Johnson. 

 

On February 18, 2014, the “Notice of Recommendation for Termination from Employment” was hand-delivered to Ms. Johnson from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Subject
PS2 Personnel Disciplinary Action - Andres Fuentes
Meeting
Mar 5, 2014 - 3. SPECIAL MEETING following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Andres Fuentes' termination from employment, effective March 20, 2014, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

DESCRIPTION:

On or about July 15, 2013, Mr. Fuentes became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon allegations of Ethical Misconduct and Failure to Follow Policy/Rule or Directive.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.

 

On April 29, 2013, Andres Fuentes struck a staff member at North Palm Beach Elementary School with a trash can.  Mr. Fuentes subsequently admitted to school police that he “lost it”  and that he “wanted to kill” the employee.  While Mr. Fuentes was under investigation, it was discovered that a second incident had been reported to school administration on May 5, 2013 concerning Mr. Fuentes making inappropriate and threatening comments to a fellow custodian at the school.  As a result of the physical altercation, Mr. Fuentes was charged with Battery.  On November 12, 2013, Mr. Fuentes was adjudicated guilty of this misdemeanor charge for the actions which occurred while he was on duty.

 

Therefore, these actions violate the following:  School Board Policies 3.02(4)(a), (4)(d) and (4)(e); 3.02(5)(i), Code of Ethics; and 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel and Staff.  Note, Mr. Fuentes has no prior discipline related to these incidents.

 

There is “just cause” to support the termination of employment of Mr. Andres Fuentes. 

 

On February 18, 2014, the “Notice of Recommendation for Termination from Employment” was hand-delivered to Ms. Johnson from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.
