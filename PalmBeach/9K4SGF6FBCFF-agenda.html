Subject
Atlantic Community High School Cafeteria Pilot Progam Update
Meeting
Apr 13, 2011 - Workshops
Category
Workshop Items
Type
Action (Consent)



Over the years, high school students have been provided less than adequate time to receive and consume their school lunch meals.  Students therefore, have assimilated to this type of inconvenient meal service environment, making poor decisions when it comes to their health and nutritional well-being during and outside the school day.



With this in mind, over the past two years, School Food Service has developed a plan of action with the cooperation of the State DOE-FNM and Atlantic Community High School to reinvent the way students perceive and receive their school meals.  This plan of action takes into consideration the adequate meal time provided, the physical facility, customer service, nutritious food offerings, and the overall dining experience.  It is our hope that with these modifications, students will enter a more relaxing, inviting, social, safe and customer driven dining experience.  Therefore, they will look forward to this time to nutritionally recharge for improved physical and mental well-being thus supporting the Districtâ€™s ultimate goal of improving academic performance.
