Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with Jupiter Health, Inc. d/b/a Jupiter Medical Center and Jupiter Medical Center Urgent Care, effective August 19, 2021 through June 30, 2026; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Jupiter High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve a Resolution authorizing the issuance of the 2021 Tax Anticipation Note, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to approve all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The resolution authorizes the issuance of the 2021 Tax Anticipation Note.
     
  • The Tax Anticipation Note, or TAN, is a short term financing instrument used to alleviate cash shortfalls in the general fund each year.  Current estimates show that additional cash will be needed in November 2021.
     
  • Local property taxes provide the majority of the revenues for the School District.  As the first receipt of tax revenues is expected in late November, it is necessary to borrow money for cash flow purposes until the tax revenues are received.
     
  • Based on projected cash needs, the District plans to issue at $115 million TAN this year.  It will be structured for the same amount as the 2020 TAN, maturing at the end of January 2022.
     
  • The sale is tentatively planned for September 21, 2021.  The transaction should close on or about October 6, 2021.  Unfortunately, due to COVID-19, the mock sale with student participation will not take place this year.  We hope to resume that tradition in 2022.
     
  • The Finance Committee reviewed the proposed tax anticipation note during their meeting on June 11, 2021 and unanimously recommended the transaction to the School Board for approval.

CONTACT:

Heather Fredrick Knust, CPA, Chief Financial Officer (Heather.Knust@palmbeachschools.org)

Leanne Evans, Treasurer (Leanne.Evans@palmbeachschools.org)

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The proceeds of the TAN will be used to meet the cash flow needs of the general fund.  Interest expense is projected at $33,000 and costs of issuance are not to exceed $115,900.  Interest earnings will be used to help offset the cost of the transaction.

  • On May 5, 2021, the School Board held a Workshop to discuss School Board Member Residence Area Redistricting. The delays were caused by data collection consequences of COVID-19.
     
  • At the workshop, staff recommended that redistricting criteria be adopted by the Board in advance of the data release, so that staff could immediately commence drawing new District boundaries when the data becomes available.
     
  • It is recommended that the Board adopt the following Redistricting criteria for the 2021 School Board Member Residence Area Redistricting, as recommended by our outside Counsel, Jerry Wilson, Esq.:

​1. Achieve equal population among the districts

2. Adhere to Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act of 1965, as amended and interpreted through case law

3. Preserve communities of interest, including municipal boundaries where reasonable

4. Follow natural and man-made boundaries

5. Geographic compactness

6. Contiguous districts, unless geographic exception required 

7. Preserve the core of existing districts

  • The recommended criteria are similar to the criteria adopted by the Board in 2011:

1. Achieve population equity among Districts

2. Adhere to Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act of 1965, as amended and interpreted through case law

3. Maintain integrity of communities of interest, including municipal boundaries where sensible

4. Follow major natural and man-made boundaries

5. Insure compactness

6. Contiguity of Districts

7. Preserve the core of the existing voter's districts

  • All the redistricting work will be conducted in-house by Planning/GIS staff using the adopted criteria, with outside counsel review of the staff's work products for compliance with Federal, State, and established case law. Staff will bring back at least one Redistricting scenario that is prepared consistent with these criteria for adoption by the Board by December 2021.

I recommend the Board approve an increase of $1 per hour for each of the four Substitute base pay levels; and approve the addition of a ‘High Needs’ Premium Pay supplement for Substitute Teachers serving in designated ‘High Needs’ schools, where such schools are not currently eligible for the $3 per hour ‘Glades’ Premium Pay supplement. Both to be effective August 10, 2021.

DESCRIPTION:

The base hourly rate increase across all four (4) base pay levels will better position the District to be competitive in the surrounding labor market for attracting and retaining Substitute Teachers District-wide. The addition of Premium Pay for Substitutes serving High Needs schools, where such schools are not currently eligible for Glades Premium Pay, will better position the District to attract and retain Substitute Teachers to serve in all the District’s High Needs designated schools.

  1. Current Base Pay:

$11.33 per hour with 30 to 59 earned college credits/hours;

$11.59 per hour with an Associate’s of Arts/Science degree; or 60 or more earned college credits/hours;

$13.39 per hour with a Bachelor’s of Arts/Science degree or higher;

$13.91 per hour for retired Teachers (both in- and out-of-state)

 

Proposed Base Pay:

 

$12.33 per hour with 30 to 59 earned college credits/hours;

$12.59 per hour with an Associate’s of Arts/Science degree; or 60 or more earned college credits/hours;

$14.39 per hour with a Bachelor’s of Arts/Science degree or higher;

$14.91 per hour for retired Teachers (both in- and out-of-state)

 

  1. Current Premium Pay:

$3.00 per hour serving Glades schools

 

Proposed Premium Pay:

 

$3.00 per hour serving Glades schools

$3.00 per hour serving High Needs schools (other than schools already designated for Glades Premium Pay)

Gonzalo La Cava, Ed.D., Chief of Human Resources

The financial impact to the School District is $1,998,409, comprised of: A) $1,551,750 to fund a $1.00 per hour base pay increase, and B) $446,659 to fund $3.00 per hour in Premium Pay for serving High Needs schools (non-Glades designated), including associated fringe benefits.
