DESCRIPTION:

  1. The School Board owns +/- 10.24 acres of property located at 900 SE Avenue G, Belle Glade, FL, which was the former Gove Elementary campus. In 2013, Gove ES was modernized and relocated to a +/- 18.74 acres site just east of and adjacent to the former Gove campus located at 1000 SE Avenue G. (See Map)
  2. Due to the deficiency of affordable housing in the Glades area, there is a shortage of economical housing for teachers in this area. As a resolution to this problem, the City of Belle Glade has requested the School District’s assistance in the acquisition of the former Gove Elementary School site to further the City’s goal of constructing affordable housing. (See 2019 Letter)
  3. Since the 2013 modernization and relocation of Gove ES, the School District has had no use for the property.
  4. The conveyance instrument includes language that the property shall only be utilized for low income housing and directly associated uses. In the event the property is not utilized in accordance with the restriction, title to the property shall automatically revert to the School Board.   
     
  5. City staff has this item tentatively scheduled for the March 16, 2020 City Commission Meeting for acceptance and approval of the property conveyance.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve an Amendment with Florida Atlantic University Board of Trustees (FAU Outreach), effective February 20, 2020 through June 30, 2020, ratify funds spent prior to the approval of Amendment, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to finalize and sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

This Board Agenda item identifies one (1) Amendment between the School Board of Palm Beach County and FAU Outreach for ratification of funds spent prior to the approval of Amendment 002.  This corrects a previous error in financial impact.

 

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

 

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement.  This agreement originated with Lake Worth High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.

 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 7000 high school students.

 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Aide, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness/Exercise Science, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Medical Skills and Services, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

I recommend the Board approve with Agreement with Florida School Retiree Benefits Consortium Agreement, effective benefit date of January 1, 2021 for medical, dental, and vision benefits for Medicare eligible retirees.

DESCRIPTION:

The Florida School Retiree Benefits Consortium (FSRBC) was established on June 15, 2012, by a group of Florida School Districts.   Its purpose is to provide Medicare eligible retirees district-sponsored benefit plans, as required by Florida Statute 112.0801.  The FSRBC is governed by eleven (11) appointed Board of Directors, including: one (1) member from the Florida School Board Association, Inc., one (1) member from the Florida Association of District School Superintendents, Inc., one (1) member from the Florida Association of School Administrators, Inc., two (2) members from the Florida Education Association, two (2) retiree members from participating school districts, selected by the consensus of the Directors, two (2) members serving as benefit managers from participating school districts and nominated and approved by the participating school districts, and two (2) members selected by the Florida Educational Risk Management Association.  The Board of Directors are subject to Florida Sunshine State Laws, and board meetings are public forums.

 

Currently, 16 of 67 Florida school districts participate in the Consortium with over 10,000 participating retirees.  Participating school districts include: Brevard, Calhoun, Charlotte, Duval, Escambia, Flagler, Gilchrist, Jackson, Manatee, Orange, Osceola, Polk, Putnam, Sarasota, Walton, and Washington.

 

Participating in the FSRBC is advantageous to the retiree, as well as the district.  The FSRBC provides retirees with more benefit plan options, administrative support, and same or better premium rates.  The retiree would be able to choose from at least 13 Medicare Advantage Plans, 6 Medicare Supplement Plans, and 6 Pharmacy plans.  The retiree would also receive access to group dental and vision plans, hearing aid discounts, ID theft, technology support, pet insurance and pet Rx discounts, year-round customer service support, an online enrollment system, and multiple payment options including FRS pension deduction. 

 

FSRBC provides the district with reduced OPEB liability, reduced administration, and a positive impact to the active plans.  The District would see an estimated savings of $400,000 (the average loss over the past 4 years) on medical claims that exceeded premium payments for the group of retirees that are Medicare eligible. Additionally, the District will see a reduction of the GASB liability by about $83 million (down from $157,651,000 to $74,651,000).  These savings do not include the internal administrative costs of providing these benefits.

DESCRIPTION:

Approve the Consultant Agreement with Creative People Consulting, Inc. to meet the professional development training needs for teachers seeking to increase their knowledge within the Computer Science curriculum, computer-programming languages, and to become industry certified in computer science certifications. The purpose of this training is to enhance teacher capacity at various schools throughout our District in the area of computer science education.

 

A total of 58 teachers will complete Microsoft Technology Associate Certification Training. Microsoft offers a number of industry certifications called Microsoft Technology Associate (MTA) exams, which address fundamental technical concepts and assess core technical knowledge. This MTA training will prepare teachers to teach computer science skills in one of two tracks (i.e. Programming and Network or Cybersecurity). Teachers who enroll in the programming courses will acquire skills in the following programming languages: Java, Python, JavaScript, and/or HTML & CC. Teachers who complete the training will be familiar with the features and capabilities of programming languages; and, they will be able to write, debug, and maintain well-formed, well-documented code in the respective programming languages. Teachers who enroll in the networking and cybersecurity courses will be trained in security, networking, and windows operating systems fundamentals. Teachers who complete the training will acquire fundamental security knowledge and skills in Windows Server, Windows-based networking, active directory, anti-malware products, firewalls, network topologies and devices, and network management tools. Teachers who complete the courses will also be prepared to sit for the related MTA exams in the aforementioned.

 

This service affects approximately 8,700 students throughout the District.

 

Creative People Consulting, Inc. is a self-reporting M/WBE and SBE vendor.

 

This purchase is considered a Professional Service as defined in Section 1010.04(4)(a)FS and Board Policy 6.14 and therefore is exempt from competition requirements based on the specialized qualifications required to perform these services.
