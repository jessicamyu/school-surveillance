DESCRIPTION:

On May 9, 2012, the School Board approve National Student Clearinghouse StudentTracker for High Schools Agreement for Districts or High Schools. This organization provides a nationwide, central repository of information on the enrollment status and educational achievements of postsecondary students. Palm Beach County utilizes the National Student Clearinghouse to obtain data on the postsecondary enrollment status and educational achievement of students who have graduated from high school in Palm Beach County.

 

By participating in an Agreement with the National Student Clearinghouse, Palm Beach County will continue to:

  • Submit and receive data enabling the District to tract postsecondary student data for research and reporting purposes.
  • Gain information from the research to sue in developing a college readiness pathway.
  • Document the effectiveness of college readiness strategies used by schools and programs within the District.

With data obtained from the National Student Clearinghouse, the Department of Research & Evaluation has been able to conduct analyses to determine which set of student characteristics best predicts successful postsecondary outcomes. This information is currently incorporated into Educational Data Warehouse (EDW) reporting and has been used in the development of college readiness strategies.

 

This agreement will be in effect from June 1, 2019, through May 31, 2020. However, this Agreement may be terminated by either parties by providing 60 days written notice.

I recommend the School Board approves the Cooperative Agreement with Sequel TSI of Florida, LLC effective July 1, 2019 - June 30, 2020; and authorized the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

 

This is a Cooperative Agreement between Sequel Youth and Family Services, LLC and the School District. Sequel Youth is the new agency that is now contracted by Florida Department of Juvenile Justice to operate the Palm Beach Juvenile Correctional Facility located at the Fairgrounds formerly operated by Youth Services International (YSI).


This program is a high risk male, residential secure facility, located in West Palm Beach; FL. The Florida Tobacco Prevention Training for Educators project was developed nine years ago. The Florida Department of Education issued a Noncompetitive Discretionary Request for Application (RFA) to the School District of Palm Beach County to provide online tobacco prevention education training to educators in all Florida school districts. Tobacco and nicotine product use by students interferes with academic achievement and behavior and is associated with other drug use. Over 20% of our high school students regularly use Electronic Nicotine Delivery Systems (ENDs), also called e-cigarettes, and 1.5% smoke cigarettes (2018 Florida Youth Substance Abuse Survey, Palm Beach Data Tables).

 

The grant will allow the training to be offered to our District's teachers and counselors, as well as K-12 educators across the state. This year the training will be a course specifically on ENDs. The Federal Drug Administration considers youth vaping an epidemic. The course was piloted locally (upon request of district school leaders and administrators) and will now be launched state-wide this year. The project has strengthened local partnerships with the Department of Health and created collaborative relationships across the state.

 

Among the requirements to receive in-service points, teachers must teach at least one ENDs prevention lesson that aligns to Florida standards.

 

The impact of this project will be measured in the numbers of teachers across the state who complete the course; teachers’ increased knowledge of tobacco and ENDs prevention education; teachers’ increased knowledge of related effective teaching strategies; increases in student knowledge; changes in students' attitudes and behavior; and numbers of students who receive prevention lessons.

No District funds will be spent until E-rate approval is received. The installs of the wireless access points are part of the infrastructure upgrade project that will increase bandwidth throughout the District.

 

The Telecommunications Act of 1996 established a fund by which Schools and Libraries across the Country could access discounts on eligible telecommunications products and services. The program is commonly known as the E-rate Program. The eligibility for discounts on internet access, telecommunications products and services, internal connection products, services and maintenance is determined by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC). Funding is made available upon application approval by the Schools and Libraries Division (SLD) of the Universal Service Administrative Company (USAC), which was established by the Act. Our District qualifies for a discount of 80%. As part of the E-Rate program, the District is responsible for any portion not covered by E-rate. Some schools have already exhausted some or all of their E-Rate funding and therefore will receive partial or no E-Rate funding from this bid, so the School District of Palm Beach County will be responsible for the balance at these locations.
