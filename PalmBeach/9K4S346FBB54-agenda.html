Personnel Disciplinary Action - Carol Raiford
Meeting
Nov 12, 2008 - Wkshops and Spec Mtgs re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action

On or about September 3, 2008, Ms. Carol Raiford, Associate Child Development, became the subject of an Employee Relations investigation based on allegations of conversion of school district funds.  As Associate Child Development, Ms. Raiford's duties included collecting fees from the parents of the students who attended the Melaleuca Elementary School's Pre-Kindergarten (Pre-K) Program.  During the process, Ms. Carol Raiford failed to comply with the Department of Education's Financial and Program Cost Accounting and Reporting for Florida Schools, 2001, incorporated by rule 6A-1.001, District Financial Records where it states all fee collections be turned into the bookkeeper within one business day for deposit.  Ms. Raiford admitted to utilizing approximately four-thousand dollars of the collected Pre-Kindergarten fees for personal use.



There is probable cause to support the termination of Carol Raiford based on findings of failure to exercise best professional judgment, ethical misconduct, conversion of School District funds and misconduct - careless disregard for policy/directive/rule.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Carol Raiford's suspension without pay, effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Carol Raiford's employment effective November 27, 2008 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

There is no financial impact to the school District.

Personnel Disciplinary Action - Jacinda Smith
Meeting
Nov 12, 2008 - Wkshops and Spec Mtgs re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action

A to Z In-Home Tutoring Service has contract with the Palm Beach County School District to provide tutoring at no cost to eligible students under a grant through the Department of Supplemental Services with Title 1 funds.  A to Z hires teachers from the School District as independent contractors to provide tutoring services.  Ms. Jacinda Smith, teacher, was hired by A to Z In-Home Tutoring.  Ms. Smith falsely submitted documents to A to Z In-Home Tutoring indicating that she had provided twenty-one hours of tutorial to two Westward Elementary students.  She received a total payment of one-thousand fifty dollars for services she did not provide.  Ms. Jacinda Smith was found to have forged the parent name of the two students on school district forms.  The forgery was brought to the attention of A to Z In-Home Tutoring by the parent of the students.



There is probable cause to support the termination of Jacinda Smith based on findings of failure to exercise best professional judgment, ethical misconduct, falsification of district documents, and conversion of school district funds.  Furthermore, the findings included violation of the Florida Department of Education Code of Ethics-Education Profession 6B-1.001, (2) (3) where "the educator's primary professional concern will always be for the student and for the development of the student's potential.  The educator will, therefore, strive for professional growth and will seek to exercise the best professional judgment and integrity and be aware of the importance of maintaining the respect and confidence of one's colleagues, of students, of parents, and other members of the community, the educator strives to achieve and sustain the highest degree of ethical conduct".



Ms. Jacinda Smith failed her obligation to the profession of education and she failed to maintain honesty in her professional dealings with A to Z In-Home Tutoring Service.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approves Jacinda Smith's suspension without pay, effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Jacinda Smith's employment effective November 27, 2008 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.
