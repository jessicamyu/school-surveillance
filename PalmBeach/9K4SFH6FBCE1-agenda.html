Anthony Bass, a senior at Seminole Ridge High School is once again the winner of NASA's annual "Cassini Scientist for a Day" essay contest.  Anthony is the first-ever essayist to win the contest twice.  "Cassini Scientist for a Day" challenges students to 'become NASA scientists' studying the planet Saturn through the robotic spacecraft Cassini. Doreen Moore, School Treasurer at North Grade Elementary School, is the 2010 Palm Beach County School-Related Employee of the Year.  Ms. Moore, a resident of Wellington, has worked for North Grade Elementary for 5 of the 16 years that she has been a district employee.  As school treasurer, Ms. Moore extends her services far beyond her excellent technical skills.  To enhance student learning, she has given up her Saturdays to tutor at North Grade, where she has become a mentor to several of the students needing the guidance of a good role model.  North Grade has an amazing group of ESE high achievers that are involved in the Special Olympics and compete on many occasions at different locations.  Ms. Moore attends to encourage and support them.  Peter Corsentino, Second Grade Teacher, observed that, "The students love seeing her and so very much appreciate her presence."



Principal Debbie Battles expressed that Ms. Moore made significant contributions to the school's positive environment by taking the initiative to create beautiful meeting areas for students and staff.  Ms. Battles said, "She was responsible for designing and setting up our staff lounge, Main Office, Principal's Office and the students' Starlight Café." 



North Grade Elementary's School-Related Employee of the Year nominating committee noted that Ms. Moore is also a Guardian ad Litem volunteer.  The nominating statement read, "In this role, Doreen Moore's goal is to look at all aspects of a child's life:  the intellectual, the spiritual, the physical and the emotional; and to identify what makes for a happy, well-adjusted child".



A check in the amount of $1000.00, provided by local business partners, will be presented to Ms. Moore at the Employee of the Year Breakfast Celebration scheduled in May.  As Palm Beach County's winner, Ms. Moore will represent our county at the state level. 



In late March or April, the Commissioner of Education will announce the 2010 Florida School-Related Employee of the Year.

Dr. Kathleen Weigel Greater Delray Beach Chamber of Commerce Educator of the Year
Meeting
Mar 17, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action (Consent)

Dr. Kathleen Weigel has been honored with the first annual Educator of the Year award by the Greater Delray Beach Chamber of Commerce.  She has been recognized for her efforts as principal of Atlantic Community High School resulting in a premier school for the City of Delray Beach.  Dr. Weigel enters her 32nd year in education including 17 years as principal at both middle and high school levels.  Opening the new Atlantic Community High School in 2005 has given this innovative educational leader another opportunity to create a strong single school culture and implement a creative curriculum that features strong ties to Delray Beach.



In addition to the prestigious International Baccalaureate programs, her school has a Army JROTC program, a First Responder program created with the Delray Beach Fire Department and a national model Criminal Justice Academy directly supported by city's police department.  Atlantic's Eagle Nest Construction Academy works with the city to build affordable housing for needy residents, even turning a profit of $37,000. 
Future plans include expanding her school to wall-to-wall career academies further enabling her students to be college and career ready.



Under her innovative direction, Atlantic Community High School has also earned national recognition for developing ninth grade and tenth grade level academies.  In 2009, 94% of her students who started at her school as freshman walked across the stage as graduates four years later.  Dr. Weigel and her team have worked with schools across the country, her home state of Florida and with teachers in her own district that are also restructuring their educational programming.  



Dr. Weigel has served as division chair of the Palm Beach County High School Principal Committee and participated in the Darden Executive Leadership School at the University of Virginia. She is a liaison for the International Center for Leadership in Education and has presented at numerous national conventions. 
 
She is an adjunct professor of Educational Leadership and Curriculum Leadership at Florida Atlantic University, and has been instrumental in nurturing the next generation of school administrators.



Numerous awards have been bestowed upon her school, her staff and for Dr. Weigel personally.  In 2004 she was the 2004 Principal of the Year for Adult and Community Education.   She received Florida's "School Administrator of the Year Award" by the African American Educator Alliance in January, 2006, and in 2007 she was selected as Career and Technical Education Principal of the Year.  In October of 2009, Dr. Weigel was proud to attend the award ceremony of an Atlantic graduate and former Criminal Justice student who received the Distinguished Alumni Award from the National Career Academy Coalition.  Her work in developing an Early Childhood Education program at Atlantic earned her the 2009 Outstanding Project from the Palm Beach County School District.



Dr. Weigel promotes reading for pleasure in her school through her Drop Everything and Read Program (DROP).  For the first fifteen minutes of first period every day, students are encouraged to read for pleasure.  Building on this initiative, Dr. Weigel wrote a grant with the Pew Trust that funded 40 unique classroom reading for pleasure libraries.  Paired with the Mayor's literacy program, Atlantic's Get Caught Reading program is very popular with students who may have been reluctant readers, but now must wait for the book they want to read.



Newsweek magazine and the Washington Post rated Atlantic Community High School as the 52nd Best high School In The Nation in 2009.

Western Academy Charter School ("Western")  and the School Board of Palm Beach County entered into a charter agreement on July 1, 2008.  The charter expires June 30, 2018.



The Governing Board has signed the Fifth Amendment to extend the contract for an additional five (5) years to commence from July 1, 2018 through June 30, 2023  (see Exhibit "A"). 



Western is an "A" Rated public school in the School District of Palm Beach County and currently holds Non-Differentiated Accountability status.



The following are requested changes to be made to the Charter Agreement, approved by the Board of Directors of Western Academy Charter School on September 17, 2009:



Charter Section - General Provisions, C, page 5:
Change language in this section to read:  "The Charter shall become effective upon the execution by both parties, and shall have a term of 15 years commencing July 1, 2008 and ending June 30, 2023, unless amended by the parties hereto or otherwise terminated in accordance with the provisions of the Charter."



Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(18)(c), "A charter may be modified during its initial term or any renewal term upon the recommendation of the sponsor or the charter school governing board and the approval of both parties to the agreement."  As per the existing Charter language and pursuant to Section 1002.33(7)(18)(b)(2), upon the Charter receiving a school grade "A" or "B" in 3 of the past 4 years this Charter Shall be extended to a term of 15 years.  Western received an "A" grade from the State of Florida for the 2008-2009 school year, thus receiving its third "A" grade within the past 4 years.



Western Academy serves students grades K-8. 



The Data Panel is attached for Western Academy Charter School.




 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Fifth Amendment for Western Academy Charter School extending the charter agreement for an additional five (5) years to June 30, 2023.

Pursuant to Florida Statute 1012.42, teachers in a district school system may be assigned to teach subject matter that is outside the field in which they are certified. Such assignments typically are made to provide students a comprehensive program of instruction.  Priority consideration is granted to teachers who have demonstrated subject matter competency through prior teaching experience.  The District provides support for out-of-field teachers though in-service opportunities to develop instructional competencies required for the assigned duty.  Partnerships have been established with colleges and universities for required coursework and exam preparation. Teachers teaching in subject areas not covered by their current certification are required to sign Out-of-Field Agreements which permit continued employment on the condition that they obtain appropriate in-field certification within a specified time frame.

PS 2 Professional Services Agreement with Capture Educational Consulting Services
Meeting
Mar 17, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

PS 2 Provider will perform the following services:  Curriculum/course scheduling for smaller learning communities (SLC) to enhance student placement.  The consultant will provide assistance to six SLC high schools to achieve optimal student scheduling within SLC's and career academies and to meet with individual schools to resolve concerns and barriers to scheduling.  Create transition from traditional scheduling to cohort scheduling within SLC. 



The main objective of the Smaller Learning Communities (SLC) Grant is to divide large schools into smaller learning communities which the research indicates will allow students to formulate better relationships with teachers and have a greater feeling of "belonging" among their peers. This in turn leads to students having a greater likelihood of staying in school and graduating.  The challenge in implementing an SLC is being able to schedule students together in their career academy as well as in their academic subject areas to form a cohort of students.  The SLC Grant provides funding to allow assistance with the scheduling challenge at the SLC Grant high schools: Atlantic, Glades Central, Lake Worth, Palm Beach Lakes, Palm Beach Gardens and Santaluces.
