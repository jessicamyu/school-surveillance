The following is a brief synopsis of the events leading to this emergency for the Alternative to Suspension Program Site Lease Agreement:



 



1.                  Due to the academic failures of students subject to out-of-school suspensions, the Superintendent, with input from the public, proposed the Alternative to Suspension (ATS) Program.



2.                  The pilot program allows students who have received an out of school suspension for up to ten days for a violation of School Board policy from three specified schools to attend a full-day interim program at an ATS site.



3.                  ATS provides students from these specified schools the opportunity to continue to progress academically without being penalized, minimizes truancy and dropout risks, and allows students to receive full credit for attendance.



4.                  Participation in ATS provides students subject to out-of-school suspension with a constructive alternative to being out-of-school and in communities.



5.                  There are three sites that have been designated as pilot sites for the ATS program.  Those sites will serve specified schools with the highest number of out of school suspensions.



6.                  The respective sites must be leased by the School Board in order for portions of the respective sites to be utilized as ATS locations.



7.                  One site at 600 North Rosemary Avenue, West Palm Beach, is currently being leased by the Salvation Army.  The Superintendent was notified yesterday that the Salvation Army will not approve an ATS site at its facility unless a lease has been fully executed by the School Board.



8.                  The ATS Program is scheduled to begin on Tuesday, January 22, 2008.  Therefore, the School Board must execute the lease for this facility as soon as possible in order for the program to begin serving students and to expedite the ability to lease this site.



9.         If the City of West Palm Beach should decide that it prefers to handle this matter through an amendment to the 1994 interlocal agreement with the School Board, the School Baord hereby approves an amendment to that interlocal agreement adding the Salvation Army to the list of facilities eligible for use by the School Board.



 



The procedure of providing notice immediately, subsequent to executing the lease, is fair.  It provides sufficient procedural protections under the circumstances, because the Board is taking action necessary to begin the ATS pilot program at this particular site  for the specified school to reduce the drop-out and truancy risks associated with high suspension rates.  Notice of this emergency meeting was posted on the School District’s website the morning of January 16, 2008, prior to 9:45 a.m.  The Board is holding other special meetings today and they were advertised in the newspaper and appeared on the District’s website several days ago, staff recently learned of the necessity of emergency action to expedite the availability of this site, and the notice of this emergency meeting will be advertised in a newspaper of general circulation within the county as soon as possible.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the lease agreement or an amendment to the 1994 interlocal agreement with the City of West Palm Beach for use of one room at the Salvation Army Facility located at 600 North Rosemary Avenue, West Palm Beach for the purpose of housing a site location for the Alternative to Out of School Suspension Program and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all necessary documents and that the School Board find that an emergency exists under Fla. Stat. Sec. 120.525(3) as to an immediate danger to the public welfare requiring emergency and immediate action to approve the aforementioned lease today and that the procedures and notice are fair and provide sufficient procedural protections for the reasons as stated within the Board report on this item.
