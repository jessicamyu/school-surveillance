Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the claim form and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

Over the past decade, many groups including the State Attorney Generals for roughly half of the States, have been working to investigate and pursue remedies for any inappropriate actions in the financial markets.  Many settlements have been reached and the School Board participated in such a settlement with GE Funding Capital Markets Corp and UBS in 2012.  As additional settlements are reached and the School Board is notified they are a possible party, staff review the information to determine if it is appropriate to join the suit. 

 

The School Board received notice of another settlement, which includes all state, local and municipal government entities that purchased derivatives between January 1992 and August 18, 2011.  During that window, the School Board purchased seven derivatives which were used to lock investment rates of debt proceeds. Since this is a multi-state effort, governmental entities, such as the School Board, have been given the opportunity to “opt in” if they want to participate in the settlement agreement. 

 

The District’s Treasury and Legal Departments has conducted a conference call with the District’s Investment Advisor regarding this proposed settlement agreement as well as several calls regarding the previous settlement in 2012.   We learned the following:

  • The District was included in the settlements based on data received from Societe Generale SA and UBS AG.  The settlement amounts were calculated by financial experts and were not based on an issue by issue analysis.  Models were created to simulate market conditions if the alleged violations had not occurred.  At this time, the settlement award for the School Board has not been determined, but is estimated at approximately $100,000.
  • When the District’s transactions were completed, the pricing was compared to market indices and were determined to be at a fair market price.  
  • PFM Asset Management, the District’s Investment Advisor has reviewed their records again and confirmed that the District’s transactions were priced appropriately based on the market conditions at the time.
  • At the time of the transactions, the District had no reason to believe any inappropriate activity occurred.  Documents were signed by the parties specifically stating that the transactions were completed based on all applicable rules and regulations.

To “opt in”, the District must indicate the results of the previous settlements received in 2012.

The School District has installed centralized computerized building automation systems over the last 25 years to efficiently control the total environment of the schools.  Approximately 175 facilities are currently on-line.  Under this agreement, facilities throughout the District will have their centralized computerized automation systems monitored and if needed, updated to achieve additional energy and related cost savings.

 

This centralized computer system enables the District to:

  • Monitor, schedule, troubleshoot and analyze data at school center facilities 
  • Save energy and reduce energy cost
  • Control and monitor HVAC equipment, temperatures, humidity, outside lighting and consequently improve the overall environment of the facilities 
  • Reduce maintenance costs through efficient operations and increase the life cycle of equipment thus reducing capital expenditure for replacement equipment. 
  • Monitor District mechanical equipment and School Food Service cooling equipment and provides an automatic staff notification in the event of system failures
  • Control and monitor equipment required for hurricane shelters

 

The Purchasing Department successfully negotiated a five percent reduction in the equipment and technical multipliers which equates to approximately a $90,000 annual cost avoidance.

 

Per District policy 6.14, this Agreement resulted from a direct negotiation with Wisch and Jackson Company of Florida, Inc.  Wisch and Jackson is the only authorized local dealer for Automated Logic Corporation which manufactures the centralized computerized building automation systems used throughout the District.
