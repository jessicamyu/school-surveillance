Subject
PM2 Business Partnership Agreement - Perry J. Cohen Foundation - Created Wetlands Habitat and Outdoor Classroom Project
Meeting
Oct 26, 2016 - 2. SPECIAL MEETING immediately following Workshop in the Board Room
Category
9. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Business Partnership Agreement with the Perry J. Cohen Foundation, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign the document.

DESCRIPTION:

Jupiter Community High School, the Jupiter Environmental Research & Field Studies Academy and the Partnership for Environmental Education have been working for a number of years to transform the existing storm water retention pond at the front of Jupiter High School into a created wetlands habitat and outdoor classroom (the “Project”).  The Project envisions native plant materials, educational information and a boardwalk meeting Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) standards will provide an opportunity for hands-on education in an attractive and ecologically productive landscape.

 

The Perry J. Cohen Foundation is a registered not for profit 501(c)(3) organization devoted to the advancement of Boating Safety Education, Environmental, Marine, & Wildlife Education & Preservation, Teenage Entrepreneurship, and The Arts. The Foundation creates awareness and education for all individuals centered on the safe enjoyment of recreational boating, fishing, and water sports, facilitating a deeper connection between America’s youth and proper boating safety standards, along with an annual scholarship program that recognizes deserving students who plan to further their education in the Marine Science and Marine Wildlife fields.  In support of these programs and the preservation of our coastlines, the Foundation has committed to contribute and raise funds to complete the Project.

 

In keeping with School Board Policy 7.03 “Naming of School District Facilities and Dedicating Areas of School District Facilities or Grounds”, upon completion, the Project is to be named the “Perry J. Cohen Wetlands Laboratory at Jupiter High School.”

 

The project will comply with Board Policy 7.26 “Facilities or Grounds Modifications Funded by Internal Accounts or Donations”.

PM1 Construction Oversight and Review Committee (CORC): Advisory Board Member Request for Waiver- Tradrick McCoy
Meeting

11. New Business

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve Mr. Tradrick McCoy's request and a waiver be granted as provided under Section 112.313(12), F.S., allowing him to serve on the Construction Oversight and Review Committee while being an employee of Prime Time Palm Beach County, Inc.

DESCRIPTION:

Tradrick McCoy serves as a member of the School Board's Construction Oversight and Review Committee (CORC).

 

The Construction Oversight and Review Committee exists to assist the School Board by oversight and review of, and making recommendations concerning, the work of responsible departments to build quality schools on time and within budget.


CORC reviews facility development and management of contracts, affiliated planning processes, construction administration, project controls, real estate transactions, minority, women owned and small business enterprise practices and other aspects of the construction of facilities.

 

Mr. McCoy is now an employee of Prime Time Palm Beach County, Inc. (“Prime Time”) a non-profit organization, where he serves as a Financial Business Specialist.  Prime Time is involved with data sharing for afterschool planning and training, and has a contract with the School Board.

 

The State of Florida Commission on Ethics has determined there is a technical conflict of interest (see attached Exhibit A), but that it is resolvable by a Board waiver.  As a member of CORC, Mr. McCoy, upon request, may receive a waiver in a particular instance by the Board when made in a public meeting after a written full disclosure is made on Commission form CE-4A.  See attached Exhibit B which is comprised of signed forms CE-4A and School Board Advisory Committee Conflict of Interest Form PBSD 2421.

 

Mr. McCoy is willing and requests that the Board approve the waiver.

 

Please note that the grant of such a waiver requires at least a two-thirds vote of the Board.
