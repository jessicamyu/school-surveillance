Recommended Action
I recommend the Board adopt the FY2016 Five Year Capital Plan and the FY2016 Tentative District Educational Facilities Plan.

DESCRIPTION:

  • Each year, the School Board is required to adopt the Tentative District Educational Facilities Plan (Work Plan) prior to the adoption of the budget.
  • The Work Plan contains the same financial data as the Five Year Capital Plan, last presented to the School Board on August 29, 2015, but in a format created by the Florida Department of Education.
  • The first year of each plan is the FY 2016 Capital Budget that will be approved with the FY 2016 budget later today.
  • As required by law, the documents submitted for adoption are balanced.  Staff has also developed a version of the Five Year Capital Plan based on the basic needs of the School District.  This plan, also attached, is not balanced and indicates a shortfall of $625 million for the five years.  A summary of the reductions required to balance the documents is also attached.
  • The projects planned for FY 2017 through FY 2020 (years 2 through 5) can and will be amended as we work through the budget process for those years.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the purchase of school buses from Sun State International Trucks, LLC.

DESCRIPTION:

Purchase of school buses from Sun State International Trucks, LLC, a bid awarded vendor on Bid 15C-902A, an Alternate Source Contract off the State of Florida Bid 2015 01.

 

By Board Action on May 12, 2015 (FMPO9), approval was given to purchase a total of 131 buses off Bid 15C-902A. This request authorizes the purchase of an additional five buses, the total number of buses purchased will increase to 136 during FY16 and further authorizes the use of Sun State International Trucks, LLC.

 

Forty buses have been purchased since May 12th from Mathews Buses and the remaining 96 buses will be purchased from Sun State International Trucks, LLC.  Although Mathews Buses were able to offer us the best prices and availability for the initial purchase of 40 buses, based upon a review of the buses, pricing and availability, Sun State International Trucks, LLC has been selected for the remaining 96 buses.  During the review, all 3 vendors brought in sample buses to the District and transportation staff, including some drivers and mechanics, conducted test drives and discussed body features, seating configuration, engine performance, service and warranty support with manufacture representatives.   While all of the buses were found to be satisfactory, the bus offered by Sun State International Trucks, LLC was the most preferred.

 

The FY16 spend with Sun State International Trucks, LLC will not exceed $10,642,850.
