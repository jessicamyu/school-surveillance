I recommend the Board accept and approve the following Head Start items: 1) Head Start Program Information Reports; 2) Board Governance Training Update; 3) Governance Screener and Certification; and 4) Health and Safety Screener and Certification, and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

The School Board of Palm Beach County is the governing body for District Head Start and Early Head Start programs.  As such, Head Start Performance Standards require the Board to review and/or certify select reports.

 

Program Information Report (PIR) – The Department of Early Childhood Education (ECE) submits Program Information Reports (PIRs) for the District’s Head Start and Early Head programs annually to provide comprehensive data on services, staff, children, and families.  According to an indicator report sent by grantee Lutheran Services Florida (LSF), ECE met all indicators.

Reports: PIR Early Head Start; PIR Head Start

 

Board Governance Training Update - The Head Start Program Performance Standards require that each delegate agency annually provide Board Governance training for its Governing Board.  ECE has summarized Board Governance information in a training PowerPoint for Board member review and use.

Document: Board Governance Training Update PP

 

Governance Screener and Certification – Head Start delegate agencies must complete the federal Office of Head Start (OHS) Governance, Leadership, and Oversight Capacity Screener within 60 days of the start of the program or school year.  ECE completed the Governance Screener, which indicates that Head Start Governance Practices are currently in place.  As the governing body, the Board must submit a signed Certification of Governance & Leadership Capacity Screening form.

Report and Certification Form:  Head Start Governance Screener; Certification of Governance & Leadership Capacity Screening

 

Health and Safety Screener and Certification – ECE has conducted the mandated screening of the health and safety environment of each participating school site.  Staff will submit a report that includes the status of the areas of Environment; Health and Safety Procedures; Supervision; and if applicable, Transportation.  Preliminary results show that all areas will be in compliance, pending minor modifications.  As the governing body, the Board must submit a signed Certification of Health and Safety Screening form after review of the completed report.

Report and Certification Form: Head Start Health and Safety Screener (Sample Report), Certification of Health and Safety Screening

I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with JFK Medical Center Limited Partnership d/b/a JFK Medical Center, effective October 19, 2017 through October 19, 2019; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to meet clinical requirements of the Health Science Education program, pursuant to the Florida Department of Education Curriculum Frameworks. This clinical component is also required by the various Boards regulating specific professions such as the Florida Board of Nursing, Florida Board of Pharmacy, Emergency Medical Services, etc.

In order to expand our students’ opportunities for clinical experiences we are adding the above referenced agreement. This agreement originated with John I. Leonard High School, but is available to all schools and students in the Medical Sciences Academies.
 

Full-time Health Science Education programs are located in the following high schools: Atlantic, Boca Raton, Boynton Beach, Forest Hill, Glades Central, Inlet Grove, John I. Leonard, Jupiter, Lake Worth, Pahokee, Palm Beach Gardens, Palm Beach Lakes, Park Vista, Royal Palm Beach, Santaluces, Wellington, West Boca Raton, Somerset Academy and South Tech Academy. The Health Science Education Programs impact an estimated 6,000 high school students.
 

The programs include Allied Health Assisting, Biomedical Sciences, Dental Aide, Dental Laboratory Assisting, Electrocardiograph Technician, Emergency Medical Responder, Health and Wellness, Health Unit Coordinator, Home Health Aide, Medical Laboratory Assisting, Nursing Assistant, Patient Care Assistant, Pharmacy Technician, Practical Nursing, Vision Care Assisting, Health Science Education Directed Study and OJT which prepares students with entry level job skills and exposes them to career opportunities. The Health Science Education program also provides the frameworks, certifications, and articulations for matriculation to post-secondary education in health occupations and related programs of study.

The focus is on students in 6th – 12th grade so they are motivated through incentive and enrichment activities to improve their academic achievement, stay in school, succeed, advance to the next grade level and build their personal strengths. Communities In Schools is focused on building self-sufficiency by providing teacher inspired and intangible methodologies that concentrate on an assets based approach delivery of services.

These students are serviced by CISPBC staff because of poor test scores, multiple retentions, poor attendance, or low grades (all characteristics of students who are at risk of dropping out of school). Research has proven that school dropouts pose an economic and social risk to the community. Communities In Schools is an evidence-based program.

The scope of service is prevention and intervention focused on strength building and self-sufficiency skills. These skills are then linked to the student at school and/or to the student and family at home, and at school. The Graduation Coach provides case management, works individually and in groups with the students and families to address the non-educational health and human services needs that are impacting the student’s ability to learn. They also get parents involved with their child’s education. Additionally, the Graduation Coach links the youth and the family, with needed community services.

  • Improved behavior
