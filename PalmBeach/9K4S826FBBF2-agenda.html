  •  The annual Comprehensive Safety Inspections were performed by District Fire and Safety Inspectors as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). All educational, auxiliary and ancillary facilities were included in these inspections, using (SREF) for casualty and sanitation, and Florida Legislative Chapter 69A-58 for fire safety. In addition, the district Charter schools were inspected for fire safety using (NFPA 101). District Fire and Safety Inspectors worked in conjunction with the local fire marshals for each facility.




  •     The District successfully reduced the number of citations for Public Schools, Alternative Schools, Charter Schools and Ancillary Facilities from 6,262 in 2009 to 3491 in 2010. This represents a 45% reduction over one year and a 93% reduction since 2000.   The District was also able to reduce the overall number of citations while increasing the facility area.  All conditions considered by the inspector to be hazardous or posing an imminent danger were reported to Maintenance at the time of discovery for immediate action.




  •     A summary of the number of citations for each school and ancillary facility is included with this report. Individual school reports are on file with the Building Department.




  •     According to SREF, Chapter 5, “The inspection reports shall be approved by the Board, and the Board shall adopt a plan and a schedule for the correction of each deficiency.” The corrective action plans for each citation were developed in conjunction with the local fire authorities and are included on each report.




  •     Upon the board approving the inspection reports, copies of the reports will be submitted to the State Fire Marshal as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). These reports must be delivered to the State Fire Marshal by June 30, 2010.

I recommend the School Board approve the Comprehensive Safety Inspection Report for FY 2010.

  • In accordance with Title I Part A of the ESEA Act as reauthorized by the NCLB Act (P. L. 107-110), Section 1116 (e) (2) and (3), SES must be offered to eligible students in schools that have not made AYP for three or more consecutive years.  The School District of Palm Beach County offers SES tutorial services in accordance with Florida’s federally approved plan for Differentiated Accountability (DA).  Under this plan, SES must be offered to eligible students in schools that have not made AYP for two or more consecutive years.




  • Students are eligible if they are receiving free or reduced-price meals and they attend an eligible school. 




  • The State of Florida provides each school district with a list of approved providers.  Each provider must sign the contract with the district.  Parents may then choose a provider from the list of approved providers who signed a contract. 




  • Parents of eligible students may choose SES tutoring instead of opting for Choice with Transportation (CWT). All Title I schools are required to offer school-based tutorial services. May families take advantage of the school's tutorial program instead of SES.




  • The services are paid from the mandated Title I 20% set aside funds.




  • For FY 2010, the state has set the per pupil amount at $1,435.00.

Debris Monitoring Services for Disaster Recovery Assistance are required to assist in debris collection during disaster response. Awarded firm(s) will provide management services and technical assistance in regard to the monitoring of disaster debris collection on an as-needed basis.



The term of the contract will be from June 3, 2010 through June 2, 2013, with the option to renew for two (2) additional one (1) year periods.   



 



The work involves pre-event and post event services, which may include, but is not limited to:



Pre-event



·         Provide assistance in the preparation for a disaster through participation in meetings, workshops, training sessions and the refinement of Debris Management Plans including data management and related systems.



·         Provide debris estimation models or aids.



·         Review temporary debris management sites (TDMS).



Post Event



·         Assist with damage assessments or debris estimations.



·         Monitor multiple truck pickups, issue load tickets, record load site information,  delineate FEMA designations, and document contractor actions in cases of damaged property or other field oversight functions.



·         Provide other field services related to dangerous trees (hangers and leaners) and stumps.



 



The response of the selected monitoring Proposer(s) to the disaster recovery process must be immediate, rapid, and efficient with acceptable cost controls, accountability procedures, written reports and submittals to assure that the District shall have the means to be reimbursed for all eligible disaster recovery costs from appropriate Federal, State and private agencies.  Response will typically be activated only in the event of an emergency and in accordance with an awarded contract.  Response activation will be through issuance of a Purchase Order. 



         



Four (4) firms responded to the RFP. Two are recommended for award. The final ranking of the Selection Committee is as follows: 



 



(1)            Post Buckley Schuh & Jernigan, Inc. (Primary)



(2)            Beck Disaster Recovery, Inc. (Secondary)

BACKGROUND



In January 2005, The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) adopted new rules that required the transition from the then-existing Instructional Television Fixed Service (ITFS)/Multipoint Distribution Service (MDS) bandplan ("Old Bandplan") to a new Educational Broadband Service (EBS) bandplan ("EBS Bandplan").  See attached Bandplan Exhibit.



In most major markets, the transition of the license holders in the market is handled by a commercial operator who acts as the proponent for the transition.  The primary obligation of a transition proponent is to migrate the license holder's programming from the licensed channel frequencies under the old Bandplan to the licensed channel frequencies under the EBS Bandplan.



The transition proponent can accomplish this migration by either; (1) providing and installing equipment that allows the license holder to broadcast and receive on the EBS Bandplan licensed frequencies; or (2) pay to the license holder the estimated cost of the license holder purchasing and installing equipment that allows the license holder to broadcast and receive on the EBS Bandplan licensed frequencies.



In the Palm Beach County market, WBSWP Licensing LLC ("WBSWP") filed with the FCC to be the transition proponent.  Based on the experience and know-how of the School Board staff at The Education Network (TEN), it was determined that negotiating an agreement that allowed the School Board to purchase and install the equipment of its choice that would be compatible with existing and future equipment used by TEN was in the School Board's best interest.



Some of the equipment required to transmit and receive has already been purchased by TEN from previous capital budget allocations.  TEN staff estimates that it will cost approximately $1.5 million in additional funds to complete the purchase and installation of the needed equipment.  Therefore, it is recommended that the funds to be received from WBSWP be specifically earmarked for the expenses related to the purchase and installation of the additional equipment to solve digital problems with reception to schools.



The essential elements of the Transition Agreement are:



WBSWP will pay to the School Board $1.75 million;



School Board is responsible for the purchase and installation of all equipment necessary to transmit and receive TEN's programming;



School Board will cease broadcasting on the Old Bandplan frequencies and begin broadcasting on the EBS Bandplan frequencies;



If the School Board leases any of its channels in the future, the School Board will not assert that the funds received from WBSWP was in excess of the School Board's eligible transition expenses and will require any future lessee to acknowledge and accept that the amount was reasonable.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the EBS Transition Agreement, that it earmark the funds received for future purchase and installation of equipment needed to transmit and receive TEN's programming, to solve digital problems with reception to schools, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all necessary documents.
