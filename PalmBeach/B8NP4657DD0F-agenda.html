 DESCRIPTION:

Commencing for the 2019-2020 school year:  Study SH-1 proposes changes in student attendance boundaries for South Grade, Highland, Barton and Palms Springs Elementary Schools.  Studies M-1 & J-1 propose changes in student attendance boundaries for Melaleuca and Pine Jog Elementary Schools and John I. Leonard and Palm Beach Central High Schools.

 

Pursuant to Policy 5.01, Student Assignment, the purpose of the Workshop is to provide a summary of the Advisory Boundary Committee’s recommendation for the 2019-20 Boundary Cycle which would shift a portion of the current Highland & South Grade attendance zones to Barton and Palm Springs Elementary, and consolidate a future residential development by shifting a portion of the development from the Pine Jog to Melaleuca Elementary zone and Palm Beach Central to John I. Leonard High zone.

 

Study SH-1 – Highland & South Grade Enrollment Relief

 

On January 17, 2019 the ABC voted 11-0 to present the following Boundary Change Proposal to the Superintendent:

 

“Study SH-1”:  Highland & South Grade Elementary Relief (Highland to Barton & Palm Springs Elementary; South Grade to Barton Elementary)

 

Highland is currently the District's second most overcrowded elementary school at 114% utilization, enrolling 152 students over its designed capacity. The school is projected to further increase its enrollment during the next five years, with projected utilization eclipsing 120% in a few years. South Grade is the District’s fourth most overcrowded elementary school at 108% utilization, also with a projected increasing enrollment that is forecast to eclipse 110% utilization in the next few years.  As such, both schools are in need of enrollment relief with an attendance zone change being the recommended solution.

 

The study recommended by the ABC provides relief for Highland & South Grade Elementary Schools by shifting the northern portion of Highland SAC 201 to Palm Springs Elementary, and shifting the southern portion of SAC 201 to Barton Elementary.  The southern portion of South Grade Elementary SAC 214B is proposed to shift to Barton Elementary.  This proposal better balances enrollment at the four schools; decreasing enrollment at Highland and South Grade while increasing enrollment at Barton and Palm Springs Elementary Schools.

 

During the December 20, 2018, Community Input Meeting at Lake Worth Middle School, there were two speakers who addressed the ABC. Both spoke in favor of Study SH-1, which included City of Lake Worth Commissioner Omari Hardy.

 

Parent concerns regarding the in-house Spanish Dual Language Programs at Highland and South Grade were brought to the attention of the respective Principals.  Spanish Dual Language Programs currently do not exist at Barton and Palm Springs Elementary, though Palm Springs is anticipated to begin the program for SY2019-20 at the kindergarten and first grade levels.  Parents of students residing in the proposed change areas, whose children are currently enrolled in the Highland or South Grade Spanish Dual Language Programs, are requesting that their children be permitted to remain at Highland and South Grade.
 

 

Studies M-1 & J-1 – Consolidate future development into singular elementary and high school zones

 

On January 17, 2019 the ABC voted 10-1 to present the following Boundary Change Proposal to the Superintendent:

 

“Studies M-1 & J-1”:  Consolidate the future Solcera development into the Melaleuca Elementary & John I. Leonard High School attendance zones

 

This future residential development will contain 109 single-family units and is located just south of Southern Blvd. at Jog Road. The development is bisected by Jog Road, with the western portion (53 units) zoned to Pine Jog Elementary & Palm Beach Central High and the eastern portion (56 units) zoned to Melaleuca and John I. Leonard High. This study consolidates the entire development into singular elementary and high school attendance zones. There are currently no students residing in this future development.

 

The developer brought the split attendance zones to the attention of District staff.  Based on an assessment of projected enrollment & utilization at the four schools, staff proposed that the future development be consolidated into the Melaleuca Elementary and John I. Leonard High attendance zones.  Upon proposing the study to the ABC, the developer disagreed with the proposed assigned schools and is instead requesting that the future development be consolidated into the Pine Jog Elementary and Palm Beach Central High zones, or else remain split among the four schools.

 

The lone dissenting ABC vote was of the opinion that with new high school "OOO" opening in the near future and associated wide-scale high school attendance zone changes, that this area be best left alone until the "OOO" attendance zone is established.
