T1 - Google Apps Educational Edition Agreement
Meeting
Oct 14, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshop re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)
This contract is for the SAAS (Software as a Service) for the use of Google Apps Education Edition. This suite of products includes e-mail, e-mail filtering/security, e-mail archive, calendar, instant messaging, web conferencing, and word processing, amongst others. This service is renewed on an annual basis, and comes at no cost for the Education sector minus the archive component. The e-mail archive component carries a per user cost which will be applied to our employees specifically.
Google Apps will allow for collaboration of our employees, schools, students, and community wherever possible; The Google Search Appliance (GSA) provides fast, relevant search of our intranet or website; moreover, the e-mail security component will provide for safe content filtering of student e-mail, while allowing communication and collaboration. Finally, the School District will save a substantial amount of money realized over the next 3 years which can be distributed across other areas.

Recommendation:

We recommend the School Board sign this agreement and authorize the Superintendent to sign all related documents and contracts.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore, Chief Operating Officer moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us
Deepak Agarwal, Chief Information Officer Deepak.Agarwal@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is $400,750 which includes Google Apps at zero cost, Email Archiving, a one time cost for Google Search Appliance (GSA) and a one time setup cost.

IN1 - Approval of Contract with United Healthcare to Administer the Employee Medical Plan
Meeting
Oct 14, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshop re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

On August 26, 2009, the Board rejected all bids for RFP 09C-007K and upon finding that an emergency existed, directed Staff to negotiate with the current carrier, United HealthCare.



United HealthCare has agreed to provide administration services at a monthly per covered employee/retiree fee of $23.55 for 2010.  Year two (2) increases will be capped at three (3) percent.  The fee also includes:





  • Disease management for asthma, diabetes, CAD, and heart failure which will be added to the existing covered programs (healthy pregnancy, kidney, cancer and transplant services).  This will enable us to cancel Health Dialogue. 

  • Nurseline, a toll-free dedicated phone number, staffed 24/7 by nurses to answer questions from members and dependents on any health issue. 

  • Cobra administration through their MWBE partner, Benefits Outsources, Inc. is included at a value of $168,000. 

  • One hundred (100) percent of pharmacy rebates will be returned to the District, versus the current eighty (80) percent (increase of approximately $500,000 returned to the District).

  • Wellness programs to include biometric screenings or flu shots as requested by the District as well as on-line coaching programs for smoking cessation, weight loss, nutrition and exercise, to name a few.


Individual stop-loss of $5 million over a $1 million individual retention is $1.44 per employee/retiree per month (approximately $374,734 based on 21,686 covered employees/retirees).



United HealthCare has agreed to performance guarantees for claims administration services, claim payment timelines, pharmacy financial guarantees, as well as achieving network discounts ($1.8 million is at risk if their discounts fall below sixty seven (67) percent).



One dollar ($1.00) per employee/retiree per month multi-line discount is applied to the administrative fee, as long as we continue our Dental Plan with United.



Two hundred thousant dollars ($200,000) is provided to the District for wellness positions and/or program funding.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board approve the contract with United HealthCare to act as servicing agent to administer the District's employee medical plan as well as provide individual stop loss insurance for a one-year period from January 1, 2010 through December 31, 2010 with an option to renew for one additional year.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore, Chief Operating Officer (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Dianne L. Howard, Director of Risk and Benefits Management (dhoward@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

Financial Impact:

$5.87 million, based on $21,686 covered employees/retirees.

PL-1 Effective Broward County-Palm Beach County Annexation Interlocal Agreement
Meeting
Oct 14, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshop re Operations
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

State lawmakers originally approved the county boundary change by adopting legislation in 2007.  The area involved is a triangle commonly called ‘Lox Road’, ‘the Golden Triangle’ or ‘the Wedge’.   The change was contingent upon a deletion of University and Riverside Drive extensions from Palm Beach County’s Comprehensive Plan which has now occurred.  As such, the county boundary change officially transferred on September 21, 2009.



14 students who reside within this area have been attending Palm Beach County Schools with transportation provided by the District.  A meeting with all affected parents and students was held at the Palm Beach County School District offices on August 13, 2009 to inform them of the annexation progress, the options for their students’ education in SY 2009-10, and to determine their wishes for SY 2009-10.  The interlocal agreement with Broward is for the 2009-10 school year with the understanding that students will attend Broward County schools with transportation if deemed appropriate for the 2010-11 school year.

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the Interlocal Annexation Agreement with the Board of Broward County/Palm Beach County School District to allow students who reside in former SAC 410N to attend Palm Beach County schools for the remainder of SY 2009-10.

Contact:

 Joseph M. Moore, Chief Operating Officer



moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us



Joseph M. Sanches, Chief of Facilities Management



sanchesj@palmbeach.k12.fl.us

Financial Impact:

None.  The appropriate FTE will be pro-rated if students depart mid-year.  Parent’s have agreed to provide transportation if Palm Beach County School District does not.
