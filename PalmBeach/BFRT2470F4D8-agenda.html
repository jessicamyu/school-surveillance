Subject
SP3 Request to the Sheriff to Establish the Coach Aaron Feis Guardian Program
Meeting
Oct 16, 2019 - 6. REGULAR BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room
Category
4. Superintendent Withdraws Items
Type
Information

DESCRIPTION:

The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida acknowledges that on May 8, 2019, Governor DeSantis signed SB 7030 Implementation of Legislative Recommendations of the Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School Public Safety Commission.

 

Since the enactment of this legislation, The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida, has worked to facilitate charter school access to all safe school officer options available under F.S. 1006.12. The safe school officer options under F.S. 1006.12 are:

  1. Establish school resource officer programs, through a cooperative agreement with law enforcement agencies.
  1. Commission one or more school safety officers for the protection and safety of school personnel, property, and students within the school district. These officers must be “certified law enforcement officers, as defined in s. 943.10(4).”
  1. At the school district’s or the charter school governing board’s discretion, as applicable, pursuant to §30.15, a school district or charter school governing board may participate in the Coach Aaron Feis Guardian Program to meet the requirement of establishing a safe-school officer.
  1. A school district or charter school governing board may contract with a security agency as defined in §493.6101(18) to employ as a school security guard an individual who holds a Class “D” and Class “G” license pursuant to chapter 493, provided certain training and contractual conditions are met.

Where Charter Schools in Palm Beach County have expressed an interest in utilizing option three (3), the Coach Aaron Feis Guardian Program, to fulfill their Safe School Officer requirements, there is a need for a Coach Aaron Feis Guardian program to be established in Palm Beach County, Florida. On June 19, 2019 the School Board acknowledged that Charter Schools have access to all four Safe School Officer positions, including the Coach Aaron Feis Guardian Program. Subsequently, on July 17, 2019 the School District requested information from Sheriff Bradshaw regarding the establishment of the Guardian Program in Palm Beach County.

 

Despite action by the Board and charter school requests for the Guardian Program, and notwithstanding the District’s request to enter into an MOU with the Sheriff, including the creation of the Guardian Program, the Palm Beach County Sheriff has not as of today’s date formally established a Guardian Program in Palm Beach County. However, the Sheriff has offered limited firearms training for the Security Guard option under F.S. 1006.12(4).

 

The establishment of a Guardian program in Palm Beach County, rather than utilizing programs established in other counties, will assist in meeting the needs of the Palm Beach County Charter Schools, as well as provide oversight and continued monitoring of the program by the local Sheriff under Florida Law.

 

Therefore, based on the foregoing and in a continued effort to facilitate access to all four options, pursuant to F.S. 30.15(k)1.a., the School Board of Palm Beach County hereby requests the Palm Beach County Sheriff establish a Coach Aaron Feis Guardian program to provide training, pursuant to F.S. 30.15(k), subparagraph 2., to charter school employees, either directly or through a contract with another sheriff’s office that has established a Guardian Program.

Given Invictus’ failure to provide training that would meet requirements established by SB 7030, the security guards were required to attend a second training conducted by the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office and to continue training with Palm Beach County School Police while waiting for the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office’s program to commence.  When the guards began their second training with the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Office and continued training with Palm Beach County School Police they had completed their 144 hour training with Invictus.   

 

Amendment 1 of the Agreements will allow the Board to pay Dynamic Integrated Security, Inc.; Metro One Loss Prevention Group, Inc. and Sphere, Inc., $38.50 per hour for those guards that attended and successfully completed the second training provided by the Palm Beach County Sheriff’s Department and the continued training with Palm Beach County School Police. Amendment 1 will further provide that upon temporary deployment to charter schools and/or District ancillary sites or schools if needed, the Board will continue to pay Dynamic Integrated Security, Inc.; Metro One Loss Prevention Group, Inc. and Sphere, Inc. a rate of $38.50 per hour per guard.     

 

While the District was waiting for the Palm Beach County Sheriff's Office (PBSO) to begin their Safe-Schools Security Guard Training Program Class #1 (SSGTP Class 1), the School Police directed the security guards to report to the School Police Department's Safe Schools Institute training facility in Boca Raton. The decision to have the security guards continue training was to ensure the District maintained a contingent of candidates until such time as they were able to attend the PBSO SSSGTP Class 1.
