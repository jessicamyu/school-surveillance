I recommend the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp consider the annual election of officers.

DESCRIPTION:

The Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp is a Florida not-for-profit corporation that has as its sole member the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida.  The corporation facilitates the School District’s lease purchase financing of school facilities, commonly referred to as COPs (or Certificates of Participation).  

 

A summary of the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp is attached.

 

The bylaws of the Leasing Corp require an annual meeting of the Board of Directors to elect corporate officers.  Palm Beach School Board Members or School District Staff may be elected to serve as officers.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Palm Beach School Board Leasing Corp approve the resolution authorizing the remarketing Certificates of Participation Series 2014A with an aggregate principal amount of $115,560,000 and authorize execution of related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • In 2002, the School Board approved the issuance of the 2002B COP issue to fund the construction of several schools.  The transaction was issued in a variable rate mode with weekly resets.  To reduce interest rate risk, an interest rate hedge was layered on top of the transaction.  When compared to a traditional fixed rate debt issue, this structure has saved approximately $7.5 million since 2002.
  • In 2011, the School Board approved a change to the interest rate mode.  Rather than a weekly reset with interest rates that could be impacted by the credit of the bank managing the transaction, the bond insurer, both of which were negatively impacted during the financial crisis, the rate was set based on an index plus a spread of 0.7% to be in place until February 2014.
  • In 2013, prior to the rate expiration scheduled for February 2014, the School Board approved a refunding of the transaction eliminating the 2002B COP issue and creating the 2014A COP issue.  The interest rate was again based on an index but with a reduced spread of 0.46% to be in place until January 30, 2017.
  • This resolution authorizes the remarketing of the 2014A COP issue prior to the January 2017 rate expiration.  The rate will again be set to an index plus 0.42%.  Rather than waiting until the expiration in February, the bank (Bank of America) is willing to waive the remaining period and allow the District to use the lower rate effective December 15th, 2016.  This rate will be in place until January 30, 2020.
  • The transaction was reviewed by the Finance Committee during their meeting on November 4, 2016 and was approved unanimously.  A draft of the meeting minutes is available online at https://www.palmbeachschools.org/treasury/FinComm2016/
