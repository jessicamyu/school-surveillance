Eighteen Jupiter Environmental Research and Field Studies Academy (JERFSA) junior and senior students will be traveling to Panama in support of their studies.   • Ecological, geological, and cultural instructions for students;
  • Broadening of personal experience for students;

I recommend the School Board approve two (2) grant awards for the 21st Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) Grants from the Florida Department of Education for up to $6,160,000, effective August 1, 2013 through July 31, 2018, and approve any no cost extension as applicable; and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District of Palm Beach County has been awarded two (2) competitive grants from the Florida Department of Education for the 21st Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) Program for up to $6,160,000 for both grants over a five-year period, from August 1, 2013 through July 31, 2018.  The District received notification of these grant awards on October 15, 2013. The Florida Department of Education will release the grant funds in November, following attendance at a required state project meeting for all awardees. Therefore, the 21st CCLC programs for the students will begin in November.

 

The 21st CCLC Grant supports community learning centers for academic enrichment opportunities during afterschool and summer. These programs will assist students in meeting standards in core academic subjects, such as reading and math; offer students a broad array of enrichment activities to complement their regular academic programs; and offer literacy and other educational services to the families of participating children.

 

The two (2) grant programs target a total of ten (10) elementary schools (five schools in each grant) with a high percentage of low income students. The 21st CCLC Kreative Kidz Project includes CO Taylor /Kirklane Elementary, Forest Park Elementary, Hope Centennial Elementary, Seminole Trails Elementary, and Westward Elementary.

 

The 21st CCLC Blazing Starz Project includes Cholee Lakes Elementary, Diamond View Elementary, Jupiter Elementary, North Grade Elementary, and Plumosa Elementary.

 

The 21st CCLC grant budgets will support 60 at-risk students in grades K-5 at each school. These students will be selected to participate based on one or more of the following criteria:

  • below grade level on recent school and/or state assessments
  • students who have been retained or in jeopardy of being retained
  • ELL and/or ESE students
  • vulnerable students: homeless or foster children

The 21st CCLC goals in both grants are: 1) improve academic performance; 2) increase social competence; 3) increase parental involvement and family literacy; and 4) improve physical fitness and lifestyle choices. The 21st CCLC Grant requires and funds an external evaluator who will assess quantitative and qualitative data to determine the project's impact on these goals.

 

Services will be provided free to students and their families, including afterschool and summer programs. Student learning activities include the following components: literacy enrichment; Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) education; arts and music; technology; tutoring and mentoring; service learning; physical education, recreation, and wellness; and dropout prevention, character education, and drug/violence prevention. Additionally, Parent/Caregiver activities focus on family literacy, including parent education in literacy and computer skills; parenting skills to support the child's learning; and inter-generational literacy: Parent and Child Together Time (PACT) to promote literacy and language development.

 

Each grant has been awarded up to $3,080,000 for a project period of five years, totaling up to $6,160,000 for both grants for five years. The maximum award per grant is $700,000 per award for years 1 and 2; and $560,000 for years 3, 4, & 5. Copies of both grants are available for review in the Board Minutes Office.

Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve the revised Interagency Agreement effective November 21, 2013 through June 30, 2015, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the agreement which are required for implementation of the Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

The biennial interagency agreement has been signed to better meet the needs of children in foster care.  Every Florida school district received a directive from the state agency to develop an Interagency Agreement based around a model to coordinate services for children in the Florida child welfare system.
 

The Interagency Agreement includes:

  • The School Board of Palm Beach County;
  • Agency for Persons with Disabilities, State of Florida, Area 9;
  • Florida Department of Children and Families, Circuit 15;
  • Florida Department of Juvenile Justice, Circuit 15;
  • ChildNet, Inc. (ChildNet); 
  • Workforce Alliance, Inc.; and
