Subject
Educational Equity Audit
Meeting
Jan 8, 2014 - 2. WORKSHOP following A/C Session in Board Room
Category
3. Workshop Items
Type

DESCRIPTION:

The purpose of an Educational Equity Audit is to provide the School District of Palm Beach County (SDPBC) with evidence of the extent to which culture, practices, and policies promote equitable opportunities for all students and staff members.  The audit will provide legally compliant recommendations for educational policies, practices, resources, and programs necessary to eliminate educational barriers and provide opportunities for employees and students.

 

Comparisons will be made to other large urban districts in Florida and the nation with student populations exceeding 150,000 students to determine the extent to which challenges identified are unique to SDPBC.  Recommendations will be based on best practices and successful examples from other large urban districts with evidence of creating a fair and equitable environment in classrooms, schools, and at the district level.

2. Discussion Item - ROBINSON - Graduation Rates
Meeting
Jan 8, 2014 - 2. WORKSHOP following A/C Session in Board Room
Category
3. Workshop Items
Type
Discussion

Dr. Robinson would like to discuss having a workshop on graduation rates.

 

Topics will include, but not be limited to:

  • Changes to graduation requirements - in recent and in coming years
  • How students who are not meeting the requirements are "caught up"
  • Impact of schools "owning" test scores of students who leave to go to alternative sites; i.e. alternative ed, jail, etc.
  • Impact of discontinuing NCLB transfers and transportation
  • Next step after review of "moving targets"
  • Micro-targeting our efforts
  • How to better address the needs of students in alternative ed
  • How are choice programs improving graduation rates of formerly low-performing students
  • Job skills and academic background for students who initially appear to not be college bound
  • How to better engage and empower parents
  • Role of guidance counselors and appropriate staffing levels
  • Outline roll-out of recommendations from the Superintendent's Task Force on the African American male graduation rate
