I recommend the School Board approve the 2013 Update of the Self Evaluation and ADA Transition Plan

DESCRIPTION:  

 

  • In 1990, Congress enacted the American with Disabilities Act (ADA) to address the problem of discrimination against persons with disabilities. Specifically, the ADA prohibits discrimination in employment (Title I), in public services and public transportation (Title II), in public accommodations (Title III), and in telecommunications (Title IV).
  • Federal regulations enacted to implement Title II of the ADA required the District to prepare a   transition plan if structural changes were undertaken to  achieve program accessibility. The federal regulations require the District’s transition plan to:

1.  Identify physical obstacles in the public entity's facilities that limit the accessibility of its programs or activities to individuals with disabilities. 

2.  Describe the methods that will be used to make the facilities accessible;

3.  Specify the schedule for taking the steps necessary to achieve compliance with this section and, if the time period of the transition plan is longer than one year, identify steps that will be taken during each year of the transition period; and

4.  Indicate the official responsible for implementation of the plan. 

  • The 2013 Update of the Transition Plan reassessed existing facilities against current ADA guidelines and evaluated program accessibility to persons with disabilities.  The primary objective of the Transition Plan is to document facility improvements necessary to provide program access. 
  • The 2013 Update of the Transition Plan recognizes existing Board policies that provide program access to students and their parents/guardians through Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 and Title II of the ADA, program access to the public as required by Title II of the ADA,  and program access for applicants/employees through Equal Employment Opportunity regulations of Title I of the ADA. 
  • The approach to completing and updating the Self Evaluation and Transition Plan is focused on providing access to our students and their parents/guardians and the general public.  A required element of the Transition Plan is to provide a schedule for taking the necessary steps to achieve compliance with Title II.  The proposed schedule to provide program access to the general public is based on a priority assignment model  as follows:

Completion Dates

High Priority           July 1, 2020

Medium Priority  July 1, 2024

Low Priority            July 1, 2026

I recommend that the School Board approve the College Board AP Insight © Agreement and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

 

AP Insight© is an online resource developed by the College Board to support instruction and student performance in AP Biology.  Based on extensive data analysis, The College Board identified 12 Challenge Areas in AP Biology that most students struggle to surpass. Based on these Challenge Areas, AP Insight© was developed to provide online instructional resources, formative assessments and feedback reports designed to support both teachers and students with a goal of improving student understanding and achievement on the AP Biology exam. In this way, AP Insight© helps teachers invest classroom time where it matters most. The formative assessments, instructional strategies and professional learning help teachers and students overcome the greatest challenges to understanding and succeeding in AP Biology by focusing on topics that are the most difficult to teach and learn, while strategically building the foundations necessary for course mastery.

 

The instructional resources help teachers activate students, anticipate likely stumbling blocks and adapt instruction on the spot.  The online resources include performance tasks, simulations and other hands-on activities designed by expert AP teachers to engage and motivate students with real-world problem-solving and applied skills. Because students can also access the online resources they are empowered to monitor their own learning. Formative assessments are provided to give both teachers and students insights to clarify misunderstandings and determine instructional needs. The feedback reports enable teachers to close learning gaps with targeted next steps while keeping the class moving successfully.

 

AP Insight© was successfully piloted last year with several districts, including Hillsborough County Public Schools in Tampa and the North East Florida Educational Consortium. The School District of Palm Beach County has been invited to collaborate with the College Board during 2013-14 by participating in the use of AP Insight© as part of their research to identify successful implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

The School District has had a partnership agreement with the Safety Council of Palm Beach County, Inc., a not-for-profit organization, to provide an on-the-road driving experience to students in Palm Beach County schools. The current program consists of the following:
 
1. The School District provides $148,500 each year to the Safety Council to pay for the administration of the on-the-road driving program. These funds assist the Safety Council for the on-the-road driving experience to provide professionally trained instructors, advertisements for the program, scheduling of students into behind-the-wheel driving sessions, and maintenance, fuel, management of the office, phone, lights, and storage of the vehicles used to teach the courses.  This is the only expense to the district for Driver Education on-the-road training.
 
2. In 2002 the State passed the Dori Slosberg Driver Education Safety Act. which assessed a $3 fee to certain moving violations to help provide for an expanded driving program for a greater number of students. Through an inter-local agreement, The School District acts as a pass-through for the funds generated in Palm Beach County, receiving funds from the County quarterly and passing them on to the Safety Council quarterly.   In April 2003 it was agreed that The School District would pay the Safety Council up to $150,000 per quarter.
 
3. In 2007 an amendment to the Dori Slosberg Driver Education Safety Act was passed, increasing the violation fee assessed from $3 to $5. Because of this increased revenue, the fee structure would change from the original maximum of $150,000 per quarter to the following:
 

 

The School District would pass to the Safety Council 90% of the funds received from the County Commission. The remaining 10% would be used by the School District as designated in the Dori Slosberg Driver Education Act for other Driver Education necessities.  Since recent quarterly collections are approaching $250,000, pass-through funding to the Safety Council up to one million dollars would be allowed.

Aug 21, 2013 - 1. SCHOOL BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. - Board Room

Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board approve a perpetual non-exclusive drainage easement to the Town of Lake Clarke Shores; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  1. The Town of Lake Clarke Shores purchased and operates the water system that includes a service area beyond the Town boundary.
  2. In 1994 the Town of Lake Clarke Shores sold to the School District part of Tract "C" of the Seminole Manor Development in support of the District to build Starlight Cove Elementary school.
  3. The details of the sale included the Town retaining their water treatment plant site (+1.58 acres) and the School District providing a 24 foot wide access easement to the Town's property.
  4. The School Board also acquired an additional parcel of land (portion of Lake Tract) immediately adjacent and north of the Town's water treatment plant site for Starlight Cove Elementary. (See Starlight Cove El./Town of Lake Clarke Shores Drainage Easement Map).
  5. For the last 19 years the Town and the District have had a cooperative working relationship between the water treatment plant site and Starlight Cove Elementary.
  6. Due to the Town constructing a warehouse on the water treatment plant site, Palm Beach County's Planning and Zoning Department is requiring the Town-owned site be platted and rezoned to Public Ownership. 
  7. One deficiency identified is the lack of legal positive outfall for the drainage from the Town's property.
  8. Drainage from the water treatment plant has always gone north through the parcel acquired by the School District utilizing an existing underground drainage pipe that runs from the Town's property under and through the School Board's contiguous property in order to discharge surface water.
