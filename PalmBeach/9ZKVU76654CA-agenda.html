EAI-1 Interagency Agreement with Children in DCF Care

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Interagency Agreement with Children in DCF Care effective September 16, 2015 through June 30, 2017; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the agreement which are required for implementation of the Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

This renewal of the interagency agreement has been implemented to better meet the needs of children in foster care.  The purpose of this Agreement are to promote collaboration to 1) ensure educational access and related care, including post-secondary pursuits, promote job training and employability skills and facilitate the delivery of services or programs to children known to the department; 2) avoid duplication of services or programs; and 3) combine resources to maximize availability or delivery of services or programs.

 

The Interagency Agreement includes the following agencies:

  • The School Board of Palm Beach County,
  • Agency for Persons with Disabilities, State of Florida, Area 9,
  • Florida Department of Children and Families, Circuit 15,
  • Florida Department of Juvenile Justice, Circuit 15,
  • ChildNet, Inc.,
  • CareerSource, Inc., and 
  • Early Learning Coalition of Palm Beach County, Inc.

This Agreement coordinates the parties' respective responsibilities for furnishing educational and vocational services and supports for children  known to the Department of Children and Families "who are being served  through ChildNet  while remaining in their own homes, and those who have been placed by ChildNet or by order of the court in a foster family or boarding home, group home, agency boarding home, child care institution, or any combination thereof, and who must receive education and related services before, during, and after the ages of compulsory school attendance." 
 

The Agreement outlines numerous and extensive responsibilities of each agency.  They include participation in a Local Interagency Collaboration Team that will meet at least quarterly.  The Team will review each agency's rules, regulations, policies, etc. as they impact the identified children; make recommendations; define and establish communication protocols; identify responsible staff, etc.  The Agreement addresses sharing of relevant information about the children, in accordance with federal law.

EAI-2 Affiliation Agreement with Gaetz Aerospace Institute, Embry-Riddle (ERAU)
Meeting
Sep 16, 2015 - 2. BOARD MEETING at 5 p.m. in the Board Room.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Agreement with Gaetz Aerospace Institute, Embry-Riddle effective August 27, 2015 through June 30, 2016; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

This Agreement is necessary to introduce selected high school students to collegiate and professional trade-level study with a focus on professional aeronautical topics leading to both high school and college credits for courses completed. In order to expand our students’ aeronautical opportunities we are adding the above referenced agreement. Boynton Aerospace Science Academy (BASA) courses taught by Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (ERAU) faculty will be transferable to appropriate ERAU academic programs, in accordance with the guidelines in force in the ERAU Undergraduate Catalog at the time the individual students matriculate into the ERAU system.

 

All individuals teaching BASA courses shall be qualified to teach under the criteria established by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS) and the State of Florida. ERAU and the District will give first and serious preference to allowing qualified individuals employed already by the District to teach BASA courses. ERAU will offer two curriculum tracks at BASA. The first, or Academic Track, will be a curriculum of introductory courses in a range of aviation career areas, including flight operations, weather, air traffic management, safety, business, unmanned aviation, and others, depending on student demand and the availability of qualified faculty. The second, or Aviation Maintenance Sciences (AMS) Track, will provide students with the General portion of the FAA Airframe and Power plant Mechanics Certificate curriculum.

 

This program has been in existence since 2007.

DESCRIPTION:

The Seal of Biliteracy is an award in recognition of high school graduating students who have attained a high level of competency in listening, speaking, reading, and writing in one or more languages in addition to English.  The purpose of the Seal of Biliteracy is to encourage students to study languages, certify attainment of biliteracy skills, provide employers with a method of identifying an individual who has language and biliteracy skills, recognize and promote World Language instruction in public schools, strengthen intergroup relationships, affirm the value of diversity, and honor the multiple cultures and languages of a community.

 

To qualify for the Seal of Biliteracy, a student must meet the following requirements:

 

1.  Earn a standard high school diploma.

 

2.  Demonstrate proficiency in English by earning a passing score on the statewide, standardized English Language Arts assessment or scores on a standardized test that are concordant with the passing scores on the statement, standardized assessment (e.g. ACT or SAT).

 

3.  Demonstrate proficiency in a World Language, including American Sign Language, by earning;

a.  a score of three (3) or high on an Advanced Placement assessment in a world language; 

b.  a score of four (4) or higher on an International Baccalaureate assessment in a world language;

c.  a score of (E) or higher on an Advanced International Certificate of Education (AICE) assessment in a world language; 

d.  an average score or higher for college-bound senior in the year the assessment was taken according to the College Board on a SAT II subject area assessment in a world language; and

e.  a score demonstrating proficiency on an alternative, district-approved world language assessment.

 

This criteria will be included in the District's Pupil Progression Plan.
