Boca Raton Middle School has been recognized as the "First U.S. School of Caregiving Youth Project." In partnership with the School District, the Caregiving Youth Partners’ Project, the first in the U.S., commenced as a pilot program in September 2006 at Boca Raton Middle School.  In January 2007 the project expanded to Congress Middle School and has added Lantana Middle School as the third school site this academic year. PL1 Lease Agreement for Head Start Facility at Aubrun Trace
Meeting
Nov 20, 2007 - School Board Meeting (Nov 20 2007)
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)


  • On April 26, 2006, the School Board approved entering a cross-easement agreement with Auburn Trace, Ltd., to accommodate drainage for a portion of the Village Academy campus.

  • At the time of the approval of the cross-easement agreement it was understood by all parties, that the City of Delray Beach or its assign (Palm Beach County) would be constructing a Head Start Facility on a portion of Tract B.  The anticipated drainage from this facility was included in the calculations for the South Florida Water Management District permit.

  • The construction of the Head Start facility on a portion of Tract B should not require an amendment to the drainage permit,    however since the County is in the process of purchasing the portion of Tract B on which the Head Start facility is to be located, the County would then be a party to the approved South Florida Water Management District permit and therefore abide by the conditions of that permit.  The drainage agreement therefore addresses drainage issues.

  • Since Auburn Trace and the School Board have an interest in Tract B retention area, are parties to the Cross Easement Agreement and are joint applicants to the South Florida Water Management District permit, the City and the County are requesting that Auburn Trace and the School Board consent to the modification of the South Florida Water Management District to include the County and make other changes if necessary.

  • Since a portion of the Tract B will be dedicated to the County for the Head Start facility and the current plat identifies the uses as recreational and drainage, the plat has to be amended to reflect the subdivision of Tract B for the Head Start facility and the change in use to include the Head Start.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the lease agreement, two consent agreements and a drainage agreement to allow for the construction of the County's Head Start facility on a portion of Tract B of the Auburn Trace plat.

  • The District currently insures 23 locations with 214 buildings valued at approximately $531 million dollars and contents valued at $33.6 million dollars located in the applicable flood zones.

  • Without this flood coverage, in the event of a federally declared flood disaster, the School District would not meet all Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) requirements for federal reimbursement. Based upon current Stafford Act regulations, all locations in Flood Zones A or V are required to purchase coverage from the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP). Without NFIP, FEMA will apply the maximum amount that could have been purchased (up to $500,000 per building and $500,000 contents) as a deductible prior to any FEMA reimbursement.

  • NFIP requires the District to purchase one policy per building, for all 214 buildings housed at these 23 locations. The total insurance coverage is $83.0 million with individual policy deductibles of $50,000 for the building and another $50,000 for the contents.

  • Four years ago FEMA allowed increased deductibles (retentions) from $5000 to $50,000 with a corresponding reduced premium of $167,779. We again recommend the $50,000 deductibles with an annual savings of $180,887.

The School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida ("Sponsor"), entered into a Charter Agreement with Everglades Preparatory Academy Charter School ("EPACS") on July 2, 2002 through June 30, 2007.



The charter school needed extended time to complete the Renewal Application.  The Renewal Application was approved by the School Board three (3) days prior to the Agreement's expiration date on June 30, 2007.



Contract negotiations have concluded and the extension is needed for the charter school to update the revisions suggested by the Legal Department.



The mission of Everglades Preparatory Charter School is through the creation of a meaningful alternative within the public school system which can promote excellence in education by providing opportunities in a school to work and college preparatory environment.



On July 18, 2007, the School Board approved an  Amendment to extend the current "Contract" until August 30, 2007 (see Exhibit "A").



During this time, there was a change in administration and the new administrator was not familiar with the "Contract" process.



Chronology of Events from September - October, 2007:



Staff made several calls and emails to the former Principal and the Governing Board Chairperson reminding them of the unfinished Charter.



The Governing Board Chairperson was out of the country for three - four weeks during this time.



The charter office was not informed of the change of administrators until the middle of August, 2007.



An Assistant Principal was hired and assigned to be in charge of the contract process.



Staff visited the charter school on October 5, 2007 to assist the Assistant Principal with the current "Model Charter" process. 



The charter school needed extra time to complete the "Model Charter" because it was comprehensive and statutorily unlike the school's initial "Charter."



Staff received the completed "Charter" on October 10, 2007 and forwarded it to Legal for review and revision.



Legal made additional modifications and the charter school needs time to make those revisions.



The Governing Board has signed a Second Amendment to extend the current "Contract" until December 28, 2007 (see Exhibit "B").

I recommend that the School Board approve the Second Amendment to extend Everglades Preparatory Academy's  current contract to December 28, 2007.

Florida will need about 17,000 new and well-trained teachers in 2007-2008.  In order to address this need, FAU and its regional partners, which includes the School District of Palm Beach County, have created a three-prong, innovative approach that is practical, research-based and scalable.



Project FIT increases the number of education majors by paying college freshmen, sophomores and others interested in teaching to provide instructional support with model teachers.  This will increase the number and quality of students entering and remaining in the teacher preparation pipeline at all levels.  Undergraduate students will be receiving extensive classroom experiences and intensive, multi-year support with retired educator mentors, as well as a minimal stipend.  The students in the school classrooms will be provided with additional, qualified and caring undergraduates to directly support mutual learning in concert with the classroom teachers.



The Substituting with Mentors and Realistic Teaching (SMaRT) Program provides classroom experience to college juniors and seniors to work as substitute teachers with mentors.  A SMaRT site is defined as an elementary, middle, or high school where public school and university personnel work together to facilitate high levels of learning by all children of the school by using Florida Atlantic University students as substitute teachers to work under the direction of an FAU mentor. The relationship will be dedicated to improving the quality of pre-service and inservice teaching.  The School District of Palm Beach County and Florida Atlantic University will combine resources and provide leadership for the development of the SMaRT sites. 



The purpose of this relationship is to enable the parties to





  • improve the performance of students;

  • extend mutual research and grant capacity;

  • apply and model promising teaching practices;

  • promote targeted pre-professional instruction and continuous professional development to improve student performance;

  • inform and improve school and university instruction, and;

  • test innovative means to develop new education professionals.


The Accelerated Induction into Teaching (AIT) Program fills high needs' teacher vacancies with selected university seniors who are ready to student teach as the actual classroom teacher with daily support from a mentor.  Both the District's Academic Business Plan and Staffing Plan call for intensive and targeted recruitment strategies to insure the availability of highly qualified teachers for high needs schools. The AIT Program is an initiative that provides for the identification and placement of high-quality teacher candidates in high needs' schools.

  • AIT will be supervised by an experienced Master Mentor hired by FAU and reviewed by the School District.  The Master Mentor will provide direct supervision/observation.  (see agreement);
