PD 1 - Personnel Disciplinary Action - Paul Sirota
Meeting
Feb 13, 2013 - Special Meeting 02-13-13 following Workshop - at Norton Museum
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

On April 3, 2012, the Office of Professional Standards received several statements which allege incidents of unprofessional conduct by Mr. Sirota. These incidents include harassment; unprofessional language and unwanted personal contact via email.  Mr. Sirota had previously received a Written Reprimand for unprofessional interaction involving one of the employees who made the new complaint, which is the basis for this disciplinary action.






On or about April 3, 2012, Mr. Sirota became the subject of a Professional Standards investigation based upon the allegations of Bullying and/or Harassment of School District Employees; Ethical Misconduct; and Insubordination-Failure to Follow Policy/Rule or Directives.  At the conclusion of the investigation, the allegations were substantiated.





Therefore, the actions violated the following:  School Board Policy 5.002(3)(e), Policy Prohibiting Bullying and Harassment; Rule 6B-1.006(5)(d), F.A.C., Principles of Professional Conduct for Educators in Florida; School Board Policy 3.02(4)(a),(4)(d),(4)(e),(4)(g) & (4)(j), The Code of Ethics; Rule 6B-1.001(3), F.A.C., The Code of Ethics of the Education Profession in Florida; School Board Policy 3.10(6), Condition of Employment; and School Board Policy 1.013(1), Responsibilities of School District Personnel.  Furthermore, it is noted that Mr. Sirota completed the mandatory training on School Board Policy 3.02, Code of Ethics, and acknowledged such on June 9, 2010.





There is "just cause" to support the suspension of Paul Sirota.





On January 28, 2013, the "Notice of Ten (10) Day Suspension Without Pay" was hand-delivered to Mr. Sirota from the Superintendent, a copy of which is attached hereto.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Paul Sirota's Ten (10) Day suspension without pay effective March 1, 2013, subject only to appeal under sec. 120.569, F.S.

The purpose of this confirming cooperative agreement is to provide a highly restrictive educational setting to a student with a disability who was placed in this facility by Syosset County School District, New York.  The student's mother relocated to Palm Beach County starting on August 1, 2012.  One staff member from the Department of Exceptional Student Education (ESE) and the Deputy General Counsel did go to the facility, observe the student and met with staff.  The student does exhibit intense behavior problems.  The student will age out of school at the end of this school year. His mother is preparing for him to go into a local group home.  The IEP team decision was for the student's IEP to be implemented in the facility since the student had been in this facility for the past four years and he had only one year left.



This confirming cooperative agreement is in compliance with State Board Rule 6A.6-0361, "Special Programs Contractual Agreements with Non-Public Schools".

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve these confirming cooperative agreements with The May Institute, Inc., in the amount of $158,179.05 for the educational costs for a student with a disability from August 20, 2012 through June 6, 2013, and authorize the Superintendent or his designee to sign all documents related to this agreement.

G 2 - Teacher Induction Program Effectiveness Grants (MetLife)
Meeting
Feb 13, 2013 - Special Meeting 02-13-13 following Workshop - at Norton Museum
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

The MetLife Foundation has awarded the School District of Palm Beach County $15,000 to look "deeper" at mentor practice as it relates to growth as mentors, the beginning teachers practice and their ability to use assessment to make strategic instructional decisions for their students, and ultimately how their work impacts  student achievement. Therefore the goals identified by our proposed study are:







  • Goal #1:  To compare student growth in first and second year teachers classrooms that are supported by NTC Mentors to the student growth of first and second year teachers in "like" high-needs schools that are not supported by NTC Mentors. 

  • Goal #2:  To determine the extent to which the NTC Mentors' analyses and calibrations of beginning teacher (BT) formative assessment data, impact the Mentors' growth on the Continuum of Mentor Development as it relates to the standard "Utilizes assessments to promote teacher learning and development?"

  • Goal #3:   To determine the extent to which the NTC Mentors' use of the Analyzing Student Work (ASW) formative assessment tool, impacts BT instructional practice as it relates to Standard 4: Assessment, on the Continuum of Teacher Development and on Design Question 4 as part of the Art of Science and Teaching Causal Teacher Evaluation Model.




 



Completing the proposed study of inquiry will provide valuable information on program impact and effectiveness which can be used to build buy-in for the program. If the three goals are met with success, the study will provide evidence that the program can be successfully replicated in high needs schools within Palm Beach County; has a direct impact on teacher retention, performance, and student achievement, and justifies that human and capital resources used to implement and maintain the program. The funds from this grant will be provided to the district's Department of Assessment. Employees will complete the research required and create the quantitative reports to measure the impact of the New Teacher Mentoring support program. There is no district match required. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board accept the grant for the Teacher Induction Program Effectiveness (MetLife) award from the New Teacher Center in the amount of up to $15,000.00, effective March 1, 2013 through November 30, 2013 and approve any no cost extension, as applicable; and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for implementation of grant activities.

G 1 - Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) Grant
Meeting
Feb 13, 2013 - Special Meeting 02-13-13 following Workshop - at Norton Museum
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

The School District of Palm Beach County has filed a Notice of Intent to Apply for the Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) grant competition from the U.S. Department of Education, with a submission deadline of March 1, 2013. The competitive MSAP program makes grant awards of up to $12,000,000 over a three-year period to support innovative, research-based thematic magnet programs that are implemented school-wide. 



To be eligible for the MSAP grant, the School Board must approve the attached MSAP Voluntary Plan to implement magnet programs in schools with a substantial number of minority students enrolled.  The MSAP Voluntary Plan meets the grant requirements for magnet schools that are designed to meet the following purposes:







  • To eliminate, reduce, or prevent minority group isolation;




  • To promote diversity through the implementation of a specialized magnet curriculum capable of attracting and bringing together students from different social, economic, ethnic and racial backgrounds, and




  • To provide all students in the magnet school with the opportunity to meet challenging academic content standards and student achievement standards.


Pending receipt of the MSAP grant, the following five new school-wide magnet programs are proposed for development and implementation beginning in school year 2013-2014. 



MSAP Magnet Schools - Magnet Themes







  • Lake Park Elementary - International Baccalaureate Primary  Years Programme (IB PYP)




  • Howell l. Watkins Middle - International Baccalaureate Middle Years Programme (IB MYP)




  • Galaxy Elementary - Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM)




  • Congress Middle - Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM)




  • Boynton Beach High - Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM)


Students who live in the attendance boundaries of these schools will participate in  and benefit from the school-wide magnet program, along with students who are recruited to attend  the magnet schools from outside each school's attendance area.  The proposed new magnet schools all have adequate space available to attract additional students to participate in the school-wide magnet program.



The MSAP project aligns with the District's Strategic Plan and specifically supports:  Goal 1: Student First, Goal 2:  Family Matters;  Goal 3: Qualified and Highly Effective Workforce. 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the MSAP Voluntary Plan and the submission of the Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) grant application to the U. S. Department of Education in an amount up to $12,000,000, effective October 1, 2013 through September 30, 2016, and approve any no cost extension as applicable; and I authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to and required for the submission of the grant application and implementation of grant activities.

The financial impact to the Special Revenue-Other Funds Budget is an increase of up to $12,000,000, if funded.  All grant related expenditures and contracts are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue.  There is no financial impact to the Operating Budget.

The consultant, who has many years of experience in teaching and character development, shall provide the following services, fully funded out of a grant-funded, fee generated revenue account in Student Intervention Services:







  • Research ethics information and scenarios to be used in the District Ethics Bowl

  • Plan and execute the District's High School Ethics Bowl

  • Provide training and mentoring to team coaches in preparation for the District Bowl

  • Provide on-going communication to all interested parties, coaches, students, schools and district staff for the Ethics Bowl.

  • Provide implementation and follow-up documentation, including a District Ethics Bowl Survey, to team coaches, judges, moderators, Palm Beach State College personnel and school district personnel

  • Assist in planning and execution of travel to the National High School Ethics Bowl at the University of North Carolina

  • Provide District Ethics Bowl information to the National Ethics Bowl Committee for posting on their website.

  • Serve as a School District Liaison to the National High School Ethics Bowl Committee and the Parr Center for Ethics at the University of North Carolina

  • Research and plan for a Middle School Ethics Bowl in the next school year




This contract will assist in District compliance with the unfunded law that requires that all public schools offer character education in all grades K-12 (Section ยง233.061, Florida Statutes). The services support the District's Strategic Plan and mission to foster the knowledge, skills, and ethics required for responsible citizenship and productive careers.  Furthermore, Ethics Bowl activities and participation align with the development of a Single District Culture as well as Strategic Plan Essentials Positive Learning Environments and Parent and Community Involvement.



The highly successful First Annual Ethics Bowl included participation by School Board members; district and school leadership; other community, legal, and business leaders; local college and university students and personnel; and parents and family members.  Eight schools participated with 13 teams.



The cost of this consultant is considerably less than hiring an employee to execute this initiative.  Because she is also coordinating efforts with Palm Beach State College to assist with the College Ethics Bowl, partial payment by the State College will be sought next year.  It is not available this year due to funding limitations.

Recommendation:

I recommend award of the Professional Services Agreement with MSEDITS, Inc. for $9,000 effective February 21, 2013 through May 24, 2013 and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to sign all necessary documents.
