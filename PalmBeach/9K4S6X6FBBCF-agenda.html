Hartford Steam Boiler provides property damage protection in the event of explosions or unexpected mechanical malfunctions of our boilers or machinery. Additionally, they provide inspections as required by Florida law and certificates for approximately 395 objects at over 203 locations.



 



The premium decreased by $1,950.70 from the expiring policy even with a slight increase in Total Insured Values and the number of boilers/locations.



 



The District is able to renew the existing $50 million policy limit, which excludes windstorm and lightning coverage (excluded by all Boiler and Machinery carriers).



 



The deductible is $10,000 for all perils.



 



 



 



A few years ago, the District recovered $63,899 from Hartford Steam Boiler for two Boiler & Machinery claims. In addition, the District is required, at a minimum, to purchase Boiler & Machinery insurance for schools funded through COPS program at a cost of approximately $27,000.



 



The District’s broker, Beacon Group Inc., recommends this Boiler and Machinery Insurance program and receives no commission on this policy.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board renew its Boiler and Machinery Insurance policy with Hartford Steam Boiler, through our broker, Beacon group, Inc., effective December 31, 2009 to December 31, 2010, for a premium of $47,952.15.

IN3 - APPROVAL TO PURCHASE PROPERTY INSURANCE

December, we purchased $95 million of coverage against total insured values (TIV) of $5.24 billion. This year we are proposing to add $5 million in catastrophic limits to a TIV of $5.31 billion.  Although property replacement cost decreased by $5 a square foot, the total inventory of property overall increased by approximately $75 million, including the addition of a new elementary school.



    



 Market capacity continues to be an issue for our estimated loss limit and premium cost.  Our probable maximum loss (PML) is estimated at $300 million for a 100-year storm event.



 



The recommended option is as follows:



 



Loss Limit:             $100,000,000



 



Deductible:         All perils other than named wind  - $10 million



 



                         Named Windstorm - 5% of values with $500,000



                         minimum at each location, plus a $10 million corridor.



                         This $10 million corridor is applied only one time per



                         policy year.



 



Estimated Premium:   $7,943.757



 



The District has previously requested and received confirmation from the State Office of Insurance Regulation and State Office of Emergency Management through the Reasonableness Letter that our property insurance program complies with the Stafford Act requirements from FEMA. The District will again request this confirmation from the state upon Board approval of this agenda item.



 



Our broker has contacted forty-eight insurers/reinsurers and assembled a consortium of carriers in order to cover our property insurance renewal.  The actual layer program and pricing is being finalized.



 



Beacon Group Inc. will be compensated at a 4% commission rate to a maximum cap of $280,000 for their services, and the services of any wholesaler/intermediary used to place the District’s property insurance program.



 



Market capacity continues to be an issue for our estimated loss limit and premium cost.  Our probable maximum loss (PML) is estimated at $300 million for a 100-year storm event. 



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the purchase of property insurance through our broker Beacon Group Inc., with limits of $100 million effective December 31, 2009 to December 31, 2010 at a premium cost not to exceed $7.95 million.

LA1 - 2010-2011 School Calendar
Meeting
Dec 9, 2009 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

Highlights of the Superintendent’s recommendations are as follows:







  • The start day for students in the 2010-2011 school year will be Tuesday, August 17, 2010 (same start day as current calendar, Tuesday, August 18, 2009)




  • The work year for returning teachers starts Wednesday, August 11, 2010, which is 4 working days before students return.  This allows the District to pay teachers a full pay check on Friday, August 20th and avoids the “pay gap” issue.  It also allows new employees who start on or before August 11, 2010, to be eligible for District insurance benefits effective September 1, 2010 instead of having to wait a month to become eligible on October 1.






  • The first semester will end Friday, December 17, 2010, just prior to Winter Break.







  • The General Election date, Tuesday, November 2, is scheduled as a non-student day.  For secondary teachers this date will be designated as all day In-Service, and as a Teacher Work Day for elementary teachers.







  • Spring recess is moved to March 14-18, 2011.  With the FCATs being scheduled April 11-22, it was determined that the week after FCATs (April 25-29) was too late in the school year for a Spring recess.







  • As a pilot, 12-month employees will be given the choice of taking September 9, 2010 (Hashanah), or April 22, 2011 (Good Friday) as a holiday, but not both days.







  • The school year end date for students is Thursday, June 2, 2011, and for teachers Friday, June 3, 2011.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida waive the provisions of s.1001.42(4)(f) F.S. pursuant to authority granted to Academically High-Performing School Districts (s.1003.621, F.S.) and adopt the recommended 2010-2011 School Calendar.
