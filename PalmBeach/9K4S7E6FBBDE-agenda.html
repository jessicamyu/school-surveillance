Subject
Lake Worth High School Intervene Plan
Meeting
Jan 27, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Convene
Type
Action

The State requires that schools in the Intervene status in 2009-10 write a plan for the 2010-20111 school year in case they do not exit Intervene status.  the State provides a template for schools to complete and the language for column one entitled "Requirements".  The district completes the other columns.



This plan was written with the assistance of a committee comprised of teachers, administrators, and parents of Lake Worth High School as well as community members and district staff.



The plan is only put into place if the school does not exit Intervene status.



The plan must be submitted to the State by February 1, 2010.

Contact:

Judith Klinek (klinek@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



 

Financial Impact:

The financial impact to the operating budget, Title I and Title II budgets is $1,494,558.



*The FDOE Intervene Plan for Lake Worth High School has some undetermined costs as data are not available.  These costs are primarily to the Operating Budget and include:







  • the potential of restaffing teachers earning less than 65% learning gains will not be available until FCAT scores arrive;




  • the recruitment of the principal and potential signing bonus varies based on experience and education; and




  • the cost of the teacher-selected professional development during the year at $23.50 per hour.

The Department of Charter Schools received an application, on August 3, 2009, from G.A.R.Y. Institute of North Area Palm Beach County ("GARY North") to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2010.



The application was evaluated using the "Florida Charter School Application Instrument" (see Exhibit "A"). 



The instrument and the application process were explained to the applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop that was held on May 28, 2009 (see Exhibit "B").



The District Reviewers were trained on the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument and Application Process on May 15, 2009 (see Exhibit "C").



The Reviewers evaluated the 21 sections of the written application.  Two of the 21 sections were rated "Partially Meets the Standard," meaning the responses addressed most of the criteria, but lacked meaningful detail and required important additional information (see Exhibit "D").



Partially Meets the Standard:
Section 9 - Budget
Section 20 - Finance



The responses to each section that did not meet the stated criteria served as the basis of inquiry for the interview.  Please refer to Step 5:  Applicant Interview (see Exhibit "E").



Step 6, second paragraph in the Overview of the Florida Charter School Application Process states, "The sponsoring board will only approve applicants that demonstrate quality in all components of the application process. An application cannot have significant weaknesses in some components of the application and still be judged to have met the standard for approval," (see Exhibit "F").  Each applicant received communication that outlined the proposed recommendation, the evaluator's second review following the interview, summary of the interview, and was granted the option to voluntarily withdraw the application or allow the application to be placed on the Board agenda for action (see Exhibit "G").



Pursuant to F.S.1002.33 (5)(c):
"An applicant may appeal any denial of that applicant's application or failure to act on an application to the State Board of Education no later than 30 calendar days, after receipt of the sponsor's decision or failure to act and shall notify the sponsor of its appeal . . ."



A copy of GARY North's application is in the Board Office for your review.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board deny the application of G.A.R.Y. Institute of North Palm Beach County  and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related contracts and documents.
