AC2: College Board Readiness System Products Agreement (SAT Agreement with the College Board)
Meeting
Jul 25, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

The SAT Agreement with the College Board provides the School District of Palm Beach County with the ability to:







  • Administer the SAT to all 11th grade students during the school day as a means to provide these students with an opportunity to meet state college readiness requirements. The school day testing removes the most common barriers (location, transportation, inability to attend on a Saturday due to work or need to care for siblings) for students of poverty who might not otherwise participate in college readiness testing.




  • Provide eligibility for one makeup test to participants who are absent from the designated test administration site.


The products provided by this Agreement include the following:







  • The SAT test for approximately 12,853 students at a discounted rate of $33.52 per exam (normal unit price is $53 per exam)




  • Free access to the Official SAT Online Course to all students participating in the school day testing (normal price is $69.95 per student)




  • Access to Online Course training for administrative personnel


  • A Data License Agreement allowing the same SAT student data sharing as previously provided in the FY2010 SAT Agreement (See Attachment B to Schedule 1: Data License Agreement)


District Key Result #6 states that all schools will increase participation and performance of students of each racial/ethnic group taking the SAT, with a particular emphasis on underrepresented populations.  Historically, nearly 50% of our students did not take the SAT. 



In an effort to improve the participation rate, a similar SAT school day testing agreement was approved in 2010, making Palm Beach County the first district in the nation to offer the SAT during the school day, and starting a nationwide trend in school day SAT testing.



The participation results for Black and Latino students from our District-wide SAT participation in 2010 also featured greatly in our selection as a finalist for the 2012 Broad Prize in Urban Education because of the progress made toward closing the achievement gap through access.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the College Board Readiness System Products Agreement with the College Board and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the Agreement.

Janis Andrews  janis.andrews@palmbeachschools.org
