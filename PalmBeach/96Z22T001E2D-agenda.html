GA1 Wells Fargo Bank Graduation Initiative Unaccompanied Homeless Youth Fund
Meeting
May 1, 2013 - 3) Special Meeting (following Workshop)
Category
11. Consent Agenda - Academics
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the receipt of the Wells Fargo Unaccompanied Homeless Youth Fund for up to $15,000 from April 12, 2013 through November 1, 2013, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to the grant which are required for implementation of the grant activities. The late start date does not prejudice the Board: award start date was effective upon receipt of the award letter dated April 12, 2013.

DESCRIPTION:

 

The School District has been awarded funding from the Wells Fargo Unaccompanied Homeless Youth Fund.  Unaccompanied homeless youth are youth who meet the definition of homeless under the McKinney-Vento Homeless Assistance Act and are also not in the physical custody of a parent or guardian. The grant will serve unaccompanied homeless secondary students who will be given survival kits with foods, first aid kits, sweatshirts, school supplies, and uniforms. They will be given opportunities to go to College is Possible Day at Palm Beach State College, the Palm Beach County Substance Awareness Coalition Leadership and Success Summit, and a college tour.  Middle school students will have an opportunity to be mentored by Big Brothers Big Sisters of Palm Beach and Martin Counties. Schools with the highest numbers of these students will receive a washer and dryer so the students can wash their clothes at school, as well as lockers to store duffel bags.  The impact of this project will be measured in the numbers of unaccompanied homeless youth who graduate in 2014 compared to 2013 and the percentage who report acceptance to a college, university, trade school, or an arm of the U.S. Military.

 

A copy of this grant is located in the Board Minutes Office for review.

 

CONTACT:

 

Cheryl A. Alligood, cheryl.alligood@palmbeachschools.org

Joseph M. Lee, Ed.D.

 

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

 

The financial impact to the Special Revenue-Other Funds Budget is an increase of up to $15,000.  All project related expenditures are authorized for payment up to the amount of revenue.  There is no financial impact to the Operating Budget.

Subject
LD2 - City of West Palm Beach Stormwater
Meeting
May 1, 2013 - 3) Special Meeting (following Workshop)
Category
13. NEW BUSINESS
Type
Action
Recommended Action
I recommend the Board authorize the General Counsel to file suit in the 15th Judicial Circuit in and for Palm Beach County, Florida, seeking an injunction and/or a declaratory judgment and/or other appropriate judicial relief against the City of West Palm Beach regarding the City's announced intent to disconnect School Board's facilities from the City's stormwater system. Also, to take all appropriate actions, including hiring experts, and authority to settle the matter subject to the approval of the School Board, and authorize the Chairman, Superintendent, and General Counsel to sign all necessary documents.
In the alternative:
I recommend that the Board direct staff to negotiate an Interlocal Agreement with the City of West Palm Beach regarding the payment of stormwater fees, subject to the approval of the School Board, and authorize the Chairman, Superintendent, and General Counsel to sign all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

On January 18, 2012, the 3rd District Court of Appeal issued an opinion in the case of City of Key West v. Florida Keys Community College with regard to a dispute over the College’s obligation to pay stormwater fees charged by Key West.  The opinion held that state entities enjoy sovereign immunity from liability for municipal stormwater fees in the absence of an agreement to pay such fees.

 

In addition to the Key West case, there has been similar on-going litigation between the City of Ocala and the Marion County School Board (“MCSB”) on the issue of MCSB’s entitlement to continue using Ocala’s stormwater system and the City’s obligation to provide stormwater services, regardless of MCSB’s non-payment of the stormwater fees.

 

 

Following a discussion with District Facilities staff, it was determined that the District was paying the City of West Palm Beach (“WPB”) approximately $284,000 per year in stormwater fees for District facilities located within WPB and approximately $550,000 annually in stormwater fees to all municipalities with stormwater utilities.

 

Based on a review of the Key West case, discussions with other schools districts and senior staff, it was determined that the District should cease paying municipal stormwater charges as of the end of April, 2012.  A letter was sent to the municipalities charging stormwater fees, including WPB in May, 2012.

 

WPB notified the District by letter dated November 19, 2012 that it intended to disconnect the schools within the WPB municipal boundary from WPB’s stormwater system at the close of the school year in June, 2013 unless the Board entered into an agreement with WPB to pay for stormwater services.

 

 

The General Counsel and senior staff met with representatives of WPB and the WPB City Attorney on January 31, 2013 to discuss potential alternatives to WPB’s plan to disconnect the District’s facilities from the WPB system.  The only alternative that WPB was willing to consider was the Board entering into an agreement with WPB to pay for the stormwater services, dating back to April 27, 2012 when the District ceased paying the fees.

 

WPB sent a draft agreement for District review on April 1, 2013.  Notwithstanding the on-going litigation with the MCSB that is factually similar to WPB’s dispute with the Board, WPB provided only an extremely narrow caveat where the District would be entitled to a refund of fees paid pursuant to the agreement.

 

Given that the end of the school year and the rainy season are both fast approaching, staff believes that it is in the best interest of the District to seek an injunction and/or a declaratory judgment against WPB to prevent it from disconnecting the District facilities from the stormwater system prior to an adjudication of the District’s rights.

 

Alternatively, the Board may elect to direct staff to further negotiate the terms of the agreement to pay WPB, subject to the Board’s and WPB’s approval of the final version of the agreement.

 

CONTACT:

Sheryl G. Wood, Esq.; General Counsel

Blair LittleJohn, Esq.; Senior Associate Counsel

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The stormwater fees charged by the City of West Palm Beach are approximately $284,000 per year and in excess of $550,000 per year for all of the municipalities that were charging stormwater fees in April, 2012.  Since April, 2012, another municipality has created a stormwater utility that District staff estimates could be between $200,000 and  $300,000 annually.  Stormwater charges, if paid, are funded out of the operating budget.
