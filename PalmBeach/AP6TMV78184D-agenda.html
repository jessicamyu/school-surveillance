Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the AVID and AVID Excel Implementation Agreements with the AVID Center effective July 1, 2017 through June 30, 2018; and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents related to the Agreements which are required for implementation.

DESCRIPTION:

Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID) is a systemic instructional approach for students in Kindergarten through higher education (K-16).  The AVID College Readiness System is a school-wide transformational effort focused on leadership, systems, instruction, and culture.  AVID is designed to increase the number of students who enroll and succeed in higher education and in their lives beyond high school.

The AVID College Readiness System is a catalyst for developing a school culture that closes the expectation and opportunity gaps many students face, and prepares all students for success in a global society.

AVID Excel is a middle school program intended to break the detrimental cycle that turns many English Language Learners (ELLs) into long-term ELLs.  AVID Excel accelerates students’ academic language acquisition and puts them on a pathway to high school AVID and college readiness.
 

The School District of Palm Beach County’s AVID participation has increased from six high schools in 2009 to 51 sites in 2017 at the elementary, middle, and high school levels.
 

Approximately 879 AVID students have successfully graduated since the first graduating class in 2012.  Of the 225 AVID seniors in 2016-17:

  • 97.8% graduated on time

  • 68% plan to attend a four-year college

  • 14% plan to attend a two-year college

  • 84% graduated “college-ready”

  • 73% took both the SAT and ACT

  • 64% took at least one AP/IB/AICE exam

Approximately 919 middle school students were enrolled in the AVID elective in 2016-17.  Of those students:

  • 63% completed Algebra 1 or higher math with a “C” or better

  • 441 have taken an honors or high school credit-bearing course other than math

Over 1,000 teachers and 100 administrators and District staff have been trained in AVID’s strategies and methodologies at Summer Institutes, Path trainings, AVID Professional Learning Workshops, and AVID District Leader Trainings.  A sustainable support system has been established, as the District now has five AVID Center Staff Developers.
 

These Agreements will allow the School District of Palm Beach County to implement AVID’s curriculum and use of the AVID logo in all 51 sites. The Agreements include District Director training, AVID curriculum materials, and implementation support.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve the Data Sharing Agreement with UPD Consulting, including all attachments and addenda, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

DESCRIPTION:

UPD Consulting is working with The School District of Palm Beach County (SDPBC), along with Miami-Dade, Volusia, and Lake County School Districts, over the next two years as a part of a Gates Foundation project aimed at increasing standards-aligned instruction and fostering network learning, all to the end of improving student learning and outcomes. With these goals in mind, UPD is working to help Palm Beach advance their strategic plan, focusing specifically on building capacity in elementary literacy (Initiative 6).
 

During the first year of the pilot (2016-2017 school year,  2nd grade teams at 5 schools in Palm Beach during the second half of the 2016-17 school year participated in professional development focused on standards-based teaching and test out incorporating standards-aligned model literacy content using Core Knowledge Language Arts. Based on the initial success of the pilot, next year it will continue with 15 schools in Palm Beach (kindergarten, 1st, and 2nd grade teacher teams in the 10 new schools and kindergarten, 1st, 2nd, and 3rd grade teacher teams from the 5 original pilot schools) to:

• Incorporate complex texts and standards-aligned instructional materials from Core Knowledge Language Arts into their existing balanced literacy or workshop structure;

• Develop deeper knowledge of Florida State Standards shifts and the practice of applying standards in their classrooms, one domain at a time;

• Build a collaborative working group to tackle this work together and support each other’s practice; and

• Inform district-provided supports for teachers in their implementation of standards-aligned instructional units.
 

In order to continue the pilot UPD Consulting requests to obtain data from the District to perform its services under the UPD Consulting Agreement.  The scope of this data includes data necessary to support the CKLA Pilot analysis, including but not limited to student demographics and assessment scores for students in kindergarten through grade cohorts SY2017-18 through SY2020-2021. UPD Consulting also requests data from schools with similar demographics not included in the pilot.
 

UPD Consulting will work with the District to facilitate the transfer of the Data assembled by the District to UPD Consulting.  They shall at all times hold and use the Data in accordance with the requirements of SDPBC.

Upon completion of the pilot, UPD Consulting will transfer all of the Data to SDPBC and remove the Data from its computer systems in accordance with the requirements described of SDPBC.
