Technical Disaster Recovery Assistance may be required to temporarily augment School District of Palm Beach County (District) Staff on an as-needed basis in the event of a disaster or other emergency.  When services are required, awarded firms will be assigned Work in a sequence based on the Final Ranking Order established by the Evaluation Committee with the #1 Ranking Firm being offered the first assignment, etc.  The term of the contract will be from July 8, 2010 through July 7, 2013, with the option to renew for two (2) additional one (1) year periods.   



 



Responsibilities shall include, but not be limited to:



a)      Assessment of Damage.



b)      Inspecting facilities and construction work and Managing



      Inspectors.



c)      Accounting and Estimating.



d)     Design services, including computations, code compliance,



      graphics and specifications.



e)      Drafting services.



f)       Preparing scopes of work to repair damage.



g)      Filling in Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA)



      forms.



h)      Clerical duties.



i)        Preparing reports, schedules and other deliverables.

FM1 - Class Size Penalties Update
Meeting
Jul 14, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

At its June meeting, the Board of Directors of the Florida School Boards Association voted to pursue legal relief from sections of House Bill 5101, Chapter 2010 -154, Laws of Florida, that impose what is believed to be an unconstitutional financial penalty and redistribution of state and local class size reduction funds as a reward or "bonus" for school districts that meet class size requirements. The Greater Florida Consortium of School Boards, of which Palm Beach is a member, has also endorsed this effort (letter attached).



 



The Florida Legislature appropriated $82 million for CSR for FY11 although the State Board of Education recommended $354 million in February, 2010.

Recommendation:

I recommend the Board adopt the Resolution to enter as a party in a proposed lawsuit to declare unconstitutional specific provisions of Chapter 2010-154, Laws of Florida; approve the Retainer Agreement and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all necessary documents.

Financial Impact:

Financial Impact $1,500.00 to the General Fund Budget.



The source of funds is the Legal Department Budget.
