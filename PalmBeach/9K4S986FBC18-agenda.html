AC1 - Literacy AmeriCorps Palm Beach County
Meeting
Dec 8, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

The District has partnered with the Literacy AmeriCorps of Palm Beach County to write a grant for AmeriCorps members.



.  The grant provides the School District with six (6) AmeriCorps members who will act as graduation coaches at the following high schools:  Boynton Beach High, Forest Hill High, John I. Leonard High, Olympic Heights High, Palm Beach Central High and Royal Palm Beach High.



.  The funding match for each AmeriCorps member is paid through a grant with the Education Foundation for a total of $26,250.00 for this year and with Palm Beach State College for $5,250.00 for this year.



.  The AmeriCorps members are college graduates.  They are trained by our district to assist in breaking down barriers for at-risk students at the schools to which they are assigned.



.  Each graduation coach is assigned a student cohort of a minimum of eighty students.  The essential job functions include the following:  
     - Collaboration with school counselors, consultation with teachers and other designated school staff to facilitate student success.
     - Development of an Individual Assistance Plan for each student at-risk of not graduating.  Meeting with students on a regular basis to monitor and update the plan as needed.
     - Monitoring of attendance, grades/course failures and other interventions of at-risk students.
     - Ongoing communication with students and parents.  Participating in parent and teacher conferences as needed.



. The data panel highlights measurable performances for the 2009-10 school year.  (See Tables 1 & 2)

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the cooperative agreement between the Literacy AmeriCorps of Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County, effective August 23, 2010 through July 31, 2011 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all related documents.

AC2 - Partnership among The SDPBC, South Florida Water Management District, & The Palm Beach Post

Dec 8, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Academics

Action (Consent)

The School District of Palm Beach County, Division of Curriculum, has partnered with the South Florida Water Management District (SFWMD) on education endeavors since 2001.



Through an annual joint collaboration, the School District of Palm Beach County, and the SFWMD designed and implemented "Everglades: An American Treasure" a newspaper tab circulated by the Palm Beach Post to grade 7 and grade 9 students.



The Division of Curriculum provides annual edits and revisions to the "Everglades" tab curriculum, teacher guides, and the online resources.



This partnership requires a pass-through of funds from the SFWMD to the School District of Palm Beach County to the Palm Beach Post.



The financial impact to the District is an increase of $2,000.

Recommendation:

I recommend that The School Board approve receipt of $49,000 from the South Florida Water Management District, to provide subsequent payment to The Palm Beach Post.
