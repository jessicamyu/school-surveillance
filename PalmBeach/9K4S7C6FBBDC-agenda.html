Becky Youngman Appointed Chevalier By French Government
Meeting
Feb 17, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category

Becky Youngman has been appointed by the French Government as a Chevalier (knight) in L'Ordre des Palmes Académiques (The Order of Academic Palms). L'Ordre des Palmes Académiques is a French Order of Chivalry for educators. Ms. Youngman has been an educator for 25 years in Palm Beach County. She taught French in the International Baccalaureate program at Atlantic Community High School and currently serves as the manager of the Advanced Placement Incentive Program.  The order was originally created in 1808 by Napoleon to honor eminent members of the University of Paris. In 1866, the scope of the award was widened to include major contributions to French national education made by anyone, including foreigners. It was also made available to French nationals outside of France who had made major contributions to the expansion of French culture in the rest of the world. Last year was the bicentennial of the order, making it one of the world's oldest civil awards. Recipients must be nominated by a French government authority and approved by the French Minister of Education.  



Ms. Youngman has served on the board of the Florida Foreign Language Association for eight years and was President in 2006.  She has served on the board of the Congrès de la culture française en Floride state French student competition. She earned National Board Certification® in 2003.  Ms. Youngman is currently one of six Senior Moderators worldwide for the IB Middle Years Programme Language B and has co-authored several curriculum documents for the International Baccalaureate Organization, including the current Language B Guide and Backward Mapping the IB Curriculum: Language B. She is also an IBMYP workshop leader and Diploma Assistant Examiner. Ms. Youngman has attended the JNCL-NCLS Legislative Day for the past three years as an advocate for world language learning and assisted in the development of the Memorandum of Understanding between France and the state of Florida in 2007.

I recommend that the School Board approve the modification to the Plan Administration fee schedule which is an attachment to the 403(b) Plan document.



 







  • This modification specifies that existing fees apply to both active and inactive participants. This is a change to  the 403(b) Plan Document which was originally adopted by the School Board on December 17, 2008 with  an effective date of January 1, 2009, as required by Section 403(b) of the Internal Revenue Code. 


 



·         The Internal Revenue Code Section 403(b) has added substantial record-keeping responsibilities to the plan sponsors.



 



·         We contract with TSA Consulting Group (TSACG) to handle the approval process for transactions; such as, distributions, enrollment, exchanges, transfers, 403(b) loans and rollovers.  This modification broadens the current fee of $1.00 per month per active participant, to now apply all participants with an existing account (Attachment B, Plan Administration Fee Schedule).



 



·         The fees for these services are paid directly to TSACG by the Investment Providers.  If we didn’t utilize TSACG, the fees would be paid to whichever company with whom we contracted. 



 



·         TSACG has now received enough data from the Investment Providers to determine that 35% of all 403(b) accounts are for inactive participants (non-contributing employees and participants who have separated from service). This group has an average of 28% more transactions than participants who are currently employed.



 



·         Investment Plan Providers have incorporated these fees into their investment products in one of two ways:  1) The fee is folded into the internal expense of the products or 2) A direct pass through.  Many inactive participants are, therefore, already funding this fee.



 



All Investment Providers have agreed to fees by completing the Investment Provider Service Agreements.  Additionally, the Independent Benefits Council (IBC) established these fees for vendors when creating the Model Plan.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the modification to the Plan Administration fee schedule which is an attachment to the 403(b) Plan document.

FACE ITSM is an award winning educational, substance abuse prevention and early intervention program for youth ages 12 to 18 and their parents or caregivers. Its mission is to empower families to support their at-risk youth in making healthy choices regarding alcohol, tobacco, and other drugs (ATOD), thereby improving their academic achievement. Students who are suspended for the possession or use of ATOD on school campuses or at school-sponsored events have the option of reducing out-of-school days by completing FACE ITSM.  Students may also self refer, be referred by their families, or be referred from Youth Court.

PS 2 Changes made to the Agreement are as follows:
This is a request to amend the current agreement between the School District of Palm Beach County and Lauren A. Steele from $1,218.00 to $2,898.00, an increase of $1,680.00. 
The reason for this request is to increase the number of hours from 58 hours to 138 hours, an increase of 80 hours.  The consultant will provide additional hours to the FACE IT (Families Acting Collaboratively to Educate and Involve Teens) Program, funded by a Federal Safe & Drug Free Schools Entitlement Grant.
Her responsibilities include Instruction for Alternative to Suspension program, FACE IT, which allows students to reduce out-of-school suspension days.



Justification of Selection of Lauren Steele for a Part-Time FACE IT Position




Lauren Steele is one of two Alcohol Grant Program Coordinators in Project SMART, funded by the U.S. Department of Education Grant to Reduce Alcohol Abuse awarded to the School Board in the fall of 2007.  She has worked for the Prevention Center in the Department of Safe Schools in that position since FY09.  She was hired based upon her valuable experience with at risk young people and families.  She has facilitated FACE ITSM for a year.  She had been originally hired upon the recommendation of the current team of FACE ITSM staff.  Lauren was chosen for her specific facilitation experiences, educational background and exceptional skills dealing positively with youth and adults.



The leadership, staffing, content, delivery method and scheduling of the FACE
ITSM program has led to its national and statewide recognition.  It was awarded the National 2009 National Exemplary Award for Innovative Substance Abuse Prevention Programs, Practices, and Policies.  In 2004, FACE ITSM was featured on the U.S.  Department of Education's television show entitled Parents: News You Can Use.  In 2007, the program received a Best Practices Award for Most Innovative Program from the Florida Department of Children and Families and the Florida Alcohol and Drub Abuse Association.  The program has served over 6000 individuals including students and their families.  Recidivism rates consistently range lower than 5%.



At the present time, there are 18 district staff members and 10 consultants working part-time with FACE ITSM. The job requires four hours per week plus additional hours for training.  Each of the consultants was personally recommended as a facilitator by our existing staff.



FACE ITSM has two administrative staff positions to serve 400-600 families annually and 100 families weekly.  Because of the job requirements and existing resources, applicants are recommended by word of mouth through our current FACE ITSM staff.  Since the program started in 2004, the Prevention Center staff has found that these word of mouth recommendations from current successful staff have been the most efficient and productive recruiting method.  The job requires: a Bachelor's Degree in Psychology, Sociology, Social Work, Education, Mental Health, Substance Abuse, or other related field; teaching or group facilitation experience; and background knowledge and/or professional experience in substance abuse prevention and/or intervention.



From this point forward, part-time positions for FACE ITSM will be advertised on the District web site.
