July 1, 2005, Gulfstream Goodwill Transitions to Life Academy was granted a five (5) year contract to serve students with specific exceptionalities of Trainable Mentally Handicapped (TMH) or Educable Mentally Handicapped (EMH) who are of the ages 14-22 in grades 9-12.  July 1, 2008, Gulfstream Goodwill Transitions to Life Academy was granted a five (5) year renewal contract.  That Charter Contract expires June 30, 2013.



The mission is to, "Provide multi-sensory, individualized, innovative programs designed to successfully transition developmentally disabled at-risk youth and young adults into post-school living. LIFE will implement programs designed to encourage self-reliance, determination, and economic independence and will therefore help its students to become increasingly well-adjusted and productive members of their community."



Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)1, "A charter may be renewed provided a program review demonstrates that the criteria in paragraph (a) have been successfully accomplished and that none of the grounds for non-renewal established by paragraph (8)(a) has been documented."  In February, 2013, the district staff conducted a Program Review.  Based on the results of the criteria outlined in the Charter Renewal Program Review, Gulfstream Goodwill Transition to Life Academy meets the criteria for renewal and does not fall under any grounds for termination.



F.S. 1002.33(8)(a)  The sponsor may choose not to renew or may terminate the charter for any of the following grounds:



1.  Failure to participate in the state's education accountability system created in s. 1008.31, as required in this section, or failure to meet the requirements for student performance stated in the charter.



2.  Failure to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management.



3.  Violation of law.



4.  Other good cause shown.



The School Board's approval of the Charter Renewal Program indicates an agreement to enter into charter contract negotiations, and if successfully negotiated by Legal and approved by the School Board, will result in entering into a Charter Contract. The Charter will be a binding agreement, which shall detail the rights and responsibilities of the School Board and the Charter School.  If the  Charter Renewal Program is not approved by the School Board, the School Board will have to send a 90-day notice letter indicating the reasons for non-renewal and the right to appeal.  The charter school then has a right to file an appeal.  If the school does not appeal or the school appeals and the School Board prevails, then the school is closed after the 90 days.



By letter dated February 27, 2013, Gulfstream Goodwill Transitions to Life Academy was notified of this Board Meeting.  (Exhibit A)



There is no Data Panel because Gulfstream Goodwill Transitions to Life Academy serves ESE students.



A copy of the Charter Renewal Program Review for Gulfstream Goodwill Transitions to Life Academy is attached.  (Exhibit B)

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Charter Renewal Program Review for Gulfstream Goodwill Transitions to Life Academy.

CS 6 Purchase of K-5 Literacy and Mathematics Textbooks for FY 2014 Implementation
Meeting
Mar 6, 2013 - Special Meeting 03-06-13 Following Workshop
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

Pursuant to School Board Policy 8.122 (3), district instructional materials adoption committees convened during 2012-2013 to evaluate and recommend new materials for adoption in the area of reading/language arts, grades K-5.  Committee membership included: classroom teachers and coaches, school level administrators, district level curriculum administrators (literacy, ESE, multicultural, African and African American, Latino, and Gender Studies), and resource teachers.  Committees reviewed all instructional materials programs submitted for district adoption and some additional submissions that were considered appropriate for committee review.  Materials recommended for district adoption are contained in Attachment A.



The currently-adopted texts are five years old and will not comply with the new standards (English/Language Arts Common Core State Standards), and their increased demands for reading and writing instruction i.e., text complexity, close reading, balance of informational and literary text, evidence-based reading and writing, types of writing, and integration across subject areas). 



The committee has recommended reading/language arts instructional materials that best meet the demands of the new standards and the diverse needs of students in Palm Beach County.  These new materials align with the current formative assessment system used in the District and will support teachers in the design of instruction to meet the needs of all learners, including English Language Learners, Exceptional Student Education learners, and students participating in Dual Language programs.  The estimated cost for the proposed K-5 literacy instructional materials is $10,630,000.



In order to comply with Common Core Standards and the associated mathematical practices, the current K-5 mathematics series will be updated to include these standards.  This will ensure that the teachers have the materials needed to effectively deliver the depth and richness in mathematics demanded by the new standards.  By staying with the currently adopted publisher (HMH) and implementing the updates, the District received a savings of $800,000.  The estimated cost for the proposed K-5 mathematics instructional materials is $2,200,000.



The recommended instructional materials will be provided in print and/or online digital formats, as available.  Some teacher materials and resources will also be available on Learning Village.  The recommended instructional materials will be provided concurrently in Spanish for dual language schools.



Staff development for instructional materials will be provided at no cost to the District by publishers and will be focused on the appropriate use of materials to teach the reading and writing processes and implement the English/Language Arts Common Core State Standards. 



The Instructional Materials Department has worked closely with the Purchasing Department to obtain bids from the various publishers.  This will secure the best possible price to help ensure a complete K-5 elementary reading/language arts implementation.



The K-5 literacy and mathematics textbook purchase will be funded from the $14 million projected FY 2014 instructional materials allocation.  The projection is based on past allocations; actual funding is subject to legislative appropriation.  While the estimated FY 2014 instructional materials budget is sufficient to cover the $12.8 million cost of the proposed K-5 literacy and mathematics adoption, the projected cost for all instructional material requirements exceeds the estimated instructional material funds available by approximately $2.8 million.  Other costs totaling $4.5 million include pass through of charter schools’ prorated portion of the instructional materials allocation, Advanced Placement courses, dual enrollment, state-mandated expenditures for library media materials and science lab supplies, program maintenance costs, and repair/replacement of prior year adoption textbooks.



Due to the critical nature of the K-5 literacy and mathematics adoption purchase and to ensure integration of the materials at the start of the FY 2014 school year, approval of the instructional materials proposal is requested at this time, with the $2.8 million projected shortfall to be addressed during FY 2014 budget development.  

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the purchase of FY2014 instructional materials totaling $17,405,000 and authorize the necessary general fund budget allocation.

Pursuant to F.S. 1002.33(7)(b)(1), "A charter may be renewed provided a program review demonstrates that the criteria in paragraph (a) have been successfully accomplished and that none of the grounds for non-renewal established by paragraph (8)(a) has been documented."  In February, 2013, the district staff conducted a Program Review.  Based on the results of the criteria outlined in the Charter Renewal Program Review, Excel Leadership Academy shows there are several deficiencies found regarding Finance, Academics and Assessment.  At the March 13, 2013, School Board meeting, the Superintendent will recommend to non-renew the Charter between the School Board and Excel Leadership Academy.



F.S. 1002.33(8)(a)  The sponsor may choose not to renew or may terminate the charter for any of the following grounds:



1. Failure to participate in the state's education accountability system created in Fla. Stat. 1008.31, as required in this section, or failure to meet the requirements for student performance stated in the charter.



2. Failure to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management.



3. Violation of law.



4. Other good cause shown.



The Superintendent’s recommendation of the proposed non-renewal of Excel Leadership Academy’s Charter is based on:



1.  Failure by Excel Leadership Academy to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (2)(b)(2) and Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (7)(a)(2)(a).



2.   Failure by Excel Leadership Academy to comply with Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (8)(a)(2) and/or Fla. Stat. 1002.33 (7)(a)(9).
