On March 13, a 16 yr-old Palm Beach Gardens High School student fell in front of the library and was having an apparent seizure.  Officer Dean Wells detected a faint pulse but the breathing and pulse then stopped.  As Officer James O'Connor made the area safe, Officer Wells called police dispatch for paramedics and for the Automatic External Deliberator (AED) over the school radio.  School Nurses Janie Edwards and Maxine Thompson arrived.  Nurse Thompson and Officer Wells began CPR on the student.  Teacher Lawrence Patterson brought the AED from the cafeteria.  As the nurses continued the CPR, Officer Wells applied the AED.  Substitute teacher Robert Gaumond and teacher Tammy Elford arrived and assisted with the chest compressions.  The student regained labored breathing and a faint pulse but lost them again.  Palm Beach Gardens Fire/Rescue arrived as CPR was being administered a third time.  They lost and brought back the student again with AED and CPR and transported him to St. Mary's Hospital Trauma Unit.  The student was admitted in critical condition. The admitting trauma nurse said the student would not have survived had the group of school employees not taken the quick and decisive action.  The student is still undergoing an extensive period of recovery.

The 2008 Sprint Ahead for Education Grant Program will be used to create a recognition and incentive program called Palm Beach County Student Characterships for middle school students to be recognized for demonstrating exceptional character. Students will document how they have applied the six character pillars (trustworthiness, respect, responsibility, caring, fairness, and citizenship) during the school year. This program is targetd at middle schools because middle school students are the least likely to participate in character education programs.



 



Proposed activities include:



 



·         Train the Trainer workshop for school character contacts, Assistant Principals and/or teaching staff from each of the 33 middle schools, to explore effective character education strategies, the positive effect of character education on academic achievement, the Characterships project, and how to market and promote participation;



·         The Characterships Incentive Program and suggested activities will be developed with input from students, teachers, character contacts, and community representatives;



·         With parental permission, students will register to receive the award and will create portfolios that document their demonstration of each pillar through reflection activities and multimedia presentations.



·         Schools will select and recognize their award winning students.



·         The District will host an event to recognize the top three honorees from each school along with their counselors, teachers, parents, and principals. The students will receive a monetary reward.



 



The impact of this project will be measured in an increase in middle school participation in character education initiatives and an increased number of students who are recognized for character as measured by a participation matrix, as well as improved climate survey results.  Students’ expanded knowledge about the character pillars will be measured by pre/post test.

I recommend that the School Board approve the application for the  2008 Sprint Ahead for Education Grant Program for $25,000, effective July 1, 2008 through June 30, 2009, and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the award which are required for implementation of the award activities.

G2-Partnerships in Character Education Program 2008 Grant
Meeting
May 21, 2008 - Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion


The Partnerships in Character Education Program 2008 grant proposes to help students develop good character by integrating ethics education into instruction at four randomly selected high schools. In order to be eligible for funding, applicants are required to use a research design to evaluate program effectiveness. The ethics courses address the six pillars of character:  trustworthiness, respect, responsibility, caring, fairness, and citizenship. The courses, developed by the Youth Ethics Project, Inc., can be integrated into classroom instruction and are consistent with Florida’s Sunshine State Standards. Youth Ethics Project’s Kenneth W. Goodman, Ph.D., the founding co-director of the University of Miami's Ethics Programs, and Robin N. Fiore, Ph.D., Special Assistant to the Dean for Ethics Initiatives at Florida Atlantic University, will assist with program training and implementation.  The United Way of Palm Beach County, which is committed to character education in the community and schools, supports the project.



 



The project includes:



·         Train the Trainer workshops for district and school based staff on ethics education and project implementation;



·         Training, observation, and support of Social Studies teachers at the four randomly selected high schools;



·         School-based contact at each school to help support, promote and coordinate project delivery;



·         Character Education Conferences integrated with the Single School Culture Conferences to motivate staff and administrators, share best practices, demonstrate connection between ethics and character, introduce community partners and the project design, share evaluation design, etc.;



·         Increased participation by the schools in existing character education efforts;



·         Development of a Blackboard on-line class for  project sustainability; and



·         Ethics project lessons delivered to every student in Social Studies classes; these lessons address social and emotional standards related to Thinking and Reasoning, Working with Others, and Self-regulation.



·         The project will be evaluated, and the randomization process conducted, by an independent evaluator.
