AC1 - Agreement with the Health Care District of Palm Beach County
Meeting
Feb 29, 2012 - Special Meeting
Category
Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)

The original Agreement with the Health Care District of Palm Beach County was approved in 2001 to pay for basic health supplies for all schools and nursing services in schools built after 2001.



The Agreement addresses the following funding items:





  • To provide $75,000 per school for a school nurse in 34 schools built after 2001.

  • To pay $81,000 per annum to help offset the cost of student medical supplies.

  • The Health Care District is responsible to pay for background checks for all of the employees participating in the School Nurse Program.


The Agreement with the Health Care District will be effective retroactive to July 1, 2011.  This action does not prejudice the School Board.   The reason this item is coming to the Board at this time is because of the decision by both parties to rewrite and update the original Agreement.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Agreement with the Health Care District for the provision of basic school health services.

The financial impact to the Operating Budget is a total of $2,631,000.00 which is $2,550,000 for nurses and $81,000 for supplies.

On August 1, 2011, the Department of Charter Schools received an application from Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach (ULA) to open a charter school in the School District of Palm Beach County in August, 2012.



There were no other Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach applications on file in the State of Florida. Additionally, Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach has no current schools in operation.



According to Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach application submitted to Palm Beach County, the mission is, "ULA will accomplish its vision through top quality teaching and child-centered programs in a safe, positive and caring environment. ULA emphasizes development instruction in the social, emotional, physical, and intellectual development of each child, allowing for individual differences and learning styles. We will strive to have our parents, teachers, and community members actively involved in our students' learning. We desire to raise up the next generation of community leaders through targeted leadership development and entrepreneurial training. This will be accomplished both through curriculum and partnerships with local businesses to create a project oriented, hands on experience for each student."



The District's charter application process was explained to applicants during the New Applicants' Workshop held on May 27, 2011 and the applicant was invited and did not attend the workshop.



A review of the application was conducted using the required Florida Charter Application Evaluation Instrument.



Projected Enrollment



First Year              K-2       90 Projected Enrollment



Second Year         K-3       198 Projected Enrollment



Third Year             K-4       198 Projected Enrollment



Fourth Year           K-5       198 Projected Enrollment



Fifth Year               K-6      450 Projected Enrollment



Education Program Design



The Educational Program Design at Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach is a K-5 elementary school similar to other elementary schools in the School District of Palm Beach County.



Location



According to Section 14: Facilities, "Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach desires to be located in the Tamarind Avenue corridor of inner-city West Palm Beach in order to be most easily accessible to the under resourced youth of this community. The school has a tenative lease agreement with Urban Youth Impact (UYI). UYI is located at 2823 North Australian Avenue, West Palm Beach, Florida."



Applicant's Founding Governing Board



Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach has five local and two out of State founding board members. The experience of the members includes advisors, supporters and several anticipated initial Directors for the Corporation, including the incorporators.



Ratings of Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach Application



Designated District staff reviewed the 19 sections of Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach Application. There were two evaluations of this application. After the first evaluation, Urban Leadership Academy of West Palm Beach has seven of 19 sections that received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard," 3 sections that received a rating of "Does Not Meet the Standard," and 1 section that received a rating of "Not Applicable."

Listed below are the three sections that received a rating of "Does Not Meet the Standard" with the reason for each rating.

  • Section 17 - Budget: The assessment of the projected sources of revenue and expenses does not ensure the financial viability of the school.




  • Section 18 - Financial Management and Oversight: There is no segregation of duties, no budget for an accountant, no procedures in place to select an external auditor for the annual audit and the procedures to be taken by the school and board if the school is declaired to be in financial emergency are not documented.


Listed below is the section that received a rating "Not Applicable" with the reason the the rating.







  • Section 11 - Education Service Provider: The applicat does not intend to enter into a contract with an Education Service Provider.

After the second review, the four sections below still received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard." The reasons for each rating is included.

  • Section 17 - Budget: The assessment of the projected sources of revenue and expenses does not ensure the financial viability of the school.

After a second review, the four sections below still received a rating of "Partially Meets the Standard" and the reason for each rating.







  • Secion 1 - Mission, Guiding Principles and Purpose: The applicant outlined three languages in the mission statement that the school will offer. However, there was no mention as per the accountability will be addressed and sustained.




  • Section 3 - Educational Program Design: In the second review, the applicant clarified the student population to be served at the school. However, the applicant added plans to replicate Ben Gamla in Broward County but did not include current student performance data beyone school year 2008-2009.




  • Section 9 - Governance: There is no clear picture of the roles and responsibilities of the governance and management structure of the Board of Directors.




  • Section 10 - Management: Applicant did not address the viable Staffing Plan for each year of the charter term. The reference Budget Worksheet in Appendix "D" of 26 teachers for Year One does not align with the projected enrollment on the cover page.


After the second review, the one section below still received a rating of "Does Not Meet the Standard."







  • Section 17 - Budget: Many of the questions of the review were unanswered by applicant. Adjustments made to the budget did not satisfy the requirements to operate a fiscally sound charter school and avoid operating in a deficit.
