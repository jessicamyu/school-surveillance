The National Association of State Alcohol and Drug Abuse Directors (NASADAD) have awarded Families Acting Collaboratively to Educate and Involve Teens (FACE IT), the 2009 National Exemplary Award for Innovative Substance Abuse Prevention Programs, Practices, and Policies. Underwritten by the federal Center for Substance Abuse Prevention (CSAP), this Exemplary Award marks the twentieth consecutive year of recognizing local, creative, and effective programs working with the community to prevent substance abuse. FACE IT, which was one of seven program awarded nationally, was developed by the Prevention Center in the Department of Safe Schools. FACE IT is a substance abuse prevention and early intervention program for youth ages 12-18 and their parents or caregivers. Youth are referred to the program as an alternative to suspension for alcohol, tobacco, or other drugs, or by local law enforcement agencies as a sanction for an arrest related to a youth’s alcohol or other drug incident in the community. In six years, the program has served over 2,700 families (over 6,000 students and their parents/caregivers). Only 4% of students commit repeat offenses. This means the youth are in school where they can focus on their academic achievement. For all six years of the program, there has been continuous 100% parent involvement, which takes much of the credit for the youths’ decisions to make healthy, drug-free choices. Linda Salzman created the program and manages it and Laura Shoemaker administers the program.

The Learning for Life Character Education Grant supports implementation of Specific Appropriation 95 of SB 2600, which authorizes the Commissioner of Education to award grant dollars for projects to selected districts to assist schools in implementing the Learning for Life Character Education Program. The funding passes through the District to the Gulf Stream Council of Boy Scouts of America, Inc. This year the funding is from the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 State Fiscal Stabilization Fund Program.



 



The sub-grantee, Gulf Stream Council of Boy Scouts of America, Inc will implement and oversee the Learning for Life Character Education program in 16 elementary schools.  The selection of schools is based on several factors: previous year participation; Title 1 status, FCAT scores, and the willingness of the Guidance Counselor and Principal to commit to the use of the program in the classrooms. The Council will: train mentors, presenters, and instructors; provide curriculum materials and on-going orientations and training for school personnel; provide teacher, student, and principal recognition; maintain records; and complete program assessment and evaluation.



 



The goals of the program are to: provide mentoring activities to improve student performance through providing teaching and learning opportunities to students; improve students’ knowledge of character traits; and improve student performance in the classroom.



 



The curriculum for each grade level consists of 60 lesson plans designed to reinforce social, ethical, and academic skills in areas such as critical and creative thinking, ethical decision making, interpersonal relationships, practical living skills, building self worth, writing and other language arts, and citizenship.



 



The results of an external evaluation contracted by the Learning for Life in SY09 indicated that students increased their knowledge of character traits.

In order to address the need for new and well-trained teachers, FAU and its regional partners, which includes the School District of Palm Beach County, have created a three-prong, innovative approach that is practical, research-based and scalable.



Project FIT increases the number of education majors by paying college freshmen, sophomores and others interested in teaching to provide instructional support with model teachers.  This will increase the number and quality of students entering and remaining in the teacher preparation pipeline at all levels.  Undergraduate students will be receiving extensive classroom experiences and intensive, multi-year support with retired educator mentors, as well as a minimal stipend.  The students in the school classrooms will be provided with additional, qualified and caring undergraduates to directly support mutual learning in concert with the classroom teachers.



The Substituting with Mentors and Realistic Teaching (SMaRT) Program provides classroom experience to college juniors and seniors to work as substitute teachers with mentors.  A SMaRT site is defined as an elementary, middle, or high school where public school and university personnel work together to facilitate high levels of learning by all children of the school by using Florida Atlantic University students as substitute teachers to work under the direction of an FAU mentor. The relationship will be dedicated to improving the quality of pre-service and inservice teaching.  The School District of Palm Beach County and Florida Atlantic University will combine resources and provide leadership for the development of the SMaRT sites. 



The purpose of this relationship is to enable the parties to





  • improve the performance of students respective of each institution;

  • extend mutual research and grant capacity;

  • apply and model promising teaching practices;

  • promote targeted pre-professional instruction and continuous professional development to improve student performance;

  • inform and improve school and university instruction; and

  • test innovative means to develop new education professionals.


The Accelerated Induction into Teaching (AIT) Program fills high needs' teacher vacancies with selected university seniors who are ready to student teach as the actual classroom teacher with daily support from a mentor.  Both the District's Academic Business Plan and Staffing Plan call for intensive and targeted recruitment strategies to insure the availability of highly qualified teachers for high needs schools. The AIT Program is an initiative that provides for the identification and placement of high-quality teacher candidates in high needs' schools.





  • AIT candidates will be selected from Florida Atlantic University's Teacher Training Program;

  • All AIT candidates will have successfully passed the CLAST and the Florida Teacher Training Program;

  • AIT will be supervised by an experienced Master Mentor hired by FAU and reviewed by the School District.  The Master Mentor will provide direct supervision/observation.  (see agreement);

  • The AIT Program management model incorporates three levels of supervision: Master Mentor, University Supervision, and Cooperating Teacher; and 

  • The AIT Program will reduce the need to employ alternative certification applicants and substitute teachers.

This Project Modification contains the following items:



Item #1







  • CCD #1 in the amount of $170,875.00.




  • Description: Replace 2,714 feet of underground chilled water pipe and 1,744 feet of underground domestic water pipe to the existing classroom buildings/pods that are not being renovated as part of this project.




  • Cause: School District Requested




  • Justification: The original buildings are over 25 years old. Underground chilled and domestic water piping has been failing, causing interruptions to the systems and the educational process of the students.  The piping was replaced over the summer of 2009 to eliminate on-going disruptions.  The Guaranteed Maximum Price contained an allowance of $100,000.00 to replace the worst sections of pipe.  After seeing the condition of the pipe Facilities Services requested that all pipe be replaced and provided the additional funding from their budget.


Item #2







  • CCD #2 in the amount of $172,647.00.




  • Description:
     1. Furnish and install underground propane tank ($12,708)
     2. Window Testing ($10,650)
     3. Upgrade building/room signage in existing structures ($27,853)
     4. Replace doors in existing Building 4 ($12,308)
     5. Repaint exterior of existing buildings ($23,510)
     6. Add card access at seven doors ($21,695)
     7. Added knockdown finish and semi-gloss paint in corridors and stairwells ($40,135)
     8. Added roof venting ($23,788)




  • Cause: School District Requested




  • Justification: Items 1 and 2 were included in the original project budget as SDPBC purchases.  Item 1 was added to the Construction Manager's (CM) scope of work to ensure the tank was installed per District guidelines.  Item 2 was added to the CM's scope of work to ensure better coordination between the window installer and testing firm.  The SDPBC's decision to include items 3, 4 and 5 was made after the GMP was approved.  Item 3 is now included in all addition projects.  An allowance should have been included in the GMP for items 4 and 5.  This is a lesson learned for future projects.  Items 6 through 8, although not presently required by the District Design Criteria and Master Specifications, were added to this project and represent changes that we anticipate will be included in the next edition of the District Design Criteria and Master Specifications.


Item #3







  • CCD #3 in the amount of $43,948.00.




  • Description: 
    1. Level Building 4 slab prior to construction new administration area ($1,932.00)
    2. Re-route main and emergency power to existing Building 11 ($34,104.00)
    3. Re-route interior roof drain in Building 4 ($7,912.00)




  • Cause: Unforeseen Conditions




  • Justification:  The poor condition of the Building 4 slab was not identified until the existing administration walls and floors were removed.  The power and emergency power feeds to Building 11 were shown incorrectly on the as-built drawings.  They were shown as being fed from Building 7, but were actually fed from the existing cafeteria which was demolished as part of this project.  An interior roof drain was not shown on the as-built drawings.  It had to be re routed because it fell in the middle of an office in the new administration area layout.


Item #4







  • CCD #4 in the amount of $ 56,414.00.




  • Description: 
    1. Add retention pond and associated fencing in center of bus loop ($43,963)
    2. Furnish and install Primex clocks for existing buildings ($12,451)




  • Cause:  School District Requested




  • Justification:  The retention pond in the center of the bus loop was added to provide capacity for the existing concreteables.  The decision has since been made to remove the concreteables. This retention pond will remain and will provide improved stormwater drainage for the existing school and provide capacity for future expansion.  The Primex clocks were added to the existing buildings so there would be one system for the entire school.
