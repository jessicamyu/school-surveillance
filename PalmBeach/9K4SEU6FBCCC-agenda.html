LA1 - Collective Bargaining Agreement Between SDPBC and PBA, re: 2010 Modifications to the 2009-2011 Collective Bargaining Agreement
Meeting
Jul 28, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action


  1. Regular wages of Officers are maintained for calendar 2010 at 2009 levels.


  2. Officers may accrue compensatory time up to twenty-four (24) hours.  This represents an increase of four (4) hours.  At the discretion of the Chief, Officers with accrued compensatory time may use such time up to a maximum of one (1) day per week during the school year.


  3. Workers Compensation payments may be paid at 100% of an Officer’s regular wages for up to six (6) months when suffering a disabling injury (as determined by a WC Doctor) in certain circumstances such as breaking-up a fight, making an arrest, in a vehicular accident/collision while operating a police cruiser pursuant to an authorized “Code Red” situation, or assisting in the protection of human life.


  4. Paid bereavement leave of up to three (3) days when an Officer is required to be absent due to the death of a member of the Officer’s immediate family.


  5. Amended retro-actively to January 1, 2010, the Attendance Incentive provisions from an annual supplement of $1,000 for perfect attendance for a full calendar year to a stipend of $700 when an Officer is not absent for the first six (6) months of the calendar year, and another $700 for not being absent the last six (6) months of the calendar year.  Sixteen (16) officers have attendance records during the first six (6) months of 2010 to earn this Attendance Incentive stipend.


  6. Agreed that Officers are to be paid only for holidays that are approved as a part of the School Board’s annually adopted School Calendar.


  7. Agreed that the District may require all Officers to be paid via direct deposit to a financial institution selected by the Officer.  In the meantime, Officers who have voluntarily elected to have their paychecks direct deposited may not revert to receiving a paper check.     


 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the 2010 Modifications to the SDPBC – PBA 2009-2011 Collective Bargaining Agreement.

RE-1 Former Suncoast High School Charter School Lease
Meeting
Jul 28, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)
1. Suncoast High School is relocating to their new campus located at 1717 Avenue "S", Riviera Beach, for the 2010-2011 school year.
2.  The former Suncoast High School has been declared as "interim" and is available for lease.
3.  On March 29, 2010 the School District distributed a Solicitation to Charter Schools to lease property at the former Suncoast High School.  (See Exhibit "A")
4.  Inlet Grove Community High School, Inc., a Conversion Charter School was the only school to submit a proposal.
5.  Inlet Grove Community High School met all the eligibility requirements.
6.  The term of the lease shall commence on August 1, 2010 and extend until July 31, 2012.
7.  Inlet Grove may request one year extensions that will require School Board approval.
8.  The tenant may not make any improvements, additions, modifications or alterations to the Leased Premises without School Board approval.
9.  The School Board shall be responsible to make repairs to the roof, structural, electrical and plumbing systems.
10. The Tenant will be allowed to lease portions of the Leased Premises in conformity with School Board Policy 7.18 Community Use of  School Facilities and retain 100% of the gross lease revenue. Tenant shall be required to spend at least 30% on School Board approved alterations.
11. The School Board and Tenant shall be responsible for the respective improvements set forth in Exhibit "C". The School Board's portion of these improvements is not to exceed approximately $305,000.
12. Tenant shall be responsible for all utility fees related to the operation of the Leased Premises.
13. Upon termination or expiration of the lease the tenant shall be permitted to re-occupy its original facility "Inlet Grove".
14. If the School Board determines that the Leased Premises shall be demolished to prepare for the construction of the New Riviera Beach Area High School, the School Board has the right to terminate the lease by providing tenant 120 days prior written notice.  The School Board shall pay all reasonable moving cost associated with Tenant's relocation back to the original facility, in an amount not to exceed $35,000.
15. Board approval is contingent upon execution of the lease by Inlet Grove.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Lease Agreement between Inlet Grove Community High School, Inc., a Conversion Charter School and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the former Suncoast High School located at 600 West 28th Street, Riviera Beach, and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

Joseph Sanches (sanchesj@palmbeachschools.org)

The financial impact to the Capital Project's Fund Budget is not to exceed $325,000.

PM1 - Amendment to New Suncoast HS ILA with Riviera Beach for Roadway Improvements
Meeting
Jul 28, 2010 - Spec Mtgs and Workshops re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)




  • On February 3, 2010, the Board approved an Interlocal Agreement ("ILA") with the City of Riviera Beach ("City") setting forth the School Board's responsibilities to construct certain roadway and traffic associated the new Suncoast High School.






  • The ILA contained an August 1, 2010 deadline for several of items of work to be completed by the School Board (specifically the work required in Paragraphs 2, 3, 4 and 5 of the ILA) along with a provision for extension of the deadline in the event that the School Board was not able to complete the work prior to August 1, 2010.






  • As required by the ILA, the School Board staff provided the City with monthly updates on the progress being made and when it was apparent that the August 1, 2010 deadline could not be met, staff timely requested an extension of the deadline in accordance with the terms of the ILA.






  • The Amendment memorializes the extension of the completion deadline until 193 calendar days from the issuance of the required permits by Palm Beach County and the City, which is December 26, 2010.

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the First Amendment to Interlocal Agreement between the City of Riviera Beach and the School Board for Roadway, Traffic, and Public Safety Improvements as it relates to the new Suncoast High School and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.
