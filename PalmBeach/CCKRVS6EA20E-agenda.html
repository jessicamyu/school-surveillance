DESCRIPTION:

This is a renewal of a cooperative agreement between the (DATA) Kelly Center and the School District. This program provides educational services for up to (24) twenty-four substance abuse, disciplinary and truant male and female youths between the ages of 13 and 18 whose families have voluntarily enrolled them in the program. 

  1. The goal of the program is to develop the social skills and academic knowledge to ensure a successful transition to their home school after completion of the program. Length of the program is ninety (90) days and is residential. Students are evaluated through diagnostic reading and math assessments.
  2. The (DATA) Kelly Center provides intensive counseling and behavior management in a therapeutic setting with student and family counseling and social services after the student has completed the program.
  3. The (DATA) Kelly Center provides room and board and all other operational expenses incurred by the program.
  4. The School District is responsible for the instructional staff and materials needed to provide the educational program.

Recommended Action
I recommend that the School Board approve the renewal of the National Student Clearinghouse StudentTracker for High Schools Agreement for Districts or High Schools and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and release or request data related to the Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

On May 9, 2012, the School Board approved the National Student Clearinghouse StudentTracker for High Schools Agreement for Districts or High Schools.  This organization provides a nationwide, central repository of information on the enrollment status and educational achievements of postsecondary students. 

 

The District will transmit to the National Student Clearinghouse its historical Graduate File dating back 10 years in 2022. Thereafter, it will submit lists of new graduates each year after conferral of diplomas. The District agrees that it will submit its Graduates Files electronically and that they will contain the data elements and configuration reasonably required by the Clearinghouse.

 

By participating in an Agreement with the National Student Clearinghouse, Palm Beach County will continue to:

  • Obtain data on the postsecondary enrollment status and educational achievement of students who have graduated from high school in Palm Beach County. 
  • Submit and receive data enabling the District to tract postsecondary student data for research and reporting purposes.
  • Gain information from the research to use in developing a college readiness pathway.
  • Document the effectiveness of college readiness strategies used by schools and programs within the District.

 

With data obtained from the National Student Clearinghouse, the Department of Research & Evaluation has been able to conduct analyses to determine which set of student characteristics best predicts successful postsecondary outcomes.  This information is currently incorporated into Educational Data Warehouse (EDW) reporting and has been used in the development of college readiness strategies.

 

This agreement will be in effect from July 1, 2022 and remains in effect until June 30, 2023.  However, this Agreement may be terminated by either parties by providing 60 days written notice.

BD1 Board Discussion - ROBINSON - Burden of Proof for the Different Investigative Units of the Palm Beach County School District

19 Board Discussion Items

School Board member Dr.  Debra Robinson will discuss the following in relation to burden of proof for the three different investigative units of the Palm Beach County School District:

 

revised discussion item:
 
It is clear to me that the different burdens of proof required by the different investigative units of PBCSD to report "findings" can create some confusion.
The criminal justice system/ school police require the highest burden of proof to recommend consequences. That is beyond a reasonable doubt.
Professional Standards on the other hand requires clear and convincing evidence to forward an investigation for administrative consequences.
The Office of the Inspector General requires the lowest burden of proof which is a preponderance of the evidence.
 
I believe this contributes to confusion when/if the IG reports a finding  but  professional standards or law enforcement finds none. It also allows one to think that investigations are duplicative when they are not. The OIG investigation answers whether or not there are findings that meet their burden of proof while Professional Standards might need to supplement the investigation by the OIG to determine whether the evidence meets their higher burden. I believe that "legally sufficient" bears the burden of the relevant investigative unit.
 
I request that the board discuss addressing this.
My proposal is that every investigation completed by any of the 3 units ends with a statement describing the 3 levels of burden of proof, which level of burden that investigation required and a statement as to whether or not the allegations were founded, unfounded or unsubstantiated based upon that investigative unit's required burden of proof. I also suggest that whenever the term "legally sufficient" is used the appropriate burden or standard is identified.
