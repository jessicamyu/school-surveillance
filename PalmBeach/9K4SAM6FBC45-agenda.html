This contract is brought to the Board reflecting a recent statewide price increase by Florida Virtual School for any new students enrolled in District Virtual Instruction Programs. 



The contract provides that Florida Virtual School will deliver a full-time, 180 day instructional program for eligible students in kindergarten through grade 12.  The contractor is to provide instructional and administrative staff and all necessary instructional materials as defined by the Department of Education for a full-time K-12 virtual school.



Florida Statute 1002.45 authorizes school districts to implement virtual instruction programs through district-operated programs or programs providers approved by the Florida Department of Education.



The School District provides the eligibility, registration, enrollment, evaluation and ongoing monitoring of students in these programs through the Virtual School Department. 



Additionally, it is important to note that the 2011 Digital Learning Now Act requires that all high school students have one virtual course as a part of their 24 credits to graduate.

Legal Advisor was hired by the School Board in March, 2005 to assist and advise the School Board and Chief Counsel with various Federal Communications Commission (FCC) matters. Under this contract, Legal Advisor provides legal advice and expertise related to the FCC licenses held by the School Board for Educational Broadband Service spectrum (EBS). A portion of the EBS-related work represents a recurring effort that is necessary and will continue for as long as the School Board holds the EBS licenses. The remainder of the EBS-related work is made of various initiatives that are finite and will ultimately be completed. Under a recommended flat fee arrangement, the Consultant shall perform the following services: 1. Finalize, file, and prosecute substantial service filings for the School Board's eight EBS licenses and respond to FCC inquiries regarding same; 2. Complete spectrum lease agreement with the South Florida Water Management District for KZB28 channel A1 and obtain FCC approval of lease; 3. Complete documentation associated with EBS band plan transition in Palm Beach; 4. Update master licensing and operational memorandum to reflect transition and other changes; 5. Providing ongoing general regulatory compliance advice concerning licensing and related matters; and 6. Assist with miscellaneous unanticipated FCC and spectrum-related issues. Work that extends beyond the scope of the flat fee arrangement, such as the potential leasing of the Board’s licensed spectrum to a commercial operator, will be performed at the discounted governmental rates shown on Exhibit “B” or as a separate flat fee arrangement. The Board needs an attorney with expertise in FCC regulatory matters, spectrum use and valuation issues to assist staff in the protection of the EBS licenses and their value, and evaluation of the alternatives available to the Board. The firm of Fish & Richardson and Attorney Ed Lavergne have provided excellent service since being hired in March 2005. Approval of this contract will allow Mr. Lavergne and his team to continue to provide the Office of General Counsel and District staff with sound advice that can be used as the basis for making decisions regarding the EBS licenses held by the School Board.

AC2 - Statement of Work 2 (SOW 2) Between the School Board of Palm Beach County, Florida and CORE K12 Education (CORE K12), a Division of CORE Education and Consulting Solutions, Inc.
Meeting
Aug 3, 2011 - Special Meetings
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)

The FY2012 Statement of Work 2 (SOW 2) with CORE K12 provides an assessment for learning system that puts real-time information about student performance into the hands of teachers and administrators. CORE K12 enables teachers to administer, score, and immediately see the results of their student's performance on test questions and assessments and to access instructional resources. The questions and assessments are based on the Next Generation Sunshine State Standards (NGSSS) benchmarks and adhere to FCAT 2.0 Item Specifications.



There are several reasons why our District provides this system to our schools.





  • Differentiated Accountability (DA):  The Florida Department of Education (FDOE) requires districts to "prescribe interim (Benchmark baseline, mid-year, and mini) assessments in reading, writing, mathematics, and science for Level 1-3 students."  Also, districts must ensure schools have "real-time access to student achievement data."

  • District Accreditation:  To be accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Council on Accreditation and School Improvement (SACS-CASI), a district must "establish and implement a comprehensive assessment system, aligned with the system's expectations for student learning, that yields information which is reliable, valid, and free of bias."

  • Research:  There is a  substantial amount of research on the positive impact of assessments for learning.  For example, the National Center for Educational Achievement has determined that school districts which have increased student achievement and reduced student performance gaps have a balanced and comprehensive assessment system.  This system includes assessments of and assessments for learning that are aligned with the district's expectations for student learning.  The assessments for learning include:



    • Classroom-based assessments

    • School-wide benchmark assessments

    • District-wide benchmark baseline and mid-year assessments.




One of the nation's leading educational researchers, Dr. Robert Marzano, has found that the following school-level practices, called Opportunity to Learn, have three times the impact on student achievement than any other school practice:





  • Students and their teachers know what students are expected to learn and how well students are learning it.

  • Teachers know if they are teaching the expected content.


It is also important to note that the State of Florida has selected the teacher evaluation model that is based on the work of Dr. Robert Marzano and the principal evaluation model that is based on the work of Dr. Doug Reeves.  Our District will be using these evaluation models in FY2012.  A key component of both models is educators using assessments for learning that are aligned with end-of-year learning goals.



In order to comply with state DA requirements, SACS-CASI standards, and to adhere to research-based best practices, the FY2012 SOW 2 with CORE K12 provides the following deliverables:







  • Real-time results on teacher-developed and district-provided assessments of learning delivered through the Web, and/or by scanners and clickers using the Assessment Center (AC3) consisting of the following components:






    • National item bank (312,259 items)




    • Florida item bank (36,894 items)




    • FCAT Test Maker Item Bank (16,550 items)




    • 288 interim benchmark assessments focused on NGSSS benchmarks, adhering to FCAT 2.0 item specifications and aligned with District's scope and sequence for reading and mathematics




    • 16 District fall and winter diagnostic assessments:  NGSSS Grades 6 Reading, Grade 10 Reading and Reading Retakes; Algebra 1, Geometry, and Biology; and SSS Grade 10 Reading and Mathematics Retakes




    • 66 additional interim benchmark assessments for NGSSS Science, Biology, and Geometry




  • Student, teacher, and parent instructional resources and community outreach through CORE K12


The CORE K12 price for the FY2012 SOW 2 is $640,500.  This is more than 50% less than the CORE K12 price for the FY2011 SOW, which was $1,316,486.45.  The reduction is a result of three actions:



1.  CORE K12 providing the following at no cost to the District:







  • uploading 66 interim benchmark assessments






    • In FY2011, the charge for assessment development and uploading was $121,493.




  • uploading diagnostic assessments






    • In FY2011, the charge for assessment development and uploading was $76,043.45.




  • data transfers from CORE K12 to the District's Educational Data Warehouse (EDW).






    • In FY2011, the charge was $16,000.




  • the FCAT Test Maker School Bank.






    • In FY2011, the charge was $144,000




    • If our schools separately purchased this item bank, at a cost of about $4,000 per school, it would cost the schools over $748,000.


2.  Change of Scope







  • project management.






    • In FY2011, the SOW charge for two CORE K12 project managers was $225,000.




    • In FY2012, the Department of Research and Evaluation will provide project management by reorganizing staff from Assessment and from Research and Evaluation.




  • Additional scan sheets






    • In FY2011, the charge  was $90,000.  The District has a surplus of scan sheets as a very high percentage of schools tested online.


3.  Cost recovery







  • CORE K12 is crediting the District $75,000 for the District's intellectual property, which is the District-developed Diagnostic Assessments and/or norms.




  • CORE K12 may provide the District additional funds for the sale of District-developed Diagnostic Assessments and/or norms, as outlined in the Cost Recovery Content Licensing Agreement.


 

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Statement of Work 2 for the Assessment for Learning System in the amount of $640,500.
