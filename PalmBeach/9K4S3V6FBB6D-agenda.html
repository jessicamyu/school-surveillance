Personnel Disciplinary Action - Jeanrousseau Paul
Meeting
Oct 22, 2008 - Workshops and Spec Mtgs re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action

On April 5, 2008, the Lake Worth Police Department was conducting an undercover drug investigation in the City of Lake Worth, Florida.  Their investigation subsequently lead to Jeanrousseau Paul who on April 5, 2008 sold illegal narcotics (cocaine) to an undercover police informant.  The drug transaction was recorded on video with audio sound.



On April 11, 2008, Jeanrousseau Paul was arrested by the Lake Worth Police Department for sale of cocaine to an undercover officer after a second drug buy was set up.  At the time of his arrest, he was found in possession of illegal narcotics (cocaine) and a .40 caliber semi-automatic weapon.



There is probable cause to support the termination of Jeanrousseau Paul based on findings of failure to self-report arrest, off-the-job use or involvement with illegal drugs, alcohol or other controlled substances and misconduct with relation to careless disregard for policy, directive and rules.  Furthermore, the findings included violations of School Board Policy 1.013, Paragraph 1, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff, 3.96, Drug and Alcohol-Free Workplace and 3.961, Drug and Alcohol-Free Workplace Policy for Employees Performing Safety-Sensitive Functions and Holders of Commercial Drivers Licenses.



On July 18, 2008, Jeanrousseau Paul plead guilty to sale of cocaine, possession of cocaine with intent to sell and person engage in criminal offense while armed; all second degree felonies.



Jeanrousseau Paul's off-the-job involvement with illegal drugs adversely affects the District's image and relationship with students, other district employees and the public.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Jeanrousseau Paul's suspension without pay, effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Jeanrousseau Paul's employment effective November 6, 2008 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.

There is no financial impact to the District.

Personnel Disciplinary Action - Kenneth Poitier
Meeting
Oct 22, 2008 - Workshops and Spec Mtgs re Academics
Category
Consent
Type

On June 3, 2008, a school police investigation began at Emerald Cove Middle School after an eight-grade student reported an inappropriate relationship with head custodian Kenneth Poitier.  Investigation revealed Kenneth Poitier had been texting the student and soliciting the student for inappropriate pictures of herself.  Mr. Poitier was also found to have given the student some Captain Morgan alcoholic rum.  During an audio sworn taped statement, Mr. Poitier admitted to having inappropriate contact/conversation with the student and giving her alcoholic rum.  These incidents took place on the school campus at Emerald Cove Middle School.  Mr. Poitier did posses alcohol on District property while on duty.  His decision to give alcoholic rum to the student while on campus posed a great danger to the student.



There is probable cause to support the termination of Kenneth Poitier based on findings of failure to exercise best professional judgment, failure to maintain and/or provide for the safety of students, misconduct - willful disregard for policy, directive, rules and inappropriate relationship and/or solicitation of students.  Furthermore, the findings included violations of School Board Policy 1.013, Paragraph 1, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and Staff, 3.96, Drug and Alcohol-Free Workplace, Florida State Statute 562.11, Giving Alcohol to a Minor, and SEIU/FPSU (supervisory unit), Collective Bargaining Agreement, Article 15 (6), Discipline of Employees (Progressive Discipline).

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Kenneth Poitier's suspension without pay, effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Kenneth Poitier's employment effective November 6, 2008 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statute.

Personnel Disciplinary Action - Johnny Worthen
Meeting
Oct 22, 2008 - Workshops and Spec Mtgs re Academics
Category
Consent
Type
Action

On May 22, 2008, Johnny Worthen allowed an armed fugitive to hide out on the school campus of Glades Central Community High School during a Code Red lockdown.  The armed fugitive was fleeing from a nearby complex after shooting his girlfriend.  The school's video surveillance camera captured Johnny Worthen assisting the fugitive and allowing the fugitive to hide out on the campus for a time period of approximately three (3) hours.  Johnny Worthen had many opportunities to alert responding law enforcement officers as well as school administrators of the armed fugitive being on campus; he chose not to.  Johnny Worthen allowing the armed fugitive to remain on campus posed great danger to the students and staff who were present on campus.



There is probable cause to support the termination of Johnny Worthen based on findings of failure to maintain and/or provide for the safety of students, failure to exercise best professional judgment, misuse of district time, technologies, facilities and/or property, failure to self-report, arrest and misconduct with relation to careless disregard for policy, directive and rules.  Furthermore, the findings included violations of School Board Policy 1.013, Paragraph 1, Responsibilities of the School District Personnel and staff and SEIU/FPSU, Collective Bargaining Agreement, Article 17 (6), Discipline of Employees (Progressive Discipline).

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve Johnny Worthen's suspension without pay, effective immediately.  I further recommend that the School Board approve the termination of Johnny Worthen's employment effective November 6, 2008 subject only to appeal under Section 120.569, Florida Statutes.
