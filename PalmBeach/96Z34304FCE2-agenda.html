Recommended Action
I recommend the School Board renew the purchase of the High School Interscholastic Athletic Insurance and Student Catastrophic Accident Insurance, effective August 1, 2013 to August 1, 2014 from the Monumental Life Insurance Company (General Agent: Bollinger Insurance) for a combined total premium of $1,609,087.

DESCRIPTION:

These are two separate policies, although they must be purchased together with this company. The first policy is the Student Catastrophic Accident Insurance policy which provides coverage of up to $5 million with a $25,000 per student deductible. This is required coverage for the District's participation in the Florida High School Activities Association (FHSAA) for sanctioned sports and covers medical expenses resulting from accidental injuries to any student while participating in school activities (including daytime activities).  The terms and conditions will remain the same and the premium for this coverage is $185,567.

 

The second policy is the High School Interscholastic Athletic plan. This policy covers up to $25,000 in medical expenses for accidental injuries to high school interscholastic athletes and cheerleaders. The FHSAA also requires some type of student accident insurance in order to participate in FHSAA sanctioned events.  Due to high claim activity, the premium will increase 12% to $1,423,520 or an increase of $175,000 over the current premium.  The primary reason for the increase is the high loss ratio of 126%. Our broker has advised that the carrier wanted a 30% increase and has agreed to divide the increase over 2 years. Thus we are facing another 12-18% increase next year. Other quotes for this year were much higher (the next quote was $2.9 mill); thus the decision to remain with this carrier.

 

Student insurance fees, as allowed by School Board Policy 5.60, traditionally cover approximately half of the premium.  Discussions with District staff and high school principal representatives are planned to discuss the corresponding student fee increase for the 2013-14 school year.  The current fee of $75.00 per student and will not generate enough funds to cover 50% of the premium.  It is anticipated that the fee for football will increase to $100 while the fee for all other sports will increase to $80. Students participating in multiple sports will only pay the appropriate fee once.

The U-FUTuRES program is a two-year (30 credit) job-embedded graduate degree program in science education.  The goal of this program is to prepare science teacher leaders to lead a transformation in middle school science.  Through a grant from the National Science Foundation, the University of Florida will provide 17 full scholarships for Palm Beach County middle school science teachers.  As a part of the U-FUTuRES program, participating middle school science teachers will implement the IQWST curriculum in their classrooms.  IQWST is an inquiry-based middle school science curriculum.
 
• Florida STEM TIPS Initiative – Teacher Induction & Professional Support (STEM TIPS)
Funded by the Florida Department of Education, this project focuses on building a prototype statewide induction model for new secondary STEM teachers.  In addition to addressing teachers’ primary instructional concerns, it will provide resources to ensure ongoing, personalized, online and on-site, subject- and grade-specific mentoring and professional development.  Funds are available to build and support the program during the first year of this proposed initiative.
 
• Palm Beach STEM Initiative
In order to improve the consistency of quality STEM education programs across the district, particularly in Title I schools, the Palm Beach STEM initiative will target schools to apply for the following tiered levels of professional development and STEM programming opportunities:

Recommended Action
I recommend that the Board approve the School Food Service plan to increase paid lunch meal prices to be effective July 1, 2013.

DESCRIPTION:  Due to federal regulation it is recommended that Palm Beach County “gradually” increase the “paid reimbursable” meal price over the next three years to begin to align with the current legislation.  School Food Service is recommending a $0.10 per lunch meal price increase per year, for three years starting with FY2014 and ending FY2016.   It is important to note that there have only been two price increases in the past twenty-four years. The provision within the “Healthy, Hunger-Free Kids Act” that was signed into law on December 13, 2010, which requires that school nutrition programs increase the “paid reimbursable” meal price to be equal to the difference between the free meal reimbursement ($2.88) and the paid meal reimbursement ($0.29) or $2.59 ($2.88 - $.029 = $2.59).  

 

If approved, the School Food Services Department will collaborate with the Public Affairs department to provide notification of the price increase through several means to ensure that communication is sufficient and parents and guardians are aware of the new prices starting FY2014.  Beyond FY2016, School Food Services will analyze the National School Lunch Program annually and will make appropriate price increases in order to continue to work towards being in compliance with public law.

 

See attachment for PLE details to include the pricing structure for paid lunch meals for the next three years.
