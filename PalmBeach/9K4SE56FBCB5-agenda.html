Subject
Redistricting Maps Scenarios
Meeting
Oct 12, 2011 - Workshops
Category
Workshop Item(s)
Type
Discussion

On September 21, 2011, the School Board adopted the following redistricting criteria:





  • Achieve population equity among Districts (10% or less variance among districts' population)

  • Adhere to Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act of 1965, as amended and interpreted through case law

  • Maintain integrity of communities of interest, where practicable

  • Maintain integrity of municipal boundaries, where practicable

  • Follow major natural and man-made boundaries

  • Insure compactness

  • Contiguity of Districts

  • Preserve the core of the existing voter's districts


Staff was directed to bring back at least two scenarios that are consistent with these criteria.  During discussion, we were also asked to look at SR 7 rather than Lyons Road as a dividing line between Districts 3 and 6.



Included with the Board Report are two base maps and three redistricting scenarios, which are summarized below:





  • 2001 Base Map - This reconciles the 2000 Census Data with the current Board Member Districts.  This map was created for reference and baseline purposes.


 





  • 2010 Base Map - This map shows current Districts and 2010 Census Data.


 





  • Scenario I - This Scenario moves Lake Park from District 7 to District 1 in an effort to achieve equal distribution of population.  This Scenario achieves the greatest equal distribution of population between the Districts.  However, it divides a number of communities.  Lyons Road, south of Lake Worth Road is used as the east/west dividing line between Districts 3 and 6 and Clint Moore Road is used as a dividing line between District 5 and Districts 3 and 4.


 





  • Scenario II - This Scenario retains Lake Park in District 7, which is pertinent because we recently learned that the County will likely be moving Lake Park from BCC District 1 to BCC District 7.  Additionally, the northeastern part of Boca Raton is moved from District 4 to 5.




SR7/441 and the Turnpike rather than Lyons Road are used as the dividing line between Districts 3 and 6, and major roadways are used wherever possible as District boundaries in an effort to reduce voter confusion.  The communities that were split in Scenario I are unified in Scenario II, including the West Palm Beach Parker Ridge Community, Westgate Estates, Town of Haverhill, Valencia Shores, Melrose, and Windsor Communities.  This scenario shows a net reduction of one city in District 4.



 





  • Scenario III - This Scenario gave preference to municipal boundaries where possible, rather than major roads.  It is generally the same as Scenario II in all other respects except it moves Atlantis back to District 3 in a further attempt to address the concern regarding the large number of cities represented by District 4.  Scenario III is a net reduction of three cities for District 4. 


All three Scenarios balance population as much as practicable, from a deviation of 1.47% to 2.65%.  All Scenarios provide for one Board Member District in the Glades and combine Wellington and the Acreage into one District.  Pursuant to Board direction, staff contacted the Glades Tri-Cities Coalition to seek their input.  The Coalition's response is provided in the attached August 31, 2011 correspondence indicating endorsement of one Board Member representing the Glades Tri-Cities.



Included in the support materials is an analysis of the cities represented by the Current School Board Member Districts, as well as each Scenario.



These scenarios have been reviewed by the Redistricting legal expert, Jerry Wilson, for consistency with traditional Redistricting principles and legal requirements. (Please refer to the report.)



At this workshop, staff is seeking direction regarding which Scenario(s) should be posted on the Redistricting web site, http://www.pbcredistricting.info/ for public review and comment.  We are also seeking direction regarding the adoption timeline.
