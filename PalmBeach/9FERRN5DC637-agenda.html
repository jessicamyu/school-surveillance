In conjunction with Black History Month, the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Coordinating Committee Oratorical contest winners will be recognized. The Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Coordinating Committee's mission is to provide cultural activities to honor Dr. King and to enable people to reflect on his life and teachings through education, cultural performances, exhibitions and events. These multi-ethnic, family-oriented activities garner the support of families, community leaders and activists to motivate students, enrich their lives, and launch them toward a career in their area of interest.

 

The School Board will also recognize Men of Tomorrow.  Men of Tomorrow focuses attention on young African-American men and their importance to society. The fraternity solicits the assistance of high school counselors, teachers, community leaders and the brotherhood to find young men who meet the qualifications set by the brotherhood.  With this group, the brothers embark upon a mission to affect the entire community – a mission of service, scholarship among youth and a desire to improve the surroundings.  

 

Each young man accepted into the organization must meet the qualifications established by the brotherhood: be a student in grade 9-12; have a 2.0 or higher grade point average; be recommended by a counselor, teacher, minister or chapter brother; be of good character; and have parental support of the organization. 

 

During the course of the year, Men of Tomorrow (MOT) members must participate in monthly church visitations, educational tutorial programs, the Project Alpha Youth Symposium, college prep workshops, community projects and joint activities designed to develop the whole person.

DESCRIPTION:

The Quantum Foundation has approved a 3-year, $200,000.00 grant for a Healthcare Immersion Project focusing on preparing Medical Science Academy students to enter the healthcare workforce. The Department of Choice & Career Options administers sixteen Medical Sciences Academies, at the high school level, serving approximately 5,000 students annually. The Medical Sciences Academy offers an innovative, integrated learning environment designed to provide students with an academic foundation in basic medical sciences and related careers. The high school program prepares students to transition into the healthcare workforce and obtain industry certifications and licensures.

 

In partnership with The Education Foundation of Palm Beach County, funding will be used to purchase durable medical equipment and supplies at all sixteen high school academies. Specifically, funds will be used to purchase medical equipment currently being used in the clinical setting (integrated diagnostic systems, EKG Machines, hospital beds, medical textbooks and instructional materials, etc.). Students will be trained to properly use equipment while maintaining patient safety and be able to access and interpret results for a clinical diagnosis.

Funding will also be used to support the clinical component of the program, which includes transportation to and from on-site training facilities. Students are required to complete at least 75 hours in the clinical setting in order to complete the medical sciences program. In addition, transportation will be provided for students attending the State Health Occupation Students of America Leadership Annual Conference and public/private post-secondary institutions that offer advanced healthcare degrees.

Heritage Elementary has been awarded LEED® - Existing Buildings, Operations & Maintenance (EBOM) certification as established by the U.S. Green Building Council and verified by the Green Building Certification Institute (GBCI).  Heritage is only the second K12 elementary school in the entire State of Florida to receive this distinction. Just a few of the school improvements completed during the certification process include installation of water-savings fixtures, new irrigation system controls, air-conditioning system upgrades, and more effective interior & exterior lighting sensors. 

 

This certification project was a three-year effort that brought together a diverse group of District experts in the environmental, construction, and maintenance departments with support from a talented consulting team and the leadership of Principals Seth Moldovan and Nina Lant. School staff including the "Green Bees" environmental club under the supervision of club sponsor, Sylvia Cerease played an integral role throughout the entire certification process.

DESCRIPTION:

State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) 6.1(7) (c) requires that, "Prior to April 1 of each year, each district shall review the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) and shall certify to the Office of Educational Facilities that the inventory is current and accurate."

 

The Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) is available to all school districts for reporting facilities information. District FISH reports are available on-line for all district staff who has access to the electronic data base.

 

The facilities inventory may be corrected by submitting on-line transactions through the Educational Facilities Information System (EFIS). A district's facilities inventory may be corrected at any time when new additions or remodeling occurs, during a validation study conducted by the district or the Office of Educational (OEF), or as the result of an approved educational plant survey.  New construction may be added to the inventory when a construction contract is issued.  Large areas that are scheduled to undergo major remodeling should be updated in the inventory as soon as a contract agreement has been made.  If a major remodeling project is conducted by district staff, the inventory should be updated when the project is substantially completed.  Each district should conduct at least an annual update of FISH.

 

Correct facilities data is essential in the District's and State's analyses for reporting necessities such as class size reduction, determination of PECO maintenance requirements which results in legislative funding.  FISH errors can significantly influence funds the District receives and distort analyses made from FISH data.

By Board Action on July 17, 2013 approval was given for the purchase of Purchasing Card Services from FIA Card Services, N. A. a Bank of America Company to operate the District P-Card program utilizing the State of Florida bid awarded contract in Fiscal Year 2013-2014.

 

The District is now implementing e-payables services using the same contract from the State of Florida.  E-Payables is a service that allows the School District to pay vendors via a credit card, rather than a check. This service will speed the payment process for vendors, eliminate the need to print and mail checks, and result in an increase in the annual rebate received from the program. The estimated rebate for the first year should be $110,000 and is expected to grow over time.

 

Since vendors who sign up for this program will be paid by Bank of America, the spend authorized for the contract needs to be increased by up to $10,000,000 through the end of June 2014.  

 

The State of Florida contract continues through January 6, 2015 and offers 110-160 basis points of revenue or 1.1-1.6% of our District wide spends from use of the e-Payables.
