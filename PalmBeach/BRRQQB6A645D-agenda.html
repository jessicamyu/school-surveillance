DESCRIPTION:

This agreement is necessary for Grove Park Elementary School to be authorized as an IB World School by International Baccalaureate (IB) Organization. Signatures confirm agreement that Grove Park Elementary School will comply with the Rules that are in force at the time of signatures of acceptance. Rules refer to the most recent documents and regulations that govern the implementation of the Primary Years Programme.

  • Programme Standards and Practices-- The standards are programme specific general requisites for Philosophy, Organization, and Curriculum established for schools to implement IB programmes. Practices are further definitions of the standards. These are the measures used to evaluate schools to ensure compliance.
     
  • Primary Years Programme: From principles into practice-This is a teacher support guide that emphasizes the central principle of student agency and threads it through the three pillars of the curriculum: the learner, learning and teaching, and the learning community.
     
  • Rules for IB World Schools: Primary Years Programme—Describes compliance rules and regulations, including payment of all applicable IB fees within published timelines.
     
  • Rules and policy for use of IB intellectual property (published at https://www.ibo.org/copyright), including “Trademarks and Logos” section).

The signatures of the Head of School, Superintendent of Schools, and authorized representative of the school’s governing body (School Board Chairman) acknowledge that the School District supports Grove Park Elementary School’s full compliance with the Rules for IB World Schools.

Recommended Action
I recommend the Board approve a Resolution authorizing the issuance of the 2020 Tax Anticipation Note, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chair to approve all necessary documents.

DESCRIPTION:

  • The resolution authorizes the issuance of the 2020 Tax Anticipation Note.
     
  • The Tax Anticipation Note, or TAN, is a short term financing instrument used to alleviate cash shortfalls in the general fund each year.  Current estimates show that additional cash will be needed in November 2020.
     
  • Local property taxes provide the majority of the revenues for the School District.  As the first receipt of tax revenues is expected in late November, it is necessary to borrow money for cash flow purposes until the tax revenues are received.
     
  • Based on projected cash needs, the District plans to issue at $115 million TAN this year.  It will be structured for the same amount as the 2019 TAN but will be scheduled to mature in January rather than August.  Normally, when interest rates are higher, interest earnings can be used to offset the additional interest expense.  However, that isn't possible in the current low interest rate environment so the note will mature earlier than last year to reduce interest expense.
     
  • The sale is tentatively planned for September 22, 2020.  The transaction should close on or about October 6, 2020.Unfortunately, due to COVID-19, the mock sale with student participation will not take place this year.  We plan to continue that tradition in 2021.
     
  • The Finance Committee will review the proposed tax anticipation note during their August 13, 2020 meeting.  A written report will be provided to the School Board at the August 19, 2020 School Board meeting.

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

The proceeds of the TAN will be used to meet the cash flow needs of the general fund.  Interest expense is projected at $70,000 and costs of issuance are not to exceed $115,900.  Interest earnings will be used to help offset the cost of the transaction.

Annual Comprehensive Safety Inspections were performed by the Building Code Services Fire and Safety Inspectors as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(1). All educational, auxiliary and ancillary facilities were included in these inspections, using the State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) for casualty and sanitation, and Florida Administrative Code Chapter 69A-58 for fire safety.

 

The number of citations for Public Schools, Alternative Schools and Ancillary Facilities rose from 1,322 in 2019 to 1,848 in 2020. This represents a 40% increase over one year.

 

The greatest increases were related to Operational citations. Of the 526 additional citations between FY19 and FY20, 488 were Operational (regular and serious). This increase is most likely attributed to schools not having the opportunity to correct Operational citations after the COVID-19 shutdown in March. The number of Serious Capital citations went down and the number of Serious Maintenance citation remained the same. All conditions considered by the inspector to be hazardous or posing an imminent danger were reported to the Maintenance and Plant Operations Department at the time of discovery for immediate action.

 

A summary report by facility, detailed individual facility reports and descriptive charts are attached. According to SREF, Chapter 5, the inspection reports shall be approved by the Board, and the Board shall adopt a plan and a schedule for the correction of each deficiency.

 

This year’s report was affected by COVID-19 as the District elected to extend the inspection period by adding six weeks to the normal inspection schedule. The State Fire Marshal and the Department of Education granted such flexibility because school and maintenance staff were disrupted from normal operations.

 

Upon the Board approving the inspection reports, copies of the reports will be submitted to the local fire protection agencies as required by Florida Statute 1013.12(2). The Board hereby certifies the completion of the inspection report to the State Fire Marshal.
