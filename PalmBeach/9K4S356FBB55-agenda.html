Ø      This Agreement will enable the School Board and the County to continue to utilize each other’s recreational facilities and provide a procedure for the approved County-affiliated recreation groups and organizations to follow in order to use the School Board’s recreational facilities.



 



Ø      The existing Interlocal is scheduled to expire on December 3, 2008, so approval of this new agreement is required to permit both parties to continue using the facilities of the other without interruption.



 



Ø      Each County User Group is required to obtain a Facility Use Permit from the County.



 



Ø      The term is for one year with an automatic renewal of up to four (4) additional consecutive one year terms.



 



Ø      With respect to the use of the Board Facilities, Board activities and programs will have first priority followed by Municipal activities and programs under a separate Interlocal Agreement that pre-dates this Agreement or  under a separate Interlocal Agreement with the School Board involving Board Facilities that have been constructed or improved with funds exceeding $100,000 contributed by the municipality or on their behalf; next would include County or County User Group activities or programs and finally Municipal activities and programs included in a separate Interlocal Agreement entered into by the Board after the effective date of the Agreement.



 



  Ø      Each party agrees to provide adequate supervision of its own activities.



 



  Ø      Each party shall reimburse the other party for the actual costs to repair any damage that occurred to the facilities being used within 60 days of a written request.



 



  Ø      County may charge a fee for use of the County’s water parks, picnic pavilions on weekends, County’s swimming pools or golf courses for summer programs and docents, instructors or tour guides in County museums or nature centers.



 



  Ø      The Board is not obligated to make the Board Facilities available for use by a County User Group for tournaments or any events where admission or concession fees or charges will be collected or imposed.



 



  Ø      Use of school facilities during weekdays will begin at the close of school until 9:30pm, Saturday 8:00am – 9:30pm, and Sunday 8:00am – 1:00pm.  When school is not in session facilities will be closed Friday through Sunday.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend the approval of the Interlocal Agreement between Palm Beach County and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the Mutual Use of Recreational Facilities; and authorize the Chairman, Superintendent and Chief Operating Officer to sign all necessary documents.

P1 Banking Services Contract
Meeting
Nov 12, 2008 - Wkshps and Spec Mtgs re Operations
Category
New Business
Type
Action (Consent)


  • The current banking services contracts expires on November 22, 2008.

  • Due to the unusual conditions in the banking industry, it is recommended that the District negotiate a contract with our current providers for one year with an option to renew for one additional year if needed.

  • The District's current banks, as well as other competing banks, have agreed it is in the best interest of the District to extend the current banking contracts.  The District's Investment Advisor has also agreed that an RFP would not be an effective tool in this financial environment.

  • The Finance Committee approved this recommendation with a unanimous vote at their meeting on October 17, 2008.

  • Once the financial markets have stabilized, an RFP will be issued to ensure the District continues to receive the required services at the most competitive prices possible.

  • There are two portions of the banking services contracts.



    • 1. The general banking services (checks, deposits, etc) are handled by Bank of America.  Lee County Schools issued an RFP and received responses in April 2008.  Bank of America has agreed to honor all the terms of our existing contract and reduce pricing when necessary to match the prices offered to Lee County Schools.  This will reduce the annual fees for general banking services by approximately $19,000 per year.

    • 2. The custody services (holding the District's investments) were originally to First Union/Wachovia Bank.  Wachovia subsequently sold that portion of their business to US Bank.  US Bank has agreed to honor the original terms of the contract as awarded in 2002.


  • The contract with Bank of America also includes reduced pricing for our schools' internal accounts.  Additionally, the contract includes special programs available to all District employees such as free or discounted banking, teacher loan programs and special mortgage programs.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Banking Contracts for General Banking Services with Bank of America and Custody Services with US Bank.

Contact:

Joseph M. Moore (moorej@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)



Leanne Evans (evansl@palmbeach.k12.fl.us)

Financial Impact:

The estimated cost of general banking services is $105,000 per year. This will be funded from the Treasury and School Food Service Department Budgets.



The estimated cost of custody services is $30,000 per year.  This will be funded from the Treasury Department Budget.
