Haitian Heritage Month
Meeting
May 19, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action

May is Haitian Heritage Month and students, schools, and the Haitian community will be celebrating with a number of exciting educational and cultural programs and events. A student essay contest was organized by the School District Haitian Heritage Month Committee and sponsored by the Wayne Barton Study Center, a community organization led by Wayne Barton, philanthropist, whose mission is to enhance the health, welfare and education of children in need in our community. Participating high school students wrote an essay on the following topic: "How can you play a concrete role in the reconstruction of Haiti after the January 12, 2010 earthquake that devastated the country?"



The goal of the Haitian Heritage Month Celebration is to promote multicultural awareness, understanding and acceptance of diversity in our society in order to better educate our student population and strengthen their self-esteem. This will contribute to improve their academic achievement and to recognize the valuable contributions made by Haitians to the history of America and the world.



Many other activities will take place county wide to celebrate Haitian Heritage Month which is a commemoration of Haitian pride everywhere in the United States where the Haitian population is significant.  Haitian and Haitian American students represent the largest ethnic minority group from a single country in the School District of Palm Beach County with more than 14,000 students. According to community activists, Palm Beach County is home to more than 90,000 Haitians.

Jeff Fessler Palm Beach County Teacher of The Year
Meeting
May 19, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action

Jeff Fessler, a teacher at U. B. Kinsey/Palmview Elementary School of the Arts, is the Palm Beach County Teacher of the Year.  Mr. Fessler teaches reading, social studies and writing and is the grade 4 team leader.  A resident of West Palm Beach, he has fourteen years of experience as an educator, six of which are at U. B. Kinsey/Palmview Elementary School of the Arts.  Mr. Fessler holds National Board Certification and he is highly sought after as a workshop facilitator.  He was selected as a presenter for the National Art Educators Association Annual Conference held in New York City in 2007.  For his powerful and innovative instruction, Mr. Fessler received the Humane Society of the United States Teacher of the Year Award in 2008 and the same year was awarded the local William Dwyer Award for Excellence in Elementary Education.  In 2009, Mr. Fessler was selected as the Palm Beach County Elementary Social Studies Teacher of the Year.  Recently, the Palm Beach County Cultural Council presented the 2010 Muse Award for Arts Educator of the Year to Jeff Fessler. 



Mr. Fessler’s passion for teaching is demonstrated by the innovative ways that he motivates and involves his students.  He provides opportunities for his students to participate in projects in collaboration with local arts organizations such as the Educational Gallery Group, the Flagler Museum, and the Blue Planet Writer’s Room.  Many of his projects teach the students the value of community service, as demonstrated by the PSAs developed for the local animal shelter and the Humane Society of the United States.



Mr. Fessler was honored on May 6 at the school district Employees of the Year Breakfast.  He was awarded $1000.00 from the School District of Palm Beach County.  Funding for the school district award is provided by local business partners. As a District Teacher of the Year, Jeff Fessler will also receive a cash award of $1000.00 from Macy’s (of which $250 will go to U. B. Kinsey/Palmview Elementary School of the Arts). 



In July, Mr. Fessler will be invited to participate in the Florida Department of Education-Macy’s Teacher of the Year gala awards celebration.  This event is held in conjunction with the Florida Department of Education’s Teacher of the Year Roundtable.  The Roundtable affords Teachers of the Year the opportunity to network with colleagues, share ideas, and provide input on educational issues of state and national importance.

John I. Leonard High School’s Finance Academy and Palm Beach Gardens Community High School’s Sports Management and Recreation Academy had their programs reviewed to evaluate how they rate on the National Standards of Practice for career academies.  A team from the National Career Academy Coalition and the Association of Career and Technical Education assessed the academies using the career academy national standards of practice.  These standards were developed by a consortium of persons actively engaged with career academies to define what components a standard career academy should have for success.  The academies received overall ranking of:  Certified Academy.



Olympic Heights High School, Palm Beach Central High School and Jupiter High School had their Project Lead The Way Engineering Academy reviewed. A Project Lead The Way representative evaluated them to ascertain if the programs are effective instructionally and as a mechanism to assure that the students are prepared for college and their respective careers.  All three academies received the honor of being named Project Lead The Way Full National Certified Academy.

CLS1 - Agreement Between SBPBC and Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University
Meeting
May 19, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Action (Consent)

This Agreement allows the District to continue the Boynton Aerospace Science Academy (BASA) at Boynton Beach High School.  Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (ERAU) will employ ERAU faculty to teach ERAU courses at Boynton Beach High School in the BASA Academy.



The objective of BASA is to provide the selected high school students with collegiate and professional trade-level study with a focus on professional aeronautical topics leading to both high school and college credits for courses completed.



BASA courses taught by ERAU faculty will be transferable to appropriate ERAU academic programs, in accordance with the guidelines in force in the ERAU Undergraduate Catalog at the time the individual students matriculate into the ERAU system.  All individuals teaching BASA courses shall be qualified to teach under the criteria established by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS) and the State of Florida.  ERAU and the District will give first and serious preference to allowing qualified individuals employed already by the District to teach BASA courses.



ERAU offers two curriculum tracks at BASA.  The first, an Academic Track, will be a curriculum of introductory courses in a range of aviation career areas, including flight operations, weather, air traffic management, safety, business, unmanned aviation and others depending on student demand and the availability of qualified faculty.  The second track is Aviator Maintenance Science (AMS) and this track will provide students with the General portion of the FAA Airframe and Powerplant Mechanics Certificate curriculum.

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the Agreement with Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (ERAU) effective July 1, 2010 through June 30, 2015 in order to continue the Boynton Aerospace Science Academy (BASA) at Boynton Beach High School and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to this agreement.

This is the annual contract agreement with the Arc of Palm Beach County that supports the provision of services specified in Section 1003.54, F.S. Teenage Parent Programs, to provide Teenage Parent Childcare Services.



The Arc of Palm Beach County will provide a high quality, developmentally appropriate infant/toddler childcare program at Gold Coast Community School.  The services will be provided in an existing portable located on the school's campus, with a total number of slots for sixteen (16) children.  These children are dependents of students who are enrolled in the Teenage Parent Program and attend the above mentioned Alternative Education Schools for at-risk students.

The School District will evaluate the program by assessing the weekly attendance of the children served in the Arc infant/toddler program, the results of the infants/toddlers Ages and Stages Assessment tool and the quality of the program by using the Environmental Rating Scale Assessment tool.

P1 Certified Athletic Trainers - Standard Contract
Meeting
May 19, 2010 - School Board Meeting
Category
New Business
Type

P1 Approve the Certified Athletic Trainers for the contract period August 1, 2010 through July 31, 2011.



Individual high schools through the Athletic Director and/or Principal will select an Athletic Trainer for their school to cover sports (boys and girls).



Athletic Trainers must be certified by the National Athletic Trainers Association (N.A.T.A.) and licensed in Florida.



The District may compensate each Athletic Trainer up to $22,500 ($15,000 for those with fewer sports) per high school based upon 1,000 hours of service of which a minimum of 250 hours will be allocated to each of the three sports season (fall, winter and spring); schools having fewer sports and teams will compensate the trainers based upon the hours needed.



The Athletic Trainers will work with the athletes and coaches based upon individual needs at the school.  Practice, game and event coverage will be coordinated and schedules agreed upon by both parties. Special attention will be given to those sports with the most potential for injuries (football, soccer, basketball and wrestling) but not to the exclusion of any other sport.



Professional liability and comprehensive general liability insurance are required from all athletic trainers/providers as indicated in the contract.



The contract period is August 1, 2010 through July 31, 2011



 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the standard contract for Certified Athletic Trainers for the contract period August 1, 2010 through July 31, 2011 in the not-to-exceed amount of $517,500.
