American Association of Caregiving Youth
Meeting
Jul 20, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Presentations
Type
Action

In response to the impact of children caring for a family member who is ill, injured, elderly or disabled, the American Association of Caregiving Youth (AACY) established the first US Caregiving Youth Project (CYP) in partnership with The School District of Palm Beach County. The CYP provides professional services in school, out of school and at home to create a myriad of needs-driven wraparound services with collaborating partners. Its evaluation-based model addresses caregiving ramifications including isolation and other stressors which hinder the child's psychosocial, emotional, developmental, physical health, education opportunities and well-being. The CYP offers in-school counseling/skills building, Lunch and Learn sessions and an educational after school club. Home assessments by a social worker validate the caregiving status, link families to respite and resources, and evaluate the need for in-home academic support. Out of school sponsored activities include a camp, cooking class, wellness days, computer classes, fishing trip and more. As students progress to high school CYP focuses on preparing youth for graduation, post secondary education and the steps to take to achieve future goals.



When child caregivers are recognized and supported their lives change! They learn they are not alone. They develop the skills they need to manage their responsibilities. Success has been measured by student-caregivers improving their grades and remaining in school.  Youth report increased skills and confidence, less isolation and better family and peer relationships. They and their families handle anger and manage stress.
 
Through its work, AACY is reaching more individuals and families, raising awareness about caregiving youth issues and seeing results as evidenced by external evaluator analyses and reports. There is compelling momentum to assist students and their families who are unaffiliated with CYP serviced schools as well as to develop a Florida network. Demonstrated success warrants expansion; children affected by caregiving reside throughout Florida and the US.



The CYP began in 2006 at Boca Middle School and now serves more than 400 caregiving youth from eight middle and 17 high schools. AACY is the national expert in child caregiving. It is working to educate legislators to change policy to include family caregivers of all ages in public funding support. It is expanding its Board to include members with national influence and corporate support. It is participating in research to further document and to publish the effects of support in the retention of students and the prevention of negative ramifications of caregiving on their health and in their lives.

This is the annual cooperative agreement regarding the High Ridge Family Center. The program will serve up to 72 disciplinary and truant male and female youths and the students will receive instruction at the Sabal Palm facility. The criteria for placement are determined by the High Ridge Family Center through an application process that is open to all Palm Beach County students between the ages of 11-15. The goal of the program is for students to develop the social skills, academic knowledge, and vocational skills necessary to ensure a successful transition to their home school. Length of the program is approximately four (4) months and is residential. Students are evaluated through the SRI reading, STAR math and FCAT tests and High Ridge provides intensive counseling and behavior management for students and families with a continuation of three (3) months counseling and family services while attending their home school. The school district is responsible for the instructional staff, custodian, and materials needed to provide the educational program. Although backdating agreements is not our standard practice, it was necessary in this particular case due to contract negotiations. It does not prejudice the rights of the School Board.

Recommendation:

  1. On January 18, 1995, the School Board approved an Interlocal Agreement (ILA) between the City of West Palm Beach and School Board of Palm Beach County for the Development and Use of the Roosevelt Replacement Middle School.  (Exhibit "A" 1995 ILA)


  2. Section 3 of the 1995 ILA required the City to grant a permanent easement of over 1.8 acres of City-owned property located within Gaines Park for Roosevelt Middle School's drainage detention. 


  3. While working on an exchange Agreement with the City for the Westward Elementary modernization and the transfer of title of the Urban League property adjacent to Roosevelt Middle School, current School District staff discovered the exchange of property interests with the City as contemplated in the 1995 ILA did not occur.


  4. The transactions for the Westward Elementary Modernization/Urban League/Roosevelt Middle School project were handled in subparts, which require certain prerequisites to occur before the project can be finalized.  Amending this ILA is one of the last perquisites needed to finalize the Exchange Agreement with the City.


  5. The 1995 ILA's Grant of Permanent Easement instrument (Exhibit "E") has been deleted and replaced with a new Exhibit "E" that requires the City's prior approval to any change in the use of the Roosevelt Middle School parcel.


  6. During the time the 1995 ILA was negotiated, the City was in the process of working on the Golf and Carver Avenue Stormwater drainage project.  Section 5 of the 1995 ILA required the School District convey a portion of the Roosevelt Middle School campus to allow for the construction of a generator building.  The Stormwater project has since been completed and the City no longer needs a portion of the Roosevelt Middle School campus, therefore the First Amendment provides for the deletion of Section 5.


  7. It was discovered that sometime after the 1995 ILA was approved, the City had re-routed the entrance road to Gaines Park in a fashion that encroached into the drainage easement parcel so it became necessary to revise the legal description of the drainage detention area, which is reflected as Exhibit "D" in the Agreement.


  8. The First Amendment provides for the deletion of the original Exhibit "D" and replaces it with the revised Exhibit "D".


  9. Section 4 of the 1995 ILA required the abandonment of a portion of the 15th street right-of-way by the City.


  10. The First Amendment also provides that the School Board shall convey a Drainage and Utility Easement in favor of the City over the abandoned portion of the right-of-way, which is reflected in Exhibit "1".

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the First Amendment to the Interlocal Agreement between the City of West Palm Beach and the School Board of Palm Beach County for the Development and Use of the Roosevelt Replacement Middle School; and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to sign all necessary documents.

G1 - Adults with Disabilities Entitlement Grant
Meeting
Jul 20, 2011 - School Board Meeting
Category
Consent
Type
Discussion

The Adults with Disabilities (AWD) Grant is a continuation grant in the amount of  $894,684.  South Tech Academy, through the School District of Palm Beach County as Local Education Agency (LEA), has historically been the source of continued support for this grant.  The school district's Department of Adult and Community Education serves as the coordinating department and works with the proper district departments to obtain School Board approval.



The AWD program has been implemented through South Tech Academy since 1983.  This program is intended to improve the quality of life of adults with disabilities.  The program uses a realistic work environment  with skilled and certified teachers who provide the training and support needed to make the student successful.  The AWD program is designed to make each student as self-sufficient as possible and for those that qualify, to become employable.



The AWD program combines job skill training with special education instructional methodology which enables the students to gain both employability skills and real world job skills.  The goal is self-sufficiency for the disabled by providing training and income.  The funding is used to train and enhance the skills and lives of approximately 600 students.  Each student's Adult Individual Education Plan (AIEP) is closely monitored to assure that AIEP goals are being met as well as tracking the number of students who obtain competitive employment and non-competitive employment.  Competitive employment is where students interview for job positions and non-competitive employment is where students do not interview for job positions and are placed in jobs according to their abilities.



The target population focuses on adult individuals who were born with a disability, acquired a disability later in life, and individuals who are 55 years of age or older and have disabilities, who are referred to as senior citizens with disabilities.



There are five liaison agencies (ARC, Habilitation Center, Jeff Industries, Palm Beach Habilitation Center, Seagull Industries) who partner to provide training.  Each agency promotes the philosophy of challenging students to attain the greatest degree of self-sufficiency in both their work skills and personal life skills.  This funding enables students that are exceptional to become employable in the community.  



The Adults with Disabilities Entitlement Grant will be effective retroactive to July 1, 2011. Although it is not our practice to back-date grants, doing so in this case does not prejudice the rights of the Board. The reason this item is coming to the Board at this time is because the Request for Application for this grant became available in late May. The submission deadline dates for the June Board meeting had already occurred.  This grant is due in Tallahassee by August 2, 2011.   

Recommendation:

I recommend that the School Board approve the continuation of the Adults with Disabilities Entitlement Grant in the amount of $894,684 effective July 1, 2011 to June 30, 2012 and authorize the Superintendent or designee to sign all documents and contracts related to the grant which are required for implementation of grant activities.

Background



The School Board holds seven (7) Educational Broadband Service (EBS) Licenses issued by the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) that permit the Board to broadcast various programming on certain frequencies within defined areas of Palm Beach County.  A portion of this licensed EBS spectrum is used by The Education Network (TEN) to transmit TEN's educational programming to our schools and other receivers such as Comcast Cable.  Another portion of the licensed EBS spectrum is suitable for use in two-way wireless broadband cellular communication services such as WiMax and Long Term Evolution (LTE),  a standard in mobile network technology, and is presently unused by the School Board. 



The South Florida Water Management District (SFWMD) owns a communications tower in the southeast corner of the Corbett Wildlife area.  SFWMD has requested that the School Board lease one channel of the licensed EBS Spectrum to SFWMD so that SFWMD can do a small water quality/quantity data collection and internet service pilot project using WiMax technology in this otherwise uninhabited area.  The information that SFWMD learns from this pilot may be helpful to the School Board as it evaluates how it wishes to utilize its other EBS spectrum in the future.  As a participant in the Palm Beach Broadband Consortium, we are pleased to be able to partner with SFWMD to pilot the use of WiMax Technology for its potential use in our District.



The essential elements of the EBS Lease are:





  • Three year term;

  • No rent due;

  • The Lease covers the Channel A-1 spectrum frequencies as they currently exist under the "old" FCC bandplan.  Upon the completion of the bandplan transition, the frequencies will automatically be converted to Channel A-1 frequencies of the "new" FCC bandplan;

  • The Lease may be cancelled in the event of default or for convenience with thirty (30) days notice;

  • SFWMD covenants to comply with all applicable rules and regulations governing use of EBS spectrum;

  • The geographic area covered by the Lease is limited to a 0.5 mile radius circle surrounding SFWMD's tower.


Staff believes that the limited geographic area covered by this Lease will not cause a diminution in the value of the spectrum in the event that the School Board decides that it wishes to lease the spectrum to a commercial wireless broadband service provider.  However, in the event that it does have an unacceptable impact on the value, the School Board could always elect to terminate the Lease for convenience.
The Lease was prepared and negotiated by staff counsel and the School Board's FCC counsel has reviewed it on a limited basis to ensure that all current FCC required provisions have been included in the Lease.

Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Educational Broadband Service Long Term De Facto Transfer Lease Agreement with the South Florida Water Management District and authorize the Chairman and Superintendent to execute all necessary documents.

Approval of the final of two additional one year renewal periods regarding the Contract for Structural Engineering Services on a Continuing Contract basis.  This contract is used to furnish planning, design, engineering, and consulting services in connection with various buildings and projects owned or operated by the School Board of Palm Beach County. Services include inspection services, engineering recommendations, individual building inspections to determine areas and reasons for requested modifications or problem solving, design, construction administration and all other necessary services to assist staff as requested. The renewal period will be from August 20, 2011 through August 19, 2012.



 



M/WBE Participation:



As specified by Board Policy 6.143(2)(c) The School District of Palm Beach County shall take all necessary, reasonable and legal actions to prevent discrimination and to ensure that all businesses, including M/WBEs, are afforded the maximum equitable opportunity to participate in the District’s procurement process for Projects that result from this Agreement.   



 



Based on the Disparity Analysis the Office of Diversity established an Evaluation Preference for District Certified M/WBE prime firms responding to the Request for Proposals (RFP). 



 



Johnson Structural Group, Inc. (American Female)



O’Donnell, Naccarato, Mignogna & Jackson, Inc.



R.C.T. Engineering, Inc.  



TLC Engineering for Architecture, Inc.



Exhibit 8 of the RFP establishes the maximum Hourly Rates which have been reviewed and approved to be consistent with the local area market conditions by Project Controls.



 

Recommendation:

I recommend approval of the Second Amendment for Continuing Contract for Structural Engineering Services to Johnson Structural Group, Inc., O’Donnell, Naccarato, Mignogna & Jackson, Inc., R.C.T. Engineering, Inc., and TLC Engineering for Architecture, Inc. and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all required documents.

Contact:

Michael J. Burke (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)



Joseph M. Sanches  (joseph.sanches@palmbeachschools.org)

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is not to exceed $25,000 for the renewal period.  The source of funds is the corresponding Capital Projects Budget.

Debris Cleanup Services for Disaster Recovery Assistance are required to assist with clean up of debris during disaster response. Awarded firms will provide cleanup services on an as-needed basis. The term of the contract will be from July 21, 2011 through July 20, 2014, with the option to renew for two (2) additional one (1) year periods. Each firm may be renewed at the sole discretion of the School Board. 



 



Small Business Enterprise (SBE) Participation: 



As specified by Board Policy 6.143(2)(c) The School District of Palm Beach County shall take all necessary, reasonable and legal actions to prevent discrimination and to ensure that all businesses, including SBE's, are afforded the maximum equitable opportunity to participate in teh District's procurement process for this Project.  



 



Based on the Disparity Analysis the Office of Diversity established an Evaluation Preference for District Certified SBE prime firms responding to the Request for Proposal (RFP). 



 



Seven (7) firms responded to the RFP. The top (5) ranked contractors by the Selection Committee are being recommended for award: 



 



(1) Ceres Environmental Services, Inc.    



(2) DRC Emergency Services, LLC 



(3) Artem, Inc. 



(4) C.S. Bensch, Inc (SBE)   



(5) The BG Group, LLC (SBE) 



Recommendation:

I recommend the School Board approve the Owner/Contractor Agreements with Artem, Inc., The BG Group, LLC, C.S. Bensch, Inc, Ceres Environmental Services, Inc. and DRC Emergency Services, LLC, and authorize the Superintendent and Board Chairman to finalize and sign all required documents for Debris Cleanup Services for Disaster Recovery Assistance.

Contact:

Michael J. Burke (mike.burke@palmbeachschools.org)



Joseph M. Sanches  (joseph.sanches@palmbeachschools.org)



 

Financial Impact:

The financial impact is from various funds in an amount not to exceed $1,000,000. per year. The source of funds is Hurricane and Project funds. In addition, should we have an emergency need arise funds used in excess of this amount will be reported to the Board in accordance with Policy 6.14.
