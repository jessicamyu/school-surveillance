RESOLUTION
HONORING
JACK D. DALE

 

     WHEREAS, Jack D. Dale was appointed Superintendent of Fairfax County Public Schools on July 1, 2004, and served with distinction for nine years; and

 

     WHEREAS, he worked diligently with the School Board to develop the beliefs, vision, mission, and student achievement goals as the Strategic Governance model was developed so that together we are “Building the future….child by child”; and

 

     WHEREAS, he led Fairfax County Public Schools through the Great Recession, and despite declining resources, focused system resources to its students and classrooms, which ultimately resulted in continuing improvements in student achievement, narrowing of achievement gaps, and expanding full-day kindergarten to all schools; and

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Dale’s belief in teacher leadership led to a program that empowered teachers to assume positions of leadership in their schools, and pyramids to build Professional Learning Communities and create successful Collaborative Learning Teams; and

 

     WHEREAS, he worked closely with the business community and the Fairfax County Chamber of Commerce to establish the Education Foundation for Fairfax County Public Schools to bring the resources of our community to targeted needs and programs in our schools; and  

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Dale was a key leader in promoting twenty-first century skills or the 4Cs: Critical Thinking, Communication, Collaboration and Creativity, and encouraging international learning experiences for both students and teachers;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its sincerest commendation and deepest appreciation to Jack D. Dale for his visionary leadership, his commitment to public education in Fairfax, the United States, and across the globe, and his unwavering dedication to the staff, students, and families in Fairfax County Public Schools; and

 

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board, on behalf of the entire Fairfax community, extends its very best wishes to Dr. Dale and his family as they embark on their new adventure.

Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. three (3) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. Meeting Category:    July 11, 2013 – Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                    FY 2013 Final Budget Review    

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                                                    

 

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2013 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2013 Third Quarter Estimate. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the FY 2014 budgeted beginning balance as included in the FY 2014 Approved Budget, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the funds available are $55.7 million.

 

FY 2013 actual revenue is $7.8 million lower than the estimate and totaled 99.7 percent of the FY 2013 Third Quarter Estimate.  State aid for FY 2013 totaled $383.1 million which is $0.9 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts are projected to total $164.4 million which is $1.9 million more than budgeted.  A decrease of $11.6 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $12.3 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2014, offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of a one-time final payment for the Education Jobs Bill of $0.4 million and a one-time final payment of $0.5 million from FEMA for February 2010 storms.  Based on estimated final year-end activity, tuition from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $1.1 million, while revenue from tuition, fees, and other was $3.8 million more than budgeted.

 

Expenditures were $51.1 million less than projected after funding the School Board’s Flexibility Reserve and accounting for the textbook replacement fund and other commitments and carryover, excluding grants. A majority of this variance results from compensation accounts being $34.6 million less than budgeted due primarily to higher compensation lapse than projected; utilities expenditures being $5.2 million less than projected due to a temperate winter, spring, and early summer; county charges for vehicle services, including fuel, being $1.9 million less than projected; and a reduction in school accounts due to limiting carryover to 25 percent of the current budget.  In addition, $12.3 million in multiyear federal grant funds were unexpended.  This funding will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2014. 

 

This agenda item also includes recommendations for $11.1 million in FY 2014 expenditure adjustments leaving $44.6 million available for the FY 2015 budgeted beginning balance.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting
Jul 11, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 1
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:          Dan Parris, Cluster 3 Assistant Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  July 11, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 01

 

Subject:                  Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school. The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

  • Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2014 is $2,552,640. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $3,481,167.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

Options:

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor's Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2013-2014.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
