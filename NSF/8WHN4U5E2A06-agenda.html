RESOLUTION

HONORING

DEAN TISTADT

 

 

     WHEREAS, Dean Tistadt has served Fairfax County Public Schools for 24 years, from 1988 until his retirement in December 2012, with excellence and perseverance, as the director of supply operations, the assistant superintendent for the Department of General Services, and for the past 10 years as the chief operating officer and assistant superintendent for the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services; and

 

     WHEREAS, during his tenure as chief operating officer, Mr. Tistadt has embraced the commitment to environmental stewardship and has made it a department core value, incorporating this value in numerous initiatives, such as lighting replacement, building    design, education specifications, and mini-grants for schools; he has overseen capital construction expenditures of more than $1.5 billion, including the renovation of 36 school facilities – 22 elementary, 5 middle, and 9 high schools for a total addition of 6,400,594 square feet; the construction of 11 new schools – 8 elementary, 2 middle, and 1 high school for a total addition of 1,543,769 square feet; the installation of 21 modular additions, the construction of 14 capacity enhancement additions to existing buildings, and the purchase and build-out of a central administration center, and the subsequent relocation of several hundred staff; and 

 

     WHEREAS, he has supported innovations in safety, security, and emergency management while overseeing numerous significant events, including September 11, 2001, the sniper incidents of 2002, and tropical storms, floods, widespread power outages, and numerous snow events; Mr. Tistadt has also developed the concept of the school liaison commander and Just-In-Time training videos for significant events to ensure that school administrators have timely and effective information; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mr. Tistadt has been dedicated to high quality customer service, meeting with representatives from principal associations and school communities several times a year and utilizing surveys and other tools to capture data on customer satisfaction in an ongoing effort to make improvements; and

 

     WHEREAS, his innovative perspective and commitment to professional development have helped establish a successful departmental leadership cohort, which the division has used as its model for the current FCPS Leadership Development Cohort; Mr. Tistadt also established the Facilities Management Cohort program in partnership with George Mason University, which has graduated 51 FCPS staff members to date;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Mr. Dean Tistadt for his dedication to public education, his passionate and tireless commitment to solving the critical problems that impede progress, his staunch support of students, teachers, administrators, and the citizens of Fairfax County, and his outstanding service to Fairfax County Public Schools.

Staff Contact:            Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     December 20, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 10

 

Subject:                     FY 2013 Midyear Budget Review

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points: 

 

In the School Operating Fund, the FY 2013 beginning balance increased $2.7 million due to final year-end and audit adjustments made after the FY 2012 Final Budget Review, including an increase of $1.8 million in sales tax revenue based on actual sales tax receipts and other revenue decreases totaling $0.3 million offset by expenditure decreases of $1.2 million. 

 

In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2013 revenue adjustments total $2.9 million. This is a result of revenue from the City of Fairfax increasing by $1.8 million based on the final tuition bill for FY 2012 reflecting actual expenditures and an increase in grant revenue totaling $1.1 million. 

 

Recommended expenditure and transfer adjustments reflect a net decrease of $4.1 million resulting primarily from: a reduction of $2.9 million in compensation accounts due to the average salaries of new hires for this school year being less than budgeted; a net increase of $1.1 million in grant expenditures, including positions, primarily from an increase in the federal award for the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act; savings of $1.0 million as a result of implementing benefits for parent liaisons and multilingual interpreters effective calendar year 2013; savings of $0.8 million from a change in the mandated timeframe for bus inspections; and a reduction in the transfer for extended school year for special education of $0.5 million. 

 

Position authorization is required for: 2.0 executive administrative assistants added to the School Board Office which were funded utilizing a portion of the placeholder approved for additional School Board staffing at the FY 2013 Approved Budget adoption; a 0.4 staff attorney funded through a reduction in legal fees; and a 1.0 building supervisor funded by the County to support School Age Child Care at the former Graham Road school facility.

 

Expenditure and transfer adjustments, combined with revenue adjustments, result in available funds totaling $9.7 million recommended to be set aside to increase the FY 2014 beginning balance from $41.6 million to $51.3 million. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment. 

 

 Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2013 Midyear Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  December 20, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 10

 

Subject:                  FY 2014-2018 Capital Improvement Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2014-2018 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five year enrollment projections and capacity calculations and identifies new construction, capacity enhancement, renovation, and infrastructure requirements.

 

The Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2014-2018 Capital Improvement Program Summary can be found in the Capital Program Recommendations chapter of the Capital Improvement Program booklet.

 

Discussion:

 

The school system is facing a challenging situation in which student enrollment growth is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings.  This will necessitate the need for increasing allocations of capital dollars to build new facilities, capacity enhancement projects and continued reliance on temporary classrooms, modular additions, and probable boundary adjustments.

 

The CIP includes funding for three new elementary schools and a new high school.  The new elementary school needs are identified in the Eastern Fairfax area, Richmond Highway Corridor, and West County area, in the vicinity of Route 28 and Dulles Airport Access Road.  Additional high school capacity need is identified in the South West County area.  In addition, it is anticipated that beyond 2018 an additional new elementary school will be needed to address future growth in the Tyson’s Corner area and in Fairfax/Oakton area.  Funding for an addition at South Lakes High School is also identified.  Allocating funds for new schools will result in delays in for some future renovation projects.  Favorable construction pricing is helping mitigate these delays but they will occur if the school system does not receive additional capital funding from the Board of Supervisors.

 

Staff has briefed this CIP to the Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) which was created to help advise the School Board and staff on facilities related issues.  FPAC was instrumental in working with facilities planning staff to revise the format of this year’s CIP.  The objective of this format revision is to improve communication, understanding and advance notice for school communities about the capital projects and/or possible changes in boundaries/programs which may impact schools.

Background:

 

At their September 10, 2012, work session, the School Board reviewed the current and projected overcrowding at Fairfax High School and its feeder, Lanier Middle School.  Fairfax High School is projected to be approximately 600 students over its capacity by the 2017-18 school year.  At the work session, the School Board acknowledged the June 13, 2012, letter from the City of Fairfax School Board asking the County School Board to commit to a boundary study of Fairfax High School and its feeders so “that the gross over-enrollment projected in the CIP is avoided.”  The Board further understood that since the overcrowding is due to the numbers of County students attending these schools, it would not be reasonable to expect Fairfax City residents to fund additional capital improvements that would benefit County students.  It should be further noted that approximately 65% of the student population for both schools are Fairfax County residents and that no City of Fairfax student would be impacted with a boundary change as a result of the study.

 

At that work session, there was consensus that a boundary study would be the only reasonable solution to solve the overcrowding at Fairfax and Lanier.  The Board accepted the recommended timeline to allow community engagement in fall 2012 for the scope of the boundary study, and the authorization of a boundary study in spring 2013, with implementation in fall 2014.

 

The Board also expressed interest in having a boundary decision sufficiently early in the spring so families impacted by the outcome of the study could have an option to move in fall 2013, before the official implementation in 2014.  An early spring decision would also allow principals of sending and receiving schools to accommodate changes in staff and curriculum in fall 2013 which may be needed for those families opting to send their student(s) to newly assigned schools earlier than the fall 2014 implementation. 

 

 

The School Board responded to the City of Fairfax School Board in a letter dated October 5, 2012, which outlined the School Board commitment to undertake a boundary study as requested and the timeline for the study.  Copies of this correspondence and additional information may be found at the Fairfax/Lanier Boundary Study web page at http://www.fcps.edu/fts/planning/fairfaxlanier/index.shtml

 

Key Points:

 

Unlike other boundary studies which seek to resolve enrollment imbalances, this study would seek to share some of the over-enrollment burden with other adjacent school pyramids.  There is insufficient capacity at surrounding schools to fully address the 600 seat capacity deficit projected for Fairfax High School.  Reducing enrollment by approximately 300 students at Fairfax High and by 150 students at Lanier Middle through a boundary change would offer partial relief to the severe enrollment pressures projected for 2017 and beyond and will be the primary objective for the study.

As staff circulated among the attendees at all of the meetings, several themes resonated:  the concern for grandfathering, the impact of changes to AAP programs currently under review; program differences between Fairfax High and the potential receiving schools and split feeders - elementary to middle and middle to high school. 

  

After review of the comments and community recommendations, staff believes that all the high schools and feeder middle schools (shown in tables above) that have contiguous attendance areas to Fairfax High and Lanier Middle should be included in the scope of study except Centreville High and Liberty Middle.  Staff believes that including all other schools contiguous to Fairfax and Lanier will offer flexibility to develop multiple boundary options/scenarios for consideration to provide overcrowding relief to Fairfax High and Lanier Middle.  More importantly, staff also believes that inclusion of these multiple high schools and feeder middle schools, as recommended, will be necessary to achieve the goal of re-assigning 300 students from Fairfax High and 150 from Lanier Middle.

 

Centreville High School is projected to have a capacity deficit of over 400 seats by the 2017-18 school years and is presently more overcrowded than Fairfax High School.  Ultimately, the construction of a new high school and/or capacity enhancements will be needed to achieve enrollment relief for Centreville High School and other western county high schools.  While Liberty Middle has some capacity, Centreville High School significantly exceeds capacity and is not recommended for inclusion in the study.  Lake Braddock Secondary was also considered and has projected capacity surplus.  However, Lake Braddock is being considered as a lab school site with George Mason University, a program which would be open to students from outside the attendance area, similar to a magnet school.  Open enrollment at the lab school might impact the projected capacity surplus with the growth of that program. Therefore, staff does not recommend inclusion of Lake Braddock Secondary in the study.  It should be noted that while Jackson Middle School is included because it is the feeder middle school to Falls Church High School, it is unlikely that Jackson Middle would be impacted by any boundary change given the projected overcrowding at Jackson, approximately 131% building utilization for the 2017 school year.
