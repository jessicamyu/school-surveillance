Recommended Action
Adopt Policy 8610.6, School Bus Transportation, Walking and Bicycling Routes, as reviewed and discussed by the Board (FTS; presented as new business 12/15/11)

[Updated: 1/13/12 - motion passed]

 

Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services     

 

Meeting Category:  January 12, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 11

 

Subject:                 School Board Policy 8610, School Bus Transportation, Walking and Bicycling Routes

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                       

 

Key Points:

  • Removes the requirement to notify the Division Superintendent of exceptions to the transportation mileage limitations due to unusual hazards.  This decision is made by the Division Superintendent’s designee.  
  • Revised language in the policy that encourages walking and biking to and from school using existing infrastructure.  Acknowledges that this parental decision is not subject to review by the principal and that parents have the ultimate responsibility for selecting their children’s routes to and from bus stops, schools, and home.  Maintains unique procedures for kindergarteners.  Confirms that FCPS will review routes for unusual hazards and collaborate with other agencies for infrastructure enhancements.   
  • Removes language referring to safety education conducted with the Fairfax County Police Department, VDOT and other organizations.  While general safety instruction is provided in the health and PE curriculum, in 2009, the Fairfax County Police Department eliminated their School Education Officers that provided this service at all elementary schools. 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 8610.6, School Bus Transportation, Walking and Bicycling Routes, as reviewed and discussed by the Board.

 

 

Motion passed:

 

I move that the School Board adopt Policy 8610.6, School Bus Transportation, Walking and Bicycling Routes, as reviewed and discussed by the Board, and as amended: (1) by adding the following language in Section IV.A.: “Routes should be designed to ensure the safety of students as a priority.  Route design also should be based upon achieving the greatest possible efficiencies and effectiveness.  Efficiency speaks to the amount of time and number of miles required and effectiveness speaks to ensuring students arrive at their destinations on time.” and (2) by not deleting Section IV.D. and by replacing the current language with the following language: “D. Safety Education:  The school system will ensure that programs of walking, bicycling and bus safety education shall be established and maintained for students in the elementary schools.  Such programs shall be conducted in accordance with Virginia Department of Education curriculums and guidelines.  Staff members will provide general safety information to school communities and respond to requests for specific information and advice when practicable.”; and further direct the Superintendent to identify and recommend moving this language from Section IV.D. to an appropriate policy dealing with instruction. (unanimously)

Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  January 12, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 11

 

Subject:                 Eleven Oaks Purchase and Sale Agreement - City of Fairfax's Provision of New Parking Area

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

On June 1, 2007, the Fairfax County School Board entered into a Purchase and Sale Agreement (the “PSA”) with the City of Fairfax (the “City”) for the transfer of the Eleven Oaks Elementary School site to the City. In return, the City paid the School Board $4 million at Closing and agreed to transfer in the future to the School Board a parcel of land sufficient to park “safely and efficiently not less than (a) 50 full size public school buses and (b) no less than 10 personal vehicles. ” In addition, the PSA obligates the City to pay all costs associated with (and including, but not limited to) acquiring, planning, developing, constructing and equipping the parcel to serve as a new parking area for the School Board. When completed, the improvements (including paving, security systems, fencing, gates, lights, cabling and wiring, access to electricity, and power for engine heaters) to the new parking area to be paid for by the City are to be consistent with the bus parking facilities the School Board has constructed elsewhere within the 5 years preceding the date of the PSA.  

 

To fulfill one of its outstanding obligations under the PSA, the City of Fairfax is proposing to acquire and to transfer to the School Board (by deed or long term lease) approximately 2.33 acres of real property located within the City limits to serve as the site for the new parking area.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (1) delegate to the Division Superintendent and Chief Operating Officer, either of whom may act individually, the authority to accept on behalf of the School Board either the 2.33 acre site proffered by the City (and described by staff in closed meeting) or an alternative site, so long as such alternate site is located wholly within the City limits, all pursuant to the terms of the Purchase and Sale Agreement, dated June 1, 2007 (the “PSA”); and (2)  authorize the Division Superintendent or Chief Operating Officer, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to take all actions necessary and appropriate on behalf of the School Board, including negotiating, executing and administering all agreements and related documentation, on such terms as they deem advisable, in order to accept the real property to serve as the site, and to obtain the improvements for the new parking area, in accordance with the terms of the PSA and with the best interests of the school division.
