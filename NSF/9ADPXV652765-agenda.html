Staff Contact:           Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     September 23, 2013 – Work Session No. 22

 

Subject:                     FY 2015 Fiscal Forecast and School Board Value Propositions

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

In order to begin the FY 2015 budget planning process, the fiscal forecast for the School Operating Fund will be presented.  The forecast includes five years of projections for all key financial areas that drive the budget development process, such as revenue assumptions and expenditure assumptions, including employee salaries and benefits, student membership, and other identified program needs. 

 

Based on projections for revenue and required expenditures, FCPS is forecasting a significant deficit for FY 2015.  A discussion of the fiscal forecast, including revenue and expenditure projections, will begin the process of providing input regarding considerations for the FY 2015 proposed budget development.

 

After the fiscal forecast presentation and a discussion of the shortfall that FCPS faces, value propositions were presented.  They were developed using values discussed by the School Board during previous year’s budget development processes.  The goal of the value proposition rating and discussion is to provide the Superintendent with preliminary input from the School Board regarding their values and priorities as the Superintendent develops the FY 2015 Proposed Budget.

 

Each School Board member was randomly assigned a number between 1 and 13, and was asked to complete and submit their value ranking.  In total, there are 18 value propositions and each School Board member was given 60 points to allocate across the 18 value propositions.  For each value proposition, they could allocate points using a value from 0 to 10 in whole numbers. 

 

After the values were rated, a summary was created to show the average points that each value received as well as the level of consensus in the individual ratings given to each individual value.  This data is presented in a summary chart.  To see how each value was rated, bar graphs were created that indicate the number of School Board members that gave each value a specific score.

Subject:                   Fairfax County School Board 2014 Draft Legislative Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:               Legislation

 

Key Points:

 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The attached 2014 Fairfax County School Board Draft Legislative Program includes additions, subtractions and formatting changes. These changes are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate, to incorporate staff recommendations, and to update, simplify and streamline existing position language.  The draft also incorporates language from the supplemental legislative positions that were adopted by the Board in January 2013 in response to legislation under consideration during the 2013 General Assembly Session.
