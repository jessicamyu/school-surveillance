African American History Month - February 2021

 

Originally established as Negro History Week in 1926 by Dr. Carter G. Woodson, a noted African-American author and scholar, this event evolved into the establishment in 1976 of February as "Black History Month." The commemoration has increasingly been referred to as "African American History Month," although both names are currently in use.

 

This year's theme, "The Black Family: Representation, Identity and Diversity", explores the wide-ranging diversity of black family life from single to two-parent households, to nuclear and extended families.  Throughout black history, factors such as inequality and poverty have put pressure on maintaining family ties, when a better life meant traveling far from home.  This may be the reason why family reunions have always remained popular within the African American communities.  Economic pressures that may pull families apart also often unite them, that is, against prejudice and bigotry.  Many black families may pool resources or find job opportunities or simply find emotional comfort within their own communities.  Throughout American history, the black community has always exhibited an unwavering understanding of the value of family - as an incomparable source of comfort, strength and even survival.

National School Counseling Week – February 1 – 5, 2021

 

National School Counseling Week 2021 (#NSCW21), "School Counselors: All In for All Students," will be celebrated from Feb 1-5, 2021, to focus public attention on the unique contribution of school counselors within U.S. school systems. National School Counseling Week, sponsored by the American School Counselor Association (ASCA), highlights the tremendous impact school counselors can have in helping students achieve school success and plan for a career. National School Counseling Week is always celebrated the first full week in February.

 

Fairfax County Public Schools employs close to 700 school counseling professionals, including school counselors, directors of student services, and career center specialists. These professionals are committed to preparing students to become lifelong learners and productive members in a global society with a focus on social emotional wellness, academic success, and college and career readiness. Thank you to all our school counseling professionals for the tremendous work they do!

 

As part of National School Counseling Week, it is an honor to congratulate Kathleen Otal, school counselor at McLean High School, who was recognized as the 2020 Virginia School Counselor Association Secondary Counselor of the Year. We would also like to congratulate Anne Lee, school counselor at Lynbrook Elementary, who was recognized as the FCPS Outstanding School-Based Professional Employee for 2020.

Recognition of National Career and Technical Education Month

 

Career and Technical Education or CTE month is a public awareness campaign held every February to celebrate CTE and the achievements and accomplishments of CTE programs across the country. This year's theme, "Celebrate Today, Own Tomorrow!" raises awareness of the innovation and excellence that exists in our CTE programs as well as the crucial role that CTE plays in readying our students for careers and our nation for economic success.

 

Career and technical education prepares both youth and adults for a wide range of careers, from nurse to computer technician. These careers may require varying levels of education--from high school to postsecondary certificates to two- and four-year college degrees.

The subject areas most commonly associated with career and technical education are programming, cyber security, automotive technician, cosmetologist, law enforcement, nursing, emergency services; culinary arts, child development, education, marketing, entrepreneurship, electronics, and engineering.

Career and technical education is offered in middle schools, high schools, two-year community and technical colleges and other postsecondary schools. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 18 of the 20 fastest growing occupations within the next decade will require career and technical education. Thank you to all our CTE teachers and staff who help students realize Portrait of a Graduate outcomes and ensure students are college and career ready!

3.03 McLean/Langley High School Boundary Study [FTS; PH 1/28/21, Action 2/4/21;approve a boundary adjustment of a portion of the Spring Hill ES split feeder area to feed from Longfellow MS and McLean HS to Cooper MS and Langley HS. Subject:  McLean/Langley High School Boundary Study

 

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                  Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category:  January 21, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closing

 

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is considering a boundary adjustment to provide capacity relief to McLean High School (HS) by using available capacity at Langley HS. The study also includes the middle schools to provide continuity in the school feeder pattern from elementary on to middle and high schools. In School Year (SY) 2019-20, McLean HS had 2,350 students with a design capacity of 1,993 students, and Langley HS had 1,972 students with a design capacity of 2,370 students. Longfellow Middle School (MS) had 1,334 students with a design capacity of 1,374 students, and Cooper MS had 992 students with a renovation that has a planned design capacity of 1,120 students.

 

 

Discussion:

 

The process for boundary adjustments is outlined in Policy 8130 and Regulation 8130.

 

Boundary scoping meetings are the first step in the process once a school has been identified for a boundary adjustment. In December 2019, the school communities were invited to attend boundary scoping meetings, one at Langley HS (December 2, 2019) and one at McLean HS (December 4, 2019), to learn about the process and provide feedback to staff about which areas should be considered in the boundary study. As a result of feedback, the School Board amended the scope to include middle schools at the work session held on March 9, 2020. The School Board voted to award a contract on May 7, 2020, to install a twelve-classroom modular at McLean HS for capacity relief, also as a result of community feedback.

 

The second step in the process, a boundary study community meeting, was held on December 7, 2020, which presented four options to the community to collect feedback for a boundary adjustment recommendation. The feedback was used to create a recommended option to the School Board.

 

Option C presented at the boundary study community meeting has been revised based on community concerns of population growth and development. The recommended option reassigns a portion of the area that currently feeds from Spring Hill Elementary School (ES) to Longfellow MS and McLean HS, to Cooper MS and Langley HS. This modified option identifies an estimated 159 students at the high school level and 73 students at the middle school level.

 

Policy 8130.7 addresses Phasing of Adjustments in Section XI and states: “When possible, adjustments under this policy shall be implemented through attrition and phasing. The School Board may approve a grade-by-grade phase-in of adjustments for students beginning with the incoming class at the middle or high school levels, when feasible. The School Board may adopt other phasing plans as appropriate to the individual boundary study.”

 

Prior to voting on the boundary adjustment, the School Board will hold a public hearing for the purpose of obtaining additional public comment on January 28, 2021. The School Board is scheduled for a vote on the boundary adjustment at the Regular Meeting on February 4, 2021.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

FCPS is recommending that the School Board approve a boundary adjustment of a portion of the Spring Hill ES split feeder area to feed from Longfellow MS and McLean HS to Cooper MS and Langley HS. Any approved boundary adjustment will not change the elementary school boundary.

Recommended Action
That the School Board approve Amendment No. 1 to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) with an effective date for the Amendment of February 1, 2021.

Staff Contact: Eliazer Martinez, executive director/CIO, ERFC

 

Meeting Category: January 21, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject: Amendment 1 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan as Restated Effective July 1, 2017 (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required: Information


Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending proposed amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) related to the designation of a Nominated Beneficiary under Option C and the definition of Nominated Beneficiary.

 

Currently, a Member who is receiving Option C retirement benefit payments may not change a Nominated Beneficiary after the Member’s Effective Retirement Date, except when the Nominated Beneficiary predeceases the member. The proposed amendment would allow a Member who is receiving retirement benefit payments under Option C to nominate a new Nominated Beneficiary at any time and for any reason. The proposed amendment also would make explicit the current Plan practice that the nomination of beneficiaries for Options A and B are irrevocable following a Member’s Effective Retirement Date except as otherwise provided under the Plan. The proposed amendment also would make conforming amendments to the definition of Nominated Beneficiary to remove the requirement that a Nominated Beneficiary designation be notarized, which is consistent with the recent changes to the pension application form approved by the Board of Trustees.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve Amendment No. 1 to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) with an effective date for the Amendment of February 1, 2021.
