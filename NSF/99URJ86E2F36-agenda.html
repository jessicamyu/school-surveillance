Children’s Mental Health Awareness Day – May 8, 2014

 

In recognition that the well-being of our community’s children has a profound, immediate, and lasting impact on the community as a whole and that a child’s mental and physical health are intertwined and must be nurtured and protected, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors has proclaimed May 8, 2014, to be Children’s Mental Health Awareness Day in Fairfax County.

 

Children’s mental health issues are real and treatable and occur with much greater frequency than many people know. Fairfax County residents and Fairfax County Public Schools recognize the importance of having a strong network of mental health services for children, youth, and families in our community that can provide support and help to those in need. Fairfax County Public Schools supports this proclamation and urges all to pause and pay attention to our children.  From that first step, help can follow. 

 

 

Children's Book Week - May 12-18, 2014

 

Children’s Book Week, a national celebration of reading, storytelling, and book-related events, observes its 95th anniversary in 2014 and is the longest-running national literacy initiative in the country.  Established to promote higher standards in children’s books, the week grew to involve librarians, publishers, and booksellers.  Nationwide, author and illustrator events take place each year and the Children’s Choice Book Awards, the only national child-chosen book awards program, are announced during the week.

RESOLUTION

NAMING THE GOVERNOR’S HEALTH SCIENCES ACADEMY AT

WEST POTOMAC HIGH SCHOOL

“CLIFFORD S. HARDISON GOVERNOR’S HEALTH SCIENCES ACADEMY” 

 

 

     WHEREAS, Clifford S. Hardison was an esteemed educator in Virginia for over four decades and worked to help young people become better citizens, scholars, and athletes; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mr. Hardison served the West Potomac High School community, first as Director of Guidance from 1992 to 1997, and then as principal from 2009 until his death in 2013;   he was committed to enhancing West Potomac High School’s reputation as a center for academic excellence while also increasing the opportunities for its students to advance athletically, emotionally and socially and to acquire those skills necessary to best contribute as 21st century citizens; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mr. Hardison not only led efforts to increase student enrollment in advanced academic programs, he also supported the creation of a dual-enrollment post-Advanced Placement course, and continued to develop career exploration in education coursework and community service programs, to include West Potomac High School’s flagship senior service learning program; and

 

     WHEREAS, under Mr. Hardison’s leadership, West Potomac High School petitioned the state to recognize the school as a center of excellence for health sciences and to expand curriculum offerings in tandem with community and regional needs; as a result of Mr. Hardison’s efforts, West Potomac High School was granted Governor’s status for its Health Sciences Academy; and

 

     WHEREAS, the naming of the Governor’s Health Sciences Academy at West Potomac High School in Mr. Hardison’s honor will be an enduring commemoration of his commitment to providing opportunities for advanced academic achievement and career exploration for all students; 

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, in recognition of his dedicated service to the West Potomac High School community and his valued contributions to Fairfax County Public Schools, hereby names the “Clifford S. Hardison Governor’s Health Sciences Academy” at West Potomac High School in his honor and memory.

Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  May 8, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 19

 

Subject:                  Award of Contract –Locker Refinishing, Refurbishing, and Installation

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:            School Board Policy 5011.2 Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) seeks to establish a contract to provide for the locker refinishing, refurbishing, and installation in FCPS buildings as requirements become known.  Generally the task orders are valued at $5,000-$50,000 depending upon the project.  However, the aggregate dollar amount of services performed under this contract can be more than $250,000 annually. 

 

While staff solicited full and open competition, only one bid for this contract was received on April 23, 2014.  Staff has reviewed the bid pricing which is in line with current industry price standards and recommends the award to Red Hill Distributors, Inc.  The contractor has performed satisfactorily on previous contracts.  Funding for the locker refinishing, refurbishing, and installation contract is provided from the Major Maintenance Fund, as well as school and department Operating Funds.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the locker refinishing, refurbishing, and installation to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, Red Hill Distributor, Inc., and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

[Updated 5/7/14 - Revised attachment #3; proposed follow-on motion added]

Staff Contact:            Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     May 8, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 19

 

Subject:                      FY 2015 Approved Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                                                          

 

Key Points:

 

On February 6, 2014, the School Board adopted the FY 2015 Advertised Budget.  The advertised budget was presented to and discussed by the School Board and County Board of Supervisors at a joint budget work session on March 18, 2014. 

 

On April 29, 2014, the County Board of Supervisors adopted their FY 2015 budget that included an increase in the transfer to the School Operating Fund of 3.0 percent above the FY 2014 Approved Budget.  The increase of $51.5 million is $46.6 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget.  While the State has not yet adopted their budget, FCPS is projecting an increase of $30.0 million over the estimate included in FCPS’ FY 2015 Advertised Budget based on the current state budget proposals available. It appears very possible that the State will not adopt their budget prior to FCPS’ approved budget adoption.

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue reflects a net reduction of $19.5 million as compared to the FY 2015 Advertised Budget.  Funding from the county is $46.6 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by a projected increase in state revenue of $30.0 million and a projected increase in sales tax revenue of $2.1 million.  Tuition revenue from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $1.0 million, and other revenue will decrease by $4.0 million based on the recommendation to eliminate fees for Advanced Placement and International Baccalaureate exams.

 

Expenditures:

In total, expenditures reflect a net reduction of $19.5 million as compared to the FY 2015 Advertised Budget and include the following reductions: $6.3 million in the cost of enrollment growth and changes in student demographics; $3.9 million in employee benefit adjustments; $1.8 million in projected energy conservation savings; and $10.4 million from delaying the implementation of step until November.  These reductions are offset by an increase of $1.1 million for support for small schools and $1.9 million for in-school suspension support.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2015 Approved Budget as detailed in the FY 2015 Advertised Budget and modified with the adjustments included in this agenda item.

 

Proposed follow-on motion:

 

I move that the School Board Chairman shall appoint members of the School Board to a joint committee with the Board of Supervisors, with the mission of the committee to articulate a constructive and collaborative budget development process that builds trust, establishes mutual expectations, and promotes joint, multi-year budgeting and planning; further move that the committee report its findings to both boards prior to the presentation of the Superintendent's FY 2016 proposed budget and the County Executive's FY 2016 advertised budget. (Patty Reed and Ted Velkoff)

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
