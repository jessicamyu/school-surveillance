National Safety Month - June 2011

Each June, the National Safety Council sponsors National Safety Month to promote safe and healthy behaviors in all aspects of our lives.  Each week carries a different theme that brings attention to current safety issues. This year, the themes are preventing overexertion at work and home, teen driving safety, preventing slips, trips, and falls, and on the road-off the phone.

Over 175,000 FCPS students depend on school employees to provide a safe environment in schools.  Prevention, identification, and abatement of hazards are integral parts of every employee’s job. The FCPS Office of Safety and Security has established emergency plans and provided appropriate training for employees.

Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Week - May 23-27, 2011

The adult and community education program of Fairfax County Public Schools began with an action of the School Board in 1955, and, in the 55 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills and completed their high school educations.

Through the years,  adults have learned new languages, acquired job skills, transitioned to new careers, and grown as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to Adult & Community Education to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education has made major contributions to the school system as a laboratory of innovation for the piloting of new programs and services in support of pre-K-12 education through family literacy, summer programs, extended day programs, enrichment courses, and driver education.

Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner – as well as provision of remediation and enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day – ACE has helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

Subject:                      FY 2012 Approved Budget    

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Key Points:

 

On February 3, 2011, the School Board adopted the FY 2012 Advertised Budget which included the following expenditure highlights:  two percent market scale adjustment for employees; step increases for eligible employees; increases in salary lapse; retirement and health rate increases; contract length reductions; membership growth and demographic changes; online Standards of Learning (SOL) test requirements for elementary schools; high school needs-based staffing; and reductions related to one-time federal stimulus funding.

 

Included in FCPS’ FY 2012 Advertised Budget was a request for increased funding from the county.  The Board of Supervisors approved a level transfer from the county to the School Operating Fund resulting in a decrease of $48.8 million as compared to the request in the advertised budget.  As a result of the impacts of the county budget, state budget, and other updates, additional adjustments are required.  Details are provided on the attached agenda. 

 

Following are the highlights of the FY 2012 Approved Budget as presented:

 

·         One percent market scale adjustment for all employees

·         Step increases for eligible employees

·         Full-day kindergarten at all elementary schools

·         Additional support for students during the discipline process

·         Elimination of Advanced Placement and International Baccalaureate test fees

·         Virginia Retirement System reserve balance of $60.6 million to address future obligations

 

Recommended changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

[Updated 5/25/11 - Attachment A]

 

 

Staff Contact:          Peter J. Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Meeting Category:   May 26, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                   Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee Members

           

School Board Action Required:  Decision                    

 

Related To:               Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

 

Key Points:

 

The last Mathematics Textbook Adoption was done in 2001. The books currently being used for instruction were published in 1999 through 2001.

 

This committee will review and recommend books for use in K-12 mathematics courses.  These materials will be considered basal materials. 

 

The Constitution of Virginia gives the Virginia Board of Education the authority to adopt textbooks for use in the schools.  Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Regulation 3004.2, Adopted Basal Instructional Materials, incorporates state adoption guidelines and requirements and specifies procedures for establishing committees to review textbooks and other basal instructional materials and make recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board for local adoption.  Each FCPS textbook review committee is composed as follows:

 

                        Teachers 50 percent

                        School-based administrators 25 percent

                        Community members 25 percent

 

Central office staff members serve as nonvoting, ex-officio members to guide and assist the work of   the committee.  All recommended books will be reviewed for content accuracy by  qualified content experts prior to public review.

 

Each School Board member shall appoint one community member to the Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee.  School-based administrators and teachers will be nominated by cluster assistant superintendents.  Participating staff members will be from the Instructional Services Department, Department of Special Services, and schools. 

 

The School Board will appoint members to review and recommend basal instructional materials to be used in Fairfax County Public Schools. 

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board appoint committee members to the Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:    May 26, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                      Legislative Recommendations for the 2012 Policies and Resolutions of the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA)

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                             

 

Key Points:

 

The Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA) annually solicits input from local school boards for changes to the Virginia School Boards Association Legislative Positions.  Recommendations for changes to VSBA positions are based on those already adopted by the Fairfax County School Board (FCSB) for its own 2011 Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program.  The FCSB Legislative Program itself will be updated  in fall 2011 in advance of the 2012 Virginia General Assembly session.

 

The School Board held a Legislative work session on May 16, 2011,  to consider proposed recommendations to VSBA.  In addition to its legislative recommendations, the Board recommended that the cover letter to VSBA (that will accompany its proposed legislative language changes) express the FCSB support for the existing VSBA legislative position regarding comprehensive study of the Local Composite Index (VSBA Position 9.6 Composite Index) and its opposition to a proposal that would allow some jurisdictions to assess their local true property values at something other than fair value.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the legislative recommendations to be forwarded to the Virginia School Boards Association Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee for their consideration, as detailed in the agenda item.
