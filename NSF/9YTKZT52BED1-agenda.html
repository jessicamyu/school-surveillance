Key Points:    

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. All of the goals contain overarching strategies, desired outcomes, actions, and monitoring metrics. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan. The purpose of this report is to provide an update on the current work that supports Goal 1, Student Success.

 

The following are the last two of four overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 1 Student Success.  The first two strategies were presented on December 17, 2015

 

Overarching Strategy 3:

Provide quality Early Childhood experiences aligned with Portrait of a Graduate outcomes that are designed to prepare students to successfully enter Kindergarten

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • All qualifying future FCPS students will have access to high quality early education programs
  • All agencies will coordinate their efforts to provide similar early childhood experiences
     

Overarching Strategy 4:

Provide students with relevant opportunities to explore options and prepare for College and Career

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • A portfolio of school options will provide multiple pathways to a high school diploma

Students will be fluent in two or more languages

Subject:  Program Evaluation – English Language Learner Study (EL)

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

Current national, state and local discussions on the needs of growing EL student population, the FCPS commitment to expand its knowledge about helping FCPS EL students (Strategic Plan), and interest in tracking the progress of an FCPS high school pilot program for EL students collectively resulted in a two year study by the Office of Program Evaluation.

 

 

This Year 1 report lays the foundation for a final Year 2 report by providing:

  • a description of the EL population growth over the past decade,
  • an analysis of the design of services for EL students (i.e., how well services align with best practices and research), and
  • a description of the historical academic performance of EL students in FCPS.

 

The Year 2 report will be completed in December 2016 to allow time and data that permit a valid discussion on the relationship between educational practices and academic performance.  The Year 2 report will provide:

  • an analysis of the implementation of services for EL students in FCPS schools,
  • an analysis of the relationship between implementation fidelity and academic outcomes, and
  • a description of costs in relation to other school divisions.

 

This presentation to the School Board focuses on the Year 1 findings and describes recommendations to the School Board and Superintendent.

 

Recommendation:

 

For the School Board: Request a staff review of relevant division policies and associated regulations that may unintentionally limit how schools are able to use organizational structures (time, space, and personnel) to provide the research-based educational experiences to EL students.

 

For the Superintendent: Direct staff to review and develop an action plan for bringing services for ELs at all school levels into alignment with the three areas of research-based educational experiences described in this report.

Subject:  Compensation Study

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS has hired Segal Group, a benefits, compensation, and human resources consulting firm, to identify a compensation structure that attracts and retains qualified employees through an optimal mix of salary and benefits. The study is a key piece of the Strategic Plan’s goal 3 – Premier Workforce – which aims to recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts, and ensure that FCPS maintains a competitive compensation package.

 

Local school districts and county governments have responded to a market study regarding the total compensation (salary and benefits) of teachers, instructional assistants, and benchmarked Unified Scale positions. The initial results on FCPS’ market position for teacher compensation were presented on December 7, 2015. Segal Group will provide a briefing on the remaining results of the market study for instructional assistants, school-based administrators, and other benchmarked Unified Scale positions, followed by initial discussion of compensation strategies.
