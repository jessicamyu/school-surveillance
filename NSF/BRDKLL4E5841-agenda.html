The School Board recognizes the hard work and unending dedication of the custodians who work for Fairfax County Public Schools. Black History Month Resolution - February 2021

     WHEREAS, the month of February is the time that we as a nation, recognize Black History Month and this year’s theme is “The Black Family: Representation, Identity, and Diversity,” to honor the resilience of the family through great adversity; and,

     WHEREAS, Virginia’s Governor Ralph Northam has declared that Black history is American history; and,

     WHEREAS, the Commonwealth of Virginia is central to discussions about Africans in America as they arrived on its shores more than 400 years ago as this commonwealth resisted the full integration of Africans into our democracy when it established the seat of the Confederacy, resisted reconstruction, formed the massive resistance, erected monument row in Richmond, Virginia in honor of confederate leaders and named schools, streets and public buildings in honor of that resistance; and,

     WHEREAS, African American families developed educational institutions across this nation to assist its people to achieve including Howard University that has now produced the first woman Vice President; and,

 

     WHEREAS, the African American family and culture have been central to the development of Virginia, this nation and its culture despite the obstacles of slavery, discrimination, and institutionalized patterns of structural racism that still plague our nation as evidenced by the murders of George Floyd and the insurrection of January 6th, 2021 where rioters sought to overturn an election while carrying a confederate flag through our nation’s capital; and,

 

     WHEREAS, those of African descent have lived in, and contributed to, the development and advancement of Fairfax County since its inception; as evidenced by the many contributions of private citizens, businesses and African American Organizations, as well as, the establishment of various communities and organizations that have enhanced the rich and vibrant culture of Fairfax County and work to preserve African American culture and the rights, of all of its residents to create an inclusive, equitable county for all; and

 

     NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County Public Schools hereby officially recognizes Black History Month in appreciation for the contributions of great Americans who have worked tirelessly and often without recognition for the advancement of both Virginia and the United States. I so move.

4.02 McLean/Langley High School Boundary Study
Meeting
Feb 4, 2021 - Hybrid Regular Meeting
Category
4. Action Items - 8:30 p.m.
Type
Action

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                  Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  February 4, 2021 – Regular Meeting


Subject:  McLean/Langley High School Boundary Study

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closing

 

Key Points:

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is considering a boundary adjustment to provide capacity relief to McLean High School (HS) by using available capacity at Langley HS. The study also includes the middle schools to provide continuity in the school feeder pattern from elementary on to middle and high schools. In School Year (SY) 2019-20, McLean HS had 2,350 students with a design capacity of 1,993 students, and Langley HS had 1,972 students with a design capacity of 2,370 students. Longfellow Middle School (MS) had 1,334 students with a design capacity of 1,374 students, and Cooper MS had 992 students with a renovation that has a planned design capacity of 1,120 students.

 

Discussion:

The process for boundary adjustments is outlined in Policy 8130 and Regulation 8130.

 

Boundary scoping meetings are the first step in the process once a school has been identified for a boundary adjustment. In December 2019, the school communities were invited to attend boundary scoping meetings, one at Langley HS (December 2, 2019) and one at McLean HS (December 4, 2019), to learn about the process and provide feedback to staff about which areas should be considered in the boundary study. As a result of feedback, the School Board amended the scope to include middle schools at the work session held on March 9, 2020. The School Board voted to award a contract on May 7, 2020, to install a twelve-classroom modular at McLean HS for capacity relief, also as a result of community feedback.

 

The second step in the process, a boundary study community meeting, was held on December 7, 2020, which presented four options to the community to collect feedback for a boundary adjustment recommendation. The feedback was used to create a recommended option to the School Board.

 

Option C presented at the boundary study community meeting was modified based on community concerns of population growth and development. The modified Option C was presented at the School Board Regular Meeting as New Business on January 21, 2021.

 

Subsequently, the School Board held a public hearing on January 28, 2021, for the purpose of obtaining public feedback. As a result of this feedback, the recommendation is Option B, as presented at the community meeting on December 7, 2020. Option B reassigns students in the Colvin Run ES split feeder area, a portion of the Spring Hill ES split feeder area, and a portion of Westbriar ES from Longfellow MS/McLean HS to Cooper MS/Langley HS.  This option identifies an estimated 190 students at the high school level and 78 students at the middle school level.

 

Policy 8130.7 addresses Phasing of Adjustments in Section XI and states: “When possible, adjustments under this policy shall be implemented through attrition and phasing. The School Board may approve a grade-by-grade phase-in of adjustments for students beginning with the incoming class at the middle or high school levels, when feasible. The School Board may adopt other phasing plans as appropriate to the individual boundary study.”

 

 

Recommendation:FCPS is recommending that the School Board approve a boundary adjustment of the Colvin Run ES split feeder area, a portion of the Spring Hill ES split feeder area, and a portion of Westbriar ES from Longfellow MS/McLean HS to Cooper MS/Langley HS. This was presented as Option B to the community and identifies an estimated 190 students at the high school level and 78 students at the middle school level. Any approved boundary adjustment will not change the elementary school boundary.

 

Board Member Proposed Motions:

Motion One: I move to adopt the FCPS recommendation of Option B, as presented to the community on December 7, 2020. This would adjust the boundaries of McLean HS, Langley HS, Longfellow MS, and Cooper MS. The change would adjust the feeder pattern of the Colvin Run ES split feeder area, a portion of the Spring Hill ES split feeder area, and a portion of Westbriar ES from Longfellow MS and McLean HS, to Cooper MS and Langley HS. The adjustment will not change the elementary school boundary.

 

In addition, the following phasing plan will implement the boundary change:
Rising 7th grade students will attend Cooper MS in the 2021-22 school year, with rising 8th graders grandfathered to remain at Longfellow MS in the 2021-22 school year. Both 7th and 8th grade students will attend Cooper MS in the 2022-23 school year.

Rising 10th, 11th, and 12th graders in the area will be grandfathered and remain at McLean HS. The high school boundary change will begin with rising 9th grade students in the 2021-22 school year; 9th and 10th grade students in the 2022-23 school year; 9th, 10th, and 11th grade students in the 2023-24 school year; and 9th through 12th grade students in the 2024-25 school year.

Motion Two: Rising 9thgraders residing in the area changed will have the option to attend Langley HS in SY 2021-22 or be grandfathered at McLean HS with transportation provided through 12thgrade in the 2024-25 school year. The option chosen for the 2021-22 school year will determine the school through the 2024-25 school year and will not be able to be changed

Subject: FY 2022-2026 Capital Improvement Program

 

 

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and  Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                  Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: February 4, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Action

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

 

Outcome:

 

The FCPS Capital Improvement Program (CIP) annually reviews current student membership analysis and facilities data to identify future capacity needs and capital requirements for new construction, capacity enhancements, renovations, and acquiring land for facilities in the future. The changing conditions of the COVID-19 pandemic and the virtual start to school have impacted several elements that are part of the CIP approach. A five-year projection set has not been produced as the possible future impact of the unique decline in membership for School Year (SY) 2020-21 will depend on many factors, including the future course of the pandemic and economic conditions. An assessment of facility capacity was not completed, and program capacity utilization was not calculated due to a virtual start to the school year, ongoing planning for a return to school, and social distancing which required using all available rooms for core instruction. The Proposed Fiscal Year (FY) 2022-26 CIP focuses on capital projects, including new school construction, capacity enhancements, and renovations, continuing progress towards completing the Renovation Queue.

 

The Proposed FY 2022-26 CIP identifies the following capital projects:

 

New School Construction: The CIP identifies the construction of the McNair Upper Elementary School formerly known as North West County elementary school (funded), the Fairfax/Oakton Area elementary school (funded), the Silver Line elementary school (partially funded), the Future Western high school (unfunded), the Route 1 elementary school (funded), and four new and/or repurposed schools (unfunded).

 

Capacity Enhancements: The CIP identifies additions at West Potomac, Justice, and Madison high schools and a modular addition, all of which are funded.

 

Renovations: The CIP identifies renovations at 29 named elementary schools, six named middle schools, and four named high schools, which includes funded, partially funded, and unfunded projects. Many of these renovation projects will include capacity enhancements.

 

Site Acquisition: The CIP identifies funds provided and needed to acquire land for facilities in the future.

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

The FCPS CIP is a planning and fiscal management tool used to coordinate the location, timing, and funding of projects over a five-year period. The CIP includes the proposed capital improvement projects, a year-by-year schedule of anticipated spending, and actual and estimated costs. The CIP is a working document and is updated annually to reflect changing conditions within our schools and communities. Additionally, it offers a broader planning schedule in order to focus staff efforts and community conversations.

That the School Board approve the Proposed FY 2022-2026 Capital Improvement Program.

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:     Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Michelle Pratt, director, Office of Procurement Services

Stephanie Gray, coordinator, School Counseling and College Success

Lynette Henry, manager, College Success Programs

 

Meeting Category: February 4, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan Goal 4:  Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Office of Counseling and College and Career Readiness recommends that the School Board extend the agreement with George Mason University (GMU) for an additional five (5) year period.  The agreement provides first-generation students and families in select Fairfax County Public Schools the opportunity to participate in GMU’s Early Identification Program (EIP).  EIP is a multi-year, regional program supported by George Mason University, and other participating local school divisions. EIP supports FCPS’ goal to close gaps in access, opportunity, and achievement.    

 

The mission of EIP is to improve students’ access to higher education and quality of life by providing academic enrichment, personal development, and community engagement programs that produce global citizens.  Established in 1987 through a grant from the State Council of Higher Education for Virginia, George Mason University and Fairfax County Public Schools partnered to provide college access programming to first-generation college bound students in Fairfax County. For over thirty years, this GMU and FCPS partnership has resulted in 100% high school graduation rates and a 95% postsecondary attendance rate for students who complete the EIP program. Currently, over 700 FCPS students are EIP alumni.   

 

Students are nominated for EIP in the spring of their seventh-grade year. Currently students from the following FCPS Middle Schools: Glasgow, Holmes, Jackson, Lanier, Poe, Sandburg and Whitman, are eligible to participate in the program.  One hundred percent of the students selected are first-generation, in addition many are from single parent homes, qualify for free or reduced meals and come from underrepresented student groups.  Over the course of their five-year commitment to the program, students receive academic mentoring and tutoring, attend three weeks of Summer Academy as well as a variety of Saturday programing that includes: STEM enrichment, Parent Engagement, and College and Career preparation and awareness.  Upon completion of the program each student who graduates with 3.2 GPA will receive a guaranteed acceptance to GMU and students who graduate with a 3.5 GPA can apply for EIP scholarships. 

 

Due to the impact of COVID-19 programing will take place in a virtual environment until it is deemed safe to return to in-person events by both FCPS and GMU.   

 

The total estimated payment to George Mason University for the 2020-2021 school year is approximately $130,000, or $650,000 over the 5 year term; Policy 5011 requires that the School Board approve “any contract or contract amendment awarded between the School Board and other governmental entity that exceeds $250,000.”  Policy 5011.3 (III)(A)(1).

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (1) extend the Memorandum of Agreement effective July 1, 2020, through June 30, 2025, and (ii) authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Subject:  Stonecroft Transportation Center – Boundary Confirmation Agreement with Fairfax County Water Authority

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category:  February 4, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Related To: Virginia Code § 22.1-129(B)

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Virginia statutory law authorizes a local school board to convey real property to adjust the boundaries of its property, where deemed proper. Virginia Code § 22.1-129 (B).

 

A boundary adjustment is necessary at the rear of the FCPS Stonecroft Transportation parcel, which is adjacent to real property acquired in 2017 by Fairfax County Water Authority (“Fairfax Water”) for use as a utility maintenance facility.

 

A long-ago survey error (believed to have occurred between 1914 and 1944) on the Stonecroft property mistakenly resulted in a narrow and irregular shaped strip of sloping land (2622 square feet in total) being identified as common to both the FCPS Stonecroft parcel and what is now the adjoining Fairfax Water parcel. If not for this survey error, this narrow strip of land would be part of the Fairfax Water parcel, only.  Due to its steep grade, this narrow strip of land is not used by FCPS now, nor is it suitable for development in the future; simply put, it is of no use to FCPS.  Fairfax Water plans to install drainage piping within this strip of land in order to capture overflow from the Stonecroft parcel, thus benefitting the FCPS property as well as the surrounding neighborhood. The survey error came into focus during the site plan review process for Fairfax Water’s maintenance facility.

 

The FCPS Office of Design and Construction has reviewed all pertinent information and confirms its concurrence with the Fairfax Water boundary survey and the need to adjust the Stonecroft boundary.

 

Once approved by the School Board, the parties will enter into a written agreement known as a Boundary Confirmation Agreement, which will be recorded in the Fairfax County land records in order to document this resolution. FCPS will not incur any transaction or recording costs.

 

This action will in no way permanently damage, interfere with or limit present or future use of FCPS property, nor will it endanger the health and safety of users of the Stonecroft Transportation Center.  The drainage improvements planned by Fairfax Water benefit the neighborhood in general and will benefit FCPS more directly, as that is also a drainage outfall for the Stonecroft Transportation Facility.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve this adjustment to the boundary of its Stonecroft Transportation Center property and authorize the Division Superintendent or the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all action necessary and appropriate on behalf of the School Board, and in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to complete, execute, and deliver the Boundary Confirmation Agreement to Fairfax County Water Authority.

 

 

Alternative:

None Recommended
