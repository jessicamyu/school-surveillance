Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

On October 15, 2018, the Office of Facilities Planning Services, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services presented information about a review of Policy 8130 and boundary policies and practices of other school divisions. This presentation introduced 16 factors that are commonly considered by school divisions in making boundary changes. As a next step, the School Board requested a review of the educational research related to boundary changes and these 16 factors.

 

Today’s presentation describes the findings from the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement’s review of the educational research on factors considered in boundary decision-making, focusing on four broad areas:

  • Student diversity (demographics, socioeconomics, achievement)

  • Travel time (geographic proximity, transportation)

  • Social connection (cohort or split feeders, health and safety of students)

  • Overcrowding (current or projected school capacity)

 

In addition, the presentation describes how FCPS practice relates to the areas identified in research.

 

Information about current FCPS boundaries and school capacity (including maps), as well as the practices of other districts around capacity and boundaries is also described.

 

The intent of the presentation is to provide background to inform the Board’s prioritization of boundary change factors, incorporating a One Fairfax lens.
