Staff Contact:            Jack Dale, Superintendent                                                                                                         

 

Other Staff Present:  Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services         

 

Meeting Category:     February 28, 2013 - Work Session No. 87 

 

Subject:                     Scope of Advanced Academic Program (AAP) Analysis        

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                          

 

Related To:                Student Achievement Goal 1. Pursue Academic Excellence

 

Motion Adopted by the School Board:

 

I move to amend the main motion by offering the following substitute motion:  that the School Board direct the Superintendent to conduct an analysis of the FCPS Continuum of Advanced Academic Services to ensure that current practices are aligned with the best practices in K-12 gifted education; further move that the scope of the analysis be presented to, and decided by, the Board by February 28, 2013, and the report on the analysis be completed by June 30, 2013.

 

Key Points:

 

The question before the Board:  What are the key issues in each of these areas you need studied by June? 

 

The following questions will be used to help guide the discussion and help board members identify the key issues needed to assist in their decision making.

 

The guiding questions are:

 

What are our current practices for AA programs?

What are best practices for AA programs?

 

The School Board will discuss the key issues to delineate a scope of study including:

 

Location/delivery/criteria of AAP Centers

Identification criteria for students eligible for AAP Centers

Ensuring equitable access, especially for underrepresented groups of students

Alternative practices AA services

 

The Board discussed the scope of the AAP analysis at a work session held on February 11, 2013.  At the February 21 Forum work session, staff provided the attached draft outline of proposed scope of AAP analysis for Board approval.  The Board consensus was to further discuss the proposed outline on February 28 before final approval.
