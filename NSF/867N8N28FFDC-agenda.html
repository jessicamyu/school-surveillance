Agenda Item: Community Based Summer School (CBSS)

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent Instructional Services

Background: Fairfax County Public Schools offers remediation and acceleration opportunities for students in summer school. In 1999, a central site model was instituted to accommodate the growing number of students attending summer school as economically and efficiently as possible. A few schools also offered site based summer school to accommodate unique needs. Last year, the Leadership Team, in collaboration with principals, determined that high need elementary and middle schools would benefit from having community based programs. During the summer of 2005, 13 elementary and 4 middle schools were community based summer school sites.

Key Issues: Community based summer school programs provide our neediest communities with the opportunity to support students in smaller, more personalized and familiar settings. Student achievement will increase where these programs are offered because students attend them, their individual needs are met, and the program is better integrated into the regular school year. As we propose continuation and expansion of this program, consideration must be given to the resources required, including additional funding, as well as transportation and facilities impacts.


School Board Discussion/Decision: School Board members will be provided information regarding the benefits and costs of the community based summer school model and plans to continue and expand it in 2006.

Staff Recommendation: Maintain the current community based summer school sites and add no more than 8 new elementary and 3 new middle schools for 2006, with the following considerations:
路 Select sites based on academic need, transportation and facilities impact
路 Ensure that there are adequate central sites in every region
路 Partner neighboring elementary schools where feasible
路 Identify CBSS and central sites by November 1, 2005

Options:
Maintain current community based summer school sites.
Return to a central summer school site model for all schools.

Agenda Item: FCPS Elementary Foreign Language Programs

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

Background: Fairfax County Public Schools currently offers the following programs:
路 Partial-immersion program to students who are selected by lottery at thirteen sites. Families who live outside the attendance area must provide transportation.
路 Foreign Language in the Elementary School (FLES) at seven sites. This program is offered during the school day to all students who live inside the attendance area.
路 Before-school and after-school Foreign Language Experience (FLEX) program at 64 sites. Parents pay for this program and must provide transportation.

Key Issues: Fairfax County Public Schools’ vision is for all students to graduate with communicative competence in one or more languages other than English. To reach this goal all elementary students should be given the opportunity to begin foreign language study during the school day.

School Board Discussion/Decision: Elementary schools should have the option of offering foreign language instruction.

Staff Recommendation: It is recommended that the FLES model be the model for future programs so that all students in a given school have the opportunity to learn a foreign language.

Note: If a school requests a FLES program, it can be phased in over 6 years starting with 1st grade. A teacher can be split between two or three schools in the first two years of implementation.

Options:
Maintain current status of foreign language instruction.

路 FLES program goals and curriculum overview

Agenda Item: Focus School Policy and Regulation

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

Background: The regulation outlines procedures a school will follow to become a focus school and addresses reauthorization procedures for existing focus schools. Currently 14 elementary and 5 middle schools are designated focus schools as listed in Attachment B.

Key Issues: Included in this Policy and Regulation:
搂 A school may consider adopting a focus initiative in response to a demonstrated need or desire to enhance student academic experience.
搂 A focus initiative must involve all students attending the school.
搂 Schools may request funds to support staff development, resources and activities related to the focus initiative.
搂 A proposal, accompanied by a letter of support from the faculty, will be submitted to the Assistant Superintendent of the particular Cluster and the Assistant Superintendent of Instructional Services for approval.
搂 If approved, a focus school committee will be established that will include school personnel, parents, and students.
搂 Community information meetings will be held and families will be given the opportunity to provide written feedback.
搂 Focus goals will be developed and included in the annual school improvement plan.
搂 The Focus Committee will develop a final proposal that includes evidence of school-community support for submission to the Cluster Assistant Superintendent, who will then present the proposal to the Leadership Team for approval.
搂 The Leadership Team will submit any approved Focus School proposal to the School Board.
搂 Any Focus School will seek reauthorization every four years.

School Board Discussion/Decision: Decision to forward policy for formal Board adoption.

Staff Recommendation: Adopt the focus school policy which authorizes the regulation to provide a procedure for schools to develop unique initiatives that meet the needs and desires of their communities. Provide funding to support focus initiatives.

Options: Do not adopt the policy as written.
