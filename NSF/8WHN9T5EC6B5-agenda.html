RESOLUTION

NAMING THE SOFTBALL FIELD AT

ROBINSON SECONDARY SCHOOL

“THE BARRY GORODNICK SOFTBALL FIELD”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Coach Barry Gorodnick began his career in Fairfax County Public Schools at Robinson Secondary School in 1988 and was the coach of the girls’ softball team, an assistant coach of the football team, a security assistant, a physical education teacher, and a school-based technology specialist; and

 

     WHEREAS, Coach Gorodnick, or “Coach G” as he was known, dedicated his time and energy to nurture the students at Robinson and encourage them to reach their full potential to accomplish their goals in school, in sports, and in life; he was an extraordinary person, who listened to his students, instinctively offered fatherly advice, and helped his colleagues in whatever task needed to be done; and

 

     WHEREAS, his 24-year career record in girls’ softball was 323 wins and 184 losses, three district titles, and one region title; under his leadership, student athletes have thrived and the softball program at Robinson has grown into a strong, competitive program of which the Robinson community is proud;  and

 

     WHEREAS, he was a mentor and father figure to a countless number of softball players; his natural ability to connect with the students was phenomenal; he knew his athletes’ individual stories and inspired them to do their best even when the odds were stacked against them; he devoted his time to prepare the softball field before a game and recruited his family’s support during games, whether it was assisting with the score boards, providing snacks for the players, or cheering the team on; and

 

     WHEREAS, Coach G was an alumnus of Robinson Secondary School, class of 1983, and was a beloved member of the Robinson community for 30 years; the naming of the softball field at Robinson in his honor will be an enduring remembrance of Coach G’s love for life and his relentless dedication to service for all those in the school and in the community who will use the facility for years to come;

 

     NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, in recognition of Barry Gorodnick’s contributions and dedicated service to the students, faculty, and families of Fairfax County Public Schools, hereby names in his memory the softball field at Robinson Secondary School, “The Barry Gorodnick Softball Field.”

Recommended Action
Approve the Strategic Facilities Plan, as developed by the Facilities Planning Advisory Council, and as detailed in the agenda item (FTS; presented as new business 11/8/12)

Staff Contact:           Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

 

Meeting Category:   November 19, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 8

 

Subject:                    Fairfax County Public Schools, Strategic Facilities Plan (SFP)

 

School Board Action Required:   Decision

 

Background:

 

The School Board has charged the Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) with developing and updating annually a long-term strategic plan for FCPS facility needs for presentation to and approval by the School Board.  The Strategic Facilities Plan (SFP) was initially presented in draft and discussed with the Board as part of FPAC’s June 2012 annual report.  At the School Board work session on September 10, 2012, FPAC presented their work entitled “Fairfax County Public Schools, Strategic Facilities Plan”, in compliance with their School Board charge.  Board discussion at the September 10 work session identified several issues with the SFP that required clarification and possible revision.

 

Key Points:

 

At its October 16, 2012, meeting, FPAC approved the attached revised SFP for submission to the School Board for approval.  The primary changes in the Plan are (1) changing the "goals" to "guidelines" and inserting language to emphasize that this is a set of guidelines to be used in the decision-making process, (2) removal of "strategies and objectives" that were to be created in the next SFP, and (3) a few minor editorial changes.  It should be noted that FPAC approval of the SFP as revised was not unanimous.  Some FPAC members are concerned that this revised document does not allow School Board endorsement of discreet criteria for making informed, prioritized resource allocation decisions.  However, FPAC recognizes that School Board approval of the revised SFP is an important step and is critical to the future work of FPAC in bringing recommendations to the Board.

 

The SFP is the culmination of an effort that began over two years ago which included community outreach that consisted of:  ‘Listening Tours’ undertaken for each school cluster, an FPAC day long retreat which included community members, ‘Listening Tours’ specific to the development of the SFP and a community blog.  The Plan provides a focus on the key areas of communication, capacity and space use, operations and maintenance, and capital improvement. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Strategic Facilities Plan, as developed by the Facilities Planning Advisory Council, and as detailed in the agenda item.
