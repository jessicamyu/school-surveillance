Food Allergy Awareness Week - May 9-15, 2021

 

Created in 1998 by the Food Allergy Research & Education Inc., Food allergy awareness week shines a spotlight on the seriousness of food allergies and helps to improve public understanding of this potentially life-threatening medical condition. By increasing awareness, we can encourage respect, promote safety, and improve the quality of life of all those affected by food allergies and anaphylaxis.

Food allergies affect as many as 32 million Americans, including 6 million children, approximately two in every classroom. Every three minutes, a severe food allergy reaction sends someone to an emergency room in the U.S.  Allergic reactions to food can range from mild symptoms to anaphylaxis, a serious allergic reaction that can occur rapidly and is potentially life-threatening. You can learn more about food allergies and anaphylaxis by visiting the Food Allergy Research and Education website at www.foodallergy.org

Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Month - May 2021

The Adult and Community Education (ACE) program of Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) began with an action by the School Board in 1955 and, in the over 60 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills. In addition, the Fairfax County Adult High School has given adults the opportunity to earn a high school diploma, or certification of high school equivalency through the GED program.

 

Through the years, the ACE program has provided adult students with the opportunity to learn English, learn new languages, acquire job skills, transition to new careers, and grow as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to ACE to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education and the Fairfax County Adult High School programs continued to provide innovative learning opportunities for adults during the pandemic and are prepared to create robust career pathway opportunities for FCPS graduates and the whole community.


Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner, as well as providing enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day, ACE and the Fairfax County Adult High School programs have helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

Asian American and Pacific Islander Heritage Month

 

WHEREAS, Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders make up 20 percent of the population in Fairfax County according to the US Census Bureau including 35,000 students in Kindergarten through twelfth grade; and,

 

WHEREAS, Asian Americans established their first settlement in 1763 and over time have endured such injustices as the Asian Exclusion Act that prohibited immigration from Asia and the Japanese Internment that established concentration camps that incarcerated Asian U.S. citizens from 1942 until 1946; and,

 

WHEREAS, Asian American and Pacific Islander students and their families reflect the rich and vibrant diversity of cultures, histories, and languages of the many different countries of Asia and the Pacific Islands, and are valued members of our community; and

 

WHEREAS, Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders have made significant contributions in areas of infrastructure, economy, and technology that have propelled our country and have played a pivotal role in America’s history; and

 

WHEREAS, Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders have achieved great accomplishments in fields of math, science, education, the arts, and civil rights that benefit past, current and future generations; and

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board joins Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders in celebrating May as 2021 Asian American and Pacific Islander Heritage Month, in recognition of the many contributions Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders have made to our society.

Subject: Lease Renewal and Expansion-Fairfax County Adult High School (FCAHS) and Consolidation of Other FCPS Instructional and Services Programming in the Herndon/Reston Area

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

Mark Greenfelder, assistant superintendent, School Improvements and Supports

Michelle Boyd, assistant superintendent, Special Services

Lisa Williams, chief equity officer

Kathleen Walts, executive director, Professional Learning and Family Engagement

Ignite Link:  Goal 1: Student Success

Summary/Background:

 

Staff recommends the School Board continue and expand the existing lease at the Herndon Centre III shopping complex or any other suitable lease location within available funding resources for the Fairfax County Adult High School (FCAHS) and consolidate other instructional and services programs, specifically:

  • Transition Support Resource Center (TSRC)
  • Adult and Community Education (ACE)
  • ACE-English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL)
  • Community Welcome Center to include Student Registration, ESOL Assessment and Community Liaison Services

Currently, these programs are operating in the Herndon/Reston area in undersized spaces that unintentionally cap enrollment and create inequity of service delivery of these programs with like programs offered in other parts of Fairfax County.

It may now be in the best interest of the School Board to enter a new lease that would:

  • Capitalize on favorable lease rates available in the commercial real estate market today that “stretch” buying power and permit the rental of additional space at a much reduced per square foot cost.
  • Enable further consolidation of FCAHS, TSRC, ACE, ACE-ESOL, Student Registration, ESOL Assessment and Community Liaison Services programming in the Herndon/Reston area.
  • Provide an appropriate classroom learning and training environment through newly expanded and designed spaces.
  • Co-location of a Welcome Center with instructional programming to provide students and families with “one-stop shopping” opportunities for student registration, ESOL assessments and connections to other community services.

Staff Contact: Sloan J. Presidio, Chief Academic Officer

 

Other Staff Present:

Carrie Reynolds, coordinator, Health, Physical, Family Life, and Driver Education, Office of PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

Noel Klimenko, director, PreK-12 Curriculum & Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category:  May 6, 2021- Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In accordance with regulation 1708, the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee (FLECAC) convenes each school year with the purpose of advising the K-12 health and physical education and FLE curriculum coordinator on Program of Studies development and implementation. The role of the committee is to provide advice and recommendations to the curriculum coordinator as goals, objectives, media, and special instructional materials are developed.  FLECAC recommendations require approval by the School Board following a 30-day community review and comment period.  FLECAC includes members representing students, teachers, administrators, and community members (including School Board appointees, health professionals, and clergy). 

Following guidelines from the Virginia Board of Education, FCPS implements a locally developed program of instruction for Family Life Education (FLE).  A community involvement team must be used in the development of the program.  The Virginia Board of Education Guidelines and Standards of Learning for Family Life Education are used by FLECAC as a reference for recommendations of lesson objectives and descriptive statements.  Community members may also bring forward recommendations and concerns for consideration by FLECAC. In addition, some topics including but not limited to benefits, challenges, responsibilities, and value of marriage; dating violence; sexual harassment; and boundaries and consent, are legislated and must be included in FLE instruction.  FLECAC receives input from medical professionals, law enforcement, faith community representatives, and Fairfax County Government offices as appropriate when developing recommendations.

During SY2019-2020, FLECAC focused on aligning FCPS objectives with Virginia Department of Education recommendations for Family Life Education at the early elementary and middle school levels, adding a new objective to the modified FLE lessons for students with low incidence disabilities in grades 1-6 and 7-12, and adding resources to support instruction.  The committee made six recommendations for School Board consideration:

  1. Revise descriptive statement for kindergarten lesson on families to be more inclusive of different family structures.
  2. Revise descriptive statement for grade 1 lesson on families to be more inclusive of different family structures.
  3. Revise descriptive statement for grade 7 lesson to remove depression and suicide as these topics are now included in the 2020 SOLs for Health Education at grade 7.
  4. Add an objective to modified FLE grades 1-6 related to safety when using technology.
  5. Add an objective to modified FLE grades 7-12 related to appropriate workplace behaviors.
  6. Add media to grade 8 to support instruction on sexting.

 

During SY2020-2021, FLECAC discussed concerns brought forward by community members including revision of lesson descriptive statements in grades 7, 8, and 10.  The committee made three recommendations for School Board consideration:

  1. Revised descriptive statement for grade 7 lesson on reproductive systems to include primary amenorrhea.
  2. Revised descriptive statement for grade 8 lesson on anatomy and reproduction to include primary and secondary amenorrhea.
  3. Revised descriptive statement for grade 10 lesson on unplanned pregnancy to be more sensitive to families who have adopted a child.

 

 

Recommendation: Recommendation to approve recommended media and lesson objectives as detailed in the agenda item and as reviewed and recommended by the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee (new business scheduled 505/06/2021, action scheduled 06/17/2021).
