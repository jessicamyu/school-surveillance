Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  April 22, 2013 - Work Session No. 106

 

Subject:                  Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) Quarterly Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

The Facilities Planning Advisory Council reports on its activities to the School Board on a quarterly basis as required by its charter.

 

Key Points:

 

Highlights of the Council’s efforts during this reporting period include: the creation of several work groups to work concurrently on multiple issues related to facilities; work with School Board Members and FCPS staff on the Capital Improvement Program; attendance of several members at meetings for the Fairfax/Lanier Boundary Study to identify potential improvements to the Boundary process; exploring non-traditional funding sources to enhance capital financing for FCPS facilities; and a brainstorming session to generate ideas on how to accommodate the projected growth in school population.

 

In addition, the Council’s work over the quarter resulted in several recommendations which are summarized below:

  • School Board revisit funding for facilities operations and maintenance, beginning with the 2013-2014 budget.
  • FCPS staff work with FPAC green and finance Work Group to review ‘179D’ and QECP programs as potential funding sources.
  • FCPS staff update and revise the proffer formula to fully recover costs of construction and bring it to the School Board for approval.
  • FCPS staff explore funding from developers in previously approved PRC zoning districts where re-development will increase school enrollments.

Members met with Fairfax County Planning Commission’s School Committee in both January and March and a meeting in April is scheduled.  In addition, FPAC is represented at the Superintendent’s Business and Community Advisory Council monthly meetings. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Facilities Planning Advisory Council April 2013 Quarterly Report.
