Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month - May 2008

Americans of Asian and Pacific ancestry have made significant contributions to the history and development of the United States in many fields of endeavor such as science, commerce, the arts, and the humanities. The Fairfax County School Board recognizes that an understanding of these contributions should be an integral part of the year-round education of all students.

The President of the United States proclaims May to be Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month and has chosen this month in recognition of the work done by Chinese immigrants on the transcontinental railroad, which was completed on May 10, 1869.

Pride in America Month - May 2008

Our country’s founders–more than 200 years ago–established a nation of unique, diverse individuals working toward the common goal of a free and open society–a nation that has accomplished much in two centuries and of which we all can be proud. Thomas Jefferson understood that we, as a people, do have a common heritage in which we can and should, as individuals, develop pride and discipline, and Jefferson envisioned what our nation’s common heritage truly is when he wrote, “All men are created equal and are endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable rights."

Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., understood and believed this when he proclaimed, “I have a dream. . .” recognizing that the principles about which our country's founders wrote were and are the cornerstone of equality and freedom and that these principles form our nation’s common heritage for its people.

The Fairfax County School Board recognizes May as Pride in America Month and everyone in Fairfax County Public Schools expresses strong support and deep gratitude to the men and women of our armed forces, and to the many Virginia-based loved ones of our military, including especially the children of military parents enrolled in Fairfax County Public Schools, for their courage, service, and sacrifice during this time

School Nurse Day - May 7, 2008

School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.

School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health and safety, intervene with actual and potential health problems, and provide case management services and are dedicated individuals who work in cooperation with families, teachers, and school administrators.

The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognizes May 7 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

Recognition of Teacher Appreciation Week - May 5-9, 2008

Every day over 13,500 teachers in Fairfax County Public Schools demonstrate their personal and professional dedication to providing an excellent education for the children in our community. Our teachers are committed to ensuring that all children develop their full potentials for academic achievement.

Teachers are influential in fostering in their students both healthy self-concepts and a sense of civic responsibility. Fairfax County Public Schools values the skills, creativity, and professionalism that characterize our teachers and we welcome this opportunity to commend our teachers and to recognize the energy, dedication, and vision that they contribute to our school system.

SUBJECT: Legislative Recommendations for the 2009 Policies and Resolutions of the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA)

TYPE: New Business

BACKGROUND:
      Each spring the VSBA Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee seeks recommendations from local school boards concerning new or revised legislative policies and resolutions to be considered for VSBA approval. The Committee reviews the local school boards’ recommendations along with other suggestions for changes to the existing policies and resolutions. The VSBA takes action on the Committee’s report at the annual fall convention. Staff recently learned that position recommendations are due to VSBA May 7, 2008.

      This process allows the School Board to make recommendations for the VSBA legislative program and to receive statewide support for some of its own legislative positions. The Fairfax County School Board 2009 Legislative Program will be developed for adoption in the fall.

      Changes are proposed to VSBA positions regarding limited English proficient students, the federal Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 (No Child Left Behind), and state funding of education.

      The recommended changes are all either directly or indirectly based on policy positions the School Board has already adopted in its own 2008 Legislative Program.
OPTIONS:

That the School Board accept the legislative recommendations to be forwarded to the Virginia School Boards Association Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee for their consideration, as detailed in the agenda item.

      Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

      SUBJECT: Memorandum of Understanding

      TYPE: New Business
        BACKGROUND:
            On October 15, 2007, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors approved rezoning application RZ 2005-PR-041 (the "Rezoning") for the property located north of and adjacent to Luther Jackson MS to permit construction of a mixed-use development as part of the Merrifield Town Center core area. In connection with the Rezoning, the applicant, Merrifield, proffered to enter into a Memorandum of Understanding with the School Board pursuant to which Merrifield would construct approximately 64 parking spaces and certain ballfield improvements on the Luther Jackson property. The MOU also is to provide for the joint use of the 64 parking spaces by Merrifield, NAI and the School Board. NAI is the owner of a portion of the Rezoning property and will retain ownership of a portion after development of the mixed-use project. NAI has been included in the MOU to ensure access to and use of the affected portion of NAI's property to implement the MOU.

            The 64 parking spaces will be constructed by Merrifield at no cost to the School Board in a location along the common property line, adjacent to the existing ballfield area. This parking will be to support use of the ballfields and for patrons of Merrifield Town Center.

            The proffers provided two options for the ballfield improvements at Luther Jackson MS - one that included a contribution toward installation of a synthetic turf field and one that did not. The intent was to give the School Board and the Providence District Supervisor the option of electing a contribution toward installation of the synthetic turf field in the event sufficient other funds were available to complete installation of the turf.

            Merrifield and Staff have been negotiating the terms of the MOU. Staff recently met with the Providence District Supervisor and was advised that sufficient funds have been identified to complete installation of the synthetic turf at Luther Jackson Middle School.

            During the work to establish the new grade for and install the turf field, the existing fields would not be available for use by Luther Jackson Middle School for most of the 2008-2009 school year. However, the turf improvements will be a substantial long-term improvement for Luther Jackson Middle School and will be substantially funded by the private sector with this opportunity.

            Therefore, Staff recommends the School Board (i) approve the MOU substantially in the form attached to this Board item, (ii) authorize the Chief Operating Officer to execute the final form of the MOU, as approved by School Board counsel, and (iii) authorize the Chief Operating Officer and FCPS Staff to implement the provisions of the MOU, including, without limitation, notifying Merrifield and NAI that the School Board elects the "turf field" alternative and wishes to accept cash-in-lieu of the field improvements to be applied to installation of the turf field.
        KEY POINTS:
            The MOU implements the rezoning proffers the Board of Supervisors approved on October 15, 2007, with the Merrifield Town Center rezoning (RZ 2005-PR-041).

            Merrifield will be contributing more than $45,000 in cash and significant grading and seeding work to facilitate installation of synthetic turf fields at Luther Jackson Middle School. The necessary work will be coordinated to minimize, to the extent possible, disruption of regular school activities at Luther Jackson Middle School. However, the existing fields likely will not be available for a good part of the 2008-2009 school year. The synthetic turf fields are expected to be available in the fall of 2009. The new turf fields will be better with greatly improved access over the existing fields.

            At no cost to the School Board, Merrifield will construct approximately 64 parking spaces on the Luther Jackson property along the northwest property line to be shared between users of the athletic fields and patrons of the adjacent Merrifield Town Center development. This will include installation of a gated access to the parking lot controlled by FCPS during school hours. The parking lot will be maintained by Merrifield, at Merrifield's expense.

        RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board approve and authorize execution of a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between the School Board, Merrifield, and National Amusements Inc., consistent with the approved rezoning proffers for the Merrifield Town Center property, adjacent to Luther Jackson Middle School, as detailed in the agenda item.

            The funding application estimated revenue for Part B monies should yield approximately $30,646,734 to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS), same as the final award for FY 2008. The VDOE has informed the Office of Special Education that, for this year, FCPS is not required to maintain a 15 percent set-aside for early intervention programs. As a result, 84.0 positions previously funded by Part B funds to support full-day kindergarten will go to the regular operating fund, and 91.0 new special education teachers and instructional assistants required due to projected membership increases will be funded by Part B, resulting in a 7.0 FTE increase in positions within the grant, bringing the total positions within the grant to 453.0 FTE.

            The Section 619, Preschool Grant Funds, actual entitlement is based on a count of students ages three through five taken on December 1, 2007. These funds ($820,196) continue to support 6.0 FTE preschool positions, ChildFind activities, parenting and transition services, and social work services for preschool-age students.

        RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board approve the 2008-2009 annual special education plan, part B funding application and Section 619 Preschool Grant Application.
