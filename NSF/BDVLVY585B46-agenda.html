Summary/Background:

 

On March 7, 2019, the School Board approved a streamlined Strategic Plan with eight areas of focus for school years 2018-2020. These areas of focus have specific strategic actions to move the Division toward its goals. Each strategic action has targeted metrics to show the Division’s current baseline data, an interim target to achieve by school year 2020, and an aspirational statement that describes what success would look like for students, families, employees, and the community.

 

This report describes actions and outcomes from SY 2018-19, related to Goal 2 of the Strategic Plan, Caring Culture, which details FCPS’ commitment to championing a welcoming environment and healthy life choices for students and staff. It represents one of the four goals (one for each goal area) that will be presented this fall.

 

All goal reports provide information about actions FCPS undertook during the year to support achievement of its aspirations, outcome data on School Board approved metrics, ROI analyses couched within a Strategic Decision-Making Framework and Future Considerations related to the results from the reporting year. The Goal 2 Caring Culture report addresses performance on indicators of welcoming environments and health life choices, specifically, those related to the following four aspirations:

  • All students, families, and staff will feel respected and included at school
  • All staff will view student behavior through a culturally responsive lens
  • All students will report healthy social-emotional skills and lifestyle behaviors on the Fairfax County Youth Survey
  • All schools will have low rates of students with frequent absences
  • No students will have disruptive behavior referrals
  • All employees will have the opportunity to take advantage of wellness resources

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 2: Caring Culture report as detailed in the agenda item.

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Governance Committee Proposed Forum Process

A. The School Board Forum process should be officially embedded into our Regular SB Meeting Agenda, and renamed “Board Matters”.

B. What now is called “Board Matters” will be referred to as “Final Remarks” or “Board Member Updates” (similar to the “Superintendent Updates”.

C. Board members should submit their “Board Matters “ topics (and brief, written summaries) to the Board Chair and the Board Clerk at least 48 hours in advance of the Regular Business Meeting, unless a topic is time-sensitive. This written proposal will be posted to Boarddocs, and at minimum emailed to the full Board, the Superintendent and Deputy Superintendent.

D. The proposed “Board Matters” will be placed on the Business Agenda, right after “Action Items”.

E. Approximately 1 hour will be allotted for “Board Matters” (with a maximum of 2 topics per meeting) unless the Board votes to extend this portion of the Business Agenda.

 

Rationale:

For more than 7 years, the School Board has experienced persistent challenges in consistently holding its Forum meetings due to the existing time-constraints of the Closed Session and the Regular Business Meeting. Having discussed and reviewed the success of the Board of Supervisors’ Board Matter process, it is recommended that the School Board adopt a similar approach.
