Staff Contact:            Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)                   

 

Other Staff Present:  Craig Herring, director, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

                                  Carol Horn, coordinator, Advanced Academic Programs, Office of PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category:     May 30, 2013 – Work Session No. 119

 

Subject:                     Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee Annual Report        

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                          

 

Related To:               Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees Report to School Board

 

Key Points:

 

For the 2012-13 school year the School Board charged the committee to:

 

“Assess the range of program opportunities available in FCPS for AAP in the secondary schools.  Include assessment of programs and best practices used including accelerated content, differentiated instruction beyond the classroom (internships, work study, student exchange programs) and special schools such as the academies.”

 

This year proved to be challenging with a bifurcated agenda of trying to be responsive to the rapid pace of the "AAP Expansion Proposal” and meeting the charge given to the AAPAC by the School Board.   AAPAC responded to the AAP Expansion Proposal in December and February, while continuing to work on the School Board charge.  AAPAC as a committee submitted comments to the School Board regarding the AAP Expansion Proposal.  It is important to note that the comments from AAPAC reflected a majority of the committee but not unanimity.  We did find that there was some overlap in the work done in support of the charge and in support of the broader discussion surrounding the AAP Expansion Proposal. 

 

Advanced Academic Advisory Committee members have made the following recommendations:

  • Recommendation: Continue to encourage AAP students to become aware of and take advantage of the rigorous opportunities available through AP courses and exams.
  • Recommendation: Support the introduction of IBMYP, where there is community support, for those high schools that offer IB diploma programs and do not currently have an IBMYP program at its feeder middle schools.
  • Recommendation: More advanced mathematics options should be made available for IB students in secondary school.
  • Recommendation: In addition to the current dual enrollment offerings, FCPS should offer dual enrollment options for higher-level world languages.
  • Recommendation: Focus on increasing awareness for middle school students and middle school and high school counselors about the opportunities available and the need for advance planning for CTE courses. 
  • Recommendation:  For the purpose of focusing on the advanced academic opportunities of CTE courses, it would be useful to identify students in IB or AP programs who have taken CTE courses to speak to middle school and elementary students transitioning to middle school about their experience and to prepare specialized brochure and website content to stress the academic opportunities offered by CTE courses and the Academies. 
  • Recommendation:  FCPS consider creating additional, geographically dispersed Governor’s Academies in Fairfax. 
  • Recommendation:  FCPS take a holistic approach to its language programs from elementary through secondary schools.
  • Recommendation:  Offer greater differentiation of instruction (an AAP best practice for classroom strategies and instruction) for World Language courses including honors levels and possibly a dual enrollment option in High School.  At the high levels, the courses should be considered as an honors level course. 
  • Recommendation: Offer parents additional opportunities to learn about the Naviance planning tool and be made aware of instances when students are using the tool as part of their curriculum planning. 
  • Recommendation: Widely communicate opportunities to students at all high schools and encourage them to take advantage of the presence of college fairs and visiting admissions counselors. 
  •  
  • Recommendation:  Work with business partners to develop more opportunities that allow students to explore different career options. 
     
  • Recommendation:  Increase teacher training and resources for differentiation to support open enrollment in Honors classes.
     
  • Recommendation:  In Mason District, students must be specifically and deliberately considered when planning secondary education curricula. It is necessary to begin helping these students prepare for their secondary education at 5th grade. 
  • Recommendation:  FCPS inform all 5th grade students and parents receiving advanced mathematics services about the role of standardized testing (SOLs and IAAT) in mathematics placement for secondary education. 
  • Recommendation:  FCPS offer a differentiated, advanced approach to language that allows advanced students who are gifted in languages to accelerate their study through honors level classes.  We also recommend that Mason District AAP students be encouraged to advance their language training by offering the 7th grade language curriculum to the 6th graders entering middle school. 
  • Recommendation:  Offer the opportunity for all elementary students to take an additional year of orchestra or band (offering these programs one year earlier) or adding a separate 6th grade music option for the Mason District Middle schools allowing these students to develop their musical appreciation on a consistent basis with their peers at other schools in the county.
     
  • Recommendation:  Develop a database of what after school/enrichment programs are available at the different schools so that parents and students can be made aware of the range of activities that are possible.

                                 Scott Campbell, Co-Chair,  Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities

 

Meeting Category:    May 30, 2013 - Work Session No. 119

 

Subject:                   Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report (2013)

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points: 

 

School Board Annual Charge Subcommittee-Diploma Options

1.  The subcommittee recommends the charge be carried over to the 2013-14 school  year in order for the ACSD to research and review current

     options and to provide guidance on implementation and/or clarification of current credit accommodations for students with disabilities.

 

Bullying Prevention Subcommittee

1.  FCPS  require all administrators acknowledge reading the 2010 Dear Colleague Letter issued by Office of Civil Rights U.S. Department of Education.

2.  FCPS issue and widely disseminate clear regulations and procedures regarding disability harassment and bullying similar to those currently existing for sexual harrassement.

3.  FCPS establish a timeline for all relevant staff to complete the FCPS Restorative Justice training program and implement its practices.

4.  FCPS:

            a. Create a blog for school administrators to share ideas and programming that they have found to be effective in addressing bullying in their schools;

            b. Include at least one session in its Annual Special Education Conference addressing a bullying prevention topic;

            c. Develop an award for social inclusion and anti-bullying to be awarded to a school at the Annual Special Education Conference.

 

Transportation Subcommittee

1.  FCPS train teachers and administrators on IEP development that emphasizes the need to look more carefully at transportation needs.

2.  FCPS teachers and administrators should involve the Office of Transportation Services in IEP and 504 meetings if the nature of the child's needs would affect the child's transportation.

3.  FCPS educate parents about transportation as a special education service, even if the transportation is provided on a general education bus.

4.  FCPS link the databases for health information, IEPs, and transportation and provide appropriate access to staff members based on roles.

 

Literacy Subcommittee

1.  FCPS create a large scale literacy instruction awareness campaign for families and staff to include navigating the IEP process for reading instruction, what options FCPS offers for reading

     instruction and intervention, and how to incorporate reading instruction and intervention in the school day.

2.  FCPS provide on-line literacy/dyslexia trainings with an emphasis on meeting the needs of individual students for all teachers, administrators, counselors, school psychologists, PSLs, and

     social workers.

3.  FCPS ensure teachers register and enter required progress monitoring information on a timely basis for each reading program, as prescribed by the

     program manual for every student in the program such as Vport for Language!  and ReadWell.  Teachers will use the results of the data entered to

     adjust instruction, monitor and report progress to parents throughout the school year.

4.  FCPS develop or purchase a simple universal reading assessment for screening K-3 students.  Screening results will help teachers and administrator

     place potentially struggling readers in the appropriate early reading intervention programs.

 

Recommendation:

 

The School Board accept the Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities school year 2012-2013 annual report.

Subject:                     Student Rights and Responsibilities, Regulation 2601.27P

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:                Legal Requirement 

 

Key Points:

 

Regulation 2601.27P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, has been rewritten and reflects consideration of the Ad Hoc Community Committee on Student Rights and Responsibilities recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board. Major proposed revisions to the SR&R include:

 

1. Preamble with overarching philosophy

2. Reduced number of offenses which carry mandated consequence 

3. Differentiated range of consequences for elementary and secondary students 

4. Emphasis on positive behavior approach

5. Highlighted commitment to collaboration with parents to prevent and address behavior concerns

6. Provide option to recommend for reassignment to an alternative program, when appropriate, instead of recommend for expulsion. This allows principal to accurately reflect their recommendation, and avoids creating anxiety for parent that student may be removed without services.

7. Authorized use of restorative conferences, alcohol and other drug intervention seminar, and restorative behavior intervention seminar and other interventions as possible alternatives to suspension

8. Provides paper review of first time possession of marijuana, shortening process and avoiding hearing for students for whom there is clear evidence of special circumstances. Provides for hearing to consider additional input to determine if there are special circumstances 

9. Reflects change in state law reducing number of weapons require mandatory recommendation for expulsion 

10.  Informs parents of limits of role of SRO in investigating disciplinary infractions

11. Includes section explaining procedural protections for students with disabilities. 

12. Includes notice that written statement is voluntary.

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.27P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.
