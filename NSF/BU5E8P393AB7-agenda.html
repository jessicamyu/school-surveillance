Subject:               2021 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact:      Michael Molloy, director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:        October 20, 2020 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link:          Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options; sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) from the Board’s 2020 legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.

 

The following are legislative position areas from the Board’s 2020 State and Federal Legislative Program -- referenced by 2020 Position Item number -- where specific state or federal policy changes directly related to Board positions and/or priorities have occurred over the past year:

 

Objectives Achieved – existing position can be deleted (with reference to 2020 Position Item number)

  • Dual Enrollment and Work Based Learning Options (HB 516/SB112) - T.7 Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning
  • School Safety Audits (HB 501) - Z.9 Student Safety

 

Objectives at least Partially Achieved – existing position may be deleted or changed

  • Enhanced staffing for English Learners (HB30 – Budget Bill) – U.7 Standards of Quality
  • “Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education” (SLIFE) (SB 933) - Board Priority Position/I.7 English Learners
  • Solar Energy Net Metering Cap (HB 572/HB 1184/HB 1526/HB 710/HB 851) - Board Priority Position, J.1 Environmental Sustainability/Renewable Energy
  • Collective Bargaining (HB 582/SB 939) - H.1 Employer Employee Relations
  • County Taxing Authority (HB 785/SB 588) - P.1 Revenue Alternatives
  • Culturally Relevant and Inclusive Education Practices Advisory Committee (HB 916/SB 853) - T.5 Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning
  • Equal Rights Amendment (HJ 1/SJ 1) - K.1 Equal Rights

 

Full strikethrough/underline draft text with all staff proposed changes, as well as a table detailing each staff proposed change will be provided prior to the October 20th Work Session.  Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 5, 2020.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2021 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program.

Subject: FY 2021 First Quarter Report

 

Staff Contact: Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category: October 20, 2020 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Summary/Background:

As a result of the coronavirus pandemic, FCPS’ revenue outlook has been continuously monitored. Since the adoption of the FY21 Approved Budget, the following activity has occurred:

 

On August 18, 2020, Governor Northam convened Special Session I of the 2020 General Assembly and introduced an amended 2020-2022 biennial budget, including an updated revenue forecast, which affects public education in Virginia.

 

On September 15, 2020, the County Board of Supervisors allocated $19.6 million in federal Coronavirus Relief Funds to FCPS to support safe and equitable instruction for all students.

 

On October 8, 2020, Governor Northam announced a new allocation of federal Coronavirus Aid, Recovery, and Economic Security (CARES) Act dollars to help K-12 public schools in Virginia. FCPS’ allocation, including the City of Fairfax totals $32.2 million. It is important to note that the eligibility period for this funding is through December 30, 2020.

 

As a result of the changes in federal and state funding, combined with the cost associated with FCPS’ response to the pandemic, a quarterly report of the FY21 revenue and expenditure budgets will be provided.
