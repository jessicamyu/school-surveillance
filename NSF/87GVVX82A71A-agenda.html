[Revised 7/14/11]

Staff Contact:         Jack D. Dale, Superintendent                                                                                                         

 

Meeting Category:  July 14, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                  Request for Waiver Related to Standards of Learning (SOL) Assessments and Testing Schedules

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision 

                                      

Related To:              Student Achievement Goals

 

Key Points:

 

Superintendents from the public schools of the counties of Albemarle, Fairfax, Henrico, Roanoke, and the city of Virginia Beach are requesting the following waivers from the Virginia Board of Education:

  1. Request a waiver to allow SOL tests to be administered in middle schools during the January Non-Writing test administration to students who have demonstrated that they are well ahead in their learning although they will not complete classes until the spring semester beginning with grade 8 reading and mathematics in 2011-12 and expanding to grades 6 through 8 reading and mathematics in 2012-13.
  2. Request a waiver to allow for retesting during the spring Non-Writing test administration of middle school students who have received additional instruction and, where necessary, intensive remediation after failing grades 6 through 8 Reading, Mathematics, and Plain English Mathematics SOL tests taken in January.
  3. Request a waiver to allow for retakes in June for all middle school students who fail grades 6 through 8 Reading, Mathematics, and Plain English Mathematics SOL tests in May.  (Both of these administrations would occur during the regular spring Non-Writing test administration windows.)
  4. Request a waiver to allow the inclusion of passing scores from all administrations of grades 6 through 8 Reading, Mathematics, and Plain English Mathematics SOL tests and retests, with passing scores overriding previous failing scores in the same school year, for Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) and Accreditation calculations.

 

Each of our school divisions is committed to preparing all of our students to graduate from high school having mastered the skills necessary to succeed as 21st century learners, workers, and citizens. Our successful attainment of this goal is grounded in building our students’ capacity with respect to the 21st century skills that are critical to their future success. These skills include, but are not limited to: critical and creative thinking, problem solving, collaboration, communication, social responsibility, and interdependence.

 

Our goal is to mirror the philosophy of mastery learning  in our assessment practices. In order to accomplish this, the assessment process must be more transparent to students and more related to students’ out-of-school experiences.  It is our belief that a more flexible assessment system, including opportunities for early test administrations as well as opportunities to retake tests not passed, will make the process more meaningful to students; and that this shift will encourage students to become more engaged in the development of personal learning plans that are focused on growth toward standards and future success.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Request for Waiver and Alternative Accreditation Plan to the Virginia Board of Education, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                      FY 2011 Final Budget Review           

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                                                         

 

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2011 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2011 Third Quarter Estimate. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the FY 2012 and FY 2013 budgeted beginning balances as included in the FY 2012 Approved Budget, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the funds available are $55.4 million.

 

FY 2011 actual revenue was $1.5 million lower than the estimate or 99.9 percent of the FY 2011 Third Quarter Estimate.  State aid is $0.7 million and sales tax projections are $3.4 more than projected.  A decrease of $11.7 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $13.5 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2012,offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of an increase of $1.2 million in Impact Aid.  The decreases in federal revenue are offset by increases of $6.2 million in tuition, fees, and other revenue.

 

After accounting for the flexibility reserve, unexpended grants, and other commitments, FY 2011 actuaI expenditures were $56.9 million, or 2.5 percent, lower than the FY 2011 Third Quarter Estimate.  Greater vacancy and turnover and continued efforts to conserve resources and achieve efficiencies resulted in these savings.  The FY 2012 budget has incorporated a recognition of higher turnover through a decrease in the salary base and includes an increase in budgeted lapse.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item, including setting aside $4.4 million for a centralized textbook fund and allocating the remaining FY 2011 ending balance from the school operating fund of $51.0 million for the FY 2013 beginning balance.

6.03 Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology - Recommendation to accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2011-2012 (Cluster III; action scheduled 7/28/11)
Meeting
Jul 14, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 1
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:            Dan Parris, assistant superintendent, Cluster III

 

Meeting Category:  July 14, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                     Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.  The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

  • Additional state funding for the regional school.  The estimate for FY 2012 is $2,187,275.  The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $3,281,167.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative. 

 

Options:  The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2011-2012.

Contact:                     Kathy L. Smith, Chairman

 

Meeting Category:    July 14, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                      2011-2012 Charges to School Board Advisory Committees

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related To:                School Board Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board


Key Points:

 

At its June 9, 2011, Forum work session, the Board discussed Advisory Committee charges for the 2011-2012 school year. School Board member committee liaisons have discussed the proposed charges with each advisory committee (either the chairman, co-chairs, or committee members) and the charges are recommended for approval as follows:

 

Adult and Community Education Advisory Committee (ACE)

 

Examine the organization and curriculum alignment between K-12 and ACE programs and identify opportunities for increased collaboration and synergy in supporting the Student Achievement Goals and increasing post-secondary workforce opportunities.  Assess current marketing to the general and workforce communities by ACE programs with particular reference to course accessibility, Student Achievement Goals, Adult High School completion, family literacy, Adult ESOL, workforce skills and economic advancement, and explore methods to increase enrollment.

 

Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee (AAPAC)

 

Review and evaluate how FCPS is meeting the needs of twice exceptional students (K-12) in advanced academic classes and provide recommendations for improvement.

 

Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD)

 

Examine the current literacy instruction for students with disabilities in FCPS across all disabilities.  Explore the current initiatives, proposed development of resources, and the overall needs of the system to include professional development for staff and dissemination of information to parents.

 

Career and Technical Education Advisory Committee (CTEAC)

 

Explore and promote partnerships that enhance and expand Career and Technical Education programs for the mutual benefit of students and the greater business community.

 

District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program

 

Identify and distribute descriptions of strategies and resources that schools can utilize to overcome barriers to family involvement (e.g., language, culture, transportation, etc.)

 

Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC)

 

Develop and update annually a long-term strategic plan for FCPS facility needs for presentation to and approval by the School Board.  The strategic plan should reflect input from all interested stakeholders and constituencies.

 

FECEP/Head Start

 

Expand opportunities for effective communication and parent engagement using technology and social media such as auto-dialing and Facebook, increase parent understanding of how volunteer hours have a monetary value for the program (in-kind), connect tuition assistance for adult education to parent participation, and plan parent meetings based on parent preferences and needs to make them more accessible.

             

Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC)

 

Examine the minority student achievement gap from a human relations perspective, and then identify non-academic barriers that could be impacting the Fairfax County Public Schools efforts to close it.

 

Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee (MSAOC)

 

Ensure that school system efforts to close all aspects of the achievement gap for underrepresented minority students remain on course and that the new Priority Schools Initiative is making a meaningful contribution to these efforts.

 

School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC)

 

Examine the development of the Resiliency Project and other prevention and intervention services that address challenges faced by students.  Work with staff to develop a communication plan so that information about the Resiliency Project and protective factors is shared with parents and community members.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the charges for 2011-2012 for the following School Board Citizen Advisory Committees: Adult and Community Education, Students with Disabilities, Advanced Academic Programs, Career and Technical Education, District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program, Facilities Planning and Advisory Council, FECEP/Head Start, Human Relations, Minority Student Achievement Oversight, and School Health, as detailed in the agenda item.

6.10 Donation of the Crouch School Building to Fairfax County School Board to be located at the Liberty Middle School Site - Recommendation to accept the donation of the Crouch School Building and authorize the Division Superintendent or his designee, with the assistance of legal counsel, to negotiate and execute all related documents and agreements on behalf of the School Board (IS; action scheduled 7/28/11)
Meeting
Jul 14, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 1
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:            Peter Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department                                                                                   

 

Meeting Category:   July 14, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                      Donation of the Crouch School Building to Fairfax County School Board

 

 School Board Action Required:  Information     

 

 Related To:                School Board Policy 5961, Donations to School

                                   

 Key Points:

 

The Crouch School is a “One Room School House” that was used from 1874 through the mid 1930’s. The William A. Crouch family donated the land for the schoolhouse at the corner of Union Mill Road and Compton Road, Clifton, Virginia. The Old Crouch School was constructed in 1874 by Fairfax County.

           

To prevent the loss of this historically significant building, the Instructional Services Department (ISD) recommends that the School Board accept the donation of the Crouch School building from the current owner and relocate the building to a designated area at the Liberty Middle School site. The Office of Design & Construction Services will assist with the coordination of relocating the building to the Liberty Middle School site. ISD will assist with the instructional aspect of the historical school house. When this school house is moved to the Liberty site it will be used to support elementary and middle school instruction by serving to help provide a “day in the life” experience of what school was like in the 1800’s. This program will support Student Achievement Goals 1 and 3 with respect to civic education and historical competence.

 

Funding to relocate the school house will be provided by the local Clifton Community. In 2009, the Providence Chapter of the Daughters of the American Revolution (DAR) selected the Crouch School as its Regent’s Project. The DAR is actively accepting donations for the Crouch School. Additionally, the Clifton community continues to raise funds from local citizens and businesses for this project. Funding to move, restore, and maintain the building and operate it as a historical exhibit will be provided by DAR and the Clifton community. To date, approximately $105,000 has been raised for this purpose by the Clifton community. No FCPS funds are planned to be used for this project.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the donation of the Crouch School Building and authorize the Division Superintendent or his designee, with the assistance of legal counsel, to negotiate and execute all related documents and agreements on behalf of the School Board.
