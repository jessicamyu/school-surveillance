Native American Heritage Month - November 2009

What started at the turn of the century as an effort to gain a day of recognition for the significant contributions the first Americans made to the establishment and growth of the U.S., has resulted in a whole month being designated for that purpose.

In 1990 President George H. W. Bush approved a joint resolution designating November 1990 "National American Indian Heritage Month." Similar proclamations, under variants on the name (including "Native American Heritage Month" and "National American Indian and Alaska Native Heritage Month") have been issued each year since 1994. This year's heritage month theme is "Celebrating Tribal Nations: America's Great Partners."

NAMING THE GYMNASIUM AT

“THE CAROL A. MURPHY GYMNASIUM”

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the gymnasium at Braddock Elementary School “The Carol A. Murphy Gymnasium” in recognition of her love and support of children, her commitment to ensure a supportive learning environment, and her valued service to her community.

4.03 Award of Contract - Award the contract for custodial services for FCPS to J & H Maintenance Services Corporation, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board (FTS; presented as new business 9/24/09)
Meeting
Oct 22, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 6
Category

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Category: October 22, 2009 - Regular School Board Meeting No. 6

Subject: Award of Contract

School Board Action Required: Decision

Related To: School Board Policy 5011.2 Authority to Contract

Key Points:
      Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) seeks to establish a contract for custodial cervices for FCPS. Existing FCPS sites are currently serviced by contract custodians under a contract that is due to expire on October 31, 2009. FCPS must continue custodial services at these locations to maintain clean and safe working environments. The aggregate annual dollar amount of services performed under this contract will be more than $500,000.

      Proposals for this contract were received on June 23, 2009, from the following firms:

      American Facility Services
      Ameriklean, Inc
      Aramark Management Services, LP
      Associated Building Maintenance Company, Inc
      C.J. Maintenance, Inc
      Classic Building Services, Inc
      GCA Services Group
      J & H Maintenance Services Corporation
      L.T. Services, Inc
      Quality Building Maintenance, Inc
      Remedy Care Management, Inc
      SSC Service Solutions


      The Selection Advisory Committee has ranked the proposals and recommends J & H Maintenance Services Corporation be awarded the contract. This contractor has performed satisfactorily on previous contracts. Funding for the Custodial Services for the Administrative Centers is provided from the Operating Funds.

      At the Board’s request, staff has updated past cost comparison analyses of in-house versus contracted services. Due to the small size of sites in the proposed contract, minimal staffing requirements (on a per building basis), geographical distances between sites, and substantially lower labor rates for contracted employees, using in-house custodial staff would result in a yearly cost increase of $368,236 for the same services.
Recommendation:
      That the School Board award the contract for custodial services for FCPS to J & H Maintenance Services Corporation, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.

          Office of PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction

Meeting Category: October 22, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 6
Subject: Secondary Course Offerings

School Board Action Required: Information

Related To: Student Achievement Goal 1.

Key Points:
      The following courses were recommended by the Course Review Committee as additions to the list of high school standard course offerings for the 2010-11 school year:

      American Sign Language 4
      Background:
      The addition of this course will provide students the opportunity to continue developing their proficiency in ASL, thereby opening career opportunities and greater understanding of Deaf culture. There are no AP options in American Sign Language.

      Computer Graphics 4: Portfolio Preparation
      Background:
      The addition of this course will provide students with the opportunity to complete a four-year sequence in Computer Graphics and begin portfolio preparation. Currently, schools are providing this opportunity but through inconsistent course coding.

      Engineering Mathematics
      Background:
      Offered as a School-Site course SY 2009-2010, Engineering Mathematics prepares students to successfully advance through the freshman calculus sequence thereby reducing attrition rates in college engineering programs.

      Geographic Information Systems 1
      Background:
      The addition of this course will increase students’ awareness of and exposure to the emerging field of geotechnology, both as an area of study and as a career path.

      Geosystems Honors
      Background:
      Currently Geosystems has only one standard offering and is the only science course that does not have an honors equivalent. The Geosystems course may lose enrollments in favor of other weighted science options.

      Photography 4: Portfolio Preparation
      Background:
      The addition of this course will provide students with the opportunity to complete a four-year sequence in Photography and begin portfolio preparation. Currently, schools are providing this opportunity but through inconsistent course coding.


      Standard and optional courses for high schools and middle schools are reviewed each year, and new proposals are submitted to the Course Review Committee for review and recommendations to the Superintendent and School Board. This process is outlined in Regulation 3202.7, Approval of New Courses and Deletion and Consolidation of Existing Courses.
Recommendation:
      That the School Board approve changes to the list of middle and high school course offerings for the 2010-2011 school year, as recommended by the Course review Committee and as detailed in the agenda item.
