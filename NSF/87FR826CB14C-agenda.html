RESOLUTION

NAMING THE TRACK AT

VIRGINIA RUN ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

“THE TERESA HICKS TRACK”

 

 

WHEREAS,  Dr. Teresa Hicks began a career in Fairfax County Public Schools in 1990 and served as Virginia Run Elementary School principal from 1996 to 2010; and

 

WHEREAS,  Dr. Hicks provided leadership that led to such instructional initiatives as adopting core virtues of the basic school model for character education two years prior to the mandate and instituting quality instructional  practices, as outlined in the Success by Eight model; and

 

WHEREAS,  Dr. Hicks continued over the years to develop and improve the education of all students by launching and teaching  the Great Beginnings mentor program, updating instructional technology throughout the school, and championing a full inclusion program for special education; and

 

WHEREAS, she helped build a school-wide relationship with the PTA, which lead to a strong community partnership and provided for a second playground, SMART Boards throughout the building, and the 20th anniversary track; and

 

WHEREAS, Dr. Hicks retired from Fairfax County Public Schools in 2010 after 20 years of dedication to students across the county and specifically at Virginia Run Elementary School over the past 14 years;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED  that the Fairfax County School Board names the track at Virginia Run Elementary School “The Teresa Hicks Track” in recognition of Dr. Hicks’ years of dedication and service to the students, faculty, and families of Virginia Run Elementary School.

A Tribute to Eileen Grattan

 

In her 37 years as an educator, the last 20 of which were in service to Fairfax County Public Schools, Eileen Grattan has served in a variety of capacities, including English teacher, school-based administrator, Coordinator of Secondary Programs, and, most recently, Hearing Officer for the Superintendent.  Ms. Grattan has consistently approached her work with a dedication, professionalism, and singular focus on student achievement that has earned her the respect and admiration of her colleagues.  Ms. Grattan’s excellence was recognized when she was presented with the Teacher of the Year award in 1988 (by her colleagues in West Islip, New York), and then again when she was the recipient of the Robert R. Spillane Leadership Award in 2006.

 

As the Hearing Officer for the Superintendent, Ms. Grattan has worked with school principals, central office administrators, and the School Board to further the goals of the school system.  She has instilled in her staff a sense of service to our schools and fairness to our students.  In so doing, she not only played a central role in ensuring that all students are educated in safe and productive learning environments, but she also provided opportunities for each child who was referred to the Hearings Office to be educated in an environment suited to his or her needs.  Fairfax County Public Schools has benefitted greatly from the leadership exhibited by Ms. Grattan over the years.  She will be sorely missed—GOODNIGHT EILEEN!

5.03 Amendment 17 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)
Meeting
Dec 16, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 10 - Canceled
Category
5. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Approve Amendment 17 to the ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purposes of (1) incorporating rules required by recent amendments to the Internal Revenue Code, (2) conforming to anticipated administrative changes connected with the completion of PensionGold, and (3) clarifying and conforming to certain administrative rules, with no anticipated material effect on the benefits payable under the Plan, as detailed in the agenda item (HR; presented as new business 12/2/10)

Staff Contact:           Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category:    December 16, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 10

    

Subject:                    Amendment 17 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:                Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

In connection with the upcoming submission of the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan to the Internal Revenue Service for a determination that the Plan continues to be qualified for favorable tax treatment under the Internal Revenue Code, the ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending several amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purposes of (1) incorporating certain rules that are required by recent amendments to the Internal Revenue Code, (2) conforming the Plan Document to anticipated administrative changes connected with the completion of the PensionGold benefits administration and retiree payroll system, and (3) clarifying and conforming to certain administrative rules.   None of the proposed changes is expected to have a material effect on the benefits payable under the Plan.

 

A summary of the proposed amendments is provided below.

 

A.  Changes required by recent amendments to the Internal Revenue Code

 

      1.  The Heroes Earnings Assistance and Relief Tax (“HEART”) Act requires all qualified pension plans to provide death benefits to the survivors of employees who leave employment for the purpose of serving in the uniformed services, who have reemployment rights under federal law, and who die before returning to employment.  It requires that these employees be given vesting credit for the period of qualified military service, and permits (but does not require) plans also to give credit for that period of service for the purpose of calculating the amount of the benefit the employee’s survivors will receive.  It permits (but does not require) plans to provide disability benefits and service credit under similar rules where the employee is prevented from returning to employment because of a disability that occurs during qualified military service.  The HEART Act requires that plans count any differential compensation the employer pays to members in military service (i.e., amounts that the employer pays to make up the difference between military pay and the compensation the member would otherwise be receiving) for purposes of testing against certain limits under the Internal Revenue Code.  It permits (but does not require) counting this differential compensation for other purposes under the plan. 

                 

                  FCPS pays differential compensation for a maximum period of two weeks for training purposes.  It would be administratively difficult to count that compensation only for testing against the Internal Revenue Code limits.  Counting that compensation for all purposes under the Plan is not expected to add any material cost to the Plan. Similarly, counting a member’s time in qualified military service for purposes of calculating death benefits and for disability benefits in the small number of cases where these rules would apply is not expected to add any material cost to the Plan.  Accordingly, the Trustees recommend that the Plan be amended to include both the mandatory and the permitted HEART Act rules for death benefits, disability benefits, and differential compensation. These amendments are reflected in the proposed amendments to Sections 1.31, 6.10, and 8.06 of ERFC and Sections 1.22 and 5.04 of ERFC 2001.

 

      2.  Changes required by the Pension Protection Act of 2006 (“PPA”).  The PPA added Roth IRAs to the list of retirement savings plans to which members can roll over certain distributions from qualified pension plans and thus defer paying income taxes on those distributions.  The Plan is required to permit such rollovers.  The amendments proposed to implement this new rule are in Sections 18.02(b) and 18.04 of ERFC and Section 13.02(b) and 13.04 of ERFC 2001.

 

B.  Changes for the purpose of conforming the Plan Document to anticipated administrative changes connected with the completion of PensionGold

 

The PensionGold system includes Web Member Services, which members can use (among other things) to make or change their beneficiary designations on-line.  The Plan Document currently requires that beneficiary designations be in writing and notarized. The Trustees recommend amending the Plan to eliminate that requirement and instead to specify that beneficiary designations must be made in accordance with regulations that the Trustees will adopt for that purpose.  By relying on regulations on this subject, the Trustees will have the flexibility to amend these procedures to adapt to experience with their new on-line security measures or to respond to changing conditions, without requiring the School Board’s approval of Plan amendments.  These proposed amendments are in Section 1.23 of ERFC and Section 1.12 of ERFC 2001.

 

C.  Changes for the purpose of clarifying and conforming to certain administrative rules

 

In the process of restating the Plan documents, several rules were identified that do not explicitly reflect current administrative practice. 

1.  Members who are on unpaid leave for the purpose of serving an employee organization in a full-time capacity are required to contribute to the Plan, based on the salary the member would be earning if not on leave, the sum of normal member contributions and the contributions that FCPS would otherwise make on that salary.  The Plan documents explicitly state that such a member receives service credit for such periods of leave, but they do not explicitly state that the member’s accumulated contributions account is credited with the amount of the member contributions made during such periods of leave, nor does ERFC explicitly state that the member receives vesting credit for such periods.  (The ERFC 2001Plan document does not use the term “vesting credit.”)  The proposed amendments to Sections 1.01 and 1.36 of ERFC and to Section 1.01 of ERFC 2001 will correct this omission, consistent with current practice.

 

2.  Amendments to Section 8.01 of ERFC and to Section 5.01 of ERFC 2001 are proposed for the purpose of clarifying the rules for eligibility for death benefits, consistent with current practice.

 

3.   Amendments to Section 16.04 of ERFC and to Section 11.04 of ERFC are proposed for the purpose of conforming the Plan documents to an administrative change regarding benefits payable to the spouse or ex-spouse of a member who will receive a portion of the member’s benefits from the Plan under a domestic relations court order.  In May 2006 the Trustees amended their regulations governing such orders to specify that the spouse or ex-spouse may not designate a beneficiary to receive such benefits following the death of the spouse or ex-spouse while the member is still living.  The proposed amendments will reflect that change in the Plan documents as well, but will not affect any domestic relations orders that were accepted before the May 2006 change in the Trustees’ regulations. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment 17 to the ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purposes of (1) incorporating rules required by recent amendments to the Internal Revenue Code, (2) conforming to anticipated administrative changes connected with the completion of PensionGold, and (3) clarifying and conforming to certain administrative rules, with no anticipated material effect on the benefits payable under the Plan, as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:            Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category:    December 16, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 10

     

Subject:                     Proposed Restatements of the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related to:                  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Trustees have periodically submitted the plan documents to the Internal Revenue Service for a determination that the Plan continues to be qualified for favorable tax treatment under the Internal Revenue Code.  By obtaining such a determination, the Trustees receive assurance that the Trust Fund does not have to pay income tax on its investment earnings and that the members do not have to pay tax on the benefits they are earning until they actually retire and begin to receive benefits.  The Internal Revenue Service has set January 31, 2011, as the deadline for governmental plans to be submitted for such determinations.

 

The IRS strongly recommends, for purposes of such a submission, that the plan documents be “restated” to incorporate all amendments that have been adopted since the last time that the plan documents were submitted to the IRS.  The ERFC and ERFC 2001 plan documents were last submitted to the IRS in 2001.  Since then, the School Board has approved 16 separate sets of amendments, and amendment 17 is presented on this same agenda for adoption.

 

Restated plan documents have been prepared.  These proposed restatements, which are attached, incorporate amendments 1 through 17.  They also include non-substantive changes for the purpose of correcting cross-references, restoring consistency in capitalization and punctuation, and eliminating unnecessary repetition.    

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve proposed restated plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001 for submission to the Internal Revenue Service with the application for a current determination letter, as detailed in the agenda item.

Recommended Action
Adopt Policy 8614, Video Surveillance, as detailed in the agenda item (FTS; presented as new business 12/2/10)

Staff Contact:            Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:    December 16, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 10

 

Subject:                    School Board Policy

 

School Board Action Required:   Decision

 

Key Points:

 

In July 2008, the School Board amended Policy 8614, Video Surveillance, to permit a pilot prevention strategy at Annandale, Mt. Vernon and Westfield High Schools, and Lake Braddock, Robinson, and South County Secondary Schools.  Video surveillance systems were placed strategically in cafeteria serving lines, combined with video monitors depicting real time images of customers, in an effort to mitigate theft, food tampering, and inappropriate behaviors. 

 

In October 2009, the School Board approved the extension of the pilot for 2009-2010 and requested a theft data comparison between the pilot schools and the schools without video surveillance systems.   A review of the data for the past year was not conclusive that the video surveillance system resulted in a significant reduction of theft. Therefore, it is recommended that the pilot be discontinued.   All references to the cafeteria serving line pilot will be removed from Policy 8614.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 8614, Video Surveillance, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                     Award of Contract - Physical Exams, Medical Evaluations, and Alcohol and Drug Testing

 

School Board Action Required:     Decision

 

Key Points:

 

The Code of Virginia, Section 22.1-178, requires school bus drivers to undergo physical examinations upon initial employment and annually thereafter.  In addition, the Virginia Department of Health and Human Services requires an occupational safety and health physical for employees who work with hazardous materials.  This includes initial examinations and annual respiratory examinations.

 

Inova Health System (INOVA) is recommended by the Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) to perform these services at a cost of $93.82 per FCPS physical examination.  INOVA services will also include physicals for employees working with hazardous materials.

 

The Federal Omnibus Transportation Employee Act of 1991 requires pre-employment and random alcohol and drug testing of all employees who possess a commercial driver’s license (CDL).  In addition, FCPS Regulation 4140.9 , Physical and Medical Examinations, and Policy 3860.4 and Regulation 3860.7, Extracurricular Activities, provide for the testing of student athletes for controlled substances.

 

FirstLab is recommended by the SAC to provide these services at a charge of $35.00 for alcohol testing, and $46.20 for drug testing.

 

The current contracts previously awarded by the School Board expire December 31, 2010.  New bids have been solicited and finalized by the County.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award contracts for (1) provision of physical examinations for school bus drivers, medical examinations as part of applications for disability retirement, and occupational safety and health to INOVA, and (2) provision of alcohol and drug testing for employees in positions requiring CDLs, employees under reasonable suspicion, and students involved in extracurricular athletic programs who are under reasonable suspicion to FirstLab, effective January 1, 2011, through December 31, 2016, as detailed in the agenda item.

The Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2012-2016 Capital Improvement Program Summary can be found in the attachment to the Capital Improvement Program booklet.

 

Discussion:

 

During FY 2007 and 2008 the CIP project costs were generally in-line with the estimates for the projects.  However, beginning FY 2009, construction costs declined rapidly.  This CIP proposes to apply the cost savings of $31 million, achieved as the result of very favorable market conditions and lower than estimated bids, towards the projects in the renovation queue.  Additional monies also are available as a result of the School Board’s decisions to close Clifton Elementary School and Pimmit Alternative High School. The application of these cost savings allows many projects to begin planning and construction sooner, ultimately resulting in the renovation start dates for many schools being moved up by 2-3 years.
