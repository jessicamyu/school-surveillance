Subject:  Unified Scale Compensation Study Update

 

 

Staff Contact:  Michael Draeger, director, Office of HR Business Services,  Department of Human Resources

 

 

Other Staff Present:  R Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

                                    De Hawley Brown, director, Office of Benefits and Compensation, Department of Human Resources

 

 

Meeting Category:  April 19, 2018 – Work Session 

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 3: Premier Workforce

 

 

Background (Key Points):  

Over the past three years, Fairfax County Public Schools has been conducting a compensation study to identify and implement a compensation structure that attracts and retains qualified employees. The study is a key piece of the Strategic Plan’s Goal 3: Premier Workforce which aims to recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts, and ensure that FCPS maintains a competitive compensation package.

 

To date, pay schedules have been recommended for the following groups of employees:

  • Teachers, July 2016

  • School-based Administrators, July 2017

  • Classroom Instructional Support, December 2016

  • Unified Scale – Schedule H, April 2017

 

The remaining positions are all currently placed on the Unified Scale and consist of over 800 position titles which cover approximately 5,000 employees, both school-based and non-school based. A recommendation to divide the current Unified Scale into three pay schedules was initially presented to the School Board in its April 20, 2017, work session, which is available on BoardDocs.

 

This presentation details the plan for the new structure of the Unified Scale:

 

Schedule A

This schedule as proposed will include approximately 3,500 employees in positions that are currently on grades US-05 through US-19, such as custodians, office assistants, and trades/facilities positions. These positions are eligible for overtime. To reflect the market on average for these positions, the proposed scale increases pay at the bottom of the scale by 10 to 11 percent and maintains pay at the top of the scale.

 

Schedule B

This schedule as proposed will include approximately 1,400 employees in positions that are currently on grades US-20 through US-26, such as social workers, psychologists, analysts, and specialists. To reflect the market on average for these positions, the proposed scale increases pay at the bottom of the scale by 11 to 12 percent and raises pay at the top of the scale by approximately three percent.

 

Schedule C

This schedule as proposed will include approximately 200 employees in positions that are currently on grades US-27 through US-33, such as department coordinator IIIs, directors, and executive principals. In keeping with the current methodology of aligning upper level and senior management positions on pay ranges equivalent to principals and assistant principals, jobs on this schedule are proposed to be placed on the School-based Administrator scale, which was reviewed and approved by the School Board during the FY 2018 budget process. Including school-based administrators, this scale will include nearly 900 employees. This structure more closely reflects the current market on average for these positions and raises pay at the bottom of the scale by 28 to 38 percent and raises pay at the top of the scale by approximately five percent.

 

 

Next Steps:

Once the scales are in place, a 5-year cyclical review of jobs within each department will be initiated. Employees within the areas for review will be given advance notice, and employee and manager input will be included through surveys and individual desk audits, as necessary.

 

 

Recommendation: 

That the School Board include the new Unified Scale structure in its FY19 approved budget.

Staff Contact:  Sean McDonald, director, Office of Talent and Acquisition Management, Department of Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:  R Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

                                    Andrea Garris, coordinator, Office of Talent Acquisition and Management, Department of Human Resources

 

Meeting Category:  April 19, 2018 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

As requested by the School Board, a review of data surrounding substitute and leave usage has been conducted.

 

Data provided in the April 20, 2017, work session has been updated to show three years of trend data. While the substitute pool has decreased, the fill rate has remained consistent at 93%. Leave usage has increased 16% over the past two years. A work group has been convened to discuss the data and potential solutions.

 

Recommendation:  A follow-up work session to be scheduled for School Board action related to this data.
