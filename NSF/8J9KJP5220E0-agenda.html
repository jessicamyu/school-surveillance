Key Points:

 

Virginia School Division Efficiency Review Program

 

The goal of the state of Virginia's School Efficiency Review Program is to ensure that non-instructional functions are running efficiently so that as much of this funding as possible goes directly into the classroom.  The program identifies savings that can be gained in the school division through best practices in divisional administration, human resources, finance, purchasing, educational service delivery costs, special education, facilities, transportation, technology, management, and food service.

 

The efficiency review report will include recommendations and projected costs and/or savings associated with the recommendations.  The local share of the participation cost has increased from 25% to 37.5%.  There is also an additional implementation cost of 25% if certain implementation targets are not met. 

 

FCPS is currently number two on the list for implementation.  It is possible that the school division which is ahead of Fairfax will choose not to participate, which would put FCPS next in line.  At this time, it is anticipated that the state review will begin in the fall of 2013 (FY 2014).  If the jurisdiction ahead of FCPS chooses not to participate, a state review could begin in the fall of 2012 (FY 2013).  The state controls the procurement process for the selection of the efficiency review team.  The review timeline is developed in consultation with the school division, and the implementation of recommendations are at the discretion of the school division.

 

Completed state efficiency review reports, including the report for Loudoun County, are posted on the VDOE web site at the following link: http://www.doe.virginia.gov/school_finance/efficiency_reviews/index.shtml

 

 

FCPS Reviews in progress or recently completed:

  • Special Services (completed)

An External Team was engaged by FCPS to review and provide insight and feedback on the operations of Special Services, its linkage with other FCPS departments, and collective strategies to support a high level of student performance.  (See Attachment A)

  • Staff Development (completed)

The review of staff development within FCPS by the Linkage Corporation resulted in implementation of the professional learning and training department. (See Attachment B)

  • Finance and Human Relations (in progress)

As part of the Fairfax County Unified System (FOCUS) Project, FCPS completed an extensive documentation and evaluation of core business processes.  Beginning in 2008, with the guidance of the Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA), the Project Team identified 64 business processes essential to our core finance, human resources, budget, and procurement functions.  These processes were formally documented in over 200 process maps.  Stakeholders including functional managers, subject matter experts, and end users participated in the process. 

 

In 2009, the Project Team evaluated the documented processes and identified areas for business process improvement.  Industry best practices, policies, mandates and long term enterprise goals were all considered.  For the past year, the team has been working to incorporate the identified business process improvements into the design of the new financial and procurement system.  The same process will follow as we begin the other phases of the project (HR, payroll and budget).

  • Transportation Routing (in progress)

Optimization study is in progress.

Staff Contact:         Michael Molloy, director, Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:  October 17, 2011 – Work Session No. 31

 

Subject:                 Fairfax County School Board 2012 Draft Legislative Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:            Legislation 

 

Key Points: 

 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The attached 2012 Fairfax County School Board Draft Legislative Program includes additions, subtractions and formatting changes. These changes are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate, to incorporate staff recommendations, and to update, simplify and streamline existing position language.

 

Based on information received from the County, United States Department of Education, and Virginia Department of Education, since the first work session held on September 12, staff recommendations have been changed on two positions:

 

  • Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and Fairfax County School Board Joint Legislative Priority Position – recommend adding data regarding change in percent of general fund allocated to K-12 education from FY09-FY12 (BOS recommendation)
  • Elementary and Secondary Education Act Reauthorization – recommend clarifying proposed language regarding waivers from components of NCLB (based on new information from USED and VDOE regarding potential waiver process)

In addition to staff recommendations, individual Board members may have specific amendments for Board consideration at the work session.

 

Options:  Modify as desired.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board accept staff recommendations for the Fairfax County School Board draft 2012 Legislative Program.

 

 

Board Member Proposed Amendments:

  1. I move to add the following position: School Board Elections - The Fairfax County School Board supports granting authority to the Fairfax County School Board to allow its members to be elected for staggered terms. (Gibson)
  1. I move to add the following language to the Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning position: The Fairfax County School Board supports Standards of Learning and SOL curriculum guidelines that strike an appropriate balance between a broad overview of a subject area and in-depth exploration of components within, and interrelationships between, subject areas. (Center)
  1. I move to add the following language (underlined) to the Freedom of Information Act position (Wilson):
  • The Fairfax County School Board supports revision and amendment of the Virginia Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) and companion regulations, such as those issued by the Library of Virginia regarding records retention, in order to achieve the appropriate balance between the considerable investment in time and money required for compliance and ensuring the people of the Commonwealth ready access to public records.  Revision and amendment are particularly important in light of the growth of the use of electronic communications to communicate more quickly and efficiently with the public, including but not limited to web pages and “social media.”
  • The FCSB supports the creation of a “third party disclosure” FOIA exemption.  For example, when one citizen emails another without writing or copying a public body, and that private email is subsequently forwarded to a public body with a message, a third party disclosure exemption would exempt the original private email from disclosure in response to a FOIA request.
  • The FCSB supports specifically allowing for reimbursement for the costs of redaction that are mandated or allowed by other federal and state statutes (e.g. redactions required under FERPA, etc.)
  • The FCSB supports changes to the current FOIA exemptions for human resources investigations and to the provisions of the Government Data Collection and Dissemination Practices Act to clarify that the subject of the an investigation may only access investigative report materials once the investigation is complete, and that witness names may be redacted from the final report. 
  1. I move to add the following position: Jobs Bill - The Fairfax County School Board supports federal legislation that supports our schools by providing funding to improve school building infrastructure and funding to protect the jobs and salaries of our teachers and support staff. (Hone)
  1. I move to add the following language to the Student Discipline position (Hone):
  • The Fairfax County School Board supports requiring school principals or their designees to notify, except in exigent circumstances, the parent or legal guardian of any student, prior to questioning that student regarding their direct involvement in activities that could be deemed a violation of the student code of conduct, if the principals or their designees reasonably believe that the violation is serious enough that it could result in a disciplinary penalty of suspension for 10 days or longer, recommendation expulsion, or criminal charges.
  • The Superintendent or his designee shall not be authorized to support or oppose any specific legislation or provision in legislation that is not expressly included in the annual legislative program adopted by the School Board.
