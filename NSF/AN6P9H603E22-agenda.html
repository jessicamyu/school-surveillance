Bullying Prevention Month- October

 

In an effort to promote awareness of school bullying, the Virginia School Board Association Board of Directors has designated the month of October as VSBA Bullying Prevention Month. Childhood bullying is a significant problem nationwide. It can cause school absenteeism, mental and physical stress, poor school performance, poor self-esteem, and in some cases, school violence. Statistics show that 160,000 children in the United States miss school each day as a result of being bullied. School board members, superintendents, teachers, and parents play a critical role in creating a climate where bullying is not tolerated. It has been proven when adults and children stand together, bullying ends.

 

 

Fire Prevention Week - October 8 - 14, 2017

 

Fire Prevention Week 2017 focuses on the fact that in a fire, second count.  The official theme, "Every second counts; Plan 2 ways out", reinforces why everyone needs to have an escape plan.  Seconds can mean the difference between residents of our community escaping safely from a fire or having their lives end in tragedy. During Fire Prevention Week, October 8 - 14, fire safety educators around the country will work with citizens to teach them about fire safety tips, encourage them to draw a map of their home marking two exits from each room and a path to the outside from each exit, practice a home fire drill twice a year, regularly test their smoke alarms and provide activities featuring Sparky the Fire Dog. 

 

 

Mental Health Awareness Week, October 1-7, 2017

 

The National Alliance on Mental Illness or NAMI and participants across the country are raising awareness of mental illness during Mental Illness Awareness Week which is October 1-7.  Each year NAMI works to fight stigma, provide support, educate the public and advocate for equal care.  NAMI believes that mental illness issues are important to address year round, but highlighting these issues during Mental Health Awareness Week provides a time for people to come together and display the passion and strength of those working to improve the lives of the tens of millions of Americans affected by mental illness. This year’s theme is I am stigma free. Being Stigma Free means learning about and educating others on mental illness, focusing on connecting with people to see each other as individuals and not a diagnosis, and most importantly, taking action on mental health issues.

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 4, Resource Stewardship.

 

The following are the two overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 4, Resource Stewardship:

 

Overarching Strategy 10

 

Maximize and align FCPS resources in order to be accountable and transparent with the community regarding the resources of FCPS

Desired Outcomes

  • A budget decision-making framework, based on financial management principles and standards, will be regularly and systematically utilized
  • Benchmarking processes will weigh major organizational operations against comparable school systems for effectiveness, efficiency, and Return on Investment

 

Overarching Strategy 11

 

Promote the value and benefits of the community’s investment in FCPS in order to increase the community’s understanding of the Division’s operating and capital needs to support student success

Desired Outcomes

  • Stakeholders will understand where and how financial resources will be utilized
  • FCPS will regularly and systematically advocate for fiscal resources at the county, state, and federal levels

Staff Contact:  Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Meeting Category:  September 28, 2017 Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Suicide Prevention, Risk Assessment, Postvention Policy

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

This is a new policy.

 

A vote on Policy 2XXX.X, Suicide Prevention, Risk Assessment, Postvention, originally scheduled for October 20, 2016 was delayed to allow for greater discussion. Following meetings with the School Board Governance Committee revisions were made to clarify the purpose of the policy and expectations for school prevention, intervention and postvention efforts. 

 

  1. This policy establishes expectations for suicide prevention efforts, individual suicide risk assessment and intervention, and response and supports for students and staff following a death by suicide.

 

  1. Prevention:
    1. Training for educators, parents and students on signs of depression and suicidal thinking and how to refer for help, and include information on reducing stigma
    2. Wellness screenings
    3. Access to school-based mental health providers (school counselors, school psychologists, school social workers)
    4. Crisis support services
    5. Response to reports of students at risk due to mental health conditions or life circumstances, including those identified on Student Health Information Form

 

  1. Suicide Risk Assessment:
    1. Procedures for responding to report of suicidal thinking by student
    2. Determination of risk
    3. Parent notification
    4. Referral to appropriate resources

 

  1. Postvention:
    1. Procedures for providing response to school community following a death by suicide
    2. Provision of grief counseling
    3. Identification of youth at risk
    4. Parent notification and referral to appropriate resources as needed

 

Following the School Board Work Session on September 18, 2017, Policy 2XXX.X was modified in section 3, paragraph 1, to include added text to the end of the paragraph:  Staff will review county data related to teen suicides and attempts to inform prevention efforts. A report will be made annually to the School Board.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the Suicide Prevention, Risk Assessment, Postvention Policy, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on June 20, 2017, the updated version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2017 session of the General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

County’s Administrative Changes Relevant to FCPS

 

  • Cooperative Procurement:  Add authority to purchase from a contract from the National Association of Counties, reference 2007 Virginia Acts of Assembly 330.  See Attachment I at page 5.
  • Definitions:  Add debar and suspension and modify definition of ineligibility to be consistent with the VPPA.  See Attachment I at pages 7, 8, and 11.
  • Exceptions:  Add exception to the requirement for competitive procurement for Finance Board Investments as provided in Code of Virginia §15.2-1548.  See Attachment I at page 24.
  • Ineligibility:  Modifies appeals period in response to a Notice of Debarment from 30 days to 10 days as provided in the Code of Virginia, §2.2-4357.  See Attachment I at page 54.
  • Ineligibility:  Modifies criteria and time periods for debarment or suspension for consistency.  See Attachment I at pages 53-55.
  • Protest of Award or Decision to Award:  Adds text to prohibit the challenge of the validity of the terms or conditions of the solicitation as provided in the Code of Virginia, §2.2-4360.  See Attachment I at page 57. 

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015 and 2016 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2017 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

  • The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  • The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopts the newly revised 2017 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee

 

School Board Action Required: Decision                       

 

Key Points:    

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents. Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.  For 2017-2018 the Board members making appointments are Megan McLaughlin (Braddock), Jane Strauss (Dranesville), Pat Hynes (Hunter Mill), Karen Corbett Sanders (Mount Vernon), Dalia Palchik (Providence), and Thomas Wilson (Sully). Last year the School Board district representatives were from Lee, Springfield, Mason, and three Members-at-Large were selected to appoint individuals.

 

Two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school.  The level will be determined by a random selection.  If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate. 

 

The student representative to the School Board appoints an individual to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.

Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009.   

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint individuals to serve a one-year term on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:  Divisionwide Comprehensive Plan

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

This document meets the requirements of the Virginia Department of Education, Standard 6 (§22.1-253.13:6) in the Standards of Quality 2011.  Standard 6 requires each local school board to adopt biennially a Divisionwide Comprehensive Plan.

The purpose of the divisionwide comprehensive plan is to provide a platform for communicating major divisionwide initiatives and operational plans.  Information provided in this plan was previously presented to the School Board during the past year in a variety of reports, but primarily in the Strategic Plan Goal 1 and Goal 2 reports. The plan consists of School Board student achievement measures, along with operational plans for the system.  The student achievement measures, which constitute the majority of the report, address the following desired outcomes, which most closely align with previous versions of the divisionwide comprehensive plan:

 

·The curriculum will reflect Portrait of a Graduate outcomes in all content areas, where students are appropriately challenged

·Achievement gaps will be eliminated

·Teachers, students, and parents will have access to contemporary and effective technology resources

·All qualifying publicly funded future FCPS students will have access to high quality early education programs

·A portfolio of school options will provide multiple pathways to a high school diploma

·Students will be fluent in two or more languages

·FCPS students will demonstrate improved life skills and self-awareness of behaviors and actions

·All students will develop global and ethical citizenship goals

·FCPS students will feel welcome and supported at school

·FCPS students will be encouraged and supported in making healthy life choices

 

The plan will be presented for public comment on October 12, 2017.  The Board is scheduled to approve the plan at its October 26, 2017 meeting.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Divisionwide Comprehensive Plan, as detailed in the agenda item.
