Staff Contact:  Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Susan Quinn, Chief Operating Officer

                                   Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:  March 13, 2017 - Work Session

 

Subject:  FY 2018 Budget & State Revenue Impact

 

School Board Action Required:  None

 

Key Points:

 

On February 9, 2017, the School Board adopted the Advertised Budget, and the FY 2018 budget documents are available at www.fcps.edu/budget.

 

The State Joint Conference Committee released recommended amendments to the Governor’s Introduced Budget which result in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) receiving additional funding totaling $0.8 million as compared to the Governor’s Introduced Budget. As was expected, the conference report eliminates funding for a 1.5 percent bonus and instead funds the state’s share of a 2.0 percent salary increase funded beginning next February (partial year funding).

 

On February 28, 2017, the Board of Supervisors advertised a real estate tax rate for FY 2018 of $1.13 per $100 of assessed value. The County’s FY 2018 Advertised Budget Plan is balanced at the existing Real Estate Tax rate of $1.13 per $100 of assessed value. Advertising an increase in the rate does not prevent the Board from lowering any advertised tax rate, but a higher tax rate cannot be imposed without advertising the higher rate. The County’s Advertised Budget includes an increase to FCPS’ School Operating Fund of $51.7 million.

 

The gap between FCPS’ FY 2018 Advertised Budget and the funding currently included in the County’s Advertised Budget is $60.9 million. After taking the state budget impact into account, the gap is reduced to $56.7 million.  Based on the recommendations included in FCPS’ FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review, additional funding of $6.5 million is recommended to be added to the budgeted beginning balance, and savings of $1.8 million in health benefit expenditures is recommended for FY 2018. When these impacts are added, the gap is reduced to $48.4 million as shown in the attachment.

 

This work session will provide an opportunity to review the state budget impact as well as the recommendations from FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review that impact FY 2018.

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The School Board has established a Human Resources Advisory Committee (HRAC) to advise the Fairfax County School Board (FCSB) on matters affecting the development and maintenance of a premier workforce within the caring culture of our school community. HRAC tasks include advising the FCSB on the objectives and implementation of the FCPS human resources programs, as well as the resources necessary for successful implementation and sustainability of such programs. HRAC By-Laws have been drafted and provided to the FCSB for consideration in accordance with Fairfax County Public Schools Policy 1710. The By-Laws provide guidance for the structure, function, and reporting requirements of the HRAC.
