Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

SUBJECT: High School Course Offerings

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board approve changes to the list of high school course offerings for the
    2005-2006 school year, as recommended by the High School Course Review Committee and as detailed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:
    A. Standard and optional courses for high schools are reviewed each year, and new proposals are submitted to the High School Course Review Committee for review and recommendation to the Superintendent and School Board. This process is outlined in Regulation 3202.5, Approval of New Courses and Deletion and Consolidation of Existing Courses.
B. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as additions to the list of standard course offerings for the 2005-2006 school year (see Attachment A for course description):
        1. Entrepreneurship
        Rationale:
          Entrepreneurship would provide students with an opportunity to learn small business ownership skills. It would be open to all students in grades 11 and 12 as a full-year course at the Marshall Academy.
        2. Network Administration II
        Rationale:
          Network Administration II is designed to continue teaching aspects of network administration focusing on management and support of network users and systems. This course would be available to students in grade 12 for two credits and would provide the necessary preparation for students to take certification exams. The course is approved by the Virginia Department of Education and would be added to the standard course offerings for all Professional Technical Studies’ academies.
C. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as additions to the list of optional course offerings for the 2005-2006 school year (see Attachment B for course description):

        1. Advanced Oral Communication
          Rationale:
          Advanced Oral Communication provides a second level to students with a higher level of speech and debate, and would provide sequential elective option for students to meet graduation requirements.


        2. Technology Assessment and Technology Transfer
          Rationale:
          Technology Assessment and Technology Transfer provide a sequence for students in grades 10-12 who have completed the Design and Technology course in grade 9. These two courses, approved by the Virginia Department of Education, provide students with the skills to integrate current and emerging technologies with other subject areas, such as math and science.
        3. Teacher Cadet
          Rationale:
          Teacher Cadet introduces students in grades 11 and 12 to a career in teaching and education. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the job opportunities for teachers over the next 10 years should be excellent, attributable mostly to the large number of teachers expected to retire. Although employment of preschool, kindergarten, elementary, middle, and secondary school teachers is expected to increase about as fast as the average for all occupations, a large proportion will be eligible to retire by 2010, creating many vacancies, particularly at the secondary level. Intense competition for highly qualified teachers is already under way among employers in many locations, with schools luring teachers from other states and districts with bonuses and higher pay.
D. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as pilots for the 2005-2006 school year (see Attachment C for course description):

        1. AP Computer Science A
          Rationale:
          The current Computer Science course includes all concepts of AP Computer Science A except for three. This would provide students with another AP course option that can be completed in one year instead of the two years required for AP Computer Science AB.
2. Multicultural Literature
          Rationale:
          Multicultural Literature has been developed and is being recommended as a pilot.
E. The following course was recommended for a deletion offering for the 2005-2006 school
    year (see Attachment D for course description):
          Introduction to Food Occupations
          Rationale:
          There has been no enrollment for the past two school years.
    ALTERNATIVE:

    None recommended.

      Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

      SUBJECT: Acquisition of Central Administration Building Complex

      RECOMMENDATION:

          That the School Board approve the acquisition of an office building known as Dominion One at Jefferson Park and the adjacent, unimproved parcel of land known as the Dominion Two site at Jefferson Park (collectively, the "Property"), and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute a Purchase Agreement with Jefferson Park Venture LLC and such other documents as may be necessary and advisable to complete the acquisition and related financing, as described below, including an agreement pursuant to which the County of Fairfax acquires title to the Property and leases the Property to the School Board.
      EXPLANATION:

          Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) staff has for some time been exploring the feasibility of consolidating some or its entire administrative staff, currently located in 17 owned and leased facilities scattered throughout Fairfax County. The Staubach Company conducted a market survey of available office alternatives for FCPS and provided a list of possible alternatives for review and consideration. The alternative sites were evaluated by FCPS staff and the Staubach Company, and the building known as Dominion One at Jefferson Park and an adjacent vacant land parcel were determined to best meet the needs of FCPS for a central administration building complex. These sites are located across from Luther Jackson Middle School in the northeast quadrant of the Route 50/Gallows Road intersection.
            The recommended consolidation site consists of an office building (Dominion One at Jefferson Park) containing 209,423 rentable square feet of space and an adjacent 3.13-acre parcel of land. The adjacent land parcel is zoned for the construction of an additional office building containing 209,423 rentable square feet of space. The total cost of the Dominion One building and the adjacent land parcel is $45.8 million. It is estimated that tenant improvements and furniture and fixtures for Dominion One will cost approximately $13.5 million. An economic analysis has been prepared showing the present value cost of remaining at the existing administrative centers compared to consolidating the centers. That analysis shows that the cost of remaining at our existing facilities is no greater than consolidating. A copy of the analysis is included as Attachment 1.
              The purchase of the building and adjacent land are expected to be financed through a mechanism that will be determined by the Board of Supervisors. The debt service on the bonds will be paid for by the school system from the savings generated by the consolidation of the administrative centers. The purchase of the building and property will not have any effect on funding available for school construction.
                It is anticipated that the consolidation of existing FCPS administrative centers will occur in two phases. Phase 1 will begin in early 2006 and will involve the consolidation into Dominion One of staff and departments located in the following administrative facilities:
              · Burkholder Center
              · City Square Building (4th floor)
              · Devonshire Center
              · Edsall Park (Human Resources Center)
              · Lacey Center
              · Providence Hill Center
              · University Drive Center
              · Westmore Center
                    Approximately 642 staff will be relocated during Phase 1 including the Superintendent’s Office, Cluster Directors offices, and the Assistant Superintendents for Financial Services, Human Resources, Special Services, Instructional Services, and Educational Accountability. Construction of the second building is scheduled to begin in FY 2007 and be completed in FY 2009. Staff and departments located in the following administrative facilities will be relocated into the second building:
                · Alan Leis Instructional Center
                · Belle Willard Center
                · City Square Building (1st and 3rd floors)
                · Dunn Loring Center
                · Eleven Oaks Center
                · Nancy Sprague Technology Center
                · Wilton Woods Center
                      Approximately 659 staff will be relocated during Phase 2 including the Assistant Superintendents for Information Technology, and Facilities and Transportation Services.
                        If the School Board approves this item, the Board of Supervisors will be asked to take action on the purchase and the financing plan at its meeting on December 6, 2004. If the Board of Supervisors approves the purchase and financing plan on December 6, 2004, they will be asked to approve the financing documents on January 10, 2005. Staff will be expected to come back to the School Board to approve documents related to the financing after the BOS determines the details of the financing plan. Closing on the purchase of the property is scheduled to occur no later than January 31, 2005.

                          None recommended.
