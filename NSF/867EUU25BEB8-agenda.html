Operational Expectations Work Session

OPERATIONAL EXPECTATIONS FOR ACCOUNTABILITY AND AUDIT

Current approved language for the Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations:

The Superintendent will maintain a system that tracks, measures, and evaluates FCPS effectiveness in realizing student achievement and business processes, including both benefits and costs, in a timely manner. The Superintendent will:

Evaluate all new programs and any programs identified by the Audit Committee and/or the School Board. That evaluation should recommend whether a program should be continued, modified, or discontinued based on its effectiveness and cost.

Cultivate an environment committed to continuous improvement.


Governance Committee recommended language:

The Superintendent will maintain a system of continuous improvement that tracks, measures, and evaluates FCPS effectiveness in realizing student achievement and business processes, including both benefits and costs, in a timely manner. The Superintendent will:”

Establish a multi-year plan for program evaluation that identifies evaluation criteria and the relationship to student achievement goals. New program proposals shall include an evaluation plan that assures its evaluation within the first three years. All evaluations should recommend whether a program should be continued, modified, or discontinued based on its effectiveness and cost.
    [Delete 2. as it is encompassed in the preface paragraph language.]


    Motion: That the School Board revise the language for the Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations (OE) as follows: (1) add “of continuous improvement” in the preface; (2) replace OE #1 as follows: "Establish a multi-year plan for program evaluation that identifies evaluation criteria and the relationship to student achievement goals. New program proposals shall include an evaluation plan that assures its evaluation within the first three years. All evaluations should recommend whether a program should be continued, modified, or discontinued based on its effectiveness and cost."; and (3) delete OE #2.

    47.02 Discussion - FY 2009 Budget; Proposed FY 2008 Departmental Budget Reductions
    Meeting
    Dec 10, 2007 - Work Session No. 45-48
    Category
    47. Budget - 2 p.m.
    Type
    Information

    Staff Contact: Deirdra McLaughlin, Chief Financial Officer

    Meeting Type: December 10, 2007, Work Session No. 47

    SUBJECT: Proposed FY 2008 Departmental Budget Reductions

    TYPE: Discussion

    BACKGROUND:
        At the November 12 School Board Work Session, the Board provided guidance to the Superintendent on how to proceed with approaching solutions to offset the projected FY 2009 budget deficit of $98.8 million. At that time, he informed the Board that central departments had been directed to identify budget reductions of 5 percent to assist in closing the budget gap projected for FY 2009.

        As a result, the Superintendent and Leadership Team identified $10.2 million in central/departmental reductions that will be implemented to offset the FY 2009 deficit. Many of these reductions will be implemented immediately, in the current fiscal year (FY 2008), to capture additional savings that can be carried into FY 2009.

    KEY POINTS:
        The Superintendent and the Leadership Team have identified nearly $2M in savings that could be realized in the current year (FY 2008) with early implementation of the proposed reductions.
        In order to realize immediate savings, an apportionment of the proposed FY 2009 budget cuts will become effective starting January 1, 2008.

    OPTIONS:
        Review the proposed departmental budget reductions identified for immediate implementation.
        Board approval will be required at the scheduled Third Quarter Review (March 27, 2008).

    RECOMMENDATION:

    Review the proposed budget reductions in preparation for the action required at Third Quarter Review.
