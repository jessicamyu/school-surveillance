Subject:                       ERFC Annual Report to the School Board

School Board Action Required: Information

Key Points:

1. As of 12/31/2009, the ERFC membership included 19,891 active members, among which 58% were covered under the ERFC 2001 benefit plan structure (The plan   provided for members hired on or after 7/1/2001), and 42% were covered under the ERFC Legacy plan structure (The plan provided for members hired prior to 7/1/2001)

2. During CY 2009, ERFC paid $138 million to its retirees and beneficiaries, 71% of whom are Virginia residents.

3. The ERFC Fund recovered with great strength in 2009, with an investment performance of 25.6% for the calendar year. ERFC has continued to perform well in the first quarter of 2010, with the Fund coming in 39% higher at the one year mark, effective 3/31/2010.

4. ERFC’s asset market value also improved significantly in 2009, rising from $1.37 billion in January to $1.65 billion at the end of the calendar year.

5. Assisted by ERFC’s improved investment performance in 2009, as well as the adjustments implemented to its Funding Policy last year, ERFC has moderately reduced its original projections for the employer contribution rate increases.

6. Although ERFC’s ratio of funding value of assets to liabilities fell slightly over the year, from 76.9% down to 76.5%, the ratio of Plan’s funding value of assets to its market value improved significantly, dropping from 125% down to 107% over the prior year.

7. The temporary increases noted in ERFC’s expense ratio between fiscal years 2008-2010, indicate a non-recurring payment period for the System’s automation upgrade.

Staff Contact:              Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Other Staff Present:   Denise James, director, Office of Facilities Planning

Meeting Category:     June 14, 2010 – Work Session No.132

Subject:                       Southwestern Regional Planning Study

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Key Points:

The Southwestern Regional Planning Committee which began meeting on October 2009 has completed Phases I and II of their assigned work. Staff commends and compliments all the members of the ad hoc planning group, particularly the leadership, including the co-chairs and sub-committee chairs, for their commitment to the work, the resulting analysis and creative solutions. Staff is especially grateful for the collegial spirit with which the committee engaged staff and for their patience as staff worked to support this first ad hoc planning process. The ideas bought forward by the committee provided new planning perspectives to staff in consideration of the focus issues: overcrowding in the region and renovation challenges with Clifton ES.

The updated staff report reaffirms the topographic and access challenges associated with renovating Clifton. One of the major, but not the only, challenge is the absence of public water and the water quality issues of the existing wells.

Unique characteristics of the Clifton Elementary School site will result in higher than normal renovation costs. Staff estimates that a full scope renovation of Clifton ES will cost about $35,287 per student which is significantly higher than similar renovations of other FCPS schools which cost about $22,805 per student. This cost factor is magnified due to the fact that the most recent projections indicate that enrollment is declining at Clifton Elementary School with an enrollment of 298 students projected for School Year 2015-16.

Should the School Board decide to close Clifton Elementary School, the building can be kept by the School Board in case of future unanticipated need. In the meantime, the School Board could consider leasing the building at a very minimal cost to the Town of Clifton to address that community’s understandable concerns about having a place to meet and gather. Other adaptive reuse options for the building and site could also be considered.

The FCPS educational specifications were developed to ensure a rich and appropriate educational environment at each of our schools. Staff does not believe that we should do a reduced scope renovation at Clifton Elementary School as a means to reduce cost unless the School Board is prepared to make similar reductions at all schools for future renovations. If existing excellent student achievement is a criterion by which to make a decision to reduce scope, we have a number of upcoming renovations for consideration.

FCPS has a history of closing schools when enrollments become too low or when site and building challenges do not allow renovations to bring a building up to current educational specifications or when the costs to do so become prohibitive. Wilton Woods, Pine Ridge, Masonville, Walnut Hill and Virginia Hills were all schools that were closed by action of the School Board. More recently, the School Board decided to move Graham Road from its current location to the Devonshire site due to constraints and costs associated with the existing site.


On Monday, at approximately 3 p.m., the School Board members and staff members are scheduled to take a tour of Clifton ES and Liberty MS.

Options:

Staff believes that the only viable option to closing Clifton Elementary School is to renovate that school and construct additions at several schools within the study area. Staff does not support keeping Clifton open and building a new school as this would be costly for both the capital program and for the operating fund.

The staff and the ad hoc committee reports offer different options to address overcrowding in the area elementary schools and to address the potential renovation of Clifton ES. The sub-committee reports outlining other solutions with pros and cons of the options is attached along with the participation results of the community outreach meetings.

Recommendations:

Staff recommends that the School Board accepts the Southwestern Regional Planning Committee Phase II report and the updated staff report.

Staff is recommending that based upon the information contained in these two reports that the School Board (1) build a new elementary school on the Liberty Middle School site; (2) build one or more additions on elementary schools in that region; (3) conduct a boundary study based upon the new school, the addition(s), and potential program moves; (4) close Clifton Elementary School upon completion of the new elementary school.

Should the School Board decide to renovate Clifton Elementary School, staff recommends that it be a complete renovation based upon the FCPS educational specifications. Furthermore, staff would recommend that additions be built on 2 or more elementary schools in the region to provide capacity to provide relief to overcrowding and conduct a boundary study based upon these additions and potential program moves.
