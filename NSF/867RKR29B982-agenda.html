Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 7 and 7:30 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

SUBJECT: Award of Contracts

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board award contracts for university intern programs to George Washington University in the amounts of $371,666, $168,000, and $71,766; to George Mason University in the amounts of $113,044 and $12,000; and to Marymount University in the amount of $112,500.

EXPLANATION:
      The George Mason University Professional Development School, the Marymount Professional Development Academy, and the George Washington University Transition to Teaching programs are ongoing efforts that train selected teacher education candidates in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS). Two other George Washington University programs provide interns in special education.

      These university programs provide access to an especially qualified group of applicants who understand the needs of FCPS students. They are funded from the substitute teacher account by trading off two instructional assistant (IA) vacancies and by placement of interns in existing IA vacancies. The universities supervise all student interns.

      State prescribed procedures are being used to award the contract to George Mason University. In the cases of George Washington and Marymount Universities, the agreements will be approved as sole source contracts on the basis that no other entities have expressed interest in these programs or can provide such a highly talented pool of substitute teachers at this time.

4.06 Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology - Continue in 2004-2005 to accept, for Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia.
Meeting
Jul 22, 2004 - Regular Meeting No. 2
Category
4. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Staff Contact: John English, Director, Cluster III

SUBJECT: Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board continue in 2004-2005 to accept , for Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia.
EXPLANATION:
      In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school. The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

· Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2005 is for $1,379,705. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $1,355,000.
· Cooperating with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
· Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
· Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

ALTERNATIVE:
      The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

Meeting
Jul 22, 2004 - Regular Meeting No. 2
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information
Staff Contact: Nicholas Fischer, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services


SUBJECT: Curriculum Advisory Committees


RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board appoint individuals to serve on the English Language Arts, Family Life Education, Fine Arts, Foreign Language, Health and Physical Education, Mathematics Curriculum Advisory Committees, and on the High School Course Review Committee, as listed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:
    Regulation 1707.6 establishes eight curriculum advisory committees for levels K through 12 to advise on Program of Studies development and implementation.
    Additionally, this agenda item covers appointments to the High School Course Review Committee, which annually identifies standard or optional course offerings for deletion, consolidation, or revision and makes recommendations for action by the Division Superintendent and the School Board (see the current version of Regulation 3202).

    The term of service for all School Board member representatives on the above-named advisory committees is one year. Representatives may be reappointed. Classroom teachers are elected to these committees and serve two-year terms, with elections being held during the spring of odd-numbered years. These terms will be effective until June 30, 2005. Other committee members serve terms as set forth in the appropriate regulation.
