    A Tribute to Mario Schiavo


    In his more than 18 years with Fairfax County Public Schools, Mario Schiavo has been an auditor, a budget coordinator, the ERFC finance coordinator, and—for the last three years—the budget director in the Department of Financial Services. His contributions in developing the program budget were invaluable, and his leadership in adapting and improving budget documents was critical for FCPS to continue to win both the Government Finance Officers Association and the Association of School Business Officials International budget awards during his tenure.

    Mario’s extraordinary work ethic, his uncompromising integrity, his respect for those he worked with, and his unfailing pursuit of excellence all have made him a model for other FCPS employees.

    Mario Schiavo will always be known as a true gentleman whose many accomplishments—which include developing a needs-based staffing model now used in all FCPS elementary and middle schools and explaining the budget to the community and thereby furthering the goal of transparency in financial operations—were achieved with grace, dedication, humility, kindness, and a wonderful sense of humor.

    Meeting

    Category

    Tonight’s public hearing on the Mountain View Sanitary Sewer Lateral Easement has been canceled because the easement was granted voluntarily by the adjacent property owner, who agreed to grant it for no compensation.  Therefore, this related agenda item that was scheduled for action by the Board tonight has also been canceled.

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

    SUBJECT: Acquisition of Sanitary Sewer Lateral and Temporary Construction Easements for Mountain View Alternative School site

    RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board (i) adopt the attached Resolution approving proposed public uses and authorizing the acquisition, if necessary by condemnation, of sanitary sewer lateral and temporary construction easements (the “Easements”) necessary for such public uses, and (ii) authorize the Chief Operating Officer to execute the necessary documents to acquire such Easements, utilizing the School Board’s power of eminent domain if necessary, and to work with the School Board attorney to accomplish such acquisition promptly.
    EXPLANATION:

            Ten modular classrooms are being added to Mountain View Alternative School and are scheduled to be available when school starts this September. These classrooms require installation of a sanitary sewer lateral across a small portion of an adjacent shopping center site. To install this sewer lateral, the School Board must obtain a permanent sanitary sewer lateral easement and a temporary construction easement from the adjacent property owner - PW Limited Partnership (“PW”).
            School officials first approached PW in the summer of 2006 to request the easements. Last fall, PW orally agreed to grant the necessary easements, provided certain matters were addressed in the easement documents. The form of the Deed of Easement was agreed upon in January, 2007. To date, PW has not signed and delivered the Deed, despite numerous requests.
            While PW could still deliver the signed Deed, PW’s delay leaves the School Board no choice but to pursue acquisition of the easements by condemnation in time to complete the modular classroom project for the 2007-08 school year. The School Board can acquire the easements pursuant to its “quick-take” authority under law. With this procedure, installation of the sanitary sewer lateral could begin immediately after a Certificate of Taking is recorded and the amount of the appraisal - $13,808.00 - is deposited with the Circuit Court.

    6.03 Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology - Recommendation to approve Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) continue in 2007-2008 to accept the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia (Cluster III; action scheduled 7/26/07)
    Meeting
    Jul 12, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 1
    Category
    6. New Business
    Type
    Information


    Staff Contact: Cecelia Krill, Assistant Superintendent, Cluster III

    SUBJECT: Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) continue in 2007-2008 to accept the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia.
    EXPLANATION:
        In accordance with Policy 3355.2, High School for Science and Technology, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school. The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a governor’s regional school is based on the following advantages:
            • Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2008 is for $1,557,846. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $2,100,000.
            • Cooperating with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
            • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
            • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

    ALTERNATIVE:
        The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

    6.04 Award of Contract - Recommendation to approve the award of contract for providing dental benefit services for FCPS employees to Aetna Inc., the current vendor, effective January 1, 2008, and through the duration of the three (3) year contract and up to three (3) one-year extensions as recommended by the Office of Benefit Services and the Selection Advisory Committee (HR; action scheduled 7/26/07)
    Meeting
    Jul 12, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 1
    Category
    6. New Business
    Type
    Information


    Staff Contact: Kevin L. North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

    SUBJECT: Award of Contract

    RECOMMENDATION:

        That the School Board approve the award of contract for providing dental benefit services for FCPS employees to Aetna Inc., the current vendor, effective January 1, 2008, and through the duration of the three (3) year contract and up to three (3) one-year extensions as recommended by the Office of Benefit Services and the Selection Advisory Committee.

    EXPLANATION:
        The Request for Proposal required that proposals be submitted based on our current plan designs. The Request for Proposal was sent to all benefit service vendors who are registered in the eVA (Virginia e-Procurement Solution). The Selection Advisory Committee received proposals from six vendors. The responsive vendors were Aetna, Inc., Cigna Healthcare, CareFirst/NCAS, Delta Dental, Dominion Dental, and United Concordia. All proposals were evaluated based on the following defined criteria:
          • Plan Design
          • Company Overview, Account Management, Member Services and Claims Processing
          • Provider Networks
          • Quality Assurance
          • Dental Management
          • Information Systems/Reporting
          • Financial
          • Communications
          • Implementation
          • HIPAA Compliance
          • Fees and Pricing
          • Funding Arrangements
          • Performance Guarantees
        The administrative fees for the DPPO self insured plan will be reduced from $3.42 to $2.35 per enrollee per month and will be guaranteed at that amount for the initial three years of the contract. This represents a 31.3 percent decrease in fees over last year’s fees. For the current plan design, the premium for the fully insured DHMO plan will remain the same with a zero percent increase in premiums over last year’s costs.

        Overall this contract yielded Dental Benefit savings of approximately $240,000 per year or $721,000 for the three (3) year contract period.
        Based on the above criteria, Aetna Inc. is the highest-rated offeror for the FCPS Dental Benefit Program.
