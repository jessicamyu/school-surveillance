Chairman Derenak Kaufax, Dr. Garza, Members of the School Board:

 

I apologize for my absence this evening.

 

I am honored to be a Senator representing the Commonwealth of Virginia at the American Legion’s Boys Nation program being held at Marymount University (Arlington, Virginia) from July 18th through the 25th.

 

I am meeting with and learning from fellow rising high school seniors who have traveled from around the nation to represent their states in Washington, DC. The program also gives me an opportunity to meet with public school students from across our nation to compare the sometimes very different programs, policies, and structures that can be found in our respective school districts. I hope these insights will better my contribution as Student Representative.

 

As for tonight’s Board meeting, I am familiar with the agenda and would like to emphasize my support for the adoption of the changes to the Master Calendar.

 

For those interested, my newsletter from my first board meeting is posted on the School Board’s website under “Student Representative;” the next installment will be posted after my return from the Boys Nation program.

 

To close, I am looking forward to attending the 2014 FCPS Leadership Conference in early August and the next Board Meeting in September.

 

Thank you for your attention and, on behalf of my 97 fellow Student Senators, I send you best wishes for a constructive board meeting and a relaxing summer.

Meeting Category:     July 24, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                       FY 2014 Final Budget Review     

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                                       

 

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2014 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2014 Third Quarter Budget Review. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the revenue variance and FY 2015 budgeted beginning balance, the flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the FY 2014 funds available total $38.2 million.

 

Revenue variances begin on page 1 of the agenda attachment, and FY 2014 actual total funds available were $7.5 million lower, or totaled 99.7 percent, of the FY 2014 Third Quarter Budget Review.  State aid for FY 2014 totaled $387.6 million which is $0.5 million more than budgeted, and sales tax receipts total $169.7 million which is $0.2 million less than budgeted.  A decrease of $10.1 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $8.7 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2015 combined with a net decrease in other federal funding.  Other revenue received was $2.3 million more than the FY 2014 Third Quarter Budget Review.  Of this net increase, tuition from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $0.5 million based on estimated final year-end activity, while revenue from tuition, fees, and other was $2.8 million more than budgeted.

 

Expenditure variances also begin on page 1 of the agenda attachment, and FY 2014 actual expenditures were $37.0 million less than projected after funding the FY 2015 beginning balance adjustments, funding the School Board’s Flexibility Reserve, and accounting for other commitments and carryover, excluding grants. A majority of this variance results from compensation accounts being $30.5 million less than budgeted due primarily to higher compensation lapse than projected; utilities expenditures being $2.5 million less than projected; and county charges for vehicle services, including fuel, being $2.5 million less than projected.  In addition, $8.7 million in multiyear federal grant funds were unexpended.  This funding will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2015. 

 

This agenda item also includes recommendations for $15.1 million in FY 2015 expenditure adjustments, including $7.6 million for full-day Mondays, $3.6 million for major maintenance, $2.4 million for bus purchases, and $1.5 for the joint Board of Supervisors/School Board Synthetic Turf Initiative; leaving $23.1 million available for the FY 2016 budgeted beginning balance.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments in the FY 2014 final budget review, as detailed in the agenda item.

Accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor's Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2014-2015 (Region 1; presented as new busines 7/10/14

Staff Contact:         Douglas Tyson, Regional Assistant Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  July 24, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                 Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.  The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

  • Additional state funding for the regional school.  The estimate for FY 2015 is $2,729,637.  The tuition for out-of-county students is $3,981,167.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor's School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.             

Options: 

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions. 

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor's Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2014-2015.

[Updated 7/21/14]

 

Staff Contact:         Pam Goddard, Executive Assistant and Clerk of the Board

 

Meeting Category:  July 24, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                 Strategic Governance Manual Revisions

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related to:            Strategic Governance Manual

 

Key Points:

 

Throughout the Board’s work this year, changes to the Strategic Governance Manual have been discussed.  Recommendations from the Governance Committee were discussed with the full Board, and there was agreement on these recommendations.  The revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual are as follows:

 

Section 3:  Board Self-Governance Policies

  1. Updated list of Board liaisons (page 38) to add Successful Child and Youth Policy Team, and  rename The Foundation for Fairfax County Public Schools
  2. Changes in citizen advisory committee membership terms from two years to one year (discussed at the April 24, 2014, forum WS) (pages 42-49)
  3. Changes in citizen advisory committee membership requested by MSAOC (pages 44-45):
    1. Add Hispanic Educators Association to the community organizations
  4. Changes in citizens advisory committee membership requested by HRAC (pages 45-46)
    1. Add American Association of University Women, Asian Educators Association, Community Ministry of Northern Virginia, Hispanic Educators Association, and Jewish Community Relations Council of Greater Washington to the community organizations
    2. Remove the Hispanic Committee of Virginia and the Organization of Chinese Americans—Northern Virginia chapter from the community organizations
    3. Revise and update three community organization’s names:  Korean Association of Virginia, High School Principals’ Association, and Boat People SOS (replaces Vietnamese Committee of Washington, Maryland, and Virginia)
    4. Change the number of members recommended by the Office of Equity and Employee Relations from six to three

Section 4:  Superintendent/Board Relationship

 

Changes in the superintendent’s evaluation due to new state requirements (S.2., page 52)

 

Section 5:  Appendices

  1. Update School Board/staff communication process (Appendix B, page 62) with the guidelines for client communication
  2. Change in meeting manager responsibilities (Appendix C, page 63) to indicate the “next steps” will be taken by the Clerk
  3. Updated list of ad hoc committees (Appendix K, page 78) by adding Superintendent’s Evaluation Ad Hoc Committee, and two joint committees with the Board of Supervisors:  Infrastructure Financing Committee and Joint Budgeting Development Committee
  4. Addition of Appendix L with the superintendent’s evaluation document
  5. Revisions notes become Appendix M

Minor edits throughout the Manual for clarity and consistency of processes, including organizational changes within FCPS effective July 1, 2014 (clusters to regions, job titles, office titles, staff liaisons).

 

The Clerk of the Board will finalize all revisions to the Manual prior to the scheduled action date of September 4, 2014.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve the proposed revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual in Sections 3, 4, and 5, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
