Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

 

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

 

The School Board will recognize Cole DePasquale from Robinson Secondary School, the recipient of the 2015 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship

SCHOOL BOARD CHARACTER AWARD

Presented To

Jorge Douglas Romero Del Cid

Lee High School

 

 

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board Chris Giovarelli and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. The Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation has generously funded the $500 award since 2007.  The award is given to a junior or senior, who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for students in Fairfax County Public Schools in day-to-day behavior. 

 

Jorge Douglas Romero Del Cid, a junior at Lee High School, is the recipient of the 2015 School Board Character Award.

 

Jorge truly embodies the traits required of the School Board Character Award. Jorge has not had a typical high school experience. In 2011, he left his parents and extended family in a country with conflict and turmoil and came to the United States with a dream of earning his high school diploma. When he arrived, he did not speak one word of English and had to adjust to a new country, a new culture, and a new language, all without the support of his parents and family. Jorge’s goal was to learn to read and speak English as quickly as possible so he could advance academically and, after four years, he has successfully achieved this goal and excels at his studies.

 

Jorge is an accomplished student in the field of Electrical Construction and Engineering at the Academy for Engineering and Scientific Technology and serves as a student ambassador. In addition to receiving several awards for outstanding performance, Jorge maintains an A average and has passed the first and second levels of the electrical apprenticeship exam. He is an intern at Mount Vernon Estates and works to ensure that the electrical system for George Washington’s residence is operational and secure.

 

Jorge is a role model and leader for other students, not only in school but also in his church and his community. He is active in his church youth group, has been involved in a community program that collects and distributes food to those less fortunate, and is an avid and talented soccer player.

 

Jorge’s willingness to accept difficult challenges, his strong sense of responsibility, and his commitment to succeed in the United States has led to his great success and provided significant opportunities for his future. He has always demonstrated an exceptional work ethic, high morals, strong integrity and admirable character and, as one of his nominators noted, “Jorge is the epitome of what we want in a student and community member.”

Fairfax County Public Schools and the Fairfax County and Fairfax City Police Departments have established a mutually beneficial relationship for more than a decade. 6.01 Process for the Approved Budget Agenda Item - Recommendation to approve the process for the approved budget main motion, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly (SB; action scheduled 6/25/15)
Meeting
Jun 11, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 21
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Contact:                   Pam Goddard, Executive Assistant and Clerk of the Board

 

Meeting Category:  June 11, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 21

 

Subject:                  Process for the Approved Budget Agenda Item

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related To:            Strategic Governance

 

Key Points:  

 

At the December 11, 2014, forum work session, the Board discussed the topic of defining the process used to establish the School Board’s main motion to adopt the approved budget during the May regular School Board meeting.  There was consensus to refer the issue to the Governance Committee, who would bring a recommendation to the full Board.

 

The Governance Committee discussed the topic on January 15, 2015, and reviewed two agenda options presented by staff that the Board used from FY 2012 to FY 2015 to adopt the approved budget at the second regular Board meeting in May.  The Committee agreed that the options would be presented to the Board for further discussion.

 

At the March 16, 2015, work session, the Board discussed the two options presented by the Governance Committee, and there was Board consensus to approve the following next steps:

  • Budget Chair and Vice Chair to craft a new omnibus motion after presentation of the Superintendent’s recommendation, receive majority support at the budget work session, and post an omnibus motion for action as the main budget motion. 

The recommendation is to include the following information in the Strategic Governance Manual as a new Appendix M, and add a note to page 38, #3.e., Annual Work Calendar and Planning Cycle (to refer to the new Appendix M).

 

Agenda Format as Presented for New Business:

At the first School Board meeting in May, the required revenue and expenditure adjustments will be listed first.  Then a placeholder will show the amount required to balance the budget.  Based on the discussion at the budget work session that is typically scheduled in late April or early May, the Superintendent’s recommendations for eliminating the placeholder will be presented. 

 

EXAMPLE:  FY 2016 Approved Budget adoption language for the main motion:

That the School Board adopt the FY 2016 Approved Budget as detailed in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget, modified by the adjustments included in this agenda item.

 

Agenda Format as Presented for Action:

For the second School Board meeting in May, the School Board will take action on the approved budget; the main motion language will be revised to include an omnibus motion.  The omnibus motion will be a substitute for the placeholder and will include adjustments to balance the budget that the School Board Budget Chair and Vice Chair have determined have consensus.  Any amendments would then be made to the main motion, which is the omnibus motion.

 

EXAMPLE:  FY 2016 Approved Budget adoption language for the main (omnibus) motion:

I move that the School Board adopt the FY 2016 Approved Budget, as detailed in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget, and as detailed in the agenda item to include Section I, Operating Fund Required Revenue Adjustments, with total reductions of $10.1 million, and Section II, Operating Fund Required Expenditure Adjustments items A through D, with total reductions of $2.5 million, and replacing Section II.E., Reduction Placeholder of $7.6 million, with the following adjustment:  reducing the 1.0 percent market scale adjustment included in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget to 0.62 percent, resulting in a savings of $7.6 million.

 

RECOMMENDATION:

 

That the School Board approve the process for the approved budget main motion, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

Subject:                  Appointment of “individual Trustee” to the Board of Trustees of the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC)

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related To:            Legal Requirement

 

Key Points: 

 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the “individual Trustee.”  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills. 

 

At a recent meeting of the ERFC Board of Trustees, Michael Burke, the Managing Director for the Wealth and Investment Management division of Barclays, was unanimously recommended for appointment as the “individual Trustee.”  If approved by the School Board, Mr. Burke will serve for his third year on the ERFC Board.  Mr. Burke meets all of the requirements of the position.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the appointment of Michael Burke to the ERFC Board of Trustees for a one-year term beginning July 1, 2015.

[Posted 6/11/15]

 

Staff Contact:          Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category:  June 11, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 21

 

Subject:                  Reappointment to Board of Trustees of the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC)

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related To:           Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is a seven-member board. According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, three of the trustees shall be representatives appointed by Fairfax County School Board.

 

The two representatives seeking approval for reappointment are:

 

Susan Quinn, chief operating officer, (trustee for past seven years)

 

Kristen Michael, chief financial officer, Financial Services (trustee for past year)

 

The third representative will be recommended for appointment at a later date.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the reappointment of Susan Quinn and Kristen Michael to the ERFC Board of Trustees for a one-year term beginning July 1, 2015, and ending June 30, 2016.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
