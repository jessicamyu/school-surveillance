Policy 4310, Authorization for Employment–Temporary Personnel

Last revised March 2013 with no changes; July 2008 prior.

Discuss:

How does this policy help or hinder efforts in situations where long-term coverage is needed, especially for long-term classroom substitutes? Would like clarification about the parameters of what this policy is aimed at achieving.

 

      Staff Response:

 

The policy specifies the division’s willingness to employ hourly/temporary personnel when needed to meet various program and activity needs within the organization. The alternative would be a rule restricting the employment of hourly/temporary personnel. In that case we would perhaps have to hire full-time or part-time personnel with an associated FTE and established work schedule which could far exceed a work requirement with a shorter term or seasonal in nature. Long-term substitute coverage is limited to 90 days by state code so it falls into the realm of a short-term/temporary assignment. Through this policy the division also permits employees who hold full-time or less-than-full-time positions to be hired for hourly/temporary assignments before or after their normal work hours or contract days – summer school, curriculum development, weekend test proctoring, etc.

 

Policy 4102, Compensation Plan for Support Personnel

I agree with the changes, however, suggest listing the jobs that are being covered in this policy - by name and/or category. Also, does our recent change in supporting collective bargaining result in any needed update here?

 

            Staff Response:

 

The definition included in the policy (and copied below) provides an overview of the covered positions. A full list of jobs would be more detail than typically found in a policy. Additionally, position classifications are continually being reviewed and adjusted as needed. Therefore, any list captured in the policy would regularly be outdated and would require the policy to be reviewed and updated on a more frequent basis and likely multiple times per year.

 

We don’t think the Board’s support of collective bargaining requires any changes. The purpose of the policy – essentially to provide a compensation plan - is still relevant regardless of how it is accomplished.

 

III. DEFINITION

 

Eligible school division operational employees include personnel engaged in assignments that support educational programs; such assignments include, but are not limited to, supervisors, specialists, technical personnel, school-based assistants and attendants, trades personnel, transportation personnel, custodial personnel, office assistant personnel, and food service workers.
