Recognition of National Career and Technical Education Month - February 2022

Career and Technical Education or “CTE” month is a public awareness campaign held every February to celebrate the achievements and accomplishments of such programs nationwide. This year's theme, "Celebrate Today, Own Tomorrow!", raises awareness of the innovation and excellence that exists in career and technical education programs such as those in FCPS, as well as the crucial role that this education has in readying students for careers and contributing to economic success. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 18 of the 20 fastest growing occupations within the next decade will require career and technical education.

Career and technical education prepares both youth and adults for a wide range of careers, requiring varied levels of education, including a high school diploma, postsecondary certificate, and two- and four-year college degrees.

Fairfax County Public Schools has programs that exemplify career and technical education, with offerings that include: Business & Information Technology -  such as Accounting, Management,  and Software Development; Family & Consumer Services - such as Early Childhood Development and Restaurant Services; Health & Medical Sciences - including Emergency Medical Technician, Pharmacy Technician, and Veterinary Sciences; Military Science and JROTC; Marketing; Engineering and Technology, such as Aerospace Science and Architectural Drawing; and Trade and Industrial Education, including Automotive Technology, Electrical Construction and Engineering, Cosmetology, Criminal Justice, Photography, and TV and Media Production.

Through these opportunities, students are able to get a sense of careers they may wish to have and understand the necessary steps to become a professional in that discipline.

Thank you to all our career and technical education educators and staff who help students realize Portrait of a Graduate outcomes and ensure students are college and career ready!

National School Counseling Week – February 7 - 11, 2022

National School Counseling Week 2022 (#NSCW22), "School Counseling: Better Together," will be celebrated from Feb 7 - 11, 2022, to focus public attention on the unique contribution of school counselors. National School Counseling Week, sponsored by the American School Counselor Association (ASCA), highlights the tremendous impact school counselors can have in helping students achieve school success and plan for a career. National School Counseling Week is always celebrated the first full week in February.

Fairfax County Public Schools employs close to 700 school counseling professionals, including school counselors, directors of student services, and college and career specialists. These professionals are committed to preparing students to become lifelong learners and productive members in a global society with a focus on social emotional wellness, academic success, and college and career readiness. Thank you to all our school counseling professionals for the tremendous work they do!

As part of National School Counseling Week, it is an honor to congratulate Kathryn Teague, director of student services at Katherine Johnson Middle School, who was recognized as the FCPS Outstanding School-Based Leader for 2021.

RESOLUTION

HONORING

ASHA-JYOTHI

 

WHEREAS, Asha-Jyothi, a volunteer-based global nonprofit organization based in Chantilly is dedicated to supporting the community through health care and education initiatives and has given over $250,000 to Fairfax County Public Schools; and

 

WHEREAS, distributed as part of Asha-Jyothi’s Educate-Innovate Initiative, the grants provide funding for the schools to enhance their technology education, build Innovation Labs and Maker Spaces, inspiring our students to be the trailblazers of tomorrow; Through the grants and the opportunities they provide, they hope that all children will be inspired to design, experiment, build and invent, while believing that anything is possible; and

 

WHEREAS, through its annual 5K run/walk, Asha-Jyothi encourages community interest in and support of technology education for area youth, matching and distributing the registration fees to the schools with representatives participating in the event, an initiative that resulted in thousands of dollars in donations to our Fairfax County Public Schools; and

 

WHEREAS, The Educate-Innovate program has given $106,000 to build the Innovation Lab at Chantilly High School in 2018. The lab offers students access to state-of-the-art 3D printing, audiovisual technology and recording, take-apart technology, writable walls, robotics and more; and 

 

WHEREAS, Rocky Run Middle School was a grand recipient of $60,000 grant in 2019 to build a maker space allowing middle school kids to create, learn and play together in the new inspiration studio. Asha-Jyothi has also donated a total of $18,000 to Fairfax County public schools in 2021 including two individual technology grants to Katherine Johnson Middle School and Little Run Elementary School; and

 

WHEREAS, through its grants to schools in the Commonwealth and throughout the country, Asha-Jyothi demonstrates a remarkable commitment to fostering the next generation of technology inventors and innovators;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its deepest gratitude and appreciation to Asha-Jyothi for their valuable contributions to enhance the education of students in Fairfax County Public Schools to help them pursue 21st-century career opportunities.

Citizens who want to address the Board at a Regular meeting, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. four (4) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. For more information, please visit the FCPS Website.

 

A total of 16 in-person, live virtual, and prerecorded video speaker slots are available per meeting. Thirteen slots will be reserved for in-person and live virtual speakers.  Three (3) slots will be reserved for students and three (3) slots will be reserved for those speakers who wish to submit a prerecorded video. The speakers list closes at 48 hours before the meeting. After the registration closes, speakers will be determined by a random lottery process.  After the random process, speakers will be notified if they have received a speaking or prerecorded video spot.   After the first 13 in-person or live virtual speakers are determined, the next ten (10) names as determined by the same random process will be chosen as the waitlist and notified that they are on the waitlist to speak.  Video submissions will not be permitted for the waitlist.  Waitlist speakers must be present at the meeting to speak in the event of a no show.  Speaker substitutions are not permitted.  A speaker may not yield their time to another individual before or during their remarks.

 

The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

Other Staff Present

Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

Maura Burke, director, Early Childhood Curriculum, ISD

Tracey Jewell, director, Support Services, IT

 

Meeting Category:  January 18, 2022 Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

FCPS received approval for a reimbursement grant from the Federal Communications Commission under the Emergency Connectivity Fund (ECF) to purchase sufficient and appropriate devices to support learning at school or at home for PreK - 2 students. FCPS would use the grant funds to refresh aging classroom student devices for our youngest learners.

 

The proposal includes purchasing more age-appropriate iPad devices with keyboard case for students in grades PreK and K, and standard Windows laptop devices for students in grades 1 and 2.

 

FCPS would invest one-time funds of $2.1M from the FY22 School Board Flexibility Reserve to support the basic device purchase and request another $1.8M in FY22 year-end, one-time funds to support the long-term warranty/repair and supplemental hardware purchase.

 

Recommendation:

 

School Board approve the purchase of up to 43,000 devices at the total cost of $18.4M with the local share of $2.1 million to be funded from School Board Flexibility Reserve and upon delivery file for reimbursement with the ECF grant fund for $16.3M.
