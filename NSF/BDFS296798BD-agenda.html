Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2020

 

The American Alliance for Theater and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development.  The theme is "Fostering a Culture of Creativity".  Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.  

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2020

 

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  This year's theme is "All Music, All People!" and is designed to raise awareness of the importance of music education for all children and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music.  It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages. 

 

Youth Art Month - March 2020

 

Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.  The theme for Youth Art Month 2020 is “Your Art, Your Story.” 

The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

VSBA Equity in Education Month – March 2020

 

WHEREAS, obtaining a quality education and having more opportunities for success beyond what was available to their own generation are shared aspirations parents have for their own children; and

 

WHEREAS, the reality, sadly, is that opportunity gaps still remain for traditionally underserved children in the United States, Virginia, and Fairfax County; and that by shortchanging students in their efforts to achieve their goals, we have impacted not only the individual students, their parents and families, but also society as a whole by limiting the lifetime benefits we would have all otherwise received from students’ fully developed skills, talents, and ideas; and

 

WHEREAS, we recognize that factors including, but not limited to, race, color, ethnicity, disability, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, national origin, and socioeconomic status have a demonstrated history of impacting the access and quality of educational opportunities provided to a student; and

 

WHEREAS, we understand that despite past efforts, disparities continue in schools that present substantial obstacles or detours in career and life success for students who are not offered equitable access to challenging courses for their abilities; school counselors; strong teachers; a safe educational environment; and buildings, supplies, or technologies that are conducive for learning; and   

 

WHEREAS, the promise of public education is for every child in our diverse county to receive the resources and supports they need to be successful in school, college, career, and in life; and

 

WHEREAS, it is important for Fairfax families, students, teachers, and school administrators to be aware of the diverse needs of learners, and to encourage discussion of the challenges as a school community and to highlight the need for a continued focus on educational equity so that all students can be all that they can be; and

 

WHEREAS, the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA) has established a Task Force on Students and Schools in Challenging Environments to make recommendations and to stay at the forefront of discussions on equity in Virginia schools;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that Fairfax County Public Schools recognizes the month of March 2020 as VSBA Equity in Education Month, with the intention that educational equity, best practices, and programming be discussed and promoted in FCPS schools, communities, and classrooms during this time, and for always.

 Subject: Electric School Bus Program (Dominion Energy)

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Other Staff Present: Francine Furby, director, Office of Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: March 5, 2020 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 1 Student Success and Goal 4 Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

In August 2019, Governor Ralph Northam announced Dominion Energy’s (“Dominion”) initiative to partner with selected Virginia school districts for a pilot of 50 electric school buses to be in operation by the close of 2020, with future plans to convert all new school buses in Virginia to electric by 2030.

 

Electric buses are designed to provide environmental and health benefits through reduced emissions as well as reduced operational and maintenance costs.

 

Following a competitive application process, Dominion announced the award of 8 electric buses to Fairfax County Public Schools, which is one of sixteen Virginia school divisions selected to participate.

 

Four of the new electric buses are scheduled to be delivered to FCPS in August 2020, with the remaining 4 to arrive in December 2020.

 

In this pilot phase, each participating school district would purchase the school bus chassis along with certain equipment (e.g., seat belts), but without a diesel engine. Dominion would fund the remaining cost of the electric school bus (approximately $200,000/per bus) as well as the cost of installing vehicle to grid charging infrastructure on district property for each participating district on district property. The vehicle to grid technology allows Dominion Energy to use the school buses’ batteries to supply the grid when the buses are not in use, creating additional energy storage.

 

Dominion and FCPS are now negotiating the terms of an agreement that provides for the operation of the school buses and the rights of the parties over the 15 year contractual term.

 

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board (i) approve the contract with Dominion Energy whereby FCPS will operate eight electric school buses for the contract term in conjunction with charging infrastructure installed on FCPS property pursuant to an agreed-upon temporary easement for the benefit of Dominion, and (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, with the advice of legal counsel, to negotiate, execute and administer this contract and all related documents on behalf of the School Board.
