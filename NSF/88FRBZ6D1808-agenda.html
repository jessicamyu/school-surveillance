Subject:                     Proposed Program/Attendance Area Adjustment Southwestern Boundary Study        

 

School Board Action Required:     Discussion

 

Related To:                 School Board Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings

 

Key Points:

 

On September 23, 2010, the School Board authorized a boundary study to address membership and capacity imbalances in elementary schools in the southwestern portion of the county and to provide a reassignment plan for students affected by the School Board’s July 8, 2010, decision to close Clifton Elementary School. The School Board’s action to initiate a boundary study was based on community planning efforts which began under the Southwestern Regional Planning Study Committee (SWRPSC), an ad hoc citizen planning committee.

The Board further provided the following objectives and parameters for the study in addition to those identified in School Board Policy 8130:

 

Objectives

  • Maximize efficient use of funding for both capital and operating budgets.
  • Achieve a building utilization between approximately 95% - 105% of program capacity at all elementary schools within the study area.

           

Parameters

  • Building additions may be considered at one or more of the following elementary schools: Bonnie Brae, Cub Run, Fairfax Villa, Fairview, Greenbriar East, and Union Mill.
  • No building additions will result in a school’s program capacity exceeding 950 students.

 

Staff conducted multiple community dialog meetings to present and receive feedback on the initial boundary options (Options A, B, and C).  Based on this initial feedback a fourth option (Option D) was developed incorporating community suggestions and addressing the need to accommodate future full-day kindergarten for some schools in the study that currently have half-day programs.  Option D was posted to the FCPS Facilities Planning web site and electronic feedback on all four options was received and posted.  The staff recommended boundary adjustments presented in the attached Appendix A reflect this community feedback, and the guidance provided by the School Board.

 

Discussion:

 

The staff recommendation substantially achieves the School Board objectives regarding utilization rates and maximum program capacities and reflects the closing of Clifton Elementary School at the end of the 2010-11 school year. 

 

The staff recommendation suggests a phased approach to implementing the boundary adjustments.  Phase I includes adjustments related to the closing of Clifton Elementary School and adjustments that take advantage of existing capacity surpluses.  All Phase I adjustments would be implemented for the 2011-12 school year.  Phase II adjustments, to be implemented for the 2013-2014 school year, rely on the additional capacity provided by permanent building additions at Fairfax Villa, Greenbriar East, and Union Mill Elementary Schools (to be completed for the 2013-14 school year), and a temporary modular relocation to Centreville Elementary School (to be completed for the 2012-13 school year).

 

Phase I

 

The Phase I adjustment sends Clifton Elementary School students to three schools: Fairview, Oak View, and Union Mill.  Phase I also includes proposed changes for Bonnie Brae, Brookfield, Bull Run, Cub Run, Deer Park, Eagle View, Fairview, Fairfax Villa, Greenbriar East, Greenbriar West, London Towne, Oak View, Poplar Tree, Providence, Virginia Run, and Willow Springs Elementary Schools, to  balance enrollment and utilization by taking advantage of existing and projected capacity surpluses.

 

Phase II

 

Phase II includes changes implemented in the 2013-14 school year for Centreville, Centre Ridge, Colin Powell, Eagle View, Fairfax Villa, Greenbriar East, and Union Mill Elementary Schools to coincide with the completion of the permanent building additions.

 

Two of the 23 schools in the study, Laurel Ridge and Sangster Elementary Schools, are not impacted by boundary change recommendations.  No changes are proposed at this time for middle and high school boundaries, except as noted below, to accommodate middle school Advanced Academic Program center assignments.  With the exception of Clifton students, the proposed boundary adjustments conform to School Board policy which allows grandfathering for rising 6th grade students.

 

Advanced Academic Program (AAP) Assignments

 

In addition to boundary changes that balance enrollments, several AAP center assignment changes are proposed. The proposed AAP center assignment changes also serve to align school boundary changes with AAP center assignments. Clifton students assigned to Fairview would attend the White Oaks Elementary AAP center (or remain at Fairview for Level IV services) and attend the Lake Braddock AAP center for middle school.  Students from Clifton that are assigned to Oak View would attend the Willow Springs AAP center (or remain at Oak View for Level IV services) and the Lake Braddock AAP center for middle school.  Clifton students moving to Union Mill would remain at the Willow Springs Elementary and Rocky Run Middle AAP centers.  AAP students from the area of Fairview Elementary moved to Oak View Elementary would attend the Willow Springs AAP Center (or remain at Fairview for Level IV services) and attend the Lake Braddock AAP center for middle school.  AAP students from the portion of Fairview assigned to Bonnie Brae Elementary will continue to attend the White Oaks AAP Center for elementary and the Lake Braddock AAP center for middle school.  AAP students from the area of Poplar Tree re-assigned to Cub Run will continue to attend the AAP center at Greenbriar West.  The staff recommendation also proposes to change the AAP center assignment of Eagle View Elementary from the Greenbriar West AAP center to the Willow Springs AAP center. 

 

The staff recommendation to build three permanent building additions maximizes efficient use of funding for capital/operating costs by placing the additional capacity where it is needed and utilizing existing building infrastructure and administration. Temporary relocation of the modular addition utilizes an asset already in the FCPS inventory.  The total estimated facilities cost of this recommendation, including interior space modifications at Fairview, is $15,120,420.

The recommended adjustments will result in the movement of students from full-day to half-day kindergarten programs for the following:

 

Eagle View to Willow Springs

Greenbriar East to Greenbriar West

Poplar Tree to Virginia Run

Centreville and Centre Ridge to Union Mill

 

The Board may wish to consider the implementation of full-day kindergarten at Virginia Run, Greenbriar West, and Willow Springs for the 2011-12 school year and at Union Mill for the 2013-14 school year in order to provide the same level of kindergarten program for those students moving from schools with full-day kindergarten to schools with half-day programs.    

 

Concerns regarding SACC placement for students affected by boundary changes were reviewed with the Fairfax County Office for Children.  The Office for Children has committed to making every effort to accommodate students affected by the proposed boundary changes; however, placement will be based on SACC guidelines and available capacity. 

 

Recommendation:

 

1. That the School Board approve adjustments to school attendance areas in order to contribute to the efficiency of the school division and as consistent with applicable School Board policy, as shown in Appendix A:

           

(a)  effective for the 2011-12 school year for the following schools: 

Bonnie Brae, Brookfield, Bull Run, Clifton, Cub Run, Deer Park, Eagle View, Fairview,

Fairfax Villa, Greenbriar East, Greenbriar West, London Towne, Oak View, PoplarTree,

Providence, Virginia Run, and Willow Springs Elementary Schools; and

 

(b)  effective for the 2013-14 school year for the following schools:

Centreville, Centre Ridge, Colin Powell, Eagle View, Fairfax Villa,

Greenbriar East, and Union Mill; and

 

2.  That the School Board authorize staff to use existing bond authority within the construction reserve account: to design and construct building additions at Fairfax Villa, Greenbriar East, and Union Mill Elementary Schools; to relocate a modular building to Centreville Elementary School; to perform interior architectural modifications to Fairview Elementary School; and to otherwise implement the Board’s action, as detailed in the agenda item.
