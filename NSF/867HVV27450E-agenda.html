    2.04 Resolution Honoring Visiting Japanese Teacher
    Meeting

    Type
    Information

    RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING
    VISITING JAPANESE TEACHER

    WHEREAS, the Fukuoka Prefecture and Fairfax County Public Schools have participated in the Regional Educational Exchange program (REX), sponsored by the Japanese Ministry of Education, since 1991; and

    WHEREAS, Mr. Minoru Noma, REX teacher at Great Falls Elementary School and Cooper Middle School has worked in the Japanese partial-immersion program since August 2005; and

    WHEREAS, he has provided, to the Japanese partial-immersion program, invaluable assistance in creating instructional materials, in teaching students, and in sharing aspects of Japanese culture both with the students participating in the partial-immersion program and with students in the regular program; and

    WHEREAS, he has fostered and promoted positive relations between the United States and Japan in helping American students and their parents learn to speak the Japanese language and know more about Japanese culture;

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Mr. Minoru Noma for his contributions and dedicated service to the educational programs at Great Falls Elementary and Cooper Middle Schools.

    5.03 Easement - Grant a sanitary sewer easement at Hayfield Secondary School to Kendrick Associates, LLC, with compensation in the amount of $2,000 (FTS; presented as new business 1/25/07)
    Meeting
    Feb 7, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 13
    Category
    5. Consent Agenda
    Type
    Action

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

    SUBJECT: Easement

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board grant the sanitary sewer easement at Hayfield Secondary School to Kendrick Associates, LLC, with compensation in the amount of $2,000.
    EXPLANATION:
        Kendrick Associates, LLC, the applicant, is proposing sanitary sewer improvements, which includes installing an underground gravity sewer pipeline and connection to an existing sewer main located at the subject property. These improvements will be confined within a 120-square-foot easement to be dedicated to the County. This sanitary sewer extension is intended to provide an outfall for proposed residential development located at the applicant property. It is the opinion of staff that this easement will serve a private benefit and, therefore, recommend compensation in the amount of $2,000 for the dedication. There are several cases where the School Board has granted such easements for private use when their establishment does not cause an impact to existing or future school facilities.
