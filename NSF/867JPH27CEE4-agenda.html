    National Inclusive Schools Week - December 4 - 8

    The 6th Annual National Inclusive Schools Week, scheduled for December 4 throught 8, highlights and celebrates the progress our nation's schools have made in using inclusive practices to ensure a quality education for an increasingly diverse student population, including students with disabilities, those from low socio-economic backgrounds, and English language learners. The theme for the 2006 celebration—Charting an Inclusive Journey through School, Work, and Life: Successful Transition Planning for All Students.

    Each year, the Week focuses on a particularly challenging area or aspect of integrating inclusive practices into the school experience. Inclusive schools and communities guide children and youth along a personalized and supportive path, which can ensure smoother transitions and, ultimately, success.

    3.02 Citizen Participation
    Meeting
    Nov 14, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 8
    Category
    3. Presentations to School Board
    Type
    Information

    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1050. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.
