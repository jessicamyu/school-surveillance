Childhood Cancer Awareness Recognition

 

The American Childhood Cancer Organization (or ACCO) has designated September as Childhood Cancer Awareness Month to spotlight the types of cancers that largely affect children and to help raise funds for research and family support.

 

According to ACCO, 15,780 children under the age of 19 are diagnosed with cancer every year in the United States; approximately 20% of them will not survive the disease. A diagnosis turns the lives of the entire family upside down.

 

ACCO offers support, information and advocacy through their community of families, survivors, and friends affected by childhood and adolescent cancer. They are dedicated to improving the lives of children and families living with childhood cancer and engaged in this life-altering struggle. FCPS students being treated for childhood cancer participate in home-bound instruction while they are undergoing treatment or recuperating.

 

We are joined tonight by Jay Coakley who is in his 40th year of teaching for Fairfax County Public Schools. Jay retired from FCPS after 30 years of teaching physical education and returned as a part-time elementary physical education teacher at Woodburn Elementary. It was at Woodburn where Jay met a little girl named Ellie, who had been diagnosed with acute lymphoblastic leukemia in the spring of 2013. By the time she entered kindergarten that fall, Ellie had lost most of her hair and was wearing hats to school every day.

 

Jay noticed her affinity for “fun and cheery hats” and organized a hat drive to provide Ellie with a broader selection of hats, and Ellie’s Hats was born! Now, thanks to the hard work of the individuals who, like Jay, took an interest in Ellie and her hats, thousands of kids across the country who are battling cancer have received new hats and so much more. Ellie’s Hats does much more than send hats to children with cancer. They have helped to provide financial assistance to local families who have children with cancer. They recently reached a milestone of $300,000 in donations to families and hospitals.

 

Ellie is now in 9th grade and will be celebrating her 14th birthday this year. 

 

On behalf of the Board, I’d like to encourage everyone to visit the website for Ellie’s Hats at ellieshats.org and the American Childhood Cancer Organization at acco.org, to learn how you can help.

Meeting Category:  September 1, 2022 Regular Meeting

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

All the FY 2022 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the nine funds. All comparisons are against the FY 2022 Revised Budget. In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for revenue and expenditure variances, School Board flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, and commitments and carryover, funds available for FY 2023 total $90.9 million.

 

Excluding School Operating Fund grants, the FY 2022 actual revenue variance totals $8.5 million, due to an increase in sales tax offset by lower Basic Aid caused by reduced enrollment; Federal revenue; offset by other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, Fairfax City tuition, and miscellaneous revenue.

 

Excluding School Operating Fund grants, the expenditure variance is $260.1 million less than budgeted. Funding available after accounting for reserves, commitments, and carryover is primarily comprised of salary and benefits fallout attributed to lower student enrollment caused by the pandemic, salary lapse, and a one-month health benefit premium holiday.

 

As a result of the revenue and expenditure variances, available funds for FY 2023 total $90.9 million. In addition, the General Assembly had not formally adopted the 2022-2024 biennial budget at the time of FCPS’ FY 2023 Approved Budget adoption. As compared to the budget passed by the General Assembly, FCPS will receive a net increase of approximately $25.3 million in additional state revenue. Of the additional revenue, $18.1 million is to support a one-time $1,000 bonus for Standards of Quality funded instructional and support positions.

 

This agenda item includes recommendations for FY 2023 expenditure adjustments, including an employee bonus and funding for staffing contingencies, stadium restrooms, major maintenance, FCPS cost share for electric buses, Young Scholars extracurricular enrichment, a shortfall in the Adult and Community Education Fund, a middle school start time study, a recruitment incentive for substitute teachers, increased electricity costs, paper inflation, a superintendent strategic reserve, food services equipment, Title IX softball field upgrades, a safety and security review, turf fields, and to maintain the beginning balance for FY 2024. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

The School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category: September 1, 2022 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

 

Related To: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary Background:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools’ (FCPS) Office of Design and Construction Services issued a Request for Proposals (RFP) from qualified civil engineering firms on June 10, 2022, for various construction projects, typically for non-related Capital Improvement Projects. Generally, these engineering projects are valued less than $50,000.00 for each project, however, depending on the size of the project, the complexity of services required, the civil engineering service for a project could range up to $250,000.00 or above.   

 

In response to the RFP, FCPS received thirteen (13) technical proposals from civil engineering firms on July 13, 2022.  The selection process for the engineering firms for these services was conducted in accordance with the process and criteria set forth in School Board Policy 8220.4 and the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA). The Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) evaluated the proposals received in response to this RFP based on the following criteria:

 

•       Size and Capability of Offeror

•       Organizational and Staff Experience

•       Approach to Tasks to be Performed

•       Capability for Timely Response

•       References

•       Overall Quality and Completeness of Proposal

•       Oral Presentations/Interviews (Short-List Firms if Necessary)

•       Other factors consistent with the RFP, Policy 8220.4, and the VPPA

 

 

Upon completion of review and evaluation, the sac recommends a list of top-ranked firms for annual contract negotiation and consideration from civil engineering firms that are determined to be most advantageous to FCPS for performance of civil engineering services for various construction projects.  The annual contracts will include three 1-year renewal options. The SAC’s evaluation report and recommendation of eight (8) firms are listed below.

Subject: Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee

 

Staff Contact: Noel Klimenko, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Meeting Category:  September 1, 2022 – General Meeting

 


School Board Action Required: Information                         

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:    

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents. Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.

 

For 2022-2023, the Board members making appointments are Tamara Derenak Kaufax (Franconia), Ricardy Anderson (Mason), Laura Jane Cohen (Springfield), Karen Keys-Gamarra (At-Large), Abrar Omeish (At-Large), and Rachna Sizemore Heizer (At-Large). Last year the School Board district representatives from Braddock, Dranesville, Hunter Mill, Mount Vernon, Providence, and Sully were selected to appoint individuals.

 

The student representative to the School Board appoints a student to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.

 

If a resident who is not a parent, guardian, or employee at a local school submits a challenge, two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school. If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate. Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009. 

 

If a resident does not bring a challenge, a committee will not meet.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint individuals to serve a one-year term on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

6.06 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution [FNS; Action 9/15/22; adopt the newly-revised 2022 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.]
Meeting
Sep 1, 2022 - Regular Meeting

6. New Business - 9:05 p.m. The following are new business agenda items; there will not be a vote tonight on these items, but action is scheduled at a future meeting.
Type
Information

 

Subject:  Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution 

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Michelle Pratt, director, Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:  September 1, 2022 – Regular Meeting 

 

School Board Action Required:  New Business

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In accordance with past practice, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted an updated version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution on July 19, 2022 (the “2022 Purchasing Resolution”) to reflect legislative changes made during the preceding session of the Virginia General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly-revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

Relevant changes to the 2022 Purchasing Resolution are summarized below, together with recommended conditions (or exceptions) to the School Board’s adoption of the 2022 Purchasing Resolution.  Consistent with past practice and as outlined below, the recommended conditions consist of (i) a restatement of the so-called “Construction Exception” to correct an inaccurate description of the Superintendent’s procurement authority; and (ii) a reservation of the School Board’s right to conduct procurements for goods, services, and insurance in accordance with school division policies and regulations and Virginia law whenever such action is in the best interests of the school division

 

Relevant changes and recommended conditions are as follows:

 

County’s Administrative Changes Relative to FCPS:

 

1.     Corrections to section headings and pagination due to changes in the document text. (Table of Contents)

2.     Several references to “he” have been updated to “their” to reflect gender neutrality of the individual roles. (Pages, various)

3.     Updates to the Background and Whereas sections, to update outdated language. (Pages, various)

4.     Updates to the Legal Remedies section to better align with existing legal authority.  (Page 67)

5.     Updated the equipment capitalization threshold to be consistent with County policy. (Page 13)

6.     Updated public announcement requirements to be consistent with the VPPA and current practices. (Pages, various)

7.     Updated the Audit requirements clause, under Mandatory Terms for Contracts in Excess of $200,000, to permit consideration of alternate requirements as may be in the best interests of the County when negotiating certain contracts. (Page 45)

8.     Updated Limitation of Liability to be consistent with current practices. (Page 46)

       

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s annual adoption of the 2009 through 2021 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2022 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  • The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the Board of Supervisors, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority: “The school division’s Superintendent or their designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  • The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board adopt the newly-revised 2022 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

6.07 ESSER III - Reallocation of Funds to support Academic Intervention and COVID Case Managers and Monitors [ISD; Action 9/15/22; approve the reallocation of funds to support Project 9 Academic Intervention and Project 27 COVID Case Managers/Supervision Monitors.]

Type

Subject: ESSER III - Reallocation of Funds to support Academic Intervention and COVID Case Managers and Monitors

 

Staff Contact:  Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

 

Other Staff Present: Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

 

Meeting Category: September 1, 2022 - Regular Meeting 

 

School Board Action Required: Decision 

 

Summary/Background  

Several amendments to the ESSER III Grant are necessary for FY23. First, FCPS is implementing similar Layered Prevention Strategies during the 2022-2023 school year and it is necessary to provide schools with sufficient funding for COVID Case Managers/Supervision Monitors. In order to provide schools with the same level of funding expended in FY22, $4.1 million in carryover from Project 27 and an additional $1.9 million will be redirected from Project 1 Special Education Teacher Contracts (this funding is available due to the salary lapse of unfilled positions in FY22). 

 

Additionally, schools received estimated academics (Project 9) and wellness (Project 2) allocation ranges in May 2022 to support planning for FY23. In order to provide schools with sufficient funding levels, an additional $0.6 million is needed for Project 9 Academics. This can be redirected from Project 1 Special Education Teacher Contracts (again due to salary lapse from unfilled positions in FY22).

 

On August 1, 2022, the Board received notification of the need to reallocate unspent funds from Project 1 Special Education Teacher Contracts to Project 9 Academic Intervention and Project 27 COVID Case Managers/Supervision Monitors for FY23. 

 

Reallocation of ESSER dollars (over $100,000) requires School Board approval. 

 

Recommendation: 

That the School Board approve the reallocation of funds to support Project 9 Academic Intervention and Project 27 COVID Case Managers/Supervision Monitors.
