7. FACILITIES AND TRANSPORTATION SERVICES
      A. Facilities: The Superintendent will provide the necessary infrastructure to support instruction and operations and maintain safe, healthful, and up-to-date classrooms and work environments that support the instructional program. The Superintendent will:
          1. Ensure facilities that are clean, sanitary, and safe.
          2. Develop and annually report on a master plan required to implement an effective preventive maintenance program.
          3. Provide for public use of facilities as long as student safety, student functions, and the instructional program are not compromised and use guidelines are administered consistently.
          4. Establish and regularly update a Facilities Comprehensive Plan, organized by regional areas, which reflects School Board policy and guides decision making to ensure sound instructional practices; a reasonable balance of capacity utilization; and wise, efficient, and environmentally-responsible use of resources.
          5. Establish a Capital Improvement Program of major maintenance, renovation, and new construction which ensures that classroom capacity and infrastructure meet instructional program and community use needs and which endeavors to be equitable across the county.
        B. Transportation: The Superintendent will assure the transportation of all students in a safe and timely manner, to instructional programs for which they are eligible or that meet their needs, in a cost-effective manner.

        C. Safety and Security and Emergency Preparedness: The Superintendent will provide physical security and safety programs that ensure the protection of students, staff members, visitors, and FCPS property.
            1. Ensure that updated emergency management plans are in place, that key personnel receive training as appropriate, and that a collaborative and effective working relationship is maintained with local, state, and federal emergency management representatives.

    Contact: Daniel G. Storck, Chairman and Mount Vernon District

    Meeting Category: July 13, 2009 – Work Session No. 5

    Subject: 2009-2010 Charges to School Board Advisory Committees

    School Board Action Required: Information

    Related To: School Board Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

    Key Points:
        At its May 7 and 21, 2009, forum work sessions, the Board discussed the process for developing charges for all the School Board citizen advisory committees for the 2009-2010 school year. At its June 8, 2009, work session, the Board reviewed the draft 2009-2010 recommended charges.

        School Board member committee liaisons have discussed the proposed charges with each advisory committee (either the chairman, co-chairs, or committee members) and are recommended for approval as follows:
          Adult and Community Education Advisory Committee
          Define the effect of the ACE reorganization on student achievement, family literacy, and course offerings that are responsive to consumer demand and workforce needs.

          Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities
          Examine the school-wide positive behavior support programs and responsive classroom programs focusing on bullying prevention, strategies, training, and implementation, with specific emphasis on the measures for Student Achievement Goal 2, Essential Life Skills.

          Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee
          Provide an assessment and report that identifies the impacts to the performance and delivery of the Advanced Academic programs based on any changes made by the School Board in the coming months. If no changes are made, provide recommendations for improvement in these programs, keeping in mind the budgetary concerns now before FCPS.

          Career and Technical Education Advisory Committee
          Develop partnership models that enhance and expand Career and Technical Education programs for the mutual benefit of students and the greater business community.

          District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program
          Find ways to develop personal relationships between home and school to strengthen family involvement.

          FECEP/Head Start
          Pursue funding for the additional FECEP/Head Start and Early Head Start classrooms due to the growing waitlist. Offer parent education and/or skill development opportunities to families so they can learn a skill in this difficult job market as the economy has greatly impacted our families. Increase parent involvement by 10%, particularly male involvement (fathers or male figures).

          Human Relations Advisory Committee
          Work with the Office of Equity and Compliance to enhance the performance of school-based human relations committees, and continue to explore methods for obtaining feedback from the economically challenged and socially marginalized.

          Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee
          Monitor and assess the effectiveness of the FCPS current plan for closing the academic achievement gap and work in partnership with staff to ensure timely, consistent, and effective deployment and implementation of the plan across schools and clusters.

          School Health Advisory Committee
          Continue to look at the coordinated health services model and ways to implement specific modules, in addition to utilizing the new student survey data to examine issues related to depression or other risk behaviors.


    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve the charges for 2009-2010 for the following School Board Citizen Advisory Committees: Adult and Community Education, Students with Disabilities, Advanced Academic Programs, Career and Technical Education, District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program, FECEP/Head Start, Human Relations, Minority Student Achievement Oversight, and School Health, as detailed in the agenda item.

    Other Staff Present: Frances Ivey, director, pre – K 12 Curriculum and Instruction
    Carol Horn, coordinator, Advanced Academic Programs

    Meeting Category: July 13, 2009 – Work Session No. 6
    Subject: Current FCPS Advanced Academic Programs

    School Board Action Required: Discussion

    Related To: Student Achievement Goal 1.

    Key Points:
        FCPS Local Plan for the Gifted outlines a continuum of gifted services that are offered in grades K – 12 and is commensurate with the last presentation by Dr. Sally Reis. FCPS serves as a model of best practice in the area of advanced academics. The plan was updated and revised through the effort of a committee that consisted of community members, central office staff, and elementary, middle, and high school administrators and teachers.

    Advanced Academic Work Session Objective: At the end of the work session, FCPS School Board members will have a deeper and broader understanding of advanced academic programs in FCPS. Further, the alignment with national best practices as outlined by Dr. Sally Reis will be evident.

      1. What is the difference between labeling services versus labeling students? Why is this distinction important?

      2. What is the benefit to all students if a full continuum of services is offered?

      3. How does a continuum of services prepare all students for advanced academic coursework in high school?

      4. How is the FCPS Program of Studies differentiated for advanced learners?

      5. Does the interaction of rigorous curriculum and grouping patterns provide opportunities to meet the academic and social/emotional needs of advanced learners? Are they (rigorous and grouping patterns) mutually exclusive?

      6. Why is ongoing K-12 professional development critical in order to maintain high quality advanced academic programs that meet the needs of a diverse group of advanced learners?

    Meeting Category: July 13, 2009 - Work Session No. 8
    Subject: Comprehensive Planning Process Proposal and Feasibility Studies

    School Board Action Required: Decision

    Related To: Facilities and Transportation Services (FTS) Operational Expectations
    Key Points:
        Feasibility studies have been conducted by Facilities and Transportation Services on the Clifton Elementary School renovation and on overcrowding in several elementary schools in the southwestern portion of Fairfax County

        The scope and complexity of the issues suggest that these issues offer an ideal opportunity to initiate a comprehensive planning process with substantive community input.

        A proposed community input process is being drafted by the Board’s comprehensive planning committee. This proposed process will be presented by the committee as a part of the work session. As it is likely that the proposed process would begin this summer and would conclude no later than February 2010, it is important that the Board give direction to staff at the conclusion of the July work session.
    Options:
        Since we have never conducted such a process before, the School Board is asked to provide its thoughts about the proposed process at the July 13 work session so that guidance can be given to staff in order to allow the process to begin this summer.

    Recommendation:
        The School Board directs staff to initiate the comprehensive planning process for Clifton Elementary School renovation and overcrowded schools as presented by the Comprehensive Planning Committee.
