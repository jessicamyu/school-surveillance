HONORING

PETER NOONAN

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Peter Noonan has served Fairfax County Public Schools for 11 years with excellence and perseverance as an assistant principal at Langley High School, the principal at Lanier Middle School, the principal at Centreville High School, the Cluster VII assistant superintendent, and most recently as the Instructional Services assistant superintendent; and

     WHEREAS, he, as the Cluster VII assistant superintendent, fostered the tenets of professional learning communities to improve collaboration within school teams, pyramid teams, and Cluster VII; and

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Noonan confidently acted as the goal champion for the School Board’s Student Achievement Goal 1. Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 21, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                  Fairfax Ridge - Additional Office Space Lease

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

A critical need for additional program, training and office space exists at Fairfax Ridge which would support the needs of the:

  • Office Training Procedures (OTP) Student Program
  • Professional Learning Training Center
  • Department of Professional Learning and Accountability’s Office of Program Evaluation

 

During the program and building consolidation in FY 2010, portions of four departments (Instructional Services, Special Services, Professional Learning and Accountability and Information Technology) were co-located to Fairfax Ridge primarily in order to return two facility sites (Lacey Center and Devonshire Center) into school use and further consolidate department programming.  Approximately 430 staff were relocated to Fairfax Ridge at that time.   

 

Fairfax Ridge currently houses over 450 staff and hosts hundreds of training events with thousands of participants a year.  The increase is primarily attributed to additional program grants and staff re-alignments from other admin centers for better control and accountability.  The current inventory of meeting rooms is insufficient to accommodate all the demands for training, program and community events.  Classroom storage space for the OTP student program is also insufficient to store program materials. 

 

The proposed new lease would remedy the space shortages at Fairfax Ridge.  There would be no fiscal impact in FY 2013 as existing resources would be used to offset the first-year lease costs.  The new lease would have the same economic terms and conditions as the existing lease which is currently 12.8 percent below current market rates.

Adopt the Virginia Retirement System resolution, as detailed in the agenda item, in compliance with Chapter 822 of the 2012 Acts of Assembly (SB497) and consistent with the Fairfax County School Board FY 2013 Approved Budget (HR; presented as new business 6/7/21))

Staff Contact:           Phyllis Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:  De Hawley Brown, director, Benefit Services

 

Meeting Category:     June 21, 2012 - Regular Meeting No.22

 

Subject:                    Virginia Retirement System (VRS) Resolution 

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:               Legal Requirement Chapter 822 of the 2012 Acts of Assembly (SB497)

 

Key Points:

 

During the 2012 session of the Virginia General Assembly, legislation was passed requiring all active participants of the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) to contribute toward the cost of retirement benefits.  The legislation provides that localities and school systems may phase in employee contributions at a rate of 1% or more per year, and that the full phase in of a 5% contribution rate must be completed by July 1, 2016.  Jurisdictions must provide corresponding increases to salaries to offset these employee contributions. Newly hired or rehired employees as of July 1, 2012, shall pay 5% of their salary immediately and shall not have their contributions phased in.

 

At the May 24, 2012, regular business meeting of the Fairfax County School Board, the Board adopted a 2% employee contribution rate for current employees for Fiscal Year 2013.  VRS requires all participating employers to execute the attached resolution documenting the agreed upon employee contribution rates.  While future year contribution rates may be adjusted upwards, the remainder of the resolution is binding and irrevocable.

 

Recommendation:   

 

That the School Board adopt the Virginia Retirement System resolution, as detailed in the agenda item, in compliance with Chapter 822 of the 2012 Acts of Assembly (SB497) and consistent with the Fairfax County School Board FY 2013 Approved Budget.

Approve the revised charter for the Facilities Planning Advisory Council, as detailed in the agenda item (FTS; presented as new business 6/7/12)

Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 21, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                 Charter Revisions, Facilities Planning Advisory Council

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Background:

 

The initial ‘charter’ for the Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) was in the form of a proposal put forth by the Comprehensive Planning Development Committee (CPDC).  The proposal was accepted and FPAC was established with the first slate of appointed representatives in September 2010.  Since that time, School Board Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board, has been amended to establish a process for replacing FPAC members and stipulating that the term of appointment to FPAC is three years, instead of the staggered terms of 1, 2, and 3 years as initially required under the FPAC proposal.  The mission of the advisory council remains unchanged - to advise and inform the staff and School Board in the development of comprehensive, long term plans for facilities needs in the most effective and efficient way.  

 

Key Points:

 

The  charter, as revised by CPDC, reflects the policy changes on terms of appointment and the practices that have evolved in the initial two years of FPAC’s existence, including:

  • the process for referral of projects to FPAC
  • modifying the Work Products section to include the annual charge from the School Board to FPAC
  • clarify that attendance is expected at 75% of regular business meetings to sign on FPAC’s annual report to the School Board

Other revisions are minor and editorial in nature to simplify the document, such as: 

  • removal of the complex communication flow charts
  • re-ordering of text to improve flow and document organization
  • text changes to eliminate redundancies

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revised charter for the Facilities Planning Advisory Council, as detailed in the agenda item.
