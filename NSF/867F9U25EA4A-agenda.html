Native American Heritage Month - November 2007

The strength of our Nation comes from its people. As the early inhabitants of this great land, the native peoples of North America played a unique role in the shaping of our Nation's history and culture. During November when we celebrate Thanksgiving, we especially celebrate their heritage and the contributions of American Indian and Alaska Native peoples to this Nation.

Since our Nation's birth, pluralism and diversity have been hallmarks of the American experience and success. In 1782, the Founding Fathers chose as our national motto "E Pluribus Unum," which means "out of many, one." Today, America's unity, derived from a mix of many diverse cultures and people, grandly embodies the vision expressed by our Founders. American Indian and Alaska Native cultures have made remarkable contributions to our national identity. Their unique spiritual, artistic, and literary contributions, together with their vibrant customs and celebrations, enliven and enrich our land.

National School Psychologist Month - November

All children and youth can learn with the proper supports, and have the inalienable right to an education that meets their needs Children’s mental health is directly linked to their learning and development and sound psychological principles are integral to instruction and learning; social, and emotional development; prevention and early intervention. School psychologists are uniquely equipped to support schools in these areas.

Schools provide an optimal context to provide mental health services because of the nature of the learning environment, and the fact that most children are accessible and familiar there and school-based mental health services are a critical part of a continuum of mental health services for children and youth; and

School psychologists are specially trained to deliver a continuum of mental health services and academic supports within the school setting and school psychologists facilitate collaboration and help parents and educators identify and reduce risk factors. They create effective, caring schools, access needed community resources, and implement research-driven prevention and intervention strategies.

    SUBJECT: Strategic Governance Manual Revision

    TYPE: Action

    BACKGROUND:
        When the SB adopted the Strategic Governance Manual in December 2006, the following notes were included as it related to School Board advisory committees:
            7.B. Legally Required Committees (page 37): NOTE: Unless otherwise noted in the Charge, all legally-required committees are deemed to support the work of the Superintendent. These committees will function for the remainder of the 2006-2007 school year as Board committees. Beginning with the 2007-2008 year, the committees will interface with appropriate staff and will issue any required reports to the Board through the Superintendent. The Superintendent will transmit the reports to the Board, along with the Superintendent’s response. While the Board will appoint members of the committee, the Superintendent may appoint additional community members if their appointment will enhance the committee’s work.

            7.C. Other Committees (page 41): NOTE: The following committees exist at the current time and are maintained for the balance of the year. Following the conclusions of the current term, these committees will be continued at the discretion of the Board and/or the Superintendent. Should the Board decide to continue any of the committees as Committees of the Board, the Board will carefully define their role and their purpose to assure that they meet the Board’s definition of Board Committees.

        As the Board is still deliberating about the roles and responsibilities of these advisory committees, the notes are no longer appropriate and should be removed from the Manual. The current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board, continues to govern all matters related to School Board advisory committees.

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the Board remove from the School Board’s adopted Strategic Governance Manual, Section 4, Board Committees, the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.B. Legally Required Committees (page 37) and the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.C. Other Committees (page 41), and confirm that all matters related to School Board advisory committees be governed by the current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board.

    Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

    Meeting Type: October 25, 2007, Regular Meeting No. 6

    SUBJECT: 2008 Draft Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program

    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:
        The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools. The attached 2008 draft Legislative Program is based on previous year’s programs as well as proposals and changes submitted this year. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance. The draft program was reviewed and discussed at the School Board’s October 15 Legislation Work Session.

    KEY POINTS:
        New Positions
          • Early Childhood Education (stand alone, and in Joint Position with BOS)
          • Funding for statewide English Language Proficiency (ELP) assessment (in Assessment of LEP Students)
          • Changes to graduation requirements (in Standards of Accreditation)
          • Childhood obesity/School Breakfast and Lunch Program reimbursements (in School Health Services and Student Wellness)
          • Safe learning environments/incentive funding for safety technologies and programs/confidentiality of school safety plan reviews/monitoring of sex offender registry (in Student Safety)

        Also changed most of the specific references to the federal No Child Left Behind Act throughout the draft Program, instead referencing federal accountability and the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 (ESEA) more generally.

        Deleted Positions (all achieved in 2007)
          • Driver’s Education (stand alone)
          • Statewide ELP assessment (in Assessment of LEP Students)
          • Counting special needs students in subgroup after exit from services (in ESEA/NCLB)
          • Side safety lights on buses/state charges for driver’s registries (in Student Transportation)

        Note: After the Work Session, and in addition to typographical corrections, a sentence was added to the Governor’s School position to clarify that Thomas Jefferson is currently the only Governor’s School affected by a state funding cap based on enrollment.

    OPTIONS:

    Modify as desired.

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the Board adopt the 2008 Draft Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program.

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

    Meeting Type: October 25, 2007, Regular Meeting No. 6

    SUBJECT: Policy 8541, Recycling Requirements for All Fairfax County Public Schools Facilities

    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:

        FCPS currently has no formal Policy or Regulation addressing the school system’s need to actively and responsibly recycle common materials found in schools (paper/cardboard, glass, metal and plastic bottle). Establishing a Policy and Regulation for recycling will both create and promote a culture of environmental stewardship within Fairfax County Public Schools

        The Regulation will require all FCPS facilities to recycle to the maximum extent possible and to recognize that FCPS must be a good steward of the environment.
    KEY POINTS:
        Policy/Regulation mandates recycling in two phases:

        Phase I – Paper and Cardboard Recycling

        Phase II – Glass, Metal and Plastic Recycling.

        Phase I is mandatory for all facilities as of the effective date of the new regulation. Phase II should be implemented by all facilities no later than the start of school year 2008/09.


    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve Policy 8541, Recycling Requirements for All Fairfax County Public Schools Facilities.

    Meeting
    Oct 25, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 6
    Category
    6. New Business
    Type
    Information


    Contact: Phillip Niedzielski-Eichner, Providence District

    Meeting Type: October 25, 2007, Regular Meeting No. 6

    SUBJECT: "Cool Schools" Resolution

    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:
        There is a consensus among the world's leading scientists that human-induced, greenhouse gas emissions are a significant contributor to global warming, and reducing those is emissions is one of the most significant challenges confronting the world today and suggest that climate stabilization will require a reduction of global warming gas emissions to 80 percent below current levels by the year 2050.

        Since the United States is responsible for producing approximately 25 percent of the world’s current global warming pollutants, a number of state, regional and local governments – including our own Fairfax County government – are adopting on a non-partisan basis emissions reduction targets and programs. This resolution commits FCPS to join this effort.

        We maintain an inventory of buildings, a transportation fleet, and real estate holdings – and we engage in various operational practices – that generate greenhouse gas emissions. We are currently, as an enterprise, demonstrating our commitment to environmental stewardship and greenhouse gas emission reduction. We have already, for example, instituted programs such as installing energy efficient infrastructure upgrades, including improved lighting, climate control systems, and window replacements. These initiatives alone save the taxpayer $2.4 million per year in energy costs and reduce carbon emissions from these buildings at the equivalent of emissions from 5,000 vehicles each day.

        In addition, while we can act as an enterprise to reduce our contribution to greenhouse gas emissions, we can also play a unique role in helping our students become educated about their environmental stewardship responsibilities, including greenhouse gas emission reduction. In this regard, we established recently new academic goals. Included among other important expectations, is that students understand and model attributes that “…contribute to an effective and productive community and the common good of all.” We also set the expectation that students be skilled in environmental stewardship.

        Finally, this initiative must be undertaken with the recognition that our current fiscal situation is as an important constraint to our ability to implement the full range of potential environmental initiatives. This being said, continued progress is both possible, desirable, and, if this resolution passes, expected.


    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve the accompanying resolution that declares Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) to be a “Cool Schools” public school system and commits FCPS to continue to take innovative and cost effective steps to help our country achieve climate stabilization; to help students become educated in environmental stewardship responsibilities; and to use their critical thinking and communication skills to debate – as may be needed – the appropriate societal, family, and personal solutions.

    Alternative:

    The Board can maintain the current state, by not approving this resolution; or the Board can adapt its content and direction by amendment.

    Staff Contact: Peter Noonan, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

    Meeting Type: October 25, 2007, Regular Meeting No. 6

    SUBJECT: Secondary Course Offerings

    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:
        Standard and optional courses for high schools and middle schools are reviewed each year, and new proposals are submitted to the Course Review Committee for review and recommendations to the Superintendent and School Board. The Course Review Committee met on October 17, 2007, to review a total of seven proposals. This process is outlined in Policy 3202, New Course Proposals and Deletion and Consolidation of Existing Courses.

    KEY POINTS:
        The following courses were recommended by the Course Review Committee as additions to the list of high school standard course offerings for the 2008-2009 school year:
        • Independent-Research-Engineering
      Background:
      This course is designed for students interested in pursuing a career in architecture and engineering which offers a work-based learning and mentorship experience. This course requires 180 contact hours in mentorship in an approved research project, research journal, and an electric digital portfolio, research/project paper, and PowerPoint presentation on research methodologies, outcomes and findings.
        • Interior and Commercial Design
        Background:
        This course is designed for students interested in pursuing a career in interior design and/or commercial design. This course will prepare students to acquire critical thinking skills, practical problem solving, and entrepreneurship opportunities within this field.
        • PreCalculus
        Background:
          This course will prepare students for AP Calculus AB, IB Math Studies, or to take the first semester of calculus in college. This course is a non honors PreCalculus course and effectively prepares students for college-level calculus.
          The following course was recommended by the Course Review Committee as an addition to the list of high school optional course offerings for the 2008-2009 school year:
        • Advanced Programming
        Background:
          This course is designed for students interested in pursuing a career in programming or gaming. This course will complement the current programming and computer science curricula, adding a focus on programming for the web and gaming technologies.
          The following course was recommended by the Course Review Committee for deletion from the list of high school standard course offerings for the 2008-2009 school year:
        • Math Analysis (deletion)
        Background:
        This course is no longer needed with the approval of PreCalculus.
          The following courses were recommended by the Course Review Committee as additions to the list of middle school optional course offerings for the 2008-2009 school year:
        • Career Search and Investigation
        Background:
        This course is designed for students to explore the 16 Career Clusters and develop an understanding of career pathways. Students will use approved career exploration resources to investigate the world of work in order to make informed career decisions.
        • Data Input Technologies
        Background:
        This course is designed for students interested in digital input technologies, keyboarding skills, digital imaging, audio devices, speech recognition, on-screen writing, and personal digital assistants (PDA).

      RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board approve changes to the list of middle and high school course offerings for the 2008-2009 school year, as recommended by the Course Review Committee and as detailed in the agenda item.
