RESOLUTION
NAMING THE GYMNASIUM AT
FAIRFAX VILLA ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
“THE ROBERT L. GLYNN, JR., GYMNASIUM”

WHEREAS, Robert (Bob) Glynn has served the Fairfax Villa Elementary School students, staff members, parents, and community members with distinction for 36 years until his retirement in 2006, as the school’s physical education teacher; and

WHEREAS, he has always promoted a caring, respectful, and harmonious learning community in which all students have the opportunity to be successful, responsible citizens in a setting that includes extremely high social and academic expectations; and

WHEREAS, he has created a greater awareness of and diligently shared the importance of health, nutrition, and physical exercise in a safe learning environment that promotes cooperation, teamwork, diversity, and individual differences; and

WHEREAS, he has overseen many student-centered community activities—such as Jump Rope for Heart—and student-centered programs—such as the Safety Patrol program—that increase self-esteem and confidence while promoting leadership and independence; and

WHEREAS, he has enriched the Fairfax Villa community of learners by sharing his wisdom and many experiences and serving as an outstanding role model who continues to positively influence our greater community;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the Fairfax Villa Elementary School gymnasium “The Robert L. Glynn, Jr., Gymnasium,” in recognition of his exceptional dedication, commitment, and many years of service to the children, staff members, and families of the Fairfax Villa community.

Legally Required Committees (page 37) and the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.C. Other Committees (page 41), and confirm that all matters related to School Board advisory committees be governed by the current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board (SB; work session scheduled 10/15/07)
Meeting
Oct 11, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 5
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information


Contact: Steve Hunt, At Large Member
SUBJECT: Strategic Governance Manual Revision

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the Board remove from the School Board’s adopted Strategic Governance Manual, Section 4, Board Committees, the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.B. Legally Required Committees (page 37) and the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.C. Other Committees (page 41), and confirm that all matters related to School Board advisory committees be governed by the current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board.

EXPLANATION:
      When the School Board adopted the Strategic Governance Manual in December 2006, the following notes were included as it related to School Board advisory committees:
           7.B.  Legally Required Committees (page 37):  NOTE:  Unless otherwise noted in the Charge, all legally-required committees are deemed to support the work of the Superintendent.  These committees will function for the remainder of the 2006-2007 school year as Board committees.  Beginning with the 2007-2008 year, the committees will interface with appropriate staff and will issue any required reports to the Board through the Superintendent.  The Superintendent will transmit the reports to the Board, along with the Superintendent’s response.  While the Board will appoint members of the committee, the Superintendent may appoint additional community members if their appointment will enhance the committee’s work.

          7.C.  Other Committees (page 41):  NOTE:  The following committees exist at the current time and are maintained for the balance of the year.  Following the conclusions of the current term, these committees will be continued at the discretion of the Board and/or the Superintendent.  Should the Board decide to continue any of the committees as Committees of the Board, the Board will carefully define their role and their purpose to assure that they meet the Board’s definition of Board Committees.

      As the Board is still deliberating about the roles and responsibilities of these advisory committees, the notes are no longer appropriate and should be removed from the Manual.  The current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board, continues to govern all matters related to School Board advisory committees.

TYPE: Information

BACKGROUND:
      Members of the School Health Advisory Committee met on September 26, 2007, and reviewed and accepted the proposed charge. In order to ensure the charge is met, the Committee agreed:
      1. To determine the elements of a comprehensive school health policy, they will utilize the Coordinated School Health Program, as measured by the CDC School Health Index, building on briefings SHAC received in February 2007, and will collect and review other comprehensive school policies.
      2. To compare those elements to the current Wellness Policy and other FCPS-health-related written policies, they will identify any gaps in current health-related policies in FCPS, recommend modifications to the current Wellness Policy to create a comprehensive school health policy, and identify tools for measuring compliance with a comprehensive school health policy.

      At its October 11, 2007, Forum work session, the Board reviewed and discussed the charge to the School Health Advisory Committee.
KEY POINTS:

OPTIONS:

RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board approve the following charge to the School Health Advisory Committee for 2007-2008: Determine the elements of a comprehensive school health policy, compare those elements to the current Wellness Policy and other FCPS health-related written policies, and determine if a comprehensive school health policy should be established for FCPS.
