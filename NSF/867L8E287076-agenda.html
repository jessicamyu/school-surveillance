        Modifications were proposed to Regulation 2601.19P and the Student Responsibilities and Rights (SR&R) booklet to clarify and strengthen the student code of conduct with respect to expected behavior, consequences of inappropriate behavior, and disciplinary procedures.

        Parallel changes will also be made in Regulation 2610, Removal (Suspension, Expulsion, or Exclusion) of Students From School.

        Substantive changes in the regulation and/or document include the following:
    A. Parent Signature Sheet has been changed to include a student signature, to include or guardian, and to point out the addition of the Acceptable Use Policy for Student Network Access on page 37, and the Standards of Conduct for Pupils Riding School Buses on page 39.

          F. Chapter II.B. Adds language “and Parents or guardian of Victims of Certain Code Violations”. (p.20)

          G. Chapter II.B.1. Changes language to read, “in addition to taking appropriate disciplinary action, report to the police department incidents that may constitute violations of law” and adds “arson” as a violation that will be reported to police. (p.20)

          H. Chapter II.B.5. Adds language “the principal shall also immediately report to the parents or guardians of any minor student who is the victim or intended victim of such act listed in B.1. that may constitute a criminal offense”. (p.20)

          I. Chapter II.C.2.d. (4). Adds language, “the hearing will not be recorded verbatim by stenographic means, tape, or other means”. (p.23)

          J. Chapter II.C.6.b. Adds language stating the student is not entitled to receive continuing educational services during any period of suspension or expulsion. (p.27)

          K. Acceptable Use Policy for Student Network Access has been added. (p.37)

          L. Standards of Conduct for Pupils Riding School Buses has been added. (p.39)

          M. Quick Guide to Rules of Conduct and Disciplinary Consequences has been removed and will be printed in separate student booklet, Quick Guide, grades 4-12.

          5.05 Award of Contract - Award the contract for the replacement of the main gymnasium floor at Chantilly High School to Warthan Associates, Inc., in the amount of $275,541, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board (FTS; presented as new business 4/27/06)
          Meeting
          May 11, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 19
          Category
          5. Consent Agenda - 10:30 p.m.
          Type
          Action (Consent)

          Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

          SUBJECT: Award of Contract


          RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board award the contract for the replacement of the main gymnasium floor at Chantilly High School to Warthan Associates, Inc., in the amount of $275,541, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.
          EXPLANATION:
            The hardwood floor currently installed at Chantilly High School has been severely water damaged over the past few years. The floor has had sections replaced over the years, but is now beyond repair.

            The Office of Facilities Management (OFM) has researched and evaluated alternative gymnasium flooring products that address problems associated with moisture while providing the durability and appearance of hardwood. Alternative gymnasium flooring is a relatively new industry to the United States, which has limited competition. The OFM has installed two different types of manufactured floors. The appearance, maintenance, and durability of the Gerflor Taraflex floor have satisfied the requirements of Fairfax County Public Schools.

            Currently there is only one vendor in our region that is approved to install the Gerflor Taraflex floor. Therefore, it is recommended that a sole source contract be awarded to Warthan Associates, Inc. The OFM is presently re-evaluating other manufacturers to expand competition.

            Bids were solicited for this project, but only one bid from the sole source provider was received on April 28, 2006. Funding for this project will be provided from FY 2006 Major Maintenance Fund. The contractor has performed satisfactorily on previous contracts.

            Warthan Associates, Inc. $275,541

          SUBJECT: Legislative Recommendations for the 2007 Policies and Resolutions of the VSBA

          RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board approve recommendations for the 2007 Policies and Resolutions of the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA).

          EXPLANATION:
            Each spring the VSBA Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee seeks recommendations from local school boards concerning new or revised legislative policies and resolutions to be considered for VSBA approval. The Committee reviews the local school boards’ recommendations along with other suggestions for changes to the existing policies and resolutions. The VSBA takes action on the Committee’s report at the annual fall convention.

            This process allows the Fairfax County School Board to make recommendations for the VSBA legislative program and to receive statewide support for some of its own legislative positions. The Fairfax County School Board 2007 Legislative Program will be developed for adoption in the fall.

            Changes are proposed to positions regarding the federal No Child Left Behind (NCLB) Act, programs for students with limited English proficiency, and federal funding for the education of students with disabilities. A new position on state budget adoption is proposed. (See attached language and summary sheet).

            By the May 11 regular School Board meeting, these recommendations will have been reviewed by the School Board’s Legislation, Policy & Future Issues Committee at the May 8, 2006, work session.

          Meeting

          Category
          6. New Business - 10:35 p.m.

          Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

          SUBJECT: 2006-2011 Local Plan for the Gifted

          RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board approve the 2006-2011 Local Plan for the Education of the Gifted.

          EXPLANATION:
            The Virginia Board of Education adopted the current Regulations Governing Educational Services for Gifted Students, which outline the requirements of the local plan for the education of the gifted that school divisions must submit to the Department of Education for approval. Currently, local plans for the gifted are renewed every five years.

            Fairfax County Public Schools’ Local Plan for the Gifted outlines a continuum of gifted services that are offered in grades K – 12. The plan was updated and revised through the efforts of a committee that consisted of members of the Gifted and Talented (GT) Advisory Committee, central office staff, and elementary, middle, and high school administrators and teachers. The major changes to the plan consist of new goals for 2006 – 2011 and language that supports a continuum of gifted services for all students K-12. In addition, the plan was posted on the GT web site; and the Keep In Touch system was used to notify community members that the plan was available for review and feedback.
