    Veterans Day - November 11
    Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict. 
       
    At 5 a.m.. on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended.  This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, closing of places of business.  All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world has never before witnessed such rejoicing.

    Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U.S. armed forces.

    American Education Week - November 12-18

    Join us the week of November 12–18, 2006, in celebrating American Education Week. This year's theme, Great Public Schools: A Basic Right and Our Responsibility, highlights the importance of bringing together educators, school staff, parents, students, and communities in a unified effort to build great public schools.

    Students will profit tomorrow from what we invest in education today because our students must meet the highest world standards and compete and succeed in the global society of the 21st century.

    National Adoption Awareness Month - November

    The purpose of National Adoption Month is to raise awareness about the 129,000 children in foster care nationwide waiting for permanent families. Recruitment efforts are highlighted throughout the National Adoption Month web site to encourage America's families to "answer the call" to ensure the safety, permanency, and well-being of our children.

    There are many ways to form a family, and over 8,000 families in Fairfax County have chosen adoption as the way to form their own family.  Fairfax County Public Schools recognizes the importance of adoption, and the School Board, Leadership Team, teachers, principals, and staff fully support Adoption Awareness Month.

    RESOLUTION
    NAMING THE COMPUTER LAB AT MOUNT VERNON HIGH SCHOOL
    "THE COOK LAB"

    WHEREAS, Judy Cook served as an instructional assistant in the computer lab at Mount Vernon High school for 26 years until her retirement in June 2006; and

    WHEREAS, Mrs. Cook enthusiastically taught and supported technology lessons in the lab, worked with teachers and students, provided level one maintenance of the computers, and worked closely with the Mount Vernon High School technology team; and

    WHEREAS, Ray Cook volunteered many hours of service assisting his wife as they provided invaluable service to teachers and students; and

    WHEREAS, Mr. and Mrs. Cook served as role models, going above and beyond, working long hours making certificates, helping with honor roll, helping with Standards of Learning online testing, and providing technology support to students and teachers during lunch shifts as well as during classes; and

    WHEREAS, Mr. and Mrs. Cook have earned the respect of the Mount Vernon High School faculty, students, and community for their dedicated service to Mount Vernon students;

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the computer lab at Mount Vernon High School, commonly referred to as "The Cook Lab" now officially named "The Cook Lab" in honor of Mr. and Mrs. Cook’s exemplary service.

    RESOLUTION
    NAMING THE MEDIA CENTER
    AT MOUNT VERNON HIGH SCHOOL
    “THE CALANTHIA TUCKER MEDIA CENTER”

    WHEREAS, Calanthia Tucker served Fairfax County Public Schools for 32 years until her retirement in June 2006 and enthusiastically supported the enhancement of the media center at Mount Vernon High School during her tenure as principal of the school by ensuring that it contained an extensive text collection and updated and current technology to meet the needs of the students and staff members; and
    WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker served with distinction as principal of Mount Vernon High School for eight years, during which time she provided exemplary instructional leadership as reflected in increased SAT scores, and was a role model for the Mount Vernon High School staff through her positive demeanor and respect for others and an earnest interest in each student and staff member as exemplified by her establishment of a teacher and staff recognition program; and
    WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker earned the respect of her staff and the Mount Vernon High School community as reflected in her recognition as the Fairfax County Public Schools Principal of the Year and her inclusion in Jay Mathews and Ian Hill’s book Supertest for her role in establishing the International Baccalaureate program at Mount Vernon High School; and
    WHEREAS, Dr. Tucker, through the establishment of programs such as the Community Resource Teams, invited the community to join with students and staff members in resolving divisive issues at a critical transition period for the entire school community and in embracing the ever-increasing diversity of Mount Vernon’s population, and worked tirelessly to establish and strengthen a strong sense of community throughout the Mount Vernon pyramid; and
    WHEREAS, throughout these years of varied changes, students were continually winning accolades for academic excellence, sports, drama, music, art, yearbook, newspaper, and athletics as well as in other areas of the school because Dr. Tucker continued to keep Mount Vernon High School on track and focused on its mission—Mount Vernon’s commitment to excellence;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the Media Center at Mount Vernon High School “The Calanthia Tucker Media Center” in honor of this exemplary educator, school administrator, assistant superintendent, colleague, friend, and community leader.

    Category
    3. Presentations to School Board

    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1050. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.
