Agenda Item: Department of Human Resources Operational Expectations and
Monitoring Report with Executive Summary of Evidence of Condition


Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Human Resources


Background: On February 22, 2007, the School Board approved the Superintendent’s reasonable interpretations and indicators for Human Resources (HR) operational expectations and indicators. At the time of approval several questions were raised by the Board. As a result, a follow-up meeting was held with the Aspen Group to further refine the reasonable interpretations and those revisions are reflected in this document. The attached HR Operational Expectations and Goal Monitoring Report provides the Superintendent’s evidence of condition for each of the indicators.


Key Issues: In addition to the performance metrics included in the Executive Summary as evidence of condition, the following opportunities exist:
    • A climate survey is needed to provide insight into employee perceptions in order to develop the best strategies to increase retention and job satisfaction.
    • Acquisition of a new applicant tracking system is necessary to provide more comprehensive applicant reporting.
    • Support evaluations need to be provided online to make the process easier to use and to enable the capture of divisionwide data related to timeliness and evaluation ratings.
    • Principals, program managers, and supervisors need to ensure timely completion of employee evaluations.
    • Exit survey questions need to be reviewed to provide more data to support retention.


School Board Discussion/Decision: Review the monitoring report and decide on the School Board’s assessment of the condition for each of the goals.

Staff Recommendation: That the School Board accept the Human Resources Operational Expectations and Monitoring Report and find each indicator is in an Acceptable Condition.


Options: None

Agenda Item: Proposed 2007 School Bond Referendum

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

Background
On January 25, 2007 the School Board adopted the FY2008-2012 Capital Improvement Program that identified school facility needs for the coming five-year period. Voter authorization to issue school bonds is needed in November 2007 to provide funding for a portion of the construction, renovation, program support, and infrastructure management projects identified the Capital Improvement Program.

Key Issues
The proposed referendum amount of $365,200,000 includes $65 million for new construction, $160.8 million for renovation of existing buildings, $18.5 million for program support, $38.6 million to offset cost escalation of projects in previous bond referenda, $29.8 million for infrastructure management projects and approximately $2.5 million in bond sales costs. The recommended bond amount also includes $50 million to fund a bus service facility. It should be noted that the debt service on this project will be paid by the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and will not count against the School Board spending cap of $155 million per year. Specific projects are outlined in the agenda item.

School Board Discussion/Decision

Staff Recommendation
That the School Board adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 6, 2007 general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $365,200,000.

Options
Modify the recommended School Bond Referendum project list; do not request a referendum in 2007.

Agenda Item: Bond Committees

Staff Contact: Jack Dale

Background: During every bond campaign, FCPS provides a great deal of factual information about the bond referendum to the public, especially in the BOND FACTS publication and Channel 21 productions. This activity would be undertaken whether or not there is a bond committee. Recent bond referenda have passed by margins in the 75-80 percent range.

Localities were contacted and most said they had not had a bond referendum in some time. Arlington said they do not have a bond committee, but the bonds pass anyway. No locality contacted had used bond committees.

Key Issues:

In recent years, it has been difficult to recruit bond committee chairs and difficult for bond committees to raise funds for their activities. Furthermore, the State of Virginia has made it difficult to close out the books on previous bond committees.

Given past experience with bond referenda, it is next to certain that a referendum would receive a strong majority vote without committee activity.

Agenda Item: School Boundary Change Process Modifications

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

Background
Some School Board members and staff have expressed an interest in modifying the current school boundary change process to better accommodate the public, lessen staff vilification, and gather greater input from the affected communities. Staff has developed a revised boundary change process that would utilize a Citizens Boundary Planning Committee (CBPC) to address the above concerns.

Key Issues
Volunteers for the CBPC would be sought from schools included in the boundary study. Final appointment to the committee would be made by cluster assistant superintendents using a lottery system. Boundary change recommendations would be developed by the CBPC working with the Facilities Planning Office as outlined in the attached working paper. The CBPC would hold community meetings to gather input on boundary issues and to review boundary proposals.

School Board Discussion/Decision

Staff Recommendation
That the School Board consider making changes to the current school boundary change process effective for the 2007-2008 school year.

Options
Modify the recommended process, continue with the current process or develop a new process different from the recommendation.

Agenda Item Transportation Re-Engineering

Staff Contact Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

Background The School Board has been considering the issue of changes to the bell schedule that would allow high schools to start later in the morning. The board also has been considering changes to current transportation services and re-engineering the current transportation operation.


Key Issues There are several substantive issues before the School Board:

        • Whether to proceed with the development of a revised bell schedule over the next two years,
        • How and when to engage the broader community in the decision making process to determine the acceptability of changes to the bell schedule,
        • What changes are needed in transportation operations to allow that organization to operate more safely, effectively, and efficiently in the future.

School Board Discussion/Decision The School Board needs to give direction to staff on the above key issues.

Staff Recommendation Specific recommendations are included in the attached document.

Options The School Board has a number of options available to it.
1. The board can direct staff to engage the community about changes to the bell schedule but this might be difficult to gain meaningful input without a specific alternative bell schedule to share.

2. The board can direct staff to begin developing a new bell schedule with the intent of sharing this schedule with the community as soon as it is available.

3.Finally, the board can direct staff to discontinue any further efforts regarding the bell schedule and continue the status quo. Regarding recommended changes to transportation operations, the board can decide not to fund these requirements and this status quo can also continue.

Agenda Item: Proposed Telecommunications Policy Revision


Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer


Background

On April 14, 2005, the School Board unanimously passed a motion to adopt Policy 8335, Wireless Telecommunication Facilities. On July 13, 2006, the Fairfax County Planning Commission approved an application to replace an existing athletic field light pole at Mount Vernon High School; action on this same application was presented to the School Board on October 19, 2006, which voted 6-5 in favor of indefinitely postponing the Mount Vernon monopole application.

At the same October 2006 meeting the School Board passed a motion to consider Policy 8335 at a future work session. The monopole policy was a topic at the March 22, 2007, School Board forum. At that time the School Board requested that staff amend Policy 8335 to clarify procedures for seeking approval of telecommunications facilities.

The proposed policy revision incorporates the philosophy of the existing policy, as well as clarifies the responsibilities of staff in determining the feasibility of school board-owned sites to house telecommunications facilities. The proposed policy also acknowledges that once the Fairfax County Planning Commission votes to approve a telecommunications facility application, the School Board can be assured that the proposed facility will be in compliance with requirements of the Federal Telecommunications Act of 1996, as stipulated in the Code of Virginia, as well as supportive of the county government’s efforts to provide adequate cellular telephone coverage for the citizens of the county.

Key Issues

The proposed policy has been revised in its entirety to clarify procedures for seeking approval of telecommunications facilities, as well as accept the Fairfax County Planning Commission’s approvals for the installation of telecommunications facilities on school board-owned property when such approval has been granted pursuant to a public hearing process. Furthermore, should an application for a telecommunications facility to





the Fairfax County Planning Commission not require that body to hold a public hearing prior to approval due to its nature as a feature already shown on the comprehensive plan, the revised policy enables the School Board to hold a public hearing on the application even when not legally required should it determine that a public hearing is in the best interest of the School Board.

School Board Discussion/Decision

The School Board needs to decide on its level of involvement in considering applications for telecommunications towers on school property. As evidenced by the Mount Vernon applications, the current policy and regulation do not provide sufficient certainty of outcome to justify the time and effort required by applicants and staff.

Staff Recommendation

That the School Board adopt the revised policy that clarifies procedures for approving the installation of telecommunications facilities on School Board-owned property.


Options

Continue with current policy and regulation processes. If the School Board makes the decision to maintain the current policy and regulation, it is unlikely that further applications for additional telecommunications towers will be processed by staff.
