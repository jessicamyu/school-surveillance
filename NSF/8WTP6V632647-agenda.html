Staff Contact:         Richard Moniuszko, Deputy Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  February 11, 2013 – Work Session No. 79

 

Subject:                   Priority Schools Initiative (PSI), Year 2 Report      

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:             Student Achievement Goal 1. Pursue Academic Excellence: All students will be literate, able to obtain, understand, analyze, integrate, communicate and apply knowledge and skills to achieve success in school and in life. Academic progress in the core disciplines will be measured to ensure that all students, regardless of race, poverty, language or disability, will graduate with the knowledge and skills necessary for college and/or employment, effectively eliminating achievement gaps.

 

Key Points: 

  1. Thirty PSI schools were identified based on Title I School Improvement status and spring 2009 SOL results (School Support Composite Index).
  2. School Board approved PSI as a three-year process, since research indicated 3-5 years are needed for stable improvement.
  3. PSI components include:  UVA School Turnaround Specialist Program, School Support Teams, Cluster Visits, Quarterly Report Monitoring, Instructional Coaches, and PSI resource allocation through School Support Team/Leadership Team.
  4. Year 1 results indicate roughly one-third of PSI schools showing gains in Reading and Math.
  5. PSI schools showing gains compared to other school divisions in Virginia.
  6. Focus for Year 2 is monitoring the effectiveness of reading and mathematics interventions, and ensuring implementation of a culture of Professional Learning Communities.

Recommendation:  Accept Year 2 PSI Report

Operational Expectation 12 - Technology

 

The Superintendent shall use technology to support teaching, learning, and the Board’s Operational Expectations and Student Achievement Goals. 

 

The Superintendent shall:

 

1.  Provide a comprehensive and functional technology infrastructure.

 

2.  Provide technology capabilities that are useful for staff members and students.

 

3.  Use technology to support diverse learning techniques and styles.

 

4.  Provide a means for interactive communication between the school system and parents, students, and the community.

 

5.  Provide information electronically about school and division programs and academic progress.

 

6.  Provide a system to access relevant and current data by appropriate users.

 

7.  Provide support to enable all students to have access to online instructional resources beyond school hours.

Contact:                 Tammy Derenak Kaufax, Committee Chairman

 

Meeting Category:  February 11, 2013 - Work Session No. 82

 

Subject:                   Recommendations to Streamline and Focus School Board Business and Meeting Agendas

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The Streamline and Focus Ad Hoc Committee was established on June 9, 2012, to analyze and recommend to the Board a more efficient and effective way to execute its work to focus on key priorities in the Student Achievement Goals and Operational Expectations goals.  The committee met on September 17, October 5 & 22, and November 28, 2012, and January 3, 2013.  Below are the recommendations from the committee to the full Board for consideration and discussion.

 

Recommendations:

 

REGULAR MEETINGS

  1. Spotlight on Learning:  need to determine purpose, and if possible should relate to a specific agenda item that night
  2. Board reports:  chairman (not meeting managers) would announce topics that were discussed during the all-day WS; then chairman would recognize committee chairmen (Governance, PEC, CPDC) to report on any topics the committee has discussed (just topics, not details) and Board liaison, as necessary; consider moving Board reports to before or after consent agenda
  3. Time clock for Board members:  use a visual reminder for time management (costs $650 to mount on camera, and three to four weeks to order/install)
  4. Debrief:  Board members to review procedures based on meeting protocol (three options are posted in BoardDocs for every regular meeting agenda)

FORUM

  1. Forum request form:  committee developed a form to be submitted electronically when requesting a forum topic (attachment)
  2. Forums: need to be scheduled regularly, efficiently, and able to deal with emerging issues

WORK SESSIONS

  1. All-day work sessions should start at 11 a.m. and end no later than 6:30 p.m. with optional start time of 10 a.m.; includes 30 minutes for lunch
  2. Reinforce posting background materials for work sessions at least a week in advance
  3. Flexibility in scheduling topics; priority should be based on time-sensitive issues

GENERAL

  1. Presentation from staff should be summary, no more than 15-20 minutes
  2. Meeting protocol:  Board to review meeting protocol from the Strategic Governance Manual (page 34) (laminated copies of meeting protocol)
  3. Require staff to provide concise issue summaries and decision matrices with pros and cons, a recommendation, and rationale
  4. Keep an ongoing list of next steps and action items to track issues and identify responsible staff members
  5. Build in time to be reflective to provide an opportunity for the Board to be productive
  6. Streamline & Focus Ad Hoc Committee reconvened as needed
