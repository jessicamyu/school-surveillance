National Gun Violence Awareness Day - June 2, 2018

 

 

National Gun Violence Awareness Day is sponsored by the American Academy of Pediatrics and the National PTA among dozens of other groups. This day of awareness is acknowledged nationwide by a movement to Wear Orange in honor of victims and survivors of gun violence. High school students in Chicago began the Wear Orange Campaign in 2013 on the birthday of their friend and gun violence victim, 15-year-old Hadiya Pendleton. Hadiya was killed in a Chicago park just after taking final exams. First Lady Michelle Obama attended her funeral, and President Obama mentioned Hadiya in his State of The Union address that year.

 

On average, 48 children and teens are shot and 7 die from their injuries each day in the United States. One tragic example comes from our own community. In November 2015, Edison High School graduate Joe Bose was a victim of a senseless and random act of gun violence in Norfolk, Virginia where he attended Hampton University. Today, Joe would be a college graduate at the beginning of a promising career in journalism. Gun violence steals the lives and potential of far too many young people.

 

The color orange symbolizes the value of human life. In 2013, teens on the South Side of Chicago asked classmates to honor their murdered friend by wearing orange. That simple call to action has grown into a national movement – and orange is becoming the symbol of gun safety.

 

Millions are coming together to fight for an end to gun violence in America. The School Board recognizes the effort to bring more awareness and remembrance to the victims of gun violence by those wearing orange on June 1 for National Gun Violence Awareness Day."

RESOLUTION HONORING STEVEN LOCKARD

 

      WHEREAS, Dr. Steven Lockard has served Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) for 4 years, with excellence, distinction and dedication, as the Deputy Superintendent since August 1, 2014, and as Interim Superintendent from November 2016 to July 2017; and;

 

         WHEREAS, Dr. Lockard has overseen the offices of the 5 FCPS Regions, the Office of the Chief Academic and Equity Officer, the Department of Human Resources, the Hearings Office, the Office of School Support, and the Office of Student Activities and Athletic Programs and supported each one with strong leadership skills, strategic forward thinking, and thoughtful intentions.  He has embraced and supported FCPS “Portrait of a Graduate” and worked to align best practices with the 4 goals of the FCPS Strategic Plan, Ignite: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship; and 

 

      WHEREAS, as the Deputy Superintendent, he led the creation and implementation of Project Momentum, a newly designed school support model that set the standard of school improvement for the Commonwealth; he ensured that Project Momentum provided support to our schools at greatest risk of losing State Accreditation and oversaw the dramatic decline of schools designated with Accredited with Warning status by VDOE; as the Interim Superintendent, he developed and created a new support structure for all schools by creating the Office of School Support; the new office expanded on his previous work of Project Momentum with the focus of increasing the academic achievement of all students; he was instrumental in ensuring that all offices worked together in a collaborative manner to move the Division forward; his motto of Winning was all about the Team and never the individual or department; and,

 

     WHEREAS, in October of 2016, Dr. Lockard was selected to serve as Interim Superintendent at a time of transition for FCPS, guided us through a difficult budget process, oversaw countless initiatives and provided strong and effective leadership until the new Superintendent was named; he then provided guidance and counsel through another important transition; and,

 

    WHEREAS, Dr. Lockard oversaw the reorganization from Clusters to Region offices in efforts to promote collaboration, cohesiveness and alignment of services between regions and our various departments to better support the 198 schools and 189,000 students; and he worked tirelessly to collaborate with administrators, staff and School Board to ensure the continued success of all students of Fairfax County Public Schools; and,

 

   NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Dr. Steven Lockard for his commitment to public education, his inspirational leadership, his unwavering dedication to and support of the students, teachers, staff and administrators of Fairfax County Public Schools, and also warmest congratulations and best wishes on his new position as Superintendent of Carroll County Public Schools. Summary/Background (Key Points): FY 2019 Approved Budget

 

FY 2019 Advertised Budget

On February 8, 2018, the School Board adopted the FY19 Advertised Budget that included a requested increase of $97.1 million, or 4.9% in the county transfer. Overall, the FY19 Advertised Budget showed a net increase of $118.9 million or 4.3%.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The State budget process has not yet concluded. FCPS state revenue projections have been updated since the Advertised budget by increasing State aid $8.7 million and an increase of $5.0 million in sales tax. Overall, the FCPS FY19 State revenue projections are consistent with those seen in the Governor’s Introduced Budget and almost all increases are due to enrollment, re-benchmarking and recalculation of the LCI. Thus, it is unlikely we would see any reductions to State revenue. Once the State budget process concludes, any additional State funds allocated to FCPS will be brought back to the School Board at a quarterly review as a revision to the FY19 Approved Budget.

 

Impact of County Budget

On May 1, 2018, the County Board of Supervisors adopted their FY19 Budget that included an increase in the transfer to the School Operating Fund of $84.74 million or 4.3% over the FY18 Approved Budget. The County acknowledged the FCPS increase in State aid of $8.7 million and the increase of $5.0 million in the Sales tax assumption. These changes allowed the County Board of Supervisors to reduce the transfer by $12.4 million. Thus, the FY19 Approved Budget is fully funded.

 

May 1, 2018 Budget Work Session

As presented at the May 1, 2018 work session, there is no shortfall in the FY19 Budget, after accounting for the State and County budget adjustments. Note that the revisions to State revenue total $13.7 million coupled with a County reduction of $12.4 million result in a $1.3 million revenue surplus. The revenue surplus will cover the net increase in other adjustments, discussed at length at the May 1, 2018 work session, and detailed below. The other adjustments primarily include the spring enrollment forecast and other minor increases and decreases in selected programs.

Award the contract for the HVAC replacement at Lorton Center to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, Harvey W. Hottel, Inc., in the amount of $1,129,580, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Subject: Award of Contract – HVAC Replacement at Lorton Center

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category: May 24, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4- Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background:

 

The heating and air conditioning system at the Lorton Center (FCPS Transportation Offices), originally constructed in the 1930’s and enlarged from time to time thereafter, requires replacement.

 

The existing HVAC needs of this facility are currently met through a combination of various heating and air conditioning equipment and systems (including “window” air conditioning units in part of the building). Those systems are old and inefficient, requiring increasing levels of maintenance. Moreover, the existing systems are not a part of the central, automated control system which FCPS uses to manage its energy consumption on a division-wide basis.

 

Accordingly, the Office of Facilities Management (OFM) commissioned the design of a single HVAC system for this facility, which is known as variable refrigerant flow simultaneous heating and cooling system. The new system will permit inclusion of Lorton Center within the FCPS automated, remote control protocol for all FCPS buildings.

 

OFM prepared an Invitation for Bid and issued a public notice as required by law. Although ten contractors picked up plans and seven attended the pre-bid meeting, the lone bid submitted was from Harvey W. Hottel, Inc., an established HVAC contractor in the Washington D.C. metropolitan area. Hottel has a track record of successful performance on prior FCPS projects. The bid price of $1,129,580 is consistent with current industry pricing and is within available funding, which will be provided from the Operating Fund.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the HVAC replacement at Lorton Center to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, Harvey W. Hottel, Inc., in the amount of $1,129,580, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Because the aggregate dollar amount paid for services performed in a given year under such a contract is expected to exceed $250,000, Policy 5011.3 requires the School Board to award the contract.

 

Funding for the hazardous materials removal contract is provided from the Major Maintenance Fund, as well as school and department Operating Funds.

 

An invitation to bid has been issued and advertised; bids were received by OFM on May 23, 2018. An award recommendation will be furnished prior to the June 14, 2018, School Board meeting.
