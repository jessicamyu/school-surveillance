Meeting Category:  September 18, 2017 – Work Session

 

Subject: FCPSOn       

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion                    

 

Key Points:    

FCPSOn, Phase one, was started in 15 schools during the 2016-2017 school year. In order to assess the effectiveness of this initiative, Johns Hopkins University conducted a comprehensive program evaluation. Demonstrated year one successes include: initial student achievement gains, development of Portrait of a Graduate skills, increases in student engagement, development of digital citizenship modules, and the development of a digital ecosystem to support learning.  Areas of focus for the upcoming school year include: providing focused support and professional development for teachers in a 1:1 environment, increasing parent and community communication about FCPSOn, and continuing to foster student development of Portrait of a graduate skills. Researchers from Johns Hopkins will be in attendance to share their first year findings.

Highlights of performance outcomes (based upon JHU evaluation and BrightBytes reporting data 2016 -2017) include:

  • Access Data:
    • Home access at e-Learning Backpack schools increased from between 60-80% to 100%
  • Stakeholder Perception Data:
    • 98% of students report that technology use in class enhances their learning.
    • 85% of FCPSOn teachers believe that technology enhances student learning and engagement after one school year of having devices in their classroom.
    • 79% of parents believe that technology use in class can enhance student learning

 

  • Student Performance Data:
    • Performance in writing increased by an average of 7% for Students with Disabilities and 3% for Economically Disadvantaged Students and English Learners in FCPSOn schools
    • Performance in Reading increased by an average of 4% for Students with Disabilities and English Learners in FCPSOn schools
    • Performance in Math increased by an average of 3% for English Learners in FCPSOn schools

 

Recommendation:

The school board will deepen understanding of the FCPSOn initiative and its importance in supporting the division’s strategic plan and Portrait of a Graduate goals.

Subject:  Suicide Prevention, Risk Assessment, Postvention Policy

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

This is a new policy.

 

A vote on Policy 2XXX.X, Suicide Prevention, Risk Assessment, Postvention, originally scheduled for October 20, 2016 was delayed to allow for greater discussion. Following meetings with the School Board Governance Committee revisions were made to clarify the purpose of the policy and expectations for school prevention, intervention and postvention efforts. 

 

  1. This policy establishes expectations for suicide prevention efforts, individual suicide risk assessment and intervention, and response and supports for students and staff following a death by suicide.
  1. Prevention:
    1. Training for educators, parents and students on signs of depression and suicidal thinking and how to refer for help, and include information on reducing stigma
    2. Wellness screenings
    3. Access to school-based mental health providers (school counselors, school psychologists, school social workers)
    4. Crisis support services
    5. Response to reports of students at risk due to mental health conditions or life circumstances, including those identified on Student Health Information Form
  1. Suicide Risk Assessment:
    1. Procedures for responding to report of suicidal thinking by student
    2. Determination of risk
    3. Parent notification
    4. Referral to appropriate resources
  1. Postvention:
    1. Procedures for providing response to school community following a death by suicide
    2. Provision of grief counseling
    3. Identification of youth at risk
    4. Parent notification and referral to appropriate resources as needed

Recommendation:

 

It is recommended that following review at the work session, the School Board forward Policy 2XXX.X Suicide Prevention, Risk Assessment, Postvention to a regular meeting for approval.

Subject:  Strategic Plan Goal 4: Resource Stewardship

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:   Strategic Plan – Goal 4 Report

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 4, Resource Stewardship.

 

The following are the two overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 4, Resource Stewardship:

 

Overarching Strategy 10

 

Maximize and align FCPS resources in order to be accountable and transparent with the community regarding the resources of FCPS

Desired Outcomes

  • A budget decision-making framework, based on financial management principles and standards, will be regularly and systematically utilized
  • Benchmarking processes will weigh major organizational operations against comparable school systems for effectiveness, efficiency, and Return on Investment

 

Overarching Strategy 11

 

Promote the value and benefits of the community’s investment in FCPS in order to increase the community’s understanding of the Division’s operating and capital needs to support student success

Desired Outcomes

  • Stakeholders will understand where and how financial resources will be utilized
  • FCPS will regularly and systematically advocate for fiscal resources at the county, state, and federal levels

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The principal selection process is being revised to incorporate a candidate pool approach for the beginning of the process. These enhancements to the process will increase and diversify the candidate pool, while providing individual schools greater access to candidates from across FCPS (and candidates external to the organization). The revised process is the first stage in the development of a systematic leadership pipeline that will be coupled with more robust and personalized leadership development for future building and system leaders. The information is provided for discussion with the Board; no Board action is required.
