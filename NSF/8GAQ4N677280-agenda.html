Subject:                        Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                         

 

Key Points:

 

On February 3, 2011, the School Board adopted the FY 2012 Advertised Budget which included the following expenditure highlights:  two percent market scale adjustment for employees; step increases for eligible employees; increases in salary lapse; retirement and health rate increases; contract length reductions; membership growth; online Standards of Learning (SOL) test requirements for elementary schools; high school needs-based staffing; and reductions related to one-time federal stimulus funding.

 

Included in FCPS’ FY 2012 Advertised Budget was a request for increased funding from the county.  The Board of Supervisors approved a level transfer from the county to the School Operating Fund resulting in a decrease of $48.8 million as compared to the request in the advertised budget.  As a result of the impacts of the county budget, state budget, and other updates, additional adjustments are required.    

 

The Superintendent’s recommendations for the approved budget include:  implementing full-day kindergarten at all schools; a one percent market scale adjustment for all employees and step increases for eligible employees; additional support for students during the discipline process (funding of $0.5 million will be set aside for discipline support recommendations presented to the School Board at the May 16 work session); elimination of Advanced Placement (AP) and International Baccalaureate (IB) test fees; and increased funding for membership and student adjustments.  FCPS’ Virginia Retirement System (VRS) reserve will be utilized to cover the increased rates, which will result in a total of $60.6 million remaining in the reserve to address projected significant rate increases and required repayments in FY 2013 and beyond. 

 

After the recommended adjustments, a shortfall of $3.5 million remains.  Three options are provided to addressing the shortfall: 

 

Option A

Recommended by the Superintendent and increases the beginning balance for FY 2012 by redirecting funding previously set aside for the beginning balance for FY 2013.  A total of $5.0 million was set aside at the FY 2011 Third Quarter Budget Review, and Option A will reduce this amount to $1.5 million.  

 

Option B

Implements full-day kindergarten over two years.  As a result, 16 schools would receive full-day kindergarten in FY 2012, and the final 20 schools would receive full-day kindergarten in FY 2013. 

 

 

Option C

Provides employees with a one percent bonus in place of a market scale adjustment. 

 

The Superintendent’s recommendations and options A, B, and C are detailed in the FY 2012 Approved Budget Recommendation Summary attachment.
