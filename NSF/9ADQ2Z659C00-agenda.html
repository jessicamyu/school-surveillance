OE-5

 

Financial Planning

 

The Superintendent shall develop and maintain a multi-year financial plan that is related directly to the Board’s Student Achievement Goals priorities and Operational Expectations goals, and that avoids long-term fiscal jeopardy to the district.

 

The Superintendent shall develop a budget that:

  1. Is in a summary format that is understandable, transparent and easily accessible by the community and presented in a manner that demonstrates the relationship between the budget and the priorities within the Student Achievement Goals and any Operational Expectations goals.
  2. Accurately describes revenues and expenditures.
  3. Shows the amount spent in each budget category for the previous three fiscal years, the amount budgeted for the current fiscal year, and the amount budgeted for the next fiscal year.
  4. Explains budget-planning assumptions, identifying significant trends and changes.
  5. Provides the Board with a fiscal forecast of needs five (5) years into the future.
  6. Reflects anticipated changes in employee compensation, including inflationary adjustments, step increases, performance increases and benefits.
  7. Includes such amounts as the Board determines to be necessary for its governing function, including School Board staff, Board member training, consultation, attendance at professional conferences and events, and other matters determined by the Board to be necessary for it to effectively perform its governance duties.

The Superintendent shall not develop a budget that:

 

8.  Plans for the expenditure in any fiscal year of more funds than are conservatively projected to be available during the year.

9.  Provides for a School Board flexibility reserve of less than $8 million.

 

OE-6

Financial Administration

The Superintendent shall not cause or allow any financial activity or condition that materially deviates from the budget adopted by the Board; cause or allow any fiscal condition that is inconsistent with achieving the Board’s Student Achievement Goals or meeting any Operational Expectations goals; or places the long-term financial health of the district in jeopardy.

 

The Superintendent shall:

  1. Assure that payroll and legitimate debts of the district are promptly paid when due.
  2. Assure that all purchases are based upon comparative prices of items of similar value, including consideration of both cost and long-term quality.
  3. All purchases shall be administered in accordance with the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, as adopted annually by the Board. 
  4. Coordinate and cooperate with the annual audit of all district funds and accounts.
  5. Make all reasonable efforts to collect any funds due the district from any source.
  6. Keep complete and accurate financial records by funds and accounts in accordance with Generally Accepted Accounting Principles.
  7. Publish a financial condition statement annually.
  8. File required financial reports accurately and on time.

 

The Superintendent shall not:

  1. Expend more funds than have been received in the fiscal year unless revenues are made available through other legal means.
  2. Indebt the organization beyond what is approved.

 

OE-7

 

Asset Protection

 

The Superintendent shall assure that all district assets are adequately protected, properly maintained, appropriately used and not placed at undue risk. 

 

The Superintendent shall:

  1. Assure that district property is insured at 100 percent of its replacement value, using reasonable application of self-insurance and commercial insurance.
  2. Maintain both Errors and Omissions and Comprehensive General Liability insurance coverage protecting board members, staff and the district itself in an amount that is reasonable for school districts of comparable size and character.
  3. Assure that all personnel who have access to material amounts of district and school funds are bonded.
  4. Protect intellectual property, information, files, records and fixed assets from loss or significant damage.

Subject:                  Proposed Revision to the Strategic Governance Manual in Section 2: Operational Expectations

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:            Strategic Governance Manual

 

Key points:

 

At the July 9, 2013, work session, there was Board consensus that Facilities staff develop recommendations for new Operational Expectations (OEs) related to energy management and conservation.  The Governance Committee would consider those recommendations and then make a recommendation to the full Board.

 

The Governance Committee met on October 23 and November 4, 2013, and reviewed and discussed staff’s recommended language for two new OEs—one related to energy management and one related to conservation.

 

The Governance Committee agreed to recommend that the two new OEs be added (excerpt of OE-11 with the recommended additions highlighted below) and the Strategic Governance Manual be updated accordingly.

 

  1. Facilities:  The Superintendent shall:
  1. Maintain a Facilities Comprehensive Plan that assures that classroom capacity and infrastructure meet instructional program and community needs and is equitable across the county. The plan will:
  1. Ensure sound instructional practices;
  2. Maintain a reasonable balance of capacity utilization;
  3. Uses resources wisely, efficiently and in an environmentally responsible way;
  4. Disclose assumptions on which the plan is based, including growth patterns and the financial and human impact that individual projects will have on other parts of the organization and the community; 
  5. Ensure that land acquisition recommendations are based upon demographic trends and growth patterns, comparative costs, construction and transportation factors and extraordinary contingency costs due to potential natural and man-made risks;
  6. Utilize energy as efficiently as possible;
  7. Ensure proper recycling programs are in use. 

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve the proposed revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual in Section 2: Operational Expectations (OE-11, Facilities and Transportation), as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  November 11, 2013 – Work Session No. 44

 

Subject:                  Renovation Queue – 5 Year Evaluation 

   

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

During the May 15, 2013, School Board work session, the Department of Facilities and Transportation was directed by the School Board to develop a timeline to implement the Renovation Queue 5 year evaluation of schools within the Queue which have not been named in a prior CIP.

 

The intent of the evaluation is to reassess the unnamed Queue schools in case conditions within a school have changed creating a need to accelerate a potential renovation. The evaluation would be conducted by an independent architectural/ engineering firm based upon the criterion approved by the School Board. The firm would be responsible not only for performing the assessments but ranking the schools as well.

 

The Renovation Queue was last approved by the School Board in January of 2009. The Renovation Queue consists of 63 schools which were either constructed or renovated prior to 1992. There are presently 34 schools which have been publicly identified in the CIP. During the May 15, 2013 work session the School Board decided that schools which have been named in the 2013 CIP would not be included in the 5 Year Evaluation. Therefore, the 29 schools which comprise the balance of the Renovation Queue will be evaluated.

 

Evaluation Timeline:

 

As noted previously, the construction of many of the projects within the Renovation Queue is already underway or will begin shortly after the vote for the 2013 Bond Referendum. Attachment A indicates which projects have been previously named within prior CIP’s and the estimated bond in which the planning monies and construction funding will become available.

 

It should be noted that these time frames are based upon our present knowledge of current enrollment, projections and estimated costs. There are a number of factors which could potentially delay one or more projects. On another note, Hybla Valley ES was ranked 33rd. Because it’s funding did not fall within the five year window last year it was not named in the CIP. We have refrained from naming additional schools until the 5 Year Evaluation has been completed. These renovations when coupled with our capacity enhancement projects will increase our capacity by approximately 10,500 seats over the next 9 years.

 

Attachment A also illustrates the fact that the currently named projects will require funding until the 2019 Bond Referendum. What this essentially means is that the earliest funding for the unnamed Queue schools would not be needed until the 2017 Bond Referendum for planning purposes.

 

Attachment B depicts items which we believe are necessary as we move forward with a reevaluation of the remaining schools within the Renovation Queue.

 

The first item identified is the update to the Educational Specifications. The intent of the revision is to bring our facilities into alignment with the current program of studies and to increase efficiency within our schools by adding to clarity regarding the needs of specific programs which have not been previously articulated. We do not believe that the proposed changes will affect the potential rating of a school since we are not necessarily proposing more square feet. Our intention is to submit the revised Educational Specifications to the board in June of 2014.

 

Although there were no changes to the Queue Ratings Criteria in the May 2013 work session, we have allotted time for the school board to consider a change if they believe it is warranted. Our preference is to have any changes to the criteria completed prior to hiring of the independent consultants by November of 2014.

 

During the winter of 2015, we will Request Proposals from qualified consultants to conduct the re-evaluation of the remaining 29 schools. The consultants will conduct site visits during the spring and summer of 2015 evaluating the facilities based upon the approved criteria. In the fall of 2015 we will submit their evaluations and rankings to the School Board for approval and adoption.

 

As previously stated (based upon our current funding and identified projects) the earliest point at which we believe these projects would begin receiving capital funding would be the 2017 Bond Referendum – the initial draft will be created in the spring of 2017.

 

Further Considerations:

 

We are aware of the interest in completing the Legacy High School Renovations as quickly as possible. It should be noted that if the joint Infrastructure Financing Committee is successful in obtaining an additional $25M per year beginning in FY 2016 it would be possible to accelerate the renovation of Falls Church HS by more than 2 years. This acceleration could be achieved while maintaining the present order and timing of other projects in the Renovation Queue.

Key Points:

 

In February 2013 FCPS initiated a contract with Prismatic Services, Inc. to complete a review of FCPS’ Food and Nutrition Services program. This review had three primary objectives:

  • suggesting innovative approaches to preparing food;
  • offering healthier, freshly prepared whole foods for school lunch and breakfast; and
  • greatly reducing use of highly processed foods and artificial additives.

The review began in February 2013, and will conclude with a presentation of the final report by Tatia Prieto, Prismatic Project Manager. After the report presentation by the consultant, staff will provide next steps related to recommendations and the remaining time will be allocated for a question and answer session.
