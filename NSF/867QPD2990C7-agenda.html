Anne Murphy, Division Counsel
CHARITY DRIVES
Legislation, Policy & Future Issues
December 6, 2004, School Board Work Session

The School Board is considering revising its policy regarding charity fundraising. Staff has identified two options for School Board consideration.

The first option is to maintain the current version of Policy 1375.1, which essentially prohibits charity fundraising in schools. This approach
· Is preferred by most principals; high school and middle school principals associations recently reviewed this issue and reaffirmed their support of current policy.
· Promotes focus on instruction; schools receive many requests for assistance from charities, and most have neither the time nor the resources to handle them.
· Responds to community members who have complained about solicitations coming through schools and PTAs.
· Probably results in fewer legal claims than a policy that provides principals discretion to pick among fundraisers.
· Results in fewer problems under the FCPS self-insurance plan.
· Eliminates need for financial reporting and record keeping requirements for charity funds.

The second option would preserve the spirit of the existing charity ban, but would allow two types of exceptions: principal-approved fundraisers meeting certain criteria, and Superintendent-approved system-wide charity drives. This approach
· Is preferred by some principals, and acknowledges that some schools and/or PTAs already conduct these fundraisers, e.g., many elementary schools/PTAs sponsor “Jump Rope for Heart”.
· Acknowledges that some community members want charity fundraisers.
· Recognizes that PTA insurance policies increasingly limit PTAs’ ability to conduct fundraisers.
· Provides opportunities for students to assist the less fortunate.
· Allows for the continuation of certain system-wide activities conducted with FCPS business partners, such as Neediest Kids.

A draft policy for this second approach is attached (Attachment 1). Also attached is a chart showing other policies and regulations that impact this issue (Attachment 2).

SCHOOL PROFFER LANGUAGE

On January 7, 2003, the Board of Supervisors adopted the Residential Development Criteria Implementation Motion that included a provision for cash proffers to schools. Since that time, no standard guidance related to the proper and uniform implementation of the proffer has existed. In order to give developers a clear understanding and standard procedure for these proffers, Fairfax County Public Schools and Fairfax County staffs have developed the following four suggested school proffers and their related procedures for implementation. The four proffer types are; dedication of land, contributions to a specific school for a specific improvement, cash contributions to county schools, and goods/services of greater or equal value in lieu of contribution. Specific language for each proffer is included in the attached information.
SUGGESTED SCHOOL PROFFERS
Revised November 22, 2004


1. DEDICATION OF LAND

At the time of site/subdivision plan approval, the applicant shall dedicate to the Board of Supervisors in fee simple approximately (number of) acres as shown on the CDP/FDP (GDP) for school purposes.

2. CONTRIBUTIONS TO A SPECIFIC SCHOOL FOR A SPECIFIC IMPROVEMENT

Per the Residential Development Criteria Implementation Motion adopted by the Board of Supervisors on September 9, 2002, effective January 7, 2003, (preceding phrase used only when contribution is consistent with the formula; if amount is different, do not include phrase) prior to subdivision/site plan approval, the applicant shall contribute a sum of $(dollar amount)* to the Board of Supervisors for transfer to the Fairfax County School Board for capital improvements identified in the School Board Improved CIP, (purpose, i.e., the installation of modular classrooms facilities) at (name of specific school) School. At that time the funds are received, other equivalent capital facilities for (name of specific school) School may be substituted, at the discretion of the Fairfax County School Board.

3. CASH CONTRIBUTIONS TO COUNTY SCHOOLS

Per the Residential Development Criteria Implementation Motion adopted by the Board of Supervisors on September 9, 2002, effective January 7, 2003, (preceding phrase used only when contribution is consistent with the formula; if amount is different, do not include phrase) prior to subdivision/site plan approval, the applicant shall contribute a sum of $(dollar figure)* to the Board of Supervisors for transfer to the Fairfax County School Board to be utilized for projects contained in the CIP for public schools within Fairfax County.

4. GOODS/SERVICES OF GREATER OR EQUAL VALUE IN LIEU OF CONTRIBUTION

Prior to subdivision/site plan approval, the applicant shall contribute a sum of $(dollar figure)* to the Board of Supervisors, which funds shall be allocated by the Board of Supervisors for capital improvements for public schools within Fairfax County. If approved by the Board of Supervisors or its authorized agent, the applicant may make an in-kind contribution for capital improvements to the Fairfax County School Board equal to, or greater in value than, the cash contribution, as determined by the Board of Supervisors or its agent.

* Instead of a specific amount, the applicant could proffer an amount per unit, based upon the $7500 per student guideline and the adopted student yield rate per type of dwelling unit, and could further proffer that if the number of units constructed were fewer than that approved, the contribution would be based upon the actual number of units constructed.
