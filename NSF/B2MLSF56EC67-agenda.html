Veterans Day - November 11, 2018

 

 Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

 Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

 The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.  Many schools have special events, to which they invite veterans and members of the school community on active duty military.

  

 

 

American Education Week - November 12-16, 2018

 

 American Education Week was established in 1921 by the National Education Association (NEA) to raise public awareness of the importance of public education in America.  This year’s theme, Reach. Educate. Inspire, reminds all Americans of the importance of public education and offers the opportunity to celebrate public education and honor those who ensure that every child receives a quality education. As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week features a special observance on each day of the week, including parents, education support professionals, and substitute educators. 

 

 

 

  

Geography Awareness Week - November 11-17, 2018

 

 Established by presidential proclamation more than 25 years ago, this annual public awareness program encourages citizens young and old to think and learn about the significance of place and how we affect and are affected by it.  Too many young Americans are unable to make effective decisions, understand geo-spatial issues, or even recognize their impacts as global citizens.  Geography Awareness week is observed in November and promotes what geography is, why it is important and the relevance of a geographic education in preparing citizens to understand and debate pressing social and environmental issues and problems.

3.04 School Psychology Awareness Week Recognition

School psychologists are uniquely qualified members of school teams serving students, staff and families.  School psychologists receive specialized advanced graduate preparation that includes coursework and practical experiences relevant to both psychology and education.  They apply expertise in mental health, learning, and behavior, to change lives by taking actions to help children and youth succeed academically, socially, behaviorally, and emotionally.  In alignment with Ignite, school psychologists help individuals realize their own abilities, develop the skills they need to cope with the normal stresses of life, to work productively and fruitfully, and be valuable contributors in the community. 

 

School psychologists collaborate with school teams in the development of universal prevention practices that foster resiliency, increase educator understanding of social/emotional development, reduce barriers to learning, and increase student protective factors.  They support effective behavior management leading to improved school climate, respond to crises, and teach and implement trauma-informed practices.   

School psychologists utilize expertise in data collection and analysis to identify concerns and to monitor interventions, which may include the formal evaluation process.  In their work with students, school psychologists help students develop the social-emotional and life skills necessary to realize personal achievement and growth, and to build resilience and a sense of belonging.  Through delivery of direct services, using evidenced-based practices, school psychologists provide individual and group counseling to address challenges and concerns.  School psychologists consult with staff and parents to further their understanding of complex student needs and to help them develop the necessary skills to appropriately respond to student needs. 

 

School psychologists support families by sharing information about student skill development and wellness.  They develop interventions that may be used at home and they connect families with available community resources.

 

All children and youth can face problems from time to time related to learning, social relationships, making difficult decisions, or managing emotions such as feeling depressed, anxious, worried, or isolated. School psychologists help students, families, educators, and members of the community understand and resolve both long-term, chronic problems and short-term issues that students may face. They are a highly skilled and ready resource in the effort to ensure that all FCPS children and youth thrive in school, at home, and in life.

Subject: 2019 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, director, Office of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category: November 8, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement:

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is both directly and indirectly affected by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing in particular the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The School Board annually adopt a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to FCPS students, teachers, staff, and community. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board (FCSB) 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions and (where applicable) formatting changes from the Board’s 2018 legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect current approaches to policy debates and issues, and to clarify, simplify and streamline existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board will discuss staff recommended changes to the FCSB 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for November 12, 2018. Full draft text as well as a complete table of proposed changes to the draft 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will be included as part of the Board’s Work Session agenda item. Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 19, 2018.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2019 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, a variety of stakeholders (School Board, Leadership Team, principals, teachers, students, and the community) provided input to prioritize the outcomes in each of the four goal areas.

 

The School Board endorsed the eight prioritized areas of focus for school years 2018-2020 in their July 23, 2018, work session. Refinements have been made based on the discussion in that work session. Aspirational goals – which show what success in each goal area would look like for students, families, employees, and the community – have been clearly defined. Baselines – which show FCPS’ recent performance – have been used to set performance targets that will be used to hold the Division accountable for short-term progress.

 

The Board will have an opportunity to review the elements that reflect success in the strategic plan Sys 2018-2020 focus: (1) what FCPS aspires to accomplish and (2) how we will measure success.

Subject: 2019 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, director, Office of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category: November 8, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement:

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is both directly and indirectly affected by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing in particular the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The School Board annually adopt a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to FCPS students, teachers, staff, and community. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board (FCSB) 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions and (where applicable) formatting changes from the Board’s 2018 legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect current approaches to policy debates and issues, and to clarify, simplify and streamline existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board will discuss staff recommended changes to the FCSB 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for November 12, 2018. Full draft text as well as a complete table of proposed changes to the draft 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will be included as part of the Board’s Work Session agenda item. Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 19, 2018.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2019 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

Subject: Strategic Plan Priorities

 

Staff Contact: Marty K. Smith, chief operating officer

 

Other Staff Present:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Resources

           Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Special Services

           Helen Nixon, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

           Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services

           Lidi Hruda, director, Office of Research and Strategic Improvement

 

Meeting Category: November 8, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan 2018-2020

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, a variety of stakeholders (School Board, Leadership Team, principals, teachers, students, and the community) provided input to prioritize the outcomes in each of the four goal areas.

 

The School Board endorsed the eight prioritized areas of focus for school years 2018-2020 in their July 23, 2018, work session. Refinements have been made based on the discussion in that work session. Aspirational goals – which show what success in each goal area would look like for students, families, employees, and the community – have been clearly defined. Baselines – which show FCPS’ recent performance – have been used to set performance targets that will be used to hold the Division accountable for short-term progress.

 

The Board will have an opportunity to review the elements that reflect success in the strategic plan Sys 2018-2020 focus: (1) what FCPS aspires to accomplish and (2) how we will measure success.

 

Recommendation:

The School Board accept the recommended changes to the strategic plan.
