    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

          6.03 Amendment to ERFC - Recommendation to approve proposed Amendment Number 10 to the Educational Employeesâ€™ of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of (A) making technical changes to conform to federal law regarding U.S. military reserve retired pay; (B) clarifying time limits for filing any legal action relating to the plan; and (C) clarifying ERFC provisions relating to any future changes that Congress might enact for Social Security benefits (ERFC; action 6/09/05)
          Meeting
          May 26, 2005 - Regular Meeting No. 20
          Category
          6. New Business - 9 p.m.
          Type
          Information

          Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

          SUBJECT: Amendments to ERFC and ERFC 2001

          RECOMMENDATION:
              That the School Board approve proposed Amendment Number 10 to the Educational Employees’ of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of (A) making technical changes to conform to federal law regarding U.S. military reserve retired pay; (B) clarifying time limits for filing any legal action relating to the plan; and (C) clarifying ERFC provisions relating to any future changes that Congress might enact for Social Security benefits.

          EXPLANATION:

          A summary of the proposed amendments in each category is provided below.

          A. Technical changes to conform to federal law regarding members who are entitled to receive U.S. military reserve retired pay.
              ERFC provides up to three years of service credit free to members who have served in the U.S. Armed Forces and permits members to purchase credit for additional military service. (The plan also grants credit, as required by federal law, for military service that interrupts a member’s ERFC-covered employment.) The plan document states that such service credit is not available if the member will be entitled to receive military retired pay for his or her military service. Federal law requires an exception to that rule in the case of service that will be counted toward U.S. military reserve retired pay. The proposed amendments to Sections 1.22, 11.02, and 16.10 of ERFC would add that exception to the Plan document. The proposed amendment to Section 1.07 of ERFC and the proposed amendment to Section 1.05 of ERFC 2001 would make clear that neither this military credit nor any other credit will be granted if it would give the member more than one year of service credit for any fiscal year.

          B. Amendment to prescribe a time limit for filing any lawsuit concerning the Plan.
              Amendments to Section 16.17 of ERFC and to Section 11.15 of ERFC 2001 are proposed for the purpose of specifying a limitations period for any kind of lawsuit that might be filed against the Plan, the Trustees, or the School Board relating to the Plan. The Virginia Code does not clearly specify the statute of limitations for such lawsuits, creating a risk of costly litigation over the simple question of whether a claim has been filed within the appropriate period of time. Rulings of the Virginia Supreme Court permit benefit plans to specify a time limit for this purpose, if the limit is at least one year. The proposed amendment would allow two years for the filing of any lawsuit relating to the Plan. For transition purposes, any potential claim now in existence would be timely if filed by July 1, 2007.

          C. Amendment to clarify the effect on ERFC of any future amendments to Social Security.
              The ERFC plan of benefits was designed to permit retirement before the age at which a member can draw Social Security benefits. ERFC pays a “lifetime” benefit that begins at retirement and continues for the member’s lifetime, plus a supplemental benefit that is paid only until the member is eligible for full Social Security. When ERFC was substantially amended in 1988, federal law included a scheduled increase in the age at which full Social Security benefits are payable, from age 65 for those born before 1938 to age 67 for those born in 1960 or later. The ERFC plan design adopted in 1988 anticipated those scheduled increases and provided that as the Social Security retirement age went up, the age at which the ERFC supplemental benefit would stop would also go up automatically. The actuary’s projections of the contributions that are required to fund the plan have taken into account those future increases in the length of time that the supplemental benefit will be paid. The actuary’s projections do not take into account any additional increases in Social Security retirement age that might be enacted in conjunction with Social Security reform proposals.
              The proposed amendment to ERFC Section 16.13 would make clear that only the currently scheduled increases in Social Security retirement age will result in automatic changes in ERFC benefits. If Congress enacts any other changes in Social Security retirement age or benefit amounts, the Trustees and the actuary would study those changes in relation to the ERFC benefit formulas and make recommendations to the School Board if any adjustments in ERFC benefit formulas seem necessary.

          SUBJECT: Citizens for 2005 School Bond Committee

          RECOMMENDATION:
              That the School Board appoint individuals to serve on the Citizens for 2005 School Bond Committee, as listed in the agenda item.
          EXPLANATION:
              When a school bond referendum has been placed on the ballot for voter approval, the School Board has traditionally appointed a group of interested citizens to serve as a committee to distribute information about bond projects and to solicit support in the community for authorizing the sale of school bonds. The work of this committee is supported by private contributions that are solicited and collected by the committee itself.
