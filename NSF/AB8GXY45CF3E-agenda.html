During its July 28, 2016, regular meeting, the Fairfax County School Board passed a motion: 

 

The committee broke into subcommittees to answer the School Board’s different charges as follows:

I move that the School Board recognize the need for further consideration of community concerns about the name of J.E.B. Stuart High School.

I further move that the Board recognize that renaming the school to one more reflective of the community it serves may be a laudable goal. The process for evaluating whether the name should be changed should engage the community in a public and transparent manner.

I further move that the School Board approve the creation of an Ad hoc Committee to consider renaming J.E.B. Stuart High School to consist of students, parents, Stuart community members, alumni, and business and community leaders to be chosen by the Superintendent in consultation with the Full Board. The Superintendent and FCPS staff will facilitate the group’s work. The working group will weigh the pros and cons of a name change; continue to ensure public engagement and determine the extent of support for change; explore public-private partnerships to finance any name change; ways to prevent added burdens on the school’s booster clubs, PTSA and Stuart school administration; the best method for transitioning from an old name to a new one; proper record retention; and the best timing for such a change, if such a change is approved by the Board. The working group will also provide ideas for ways to honor and preserve the school’s history, traditions and past achievements and to memorialize its decades as J.E.B. Stuart High School. The working group will report on its findings and recommendations to the School Board by March 2017.

The committee broke into subcommittees to answer the School Board’s different charges as follows:

  • Subcommittee 1 – Weigh the pros and cons of a name change
  • Subcommittee 2 – Determine the support for a change
  • Subcommittee 3 and 4 – Explore public private partnerships to finance any name change and to identify ways to prevent added burdens on the schools booster clubs, PTSA and school administration

Originally, the committee was scheduled to submit their reports in March, however, the committee needed more time to work through the charges, and a new completion date of May 24, 2017 was recommended.

The Ad Hoc Committee submitted their reports to the Superintendent on May 24, 2017 and were in turn submitted to the School Board for their consideration. There are seven reports: one pro name change and one against name change from each of the subcommittees and one individual who submitted their own.

 

Recommendation:

 

The School Board will now consider the reports and what action, if any, they will take on this item. In the work session on June 12, the School Board will hear from members of the Ad Hoc Committee. The School Board will make a determination for further action.

Other Staff Present:  Jennifer Glaser, director, Counseling and College and Career Readiness, ISD

                            

Meeting Category:  June 12, 2017 – Work Session

 

Subject: Pathways to Graduation

 

School Board Action Required: Information and Review of Local Graduation Requirements, Pathway Proposal Updates, and Relevant VDOE Discussions 

 

 

Related to:  FCPS Strategic Plan, Goal 1: Student Success                

 

 

 

Key Points:    

 

VDOE decisions about changes to graduation requirements for 2018-19 and beyond are expected by December 2017. The FCPS proposal for multiple pathways to graduation includes additional flexibility for local FCPS requirements while also adding options for students to pursue a pathway related to one of the nationally recognized career clusters. The pathway proposal addresses goals outlined in the strategic plan and legislative priorities related to college and career readiness while ensuring that additional flexibility in graduation requirements does not limit a student’s postsecondary opportunities. The proposal aligns with current workforce trends, FCPS student interests, and existing course offerings to increase the likelihood of community and industry support, student engagement and participation in the pathway options, and a school’s ability to successfully implement the proposed changes.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board allow time to interpret and apply pending VDOE actions prior to moving forward with changes in local FCPS graduation requirements including pathway options, and that the School Board support and continue advocacy to the Virginia Board of Education for additional course and exam substitutions to increase flexibility for students to pursue pathway options.

                                  Richard Pollio, director, Office of ESOL Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 12, 2017– Work Session

 

Subject:  English Learner Study

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

Current national, state, and local growth trends for the English learner have prompted questions about the needs and services provided by school divisions for this population. In this context, Fairfax County Public Schools made a commitment in its Strategic Plan to expand its knowledge about the needs and performance of students receiving the services for English learners and those participating in a new high school pilot program. A two year study was undertaken by the Office of Program Evaluation. Year 1 of the study was presented to the School Board during the 2015-2016 school year.

 

This final report combines the results from both years to provide:

  • A description of the English learner population growth over the past decade,
  • An analysis of how well FCPS services align with best practices in their design and implementation,
  • A discussion on outcomes for English learners, and
  • A description of costs, including the return on investment associated with the delivery of the services.

 

Based on the findings and conclusions, the following recommendations are made to the Superintendent:

 

1. Designate a division-level unit (position, office, team, etc.) to oversee divisionwide coordination of the delivery of all three learning experiences (inclusive culture, explicit English instruction, rigorous curriculum) needed by English learners.

 

2. Direct the division-level unit to explore the concerns of schools about the constraints of time and personnel and report findings to the Superintendent.  If determined necessary, the report should propose alternatives to the use of time and personnel or to the delivery of English learner services that would provide greater flexibility in schools’ organizational structures, particularly at the elementary level.  Reporting of proposed alternatives should specify costs, benefits, and challenges of each alternative.

 

3. Direct the division-level unit to create, disseminate, and monitor specific expectations to schools for managing organizational structures and learning experiences for English learners at elementary, middle, and high school levels.

 

4. Ensure that the division’s professional development plan includes opportunities to increase school staff’s knowledge and skills in using time, space, and personnel more effectively for English learners as well as the provision of the set of three learning experiences for students.

 

5. Provide all high schools with the resources to deliver English learner services consistent with the original pilot and monitor the use of resources and impacts at all schools.
