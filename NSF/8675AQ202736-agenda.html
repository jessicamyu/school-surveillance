Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Other Staff Present: Denise James, director, Office of Facilities Planning

Meeting Category: April 29, 2010 - Work Session No. 112

Subject: Annandale Regional Study

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Key Points:

Annandale High School remains the most overcrowded high school, both in the region and in the county, with a current building utilization of 120%, and which is projected to go up to 127% by the 2014-15 school year. Poe Middle School is also very overcrowded with a projected out-year building utilization of 132%. The small boundary adjustment in 2009, which re-assigned areas along the southwestern boundaries of Annandale High School and an attendance island to Falls Church High and Lake Braddock Secondary Schools, is scheduled to be implemented this fall (2010-11 school year). At that time it was also recognized that a much more far reaching and in-depth study would be required in order to achieve the best possible outcome for students through more balanced enrollments and efficient student accommodation planning to meet the needs for existing and future students in the region.

Surrounding Annandale High School are several elementary schools which are also among the most crowded in both the region and the county. Annandale Terrace, Beech Tree, Woodburn, and Westlawn Elementary Schools are all projected to have building utilizations that range from 103-149% by the 2014-15 school year. The construction and opening of a new elementary school at the Lacey site will provide relief to some of the schools in the area. Also, decisions must be made regarding whether the new elementary should open as a K-5 or a K-6 school, which will in turn impact the potential feeder middle and high school assignments.

Options:

Since the issues here are two fold, middle and high school overcrowding and establishing an attendance area for a new elementary school, the School Board is asked to consider two ad hoc committees to review the issues and explore all possible solutions. It is understood that liaison between the two ad hoc study committees will be required.

Recommendation:

That the School Board initiate the ad hoc community engagement process and authorize establishment of two Annandale Regional Planning Study Committees to review the issues and explore all possible solutions, including pros and cons, to 1) relieve overcrowding at Annandale High and Poe Middle Schools and 2) review attendance area options for a new elementary school at the Lacey site.

    Meeting Category: April 29, 2010 - Work Session No.112

    Subject: Facilities Planning Advisory Council

    School Board Action Required: Discussion (Decision at a future meeting)

    Key Points:

        On April 5, 2010, the Comprehensive Planning Development Committee (CPDC) presented a recommendation for the establishment of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC). The recommended proposal outlines the organization and function of a Board appointed citizen council, including mission, objectives, function and operations, work products and plans, communications flow, community engagement, application criteria and process for council selection.

        As a follow-up to discussion from the April 5 work session, Attachment A contains the proposal for a Facilities Planning Advisory Council with revisions highlighted for ease of reference; Attachment B addresses the multiple points of discussion and clarifications raised by the Board.

        If accepted, the FPAC would become a standing committee of the School Board, subject to the guidelines contained in School Board Policy 1710.11, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board. It is further noted that modifications to Policy 1710.11 may be required to accommodate the unique features of the proposed advisory council; the modifications to the policy will be brought forward for Board review and approval pending the outcome of the Board’s action on the FPAC proposal.

        As noted above, a decision on establishing the FPAC will be made at a future Board meeting.
    Options:
        Since we have never conducted such a process before, the School Board is asked to provide its thoughts about the proposal.

    Recommendation:
        That the School Board approve the establishment of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council, as proposed by the Comprehensive Planning Development Committee and as detailed in the agenda item.
