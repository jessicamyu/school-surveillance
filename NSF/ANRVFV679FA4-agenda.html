Subject:  State Standards of Accreditation

 

Staff Contact:  Francisco Durán, chief academic and equity officer

 

Other Staff Present:  Bettrys Huffman, director, Office of Student Testing

 

Meeting Category:  April 16, 2018 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Ignite Link:  Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Problem Statement:

 

Revisions to the Virginia Standards of Accreditation (SOAs) will have substantial implications for how schools monitor and communicate their success within their communities.

 

Outcome:

 

Understand changes to the state accreditation system, graduation requirements, and testing expectations. Recognize connections to existing division priorities as groundwork for communication across the FCPS community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Virginia Board of Education adopted substantial revisions to the SOAs in fall of 2017, to be fully implemented by school year 2018-19. Schools’ 2018-19 school accreditation status, which is based on 2017-18 performance outcomes, will continue to be calculated under the current system, unless the revised system is more beneficial to the school. All schools will be assigned 2018-19 accreditation designations using terminology from the new SOA system.

 

New accreditation metrics introduce accountability for achievement gaps and chronic absenteeism as well as new ways to measure high school success. Overall, the revised system offers both a more flexible approach to reaching performance targets and a more comprehensive look at schools’ performance across both academic and non-academic indicators. Example school reports produced by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) demonstrate that schools’ performance status may differ from historical trends under the current accreditation system. This offers both an opportunity to offer additional monitoring and support to schools showing new areas of need as well as an opportunity to engage in different types of equity dialogues within school communities. 

 

In addition to accreditation changes, the revised SOAs bring numerous changes in graduation and testing requirements for high school students. Incoming ninth grade students in 2018-19 will need fewer verified credits for graduation, which will necessitate less Standards of Learning (SOL) testing. The revised SOAs also establish expectations and opportunities for expanded workplace learning, advanced coursework, and performance-based assessments. As they roll out, these changes promise to be positive for FCPS students and support division goals toward equity.

 

Existing priorities and structures within FCPS position the division for efficient and effective implementation of the revised SOA provisions. Professional development and resources currently underway are aimed at providing school leaders with the understandings, data, and tools they need to support their staff and communities in embracing these new state accountability standards as a means to attain and maintain equity within and across Fairfax County.

Subject: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Staff Contact: Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Other Staff Present: Francisco Durán, chief academic and equity officer

 

Meeting Category: April 16, 2018 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

As outlined in the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success, FCPS is committed to reaching, challenging, and preparing every student for success in school and life. This report highlights successes as well as important challenges facing the school system, including continued gaps in opportunity, access, and achievement among student sub-groups.

The report format includes separate chapters for each of the four strategies under Goal 1. Each chapter includes a strategy introduction, the related desired outcomes, student achievement data, and analysis to highlight the associated successes and challenges. The report’s appendix includes additional details on the division’s student achievement results.

 

Overarching Strategy 1: Enhance instructional practices to ensure that all students receive an education in a dynamic environment designed to foster life-long learning and support them in achieving their full potential.

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • The curriculum will reflect POG outcomes in all content areas, where students are appropriately challenged

  • Achievement gaps will be eliminated

  • Teachers, students, and parents will have access to contemporary and effective technology resources

  • All students will demonstrate digital citizenship skills

  • Centralized Support will be available for schools and school staff based on student achievement needs

     

Highlights

  • Gains in reading and mathematics SOL pass rates across all subgroups over multi-year timeframe

  • Increase in number of English Learners earning high school credits

  • Increase in available student, teacher, and parent resources to support Digital Citizenship

  • Increase in number of FCPS schools that are fully accredited

     

    Challenges

  • Closing gaps in opportunity, access, and achievement among student sub-groups

  • Providing equitable access to technology

     

    Overarching Strategy 2: All students will achieve their full potential through the use of assessment and data systems for decision making that support student attainment of the outcomes defined by the Portrait of a Graduate

     

    Desired Outcomes:

     

  • A fully-balanced assessment system will reflect POG outcomes in all content areas

  • Data tools to monitor progress

  • Grading will be an accurate reflection of learning

Highlights

  • Increased use of performance-based assessment for measuring content knowledge and Portrait of a Graduate skills

  • Expanded pilot of Capstone Experiences

  • Increased availability of data tools to teachers and school-based administrators

Challenges

  • Need for state policy flexibility for use of performance-based assessment to meet state accountability requirements

  • Supporting teacher data literacy skills to enhance data-based decision-making practices

 

Overarching Strategy 3: Provide quality Early Childhood experiences aligned with Portrait of a Graduate outcomes that are designed to prepare students to successfully enter Kindergarten.

 

Desired Outcomes:

 

  • All qualifying future FCPS students will have access to high quality early education programs

  • All agencies will coordinate their efforts to provide similar early childhood experiences

Highlights

  • Students that participate in PreK experiences out-perform students that do not have a PreK experience

  • Increased PreK opportunities for preschoolers and infants and toddlers in FCPS and County

  • Increased access to professional learning for early childhood community educators

  • Development of the Fairfax County’s Equitable School Readiness Strategic Plan

Challenges

  • Limited number of PreK opportunities for qualifying students

  • Neediest communities have higher numbers of students entering without a PreK experience

 

Overarching Strategy 4: Provide students with relevant opportunities to explore options and prepare for College and Career

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • A portfolio of school options will provide multiple pathways to a high school diploma

  • Students will be fluent in two or more languages

Highlights

  • Expansion of high school pathways that include coursework leading to in demand career preparation

  • Increased dual enrollment offerings

  • Increased “resume ready” credentials

  • Increased work-based learning experiences

Challenges

  • Division accountability systems for college and career indicators

  • Expansion of work-based learning opportunities

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success report as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:  Update on Options for Teachers on the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes, Master’s Cohort Program, and Tuition Reimbursement

 

 

Staff Contact:   Michael Draeger, director, Office of Business Services, Department of Human Resources

                          R Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Kathleen Walts, executive director, Office of Professional Learning and Family Engagement

 

 

Meeting Category:  April 16, 2018 –Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 3: Premier Workforce

 

 

Problem Statement (If applicable): 

The new teacher salary scale was designed to provide teachers a salary that is market competitive, which is defined as career earnings between 95 to 105 percent of the market average. While the majority of lanes on the former scale were below market, FCPS was above market for the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes. To maintain a market competitive scale, the new teacher scale capped the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes at 19 steps. This limited the career earnings of teachers on the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes.

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

For the FY 2018 school year, as part of the FY 2017 Final Budget Review in July, the school board approved a one-year monetary step increase for all teachers at step 19 and above on the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes. 

 

In November 2017, FCPS recommended a program to provide a 3% bonus to teachers for 3 years on the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes from Step 19 up to the last step on the FY 2017 teacher salary scale. In addition, a tuition-sharing Master's Cohort Program was established to provide teachers on the top steps of the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes an opportunity to earn a Master’s Degree in as little as two years. As part of this program, FCPS also reinstated tuition reimbursement, which is available to all employees, with priority provided to teachers on the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes at Step 19 and higher.

 

As part of the FY 2018 Midyear Budget Review, the Board set-aside $1.1 million to address options for teachers on the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes at Step 19 and higher and allocated $0.3 million for the Master's Cohort Program and tuition reimbursement to begin in the second half of FY 2018. In order to mitigate the impact of the new scales on teachers at the top of the Bachelor’s Salary Lanes, the School Board requested costing information for additional options.

 

Today’s work session will include the cost of the options as well as the current state of the Master's Cohort and tuition reimbursement programs.

 

 

Recommendation: 

School Board consensus on the plan to mitigate the impact of the new scales for teachers on the top of the bachelor’s salary lanes.
