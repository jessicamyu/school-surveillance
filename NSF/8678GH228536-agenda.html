RESOLUTION
NAMING A GARDEN AT
STUART HIGH SCHOOL
“THE NANCY SVENDSEN MEMORIAL GARDEN”

WHEREAS, Nancy Svendsen served Stuart High School for 16 years, until her death in October 2007, as a teacher of English for speakers of other languages (ESOL); and

WHEREAS, she was a primary developer of a science curriculum for ESOL students to prepare them for biology classes; and

WHEREAS, she helped train other teachers in Fairfax County Public Schools every year with in-service meetings to familiarize them with the materials and strategies for Concepts Science; and

WHEREAS, she mentored many students and beginning teachers tirelessly, tutoring after school and during the summer, welcoming visitors into her classes to observe her expertise, and providing material support to needy students and families; and

WHEREAS, she participated in the Stuart community for many years, watching her son attend and graduate from Stuart and participating in parent support groups even before becoming a member of the faculty;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the garden in the right front corner of Stuart High School, “The Nancy Svendsen Memorial Garden” in recognition of Mrs. Svendsen’s years of dedication and service to the students, families, and faculty of Stuart High School.

WHEREAS, his high expectations inspired those he met, and he has earned the respect and gratitude of the students, parents, faculty, and extended community of McLean High School, and his passion for learning, for music, and for living inspired his students to incorporate a love for the same into their own lives;

RESOLUTION HONORING
BETSY GOODMAN
WHEREAS, Betsy Goodman served Fairfax County Public Schools for 37 years with dedication and distinction as a science teacher, a curriculum specialist, an assistant principal, a science curriculum coordinator, an associate principal, a high school principal, a director of high school instruction and K-12 curriculum services, and the Cluster VIII assistant superintendent, where she effectively, efficiently, and energetically represented and served the interests of her students, schools, and community; and

WHEREAS, she proactively reached out to lead in multiple directions, earning awards for meeting the needs of students with diverse backgrounds, of students who were struggling, and of students who needed challenges, and she consistently searched for the best and most promising practices, sharing them with colleagues in the county, state, and nation; and

WHEREAS, in her eight years as principal at Marshall High School, Dr. Goodman initiated block scheduling, the International Baccalaureate program, and the Academy for International Studies and Business. In addition, she established a computer lab, raised awareness of safety and security, implemented after-school tutorial programs, enriched gifted and talented and upper level courses, and strengthened athletic programs, all of which resulted in attracting more students to the school. She worked with dedication to help the faculty increase achievement and pride in their school; and

WHEREAS, as Cluster VIII assistant superintendent, Dr. Goodman expertly fulfilled many roles, including overseeing the selection of thirty-four principals, six of whom were nominated for Principal of the Year and three of whom won the award, and seven of whom were nominated for First-Year Principal of the Year and two of whom won the award; and

WHEREAS, Dr. Goodman is a model of integrity, compassion, and genuineness and she exhibits these qualities in her daily interactions with all those she encounters, especially the elementary student she mentors. She will be remembered as an educator who was fair, informed, and in touch with all her constituents. Dr. Goodman stands by her name, good in all ways that have benefited students in Fairfax County Public Schools;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County Public School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Betsy Goodman for her inspirational leadership and outstanding service to Fairfax County Public Schools and for her unwavering dedication to children and learning.

HONORING
AUDRA SYDNOR


WHEREAS, Audra Sydnor served Fairfax County Public Schools for 27 years with dedication and distinction as a teacher at Edison High School and Lake Braddock Secondary School, as a minority student achievement monitor, as an English and ESOL curriculum specialist, as the coordinator for secondary programs with Area IV, as an assistant principal at Robinson Secondary School, as a principal at Lanier and Liberty Middle Schools, and, most recently, as the Cluster I assistant superintendent, where she graciously served students, families, and staff members and effectively represented their interests; and

WHEREAS, she made significant contributions to ensure high quality education for all students through her spirited leadership, caring manner, uncompromising integrity, and strong support and encouragement of teachers and administrators to continuously improve instructional programs and services to meet the needs of all students; and

WHEREAS, Audra’s extraordinary leadership and creative ability helped teachers, administrators, and educational personnel to build strong pyramidwide relationships, to strive for excellence in the classroom, to focus on the power of professional learning communities, to view the school improvement plan as a living document that serves as a catalyst for improving student achievement, and to become familiar with a broad range of instructional best practices based on research; and

WHEREAS, she fostered links of understanding as she worked closely with all parents, especially those actively involved with the Cluster I parent advisory council, to make decisions that were in the best interests of students—always listening carefully, judging fairly, and acting honestly;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Audra Sydnor for her inspirational leadership and outstanding service to Fairfax County Public Schools and to the students and for her unwavering dedication to the children of this community.

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Category: June 25, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 22
Subject: Leasing of Administrative Office Space

School Board Action Required: Decision

Related To: N/A

Key Points:

There is a need to lease commercial office space to accomplish all or part of the following three primary objectives:
    • Leases are expiring at City Square, Democracy Lane, and the Brown Building. None of these locations are consistent with the current needs of the school system either in terms of proximity to other FCPS staff or to delivering services to parents and community members.
    • Vacating the Devonshire and Lacey administrative centers so as to allow the return of these two sites to use as elementary schools. Funds are included in the FCPS capital improvement program for these two sites to be rebuilt as elementary schools.
    • Consolidate each of the departments of instructional services, student services, professional learning and accountability, and communications and community outreach. While it won’t be possible for fiscal reasons to complete the co-location and consolidation of these four departments, there is the opportunity to make significant progress depending upon the results of final lease negotiations and the determination of which building space(s) will be successfully leased.

There is recognition of the school system’s ongoing budget challenges that might require that we phase in this leasing initiative over a period of time but this must be balanced against favorable lease rates available in today’s market and against the need to allow these departments to locate staff with adjacencies that enhance effective and efficient operations.

Funds that are currently used to pay for these expiring leases will be reutilized to offset costs with any new commercial leases. There also will be operating savings associated with vacating Lacey and Devonshire that can be applied to commercial leases.

Recommendation:

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Chief Operating Officer, (i) to enter into one or more office lease agreements, on terms negotiated by school system officials and at rates within the school system’s budget, and (ii) to execute such lease agreements and related documents, all on behalf of the School Board, in order to house certain FCPS staff and administrative office programs.
