Staff Contact:         Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  May 13, 2013 – Work Session No. 113

 

Subject:                 Renovation Queue Criteria

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

At the July 16, 2012, work session the Board discussed the current process used for the Renovation Queue Criteria.  As a result of the discussion, the Board requested that the Facilities staff develop a community engagement process and ultimately present a recommendation to the School Board regarding the weighting assigned to the criterion based upon the public engagement feedback.

 

The community meeting to conduct a public review of the Renovation Queue Criteria was held on November 27, 2012.  Notice of the meeting was scheduled with the assistance of the Fairfax County Council of PTAs.  All PTA members were invited to participate in the public meeting.  Members of the School Board’s Facilities & Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) attended the meeting along with the PTA participants.  The purpose of the review and comment by the public was to provide guidance to the Board in evaluating the validation of the criteria in three specific areas:

  1. Do the criteria make sense and are logical?
  2. What changes if any should be made to the criteria?
  3. Are there other criteria which should be added?

During the meeting introduction, the participants were provided with background pertaining to the history of the FCPS Renovation Program and the first Renovation Queue in 1988 (See attachment A).  The meeting facilitator presented descriptions of the following criterion which currently form the basis of the Renovation Queue Ranking and Evaluation:

  1. Fundamental Educational Requirements – weighted at 40%
  2. Facility Condition Assessment – weighted at 30%
  3. Supplemental Educational Requirements – weighted at 10%
  4. B – County Council of PTA Meeting Summary

Staff Contact:         Elizabeth Schultz, Springfield District Representative

  

Meeting Category:  May 13, 2013 – Work Session No. 113

 

Subject:                 Capital Needs Task Force

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points: 

 

At the February 7, 2013, forum, there was consensus for the Board to schedule a work session (after the March 12 Board of Supervisors budget meeting) to further discuss Ms. Schultz’s advocacy to create a Capital Needs Task Force. 

 

Advocacy: Create a Capital Needs Task Force among Fairfax County Economic Development Authority, School Board and LT, and Fairfax County Chamber of Commerce to evaluate and provide suggestions for addressing the capital needs of FCPS.

 

The objective is to:

 

1. Engage the business community and Economic Development Authority in a needs assessment evaluation;

2. Develop the capital needs continuum;

3. Identify alternate revenue streams; and

4. Determine what other aspects of capital needs and funding need to be addressed in the conversation.
