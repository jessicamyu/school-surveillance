U. S. Constitution Day and Citizenship Day - September 17, 2012

 

Constitution Day and Citizenship Day are observed each year on September 17 to commemorate the signing of the Constitution on September 17, 1787, and “recognize all who, by coming of age or by naturalization, have become citizens.”  Recent legislation has renamed the day as Constitution Day and Citizenship Day and urges proper observance of the day. This year, the official observance day is Monday, September 17, and is the 225th anniversary of the nation’s founding document.

 

In observance of Constitution Day and Citizenship Day, all schools in FCPS will be observing this day with activities that recognize the adoption of the United States Constitution and those who have become citizens under the Constitution.  Schools will use a variety of curriculum resources to teach students about the importance of the Constitution and responsible citizenship.  This may relate to the School Board’s Student Achievement Goal 3. Demonstrate Responsibility to the Community and the World.  Throughout the school year, high school students will have an opportunity to participate in service learning projects related to responsible citizenship, which can lead to a Civics Seal on their diploma.

 

 

National Hispanic Heritage Month - September 15-October 15, 2012

 

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-American community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.  This year’s theme is “Many Backgrounds, Many Stories…One American Spirit.”

 

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

 

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

Contact:                  Jane Strauss, Audit Committee Chairman and Dranesville District Representative

 

Meeting Category:  September 6, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 3

 

Subject:                  School Board Audit Committee Membership

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Key Points: 

 

At the March 29, 2012, forum work session, there was a recommendation that the Board consider adding another School Board member as a member of the Audit Committee, thereby increasing the current membership from two Board members to three Board members.  (At that time, Janie Strauss served as chairman of the committee, and Pat Hynes served as the second Board member.)   There was consensus at the forum to refer the recommendation to the Governance Committee.

 

At the April 16 Governance Committee work session, the recommendation was discussed.  The members of the Governance Committee agreed to support the recommendation that the Strategic Governance Manual be revised to reflect an increase in the number of Board members on the Audit Committee from two members to three members.  During the all-day work sessions on April 16, the Governance Committee chairman presented the recommendation to the full Board to increase the Audit Committee membership from two to three members, and there was consensus to schedule the issue for new business on April 26 and action on May 10.

 

At the May 10 Board meeting, after discussion of the main motion (and a proposed amendment that was not successful), the Board approved postponing the main motion until the first School Board meeting in September 2012 (which is scheduled for September 6, 2012) to allow for further discussion.  The Board discussed the topic of the committee membership at the July 16 Audit Committee work session, and there was consensus that a third Board member be added to the committee.  There was also consensus that the Superintendent and the Deputy Superintendent would be non-voting members (see proposed amendment below).  (The Chief Financial Officer, who is a member of the committee, and the School Board Auditor, who is the staff liaison to the committee, are non-voting members.)

 

In accordance with the motion to postpone that was approved by the Board on May 10, the recommendation to change the Audit Committee membership from two Board members to three will be scheduled for action on September 6, 2012.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve increasing the Audit Committee membership from two to three School Board members, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

 

Proposed amendment:

 

I move to amend the main motion by designating the Superintendent and Deputy Superintendent as non-voting members of the Audit Committee. (Jane Strauss)
