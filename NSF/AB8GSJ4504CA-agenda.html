Subject:  FY 2018 Budget Planning

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                    

 

 

Key Points:

 

To begin the FY 2018 budget planning process, the fiscal forecast for the School Operating Fund was presented to the School Board on September 19, 2016, and at a joint School Board/Board of Supervisors on October 4, 2016. The forecast included projections for key financial areas that drive the budget development process, such as revenue assumptions and expenditure assumptions, including employee salaries and benefits, student membership, and other strategic plan investments.   

 

A discussion of the fiscal forecast, including revenue and expenditure projections, begins the process of providing input regarding considerations for the FY 2018 proposed budget development. The School Board stated their commitment for how the potential additional revenue from a meals tax would be used. 

 

In this work session, three scenarios will be presented that take into account various revenue scenarios and potential expenditure adjustments.

Meeting Category:  October 24, 2016 – Work Session        

 

Subject:  Youth Suicide Review Team – 2016 Annual Report of Findings and Recommendations     

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                              

 

Key Points:

 

The Youth Suicide Review Team (YSRT) reviewed 15 cases to identify systems, policy, and practice changes to inform suicide prevention efforts and to identify trends in suicide and common risk factors for youth suicide in Fairfax County that can inform and improve efforts related to suicide prevention. Common risk factors along with recommendations for families, schools and communities were identified.

 

Most commonly occurring risk factors included, previously expressed thoughts of suicide or death; a diagnosis of depression; private provider treatment; and untreated (or potentially harmful treatment of) mental illness.

 

Please see executive summary for recommendations.

Meeting Category: October 24, 2016 – Work Session

 

Subject: Project Momentum Update

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To: Strategic Plan, Goal 1: Student Success                

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS will provide differentiated tiers of support for all schools to raise the bar and close gaps in student achievement.

 

Update on the status of Project Momentum Schools.  Intensive and Targeted schools will be reviewed along with supports that were in place for the 2015-2016 school year.

 

Included will be data showing the positive growth of schools in Project Momentum along with additional supports provided for the 2016-2017 school year.

 

Project Momentum was initiated in 2014-2015 taking lessons learned from FCPS and National school improvement initiatives and compromising them into a unified and target approach for our schools most at risk of VDOE State Accreditation.  Project Momentum narrows the scope and focus to create a sustainable research-based improvement strategy; an accelerated school improvement timeline; dedicated school support team resource and differentiated staffing; and, specific expected outcomes for each school.

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Key Points: 

 

There are a number of new developments and rezoning projects which are part of revisioning initiatives across Fairfax County, some of which may impact enrollment within Fairfax County Public Schools. It is important to note that many of these projects will take many years until the development is actually completed.                                     

 

Discussion:

 

Both Fairfax County staff and Fairfax County Public Schools staff discuss and collaborate on rezoning applications which may affect our facilities primarily in regard to student yields and their corresponding costs throughout the entire zoning process.  Staff from both the County and FCPS will be discussing significant developments within the County and how we work together to ensure FCPS is well positioned to receive any student impact.
