Ignite Link: Caring Culture

 

Problem Statement: Regulation 2601, Student Rights and Responsibilities annual review

 

Outcome: Review proposed changes to Regulation 2601 and receive School Board feedback

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): Executive Summary

The presentation of the Student Rights and Responsibilities (SR&R), Regulation 2601, provides an overview of revisions to the SR&R based on stakeholder feedback and legislative updates, as applicable. The proposed 2022-23 SR&R is aligned with the Virginia Board of Education Model Guidance for Positive and Preventative Code of Student Conduct Policy and Alternatives to Suspension and is being reviewed as a part of the annual process established by the School Board.

 

The following changes were made to the proposed 2022-23 SR&R:

 

  • Clarified expectations regarding acceptable use of technology;
  • Clarified expectations regarding alcohol, tobacco, and other drugs;
  • Clarified processes outlined in the SR&R to foster consistent implementation of the discipline process;
  • Adapted processes, systems, and expectations to meet the needs of our students and families;
  • Clarified language in the SR&R regarding distribution of the regulation and stakeholder orientation; and
  • Revised the behavior charts and narratives to clarify alignment with the state codes.

 

Recommendation:  The administration recommends that the Fairfax County School Board adopt Regulation 2601.36P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed, at the May 12, 2022 School Board Meeting.

Subject:  Elementary School Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee

 

Staff Contact:  Noel Klimenko, assistant superintendent, Instructional   Services Department (ISD)                                                                   

 

Other Staff Present: Colleen Eddy, director, PreK-12 Curriculum and    Instruction, ISD

Donna McConnell, coordinator, Elementary Language   Arts, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

                                                                  

Meeting Category:  April 26, 2022- Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Presentation

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS previously purchased basal resources for elementary language arts in 2020. Schools are using school-curated texts and the core units and lessons developed by FCPS as well as print and digital works available through various sources. To provide equitable resources for students in elementary Language Arts, Instructional Services began the process for new basal instructional materials as defined in FCPS Regulation 3004 Adoption Basal Instructional Materials in 2020.

 

A timeline of the process for the Language Arts basal resource review can be found in attachment A.  In October 2021, the School Board approved the committee members to review and recommend basal instructional materials for elementary Language Arts curriculum. The Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee convened 6 times between February 9, 2022, and March 9, 2022, to review resources for Language Arts that were submitted by vendors in response to an RFP for consideration as basal instructional materials for elementary Language Arts as well as to review the over 300 community review submissions.

 

During the last months of the adoption process, the Virginia legislature approved the Virginia Literacy Act, which requires evidence-based literacy instruction be implemented in all early learning classrooms, including new and amended requirements for instruction and intervention programs.  Staff has been notified that the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) will provide a list of approved resources in the coming months. In order to ensure compliance and maximize FCPS investment, the recommendations before the School Board (Attachment B) are a subset of the recommendations of the Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee (Attachment C).

 

In accordance with the Code of Virginia, regulations of the Virginia Board of Education, and Fairfax County Public Schools regulations, the titles listed in Attachment B are recommended for adoption at the division level in conjunction with the Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee, the Selection Advisory Committee, and the Technical Advisory Committee.   Additionally, a summary of the committee rationales for the recommended materials and community input received are provided in attachments C and D.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the titles listed for elementary Language Arts as recommended by Staff.
