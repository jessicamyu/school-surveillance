    SUBJECT:     Proposed 2008-2009 Program/Attendance Area Adjustment

    TYPE:     Discussion

    BACKGROUND:

            In July 2007, the School Board authorized and directed staff to undertake two boundary studies: 1) Springfield Estates/Forestdale Elementary Schools and, 2) the West County High School study which includes Chantilly, Herndon, Oakton, South Lakes, and Westfield High Schools. Also included in the study is the Madison High School attendance area island north of the Dulles Access Road and its feeder schools which impact Hughes and Thoreau Middle Schools and Sunrise Valley and Wolftrap Elementary Schools.

    KEY POINTS:

            The Springfield Estates/Forestdale Elementary School Study implements the return of an attendance island (Springfield Crossing Apartments) that had been administratively assigned to Springfield Estates, to its base school Forestdale Elementary.

            The West County Boundary Study addresses programmatic issues related to low and high enrollment schools by adjusting enrollments between South Lakes High School, which is under-enrolled, and Chantilly and Westfield High Schools which have high enrollment.
    RECOMMENDATION:

            1. That the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for Springfield Estates Elementary School and Forestdale Elementary School as shown in Appendix A.

            2. That the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for South Lakes, Oakton, Chantilly, Madison and Westfield High Schools as shown in Appendix B.

            3. That the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for Thoreau and Hughes Middle Schools and Wolftrap and Sunrise Valley Elementary Schools as shown in Appendix B.

    BOUNDARY ADJUSTMENT RECOMMENDATIONS

    In October 2002, the School Board adopted revised Policy 8130.5 governing the adjustment of special program center locations and school/program attendance areas. The policy requires that, under most circumstances, conditions necessitating program location or attendance area adjustments be evaluated jointly with the affected communities. Solutions emerging from the “Community Boundary Town Meeting” process are to be recommended to the School Board for public hearing and action. The recommendations contained in this agenda item reflect input from a series of community town meetings conducted to review adjustments to attendance areas of existing schools. Meetings were announced via press release, mail outs, “backpack” and “Keep in Touch” notices to parents, and web-site postings. Citizen comment from each meeting was captured in summaries posted to the FCPS web page after each meeting. Scenario details (maps and impact tables) were similarly posted.

    The following section summarizes the reviews undertaken with affected communities and resulting boundary change recommendations. Detailed outlines of these recommendations describing the geographic areas and numbers of projected students are presented in Appendices A and B.

    GENERAL EDUCATION SCHOOL ATTENDANCE AREA REALIGNMENTS

    Appendix A Boundary adjustments between Springfield Estates and Forestdale Elementary Schools
    Realign attendance areas to relieve overcrowding at Springfield Estates Elementary School. The study area included the Springfield Estates and Forestdale Elementary School attendance areas.

    Appendix B Boundary adjustments between Chantilly, Madison, Westfield, Oakton and South Lakes High Schools

    Realign attendance areas to relieve overcrowding and program issues at Chantilly, Madison, Oakton, Westfield and South Lakes High Schools. The study area included Chantilly, Herndon, Oakton, Westfield and South Lakes High Schools and a portion of the Madison High School attendance area.

    Appendix B Boundary Adjustments between Hughes/Thoreau Middle Schools and Sunrise Valley/Wolftrap Elementary Schools
    Realign attendance areas to relieve overcrowding at Thoreau, reduce time and distance traveled and correspond with recommended adjustments at the high school level.

    SUBJECT: School Board Technology Support, FOIA, and Electronic Records Retention

    TYPE: Discussion

    BACKGROUND:
        The attached presentation reviews technology support for School board members, appropriate use of FCPS network and Internet resources, and records retention of School Board member e-mails. The latter topic also addresses high level information regarding access to records via the Freedom of Information Act (FOIA).
    KEY POINTS:
        · School Board members can obtain technology support through the IT Service Desk. IT will work to resolve Board member requests within published service levels. Residential support will be scheduled during business hours, as needed.
        · Regulation 6410 specifies requirements for appropriate use of FCPS network resources. The FCPS Acceptable Use Policy applies to all users of these resources.
        · The Virginia Public Records Act applies equally to paper or electronic records, including e-mail.
        · FOIA applies to public records regardless of format. E-mails are accessible through FOIA except when specific exemptions apply. These exemptions may not apply during discovery for litigation.
        · A technology device is now being used to automatically capture electronic correspondence sent to and from School Board member FCPS e-mail boxes.
