Staff Contact:  Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:  Susan Quinn, Chief Operating Officer

                                  Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Meeting Category:  April 20, 2017 – Work Session

 

Subject:  Non-teacher Compensation Scales

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:  Goal 3 – Premier Workforce

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS has hired Segal Group, a benefits, compensation, and human resources consulting firm, to identify a compensation structure that attracts and retains qualified employees through an optimal mix of salary and benefits. The study is a key piece of the Strategic Plan’s goal 3 – Premier Workforce – which aims to recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts, and ensure that FCPS maintains a competitive compensation package. To date, proposed scales have been presented for teachers, school based administrators, and classroom instructional support positions including public health training assistants, public health attendants, and instructional assistants. Public health attendants and public health training assistants had previously been paid on the Unified Scale.

 

In this meeting, recommendations for a separate scale for contracted hourly positions, to include bus attendants, bus drivers, and select food service workers will be presented. Additional information on the status of a newly designed Unified Scale for the remainder of employees will be shared. Timelines and tasks for a classification review of all Unified Scale positions will be included.

Subject:  Parent Liaisons

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:  FY18 Advertised Budget

 

Key Points:

 

As requested at the February 9, 2017 School Board meeting, a menu of options for parent liaison compensation has been developed and will be presented.

 

The options include:

  1. Keeping the current pay framework.
  2. Providing step increases.
  3. Providing step increases and health benefits.
  4. Providing full contracted benefits: step increases, health benefits, and other benefits (such as retirement, life insurance, leave plans, and flexible spending accounts).
