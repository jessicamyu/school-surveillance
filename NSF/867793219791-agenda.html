Veterans Day - November 11, 2009


Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict. 
   
At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended.  This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, closing of places of business.  All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world has never before witnessed such rejoicing.

Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U.S. armed forces.

I would like to remind everyone that every Fairfax County school is required to plan an activity honoring our veterans.

American Education Week - November 15-21, 2009


    The National Education Association's (NEA) 88th annual American Education Week spotlights the importance of providing every child in America with a quality public education from kindergrten through college, and the need for everyone to do his or her part in making public schools great.
    The NEA's annual tagline, Great Public Schools: A Basic Right and Our Responsibility, reflects the Association's calling upon America to provide students with quality public schools so that they can grow, prosper, and achieve in the 21st century.
National Adoption Awareness Month - November

    The purpose of National Adoption Month is to raise awareness about the over 129,000 children in foster care nationwide waiting for permanent families. Recruitment efforts are highlighted throughout the National Adoption Month web site to encourage America's families to "answer the call" to ensure the safety, permanency, and well-being of our children.

      There are many ways to form a family, and more than 8,000 families in Fairfax County have chosen adoption as the way to form their own family.  Fairfax County Public Schools recognizes the importance of adoption, and the School Board, leadership team, teachers, principals, and staff fully support Adoption Awareness Month.

School Psychology Awareness Week - November 9-13, 2009

The theme of this year's School Psychology Awareness Week, "See the possibilities in you. We do!" focuses on shining a light on the positive work school psychologists do to promote the endless possibilities for academic and personal success in the lives of the students they serve.

School psychologists work with students in early childhood and elementary and secondary schools. They collaborate with teachers, parents, and school personnel to create safe, healthy, and supportive learing environments for all students. School psychologists address students' learning and behavioral problems, suggest improvements to classroom management strategies or parenting techniques, and evaluate students with disabilities and gifted and talented students to held determine the best way to educate them.

They improve teaching, learning, and socialization strategies based on their understanding of the psychology of learning environments. They also may evaluate the effectiveness of academic programs, prevention programs, behavior management procedures, and other services provided in the school setting.

National Career Development Month - November 2009

Career center specialists, school counselors, and educators are employed to help students reach their full potential and successfully transition from school to further education or work and they are actively committed to helping students explore their abilities, strengths, interests, and talents as these traits relate to career awareness and development and life long planning.

Meeting Category: November 5, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 7
Subject: Secondary Course Offerings

School Board Action Required: Decision

Related To: Student Achievement Goal 1.

Key Points:
      The following courses were recommended by the Course Review Committee as additions to the list of high school standard course offerings for the 2010-11 school year:

      American Sign Language 4
      Background:
      The addition of this course will provide students the opportunity to continue developing their proficiency in ASL, thereby opening career opportunities and greater understanding of Deaf culture. There are no AP options in American Sign Language.

      Computer Graphics 4: Portfolio Preparation
      Background:
      The addition of this course will provide students with the opportunity to complete a four-year sequence in Computer Graphics and begin portfolio preparation. Currently, schools are providing this opportunity but through inconsistent course coding.

      Engineering Mathematics
      Background:
      Offered as a School-Site course SY 2009-2010, Engineering Mathematics prepares students to successfully advance through the freshman calculus sequence thereby reducing attrition rates in college engineering programs.

      Geographic Information Systems 1
      Background:
      The addition of this course will increase students’ awareness of and exposure to the emerging field of geotechnology, both as an area of study and as a career path.

      Geosystems Honors
      Background:
      Currently Geosystems has only one standard offering and is the only science course that does not have an honors equivalent. The Geosystems course may lose enrollments in favor of other weighted science options.

      Photography 4: Portfolio Preparation
      Background:
      The addition of this course will provide students with the opportunity to complete a four-year sequence in Photography and begin portfolio preparation. Currently, schools are providing this opportunity but through inconsistent course coding.


      Standard and optional courses for high schools and middle schools are reviewed each year, and new proposals are submitted to the Course Review Committee for review and recommendations to the Superintendent and School Board. This process is outlined in Regulation 3202.7, Approval of New Courses and Deletion and Consolidation of Existing Courses.
Recommendation:
      That the School Board approve changes to the list of middle and high school course offerings for the 2010-2011 school year, as recommended by the Course review Committee and as detailed in the agenda item.

Category

Information



Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

Other Staff Present: Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

Meeting Category: November 5, 2009 – Regular Meeting No. 7
Subject: FY 2010 Midyear Budget Review

School Board Action Required: Information

Key Points:
      The FY 2010 beginning balance decreased $1.3 million due to audit adjustments made after the FY 2009 Final Budget Review, including a revenue reduction of $0.8 million in sales tax revenue based on actual sales tax receipts as well as other audit adjustments of $0.5 million.

      The FY 2010 sales tax estimate is reduced by $11.2 million based on the Governor’s midyear budget reductions but is offset by an increase of $8.7 million in other state revenue accounts. In recognition of the state’s revenue shortfall, projections in the FY 2010 Approved Budget were conservative and mitigated the impact of the Governor’s reductions. The Governor’s midyear reduction plan also included an acceleration of spending the State Fiscal Stabilization Funds provided through the federal American Recovery and Reinvestment Act, which will reduce funds available to school divisions in FY 2011. In addition to state revenue adjustments, a net increase of $0.3 million in federal grants and entitlements is recognized, resulting in an overall revenue decrease of $3.5 million in the operating fund.
      To address the revenue shortfall, the School Board’s flexibility reserve will be reduced by $3.8 million. As part of the Third Quarter Budget Review, any available funding will be used to restore the flexibility reserve.

Recommendation:
      That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2010 Midyear Budget Review, as detailed in the agenda item.
