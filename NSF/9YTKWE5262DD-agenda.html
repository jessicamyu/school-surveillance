Key Points:

 

FY 2017 Fiscal Forecast

In order to continue the FY 2017 budget planning discussion, an updated fiscal forecast for the School Operating Fund for FY 2017 will be presented (Attachments #1 and #2). The forecast includes projections for the key financial areas that drive the budget development process, such as revenue assumptions and expenditure assumptions, including employee salaries and benefits, student enrollment, and other requirements. 

 

Based on updated projections for revenue and required expenditures and after including a three percent transfer increase as included in the County’s budget guidance, FCPS is forecasting a deficit of $72 million for FY 2017. The budget shortfall could fall above or below the estimate. The shortfall contains uncertainty because there are costs and funding that are not determined until later in the budget process. Funding from the State of Virginia will not be determined until March 2016, changing enrollment projections evolve throughout next spring, and the amount of funding Fairfax County transfers to FCPS is not determined until April 2016. As a result, budget projections for FY 2017 will be updated throughout the budget process as information is available. 

 

Budget Task Force September Update

The FY 2017 Budget Task Force has met six times, including an additionally scheduled meeting that was held on August 20. In order to complete its work, the Task Force has adjusted the delivery of its final report to Dr. Garza from October 7 to October 15, and will use the October 7 date for a Task Force meeting. An updated calendar is included as Attachment #3.

 

During its August 4 meeting, the Task Force considered and finalized the list of potential reductions and fees that would be included in the Budget Proposal Tool. On August 20, staff presented the Task Force with additional information about the budget (Attachment #4) and answered questions. Staff also demonstrated the Budget Proposal Tool to the Task Force who provided valuable input into the final tool design.

 

To assist the Superintendent and the Budget Task Force with its work and to continue to engage the community, two community meetings were held on September 9, at South Lakes High School, and September 12, at Mount Vernon High School. Attendees viewed a video about the FCPS budget, received an overview of the FCPS budget, and participated in a question and answer session. Attachments #5, #6, #7, and #8 are the handouts and presentations that were provided at the community meetings. The Budget Proposal Tool was demonstrated, and attendees were given the opportunity to examine the tool and submit $50 and $75 million budget reduction proposals. All of the materials from the Community Information meetings, including the budget video, presentation, and the Budget Proposal Tool are available at http://www.fcps.edu/news/fy2017.shtml

 

To date, over 850 individuals have submitted proposals using the Budget Proposal Tool. Survey results thus far indicate that over 80 percent of the users have found the tool easy to use.

 

During its meeting on September 16, the Task Force reviewed preliminary output data from the Budget Proposal Tool.  During its September 30 and October 7 meetings, the Task Force will continue the consensus building process in order to develop its recommendations and prepare its final report.

Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

Subject:                   Proposed Freedom Hill/Vienna Expedited Boundary Adjustment

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

An expedited boundary study for Freedom Hill and Vienna Elementary Schools has been initiated by the Region 1 and 2 Assistant Superintendents and the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services in response to community concerns raised over the past few years.  The purpose of the boundary study is to align the feeder pattern of students who live in the Town of Vienna to the Madison High School Pyramid to maximize efficiency.  Guidance has been provided to give careful consideration to minimizing impact on demographics, specifically English Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) and the Free and Reduced Meals (FRM) programs.

 

Key Points:

 

The study area is a section of the Freedom Hill ES attendance area that is situated in the Town of Vienna.  Students in this study area attend Freedom Hill ES which is located in the Marshall HS pyramid, and then they attend Thoreau MS and Madison HS in the Madison HS pyramid.

 

Freedom Hill ES has 640 students and is overcapacity by 28 students. According to the Spring 2015 student enrollment projections, Freedom Hill ES is projected to be overcapacity by 142 students by SY2020-21.  Vienna ES has 427 students and is overcapacity by six students.  According to the Spring 2015 student enrollment projections, Vienna ES is projected to be overcapacity by 35 students by SY2020-21.

 

Discussion:

 

In June 2015, staff proposed four options to the community.  Option 1 proposed the reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill ES to Vienna ES in September 2015.  Sixth grade students would remain at Freedom Hill ES.  Option 2 proposed “status quo” which involved no adjustment of attendance areas.  Option 3 proposed a phase-in process, beginning with rising kindergarteners in September 2015, along with flexibility for school assignments of siblings.  Option 4 proposed the reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill ES to Vienna ES in September 2016.

 

In general, staff found that the community was supportive of the reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill ES to Vienna ES effective September 2016 (Option 4).  Input was received during the two community meetings held at each school, and is posted on each school’s respective website.

 

Families in the study area from Freedom Hill ES who currently receive School Age Child Care (SACC) services will continue to receive these services at Vienna ES when the boundary change is made.

 

The middle and high school assignments of students in the study area would remain the same.  This boundary adjustment would align students in the study area to the Madison High School Pyramid.

 

Capacity relief will be needed at Vienna ES through interior modifications.

 

This staff recommendation is scheduled for new business on September 24, and Board action October 8, 2015.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill Elementary School to Vienna Elementary School, effective for the 2016-17 school year, in order to balance enrollment and utilization of school facilities; and pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4); and Policy 8130.7, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings; and in accordance with Regulation 2230.12, Exceptions for Attendance at Other Than Base School and Procedure for Applying for Intracounty Exceptions; approve rising 6th grade students affected by the boundary change the option to remain at Freedom Hill ES.

Subject:                       Health Benefits Update

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

Open Enrollment for Calendar Year (CY) 2016 will be held from October 19 – November 13.   During this time, employees will have an opportunity to revisit their optional benefit elections, including health care plans.

 

All industries, including FCPS, are experiencing large increases in health care costs due to a variety of factors, including increased use of specialty drugs, medical technology, and compliance with Federal and state mandates.   The majority of FCPS health plans are self-insured, and premiums are established to match expected claims and needed reserves. Over the last few years, Health Fund reserves have declined due to larger than expected claims. 

 

While FCPS was able to keep premium costs level in CY 2015, plan and premium changes will be required for CY 2016.  Changes include the addition of in-network deductibles and an increase in the out-of pocket maximum.  These plan design elements are consistent with plans in other jurisdictions.

 

Staff continues to evaluate health plan options in order to minimize the impact to employees and retirees.  Details regarding final plan design and premiums will be available prior to Open Enrollment, and will be distributed through a variety of multi-media venues, including video, Principal/Association Briefings, myFCPS Employee, and Benefit Briefings mailed to employee and retiree home addresses.
