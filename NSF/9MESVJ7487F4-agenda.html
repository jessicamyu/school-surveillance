National Dyslexia Awareness Month, October 2014

 

October is National Dyslexia Awareness Month.  Dyslexia is a language-based learning disability that results in people having difficulties learning to read, spell, write and pronounce words. Recognizing the markers of dyslexia and intervening early helps to provide opportunities for future success. This month the Office of Special Education Instruction will be offering a series of events in an effort to raise awareness about dyslexia.  On Friday, October 10, a parent workshop on "Strategies to Support Students with Dyslexia" is scheduled at the Parent Resource Center and will highlight strategies, accommodations, resources and FREE tech tools to help support students with dyslexia. Please check our FCPS web site for information.

 

National School Lunch Week, October 13-17, 2014

Using the theme Get in the Game with School Lunch, the 2014 National School Lunch Week will encourage students to find a balance between healthy eating and physical activity and exercise. The 2014 event will also spotlight the healthy foods and positive changes—including the addition of more fruits and vegetables, more whole grains, and less fat and sodium—that FCPS has incorporated into its menus.  The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own, nutrition habits in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

Teen Read Week, October 12-18, 2014

 

Teen Read Week encourages teens to go beyond the traditional book and explore poetry, audiobooks, books about music, and more at their school and local library.  The 2014 theme Turn Dreams Into Reality @ Your Library encourages teens to learn about the inspirational stories, career resources, and how-to books available at their local libraries.  Teen Read Week has been observed since 1998 in an effort to engage teens in reading for pleasure and fun and to use the services at the library.  Research shows that teens who read for fun have better test scores and are more likely to succeed in the workforce.  The School Board encourages all FCPS teenage students to take advantage of the resources in their school and public library and to become lifelong readers and learners.

National School Bus Safety Week, October 20-24, 2014

 

The week of October 20-24 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. Based on the theme At My Stop, You Stop!,  the week focuses on student safety when waiting for and boarding a school bus while reminding motorists to stop when a school bus has its red lights flashing.  School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 130,000 students in over 1,500 school buses traveling nearly 17 million operational miles each year.

6.01 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution - Recommendation to adopt the newly revised 2014 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item (FS; action scheduled 10/23/14)
Meeting
Oct 9, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 5
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:            Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Roger L. Ball, director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:     October 9, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 5

 

Subject:                    Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 1, 2014, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2014 session of the General Assembly.  Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

County’s Administrative Changes

  1. Formal solicitation threshold:  The General Assembly passed SB1107 in 2011, a non-mandatory bill that increased the small purchase threshold from $50,000 to $100,000 for goods and non-professional services.  The same bill increased the small purchase threshold from $50,000 to $60,000 for professional services.  Subsequent to this change, the County will have the same thresholds the state implemented in 2011.
  1. Public posting requirement (emergency and sole source contracts):  The public posting requirement for emergency and sole source contracts is increased to $100,000 in accordance with SB 1107.
  1. Drug-free workplace contract provision:  The threshold for requiring a drug-free workplace contract provision is increased from $50,000 to $100,000.
  1. State Corporation Commission (SCC) authorization:  The threshold for contractors to obtain a SCC authorization is increased from $50,000 to $100,000.
  1. Design-Build Contracts:  The requirement for post project review by the Design-Build/Construction Management Review Board is removed.  The Review Board was eliminated by the General Assembly in 2011.  All other references to the Review Board were previously removed from the Purchasing Resolution.
  1. Construction Prequalification:  A provision to limit the remedy available to a contractor denied prequalification is added to the Purchasing Resolution.  The change will limit relief to restoration of eligibility, consistent with the VPPA.
  1. Article 6, Supply Management:  Text changes to clarify the consumable and fixed assets, as well as, surplus property duties of the Purchasing Agent for Fairfax County Government and Fairfax County Public Schools.

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, and 2013 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2014 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

  1. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2014 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
