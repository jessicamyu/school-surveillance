VSBA Bullying Prevention Month - January 2014

 

The Virginia School Boards Association has designated the month of January 2014 as VSBA Bullying Prevention Month. Childhood bullying is a significant problem nationwide. It can cause school absenteeism, mental and physical stress, poor school performance, poor self-esteem, and, in some cases, school violence. Statistics show that 160,000 children in the United States miss school each day as a result of being bullied. School administrators, teachers, and parents play a critical role in creating a climate where bullying is not tolerated. It has been proven when adults and children stand together, bullying ends.  It is important for FCPS parents, students, teachers, and school administrators to be aware of bullying, and to encourage discussion of the problem as a school community.

6.01 Appointment of Hearing Officers for Teacher Dismissal Hearings - Recommendation to appoint Rosemarie Annunziata, Lois Berlin, William Chapman, Edward Clymore, and John Robinson to serve as Hearing Officers for Teacher Dismissal Hearings pursuant to Section 22.1-311 of the Code of Virginia, effective immediately (HR; action scheduled 1/23/14)

[Revised 1/6/14 - Recommendation]

Staff Contact:          Phyllis Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Meeting Category:  January 9, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 11

 

Subject:                  Appointment of Hearing Officers for Teacher Dismissal Hearings

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points: 

 

During its 2013 session, the General Assembly amended and repealed several sections of the Code of Virginia regarding the procedures for the dismissal of teachers and appeal hearings requested by the teacher.  Among them, the procedure for a three member fact-finding panel has been repealed and Section 22.1-311 of the Code has been amended to provide that hearings are conducted by the school board or by an impartial hearing officer appointed by the school board.   Prior to the amendment to the Code of Virginia, the Board had determined that hearings would be conducted by a fact-finding panel.  Now, with the elimination of fact finding panels under Section 22.1-311, all such hearings may be conducted by a hearing officer. Rather than have the Board appoint a hearing officer each time one is needed for a hearing, it is instead recommended that the Board appoint a pool of hearing officers. An individual hearing officer can be drawn from this pool as needed for a teacher dismissal hearing.

 

The Code requires the hearing officer to possess some knowledge of and expertise in public education and education law and be capable of presiding over an administrative hearing. Staff is recommending the appointment of the following group of hearing officers: Hon. Rosemarie Annunziata (Ret.), Ms. Lois Berlin, Mr. William Chapman, Mr. Edward G. Clymore, and Mr. John Robinson, all of whom meet the requirements of the Code. 

 

Hon. Rosemarie Annunziata has 30 years of judicial and legal experience as an appellate judge, trial judge, and trial attorney.  She has experience adjudicating employment matters such as workers compensation disputes, discrimination and sexual harassment and retaliation claims, and termination disputes. Judge Annunziata has served as a Hearing Officer for Special Education Appeals for the Commonwealth of Virginia, Hearing Officer and Counsel, Fairfax County Civil Service Commission, and currently serves as Chair, Fairfax County Civil Service Commission.  Judge Annunziata has presided over several fact-finding hearings for Fairfax County Public Schools as the neutral chair of the three person panel.

 

Lois Berlin has over 30 years of experience as a teacher, principal, assistant superintendent, and superintendent in Virginia schools, including having served as Superintendent of Falls Church City Public Schools for over 7 years.

 

William Chapman has over 40 years of legal experience representing School Boards throughout Virginia, including Loudoun County, King George County and Tazewell County. He also has extensive experience in teacher and student discipline cases. He has litigated several freedom of speech and religion cases in state and federal court and has conducted training seminars for educators at VSBA and other association meetings.

 

Edward G. Clymore has over 45 years of experience as a teacher, principal, assistant superintendent, and superintendent in Virginia schools, including having served as Superintendent of Augusta County Public Schools for 17 years.

 

John Robinson has over 35 years of experience in human resources and public education. He is the past director of personnel services for Northern Virginia Community College and he served as the Executive Director of the Fairfax County Civil Service Commission for 22 years, which adjudicated employee grievances and appeals.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint Rosemarie Annunziata, Lois Berlin, William Chapman, Edward Clymore, and John Robinson to serve as Hearing Officers for Teacher Dismissal Hearings pursuant to Section 22.1-311 of the Code of Virginia, effective immediately.

Meeting Category:  January 9, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 11

 

Subject:                  Mount Vernon Woods Elementary School – Deed of Vacation and Release

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

A neighboring landowner in the vicinity of Mt. Vernon Woods Elementary School has requested that the School Board vacate and release certain outdated development restrictions applicable to the landowner’s real property.

 

Both the Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County Park Authority, each of which also owns real property in this area, have already granted the landowner’s request.

 

The development restrictions applicable to the landowner’s real property date to a 1955 deed from Joseph and Wilma Baker, who at the time owned a 100 acre parcel of land in the Lee Magisterial District from which the School Board acquired its own 10 acre site which is now Mt. Vernon Woods Elementary School.  The 1955 development restrictions are as follows:

 

“That neither the parties of the second part nor the heirs, successors or assigns of the parties of the second part shall or will erect, or cause or suffer to be erected, or use or cause or suffer to be used on any portion of said premises any building except 2 dwelling houses for one family each which building shall cost to erect, at least sixteen thousand ($16,000.00) Dollars each, and which building shall be only one story high above the ground.”  The deed also went on to restrict the type of fencing and hedges allowed.

 

The 1955 development restrictions serve no present day purpose, and the School Board’s interests are not adversely affected by vacating these development restrictions. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services, either of whom may act without the other, to do all things necessary and appropriate on behalf of the School Board, and with the advice of legal counsel for the school division, to execute and to deliver a deed of vacation and release of the development restrictions of record applicable to certain real property, known as Fairfax County Tax Map # 101-2-01-0010C.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
