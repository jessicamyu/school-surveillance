Subject: 2021-2022 Standard School Year Calendar

 

Staff Contact:  Sean McDonald, interim assistant superintendent, Human Resources Department

                      Michael Draeger, director, HR Business Services

 

Meeting Category: March 2, 2021 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

A committee was assembled for a third consecutive year to assist in the development of the calendar. The committee was composed of representatives from a variety of internal and external constituent groups which included community religious organization representatives, teachers, employees and principal association representatives, along with central office staff. The 2021-2022 Standard School Year Calendar was developed with a focus on instruction and learning time for students. While teaching and learning was the priority, a multitude of factors were considered and balanced during the development of the calendar. Additionally, the calendar committee received input and feedback from the recent Religious Observance Task Force and thus the draft calendars were created with an equity lens focus and provide consideration for four religious observance holidays – Rosh Hashanah (9/7/2021), Yom Kippur (9/16/2021), Diwali (11/4/2021), and Eid al Fitr (5/3/2022).  Two calendar drafts, Version A and Version B, were developed by the calendar committee to fill these aims.

 

An additional calendar draft, Version C, has been developed for School Board consideration that more closely mirrors the current school year calendar.  This calendar sets a fixed week for spring break; the second full week in March.

 

 

 

Recommendation: That the School Board adopt the 2021-2022 standard school year calendar.

Motions:

 

1. Ms. Omeish moved, and Ms. Meren seconded, to direct the superintendent to develop a new calendar option, to account for but not limited to:

  • legal considerations
  • instructional concerns and disruption
  • student wellness, both socio-emotional and academic
  • support staff pay
  • absenteeism data
  • operational disruption
  • staff days off and planning time
  • survey preferences
  • transparency and community climate
  • any and all equity considerations
  • inclusiveness
  • consider use of two floating holidays that could be used for religious observances or cultural celebrations​​​​​​

The motion passed unanimously.

2. Ms. Derenak Kaufax moved, and Ms. McLaughlin seconded, to amend the main motion to add a bullet point to read: consider use of two floating holidays that could be used for religious observances or cultural celebrations​​​​​​.  The motion  passed 7-5-0: Ms. McLaughlin, Dr. Anderson, Ms. Pekarsky, Ms. Tholen, Ms. Keys-Gamarra, Mrs. Corbett Sanders and Ms. Derenak Kaufax voted "aye;"  Ms. Omeish, Mr. Frisch, Ms. Cohen, Ms. Meren and Ms. Sizemore Heizer voted "no."

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

This Presentation will provide an overview of the School Improvements and Support (DSIS) budget which support the Division in:

  • Providing support for all schools to be fully accredited with the Virginia Department of Education
  • To develop best practices and support of teachers and school teams in strategies for closing achievement gaps through Region Support Teams
  • Provide professional development and support in the areas of MTSS to ensure consistent practices through Region Support Teams
  • Centralize practices for Assessment and Reporting and ensure compliance with VDOE policy
  • Provide central guidance and support for the successful implementation and operation of VHSL Athletics and Activities and ensure a robust and inclusive clubs program for all students
  • Ensure opportunities and access for students to participate in alternative pathways to graduation through our Nontraditional programs
