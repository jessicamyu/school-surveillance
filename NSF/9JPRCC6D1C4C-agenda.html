Subject:                    Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

 

Key Points: 

 

School Board Annual Charge-Curriculum

  1. FCPS develop or adopt a screening tool to be used as part of the planning for the annual IEP for students with disabilities.  This tool will be used to identify life skills deficits (i.e. deficits in the areas of communication, collaboration, creativity, critical thinking and/or self-direction/organization) and be combined with a list of possible resources for staff to address the deficits.

 School Board Annual Charge- After-School Programs, Clubs and Sports

  1. FCPS will ensure the job announcements; job descriptions and interview questions for those supporting after-school programs include a focus on working with students with disabilities of varying abilities.
  2. FCPS will provide on-going professional development for staff involved in after-school programs on best practices for the inclusion of students with disabilities.
  3. FCPS will provide a training breakout session during the Special Education Conference for parents and staff regarding the benefits of after school programs and/or extra-curricular activities for students with disabilities.
  4. FCPS will add a section on after-school or extracurricular activities in the special education survey to gather feedback on the participation in, and satisfaction of, students with disabilities.
  5. FCPS will train staff to include discussion of appropriate after-school activities for students with disabilities for IEP/504 plans.
  6. FCPS will provide assistance to staff with grant writing to acquire additional funding and resources to support after-school programming.

School Board Charge-Vocational Programming Sub Committee

  1. FCPS will ensure the job announcements, job descriptions, and interview questions for those supporting after-school programs include a focus on working with students with disabilities of varying abilities.
  2. FCPS will provide on-going professional development for staff involved in after-school programs on best practices for the inclusion of students with disabilities.
  3. FCPS will provide a training breakout session during the Special Education Conference for parents and staff regarding the benefits of after school programs and/or extra-curricular activities for students with disabilities.
  4. FCPS will add a section on after-school or extracurricular activities in the special education survey to gather feedback on the participation in, and satisfaction of, students with disabilities.
  5. FCPS will train staff to include discussion of appropriate after-school activities for students with disabilities for IEP/504 plans.
  6. FCPS will provide assistance to staff with grant writing to acquire additional funding and resources to support after-school programming.

 School Board Charge-Vocational Programming Sub Committee

  1. FCPS develop an awareness campaign to inform students and their families and/or support networks of the Extended Graduation option (5th year) for students with disabilities as early as middle school.
  2. FCPS improve upon informing parents of and promoting course options for AVID, WAT and EFE classes as students transition to middle school.
  3. FCPS inform parents of the role of, and promote consultation with, the ETR each year, beginning in the student’s transition year, before the annual IEP.
  4. FCPS explore flexible scheduling for adding an Academy class before or after regular school hours so that graduation within four years is more feasible for students with disabilities.
  5. FCPS create in-depth workshops for parents and students on Academy programs, specifically for students with disabilities.
  6. FCPS ensure that all middle and high school counselors are trained in Academy offerings and prerequisites.

School Board Annual Charge-Assistive Technology Services

  1. FCPS train teachers, case managers, and administrators on the technology available to prepare students for life after high school.
  2. FCPS create workshops that raise the awareness of students and families about the resources available to them to obtain needed technology in college and the work place, to include awareness of DARS, AT Act, and college disability services. 
  3. FCPS reinforce the need to discuss AT at all IEPs at the middle and high school level, even when a student is not receiving ATS services, so that students and families gain a greater awareness of how technology supports the students’ learning.
  4. FCPS teachers and administrators offer the including staff from Assistive Technology Services and Career and Transition Services to IEP and 504 meetings if the nature of the students’ needs may require ATS and/or CTS technology training and services.
  5. FCPS focus on increasing the self-advocacy skills of all youth with disabilities around the use of technology, building upon and expanding programs like the AT Ambassadors.
  6. FCPS promote technology supports in the Public-Private Partnerships developed with the business community to move students with disabilities into the work force.

Related To:  Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees  to the School Board 

 

Key Points:

 

Building on the recommendations and observations delivered by AAPAC to the School Board in June 2013, and the FCPS AAP 2013 Program Review, the School Board charged this committee to:

  • Identify best practices for ensuring that FCPS’ AAP services are implemented successfully at the local school level and identify avenues of communication that will ensure that all members of the community are aware of the continuum of advanced academic services available to students who have the potential to succeed.

The implementation and quality of advanced academic services (across the full continuum of service) is inconsistent across the county, within clusters, and within pyramids.  Fidelity of implementation requires specific metrics, timelines, and accountability for results.

To this end, AAPAC recommends that FCPS adopt and implement nine recommendations:

  1. Embrace and promote a culture that supports advanced academic programs at all levels.
  2. Phase in a full time AART in every elementary school over the next 5 years (budget implications)
  3. Establish tools to systematically assess advanced academics services at each school.
  4. Encourage peer-to-peer mentoring between school professionals across the county.
  5. Require professional development for AAP teachers and Administrators.
  6. Provide tools to AAP students and their parents to develop meaningful individualized academic plans to pursue robust secondary and higher education.
  7. Define a consistent methodology for identifying students for acceleration in mathematics.  
  8. Communicate opportunities for mathematics acceleration to parents.  
  9. Implement advanced mathematics programs for the small percentage of students that may ultimately be ready for algebra in elementary school on a consistent basis throughout FCPS.
