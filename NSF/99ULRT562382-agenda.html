6.01 Suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure - Recommendation to suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period effective March 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2016; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations for the same period (HR; action scheduled 2/20/14)
Meeting
Feb 6, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 13
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:         Phyllis Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Meeting Category:  February 6, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 13

 

Subject:                  Suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:     Legal Requirement

 

Background:

 

Policy 4680, Salary Redline Procedure, authorizes redlining of salaries for up to two years for those employees whose salaries have been reduced as the result of a change in position, if that change was made for the benefit of the school system, rather than a voluntary choice of the employee. The policy provides that redlining may be modified by the Board if deemed necessary for budgetary reasons.
 

Key Points:

 

At the December 18, 2008, regular meeting, the School Board approved the suspension of Policy 4680 for the period beginning January 1, 2009, and ending June 30, 2011, due to budgetary reasons.  At the February 3, 2011, regular meeting, the School Board approved continuing the suspension of the policy for the period beginning July 1, 2011, and ending June 30, 2013.

 

The budget climate that resulted in the previous redline suspension continues to persist and if there are to be planned savings realized from reorganizations and budget reductions, it is necessary to suspend the policy again.

 

Failure to suspend the redlining policy will mean that very little, if any, funds will be saved in reorganization and in budget-related reductions that involve employees moving to shorter contract lengths or to lower-level positions.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period effective March 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2016; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations for the same period.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
