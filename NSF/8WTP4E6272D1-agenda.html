Staff Contact:           Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     October 15, 2012 - Work Session No. 31

 

Subject:                     Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

In preparation for the development of the FY 2014 Proposed Budget, the School Board will discuss budget priorities.   Discussion categories include:  Virginia Retirement shift, employee salary increases, and other programs.  A summary of the fiscal forecast for FY 2014 is included as attachment #1.

 

For FY 2013, a list of potential items identified by the Superintendent as consideration for potential reductions to offset the math textbook purchase is provided.  The School Board directed the Superintendent as part of the FY 2012 Final Budget Review to provide a list in excess of $7.7 million for the Board’s consideration in October 2012 and their determination by November 8, 2012, of the specific reductions to implement.

 

For the fiscal year in process, it is challenging to identify reductions because a majority of FCPS’ budget is committed primarily through salary and benefit expenditures for employees who have been hired for the school year.

 

The list of potential FY 2013 reductions for the School Board’s consideration includes items that were added as part of the FY 2013 budget process.  The first category is noninstructional positions that were added and not yet filled along with the unspent portion of initiatives to fund replacement and network equipment where identified.  The second category is recently added instructional initiatives that are not yet fully expended, and the last category is yet-to-be spent expenditure items that could potentially be deferred for the remainder of the fiscal year.

Staff Contacts:          Richard Moniuszko, Deputy Superintendent

                                  Jim Johnson, PLA Special Projects

                                                                  

Other Staff Present:  Members of Charter School Review Committee

 

Other Presenter:       Eric Wolf Welch, Executive Director, Fairfax Leadership Academy

 

Meeting Category:     October 15, 2012 – Work Session No. 32

 

Subject:                     Fairfax Leadership Academy Charter School Application  

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

  • Code of Virginia authorizes parents, teachers and community members to submit an application to establish a charter school to stimulate development of innovative programs and develop models for replication;
  • Fairfax Leadership Academy has made application, determined by Virginia Board of Education to meet its criteria;
  • FCPS School Board must conduct a public hearing, on the proposed charter school application and decide whether the application is complete and responsive; whether the proposal is sound in all aspects; and whether the proposed charter school is in the public interest and serves the welfare of the students within the Fairfax County Public Schools.
  • Per Policy/Regulation 1400, the Board may take any one or more of the following actions with respect to the application: accept without conditions, accept with conditions, reject, place on the waiting list, return with suggested modifications, defer action, or return with request for additional information

 

The Charter School Review Committee has completed its review of the Fairfax Leadership Academy Charter Application, and will present its findings and recommendation to the School Board.

 

Recommendation

 

That the School Board not accept the Fairfax Leadership Academy Charter School application as submitted unless the issues detailed in the agenda item are satisfactorily resolved by December 1, 2012.

Staff Contact:            Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations                                                              

 

Meeting Category:    October 15, 2012 – Work Session No. 33 

 

Subject:                     Fairfax County School Board 2013 Draft Legislative Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                          

 

Related To:                Legislation

 

Key Points:

 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The attached 2013 Fairfax County School Board Draft Legislative Program includes additions, subtractions and formatting changes. These changes are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate, to incorporate staff recommendations, and to update, simplify and streamline existing position language.

 

Options:        

 

Modify as desired.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept staff recommendations for the Fairfax County School Board draft 2013 Legislative Program.
