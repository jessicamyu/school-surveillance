Bullying Prevention Month- October

 

In an effort to promote awareness of school bullying, the Virginia School Board Association Board of Directors has designated the month of October as VSBA Bullying Prevention Month. Childhood bullying is a significant problem nationwide. It can cause school absenteeism, mental and physical stress, poor school performance, poor self-esteem, and in some cases, school violence. Statistics show that 160,000 children in the United States miss school each day as a result of being bullied. School board members, superintendents, teachers, and parents play a critical role in creating a climate where bullying is not tolerated. It has been proven when adults and children stand together, bullying ends.

 

 

Fire Prevention Week - October 9 - 15, 2016

 

Fire Prevention Week 2016 focuses on the importance of smoke alarms.  The official theme, Don't Wait - Check the Date, encourages citizens to replace smoke alarms every 10 years. Working smoke alarms cut the risk of dying in home fires in half. During Fire Prevention Week, October 9 - 15, fire safety educators around the country will work with citizens to teach them about fire safety tips, encourage them to regularly test their smoke alarms and to install smoke alarms on every level of their homes, and provide activities featuring Sparky the Fire Dog.

Appoint individuals to serve a one-year term on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)                                                                             

 

Other Staff Present: Priscille Dando, coordinator, Library Information Services, ISD

                               

Meeting Category:  September 22, 2016 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject: Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                             

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:    

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents. Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.  For 2016-2017 the Board members making appointments are Tamara Derenak Kaufax (Lee), Sandy Evans (Mason), Elizabeth Schultz (Springfield), Ryan McElveen (At Large), Ilryong Moon (At Large), and Jeanette Hough (At Large). Last year the School Board district representatives from Braddock, Dranesville, Hunter Mill, Mount Vernon, Providence and Sully were selected to appoint individuals.

 

Two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school.  The level will be determined by a random selection. If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate. 

 

The student representative to the School Board appoints an individual to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.

Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009.   

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint individuals to serve a one-year term on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.
