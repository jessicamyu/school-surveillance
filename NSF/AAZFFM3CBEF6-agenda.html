Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2017

 

The American Alliance for Theatre and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development.  The theme is Fostering a Culture of Creativity.  Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.  

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2017

 

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  This year's theme is Music Inspires! and is designed to highlight the importance of comprehensive, sequential music education taught by exemplary music educators for all  children - and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music.  It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages. 

 

Youth Art Month - March 2017

 

Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.  The theme for Youth Art Month 2017 is “The Power of Art.” 

The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

Subject:  Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Approval

 

School Board Action Required: Information                

 

Key Points:

 

The Virginia Department of Education revised the Standards of Learning for History and Social Sciences in 2015. The social studies team in Instructional Services has been revising curriculum documents to align with these new standards and providing professional development for teachers and administrators about the revised SOLs which place an emphasis on the development of social studies skills to learn required content. 

 

The contracts for instructional resources currently in use in FCPS schools expire during summer 2017. As a result, students and teachers would no longer have access to these resources without some action. Therefore in January 2016, Instructional Services began the process for new basal instructional materials as defined in FCPS Regulation 3004.

 

A timeline of the process for the social studies basal resource review can be found in Attachment A. On June 30, 2016 the School Board approved the committee members to review and recommend basal instructional materials for Social Studies. The Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee convened in August and September 2016 to review resources for K- 12 that were submitted by vendors in response to a RFP for consideration as basal instructional materials for Social Studies. No titles for grade 4-Virginia Studies were reviewed by the committee due to the lack of submissions to the RFP.  Instructional Services plans to issue a new RFP later this year for grade 4-Virginia Studies with the Community Review Committee to convene in fall 2017. 

 

In accordance with the Code of Virginia, regulations of the Virginia Board of Education, and Fairfax County Public Schools regulations, the Basal Instructional Materials Adoption committee in conjunction with the Selection Advisory Committee and the Technical Advisory Committee recommends the titles listed in Attachment B for adoption at the division level. Additionally, a summary of the committee rationales for the recommended materials and community input received are provided in attachments C and D.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the titles listed for Kindergarten through grade 12 social studies, which does not include grade 4-Virginia Studies, as recommended by the Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee and the Selection Advisory Committee.

Subject:  FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review        

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2017 budget and to make revisions as necessary. The FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 15, 2016.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund revenue adjustments reflect a decrease of $0.2 million in state revenue for National Board Certified Teacher (NBCT) pass-through payments based on the number of FCPS teachers eligible to receive a stipend from the state in FY 2017.  This grant revenue reduction is offset by corresponding expenditure adjustments. 

 

Recommended School Operating Fund expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $6.7 million. These adjustments include the corresponding expenditure reductions for NBCT totaling $0.2 million, as well as savings of $2.0 million from public system transportation radios resulting from lower cost options identified for replacing bus radios; savings of $1.3 million from transportation fuel; savings totaling $1.4 million from bus driver salary accounts as a result of bus driver and attendant vacancies; and savings of $1.8 million resulting from changes in health plan participation as compared to the projection.

 

In total, in the School Operating Fund, this agenda recognizes decreased revenue totaling $0.2 million and decreased disbursements totaling $6.7 million, which results in available funds totaling $6.5 million. It is recommended that the available funding is added to the FY 2018 Beginning Balance.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.  Also, attached is an update on the status of grants for FY 2017.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure adjustments as reflected in the FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review and as detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
