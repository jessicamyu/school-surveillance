National School Lunch Week, October 9 - 13 2017


National School Lunch Week is officially scheduled for October 9-13 by an act of Congress. Using the theme "School Lunch: Recipes for Success", National School Lunch Week promotes excitement about the benefits of students consuming wholesome, healthy school lunches of fresh fruits and veggies, whole grains and milk.  The 2017 event will encourage school food professionals to highlight the secrets to school lunch success, healthy food choices and of course our students.  The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own, nutrition habits in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

 

Teen Read Week, October 8 - 14, 2017

 

Teen Read Week encourages teens to go beyond the traditional book and explore poetry, audiobooks, books about music, and more at their school and local library.  The 2017 theme "Unleash Your Story" encourages teens to tell their own stories as well as find stories, biographies, autobiographies, and folktales in their local library.  Teen Read Week has been observed since 1998 in an effort to engage teens in reading for pleasure and fun and to use the services at the library.  Research shows that teens who read for fun have better test scores and are more likely to succeed in the workforce.  The School Board encourages all FCPS teenage students to take advantage of the resources in their school and public library and to become lifelong readers and learners.

 

 

Disability Employment and Awareness Month

 

Did you know that October is National Disability Employment and Awareness Month? This year’s theme is “Inclusion drives Innovation”. Each year, Fairfax County Public Schools participates in the National Disability Employment Awareness Month (NDEAM) which promotes career development for individuals with disabilities through hands-on career exploration and job shadowing in the following fields: Administrative Support, Animal Care, Business Management, Communications, Information Technology, and Parks and Recreation.

NDEAM strives to encourage students to explore potential career or employment opportunities, to learn more about businesses in their community, and begin to plan to achieve more successful employment outcomes.

The National PTA, the Fairfax County Council of PTAs, and the Fairfax Education Association recognize October as LGBT (Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Transgender) History Month. 

 

According to the National PTA, “negative attitudes toward lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender students puts them at an increased risk for violence and bullying.  Negative attitudes also impact a student's ability to learn at school.”

 

LGBT History Month is recognized in October nationwide and in FCPS to acknowledge and celebrate that LGBT students, staff and families are an integral component of our nation, and of Fairfax County Public Schools.

This special month was first recognized in 1994; October was chosen to commemorate National Coming Out Day on October 14, and the First March on Washington for Gay and Lesbian Rights on October 14, 1979.

One of the first Gay-Straight Alliances in Virginia was founded at Hayfield High School in 2000, and now students are involved in clubs at many of the high schools in the county, welcoming not only LGBT students but their friends and those interested in creating safe schools.

 

LGBT History Month in October is an opportunity for Fairfax County Public Schools staff and students to celebrate, and to learn more about, these important constituents in our schools and neighborhoods.

Dyslexia Awareness Month October 2017

 

     WHEREAS, dyslexia is a language-based learning disability that causes difficulties with specific language skills such as reading, writing, spelling, and word pronunciation; and

 

     WHEREAS, the onset and severity of dyslexia varies for each individual and impacts up to 15-20% of the population as a whole; and

 

     WHEREAS, literacy is a critical skill needed for educational success and individuals with dyslexia benefit from effective teaching approaches that are multisensory, systematic, and explicit; and

 

     WHEREAS, Governor Terry McAuliffe has recognized October 2017 as DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH in Virginia to acknowledge educators specializing in effective teaching strategies for individuals with dyslexia; and to celebrate the many achievements of adolescents, students, and adults with dyslexia; and

 

     WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools has instituted dyslexia awareness training for all elementary and secondary instructional staff and administrators; and

 

     WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools is launching the FCPS Online Dyslexia Handbook to provide information and resources to FCPS schools and families alike as they support students with dyslexia; and

 

     WHEREAS, parents, educators, and students will benefit from increased awareness about the nature of dyslexia and the combination of strengths and difficulties that individuals with dyslexia can have;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, the Fairfax County School Board, recognizes October 2017 as DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH and encourages all residents to learn more about dyslexia and to offer support to parents, educators and individuals affected by dyslexia.

National School Bus Safety Week, October 16-20, 2017

 

The week of October 16-20 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. Based on the theme "#Stop on Red!", the week focuses on student safety when waiting for and boarding a school bus.  School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 142,000 students in over 1,600 school buses on more than 6,500 separate routes traveling over 17 million operational miles each year.

 

Joining us in the audience this evening is FCPS bus driver, Anne Kronebusch.  Anne has always been driven to do great things for students outside of our school division.  With tremendous support from the Office of Transportation Services staff, and local churches, Anne drove more than 1000 miles to personally deliver 60 boxes of school supplies to Dade County Florida students who have been significantly impacted by recent hurricanes.  Anne loves and looks out for kids, the ones she is fortunate to drive every day as well as those whom she will never meet.

           Twenty-two Fairfax County public schools received the 2017 Board of Education Distinguished Achievement Award, a recognition of schools that have met all state and federal benchmarks and are making progress toward designated goals.  Schools receiving the Distinguished Achievement Award include: Centreville High, Crossfield Elementary, Flint Hill Elementary, Franklin Middle, Hunt Valley Elementary, Irving Middle, Elementary, Laurel Ridge Elementary, Lees Corner Elementary, Lemon Road Elementary, McNair Elementary, Oak View Elementary, Oakton Elementary, Powell Elementary, Ravensworth Elementary, Sherman Elementary, Silverbrook Elementary, South County High, Stratford Landing Elementary, Thoreau Middle, Union Mill Elementary, Vienna Elementary and West Springfield Elementary schools.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
