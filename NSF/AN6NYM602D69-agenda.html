Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2018

 

The American Alliance for Theater and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development.  The theme is Fostering a Culture of Creativity.  Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.  

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2018

 

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  This year's theme is Music Inspires! and is designed to highlight the importance of comprehensive, sequential music education taught by exemplary music educators for all  children - and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music.  It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages. 

 

Youth Art Month - March 2018

 

Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.  The theme for Youth Art Month 2018 is “Building Community Through Art.” 

The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

3.04 Recognition of the Frost Middle School CyberPatriot Team Earning First Place in the Virginia Competition
Meeting
Mar 8, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type

On behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, it’s an honor for my colleague Dalia Palchik and me to recognize Frost Middle School’s Millennium Falcons, an 8th grade first-year CyberPatriot team, which earned a first place finish in Virginia for the Open Division of the Air Force Association’s National Youth Cyber Defense Competition.

 

This year was the first time the Millennium Falcons competed and the second year that Frost’s teams have entered the competition. The Millennium Falcons excelled by winning first place, making it the second consecutive year that Frost’s teams have won the highest award at the state level.

The National Youth Cyber Defense Competition is designed to excite, educate, and motivate students toward careers in cyber security and other (STEM) disciplines. The teams in this competition are challenged to find and resolve cybersecurity vulnerabilities in simulated environments.

 

Congratulations to Coach Julie Healy who led this team to their first state win, to team mentor Vladimir Leonov, and to the Millennium Falcons team on your remarkable and well-deserved achievement!

 

We wish you all the best in your future endeavors!

3.05 Recognition of the Lake Braddock SS Band winner of the 2017 Sudler Flag of Honor
Meeting
Mar 8, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type

On behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, it gives me great pleasure to honor the Lake Braddock Secondary School Concert Band who was selected to receive the 2017 Sudler Flag of Honor by the John Philip Sousa Foundation, which is only one of two FCPS schools this year to receive this prestigious honor. This is an international award recognizing high school concert bands of outstanding musical excellence.

 

To name of a few of the accomplishments to meet the criteria for this award: the band had to achieve and maintain a high standard of excellence in the concert area over a period of several years, had to perform and receive superior ratings at state regional or national levels, and students from the band had to participate in district and all-state honor bands.

 

Congratulations to Lake Braddock High School Band Director Mike Luley, Middle School Director Lou Bean, Associate Director Jason Miller, and the Lake Braddock Secondary Concert Band on your remarkable and well-deserved accomplishment. I personally look forward to seeing your recognition and flag ceremony by a member from the John Philip Sousa executive board at your spring concert in May.

WHEREAS, school board clerks in each locality throughout our great Commonwealth are appointed by law to fulfill their duties and responsibilities; and

WHEREAS, school board clerks are responsible for keeping accurate records of the meetings and proceedings of the school board, a record of all receipts and disbursements, and a record of all official acts; and

WHEREAS, school board clerks perform such other duties in connection with the school business of their county and city as may be required by the school board; and

WHEREAS, school board clerks maintain frequent contact with the public, including parents, employees and the media, on behalf of the school board and superintendent; and

WHEREAS, school board clerks, in the performance of their duties, are often required to work extra hours attending school board meetings; and

WHEREAS, school board clerks provide an invaluable service for school board members, superintendents, and the VSBA Board of Directors, and the Commonwealth of Virginia; and

WHEREAS, the Fairfax County School Board joins the Virginia School Boards Association in recognizing the many and varied contributions of school board clerks;

NOW, THEREFORE, be it resolved that at its meeting on this 8th day of March, 2018, the Fairfax County School Board officially recognizes our three clerks with appreciation for their hard work to support each of us, as well as, our numerous school board meetings. We extend our sincere gratitude to each of you for making our jobs easier and more manageable.  We would be lost without you. I so move.

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2018 budget and to make revisions as necessary. The FY 2018 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 14, 2017.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund revenue adjustments reflect a net decrease of $0.2 million. These adjustments include a decrease in state revenue for National Board Certified Teacher (NBCT) pass-through payments based on the number of FCPS teachers eligible to receive a stipend from the state in FY 2018, and an increase of $8,000 in the IDEA Parent Resource federal grant award.
