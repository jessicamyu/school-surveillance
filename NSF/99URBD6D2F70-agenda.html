Teacher Appreciation Week - May 5-9, 2014

 

Every day, over 16,000 teachers in Fairfax County Public Schools demonstrate their personal and professional dedication to providing an excellent education for the children in our community. Our teachers are committed to ensuring that all children develop their full potentials for academic achievement. Teachers are influential in fostering in their students both healthful self-concepts and a sense of civic responsibility. Fairfax County Public Schools values the skills, creativity, and professionalism that characterize our teachers.

 

Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month - May 2014

 

Americans of Asian and Pacific ancestry have made significant contributions to the history and development of the United States in many fields of endeavor such as science, commerce, the arts, and the humanities. The Fairfax County School Board recognizes that an understanding of these contributions should be an integral part of the year-round education of all students.

Since 1992, the President of the United States has proclaimed May to be Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month and has chosen this month to pay tribute to the generations of Asian and Pacific Islanders who have enriched American’s history and are instrumental in its future success.

 

School Nurse Day - May 7, 2014

School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.  This annual event recognizes the contributions that school nurses make every day to improve the health and success of our nation’s children.  This year’s theme, “The School Nurse:  Caring for Others, Caring for Ourselves,” reflects the significant roles that school nurses have in health care in their school communities, as well as in modeling health and resiliency.

School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health, safety, prevention, and wellness;  intervene with actual and potential health problems; and provide case management services.  They are an invaluable bridge connecting health care providers, public health, and families.

The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognized May 7 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

Recognition of Better Hearing and Speech Month - May 2014

 

Since 1927, the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) has celebrated Better Hearing and Speech Month every year in May. The theme this year is “Identify the Signs,” an effort to educate the public about the warning signs of communication disorders. This annual event provides opportunities to promote services that can improve the quality of life for those who experience problems with speaking and listening. An estimated 42 million people have communication disorders and they impact one in ten families. 

 

Currently, Fairfax County Public Schools provides speech and language services to more than 10,000 students.  Audiologists and special education teachers also serve more than 500 deaf and hard of hearing students in class-based programs, via base school itinerant services, and in early childhood programs.  FCPS annually observes and celebrates Better Hearing and Speech Month and encourages all citizens in our school community to recognize the efforts, dedication, and skills of the speech-language clinicians, audiologists, and teachers of students who are deaf and hard of hearing.

RESOLUTION

SUPPORTING THE UNIFIED PREVENTION COALITION OF FAIRFAX COUNTY

AND ITS “PARENTS WHO HOST, LOSE THE MOST” CAMPAIGN

 

 

     WHEREAS, 65 percent of underage youth say they get alcohol from family and friends, even though it is illegal in Virginia for a person under 21 years of age to consume or possess alcohol, and it is illegal for an adult to provide alcohol to a person under 21 years of age; and
 

     WHEREAS, alcohol is the most commonly used illegal drug by Fairfax County students; and underage alcohol use is associated with traffic crashes and fatalities, other drug use, violence and gang involvement, rape, depression and suicide, and educational failure; and
 

     WHEREAS, the 2012-2013 Fairfax County Youth Survey reported that 35 percent of high school seniors and 19 percent of tenth graders had consumed alcohol in the last 30 days, and that 18.2 percent of seniors had engaged in binge drinking in the last two weeks; and
 

     WHEREAS, the Unified Prevention Coalition of Fairfax County, an independent non-profit organization of over 60 collaborative partners, including school officials, county government agencies, concerned residents, students, and community groups, has been instrumental in addressing underage drinking and binge drinking, and is now in the eighth year of a campaign known as “Parents Who Host, Lose the Most: Don’t be a party to teenage drinking,” to educate parents and to increase awareness of and compliance with Virginia underage drinking laws; and

 

     WHEREAS, prevention efforts, including parents talking with their teens and modeling appropriate behavior, are critical, particularly during prom and graduation season;
 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board supports the Unified Prevention Coalition of Fairfax County and recognizes the month of May 2014 as “Parents Who Host, Lose The Most” month in Fairfax County.

A Resolution of the Fairfax County School Board

Commemorating the 60th Anniversary of the Historic U.S. Supreme Court

Decision in Brown v. the Board of Education

 

 

     WHEREAS, public education is essential to an informed citizenry, the foundation of our democratic society, and provides the gateway to opportunity, our nation's promise to all; regardless of race, religion, ethnic background, or economic circumstance, access to public education is a fundamental civil right in a democracy; and

 

     WHEREAS, on May 17, 1954, in an historic and unanimous decision, the United States Supreme Court in Brown v. Board of Education ruled that public education is subject to the equal protection provisions of the United States Constitution; the Court based its decision on the premise that to separate children according to their race was unfair, diminishing their hopes and their future aspirations; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Court's pronouncement in Brown v. Board of Education validated the struggle and remarkable actions of countless Americans who challenged the destructive effects of segregation in our society through peaceful, lawful means; the contributions of civil rights leaders and volunteers, parents and students are recognized, for it is only through their courage, conviction and sacrifices that Brown v. Board of Education became a reality; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Court’s decision has had a profound, significant, and beneficial impact on all aspects of life in the United States and continues to inspire our nation’s children, regardless of age, gender, race, ethnicity, religion, sexual orientation, disability or economic status, to appreciate the value of public education and public service as a means to further the objectives of democracy, justice and equality; and

 

     WHEREAS, on the date of May 17th, students in our county’s public schools are encouraged to mark the anniversary of Brown v. Board of Education with appropriate ceremony and remembrance and to foster a personal commitment to democracy through essays, creative arts, lectures, research and writing, community projects, and other activities;  

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Fairfax County School Board recognizes that May 17, 2014, marks the 60th anniversary of the landmark U.S. Supreme Court decision in Brown v. Board of Education and that the unanimous 1954 Supreme Court decision is a touchstone to further a nationwide appreciation for the advancement of democratic principles through our system of law and justice.

School Nutrition Employee Week, May 5-9, 2014

 

Caring. Trained. Thoughtful. Professional. Dedicated.  These are just a few words that describe today's school nutrition professional.

 

School nutrition employees in Fairfax County Public Schools demonstrate daily their professional commitment to providing students with nutritious menu choices that reflect current research and meet the Dietary Guidelines for Americans. School nutrition employees must balance many roles and follow numerous federal, state and local regulations to ensure safe and healthy meals are available in schools. They are trained sanitation and food safety experts and must manage financially self-sufficient programs. School nutrition professionals also provide nutrition education to students, as well as healthy catering services to their communities. They use their creativity to make the cafeteria a fun and welcoming place all year long and perform their jobs each day because they care passionately about the children they serve.

 

FCPS school nutrition employees are committed to imparting food and nutrition knowledge and skills to students, using the school cafeteria as the nutrition laboratory, and are influential in preparing students to learn.

Staff Contact:         Kim P. Dockery, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Meeting Category:  April 24, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 18

 

Subject:                  Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2014-2015

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:            Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:                                   

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2014-2015, as required by VDOE Superintendent’s memo #335-13, dated December 20, 2013.  This report will be used by the Virginia Department of Education in preparing the state plan for special education.  As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-proscribed abbreviated format on a date no later than May 16, 2014.  

 

The 2014-2015 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $32,313,328. This amount is equal to the final 2013-2014 award as stated in by Superintendent’s memo #189-13 dated July 26, 2013 (revised August 23, 2013). Estimated expenditures include cost associated with maintaining a mandatory 15% set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS), and a mandatory private school set-aside of 0.5%.  Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 417.1 FTE positions.

 

The 2014-2015 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $730,977. This amount is equal to the 2013-2014 award.  These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for preschool-age students.  Estimated expenditures include cost associated with a mandatory private school set-aside of 1.3%. Section 619 funds six FTE positions.

 

The amounts above reflect no change from the 2013-14 award.  Adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.  None of the above represents new or additional operating resources.

Authorize the Division Superintendent, or Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute the Storm Water Enhancement Project Contract Change Order on behalf of the School Board, as detailed in the agenda item, and as shown on the County approved site plan to Dustin Construction Inc. for the amount of $2,057,460 (FTS; presented as new business 4/10/14)

Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  April 24, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 18

 

Subject:                  Change Order Marshall HS Project - County Initiated Storm Water Enhancement Project

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Department of Public Works & Environmental Services Storm Water Planning Division (SWP) approached the Office of Design and Construction to initiate a Storm Water Enhancement Project (SWEP) on the George C. Marshall High School campus during the renovation project.  This initiative is part of an on-going collaboration between SWP and D&C to identify and install storm water facilities on FCPS property that will help to meet the County’s future MS-4 permit obligations.

 

The SWEP consists of the installation of a large below-grade cistern (concrete tank) that will intercept the piped storm water collected from the entire campus and building.  The water captured by the cistern will be filtered, sanitized, and used to irrigate the existing natural turf athletic fields.  The work requires replacement of existing storm sewer pipes, installation of a large underground cistern, installation of a small building to house the pumps and other equipment; installation of a brand-new irrigation system on all natural turf athletic fields, installation of a paved access road to the pump building from the parking lot; installation of a new water main to the pump building to be used during periods of insufficient rainfall; installation of an electric service; and installation of an internet connection in order to monitor the pump operation remotely.

 

SWP has committed to fund the entire construction of the Cistern Project.  They have also committed to monitoring and maintaining the new facility.

 

Dustin Construction Inc. (the Contractor currently in charge of the renovation of Marshall High School) has submitted a proposed change order in the amount of $2,057,460.

 

To-date, SWP has transferred a total of $2,072,175 to D&C to fund this project.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent, or Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute the Storm Water Enhancement Project Contract Change Order on behalf of the School Board, as detailed in the agenda item, and as shown on the County approved site plan to Dustin Construction Inc. for the amount of $2,057,460.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
