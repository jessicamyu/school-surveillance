Resolution of the Fairfax County School Board to Condemn Anti-Asian Racism and Commit to Action

 

WHEREAS, since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, racial shunning, harassment, discrimination, violence, threats of violence, and acts of discrimination targeting Asian and Asian-American community, particularly impacting women, children, seniors and Asian owned businesses have increased throughout the country by 150% over the past year, resulting in the tragic loss of life in Atlanta this week, and

 

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools has over 184,000 students and almost 20% identify as Asian, Asian American or Asian Pacific Islander; Fairfax County Public Schools is committed to global citizenship and a caring culture – where all students and staff deserve access to the benefits of a public education without fear of hate based violence in the classroom, stands against the hate and bigotry against all our students, staff, families and community; and

 

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools maintains and enforces a racial equity policy; gender inclusion policy; an equal opportunity/non-discrimination policy; an anti-bullying policy; and an anti-harassment, discrimination, violence, and retaliation policy, which are applicable to the entire District community by way of incorporation into staff expectations and the Student Rights and Responsibilities Handbook; and

 

WHEREAS, the school system provides educators with the expectations, resources, and support to provide instruction to students in digital citizenship, bullying prevention and specific lessons on coronavirus stereotypes, fears and racism; and will continue to commit resources to educate its employees and identify resources to ensure curriculum and equity trainings are inclusive of Asia/API communities; and

 

WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Public Schools continues to engage in outreach to members of the API community to explain how to file a complaint of discrimination with FCPS and encourage these communities to utilize the resources of FCPS for anyone who has been discriminated against in FCPS.

 

THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board condemns violence and discrimination targeting the Asian and Asian-American community; and rejects any language that associates the ongoing public health crisis with a particular national or ethnic group, recognizing that discriminatory language is counterproductive to defeating a virus that observes no national or ethnic boundaries; and be it

 

RESOLVED, that the School Board reaffirms and proactively implements all policies, practices and procedures including working with public and private institutions/organizations working to address xenophobia and racism to ensure that our API students, staff, families and community are not subjected to bias, harassment, discrimination, violence or retaliation.

Autism Awareness Month Recognition

 

April is National Autism Awareness Month and the fourteenth annual World Autism Awareness Day is April 2. Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication skills. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because each child is different and is affected in unique ways, with many strengths and challenges. Students with autism attend many of our schools in Fairfax County. The Autism Society of America recognizes that the prevalence of autism in the United States has risen from 1 in 125 children in 2010, to 1 in 59 in 2020. National Autism Awareness Month provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to increase awareness and acceptance of autism and celebrate the unique aspects and talents of all people. The Autism Society’s “Celebrate Differences” campaign, which focuses on providing information and resources for communities to be more aware of autism, promote acceptance, and encourage increased community partnerships with businesses and organizations dedicated to building more inclusive experiences.

WHEREAS, we recognize that inequities in resources, including educational settings and buildings, supplies, and technology impedes student learning and it is our responsibility to directly address these inequities;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, The Fairfax County School Board recognizes March 2021, as VIRGINIA SCHOOL BOARDS ASSOCIATION EQUITY IN EDUCATION MONTH in Fairfax County, and we  call this observance to the attention of all our citizens and residents.

Key Points:

On March 4, 2021, the School Board was presented three calendar options for School Year 2021-22. These calendars included input from a calendar committee composed of representatives from a variety of internal and external constituent groups which included community religious organization representatives, teachers, employees and principal association representatives, along with central office staff. Additionally, the calendar committee received input and feedback from the recent Religious Observance Task Force.

 

Two calendar drafts, Version A and Version B, were developed by the calendar committee. These draft calendar versions were originally presented to the School Board on June 18, 2020.

 

Draft calendar Version C was presented to the School Board February 2, 2021, which was developed to more closely mirror the current school year calendar.

 

On March 2, 2021, the School Board passed a motion to direct the Superintendent “to develop a new calendar option, to account for but not limited to:

  • legal considerations
  • instructional concerns and disruption
  • student wellness, both socio-emotional and academic
  • support staff pay
  • absenteeism data
  • operational disruption
  • staff days off and planning time
  • survey preferences
  • transparency and community climate
  • any and all equity considerations
  • inclusiveness
  • consider use of two floating holidays that could be used for religious observances or cultural celebrations​​​​​​”

On March 4, 2021, the School Board passed a follow-on motion to direct the Superintendent to “to identify religious and culturally significant days that could result in significant absenteeism and create regulations that do not currently exist for the manner in which FCPS will designate impermissible activities on said days to prevent negative impact upon the academic, social or emotional well-being of those students who engage in such observances.”

An additional draft calendar, Version D, is included for School Board consideration. This calendar was designed to respond to the School Board’s motion on March 2, 2021 and follow-on motion on March 4, 2021.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board adopt calendar D, as recommended by the Superintendent, for School Year 2021-22.

That the School Board approve the Pathway to the Baccalaureate Program Agreement with Northern Virginia Community College, effective July 1, 2020 through June 30, 2025, and authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Michelle Pratt, director, Office of Procurement Services

Lynette Henry, manager, College Success Programs

 

Meeting Category:  March 18, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan Goal 4:  Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Office of Counseling and College and Career Readiness recommends that the School Board award a contract for the Pathway to Baccalaureate Program to Northern Virginia Community College.

 

The Program uses a three-step, student-centered approach to increase access to higher education for underrepresented students. Step one is to prepare students for a successful transition to college by providing one-on-one meetings with a Pathway counselor to develop individualized transition plans. Step two is to support students while in college through continued one-on-one counseling, academic advising, and academic and leadership skill building. Step three includes customized degree planning, assistance with the transfer process, and continued access to program supports at George Mason University.

 

This contract provides four NVCC counselors to FCPS for the purpose of implementing the Pathway to the Baccalaureate program at 16 participating FCPS high schools.

 

Policy 5011.4 requires that the School Board approve any contract or contract amendment between the School Board and another government entity that exceeds $250,000.

 

The Pathway to the Baccalaureate is a multi-year, regional program supported by NVCC, George Mason University, and other participating local school divisions. The 2020-21 school year will be the 14th year of our partnership and participation in the Pathway program. Since 2005, this program has increased from four FCPS high schools with approximately 265 students to 16 high schools and approximately 1,300 students.

 

FCPS expenditures for this contract will not exceed $300,000 for the 2020-2021 school year. The contract includes a provision to adjust the funding if positions are not fully staffed during the term of the agreement.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Pathway to the Baccalaureate Program Agreement with Northern Virginia Community College, effective July 1, 2020 through June 30, 2025, and authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Subject: School Board Notice 3310 - Recommendation to rescind Policy 3310 and Regulation 3310, Focus School Initiative and Implementation (K-12)

 

School Board Action Required: New Business

 

Key Points:

Policy 3310 and Regulation 3310—Focus School Initiative and Implementation (K-12) are rescinded because the focus school initiative, which originally included an application process and funding, is no longer supported at the division level. There have been no new schools apply since 2009. At the time of this notice, only two schools of the fifteen schools selected, over the period of 1998-2003, continue to utilize their focus as part of their school identity. Those schools could choose to continue that practice without a need for policy.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board rescind Policy 3310 and Regulation 3310-- Focus School Initiative and Implementation (K-12)
