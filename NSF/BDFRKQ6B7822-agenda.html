School Nurse Day - May 26, 2020

School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.  This annual event recognizes the contributions that school nurses make every day to improve the health and success of our nation’s children.  This year’s theme, “School Nurses:  Better Health. Better Learning,” reflects the significant roles that school nurses have in health care in their school communities, as well as in modeling health and resiliency.

School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health, safety, prevention, and wellness; intervene with actual and potential health problems; and provide case management services.  They are an invaluable bridge connecting health care providers, public health, and families.

The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognized May 26 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

 

National Children’s Mental Health Awareness Day – May 7, 2020

 

The well-being of children has a profound, immediate and lasting impact on our community. Nationally over one in five children and youth have a mental health issue and only half of children and youth with behavioral health issues receive treatment.

 

Fairfax County Public Schools is a partner with county agencies and non-profit organizations in the Healthy Minds Fairfax initiative to increase awareness of signs of mental distress and to address barriers to accessing services through the coordination of prevention, early intervention, and treatment services.


The School Board is pleased to recognize May 7, 2020, as National Children’s Mental Health Awareness Day in Fairfax County. This day is observed each year to raise awareness about the importance of children’s mental health and how vital positive mental health is to a child’s development. This is an opportunity to celebrate the work of our school division to increase awareness of the connection between mental health and social, emotional, and psychological well-being. Our students are contributing to this increased awareness through their work on the Student Advisory Council and through participation in wellness clubs and activities in their schools.

Recognition of National Gun Violence Awareness Day – June 5, 2020

 

The first Friday in June is recognized as National Gun Violence Awareness Day. Also known as Wear Orange Day, it was first organized by high school students in Chicago to memorialize the 2013 death of their friend and gun violence victim, 15-year-old Hadiya Pendleton. After her death, they asked us to stand up, speak out, and Wear Orange to raise awareness about gun violence. That simple call to action has grown into a national movement.

 

In the US, 3062 children and teens die by guns every year.  Firearms are the leading cause of death among children and teens in the United States.  One tragic example comes from our own community. In November 2015, Edison High School graduate Joe Bose was a victim of a senseless and random act of gun violence in Norfolk, Virginia where he attended Hampton University.

 

Every student deserves to learn and grow in an environment that is safe. Sadly, the threat of violence for students – including gun violence – has grown across the country. National Gun Violence Awareness Day is used to raise awareness of the issue and the importance of preventing and eliminating gun violence in schools and communities.

 

Millions are coming together to fight for an end to gun violence in America. The School Board recognizes the effort to bring more awareness and remembrance to the victims of gun violence by those wearing orange on June 5 for National Gun Violence Awareness Day.

FCPS Eco-Schools

 

The FCPS Get2Green program began in 2009 to support student-led and school-based environmental stewardship in our schools. It has grown to have support from most departments across FCPS including Food and Nutrition, Facilities and Maintenance, and Design and Construction.  Much of this work is carried out through Get2Green’s Ignite partnership with the National Wildlife Federation (NWF). NWF’s Eco-Schools USA program provides a framework for student-led action on environmental topics or pathways. Students at 136 Eco-Schools in FCPS have conducted audits and developed action plans to improve the sustainability of their schools on such environmental pathways as Consumption and Waste, Energy, Schoolyard Habitat, and Sustainable Food.

 

Today I am proud to have the school board recognize the teachers, staff, and students in FCPS schools who have earned awards from Eco-Schools by taking action to improve the sustainability of their schools and communities while developing Portrait of a Graduate skills. We are pleased to recognize the schools that have earned Green Flags and Silver awards for addressing multiple sustainability pathways through Eco-Schools.

FCPS High School Voter Registration Challenge
 
The FCPS High School Voter Registration Challenge was created to encourage students to register to vote. Voting is a cornerstone of our democracy and providing access to voter registration prepares students to exercise their voting rights. Students who are registered voters will be able to participate in decision-making at the local, state, and federal level.

Summary/Background (Key Points): FY 2021 Approved Budget

 

FY 2021 Advertised Budget

On February 6, 2020, the School Board adopted the FY 2021 Advertised Budget that included a requested increase of $89.7 million, or 4.2 percent in the county transfer.  Overall, the FY 2021 Advertised Budget showed a net increase of $172.2 million, or 5.8 percent, over the FY 2020 Approved Budget.

 

Impact of County Budget

On February 25, 2020 the Fairfax County Executive presented the FY 2021 Advertised Budget Plan and recommended a transfer increase of $85.5 million, or 4.0 percent, for school operations. The proposed transfer was $4.2 million less than FCPS requested in the advertised budget, as the county anticipated that FCPS would be able to meet the needs through increased funding from the state.

 

Due to the changing economic conditions resulting from the Coronavirus Pandemic (COVID-19), it was necessary to revise the FY 2021 Advertised Budget.

 

On May 12, 2020, the County Board of Supervisors adopted the revised budget proposal.  The revised budget proposal reduced the transfer increase to the School Operating Fund from $85.5 million to $7.3 million.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

On Monday, April 13, 2020, Governor Northam released his amendments to the 2020-2022 biennial budget. The Governor’s amendments are largely in response to the changing state general fund revenue conditions resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic since the budget was adopted by the General Assembly in mid-March. 

 

Based on the amendments adopted by the General Assembly at the Reconvened Session on April 22, 2020, state aid is decreasing by $4.8 million.

Summary/Background:

Staff recommends that the School Board continue to lease Food and Nutrition Services (FNS) 30,000 square feet of office/warehouse space, located in Springfield near the Capitol Beltway and known as the Energy Zone Warehouse.

 

FNS administers USDA child nutrition programs that provide a variety of healthy food choices, enhancing educational readiness, and student success concurrently with the FCPS portrait of a graduate. FNS operates within established government regulations while remaining financially self-supporting.

 

The Energy Zone FNS central office/warehouse leased facility is part of the food and nutrition service distribution and delivery system to serve students at FCPS schools and centers. The separate Woodson complex location, Food Service Center warehouse facility is FCPS-owned. FNS serves nutritious breakfasts, lunch, SACC snacks, Super Snack supper meals, and Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Program snacks daily throughout the school year; while operating the USDA Summer Food Service Program to offer nutrition support year-round during the summer months.

 

School system warehouse needs are projected to remain constant. Neither FCPS nor Fairfax County facilities are available to meet this need. This warehouse facility has been under lease by FCPS since 1989. The FNS warehouse is strategically located adjacent to FCPSs Forte Support Center warehouse and collaterally provides support to the Office of Transportation by use of its parking lot for bus parking for the Bus Academy located in that center.
