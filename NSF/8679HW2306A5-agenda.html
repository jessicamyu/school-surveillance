HUMAN RESOURCES: The Superintendent will recruit, select, and retain a high quality and diverse workforce that enables FCPS to deliver a world-class education to every student in an environment characterized by high staff morale and loyalty to the FCPS mission and goals and in compliance with relevant laws and regulations.

Staff Contact: Peter Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services

Meeting Type: April 20, 2009 - Work Session No. 96

SUBJECT: Grading Scale

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      A School Board’s decision in January stated the expectation that FCPS have a variation of a
      ten-point grading scale. Without diminishing standards for passing a course, the School Board wanted to see gradations in the scale to recognize different levels of performance within a given letter grade. A variation of the College Board grading scale is recommended at this time.

KEY POINTS:
      A whitepaper has been written to provide an historical perspective of grading, a review of the issues surrounding current practices, and recommendations for future directions. This has been shared with principals, and they have been asked to provide feedback from their teachers.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board accepts the recommendation to use the grading scale that is a variation of the College Board’s scale.
