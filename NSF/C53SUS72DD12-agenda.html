Summary/Background (Key Points): FY 2023 Approved Budget

 

FY 2023 Advertised Budget

On February 24, 2022, the School Board adopted the FY 2023 Advertised Budget. The budget totaled $3.3 billion, a decrease of $78.5 million, or 2.3 percent, over the FY 2022 Approved Budget. For FY 2023, funds are budgeted to prioritize investments to ensure all students receive the rigorous instruction and comprehensive supports they need to thrive.

 

Remaining School Board Meetings to discuss the FY 2023 Approved Budget are as follows:

 

DATE           ACTIVITY

May 10        School Board Conducts Budget Work Session

May 12        School Board Holds Public Hearing on Budget (if needed)

May 26        School Board Adopts Approved Budget

 

County Budget Impact

The County Executive presented the FY 2023 Advertised Budget Plan on February 22, 2022, which included a School Operating Fund transfer increase of $112.6 million, 5.2 percent, as compared to FY 2022. The Board of Supervisors Markup of the County Executive’s FY 2023 Advertised Budget results in a transfer to schools that is $10.0 million less than requested.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The General Assembly adjourned March 12, 2022 without adopting a state budget.  The General Assembly met briefly on April 4, 2022, to convene a Special Session, for the purpose of finishing the biennial budget but did not act on any legislation and instead went immediately into recess to be recalled at an uncertain future date once budget conferees have come to consensus. The Governor had until April 11, 2022 to sign, propose amendments, or veto legislation from the General Assembly’s Regular Session.  The General Assembly will return on April 27, 2022, in a Reconvened Session to consider the Governor’s actions.   

 

The Superintendent will provide recommendations to the budget based on county and state funding levels.

 

A short presentation will be provided including potential adjustments to address the reduced level of funding and the follow-on motion.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In accordance with regulation 1708, the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee (FLECAC) convenes each school year with the purpose of advising the K-12 health and physical education and FLE curriculum coordinator on Program of Studies development and implementation. The role of the committee is to provide advice and recommendations to the curriculum coordinator as goals, objectives, media, and special instructional materials are developed.  FLECAC recommendations require approval by the School Board following a 30-day community review and comment period.  FLECAC includes members representing students, teachers, administrators, and community members (including School Board appointees, health professionals, and clergy). 

Following guidelines from the Virginia Board of Education, FCPS implements a locally developed program of instruction for Family Life Education (FLE).  A community involvement team must be used in the development of the program.  The Virginia Board of Education Guidelines and Standards of Learning for Family Life Education are used by FLECAC as a reference for recommendations of lesson objectives and descriptive statements.  Community members may also bring forward recommendations and concerns for consideration by FLECAC. In addition, some topics including but not limited to benefits, challenges, responsibilities, and value of marriage; dating violence; sexual harassment; and boundaries and consent, are legislated and must be included in FLE instruction.  FLECAC receives input from medical professionals, law enforcement, faith community representatives, and Fairfax County Government offices as appropriate when developing recommendations.

During the 2021-2022 meetings, FLECAC addressed concerns brought forward by School Board members, committee members, and teachers.  FLECAC considered moving from gender separate instruction to gender combined instruction in elementary and middle school, new media to support puberty instruction in elementary, and removal of media in grade 10.  In addition, the committee reviewed the current objectives and descriptive statements related to consent, reviewed input and recommendations from several sources, and began discussion of revision of this topic across grade levels. 

Summary of FLECAC 2021-2022 Recommendations:

  • Adopt gender combined instruction for the Human Growth and Development unit in grades 4-8.
  • Remove the testicular self-exam video from grade 10 lesson [lesson1, Health Awareness and Self Examinations] and combine genders for the entire grade 10 lesson.
  • Revise objective for grade 10 lesson 5, Human Sexuality, to include gender.
  • Revise Human Growth and Development objectives and descriptive statements in grades 4-8 and 10 to support gender combined instruction (if approved).
  • Approve use of media Hormones, Body Odor, and Acne, Oh My!  Puberty 101 for grade 6 and grade 7.
  • Explore instruction of gender identity in elementary school and a more inclusive FLE curriculum overall.

 

Recommendation: Pursue further study and extended community review next year before Board action on recommendations
