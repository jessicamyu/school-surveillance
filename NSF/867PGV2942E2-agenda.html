National Autism Month - April 2005

The School Board is proud to support National Autism Month during April.

Autism is a severely incapacitating, lifelong developmental disability, resulting in significant impairment of a child’s ability to learn, develop healthy interactive behaviors, and understand verbal, nonverbal, and reciprocal communication.

Autism affects over 900 children in Fairfax County Public Schools.

Although a cure for Autism has not been found, children with Autism can be helped to reach their greatest potential. Accurate, early diagnosis, and the resulting appropriate education and intervention are vital to the future growth and development o the child.

Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month - May 2005

Americans of Asian and Pacific ancestry have made significant contributions to the history and development of the United States in many fields of endeavor such as science, commerce, the arts, and the humanities.

The Fairfax County School Board recognizes that an understanding of these contributions should be an integral part of the year-round education of all students.

Pride in America Month - May 2005

Our country’s founders–more than 200 years ago–established a nation of unique, diverse individuals working toward the common goal of a free and open society–a nation that has accomplished much in two centuries and of which we all can be proud.

Fairfax County Public Schools and the Fairfax County Police Department have established a mutually beneficial relationship over the past decade. One of the hallmarks of this relationship has been the School Resource Officer (SRO) program. School resource officers help to ensure a safe and peaceful learning environment for students, and provide school administrators and staff with the law enforcement resources and expertise necessary to maintain order, prevent truancy, and enforce rules and codes of student conduct.

This school year the School Board established the Distinguished School Resource Officer Award to annually recognize the efforts of outstanding SROs. The criteria for the award include:

· Establishing positive relationships with students and staff
· Enhancing curriculum through classroom participation
· Developing and implementing safety programs
· Demonstrating commitment to the school community
· Developing innovative solutions to unique safety and security issues
· Acting with valor
· Other contributions that merit recognition

There were a total of nine School Resource Officers nominated by their principals. These nominees are stellar examples of the outstanding officers working in our schools. They should be very proud of their nomination. The nominees are:

Office Paul Marinero, Liberty Middle School
Officer Marvin Goodley, Sandburg Middle School
Officer Anthony Janowski, Key Middle School
Officer Rudolph Coffield, Lee High School
Officer Ilsa Blaney, Thoreau Middle School
Officer Tracy Sontheimer, Holmes Middle School
Officer Dave Tipton, South Lakes High School
Officer Robert Miller, Whitman Middle School
Master Police Officer David Poach, Langley High School

The Fairfax County School Board is pleased to award the 2005 Distinguished School Resource Officer Award to Master Police Officer David A. Poach, of Langley High School. MPO Poach has been an SRO for eleven years and has been recognized by his peers and the community for his outstanding contributions and commitment to Langley High School. He has received awards for his work with the Safe Community Coalition of McLean for his work on the Teen Summit and Teen Drinking issues; he was instrumental in working with the community to eliminate graffiti and apprehend those responsible; MPO Poach volunteers to speak with parent groups about teen driving, substance abuse and criminal mischief; he presents classes to students on criminal and traffic laws; as well as perform all of the routine SRO duties at Langley High School Clearly, MPO Poach is an exemplary SRO and is a fitting awardee for the first Distinguished School Resource Officer Award to be presented.

Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. The speakers list is full. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    5.01 School Bond Referendum – Adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 8, 2005, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $244,326,000 (FTS; presented as new business 4/14/05))
    Meeting
    Apr 28, 2005 - Regular Meeting No. 18
    Category
    5. Other Action Items - 8:05 p.m.
    Type
    Action
    Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

    SUBJECT: Proposed 2005 School Bond Referendum

    RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 8, 2005, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $244,326,000.
    EXPLANATION:
        On January 27, 2005, the School Board adopted the FY2006-2010 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the coming five-year period. Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period.
          Voter authorization to issue bonds is needed in November 2005 to provide construction funds for one new elementary school, planning funds for two new elementary schools, modular additions to two existing schools, and site acquisition for future school needs.

          The recommended 2005 referendum would provide funds to renovate older general education facilities, including eight elementary schools and one high school. The proposed referendum also includes funds to plan the renovation of eight elementary schools, one middle school, and one high school. These planning funds will prepare the school system to accelerate the pace of renovations at such time as additional capital improvement cash flow is approved by the Board of Supervisors.
            The proposed referendum also includes funds to continue significant upgrades of technology networks in our school facilities. Additional allowances have been made in the referendum for the following: roof, boiler and air conditioning replacement; Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) improvements; asphalt repaving, and security enhancements. Finally, the proposed package includes funding for bonding expenses and the cost of directing the design and construction of projects authorized through the referendum.
              The scale and content of this referendum have been significantly influenced by two considerations: the requirement to address overcrowding, as well as, renovation and infrastructure needs countywide; and current limits on the county’s ability to cash-flow debt.
                To facilitate the orderly financing of identified capital improvement needs, it is recommended that the Proposed 2005 School Bond Referendum be offered for voter approval in the November 8, 2005, general election. It is believed that this proposal is a well-balanced presentation of needs countywide. The revised amount reflects the additional $1 million for site acquisition per the School Board’s direction at the April 4, 2005, Facilities and Operations work session.

                A School Board work session on this proposal was held on April 4, 2005; a public hearing and School Board action is scheduled for April 28, 2005.

                That the School Board further amend the contract for the Energy Performance Services with Noresco, LLC, dated November 8, 2002, to include improvements for a fifth group of schools, at an additional cost of $1,999,905—all the terms negotiated by school officials, with the advice of the School Board attorney; and that the School Board authorize Dean Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute the agreements and related documents for these services/improvements on behalf of the School Board.
                  EXPLANATION:

                  On November 7, 2002, the School Board awarded a contract for energy performance services to Noresco, LLC for 14 schools, referred to as Bundle No. 1 (the “Contract”). Subsequently, on May 22, 2003, the School Board approved the amendment of the Contract to add improvements for 7 additional schools, which are referred to as Bundle No. 2; on September 11, 2003, an additional 20 buildings, which are referred to as Bundle No. 3; on September 23, 2004, an additional 20 buildings, which are referred to as Bundle No. 4. Work at buildings in Bundles No. 1 and No. 2 is complete; work at buildings in Bundles No. 3 and No. 4 is proceeding satisfactorily. The contract expressly contemplates that the scope of work may be modified to include additional schools upon terms mutually agreeable to the parties.

This contract amendment, like previous amendments to add school Bundles, will be based on a detailed energy audit for energy performance services that encompass lighting retrofits and energy management controls. Staff may recommend that additional schools are included in future Bundles.
                      At the Fairfax County School Board meeting of July 22, 2004, $2 million was allocated to fund Bundle No. 5; therefore, this bundle will be “self-funded” and will not require third party financing.
