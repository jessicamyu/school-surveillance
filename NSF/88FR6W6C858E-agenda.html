Subject:                    Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations Monitoring Report      

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision 

 

Related To:               Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations

 

Key Points:

 

The Accountability function has met and/or exceeded its operational expectations (OE), and in doing so expanded the scope and impact of the Department by, among other accomplishments:  produced three Multi-Year Plans that reflect programs, projects, and initiatives established in its evaluation agenda; published the Online Campus Year One Executive and Box Score Reports; completed a Program Exploratory Evaluation Report (PEER) for eCART; completed a Program Development Report for the Homebound/Home-based Instruction program; and, drafted a Program Evaluation Design for eCART.  In addition, this OE report describes the expanded scope of PLA work based on new division priorities, including: A Framework for SOAR (School Overview Achievement Report) Baseline and Annual Monitoring Reports that describes the set of data that will be provided to Priority Schools on conditions that research suggests have the greatest impact on student achievement; an Annual Summary Achievement Report that tables SOL achievement for each Priority School; and a Framework for Project Management Oversight Committee (PMOC) projects identifying critical areas that should be addressed by PMOC project evaluation plans and a process for assuring the adequacy of those plans.

 

The Internal Audit function has also met and/or exceeded its operational expectations by, among other accomplishments: securing the Internal Audit Committee’s approval of the Annual Audit Plan on June 24, 2010; securing the School Board’s approval of the Annual Audit Plan on July 22, 2010; presenting the Quarterly Status Report to the Audit Committee on June 15, 2010; and posting all completed audit reports to the Internal Audit website and simultaneously making public announcements of the reports’ availability.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations Monitoring Report.

Meeting Category:      October 18, 2010 – Work Session No. 32

 

Subject:                       FY 2012 Budget Development  

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                       

 

Key Points:


In July 2010, the School Board was presented with the School Operating Fund fiscal forecast for FY 2012-FY 2016.  The forecast included all key financial areas that drive the budget development process, such as revenue assumptions and expenditure assumptions, including employee salaries and benefits, student membership, and instructional initiatives.  An updated version of the forecast for FY 2012 is posted under the Budget agenda item 3.04, Fiscal Forecast.

 

Since the forecast was developed, the Education Jobs Fund program was initiated, and a summary of the information presented at the September work session is posted under the Budget agenda item 3.03, Education Jobs Fund.  This federal program provides $10 billion in assistance to states to save or create education jobs for the 2010-2011 school year.  Funds remaining after the 2010-2011 school year may be used through September 30, 2012.  Jobs funded under this program include those that provide educational and related services for early childhood, elementary, and secondary education.  FCPS will receive $21.3 million from the Education Jobs Fund program, and options and recommendations for expending this funding will be discussed and are posted under the Budget agenda item 3.03 Education Jobs Fund.  To clarify the positions impacted by the stimulus funding cliff, a listing of positions funded through the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act is posted under the Budget agenda item 3.03, Education Jobs Fund.

 

To provide detailed data by program for FY 2012 budget discussions, the FY 2011 Program Budget is now available at http://www.fcps.edu/fs/budget/documents/approved/2011/FY2011ProgramBudget.pdf. The FY 2011 Program Budget presents the total resources allocated to each FCPS program or support activity including detailed expenditures, positions, offsetting revenue, net cost to the School Operating Fund, grant revenue, Student Achievement Goal and sub-goal that each program supports, program contacts, list of mandates, related outcome measures, program description, method of service provision, and  explanation of cost.  For FY 2011, the program budget now includes activities that occur in all of FCPS’ Governmental Funds.  An overview and comparison to how data is presented in the approved budget is provided in the introduction section, and the summary charts included in Instructional and Divisionwide Support sections of the program budget are posted under Budget agenda item 3.05, Program Budget Summary.  As a reference, the Reasonable Interpretation of School Board Budget/Program Review Criteria updated by the School Board at the July retreat is posted under the Budget agenda item 3.04.

3.06 Budget Question Guidelines
Meeting
Oct 18, 2010 - All-day Work Sessions Nos. 30-33
Category
3. WS No. 32 - Budget - 1 p.m.
Type
Discussion

As we all know, our central administration staff has been cut over the past two years, and our finance and budget offices in particular will be further impacted this year with the continuing work on our ERP systems.

 

As a result, I am recommending that this year we triage our budget questions.  I would ask that each Board member rate each budget question in the following way:

 

Priority One:     This question represents information that I, as a Board Member, need to make an informed decision on the budget, or to propose an amendment.

 

Priority Two:     This is a question which has been asked by a member of the community. 

 

Priority Three:   This is information I would like to have, but it will not impact major decisions on the budget.

 

Each and every budget question will be answered, but staff will place their emphasis on answering questions listed as priority number one first. 

 

The others will be answered as time permits, with the understanding that all questions will be answered as quickly as possible.

 

School Board members will rank their own questions, but the Budget staff may contact School Board members to clarify the ranking if there are any questions.

 

In addition, our budget staff will consolidate all budget questions and answers from the last three years and will provide a general index for all of those questions.  If your question can be answered by a prior response, staff will point you to the prior answer.  If that answer does not provide all the information you need, you will be able to ask further clarifying questions.

 

Tessie Wilson, Budget Chairman
