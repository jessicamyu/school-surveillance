Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2019

 

The American Alliance for Theater and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development.  The theme is "Fostering a Culture of Creativity".  Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.  

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2019

 

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  This year's theme is "All Music, All People!" and is designed to raise awareness of the importance of music education for all children and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music.  It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages. 

 

Youth Art Month - March 2019

 

Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.  The theme for Youth Art Month 2019 is “Your Art, Your Story.” 

The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

 

National Sleep Awareness Week - March 10-16, 2019

 

 

The National Sleep Foundation is celebrating its annual Sleep Awareness Week March 10-16, 2019, to raise awareness of the health benefits of sleep and its importance to safety and productivity. This year's theme "Begin with Sleep" highlights the importance of good sleep health for individuals to best achieve their personal, family, and professional goals.  The National Sleep Foundation believes the value of a good night's sleep which carries in to one's day to improve not just health, but mood, productivity, well-being and overall quality of life.   Most of us know that getting a good night’s sleep is important, but too few of us actually make those eight or so hours a priority. So many of us have forgotten what “being really, truly rested” feels like. To further complicate matters, stimulants like coffee and energy drinks, alarm clocks, and external lights, including those from electronic devices, interfere with our natural sleep/wake cycle.

 

Sleep needs vary across ages and are especially impacted by lifestyle and health. To determine how much sleep you need, it's important to examine what lifestyle factors are affecting the quality and quantity of your sleep such as work schedules and stress. For more information on healthy sleep, visit the National Sleep Foundation’s website at Sleep.org.

3.04 Recognition of Joe McDonald, Selected as a Virginia Most Outstanding Crossing Guard
Meeting
Mar 7, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type

On February 13, Fairfax County Public Schools participated in Crossing Guard Appreciation Day, a statewide event, as a way to say thank you to those individuals who help students cross the street safely on their way to and from school.  We are grateful for the 80 crossing guards who manage 174 school-related crossings every day.  Crossing guards, who are actually employees of the Fairfax County Police Department, do so much for our students and families and are often among the first adults to interact with students each day.  We can’t thank them enough for their daily efforts to watch over and protect our students.

 

Tonight, I am pleased to recognize Mr. Joe McDonald, who was recently selected as one of Virginia’s Most Outstanding Crossing Guards in 2018.  He is one of six crossing guards to receive this honor.  Joe McDonald is a familiar sight to many in Fairfax County Public Schools as he performs his duties flawlessly and greets students every day at Coates Elementary School. 

 

As you may know, Coates ES has a large population of students who walk to school, so Joe’s post is a busy one.  Additionally, Joe works on a regular basis as a crossing guard at Langston Hughes MS and Hutchison ES, and fills in as needed at South Lakes HS.  Like other dedicated crossing guards, Joe McDonald can be found at his post in all kinds of weather, helping children to cross the street safely, assisting parents with negotiating the kiss and ride areas, and managing the buses.  Joe has been a crossing guard for 8 years now and has built relationships with parents, teachers, administrators, and students during that time.  He says he finds the job to be very rewarding because it gives him an opportunity to get to know people and to develop a bond and trust with the students and parents.  Joe McDonald is very much an integral part of the community, giving of himself in his spare time.  In fact, he is part of the mentoring program at Coates ES, where he mentors two sets of boys at Coates ES on Tuesdays and two sets of girls at Hutchison ES. 

 

To keep himself busy between his morning and afternoon crossing responsibilities, Joe McDonald volunteers on Wednesdays in the Fair Oaks Hospital post-surgery care unit.  You can find Joe volunteering with the City of Fairfax Fire Department where he helps with administrative chores on Thursdays and with bingo on Fridays.  With all that he is doing, Joe manages to save some of his time and energy on Mondays for ice hockey lessons so that he can keep young and stay in shape. Bravo!

 

Please join me in congratulating Joe McDonald on being selected as a Virginia Most Outstanding Crossing Guard.  He is truly deserving of this honor and of our thanks for keeping a watchful eye on our students.  He is a fine example of a caring, contributing member of our community.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

FCPS implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, a variety of stakeholders (School Board, Leadership Team, principals, teachers, students, and the community) provided input to prioritize the outcomes in each of the four goal areas.

 

The result of this work is a streamlined Strategic Plan with eight areas of focus for school years 2018-2020. These areas of focus have specific strategic actions to move the Division toward its goals. Each strategic action has targeted metrics to show the Division’s current baseline data, an interim target to achieve by school year 2020, and an aspirational statement that describes what success would look like for students, families, employees, and the community.

 

At this work session, the Board will have an opportunity to review enhancements that have been made to the Strategic Plan since the November 19, 2018, School Board meeting.

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Virginia Department of Education revised the Standards of Learning for English in 2017. The Secondary Language Arts team in Instructional Services has been revising curriculum documents to align with these new standards and providing professional development for teachers and administrators about the revised SOLs which will be fully implemented in the 2019-2020 school year.

 

The current high school language arts textbooks were purchased in 2006. School have been utilizing these texts and supplementing with novels as well as print and digital works available through various sources. To provide equitable resources for students in all high school language arts courses, Instructional Services began the process for new basal instructional materials as defined in FCPS Regulation 3004-Adopted Basal Instructional Materials in 2018.

 

A timeline of the process for the language arts basal resource review can be found in attachment A. In October 2018, the School Board approved the committee members to review and recommend basal instructional materials for high school language arts. The Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee convened in November and December to review resources for high school language arts that were submitted by vendors in response to an RFP for consideration as basal instructional materials for high school language arts. In accordance with the Code of Virginia, regulations of the Virginia Board of Education, and Fairfax County Public Schools regulations, the Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee in conjunction with the Selection Advisory Committee and the Technical Advisory Committee recommends the titles listed in Attachment B for adoption at the division level. Additionally, a summary of the committee rationales for the recommended materials and community input received are provided in attachments C and D.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the titles listed for high school language arts as recommended by the Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee and the Selection Advisory Committee.
