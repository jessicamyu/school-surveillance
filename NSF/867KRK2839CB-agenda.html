    5.03 Amendment to ERFC and ERFC 2001 - Approve proposed Amendment Number 12 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of (A) clarifying the ERFC formula for service-connected death benefits, (B) adopting technical corrections to the ERFC plan language regarding limits on the combined ERFC/VRS benefit payable to members at retirement, and (C) conforming both ERFC and ERFC 2001 to Internal Revenue Service Regulations on Payment of Joint and Survivor Benefits (ERFC; presented as new business 6/8/06)
    Meeting
    Jun 22, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 22
    Category
    5. Consent Agenda - 10 p.m.
    Type
    Action (Consent)
    1
    Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

    SUBJECT: Amendment Number 12 to ERFC and ERFC 2001

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve proposed Amendment Number 12 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan
    (ERFCand ERFC 2001)for the purpose of (A) clarifying the ERFCformula for service-connected death benefits, (B) adopting technical corrections to the ERFCplan language regarding limits on the combined ERFC/VRS benefit payable to members at retirement, and (C) conforming both ERFCand ERFC 2001to Internal Revenue Service Regulations on Payment of Joint and Survivor Benefits.

    A summary of the proposed amendments in each category is provided below:

    A. Clarifying the ERFC formula for service-connected death benefits.
        Death from service-connected causes has been extremely rare for ERFCmembers. In the course of updating the members’ handbook, an ambiguity in the formula for calculating those benefits was discovered. The proposed amendment will correct that ambiguity.
    B. Adopting technical corrections to the ERFCplan language regarding limits on the combined ERFC/VRS benefit payable to members at retirement.
        The ERFC plan includes a limit on the combined annual benefit that the member can receive at retirement from ERFCand VRS. Four separate sections describe that limit in different ways. These provisions need clarification with respect to their interaction and how the limit is applied to optional forms of benefits that are available to members who have service credit for periods before July 1, 1988, to limits applicable to members selecting a level lifetime benefit, and to the 3 percent increase that was adopted when ERFC pensions became subject to Virginia income tax. The proposed amendment clarifies the application of the limit and eliminates Plan provisions that provide for overlapping limits.
    C. Conforming to Internal Revenue Service Regulations on Payment of Joint and Survivor Benefits (Option A and Option B)
        IRS regulations limit the amount of benefits that can be paid to survivors following a member’s death. In order to comply with these regulations with minimum administrative burden and expense, the proposed amendments would limit the 100 percent survivor benefit (Option A) to spouses and to former spouses if required by a court’s domestic relations order. The 50 percent survivor benefit would remain available for other beneficiaries, as specified in proposed Section 4.06(a)(7) of ERFCand Section 4.03(a)(5) of ERFC 2001.
