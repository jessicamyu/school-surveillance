National Safety Month - June 2017
 

Each June, the National Safety Council sponsors National Safety Month  This year’s theme, Safe for Life, reminds us that safety is no accident, it is a choice we need to make throughout our lives as we're all empowered to make safe decisions for ourselves and those we care about.

 

 Over 186,000 FCPS students depend on school employees to provide a safe environment in schools.  Prevention, identification, and abatement of hazards are integral parts of every employee’s job. The FCPS Office of Safety and Security has established emergency plans and provided appropriate training for employees to cultivate safety throughout the school district.

National Gun Violence Awareness Day - June 2, 2017

 

National Gun Violence Awareness Day is sponsored by the National Academy of Pediatrics and the National PTA among dozens of other groups. This day of awareness is acknowledged nationwide by a movement to Wear Orange in honor of victims and survivors of gun violence.  High school students in Chicago began the Wear Orange Campaign in 2013 on the birthday of their friend and gun violence victim, 15-year-old Hadiya Pendleton. Hadiya was killed in a Chicago park just after taking final exams.  

 

On average, 48 children and teens are shot and 7 die from their injuries each day in the United States. One tragic example comes from our own community. In November 2015, Edison High School graduate Joe Bose was a victim of a senseless and random act of gun violence in Norfolk, Virginia where he attended Hampton University. 

 

The color orange symbolizes the value of human life. In 2013, teens on the South Side of Chicago asked classmates to honor their murdered friend by wearing orange. That simple call to action has grown into a national movement – and orange is becoming the symbol of gun safety.

Millions are coming together to fight for an end to gun violence in America. The School Board recognizes the effort to bring more awareness and remembrance to the victims of gun violence by those wearing orange on June 2 for National Gun Violence Awareness Day.

Key Points:

 

FY 2018 Advertised Budget

On February 9, 2017, the School Board adopted the Advertised Budget for the School Operating Fund totaling $2.8 billion. This is an increase of $135.1 million, or 5.1 percent, over the FY 2017 Approved Budget. The advertised budget was presented to and discussed by the School Board and County Board of Supervisors at a joint budget work session on February 21, 2017.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The Virginia General Assembly’s adopted 2016-2018 budget provides an increase of $4.1 million in revenue as compared to FCPS’ FY 2018 Advertised Budget.

 

County Budget Impact

On May 2, 2017, the County Board of Supervisors adopted their FY 2018 budget that included an increase in the transfer to the School Operating Fund of 2.79 percent above the FY 2017 Approved Budget. The increase of $53.4 million is $59.1 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget.

 

May Budget Work Session

As presented at the May 1, 2017, work session, the projected shortfall in the FY 2018 Advertised Budget is $46.7 million, after accounting for the state and county budget adjustments and the impact from the FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
