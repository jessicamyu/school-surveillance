Autism Awareness Month - April 2013

 

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.


Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about autism. It is sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA). Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

 

 

Mathematics Awareness Month - April 2013

Mathematics Awareness Month is held each year in April to increase public understanding of and appreciation for mathematics. Sponsored by the Joint Policy Board for Mathematics, the goal is to increase the visibility of mathematics as a field of study and to communicate the power and intrigue in mathematics to a larger audience.  The 2013 theme is “Mathematics of Sustainability,” which focuses on how mathematics helps people understand complex issues and how it is used in a wide range of fields to seek creative solutions for a sustainable way of life.

 National Library Week/School Library Month - April 2013

 

National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association and libraries across the country each April.  It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate.  This year, National Library Week will be observed April 14-20 with the theme, "Communities matter @ your library. ”

 

School Library Month is the American Association of School Librarians' celebration of school librarians and their programs. Every April school librarians are encouraged to create activities to help their school and local community celebrate the essential role that strong school library programs play in a student's educational career.

 

The School Board welcomes this opportunity to commend Fairfax County Public Schools’ librarians and to express its gratitude for the energy, knowledge, and vision that they tirelessly and unselfishly provide to the school system.

Recommended Action
Award the contract for the installation and commissioning of commercial grade, wastewater treatment plant improvements at Gunston Elementary School to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, American Contracting & Environmental Services, Inc., in the amount of $547,800, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board (FTS; presented as new business 3/7/13)

Staff Contact:           Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  March 21, 2013 – Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                     Award of Contract – Wastewater Treatment Plant Improvements – Gunston Elementary School

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:               School Board Policy 5011.2 Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

This project consists of the installation and pre-operation testing (known in the construction industry as “commissioning”) of a commercial grade, wastewater treatment plant (“WWTP”) for Gunston Elementary School.  For Gunston, the WWTP will perform the process of sewage treatment at that school which does not have access to public sewers.  All other FCPS schools have either access to a public sewer system or an on-site drainfield, and thus, do not require their own, individual on-site WWTP.

 

Because the installation and commissioning of such a WWTP is not considered a routine project within FCPS, staff was particularly concerned that bidders possess suitable experience in this particular type of work.  To accomplish this, the invitation to bid for this project was written to include a provision setting forth the requisite qualifications of potential contractors in accordance with the Virginia Public Procurement Act (“VPPA”).  Va. Code Ann. §2.2-4301.

 

As a result, the Instructions to Bidders for this Project required each bidder to “demonstrate to the Owner’s satisfaction that the bidder has successfully completed the installation and commissioning of at least three (3) commercial grade Wastewater Treatment Plants (WWTP) in the last ten (10) years with a minimum of one (1) being completed within the last two (2) years from the date of February 1, 2013.”  Bidders also were required to submit - - “as a part of, and at the time of, Bid Submission” such information in the form of references for prior WWTP projects, which could be checked by staff before contract award. 

 

Bids were received on February 26, 2012, and a bid tabulation is furnished below. Funding for this project will be provided from the Major Maintenance Fund. 

 

Staff reviewed the bids submitted and checked project references for the bidders.  The bid of the apparent low bidder, Caldwell and Santmyer, Inc., has been determined to be not responsive and not responsible in this instance.  Caldwell’s bid is not responsive because Caldwell failed to submit with its bid submission the required reference information (which was expressly required to be submitted “as part of, and at the time of Bid submission”).  Specifically, Caldwell submitted references for only two, not three, prior wastewater treatment plant (WWTP) projects.  One of the three references submitted was for a non-WWTP project.  In addition, one project reference submitted by Caldwell with its bid yielded negative commentary and failed to establish that Caldwell had “successfully completed the installation and commissioning” of the required number of WWTP projects in the specified timeframe.  Thus, Caldwell’s bid is neither responsive nor responsible in this instance; a determination of either (not responsive or not responsible) warrants a bidder’s disqualification from contract award under the VPPA.  The VPPA requires that where an award is made, the contract must be awarded to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder.  Va. Code Ann. §2.2-4318.

 

Accordingly, staff recommends that this contract award be made to American Contracting & Environmental Services, Inc., a contractor which has been determined to be the lowest responsive and responsible bidder on this project.

Recommendation:

 

That, for the reasons stated in this Agenda Item, the School Board award the contract for the installation and commissioning of commercial grade, wastewater treatment plant improvements at Gunston Elementary School to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, American Contracting & Environmental Services, Inc., in the amount of $547,800, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
