National Hispanic Heritage Month - September 15-October 15, 2014

 

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-American community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.  This year’s theme is “Hispanics: A legacy of history, a present of action, and a future of success.”

 

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

 

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

 

 

U. S. Constitution Day and Citizenship Day - September 17, 2014

 

Constitution Day and Citizenship Day are observed each year on September 17 to commemorate the signing of the Constitution on September 17, 1787, and “recognize all who, by coming of age or by naturalization, have become citizens.”  Recent legislation has renamed the day as Constitution Day and Citizenship Day and urges proper observance of the day. This year, the official observance day is Wednesday, September 17, and is the 226th anniversary of the nation’s founding document.

 

In observance of Constitution Day and Citizenship Day, all schools in FCPS will be observing this day with activities that recognize the adoption of the United States Constitution and those who have become citizens under the Constitution.  Schools will use a variety of curriculum resources to teach students about the importance of the Constitution and responsible citizenship.  This may relate to the School Board’s Student Achievement Goal 3. Demonstrate Responsibility to the Community and the World.  Throughout the school year, high school students will have an opportunity to participate in service learning projects related to responsible citizenship, which can lead to a Civics Seal on their diploma.

Staff Contact:         Pam Goddard, Executive Assistant and Clerk of the Board

 

Meeting Category:  September 4, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 3

 

Subject:                Strategic Governance Manual Revisions

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related to:                Strategic Governance Manual

 

Key Points:

 

Throughout the Board’s work this past year, revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual have been discussed.  Recommendations from the Governance Committee were discussed with the full Board, and there was Board agreement on these recommendations.  On July 1, 2014, FCPS was reorganized (eight clusters reduced to five regions, new positions of Chief Operating Officer, Chief Academic Officer, and Chief of Staff).  The revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual include:

 

Section 2:  Operational Expectations

  1. Added “Superintendent’s” to the OE-8 title (page 21)
  2. Added a sentence to the introductory paragraph

 

Section 3:  Board Self-Governance Policies

  1. Updated list of Board liaisons to other organizations (page 38) to add Successful Child and Youth Policy Team, remove Partnership for Youth, and rename The Foundation for Fairfax County Public Schools
  2. Updated committee staff liaisons where applicable due to FCPS reorganization (pages 40-49)
  3. Changed all citizen advisory committee membership terms from two years to one year (confirmed at the April 24, 2014, forum WS) (pages 42-49)
  4. Listed organizations that are part of a committee membership alphabetically
  5. Decreased maximum membership of ACSD from 35 to 32 (from eight clusters to five regions) (page 42)
  6. Changed membership for MSAOC (pages 44-45):
    1. Added Hispanic Educators Association as one of the four community organizations that are members of the committee
    2. Removed the Superintendent’s Business and Community Advisory Council (no longer exists) as a member of the committee
    3. Designated that two organizations—ESOL Advisory Committee and College Partnership Advisory Board—are no longer active and will be updated
    4. Decreased staff membership (from eight clusters to five regions)
    5. Decreased maximum membership from 40 to 36 members
  7. Changed membership for HRAC (pages 45-46)
  8. Added five community organizations as members of the committee:  American Association of University Women, Asian Educators Association, Community Ministry of Northern Virginia, Hispanic Educators Association, and Jewish Community Relations Council of Greater Washington
  9. Removed two community organizations as members of the committee:  the Hispanic Committee of Virginia and the Organization of Chinese Americans—Northern Virginia chapter
  10. Updated three community organization’s names: Korean Association of Virginia, High School Principals’ Association, and Boat People SOS
  11. Changed the number of members nominated by the Office of Equity and Employee Relations from six to three
  12. Changed membership for AAPAC staff nominations from clusters to regions
  13. Changed District Advisory Council Title I (DAC) purpose description from "No Child Left Behind Act of 2001" to "Elementary and Secondary Education Act" to reflect the current Federal policy and guidance, as requested by DAC (page 46)

 

Section 4:  Superintendent/Board Relationship

Added updated superintendent’s evaluation form based on new state requirements (S.2., page 52)

 

Section 5:  Appendices

  1. Clarified reimbursement for expenses related to non-political education-related events for Board members shall be authorized by Board chair (Appendix A, page 54)
  2. Updated School Board/staff communication process (Appendix B, page 62) with the current guidelines for Client Communication.
  3. Updated meeting manager responsibilities (Appendix C, page 63) to indicate that “next steps” will be taken by School Board Office staff.
  4. Updated School Board recognitions (Appendix G, page 71) to reflect current practice of individual Board members initiating a certificate of recognition for presentation.
  5. Added Client Communication process to Board member options for issue education, advocacy, or policy (Appendix J, page 76) and updated process used with Forum Topic Request form (form was included)
  6. Updated history of ad hoc committees (Appendix K, page 78-79) with Infrastructure Financing Committee (joint committee with the Board of Supervisors), Superintendent’s Evaluation Ad Hoc Committee, and Joint Budget Development Committee (joint committee with the Board of Supervisors)
  7. Added Appendix L (page 80-85) with the updated superintendent’s summative evaluation form
  8. Renumbered Revisions Notes as Appendix M (page 86-88).

 

Minor edits were included throughout the Manual for clarity and consistency of processes, including organizational changes within FCPS effective July 1, 2014 (clusters to regions, job titles, office titles, staff liaisons).  All revisions are highlighted throughout the document attached.  Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board, will be updated in October 2014 to reflect similar changes regarding these committees.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the proposed revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual in Sections 2, 3, 4, and 5, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

Staff Contact:           Division Counsel, John E. Foster

 

Meeting Category:  September 4, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 3

 

Subject:                     Tobacco-free Signage for all FCPS Fields and Playgrounds

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related to:                School Board Policy 4419, Smoke-Free Environment

 

Key Points:

 

School Board Policy 4419, Smoke-Free Environment, was adopted January 13, 1994, to prohibit smoking and the use of tobacco products in all Fairfax County Public schools, administrative offices, facilities, and FCPS vehicles. This policy authorizes the Superintendent to post signs and otherwise advise employees and the community of this policy.

 

The School Board further supports tobacco-free FCPS fields and playgrounds. Funding is available to FCPS from the Live Healthy Fairfax Transformation grant through late September 2014, which would pay for the creation and installation of tobacco-free signage for all FCPS-owned fields and playgrounds. The signs would read "Tobacco-Free Play Zone. Thank you for your cooperation. If you need help to quit smoking, call 1-800-QUIT-NOW or visit www.smokefree.gov "

 

Numerous scientific and medical studies, including those by the Centers for Disease Control, have concluded that smoking harms nearly every organ in the human body, and causes many diseases, including heart disease, lung cancer, and stroke.

 

Consensus on this issue was obtained by the School Board on July 24, 2014, at the forum work session to proceed with this effort to place signs on FCPS fields and playgrounds.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the installation of tobacco-free signage, in cooperation with the Fairfax County Park Authority and the Live Healthy Fairfax Transformation Grant, on all Fairfax County Public Schools fields and playgrounds.

Certify closed meeting compliance

In order to comply with Section 2.2-3712 (D) of the Code of Virginia, it is necessary for the Board to certify that since the Fairfax County School Board convened a closed meeting on September 4, 2014, to the best of each member’s knowledge, only public business matters lawfully exempted from open meeting requirements and only such public business matters as were identified in the motion convening the closed meeting were heard, discussed, or considered by the Board during the closed meeting.


The foregoing also shall be deemed to include the certification of each School Board member who served on a student disciplinary committee that those closed committee meetings are lawfully exempted from open meeting requirements by Section 2.2-3711(A)(2) of the Code of Virginia, and that only student disciplinary matters were heard, discussed, or considered in those closed committee meetings.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
