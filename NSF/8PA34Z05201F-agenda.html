Staff Contact:             Peter J. Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department  (ISD)    

 

Other Staff Present:  Teddi Predaris, director, Office of Language Acquisition and Title I, ISD

                                Catherine Wagner, coordinator, Title I, ISD

                                Karin Aycox, family involvement resource teacher, Title I, ISD

 

Meeting Category:    May 30, 2012 – Work Session No 129

 

Subject:                   Title I District Advisory Council Annual Report     

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:              Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points: 

 

Federal and state guidelines for parent involvement under Title I of the No Child Left Behind law require participation of parents in the design and evaluation of Title I programs.  This report gives an overview of the 2011-12 Title I District Advisory Council and Executive Board’s efforts, highlights parent involvement programs and activities in schools, reports on survey findings, and makes recommendations for 2012-13.

 

Title I District Advisory Council members have made the following recommendations:

 

  • Recommendation: Provide schools with resources and materials to develop parents’ awareness of how technology is integrated throughout the school day.
  • Recommendation: Provide schools with resources and materials to develop parents’ technology skills and Internet safety strategies.
  • Recommendation: Provide schools with resources and materials that support family engagement through the use of technology (e.g., family engagement opportunities and programs through a Title I FI monthly blog, parent workshop/coffee presentations and materials created by and made available online for schools, etc.).
  • Recommendation: Identify and include descriptions of FCPS family engagement practices with integrated technology in the Family Involvement Ideas Notebook.

Subject:                    Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC) Annual Report
 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:               Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

Key Points: 

   

 The School Board charged the HRAC to “Examine the minority student achievement gap from a human relations perspective, and then identify non-academic barriers that could be impacting the Fairfax County Public Schools efforts to close it.”

 

In response to the School Board’s charge; speakers from the community and staff were invited to share information about their efforts and findings regarding the minority student achievement gap.  In addition, committee members reviewed various sources of data compiled by local and nation experts. Committee members met in smaller work groups to examine the given subject.

Subject:                    School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) 2011-2012 Annual Report  

 

School Board Action Required:     Discussion             

 

Key Points: 

 

The School Health Advisory Committee’s (SHAC) charge for the school year 2011-2012 was “Examine the development of the Resiliency Project and other prevention and intervention services that address challenges faced by students.  Work with staff to develop a communication plan so that information about the Resiliency Project and protective factors is shared with parents and community members.”  SHAC focused on:  exploring health and school related topics that foster the health and well-being of FCPS students and staff, and staying abreast and involved with programs, policies, and ongoing discussions related to school health.

 

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board accept the School Health Advisory Committee School Year 2011-2012 annual Report
