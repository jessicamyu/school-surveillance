    RESOLUTION
    HONORING
    LILLIAN M. LOWERY

    WHEREAS, Lillian Lowery served with distinction and dignity as a teacher, an administrator, and, most recently, a cluster superintendent in Fairfax County Public Schools for 16 years, completing a total of 28 years in the field of education; and
    WHEREAS, her keen insight and educational leadership inspired administrators to strive for excellence in preparing students for the 21st century, to continue their pursuit of educational opportunities, to close the gap between minority and majority students, and to provide challenging programs for all students and acceleration for those who need them; and
    WHEREAS, having a strong appreciation for involving parents and community members in the quest for a quality education for future citizens, she served on community boards and committees—always exhibiting great poise, intellect, and cooperation as she related to the citizens. She fostered a great partnership with Fairfax City schools and was just and kind in serving the parents and students within all of Cluster VII, always governing by the policies and regulations established; and
    WHEREAS, she was dedicated to staff members and to community members, showing mutual respect, compassion, and understanding when dealing with challenging situations that required fair and honest judgment. She was committed to seeing all students succeed and to seeing their rights respected as she supported administrators with their daily interactions with parents and students. Her integrity, high expectations of herself and others, and positive spirit gained her the respect of many and the admiration of the people she encountered;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Lillian Lowery for her outstanding service to Fairfax County Public Schools and to the students and citizens of this great community.

    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    SUBJECT: FY 2006 Third-Quarter Budget Review

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve the adjustments reflected in the FY 2006 Third-Quarter Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.
    EXPLANATION:
        Included in this agenda are adjustments to set aside the $30.0 million in budgeted beginning balance included in the FY 2007 advertised budget. Of this amount, $15.0 million is from FY 2005 balances and $15.0 million is from FY 2006.

        The agenda item restores the $3.2 million borrowed from the School Board’s Flexibility Reserve as part of the FY 2006 Midyear Budget Review. Also, based on lower than projected membership growth, savings of $0.7 million is recognized for 12.1 positions that remain unallocated in the staffing reserve.

        Based on rising energy costs, the utility budget is increasing $4.6 million.

        Additional funding from the county is recognized for several new initiatives. An amount of $1.0 million will be transferred to the Grants and Self-Supporting Programs Fund for the purchase of technology equipment. This fund will also receive a transfer of $482,598 from the county to cover the cost of generators not funded by the State Homeland Security Grant program. The School Operating Fund will receive $903,331 from the county to support expansion of the middle school after-school program.

        This agenda includes position authority for an assistant superintendent position to oversee the newly established Department of Professional Learning and Training.

        The Operating Fund transfer to the Adult and Community Education Fund (ACE) is increased $0.5 million to help offset lower than projected tuition receipts in the ACE fund.

        Adjustments to other funds to recognize changes in revenues, expenditures, and grant awards are also included in this agenda item.

        A summary of grants activity is attached.

      6.04 CTEMS Annual Plan 2006-2007 - Recommendation to approve the Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) annual local education plan and the 2006-2007 Application for State and Federal Career and Technical Education funds (IS; action scheduled 3/23/06)
      Meeting
      Mar 9, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 15
      Category
      6. New Business - 9:35 p.m.
      Type
      Information


      Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services


      SUBJECT: CTEMS Annual Plan and 2006-2007 Application for Career and Technical
      Education Funds

      RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve the Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) annual local education plan and the 2006-2007 Application for State and Federal Career and Technical Education funds.
      EXPLANATION:
        The Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) document provides the Virginia Department of Education with each local education agency’s (LEA) plan for providing and implementing career and technical education instruction to both public school and adult students. The CTEMS document specifies all of the activities and programs available to students as a result of local, state, and federal funds. The CTEMS document is prepared in the preset uniform format (including assurances, charts, and schedules) as provided by the Virginia Department of Education.

        Changes to the previous 2005-2006 CTEMS document have been bolded throughout.

        Federal funding from the Carl D. Perkins Vocational Technical Act of 1998 (Perkins III), which is a continuing grant provided to all local education agencies (LEA) in the Commonwealth of Virginia, is available to assist school districts in providing elective career and technical programs for middle and high school students. Formal application for federal moneys is one component of the annual CTEMS document to the Virginia Department of Education.

        Schedule 2-B, page 51, of the CTEMS document specifies the expenditures of the Perkins federal grant in improving career and technical education programs according to the “Appropriate Use of Federal Funds” on page 67 of the document.

        Focusing on current priority needs for this school division, the CTEMS local plan continues a cycle in identifying the goals, objectives, and strategies for locally providing professional technical studies programs.

        The Career and Technical Education Committee (CTEAC) has endorsed the application.
