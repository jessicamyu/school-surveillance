School Nurse Day - May 12, 2010

School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.

School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health and safety, intervene with actual and potential health problems, and provide case management services and are dedicated individuals who work in cooperation with families, teachers, and school administrators.

The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognizes May 12 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

The winner of the 2010 Distinguished School Resource Officer award is MPO John S. Morris, Longfellow Middle School.

Fairfax County Public Schools and the Fairfax County Police Department have established a mutually beneficial relationship for more than a decade. One of the hallmarks of this relationship has been the School Resource Officer (SRO) program. SROs help ensure a safe and peaceful learning environment for students, and provide school administrators and staff with the law enforcement resources and expertise necessary to maintain order, prevent truancy, and enforce the rules and codes of student conduct.

A committee was created to establish and select the Distinguished School Resource Officer Award to annually recognize the efforts of outstanding SROs. The 2010 selection committee members are Fred Ellis, Director of the Office of Safety and Security, Scott Brabrand, Cluster IV Assistant Superintendent, and Lieutenant Ken Baine, School Liaison Commander.

Other Staff Present: Lee Ann Pender, director, Administrative Services

Meeting Category: May 6, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 19

Subject: School Board Policy 8335.1, Wireless Telecommunications Facilities

School Board Action Required: Information

Background:

School system staff has been working with Fairfax County’s Department of Planning and Zoning and the County’s Planning Commission to coordinate use of School Board-owned sites for telecommunication uses. Thus far, uses have primarily been at high schools, with a few non-high school exceptions where light poles existed previously. On January 21, 2010, FCPS staff met with the Fairfax County Planning Commission’s Policy and Procedures Committee to review a potential change in School Board policy that would allow inconspicuous poles, such as tree poles, at FCPS locations to include elementary and middle school sites. In addition, staff met with the School Board at the April 5, 2010, work session to advise the School Board of the potential benefits that could result from expansion of the FCPS monopole program. At that time the School Board directed staff to forward its recommendations for information, discussion, and action by the School Board.
Key Points:

1. Public sites, such as School Board-owned property, remain the preferred locations for new telecommunication facilities.

2. Ideally, telecommunication facilities should be constructed in areas to blend with an existing pattern of tall structures, and should be inconspicuous so as to resemble other man-made structures, such as light poles, and natural features, such as trees.

3. The FCPS monopole program generates revenues that support the instructional program, communications safety initiatives, as well as local school programs/activities.

4. FCPS staff will continue to coordinate with Fairfax County’s Department of Planning and Zoning and the County’s Planning Commission to expand the FCPS monopole program, including the introduction and implementation of telecommunication facilities at elementary and middle school sites, in order to:
                  a. Increase revenues to the school system
                  b. Increase services to the community
                  c. Enhance the instructional program by taking a critical step towards providing Internet services to all students
Recommendation:

That the School Board adopt Policy 8335.2, Wireless Telecommunications Facilities, as reviewed and discussed at the Policy work session.

Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

Other Staff Present: Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

Meeting Category: May 6, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 19
Subject: FY 2011 Approved Budget

School Board Action Required: Information

Key Points:
      In its FY 2011 Adopted Budget Plan, the BOS approved a one percent decrease in the county transfer to the school operating fund, resulting in a decrease of $98.2 million as compared to the request in the advertised budget. Final State General Assembly actions resulted in an increase of $34.2 million including State Fiscal Stabilization Funding, and revenue from the City of Fairfax decreased $1.2 million as compared to the advertised budget. All revenue adjustments combined total a reduction of $65.2 million.

      Expenditure adjustments for the school operating fund result in a net decrease in expenditures of $113.1 million. Final State General Assembly actions result in the following reductions: a decrease of $109.1 million for VRS retirement; $5.8 million for the VRS retiree health care credit; $5.7 million for state life insurance; and $0.6 million for the triennial census. Additional expenditure reductions of $1.3 million result from contract length reductions for career and technical education and academy teachers and school-based technology specialists. Expenditure increases for County retirement and life insurance require an additional $0.9 million, and funding of $3.0 million is provided for the Priority Schools Initiative which increases the funding available to serve these schools identified based on student need to a total of $4.3 million. To address changes in student membership and demographics, $5.4 million is required primarily due to an increase in the number of students eligible for free and reduced-price meals. This increase results in additional teachers provided to elementary and middle schools through FCPS’ needs-based staffing formula.

      The General Assembly adopted a significantly lower rate, deferring contributions to the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) to mitigate the state budget cuts to localities. In addition to anticipated rate increases, school systems will be required to repay the deferred amounts with interest over a 10 year period. Therefore, the revenue reduction of $65.2 million combined with the net expenditure reduction of $113.1 million results in an ending balance of $48.0 million which will be placed in a reserve to address future VRS requirements.

      Highlights of the FY 2011 School Operating Fund Approved Budget:

          • $2.2 billion budget, a decrease of $72.3 million, or 3.2 percent from the estimate; and $34.5 million, or 1.6 percent, from the FY 2010 Approved
          • Nearly $200 million in budget reductions and cost avoidances including the elimination of 204.3 positions, nearly half of which are from central offices
          • $14.4 million required for membership growth of 1,723 students and demographic shifts resulting in increases in needs-based staffing
          • Cost per pupil decreased by approximately $300 from FY 2010; this is the third consecutive year of decreased spending per pupil
Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board adopt Fairfax County Public Schools’ FY 2011 Approved Budget as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: Facilities Planning Advisory Council

School Board Action Required: Information

Key Points:

      On April 5, 2010, the Comprehensive Planning Development Committee (CPDC) presented a recommendation for the establishment of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC). The recommended proposal outlines the organization and function of a Board appointed citizen council, including mission, objectives, function and operations, work products and plans, communications flow, community engagement, application criteria and process for council selection.

      As a follow-up to discussion from the April 5 work session, the attachment contains the proposal for a Facilities Planning Advisory Council. The School Board at their work session on April 29, 2010, approved the revised FPAC proposal and agreed to include it on the agenda of May 6, 2010, regular meeting as new business.

      If accepted, the FPAC would become a standing committee of the School Board, subject to the guidelines contained in School Board Policy 1710.11, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board. It is further noted that modifications to Policy 1710.11 may be required to accommodate the unique features of the proposed advisory council; the modifications to the policy will be brought forward for Board review and approval pending the outcome of the Board’s action on the FPAC proposal.


Recommendation:
      That the School Board approve the establishment of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council, as proposed by the Comprehensive Planning Development Committee and as detailed in the agenda item.
