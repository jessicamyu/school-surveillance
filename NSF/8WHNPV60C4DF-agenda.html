Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2013

 

The American Alliance for Theatre and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development. The theme for 2013 is Fostering a Culture of Creativity.  Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.

 

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2013

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  Based on the 2013 theme, Music Education:  Orchestrating Success, the month is designed to raise awareness of the importance of music education for all children - and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music. It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages.

 

National Sleep Awareness Week - March 3-10, 2013


National Sleep Awareness Week, which takes place March 3-10, 2013, is an annual public education and awareness campaign to promote the importance of sleep. Sleep Awareness Week brings attention to the importance of adequate sleep for health and safety, and a reminder of sleep-related responsibilities, such as not driving while drowsy and parents ensuring that their children get the sleep they need to learn effectively. The National Sleep Foundation suggests that healthy adults need 7-9 hours of sleep per day, and school-age children might require 10-11 hours of sleep.  The results of the National Sleep Foundation’s 2013 Sleep in America® poll show a compelling association between exercise and better sleep.

RESOLUTION
NAMING THE WALL IN THE MAIN ENTRANCE AT
MOUNT VERNON HIGH SCHOOL
“THE ELIZABETH PARKER CALHOON WALL OF 7”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Elizabeth Parker Calhoon served with distinction as the International Baccalaureate (IB) Program coordinator at Mount Vernon High School from 1994 until her retirement in 2003 from Fairfax County Public Schools, after sixteen years of service; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mrs. Calhoon was the first coordinator of the IB Program at Mount Vernon High School, and during her tenure, she identified and recruited the diverse student population to enroll in the IB program; she enthusiastically supported and encouraged students to excel and do their best in the challenging coursework and examinations and she ensured that the teachers were fully trained in their teaching practice; and

 

     WHEREAS, she earned the respect of the entire Mount Vernon High School community with her inclusion in Jay Mathews’ and Ian Hill’s book, Supertest, How The International Baccalaureate Can Strengthen Our Schools, which documented her tireless efforts to establish the rigorous program at Mount Vernon; and

 

     WHEREAS, her efforts with, and commitment to, the socioeconomically diverse student population at Mount Vernon High School helped the school earn “The Inspiration Award” from the International Baccalaureate Americas office;  Mount Vernon High School was one of two schools in the world to be honored with this distinction; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mrs. Calhoon’s dedication to her students is currently in evidence at the main entrance of Mount Vernon High School, where the names of students who emulated her passion for excellence by achieving a perfect score of seven on an IB exam are displayed on a wall;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names this wall in the main entrance of Mount Vernon High School  “The Elizabeth Parker Calhoon Wall of 7” in memory and honor of this passionate, dedicated, and visionary educator who established an educational program to help many students work towards academic excellence and realize their collegiate dreams.

Staff Contact:          Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     March 7, 2013 – Regular Meeting No. 15

 

Subject:                     FY 2013 Third Quarter Budget Review

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2013 budget and to make revisions as necessary.  The FY 2013 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 20, 2012.

 

A revenue adjustment for sales tax is recommended for the School Operating Fund.  Based on current receipts, it is anticipated that sales tax revenue will exceed the projection included in the FY 2013 Approved Budget by $1.6 million. Sales tax revenue will continue to be monitored closely for the remainder of FY 2013.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $13.0 million from compensation, utilities, and vehicle fuel.  A reduction of $9.8 million in compensation accounts is due to the return of 74.6 staffing reserve positions and reductions in employee benefit accounts.  There are currently 79.6 positions remaining in the staffing reserve, and five positions will be left in the reserve to address any special education staffing needs that cannot be addressed with the use of substitutes until the end of the year.  Based on the actual health and dental elections made by parent liaisons and multilingual interpreters and the impact of the open enrollment changes made by FCPS employees, a reduction in employee benefits is recommended.  A reduction of $2.0 million in utilities is recommended based on a new contract for natural gas, and a reduction of $1.1 million is possible based on fuel expenditures to date.

 

In total, in the School Operating Fund, this agenda recognizes increased revenue totaling $1.6 million and decreased expenditures totaling $13.0 million.  After recognizing the requirement for the FY 2014 budgeted beginning balance, the FY 2013 Third Quarter adjustments result in $14.4 million available to be added to the FY 2014 beginning balance. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure adjustments as reflected in the FY 2013 Third Quarter Budget Review and as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:           Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Ronald A. Hull, coordinator, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:     March 7, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 15

 

Subject:                    Award of Contract  – Blueprint for Change

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

Request for Proposals (RFP) 2000000433 entitled Blueprint for Change was issued on September 6, 2012. The purpose of the RFP was to form a consulting services contract to assist the School Board with the goal of establishing high school start times at 8:00 AM or later.

 

The Office of Procurement Services directly solicited 10 potential offerors. The RFP was posted on Virginia’s electronic procurement portal (eVA) which notified 3129 potential offerors, and was also publicly advertised on the Fairfax County Department of Purchasing and Supply Management’s website. The RFP closed on November 13, 2012, with two proposals received from offerors.

 

A Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) was appointed, comprised of two community members, and one FCPS employee. The SAC met on December 4, 2012, for an organizational meeting. At a meeting on December 10, 2012, the SAC discussed the technical proposals and ranked them based upon the evaluation criteria established in the RFP. The SAC met on December 17, 2012, to evaluate the business proposals of the offerors. Negotiations were held on January 15, 2013, with the top ranked offeror.  On January 15, 2013, the SAC Chairperson submitted a recommendation to award a contract to Children’s National Medical Center. Children’s National Medical Center agreed to the memorandum of negotiations on February 14, 2013.

 

Recommendation:

 

In accordance with policy 5011.2, it is recommended that the School Board approve a contract award to Children’s National Medical Center in the amount of $143,912.43 for the provision of consultant services.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
