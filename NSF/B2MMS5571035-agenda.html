Tonight I am pleased to share with everyone news of a collaborative project involving the Faith Alliance for Climate Solutions (FACS), Fairfax County Public Schools, and Trex, a leading recycled materials manufacturer of wood-alternative decking, railings, and other outdoor items.  Many of you may have Trex materials in your own yards and so may already be familiar with some of the company’s products.

 

For a period of about four months, three congregations worked together to collect more than 700 pounds of single use plastic bags --- that’s more than 60,000 bags --- for recycling.  The Trex Company worked its magic and turned these recycled bags into a very useful, attractive outdoor bench.  This is a wonderful example of individuals, communities, and businesses that are mindful of our natural resources and the need to embrace and incorporate environmentally conscious alternatives into our daily lives.  

But wait --- it gets better!  I am delighted to announce that the Faith Alliance for Climate Solutions decided to donate the bench to Luther Jackson Middle School, and it was recently placed in a courtyard at the school where students often eat lunch.  I encourage everyone to take a close look at the bench at your next opportunity and to read the attached plaque that is inscribed with the words, “More than 40,000 plastic bags were recycled to earn this bench.  Join the efforts to protect our one common home, the Earth.” 

 

On behalf of the School Board, I wish to express appreciation to the Faith Alliance for Climate Solutions, Trex, and members of the community for their efforts to bring this worthy project to fruition.  Additionally, I am hopeful that this example may inspire interest in our schools, PTA organizations, students, and community members to partner in similar recycling projects that benefit us all.

Meeting Category: June 20, 2019 – Regular Meeting

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee (FLECAC), which includes thirty voting members and six non-voting members representing students, teachers, administrators, and community members (including School Board appointees, health professionals, and clergy) meets each year to consider revisions to the K-12 Family Life Education curriculum and make recommendations to the School Board.

During SY2017-18, FLECAC conducted a full K-12 scope and sequence review of the FLE curriculum. This school year the committee concentrated on gaps identified during that review. The 2018-2019 FLECAC has made three recommendations for School Board consideration:

  1. Revise lesson objective to expand instruction on the topic of sexting, currently only in grade 8, to grades 6, 7, and 9 due to the pervasive use of social media and the desire to ensure that students understand the implications of their decisions.
  • Grade 6 instruction will include positive aspects and misuse of social media to include consequences of engaging in cyberbullying and sexting (does not include connection to child pornography).
  • Grade 7 instruction will include appropriate and inappropriate personal and social use of technology and that sexually explicit communication, including sexually explicit shared/posted images and videos of minors, may be considered child pornography.
  • Grade 9 will expand the instruction on the topic of abuse and exploitation to include sexting.

 

  1. Add lesson objective to Grade 9 on the topic of pornography due to growing concern over accidental exposure and increased access to pornography on the Internet.
  • This is a new topic which has not been included in FLE at any grade level prior to this recommendation and grade 9 is the only grade level at which it would be instructed if approved.
  • Instruction will include definition of pornography and implications for healthy relationships; and short- and long-term consequences of recording, possessing, sharing, and distributing sexually explicit media.

 

  1. Addition of new media to support current instruction for bystander awareness in grade 9 - Bystander Intervention: Putting a Stop to Sexual Assault, 2017. Human Relations Media

 

Community Review: Community review of all new Family Life Education (FLE) materials was conducted online from May 9 to June 7. All comments were conducted through email. Summary and narratives of all comments received are provided.

 

Recommendation: Recommendation to approve recommended media and lesson objectives as detailed in the agenda item and as reviewed and recommended by the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee.

Ignite/Strategic Plan:  Goal 1 Student Success, Strategy 4: Provide students with relevant opportunities to explore options and prepare for college and career

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Office of Counseling and College and Career Readiness recommends that the School Board renew its contract with NVCC in the form of Addendum No. 10, which updates certain provisions of the original Memorandum of Agreement, executed in 2008. This contract currently provides four counselors implementing the Pathway to the Baccalaureate program at 16 participating FCPS high schools. 

 

Policy 5011.3 requires that the School Board approve any contract or contract amendment between the School Board and another Government entity that exceeds $250,000.

 

The Pathway to the Baccalaureate is a multi-year, regional program supported by NVCC, George Mason University, and other participating local school divisions. The 2019-20 school year will be the 14th year of our partnership and participation in the Pathway program. Since 2005, this program has increased from four FCPS high schools with approximately 265 students to 16 high schools and approximately 1,300 students.     

 

The program uses a three-step, student-centered approach to increase access to higher education for selected, underrepresented students. Step one is to prepare students for a successful transition to college by providing one-on-one meetings with a Pathway counselor to develop individualized transition plans. Step two is to support students while in college through continued one-on-one counseling, academic advising, and academic and leadership skill building. Step three includes customized degree planning, assistance with the transfer process, and continued access to program supports at George Mason University.   

 

FCPS expenditures for this contract will not exceed $280,000 for the 2019-20 school year. Provisions have been included to adjust the funding if positions are not fully staffed during the term of the agreement.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Addendum No. 10 to the Memorandum of Agreement with Northern Virginia Community College to the Pathway to Baccalaureate Program, effective July 1, 2019 through June 30, 2020, and authorize the Division Superintendent or Deputy Superintendent to execute and administer such contract on behalf of the Fairfax County School Board.
