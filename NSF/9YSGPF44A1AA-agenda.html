Veterans Day - November 11, 2015

 

 Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

 Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

 The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.  Many schools have special events, to which they invite veterans and members of the school community on active duty military.

  

 

 

American Education Week - November 16-20, 2015

 

 American Education Week was established in 1921 by the National Education Association (NEA) to raise public awareness of the importance of public education in America.  This year’s theme, Great Public Schools:  A Basic Right and Our Responsibility, reminds all Americans of the importance of public education and offers the opportunity to celebrate public education and honor those who ensure that every child receives a quality education. As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week features a special observance on each day of the week, including parents, education support professionals, and substitute educators. 

 

 

 

  

Geography Awareness Week - November 15-21, 2015

 

 Geography Awareness Week is an opportunity to focus on geography as a critical component of a 21st century education.  The 2015 theme, Explore!  The Power of Maps, focuses on developing spatial thinking through maps, a critical skill that students can develop as they learn geography, and Earth and environmental sciences.

 

 Geography Awareness Week was established by presidential proclamation in 1987 to underscore the importance of geographic understanding to ensure our nation’s economic competitiveness, national security, environmental sustainability, and livability of our communities in the 21st century.

School Psychology Awareness Week - November 9-13, 2015

 

 The theme for the 2015 School Psychology Awareness Week is Connect the Dots and THRIVE!, with a focus on engaging students at all levels of learning and development to promote personal achievement, growth, and resilience, as well as a sense of connectedness and wellbeing. 

 

 School psychologists work in all educational settings to positively impact the academic and social/emotional development of students through the provision of mental health services that build resilience, promote developmental assets and foster good citizenship.  Their most important responsibility is to ensure the welfare and safety of students.  To fulfill that responsibility, school psychologists strive to attain and maintain high standards of empirically-based practice and to promote professional ethics in their daily work.

 

 School psychologists collaborate with teachers, parents, and school personnel to create safe, healthy, and supportive learning environments for all students.  School psychologists collaborate with school teams to address students' learning and behavioral problems through implementation of multi-tiered prevention and intervention strategies.  They suggest improvements to classroom management strategies or parenting techniques, bring knowledge of assessment and data collection to measure progress and outcomes of interventions, and encourage practices that effectively promote student learning and mental wellness.

RESOLUTION

NAMING THE GYMNASIUM AT HUTCHISON ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

“THE STUART “TIM” RUTHERFORD GYMNASIUM”

 

 

          WHEREAS, Stuart (Tim) Rutherford served Fairfax County Public Schools for thirty-six years with excellence and dedication; he began his professional career as a physical education teacher at Hutchinson Elementary School; after a brief retirement in 2009, Mr. Rutherford returned to FCPS as a substitute teacher at Hutchinson and Herndon Elementary Schools and provided administrative support to Hutchinson’s staff until his retirement at the end of the 2014-2015 school year; and

 

          WHEREAS, Mr. Rutherford taught his students the significance of physical fitness, a proper diet, and adequate rest to maintaining a healthy mind and body; he encouraged students to develop a positive competitive spirit and emphasized the importance of supporting classmates and showing consideration and sportsmanship to competitors; and

 

          WHEREAS, Mr. Rutherford was an advocate for his students and made a significant impact in their lives; he set high academic and behavioral expectations for his students and inspired them to focus on their goals, to work hard, and to commit themselves to become better scholars and athletes; and

 

          WHEREAS, Mr. Rutherford demonstrated integrity, fairness, and respect and he expected nothing less than excellence from his colleagues; he was a valued faculty member and a talented mentor to novice teachers; and  

 

          WHEREAS, Mr. Rutherford established positive relationships with students, staff, and the community; he was an enthusiastic champion of Hutchinson and attended school events and extracurricular activities to support students and faculty and to share in their successes; he had a strong work ethic and helped to keep the school a vibrant and effective learning environment for students;

 

          NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board, in recognition and appreciation of Stuart T. Rutherford’s dedicated service to Hutchinson Elementary School and his valued contributions to Fairfax County Public Schools, hereby names the gymnasium at Hutchison Elementary School “The Stuart “Tim” Rutherford Gymnasium”.

Key Points:

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 3, Premier Workforce. In addition, a report on the work accomplished for the implementation of the class size regulation will be presented.

 

The following are the two overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 3, Premier Workforce:

 

Overarching Strategy 8

Recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts

 

Desired Outcomes

 

  • Recruit, hire, and retain employees to achieve a premier workforce
  • Achieve and preserve a culture of equity and fairness that will be representative of the demographics of Fairfax County
  • FCPS will embrace and value innovation
  • Employees will benefit from competitive compensation packages

 

Overarching Strategy 9

Invest in growth and leadership opportunities for all employees by providing meaningful professional development and quality feedback on performance

 

Desired Outcomes

 

  • Dynamic professional development opportunities will be available for all employees
  • FCPS will develop and maintain a performance evaluation process that is valued by all employees

Key Points:

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 3, Premier Workforce. In addition, a report on the work accomplished for the implementation of the class size regulation will be presented.

 

The following are the two overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 3, Premier Workforce:

 

Overarching Strategy 8

Recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts

 

Desired Outcomes

 

  • Recruit, hire, and retain employees to achieve a premier workforce
  • Achieve and preserve a culture of equity and fairness that will be representative of the demographics of Fairfax County
  • FCPS will embrace and value innovation
  • Employees will benefit from competitive compensation packages

 

Overarching Strategy 9

Invest in growth and leadership opportunities for all employees by providing meaningful professional development and quality feedback on performance

 

Desired Outcomes

 

  • Dynamic professional development opportunities will be available for all employees
  • FCPS will develop and maintain a performance evaluation process that is valued by all employees

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
