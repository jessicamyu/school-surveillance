Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

 

 

The School Board will recognize Kurien Thomas from Robinson Secondary School, the recipient of the 2017 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board Chris Giovarelli and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. The Apple Federal Credit Union and its Foundation have generously funded this award.  The award is given to a junior or senior who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for students in Fairfax County Public Schools in day-to-day behavior.

 

The recipient of the 2017 School Board Character award is Roza Yahya Ahmed Al-Barznji, a junior at Mt. View High School.

 

Roza embodies all of the traits showcased by this award and demonstrates them daily through her words and actions. She is a positive role model for her peers and a powerful example to the ESOL population within Mt. View High School.

 

Roza arrived at Mt. View High School as a refugee from Kurdistan with Level 2 ESOL. Although her English was limited, she quickly found her voice in photography by becoming a student journalist in Government class and a photographer for the school’s newspaper. As Roza’s skills in English progressed, she published several stories describing her personal journey as a Kurdish American, the plight of the Syrian people, the refugees in our communities and the value of mentoring in a school community. She also created a documentary on American Thanksgiving traditions after she interviewed three generations of a family.

 

In addition to her work as the school photographer and for the school newspaper, Roza works as a special occasion photographer and volunteers at a local food bank. Roza’s determination and exceptional work ethic are an inspiration to the whole FCPS community. According to one teacher, “As a teacher I am excited about the learning we are doing together and the message that this outstanding student and young global citizen brings to all of us. She is resilient, curious and a dependable advocate for the truth that many seek to hide”. Her high morals, strong integrity, and resilience epitomize the character traits we want to see in all of our students.  Roza has earned and truly deserves the School Board Character Award.

4.02 Resolution directing Division Counsel to file a petition with the Circuit Court of Fairfax County for issuance of a writ of election to authorize a special election
Meeting
Jun 8, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Action Items - 8:45 p.m.
Type

Whereas, effective May 31, 2017, a vacancy occurred on the School Board due to the resignation of a member of the Board; and

 

Whereas, the School Board is required by Virginia law to petition the Fairfax County Circuit Court within 15 days to issue a writ of election to fill the vacancy; and

 

Whereas, the county-wide special election to fill the existing vacancy may cost up to $250,000; and

 

Whereas, the School Board strongly prefers that a November special election be held to fill the vacancy in conjunction with the November general election in order to reduce election costs and increase voter turnout; and

 

Whereas, Virginia law provides that the special election for this purpose shall be held “not fewer than 45 days nor more than 90 days after the occurrence of the vacancy;” and

 

Whereas, the 90th day is August 29, 2017, based on the rules of statutory construction set forth in the Code of Virginia; and

 

Whereas, the School Board has been informed that its preference for a November election is not allowed under Virginia law, which requires that there be a special election by August 29;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, I HEREBY MOVE that the School Board direct legal counsel to file a petition with the Circuit Court of Fairfax County for issuance of a writ of election to authorize a special election to be held on Tuesday, August 29, 2017, for the purpose of filling the vacancy on the Fairfax County School Board.

I further move that the Board recognize that renaming the school to one more reflective of the community it serves may be a laudable goal. The process for evaluating whether the name should be changed should engage the community in a public and transparent manner.

I further move that the School Board approve the creation of an Ad hoc Committee to consider renaming J.E.B. Stuart High School to consist of students, parents, Stuart community members, alumni, and business and community leaders to be chosen by the Superintendent in consultation with the Full Board. The Superintendent and FCPS staff will facilitate the group’s work. The working group will weigh the pros and cons of a name change; continue to ensure public engagement and determine the extent of support for change; explore public-private partnerships to finance any name change; ways to prevent added burdens on the school’s booster clubs, PTSA and Stuart school administration; the best method for transitioning from an old name to a new one; proper record retention; and the best timing for such a change, if such a change is approved by the Board. The working group will also provide ideas for ways to honor and preserve the school’s history, traditions and past achievements and to memorialize its decades as J.E.B. Stuart High School. The working group will report on its findings and recommendations to the School Board by March 2017.

The committee broke into subcommittees to answer the School Board’s different charges as follows:

  • Subcommittee 1 – Weigh the pros and cons of a name change
  • Subcommittee 2 – Determine the support for a change
  • Subcommittee 3 and 4 – Explore public private partnerships to finance any name change and to identify ways to prevent added burdens on the schools booster clubs, PTSA and school administration

Originally, the committee was scheduled to submit their reports in March, however, the committee needed more time to work through the charges, and a new completion date of May 24, 2017 was recommended.

The Ad Hoc Committee submitted their reports to the Superintendent on May 24, 2017 and were in turn submitted to the School Board for their consideration. There are seven reports: one pro name change and one against name change from each of the subcommittees and one individual who submitted their own.

 

Recommendation:

 

The School Board will now consider the reports and what action, if any, they will take on this item. In the work session on June 12, the School Board will hear from members of the Ad Hoc Committee. The School Board will make a determination for further action.

Meeting Category:  June 22, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Proposed Restatement of the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Action

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

On April 27, the School Board approved ERFC Plan modifications effective July 1, 2017.  The School Board directed the ERFC Board of Trustees to initiate the process to implement the ERFC Plan modifications with the amended Plan documents submitted to the School Board for approval by June 2017.  The Plan amendments are summarized below: 


ERFC and ERFC 2001 Plan Documents

Proposed amendments to the ERFC and ERFC 2001 Plans provide for a 4 percent interest rate on accumulated contributions for periods after June 30, 2017. The rate is reduced from the 5 percent currently credited on accumulated contributions.    
 

Proposed administrative amendments to the ERFC and ERFC 2001 Plans clarify that the cost-of-living adjustment is calculated without regard to any temporary adjustments to a member’s benefit and update the Internal Revenue Code compensation limit. 

 

ERFC 2001 Plan Document

 

Proposed amendments that are applicable to members who first enter eligible employment on or after July 1, 2017, include:  instituting retirement eligibility requirements that age and service equal 90 or attainment of full Social Security retirement age with at least five years of service; increasing the period for calculating the final average salary from 3 years to 5 years; and changing the cost-of-living adjustment to equal 100 percent of the Consumer Price Index (CPI-U) for the Standard Metropolitan Statistical Area that includes Fairfax County with a cap of 4 percent.  In circumstances where the ERFC Board of Trustees is allowed to suspend cost of living adjustments (COLAs), the Board may not reduce the COLAs that were previously granted below 3 percent for members hired prior to July 1, 2017, or the lesser of 4 percent or the increase in the CPI-U for members hired on or after July 1, 2017.    

 

Proposed administrative amendments to the ERFC 2001 Plan document include:

clarifications that a member may nominate a beneficiary consistent with administrative procedures adopted by the ERFC Board of Trustees and that a spouse who is eligible for a 100 percent and a 50 percent survivor option (Options A and B) will receive benefits under the 100 percent option (Option A) in the event of an active member death.  Other administrative amendments provide that a member who leaves eligible employment and returns to work without receiving a refund of contributions will be treated as hired before July 1, 2017, for all purposes under the Plan; conversely, a member who leaves eligible employment, receives a refund and returns to work will be treated as hired on or after July 1, 2017, even if the member redeposits the withdrawn contributions.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve proposed restated plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001.
