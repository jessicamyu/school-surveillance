Bullying Prevention Month- October

 

In an effort to promote awareness of school bullying, the Virginia School Board Association Board of Directors has designated the month of October as VSBA Bullying Prevention Month. Childhood bullying is a significant problem nationwide. It can cause school absenteeism, mental and physical stress, poor school performance, poor self-esteem, and in some cases, school violence. Statistics show that 160,000 children in the United States miss school each day as a result of being bullied. School board members, superintendents, teachers, and parents play a critical role in creating a climate where bullying is not tolerated. It has been proven when adults and children stand together, bullying ends.

 

 

Fire Prevention Week - October 4-10, 2020

 

 

This year's theme is Serve Up Fire Safety in the Kitchen!" Fire Prevention Week 2020 focuses on cooking safety and preventing kitchen fires at home.  The number one cause of home fires is unattended cooking and is also the second leading cause of all burn injuries.  Hot liquids from coffee and even microwaved soup can cause devastating injuries.  The fire prevention week campaign works to educate people of all ages of the importance of having and practicing a home fire escape plan.   A home escape plan includes working smoke alarms on every level of the home, in every bedroom, and near all sleeping areas.  It also includes two ways out of every room, usually a door and a window, with a clear path to an outside meeting place that's a safe distance from the home.  Home escape plans should be practiced twice a year by all members of the household.

 

 

 

Mental Health Awareness Week - October 4-10, 2020

The National Alliance on Mental Illness or NAMI and participants across the country are raising awareness of mental illness during Mental Illness Awareness Week which is October 4-10. Each year NAMI works to fight stigma, provide support, educate the public and advocate for equal care. NAMI believes that mental illness issues are important to address year round, but highlighting these issues during Mental Health Awareness Week provides a time for people to come together and display the passion and strength of those working to improve the lives of the tens of millions of Americans affected by mental illness. This year’s theme is Cure Stigma. There's a virus spreading across America, harming the 1 in 5 Americans affected by mental health conditions. It shames them into silence. It prevents them from seeking help. The virus we are talking about is stigma against people with mental health conditions. But there's good news, stigma is 100% curable. Compassion, empathy and understanding are the antidote. Your voice can spread the cure.

RESOLUTION
NATIONAL DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH

WHEREAS, dyslexia is a language-based learning disability that causes difficulties with specific language skills such as reading, writing, spelling, and word pronunciation; and

 

WHEREAS, the onset and severity of dyslexia varies for each individual and impacts up to 15-20% of the population as a whole; and

 

WHEREAS, literacy is a critical skill needed for educational success and individuals with dyslexia benefit from effective teaching approaches that are multisensory, systematic, and explicit; and

 

WHEREAS, Governor Ralph Northam has recognized October 2020 as DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH in Virginia to acknowledge educators specializing in effective teaching strategies for individuals with dyslexia; and to celebrate the many achievements of adolescents, students, and adults with dyslexia; and

 

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools has instituted dyslexia awareness training for all elementary and secondary instructional staff and administrators; and

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools has launched the FCPS Online Dyslexia Handbook to provide information and resources to FCPS schools and families alike as they support students with dyslexia; and

WHEREAS, parents, educators, and students will benefit from increased awareness about the nature of dyslexia and the combination of strengths and difficulties that individuals with dyslexia can have;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED the Fairfax County School Board recognizes October 2020 as DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH and encourages all residents to learn more about dyslexia and to offer support to parents, educators and individuals affected by dyslexia.

Subject: West Springfield High School - Grant of Storm Drainage Easement and Temporary Construction Easement to Fairfax County

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: September 17, 2020 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

 

Related To: Policy 8330.4

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Christopher Land, LLC (“Developer”) has requested a Storm Drainage Easement and Temporary Construction Easement over a portion of West Springfield High School property, Fairfax County Tax Map 079-3-((04))-0001A. The easement is in connection to installation of storm drainage culverts under Tuttle Road and the existing trail system on School property and improving the existing open channel through the School property.

 

The storm drainage improvements are part of the Developer’s project for a new single-family residential development called Residences at McConnell Manor (“Project”), located adjacent to School property on the opposite side of Tuttle Road. Tuttle Road currently experiences roadway flooding due to inadequate storm conveyance system. The Project storm design will provide an adequate storm conveyance system across Tuttle Road and extend improvements through the existing outfall channel. The storm design is intended to reduce the flooding and overtopping of Tuttle Road. The Storm Drainage Easement would be granted to the Fairfax County.

 

The Office of Design and Construction Services staff has determined that the Easement will in no way permanently damage, interfere with or limit present or future use and will not endanger the health and safety of users of West Springfield High School. The outfall improvements help the neighborhood in general and will benefit the School more directly as that is also a drainage outfall to the School property. Furthermore, staff has asked for incidental restorative landscaping and BMP credits of 0.25 lbs. for the benefit of the High School property, which the Developer has agreed to.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In accordance with past practice, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted an updated version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution on July 28, 2020 (the “2020 Purchasing Resolution”) to reflect legislative changes made during the preceding session of the Virginia General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly-revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

County’s Administrative Changes Relative to FCPS:

  1. Clarified that the updated Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution is effective at the start of the fiscal year, July 1, 2020 (Page 1).
  2. Amended language to conform to County’s One Fairfax Policy on racial and social equity and its recognition that economic inclusivity benefits all (Page 13).
  3. Change to align procurement threshold with increase from $100,000 to $200,000 established under HB 452 (Page 16).
  4. Change to align procurement threshold with increase from $100,000 to $200,000 established under HB 452 and allow for notification to the School Board prior to contract execution (Page 16-17).
  5. Change to align procurement threshold with increase from $100,000 to $200,000 established under HB 452 and allow for notification to the School Board prior to contract execution (Page 19).
  6. Change to increase small purchase threshold from $5,000 to $10,000 commensurate with other purchase limit thresholds (Page 24).
  7. Changed to reflect the County’s current practices regarding HIPAA Compliance for its contractors (Page 36).
  8. Changed to reflect current practice (Page 38).
  9. Changed to correct typographical error (Page 39).
  10. Changed to reflect new small purchase threshold (Page 40).
  11. Changed to allow flexibility in contracting given that drug free workplace requirements vary state-to-state (Page 40).

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018 and 2019 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2020 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  • The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.”

 

[Note: The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority: “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts. Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  • The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopts the newly revised 2020 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.
