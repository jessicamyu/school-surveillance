The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report for the 2020-2021 school year, as required by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent’s Memo #288-19

2020-2021 Special Education Annual Plan/Part B Flow-Through Application-This is a Word document. dated December 20, 2019. This report will be used by VDOE in preparing the state plan for special education. As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 8, 2020.

 

The 2020-2021 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $37,414,692. This amount is equal to the preliminary IDEA Section 611 funding allocation for Federal Fiscal Year (FFY) 2020, July 1, 2020 through September 30, 2022, as communicated in the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent’s Email dated March 18, 2020. Estimated Section 611 expenditures include costs associated with the voluntary 15 percent set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS); a mandatory proportionate set-aside, which ensures that FCPS spends a proportionate share of funds on special education services for parentally-placed private school children with disabilities; materials/supplies; and state restricted indirect costs of 1.1 percent. The IDEA Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 292.60 FTE positions.

 

The 2020-2021 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $836,008. This amount is equal to the preliminary IDEA Section 619 funding allocation for Federal Fiscal Year (FFY) 2020, July 1, 2020 through September 30, 2022, as communicated in the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent’s Email dated March 18, 2020. These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students. Estimated expenditures include costs associated with a mandatory proportionate set-aside and state restricted indirect costs of 1.1 percent. The IDEA Section 619 (Preschool), award will fund 6.0 FTE positions.

 

The revenue amounts above reflect a combined increase of $1.2 million, as compared to the 2019-2020 awards. The 2020-2021 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2019-2020 application. Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, materials/supplies, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.

 

The 2020-2021 Annual Plan was presented to the Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) at the March 11, 2020 meeting.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the 2020-2021 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool grant funding application, as detailed in the agenda item.

Recommended Action
That the School Board name the new building on the campus at the Northwest County Elementary School site “McNair Upper Elementary School.”

Subject: McNair ES Renaming

 

 

Staff Contact: Becky Baenig, assistant superintendent, Region 5

 

 

Meeting Category: Action: Naming of Northwest County Elementary School

 

 

Background:

The new elementary school, temporarily named Northwest County Elementary School, is being constructed on the existing McNair Elementary campus to provide capacity relief.  The building is expected to open in August 2020 and will serve students in grades 3-6, while the existing building will serve students in grades preK-2.  In accordance with Regulation 8170.8, a one-month period of public comment was initiated beginning January 29, 2020. It included a community meeting held on February 19, 2020 for the purpose of outlining the process and soliciting name suggestions from the community. A public hearing was held on February 20, 2020 and no stakeholders signed up to comment on the naming of the new school on the McNair Elementary School site.  After compiling stakeholder feedback, the Superintendent made a recommendation to School Board on March 5, 2020 to name the new facility, McNair Upper Elementary School.

 

As a result of the FCPS closure on March 13, we were unable to hold a second community meeting and public hearing during the one-month comment period following New Business.  The public was provided with the opportunity to submit recommendations and feedback via an email link that was communicated in the community meeting on January 29, 2020, on the FCPS and McNair Elementary websites, and in multiple emails to parents in the McNair community.  To date, no additional comments have been received. 

 

Key Points:

After receiving input from the McNair community, the Superintendent put forth the recommendation to the School Board on March 5, 2020, to name the new building “McNair Upper Elementary School” as this was the most frequently suggested name by stakeholders in the community.  Furthermore, the recommended name is in alignment with the naming convention at other FCPS paired schools, such as Fort Belvoir Upper and Bailey’s Upper.  No additional feedback from the community has been received. 

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board name the new building on the campus at the Northwest County Elementary School site “McNair Upper Elementary School.”

Subject: Electric School Bus Program (Dominion Energy)

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Francine Furby, director, Office of Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: April 23, 2020

 

School Board Action Required: Action

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 1 Student Success and Goal 4 Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

In August 2019, Governor Northam announced Dominion Energy’s (“Dominion”) initiative to partner with selected Virginia school districts for a pilot of 50 electric school buses to be in operation by the close of 2020, with future plans to convert all new school buses in Virginia to electric by 2030.

 

Electric buses are designed to provide environmental and health benefits through reduced emissions as well as reduced operational and maintenance costs.

 

Following a competitive application process, Dominion announced the award of 8 electric buses to Fairfax County Public Schools, which is one of sixteen Virginia school divisions selected to participate.

 

Four of the new electric buses are scheduled to be delivered to FCPS in August 2020, with the remaining 4 to arrive in December 2020.

 

In this pilot phase, each participating school district would purchase the school bus chassis along with certain equipment (e.g., seat belts), but without a diesel engine. Dominion would fund the remaining cost of the electric school bus (approximately $200,000/per bus) as well as the cost of installing vehicle to grid charging infrastructure on district property for each participating district. The vehicle to grid technology allows Dominion Energy to use the school buses’ batteries to supply the grid when the buses are not in use, creating additional energy storage.

 

 

Dominion and FCPS are now negotiating the terms of an agreement that provides for the operation of the school buses and the rights of the parties over the 15 year contractual term.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (i) approve the contract with Dominion Energy whereby FCPS will operate 8 electric school buses for the contract term in conjunction with charging infrastructure to be installed on FCPS property pursuant to an agreed-upon temporary easement for the benefit of Dominion; (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, with the advice of legal counsel, to negotiate, execute and administer this contract and all related documents (including the deed of easement) on behalf of the School Board; and (iii) authorize procurement of the bus chassis and other associated equipment (consistent with Dominion’s specifications) through the director or coordinator of the FCPS Office of Procurement Services in accordance with applicable law, including the Virginia Public Procurement Act and the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution.
