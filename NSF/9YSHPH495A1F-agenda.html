Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2016

 

The American Alliance for Theatre and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development.  The theme is Fostering a Culture of Creativity.  Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.  

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2016

 

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  This year's theme is Music Inspires! and is designed to highlight the importance of comprehensive, sequential music education taught by exemplary music educators for all  children - and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music.  It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages. 

 

Youth Art Month - March 2016

 

Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.  The theme for Youth Art Month 2016 is “The Power of Art.” 

The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

 

 

National Sleep Awareness Week - March 6-13

 

The National Sleep Foundation is celebrating its annual Sleep Awareness Week March 6th through the 13th, to raise awareness of the health benefits of sleep and its importance to safety and productivity. Most of us know that getting a good night’s sleep is important, but too few of us actually make those eight or so hours a priority. So many of us have forgotten what “being really, truly rested” feels like. To further complicate matters, stimulants like coffee and energy drinks, alarm clocks, and external lights, including those from electronic devices, interfere with our natural sleep/wake cycle.

 

Sleep needs vary across ages and are especially impacted by lifestyle and health. To determine how much sleep you need, it's important to examine what lifestyle factors are affecting the quality and quantity of your sleep such as work schedules and stress. Most importantly, make sleep a priority and don’t make it the thing you do only after everything else is done – stop doing other things so you get the sleep you need. For more information on healthy sleep, visit the National Sleep Foundation’s website at Sleep.org.

Meeting Category:  March 10, 2016 - Regular Meeting No. 15

 

Subject:  FY 2016 Third Quarter Budget Review

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

 

Key Points:

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2016 budget and to make revisions as necessary. The FY 2016 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 17, 2015.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund revenue adjustments reflect a net increase of $2.8 million. These adjustments include an increase of $3.0 million in sales tax revenue based on higher actual trend, a decrease of $0.1 million in state revenue for National Board Certified Teacher (NBCT) pass-through payments based on the number of FCPS teachers eligible to receive a stipend from the state in FY 2016, and a decrease of $59,358 in the IDEA Section 611 grant award.  These two grant revenue reductions are offset by corresponding expenditure adjustments.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $2.5 million. These adjustments include the corresponding expenditure reductions for NBCT and IDEA Section 611 totaling $0.2 million, as well as savings from compensation accounts as a result of the positions in the staffing reserve that were not allocated to schools totaling $7.0 million and savings of $3.0 million from transportation fuel.  These decreases are offset by increases of $7.2 million for snow removal and $0.5 million for one-time school start-up costs for Ft. Belvoir.

 

In total, in the School Operating Fund, this agenda recognizes increased revenue totaling $2.8 million and decreased disbursements totaling $2.5 million, which results in available funds totaling $5.3 million. The available funding is recommended to be unallocated until the FY 2016 Final Budget Review providing the School Board with flexibility for addressing one-time needs or FY 2018 beginning balance requirements.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.  Also, attached is an update on the status of grants for FY 2016.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure adjustments as reflected in the FY 2016 Third Quarter Budget Review and as detailed in the agenda item.

Key Points:

 

Facilities staff has filed, on behalf of the School Board, a Proffered Condition Amendment ("PCA") application [Application No. PCA 89-D-007] in order to amend the proffers previously approved for Herndon High School. 

 

The purpose of this PCA application is to increase the maximum permitted gross floor area on the property to allow an expansion of the existing school building.  In keeping with County policy, the applicant also must submit a signed Proffer Statement (a draft of which is attached) thirty days prior to the public hearing held by the Board of Supervisors.  Typically, such a Proffer Statement requires minor revisions as the PCA application is reviewed by County staff and by the Planning Commission in preparation for the Board of Supervisors’ public hearing.  The final version of the Proffer Statement adopted by the Board of Supervisors will go into effect, superseding all prior drafts.  The hearing before the Planning Commission is currently scheduled for March 16, 2016, with a hearing before the Board of Supervisors to be scheduled thereafter.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all actions necessary and appropriate, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to pursue approval of Proffered Condition Amendment Application No. PCA 89-D-007, including negotiating, signing, and submitting the final Proffer Statement and any other documents on behalf of the School Board.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
