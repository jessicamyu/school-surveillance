Background:

Within this general area, Lemon Road Elementary is the one school that has existing available capacity and which is of a size that suggests that it should be expanded.  Using the existing and expanded capacity at Lemon Road offers the opportunity to solve at least some of the overcrowding at adjacent schools.  The most pressing of the overcrowding issues is at Freedom Hill Elementary where enrollments have risen significantly.  Between September 2006 and September 2011, enrollment at Freedom Hill Elementary increased from 473 to 709.  In September 2016, a total of 828 students are projected.   Currently, Freedom Hill has a capacity utilization of 116% which is projected to increase to 136% by 2016.  To accommodate continuing enrollment pressure, Freedom Hill relies on eight classroom trailers to accommodate students.

 

To address overcrowding in this region and to take advantage of capacity at Lemon Road, staff is recommending the following actions:

 

  1. Construct an eight (8) room addition at Lemon Road which will increase that school’s capacity to approximately 550 students (the School Board has already authorized the architectural contract for this addition).
  1. Conduct a limited boundary study spring 2012 to consider moving approximately 100 students from Freedom Hill to Lemon Road effective the 2012–2013 school year.
  1. Conduct a boundary study in either the spring of 2013 or 2014 to move additional students to Lemon Road once the addition is complete.  At a minimum, the study should involve the following schools:
    1. Freedom Hill
    2. Shrevewood
    3. Westgate

Key Points:

 

For the initial spring 2012 study, staff has focused on a triangular portion of the Freedom Hill attendance area that is situated south of Rt. 7 and east of I-495, and north of I-66.  This section of the attendance areas is closest to Lemon Road (see attached map).  This study area contains approximately 325 elementary students attending at Freedom Hill.

 

For this spring 2012 boundary study staff proposes to move some of this triangular area to Lemon Road Elementary with implementation in fall 2012 to take advantage of existing capacity surplus of approximately 100 spaces.  Limiting the scope to these two schools at this time would allow for some immediate, meaningful enrollment relief for Freedom Hill.  Furthermore, a limited boundary study would allow adequate time for Lemon Road Elementary to position its resources and programs to receive an influx of students, many of whom participate in both ESOL and FRM programs.  For this limited study, no changes would be proposed at the middle or high school level since the study area for Freedom Hill and the majority of Lemon Road both feed to Kilmer Middle and Marshall High Schools.

 

The following schools are in this region but are not recommended, at this time, for inclusion in either boundary study. 

  • Haycock Elementary has a projected capacity deficit which could be addressed with the construction of additional capacity as part of its planned renovation.  Furthermore, enrollment pressures at Haycock could also be relieved with adjustments to their Advanced Academic Program (AAP) which continues to be a significant part of the total enrollment.
  • Westbriar Elementary is shown to also have capacity deficit.  However, the FY 2013-17 Capital Improvement Plan, currently under review by the School Board, proposes a future capacity enhancement project to address the elementary level capacity deficit in Tysons Corner which includes the Westbriar attendance area.
  • Stenwood Elementary has some capacity deficit projected which could likely be accommodated with interior architectural modifications and/or additional classroom trailers. 
  • Vienna Elementary does not have sufficient surplus capacity to be a part of the solution for overcrowding. 
  • Kent Gardens Elementary is projected to have a declining capacity deficit which would not require any solution over the 5-year projection period.

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board authorize a limited scope boundary study for Freedom Hill and Lemon Road Elementary Schools for spring 2012; that the School Board authorize an 8-classroom building addition at Lemon Road Elementary to be completed by fall 2013; and further direct the Superintendent to present the School Board with a recommendation for an expanded scope of an additional boundary study for consideration no later than spring 2014 that would take advantage of additional capacity scheduled to be constructed at Lemon Road Elementary School.

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools' staff, on behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, has filed Rezoning ("RZ") Application No. RZ 2011-PR-030 to rezone the Oakton Elementary School site from the R-1 District to the R-2 District to increase the maximum permitted floor area ratio ("FAR") on the school site.  The increase in FAR will provide the opportunity in the future to expand the school building to address enrollment needs.  The FAR under the current zoning was exhausted by the current renovation/expansion. That the School Board authorize the Chief Operating Officer and his staff to take all actions necessary to pursue approval of Rezoning Application No. RZ 2011-PR-030 in conjunction with School Board counsel, and to negotiate, finalize and execute the final Proffers as approved by School Board counsel.

6.02 Naming of School - Recommendation to name the new elementary school to be opened in September 2012 at the Lacey site, as detailed in the agenda item (Cluster III; action scheduled 2/23/2012)
Meeting
Feb 9, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 13
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:           Dan Parris, assistant superintendent, Cluster III

 

Other Staff Present:  Jim Kacur, assistant superintendent, Cluster II

 

Meeting Category:     February 9, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 13

 

Subject:                    Naming of School

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Background: 

 

On January 18, 2012, a meeting was held at Falls Church High School for the purpose of naming the new elementary school at the Lacey site.  Those present included Mason District School Board member Sandy Evans, Cluster II Assistant Superintendent Jim Kacur, Cluster III Assistant Superintendent Dan Parris, Principal Brian Butler and representatives from the community.  In accordance with Regulation 8170.4, public notice included information on the FCPS website, e-mails and letters sent to parents in the affected communities.

 

Key Points: 

 

Parents, students, staff and community members attended a meeting to consider and recommend names for the new school.  As residents checked in to vote, they were given four different-colored dots.  Three would be used in the first round of voting using a weighted system to vote on the entire list of names recommended for the new school.  The fourth dot would be used in a second round of unweighted voting, if it was needed.  After presentations, questions and audience discussion, a total of nine names were recommended and the voting process was explained as follows:

 

-Each voter would place their first three dots on their first, second and third choices.

-They could vote only once for any particular name.

-It was explained that if no one name received more than fifty percent of the points, there would be another round of voting with the two names that received the most points.

 

Participants voted for their top three choices using a weighted point system (first choice=3 points, second choice=2 points, and third choice=1 point).  The two names that received the highest point total during the first round of voting were Mason Crest Elementary School and Masonville Elementary School.  The complete set of data derived from the first round of voting is listed below.  Eighteen residents registered to vote, with one eligible voter leaving before the voting began.

 

First Round of Voting

 

Mason Crest - 33 points

Masonville - 22 points

Mason - 11 points

Ruby Bridges - 7 points

Crestview - 4 points

Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis - 3 points

Obama - 3 points

Mason Hills - 2 points

North Annandale - 0 points

 

Using their fourth dot, each voter was asked to vote for one name.  Each dot represented one vote.  There was no weighting.

 

Second Round of Voting

 

Mason Crest - 10 votes

Masonville - 7 votes

 

 

Recommendation (two options): 

  • That the School Board name the new elementary school to be opened in September 2012 at the Lacey site "Mason Crest Elementary School."

         OR

  • That the School Board name the new elementary school to be opened in September 2012 at the Lacey site "Masonville Elementary School."
