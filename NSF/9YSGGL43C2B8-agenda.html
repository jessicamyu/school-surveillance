Bullying Prevention Month- October

 

In an effort to promote awareness of school bullying, the Virginia School Board Association Board of Directors has designated the month of October as VSBA Bullying Prevention Month. Childhood bullying is a significant problem nationwide. It can cause school absenteeism, mental and physical stress, poor school performance, poor self-esteem, and in some cases, school violence. Statistics show that 160,000 children in the United States miss school each day as a result of being bullied. School board members, superintendents, teachers, and parents play a critical role in creating a climate where bullying is not tolerated. It has been proven when adults and children stand together, bullying ends.

 

 

Fire Prevention Week - October 4-10, 2015

 

Fire Prevention Week 2015 focuses on the importance of smoke alarms.  The official theme, Hear the Beep Where You Sleep, encourages citizens to equip each bedroom with a smoke alarm in working condition. Working smoke alarms cut the risk of dying in home fires in half. During Fire Prevention Week, October 4-10, fire safety educators around the country will work with citizens to teach them about fire safety tips, encourage them to regularly test their smoke alarms and to install smoke alarms on every level of their homes, and provide activities featuring Sparky the Fire Dog.

Key Points:    

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents.  Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.  For 2015-2016 the Board members making appointments are Megan McLaughlin (Braddock), Jane Strauss (Dranesville), Pat Hynes (Hunter Mill), Dan Stork (Mount Vernon), Patty Reed (Providence), and Kathy Smith (Sully). Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  September 24, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 4

 

Subject:                   Proposed Freedom Hill/Vienna Expedited Boundary Adjustment

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Background:

 

An expedited boundary study for Freedom Hill and Vienna Elementary Schools has been initiated by the Region 1 and 2 Assistant Superintendents and the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services in response to community concerns raised over the past few years.  The purpose of the boundary study is to maximize efficiency.  Guidance has been provided to give careful consideration to minimizing impact on demographics, specifically English Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) and the Free and Reduced Meals (FRM) programs.

 

Key Points: 

 

The study area is a section of the Freedom Hill ES attendance area that is situated in the Town of Vienna.  Students in this study area attend Freedom Hill ES which is located in the Marshall HS pyramid, and then they attend Thoreau MS and Madison HS in the Madison HS pyramid.

 

Freedom Hill ES has 640 students and is overcapacity by 28 students. According to the Spring 2015 student enrollment projections, Freedom Hill ES is projected to be overcapacity by 142 students by SY2020-21.  Vienna ES has 427 students and is overcapacity by six students.  According to the Spring 2015 student enrollment projections, Vienna ES is projected to be overcapacity by 35 students by SY2020-21.

 

Discussion:

 

In June 2015, staff proposed four options to the community.  Option 1 proposed the reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill ES to Vienna ES in September 2015.  Sixth grade students would remain at Freedom Hill ES.  Option 2 proposed “status quo” which involved no adjustment of attendance areas.  Option 3 proposed a phase-in process, beginning with rising kindergarteners in September 2015, along with flexibility for school assignments of siblings.  Option 4 proposed the reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill ES to Vienna ES in September 2016.

 

In general, staff found that the community was supportive of the reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill ES to Vienna ES effective September 2016 (Option 4).  Input was received during the two community meetings held at each school, and is posted on each school’s respective website.

 

Families in the study area from Freedom Hill ES who currently receive School Age Child Care (SACC) services will continue to receive these services at Vienna ES when the boundary change is made.

 

The middle and high school assignments of students in the study area would remain the same.  This boundary adjustment would align students in the study area to the Madison High School Pyramid.

 

Capacity relief will be needed at Vienna ES through interior modifications.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve reassignment of students in the study area from Freedom Hill Elementary School to Vienna Elementary School, effective for the 2016-17 school year, in order to balance enrollment and utilization of school facilities; and pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4); and Policy 8130.7, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings; and in accordance with Regulation 2230.12, Exceptions for Attendance at Other Than Base School and Procedure for Applying for Intracounty Exceptions; approve rising 6th grade students affected by the boundary change the option to remain at Freedom Hill ES.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
