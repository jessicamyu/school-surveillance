    RESOLUTION
    NAMING THE LIBRARY AT
    HUNT VALLEY ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
    “THE MARY BARKER MEDIA CENTER”


    WHEREAS, Mary Barker has served as a distinguished teacher, assistant principal, principal, and community leader for 30 years until her retirement on June 30, 2007, always putting children she comes in contact with first; and
    WHEREAS, her passion to provide a quality education for thousands of young learners over the years has left an impression on each child she has supported, and her belief in the ability of every child to be successful in school has created a schoolwide enthusiasm for helping every student reach his or her full potential; and
    WHEREAS, Mrs. Barker’s warm and loving personality has made her smile a welcome sight to everyone at Hunt Valley Elementary School; and
    WHEREAS, Mrs. Barker has created a nurturing and safe environment for all students and families in the school and has earned the trust and respect of the school’s parents, students, and staff members; and
    WHEREAS, her tireless commitment to Hunt Valley staff members resulted in individual growth and leadership development for all;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that, upon her retirement, the Fairfax County School Board names the media center at Hunt Valley Elementary School “The Mary Barker Media Center” in recognition of Mrs. Barker’s contributions to the individuals, communities, and school division in which she so lovingly and productively served, thereby leaving a legacy of educational excellence in Fairfax County Public Schools.

    5.03 CTEMS Annual Plan and 2006-2007 Application for Career Technical Education - Approve the Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) Annual Plan and 2007-2008 Application for Career and Technical Education funds, as detailed in the agenda item (IS; presented as new business 5/10/07)
    Meeting
    May 24, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 20
    Category
    5. Consent Agenda
    Type
    Information
    Staff Contact: Ann Monday , Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services

    SUBJECT: CTEMS Annual Plan and 2007-2008 Application for Career and Technical Education Funds

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve the Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) Annual Plan and 2007-2008 Application for Career and Technical Education funds, as detailed in the agenda item.


    EXPLANATION:
        The Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) document provides the Virginia Department of Education with each local education agency’s (LEA) plan for providing and implementing career and technical education instruction to both public school and adult students. The CTEMS document specifies all of the activities and programs available to students as a result of local, state, and federal funds. The CTEMS document is prepared in the preset uniform format (including assurances, charts, and schedules) as provided by the Virginia Department of Education.

    Changes to the previous 2006-2007 CTEMS document have been bolded throughout.

        Federal funding from the Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Act of 2006 (Perkins IV), which is a continuing grant provided to all local education agencies (LEA) in the Commonwealth of Virginia, is available to assist school districts in providing elective career and technical programs for middle and high school students. Formal application for federal moneys is one component of the annual CTEMS document to the Virginia Department of Education.Schedule 2-B, page 47, of the CTEMS document specifies the expenditures of the Perkins federal grant in improving career and technical education programs according to the “Appropriate Use of Federal Funds” on page 61 of the document.

        Focusing on current priority needs for this school division, the CTEMS local plan continues a cycle in identifying the goals, objectives, and strategies for locally providing career and technical education programs.

    Attachment: Local Plan Update/Budget for Career and Technical Education Funds 2007-2008

    RECOMMENDATION:

    EXPLANATION:
        As explained in the attached Requests for Waiver of the Verified Credit Requirements for a Standard of Advanced Studies Diploma, two Fairfax County Public Schools students were unable to meet the Standards of Learning (SOL) verified credit requirements for a diploma and the students’ previous non-Virginia schools would not award diplomas.
