Staff Contact:   Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

                                 Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:  May 18, 2017 - Work Session 

 

Subject: FY 2018 Budget 

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion 

 

Key Points:

 

FY 2018 Advertised Budget

On February 9, 2017, the School Board adopted the Advertised Budget for the School Operating Fund totaling $2.8 billion. This is an increase of $135.1 million, or 5.1 percent, over the FY 2017 Approved Budget. The advertised budget was presented to and discussed by the School Board and County Board of Supervisors at a joint budget work session on February 21, 2017.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The Virginia General Assembly’s adopted 2016-2018 budget provides an increase of $4.1 million in revenue as compared to the FCPS’ FY 2018 Advertised Budget.

 

County Budget Impact

On May 2, 2017, the County Board of Supervisors adopted their FY 2018 budget that included an increase in the transfer to the School Operating Fund of 2.79 percent above the FY 2017 Approved Budget. The increase of $53.4 million is $59.1 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget.

 

May Budget Work Session

As presented at the May 1, 2017, work session, the projected shortfall in the FY 2018 Advertised Budget is $46.7 million, after accounting for the state and county budget adjustments and the impact from the FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

Starting with the FY 2018 Advertised Budget, following are the required adjustments in millions:

 

Required Revenue Adjustments:

 

County Transfer                                                     ($59.1)

State Aid                                                                  $6.5

Sales Tax                                                                ($2.3)   

Beginning Balance (From FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review)  $6.5

 

Required Expenditure Adjustments:

Enrollment and Demographic Adjustments                   $2.7    

ERFC Retirement                                                     ($2.3)

Health Insurance                                                     ($1.8)

Assessment Tool (Universal Screener)                       ($1.0)

 

After the required adjustments, the remaining gap totals $46.1 million. To balance the budget, the Superintendent has recommended the following revenue and expenditure adjustments in millions: 

 

Recommended Revenue Adjustments:

Athletic Fees                                                           $1.5

AP/IB Test Fees                                                      $0.6

Pupil Placement Fees                                                $0.5

 

Recommended Expenditure Adjustments:

 

Staffing Ratio Increase of 0.5                                ($14.7)

ES Class Size Formula Floor                                    ($1.0)

ESOL Transitional High School                                 ($1.4)

Nonteacher MSA Elimination                                    ($7.9)

Nonteacher Scale Delay                                           ($5.0)

Teacher Scale Implementation Slowdown                  ($6.6)

Reduction from Central Department                         ($3.1)

   Operating Accounts

Travel for Professional Development                         ($0.5)

Summer School                                                     ($1.0)

School Instructional Materials                                  ($2.4)

Bus Fuel                                                               ($0.2)   

Parent Liaison Compensation                                    $0.4

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2018 Approved Budget as detailed in the FY 2018 Advertised Budget, modified by the adjustments included in this agenda item.
