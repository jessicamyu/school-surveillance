Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Overview

On February 7, 2019, the School Board adopted the FY 2020 Advertised Budget that includes a requested increase of $84.4 million, or 4.1 percent, in the county transfer. Expenditure adjustments total $117.4 million and 28.3 positions. The FY 2020 Advertised Budget focuses on employee compensation. FCPS has enhanced teacher pay through a multiyear effort, beginning with a compensation study in FY 2016. Included in this budget are adjustments for enrollment and student demographic changes, a step increase for all eligible employees, funding to continue implementation of the teacher salary scale, a one percent market scale adjustment for nonteacher salary scales, staffing initiatives, a learning management system, the FCPSOn initiative, and the adoption of language arts instructional resources for high school.

 

County’s Advertised Budget and Advertised Tax Rate

The County Executive presented his FY 2020 Advertised Budget on February 19, 2019, which includes a school operating fund transfer increase of $84.4 million or 4.1 percent. After accounting for additional state funding included in the Governor’s Introduced Budget, the FY 2020 Advertised Budget is fully funded. The County’s advertised budget maintains the current real estate tax rate of $1.15 per $100 of assessed value.

 

On February 26, 2019, the School Board and County Board of Supervisors held a joint budget work session to discuss both advertised budgets and the impact of the state budget.

 

On March 5, 2019, the County voted to advertise the real estate tax rate for FY 2020 at $1.15 per $100 of assessed value. Advertising the tax rate allows the Board of Supervisors to adopt the advertised rate or one that is lower, but a higher tax rate cannot be imposed.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS’ School Operating Fund

On February 24, 2019, the General Assembly (GA) adopted amendments to the Governor’s 2018-2020 Introduced Budget (HB 1700/SB 1100). The amendments adopted by the GA result in FCPS receiving a decrease of $3.7 million in the School Operating Fund for FY 2020, as compared to the Governor’s Introduced Budget.

 

Based on the final budgets adopted by the state and county, any shortfall that remains will require additional revenue and expenditure adjustments to balance FCPS’ FY 2020 Approved Budget. Staff is developing plans to resolve the state revenue shortfall which will be shared with the School Board in April.

2019 Virginia General Assembly Session

 

HB 1700 (the Appropriations Act) was one of over 2,000 pieces of legislation considered by the General Assembly during its 2019 Regular Session. The 45 (technically, 46) day “short” Session adjourned on February 24, 2019. Of those 2,000 introduced bills, about 880 in total passed both the House and Senate.

 

During Session, over 400 bills were routed to relevant FCPS departments across 13 different subject categories as being of some potential interest and/or having some possible impact on FCPS students, staff, or on the FCPS community in general. The following passed bills are among those that were directly related in part or in whole to one or more FCSB legislative positions as reflected in the Board’s adopted 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program:

 

  • School Calendar (HB 1652 and SB 1005) Board Priority Position, Board Position Q.1
  • School Bus Stop Arm Cameras (HB 2344 and SB 1520) Board Position AA.2
  • Teacher Licensure Flexibility (SB 1575, HB 2037, SB 1397, and HB 2486) Board Position AB (parts)

 

The Governor has until March 26th in most cases to sign, amend, or veto any passed legislation, including the Appropriations Act, and many passed bills have already been signed. The General Assembly will return for its Reconvened Session on April 3 to consider Governor’s actions. Adopted legislation goes into effect July 1, 2019 unless otherwise noted in the bill itself.

 

Information on all education-related legislation from the 2019 General Assembly Session is posted on the FCPS Office of Government Relations website and will be updated as necessary.

Subject: High School English Basal Instructional Materials Approval

 

Staff Contact: Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present: Noel Klimenko, director, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category: March 25, 2019, Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Key Points:

 

The Virginia Department of Education revised the Standards of Learning for English in 2017. The Secondary Language Arts team in Instructional Services has been revising curriculum documents to align with these new standards and providing professional development for teachers and administrators about the revised SOLs which will be fully implemented in the 2019-2020 school year.

 

The current high school language arts textbooks were purchased in 2006. School have been utilizing these texts and supplementing with novels as well as print and digital works available through various sources. To provide equitable resources for students in all high school language arts courses, Instructional Services began the process for new basal instructional materials as defined in FCPS Regulation 3004-Adopted Basal Instructional Materials in 2018.

 

A timeline of the process for the language arts basal resource review can be found in attachment A. In October 2018, the School Board approved the committee members to review and recommend basal instructional materials for high school language arts. The Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee convened in November and December to review resources for high school language arts that were submitted by vendors in response to an RFP for consideration as basal instructional materials for high school language arts. In accordance with the Code of Virginia, regulations of the Virginia Board of Education, and Fairfax County Public Schools regulations, the Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee in conjunction with the Selection Advisory Committee and the Technical Advisory Committee recommends the titles listed in Attachment B for adoption at the division level. Additionally, a summary of the committee rationales for the recommended materials and community input received are provided in attachments C and D.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the titles listed for high school language arts as recommended by the Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee and the Selection Advisory Committee.
