National Red Ribbon Week, October 23-31, 2015

 

National Red Ribbon Week was established in memory of Enrique Camarena, a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration agent who was killed in the line of duty while investigating a major drug cartel in Mexico.  To honor his battle against illegal drugs, friends and neighbors began to wear red badges of satin and some parents began to form coalitions.  National Red Ribbon Week grew from these grassroots efforts to form a national movement against the use of illegal drugs.  Its annual campaign encourages students to take a visible stand against substance abuse and make a personal commitment to follow a drug-free lifestyle.

 

 

Native American Heritage Month - November 2015

 

Native American Heritage Month started in 1915 as American Indian Day by the Boy Scouts of America.  Over the years, individual states recognized Native American days, but it wasn’t until 1990 that President George H.W. Bush designated November as National American Indian Heritage Month.  Since 1994, a presidential proclamation has proclaimed the month to be Native American Heritage Month in honor of the significant contributions made by the first Americans to the establishment and growth of the U.S.

National Career Development Month - November 2015

 

November is National Career Development Month and, in October, Virginia celebrated Career Week. Career center specialists, school counselors, career experience specialists, employment and transition representatives play unique roles in career development for students. With a focus on readiness for postsecondary endeavors, these educators are employed to help students explore and develop their abilities, interests, strengths, and talents as they relate to further education and entry into the workforce.

 

These educators collaborate with parents to enhance academic and career planning for students. All educators in PreK-12 help students investigate skills, qualities, and academic opportunities that relate to career fields, encourage lifelong learning, and promote future readiness. Students explore careers and other postsecondary options through classroom instruction, the student learning plan, academic advising, and related activities. Through career exploration and development, students learn the skills and training necessary to be successful in future careers and achieve gainful employment and personal fulfillment.

Staff Contact:              Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services                                      

 

Other Staff Present:    Roger L. Ball, director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:       October 22 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 5

 

Subject:                       Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 1, 2015, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2015 session of the General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

Code Changes

 

  1. Cooperative Procurement. Authorizes use of contracts solicited and established by the Metropolitan Washington Council of Governments. This increases the number of cooperative purchasing agreements available to FCPS, providing greater access to competitively-priced goods and services. Page 5, Section 5.

 

  1. Eliminates the requirement to create a written justification for the file prior to using competitive negotiations. Prior to this change, a written justification was required before using competitive negotiations to purchase goods and services. As a result of this change, written justifications are no longer required. Page 14, Section 2 – Methods of Procurement.

 

  1. Request for Proposal Scoring. The numerical scoring system and weight assigned to individual components of a proposal must be included in the Request for Proposal.  Prior to this change, this information was normally developed after Request for Proposals were issued and prior to the evaluation of proposals. This change is designed to provide offerors an opportunity to better structure and align their proposals with the critical factors that will be used to evaluate proposals. This change also requires that the weight or point values be included in the Request for Proposal or publicly posted prior to the due date and time for receipt of proposals. Page 14, Section 2 – Methods of Procurement.

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, and 2014 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2015 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

  1. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2015 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:  Dr. Susan Marks, interim assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present: De Hawley Brown, director, Office of Benefit Services

 

Meeting Category: October 22, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 6

 

Subject: Virginia Retirement System (VRS) Resolution

 

School Board Action Required: Action (Consent)

 

Related to: Legal Requirement

 

 

 

Key Points:  

 

Currently, employees who participate in the VRS Hybrid Retirement Plan direct both the mandatory and voluntary portions of the defined contribution (DC) component to the VRS deferred compensation plan.    

 

With the 2015 legislation, Senate Bill 1162 and House Bill 2178, the School Board has an option to create an employer-sponsored plan for the voluntary contributions.  The participants can then choose to direct their voluntary contributions to this newly created employer-sponsored plan.  The legislation requires that the School Board adopt a resolution (delivered no later than October 30, 2015) to either:   

 

  • Allow eligible employees the option to direct the voluntary portions of their contributions to an employer sponsored hybrid 403(b) plan; or
  • Not offer this option, so that both the mandatory and voluntary contributions continue to be directed into one plan, the VRS Hybrid 457 Deferred Compensation Plan

 

This election can be revisited on an annual basis.

 

Should FCPS elect to offer an employer-sponsored plan, the following are some of the challenges:

 

  • Meeting a November 1, 2015 deadline
  • Challenging communication requirements
  • Administering multiple 403(b) participant groups (hybrid vs. non-hybrid)
  • Overpaying VRS employer defined benefit contribution with lagged reimbursement
  • Increased exposure with additional fiduciary liability
  • Increased resource requirements for a small portion of our employee population.

 

Additionally, employees who elect to participate in an employer-sponsored plan will:

  • Be required to research an additional vendor to make an election to direct the voluntary contributions to the employer-sponsored plan or to remain with the VRS deferred compensation plan. This is also an annual election for employees, requiring an annual open enrollment activity for this plan alone.
  • Be subject to additional fees and complexity as the FCPS plan election would be in addition to, and not replace, the VRS defined contribution component
  • Lose access to a consolidated view of their primary retirement benefit

 

At this time, we do not recommend an employer-sponsored plan due to the additional complexity and expense of creating another level of investment under the VRS Hybrid Retirement Plan.  This allows the current process to be in place where both the mandatory and voluntary portions continue to be directed to the VRS deferred compensation plan.

 

Recommendation: 

That the School Board pass the resolution not to elect using an employer-sponsored hybrid 403(b) plan.

Subject:                     Town of Vienna – Request for Easements at Vienna Elementary School 

     

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Town of Vienna has requested that the School Board grant certain easements at the Vienna Elementary School site in connection with the Town’s renovation and expansion of its Community Center, located on the adjacent parcel.  The easements are for underground utilities and for grading/construction.

 

Such a request is governed by Policy 8330.3, a copy of which is attached.

 

In accordance with the Policy, FCPS Facilities staff has retained a real estate appraiser to provide an independent opinion as to the fair market value of the land area of the proposed easements.  Staff expects to receive that opinion prior to the November 5, 2015 School Board meeting, when this matter is scheduled for action.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all actions necessary and appropriate, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to negotiate, execute, and administer such deeds and agreements (as well as any other documents) deemed necessary and appropriate to respond to the Town’s request, all as in accordance with the parameters discussed by the School Board in closed meeting.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
