Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

Staff Contact: Deirdra McLaughin, Chief Financial Officer, Department of Financial Services

SUBJECT: Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board adopt the revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution.

EXPLANATION:

    The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors approved revisions to the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution effective July 12, 2004. The purchasing resolution was modified to incorporate changes resulting from legislation enacted during the 2004 session of the Virginia General Assembly related to procurement and/or contracts, and other administrative changes recommended by staff. The most significant changes include:

· Cooperative Purchasing – expands the use of cooperative purchasing by authorizing state and local entities to enter into a cooperative procurement agreement to purchase from any contract of the U.S. General Services Administration (GSA) or the contract of any other federal agency provided the terms of the contract permit such purchases. The Department of Purchasing and Supply Management will be developing guidelines for using these types of contracts in the near future.
    · Construction Contracts – raises the limit for using competitive negotiation for construction contracts from $500,000 to $1 million. This change also provides that for design-build or construction management contracts under $1 million, public bodies do not have to obtain approval of the Design-Build Review Board.

      Staff Contact: Thomas M. Brady, Chief Operating Officer



      SUBJECT: Pohick School Site


      RECOMMENDATION:
        That, following the October 18, 2004 approval of the Board of Supervisors, the School Board declare as surplus and thereafter sell, on the terms previously discussed in closed meeting, the Pohick School Site, with the proceeds from such sale being retained and accrued to the school system's capital improvement fund for use for new school construction and school renovation projects in accordance with Section 22.1-129(A) of the Code of Virginia, as amended, and as set forth in the attached Resolution.

      EXPLANATION:
        The School Board is the owner of approximately 35 acres of undeveloped real property located in the Springfield Magisterial District near South Run District Park known as the Pohick School Site. The land was dedicated to the School Board in 1967 but, due to subsequent disputes between the parties, was not conveyed to the School Board until 1989. The deed of conveyance contained restrictions to the effect that if the site was sold or developed for a purpose other than a secondary or high school, title to the site would revert back to the party that conveyed it to the School Board. Recently, school system staff and the successor-in-interest to the original conveying party have had discussions about the possible sale of the site. Negotiations are ongoing. The School Board has been briefed about the recommended terms of the sale in closed session in order to maintain the bargaining position and negotiating strategy of the school system, as authorized by Va. Code Ann. § 2.2-3711(A)(3).

        The sale of this residentially-zoned parcel would generate significant revenue, which would then be available to meet urgent school facilities needs. Under applicable law, a School Board property must be declared surplus, following a public hearing, in order to be sold. The School Board held this statutorily required hearing for the Pohick School Site on September 23, 2004. The Board of Supervisors held its own public hearing on October 18, 2004, before approving the School Board’s intended action with regard to this site. If the School Board members wish to proceed, the School Board may vote on October 21 to declare the Pohick School Site surplus and to authorize its sale on such terms as the School Board finally approves, with the proceeds of the sale being retained and accrued to the school system’s capital improvement fund.

        The attached Resolution, if approved by the School Board, would be recorded along with the deed among the land records of Fairfax County in the event the sale is consummated, as required by Va. Code Ann. § 22.1-129(A).
