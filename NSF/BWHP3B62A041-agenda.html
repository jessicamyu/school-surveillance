Subject: FY 2022 Budget

 

Staff Contact: Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                 Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

                                 Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

 

Meeting Category: January 27, 2021 – Budget Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

On January 7, 2021, the Superintendent presented the FY 2022 Proposed Budget. This budget reaffirms our commitment to keep the main focus on students, teachers, and classroom instruction.

 

The FY 2022 Proposed Budget presentation and document are available at https://www.fcps.edu/about-fcps/budget/budget-documents

 

The remaining FY22 budget development process will be discussed including budget priorities and amendments.

 

Preliminary information regarding FCPS’ potential usage of ESSER II funding will be provided.

 

Information will be provided on the proposed extended learning program for students needing additional support in summer 2021.

Subject:  Anti-Bias and Anti-Racism Education Policy Development

 

Staff Contact: Lisa Williams, chief equity officer, Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS)

Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer, FCPS

 

Other Staff Present:

Kathleen Walts, executive director, Office of Professional Learning and Family Engagement

Leona Smith, director, Equity and Family Engagement

Noel Klimenko, director, Office of K-12 Curriculum and Instruction

Nina Thomas, coordinator, Professional Learning and Cultural Responsiveness

Alicia Hunter, coordinator, K-12 Social Studies

Colleen Eddy, coordinator, Curriculum Integration & Management

 

Meeting Category:  January 27, 2021 Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Presentation

 

Related To: Goal 1: Student Success; Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

At the work session on September 15, 2020, the School Board voted to pursue policy revision and development supporting anti-bias and anti-racism education. Today’s presentation will focus on the following considerations for this policy work:

  • The scope of stakeholder engagement that will inform the creation of a new policy for anti-bias and anti-racism education, and revision of the existing Controversial Issues Policy
  • The role of consultants and the role of staff

Recommendation:

School Board support recommendations of the committee.
