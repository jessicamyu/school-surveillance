    National Volunteer Week - April 23-29, 2006

    Volunteering is central to the American character and is a fundamental expression of responsible citizenship. From our nation's earliest days, people came together to do whatever was needed for the public good - from raising barns and providing mutual security to organizing educational activities and caring for their neighbors.

    During the 2004-2005 school year, 172 schools, centers, academies, and central offices reported that 89,656 volunteers had donated 1, 553, 445 hours of volunteer service to FCPS. Over the past 20 years, 1, 100, 809 volunteers have provided a reported total of 22,928,797 hours of service to our schools. The monetary value of this contribution is over $240 million. If our volunteers had been paid at the entry-level instructional assistant salary of $11.45 per hour, the bill for 2004-2005, alone, would have totaled almost $18 million.

    Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month - May 2006

    Americans of Asian and Pacific ancestry have made significant contributions to the history and development of the United States in many fields of endeavor such as science, commerce, the arts, and the humanities. The Fairfax County School Board recognizes that an understanding of these contributions should be an integral part of the year-round education of all students.

    The President of the United States proclaims May to be Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month and has chosen this month in recognition of the work done by Chinese immigrants on the transcontinental railroad, which was completed on May 10, 1869.

    Pride in America Month - May 2006

    Our country’s founders–more than 200 years ago–established a nation of unique, diverse individuals working toward the common goal of a free and open society–a nation that has accomplished much in two centuries and of which we all can be proud. Thomas Jefferson understood that we, as a people, do have a common heritage in which we can and should, as individuals, develop pride and discipline, and Jefferson envisioned what our nation’s common heritage truly is when he wrote, “All men are created equal and are endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable rights."

    Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., understood and believed this when he proclaimed, “I have a dream. . .” recognizing that the principles about which our country's founders wrote were and are the cornerstone of equality and freedom and that these principles form our nation’s common heritage for its people.

    The Fairfax County School Board recognizes May as Pride in America Month and everyone in Fairfax County Public Schools expresses strong support and deep gratitude to the men and women of our armed forces, and to the many Virginia-based loved ones of our military, including especially the children of military parents enrolled in Fairfax County Public Schools, for their courage, service, and sacrifice during this time

    School Nurse Day - May 10, 2006

    School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.

    School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health and safety, intervene with actual and potential health problems, and provide case management services. They reassure parents that their children’s health needs and injuries are appropriately handled during the school day and allow teachers to devote their time to educating students by taking care of any health care needs or emergencies that may arise. School nurses are dedicated individuals who work in cooperation with families, teachers, and school administrators.

    The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognizes May 10 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

    SUBJECT: Transfer of Certain School Properties Not Currently Used for Instruction In Return for Increased CIP Funding from the County

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board (i) declare as surplus to the needs of the school system and transfer to the Board of Supervisors of Fairfax County the real properties listed on Attachment A, in return for an annual increase of $25 million in capital bond funding for each of the next six years beginning in FY 2007; and (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent or his designee to execute on behalf of the School Board such declarations, deeds and other documents (including the Lease Amendment for the Lawyers Road parcel), and to take such further actions, as may be deemed necessary or desirable in order to effectuate the foregoing in accordance with the explanation set forth below.

    EXPLANATION:
        The School Board has an urgent need to increase substantially the available funding for its Capital Improvement Program (CIP) in order to meet current and future instructional requirements. At present, and without the action recommended above, the annual bond funding available to Fairfax County Public Schools is projected to be $130 million.

        Discussions between school and County officials resulted in a consensus that recognized the mutual benefits of the County increasing the school system’s CIP funding over a designated period of time, in return for the school system transferring certain unneeded school properties to the Board of Supervisors.

        At its regular meeting on February 23, 2006, the School Board agreed to pursue a formal process that would implement transfers of unneeded school properties to the Board of Supervisors, in return for the increased CIP funding for school projects. In order to ensure that this process is open to the public, the School Board held a public hearing on April 17, 2006, for the purpose of obtaining public comment about the properties being considered for transfer. The public hearing was advertised in a newspaper of general circulation in the County, and school staff sent notices to adjoining property owners by U.S. Mail. Approximately ten speakers addressed the School Board on this subject at the April 17th public hearing.

        At the present time, there are 12 parcels of real property that are not being used for instructional purposes, and for which no future instructional purpose is anticipated. These properties are being considered for possible transfer. The list of these properties is attached hereto as Attachment A. Certain of these parcels are currently used by, and are subject to pre-existing agreements with, the Fairfax County Park Authority. There are two forms of pre-existing agreements: (i) an interim use agreement which provides for termination with six months notice; and (ii) a lease agreement pertaining to the Lawyers Road parcel. The Park Authority has requested that, prior to any transfer of the Lawyers Road parcel to the Board of Supervisors, the School Board first expressly confirm that the transfer of that particular parcel is subject to the terms of the lease agreement by executing an agreed-upon amendment to the existing lease agreement. County staff has been advised of the Park Authority’s request and has no objection to the transfer being made subject to the Park Authority rights under the lease agreement.
    The terms of the prospective property transfer are outlined in Attachment B, attached hereto.

                    The funding application estimated revenue for Part B monies should yield approximately $30,196,189 to Fairfax County Public Schools. This year’s decrease in funds, an estimated $800,000, combined with increases in FCPS salary and benefit costs, results in an 8.0 FTE decrease in positions within the grant, bringing the total positions within the grant to 440.0 FTE.

                    The Section 619 Preschool Grant Funds actual entitlement is based on a count of students ages three through five taken on December 1, 2005. Funding of $820,359 supports Childfind activities, parenting and transition services, and social work services for preschool-age students.

                    The Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities reviewed the plan and funding application at its April 19, 2006, meeting.
