Other Staff Present:  Jeff Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation

                                    Tom Vaccarello, director, Office of Safety and Security

 

 

Meeting Category: Regular meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Action

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

This MOU was a collaborative effort between Fairfax County Public Schools, the Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County Police Department. A group of key community stakeholders was convened and met three times in July to provide input into the MOU. The revised MOU includes the following changes to the 2014 document:

 

  • Establishes a clear division between the role of the SRO in criminal matters and the FCPS administrative staff on student discipline matters.
  • Clarifies that SROs are not involved in determining student discipline under the SRR.
  • Removes “stop and frisk” from the MOU.
  • Removes reference to “cultural and social influences” from the MOU.
  • Adds SRO training focused on Implicit Bias, Disability Awareness, Crisis Intervention training, Restorative Justice, and Cultural Competency.
  • Stresses partnership between School administrators, special education staff, and the police department.

                                                                                           Updated 7/10/18

 

Subject:  FY 2018 Final Budget Review  

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:    Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services  

 

Meeting Category:    July 26, 2018 – Regular Meeting     

                    

Ignite Link:  Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background:

 

All of the FY18 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the nine funds. All comparisons are against the FY18 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the revenue variance and the FY19 budgeted beginning balance, the flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, textbook reserve, and other commitments, the FY18 funds available total $31.7 million.

 

Revenue variances begin on page 1 of the agenda attachment, and FY18 actual total funds available are $5.8 million less, or 0.2 percent, of the FY18 Third Quarter Budget Review projection. State Aid for FY18 totals $437.9 million, which is $0.8 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts total $196.9 million, $0.2 million more than budgeted. Federal revenue totals $45.8 million, which is $5.8 million less than budgeted primarily as a net result of unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and re-appropriated in FY19. Other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, and miscellaneous revenue, are $0.6 million more than the third quarter estimate.

 

Expenditure variances on page 2 of the agenda attachment, total $37.5 million. The total expenditure variance of $37.5 million is 1.3 percent of the FY18 Third Quarter Budget and is largely attributed to unanticipated lapse and turnover of $18.4 million from salaries, and $11.3 million from employee benefits. Additionally, $2.2 million from other accounts was unexpended. Multiyear unspent federal grant funds of $5.6 million is carried forward and re-appropriated for FY19.

 

As a result of the revenue and expenditure variances, the total funding available from FY18 totals $31.7 million. This agenda item includes recommendations for FY19 expenditure adjustments. Items identified as part of the FY19 Approved Budget include placeholder funding of $1.1 million for a one-year step increase for grandfathered teachers on the BA lanes and classroom instructional support employees as well as $0.8 million for a bonus for employees who did not receive a salary increase in FY19. Prior committed priorities and requirements include $3.6 million to continue FCPS’ annual commitment to allocate funding from year-end to major maintenance projects; and $40,338 to address a shortfall in Adult and Community Education (ACE). Other funding to support the online campus program is included at $0.6 million.

 

On June 18, 2018, staff provided the results of an internal security review and made recommendations to the School Board for opportunities for improvement. FY18 non-recurring, year-end funds of $1.0 million were recommended for the replacement of classroom locks as well as 8.0 positions to provide greater school-based training and increased frequency of training. Additionally, $2.2 million was recommended to fund 18.0 positions for mental health support and threat assessment follow-up. The positions recommended for safety and security are recurring and funded with additional state revenue.

 

All of the remaining funding available, $24.6 million, is recommended to be set aside for the FY20 budgeted beginning balance. The FY19 beginning balance is $24.2 million.

Subject:  FY19 Office of Auditor General (OAG) Risk Assessment and Audit Plan

 

Staff Contact:   Esther Ko, Auditor General

 

Meeting and Date:  July 26, 3018 - Work Session

           

 

  1. Objective

The objectives of the FY19 audit plan are:

  • To accomplish OAG’s mission, which is to independently determine whether the ongoing processes for FCPS are adequately designed, functioning in an efficient, effective manner, and fully accountable to the Fairfax County citizens, and
  • To promote FCPS’ achievement of strategic goals, particularly in the area of resource stewardship.

OAG adopted a risk assessment process to identify and prioritize audit topics.   

 

  1. Summary / Background

OAG evaluated financial, reputational and operational factors, as well as areas of opportunity for improvement in determining the risk level of the proposed audit topics.  Based upon the results, OAG assessed audit topics into three categories: primary, secondary, and long-term priorities.  OAG proposes to conduct audits listed as primary priorities in FY19.  These audit topics in alphabetical order are: (1) Business Process/Turnover Audits, (2) Local School Activity Funds Audit for Year ending June 30, 2018, (3) Local School Activity Funds Audit for Year ending June 30, 2019, (4) Medicaid Reimbursement Program, (5) Payroll Oversight and Process, (6) Safety and Security Compliance, (7) Specific Contracts, (8) Use of Trades and Staffing Reserves.

 

  Definition* FY19 OAG Audits
Financial Audits To provide an independent assessment of whether an entity's reported financial information are presented in accordance with recognized criteria. (2) & (3) Local School Activity Funds
Performance Audits To provide findings or conclusions based on an evaluation of sufficient, appropriate evidence against criteria (1) Business Process/Turnover, (4) Medicaid Reimbursement Program, (5) Payroll Oversight and Process, (6) Safety and Security Compliance, (7) Specific Contracts and (8) Use of Trades and Staffing Reserves

*Source: Government Auditing Standards - 2011 Revisions

 

OAG adopts the following internal audit process:

Risk assessment  -->   Audit Plan -->  Audit Execution -->  Audit Reporting --> Audit Follow-up   

 

  1. Outcome

As a result of FY19 risk assessment, OAG identified seven FY19 audit topics (same number of audit topics were identified for FY18) and one carry forward audit topic – Local School Fund Activity for Year ending June 30, 2018.  In addition, OAG will perform other duties, including (a) audit recommendations follow-up, (b) FY20 risk assessment, (c) fraud, waste and abuse inquiry management, (d) increase the use of audit technology, and (e) professional education requirements.

 

  1. Next Steps/Action Needed

Audit Committee reviewed and concurred with the FY19 OAG risk assessment and audit plan on May 29 and June 27.  On July 12, the risk assessment and audit plan are presented as new business with School Board.  There will be a work session on July 23.  The final approval and action will take place on July 26.  That the Board approve the FY19 Office of Auditor General (OAG) Risk Assessment and Audit Plan as detailed in the agenda item.

Human Resources

In alignment with the Strategic Plan goals of creating a Premier Workforce and embracing a Caring Culture, the HRAC focus for school year 2018 – 2019 will be employee retention. The HRAC is charged with exploring various factors that impact employee retention. These elements could include, but are not limited to:

  • Workload
  • Compensation (to include benefits)
  • Professional development
  • Leadership and promotion opportunities
  • Connection to the community
  • Work environment
  • Other areas developed by the HRAC

The HRAC will explore what motivates employees to remain with FCPS, as well as consider best practices being utilized across the area and nation for increasing employee retention. HRAC will develop a report that provides suggestions for ways FCPS can support employee retention. The report should include recommendations for areas of focus in a retention strategy that is incorporated into Goal 3: Premier Workforce.

Staff Contact:  Fabio Zuluaga, Region 2 Assistant Superintendent

 

 

Meeting Category: July 26, 2018 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: - Decision

 

 

Problem Statement:

 

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school. The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

  • Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2019 is $3,477,151. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $5,610,843.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

 

 

Outcome:

 

To continue to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.

 

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2018-2019.
