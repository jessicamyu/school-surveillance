RESOLUTION

HONORING

KAYE KORY

 

     WHEREAS, Kaye Kory was elected as the Mason District representative to the Fairfax County School Board in June 1999, and she served with distinction as a member of this Board for more than ten years, serving as Board vice chairman in 2004, as chairman of two of the Board’s standing committees—Facilities and Human Resources, as the School Board’s state legislative liaison, and as a Board liaison to numerous committees including the Fairfax County Athletic Council, Fairfax County Park Authority, and Title I-FECEP-Head Start; and

 

     WHEREAS, Kaye gravitated to the educational arena, seeking prekindergarten options when her children where young, and soon emerged as chairman of the Montessori School of Northern Virginia, one of the oldest in the region, and then she transitioned into PTA leadership positions at Sleepy Hollow Elementary School, Glasgow Middle School, and Stuart High School; and

 

     WHEREAS, she displayed a stellar record of community activism through her participation with a diverse range of community organizations such as the Boys and Girls Clubs of Fairfax County, the Fairfax Partnership for Youth, the Fairfax County Council of PTAs, the Fairfax County Association for the Gifted, the Annandale Rotary Club, and the J.E.B. Stuart Educational Foundation where she served as founding president; and

 

     WHEREAS, she also passionately promoted environmental stewardship throughout Fairfax County schools and facilities, demonstrated by the construction of Glasgow Middle School with its numerous energy-saving and environmentally-friendly features; and

 

     WHEREAS, she embraced the diversity of Fairfax County, and effectively, patiently, and energetically represented the interests of the community and articulated the characteristics and needs of the Mason District, approaching education issues with deliberate seriousness ensuring that the school system achieves the results expected by the community and deserved by the students it serves;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Kaye Kory for her valuable contributions and dedicated service to the Board, to Fairfax County Public Schools, and to the children and citizens of Fairfax County; and

 

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that School Board members and the Fairfax County Public School community send their sincere congratulations and very best wishes to Kaye in her new position as member of the Virginia House of Delegates for the 38th District.

5.06 Amendment 16 to the ERFC Benefit Plan
Meeting
Jun 24, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 22
Category
5. Consent Agenda - 9:35 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Approve Amendment 16 to the ERFC Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of simplifying administration and clarifying the application of certain administrative rules without changing benefits (HR; presented as new business 6/10/10)


Staff Contact:          Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

Meeting Category: June 24, 2010 – Regular Meeting No.22

Subject:                    Amendment 16 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

School Board Action Required: Decision

Related To:              Legal Requirement


Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending several amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFCand ERFC 2001) for the purpose of simplifying administration and clarifying the application of certain administrative rules. None of the proposed changes is expected to affect the benefits payable under the Plan.


             A summary of the proposed amendments is provided below.

    • A. Simplifying the administration of benefits for certain members who have credit for service before July 1, 1988
    • The benefit structure in ERFC was substantially amended in 1988 because of changes in the benefits provided by VRS. One benefit provision – an alternative calculation - was adopted at that time to preserve the value of certain benefits that members had expected under the pre-1988 plan. Now, more than 20 years later, the formula under the amended plan consistently produces a higher amount of benefits. In retirement calculations performed for retiring members over the past two and a half years, the alternative calculation has not produced a higher benefit for even one retiree. Analysis of the formula, moreover, demonstrates that it is extremely unlikely that this alternative calculation will produce a higher benefit for any future retirees. In connection with the recent automation of benefit calculations, the Trustees recommend amending section 5.02(b)(3) of the ERFC Plan document to streamline this administrative function by removing the alternative calculation.

             B. Clarifying rules for purchase of service credit in ERFC

 

            C. Clarifying the rules for leaving accumulated contributions on deposit

 

    • Members of ERFC (but not ERFC 2001) are permitted to purchase service credit for work that is not otherwise credited in the Plan. Under rules adopted by the Trustees, these purchases are more costly as a member approaches retirement; a purchase made less than five years before actual retirement is subject to a substantial monetary penalty both to encourage earlier purchases and to give the Plan a longer period to invest the proceeds of a purchase before the Plan begins to pay out benefits to the member who purchased the credit. When a member makes such a purchase with the intent of waiting five years before retiring but later changes his or her plans and retires sooner, the amount of the credit is recalculated so that the penalty is appropriately charged to the member. The Trustees recommend amending section 11.01(b) of the ERFC Plan document to clarify that the Plan will not refund the purchase price of the service credit to a member whose purchase is subject to such a recalculation.
  •  
    • Members who leave FCPS positions that are covered by ERFC or ERFC 2001 have the option of leaving their member contributions on deposit, and thus preserving their rights under the Plan in the event they return to covered employment at a later date. If the member was not vested in the Plan and ten years passes without the member returning to covered employment or requesting a refund of the member contributions, the Plan provides that the contributions shall become the property of the Plan, subject to refund (but without any additional interest) if the member or the member’s estate later requests the refund. The Trustees recommend amending the ten-year rule, in Section 12.02 of the ERFC Plan and in Section 7.02 of ERFC 2001, in order to clarify that it operates only if there is a single continuous ten-year absence from covered employment. D. Clarifying the rules for redeposit of member contributions after returning to covered employment
    • In ERFC 2001, a member who leaves covered employment and withdraws his or her member contributions has the right to redeposit those contributions and reinstate all prior credit in the Plan upon returning to covered employment within five years. (This rule does not apply in ERFC, because that is a closed Plan; a member returning to covered employment after withdrawing contributions from ERFC is not permitted to re-enter ERFC.) The Trustees recommend amending Section 7.04 of ERFC 2001 to clarify that the member must make this redeposit on his or her first return to covered employment after withdrawing the member contributions. E. Clarifying the time limits for taking legal action against ERFC or ERFC 2001
    • Both ERFC and ERFC 2001 contain two-year time limits for the commencement of any legal action against the Plan. These limits were adopted to protect the Plan, the Trustees, and the School Board from claimants seeking additional benefits many years after benefit payments had begun, and to provide a definite time period where the Virginia Code is unclear about the applicable time limits. The Trustees recommend amending Section 16.17 of the ERFC Plan and Section 11.15 of ERFC 2001 to clarify that in cases where the Plan has made erroneous benefit payments, the Trustees can file a suit (if necessary) to recover that money within two years after they determine that such an erroneous payment occurred.



Recommendation:

    • That the School Board approve Amendment 16 to the ERFC Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of simplifying administration and clarifying the application of certain administrative rules without changing benefits.

Subject:                    VRS Resolution

School Board Action Required: Decision

Related To:                Legal Requirement, Virginia House Bill 1189, Retirement System

Key Points:

The Virginia General Assembly, in its 2010 session passed legislation creating a separate retirement plan for employees hired on or after July 1, 2010 (hereafter referred to as “Plan 2” employees). The legislation amended Virginia Code to provide that Plan 2 employees will pay their 5 percent member contribution and that, local public school boards may pick-up, in whole or in part (in 1 percent increments), the 5 percent member contribution as an additional benefit.

Strategically, we do not wish to segment the workforce, effectively reducing starting salaries for newly hired VRS eligible employees by 5 percent. Therefore, the recommendation is to continue to pick up the employee contribution for all VRS eligible employees. This election is made by School Board resolution and is revocable and can be revisited annually if desired.


Recommendation:

That the School Board accept the VRS resolution as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:           Cluster III Assistant Superintendent


Meeting Category:  June 24, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 22


Subject:                    Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology


School Board Action Required:  Information


Related to:                N/A


Key Points: 


In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.  The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:


  • Additional state funding for the regional school.  The estimate for FY 2011 is $2,098,140.  The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $2,664,167.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor's School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

Options:  The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.


Recommendaton:


That the School Board approve Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) continue in 2010-2011 to accept the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor's Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia.

Subject
10.02 Certification of Closed Meeting Compliance
Meeting
Jun 24, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 22
Category
10. Closed Meeting
Type
Action

In order to comply with Section 2.2-3712 (D) of the Code of Virginia, it is necessary for the Board to certify that since the Fairfax County School Board convened a closed meeting on June 24, 2010, to the best of each member’s knowledge, only public business matters lawfully exempted from open meeting requirements and only such public business matters as were identified in the motion convening the closed meeting were heard, discussed, or considered by the Board during the closed meeting.


The foregoing also shall be deemed to include the certification of each School Board member who served on a student disciplinary committee that those closed committee meetings are lawfully exempted from open meeting requirements by Section 2.2-3711(A)(2) of the Code of Virginia, and that only student disciplinary matters were heard, discussed, or considered in those closed committee meetings.
