Subject:                  FY 2016 Budget and State Revenue Impact

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                        

 

Key Points:

 

Overview

On February 5, 2015, the School Board adopted the FY 2016 Advertised Budget that includes a requested increase in the county transfer of $70.6 million, or 3.99 percent, to help with the costs for enrollment growth and student demographic adjustments; full-day Mondays; step increases for eligible employees; and a 1.0 percent market scale adjustment for all employees. To balance the FY 2016 Advertised Budget, a shared approach of making expenditure reductions coupled with a requested increase in funding from Fairfax County was utilized. The Advertised Budget includes budget reductions totaling $55.4 million.

 

County’s Advertised Budget and Advertised Tax Rate

The County Executive presented his FY 2016 Advertised Budget on February 17, 2015, which included a school operating fund transfer increase of $56.65 million or 3.2 percent (3 percent plus the cost of Full-Day Mondays). The recommended transfer to the School Operating Fund results in a shortfall of $14.0 million as compared to FCPS' FY 2016 Advertised Budget. The County’s advertised budget recommends no increase in the current real estate tax rate of $1.09 per $100 of assessed value, and defers the funding of $13.1 million included in the Infrastructure Financing Committee recommendations approved by both boards until FY 2017 (leaving an unfunded gap in FCPS’ Capital Improvement Plan for FY 2016).

 

On February 27, the School Board and County Board of Supervisors held a joint budget work session to discuss both advertised budgets and the impact of the state budget, as approved by the General Assembly, on both the County’s and FCPS’ advertised budgets.

 

Since the joint meeting, the County advertised a real estate tax rate for FY 2016 of $1.09 per $100 of assessed value. Advertising an increase in the rate does not prevent the Board from lowering any advertised tax rate, but a higher tax rate cannot be imposed without advertising the higher rate. In FY 2016, the assessed value of residential real property is expected to increase by 3.4 percent and nonresidential property is expected to decrease by 0.6 percent.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

Based on data from the Virginia Department of Education calculated from the General Assembly’s adopted amendments which have been sent to the Governor, the General Assembly’s adopted amendments result in a net increase of $9.9 million as compared to FCPS’ advertised budget. Below is a summary of the adjustments as a result of the state budget:

 

  • $4.7 million increase in revenue for the compensation supplement
  • $0.8 million decrease in revenue due to the VRS rate decrease to 14.06 percent and the basic aid offset
  • $6.0 million decrease in expenditures due to the reduced VRS rate

 

Additional Considerations and Unknowns

Each spring, FCPS updates the enrollment and student demographic projections that are used to calculate staffing for the following school year. Staffing is calculated for each school at both the proposed (fall) and approved (spring) stages of the budget process. The final cost of enrollment growth and changes in student demographics is calculated in the spring, and any changes from the projected cost included in the proposed budget become part of the approved budget adjustments.

 

To date, the special education revised projections have been received and reflect an increased cost of $4.0 million. General Education, Advanced Academics, and English for Speakers of Other Languages projections are not yet complete.

Next Steps

The first step will be to calculate the impact of the outstanding enrollment projections. Once those are completed, a revised shortfall will be shared with the School Board. Any shortfall that remains after the state and county budgets are adopted will require additional expenditure adjustments to balance FCPS’ FY 2016 Approved Budget.
