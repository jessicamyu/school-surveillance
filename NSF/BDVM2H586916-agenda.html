Subject: CIP Amendment

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                 Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: September 16, 2019 – All-Day Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 - Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

In its discussion of the Capital Improvement Program (CIP) on January 22, 2018, the Board requested a review of Policy 8130.7, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings. The Board has held several work sessions to conduct its review.

 

Staff provided a draft Policy 8130 for discussion as requested by the School Board in the July 22, 2019, work session. At that meeting, the School Board directed the Superintendent and staff to work with the Board to identify boundary “hot spots” which should be included in the FY2020-24 CIP.

 

In addition to the challenges currently identified in the CIP, one hot spot has been named by the Board and the Superintendent: the McLean HS boundary. As such, an amendment to the FY2020-24 CIP is being proposed to include the scoping of the McLean HS boundary.

 

Recommendation:

 

The Board is asked to consider whether this amendment should be included in the FY2020-24 CIP.

 

 

Proposed Board Member Amendment:

 

I move to amend the FY2020-2024 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) by including Langley and McLean High Schools on the list of Priority Recommended Boundary Adjustments, as noted on page 46 of the CIP. (Strauss)

 

Additional Information:

The inclusion of these schools would allow staff to begin the scoping process this fall and make recommendations for potential boundary adjustments. It is important to state that any potential boundary adjustment will be discussed and decided through a transparent process that fully engages the community.

 

Page 89 of the CIP highlights potential solutions for the over-capacity situation at McLean HS:

A. Increase efficiency by reassigning instructional spaces within a school to accommodate increase in membership

C. Minor interior facility modifications to create additional instructional space and help to accommodate capacity deficit

D. Add temporary classrooms to accommodate short-term capacity deficit

F. Capacity enhancement through either a modular or building addition

H. Potential boundary adjustment with schools having a capacity surplus

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 1: Student Success; Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

Outcome:

 

To raise student achievement, eliminate achievement gaps, and support equitable opportunities for all students.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

On December 14, 2017, the School Board voted to allocate one-time funding of $0.25 million to the Office of the Chief Equity Officer (CEO) to support equity-related work.  At a work session held on March 12, 2018, the Board reviewed and discussed the Equity Resources and Initial Plan.  That plan described how the CEO departments and offices would use the $0.25 in new funding to focus more strategically on ways to provide equitable access and opportunities for all FCPS students.  

 

The purpose of this update to the Board is to provide information on how those resources have been used by the CEO and the Instructional Services Department to support equity work and progress in the following key areas:

  1. Implementing Strategic Plan goals of Student Success and Caring Culture.
  2. Ensuring anti-bias curriculum that promotes cultural understanding and historic integrity.
  3. Establishing the Office of the Family and Student Ombudsman and creating the first annual Ombudsman End-of-Year Report for School Year 2018-19.

Overall, it is critical that equity is embedded in all work throughout FCPS and is the responsibility of all offices and departments.
