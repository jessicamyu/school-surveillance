    RESOLUTION NAMING
    THE MEDIA CENTER AT ROBINSON SECONDARY SCHOOL
    “THE ANN MONDAY MEDIA CENTER”

    WHEREAS, Ann Monday served Fairfax County Public Schools for nearly 35 years with dedication and distinction as a teacher, an assistant principal, a principal, a cluster assistant superintendent, and, most recently, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department where, during the last three years, she energetically and effectively provided instructional leadership and guidance to the entire school division; and

    WHEREAS, she made significant contributions to ensure high quality education for all students through her spirited leadership, her tenacity, her uncompromising integrity, and her strong support and encouragement of school staff members to continuously improve instructional programs and services to meet the needs of all students; and

    WHEREAS, through extraordinary leadership and creative ability, she found and allocated the necessary resources to get the toughest projects completed, recognized the need for seamless preK-12 curriculum articulation in all instructional program areas, and encouraged and promoted exploration of different ways to think about the future of education in Fairfax County; and

    WHEREAS, throughout her career, Ann consistently demonstrated a clear understanding of the challenges facing students and staff members and approached these challenges with a commitment to the future while keeping an eye on the past and, as a leader, modeled a strong work ethic marked by commitment, courage, fairness, and determination; and

    WHEREAS, she fostered links of understanding as she worked closely with a wide variety of stakeholders—always listening carefully, judging fairly, and acting with honesty and integrity;

    WHEREAS, during her 14 years as Robinson Secondary School associate principal and principal, she compiled an impressive list of accomplishments that includes establishing the International Baccalaureate program, leading the reorganization and renovation process, consolidating and supervising the media center, providing the vision for academic excellence, and serving across all curricular and extracurricular arenas as the greatest Rams fan ever; and

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board takes great pride in naming the media center at Robinson Secondary School “The Ann Monday Media Center” in recognition of her steadfast belief in and commitment to life-long learning, and as a lasting tribute from all those whose lives she has touched; and

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Ann Monday for her inspirational leadership and outstanding service to Fairfax County Public Schools and for her unwavering dedication to the children of this community.

    Contact: Daniel G. Storck, Chairman and Mount Vernon District

    Subject: School Board Member Salary

    Recommendation:

        That, effective January 1, 2008, the annual salary for each member of the Fairfax County School Board be set at $20,000, and that the Chairman be paid an additional annual salary of $2,000.

    Explanation:

        According to the Code of Virginia, the Fairfax County School Board is required to approve any salary change prior to July 1 in the year in which its members stand for election and by a recorded vote of a majority of the School Board Members present at a public meeting. By law, the salary of the members of the Fairfax County School Board shall not exceed that which the members of the Board of Supervisors receive ($75,000 effective January 1, 2008), except that the Board may vote to pay its chairman an additional salary not exceeding $2,000 per year. There is no minimum salary requirement.

        Salaries for Board Members were last increased in 1999 from $8,000 to $12,000 and prior to that in 1987 from $5,500 to $8,000.

        Board Members spend more than 30 hours a week attending board, school, PTA, and community meetings, visiting schools, reviewing materials, attending training programs, and communicating with students, parents, staff, and community members. The compensation proposed for Board Members, on an equivalent hourly basis, will continue to remain lower than the hourly value of the wages and benefits that are paid to the lowest paid FCPS employee.

        FCPS is the 13th largest district in the nation and the largest school district in Virginia, with over 164,000 students and an annual budget of more than $2.2 billion. It is more than twice as large as the next largest Virginia district. School board member compensation for the largest nearby districts range from $18,000 to $20,000 per year.

    Meeting

    Category

    Contact: Phillip Niedzielski-Eichner, Providence District

    SUBJECT: Resolution in Support of Improving the No Child Left Behind Act

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve a resolution in support of improving the No Child Left Behind Act, urging the U.S. Congressional Delegation of the Commonwealth of Virginia to fully support H.R. 648 - also known as the No Child Left Behind Improvements Act of 2007- by becoming a co-sponsor of the bill or by co-sponsoring a comparable Senate version, as detailed in the agenda item.

    EXPLANATION:

        The National School Boards Association (NSBA) put together, in consultation with its affiliate state organizations, including the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA), for whom Fairfax County School Board member Stu Gibson is Federal Relations Chair. NSBA's positions are consistent with the School Board's 2006 Legislative Program as approved at the end of last year.

        H.R. 648, submitted by Rep. Don Young (R-AK-At Large), fully tracks NSBA's draft legislation to strengthen accountability for improving the academic performance of children enrolled in the nation's public schools. Building on the major reforms of NCLB, H.R. 648 offers improvements that address many of the unintended consequences of the federal legislation – and its implementing regulations -- while holding states and school districts accountable for the performance of children in their care. Specifically, H.R. 648 will:
          • Increase the flexibility for states to use additional types of assessments for measuring AYP—including growth models.
          • Grant states more flexibility in assessing students with disabilities and students not proficient in English for AYP purposes.
          • Create a student testing participation range, providing flexibility for uncontrollable variations in student attendance.
          • Allow schools to target resources to those student populations who need the most attention by applying sanctions only when the same student group fails to make adequate yearly progress (AYP) in the same subject for two consecutive years.
          • Ensure that students are counted properly in assessment and reporting systems.
          • Allow supplemental services to be offered in the first year of “improvement.”
          • Strengthen federal responsibility for funding.
          • Require NCLB testing and reporting for non-public schools for students receiving Title I services.

        A similar Senate version is expected to be introduced in the near future and the resolution therefore calls upon our Virginia Senators Warner and Webb to become co-sponsors.

    Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

    SUBJECT: Amendments to ERFC and ERFC 2001

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve proposed Amendment Number 13 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFCand ERFC 2001) for the purpose of (A) offering beneficiaries other than spouses an opportunity to roll over distributions received due to the death of a member, (B) clarifying the age at which standard benefit payments from ERFC are reduced after retirement, (C) permitting members who are working part-time for FCPS after age 65 to receive their benefits and continue working in their part-time position, (D) clarifying the effect under the plan of small benefits that are paid as a lump-sum actuarial equivalent, (E) clarifying the plan’s compliance with federal law for members who return to FCPS after a period of military service, (F) clarifying that members must currently be in eligible employment to purchase service credit in ERFC; and (G) updating cross-references in the plan documents.

    EXPLANATION:
        A summary of the proposed amendments in each category is provided below.
      A. Offering beneficiaries other than spouses an opportunity to roll over distributions received due to the death of a member.

        Until the Pension Protection Act (PPA) was signed into law in August 2006 federal law permitted only members and spouses to roll over distributions they receive from a qualified retirement plan into an IRA in order to continue deferring federal taxes on the distribution. Effective January 1, 2007, the PPA permits retirement plans to offer a roll-over opportunity to other beneficiaries who receive distributions due to the death of a member. The proposed amendment would allow ERFC beneficiaries to take advantage of this opportunity for tax deferral to the full extent of federal law.
      B. Clarifying the age at which standard benefit payments from ERFC are reduced after retirement.

        The ERFC plan was designed to supplement benefits that members receive from both VRS and Social Security. If a member retires before the age at which full Social Security benefits can begin, the standard benefit formula under ERFC pays a larger amount at retirement and a reduced amount after the member becomes eligible for full Social Security benefits. (The plan offers optional forms of benefit, with equivalent actuarial value, for those members who prefer to receive level lifetime benefits.) Under federal law, full Social Security benefits used to begin at age 65; that age now scales up to 67 based upon the individual’s birth date. The ERFC plan document currently refers to the age at which benefits change as “the member’s 65th birthday” but contains a general provision that the actual benefit change will occur when the member becomes eligible for unreduced Social Security benefits. The proposed amendment would add a definition of “Full Social Security Age” and use that term consistently to describe the age at which benefits will be reduced, so that the plan will be easier for members to understand.
      C. Permitting members who are working part-time for FCPS after age 65 to draw their ERFC benefits and continue to work for FCPS part-time.

        Currently, both ERFC and ERFC 2001 prohibit a deferred vested member from beginning ERFC payments while working in any capacity for Fairfax County Public Schools. This proposed amendment would allow deferred vested members, who reach age 65 while working part-time for FCPS (substitute teachers, part-time teachers, etc,), to draw their deferred vested ERFC benefit and continue to work part-time for FCPS.

      D. Clarifying the effect under the plan of small benefits that are paid as a lump-sum actuarial equivalent.

        The plan authorizes the trustees to provide by regulation for payment of small benefits in the form of a lump-sum actuarial equivalent. Such payments save the administrative costs of processing small checks. (Currently, the trustees’ regulation provides for lump-sum payment of benefits that are $100 or less per month, unless such a payment would cause hardship for the retiree.) The plan contains rules about retirees who return to work for FCPS while receiving monthly benefits, but it does not contain rules about retirees who return to work for FCPS after receiving a lump-sum payment of a small pension. The proposed amendment would provide that for the purposes of administering retirement benefits under ERFC, such a retiree would be treated the same as a member who withdrew his or her accumulated contributions upon leaving FCPS employment: the retiree would not have to pay back the lump sum he or she had received; if the retiree worked in eligible employment, he or she would be enrolled in ERFC 2001; and upon retiring a second time the retiree would receive the amount of any new benefits he or she had earned or a refund of accumulated contributions for that period of re-employment.

      E. Clarifying the plan’s compliance with federal law for members who return to FCPS after a period of military service.

        Federal law defines the rights of employees who leave civilian employment for a period of military service and return to the same civilian employer within a certain time after the military service ends. One of those rights is to redeposit (with interest) any retirement plan contributions that the employee withdrew in connection with the period of military service. Federal law defines the amount of time that the employee must be given to make that redeposit. ERFC 2001 contains provisions for a redeposit of service credit, but specifies a shorter period of time for that redeposit to be made. The proposed amendment would clarify that a member returning from a period of military service will be permitted to make such a redeposit within the time permitted by federal law but would not affect the period for other members to redeposit their withdrawn contributions.
      F. Clarifying that members must currently be in eligible employment to purchase service credit in ERFC.

        Members of ERFC are permitted to purchase service credit in the plan in order to qualify for a benefit sooner than they otherwise would or to increase their benefit amount. Until recently federal law has permitted such a purchase to be made only while actively employed, and the plan did not separately state this rule. Recent amendments to federal law permit purchases to be made at other times, but the Board of Trustees does not recommend liberalizing the plan’s practice. The proposed amendment would maintain the plan’s current practice by adding plan language to the effect that service credit can be purchased only when the member is in eligible employment, as defined by the plan.
      G. Updating cross-references in the plan documents.

        The final category of changes in the proposed amendment would simply update cross-references in the plan documents to reflect changes in numbering in the Virginia Code and in the plan documents.

    Meeting

    Category
    6. New Business
    Type

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

    SUBJECT: Acquisition of Sanitary Sewer Lateral and Temporary Construction Easements for Mountain View Alternative School site

    RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board (i) adopt a Resolution (see attached) approving proposed public uses and authorizing the acquisition, if necessary by condemnation, of sanitary sewer lateral and temporary construction easements (the “Easements”) necessary for such public uses, and (ii) authorize the Chief Operating Officer to execute the necessary documents to acquire such Easements, utilizing the School Board’s power of eminent domain if necessary, and to work with the School Board attorney to accomplish such acquisition promptly.
    EXPLANATION:

            Ten modular classrooms are being added to Mountain View Alternative School and are scheduled to be available when school starts this September. These classrooms require installation of a sanitary sewer lateral across a small portion of an adjacent shopping center site. To install this sewer lateral, the School Board must obtain a permanent sanitary sewer lateral easement and a temporary construction easement from the adjacent property owner - PW Limited Partnership (“PW”).
            School officials first approached PW in the summer of 2006 to request the easements. Last fall, PW orally agreed to grant the necessary easements, provided certain matters were addressed in the easement documents. The form of the Deed of Easement was agreed upon in January, 2007. To date, PW has not signed and delivered the Deed, despite numerous requests.
            While PW could still deliver the signed Deed, PW’s delay leaves the School Board no choice but to pursue acquisition of the easements by condemnation in time to complete the modular classroom project for the 2007-08 school year. The School Board can acquire the easements pursuant to its “quick-take” authority under law. With this procedure, installation of the sanitary sewer lateral could begin immediately after a Certificate of Taking is recorded and the amount of the appraisal - $13,808.00 - is deposited with the Circuit Court.
