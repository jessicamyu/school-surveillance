Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Week - May 21-27, 2012

The adult and community education program of Fairfax County Public Schools began with an action of the School Board in 1955, and, in the 57 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills and completed their high school educations.

Through the years,  adults have learned new languages, acquired job skills, transitioned to new careers, and grown as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to Adult & Community Education to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education has made major contributions to the school system as a laboratory of innovation for the piloting of new programs and services in support of pre-K-12 education through family literacy, summer programs, extended day programs, enrichment courses, and driver education.

Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner – as well as provision of remediation and enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day – ACE has helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

RESOLUTION

NAMING THE MAIN GYMNASIUM

“THE RONALD WILSON GYMNASIUM”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Coach Ronald Wilson dedicated his entire 37-year career in Fairfax County Public Schools to the students at J. E. B. Stuart High School, from 1974 until his retirement in 2011, and was a compassionate and patient teacher in both the physical education and special education departments, mentoring many students and helping them gain confidence to achieve their highest potential; and

 

     WHEREAS, Coach Wilson coached three sport seasons in cross country, basketball, and spring track, for 35 years of his career at J. E. B. Stuart High School, leading and inspiring countless numbers of young men and women to strive for athletic excellence while maintaining their academic integrity; and

 

     WHEREAS, he coached 37 years in the girls’ basketball program and for 27 of those years he was the varsity head coach in charge of all aspects of the program, and served as a reliable, responsible, and positive role model for his players, staff, school, and community; and

Belvedere Elementary School Named Title I Distinguished School

 

Belvedere Elementary School, a Fairfax County public school, has been named a Title I Distinguished School by the Virginia Board of Education. The awards recognize schools that have raised academic achievement for economically disadvantaged students and are based on state assessments during the 2009-10 and 2010-11 school years.

 

Belvedere Elementary is among 92 schools statewide that are recognized for maintaining full state accreditation under Virginia’s Standards of Learning (SOL) program for two consecutive years, meeting benchmarks of the No Child Left Behind Act (NCLB) in reading and math, and posting average test scores in reading and math at the 60th percentile or higher.           

 

Title I of NCLB provides funding to school divisions and schools for programs to raise the achievement of students identified as being at risk of academic failure. The federal law requires schools and school divisions to meet annual objectives for increasing student achievement on statewide assessments in reading and/or language arts and math.

Staff Contact:          Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present: Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:    May 24, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                      FY 2013 Approved Budget    

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                                               

 

Key Points:

 

On February 9, 2012, the School Board adopted the FY 2013 Advertised Budget.  The County Board of Supervisors subsequently adopted their FY 2013 budget on May 1, 2012, and included funding that was $63.3 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget. The state budget is expected to be finalized on May 14, 2012.  Based on the budget passed by the General Assembly during the 2012 special session and submitted to the Governor, FCPS projects increased state revenue of $44.0 million as compared to the advertised budget. 

 

Based on the total available revenue for FY 2013, and in response to the state adopted mandate regarding employee contributions to the Virginia Retirement System, the following adjustments to the FY 2013 Advertised Budget are recommended:

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue reflects a net reduction of $14.3 million as compared to the advertised budget.  Funding from the county is $63.3 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by an increase is state revenue of $44.0 million based on the projected state budget and an estimated increase of $5.0 million in revenue from Fairfax City.

 

Expenditures:

Required expenditure adjustments total $22.4 million compared to the advertised budget and include the following: $8.1 million as a result of retirement rate increases above the increase projected in the advertised budget; $6.7 million to implement the first year of a mandatory shift in the employee contribution for the Virginia Retirement System, which is required to be offset by a salary increase; $4.9 million for life insurance rate increases; $2.3 million for membership and demographic adjustments based on the updated spring enrollment projections; $0.5 million in support of high school band programs; and a decrease of $0.1 million for world languages based on the enrollment at the schools selected to implement the Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools program in FY 2013.  These required adjustments will be offset by reductions of $36.7 million that the School Board will identify prior to the budget being adopted on May 24, 2012.

 

To meet the required level of expenditure reductions, the Superintendent has identified reductions totaling $36.7 million which are detailed on pages 4-7 of the agenda item.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2013 Approved Budget, as detailed in the FY 2013 Advertised Budget and modified with the adjustments detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:  May 24, 2012- Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                 Science Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee Members

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:            Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

 

Key Points:

 

This committee will review and recommend instructional materials for use in K-12 science courses.  These materials will be considered basal materials. 

 

The Constitution of Virginia gives the Virginia Board of Education the authority to adopt instructional materials for use in the schools.  Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Regulation 3004.3 Adopted Basal Instructional Materials incorporates state adoption guidelines and requirements and specifies procedures for establishing committees to review textbooks and other basal instructional materials and make recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board for local adoption.  Each FCPS  review committee is composed as follows:

  • Teachers 50%
  • School-based administrators 25%
  • Community Members 25%

 

Central office staff members serve as nonvoting, ex-officio members to guide and assist the work of   the committee.  All recommended materials will be reviewed for content accuracy by a qualified content expert prior to public review.

 

Each School Board member shall appoint one community member to the Science Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee.   School based administrators and teachers will be nominated by Cluster Assistant Superintendents.  Participating staff members will be from the Instructional Services Department, Department of Special Services, and schools. 

 

The School Board will approve members to review and recommend basal instructional materials to be used in Fairfax County Public Schools.

 

Recommendation:   

 

That the School Board appoint committee members to the Science Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

The School Board Office ensures that school system policies are reviewed at least every five years, and revised as needed, in compliance with the Code of Virginia.  Those policies with no change, minimal change, or change in department of responsibility are posted on the directives page of the School Board web site for a two-week review period; those policies are then scheduled for Board approval.
