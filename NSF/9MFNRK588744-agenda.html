Superintendent’s Relationship With the Board

 

The Board values a harmonious and positive relationship with the Superintendent characterized by excellent communication, mutual respect, personal ethics, and professional integrity and guidance.  The Board values collaboration to promote a strong team of 13.  To assure this result, the Superintendent shall assure that the Board is fully and adequately informed about matters relating to Board work and significant district concern.

 

The Superintendent shall:

1.      Submit required monitoring data (see S.1, Superintendent Delegated Authority, or S.2, Accountability and Evaluation) in a thorough, accurate and understandable fashion, according to the Board’s annual work plan schedule, and including both Superintendent interpretations and relevant data to substantiate district operational condition or reasonable progress.

2.      Present information in simple and concise form, indicating clearly whether the information is incidental, intended for decision preparation, or for formal monitoring.

3.      Provide for the Board in a timely manner information about trends, facts and other information relevant to the Board’s work, informing the Board of:

a. significant transfers of money within funds or other changes substantially affecting the district’s financial condition;

b. anticipated significant media coverage;

c. administrative actions and decisions that are delegated to the Superintendent, but required by law to be approved by the Board;

d. administrative disposition of matters referred to the Superintendent by the Board;

e. significant initiatives, and program and operational changes;

f. important personnel issues.

4       Serve and lead the Board as its professional education expert by:

a. offering professional advice to the Board with recommendations based on thorough study and analysis supported by ample information, including assessment of alternatives, to enable the Board to make informed decisions;

b. serving as thought leader to the Board on current and emerging trends that impact public education;

5.      Maintain an ethical and harmonious working relationship with the entire Board by:

  1. treating all members impartially and assuring that all members have equal access to information;
  2. refraining from criticism of individual members;
  3. responding to Board and individual member concerns in a timely and courteous manner;
  4. communicating immediately and directly with the Board or individual Board members when an honest difference of opinion exists;
  5. promoting a positive relationship in Board meetings;
  6. encouraging an open exchange of information between Board and staff members;
  7. discussing with the Board or individual members any perceived violation of Governance Culture or Superintendent (S.1 or S.2) policies, or any encroachment by an individual member or the Board into areas of responsibility assigned to the Superintendent.

Subject:                    FY 2017 Budget      

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                         

 

Key Points:

 

Background

Since FY 2008, FCPS’ enrollment has consistently increased, resulting in FCPS being the tenth largest school system in the United States. The increase in the number of FCPS’ students is almost the size of Arlington County Public Schools’ total enrollment. While experiencing significant enrollment growth and changes in student demographics, revenues have not kept pace with expenditures. 

 

To balance the budget, FCPS has taken significant budget reductions since FY 2008 that total nearly half a billion dollars. In FY 2015, budget reductions totaled nearly $100 million and included the elimination of 724 full-time equivalent positions. With the significant financial challenges projected for FY 2017, FCPS took a strategic and targeted approach to the FY 2016 budget including $61.4 million in targeted reductions protecting the classroom and student programs which were significantly impacted by the prior reductions.

 

FY 2017 Preliminary Fiscal Forecast

Fairfax County Public Schools expects the school year 2016-17 (FY 2017) budget to be a tremendous challenge with an anticipated deficit of more than $100 million prior to any increase in the County transfer. Attached is a chart detailing the preliminary fiscal forecast for FY 2017.

 

Budget Planning Process for FY 2017

Due to multiple years of insufficient revenue coupled with significant enrollment growth and increasing student needs, and without a significant increase in funding for FY 2017, FCPS will not be able to sustain the current quality nor the full range of academic programming we currently offer our students.

 

We have sought to protect the classroom to date, but with the scale of the FY 2017 shortfall that has been forecasted and discussed over the last year, FCPS will have to consider increasing fees and reducing, eliminating, and/or redesigning programs and services for the 2016-17 school year. These fees and budget reductions may affect all current academic programming including limiting elective choices, reducing career and technical programs, impacting advanced offerings, and again raising class sizes at all levels.

 

Budget Timeline

In order to ensure a smooth opening for FY 2017, schools need to develop course offerings and students need to select their courses during the winter. As a result, difficult decisions will need to be made in time to be included in the proposed budget providing the necessary planning time for students and schools. Attached is a detailed budget timeline.   

 

Budget Task Force

FCPS is forming a Budget Task Force of stakeholders to assist the Superintendent in the development of the FY 2017 budget. To begin the process of strategically developing budget scenarios to address FCPS’ projected deficit for FY 2017, the Budget Task Force will work to:

  • Identify a comprehensive list of potential fee increases and program reductions
  • Obtain community input to identify reductions and fee increases and then to help in prioritizing the potential reductions and fee increases
  • Develop three scenarios ranging from $50 million to $100 million in reductions and fees.
