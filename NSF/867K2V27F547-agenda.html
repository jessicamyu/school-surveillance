Agenda Item: School-based GT Centers

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services

Background

The GT programs office provides a continuum of gifted services for students beginning in kindergarten. The fourth and highest level of service for elementary students is a GT center program. Currently FCPS has 23 GT centers at the elementary level and eligible students are bussed to the GT centers from surrounding schools. School-based GT centers began in FY04 as an option for gifted learners who need the challenge of a GT center curriculum and do not want to leave their local school. There are currently 17 elementary schools offering the GT center curriculum to center eligible students and other advanced learners. School-based GT centers also provide another avenue of access to Level IV gifted services for historically underrepresented populations.

Key Issues
    GT school-based centers may have smaller groups of center eligible students; therefore more flexible grouping is needed.
    GT school-based center teachers have a smaller cadre of colleagues who are trained in gifted education for planning and collaboration.
    Offering the school-based GT center option may impact GT centers (decrease size).
    Increased student enrollment may impact school facility at school-based center site.
    More center eligible students are receiving Level IV services and this may increase numbers in middle school GT centers.
    School-based centers provide greater access to GT curriculum.
    School-based centers have higher levels of participation by Asian, Black, and Hispanic students.
