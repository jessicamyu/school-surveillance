                      RESOLUTION HONORING
                      PATRICK K. MURPHY
                      WHEREAS, Pat Murphy served Fairfax County Public Schools for 19 years with dedication and distinction as a physical education teacher, a director of student activities, an assistant principal, an associate principal, a middle school principal, a cluster assistant superintendent, and the assistant superintendent for the Department of Accountability where he effectively, efficiently, and energetically represented and served the interests of his students, schools, department, and community; and

                      WHEREAS, as cluster assistant superintendent Pat supervised and evaluated 23 principals, K-12, and two centers supporting a diverse population of approximately 22,000 students, consistently inspiring people to do their best and to utilize their strengths;

                      WHEREAS,Dr. Murphy proactively and successfully led, managed, and facilitated front-line community and parent issues on grading and reporting and school bell schedules that brought high levels of interaction with parents, School Board members, and the community and, in doing so, built relationships and set high expectations to cultivate and foster the passion for all members of the community to be advocates for children; and

                      WHEREAS, Pat monitored, evaluated, and forecast student achievement performance data and program evaluation for divisionwide budget, funding, and program decision making and, thereby, improved school division cost efficiencies and operational management; and

                      WHEREAS, Dr. Murphy implemented a data-driven strategic plan with the School Board and the Superintendent to achieve outstanding student academic and operational performance goals and objectives and consistently demonstrated outstanding accountability measures in annual monitoring reports, receiving two commendations from the School Board in the last three years; and

                      NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Dr. Pat Murphy for his inspirational leadership and outstanding service to Fairfax County Public Schools and for his unwavering dedication to children and learning.

                      Subject: FY 2009 Final Budget Review

                      School Board Action Required: Information

                      Key Points:
                          All of the FY 2009 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual independent audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the funds under the control of the School Board. All comparisons are against the FY 2009 estimate. The unaudited FY 2009 ending balance, after meeting the FY 2010 budgeted beginning balance and other commitments, is $25.2 million.

                          FY 2009 actual revenue was $0.7 million higher than the estimate due to a net decrease in state revenue of $0.6 million resulting from a $4.1 million decrease in sales tax revenue offset by a $3.5 million increase in basic aid; a net reduction of $3.6 million in federal funding resulting from $6.7 million in unspent multi-year federal grant funds that will be carried forward offset by an increase of $3.1 million in Impact Aid and E-rate payments; and a net increase of $5.0 million in other revenue.

                          In recognition of budget reductions included in the FY 2010 Approved Budget, schools and departments conserved resources where possible during FY 2009. In addition, compensation savings resulted from positions held vacant prior to being eliminated. Expenditure savings were primarily realized from $9.0 million in compensation; $6.7 million in unspent federal grant funds that will carry forward to FY 2010; $4.1 million in vehicle services and fuel; and $2.4 million in utilities.

                          After the annual independent audit is completed, the available balance from FY 2009 will be available to be set aside for the FY 2011 budgeted beginning balance. This action will be addressed during the FY 2010 Midyear Budget Review.

                          The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:
