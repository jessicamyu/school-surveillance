RELATIONSHIP WITH THE BOARD: The Board values a harmonious and positive relationship with the Superintendent characterized by excellent communication, mutual respect, personal ethics, and professional integrity and guidance. To assure this result, the Superintendent will:
      1. Maintain an ethical and harmonious working relationship with the entire Board. The Superintendent will:
          • treat all members impartially;
          • refrain from criticism of individual members;
          • respond to Board concerns in a timely and courteous manner;
          • communicate immediately and directly with the Board or individual Board members when an honest difference of opinion exists; and
          • promote a positive relationship in Board meetings.
      2. Keep the Board informed on issues, needs, policies, and operation of the school system and:
          • offer professional advice to the Board with appropriate recommendations based on thorough study and analysis supported by ample information, including assessment of alternatives, to enable Board members to make informed decisions.
      3. Encourage communication and cooperation between Board members and staff members so as to be responsive to concerns of Board members.
      4. Interpret, implement, and support Board policy to staff members and the public, including the implementation of Board priorities (goals) in budget planning and in all operational areas defined and monitored by the Board.
      5. Interpret needs of school personnel and students to the Board and
          • keep Board members informed of personnel problems and methods used to solve problems.

Presentation on Best Practices in Advanced Academics by:
Sally M. Reis, Ph.D., Board of Trustees Distinguished Professor Teaching Fellow, Department of Educational Phychology, University of Connecticut

Dr. Sally M. Reis, a national leader in gifted education will make a presentation on research-based best practices in the field. Dr. Reis is a Board of Trustees Distinguished Professor and Teaching Fellow in Educational Psychology at the University of Connecticut where she also serves as Principal Investigator of the National Research Center on the Gifted and Talented. She was a teacher for 15 years, 11 of which were spent working with gifted students on the elementary, middle, and high school levels. She has authored more than 130 articles, 14 books, 60 book chapters, and numerous monographs and technical reports.

Her research interests are related to special populations of gifted and talented students, including: students with learning disabilities, gifted females, and diverse groups of talented students. She is co-author of The Schoolwide Enrichment Model, The Secondary Triad Model, Dilemmas in Talent Development in the Middle Years, and a book about talent development in females entitled Work Left Undone: Choices and Compromises of Talented Females. Dr. Reis serves on the editorial board of Gifted Child Quarterly, and is a past-president of the National Association for Gifted Children. She recently was honored with the highest award in her field, the Distinguished Scholar of the National Association for Gifted Children.

Other Staff Present: Denise James, Director, Facilities Planning Services

Meeting Category: June 8, 2009 - Work Session No. 121
Subject: Proposed Program/Attendance Area Adjustment

School Board Action Required: Information

Related To: School Board Policy 8130.5, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings

Key Points:
      The Board authorized two spring boundary studies to provide enrollment relief to Ft. Belvoir Elementary School and to Annandale High and Poe Middle Schools. Both of these studies attempt to address the current severe overcrowding that is projected to continue at these schools. The format of these studies is in accordance with the scope authorized by the Board, which brings forward a single option for consideration for each study. Both studies have been undertaken in a slightly modified calendar and process, which included community informational meetings and outreach, backpack notices, press releases, and web site postings.

      Ft. Belvoir: This study is limited to Ft. Belvoir Elementary and Woodlawn Elementary Schools. A single option is recommended in order to provide relief to Ft. Belvoir Elementary with the reassignment of the Woodlawn Village housing area to Woodlawn Elementary School. Implementation is proposed for the 2009-10 school year. Rising 6th grade students would be grandfathered consistent with Board policy. This boundary change should be considered as an interim measure until such time as additional elementary school facilities can be provided on Ft. Belvoir.

      Annandale: This study is limited to Annandale and Falls Church High Schools and Lake Braddock Secondary School and Poe and Glasgow Middle Schools. A single option is recommended in order to relieve over-enrollment at Annandale High and Poe Middle Schools. Implementation is proposed for the 2010-11 school year. Rising 8th and 12th graders from Poe Middle and Annandale High Schools would be grandfathered consistent with Board policy. Staff also recomemends grandfathering those 11th grade Annandale students who are declared IB diploma candidates. Staff is reviewing potential transfer options for students at the middle school level who may wish to move to their new school for the 2009-10 school year to avoid having to attend three different schools over a three-year period.
Recommendation:
          1. That the School Board approve adjustments to school attendance areas, effective for the 2009-10 school year, related to the Fort Belvoir Elementary School as shown in Appendix A and pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4), and Policy 8130.5, Local School Boundaries-Program Assignments and School Closing.
          2. That the School Board approve adjustments to school attendance areas, effective for year 2010-11 school year, related to Annandale High School and Poe Middle School as shown in Appendix B and pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4), and Policy 8130.5, Local School Boundaries-Program Assignments and School Closing.

Meeting Category: June 8, 2009 - Work Session No. 122
Subject: ERFC Annual Report to the School Board

School Board Action Required: Information

Related To: N/A

Key Points:
          1. In FY 2009, members hired on or after July 1, 2001 (and thereby covered under the ERFC 2001 benefit structure) comprised 55 percent of ERFC’s total active membership.

          2. During CY 2008, ERFC paid benefits totaling more than $134 million. ERFC paid $95 million or 71 percent of all benefits, to Virginia residents.

          3. ERFC maintained stable strategic investment targets during CY 2008. Nevertheless, as the equity markets deteriorated significantly during the 3rd and 4th quarters, the Portfolio grew overweight to fixed income.

          4. ERFC’s 1-year and 3-year performance figures lagged against the System’s benchmarks in CY 2008 due to the managers’ poor relative performance. However, the Plan measured slightly ahead of its benchmarks at the 5-year and 10-year marks.

          5. Very poor returns in the capital markets caused ERFC’s asset market value to decline in CY 2008, from just below $2 billion in January to slightly less than $1.4 billion by year end.

          6. Due to significant asset losses during CY 2008, the Employer Contribution rate is expected to increase beginning in FY 2012. However, two changes in ERFC’s Funding Policy will help to mitigate the amount of the increase.

          7. ERFC’s Funding Policy will result in a NET Pension Obligation (NPO) on FCPS’s financial statements for FY 2011.

          8. ERFC’s Funding Ratio fell from 88 percent at the end of CY 2007, to 77 percent at the end of CY 2008. Unless the capital markets rebound significantly prior to year-end, ERFC expects its Funding Ratio will fall further at the end of CY 2009.

          9. ERFC’s expense ratio increased from 0.60 percent to 0.64 percent in CY 2008 due primarily to asset deterioration in the fourth quarter of the year. Investment manager expenses account for approximately two-thirds of total expenses.
