Read Across America Day - March 2, 2012

 

National Education Association's (NEA) Read Across America is an annual reading motivation and awareness program that calls for every child in every community to celebrate reading on or around Dr. Seuss's birthday.  This year’s celebration showcases The Lorax, Dr. Seuss’ ecological warning, first published in 1971. In cities and towns across the nation, teachers, teenagers, librarians, politicians, actors, athletes, parents, grandparents, and others develop NEA's Read Across America activities to bring reading excitement to children of all ages. Governors, mayors, and other elected officials recognize the role reading plays in their communities with proclamations and floor statements. Athletes and actors issue reading challenges to young readers. Teachers and principals seem to be more than happy to dye their hair green or be duct-taped to a wall if it boosts their students' reading.

 

National Foreign Language Week, March 5-9, 2012

 

The week of March 5-9, 2012, has been designated as National Foreign Language Week.  The Alpha Mu Gamma foreign language honor society has sponsored National Foreign Language Week since 1957, and the theme for 2012 is “You’re Connected…Now Communicate."  Schools are encouraged to use this opportunity to highlight the value of learning world languages and learning about other cultures, and to celebrate the successes of world language students and teachers.

 

National School Breakfast Week, March 5-9, 2012

 

National School Breakfast Week was launched in 1989 by the School Nutrition Association to raise awareness of the availability of the School Breakfast Program to all children and the links between eating a good breakfast, academic achievement and healthy lifestyles.  The theme for 2012, “School Breakfast—Go for Gold,” highlights how eating a healthy balanced breakfast at school helps students shine.  The campaign will help students learn about the importance of eating healthy and being active,and aligns with the USDA’s HealthierUS School Challenge and the First Lady's Let's Move Goals.

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2012

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  The purpose of Music in Our Schools Month is to raise awareness of the importance of music education for all children - and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music. It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages.

National Athletic Training Month - March 2012

 

Certified athletic trainers employed by Fairfax County Public Schools provide quality health care for individuals involved in physical activity based on specific tasks, knowledge, and skills acquired through their nationally regulated educational processes. 

Athletic training services provided by certified athletic trainers include the prevention of athletic injuries; the recognition and evaluation of athletic injuries; the treatment, rehabilitation, and disposition of athletic injuries; determining return to participation status; and the education and guidance of athletes. 

 

The FCPS Athletic Training Program is nationally recognized for leadership in concussion research, and FCPS athletic trainers are the recipients of numerous awards in the athletic training field.

Staff Contact:            Dan Parris, assistant superintendent, Cluster III

 

Other Staff Present:  Jim Kacur, assistant superintendent, Cluster II

 

Meeting Category:     February 23, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 14

 

Subject:                    Naming of School

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Background: 

 

On January 18, 2012, a meeting was held at Falls Church High School for the purpose of naming the new elementary school at the Lacey site.  Those present included Mason District School Board member Sandy Evans, Cluster II Assistant Superintendent Jim Kacur, Cluster III Assistant Superintendent Dan Parris, Principal Brian Butler and representatives from the community.  In accordance with Regulation 8170.4, public notice included information on the FCPS website, e-mails and letters sent to parents in the affected communities.

 

Key Points: 

 

Parents, students, staff and community members attended a meeting to consider and recommend names for the new school.  As residents checked in to vote, they were given four different-colored dots.  Three would be used in the first round of voting using a weighted system to vote on the entire list of names recommended for the new school.  The fourth dot would be used in a second round of unweighted voting, if it was needed.  After presentations, questions and audience discussion, a total of nine names were recommended and the voting process was explained as follows:

 

-Each voter would place their first three dots on their first, second and third choices.

-They could vote only once for any particular name.

-It was explained that if no one name received more than fifty percent of the points, there would be another round of voting with the two names that received the most points.

 

Participants voted for their top three choices using a weighted point system (first choice=3 points, second choice=2 points, and third choice=1 point).  The two names that received the highest point total during the first round of voting were Mason Crest Elementary School and Masonville Elementary School.  The complete set of data derived from the first round of voting is listed below.  Eighteen residents registered to vote, with one eligible voter leaving before the voting began.

 

First Round of Voting

 

Mason Crest - 33 points

Masonville - 22 points

Mason - 11 points

Ruby Bridges - 7 points

Crestview - 4 points

Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis - 3 points

Obama - 3 points

Mason Hills - 2 points

North Annandale - 0 points

 

Using their fourth dot, each voter was asked to vote for one name.  Each dot represented one vote.  There was no weighting.

 

Second Round of Voting

 

Mason Crest - 10 votes

Masonville - 7 votes

 

 

Recommendation (two options): 

  • That the School Board name the new elementary school to be opened in September 2012 at the Lacey site "Mason Crest Elementary School."

         OR

  • That the School Board name the new elementary school to be opened in September 2012 at the Lacey site "Masonville Elementary School."

On February 8, 2012, eight bidders submitted sealed bids for the above-referenced Projects, consisting of the Spring Hill Elementary School Addition (the “Base Bid” or “Spring Hill”) and the Wolftrap Elementary School Innovation Lab Addition (the “Add Alternate” or “Wolftrap”). The Projects are subject to separate funding sources. Funding for the Spring Hill Project will be provided from the 2009 School Bond Referendum, and the lowest bid amount is within those available funds.  The planned source of funds for the Wolftrap Project is an independent third-party grant, the amount of which is currently less than the winning bid. Following the public opening of the bids, school system officials informed the grantor of this information, and the grantor is considering the possibility of increasing the grant amount so that the Wolftrap project also may proceed at this time.    

 

The Instructions to Bidders required each bidder to submit a separate price on its bid form for each Project.  The Instructions also specified that, provided the bid amount for each Project was within available funds as established within that Project’s respective funding source, the combined Projects would be awarded based on the aggregate amount of the prices as set forth in the lowest bid.  The Instructions also stated that in the event the Add Alternate (Wolftrap) bid price exceeded available funds from the third-party grant, the Base Bid would be awarded alone, provided that the lowest bid for the Base Bid (Spring Hill) was within its available funding limit.  Whether bid prices are evaluated on an aggregate basis, or solely on the basis of the Base Bid (Spring Hill), Meridian Construction Company, Inc., is the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, as shown below.

Meeting Category:     February 23, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 14

 

Subject:                      Naming of School

 

School Board Action Required:   Information

 

Background:

 

On February 13, 2012, a meeting was held at South County Secondary School for the purpose of recommending names for the new middle school at the South County site.  Those present included Mount Vernon District School Board Member Dan Storck, Cluster V Assistant Superintendent Frances W. Ivey, Principal Marsha Manning and representatives from the community.  In accordance with Regulation 8170.4, public notice of the meeting included information on the FCPS website, e-mails and letters sent to parents in the affected communities.  

 

 

Key Points:

  

 Parents, students, staff and community members attended the meeting to consider and recommend names for the new school.  As residents checked in to vote, they were given seven different-colored dots.  Three were used in the first round using a weighted system to vote on the entire list of names suggested for the new school.  Another set of three different-colored dots were used in a second round of voting, again using a weighted system.  The last dot was to be used in a third round of unweighted voting, if it was needed to break a tie.  After presentations, questions and audience discussion, a total of twenty-eight names was recommended and the voting process was explained as follows:

 

Voters would place their first three dots on their first, second and third choices.

They could vote only once for any particular name.

It was explained that after the tallies were completed on the first round, a second round of voting would be conducted to further narrow the list of names. 

 

Participants voted for their top three choices using a weighted point system (first choice = 3 points, second choice = 2 points, and third choice = 1 point).  The five names that received the highest point totals during the first round of voting were Harold J.
