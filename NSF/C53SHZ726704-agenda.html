RESOLUTION

HONORING

WOMEN’S HISTORY MONTH

MARCH 2022

 

 

 

WHEREAS, March is Women’s History Month and this year’s theme “Women Providing Healing, Promoting Hope”, is both a tribute to the ceaseless work of caregivers and frontline workers during this ongoing pandemic and also a recognition of the thousands of ways that women of all cultures have provided both healing and hope throughout history; and

 

WHEREAS, Women’s History Month began as “Women’s History Week” in Santa Rosa, California in 1978, to coincide with “International Women’s Day” on March 8.  The goal of this event was to bring attention to women’s historical achievements.  Many communities across the country adopted this special recognition.  This movement became so widespread that in 1987 Congress ultimately designated the month of March as Women’s History Month; and

 

WHEREAS, we honor those who gathered at Seneca Falls in 1848 for the first women’s rights convention, organized by Elizabeth Cady Stanton and Lucretia Mott, launching the women’s suffrage movement.  The convention’s manifesto “The Declaration of Sentiments,” drew its inspiration from Thomas Jefferson’s Declaration of Independence but added the words “woman” and “women” throughout to call upon “women to fight for their constitutionally guaranteed right to equality as U.S. citizens.”; and

 

WHEREAS, the ratification of the 19th Amendment to the Constitution of the United States, which provides, “The right of citizens of the United States to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any state on account of sex” and the enactment of the Voting Rights Act of 1965, which extended the protection of the right to vote to women of color and language minorities; and

 

WHEREAS, Susan B. Anthony, one of the most known suffragists, traveled the country giving speeches to persuade people to support a woman's right to vote.  Virginia's own Ellen Glasgow, an author, who won the Pulitzer Prize for literature, and is the namesake of our own Glasgow Middle School here in Fairfax County, was also active in the suffrage movement; 

 

NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, on behalf of the Fairfax County Commission for Women and all its residents, hereby proclaims March 2022 as Women’s History Month, and a time to remember and honor the countless women who fought relentlessly for the rights of all women in our great Nation.

National Athletic Training Month - March 2022

 

March is National Athletic Training Month and this year’s theme is “Providing Health Care Everywhere” an appropriate slogan reflecting the important role certified athletic trainers employed by Fairfax County Public Schools play in keeping our students safe.  FCPS Athletic Trainers are licensed medical professionals who dedicate themselves to promoting the health and safety of our student-athletes before, during and after practices and competitions.  FCPS’ 50 athletic trainers consistently go above and beyond to support our athletic programs and without question are essential members of each school’s activities program.  FCPS athletic programs have been operating nonstop since July, 2020, offering both in-season and out-of-season opportunities for high school students.  These activities, directly supported by school-based Activities Office staff members including Certified Athletic Trainers, facilitated over 337,000 contacts between students and their coaches, teammates and friends during the first year of the pandemic.  Since July 2020 through February, 2022, FCPS athletic trainers have provided care to over 8500 individual student-athletes, treating more than 23,000 cuts, scrapes, bumps, bruises, sprains, strains, and fractures.  Even when faced with unprecedented circumstances, FCPS athletic trainers play a vital role in our overall efforts to support athletic opportunities for all FCPS students.

RESOLUTION

HONORING THE

“SPREAD THE WORD: INCLUSION” CAMPAIGN

 

WHEREAS, individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities, a group made up of over 200 million people of all backgrounds, continue to be excluded and isolated from their peers in schools, workplaces, and communities around the world; and

 

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools has committed to fostering a responsive, caring, and inclusive culture where all feel valued, supported, and hopeful; and

 

WHEREAS, studies show students with disabilities are two to three times more likely to be bullied and inclusive school settings can help reduce bullying, improve academic and social outcomes for students with disabilities, and positively impact the moral and ethical development of all students; and

 

WHEREAS, “mental retardation” was a medical term introduced in 1961 for people with intellectual disabilities that has become a hurtful and bullying slur that remains prevalent, especially in social media posts; and

 

WHEREAS, Special Olympics, Best Buddies and other advocacy organizations have collected millions of pledges to end the use of the R-word to be respectful and inclusive to those with intellectual and developmental disabilities through the “Spread the Word to End the Word” campaign; and

 

WHEREAS, the “Spread the Word to End the Word” campaign became “Spread the Word: Inclusion” to focus beyond the use of one word to promoting the inclusion of people with intellectual and developmental disabilities in all aspects of our society; 


NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board encourages all school and community members to pledge to end the use of the R-word and work to promote respect for and inclusion of those with intellectual and developmental disabilities in the school, the workforce, and the community. I so move.

National Social Work Month - March 2022

 

March is National Social Work Month and this year’s theme is “Time to Shine”.  School Social workers are professionals who are trained to examine and address life’s challenges in a holistic way.  They have expertise in many areas including mental health, human development, behavior, trauma, and family systems.

 

School Social workers in Fairfax County Public Schools use their training, experience, and skills to provide services to children, families, and school staff members to remove personal and environmental obstacles for supporting student academic performance.  They are committed to helping every child achieve, ensuring equitable access and opportunity, building relationships to strengthen student and family engagement, and supporting mental health wellness to help students succeed in school and in life.

Citizens who want to address the Board at a Regular meeting, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. four (4) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. For more information, please visit the FCPS Website.

 

A total of 16 in-person, live virtual, and prerecorded video speaker slots are available per meeting. Thirteen slots will be reserved for in-person and live virtual speakers.  Three (3) slots will be reserved for students and three (3) slots will be reserved for those speakers who wish to submit a prerecorded video. The speakers list closes at 48 hours before the meeting. After the registration closes, speakers will be determined by a random lottery process.  After the random process, speakers will be notified if they have received a speaking or prerecorded video spot.   After the first 13 in-person or live virtual speakers are determined, the next ten (10) names as determined by the same random process will be chosen as the waitlist and notified that they are on the waitlist to speak.  Video submissions will not be permitted for the waitlist.  Waitlist speakers must be present at the meeting to speak in the event of a no show.  Speaker substitutions are not permitted.  A speaker may not yield their time to another individual before or during their remarks.

 

The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.
