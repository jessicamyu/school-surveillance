Subject: FY 2020 Midyear Budget Review

 

Staff Contact: Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category: December 9, 2019 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

In the School Operating Fund, funds available are increasing by $0.7 million. The FY 2020 beginning balance is increasing by $0.7 million due to final year-end adjustments made after the FY 2019 Final Budget Review.

 

The adjustments include an increase of $1.2 million in revenue primarily in sales tax based on actual receipts; City of Fairfax tuition based on actual expenditures for FY 2019; offset by an expenditure increase of $0.5 million primarily in utilities. In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, FY 2020 revenue adjustments include a slight decrease in federal grant revenue.

 

Recommended FY 2020 expenditure adjustments reflect a net increase of $0.7 million to provide security surveillance cameras at eight middle schools offset by a slight decrease in federal grant expenditures. Additionally, it is recommended that $0.6 million be allocated from the School Board flexibility reserve to provide School Board staffing support.

 

Expenditure adjustments offset revenue adjustments, resulting in no change to the set aside of the FY 2021 beginning balance of $24.4 million.

 

Changes to other School Board funds for FY 2020 are detailed in the attachment. Also attached is an update on the status of grants in FY 2019 and FY 2020.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2020 Midyear Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Governance Committee Proposed Forum Process

A. The School Board Forum process should be officially embedded into our Regular SB Meeting Agenda, and renamed “Board Matters”.

B. What now is called “Board Matters” will be referred to as “Final Remarks” or “Board Member Updates” (similar to the “Superintendent Updates”.

C. Board members should submit their “Board Matters “ topics (and brief, written summaries) to the Board Chair and the Board Clerk at least 48 hours in advance of the Regular Business Meeting, unless a topic is time-sensitive. This written proposal will be posted to Boarddocs, and at minimum emailed to the full Board, the Superintendent and Deputy Superintendent.

D. The proposed “Board Matters” will be placed on the Business Agenda, right after “Action Items”.

E. Approximately 1 hour will be allotted for “Board Matters” (with a maximum of 2 topics per meeting) unless the Board votes to extend this portion of the Business Agenda.

 

Rationale:

For more than 7 years, the School Board has experienced persistent challenges in consistently holding its Forum meetings due to the existing time-constraints of the Closed Session and the Regular Business Meeting. Having discussed and reviewed the success of the Board of Supervisors’ Board Matter process, it is recommended that the School Board adopt a similar approach.
