All-Night Graduation Celebrations

 

A final reminder to parents and students: All-Night Graduation Celebrations have been a great success in providing safe and exciting celebrations as a capstone to our graduates’ years of hard work and achievement. But it’s just as important to keep our students safe the next morning, when they have stayed up all night without sleep and shouldn’t be driving.  Parents, please pick up your students from these events or arrange for another well-rested person to do so. Drowsy driving, like driving under the influence of drugs or alcohol, is "impaired” driving.  We want our graduates to celebrate, enjoy the party, and arrive home safely in the morning.

Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

 

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

SCHOOL BOARD CHARACTER AWARD

Juan C. Castro

Westfield High School

 

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board Chris Giovarelli and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began funding a $500 award for the winner.  The award is given to a junior or senior, who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for other Fairfax County Public Schools students in day-to-day behavior. 

 

Juan C. Castro is the recipient of the 2012 School Board Character Award, chosen from a pool of outstanding nominees.

 

Humble.  Polite.  Hard Working. Selfless. Compassionate. These are just a few of the many words used to describe Juan.  Juan is a senior at Westfield High School and an outstanding student, maintaining good grades while working a job and actively volunteering in his school and community.  To quote from one of his recommendations: “He consistently demonstrates good character, high morals, and strong integrity by giving of himself and what he has as well as by rejoicing in the happiness of others.”

 

He is a former ESOL student, who befriends and mentors countless ESOL students new to the country and the school system, helping them with translations, motivating them to work hard and to excel in their studies.    He connects with all of the students and exhibits patience and humor immediately putting them at ease.  This role is one he voluntarily took on because he wanted to help other students achieve success. He is truly an inspiration to students who often feel discouraged by the challenges of learning English and adapting to life in the United States.

 

Two years ago, Juan founded the EDGE Club at Westfield High School, a bible study club that meets regularly, sometimes with a guest speaker, all organized under Juan’s leadership.  He is also a mentor with the Hispanic Student Success Council, where he meets with students struggling in their classes and helps them stay motivated. 

 

One of Juan’s teachers described him as having an infectious laugh and the ability to move across and within social and cultural groups, having friends from all walks of life. On his application, Juan mentioned the following quote: “We enter to learn and get out to serve.”  Juan exemplifies that in his personal and school life and is most deserving of this honor.

   Strikethrough version summary of the following recommendations:

 

Changes to SR&R:

 

A.  In section V., Summer School and Intersession Attendance–removed all references regarding “intersession(s).”

 

B.  In chapter I.A., Guidelines for Student Rights and Responsibilities, moved to parent companion guide.

 

C.  In chapter II., Alcohol and Drug Youth Services (ADYS) resource specialist was changed to read “Community Services Board (CSB) liaison.”

 

D.  In Glossary of SR&R Terms, definitions for bullying and weapon were rewritten on pages 4 and 6.

 

E.  In chapter II.A.2.d., bullying based protected status (e.g. race, color, disability, sexual orientation) was added to list of inappropriate behaviors on page 19.

 

F.  In chapter II.A.2.m., clarifying the use of personally owned electronic device on page 19.

 

G.  In chapter II.A.3.a.(1), changed sentence from “For a first violation related to tobacco products, a student shall participate in the FCPS Tobacco Intervention Seminar in lieu of suspension” to read “For a first violation related to tobacco products, a high school student shall participate in the FCPS Tobacco Intervention Seminar in lieu of suspension.  Elementary school and middle school students shall participate in an in-school intervention support program conducted by Student Safety and Wellness Office” on page 20.

 

H.  In chapter II.A.3.d.(1), ““synthetic marijuana” was added to list of prohibited substances on page 22.

 

I.  In chapter II.A.5.c., “spring loaded pellet guns” was added to the list of non-statutory weapons that fall under principal discretion when determining disciplinary response on page 26.

 

J.  In chapter II.A.5.d., the section “Law and Policy” was added to clarify Gun-Free Schools Act on page 26.

 

K.  In chapter II.B.1., clarifying what incidents must be reported to police on page 27.

 

L.  In chapter II.C.1., “With approval of the principal and concurrence of all involved students, students may be invited to participate in peer mediation or restorative justice practices to resolve disputes or address student behavior.  This does not preclude the possibility of disciplinary action for prohibited conduct” was added on page 28.

 

M.  In chapter II, Removed Virginia High School League Eligibility and relocated in the Parent Companion Guide.

 

N.  Removed chapter III, Student Activities and relocated in the Parent Companion Guide.

 

O.  The following updates were made to present a short and parent friendly version of the Parent Guide:

  • Required signature page moved to front
  • Moved definitions to front (page 7)
  • Removed sections "Participation in Student Activities" and "VHSL Participation Eligibility" from Chapter 3 of SRR. This information can now be found in the Parent Companion Guide to SRR.
  • Dress code put into bullets
  • Chart on possible disciplinary consequences reformatted to organize from least to most serious consequences
  • Student activities and VHSL eligibility rules added for easy parent reference
  • URL and QR codes added to allow reader to go to Parent video on SRR, Bullying Prevention video and parent information, and VHSL website for additional information about eligibility to play sports. (Note, these codes appear in the attached version, but not on the printed version sent to school board office today. Codes will not be “live” until booklet is published). 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.26P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.  

 

Proposed Board Member Amendments (language subject to change):

 

1.  Dan Storck (separate motion):

 

I move that the School Board establish a special committee of the Board to perform a comprehensive review of the Fairfax County Public Schools’ Student Rights and Responsibilities (SR&R), and those manuals of other school districts, to recommend changes to the School Board by March 2013 that would simplify the language and substantially reduce the size of SR&R and ensure consistency with our community’s values, School Board policies, and Virginia laws.  This committee shall be composed of representatives of parents, students, school administrators, teachers, student specialists (e.g., counselors, social workers, psychologists, hearings officers or SROs), juvenile services specialists and/or such other community members as the School Board shall determine appropriate.

 

2.  Ryan McElveen:

 

I move to postpone Board consideration on amendments to SR&R until the Board receives recommendations from the newly established special committee reviewing SR&R by March 2013.

 

3.  Ilryong Moon:

 

I move to amend the main motion by adding the following language to Chapter II, Section C., Disciplinary Procedures, page 27, at the end of the paragraph:  “Notwithstanding any other provision of SR&R, the principal shall have the right to permit a student who has appealed a short-term suspension or a recommendation for more serious disciplinary action, or both, to remain in school while such appeal is pending before school officials, provided that the principal determines in his sole judgment that the student would not pose a danger to persons or property or an ongoing threat of disruption of the school’s educational programs. The foregoing permission is subject to review and revocation at any time by the principal, Division Superintendent, or School Board.”

 

4.  Elizabeth Schultz (3):

 

I move to amend the main motion by revising Chapter II, Section C., Disciplinary Procedures, page 27, by striking the third sentence “The principal will make a reasonable effort to notify parents or guardians at the earliest opportunity regarding student disciplinary actions.” and inserting the following language: 

“Except under exigent circumstances, the principal or his administrative designee shall notify parents or guardians of any student suspected of committing a serious violation of School Board policy.

 

In cases where a serious violation, as set forth in this section, necessitates the questioning of any student, the principal or his administrative designee may only question a student, except under exigent circumstances, after the student’s parents have been notified.

 

For purposes of this section, a "serious violation" means a violation of a School Board policy or compulsory school attendance requirements when such violation could result in

 

     a.  the student's suspension or expulsion;

     b.  the notification of law enforcement; or

     c.  the filing of a court petition, whether or not the school administration has imposed such disciplinary action or filed a petition.”

 

I move the following amendment in the form of a substitute motion:  I move to amend the main motion by adding the following language to Chapter II, Section C., Disciplinary Procedures, page 27, before the sentence “Parents and guardians seeking information on the hearing process should contact the Hearings Office”:  “Under no circumstances shall the principal or his administrative designee ask or compel any written statement or signature on a document from any student without the consent of a parent or guardian.”

 

I move to amend the main motion by adding the following language to Chapter II, Section C., Disciplinary Procedures, page 27 before the sentence “Parents and guardians seeking information on the hearing process should contact the Hearings Office”:  “If a student elects to remain silent, the student shall not be deemed to be uncooperative.” 

 

5.  Sandy Evans (3):

Key Points:

 

During the 2012 session of the Virginia General Assembly, legislation was passed requiring all active participants of the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) to contribute toward the cost of retirement benefits.  The legislation provides that localities and school systems may phase in employee contributions at a rate of 1% or more per year, and that the full phase in of a 5% contribution rate must be completed by July 1, 2016.  Jurisdictions must provide corresponding increases to salaries to offset these employee contributions.
