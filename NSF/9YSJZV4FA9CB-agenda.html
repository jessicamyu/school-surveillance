National Volunteer Week - April 10 - 16, 2016

 

Established in 1974, National Volunteer Week has grown exponentially in scope each year since, drawing the support and endorsement of all subsequent U.S. presidents, governors, mayors and other respected elected officials.

 

National Volunteer Week is about inspiring, recognizing, and encouraging people to seek out imaginative ways to engage in their communities. It’s about demonstrating to the nation that by working together, in unison, we have the fortitude to meet our challenges and accomplish our goals. National Volunteer Week is about taking action, encouraging individuals and their respective communities to be at the center of social change—discovering and actively demonstrating their collective power to foster positive transformation.

 

During the 2014-2015 school year, 185 schools, centers, and alternative high schools reported that 60,139 volunteers donated 1,306,727 hours of volunteer service to FCPS schools.

National Library Week and School Library Month - April 2016

 

National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association and libraries across the country each April.  It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate.  This year, National Library Week will be observed April 10-16 with the theme, "Libraries Transform.”

 

School Library Month is the American Association of School Librarians' celebration of school librarians and their programs. Every April school librarians are encouraged to create activities to help their school and local community celebrate the essential role that strong school library programs play in a student's educational career.

 

The School Board welcomes this opportunity to commend Fairfax County Public Schools’ librarians and to express its gratitude for the energy, knowledge, and vision that they tirelessly and unselfishly provide to the school system.

Month of the Military Child

 

April is the Month of the Military Child.  It is a time to recognize our military and veteran-connected students for their service and sacrifice. Military kids sacrifice and serve our country in their own way.  Here in Fairfax County, we are extremely fortunate to have 8,066 students who are part of a military family as well as part of our Fairfax County Public Schools family.

 

Military-connected children and youth live with numerous challenges presented by frequent moves, parental deployments, and a host of life transitions that include reintegration and dealing with profoundly changed parents. The following are just a few of the challenges military children face:  moving and starting over on an average of every 1-3 years, attending 6-9 different schools from kindergarten through 12th grade, and multiple deployments of a parent.  Throughout all of this, military children are resilient and patriotic.  They know first-hand what it feels like to be the new kid, so they are usually the first one to welcome a new student to school.

 

Celebrating the Month of the Military Child provides a powerful opportunity to raise awareness and build support to help these children meet the unique challenges they face! It is an opportunity to recognize and thank military children and youth for their heroism, character, courage, sacrifices and continued resilience.

RESOLUTION

HONORING

CHARLES W. DANE

 

 

WHEREAS, Dr. Charles W. Dane was born in Washington, D.C., attended Woodrow Wilson High School, achieved the rank of Eagle Scout, received Bachelor and Master of Science degrees from Cornell University, served in the United States Air Force Strategic Air Command, and received a PhD from Purdue University; and

 

 

WHEREAS, Dr. Dane had a long career with the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service as a research biologist and supervisor of migratory bird research, retiring as Chief of Scientific Authority, a position which enabled him to travel the world to negotiate and implement treaties with the Convention on International Trade of Endangered Species; and

 

 

WHEREAS, he was elected to the Jamestown, North Dakota School Board, served as Biology Department Chair at Jamestown College, held many positions with the Annandale, Virginia Chapter of the National Active and Retired Federal Employees, and was appointed by two Braddock District Supervisors to the Fairfax County Commission on Organ and Tissue Donation; and

 

 

WHEREAS, Dr. Dane was active in his church and the Boy Scouts, annually reviewed scholarship applications submitted to College Access Fairfax and was a full-time Fairfax County volunteer extraordinaire, representing his civic association on the Fairfax County Federation of Citizens Associations for almost 20 years, often chairing its Education Committee and briefly serving as Chair of the Land Use Committee; and

 

 

WHEREAS, Charlie Dane served on the Fairfax County School Board Human Relations Advisory Committee and the Superintendent’s Business and Community Relations Advisory Council as well as a variety of task forces, and attended many School Board meetings and work sessions, often following through with feedback to the School Board members on practices to improve public education; and

 

 

WHEREAS, Dr. Dane was passionate about education and generated conversations with School Board members regarding his particular interests including but not exclusive to literacy, strengthening the role of School Board Advisory Committees, the budget process, and the 2013 journey to hire an excellent superintendent for Fairfax County Public Schools;

 

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Dr. Charles W. Dane for his outstanding service and dedication to Fairfax County Public Schools and to the children and citizens of this community.

Meeting Category:  April 14, 2016 – Regular Meeting No. 17

 

Subject:  Strategic Plan Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan

 

Key Points:

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 2, Caring Culture.

 

The following are the three overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 2, Caring Culture:

 

Overarching Strategy 5

Establish a school division and culture that is highly responsive to stakeholders and creates a model environment in which to learn and work

Desired Outcomes

  • FCPS will utilize a consistent approach to customer service
  • FCPS will demonstrate cultural competency and sensitivity when supporting families, students and employees

Overarching Strategy 6

Foster active and positive communication and engagement among students, staff, families, and the community in order to ensure that stakeholders are engaged and well-informed partners

 

Desired Outcomes

  • Communication tools, processes, and content will enable access to key information anytime, anywhere, on any device
  • Under-engaged communities will be provided additional communication and community support
  • Proactive and dynamic communications will better engage the community in critical issues
  • School-based staff will be better engaged and supported in communications and information sharing with their communities
  • All students will demonstrate digital citizenship skills

 

Overarching Strategy 7

Promote overall health and well-being of students and staff in order to encourage healthy life choices and an increased quality of life

Desired Outcomes

  • FCPS students will demonstrate improved life skills and self-awareness of behaviors and actions
  • FCPS students and staff will feel welcome and supported at school
  • FCPS students and staff will be encouraged and supported in making healthy life choices

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 2 Caring Culture report as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:  April 14, 2016 - Regular Meeting No. 17

 

Subject:  Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2016-2017

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision 

 

Related to:  Legal Requirement        

 

Key Points:

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report for the 2016-2017 school year, as required by VDOE Superintendent’s memo #290-15, dated December 4, 2015.  This report will be used by the Virginia Department of Education in preparing the state plan for special education.  As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 13, 2016.

 

The 2016-2017 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $33,628,048.  This amount is equal to the final 2015–2016 award as stated in Superintendent’s memo #037-16, dated February 26, 2016.  Estimated expenditures include cost associated with the voluntary 15% set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS), and a mandatory private school set-aside of 0.4%.  Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 417.1 FTE positions.

 

The 2016-2017 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $730,514.  This amount is equal to the final 2015-2016 award as stated in Superintendent’s memo #185-15, dated July 31, 2015.  These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students.  Estimated expenditures include costs associated with a mandatory private school set-aside of 1.7%.  Section 619 funds six FTE positions.

 

The revenue amounts above reflect no change from the 2015-2016 awards.  The 2016-2017 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2015-2016 application.  Small adjustments in both sections of 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, materials/supplies and the state restricted indirect cost rate.

 

The Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) reviewed the plan and funding application at the March 9, 2016 meeting.

 

 

Options:

  1. Approve the plan and application as detailed in the agenda item.
  2. Amend or reject the plan and application and provide direction to staff for further action.

 

Recommendation:

 

It is recommended that the School Board approve the 2016-2017 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool grant funding application as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: School Board Policies for Fraud, Waste and Abuse and, Protection Against Retaliation

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

 

Key Points:  The School Board does not tolerate unethical behavior and protects those who report allegations of suspected activities of wrong doing.  FCPS has existing regulations that direct the Superintendent’s office as well as the Office of Auditor General to execute and enforce actions that protect the Division against unethical behavior.  These two proposed school board policies memorialize the School Board’s current positions on fraud and anti-retaliation.  Each attached, proposed school board policy references the corresponding regulations.

The policy on Fraud, Waste and Abuse defines waste and abuse, which correspond to the fraud, waste and abuse hotline. These definitions will be reflected in the revisions to the corresponding regulations for reporting and investigating fraud, waste and abuse by the offices of Auditor General, Division Counsel, Human Resources, Risk Management and other  FCPS offices.  The policy on Protection against Retaliation akin to a “whistleblower” policy, and coordinates the purpose and responsibility of the offices of Division Counsel and Human Resources.

 

 

Recommendation: 

The audit committee and the auditor general recommend adoption of  two new school board policies which reflect and reinforce the current practices and corresponding FCPS regulations.

Meeting Category:  April 14, 2016 – Regular Meeting No. 17

 

Subject:  Strategic Plan Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan

 

Key Points:

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 2, Caring Culture.

 

The following are the three overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 2, Caring Culture:

 

Overarching Strategy 5

Establish a school division and culture that is highly responsive to stakeholders and creates a model environment in which to learn and work

Desired Outcomes

  • FCPS will utilize a consistent approach to customer service
  • FCPS will demonstrate cultural competency and sensitivity when supporting families, students and employees

Overarching Strategy 6

Foster active and positive communication and engagement among students, staff, families, and the community in order to ensure that stakeholders are engaged and well-informed partners

 

Desired Outcomes

  • Communication tools, processes, and content will enable access to key information anytime, anywhere, on any device
  • Under-engaged communities will be provided additional communication and community support
  • Proactive and dynamic communications will better engage the community in critical issues
  • School-based staff will be better engaged and supported in communications and information sharing with their communities
  • All students will demonstrate digital citizenship skills

 

Overarching Strategy 7

Promote overall health and well-being of students and staff in order to encourage healthy life choices and an increased quality of life

Desired Outcomes

  • FCPS students will demonstrate improved life skills and self-awareness of behaviors and actions
  • FCPS students and staff will feel welcome and supported at school
  • FCPS students and staff will be encouraged and supported in making healthy life choices

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 2 Caring Culture report as detailed in the agenda item.
