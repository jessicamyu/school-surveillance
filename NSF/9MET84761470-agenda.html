Veterans Day - November 11, 2014

 

Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.  Many schools have special events, to which they invite veterans and members of the school community on active duty military.

 

 

American Education Week - November 16-22, 2014

 

American Education Week was established in 1921 by the National Education Association (NEA) to raise public awareness of the importance of public education in America.  This year’s theme, Raise Your Hand for Student Success, presents all Americans with the opportunity to celebrate public education and honor those who ensure that every child receives a quality education. As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week features a special observance on each day of the week, including parents, education support professionals, and substitute educators. 

 

 

Geography Awareness Week - November 16-22, 2014

 

Geography Awareness Week is an opportunity to focus on geography as a critical component of a 21st century education.  The 2014 theme, The Future of Food, focuses on the environmental and societal problems that involve food, staple food crops of the world, and food education.

 

Geography Awareness Week was established by presidential proclamation in 1987 to underscore the importance of geographic understanding to ensure our nation’s economic competitiveness, national security, environmental sustainability, and livability of our communities in the 21st century.

School Psychology Awareness Week - November 10-14, 2014

 

The theme for 2014 School Psychology Awareness Week is Strive. Grow. THRIVE!, with a focus on engaging students at all levels of learning and development to promote personal achievement, growth, and resilience, as well as a sense of connectedness and wellbeing. 

 

School psychologists work in all educational settings to positively impact the academic and social/emotional development of students through the provision of mental health services that build resilience, promote developmental assets and foster good citizenship.  Their most important responsibility is to ensure the welfare and safety of students.  To fulfill that responsibility, school psychologists strive to attain and maintain high standards of empirically-based practice and to promote professional ethics in their daily work.

 

School psychologists collaborate with teachers, parents, and school personnel to create safe, healthy, and supportive learning environments for all students.  School psychologists collaborate with school teams to address students' learning and behavioral problems through implementation of multi-tiered prevention and intervention strategies.  They suggest improvements to classroom management strategies or parenting techniques, bring knowledge of assessment and data collection to measure progress and outcomes of interventions, and encourage practices that effectively promote student learning and mental wellness.

Subject:                  2015 Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:            Legislation

 

Key Points:

 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.  The attached proposed 2015 FCSB Legislative Program includes additions, subtractions and formatting changes. These changes are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate, to incorporate staff recommendations, and to update and streamline existing position language. 

 

Major Changes to the proposed 2015 Legislative Program include:

  • Rephrased most paragraph-based position language to fit a bullet-point format
  • Added language supporting the use of high growth as a factor in state funding formulas (Education Funding – State)
  • Added support for expanding the ESL student subgroup to account for students who exit due to achieving language proficiency (English Language Learners)
  • Added language referencing “Portrait of a Graduate;” supporting multiple pathways to graduation, including changes to personal finance graduation requirement, identification of additional courses as fulfilling graduation requirements, and revisiting student verified credits; and supporting simplified mechanisms for submitting substitute assessment scores to VDOE (Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning)

NOTE that all changes to the Proposed 2015 Legislative Program are documented in the Change Summary document, as attached to the agenda item.

 

In addition, the following editing changes have been made to the proposed Program document since the Board’s October 6th work session:

  • Board Priority Position Language - based on existing Board Legislative Positions.
  • Clarified language in Joint FCBS/FCSB Priority Position related to impact of special education services
  • Rephrased School Calendar position to better align new bulleted language format with previously adopted Board position

Modify as desired.

That the School Board adopt the 2015 Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                    Policy 1450, Nondiscrimination

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Key Points:

 

School Board Policy 1450, Nondiscrimination, was adopted July 1, 1986, to ensure conditions of nondiscrimination and equal opportunity in the school system.

Due to recent decisions by the US Supreme Court and the Fourth Circuit Court of Appeals, effective October 6, 2014, the Commonwealth of Virginia recognizes same-sex marriage. As a result, Virginia employers must provide benefits to same-sex couples. 

 

In keeping with the express intent of the School Board that every policy, practice, and procedure shall conform to all applicable requirements of federal and state law, it is imperative that Policy 1450 be revised to include protection for students, employees, and applicants against discrimination based on sexual orientation.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 1450, Nondiscrimination, as detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
