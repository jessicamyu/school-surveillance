                                                                                        Updated 9/13/18

 

Subject: Local Graduation Requirements for Science and Social Studies for the Standard Diploma and Updates on Options for Health and Physical Education and World Languages (High School Pathways Updates)

 

Staff Contact: Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present: Jennifer Glaser, director, Counseling and College and Career Readiness, ISD

          Noel Klimenko, director, K-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category: September 17, 2018, Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 1 Student Success

 

Problem Statement:

The local graduation requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma for FCPS students are more rigorous than required for the state.

 

Outcome:

School Board vote to determine if FCPS will maintain local graduation requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma or change to match the VDOE requirements.

 

Summary/Background:

Local graduation requirements for science and social studies for the Standard Diploma for FCPS students are more rigorous than required by VDOE. For science, FCPS requires that students complete three credits in three different disciplines, while VDOE requires that students complete three credits in two different disciplines. For social studies, FCPS requires that students complete two credits in world history and geography, while VDOE requires that students complete one credit in world history and geography. Matching the VDOE requirements for the Standard Diploma would offer students pursuing a Standard Diploma additional flexibility in course taking for science and would allow students pursing a Standard Diploma an additional elective choice by removing one of the currently required social studies credits.

 

Additionally, this is a follow up to previous School Board inquiries about additional flexibility for Health and Physical Education and World Languages.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve changing the local FCPS graduation requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma to match the VDOE requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma and as recommended by the FCPS Leadership Team and a steering committee with representation from school and central office leadership.

Staff Contact: Jeffrey Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services, F&TS

 

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                  Kevin Sneed, special projects administrator, Design and Construction, F&TS

                                  Jessica Gillis, director, Facilities Planning Services, F&TS

 

 

Meeting Category: September 17, 2018 – Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Office of Facilities Planning Services, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services reviewed Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings, and compared it to other school districts’ boundary adjustment policies and practices.

 

The purpose is to review how other school districts implement policy regarding school boundaries. Information provided will inform discussion related to school boundaries in order for Fairfax County Public Schools to ensure excellence by creating an exceptional and comprehensive foundation for the boundary adjustment process, equity by allowing boundary adjustments to be implemented impartially and consistently, and effectiveness by promoting boundary adjustments that will increase the efficiency of FCPS.

 

The attached matrix includes FCPS School Board Policy 8130 and practices related to boundary adjustments to a sample of school districts in Virginia, the Washington metropolitan area, and a sample of the top fifty school districts by enrollment size in the United States.
