National Mentoring Month - January 2022

 

National Mentoring Month highlights mentoring and the positive impact it can have on young lives.  This month-long outreach campaign focuses national attention on the need for mentors, as well as how each of us—individuals, businesses, government agencies, schools, faith communities and nonprofits—can work together to increase the number of mentors and assure brighter futures for our young people.  Positive relationships between mentors and their mentees have been shown to encourage young people to stay in school, achieve personal growth, believe in themselves, and live up to their potential.  We have a tremendous need for mentors at all levels in FCPS.  If you would like to learn more about how you can mentor a student, please visit FCPS.edu and search “Become a Mentor” and make a difference in a child’s life TODAY!

Citizens who want to address the Board at a Regular meeting, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. four (4) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. For more information, please visit the FCPS Website.

 

A total of 16 in-person, live virtual, and prerecorded video speaker slots are available per meeting. Thirteen slots will be reserved for in-person and live virtual speakers.  Three (3) slots will be reserved for students and three (3) slots will be reserved for those speakers who wish to submit a prerecorded video. The speakers list closes at 48 hours before the meeting. After the registration closes, speakers will be determined by a random lottery process.  After the random process, speakers will be notified if they have received a speaking or prerecorded video spot.   After the first 13 in-person or live virtual speakers are determined, the next ten (10) names as determined by the same random process will be chosen as the waitlist and notified that they are on the waitlist to speak.  Video submissions will not be permitted for the waitlist.  Waitlist speakers must be present at the meeting to speak in the event of a no show.  Speaker substitutions are not permitted.  A speaker may not yield their time to another individual before or during their remarks.

 

The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

Summary Background:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools’ (FCPS) Office of Design and Construction Services issued a Request for Proposals (RFP) for Architectural and Engineering Services (A&E) on October 5, 2021, for design and design-related services for the anticipated Capital Improvement Projects identified below.

 

Anticipated Capital Improvement Projects:   

 

Centreville High School

Armstrong Elementary School

Bren Mar Park Elementary School

Brookfield Elementary School

Dranesville Elementary School

Herndon Elementary School

Lees Corner Elementary School

Willow Springs Elementary School

 

In response to the RFP, FCPS received twenty-eight (28) technical proposals from A&E firms on October 27, 2021. The selection process for A&E firms for these services was conducted in accordance with the process and criteria set forth in School Board Policy 8220.4 and the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA). The Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) evaluated the proposals received in response to this RFP based on the following criteria:

 

  • Project Team Credentials
  • Management Skills and Technical Expertise
  • Project Methodology and Quality Control, Capability for Timely Response
  • References
  • Overall Quality and Completeness of Proposal
  • Oral Presentations/Interviews (Short-List Firms only)
  • Other factors consistent with the RFP, Policy 8220.4, and the VPPA

 

Upon completion of its review and evaluation, the SAC recommends a list of top-ranked firms for contract negotiation and consideration from those A&E firms that determined to be most advantageous to FCPS for performance of design and design-related services for the anticipated Capital Improvement Projects. Alternate firms have been identified in the event that successful negotiations cannot be completed with primary nominees and/or design services are required for projects other than those listed below. The recommended top-ranked firms are listed below:

 

Recommended Firms:

Grimm and Parker Architects

Quinn Evans Architects

Architecture, Inc.

Staff Contact: 

Noel Klimenko, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

Gautam Sethi, assistant superintendent & chief information officer, Information Technology (IT)

 

Other Staff Present

Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

Maura Burke, director, Early Childhood Curriculum, ISD

Tracey Jewell, director, Support Services, IT

 

Meeting Category:  January 13, 2022 Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

FCPS received approval for a reimbursement grant from the Federal Communications Commission under the Emergency Connectivity Fund (ECF) to purchase sufficient and appropriate devices to support learning at school or at home for PreK - 2 students. FCPS would use the grant funds to refresh aging classroom student devices for our youngest learners.

 

The proposal includes purchasing more age-appropriate iPad devices with keyboard case for students in grades PreK and K, and standard Windows laptop devices for students in grades 1 and 2.

 

FCPS would invest one-time funds of $2.1M from the FY22 School Board Flexibility Reserve to support the basic device purchase and request another $1.8M in FY22 year-end, one-time funds to support the long-term warranty/repair and supplemental hardware purchase.

 

Recommendation:

 

School Board approve the purchase of up to 43,000 devices at the total cost of $18.4M with the local share of $2.1 million to be funded from School Board Flexibility Reserve and upon delivery file for reimbursement with the ECF grant fund for $16.3M.

Other Staff Present:     Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                      Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

                                      Nardos King, chief equity officer

                                      Michael Draeger, director, Office of Business Services

 

Meeting Category:        January 13, 2022 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject:     School Year 2022/23 Calendar

 

School Board Action Required:       Information

 

Related To:           Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

In March 2021 the School Board directed the superintendent to provide clarity on the criteria and priorities used to establish annual calendars in the future. As a result, the calendar development process for 2022-23 was enhanced to include a revised staff and parent survey, the creation of a survey for high and middle school students, and an expanded Calendar Feedback Committee.

 

A draft calendar for School Year 2022-23 will be presented. This calendar was developed using the following criteria:

  • Federal, state and Virginia Department of Education guidance
  • Superintendent’s priorities
  • School Board input
  • Student absentee data
  • Staff absentee data
  • Surrounding jurisdictions’ calendars
  • Survey Results
  • Calendar Feedback Committee input

 

 

Recommendation:

School Board approve the draft 2022-23 calendar
