Item #2: 

 

Proposed Forum Process

A. The School Board Forum process should be officially embedded into our Regular SB Meeting Agenda, and renamed “Board Matters”.

B. What now is called “Board Matters” will be referred to as “Final Remarks” or “Board Member Updates” (similar to the “Superintendent Updates”.

C. Board members should submit their “Board Matters “ topics (and brief, written summaries) to the Board Chair and the Board Clerk at least 48 hours in advance of the Regular Business Meeting, unless a topic is time-sensitive. This written proposal will be posted to Boarddocs, and at minimum emailed to the full Board, the Superintendent and Deputy Superintendent.

D. The proposed “Board Matters” will be placed on the Business Agenda, right after “Action Items”.

E. Approximately 1 hour will be allotted for “Board Matters” (with a maximum of 2 topics per meeting) unless the Board votes to extend this portion of the Business Agenda.

 

Rationale:

For more than 7 years, the School Board has experienced persistent challenges in consistently holding its Forum meetings due to the existing time-constraints of the Closed Session and the Regular Business Meeting. Having discussed and reviewed the success of the Board of Supervisors’ Board Matter process, it is recommended that the School Board adopt a similar approach. Listed below is an example of individual BOS Board Matter, as well as links to many more Board Matters:

 

Supervisor Cook Board Matter

December 4, 2018

 

Madam Chairman:

 

Recently you mentioned the shortage of school nurses. This has been a longstanding issue in Fairfax County. While school nurses operate in our schools, they are employees of our Health Department and therefore part of the county budget.

 

The school nurse program is severely under staffed and underfunded. One nurse may be responsible for up to 4,000 students spread over four schools. When the nurse is ill or on extended leave, absent replacement coverage the duties can fall to the front office staff or parents, which causes a potential liability issue. At one school in my district, Bonnie Brae Elementary, the nurse has not been in school one day this year and is out until mid-March on extended medical leave. Apparently there is no back up coverage. This leaves the 800 students without a clinic nurse.

 

This is not a simple issue. Nurses are in short supply and the program is expensive. But the health of our children cannot be compromised. Therefore, Madam Chairman, I move without objection that the issue of school nurses and funding be brought before an upcoming meeting of the Budget Committee for further discussion. It would be appropriate for our colleagues on the School Board to be part of this discussion.” (Here’s the actual link to his Board Matter)
