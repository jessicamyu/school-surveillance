National Red Ribbon Week - October 23-31, 2021

 

National Red Ribbon Week is celebrated each year from October 23rd-October 31st.  It was established in 1985 in memory of Enrique Camarena, a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration agent who was killed in the line of duty while investigating a major drug cartel in Mexico. To honor his battle against illegal drugs, friends and neighbors began to wear red badges of satin and some parents began to form coalitions. National Red Ribbon Week grew from these grassroots efforts to form a national movement against the use of illegal drugs. Its annual campaign encourages students to take a visible stand against substance abuse and make a personal commitment to follow a drug-free lifestyle. The 2021 Red Ribbon Theme is "Drug Free Looks Like Me” is a reminder that everyday Americans across the county make significant daily contributions to their communities by being the best they can be because they live Drug-Free!

RESOLUTION

HONORING

LGBTQIA+ HISTORY MONTH

 

WHEREAS, LGBTQIA+ History Month is recognized in October across the United States and in Fairfax County Public Schools to acknowledge and celebrate the contributions and experiences of well-known and too often overlooked or silenced LGBTQIA+ people, communities, and movements throughout our history; and,

 

WHEREAS, this special month was founded in 1994 by Missouri high school history teacher Rodney Wilson who chose October for the inaugural celebration to coincide with National Coming Out Day on October 14 and to commemorate the first march on Washington for LGBTQIA+ rights in October 1979; and,

 

WHEREAS, the National PTA states "negative attitudes toward lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender students puts them at an increased risk for violence and bullying," and that these "negative attitudes also impact a student's ability to learn at school;" and,

 

WHEREAS, students are involved in Gender Sexuality Alliance (GSA) clubs at many middle and high schools throughout Fairfax County Public Schools, where they can network, socialize, and educate their communities through forums, announcements, and other activities; and these clubs are an asset to our schools, with research indicating that schools with GSAs have better mental health outcomes for all students; and,

 

WHEREAS, the Fairfax County School Board celebrates its richly diverse community of LGBTQIA+ students, families, and staff, recognizes the urgency of its charge to foster a school community where all feel valued, supported, and hopeful, and advances policies that protect LGBTQIA+ students and staff so they can live without fear of prejudice, discrimination, or violence;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board, on behalf of the students, educators, and families of Fairfax County Public Schools, does hereby proclaim October 2021 as LGBTQIA+ History Month in Fairfax County Public Schools; and in the spirit of One Fairfax, that the Fairfax County School Board urges all to learn more about the many historical and contemporary contributions and experiences of this diverse community, and to build a culture of inclusivity and equity, not only during LGBTQIA+ History Month but throughout the entire year.

RESOLUTION

HONORING

NATIONAL DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH

 

WHEREAS, the Virginia Department of Education defines dyslexia, in part, as a specific learning disability that is neurobiological in origin that is characterized by difficulties with accurate and/or fluent word recognition and by poor spelling and decoding abilities; and

 

WHEREAS, the onset and severity of dyslexia varies for each individual and impacts up to 15-20% of the population according to the International Dyslexia Association; and

 

WHEREAS, dyslexia can not only present challenges with reading, writing, and spelling but also affect speaking, remembering, understanding, speed, movement, and basic maths; and

 

WHEREAS, dyslexia is a different way of learning and thinking, though with the right teaching most dyslexics learn to read and write; and

 

WHEREAS, Governor Ralph Northam has recognized October 2021 as DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH in Virginia to acknowledge educators specializing in effective teaching strategies for individuals with dyslexia; and to celebrate the many achievements of children and adults - students and working professionals - who have dyslexia; and

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools is developing a division-wide literacy plan that ensures comprehensive, PreK-12 research-based reading instruction that is aligned with the Science of Reading so that all students may achieve their full potential; and

WHEREAS, students, educators, and families will benefit from increased awareness about the nature of dyslexia and the combination of strengths and difficulties that individuals with dyslexia can have:

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Fairfax County School Board recognizes October 2021 as DYSLEXIA AWARENESS MONTH and encourages all residents and FCPS staff to learn more about dyslexia, including identifying dyslexia; knowing supports available for use by educators and families; and highlighting the success that individuals with dyslexia can have in their careers and life.

Recommended Action
The School Board adopt the resolution requesting that the Board of Supervisors issue and sell school refunding bonds on behalf of the School Board and for the benefit of the Fairfax County Public Schools.

Subject: School Board Authorization to Refinance Fairfax County Economic Development Authority Revenue Bonds originally issued for School Facilities

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: October 7, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Fairfax County's Department of Management and Budget has advised school system staff that the Board of Supervisors plans to sell Economic Development Authority (EDA) Revenue Refunding Bonds in late October 2021 in order to refund a prior bond issue to achieve interest rate savings.  The Board of Supervisors was scheduled to authorize the issuance and sale at its meeting on October 5, 2021.

 

The planned October 2021 bond sale is a result of a monthly review of outstanding debt performed by Fairfax County's Financial Advisor to determine which prior bond issues are candidates for refunding.  Given the low interest rate environment, this is an opportunity to provide debt service savings related to two issuances of bonds for Fairfax County Public Schools.   In 2003, the County sold bonds for the Laurel Hill Facilities Project, which financed the South County High School (then South County Secondary School) and were last refinanced in 2012.  In 2005, the County sold bonds for the School Administration Building and were last refinanced in 2014. 

 

Further, it was determined that County General Fund debt service savings can be achieved by again refinancing the remaining outstanding debt on these bonds. 

 

The attached document has been provided by bond counsel.

 

 

Recommendation

 

The School Board adopt the resolution requesting that the Board of Supervisors issue and sell school refunding bonds on behalf of the School Board and for the benefit of the Fairfax County Public Schools.

Type
Information

Staff Contact:  Eliazer Martinez, executive director/CIO, ERFC

 

Meeting Category:  October 7, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Amendment 3 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Plan Document and Regulations effective July 1, 2017 (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending proposed amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) to release members and employer contributions made in error as permitted under §401(a) of the Internal Revenue Code. The effective date for the proposed amendment for both Plans would be July 1, 2021. 

 

Proposed Amendments to the Plan Documents

 

ERFC Plan Document

 

  • Section 3.02 Member Contributions. – A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that accumulated contributions may be returned to an employee who has erroneously been included in ERFC membership.

 

  • Section 3.05 Employer Contributions. –  A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that ERFC may return Employer contributions that are made in excess of the correct amount due.

 

  • Section 14.03 No Diversion of Assets. – A proposed amendment to this section adds new language that permits ERFC to return up to one year of Employer contributions, without interest, that occur because of mistake of fact to the extent permitted under §401(a) of the Internal Revenue Code. 

 

ERFC 2001 Plan Document

 

  • Section 3.03 Member Contributions. – A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that accumulated contributions may be returned to an employee who has erroneously been included in ERFC membership.

 

  • Section 3.05 Employer Contributions. –  A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that ERFC may return Employer contributions that are made in excess of the correct amount due.

 

  • Section 9.03 No Diversion of Assets. – A proposed amendment to this section adds new language that permits ERFC to return up to one year of Employer contributions, without interest, that occur because of mistake of fact to the extent permitted under §401(a) of the Internal Revenue Code. 

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment No. 3 to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) with an effective date for the Amendment of July 1, 2021.
