Agenda Item: Transportation Re-Engineering

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer,
Department of Facilities and Transportation Services

Background:

The MPS report to the School Board addressed the potential for changing bell schedules to allow high schools to start later in the morning. MPS reported, however, that the costs for this change as included in their report were based upon an optimized routing system that takes a period of time to achieve. The real cost of changing the bell schedules would be much greater initially as it does take a period of time to make changes and adjustments as we have done over many years with the current routing structure. MPS also made note of the current stress under which transportation operates and indicated that, in their opinion, this stress means that the current system has little to no capacity to assimilate even modest increases in or changes to service demands.

Based on the MPS findings, staff began exploration of two issues. One, what would be the actual cost to implement changes to bell schedules including the fact that we would lose many current routing and timing efficiencies. Current “efficiencies” include but are not limited to:
    bus runs that are too long,
    bus runs that are too crowded,
    bus runs that arrive too early to high schools,
    bus runs that arrive consistently late to schools.

We believe that once we have calculated the cost of these efficiencies, this cost can be added to the costs in the MPS report to more accurately define the cost of changing the current bell schedules. As this is a complex undertaking, the results will not be known for several more weeks.

Second, we prepared the attached document to address factors that contribute to the current stress levels in transportation. Some of these factors are operational in nature such as how and to whom we provide bus transportation. Other factors are organizational in nature such as the current structure of three area offices for general education and one very large office managing all of special education transportation. The final factor addresses the challenges that transportation currently faces with inadequate office spaces and insufficient secured bus parking.

Key Issues

Accomplishment of information gathering about and decisions on the factors discussed above is the critical next step in consideration of potential changes to the current bell schedule. It is impossible to make changes to the current bell schedule without providing some relief to transportation. The School Board also must have a clear understanding of the real cost of such changes that were only partially contained in the MPS report (and acknowledged by the consultant as such in the report).

School Board Discussion/Decision

The School Board is not being asked to make any decisions as a result of this work session and consideration of the re-engineering document. Specifically, staff is seeking guidance on potential changes that the School Board might be willing to consider that might be brought back to the Board with additional supporting information.

Staff Recommendation

Staff recommends that the School Board give serious consideration to all potential actions in the attached document.

Options None recommended.

Agenda Item: TJHSST Task Force Report

Staff Contact: Dr. Rich Moniuszko, Deputy Superintendent

Background: In response to concerns from the Thomas Jefferson HS community about the aging facility, the school board appointed a Task Force in July 2006 to investigate both program and facilities issues, and make recommendations to the board.

Key Issues:
    Some program recommendations, such as increased outreach and professional development activities for all FCPS teachers, impact future facility needs.
    Specialized laboratory facilities require additional expense to establish and maintain
    Partnerships with the technology community and higher education need to be strengthened
    Some program recommendations will require additional operational expenses
    George Mason University is not interested in Thomas Jefferson having a significant presence on their Fairfax campus.
    Due to the lack of an appropriate site for a new campus, substantial renovations or a new facility should be constructed on the present site.

School Board Discussion/Decision:
No immediate decision is needed, but the issues for discussion include:
Is the school board in agreement with the future direction of the school as outlined in the Task Force report?
Given the specialized nature of TJHSST, does the school board wish to provide more than the $87 million currently in the CIP for construction/renovation?
Is the school board amenable to the TJ community establishing a building fund and soliciting outside resources (foundations, state government, business partners, grants) to enhance the facility beyond what the school board allocates in the CIP in order to meet its program needs?
Does the school board want staff to develop additional alternatives for TJHSST to be considered in next years’ CIP?

Staff Recommendation:
Endorsement of the program recommendations and establish a ceiling for the amount of funding for TJHSST in the Capital Improvement Program.
