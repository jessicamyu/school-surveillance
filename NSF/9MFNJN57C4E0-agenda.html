Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  January 12, 2015 - Work Session No. 65

 

Subject:                 FY 2016-2020 Capital Improvement Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2016-2020 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five-year enrollment projections and capacity calculations, as well as identification of new construction, capacity enhancement, renovation, special program facilities, and site acquisition.

 

The Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2016-2020 Capital Improvement Program Summary can be found in the Capital Program Recommendations chapter of the Proposed Capital Improvement Program booklet.

 

Discussion:

 

The school system continues to face a challenging situation in which student enrollment is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities, provide capacity enhancement projects, and it will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions, and boundary adjustments to accommodate growing enrollment needs.

 

The CIP proposes four new elementary schools. The new elementary school needs are identified as follows: one in the Richmond Highway Corridor (Route 1 area) to relieve overcrowding in the Groveton/Hybla Valley area; one in the Northwest County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and Dulles Airport Access Road; one to relieve overcrowding in the Fairfax/Oakton area; and one on the army base to relieve overcrowding at Fort Belvoir Elementary School which is jointly funded with the Department of Defense. 

 

Funding for additions at South Lakes High School and Westbriar are also identified. Capacity enhancements are proposed at Langley, West Springfield, Herndon and Oakton High Schools as part of their renovations. Furthermore, the CIP, from 2013 bond referendum, includes construction funds for renovation at nine elementary and one middle school, plus a renovation at one high school, as well as planning funds for renovations at eight elementary, one middle, and two high schools. Many of the renovation projects will also include capacity enhancements.

 

Project costs have also been updated in this document to reflect those currently being experienced. The current and anticipated enrollment increases continue to present a major challenge as the school system struggles to provide sufficient capacity in our schools. Providing the additional new schools and capacity enhancements required to accommodate enrollment growth will cause delays in the schedule of many future renovation projects. We anticipate the next bond referendum will be in the fall of 2015. In addition, beginning in FY 2016, the Board of Supervisors approved an additional $13.1 million to offset future infrastructure replacement requirements. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2016-2020 Capital Improvement Program as described in the narrative and in the Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2016-2020 Capital Improvement Program Summary.

Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:   January 12, 2015 – Work Session No. 65

 

Subject:                   Development Review and Proffer Process

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The Development Review and Proffer processes are a method the school system uses to evaluate, plan for, and to an extent, mitigate, the impacts of additional students generated from proposed residential developments.  These processes include: the use of Development Student Yield Ratios; the evaluation of the impact of proposed residential development on schools; the use of a per student monetary proffer contribution; as well as the distribution of proffer funds.

 

Discussion:

 

The Development Review and Proffer process includes the review of primarily rezoning applications and Comprehensive Plan amendments.  In 2002, the Board of Supervisors adopted the Residential Development Criteria.  As part of its implementation motion, attachments were provided to the motion, which included a methodology and formula to calculate a per student proffer contributions and Development Student Yield Ratios. 

 

Development Student Yield Ratios are used for the Development Review and Proffer processes.  These ratios represent a county wide average of students generated by housing unit.  An estimate of the number of students generated from proposed housing can be created using these ratios.  This estimate is used to evaluate the impact of proposed residential development on school facilities to assist with both the school system’s internal planning efforts, as well as, the County’s planning efforts as part of their Comprehensive Plan.  This estimate is also used to calculate the recommended proffer contribution for rezoning applications.

 

The per student monetary proffer contribution is based on a methodology and formula which calculate the cost to provide physical space to accommodate a student.  This per student contribution is used to calculate a recommended proffer contribution for the net additional student that will be generated as a result of a rezoning application.  Upon the receipt of proffer funds, the funds are then distributed among impacted schools towards projects which enhance the capacity of the schools to help mitigate the impact of the proposed residential development.

 

The purpose of the meeting is to provide information about the Development Review and Proffer process.

Contact:                  Ryan McElveen, Comprehensive Planning Development Committee Chairman and At Large School Board Member

 

Meeting Category:  January 12, 2015 - Work Session No. 66

 

Subject:                   Refinement of the FPAC Charter

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The Board first discussed proposed revisions to the Facilities Planning and Advisory Council (FPAC) charter at the July 24, 2014, forum work session.  Board consensus was to refer to the Comprehensive Planning Development Committee (CPDC) a review of the proposed revised Charter, including those concerns raised at the forum by Board members, and then schedule a work session for a comprehensive discussion about FPAC and its mission, including any recommendations from CPDC. 

 

CPDC met on November 12 to discuss the proposed FPAC charter revisions and is recommending grammatical and clarifying revisions for the Board’s consideration, including:

  • adding language in Section V that the FPAC shall include the chairman of the Comprehensive Planning and Development Committee (CPDC), as School Board liaison, and a member of the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services Staff, each of whom shall be non-voting members.  The Strategic Governance Manual will be updated accordingly;
  • adding language in Section VI specifically stating that no current FCPS employee shall be appointed to the FPAC, nor may an FPAC member be employed by the FCPS during their appointed tenure. If such an FPAC member becomes employed by the FCPS, they must resign their appointment prior to the next regularly scheduled FPAC meeting; 
  • striking language in Section VII pertaining to information and technical support provided by the Department of Facilities and Transportation staff and such other FCPS departments as may be required and requested;
  • amending language in Section VII that the FPAC shall identify and prioritize facility-related concerns, recommend staff or ad hoc studies, and convene community and public meetings to fulfill its mission;
  • specifying in Section VIII that the FPAC shall provide semi-annual written reports to the Board on its progress on matters referred to or considered by the FPAC;

 

Board-approved changes to the FPAC proposed charter will be posted on the FPAC web page, http://www.fcps.edu/schlbd/committees/fpac.shtml.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the recommended revisions to the Facilities Planning Advisory Council charter, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.
