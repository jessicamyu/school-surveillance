Subject:  Family Engagement Survey Results

 

Staff Contacts: Nardos King, Chief Equity Officer

                         Kathleen Walts, Executive Director, Office of Professional Learning and Equity

 

Other Staff Present:        

Renee LaHuffman-Jackson, Coordinator, Family and School Partnerships

JoVon Rogers, Principal, Mount Vernon High School

Jamie Gadley, Principal, Dranesville Elementary School

Alisha Martinez, Project Manager, Educational Support

 

                                 

Meeting Category:  September 13, 2022 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

Outcome:  To understand and discuss the results of the second administration of the division-wide family engagement survey.    

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Research tells us that families have a major influence on their children’s achievements through school and through life. When schools build partnerships with families, respond to their concerns, and honor their contributions, they promote a range of benefits for students, including improved school readiness, higher student achievement, better social skills and behavior, and increased likelihood of high school graduation. In spring 2022, FCPS, in collaboration with K12 Insight, developed and administered the second family engagement survey across the division. The survey was made available in 9 languages, including English, and more than 35,000 responses were received. The survey questions were focused on the topics of welcoming environment, communication, student success, advocacy, family and school partnerships, and collaboration with the community.

 

The survey was open from March 24 to April 14. Email invitations with unique survey links were sent to parents. Parents could also participate via a public link on the division’s website. Paper surveys were available on request. Parents with children attending more than one school could take the survey for each school their child attends.
