Recognition of Child Nutrition Employee Week - May 4-8, 2009


Caring. Trained. Thoughtful. Professional. Dedicated. These are just a few words that describe today's child nutrition professional.

Child nutrition employees in Fairfax County Public Schools demonstrate daily their professional commitment to providing students with nutritious menu choices that reflect current research and meet the Dietary Guidelines for Americans. School nutrition employees must balance many roles and follow numerous federal, state and local regulations to ensure safe and healthy meals are available in schools. They are trained sanitation and food safety experts and must manage financially self-sufficient programs. School nutrition professionals also provide nutrition education to students, as well as healthy catering services to their communities. They use their creativity to make the cafeteria a fun and welcoming place all year long and perform their jobs each day because they care passionately about the children they serve.

FCPS child nutrition employees are committed to imparting food and nutrition knowledge and skills to students, using the school cafeteria as the nutrition laboratory and are influential in preparing students to learn.

Recognition of Better Hearing and Speech Month - May 2009

Since 1927, the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association has celebrated Better Hearing and Speech Month each May to raise public awareness of communication disorders that affect an estimated 42 million people. One in 10 families is affected by a communication disorder.

Currently, Fairfax County Public Schools provides speech and language services to 10,352 students and deaf and hard of hearing services to 452 students. Fairfax County Public Schools annually observes and celebrates Better Hearing and Speech Month and encourages all citizens in our school community to recognize the efforts, dedication, and skills of speech-language clinicians, audiologists, and teachers of the deaf and hard of hearing.

      Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

      Meeting Type: May 7, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 19

      SUBJECT: Award of Contract

      TYPE: Action

      BACKGROUND:

      FCPS offers two deferred compensation programs, 403(b) and 457(b), that provide employees the opportunity to save for retirement and supplement the benefits provided under the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) and the Educational Employees Supplemental Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC), or the Fairfax County Employees Retirement System. These deferred compensation plans are funded completely with employee money – no employer contribution is involved.

      The contracts for the current 403(b) plan expire on June 30, 2009. On September 8, 2008, Fairfax County Public School’s Office of Procurement Services issued a Request for Proposal RFP09-995675-73, soliciting offers from qualified firms to provided 403(b) investment services. The 403(b) Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) has completed an extensive RFP process to arrive at award recommendations for new 403(b) contracts. Given the complexity of the transition to new providers and extensive counseling needed on new fund choices, staff is working with the current 403(b) vendors to obtain contract extensions until December 31, 2009. It is recommended that the contract period for the new contracts awarded as a result of this RFP begin on September 1, 2009.

      KEY POINTS:

      Previously, contracts executed under 403(b) plans were considered individual investment arrangements with no employer involvement other than the taking a payroll deduction. Over the last few years there has been increased scrutiny of these plans, which prompted the federal government to enact regulatory changes that now require the employer to provide increased oversight and compliance monitoring for 403(b) plans.
In concert with these regulatory changes, most employers are substantially reducing the number of vendors which are available under the plan. Vendor reduction allows the employer to appropriately monitor compliance with plan limits and contractual terms, but more importantly, pools the employees’ investment dollars which can then be leveraged to secure lower investment management fees. The contract award recommendations made as a result of this RFP accomplish the above objectives for FCPS. Currently, the FCPS 403(b) plan consists of six active and two grandfathered vendors, which in total constitute thousands of individual contracts that employees have executed with plan providers.


The Request for Proposal required that proposals be submitted based on offering ‘best in class’ fund selections and a variety of investment structures in order to meet the needs of FCPS employees. The Request for Proposal was sent to current 403(b) providers as well as those who are registered in the eVA (Virginia e-Procurement Solution). The SAC received proposals from eleven vendors/offerors, ten of whom were considered responsive. The responsive vendors/offerors were: Great West Retirement Services, Metropolitan Life Insurance Company, Variable Annuity Life Insurance Company of America (VALIC), ING Life Insurance and Annuity Company, Teachers Insurance and Annuity Association - College Retirement Equities Fund (TIAA-CREF), Lincoln Financial Group, AXA, Ameriprise, Horace Mann, and Lincoln Investment Planning. All proposals were evaluated based on the following defined criteria:
        • Company Overview
        • Organizational Structure
        • Account Management & Member Services
        • Investments, Plan Procedures and Recordkeeping
        • Regulatory Actions and Compliance
        • Information Systems and Reporting
        • Financial Stability and Insurance Coverage
        • Communications
        • Participant Education/Field Services Reps, Investment Advisory Services
        • Implementation
        • Investment Structure
        • Mutual Funds
        • Fixed Variable Annuities
        • Mutual Fund Window
        • Other Fees

Upon completion of the final evaluations and negotiations, the Office of Benefit Services believes three of these vendors/offerors (Great West Retirement Services, TIAA-CREF, and VALIC) provide employees with the best array of investment options, competitive fees, and responsive member services, and is recommending contract award to these vendors. The Selection Advisory Committee concurs with this assessment. Of the three vendors/offerors recommended for contract award, only VALIC currently has a contract with FCPS for 403(b) plan services. Great West Retirement Services currently has a contract with FCPS for 457(b) plan services, and TIAA-CREF, a vendor nationally recognized in the education marketplace, is not currently offered. Contracts for five vendors that are currently in effect (ING Financial Advisors, Metropolitan Life Insurance Company, Lincoln Financial Group, Financial Directors, and Horace Mann) will expire effective December 31, 2009, however, new enrollments to these plans will not be permitted after August 2009.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board approve the contract awards for providing IRS Section 403(b) Plan deferred compensation services for FCPS employees to Great West Retirement Services, TIAA-CREF and VALIC effective September 1, 2009, and through the duration of the three (3) year contract as recommended by the Office of Benefit Services and endorsed by the Selection Advisory Committee (SAC).

SUBJECT: CTEMS Annual Plan and 2009-2010 Application for Career and Technical Education Funds

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:
      The Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) document provides the Virginia Department of Education with each local education agency’s (LEA) plan for providing and implementing career and technical education instruction to both public school and adult students. The CTEMS document specifies all of the activities and programs available to students as a result of local, state, and federal funds. The CTEMS document is prepared in the preset uniform format (including assurances, charts, and schedules) as provided by the Virginia Department of Education.
      Federal funding from the Carl D. Perkins Career and Technical Education Act of 2006 (Perkins IV), which is a continuing grant provided to all local education agencies (LEA) in the Commonwealth of Virginia, is available to assist school districts in providing elective career and technical programs for middle and high school students. Formal application for federal moneys is one component of the annual CTEMS document to the Virginia Department of Education.

      Since this plan is an update of a multi-year plan, other than proposed expenditures, there are very few changes. The changes from the 2008-2009 approved plan are noted in bold print throughout the document.
KEY POINTS:
      Schedule 2-B, page 52, of the CTEMS document specifies the expenditures of the Perkins federal grant in improving career and technical education programs according to the “Appropriate Use of Federal Funds” on page 66 of the document.

      Focusing on current priority needs for this school division, the CTEMS local plan continues a cycle in identifying the goals, objectives, and strategies for locally providing career and technical education programs.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board approve the CTEMS Annual Plan and 2009-2010 Application for Career and Technical Education Funds, as detailed in the agenda item.

SUBJECT: FY 2010 Approved Budget

TYPE: New Business

BACKGROUND:
      This agenda reflects actions by the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors (BOS) on the School Board’s FY 2010 budget request, changes in state revenue due to final actions taken by the General Assembly, and changes due to pending receipt of federal stimulus funds from the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 (ARRA) in FY 2010.
KEY POINTS:
      In its FY 2010 Adopted Budget Plan, the BOS did not approve an increase in the county transfer, which holds the transfer to the amount received in FY 2009 and is $56.8 million less than the School Board’s original request. Final General Assembly actions resulted in a reduction of $2.1 million in sales tax revenue offset by an increase in basic aid.

      Federal ARRA funding includes $23.7 million from the State Fiscal Stabilization Fund (SFSF), $18.9 million in IDEA funds, and $6.7 million in Title I funds (reflected in the Grants and Self-Supporting Fund). Using ARRA funds, $5.6 million in advertised budget reductions were restored, more than $32.2 million in grim budget reductions were averted, and $4.9 million will be used to implement other innovative programs. Title I ARRA funds will be used to provide extended learning opportunities and other initiatives in Title I schools.

      Using the most recent data, the fuel budget is decreased by $2.6 million, and the membership growth cost is reduced by $3.8 million. This agenda item also includes a $1.4 million increase in the annual IDEA award, a $0.2 million increase for AP/IB tests, $0.3 million for student achievement goal projects, and $4.7 million in savings from bell schedule adjustments.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board adopt Fairfax County Public Schools’ FY 2010 Approved Budget as detailed in the agenda item.
