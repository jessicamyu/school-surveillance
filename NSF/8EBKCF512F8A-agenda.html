Staff Contact:     Kim P. Dockery, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Meeting Type:    June 8, 2011 - Work Session No. 124

 

Subject:              School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) 2010-2011 Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To:         Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics and Goal 2. Essential Life Skills

 

The School Health Advisory Committee is mandated by the Commonwealth of Virginia to assist with the development of health policy in the school division and the evaluation of the status of school health, health education, the school environment, and health services.

 

Key Points:

Summary of Recommendations

 

  • SHAC reaffirms its recommendation from the 2009-2010 school year regarding the implementation of a tobacco-free school system policy. 
  • SHAC recommends that information about managing food allergies in schools be made available on the FCPS website.  SHAC also recommends implementing a comprehensive education program on food allergies for staff, students and parents including the ability to recognize signs and symptoms of a food allergy reaction.
  • SHAC recommends that the School Board fully embrace Safe Routes to School. This could include incorporating safe routes to school in the design of any new or renovated school construction; providing guidance to principals; and working with Fairfax County Board of Supervisors to reinstate school education officers.    
  • SHAC recommends required implementation of the health curriculum in elementary schools to address health literacy.  These measures can help the division meet and/or exceed the 150 minutes that legislative efforts are trying to impose.  More importantly, happy, healthy, fit, and active students are the right initiative for Fairfax County Public Schools.

Subject:                        Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC) Annual Report

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Related to:                    Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board
 

Key Points: 

 

The School Board charged the HRAC to “Explore the issue of student bullying and harassment and compile a compendium of efforts FCPS is making to address the issue, in order to educate all about this critical issue. ” 

 

In response to the School Board’s charge; the committee invited speakers from the community and staff to share information about their efforts to combat bullying.  In addition, they reviewed various sources of data compiled by local and nation experts.  Unlike previous reports, the HRAC was not asked to provide recommendations; nonetheless they included a few observations.
