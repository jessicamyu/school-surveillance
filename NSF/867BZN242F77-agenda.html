National Hispanic American Heritage Month - September 15 - October 15

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-american community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

A JOINT RESOLUTION TO UNDERTAKE A DETAILED REVIEW OF COUNTY LINES OF BUSINESS
AND SCHOOL PROGRAMS TO IDENTIFY OPPORTUNITIES FOR STREAMLINING, CONSOLIDATIONS, AND ELIMINATIONS

WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board have a history of working cooperatively together on budget and financial issues; and,

WHEREAS, the FY 2010 budget will present significant challenges for Fairfax County and Fairfax County Public Schools, with a combined total deficit projected of over $430 million as a result of unprecedented declines in the residential real estate market ; and,

WHEREAS, the projected imbalance in the FY 2010 budget and minimal revenue growth in FY 2011 and FY 2012 will require further elimination, modification, reorganization, and/or reduction of County and School programs beyond those achieved for FY 2009, as well as other sustainable strategies to align County and School spending to available resources; and,

WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board are proud of the nationally and internationally recognized high-caliber services and educational programming provided by their respective organizations and firmly believe that Fairfax County residents receive an excellent return on the investment of their tax dollars;

WHEREAS, it is the desire of the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board to preserve the high quality of life for Fairfax County residents and the outstanding educational opportunities for its students and to ensure that services are provided in the most effective, efficient, affordable, and customer friendly manner possible; and now, therefore, be it

RESOLVED, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board will undertake a detailed review of County Lines of Business and School programs to identify multi-year opportunities for cost reduction and savings to address the projected deficits that include program delivery options and possible service consolidations and eliminations and,

RESOLVED FURTHER, both Boards will engage in a dialogue with the public and employees to secure suggestions and comments regarding the budget opportunities and alternatives for adjustment throughout the development of the FY 2010 budget; and,

RESOLVED FURTHER, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board will review current joint and shared service functions and examine opportunities for further collaboration and consolidation for efficiencies; and

RESOLVED FURTHER, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board are committed to sustaining our quality of life, managing the expectations of County residents and recognizing the economic realities of the foreseeable future; and

RESOLVED FURTHER, the Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board recognize that each must meet its proportionate share of the deficit reductions that will be required in FY 2010.

SUBJECT: Establishment of the Program Review Committee

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:

The School Board and the Board of Supervisors and met on July 18, 2008 to discuss the FY 2010 fiscal forecast and the current budget situation. Approximately 75% of FCPS’s total funding is derived from locally generated revenues as appropriated by the Board of Supervisors. Those present agreed to sponsor a joint resolution pledging to cooperate fully in a detailed review of County lines of business and school programs and operations to identify opportunities for streamlining, consolidations, and eliminations, and to identify multi-year opportunities for cost reductions and savings to address the potential deficits. Each Board will take formal action to approve the resolution at a future business meeting (the School Board’s approval is scheduled for September 4).

The County and Fairfax County Public Schools have scheduled and will jointly sponsor over 20 community meetings to gather feedback from their constituents on the budget.

KEY POINTS:

At the July 19, 2008, School Board retreat, there was discussion to establish an ad hoc committee to review FCPS programs and operations with the Superintendent and the staff. This review is recognized by the School Board and Superintendent as a necessary and important task. The outcome will inform the Superintendent’s proposed budget for FY2010. The program review will also inform the School Board’s expenditure and revenue decisions that will be reflected in the advertised budget, including the revenue that will be requested from the Board of Supervisors. The program review will be focused on sustaining high-quality educational services to the greatest extent possible in a period of fiscal austerity, while also meeting Federal and state mandates, many of which are unfunded. The results of the reviews by FCPS and the County will be the subject of a subsequent joint meeting on November 14, 2008.

The Program Review Committee (PRC), if its formation is approved by the School Board, will first work with the Superintendent to design a program review process that ensures a thorough review of the cost effectiveness of each program and operation under his direction. The PRC will then support the full-Board’s understanding of the Program Review results and Superintendent’s ultimate recommendations; and will identify, as appropriate, selected programs for a more detailed review by the PRC and/or School Board.

All PRC meetings will be public work sessions. Although seven members are by this action appointed to serve on the PRC, all School Board members are welcomed and encouraged to participate in any of the meetings.

The candidate PRC members met in preliminary meetings on August 14th to discuss process and logistics and on August 25th to discuss at the September 8th work session of the full School Board on the subject of budget priorities and funding criteria. PRC’s tentative meeting schedule is as follows:

The Program Review Committee’s work will be completed by December 2008, unless extended by a vote of the entire School Board.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board establish the Program Review Committee (“the Committee”) to work with the Superintendent to design a program review process that ensures a thorough review of the cost effectiveness of each program and operation under his direction; the PRC will also monitor the implementation of the program review; keep the full School Board appropriately informed, and recommend if a program should be reviewed by the School Board; and; further move that Brad Center, Ilryong Moon, Phillip Niedzielski-Eichner, James Raney, Kathy Smith, Jane Strauss, and Tessie Wilson be appointed the members of the Committee, with the Budget Liaison designated as the committee chairman.

SUBJECT: Fall 2008 Scope of Boundary Studies

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:

On July 14, 2008, staff presented the recommended fall 2008 Scope of Boundary Studies for new elementary schools at Coppermine and Laurel Hill sites. The Board concluded that 1) additional information was needed and that 2) the Board would consider the scope of the fall boundary studies at its regular meeting scheduled for September 4, 2008, instead of an informal consensus vote at a work session.

At the July 18, 2008, retreat, the School Board used the Trego Education Forum Decision Analysis process to develop criteria for consideration in selecting schools to be included in the boundary studies for elementary schools at the Coppermine and Laurel Hill sites. Staff was directed to utilize these criteria to assist in an analysis of schools to be considered for the 2008 fall boundary studies.

KEY POINTS:

      The purpose of the 2008 fall studies is to establish attendance areas for new elementary schools at the Coppermine and Laurel Hill sites, both of which are under construction and scheduled to open in fall 2009. Staff has reviewed the surrounding elementary schools for both sites and provided an assessment of capacities, enrollments and projections.

      In order to provide information to assist the Board in the determination of which schools should be included in the boundary studies for schools at Coppermine and Laurel Hill, the Board asked staff to assess a group of schools against the 12 criteria, developed at the July 18 retreat. Although many of these factors are not subject to precise measurement, staff has created the numerical rankings, using available data and their best judgment.

      Staff’s analysis using the 12 criteria is provided in the attached matrix, “Analysis of Factors Impacting Selection of Schools for Boundary Study”.

      As staff applied criteria to the Coppermine study, they recommend that Fox Mill Elementary School not be included because of their involvement in a recent boundary study; staff does recommend that Hutchison Elementary School be included in the study because of its available capacity.

      The additional capacity analysis information requested by the Board is contained in the attached countywide capacity analysis; this analysis will be updated with the 2008 Fall CIP to include new 5 year projections.

OPTIONS:
1) Approve staff’s initial recommendation;
2) Amend staff’s recommendation to reflect a different group of schools to be involved in the study.
    RECOMMENDATION:
        1) That the School Board adopts the following criteria for the purpose of selecting schools to be included in the boundary studies for the new elementary schools at the Coppermine and Laurel Hill sites: help balance enrollment; accommodate projected enrollment; have geographic proximity; provide socio-economic and ESOL balance; adhere to core capacity; respect man-made and natural barriers; accommodate current enrollment; reduce boundary lines; minimize split neighborhoods; minimize split feeder assignments; maximize program stability; and minimize additional capital/operating costs; further move that the Board shall make the final designation of schools to be included in the boundary study, considering not only these criteria, but also any other factors that will contribute to the improved operational efficiency and instructional effectiveness of the school system, pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments and School Closings.

        2) That the School Board directs the Superintendent to conduct a study of Floris, Herndon, Hutchison, McNair, and Oak Hill Elementary Schools and develop preliminary boundary options for populating the new elementary school at the Coppermine site, pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments and School Closings.

    3) That the School Board directs the Superintendent to conduct a study of Gunston, Halley, Lorton Station, Newington Forest, and Silverbrook Elementary Schools and develop preliminary boundary options for populating the new elementary school at the Laurel Hill site pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments and School Closings.

    TYPE: New Business


    BACKGROUND:
        The Fairfax County School Board voted to build a South County Middle School directly adjacent to the current South County Secondary School by 2012. In an effort to meet this accelerated schedule, design work has to begin immediately.

        The Office of Design and Construction Services has determined that the most efficient and effective school design plan for the middle school property in Lorton is the “Glasgow” Middle School design. This design created for the construction of the Glasgow Middle School by the architectural firm of by Ballou Justice Upton Architects (BJUA).

        Site adapting the Glasgow Middle School plans will be both a cost effective and efficient means to meet the construction schedule for the South County Middle School. As the original architect for the Glasgow Middle School design BJUA owns the original Glasgow design drawings and all specifications and electronic data as the “Instruments of Service” for that original project. Under the terms of our original agreement with the Architect the “Instruments of Service” cannot be used for any new purpose without significant liability and risk to the Owner. In addition, BJUA has the required expertise that will be required to ensure the integrity of the final redesign of the facility plans.


    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board award the contract for architectural and engineering services for the design of South County Middle School to Ballou Justice Upton Architects, in the amount of $2,555,000, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.
