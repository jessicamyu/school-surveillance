RESOLUTION

NAMING THE JROTC CLASSROOM AT

WEST POTOMAC HIGH SCHOOL

 “THE COLONEL JESSE LEE WATSON, III CLASSROOM”

 

 

          WHEREAS, Colonel Jesse Lee Watson, III served on active duty as a commissioned officer in the United States Army for 23 years and upon retirement chose to dedicate his remaining professional life to helping young people become  better citizens and scholars; and
 

          WHEREAS, Colonel Watson began his career in 1994 with Fairfax County Public Schools as the first senior Army Junior Reserve Officers Training Corps (JROTC) instructor at West Potomac High School; he later served as a resource teacher at West Potomac High School before he transferred to Lake Braddock Secondary School, where he was the sub-school principal until his death in April 2011; and

 

          WHEREAS, during his tenure as the JROTC instructor, he provided excellent instruction and a variety of extra-curricular activities and community service opportunities, including an outstanding drill team, Raider challenge team, rifle team, a tutoring program where cadets mentor students at Belleview and Bucknell Elementary Schools, and the West Potomac High School’s Color Guard, which has participated in many installation ceremonies such as the Presidential inauguration; and  

 

          WHEREAS, the West Potomac High School’s JROTC battalion earned Honor Unit with Distinction status during the battalion’s first year of activation in 1995, and that standard of excellence Colonel Watson established has continued as the JROTC battalion has earned Honor Unit with Distinction status after five subsequent triennial formal inspections; and

 

          WHEREAS, his efforts inspired hundreds of young men and women to strive for academic excellence, while incorporating leadership skills and the core military values in their lives; during his tenure, twenty-one cadets earned military scholarships, including an appointment to West Point;

 

          NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, in recognition of Colonel Watson’s love for life, relentless dedication to service, and contributions to Fairfax County Public Schools, hereby names, in his memory, the Army JROTC classroom at West Potomac High School, “The Colonel Jesse Lee Watson, III Classroom.”

Key Points:

 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the “individual Trustee.”  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.

 

At a recent meeting of the ERFC Board of Trustees, Michael Burke, the Managing Director for the Wealth and Investment Management division of Barclays, was unanimously recommended for appointment as the “individual Trustee.”  If approved by the School Board, Mr. Burke will serve for his first year on the ERFC Board.  Mr. Burke meets all of the requirements of the position.

[Updated 6/20/13 - Attachment]

 

Staff Contact:         Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category:  June 20, 2013 – Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                  Amendment 3 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:            Administrative Clarification

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending technical amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of (1) clarifying that Member and Employer contributions will be paid for each month during which the Member received pay, in accordance with uniform and nondiscriminatory regulations adopted by the Trustees; and (2) identifying the default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits under ERFC if no Named Beneficiary survives the Member. Neither of the proposed changes is expected to affect the benefits payable under the Plan.

 

Member and Employer Contributions: The current plan documents provide that the Employer and each Member shall contribute a percentage of the Member’s Salary during the Member’s Eligible Employment. Both ERFC and ERFC 2001 should be amended to clarify that Member and Employer contributions will be paid for each month during which the Member received pay, in accordance with uniform and nondiscriminatory regulations adopted by the Trustees. This amendment is necessary to conform to the changes under the Virginia Retirement System modernization project.

 

Default Beneficiary: The current ERFC plan document does not provide for a default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits if no Named Beneficiary survives the Member. Consistent with the default beneficiary rules for Death Benefits, ERFC should be amended to provide for a default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment 3 to ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purposes of (1) clarifying that Member and Employer contributions will be paid for each month during which the Member received pay, in accordance with uniform and nondiscriminatory regulations adopted by the Trustees; and (2) identifying the default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits under ERFC if no Named Beneficiary survives the Member.

Staff Contact:           Phyllis Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:  De Hawley Brown, director, Benefit Services

 

Meeting Category:     June 20, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                    Virginia Retirement System (VRS) Resolution

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To:               Legal Requirement Chapter 822 of the 2012 Acts of Assembly (SB497)

 

Key Points:

 

During the 2012 session of the Virginia General Assembly, legislation was passed requiring all active participants of the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) to contribute toward the cost of retirement benefits. The legislation provides that localities and school systems may phase in employee contributions at a rate of 1% or more per year, and that the full phase in of a 5% contribution rate must be completed by July 1, 2016. Jurisdictions must provide corresponding increases to salaries to offset these employee contributions. Newly hired or rehired employees as of July 1, 2012, shall pay 5% of their salary immediately and shall not have their contributions phased in. The Board adopted a 2% employee contribution rate for employees for Fiscal Year 2013.

 

At the May 23, 2013, regular business meeting of the Fairfax County School Board, the Board adopted the remaining 3% contribution for a total 5% employee contribution rate for current employees for Fiscal Year 2014. VRS requires all participating employers to execute the attached resolution documenting the agreed upon employee contribution rates. The resolution is binding and irrevocable.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the Virginia Retirement System resolution, as detailed in the agenda item, in compliance with Chapter 822 of the 2012 Acts of Assembly (SB497) and consistent with the Fairfax County School Board FY 2014 Approved Budget.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
