Staff Contact:  Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:  Michael Draeger, director, HR Business Services

 

Meeting Category:  March 14, 2016 - Work Session No. 79

 

Subject:  Compensation Study

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan, Goal 3

 

Key Points: 

 

FCPS has hired Segal Group, a benefits, compensation, and human resources consulting firm, to identify a compensation structure that attracts and retains qualified employees through an optimal mix of salary and benefits. The study is a key piece of the Strategic Plan’s goal 3 – Premier Workforce – which aims to recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts, and ensure that FCPS maintains a competitive compensation package.

 

For the first phase of the compensation study, Segal Group presented on December 7, 2015 and February 8, 2016 the initial results on FCPS’ market position for compensation for teachers, instructional assistants, school-based administrators, and other benchmarked Unified Scale positions. 

 

The second phase of the study requires recommending strategic compensation structures and models.  This includes the collection and evaluation of input and feedback from employees through a survey and focus groups regarding compensation program options, which will be compiled by mid-April.

 

For today’s discussion, Segal Group will share compensation strategies for the purpose of narrowing down on a compensation philosophy for FCPS.

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                          

 

Key Points:

 

Overview

On February 4, 2016, the School Board adopted the FY 2017 Advertised Budget that includes a requested increase in the county transfer of 6.7 percent. Expenditure adjustments total $149.2 million and 127.0 positions. The FY 2017 Advertised Budget increase includes funding to address large class sizes in elementary schools and to eliminate elementary class sizes of 30 and above, where classroom space is available. The budget also includes funding to provide a step increase for eligible employees and a 1.0 percent market scale adjustment (MSA) for all employees. Funding is provided for an initial investment in the teacher salary scales to begin to address the findings from the external compensation study that is currently underway that showed FCPS is significantly below market on teacher salaries. Other expenditure increases in the FY 2017 Advertised Budget include a living wage adjustment, retirement rate increases, and health insurance increases. Collectively, these are offset by expenditure reductions totaling $27.7 million.

 

County’s Advertised Budget and Advertised Tax Rate

The County Executive presented his FY 2017 Advertised Budget on February 16, 2016, which includes a school operating fund transfer increase of $54.75 million or 3.0 percent. The recommended transfer to the School Operating Fund results in a shortfall of $67.9 million as compared to FCPS' FY 2017 Advertised Budget. The County’s advertised budget recommends a 4 cent increase in the current real estate tax rate, which would increase the rate from $1.09 to $1.13 per $100 of assessed value.

 

On February 26, 2016, the School Board and County Board of Supervisors held a joint budget work session to discuss both advertised budgets and the impact of the state budget, as currently proposed by the house and senate amendments to the Governor’s Introduced Budget.

 

On March 1, 2016, the County voted to advertise the real estate tax rate for FY 2017 at $1.13 per $100 of assessed value. Advertising the tax rate allows the Board of Supervisors to adopt the advertised rate or one that is lower, but a higher tax rate cannot be imposed. The County’s FY 2017 Advertised Budget has funding of $22.53 million available for the Board of Supervisor’s consideration assuming the advertised tax rate increase of 4 cents is adopted.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS’ School Operating Fund

On February 25, 2016, the House and Senate separately adopted amendments to the Governor’s Introduced 2016-2018 Biennial Budget (HB/SB 30).  A summary of the state revenue impacts to FCPS’ operating budget based on the Governor’s Introduced Budget and the proposed amendments from the House and Senate, as compared to FCPS’ FY 2017 Advertised Budget is provided in the attachment.

 

 

Additional Considerations and Unknowns

Each spring, FCPS updates the enrollment and student demographic projections that are used to calculate staffing for the following school year. Staffing is calculated for each school at both the proposed (fall) and approved (spring) stages of the budget process. The final cost of enrollment growth and changes in student demographics is calculated in the spring, and any changes from the projected cost included in the proposed budget become part of the approved budget adjustments.

 

Based on the final budgets adopted by the state and county, any shortfall that remains will require additional revenue and expenditure adjustments to balance FCPS’ FY 2017 Approved Budget.
