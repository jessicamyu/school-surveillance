                              2005 Year of Languages

                              On June 12, 2003, Senator Christopher Dodd submitted U.S. Senate Resolution 170 designating 2005 as The Year of Languages.  Governor Mark Warner joins Senators Dodd and Cochran, as well as prominent business leaders, as members on the honorary committee for the 2005 Year of Languages. 

                              Fairfax County Public Schools is participating in this national initiative by highlighting the importance of language learning for all young Americans.   The School Board is committed to preparing students for a world class workforce that is competitive in the global economy of the 21st century.  Knowledge of more than one language helps equip students with the skills needed in today’s job market and higher education.

                              Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
                              Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

                                Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

                                SUBJECT: Award of Annual Contract – Lease/Purchase of Classroom Trailers

                                RECOMMENDATION:
                                  That the School Board award the annual contract for the lease/purchase of classroom trailers to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute one or more contracts as requirements become known.
                                EXPLANATION:
                                  Annually, Fairfax County Public Schools may have a requirement for additional temporary facilities to accommodate overcrowding at some school locations and to support renovations and other construction projects. Specifications have been prepared and the solicitation advertised. A bid tabulation and recommendation for award will be furnished prior to the January 27, 2005, School Board meeting.

                                  The solicitation was structured to allow for unit lease pricing, set up and skirting for a duplex, quad, or toilet trailer as may be required. The option exists for the School Board to acquire the leased facilities at the end of the 36-month lease term. Funding will be provided from the operating budget and the construction fund.

                                  The solicitation allows for the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to exercise options that could increase the number of classroom trailers ordered under this solicitation from one up to a total of 15 classroom trailers. At present, we do not have a requirement for temporary classrooms. However, an annual contract will allow the school staff the flexibility to exercise options to this contract in the event a temporary classroom is needed during the year.
