African American History Month - February 2006

Originally established as Negro History Week in 1926 by Dr. Carter G. Woodson, a noted African-American author and scholar, this event evolved into the establishment in 1976 of February as "Black History Month." This commemoration has increasingly been referred to as "African-American History Month," although both names are currently in use.

Since 1926, The Association for the Study of Afro-American Life and History (ASALH) has established the national theme for the month-long celebration of African American History Month. The National Theme for the celebration of Black History Month in the year 2006 is Celebrating Community: A Tribute to Black, Fraternal, Social, and Civic Institutions.

        6.04 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution – Recommendation to adopt the revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (FS; action scheduled 2/9/06)
        Meeting
        Jan 26, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 12
        Category
        6. New Business - 8:40 p.m.
        Type
        Information


        Staff Contact: Deirdra McLaughin, Chief Financial Officer, Department of Financial Services

        RECOMMENDATION:

          That the School Board adopt the revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution.
        EXPLANATION:
          The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors approved revisions to the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution effective November 21, 2005. This year, only administrative changes were made to the document; no actions on the part of the 2005 General Assembly affected the Purchasing Resolution. The most significant changes include:

        Identification of consultant services as a stand-alone definition and process, separate from professional services.
                Professional services and consultant services are now defined as separate service categories. Consultant services are defined as, “any type of services required by the County, but not furnished by its own employees, which is so unique that it should be obtained by negotiation on the basis of demonstrated competence and qualification.” In contrast, professional services are a distinct service category under the Virginia Code, requiring professional licensure and a greater focus on qualifications during the selection process. Establishing a separate definition and process for consultant services will not change the existing contract review and approval requirements.

        Modification of the process for negotiating a contract with a consultant, establishing consistency with the Virginia Public Procurement Act §2.2-4301 3b.
                Modification of the process for procurement of consultant services changes the dynamic in which the County negotiates with vendors. Until enactment of the 2005 Purchasing Resolution, the County conducted negotiations serially; that is, negotiations were conducted with the top rated offeror and a contract awarded if one could be agreed upon. If the County was unable to negotiate successfully with the top rated offeror, then negotiations were formally terminated and negotiations commenced with the second rated offeror and a contract awarded if one could be agreed upon, and so forth. The new process allows concurrent negotiations providing the County with the ability to negotiate with multiple offerors. Conducting negotiations with multiple offerors positions the County to compare and contrast competing offers simultaneously, tailoring the final agreement to best meet the needs of the County.
