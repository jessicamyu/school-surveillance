SUBJECT: Location of the Graham Road/Devonshire Renovation

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:
      For the past several years we have been studying the needs of the Graham Road school community and whether those needs would best be met by partnering with Fairfax County Government to include a Community School component into the renovated facility by either renovating Graham Road Elementary School or by relocating the school to the Devonshire site and renovating/adding to the building at that location.

      In summer 2005, a community task force was created to consider the options. In a January 2007 report to the School Board, the task force opined that the school should include a Community School component and remain at the Graham Road facility if the school system was able to expand the current site size (only 4.6 acres). Based upon this finding, significant efforts were made to acquire additional property adjacent to the site; however; we were not successful.

      In winter 2008, the task force reconvened and reconsidered the issues based upon current information to include our inability to expand the Graham Road site.

      Copies of both task force reports are attached.
OPTIONS:
      There are two options. Renovate the existing school at Graham Road or renovate/add to the former school at the Devonshire site. Staff has no recommendation at this time. Both options are considered viable.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board 1.) direct the Fairfax County Public Schools’ (FCPS) staff to begin immediately the design and architectural planning necessary to renovate and add to the facility at the Devonshire site for the purpose of relocating the Graham Road Elementary School to this site. The Devonshire site should be designed to accommodate up to 500 students;

2.) that the Devonshire site be renamed the Graham Road Elementary School after the Devonshire renovation is completed; and that once the school has been moved to its new location, the old school building be designated as the Graham Road Community Building, until such time as its future use is determined;
      3.) that FCPS staff is directed to develop plans for relocating staff and programs currently housed at Devonshire. Such plans should consider the most cost effective means of relocation by utilizing existing administrative capacities while ensuring continuity of services for students and community members;

      4.) that the future Graham Road Community Building remain in the FCPS inventory of properties. FCPS staff is directed, however, to begin discussions with the County immediately regarding collaboration opportunities for the delivery of County services from Graham Road Community Building. If appropriate, consideration may be given to transferring the property to the County should that organization provide the majority of its services and mutual agreement can be reached regarding such a transfer;

      5.) that FCPS staff is directed to negotiate a shared-use agreement with Fairfax County, which captures the opportunities to collaborate and ensures that the building remains a viable and valuable community asset serving a variety of educational, public services and recreational needs. Considerations for the shared-use agreement terms shall include (a.) County role in staffing the Graham Road Community Building; (b.) a description of the minimal renovation or improvement needs of the Graham Road Community Building once the school is moved to the Devonshire site; and (c.) a shared funding plan proportionate to the projected amount of County and FCPS use. SUBJECT: FY 2009 Approved Budget

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:
    This agenda reflects actions by the Board of Supervisors (BOS) on the School Board’s FY 2009 budget request and changes in state aid due to final actions taken by the General Assembly.

    The BOS adopted a FY 2009 county transfer increase of $40 million (2.5 percent), which is $23.7 million less than the School Board’s original request. Final General Assembly actions resulted in a loss of $16.7 million in revenue and an expenditure reduction of $21.8 million, resulting in a net increase of $5.1 million compared to the FY 2009 Advertised Budget.

    The agenda item includes $0.7 million in ongoing expenditures that were approved in the FY 2008 Third-Quarter Budget Review. Also included are details on the program and summer school redesign, membership adjustments, and budget reductions needed to meet the reduced transfer. As part of the FY 2009 approved budget process, the School Board approval is required to complete the GASB 45 implementation.

KEY POINTS:
    The FY 2009 Approved Budget includes a 2.5 percent increase in the county transfer and a net reduction of $39.7 million and 104.1 positions compared to the Advertised Budget. The market scale adjustment was reduced from 3 percent to 2 percent, and savings were realized from program redesign and lower Virginia Retirement System and group life insurance rates. Expenditure increases are required for the membership increase of 992 additional students for a total of 3,541 students from the FY 2008 approved, bus driver salaries, and for bus/vehicle fuel and labor costs.

    Due to the complexity of the FY 2009 budget, a summary reflecting actions taken from the proposed to the approved is presented first.

    Implementation of GASB 45 is also addressed in this agenda. The Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) Statement 45 directs how the school system should account for and report costs related to post-employment health care and other non-pension benefits, such as the retiree health benefit subsidy. This requirement is similar to those for pension benefits. To prepare for GASB 45, the School Other Post-Employment Benefits (OPEB) Trust Fund was established to accumulate and invest assets towards reducing the unfunded actuarial accrued liability as part of the FY 2008 Approved Budget. Beginning in FY 2008, FCPS is required to implement this standard.

RECOMMENDATION:
    1. That the School Board adopt the FY 2009 Approved Budget, to include revenue and expenditure adjustments, as detailed in the agenda item, and with the following revisions: (a) re-allocating $1 million from the instructional supply set-aside to the staffing reserve to be used to mitigate staffing issues which may arise from the increase of .5 students in small schools, the loss of Title I funding, or other situations which cause schools to lose a disproportionate amount of staffing, or where class size exceeds 30 students in elementary schools; (b) restoring funding of $1.2 million to expand full-day kindergarten to four schools; funding of $1.2 million will result from reducing replacement equipment by $0.2 million and reducing funding for the replacement of the student information system (SASI) by $1.0 million; and (c) restoring funding of $0.2 million to expand Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools (FLES) to four schools; funding of $0.2 million will result from reducing replacement equipment by $0.2 million.

    2. That the School Board approve the attached resolution that establishes a trust for the purpose of accumulating and investing assets to fund other post-employment benefits and pre-fund these benefits by participating in the “Virginia Pooled Other Post-Employment Benefits Trust Fund” administered by the Virginia Municipal League and the Virginia Association of Counties, as detailed in the agenda item.

    SUBJECT: Budget Direction for FY 2010

    TYPE: Action
     
    BACKGROUND:
     
        This motion directs the Superintendent to design a comprehensive program and operations review.  The reason for this review has been discussed by the Board and the Superintendent.  Market trends suggest that residential real estate assessments will result in negative growth in FY2010 as a result of the continued deterioration of the housing market.  In the absence of County intervention, revenues for school operations will realize a commensurate impact and will remain flat or decline in FY2010 and possibly for several years beyond.  The Fairfax community has historically supported School needs -- even in the most difficult fiscal times – once priorities were reassessed, program effectiveness was evaluated, and overhead spending has received a hard look.
         
        The Superintendent and his staff completed a first phase analysis prior to submitting his FY2009 budget.  His approach was predominantly “qualitative” in its design, relying heavily upon the best professional judgment of the FCPS central and school leadership.  This initial effort resulted in FCPS realizing a $33 million reduction in expenses.
         
        The next phase design should be substantially more quantitative in its focus by drawing upon program and operations performance information, augmented by his and his staff’s best professional judgment.  While this motion provides flexibility to the Superintendent to propose the design, academic programming could be broken down, for example, into the following components:  special education, general education, advanced education, alternative education, and adult education.  Similarly, operations could be broken down into the following, as examples:  facilities, transportation, information technology, and central administration and overhead functions. 


    RECOMMENDATION:
     
      That the School Board direct the Superintendent to:

      1.) Design a comprehensive program and operations review that will involve the School Board in focused discussions with the leadership team, central instructional staff, support functions, and school leaders.  Focused reviews should be undertaken regarding program and service priorities in relation to sustaining high-quality educational services and meeting the School Board’s student achievement goals, as well as federal and state mandates.  The program and operations review design should target possible cost savings or avoidances through alternative service delivery models and opportunities for reorganization, consolidation, reduction, and/or elimination;
     
      2.) Design a community engagement process that ensures that the program and operations review is conducted openly and transparently and captures community input into the proceedings effectively and efficiently;
     
      3.) Report to the School Board on the proposed designs during the scheduled July 2008 work session.

    SUBJECT: Approval of Agreement – Junior Achievement Finance Park

    TYPE: Action

    BACKGROUND:
        Junior Achievement (JA) is a non-profit group which promotes economic and financial literacy education through business partnerships and curriculum programs. A Capstone Program called Finance Park which is targeted for a middle school audience consists of 26 forty-five minute lessons on personal financial literacy, supported by teacher instruction and student workbooks. This program culminates in a field trip to an interactive site where students apply the knowledge and skills they have learned in the classroom. When students arrive at the Finance Park site, they become “an adult for the day”. The students are given an identity card with a description of a person's personal life: their marital status, children, income and occupation. Students are then faced with making decisions regarding household expenses. The goal is for them to develop a balanced budget.

        The Finance Park site is divided into stations which represent the various components of a household budget such as utilities, savings, entertainment, housing, automobiles, etc. At each station, there is a computer in which students will gather information about the related expenses and later enter their own information based upon the decisions they have made. At the end of the experience, students receive a printout with their budget.

        FCPS has had access to this interactive site which is presently a mobile unit located on the grounds of Capital One headquarters in McLean. In fall 2006, eighth grade students from Rocky Run MS participated in a pilot of this program. Since then the mobile unit has been brought back to the area and students from the summer school program for civics (summer 2007), and six additional middle schools have participated in the program and visited the Finance Park mobile unit. The overall response from participation in this interactive program has been positive by all involved: students, parents, and teachers.

        The Washington Capital Region of Junior Achievement is now proposing a long term partnership with FCPS to build a permanent structure that would house this program and allow all eighth grade students in FCPS to take part in this personal financial literacy curriculum. Forming this partnership would facilitate all of our middle school students meeting many of the Economics Education and Financial Literacy Objectives required by Virginia Department of Education in April 2006.

        Estimated annual expenses for implementation of JA Finance Park include the following:

        Student fee ($20 per student X 11,500 students)$ 230,000
        One Resource teacher (218 contract to facilitate training, scheduling, Curriculum implementation) $ 80,055
        Substitutes (4 per school x $125; will vary according to the needs
        of the school)         		$ 13,500
        Teacher training stipend (approx 250 teachers -- grade 8 civics &
        250 grade 8 mathematics & Algebra teachers X $100)        		$ 50,000
        Student transportation $ 88,220
        Facility maintenance and operations (includes utilities, grounds,
        major maintenance/infrastructure replacement, custodial, insurance)        		$ 84,900
        Total$ 546,775


        In addition to the costs outlined above, FCPS will lease to JA, for one dollar ($1) per year, a parcel of property on the grounds of the Woodson/Frost campus upon which JA will construct a facility to house the Finance Park program operations. While there is virtually no initial cost to FCPS for the facility, per the agreement with JA, FCPS will fund all ongoing operations and maintenance costs to support the facility. Moreover, should it be determined that termination of the agreement with JA is in the best interest of the School Board, we would be obligated to purchase the facility for its then fair market value.

        The agreement between FCPS and JA will establish an initial fifteen (15) year period; four (4) additional five (5) year periods are available should we mutually agree to continue the program after the initial period.
