National Hispanic Heritage Month - September 15-October 15, 2016

 

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-American community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.  This year’s theme is “Honoring Our Heritage.  Building Our Future.”

 

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

 

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

 

U. S. Constitution Day and Citizenship Day - September 17, 2016

 

Constitution Day and Citizenship Day are observed each year on September 17 to commemorate the signing of the Constitution on September 17, 1787, and “recognize all who, by coming of age or by naturalization, have become citizens.”  Recent legislation has renamed the day as Constitution Day and Citizenship Day and urges proper observance of the day. This year, the official observance day is Saturday, September 17, and is the 228th anniversary of the nation’s founding document.

 

In observance of Constitution Day and Citizenship Day, all schools in FCPS will be observing this day with activities that recognize the adoption of the United States Constitution and those who have become citizens under the Constitution.  Schools will use a variety of curriculum resources to teach students about the importance of the Constitution and responsible citizenship.  Throughout the school year, high school students will have an opportunity to participate in service learning projects related to responsible citizenship, which can lead to a Civics Seal on their diploma.

 

Childhood Cancer Awareness Month

 

September has been designated as Childhood Cancer Awareness Month to increase awareness of the types of cancer that strike children, focus on issues affecting survivors, and raise funds for research and family support.  In the United States, pediatric cancer strikes nearly 16,000 children under the age of 21 every year.  Of those FCPS students being treated for childhood cancer, many participate in home-bound instruction while they are undergoing treatment or recuperating.  Research has helped improve treatments that have decreased mortality rates for many types of childhood cancer by more than 50 percent during the past 30 years.  FCPS physical education teacher Jay Coakley, a 33-year veteran FCPS employee, was so inspired by one of his students with leukemia  that he founded Ellie’s Hats, a nonprofit organization that provides warm and colorful hats to children undergoing cancer treatment, strives to raise awareness of childhood cancer, and provides support to children and their families.  The Board encourages the community to support children and their families who are battling cancer, and to remember those whose lives were cut short by pediatric cancer.

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)                                                                             

 

Other Staff Present: Priscille Dando, coordinator, Library Information Services, ISD

                               

Meeting Category:  September 8, 2016

 

Subject: Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee

 

School Board Action Required: Information                                   

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:    

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents. Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.  For 2016-2017 the Board members making appointments are Tamara Derenak Kaufax (Lee), Sandy Evans (Mason), Elizabeth Schultz (Springfield), Ryan McElveen (At Large), Ilryong Moon (At Large), and Jeanette Hough (At Large). Last year the School Board district representatives from Braddock, Dranesville, Hunter Mill, Mount Vernon, Providence and Sully were selected to appoint individuals.

 

Two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school.  The level will be determined by a random selection. If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate. 

 

The student representative to the School Board appoints an individual to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.

Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009.   

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint individuals to serve a one-year term on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:  Fairfax County Public Schools Strategic Plan Update

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan

 

Key Points:

 

During the 2014-15 School year, Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite. Four Ignite Goal Reports were presented to the School Board, highlighting staff and student progress. In July, the School Board and FCPS staff held separate retreats to assess the progress of the strategic plan. The Leadership Team reviewed the input and updated the following:

 

  • Desired Outcomes
  • Actions
  • Metrics
  • Timelines
  • Cost estimates for associated Actions (new)

The completed plan, highlighting all updates and changes, will be posted for the WS scheduled for September 19, 2016.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board adopt the changes to the four goal areas of the Fairfax County Public Schools Strategic Plan: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship.
