Key Points:

 

FY 2019 Midyear Budget Review

In the School Operating Fund, funds available are increasing by $1.5 million. The FY 2019 beginning balance is increasing by $2.6 million due to final year-end adjustments made after the FY 2018 Final Budget Review. The adjustments include an increase of $2.2 million in revenue primarily in sales tax based on actual receipts; and expenditure decreases of $0.4 million. In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2019 revenue adjustments include a net increase of $1.5 million in federal grant revenue, offset by a decrease of $2.6 million in state aid due a decrease in the average daily membership projected for

FY 2019. 

 

Recommended FY 2019 expenditure adjustments reflect a net increase of $1.5 million resulting primarily from $1.5 million federal grant expenditures. Other adjustments include funding for annual service awards of $15,000, and a transfer to the Adult and Community Education Fund of $46,146.

 

Expenditure adjustments offset revenue adjustments, resulting in no change to the set aside of the FY 2020 beginning balance of $24.5 million. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds and an enrollment update for FY 2019 are detailed in the attachment. Also attached is an update on the status of grants in FY 2018 and FY 2019.

 

FY 2020 Budget Discussion

Staff will provide a short presentation about selected items in the FY20 Fiscal Forecast including a high-level review of enrollment, potential budget increases, staffing standard refinement, the budget question process and the budget timeline. The bulk of the time on this topic will be allotted for School Board discussion.

 

BA Lanes

Over the summer, a work group, including representatives from the three teacher associations along with central office staff, reviewed options to mitigate the lost potential earnings for teachers on the top of the Bachelor’s lanes. At the October 15, 2018 School Board work session, information on the cost of grandfathering teachers on Step 11, Step 14, Step 16 and Step 18 was provided. Additionally the School Board asked staff to provide information about addressing the needs of those at the top of the scale. Next Steps from the October 15th, work session are attached.

 

Substitute Pay

The same work group provided data during the October 15th School Board work session about the composition of the substitute pool, demand for substitutes, and a menu of options for increasing fill rates. The options include:

  • adding a new substitute pay rate for non-retirees who hold at least a bachelor’s degree;
  • increase the rate of pay for all non-retiree substitutes to 100% of the market;
  • raise retiree substitute pay rates to the levels outlined in the FY20 fiscal forecast; and
  • institute a leave buy-back program.

Ignite Link:  Strategic Plan – Goal 4 Report

 

Summary/Background:

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 4, Resource Stewardship.
