Agenda Item: FCPS Elementary Foreign Language Programs

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

Background: Fairfax County Public Schools currently offers the following programs:
路 Partial-immersion program to students who are selected by lottery at thirteen sites. Families who live outside the attendance area must provide transportation.
路 Foreign Language in the Elementary School (FLES) programs at seven sites. This program is offered during the school day to all students who live inside the attendance area.
路 Before-school and after-school Foreign Language Experience (FLEX) programs at 64 sites. Parents pay for this program and must provide transportation.
路 Foreign language as an elective course of study in grades 7-12.

Key Issues: Fairfax County Public Schools’ vision is for all students to graduate with communicative competence in one or more languages other than English. To reach this goal all elementary students should be given the opportunity to begin foreign language study during the school day.

School Board Discussion/Decision: Elementary schools should have the option of offering foreign language instruction.

Staff Recommendation: It is recommended that the FLES model be the model for future programs so that all students in a given school have the opportunity to learn a foreign language.

Note: If a school requests a FLES program, it can be phased in over 6 years starting with 1st grade. A teacher can be shared between two schools in the first two years of implementation.

Options: Maintain current status of foreign language instruction.

Staff Contact: Kevin L. North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

SUBJECT: Award of Contract—Vision Benefit Plan Administration

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board approve the award of contract for providing vision benefit plan administration for FCPS employees who elect a FCPS health plan to Spectera, Inc. effective January 1, 2007, and through the duration of the three-year contract in the amount of $53,200 annually, as recommended by the Office of Benefit Services and the Selection Advisory Committee (SAC).

EXPLANATION:
    Current vision services are limited to medical needs coverage and discounts for vision testing and corrective materials. Staff is recommending the addition of an enhanced vision benefit for both employees and retirees to help defray out of pocket costs associated with routine eye exams, frames, lenses or contacts lenses. This enhanced vision benefit would be ‘bundled’ with medical coverage, therefore employees and retirees who elect medical coverage would automatically receive the improved vision benefit.
    The SAC received proposals from three vendors. The responsive vendors were Davis Vision, Spectera, Inc. and Vision Service Plan, Inc. All proposals were evaluated based on the following defined criteria:

Organizational Capabilities and General Information
Account Management
Vision Benefits and Services
Customer Service
Eligibility and Administration
Claims Administration
Provider Network and Administration
Quality Assurance
Data Management and Support
Communications
Reports
Costs
    Based on the above criteria, Spectera, Inc. is the highest-rated offeror. Each vendor was asked to provide a proposal for a new plan with industry standard vision benefits.
