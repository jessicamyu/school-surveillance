Established in 1974, National Volunteer Week has grown exponentially in scope each year since, drawing the support and endorsement of all subsequent U.S. presidents, governors, mayors and other respected elected officials.

 

National Volunteer Week is about inspiring, recognizing, and encouraging people to seek out imaginative ways to engage in their communities. It’s about demonstrating to the nation that by working together, in unison, we have the fortitude to meet our challenges and accomplish our goals. National Volunteer Week is about taking action, encouraging individuals and their respective communities to be at the center of social change—discovering and actively demonstrating their collective power to foster positive transformation.

3.04 Month of the Military Child
Meeting
Apr 4, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.

April is the Month of the Military Child. It is a time to recognize our military and veteran-connected students for their service and sacrifice. Military kids sacrifice and serve our country in their own way. Here in Fairfax County, we are extremely fortunate to have over 11,000 students who are part of a military family as well as part of our Fairfax County Public Schools family.

 

Our military connected students bring a strong sense of community and a wealth of life experiences to our schools. They are resourceful and adaptable while living with numerous challenges presented by frequent moves, parental deployments, and a host of life transitions that often include reintegration and dealing with profoundly changed parents. The following are just a few of the challenges military children face: moving and starting over on an average of every 1-3 years, attending 6-9 different schools from kindergarten through 12th grade, and multiple deployments of a parent. Throughout all of this, military children are resilient and patriotic. They know first-hand what it feels like to be the new kid, so they are usually the first one to welcome a new student to school.

 

Celebrating the Month of the Military Child provides a powerful opportunity to raise awareness and build support to help these children meet the unique challenges they face! It is an opportunity to recognize and thank military children and youth for their heroism, character, courage, sacrifices and continued resilience.

National Library Week and School Library Month - April 2019

 

 

National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association and libraries across the country each April. It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate. National Library week is April 7-13 and the theme is "Libraries = Strong Communities: Everyone Belongs at Your School Library.”

 

School Library Month is the American Association of School Librarians' celebration of school librarians and their programs. Strong school library programs play in an essential role in a student's educational career, and FCPS school librarians are dedicated to meeting the needs of all students through literacy development and relevant inquiry-based instruction that fosters curiosity, innovation, and life-long learning while strengthening students’ growth in Portrait of a Graduate skills.

 

The School Board welcomes this opportunity to commend Fairfax County Public Schools’ librarians and to express its gratitude for the energy, knowledge, and vision that they tirelessly and unselfishly provide to the school system.

Resolution Naming the Library at Bull Run Elementary School the "Sue Nieto" Library

 

WHEREAS, Susan Nieto started working at Bull Run Elementary School on November 12, 1999, the year Bull Run Elementary opened.  Being that she was the first Library Assistant, she made it a priority to know everyone personally; and

 

WHEREAS, Sue played a vital role in fostering a love of reading with all students.  She had an outstanding rapport with students and her caring demeanor made each child feel welcomed and confident knowing that she was there to assist them in the library.  She always went the extra mile to make sure each person had a positive experience when visiting; and

 

WHEREAS,  Sue was essential in the development of Camp Read-A-Lot, a fun way for staff and parents to engage and collaborate with students by decorating the Library as a camp site and reading to students during their scheduled library time.  This program became an anticipated yearly event at our school as Sue recruited readers from staff to a variety of community members; and

 

WHEREAS, Sue was instrumental in the organization of tasks for our business partner, the Sully Seniors, a group of senior citizens who regularly visit our school to assist teachers, staff and the office with certain tasks supporting the education of students; and

 

WHEREAS, Sue worked tirelessly making the Fall and Spring Book Fairs a success by donating personal time at the end of her work day.  Students were excited for their parents to come and see all the wonderful books on display knowing that Sue had a wealth of knowledge of the new books being offered; and

 

WHEREAS, Sue was always involved with our Volunteer Luncheon, making it a memorable event for the numerous volunteers that frequent our school.  When volunteers would come, they were always greeted with her smile.  She was also a very conscientious person and maintained the highest level of confidentiality and integrity - She was Bull Run's finest;

 

NOW THEREFORE, BET IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board names the Library at Bull Run Elementary School the "Sue Nieto Library" to honor the kind and vibrant spirit in which Sue served our school and community for 18 years! I so move.

Subject:  Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2019-2020

 

 

Staff Contact:  Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services (DSS)

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Lea Skurpski, director; Operations and Strategic Planning, DSS

           Jacqueline Prentice, coordinator, Financial Management, DSS

 

 

Meeting Category:  April 4, 2019 - Regular Meeting

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report for the 2019-2020 school year, as required by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent's Memo #336-18  dated December 7, 2018.  This report will be used by VDOE in preparing the state plan for special education.  As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 10, 2019.

 

The 2019-2020 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $36,241,914.  This amount is equal to the 2018–2019 award as stated in Superintendent's Memo #193-18, dated July 27, 2018.  Estimated expenditures include costs associated with the voluntary 15 percent set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS); a mandatory proportionate set-aside, which ensures that FCPS spends a proportionate share of funds on special education services for parentally-placed private school children with disabilities; materials/supplies; and state restricted indirect costs of 1.3 percent. The IDEA Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 381.6 FTE positions.

 

The 2019-2020 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $807,316.  This amount is equal to the 2018-2019 award as stated in Superintendent's Memo #193-18, dated July 27, 2018.  These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students.  Estimated expenditures include costs associated with a mandatory proportionate set-aside and state restricted indirect costs of 1.3 percent. IDEA Section 619 (Preschool) funds 6.0 FTE positions.

 

The revenue amounts above reflect no change from the 2018-2019 awards.  The 2019-2020 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2018-2019 application.  Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, materials/supplies, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.  

The 2019-2020 Annual Plan was presented to the Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) at the March 13, 2019 meeting.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the 2019-2020 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool grant funding application, as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category: April 4, 2019 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 - Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

On January 24, 2019, the School Board adopted the FY 2020-2024 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the referenced five-year period. Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period. Voter authorization in November 2019 will be necessary in order to issue school bonds to provide:

 

Construction funds for:

  • 1 new elementary school

  • 1 modular building relocation

  • 3 high school additions

  • 5 elementary school renovations

  • 2 middle school renovations

 

Planning funds for:

  • 1 new elementary school

  • 5 elementary school renovations

 

To protect the school division’s capital investment in school facilities and to meet the School Board’s obligations, the adopted FY2020-2024 CIP identified requirements for renovation of certain existing school facilities. It is the School Board’s commitment that renovations should be programmed to accommodate a 20-25 year cycle instead of our current renovation cycle of 37 years. Also, the School Board has announced its commitment that all schools contain facilities necessary to support the entirety of current education programing.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the attached resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and to take all necessary and appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 2019 general election, a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $360,000,000.

Subject:  Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2019-2020

 

 

Staff Contact:  Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services (DSS)

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Lea Skurpski, director; Operations and Strategic Planning, DSS

           Jacqueline Prentice, coordinator, Financial Management, DSS

 

 

Meeting Category:  April 4, 2019 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 1: Student Success

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report for the 2019-2020 school year, as required by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent's Memo #336-18  dated December 7, 2018.  This report will be used by VDOE in preparing the state plan for special education.  As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 10, 2019.

 

The 2019-2020 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $36,241,914.  This amount is equal to the 2018–2019 award as stated in Superintendent's Memo #193-18, dated July 27, 2018.  Estimated expenditures include costs associated with the voluntary 15 percent set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS); a mandatory proportionate set-aside, which ensures that FCPS spends a proportionate share of funds on special education services for parentally-placed private school children with disabilities; materials/supplies; and state restricted indirect costs of 1.3 percent. The IDEA Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 381.6 FTE positions.

 

The 2019-2020 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $807,316.  This amount is equal to the 2018-2019 award as stated in Superintendent's Memo #193-18, dated July 27, 2018.  These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students.  Estimated expenditures include costs associated with a mandatory proportionate set-aside and state restricted indirect costs of 1.3 percent. IDEA Section 619 (Preschool) funds 6.0 FTE positions.

 

The revenue amounts above reflect no change from the 2018-2019 awards.  The 2019-2020 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2018-2019 application.  Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, materials/supplies, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.  

The 2019-2020 Annual Plan was presented to the Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) at the March 13, 2019 meeting.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the 2019-2020 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool grant funding application, as detailed in the agenda item.
