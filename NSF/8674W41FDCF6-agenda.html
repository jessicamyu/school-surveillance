124.07 Discussion - Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report 2010
Meeting
May 26, 2010 - Work Session No. 124
Category
124. Instruction and Special Services - 7 p.m.
Type
Discussion

Staff Contact: Kim Paddison Dockery, assistant superintendent, Special Services

Meeting Category: May 26, 2010 - Work Session No. 124

Subject: Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report 2010

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Related To: Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics; Legal Requirement (Individuals with Disabilities Act – IDEA)

Background:
    Regulations Governing Special Education Programs for Children with Disabilities in Virginia require that a local advisory committee for special education, appointed by each local school board, advise the School Board through the division superintendent of unmet needs in the education of children with disabilities. The regulations state that the composition of the committee shall include parents of children with disabilities and person with disabilities. Approval by ACSD is required according to Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board.

    Key Points:

    The charge from the School Board asked the committee to identify and recommend the best practices for teaching and assessing essential life skills for students with disabilities as defined in Student Achievement Goal 2. Essential Life Skills. Two of three ad-hoc committees specifically addressed this charge. The ACSD convened one other ad-hoc committee that addressed the evaluation of the special services reorganization. Recommendations from the committees addressing the School Board charge are below. Recommendations from the other ad-hoc committee is contained within the executive summary.

    Annual Charge Subcommittee
      1. FCPS provide (to the committee) suspension/expulsion statistics for schools with and without PBS. This data would be disaggregated each year by General Education and Special Education populations to allow suspension/expulsion reporting and trend analysis.
      2. FCPS measure the difference in suspensions/expulsions at High School due to students from “feeder” middle schools that don’t SW-PBS.
      3. FCPS offer PBS through dedicated curriculum time in Health and Physical Education (PE) classes in secondary schools.
      4. FCPS assign extra points for PBS and conflict resolution training and experience at staff candidate interviews.
      5. FCPS support the maintenance of a “conflict resolution cadre” at each school. Next year FCPS should report how many staff are trained at each school/center; assistant principals are key to adoption, and should be included in the reporting.

    Extended Goal 2 Subcommittee
      1. FCPS institute a deadline for case managers or ETR personnel to facilitate an intake with Department of Rehabilitative Services (DRS) for all students receiving:
          · Level II special education services (more than 50% of their day spent in a special education setting only) no later than when the student reaches 16 years old.
          · Level I special education services (less than 50% of their day spent in special education setting only) in their junior year of high school.
      2. FCPS partner with DRS to investigate and provide more appropriate life/vocational/leisure skills training for eligible students during their school day or during Extended School Year (ESY).
      3. FCPS facilitate life / social / leisure skills training for students with disabilities in Middle School by:
          · Assessing life skills, social skills and leisure skills for all levels of students receiving special education upon entry into middle school
          · Offering some form of personal development class for all levels of students,
          · Making a Partners Club or a Social Skills Club available to middle school students
      4. FCPS administer comprehensive vocational assessments at the end of 9th grade in order to appropriately incorporate recommended goals into their IEPs, thus allowing the student sufficient time to learn the skills.
      5. FCPS expand their Work and Transition (WAT) classes by:
    including them in every middle and high school site, creating WAT curriculum that will
    appropriately service all levels of students.
    6. Davis and Pulley Centers expand their course/job offerings to include more variety for all levels of students.
    7. FCPS partner with local colleges to expand their real-life readiness courses across all levels of functioning.
    8. FCPS explore with parents and students the idea of a 3rd year of middle school and/or a 5th year or more of high school for all levels of students receiving special education at IEP meetings.

    Recommendation: That the School Board accept the Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities Annual Report 2010.
