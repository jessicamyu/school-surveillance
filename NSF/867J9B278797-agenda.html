              Meeting

              Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer
              SUBJECT: Memorandum of Understanding

              RECOMMENDATION:
                  That the School Board approve and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute a Memorandum of Understanding between the School Board and the Town of Vienna to confirm the longstanding understandings between the parties regarding the use, maintenance, supervision, and management of Waters and Caffi Fields located adjacent to Vienna Elementary School.
              EXPLANATION:
                  The School Board owns the approximately 6.74-acre property in the Town of Vienna on which Waters and Caffi Fields are located. The subject property is adjacent to Vienna Elementary School. For many years, this property has been operated, maintained and supervised as a recreational facility by the Town of Vienna.

                  Over the years, Waters and Caffi Fields have become an integral community focal point for Vienna. The Town has made significant improvements to the fields and has maintained them, at the Town’s expense and with contributions from various user groups. The Town has maintained insurance coverage for use of the fields and has assumed all responsibility for the operation, maintenance and supervision of the facilities. Vienna Elementary School students do use these fields, as do students from surrounding schools, such as James Madison High School.

                  The creation of the proposed Memorandum of Understanding was initiated by the Town of Vienna and will serve to memorialize the longstanding understandings and agreements between the Town and the School Board. Staff believes this action to be in the best interest of the School Board.

              6.01 LEP Resolution - Recommendation to adopt a resolution regarding appropriate reading assessment for Limited English Proficient students (SB; action scheduled 1/25/07)
              Meeting
              Jan 11, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 11
              Category
              6. New Business
              Type
              Information


              CONTACT: Phil Niedzielski-Eichner, Providence District
              LEP Testing Resolution

              Whereas, on December 11, 2006, Virginia’s Superintendent of Public Instruction, a member of the Virginia State Board of Education, selected Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) administrators, and representatives from selected Virginia school divisions met with U.S. Education Department (USED) officials to advocate for fair and appropriate reading assessment for limited English proficient (LEP) students; and

              Whereas, the group requested the following decisions from USED officials:
                (1) to allow Virginia’s continued use of the Stanford English Language Proficiency (SELP) test as a proxy for the grade level Standards of Learning (SOL) reading tests for Level 1 and 2 students in grades 3 through 8 for one additional year (2006-07), and
                (2) to extend the exemption from the SOL reading tests (grades 3 through 8) for LEP students who have been in U.S. schools for fewer than 12 months to include the LEP students who are attending our schools and have been in the U.S. schools for fewer than 24 months; and

              Whereas, the Fairfax County Public Schools supports strongly the requests of the Virginia Superintendent of Public Instruction, it also wishes to prepare for the possibility that his requests will be denied; and

              Whereas, Fairfax County Public Schools holds high expectations and standards for all students and is committed to measuring our progress in relation to these high expectations and standards; and

              Whereas, Fairfax County Public Schools believes, however, that LEP students must be assessed in fair, valid, reliable, and appropriate ways for their current level of English proficiency;

              Now, therefore, be it resolved, that Fairfax County Public Schools will continue to work with other Virginia school divisions to advocate collectively for fair, valid, reliable, and appropriate assessment for all LEP students; and

              Therefore, be it further resolved, that all Level 1 and 2 LEP students in Fairfax County Public Schools will have their English language proficiency assessed in an educationally appropriate manner to determine their progress toward such proficiency; and

              Therefore, be it further resolved, that Fairfax County Public Schools will continue to measure and publicly report on LEP student progress toward English Language Proficiency, as well as student and school performance in relation to the Virginia Accreditation Standards and the School Board’s own adopted Student Achievement Goals.

              Staff Contacts: Maribeth Luftglass, Chief Information Officer, Information Technology
              Ted Davis, Director, Enterprise Information Systems, Information Technology

              SUBJECT: Award of Contract

              RECOMMENDATION:
                  That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute a contract in the amount of $1.1 million over a five-year term for the provision of an Identity Management Software Enterprise Environment system for FCPS with 1.) TriVir LLC for implementation, training and consulting services; 2.) Advanced Logic Industries, Inc., (ALI) for the provision of associated software licenses and related services; and 3.) Novell, Inc., for the provision of associated software licenses and services, as recommended by the Selection Advisory Committee.

              EXPLANATION:
                  The FCPS Office of Procurement Services issued a Request for Proposal RFP06-855021-69, soliciting offers from qualified sources to provide a Commercial Off the Shelf (COT) Identity Management Software Environment solution for FCPS DIT staff to integrate enterprise directories, provide reduced web-sign-on, audit security privileges, and automate user account lifecycle workflow. With many enterprise applications used by thousands of individuals to manage information across the school division, the solution will establish scalable and secure procedures to manage user identities. The Identity Management Software Environment will provide for: centralized provisioning and password management, centralized logging and auditing, consolidated sign-on, and application access management.

                  The Identity Management Software Environment RFP was publicly advertised to over 1,100 vendors, with six (6) firms submitting proposals. A Selection Advisory Committee (SAC), comprised of the DIT Enterprise Information Services Director and other senior administrators from FCPS DIT and Human Resources, evaluated the proposals in accordance with the criteria and procedures established in the RFP. Based upon the proposals that were reviewed, oral presentations with the four top candidates were conducted. Final proof of concepts were also conducted and the SAC recommended award to the TriVir Team, consisting of TriVir LLC, ALI and Novell, Inc.

              Funding will be provided by the FCPS technology plan.

              6.03 Award of Contract - Recommendation to award a contract to The Aspen Group International, Inc., to provide continuing consultation and support for the School Board and the staff on the implementation of strategic governance; that the contract, in the amount of $60,000, be effective February 1, 2007, and be authorized for 12 months through January 31, 2008 (SB; action scheduled 1/25/07)
              Meeting
              Jan 11, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 11
              Category
              6. New Business
              Type
              Information

              Staff Contract: Brad Center, Lee District Member and Chairman, Strategic Governance Committee


              RECOMMENDATION:
                  That the School Board award a contract to The Aspen Group International, Inc., to provide continuing consultation and support for the School Board and the staff on the implementation of strategic governance; that the contract, in the amount of $60,000, be effective
                  February 1, 2007, and be authorized for 12 months through January 31, 2008.


              EXPLANATION:
                  Over the past year and a half, the School Board has worked extensively with The Aspen Group in launching a strategic governance initiative for Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) which includes new beliefs, vision and mission statements and student achievement goals to provide a more concentrated focus on student achievement and to establish clearer accountability for both academic and operational performance. In December 2006, the Board committed to a new governing process when it adopted the Strategic Governance Manual. The Board-established Strategic Government Initiative begins with the establishment of student achievement goals, which include academics, essential life skills, and responsibility to the community. Expectations for operational performance for every department are also specified. Strategic Governance ensures that clarity exists between the School Board and the Superintendent’s respective roles and responsibilities for FCPS’ continued success. The Initiative also establishes a framework for demonstrating performance and establishing accountability.

                  The Strategic Governance Initiative is a complex design. Extending the contract with The Aspen Group for another 12 months will provide an opportunity for the School Board to benefit from the continued coaching and support offered by The Aspen Group as the school system transitions into the practice of strategic governance during the critical first year of implementation. The Board has invested considerable time and resources in the development of the strategic governance infrastructure, and the initial implementation phase is critical to the integration of the Board’s stated governing values into its operating culture. Funds will be transferred at the FY 2007 third-quarter budget review to cover this contract.
