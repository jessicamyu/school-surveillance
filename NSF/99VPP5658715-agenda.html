RESOLUTION

NAMING THE LIBRARY AT

PARKLAWN ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

‘THE SUSAN G. AKROYD LIBRARY’

 

 

     WHEREAS, Susan G. Akroyd has served Fairfax County Public Schools with dedication and excellence for 41 years; as the principal of Parklawn Elementary School for 27 of those 41 years, Ms. Akroyd led the students, families, and staff of Parklawn Elementary School with integrity and distinction; and

 

     WHEREAS, Ms. Akroyd understood the importance and value in educating the whole child and she endeavored to provide students with opportunities outside of the classroom, including extracurricular programs that showcased her students’ academic abilities, athletic skills, and performing arts talents; she recognized the importance of providing students with opportunities to meet their role models and arranged for Former First Lady Barbara Bush and First Lady Michelle Obama, among others, to visit Parklawn Elementary School; and

 

     WHEREAS, Ms. Akroyd has devoted her life’s work to providing service and guidance to others; she reached out to families in the Parklawn community and ensured that they were welcomed in the school and felt supported in their goals for their student’s education; she mentored numerous staff members, guiding them to recognize their potential and encouraging them to achieve success; and

 

     WHEREAS, Ms. Akroyd’s passion for reading and literacy was evident in her interactions with students and staff at Parklawn Elementary School; she would often read a book or share a poem of her own to open a staff meeting; she arranged for guest authors to read at the school and she encouraged students to submit their own works for publication; Ms. Akroyd enjoyed reading to students in the classrooms and was always in costume for Dr. Seuss’ birthday; and

 

     WHEREAS, while serving at Parklawn Elementary School and in FCPS, Ms. Akroyd demonstrated her strong work ethic, her love of literacy, and her commitment to education to thousands of children, families, and staff members every day; her love for Parklawn Elementary School was evident by the countless hours she devoted to creating a caring environment within a beautiful school; Ms. Akroyd’s commitment to learning and her determination to see her students succeed was extraordinary; 

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, in recognition of her dedicated service to the students, staff and families of the Parklawn community and her valuable contributions to Fairfax County Public Schools, hereby names the library at Parklawn Elementary School, “The Susan G. Akroyd Library” in her honor.

Recommended Action
Approve the Master Calendar Revision for the 2014-2015 school year, which modifies the approved 2014-2015 Standard School Year Calendar, as detailed in the agenda item. (S; work sessions held 5/28/14 and 6/16/14)

[Updated 6/26/14 - Revised calendar attachment]

 

Staff Contact:         Karen K. Garza, Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  June 26, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                 Master Calendar Revision 2014-2015

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:           Student Achievement Goals 1, 2 and 3

 

Key Points:

  • A uniform elementary day would increase instructional time for all elementary students and would allow for 20 minutes of daily recess for children.
  • FCPS currently does not meet the 990 hour requirement in state law due to our current early Monday, thus we had to make-up three instructional days beyond the advertised last day of school so that we met the 180 day requirement.  Having a uniform elementary school day – Monday through Friday – will prevent us from having to make-up days if we have fewer than 13 inclement weather days. 
  • A uniform elementary day also provides us with the opportunity to utilize an innovative school year calendar that builds in consistent holidays, such as a full two weeks at the Winter Break, and it provides for strategic planning days throughout the year. 
  • Our bi-annual working conditions survey has consistently revealed that our elementary teachers need more guaranteed, self-directed time for planning.  This new uniform elementary day would only be implemented with new regulations/rules that provided all elementary teachers with at least 60 minutes of self-directed time four times per week and at least 60 minutes one time per week for collaborative team planning equating to a total minimum planning time per week of 300 minutes. 
  • A uniform elementary day is responsive to our parents.  Our FCPS parents have consistently asked for the elimination of the early Mondays due to the inconvenience this causes for many of our working families.
  • This proposed change in our current elementary schedules on Mondays and to provide elementary teachers with dedicated planning time is estimated to cost a maximum of $7.6 million.  This is difficult in these tight economic times, but we believe that this is an important investment in our students and our teachers.  As such, a growing number of County Supervisors have expressed that they will provide us financial support for this important change.  They have some monies available that were not budgeted due to unanticipated revenues from law enforcement activities. 
  • In addition, there are no changes anticipated in Music, Art, or Physical Education, nor would there be changes to Regulation 4422, which limits the number of hours that librarians, and other school-based professionals, can be dedicated to providing for planning time coverage.
  •  The All County Choral Festival will continue next year and is tentatively scheduled for April 24-25, 2015. The general music teacher association and the fine arts office are discussing options should early-close Mondays be eliminated. Typically, one site rehearsal is scheduled in each of the three months prior to the concert. One option being considered is to schedule the site rehearsals on the afternoons of the February and April strategic planning days, plus one afterschool site rehearsal in March.  Once the 2014-2015 school calendar is finalized, adjustments to the rehearsal schedule will be made in order to coordinate with the approved calendar.
  • There are currently nine schools on the waiting list for FLES. We will work with the schools to see if a fall implementation would be possible. 

Implementation Plan:

  • In July, we will convene focus groups of teachers, including special education teachers, music and fine arts teachers, counselors, librarians and parents along with elementary principals to provide us input into the design of regulations and rules related to the changes in elementary schedules.
  • Create a cadre of elementary principal master schedule leaders by region to work with all other elementary principals over the summer on the design of master schedules for all elementary schools.
  • Once elementary principals have completed the design of their new schedule, we will ask that they convene a meeting of their parents prior to the start of school to inform parents of this new change in schedule and how it will benefit their children.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Master Calendar Revision for the 2014-2015 school year, which modifies the approved 2014-2015 Standard School Year Calendar, as detailed in the agenda item.

[Updated 6/26/14 - Key Points, item no. 13 language changed]

 

Staff Contact:            Kim P. Dockery, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Other Staff Present:  MaryAnn Panarelli, Director, Office of Intervention and Prevention

                                 Grady Carlson, Assistant Division Counsel

                                 Dana Scanlan, Administrative Hearing Officer

                                 Clarence Jones, Coordinator, Student Safety and Wellness Office

 

Meeting Category:     June 26, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                    Student Rights and Responsibilities, Regulation 2601.28P

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:               Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

Regulation 2601.28P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, has been reviewed and revised to better align with best practice, to reduce suspensions, and to include changes in the Code of Virginia. Major proposed revisions:

  1. Increase focus on school-based interventions
  2. Reduce number of offenses which carry mandated consequence
  3. Change “recommendation for reassignment” and “recommendation for expulsion” to “referral to the Division Superintendent”
  4. Limit suspensions for violations which are not being referred to the Division Superintendent to a maximum of five days
  5. Permit, but does not require suspension of up to ten days when there is a referral to the Division Superintendent
  6. Extend expedited review to first time use of marijuana which does not occur on school grounds during the school day, or during school sponsored activities
  7. Establish a cluster assistant superintendent (effective July 1, 2014, regional assistant superintendent) records review process to address chronic misconduct
  8. Require records review for students in K-6 before referral to Division Superintendent for chronic misconduct
  9. Add smoking devices such as electronic cigarettes and hookah pens under “tobacco products”
  10. Clarify need to have a note to leave school early
  11. Require principals to document investigation of bullying and to report all allegations of sexual harassment and discriminatory harassment to the office of Equity and Employee Relations
  12. Permit principals to take action other than referral to Division Superintendent when a student self-reports and voluntarily turns in knife or other non-statutory weapon
  13. Include description of the use, in certain circumstances, of a letter of agreement from the Division Superintendent

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.28P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.

5.04 Amendment 4 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)
Meeting
Jun 26, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 22
Category
5. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
Approve Amendment 4 to ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purpose of deleting the reference to the GASB requirement for amortization of the unfunded liabilities (HR; presented as new business 6/5/14)

Staff Contact:         Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category:  June 26, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                 Amendment 4 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:            Administrative Clarification

 

Key Points:                               

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending a technical amendment to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of deleting the reference to "GASB" in the requirement for amortization of the unfunded liability in ERFC's funding policy.  Under the new GASB Statements 67 and 68, the accounting and funding measures are disconnected and the funding policy should not reference GASB. The proposed change does not affect the benefits payable under the Plan.

 

Funding Policy - General Financial Objective and Employer Contributions:  The current Plan document contains a funding policy for the Plan which states that the objective shall be "to establish and receive contributions which will remain approximately level from generation to generation of citizens and which, when combined with other assets and investment return, will be sufficient to pay benefits when due, while providing a reasonable margin for adverse experience."  The policy further states that the Trustees and the actuary should determine reasonable assumptions regarding future experience.  The final criterion states that any unfunded accrued liabilities shall be amortized over a period not to exceed 30 years, in accordance with GASB requirements.

 

GASB has stated that "it is not within the scope of the Board's activities to set standards that establish specific methods of financing the benefits (that being a policy decision for government officials...)" Therefore, actuarial standards of practice that apply to public sector plans should govern the establishment of the amortization period for unfunded liabilities rather than GASB.  The ERFC Board of Trustees, in conjunction with its actuary, has established a remaining amortization period of 25 years in the December 31, 2013 valuation.  The Board intends to decrease the amortization period annually until the Plan is 100 percent funded.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve Amendment 4 to ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purpose of deleting the reference to the GASB requirement for amortization of the unfunded liabilities.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
