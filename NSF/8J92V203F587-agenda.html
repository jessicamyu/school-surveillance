Exceptional Children’s Week, May 8-12, 2012

 

Governor McDonnell has designated the week of May 8-12, 2012, as Exceptional Children’s Week in the Commonwealth of Virginia.  Across the Commonwealth, there are approximately 18,000 infants, toddlers, and preschoolers with identified disabilities.  Exceptional Children’s Week is intended to promote and recognize the work and the meaningful activities of early childhood special educators, related service providers, paraeducators, and families of infants and young children with disabilities.

 

School Nurse Day - May 9, 2012

School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.  This annual event recognizes the contributions that school nurses make every day to improve the health and success of our nation’s children.  This year’s theme, Advocacy, Access, Achievement: Making the Connection, reflects the positive impact that school nurses have on the health and well-being of students.

School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health and safety, intervene with actual and potential health problems, and provide case management services and are dedicated individuals who work in cooperation with families, teachers, and school administrators.

The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognized May 9 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

RESOLUTION

NAMING THE BAND ROOM AT

OAKTON HIGH SCHOOL

“THE DR. CHERYL NEWTON BAND ROOM”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Cheryl Newton has served the students of Oakton High School and the surrounding community as an innovative music educator for 28 years, from 1984 until her retirement in June 2012, and has directed Oakton High School’s marching and concert bands, which have received the Virginia Honor Band recognition for 19 of the last 20 years; and

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Newton has taken the marching band to Orkney Springs, Virginia, every August for one week to provide a unique experience, preparing the fall season show for marching band competitions and creating a nationally recognized marching band program that performs at every home football game, with cannon in tow, and that hosts the annual Oakton Classic Marching Band Competition; and

 

     WHEREAS, she designed an interdisciplinary curriculum as an adjunct to the FCPS Music Program of Studies that enriches the knowledge base of students and enhances their appreciation of music through Advanced Placement Music Theory and Guitar; she gave her students the once in a life-time experience of performing a commissioned piece of music, “The Atlantic Odyssey,” by composer Philip Sparke; and

 

     WHEREAS, she is recognized by her peers as a Bandworld Legion of Honor Laureate, and is actively involved in the music education profession by her membership in 11 professional organizations and the giving of her talents by guest conducting and holding clinics for schools statewide and nationally; and

 

     WHEREAS, through her guidance and high standards, she has taught her students not only to be good musicians, but also to be good citizens in their community; she has inspired many of her own students to become music educators;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the band room at Oakton High School “The Dr. Cheryl Newton Band Room” in appreciation of Dr. Newton’s contributions to the individuals, communities, and school division, thereby leaving a legacy of educational excellence to Fairfax County Public Schools.

The winner of the 2012 Distinguished School Resource Officer award is Master Police Officer Lou Munoz, Westfield High School.


Fairfax County Public Schools and the Fairfax County Police Department have established a mutually beneficial relationship for more than a decade. One of the hallmarks of this relationship has been the School Resource Officer (SRO) program. SROs help ensure a safe and peaceful learning environment for students, and provide school administrators and staff with the law enforcement resources and expertise necessary to maintain order, prevent truancy, and enforce the rules and codes of student conduct.

A committee was created to establish and select the Distinguished School Resource Officer Award to annually recognize the efforts of outstanding SROs.  The 2012 selection committee members were Fred Ellis, Director of the Office of Safety and Security, Dan Parris, Cluster II Assistant Superintendent, and Lieutenant Ken Baine, School Liaison Commander.

There were a total of seven School Resource Officers nominated by their principals. These nominees are stellar examples of the outstanding officers working in our schools. They should be very proud of their nomination.

Staff Contact:           Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

              

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     May 10, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 19

 

Subject:                       FY 2013 Approved Budget    

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                                                         

 

Key Points:

 

On February 9, 2012, the School Board adopted the FY 2013 Advertised Budget.  The County Board of Supervisors subsequently adopted their FY 2013 budget on May 1, 2012, and included funding that was $63.3 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget. The state budget is expected to be finalized on May 14, 2012.  Based on the budget passed by the General Assembly during the 2012 special session and submitted to the Governor, FCPS projects increased state revenue of $44.0 million as compared to the advertised budget. 

 

Based on the total available revenue for FY 2013, and in response to the state adopted mandate regarding employee contributions to the Virginia Retirement System, the following adjustments to the FY 2013 Advertised Budget are recommended:

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue reflects a net reduction of $14.3 million as compared to the advertised budget.  Funding from the county is $63.3 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by an increase is state revenue of $44.0 million based on the projected state budget and an estimated increase of $5.0 million in revenue from Fairfax City.

 

Expenditures:

Required expenditure adjustments total $22.4 million compared to the advertised budget and include the following: $8.1 million as a result of retirement rate increases above the increase projected in the advertised budget; $6.7 million to implement the first year of a mandatory shift in the employee contribution for the Virginia Retirement System, which is required to be offset by a salary increase; $4.9 million for life insurance rate increases; $2.3 million for membership and demographic adjustments based on the updated spring enrollment projections; $0.5 million in support of high school band programs; and a decrease of $0.1 million for world languages based on the enrollment at the schools selected to implement the Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools program in FY 2013.  These required adjustments will be offset by reductions of $36.7 million that the School Board will identify prior to the budget being adopted on May 24, 2012.

 

To meet the required level of expenditure reductions, the Superintendent has identified reductions totaling $36.7 million which are detailed on pages 4-7 of the agenda item.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2013 Approved Budget, as detailed in the FY 2013 Advertised Budget and modified with the adjustments detailed in the agenda item.

No synthetic turf fields will be installed on FCPS properties under the auspices of these agreements without first being brought to the School Board for specific project approval. No operating or bond funds will be used on any of these projects.

 

A Memorandum of Agreement between FCSB and the Contributing Parties will be negotiated to memorialize the rights and responsibilities of the parties to the Agreement.  The Memorandum of Agreement for the synthetic turf field at McLean High School was approved by the School Board on July 28, 2011.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board enter into Agreements for the Synthetic Turf Fields at Centreville High, Langley High and Robinson Secondary Schools with the Contributing Parties memorializing the rights and responsibilities of the parties to the Agreements  and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Chief Operating Officer, to execute and administer the Agreements on behalf of the School Board.
