Subject: 2020-2021 Advisory Committee Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report

 

Staff Contact: Dr. Michelle Boyd, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Other Staff Present: Michael Bloom, Director, Special Education Instruction

                                 Jane Strong, Director, Office of Special Education Procedural Support

                                    Connie Lorentzen, Chair, ACSD

 

Meeting Category: School Board Work Session (May 25, 2021)

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points: The report is to review ACSD recommendations related to the following School Board Annual charge.

 

ACSD School Board Charge:

 

Committee Charge for 2020-2021

  • Examine current FCPS practices regarding school-based compliance with special education procedures and practices.
  • Make recommendations designed to improve the utilization of Department of Special Services (DSS) resources to increase divisionwide consistency related to the implementation of special education procedures and practices.
  • Make recommendations designed to improve school-based administrative oversight of special education instructional programming and procedural practices.

ACSD Subcommittees: Family Engagement and Outreach, Student Achievement and Outcomes, Policy and Regulations, and School Board Charge.

 

Highlighted Commendations and Subcommittee Recommendations:

 

Commendations:

  • New positions, neuro-diversity specialist, and a social-emotional learning specialist.
  • DSS support for a successful ban on seclusion and reduction of restraint.
  • Providing evidence-based programs that reduce the need for restraint and seclusion and provide safety tools for the de-escalation process; Ross Greene’s collaborative and proactive solutions (CPS) Model, Grafton’s Ukeru Systems .
  • Continue to provide livestream and recorded ACSD subcommittee and regular meetings, if the ACSD meets in-person.
  • Continue to ensure video, audio and written public testimony submissions remain available.
  • ACSD must continue to provide periodic reports with recommendations on current School Board action items relevant to the education of students with disabilities.
  • DSS staff liaison and administrative support for the Committee.
  • PRC and their research evidence-based webinars and YouTube videos.

Recommendations:

  • Create a policy to require the translation of all eligibility, testing and IEP documents provided by FCPS, to all parents whose native language is not English; in order to promote engagement and ensure equity to all students with disabilities and their families.
  • Install video cameras in the remaining seclusion rooms and hallway leading to the room.
  • Provide electronic reporting of restraint and seclusion.
  • Ensure all students are provided evidence-based sequential literacy curriculum.
  • Require training on evidence-based dyslexia-specific interventions, including regional evidence-based training for all K-3 teachers.
  • Ensure parents are consistently made aware of the dyslexia specific interventions available for their children, their published metrics, and the progress of their children on these programs in quarterly progress reports.
  • Revise the high incidence specialized literacy instruction matrix to reflect MTSS.
  • Provide required job-embedded professional development, for all general education teachers’ K-3 and literacy intervention teachers.
  • Ensure all programs are implemented with fidelity; to achieve results as directed by publishers.
  • Build supervisory tools into dyslexia interventions and implement them consistently across FCPS.
  • Ensure that FCPS offers competitive wages and benefits to recruit and retain highly trained special education staff.
  • Create a forum for staff to discuss challenges in delivering special education services and supports, and to increase awareness of evidence-based practices pertaining to students with disabilities.
  • Create an internal channel to communicate with staff and programs that do not have county-wide recognition but primarily address the needs of students with disabilities.
  • Ensure IEP teams provide timely training, at the local school, to meet the projected transition needs of students from year to year.
  • Provide required training on IEP development to school SPED chairs and lead teachers.
  • Identify and take steps necessary to increase staff awareness and use of the assistant ombudsman for special education.
  • Utilize the Office of the Auditor General (OAG) to identify issues associated with disparities in outcomes for students with disabilities related to inconsistent special education practice.
  • Implement operational changes that allow DSS to proactively identify building-level administrators and employees in need of additional training, coaching, and other supports.
  • Ensure a climate that supports school-based teams and administrators requesting or receiving assistance from DSS feel no fear of reprisals of any kind.

Summary/Background:

The Human Resources Advisory Committee reports to the School Board on its activities for the school year 2020-21.  The annual report focuses on the recommendations from HRAC in response to its annual charge from the School Board.
