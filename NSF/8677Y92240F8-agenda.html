National Hispanic Heritage Month - September 15-October 15, 2009

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-American community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

5.02 School Board Policy - Recommendation to adopt Policy 2611, Procedures for Hearings and Appeals to School Board, as detailed in the agenda item (SB; action scheduled 9/24/09)

Sep 10, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 3
Category
5. New Business - 9:35 p.m.

Staff Contact: Pam Goddard, Executive Assistant and Clerk of the School Board

Meeting Category: September 10, 2009 – Regular Meeting No. 3

Subject: School Board Policy

School Board Action Required: Information

Related: Regulation 2601, Student Responsibilities and Rights (SR&R)

Background:

Policy 2611 outlines the process for appealing certain student disciplinary decisions to the School Board. In October 2008, the Board revised the policy to provide parents the opportunity to appeal a hearing officer’s decision to the School Board to review on the written record those cases which the hearing officer had not forwarded the school’s expulsion recommendation. The revised policy also resulted in school assignment appeals being consistent with the appeal process for long-term suspensions and reassignments as a result of charges for crimes in the community.
    Key Points:

    Staff is recommending the addition of language which clarifies the policy concerning consideration of appeals to the School Board. The additional language states expressly that the School Board will not consider appeals that challenge peripheral matters such as “other disciplinary consequences.” The recommended clarifying language will confirm the longstanding practice of the Board concerning student disciplinary appeals, which is to review the disciplinary sanction or the disciplinary decision of the assignment to another school or alternative program, or both.
      Recommendation:

      That the School Board adopt Policy 2611.9, Procedures for Hearings and Appeals to the School Board, as detailed in the agenda item.
