The Board will now make a motion to go into closed meeting (1) to discuss and consider disciplinary matters concerning students pursuant to Section 2.2-3711(A)(2) of the Code of Virginia; (2) to protect the privacy of individuals in personal matters not related to public business pursuant to Section 2.2-3711(A)(4) of the Code of Virginia;(3) to discuss and consider the performance and employment of specific public employees pursuant to Section 2.2-3711(A)(1) of the Code of Virginia; and (4) to discuss the  acquisition of real property for a public purpose in connection with an elementary school site and/or disposition of publicly held real property, where discussion in an open meeting would adversely affect the bargaining position or negotiating strategy of the School Board in accordance with Section 2.2-3711(A)(3) of the Code of Virginia.

Recommended Action
Certify closed meeting compliance

In order to comply with Section 2.2-3712 (D) of the Code of Virginia, it is necessary for the Board to certify that since the Fairfax County School Board convened a closed meeting on March 26, 2015, to the best of each member’s knowledge, only public business matters lawfully exempted from open meeting requirements and only such public business matters as were identified in the motion convening the closed meeting were heard, discussed, or considered by the Board during the closed meeting.
