National Volunteer Week - April 15-21, 2007

National Volunteer Week, which takes place April 15-21, 2007, began in 1974 when President Nixon signed an executive order establishing the week as an annual celebration of volunteering. Since then, every U.S. President has signed a proclamation promoting National Volunteer Week.

During the 2005-2006 school year, the schools, centers, academies, and central offices reported that 91,640 volunteers donated 1,535,829 hours of service to FCPS.

Over the past 21 years, since records have been kept, a total of 1,192,449 volunteers have provided 24,464,626 hours of service to our schools. The monetary value of this contribution is close to $260 million. If our volunteers had been paid at the entry-level instructional assistant salary of $11.80 per hour, the bill for 2005-2006 alone would have exceeded $18 million. The educational value of the work of our volunteers is, of course, incalculable.

On behalf of the School Board, I am pleased and honored to express the deep appreciation for the many and varied contributions made to FCPS by our citizens through volunteer service. We are greatly indebted to them for their valued assistance.

NAMING THE CAFETERIA AT

FOR THE ARTS AND SCIENCES
“THE CLARA JOHNSON CAFE”

WHEREAS, Clara Johnson has served Hunters Woods Elementary School as building supervisor and custodian for 28 years, earning numerous custodial accolades, preparing the building for summer programs and the start of school, and maintaining a safe environment for students and staff members; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Johnson has interacted positively and patiently with all students, staff members, and parents, using the proper blend of friendliness and authority, rewarding and acknowledging proper cafeteria behavior, and being constantly aware of and responsive to students’ needs in the school and cafeteria, particularly, special needs students; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Johnson has served as a model individual, possessing a strong work ethic and practicing quiet and dignified leadership, and she has launched the supervisory careers of several custodians; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Johnson has willingly volunteered to help before anyone requested her assistance, provided suggestions to help make all school activities flow more smoothly, and helped to instill a sense of respect and responsibility in all students through her daily presence in the cafeteria during breakfast and lunch;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the School Board of Fairfax County, Virginia, honors Clara Johnson by naming the Hunters Woods Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences’ cafeteria “The Clara Johnson Cafe" in recognition of her service to the entire Hunters Woods community through the caring way she maintained the school and served the students during her time as building supervisor at Hunters Woods Elementary School.

6.03 Proposed 2007 School Bond Referendum - Recommendation to adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 6, 2007, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $365,200,000 (FTS; work session scheduled 4/23/07; public hearing and action scheduled 4/26/07)
Meeting
Apr 12, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 17
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services
SUBJECT: Proposed 2007 School Bond Referendum


RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 6, 2007, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $365,200,000.


EXPLANATION:
        On January 25, 2007, the School Board adopted the FY2008-2012 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the coming five-year period. Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period.

        Voter authorization to issue bonds is needed in November 2007 to provide construction funds for two new elementary schools, a permanent addition to one elementary school, and modular additions to four existing schools. Funds are also included to support program enhancements such as Full-day Kindergarten and High Schools of the Future. Also included are funds for an addition to the Pimmit Alternative High School and planning funds for a West County Adult Education facility.

        The recommended 2007 referendum would provide funds to renovate older general education facilities, including six elementary schools and one middle school. The proposed referendum also includes funds to plan the renovation of two elementary schools and two high schools. These planning funds will prepare the school system to accelerate the renovations at such time as construction funds become available.

        The recent unprecedented inflation in construction costs has made it necessary to seek additional bond authorization for 15 construction projects approved in previous school bond referenda. The recommended 2007 referendum includes funds to offset the inflation adjustment for these projects.

        The proposed referendum also includes funds to continue upgrades of technology networks in our school facilities. Additional allowances have been made in the referendum for the following: roof and HVAC replacement; Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) improvements; and asphalt repaving. Funds supporting a cooperative effort between Fairfax County Public Schools and the Fairfax County government to provide a school bus service facility are included in the proposed Bond Referendum. It should be noted that the debt service for this facility will be paid by the county government and will not impact the $155 million annual spending cap for school capital improvements. Finally, the proposed package includes funds for bonding expenses and the cost of directing the design and construction of projects authorized through the referendum.

        The scale and content of this referendum have been significantly influenced by two considerations: the requirement to address overcrowding, as well as, renovation and infrastructure needs countywide; and current limits on the county’s ability to cash-flow debt.

        To facilitate the orderly financing of identified capital improvement needs, it is recommended that the Proposed 2007 School Bond Referendum be offered for voter approval in the November 6, 2007, general election. It is believed that this proposal is a well-balanced presentation of needs countywide. A School Board work session on this proposal will be held on April 23, 2007, a public hearing and School Board action is scheduled for April 26, 2007.

Meeting
Apr 12, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 17
Category

Staff Contact: Michael A. Molloy, Director of Government Relations

SUBJECT: Legislative Recommendations for 2008

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board accept the legislative recommendations to be forwarded to the VSBA Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee for their consideration, as detailed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:
      Each spring the VSBA Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee seeks recommendations from local school boards concerning new or revised legislative policies and resolutions to be considered for VSBA approval. The Committee reviews the local school boards’ recommendations along with other suggestions for changes to the existing policies and resolutions. The VSBA takes action on the Committee’s report at the annual fall convention. Staff recently learned that position recommendations are due to VSBA May 7, 2007.

      This process allows the School Board to make recommendations for the VSBA legislative program and to receive statewide support for some of its own legislative positions. The Fairfax County School Board 2008 Legislative Program will be developed for adoption in the fall.

      Changes are proposed to positions regarding the Federal No Child Left Behind Act, Standards of Quality; Students with Disabilities-Cooperative Services; and the Composite Index.

      The recommended changes are all directly based on policy positions the School Board has already adopted in its 2007 Legislative Program.
