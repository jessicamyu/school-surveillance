Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, director, Office of Government Relations

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

 

Meeting Category: November 12, 2018 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement:

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly affected by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing in particular the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The School Board annually adopt a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.

The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions and (where applicable) formatting changes from the Board’s 2018 legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect current approaches to policy debates and issues, and to clarify, simplify and streamline existing position language where appropriate.

 

Proposed staff recommended changes to the FCSB 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will be presented at the Board’s November 8, 2018 regular meeting. Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 19, 2018.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board accept staff recommendations for the 2019 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

Other Staff Present:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Resources

 Helen Nixon, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services

 Lidi Hruda, director, Office of Research and Strategic Improvement

 

Meeting Category: November 12, 2018 – Work Session

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan 2018-2020

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, a variety of stakeholders (School Board, Leadership Team, principals, teachers, students, and the community) provided input to prioritize the outcomes in each of the four goal areas.

 

The School Board endorsed the eight prioritized areas of focus for school years 2018-2020 in their July 23, 2018, work session. Refinements have been made based on the discussion in that work session. Aspirational goals – which show what success in each goal area would look like for students, families, employees, and the community – have been clearly defined. Baselines – which show FCPS’ recent performance – have been used to set performance targets that will be used to hold the Division accountable for short-term progress.

 

The Board will have an opportunity to review the elements that reflect success in the strategic plan Sys 2018-2020 focus: (1) what FCPS aspires to accomplish and (2) how we will measure success.

 

Recommendation:

The School Board accept the recommended changes to the strategic plan.

Subject:  Supporting Fairfax County Public Schools Discipline Related News

 

Staff Contact:   Francisco Durán, chief equity officer

 

 

Other Staff Present:   Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                     Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Special Services

                                     Lidi Hruda, director, Office of Research and Strategic Improvement

 

 

Meeting Category:    November 12, 2018 – work session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In May 2018, the School Board directed the Superintendent and his staff to embark on a year-long review of disciplinary best practices to address suspension rates and referral rates to the Hearings Office.  Included in the request was a full analysis of suspension rates and Hearings Office referrals by ethnic group, special education status, or other factors that may indicate disproportionality in discipline.

 

During the May work session, there was a budget amendment to eliminate one Assistant Hearings Office Position totaling $0.1 million and to allocate these funds in a placeholder (totaling $0.1 million). The Board directed the Superintendent to report at a fall work session his findings and recommendations on how to best use the $0.1 million set aside to support the Division’s discipline-related needs.

 

This presentation is now complete and includes preliminary information on trends in out-of-school suspensions and Hearings Office referrals, as well as disproportionality in discipline data and the Superintendent’s recommendation for the best use of the $0.1 million set aside.  All data was collected by the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement (ORSI).  A plan for a more detailed study and analysis of trends and the disproportionality data (including external review) will be presented to the School Board in January 2019.  A final report will be presented to the School Board in June 2019.

 

In addition, as requested by the School Board, this presentation includes a crosswalk of the School Resource Officer (SRO) Memorandum of Understanding and the Student Rights & Responsibilities (SR&R) document.  Recommendations for changes to the SR&R based on the crosswalk will be discussed for implementation in SY 2019-20.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board accept the Superintendent’s recommendations.
