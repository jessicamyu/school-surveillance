Youth Art Month - March 2012

 

Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.

The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

 

 

Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2012

 

The American Alliance for Theatre and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development. Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.

 

 

National Sleep Awareness Week - March 5-11, 2012


National Sleep Awareness Week, which takes place March 5-11, 2012, is an annual public education and awareness campaign to promote the importance of sleep. Sleep Awareness Week brings attention to the importance of adequate sleep for health and safety, and a reminder of sleep-related responsibilities, such as not driving while drowsy and parents ensuring that their children get the sleep they need to learn effectively. The National Sleep Foundation suggests that healthy adults need 7--9 hours of sleep per day, and school-age children might require 10--11 hours of sleep.  Let’s all remember as we set our clocks to daylight saving time this weekend - “Sleep; As Important as Diet and Exercise, Only Easier. ”

That the School Board direct the Superintendent to require high school principals to recognize at commencement exercises those graduating seniors who have enlisted in the military; the recognition should be in a manner consistent with the decorum of each individual school’s ceremony.

 

Proposed Amendments:

 

1. I move that the main motion be amended by:  (1) inserting the words “by the wearing of cords of unique color(s)” following the word “recognize; (2) inserting the words “enrolled in one of the military academies, or accepted ROTC scholarships” following the word “military”; (3) striking the word “should” and inserting the word “shall”; and (4) striking the words “with the decorum of each individual school’s ceremony” and inserting the words “across all high schools.”  The motion, as amended, would read as follows:  “That the School Board direct the Superintendent to require high school principals to recognize by the wearing of cords of unique colors at commencement exercises those graduating seniors who have enlisted in the military, enrolled in one of the military academies, or accepted ROTC scholarships; the recognition shall be in a manner consistent across all high schools.” (Ted Velkoff)

failed

Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  March 8, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 15

 

Subject:                  Sale of School Bonds

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

  

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County’s Department of Management and Budget has advised school system staff that the Board of Supervisors plans to sell Economic Development Authority Revenue Refunding Bonds in early April 2012 in order to refund a prior bond issue to achieve interest rate savings. The Board of Supervisors is scheduled to authorize the issuance and sale at its meeting on March 20, 2012.

 

The planned April 2012 bond sale is a result of a monthly review of outstanding debt performed by Fairfax County’s Financial Advisors to determine which prior bond issues are candidates for refunding. Given the low interest rate environment, this is an opportunity to provide the County significant debt service savings. The bond sale is scheduled for early April in order to take advantage of this market opportunity at the earliest possible moment consistent with necessary legal approvals and marketing efforts. 

 

Further, it was determined that significant County General Fund debt service savings could be achieved by refunding the Series 2003, Laurel Hill Public Facilities Project which included funds for the South County Secondary School Project. Taking action quickly, the County General Fund will be able to take advantage of the current market conditions and experience savings resulting from historically low interest rates. 

 

The attached documents have been provided by bond counsel.  

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the "First Amendment to the Use and Funding Agreement," and approve and adopt the resolution requesting that the Board of Supervisors issue and sell school bonds on behalf of the School Board and for the benefit of the Fairfax County Public Schools.

Staff Contact:              Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:     Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:        March 8, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 15

 

Subject:                        FY 2012 Third Quarter Budget Review

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2012 budget and to make revisions as necessary.  The FY 2012 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and recommendations that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review approved on December 1, 2011.

 

This agenda recognizes adjustments in the Grants and Self-Supporting Programs, Adult and Community Education, and School Construction funds.  Since the FY 2012 Midyear Budget Review, new and revised grant awards result in an increase of $0.4 million and 2.0 positions in the Grants and Self-Supporting Programs Fund.  In the Adult and Community Education (ACE) Fund, an adjustment of $50,000 in federal revenue results from the English Literacy/Civics Education grant. Tuition receipts, which are trending lower than projected, will continue to be monitored. In an effort to provide the most valuable instructional model and manage the increasing costs of providing adult and community education courses, the ACE program is currently reviewing operations and developing recommendations for the FY 2013 Approved Budget adoption in May.  Recommendations to address any ACE shortfall that occurs in FY 2012 will be included in the FY 2012 Final Budget Review. Finally, in the Construction Fund, an increase of $14.9 million is due to earlier timing of the Canterbury Woods Elementary School project. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure adjustments as reflected in the FY 2012 Third Quarter Budget Review and as detailed in the agenda item.
