Subject: Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee (MSAOC) Annual Report

 

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, chief academic officer,

 Nardos King, Interim chief equity officer

 

Other Staff Present:

George Becerra, co-chairs, MSAOC

Kenneth White, co-chairs, MSAOC

 

Meeting Category: June 28, 2022, Work Session    

 

Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To: Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Summary: 

MSAOC presents its Twenty-Eighth (28th) Annual Report to the School Board as required and in response to the 2021-22 School Board charge.

We greatly appreciate the Board’s actions to keep us safe on returning to in-person instruction. We support efforts to defend against legal challenges to the new TJ admissions process which has shown that the Class of 2025 has the highest GPA in recent memory, is the most representative for underrepresented groups including girls, Black and Hispanic students, low-income students, English language learners, and students with disabilities—many of whom are Asian.

The Committee recommends that the Chief Equity Officer’s position be advertised in SY 21-22 after the adoption of equity policy, funding dedicated staff reporting directly to CEO and the Superintendent reassigning unrelated offices currently under CEO

Since the Committee has been reporting on closing academic gaps, SOL data show that the achievement gap is at a standstill and most recently widen between Black, Hispanic, students with disabilities and those in schools with high concentration of EL and poor students and their classmates. We know there is a solution in that the College Success Program—an evidenced-based and hidden gem, whose participants consistently outperform their FCPS demographic group in SOL pass rates and enrollment in rigorous, honor courses and college preparation. We ask that you adopt our recommendation to greatly expand the CSP with the funds and support it needs for FCPS to close the academic gaps. We continue to see that subjective decision-making in student discipline and teacher hiring have resulted in racial/ethnic disparities. Any coherent equity training must address both cultural competency and implicit bias in decision making by schools head-on.

Finally, the pandemic has no doubt widen the achievement gap for low-income students of color, those with disabilities and needing language assistance and their more fortunate classmates. We urge FCPS to find additional ways to innovate to meet these challenges.

Recommendation: 

The committee will address the School Board Charge and present recommendations as documented in the attachments.
