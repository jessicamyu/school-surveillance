                                Teddi Predaris, director, Office of Language Acquisition and Title I, ISD

                                Paul Wardinski, director, Office of Professional and Life Skills, ISD

                                Karin Williams, director, Office of Operations and Strategic Planning, ISD

                                Mary Ann Panarelli, director, Intervention and Prevention Services, Department of Special Services

                                Sheryl Granzow, coordinator, Adult and Community Education, ISD

 

Meeting Category:     June 4, 2012 - Work Session No. 131

 

Subject:                       Adult and Community Education Advisory Committee Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:                Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points: 

 

School Board Charge: 

1.  Examine the organizational and curriculum alignment between K-12 and ACE programs, and identify opportunities for increased collaboration and synergy in supporting the Student Achievement Goals, and increasing post-secondary workforce opportunities.

 

2.  Assess current marketing in the general and workforce communities by ACE programs, with particular reference to course accessibility, Student Achievement Goals, Adult High School Completion, and Family Literacy, Adult ESOL, workforce skills and economic advancement, and explore methods to increase enrollment in ACE programs.

 

Response:  

In FY 2013, ACE will establish an organization structure that concentrates on Adult ESOL, Career and Workplace training, and highly specialized enrichment courses. The mission is to preserve a core adult education program that meets state and local requirements (Adult ESOL), and provides opportunities for better alignment with the division’s mission of career and workforce readiness while creating operational efficiencies. The ACE staff, the FCPS leadership, and the ACE Advisory Committee are to develop a five-year plan for the ACE program that is fiscally responsible and supportive of the needs of the community. 

 

Committee Observations: 

The Advisory Committee is very concerned about the reduction in the number of program specialists, from nine to four, which has the effect of placing the responsibility for several program curricula on a single person.

 

The Committee is very concerned about the impact the reorganization on ACE programs, with particular regard to Adult ESOL funding.  Adult ESOL operates under a State-mandate and receives Federal and State funds which require a local operating fund match. This program’s importance lies in the opportunities that become available to non-English-speaking persons once they are able to communicate in the workplace. The ACE Community Education and Adult ESOL programs are funded from a single account. The Committee believes that the continued viability of both programs requires an increase in the Operating Fund transfer for Adult ESOL, and a strong marketing program to attract more Community Education enrollments.

Staff Contact:          Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 4, 2012 – Work Session No. 131

 

Subject:                    Facilities Planning Advisory Council 2011-12 Annual Report to the School Board

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

On September 23, 2010, the School Board approved the formation of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) as a new advisory council of the Board.  The mission of the advisory council is to advise and inform the staff and School Board in the development of comprehensive, long term plans for facilities needs in the most effective and efficient way.  FPAC is made up of 13 appointed members – one from each Fairfax County magisterial district, one from the City of Fairfax, and three at-large members.  Facilities-related issues that may be considered by the advisory council may include, but are not limited to:

 

  • school program capacity
  • enrollment and projections
  • transportation and operating efficiencies related to facilities planning
  • Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) prioritization
  • creative financing and construction strategies
  • scope of renovations
  • school closures and new schools
  • student accommodation planning (building additions/modular relocations/ review of school boundaries)

 

FPAC has also initiated its charge from the School Board to develop a strategic plan for facilities with input from all stakeholders and expects to present the plan to the School Board in October 2012. 

 

 

Key Points:

 

Consistent with the School Board guidelines for advisory committees, FPAC submits its annual report to the School Board, including an executive summary and recommendations, list of meeting dates, attendance reports, and voting records.
