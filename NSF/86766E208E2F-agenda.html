4.01 FY 2011 Advertised Budget - Adopt the FY 2011 Advertised Budget, as detailed in the Superintendent’s FY 2011 Proposed Budget, and with the following revisions: 1) restore funding of $17.1 million to eliminate the class size increase of one student per teacher for all classes except special education; 2) restore funding of $1.4 million for assistant principal positions; 3) restore funding of $0.8 million for instructional assistant positions; 4) restore funding of $1.6 million for instructional coaches positions; and 5) recognize additional reductions in central office support to schools of $0.9 million; and thereby increase the transfer request by $20.0 million, for a total request of $77.8 million. (FS; new business 1/7/10; public hearings held 1/25/10, 1/26/10, and 1/27/10; work sessions held 1/11/10 and 1/28/10)

Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

Other Staff Present: Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

Meeting Category: February 4, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 13
Subject: FY 2011 Advertised Budget

School Board Action Required: Action

KEY POINTS:

On January 7, 2010, the Superintendent presented the FY 2011 Proposed Budget to the School Board. The School Board (SB) held a work session on January 11, 2010, to discuss the Superintendent’s recommendations, and the SB held public hearings on January 25, 26, and 27. The SB held an additional work session on January 28, 2010.

The FY 2011 Proposed Budget totals $2.3 billion and includes fee enhancements, budget reductions, and cost avoidances across the entire division totaling $108.2 million. More than 594 positions will be eliminated as a result of the projected shortfall. For the second year in a row, the budget does not include either a market scale adjustment or step increase for employees. Support departments will undergo another year of significant budget reductions. Instructional programs to be eliminated include general education summer school and Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools (FLES). Pimmit Alternative High School will be closed, with students redirected to another FCPS program or school. General education class sizes will increase 1.0 student per teacher. This will result in more multi-grade elementary school classes and fewer high school electives. New fees for athletic participation, AP/IB and PSAT tests, and increases to community use of FCPS facilities and monopole fees are also included.

Even with these deep program cuts and position reductions, the proposed budget requests additional funding of $57.8 million in order to preserve such vital programs and services as full-day kindergarten, elementary band and strings, and elementary foreign language immersion; and to avoid further increases to class sizes for general education and advanced academics, including those at Thomas Jefferson School for Science and Technology.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board adopt the FY 2011 Advertised Budget, as detailed in the Superintendent’s FY 2011 Proposed Budget, and with the following revisions: 1) restore funding of $17.1 million to eliminate the class size increase of one student per teacher for all classes except special education; 2) restore funding of $1.4 million for assistant principal positions; 3) restore funding of $0.8 million for instructional assistant positions; 4) restore funding of $1.6 million for instructional coaches positions; and 5) recognize additional reductions in central office support to schools of $0.9 million; and thereby increase the transfer request by $20.0 million, for a total request of $77.8 million.

BOARD MEMBER PROPOSED AMENDMENTS:

1. Restore $2.2 million for the Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools (FLES) teacher positions in elementary schools, as well as the FLES support position in Instructional Services. (Smith/Moon)

(Bradsher/Moon)
    The amendment passed 10-1 with Ms. Bradsher, Mr. Center, Mr. Gibson, Ms. Hone, Mr. Moon, Mrs. Reed, Mrs. Smith, Mr. Storck, Mrs. Strauss, and Mrs. Wilson voting "aye"; Mr. Raney voting "nay"; with the Mason district seat vacant.

    The amendment failed 3-8 with Mr. Center, Ms. Hone, and Mr. Storck, voting "aye"; Ms. Bradsher, Mr. Gibson, Mr. Moon, Mr. Raney, Mrs. Reed, Mrs. Smith, Mrs. Strauss, and Mrs. Wilson voting "nay"; with the Mason district seat vacant.
