Speakers have three minutes to address the Board
 


Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so online starting at 6 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 7:30 and 8 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

On May 27, 2010, the Virginia Board of Education adopted revised Regulations Governing Educational Services for Gifted Students (8 VAC 20-40-10 et. seq.).  These revisions were made in order to strengthen the regulations and to bring them into conformity with best practices in gifted education. The Regulations Governing Educational Services for Gifted Students became effective for school divisions beginning in the 2010-2011 school year.  The Regulations are available for use in planning and developing a local plan for the education of gifted students.

 

Among the new revisions is the requirement that local school boards must approve their division’s local plan for the education of gifted students.  Upon approval, the school board shall submit a copy of its plan to the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) for technical review on a schedule determined by the VDOE.

 

Fairfax County Public Schools’ Local Plan for the Gifted 2011-2016 outlines a continuum of gifted services that are offered in grades K-12.  The plan was updated and revised through the efforts of a committee that consisted of members of the Advanced Academic Advisory Committee, central office staff, elementary, middle, and high school administrators and teachers.

4.01 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution - Recommendation to adopt the newly revised 2011 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item (FS; action scheduled 10/6/11)
Meeting
Sep 22, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 4

Staff Contact:              Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services    

 

Other Staff Present:  Ronald A. Hull, acting director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:     September 22, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 4

 

Subject:                      Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on June 21, 2011, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2011 session of the General Assembly.  Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

During the 2011 session of the General Assembly, sixteen (16) Bills were approved relating to governmental procurement and/or contracts.  Four of these Bills modified the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA) in a manner which affects the operations of Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS).  The remaining Bills affected state agencies only or were not otherwise applicable to FCPS.    

 

2011 Changes to the VPPA relevant to FCPS

  1. House Bill 1592, Code of Virginia §2.2-4303, adds a requirement to post all emergency and sole source procurements on the Department of Purchasing and Supply Management web site or other appropriate web sites.  Although this Bill does not change a mandatory provision of the VPPA, school system staff nonetheless concurs that it be incorporated into the Purchasing Resolution because posting emergency and sole source purchases increases transparency.
  1. House Bill 1951, Code of Virginia §2.2-4336 and 2.2-4337, raises the minimum construction contract amount required for bid, performance, and payment bonds from $100,000 to $500,000. This Bill changes a mandatory section of the VPPA.
  1. House Bill 2520, Code of Virginia §2.2-4303 and 2.2-4308, eliminates the state design-build review board.  Historically, FCPS has not made use of the design-built construction method and does not intend to use it currently.  If FCPS were to use this methodology in the future, written procedures consistent with the VPPA would need to be adopted at that time. 

 

  1. Senate Bill 1425, Code of Virginia §2.2-4330, requires that, within five days of a request, a public body furnish written notice of its decision to a bidder, who requests permission to withdraw its bid on a construction project due to the bidder’s own error.

 

County’s Administrative Changes applicable to FCPS

 

  1. Adds a process, when elected, for contractor prequalification for procurements other than construction services.  The process establishes the application procedures and remedies for potential contractors and is substantially the same as prequalification for construction contracts.
  1. Adds additional criteria for vendor suspension or debarment.
  1. Removes obsolete language and makes technical corrections to reflect updates in County practices, such as the implementation of FOCUS.  These changes do not have a material effect on County or FCPS procurement policy. 

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009 and 2010 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2011 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

  1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  1. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

4.04 School Board Policy - Recommendation to adopt Policy 1102.4, Development of Directives, and update Section 4, Board Roles and Responsibilities, Policy Development and Review, of the Strategic Governance Manual, as detailed in the agenda item (SB; action scheduled 10/6/11)
Meeting
Sep 22, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 4

Information

Staff Contact:            Pamela Goddard, Executive Assistant to the School Board

 

Meeting Category:    September 22, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 4

 

Subject:                    School Board Policy 1102, Development of Directives

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                             

 

Key Points:   

 

On September 12, 2011, the Governance Committee discussed possible revisions to Policy 1102, Development of Directives.  These revisions would affect the current procedure regarding Board approval of only those policies with no change, minimal change, or a change in department of responsibility.

 

Currently, the Governance Committee and then the entire School Board and the Leadership Team review those no-change policies electronically via the no-change memo.  If there are no objections after a three-week review period, the updated policies are then published on the web site.  Under the proposed revised procedure, the review period would be increased to four weeks and the policies under review would also be posted on the public web site.  After the review period, an agenda item listing all the policies under review would then be added to the consent agenda for the next Board regular meeting for action.  After the Board’s approval, the updated policies would be published on the web site.

 

The current procedures for policies other than those on the no-change memo will remain unchanged.  Policies that have been referred by the Board or the Superintendent will be scheduled for a work session discussion.  All policies (except those on the no-change memo) will be scheduled for new business and action at a regular Board meeting.

 

The Governance Committee is recommending approval of this new procedure regarding Board approval of only those policies with no change, minimal change, or a change in department of responsibility by:

  1. Updating Policy 1102, Development of Directives, to reflect the procedures (the specifics of the procedure will be incorporated into Regulation 1102, Procedure for Maintenance of Directives)
  2. Updating Section 4, Board Roles and Responsibilities, Policy Development and Review, of the Strategic Governance Manual, to reflect the new procedure for those policies with no change, minimal change, or a change in department of responsibility

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 1102.4, Development of Directives, and update Section 4, Board Roles and Responsibilities, Policy Development and Review, of the Strategic Governance Manual, as detailed in the agenda item.

On September 9, 2011, the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA) Board of Directors approved a resolution supporting public education in Virginia.  Local school boards have been encouraged by the VSBA to adopt a similar resolution.  The following resolution is being recommended for adoption:

 

A RESOLUTION IN SUPPORT OF PUBLIC EDUCATION IN VIRGINIA

WHEREAS, per pupil state funding for public education in Virginia has been reduced by more than 13% since 2009; and

 

WHEREAS, neither the requirements of the Virginia Standards of Learning and of the federal No Child Left Behind Act, nor the overall costs of operating the public schools have been reduced; and

 

WHEREAS, state funding for public education does not reflect the true cost of constructing, staffing, equipping, operating and maintaining schools that perform at the level needed to meet the requirements of the Standards of Quality and the Standards of Learning; and

 

WHEREAS, none of the $544.8 million FY2011 budget surplus has been proposed to be allocated to K-12 public education;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board respectfully requests the Governor to allocate a fair share of the $544.8 million FY2011 budget surplus to K-12 public education, to begin to restore funds cut from K-12 public education in Fiscal Years 2009, 2010, and 2011.
