                                    Maura Burke, director, Office of Early Childhood and Grant Management

 

Meeting Category:  June 13, 2016 – Work Session No. 127

 

Subject:  Study of the Impact of Pre-Kindergarten Experiences on FCPS Students

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Key Points (Findings):

  • Participating in the Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP) provides achievement advantages over not participating.
  • Participating in FECEP for two years, rather than one, further advantages students. 
  • Students who come to FCPS having attended any type of formal pre-kindergarten experience usually perform better than students who have had no formal pre-k experience. 
  • Diminishing achievement advantages over time among students with formal pre-kindergarten experiences mirror national findings.
  • Additional academic-related benefits of formal pre-kindergarten (such as FECEP) include reduced need for support services (Special Education or ESOL) and increased participation in advanced academic opportunities.
  • Cost avoidance extends the value of the FECEP program beyond academic benefits for students to financial benefits for FCPS.  
  • FCPS’ ability to expand the number of students served by FECEP is limited by the current state funding structures.
  • FCPS’ understanding of the pre-kindergarten experiences of its students is limited by the way in which information is gathered during the student registration process.

 

Recommendations:

 

Superintendent:

  • Continue to provide FECEP as a two-year experience as recommended by current federal law for funding.
  • Continue efforts to decrease the four- and three-year-old waitlists to meet the needs of more Fairfax County preschoolers.
  • Continue collaborative efforts with the county to expand access to preschool services for all eligible children in the county.

 

 

School Board:

  • Support the reduction of the waitlists for three- and four-year-old children eligible for FECEP participation, through (a) advocacy to the state for modifications to funding procedures that promote greater access by FCPS to Virginia Preschool Initiative (VPI) funds, and (b) inclusion of local funds for expansion of FECEP in future budgets.

Meeting Category:  June 13, 2016 – Work Session No. 128

 

Subject:  Compensation Study

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS has hired Segal Group, a benefits, compensation, and human resources consulting firm, to identify a compensation structure that attracts and retains qualified employees through an optimal mix of salary and benefits. The study is a key piece of the Strategic Plan’s goal 3 – Premier Workforce – which aims to recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts, and ensure that FCPS maintains a competitive compensation package.

 

On March 14, 2016, Segal Group presented various options for teacher pay progression, which took into consideration findings from the market study of FCPS’ competitors as well as focus groups of its employees, which were previously presented to the Board. Beginning the final phase in this process, Segal Group has designed new proposed future scales for both teachers and administrators, to be presented at this meeting. Potential Unified and Instructional Assistant scales will be presented in the fall.

Meeting Category:  June 13, 2016 - All day Work Session No. 130

 

Subject:  Human Relations Advisory Committee Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points: 

 

The School Board charged “The HRAC to work with the Office of Equity and Employee Relations in providing evaluation of and feedback of Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) efforts related to peer-to-peer behaviors including bullying, harassment, and sexual misconduct. The committee's areas of focus will be on definitions, applications, reporting, training, monitoring, addressing the areas of concern, education, and prevention.”

 

In response to the School Board’s charge, the committee divided into three subcommittees to accomplish its charge as a continuation of the 2014-2015 HRAC charge. The three subcommittees were:  Reporting, Monitoring and Application, Training and Education, and Prevention which was focused on Cultural Competency and Technology. The subcommittees provided several key recommendations related to preventing adverse peer-to-peer behaviors to include bullying, harassment, and sexual misconduct.
