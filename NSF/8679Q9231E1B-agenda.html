Childhood Immunization Month - April 2009

The Fairfax County School Board recognizes the efficacy of immunization programs in the prevention of the spread of childhood diseases, the School Board promotes sound health practices for all children, and the School Board establishes policies that facilitate access to health services for all children.

Immunizations are available to Fairfax County parents and their children from public and private resources, and, for every $1 spent on immunizations, $10 can be saved in future medical costs.

Autism Awareness Month - April 2009

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.

Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about autism. It is sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA). Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

National Library Week/School Library Media Month - April 12-18, 2009

National Library Week is an annual celebration of the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate. National Library Week 2009 will be celebrated with the them, "Worlds connect @ your library."

First sponsored in 1958, National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association (ALA) and libraries across the country each April. It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate.

In celebration of National School Libraries Month, a miniature Art in the Pages statuette is being presented to the School Board and Leadership Team from the Fairfax Library Foundation.

Art in the Pages is the first public art sculpture project for Fairfax County and the City of Fairfax. This rare one-time project showcases the talent of local artists while celebrating the joy of art and reading among by the entire community – all ages and all cultures! Thirty-one 4' high statues are on display outside public library branches, schools and businesses. Some artists are FCPS art teachers and two are on display at public schools.

With the assistance of the Library Foundation's Secretary, Susan Thorniley (Coordinator of FCPS Library Information Services who serves as the School Board's appointed Trustee for the Fairfax County Public Library Board), each Fairfax County Public school will receive a miniature statuette that may be creatively decorated with paint or other artistic means, perhaps representing the school, and placed on display in the school library.

SUBJECT: Proposed Bell Schedule

TYPE: Action

EXPLANATION:

At the March 9 work session, the School Board will be reviewing in great depth the school start and end time proposal and changes presented over the past several months. The inclusion of this motion on the March 5, 2009, regular meeting agenda as new business is intended to establish the timeline for a formal vote of the School Board, fully recognizing that based on the March 9 discussion, this motion could be modified prior to School Board action (motion scheduled for action on March 19).

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board reject the proposed bell schedule change (known as Iteration 3) and confirm the existing structure.

BOARD MEMBER AMENDMENTS:

1. Amendment in the form of a substitute motion:

A. That the draft bell schedule known as “Iteration 3” is rejected.

B. That the School Board’s Transportation Operational Expectation is revised as follows:
      The Superintendent will provide safe and cost-effective student transportation services to instructional programs for which they are eligible or that meet their needs and are a “best fit” solution to the following requirements: (1) maintains reasonable student pick-up and drop-off times and sensible ride times; (2) enables age-appropriate school start times to the extent practicable, while ensuring that quality supplementary academic programming and extracurricular student activities are not diminished; and (3) implements any changes, as may be needed, to avoid abrupt disruptions to family, employee, and community daily schedules and patterns.
C. That the Superintendent present to the School Board his reasonable interpretation of this revised operational expectation for School Board review no later than June 30, 2009.

D. That the Superintendent prepare, if needed, a proposed resource plan, for inclusion in the final FY 2010 Approved Budget, which (1) identifies the staffing, consultant support, hardware and software acquisition he believes is required to design transportation routing to meet the School Board’s amended operational expectation; and (2) is funded through efficiencies gained through transportation system reengineering and optimization.

E. That future transportation implementation costs will not grow beyond FY 2009 levels, which represents the base year against which costs will be evaluated, while accounting for inflation and fluctuations in the cost of equipment, fuels, and personnel, and student growth. (Niedzielski-Eichner)

2. Amend the main motion by adding the words "…for the 2009-2010 school year" at the end of the sentence. (The main motion amended would read: “I move to reject the proposed bell schedule (known as Iteration 3) and confirm the existing structure for the 2009-2010 school year.”) (Center)

3. Amend the main motion to direct the Superintendent to report to the School Board no later than January 31, 2010, with an analysis of the issues related to the sleep needs of our high school students and the potential impacts related for high school families, students, and staff if a future change to the bell schedule were made. The Superintendent’s analysis would: 1) examine alternate strategies (those not related to changing bell schedules) to help students affected by sleep issues; 2) identify the most important issues for families, students, and staff related to a change in a bell schedule (to move high schools later) based upon the feedback received from the Study Circles, Survey, Community Dialogues, and e-mails to FCPS; these include such issues as sports and other after-school programs, availability of community use facilities, issues related to traffic and others; a) determine the impacts to families, students, and staff; b) identify possible solutions or mitigation strategies; c) identify costs of solutions and mitigations (if applicable); and d) provide an assessment of the viability of the solutions or mitigation strategies. (Center)

    SUBJECT: Scope of Spring 2009 Boundary Studies for Ft. Belvoir Elementary School
    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:

    On March 9, 2009, staff presented a recommendation for a spring boundary study for Ft. Belvoir Elementary. This initiative is based on a critical need to provide enrollment relief to Ft. Belvoir Elementary School by utilizing available capacity at Woodlawn Elementary School and reflects a change to the traditional fall time frame for boundary studies. This study would be undertaken in a slightly modified calendar and process which would include town meetings, community outreach and notices, but would not include small focus group meetings, multiple scenarios proposed by staff or a long period for public comment.

    KEY POINTS:

    This study is proposed to be limited to Ft. Belvoir Elementary and Woodlawn Elementary as the receiving school. A single Ft. Belvoir housing complex known as Woodlawn Village located along Pole Road is proposed to be re-assigned to Woodlawn Elementary School. There are approximately 200 students residing in this housing area and Woodlawn Elementary is projected to have a surplus balance of approximately 230 student spaces. The housing area proposed to be re-assigned is adjacent to the school and at least a portion of the students to be re-assigned could potentially walk to Woodlawn Elementary. This change could be implemented for the 2009-10 school year in order to provide needed interim enrollment relief at Ft. Belvoir Elementary until and if such time as a second school site could be planned and constructed on the base by the Department of Defense or Army. This proposal represents a single option. No other adjacent school has capacity to receive additional students from Ft. Belvoir Elementary. A map and background information describing this proposal is contained in Appendix A.

    OPTIONS:

    Due to the severity of overcrowding at Ft. Belvoir, there are no options.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve the spring 2009 scope of boundary study for Fort Belvoir Elementary and Woodlawn Elementary Schools based on a critical need to alleviate existing and projected over enrollment, pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments and School Closing.

    SUBJECT: Scope of Spring 2009 Boundary Studies for Annandale High School
    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:

    On March 9, 2009, staff presented a recommendation for a spring boundary study for Annandale High School. This initiative is based on a critical need to provide enrollment relief to Annandale High School by utilizing available capacities at Falls Church and Lake Braddock High Schools. In addition, this study would also re-align feeder middle schools at Glasgow and Lake Braddock and provide needed enrollment relief to Poe Middle School This also reflects a change to the traditional fall time frame for boundary studies. This study would be undertaken in a slightly modified calendar and process which would include town meetings, community outreach and notices, but would not include small focus group meetings, multiple scenarios proposed by staff or a long period for public comment.

    KEY POINTS:

    This study is proposed to be limited to Annandale and Falls Church High Schools and Lake Braddock Secondary School and Poe and Glasgow Middle Schools. This study also
    represents a single option with several changes to be considered in order to relieve over-enrollment at Annandale, align middle and high school feeders and eliminate an attendance island. The proposal is summarized below:
    • From Annandale High School to Falls Church High School: That portion of the Annandale High School north of Rt. 236 and west of Columbia Pike that wraps around the Falls Church attendance island is proposed to be re-assigned to Falls Church High School. This has the effect of eliminating an attendance island and more importantly, potentially shifts approximately 175 students from Annandale to Falls Church.
    • From Poe Middle to Glasgow Middle: That portion of Poe which extends north and south of Rt. 50 is proposed to be re-assigned to Glasgow. This area of Poe is currently assigned to Stuart High School. This proposal has the effect of eliminating a split feeder for this portion of Poe Middle since Glasgow Middle feeds to Stuart High School and could further reduce high enrollment at Poe by approximately 130 students.
    • From Annandale High School and Poe to Lake Braddock Secondary: That portion of Annandale High School and the underlying attendance area of Poe Middle School south of Braddock Road and west of I-495 to Lake Braddock Secondary. This change potentially shifts approximately 100 students from Annandale to Lake Braddock and would also reassign approximately 50 students from Poe Middle to Lake Braddock Secondary resulting in additional relief to over-enrollment at Poe Middle School.

    This change could be implemented for the 2010-11 school year and would represent an interim step in addressing overcrowding at Annandale High School and Poe Middle School. Staff further believes that this initiative is based on the severity of current overcrowding at Annandale but would not preclude future review and planning for ultimate solutions to address capacity balance, feeder alignments and transportation efficiencies for elementary, middle and high schools in the region. A map and background information describing this proposal is contained in Appendix A.

    OPTIONS:

    Due to the severity of overcrowding at these schools, there are no options.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve the spring 2009 scope of boundary study for Annandale, Lake Braddock, and Falls Church High Schools and Poe, Glasgow and Lake Braddock Middle Schools, based on a critical need to alleviate existing and projected over-enrollment at Annandale High and Poe Middle Schools, pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments and School Closing.
