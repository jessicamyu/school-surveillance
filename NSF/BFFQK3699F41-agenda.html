October 2, 2019 Agenda:

  1. Policies from No Change Memo:
    • Policy 2115 Child Abuse Reporting [no changes proposed] 


      Recent changes to state code, outlined below, are not expected to impact this policy.  
      HB 2597 and SB 1661 amended and reenacted §§ 63.2-1506, 63.2-1508, and 63.2-1517 of the Code of Virginia by requiring Local Department of Social Services
      (LDSS) respond to all complaints or reports involving child human trafficking. HB 2597 and SB 1661 amended and reenacted § 63.2-1508 of the Code of Virginia by establishing that the alleged victim’s parent, caretaker, or any other person suspected of trafficking a child may meet the caretaker criteria needed to determine the validity of a complaint or report involving the alleged human trafficking of a child. Additionally, HB 2597 and SB 1661 created a new section 63.2-1506.1 of the Code of Virginia which requires LDSS conduct a Human Trafficking assessment on all reports involving the human trafficking of a child, unless an investigation or family assessment is required by law or needed to ensure the safety of the child. HB 2597 and SB 1661 also amended and reenacted §63.2-1517 of the Code of Virginia by allowing the LDSS assume emergency custody of child victims of human trafficking during the course of the human trafficking assessment, for up to 72 hours without prior approval from the child’s parent or guardian to ensure the safety of the child.
