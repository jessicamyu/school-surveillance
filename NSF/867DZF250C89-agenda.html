SUBJECT: Response to Intervention

TYPE: Presentation

RELATED TO: Student Achievement Goal 1 Goal 2 Goal 3

BACKGROUND:

The reauthorization of IDEA introduced a process called Response to Intervention (RtI) that could serve to ensure early intervention to students who are struggling, prevent potential learning problems, and provide documentation that can be used to assist in identifying children with disabilities.

KEY POINTS:

The components of RtI are well researched strategies within the educational community. These components are: universal screening; systematic tiered support and intervention; and, monitoring student progress. All Fairfax County schools examine student achievement data and engage in a process of assessment, instructional intervention and evaluation. RtI supports the instructional intervention processes we use in FCPS, but requires that these data driven interventions are systematized and student progress is well documented. The robust data collected from this type of intervention process has the potential to inform eligibility decisions. Parents remain important partners in any intervention process that engages their children and have the right to request that their child’s progress is reviewed by the local screening committee.

OPTIONS:

This is an information session requested by the School Board.

RECOMMENDATION:
      Continue to respond to School Board requests for information.

SUBJECT: Proffers
TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      On May 24, 2007, the county released to FCPS a total of $5.4M in residential rezoning proffer funds that had been accumulating since 2003. Subsequent to our receipt of these funds staff has been working with the county to clarify what are to be considered appropriate uses of these funds since many of the funds are “restricted” and/or the original intent of the proffer is no longer possible to fulfill due to the delayed release of the funds.

KEY POINTS:

      Staff has reviewed the proffer for each of the residential rezoning cases associated with this $5.4M lump sum transfer and made the following determinations about potential uses of these funds:
          · $2.28M was proffered without restriction for capital improvements and may be disbursed according to the specific proffer language and as determined by FCPS;

          · $3.1M is restricted based on specific proffer language;

          · $1.62M of the $3.1M (noted above) is associated with schools or areas where FCPS has already made capital improvements in accordance with the approved Capital Improvement Program (CIP).

      After making the above determinations, staff contacted the county to request that $1.62M in restricted proffers associated with prior completed projects be newly interpreted as unrestricted. This would enable FCPS to add $1.62M to $2.28M in already unrestricted funds, thus increasing the unrestricted pool to $3.9M and further enabling FCPS to make additional funds available for unrestricted capital needs.


RECOMMENDATIONS:
      1. We recommend that the School Board authorize use of these funds to target capacity enhancements at elementary schools to include modular buildings, internal building modifications, or additions.

      2. A second option would be to use these funds as additional cash and thereby accelerate all projects.

      3. As a third option, the School Board might want to utilize proffer funds within the specific magisterial district in which they were generated, at the discretion of the individual school board member and consistent with the approved CIP.

SUBJECT: Criteria for Reexamining the Renovation Queue

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      The current renovation queue was developed in 2000 and it won’t be completed until after 2020. Furthermore, all high schools in the queue have been completed or are underway while many middle and elementary schools remain. Staff believes that an updated renovation queue is needed that considers changes to school conditions since 2000. Staff also believes that an updated renovation queue should have a shorter completion horizon.
KEY POINTS:
      • Engage a consultant to conduct a study for the purpose of developing an updated renovation queue that would begin after all currently voter approved projects are completed or underway.
      • Develop a queue that has an approximate 5 year completion horizon.
      • Give greater weight in the study to a school’s ability to deliver mandatory instructional programs.


OPTIONS:

Maintain the status quo.

RECOMMENDATION:

Direct staff to initiate an updated renovation queue study per the attachment.
