Summary/Background:

 

The Membership Analysis and Trends Report is updated annually by the Office of Facilities Planning Services. The report informs the student membership projection set and school capacity evaluations, both of which form the basis for additional capital planning and new construction recommendations that are part of the Capital Improvement Program (CIP).

 

Total membership for School Year (SY) 2019-20 increased by 992 students: elementary school membership increased by 198 students; middle school membership increased by 296 students; and high school membership increased by 617 students. Although membership increased at elementary, middle, and high schools, the change in total membership includes a decrease of -79 at centers and alternative programs; and a decrease of -40 within the other categories which are at elementary and high school levels.

 

Three fundamental factors affect the counts within student membership and projections from year-to-year: the birth to kindergarten ratio, net migration, and transfers. These factors were reviewed in this year’s report to aid in the evaluation of the change in total membership from school year (SY) 2018-19 to SY 2019-20:

 

  • The ratio between the number of births to kindergarten membership counts remained unchanged at 87 percent.

 

  • Net migration changed from -357 students in SY 2018-19 to 1,725 students in SY 2019-20; this indicates that 2,082 more students entered the division than withdrew.

 

  • The total number of transfers was 19,385, or 10.3 percent of total membership. The top three reasons provided for transfers, by percentage of total transfers, are: 26.4 percent to access an advanced academic program (AAP), 21.0 percent to enter special education programs, and 17.9 percent were related to reasons considered within the student transfer regulation (Regulation 2230).

 

Summary data tables are included in the report to compare these factors in the current and prior years by region, high school pyramid and by school level (elementary, middle, and high). It should be noted that data utilized each year is adjusted for accuracy at such times as actual numbers have been verified; this can result in a difference between counts from year-to-year for the same data point. Therefore, strict comparisons cannot be made between reports presented to the School Board in previous years. Additionally, integers have been included in all cases where percentages are used in this report, as requested by the Board in 2019.

Summary/Background: 

 

This report describes actions and outcomes from SY 2018-19 related to the Strategic Plan, Goal 1: Student Success, which details FCPS’ commitment to reaching, challenging, and preparing every student for success in school and life.

 

The report and presentation focus on the following three desired outcomes and will detail the Division’s performance in school year 2018-19 as it relates to the revised metrics in the Strategic Plan.

  • FCPS will eliminate gaps in opportunity, access, and achievement for all students.
  • All future FCPS families will access high-quality early learning experiences.
  • All FCPS PreK-12 students will continuously progress in their development of Portrait of a Graduate (POG) attributes.

 

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success report as detailed in the agenda item.
