National Sleep Awareness Week - March 2-9, 2014

 

National Sleep Awareness Week, which takes place March 2-9, 2014, is an annual public education and awareness campaign to promote the importance of sleep.  Adequate sleep is an integral part of a healthy lifestyle, critical to physical and mental health, as well as to learning effectively.  It’s also important to get enough sleep to reduce sports injuries, accidents and the dangers of drowsy driving.  The National Sleep Foundation suggests that adults need 7-9 hours of sleep per night, elementary-school-age children about 10-11 hours, and teens 8-1/2 to 9-1/4 hours of sleep a night for good health.  According to the Virginia Academy of Sleep Medicine, "Sleep affects how children feel and function. By helping your child to get the recommended amount of sleep, you can improve your child’s health, psychological well-being, and safety.  Sleep is key to your child’s growth and development as well as his/her ability to learn.”

 

 

Theater In Our Schools Month - March 2014

 

The American Alliance for Theatre and Education has designated March as Theater In Our Schools Month to celebrate and increase public awareness of the benefits of theater education for youth development.  The theme for 2014 is Fostering a Culture of Creativity.  Theater education has the power to raise academic achievement and to stimulate innovation and creativity among students.  The Alliance encourages citizens to celebrate the positive impact of theater in the classroom and to remember that all students should have access to quality theater education in their lives.

 

 

Music in Our Schools Month - March 2014

 

March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.  Based on the 2014 theme, Music Makes Me____!, the month is designed to highlight the importance of comprehensive, sequential music education taught by exemplary music educators for all  children - and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music.  It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages.

 

Youth Art Month - March 2014

 

Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.  The theme for Youth Art Month 2014 is “Start With Art, Learn for Life.” 

The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

Key Points:

 

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2014 budget and to make revisions as necessary.  The FY 2014 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 19, 2013.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund revenue adjustments reflect a net decrease of $0.1 million due to a decrease of $80,000 in state revenue for National Board Certified Teacher (NBCT) pass-through payments based on the number of FCPS teachers eligible to receive a stipend from the state in FY 2014, offset by an increase in state funding of $5,000 for the monetary award for the Teacher of the Year Award.  These revenue adjustments are offset by corresponding expenditure adjustments.  While no adjustment is recommended at this time, sales tax revenue will continue to be monitored closely for the remainder of FY 2014.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund disbursement adjustments reflect a net decrease of $0.1 million and include the corresponding expenditure adjustments for NBCT and Teacher of the Year, as well as adjustments to compensation accounts for the staffing reserve, library and equipment expenditures for the Eastern Fairfax Elementary School (Bailey’s area), and major maintenance.

 

A one-time reduction of $1.4 million in compensation accounts is due to the return of 9.3 staffing reserve positions and adjustments to the staffing reserve position distribution.  There are currently 14.3 positions remaining in the staffing reserve, and 5.0 positions will be left in the reserve to address staffing needs until the end of the year.  To facilitate the opening of the Eastern Fairfax Elementary School, one-time funding of $0.3 million will be provided for library materials, one-time funding of $0.5 million will be provided for equipment for the new school site, and additionally, $0.6 million in one-time funding will be provided for major maintenance needs.

 

In total, in the School Operating Fund, this agenda recognizes decreased revenue totaling $0.1 million and decreased disbursements totaling $0.1 million.

RESOLUTION
HONORING THE 75th ANNIVERSARY
OF LOUISE ARCHER ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

 

 

     WHEREAS, in 2014, Louise Archer Elementary School, located in Vienna, Virginia, will proudly celebrate its 75th anniversary of providing an outstanding education to its students, having served as a model of academic excellence and having been recognized for many accomplishments including the Virginia Governor’s Award for Excellence, and the Golden Wellness Award for promoting health and fitness; and

 

     WHEREAS, the original Vienna Colored School was built in 1939, and then, in 1950, the School Board officially changed the school name to Louise Archer Elementary School to honor its first educator; teacher and principal, Mrs. Louise Archer; and

 

     WHEREAS, Louise Archer Elementary School works collaboratively with the Parent Teacher Association and the local community to provide opportunities for service learning and extra-curricular activities to enhance its students’ educational experience; and 

 

     WHEREAS, Louise Archer Elementary School has developed a close working relationship with its community-based business partners, Northwest Federal Credit Union and Coldwell Banker, and these partnerships work to support the students and local community through donations, mentoring, and financial awareness programs; and

 

     WHEREAS, Louise Archer Elementary School students have always received a comprehensive education from dedicated teachers and staff, in a nurturing and positive environment, “Where the Sun Always Shines”, with the support of school leaders and involved parents;

 

     NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, honors Louise Archer Elementary School on the occasion of its 75th anniversary of providing educational excellence and enrichment opportunities to students and the community.

Staff Contact:         Terri Breeden, assistant superintendent, Professional Learning and Accountability

 

Meeting Category:  March 6, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 15

 

Subject:                 Alternative Accreditation Plans Requests for Key and Kilmer Centers, Bryant and Mountain View Alternative High Schools, and Fairfax Adult High School

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:            Student Achievement Goal 1. Pursue Academic Excellence 

 

Key Points:

 

Every three years VDOE allows any “Special Purpose School” to submit an alternative accreditation plan.  Five schools in Fairfax County - Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School – once again are pursuing the approval of alternative accreditation plans in order to be able to be “Fully Accredited” in 2014-2015, 2015-2016, and 2016-2017.

 

Because the five schools listed above are classified as “Special Purpose” schools, serving unique populations of students for whom the measures of accreditation are inappropriate, staff in the Office of School Improvement at the Virginia Department of Education recommend that alternative accreditation plans be submitted to the Virginia Board of Education for approval.  This recommendation is based on the Regulations Establishing Standards for Accrediting Public Schools in Virginia, Section 8 VAC 20-131-280.C. The regulation states: 

 

"Subject to the provisions of 8 VAC 20-131-350, the governing school board of special purpose schools such as those provided for in § 22.1-26 of the Code of Virginia, Governor’s schools, special education schools, alternative schools, or career and technical schools that serve as the student's school of principal enrollment may seek approval of an alternative accreditation plan from the Board of Education. Schools offering alternative education programs and schools with a graduation cohort of 50 or fewer students as defined by the graduation rate formula adopted by the board may request that the board approve an alternative accreditation plan to meet the graduation and completion index benchmark. Special purpose schools with alternative accreditation plans shall be evaluated on standards appropriate to the programs offered in the school and approved by the board prior to August 1 of the school year for which approval is requested. Any student graduating from a special purpose school with a Standard, Advanced Studies, or Modified Standard Diploma must meet the requirements prescribed in 8 VAC 20-131-50."

 

Options:

 

 1. Approve the request for approval of the Alternative Accreditation Plans for Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School for submission to the Virginia Board of Education.

2. Continue to be evaluated under the current state accreditation regulations from the Virginia Department of Education and have Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School rated “Accredited with Warning” or “Provisionally Accredited-Graduation Rate” and undergo an academic review and prepare a school improvement plan in accordance with the guidelines adopted by the Virginia Board of Education, per Section 8 VAC 20-131-300.C of the Regulations Establishing Standards for Accrediting Public Schools in Virginia.   

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve the Alternative Accreditation Plan requests for Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School for submission to the Virginia Board of Education, as detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
