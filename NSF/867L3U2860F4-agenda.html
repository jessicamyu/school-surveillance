    SUBJECT: Requests for Waiver of Verified Credit (SOL)

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board request waivers of Standards of Learning (SOL) verified credit requirements for certain identified students as detailed in the agenda item.

    EXPLANATION:
      As explained in the attached Request for Waiver of the Verified Credit Requirements for a Standard of Advanced Studies Diploma, seven Fairfax County Public Schools students were unable to meet the verified credit requirements for a diploma and the students’ previous non-Virginia schools would not award diplomas.     5.04 2006-2011 Local Plan for the Gifted - Approve the 2006-2011 Local Plan for the Education of the Gifted (IS; presented as new business 5/11/06)
    Meeting
    May 25, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 20
    Category
    5. Consent Agenda - 9:30 p.m.
    Type
    Action (Consent)

    Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

    SUBJECT: 2006-2011 Local Plan for the Gifted

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve the 2006-2011 Local Plan for the Education of the Gifted.

    EXPLANATION:
      The Virginia Board of Education adopted the current Regulations Governing Educational Services for Gifted Students, which outline the requirements of the local plan for the education of the gifted that school divisions must submit to the Department of Education for approval. Currently, local plans for the gifted are renewed every five years.

      Fairfax County Public Schools’ Local Plan for the Gifted outlines a continuum of gifted services that are offered in grades K – 12. The plan was updated and revised through the efforts of a committee that consisted of members of the Gifted and Talented (GT) Advisory Committee, central office staff, and elementary, middle, and high school administrators and teachers. The major changes to the plan consist of new goals for 2006 – 2011 and language that supports a continuum of gifted services for all students K-12. In addition, the plan was posted on the GT web site; and the Keep In Touch system was used to notify community members that the plan was available for review and feedback. An executive summary is attached.

    Staff Contact: Michael A. Molloy, Director of Government Relations

    SUBJECT: Legislative Recommendations for the 2007 Policies and Resolutions of the VSBA

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve recommendations for the 2007 Policies and Resolutions of the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA).

    EXPLANATION:
      Each spring the VSBA Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee seeks recommendations from local school boards concerning new or revised legislative policies and resolutions to be considered for VSBA approval. The Committee reviews the local school boards’ recommendations along with other suggestions for changes to the existing policies and resolutions. The VSBA takes action on the Committee’s report at the annual fall convention.

      This process allows the Fairfax County School Board to make recommendations for the VSBA legislative program and to receive statewide support for some of its own legislative positions. The Fairfax County School Board 2007 Legislative Program will be developed for adoption in the fall.

      Changes are proposed to positions regarding the federal No Child Left Behind (NCLB) Act, programs for students with limited English proficiency, and federal funding for the education of students with disabilities. A new position on state budget adoption is proposed. (See attached language and summary sheet).

      By the May 11 regular School Board meeting, these recommendations will have been reviewed by the School Board’s Legislation, Policy & Future Issues Committee at the May 8, 2006, work session.

    SUBJECT: Award of Contracts for Contribution, Use, and Installation of Synthetic Turf Field at West Springfield High School’s Main Stadium

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board (i) enter into a Contribution and Use Agreement with The Springfield, Virginia Youth Club, Inc. (“SYC”) for the funding and use of a synthetic turf field at the main stadium of West Springfield High School (“WSHS”); (ii) award a contract to Atlas Tracks, Inc., for provision and installation of a synthetic turf field at WSHS; and (iii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Chief Operating Officer, to further negotiate the contractual terms, if deemed advisable, and to execute the above-referenced contract documents on behalf of the School Board.

    EXPLANATION:
        SYC and school system staff have for some time been engaged in discussions, resulting in a proposal by SYC to fund the installation of an synthetic turf field at the main stadium of West Springfield High School. The total cost of the field (including related site work and installation) is estimated to be $800,000.00. SYC would deposit that amount with the School Board concurrent with the parties’ execution of the Contribution & Use Agreement, which has been negotiated by SYC and school system staff. In exchange, SYC will be afforded second priority use of the artificial turf field for an initial term of up to ten years, subordinate to the school’s use for its instructional program and to VHSL athletic uses as well as to certain revenue generating activities to provide funding assistance to the school for future, replacement field surfaces at this stadium.

        Following the execution of the Contribution and Use Agreement and SYC’s payment of $800,000.00, the contract for provision and installation of the field will be executed. Atlas Track & Tennis has been selected to perform the installation (and related site work) in accordance with a cooperative procurement issued in 2004 by the Association of Educational Purchasing Agencies (AEPA), a national educational purchasing cooperative. This AEPA procurement has secured competitive pricing which is considered favorable by the Office of Design and Construction Services. The school system’s Office of Design and Construction will be responsible for construction contract administration and for inspection of the field improvement work.
