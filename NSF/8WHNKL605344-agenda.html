Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. three (3) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. The speakers list closes at 4:30 p.m. on the last business day prior to the meeting date.  Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. 6.02 Award of Contract - Recommendation to approve a contract award to Prismatic Services, Inc., in the amount of $179,999.00 for the provision of food and nutrition services assessment (FS; action scheduled 1/24/13)
Meeting
Jan 10, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 11
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:            Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Ronald A. Hull, coordinator, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:     January 10, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 11

 

Subject:                     Award of Contract – Food and Nutrition Services Assessment

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:                School Board Policy 5011.2, Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

During the adoption of the FY 2013 Advertised Budget meeting, the School Board directed staff to create a placeholder of $0.2 million to fund a consultant study of the Food and Nutrition Services Program to identify cost-effective ways of making FCPS school food healthier, with the $0.2 million funding to be redirected within the Food and Nutrition Services Fund. The RFP was issued on June 29, 2012. The Office of Procurement Services directly solicited 15 potential offerors. Additionally, the RFP was publicly advertised on Virginia’s electronic procurement portal (eVA), as well as the Fairfax County Department of Purchasing and Supply Management’s website. The RFP closed on August 9, 2012, with six proposals received from offerors.

 

A Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) was appointed, comprised of three community members, one Fairfax County Health Department employee and one Office of Food and Nutrition Services employee.

 

The SAC met on September 24, 2012, for an organizational meeting. At a meeting on November 7, 2012, the SAC discussed the technical proposals and ranked them based upon the evaluation criteria established in the RFP. The SAC met on November 19, 2012, to evaluate the business proposals of the three highest ranked offerors. Negotiations were held on December 3 and December 4, 2012, with the top three offerors. Best and final offers were received on December 6, 2012.  On December 6, 2012, the SAC Chairperson submitted a recommendation to award a contract to Prismatic Services, Inc.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve a contract award to Prismatic Services, Inc., in the amount of $179,999.00 for the provision of food and nutrition services assessment.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
