NATIONAL INCLUSIVE SCHOOLS WEEK RESOLUTION

December 5-9, 2022
 

WHEREAS, since its inception in 2001, National Inclusive Schools Week has celebrated the progress that schools have made in providing a supportive and quality education to an increasingly diverse student population, including students who are marginalized due to disability, gender, socio-economic status, cultural heritage, language preference, and other factors; and

 

WHEREAS, Inclusive Schools Week is an annual event sponsored by the Inclusive Schools Network (ISN) and is held during the first full week of December; and

 

WHEREAS, this year’s theme, “Unity Within Community—Inclusion Around the World." United we are stronger to focus on our common need—inclusive practices in our schools and in our communities.; and

 

WHEREAS,  to ensure that our children belong, thrive, and feel supported by their teachers, peers, family members, and neighbors we must continue to learn more about the work of other organizations and groups in our community and share ideas, resources, and support; and

 

WHEREAS, we must teach our children by our actions how to treat others, how to communicate and build relationship, and how to work in harmony to address our common needs.; and

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board reminds the entire FCPS community that education takes on a beautiful shape when all children are included.

Citizens who want to address the Board at a Regular meeting, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. four (4) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. For more information, please visit the FCPS Website.

 

A total of 16 in-person, live virtual, and prerecorded video speaker slots are available per meeting. Thirteen slots will be reserved for in-person and live virtual speakers.  Three (3) slots will be reserved for students and three (3) slots will be reserved for those speakers who wish to submit a prerecorded video. The speakers list closes at 48 hours before the meeting. After the registration closes, speakers will be determined by a random lottery process.  After the random process, speakers will be notified if they have received a speaking or prerecorded video spot.   After the first 13 in-person or live virtual speakers are determined, the next ten (10) names as determined by the same random process will be chosen as the waitlist and notified that they are on the waitlist to speak.  Video submissions will not be permitted for the waitlist.  Waitlist speakers must be present at the meeting to speak in the event of a no show.  Speaker substitutions are not permitted.  A speaker may not yield their time to another individual before or during their remarks.

 

The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

5.02 Solar Power Purchase Agreement for Mason Crest Elementary School [FTS; NB 11/14/22]

Subject:  Solar Power Purchase Agreement for Mason Crest Elementary School

 

 

Staff Contact:  Charles Fanshaw, interim assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Justin Moss, director, Facilities Management

 

 

Meeting Category:  December 1, 2022 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

On March 4, 2021, the School Board approved participation by FCPS in the solar power purchase program that was procured by Fairfax County Government on behalf of itself, certain County authorities, and FCPS.  Since that time, the County terminated one of the three contracts awarded for solar power purchase services (PPA Service Contracts), leaving two solar contractors eligible to perform these services on behalf of the participating entities.  Thereafter, the County amended its contracts with the two remaining solar contractors effective January 14, 2022, to, among other things, amend the fee schedule and eliminate the primary, secondary and tertiary contractor rankings.

 

Following the January 2022 contract amendment, FCPS’s Office of Facilities Management (OFM) requested proposals from both remaining solar contractors for FCPS’s first rooftop solar power purchase project at Mason Crest Elementary School.  Both of the remaining solar contractors submitted proposals to FCPS for this project and those proposals were analyzed by the Energy Solutions Division of CMTA, Inc. (CMTA), a third-party engineering firm, for purposes of evaluating and advising OFM on projected energy savings and other aspects of proposals submitted for PPA services.  CMTA identified the proposal submitted by Sun Tribe, LLC (“Sun Tribe”) as providing FCPS with higher energy savings over the course of the 25-year term than the other PPA proposal for this site.  Consequently, OFM wishes to proceed at this time with implementation of Sun Tribe’s proposal for installation of rooftop solar panels at Mason Crest Elementary School.

 

The next step in this process involves execution by FCPS and Sun Tribe of a Solar Power Purchase Agreement (Solar PPA) in the form attached to this agenda item.  The Solar PPA sets forth the process for finalizing the design of the solar facilities to the satisfaction of FCPS, and provides for installation, commissioning, and operation of the facilities by Sun Tribe.  Sun Tribe will retain ownership of the solar facilities throughout the term, subject to exercise of FCPS’s purchase option. The Solar PPA further memorializes Sun Tribe’s commitment to sell (and FCPS’s obligation to purchase) all electricity generated by the rooftop solar facilities for a period of 25 years at a rate of $0.1098 per kilowatt hour without escalation during the term.  Sun Tribe’s rooftop solar facilities will be connected to the electrical distribution system maintained by FCPS’s utility provider so as to permit transfer of solar-generated electricity that is not immediately usable at the site to the utility under a net metering arrangement to be agreed upon by FCPS and the utility.  Under that net metering arrangement, FCPS expects to receive credits for all electricity transferred to the utility’s distribution system.

 

While energy cost savings to be realized by FCPS over the course of the Solar PPA’s 25-year term will be impacted by factors that include utility cost increases during that period, CMTA estimates energy cost savings of $26,288.44 per contract year, on average, for this site.  In addition to the anticipated energy cost savings, the solar power purchase program proposed for Mason Crest Elementary School will further FCPS’s efforts in responding to the renewable energy recommendations of the Joint Environmental Task Force.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the implementation of a rooftop solar power purchase program at Mason Crest Elementary School and authorize the Division Superintendent and the interim Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to execute the Solar PPA and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Subject: Proposed New Policy 3290, Parental Notification Regarding Instructional Materials with Sexually Explicit Content

 

Staff Contact:  Noel Klimenko, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Meeting Category:  December 1, 2022--Regular Meeting

  

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

 

Proposed Policy 3290 states the requirements for identification of instructional materials with sexually explicit content, parental notification of those instructional materials, opportunity for parental review, and option of alternative instructional materials. The policy is based on the model policy developed by the Virginia Department of Education in compliance with the new law passed by Virginia General Assembly in 2022.

 

Existing directives in FCPS provide for departmental and school review of the appropriateness of instructional materials, communication with parents, and options for alternative instructional materials (P3003, R3004, R3005, R3007). The new policy establishes specific criteria for defining and identifying sexually explicit content, criteria for parental notification and review of that content, and a documented process for requesting alternative materials if parents they determine the sexually explicit content is not appropriate for their child.  

 

The new policy is recommended to ensure consistency with the requirements of the Code of Virginia (§ 22.1-16.8).

 

 

Recommendation: That the School Board review and approve proposed Policy 3290 prior to January 1, 2023.

Subject: Alternative Accreditation Plan Application to the Virginia Board of Education

 

Staff Contact:  Mark Greenfelder, assistant superintendent, Department of School Improvement and Supports (DSIS)

 

Other Staff Present: Bettrys Huffman, director, Office of Assessment and Reporting, (DSIS)

Meeting Category: December 1, 2022 - Regular Meeting    

  

School Board Action Required: New Business

 

Key Points:

 

State Superintendent’s Memo #252-22 requires divisions to apply for renewal of alternative accreditation plans in special purpose schools to affect 2023-24 accreditation ratings, based on 2022-23 school year outcomes. FCPS has five (5) special purpose schools eligible to request alternative accreditation plans: Bryant High School, Fairfax County Adult High School, Mountain View High School, Key Center School, Kilmer Center School.

 

Since 2009, FCPS has leveraged flexibility within the Standards of Accreditation (8 VAC 20-131-420.D) to request alternative accreditation plans for its qualifying special purpose schools. Current alternative accreditation plans for FCPS special purpose schools were approved by the Virginia Board of Education on April 21, 2022, affecting accreditation ratings for the current year, based on last year’s student outcomes.

 

Alternative accreditation plan applications describe the special population served by each school, explain why the standard approach for calculating accreditation is not appropriate to reflect school outcomes based on the unique needs of the student population, and outline modified methodology for calculating accreditation to more equitably report school quality. Upon approval by the Virginia Board of Education, the five FCPS special purpose schools will use the modified methodology to determine performance levels for each school quality indicator and define the overall school accreditation for 2023-24, based on outcomes attained in the current school year.

 

Recommendation: 

That the School Board approve the alternative accreditation plan applications for submission to the Virginia Board of Education, as detailed in the agenda item.
