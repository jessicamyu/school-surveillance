Subject: Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC) Annual Report

School Board Action Required: Information

Related to: Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

Key Points:
      The School Board asked the HRAC to review successes and concerns at selected school-based human relations committees, and identify how to improve the distribution of what works at successful schools. Subject: District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education, Title I, Annual Report

School Board Action Require: Information

Related To: Student Achievement Goal

Key Points:

Title I District Advisory Council members have made the following recommendations:
    • Continue to provide guidance and support for parents on how to help their children practice reading, writing, and mathematics skills at home.
    • Use the District Advisory Council (DAC) meetings to provide more information to DAC representatives about family involvement resources available within Fairfax County Public Schools.
    • Provide support for schools in developing or adopting family literacy programs for grades 3-6.
    • Have Title I information for school websites translated into other languages.
    • Provide resources and information to schools to assist them in strengthening relationships with parents as an important support for student achievement.
Recommendation:

That the School Board accept 2008-2009 Annual Report Title I District Advisory Council

Staff Contact: Kim Dockery, assistant superintendent, Special Services

Meeting Type: May 28, 2009 - Work Session No. 115

Subject: School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) 2008-2009 Annual Report

Type: Information

Related to: Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

      The School Health Advisory Committee is mandated by the Commonwealth of Virginia to assist with the development of health policy in the school division and the evaluation of the status of school health, health education, the school environment, and health services.

KEY POINTS:
      SHAC supports and recommends:
          · Continue the collaboration between Fairfax County Public Schools and Fairfax County Health Department and work to formalize the relationship with a Memorandum of Understanding.
          · Increase support for parental education and information regarding cyber bullying and social networking sites.
          · Increase promotion of the Golden Wellness Award to help expand participation of schools.
          · Consider promoting the Northern Virginia Healthy Kids Coalition “9-5-2-1-0 For Health Program” which promotes ways to remind families about five health habits for overall good health and weight.
          · Continue to explore opportunities to increase parental involvement.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board accept the School Health Advisory Committee report.
