Meeting Category: December 14, 2021-Work Session

Summary/Background (Key Points):

In December 2020, the Board directed the Superintendent to explore developing an academy program for the newly named John R. Lewis High School that not only puts the name on the walls of the school, but adopts the principles of his life in a curriculum that focuses on government, public policy, leadership, and human rights; and to begin work on this proposal in the summer of 2021.

Staff will share the exploratory actions undertaken in response to the 12/01/20 Forum, present a conceptual model for instructional programming, and seek Board guidance on next steps for stakeholder engagement and program development.

Recommendation:

That the School Board provide guidance on next steps for stakeholder engagement and program development for the John R. Lewis Leadership Program.

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                 Brian Schoester, coordinator, Facilities Planning Services, Facilities and Transportation Services

                                 Mike Raisor, MGT Consulting

Meeting Category: December 14, 2021 – Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4: Resource Stewardship, as well as Goals 1 (Student Success) and 2 (Caring Culture)

 

 

Summary/Background:

In 2018, the School Board began discussions regarding FCPS Policy 8130, Facilities Planning, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings. The School Board held four work sessions to discuss boundary policy on October 15, 2018, February 25, 2019, March 11, 2019, and July 22, 2019.

 

At the July 22, 2019, work session, the School Board requested the Superintendent hire an outside consultant to work with the Board to identify best practices in boundary policy and engage the community in the discussion.

 

FCPS contracted MGT Consulting Group (MGT), an education consulting firm, in 2020 to conduct this work, which has been delayed due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

 

MGT developed a parent survey and a community survey to engage the community. MGT also held three virtual community meetings to share information about the boundary policy review and collected stakeholder input. The meetings were held on September 13, 14, and 20, 2021. The surveys closed on September 24, 2021.

 

The purpose of the work session is to have MGT present its results on community engagement and best practices review.
