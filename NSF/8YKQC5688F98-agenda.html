Subject:                  Facilities Planning Advisory Council 2012-13 Annual Report to the School Board

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

On September 23, 2010, the School Board approved the formation of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) as a new advisory council of the Board. The mission of the advisory council is to advise and inform the staff and School Board in the development of comprehensive, long term plans for facilities needs in the most effective and efficient way.  FPAC is made up of 13 appointed members – one from each Fairfax County magisterial district, one from the City of Fairfax, and three at-large members.  Facilities-related issues that may be considered by the advisory council may include, but are not limited to:

  • school program capacity
  • enrollment and projections
  • transportation and operating efficiencies related to facilities planning
  • Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) prioritization
  • creative financing and construction strategies
  • scope of renovations
  • school closures and new schools
  • student accommodation planning (building additions/modular relocations/ review of school boundaries)

Key Points:

 

Consistent with the School Board guidelines for advisory committees, FPAC submits its annual report to the School Board, including an executive summary and recommendations, list of meeting dates, attendance reports, and voting records. 

 

The report consolidates and reports progress on the recommendations made throughout the year; no additional recommendations are presented.

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:               Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points: 

 

The ACE Advisory Committee makes the following recommendations:

 

Recommendation 1: Adult ESOL Provide an FCPS Operating Fund transfer for the Adult ESOL program sufficient to meet matching requirements for Federal and State grants while meeting maintenance of effort requirements for grant eligibility.

 

Recommendation 2: Adult High School Completion Initiate a task force to assess what Fairfax County residents are willing to fund for Adult High School Completion and to assure that adequate resources are available for enrollees. Also recommend that changes in the staffing formula for Adult High School Completion, as requested by the Adult High School Completion Program, be implemented.

 

Recommendation 3: Impact of FY 2013 Reorganization Assess, evaluate and quantify the impact of new resource constraints on the delivery, quality and cost of services, based on FY 2013 reorganization.

 

Recommendation 4: Internal Controls Provide additional support and collaboration, especially in the area of information technology from FCPS, for ACE to continue the development of effective internal control procedures to avoid commitment of funds before being assured that funds are actually available.

 

Recommendation 5: Marketing Increase marketing efforts to make county residents aware of career opportunities as economic conditions improve and employment opportunities increase in the county.

 

Recommendation 6: Vertical Program Coordination Continue to coordinate with the K-12 Career and Technical programs which align with the ACE Career and Work Readiness Programs.

 

Recommendation 7: Review of funding for ACE Administrator staff positions Review how local agencies within the Adult Education and Family Literacy Act (AEFLA) Region 8 grant area and throughout the state of Virginia fund the Adult and Community Education administrator positions.

Key Points:

 

Members of the CTEAC are interested in the following and will continue to explore meaningful avenues in the following:

  • Investigating potential methods of marketing the CTE courses and programs to a broader population of students.
  • Researching and discovering areas where the committee can recommend ways to improve communication efforts with students, their parents and the FCPS guidance and school leadership personnel.
  • Members also investigated course delivery methods to consider the viability of including electronic learning for online, hybrid options for CTE courses.
  • Developing recommended CTE programs and academic course sequences to map a student’s curriculum plans.
  • Exploring CTE programs with each of the six CTE program managers in regards to successes, challenges, and plans for future programs/courses.
