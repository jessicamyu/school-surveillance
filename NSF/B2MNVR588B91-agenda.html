Ignite Link: Goal 1: Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

On March 7, 2019, the School Board approved a streamlined Strategic Plan with eight areas of focus for school years 2018-2020. These areas of focus have specific strategic actions to move the Division toward its goals. Each strategic action has targeted metrics to show the Division’s current baseline data, an interim target to achieve by school year 2020, and an aspirational statement that describes what success would look like for students, families, employees, and the community.

This Student Success report is the first report under the new reporting structure and describes actions and outcomes from SY 2017-18, which transpired prior to the recent approval of the enhanced strategic plan. While these actions were not intentionally aligned to the current metrics approved in March 2019, they did further the majority of these new metrics. Future reports will describe intentionally-aligned actions implemented to help the division reach its desired outcomes.

Goal 1 of the Strategic Plan details FCPS’ commitment to Student Success and will focus on:

  • Elimination of Gaps
  • Early Education
  • Portrait of a Graduate Attributes

A presentation and a narrative report will detail the Division’s performance in school year 2017-2018 as it relates to the revised metrics in the Strategic Plan.

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success report as detailed in the agenda item.

Summary/Background:

 

The School Board approved the Fiscal Year (FY) 2020-24 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) on January 25, 2019. The CIP contains a list of Priority Recommended Boundary Adjustments along with potential timeline information. Four Priority Recommended Boundary Adjustments are listed for potential scoping in spring 2019 along with a potential boundary study in fall 2019. The anticipated effective date listed for these boundary adjustments is SY 2020-21. In accordance with School Board Regulation 8130.9 on Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings, a scope of study is being presented to the School Board for approval.

 

Discussion:

 

Four Priority Recommended Boundary Adjustments were identified by the School Board as part of the FCPS FY2020-24 Capital Improvement Program process. These are listed on page 46 of the CIP for potential scoping in spring 2019 along with a potential boundary study in fall 2019. The anticipated effective date listed for these boundary adjustments is SY 2020-21.

 

The four boundary adjustments listed for study, along with their objectives, are as follows:

  • Wakefield Forest ES for Capacity Relief
  • Oak View ES/Olde Creek ES to Eliminate Attendance Island
  • Little Run ES for Capacity Balance
  • Glen Forest Elementary School (ES) for Capacity Relief

 

Staff Recommendation:

 

Staff recommends the following scope for elementary school boundary studies with the middle and high school feeder patterns remaining unchanged:

  • The Wakefield Forest ES, Oak View/Olde Creek ES Boundary Studies be combined as one study and continue to monitor Little Run ES as most recent projections forecast a significant increase. This study should include the following schools: Canterbury Woods ES, Kings Glen ES, Kings Park ES, Little Run ES, Oak View ES, Olde Creek ES, and Wakefield Forest ES
  • The Glen Forest ES Boundary Study to include the following elementary schools: Bailey's ES, Bailey's Upper ES, Glen Forest ES, Parklawn ES
