Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

FY 2022 Advertised Budget

On February 18, 2021, the School Board adopted the FY 2022 Advertised Budget. The budget totaled $3.2 billion, an increase of $75.5 million, or 2.4 percent, over the FY 2021 Approved Budget.

 

For FY 2022, funds are budgeted that will allow FCPS to meet the most pressing needs of the school system in an unprecedented environment. The budget includes a request for an additional $104.4 million, or 4.9 percent, in the County transfer to provide a three percent compensation increase for employees; to continue expanding preschool special education class offerings; to address retirement rate increases and rising health care costs; to expand and support our response to meet student technology, social emotional, and instructional needs arising from the pandemic; and to offset decreasing revenue from state, federal, and other sources.

 

County Budget Impact

The County Executive presented his FY 2022 Advertised Budget Plan on February 23, 2021, which included a School Operating Fund transfer increase of $14.1 million, or 0.7 percent, as compared to FY 2021. As part of the County’s budget markup, additional funding of $15.2 million was recommended for FCPS.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The final state budget results in an increase in state funding of $5.2 million as compared to FCPS’ FY 2022 Advertised Budget.

Subject:  Boundary Adjustment for Elementary Schools in the Justice High School (HS) Pyramid

 

 

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent,

Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

 

 

Meeting Category:  May 20, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings

 

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is considering a boundary adjustment to provide capacity relief to Glen Forest Elementary School (ES) by using available capacity at other elementary schools in the Justice HS pyramid. In School Year (SY) 2019-20, 1,092 students were attending Glen Forest ES, which has a design capacity of 1,344 students and a program capacity of 1,076 students.

 

 

Discussion:

 

The process for boundary adjustments is outlined in Policy 8130 and Regulation 8130.

 

Boundary scoping meetings are the first step in the process once a school has been identified for a boundary adjustment. In June 2019, the school communities of Bailey's ES, Bailey's Upper ES, Glen Forest ES, and Parklawn ES were invited to attend a boundary scoping meeting to learn about the process and provide feedback to staff about the areas that should be considered in the boundary study. As a result of feedback, the School Board amended the scope at the February 24, 2020, work session to include all elementary schools within the Justice HS pyramid. Additional boundary scoping meetings were held with the school communities on March 9 and 10, 2021.

 

The second step in the process included boundary study community meetings, held on April 19 and 21, 2021, where three options were presented to collect feedback for a boundary adjustment recommendation. The three options were informed by the feedback from the previous boundary scoping meetings.

 

Policy 8130.7 addresses Phasing of Adjustments in Section XI and states: “When possible, adjustments under this policy shall be implemented through attrition and phasing. The School Board may approve a grade-by-grade phase-in of adjustments for students beginning with the incoming class at the middle or high school levels, when feasible. The School Board may adopt other phasing plans as appropriate to the individual boundary study.”

 

Prior to voting on the boundary adjustment, the School Board will hold a public hearing for the purpose of obtaining additional public comment on June 8, 2021. The School Board is scheduled for a vote on the boundary adjustment at the regular meeting on June 17, 2021.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

FCPS is recommending that the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for seven elementary schools in the Justice HS pyramid as proposed in slide 11 of the attach presentation. This boundary adjustment will reassign students from Sleepy Hollow ES to Beech Tree ES, Belvedere ES to Bailey’s ES and Bailey’s Upper ES, Parklawn ES to Belvedere ES, and Glen Forest ES to Parklawn ES. The recommendation will affect an estimated 378 students at the elementary school level. Any approved boundary adjustment will not change the middle or high school boundaries.

Recognition of National Gun Violence Awareness Day – June 4, 2021 and Wear Orange Weekend June 4-6, 2021

 

The first Friday in June is recognized as National Gun Violence Awareness Day. Also known as Wear Orange Day, it was first organized by high school students in Chicago to memorialize the 2013 death of their friend and gun violence victim, 15-year-old Hadiya Pendleton. After her death, they asked us to stand up, speak out, and Wear Orange to raise awareness about gun violence. That simple call to action has grown into a national movement.

 

In the US, 3062 children and teens die by guns every year. Firearms are the leading cause of death among children and teens in the United States. One tragic example comes from our own community. In November 2015, Edison High School graduate Joe Bose was a victim of a senseless and random act of gun violence in Norfolk, Virginia where he attended Hampton University. Just a month ago, FCPS suffered yet another senseless tragedy when we lost two South County High School students to gun violence.

 

Every student deserves to learn and grow in an environment that is safe. Sadly, the threat of violence for students – including gun violence – has grown across the country. National Gun Violence Awareness Day is used to raise awareness of the issue and the importance of preventing and eliminating gun violence in schools and communities

 

Millions are coming together to fight for an end to gun violence in America. The School Board recognizes the effort to bring more awareness and remembrance to the victims of gun violence by wearing orange on June 4 for National Gun Violence Awareness Day and joining the VA & DC Statewide Wear Orange Evening of Hope virtual event on June 5 as part of Wear Orange Weekend at wearorange.org.

Subject: Appointment of “individual Trustee” to the Board of Trustees of the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC)

 

 

Staff Contact: Eliazer Martinez, ERFC executive director/CIO

 

Meeting Category: May 20, 2021– Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Ordinance, Ch. 3, Art. 4, Section 3-4-3 (the “Enabling Ordinance”), provides that the ERFC Board of Trustees (“ERFC Board”) shall consist of seven people, one of whom shall be the “individual Trustee.” The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by: Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County. The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six Trustees on the ERFC Board, subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board. Under the Enabling Ordinance, the ERFC Board recommendation must be based on a determination that the “individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.”

 

The ERFC Board unanimously voted to recommend Adam McConagha for appointment as the individual Trustee. Mr. McConagha serves as Managing Director and Co-lead of Origination for Private Equity and Venture Capital at the U.S. International Development Finance Corporation (“DFC”), the U.S.’s development finance institution. At DFC, Mr. McConagha is responsible for overseeing underwriting and strategic initiatives related to DFC’s equity funds and co-investment programs. Prior to joining DFC, Mr. McConagha worked in Asian capital markets as a banker, equity analyst, and trader, and covered the region for nearly two decades at banks in New York and Hong Kong. Mr. McConagha earned B.S. and M.S. degrees from Georgetown University’s School of Foreign Service and an MBA from Georgetown’s School of Business.

 

The Enabling Ordinance provides that the individual Trustee serves on the ERFC Board for a one-year term beginning July 1 of each year, subject to removal by the other Trustees. The ERFC individual Trustee for this term, Michael Burke, died in December 2020, and therefore, was not able to complete his term as the individual Trustee. Because the individual Trustee position is currently vacant, the ERFC Board recommends that the School Board approve the appointment of Mr. McConagha as the individual Trustee to serve for the remaining portion of his predecessor’s term (May 20, 2021 through June 30, 2021).

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the appointment of Adam McConagha to the ERFC Board of Trustees effective May 20, 2021, for the remainder of the current term (through June 30, 2021).

Meeting Category: May 20, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Consent

 

 

Related To: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

On February 4, 2021, the School Board adopted the FY2022-2026 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the referenced five-year period.  Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period. Voter authorization in November 2021 will be necessary in order to issue school bonds. The preliminary expected uses of such proceeds include, without limitation, the following projects:

 

Construction funds for:

 

  • 5 elementary school renovations
  • 1 high school renovation

 

Planning and Design funds for:

  • 7 elementary school renovations
  • 1 high school renovation

 

Site Acquisition funds for:

  • Future Western High School

 

To protect the school division’s capital investment in school facilities and to meet the School Board’s obligations, the adopted FY2022-2026 CIP identified requirements for renovation of certain existing school facilities.  It is the School Board’s commitment that renovations should be programmed to accommodate a 20-25 year cycle instead of our current renovation cycle of 37 years.  It is also the School Board’s commitment that all schools contain facilities necessary to support the entirety of current education programming.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the attached resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and to take all necessary and appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 2021 general election, a School Bond Referendum in the principal amount of $360,000,000.

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: May 20, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

MetroDuct Systems of VA LLC (“MetroDuct”), which is licensed by the Virginia State Corporation Commission to furnish local exchange telecommunications services, seeks a ten foot wide easement along both the east and west sides of Stonecroft Boulevard including on FCPS properties presently used for Westfield High School and for Stonecroft Transportation Center (the “Easements”). These Easements are for the purpose of connecting data centers with telecommunication facilities.

 

To expedite its request, MetroDuct has already tendered payment to FCPS in the total amount of $405,938.75 for the Easements. An independent real estate appraiser engaged by FCPS counsel has confirmed that the offered compensation is within the range of market values for the easements being acquired.

 

The Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (DFTS) has determined that the Easements will have no material negative impact on School Board property and are a technology improvement that is generally beneficial to Northern Virginia. MetroDuct also will be responsible for the appraiser’s fee as well as any outside counsel fees, so that no costs are incurred by FCPS.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve the granting of the Easements and authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities & Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all action necessary and appropriate, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to negotiate, execute, grant and administer on behalf of the Board, the Easements on the property of Westfield High School and Stonecroft Transportation Center and to receive payment thereafter.

5.05 Contract Modification Belleview Medical - COVID 19 Testing Pilot Program
Meeting
May 20, 2021 - Hybrid Regular Meeting
Category
5. Consent Agenda - 9:45 p.m. Our adopted rules of Parliamentary Procedure, Robert's Rules, provide for a consent agenda listing several items for approval of the Board by a single motion.

Subject: Contract Modification Belleview Medical – COVID-19 Testing Pilot Program

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, Chief Operating Officer

Michelle Boyd, Assistant Superintendent Special Services

Lorraine Trouton, Coordinator, School Health Services

Michelle Pratt, Director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category: May 20, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

Ignite Link:     Strategic Plan Goal 4:  Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Contractual Background.  On October 15, 2019, FCPS entered into contract 4400009420 with Belleview Medical Partners, PC for the provision of a licensed, qualified physician for Medical Consultant Services to support the Department of Special Services. Pursuant to that contract, Dr. Vivek Sinha of Belleview Medical Partners provides FCPS with four (4) hours of medical consultation services each month at a cost of $9,800/year.  The contract term expires April 30, 2022, with the option to renew for up to two additional one-year periods. 

 

On February 19, 2021, this contract was modified (via Amendment 1) to add N95 Mask fit testing.  Amendment 1 was entered into in response to guidance from the Fairfax County Health Department (FCHD) stating that any student requiring nebulization treatments would have to be supported by FCPS staff in accordance with the Code of Virginia 22.1-274 and Regulation 2104  ‘Health Services—School Health-Related Needs”.   The cost of the one-time fit testing service provided under Amendment 1 was $50,000.

VDH Pilot Program.  The CDC and the Virginia Department of Health (VDH) recommend diagnostic and screening testing as additional layers of prevention in schools, as feasible. The VDH has launched two VOLUNTARY pilot programs for diagnostic testing and/or screening for COVID-19 in schools. School divisions in Virginia may choose to participate in one or both pilot programs, with VDH providing participating schools with free BinaxNOW tests.

 

FCPS has elected to participate in the VDH Pilot Program for: (a) screening of students for COVID-19; and (b) diagnostic testing of students and staff for COVID-19 at the following schools:  Cameron ES, Hybla Valley ES, Rocky Run MS and Franklin MS.

 

Under the terms of the proposed Amendment 3, Belleview Medical Partners will provide the medical services needed for FCPS to participate in the VDH Pilot Program.  Through Belleview Medical Partners, Dr. Sinha will provide the medical oversight and certificate of waiver from CLIA (the Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments) required for performance of this type of diagnostic medical testing.  The fully-trained Belleview Medical team will conduct screenings on a weekly basis as pre-determined by those families who have consented to have their students participate; and daily diagnostic testing for those families and staff who have consented to participate.  All PPE will be provided by Belleview Medical Partners PC.  Dr. Sinha and his staff will provide on-site consultation, phone, text, email or video calls to FCPS.   In addition, FCPS staff will have the opportunity to observe the process for possible implementation in the Fall.  Negative test results will be reported by Dr. Sinha to VDH, most likely on an aggregate basis. FCPS will follow the current protocols from the VDH and FCHD for positive cases of COVID-19.  

 

Contract Amendment:  The proposed contract modification (Amendment 3) will add medical services needed (as described above) to provide COVID-19 testing at four (4) schools through the end of the school year at a total cost of $85,000.   

 

The total contract value at the time of contract award was $38,800.  Amendment 1 for N95 Fit Testing added $50,000 (new total contract value $88,800).  The contract amendment to add COVID-19 Testing will add an additional $85,000 to the total contract value.  If approved, the new total contract value will be $173,800. 

 

Policy 5015.3 requires School Board approval of any contract or contract amendment for Professional Services where the contract total compensation exceeds $100,000. 

 

Rationale/Justification: As a result of participation in the VDH Pilot Program, FCPS anticipates that it will be better positioned to implement in-school screening and testing in the Fall, as needed. Participation in the VDH Pilot Program is expected to confer health benefits within the participating schools and provide FCPS with a better understanding of the challenges, staff responsibilities, data collection process, reporting requirements, and follow-up necessary to fully operationalize screening and diagnostic testing in the Fall.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the proposed Amendment 3 for COVID-19 Testing Pilot Program in the amount of $85,000 and authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract amendment, as well as the modified contract, on behalf of the School Board.

Subject: Amendment No. 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan as Restated Effective January 1, 2017 (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

Staff Contact: Eliazer Martinez, executive director/CIO, ERFC

 

Meeting Category: May 20, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees recommends that the School Board adopt the attached proposed amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001). The amendments would implement changes permitted under the Setting Every Community Up for Retirement Enhancement Act of 2019 (“SECURE Act”), which relate to the date by which a member must commence benefits under the Plan. The effective date for the proposed amendments is January 1, 2020

 

As permitted under the SECURE Act, the proposed amendments change a Member’s required beginning date under ERFC and ERFC 2001 to the April 1 following the later of the calendar year in which the Member attains age 72, or the calendar year in which the Member retires. The proposed changes would apply to Members who were born after June 30, 1949.

 

The proposed amendment also provides that if a Member who is definitely located fails, following reasonable notification, to elect a type of service Pension benefit and form of benefit by the Member’s required beginning date, that the Member’s Pension benefit, including the form of benefit, will be paid as determined under ERFC regulations. Such regulations will set forth the type of service Pension benefit and form of benefit to be paid.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment No. 2 to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) with an effective date for the Amendment of January 1, 2020.

Subject:  Boundary Adjustment for Elementary Schools in the Justice High School (HS) Pyramid

 

 

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent,

Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

 

 

Meeting Category:  May 20, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings

 

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is considering a boundary adjustment to provide capacity relief to Glen Forest Elementary School (ES) by using available capacity at other elementary schools in the Justice HS pyramid. In School Year (SY) 2019-20, 1,092 students were attending Glen Forest ES, which has a design capacity of 1,344 students and a program capacity of 1,076 students.

 

 

Discussion:

 

The process for boundary adjustments is outlined in Policy 8130 and Regulation 8130.

 

Boundary scoping meetings are the first step in the process once a school has been identified for a boundary adjustment. In June 2019, the school communities of Bailey's ES, Bailey's Upper ES, Glen Forest ES, and Parklawn ES were invited to attend a boundary scoping meeting to learn about the process and provide feedback to staff about the areas that should be considered in the boundary study. As a result of feedback, the School Board amended the scope at the February 24, 2020, work session to include all elementary schools within the Justice HS pyramid. Additional boundary scoping meetings were held with the school communities on March 9 and 10, 2021.

 

The second step in the process included boundary study community meetings, held on April 19 and 21, 2021, where three options were presented to collect feedback for a boundary adjustment recommendation. The three options were informed by the feedback from the previous boundary scoping meetings.

 

Policy 8130.7 addresses Phasing of Adjustments in Section XI and states: “When possible, adjustments under this policy shall be implemented through attrition and phasing. The School Board may approve a grade-by-grade phase-in of adjustments for students beginning with the incoming class at the middle or high school levels, when feasible. The School Board may adopt other phasing plans as appropriate to the individual boundary study.”

 

Prior to voting on the boundary adjustment, the School Board will hold a public hearing for the purpose of obtaining additional public comment on June 8, 2021. The School Board is scheduled for a vote on the boundary adjustment at the regular meeting on June 17, 2021.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

FCPS is recommending that the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for seven elementary schools in the Justice HS pyramid as proposed in slide 11 of the attach presentation. This boundary adjustment will reassign students from Sleepy Hollow ES to Beech Tree ES, Belvedere ES to Bailey’s ES and Bailey’s Upper ES, Parklawn ES to Belvedere ES, and Glen Forest ES to Parklawn ES. The recommendation will affect an estimated 378 students at the elementary school level. Any approved boundary adjustment will not change the middle or high school boundaries.
