Recommended Action
Approve and award a contract to the University of Virginia for the provision of the Accelerated Certification Cohort educational program, effective May 1, 2018 through June 30, 2023, and authorize the Division Superintendent or Deputy Superintendent to execute and administer such contract on behalf of the School Board.

                                                                                                                                                            Updated 6/11/18

 

Subject: Contract Award for Provision of an Accelerated Certification Cohort for FCPS teachers

 

 

Staff Contact: Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Michelle R. Pratt, director, Office of Procurement Services, Kathleen Walts, executive director, Office of Professional Learning & Family Engagement

 

 

Meeting Category: June 28, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal: Goal 4 - Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Office of Professional Learning and Family Engagement recommends that the School Board award a contract to the University of Virginia (UVA) for provision of a customized education program for FCPS teachers and counselors who aspire to be future school administrators.

 

This educational program is known as the Accelerated Certification Cohort (ACC), which is designed to provide FCPS with a highly qualified pipeline of future school administrators (including assistant principals), while also offering an incentivized path for FCPS teachers who wish to become school administrators. Thus, the ACC also serves as a teacher recruitment tool for FCPS.

 

ACC is a one year program which is approved by the Virginia Department of Education for an alternative certificate in administration. At the conclusion of the program, participants receive a Pre K-12 Administration and Supervision endorsement on their Virginia teacher and educator’s license. Approximately, 14-20 FCPS teachers enroll in the ACC each year. The 2018-19 school year will be the 14th year of operation for the ACC program in FCPS.

 

During the course of the program, ACC participants are mentored by experienced school administrators, and develop a project focused on improving student achievement in FCPS schools. All coursework is instructed by UVA faculty. The FCPS Leadership Development Team and UVA integrate a united vision to support alignment and consistency to the program in FCPS, and collaborate to incorporate the foci of the School Board’s Strategic Plan. ACC participants also serve as administrative interns in the FCPS extended school year program.

 

Estimated contract value over the five (5) year term is not expected to exceed $325,000, or $65,000 per academic year. ACC participants are invoiced for, and obligated to pay, 50 percent of the established tuition rate, thereby reducing the amount to be paid by FCPS to UVA in connection with this contract.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve and award a contract to the University of Virginia for the provision of the Accelerated Certification Cohort educational program, effective May 1, 2018 through June 30, 2023, and authorize the Division Superintendent or Deputy Superintendent to execute and administer such contract on behalf of the School Board.

Staff Contact:   Eliazer Martinez, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

 

Meeting Category:  June 28, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Action

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the “individual Trustee.” The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County. The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.

 

At a recent meeting of the ERFC Board of Trustees, Michael Burke was unanimously recommended for appointment as the “individual Trustee.” Mr. Burke is a Managing Director in the Washington, D.C. Private Client Group of Stifel, a wealth management and investment firm. If approved by the School Board, Mr. Burke will serve for his sixth year on the ERFC Board. Mr. Burke meets all of the requirements of the position.
