    SUBJECT: FY 2008 Final Budget Review

    TYPE: Discussion

    BACKGROUND:
        All of the FY 2008 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual independent audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the funds under the control of the School Board. All comparisons are against the FY 2008 estimate.

    KEY POINTS:
        FY 2008 actual revenue was $4.3 million lower than the estimate due to a $3.8 million decrease in sales tax revenue, offset by a $1.3 million increase in state aid; and a net reduction of $8.9 million in federal funding primarily due to $10.1 million in unspent federal grant funds that will be carried forward; and $7.0 million in higher than projected revenue from tuition, fees, and rebates.

        Recommended expenditure adjustments to FY 2009 include $2.7 million for electricity rate increases, $1.2 million for restoring the flexibility reserve, and reductions in central office support to schools to fund the one-time stipend for lower wage employees included in the FY 2009 Approved agenda.

        Funding for energy-related items including fuel, electricity, and other transportation items continues to be a concern for FY 2009. These accounts will be monitored and, if necessary, addressed in the FY 2009 Mid-Year Budget Review.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

      8.04 Discussion - Policy 7325, Video Surveillance
      Meeting
      Jul 14, 2008 - Work Session No. 3-8
      Category
      8. Policy - 4:30 p.m.
      Type
      Discussion



      Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, Acting Chief Financial Officer, Department of Financial Services

      Meeting Type: July 14, 2008 – Work Session No. 8

      SUBJECT: School Board Policy

      TYPE: Action

      BACKGROUND:
          Waiver to Policy 7325, Video Surveillanc, is required to implement a closed circuit TV technology pilot in four high school serving areas and storerooms. The schools would be Mount Vernon, McLean, Robinson, and Lake Braddock Secondary School.
      KEY POINTS:
          The goal is to reduce the estimated $1.2 million loss due to student theft and mitigate food tampering. In a recent high school student survey 8.5 percent of 10,000 students admitted taking food without payment. In the past five years, Food and Nutrition Services has piloted a variety of activities to reduce losses. These included offering free meals to adults in return for lunch line supervision, limiting number of students entering service areas, and posters and public announcements designed by the Fairfax County Public Schools Student Advisory Council. Despite these efforts student theft continues to rise.

          Safety and Security have researched the closed circuit TV technology and the monitors would be placed on each serving line and snack bar. The monitors would be in plain view; students would see their image on the screen while moving through the lines; and the images would not be recorded for review. Cameras would be placed in the storerooms to record presence of unauthorized personnel.

          The purchase of the equipment and installation would be coordinated with Safety and Security to ensure compliance with procurement and maintenance standards. The estimated $60,000 cost would be with Food and Nutrition Services funds.

          When cameras were installed in the school cafeterias in Brevard County, Florida and Duke University in North Carolina, the food service programs experienced an increase in revenue.

      RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board accept the waiver to Policy 7325, Video Surveillance, to permit a pilot for closed circuit TV technology at Mount Vernon and McLean High Schools and Robinson and Lake Braddock Secondary Schools.
