    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

      Contact: Phillip Niedzielski-Eichner, Chairman and Providence District Board Member

      SUBJECT: Award of Contract

      RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board award a contract to The Aspen Group International, Inc., to provide consultation, facilitation, and expert services in support of the design and implementation of a strategic planning and governing approach; that the contract, in the amount of $72,000, be effective upon approval by the School Board and authorized for 12 months through November 2006.

      EXPLANATION:
          Consistent with the high performance expectations that are the underpinning of FCPS’s success to date, the School Board believes that FCPS can and must perform even better in its service to its students and the community. This expectation is true for both academic programming and the management of non-academic operations. The Board also believes Fairfax children will experience a future that will be dramatically different than the world experienced by today’s adults – for example, their day-to-day personal and working lives will be more global in scope -- and that our programming must help prepare them for this reality. The School Board is therefore engaged in an intensive exercise of setting strategic goals, strengthening accountability systems, and clarifying the respective roles of the Board and the Superintendent in governing the complex FCPS education enterprise. A key component of this effort will be to engage the community in a dialogue about its expectations and hopes for its public school system.
          Over the past four months, the School Board has been exploring different governing models, while drafting new vision, mission, and goal statements, to provide a more concentrated focus on student achievement and to establish clearer accountability through monitoring goals and regular reporting on progress made. The Aspen Group has extensive experience working with school boards across the nation in facilitating group discussions about issues such as identifying Board roles and responsibilities, defining monitoring reports, and developing a community engagement plan for strategic Board communications .

      4.03 High School Course Offerings - Approve changes to the list of high school course offerings for the 2006-2007 school year, as recommended by the High School Course Review Committee, and as detailed in the agenda ite (IS; presented as new business 11/3/05)
      Meeting
      Nov 15, 2005 - Regular Meeting No. 8

      4. Action Items - 7:40 p.m.

      Action (Consent)

      Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

      SUBJECT: High School Course Offerings

      RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board approve changes to the list of high school course offerings for the 2006-2007 school year, as recommended by the High School Course Review Committee, and as detailed in the agenda item.

      EXPLANATION:
            A. Standard and optional courses for high schools are reviewed each year, and new proposals are submitted to the High School Course Review Committee for review and recommendations to the Superintendent and School Board. This process is outlined in Regulation 3202.5, Approval of New Courses and Deletion and Consolidation of Existing Courses.
            B. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as additions to the list of standard course offerings for the 2006-2007 school year. (See Attachment A for course descriptions):
                1. Pharmacy Tech
                Rationale: This course is designed for students interested in pursuing a pharmaceutical career. This course specifically addresses the career path of pharmacy technician. Upon completion, students will be eligible to earn certification as a pharmacy technician.

                2. Construction Management
                Rationale: This course is designed for students interested in pursuing a career in construction management. The course is designed as a follow-up to related curricula. It will prepare students with entry-level knowledge and for post-secondary education in the field.

                3. Landscape Architecture and Turf Management
                Rationale: This course is designed for students interested in pursuing a career in landscape architecture or turf management. This course provides students with entry-level knowledge and prepares students for post-secondary education in the field.
            C. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as additions to the list of optional course offerings for the 2006-2007 school year (see Attachment B for course descriptions):
                1. Animation
                Rationale: The course is designed for students who seek specialization in art and animation. Currently Computer Graphics courses do not allow for in-depth development and creation of 2D and 3D animation. A full year of study is necessary to develop knowledge and skills in this field.

                2. Law in Action
                Rationale: This course provides practical information and problem-solving opportunities regarding the law. Students develop knowledge of law and learn skills necessary for survival in our law-based society. Students in the course engage in active learning through real-life experiences such as mock trials, case studies, and simulations. This course may be offered as either a year-long or semester elective for 11th and 12th graders.
                3. Applied Statistics
                Rationale: This year-long course is a project-based introduction to statistics that emphasizes working with data and statistical ideas. All computational work and graphs are computer-based using Minitab statistical software. Assessment is project-based with students demonstrating proficiency by producing professional quality statistical analyses and documents.

            D. The following course was recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as a year long dual credit course taught in partnership with James Madison University (JMU) and as a pilot course at Herndon High School with an evaluation of its success at the end of both the first and second years. (See Attachment C for course description):
                Geospatial Analysis
                Rationale: Students will engage in classroom study involving spatial reasoning and decision-making and they will perform community-based research projects. The final for the course will unite students, teachers, and community leaders as students present their research projects. JMU will be a partner in the endeavor and provide technical consulting as well as training in project identification and definition. Students will receive JMU credit with a reduced fee structure provided by the university. The course will be open to seniors who have previously completed 3 lab science courses.

      Attachment B: Course Descriptions of Recommended optional course offerings
      Attachment C: Course Description of Recommended dual credit course taught in partnership with James Madison University. This is recommended as a pilot course offering at Herndon High School.

        Meeting
        Nov 15, 2005 - Regular Meeting No. 8
        Category
        6. New Business - 7:50 p.m.
        Type
        Information

        REVISED 11/11/05

        Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

        SUBJECT: Award of Contract


        RECOMMENDATION:

          That the School Board award the contract for emergency power upgrades at Chantilly and Marshall High Schools, in support of the FY 2004 State Homeland Security Grant Program, to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder(s) and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.

        EXPLANATION:
          Fairfax County emergency plans call for the potential use of schools as shelter sites, managed by the American Red Cross.  In the past, such as during hurricane Isabel, it was determined that the schools’ emergency electrical systems did not meet the operational needs of the shelter.  Normally, only life-safety systems are connected to emergency generators.  Shelter operations require many other circuits to be operational, such as lighting, HVAC, food service, etc.  Fairfax County has recognized this logistical need and approved this project through the Fairfax County Emergency Management Committee.  The initiative will provide for the expansion of emergency electrical generation for Chantilly and Marshall High Schools.
         
          Funding for this project will be provided from the FY 2004 State Homeland Security Grant.  Specifications have been prepared and the project has been advertised.  Bids are due to be received on November 22, 2005.  A bid tabulation and recommendation for award(s) will be furnished prior to the December 1, 2005, School Board meeting.
