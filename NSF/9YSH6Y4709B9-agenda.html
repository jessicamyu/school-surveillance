School Board Action Required:   Information

Key Points:

At the November 16, 2015 School Board Forum, consensus was reached to propose a revision to Policy 8170.5, Naming School Facilities and Dedicating Areas of School Facilities or Grounds.  The Board will consider adding a sentence to the existing policy to enable school names to be changed for a compelling need other than a change in purpose or function of the facility.  The following sentence is proposed to be added to the end of the policy:

This sentence is the only change proposed to the policy.  The remainder of the policy is unchanged.

Staff Contact:   Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category: December 3, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 9

 

Subject: Proposed Restatements of the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:   LEGAL REQUIREMENT

 

 

Key Points: 

The ERFC Trustees have periodically submitted the plan documents to the Internal Revenue Service for a determination that the Plan continues to be qualified for favorable tax treatment under the Internal Revenue Code.  By obtaining such a determination, the Trustees receive assurance that the Trust Fund does not have to pay income tax on its investment earnings and that the members do not have to pay tax on the benefits they are earning until they actually retire and begin to receive benefits.  The Internal Revenue Service has set January 31, 2016, as the deadline for governmental plans to submit plans for review in Cycle E.   Fairfax County Public Schools may submit the plan for review by the IRS in Cycle E by filing a determination letter application for the plan by January 31, 2016.

 

The IRS requires, for purposes of such a submission, that the plan documents be “restated” to incorporate all amendments that have been adopted since the last time that the plan documents were submitted to the IRS.  TheERFC and ERFC 2001 plan documents were last submitted to the IRS in 2011.  Since then, the School Board has approved 4 separate sets of amendments.   

 

Restated plan documents have been prepared.  These proposed restatements, which are attached, incorporate amendments 1 through 4.

 

 

 

Recommendation:  That the School Board approve proposed restated plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001 for submission to the Internal Revenue Service with the application for a current determination letter.

Subject:                       FY 2016 Midyear Budget Review          

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, funds available are decreasing by $0.3 million.  The FY 2016 beginning balance is increasing by $2.5 million due to final year-end adjustments made after the FY 2015 Final Budget Review. The adjustments include an increase of $0.9 million in sales tax revenue based on actual receipts; a $0.2 million increase in other revenue; and expenditure decreases totaling $1.4 million.  In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2016 revenue adjustments include a decrease in state aid of $4.0 million based on updated enrollment numbers resulting in lower projected average daily membership (ADM); an increase of $1.1 million for City of Fairfax tuition due to an increase in the City’s ADM for FY 2015; and a net increase of $0.1 million in grant revenue. 

 

Recommended FY 2016 expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $0.3 million resulting primarily from savings in instructional materials and supplies based on lower than projected enrollment growth this year, offset by a net increase in grant expenditures. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment. Also attached is an update on the status on grants for FY 2015 and FY 2016.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
