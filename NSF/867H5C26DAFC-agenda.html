    Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month

    Americans of Asian and Pacific ancestry have made significant contributions to the history and development of the United States in many fields of endeavor such as science, commerce, the arts, and the humanities. The Fairfax County School Board recognizes that an understanding of these contributions should be an integral part of the year-round education of all students.

    The President of the United States proclaims May to be Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month and has chosen this month in recognition of the work done by Chinese immigrants on the transcontinental railroad, which was completed on May 10, 1869.

    Pride in America Month - May 2007

    Our country’s founders–more than 200 years ago–established a nation of unique, diverse individuals working toward the common goal of a free and open society–a nation that has accomplished much in two centuries and of which we all can be proud. Thomas Jefferson understood that we, as a people, do have a common heritage in which we can and should, as individuals, develop pride and discipline, and Jefferson envisioned what our nation’s common heritage truly is when he wrote, “All men are created equal and are endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable rights."

    Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., understood and believed this when he proclaimed, “I have a dream. . .” recognizing that the principles about which our country's founders wrote were and are the cornerstone of equality and freedom and that these principles form our nation’s common heritage for its people.

    The Fairfax County School Board recognizes May as Pride in America Month and everyone in Fairfax County Public Schools expresses strong support and deep gratitude to the men and women of our armed forces, and to the many Virginia-based loved ones of our military, including especially the children of military parents enrolled in Fairfax County Public Schools, for their courage, service, and sacrifice during this time

    School Nurse Day - May 9, 2007

    School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.

    School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health and safety, intervene with actual and potential health problems, and provide case management services. They reassure parents that their children’s health needs and injuries are appropriately handled during the school day and allow teachers to devote their time to educating students by taking care of any health care needs or emergencies that may arise. School nurses are dedicated individuals who work in cooperation with families, teachers, and school administrators.

    The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognizes May 9 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

      Establishing positive relationships with students and staff

      Acting with valor

      Other contributions that merit recognition

    “THE WENDELL G. BYRD GYMNASIUM”

    WHEREAS, Wendell G. Byrd served as a second grade teacher at Hutchison Elementary School for 31 years, as assistant basketball coach at South Lakes High School for 5 years, and as the head coach for 23 years, through the 2006-2007 season, with a record of 438-152; and

    WHEREAS, Mr. Byrd made invaluable contributions to Hutchison, South Lakes, and the community as a whole through his Readers Are Leaders mentor program, which paired high school students with elementary school students, his Summer Basketball Camp, which has educated hundreds of boys and girls about both the sport and sportsmanship, and his volunteer work in establishing and continuing to contribute to the Herndon-Reston YMCA; and

    WHEREAS, he consistently sought to meet and serve the needs of all students and players with whom he worked and to ensure that every child received an equal opportunity to succeed; and

    WHEREAS, Mr. Byrd received the 1990 Disney Teacher Award, countless Coach of the Year awards, and the 2007 Best of Reston Award recognizing his achievements in service to his community; and

    WHEREAS, he has dedicated himself to making a positive impact on the lives of others and has served as a role model by virtue of his strong moral code, his work ethic, his commitment to education, his sense of justice, and his high standard of sportsmanship on and off the court and has earned respect, affection, and gratitude from the students, athletes, parents, coaches, and staff members of South Lakes High School and its extended community;

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the gymnasium at South Lakes High School “The Wendell G. Byrd Gymnasium” in honor of Mr. Byrd’s exemplary contributions to South Lakes students and the South Lakes community for over two decades.

    2.06 Resolution Supporting the County-Wide Initiative to Reduce Underage Drinking
    Meeting
    Apr 26, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 18
    Category
    2. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m. - Dan Storck, Chairman
    Type

    RESOLUTION
    SUPPORTING COUNTYWIDE INITIATIVE
    TO REDUCE UNDERAGE DRINKING


    WHEREAS, alcohol is the single most used drug by young people, and the acting surgeon general, Dr. Kenneth Moritsugu, in March 2007, called on citizens to do more to keep America’s 11 million underage drinkers from continuing to use alcohol and to stop other youth from starting in the first place; and

    WHEREAS, based on the 2005 Fairfax County Youth Survey, one in every two high school seniors has consumed alcohol in the last 30 days, and one in every four seniors has reported binge drinking in the last two weeks; and 45 percent of the male and 35 percent of the female respondents who report an age of first use tried alcohol at age 12 or younger; and 19.9 percent of those 16 and older have driven a car after drinking; and

    WHEREAS, Virginia law states that it is illegal for anyone under age 21 to buy, consume, or possess alcohol, subject to penalties including fines, loss of driver’s license, and up to 12 months in jail, and an adult providing alcohol to a person under the age of 21, other than his or her own child in his or her own home, can face a $2,500 fine per young person served in addition to losing his or her driver’s license and/or face up to 12 months in jail; and

    WHEREAS, the 19 FCPS school-community coalitions have brought together concerned residents, school officials, county government agencies, local law enforcement groups, the County Council of PTAs, Northern Virginia MADD, the Fairfax Partnership for Youth, Fairfax-Falls Church Community Services Board, faith community leaders, and INOVA HealthSource to support a countywide initiative to reduce underage drinking; and

    WHEREAS, the Countywide Initiative to Reduce Underage Drinking is launching a new campaign, known as “Parents Who Host Lose the Most: Don’t be a party to teenage drinking”;

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board supports the school-community coalitions’ Countywide Initiative to Reduce Underage Drinking and the “Parents Who Host Lose the Most ” public awareness campaign to be conducted during the months of May and June 2007.

    4.03 Proposed 2007 School Bond Referendum - Adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 6, 2007, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $365,200,000 (FTS; presented as new business 4/12/07; work session held 4/23/07; public hearing held 4/26/07)
    Meeting
    Apr 26, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 18
    Category
    4. Action Items
    Type
    Action


    Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services
    SUBJECT: Proposed 2007 School Bond Referendum


    RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 6, 2007, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $365,200,000.


    EXPLANATION:
          On January 25, 2007, the School Board adopted the FY2008-2012 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the coming five-year period. Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period.

          Voter authorization to issue bonds is needed in November 2007 to provide construction funds for two new elementary schools, a permanent addition to one elementary school, and modular additions to four existing schools. Funds are also included to support program enhancements such as Full-day Kindergarten and High Schools of the Future. Also included are funds for an addition to the Pimmit Alternative High School and planning funds for a West County Adult Education facility.

          The recommended 2007 referendum would provide funds to renovate older general education facilities, including six elementary schools and one middle school. The proposed referendum also includes funds to plan the renovation of two elementary schools and two high schools. These planning funds will prepare the school system to accelerate the renovations at such time as construction funds become available.

          The recent unprecedented inflation in construction costs has made it necessary to seek additional bond authorization for 15 construction projects approved in previous school bond referenda. The recommended 2007 referendum includes funds to offset the inflation adjustment for these projects.

          The proposed referendum also includes funds to continue upgrades of technology networks in our school facilities. Additional allowances have been made in the referendum for the following: roof and HVAC replacement; Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) improvements; and asphalt repaving. Funds supporting a cooperative effort between Fairfax County Public Schools and the Fairfax County government to provide a school bus service facility are included in the proposed Bond Referendum. It should be noted that the debt service for this facility will be paid by the county government and will not impact the $155 million annual spending cap for school capital improvements. Finally, the proposed package includes funds for bonding expenses and the cost of directing the design and construction of projects authorized through the referendum.

          The scale and content of this referendum have been significantly influenced by two considerations: the requirement to address overcrowding, as well as, renovation and infrastructure needs countywide; and current limits on the county’s ability to cash-flow debt.

          To facilitate the orderly financing of identified capital improvement needs, it is recommended that the Proposed 2007 School Bond Referendum be offered for voter approval in the November 6, 2007, general election. It is believed that this proposal is a well-balanced presentation of needs countywide. A School Board work session on this proposal will be held on April 23, 2007, a public hearing and School Board action is scheduled for April 26, 2007.

    SUBJECT: Legislative Recommendations for 2008

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board accept the legislative recommendations to be forwarded to the VSBA Legislative, Policies, and Resolutions Committee for their consideration, as detailed in the agenda item.

    EXPLANATION:
        Each spring the VSBA Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee seeks recommendations from local school boards concerning new or revised legislative policies and resolutions to be considered for VSBA approval. The Committee reviews the local school boards’ recommendations along with other suggestions for changes to the existing policies and resolutions. The VSBA takes action on the Committee’s report at the annual fall convention. Staff recently learned that position recommendations are due to VSBA May 7, 2007.

        This process allows the School Board to make recommendations for the VSBA legislative program and to receive statewide support for some of its own legislative positions. The Fairfax County School Board 2008 Legislative Program will be developed for adoption in the fall.

        Changes are proposed to positions regarding the Federal No Child Left Behind Act, Standards of Quality; Students with Disabilities-Cooperative Services; and the Composite Index.

        The recommended changes are all directly based on policy positions the School Board has already adopted in its 2007 Legislative Program.
