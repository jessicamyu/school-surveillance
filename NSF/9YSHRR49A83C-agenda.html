Autism Awareness Month - April 2016

 

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.

Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about and acceptance of autism. Sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA), this year’s goal is to encourage friends and collaborators to work towards acceptance and appreciation of the unique aspects and talents of all people.  Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

 

 

Mathematics Awareness Month - April 2016

Mathematics Awareness Month is held each year in April to increase public understanding of and appreciation for mathematics. Sponsored by the Joint Policy Board for Mathematics, the goal is to increase the visibility of mathematics as a field of study and to communicate the power and intrigue in mathematics to a larger audience.  The 2016 theme is “The Future of Prediction,” a look at how math and statistics are the future of prediction and how they provide insights and drive innovation.

Staff Contact: Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

 

Meeting Category: March 31, 2016 – Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject: Teacher Compensation and FY 2017 Budget

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

On February 4, 2016, the School Board adopted the FY 2017 Advertised Budget that includes an initial investment of $40 million in the teacher salary scales to begin to address the findings from the external compensation study. This study showed FCPS is significantly below market on teacher salaries and total compensation (salaries and benefits).

 

The March 14, 2016, work session included discussion of options for allocating this first investment in teacher salaries to best address the areas of greatest concern. Consensus was reached to target the areas of greatest need. During this work session, various models will be presented to inform the implementation of this initial investment as part of the FY 2017 budget process.
