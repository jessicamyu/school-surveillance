2.06 School Board Character Award Recognition
Meeting
Jun 17, 2021 - Hybrid Regular Meeting
Category
2. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board, Chris Giovarelli, and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. The Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation has generously funded the $500 award since 2007. The award is given to a junior or senior who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for students in Fairfax County Public Schools in day-to-day behavior.


The recipient of the 2021 School Board Character award is Gurleen Kaur, a senior at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology.


Gurleen is a powerful source of inspiration for the TJHSST community. She is extremely intelligent, compassionate, and incredibly dedicated to nurturing the less fortunate. She devotes countless volunteer hours weekly helping others. Gurleen assisted in establishing a $2,000 scholarship program for low-income students; coordinated volunteers for Student Diversity Initiative/Outreach Club that host 10+ events yearly; tutored and mentored 1-2 students a year in Learning and Inquiry Through Fellowship and Tutoring (LIFT) on Saturdays; was a facilitator and then camp director for Kids are Scientist Too (KAST) that encourage STEM; and she is also deeply interested in environmental sciences and shares her passion with 2nd-5th grade students through Marine and Geoscience: Novel interactions For Youth (MAGNIFY).

 

Gurleen has served as the president of the Humanitarian Aid to North Korea Club; co-wrote a proposal to the School Board advocating for changes to the Advanced Academics Program (AAP) admission process; volunteered for Magic of Mothers that provides STEM opportunities to students from low-income communities; coordinated the annual STEMFest that provides fair like STEM activities to students from over 70 elementary; and she has left her mark on countless other projects.

 

Gurleen is a dedicated student who has always faced challenges as an opportunity to inspire others. She possesses a positive spirit and strives to create an inclusive community. She is an inspiration to all who meet her. Gurleen has earned and truly deserving of the 2021 School Board Character Award.

Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship. In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually. We appreciate their continued generosity.

 

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

 

The School Board will recognize Madeline Miles from Centreville High School, the recipient of the 2021 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.

2.08 LGBTQIA+ Pride Month Recognition
Meeting
Jun 17, 2021 - Hybrid Regular Meeting
Category
2. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.

June marks the anniversary of the first LGBTQIA+ Pride Parade more than fifty years ago, which served as a call to collective action just one year after the Stonewall riots where LGBTQIA+ people, led by transgender women of color like Marsha P. Johnson and Sylvia Rivera, resisted police harassment and violence during an early morning bar raid in New York City.

 

The decades since have been one of struggle and celebration for the LGBTQIA+ community. Despite increased understanding, LGBTQIA+ young people still face many challenges. Without accepting families, they are far more likely to attempt suicide or experience homelessness.

 

In fact, recent studies show most LGBTQIA+ middle and high school students in Virginia say they have been verbally harassed because of their sexual orientation and often hear negative remarks about transgender people in school. Additionally, about ten percent of LGBTQIA+ middle and high school students in Virginia report experiencing physical assaults because of their LGBTQIA+ status. Students of color are disproportionately impacted by anti-LGBTQIA+ hostility, with recent surveys showing large percentages of Latinx and Black LGBTQIA+ middle and high school students experiencing bullying.

 

The Fairfax County School Board celebrates its LGBTQIA+ students and staff, recognizes the urgency of its charge to foster a responsive, caring, and inclusive culture where all feel valued, supported, and hopeful, and advances policies that seek to protect LGBTQIA+ students and staff and enhance their experience in Fairfax County Public Schools.

 

Furthermore, the Fairfax County School Board appreciates the many contributions made by LGBTQIA+ students, staff, and families to the vibrancy of the broader school community. Fairfax County has a richly diverse LGBTQIA+ community that includes people from all walks of life who should be able to live without fear of prejudice, discrimination, harassment, or violence.

 

On behalf of the students, educators, and families of Fairfax County Public Schools, the Fairfax County School Board proclaims June 2021 as LGBTQIA+ Pride Month in Fairfax County Public Schools. In the spirit of One Fairfax, the Fairfax County School Board urges all to respect and honor our diverse community, and to build a culture of inclusivity and equity, not only during LGBTQIA+ Pride Month but throughout the entire year as well.

2.09 Juneteenth Recognition
Meeting
Jun 17, 2021 - Hybrid Regular Meeting
Category
2. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type

 

The month of June is the time that many in our nation, recognize Juneteenth as the holiday that celebrates Emancipation Day. Juneteenth marks a day in 1865 when enslaved Texans learned they would be free—two months after Robert E. Lee surrendered and ended the Civil War and two and a half years after President Abraham Lincoln issued the Emancipation Proclamation. June 19th also marks the day that African Americans in the southern states exercised independence from those who benefitted from their enslavement and labor in the founding of this nation.

 

 

The educational institutions of this nation, including Fairfax County Public Schools has a particular responsibility to recognize the painful realities of our history and to make sure that our curriculum reflects these realities. Working with the community as One Fairfax, this Board is committed to identifying actionable items to improve policies and procedures to enhance access to opportunities to ensure that students, families, and staff do not experience inequities in FCPS.

 

We value diversity and are united in our opposition to racism and hate. We stand in solidarity with our African American students, educators, staff, and their families. Fairfax County Public Schools hereby officially commemorates Juneteenth as a day to celebrate and emphasize a collective call to action against injustice of any kind.

Subject:  Boundary Adjustment for Elementary Schools in the Justice High School (HS) Pyramid

 

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

 

Meeting Category:  June 17, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required:  Action

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is considering a boundary adjustment to provide capacity relief to Glen Forest Elementary School (ES) by using available capacity at other elementary schools in the Justice HS pyramid. In School Year (SY) 2019-20, 1,092 students were attending Glen Forest ES, which has a design capacity of 1,344 students and a program capacity of 1,076 students.

 

Discussion:

 

The process for boundary adjustments is outlined in Policy 8130 and Regulation 8130.

 

Boundary scoping meetings are the first step in the process once a school has been identified for a boundary adjustment. In June 2019, the school communities of Bailey's ES, Bailey's Upper ES, Glen Forest ES, and Parklawn ES were invited to attend a boundary scoping meeting to learn about the process and provide feedback to staff about the areas that should be considered in the boundary study. As a result of feedback, the School Board amended the scope at the February 24, 2020, work session to include all elementary schools within the Justice HS pyramid. Additional boundary scoping meetings were held with the school communities on March 9 and 10, 2021.

 

The second step in the process included boundary study community meetings, held on April 19 and 21, 2021, where three options were presented to collect feedback for a boundary adjustment recommendation. The three options were informed by the feedback from the previous boundary scoping meetings.

 

Policy 8130.7 addresses Phasing of Adjustments in Section XI and states: “When possible, adjustments under this policy shall be implemented through attrition and phasing. The School Board may approve a grade-by-grade phase-in of adjustments for students beginning with the incoming class at the middle or high school levels, when feasible. The School Board may adopt other phasing plans as appropriate to the individual boundary study.”

 

Prior to voting on the boundary adjustment, a recommendation for a boundary change was presented to the School Board on May 20, 2021. The School Board held a public hearing for the purpose of obtaining additional public comment on June 8, 2021. The recommendation has been revised.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for seven elementary schools in the Justice HS pyramid as proposed in the attached document. This boundary adjustment will reassign students from Sleepy Hollow ES to Beech Tree ES, Belvedere ES to Bailey’s ES and Bailey’s Upper ES, Parklawn ES to Belvedere ES, Glen Forest ES to Parklawn ES, and Glen Forest ES to Sleepy Hollow ES. The boundary change will affect an estimated 497 students at the elementary school level. Any approved boundary adjustment will not change the middle or high school boundaries.

 

Motions:

 

I move to adopt the revised FCPS recommendation as posted in the attachment. This would adjust the boundaries of Bailey’s, Bailey’s Upper, Beech Tree, Belvedere, Glen Forest, Parklawn, and Sleepy Hollow elementary schools. The adjustment will not change the middle and high school boundaries.

 

 

  

I move to adopt the following phasing plan to implement the boundary change:

Rising Kindergarten and 1st grade students will be reassigned to attend the newly assigned school for the 2021-22 school year.  

Rising 2nd, 3rd, 4th, and 5th grade students have the option to attend the newly assigned school or remain at their prior assigned school for the 2021-22 school year and until they matriculate to middle school.

Carrie Reynolds, coordinator, Health, Physical, Family Life, and Driver Education, Office of PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category: June 17, 2021 – Regular Meeting

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In accordance with regulation 1708, the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee (FLECAC) convenes each school year with the purpose of advising the K-12 health and physical education and FLE curriculum coordinator on Program of Studies development and implementation. The role of the committee is to provide advice and recommendations to the curriculum coordinator as goals, objectives, media, and special instructional materials are developed. FLECAC recommendations require approval by the School Board following a 30-day community review and comment period. FLECAC includes members representing students, teachers, administrators, and community members (including School Board appointees, health professionals, and clergy).

 

Following guidelines from the Virginia Board of Education, FCPS implements a locally developed program of instruction for Family Life Education (FLE). A community involvement team must be used in the development of the program. The Virginia Board of Education Guidelines and Standards of Learning for Family Life Education are used by FLECAC as a reference for recommendations of lesson objectives and descriptive statements. Community members may also bring forward recommendations and concerns for consideration by FLECAC. In addition, some topics including but not limited to benefits, challenges, responsibilities, and value of marriage; dating violence; sexual harassment; and boundaries and consent, are legislated and must be included in FLE instruction. FLECAC receives input from medical professionals, law enforcement, faith community representatives, and Fairfax County Government offices as appropriate when developing recommendations.

 

During SY2019-2020, FLECAC focused on aligning FCPS objectives with Virginia Department of Education recommendations for Family Life Education at the early elementary and middle school levels, adding a new objective to the modified FLE lessons for students with low incidence disabilities in grades 1-6 and 7-12, and adding resources to support instruction. The committee made six recommendations for School Board consideration:

  1. Revise descriptive statement for kindergarten lesson on families to be more inclusive of different family structures.
  2. Revise descriptive statement for grade 1 lesson on families to be more inclusive of different family structures.
  3. Revise descriptive statement for grade 7 lesson to remove depression and suicide as these topics are now included in the 2020 SOLs for Health Education at grade 7.
  4. Add an objective to modified FLE grades 1-6 related to safety when using technology.
  5. Add an objective to modified FLE grades 7-12 related to appropriate workplace behaviors.
  6. Add media to grade 8 to support instruction on sexting.

 

During SY2020-2021, FLECAC discussed concerns brought forward by community members including revision of lesson descriptive statements in grades 7, 8, and 10. The committee made three recommendations for School Board consideration:

  1. Revised descriptive statement for grade 7 lesson on reproductive systems to include primary amenorrhea.
  2. Revised descriptive statement for grade 8 lesson on anatomy and reproduction to include primary and secondary amenorrhea.
  3. Revised descriptive statement for grade 10 lesson on unplanned pregnancy to be more sensitive to families who have adopted a child.

 

Community Review: Community review of all new Family Life Education (FLE) materials was conducted online from May 7 to June 7. All comments were received through email. Summary and narratives of all comments received are provided.

 

Recommendation: Recommendation to approve recommended media and lesson objectives as detailed in the agenda item and as reviewed and recommended by the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee.

Subject: Amendment No. 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan as Restated Effective January 1, 2017 (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

Staff Contact: Eliazer Martinez, executive director/CIO, ERFC

 

Meeting Category: June 17, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees recommends that the School Board adopt the attached proposed amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001). The amendments would implement changes permitted under the Setting Every Community Up for Retirement Enhancement Act of 2019 (“SECURE Act”), which relate to the date by which a member must commence benefits under the Plan. The effective date for the proposed amendments is January 1, 2020

 

As permitted under the SECURE Act, the proposed amendments change a Member’s required beginning date under ERFC and ERFC 2001 to the April 1 following the later of the calendar year in which the Member attains age 72, or the calendar year in which the Member retires. The proposed changes would apply to Members who were born after June 30, 1949.

 

The proposed amendment also provides that if a Member who is definitely located fails, following reasonable notification, to elect a type of service Pension benefit and form of benefit by the Member’s required beginning date, that the Member’s Pension benefit, including the form of benefit, will be paid as determined under ERFC regulations. Such regulations will set forth the type of service Pension benefit and form of benefit to be paid.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment No. 2 to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) with an effective date for the Amendment of January 1, 2020.

School system needs for these programs have only increased since this instructional facility was first leased in 2000 with no corresponding space increases in the last 21-years.  Neither FCPS nor Fairfax County facilities are available to meet this need.  The expansion needs for all these instructional and services programs could be as much as 30,000 square feet.

Staff Contact:  Eliazer Martinez, ERFC executive director/CIO, ERFC

 

Meeting Category:     June 17, 2021– Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required:   Decision

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Ordinance, Ch. 3, Art. 4, Section 3-4-3 (the “Enabling Ordinance”), provides that the ERFC Board of Trustees (“ERFC Board”) shall consist of seven people, one of whom shall be the “individual Trustee.”  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by: Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six Trustees on the ERFC Board, subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board.  Under the Enabling Ordinance, the ERFC Board recommendation must be based on a determination that the “individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.”

 

The ERFC Board unanimously voted to recommend Adam McConagha for appointment as the individual Trustee.  Mr. McConagha serves as Managing Director and Co-lead of Origination for Private Equity and Venture Capital at the U.S. International Development Finance Corporation (“DFC”), the U.S.’s development finance institution.  At DFC, Mr. McConagha is responsible for overseeing underwriting and strategic initiatives related to DFC’s equity funds and co-investment programs.  Prior to joining DFC, Mr. McConagha worked in Asian capital markets as a banker, equity analyst, and trader, and covered the region for nearly two decades at banks in New York and Hong Kong.  Mr. McConagha earned B.S. and M.S. degrees from Georgetown University’s School of Foreign Service and an MBA from Georgetown’s School of Business. The School Board recently approved Mr. McConagha’s appointment to the ERFC Board of Trustees as the individual Trustee to serve for the remaining portion of his predecessor’s term (May 20, 2021 through June 30, 2021).

 

Recommendation:  That the School Board approve the appointment of Adam McConagha to the ERFC Board of Trustees for the one-year term beginning July 1, 2021

Subject:  Alternative Accreditation Plan Application to the Virginia Board of Education

 

Staff Contact: Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

 

Other Staff Present: Mark Greenfelder, assistant superintendent, Department of School Improvement and Supports (DSIS)

                                          Bettrys Huffman, director, Office of Assessment and Reporting, DSIS

 

Meeting Category: Regular Meeting – June 17, 2021

 

School Board Action Required: New Business

 

Ignite Link: Goal 1: Student Success

 

Related to: Standards of Accreditation (8 VAC 20-131-10)

 

Summary/Background:

 

State Superintendent’s Memo #082-21 invited divisions to request alternative accreditation plans for special purpose schools’ accreditation ratings in 2022-23, based on outcomes attained in the upcoming 2021-22 school year. Five (5) special purpose schools in FCPS are eligible to apply for renewed alternative accreditation plans: Key Center School, Kilmer Center School, Fairfax County Adult High School, Bryant High School, Mountain View High School.

 

Since 2009, FCPS has leveraged flexibility within the Standards of Accreditation (8 VAC 20-131-420.D) to request alternative accreditation plans for its special purpose schools, which are designated by the Virginia Department of Education as the “reporting” or “responsible” school to serve special student populations from across the division. Current alternative accreditation plans for FCPS special purpose schools were approved by the Virginia Board of Education on January 23, 2020, affecting accreditation ratings through 2021-22, based on school outcomes attained through 2020-21.

 

Alternative accreditation plan applications describe the special population served by each school, explain why the standard approach for calculating accreditation is not appropriate to reflect school outcomes based on the unique needs of the student population, and outline modified methodology for calculating accreditation to more equitably report school quality. Upon approval by the Virginia Board of Education, the five FCPS special purpose schools will use the modified methodology to determine performance levels for each school quality indicator and define the overall school accreditation for 2022-23, based on outcomes attained in school year 2021-22.

 

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the alternative accreditation plan applications for submission to the Virginia Board of Education, as detailed in the agenda item.
