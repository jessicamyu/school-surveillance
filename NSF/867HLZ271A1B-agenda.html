    National Sleep Awareness Week - March 5-11

    The School Board recognizes National Sleep Awareness Week March 5 through 11.  Sleep Awareness Week brings attention to the importance of adequate sleep for health and safety, and a reminder of sleep-related responsibilities, such as not driving while drowsy and parents ensuring that their children get the sleep they need to learn effectively.  Let’s all remember as we set our clocks to daylight saving time on March 11 - “Sleep; As Important as Diet and Exercise, Only Easier.”

    National Athletic Training Month - March
        Certified athletic trainers employed by Fairfax County Public Schools provide quality health care for individuals involved in physical activity based on specific tasks, knowledge, and skills acquired through their nationally regulated educational processes.

        Athletic training services provided by certified athletic trainers include the prevention of athletic injuries; the recognition and evaluation of athletic injuries; the arrangement, treatment, rehabilitation, and disposition of athletic injuries; the organization and administration of health care programs; and the education and guidance of athletes.
    One of our own, Jon Almquist, Athletic Training Program Specialist in the Student Activities and Athletic Programs Office, was named the 2006 Most Distinguished Athletic Trainer Recipient by the National Athletic Trainers' Association.  This is a prestigious honor that recognizes NATA members for their exceptional and unique contributions to the athletic training profession and reflects a lifetime of dedication to the association and the field of athletic training on the national, district, and local level.  Jon is not able to join us tonight because he is filming the second educational video on fluid replacement. This video will be nationally distributed to soccer and basketball organizations. Thanks to Jon's efforts, FCPS is recognized as a leader in the area of fluid replacement. The first video that Jon filmed was for football and we happen to have a short clip of that video.

    RESOLUTION
    NAMING THE LIBRARY AT
    FRANCONIA ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
    “THE JUDITH JACKSON LIBRARY”


    WHEREAS, Judith Jackson has served as a distinguished teacher, principal, and community leader until her retirement in June 2007, always putting the children she comes in contact with first; and
    WHEREAS, because her passion for reading and the arts has helped to provide a quality education for thousands of young learners, she will always be remembered as the “Reading Principal”; and
    WHEREAS, her belief in the ability of every child to be successful in school has created a schoolwide enthusiasm to help every student find success at every level; and
    WHEREAS, Dr. Jackson’s warm and loving personality have made her smile a welcome sight in Franconia Elementary School, and she has created a nurturing and safe environment for all students and families in the school and has earned the trust and respect of all the parents, students, and staff members; and
    WHEREAS, her tireless commitment to effectively mentoring all staff members resulted in individual growth and leadership development for all;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the library at Franconia Elementary School “The Judith Jackson Library” in recognition of Dr. Jackson’s contributions to the individuals, communities, and school division in which she so lovingly and productively served, thereby leaving a legacy of educational excellence in Fairfax County Public Schools.

    Contact: Stuart Gibson, Hunter Mill Representative

    SUBJECT: Motion to Amend

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board amend the motion previously adopted by the Board on February 22, 2007, dealing with the approval of service area adjustments involving Kilmer, Frost, and Glasgow Middle School’s Gifted and Talented Centers as related to the opening of a new Gifted and Talented Center at Jackson Middle School, effective for the 2007-2008 school year, and as detailed in the agenda item, by striking out the words ‘and the Marshall Road Elementary School attendance area’ in paragraph 1 and striking out the words ‘other than those in the Marshall Road Elementary School attendance area’ in paragraph 2.
    EXPLANATION:
    At the February 22, 2007, regular business meeting the Board voted to amend the main motion by assigning the seven students living in Vienna, whose base school is Marshall Road Elementary and who attend the GT Center at Mosby Woods, to the new GT Center at Jackson Middle School for two years, and then these seven students would return to high school at Madison. Under the main motion, the other rising 7th grade GT Center students who live in Vienna would be assigned to Kilmer GT Center.

    If the proposed amendment passes, the final boundary motion for the new GT Center at Jackson Middle School would be as follows:
        Approve service area adjustments involving Kilmer, Frost, and Glasgow Middle Schools’ Gifted and Talented Centers as related to the opening of a new Gifted and Talented Center at Jackson Middle School, effective for the 2007-2008 school year and as detailed in the agenda item, and as amended, so that the change is phased in over three years instead of two years, as follows: 1) begin the GT Center at Jackson MS with rising 7th grade students in the fall of 2007, by moving students living in the Jackson base school attendance area and the Marshall Road Elementary School attendance area from their respective GT centers at Frost, Kilmer, and Glasgow, to the GT center at Jackson, and 2) move rising 7th graders living in the Thoreau base school attendance area other than those in the Marshall Road Elementary School attendance area from the GT Center at Kilmer to the GT center at Jackson beginning in the fall of 2008.
