Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 7:30 and 8 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

SUBJECT: Contract Amendment - Energy Performance Contracting Services (Bundle No. 4)


RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board further amend the contract for the Energy Performance Services with Noresco, LLC, dated November 8, 2002, to include improvements for a fourth group of schools at an additional cost of $3,436,269; enter into a lease/purchase financing agreement for such services and improvements with a recognized commercial financing company, all on the terms negotiated by school officials, with the advice of the School Board attorney, and authorize Thomas M. Brady, chief operating officer, or Dean Tistadt, assistant superintendent for the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute the agreements and related documents for these services/improvements and the lease/purchase financing on behalf of the School Board.

EXPLANATION:
    On November 7, 2002, the School Board awarded a contract for energy performance services to Noresco, LLC for 14 schools, referred to as Bundle No. 1 (the “Contract”). Work at those schools is proceeding satisfactorily. Subsequently, on May 22, 2003, the School Board approved the amendment of the contract to add improvements for 7 additional schools, which are referred to as Bundle No. 2, and on September 11, 2003, an additional 20 buildings, which are referred to as Bundle No. 3. The contract expressly contemplates that the scope of work may be modified to include additional schools upon terms mutually agreeable to the parties.

    The nineteen school locations and one center location to be added at this time, as Bundle
    No. 4, are Bren Mar Park, Crossfield, Cub Run, Forestdale, Hollin Meadows, Lees Corner, Little Run, Mount Vernon Woods, Rose Hill, Silverbrook, Springfield Estates, Westgate, Weyanoke, White Oaks, and Woodley Hills Elementary Schools, Twain and Frost Middle Schools, Falls Church and Langley High Schools, and Sideburn Support Center. This contract amendment, like the previous bundles, will be based on a detailed energy audit for energy performance services, encompassing lighting retrofits and energy management controls. These improvements will be completed as part of the multiple bundles that will be self-funded from energy savings generated from energy conservation measures (ECMs). Staff may recommend that additional schools be included in the future.

None recommended

SUBJECT: State Technology Grant Resolution

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board adopt a resolution stating the Board’s intention to be reimbursed for certain equipment purchases with proceeds of the FY 2005 and FY 2006 state technology grant which is funded with Virginia Public School Authority bonds.

EXPLANATION:

The General Assembly’s adopted 2004-2006 biennial budget authorizes the Virginia Public School Authority (VPSA) to conduct an educational technology grant program in FY 2005 and FY 2006. 5.02 Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology - Increase the number of students who are offered admission into Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology by 50, starting with the freshman class that will enter Thomas Jefferson in September 2005.
Meeting
Sep 23, 2004 - Regular Meeting No. 4
Category
5. Other Action Items
Type
Action
Contact: Ilryong Moon, At Large

RECOMMENDATION
      That the School Board increase the number of students who are offered admission into Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology by 50, starting with the freshman class that will enter Thomas Jefferson in September 2005.
EXPLANATION

Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJ) is the best high school in the country and provides unique opportunities to those students who are motivated to study math, science, and technology in depth. Thousands of students apply to TJ each year to earn admission to the school, but with the limited number of available seats at the school, unfortunately we turn away many highly qualified students who may have otherwise benefited from the program. With industry, society, and our daily life becoming continuously more technology-oriented, I can only predict that the number of students interested in the opportunities TJ offers will increase each year. We need to accommodate the needs of these students.

Although the number of students attending our Fairfax County high schools has substantially increased since TJ opened in 1985, the number of students at the school has only shown a modest rise. FCPS high school enrollment has grown by more than 6,000 students since 1985. The number of students attending other school districts (Arlington, Falls Church, Fauquier, Loudoun, and Prince William) which also send students to TJ has also increased by more than 50% since 1995. With the growth in both the number of students and those interested in studying math, science, and technology, it is only fair that the number of students offered admission to TJ be raised. The School Board has heard from many parents over the course of last several months that we need to do so.
