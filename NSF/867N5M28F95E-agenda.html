Food and Nutrition Services
Description of Presentation:Food and Nutrition Services known as the Energy Zone is an extension of the educational program. It operates with the philosophy “we talk, serve, and teach nutrition,” and strives to ensure all children are “ready to learn.” The program provides appealing, nutritious, high quality, safe food, at minimum cost in accordance with federal law, state regulation, and local policy. Special emphasis is being placed on the new Food Guide menu modifications that reflect current nutrition research; and concern with the growing childhood overweight issue.

The video includes scenes from a Fairfax Kids Cooking class with grade 4 students from Cub Run Elementary School. The students review nutrition for good health and prepare and taste a variety of healthy snacks. Fairfax Kids Cooking attempts to involve families through interesting at-home activities included in the booklet.

    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Ten slots are available. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

    SUBJECT: Contract Amendment

    RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board further amend the contract for Energy Performance Services with Noresco, LLC, dated November 8, 2002, to include improvements for a sixth group of school system buildings; enter into a lease/purchase financing agreement for such services and improvements with a recognized commercial financing company, all on the terms negotiated by school officials with the advice of the School Board attorney; and authorize Dean Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute the agreements and related documents for these services/improvements and the lease/purchase financing on behalf of the School Board.
    EXPLANATION:
      On November 7, 2002, the School Board awarded a contract for energy performance services to Noresco, LLC for 14 schools, referred to as Bundle No. 1 (the “Contract”). Subsequently, on May 22, 2003, the School Board approved the amendment of the Contract to add improvements for 7 additional schools, which are referred to as Bundle No. 2, and on September 11, 2003, an additional 20 buildings, which are referred to as Bundle No. 3, and on September 23, 2004 an additional 20 buildings, which are referred to as Bundle No. 4, and on May 12, 2005, an additional 17 buildings, which are referred to as Bundle No. 5. Work at buildings in Bundle Nos. 1 - 4 is complete. Work at buildings in Bundle No. 5 is proceeding satisfactorily. The Contract expressly contemplates that the scope of work may be modified to include additional schools upon terms mutually agreeable to the parties.
      The 28 locations to be added at this time, as Bundle No. 6, are Aldrin, Annandale Terrace, Belvedere (addition), Belle View, Bonnie Brae, Brookfield/Brookfield Center, Cameron, Canterbury Woods, Cherry Run, Clearview, Dranesville, Fox Mill, Haycock, Laurel Ridge (Special Ed addition), Mosby Woods, Newington Forest, Oak Hill, Pine Spring (addition), Ravensworth, Rolling Valley (addition), Saratoga/Saratoga Center, Spring Hill (addition) Stenwood, Washington Mill Elementary Schools; Herndon and Irving Middle Schools; and Johnie Forte and Woodson Support Centers. This contract amendment, like the previous Bundles, will be based on a detailed energy audit for energy performance services, encompassing lighting retrofits and energy management controls.
