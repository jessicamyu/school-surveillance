Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                 Kevin Sneed, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

                                 Jessica Gillis, director, Facilities Planning Services

 

Meeting Category: January 14, 2019 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

Outcome:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools continues to face a challenging situation whereby student membership (in certain locations) is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities, provide capacity enhancement projects and it will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions, and boundary adjustments to accommodate growing enrollment needs.

 

New School Construction: The Capital Improvement Program (CIP) proposes three new elementary schools for the next five-year horizon through FY 2024: 1) North West County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and the Dulles Airport access road (funded); 2) Fairfax/Oakton area (partially funded); and 3) Silver Line Metro Innovation Center Station area (unfunded). To meet needs beyond the five year horizon the following are unfunded proposals: 1) a new western high school in the vicinity of the Herndon area Silver Line Metro to alleviate capacity concerns at multiple high schools; and, 2) five new and/or repurposed schools.

 

Capacity Enhancements: The CIP proposes additions at West Potomac High School, Justice High School, and Madison High School, which are all partially funded. The CIP proposes the relocation of three modular additions (2 funded, 1 unfunded).

 

Renovations: Funding for renovations at five named high schools are proposed in the CIP. Additionally, funds for the renovation of five named middle schools and one unnamed middle school are proposed. Lastly, funds for renovations at 30 named elementary schools and three unnamed elementary schools are included. Many of these renovation projects will include capacity enhancements.

 

Site Acquisition: Funds have been provided as a placeholder to acquire a school site in the future.

 

The FY 2020–24 CIP also recommends possible solutions for future consideration to resolve capacity deficits. These options include interior modifications, temporary classrooms, program location suggestions, and boundary adjustments. Any boundary adjustment or program change would be discussed and decided through a transparent process that engages the community in accordance with School Board Policies and Regulations.

 

Included in the CIP is an environmental sustainability report, which includes a summary of the renewable energy report that the board requested at its May 28 forum meeting. At the board’s January 14, 2019 work session, the full renewable energy report will be presented and discussed.

 

Summary/Background:

 

The FY 2020-2024 CIP includes five-year student membership projections and capacity calculations, as well as the Capital Construction Cash Flow which identifies plans for new construction, capacity enhancements, renovations, and site acquisition to accommodate future capacity needs.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the FY 2020-2024 Capital Improvement Program.

Summary/Background: 

 

Student membership in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) showed a decline this year.  As has previously been communicated, various factors alter year to year membership changes and student membership projections.  The two most important factors that influence the overall FCPS membership changes and student membership projections are the birth to kindergarten ratio and net migration. Net migration is the total number of students gained or withdrawn from the school system once new students and the number of students who withdraw are added together. A third factor that alters overall membership and the student membership for individual schools from year to year is student transfers due to program access needs and the student transfer regulation.  Worth noting is that net migration was negative this year, unlike the past seven years which were positive.  A negative net migration indicates that more students withdrew from the system than the number of students who entered the school system.  Also worth noting is that the birth to kindergarten was the same as last year. 

 

The Facilities and Transportation Services’ staff have prepared a Membership Analysis and Trends SY 2018-19 report.  The report’s information informs membership projections and the Capital Improvement Program (CIP) recommendations.  The report provides detailed information and tables that show the current and prior year membership, births, net migration, and transfer conditions for the system overall and by school level (elementary, middle, and high).  Detailed transfer information was requested by the Board last year and has been incorporated into the main report this year.  The report provides the latest five-year projections for the Board’s consideration, as well as information about the accuracy of student membership projections.  These projected conditions lead to the Proposed FY 2020-24 CIP recommendations, which are being presented the same day as the Membership and Trends Analysis, December 20, 2018.  A CIP public hearing is scheduled for January 8, 2019.  A School Board work session is scheduled for January 14, 2019.  The action to vote on the FY 2020-24 Capital Improvement Program is scheduled for January 24, 2019.

 

The continued development of the membership projection methodology and quality data to inform projections is necessary to account for the complex factors that influence membership data each year.  The evaluation of new data sets is continual in order to identify trends related to economic and demographic as they impact student membership projections.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

On October 11, 2018, the Fairfax County School Board passed a resolution on Climate Change Action. The resolution acknowledged the immediate impact of climate change on our region and how, given its overall size and physical footprint, FCPS offers an unparalleled opportunity to advance the use of renewable energy sources and reduce greenhouse gas emissions in Northern Virginia.

 

In 2015, the department of Facilities and Transportation Services (FTS) began conducting feasibility studies with solar companies across the state. Last year, we worked with Arlington Public Schools (APS) during their development of a solar Power Purchase Agreement (PPA). However, the APS contract is not eligible for cooperative procurement by FCPS since it is tied to the division’s capital improvement program. We are currently working with Fairfax County Government operations staff on a Request for Proposals (RFP) for a solar PPA, one that FCPS will be able to utilize and that is expected to be made public in early 2019.

 

A full report will be provided on work to date with next steps.
