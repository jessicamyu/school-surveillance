National Women's History Month - March 2008


To honor the originality, beauty, imagination, and multiple dimensions of women’s lives, the National Women's History Project has chosen Women's Art: Women’s Vision as the 2008 theme for National Women’s History Month.

The history of women and art is quintessential women’s history.  It is the story of amazing women’s accomplishments acclaimed at the time but written out of history.

This year’s theme provides a special opportunity to discover and celebrate women’s visual arts in a variety of forms and mediums that help expand our perceptions of ourselves and each other.

RESOLUTION
HONORING
VISITING JAPANESE TEACHER


WHEREAS, the Fukuoka Prefecture and Fairfax County Public Schools have participated in the Regional Educational Exchange (REX) program, sponsored by the Japanese Ministry of Education, since 1991; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Yumiko Uto, REX teacher at Floris Elementary School, has worked in the Japanese partial-immersion program since August 2006; and

WHEREAS, she has provided, to the Japanese partial-immersion program, invaluable assistance in creating instructional materials, in teaching students, and in sharing aspects of Japanese culture both with the students participating in the partial-immersion program and with students in the regular program; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Uto has fostered and promoted positive relations between the United States and Japan in helping American students and their parents learn to speak the Japanese language and to know more about Japanese culture;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Ms. Yumiko Uto for her contributions and dedicated service to the educational programs at Floris Elementary School.

Fairfax County Chamber of Commerce Resolution
Offered February 14, 2008

Fairfax County Public Schools Success


WHEREAS, Fairfax County’s Thomas Jefferson High School was named the top high school in America by U.S. News & World Report; and

WHEREAS, six other high schools in Fairfax County — Langley High School, Oakton High School, Robinson High School, James Madison High School, J.E.B. Stuart High School, and George C. Marshall High School — were also recognized among the best in the nation; and

WHEREAS, there has been a longstanding partnership between the Fairfax County Public School system and the Fairfax County Chamber and the Fairfax County business community; and

WHEREAS, this longstanding partnership has been a critical element in the success of Thomas Jefferson High School and the other Fairfax County schools, and

WHEREAS, the vital members of this partnership include the Fairfax County Public Schools, the Thomas Jefferson Partnership Fund, the Fairfax Education Foundation, and business leader and Chamber members including Dan Bannister, James Dyke, Michael Anzilotti, Michael Daniels, Marty Irving, and others; be it

RESOLVED THAT, the Fairfax County Chamber of Commerce would like to offer its congratulations to Thomas Jefferson High School and the other successful Fairfax County high schools; and be it

FURTHER RESOLVED THAT, the Chamber commends those considered vital to the partnership for their foresight and continued support for public-private-partnership for sustained excellence in secondary education; and be it

FURTHER RESOLVED THAT, the Chamber hopes these achievements will serve as a reminder to the business community as to why we are and need to continue to be dedicated to our public schools.

Adopt the FY 2009 Advertised Budget, as detailed in the agenda item, and with the following revisions: 1) eliminate student activity and athletic fees, reducing revenue by $2.7 million; 2) eliminate student test fees for AP/IB/PSAT, reducing revenue by $2.5 million; 3) restore funding of $1.0 million for the elementary focus program; 4) restore funding of $0.4 million to continue the modified calendar program at three schools; 5) restore funding of $1.8 million for 55 general education instructional assistant positions; 6) restore funding of $1.7 million for transportation of GT, TJHSST, and administratively-placed students; 7) restore funding of $0.7 million for the College Partnership Program; 8) restore funding of $5.9 million for the special education preschool program; 9) restore funding of $0.1 million to reinstate the following central office cuts—National Board Certified Teachers, ABA Secondary Program, and College Partnership Program; 10) reallocate the $9.2 million currently budgeted for Teacher Leadership to a central account to fund the redesign of summer school; 11) reduce funding by $3.4 million from the $9.2 million budgeted for program redesign, leaving $5.8 million for redesign of the Excel Program components, Quest, Student Accountability, Summit, and Young Scholars programs; 12) reduce funding for Full Day Kindergarten by $2.7 million, which will reduce the expansion from 21 schools to 10 schools; 13) reduce funding by $0.6 million for the FLES program, reducing expansion of new FLES schools from 16 to 8, and continuing the program implementation begun in 2008 at 16 schools; 14) defer completion of the elementary technology specialist model and eliminate $1.5 million and 16 TSSpec positions; 15) reduce funding for SASI by $1.0 million; 16) restore $0.2 million for the Middle School and High School Enhancement Program; and 17) increase the amount of the County transfer request by $7.9 million for a total increase of $63.7 million or 4%.

SUBJECT: Award of Contract

TYPE: New Business

BACKGROUND:
      Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) seeks to establish a contract for hazardous material removal services for substances such as asbestos, and lead in FCPS buildings as requirements become known. Generally, the task orders are valued at less than $5,000 each. However, the aggregate dollar amount of services performed under the contract can be more than $250,000 annually. Funding for hazardous material removal contract is provided from the Major Maintenance and Construction Funds, as well as school and department Operating Funds. An invitation for bid has been issued and advertised and bids are due to be received on February 14, 2008. A bid tabulation and recommendation will be furnished prior to the February 28, 2008, School Board meeting.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board award the contract for the hazardous material removal contract to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder(s) and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.
