U. S. Constitution Day and Citizenship Day - September 17, 2020

 

Constitution Day and Citizenship Day are observed each year on September 17 to commemorate the signing of the Constitution on September 17, 1787, and “recognize all who, by coming of age or by naturalization, have become citizens.”  Recent legislation has renamed the day as Constitution Day and Citizenship Day and urges proper observance of the day. This year, the official observance day is Thursday, September 17, and is the 233rd  anniversary of the nation’s founding document.

 

All schools in FCPS will observe this day with activities that recognize the adoption of the United States Constitution and those who have become citizens under the Constitution.  Schools will use a variety of curriculum resources to teach students about the importance of the Constitution and responsible citizenship.  Throughout the school year, high school students will have an opportunity to participate in service learning projects related to responsible citizenship, which can lead to a Civics Seal on their diploma.

Childhood Cancer Awareness Recognition

 

The American Childhood Cancer Organization (or ACCO) has designated September as Childhood Cancer Awareness Month to spotlight the types of cancers that largely affect children and to help raise funds for research and family support.

 

According to ACCO, 15,780 children under the age of 21 are diagnosed with cancer every year in the United States; approximately 1/4 of them will not survive the disease. A diagnosis turns the lives of the entire family upside down.

 

ACCO offers support, information and advocacy through their community of families, survivors, and friends affected by childhood and adolescent cancer. They are dedicated to improving the lives of children and families living with childhood cancer and engaged in this life-altering struggle. FCPS students being treated for childhood cancer participate in home-bound instruction while they are undergoing treatment or recuperating.

 

We are joined tonight by long-time teacher Jay Coakley. Retired from FCPS after 30 years of teaching physical education, Jay returned to FCPS as a part-time elementary physical education teacher at Woodburn Elementary. It was at Woodburn where Jay met a little girl named Ellie, who had been diagnosed with acute lymphoblastic leukemia in the spring of 2013. By the time she entered kindergarten that fall, Ellie had lost most of her hair and was wearing hats to school every day.

 

Jay noticed her affinity for “fun and cheery hats” and organized a hat drive to provide Ellie with a broader selection of hats, and Ellie’s Hats was born! Now, thanks to the hard work of the individuals who, like Jay, took an interest in Ellie and her hats, thousands of kids across the country who are battling cancer have received new hats and so much more. Ellie’s Hats does much more than send hats to children with cancer. They have helped to provide financial assistance to local families who have children with cancer. They recently reached a milestone of $100,000 in donations to local families.

 

We are delighted that Ellie and her mother, Jennica, joined the meeting tonight. Ellie is now in 7th grade, will be celebrating her 12th birthday this Saturday, and is into Marvel, Harry Potter and drama. 

 

On behalf of the Board, I’d like to encourage everyone to visit the website for Ellie’s Hats at ellieshats.org and the American Childhood Cancer Organization at acco.org, to learn how you can help.

Hispanic Heritage Month Recognition

 

Each year, Americans observe National Hispanic Heritage Month by celebrating the histories, cultures and contributions of Americans whose ancestors came from Spain, Mexico, the Caribbean and Central and South America, most of whom are in fact an extension of “The Americas” that we share, and many of whom are indigenous or represent mixed ancestries. This observation started in 1968 amidst the civil rights era as Hispanic Heritage Week and later expanded in 1988 to an observation period of 30-days starting on September 15 and ending on October 15. It was enacted into law August 17, 1988 and is a long-standing American celebration of many decades.

 

National Hispanic Heritage Month is is a tangible reinforcement of the School Board’s belief that “Our diversity is a strength that creates resilient, open, and innovative global citizens.” More importantly, it is a wonderful opportunity for our schools and community to celebrate the richness of the many nationalities, cultures, languages, religions, and traditions within this diverse ethnic group. We celebrate the likes of Cesar Chavez, Dolores Huerta, and Ramon Rahim Ocasio, American civil rights and labor leaders of Hispanic heritage.

 

In FCPS, one in four of our students is of Hispanic Heritage, representing multilingual talents and origins from tens of countries. 10.5%-- more than 1 in 10 of every employee in our system-- contribute to this richness as leadership team members, principals, teachers, cafeteria workers, interpreters, parent liaisons, custodians, bus drivers, and staff all across our division. These cultures in turn enrich our own school community.

 

I encourage our schools to set the tone in recognition of the need to reinvigorate the celebration of heritage, culture, and an empowered narrative of our friends and neighbors here in FCPS and encourage community members to participate in the programs and events taking place during this time.

 

FCPS values its diversity and acknowledges that all people contribute to the well-being of the community. The vast diversity and richness within the Hispanic experience include the longevity of Hispanic heritage in this country—for centuries before Columbus arrived on our shores, as well as the continued struggle and reality for migrant Americans of our brothers and sisters in mixed status families, those whom are undocumented, those whom are “dacamented,” dreamers, some whose parents are on TPS, and many others whom we welcome.

 

FCPS is fortunate to have many groups whose members work with our Latinx and immigrant students empowering them to be the next generation of leaders and professionals. These include, but are not limited to the staff mentioned earlier as well as the Hispanic Educators Association, Edu-Futuro, LUPEE, CASA, La Colectiva, and our own Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee.

FCPS High School Voter Registration Challenge

Resolution – September 2020

 

WHEREAS, voting is the most important civic opportunity of United States citizens and is the cornerstone of our democracy; and

 

WHEREAS, a new Virginia law requires every public high school to provide opportunities for eligible students to register to vote during the school day; and

 

WHEREAS, the Ethical and Global Citizen attribute of the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate is an expectation for all students, and schools are committed to developing students who will exercise their rights and responsibilities as citizens, providing fair and equal access to voting is an ongoing effort in our country; and 

 

WHEREAS, civic education contributes to the development of engaged citizens and increases the likelihood of future participation in voting, providing information to students about voter registration and how, when, and where to vote prepares students to exercise their voting rights; and


WHEREAS, young people who are registered to vote participate in numbers close to the participation rates of older voters, voting is habit forming. When young people exercise their voting rights, they are likely to become lifelong voters, exercising their duties and responsibilities as citizens;


NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board recognizes the efforts of the League of Women Voters of the Fairfax Area, the Fairfax County Office of Elections, Fairfax County Council of PTAs, and the FCPS social studies team and teachers who provide voter registration opportunities and nonpartisan information about voting procedures to eligible students;


THEREFORE, BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board will recognize schools that make significant contributions to student voter registration via the redesigned High School Voter Recognition Challenge. This recognition is sponsored by the League of Women Voters of the Fairfax Area and the Fairfax County Council of PTAs.

That the School Board (i) approve contract change order #7 for the Mason Crest geothermal project (the "Project") in the total amount of $60,315.94 and (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities & Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to accept, execute, deliver and administer change order #7, as well as any future change orders for the Project on behalf of the School Board, with a copy of any such future executed change orders to be promptly filed with the Clerk of the Board for distribution to the members for their information.

Subject: Geothermal Piping Replacement at Mason Crest Elementary School Contract Change Order

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: September 3, 2020 Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information/Decision

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4

 

 

Summary/Background

 

Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (DFTS) recommends that the School Board accept and approve change order #7 to the geothermal replacement piping work at Mason Crest, for which the Board awarded a contract on February 20, 2020, in the original amount of $418,925.00.

 

School Board approval of change order #7 is required because the total of all change orders (#1 through #7, the first six of which were previously executed and are in place now) would exceed 25% of the original contract amount, per the Virginia Public Procurement Act. Given the open nature of the work site and the current permit, the work contemplated by change order #7 is time-sensitive and necessary in order for the school division to receive the full value of the project.

 

The original geothermal piping at Mason Crest, which was installed in 2012, proved to be defective and to require replacement. The School Board awarded the replacement contract to Negleys Well Drilling on February 20, 2020. Funding for this project is provided from the Infrastructure Replacement Fund, and oversight is provided by DFTS through its Office of Facilities Maintenance (OFM).

 

After commencing the replacement piping project, the Contractor discovered unknown subsurface conditions that had to be addressed. The unknown subsurface conditions yielded approval of change orders #1 through #6, that included necessary site, testing, and grouting changes to address the uncovered deficiencies. The total of those change orders was $63,371.34 and were within the allowed amount of 25% increase as allowed per the Virginia Public Procurement Act.

 

The recommended additional work requires one additional change order - #7 in the amount of $60,315.94. This change order would include six (6) additional bores and additional grouting of original pipes. OFM and its 3rd party engineering consultant concludes that if the additional vertical bores are not installed the geothermal system will not meet its original design parameters or lifespan.

 

 

Recommendation:


That the School Board (i) approve contract change order #7 for the Mason Crest geothermal project (the "Project") in the total amount of $60,315.94 and (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities & Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to accept, execute, deliver and administer change order #7, as well as any future change orders for the Project on behalf of the School Board, with a copy of any such future executed change orders to be promptly filed with the Clerk of the Board for distribution to the members for their information.

Subject: West Springfield High School - Grant of Storm Drainage Easement and Temporary Construction Easement to Fairfax County

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: September 3, 2020 Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Related To: Policy 8330.4

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Christopher Land, LLC (Developer) has requested a Storm Drainage Easement and Temporary Construction Easement over a portion of West Springfield High School property, Fairfax County Tax Map 079-3-((04))-0001A. The easement is in connection to installation of storm drainage culverts under Tuttle Road and the existing trail system on School property and improving the existing open channel through the School property.

 

The storm drainage improvements are part of the Developers project for a new single-family residential development called Residences at McConnell Manor (Project), located adjacent to School property on the opposite side of Tuttle Road. Tuttle Road currently experiences roadway flooding due to inadequate storm conveyance system. The Project storm design will provide an adequate storm conveyance system across Tuttle Road and extend improvements through the existing outfall channel. The storm design is intended to reduce the flooding and overtopping of Tuttle Road. The Storm Drainage Easement would be granted to the Fairfax County.

 

The Office of Design and Construction Services Staff has determined that the Easement will in no way permanently damage, interfere with or limit present or future use and will not endanger the health and safety of users of West Springfield High School. The outfall improvements help the neighborhood in general and will benefit the School more directly as that is also a drainage outfall to the School property. Furthermore, Staff has asked for incidental restorative landscaping and BMP credits of 0.25 lbs. for the benefit of the High School property, which the Developer has agreed to.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In accordance with past practice, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted an updated version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution on July 28, 2020 (the 2020 Purchasing Resolution) to reflect legislative changes made during the preceding session of the Virginia General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly-revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

Countys Administrative Changes Relative to FCPS:

  1. Clarified that the updated Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution is effective at the start of the fiscal year, July 1, 2020 (Page 1).
  2. Amended language to conform to Countys One Fairfax Policy on racial and social equity and its recognition that economic inclusivity benefits all (Page 13).
  3. Change to align procurement threshold with increase from $100,000 to $200,000 established under HB 452 (Page 16).
  4. Change to align procurement threshold with increase from $100,000 to $200,000 established under HB 452 and allow for notification to the School Board prior to contract execution (Page 16-17).
  5. Change to align procurement threshold with increase from $100,000 to $200,000 established under HB 452 and allow for notification to the School Board prior to contract execution (Page 19).
  6. Change to increase small purchase threshold from $5,000 to $10,000 commensurate with other purchase limit thresholds (Page 24).
  7. Changed to reflect the Countys current practices regarding HIPAA Compliance for its contractors (Page 36).
  8. Changed to reflect current practice (Page 38).
  9. Changed to correct typographical error (Page 39).
  10. Changed to reflect new small purchase threshold (Page 40).
  11. Changed to allow flexibility in contracting given that drug free workplace requirements vary state-to-state (Page 40).

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Boards adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018 and 2019 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2020 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  • The FCPS Construction Exception, which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with 22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Boards procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.

 

[Note: The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Boards adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendents authority: The school divisions Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts. Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.]

 

  • The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopts the newly revised 2020 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.
