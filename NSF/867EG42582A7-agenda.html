SUBJECT: FY 2009 Proposed Budget

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:

On January 10, 2008, the Superintendent presented the FY 2009 Proposed Budget to the School Board. On January 14, 2008, the Superintendent delivered the proposed budget presentation at a joint work session of the School Board and the County Board of Supervisors.

This is a School Board (SB) informal work session to clarify with the staff the implications of the Superintendents expenditure recommendations. The SB will hold a public hearing on February 6th, convene in a second work session on February 11th, and adopt its “advertised” budget on February 14th.

KEY POINTS:

The Superintendent has proposed a budget for FY2009 that contains four priorities: a 3.0 percent cost-of-living adjustment for employees, continued expansion of full-day kindergarten, replacing the student information system, and continued expansion of the Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools Program. After funding these priorities, significant budget reductions were necessary to address a deficit of nearly $100 million.
      The budget identifies expenditure reductions totaling $45.6 million and 521.3 positions, as presented in three categories: eliminate, reduce, and streamline. He proposes that the remaining $55 million gap be closed by a 3.5% increase in the transfer from the County, instead of the 0% guidance established by the Board of Supervisors.

      During this work session, Dr. Dale will provide a brief presentation that addresses issues related to priorities and budget reductions. The School Board and the Superintendent and staff will then engage in an informal discussion. The key topics to be covered include the following:
        1. Competiveness of salary/benefits relative to attracting and retaining high-quality personnel
        2. Serving disadvantaged students in all schools [staffing formula; special programs]
        3. Restructuring/streamlining/redesign of programs
            • Cross-walk between extended teacher contracts and program streamlining
          • Summer school redesign
          • Focus schools
          • TSSpecs as offsets to IAs
          • Inclusive schools resource teachers

        4. Student transportation reductions
        5. Options for FDK and FLES
        6. Comparisons of student membership and class size
        7. Special education implications - program and revenue
        8. Consequences if we don’t receive $55 million from the county
        9. Other questions from the School Board

OPTIONS:

No decisions or a sense of the Board will be attempted at this work session.
