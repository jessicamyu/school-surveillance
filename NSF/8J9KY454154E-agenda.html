School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:             Strategic Governance

 

Key Points: 

 

Instructional Program Operational Expectations:

 

The Superintendent shall maintain a program of instruction that offers challenging and relevant opportunities for all students to achieve at levels defined in the Board’s student achievement goals.  The Superintendent will:

 

     A.  Ensure that instructional programs are based on a comprehensive and objective review of best practices research.

     B.  Base instruction on district academic standards that meet or exceed state and/or nationally-recognized model standards.

     C.  Effectively measure each student’s progress toward achieving or exceeding the standards.

     D.  Ensure that the instructional program includes opportunities for students to develop talents and interests in specialized areas.

     E.  Ensure that the instructional program accommodates the different learning styles of students and differentiates instruction to meet

           the needs of students of various backgrounds and abilities.

     F.  Encourage new and innovative programs, carefully monitoring and evaluating the effectiveness of all such programs.

     G.  Ensure that all instructional programs are regularly evaluated to assure their continuing effectiveness.  All evaluations should

          recommend whether a program should be continued, modified, or discontinued based on its effectiveness and cost.

     H.  Ensure appropriate input from students, parents, teachers, administrators and instructional staff members as textbooks are

          reviewed and selected.

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board accept the Instructional Program Operational Expectations Monitoring Report.

Subject:                      2012 FCPS Working Conditions Survey        

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:             Human Resources Operational Expectations

 

Key Points: 

 

From January 9 to February 3, 2012, FCPS in conjunction with the New Teacher Center conducted the third iteration of the FCPS Working Conditions Survey. The survey assesses whether critical teaching and learning conditions are present in schools across the district.

 

The anonymous survey captured the responses of 12,406 licensed educators (80 percent). School level data reports are available for every school in the district and results may be accessed online at www.fcpswcs.org.

 

The FCPS Working Conditions Survey assesses eight teaching conditions areas, including Time; Facilities and Resources; Community Support and Involvement; Managing Student Conduct; Teacher Leadership; School Leadership; Professional Development; and Instructional Practices and Support.

 

Additionally, questions about induction were asked of novice teachers (those in their first three years in the profession) and principals were surveyed about supports they receive from their school district.

 

The presentation highlights the results of the 2012 FCPS Working Conditions Survey.

Meeting Category:     May 14, 2012 – Work Session No. 126

 

Subject:                       FY 2013 Approved Budget    

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                 

 

Key Points:

 

On February 9, 2012, the School Board adopted the FY 2013 Advertised Budget.  The County Board of Supervisors subsequently adopted their FY 2013 budget on May 1, 2012, and included funding that was $63.3 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget. The state budget is expected to be finalized on May 14, 2012.  Based on the budget passed by the General Assembly during the 2012 special session and submitted to the Governor, FCPS projects increased state revenue of $44.0 million as compared to the advertised budget. 

 

Based on the total available revenue for FY 2013, and in response to the state adopted mandate regarding employee contributions to the Virginia Retirement System, the following adjustments to the FY 2013 Advertised Budget are recommended:

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue reflects a net reduction of $14.3 million as compared to the advertised budget.  Funding from the county is $63.3 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by an increase is state revenue of $44.0 million based on the projected state budget and an estimated increase of $5.0 million in revenue from Fairfax City.

 

Expenditures:

Required expenditure adjustments total $22.4 million compared to the advertised budget and include the following: $8.1 million as a result of retirement rate increases above the increase projected in the advertised budget; $6.7 million to implement the first year of a mandatory shift in the employee contribution for the Virginia Retirement System, which is required to be offset by a salary increase; $4.9 million for life insurance rate increases; $2.3 million for membership and demographic adjustments based on the updated spring enrollment projections; $0.5 million in support of high school band programs; and a decrease of $0.1 million for world languages based on the enrollment at the schools selected to implement the Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools program in FY 2013.  These required adjustments will be offset by reductions of $36.7 million that the School Board will identify prior to the budget being adopted on May 24, 2012.

 

To meet the required level of expenditure reductions, the Superintendent has identified reductions totaling $36.7 million which are detailed on pages 4-7 of the agenda item.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2013 Approved Budget, as detailed in the FY 2013 Advertised Budget and modified with the adjustments detailed in the agenda item.
