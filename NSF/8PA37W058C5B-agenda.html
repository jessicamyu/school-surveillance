                                Lawrence Bussey, staff liaison

 

Meeting Category:     May 31, 2012 - Work Session 130

 

Subject:                     Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee (MSAOC) Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To:                Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points: 

 

The committee’s chair will address the School Board charge and present the following recommendations from the committee:

  • Establishing a new base line in math and reading by using the results from the 2012 math SOL test to begin a three-year average; also using the results of the 2013 reading SOL tests as the first of three-year average for determining the growth over time.
  • Continue to monitor the seven subgroups for the purpose of continuing to monitor the closing of the gap and preparing students for college and/or career.
  • Fund universal preschool programs for children and families eligible under income guidelines. Although the number of eligible children served increased in 2011, it did not keep pace with expanding the number of poor children and families in Fairfax County.
  • Replicate programs in schools that have shown success at closing the achievement gap as models for other schools in the division.
  • Provide more community outreach to assist parents to understand the initiative concerning college and career readiness and how they can support it.
  • All division employees should participate in the Cultural Competence training developed by Darryle Craig.
  • We recommend the School Board refer to staff for the development of a plan to encourage schools to celebrate our National history and heritage months to recognize the diversity of our school community. To coincide with the monthly celebrations, FCPS should provide resource- related learning activities tied to the Virginia Standards of Learning (SOLs), and suggestions for honoring individuals and groups who made a difference in Virginia.

Meeting Category:     May 31, 2012 - Work Session No. 130

 

Subject:                    Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report (2012)

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:  

 

Regulations Governing Special Education Programs for Children with Disabilities in Virginia require that a local advisory committee for special education, appointed by each local school board, advise the school board through the division superintendent of unmet needs in the education of children with disabilities. The regulations state that the composition of the committee shall include parents of children with disabilities and person with disabilities.

 

The charge from the School Board was to examine the current literacy instruction for students with disabilities in FCPS across all disabilities.  Explore the current initiatives, proposed development of resources, and the overall needs of the system to include professional development for staff and dissemination of information to parents.

 

The following sub-committees of ACSD addressed the above charge with the following recommendations:

  1. Communication Sub-Committee:
  1. Provide all parents (of students in general and special educations) with information about the literacy programs used in their child’s school, as well as resources that promote a common understanding of literacy and literacy learning in a manner that supports a collaborative partnership between home and school.
  2. Adopt a flexible communication plan.
  3. Build on the literacy strand at the annual FCPS Special Education Conference.
  4. Conduct Parent Focus Group meetings during the year and inform the greater school community on the results of these focus groups.
  1. Literacy Instruction Sub-Committee:
  1. Systematically increase staff development and parent outreach to ensure awareness and availability of interventions to address reading deficits.
  2. Take appropriate steps to provide a comprehensive, cohesive and seamless continuum of K-12 reading, writing, and language instruction for all students.
  3. Increase staff development and other technical assistance resources to implement specialized reading programs with fidelity. Through cluster assistant superintendents, hold principals accountable for maintaining fidelity of specialized reading programs implemented in their schools.
  4. Ensure that the needs of students with specific reading deficits (including dyslexia) are appropriately assessed and identified in a timely manner (before students experience significant reading failure) and are addressed through the provision of research-based specialized reading intervention programs as early as kindergarten.
  5. Use a multi-pronged approach to early identification of specific learning disabilities, including revising the FCPS Basis for Committee Decision for eligibility under “Specific Learning Disabilities” to be consistent with the definition of Specific Learning Disability in the Virginia Department of Education regulations.  See the definition of dyslexia on page 12 of this subcommittee report.
  1. Follow-Up Sub-Committee:
  1. Publish a status report of the ongoing recommendations made by the ACSD on the FCPS website.
  2. The Follow-Up Committee meets with staff quarterly to discuss and analyze the  proposed recommendations to the School Board and the FCPS staff response to those recommendations.

Staff Contact:            Peter J. Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD) 

 

Other Staff Present:  Craig Herring, director, Office of PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

                                Carol Horn, coordinator, Advanced Academic Programs, Office of PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category:      May 31, 2012 - Work Session 130

 

Subject:                    Advanced Academic Advisory Committee Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:               Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board 

 

Key Points: 

 

The AAPAC charge focused on a review and evaluation of how FCPS is meeting the needs of twice exceptional (2E) students (K-12) in advanced academic classes with a goal of providing recommendations for areas of improvement.

 

The committee developed its work program and recommendations around two areas of activities:

  • Best practices for instruction and accommodations for the twice exceptional student, and
  • Parent education and advocacy. 

The committee met with academic experts, parents and students throughout the year to gain a better understanding of what is currently being done in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) to support the needs of twice exceptional learners.  Panel discussions were followed by discussions within the committee to identify areas of strength and to develop recommendations where FCPS could strengthen its efforts.

 

Specific recommendations include:

 

Best practices for instruction and accommodations:

  1. Develop programs to assist twice exceptional students in developing executive function and self advocacy skills beginning in the elementary grades.
  2. Continue to expand the use of advanced academic strategies and resources in the general education classroom.
  3. Expand Responsive Instruction practices to ensure that the needs of students who may be twice exceptional are met.
  4. Enable students, family members, and educators to meet on an annual basis (at all grade levels K-12) to determine which accommodations were most successful.
  5. Encourage high school counselors, advisors, and mentors to meet with twice exceptional students in order to help them prepare for their post high school academic experience

Parent education and advocacy:

  1. Develop additional web resources to be prominently posted on the AAP web page in order to provide parents a better understanding of what it means to be twice exceptional and the resources available to them.
  2. Expand outreach by school staff to parents of students who may be twice exceptional (especially those that may be from under-represented populations and/or qualify for free and reduced meals) to educate them on the services available to their students.
  3. Through the collaborative efforts of AAP and Special Services, convene an ad-hoc staff, student, and parent focus group on communications to determine what resources would be most useful to the constituents.
