Staff Contact:            Kim P. Dockery, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services                                                                                                               

 

Other Staff Present: Irene Meier, director, Office of Special Education Instruction

 

Meeting Category:    June 2, 2011 - Work Session No. 122

 

Subject:                      Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report (2011)

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                        

 

Related To:                 Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

                                    Legal Requirement (Individuals with Disabilities Act – IDEA)

                                    Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points:

 

Regulations Governing Special Education Programs for Children with Disabilities in Virginia require that a local advisory committee for special education, appointed by each local school board, advise the school board through the division superintendent of unmet needs in the education of children with disabilities. The regulations state that the composition of the committee shall include parents of children with disabilities and person with disabilities.

 

The charge from the School Board was to assist parents in removing barriers to ensure that their students receive the appropriate special education services for success by addressing the following areas:

  • Communication between general and special education teachers
  • Information related to assisting parents to become better advocates
  • Difficult processes (twice exceptional)
  • Personnel and their roles

 

Four sub-committees of ACSD addressed the above charges with recommendations as follows:

 

  1.  Internal Communication (between general and special education):
    • FCPS survey their administrators, teachers, parents, students, case managers, and other pertinent school personnel on the current state of communication between staff (general and special education) who work with students who have disabilities.
  2. Survey data should be presented to committee by July, 2012
  3. FCPS provide training that will enhance staff capacity to work collaboratively
  4. Removing Barriers:
    • Define the role of the following in the media: Procedural Support Liaisons, Behavior Intervention Teachers, and Applied Behavior Analysis Coaches.
    • Define how parents can access above personnel
    • Ensure FCPS teachers understand the role of the Procedural Support Liaison
    • Measure the number of procedural issues not resolved at the IEP team level.
    • Feature a mission statement on the homepage with phrase “Promoting parent participation in the education of students with special needs.”
    • Better utilization of Parent Resource Center (PRC) by schools
  5. Collaboration: Families, Schools and Community:
    • FCPS school board adopt as policy for use in FCPS the six National Standards for Family-School Partnerships and that it monitor policy implementation for effectiveness using established assessment tools.
    • FCPS School Board appoints a school-wide special education parent ombudsman that reports directly to the board.
  6. Twice Exceptional:
  • Develop a Twice Exceptional Manual based on best practices
  • Revise the Specific Learning Disability Basis for Committee Decision form (see report)
  • Provide assessment battery for gifted students with learning disabilities to include measures of student’s functional level within the district’s curriculum.
  • Increase awareness of the current FCPS Section 504 Manual
  • Increase access for all students with a disability to assistive technology services
  • Build capacity for ISD and DSS to collaborate on a multi-sensory curriculum for the visual spatial learner
  • Develop a curriculum to further develop organization skills and other executive functions.

Subject:                        Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee (AAPAC) Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:                   Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points:

 

AAPAC makes recommendations to the School Board in three areas of policy.  These include: professional development, scope and sequencing and acceleration, breadth and depth.  These areas of policy development were identified by committee members during the course of the year and include the following recommendations:

 

  • FCPS should consider funding staffing for a full-time advanced academic resource teacher (AART) in every elementary and middle school.
  • Ensure administrators and school staffs have a strong understanding of what differentiation of instruction in mathematics entails.
  • Forge FCPS alumni follow-up programs and university partnerships to assess the efficacy of the FCPS math curriculum. 
  • Examine the entry points and processes for students to enter advanced mathematics classes K-12; make adjustments as warranted to increase access points throughout a student’s career in FCPS.
  • Systematically increase opportunities for community involvement in schools by recruiting volunteers to develop and lead mathematics-based school enrichment programs.
