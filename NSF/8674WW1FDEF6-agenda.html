National Safety Month - June 2010

Each June, the National Safety Council sponsors National Safety Month to promote safe and healthy behaviors in all aspects of our lives. Each week carries a different theme that brings attention to current safety issues. This year, the themes are prescription drug overdose prevention, teen driving safety, preventing overexertion at work and home, dangers of cell phone use while driving, and summer safety.

Over 172,000 FCPS students depend on school employees to provide a safe environment in schools. Prevention, identification, and abatement of hazards are integral parts of every employee’s job. The FCPS Office of Safety and Security has established emergency plans and provided appropriate training for employees.

Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Week - May 24-28, 2010

The adult and community education program of Fairfax County Public Schools began with an action of the School Board in 1955, and, in the 55 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills and completed their high school educations.

Through the years adults have learned new languages, acquired job skills, transitioned to new careers, and grew as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to Adult & Community Education to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education has made major contributions to the school system as a laboratory of innovation for the piloting of new programs and services in support of pre-K-12 education through family literacy, summer programs, extended day programs, enrichment courses, and driver education.

Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner – as well as provision of remediation and enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day – ACE has helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

Meeting Category: May 20, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 20
Subject: FY 2011 Approved Budget

School Board Action Required: Decision

Key Points:
      In its FY 2011 Adopted Budget Plan, the BOS approved a one percent decrease in the county transfer to the school operating fund, resulting in a decrease of $98.2 million as compared to the request in the advertised budget. Final State General Assembly actions resulted in an increase of $34.2 million including State Fiscal Stabilization Funding, and revenue from the City of Fairfax decreased $1.2 million as compared to the advertised budget. All revenue adjustments combined total a reduction of $65.2 million.

      Expenditure adjustments for the school operating fund result in a net decrease in expenditures of $113.1 million. Final State General Assembly actions result in the following reductions: a decrease of $109.1 million for VRS retirement; $5.8 million for the VRS retiree health care credit; $5.7 million for state life insurance; and $0.6 million for the triennial census. Additional expenditure reductions of $1.3 million result from contract length reductions for career and technical education and academy teachers and school-based technology specialists. Expenditure increases for County retirement and life insurance require an additional $0.9 million, and funding of $3.0 million is provided for the Priority Schools Initiative which increases the funding available to serve these schools identified based on student need to a total of $4.3 million. To address changes in student membership and demographics, $5.4 million is required primarily due to an increase in the number of students eligible for free and reduced-price meals. This increase results in additional teachers provided to elementary and middle schools through FCPS’ needs-based staffing formula.

      The General Assembly adopted a significantly lower rate, deferring contributions to the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) to mitigate the state budget cuts to localities. In addition to anticipated rate increases, school systems will be required to repay the deferred amounts with interest over a 10 year period. Therefore, the revenue reduction of $65.2 million combined with the net expenditure reduction of $113.1 million results in an ending balance of $48.0 million which will be placed in a reserve to address future VRS requirements.

      Highlights of the FY 2011 School Operating Fund Approved Budget:

          • $2.2 billion budget, a decrease of $72.3 million, or 3.2 percent from the estimate; and $34.5 million, or 1.6 percent, from the FY 2010 Approved
          • Nearly $200 million in budget reductions and cost avoidances including the elimination of 204.3 positions, nearly half of which are from central offices
          • $14.4 million required for membership growth of 1,723 students and demographic shifts resulting in increases in needs-based staffing
          • Cost per pupil decreased by approximately $300 from FY 2010; this is the third consecutive year of decreased spending per pupil
Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board adopt the FY 2011 Approved Budget, to include the Superintendent’s proposed revenue and expenditure adjustments, as detailed in the agenda item, and with the following revisions: (1) directing the Superintendent to begin video streaming on the Fairfax County Public Schools website the School Board meetings that are broadcast on FCPS Red Apple Channel 21 at an estimated cost of $8,000 beginning as soon as practicable by reallocating funding within the Cox Communications grant, and (2) restoring funding of $0.2 million for Advanced Academic Center student transportation and reducing the staffing reserve by $0.2 million.
