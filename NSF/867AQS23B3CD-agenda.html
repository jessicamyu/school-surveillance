Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

Meeting Type: December 18, 2008 – Regular Meeting No.10

SUBJECT School Board Policy

Type: Action

BACKGROUND:

Policy 4680 authorizes redlining of salaries for up to two years for those employees whose salaries have been reduced as the result of a change in position, if that change was made for the benefit of the school system, rather than a voluntary choice of the employee. The policy provides that redlining may be modified by the Board if deemed necessary for budgetary reasons.

KEY POINTS:

The redlining policy plays a valuable role in minimizing morale problems and easing the transition for employees to lower level jobs by insuring that their salary remains at a higher level. However, this policy was envisioned to ease the transition to lower level positions during normal times. The current budget situation places us in extraordinary times and if we are to realize planned savings from reorganizations and budget reductions, the policy needs to be suspended.

Failure to suspend the redlining policy will mean that very little, if any, money is saved in reorganization and in budget-related reductions that involve people moving to lower level positions or shorter contract lengths. As a result, in order to meet the budgetary targets, it will be necessary to conduct additional reductions in force, in which more employees lose their FCPS employment entirely. In order to maximize the number of employees in employment, while spreading the financial burden of budget cuts equitably, staff recommends the Board suspend the redlining policy effective January 1, 2009, until further notice.

The recommended suspension of redlining would be prospective only, and would not impact the salaries of those currently in redline status---unless those employees were subsequently subject to reorganization or RIF. The suspension also would not preclude the Superintendent or Assistant Superintendent for Human Resources to extend salary levels for short periods in order to settle legal disputes.

OPTIONS:

The School Board could maintain the redlining policy in its current form and make compensating budget cuts in programs, or it could implement budget cuts by laying off larger numbers of staff.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons effective January 1, 2009; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations, effective January 1, 2009; the suspension of the policy and implementing regulations shall remain in effect until further School Board action.

PROPOSED BOARD AMENDMENT (in the form of a substitute motion):

Suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period beginning January 1, 2009, and ending June 30, 2011; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations, for the period beginning January 1, 2009, and ending June 30, 2011; the School Board must approve the continued suspension of the policy and implementing regulations beyond June 30, 2011. (Niedzielski-Eichner).

SUBJECT: Early Head Start (EHS)/Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP)/Head Start (HS) Policy Committee

TYPE: New Business

RELATED TO: Student Achievement Goal 1


BACKGROUND:
      According to the Head Start Program Performance Standard 1304.50, a formal structure of program governance provides parents and community representatives with the authority and opportunity to participate in shared decision-making concerning program design and implementation. Parent Committees at each school provide every parent of an enrolled child the opportunity to develop leadership skills, share information, assist in the development of activities that address their interests and needs, and support the education and healthy development of their children. Representatives and alternates from each of the Parent Committees represent their respective schools on the FCPS Early Head Start (EHS) / Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP) / Head Start (HS) Policy Committee, the system-wide program governing body. Representatives and alternates from the FCPS Policy Committee also serve on the county-wide governing body, the Fairfax County EHS/HS Policy Council.

KEY POINTS:
      In accordance with Head Start Program Performance Standards, all FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS parents have the opportunity to participate in shared decision-making concerning the program’s design and implementation through several levels of governing bodies.

      At the school level, parents develop leadership/educational support skills and share information or concerns during their respective parent committee meetings and activities, with the support of FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS program personnel.

      Parent Committee representatives and alternates participate in information sharing and decision-making activities at the school-system level through the FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS Policy Committee, also with support of FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS program personnel.

      Policy Committee representatives and alternates perform similar sharing and decision-making activities on the county-wide EHS/HS Policy Council, with support of the Grantee, Fairfax County Office for Children personnel.


RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board confirms the elected members of the Fairfax County Public Schools Early Head Start (EHS)/Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP)/Head Start (HS) Policy Committee, as listed in the agenda item.
