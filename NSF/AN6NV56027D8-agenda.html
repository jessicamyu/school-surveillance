Crossing Guard Appreciation Day - February 14, 2018

 

The Virginia Safe Route to School program has designated the second Wednesday in February as Crossing Guard Appreciation Day. This is a statewide event to say thank you to the individuals who help students cross the street safely on their way to and from school and will be celebrated in Virginia on Wednesday, February 14.  Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) will observe the day by honoring the 80 crossing guards who manage 174 school-related crossings every day.  FCPS crossing guards are employees of the Fairfax County Police Department.

 

While the focus of Crossing Guard Appreciation Day is on crossing guards and the work that they do, it can also be a good time to remind students how to safely cross the street.

3.08 Recognition - James Madison HS National Winner of the National Band Association's Blue Ribbon Award
Meeting
Feb 8, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category

Recognition

James Madison HS National Band

Winner of the National Band Association's Blue Ribbon Award

 

On behalf of School Board member Pat Hynes and myself, we are pleased to have the Board recognize James Madison’s High School Band this evening on their selection as a National Winner of the prestigious National Band Association's Blue Ribbon Award. The band qualified for national consideration by earning first place in the Southern Division competition last fall and moved forward as the division’s representative. Madison’s band is one of only four national recipients to earn this award.

Congratulations to Director Michael S. Hackbarth, Principal Greg Hood, and band members!

 

Blue Ribbon Award selections are based on a comprehensive assessment of a band program’s consistency in performing at the highest level. Applications must include a minimum of 35 minutes of recorded music, from at least two different school years, with excerpts from multiple ensembles within the band program.  

Written substantiation from the past two to four consecutive years is required to include:

  • a list of all honors and awards received
  • four complete concert band programs
  • rating forms and adjudicator sheets from festivals
  • a comprehensive repertoire list of all works performed
  • a report on the number of students accepted by audition to regional and state honor bands
  • a report of solo and ensemble participants with ratings
  • an estimated number of graduates who continue to perform after high school graduation

 

It is very clear from this detailed assessment of excellence that only the highest caliber band programs qualify for award consideration.   Congratulations on this outstanding achievement, we applaud your talent, dedication, and commitment to excellence!

RESOLUTION HONORING

SUSAN QUINN

 

 

WHEREAS, Susan Quinn has served Fairfax County Public Schools with distinction for a total of 24 years in different roles, including comptroller, assistant superintendent of the Department of Financial Services, and chief operating officer; and

 

WHEREAS, she championed and led the division’s efforts to replace its legacy financial system with an Enterprise Resource Planning system, implemented online travel, and received clean audit opinions every year during her tenure, thereby enhancing support for employees and increasing resource stewardship, and led the district financial management system with unparalleled transparency which brings national awards every year; and

 

WHEREAS, she served on the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC) Board of Trustees, the local 457 supplemental retirement savings plan board, and was chair of the state Other Post-Employment Benefits (OPEB) board; and

 

WHEREAS, she strengthened relationships between County and school staff and government and celebrated employee achievement; and

 

WHEREAS, she gave of herself tirelessly to Fairfax County Public Schools as an employee, a Foundation board member, and as a friend.

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its deepest appreciation to Susan Quinn for her outstanding service and dedication to Fairfax County Public Schools and to our students and community members, and also congratulations and best wishes on her retirement and new journey in life.

Meeting Category: February 8, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject: Jackson Middle School Proposed Boundary Adjustment

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related To: School Board Regulation 8130.9, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closing

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is considering a boundary adjustment to provide capacity relief to Jackson Middle School. Currently, Jackson has more than 1,400 students in a building with design capacity of 1,314 students. Thoreau Middle School, which is close in proximity to Jackson Middle School, recently completed a renovation that increased its design capacity to 1,395 students. Current enrollment at Thoreau is about 900. Enrollment at Thoreau Middle School is projected to have surplus available capacity in the future.

 

Discussion:

 

The process for a standard boundary adjustment is outlined in FCPS Regulation 8130.9. Boundary scope meetings are the first step in the process. In June 2017, parents and guardians were invited to attend three boundary scope meetings to learn about the process and work in small groups to provide feedback to staff about what schools should be considered in the boundary study. Two community boundary study meetings were held on November 30 and December 4, 2017. After the presentation, community members worked in small groups to discuss the information and provide feedback on three options. The feedback was used to provide a recommendation of Option A to the School Board. Option A would reassign Marshall Road, Mosby Woods, and Oakton elementary schools, from Jackson Middle School to Thoreau Middle School, which would affect 309 students. With these reassignments, Thoreau Middle School would be at a capacity level of approximately 90 percent and Jackson Middle School would be at a capacity level of approximately 87 percent. This option would eliminate two existing split-feeders at the elementary-to-middle school level as all Oakton and Marshall Road Elementary school students would now go to Thoreau Middle School. Under Option A, Thoreau Middle School students would progress to Oakton in addition to Madison and Marshall high schools. This option would not create any problems for transportation services.

 

Recommendation:

 

FCPS is recommending that the School Board approve a standard boundary adjustment of some Jackson Middle School students to Thoreau Middle School. Any approved boundary adjustment related to Jackson Middle School will not change the high school boundary. Some students who attend Oakton, Mosby Woods and Marshall Road elementary schools would be reassigned from Jackson Middle School to Thoreau Middle School, if the boundary adjustment Option A is approved.
