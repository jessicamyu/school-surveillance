Summary/Background:

 

The Joint Environmental Task Force, or JET, was formed in April 2019 by the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board.  The JET's mission is to join the political and administrative capabilities of the county and the school system to proactively address climate change and environmental sustainability.

Since its inception, the JET’s primary aim has been to set and meet aggressive goals in areas of common influence such as workforce development, infrastructure and sustainability of public facilities and transportation, land use planning, communication, and community engagement.  The JET provides a forum for collaboration and alignment of institutional policies and practices.

In October 2020, the JET provided its final report which included 28 individual recommendations.  All recommendations fall under one of the following overarching goals:

  • Commit to being carbon neutral by 2040.
  • Transition to electric or zero-carbon alternatives for municipal buses by 2030, and for school buses and eligible fleet vehicles by 2035.
  • Commit to being zero waste by 2030.
  • Partner to create and enhance educational resources, training programs, ad green career opportunities for students, adult learners and working professionals.

FCPS staff have worked collaboratively with the JET since its inception and meet regularly with School Board representatives to the JET, Elaine Tholen and Karl Frisch, to provide progress updates for each recommendation.  A joint response was developed by county and school staff and was presented to the Board of Supervisor’s Environmental Committee (BOSEC) during their April 6, 2021 meeting.  FCPS plans to review the goal recommendations in an upcoming School Board Work Session on June 22, 2021.

The JET goals, while ambitious, present FCPS with an opportunity to review its existing policies and procedures to better align them to meet environmental sustainability – especially regarding transportation, capital improvement, recycling, and workforce development.  They also present challenges that need to be prioritized so FCPS can meet, or substantially implement, each recommendation by the target dates.  FCPS staff will continue working with county staff and the School Board to better define each recommendation and operationalize each goal area to ensure a more sustainable future in Fairfax County.

Looking ahead, the JET will require the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board to establish which recommendations receive the highest priority. Additionally, the Boards will need to work collaboratively to identify funding for the priority recommendations. 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the JET Final Report.
