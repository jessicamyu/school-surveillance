Ignite Link:  Goal 4

 

                    

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Overview

On February 8, 2018, the School Board adopted the FY 2019 Advertised Budget that includes a requested increase of $97.1 million, or 4.9 percent in the county transfer. Expenditure adjustments total $148.2 million and 207.5 positions. The FY 2019 Advertised Budget focuses on employee compensation. Included are funds for enrollment and student demographic adjustments; a step increase for all eligible employees; continued enhancement of the teacher salary scale; and the implementation of nonteacher salary scales to ensure market competitive salaries for all FCPS positions. Other expenditure increases in the FY 2019 Advertised Budget include retirement rate increases, health insurance increases, and the adoption of math and grade 4 social studies instructional resources, Masters Cohort/Tuition Sharing program to support teachers obtaining a Master’s degree and a Tuition Reimbursement Program for all employees, support for equity-related work, and funding to provide wrap-around services to newcomer ESOL students at the high school. Collectively, these are offset by expenditure reductions totaling $29.3 million for a net increase of $118.9 million.

 

County’s Advertised Budget and Advertised Tax Rate

The County Executive presented his FY 2019 Advertised Budget on February 20, 2018, which includes a school operating fund transfer increase of $88.35 million or 4.49 percent. After accounting for additional state funding included in the Governor’s Introduced Budget of $8.7 million, the FY 2019 Advertised Budget is fully funded. The County’s advertised budget recommends a 2.5 cent increase in the current real estate tax rate, which would increase the rate from $1.13 to $1.155 per $100 of assessed value.

 

On February 27, 2018, the School Board and County Board of Supervisors held a joint budget work session to discuss both advertised budgets and the impact of the state budget.

 

On March 6, 2018, the County voted to advertise the real estate tax rate for FY 2019 at $1.155 per $100 of assessed value. Advertising the tax rate allows the Board of Supervisors to adopt the advertised rate or one that is lower, but a higher tax rate cannot be imposed.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS’ School Operating Fund

On February 18, 2018, the House and Senate separately adopted amendments to the Governor’s Introduced 2018-2020 Biennial Budget (HB/SB 30). A summary of the state revenue impacts to FCPS’ operating budget based on the Governor’s Introduced Budget and the proposed amendments from the House and Senate, as compared to FCPS’ FY 2019 Advertised Budget are attached.

 

The General Assembly was unable to reach consensus on its budget by the adjournment date of March 10, 2018. On March 13, 2018, Governor Northam announced that General Assembly will convene its Special Session on April 11, 2018 to complete its budget work.

 

Additional Considerations

Each spring, FCPS updates the enrollment and student demographic projections that are used to calculate staffing for the following school year. Staffing is calculated for each school at both the proposed (fall) and approved (spring) stages of the budget process. The final cost of enrollment growth and changes in student demographics is calculated in the spring, and any changes from the projected cost included in the proposed budget become part of the approved budget adjustments.

 

Based on the final budgets adopted by the state and county, any shortfall that remains will require additional revenue and expenditure adjustments to balance FCPS’ FY 2019 Approved Budget.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

FCPS implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, the School Board and Leadership Team have collaborated to prioritize the plan’s Desired Outcomes within each of the four goal areas to allow for greater focus and alignment over time.

 

The result of this collaboration is a prioritized strategic plan, with four categories of priority:

  • Short-term priority outcomes for 2018-2020;
  • Mid-term priority outcomes for 2020-2022;
  • Long-term priority outcomes for 2012-2024;
  • Sustaining outcomes to be continued during 2018-2024.

 

Refined language for some Desired Outcomes and Actions are being recommended. Metrics have been refined to more accurately reflect the current work of the Division, and Performance Targets have been proposed to gauge progress.

 

Recommendation:

The School Board accept the recommended changes to the Strategic Plan.
