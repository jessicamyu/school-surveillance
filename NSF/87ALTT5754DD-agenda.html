American Education Week - November 14-20, 2010

 

For 89 years, the National Education Association (NEA) has highlighted the importance of public education in America.  The organization’s annual tagline, Great Public Schools:  A Basic Right and Our Responsibility, this year focuses on the critical role parents play, the invaluable services provided by education support professionals, and the importance of substitute teachers.  As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week spotlights the importance of providing each child in America with a quality public education from kindergarten through college, along with the need for everyone to do his or her part to make public schools great.

 

Geography Awareness Week - November 14-20, 2010

 

Geography Awareness Week is an opportunity to focus on geography as a critical component of a 21st century education.  The 2010 theme focuses on fresh water and challenges students to determine if the planet has enough water to support a growing global population, and how a changing climate will affect access to fresh water around the world. 

 

Geography Awareness Week was established by presidential proclamation in 1987 to underscore the importance of geographic understanding to ensure our nation’s economic competitiveness, national security, environmental sustainability, and livability of our communities in the 21st century.

Subject:                     Revised Operational Expectations

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Background (additional explanation):

 

As part of the School Board’s Strategic Governance framework, the School Board adopted a set of Operational Expectations (OEs) for the major functional operations of the school system.  These include such areas as Human Relations, Facilities and Transportation, Technology and others.  These were incorporated into the Board’s Strategic Governance Manual.  In essence these OEs are a set of values/goals/objectives in each of these areas.  After their adoption, the Superintendent and staff then reviewed these OEs and provided back to the Board their Reasonable Interpretation of these values – how they would interpret and “operationalize” them along with a set of measures or indicators.  Since 2006, FCPS has operated based on these OEs. 

 

About a year ago the Board decided it was time to review these OEs and to review the format we used to report on the OEs (called a Monitoring Report).  The Governance Committee led this effort and working with staff, the Board, and the community have already made some significant changes to the format of these Monitoring Reports, to include the following changes: 

 

  • Revising how our indicators/measures are organized – focusing on effectiveness and efficiency measures
  • Adding a section that reports on Customer Satisfaction
  • Adding a section that reports on completed initiatives and their impact
  • Adding a section on issues and trends
  • Adding a section on potential budget implications

Key Points:

 

At the July 23-24, 2010, retreat, the School Board accepted the new Operational Expectations monitoring report format. The Board agreed to a review of all current departmental Operational Expectations to clarify the Board’s values and to ensure alignment with the new monitoring report format.

 

The Governance Committee reviewed all the departmental Operational Expectations on October 13, and recommended several revisions, including the following:

  • Adding a Global Operational Expectation
  • Revising language for Human Resources, Professional Learning and Training, Budget and Financial Management, Facilities and Transportation Services, Community Relations, and Relationship with the Board
  • Revising language for the Instructional Program and Treatment of Students Operational Expectations, resulting in the creation of two separate Operational Expectations—Instructional Program and Learning Environment
  • Incorporating Accountability Operational Expectations into the new Instructional Program Operational Expectations
  • Continuing the audit procedures currently included in the Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations as a function of the Board’s Audit Committee (which is comprised of two School Board members, the Superintendent, and staff) and continuing to provide public access to the information
  • Keeping the Technology Operational Expectations unchanged

The Governance Committee’s recommended revisions were discussed by the entire Board at the October 18 and the November 8 work sessions, and some additional revisions were suggested to Budget and Financial Management and Facilities and Transportation Services Operational Expectations.  All the recommended revisions have been incorporated into the Operational Expectations being recommended for final approval by the Board on November 15.  The Strategic Governance Manual will be updated after Board approval.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve revisions to the Operational Expectations, as detailed in the agenda item.

In the School Operating Fund, the FY 2011 beginning balance increased $84,400 due to audit adjustments made after the FY 2010 Final Budget Review, including an increase of $1.2 million in sales tax revenue based on actual sales tax receipts as well as other revenue adjustments totaling $0.7 million offset by expenditure adjustments totaling $1.8 million.

Staff Contact:            Peter Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services        

 

Meeting Category:    November 15, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 8

 

Subject:                    School Board Policy

 

School Board Action Required:  Information              

 

Key Points:

 

Policy 1345, Modified School Calendar is being rescinded because the district will no longer operate schools using a modified calendar due to budget reductions.  After the Board has rescinded this policy, the appropriate notice will be posted.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board rescind Policy 1345, Modified School Calendar.

6.05 Award of Contract - Recommendation to authorize staff to negotiate a contract for the baseball field lighting project at Jackson Middle School with the top-ranked offeror and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board (FTS; action scheduled 12/2/10)

Nov 15, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 8
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:            Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

                                                                                                           

Meeting Category:    November 15, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 8

 

Subject:                      Award of Contract – Jackson Middle School Sports Field Lighting Replacement Project      

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                     

 

Related To:                School Board Policy 5011.2,  Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

Funding for this project will be provided from the 2009 School Bond Referendum.  Specifications have been prepared and the project has been advertised.  A Request for Proposal has been issued.  Proposals are due November 11, 2010.   A Selection Advisory Committee has been established to review the proposals and will recommend the top-ranked offeror for contract negotiation considerations prior to the November 15, 2010, School Board meeting.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize staff to negotiate a contract for the baseball field lighting project at Jackson Middle School with the top-ranked offeror and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.
