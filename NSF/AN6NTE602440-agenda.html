National Hispanic Heritage Month - September 15-October 15, 2017

 

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-American community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.  This year’s theme is “Shaping the Bright Future of America”

 

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

 

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

 

U. S. Constitution Day and Citizenship Day - September 17, 2017

 

Constitution Day and Citizenship Day are observed each year on September 17 to commemorate the signing of the Constitution on September 17, 1787, and “recognize all who, by coming of age or by naturalization, have become citizens.”  Recent legislation has renamed the day as Constitution Day and Citizenship Day and urges proper observance of the day. This year, the official observance day is Sunday, September 17, and is the 229th anniversary of the nation’s founding document.

 

In observance of Constitution Day and Citizenship Day, all schools in FCPS will be observing this day with activities that recognize the adoption of the United States Constitution and those who have become citizens under the Constitution.  Schools will use a variety of curriculum resources to teach students about the importance of the Constitution and responsible citizenship.  Throughout the school year, high school students will have an opportunity to participate in service learning projects related to responsible citizenship, which can lead to a Civics Seal on their diploma.

The American Childhood Cancer Organization (or ACCO) has designated September as Childhood Cancer Awareness Month to spotlight the types of cancer that largely affect children, survivorship issues, and to help raise funds for research and family support. 

 

According to ACCO, 15,780 children under the age of 21 are diagnosed with cancer every year in the United States; approximately 1/4 of them will not survive the disease. A diagnosis turns the lives of the entire family upside down.

 

ACCO offers support, information and advocacy through their community of families, survivors, and friends affected by childhood and adolescent cancer.  They are dedicated to improving the lives of children and families living with childhood cancer and engaged in this life-altering struggle.  FCPS students being treated for childhood cancer participate in home-bound instruction while they are undergoing treatment or recuperating.

 

We are joined tonight by some of our FCPS family who have been personally impacted by childhood cancer.  Long-time teacher Jay Coakley retired from FCPS after 30 years of teaching physical education, and returned to FCPS as a part-time elementary physical education teacher at Woodburn Elementary.  It was at Woodburn where John met a little girl named Ellie, who had been diagnosed with acute lymphoblastic leukemia in the spring of 2013. By the time she entered kindergarten that fall, Ellie had lost most of her hair and was wearing hats to school every day. 

 

Jay noticed her affinity for “fun and cheery hats” and organized a hat drive to provide Ellie with a broader selection of hats, and Ellie’s Hats was born!  Now thanks to the hard work of the individuals who, like Jay, took an interest in Ellie and her hats, thousands of kids across the country who are battling cancer have received new hats and so much more.  

 

On behalf of the Board, I’d like to encourage everyone to visit the website for Ellie’s Hats at ellieshats.org and the American Childhood Cancer Organization at acco.org, to learn how you can help. 

 

At this time, I would like to invite Jay Coakley, Ellie and her mother, Jennica Whitfield to join the Board at the dias for a photo.

GrandInvolve Recognition

 

In 1979 President Jimmy Carter officially proclaimed the first Sunday after Labor Day each year as National Grandparents Day. This year, September 10th marks the 38-year anniversary of National Grandparents Day.

 

This special day was created with the purpose of honoring grandparents, and to help children recognize the strength, wisdom and resilience that can be learned from older generations. Grandparents Day is a way for the whole family to spend time together by volunteering together.  GrandInvolve received the Outstanding Community Organization Award at the Governor's Volunteerism and Commnity Service Award Ceremony.

 

GrandInvolve began in 2014 when a group of 16 volunteers met and began a pilot program in Crestwood Elementary School involving grandparents in volunteering in the school. GrandInvolve has taken this very important concept of building up our communities by involving grandparents in the lives of younger people and have implemented this model in a number of Fairfax County Public Schools throughout the school year.

 

GrandInvolve has grown to become 108 volunteers serving at 6 schools with 3,016 hours of classroom service time just last year. Currently, GrandInvolve is planning expansion into all Title 1 schools in the Mount Vernon District during the 2017-2018 school year.

 

GrandInvolve serves as an exemplary model of volunteers working together for the betterment of our FCPS community. They have taken the initiative of building intergenerational relationships in our school communities and will continue to make a significant positive impact on our students. On behalf of the Board, I would like to recognize the tremendous efforts of GrandInvolve and encourage grandparents in the audience to consider joining GrandInvolve this year.

 

At this time, I would like to invite Dorothy Keenan and all representatives from GrandInvolve in the audience to join the Board at the dias for a photo.

3.07 Recognition of Dr. Recardo Sockwell
Meeting
Sep 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type

We would like to take a moment this evening to recognize one of our valued employees who is retiring with almost 20 years of experience with FCPS, Dr. Recardo Sockwell. 

When Recardo first arrived in FCPS 18 years ago, he brought the concept of accountability and strategic targets to the school division.  His impact started with drafting the original 10 Targets to streamline division-level instructional focus.  These later evolved into the three Student Achievement Goals, and more recently into the instructional goals in Ignite.  In each case, the legacy of Recardo’s original vision for Target reporting – that is, publicly reporting on evidence for set metrics and making judgments about whether we are making progress – has been continued.

 

Recardo also impacted the way FCPS conceptualizes its management of programs and budgeting for programs.  He pioneered the concept of an identified program manager for each program, who would be officially responsible for providing guidance about each FCPS program and the use of resources allocated to the program.  Implementation of the program manager concept by the school division then allowed FCPS to develop a program budget document that captured the funding for and use of funds for each program. For the first time, program budgets were developed by Financial Services and directly linked to the program manager.

 

The program manager concept was further solidified when the Office of Program Evaluation took on the task of developing the School Board’s suggestion to create a system to evaluate all programs. The outcome of this request was the institution of the Quality Programs Assurance System, QPAS, developed by Recardo and his office.  QPAS had several levels that gave responsibilities to both Recardo’s office for the evaluation of programs, as well as to program manager’s for documenting and reviewing their programs in relation to set goals.  The Transparency project with the county linking to FCPS’ program evaluations and program profiles would not exist if not for his work.

 

Recardo was also the genesis of FCPS’ adoption of Deming’s Plan-Do-Study-Act cycles of continuous improvement over a decade ago. PDSA has been adopted by FCPS schools as a way to improve instruction and continues on, as well, at the division-level in FCPS’ current approach to decision-making.

 

The list of other tools, surveys, and processes that Recardo developed himself or made better through influence is too long to mention in its entirety but includes SAG performance bands, a survey to track the development of PLCs in our schools, a taxonomy of different stakeholders to data evidence questions, a set of research-based standards for professional development, and a process for systematic selection of Teacher Leader grant recipients. The impact of the myriad tools, surveys, and processes will continue on in their present or evolved form for many years to come.

 

His legacy includes an office staffed with individuals who have always been pushed to think beyond the surface of the work to what would be most impactful in moving the division toward improvement that was best for students.  He always provided whatever level of support was needed to ensure office products exceeded expectations and always gave the credit for the final product to staff, not himself.

 

Recardo will forever be the one and only director of the Office of Program Evaluation and the reason its name was appropriately changed to the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement.  The new name represents the broader and strategic impact he always brought to any task his office took on and may represent the most long-lasting legacy of his tireless work over two decades to help FCPS think and act more strategically.

 

We wish Dr. Sockwell and his family all the best in his retirement and thank him for his years of dedication to the students and staff of Fairfax County Public Schools.  At this time I would like to invite Dr. Sockwell and his guests to join me at the dais for a picture with the Board.

5.02 Modification of the Contract – Additional Site Work for Synthetic Turf Replacement Project at Sandburg Middle School
Meeting
Sep 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Action Items - 8:30 p.m.
Type
Action

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category: September 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Modification of the Contract – Additional Site Work for Synthetic Turf Replacement Project at Sandburg Middle School

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

 

Related To: School Board Policy 5011.3 Authority to Contract  

 

 

Key Points:

 

At a public meeting on April 27, 2017, the School Board voted to award a contract to Sprinturf, LLC in the amount of $483,865 for the synthetic turf field replacement project at Sandburg Middle School. The project was initiated at the request of, and fully funded by, Fairfax County Park Authority (the “Park Authority”).

 

Following contract award and commencement of on-site construction activities, the parties discovered that the current condition of the subgrade, upon which a stone base was installed during the initial field installation in 2007, was not sufficient to support the new turf field and its activities at this time. Areas of soil instability and subsequent subsurface investigations confirmed a high water table and an expansive amount of clay material at that level. This subsurface soil condition was not anticipated, since the 2007 installation had proceeded smoothly and without subsurface issues.

 

With the assistance of an independent geotechnical firm, FCPS Office of Design and Construction Services (D&C) decided upon a remediation plan that would provide a 6% Portland cement injection into the soil at a depth of 12 inches in order to stabilize the subgrade below the field. This change will allow the field to be reconstructed in accordance with FCPS and industry standards, as well as to maintain the specified warranty.

 

D&C requested and negotiated pricing for this additional work from Sprinturf, the funding for which has been approved by the Park Authority. D&C recommends that Sprinturf be assigned this additional work so that Sprinturf is responsible for the warranty of the entire field. Sprinturf has sufficient local manpower to execute this change in the scope of work in an efficient and timely manner. 

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the modification of Sprinturf’s contract to include performance of additional site work (involving the stabilization of the subgrade of the field at Sandburg Middle School) in return for payment of  the additional amount of $412,290 and, upon receipt of such funds from the Park Authority, hereby authorize the Division Superintendent or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute, deliver, and administer the modified contract on behalf of the School Board.

  1. This policy establishes expectations for suicide prevention efforts, individual suicide risk assessment and intervention, and response and supports for students and staff following a death by suicide.
  1. Prevention:
    1. Training for educators, parents and students on signs of depression and suicidal thinking and how to refer for help, and include information on reducing stigma
    2. Wellness screenings
    3. Access to school-based mental health providers (school counselors, school psychologists, school social workers)
    4. Crisis support services
    5. Response to reports of students at risk due to mental health conditions or life circumstances, including those identified on Student Health Information Form
  1. Suicide Risk Assessment:
    1. Procedures for responding to report of suicidal thinking by student
    2. Determination of risk
    3. Parent notification
    4. Referral to appropriate resources
  1. Postvention:
    1. Procedures for providing response to school community following a death by suicide
    2. Provision of grief counseling
    3. Identification of youth at risk
    4. Parent notification and referral to appropriate resources as needed

Subject: Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on June 20, 2017, the updated version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2017 session of the General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

County’s Administrative Changes Relevant to FCPS

 

  • Cooperative Procurement:  Add authority to purchase from a contract from the National Association of Counties, reference 2007 Virginia Acts of Assembly 330.  See Attachment I at page 5.
  • Definitions:  Add debar and suspension and modify definition of ineligibility to be consistent with the VPPA.  See Attachment I at pages 7, 8, and 11.
  • Exceptions:  Add exception to the requirement for competitive procurement for Finance Board Investments as provided in Code of Virginia §15.2-1548.  See Attachment I at page 24.
  • Ineligibility:  Modifies appeals period in response to a Notice of Debarment from 30 days to 10 days as provided in the Code of Virginia, §2.2-4357.  See Attachment I at page 54.
  • Ineligibility:  Modifies criteria and time periods for debarment or suspension for consistency.  See Attachment I at pages 53-55.
  • Protest of Award or Decision to Award:  Adds text to prohibit the challenge of the validity of the terms or conditions of the solicitation as provided in the Code of Virginia, §2.2-4360.  See Attachment I at page 57. 

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015 and 2016 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2017 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

  • The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  • The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopts the newly revised 2017 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.
