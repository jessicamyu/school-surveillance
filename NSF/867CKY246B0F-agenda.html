RESOLUTION HONORING
DEIRDRA MCLAUGHLIN

    WHEREAS, Deirdra McLaughlin has served Fairfax County Public Schools for nearly 23 years with dedication and distinction as a budget analyst, budget coordinator, payroll director, budget director, and, most recently, chief financial officer, always demonstrating prudent stewardship of financial resources; and

    WHEREAS, throughout her tenure with Fairfax County Public Schools, the Department of Financial Services consistently and willingly provided the School Board and stakeholders with accurate, timely, and relevant financial information and guidance, fostering effective decision making and ensuring a sound foundation for our educational programs; and

    NAMING THE AUDITORIUM AT
    OAKTON HIGH SCHOOL
    “THE ROBERT ‘SKIP’ BROMLEY AUDITORIUM”


    WHEREAS, Robert Bromley has served the students of Oakton High School and the surrounding community as an innovative drama teacher for 19 years, during which time he has directed and produced more than 125 theatrical productions performed by Oakton students, one of which received first place at the Virginia High School League One Act Play Festival and a number of other plays that were chosen to compete at the VHSL district level; and

    WHEREAS, he has initiated and supported Oakton High School’s Improv group which has been highly ranked and come in first place in a number of competitions and has consistently performed at a variety of events at Oakton and at many other venues; and

    WHEREAS, he has been actively involved in the maintenance and updating of Oakton’s auditorium and directed a highly professional yearly musical, drawing very large audiences and building Oakton’s reputation as a viable school and community theater; and

    WHEREAS, he has designed an interdisciplinary curriculum as an adjunct to the FCPS theater program of studies that enriched the knowledge base of his students and enhanced their appreciation of the performing arts, literature, history, and life skills and has expanded an inherited theater program that appeals to a diverse group of students who have greatly benefited from his unparalleled teaching style; and

    WHEREAS, he has created a sense of community for a wide range of students, some seeking the safety to express their individuality in less traditional ways, some desiring a respite from the traditional educational setting, some desiring to hone their dramatic skills, some wanting to simply experience joy; all welcome and encouraged to maximize their potentials as students, performers, and human beings;

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the auditorium at Oakton High School “The Robert ‘Skip’ Bromley Auditorium” in appreciation of Mr. Bromley’s contributions to the individuals, communities, and school division that he so happily served, thereby leaving a legacy of educational excellence in Fairfax County Public Schools.

      4.05 South County Secondary School - Approve an addition to South County Secondary School to meet enrollment needs of the school in the near term and as a less expensive option to building a new middle school (FTS; presented as new business 6/5/08; work session held 6/9/08)
      Meeting
      Jun 19, 2008 - Regular Meeting No. 22
      Category

      Action

      Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities & Transportation Services

      Meeting Type: June 19, 2008 - Regular No. 22

      SUBJECT: South County Secondary School

      TYPE: Action

      BACKGROUND:

          The South County Secondary School (SCSS) is operating on a 9-period day as a means to manage its overcrowding. While designed to be a high school with a capacity of 2,500 students, it has operated since it opened in 2005 as a secondary school. Its current enrollment is approximately 2,960 students. It is necessary to provide relief to the school either by enhancing capacity or by reducing enrollment, or some combination thereof.

      KEY POINTS:
              · Comparison of projected and actual student yield in the South County Secondary School attendance area.
              · Impacts of anticipated residential growth, by-right development and rezoning.
              · Impacts of BRAC (Base Realignment and Closure).
              · Projected enrollments for South County Middle School’s feeder schools.

      OPTIONS: 
          Solution options that were considered:
                1.              Boundary changes.

                2.              Build a new middle school to serve this area.

                3.              Build an addition to the existing school.

                4.              Relocate programs.

          Based upon the fact that we do not project significant increases in enrollment at SCSS, we recommend an addition to the existing school.  This option is less expensive than building a new middle school and will provide sufficient capacity to meet the enrollment needs of the school in the near term.  Assuming cooperation from the county and the absence of unusual circumstances, we believe the addition can be built in time for the opening of the 2010-2011 school year. 
       RECOMMENDATION:

        That the School Board approve an addition to South County Secondary School to meet enrollment needs of the school in the near term and as a less expensive option to building a new middle school.

      MEETING TYPE: June 19, 2008 – Regular Meeting No. 22
      SUBJECT: Amendments to ERFC and ERFC 2001and to the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County Benefit Restoration Plan (BRP)
      TYPE: Action

      BACKGROUND:
        The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending several technical amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) and to the ERFC Benefit Restoration Plan (BRP).

      A summary of the proposed amendments in each category is provided below.
          A. Describing How the Plan Complies with Certain Regulations Recently Issued by the Internal Revenue Service.
          The proposed amendments in paragraphs 6 through 9 (amending sections 17.01 and 17.03 of ERFC and sections 12.01 and 12.03 of ERFC 2001) are for the purpose of specifying how ERFC and ERFC 2001 will comply with the final regulations that IRS recently issued regarding the limits on benefits payable under defined benefit plans. The new regulations are complex and require each plan to adopt plan language specifying how it will comply with the regulations with regard to certain practices where the regulations permit alternative methods of compliance.

          To the extent that the regulations require such specification, the proposed amendments specify the practices that ERFC and ERFC 2001 will follow.

          If approved, these proposed amendments would be effective January 1, 2009. The IRS regulations permit public employee plans to adopt a delayed effective date, measured from the end of the first legislative session beginning after the final regulations were published. Because benefits under ERFC and ERFC 2001 must be aggregated, for at least some purposes, with benefits payable from VRS, and VRS benefits can be amended only in Virginia Legislative sessions, the effective date for the amendments to ERFC and ERFC 2001 will conform to the rule applicable to VRS amendments.

          B. Enabling the Retirement Office to Simplify an Interest Calculation for Certain Refunds
          In certain limited circumstances, ERFC permits members to obtain refunds of money they have paid for purchased service credit, with interest. Such refunds are infrequent. The current process of designing a new automated system for plan administration has identified a simplified method of calculating the interest on those refunds that would significantly reduce the administrative burden with only a negligible increase in the amount of the interest. The proposed amendment in paragraph 3 (amending section 11.07 of ERFC) would allow the trustees to adopt a regulation implementing that simplified method for calculating interest.
          C. Making Other Technical Corrections and/or Clarifications without Changing Benefits or Administrative Practices
          The proposed amendments in paragraphs 1, 2, 4, and 5 (amending sections 11.01, 11.03, and 12.02 of ERFC and section 7.02 of ERFC 2001) are solely for the purpose of making technical corrections (such as correcting cross-references) and clarifying plan provisions without affecting benefits or administrative practices.
          D. Clarification in the Benefit Restoration Plan
          Proposed Amendment Number 1 to the BRP is for the purpose of clarifying that the School Board has retained broad discretion, in the event it ever were to terminate the BRP, to decide whether and in what form to continue payment of any benefits under that plan.
          FOR YOUR INFORMATION:
          Before January 31, 2009, the plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001 must be submitted to the Internal Revenue Service for a determination that the plan continues to comply with applicable provisions of the Internal Revenue Code. In order to make that submission, ERFC will prepare restated plan documents (which will simply incorporate into each document all of the amendments that the School Board has approved since the last restatement, which occurred in 2001) and submit them to the School Board for formal approval. ERFC expects to submit those restated documents for approval in December 2008.
      RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board: (1) approve proposed Amendment Number 14 to ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purposes of (A) describing h ow the plan complies with certain regulations recently issued by the Internal Revenue Service; (B) enabling the retirement office to simplify an interest calculation for certain refunds; and (C) making other technical corrections and/or clarifications without changing benefits or administrative practices, and (2) approve proposed Amendment Number 1 to BRP to clarify certain provisions.

          Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

          Meeting Type: June 19, 2008 - Regular Meeting No. 22

          SUBJECT: Purchase and Sale Agreement for 8111 Gatehouse Road

          TYPE: New Business

          BACKGROUND:
              On November 16, 2004, the School Board approved the consolidation of Fairfax County Public Schools administrative offices into a central campus in Merrifield. Once completed, this action will combine approximately 1,700 personnel from twelve School Board-owned properties and five leased offices into a single campus.

              Phase I of the consolidation was completed in May 2006 with the acquisition of the existing campus properties at Jefferson Park consisting of one building (Gatehouse Administration Center I) with approximately 208,000 square feet, that houses approximately 650 staff members. In addition, an adjacent 3.13-acre parcel was acquired that is currently undeveloped and used for overflow parking.

              The debt service costs for Phase I of the consolidation are fully supported through savings achieved from the expiration of leases; efficiencies, expense, and staff reductions; and the avoidance of future renovation and major maintenance costs for those owned properties that became surplus and were transferred to Fairfax County as a result of the consolidation. This transaction benefited the school system’s capital program through an increase in capital funds resulting from the transfer of surplus properties to the County. These increased funds for the capital program have been and will be used only for the construction and renovation of schools.

              The second and final phase of the consolidation can be achieved with the acquisition of an approximately 275,000 square foot facility that is located at the Jefferson Park complex. The negotiated purchase price of the facility is $52 million, plus an amount up to $5 million to reimburse the Seller for certain documented renovations made by the seller to the property. In addition to these acquisition costs, the School Board is expected to finance approximately $53.1 million to renovate and furnish the facility.

              It is anticipated that the acquisition of this property will be funded initially by Bond Anticipation Notes, and then permanently financed over a 26.5-year period by bonds issued by the Economic Development Authority. Under the anticipated financing structure, the School Board will not begin paying debt service until FY2013. The debt service and building operating costs will be fully funded from savings from lease expirations, position reductions, and operational efficiencies as delineated in the attached business model. These savings also will provide an additional $1 million per year, which can be used for instructional needs within the school system, or can be classified as overall expenditure savings.
              The Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA), upon execution, provides for a 60-day Due Diligence Period that will serve a variety of useful purposes. First of all, staff and technical specialists will have the opportunity to inspect and evaluate the physical property and all building systems. Equally important, this 60-day period will provide time for the public and other stakeholders to review and comment on the proposed transaction. Under the PSA, the School Board has the right to terminate the PSA for any reason during the 60-day Due Diligence Period and to receive a refund of the $500,000 deposit. Finally, the School Board’s obligations under the PSA are expressly contingent, among other things, upon action by the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors in connection with the financing.

          KEY POINTS:
              Debt Service costs will continue to be supported through operational savings, thus there will be no direct or indirect negative impact to the capital program.

              The size of the 8111 Gatehouse Road facility will enable the School Board to elect to vacate the following administrative facilities, which may thereafter be used for other functions: Devonshire Center, Dunn Loring Center, and Lacey Center.

          OPTIONS:

          None recommended.

          RECOMMENDATION:
              That the School Board 1.) authorize school system staff to negotiate a Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA) with 8111 Gatehouse Investors, LLC, as seller, that will provide for the purchase of the real property and improvements located at 8111 Gatehouse Road, Merrifield, Virginia; the payment by the School Board of a $52 million purchase price and the reimbursement to seller for certain pre-closing renovation costs not to exceed $5 million; and such other provisions as are customary for such transactions and consistent with the terms presented in closed session; 2.) authorize the Chief Operating Officer to execute and deliver, after consultation with the School Board’s attorney, a PSA in substantial conformity with the foregoing; 3.) direct staff to engage the community and obtain feedback about the transaction and to report back to the School Board on September 4, 2008, with the results of the community engagement process and with a final recommendation.
