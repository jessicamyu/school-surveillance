Agenda Item: Succession Planning and LEAD Fairfax Update

Staff Contact: Kevin North

Background
    Succession Management continues to be a key issue for FCPS. Since 2000, we have experienced 60% turnover in principal positions. A $5 million grant from the Wallace Foundation entitled “LEAD Fairfax” helps support our leadership development efforts. LEAD Fairfax has been successful in preparing and developing leaders and integrates a variety of programs designed to meet the development needs of each group. Support is provided along a continuum to teacher’s aspiring to become administrators, to assistant principals to principals. A variety of methods are used, including learning cohorts and internships, to achieve developmental objectives. We are now completing the third year of this five-year grant.

Key Issues
· Anticipated high levels of turnover in leadership positions
· Need to prepare and develop the next generation of leaders to meet future needs

Agenda Item: Medicare Rx


Staff Contact: Kevin North, 703-750-8450


Background

FCPS, along with all employers providing drug benefits in their Medicare eligible health plans, are reviewing the ongoing release of information in order to comply with the Medicare Drug Improvement and Modernization Act (MMA) of 2003. MMA provides Medicare beneficiaries with more choices and strengthens the current Medicare program relating to prescription drug benefit. It also requires all employers providing drug benefits to Medicare participants, both active and retirees, to take action relating to the provision of drug benefits. Effective January 1, 2006, FCPS will be required to provide standardized reporting to the Center for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) and also qualifies for significant federal subsidy funds available for providing drug benefits as our plans are structured today.

Key Issues

· Communications components
· Required compliance relating to federal reporting and benefit levels
· Federal subsidy opportunity in first year estimated of $1.9 million
· Retiree subsidy program commitments

School Board Discussion/Decision

This presentation is provided to inform the Board of the impact of Medicare Rx and compliance implications going forward. FCPS, along with all employers providing drug benefits in their health plans with Medicare eligible participants, are reviewing the ongoing release of information in order to comply with the Medicare Drug Improvement and Modernization Act (MMA) of 2003. Discussion points include:
· Medicare changes effective January 1, 2006.
· Qualification for subsidy funding.
· Challenges.
· 2007 and beyond.

Staff Recommendation

Effective January 1, 2006, FCPS will participate in the MMA employer subsidy offering, allowing the following to occur:
· Report to CMS required participant information.
· Qualify for and receive an estimated $1.9 million dollars from the Federal government back as a subsidy for providing this level of benefit to our Medicare eligible population.
· Continue to monitor market developments relating to the provision of Medicare eligible participants in our plans to allow us to maximize both the member benefit and the system’s cost containment.
