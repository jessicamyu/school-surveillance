Childhood Immunization Month - April 2007
The Fairfax County School Board recognizes the efficacy of immunization programs in the prevention of the spread of childhood diseases, the School Board promotes sound health practices for all children, and the School Board establishes policies that facilitate access to health services for all children.

Immunizations are available to Fairfax County parents and their children from public and private resources, and, for every $1 spent on immunizations, $10 can be saved in future medical costs.

Autism Awareness Month - April 2007

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.

Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about autism. It is sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA). Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

Mathematics Awareness Month - April 2007

The definition of mathematics is associated with the investigation of patterns of structure, change, and space. It is often referred to as the study of “figures and numbers.” Although not considered a natural science, mathematics is the vehicle used to investigate and add order to the natural sciences. Mathematics as an art form rather than as a practical or applied science lends aesthetics and order to the arts and architecture. Mathematics as a literacy allows all citizens full participation in a global society. In our lives, mathematics structures our time, patterns of change, and our space. In our daily lives, “math matters.”

Mathematics Awareness Month is held each year in April. Its goal is to increase public understanding of and appreciation for mathematics. Mathematics Awareness Month began in 1986 as Mathematics Awareness Week with a proclamation by President Ronald Reagan. In 1999, Mathematics Awareness Week became Mathematics Awareness Month.

6.04 Memorandum of Agreement - Recommendation to approve the Memorandum of Agreement with the Fairfax County Park Authority and Board of Supervisors for the installation of turf fields on school sites, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the Memorandum of Agreement on behalf of the School Board (FTS; action scheduled 4/12/07)
Meeting
Mar 22, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 16
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information



Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services
SUBJECT: Memorandum of Agreement

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve the Memorandum of Agreement with the Fairfax County Park Authority and Board of Supervisors for the installation of turf fields on school sites, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the Memorandum of Agreement on behalf of the School Board.
EXPLANATION:
      In FY 2006 $10 million in park bonds was authorized for the conversion of up to 12 existing natural turf rectangular fields to synthetic turf. The FCPA desires to construct certain of these synthetic turf fields on school sites and in collaboration with FCPS staff. The Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) is specific to the installation of turf fields that will be 100 percent funded by Fairfax County Park Authority bond proceeds. The agreement covers not only initial installation but also maintenance and use of these fields in support of county programs.

      Specific requests for approval of synthetic turf installation contracts at school sites will be brought to the School Board as FCPA bond funds are made available to FCPS. Approval of the MOA does not obligate FCPS to approve installation of turf fields at any school sites.

      No FCPS appropriated funds will be utilized to install turf fields as a part of this agreement.
