    National Women's History Month - March 2005

    Women of all races, creeds, and ethnic origins have contributed significantly to the history and development of the United States, and modern American women are world leaders in many fields of endeavor.

    The study of women's history reminds us that outstanding women achieved much in times when little was expected of them and before public education provided an avenue to equal opportunity.


    Purchasing Month - March 2005

    Public procurement and fixed asset management professionals support and enhance the delivery of government services by promoting ethical purchasing practices.

    Public purchasing involves managing goods, establishing sources and shipping through inventory and warehousing, disposing of surplus items, and determining which product or service to buy to meet a specific need at the lowest possible price within the required deadline without sacrificing quality or service.

    National Sleep Awareness Week 2005

    The School Board recognizes National Sleep Awareness Week March 28 through April 3rd.  Sleep Awareness Week brings attention to the importance of adequate sleep for health and safety, and a reminder of sleep-related responsibilities, such as not driving while drowsy and parents ensuring that their children get the sleep they need to learn effectively.  Let’s all remember as we set our clocks to daylight savings times on April 3rd - “Sleep Well Tonight for a Better Tomorrow.”

    NAMING
    THE LIBRARY AT KILMER CENTER
    “THE DEBBIE LEWIS LIBRARY”

    WHEREAS, Debbie Lewis, who passed away on May 19, 2004, proudly served the students at the Kilmer Center for 24 years as a public health attendant and, as a life long citizen of Vienna and graduate of Fairfax County Public Schools, was respected by all who knew her; and
    WHEREAS, she viewed every part of her job as highly important, never wavered in her desire to become better at what she did, and was a tireless worker who took pride in her job and seldom missed a day of work; and
    WHEREAS, she dedicated her work to her “kids,” always placing their needs above her own, and learned how to read so that she could read to them each day; and
    WHEREAS, she excelled as a Special Olympian, the consummate teammate in soccer, basketball, and bowling; and
    WHEREAS, she was valued as a friend, coworker, and babysitter, offering unconditional loyalty to those with whom she worked, and she was truly a teacher to all privileged enough to have known her and worked with her;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the Kilmer Center library “The Debbie Lewis Library” in memory of Debbie Lewis and in recognition of her dedication and commitment to the special needs of her “kids” at the Kilmer Center.

    NAMING THE SOFTBALL FIELD AND FACILITY AT
    WEST SPRINGFIELD HIGH SCHOOL
    “THE ROBBIE BENTON PARK”

    WHEREAS, Robbie Benton graduated from West Springfield High School in 1969 and returned to serve his alma mater with distinction for 31 years as a teacher, a coach, the math department chair, and the assistant director of student activities; and
    WHEREAS, Robbie Benton coached the West Springfield High School softball team from 1980 through 1999, during which time his teams won eight District titles, two Regional titles, and the 1983 State Championship; and
    WHEREAS, he raised the money necessary to install the first set of lights on a Fairfax County high school softball field, and he personally built the dugouts, snack bar, backstop, and outfield fence and set, by hand, the first electric softball scoreboard on that softball field; and
    WHEREAS, he has promoted softball throughout the entire area, serving as Regional Coaches Association president for ten years and being inducted into the Greater Washington Fast Pitch Softball Hall of Fame; and
    WHEREAS, Robbie Benton has served as an esteemed mentor, a role model, and an inspiration to hundreds of student-athletes as “Coach Benton”;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the softball field and facility at West Springfield High School, “The Robbie Benton Park” to honor this teacher, coach, and eternal Spartan for his tireless efforts to make this park the model for all.

    Staff Contact: Betsy Fenske, Assistant Superintendent, Cluster V

    SUBJECT: Naming of School

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board name the new secondary school to be opened in September 2005 at the South County site “South County Secondary School.”

    EXPLANATION:
        On February 7, 2005, a meeting was held at Halley Elementary School for the purpose of naming the new secondary school at the South County site. Those present included Mount Vernon District School Board member Daniel Storck, At Large School Board members Stephen Hunt and Janet Oleszek, Cluster V Coordinator Judy Matlock, Principal Dale Rumberger, and representatives from the community. In accordance with Regulation 8170.3, public notice of the meeting included community newspaper articles, information on the FCPS website, and letters sent to parents in the affected communities.

        The parents, students, staff members, and community members who attended the meeting recommended and considered a total of 55 names for the new school. By consensus, the list was narrowed to 37 recommended names (see attachment). After presentations, questions and audience discussion, the participants voted for their top three choices using a weighted point system (first choice = 3 points, second choice = 2 points, and third choice = 1 point). The name “South County Secondary School” received the most support.

    SUBJECT: Amendment to Contract for Purchase of Electric Service

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve the current contractual amendments for the purchase of electric service from Northern Virginia Electric Cooperative (NOVEC) and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, on behalf of the School Board, to negotiate future contractual amendments with NOVEC periodically and to execute all such current and future contract documents with NOVEC on behalf of the School Board.

    EXPLANATION:
      NOVEC provides electric service to portions of the Chantilly, Centreville and Clifton areas of Fairfax County.

      Along with other local governmental entities, including the Board of Supervisors of Fairfax County, the Fairfax County Public Schools participated in a collective negotiation of certain contractual amendments with NOVEC. These negotiations have resulted in a number of favorable changes, including:
    · A contract extension through January 31, 2011.
    · A rate reduction of 4.28 percent below current rates.
    · Possibility of another rate reduction during the period 2008-2011 based on NOVEC’s rate of return on capital.
    · Establishment of enhanced rights of audit and customer service for FCPS.

      It is estimated the rate reduction, which has been negotiated, would save FCPS $67,500 per year, or $405,000 over the remaining six year period of the contract, as extended by this amendment.

      During the negotiations, the local government customer group has been assisted by an outside consulting firm and by special counsel. The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors approved a similar contractual amendment at its February 28, 2005, meeting.

    Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

    SUBJECT: Award of Contracts

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board award contracts for: (1) provision of physical examinations for school bus drivers, medical examinations as part of applications for disability retirement, and occupational safety and health to INOVA Urgent Care Health Systems (INOVA); and (2) provision of alcohol and drug testing for employees in positions requiring commercial drivers licenses (CDLs), employees under reasonable suspicion, and students involved in extracurricular athletic programs who are under reasonable suspicion to ChoicePoint Services Inc., effective April 1, 2005, through December 31, 2010, as recommended by the Selection Advisory Committee (SAC).

    EXPLANATION:
        The Code of Virginia, Section 22.1-178, requires school bus drivers to undergo physical examinations upon initial employment and annually thereafter. In addition, the Virginia Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) requires an occupational safety and health physical for employees who work with hazardous materials. This includes initial examinations and annual respiratory examinations. INOVA is recommended by the SAC to perform these services at a cost of $88.00 per FCPS physical examination. INOVA services will also include physicals for employees working with hazardous materials.
        The Federal Omnibus Transportation Employee Act of 1991 requires pre-employment and random alcohol and drug testing of all employees who possess a CDL. In addition, FCPS Policy 4140.1, Physical and Mental Examinations, and Policy 3860.3 and Regulation 3860.6, Extracurricular Activities, provide for the testing of student athletes for controlled substances. ChoicePoint is recommended by the SAC to provide these services at a charge of $ 32.00 for alcohol testing, and $ 40.75 for drug testing.

        The projected cost for the first two years of the five-year contracts is $185,617. For the final three years of the contracts, the cost for ChoicePoint remains the same, and the cost for INOVA increases three percent each year.

        The Constitution of Virginia gives the Virginia Board of Education the authority to adopt textbooks for use in schools.

      Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Regulation 3004.2, Adopted Basal Instructional Materials, incorporates state adoption guidelines and requirements and specifies procedures for establishing committees to review state-adopted and other textbooks and basal instructional materials and to make recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board for local adoptions.       · Teachers – 24
      · School-based administrators – 12
      · Community members – 12

School-based administrators and teachers will be nominated by cluster assistant superintendents and staff members from the Instructional Services Department, Department of Special Services, and schools.
