Virginia Career Week, October 10-16, 2010

 

Recognizing the importance of preparing all young citizens of the Commonwealth to make sound career decisions, Governor McDonnell has declared October 10-16 as Virginia Career Week.  There are hundreds of teachers, counselors, career center and career experience specialists in our system who work to assist students in their career decisions and preparation.

 

 

National School Lunch Week, October 11-15, 2010

Using the theme School Lunch-What’s on Your Tray? National School Lunch Week focuses on helping students learn how eating a healthy and nutritious lunch will help them succeed in school as well as at their other activities.  The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own nutrition habits, in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

 

Teen Read Week, October 17-23, 2010

 

Teen Read Week encourages teens to go beyond the traditional book and explore poetry, audiobooks, books about music, and more at their school and local library.  Using the theme Books With Beat @ Your Library®, Teen Read Week has been observed since 1998 in an effort to engage teens in reading for pleasure and fun and to learn about the free resources that are available at the library.  Research shows that teens who read for fun have better test scores and are more likely to succeed in the workforce.  The School Board encourages all FCPS teenage students to take advantage of the resources in their school and public library and to become lifelong readers and learners.

National School Bus Safety Week - October 18-22, 2010

The week of October 18-22 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. The safe transportation of children in Fairfax County Public Schools is a matter of concern for all citizens of Fairfax County, and the reduction of accidents requires vigilance on the part of all motorists.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 110,000 students in over 1,500 school buses traveling almost 15 million operational miles each year.

Custodian Appreciation Week - October 11-15, 2010

The School Board recognizes the hard work and unending dedication of the custodians who work for Fairfax County Public Schools. Students, faculty, and community members continually benefit from the tireless efforts of these individuals. Our custodians are the first employees to arrive in the morning and the last employees to leave at night. By providing clean and safe school environments, the custodians at FCPS have a direct impact on the educational success of our students. The week of October 11-15 has been named Custodian Appreciation Week to recognize and thank our dedicated Fairfax County Public Schools custodians.

FAIRFAX COUNTY SCHOOL BOARD
Jackson Middle School, Falls Church, VA
Regular Meeting #5
October 7, 2010

Speakers have three minutes to address the Board
 
 

Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so online starting at 6 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.
