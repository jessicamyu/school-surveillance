Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

 

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

SCHOOL BOARD CHARACTER AWARD

Jamie Michelle Frear

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board Chris Giovarelli and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began funding a $500 award for the winner.  The award is given to a junior or senior, who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for other Fairfax County Public Schools students in day-to-day behavior. 

 

Jamie Michelle Frear is the recipient of the 2013 School Board Character Award, chosen from a pool of outstanding nominees.

 

Jamie is an exceptional young woman.  A senior at Mountain View Alternative High School, Jamie has been described as “well beyond her years in terms of responsibility and maturity” and having care and empathy as natural traits.  She is an outstanding student and an extraordinary role model and mentor to her peers and teachers.  Jamie’s work ethic is impeccable and her commitment to the community is infallible.  Her dedication to her studies is impressive, as she has earned enough credits to graduate a year early.

 

As a member of the Student Congress and president of the Environmental Club, she is a leader in her school community.  She has spoken at new student orientations, volunteered to read to elementary school students through a program called Reading Buddies, and created a video announcement for the Service Learning Committee. She has represented her peers on the School Improvement plan and attended a leadership training hosted by the Alliance for Climate Education.  When she’s not doing school work or involved in community service or fundraisers, she plays soccer and works part-time.  Jamie does all these activities while being a single mom to her daughter, Abby. 

 

Jamie has been an inspiration to not only her peers, but to her teachers, who have described her as “wise beyond her years.” Congratulations Jamie, and best wishes as you continue your studies to become an elementary school teacher.

Meeting Category:     June 6, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 21

 

Subject:                     Student Rights and Responsibilities, Regulation 2601.27P

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:                Legal Requirement 

 

Key Points:

 

Regulation 2601.27P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, has been rewritten and reflects consideration of the Ad Hoc Community Committee on Student Rights and Responsibilities recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board. Major proposed revisions to the SR&R include:

 

1. Preamble with overarching philosophy

2. Reduced number of offenses which carry mandated consequence 

3. Differentiated range of consequences for elementary and secondary students 

4. Emphasis on positive behavior approach

5. Highlighted commitment to collaboration with parents to prevent and address behavior concerns

6. Provide option to recommend for reassignment to an alternative program, when appropriate, instead of recommend for expulsion. This allows principal to accurately reflect their recommendation, and avoids creating anxiety for parent that student may be removed without services.

7. Authorized use of restorative conferences, alcohol and other drug intervention seminar, and restorative behavior intervention seminar and other interventions as possible alternatives to suspension

8. Provides paper review of first time possession of marijuana, shortening process and avoiding hearing for students for whom there is clear evidence of special circumstances. Provides for hearing to consider additional input to determine if there are special circumstances 

9. Reflects change in state law reducing number of weapons require mandatory recommendation for expulsion 

10.  Informs parents of limits of role of SRO in investigating disciplinary infractions

11. Includes section explaining procedural protections for students with disabilities. 

12. Includes notice that written statement is voluntary.

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.27P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                  Appointment of “individual Trustee” to the Board of Trustees of the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC)

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:            Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the “individual Trustee.”  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.

 

At a recent meeting of the ERFC Board of Trustees, Michael Burke, the Managing Director for the Wealth and Investment Management division of Barclays, was unanimously recommended for appointment as the “individual Trustee.”  If approved by the School Board, Mr. Burke will serve for his first year on the ERFC Board.  Mr. Burke meets all of the requirements of the position.    

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the appointment of Michael Burke to the ERFC Board of Trustees for a one year term beginning July 1, 2013.

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending technical amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of (1) clarifying that Member and Employer contributions will be paid for each month during which the Member received pay, in accordance with uniform and nondiscriminatory regulations adopted by the Trustees; and (2) identifying the default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits under ERFC if no Named Beneficiary survives the Member. Neither of the proposed changes is expected to affect the benefits payable under the Plan.

 

Member and Employer Contributions: The current plan documents provide that the Employer and each Member shall contribute a percentage of the Member’s Salary during the Member’s Eligible Employment. Both ERFC and ERFC 2001 should be amended to clarify that Member and Employer contributions will be paid for each month during which the Member received pay, in accordance with uniform and nondiscriminatory regulations adopted by the Trustees. This amendment is necessary to conform to the changes under the Virginia Retirement System modernization project.

 

Default Beneficiary: The current ERFC plan document does not provide for a default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits if no Named Beneficiary survives the Member. Consistent with the default beneficiary rules for Death Benefits, ERFC should be amended to provide for a default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment 3 to ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purposes of (1) clarifying that Member and Employer contributions will be paid for each month during which the Member received pay, in accordance with uniform and nondiscriminatory regulations adopted by the Trustees; and (2) identifying the default beneficiary for Service-Connected Death Benefits under ERFC if no Named Beneficiary survives the Member.

The legislation provides that localities and school systems may phase in employee contributions at a rate of 1% or more per year, and that the full phase in of a 5% contribution rate must be completed by July 1, 2016.
