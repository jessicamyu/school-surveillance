Subject:  ESOL Implementation Plan

 

Staff Contact:  Francisco Durán, chief academic and equity officer

 

Other Staff Present: Rich Pollio, director, ESOL Services, Instructional Service Department

 

Meeting Category:  May 1, 2018 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 1: Student Success

 

Problem Statement:

 

Many of our high schools have enrolled a significant number of English learners at WIDA ELP level 1 and students with interrupted formal education. These English learners need additional supports to address their academic and social emotional needs and keep them on a pathway to graduation.           

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

At its regular meeting on February 8, 2018, the School Board passed a motion to establish a $1.0 million set aside in the FY 2019 Advertised Budget to provide additional resources to high schools with the highest percentage of newcomer ESOL students to support wrap-around services.  The Superintendent was directed to provide a detailed implementation plan for discussion at a budget work session.

 

The proposed implementation plan provides additional wrap-around resources for select high schools with high percentages of recently arrived English learners and Students with Interrupted Formal Education (SIFE).

Summary/Background (Key Points): FY 2019 Advertised Budget

 

On February 8, 2018, the School Board adopted the FY 2019 Advertised Budget that includes a requested increase of $97.1 million, or 4.9% in the county transfer. Overall, the FY 2019 Advertised Budget shows a net increase of $118.9 million or 4.3%

 

For FY 2019, funds are budgeted that will allow FCPS to meet the basic needs of the school system and make continued investments in our teachers and in our classrooms in critical areas. Expenditure increases/(decreases) include:

  • new teacher salary scale enhancement $53.1 million;
  • step increases for eligible employees $43.9 million;
  • recurring cost of step on BA lane $1.3 million;
  • new salary scales plus 1% market adjustment for non-teacher positions $17.9 million;
  • enrollment and student demographics $15.8 million;
  • employee benefit increases for health coverage, and Fairfax County Employees’ Retirement System (FCERS) rates offset by VRS rate reductions and static ERFC rates $7.0 million;
  • Instructional program enhancements of $5.1 million detailed as follows;
    • adoption of math and grade 4 social studies instructional resources
    • a Masters Cohort/Tuition Sharing program for teachers obtaining a Master’s degree
    • a Tuition Reimbursement Program for all employees
    • support for the Family and Early Childhood Program (FECEP)
  • program operations and infrastructure $4.0 million; and
  • expenditure reductions totaling ($29.3) million.

 

County Executive’s Advertised Budget

The County Executive presented his FY 2019 Advertised Budget on February 20, 2018, which included a School Operating Fund transfer increase of $88.4 million or 4.5%. After accounting for additional State funding included in the Governor’s

 

Introduced Budget of $8.7 million, the FY 2019 FCPS Advertised Budget is fully funded. The County’s advertised budget recommends a 2.5 cent increase in the current real estate tax rate, which would increase the rate from $1.13 to $1.155 per $100 of assessed value.

 

On February 27, 2018, the School Board and County Board of Supervisors held a joint budget work session to discuss both advertised budgets and the impact of the state budget.

 

On March 6, 2018, the County voted to advertise the real estate tax rate for FY 2019 at $1.155 per $100 of assessed value. Advertising the tax rate allows the Board of Supervisors to adopt the advertised rate or one that is lower, but a higher tax rate cannot be imposed.

 

On April 24, 2018, the County Board of Supervisors conducted its mark-up of its FY 2019 Advertised Budget and reduced the tax rate by a half cent to $1.15 per $100 of assessed value. This results in an increase in the County Transfer to the FCPS School Operating fund of $84.74 or 4.3% over FY18.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The State budget process has not yet concluded. Current FCPS State revenue projections are very similar to those seen in the Governor’s Introduced Budget and almost all increases are due to enrollment, re-benchmarking and recalculation of the LCI. Thus, it is unlikely we would see any reductions to State revenue.
