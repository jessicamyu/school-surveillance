Fire Prevention Week - October 7-13, 2012

 

Fire Prevention Week 2012 focuses on the importance of having a fire escape plan.  The official theme, Have Two Ways Out, encourages citizens to have multiple escape routes from their home in the event of a fire and to practice those routes.

 

During Fire Prevention Week, October 7-13, fire safety educators around the country will work with citizens to teach them about the leading causes of home fires, how to use life-saving technologies, and the importance of creating a home escape plan.

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:             Student Rights and Responsibilities, Regulation 2601

 

Key Points:

 

At the June 7, 2012, regular School Board meeting, the Board unanimously approved the following motion:

 

That the School Board establish a special committee of the Board to perform a comprehensive review of the Fairfax County Public Schools’ Student Rights and Responsibilities (SR&R), and those manuals of other school districts, to recommend changes to the School Board by March 2013 that would simplify the language and substantially reduce the size of SR&R and ensure consistency with our community’s values, School Board policies, and Virginia laws.  This committee shall be composed of representatives of parents, students, school administrators, teachers, student specialists (e.g., counselors, social workers, psychologists, hearings officers or SROs), juvenile services specialists and/or such other community members as the School Board shall determine appropriate.

 

The committee will have 29 40 members, with the Deputy Superintendent, the Assistant Superintendent for Special Services, and one of the School Board member appointees all serving as co-chairs of the committee.  The determination of which School Board member appointee will serve as co-chair will be made at the first meeting of the committee, by and among the 12 School Board member appointees with the Chairman of the Committee elected by and among the School Board member appointees, and the Deputy Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent for Special Services serving as advisors and facilitators to the Committee.  The membership will be as follows:

 

12 24 School Board member appointees (one two representatives by each School Board member from his or her district; one two representatives, regardless of district, by each at-large School Board member)

 

1 representative from the Fairfax City School Board

1 Elementary School Principal

1 Middle School Principal

1 High School Principal

1 Middle School Teacher

1 High School Teacher

1 Teacher (nominated by the Fairfax Education Association)

1 Teacher (nominated by the Fairfax County Federation of Teachers)

1 Teacher (nominated by the Association of Fairfax Professional Educators)

1 School Psychologist

1 School Social Worker

1 Elementary Counselor

1 Middle School Counselor

1 High School Counselor

1 Hearings Officer

1 County Government Representative

1 Deputy Superintendent (Co-Chair  advisor/facilitator)

1 Assistant Superintendent for Special Services (Co-Chair advisor/facilitator)

2 High School students (from the Student Advisory Council)

 

The charge to the Community Committee on SR&R is detailed in the motion that was approved by the Board.  The Committee will present their recommendations to the School Board by March 2013.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board confirm membership of the Ad Hoc Community Committee on Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:                           

 

The School Board adopted Policy 4510, Communication with Employee Organizations, to establish lines of communication between the School Board and employee organizations.

 

The Governance Committee discussed possible revisions to the policy on October 12, 2011, and January 23, 2012, and the Committee agreed to schedule a joint meeting with the Public Engagement Committee to discuss the recommendations. The joint meeting was held on May 14, 2012, and there was consensus to recommend the following proposed revisions to the full Board for approval: attendance at meetings by the School Board chairman and an additional School Board member, to be determined on a rotating basis; meetings would include the three major teacher organizations (FEA, FCFT, AFPE) on a regular basis, and representatives of certified organizations twice a year; meeting minutes (developed by the Department of Human Resources) would be distributed to Board members on a regular basis.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 4510.4, Communication with Employee Organizations, as reviewed and discussed by the Board.

Meeting Category:  September 20, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 4

 

Subject:                 Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee         

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points: 

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents.  Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee. 

 

For 2012-2013 the Board members making appointments are Tamara Derenak-Kaufax, Sandy Evans, Elizabeth Schultz, Ryan McElveen, Ilryong Moon,and Ted Velkoff. Two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school.  The level will be determined by a random selection.  If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate. 

 

The student representative to the School Board appoints an individual to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.

 

Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009.

                                  

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint individuals to serve a one-year term on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                    Fairfax Leadership Academy Charter School Application  

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

  • Code of Virginia authorizes parents, teachers and community members to submit an application to establish a charter school to stimulate development of innovative programs and develop models for replication;
  • Fairfax Leadership Academy has made application, determined by Virginia Board of Education to meet its criteria;
  • FCPS School Board must conduct a public hearing, on the proposed charter school application and decide whether the application is complete and responsive; whether the proposal is sound in all aspects; and whether the proposed charter school is in the public interest and serves the welfare of the students within the Fairfax County Public Schools.
  • Per Policy/Regulation 1400, the Board may take any one or more of the following actions with respect to the application: accept without conditions, accept with conditions, reject, place on the waiting list, return with suggested modifications, defer action, or return with request for additional information

 

The Charter School Review Committee has completed its review of the Fairfax Leadership Academy Charter Application, and will present its findings and recommendation to the School Board.

Fairfax County Public Schools' staff, on behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, has filed Proffered Condition Amendment ("PCA") Application No. PCA 89-L-008 to amend the proffers previously approved for the Garfield Elementary School site to increase the maximum permitted gross floor area on the property to allow an expansion of the existing school building.  Garfield Elementary School was approved for renovation in the 2011 School Bond referendum.  The property was rezoned in 1989 subject to proffers that included a limitation on the amount of allowed future gross floor area (“GFA”).  Although the approved proffers allow for future expansion, the proposed renovation/expansion exceeds the permitted GFA by 4,064 square feet.  The existing school is a one and two story, U-shaped building with a gross floor area of 58,881 square feet.  The purpose of the expansion is to provide additional classroom space on the rear portion of the building along Old Keene Mill Road as well as to accommodate a renovation of the front portion of the building to relocate the main entrance, expand the administrative offices and provide for additional storage and mechanical equipment.  The cumulative gross floor area for the proposed expansion/renovation is approximately 19,000 square feet. The proffer statement (the "Proffers") associated with the PCA must be submitted to the County 30 days prior to the Board of Supervisors public hearing, which has been scheduled for November 20, 2012 on an expedited basis.  School Board counsel has been coordinating with Facilities and Transportation Services in the drafting of the attached draft Proffers.  The Proffers will likely require minor revisions as the PCA application is reviewed by County Staff and the Planning Commission in preparation for the Board of Supervisors public hearing.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Chief Operating Officer and his staff to take all actions necessary to pursue approval of Proffered Condition Amendment Application No. PCA 89-L-008 in conjunction with School Board counsel, and to negotiate, finalize and execute the final Proffers as approved by School Board counsel.

Subject:                    Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 10, 2012, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2012 session of the General Assembly.  Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

2012 VA Code Changes relevant to FCPS:

House Bill 1222, Code of Virginia §2.2-4301 and §2.2-4344, definition of employment services organizations.  Updates outdated language referring to sheltered workshops by replacing that term with "employment services organizations." The bill defines employment services organizations. The bill also replaces the term "handicapped" with "individuals with disabilities."

County’s Administrative Changes

Contractual Disputes:  Changes the identity of the party authorized to make a decision on a claim from “public body” to “county purchasing agent.”  This will make the term consistent with the remainder of the provision.

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, and 2011 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2012 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

  1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  1. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2012 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.
