Agenda Item: Professional Learning and Training, Operational Expectations Monitoring Report

Staff Contact: Terri L. Breeden, Assistant Superintendent, Professional Learning and Training (PLT)

Background: On April 26, 2007, the School Board approved the indicators for the Department of Professional Learning and Training. This is the first Monitoring Report for PLT.

Key Issues:
    • The Operational Expectations for PLT were approved on April 26, 2007. This report only contains information for May, June, July and August 2007.
    • There are three PLT Operational Expectations and they are appropriate at this time for the new Department of Professional Learning and Training. However, in the future, changing some of the indicators may be appropriate.
    • FCPS now has My PLT, a learning management system (LMS), which will increase the capacity for FCPS to align, plan, implement, manage and evaluate professional learning. Board Members will be participating in a brief demonstration of My PLT.
School Board Discussion/Decision: After adopting the Monitoring Report of Professional Learning and Training for 2007, the School Board may choose to look at the indicators and decide whether they are appropriate for future reports.

An essential question to ask for future monitoring is: After reading the entire PLT Monitoring Report and viewing the power point, do you feel the present Operational Expectations and Indicators give the School Board sufficient information on the status of PLT operations?

Staff Recommendation:
    • Adopt the 2007 PLT Monitoring Report
    • Agree to the rewording of PLT Operational Expectation # 3 in the 2008 report to read: Support staff members in their efforts to help students meet high academic standards by increasing individual and system effectiveness.
    • Allow staff to use the present Operational Expectations and Indicators for the 2008 PLT Monitoring Report.
    • Authorize staff to reword Operational Expectation #1 indicator for the 2009 report to include the concept of superior training mentioned in the opening sentence of Professional Learning and Training’s Operational Expectations document.
    • Consent to allow staff to study and adjust the PLT Operational Expectations and Indicators as the Department matures over the next two years.

Agenda Item: Department of Human Resources Operational Expectations--SEBAC Update


Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Human Resources


Background: This monitoring report is in response to a forum question by Mr. Stu Gibson regarding the School Employees’ Benefits Advisory Council (SEBAC) and concerns expressed regarding the role of and input from SEBAC.


Key Issues: The transition from the Employee Advisory Committee (EBAC) to the School Employees' Benefits Advisory Council on Benefits (SEBAC) has had limited success primarily due to three issues; the influence of a single employee association in the elections process, the dominance of the retiree participants, and benefit discussions held with each employee association creates redundant discussions and a lack of clarity regarding roles and responsibilities of the various groups.


    School Board Discussion/Decision: Review and discuss the monitoring report.

    Staff Recommendation: That the School Board accept the Human Resources Monitoring Report SEBAC Update.

    Agenda Item: Student Information System Replacement: Focus Group Session


    Staff Contact: Maribeth Luftglass, Assistant Superintendent, Information Technology


    Background: On May 14, 2007 the School Board approved the Student Information System (SIS) replacement project, and asked the FCPS team to “fast-track” the project. The FCPS team has been holding focus group sessions with stakeholder groups to gather needs. These needs will be addressed in the final requirements document, which forms the basis of the Request for Purchase (RFP). The vendors’ responses to the RFP will be evaluated according to standard FCPS procurement procedures, which will enable the selection and implementation of the best solution for FCPS. The FCPS team plans to submit a contract for School Board approval in May 2008.


    School Board Focus Group Session
      • The SIS team will present an overview of the project status, followed by the Focus Group session.
      • The SIS team will gather the School Board’s input on their expectations for a new Student Information System (SIS) to replace SASI.
      • The group discussion will be led by a facilitator who will review the areas of requirements (e.g., Student Management, Access, Academics).
      • Additional members of the SIS core project team will be in attendance to answer questions and record the School Board's comments.

    Key Issue: Board members should review the attachment “FCPS SIS High Level Requirements” prior to the focus group, as the facilitator will be asking for needs that have not yet been identified or priorities that should be considered.

    Agenda Item: Systemwide Emergency Operations Plan, Crisis Management Workbook, and Pandemic Flu Plan

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

    Background:
    Virginia law (22.1-279.8, D) requires local school boards to annually review school emergency plans. Additionally, the School Board requested a briefing on the FCPS Pandemic Influenza Response Plan.

    Key Issues:
    • School emergency plans consist of two (2) components; the Crisis Management Workbook and the schools’ specific plans, completed in an FCPS intranet application.
    • FCPS has a systemwide emergency operations plan that provides guidance for large scale operations.
    • FCPS’ Pandemic Influenza Response Plan has been developed in cooperation, and is consistent with, local, state, and federal planning.
    • All emergency operations plans are “living documents” and subject to continual improvement and updating.
    • FCPS utilizes appropriate technology to support emergency management operations.
    • The Division Superintendent must certify to the Virginia Center on School Safety that the School Board has reviewed the school emergency operations plans.

    School Board Discussion/Decision:


    Staff Recommendation:
    The School Board should direct the Superintendent to complete the review certification process and submit it to the Virginia Center on School Safety.

    The Board will now make a motion to go into closed meeting:

    1. to review, consider, and discuss certain reports and plans—including school crisis, emergency management, and medical response plans, the School Board’s review of which is required by Virginia law—and all of which reports and plans are related to the security of schools and other facilities of the Fairfax County Public Schools, or are related to the safety of persons (including students, staff, and volunteers) using these schools and facilities, or both, pursuant to Va. Code Ann. § 2.2-3711(A)(19).


    Virginia statutory law requires the development of a school crisis, emergency management, and medical emergency response plan for each school in the school division. As of July 1, 2007, each local school board, such as the Fairfax County School Board, is required to conduct an annual review of these plans and is authorized to withhold or limit the review of any security plans and specific vulnerability assessment components. Va. Code Ann. § 22.1 - 279.8.

    School system staff also studies and develops security and safety plans for other facilities and structures used by the Fairfax County Public Schools.

    The Virginia Freedom of Information Act was amended, effective July 1, 2007, to broaden the authority of public bodies in Virginia to discuss and consider in closed session reports or plans related to the security of any governmental facility, building, or structure, or related to the safety of persons using such facilities. Va. Code Ann. § 2.2 - 3711 (A)(19).

    The Fairfax County School Board believes that it is both prudent and advisable to utilize the legal protections afforded by these recent statutory amendments in order to safeguard certain information pertaining to the security of school system facilities as well as to provide for the safety of those who are using all of our facilities.
