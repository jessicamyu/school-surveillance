RESOLUTION
HONORING PHILLIP A. NIEDZIELSKI-EICHNER

WHEREAS, Phillip A. Niedzielski-Eichner was elected as the Providence District representative to the Fairfax County School Board in November 2003 and has served with distinction since January 1, 2004, serving as Board chairman in 2005, as chairman of several of the Board’s standing committees—Strategic Planning and Evaluation, Facilities and Operations, Resource Management, and Linkage—and as budget liaison for the past two years; and
WHEREAS, he realized his vision as he led the School Board’s effort to establish a set of 21st century student achievement goals and to increase accountability for the performance of the school division through the bold and innovative strategic governance initiative; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Niedzielski-Eichner has been a hard-nosed champion for minority student achievement and worked determinedly to close gaps and promote the highest levels of achievement for all students; and
WHEREAS, he steered the School Board’s budget during unprecedented fiscal challenges to achieve responsible annual budgets that have been grounded in comprehensive program and operations evaluation and redesign; and
WHEREAS, he believes strongly that each of us has a responsibility to protect the Earth and has worked diligently with the Board to promote environmental stewardship throughout all Fairfax County schools and facilities; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Niedzielski-Eichner brought to the Board a notable record of community involvement, which included service as an at-large member of the Fairfax Park Authority Board, vice president and chair of the budget committee for the Fairfax County Council of PTAs, president and vice president of the Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology PTSA, and president of Rocky Run Middle School PTA;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Phillip A. Niedzielski-Eichner for his valuable contributions and dedicated service to the Board, to Fairfax County Public Schools, and to the children and citizens of Fairfax County—he will be missed!

RESOLUTION
NAMING BRIAN TROMPETER
TO 2009 STATEWIDE MEDIA HONOR ROLL

Whereas, in each community, the public schools are shaped by the community to provide the most appropriate and effective education for the schoolchildren who attend those schools; and
Whereas, public schools are dependent on the community’s support in countless ways, including support in setting goals for the education of our children, in establishing successful volunteer and booster programs at the schools, in providing annual funding of operations, in holding periodic bond elections, and in electing qualified school board members; and

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board places the name of Brian Trompeter on the statewide Media Honor Roll, calling attention to the ongoing responsible and exemplary nature of reporting on Fairfax County Public Schools. We express our appreciation for your balanced and accurate approach to sharing with this community both the challenges facing our schools and the successes achieved by the teachers and students. Your work has helped this community focus on the goal of providing the best public schools possible for the children of Fairfax County.

Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, Chief Financial Officer

Other Staff Present: Kristen Michael, Director, Office of Budget Services

Meeting Category: July 23, 2009 – Regular Meeting No. 2
Subject: FY 2009 Final Budget Review

School Board Action Required: Action

Key Points:
      All of the FY 2009 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual independent audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the funds under the control of the School Board. All comparisons are against the FY 2009 estimate. The unaudited FY 2009 ending balance, after meeting the FY 2010 budgeted beginning balance and other commitments, is $25.2 million.

      FY 2009 actual revenue was $0.7 million higher than the estimate due to a net decrease in state revenue of $0.6 million resulting from a $4.1 million decrease in sales tax revenue offset by a $3.5 million increase in basic aid; a net reduction of $3.6 million in federal funding resulting from $6.7 million in unspent multi-year federal grant funds that will be carried forward offset by an increase of $3.1 million in Impact Aid and E-rate payments; and a net increase of $5.0 million in other revenue.

      In recognition of budget reductions included in the FY 2010 Approved Budget, schools and departments conserved resources where possible during FY 2009. In addition, compensation savings resulted from positions held vacant prior to being eliminated. Expenditure savings were primarily realized from $9.0 million in compensation; $6.7 million in unspent federal grant funds that will carry forward to FY 2010; $4.1 million in vehicle services and fuel; and $2.4 million in utilities.

      After the annual independent audit is completed, the available balance from FY 2009 will be available to be set aside for the FY 2011 budgeted beginning balance. This action will be addressed during the FY 2010 Midyear Budget Review.
Recommendation:
      That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

School Board Action Required: Action

Related To: N/A

Key Points:
      The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:
          Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2010 is $1.6 million. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $2.9 million.
          Cooperating with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
          Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
          Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.
Options:
      The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

Recommendation:
      That the School Board approve Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) continue in 2009-2010 to accept the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia.

Action

Contact: Daniel G. Storck, Chairman and Mount Vernon District

Meeting Category: July 23, 2009 – Regular Meeting No. 2

Subject: 2009-2010 Charges to School Board Advisory Committees

School Board Action Required: Action

Related To: School Board Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

Key Points:
      At its May 7 and 21, 2009, forum work sessions, the Board discussed the process for developing charges for all the School Board citizen advisory committees for the 2009-2010 school year. At its June 8, 2009, work session, the Board reviewed the draft 2009-2010 recommended charges.

      School Board member committee liaisons have discussed the proposed charges with each advisory committee (either the chairman, co-chairs, or committee members) and are recommended for approval as follows:
        Adult and Community Education Advisory Committee
        Define the effect of the ACE reorganization on student achievement, family literacy, and course offerings that are responsive to consumer demand and workforce needs.

        Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities
        Examine the school-wide positive behavior support programs and responsive classroom programs focusing on bullying prevention, strategies, training, and implementation, with specific emphasis on the measures for Student Achievement Goal 2, Essential Life Skills.

        Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee
        Provide an assessment and report that identifies the impacts to the performance and delivery of the Advanced Academic programs based on any changes made by the School Board in the coming months. If no changes are made, provide recommendations for improvement in these programs, keeping in mind the budgetary concerns now before FCPS.

        Career and Technical Education Advisory Committee
        Develop partnership models that enhance and expand Career and Technical Education programs for the mutual benefit of students and the greater business community.

        District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program
        Find ways to develop personal relationships between home and school to strengthen family involvement.

        FECEP/Head Start
        Pursue funding for the additional FECEP/Head Start and Early Head Start classrooms due to the growing waitlist. Offer parent education and/or skill development opportunities to families so they can learn a skill in this difficult job market as the economy has greatly impacted our families. Increase parent involvement by 10%, particularly male involvement (fathers or male figures).

        Human Relations Advisory Committee
        Work with the Office of Equity and Compliance to enhance the performance of school-based human relations committees, and continue to explore methods for obtaining feedback from the economically challenged and socially marginalized.

        Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee
        Monitor and assess the effectiveness of the FCPS current plan for closing the academic achievement gap and work in partnership with staff to ensure timely, consistent, and effective deployment and implementation of the plan across schools and clusters.

        School Health Advisory Committee
        Continue to look at the coordinated health services model and ways to implement specific modules, in addition to utilizing the new student survey data to examine issues related to depression or other risk behaviors.


RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve the charges for 2009-2010 for the following School Board Citizen Advisory Committees: Adult and Community Education, Students with Disabilities, Advanced Academic Programs, Career and Technical Education, District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program, FECEP/Head Start, Human Relations, Minority Student Achievement Oversight, and School Health, as detailed in the agenda item.

6.03 Revision to Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics - Recommendation to add the following sentence to the preamble statement for Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics: Academic progress in the core disciplines will be measured to ensure that all students, regardless of race, poverty, language or disability, will graduate with the knowledge and skills necessary for college and/or employment, effectively eliminating achievement gaps and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly (SB; action scheduled 9/10/09)

6. New Business - 8:50 p.m.

Information


Staff Contact: Kathy L. Smith, Chairman and Sully District

Meeting Category: July 23, 2009 – Regular Meeting No. 2

Subject: Revision of Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

School Board Action Required: Information

Related To: Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

Key Points:
        At its July 17 retreat, the School Board identified language that it asked the Governance Committee to review for inserting into the preamble for Goal 1. Academics.
        The identified language is derived from the Strategic Governance Manual’s “Vision” section and “Accountability” subsection and it specifically highlights the Board’s commitment to eliminating achievement gaps.

        The language reaffirms the Board’s commitment in the existing Goal 1. Academics preamble that “all students” means “all students”.
Options:
        1. Leave existing language and section as is.
        2. Accept recommended changes by the Governance Committee.
        3. Modify the existing language or recommended language changes.

Recommendation:
      That the following sentence be added to the preamble statement for Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics: “Academic progress in the core disciplines will be measured to ensure that all students, regardless of race, poverty, language or disability, will graduate with the knowledge and skills necessary for college and/or employment, effectively eliminating achievement gaps” and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.


Attachment: Revised Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics
