RELATIONSHIP WITH THE BOARD:  The Board values a harmonious and positive relationship with the Superintendent characterized by excellent communication, mutual respect, personal ethics, and professional integrity and guidance. To assure this result, the Superintendent will:

 

  1. Maintain an ethical and harmonious working relationship with the entire Board. The Superintendent will:
  1. treat all members impartially;
  2. refrain from criticism of individual members;
  3. respond to Board concerns in a timely and courteous manner;
  4. communicate immediately and directly with the Board or individual Board members when an honest difference of opinion exists; and
  5. promote a positive relationship in Board meetings.
  1. Keep the Board informed on issues, needs, policies, and operation of the school system:
  1. offer professional advice to the Board with appropriate recommendations based on thorough study and analysis supported by ample information, including assessment of alternatives, to enable Board members to make informed decisions;
  2. report on significant system-wide initiatives;
  3. inform the Board of anticipated significant media coverage;
  4. serve as the thought leader to the Board on current and emerging trends that impact public education
  5. keep Board members informed of personnel issues.
  1. Encourage communication and cooperation between Board members and staff members so as to be responsive to concerns of Board members.
  2. Interpret, implement, and support Board policy to staff members and the public, including the implementation of Board priorities (goals) in budget planning and in all operational areas defined and monitored by the Board.

Subject:                   Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC) Annual Report to the School Board

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:          

  1. As of 12/31/2011, the ERFC membership included 20,976 active members, among which 65% were covered under the ERFC 2001 benefit structure (The plan provided for members hired on or after 7/1/2001) and 35% were covered under the ERFC Legacy structure (The plan provided for members hired prior to 7/1/2001).
  1. During CY 2011, ERFC paid $149 million to its retirees and beneficiaries, 72% of whom are Virginia residents.
  1. The ERFC Fund in 2010 and 2011, had investment returns of 15.1% and -0.5% respectively for the calendar years, indicating the continued volatility in the investment markets.   ERFC performed well in first quarter 2012 with the Fund measuring a 3.6% increase since year-end.  However, investment returns have not been as favorable in April and May.
  1. ERFC’s asset market value was $1.74 billion at the end of the 2011 calendar year.
  1. ERFC’s ratio of funding value of assets to liabilities was 75.8% as of 12/31/2011.
  1. ERFC’s administrative expense ratio is lower than the average fund in the $1-$5 billion range as measured in the survey conducted by the investment consulting firm RV Kuhns and Associates.
  1. ERFC renegotiated its lease during FY 2012 achieving a total savings of approximately $900,000 over the 10-year renewal term. The document management system will be replaced in FY 2013 with 10 year savings of $100,000.

[Updated 6/9/12 - Resolution Attachment added]

Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 11, 2012 – Work Session No. 138

 

Subject:                 Transportation Bell Schedule Study History and Comparative Information Related to Later School Openings

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

At the request of the School Board, staff has performed preliminary research and is submitting to the board three key sets of information:

  • A history of FCPS examining school start times.
  • A survey of the 40 largest suburban school systems in the country with information on their school start times, numbers of students, and numbers of buses.
  • A more detailed survey of those school systems that have high schools starting after 8:00 a.m.

Key Points:

 

While the great majority of the largest suburban school systems have high schools starting on the first bell as is the case in Fairfax County, there are a number of systems that do have later high school start times.  What is not yet known is how those systems with later start times were able to make this happen.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board decide on next steps which might include more detailed research in those school systems with later start times.  Considering the need to ensure that this research is performed timely and objectively, the board might want to consider hiring a consulting firm.  A reasonable expectation of such a contract would be for the firm to perform the research, determine what actions were taken that make the change successful, and to prepare a specific plan for Fairfax County based upon their research.
