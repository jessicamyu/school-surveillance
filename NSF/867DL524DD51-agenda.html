SUBJECT: Proposed Virginia Special Education Regulations

TYPE: Discussion

RELATED TO: Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)

BACKGROUND:
      The Regulations Governing Special Education Programs for Children with Disabilities in Virginia, effective March 27, 2002, (the Virginia Regulations), must be revised to comply with the changes outlined in the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004 (IDEA ’04), and its federal implementing regulations. KEY POINTS:
      DSS staff agrees with the proposed Regulations for the most part and the Virginia Department of Education’s efforts not to exceed federal requirements. The ACSD objects to a variety of sections.

SUBJECT: Capacity Study

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      Due to the shifting demographics and educational needs within FCPS, FTS has experienced difficulty in accurately reflecting the building and programmatic capacity of its facilities. In July of 2007 FTS hired a nationally recognized educational planning firm, DeJong, to study a representative group of FCPS facilities. The DeJong report is attached to the presentation.

      As a result of the DeJong report, the Office of Design and Construction was instructed to undertake a system-wide capacity survey. A description of the FTS methodology is contained in this presentation. It is essential that a unified capacity calculation model is adopted to ensure that each school is evaluated on an equitable basis. The new methods being applied attempt to provide the same utilization factors to spaces contingent on their educational and capacity impact within our current educational model.

KEY POINTS:

      The Office of Design and Construction is currently conducting a system-wide building capacity survey. The survey will determine which rooms can be used for instructional purposes and what capacity impact they may have on a facility. We anticipate that the survey will conclude in early July 2008. There are multiple benefits to the capacity survey. The primary benefit will be the accurate calculation of each school’s capacity. The survey will provide the type of data necessary for FCPS to make informed decisions regarding the placement of new programs, and whether existing programs or facilities require modifications to better accommodate the students.
OPTIONS:

Approve/Disapprove Proposed Methodology

RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board approves FTS to proceed with the proposed course of action based on review of the presentation.

SUBJECT: Next Steps – Transportation Task Force

TYPE: Decision Required on April 14, 2008

BACKGROUND:

      The Transportation Task Force has completed is work and has submitted to the School Board both a Majority Report and a Minority Report. The majority report recommends to the School Board changes in certain service parameters (detailed on pages 3 and 4 of the report) and a proposed change to the current bell schedule so that adolescents would start school later in the morning.
      Conversely, the Minority Report expresses reservations about the composition of the task force, the process followed by the task force, and the potentially negative implications of the proposed changes to the bell schedule and service parameters.
      The attached document was prepared by the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (FTS) for the purpose of articulating the implications/considerations of each of the proposed changes to service parameters.

KEY POINTS:
      Many of the proposed changes to service parameters have the potential to increase transportation efficiencies. Certain other changes have the potential to decrease efficiencies and increase resource requirements. Changes to the current bell schedule will have profound and substantive implications to schools, students, faculty, administrators, parents, and other community members. Much of the implications might be somewhat transient in nature and will only require adaptation by those impacted. Other changes, however, are more long term in nature and it is important that we understand those differences.

      This is not the first time that this topic has been studied. There was a committee that considered this topic in the late 1980’s. A community task force similar to this one reviewed this issue in the late 1990’s and could not reach consensus. The topic was studied by a consultant, MPS, several years ago. All of these prior activities concluded that it would increase transportation costs to change the bell schedule

      Staff is of the opinion that there are omissions in the task force’s majority report. The report fails to address the transportation of students to Thomas Jefferson in the morning if we move elementary schools to the first bell. The report suggests that elementary and middle school students can be dropped off at school as much as 25 minutes before the first bell which ignores the teacher contractual implications of this action. The report gives inadequate consideration to transportation consideration of having elementary schools on both the first and last bells and the additional resources that will be required to ensure that elementary age students are not picked up or dropped off before or after civil twilight. Finally, the report implies that the service parameter changes should improve services and reduce costs which fails to acknowledge that many of these changes such as bus run time limitations will actually increase costs.
OPTIONS:

It appears that the School Board has three options:
    1. Thank the task force for its great work but shelve the issue for the time being due to the enormous budget difficulties facing the school system over the next several years.
    2. Share the task force’s reports with the community and attempt to determine the community’s interest in implementing the recommended changes.
    3. Direct staff to prepare a detailed and realistic bell schedule based upon the recommendations of the task force. Share this schedule, and its resource implications, with the community in order to determine its willingness to accept the specific changes to each school’s bell schedule.

RECOMMENDATION:
      Considering that this topic has been studied in the past and considering that the task force’s report lacks sufficient depth and breadth to gain a true understanding of the implications of changing the bell schedule, staff recommends the following:
        1. Direct FTS staff to begin immediately to prepare a revised bell schedule consistent with that recommended in the Majority Report. FTS is prepared to assign transportation staff to this project but it must be completed prior to peak workload times the month prior to the start of the next school year.

        2. In preparing the bell schedule, staff should not make other changes to service parameters identified in the Majority Report. (This recommendation is based on the intent to not have the other changes obscure the actual implications of just changing the bell schedule.

        3. The bell schedule should be prepared recognizing more realistic bus arrival time parameters for elementary and middle schools that are consistent with current teacher contracts.

        4. The bell schedule that is presented to the School Board and the Transportation Task Force should identify specific bell schedules for each school and center and should clearly identify the resource requirements necessary for implementation.

        5. The cost of preparing this report will be funded by the monies set aside for this purpose in this year’s budget.

Attachment: List of service parameters changes and important considerations of each change.
