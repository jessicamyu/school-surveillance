The Superintendent will provide a learning environment and program of instruction, drawing on innovative and best practices, aligned to achieve the School Board's goals and meet the community's expectations. The Superintendent will:

1. Provide equal educational opportunity in a safe, healthful, non-discriminatory, and secure environment for all students.

2. Improve instructional programs and services based on research and best practices, while encouraging innovation, in order to meet the needs of all students, addressing their diverse learning styles.

3. Measure effectively each student's progress toward achieving or exceeding performance standards.

4. Maintain a climate characterized by support and encouragement by all adults for high student achievement and student well-being.

5. Maintain a safe and healthful learning environment free from disruption and violence.

6. Appropriately involve stakeholders in curriculum development and the selection of instructional materials.

Meeting Category: May 10, 2010 - Work Session No.120

Subject: Southwestern Regional Planning Study

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Key Points:

The Southwestern Regional Planning Committee began meeting on October 29, 2009. The committee consisted of two (2) persons from each of the twenty-three (23) schools, appointed by PTA/PTO leadership. In conformance with the guidelines for the ad hoc community engagement process, the co-chairs of the Southwestern Regional Planning Committee presented the Phase I Executive Summary (including appendices from all sub-committees) to the School Board at the Jan. 11, 2010, work session. The Board accepted the Committee's report which recommended the addition of five other elementary schools into the scope of study (Navy, Lees Corner, Waples Mill, Terra Centre, and Silverbrook) and continued to push for additional information to validate the data provided by FCPS staff, particularly with regard to projections, school capacity and cost estimates. For Phase II the School Board directed the ad hoc group to continue to gather information related to the issues identified in phase I; develop a list of possible solutions including advantages and disadvantages of each solution; and to hold community engagement sessions to inform the community and seek their input. The School Board also extended the timeline for the final phase II report from February 2010 to May 2010.

In conformance with the guidelines for the ad hoc community engagement process, staff is providing a follow-up report at this time. This report is intended to re-examine and update the information provided in the July 2009 feasibility studies and to recommend options for the School Board to consider.

The staff update report now includes twenty-eight elementary schools with a total projected enrollment of about 21,753 students for SY 2015. Twelve of these schools are projected to be overcrowded by approximately 1,500 students. While there continues to be sufficient capacity at the remaining schools in the scope of the study, staff continues to believe that it would be impossible to address the overcrowding issue entirely by boundary changes. Additional capacity in the region must be constructed.

The updated staff report reaffirms the topographic and access challenges associated with renovating Clifton. One of the major, but not the only, challenge is the absence of public water and the water quality issues of the existing wells. The school does not comply with the current FCPS educational specifications. Staff estimates that a full scope renovation of Clifton ES will cost about $35,287 a student which is significantly higher than similar renovations of other FCPS schools which cost about $22,805 a student.

The staff report updates information associated with well and water quality. Clifton Elementary School currently has two operational wells, one of which is a poor quantity producer. Both wells have had sporadic quality issues since the mid-1990’s and are subject to continual maintenance and enhancements. Because of these issues, a third well was drilled in 2001 to serve as a backup contingency, in the event either of the existing wells were to fail. The third well has not been operational due the presence of higher than allowable limits of radium, gross alpha activity, iron and manganese. In the event that the renovation requires abandonment of one of the operational wells, FCPS would need to address the quality of the water from the third well through extensive treatments and systems. Absent a renovation, FCPS remains interested in addressing the issues with the third well, as the possibility of a failure to one (1) of the existing wells remains. The FCPS projected estimate for suggested improvements include $302,400 in initial costs and more than $90,000 of annual costs.

Options:

The staff and the ad hoc committee reports offer different options to address overcrowding in the area elementary schools and to make a decision regarding renovation of Clifton ES

Recommendation:

That the School Board accept the Southwestern Regional Planning Committee Phase II report

That the School Board direct staff to exercise one or more of the options listed in the reports to address the issues regarding the renovation of Clifton ES and relieving overcrowding at the area schools.
