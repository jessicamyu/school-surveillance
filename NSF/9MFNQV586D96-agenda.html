Subject:

Staffing Process and Class Size

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points: 

 

The process for allocating staffing to schools, the improvements implemented in the hiring process, and the standards established in the new class size regulation will be presented.

 

The intent of the new class size standards and regulation is to ensure that class sizes are equitable and reasonable. The FY 2016 Advertised Budget includes a placeholder of $3.1 million to address any classes that exceed the standard established in the new regulation. Additionally, during the FY 2015 Midyear Budget Review the School Board approved $0.8 million of one-time funding to bring the total to $3.9 million in FY 2016 to mitigate the class sizes above the established standards.

 

The regulation will also address removing positions from schools through the tenth instructional day when the enrollment does not generate the allocated positions. Exceptions will be made when needed to meet the class size standards. In the late fall, the School Board will receive class size information for the 2015-2016 school year which will be used to assess the impact of the additional funding allocated to address large classes at elementary schools and the impact of removing positions when schools’ enrollment is below the projection.

Meeting Category:  May 11, 2015 – Work Session No. 112

 

Subject:                  Update on Region 3 Community Engagement Meetings

 

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) held four meetings in Region 3 to share information on programs and enrollment projections and gather feedback from parents, employees, and community members. Meetings were held at Twain, Sandburg and Whitman Middle Schools and Hayfield Secondary School in March and April, 2015. Approximately 370 people attended.  Region 3 consists of schools in the Edison, Lee, Hayfield, Mount Vernon, and West Potomac Pyramids.

 

Participants learned about the process used by FCPS to determine student enrollment projections, school building renovation needs, programming at schools, and possible solutions to facility needs in the Mount Vernon and Lee Districts. A presentation about the CIP guiding principles currently under consideration by the School Board and an overview of Region 3 school programs and enrollment projections was provided.  Following the presentation, participants gathered in small groups to discuss and provide feedback on three areas:

  • CIP Guiding Principles
  • Program offerings in Region 3 and throughout the division
  • The strengths and challenges of the proposed solutions for Region 3 capacity issues, which were building a new school in the Pinewood Lake area or renovating and repurposing Virginia Hills

The overarching themes from the community feedback on the three areas will be shared.
