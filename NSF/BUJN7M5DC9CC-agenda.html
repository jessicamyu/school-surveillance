Summary/Background:

The budget development process will incorporate a review of the FY21 Budget by department through a phased approach. The budget review will include the department mission, functions of each office, and the allocation of resources to accomplish the department’s work and department-associated initiatives. Each department will provide programmatic information, connecting to strategic themes, as well as work in the schools that the department supports.

 

To kick-off the process, an overview of the FY21 Budget will include the budget development process, information by category of systemwide revenue and expenditures, information about the department review approach and the updated schedule of upcoming department review dates.

Subject: Membership Trends Report

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                 Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category: November 4, 2020 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Membership Trends Report is updated annually by the Office of Facilities Planning Services. The report informs the student membership projection set and school capacity evaluations, both of which form the basis for additional capital planning and new construction recommendations that are part of the Capital Improvement Program (CIP).

 

The COVID-19 pandemic has had an impact on FCPS membership. FCPS saw a decline in student membership, a lower birth to kindergarten ratio, and a negative net migration for School Year (SY) 2020-21. Whether these trends are temporary for SY 2020-21 will depend on many factors, including the future course of the pandemic and economic conditions.

 

Total membership for SY 2020-21 decreased by -8,859 students:

  • Elementary school membership decreased by -7,729 students;
  • Middle school membership decreased by -217 students;
  • High school membership decreased by -392 students;
  • Center and alternative program membership decreased by -356 students; and
  • Other category membership decreased by -165 students.

 

Three fundamental factors affect the counts within student membership and projections from year-to-year: the birth to kindergarten ratio, net migration, and transfers. Student withdrawals over the summer have been included in the report along with these three factors to aid in the evaluation of the change in total membership from SY 2019-20 to SY 2020-21:

  • The kindergarten membership decreased by -2,208 students, resulting in a decrease in the birth to kindergarten ratio from 87 percent in SY 2019-20 to 75 percent in SY 2020-21;
  • Net migration changed from 1,725 students in SY 2019-20 to -4,953 students in SY 2020-21. This indicates that 6,678 more students withdrew from the division than entered;
  • The total number of summer withdrawals was 12,317. The top three withdrawal reasons provided in SY 2019-20 were as follows: 3,392 (38 percent of withdrawals) to attend a non-VA US public school, 2,368 (27 percent of withdrawals) to attend a public school in VA, but not FCPS, and 1,188 (13 percent of withdrawals) to attend a school outside the US. The top three withdrawal reasons provided in SY 2020-21 are as follows: 3,239 (26 percent of withdrawals) to attend a non-Virginia (VA) United States (US) public school, 2,619 (21 percent of transfers) to attend a public school in VA, but not FCPS, and 1,899 (15 percent of withdrawals) were related to home schooling; and
  • The total number of transfers in for SY 2020-21 was 17,423 which is a decrease from SY 2019-20 of 19,385. The top three reasons provided for SY 2019-20 were as follows: 5,120 (26 percent of transfers) to access an Advanced Academic Program (AAP), 4,080 (21 percent of transfers) to enter special education programs, and 3,471 (18 percent of transfers) were related to reasons considered within the student transfer regulation (Regulation 2230). The top three reasons provided for SY 2020-21 are as follows: 4,992 (29 percent of transfers) to access an Advanced Academic Program (AAP), 3,674 (21 percent of transfers) to enter special education programs, and 3,088 (18 percent of transfers) were related to reasons considered within the student transfer regulation (Regulation 2230).

 

Summary data tables are included in the report to compare these factors in the current and prior years by school level (elementary, middle, and high), region, high school pyramid, and by school. It should be noted that data utilized each year is adjusted for accuracy at such times as actual numbers have been verified; this can result in a difference between counts from year-to-year for the same data point. Therefore, strict comparisons cannot be made between reports presented to the School Board in previous years.
