Technology (OE-12)

 

The Superintendent shall use technology to support teaching, learning, and the Board’s Operational Expectations and Student Achievement Goals.

 

The Superintendent shall:

1.      Provide a comprehensive and functional technology infrastructure.

2.      Provide technology capabilities that are useful for staff members and students.

3.      Use technology to support diverse learning techniques and styles.

4.      Provide a means for interactive communication between the school system and parents, students, and the community.

5.      Provide information electronically about school and division programs and academic progress.

6.      Provide a system to access relevant and current data by appropriate users.

7.      Provide support to enable all students to have access to online instructional resources beyond school hours.

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:                Legal Requirement

 

Background:

 

The proposed school calendar has been developed according to Regulation 1344, Standard School Year Calendar.

 

Key Points:

 

In preparation for developing the SY 2015-2016 Standard School Calendar, staff conducted the Master Schedule and Standard Calendar Survey in December 2014.  All principals and 158 teachers at each level (elementary, middle, and high school) were asked to complete the survey.

 

Staff also reviewed employee and constituent comments and/or met with several groups to collaborate and receive feedback such as:

  • Human Relations Advisory Council
  • Superintendent’s Teacher Advisory Council
  • High School Principals Association
  • Middle Schools Principals Association
  • Fairfax Association of Elementary School Principals – HR and Finance Committee

 

School year 2015-2016 will begin on Tuesday, September 8, 2015, the day after Labor Day.  FCPS does not currently qualify for the Virginia pre-Labor Day waiver.  To qualify, a school division must be closed an average of eight days during any five of the last ten years due to severe weather conditions, energy shortages, power failures, or other emergency situations.  FCPS must be closed a total of eleven days this school year in order to qualify for the waiver.

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

In the fall of 2012, the Office of Design & Construction began meeting with the School Board discussing various aspects of the Renovation Queue. The four School Board Work Sessions over a two year time span covered the following topics as they related to the queue:

  • Status of the queue
  • Validity of the criteria used
  • Capacity added into criteria and impact on ranking
  • Project timing
  • Unfunded or unnamed projects possible re-ranking and  potential timeline

The most recent criteria adopted by the School Board for the current queue include the following:

  • Fundamental Educational Requirements
  • Facility Condition Assessment
  • Supplemental Educational Requirements
  • Facility Age
  • Code Compliance
