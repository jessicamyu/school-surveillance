Summary/Background:

 

The Office of Facilities Planning Services, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services reviewed Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings, and compared it to other school districts’ boundary adjustment policies and practices.

Subject: BA Lanes

 

Staff Contact: Michael Draeger, director, Office of Business Services, Department of Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:      Marty K. Smith, chief operating officer

                                       Helen Nixon, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

Becky Ferrick, president, Association of Fairfax Professional Educators

Kevin Hickerson, president, Fairfax Education Association

Larry Little, teacher, Franconia Elementary School

Durann Thompson, teacher, Woodley Hills Elementary School

Tina Williams, president, Fairfax County Federation of Teachers

 

Meeting Category: October 15, 2018 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

 

In 2016, teacher salary scales were redesigned to provide market competitive career earnings for each pay lane. To provide greater increases at the beginning of the scale and better match the market, fewer steps were provided on the scale for the Bachelor’s lanes. Teachers on the top of the Bachelor’s lanes lost earning potential when the highest steps on the scale were eliminated.

 

The FY 2019 Approved Budget included an amendment directing the Superintendent to engage the teacher associations in developing potential future BA Lane salary scale options, including but not limited to options to grandfather BA lane teachers starting from Step 14, from Step 16, and from Step 18, for consideration, discussion, and adoption by the School Board.

 

A work group, including representatives from the three teacher associations along with staff from central office, reviewed options for mitigating the lost potential earnings for teachers on the top of the Bachelor’s lanes. The work group is recommending grandfathering teachers on step 11 and higher as of FY 2019 since those teachers will likely not reach the top salary of the FY 2017 scale, assuming the redesigned teacher scale grows by an annual average MSA of 1%. Teachers on Step 10 and below will likely receive salaries at the top of the redesigned scale that will exceed the top salaries of the FY 2017 scale. Additionally, the work group is recommending that the School Board continue to provide a bonus to employees at the top of the salary scale who will not receive a salary increase.

 

Recommendation: That the Board reach consensus on a long-term solution for the salary of teachers on the top of the BA Lanes.

Subject: Substitute Pay

 

Staff Contact: Sean McDonald, director, Office of Talent and Acquisition Management, Department of Human Resources 

 

Other Staff Present:      Marty K. Smith, chief operating officer

                                      Helen Nixon, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

Andrea Garris, coordinator, Office of Talent Acquisition and Management, Department of Human Resources

 

Meeting Category: October 15, 2018 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

 

The Board last reviewed substitute usage in its April 19, 2018, work session. In that session, the three year trend in the substitute pool composition and the trend in demand for substitutes was presented. The data showed that, while the substitute fill rate remained consistent at 93%, the substitute pool has been decreasing while the demand for substitutes is increasing. The Board requested that a future meeting be scheduled with a menu of options to consider for both increasing the substitute pool and decreasing the demand for substitutes.

 

Since that time, two working groups have been convened to discuss the data and develop potential solutions. Additionally, a survey was sent to teachers to get their feedback on solutions for growing the substitute pool, increasing the quality of the substitute pool, and for decreasing leave usage in order to shrink the demand for substitutes.

 

In this work session, a menu of options for increasing the substitute pool and strategies to reduce leave usage will be presented.

  

Recommendation: The Board come to consensus on solutions for substitute supply and demand.

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement.
