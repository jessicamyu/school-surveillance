Subject: 2020 Virginia General Assembly Post Session Report

Meeting Category: April 27, 2020 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 - Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The Virginia General Assembly Session adjourned its 2020 Regular Session on March 7, 2020 after having considered over 3,900 bills and resolutions during its 60-day Session. Of those 3,900, over 2,200 bills and resolutions passed both the House and Senate and another 385 were “Continued” to the 2021 Session. Upon a bill’s passage, the Governor has the opportunity to sign, amend, or veto passed legislation.

 

During Session, over 600 bills were routed to relevant FCPS departments across 13 different subject categories as being of some potential interest and/or having some possible impact on FCPS students, staff, or on the FCPS community in general. During and after the Session, weekly updates were provided to the Board and Leadership Team on the content and status of bills of particular interest.

 

The following passed bills are among those that were directly related in part or in whole to one or more FCSB legislative positions as reflected in the Board’s adopted 2020 State and Federal Legislative Program (as amended 2/20/2020):

  • Budget Bill (HB 30) - Board Priority Positions (specifically, fully funded re-benchmarking and additional resources for pre-K). Also U.7 Standards of Quality (enhanced staffing for English Learners)
  • “Students with Limited or Interrupted Formal Education” (SLIFE) (SB 933) - Board Priority Position/I.7 English Learners
  • Solar Energy Net Metering Cap (HB 572/HB 1184/HB 1526/HB 710/HB 851) - Board Priority Position, J.1 Environmental Sustainability/Renewable Energy
  • Collective Bargaining (HB 582/SB 939) - H.1 Employer Employee Relations
  • County Taxing Authority (HB 785/SB 588) - P.1 Revenue Alternatives
  • Culturally Relevant and Inclusive Education Practices Advisory Committee (HB 916/SB 853) - T.5 Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning
  • Dual Enrollment and Work Based Learning Options (HB 516/SB112) - T.7 Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning
  • Equal Rights Amendment (HJ 1/SJ 1) - K.1 Equal Rights
  • School Safety Audits (HB 501) - Z.9 Student Safety

The General Assembly will return on April 22 for a Reconvened Session to consider amendments proposed by the Governor to 102 bills in total, including 37 separate amendments to HB 29 (the “Caboose” budget, which finishes out the current biennium) and 144 amendments to HB 30 (the budget covering Fiscal Years 2021 and 2022).

 

Adopted legislation goes into effect July 1, 2020 unless otherwise noted in the bill itself.

 

Information on all education-related legislation from the 2020 General Assembly Session is posted on the FCPS Office of Government Relations website and will be updated as necessary.

Problem Statement: Regulation 2601, Student Rights and Responsibilities, needs to be updated to reflect legislative changes and to address concerns raised by principals and the community.

 

Outcome:  Provide input regarding recommended changes and presentation materials.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

  1. Regulation 2610 has been renamed 2602.

 

  1. Section IV, new distribution protocols are outlined.

 

  1. Chapter 1.A., clarifying attendance language was added.

 

  1. Chapter 1.C., Dress Code language updated to reflect state code.

 

  1. Chapter I.K.4., added phone number for Ombudsman.

 

  1. Chapter II, Introduction, language was added to include social emotional learning, multi-tiered system of support, restorative practices and the term unbiased.

 

  1. Chapter II, Administrative Response Guide, clarifying language was added and charts were moved below Leveled Responses to Student Behaviors Charts.

 

  1. Chapter II, Leveled Responses to Student Behaviors Charts, updated to align with new state codes and moved above Administrative Response Guide.

 

  1.       Chapter II, DV Code Key, removed due to changes in state codes.

 

  1.       Chapter II.A., added the phrase as outlined in this regulation in three locations to clarify when certain sanctions can be imposed.

 

  1. Chapter II.A., footnote 7, added the word consecutive.

 

  1. Chapter II.A., threat assessment language was changed from may to will.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.1.a.(5)., footnote 8 updated should to shall.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.2., Restorative Behavior Intervention Specialist updated to Restorative Justice Practitioner.

 

  • Chapter II.A.2.a.(8)., added footnote to address self-defense.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.2.b.(2)., footnote 10 updated should to shall.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.3.a.(1)., updated parent AOD information.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.3.b and e., deleted last sentence of second paragraph

 

  1. Chapter II.A.3.e.(1).(b), added in lieu of a five day out of school suspension.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.3.e.(1).(b).(ii), changed to shall result in an out of school suspension for three days.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.3.e.(2).(a)., added or imitation drugs.

 

  1. Chapter II.B.1., updated to reflect mandatory and discretionary reporting to police.

 

  1. Chapter II.C.3., added words consecutive and for a violation in the first sentence.

 

  1. Chapter II.D.5., clarified IEP team language.

 

  1. Glossary, updated the Alcohol and Other Drug Intervention Seminar to reflect current Alcohol and Other Drug Intervention practices.

 

  1. Glossary, removed Restorative Behavior Intervention (RBI) definition.

 

Recommendation:  That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.34P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.
