Meeting Category: December 15, 2016 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject: FY 2018-2022 Capital Improvement Program

 

School Board Action Required:   Information

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2018-2022 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five-year student enrollment projections and capacity calculations, as well as the Capital Construction Cash Flow which identifies plans for new construction, capacity enhancements, renovations, and site acquisition to accommodate future capacity needs.

 

Discussion:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools continues to face a challenging situation whereby student enrollment in certain locations is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities, provide capacity enhancement projects and it will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions and boundary adjustments to accommodate growing enrollment needs.

 

New School Construction: The CIP proposes three new elementary schools: one in the Richmond Highway Corridor (Route 1 area); one in the North West County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and the Dulles Airport access road; and one to relieve overcrowding in the Fairfax/Oakton area. It also proposes construction of a new high school in the Western end of the Silver Line metro vicinity to alleviate capacity concerns at multiple high schools.   

 

Capacity Enhancements: Funding for additions at South Lakes High School, West Potomac High School, Stuart High School, and Madison High School are proposed. Funds are also proposed for the relocation of four modular additions.

 

Renovations: Funding for renovations at five named high schools and one unnamed high school are proposed in the CIP.  Additionally, funds for renovations at 21 named elementary schools and 11 unnamed elementary schools are included. Lastly, funds for the renovation of four named middle schools and one unnamed middle school are proposed. Many of these renovation projects will include capacity enhancements.

 

Site Acquisition: Funds have been proposed for a site acquisition in the future.

 

Like the past two years, the FY 2018–22 CIP also recommends possible solutions for future consideration to resolve capacity deficits. These options include interior modifications, temporary classrooms, program location suggestions, and boundary adjustments. Any boundary adjustment or program change would be discussed and decided through a transparent process that engages the community in accordance with School Board Policy and Regulations.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2018-2022 Capital Improvement Program.

Subject: Strategic Plan Goal 1, Strategies 1 & 2—Student Success

 

School Board Action Required: Presentation        

 

Related To: Strategic Plan—Goal 1

 

Key Points:    

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS), which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. All of the goals contain overarching strategies, desired outcomes, actions, and monitoring metrics. The purpose of this report is to provide an update on the current work that supports Goal 1, Student Success.

 

The following are two of the four overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 1 Student Success. The remaining two strategies will be presented on January 26, 2017.

 

Overarching Strategy 1:

Enhance instructional practices to ensure that all students receive an education in a dynamic environment designed to foster life-long learning and support them in achieving their full potential.

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • The curriculum will reflect Portrait of a Graduate outcomes in all content areas, where students are appropriately challenged
  • Achievement gaps will be eliminated
  • Teachers, students and parents will have access to contemporary and effective technology resources
  • Centralized support will be available for schools and school staff based on student achievement needs.

 

Overarching Strategy 2:

All students will achieve their full potential through the use of assessment and data systems for decision making that support student attainment of the outcomes defined by the Portrait of a Graduate

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • A fully-developed balanced assessment system will reflect Portrait of a Graduate outcomes in all content areas
  • Data tools will be available to schools, school teams, and individual teachers to diagnose and monitor individual student progress
  • Grading will be an accurate reflection of learning 

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan - Goal 1 Student Success Strategies 1 and 2 as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:  Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services  

                                                                                        

Other Staff Present:  Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:  December 15, 2016 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  FY 2017 Midyear Budget Review      

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, funds available are decreasing by $5.1 million. The FY 2017 beginning balance is decreasing by $1.3 million due to final year-end adjustments made after the FY 2016 Final Budget Review. The adjustments include an increase of $0.7 million in sales tax revenue based on actual receipts and expenditure increases totaling $2.1 million. In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2017 revenue adjustments include a decrease in state aid of $4.4 million due to the reduction in the compensation supplement for pay increases, including the compensation supplement component for the Academic Year Governor’s School as a result of the state’s significant revenue shortfall for FY 2016; an increase of $0.5 million for City of Fairfax tuition based on actual expenditures for FY 2016; and a net increase of $0.1 million in federal grant revenue. 

 

Recommended FY 2017 expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $9.9 million resulting primarily from a reduction of $6.8 million in compensation accounts due to higher turnover experienced beyond the initial projection; a decrease of $2.5 million resulting from savings in health plan rates due to the new pharmacy benefit contract; a decrease of $1.3 million in outstanding encumbered obligations due to expenditures reported after the FY 2016 Final Budget Review, offset by an increase of $0.5 million for early hire teacher incentives and $0.1 million in federal grant expenditures.

 

Expenditure adjustments, combined with revenue adjustments, result in available funds totaling $4.8 million recommended to be set aside to increase the FY 2018 beginning balance from $22.2 million to $27.0 million. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds and an enrollment update for FY 2017 are detailed in the attachment. Also attached is an update on the status of grants in FY 2016 and FY 2017.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2017 Midyear Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.
