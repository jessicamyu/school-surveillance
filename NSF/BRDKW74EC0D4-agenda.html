2.05 Month of Military Child Recognition

Apr 8, 2021 - Hybrid Regular Meeting

2. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.

April is the Month of the Military Child. This year’s theme is “Military children and youth: A resilient force in a changing world”. It is a time to recognize our military and veteran-connected students for their service and sacrifice. Military kids sacrifice and serve our country in their own way. Here in Fairfax County, we are extremely fortunate to have over 11,000 students who are part of a military family as well as part of our Fairfax County Public Schools family.

 

Our military connected students bring a strong sense of community and a wealth of life experiences to our schools. They are resourceful and adaptable while living with numerous challenges presented by frequent moves, parental deployments, and a host of life transitions that often include reintegration and dealing with profoundly changed parents. The following are just a few of the challenges military children face: moving and starting over on an average of every 1-3 years, attending 6-9 different schools from kindergarten through 12th grade, and multiple deployments of a parent. Throughout all of this, military children are resilient and patriotic. They know first-hand what it feels like to be the new kid, so they are usually the first one to welcome a new student to school.

 

Celebrating the Month of the Military Child provides a powerful opportunity to raise awareness and build support to help these children meet the unique challenges they face! It is an opportunity to recognize and thank military children and youth for their heroism, character, courage, sacrifices and continued resilience.

 

We invite you to join us on April 21st for “Purple Up Day” as we encourage everyone to wear purple to celebrate our military connected students.

National Library Week and School Library Month - April 2021

 

National Library Week is April 4-10 and April is School Library Month, the American Association of School Librarians' celebration of school librarians and their programs. Strong school library programs play an essential role in a student's educational career, and FCPS school librarians have made tremendous contributions to students and staff this past year during the pandemic.

 

FCPS librarians sprang into action ensuring students had access to books when buildings closed. Whether through curbside checkout, mobile checkout locations in neighborhoods, buses turned into book mobiles, or by personally delivering books directly to students at home, librarians kept families reading. Many librarians partnered with transportation and food services employees to manage the process and ensure everyone was safe. This year, 4.2 million FCPS library books have been checked out in addition to more than 530,000 digital books used by students. Library online resources have been essential to student learning in a virtual environment. Librarians shared these centrally purchased research databases and ebook platforms with teachers and families to support learning. A record 10.5 million searches have taken place on FCPS library online resources this year.

 

The greatest assets for school library programs are the librarians themselves. They provide instruction on information literacy skills and digital citizenship, co-plan and co-teach with teachers through an inquiry-based approach to learning, develop maker activities, host book clubs, and more. Most importantly, they provide an environment for students to be themselves, pursue their interests, and know that they belong.

 

The School Board welcomes this opportunity to commend Fairfax County Public Schools’ librarians and to express its gratitude for their dedication to our students, their commitment to equity, and the wide-ranging services they provide to the school system.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan/Part B Flow-Through Application and Report for the 2021-2022 school year, as required by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent’s Memo #314-20,

2021-2022 Special Education Annual Plan/Part B Flow-Through Application, dated December 4, 2020. This report will be used by VDOE in preparing the state plan for special education. As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 14, 2021.

 

The 2021-2022 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $37,414,692. This amount projects a 2021-2022 award amount equal to the final IDEA Part B, Section 611 Flow-Through allocation that FCPS received for Federal Fiscal Year (FFY) 2021, as communicated in the VDOE Superintendent’s Memo #182-20 dated July 17, 2020. Estimated Section 611 expenditures include costs associated with the voluntary 15 percent set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS); a mandatory proportionate set-aside, which ensures that FCPS spends a proportionate share of funds on special education services for parentally-placed private school children with disabilities; materials/supplies; and state restricted indirect costs of 1.1 percent. The IDEA Section 611 Part B, award will fund a total of 295.60 FTE positions.

 

The 2021-2022 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $836,008. This amount projects a 2021-2022 award amount equal to the final IDEA Part B, Section 619 Flow-Through allocation that FCPS received for Federal Fiscal Year (FFY) 2021, as communicated in the VDOE Superintendent’s Memo #182-20 dated July 17, 2020. These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students. Estimated expenditures include costs associated with a mandatory proportionate set-aside and state restricted indirect costs of 1.1 percent. The IDEA Section 619 (Preschool), award will fund 6.0 FTE positions.

 

The 2021-2022 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2020-2021 application. Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, and materials/supplies. The plan projects a state restricted indirect cost rate of 1.1 percent, which is unchanged from FY 2021.

 

The 2021-2022 Annual Plan was presented to the Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) at the March 10, 2021 meeting.

 

Recommendation: It is recommended that the School Board approve the 2021-2022 Local Special Education Annual Plan/Part B Flow-Through Application and Report, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool grant funding application, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: School Board Notice 3310 - Recommendation to rescind Policy 3310 and Regulation 3310, Focus School Initiative and Implementation (K-12)

 

School Board Action Required: New Business

 

Key Points:

Policy 3310 and Regulation 3310—Focus School Initiative and Implementation (K-12) are rescinded because the focus school initiative, which originally included an application process and funding, is no longer supported at the division level. There have been no new schools apply since 2009. At the time of this notice, only two schools of the fifteen schools selected, over the period of 1998-2003, continue to utilize their focus as part of their school identity. Those schools could choose to continue that practice without a need for policy.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board rescind Policy 3310 and Regulation 3310-- Focus School Initiative and Implementation (K-12)

Summary/Background:

 

The Office of Facilities Management (“OFM”) seeks to establish a job order contract, as authorized by the Virginia Public Procurement Act (the “Act”), to provide for hazardous materials removal services for substances such as asbestos and lead-based paint in FCPS buildings as requirements become known. Generally, the task orders are valued at $5,000-$80,000 depending upon the scope of the project.

 

Because the aggregate dollar amount paid for services performed in a given year under such a contract is expected to exceed $250,000, Policy 5011.4 requires the School Board to award the contract.

 

Funding for the hazardous materials removal contract is provided from the Major Maintenance Fund, as well as school and department Operating Funds.

 

An invitation to bid has been issued and advertised and bids are to be received by OFM on April 8, 2021. An award recommendation will be furnished prior to the May 6, 2021, School Board meeting. The invitation to bid authorizes awards to a primary and secondary contractor in order to promptly fulfill FCPS requirements as they arise from time to time.  OFM intends to recommend award on a primary and secondary basis in the event the bids received are advantageous to the interests of the school division.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract(s) for the hazardous materials removal contract to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder(s) and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer such contract(s) on behalf of the School Board.

Key Points:

The SDI designation is an initiative from the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) intended to encourage divisions to develop innovative practices that challenge current methods and strategies to support the academic success of all students and educators. Each year, VDOE invites divisions to apply for a SDI designation by submitting their innovation plan. Division applications are reviewed and approved by the Virginia Board of Education and are valid for three years.

To apply for SDI designation in 2021, FCPS’ innovation plan seeks to extend and expand the use of authentic assessments, wherein students are engaged in application of knowledge and skills, complex thinking, and problem-solving to create a unique product or response.

SDI designation would give added weight to scaling up the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate Presentations of Learning (POG-POL) Goal 1 metric divisionwide. SDI designation would additionally provide FCPS the opportunity to extend temporary waivers that VDOE offered related to performance assessments in 2020-21, leveraging the time and effort division staff (school and central) put into performance assessment practices this year.

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve FCPS’ 2021 application for a School Division of Innovation (SDI) designation.
