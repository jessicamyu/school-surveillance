Meeting Category:    July 19, 2010 – Work Session No.6


Subject:                    Fairfax County Unified System (FOCUS) Project           

                                 iSIS (integrated Student Information System) Project Update

                                 Elementary School Progress Report Update

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  N/A

 

Key Points:


Fairfax County Public Schools and  Fairfax County government are collaborating on a multi-year initiative that will modernize the portfolio of enterprise systems that support finance (FAMIS), budget (BPREP), procurement (CASPS), and human resources and payroll (Lawson: FCPS and PRISM: government).  A contract was awarded June 23, 2010, to SAP Consulting. The fiscal impact to the county for the ERP System Implementation is approximately $40.6 million.  FCPS will contribute significant staff resources over the next three years during system implementation.  Post implementation support and maintenance costs for FCPS have yet to be determined. 


The activities for the iSIS (integrated Student Information System) project to replace the outdated SASI system are increasing as FCPS prepares for a fall pilot in the elementary schools.  The FOCUS and iSIS projects’ major milestones have significant overlap over the next few years. The implementation of these major mission-critical systems in all schools will impact every employee, student, and parent in FCPS, and the impact on scarce staff resources will remain high.


The elementary school progress report is being revised and will be standards-based.  A version of the draft was field-tested in 2009-2010.  Feedback from various constituents has been collected and will be used to revise versions of the new progress report.  Schools will begin using the new progress report online beginning in September 2011.

4.03 Review of New Operational Expectations Monitoring Report Format
Meeting
Jul 19, 2010 - Work Sessions
Category
4. Governance (WS No. 8) - 4 p.m.
Type
Discussion

The Governance Committee and Leadership Team have developed a set of criteria for all future Operational Expectations. Some of these are now embedded in the new format but others are specific to the data used to measure Operational Expectations.  These include:


  • Format
    • Effectiveness Measures measuring how effective we are in delivering results)
    • Efficiency Measures (measuring how efficient we are in achieving these results)
    • Customer Satisfaction Measures
    • Completed Inititiaves (a listing of the inititives completed in the previous year and the value or importance of these inititives)
    • Issues and Trends (identifying policy implications)
    • Budget Implications for Future Inititives

  • Data Criteria
    • All data must be put in context -- either external benchmarks, time lines, trend data etc.
    • All data must be quantifiable except when no baseline data is available
    • All information must be clearly understandable to the general public
    • Data must answer the "so what" question. 
    • Data must help the Board in clearly understanding how staff are accomplishing their objectives and help drive Board decisions
