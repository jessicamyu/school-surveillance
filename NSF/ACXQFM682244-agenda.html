                                 Carol Horn, coordinator, Advanced Academic Programs

 

Meeting Category:  June 1, 2017 – Work Session

 

Subject: Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee (AAPAC) Annual Report to the School Board 2016-2017

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                          

 

Related To:   Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points:

AAPAC studied and reviewed current elementary school-based services in order to make recommendations for strengthening the continuum of advanced academic services in every elementary school.

 

  • To ensure equity of access and consistency of implementation of the AAP continuum of services, to include early identification, move towards a full time Advanced Academic Resource Teacher (AART) in every school.  The AART’s role should mirror that of other instructional coaches within the school to support teacher and student learning.

 

  • Increase availability of professional development related to AAP curriculum and topics about how to identify and provide services for advanced learners in elementary school.

 

  • Strengthen equity of access to advanced academic curriculum and strategies for all students, regardless of AAP level of service.

 

  • Conduct a longitudinal study to determine the impact of early AAP identification in elementary school on secondary and post/secondary achievement.

  •  

Recommendations: 

 

That the School Board review the recommendations and support efforts to strengthen the continuum of advanced academic services in every elementary school.

                                  Cheri Belkowitz, Co-Chair, Advisory Council for Students with Disabilities

Subject:  Advisory Committee Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:  The report is to review ACSD recommendations related to the following School Board Annual charge on inclusive practices for students with disabilities.

 

ACSD School Board Charge:

 

The ACSD should examine practices throughout the school division related to providing services at the building level for students with disabilities in grades preK-12 for the purposes of maximizing inclusion in educating students with disabilities in their base school.

  • Examine and define successful nationwide evidence-based practices in delivering services at the building level (grades preK-12) and identify FCPS schools, which model best practices in order to scale and implement practices to schools at all levels (preschool, elementary, secondary).
  • Examine how students with disabilities are accessing the general education curriculum through the continuum of services offered by FCPS.

Due to the importance of the issue of inclusion in FCPS, the ACSD intends to continue its investigation of, and recommendations for, inclusive practices throughout the school system.

 

Summary of ACSD Recommendations

 

​A.  Gather data regarding current FCPS inclusion practices, assess efficacy of inclusion initiatives and assess student social, academic, and behavioral outcomes across the school environment. Data   gathering needs to include school-by-school data on students referred to more restrictive settings. Depending on what results show, principal and administrative training on inclusion practices may need to be done.

  1. Develop a district-wide definition of inclusion, aligned with expert guidance from the National Association for the Education of Young Children (NAEYC) and the Council for Exceptional Children.
  2. Develop and deploy an “Inclusion Assessment Tool” to gather data regarding current FCPS inclusion practices and assess student social, academic, and behavioral outcomes to determine current levels of meaningful “Total School” inclusion across the entire school environment.
  3. Develop online inclusion resources, including an FCPS Staff Inclusion Resource and training web page for all staff, and provide additional information and resources on inclusion to parents and the community on the FCPS public webpage.
  4. Continue to expand the current Inclusion Working Group to include Instructional Services Department staff to promote collaboration between general education and special education staff and to develop specific timeframes within which to implement initiatives. This group should help schools implement practices designed to support social and academic inclusion across the entire school environment.

 

B.  Develop mandatory training sessions on inclusion that focus on improving social, as well as academic, inclusion. Special education, general education, nonacademic (elective), extra curricula teachers and local school administrators should attend these trainings. Trainings for administrators should include trainings on master scheduling and other barriers to inclusion and aimed at creating a culture of inclusion as well as sharing best practices for inclusion.

 

  1. Assess efficacy of inclusion initiatives and provide support to ensure school teams and personnel implement effective inclusive practices across the entire school environment.
  2. Provide tier three executive functioning, social, and academic intervention within the special education and general education core academic classes at the secondary level to ensure students with disabilities can have their needs met without compromising their ability to choose general education electives.
  3. Revise IEP process and forms to include language and processes creating a paradigm shift from a focus on time spent in special education to a focus on meaningful time spent in general education.
