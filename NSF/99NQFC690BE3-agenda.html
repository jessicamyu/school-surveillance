Fire Prevention Week - October 6 – 12, 2013

 

Fire Prevention Week 2013 focuses on fire prevention in the home.  The official theme, Prevent Kitchen Fires, encourages citizens be aware of the potential dangers in home kitchens and how to prevent a cooking fire.  On average, there are roughly 3,000 reported kitchen fires per day in the United States.

 

During Fire Prevention Week, October 6-12, fire safety educators around the country will work with citizens to teach them about fire safety tips, encourage them to keep a family safety checklist, and provide activities featuring Sparky the Fire Dog.

RESOLUTION

HONORING

OAKTON HIGH SCHOOL VARSITY ATHLETES AND COACHES

 

 

     WHEREAS, the Oakton High School varsity athletes and coaches have set high expectations for themselves and demonstrated commitment and passion as competitors to set a new school record by earning 11 district titles in baseball, girls’ cross country, boys’ soccer, softball, boys’ and girls’ swim and dive, boys’ and girls’ tennis, girls’ indoor track and field, girls’ outdoor track and field, and girls’ volleyball; and

 

     WHEREAS, the varsity football, boys’ soccer, girls’ swim and dive, and girls’ tennis teams won regional championship titles during the post-season; and

 

     WHEREAS, the teams have excelled at the state level with the girls’ swim and dive team winning the state championship, the football team earning a semi-finals ranking, baseball, boys’ soccer, and girls’ tennis teams earning a quarter-finalist ranking, girls’ indoor track and field and boys’ swim and dive teams placing third, and boys’ track and field placing eighth; and

 

     WHEREAS, the varsity teams have performed the best in state-level competitions across all sports, accruing points in the Wells Fargo Cup competition and receiving the Claudia Dodson Virginia High School League Sportsmanship, Ethics, and Integrity Award in recognition of Oakton High School’s policies to make sportsmanship a priority and an expectation within the school and school community; and

 

     WHEREAS, for the first time in the school’s history, Oakton High School’s athletes won the 2012-2013 Wells Fargo Cup for Athletics, the Virginia High School League’s highest honor recognizing the league’s state champions of state champions, and eclipsed their competition in the Group AAA division by scoring 405 points, 44 percent more than the second place team;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board honors the Oakton High School varsity teams and coaches for their outstanding success and record- setting season.

Key Points: 

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents.  Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.  For 2013-2014 the Board members tasked with making appointments are Megan McLaughlin (Braddock), Jane Strauss (Dranesville), Pat Hynes (Hunter Mill), Dan Storck (Mount Vernon), Patty Reed (Providence), and Kathy Smith (Sully). Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009.

4.03 Resolution to Opt-Out of the VRS VLDP - Recommendation to adopt a resolution to opt-out of the Virginia Local Disability Program administered by the Virginia Retirement System (HR; action scheduled 10/10/13)
Meeting
Sep 26, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 4
Category
4. New Business
Type
Information

[Updated 9/27/13 - additional information attached]

 

Staff Contact:            Phyllis Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:  De Hawley Brown, Melissa Blanda

 

Meeting Category:     September 26, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 4

 

Subject:                    Resolution to Opt-Out of the VRS VLDP

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:               State Resolution Requirement 

 

Key Points: 

 

1. The Virginia Retirement System (VRS) has mandated that the newly designed VRS Hybrid Plan’s participating employers offer a disability program to all new hires post-CY2013 either through a VRS administered disability program, or through the local employer.  The employer disability program has to be at least comparable to the VRS’ Virginia Local Disability Program (VLDP).  The school division can either opt-into the VLDP, or opt-out of the disability program if they offer a program that is comparable or greater.
 

2. FCPS’ current Integrated Disability Management program meets the VRS comparability requirement.  FCPS has a third-party analysis comparing the two programs to ensure that FCPS’ current disability programs are comparable or more than comparable.
 

3. Through a recent informal survey, all local school jurisdictions responding to inquiry have indicated they do not intend to participate in the VLDP, with the exception of Loudoun County.
 

4. To ensure future comparability and appropriate plan/cost management, FCPS has developed disability program design considerations and will be using the upcoming procurement process to address options for continuing quality benefits for our employees and maintaining comparability to the VRS programming, while taking cost considerations as a priority.

 

FCPS currently provides employees with a comprehensive disability program that is more than comparable to the VLDP while fitting within the FCPS culture.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt a resolution to opt-out of the Virginia Local Disability Program administered by the Virginia Retirement System.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
