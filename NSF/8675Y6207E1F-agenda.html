Recognition of Facilities and Transportation Services staff members
      In addition to the hundreds of school based custodians and other staff who worked many long hours to clear school sidewalks, walking paths, and doorways, there were many members of the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (FTS) who worked both during and after the snowstorm to prepare schools to re-open. Of particular note in FTS was the heroic work done by the 52 individuals in the grounds section who worked over 7,500 hours continuously during a more than two-week period to clear school parking lots and entrance roads. Without the great work done by the grounds staff, and the great leadership of that group by Dale Taylor, we would not have been able to open schools successfully yesterday. Another effort worthy of note was that made by staff from design and construction and facilities management to go onto the roofs of every school and clear the roof drains to ensure that we would not suffer roof collapses from excess weight. These 55 individuals cleared literally hundreds of roof drains on snow covered and icy roofs this past Sunday and Monday. They also inspected and removed snow from trailers as warranted. Throughout the storm, mechanical and electrical staff in facilities management responded to numerous school power outage reports and loss of heat problems. Staff from the transportation office drove most county streets over the weekend to determine where buses could and could not safely navigate and where bus stops were still viable and safe for students, and worked in coordination with grounds staff to ensure that all school buses were cleared and able to move when school re-opened. Finally, behind the scenes staff in administrative services ensured that needed materials were available, that records were kept of expenses, and that communication continued with county recreation staff on community use issues.

      The school system needs to recognize the unbelievable volunteerism by parents and community members in helping schools clear sidewalks, walking paths, and bus stops both on school property and off. The support was fantastic and incredibly helpful. The school system has gotten great support from the Board of Supervisors, the Police Department, and even the Sheriff’s Department to help enhance student safety.

6.01 Snow Makeup Days - Recommendation that the School Board extend the school day by 30 minutes from March 8, 2010 - June 21, 2010, and use the scheduled teacher workday on April 12, 2010, as a student day in order to make up missed days due to inclement weather for the 2009-2010 school year; and increase the school day in modified calendar schools in the same manner with the exception of using April 16, 2010, as the full makeup day instead of April 12, 2010. (HR; action scheduled 3/4/10)
Meeting
Feb 17, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 14
Category
6. New Business - 8:30 p.m.
Type
Information

Staff Contact: Kevin L. North, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

Meeting Category: February 17, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 14
Subject: Snow Makeup Days

School Board Action Required: Information Only

Related To: Legal Requirement

Key Points:

Virginia Code 22.1-98 states “The length of every school’s term in every school division shall be at least 180 teaching days or 990 teaching hours in any school year…” As of February 16, 2010, the unprecedented snow storms resulted in the loss of 10 days of school. Our current calendar has three built-in snow days and the State waives three additional days, resulting in the need to make up the remaining four days. In addition to our standard option of adding makeup days to the end of the school year, two other options are presented that add time to the each school day, which is more instructionally beneficial than adding multiple days to the end of the year.

Option 1:
      For standard calendar schools, April 12, which was previously scheduled as a teacher workday, will become a student day, and 30 minutes will be added to each school day for the period of March 8, 2010 – June 21, 2010. This will result in enough total instructional time to meet the 990 hour requirement and will generate an additional 7 hours (or 1 day and 1.5 hours) as a reserve for any remaining closures or delayed openings/early dismissals.
      For modified calendar schools, April 16, which was previously an intersession day, will become a student day, and 30 minutes will be added to each school day for the period of March 8, 2010 – June 21, 2010. This will result in enough total instructional time to meet the 990 hour requirement and will generate an additional 4 hours as a reserve for any remaining closures or delayed openings/early dismissals.

Option 2:
      Option 2 is the same as Option 1 with the exception that the amount of time added to each day is increased from 30 to 45 minutes per day. This will cover the necessary makeup time and generate 24.25 additional hours (or 4 days and 2.25 hours) as a reserve for any remaining closures or delayed openings/early dismissals for standard calendar schools. For modified calendar schools this will generate 18.75 additional hours (or 3 days and 2.25 hours).

Option 3:
      Use April 12, 2010, (April 16, 2010, for modified calendar schools) as the first makeup day and add the remaining three makeup days to the end of the year on June 23, 24, and 25.

We are in the process of obtaining additional principal input on these options. Pending this feedback, Option 1 is recommended. It adds a manageable amount of time to each day during a time that is more valuable for instruction. The potential drawback to this option is if we need more than 7 hours of makeup time between now and the end of the year. If we exceed these hours, additional time would need to be added to the end of the year.

Option 2 builds in enough time to provide more makeup days, however, as additional time continues to be added, we reach a point of diminished returns in terms of student engagement, particularly in the earlier grades. The additional 15 minutes also releases students at a time when increased traffic becomes more of a problem. It may also interfere with students who work or have other after-school activities and responsibilities.

Recommendation:

That the School Board extend the school day by 30 minutes from March 8, 2010 - June 21, 2010, and use the scheduled teacher workday on April 12, 2010, as a student day in order to make up missed days due to inclement weather for the 2009-2010 school year; and increase the school day in modified calendar schools in the same manner with the exception of using April 16, 2010, as the full makeup day instead of April 12, 2010.
