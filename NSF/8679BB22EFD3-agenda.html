    SUBJECT: Proposed 2009-2010 Bell Schedule

    TYPE: Discussion

    BACKGROUND:

    In the course of working through bell schedule iterations requested by the School Board, transportation staff noted efficiencies that could be realized, largely through adjustments to the existing bell schedule structure. At the completion of the School Board iterations, transportation specialists spent almost two months looking for the optimal and acceptable bell schedule with the same basic configuration now in use – High Schools/Middle Schools/Elementary Schools.

    These adjustments are separate and apart from the proposal to change school start times that the Fairfax County School Board considered and voted to reject in early 2009. The adjustments contained within this document focuses on achieving efficiencies within the current bell structure, which the Fairfax County School Board voted to retain.

    KEY POINTS:

    Transportation reengineering has four parts:
      1. Transportation has presented a proposed 2009-2010 bell schedule to the division superintendent that provides significant savings. The proposed school start times are based upon the most efficient use of resources. Thus, any alterations to the proposed schedule will result in decreases in savings and will require reconstitution of the revision group to work through the details and implications.
      2. Transportation reorganization is now under way. This reorganization is necessary to make permanent the lessons learned from this process and to ensure transportation can carry forward on improved operational procedures. We are creating a centralized routing group that will use the techniques we have mastered in the intensive iterative process. The new organization structure and requested additional resources can be expected to maintain and probably expand operational efficiencies. The centralized routing group is part of a new section in transportation services called Routing and Planning.
      3. The Routing and Planning section is responsible not just for routing, but will manage our new fleet of 20 mini-vans. These vans will take over some of the transportation handled now by taxi cabs. The section will also have oversight responsibility for remaining taxi cab transportation. It will be the primary contact point for all out-of-boundary (both school and special programs) placements.
      4. Also included in the reorganization now in process is the decentralization of special education transportation. The significant increase in special education demands has made it impossible to maintain just one office for all county-wide demands. We make particular note that decentralization of special education transportation is a priority but will require that we be able to install GPS devices on all special education buses. We have found these devices to be a hugely important tool in the effective and efficient management of transportation resources.

    RECOMMENDATION:
      • Accept the bell schedule as designed. This will result in operational savings in FY 2010 of $4.75 million.
      • Approve the funding for the additional positions and transitional funds needed to reengineer transportation. This cost in FY 2010 will be approximately $.4 million.
      • The net budget recurring annual savings will be approximately $4.3 million.
