The South County High School Marching Band traveled to Allentown on Sunday, November 5th to compete in the USBands A-Class National Championships.  Out of 20 competing bands in their class, South County placed first, and received captions awards for having the best visual performance, best overall effect, and best percussion.  This is the first time in 9 years that the band has competed in this show and received this award.  This capped off an undefeated season for the marching stallions, who placed first in their class at the USBands Northern Virginia Regional at Herndon HS, the USBands Eastern Panhandle Classic at Washington HS and the JMU Parade of Champions.  The band also received a superior rating at the Virginia Band and Orchestra Director’s Association (VBODA) state marching band assessment, the first step towards receiving the organization’s honor band award.  These awards are a testament to the hard work the staff, parents, and especially the students put in throughout the season.  They worked countless hours to put on great performances and bring back this amazing recognition to the band program and the school.

Meeting Category: December 7, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  McNair Elementary School - Authorization to Negotiate and Execute Proffer Statement

                                                                        

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

 

Key Points:

 

The Office of Design & Construction has filed, on behalf of the School Board, Proffered Condition Amendment ("PCA") applications [PCA 87-C-060 and PCA 93-H-045] in order to amend the proffers previously approved for McNair Elementary School. 

 

The purpose of the PCA applications is to permit the construction of an additional elementary school building (with a gross floor area of 115,000 square feet, providing capacity for an additional 750 students) and related modifications to be sited on the existing McNair property as well as an adjacent 1.36 acre parcel, which was previously dedicated to the County for school and park use. Once the additional building is constructed, the school building area at the site will have been more than doubled, and the existing modular classrooms will then be removed. This new, combined site also would allow for a potential 15,000 square foot building addition in the future. 

 

In accordance with County practice, FCPS as applicant must submit a signed Proffer Statement (a draft of which is attached) thirty days prior to the public hearing held by the Board of Supervisors. Typically, such a Proffer Statement requires minor revisions as the PCA application is reviewed by County staff and by the Planning Commission in preparation for the Board of Supervisors’ public hearing. The final version of the Proffer Statement adopted by the Board of Supervisors will go into effect, superseding all prior drafts and previously approved proffers for this property. 

 

The hearing before the Planning Commission is currently scheduled for December 7, 2017, with a hearing before the Board of Supervisors scheduled for January 23, 2018.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all actions necessary and appropriate, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to pursue approval of Proffered Condition Amendment applications for the combined McNair Elementary School site, including negotiating, signing, and submitting the final Proffer Statement and any other documents on behalf of the School Board.

A timeline of the process for the mathematics basal resource review can be found in Attachment A. In June 2017, the school board approved the committee members to review and recommend basal instructional materials for mathematics. The Mathematics Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee convened in July 2017 to review resources for K-12 that were submitted by vendors in response to an RFP for consideration as basal instructional materials for mathematics. In accordance with the Code of Virginia, regulations of the Virginia Board of Education, and Fairfax County Public Schools regulations, the Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee in conjunction with the Selection Advisory Committee and the Technical Advisory Committee recommends the titles listed in Attachment B for adoption at the division level. Additionally, a summary of the committee rationales for the recommended materials and community input received are provided in attachments C and D.

Meeting Category:  December 7, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Grant of Perpetual Street Easement to Virginia Department of Transportation for Public Street Purposes at George C. Marshall High School

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 8330.3 

 

 

Key Points:

 

The Office of Design & Construction has reviewed and found acceptable a proposal for the Virginia Department of Transportation (“VDOT”) to maintain the entirety of George C. Marshall Drive (the “Street”), which runs along the eastern portion of the Marshall High School (the “School”) site.

 

Currently, VDOT maintains most of the Street, beginning at its intersection with Route 7; a portion of the Street (at its opposite terminus) is presently maintained by the adjacent property owner, a condominium association. VDOT’s maintenance of the entire Street as a public roadway would improve its reliability and be advantageous to the School, which uses the Street for additional ingress/egress, student parking, and bus turn-arounds.

 

In order for VDOT to accept the remaining portion of the Street, and thereby become solely responsible for maintenance of the entire Street, the condominium association must perform certain roadway improvements to its portion of the Street to meet current standards. The School Board would grant a Perpetual Street Easement in favor of VDOT for this remaining portion of the Street to accomplish the transfer of future maintenance obligations to VDOT.

 

The Office of Design & Construction has determined that the School Board’s granting the above-referenced Easement is of direct benefit to the School and to the operation of the school system. Per Policy 8330.3(v). no charge would be made by the school system for granting this easement.

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To: Calendar

 

Key Points: 

The 2018 – 2019 Standard School Year Calendar was developed with a focus on instruction and learning time for students, as well as improved professional development opportunities for staff. While teaching and learning was the priority, a multitude of factors were considered and balanced during the development of the calendar. A calendar committee was formed this year to assist in the development of the calendar. The committee was composed of representatives from a variety of internal and external constituent groups. It included representatives of VOICE, FCCPTA, FCPS teacher and staff associations, testing services, curriculum and instruction, professional development and student activities. Highlights of the proposed calendar are included in the following table:

 

 

 

First Teachers’ Day

Aug 17

First Day of School

Aug 28

Thanksgiving Break

Nov 22 – 23 (2 days)

Winter break

Dec 24 –Jan 4 (10 days)

Last Day of School

June 13

Last Teacher Day

June 14

Early Release (ER) Days

Six early release days – 3 hour early release
