Subject:  Staff Response to AAP External Review Recommendations

 

Staff Contact: Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present: Noel Klimenko, director, PreK-12 Curriculum & Instruction; Kirsten Maloney, coordinator, K-12 Advanced Academic Programs

 

Meeting Category:  October 27, 2020-Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To:

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

On May 18, 2020, four researchers presented recommendations from their external review to improve equity in FCPS Advanced Academic Programs (AAP) continuum of services.  The recommendations fall into five categories: ensuring understanding of FCPS AAP goals, revisions to the screening processes for full-time AAP services (level IV), focusing on frontloading, divisionwide professional development, and changing perceptions about available opportunities in FCPS high schools.  Staff will present a response to the recommendations with proposed timeline and budget information.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board review the proposed timeline and costs associated with implementing the external review recommendations and support efforts to strengthen equity in FCPS AAP.

Subject:               2021 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact:      Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:        Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link:          Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options; sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) from the Board’s 2020 legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board discussed proposed changes to the 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program at an October 20th Work Session.  The Work Session will focus on Board priorities for its 2021 State and Federal Priority Positions.  Final Action on the 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program agenda item is scheduled for November 5, 2020.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2021 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program.

Subject: 2021-2022 Standard School Year Calendar

 

Staff Contact:  Sean McDonald, interim assistant superintendent, Human Resources Department

                      Michael Draeger, director, HR Business Services

 

Meeting Category:  October 27, 2020 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

A committee was assembled for a third consecutive year to assist in the development of the calendar. The committee was composed of representatives from a variety of internal and external constituent groups which included community religious organization representatives, teachers, employees and principal association representatives, along with central office staff. The 2021-2022 Standard School Year Calendar was developed with a focus on instruction and learning time for students. While teaching and learning was the priority, a multitude of factors were considered and balanced during the development of the calendar. Additionally, the calendar committee received input and feedback from the recent Religious Observance Task Force and thus the draft calendars were created with an equity lens focus and provide consideration for four religious observance holidays – Rosh Hashanah (9/7/2021), Yom Kippur (9/16/2021), Diwali (11/4/2021), and Eid al Fitr (5/3/2022).

 

Recommendation: That the School Board adopt the 2021-2022 standard school year calendar.
