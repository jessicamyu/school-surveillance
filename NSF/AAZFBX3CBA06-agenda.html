Autism Awareness Month - April 2017

 

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.

Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about and acceptance of autism. Sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA), this year’s goal is to encourage friends and collaborators to work towards acceptance and appreciation of the unique aspects and talents of all people.  Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

 

 

 Mathematics Awareness Month - April 2017

Mathematics Awareness Month is held each year in April to increase public understanding of and appreciation for mathematics. Sponsored by the Joint Policy Board for Mathematics, the goal is to increase the visibility of mathematics as a field of study and to communicate the power and intrigue in mathematics to a larger audience.  The 2017 theme is “Mathematics and Statistics" which takes a look at how both subjects play a significant role in addressing many real world problems  such as internet security, sustainability, disease and much more.  Research in these and other areas is ongoing, revealing new results and applications every day in fields such as medicine, manufacturing, energy, biotechnology, and business.  Mathematics and statistics are important drivers of innovation in our technological world, in which new systems and methodologies continue to become more complex. This April, let's celebrate mathematics and statistics and the diverse researchers and students in these fields who are contributing much to furthering discoveries, solving problems, and finding beauty in our world.

The 2016 Family Career and Community Leaders of America - Glasgow Team

   

I’m delighted to recognize tonight the members of the 2016 Glasgow Middle School team that won the Gold Award at the Family Career and Community Leaders of America competitions at BOTH the state and national levels.  

The School Board extends our hearty congratulations to team members Lacy Callahan, Susan Connell and Zara Green, as well as to their Lead Advisor, Angela DeHart, who teaches Family and Consumer Science at Glasgow.  

 

The team competed in the STAR Food and Innovation event.  Participants were required to demonstrate knowledge of the basic concepts of food product development by creating an original prototype formula, testing the product through focus groups, and developing a marketing strategy.  To make the challenge even more difficult, the teams were limited to using an ancient grain. 

 

Teams had five minutes to set up a display, unassisted, and ten minutes to deliver an oral presentation introducing the audience to a new food, attractively packaged and displayed. 

Lacy, Susan, and Zara demonstrated excellent planning and communications skills in working together.  Their oral presentation which introduced the judges to Magic Toaster Pastries was informative, creative, and delivered with poise.  Earning a Gold Award was a well-deserved honor.

 

I look forward to the day we will see Magic Toaster Pastries on our grocery store shelves -- and we’ll remember the evening when we met and congratulated the inventors. .  At this time I would like to invite Lacy, Susan, and Zara, their family, friends and staff from Glasgow Middle School to join me at the dais for a photo with the Board.

Subject:  FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review        

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2017 budget and to make revisions as necessary. The FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 15, 2016.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund revenue adjustments reflect a decrease of $0.2 million in state revenue for National Board Certified Teacher (NBCT) pass-through payments based on the number of FCPS teachers eligible to receive a stipend from the state in FY 2017.  This grant revenue reduction is offset by corresponding expenditure adjustments. 

 

Recommended School Operating Fund expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $6.7 million. These adjustments include the corresponding expenditure reductions for NBCT totaling $0.2 million, as well as savings of $2.0 million from public system transportation radios resulting from lower cost options identified for replacing bus radios; savings of $1.3 million from transportation fuel; savings totaling $1.4 million from bus driver salary accounts as a result of bus driver and attendant vacancies; and savings of $1.8 million resulting from changes in health plan participation as compared to the projection.

 

In total, in the School Operating Fund, this agenda recognizes decreased revenue totaling $0.2 million and decreased disbursements totaling $6.7 million, which results in available funds totaling $6.5 million. It is recommended that the available funding is added to the FY 2018 Beginning Balance.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.  Also, attached is an update on the status of grants for FY 2017.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure adjustments as reflected in the FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review and as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:  March 23, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Mathematics Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee Members

 

School Board Action Required: Information                

 

Key Points:

 

This committee will review and recommend instructional materials for use in grade K-12 mathematics courses. These materials will be considered basal materials.

 

The Constitution of Virginia gives the Virginia Board of Education the authority to adopt instructional materials for use in the schools. Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Regulation 3004.3 Adopted Basal Instructional Materials incorporates state adoption guidelines and requirements and specifies procedures for establishing committees to review textbooks and other basal instructional materials and make recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board for local adoption. Each FCPS review committee is composed as follows:

 

• Teachers 50%

• School-based administrators 25%

• Community Members 25%

 

Central office staff members serve as nonvoting, ex-officio members to guide and assist the work of the committee.

• Each School Board member shall appoint one community member to the Mathematics Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee by Friday, April 29, 2017.

• Participating staff members will be from the Instructional Services Department and the Department of Special Services.

• School based administrators will be nominated by Region Assistant Superintendents and principal associations.

• Teacher representatives will be selected from applications submitted to Instructional Services.

 

• Basal instructional materials adoption committee meeting dates are scheduled for:

  • July 10, 17, 24 and 31 from 4:00 to 9:00 pm

     

New Mathematics Standards of Learning have been adopted by the Virginia Department of Education and textbook contracts expire June 2018. To address these changes, the Mathematics textbook adoption review process will begin July 2017.

                         

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint committee members to the Mathematics Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

 

Names for committee members should be sent to Rose Moore, Coordinator, K-12 Mathematics (crmoore@fcps.edu)

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
