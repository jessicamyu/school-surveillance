National Hispanic American Heritage Month - September 15 - October 15

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-american community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

Presentation: September 6, 2007

Staff Contact: Richard Moniuszko, Deputy Superintendent

SUBJECT: Revisions to School Planning Process

BACKGROUND: As a result of the Strategic Governance initiative undertaken by the School Board, the School Improvement Planning Process has been revised to focus on the three student achievement goals.

At the school district level, project management will be used to organize the work into approximately 35 district projects to be completed over the next 3-5 years. A Project Management Oversight Committee (PMOC) will set priorities for the work to be done, approve projects, and identify resources, as well as monitor progress through completion. Approval of these projects and the project managers is still in process.

A project team redesigned the School Improvement Plan (SIP) required by the state and Southern Association of Colleges and Schools (SACS) to streamline the process, and align SIPs with the Strategic Goals. Revised School Improvement Plans will be completed by November 1. Two principals (Scott Braband, Fairfax HS and Stephanie Bisson, Woodlawn ES) will attend the School Board meeting to discuss their experience working with faculty and the community using the new process.

Contact: Dan Storck, Chairman

SUBJECT: Transportation Task Force

RECOMMENDATION:
That the School Board appoint citizens and staff members to serve on the Transportation Task Force, as listed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:
      Over the past year, the School Board has been discussing the possibility of reengineering the transportation system of Fairfax County Public Schools to reduce operational risk and to address minimum service levels not currently being met, and possibly to consider changing school start times, particularly for high schools.

      At the July 16, 2007, work session, the Board agreed to establish a Transportation Task Force with representatives from a wide range and variety of constituencies and stakeholders to assess transportation reengineering options and the implications for the community, and to identify pros and cons of adjusting parameters and bell schedule alternatives. The Task Force will help the School Board and staff members determine the project parameters before the technical work begins and offer an assessment of any recommendations.

      The Task Force will have up to 70 members, with the Chairman of the School Board selecting, in consultation with the entire Board, a chairman and vice chairman of the Task Force. The first meeting of the Task Force will be in September 2007 with a final report from the Task Force to be presented to the School Board in January 2008.

Staff Contact: Deirdra McLaughin, Chief Financial Officer, Department of Financial Services

SUBJECT: Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board adopt the revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution.
      EXPLANATION:
          The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution on June 26, 2006. During the 2007 session of the General Assembly, 35 bills were introduced relating to procurement and/or contracts. Of this number, 14 bills passed; however, none modified a mandatory section of the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA). The following procedural changes are recommended.
              • The definition for an ‘independent contractor’ is added to make County terminology consistent with Internal Revenue Service definitions. The definition is added in correlation with the elimination of the term ‘personal services,’ thus establishing consistency.
              • The process for selection, negotiation, and approval of proposals for consultant services has been revised to establish consistency with other contracts awarded through competitive negotiations.
              • All text relating to solicitation, evaluation, and award of construction contracts was consolidated into Article 3 to provide easier reference and identification of such provisions.

      Administrative revisions to the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution were approved by the Board of Supervisors on July 9, 2007.
