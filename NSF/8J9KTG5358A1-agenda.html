TECHNOLOGY OPERATIONAL EXPECTATION

 

The Superintendent will use technology to support teaching, learning, and the Board’s operational expectations and student achievement goals.  The Superintendent will:

 

  1. Provide a comprehensive and functional technology infrastructure.
  2. Provide technology capabilities that are useful for staff members and students.
  3. Use technology to support diverse learning techniques and styles.
  4. Provide a means for interactive communication between the school system, parents, students, and the community.
  5. Provide information electronically about school and division programs and academic progress.
  6. Provide a system to access relevant and current data by appropriate users.

[Posted 2/10/12]

Staff Contact:        Richard Moniuszko, Deputy Superintendent  

 

Meeting Category: February 13, 2012 - Session No. 80

 

Subject:                 Recognition for High School Graduates who Enlist in the Military Service

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points: 

  • At the January 26, 2012 Forum work session, the School Board agreed to schedule a work session to discuss how to appropriately recognize High School Graduates who have enlisted in the military service.
  • High Schools maintain individualized graduation ceremonies while following system guidelines.

Recommendation:  

  • To be consistent with other recognitions at graduations, it is recommended that the graduates who enlisted in the military, as well as those who accepted ROTC scholarships or enrolled in one of the military academies be provided a cord for military service recognition at graduation. 
  • The meaning of the military service cord will be noted in the Commencement Exercises program, consistent with other cords.
