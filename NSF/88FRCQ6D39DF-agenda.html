Meeting Category:               March 14, 2011 – Work Session No. 88

 

Subject:                                 Budget – Test Fees    

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2011 Approved Budget included two new student fees: test fees for Advanced Placement (AP) and International Baccalaureate (IB) tests and athletic participation fees.  This school year, FCPS invoiced $2,785,800 in AP/IB Fees.  As of January 20, the division had received $2.0 million in payments. For FY 2011, FCPS budgeted $2.0 million in revenue from AP/IB Test Fees.

 

On January 28, 2011, Kenneth T. CuccineIli, II, the Attorney General for Virginia issued an opinion stating, “It is my opinion that a local school board cannot impose a mandatory fee on students taking advanced placement courses for the required taking of the Advanced Placement Examination. ”  The full text of the opinion is available at http://www.oag.state.va.us/OPINIONS/2011opns/10-121-Marsden.pdf

 

As a result of the Attorney General’s opinion, on February 3, 2011, when the School Board adopted the FY 2012 Advertised Budget they included a directive to the Superintendent to develop options in response to the recent Attorney General’s opinion on AP/IB fees and to present a recommendation to the Board at the March 14 Budget work session.

 

Opinions available to the School Board for complying with this ruling are to:

  1. Make the AP/IB tests not mandatory for participation in the related courses and continue charging test fees
  2. Continue to require students enrolled in AP/IB courses to take the related test and eliminate test fees.  FCPS would resume paying the full cost of AP/IB tests, as was the practice prior to the fee implementation

 

The recommendation is to eliminate the AP/IB test fees.  All test fee payments received will be refunded.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Superintendent’s recommendation to continue to mandate test participation for AP/IB courses and eliminate the related test fees.  Payments received by FCPS for this school year will be refunded.

Staff Contact:              Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:      March 14, 2011 – Work Session No. 89

 

Subject:                       Annandale Regional Planning Study – Non-Boundary Solutions

 

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Background:

 

On April 29, 2010, the School Board authorized the formation of an ad hoc committee for the Annandale Regional Planning Study.  The mission of the ad hoc study group was to examine overcrowding at the middle and high school levels, particularly Annandale High School, and review attendance area options for a new elementary school at the Lacey Site in the Annandale area of the county.  Since the issues are two-fold, two separate study committees were formed 1) to focus on the high and middle schools and 2) to focus on the elementary schools. 

 

The committee members, appointed by PTA and PTO leadership, met from July through December 2010. The committees conducted community outreach in January and February 2011 to inform the affected communities about the process and issues; feedback from community outreach efforts will continue to be collected on a web survey until April 1, 2011.  The schools currently included in the regional study are the following: Annandale, Edison, Falls Church, Lee, Stuart, West Springfield, and Woodson High Schools; Lake Braddock Secondary School; Frost, Glasgow, Holmes, Jackson, and Poe Middle Schools; and Annandale Terrace, Beech Tree, Belvedere, Braddock, Columbia, Sleepy Hollow, Westlawn, and Woodburn Elementary Schools.

 

On January 10, 2011, both the elementary and middle/high school study groups presented their reports and findings to the School Board.  The reports contain the different boundary solution options for both a new elementary school and for addressing overcrowding at Annandale High School.  Background information on the formation of this ad hoc study, their reports and presentations to the School Board and supporting staff materials may be found at  http://www.fcps.edu/fts/planning/annandalestudy/index.htm.  The middle/high school planning group presented a series of non-boundary solutions to address overcrowding for Annandale High School which is the focus of the discussion and staff analysis below.

 

Discussion:

 

Staff has prepared the attached matrix which identifies the advantages and challenges/solutions for the non-boundary solution options that were presented to the School Board by the middle and high school committee.  Feedback has been provided by those departments that would be central to implementing changes needed to accommodate the non-boundary options.

 

In order to evaluate these options, staff has identified the following considerations which have been specifically stated or implied during the process to consider possible options for addressing overcrowding at Annandale HS and in the surrounding area schools.  These considerations are listed alphabetically and include:

  

  • Community Stability– Keep neighborhoods together over time to develop/enhance a true sense of community.  The planning committee has pursued non-boundary options mainly because of the value they place on the stability of the communities that individually and collectively comprise Annandale HS.  A related objective is to minimize the number of split feeders that cause students to lose a peer group as they part ways at school transition points.

 

  • Demographic Balance– Maintain/enhance balance of demographics within and across schools.  The planning committee has explicitly stated the importance of maintaining communities as part of Annandale HS who comprise a large percentage of those involved in various academic and extracurricular programs at the school.  The reason for this consideration is that losing these communities to a boundary change would have a disproportionate impact on the make-up of the school and its ability to sustain certain programs.

 

  • Economic Efficiency– Exercise appropriate financial stewardship.  Careful consideration should be given to ensure that capital and program expenditures are aligned with the long-term goals/direction of the district and reflect a fair allocation of financial resources as compared to other regions facing similar challenges within FCPS.

 

  • Instructional Support/Resource Alignment– Align schools so that they can best access the resources of FCPS and the delivery of instructional support in the most efficient manner.  A large majority of schools within FCPS are organized as K-6, 7-8 and 9-12.  Experience with the K-5 and 6-8 configurations in parts of FCPS have surfaced a number of challenges with resource allocation/utilization and with maximizing the ability to communicate and partner for improvement (benchmarking).  The creation of new configurations at the secondary level may create similar challenges and generate additional costs to initiate and sustain the unique configurations.

 

  • Manageable Timeline– Enable the timeline for change and transition to be manageable in light of the many other changes currently underway at Annandale HS.  Significant change is underway at Annandale HS with the retirement of the principal and two other key administrative positions.  Change timelines need to account for the overall impact on the culture of the school, staff, and students as they work to successfully implement the required adjustments.

 

  • Minimal Transitions– Minimize the number of school transitions for a student.  Research suggests that the more transitions a student has in his/her K-12 experience, the more this is a potential barrier to school success.

 

  • Outreach/Community Support– Engage the support of the community.  It is important that all stakeholders be invited, informed, engaged and considered as options to alleviate the region’s overcrowding are reviewed.  Outreach efforts should be inclusive of all languages, cultures, and neighborhoods.  Community support of the final decision will be a vital component in achieving a successful outcome.

 

  • Space Utilization– Effectively utilize space and eliminate overcrowding.  To steward the public resources in the most effective manner over time, schools need to have enough space to meet the needs of the students they currently serve while maintaining some flexibility to respond to projected changes in enrollment.  In addition, when schools become too small, especially at the secondary level, they are no longer able to provide the range of program opportunities (both academic and extra-curricular) of larger schools.  This can also create the need for mid-day transportation shuttles, which present cost and logistical concerns.

 

Staff believes that these considerations will be helpful to the Board in the evaluation of the non-boundary solutions options as provided in the attached matrix.  

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board direct staff to identify a regional scope for a study to alleviate overcrowding at Annandale High School using boundary and at least one non-boundary solution.

 

That the School Board direct staff to identify a scope of study and grade model (K-5 or K-6) in order to establish an attendance area for a new elementary school at the Lacey Site.

 

That staff return to the School Board with these recommendations by April 2011.
