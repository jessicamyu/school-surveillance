Summary/Background (Key Points)

 

In May 2018, the School Board directed the Superintendent to work with the chief equity officer and the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement (ORSI) to plan for and conduct an internal and external review of race and income disproportionality in discipline outcomes in FCPS (known as Phase III of the Discipline Study). This work on student behavior, discipline, and disproportionality follows earlier reporting to the School Board in November 2018 (Phase I) and presentation of the plan for the internal and reviews presented to the School Board in February 2019 (Phase II).

 

The School Board specified that staff return in June 2019 with the outcome of the internal and external reviews. Today’s presentation reflects the findings, conclusions, and recommendations from the three studies (two external and one internal). Each of the internal and external reviews provides recommendations that fall broadly within three areas, several of which require professional development to support their implementation:

  • Division Expectations (including those captured in policy)
  • Implementation of School Practices
  • Division Infrastructure (e.g., processes for collection of discipline data, stakeholder involvement).

In response to the recommendations found in the three studies, and in keeping with the School Board’s request, the chief equity officer has developed FCPS’ Equity Plan for Discipline Policy and Practices, which will also be presented. The Equity Plan is designed to guide FCPS with recommended actions that will promote assessment and analysis, build awareness, ensure system alignment, and explore research-based approaches that support student success and create shifts in practice as it relates to equitable discipline.

 

The Equity Plan is broken down into four main components:

  • Professional Development and Training
  • Policies
  • School Practices
  • Resources and Budget

Summary/Background:

 

On March 7, 2019, the School Board approved a streamlined Strategic Plan with eight areas of focus for school years 2018-2020. These areas of focus have specific strategic actions to move the Division toward its goals. Each strategic action has targeted metrics to show the Division’s current baseline data, an interim target to achieve by school year 2020, and an aspirational statement that describes what success would look like for students, families, employees, and the community.

 

This Caring Culture report is the first report under the new reporting structure and describes actions and outcomes from SY 2017-2018, which transpired prior to the recent approval of the enhanced strategic plan. Future reports will describe intentionally-aligned actions implemented to help the division reach its desired outcomes.

 

Goal 2 of the Strategic Plan details FCPS’ commitment to Caring Culture and will focus on:

  • Welcoming Environment
  • Healthy Life Choices

 

A presentation and a narrative report will detail the Division’s performance in school year 2017-2018 as it relates to the revised metrics in the Strategic Plan.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 2: Caring Culture report as detailed in the agenda item.
