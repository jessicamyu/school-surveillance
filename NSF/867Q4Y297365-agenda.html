Fairfax County Public Schools
Regulation 2245, Home Instruction by Parents and
Part-Time Enrollment of Home-Schooled Students
February 24, 2005

Overview
At the request of the School Board, staff members from the Departments of Instructional Services, Special Services, and Information Technology, as well as Division Counsel and representatives from principals’ organizations, explored options for the partial enrollment of home-schooled students. Regulation 2245 allows high school-aged home-schooled students to enroll in courses through the Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Office of Adult and Community Education (ACE). Courses may be customized for student groups. Prerequisites for courses must be satisfied. According to ACE, there are no home-schooled students presently enrolled. This current practice is the result of the School Board’s consideration of a proposal for partial enrollment of home-schooled high school students in January 1999.

Statistics on Home-Schooled
Elementary School 1213
Middle School 230
High School 306
Secondary School 81


Underlying Principles
Staff based its explorations on the following underlying principles:
· The current regulation is consistent with current staffing and resource levels.
· Virginia school divisions are not obligated to offer public school classes and programs to students who are home-schooled. The decision regarding the partial enrollment of these students is left to the discretion of local school boards. As indicated by the attached charts, Loudoun, Prince William, Stafford, Fauquier, and Falls Church allow partial enrollment; Alexandria and Arlington do not. Elsewhere in Virginia, Chesterfield allows such enrollment; Henrico, Spotsylvania, and Virginia Beach do not. In Maryland, Montgomery, Prince Georges, and Frederick counties do not allow partial enrollment.
· Students attending part-time will not have the advantage of a planned sequence of courses and a planned group of activities that enables students to grow intellectually, academically, socially, and emotionally.

Options for Partial Enrollment
A continuum of options for part-time enrollment of home-schooled students includes:

1. No change to the current regulation.
2. Modify the current regulation to permit home-schooled high school-aged students (grades 9 through 12) to enroll in FCPS Adult and Community Education (ACE) and or FCPS Online Campus for a maximum of two courses tuition free, with tuition being charged for additional courses.
3. Modify the current regulation to permit home-schooled high school-aged students (grades 9 through 12) to enroll in FCPS for a maximum of two core courses on a space available basis.

The greater the change to the existing policy, the greater will be the impact on schools, staff, and resources. Option 2 would cause minimal disruption to the school-based instructional program, while still providing access to certified teachers and curriculum both in a classroom setting and in 32 online courses. Option 3 would require a review of records to verify that any prerequisites have been completed and that the student, through prior course work, is capable of achieving success in the requested class. Placement testing may be required.

Potential Implications and Issues
The following potential implications of partial enrollment should be considered in the decision-making process:

SOLs/AYP/NCLB
Any student enrolled in a course with a corresponding end-of-course SOL test must take that test. Thus, schools would have increased responsibility for:
· Remediating gaps in the academicpreparation of part-time students.
· Maintaining AYP participation ratesalthough partially enrolled students may be less motivated to take (and pass) SOL tests.
· Maintaining accreditation ratings although partially enrolled students may not have had prior instruction by highly qualified teachers.

Staffing
Partial enrollment may have the following impact:
· Additional staff required for tasks related to:
§ Implementing routine school registration requirements, e.g., residency, health, prior education documentation
§ Processing applications of potential part-time enrollees in a timely manner in the home instruction office
§ Monitoring class size and addressing issues related to limited capacity in upper level courses, AP and IB courses, and some specialty program classes
§ Managing partial enrollment for administrative and budgeting purposes
§ Providing catch-up or remedial work in a subject
§ Monitoring students entering and exiting the building at off hours
§ Accommodating students with gaps in their schedule when two classes are not taken consecutively
· Increased demand for counseling, health-related services, and college advising
· Additional resources may be required to accommodate students with disabilities
· Difficulties in monitoring compliance with compulsory attendance law (22.1-254) may result in increased workload for attendance officers and support staff

Costs
Students enrolled on a part-time basis in certain courses , including core courses, are counted in the average daily membership (ADM) on a pro rata basis. It is estimated that a counselor may need to spend two hours at a cost of $85.00 reviewing a student’s record, administering placement tests, and performing other administrative work to enroll a home-schooled student. The cost of textbooks and other supplies is estimated at $25.00 per student per class.

Access
· Increased enrollment of part-time students in online courses may limit availability of online classes for homebound and full-time FCPS students.
· Part-time enrollees may limit course selection for students transferring from other FCPS schools or for students enrolling full-time after the school year has begun.

Parent/Student Expectations
· Allowing limited part-time enrollment may increase demand for expanded part-time services.
· Parents of full-time students may request to opt their children out of a class and provide it through home-schooling (Requests for this have been received.).
Logistical Issues
· Providing timely verification of compliance with the Code of Virginia August 15 deadline that would establish eligibility for part-time enrollment.
· Modified calendar schools begin the school year in mid August which provides insufficient time to meet the intent and progress reporting deadline for home instruction set by the Code of Virginia.
· Difficulty of providing equitable discipline for part-time students since administrative options for appropriate corrective actions (e.g., detention, ISS, counseling, alternative education programs) would be impacted.

Student-Specific Issues
Challenges related to needs of specific home instructed students, such as:

· Many home-schooled students are on an accelerated schedule, may be much younger than their high school classmates, and may have limited experience dealing with high school social issues and peer pressure.
· Some students are being home instructed because of disciplinary problems when enrolled in school.
· Home-schooled students may have special health needs that would have to be met.

Recommended Limits of Change
If part-time enrollment of home-schooled students is approved, staff recommends the following:

· Part-time enrollment should be limited to core academic courses only.
· Part-time students should be enrolled on a space available basis only.
· Part-time students should be required to satisfy enrollment criteria (prerequisite courses) for enrollment in credited courses: review of grades, transcripts, or administration of prerequisite course assessment(s).
· Completion of all prerequisites for courses should be documented and reviewed by FCPS staff prior to enrollment.
· Prohibit awarding of FCPS diploma and participation in graduation.
· Register part-time students centrally through Student Registration, in cooperation with ISD, to verify eligibility for part-time enrollment, review records, and provide placement testing.
· Require part-time students to meet the same entrance requirements as other students, e.g. residence, immunizations, etc.
· Provide no FCPS transportation.
· Permit only base school enrollment, unless course requested has no space available or is not offered at base school.
· Limit part-time students’ presence on campus to the times of their class(es).
· Prohibit enrollment of part-time students in a class after the first week of school.
· Require compliance with all requirements of the selected class(es), e.g. SOLs, AP/IB tests, etc.
· Require parent to sign an agreement form acknowledging understanding of rights and limits of part-time enrollment.
· Enforce SR&R and all other applicable school rules for part-time students.
· Provide additional resources for schools and offices as necessary.
