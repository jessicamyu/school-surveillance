DECLARING THE FIRST FRIDAY IN JUNE TO BE

NATIONAL GUN VIOLENCE AWARENESS DAY

 

 

This resolution declares the first Friday in June to be National Gun Violence Awareness Day in  Fairfax County Public Schools to honor and remember all victims and survivors of gun violence and to declare that we as a country must do more to reduce gun violence.

 

     WHEREAS, every day, 100 Americans are killed by gun violence and on average there are nearly 13,000 gun homicides every year; and

 

     WHEREAS, Americans are 25 times more likely to be killed with guns than people in other high-income developed countries; and

 

     WHEREAS, protecting public safety in the communities they serve is the district’s highest responsibility; and

 

     WHEREAS, support for the Second Amendment rights of law-abiding citizens goes hand-in-hand with keeping guns away from dangerous people with dangerous histories; and

 

     WHEREAS, in January 2013, Hadiya Pendleton, a teenager who marched in President Obama’s second inaugural parade and was tragically shot and killed just weeks later, should be now celebrating her 22nd birthday; and

 

     WHEREAS, to help honor Hadiya – and the 100 Americans whose lives are cut short and the countless survivors who are injured by shootings every day – a national coalition of organizations has designated June 7, 2019, the first Friday in June, as the 5th National Gun Violence Awareness Day; and

 

     WHEREAS, the idea was inspired by a group of Hadiya’s friends, who asked their classmates to commemorate her life by wearing orange; they chose this color because hunters wear orange to announce themselves to other hunters when out in the woods and orange is a color that symbolizes the value of human life; and

 

     WHEREAS, anyone can join this campaign by pledging to Wear Orange on June 7th, the first Friday in June in 2019, to help raise awareness about gun violence; and

 

     WHEREAS, by wearing orange on June 7, 2019 Americans will raise awareness about gun violence and honor the lives of gun violence victims and survivors; and

 

     WHEREAS, we renew our commitment to reduce gun violence and pledge to do all we can to keep firearms out of the wrong hands, and encourage responsible gun ownership to help keep our children safe.

 

     NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board declares the first Friday in June, June 7, 2019, to be National Gun Violence Awareness Day. We encourage all schools to support their local communities’ efforts to prevent the tragic effects of gun violence and to honor and value human lives.  I so move.

FCPS High School Voter Registration Challenge

 

The FCPS High School Voter Registration Challenge was created to encourage students to register to vote. Encouraging voter registration and providing registration opportunities was a vigorous endeavor supported by high school social studies teachers, the League of Women Voters of the Fairfax Area, the Fairfax County Board of Elections, Fairfax County Council of PTAs, Inspire Virginia, and the Instructional Services social studies team. Many voter registration events were held in schools. These events provided practical opportunities for students to make real life connections between their course work and adult civic responsibility. Voting is a cornerstone of our democracy and providing access to voter registration prepares students to exercise their voting rights. Students who are registered voters will be able to participate in decision-making at the local, state, and federal level.

 

Tonight, as part of the FCPS High School Voter Registration Challenge, we recognize teachers, staff, and organizations who supported these voter registration efforts. Most importantly, we are pleased to recognize the schools that registered at least 50 percent of the eligible members of their senior class:

  • Fairfax High School
  • Fall Church High School
  • Hayfield Secondary School
  • Herndon High School
  • Lake Braddock High School
  • Lee High School
  • Mt. Vernon High School
  • Quander Road School
  • Robinson Secondary School
  • Thomas Jefferson School for Science and Technology

Summary/Background:

On March 7, 2019, the School Board approved a streamlined Strategic Plan with eight areas of focus for school years 2018-2020. These areas of focus have specific strategic actions to move the Division toward its goals. Each strategic action has targeted metrics to show the Division’s current baseline data, an interim target to achieve by school year 2020, and an aspirational statement that describes what success would look like for students, families, employees, and the community.

This Student Success report is the first report under the new reporting structure and describes actions and outcomes from SY 2017-18, which transpired prior to the recent approval of the enhanced strategic plan. While these actions were not intentionally aligned to the current metrics approved in March 2019, they did further the majority of these new metrics. Future reports will describe intentionally-aligned actions implemented to help the division reach its desired outcomes.

Goal 1 of the Strategic Plan details FCPS’ commitment to Student Success and will focus on:

  • Elimination of Gaps
  • Early Education
  • Portrait of a Graduate Attributes

A presentation and a narrative report will detail the Division’s performance in school year 2017-2018 as it relates to the revised metrics in the Strategic Plan.

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success report as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:  Award of Contract

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

                       

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                  Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services

                                   Michelle R. Pratt, director, Procurement Services

                                  Richard Pollio, director, ESOL Services

 

Meeting Category: May 23, 2019 â€“ Regular Meeting

 

Ignite Link:  Strategic Plan Goal 1:  Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Instructional Services Department recommends that the School Board award a contract to the University of Wisconsin for the provision of English language proficiency (ELP) assessments.  These assessments are mandated by the Virginia Department of Education for use by all public schools in Virginia.

 

The total estimated payment to the University of Wisconsin for this assessment for the 2018-2019 school year is expected to be $1.1M; the payment amount is based upon the total number of assessments submitted for scoring in a given school year.  Policy 5011 requires that the School Board approve â€œany contract or contract amendment awarded between the School Board and other governmental entity that exceeds $250,000.â€  Policy 5011.3 (III)(A)(1).

 

These assessments also comply with federal legislation [Section 3113(b)(2) and Section 1111 (b) (7) of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 2015] and related guidance from the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE), which requires that all English learners (ELs):

 

  1. be assessed in English language proficiency upon entry into a Virginia public school; and
  2. be assessed annually in English language proficiency.  

 

This assessment suite from the University of Wisconsin, developed by the World-Class Instructional Design and Assessment (WIDA) Consortium, measures an English learnerâ€™s proficiency in English reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills for the purpose of establishing the studentâ€™s eligibility for English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) services and monitoring the studentâ€™s progress in acquiring English proficiency.

 

 

Assessments within the WIDA suite are:

 

  • WIDA Assessing Comprehension and Communication in English State to State for English Language LearnersÂ® 2.0 (WIDA ACCESS for ELLs 2.0), used for annual assessment of most ELs
  • WIDA Alternate ACCESS for ELLs, used for annual assessment of certain ELs with significant cognitive abilities
  • WIDA-ACCESS Placement Test (W-APT), used for entering kindergarten ELs
  • WIDA Screener, used for ELs entering or re-entering FCPS from outside Virginia public schools
  • WIDA Measure of Developing English Language (MODEL), used for ELs entering in second semester of kindergarten or first semester of grade 1

 

The Virginia Board of Education adopted the WIDA ACCESS for ELLs 2.0 in 2007 and the WIDA Alternate ACCESS for ELLs in 2013 as the state-approved ELP annual assessments for EL students. 

 

In FCPS, these assessments are used to:

 

  • Determine studentsâ€™ ELP level, eligibility for ESOL services, and eligibility for testing accommodations and exemptions;
  • Calculate EL progress as part of the combined rate for English Achievement and English Achievement Gap school quality indicators within the Standards of Accreditation (SOA);
  • Help teachers and families understand studentsâ€™ current level of English language proficiency and progress toward proficiency along the developmental continuum;
  • Provide teachers with information they can use to plan for and support student learning;
  • Produce data necessary to calculate annual ESOL staffing allocations for each school; and
  • Generate information required for annual application for federal Title III, Part A funding.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (i) award the contract to the University of Wisconsin for English language proficiency assessments for the period of time required by the Virginia Department of Education, beginning with 2018-2019 school year, and (ii) authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.
