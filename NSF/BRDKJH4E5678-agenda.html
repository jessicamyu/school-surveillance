 Equity in Education Month – March 2021

 

WHEREAS, we as a county have verbalized the words and promised to establish a One Fairfax as an aspiration, and understand the long path ahead to actualize this imperative.

 

WHEREAS, we first must acknowledge the traditional, ancestral, unceded territory of the Manahoac People on which we are learning, working and gathering today. We also must acknowledge the history of genocide, slavery, and exclusion in this area and throughout the United States. This leaves us with a legacy that persists today as we continue to work towards justice, equity, liberation, and the decolonization of our minds here in Fairfax County; and

 

WHEREAS, we remember that at the very founding of the concept of the United States of America, forged by immigrants seeking asylum from persecution in their own homelands, a commitment was made to prevent a tyranny of the majority; and

 

WHEREAS, we recognize that factors including, but not limited to, race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, religion, differing ability, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, documentation, language, and the varying ways in which these factors intersect have a demonstrated and deep history of impacting the quality of educational opportunities provided to a student; and

 

WHEREAS, we know that this history includes an active and deliberate attempt to undermine equity in education specifically to resist civil rights attempts to correct the wrongs of this legacy, from the very aftermath of the Supreme Court case Brown v. Board of Education, where our own Fairfax County Public School leaders stood on the wrong side, and where today we affirm our commitment otherwise; and

 

WHEREAS, we know that at the root of these inequities is dehumanization of the other, and the resulting maldistribution of power-- power that is not as available to everyone, and that lives in a context where decisions were made to establish legal and structural obstacles and barriers to preserve the interests of the majority, of the most powerful, and of those with the loudest voices… power that we hold and carry moral burdens and obligations to honor; and

 

WHEREAS, as School Board Members, we recognize that the work of equity starts with ourselves, and that it requires deep and deliberate introspection, empathy, and self-improvement, and that we must sensitize ourselves to differences, explore our own privileges and biases, and find the humanity in each other in order to become more worthy and effective public servants; and

 

WHEREAS, we hear and see our students, and understand that despite past efforts, their experiences in our schools are not all the same. We are prepared to rectify and have no tolerance for any and all experiences that lead a child to feel anything less than supported, proud, and loved.

 

WHEREAS, we acknowledge the courage required to tackle disparities that continue in resource and opportunity gaps that have become all too predictable. It is not lost on us that we have achievement gaps that have barely narrowed in over ten years, as well as boundaries and programming shaped by the legacies of our unjust past. We understand that our children learn a curriculum that they may not all relate to or feel motivated by, and we know that experiences of difference exist from and among both students and adults.

 

WHEREAS, we must interrogate our system in all its dimensions-- seeking equity for staff as well. We must examine our policies and practices vertically and horizontally, above and below the surface. 

 

WHEREAS, the the COVID-19 pandemic and corresponding economic crisis has exacerbated and compounded the inequities already experienced by too many in our community, exposing what has and always should have been urgent, it is more important that we as advocates and servants of public education are addressing inequities head on rather than with platitudes and half measure; and

 

WHEREAS, We can no longer be naive to the fact that to some establishing equity can feel like injustice when it has persisted for so long, and as we continue our work we must strive to face our challenges confidently and seek to advance the interests of all even at and especially when they are at a cost to ourselves.

 

WHEREAS, it is important for Fairfax families, students, teachers, school staff and administrators to encourage and center robust and ongoing discussion, reflection, and confrontation of the inequities present within their school communities. 

 

WHEREAS, We see our challenges as opportunities, and we look to our potential. We are Fairfax County Public Schools. We call upon all to tap into our deeper humanity and fearlessly imagine how far we can go when we face our fears and advance together.

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that Fairfax County Public Schools recognizes the month of March 2021 as VSBA Equity in Education Month, with the intention that the system-wide work of equity starts right here, with our very selves.

Ignite Link: Caring Culture, Student Success

Summary/Background: In order to support students and families who may be relocating during the COVID-19 emergency, for the remainder of SY 2020-2021, FCPS is requesting a temporary exception to Policy 2202. Currently, P2202, Section III. I., allows for families becoming residents in another jurisdiction within 60 calendar days of the end of the school year to request to continue attending his or her previously assigned school on a non-tuition bases until the completion of the school year. For SY 2020-2021, this would allow for these requests to be considered as of April 13, 2021. However, in order to support students and families, FCPS is requesting we make a temporary exception to this policy to allow requests, as outlined in the recommended action.


This temporary exception will allow for families who are permanently relocating outside of Fairfax County to request for their child to remain enrolled in their current school of enrollment within FCPS (either virtually or in-person) for the remainder of SY 2020-2021 . Per P2202, students would no longer be eligible for enrollment after the end of SY 2020-2021 and will be required to transition to their new district school of residence in SY 2021-2022.

 

 

Recommended Action:    The School Board to review P2202, Section III. I. to consider a temporary exception, pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-5(A)(4), that will allow for families who are permanently relocating outside of Fairfax County to a state or jurisdiction that is near the Commonwealth of Virginia to request for their child to remain enrolled in their current school of enrollment within FCPS (either virtually or in-person) for the remainder of SY 2020-2021.

Subject: Solar Facilities Contracts

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Justin Moss, director, Facilities Maintenance

 

 

Meeting Category: March 4, 2021, Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Related to: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background (Key points):

 

Beginning in July 2019, Fairfax County Government (acting as procurement agent for itself, certain County authorities, and FCPS) issued its request for proposals for a turn-key solar power purchase program for use by the designated governmental entities.  The County Government evaluated offerors’ proposals and negotiated contractual agreements with primary, secondary, and tertiary contractors for rooftop installations, and also for installation of canopy systems.

 

Pursuant to the County program (which is effective through 12/31/2022 with four additional one-year renewal options), each designated governmental entity (including FCPS) would enter into a separate project-specific agreement for each of its facility locations where solar panels are to be installed.

 

The implementation of the County’s overall program was delayed by certain legal issues, which were resolved recently according to County officials. On February 11, 2021, amended contract documents for rooftop solar photovoltaic panels were executed by the primary contractor and Fairfax County Government on behalf of County entities and FCPS.  Contracts for the secondary and tertiary contractors are also in place.

 

Under the solar program, one of the selected solar contractors will design, permit, install, and operate rooftop and/or canopy-mounted solar panels at governmental facilities and sell the electricity generated by the solar panels to the participating County entity or FCPS for use at that facility.  County entities and FCPS will purchase such electricity at a fixed rate and over a fixed term of 25-28 years, depending on the type of solar panels used.

 

This program allows FCPS to purchase on-site renewable energy with little or no upfront or operational costs, and with the anticipated benefit of reducing FCPS’ greenhouse gas emissions and electricity costs.

 

As part of each project-specific agreement, FCPS will lease or license use of certain school property to the solar contractor to the extent required for installation, operation, and maintenance of the site’s solar panels (and associated equipment) for the purpose of generating electricity.

 

DFTS will interface with, and review the plans submitted and work performed by, each such contractor, as is customary with FCPS facility improvement projects. 

 

To date, DFTS has identified some 160 FCPS facilities to participate in the solar program.  The final number of solar locations within the school division is dependent, in part, upon the duration of the overall program as determined by Fairfax County Government, as well as the demand for solar installations by County entities and the capacity of the selected contractors to perform the design and installation services throughout the County.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve participation in the Fairfax County solar program and authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all action necessary and appropriate on behalf of the School Board to negotiate, execute and administer project contracts (and such other related documentation, including associated leases and/or site licenses) with an authorized solar contractor as a part of the solar program for the benefit of the school division.

 

 

Alternative: None recommended

Subject: Stonecroft Transportation Center – Boundary Confirmation Agreement with Fairfax County Water Authority

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Jessica Gillis, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: March 4, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

 

Related To: Virginia Code § 22.1-129(B)

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Virginia statutory law authorizes a local school board to convey real property to adjust the boundaries of its property, where deemed proper. Virginia Code § 22.1-129 (B).

 

A boundary adjustment is necessary at the rear of the FCPS Stonecroft Transportation parcel, which is adjacent to real property acquired in 2017 by Fairfax County Water Authority (“Fairfax Water”) for use as a utility maintenance facility.

 

A long-ago survey error (believed to have occurred between 1914 and 1944) on the Stonecroft property mistakenly resulted in a narrow and irregular shaped strip of sloping land (2622 square feet in total) being identified as common to both the FCPS Stonecroft parcel and what is now the adjoining Fairfax Water parcel. If not for this survey error, this narrow strip of land would be part of the Fairfax Water parcel, only.  Due to its steep grade, this narrow strip of land is not used by FCPS now, nor is it suitable for development in the future; simply put, it is of no use to FCPS.  Fairfax Water plans to install drainage piping within this strip of land in order to capture overflow from the Stonecroft parcel, thus benefitting the FCPS property as well as the surrounding neighborhood. The survey error came into focus during the site plan review process for Fairfax Water’s maintenance facility.

 

The FCPS Office of Design and Construction has reviewed all pertinent information and confirms its concurrence with the Fairfax Water boundary survey and the need to adjust the Stonecroft boundary.

 

Once approved by the School Board, the parties will enter into a written agreement known as a Boundary Confirmation Agreement, which will be recorded in the Fairfax County land records in order to document this resolution. FCPS will not incur any transaction or recording costs.

 

This action will in no way permanently damage, interfere with or limit present or future use of FCPS property, nor will it endanger the health and safety of users of the Stonecroft Transportation Center.  The drainage improvements planned by Fairfax Water benefit the neighborhood in general and will benefit FCPS more directly, as that is also a drainage outfall for the Stonecroft Transportation Facility.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve this adjustment to the boundary of its Stonecroft Transportation Center property and authorize the Division Superintendent or the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all action necessary and appropriate on behalf of the School Board, and in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to complete, execute, and deliver the Boundary Confirmation Agreement to Fairfax County Water Authority.

 

 

Alternative:

None Recommended

Subject:  Award of Contract to Northern Virginia Community College – Pathway to Baccalaureate Program

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Michelle Pratt, director, Office of Procurement Services

                                  Stephanie Gray, coordinator, School Counseling and College Success

Lynette Henry, manager, College Success Programs

 

Meeting Category:  March 4, 2021 - Regular Meeting

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan Goal 4:  Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Office of Counseling and College and Career Readiness recommends that the School Board award a contract for the Pathway to Baccalaureate Program to Northern Virginia Community College.

 

The Program uses a three-step, student-centered approach to increase access to higher education for underrepresented students. Step one is to prepare students for a successful transition to college by providing one-on-one meetings with a Pathway counselor to develop individualized transition plans. Step two is to support students while in college through continued one-on-one counseling, academic advising, and academic and leadership skill building. Step three includes customized degree planning, assistance with the transfer process, and continued access to program supports at George Mason University.

 

This contract provides four NVCC counselors to FCPS for the purpose of implementing the Pathway to the Baccalaureate program at 16 participating FCPS high schools.

 

Policy 5011.4 requires that the School Board approve any contract or contract amendment between the School Board and another government entity that exceeds $250,000.

 

The Pathway to the Baccalaureate is a multi-year, regional program supported by NVCC, George Mason University, and other participating local school divisions. The 2020-21 school year will be the 14th year of our partnership and participation in the Pathway program. Since 2005, this program has increased from four FCPS high schools with approximately 265 students to 16 high schools and approximately 1,300 students.

 

FCPS expenditures for this contract will not exceed $300,000 for the 2020-2021 school year. The contract includes a provision to adjust the funding if positions are not fully staffed during the term of the agreement.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Pathway to the Baccalaureate Program Agreement with Northern Virginia Community College, effective July 1, 2020 through June 30, 2025, and authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.
