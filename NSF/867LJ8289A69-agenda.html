        Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation

    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1050. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    4.01 Naming of School - Name the new elementary school to be opened in September 2006 at the west Fairfax site “Eagle View Elementary School” (Cluster VII; presented as new business 3/23/06)
    Meeting
    Apr 6, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 17
    Category
    4. Action Items - 8:15 p.m.
    Type
    Action

    Staff Contact: Rebecca Pearson, Director, Cluster VII

    SUBJECT: Naming of School

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board name the new elementary school to be opened in September 2006 at the west Fairfax site “Eagle View Elementary School.”

    EXPLANATION:

    On March 15, 2006, a meeting was held at Liberty Middle School for the purpose of naming the new elementary school at the West Fairfax site. In accordance with Regulation 8170.3, public notice of the meeting included community newspaper articles, information on the FCPS web site, and letters sent to parents in the affected communities. Twelve community members attended the meeting.

    The parents, students, staff members, and community members who attended the meeting considered a total of 10 names for the new school. By consensus, the list was narrowed to 3 recommended names. After presentations, questions, and audience discussion, the participants voted for their top choice using a weighted point system (first choice = 3 points, second choice = 2 points, and third choice = 1 point). The name Eagle View Elementary School received the most support.

    Action

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

    SUBJECT: Award of Contract - Transportation Consultant Services

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board award a firm, fixed-price contract for transportation consultant services to Management Partnership Services, Inc., in an amount not to exceed $150,000; and to authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designees, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.

    EXPLANATION:
      In 2005, the Board authorized Management Partnership Services, Inc., (MPS) to conduct a Phase 1 transportation study, and the results of that study were presented to the School Board in the fall of 2005. The Board desired to further explore the cost of options that might enable the Board to adjust bell schedules so that high schools could start later in the morning, and therefore discussed the possibility of a Phase 2 study.
        At the February 9, 2006, regular Board meeting, a motion was presented to approve a Phase 2 contract with MPS for consultant services for K-12 pupil transportation services in the amount of $45,600. After some discussion, an amendment in the form of a substitute motion was made to broaden the proposed scope to include a broader set of transportation-related considerations in the Phase 2 study. A motion to postpone was then approved to defer consideration of the contract with MPS until the March 9 Board meeting. At the March 9, 2006, regular Board meeting, the Board further postponed consideration until the April 6 Board meeting to consider the scope of work at a work session. The issue was then discussed at the April 3 Facilities & Operations work session, and the consensus of Board members was to consider a revised substitute motion that had been presented at the work session. The details of that revised substitute motion are included in this agenda item.

        Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

        SUBJECT: Transfer of Certain School Properties Not Currently Used for Instruction In Return for Increased CIP Funding from the County

        RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board consider the advisability of transferring to the Board of Supervisors certain real properties not currently used for instruction, in return for an annual increase of $25 million in capital bond funding for each of the next six years beginning in FY 2007.

        EXPLANATION:
            The School Board has an urgent need to increase substantially the available funding for its Capital Improvement Program (CIP) in order to meet current and future instructional requirements. At present, and without the action recommended above, the annual bond funding available to Fairfax County Public Schools is projected to be $130 million.

            Recently, discussions between school and County officials resulted in a consensus that recognized the mutual benefits of the County increasing the school system’s CIP funding over a designated period of time, in return for the school system transferring certain unneeded school properties to the Board of Supervisors.

            At its regular meeting on February 23, 2006, the School Board agreed to pursue a formal process that would implement transfers of unneeded school properties to the Board of Supervisors, in return for the increased CIP funding for school projects. In order to ensure that this process is open to the public, the School Board has scheduled a public hearing to occur on April 17, 2006, for the purpose of obtaining public comment about the properties being considered for transfer. The public hearing will be advertised in a newspaper of general circulation in the County, and school staff has been directed to send notices to adjoining property owners.

            At the present time, there are 12 parcels of real property that are not being used for instructional purposes and that are being considered for possible transfer. The list of these properties is attached hereto as Attachment A.

            The terms of the prospective property transfer are outlined in Attachment B, attached hereto.

        Staff Contact: Alice Farling, Assistant Superintendent, Special Services

        SUBJECT: Student Responsibilities and Rights

        RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board approve proposed modifications to the student code of conduct and related procedures to be included in Regulation 2601.19P, Student Responsibilities and Rights, as reviewed and discussed by the Student Services Committee.
        EXPLANATION:

            Modifications were proposed to Regulation 2601.19P and the Student Responsibilities and Rights (SR&R) booklet to clarify and strengthen the student code of conduct with respect to expected behavior, consequences of inappropriate behavior, and disciplinary procedures.

            Parallel changes will also be made in Regulation 2610, Removal (Suspension, Expulsion, or Exclusion) of Students From School.

            Substantive changes in the regulation and/or document include the following:
        A. Parent Signature Sheet has been changed to include a student signature, to include or guardian, and to point out the addition of the Acceptable Use Policy for Student Network Access on page 37, and the Standards of Conduct for Pupils Riding School Buses on page 39.
          B. Reference changed shaded areas to bold print for better readability.
            C. Chapter II.A.2.m. Changed language from “possession of” to “students may possess”, added “including school buses”, and changed “possession” to “unauthorized”. (p. 14)
              D. Chapter II.A.3.b. Adds Coricidin, Dramamine, Nyquil, or their generic equivalents, and cough syrup to list of nonprescription drugs not authorized as medication under the current version of Regulation 2102. (p.15)

              E. Chapter II.A.5. Language has been rewritten for clarification of weapons violations and disciplinary action. (p.18)

              F. Chapter II.B. Adds language “and Parents or guardian of Victims of Certain Code Violations”. (p.20)

              G. Chapter II.B.1. Changes language to read, “in addition to taking appropriate disciplinary action, report to the police department incidents that may constitute violations of law” and adds “arson” as a violation that will be reported to police. (p.20)

              H. Chapter II.B.5. Adds language “the principal shall also immediately report to the parents or guardians of any minor student who is the victim or intended victim of such act listed in B.1. that may constitute a criminal offense”. (p.20)

              I. Chapter II.C.2.d. (4). Adds language, “the hearing will not be recorded verbatim by stenographic means, tape, or other means”. (p.23)

              J. Chapter II.C.6.b. Adds language stating the student is not entitled to receive continuing educational services during any period of suspension or expulsion. (p.27)

              K. Acceptable Use Policy for Student Network Access has been added. (p.37)

              L. Standards of Conduct for Pupils Riding School Buses has been added. (p.39)

              M. Quick Guide to Rules of Conduct and Disciplinary Consequences has been removed and will be printed in separate student booklet, Quick Guide, grades 4-12.
