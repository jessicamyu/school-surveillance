Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so online starting at 6 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

6.01 Sanitary Sewer Easement Vacation - Recommendation to vacate the easement at Westfield High School and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the deed on behalf of the School Board and waive the compensation requirement. (FTS; scheduled for action 1/21/10).
Meeting
Jan 7, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 11
Category
6. New Business - 8:55 p.m.
Type
Information




Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Category: January 7, 2010, Regular Meeting No. 11
Subject: Sanitary Sewer Easement Vacation – Westfield High School

School Board Action Required: Information

Related To: N/A

Key Points:
      The school principal has requested vacating a portion of an existing sanitary easement at the subject school in order to comply with County requirements related to the on-site improvements. The work involves constructing a concession stand with toilets and was authorized as a DC-407 work order. The proposed vacation will permit an existing section and new sanitary sewer extension to be maintained by FCPS, thereby relieving the County of that obligation. County Code requires a site plan for public utility installations. This approach will exempt the project from this requirement and will result in significant design cost savings. Both design and construction costs for the project are being paid for by school funds. This design measure has been practiced in previous projects and achieves project permitting goals while ensuring FCPS compliance to building codes.

Recommendation:
      That the School Board vacate the easement at Westfield High School and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the deed on behalf of the School Board and waive the compensation requirement.
