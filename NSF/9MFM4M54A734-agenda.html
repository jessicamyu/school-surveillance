RESOLUTION

HONORING

DEBORAH L. TYLER

 

           

     WHEREAS, Deborah L. Tyler has served Fairfax County Public Schools for 29 years with excellence and dedication, effectively and energetically representing the interests of students, staff, schools, and diverse communities in a variety of positions, including special education teacher at Bucknell Elementary School, special education and general education teacher, assistant principal and principal of Pine Spring Elementary School, principal of Eagle View Elementary School, Cluster 4 Director and Assistant Superintendent, and most recently, Region 3 Assistant Superintendent; and

 

     WHEREAS, as principal of Pine Spring Elementary School, Ms. Tyler’s support of the pursuit of academic excellence resulted in a 12-point school-wide achievement index gain, the largest of all Project Excel schools; during her tenure, Pine Spring Elementary School also earned the Distinguished Title I School award from the Virginia Department of Education and Ms. Tyler was awarded the the Milken Family Foundation National Educator award; and

 

     WHEREAS, in her position as Cluster 4 Assistant Superintendent, Ms. Tyler championed the Cluster 4 Literacy Leaders initiative to close the literacy gap in all Cluster 4 schools; she worked collaboratively with her colleagues and shared practices and techniques to expand Cluster 4 instructional programs and improve student achievement; and

 

     WHEREAS, Ms. Tyler, in her leadership role as the Region 3 Assistant Superintendent, fostered the tenets of professional learning communities and encouraged all Region 3 school and pyramid teams to collaborate, exchange ideas, and share strengths in order to create the best learning environment possible for all Region 3 students; and

 

     WHEREAS, Ms. Tyler’s compassion, enthusiasm, and unwavering commitment to improve the quality of public education in Fairfax County inspired her colleagues to develop initiatives to enhance student performance and achievement and to strive for excellence in the classroom;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Deborah L. Tyler for her inspirational leadership and outstanding service to Fairfax County Public Schools and for her unwavering dedication to students, teachers, and administrators.

Recommended Action
Approve the process for the approved budget main motion, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly (SB; presented as new business 6/11/15)

Contact:                   Pam Goddard, Executive Assistant and Clerk of the Board

 

Meeting Category:  June 25, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                  Process for the Approved Budget Agenda Item

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To:            Strategic Governance

 

Key Points:  

 

At the December 11, 2014, forum work session, the Board discussed the topic of defining the process used to establish the School Board’s main motion to adopt the approved budget during the May regular School Board meeting.  There was consensus to refer the issue to the Governance Committee, who would bring a recommendation to the full Board.

 

The Governance Committee discussed the topic on January 15, 2015, and reviewed two agenda options presented by staff that the Board used from FY 2012 to FY 2015 to adopt the approved budget at the second regular Board meeting in May.  The Committee agreed that the options would be presented to the Board for further discussion.

 

At the March 16, 2015, work session, the Board discussed the two options presented by the Governance Committee, and there was Board consensus to approve the following next steps:

  • Budget Chair and Vice Chair to craft a new omnibus motion after presentation of the Superintendent’s recommendation, receive majority support at the budget work session, and post an omnibus motion for action as the main budget motion. 

The recommendation is to include the following information in the Strategic Governance Manual as a new Appendix M, and add a note to page 38, #3.e., Annual Work Calendar and Planning Cycle (to refer to the new Appendix M).

 

Agenda Format as Presented for New Business:

At the first School Board meeting in May, the required revenue and expenditure adjustments will be listed first.  Then a placeholder will show the amount required to balance the budget.  Based on the discussion at the budget work session that is typically scheduled in late April or early May, the Superintendent’s recommendations for eliminating the placeholder will be presented. 

 

EXAMPLE:  FY 2016 Approved Budget adoption language for the main motion:

That the School Board adopt the FY 2016 Approved Budget as detailed in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget, modified by the adjustments included in this agenda item.

 

Agenda Format as Presented for Action:

For the second School Board meeting in May, the School Board will take action on the approved budget; the main motion language will be revised to include an omnibus motion.  The omnibus motion will be a substitute for the placeholder and will include adjustments to balance the budget that the School Board Budget Chair and Vice Chair have determined have consensus.  Any amendments would then be made to the main motion, which is the omnibus motion.

 

EXAMPLE:  FY 2016 Approved Budget adoption language for the main (omnibus) motion:

I move that the School Board adopt the FY 2016 Approved Budget, as detailed in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget, and as detailed in the agenda item to include Section I, Operating Fund Required Revenue Adjustments, with total reductions of $10.1 million, and Section II, Operating Fund Required Expenditure Adjustments items A through D, with total reductions of $2.5 million, and replacing Section II.E., Reduction Placeholder of $7.6 million, with the following adjustment:  reducing the 1.0 percent market scale adjustment included in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget to 0.62 percent, resulting in a savings of $7.6 million.

 

RECOMMENDATION:

 

That the School Board approve the process for the approved budget main motion, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

Key Points: 

 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the “individual Trustee.”  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.

Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 25, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:                   Award of Contract – Hazardous Materials Removal Services

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:              School Board Policy 5011.2 Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) seeks to establish a contract to provide hazardous materials removal services for substances such as asbestos, lead-based paint, and other such related hazardous materials in FCPS buildings as requirements become known.  Generally the task orders are valued at $5,000-$80,000 depending upon the project.  However, the aggregate dollar amount of services performed under the contract can be more than $250,000 annually.  Funding for the hazardous materials removal services contract is provided from the Major Maintenance Fund, as well as school and department Operating Funds.

 

Bids were opened on June 11, 2015.  The apparent low bidder, JJPS, Inc., requested and was allowed to withdraw its bid due to a clerical mistake in accordance with Section 2.2-4330 of the Code of Virginia and Section 10 of the Fairfax County Public Schools Instructions to Bidders for this contract.  Staff conducted a comprehensive review of the next low bidder, Goel Services, Inc., and recommends as the primary contract award.  The next low bidder, Waco, Inc., has performed satisfactorily on previous contracts with the School Board and staff recommends secondary contract award to Waco, Inc. The bids of Goel Services, Inc. and Waco, Inc. are within budget. A bid tabulation is listed below.

 

Bidders                        Total Bid Price*

 

Goel Services, Inc.

$124,651.40

Waco, Inc.

$148,575.00

Southern Insulation, Inc.

$199,909.00

BARCO Enterprises, Inc.

$240,050.00

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the hazardous materials removal services to the lowest responsive and responsible bidders Goel Services, Inc. and Waco, Inc. and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

 


* Bidders were tasked with submitting prices for a hypothetical scope of work for the purpose of comparison. The initial, apparent low bidder, JJPS, Inc. was permitted to withdraw its bid of $74,742.50 in accordance with Virginia law.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
