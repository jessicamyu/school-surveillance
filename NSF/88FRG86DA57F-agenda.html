6.         INSTRUCTIONAL PROGRAM:  The Superintendent shall maintain a program of instruction that offers challenging and relevant opportunities for all students to

             achieve at levels defined in the Board’s student achievement goals.  The Superintendent will:

 

A.    Ensure that instructional programs are based on a comprehensive and objective review of best practices research.

B.    Base instruction on district academic standards that meet or exceed state and/or nationally-recognized model standards.

C.   Effectively measure each student’s progress toward achieving or exceeding the standards.

D.   Ensure that the instructional program includes opportunities for students to develop talents and interests in specialized areas.

E.    Ensure that the instructional program accommodates the different learning styles of students and differentiates instruction to meet the needs of students of various backgrounds and abilities.

F.    Encourage new and innovative programs, carefully monitoring and evaluating the effectiveness of all such programs.

G.   Ensure that all instructional programs are regularly evaluated to assure their continuing effectiveness.  All evaluations should recommend whether a program should be continued, modified,

        or discontinued based on its effectiveness and cost.

H.   Ensure appropriate input from students, parents, teachers, administrators and instructional staff members as textbooks are reviewed and selected.

7.         LEARNING ENVIRONMENT:  The Superintendent shall establish and maintain a learning environment that is safe, healthful, nondiscriminatory, respectful, and conducive to effective learning. 

            The Superintendent will:

 

A.    Maintain a climate that is characterized by support and encouragement for high student achievement.

B.    Ensure that student behavior expectations are clearly defined, communicated, supported, and enforced.

C.   Ensure behaviors, actions, or attitudes by adults who have contact with students do not hinder the academic performance or the well-being of students.

Key Points:

 

At the December 6, 2010, work session, School Board member Sandy Evans requested that the Governance committee consider several revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual in Section 2: Operational Expectations, based on comments received from an ad hoc committee of the Fairfax County Council of PTAs and the Fairfax Education Coalition.

 

The Governance Committee met on February 9, March 24, and April 13, 2011, and reviewed and discussed the recommended revisions to the Operational Expectations with Ms. Evans.  The Governance Committee agreed to recommend to the full Board that the Strategic Governance Manual be revised as highlighted below: (Attached is the current version of both OEs.)

 

HUMAN RESOURCES: 

 

B.   Staff Treatment.  The Superintendent will:

1.   Encourage full use of talents and energies.

2.   Establish a culture of respect that treats all staff members in a fair and balanced manner.

3.   Establish an atmosphere of openness, civility and responsiveness that seeks broad-based staff participation in identifying organizational needs

          and means of implementing improvements; consider various viewpoints and reasoned dissent while achieving sound decision-making.

4.    Administer clear personnel rules and procedures for employees.

5.    Promote and maintain favorable working conditions for employees.

 

COMMUNITY RELATIONS: 

The Superintendent will:

 

B.    Assure the timely flow of accurate and meaningful information,.  Assure appropriate input, and ongoing, two-way, strategic, and meaningful dialog between the district and the stakeholders that builds understanding for Board decisions and support for district efforts.  to reinforce a collaborative relationship.

 

To read:   Assure the timely flow of accurate and meaningful information.  Assure appropriate input and ongoing, two-way, strategic, and meaningful dialog between the district and stakeholders to reinforce a collaborative relationship.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the proposed revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual in Section 2: Operational Expectations, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

Meeting Category:    May 16, 2011 – Work Session No. 116

 

Subject:                      FY 2012 Approved Budget    

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

On February 3, 2011, the School Board adopted the FY 2012 Advertised Budget which included the following expenditure highlights:  two percent market scale adjustment for employees; step increases for eligible employees; increases in salary lapse; retirement and health rate increases; contract length reductions; membership growth and demographic changes; online Standards of Learning (SOL) test requirements for elementary schools; high school needs-based staffing; and reductions related to one-time federal stimulus funding.

 

Included in FCPS’ FY 2012 Advertised Budget was a request for increased funding from the county.  The Board of Supervisors approved a level transfer from the county to the School Operating Fund resulting in a decrease of $48.8 million as compared to the request in the advertised budget.  As a result of the impacts of the county budget, state budget, and other updates, additional adjustments are required.  Details are provided on the attached agenda. 

 

Following are the highlights of the FY 2012 Approved Budget as presented:

 

·         One percent market scale adjustment for all employees

·         Step increases for eligible employees

·         Full-day kindergarten at all elementary schools

·         Additional support for students during the discipline process

·         Elimination of Advanced Placement and International Baccalaureate test fees

·         Virginia Retirement System reserve balance of $60.6 million to address future obligations

 

Recommended changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Fairfax County Public Schools’ FY 2012 Approved Budget as detailed in the agenda item.
