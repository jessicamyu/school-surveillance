SUBJECT: Proposed 2008-2009 Program/Attendance Area Adjustment

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:

          In July 2007, the School Board authorized and directed staff to undertake two boundary studies: 1) Springfield Estates/Forestdale Elementary Schools and, 2) the West County High School study which includes Chantilly, Herndon, Oakton, South Lakes, and Westfield High Schools. Also included in the study is the Madison High School attendance area island north of the Dulles Access Road and its feeder schools which impact Hughes and Thoreau Middle Schools and Sunrise Valley and Wolftrap Elementary Schools.

KEY POINTS:

          The Springfield Estates/Forestdale Elementary School Study implements the return of an attendance island (Springfield Crossing Apartments) that had been administratively assigned to Springfield Estates, to its base school Forestdale Elementary.

          The West County Boundary Study addresses programmatic issues related to low and high enrollment schools by adjusting enrollments between South Lakes High School, which is under-enrolled, and Chantilly and Westfield High Schools which have high enrollment.
RECOMMENDATION:

          1. That the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for Springfield Estates Elementary School and Forestdale Elementary School.

          2. That the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for South Lakes, Oakton, Chantilly, Madison, and Westfield High Schools.

          3. That the School Board approve a boundary adjustment for Thoreau and Hughes Middle Schools and Wolftrap and Sunrise Valley Elementary Schools.

BOUNDARY ADJUSTMENT RECOMMENDATIONS

In October 2002, the School Board adopted revised Policy 8130.5 governing the adjustment of special program center locations and school/program attendance areas. The policy requires that, under most circumstances, conditions necessitating program location or attendance area adjustments be evaluated jointly with the affected communities. Solutions emerging from the “Community Boundary Town Meeting” process are to be recommended to the School Board for public hearing and action. The recommendations contained in this agenda item reflect input from a series of community town meetings conducted to review adjustments to attendance areas of existing schools. Meetings were announced via press release, mail outs, “backpack” and “Keep in Touch” notices to parents, and web-site postings. Citizen comment from each meeting was captured in summaries posted to the FCPS web page after each meeting. Scenario details (maps and impact tables) were similarly posted.

SUBJECT: FY 2009 Advertised Budget

TYPE: Discussion


BACKGROUND:
      On January 10, 2008, the Superintendent presented the FY 2009 Proposed Budget to the School Board. On January 14, 2008, the Superintendent delivered the proposed budget presentation at a joint work session of the School Board and the County Board of Supervisors.

      The School Board (SB) held an informal work session on January 28, 2008, to clarify with the staff the implications of the Superintendents expenditure recommendations. The SB will hold a public hearing on February 6      th. This will be the second work session prior to the SB adopting its “advertised” budget on February 14th.
KEY POINTS:
      The Superintendent has proposed a budget for FY 2009 that contains four priorities: a 3.0 percent cost-of-living adjustment for employees, continued expansion of full-day kindergarten, replacing the student information system, and continued expansion of the Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools Program. After funding these priorities, significant budget reductions were necessary to address a deficit of nearly $100 million.

      The budget identifies expenditure reductions totaling $45.6 million and 521.3 positions, as presented in three categories: eliminate, reduce, and streamline. He proposes that the remaining $55 million gap be closed by a 3.5 percent increase in the transfer from the County, instead of the 0 percent guidance established by the Board of Supervisors.

      During this work session, School Board members will have an opportunity to ask additional questions or follow-up on responses received to previously submitted budget questions.

OPTIONS:

No decisions or a sense of the Board will be attempted at this work session.
