Subject:                    School Board Policy 1400, Charter Schools

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

1. The Virginia General Assembly revised the Charter School Law, effective July 1, 2010.

 

2. Charter schools are now required to submit their application to the Virginia Board of Education, and then to the local School Board (including Virginia Board of Education comments).

 

3. The local School Board must now publish the application procedure and provide public notice and comment.

 

4. Written reasons must be given for denial if the application is not approved.

 

5. FCPS revised policy now specifies broad parameters in compliance with legislation with detailed charter application process now in the regulation.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revised Policy 1400, Charter Schools.

Meeting Category:        December 6, 2010 – Work Session No. 53

 

Subject:                           Budget Tool Kit      

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                         

 

Key Points:

 

At the School Board work session on November 8, 2010, a proposal for a budget tool kit was discussed and accepted.  Attached is a draft of the contents for the proposed budget tool kit.  Tool kits are being provided to School Board members at the work session, and the preliminary items included in the tool kits are highlighted on the table of contents.  School Board members will receive assistance with updating the tool kit, for example obtaining copies of the latest budget question responses from the School Board Office throughout the budget process.  Following is the tool kit proposal shared with the School Board at the November work session.

 

BUDGET TOOL KIT PROPOSAL:

  • The budget office will prepare a PowerPoint presentation with talking points for School Board members to use to present to the public.  The FY 2012 budget presentation should be available shortly after the Superintendent presents his budget in January 2011.
  • Each slide will have a “fact sheet” for use by the presenter to augment the material on the slide.   
  • Additional reference materials will be included by the budget office.  For example, a copy of the County Executive's fiscal outlook presentation would be a good “tool” for the presenters to have, as it answers some general questions about the County’s assumptions on the budget.
  • As Board members do presentations, any unanswered questions about the PowerPoint presentation should be sent to the budget office.  The budget office will update the underlying “fact sheet” as necessary for the presenters.
  • Board members will be given a supply of “Stump the Board Member” cards.  When a question is asked that the Board member cannot answer (and which is outside the parameters of the PowerPoint presentation), the public will be asked to complete the card.  Those cards will be returned to the budget office, and answers will be provided and posted to a web site.  (These will be priority three questions.)
  • The budget staff will also provide at least two training sessions for School Board members on the PowerPoint presentation.  This will not only help Board members with the presentation, but it will also give staff a good indication of the type of information which should be on the fact sheet.
  • Coordination will occur between the budget chair and co-chair and the Public Engagement Committee.
  • Each School Board member will be expected to conduct a minimum number of presentations.
  • The Public Engagement Committee will prepare a list of organizations other than PTAs/PTOs that should be contacted about presentations.
  • A Board member may request that a budget staff member attend an event and assist with the presentation .
