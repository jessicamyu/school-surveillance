Key Points:

This work session will continue the discussion that began at the January 23, 2017, work session. In January, the School Board began the review of the recommendations of the Budget Task Force. The School Board provided guidance by indicating if planning should begin to ensure the least disruptive implementation for next school year. At this work session, the Board will continue its discussion of potential reductions and fees starting with items with a critical decision date of February.

Staff Contact:  Marty K. Smith, Chief of Staff

 

Other Staff Present:  Jeremy Shughart, director, TJHSST Admissions Office

 

Meeting Category:  February 13, 2016 – Work Session

 

Subject:  New TJHSST Admissions Test

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

 

Key Points:

 

In spring 2016, Pearson notified the TJHSST Admissions Office it would no longer be producing the TJHSST Admissions exam beyond the 2016-2017 admissions cycle.  The current Pearson exam is comprised of customized multiple choice exam of mathematics and verbal batteries.

 

During the fall an advisory workgroup was assembled to review possible options for replacement.  The workgroup reviewed and considered multiple assessments.  Staff reviewed committee recommendations.

 

Based on a review of a variety of assessments and committee recommendations, staff recommended to the Superintendent the use of two assessments to replace the current exam; the Quant-Q and the ACT Aspire Reading and Science batteries.  The Quant-Q will replace the Pearson mathematics battery, while the ACT Aspire Reading will replace the Pearson verbal battery. 

 

Additionally, the ACT Aspire Science battery was recommended for inclusion in the admissions exam.  The inclusion of a science battery will provide additional information for evaluators to consider in a holistic review of the application.
