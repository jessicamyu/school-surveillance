Key Points:

 

In order to begin the FY 2014 budget planning process, the fiscal forecast for the School Operating Fund will be presented.  The forecast includes five years of projections for all key financial areas that drive the budget development process, such as revenue assumptions and expenditure assumptions, including employee salaries and benefits, student membership, and other identified program needs.

Key Points:

 

At the June 7, 2012, regular School Board meeting, the Board unanimously approved the following motion:

 

That the School Board establish a special committee of the Board to perform a comprehensive review of the Fairfax County Public Schools’ Student Rights and Responsibilities (SR&R), and those manuals of other school districts, to recommend changes to the School Board by March 2013 that would simplify the language and substantially reduce the size of SR&R and ensure consistency with our community’s values, School Board policies, and Virginia laws.  This committee shall be composed of representatives of parents, students, school administrators, teachers, student specialists (e.g., counselors, social workers, psychologists, hearings officers or SROs), juvenile services specialists and/or such other community members as the School Board shall determine appropriate.

12 School Board member appointments (one representative by each School Board member from his or her district; one representative, regardless of district, by each at-large School Board member)

1 representative from the Fairfax City School Board

1 Elementary School Principal

Staff Contact:          Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

 

Meeting Category: September 10, 2012 - Work Session No. 22

 

Subject:                    Fairfax High School Boundary Study

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

In their June 4, 2012, annual report to the School Board, the Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) identified the current and projected overcrowding at Fairfax High School and its feeder, Lanier Middle School, as one of several FCPS facility priorities that should be addressed in the near future.  Both Fairfax High and Lanier Middle underwent recent renovations and capital improvements funded by City of Fairfax residents.  Building additions to solve the overcrowding are not options as the overcrowding is due to the numbers of County students attending these schools and it is not reasonable to expect Fairfax City residents to fund capital improvements that would benefit County students.  It should be further noted that approximately 65% of the student population for both schools are Fairfax County residents.

 

There is some existing and projected available capacity at contiguous high and middle schools that can be used to provide relief to Fairfax and Lanier.

 

It is clear that a boundary study is the only reasonable solution to solve the overcrowding at Fairfax and Lanier.

 

Key Points:

 

The table below provides the current program capacity at Fairfax and Lanier, the projected 2012 enrollment and the 2012 and 2017 projected capacity deficits.

The Fairfax High/Lanier Middle school boundary is contiguous to six other high schools which are contained within Clusters 3, 6, 7, and 8; the majority of Fairfax/Lanier students reside in the Springfield, Braddock, Providence, and Sully Magisterial Districts.  Experience has shown that for a study of regional magnitude at multiple levels, a deliberate and thoughtful process of community engagement is required to ensure communities understand and accept the legitimacy of the problem and the scope of the boundary study that must be undertaken to provide needed relief.

 

Staff has begun to meet with the various Cluster Assistant Superintendents to determine the best approach to inform their school communities about the overcrowding issues at Fairfax High and Lanier Middle, and the need for a boundary study to resolve the issues.  It is anticipated that both cluster and planning staffs will be reaching out to meet with principals and their school communities beginning in October 2012.  In addition, staff analysis will be undertaken this fall to identify potential receiving and sending schools based on school capacities, projections, and actual September 30, 2012, enrollment.  This process will culminate in staff recommendations to the School Board on the scope of boundary study.  Informational materials and web pages will also need to be developed regarding the overcrowding issues in the coming weeks.

 

With School Board approval of a scope of study in the January/February 2013 time frame, staff anticipates initiating a boundary study in spring 2013 with Board action taking place in May or June.  Boundary changes which are approved by the School Board as a result of the study could be implemented with the beginning of the 2014-15 school year. 

 

It is noted that the Fairfax City School Board has expressed a desire that relief be provided to these schools starting in the fall of 2013 as Fairfax High School is already struggling to meet the facility needs of its increasing enrollment.  The only means by which this could occur would be to conduct the boundary study in the fall of 2012 as approval of spring studies occurs after the deadlines for course registrations at the secondary school level.  In order to conduct a fall 2012 study, the School Board would have to agree to a scope of study with little to no community engagement or dialog prior to that decision.  This accelerated process would jeopardize the success of the boundary study and the acceptance of the results by the affected communities.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the timeline shown below to address overcrowding at Fairfax High School and Lanier Middle School:

  • Fall 2012:            Community engagement on problem and possible scope of boundary study
  • December 2012:   School Board decision on boundary study scope
  • Spring 2013:        Boundary study
  • June 2013:           School Board decision on boundaries
  • September 2014:   Implementation of boundary study

Meeting Category:  September 10, 2012 - Work Session No. 22

 

Subject:                     Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) Quarterly Report (Strategic Facilities Plan)

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

The Facilities Planning Advisory Council reports on its activities to the School Board on a quarterly basis as required by its charter.  For this quarterly report, FPAC submits their Fairfax County Public Schools Strategic Facilities Plan, the draft of which was presented and discussed as part of FPAC’s June 2012 annual report.

 

Key Points:

 

The School Board has charged FPAC to develop and update annually a long-term strategic plan for FCPS facility needs for presentation to and approval by the School Board.  The attached Strategic Plan is the culmination of an effort that began over a year ago which included community outreach ‘Listening Tours’ undertaken for each school cluster.  The Strategic Plan provides a focus on the key areas of communication, capacity and space use, operations and maintenance, and capital improvement.  As the Strategic Plan continues to develop and evolve, an additional focus area on transportation is envisioned.  Each focus area will include:

  • Goals – broad outcomes
  • Strategies – approaches to be taken
  • Objectives – measurable steps to achieve the strategies

This first iteration of the Strategic Facilities Plan lists the goals.  It is anticipated that future versions will contain strategies to define potential means for achieving the goals and objectives for measuring progress.  While the goals are likely to remain steady over time, the strategies will be updated periodically as conditions change in Fairfax County or as new concepts are developed.  The objectives will be defined and redefined with greater frequency as FCPS staff and the School Board address specific issues at particular points in time.  The Strategic Facilities Plan is intended to be a living, dynamic document to be continually managed, monitored, and validated by the community at-large.  Appendix A in the Plan provides example strategies and objectives.
