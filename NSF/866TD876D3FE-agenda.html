4.02 Clifton Elementary School Facilities Issues

Recommended Action
Approve closing Clifton Elementary School, with the specific closure date to be determined by the Board after the conclusion of the Southwestern boundary study (FTS; presented as new business 6/10/10, work sessions held 6/14/10 and 6/29/10; public hearing held 6/28/10)

Staff Contact:            Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Category:     July 8, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 1

Subject:                     Clifton Elementary School Facilities Issues

School Board Action Required:  Decision

Key Points:

Based upon the independent and objective evaluation of building conditions conducted in 1999, Clifton Elementary School was placed in the renovation queue that was approved by the School Board.  The Board has used this queue to determine the sequence of renovations to be included in the Board’s adopted Capital Improvement Program.  Funding for planning this renovation was included in the 2005 and 2007 referenda.  Planning has not begun, however, because of the unique and challenging aspects of the Clifton site.


The decision to close or renovate Clifton ES School should be based on the issues of the reliability of the water supply, declining enrollment and renovation costs and not by the potential impact on future boundary studies.


The School Board has two clear options available:

  1. Close Clifton ES
  2. Renovate Clifton ES

After updating its own feasibility study and after considering the work of the Southwestern region ad hoc study committee, staff is recommending that Clifton Elementary School be closed.


The specific reasons for staff’s recommendation are as follows:

  • The unique topographical and well water challenges of the Clifton Elementary School site will result in higher than normal renovation costs. 
  • Because the school will continue to rely upon well water, there is some level of risk that, even after a renovation, there could continue to be problems with water quantity and quality.
  • Enrollment is declining at Clifton Elementary School with the 2015 projection being 298 students.
  • Due to the higher than normal construction costs, and the low student enrollment, the cost per student to renovate Clifton will be much greater than other recent renovations.  We estimate the Clifton cost per student to be $35,287 (renovated for a capacity of 312 students).  The currently underway renovation of Mount Eagle, a similar sized school, has a renovation cost per student of only $24,740.
  • FCPS has a history of closing schools when enrollments become too low or when site and building challenges do not allow renovations to bring a building up to current educational specifications or the costs to so become prohibitive.  More recently, the School Board decided to move Graham Road ES from its current location to the Devonshire site for reasons similar to Clifton - site constraints and higher costs associated with renovation of the existing school.  In prior years, Chapel Square, Wilton Woods, Pine Ridge, Masonville, Walnut Hill and Virginia Hills were all schools that were closed by action of the School Board.   
  • The capital program annual spending limit of $155 million, established by the Board of Supervisors, is inadequate to meet the capital needs of the school system as shown in the School Board’s annual capital improvement program.  The lack of adequate funding means that many schools are not being renovated in a timely manner and too many schools have trailers to address overcrowding.  In order to address needed renovations and overcrowding, the School Board must use the monies that are available in the most effective manner possible. 

There are consequences for each of the two options available to the School Board, including the following more substantive concerns.



Close Clifton Elementary School

Closing this school will mean that funds identified in the Capital Improvement Program for its renovation can be reassigned by the School Board to other capital projects such as renovations, capacity enhancements (new schools, building additions, modulars), or special programs such as all day kindergarten.

 

Closing the school will add to the elementary school capacity deficit in that region.  Some of this deficit can be solved by moving programs and/or changing existing boundaries but solving the deficit will require capacity enhancement projects.  Staff estimates that these capital projects would have to add about 43 classrooms depending upon the results of the boundary process.

 

Options for the use of the site will be pursued.

 

Renovate Clifton Elementary School

If the School Board decides to renovate Clifton Elementary School, staff continues to recommend that a complete renovation be conducted.  The estimated cost per student referenced in this agenda item is based upon a full renovation.  Staff notes that the School Board has never reduced the scope of prior renovations as a means to reduce costs. Depending on the boundary study, staff estimates that about 30 classrooms would have to be constructed to help address overcrowding in this region of the county.  Clifton would be part of the boundary study.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve closing Clifton Elementary School, with the specific closure date to be determined by the Board after the conclusion of the Southwestern boundary study.


Proposed Amendment:

 

I move to postpone the School Board’s consideration of closing and renovating Clifton Elementary School until no later than October 2013. (Hone)

Staff Contact:           Cluster III Assistant Superintendent


Meeting Category:    July 8, 2010 - Regular Meeting No.1


Subject:                    Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology


School Board Action Required: Decision


Related to:                N/A


Key Points: 


In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.  The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:


  • Additional state funding for the regional school.  The estimate for FY 2011 is $2,098,140.  The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $2,664,167.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor's School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

Options:  The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.


Recommendaton:


That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2010-2011.

Meeting Category:     July 8, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 1


Subject:                     FY 2010 Final Budget Review     


School Board Action Required:  Information                                                        


Key Points:


All of the FY 2010 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the funds under the control of the School Board.  All comparisons are against the FY 2010 estimate. 


In the School Operating Fund, the unaudited FY 2010 ending balance, after accounting for the FY 2011 budgeted beginning balance, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, is $33.9 million.


FY 2010 actual revenue was $9.1 million lower than the estimate.  A decrease of $13.3 million in state aid is due to the state accelerating the spending of federal State Fiscal Stabilization Funds (SFSF) in FY 2010 combined with minor adjustments in state aid due to actual membership. The decision to replace $13.7 million in state basic aid with federal stimulus funds (SFSF) was made too late in the fiscal year for FCPS to document and claim the accelerated portion of reimbursements.  As a result, the SFSF funding not utilized in FY 2010 will be reappropriated and included with federal funds in FY 2011.  Since the FY 2011 budget is balanced, this increase in federal revenue in FY 2011 will allow FCPS to set aside funding of $13.7 million for FY 2012. 


A net decrease of $3.6 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $5.9 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and appropriated again in FY 2011.  This amount is offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of an increase of $2.0 million in Impact Aid and E-rate payments.  The decreases in state aid and federal revenue are offset by increases of $1.8 million in sales tax and $6.0 million in tuition, fees, and other revenue.


After accounting for other commitments, FY 2010 actuaI expenditures were $43.0 million lower than the estimate.  In anticipation of budget reductions in the FY 2011 Approved Budget, schools and departments conserved resources where possible during FY 2010.  Salaries were $18.3 million less than budgeted due to vacancies being held open to offset future budget reductions combined with the reduction in FY 2010 budgeted lapse.  Related benefit savings combined with additional holiday savings in VRS related accounts totaled $11.9 million.  Additional savings resulted primarily from utilities ($2.0 million), vehicle fuel and bus maintenance ($4.1 million), and travel restrictions for staff development ($2.0 million).


Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.


Recommendation:


That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item, including setting aside the following for FY 2012 beginning balance:  the FY 2010 ending balance from the school operating fund of $33.9 million, an additional $1.1 million to be identified during FY 2011, and the FY 2011 $13.7 million impact of the reappropriation of SFSF.
