Problem Statement:  School Board request to review the current FCPS practices to prevent and respond to sexual harassment and sexual assault.

 

Outcome:  The School Board will have a better understanding of current practices in FCPS related to sexual harassment/assault curriculum, training and prevention efforts.  The review includes information about student curriculum and prevention efforts, and staff training.  The Board will have an opportunity to discuss potential future actions or changes to current efforts.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):  Sexual harassment and sexual assault are addressed through a continuum of prevention and intervention strategies through progressive, developmentally appropriate lessons. Development of the curricular materials was completed after review of national best practices and lessons were approved by the Office for Civil Rights. These lessons are provided annually. Responses to the questions on the Youth Survey are used to review and improve lessons annually.

 

Professional development is provided to all staff regarding both student and adult behavior. Administrators are trained annually to investigate and respond to allegations of bullying and harassment to ensure practices are implemented with fidelity.  A data system was developed to document allegations and school responses.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

FCPS implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, the School Board and Leadership Team have collaborated to prioritize the plan’s Desired Outcomes within each of the four goal areas to allow for greater focus and alignment over time.

Refined language for some Desired Outcomes and Actions are being recommended. Metrics have been refined to more accurately reflect the current work of the Division, and Performance Targets have been proposed to gauge progress.

 

Recommendation:

The School Board accept the recommended changes to the Strategic Plan.

Subject:  Equity Resources and Initial Plan

 

Staff Contact:  Dr. Francisco Durán, chief academic and equity officer

 

Meeting Category:  March 12, 2018 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Ignite Links: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success; Goal 2: Caring Culture; Goal 3: Premier Workforce; Goal 4: Resource Stewardship

 

Outcome:

 

To raise student achievement, eliminate achievement gaps, and support equitable opportunities for all students.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

At its regular business meeting on December 14, 2017, the School Board voted to allocate one-time funding of $0.25 million to the Office of the Chief Academic and Equity Officer (CAEO) to support equity-related work.  The Superintendent was directed to update the Board at a future work session on how the $0.25 million in new funding will be used to improve equity within FCPS.

 

The Equity Resources and Initial Plan (ERIP) for discussion describes how the CAEO departments and offices (Instructional Services, Special Services, Professional Learning and Family Engagement, Student Testing, and TJHSST Admissions) will continue current work while focusing more strategically on ways to provide equitable access and opportunities for all FCPS students.

 

The ERIP is designed to achieve the strategic goals of Ignite and support the FCPS focus on equity and involves the following three (3) actions:

  1. Create seven (7) new positions with a sole focus on equity and provide support for a school-based equity liaison at each school.
  2. Establish an Equity Profile which will publish data that can be disaggregated and analyzed.
  3. Review and assess existing curriculum, initiatives, programs, and policies to ensure equitable access and opportunities are available to all students.

Overall, the ERIP helps to ensure that equity is embedded in all work throughout FCPS and not assigned to a single office or department.

Subject: Office of School Support Structure 2018-2019

 

 

Staff Contact: Steve Lockard, deputy superintendent

                       Mark Greenfelder, executive director, Office of School Support

 

 

Meeting Category: March 12, 2018 Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success, Goal 4: Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement:

 

New SOA calculations will impact more schools in 2018-19. FCPS will have more schools identified with achievement gaps in Language Arts, Math, and Science with a VDOE expectation of FCPS supporting those schools.

 

Rationale:

 

This change supports the Superintendents’ goals for Excellence, Equity and Effectiveness and School Board goals for student success and resource stewardship.

Excellence – To be the premier school system, FCPS must see continuous improvement. Student outcome results have been flat the last 3 years except for Project Momentum (PM) schools.

Equity – To support every child by name and by need, we need to support every school by name and by need. New SOA regulations requires support for all schools, not just a few.

Effectiveness – Current approach to support schools through only staffing is not enough. The winning formula through 4 years of PM is a combination of needs based staffing and support.

Outcome:

 

More targeted support to schools through Regional Support Teams from OSS. This will be a collaborative effort of OSS, IS, OPLFE, DSS and Regions and provide a more integrated approach to closing achievement gaps and ensuring schools meet VDOE accreditation benchmarks.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Office of School Support will continue to provide “Intensive” and “Targeted” support to schools in Project Momentum to ensure that all schools meet or exceed VDOE benchmarks. Additionally, the redesigned structure will increase the capacity of the Office of School Support to more fully meet the desired vision and mission to support all schools across FCPS through Regional deployment. Increased capacity, that is cost neutral to the Division, will allow for expanded reach to all schools, across all five Regions, and begin the challenging work of narrowing the equity and achievement gaps that have long existed in FCPS. This will ensure support for those schools identified by the new SOA accreditation standards as having achievement gaps.

Office of School Support’s Five Key Drivers for School Improvement and Closing Achievement Gaps:

  1. Collaborative teams that meet and engage in the learning cycle on a regular basis and with fidelity
  2. High Quality Tier 1 instruction aligned to a learning target that is connected to a state standard, big idea, and essential understanding
  3. A coherent lesson plan aligned to the planning and pacing guide that is collaboratively created as a team
  4. Short formative assessments that are aligned to state standards given on a regular basis that are reflected and acted upon by the team
  5. Structured Response to Intervention protocols that meet the needs of all students

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board support the recommendation to align elementary, middle, and high school office support staffing by changing the allocation for elementary support staffing to the same model used for middle and high schools. The change would be to calculate elementary office staffing by the number of students rather than the current methodology of number of teachers. The savings generated would be approximately $2.1 million from needs-based staffing funds. In addition, it is recommended to raise the entry point for needs-based staffing to take effect from 20% to 25% poverty at a savings of $1.1 million.
