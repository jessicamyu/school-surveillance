Veterans Day - November 11, 2010

 

Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.

 

 

National Adoption Awareness Month – November

 

There are many ways to form a family, and many families in Fairfax County have chosen adoption as the way to form their own family. Fairfax County Public Schools recognizes the importance of adoption, and the School Board, leadership team, teachers, principals, and staff fully support Adoption Awareness Month.

 

The purpose of National Adoption Month is to raise awareness about the over 115,000 children in foster care nationwide waiting for permanent families. Recruitment efforts are highlighted throughout the National Adoption Month web site to encourage America's families to "answer the call" to ensure the safety, permanency, and well-being of our children.

School Psychology Awareness Week - November 8-12, 2010

 

The theme of this year’s School Psychology Awareness Week, "Today is a good day to… SHINE!" focuses on shining a light on the positive work school psychologists do to promote students’ academic and personal success.

 

School psychologists work with students in early childhood and elementary and secondary schools. They collaborate with teachers, parents, and school personnel to create safe, healthy, and supportive learning environments for all students. School psychologists address students' learning and behavioral problems, suggest improvements to classroom management strategies or parenting techniques, and evaluate students with disabilities and advanced academic students to help determine the best way to educate them.

They improve teaching, learning, and socialization strategies based on their understanding of the psychology of learning environments. They also may evaluate the effectiveness of academic programs, prevention programs, behavior management procedures, and other services provided in the school setting.

Recommended Action
Adopt the newly revised 2010 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the two conditions detailed in the agenda item (FS; presented as new business 10/21/10)

Staff Contact:             Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services    

 

Other Staff Present:   Ronald A. Hull, acting director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:      November 4, 2010 - Regular Meeting No. 7

 

Subject:                      Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:     Decision

 

Key Points:

 

   

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 13, 2010, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, as a result of legislative changes enacted by the 2010 General Assembly actions.  Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

During the 2010 session of the General Assembly, eleven (11) bills were approved relating to governmental procurement and/or contracts. Three of these bills modified the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA) in a manner which affects the operations of Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS).  The remaining bills affected state agencies only or were not otherwise applicable to FCPS.    

 

2010 Changes to the VPPA relevant to FCPS

  • House Bill 789, Code of Virginia §2.2-4303, increases the maximum amount from $1 million to $1.5 million for which the use of competitive negotiations for construction contracts is permissable.
  • Senate Bill 567, Code of Virginia §2.2-4305, increases the amount from $30,000 to $50,000 for the required use of competitive bidding or competitive negotiations for state-aid construction projects.
  • House Bill 831, Code of Virginia §2.2-4311.2, adds requirements to include in every contract over $50,000 a provision that the contractor must be authorized to transact business in Virginia as a domestic or foreign business entity as required by the State Corporation Commission.

 

County’s Administrative Changes applicable to FCPS

 

In addition to revisions based on changes to the VPPA, which became effective as of July 1, 2010, school system staff recommends one (1) administrative change to the Purchasing Resolution:

  • Add Personal Conflicts of Interest section to Article 5, Ethics in County Contracting.

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009 Purchasing Resolution, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2010 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

1.  The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

2.  The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2010 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the two conditions detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:   November 4, 2010 – Regular Meeting No. 7

 

Subject:                     Review of Departmental Operational Expectations

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

At the July 23-24, 2010, retreat, the School Board accepted the new Operational Expectations monitoring report format. The Board agreed to a review of all current departmental Operational Expectations to clarify the Board’s values and to ensure alignment with the new monitoring report format.

 

The Governance Committee reviewed all the departmental Operational Expectations on October 13, and recommended several revisions, including the following:

  • Adding a Global Operational Expectation
  • Revising language for Human Resources, Professional Learning and Training, Budget and Financial Management, Facilities and Transportation Services, Community Relations, and Relationship with the Board
  • Revising language for the Instructional Program and Treatment of Students Operational Expectations, resulting in the creation of two separate Operational Expectations—Instructional Program and Learning Environment
  • Incorporating Accountability Operational Expectations into the new Instructional Program Operational Expectations
  • Continuing the audit procedures currently included in the Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations as a function of the Board’s Audit Committee (which is comprised of two School Board members, the Superintendent, and staff) and continuing to provide public access to the information
  • Keeping the Technology Operational Expectations unchanged

The Governance Committee’s recommended revisions were discussed by the entire Board at the October 18 work sessions, and some additional revisions were suggested to Budget and Financial Management and Facilities and Transportation Services Operational Expectations.   All the recommended revisions have been incorporated into the Operational Expectations being recommended for final approval by the Board on November 15.  The Strategic Governance Manual will be updated after Board approval.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve revisions to the Operational Expectations, as detailed in the agenda item.
