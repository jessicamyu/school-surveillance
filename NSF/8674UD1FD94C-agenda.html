126.05 Discussion - School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) 2009-2010 Annual Report to School Board
Meeting
Jun 3, 2010 - Work Session No. 126
Category
126. Special Services, Human Resources, and Instruction - 7 p.m.
Type
Discussion

Staff Contact: Kim Dockery, assistant superintendent, Special Services

Meeting Category: June 3, 2010 - Work Session No. 126

Subject: School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) 2009-2010 Annual Report

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Related To: Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics and Goal 2. Essential Life Skills

Background:

      The School Health Advisory Committee is mandated by the Commonwealth of Virginia to assist with the development of health policy in the school division and the evaluation of the status of school health, health education, the school environment, and health services.

Key Points:
    • SHAC recommends adopting a tobacco-free school system policy which would prohibit the use of tobacco products on any FCPS property at all times (24/7) by all students, staff members and visitors in all FCPS facilities.
    • SHAC recommends the insertion of specific wording to include Gay, Lesbian, Bisexual, Transgender and Questioning (GLBTQ) students into the appropriate non-discrimination policies and other relevant regulations in the school system.
    • SHAC supports the implementation of the “9-5-2-1-0 Zip Code for Your Health” program in all schools. SHAC also recommends that information about this program be shared with the Department of Communication and Community Outreach (DCCO).
    • SHAC continues to advocate for and supports the efforts of FCPS to provide nutritious meals, physical education programs, recess, and after-school programs. These efforts help to improve the health of students and help to prevent childhood obesity.
    • SHAC supports the continual need for a full complement of School Health Aides (SHA) and school Public Health Nurses (PHN) in the school system because of the key role school health services plays in promoting the physical, mental and social health of all students.


Recommendation:
      That the School Board accept the School Health Advisory Committee 2009-2010 Annual Report.

Other Staff Present: Sherry Brathwaite, director, Office of Equity and Compliance

Subject: Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC) Annual Report

School Board Action Required: Information

Related to: Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

Key Points:
      The School Board asked the HRAC to work with the Office of Equity and Compliance to enhance the performance of school-based human relations committees, and continue to explore methods for obtaining feedback from the economically challenged and socially marginalized. In response to the School Board’s charge, the committee formed a subcommittee focused on recommendations to enhance school-based human relations committees. The HRAC committee also invited speakers from the community to share information about their work, and speakers representing religious groups to present human relations issues and religious beliefs.
Recommendation:
      That the School Board accept the Human Relations Advisory Committee 2009-2010 annual report as presented.
