National Inclusive Schools Week
December 4 - 8, 2017

 

   WHEREAS, since its inception in 2001, National Inclusive Schools Week has celebrated the progress that schools have made in providing a supportive and quality education to an increasingly diverse student population, including students who are marginalized due to disability, gender, socio-economic status, cultural heritage, language preference, and other factors; and

 

    WHEREAS, Inclusive Schools Week is an annual event sponsored by the Inclusive Schools Network (ISN) and is held during the first full week of December; and

 

    WHEREAS, this year’s theme is “Stand Up Sit Tall for Inclusion", because inclusive practices enable us to model for the world that our strength comes from our diversity; and

 

    WHEREAS, with these examples available to all, we can learn more ways to Stand Up, Sit Tall for Inclusion because inclusion results in improved outcomes for all students; and

 

     WHEREAS, the week also provides an important opportunity for educators, students, and parents to discuss what else needs to be done in order to ensure that their schools continue to improve their ability to successfully educate all children.

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, the Fairfax County School Board encourages the entire FCPS community to Stand Up, Sit Tall For Inclusion!

3.05 Belvedere Elementary National Title I Distinguished School Recognition
Meeting
Nov 20, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Regular Business Meeting - 7 p.m.

Tonight, we have the honor and pleasure to recognize Belvedere Elementary School as a 2017-2018 National Title I Distinguished School. This recognition program is sponsored by the National Association of State Title I directors for the purpose of acknowledging high-performing Title I schools and their outstanding achievements in education.

 

It is not an easy distinction to earn! Only TWO schools from EACH state are selected annually as a National Title I Distinguished School. I’m thrilled that Belvedere is one of those two and know that it is very well-deserved.

 

To be considered, a school must maintain full state accreditation, have a poverty rate of at least 35% for the two most recent school years, and must demonstrate high academic achievement.

 

I am pleased to congratulate Principal Cecilia Vanderhye and her staff on this outstanding accomplishment. It is truly a team effort! Through dedication, perseverance, and hard work, the staff helps every student to be successful. Belvedere is Fairfax County’s first elementary school to offer the prestigious International Baccalaureate Primary Years Program, which gives all of its students the opportunity to have the IB experience from Kindergarten through 12th grade.  The IB curriculum challenges students to become internationally-minded thinkers, inquirers, and problem-solvers. 

 

The IB primary Years Program is only one of a variety of challenging opportunities at Belvedere.  The faculty and staff work together as a collaborative team to plan and execute high quality instruction for all students.  With encouragement, each student is empowered to build on his or her unique knowledge in a supportive school culture.

 

It is easy to understand why Belvedere Elementary was selected for this distinction. Congratulations!

Staff Contact:  Francisco Durán, Chief Academic and Equity Officer

 

Meeting Category:  November 20, 2017 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  One Fairfax Policy

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

In July 2016, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board jointly adopted the One Fairfax Resolution that directed the development of policies and strategic actions to advance opportunity and achieve racial and social equity in Fairfax County.

 

In June 2017, a draft One Fairfax Policy was completed and presented at a Joint Boards work session on June 13, 2017.  The Successful Children and Youth Policy Team (SCYPT) Executive Committee reviewed the draft policy and individual Board members, paired by district, were briefed on the policy July-September 2017. 

 

The proposed One Fairfax Policy defines expectations for consideration of racial and social equity, and in particular, meaningful community involvement when planning, developing, and implementing policies, practices, and initiatives.  It provides a framework to advance equity in alignment with the stated visions and priorities of Fairfax County.  The policy is intended to inform all other policies and applies to all publicly delivered services in Fairfax County Government and Fairfax County Public Schools.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the One Fairfax Policy, as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:  Michael Molloy, director, Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:  November 20, 2017 Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Fairfax County School Board 2018 State and Federal Legislative Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Action

 

Related To:  Legislation

 

Key Points:

 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to Fairfax County Public Schools. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The 2018 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions and (where applicable) formatting changes from the Board’s 2017 legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate and policy debates, and to clarify, simplify and streamline existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board will discuss the draft 2018 State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for November 13, 2017. Text of the 2018 State and Federal Legislative Program and Board Priorities will be available after the incorporation of any Board-recommended changes to the draft from the work session.

 

The posted 2018 State and Federal Legislative Program document incorporates clarifying changes and edits as discussed at the Board’s November 13th work session. Changes were made as follows:

 

  • FCSB/BOS Joint Legislative Priority Positions – order of support position bullets reordered to match BOS copy
  • Q. School Construction – clarified intended scope of proffer position under Q.3 (as discussed at CIP Impact of New Development Work Session, Nov. 13)
  • S. Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning – clarified intended scope of new position language regarding English learners and entry into GCI cohorts under S.14 and S.15
  • FED. Federal Education Funding and Policy – clarified intended scope of E-Rate funding position under FED.15

 

All changes are also documented on the attached Change Summary table.

 

 

Recommendation:

Adopt the Fairfax County School Board’s 2018 State and Federal Legislative Program.

 

Proposed Board member amendments:

 

1. The Fairfax County School Board supports providing local school divisions with greater flexibility in determining the appropriate allocation of instructional time in elementary schools across specific subject areas. Such flexibility would allow school divisions the opportunity to restructure the educational environment based on current research and to provide adequate time for recess. (McElveen)

 

2. Proposed New Language to Replace Section X1-X5 (McLaughlin):

X.1) The Fairfax County School Board (FCSB) supports significantly increased state resources devoted to programs and staffing (including school psychologists, school nurses, and school social workers) to help school divisions serve the increasing school health and wellness needs of our student population; and to assist with responses to issues such as childhood obesity, child and adolescent sleep, student mental health, and suicide prevention.


X.2) The FCSB supports maintaining locally-based authority and flexibility to determine the school health model that most appropriately meets a division’s needs via local policies and staffing, including preservation of the role that local health departments play in the provision of school health services. Should the General Assembly choose to mandate particular health-related programs or staffinganyway, adequate and appropriate state resources must be allocated before local school divisions would be required to implement changes.


X.3) The FCSB supports increased state reimbursement for both the school lunch and school breakfast programs.

 

 

3. The FCSB supports a uniform set of rules and guidelines that would at least maintain current Medicaid funding and eligibility standards for our students and that would continue to allow school divisions to claim administrative and transportation expenses associated with Medicaid eligible services to students served under the federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act (IDEA). (Corbett Sanders)

 

4. The FCSB supports federal funding reauthorization of the Children’s Health Insurance Program (CHIP) which helps serve children’s health care needs in low income households.(Corbett Sanders)

Subject: Mathematics Basal Instructional Materials Approval

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Virginia Department of Education revised the Standards of Learning for Mathematics in 2016. The mathematics team in Instructional Services has been revising curriculum documents to align with these new standards and providing professional development for teachers and administrators about the revised SOLs which will be fully implemented in the 2018-2019 school year.

 

The contracts for mathematics instructional resources currently in use in FCPS expire during summer 2018. As a result, students and teachers would no longer have access to the digital student and teacher resources without some action. Therefore in January 2017, Instructional Services began the process for new basal instructional materials as defined in FCPS Regulation 3004--Adopted Basal Instructional Materials.

 

A timeline of the process for the mathematics basal resource review can be found in Attachment A. In June 2017, the school board approved the committee members to review and recommend basal instructional materials for mathematics. The Mathematics Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee convened in July 2017 to review resources for K-12 that were submitted by vendors in response to an RFP for consideration as basal instructional materials for mathematics. In accordance with the Code of Virginia, regulations of the Virginia Board of Education, and Fairfax County Public Schools regulations, the Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee in conjunction with the Selection Advisory Committee and the Technical Advisory Committee recommends the titles listed in Attachment B for adoption at the division level. Additionally, a summary of the committee rationales for the recommended materials and community input received are provided in attachments C and D.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the titles listed for kindergarten through grade 12 mathematics as recommended by the Mathematics Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee and the Selection Advisory Committee.

Meeting Category: November 20, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  McNair Elementary School - Authorization to Negotiate and Execute Proffer Statement

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Key Points:

 

The Office of Design & Construction has filed, on behalf of the School Board, Proffered Condition Amendment ("PCA") applications [PCA 87-C-060 and PCA 93-H-045] in order to amend the proffers previously approved for McNair Elementary School. 

 

The purpose of the PCA applications is to permit the construction of an additional elementary school building (with a gross floor area of 115,000 square feet, providing capacity for an additional 750 students) and related modifications to be sited on the existing McNair property as well as an adjacent 1.36 acre parcel, which was previously dedicated to the County for school and park use. Once the additional building is constructed, the school building area at the site will have been more than doubled, and the existing modular classrooms will then be removed. This new, combined site also would allow for a potential 15,000 square foot building addition in the future. 

 

In accordance with County practice, FCPS as applicant must submit a signed Proffer Statement (a draft of which is attached) thirty days prior to the public hearing held by the Board of Supervisors. Typically, such a Proffer Statement requires minor revisions as the PCA application is reviewed by County staff and by the Planning Commission in preparation for the Board of Supervisors’ public hearing. The final version of the Proffer Statement adopted by the Board of Supervisors will go into effect, superseding all prior drafts and previously approved proffers for this property. 

 

The hearing before the Planning Commission is currently scheduled for December 7, 2017, with a hearing before the Board of Supervisors scheduled for January 23, 2018.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all actions necessary and appropriate, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to pursue approval of Proffered Condition Amendment applications for the combined McNair Elementary School site, including negotiating, signing, and submitting the final Proffer Statement and any other documents on behalf of the School Board.
