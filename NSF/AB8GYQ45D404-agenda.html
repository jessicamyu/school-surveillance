Subject:  Strategic Plan Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. School year 2016-2017, marks our second year of implementation of the Strategic Plan.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received.  In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 2, Caring Culture.

 

The following are the three overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 2, Caring Culture:

 

Overarching Strategy 5

 

Establish a school division and culture that is highly responsive to stakeholders and creates a model environment in which to learn and work.

 

Desired Outcomes

  • FCPS will utilize a consistent approach to customer service
  • FCPS will demonstrate cultural competency and sensitivity when supporting families, students and employees
  • FCPS will recognize and celebrate employee success

 

Overarching Strategy 6

 

Foster active and positive communication and engagement among students, staff, families, and the community in order to ensure that all stakeholders are engaged and well-informed partners.

 

Desired Outcomes

  • Communication tools, processes, and content will enable access to key information anytime, anywhere, on any device
  • Under-engaged communities will be provided additional communication and community support
  • Staff will be better engaged and supported in communications and information sharing with their communities
  • Proactive and dynamic communications will better engage the community in critical issues
  • Employees will be better engaged in their work and more connected to the work environment

 

Overarching Strategy 7

 

Promote overall health and well-being of students and staff in order to encourage healthy life choices and an increased quality of life.

 

Desired Outcomes

  • FCPS students will demonstrate improved life skills and self-awareness of behaviors and actions
  • All students will develop global and ethical citizenship skills
  • FCPS students will feel welcome and supported at school
  • FCPS students will be encouraged and supported in making healthy life choices
  • FCPS staff will feel welcome and supported at their work location
  • FCPS staff will be encouraged and supported in making healthy life choices

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 2, Caring Culture, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: ERFC Analysis

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

On February 9, 2017, the School Board postponed action on proposed ERFC Plan modifications pending the receipt of additional information.   The proposed ERFC Plan modifications under consideration included: 

For all ERFC members:

  1. Lowering the interest crediting rate on ERFC member accounts from 5 percent to 4 percent

For ERFC 2001 members hired on or after July 1, 2017, and non-vested members as of June 30, 2017:

 

      2.  Instituting a minimum retirement age of 55

      3.  Increasing the period for calculating the final average salary from 3 years to 5 years

      4.  Changing the cost-of-living adjustment to equal 100 percent of the Consumer Price Index (CPI-U) with a cap of 4.0 percent.

 

 

The School Board requested an analysis of fund management alternatives (e.g., the mix of passive and active management) that may produce savings for the fund without harm to the interests of employees and retirees.  The analysis was to include (a) an analysis and justification for investment choices; (b) reporting of results net of fees; (c) comparison of any active management selections against the related index used for comparison; and (d) inclusion of no less than “Year to Date,” “Trailing 12 Months,” “Previous 3 Year,” “Previous 5 Year,” and “Previous 10 Year” periods. 

 

The School Board also requested an analysis of the fiscal impact of a retirement “rule of 90” for new and non-vested employees, including the calculated short-term and long-term savings.   The “rule of 90” retirement was to be substituted for the “minimum retirement age of 55” rule in the Plan modifications under consideration. 

 

At this work session, the details of the two analyses will be discussed.  Staff will review the ERFC Board of Trustees investment process in developing an asset allocation structure, its investment policy and guidelines, the history of ERFC’s investment philosophy, the current asset structure, implementation choices (active and passive) and performance monitoring.  A representative from New England Pension Consultants, LLC (NEPC) will discuss recent performance results for the varying time periods requested.  An actuary from Aon Hewitt will review the development of the ERFC employer contribution rate and the impact of benefit/investment changes on the rate. 

 

Finally, the employer cost savings associated with the same options listed above except substituting the “rule of 90” retirement will be discussed.  The savings will be provided if ERFC 2001 new hires only are included and if the changes apply to both ERFC 2001 new hires and non-vested employees. 

 

Staff is requesting direction on potential plan changes to ERFC, as SB action is scheduled for the 4/27 School Board meeting.

Subject:  FY 2018 Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                          

 

Key Points:

 

FY 2018 Advertised Budget

On February 9, 2017, the School Board adopted the Advertised Budget for the School Operating Fund totaling $2.8 billion. This is an increase of $135.1 million, or 5.1 percent, over the FY 2017 Approved Budget.

 

For FY 2018, funds are budgeted that will allow FCPS to meet the basic needs of the school system and make continued investments in our teachers and in our classrooms in critical areas. Expenditure increases include:

  • step increase for eligible employees - $44.0 million
  • new teacher salary scale implemented over four years - $33.2 million
  • new salary scales for school-based administrators, classroom instructional support positions (i.e., instructional assistants, public health training assistants, and public health attendants), and for other unified scale positions - $7.0 million
  • one percent market scale adjustment for nonteacher scales - $7.9 million
  • enrollment and student demographics - $10.0 million
  • support for current preschool classrooms - $0.5 million
  • increases for the Virginia Retirement System (VRS), Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System (ERFC) and Fairfax County Employees’ Retirement System (FCERS) rates - $41.2 million
  • employee health coverage - $4.7 million
  • textbook adoption for social studies - $7.8 million, of which $2.7 million is a new investment
  • an assessment tool, universal screener, to identify remediation and accelerations needs - $2.4 million
  • program operations and infrastructure - $3.8 million

Collectively, these expenditure increases are offset by expenditure reductions totaling $27.5 million. The FY 2018 Advertised Budget includes a requested increase in the county transfer of $112.5 million, or 5.9 percent.

 

County Executive’s Advertised Budget

The County Executive presented his FY 2018 Advertised Budget Plan on February 14, 2017, which included a School Operating Fund transfer increase of $51.7 million, or 2.7 percent, which leaves a gap of $60.9 million.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The final state budget results in an increase in state funding of $4.2 million as compared to FCPS’ FY 2018 Advertised Budget.

 

Projected Gap from the March Work Session

As presented at the March 13, 2017, work session, the projected shortfall in the FY 2018 Advertised Budget is $48.4 million. This includes the 2.7 percent transfer increase as recommended by the County Executive, the impact of the final state budget, and the impacts from FCPS’ FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

Discussion Items for This Work Session

  • Updates from the April 21, 2017, County Board of Supervisors Budget Pre-Markup will be provided
  • The projected gap will be updated by:
    • The County’s budget pre-mark-up
    • Enrollment update from spring projections
    • Adjustments to budget reductions for class size and the class size formula floor. These reductions are currently being planned for and are impacted by spring enrollment projections
  • Discuss options for closing the gap:
    • Reductions and fees on the Superintendent’s list of potential reductions that have not already been discussed will be reviewed to determine Board consensus for staff to begin planning for implementation
    • Address questions regarding compensation adjustments based on the tool provided to the Board at the March 13th work session
  • Next steps
    • Summarize outstanding budget questions and prioritize any new questions from today’s work session
    • Review FCPS’ timeline for remaining budget work sessions, public hearings, and board meetings
