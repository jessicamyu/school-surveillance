    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 5:30 and 6 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

      Compensatory Education, Title I regulations require that the local education agency establish a District Advisory Council.     5.01 Central Office Building - That the School Board approve the lease and financing plan, including the form of the attached resolution, for the central administration complex and authorize the Division Superintendent or his designee, with the counsel of the School Board attorney, to negotiate and execute on behalf of the School Board such agreements and related documents as are necessary and advisable in order to consummate the lease and financing plan for the central administration complex, as described in the agenda item (FTS; presented as new business 12/2/04)
    Meeting
    Dec 16, 2004 - Regular Meeting No. 10
    Category
    5. Other Action Items - 7:35 p.m.
    Type
    Action

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

    SUBJECT: Lease and Financing Plan for the Central Administration Complex


    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve the lease and financing plan, including the form of the attached resolution, for the central administration complex and authorize the Division Superintendent or his designee, with the counsel of the School Board attorney, to negotiate and execute on behalf of the School Board such agreements and related documents as are necessary and advisable in order to consummate the lease and financing plan for the central administration complex, as described in the agenda item.

    EXPLANATION:
      On November 16, 2004, the School Board approved the acquisition and related financing of an office building and an adjacent, unimproved building site at 8115 Gatehouse Road (across the street from Luther Jackson Middle School and near the intersection of Route 50 and Gallows Road) for the purpose of consolidating various school system administrative functions currently housed in various locations throughout the County. The office building has 209,423 usable square feet and the additional building site is approximately 3.11 acres in size (collectively, the “Properties”).

      Thereafter, on December 6, 2004, the Board of Supervisors approved the School Board’s acquisition plan for the central administration complex and adopted a financing plan that includes the following:
  • Assignment of purchase rights to the Properties by the School Board to the Economic Development Authority (EDA), as permitted by the terms of the School Board/Jefferson Park LLC purchase agreement.
    • Issuance of bonds and acquisition of title to the Properties by the Fairfax County EDA.
      • Purchase of the Properties from the EDA by the County through an installment purchase contract, the semi-annual payments of which are sufficient to cover the EDA’s debt service.
  • Leasing the Properties by the County to the School Board through a lease agreement for the term of the EDA bonds. The lease payments made by the School Board are used by the County to make its payments to the EDA.
  • Transfer of title to the Properties by the County to the School Board after the bonds are retired.
  • Approved a plan of finance to provide funds for the purchase and fit-up of Phase 1 only. Financing for Phase 2 is subject to the approval of the Board of Supervisors in the future.
      The original financing plan contemplated the incurrence of County debt up to $120 million in two phases for the acquisition of the Properties, tenant improvements to the existing office building, and the construction and equipping of an additional office building on the now unimproved building site.

      Phase 1 of the Plan will be to purchase the land and the existing office building, and provide for the necessary tenant improvements to the existing office building. Tenant improvements will begin immediately upon acquisition of the Properties in January 2005, to be completed within one year and ready for occupancy in early 2006. Under current planning, Phase 2 of the Plan, when approved, will provide for the construction of a new 168,000 square foot building on the vacant building site.

      The total cost of Phase 1 is expected to include $39.8 million purchase price for the building and improvements (and the land on which they are located); $6.0 million for the purchase of the vacant building site; $13.5 million for tenant improvements and furniture, fixtures and equipment; and approximately $2.7 million in financing costs including approximately eight months of capitalized interest. The total par amount of the bonds for Phase 1 is expected to be approximately $61.4 million plus interest costs over the life of the bonds. The financing documents will permit a total issue size not to exceed approximately $64 million in order to allow for potential changes in the market prior to the time of sale.

      Phase 2 costs total approximately $51.6 million including construction; furniture, fixtures and equipment; telecommunications and financing costs. Under current planning, the Phase 2 bonds would be issued about January 2007, subject to the approval of the Board of Supervisors.

      Total debt to be incurred will be approximately $115 million based on today’s market conditions. However, a higher principal amount could be required to accommodate adverse market conditions and increased capitalized interest during construction and the issuance of discount bonds to achieve a more favorable net interest cost.

      RECOMMENDATION:

      EXPLANATION:
        Request for proposals are being solicited for a design team for the tenant build-out of the central administration building. Proposals from interested firms are due December 21, 2004. Pursuant to School Board Policy 8220.1, a Selection Advisory Committee has been formed to review submitted proposals, engage in interviews and presentations, and conduct negotiations with the firm or firms (beginning with the one ranked first) whose professional qualifications and proposed services are deemed most meritorious.
