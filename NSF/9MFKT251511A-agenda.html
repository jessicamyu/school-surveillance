Read Across America Day – March 2, 2015

 

National Education Association's (NEA) Read Across America, now in its 18th year, is an annual reading motivation and awareness program that calls for every child in every community to celebrate reading on or around Dr. Seuss's birthday.  This year’s celebration is based on the theme, “Oh, the Places You’ll Go when you read!" to encourage students to delve into books to learn as much as possible while becoming lifelong readers.   In cities and towns across the nation, teachers, teenagers, librarians, politicians, actors, athletes, parents, grandparents, and others develop NEA's Read Across America activities to bring reading excitement to children of all ages. Governors, mayors, and other elected officials recognize the role reading plays in their communities with proclamations and floor statements. Athletes and actors issue reading challenges to young readers. Teachers and principals seem to be more than happy to dye their hair green or wear Seuss-inspired costumes if it boosts their students' reading.

 

National Foreign Language Week, March 9-15, 2015

 

The week of March 9-15, 2015, has been designated as National Foreign Language Week.  The Alpha Mu Gamma foreign language honor society has sponsored National Foreign Language Week since 1957, and the theme for 2015 is “Learn a Foreign Language; Gain a New Perspective."  Schools are encouraged to use this opportunity to highlight the value of learning world languages and learning about other cultures, and to celebrate the successes of world language students and teachers.

Recognition of National Social Work Month - March 2015

 

March is National Social Work Month and this year’s theme is “Social Work Paves the Way for Change.”  Over the past six decades, professional social workers have worked to improve the rights of women, African Americans and other ethnic minorities, and the LGBT community while strengthening the social safety net through programs such as Medicaid, Medicare, and the Affordable Care Act. They have also advocated for social justice initiatives such as the Voting Rights Act and the Community Mental Health Act.

 

Social workers in Fairfax County Public Schools use their training, experience, and skills to provide services to children, families, and school staff members in the effort to remove personal and environmental obstacles to optimal student academic performance. Social workers are committed to helping every child achieve in school and become a self-sufficient, emotionally healthy, socially well-adjusted, productive member of society.

National Athletic Training Month - March 2015

 

March is National Athletic Training Month and this year’s theme is “We prepare, you perform,” an appropriate slogan reflecting the important role certified athletic trainers employed by Fairfax County Public Schools play in keeping our students safe.  Through educational programs for students, parents and coaches, pre-season sports screenings and emergency planning, these nationally certified and state licensed medical professionals are dedicated to promoting the health and safety of our student-athletes before, during and after practices and competitions.

 

The athletic healthcare services provided by certified athletic trainers include the prevention, recognition, evaluation, treatment, and rehabilitation of athletic injuries and illness.  FCPS continues to be recognized as a leader in adolescent injury epidemiology research and sports-related concussion management for high school athletes.  Our athletic trainers work collaboratively with parents, school staff and community medical providers to promote safe participation for over 23,000 student-athletes each year. 

 

FCPS is nationally recognized as a leader among high school athletic training programs.  All 25 FCPS high schools have been designated as 1st Team Safe Sports Schools through the National Athletic Trainers’ Association and our athletic trainers are recipients of numerous awards in the athletic training field.

Recommended Action
Approve the recommended revisions to the Facilities Planning Advisory Council charter, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly (SB; presented as new business 2/5/15)

Contact:                  Ryan McElveen, Comprehensive Planning Development Committee Chairman and At Large School Board Member

 

Meeting Category:  February 19, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 14

 

Subject:                  Refinement of the FPAC Charter

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Key Points:

 

The Board first discussed proposed revisions to the Facilities Planning and Advisory Council (FPAC) charter at the July 24, 2014, forum work session.  Board consensus was to refer to the Comprehensive Planning Development Committee (CPDC) a review of the proposed revised Charter, including those concerns raised at the forum by Board members, and then schedule a work session for a comprehensive discussion about FPAC and its mission, including any recommendations from CPDC. 

 

CPDC met on November 12 to discuss the proposed FPAC charter revisions and is recommending grammatical and clarifying revisions for the Board’s consideration, including:

  • adding language in Section V that the FPAC shall include the chairman of the Comprehensive Planning and Development Committee (CPDC), as School Board liaison, and a member of the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services Staff, each of whom shall be non-voting members.  The Strategic Governance Manual will be updated accordingly;
  • adding language in Section VI specifically stating that no current FCPS employee shall be appointed to the FPAC, nor may an FPAC member be employed by the FCPS during their appointed tenure. If such an FPAC member becomes employed by the FCPS, they must resign their appointment prior to the next regularly scheduled FPAC meeting; 
  • striking language in Section VII pertaining to information and technical support provided by the Department of Facilities and Transportation staff and such other FCPS departments as may be required and requested;
  • amending language in Section VII that the FPAC shall identify and prioritize facility-related concerns, recommend staff or ad hoc studies, and convene community and public meetings to fulfill its mission;
  • specifying in Section VIII that the FPAC shall provide semi-annual written reports to the Board on its progress on matters referred to or considered by the FPAC;

At the January 12 work session, the Board reviewed the proposed revisions to the FPAC charter and reached consensus that the language in section IV of the FPAC charter be revised to allow FPAC to make recommendations regarding the impact of program placement on capacity. 

 

Board-approved changes to the FPAC proposed charter will be posted on the FPAC web page, http://www.fcps.edu/schlbd/committees/fpac.shtml.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the recommended revisions to the Facilities Planning Advisory Council charter, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

Approve the proposed local academic assessment plan as an option to certify highly qualified paraprofessionals within FCPS under section 1119 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act, as detailed in the agenda item (IS; presented as new business 2/5/15)

Staff Contacts:          Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present:  Kathleen Walts, director, Professional Learning & School Support, ISD

                                 Kathleen Crenshaw, director, Talent Acquisition and Management, Human Resources

                                 Irene Meier, director, Special Education Instruction, Department of Special Services

 

Meeting Category:     February 19, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 14

 

Subject:                     Highly Qualified Paraprofessional Local Assessment Plan

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                              

 

Related To:               Elementary and Secondary Education Act, Title I, Part A, Section 1119

 

Key Points:

 

The Highly Qualified Paraprofessional Local Assessment Plan offers an alternate academic assessment option to certify highly qualified (HQ) paraprofessionals under section 1119 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act. The local assessment plan is designed to offer a rigorous standard of quality by which an instructional assistant (IA) may demonstrate knowledge of and ability to assist in instructing reading, writing, and mathematics. School Board approval of the proposed local assessment plan will:

  • increase accessibility to HQ certification for current and prospective FCPS IAs by providing an alternate test option with improved accommodations, absence of testing fee, and local administration;
  • connect HQ testing for IAs to a 12-hour focused professional development experience to strengthen staff content knowledge and pedagogy to more effectively support student learning, particularly in high needs schools and programs; 
  • contribute to compliance by June 30, 2015 to meet highly qualified paraprofessional expectations within section 1119 of the ESEA, as required within a Title I corrective action plan approved by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE);
  • address long-term staffing shortages by potentially broadening the pool of eligible IAs to serve high needs schools and programs, including Title I and special education.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the proposed local academic assessment plan as an option to certify highly qualified paraprofessionals within FCPS under section 1119 of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act, as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:     February 19, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 14

 

Subject:                      New Advanced Academic Center at Poplar Tree Elementary School

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:                Student Achievement Goals

 

Key Points:

  • Greenbriar West Elementary School has more than 1,100 students, 265 students over capacity.
  • Greenbriar West has 600 students in the Advanced Academic Program (AAP) Center.
  • If no change is made, Greenbriar West will not be able to accept new students into the AAP Center.
  • Poplar Tree Elementary School has capacity for additional students.
  • If approved, the Poplar Tree AAP Center will serve students from Poplar Tree, Brookfield, and Cub Run elementary schools; the Greenbriar West Center will serve students from Greenbriar West, Greenbriar East, and Powell elementary schools; and the Bull Run Center will serve students from Bull Run, Virginia Run, Deer Park, London Towne and Centre Ridge elementary schools.

                                  

Recommendation: Level IV Advanced Academic Program Expansion

 

FCPS staff recommend that the School Board authorize the establishment of a new advanced academic program (AAP) center in September 2015 at Poplar Tree Elementary School for the purpose of relieving severe overcrowding at Greenbriar West Elementary School.

 

FCPS staff further recommend that the new center placements at Poplar Tree begin at grade 3. Rising grade 4 students may request a pupil transfer to the new Poplar Tree center. Rising grade 5 and grade 6 students will remain at their current AAP centers. Future center eligible siblings will not be granted pupil transfer consideration for center placement.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
