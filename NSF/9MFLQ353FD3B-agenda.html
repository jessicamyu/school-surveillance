National Safety Month - June 2015

Each June, the National Safety Council sponsors National Safety Month to promote safe and healthy employee wellness and the President’s Active Lifestyle Award+ challenge, ergonomics, preventing slips, trips, and falls, and driving safety. This year’s theme, What I Live For, encourages employees to engage in safe behaviors no matter what activity they participate in, so they can live for what matters to them.

Over 185,000 FCPS students depend on school employees to provide a safe environment in schools.  Prevention, identification, and abatement of hazards are integral parts of every employee’s job. The FCPS Office of Safety and Security has established emergency plans and provided appropriate training for employees to cultivate safety throughout the school district.

[Updated 5/21/15 - Proposed amendments added]

 

Staff Contact:        Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Meeting Category:  May 21, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                   FY 2016 Approved Budget       

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                                         

 

Key Points:

 

On February 5, 2015, the School Board adopted the FY 2016 Advertised Budget. The advertised budget was presented to and discussed by the School Board and County Board of Supervisors at a joint budget work session on February 27, 2015.

 

On April 28, 2015, the County Board of Supervisors adopted their FY 2016 budget that included an increase in the transfer to the School Operating Fund of 3.2 percent above the FY 2015 Approved Budget. The increase of $56.7 million is $14.0 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget. The Virginia General Assembly adopted its 2014-2016 budget, as amended on April 15, 2015, which provides an increase of $3.9 million in revenue and a decrease in expenditures of $6.0 million as compared to the estimate included in FCPS’ FY 2016 Advertised Budget.

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue in the School Operating Fund reflects a net reduction of $10.1 million as compared to the FY 2016 Advertised Budget.  Funding from the county is $14.0 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by a projected increase in state revenue of $3.9 million.

 

Expenditures:

In total, expenditures in the School Operating Fund reflect a net reduction of $10.1 million as compared to the FY 2016 Advertised Budget. This includes $6.0 million in savings due to a decrease in the employer contribution rate for the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) from 14.50 percent to the rate of 14.06 percent for FY 2016 and a reduction placeholder of $7.6 million. These reductions are offset by an increase of $3.5 million for the cost of enrollment growth and changes in student demographics and $0.1 million for School Board Member salary increases. In order to balance the budget, the School Board will need to identify reductions of $7.6 million prior to the budget being adopted on May 21, 2015. To meet the required level of expenditure reductions, the Superintendent has recommended a reduction totaling $7.6 million, which is detailed on page three of the agenda item.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2016 Approved Budget, as detailed in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget, and as detailed in the agenda item to include Section I, Operating Fund Required Revenue Adjustments, with total reductions of $10.1 million, and Section II, Operating Fund Required Expenditure Adjustments items A through D, with total reductions of $2.5 million, and replacing Section II.E., Reduction Placeholder of $7.6 million, with the following adjustment:  reducing the 1.0 percent market scale adjustment included in the FY 2016 Advertised Budget to 0.62 percent, resulting in a savings of $7.6 million.

 

Proposed Board member amendments:

 

1.  I move to amend the main motion by:

  1. Eliminating recurring funding for and the process of having FCPS pay students’ Advanced Placement and International Baccalaureate test fees at a savings of $5.0 million
  2. Directing the Superintendent to identify one-time funding at the FY 2015 Final Budget Review to continue to pay students’ AP and IB test fees in the 2015-2016 school year
  3. Increasing the budgeted lapse percent from 2.10 percent to 2.23 percent for a savings of $2.6 million
  4. and Increasing the market scale adjustment from 0.62 percent to 1.0 percent at a cost of $7.6 million (McLaughlin)

2. I move to amend the amendment by:

  1. Adjusting the change to the lapse rate with the savings from eliminating the School Board salary increase (Schultz)

3.  I move to amend the main motion by:

  1. Eliminating recurring funding for and the process of having FCPS pay students’ Advanced Placement and International Baccalaureate test fees at a savings of $5.0 million
  2. Directing the Superintendent to identify one-time funding at the FY 2015 Final Budget Review to continue to pay students’ AP and IB test fees in the 2015-2016 school year
  3. Directing the Superintendent to reduce the funding for professional services or another expenditure by $2.6 million
  4. and Increasing the market scale adjustment from 0.62 percent to 1.0 percent at a cost of $7.6 million (McLaughlin)

4. I move to amend the amendment by:

  1. Adjusting the reductions to professional services or other expenditures with the savings from eliminating the School Board salary increase (Schultz)

Proposed follow-on motions:

 

1. I move to direct the Superintendent to develop an FY 2017 Proposed Budget that includes compensation increases for employees, with salary increases comparable to step increases and at least a 1.5 percent market scale adjustment as the Board’s preference; and that the School Board, by this motion, commits itself to compensation increases in FY 2017. (Storck)

 

2. I move to direct the Superintendent to formally accept the Board of Supervisors recommendation to have FCPS participate in the Lines of Business Review as part of the FY 2017 budget development process. (Schultz)

 

3. I move to amend the motion, in the form of a substitute motion:  I move to direct the Superintendent, in the preparation of the FY 2017 budget, to thoroughly analyze all Division operating expenditure budgets to identify any potential cost savings and/or reductions using a methodology commensurate to that of zero-based budgeting. (Hynes)

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
