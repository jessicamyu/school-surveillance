April is Mathematics and Statistics Awareness Month, which is a collaborative effort of the American Mathematical Society, The American Statistical Association, the Mathematical Association of America, and the Society for Industrial and Applied Mathematics.  It marks a time to increase the understanding and appreciation of mathematics and statistics.  Why?  Both subjects play a significant role in addressing many real-world problems such as internet security, sustainability, disease, climate change, the data deluge, and much more.  Research in these and other areas is ongoing, revealing new results and applications every day in fields such as medicine, manufacturing, energy, biotechnology, and business.  Mathematics and statistics are important drivers of innovation in our technological world, in which new systems and methodologies continue to become more complex.

Autism Awareness Month Recognition

 

April is National Autism Awareness Month, and World Autism Awareness Day is April 2. Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication skills. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because each child is different and is affected in unique ways, with many strengths and challenges. Students with autism attend many of our schools in Fairfax County. The Autism Society of America recognizes that the prevalence of autism in the United States has risen from 1 in 125 children in 2010, to 1 in 59 in 2020. National Autism Awareness Month provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to increase awareness and acceptance of autism and celebrate the unique aspects and talents of all people. The Autism Society’s “Celebrate Differences” campaign, which focuses on providing information and resources for communities to be more aware of autism, promote acceptance, and encourage increased community partnerships with businesses and organizations dedicated to building more inclusive experiences.

Glasgow KidWind Team Recognition

 

The KidWind Challenge engages student teams to design, construct and test small scale wind turbines and solar structures at events all over the world. These events strengthen students’ critical thinking skills by introducing them to renewable energy solutions with real-world applications. They learn to work as a team, collaborating and learning from one another to problem-solve and create. They also work on their communication skills, both within the team and as presenters of their design.

 

In March and April of 2019, Team Zephyr, from Glasgow Middle School, competed in two regional competitions. They won first place and the best blade design award at a regional competition in Washington, D.C. Their second place win, in the open generator competition at the Northern Regional Dominion Energy KidWind Challenge, qualified them to compete at the national level.

 

In May 2019, more than 280 students, representing 82 schools from across the country, competed in the 2019 National KidWind Challenge. Team Zephyr won the national middle school champion and wind tracker awards.

 

Two of the team members recently appeared as guests on WUSA’s Great Day Washington program with their after school STEM teacher, Angela DeHart.

 

Tonight, we’d like to recognize, from Team Zephyr: Arthur Prudius, Darren Nguyen, and Edward Meagh.

Recommended Action
That the School Board (i) approve the contract with Dominion Energy whereby FCPS will operate 8 electric school buses for the contract term in conjunction with charging infrastructure to be installed on FCPS property pursuant to an agreed-upon temporary easement for the benefit of Dominion; (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, with the advice of legal counsel, to negotiate, execute and administer this contract and all related documents (including the deed of easement) on behalf of the School Board; and (iii) authorize procurement of the bus chassis and other associated equipment (consistent with Dominion’s specifications) through the director or coordinator of the FCPS Office of Procurement Services in accordance with applicable law, including the Virginia Public Procurement Act and the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution.

Subject: Electric School Bus Program (Dominion Energy)

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Francine Furby, director, Office of Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: March 19, 2020 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Action

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 1 Student Success and Goal 4 Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

In August 2019, Governor Northam announced Dominion Energy’s (“Dominion”) initiative to partner with selected Virginia school districts for a pilot of 50 electric school buses to be in operation by the close of 2020, with future plans to convert all new school buses in Virginia to electric by 2030.

 

Electric buses are designed to provide environmental and health benefits through reduced emissions as well as reduced operational and maintenance costs.

 

Following a competitive application process, Dominion announced the award of 8 electric buses to Fairfax County Public Schools, which is one of sixteen Virginia school divisions selected to participate.

 

Four of the new electric buses are scheduled to be delivered to FCPS in August 2020, with the remaining 4 to arrive in December 2020.

 

In this pilot phase, each participating school district would purchase the school bus chassis along with certain equipment (e.g., seat belts), but without a diesel engine. Dominion would fund the remaining cost of the electric school bus (approximately $200,000/per bus) as well as the cost of installing vehicle to grid charging infrastructure on district property for each participating district. The vehicle to grid technology allows Dominion Energy to use the school buses’ batteries to supply the grid when the buses are not in use, creating additional energy storage.

 

Dominion and FCPS are now negotiating the terms of an agreement that provides for the operation of the school buses and the rights of the parties over the 15 year contractual term.

 

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board (i) approve the contract with Dominion Energy whereby FCPS will operate 8 electric school buses for the contract term in conjunction with charging infrastructure to be installed on FCPS property pursuant to an agreed-upon temporary easement for the benefit of Dominion; (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, with the advice of legal counsel, to negotiate, execute and administer this contract and all related documents (including the deed of easement) on behalf of the School Board; and (iii) authorize procurement of the bus chassis and other associated equipment (consistent with Dominion’s specifications) through the director or coordinator of the FCPS Office of Procurement Services in accordance with applicable law, including the Virginia Public Procurement Act and the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution.
