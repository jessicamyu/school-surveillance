RESOLUTION

ON

HUMAN TRAFFICKING AWARENESS MONTH

January 2018

 

WHEREAS, students in Fairfax County continue to fall victim to the enduring and growing threat of human trafficking, a threat that remains largely under the radar, understood little or not at all by many students and a majority of the community, this widespread complacency endures despite the expanding reach of traffickers around the world and here in our county, with criminals skillfully exploiting the lack of knowledge of students and their families; and

 

WHEREAS, human traffickers continue to seek out students that are vulnerable in county middle and high schools and even elementary schools, a group which includes nearly every student at some point during their academic career, so that victims have been identified from every high school, most middle schools, and multiple elementary schools across the school district; and

 

WHEREAS, these targeted children and young people come from every background and social and economic group, and are pursued in person at all the places that students gather or spend time, including: shopping malls, restaurants, and bus stops, and on-line through virtually every social media application, especially those that mask the user’s identity; and

 

WHEREAS, the trafficking awareness curriculum pioneered by Fairfax County Public Schools, with the support of the Just Ask Prevention Project, remains the “Gold Standard” for building a realistic sense of the danger among students and their families and educating them on warning signs to look for, with other districts across the nation continuing to reach out to learn from the example set here; and 

 

WHEREAS, clubs and groups of students from county high schools and middle schools have continued to grow and thrive, holding awareness campaigns to raising awareness about teen sex trafficking, and providing invaluable support to community events, such as the Just Ask Walk/Run for Freedom, and through these efforts have inoculated thousands of their classmates against the threat, resulting in students who are awake to the danger contacting law enforcement on multiple occasions, leading to severe arrests; and

 

WHEREAS, working in conjunction with these dedicated young people, educators, parents, medical professionals, law enforcement officials, civic organizations, and the business community are also playing an invaluable role in spreading the word and protecting the community, with a shared goal of establishing a community free from this dire threat to our students and caring for the victims;  

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board again joins the county Board of Supervisors in recognizing the month of January 2018 as Human Trafficking Awareness Month in Fairfax County and supports the efforts of all those who are seeking to eradicate human trafficking and end its devastating impact on our students and our community.

5.02 Recommendation to Open New Advanced Academic Center at Lanier Middle School; ISD; NB 12/14/18
Meeting
Jan 11, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Action Items - 8:15 p.m.
Type
Action, Information

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department                                   

 

Other Staff Present:  Phyllis Pajardo, Superintendent, Fairfax City Schools

                                  Francis Ivey, assistant superintendent, Region 5

                                  Erin Lenart, principal, Lanier Middle School

                                  Noel Klimenko, director, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD           

                                  Kirsten Maloney, coordinator, Advanced Academics Program, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category:  January 11, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject:   Establishment of New Level IV Advanced Academic Center at Lanier Middle School + Adoption of new pupil assignment plan

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 


Related To:  Strategic Plan-Goal 1; Policy 8130

 

Key Points:

● City of Fairfax School Board requested in 2016 that FCPS explore options for Fairfax HS pyramid students to receive Level IV AAP services without leaving the pyramid.

  • The number of Lanier MS-based AAP Level IV eligible students meets FCPS critical mass criteria.
  • Lanier MS has facility capacity to house the Level IV AAP Center Program.
  • Lanier MS has committed leadership, and the school is well-prepared to offer a high quality Level IV AAP center program.
  • Seventy people attended the community meeting on October 19, 2017. 
  • There were 407 respondents to the electronic survey.
    • 77% of survey participants reported having an AAP Level IV eligible student
    • 350 participants selected a preference for one of the two options offered:  
      • 53% preferred option 1 for SY 2018-19 to be a choice year for rising grades 7 & grade 8 (185 people)
      • 47% preferred option 2 to begin reassignment of rising 7th grade students without choice in SY 2018-19  (165 people)
  • Prior to voting on the establishment of the Lanier Level IV Center and the adoption of a new pupil assignment plan, the School Board will hold a public hearing for the purpose of obtaining additional public comment as a part of its scheduled meeting of January 11, 2018, which public hearing will be the subject of at least 10 days advance notice to the public (including notice published in a newspaper of general circulation in the school division).

 

 

Recommendation:    

That, in order to contribute to the efficiency of the school division, the School Board approve the establishment of a new Level IV advanced academic center program in fall 2018 at Lanier Middle School, and also adopt the following pupil assignment plan: in fall 2018 Lanier based rising 7th and 8th grade center-eligible students currently assigned to Rocky Run MS would be afforded the option to attend the new Lanier MS Level IV center or their current Level IV center at Rocky Run MS.  In fall 2019, Lanier based rising 7th grade students currently assigned to Rocky Run will be required to attend Lanier’s Level IV center. Rising 8th grade students in fall 2019 would be afforded the option to attend Lanier Level IV center or their current Level IV center at Rocky Run MS.  In fall 2020 Lanier based rising 7th and 8th grade center-eligible students will be required to attend the Lanier Level IV center.
