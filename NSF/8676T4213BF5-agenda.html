HUMAN RESOURCES: The Superintendent will recruit, select, and retain a high quality and diverse workforce that enables FCPS to deliver a world-class education to every student in an environment characterized by high staff morale and loyalty to the FCPS mission and goals and in compliance with relevant laws and regulations.

A. Recruitment, Selection, and Retention. The Superintendent will:
          1. Recruit and hire qualified employees of diverse backgrounds.
          2. Retain a highly qualified and diverse workforce with a reasonable annual turnover rate.
          3. Ensure that all staff members are qualified to perform the responsibilities assigned to them.
          4. Administer appropriate and thorough background inquiries and checks prior to hiring.
B. Staff Treatment. The Superintendent will:
          1. Encourage full use of talents and energies.
          2. Establish a culture of respect that treats all staff members in a fair and balanced manner.
          3. Establish an atmosphere of openness, civility and responsiveness that seeks broad-based staff participation in identifying organizational needs and means of implementing improvements; consider various viewpoints and reasoned dissent while achieving sound decision-making.

C. Staff Evaluation. The Superintendent will:
          1. Evaluate regularly all personnel based on job performance expectations aligned to the Board’s goals and values, recognizing excellence and providing constructive suggestions for improvement.
          2. Recognize excellence and provide programs that celebrate outstanding contributions to our workplace and student success.
D. Staff Compensation. The Superintendent will:
          1. Develop compensation and benefit plans that are fair and equitable and consider the minimum living wage in an effort to attract and retain the highest quality employees competitive with the applicable marketplace.

Contact: Kathy L. Smith, Chairman and Sully District Representative

Meeting Category: December 7, 2009 – Work Session No. 56

Subject: 2009-2010 Charge to School Health Advisory Committee

School Board Action Required: Action
    Related To: School Board Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

    Key Points:
        At work sessions in May and June 2009, the Board reviewed the recommended charges for all the School Board advisory committees. The Board approved the final charges on July 23, 2009.

        The School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) met this fall and requested that their charge be further refined to give more specific guidance to their work and to be more aligned with the Coordinated School Health Model. School Board member Liz Bradsher, who serves as the School Board’s SHAC liaison, concurs with this change to the SHAC charge for 2009-2010.

          School Health Advisory Committee—recommended new charge:

          Use the Coordinated School Health Program model to evaluate FCPS resources and strategies that are in place to address issues that are identified in the Youth Risk Behavior Survey.

          Previously approved charge:
          Continue to look at the coordinated health services model and ways to implement specific modules, in addition to utilizing the new student survey data to examine issues related to depression or other risk behaviors.


    RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board approve the following revised charge for 2009-2010 for the School Health Advisory Committee: Use the Coordinated School Health Program model to evaluate FCPS resources and strategies that are in place to address issues that are identified in the Youth Risk Behavior Survey.

    Presentation

    Staff Contact: Peter Noonan, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services

    Other Staff Present: Darryle Craig, Program Manager, College Success Program

    Meeting Category: December 7, 2009 – Work Session No. 59
    Subject: College Success Program

    School Board Action Required: Information

    Related To: Operational Expectation: Instructional Program and Treatment of Students

    Key Points:

    In the spring of 2009, a decision was made to reorganize several offices within the Instructional Services and Special Services Departments. Additionally, and within the reorganizational structure, many programs were also redesigned. Consequently, the Office of School Counseling was moved from the Department of Special Services, along with discrete programs that support college success for students that are traditionally underrepresented, to the Instructional Services Department. In an effort to align alike programs and leverage resources, an overarching structure was developed called College Success Program. The College Success Program office falls under the Office of School Counseling and is the responsibility of Dr. Darryle Craig. Further, the College Success Program structure incorporates the following programs: College Partnership, Pathways to the Baccalaureate, Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID), Early Identification Program (EIP), and a newly garnered program from Fairfax County – Project Discovery.

    The attached PowerPoint, and subsequent presentation, will provide information to the School Board about how these programs have been collocated, foundational information about each program, and their respective growth over the past year.

    Recommendation:

    That the School Board continue to support the programs that provide college access to traditionally underrepresented students
