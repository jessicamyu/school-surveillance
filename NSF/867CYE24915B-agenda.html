112.04 Discussion - Title I Annual Report
Meeting
May 29, 2008 - Work Session No. 112
Category
112. Human Resources and Special Services - 7 p.m.
Type
Report

      Staff Contact: Peter Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

      Meeting Type: May 29, 2008 - Work Session No. 112

      SUBJECT: District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education, Title I, Annual Report

      TYPE: Discussion

      RELATED TO: Student Achievement Goals

      BACKGROUND:

          Federal and state guidelines for parent involvement under Title I of the No Child Left Behind law require participation of parents in the design and evaluation of Title I programs. This report gives an overview of the Title I program in FCPS, highlights parent involvement programs and activities in schools, reports on survey findings, and makes recommendations for 2008-2009.

      KEY POINTS:
          Title I District Advisory Council members have made the following recommendations:
              • Continue to provide guidance and support for parents on how to help their children practice reading, writing, and math skills at home.
              • Continue to purchase dual-language books for distribution to families through the Partners in Print family literacy programs so that parents can share books with their children in either English or their home language. Expand the purchase of dual language books to include languages other than Spanish.
              • Encourage and assist schools to place their Family Involvement Policies and School-Home Compacts on their school web sites to provide parents easy access to these documents. Work with schools to standardize the location of the Title I information on their web sites.
              • Continue sponsoring and coordinating Family Field Trips for Title I schools to provide opportunities for intergenerational learning.
              • Use the District Advisory Council (DAC) meetings to provide more information to DAC representatives about family involvement resources available within Fairfax County Public Schools.
              • Develop additional informational material, including a PowerPoint presentation, that Title I schools can use to explain Title I to parents.
              • Research options for family literacy programs for grades 4-6 that can be used at schools to provide support for parents of older students.
              • Encourage and provide support and resources to assist schools in opening or expanding Parent Centers.
      RECOMMENDATION:

      Acceptance of Title I DAC Annual Report

Staff Contact: Alice Farling, assistant superintendent, Special Services

Meeting Type: May 29, 2008 - Work Session No. 112

SUBJECT: School Health Advisory Committee Annual Report

TYPE: Information

RELATED TO: Student Achievement Goals

BACKGROUND:
      The School Health Advisory Committee is mandated by the Commonwealth of Virginia to assist with the development of health policy in the school division and the evaluation of the status of school health, health education, the school environment, and health services

KEY POINTS:
      SHAC supports and recommends:
        1. Consider the implementation of a Coordinated School Health (CSH) plan along the lines outlined by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and to assess the plan using the CDC’s School Health Index (SHI).
        2. Increase support for student mental health issues by increasing mental health staff (psychologists and social workers) and provide information and training to staff, students and parents on mental health issues.
        3. Continue the collaboration with county staff to coordinate the county’s Three Common Goals: 1. Children are physically fit with good nutritional habits, 2. Children enter kindergarten fully ready to succeed, 3. Children and youth are safe from violence and bullying.
        4. Continue to support and advertise to a diverse group of parents the Partners for Safe Teen Driving Program.
        5. Consider ways to increase participation in the school breakfast program.


RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board accept the Advisory Committee report.
