National Inclusive Schools Week
December 3 - 7, 2018

 

   WHEREAS, since its inception in 2001, National Inclusive Schools Week has celebrated the progress that schools have made in providing a supportive and quality education to an increasingly diverse student population, including students who are marginalized due to disability, gender, socio-economic status, cultural heritage, language preference, and other factors; and

 

     WHEREAS, Inclusive Schools Week is an annual event sponsored by the Inclusive Schools Network (ISN) and is held during the first full week of December; and

 

    WHEREAS, this year’s theme is “Kaleidoscope of Friends".  A Kaleidoscope is an infinite array of patterns of brilliant light created by repeated reflection.  The word Kaleidoscope means beautiful shape and indeed, education takes on a beautiful shape when all children belong and are valued as contributing members of the school community.  The vision of inclusive education is to drive decisions, actions, and core beliefs.  Repeated reflection helps shape effective practices to meet the needs of all students.

 

     WHEREAS, by adding the word "friends", the result is a myriad of relationships that provide opportunities for the support and growth of all children.

 

     WHEREAS, the week also provides an important opportunity for educators, students, and parents to discuss what else needs to be done in order to ensure that their schools continue to improve their ability to successfully educate all children.

 

     NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board reminds the entire FCPS community that Education takes on a beautiful shape when all children are included.

RESOLUTION HONORING

THE 50th ANNIVERSARY

OF HAYFIELD SECONDARY SCHOOL

 

WHEREAS, Hayfield Secondary School, the first public secondary school to open in Fairfax County, Virginia, will proudly celebrate its 50th anniversary, and its reputation for providing generations of students from a richly diverse community with an excellent academic foundation; and

 

WHEREAS, Hayfield is a high performing secondary school dedicated to providing a strong instructional program in an inviting school environment for its students, who represent over 71 countries and speak 38 different languages, and has enriched students through its English Learner, Special Education, Advanced Academics programs, along with Young Scholars, Career and Technical Education Certifications, Fine and Performing Arts Experiences, CAREing Mentors, Student 2 Student Ambassador program, AVID and College Partnership Programs; and

 

WHEREAS, the school has continued to provide the best educational tools and learning opportunities for its students by embracing new initiatives, implementing, recognizing the importance of arts and career and technical education in developing creative minds and providing alternative avenues for learning, renovating its facilities and equipping classrooms with the latest educational technology, and implementing 1:1 computer access for all students; and

 

WHEREAS, the faculty, parents and community of Hayfield are committed to increasing the emotional well-being and academic achievement of all students through increased access to rigor in addition to academic and socio-emotional supports, while viewing its diversity as a unique opportunity and strength that enriches and broadens the educational experiences of all students and prepares them to be 21st century learners; and

 

WHEREAS, thousands of students have passed through the doors at Hayfield Secondary and benefitted from a comprehensive education and the efforts of dedicated and caring teachers and staff whose vision it is that all students graduate as Caring Citizens, Critical Thinkers, and Creative Collaborators.

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board honors Hayfield Secondary School on the occasion of its 50th Anniversary, commends the school on its history of providing educational excellence and enrichment opportunities to the students and community, and conveys its most heartfelt best wishes for a successful anniversary celebration. Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, director, Office of Government Relations

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

 

Meeting Category: November 19, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Action

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement:

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly affected by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing in particular the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The School Board annually adopt a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.

The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions and (where applicable) formatting changes from the Board’s 2018 legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect current approaches to policy debates and issues, and to clarify, simplify and streamline existing position language where appropriate.

 

Proposed staff recommended changes to the FCSB 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program will be presented at the Board’s November 8, 2018 regular meeting. Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 19, 2018.

 

Recommendation:

Adopt the Fairfax County School Board's 2019 State and Federal Legislative Program

1.  Because students need trained health professionals (school nurses) to help them effectively manage their health and optimize their learning outcomes, the Board directs the Superintendent and staff to work with the County in reviewing Current Practices as well as Best Practices for School-Based Clinics, in order to provide legislative recommendations for the 2020 State Legislative Program. 2.  Research suggests that there is a positive correlation between access to menstrual supplies and school attendance. Since the State of Virginia has made it clear that school attendance is linked to a student’s ability to access the public education process and access to menstrual protection is essential to the productivity and development of girls in schools, FCPS should evaluate how to serve our female student population to address this issue. In the alternative, the Superintendent may direct the staff to explore options of supporting this population particularly in schools with a high FRM population. The Superintendent is directed to provide a report that includes costs for said program to the Board within three months or on a date certain. (Keys-Gamarra)

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan 2018-2020

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, a variety of stakeholders (School Board, Leadership Team, principals, teachers, students, and the community) provided input to prioritize the outcomes in each of the four goal areas.

 

The School Board endorsed the eight prioritized areas of focus for school years 2018-2020 in their July 23, 2018, work session. Refinements have been made based on the discussion in that work session. Aspirational goals – which show what success in each goal area would look like for students, families, employees, and the community – have been clearly defined. Baselines – which show FCPS’ recent performance – have been used to set performance targets that will be used to hold the Division accountable for short-term progress.

 

The Board will have an opportunity to review the elements that reflect success in the strategic plan Sys 2018-2020 focus: (1) what FCPS aspires to accomplish and (2) how we will measure success.

 

Recommendation:

The School Board accept the recommended changes to the strategic plan.

Problem Statement: At the Board's December 15, 2016 forum, Ms. Hynes and Mr. Wilson requested School Board review of Policy 8335, which was inherited by the current Board, to ensure the policy reflects the wishes of the Board and takes into account any new relevant factors or technological advancements that may have been made in the almost ten years since the policy's adoption.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

At the December 15, 2016 forum, the Board requested that the Governance Committee review Policy 8335 - Wireless Telecommunication Facilities and make a recommendation to the Board.  The Governance Committee discussed the policy and regulation during several committee meetings attended by interested community members. 

 

The Committee recommends revisions to the policy to provide earlier involvement and increased input from the community and School Board and to ensure that any proposed changes are in compliance with applicable Federal, State and local laws and regulations, as detailed in the agenda item.  The revisions recommend adding language to reserve the right of the School Board to review and approve/disapprove  any application for review prior to any government approval, and ensure FCPS sites only be considered after the magisterial district(s) and at-large School Board member(s) has been informed about the site application and briefed on placement.

 

The policy was reviewed by the Governance Committee on September 13, 2017, December 13, 2017 and January 10, 2018, by the Board at the May 14, 2018 work session,  by the Governance Committee again on July 18, 2018 and by the School Board at the October 29, 2018 work session.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

 

That the School Board approve proposed revisions to Policy 8335 - Wireless Telecommunication Facilities as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category: November 19, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

Summary Background:

 

On October 24, 2018, proposals were received for RFP #19-010, for Relocation Services (Disassembly, Assembly, Transportation, and Demolition) for Portable Classroom Trailers. The Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) is evaluating the proposals in accordance with the criteria set forth in School Board Policy 8240.6.

 

To handle the volume of disassembling, transporting, assembling, and demolition of trailers, it is in the best interest of FCPS to engage multiple contractors as opposed to awarding a contract to one offerer. Awarding a contract to multiple contractors provides FCPS the opportunity to take advantage of the lowest price, and creates flexibility and classroom readiness by the start of the next school year. Funding for these projects will be provided from either operating or bond funds. The SAC will recommend a list of qualified contractors for notice of award prior to the December 19, 2018, School Board meeting.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize staff to issue a Notice of Award to multiple contractors for Relocation Services for Portable Classroom Trailers, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute, deliver, and administer contracts on behalf of the School Board.
