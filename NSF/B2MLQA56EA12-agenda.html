National School Lunch Week, October 15-19, 2018


National School Lunch Week is officially scheduled for October 15-19 by an act of Congress. Using the theme "School Lunch:  Lots 2 Love", National School Lunch Week promotes excitement about the benefits of students consuming wholesome, healthy school lunches of fresh fruits and veggies, whole grains and milk.  The 2018 event was designed to help students and school nutrition professionals connect and share with parents, school officials, the media and the public what each loves most about school lunches.  The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own, nutrition habits in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

 

 

 

Disability Employment and Awareness Month

 

Did you know that October is National Disability Employment and Awareness Month? This year’s theme is “America's Workforce: Empowering All".  Each year, Fairfax County Public Schools participates in the National Disability Employment Awareness Month (NDEAM) which promotes career development for individuals with disabilities through hands-on career exploration and job shadowing in the following fields: Administrative Support, Animal Care, Business Management, Communications, Information Technology, and Parks and Recreation.

NDEAM strives to encourage students to explore potential career or employment opportunities, to learn more about businesses in their community, and begin to plan to achieve more successful employment outcomes.

The Fairfax County School Board along with the  National PTA, the Fairfax County Council of PTAs, and the Fairfax Education Association recognize October as LGBT (Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Transgender) History Month. 

 

According to the National PTA, “negative attitudes toward lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender students puts them at an increased risk for violence and bullying.  Negative attitudes also impact a student's ability to learn at school.”

 

LGBT History Month is recognized in October nationwide and in FCPS to acknowledge and celebrate that LGBT students, staff and families are an integral component of our nation, and of Fairfax County Public Schools.

 

This special month was first recognized in 1994; October was chosen to commemorate National Coming Out Day on October 14, and the First March on Washington for Gay and Lesbian Rights on October 14, 1979.

 

One of the first Gay-Straight Alliances in Virginia was founded at Hayfield High School in 2000, and now students are involved in clubs at many of the high schools in the county, welcoming not only LGBT students but their friends and those interested in creating safe schools.

 

LGBT History Month in October is an opportunity for Fairfax County Public Schools staff and students to celebrate, and to learn more about, these important constituents in our schools and neighborhoods.

National School Bus Safety Week, October 22-26, 2018

 

The week of October 22-26 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. Based on the theme "My Driver - My Safety Hero!", the week focuses on student safety when waiting for and boarding a school bus. School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 142,000 students in over 1,600 school buses on more than 6,500 separate routes traveling over 17 million operational miles each year.

 

Motorists are encouraged to practice patience and drive gently around school buses in our neighborhoods and school communities.

With these efforts, FCPS can reduce bullying, fight stereotypes, and build relationships, all leading to a Unified Generation that Chooses to Include. Congratulations again to Lee, Robinson, Frost, and Bush Hill!

RESOLUTION OF THE FAIRFAX COUNTY SCHOOL BOARD

CALLING FOR STATE AND FEDERAL ACTION ON CLIMATE CHANGE

 

     WHEREAS, an overwhelming majority of credentialed scientists, in the U.S. and abroad, support the finding that climate change is happening and that human activity is a key contributor; and

 

     WHEREAS, if left unaddressed, the consequences of climate change will harm all Americans, most especially children and those living in poverty, and saddle future generations with the costly burden of a dangerously damaged planet; and

 

     WHEREAS, climate instability is a global challenge requiring bold, innovative, and sustained actions at all levels of government, local, state, and federal; and 

 

     WHEREAS, the size of Fairfax County Public Schools’ physical footprint provides an unparalleled opportunity to advance the use of renewable energy sources and reduce greenhouse gas output in Northern Virginia; and 

 

     WHEREAS, pursuant to School Board Environmental Stewardship Policy 8542, FCPS leads the nation in energy efficiency, the development of green building design standards, and award-winning classroom opportunities for student advocacy and learning through the Get 2 Green program; and  

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County School Board’s commitment to the safety, well-being, and future success of all children in our community also demands a high priority on reducing carbon consumption in our decisions regarding capital improvement, energy use, transportation, and other policy priorities within the Board’s control; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County School Board depends on committed partners in local, state, and federal government to realize our climate action goals, and recognizes the efforts and progress made to date, especially Fairfax County Government’s recently announced request for proposals for solar installations on public buildings, to include schools;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, be it resolved that the Fairfax County School Board: 1) calls on the members of the Virginia General Assembly and the United States Congress to act boldly on climate change and provide a regulatory framework that removes barriers to progress on climate action and encourages the rapid replacement of fossil fuels with renewable energy technology; and 2) directs the Superintendent to report timely to the Board changes in state and federal policy that support the goal of reducing carbon consumption, along with staff proposals to make best use of those opportunities in facilities and transportation planning.

Type

Recommended Action
That the School Board approve changing the local FCPS graduation requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma to match the VDOE requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma as recommended by a steering committee with representation from school and central office leadership.

Subject: Local Graduation Requirements for Science and Social Studies for the Standard Diploma

 

Contact: Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present: Jennifer Glaser, director, Counseling and College and Career Readiness, ISD

Noel Klimenko, director, K-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category: October 11, 2018—Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Action

 

Ignite Link: Goal 1: Student Success

 

Problem Statement:

The local graduation requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma for FCPS’ students are more rigorous than required by the state.

 

Outcome:

School Board vote to determine if FCPS will maintain local graduation requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma or change to match the VDOE requirements.

 

Summary/Background:

Local graduation requirements for science and social studies for the Standard Diploma for FCPS’ students are more rigorous than required by VDOE. For science, FCPS requires that students complete three credits in three different disciplines, while VDOE requires that students complete three credits in two different disciplines. For social studies, FCPS requires that students complete two credits in world history and geography, while VDOE requires that students complete one credit in world history and geography. Matching the VDOE requirements for the Standard Diploma would offer students pursuing a Standard Diploma additional flexibility in course taking for science and would allow students pursing a Standard Diploma an additional elective choice by removing one of the currently required social studies credits.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve changing the local FCPS graduation requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma to match the VDOE requirements in science and social studies for the Standard Diploma as recommended by a steering committee with representation from school and central office leadership.

Contact: Sloan J. Presidio, assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present: Noel Klimenko, director, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

                                 Paige Whitlock, coordinator, Secondary Language Arts, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category: October 11, 2018-Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

New K-12 English Standards of Learning were adopted by the Virginia Department of Education with implementation to begin in September 2018 and to be fully assessed in 2019-2020. The planned order for adopting new basal resource materials to align with the new SOLs for language arts is high school 2018-2019; middle school 2019-2020; and elementary 2020-2021 with three to six School Board members selecting a community member each fall.

 

School Board Member selection of appointees for K-12 Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee will commence in the following order.

 

  • Fall 2018—Ms. Keys-Gamarra, Braddock, and Dranesville

  • Fall 2019—At-large Member, Hunter Mill, and Lee

  • Fall 2020—At-large Member, Mason, Mount Vernon, Providence, Springfield, and Sully

 

The High School Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee will meet in this school year to review and recommend instructional materials for use in high school language arts courses. These materials will be considered basal materials or those that are provided to all students who are enrolled in high school language arts courses.

 

The High School Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee is composed of 12 members.

 

  • 6 teachers selected from applications submitted to Instructional Services

  • 3 school-based administrators nominated by Region Assistant Superintendents and principal associations

  • 3 community members appointed by School Board members by November 1, 2018

     

 

The High School Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee is scheduled to meet November 7, November 28, December 5, December 12, and December 19 from 4:00 to 9:00 p.m.

 

Recommendation:

 

That Ms. Keys-Gamarra, At-Large School Board member,  and School Board members from Braddock and Dranesville shall select committee appointees to the High School Language Arts Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

 

Names for committee members should be sent to Paige Whitlock, coordinator, Secondary Language Arts at pewhitlock@fcps.edu.
