NAMING THE TRACK AT

VIRGINIA RUN ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

WHEREAS,  Dr. Teresa Hicks began a career in Fairfax County Public Schools in 1990 and served as Virginia Run Elementary School principal from 1996 to 2010; and

 

WHEREAS,  Dr. Hicks provided leadership that led to such instructional initiatives as adopting core virtues of the basic school model for character education two years prior to the mandate and instituting quality instructional  practices, as outlined in the Success by Eight model; and

 

WHEREAS,  Dr. Hicks continued over the years to develop and improve the education of all students by launching and teaching  the Great Beginnings mentor program, updating instructional technology throughout the school, and championing a full inclusion program for special education; and

 

WHEREAS, she helped build a school-wide relationship with the PTA, which lead to a strong community partnership and provided for a second playground, SMART Boards throughout the building, and the 20th anniversary track; and

 

WHEREAS, Dr. Hicks retired from Fairfax County Public Schools in 2010 after 20 years of dedication to students across the county and specifically at Virginia Run Elementary School over the past 14 years;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED  that the Fairfax County School Board names the track at Virginia Run Elementary School “The Teresa Hicks Track” in recognition of Dr. Hicks’ years of dedication and service to the students, faculty, and families of Virginia Run Elementary School.

Suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period beginning July 1, 2011, and ending June 30, 2013; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations, for the same period; the School Board must approve the continued suspension of the Policy and implementing regulations beyond June 30, 2013.(HR; work session held 1/10/11; presented as new business 1/20/11)

Staff Contact:           Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

Meeting Category:  February 3, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 13

Subject:                   Suspension of Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure

School Board Action Required:  Decision

Background:

 

Policy 4680, Salary Redline Procedure, authorizes redlining of salaries for up to two years for those employees whose salaries have been reduced as the result of a change in position, if that change was made for the benefit of the school system, rather than a voluntary choice of the employee.  The policy provides that redlining may be modified by the Board if deemed necessary for budgetary reasons.

Key Points:

 

At the December 18, 2008, regular meeting, the School Board approved the suspension of the redlining policy for the period beginning January 1, 2009, and ending June 30, 2011. The redlining policy plays a valuable role in minimizing morale problems and easing the transition for employees to lower level jobs by insuring that their salary remains at a higher level.  However, this policy was envisioned to ease the transition to lower level positions during normal times.  The budget situation that resulted in the current redline suspension continues to persist and if we are to realize planned savings from reorganizations and budget reductions, the policy suspension needs to be continued.

 

Failure to suspend the redlining policy will mean that very little, if any, money is saved in reorganization and in budget-related reductions that involve people moving to lower level positions or shorter contract lengths. 

 

The suspension would not preclude the Superintendent or Assistant Superintendent for Human Resources to extend salary levels for short periods in order to settle legal disputes.

 

Options:

 

The School Board reinstate the redlining policy and make compensating budget reductions as required.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period beginning July 1, 2011, and ending June 30, 2013; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations, for the same period; the School Board must approve the continued suspension of the Policy and implementing regulations beyond June 30, 2013.

Subject:                      Award of Contract – Rooftop Unit Replacements at Falls Church High School

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:                Policy 5011, Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

Funding for this project will be provided from the HVAC Infrastructure Bond Funds.  Specifications have been prepared and the project has been advertised.  An invitation for bid has been issued and advertised; bids were due to be received on January 20, 2011.  A bid tabulation and recommendation will be furnished prior to the February 24, 2011, School Board meeting.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the rooftop unit replacements at Falls Church High School to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder(s) and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.
