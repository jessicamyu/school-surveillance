Staff Contact:          Dan Parris, Interim Deputy Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  March 13, 2014 – Work Session No. 92

 

Subject:                  Strategic Planning - Update from ECRA Consultants       

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion           

 

Key Points:

 

The ECRA group will partner with Fairfax County Public Schools to develop a division-wide strategic plan. The proposal outlines a three-phase process that includes conducting research, developing the strategic plan and identifying steps for implementation. Phase I will focus on documenting the current state of reality and desired future position of the school division. Phase II will create a framework for broad direction and priorities for the future in a formal strategic plan document. Phase III will identify the specific steps that will be taken in order to execute the objectives identified in the strategic plan.

 

ECRA will engage with staff to fulfill the identified tasks that include:

 

Work with School Board members and others stakeholder to assess and revise as needed School Board belief statements, vision, mission, student achievement goals and operational objectives in order to assist the School Board in clarifying priorities and affirming a guiding strategic direction for the division.

 

Assist in a “backward design” process from the Portrait of a Graduate work to define a balanced assessment framework that helps to streamline and focus measurable, rigorous and relevant performance indicators at salient passage points in student development.

 

Conduct a thorough analysis of relevant division-level information to assist in assessing the current state of performance. Use these findings to identify key drivers for advancing student outcomes and operational efficiencies.

 

Engage with internal stakeholders to ascertain long-term resource needs and develop action plans to achieve the priorities and goals for the division.

 

Work with FCPS staff to provide a detailed framework and schedule/timelines for developing shorter-term tactical improvement work plans at the school level that are aligned closely to the strategic priorities and goals established for the district.
