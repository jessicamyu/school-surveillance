Subject:                     Joint Boards Synthetic Turf Task Force Presentation

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:                       

 

On December 15, 2011, the School Board (SB) adopted a resolution recommending the creation of a County/Schools Joint Task Force on Synthetic Turf Athletic Fields to focus on the development and maintenance of synthetic turf fields, as well as fair and equitable access county-wide to such fields. In February 2012, a request was sent by the SB to the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors (BOS) and Park Authority (FCPA) Board requesting their support in the synthetic turf efforts that would benefit FCPS and all Fairfax County communities. On April 12, 2012, the BOS and FCPA confirmed their support of the issues and referred them to staff for further efforts and the formation of the Joint Boards Synthetic Turf Task Force.

 

The director of the Fairfax County Department of Neighborhood and Community Services (NCS) was appointed as the Synthetic Turf Task Force (STTF) chairman responsible for determining the task force membership and leading the effort on behalf of the Joint Boards. The STTF has completed its work and now submits its Final Report to the SB for consideration. The report includes an overview and findings and recommendations related to the development, maintenance and replacement of synthetic turf fields throughout Fairfax County. The Final Report was prepared by the STTF for the purpose of articulating the overall considerations and presenting its findings and options related to development, maintenance and replacement of synthetic turf fields.

 

Key Points:

 

Synthetic turf field development in Fairfax County began in the early 2000’s. As youth and adult athletic programs’ participation steadily increased, the inventory of athletic fields was recognized as insufficient to meet increasing demands. However, the cost of new field development -- $600,000 to $900,000 -- and availability of sites were significant challenges to new field development. Over the past decade turf fields have been developed at FCPS and FCPA sites, which brings the turf field inventory to 67 rectangular fields, including 20 that are under construction, eleven of which are being built at five FCPS high schools. Funding for the development of these 67 fields has been provided from a variety of sources including public-private partnerships, oftentimes including community youth athletic leagues and school boosters organizations supported by the NCS mini-grant program, which leverages a portion of the NCS Athletic Services application fee revenues; private donations; public bond financing; and development proffer funds. 

 

A study conducted by staff reported that conversion of an existing lighted natural grass field to synthetic turf increases the use capacity by an additional 62 percent of playable time, as synthetic turf surfaces can be utilized year round and during some inclement weather, such as both during and after rain events. In 2009, Marshall High School became the first FCPS site to “host” the preferred two-field model which increases field availability for all users. There are now eight FCPS high schools with two turf fields and one currently under construction that will “host” the first three-field model.   

 

The current synthetic turf field inventory would not have been developed without the significant contributions by various community members and their leaders. When the current field projects are completed, the 67 turf fields will provide athletic use capacity for over 130,000 Fairfax County residents in six sports types. When the latest development phase is complete, Fairfax County will have the largest inventory of synthetic turf fields of all jurisdictions within the Washington D.C. metropolitan area. However, FCPS will still have eight high/secondary schools without synthetic turf fields to benefit their students and community members. These eight schools are: Annandale, Edison, Hayfield, Mount Vernon, South County, Stuart, Thomas Jefferson and West Potomac.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Synthetic Turf Task Force report and consider the recommendations given for synthetic turf field development, maintenance and replacement, including that fifteen synthetic turf fields be constructed at the eight remaining high/secondary schools, which will greatly help to resolve existing turf field access inequities at FCPS schools and in key areas of Fairfax County.

Subject:                  Fort Belvoir Grant Update     

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

Presently, there are 1,515 elementary aged students who are housed within Fort Belvoir. Approximately 1,133 of these students attend school at the elementary school housed on the base (program capacity of 1,140). The remaining 385 students who live on Fort Belvoir attend five other FCPS schools off base.

 

The five year projections indicate that an additional 230 students from within Fort Belvoir can be expected; exacerbating the overcrowding within the base school and the other surrounding FCPS facilities. These additional students will increase the overall elementary student yield from the base to approximately 1,750 students. In addition, the base has the ability to expand their housing by another 170 units.

 

The base has expressed their desire to keep all of their elementary aged on the base. The Department of Defense has begun issuing grants to bases and localities based upon need in an effort to meet the goal of providing educational facilities on their bases. (More detail contained in the attachment)

 

FTS and FNS have been working with staff from both Fort Belvoir and the Department of Defense in order to submit a grant to construct a new elementary school adjacent to the existing facility; with an opening in the fall of 2015. The deadline for the grant submission is September 3, 2013.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorizes the Chairman to provide a letter of commitment for the 20% Ft. Belvoir project match, meeting the grant requirement.

Subject:                    FY 2013 Final Budget Review    

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                             

 

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2013 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2013 Third Quarter Estimate. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the FY 2014 budgeted beginning balance as included in the FY 2014 Approved Budget, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the funds available are $55.7 million.

 

FY 2013 actual revenue is $7.8 million lower than the estimate and totaled 99.7 percent of the FY 2013 Third Quarter Estimate.  State aid for FY 2013 totaled $383.1 million which is $0.9 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts are projected to total $164.4 million which is $1.9 million more than budgeted.  A decrease of $11.6 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $12.3 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2014, offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of a one-time final payment for the Education Jobs Bill of $0.4 million and a one-time final payment of $0.5 million from FEMA for February 2010 storms.  Based on estimated final year-end activity, tuition from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $1.1 million, while revenue from tuition, fees, and other was $3.8 million more than budgeted.

 

Expenditures were $51.1 million less than projected after funding the School Board’s Flexibility Reserve and accounting for the textbook replacement fund and other commitments and carryover, excluding grants. A majority of this variance results from compensation accounts being $34.6 million less than budgeted due primarily to higher compensation lapse than projected; utilities expenditures being $5.2 million less than projected due to a temperate winter, spring, and early summer; county charges for vehicle services, including fuel, being $1.9 million less than projected; and a reduction in school accounts due to limiting carryover to 25 percent of the current budget.  In addition, $12.3 million in multiyear federal grant funds were unexpended.  This funding will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2014. 

 

This agenda item also includes recommendations for $11.1 million in FY 2014 expenditure adjustments leaving $44.6 million available for the FY 2015 budgeted beginning balance.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:            Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     July 15, 2013 – Work Session No. 9

 

Subject:                    Transparency Initiative

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

In response to the public’s increasing desire for openness of government, Fairfax County Public Schools and Fairfax County Government will soon be launching a new transparency initiative. FCPS and the County will each have a landing page on the web as well as a web-based financial transparency application. This joint transparency initiative has been developed by staff from Fairfax County Government and Fairfax County Public Schools.

With the creation of FCPS’ transparency landing page, residents will have one webpage that will provide easy access to both financial and programmatic accountability information. The FCPS and County web-based financial transparency applications are an interactive reporting tool that allows residents to view budget and expenditure data and specific vendor payments for the current fiscal year-to-date. The financial data for the current fiscal year will be updated on a monthly basis by the end of the following month from FCPS’ financial system, FOCUS.

FCPS’ transparency landing page and web-based application are scheduled to go-live in August 2013, and today’s presentation will provide an overview of both items.
