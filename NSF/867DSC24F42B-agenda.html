Recognizing April as Childhood Immunization Month - April 2008

The Fairfax County School Board recognizes the efficacy of immunization programs in the prevention of the spread of childhood diseases, the School Board promotes sound health practices for all children, and the School Board establishes policies that facilitate access to health services for all children.

Immunizations are available to Fairfax County parents and their children from public and private resources, and, for every $1 spent on immunizations, $10 can be saved in future medical costs.

Autism Awareness Month - April 2008

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.

Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about autism. It is sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA). Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

National Library Week/School Library Media Month - April 13-19, 2008

National Library Week will be observed April 13-19, 2008, with the theme, "Join the circle of knowledge @ your library."

First sponsored in 1958, National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association (ALA) and libraries across the country each April. It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate.

Many school libraries also celebrate the month of April as School Library Media Month sponsored by the American Association of School Librarians, a division of ALA, with the same theme as National Library Week.  National Library Workers Day, celebrated the Tuesday of National Library Week (April 15, 2008), is a day for library staff, users, administrators and friends groups to recognize the valuable contributions made by all library workers.

RESOLUTION SUPPORTING COUNTYWIDE INITIATIVE
TO REDUCE UNDERAGE DRINKING


WHEREAS, alcohol is the single most used drug by young people; and underage alcohol use is associated with traffic fatalities, violence and gang involvement, rape, depression and suicide, and educational failure,

WHEREAS, our young people report through the biennial Fairfax County Youth Survey that access to alcohol through friends and family is easy and one in every two high school seniors has consumed alcohol in the last 30 days and one in every four seniors has reported binge drinking in the last two weeks,

WHEREAS, Virginia law states that it is illegal for anyone under age 21 to buy, consume or possess alcohol; and an adult providing alcohol to a person under the age of 21, other than their own child in their own home, can face a $2,500 fine per young person served in addition to losing their driver’s license, and/or face up to 12 months in jail,

WHEREAS, the FCPS school-community coalitions have formed a task force of concerned residents, school officials, county government agencies, local law enforcement, the County Council of PTAs, Northern Virginia MADD, the Fairfax Partnership for Youth, Fairfax-Falls Church Community Services Board, faith community leaders, pediatrician associations, athletic organizations, GMU and NOVA administrators, juvenile court programs, and INOVA HealthSource to implement a countywide initiative to reduce underage drinking,

WHEREAS, the Countywide Initiative in its first year distributed over 156,000 informational materials in multiple languages, developed media pieces, advertised in local newspapers, and supported Town Hall Meetings on underage drinking and is continuing to implement the public awareness and educational campaign, known as “Parents Who Host, Lose The Most: Don’t be a party to teenage drinking,”

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board wholeheartedly supports the Unified Coalition of Fairfax County Public Schools Countywide Initiative to Reduce Underage Drinking Task Force and recognizes the month of April 2008 as “Parents Who Host, Lose The Most ” month in Fairfax County.

    Meeting
    Mar 27, 2008 - Regular Meeting No. 16
    Category
    6. New Business - 9:35 p.m.
    Type
    Information



    Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

    Meeting Type: March 27, 2008 - Regular Meeting No.16

    SUBJECT: South County Secondary School

    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:
            At the March 10, 2008, School Board work session, the Board agreed to pursue discussions and negotiations with the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors to consider adding a wing to South County Secondary School to alleviate overcrowding as soon as possible with the understanding that this would not take the concept of a stand alone South County Middle School off the table. The County would provide $10 million over two years from their sources of funds and the School Board would provide $2 million from our Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that is currently directed to planning for the middle school. The Board also agreed that a motion on this matter as new business may be considered on March 27, 2008, for action on April 10, 2008. Staff was directed to report on progress to the Board during Forum on March 13.


    KEY POINTS:
            · Due to present overcrowding and use of the building as a secondary school, a 9-period day was created to allow middle and high school students to have overlapping schedules to allow maximum use of shared space in the building
            · While the 9-period day has been effective, this schedule has placed a significant strain on school staff and cannot be continued indefinitely or considered a permanent solution.
    OPTIONS:
            1. Build a new middle school which is currently shown in the CIP for FY2014-2018 and is unfunded.
            2. Conduct a boundary study in Fall 2008 to move additional students.
            3. Build an addition onto South County Secondary similar to that added to Westfield High School.

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve a building addition to South County Secondary School using the $10 million provided by county funding and the $2 million in FCPS bond funding currently designated for the planning and design of a South County middle school, and direct staff to begin immediately to take steps to plan and complete the building addition.
