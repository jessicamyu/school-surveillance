RESOLUTION NAMING THE SPORTS COMPLEX AT

WESTFIELD HIGH SCHOOL

“THE FRANCIS L. DALL SPORTS COMPLEX”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Francis L. Dall served Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) for 32 years with excellence and dedication, working as a teacher, coach, Assistant Director and then Director of Student Activities; his leadership, enthusiasm, and commitment to FCPS and the Westfield High School community inspired countless numbers of his student-athletes and colleagues to work hard to achieve their goals and reach their full potential; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mr. Dall was the founding Director of Student Activities at Westfield High School and a visionary for the creation of the Westfield sports complex; during Mr. Dall’s tenure, Westfield athletic groups gained district, regional, and state championship titles and Westfield was awarded the VHSL Academic Wachovia Cup; he collaborated with educational, political, business, and community leaders to organize the State Jubilee, a showcase of six AAA State Championship athletic events, which Westfield continued to host for six consecutive years; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mr. Dall was a true leader, a man whose actions spoke much louder than his words; he encouraged his coaches, club sponsors, and student-athletes, to be competitive yet to act with sportsmanship, integrity, and honor, and he demonstrated those qualities through his own actions and leadership style; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mr. Dall’s success was recognized with a number of honors and awards which included: Nomination to the VHSL Hall of Fame, Fairfax High School Hall of Fame, VHSL Torch of Honor Recipient, Washington Post Donald Huff Award, VHSL Regional Award of Merit-Administrator, Westfield High School Hall of Fame, Fairfax County Football Hall of Fame, and Karl Davey Memorial Award for Community Achievement; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mr. Dall was a passionate, enthusiastic, and visionary educator; he dedicated his life to the enrichment of student-athletes and encouraged them to strive for excellence in the classroom and on the sports field; he was a mentor and friend to his colleagues and provided continuous encouragement and support as he helped them develop coaching and leadership skills;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, in memory and honor of Francis L. Dall’s dedicated service and valued contributions to FCPS and the Westfield High School Community, hereby names the sports complex at Westfield High School “The Francis L. Dall Sports Complex.”

RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING THE MONTH OF JANUARY 2015 AS

HUMAN TRAFFICKING AWARENESS MONTH IN FAIRFAX COUNTY

 

The Fairfax County School Board

 

 

     WHEREAS, human trafficking is the fastest-growing criminal enterprise in the world, with human traffickers targeting men, women and children, isolating them from society and supportive networks, and exploiting them for personal and monetary gain; and

 

     WHEREAS, victims of human trafficking suffer emotional, psychological and physical horrors at the hands of traffickers who instill fear to keep them enslaved; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Northern Virginia Human Trafficking Task Force reports that in its first year, October 2013 to September 2014, they identified 253 human trafficking victims and 69 suspects, and 44% of their leads concerned juvenile sex trafficking; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Public Schools and the Just Ask Prevention Project have instituted a teen sex trafficking prevention curriculum in grades 6-10 and 12 and developed effective awareness campaigns that have become models for other jurisdictions; and

 

     WHEREAS, student groups at Fairfax County high schools are raising awareness about teen sex trafficking and activating thousands of teens through their student-run campaigns;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board recognizes the month of January 2015 as Human Trafficking Awareness Month in Fairfax County and supports the efforts of all those who are working to eliminate human trafficking.

Subject:                 Revised Beliefs and Mission

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key points:

 

At the School Board’s retreat on July 18-19, 2014, there was a review of the Board’s beliefs, vision, and mission.  There were suggested revisions to the beliefs and mission, and agreement that those revisions would be discussed at a future date.  There were no recommended changes to the vision statement.

 

The Governance Committee reviewed the suggested revisions on November 24, 2014, and at the December 8 work session, the Committee recommended to the full Board that the revisions to the beliefs and mission be approved. 

 

The changes to the beliefs included a new list order of the beliefs; the proposed beliefs and the current beliefs are listed below, with changes marked. One additional revision—to add the word “and ethical” in the last belief—was to be consistent with Board action when the Portrait of a Graduate was approved.

Contact:                  Ilryong Moon, At Large Board Member

 

Meeting Category:  January 8, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 11

 

Subject:                  School Board Member Salary

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

According to the Code of Virginia, any change in the salary of School Board members is required to be approved by the School Board prior to July 1 in the year in which there is a School Board election and by a recorded vote of a majority of the School Board members present at a public meeting.  Moreover, such change only affects the salary of the School Board members elected in the new election (for example, the election is scheduled in November 2015, and those elected will take office effective January 1, 2016).  A sitting school board cannot raise the salary for its own members and, therefore, the salary increases being proposed are for the next School Board (2016-2019 term) and not the current School Board (2012-2015 term). 

 

By law, the salary of the members of the Fairfax County School Board shall not exceed the salary of the members of the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors (which is currently $75,000, effective January 1, 2008), with the exception that the School Board may vote to pay its chairman an additional salary not to exceed $2,000 per year.  Salaries for School Board members were last increased in 2007 from $12,000 to $20,000 per year, and prior to that in 1999 from $8,000 to $12,000 per year.  If a salary increase is not approved by this School Board by June 30, 2015, the next opportunity will not be available for another four years.

 

Board members are responsible for setting general school policy, approving the annual budget, and hiring the Superintendent.  Board members spend many hours attending Board, school, PTA, and community meetings, visiting schools, reviewing meeting materials in preparation for Board meetings, attending required professional development programs, and communicating with students, parents, staff, and community members. 

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is the 10th largest school district in the nation and with over 185,000 students is the largest school district in Virginia, more than twice as large as the next largest Virginia district.  The School Board members are responsible for overseeing an annual budget of more than $2.5 billion and serving over 1.1 million constituents.  FCPS is also the second largest employer in the Commonwealth of Virginia.

 

Recommendation:

 

That, effective January 1, 2016, the annual salary for each member of the Fairfax County School Board be set at $40,000, and that the School Board Chairman be paid an additional annual salary of $2,000.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
