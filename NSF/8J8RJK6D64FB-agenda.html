Veterans Day - November 11, 2011

 

Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.

 

American Education Week - November 13-19, 2011

 

This is the 90th anniversary of American Education Week, established in 1921 by the National Education Association (NEA) to raise public awareness of the importance of public education in America.  The organization’s annual tagline, Great Public Schools:  A Basic Right and Our Responsibility, focuses on the critical role parents play, the invaluable services provided by education support professionals, and the importance of substitute teachers.  As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week spotlights the importance of providing each child in America with a quality public education from kindergarten through college, along with the need for everyone to do his or her part to make public schools great.

 

Geography Awareness Week - November 13-19, 2011

 

Geography Awareness Week is an opportunity to focus on geography as a critical component of a 21st century education.  The 2011 theme promotes the idea that using geography can be used to understand every community, no matter its size or age. 

 

Geography Awareness Week was established by presidential proclamation in 1987 to underscore the importance of geographic understanding to ensure our nation’s economic competitiveness, national security, environmental sustainability, and livability of our communities in the 21st century.

 

Bullying Prevention Awareness Week - November 14-18, 2011

 

Bullying Prevention Awareness Week is intended to raise awareness about bullying while promoting positive relationships and providing youth with effective strategies for responding to these negative behaviors. This week is consistent with FCPS efforts to ensure a safe and positive learning environment in all schools.  Schools are encouraged to highlight bullying prevention and intervention efforts with students, staff, and parents during Bullying Prevention Awareness Week.

School Psychology Awareness Week - November 14 –18, 2011

 

The theme of this year’s School Psychology Awareness Week, "Every link matters.  Make a connection.” focuses on the work school psychologists do to help students strengthen positive relationships and increase positive connections within the school community, their academics and their lives.

 

School psychologists work with students in early childhood settings and elementary and secondary schools.  They collaborate with teachers, parents, and school personnel to create safe, healthy, and supportive learning environments for all students.  School psychologists address students' learning and behavioral problems, suggest improvements to classroom management strategies or parenting techniques, and evaluate students with disabilities and advanced academic students to help determine the best way to educate them.

They improve teaching, learning, and socialization strategies based on their understanding of the psychology of learning environments.  They also may evaluate the effectiveness of academic programs, prevention programs, behavior management procedures, and other services provided in the school setting.

6.01 Amendment 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County (ERFC) Defined Benefit Plan - Recommendation to approve Amendment 2 to the ERFC for the purpose of preserving ERFC’s status as a source of supplemental benefits only. (HR; action scheduled 11/21/11)
Meeting
Nov 10, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 7
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:        Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category: November 10, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 7

 

Subject:                  Amendment 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County (ERFC) Defined Benefit Plan

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related to:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending an amendment to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC) as the result of a VRS amendment that the Virginia Legislature adopted in the 2011 Session.  The proposed ERFC amendment would maintain ERFC’s status as a source only of supplemental benefits.

 

ERFC, the Legacy Plan, was designed to supplement benefits that members receive from VRS. The benefit formulas achieve this purpose by specifying a percentage of the member’s final average salary (the “total promised benefit”) and calculating the ERFC benefit as that total amount minus the amount that VRS is expected to pay based on the member’s creditable service under VRS (“the VRS benefit reduction”). 

 

When the benefit formulas were drafted, there was only one circumstance in which a member might forfeit his or her VRS service credit, with the result that the VRS benefit reduction would be zero.  That circumstance was the member’s voluntary withdrawal of accumulated VRS member contributions. In order to protect ERFC from paying the total promised benefit in such a situation, the ERFC Plan document provides that such forfeited VRS service credit continues to be counted in calculating the VRS benefit reduction. 

 

In the 2011 Legislative Session, the Virginia Legislature amended the VRS statute to add another way that a member’s VRS service credits can be forfeited.  An amendment to Virginia Code § 51.1-124.13 provides that a VRS member who commits a felony in connection with his or her VRS-covered employment after July 1, 2011, will lose the right to VRS benefits based on service previously accumulated.  All VRS service credit up to that date is considered to have been “relinquished.” 

 

In order to make certain that any such relinquishment of VRS service credit will not result in a VRS benefit reduction equal to zero, the Trustees recommend a clarifying amendment to the ERFC Plan document.  The recommended amendment would provide that any service credit relinquished pursuant to Virginia Code § 51.1-124.13 will be counted in calculating the VRS benefit reduction, in the same manner that service forfeited by withdrawal of VRS contributions is counted.

 

No similar amendment is needed for ERFC 2001 Plan document because  ERFC 2001 benefits are based on a different formula that does not include a VRS benefit reduction.

 

A proposed Amendment Number 2 is attached for review and approval.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan for the purpose of preserving ERFC’s status as a source of supplemental benefits only.

Staff Contact:         Phyllis Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Meeting Category:  November 10, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 7

 

Subject:                 School Board Policy 4510, Communication with Employee Organizations

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related to:             Legal Requirement

Key Points:

 

The School Board adopted Policy 4510 on July 1, 1986, to establish lines of communication between the School Board and employee organizations.  The Governance Committee discussed the role of the School Board in discussions with employee associations at the June 15 and September 12, 2011, work sessions.  After these discussions, it was the consensus of the Governance Committee to discuss the proposed revisions to Policy 4510 with the entire Board at the October 17 work session.  The policy has been revised to invite the School Board Chair, or his or her designee, to meetings with employee organizations and committees.

 

The consensus of the Board at the October 17 work session was to recommend approval of the revisions as presented, and to add wording to the recommendation to allow the newly-elected Board the opportunity to review and provide further recommendations by July 31, 2012.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 4510.3, Communication with Employee Organizations, as detailed in the agenda item; and further move that the School Board review this policy by July 31, 2012.

Staff Contact:        Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category: November 10, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 7

 

Subject:                  Sale of School Bonds 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information            

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County’s Department of Management and Budget has advised school system staff that the Board of Supervisors plans to sell general obligation bonds in the near future.  The Board of Supervisors is scheduled to authorize the issuance and sale at its meeting on January 16, 2012.  School system staff recommends that school bonds be sold at the same time as the general obligation bonds are sold.  A resolution prepared by bond counsel for Fairfax County, and reviewed and approved by Division Counsel, will be provided at the November 21, 2011, School Board meeting (the “Resolution”).

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve and adopt the Resolution, requesting that the Board of Supervisors issue and sell school bonds in the amount of $167,300,000, on behalf of the Fairfax County Public Schools.
