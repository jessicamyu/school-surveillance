                                    Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

                                    Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Meeting Category: September 19, 2016 - Work Session

 

Subject: ERFC Options

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

 

Key Points: 

 

FCPS has hired Aon Hewitt, a provider of retirement consulting and actuarial services, to conduct a study which benchmarks FCPS’ retirement benefit levels to surrounding school divisions, Fairfax County, and the federal government.  The study also included the adequacy of income replacement at retirement provided by the retirement programs that FCPS offers. 

 

On May 12, 2016, ERFC presented the history of the FCPS retirement programs along with the key provisions of the existing plans.  Aon Hewitt then provided a briefing on the results of the study, illustrating retirement benefit levels for FCPS employees at various age and service combinations with comparisons to the peer group.  Aon Hewitt also presented ERFC options for possible future consideration.

 

On July 21, 2016, as a follow-up to the May work session and as requested by the School Board, the benefit impact of the options for possible consideration were presented. 

 

At this work session, the employer cost savings associated with those options will be discussed.

Staff Contact:  Francisco Duran, chief academic officer

     Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department  

                                                                          

Other Staff Present:  Jennifer Glaser, director, Counseling and College and Career Readiness

                               

Meeting Category:  September 19, 2016 – School Board Work Session

 

Subject: Pathways to Graduation

 

School Board Action Required: Information and Review of Local Graduation Requirements to Provide Increased Flexibility for Students                   

 

Related To: Strategic Plan, Goal 1: Student Success

 

Key Points:    

 

The proposal for multiple pathways to graduation includes additional flexibility for local FCPS requirements while also adding options for students to pursue a pathway related to one of the nationally recognized career clusters. The pathway proposal addresses goals outlined in the strategic plan and legislative priorities related to college and career readiness while ensuring that additional flexibility in graduation requirements does not limit a student’s postsecondary opportunities. The proposal aligns with current workforce trends, FCPS student interests, and existing course offerings to increase the likelihood of community and industry support, student engagement and participation in the pathway options, and a school’s ability to successfully implement the proposed changes.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board support additional flexibility in the local FCPS graduation requirements including pathway options, adopt a policy to grant waivers from the 140 seat hour requirement, and continue advocacy to the Virginia Board of Education for additional course and exam substitutions.
