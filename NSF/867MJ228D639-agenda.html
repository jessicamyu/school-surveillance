RESOLUTION OF COMMENDATION FOR
STEPHEN HOCKETT

WHEREAS, Stephen Hockett, principal of McNair Elementary School, is an inspiring educational leader, and an outstanding school administrator and a visible and involved principal who delights in meeting and greeting students, staff members, and parents with a bright smile and a hearty handshake; and
WHEREAS, under Mr. Hockett’s guidance and direction, Hunters Woods Elementary School was successfully renovated with community acceptance and approval, and Mr. Hockett developed and found funding for a unique distance learning laboratory for students and was able to find funding to keep the school library open during the summer months; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hockett knows how to balance two full-time jobs as he demonstrated by teaching a fourth grade class for almost six weeks until a new teacher was hired; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Hockett’s ability to show students that learning can be fun is evidenced by Sunshine Emergency drills that cause students to rush outside on sunny days to read for 20 minutes;
WHEREAS, Mr. Hockett’s positive, caring, and can-do attitude permeates everything he does, and he knows the value of collaborative decision making and promotes creative professional development opportunities;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the School Board of Fairfax County, Virginia, honors Stephen Hockett as the 2005-2006 Fairfax County Public Schools Principal of the Year and the recipient of The Washington Post Distinguished Educational Leadership Award. The Board expresses its appreciation to Mr. Hockett for his leadership and bestows on him its highest commendation for having achieved these distinctions.

National Mentoring Month

The future course of our nation rests on the shoulders of its youth, and educated, confident, and nurtured children will give rise to a stronger nation. Mentoring is a proven, effective strategy matching a caring, responsible adult with a student to provide guidance and build confidence, stability, and direction for that student. Research has shown that mentoring has a definitive impact on young people by increasing attendance at school, improving rates of high school graduation and college attendance, and decreasing involvement with drugs, alcohol, and violent behaviors.

National Mentoring Month emphasizes the importance of mentoring and recognizes with praise and gratitude the many Americans that are involved in mentoring.

    That the School Board award the contract for emergency power upgrades at Chantilly and Marshall High Schools, in support of the FY 2004 State Homeland Security Grant Program, to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder(s) and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.

EXPLANATION:
    Fairfax County emergency plans call for the potential use of schools as shelter sites, managed by the American Red Cross.  In the past, such as during hurricane Isabel, it was determined that the schools’ emergency electrical systems did not meet the operational needs of the shelter.  Normally, only life-safety systems are connected to emergency generators.  Shelter operations require many other circuits to be operational, such as lighting, HVAC, food service, etc.  Fairfax County has recognized this logistical need and approved this project through the Fairfax County Emergency Management Committee.  The initiative will provide for the expansion of emergency electrical generation for Chantilly and Marshall High Schools.
 
    Funding for this project will be provided from the FY 2004 State Homeland Security Grant.  Specifications have been prepared and the project has been advertised.  Bids are due to be received on November 22, 2005.  A bid tabulation and recommendation for award(s) will be furnished prior to the January 12, 2006, School Board meeting.

Meeting

Category
5. Consent Agenda - 8:15 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)

Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

SUBJECT: Amendment to ERFC and ERFC-2001


RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve proposed Amendment Number 11 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC-2001)for the purpose of A.) providing for updates in the assumptions used in calculating actuarial equivalent benefits; and B.) authorizing the Trustees to adopt a benefit restoration plan if and when the Legislature authorizes local governments that participate in the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) to do so.

      A summary of the proposed amendments in each category is provided below.

A. Providing for updates in the assumptions used in calculating actuarial equivalent benefits.
    Each plan provides optional forms of benefits that can be elected by members at retirement. For example, a benefit form similar to one available to federal employees permits a retiring member to elect a partial lump sum up to the amount of the member’s accumulated contributions. The member’s annuity is then reduced by the actuarial equivalent of that lump sum payment. The interest rate index that is used in calculating that reduction was adopted at a time when interest rates were close to the Plan’s actuarial interest-rate assumption. At most times since it was adopted, the interest rates produced by that index have been lower than the Plan’s actuarial interest-rate assumption. The Trustees have recently reviewed that interest rate and concluded that the benefit will be more truly cost-neutral to the Plan if the Plan’s actuarial interest-rate assumption is used instead. The change that they propose is expected to yield a small long-term saving for the plan.

    In the course of preparing proposed amendments to revise that interest rate, the Trustees have determined that a more general change in the current plan provisions would assist them in maintaining the actuarial equivalency among various optional forms of benefits. The current plan documents contain specific actuarial assumptions, or refer to particular actuarial valuations from which the assumptions are drawn. Whenever the Trustees determine that the existing assumptions are not yielding true actuarial equivalence, they must propose plan amendments for the School Board’s approval.

    The proposed amendment will remove from the plan documents specific assumptions used for calculating actuarial equivalents. It will require the Trustees to adopt regulations prescribing appropriate assumptions to maintain the actuarial equivalence of the optional forms of benefits. If this amendment is approved, the Trustees will be able to update the actuarial assumptions used for that purpose in the future without having to amend the Plan.
B. Authorizing the Trustees to Establish a Benefit Restoration Plan
    Section 415 of the Internal Revenue Code in some circumstances may prohibit a qualified pension plan (which ERFC and ERFC-2001 are) from paying benefits in the full amounts that are provided for by the plan benefit formulas. That raises special concerns in a plan to which members are required to make contributions. VRS has addressed the same problem at the state level by obtaining authorization from the Virginia Legislature to establish a benefit restoration plan under Section 415(m) of the Internal Revenue Code. Such a plan is permitted to pay any amount of benefits that are provided by the plan benefit formulas but that cannot be paid by the qualified plan under Section 415. Those “restored” benefits are paid out of the employer’s contributions. Currently, Section 51.1-1302 of the Virginia Code does not provide for local governments participating in VRS to establish benefit restoration plans for their supplemental benefits, but VRS will submit a proposed amendment to the Virginia Legislature in the upcoming session to authorize the establishment of benefit restoration plans by local governments participating in VRS. The proposed amendment to ERFC and ERFC-2001 would authorize the Board of Trustees to adopt such a plan if and when it is permitted by the Legislature.
