Subject: Family Engagement Policy and Plan

 

Staff Contacts: Francisco Durán, chief equity officer

Other Staff Present: Leona Smith, director, Equity and Family Engagement

                                 Melanie Crosby-Hardy, Family Engagement Region Representative

                                 Mike Woltz, Title I Parent Advisory Committee Chair

 

Meeting Category: February 10, 2020 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Caring Culture and Student Success

 

Problem Statement:

 

This would be the first time FCPS has implemented a Family Engagement policy, which will inform family engagement practices across the division. Following the first administration of the FCPS Family Engagement Survey, it is important for the FCPS community to understand the principles and beliefs around family engagement, leading to specific actions of the division.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

 

The FCPS Family Engagement policy is based on the National Standards for Family and School Partnerships, including welcoming all families, communicating effectively, supporting student success, speaking up for every child, sharing power, and collaborating with the community, and includes the USDOE Dual Capacity framework, which ensures that family engagement is relational, builds capacity of families and staff, and is linked to student learning.

 

The Office of Professional Learning and Family Engagement staff researched family engagement policies from surrounding school divisions, as well as those outside of the region. Additionally, diverse stakeholder groups were convened to provide feedback and input to the policy.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board adopt the Family Engagement policy, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: Merits of Engaging an Independent Third-Party Wireless Telecommunications Expert on the Evaluation of all FCPS Telecommunications Facilities Applications

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Bryan Botello, planner, Fairfax County Department of Planning and Development – 

                                     Telecommunications 2232 Application Process Presentation

                                 Ken Schmidt, J.D., President-Steel in the Air (SITA) Independent Third-Party Wireless

                                     Telecommunications Expert Presentation

                         Bob Cordova, coordinator, Office of Design and Construction

                         Tom Casey, wireless infrastructure specialist, Office of Design and Construction

 

 

Meeting Category: February 10, 2020 – Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

The School Board directed staff to review and report on the merits of engaging an independent, third-party wireless telecommunications expert to consult on the evaluation of all FCPS telecommunications facilities applications, including:

  1. Validation of the need for additional wireless telecommunications coverage related to the area of the specific application, and

  2. Recommendations to the physical placement of any telecommunications facility on any FCPS site.

A presentation on existing FCPS and County review/approval processes as they pertain to wireless telecommunications facility applications will be provided as well as a presentation by an independent third-party wireless telecommunications expert. Discussion of the subject matter will follow the presentations

 

 

Recommendation:

That engaging an independent third-party wireless telecommunications expert to consult on the evaluation of all FCPS telecommunications facilities applications is not recommended at this time. Staff expertise (both Fairfax County and FCPS) in combination with Fairfax County’s land use review/approval process and FCPS’s policy, regulations and vetting processes, precludes the need for such independent services.
