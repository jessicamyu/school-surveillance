2.02 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship
Meeting
Jun 5, 2008 - Regular Meeting No. 21
Category
2. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type
Information


Laura Ashley Piper, was a former student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987, a 1987 graduate of Robinson Secondary School, an Air Force Academy graduate, and a commissioned Second Lieutenant in the Air Force, and was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. In recognition of this outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader, the School Board in 1994 established an annual $1,000 scholarship to honor Laura’s strong commitment to student participation, her high standards of achievement, and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime.

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Guidance and Career Services Office, Department of Special Services, considers the nominees. The Braddock District School Board member – Tessie Wilson – served on the selection committee. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

RESOLUTION
NAMING
ASIA ECHARD
THE 2008 LAURA ASHLEY PIPER SCHOLARSHIP RECIPIENT


WHEREAS, Asia Echard has, through her years at Robinson Secondary School, remained consistently committed to excellence in academics, sports, music, and student leadership responsibilities; and
WHEREAS, her extraordinary athletic talents have brought her membership on the Robinson Secondary School varsity basketball and outdoor track teams, as well as the distinctions of 2007 Concord District discus champion and 2007 Concord District shot put All-District, varsity letters for three years in basketball and four years in outdoor track, and participation in AAA states competition for four years and the 2007 AAA states ninth place discus winner; and
WHEREAS, she has contributed greatly to her community and the world around her by running a summer strings studio teaching young violin and viola students, organizing strings recitals in the community, volunteering with a basketball league and club, and sharing her insights in an article in the American String Teacher journal titled “On Mentorship: Sharing the Gift of Music—A Perspective From a Teen-Age Strings Teacher”; and
WHEREAS, she has shown her great potential for continued academic success by maintaining a 3.8 grade point average, by earning a varsity letter in academics, by participating in the International Baccalaureate program and being a student who will have completed seven IB courses by graduation, by being chosen for membership in the National Honor Society, by earning the Athletic Scholars Award, by being named to the Robinson Honor Roll for four years, by earning Robinson’s “All A’s Award” in mathematics, English, and science, and by actively participating in the Robinson Advanced Orchestra for four years; and
WHEREAS, her determination, strong work ethic, and ability to lead by example have earned her the admiration of her peers and teachers, and her school counselor describes her as “a true leader, an admired peer model, and an outstanding student. . . [who] is one of the most enthusiastic seniors at Robinson,” and through her consistently excellent achievements, her commitment to working hard, and her potential for continued leadership and involvement throughout her life, she in every way personifies the characteristics and ideals to which Laura Ashley Piper adhered;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board awards the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship to Asia Echard in memory of Lt. Piper and in recognition of the drive, determination, and dedication to excellence of both.

    Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director
    MEETING TYPE: June 5, 2008 – Regular Meeting No. 21
    SUBJECT: Amendments to ERFC and ERFC 2001and to the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County Benefit Restoration Plan (BRP)
    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:
      The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending several technical amendments to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) and to the ERFC Benefit Restoration Plan (BRP).

    A summary of the proposed amendments in each category is provided below.
        A. Describing How the Plan Complies with Certain Regulations Recently Issued by the Internal Revenue Service.
        The proposed amendments in paragraphs 6 through 9 (amending sections 17.01 and 17.03 of ERFC and sections 12.01 and 12.03 of ERFC 2001) are for the purpose of specifying how ERFC and ERFC 2001 will comply with the final regulations that IRS recently issued regarding the limits on benefits payable under defined benefit plans. The new regulations are complex and require each plan to adopt plan language specifying how it will comply with the regulations with regard to certain practices where the regulations permit alternative methods of compliance.

        To the extent that the regulations require such specification, the proposed amendments specify the practices that ERFC and ERFC 2001 will follow.

        If approved, these proposed amendments would be effective January 1, 2009. The IRS regulations permit public employee plans to adopt a delayed effective date, measured from the end of the first legislative session beginning after the final regulations were published. Because benefits under ERFC and ERFC 2001 must be aggregated, for at least some purposes, with benefits payable from VRS, and VRS benefits can be amended only in Virginia Legislative sessions, the effective date for the amendments to ERFC and ERFC 2001 will conform to the rule applicable to VRS amendments.

        B. Enabling the Retirement Office to Simplify an Interest Calculation for Certain Refunds
        In certain limited circumstances, ERFC permits members to obtain refunds of money they have paid for purchased service credit, with interest. Such refunds are infrequent. The current process of designing a new automated system for plan administration has identified a simplified method of calculating the interest on those refunds that would significantly reduce the administrative burden with only a negligible increase in the amount of the interest. The proposed amendment in paragraph 3 (amending section 11.07 of ERFC) would allow the trustees to adopt a regulation implementing that simplified method for calculating interest.
        C. Making Other Technical Corrections and/or Clarifications without Changing Benefits or Administrative Practices
        The proposed amendments in paragraphs 1, 2, 4, and 5 (amending sections 11.01, 11.03, and 12.02 of ERFC and section 7.02 of ERFC 2001) are solely for the purpose of making technical corrections (such as correcting cross-references) and clarifying plan provisions without affecting benefits or administrative practices.
        D. Clarification in the Benefit Restoration Plan
        Proposed Amendment Number 1 to the BRP is for the purpose of clarifying that the School Board has retained broad discretion, in the event it ever were to terminate the BRP, to decide whether and in what form to continue payment of any benefits under that plan.
        FOR YOUR INFORMATION:
        Before January 31, 2009, the plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001 must be submitted to the Internal Revenue Service for a determination that the plan continues to comply with applicable provisions of the Internal Revenue Code. In order to make that submission, ERFC will prepare restated plan documents (which will simply incorporate into each document all of the amendments that the School Board has approved since the last restatement, which occurred in 2001) and submit them to the School Board for formal approval. ERFC expects to submit those restated documents for approval in December 2008.
    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board:
    (1) approve proposed Amendment Number 14 to ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purposes of (A) describing how the plan complies with certain regulations recently issued by the Internal Revenue Service; (B) enabling the retirement office to simplify an interest calculation for certain refunds; and (C) making other technical corrections and/or clarifications without changing benefits or administrative practices, and

        (2) approve proposed Amendment Number 1 to BRP to clarify certain provisions.

    SUBJECT: South County Secondary School

    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:

        The South County Secondary School (SCSS) is operating on a 9-period day as a means to manage its overcrowding. While designed to be a high school with a capacity of 2,500 students, it has operated since it opened in 2005 as a secondary school. Its current enrollment is approximately 2,960 students. It is necessary to provide relief to the school either by enhancing capacity or by reducing enrollment, or some combination thereof.

    KEY POINTS:
            · Comparison of projected and actual student yield in the South County Secondary School attendance area.
            · Impacts of anticipated residential growth, by-right development and rezoning.
            · Impacts of BRAC (Base Realignment and Closure).
            · Projected enrollments for South County Middle School’s feeder schools.

    OPTIONS: 
        Solution options that were considered:
              1.              Boundary changes.

              2.              Build a new middle school to serve this area.

              3.              Build an addition to the existing school.

              4.              Relocate programs.

        Based upon the fact that we do not project significant increases in enrollment at SCSS, we recommend an addition to the existing school.  This option is less expensive than building a new middle school and will provide sufficient capacity to meet the enrollment needs of the school in the near term.  Assuming cooperation from the county and the absence of unusual circumstances, we believe the addition can be built in time for the opening of the 2010-2011 school year. 
     RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board approve an addition to South County Secondary School to meet enrollment needs of the school in the near term and as a less expensive option to building a new middle school.
