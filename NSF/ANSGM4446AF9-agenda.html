Subject: English Learner Study

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

Current national, state, and local growth trends for English learners have prompted questions about the needs and services provided by school divisions for this population. In this context, Fairfax County Public Schools made a commitment in its Strategic Plan to expand its knowledge about the needs and performance of students receiving the services for English learners and those participating in a new high school pilot program. A three year study was undertaken by the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement (formerly the Office of Program Evaluation). Year 1 of the study was presented to the School Board during the 2015-16 school year. Year 2 of the study was scheduled for presentation in June 2017 but postponed due to time constraints in the School Board’s agenda. Postponement allowed analysis of a third year of data, which is captured in an addendum to the Year 2 report.

 

This final reporting on the English Learner Study combines the results from Years 1 and 2, as well as the addendum to provide:

  • An examination of how well FCPS services align with best practices in their design and implementation,
  • An analysis of outcomes for English learners, including those participating in the revised ESOL high school program, and
  • A description of costs, including the return on investment associated with the delivery of the services.

Based on the final findings and conclusions, the following recommendations are made to the Superintendent:

 

  1. Designate a single FCPS division-level unit to oversee divisionwide coordination of the delivery of all three learning experiences for English learner students.
  2. Direct this division-level unit to explore the concerns of schools about the constraints of time and personnel. If deemed necessary, propose alternatives to time, personnel, or the delivery of English learner services to provide greater flexibility in use of time and personnel, particularly at the elementary level, and report findings to the Superintendent, including alternatives, costs, benefits, and challenges of each alternative.
  3. Direct this division-level unit to create, disseminate, and monitor specific expectations to schools for managing organizational structures and learning experiences for English learner students at elementary, middle, and high school levels.
  4. Ensure that the Division’s professional development plan includes opportunities to increase school staff knowledge and skills in using time, space, and personnel more effectively for English learner students, as well as the provision of the set of three learning experiences for students.
  5. Consider revising the high school level school counselor staffing standard such that it uses a weighted needs-based staffing factor for English learner Level 1 and 2 students, as repurposed or new funding allows.

Subject: Standards-Based Study of FCPS’ Professional Development

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

School systems have historically found great utility in providing professional development for staff, particularly instructional staff. In fact, professional development experts espouse the belief that notable improvements in education rarely take place in the absence of professional development. Thus, organizations targeting improvements or advances typically rely upon professional development as a foundational strategy. In this context, Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) made several commitments regarding professional development in its Strategic Plan, Ignite. Specifically, Ignite committed FCPS to a comprehensive evaluation of FCPS’ professional development functioning and performance in relation to supporting the improvements and advances forwarded by Ignite. The information presented here represents the Year 1 reporting for the comprehensive evaluation study undertaken by the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement (ORSI).

 

The Year 1 reporting provides:

A description of research-based standards for an organization’s professional development system to function strategically

  • An analysis of FCPS’ current professional development functioning in relation to the identified standards
  • An investigation of outcomes associated with FCPS’ professional development offerings, and
  • A description of costs, including the return on investment, associated with the FCPS’ current professional development.

Based on the final findings and conclusions, the following recommendations are made to the Superintendent:

 

  1. Establish a work group with representatives from all instructional and operational departments to develop and submit:
    • an action plan for development and implementation of a strategic PD system focused around meeting the PD standards for cultural norms, organizational infrastructure and PD offerings;
    • recommendations for agreed upon authority and responsibilities of the various units involved in FCPS' PD approach, especially OPLFE, schools, regions and central office;
    • recommendations to equitably balance PD for both instructional and operational staff in support of FCPS’ Ignite goals.
  2. Request an alignment study of the goals and desired outcomes in Ignite with the PD offerings currently available and needed to meet those strategic goals and objectives.
  3. Direct staff to develop a data-driven approach for gauging PD's functioning and performance that would allow FCPS to engage in continuous improvement practices for PD.
  4. Ensure PD days allocated for school-based staff target strategic aims.
