Key Points:

 

FY 2018 Advertised Budget

On February 9, 2017, the School Board adopted the Advertised Budget for the School Operating Fund totaling $2.8 billion. This is an increase of $135.1 million, or 5.1 percent, over the FY 2017 Approved Budget.

 

For FY 2018, funds are budgeted that will allow FCPS to meet the basic needs of the school system and make continued investments in our teachers and in our classrooms in critical areas. Expenditure increases include:

  • step increase for eligible employees - $44.0 million
  • new teacher salary scale implemented over four years - $33.2 million
  • new salary scales for school-based administrators, classroom instructional support positions (i.e., instructional assistants, public health training assistants, and public health attendants), and for other unified scale positions - $7.0 million
  • one percent market scale adjustment for nonteacher scales - $7.9 million
  • enrollment and student demographics - $10.0 million
  • support for current preschool classrooms - $0.5 million
  • increases for the Virginia Retirement System (VRS), Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System (ERFC) and Fairfax County Employees’ Retirement System (FCERS) rates - $41.2 million
  • employee health coverage - $4.7 million
  • textbook adoption for social studies - $7.8 million, of which $2.7 million is a new investment
  • an assessment tool, universal screener, to identify remediation and accelerations needs - $2.4 million
  • program operations and infrastructure - $3.8 million

Collectively, these expenditure increases are offset by expenditure reductions totaling $27.5 million. The FY 2018 Advertised Budget includes a requested increase in the county transfer of $112.5 million, or 5.9 percent.

 

County Executive’s Advertised Budget

The County Executive presented his FY 2018 Advertised Budget Plan on February 14, 2017, which included a School Operating Fund transfer increase of $51.7 million, or 2.7 percent, which leaves a gap of $60.9 million.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The final state budget results in an increase in state funding of $4.2 million as compared to FCPS’ FY 2018 Advertised Budget.

 

Projected Gap from the March Work Session

As presented at the March 13, 2017, work session, the projected shortfall in the FY 2018 Advertised Budget is $48.4 million. This includes the 2.7 percent transfer increase as recommended by the County Executive, the impact of the final state budget, and the impacts from FCPS’ FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

Discussion Items for This Work Session

  • Updates from the April 21, 2017, County Board of Supervisors Budget Pre-Markup will be provided
  • The projected gap will be updated by:
    • The County’s budget pre-mark-up
    • Enrollment update from spring projections
    • Adjustments to budget reductions for class size and the class size formula floor. These reductions are currently being planned for and are impacted by spring enrollment projections
  • Discuss options for closing the gap:
    • Reductions and fees on the Superintendent’s list of potential reductions that have not already been discussed will be reviewed to determine Board consensus for staff to begin planning for implementation
    • Address questions regarding compensation adjustments based on the tool provided to the Board at the March 13th work session
  • Next steps
    • Summarize outstanding budget questions and prioritize any new questions from today’s work session
    • Review FCPS’ timeline for remaining budget work sessions, public hearings, and board meetings
