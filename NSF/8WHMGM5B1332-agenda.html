National School Lunch Week, October 15-19, 2012

Using the theme School Lunch-What’s Cooking?, the 2012 National School Lunch Week is a celebration of the positive changes that are being made in school lunch programs.  The theme encourages school districts to showcase new meal patterns, chef-inspired recipes, and freshly prepared meals.  The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own nutrition habits, in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

Teen Read Week, October 14-20, 2012

 

Teen Read Week encourages teens to go beyond the traditional book and explore poetry, audiobooks, books about music, and more at their school and local library.  The 2012 theme is It Came From the Library! and dares teens to read for the fun of it.  Teen Read Week has been observed since 1998 in an effort to engage teens in reading for pleasure and fun and to learn about the free resources that are available at the library.  Research shows that teens who read for fun have better test scores and are more likely to succeed in the workforce.  The School Board encourages all FCPS teenage students to take advantage of the resources in their school and public library and to become lifelong readers and learners.

National School Bus Safety Week - October 22 - 26, 2012

The week of October 22 - 26 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. Based on the theme, “I See the Driver.  The Driver Sees Me,” the week focuses on student safety when boarding and leaving a school bus.  School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 110,000 students in over 1,500 school buses traveling nearly 14 million operational miles each year.

The School Board recognizes the hard work and unending dedication of the custodians who work for Fairfax County Public Schools. Staff Contact:            Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services      

 

Other Staff Present:  Anthony E. Crosby, director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:     October 11, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 5

 

Subject:                    Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 10, 2012, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2012 session of the General Assembly.  Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

2012 VA Code Changes relevant to FCPS:

House Bill 1222, Code of Virginia §2.2-4301 and §2.2-4344, definition of employment services organizations.  Updates outdated language referring to sheltered workshops by replacing that term with "employment services organizations." The bill defines employment services organizations. The bill also replaces the term "handicapped" with "individuals with disabilities."

County’s Administrative Changes

Contractual Disputes:  Changes the identity of the party authorized to make a decision on a claim from “public body” to “county purchasing agent.”  This will make the term consistent with the remainder of the provision.

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, and 2011 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2012 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

  1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  1. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2012 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  October 11, 2012, Regular Meeting No. 5

 

Subject:                 Authority to Execute Proffers for Garfield Elementary School

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools' staff, on behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, has filed Proffered Condition Amendment ("PCA") Application No. PCA 89-L-008 to amend the proffers previously approved for the Garfield Elementary School site to increase the maximum permitted gross floor area on the property to allow an expansion of the existing school building.  Garfield Elementary School was approved for renovation in the 2011 School Bond referendum.  The property was rezoned in 1989 subject to proffers that included a limitation on the amount of allowed future gross floor area (“GFA”).  Although the approved proffers allow for future expansion, the proposed renovation/expansion exceeds the permitted GFA by 4,064 square feet.  The existing school is a one and two story, U-shaped building with a gross floor area of 58,881 square feet.  The purpose of the expansion is to provide additional classroom space on the rear portion of the building along Old Keene Mill Road as well as to accommodate a renovation of the front portion of the building to relocate the main entrance, expand the administrative offices and provide for additional storage and mechanical equipment.  The cumulative gross floor area for the proposed expansion/renovation is approximately 19,000 square feet. The proffer statement (the "Proffers") associated with the PCA must be submitted to the County 30 days prior to the Board of Supervisors public hearing, which has been scheduled for November 20, 2012 on an expedited basis.  School Board counsel has been coordinating with Facilities and Transportation Services in the drafting of the attached draft Proffers.  The Proffers will likely require minor revisions as the PCA application is reviewed by County Staff and the Planning Commission in preparation for the Board of Supervisors public hearing.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Chief Operating Officer and his staff to take all actions necessary to pursue approval of Proffered Condition Amendment Application No. PCA 89-L-008 in conjunction with School Board counsel, and to negotiate, finalize and execute the final Proffers as approved by School Board counsel.
