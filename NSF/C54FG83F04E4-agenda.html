Meeting Category:  February 22, 2022 Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

In the fall of 2021, FCPS received Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief (ESSER III) funding to help safely reopen and sustain the safe operation of schools and address the impacts of COVID-19 on students by addressing their academic, social, emotional, and mental health needs. Funds are allocated for FY22, FY23, and FY24. 

  

Today’s report will provide an update on the Division Academic and Wellness Support Plans, ESSER III Spending, and Accountability Reporting.

Subject:  Fairfax County Successful Children and Youth Policy Team (SCYPT): Community Schools

 

Staff Contact:  Sloan Presidio, chief academic officer

 

Other Staff Present: 

 

Frances Ivey, deputy superintendent

Michelle Boyd, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services

Craig Herring, principal, Whitman Middle School

 

Meeting Category:  February 22, 2022 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion 

 

Key Points/Background:

 

The purpose of this presentation is to provide an overview and information on the Fairfax County Successful Children and Youth Policy Team (SCYPT) partnership between the Fairfax County government and Fairfax County Public Schools. The presentation will provide an in-depth review the Community Schools Framework, highlight existing Community Schools in FCPS, and obtain School Board input on the Community Schools site selection process.
