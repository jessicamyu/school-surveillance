Subject: School Readiness Resources Panel Recommendations

 

Staff Contacts:  Francisco Durán, chief equity officer, Fairfax County Public Schools

                          Anne-Marie Twohie, director, Fairfax County Office for Children

 

Meeting Category:  October 14, 2019 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 1: Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

 

At its meeting on October 4, 2017, the Successful Children and Youth Policy Team (SCYPT) endorsed the Equitable School Readiness Strategic Plan.  Implementation of the plan began in FY 2019.  Key initiatives have included:

  • Implementing and expanding parent-child playgroups and parent workshops that use the Mind in the Making framework to support the development of executive function skills;
  • Piloting the Early Development Instrument in Fairfax County public schools;
  • Increasing slots in early childhood programs;
  • Expanding the Nurse-Family Partnership program; and
  • Increasing the Child Care Assistance and Referral provider maximum reimbursable rate.

In spring 2019, the Board of Supervisors directed a School Readiness Resources Panel to identify expansion goals and long-term funding strategies to support an ongoing priority to expand early childhood programs.  The panel identified the following goal:

Goal:  All families with children under age 5 living in households with income below 300 percent of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL) have access to publicly funded early childhood programs in the public and private sectors.

In addition, the panel developed 16 recommendations, which are included in the attached presentation document.  The recommendations are intended to support a comprehensive approach to advancing and expanding the system and cover six key areas:

  • Legislative and state actions
  • Land use and development
  • Dedicated early childhood fund
  • Public-private partnerships
  • Community engagement
  • Ongoing reporting

At its meeting on September 25, 2019, SCYPT endorsed the School Readiness Resources Panel Recommendations.

Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, director, Government Relations

 

Meeting Category: October 14, 2019 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement:

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly affected by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff, and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2020 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions and (where applicable) formatting changes from the Board’s 2019 legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments, to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues, and to clarify, simplify and streamline existing position language where appropriate.

 

Proposed staff recommended changes to the FCSB 2020 State and Federal Legislative Program will be presented as New Business at the Board’s October 10, 2019 Regular Meeting. Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for the Board’s October 24, 2019 Regular Meeting.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2020 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program
