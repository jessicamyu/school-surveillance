National School Lunch Week, October 14-18, 2013

Using the theme School Lunch Across the U.S.A., the 2013 National School Lunch Week will include events and activities that promote the benefits of healthy school lunches while celebrating the flavors, ingredients, and culture of various regions of the USA. The 2013 event will also spotlight the healthy foods and positive changes that FCPS has incorporated into its menus.  The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own nutrition habits, in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

Teen Read Week, October 13-19, 2013

 

Teen Read Week encourages teens to go beyond the traditional book and explore poetry, audiobooks, books about music, and more at their school and local library.  The 2013 theme is Seek the Unknown@your library and encourages teens to explore and learn about the unknown through mystery, adventure, science fiction, and fantasy books.  Teen Read Week has been observed since 1998 in an effort to engage teens in reading for pleasure and fun and to learn about the free resources that are available at the library.  Research shows that teens who read for fun have better test scores and are more likely to succeed in the workforce.  The School Board encourages all FCPS teenage students to take advantage of the resources in their school and public library and to become lifelong readers and learners.

 

National Dyslexia Awareness Month, October 2013

 

October is National Dyslexia Awareness Month.  Dyslexia is a language based learning disability that results in people having difficulties learning to read, spell, write and pronounce words. Dyslexia is not due to either a lack of intelligence or desire to learn. In an effort to bring awareness to this learning disability, the Office of Special Education Instruction will be hosting a series of events in support of Dyslexia Awareness Month.  On Friday, October 25, a parent workshop on “Strategies to Support Students with Dyslexia” is scheduled at the Parent Resource Center, in the Dunn Loring Administrative Center, where parents can learn about accommodations for children with dyslexia, tech tools that provide support, and resources available.  Please check our FCPS web site for more information.

National School Bus Safety Week - October 21-25, 2013
 

The week of October 21-25 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. Based on the theme, “Stand Back from the Yellow and Black,” the week focuses on student safety when waiting for and boarding a school bus.  School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 110,000 students in over 1,500 school buses traveling nearly 17 million operational miles each year.

Recommended Action
Adopt a resolution to opt-out of the Virginia Local Disability Program administered by the Virginia Retirement System (HR; presented as new business 9/26/13)

Staff Contact:            Phyllis Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Other Staff Present:  De Hawley Brown, Melissa Blanda

 

Meeting Category:     October 10, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 5

 

Subject:                    Resolution to Opt-Out of the Virginia Retirement System's Virginia Local Disability Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:               State Resolution Requirement 

 

Key Points: 

 

1. The Virginia Retirement System (VRS) has mandated that the newly designed VRS Hybrid Plan’s participating employers offer a disability program to all new hires post-CY2013 either through a VRS administered disability program, or through the local employer.  The employer disability program has to be at least comparable to the VRS’ Virginia Local Disability Program (VLDP).  The school division can either opt-into the VLDP, or opt-out of the disability program if they offer a program that is comparable or greater.
 

2. FCPS’ current Integrated Disability Management program meets the VRS comparability requirement.  FCPS has a third-party analysis comparing the two programs to ensure that FCPS’ current disability programs are comparable or more than comparable.
 

3. Through a recent informal survey, all local school jurisdictions responding to inquiry have indicated they do not intend to participate in the VLDP, with the exception of Loudoun County.
 

4. To ensure future comparability and appropriate plan/cost management, FCPS has developed disability program design considerations and will be using the upcoming procurement process to address options for continuing quality benefits for our employees and maintaining comparability to the VRS programming, while taking cost considerations as a priority.

 

FCPS currently provides employees with a comprehensive disability program that is more than comparable to the VLDP while fitting within the FCPS culture.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt a resolution to opt-out of the Virginia Local Disability Program administered by the Virginia Retirement System.

The attached proposed 2014 FCSB Legislative Program includes additions, subtractions and formatting changes. Meeting Category:    October 10, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 5

 

Subject:                    Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 9, 2013, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2013 session of the General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

2013 Virginia Code Changes relevant to FCPS:

  1. Senate Bill 902, Code of Virginia §2.2-4338; alternative forms of security. Authorizes the acceptance of a cashier's check in lieu of a bid, payment, or performance bond. Currently the only acceptable alternative forms of security are a certified check or cash escrow. This bill changes a mandatory section of the Virginia Public Procurement Act, and accordingly this amendment is required by law.

County’s Administrative Changes

  1. Method of Public Notice:  Change the website location for making public notice of invitation for bids and requests for proposals from “the Department of Purchasing and Supply Management” to “Fairfax County government.” The change will establish authority for the capital construction departments to post notices on the respective departmental web site.
  1. Contractual Disputes:  Change the period for the Purchasing Agent to make a decision on a contractual claim from 30 days to 90 days. This change conforms with the existing threshold in the Code of Virginia (ref. §2.2-4363.C.2 )

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, and 2012 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2013 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts. Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

  1. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Options:

 

None Recommended.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2013 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
