SUBJECT: Renovation Queue and the CIP

TYPE: Discussion


BACKGROUND:
      On September 8, 2008, the School Board directed staff to re-examine the new Renovation Queue regarding the inclusion of enrollment/capacity as a factor in the school ranking criteria.
KEY POINTS:
      The attachment compares the Renovation Queue both with enrollment/capacity as a factor and with this factor neutralized (all schools given the same score versus removing the factor from the rating and making it a 90 point scale). As can be noted, neutralizing the enrollment/capacity factor does have a significant impact upon the ranking of many schools.

      Staff has been considering the issue very carefully and offers the following thoughts:
            • Inclusion of enrollment/capacity in the renovation queue creates an element of volatility to the queue that may cause confusion to the public. Since many schools will be impact each year by changes in enrollment due to growth, new school openings, boundary changes, and program moves, inclusion of enrollment/capacity will require frequent re-ranking of schools within the queue.
            • Recent enrollment growth is going to require that the School Board consider increasing the amount of bond funds used for capacity enhancement projects. Assuming this is the case, both the capacity enhancement program and the renovation program will be driven at least in part by the same factor of enrollment/capacity. This would cause disproportional consideration of this factor.
            • Elimination of enrollment/capacity as a factor results in greater consideration of building condition and ability to deliver instructional programs in determining the queue. This more rational approach focuses on the purpose of a renovation program and on our mission: to ensure that all students are provided instructional spaces that are clean, safe, comfortable, and conducive to efficient and effective educational activities.

OPTIONS:

There are two choices available to the School Board.
        1. Approve the prior rendition of the renovation queue which incorporated enrollment/capacity as a factor, or
        2. Approve the queue with enrollment/capacity neutralized to create a more stable and reliable queue.

RECOMMENDATION:
      Staff recommends that the School Board accept the renovation queue with the enrollment/capacity factor neutralized in the ranking criteria. Staff further recommends that the renovation queue and all related documents to include the consultant’s working documents for each school be posted to the FCPS website so that it is available to the public.
