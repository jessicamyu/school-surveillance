Subject: Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee 24th Annual Report

 

Staff Contacts: Francisco Durán, chief academic and equity officer

                         Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present: Judith Hall Howard, chair

                                 Wilfred A. Grant, Jr., chair-in-waiting

 

Meeting Category: June 26, 2018 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To: Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Summary/Background:

 

For twenty-five years, MSAOC has made an impact on FCPS programs and policies related to minority student achievement, on closing achievement gaps, and on providing for a more equitable and inclusive environment in our schools and our County.

In looking back, we are struck by the fact that many of the issues upon which MSAOC has been charged to provide findings and recommendations in recent years are the very same issues that MSAOC addressed in its first report to the School Board and in many reports thereafter.

We are optimistic, but cautiously so as there still is much to do to insure equity across our school system and that FCPS shows through its words and actions that it is committed to insuring every child receives a first-class education from a professional workforce that reflects those children, and that every child is treated fairly and equitably throughout their time in FCPS.

 

Our charge this year was to produce a prototype Parent Advocacy Handbook that would form the basis for funding in FY 2019, in order to produce both a hard-copy handbook for distribution to parents and to further develop content (toolkit) for use on other platforms as well.

 

Recommendation:

  • Provide upwards to $80,000 for the design, printing, consultant help, training, and e-book software to complete the development and implementation of the Parent Advocacy Handbook.

     

  • Provide expedited assistance with translation of the Parent Advocacy Handbook into the seven major languages in Fairfax County.
