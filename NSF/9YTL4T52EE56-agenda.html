Subject:   Program Evaluation – SR&R: A Study of Changes in Discipline Regulation and Practices: School Years 2004-05 through 2015-16

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points (Findings):

 

This report provides a longitudinal look at the major changes implemented in the division to promote continuous improvement in discipline practices and outcomes. The study is not intended to replace or provide the level of detailed information offered by the Hearings Office Annual Report, the Department of Special Services’ annual presentation on Student Rights and Responsibilities, or the Virginia Department of Education’s Annual Discipline, Crime, and Violence Report.

FCPS’ changes to discipline regulation and other practices over the past 12 years most often have resulted in improvements in outcomes for students.  The report describes and validates a set of major findings that support this overall conclusion.  In general, these findings relate to:

1)   maintain school safety,

2)   reducing suspensions and referrals to the Hearings Office,

3)   reducing disproportionality, and

4) reducing discipline-related interruptions to instruction.

 

Recommendations for the School Board

  • Maintain the direction of the current discipline policies.
  • Support the direction of the current discipline regulations and practices.
  • Require disproportionality data as a part of strategic plan reporting to guide school board feedback to the superintendent about discipline practices.

Meeting Category:  April 18, 2016 – Work Session No. 97

 

Subject:  FY 2017 Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                            

 

Key Points:

 

Overview

On February 4, 2016, the School Board adopted the FY 2017 Advertised Budget that includes a requested increase in the county transfer of $122.7 million, or 6.7 percent. Expenditure adjustments total $149.2 million and 127.0 positions. The FY 2017 Advertised Budget increase includes funding to address large class sizes in elementary schools and to eliminate elementary class sizes of 30 and above, where classroom space is available. The budget also includes funding to provide a step increase for eligible employees and a 1.0 percent market scale adjustment (MSA) for all employees. Funding is provided for an initial investment in the teacher salary scales to begin to address the findings from the external compensation study that is currently underway that showed FCPS is significantly below market on teacher salaries. Other expenditure increases in the FY 2017 Advertised Budget include a living wage adjustment, retirement rate increases, and health insurance increases. Collectively, these are offset by expenditure reductions totaling $27.7 million.

 

County Executive’s Advertised Budget

The County Executive presented his FY 2017 Advertised Budget Plan on February 16, 2016, which included a School Operating Fund transfer increase of $54.75 million, or 3.0 percent, which leaves a gap of $67.8 million.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The final state budget results in an increase in state funding of $16.8 million as compared to FCPS’ FY 2017 Advertised Budget.

 

In addition to the increase in revenue, FCPS’ expenditures will increase by $1.2 million due to higher rates set by the state for mandatory group life insurance.

 

Discussion

As presented at the March 31, 2016, work session, the current projected shortfall in the FY 2017 Advertised Budget is $49.3 million. This includes the 3 percent transfer increase as recommended by the County Executive; the state revenue and expenditure adjustments; and updated Spring enrollment and demographic projections.

 

Updates from the April 15, 2016, County Board of Supervisors Pre-Budget Markup will be provided. Outcomes from this update will shape future discussions regarding balancing the FY 2017 Budget.

 

Calendar

Following is the calendar of upcoming public hearings and budget work sessions:

 

FY 2017 Budget Calendar
April 19, 2016 County BOS FY 2017 Budget mark-up to determine budget package and tax rate
April 26, 2016 County BOS approves the FY 2017 Adopted County Budget, tax rate resolution, and transfer amount to schools
May 2, 2016 School Board conducts budget work session
May 12, 2016 School Board FY 2017 Approved Budget presented for new business
May 17, 2016 School Board holds public hearings on budget (May 18 if needed)
May 19, 2016 School Board conducts budget work session
May 26, 2016 School Board adopts FY 2017 Approved Budget
July 1, 2016 FY 2017 begins

Subject: Consideration of options for potential redesign of Board’s Standing and Advisory committees

 

Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

At its January 11, 2016 work session, the Board agreed upon a timeline for discussing the redesign of the Board’s Standing and Advisory Committees.  The timeline states that at the April 18 Board Work Session, Board liaisons share feedback received from the advisory committees.

 

At the March 14 Board work session, Board members received recommendations to the structure of citizen advisory committees from the Governance Committee (attached).  Board members were asked to solicit feedback from their committee chairpersons on the draft recommendations.  The purpose of today’s discussion is to share that feedback.

 

In addition, the Board is scheduled to discuss and reach tentative agreements on redesign of both the standing and advisory committee structure on May 9.  In preparation for that discussion, the Governance Committee has developed recommendations (attached), which include two options for restructuring the Board’s standing committees to more closely align with organizational goals and functions.

 

Recommendation:
 

That the Board:

  1. Discuss the feedback from the advisory committee chairpersons on redesign of citizen advisory committees, and
  2. Consider the Governance Committee recommendations on the options for restructuring Board standing committees, to be discussed in depth at the May 9 work session.
