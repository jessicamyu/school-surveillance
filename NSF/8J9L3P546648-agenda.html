Staff Contact:         Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  July 16, 2012 – Work Session No. 7

 

Subject:                 Transportation Safety Issues/Safe Routes to Schools

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background: 

 

The Department of Facilities and Transportation Services deals with a number of transportation and safety issues that are recurring in nature and that often come to the attention of School Board members.  The intention of this document is to inform School Board members of these issues and to ensure that staff practices are supported by the School Board.

 

Key Points:  

  • The Safe Routes to School Program (SRTS) is a federally-funded program managed by the Virginia Department of Transportation (VDOT).  SRTS grants provide funding to enable and encourage walking and biking to school.  The office of safety and security assists schools who are interested in applying for SRTS grants and coordinates with Fairfax County Department of Transportation VDOT.
  • FCPS does not have portal to portal responsibility to supervise students or guarantee their safety while they are walking to and from school, bus stops or while at bus stops.
  • The office of safety and security reviews walking routes and school bus stops for unusual hazards when requested.  The concept of unusual hazards recognizes that normal life activities, such as walking to school or to a bus stop, involve some level of risk from normally occurring hazards.
  • The Virginia State Police maintains the Sex Offender and Crimes Against Minors Registry.  The registry is based on the Virginia General Assembly's decision to facilitate access to publicly-available information about persons convicted of specified violent and sexual offenses.  On occasion, parents request moving a bus stop due to the existence of an individual in the registry whose home is in proximity to the stop.  It is the practice of FCPS to move the stop if doing so will not compromise student safety from vehicular concerns.
  • Wink-O-Matic lights (WOM’s) are (with a few exceptions for private schools) owned and operated by FCPS and are placed in the VDOT right-of-way by permit.  The criteria used by FCPS for decisions regarding WOM installations were developed jointly by FCPS, VDOT, and the Fairfax County Police Department (FCPD).  On occasion, these criteria have been challenged by citizen groups and elected officials.  The criteria have been reviewed periodically and both FCPS and FCPD believe the criteria remain appropriate.

Subject:                     Renovation Queue Methodology

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

For the past 20+ years, the School Board has established the renovation queue by using an independent expert firm to evaluate schools and to establish the queue in a fair, impartial manner.  This has been very successful in ensuring that renovation decisions are not political. 

 

In 2008, the School Board approved the current renovation queue criteria and their relative weights.  At that time the board also approved a process that requires a re-evaluation of schools every five years which would include those schools that have not yet gone to referendum for vote by citizens.  This re-evaluation should occur in 2013.  With this pending re-evaluation, the board expressed an interest in considering whether changes should be made to the criteria prior to that re-evaluation.

 

The attached document shows the 2008 board approved criteria, their relative weights, and an explanation of each criterion.

 

Recommendation:

 

Staff recommends that the School Board convene a task force under the direction of the Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) for the purpose of evaluating the existing criteria and bringing back to the School Board recommendations on the criteria that should be used in 2013.  The task force should solicit input from the community and experts in the field of school design, condition assessment, and instruction.

Subject:                     FY 2012 Final Budget Review           

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                     

 

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2012 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2012 Third Quarter Estimate. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the FY 2013 budgeted beginning balances as included in the FY 2013 Approved Budget, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the funds available are $52.8 million.

 

FY 2012 actual revenue was $3.6 million lower than the estimate or 99.9 percent of the FY 2012 Third Quarter Estimate.  State aid is $1.1 million and sales tax projections are $3.4 more than projected.  A decrease of $11.5 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $12.0 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2013, offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of an increase of $0.4 million in Impact Aid.  The net decrease in federal revenue is offset by increases of $3.4 million in tuition, fees, and other revenue.

 

After accounting for the flexibility reserve and other commitments, FY 2012 actuaI expenditures (excluding federal grants) were $44.4 million, or 1.9 percent, lower than the FY 2012 Third Quarter Estimate.  A majority of this variance results from compensation accounts being $34.3 million less than budgeted due primarily to savings resulting from higher turnover than projected; utilities expenditures being $7.1 million less than projected due to a temperate winter, spring, and early summer; and county charges for vehicle services, including fuel, being $1.7 million less than projected. In addition, $12.0 million in multiyear federal grant funds were unexpended.  This funding will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2013. 

 

This agenda item also includes recommendations for $11.2 million in FY 2013 expenditure adjustments leaving $41.6 million for the FY 2014 budgeted beginning balance.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.
