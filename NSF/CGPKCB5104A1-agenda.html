Subject:  2021-2022 Advisory Committee Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual Report

Staff Contact:  Dr. Michelle Boyd, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Special Services

Other Staff Present:  Michael Bloom, Director, Special Education Instruction                                             Dawn Schaefer, Director, Office of Special Education Procedural Support

Meeting Category:  School Board Work Session (October 18, 2022)

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

Related To:  Legal Requirement

Key Points:  The report is to review ACSD recommendations related to the following School Board Annual charge.

ACSD School Board Charge:

Committee Charge for 2021-2022

● Examine current FCPS practices regarding school-based accountability and fidelity related to the implementation of best practices on the identification, evaluation, eligibility, and instruction of students with disabilities. 

● Make recommendations designed to improve division wide consistency related to the fidelity of the implementation of special education procedures and practices. 

● Make recommendations designed to ensure equitable use of Department of Special Services (DSS) resources for all students with disabilities - regardless of school, socioeconomic status, race, ethnicity, or gender.

ACSD Subcommittees: Family Engagement and Outreach, Student Achievement and Outcomes, Policy and Regulations, and School Board Charge.

Highlighted Subcommittee Recommendations:

Recommendations:

  • Continue to record ACSD Subcommittee and Regular Meetings, whether the ACSD meets virtually or in-person, to ensure equitable access by students with disabilities, their families, and other community stakeholders. 
  • Continue to accept video, audio, and written public comment, in addition to in-person testimony. Encourage testimony in the speaker’s native language and provide interpretation or translation after the fact.
  • Post PDFs of the updated ACSD brochure and translated versions to the ACSD webpage annually so that it is easy to find and link to. 
  • Advertise the ACSD via a blurb for School Board Member newsletters and direct FCPS communications to special education families. Distribute the blurb to community organizations so that they may share with their constituents. 
  • Require the translation of all Eligibility, Testing, and Individualized Education Program (IEP) Documents provided by FCPS to all parents whose native language is not English. Investigate whether a translation option could be built into the SEASTARS application.
  • FCPS should investigate how to make the Applied Studies diploma eligible for post-secondary financial aid. If this needs to be addressed at the state level, then the School Board should add this to their legislative agenda in 2022-2023.
  • FCPS must improve communication to families regarding the limitations of the applied studies diploma.
  • Review the multi-tiered systems of support across all FCPS schools for consistent implementation, especially at schools demonstrating disproportionality in special education identification. I.e., “Create services not placements.”
  • Examine data that reflects representation of SWD in non-academic and extracurricular activities.
  • Expand family partnerships by offering annual parent training opportunities in FCPS programs such as applied behavior analysis (ABA), Collaborative and Proactive Solutions (CPS), and social-emotional strategies akin to FCPS’s Second Step programming.
  • Category B and all teachers of deaf/hard of hearing, blind/low vision, and AAC users must receive professional development in the science of reading and available research that all students, regardless of ability, benefit from high expectations and literacy instruction based on the science of reading. 
  • Teachers must receive additional compensated time to become fully trained in all the literacy curriculums their students are likely to access prior to the start of the school year. 
  • All the necessary resources for the literacy instruction of all students and materials necessary for any required adaptations must be available from the first day of school. 
  • Universal literacy screeners must be provided in accessible text formats. 
  • All digital content must be verified to be accessible and verified to be fully compliant with the most updated version of Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) prior to purchase/endorsement.
  • All teachers and reading specialists must receive training in accessible design. 
  • Deaf/hard of hearing students must be allowed to use Cued Speech for literacy instruction even if they use other communication modalities for other subjects.
  • The teacher support, coaching, and accountability measures embedded in the EAL Plan must also apply to Category B teachers and all teachers of deaf/hard of hearing, blind/low vision, and AAC users.
  • All teachers must have equal access to ready-to-go-lessons in phonics/phonemic awareness. 
  • Category B teachers and all teachers of deaf/hard of hearing, blind/low vision, and AAC users must also receive new/updated basal resources appropriate to their student’s needs that reflect the science of reading - and the training to implement them effectively.
  • Develop and institute a method to track the results of students who access the Brigance in lieu of universal screeners, to ensure accountability for their growth. 
  • Revise the Adapted Curriculum Reading Matrix to ensure students have access to Science of Reading based instruction, as is required by the Virginia Literacy Act, SB616/HB319. 
  • FCPS should audit the adapted curriculum literacy programs being used to ensure they are being implemented with fidelity, to the grade levels for which they are designed.
  • FCPS must address the disparity of speech service hours for AAC users.
  • Pay that is a clear step above what would be paid at the less stressful and taxing summer jobs obtainable by teachers
  • Hiring some positions as year-round, keeping teachers at the same school or with the same students for ESY as much as possible. 
  • Guaranteeing SACC or other childcare and/or child care stipends for ESY staff 
  • Paid pre-ESY preparation time of a minimum of 3 days. IEPs must be available by the beginning of the first preparation day. Any training needed for student-specific needs is provided prior to student introduction.
  • Provide teachers ready-to-go lessons complete with materials. Provide all necessary and differentiated classroom materials and student supplies.
  • Provide mandatory training to teachers hired under authorities that circumvent traditional routes to licensure to address deficits in special education policy fundamentals and classroom management techniques
  • Increase use of year-round contracts to bolster specialized staff for ESY and summer programming (for interested candidates and current staff) 
  • Require mandatory training in fundamentals in special education for all staff, including those in general education 
  • Increase pay and promote training opportunities for special needs substitute teachers 
  • Require at least one administrator at each school involved in development of IEPs to complete Back to Basics: Special Education Law Modules training  
  • Reinstate payment for summer training for all SPED teachers to have fundamental hands-on training, including ABA hands-on training
  • Provide SPED teachers in non-cluster site special education programs (base schools) who are now addressing the needs of the students with complex behavioral and learning challenges to have training commensurate with teachers in cluster sites for ABA
  • To enhance retention, allow certified SPED teachers more flexibility to move between educational levels
  • Provide clear guidance about receiving recovery services to FCPS staff and parents
  • Use differential pay to recruit and retain Special Education staff. 
  • To the extent practicable, allow qualified teaching staff flexible work schedules including part-time schedules or shared job positions. 
  • Provide incentives for staff referrals to recruit teaching staff.
  • FCPS perform data collection and efficacy analysis on all behavior management and crisis prevention/de-escalation programs used in the division to guide decisions on what programs must be equitably available among the various schools, sites, programs, and grade levels.
  • Ensure that Dr. Ross Greene’s Collaborative and Proactive Solutions model is introduced to all teachers/staff with at least annual training opportunities at all schools, sites, settings, and grade levels.
  • Ensure the Ukeru model is introduced to all teachers and staff of all EAC, ID, IDS, and secondary self-contained special education classrooms, with at least annual training opportunities for these staff and their administrators.
  • FCPS must survey staff for their concerns on current FCPS behavior management and crisis prevention/ intervention practices.
  • FCPS needs streamlined communication between the Central Office, administrators, teachers and staff in classrooms, and parents.
  • FCPS should formalize multiple direct channels to ensure communications are received by all intended.
  • The Special Education Handbook for Parents (2021) should be directly received by the parent(s) of every student identified for special education (IEP + 504).
  • A physical copy of the Transition Resource Guide (2020) should be in the room during all IEP meetings for middle and high school students, and copies should be offered to the parent(s).
  • Central Office staff should spend one full day every week in a different school to directly monitor awareness and utilization of available FCPS resources. 
  • FCPS should provide additional resources to support English Learners (ELs) and twice-exceptional (2e) learners.
  • All students should have true access to the supports individually needed in every course and placement.
  • IEP documents should be translated into the preferred language of the parent(s).
  • A physical copy of the 2e Handbook should be in the room during all IEP meetings for 2e students, and copies should be offered to the parent(s).
  • The FCPS Department of Special Services should publish an annual Special Report on issues of cultural norms, gender equality, ableism, language barriers, and societal stigma as they pertain to special education access and implementation in FCPS.
  • FCPS should offer an additional step in explaining the process of understanding special education procedures when the family of a student with intersectional factors is identified by the Local Education Agency (LEA) as having a known or suspected disability.
  • FCPS should raise the standard of practice for defending an IEP team’s decision not to reevaluate at a triennial update. 
  • FCPS should increase meaningful inclusion opportunities. 
  • All current third-graders’ files, now inclusive of CogAT scores, should be reexamined for eligibility for AAP. This resolves the inequity of a different process used due to covid and prevents a two-year delay impact due to a one-year delay in testing.
