Agenda Item: Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) Annual
          Report 2007

Staff Contact: Alice Farling, Assistant Superintendent, Special Services

Background:
Regulations Governing Special Education Programs for Children with Disabilities in Virginia require that a local advisory committee for special education, appointed by each local school board, advise the school board through the division superintendent of unmet needs in the education of children with disabilities. The regulations state that the composition of the committee shall include parents of children with disabilities and persons with disabilities.

Key Issues: The ACSD made the following recommendations:
    The ACSD recommends that the School Board change the composition of the Committee in order to have a seat for a representative from Fairfax County’s Therapeutic Recreation Services.
    The ACSD recommends that a detailed, building-by-building plan for increasing access to the general curriculum for all students with disabilities be developed, disseminated widely, and discussed publicly.
    FCPS will provide staff with increased access to non-traditional training opportunities.
    FCPS will support full, rapid, district-wide implementation of the Learning Management System software.
    FCPS will make essential areas of training mandatory.
    Principals will be accountable for developing, implementation and sustaining professional training plans for their schools.
    FCPS will designate monies in the budget for 1) supplies, reinforcers and “booster” training funds for the PBS sites, 2) additional ABA Coaching Model positions on both the Elementary and Secondary levels, and 3) an extension of the three-year Cluster-Based Instructional Coaches Pilot Program to at lease five years.
    FCPS will implement strategies to increase awareness and access to Career and Technical Education courses, the Academy programs, and transition and career planning tools and curriculum to students with disabilities and their parents.
    FCPS will restructure the Davis and Pulley Centers’ budgets to more properly reflect the students’ needs and provide the supports that they require. FCPS needs to recognize the growth in student population that will continue to put pressure on the Centers and provide the extra funds and flexibility required.

School Board Discussion/Decision:
For information and discussion

Staff Recommendation:
Accept the Advisory Committee report.

Agenda Item: Gifted and Talented Advisory Committee Report (GTAC)

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

Background:
The report of the Gifted and Talented Advisory Committee was written in response to the charge of the Board regarding delivery of level four services in the elementary schools. GTAC worked on the report for several months, providing time for participants to contribute and revise. This report is the first attachment. The report also meets the state requirement that staff provide an update on progress made toward the goals of the Local Plan for the Gifted. The update of the Local Plan shared with GTAC is also attached.

In discussions with GTAC members during the year, staff was asked if FCPS would add another level of GT service beyond the four levels currently being provided. Staff made it clear that this change was not being considered. Members of the committee who advocate for an additional level of service prepared a minority report that was presented to the entire committee via email on April 30th prior to the last meeting on May 1st. The minority report recommends an additional level of service for selected students. The minority report is the final attachment.

Key Issues:
    The delivery of level four services at the local elementary school
    The name of the gifted and talented programs
    Progress toward the five years goals of the Local Plan for the Gifted
    The position taken in the minority report that an additional level of service is needed
School Board Discussion/Decision:
The School Board will consider the recommendations in the reports. No specific decision around these recommendations is required.

Staff Recommendation:
Accept the Advisory Committee report.

Options:
None recommended.
