Meeting Category:      December 7, 2015 - Work Session No. 45

 

Subject:                      FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program Preliminary Summary

                               

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2017-2021 Proposed Capital Improvement Program (CIP) will be available for the School Board in its entirety for new business on December 17, 2015. A public hearing will be held on January 5, 2016. A School Board work session will take place on January 11, 2016. On January 21, 2016, the Proposed Capital Improvement Program will be considered as an action item for School Board approval. The attachments listed below are intended to provide a preview of the information that will contained in the Proposed CIP. 

 

 Discussion:

 

Although the overall growth in the school system appears to be moderating, current and projected student enrollment increases exceed our ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. As staff has previously communicated, the capital funding is insufficient to meet the majority of our capital requirements, which results in difficult choices in establishing project priority for the School Board.

 

The purpose of the discussion today is for staff to describe the content of the Capital Construction Cash Flow, the timing of projects within the five-year window, and to present changes from last year. In addition to the cash flow, staff will provide a summary of potential future boundary adjustments. Lastly, staff will preview options to solve future capacity problems, similar to the potential capacity solutions provided in last year’s FY 2016-2020 CIP. In order to minimize disruption to the Capital Construction Cash Flow, the majority of the recommended solutions coincide with capital projects that are already planned.

Staff Contact:      Susan Quinn, Chief Operating Officer


Other Staff Present:     Susan Marks, interim assistant superintendent, Human Resources

           

Meeting Category:        December 7, 2015 – Work Session

 

Subject:     Compensation Study

 

School Board Action Required:        Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS has hired Segal Group, a benefits, compensation, and human resources consulting firm, to identify a compensation structure that attracts and retains qualified employees through an optimal mix of salary and benefits. The study is a key piece of the Strategic Plan’s goal 3 – Premier Workforce – which aims to recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts, and ensure that FCPS maintains a competitive compensation package.

 

Local school districts and county governments have responded to a market study regarding the total compensation (salary and benefits) of teachers, instructional assistants, and benchmarked Unified Scale positions.  Initial results on FCPS’ market position for teacher compensation have been compiled. Segal Group will provide a briefing on the results of this portion of the study, to include comparisons of pay structures, wages, and compensation strategies for teachers in local school districts.

Meeting Category:      December  7, 2015 - Work Session No. 48

 

Subject:                       FY 2016 Midyear Budget Review          

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, funds available are decreasing by $0.3 million.  The FY 2016 beginning balance is increasing by $2.5 million due to final year-end adjustments made after the FY 2015 Final Budget Review. The adjustments include an increase of $0.9 million in sales tax revenue based on actual receipts; a $0.2 million increase in other revenue; and expenditure decreases totaling $1.4 million.  In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2016 revenue adjustments include a decrease in state aid of $4.0 million based on updated enrollment numbers resulting in lower projected average daily membership (ADM); an increase of $1.1 million for City of Fairfax tuition due to an increase in the City’s ADM for FY 2015; and a net increase of $0.1 million in grant revenue. 

 

Recommended FY 2016 expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $0.3 million resulting primarily from savings in instructional materials and supplies based on lower than projected enrollment growth this year, offset by a net increase in grant expenditures. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment. Also attached is an update on the status on grants for FY 2015 and FY 2016.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2016 Midyear Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.
