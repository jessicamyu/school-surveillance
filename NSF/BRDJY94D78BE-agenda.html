School Psychology Awareness Week Recognition

 

School psychologists are uniquely qualified members of school teams serving students, staff and families. School psychologists receive specialized advanced graduate preparation that includes coursework and practical experiences relevant to both psychology and education. They apply expertise in mental health, learning, and behavior, to change lives by taking actions to help children and youth succeed academically, socially, behaviorally, and emotionally. In alignment the FCPS Strategic Plan and Portrait of A Graduate, school psychologists help individuals realize their own abilities, develop the skills they need to cope with the normal stresses of life, to work productively and fruitfully, and be valuable contributors in the community.

 

In their work with students, school psychologists help students develop the social, emotional, and life skills necessary to realize personal achievement and growth, and to build resilience and a sense of belonging. Through delivery of direct services, using evidenced-based practices, school psychologists help students build social and emotional competence, and skills required to actively engage in learning.

 

School psychologists collaborate with school teams in the development of universal prevention and intervention practices across a tiered system of supports. These practices foster resilience, increase educator understanding of social and emotional development, reduce barriers to learning, and increase student protective factors. School psychologists bring knowledge of data collection and analysis to team discussions, which informs intervention development and monitoring of student progress.

 

They intervene when students experience mental health issues which may require effective behavior management and individualized plans. They are members of crisis response teams, threat assessment teams, assess students at risk of harming themselves, and teach and implement trauma-informed practices.

 

School psychologists consult with staff and parents to further their understanding of complex student needs and to help them develop the necessary skills to appropriately respond to student needs.

 

All children and youth can face problems from time to time related to learning, social relationships, making difficult decisions, or managing emotions such as feeling depressed, anxious, worried, or isolated. School psychologists help students, families, educators, and members of the community understand and resolve both long-term, chronic problems and short-term issues that students may face. They are a highly skilled and ready resource in the effort to ensure that all FCPS children and youth thrive in school, at home, and in life.

Problem Statement:

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly affected by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program includes additions, deletions and formatting changes from the Board’s 2020 legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues, and to clarify, simplify and streamline existing position language where appropriate.

 

Proposed recommended changes to the FCSB 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program were introduced in a presentation and discussion at the Board’s Regular Meeting on October 6, 2020. Those and other changes were further discussed at the Board’s October 20, 2020 legislative work session and revised State and Federal Priorities were briefly discussed at an October 27 Work Session. Proposed revisions and Board priorities as discussed both on October 6 and 20 have been incorporated into the current version of the Legislative Program.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2021 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

Subject:  School Bus Communications System – Associated Tower Licenses

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey Platenberg, Assistant Superintendent

 

Other Staff Present: Francine Furby, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services;
                                  James Jenkins, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category:  Regular Meeting, November 16, 2020

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Department of Facilities & Transportation Services (“FTS”) is in the process of replacing its existing school bus communication system which was first used in 1996 and which is receiving limited support by its manufacturer.  This existing system experiences increasingly frequent failures, diminished capacity, and elevated maintenance/repair costs.

 

Pursuant to the Purchasing Resolution adopted annually by the School Board, the Office of Procurement Services is currently taking steps to procure a modern digital system for this purpose which is known as Land Mobile Radio (“LMR”).  LMR systems include land-based towers, and dispatch stations, as well as radios in buses and other vehicles.

 

To support this new LMR, the school division will need to enter into tower license agreements for antenna locations. Current plans call for at least three tower locations to be dispersed throughout the County.  Each tower location will house various items of equipment (including antenna, back-up power and computer), and each will provide for a portion of the County signal coverage for send and receive radio transmissions from school buses to dispatchers and other users.

 

Each tower license agreement is for a multi-year term with one or more renewable extensions.  Although rates vary by location, the current annual cost of a given tower license can range from $18,000 to $21,000.  Over a seven-year initial term with payments for electrical power as well as annual increases, the projected cost per tower can be $145,000 to $170,000.  Ultimately, the number of tower locations will depend upon the actual coverage provided by the first three tower locations and by the impact of future developments (including the level of radio traffic from other users).

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other), to take all action necessary and appropriate, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to negotiate, execute and administer on behalf of the School Board such tower license agreements (as well as any other documents) as are deemed necessary to support the efficient operation of the new school bus communications system.
