National Inclusive Schools Week
December 2-6, 2019

 

WHEREAS, since its inception in 2001, National Inclusive Schools Week has celebrated the progress that schools have made in providing a supportive and quality education to an increasingly diverse student population, including students who are marginalized due to disability, gender, socio-economic status, cultural heritage, language preference, and other factors; and

 

WHEREAS, Inclusive Schools Week is an annual event sponsored by the Inclusive Schools Network (ISN) and is held during the first full week of December; and

 

WHEREAS, this year’s theme is “Charting the Course for Inclusive Schools," providing educators, students, parents, schools, agencies, and communities with the opportunity to think about our current philosophies and practices regarding inclusion and to plan for “next steps” in the process; and

 

WHEREAS, many of us have seen positive changes in awareness of inclusion as a requirement for achieving greater access, equitable opportunities and more authentic relationships recognizing that we have many more steps to take to reach our goal of a fully inclusive school culture; and

 

WHEREAS, the week also provides an important opportunity for educators, students, and parents to discuss what else needs to be done in order to ensure that their schools continue to improve their ability to successfully educate all children;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board reminds the entire FCPS community that education takes on a beautiful shape when all children are included.

School Psychology Awareness Week Recognition
 

 

School psychologists are uniquely qualified members of school teams serving students, staff and families. School psychologists receive specialized advanced graduate preparation that includes coursework and practical experiences relevant to both psychology and education. They apply expertise in mental health, learning, and behavior, to change lives by taking actions to help children and youth succeed academically, socially, behaviorally, and emotionally. In alignment the FCPS Strategic Plan and Portrait of A Graduate, school psychologists help individuals realize their own abilities, develop the skills they need to cope with the normal stresses of life, to work productively and fruitfully, and be valuable contributors in the community.

 

In their work with students, school psychologists help students develop the social, emotional, and life skills necessary to realize personal achievement and growth, and to build resilience and a sense of belonging. Through delivery of direct services, using evidenced-based practices, school psychologists help students build social and emotional competence, and skills required to actively engage in learning.

 

School psychologists collaborate with school teams in the development of universal prevention and intervention practices across a tiered system of supports.  These practices foster resilience, increase educator understanding of social and emotional development, reduce barriers to learning, and increase student protective factors. School psychologists bring knowledge of data collection and analysis to team discussions, which informs intervention development and monitoring of student progress. 

 

They intervene when students experience mental health issues which may require effective behavior management and individualized plans.  They are members of crisis response teams, threat assessment teams, assess students at risk of harming themselves, and teach and implement trauma-informed practices.

 

School psychologists consult with staff and parents to further their understanding of complex student needs and to help them develop the necessary skills to appropriately respond to student needs.

 

All children and youth can face problems from time to time related to learning, social relationships, making difficult decisions, or managing emotions such as feeling depressed, anxious, worried, or isolated. School psychologists help students, families, educators, and members of the community understand and resolve both long-term, chronic problems and short-term issues that students may face. They are a highly skilled and ready resource in the effort to ensure that all FCPS children and youth thrive in school, at home, and in life.

Key Points:  Virginia Code Section 22.1-311 outlines the procedures for dismissal of teachers and appeal hearings requested by the teacher, requiring that hearings are to be conducted by the school board or by an impartial hearing officer appointed by the school board. In 2014, the Board approved a pool of hearing officers for this purpose. An individual hearing officer can be drawn from this pool as needed for a teacher dismissal hearing. The pool of available hearing officers appointed by the Board in 2014 has diminished over time.

 

The Code requires that the hearing officer possess some knowledge of and expertise in education law and be capable of presiding over an administrative hearing. 

 

John V. Robinson

 

Mr. Robinson has over 22 years of experience in conducting administrative hearings in various tribunals, including the Virginia Department of Education, the Supreme Court of Virginia, and the Virginia Department of Human Resource Management’s Office of Equal Employment and Dispute Resolution. Most recently, Mr. Robinson has been retained as a North Carolina Hearing Review Officer, reviewing decisions appealed from North Carolina Administrative Law Judges. As a licensed attorney, Mr. Robinson is a member of the Virginia State Bar and is admitted to practice in all Virginia state courts, the U.S. Supreme Court, U.S. District Courts for the Eastern and Western Divisions of Virginia, and also in the U.S. Court of Federal Claims. He is an adjunct professor of Law at the University of Richmond since 1999, is a presenter on a variety of topics, and provides training for Virginia Supreme Court Hearing Officers.

 

 

Staff recommends the appointment of John V. Robinson, who meets the requirements of the Code.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint John V. Robinson to serve as a Hearing Officer for Teacher Dismissal Hearings pursuant to Section 22.1-311 of the Code of Virginia, effective immediately.
