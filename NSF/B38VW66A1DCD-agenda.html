Subject: Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee 26th Annual Report

 

Staff Contacts: Francisco Durán, Chief Equity Officer
                         Sloan J. Presidio, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Other Staff Present: Wilfred A. Grant, Jr., Chair
                                 Subcommittee chairs

 

Meeting Category: June 27, 2019 - Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To: Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Summary/Background:

MSAOC applauds recent initiatives instituted to improve academic education for all FCPS students, particularly the work of the Chief Equity Officer (CEO). Dr. Francisco Duran and the Equity Profile that will further advance our closing the achievement gap efforts and the One Fairfax initiative. Mr. Armando Peri, FCPS’s first Ombudsman, has worked closely with MSAOC and other community groups dealing with issues brought to our attention by families and the community.

There is work to be done. We are hopeful that Strategic Plan 2019 will be the unifying vision to advance equity and closing the achievement gap. As we highlighted in this and earlier reports that the achievement gap – the disparity between Black and Hispanic students and their White and Asian classmates -- persists. There are schools in 2018-19 employing few, if any, teachers of color and we have not yet seen a plan to ensure more diversity within our teaching ranks. Black and Hispanic students still are disproportionally subject to disciplinary consequences for a variety of infractions where questions of implicit bias may arise. And, there still is underrepresentation of Black and Hispanic students in Level IV Advanced Academic Program participation.

Recommendation:

We have included all of our recommendations in the Annual Report.
