    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Human Resources

    SUBJECT: Award of Contracts

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board award contracts for university intern programs to George Washington University in the amounts of $383,180, $198,000, and $86,253; to George Mason University in the amounts of $83,612 and $9,600; and to Marymount University in the amount of $77,500.

    EXPLANATION:

    The George Mason University Professional Development School program, the Marymount Professional Development School program, (formerly known as the Professional Development Academy) and the George Washington University Transition to Teaching program are ongoing programs that train selected teacher education candidates in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS). Two other George Washington University programs provide interns in special education.

    These university programs provide access to an especially qualified group of applicants who understand the needs of FCPS students. Costs for these programs are funded from the substitute teacher account by trading off two instructional assistant (IA) vacancies and by placement of interns in existing IA vacancies. The universities supervise all student interns.

    State prescribed procedures are being used to award the contract to George Mason University. In the cases of George Washington and Marymount Universities, the agreements will be approved as sole source contracts on the basis that no other entities have expressed interest in these programs or can provide such a highly talented pool of substitute teachers at this time.

    SUBJECT: Land Acquisition

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve the transaction to purchase the property located at 8429 Woodlawn Street, Alexandria, VA 22310 and authorize the Division Superintendent or his designee, with the counsel of the School Board attorney, to negotiate and execute on behalf of the School Board such agreements and related documents as are necessary and advisable.

    EXPLANATION:
      Two parcels of land that adjoin the Woodlawn Elementary School property are owned by Walter Cullen. These two parcels are elongated and physically abut the school property. Mr. Cullen was originally asking $500,000 for the property. We have verbally agreed on a purchase price of $450,000, with Mr. Cullen donating up to $100,000, which would reduce the School Board’s capital outlay to $350,000.

      If the School Board approves this acquisition for the $450,000 purchase price, this property will be utilized to increase the parking capacity at the school, as well as enhance the safety to the Kiss “N Ride and general traffic flow in and out of the school. An area may also be added to the existing play area. These improvements will not be made until the school is renovated.

    Category
    6. New Business - 8 p.m.

    Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services

    SUBJECT: Curriculum Advisory Committees


    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board appoint individuals to serve on the English Language Arts, Family Life Education, Fine Arts, Foreign Language, Health and Physical Education, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies Curriculum Advisory Committees, and on the High School Course Review Committee, as listed in the agenda item.

    EXPLANATION:
      Regulation 1707.6 establishes eight curriculum advisory committees for levels K through 12 to advise on Program of Studies development and implementation.
      Additionally, this agenda item covers appointments to the High School Course Review Committee, which annually identifies standard or optional course offerings for deletion, consolidation, or revision, and makes recommendations for action by the Division Superintendent and the School Board (see the current version of Regulation 3202).

      FCPS does not appoint Curriculum Advisory Committees in years when there is a textbook adoption taking place. However, in recent years, the adoption process in several subject areas has been scheduled for more than one year to decrease the impact on the schools and the budget. For example, last year, we adopted science textbooks for high school; in 05-06 we will adopt science textbooks for middle school, grades 6-8. Similarly, the textbook adoption for English/language arts scheduled for 05-06 is for high school only. Because we believe that we should not suspend Advisory Committees for more than one year and because 05-06 English/language arts and science adoptions are limited to one level, IS will convene Curriculum Advisory Committees in these subject areas next year. The work of the adoption committees will be completed by early September; therefore, it is very possible that School Board appointees may be willing to continue to serve on advisory committees.
      The term of service for all School Board member representatives on the above-named advisory committees is one year. These terms will be effective until June 30, 2006. Representatives may be reappointed. Classroom teachers are elected to these committees and serve two-year terms, with elections being held during the spring of odd-numbered years. These terms will be effective until June 30, 2007. Other committee members serve terms as set forth in the appropriate regulation.

      Regulation 3009.8 establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges
      of library and instructional materials by residents.
