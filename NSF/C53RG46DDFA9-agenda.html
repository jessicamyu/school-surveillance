Native American Heritage Month - November 2021

 

Native American Heritage Month started in 1915 as American Indian Day by the Boy Scouts of America. Over the years, individual states recognized Native American days, but it wasn’t until 1990 that President George H.W. Bush designated November as National American Indian Heritage Month. Every President since 1995 has issued annual proclamations designating the month of November as the time to celebrate the culture, accomplishments, and contributions of people who were the first inhabitants of the United States.

November 2021 is National Career Development Month

In FCPS, students in grades PreK-12 engage in meaningful learning experiences to spark and sustain interest and cultivate skills to foster the development of successful careers.

 

Students build understanding of potential careers and what it may take to achieve those careers through many opportunities, just some of which can include speakers and class visitors in early grades, and then in later grades, internships, apprenticeships, and work-based learning.

 

All middle and high school students in FCPS have access to career and technical education (CTE) courses as well as additional pathway elective courses across content areas at each of our high schools.

 

Furthermore, FCPS offers highly-specialized career academies and courses that prepare students for industry certifications so learners can engage in deeper learning and preparation for a chosen career field.

 

Staff in FCPS serve strategic roles in career development efforts, including:

  • College and career specialists
  • School counselors
  • Career experience specialists
  • Employment and transition representatives
  • Career and technical educators and
  • Career and transition teachers

 

These educators and staff members collaborate with families to enhance academic and career planning with students, and also nurture community employer partnerships to connect students with available careers per trend analysis in the economy. 

 

Finally, the School Board shows further commitment to career development by hosting its own Career and Technical Education Advisory Committee (CTEAC), which seeks to match interests and needs between students and employers.

 

On behalf of the FCPS School Board, I would like to thank and celebrate our dedicated career development professionals and partners for your leadership and collaboration in career development.

RESOLUTION

HONORING

INTERNATIONAL AUGMENTATIVE AND ALTERNATIVE COMMUNICATION

AWARENESS MONTH

 

 

WHEREAS, October 2021 is International Augmentative and Alternative Communication Awareness Month, and the theme is “Get Out, Speak Up and Break Through the Screen in a Recovering World”; and

 

WHEREAS, the United States Society for Augmentative and Alternative Communication (USSAAC) defines Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) as including all forms of communication (other than oral speech) that are used to express thoughts, needs, wants, and ideas; and

 

WHEREAS, communication – in all its forms - is a basic human right and is specifically protected under the Americans with Disabilities Act; and

 

WHEREAS, AAC includes, but is not limited to, gestures and facial expressions; writing; drawing; spelling words by pointing to letters; pointing to photos, pictures, or written words; using an app on an iPad or tablet to communicate, or using a computer-based speech-generating device; and

 

WHEREAS, AAC strategies, tools, and technologies enable more than 2 million children and adults in the United States to access their fundamental right to communication; and

 

WHEREAS, research supports that there are no prerequisites required in order for individuals to benefit from AAC access; that AAC users are presumed competent and given access to the tools and instruction they need to learn, regardless of their speech impediment, diagnosis or degree of difference; and

 

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools is developing a division-wide literacy plan that ensures comprehensive, PreK-12 research-based reading instruction that is aligned with the Science of Reading so that all students may achieve their full potential; and

 

WHEREAS, students, educators, and families will benefit from increased awareness about AAC users and technology, and the combination of strengths and difficulties that AAC users can have;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board recognizes October 2021 as AAC Awareness Month and encourages all residents and FCPS staff to learn more about Augmentative & Alternative Communication, including identifying students that can benefit from AAC; understanding the core of “presuming competence,” knowing and making available supports for use by educators and families; and highlighting the success that AAC users can have in their careers and life.

Subject:               2022 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact:      Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:        Presentation

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link:          Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options; sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a State and Federal Legislative Program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board annually considers additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) to the Board’s previous year legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.  Existing legislative positions have historically carried over from year to year, unless specifically changed by the Board.

 

The presentation will provide the Board with an overview of the existing Legislative Program structure and positions, highlight initial changes which can be made due to previous year policy actions, and preview legislative and policy issues and staff recommendations that the Board may wish address in Program position language. 

 

The Board will discuss any proposed staff recommended changes as well as Board-directed changes to the FSCB 2022 State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for October 25, 2021.  Final Action on the Legislative Program will be scheduled for later in November.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board receive the Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program presentation.

Proposed Amendments to the Plan Documents

 

ERFC Plan Document

 

  • Section 3.02 Member Contributions. – A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that accumulated contributions may be returned to an employee who has erroneously been included in ERFC membership.

 

  • Section 3.05 Employer Contributions. –  A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that ERFC may return Employer contributions that are made in excess of the correct amount due.

 

  • Section 14.03 No Diversion of Assets. – A proposed amendment to this section adds new language that permits ERFC to return up to one year of Employer contributions, without interest, that occur because of mistake of fact to the extent permitted under §401(a) of the Internal Revenue Code. 

 

ERFC 2001 Plan Document

 

  • Section 3.03 Member Contributions. – A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that accumulated contributions may be returned to an employee who has erroneously been included in ERFC membership.

 

  • Section 3.05 Employer Contributions. –  A proposed amendment to this section clarifies that ERFC may return Employer contributions that are made in excess of the correct amount due.

 

  • Section 9.03 No Diversion of Assets. – A proposed amendment to this section adds new language that permits ERFC to return up to one year of Employer contributions, without interest, that occur because of mistake of fact to the extent permitted under §401(a) of the Internal Revenue Code.

Subject:               2022 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact:      Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:        Presentation

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link:          Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options; sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a State and Federal Legislative Program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board annually considers additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) to the Board’s previous year legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.  Existing legislative positions have historically carried over from year to year, unless specifically changed by the Board.

 

The presentation will provide the Board with an overview of the existing Legislative Program structure and positions, highlight initial changes which can be made due to previous year policy actions, and preview legislative and policy issues and staff recommendations that the Board may wish address in Program position language. 

 

The Board will discuss any proposed staff recommended changes as well as Board-directed changes to the FSCB 2022 State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for October 25, 2021.  Final Action on the Legislative Program will be scheduled for later in November.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board approve the Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program.
