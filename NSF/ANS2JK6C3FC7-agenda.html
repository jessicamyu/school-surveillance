Subject: Security Review

 

 

Staff Contact:  Marty K. Smith, chief operating officer

                        Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Thomas Vaccarello, director, Office of Safety and Security

 

 

Meeting Category: June 18, 2018 – Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion
 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 2 - Caring Culture
 

 

Problem Statement:
 

A safe environment is at the core of effective learning and teaching. As a result of recent school shootings, an internal security review was ordered by the Division Superintendent. The security review examined the current program, opportunities for enhanced security improvements to existing programs, and consideration of new security initiatives to further mitigate risk in the short and long term. The dynamics of any security program in a large school district are complex and challenging.
 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):
 

The Superintendent requested an internal security review and report for consideration after the Parkland Florida shooting on February 14, 2018. The review was conducted exclusively by staff assigned to the Office of Safety and Security (OSS) directed by Marty Smith, chief operating officer, and Jeff Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services. Principals, teachers, staff, students, parents, law enforcement, academia, Virginia state officials, and security professionals contributed to this process, with emphasis being placed on the collaboration with principals.
 

Discussions were held regarding existing security programs and what improvements can be made to provide an increased security presence and enhance training.
 

The Office of Safety and Security staff conducted the review and electronic communications on the topic were managed within the FCPS Client Communication process. All groups were tasked by OSS staff to develop prioritized lists regarding school security. The topics were then discussed internally with the group, followed by an interactive question and answer discussion with OSS staff. Ultimately, a prioritized list was developed that led to the identified three topical areas of training, physical and technical security, and human capital addressed in this review.
 

 

Recommendation:
 

Upon review of the report, provide staff guidance on prioritization and implementation.

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The 2019 – 2020 Standard School Year Calendar was developed with a focus on instruction and learning time for students. Summary/Background (Key Points): 

2018 marked the third year of the FCPS Employee Engagement Survey. 21,203 employees (approximately 75 percent) completed the survey. This is a participation increase of 10 percentage points from last school year. Overall employee engagement increased for the third straight year. When asked what word best describes how you feel about your work, 8 of the top 10 one word responses were positive, including Happy, Rewarding, and Exciting; however, Overwhelmed remains the top word used to describe how employees feel about their work.
