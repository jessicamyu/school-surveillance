Veterans Day - November 11, 2019

 

Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.  Many schools have special events, to which they invite veterans and members of the school community on active duty military.

  

 

 

American Education Week - November 18-22, 2019

 

American Education Week was established in 1921 by the National Education Association (NEA) to raise public awareness of the importance of public education in America.  This year’s theme, Reach. Educate. Inspire, reminds all Americans of the importance of public education and offers the opportunity to celebrate public education and honor those who ensure that every child receives a quality education. As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week features a special observance on each day of the week, including parents, education support professionals, and substitute educators. 

 

 

 

  

Geography Awareness Week - November 10-16, 2019

 

Established by presidential proclamation more than 25 years ago, this annual public awareness program encourages citizens young and old to think and learn about the significance of place and how we affect and are affected by it.  Too many young Americans are unable to make effective decisions, understand geo-spatial issues, or even recognize their impacts as global citizens.  Geography Awareness week is observed in November and promotes what geography is, why it is important and the relevance of a geographic education in preparing citizens to understand and debate pressing social and environmental issues and problems.

Subject:  Award of Contract - University of Wisconsin ELP Assessments

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

                       

Other Staff Present:  Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                   Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services

                                   Michelle R. Pratt, director, Procurement Services

                                  Richard Pollio, director, ESOL Services

 

Meeting Category:  November 7, 2019 – Regular Meeting

 

Ignite Link:  Strategic Plan Goal 1:  Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Instructional Services Department recommends that the School Board award a contract to the University of Wisconsin for the provision of English language proficiency (ELP) assessments.  These assessments are mandated by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) for use by all public schools in Virginia.

 

The total estimated payment to the University of Wisconsin for this assessment for the 2019-2020 school year is expected to be $1.1M; the payment amount is based upon the total number of assessments submitted for scoring in a given school year.  Policy 5011 requires that the School Board approve “any contract or contract amendment awarded between the School Board and other governmental entity that exceeds $250,000.”  Policy 5011.3 (III)(A)(1).

 

These assessments also comply with federal legislation [Section 3113(b)(2) and Section 1111 (b) (7) of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 2015] and related guidance from the VDOE, which requires that all English learners (ELs):

 

     1)  be assessed in English language proficiency upon entry into a Virginia public school; and

     2)  be assessed annually in English language proficiency.  

 

The assessment suite from the University of Wisconsin, developed by the World-Class Instructional Design and Assessment (WIDA) Consortium, measures an English learner’s proficiency in English reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills for the dual purposes of establishing the student’s eligibility for English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) services and monitoring the student’s progress in acquiring English proficiency.

 

Assessments within the WIDA suite are:

  • WIDA Assessing Comprehension and Communication in English State to State for English Language Learners® 2.0 (WIDA ACCESS for ELLs 2.0), used for annual assessment of most ELs
  • WIDA Alternate ACCESS for ELLs, used for annual assessment of certain ELs with significant cognitive abilities
  • WIDA-ACCESS Placement Test (W-APT), used for entering kindergarten ELs
  • WIDA Screener, used for ELs entering or re-entering FCPS from outside Virginia public schools
  • WIDA Measure of Developing English Language (MODEL), used for ELs entering in second semester of kindergarten or first semester of grade 1

 

The Virginia Board of Education adopted the WIDA ACCESS for ELLs 2.0 in 2007 and the WIDA Alternate ACCESS for ELLs in 2013 as the state-approved ELP annual assessments for EL students. 

 

In FCPS, these assessments are used to:

  • Determine students’ ELP level, eligibility for ESOL services, and eligibility for testing accommodations and exemptions;
  • Calculate EL progress as part of the combined rate for English Achievement and English Achievement Gap school quality indicators within the Standards of Accreditation (SOA);
  • Help teachers and families understand students’ current level of English language proficiency and progress toward proficiency along the developmental continuum;
  • Provide teachers with information they can use to plan for and support student learning;
  • Produce data necessary to calculate annual ESOL staffing allocations for each school; and
  • Generate information required for annual application for federal Title III, Part A funding.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (i) award the contract to the University of Wisconsin for English language proficiency assessments as required by the Virginia Department of Education, for the 2019-2020 school year, and (ii) authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

7.01 Governance Committee recommended revisions to Strategic Governance Manual including Citizen Participation and Forum Process[SB;WS 11/14/19; Action 11/18/19; accept the Governance Committee recommendations for revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual including Citizen Participation and Forum Process]

Type

Meeting Category: Regular Meeting, November 7, 2019

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Governance Committee Proposed Forum Process

A. The School Board Forum process should be officially embedded into our Regular SB Meeting Agenda, and renamed “Board Matters”.

B. What now is called “Board Matters” will be referred to as “Final Remarks” or “Board Member Updates” (similar to the “Superintendent Updates”.

C. Board members should submit their “Board Matters “ topics (and brief, written summaries) to the Board Chair and the Board Clerk at least 48 hours in advance of the Regular Business Meeting, unless a topic is time-sensitive. This written proposal will be posted to Boarddocs, and at minimum emailed to the full Board, the Superintendent and Deputy Superintendent.

D. The proposed “Board Matters” will be placed on the Business Agenda, right after “Action Items”.

E. Approximately 1 hour will be allotted for “Board Matters” (with a maximum of 2 topics per meeting) unless the Board votes to extend this portion of the Business Agenda.

 

Rationale:

For more than 7 years, the School Board has experienced persistent challenges in consistently holding its Forum meetings due to the existing time-constraints of the Closed Session and the Regular Business Meeting. Having discussed and reviewed the success of the Board of Supervisors’ Board Matter process, it is recommended that the School Board adopt a similar approach.

 

 

Recommendation: That the Board accept the Governance Committee recommended revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual and Forum process, and Public Engagement Committee recommended revisions to the Citizen Participation procedures.
