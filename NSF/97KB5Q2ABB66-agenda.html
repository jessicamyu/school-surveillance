Staff Contact:            Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:    May 16, 2013 – Work Session No. 117

 

Subject:                    FY 2014 Approved Budget       

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion                                                  

 

Key Points:

 

 

 On February 7, 2013, the School Board adopted the FY 2014 Advertised Budget.  The County Board of Supervisors subsequently adopted their FY 2014 budget on April 30, 2013, and included funding that was $61.7 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget.  The Virginia General Assembly adopted its 2012-2014 budget, as amended, on April 3, 2013. 

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue reflects a net reduction of $44.7 million as compared to the FY 2014 Advertised Budget.  Funding from the county is $61.7 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by an increase in state revenue of $2.6 million and a $14.4 million increase in the budgeted beginning balance resulting from savings realized during the FY 2013 Third Quarter Budget Review.  Revenue from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $0.7 million offset by a $0.7 million increase in tuition, fees, and other revenue.

 

Expenditures:

In total, expenditures reflect a net reduction of $44.7 million as compared to the FY 2014 Advertised Budget and include the following reductions: $3.7 million in salary adjustments; $7.0 million in employee benefit adjustments; $0.2 million in the cost of membership growth and changes in student demographics; $1.5 million from a freeze in the growth of school-based clerical positions; and $2.0 million in utilities.  These reductions are offset by an increase of $0.2 million for EpiPen replacement. In addition to these required adjustments, the School Board will need to identify reductions of $30.5 million prior to the budget being adopted on May 23, 2013.

 

To meet the required level of expenditure reductions, the Superintendent has identified reductions totaling $31.9 million, which are detailed on page four of the agenda item.

 

The county included a reduction to the middle school after school program of $0.2 million and suggested that FCPS charge fees to make up for the decrease in county funding.  FCPS has elected to reduce program expenditures rather than implement fees.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2014 Approved Budget as detailed in the FY 2014 Advertised Budget and modified with the adjustments included in this agenda item.

 

FY 2014 Approved Budget Main Motion:

 

I move that the School Board adopt the FY 2014 Approved Budget, as detailed in the FY 2014 Advertised Budget, and as detailed in the agenda item to include Section I, Operating Fund Revenue Adjustments, with total reductions of $44.7 million, and Section II, Operating Fund Required Expenditure Adjustments items A through E, with total reductions of $14.2 million, and with the following modifications to the agenda item: 

1) replacing Section II.F., Reduction Placeholder of $30.5 million, with the following:

a) increasing funding to change the one percent market scale adjustment (MSA) to a two percent MSA effective January 1, 2014, at $3.0 million

b) recognizing additional state incentive compensation revenue of $6.3 million

c) eliminating funding for a one percent ERFC shift at $13.5 million

d) eliminating the increase in funding for additional advanced academic teachers at $2.0 million

e) eliminating funding for extended time for teachers at $3.5 million

f)  eliminating the funding for pre-k (FECEP) expansion at $3.0 million

g) reducing the funding for world languages expansion by $0.7 million and 9 positions

h) reducing the increase in funding for bus replacement by $1.0 million

i)  eliminating the increase in funding for major maintenance at $3.6 million
