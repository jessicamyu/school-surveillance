Designate the second campus at the Eastern Fairfax Elementary School site "Bailey's Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences" (Cluster 3; presented as new business 3/20/14)

[Note: This agenda item has been updated since March 20, 2014, to reflect a correction to the “West Campus” total voting points and the “Thurgood Marshall Campus” proposed name, and to clarify the language of the recommended Board action.]

 

Staff Contact:         Douglas A. Tyson, Assistant Superintendent Cluster 3

 

Meeting Category:  April 10, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 17

 

Subject:                  Naming of Campus - Eastern Fairfax Elementary School site (Bailey's campus)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

 

Key Points: 

 

Parents, students, staff and community members attended the March 19 meeting to consider and recommend names for the new campus.  As residents checked in to vote, they were given six different-colored dots.  Three would be used in the first round using a weighted system to vote on the entire list of names recommended for the new campus.  The fourth and fifth dots would be used in a second round of weighted voting, if it was needed.  After presentations, questions and audience discussion, a total of eight names were recommended and the voting process was explained as follows:

  • Each voter would place their first three dots on their first, second, and third choices.
  • They could vote only once for any particular name.
  • Participants voted for their top three choices using a weighted point system (first choice = 3 points, second choice = 2 points, and third choice = 1 point).

The three names that received the highest point total during the first round of voting were Bailey's Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences, Bailey's Rising Tigers Elementary School and Bailey's Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - Thurgood Marshall Campus.  The complete set of data derived from the first round of voting is listed below.  Sixty three parents, staff, and community members registered to vote.

 

First Round of Voting

 

Bailey's Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 49 points

Bailey's Elementary School - West Campus - 47 points

Bailey's Corners Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 45 points

Bailey's Elementary Rock Stars - 39 points

Bailey's Tigers School - 8 points

Bailey's Rising Tigers Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 60 points

Bailey's Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - Thurgood Marshall Campus - 90 points

Bailey's Fort Buffalo School for the Arts and Sciences - 18 points

 

Using their fourth and fifth dots, each voter was asked to vote for one name.  Each dot represented one vote.  Votes were weighted.  First choice = 3 points, second choice = 2 points.

 

Second Round of Voting

 

Bailey's Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 99 points

Bailey's Rising Tigers Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 101 points

Bailey's Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - Thurgood Marshall Campus - 103 points

 

Using their sixth and final dot, each voter was to vote for one name.  There was no weighting.

 

Third Round of Voting

 

Bailey's Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 29 points

Bailey's Rising Tigers Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 7 points

Bailey's Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - Thurgood Marshall Campus - 23 points

 

Because the vote was so close for the top two, the consensus in the room was to have a run off of the top two choices.  All voters in the room were recertified and each voter was given one more voting dot which they used to select their top choice.

 

Run Off

 

Bailey's Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - 38 points

Bailey's Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences - Thurgood Marshall Campus - 25 points

 

It should be noted that the name for the existing Bailey's Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences will remain intact and will not be impacted by the name of the new campus.  The new campus is scheduled to open in September 2014

 

Recommendation:    

 

That the School Board designate the second campus at the Eastern Fairfax Elementary School site "Bailey's Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences."

Subject:                  Standard School Year Calendar - Snow Makeup Days

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:             Legal Requirement                                  

 

Key points:

 

On December 6, 2012, the Board approved the 2013-2014 school calendar.  That calendar provided a plan for 10 makeup days if school was cancelled throughout the school year.  During the 2013-2014 school year, there were 11 days when school was cancelled, resulting in the requirement for another makeup day to be added to the calendar.  The 11th snow day occurred on March 17, 2014.  Therefore, the staff recommendation is to add Wednesday, June 25, 2014, as the 11th makeup day and designate June 25 as the last day of school for 2013-2014.

 

Employees:  Recommendations below

 

Keep as currently published on the employee calendar.  This will be the same day as the last day for students for the following contract lengths:

 

June 25

 

 

 

194-day teacher- and unified-scale personnel last workday

195-day food services personnel last workday

197-day food services personnel last workday

198-day teacher-scale personnel last workday

Keep as currently published on the employee calendar:

June 26

 

199-day unified-scale personnel last workday (office assistants)

 

Several summer programs including on-line courses, credit recovery, and SOLs require staff to report on June 26, 2014, therefore it will not be necessary to modify the schedule of these programs.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (1) reaffirm the approved 2013-2014 Standard School Year Calendar, including the employee contract end dates, as detailed in the agenda item, and (2) identify an additional school day by designating Wednesday, June 25, 2014, as the last day of school for students.

Key Points:                                   

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2014-2015, as required by VDOE Superintendent’s memo #335-13, dated December 20, 2013.  This report will be used by the Virginia Department of Education in preparing the state plan for special education.  As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-proscribed abbreviated format on a date no later than May 16, 2014.  

 

The 2014-2015 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $32,313,328. This amount is equal to the final 2013-2014 award as stated in by Superintendent’s memo #189-13 dated July 26, 2013 (revised August 23, 2013). Estimated expenditures include cost associated with maintaining a mandatory 15% set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS), and a mandatory private school set-aside of 0.5%.  Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 417.1 FTE positions.

 

The 2014-2015 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $730,977. This amount is equal to the 2013-2014 award.  These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for preschool-age students.  Estimated expenditures include cost associated with a mandatory private school set-aside of 1.3%. Section 619 funds six FTE positions.

 

The amounts above reflect no change from the 2013-14 award.  Adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.  None of the above represents new or additional operating resources.

 

The Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) reviewed the plan and funding application at its March 12, 2014 meeting.

2.  Amend or reject the plan and application and provide direction to staff for further action.

That the School Board approve the 2014-2015 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool Grant Application, as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:          Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  April 10, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 17

 

Subject:                  Change Order Marshall HS Project - County Initiated Storm Water Enhancement Project

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Department of Public Works & Environmental Services Storm Water Planning Division (SWP) approached the Office of Design and Construction to initiate a Storm Water Enhancement Project (SWEP) on the George C. Marshall High School campus during the renovation project.  This initiative is part of an on-going collaboration between SWP and D&C to identify and install storm water facilities on FCPS property that will help to meet the County’s future MS-4 permit obligations.

 

The SWEP consists of the installation of a large below-grade cistern (concrete tank) that will intercept the piped storm water collected from the entire campus and building.  The water captured by the cistern will be filtered, sanitized, and used to irrigate the existing natural turf athletic fields.  The work requires replacement of existing storm sewer pipes, installation of a large underground cistern, installation of a small building to house the pumps and other equipment; installation of a brand-new irrigation system on all natural turf athletic fields, installation of a paved access road to the pump building from the parking lot; installation of a new water main to the pump building to be used during periods of insufficient rainfall; installation of an electric service; and installation of an internet connection in order to monitor the pump operation remotely.

 

SWP has committed to fund the entire construction of the Cistern Project.  They have also committed to monitoring and maintaining the new facility.

 

Dustin Construction Inc. (the Contractor currently in charge of the renovation of Marshall High School) has submitted a proposed change order in the amount of $2,057,460.

 

To-date, SWP has transferred a total of $2,072,175 to D&C to fund this project.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent, or Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute the Storm Water Enhancement Project Contract Change Order on behalf of the School Board, as detailed in the agenda item, and as shown on the County approved site plan to Dustin Construction Inc. for the amount of $2,057,460.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
