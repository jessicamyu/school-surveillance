Subject:  Staffing Standards Review by Educational Resource Strategies

 

Staff Contact: Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services  

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                 Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category: February 8, 2022 – Work Session   

 

School Board Action Required: Information

                    

Ignite Link: Goal 4 - Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background:

On February 18, 2021, as part of the omnibus motion to adopt the FY22 Approved Budget, the following amendment was included:

“that the Superintendent be directed to review staffing standards for counselors, psychologists, social workers, nurses, ESOL teachers, reading/math specialists and classroom teacher positions, including a multi-year plan to achieve the best practices ratios, prioritized with an equity focus, and to be completed in the fall prior to the preparation for the FY23 Proposed Budget.”

The motion was unanimously approved by the School Board.

In late July 2021, Education Resource Strategies (ERS) was awarded the contract to do this work. ERS is a national non-profit that partners with school districts, individual schools, and state leaders to assist them in how they use resources (people, time, and money).

ERS has partnered with more than 40 school systems since 2005, as well as several states. They focus on the big picture—how resources work together to create strategic systems that support strong schools.

In addition to their vast experience with K-12 generally, they also provide access to an invite-only membership-based organization that brings together the strategic financial leaders of large county-wide and suburban school divisions. The FCPS Superintendent also is a member of a national consortium for county-wide and suburban Superintendents through ERS.

ERS’ knowledge of FCPS and its systems, its expertise in K-12 funding, as well as its existing relationships with other divisions have resulted in a report that provides an analysis of FCPS staffing ratios by campus for targeted student-facing positions as well as a comparison to State Standards of Quality (SOQs) and practices of other large county school systems to identify patterns of difference between systems and across FCPS schools.
