Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Category: January 11, 2010 - Work Session No. 70

Subject: Ad Hoc Committee Report, Phase I, Southwestern Regional Planning Study

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Key Points:

The Ad Hoc Committee (AHC) for the Southwestern Regional Planning Study (SRPS) was formed with a primary objective to review and validate/determine the issues and the appropriate scope of study for the SRPS. The attached report contains an executive summary with reports attached from each of five (5) subcommittees. The Phase I report included review of school capacity calculations, enrollment projections and demographics, capacity balances, feeder school and program information. The committee specifically reviewed renovations challenges and costs for Clifton Elementary School and the feasibility of the Liberty Middle School site for the development of a new elementary school. Staff provided data, maps, and information as requested by committee chairs and the various subcommittees.

DISCUSSION:

The committee recognizes that the epicenter of the overcrowding to be generally north of Route 29 between Eagle View and Colin Powell Elementary Schools. The committee generally believes that the scope of the study could be expanded to include alternate sites which may be closer to the epicenter of overcrowding than the Liberty site. It has also been suggested that additional schools be added to the scope of study to include Waples Mill, Navy, Lees Corner, Silverbrook, and Terra Centre to the study. If additional schools are included, representatives from these schools will need to be appointed to participate in Phase II of the Ad Hoc Committee’s regional planning study.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board accept the Phase I report and provide guidance on expanding the scope of study and issues for Phase II, exploring solutions and the advantages and disadvantages of potential solutions.

    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services

    Meeting Type: January 11, 2010 - Work Session No. 70

    Subject: FY 2011-2015 Capital Improvement Program

    Type: Discussion

    KEY POINTS:

    The FY 2011-2015 Capital Improvement Program provides five year enrollment projections, capacity calculations, and renovation and infrastructure requirements. Detailed background information supporting CIP recommendations are contained in the Cluster Data compact disc attached to the FY 2011-2015 Capital Improvement Program booklet and in the attachment to this agenda item.

    The Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2011-2015 Capital Improvement Program Summary can be found in the attachment to the Capital Improvement Program booklet.

    DISCUSSION:

    Due to the uncertainty of budget reductions in the operating fund that could have significant impacts on facilities, there are no recommended new capacity enhancement projects contained in this year’s CIP. Examples of potential program changes that could impact facilities include full day kindergarten, music, art, and foreign language immersion. Even increases in class size could have major impacts on school facilities.

    There also are no recommended changes to the current schedule of renovation projects pending decisions on whether capacity enhancement projects are needed and pending the recommended discussion on our current scope of renovations (see below). Capital funds not yet committed to projects are recommended to be held in reserve until operating fund decisions are known.

    An analysis of the current funding stream for the renovation cycle is provided for consideration. The analysis reflects on the School Board’s goal to renovate all schools within a 25-30 year cycle and the reality of school renovations occurring on a 40 year cycle given the current funding constraints. The Board is asked to discuss and consider an option which would reduce the scope of renovations as a means by which we could achieve a renovation cycle shorter than 40 years.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve the FY 2011-2015 Capital Improvement Program as described in the narrative and in the Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2011 -2015 Capital Improvement Program Summary.
