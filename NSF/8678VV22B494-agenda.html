The winner of the 2009 Distinquished School Resource Officer award is PFC Fred Chambers, Carson Middle School.

Fairfax County Public Schools and the Fairfax County Police Department have established a mutually beneficial relationship for more than a decade. One of the hallmarks of this relationship has been the School Resource Officer (SRO) program. SROs help ensure a safe and peaceful learning environment for students, and provide school administrators and staff with the law enforcement resources and expertise necessary to maintain order, prevent truancy, and enforce the rules and codes of student conduct.

A committee was created to establish and select the Distinguished School Resource Officer Award to annually recognize the efforts of outstanding SROs. The 2009 selection committee members are Fred Ellis, Director of the Office of Safety and Security, Cecy Krill, Cluster III Assistant Superintendent, and Lieutenant Daniel Townsend, School Liaison Commander.

There were a total of 13 School Resource Officers nominated by their principals. These nominees are stellar examples of the outstanding officers working in our schools. They should be very proud of their nomination.

RESOLUTION
NAMING ROOM 403 AT
ROBINSON SECONDARY SCHOOL
“THE DENTON D. STOKES MUSIC ROOM”

WHEREAS, Denton D. Stokes has given 32 years of service to Fairfax County Public Schools as director of bands at Fort Hunt High School, at Marshall High School, and, for the last 11 years, at Robinson Secondary School; and

WHEREAS, he has brought pride, dignity, and recognition to Robinson Secondary School through performances at local, state, and national events and through accolades such as the Virginia Honor Band and the Sudler Flag of Honor; and

WHEREAS, he has served as an ambassador of Robinson Secondary School and Fairfax County Public Schools through his service to the Virginia Music Educators Association both as district chairperson and as president of the Virginia Band and Orchestra Directors Association; and

WHEREAS, he has guided three decades of students to be more than they thought they could be by demanding diligence, self-discipline, and self-respect, and, through his leadership, he has motivated students to become better citizens by fostering an awareness of and respect for the importance of the individual to the success of the whole; and

WHEREAS, his passion for learning, for music, and for living have inspired his students to incorporate a love for the same in their own lives;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names room 403 at Robinson Secondary School “The Denton D. Stokes Music Room” in appreciation of Mr. Stokes’s service, dedication, and inspiration to the students of Robinson Secondary School, the citizens of Virginia, and the cause of music education in the United States.

Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Week - May 25-29, 2009

The adult and community education program of Fairfax County Public Schools began with an action of the School Board in 1955, and, in the 54 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills and completed their high school educations.

Through the years adults have learned new languages, acquired job skills, transitioned to new careers, and grew as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to Adult & Community Education to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education has made major contributions to the school system as a laboratory of innovation for the piloting of new programs and services in support of pre-K-12 education through family literacy, summer programs, extended day programs, enrichment courses, and driver education.

Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner – as well as provision of remediation and enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day – ACE has helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, Chief Financial Officer, Financial Services

Meeting Type: May 21, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 20

SUBJECT: FY 2010 Approved Budget

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:
      This agenda reflects actions by the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors (BOS) on the School Board’s FY 2010 budget request, changes in state revenue due to final actions taken by the General Assembly, and changes due to pending receipt of federal stimulus funds from the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009 (ARRA) in FY 2010.
KEY POINTS:
      In its FY 2010 Adopted Budget Plan, the BOS did not approve an increase in the county transfer, which holds the transfer to the amount received in FY 2009 and is $56.8 million less than the School Board’s original request. Final General Assembly actions resulted in a reduction of $2.1 million in sales tax revenue offset by an increase in basic aid.

      Federal ARRA funding includes $23.7 million from the State Fiscal Stabilization Fund (SFSF), $18.9 million in IDEA funds, and $6.7 million in Title I funds (reflected in the Grants and Self-Supporting Fund). Using ARRA funds, $5.6 million in advertised budget reductions were restored, more than $32.2 million in grim budget reductions were averted, and $4.9 million will be used to implement other innovative programs. Title I ARRA funds will be used to provide extended learning opportunities and other initiatives in Title I schools.

      Using the most recent data, the fuel budget is decreased by $2.6 million, and the membership growth cost is reduced by $3.8 million. This agenda item also includes a $1.4 million increase in the annual IDEA award, a $0.2 million increase for AP/IB tests, $0.3 million for student achievement goal projects, and $4.7 million in savings from bell schedule adjustments.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board adopt the FY 2010 Approved Budget, to include the Superintendent’s proposed revenue and expenditure adjustments, as detailed in the agenda item, and with the following revisions: (1) restoring funding of $0.6 million for Advanced Academic Centers and Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology student transportation and reducing the amount maintained in the energy volatility reserve by $0.6 million; and (2) increasing high school annual parking fees from $150 to $200 and allocating the additional projected revenue of $0.3 million to the staffing reserve.

BOARD MEMBER AMENDMENTS:
      1. Amend the main motion by restoring funding of $0.4 million for transportation of all academy students and reducing the amount maintained in the energy volatility reserve by $0.4 million. (Hone)

      The amendment passed 7-5 with Ms. Bradsher, Mr. Center, Ms. Hone, Mrs. Kory, Mr. Raney, Mr. Storck, and Mrs. Strauss voting "aye"; and Mr. Gibson, Mr. Moon, Mr. Niedzielski-Eichner, Mrs. Smith, and Mrs. Wilson voting "nay."

      2. Amend the main motion by directing that the first Advanced Placement (AP) test taken by a student each year be funded by FCPS, instituting a $25 fee for the second AP test, and instituting a fee of $50 for each additional test beyond the second test in a single year, with the exception of students who are eligible for free and reduced-price meals, and allocating the projected additional revenue of $0.5 million to the staffing reserve. (Niedzielski-Eichner)

      The amendment failed 5-7 with Mr. Center, Mr. Moon, Mr. Niedzielski-Eichner, Mr. Raney, and Mrs. Smith voting "aye"; and Ms. Bradsher, Mr. Gibson, Ms. Hone, Mrs. Kory, Mr. Storck, Mrs. Strauss, and Mrs. Wilson voting "nay."

The format of these studies is in accordance with the scope authorized by the Board, which brings forward a single option for consideration for each study. Both studies have been undertaken in a slightly modified calendar and process, which included community informational meetings and outreach, backpack notices, press releases, and web site postings.

Belvoir Elementary with the reassignment of the Woodlawn Village housing area to Woodlawn Elementary School. Implementation is proposed for the 2009-10 school year.         Annandale: This study is limited to Annandale and Falls Church High Schools and Lake Braddock Secondary School and Poe and Glasgow Middle Schools. A single option is recommended in order to relieve over-enrollment at Annandale High and Poe Middle Schools. Implementation is proposed for the 2010-11 school year. Rising 8th and 12th graders from Poe Middle and Annandale High Schools would be grandfathered consistent with Board policy. Staff also recomemends grandfathering those 11th grade Annandale students who are declared IB diploma candidates. Staff is reviewing potential transfer options for students at the middle school level who may wish to move to their new school for the 2009-10 school year to avoid having to attend three different schools over a three-year period.

      Meeting
      May 21, 2009 - Regular meeting No. 20
      Category
      6. New Business - 11:05 p.m.
      Type
      Information

      Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

      Meeting Category: May 21, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 20
      Subject: Condemnation of Property

      School Board Action Required: Information

      Related To: Operational Expectation B. Transportation

      Key Points:
          The Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (the "Department") has planned certain traffic flow and parking improvements at the Lorton Center administrative site, including an off-site drive aisle, to improve bus parking and circulation (the "Project").

          The Department has negotiated with an adjacent property owner, Inova Health Care Services ("Inova"), to convey to the School Board a temporary access easement and a grading and temporary construction easement (collectively, the "Easements") across a portion of Inova's property adjacent to Lorton Center to allow construction of the drive aisle. In the course of negotiating the Easements, the Department's surveyor/engineer discovered a small 0.031-acre triangle shaped parcel of land (the "Property") located directly in the path of the proposed drive aisle at the common boundary of the Lorton Center parcel (Tax Map Parcel 107-4-((1))-74) and the two Inova parcels (Tax Map Parcels 107-4-((1))-75A and 80). Neither Inova nor the School Board owns the Property. The School Board needs to acquire the Property in order to construct the drive aisle and complete the Project.

          A subsequent title report prepared by Walker Title, Inc. determined that the ownership status of the Property is unclear. The Property was either improperly described or inadvertently omitted from legal descriptions in deeds which form the chain of title to the Inova parcels and the School Board's Lorton Center site.

          Because of these deed errors, Walker Title was unable to determine the true legal owner of the Property. Since this parcel was omitted from the various conveyances that have occurred with the surrounding School Board and Inova properties, the title company determined that title likely is held by one of two different owners from the 1920s and 1930s, and/or their possible heirs. Given the amount of time that has passed since the named parties would have held title, it is reasonable to assume that the heirs now hold title. Also, identifying the owners is difficult in part because they sold all of their interests in the surrounding properties years ago. Because of these ambiguities, the true owner is "unknown." It is on that basis that the School Board can proceed with condemnation without attempting to purchase the property through negotiations. Pursuant to Va. Code Ann. § 25.1-204, no effort to purchase is required where the owner is "unknown."

          In its current state, the Property has no access and no material value to anyone. The condemnation process will allow the School Board to take title to the Property and proceed with the Project. A sum equal to the appraised value of the land, $1,350, will be deposited with the Circuit Court. This compensation will be held by the Court on behalf of the true owners if and when they ever come forward.

          The School Board has the authority to condemn this property pursuant to Va. Code § 22.1-127. It is required by Va. Code Ann. § 15.2-1903(B) to hold a public hearing to approve the condemnation prior to initiating condemnation proceedings.

      Recommendation:
          That the School Board (i) determine that the Project (hereinafter defined) is a necessary public use and authorize its completion, (ii) authorize the acquisition of the Property (hereinafter defined) by condemnation or other means to allow construction of the Project and (iii) authorize the Chief Operating Officer to execute on behalf of the School Board all necessary documents to accomplish such acquisition, including, without limitation, a Certificate of Take, all as approved by School Board counsel.
