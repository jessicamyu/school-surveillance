In honor of “Disability History Awareness Month” in October, students with disabilities have developed a resource called “The Guidebook to Planning an Inclusion Project” to help school communities raise awareness of disabilities and the importance of building inclusive school communities. The guidebook was created by students with the Virginia Board for People with Disabilities’ Youth Leadership Forum in collaboration with the Virginia Department of Education’s I’m Determined Project.

 

The guidebook includes sample activities and resources for use by all grade levels to promote disability awareness and inclusiveness. It also provides resources and templates schools can use to plan and carry out their own Inclusion Project events. The sample activities in the guide were successfully piloted during 2015-2016 by schools in Northumberland County and Radford.

The Guide to Planning an Inclusion Project is now available on the Virginia Department of Education website at doe.virginia.gov.

Staff Contact:  Marty Smith, Chief of Staff

 

Meeting Category:  December 1, 2016 – Regular Meeting  

 

Subject: 2017-2018 School Year Calendar

 

School Board Action Required:  Action

 

Key Points:

 

As directed by the School Board, staff developed a calendar with a Pre-Labor Day start.  Section 22.1-79.1 of the Virginia Code gives local school boards the option to apply for a Pre Labor Day Start waiver from VDOE.  FCPS currently qualifies for the waiver because of the number of snow days in recent years. 

 

In developing the Standard School Calendar 2017-2018, staff sought feedback from various stakeholder groups, including the Superintendent’s Teacher Advisory Council, the teacher associations, Fairfax County Council of PTAs, the principal associations, and the support employee associations.  Two versions of the calendar, Draft 7 and Draft 8, were shared with the School Board during the November 7, 2016, Work Session. 

 

A survey was developed for stakeholder input for a preferred draft and remained open until November 22nd.  Based on staff and community feedback, the recommended calendar has been posted for School Board approval.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the recommended 2017-2018 School Year Calendar.

Adopt the Fairfax County School Board’s 2017 State and Federal Legislative Program

Staff Contact:  Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category: December 1, 2016 Regular  

 

Subject: Fairfax County School Board 2017 State and Federal Legislative Program

 

School Board Action Required:   Action

 

Related To:  Legislation  

 

Key Points:

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to Fairfax County Public Schools.  The Program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.  The Program also identifies the Board’s policy priorities for the upcoming year.

 

The Fairfax County School Board’s 2017 State and Federal Legislative Program includes additions, subtractions and formatting changes. These changes are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate, to incorporate staff recommendations, and to update, simplify and streamline existing position language. 

 

The Board discussed staff recommendations for changes to the draft 2017 State and Federal Legislative Program at their November 7, 2016 work session.  Consensus recommendations from that work session, including the incorporation of a new letter/number position identifier system throughout the document, are reflected in the current draft.

 

Note that all changes found in the 2017 State and Federal Legislative Program as compared to the Board’s 2016 document, including those occurring post-work session, are identified in the Change Summary table, as attached to the Agenda Item.

 

 

Options:

Modify as desired

Proposed Board member amendments:

 

I move to amend the 2017 State and Federal Legislative Program by inserting “Impact Aid” as a new sub-header after the Title I sub-header with the position language included below, and by deleting the phrase “to offset the local impact of federally-connected students and impacts associated with federal Base Realignment and Closure Commission’s (BRAC) relocation decisions (Impact Aid);” from position FED.10 (Corbett Sanders).

  • The FCSB supports full funding for the federal Impact Aid program, which is intended to offset the local impact of federally-connected students as well as those associated with federal military base relocation decisions.
  • The FCSB supports additional Impact Aid funding support to address the impacts of particular concentrations of military-connected families within localities.
  • The FCSB supports additional Impact Aid funding support for students with disabilities who receive special needs exemptions to attend military-connected schools.
  • The FCSB supports revisions to the Impact Aid eligibility identification process which would automatically “opt in” any student with a military student identifier (newly created under ESSA)

Staff Contact:  Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services  

                                                                                        

Other Staff Present:  Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:  December 1, 2016 – Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  FY 2017 Midyear Budget Review      

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, funds available are decreasing by $5.1 million. The FY 2017 beginning balance is decreasing by $1.3 million due to final year-end adjustments made after the FY 2016 Final Budget Review. The adjustments include an increase of $0.7 million in sales tax revenue based on actual receipts and expenditure increases totaling $2.1 million. In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2017 revenue adjustments include a decrease in state aid of $4.4 million due to the reduction in the compensation supplement for pay increases, including the compensation supplement component for the Academic Year Governor’s School as a result of the state’s significant revenue shortfall for FY 2016; an increase of $0.5 million for City of Fairfax tuition based on actual expenditures for FY 2016; and a net increase of $0.1 million in federal grant revenue. 

 

Recommended FY 2017 expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $9.9 million resulting primarily from a reduction of $6.8 million in compensation accounts due to higher turnover experienced beyond the initial projection; a decrease of $2.5 million resulting from savings in health plan rates due to the new pharmacy benefit contract; a decrease of $1.3 million in outstanding encumbered obligations due to expenditures reported after the FY 2016 Final Budget Review, offset by an increase of $0.5 million for early hire teacher incentives and $0.1 million in federal grant expenditures.

 

Expenditure adjustments, combined with revenue adjustments, result in available funds totaling $4.8 million recommended to be set aside to increase the FY 2018 beginning balance from $22.2 million to $27.0 million. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds and an enrollment update for FY 2017 are detailed in the attachment. Also attached is an update on the status of grants in FY 2016 and FY 2017.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2017 Midyear Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.
