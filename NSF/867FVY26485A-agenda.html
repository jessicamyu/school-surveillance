Agenda Item: Individualized Education Program (IEP) Development and Implementation
              Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA) and Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP)

Staff Contact: Alice Farling, Assistant Superintendent, Department of Special Services


Background: IDEA requires IEP teams to address the academic performance of students with disabilities and their behavior if it interferes with their learning or with the learning of others. Parents and school staff work together in the development of every child’s IEP. Functional behavior assessments and behavior intervention plans are important tools in addressing problematic behaviors.

Key Issues: Responsibilities
              Support and Training
              Questions:
                • Why are BIPs/FBAs separate documents from IEPs?
                • How is IEP information on special education students communicated to general education teachers?
                • What is being done to improve this communication?
