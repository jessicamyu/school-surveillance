Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

Meeting Type: October 15, 2007, Work Session

SUBJECT: 2008 Draft Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools.

      The attached 2008 draft Legislative Program is based on previous year’s programs as well as proposals and changes submitted this year. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.
KEY POINTS:

New Positions
    Early Childhood Education (stand alone, and in Joint Position with BOS)
    Funding for statewide English Language Proficiency (ELP) assessment (in Assessment of LEP Students)
    Changes to graduation requirements (in Standards of Accreditation)
    Childhood obesity/School Breakfast and Lunch Program reimbursements (in School Health Services and Student Wellness)
    Safe learning environments/incentive funding for safety technologies and programs/confidentiality of school safety plan reviews/monitoring of sex offender registry (in Student Safety)
      Also changed most of the specific references to the federal No Child Left Behind Act throughout the draft Program, instead referencing federal accountability and the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 (ESEA) more generally.

Deleted Positions (all achieved in 2007)
    Driver’s Education (stand alone)
    Statewide ELP assessment (in Assessment of LEP Students)
    Counting special needs students in subgroup after exit from services (in ESEA/NCLB)
    Side safety lights on buses/state charges for driver’s registries (in Student Transportation)


OPTIONS:

Modify as desired.

RECOMMENDATION:
      Accept staff recommendations for the draft 2008 FCSB Legislative Program.

Staff Contact: Deirdra McLaughlin, Chief Financial Officer

Meeting Type: October 15, 2007, Work Session

SUBJECT: Revised Fiscal Forecast for FY 2009

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
    On July 16, 2007, the Chief Financial Officer presented the FY 2009 Fiscal Forecast for Fairfax County Public Schools based upon an assumption of a zero percent county transfer which was included in the FY 2009 budget guidelines advanced by the Board of Supervisors. The fiscal forecast accounted for estimated baseline costs associated with increased costs for health and retirement, inflation, worker’s compensation, insurance, GASB, and other commitments earmarked for program expansion. It also included a 3.0% market scale adjustment for all employees resulting in a deficit of $145.2 million once all liabilities were considered.

    Subsequent to this, we have received updated information on the costs for health, retirement and insurance which necessitates a revision to our original forecast. Additionally, we anticipate the availability of an additional $20.0 million which will be added to the beginning balance. The total adjustment to the original forecast amounts to $40.2 million which decreases the forecasted deficit to $105.0 million.

    Elimination of the 3.0% market scale adjustment and all planned commitments for expansion of programs like teacher leadership, FLES, SASI, and full-day kindergarten leaves the system with a deficit of $22.9 million.

KEY POINTS:
    The original fiscal forecast presented a deficit of $145.2 million. The deficit of $22.9 million takes into account the following actions:
        • Lowers the assumptions for health insurance and retirement rate increases
        • Decreases funding for inflation and health, workers compensation, and GASB reserves
        • Increases the beginning balance to $50 million
        • Eliminates the market scale adjustment (COLA) for employees
        • Removes funding for employee initiatives
        • Stops planned expansion of Full-day Kindergarten, Foreign Language in the Elementary Schools, and the Teacher Leadership Programs
        • Stops the replacement of the student information system (SASI)
        • Eliminates funding for bus radios
        • Cancels the revision of the high school staffing formula
        • Rejects the High Schools of the Future Program proposal

    Even after eliminating the market scale adjustment and planned program expansions that support FCPS’ strategic governance initiative, FCPS is facing a deficit of $22.9 million.

OPTIONS:
    Two sets of options to eliminate the deficit have been prepared for consideration. One set of options reflects systemwide budget reductions that could be implemented to address the gap. An additional set of options have been prepared that reflect specific programmatic cuts that could be considered to reach reduction targets.

SUBJECT: Policy 1710.6, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board and deleting the "note" paragraphs on pages 37 and 41 in the Strategic Governance Manual regarding Committees

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      At the September 17, 2007, work session there was consensus for the Board to have further discussion on Policy 1710.6, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board at the October 15, 2007, work session. At the September 27, 2007, regular Board meeting, Mr. Hunt introduced a motion that involved information about the advisory committees that was included in the Strategic Governance Manual, and Mr. Storck determined that the discussion on the proposed motion should be included with any discussion on Policy 1710.

      Mr. Hunt's proposed motion: That the Board remove from the School Board’s adopted Strategic Governance Manual, Section 4, Board Committees, the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.B. Legally Required Committees (page 37) and the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.C. Other Committees (page 41), and confirm that all matters related to School Board advisory committees be governed by the current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board.
