Subject:    Strategic Plan Goal 3: Premier Workforce

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:   Strategic Plan – Goal 3 Report

 

Key Points:

 

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan.

 

The School Board is to receive quarterly updates/reports on the Strategic Plan, focused on the work accomplished and the results received. In addition, the report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 3, Premier Workforce. In addition, a report on the work accomplished for the implementation of the class size regulation will be presented.

 

The following are the two overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 3, Premier Workforce:

 

Overarching Strategy 8

Recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts

 

Desired Outcomes

 

  • Recruit, hire, and retain employees to achieve a premier workforce
  • Achieve and preserve a culture of equity and fairness that will be representative of the demographics of Fairfax County
  • FCPS will embrace and value innovation
  • Employees will benefit from competitive compensation packages

 

Overarching Strategy 9

Invest in growth and leadership opportunities for all employees by providing meaningful professional development and quality feedback on performance

 

Desired Outcomes

 

  • Dynamic professional development opportunities will be available for all employees
  • FCPS will develop and maintain a performance evaluation process that is valued by all employees

Subject:                  Student Enrollment and Projections Summary

 

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

Student enrollment in Fairfax County Public Schools showed slight growth again this year. Although the forecast of moderated student growth was communicated in November 2014, the contraction continues at a greater rate than anticipated. Factors such as changing patterns in the size of the entering kindergarten class and net migration totals are the primary reasons.

 

Additionally, staff is in the process of developing a new student enrollment and demographics projection methodology based on best practices. This includes the use of Geographic Information System (GIS) technology. GIS technology pulls Student Information System (SIS) data by student ID number. It allows for improved sorting, tracking, and mapping of students based on their residence and actual school of attendance. This precision in data analysis and ability to account for each student in the projection effort is expected to improve better pyramid and school by school projection accuracy rates.

 

Further, the customized enrollment and demographic projection methodology will be based on specialized projection methodologies for unique groups of students such as students who transfer in/out of assigned schools for various program needs. It also uses smaller, neighborhood planning units within each school attendance area for the projection effort. This will allow for individually tailored projections within each school boundary based on past trends and forecasts.  

 

For today’s work session, the Facilities and Transportation Services staff have prepared information and charts that show the current conditions at our schools by pyramid and school level. Staff will provide information about the new student enrollment projection methodology which will be customized for the unique needs of Fairfax County Public Schools.

Subject:                       Update on FY 2017 Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                   

 

Key Points:

 

Agenda

  1. Presentation on the Budget Task Force’s Report
  2. Questions on the Budget Task Force’s Process
  3. Discussion of reduction themes that have emerged, both from the Task Force Report and the community during the FY 2017 Budget development process
  4. Solicitation of new fees, budget reductions, or other ideas from School Board members
  5. Fiscal forecast update

 

Budget Task Force

The Budget Task Force held 11 group meetings, participated in discussions, and engaged the community from June through November as they thoughtfully deliberated FCPS’ budget and developed recommendations to address a $50 million and a $75 million budget reduction scenario.  Their full report is attached, and a presentation summarizing the report will be presented. (Attachments #1 and #2)

 

Following the presentation, a discussion of the Budget Task Force’s process and report will follow.  As part of the discussion, the School Board is encouraged to identify themes for addressing the FY 2017 budget shortfall or themes of items that should be preserved and to discuss these themes.  Additional suggestions for addressing the FY 2017 budget that have not yet identified will be solicited from the School Board.

 

FY 2017 Fiscal Forecast Update

FCPS’ forecast includes projections for the key financial areas that drive the budget, such as revenue assumptions and expenditure assumptions, including employee salaries and benefits, student enrollment, and other requirements. In order to continue the FY 2017 budget planning discussion, an update to the fiscal forecast for the School Operating Fund for FY 2017 will be presented. (Attachment #3)

 

The shortfall contains uncertainty because there are costs and funding that are not determined until later in the budget process. Funding from the State of Virginia will not be determined until March 2016, changing enrollment projections evolve throughout next spring, and the amount of funding Fairfax County transfers to FCPS is not determined until April 2016. As a result, budget projections for FY 2017 will be updated throughout the budget process as information becomes available.
