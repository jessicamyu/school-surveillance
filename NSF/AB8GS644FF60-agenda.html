Subject:  Strategic Plan Goal 3: Premier Workforce

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion at November 7 worksession and consideration of approval at the November 14 regular meeting

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan – Goal 3 Report

 

Key Points:

 

In September 2016, the School Board approved updates to Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. This school year, 2016-2017, marks the second year of implementation of the Strategic Plan.

 

The report will outline major Strategic Plan activities for the coming year that are designed to address areas in need of improvement. This presentation and report will provide an update on the monitoring metrics and current work that support Goal 3, Premier Workforce. In addition, a report on the work accomplished for the implementation of the class size regulation will be presented.

 

The following are the two overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 3, Premier Workforce:

 

Overarching Strategy 8

Recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts 

 

Desired Outcomes

  • Recruit, hire, and, retain employees to maintain a premier workforce
  • Achieve and preserve a culture of equity and fairness for all FCPS employees
  • Employees will benefit from competitive compensation packages

 

Overarching Strategy 9

Invest in growth and leadership opportunities for all employees by providing meaningful professional development and quality feedback on performance

 

Desired Outcomes

  • Dynamic professional development opportunities will be available for all employees
  • FCPS will develop and maintain a performance evaluation process that is meaningful to all employees

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board discuss the Strategic Plan Goal 3 - Premier Workforce report as detailed in the agenda item at the November 7 worksession and consider approval of the report at the November 14 regular meeting.

Subject: 2017-2018 School Year Calendar

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

In developing the Standard School Calendar 2017-2018, staff sought feedback from various stakeholder groups, including the Superintendent’s Teacher Advisory Council, the teacher associations, Fairfax County Council of PTAs, the principal associations, and the support employee associations.  Two versions of the calendar were shared with stakeholder groups, Draft 7 and Draft 8.  The recommended calendar (Draft 8) includes a 9-Day Winter Break and Thursday, June 14, 2018, as the last day of school for students. 

 

As directed by the School Board, staff developed a calendar with a Pre-Labor Day start.  Section 22.1-79.1 of the Virginia Code gives local school boards the option to apply for a Pre Labor Day Start waiver from VDOE.  FCPS currently qualifies for the waiver because of number of snow days in recent years.  Some of the highlights from Draft 8 are below:

 

  • First Day back for Teachers      August 21
  • First Day of School                  August 28
  • Thanksgiving Break                 November 23-24
  • Winter Break                          December 20-January 1
  • Spring Break                          March 26-30
  • Last Day of School                  June 14

 

 

Recommendation:

 

School Board approve the 2017-2018 Standard School Year Calendar

Meeting Category:  November 7, 2016 – School Board Work Session

 

Subject: Pathways to Graduation

 

School Board Action Required: Information and Review of Local Graduation Requirements to Provide Increased Flexibility for Students                

 

Related To: FCPS Strategic Plan, Goal 1: Student Success

 

Key Points:    

 

The proposal for multiple pathways to graduation includes additional flexibility for local FCPS requirements while also adding options for students to pursue a pathway related to one of the nationally recognized career clusters. The pathway proposal addresses goals outlined in the strategic plan and legislative priorities related to college and career readiness while ensuring that additional flexibility in graduation requirements does not limit a student’s postsecondary opportunities. The proposal aligns with current workforce trends, FCPS student interests, and existing course offerings to increase the likelihood of community and industry support, student engagement and participation in the pathway options, and a school’s ability to successfully implement the proposed changes.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board support additional flexibility in the local FCPS graduation requirements including pathway options, adopt a policy to grant waivers from the 140 seat hour requirement, and continue advocacy to the Virginia Board of Education for additional course and exam substitutions.

Meeting Category:  November 7, 2016 – Work Session

 

Subject:   ERFC Options

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:                   

 

FCPS has hired Aon Hewitt, a provider of retirement consulting and actuarial services, to conduct a study which benchmarks FCPS’ retirement benefit levels to surrounding school divisions, Fairfax County and the federal government.  The study also included the adequacy of income replacement at retirement provided by the retirement programs that FCPS offers. 

 

On May 12, 2016, ERFC presented the history of the FCPS retirement programs along with the key provisions of the existing plans.  Aon Hewitt then provided a briefing on the results of its study, illustrating retirement benefit levels for FCPS employees at various age and service combinations with comparisons to the peer group.  Aon Hewitt also presented ERFC options for possible future consideration.

 

On July 21, 2016, as a follow-up to the May work session and as requested by the School Board, the benefit impact on new hires of the options for possible consideration were presented.  On September 19, 2016, the employer cost savings associated with those options for new hires was discussed. 

 

At this work session, the employer cost savings associated with expanding those options to include new hires and non-vested employees will be discussed.  In addition, the cost implications of closing ERFC to new hires will be discussed.  An information update on Fairfax County’s retirement system review and comparative data on teacher retirement programs nationally also will be presented. 

 

In order to incorporate any changes in the FY 18 Proposed Budget, School Board consensus on modifications, including employee groups impacted, is needed by the December 5th work session.
