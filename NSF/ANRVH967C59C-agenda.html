Subject: Justice High School – Implementation Plan and Community Fundraising Update

 

Staff Contact: Steve Lockard, Deputy Superintendent

 

Other Staff Present: Penny Gros, Principal Stuart High School, Fabio Zuluaga, Assistant Superintendent Region 2, Marty Smith, Chief Operating Officer, Leigh Burden, Assistant Superintendent Financial Services, Shakeel Yusuf, Comptroller

 

Meeting Category: Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Information, Discussion, Decision

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 2 Caring Culture

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

  • Updates will be provided on the Justice High School Implementation Plan presented to the School Board in December of 2017.
  • The School Board voted on July 27, 2017 to rename J.E.B. High School and on October 26, 2017, voted for the new name to be Justice High School.  The July motion directed staff to create a mechanism for private funding, with an expectation that private funding would pay for a substantial portion of the costs of the name change.  At its December 11, 2017 work session, the School Board reviewed the Superintendent’s implementation plan and approved the use of $283,191 from the FY2018 Flexibility Reserve, and $160,671 from the FY2019 Flexibility Reserve for identified name change costs, with any raised/donated funds used to reimburse the Flexibility Reserve.
  • Staff created a fundraising portal on the FCPS website and have worked with a volunteer community group called “The Committee for Justice High School.”  This committee has an email address to use for its fundraising efforts (committee4justicehighschool@gmail.com), but all donors are directed to the fundraising portal on the FCPS website to ensure all funds raised go through the FCPS Department of Financial Services.
  • The Committee for Justice High School proposes to pursue three components to their fundraising efforts:
    • Community based fundraising – individual events that support the name change, i.e.- “Day of Giving” planned for June
    • Grant funding – identification of corporations and foundations that have grant money
    • Establishment of an Advisory Board – to support fundraising efforts
  • The Committee for Justice High School has also provided and offered additional support to other components of the implementation plan.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board make a decision on future fundraising efforts:

  • What level of support does the School Board wish to provide to the Committee for Justice High School?

 

Considerations:

    • Continue as we have with committee fundraising to date:
      • Limited staff involvement
      • A letter of support from Superintendent

 

    • Establish a set date of September 30, 2018 to transition to school based fundraising efforts

 

    • Other

 

Superintendent’s Recommendation:

In an effort to be consistent with all other fundraising efforts happening throughout FCPS, the superintendent’s recommendation is to establish a date of September 30, 2018 to transition the work of fundraising under the purview of the school, and to provide the committee a letter of support from the superintendent.

Subject:  Regulation 2601 (SR&R)

 

Staff Contact: Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, DSS

 

Other Staff Present: Mary Ann Panarelli, director, Intervention and Prevention Services, DSS

                                  Stefan Mascoll, coordinator, Student Safety and   Wellness Office, DSS

 

Meeting Category:  Work Session - Discussion

 

School Board Action Required:  Review proposed changes

 

Ignite Link: Caring Culture

 

Problem Statement:  Regulation 2601 needs to be updated to reflect legislative changes, to clarify items, and to address concerns raised by principals and the community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

  1. Chapter I.A.1., removed sentence defining Justifiable Absence.

 

  1. Chapter I.A.2., added definition of Chronic Absence.

 

  1. Chapter I.A.3., changed definition of Unexcused Absences.

 

  1. Chapter I.A.4., updated consequences of Five or More Unexcused Days of Absences.

 

  1. Chapter I.D., added “Social Media” parental guidance. 

 

  1. Chapter I.J.1., removed “director, Office of Equity and Employee Relations”, and replaced with “Family and Student Ombudsman.”

 

  1. Chapter I.K.2., updated to refer discrimination issues to the Family and Student Ombudsman.

 

  1. Chapter II.A., added footnote stating “Students who are in kindergarten through third grade ordinarily are not subject to suspensions from school of more than three days.  Virginia law provides for exceptions where such students engage in dangerous misconduct. (See chapter II.C.3 o this regulation).”

 

  1. Chapter II.A.2.a.12., Added "(12)Possession or use of a smoking or vaping device (e.g. a vape, a juul, or a like device).  To the extent the foregoing contains alcohol, nicotine, or an illegal drug, specific consequences are listed below in this regulation." to forms of Disruptive or Inappropriate Behavior.

 

  1. Chapter II.A.3.a., clarified the term Smoking Device.

 

  1. Chapter II.C.3., added section regarding Suspension of Students in Grades K-3.

 

      L. Chapter II.C.4.d., added parenthetical "without a referral to the Division Superintendent or recommendation for reassignment.”

 

      M. Chapter II.C.4.d., II.C.7.b., added footnote stating “Any appeal of short-term suspension in conjunction with a referral to the Division Superintendent or recommendation for reassignment shall be directed to the Division Superintendent.”

 

  1. Definition of SR&R Terms Used Throughout Regulation, added definition of “Aggravating Circumstances”.

 

      O. Definition of SR&R Terms Used Throughout Regulation, updated definition of “Long-Term Suspension”.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.32P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:  Governance Committee Proposed Revisions to Policy 8335 - Wireless Telecommunication Facilities

 

Meeting Category:  May 14, 2018 - Work Session

 

Problem Statement: At the Board's December 15, 2016 forum, Ms. Hynes and Mr. Wilson requested School Board review of Policy 8335, which was inherited by the current Board, to ensure the policy reflects the wishes of the Board and takes into account any new relevant factors or technological advancements that may have been made in the almost ten years since the policy's adoption.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

At the December 15, 2016 forum, the Board requested that the Governance Committee review Policy 8335 - Wireless Telecommunication Facilities and make a recommendation to the Board.  The Governance Committee discussed the policy and regulation during several committee meetings attended by interested community members. 

 

The Committee recommends revisions to the policy to provide earlier involvement and increased input from the community and School Board and to ensure that any proposed changes are in compliance with applicable Federal, State and local laws and regulations, as detailed in the agenda item.  The revisions recommend adding language to reserve the right of the School Board to review and approve/disapprove  any application for review prior to any government approval, and ensure FCPS sites only be considered after the magisterial district(s) and at-large School Board member(s) has been informed about the site application and briefed on placement.

 

Recommendation:

 

 

That the School Board approve proposed revisions to Policy 8335 - Wireless Telecommunication Facilities at a future meeting.
