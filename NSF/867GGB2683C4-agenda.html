Agenda Item: Capital Renovation Program Options

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

Background:
      The paper and presentation were created in response to inquiries from various School Board members regarding the scale and scope of renovation projects. This paper presents the history of school renovations in FCPS, the current status of our school buildings, and what changes might be considered to accelerate renovations.
Key Issues:
      The current funding level for capital projects means that many schools will not be renovated within the 25 year cycle desired by the school system. With this in mind, the school system should consider options to address this situation while recognizing that changes are not without consequences.
School Board Discussion/Decision

      Should the School Board accept any or all of the recommended changes to the renovation program?

Staff Recommendation
      FTS believes that the reduction and reallocation of equipment funding (option 1) and the implementation of scope changes (option 2) are viable options to help reduce the length of the renovation cycle, address technology equity between schools, and provide additional funding for infrastructure needs.
Options

      1. Maintain the renovation program in its current form.
      2. Upgrade building infrastructure and utilities only.

Agenda Item: Transportation Re-Engineering


Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer


School Board Discussion/Decision:

The Office of Transportation Services will re-engineer transportation bell schedules and routing to change high school start times and to address minimum service levels not currently being accomplished. The project will be staffed by existing experts within the office who will be pulled from existing assignments. This will require that we backfill their positions during this time period. The project will be funded by the monies set aside by the School Board for this purpose in the FY 2008 budget.

The exact parameters that will be used to re-engineer the system need to be identified and agreed to by the School Board prior to beginning actual development of a new bell schedule, bus routes, etc. To this end, transportation will obtain input from interested constituencies to include specifically: principals of high, middle, elementary, and special needs schools. Because of their involvement in the MPS study, we believe we have a clear idea of the points of view of SLEEP, the County Council of PTA’s, and the Chamber of Commerce. These and other organizations certainly should be part of the process of confirming or validating the project parameters before the technical work begins.

The following time line is being considered at this time:
    Develop and confirm re-engineering parameters - May to October 2007
    Develop alternative bell schedule and performance improvements - November 2007 to February 2008
    Share schedule with community to gain their feedback - March to April 2008
    Board makes decision - April to May 2008

Options:

The work session discussion will focus on two issues:
      1. Determine the timing and participation of stakeholders.
      2. Decide whether the re-engineering be performed in-house or by consultants.

99.02 Discussion - Resolution in Support of Improving the No Child Left Behind Act
Meeting
Jun 11, 2007 - Work Session No. 94-100
Category
99. Legislative - 4:15 p.m.
Type
Information

Contact: Phillip Niedzielski-Eichner, Providence District

SUBJECT: Resolution in Support of Improving the No Child Left Behind Act

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve a resolution in support of improving the No Child Left Behind Act, urging the U.S. Congressional Delegation of the Commonwealth of Virginia to fully support H.R. 648-- also known as the No Child Left Behind Improvements Act of 2007--by becoming a co-sponsor of the bill or by supporting a comparable Senate version when it is introduced, as detailed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:

      The National School Boards Association (NSBA) put together, in consultation with its affiliate state organizations – and specifically with the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA) and Fairfax County School Board member Stu Gibson, as VSBA’s Federal Relations Chair -- a comprehensive proposal to improve NCLB.

      H.R. 648, submitted by Rep. Don Young (R-AK-At Large), fully tracks NSBA's draft legislation to strengthen accountability for improving the academic performance of children enrolled in the nation's public schools. Building on the major reforms of NCLB, H.R. 648 offers improvements that address many of the unintended consequences of the federal legislation – and its implementing regulations -- while holding states and school districts accountable for the performance of children in their care. Specifically, H.R. 648 will:
        • Increase the flexibility for states to use additional types of assessments for measuring AYP—including growth models.
        • Grant states more flexibility in assessing students with disabilities and students not proficient in English for AYP purposes.
        • Create a student testing participation range, providing flexibility for uncontrollable variations in student attendance.
        • Allow schools to target resources to those student populations who need the most attention by applying sanctions only when the same student group fails to make adequate yearly progress (AYP) in the same subject for two consecutive years.
        • Ensure that students are counted properly in assessment and reporting systems.
        • Allow supplemental services to be offered in the first year of “improvement.”
        • Strengthen federal responsibility for funding.
        • Require NCLB testing and reporting for non-public schools for students receiving Title I services.

      A similar Senate version is expected to be introduced in the near future and the resolution therefore calls upon our Virginia Senators Warner and Webb to support and be co-sponsors.

Agenda Item: Draft Policy 8120.1, School Planning


Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer


Background:
      The current policy specifies new middle schools shall have a maximum program capacity of 1,000 students and new high schools will have a maximum program capacity of 2,000 students. Recent new middle and high schools have been built with capacities exceeding these limits. Carson and Liberty middle schools were built with a capacity of 1250 students, Westfield and South County high schools were built to accommodate 2500 students.


Key Issues:
      Past discussions with the School Board have indicated a preference for smaller schools. It needs to be determined if the current policy guidance of 1000 student capacity middle and 2000 student capacity high schools should be maintained or other capacity limits should be adopted.

School Board Discussion/Decision:
      Review program capacity for middle schools and high schools.


Staff Recommendation:
      Staff recommends adoption of this policy but acknowledges that waivers may be required and requested if greater capacity is needed at specific sites in the future.

Options:
      Adjust program capacity limits for new middle schools and high schools.
