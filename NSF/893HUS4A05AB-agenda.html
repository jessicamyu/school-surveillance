Subject:                              Facilities Planning Advisory Council

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

On May 20, 2010, the School Board approved the formation of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) as a new standing committee of the Board.  FPAC will consist of 13 appointed members, one from each Fairfax County magisterial district, three at-large representatives, and one representative from the City of Fairfax. This initiative is a recommendation from the School Board’s Comprehensive Planning Development Committee (CPDC), which had been charged with designing a comprehensive facilities planning process.

 

The establishment of the FPAC will allow its members to advise and inform the School Board and school staff in the development of comprehensive, long-term plans for facilities needs in the most effective and efficient way.  Issues which may be considered by the advisory council shall include, but not be limited to, Capital Improvement Plan prioritization, program based school capacities, size and location of schools, and periodic boundary adjustments.

 

Applications for appointment to FPAC were accepted until August 15, 2010, and approximately 50 applications were received.  Each School Board member was requested to forward a maximum of three preferred candidates to the CPDC for consideration in the development of a slate of candidates representing all magisterial districts, at-large members, and the City of Fairfax.
