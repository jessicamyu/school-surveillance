RESOLUTION
NAMING THE CAFETERIA AT
CUB RUN ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
“THE CATHY BLEVINS CAFE”

WHEREAS, Cathy Blevins has been a beloved building supervisor to students, the community, and staff members at Cub Run Elementary School since the school opened in September 1986 until her retirement in March 2008; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Blevins is the first person that staff members and students see every day, and her warm smile and caring attitude show her genuine concern for students, staff members, and parents; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Blevins takes great pride in the school environment and sets high standards for herself and her team regarding how the school looks–both inside and outside. Not only is the building exceptionally clean, but she ensures a safe environment for students and staff members by keeping the cafeteria and common areas clean, the floors safe from spills, food, and water puddles, the outside walkways and entrances clear of ice and snow, and the grounds free of trash; and

WHEREAS, over the years, Mrs. Blevins has supervised a very diverse custodial staff, and she has been able to meet with each custodial staff member in a non-confrontational manner and encourage each worker to join together and work as a team. Because of this team effort, Cub Run’s custodial staff has won many awards over the years for building cleanliness and security; and

WHEREAS, all who know and work with Mrs. Blevins know that her work ethic of professionalism, dedication, and integrity also supports Cub Run’s motto: Respect, Responsibility, and Kindness;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the cafeteria at Cub Run Elementary School “The Cathy Blevins Cafe,” in recognition of her love and support of children, her commitment to a safe and clean learning environment, and her valued service to her community.

SUBJECT: FY 2008 Third Quarter Budget Review

TYPE: Information

BACKGROUND:
      This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2008 budget and to make revisions as necessary. This FY 2008 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on November 29, 2007.

KEY POINTS:
      A reduction in state sales tax revenue and the receipt of state funding to provide National Board Certified Teacher stipends, coupled with changes in federal and private grants, results in an overall decrease in revenue of $1.2 million. In anticipation of reduced revenue as detailed in the FY 2009 Advertised Budget, departments were directed to initiate expenditure reductions for the remainder of FY 2008 amounting to $1.9 million. Funding of $0.1 million from the Flexibility Reserve is being used to balance the third quarter agenda. Funding is not currently available to restore the Flexibility Reserve. We will address restoring this reserve at the FY 2008 Year End Budget Review.

      The net result of the revenue and expenditure adjustments is zero.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes in the FY 2008 Third Quarter Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.
