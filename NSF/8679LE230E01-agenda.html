RESOLUTION NAMING THE GYMNASIUM AT
TIMBER LANE ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
“THE DIANE CONNOLLY GYMNASIUM”


WHEREAS, Diane Connolly has served Timber Lane Elementary School as a beloved teacher, intersession coordinator, assistant principal, and principal, for 25 years; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Connolly fostered, in her students, a love of learning and a love for physical education and continued to nurture students throughout her years as a teacher and an administrator; and

WHEREAS, during her tenure at Timber Lane Elementary, Ms. Connolly was instrumental in implementing the modified calendar and served as the school’s first intersession coordinator, providing students with extensive intervention strategies and enrichment opportunities; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Connolly took great pride in the school environment and set high standards for herself and her staff, ensured a safe learning environment for students and staff members, and, as principal, remained committed to embracing diversity within the student body and staff and fostered a strong relationship between the community and Timber Lane Elementary; and

WHEREAS, Ms. Connolly always shared her love for physical education with students, staff members, and the community and, upon her retirement, expressed a desire for the staff to contribute to the gymnasium equipment in lieu of any gift; it is therefore fitting that the gymnasium, a place where every student may achieve physical dreams, be dedicated to Diane Connolly for her unparalleled commitment to the well-being of Timber Lane students;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the School Board of Fairfax County, Virginia, honors Diane Connolly by naming the Timber Lane Elementary School gymnasium “The Diane Connolly Gymnasium" in recognition of her many tireless years of service to the entire Timber Lane community.

National Volunteer Week - April 19-25, 2009

National Volunteer Week, which takes place April 19-25, 2009, began in 1974 when President Nixon signed an executive order establishing the week as an annual celebration of volunteering. Since then, every U.S. President has signed a proclamation promoting National Volunteer Week.

During the 2007-2008 school year, 178 schools, centers, academies, and central offices reported that 70,202 volunteers donated 972,626 hours of volunteer service to FCPS schools.

Over the past 23 years, 1,346,369 volunteers have provided a reported total of 26,588,088 hours of service to our school. The monetary value of this contribution is almost $290 million.

If our volunteers had been paid at the entry-level instructional assistant salary of $13.67 per hour, the bill for 2007-2008, alone, would have totaled over $13 million. The educational value of the work performed by our volunteers is, of course, incalculable.

    SUBJECT: Scope of Spring 2009 Boundary Studies for Ft. Belvoir Elementary School
    TYPE: Action

    BACKGROUND:

    On March 9, 2009, staff presented a recommendation for a spring boundary study for Ft. Belvoir Elementary. This initiative is based on a critical need to provide enrollment relief to Ft. Belvoir Elementary School by utilizing available capacity at Woodlawn Elementary School and reflects a change to the traditional fall time frame for boundary studies. This study would be undertaken in a slightly modified calendar and process which would include town meetings, community outreach and notices, but would not include small focus group meetings, multiple scenarios proposed by staff or a long period for public comment.

    KEY POINTS:

    This study is proposed to be limited to Ft. Belvoir Elementary and Woodlawn Elementary as the receiving school. A single Ft. Belvoir housing complex known as Woodlawn Village located along Pole Road is proposed to be re-assigned to Woodlawn Elementary School. There are approximately 200 students residing in this housing area and Woodlawn Elementary is projected to have a surplus balance of approximately 230 student spaces. The housing area proposed to be re-assigned is adjacent to the school and at least a portion of the students to be re-assigned could potentially walk to Woodlawn Elementary. This change could be implemented for the 2009-10 school year in order to provide needed interim enrollment relief at Ft. Belvoir Elementary until and if such time as a second school site could be planned and constructed on the base by the Department of Defense or Army. This proposal represents a single option. No other adjacent school has capacity to receive additional students from Ft. Belvoir Elementary. A map and background information describing this proposal is contained in Appendix A.

    OPTIONS:

    Due to the severity of overcrowding at Ft. Belvoir, there are no options.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve the spring 2009 scope of boundary study for Fort Belvoir Elementary and Woodlawn Elementary Schools based on a critical need to alleviate existing and projected over enrollment, pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments and School Closing.

    SUBJECT: Scope of Spring 2009 Boundary Studies for Annandale High School
    TYPE: Action

    BACKGROUND:

    On March 9, 2009, staff presented a recommendation for a spring boundary study for Annandale High School. This initiative is based on a critical need to provide enrollment relief to Annandale High School by utilizing available capacities at Falls Church and Lake Braddock High Schools. In addition, this study would also re-align feeder middle schools at Glasgow and Lake Braddock and provide needed enrollment relief to Poe Middle School This also reflects a change to the traditional fall time frame for boundary studies. This study would be undertaken in a slightly modified calendar and process which would include town meetings, community outreach and notices, but would not include small focus group meetings, multiple scenarios proposed by staff or a long period for public comment.

    KEY POINTS:

    This study is proposed to be limited to Annandale and Falls Church High Schools and Lake Braddock Secondary School and Poe and Glasgow Middle Schools. This study also represents a single option with several changes to be considered in order to relieve over-enrollment at Annandale, align middle and high school feeders and eliminate an attendance island. The proposal is summarized below:
            • From Annandale High School to Falls Church High School: That portion of the Annandale High School north of Rt. 236 and west of Columbia Pike that wraps around the Falls Church attendance island is proposed to be re-assigned to Falls Church High School. This has the effect of eliminating an attendance island and more importantly, potentially shifts approximately 175 students from Annandale to Falls Church.
            • From Poe Middle to Glasgow Middle: That portion of Poe which extends north and south of Rt. 50 is proposed to be re-assigned to Glasgow. This area of Poe is currently assigned to Stuart High School. This proposal has the effect of eliminating a split feeder for this portion of Poe Middle since Glasgow Middle feeds to Stuart High School and could further reduce high enrollment at Poe by approximately 130 students.
            • From Annandale High School and Poe to Lake Braddock Secondary: That portion of Annandale High School and the underlying attendance aarea of Poe Middle School south of Braddock Road and west of I-495 to Lake Braddock Secondary. This change potentially shifts approximately 100 students from Annandale to Lake Braddock and would also reassign approximately 50 students from Poe Middle to Lake Braddock Secondary resulting in additional relief to over-enrollment at Poe Middle School.

    This change could be implemented for the 2010-11 school year and would represent an interim step in addressing overcrowding at Annandale High School and Poe Middle School. Staff further believes that this initiative is based on the severity of current overcrowding at Annandale but would not preclude future review and planning for ultimate solutions to address capacity balance, feeder alignments and transportation efficiencies for elementary, middle and high schools in the region. A map and background information describing this proposal is contained in Appendix A.

    OPTIONS:

    Due to the severity of overcrowding at these schools, there are no options.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve the spring 2009 scope of boundary study for Annandale, Lake Braddock, and Falls Church High Schools and Poe, Glasgow and Lake Braddock Middle Schools, based on a critical need to alleviate existing and projected over-enrollment at Annandale High and Poe Middle Schools, pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments and School Closing

    Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

    Meeting Type: April 2, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 17

    SUBJECT: Proposed 2009 School Bond Referendum
    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:

    On January 22, 2009, the School Board adopted the FY 2010-2014 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the coming five-year period. Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period. Voter authorization to issue bonds is needed in November 2009 to provide construction funds for a new middle school, construction of several additions to existing schools, renovation of one high school, planning funds for the renovation of a middle school, and funding for relocation for modular building additions to meet critical capacity needs.

    KEY POINTS:

    In the past two years, school membership has grown by approximately 5,200 students and continued growth is projected. By 2013 approximately 50 schools will be over capacity by more than 10 percent and 31 schools will exceed capacity by more than 20 percent. The schools identified for capacity enhancements were selected based on the severity of the capacity deficit ratio which, for the schools selected, range from 1.24 to 1.45, and are limited to those schools where the capacity deficit can not be solved by other solutions such as program moves, boundary changes, planned renovations, or construction of new schools. For example, while Poe Middle School is severely overcrowded, we have proposed a boundary study that should provide relief to that school.
        Staff is confident that the schools selected will continue to need additional capacity based on the following factors: sustained high enrollment and/or growth experienced in the past several years; the continued need for temporary classrooms; the schools are located in high growth areas as defined by the County Comprehensive Plan; development in the pipeline (planned, approved and/or under construction); strong growth at the kindergarten and primary grades; and demographic characteristics. Based on an analysis of these factors, when reviewed together with the severity of the existing and projected capacity deficits, staff strongly believes that the high enrollments at these schools will continue and that permanent building capacity enhancements as identified in the attachment will be required for the long term needs of these schools.

        The proposed bond includes construction funding for capacity enhancements at eight elementary schools and one middle school to meet existing and growing enrollments. Funds are also included to support essential infrastructure, such as roof and HVAC replacements, athletic infrastructure, and upgrades to facilities to meet Americans with Disabilities (ADA) requirements.
    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 3, 2009, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $232,577,000.

    Category
    6. New Business - 10:15 p.m.
    Type
    Information



    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

    Meeting Type: April 2, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 17
    SUBJECT: Award of Contract
    TYPE: New Business

    BACKGROUND:

        On January 10, 2008, the School Board awarded the Master Agreement contract for synthetic turf, athletic surfaces, and related products and services to General Sports Venue/Astroturf. Pricing under this contract is favorable.

        No synthetic turf fields will be installed on FCPS properties under the auspices of this contract without first being brought to the School Board for specific project approval.
    KEY POINTS:

        Funding for the Bailey’s field will come from a Park Authority bond referendum. No FCPS funds will be needed.

        The Memorandum of Agreement between FCPS and the Park Authority has been negotiated to cover situations in which the Park Authority funds synthetic turf fields on FCPS sites.

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board award the contract for the installation of a synthetic turf field at Bailey’s Elementary School to General Sports Venue/Astroturf, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.
