Veterans Day - November 11, 2012

 

Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.

 

American Education Week - November 11-17, 2012

 

American Education Week was established in 1921 by the National Education Association (NEA) to raise public awareness of the importance of public education in America.  The organization’s annual tagline, Great Public Schools:  A Basic Right and Our Responsibility, focuses on the critical role parents play, the invaluable services provided by education support professionals, and the importance of substitute teachers.  As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week features a special observance on each day of the week, including veterans, parents, education support professionals, and substitute educators. 

 

Geography Awareness Week - November 11-17, 2012

 

Geography Awareness Week is an opportunity to focus on geography as a critical component of a 21st century education.  The 2012 theme, Declare Your Interdependence, focuses on how geography instills an awareness of the interdependence and interconnectedness in everyone’s lives. 

 

Geography Awareness Week was established by presidential proclamation in 1987 to underscore the importance of geographic understanding to ensure our nation’s economic competitiveness, national security, environmental sustainability, and livability of our communities in the 21st century.

School Psychology Awareness Week - November 12 –16, 2012

 

The theme of this year’s School Psychology Awareness Week, "Know Your Own Strengths” focuses on helping students find and recognize their own strengths and share them within the school community, their academics, and their lives.  The program involves resources and activities that school psychologists can use to help students realize how those strengths can help them develop relationships, deal with stress, and promote optimal development.

 

School psychologists work with students in early childhood settings and elementary and secondary schools.  They collaborate with teachers, parents, and school personnel to create safe, healthy, and supportive learning environments for all students.  School psychologists address students' learning and behavioral problems, suggest improvements to classroom management strategies or parenting techniques, and evaluate students with disabilities and advanced academic students to help determine the best way to educate them.

Key Points:

 

The Board reviewed the Strategic Governance Manual at their June 8-9 and July 20-21, 2012, retreats.  There were numerous Manual revisions proposed and discussed by Board members during the two retreats, and there was consensus to proceed with the revisions.  The order of some sections of the Manual was rearranged, and some information that had previously been part of a specific policy was incorporated into new appendices. 

 

The Governance Committee reviewed all the proposed revisions from the June and July retreats at the September 10, September 18, and October 1 committee meetings.  The Governance Committee did include some questions for further Board discussion related to three OEs (see details below).  The following highlights the major changes:

  • Section 2:  OEs—additions, edits, and rearrangement of order
    • Questions for further Board discussion are noted on OE 5 (Financial Planning), OE 7 (Asset Protection), and OE 9 (Instructional Program)
  • Section 3:  Board of Self-Governance Policies—additions, edits, and rearrangement of order
    • “Conflict of Interest” was added as a separate policy (had previously been part of “Code of Conduct”) after legal review
    • “Process for Addressing Violations”—revised after legal review
    • “Board Liaisons and Committees” were discussed at the September 10, 2012, work session; the discussion of whether or not any changes will be made will continue at a future meeting, so no changes are reflected in this version of the Manual except for the terms of members of the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee, which was changed from one to two years
  • Section 4:  Superintendent/Board Relationship—additions and edits
    • “Superintendent Delegated Authority” was new
  • Section 5:  Appendices—additions and edits
    • "School Board attorney" was changed to "outside counsel"
    • New appendix added—“History of Ad Hoc Committees”
    • Information that had previously been part of a specific policy (“Policy Development and Review” and “Board member options for issue education, advocacy, or policy”) moved to a separate appendix

For specific details of all the revisions, refer to the crosswalk document attached.

 

RECOMMENDATION: 

 

That the School Board approve the proposed revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual in Sections 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

Fairfax County School Board Resolution on

Federal Budget Sequestration

 

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County School Board believes that each student is entitled to an excellent education that meets his or her individual needs; that effective educators are essential to student success; and that a well-rounded education enables students to lead productive, fulfilling, creative and culturally rich lives; and

 

     WHEREAS, the federal Budget Control Act of 2011 includes a provision to impose $1.2 trillion in across-the-board budget cuts to almost all federal programs including education that would become effective January 2, 2013; and

 

     WHEREAS, these across-the-board budget cuts would become effective during the 2013-14 school year, with the exception that the potential reduction to the Impact Aid program (funding intended to assist school divisions that have high concentrations of federally connected students or tax-exempt Federal property) would occur in the current 2012-2013 school year; and

 

     WHEREAS, these across-the-board budget cuts, also known as sequestration, would impact education by a potential reduction in funds of 8.2 percent or more and could result in additional cuts to important educational programs already under pressure from recent state and local budget cuts; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Public Schools initial estimates are that such a flat rate cut could result in a loss of at least $6 million in federal funds in FY 2014 for programs such as Title I grants for disadvantaged students, the Individuals With Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), English Language Acquisition, Perkins Career and Technical Education funding, 21st Century Community Learning Centers, E-Rate and more; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Public Schools, as well as other public schools, would be impacted nationwide by an estimated $2.7 billion loss from just three programs alone – Title I grants, IDEA special education state grants and Head Start – that serve a combined 30.7 million of our most at-risk students; and

 

     WHEREAS, federal funding for K-12 programs was already reduced by more than $835 million in Fiscal Year 2011, and state and local funding for education continues to be impacted by state budget cuts; and

 

     WHEREAS, states and local governments have very limited capacity to absorb further education budget cuts without cuts to core programs, as the Fairfax County schools have already implemented multiple rounds of reductions based on difficult state and local budget conditions; and

 

     WHEREAS, sequestration would disproportionately impact the underlying economy and local tax base in Fairfax County, compounding the negative impacts of education program funding cuts and eroding our ability to carry out our core mission to inspire and empower students to meet high academic standards, lead ethical lives, and be responsible and innovative global citizens;

 

     NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board urges Congress and the Administration to amend the Budget Control Act to mitigate the drastic cuts to education that would affect our students and communities, and to protect education as an investment critical to economic stability and American competitiveness.

6.03 Fairfax County Public Schools, Strategic Facilities Plan - Recommendation to approve the Strategic Facilities Plan, as developed by the Facilities Planning Advisory Council, and as detailed in the agenda item (FTS; action scheduled 11/19/12)
Meeting
Nov 8, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 7
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Staff Contact:           Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

 

Meeting Category:   November 8, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 7

 

Subject:                    Fairfax County Public Schools, Strategic Facilities Plan (SFP)

 

School Board Action Required:   Information

 

Background:

 

The School Board has charged the Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) with developing and updating annually a long-term strategic plan for FCPS facility needs for presentation to and approval by the School Board.  The Strategic Facilities Plan (SFP) was initially presented in draft and discussed with the Board as part of FPAC’s June 2012 annual report.  At the School Board work session on September 10, 2012, FPAC presented their work entitled “Fairfax County Public Schools, Strategic Facilities Plan”, in compliance with their School Board charge.  Board discussion at the September 10 work session identified several issues with the SFP that required clarification and possible revision.

 

Key Points:

 

At its October 16, 2012, meeting, FPAC approved the attached revised SFP for submission to the School Board for approval.  The primary changes in the Plan are (1) changing the "goals" to "guidelines" and inserting language to emphasize that this is a set of guidelines to be used in the decision-making process, (2) removal of "strategies and objectives" that were to be created in the next SFP, and (3) a few minor editorial changes.  It should be noted that FPAC approval of the SFP as revised was not unanimous.  Some FPAC members are concerned that this revised document does not allow School Board endorsement of discreet criteria for making informed, prioritized resource allocation decisions.  However, FPAC recognizes that School Board approval of the revised SFP is an important step and is critical to the future work of FPAC in bringing recommendations to the Board.

 

The SFP is the culmination of an effort that began over two years ago which included community outreach that consisted of:  ‘Listening Tours’ undertaken for each school cluster, an FPAC day long retreat which included community members, ‘Listening Tours’ specific to the development of the SFP and a community blog.  The Plan provides a focus on the key areas of communication, capacity and space use, operations and maintenance, and capital improvement. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Strategic Facilities Plan, as developed by the Facilities Planning Advisory Council, and as detailed in the agenda item.
