Children's Book Week -

 

Children’s Book Week, a national celebration of reading, storytelling, and book-related events, observes its 97th anniversary in 2016 and is the longest-running national literacy initiative in the country.  Established to promote higher standards in children’s books, the week grew to involve librarians, publishers, and booksellers and is administered by Every Child a Reader, a 501(c)(3) literacy organization dedicated to instilling a lifelong love of reading in children. Nationwide, author and illustrator events take place each year and the Children’s Choice Book Awards, the only national child-chosen book awards program, are announced during the week.

 

Food Allergy Awareness Week - May 14 - 20

 

Created in 1998 by the Food Allergy Research & Education Inc., Food allergy awareness week shines a spotlight on the seriousness of food allergies and helps to improve public understanding of this potentially life threatening medical condition.  By increasing awareness, we can encourage respect, promote safety, and improve the quality of life of all those affected by food allergies and anaphylaxis.  Food allergies affect up to 15 million Americans, including nearly 6 million children, roughly two in every classroom.  The number of children with food allergies in the U.S. increased 50 percent between 1997 and 2011, but there is no clear answer as to why.  Every three minutes, a food allergy reaction sends someone to the emergency room in the U.S. You can learn more about food allergies and anaphylaxis by visiting the Food Allergy Research and education website at www.foodallergy.org.

Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Week - May 15-19, 2017

The Adult and Community Education (ACE) program of Fairfax County Public Schools began with an action by the School Board in 1955 and, in the over 60 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills and completed their high school educations.

Through the years, adults have learned new languages, acquired job skills, transitioned to new careers, and grown as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to ACE to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education has made major contributions to the school system as a laboratory of innovation for the piloting of new programs and services in support of PreK-12 education through family literacy, summer programs, extended day programs, enrichment courses, and driver education.

Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner, as well as providing enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day, ACE has helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

Madison High School 6A Girls Swim and Dive Team

The Fairfax County School Board is delighted to recognize the Madison Girls Swim and Dive team and their coaches for their dedicated team work during this impressive season and extends our hearty congratulations on their first state championship title.

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category: May 11, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject: Proposed 2017 School Bond Referendum

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Background:

 

On January 26, 2017, the School Board adopted the FY 2018-2022 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the referenced five-year period.  Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period.  Voter authorization in November 2017 will be necessary in order to issue school bonds to provide:

 

Construction funds for:

  • 1 new elementary school
  • 5 elementary school renovations
  • 2 middle school renovations
  • 1 high school renovation
  • 1 modular building relocation

Planning funds for:

  • 1 new elementary school
  • 5 elementary school renovations
  • 1 middle school renovation
  • 1 high school renovation
  • 3 high school additions

 

Key Points:

 

The adopted FY 2018-2022 CIP identified what are projected to be substantial enrollment increases over the 5-year planning period and beyond.  Specifically, these projected enrollment increases will require FCPS to accommodate more than 3,300 additional students by school year 2021 -2022, with additional growth expected to exceed 190,600 students. 

 

The 2017 school bonds are expected to provide funds for construction, and planning for new facilities to help address the expected capacity deficits; in addition, renovation projects continue to include additional capacity in order to address the student membership growth in many schools. 

 

Facilities staff members have been working with the County staff to reconcile unused authority from multiple bond referendums over an extended period of time – an exercise which is performed periodically for all local governmental entities which utilize bond funds.  This reconciliation does not alter in anyway the current annual cash flow contribution from the County of $155 million; nor will it impact the timing of any projects currently listed in the CIP.  It reflects cumulative project savings over time allowing FCPS to request less bonding authority for the projects currently identified for funding.  Out of the $353.3 million CIP listed in the bond referendum, the County currently already has bonding authority for $38.3 million.  As a result, FCPS’ remaining need is for new bond sales in the amount stated in the “Recommendation” section, below.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the attached resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and to take all necessary and appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 2017 general election, a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $315,000,000.

On February 9, 2017, the School Board adopted the Advertised Budget for the School Operating Fund totaling $2.8 billion. This is an increase of $135.1 million, or 5.1 percent, over the FY 2017 Approved Budget. The advertised budget was presented to and discussed by the School Board and County Board of Supervisors at a joint budget work session on February 21, 2017.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The Virginia General Assembly’s adopted 2016-2018 budget provides an increase of $4.1 million in revenue as compared to the FCPS’ FY 2018 Advertised Budget.

 

County Budget Impact

On May 2, 2017, the County Board of Supervisors adopted their FY 2018 budget that included an increase in the transfer to the School Operating Fund of 2.79 percent above the FY 2017 Approved Budget. The increase of $53.4 million is $59.1 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget.

 

May Budget Work Session

As presented at the May 1, 2017, work session, the projected shortfall in the FY 2018 Advertised Budget is $46.7 million, after accounting for the state and county budget adjustments and the impact from the FY 2017 Third Quarter Budget Review.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
