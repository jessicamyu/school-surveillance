RESOLUTION OF COMMENDATION
FOR
BRIAN HULL


WHEREAS, Brian Hull, principal of Colin L. Powell Elementary School, is an inspiring educational leader and an outstanding school administrator who has been an advocate for children in Fairfax County for over 25 years; and

WHEREAS, he successfully planned and led the opening of Powell Elementary School and has created both a dynamic learning community and an environment of cooperation and collaboration at the school; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Hull is a visible and involved yet calm and caring principal who leads with enthusiasm, skill, and compassion, who is dedicated to excellence, who values lifelong learning, and who works to meet the needs of others; and

WHEREAS, he takes an innovative approach to learning as demonstrated by his establishment of professional learning communities and the formation of the Positive Behavior Support program, for which Powell was named a Positive Behavior Support Exemplar School; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Hull is a respected instructor in the Fairfax County Public Schools Great Beginnings program and is an educator of and advocate for children returning to school after chronic illnesses;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the School Board of Fairfax County, Virginia, honors Brian Hull as the 2007-2008 FCPS Principal of the Year and the recipient of the Washington Post Distinguished Educational Leadership Award. The Board expresses its appreciation to Mr. Hull for his leadership and bestows on him its highest commendation for having achieved these distinctions.

RESOLUTION
HONORING
CATHERINE A. BELTER

WHEREAS, Catherine A. Belter was elected as the Springfield District representative to the Fairfax County School Board in November 1999 and has served with distinction since January 1, 2000, serving as vice chairman of the Board in 2003 and as chairman of several of the Board’s standing committees, including Human Resources, Audit, Legislation, Instruction, and Special Services; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Belter brought to the Board an extensive record of community involvement in citizen organizations, which included service as a member of the Virginia Advisory Committee for the Education of the Gifted, as a PTA board member, as President of the Virginia PTA for two years, and as a member of the National PTA Board of Directors for ten years; and

WHEREAS, as a former Fairfax County librarian, she has championed the interests and needs of children and schools in the Springfield District and recognized the importance of early reading programs for student achievement and success; and

WHEREAS, during Mrs. Belter’s service to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools, she has demonstrated selfless commitment through gentle persuasion and steady perseverance, encouraging others to share her vision of excellence for children everywhere;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Catherine A. Belter for her outstanding contributions and dedicated service to this Board, to Fairfax County Public Schools, and to the children and citizens of Fairfax County.

      Staff Contact: Peter Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instruction Services

      Meeting Type: December 20, 2007, Regular Meeting No. 10

      SUBJECT: Early Head Start (EHS)/Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP)/Head Start (HS) Policy Committee

      TYPE: New Business

      RELATED TO: Student Achievement Goal 1

      BACKGROUND:
          According to the Head Start Program Performance Standard 1304.50, a formal structure of program governance provides parents and community representatives with the authority and opportunity to participate in shared decision-making concerning program design and implementation. Parent Committees at each school provide every parent of an enrolled child the opportunity to develop leadership skills, share information, assist in the development of activities that address their interests and needs, and support the education and healthy development of their children. Representatives and alternates from each of the Parent Committees represent their respective schools on the FCPS Early Head Start (EHS) / Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP) / Head Start (HS) Policy Committee, the system-wide program governing body. Representatives and alternates from the FCPS Policy Committee also serve on the county-wide governing body, the Fairfax County EHS/HS Policy Council.

      KEY POINTS:
          • In accordance with Head Start Program Performance Standards, all FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS parents have the opportunity to participate in shared decision-making concerning the program’s design and implementation through several levels of governing bodies.
          • At the school level, parents develop leadership/educational support skills and share information or concerns during their respective parent committee meetings and activities, with the support of FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS program personnel.
          • Parent Committee representatives and alternates participate in information sharing and decision-making activities at the school-system level through the FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS Policy Committee, also with support of FCPS EHS/FECEP/HS program personnel.
          • Policy Committee representatives and alternates perform similar sharing and decision-making activities on the county-wide EHS/HS Policy Council, with support of the Grantee, Fairfax County Office for Children personnel.


      RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board confirms the elected members of the Fairfax County Public Schools Early Head Start (EHS)/Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP)/Head Start (HS) Policy Committee, as listed in the agenda item.
