Agenda Item: FCPS Curriculum and Assessment Resources

Staff Contact: Maribeth Luftglass and Ann Monday

Background
FCPS currently uses multiple commercial assessment tools. None of these meet the division’s functional, content, or intellectual property requirements. The opportunity exists to leverage the capabilities of FCPS 24-7 Learning and EDSL to provide teachers a single point of access to up-to-date assessments, assessment results, and associated curriculum resources.

Key Issues
· Continuing the use of current assessment tools, expanded to provide testing to all ES and MS for language arts and mathematics, requires an additional $581,200 from EOY 2005 funding.

· Beginning enhancements to provide a comprehensive curriculum and assessment solution for FCPS requires $2,740,450 from EOY 2005 funding.

School Board Discussion/Decision

· Agreement to expand availability of current assessment tools for FY 2006 – 2008
· Authorization to fund the expansion of the current assessment tools (BART I, BART II)
· Authorization for the first increment of funding needed to develop a comprehensive FCPS curriculum, assessment, and results reporting capability

Staff Recommendation

That FCPS extend the current assessment program and begin enhancements to FCPS 24-7 Learning to provide a comprehensive online curriculum and assessment capability for FCPS.

Options

No viable options exist to meet FCPS needs.

Agenda Item: HRAC 2004-2005 Annual Report

Staff Contact: Kevin North

Background

Attached is the annual report responding to the Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC) charge to examine the role of the school-based human relations committees (HRC). The committee conducted a survey of principals and collected information to respond to five core questions:

· How are local school human relations committees faring?
· How useful are human relations committees to principals?
· What kinds of issues or concerns come to the attention of these committees?
· Are there other school systems that have similar committees and, if so, are there “best practices” that FCPS can learn from?
· What can the School Board do to ensure that human relations committees remain viable and valuable?

Key Issues

The HRAC makes five specific recommendations as follows:
· Modify Regulation 4910, Duties, Responsibilities, and Rights of Employees, by writing up the requirement to have a school based HRC in a separate regulation or policy statement.
· Formulate a clear statement of the goals, participants, and expectations of a HRC within the school so that the principal has the necessary guidance as to why an actively functioning HRC benefits the school community.
· Incorporate an enforcement mechanism, for example, by including this in the performance evaluation or asking principals to report on HRC activities at cluster meetings now and then.
· Address staff training issues as it relates to human relations and the school climate.
· Improve communication between the school and the cluster and/or area office on human relations issues in the schools.


School Board Discussion/Decision

· Information only

Staff Recommendation

Staff response and final recommendations to report will be provided following a detailed analysis of the recommendations by Leadership Team and appropriate stakeholders.

Agenda Item: Focus School Policy

Staff Contact: Ann Monday

Background:
Focus Schools offer unique schoolwide instructional opportunities to help individual schools explore instructional initiatives that would benefit their students and community. The Focus initiative could be a concentration of a particular discipline, additional instructional strategies, resources, and staffing. Focus Schools provide a special instructional initiative for their students living within the school’s specified boundaries.

Focus Schools are unique in that they build on existing strengths in the school community and cultivate them in innovative and engaging ways resulting in strong community and parental support, involvement and satisfaction.

Currently schools seeking a Focus initiative contact the Instructional Services Department (ISD) to discuss the proposal and are provided an application. Schools complete the application and submit the application to ISD. Staff presents to the Board, on a yearly basis, a Focus School report that outlines the current funding status of the Focus schools and presents proposals for consideration.

In addition to the Focus School concept, Fairfax County Public Schools established two other types of programs: magnet schools and county-initiated instructional initiatives. Magnet and foreign language immersion schools are schools that receive additional resources to enhance and enrich the Program of Studies for students, usually chosen through a lottery system, from both in and outside of their immediate attendance areas. Instructional initiatives, such as Project Excel, Success by Eight, and Focus 2007 schools, are designed by the county and placed in select schools to improve student achievement through alternative learning models, increased time for learning and targeted staff development.

On July 12, 2004, staff presented a Focus School report that included proposals for Terraset to become a Focus School and Kilmer to receive funding to support its Focus program. However, the School Board took no action on the proposals.

At the March 10, 2005 School Board meeting, board members requested ISD to establish guidelines for approval of Focus Schools.



Key Issues
· The average cost of a Focus School initiative is approximately $170,000.
· Does FCPS support the Focus School concept?
· If Focus Policy is adopted, the School Board may consider identifying a funding source to support implementation of Focus initiatives.

School Board Discussion/Policy Decision
At the March 10, 2005 School Board meeting, board members requested staff to establish guidelines for adoption of Focus Schools based on the following criteria:
· Consensus that parent involvement and buy-in of the Focus Schools concept was necessary.
· Equity and balance throughout the County should be a factor; strategic approach would be needed to provide that balance.
· Modified calendar school process should remain separate.
· In communities where parents might not be as involved, staff might bring program recommendation forward.
· Policy must include an evaluation process of Focus Schools.
· Staff is to develop draft policy for Board review.

Staff Recommendation
· Adopt a Focus School Policy and identify funding source for Focus initiatives.

Options
· Adopt staff recommendation.
· Continue with the current Focus School process.
