      Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

      Meeting Type: March 9, 2009 - Work Session No. 85

      SUBJECT: Scope of Spring 2009 Boundary Studies

      TYPE: New Business

      BACKGROUND:

      Pursuant to previous discussions with the School Board, staff is seeking authorization to undertake two boundary studies beginning in spring 2009. This initiative is based on a critical need to provide enrollment relief to Ft. Belvoir Elementary School and Annandale High School and reflects a change to the traditional fall time frame for boundary studies. Both studies would be undertaken in a slightly modified calendar and process which would include town meetings, community outreach and notices, but would not include small focus group meetings, multiple scenarios proposed by staff or a long period for public comment. The proposed studies would be limited in scope and reflect a single option for each study in order to provide the immediate relief needed for both schools.

      KEY POINTS:

      Ft. Belvoir Elementary School: This boundary study is proposed to be limited to Ft. Belvoir Elementary and Woodlawn Elementary as the receiving school. A single Ft. Belvoir housing complex known as Woodlawn Village located along Pole Road in the northeastern section of Ft. Belvoir is proposed to be re-assigned to Woodlawn Elementary School. There are approximately 200 students residing in this housing area and Woodlawn Elementary is projected to have a surplus balance of approximately 230 student spaces. The housing area proposed to be re-assigned is adjacent to the school and at least a portion of the students to be re-assigned could potentially walk to Woodlawn Elementary. This change could be implemented for the 2009-10 school year in order to provide needed interim enrollment relief at Ft. Belvoir Elementary until and if such time as a second school site could be planned and constructed on the base by the Department of Defense or Army. This proposal represents a single option. No other adjacent school has capacity to receive additional students from Ft. Belvoir Elementary. A map and background information describing this proposal is contained in Appendix A.

      Annandale High School: This study is proposed to be limited to Annandale and Falls Church High Schools, and Lake Braddock Secondary School and Poe and Glasgow Middle Schools. Although somewhat more complex, this study also represents a single option with several changes to be considered in order to relieve over-enrollment at Annandale, align middle and high school feeders, and eliminate an attendance island. The proposal is summarized below:

      From Annandale High School to Falls Church High School: That portion of the Annandale High School north of Rt. 236 and west of Columbia Pike that wraps around the Falls Church attendance island is proposed to be re-assigned to Falls Church High School. This has the effect of eliminating an attendance island and more importantly, potentially shifts approximately 175 students from Annandale to Falls Church.

      From Poe Middle to Glasgow Middle: That portion of Poe which extends north and south of Rt. 50 to Glasgow. This area of Poe is currently assigned to Stuart High School. This proposal has the effect of eliminating a split feeder for this portion of Poe Middle since Glasgow Middle feeds to Stuart High School and further reduces high enrollment at Poe by approximately 130 students.

      From Annandale High School and Poe to Lake Braddock Secondary: That portion of Annandale High School and the underlying attendance area of Poe Middle School south of Braddock Road and west of I-495 to Lake Braddock Secondary. This change potentially shifts approximately 100 students from Annandale to Lake Braddock and would also reassign approximately 50 students from Poe Middle to Lake Braddock Secondary resulting in additional relief to over-enrollment at Poe Middle School.

      This change could be implemented for the 2010-11 school year and would represent an interim step in addressing overcrowding at Annandale High School and Poe Middle School. Staff further believes that this initiative is based on the severity of current overcrowding at Annandale but would not preclude future review and planning for ultimate solutions to address capacity balance, feeder alignments, and transportation efficiencies for elementary, middle, and high schools in the region. A map and background information describing this proposal is contained in Appendix B.

      OPTIONS: Due to the severity of overcrowding at these schools, there are no options.


      RECOMMENDATION:

      Approve the spring 2009 scope of boundary studies for Annandale High School and Ft. Belvoir Elementary School as proposed in the March 9 Agenda Item and Appendix A and B attached.

SUBJECT: Results of Online Survey on Proposed Bell Schedule

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:

Please find attached the results of the online survey on the proposed changes to the current bell schedule. These will be discussed at the School Board Work Session on Monday, March 9.

KEY POINTS:

Highlights of the survey include:
    • A total of 94,129 respondents completed the survey during the approximate three-week time period of February 9 – 28, 2009.
    • All four groups of survey respondents—students in grades 6-12, parents, staff, community/business members—oppose the proposed bell schedule change.
    • Specifically, approximately 71 percent of students oppose, 63 percent of parents oppose, 72 percent of staff oppose, and 78 percent of community/business parents oppose the proposed bell schedule change.
    • Concerns indicated by parents who oppose the change include finding child care (38 percent); not enough time for homework after sports and activities (58 percent); not able to participate in after-school sports and activities (46 percent); and parent work schedule adjustments (46 percent); and less family time (76 percent).
    • IP addresses were monitored for multiple survey responses. The monitoring indicated a small percentage of multiple responses could be traced to the same IP address, but this percentage would not have had a significant impact on the survey results.

SUBJECT: Results from the Community Dialogues on Proposed Changes to the FCPS Bell Schedule

TYPE: Discussion

ACKGROUND:

Community dialogues about the proposed bell schedule were held at seven sites across Fairfax County on February 24, 25, and 26, 2009. These sites were Annandale High School, Herndon High School, Longfellow Middle School, Oak View Elementary School, Providence Elementary School, Rocky Run Middle School, and Whitman Middle School. The purpose for the community dialogues was two-fold: (1) To present the background on the TTF recommendations, including ensuring participants receive sufficient information on teen sleep needs identified in the report and (2) To present a blueprint of the proposed bell schedule change to families to gauge their acceptability of such a change.

KEY POINTS:

Highlights of the community dialogue results include:

路 Approximately 1,600 parents, staff, students, and community members participated in the dialogues.

路 A total of six questions were discussed in small groups—three were qualitative, three were quantitative.

路 Clear themes emerged out of the qualitative questions that asked the group to explore the pros and cons of the current and proposed bell schedules, and the adjustments that their families would have to make with the proposed bell schedule.

路 Likewise, a clear majority of dialogue participants indicated their preferences on each of the quantitative questions.
