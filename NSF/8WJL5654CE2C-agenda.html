RESOLUTION

NAMING THE PLAYGROUND AT

ORANGE HUNT ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

“THE DR. JUDY RYAN PLAYGROUND”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Judy Ryan served as the principal of Orange Hunt Elementary School for seven years until her retirement in 2011 after more than 26 years of dedicated service to Fairfax County Public Schools as a teacher, assistant principal, elementary school principal, and cluster director; and

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Ryan was a model principal, mentor, and inspirational leader to her staff and students; she pursued her passion by dedicating herself to the school communities she led; she became involved in parents’ concerns and sought equitable solutions that better served the school community; her understanding of learning theories, familiarity with policies, past experiences as a teacher, and extraordinary interpersonal skills contributed to the successes at Orange Hunt Elementary School; and  

 

     WHEREAS, she reached out to all students in the school and made them feel welcomed; she was a good listener and helped students with the obstacles they encountered; under her leadership, she inspired many students to achieve their academic and personal best; and

 

     WHEREAS, during her tenure at Orange Hunt Elementary School, she strengthened the relationship between the school and community; she gave her time freely and unselfishly to many activities, often supporting the PTA’s efforts in fundraising and participating in countless of activities with students, from playing games at the annual fall festival to cleaning the school on clean-up day; and

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Ryan implemented traditions at Orange Hunt Elementary School that continue today, such as the annual Veterans’ Day celebration, where students in the school band and chorus perform patriotic music to honor the community members who serve in the Armed Forces;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the playground at Orange Hunt Elementary School “The Dr. Judy Ryan Playground” in recognition of Dr. Ryan’s years of dedication and service to the students, faculty, and families of Orange Hunt Elementary School.

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2013 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2013 Third Quarter Estimate. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the FY 2014 budgeted beginning balance as included in the FY 2014 Approved Budget, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the funds available are $55.7 million.

 

FY 2013 actual revenue is $7.8 million lower than the estimate and totaled 99.7 percent of the FY 2013 Third Quarter Estimate.  State aid for FY 2013 totaled $383.1 million which is $0.9 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts are projected to total $164.4 million which is $1.9 million more than budgeted.  A decrease of $11.6 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $12.3 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2014, offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of a one-time final payment for the Education Jobs Bill of $0.4 million and a one-time final payment of $0.5 million from FEMA for February 2010 storms.  Based on estimated final year-end activity, tuition from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $1.1 million, while revenue from tuition, fees, and other was $3.8 million more than budgeted.

 

Expenditures were $51.1 million less than projected after funding the School Board’s Flexibility Reserve and accounting for the textbook replacement fund and other commitments and carryover, excluding grants. A majority of this variance results from compensation accounts being $34.6 million less than budgeted due primarily to higher compensation lapse than projected; utilities expenditures being $5.2 million less than projected due to a temperate winter, spring, and early summer; county charges for vehicle services, including fuel, being $1.9 million less than projected; and a reduction in school accounts due to limiting carryover to 25 percent of the current budget.  In addition, $12.3 million in multiyear federal grant funds were unexpended.  This funding will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2014.

Authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Chief Financial Officer, to submit a grant application to the Department of Defense to include a commitment from Fairfax County Public Schools to provide 20 percent match associated with the costs of renovation and construction of elementary schools located on the Fort Belvoir military installation and pursuant to a ground lease to the School Board. (FTS)

Staff Contact:         Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  July 25, 2013 – Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                  Grant for School Renovation and Construction on Ft. Belvoir

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

As discussed at the July 15, 2013, School Board Work Session, FCPS has been invited by the U.S. Department of Defense (DOD) to submit a preliminary grant application for funding under DOD’s Program for Construction, Renovation, Repair or Expansion of Public Schools on Military Bases.  Under this program, successful Local Education Agencies (LEA’s) receive 80 percent of their project costs via this federal grant, with the remaining 20 percent provided by the LEA.  Applications are given a 90-day turn-around for the initial invitation; FCPS’ application is due on September 3, 2013.  

 

While the DOD report that placed Ft. Belvoir Elementary School (FBES) on DOD’s priority list found FBES to be in excellent overall condition, the report noted that the facilities did not comply with Anti-Terrorism Force Protection standards; that in 2011, a minimum of 2 additional classrooms were necessary; general facility maintenance issues would be required for expired systems; and minor flood remediation repairs were necessary near the main entry of the school.  The largest observation, however, was the student enrollment at FBES and the projected increase in membership in the coming years.  In addition to the number of students attending FBES, the school has a significant population of students with additional needs, and growth within the Ft. Belvoir Military Installation will result in even more growth in the coming years. 

 

Presently, there are 1,515 elementary aged students who are housed on Fort Belvoir. Approximately 1,133 of these students attend school at the base elementary school (program capacity of 1,140). The remaining 385 students who live on Fort Belvoir attend five other FCPS schools off base.

 

The five year projections indicate that an additional 230 students from within Fort Belvoir can be expected; exacerbating the overcrowding within the base school and the other surrounding FCPS facilities. These additional students will increase the overall elementary student yield from the base to approximately 1,750 students. In addition, the base has the ability to expand their housing by another 170 units.

 

The base has expressed their desire to keep all of their elementary-aged children on the base. The Department of Defense has begun issuing grants to bases and localities based upon need in an effort to meet the goal of providing educational facilities on their bases. (Fact Sheet attached).

 

The Department of Financial Services and Facilities and Transportation Services have been working with staff from both Fort Belvoir and DOD in order to submit a grant that addresses issues raised by DOD in regards to the existing Ft. Belvoir Elementary School and construct a new elementary school adjacent to the existing facility; with an opening in the fall of 2015. The deadline for the grant submission is September 3, 2013.

 

Recommendation:

 

I move that the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Chief Financial Officer, to submit a grant application to the Department of Defense to include a commitment from Fairfax County Public Schools to provide 20 percent match associated with the costs of renovation and construction of elementary schools located on the Fort Belvoir military installation and pursuant to a ground lease to the School Board.

Recommended Action
Accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor's Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2013-2014 (Cluster 3; presented as new business 7/11/13)

Staff Contact:         Dan Parris, Cluster 3 Assistant Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  July 25, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                  Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school. The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

  • Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2014 is $2,552,640. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $3,481,167.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

Options:

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

 

Recommendation:

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
