125.07 Discussion - Adult and Community Education Committee Annual Report
Meeting
May 27, 2010 - Work Session No. 125
Category
125. Instruction - 7 p.m.
Type
Discussion

Staff Contact: Peter Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

Other Staff Present: Herbert Persil, chairman; Lois Passman, vice chairman; Teddi Predaris, director, Language Acquisition and Title I; Diane Pruner, director, Professional and Life Skills; Karin Williams, director, Operations and Strategic Planning; Teresa Zutter, director, Intervention and Prevention Services; James Ginther, coordinator, Adult and Community Education

Meeting Category: May 27, 2010 - Work Session No. 125

Subject: Adult and Community Education Advisory Committee Annual Report

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Related To: School Board Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Commiittees to the School Board

Key Points:
    School Board Charge: Define the effect of the Adult and Community Education (ACE) reorganization on student achievement, family literacy, and course offerings that are responsive to consumer demand and workforce needs.

    Response: Changes to the organization for Adult and Community Education Programs, at the direction of the Superintendent of Fairfax County Public Schools, were made effective at the beginning of school year/fiscal year 2009-2010. Since the changes have been settling into place through much of the current school year and fiscal year, a fair and realistic assessment of the reorganization should be made after a year or more of operation.

    Committee Observations: After extensive discussions with key staff, the advisory committee found no diminution in the energy and enthusiasm of the staff and in their efforts to provide high quality instruction. The discussions did not reveal any significant problems directly attributable to the new organization.

    The committee noted that ACE Community Education Programs and Adult ESOL Program enrollments have been in a steady decline since FY 2005. While all the reasons are not known at this time, the local economy may have been a significant cause in the most recent years. A continuing decline in enrollments could result in reduced revenue and funding, and thus an inability to sustain current course offerings and support staff including teachers.
Recommendation:

That the School Board accept the Adult and Community Education Annual Report.
