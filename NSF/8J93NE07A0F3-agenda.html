5.09 School Board Policies

 


Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. three (3) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. A maximum of ten (10) speaker slots are available, first come, first served. The speakers list closes at 4:30 p.m. on the last business day prior to the meeting date.  Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

Meeting Category:     July 26, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 3

 

Subject:                     FY 2012 Final Budget Review           

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                                                   

 

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2012 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2012 Third Quarter Estimate. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the FY 2013 budgeted beginning balances as included in the FY 2013 Approved Budget, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the funds available are $52.8 million.

 

FY 2012 actual revenue was $3.6 million lower than the estimate or 99.9 percent of the FY 2012 Third Quarter Estimate.  State aid is $1.1 million and sales tax projections are $3.4 more than projected.  A decrease of $11.5 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $12.0 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2013, offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of an increase of $0.4 million in Impact Aid.  The net decrease in federal revenue is offset by increases of $3.4 million in tuition, fees, and other revenue.

 

After accounting for the flexibility reserve and other commitments, FY 2012 actuaI expenditures (excluding federal grants) were $44.4 million, or 1.9 percent, lower than the FY 2012 Third Quarter Estimate.  A majority of this variance results from compensation accounts being $34.3 million less than budgeted due primarily to savings resulting from higher turnover than projected; utilities expenditures being $7.1 million less than projected due to a temperate winter, spring, and early summer; and county charges for vehicle services, including fuel, being $1.7 million less than projected. In addition, $12.0 million in multiyear federal grant funds were unexpended.  This funding will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2013. 

 

This agenda item also includes recommendations for $11.2 million in FY 2013 expenditure adjustments leaving $41.6 million for the FY 2014 budgeted beginning balance.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

Award the site work for synthetic turf field installation at Centreville High School to the Strittmatter Metro, Inc., in the amount of $960,000 and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Chief Operating Officer, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board (FTS)

[Posted 7/25/12]

Staff Contact:          Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  July 26, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                    Award of Contract – Site Work for Synthetic Turf Installation Centreville High School  

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:              School Board Policy 5011, Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

On May 24, 2012, the School Board awarded a contract to Shaw Contracting Flooring Services, Inc. dba Shaw Sportexe (Shaw) in the amount of $3,866,325 for the synthetic turf field installation projects for summer completion.  The projects consist of six fields at four schools- McLean High School, Langley High School, Centreville High School and Robinson Secondary School.  All projects are to be substantially completed by August 20, 2012.

 

Shaw did not properly account for certain site work, including asbestos abatement and removal for the Centreville High School project and requested a change order for that item of work on this project.  The FCPS office of design and construction services (D&C) rejected Shaw’s change order request and met with Shaw’s representatives to discuss the resolution of this issue on July 11, 2012.  In order to meet the established substantial completion date, it was agreed by the senior management of Shaw and D&C that FCPS would engage a third-party site work contractor to provide supplemental work in accordance with the FCPS/Shaw contract documents (section 4.05(b) of the general conditions). 

 

The cost of this supplemental work will be deducted from Shaw’s contract value for the Centreville High School project.  Thus, there will be no fiscal impact to FCPS or to the contributing parties that funded the Centreville project.

 

D&C requested pricing on an emergency basis from Strittmatter Metro, Inc. (Strittmatter) for the site work at Centreville High School.  Strittmatter was selected based on its expertise and its available manpower.  Strittmatter is considered a leader in the Washington Metropolitan area and has the resources to meet the very aggressive site work completion timeframe.  FCPS’ normal synthetic field installation construction periods are approximately 90 to 120 days.  The construction period for the Centreville High School project is now less than 30 days.  It is the expert opinion of D&C professionals that Strittmatter Metro Inc. is the only practical source available to provide the critical services within the required timeframe to avoid negatively impacting the FCPS instructional and community programs that are scheduled on these fields that are now under construction.     

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the site work for synthetic turf field installation at Centreville High School to the Strittmatter Metro, Inc., in the amount of $960,000  and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Chief Operating Officer, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Staff Contact:         Dan Parris, Assistant Superintendent, Cluster III

 

Meeting Category:  July 26, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                 Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:                             

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.  The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

  • Additional state funding for the regional school.  The estimate for FY 2013 is $2,423,168.  The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $3,281,167.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor's School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Great visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

Options:

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions. 

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor's Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2012-2013.

5.04 School Board Citizen Advisory Committee Charges
Meeting
Jul 26, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 2

Type

Recommended Action
Approve the charges for 2012-2013 for the following School Board Citizen Advisory Committees: Adult and Community Education, Students with Disabilities, Advanced Academic Programs, Career and Technical Education, District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program, Facilities Planning Advisory Council, Family and Early Childhood Education Program/Head Start, Human Relations, Minority Student Achievement Oversight, and School Health, as detailed in the agenda item (SB; presented as new business 7/12/12)

[Updated 7/26/12]

Contact:                  llryong Moon, chairman

 

Meeting Category:  July 26, 2012 – Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                  2012-2013 Charges to School Board Advisory Committees

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To:            School Board Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

 

Adult and Community Education Advisory Committee (ACE)

 

Promote an adult and community education program that is fiscally responsible, offers programs that meet community needs and fosters partnerships with businesses and community groups.  Seek to identify opportunities for continuous improvement, innovation and enrollment growth.

 

Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee (AAPAC)

 

Assess the range of program opportunities available in FCPS for AAP in the secondary schools; include assessment of programs and best practices used, including accelerated content, differentiated instruction beyond the classroom (internships, work study, student exchange programs) and special schools such as the academies.

 

Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD)

 

Research and develop recommendations for the Local Education Agency (FCPS) and VDOE regarding diploma options for students with disabilities.

Explanation:  The Virginia Department of Education has recently released guidance that for students with disabilities entering ninth grade in fall of 2013 there will be no option of the modified diploma as in past years. In 2011 there were approximately 569 FCPS students with disabilities who graduated with a modified diploma. While VDOE has indicated that there will be other options for students with disabilities these options have not yet been developed.

 

Career and Technical Education Advisory Committee (CTEAC)

 

Explore, investigate and recommend communication methods, marketing, delivery modalities and partnerships that enhance and expand Career and Technical Education programs for the mutual benefit of students and the greater business community.

 

District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program

 

Discover and develop ways in which schools can partner with families to support 21st century learning for all students.

 

Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP)/Head Start

 

Identify additional means to address the needs of the children in Fairfax County who are on the FECEP/Head Start waitlist (e.g., funding opportunities to expand FECEP/HS program, other school opportunities).

 

Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC)

 

Develop and update annually a long-term strategic plan for FCPS facility needs for presentation to and approval by the School Board.  The strategic plan should reflect input from all interested stakeholders and constituencies.

         

Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC)

 

Examine the role of the parent liaison in impacting parental involvement as it relates to closing the student achievement gap.

 

Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee (MSAOC)

 

Ensure that school system efforts to close all aspects of the achievement gap for underrepresented minority students remain on course and that the new Priority Schools Initiative is making a meaningful contribution to these efforts; also monitor the projected impact of the demographic influx/changes with language minority students in FCPS.

 

School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC)

 

Research the work of Partnership for a Healthier Fairfax (PFHF) and the Community Transformation Grant that was awarded to Fairfax County by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. Explore linkages and opportunities to coordinate efforts between FCPS, Fairfax County government, and the Fairfax community to increase local participation in the national effort to improve the health of our students and increase the capacity of our community to develop a health workforce for the future.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the charges for 2012-2013 for the following School Board Citizen Advisory Committees: Adult and Community Education, Students with Disabilities, Advanced Academic Programs, Career and Technical Education, District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program, Facilities Planning Advisory Council, Family and Early Childhood Education Program/Head Start, Human Relations, Minority Student Achievement Oversight, and School Health, as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:  July 26, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 2

 

Subject:                  School Board Audit Committee Membership

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points: 

 

At the March 29, 2012, forum work session, there was a recommendation that the Board consider adding another School Board member as a member of the Audit Committee, thereby increasing the current membership from two Board members to three Board members.  (At that time, Janie Strauss served as chairman of the committee, and Pat Hynes served as the second Board member.)   There was consensus at the forum to refer the recommendation to the Governance Committee.

 

At the April 16 Governance Committee work session, the recommendation was discussed.  The members of the Governance Committee agreed to support the recommendation that the Strategic Governance Manual be revised to reflect an increase in the number of Board members on the Audit Committee from two members to three members.  During the all-day work sessions on April 16, the Governance Committee chairman presented the recommendation to the full Board to increase the Audit Committee membership from two to three members, and there was consensus to schedule the issue for new business on April 26 and action on May 10.

 

At the May 10 Board meeting, after discussion of the main motion (and a proposed amendment that was not successful), the Board approved postponing the main motion until the first School Board meeting in September 2012 (which is scheduled for September 6, 2012) to allow for further discussion.  The Board discussed the topic of the committee membership at the July 16 Audit Committee work session, and there was consensus that a third Board member be added to the committee.  There was also consensus that the Superintendent and the Deputy Superintendent would be non-voting members (see proposed amendment below).  (The Chief Financial Officer, who is a member of the committee, and the School Board Auditor, who is the staff liaison to the committee, are non-voting members.)

 

In accordance with the motion to postpone that was approved by the Board on May 10, the recommendation to change the Audit Committee membership from two Board members to three will be scheduled for action on September 6, 2012.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve adding another School Board member as a member of the Board’s Audit Committee, resulting in three Board members serving on the Audit Committee, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

 

Proposed amendment:

 

I move to amend the main motion by designating the Superintendent and Deputy Superintendent as non-voting members of the Audit Committee. (Jane Strauss)
