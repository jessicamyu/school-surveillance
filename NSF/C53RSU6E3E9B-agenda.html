U. S. Constitution Day and Citizenship Day - September 17, 2022

 

Constitution Day and Citizenship Day are observed each year on September 17 to commemorate the signing of the Constitution on September 17, 1787, and “recognize all who, by coming of age or by naturalization, have become citizens.”  Recent legislation has renamed the day as Constitution Day and Citizenship Day and urges proper observance of the day. This year, the official observance day is Saturday, September 17, and is the 235th anniversary of the nation’s founding document.

 

All schools in FCPS will observe this day on Friday, September 16th or Monday, September 19th with activities that recognize the adoption of the United States Constitution and those who have become citizens under the Constitution.  Schools will use a variety of curriculum resources to teach students and build awareness about the importance of the Constitution and responsible citizenship.

National Hispanic Heritage Month Recognition

 

Each year, Americans observe National Hispanic Heritage Month by celebrating the histories, cultures, and contributions of Americans whose ancestors came from Spain, Mexico, the Caribbean and Central and South America. This observation started in 1968 as Hispanic Heritage Week under President Lyndon Johnson. In 1988, President Ronald Reagan expanded the observation period to 30-days starting on September 15 and ending on October 15. It was enacted into law August 17, 1988.

 

National Hispanic Heritage Month is a wonderful opportunity for our schools and community to celebrate various cultures and traditions, and a tangible reinforcement of the School Board’s belief that “Our diversity is a strength that creates resilient, open, and innovative global citizens.” In FCPS, one in four of our students is of Hispanic Heritage and boast ancestry from a variety of countries and cultures. These cultures in turn enrich our own school community. I encourage you to participate in the programs and events taking place during this time in your schools and community.

 

FCPS values its diversity and acknowledges that all people contribute to the well-being of the community. FCPS is fortunate to have many groups whose members work with our Latino students empowering them to be the next generation of leaders and professionals.

FCPS High School Voter Registration and Education Recognition

 

The FCPS High School Registration Challenge was created to encourage students to register to vote. When young people exercise their voter rights, they are likely to become lifelong voters, engaged in their duties and responsibilities as citizens. A Virginia law requires every public school to provide opportunities for eligible students to register to vote during the school day. The Ethical and Global Citizen attribute of FCPS’s Portrait of a Graduate affords all students an understanding of the foundations of our country, while also valuing the rights, privileges, and responsibilities of being an engaged community member. FCPS is committed to providing information to students about voter registration, voting procedures, and how to become informed about election issues thereby preparing students to exercise their voter rights and increasing the likelihood of future voting. In turn, a social studies teacher in every FCPS high school acts as a voter registration liaison who facilitates voter registration events at least twice during the school year.  Students who are registered voters will be able to participate in decision-making at the local, state, and federal levels, and it is important to understand that all elections matter.


Tonight, the Fairfax County School Board recognizes and supports the collaboration of the League of Women Voters of the Fairfax Area, the Fairfax County Office of Elections, Fairfax County Council of PTAs, and the FCPS Instructional Services Department social studies team and teachers to provide voter registration opportunities and nonpartisan information about voting to eligible students.  Thank you for your service to help FCPS equip our students to participate as prepared citizens in the United States.

Ignite Link:          Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy actions.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state, and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, parents, and the broader Fairfax community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of both current and ongoing significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board’s 2023 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) from the Board’s 2022 legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board will discuss proposed staff recommended changes as well as Board-directed changes to the 2023 FSCB State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for October 4, 2022.  In addition, the Board will consider both state and federal legislative priorities.

 

Full draft text as well as a complete table of proposed changes to the draft 2023 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program will be included as part of the Board’s October Work Session agenda item.  Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 3, 2022.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2023 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

Meeting Category: September 15, 2022 - Regular Meeting

Summary/Background  

Several amendments to the ESSER III Grant are necessary for FY23. First, FCPS is implementing similar Layered Prevention Strategies during the 2022-2023 school year and it is necessary to provide schools with sufficient funding for COVID Case Managers/Supervision Monitors. In order to provide schools with the same level of funding expended in FY22, $4.1 million in carryover from Project 27 and an additional $1.9 million will be redirected from Project 1 Special Education Teacher Contracts (this funding is available due to the salary lapse of unfilled positions in FY22). 

 

Additionally, schools received estimated academics (Project 9) and wellness (Project 2) allocation ranges in May 2022 to support planning for FY23. In order to provide schools with sufficient funding levels, an additional $0.6 million is needed for Project 9 Academics. This can be redirected from Project 1 Special Education Teacher Contracts (again due to salary lapse from unfilled positions in FY22).

 

On August 1, 2022, the Board received notification of the need to reallocate unspent funds from Project 1 Special Education Teacher Contracts to Project 9 Academic Intervention and Project 27 COVID Case Managers/Supervision Monitors for FY23. 

 

Reallocation of ESSER dollars (over $100,000) requires School Board approval. 

 

Recommendation: 

That the School Board approve the reallocation of funds to support Project 9 Academic Intervention and Project 27 COVID Case Managers/Supervision Monitors.

Subject:  Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution 

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Michelle Pratt, director, Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:  September 15, 2022 – Regular Meeting 

 

School Board Action Required:  New Business

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In accordance with past practice, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted an updated version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution on July 19, 2022 (the “2022 Purchasing Resolution”) to reflect legislative changes made during the preceding session of the Virginia General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly-revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

Relevant changes to the 2022 Purchasing Resolution are summarized below, together with recommended conditions (or exceptions) to the School Board’s adoption of the 2022 Purchasing Resolution.  Consistent with past practice and as outlined below, the recommended conditions consist of (i) a restatement of the so-called “Construction Exception” to correct an inaccurate description of the Superintendent’s procurement authority; and (ii) a reservation of the School Board’s right to conduct procurements for goods, services, and insurance in accordance with school division policies and regulations and Virginia law whenever such action is in the best interests of the school division

 

Relevant changes and recommended conditions are as follows:

 

County’s Administrative Changes Relative to FCPS:

 

1.     Corrections to section headings and pagination due to changes in the document text. (Table of Contents)

2.     Several references to “he” have been updated to “their” to reflect gender neutrality of the individual roles. (Pages, various)

3.     Updates to the Background and Whereas sections, to update outdated language. (Pages, various)

4.     Updates to the Legal Remedies section to better align with existing legal authority.  (Page 67)

5.     Updated the equipment capitalization threshold to be consistent with County policy. (Page 13)

6.     Updated public announcement requirements to be consistent with the VPPA and current practices. (Pages, various)

7.     Updated the Audit requirements clause, under Mandatory Terms for Contracts in Excess of $200,000, to permit consideration of alternate requirements as may be in the best interests of the County when negotiating certain contracts. (Page 45)

8.     Updated Limitation of Liability to be consistent with current practices. (Page 46)

       

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s annual adoption of the 2009 through 2021 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2022 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  • The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the Board of Supervisors, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority: “The school division’s Superintendent or their designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  • The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board adopt the newly-revised 2022 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:    

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents. Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.

 

For 2022-2023, the Board members making appointments are Tamara Derenak Kaufax (Franconia), Ricardy Anderson (Mason), Laura Jane Cohen (Springfield), Karen Keys-Gamarra (At-Large), Abrar Omeish (At-Large), and Rachna Sizemore Heizer (At-Large). Last year the School Board district representatives from Braddock, Dranesville, Hunter Mill, Mount Vernon, Providence, and Sully were selected to appoint individuals.

 

The student representative to the School Board appoints a student to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.

 

If a resident who is not a parent, guardian, or employee at a local school submits a challenge, two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school. If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate. Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009. 

 

If a resident does not bring a challenge, a committee will not meet.

That the School Board appoint individuals to serve a one-year term on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:               2023 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact:      Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:        New Business

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link:          Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options, sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy actions.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state, and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, parents, and the broader Fairfax community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of both current and ongoing significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board’s 2023 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) from the Board’s 2022 legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board will discuss proposed staff recommended changes as well as Board-directed changes to the 2023 FSCB State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for October 4, 2022.  In addition, the Board will consider both state and federal legislative priorities.

 

Full draft text as well as a complete table of proposed changes to the draft 2023 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program will be included as part of the Board’s October Work Session agenda item.  Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 3, 2022.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2023 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program
