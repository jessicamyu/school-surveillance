Photographs of this presentation will be available.


Laura Ashley Piper, was a former student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987, a 1987 graduate of Robinson Secondary School, an Air Force Academy graduate, and a commissioned Second Lieutenant in the Air Force, and was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. In recognition of this outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader, the School Board in 1994 established an annual $1,000 scholarship to honor Laura’s strong commitment to student participation, her high standards of achievement, and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime.

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Guidance and Career Services Office, Department of Special Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.


RESOLUTION NAMING
SHELBY MARIE MORAN
THE 2009 LAURA ASHLEY PIPER SCHOLARSHIP RECIPIENT

WHEREAS, Shelby Moran has, through her years at Edison High School, remained consistently committed to excellence in academics, sports, and student leadership responsibilities as evidenced by her maintaining a high cumulative grade point average, taking 11 International Baccalaureate courses, participating in junior varsity and varsity field hockey for four years and in varsity indoor track, being chosen for membership in the National Honor Society, serving as president of her sophomore class, serving as vice president of the Student Government Association, participating in the International Thespian Society, and participating in Virginia Girls’ State; and

WHEREAS, her extraordinary athletic talents have brought her membership on the Edison High School varsity field hockey and indoor track teams, as well as the distinction of winning the field hockey team’s academic award all four years for the highest grade point average on the team each year; and

WHEREAS, she has contributed greatly to her community and the world around her by helping organize a walk-a-thon to benefit cancer research and suicide prevention, tutoring peers to help them hone their writing skills while working to become a better writer herself, helping underprivileged families rebuild their homes in Farmville, Virginia, writing and directing a one-act play that became a Virginia High School League National District Runner Up in competition, and participating in the Key Club community service club and her church youth group; and

WHEREAS, she has shown her great potential for continued academic success by maintaining a 4.182 grade point average, by earning two academic letters, by completing 11 International Baccalaureate courses, by attending Virginia Girls’ State where she was elected mayor of her “city,” by being chosen for membership in the National Honor Society, and by being chosen both her junior and senior years to sing in the District X Honors Choir; and

WHEREAS, her determination, strong work ethic, and ability to lead by example have earned her the admiration of her peers and teachers, and her school counselor describes her as “one of the top students I have known” and “a leader in every way.” She demonstrates qualities of initiative and trustworthiness on a daily basis, always ready with a helping hand, both in her responsibilities and beyond them, and through her consistently excellent achievements, her commitment to working hard and completing all tasks, her ability to balance a busy existence, and her potential for continued leadership and involvement throughout her life, she in every way personifies the characteristics and ideals to which Laura Ashley Piper adhered;

        Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

        Meeting Category: June 11, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 21
        Subject: Condemnation of Property

        School Board Action Required: Decision

        Related To: Operational Expectation B. Transportation

        Key Points:
            The Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (the "Department") has planned certain traffic flow and parking improvements at the Lorton Center administrative site, including an off-site drive aisle, to improve bus parking and circulation (the "Project").

            The Department has negotiated with an adjacent property owner, Inova Health Care Services ("Inova"), to convey to the School Board a temporary access easement and a grading and temporary construction easement (collectively, the "Easements") across a portion of Inova's property adjacent to Lorton Center to allow construction of the drive aisle. In the course of negotiating the Easements, the Department's surveyor/engineer discovered a small 0.031-acre triangle shaped parcel of land (the "Property") located directly in the path of the proposed drive aisle at the common boundary of the Lorton Center parcel (Tax Map Parcel 107-4-((1))-74) and the two Inova parcels (Tax Map Parcels 107-4-((1))-75A and 80). Neither Inova nor the School Board owns the Property. The School Board needs to acquire the Property in order to construct the drive aisle and complete the Project.

            A subsequent title report prepared by Walker Title, Inc. determined that the ownership status of the Property is unclear. The Property was either improperly described or inadvertently omitted from legal descriptions in deeds which form the chain of title to the Inova parcels and the School Board's Lorton Center site.

            Because of these deed errors, Walker Title was unable to determine the true legal owner of the Property. Since this parcel was omitted from the various conveyances that have occurred with the surrounding School Board and Inova properties, the title company determined that title likely is held by one of two different owners from the 1920s and 1930s, and/or their possible heirs. Given the amount of time that has passed since the named parties would have held title, it is reasonable to assume that the heirs now hold title. Also, identifying the owners is difficult in part because they sold all of their interests in the surrounding properties years ago. Because of these ambiguities, the true owner is "unknown." It is on that basis that the School Board can proceed with condemnation without attempting to purchase the property through negotiations. Pursuant to Va. Code Ann. § 25.1-204, no effort to purchase is required where the owner is "unknown."

            In its current state, the Property has no access and no material value to anyone. The condemnation process will allow the School Board to take title to the Property and proceed with the Project. A sum equal to the appraised value of the land, $1,350, will be deposited with the Circuit Court. This compensation will be held by the Court on behalf of the true owners if and when they ever come forward.

            The School Board has the authority to condemn this property pursuant to Va. Code § 22.1-127. It is required by Va. Code Ann. § 15.2-1903(B) to hold a public hearing to approve the condemnation prior to initiating condemnation proceedings.

        Recommendation:
            That the School Board (i) determine that the Project (hereinafter defined) is a necessary public use and authorize its completion, (ii) authorize the acquisition of the Property (hereinafter defined) by condemnation or other means to allow construction of the Project and (iii) authorize the Chief Operating Officer to execute on behalf of the School Board all necessary documents to accomplish such acquisition, including, without limitation, a Certificate of Take, all as approved by School Board counsel.

        6.02 Amendment 15 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC) - Recommendation to approve Amendment 15 to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan, as detailed in the agenda item (ERFC; action scheduled 6/25/09)
        Meeting
        Jun 11, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 21
        Category
        6. New Business - 9:25 p.m.
        Type
        Information

        Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

        Meeting Category: June 11, 2009 – Regular Meeting No. 21
        Subject: Amendment 15 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC)

        School Board Action Required: Information

        Related To: Legal Requirement

        Key Points:
            The ERFC Board of Trustees recommends amending Sections 6.05 and 6.06 of the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC) to clarify that if a Member’s disability benefits are terminated, the Member retains all years of Vesting Service and Credited Service that had accrued before the disability retirement commenced. Furthermore, the Member remains entitled to any benefits payable under the Plan that would be payable to a Member with the same Age and with the same Credited Service.
            If the Member is re-employed by FCPS and the Member subsequently re-applies for and receives a new disability retirement, the new disability retirement benefit shall be computed in a manner parallel to the computation of the benefits provided for other re-employed members.
