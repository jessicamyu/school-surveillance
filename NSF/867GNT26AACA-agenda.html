    Agenda Item: District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education, Title I

    Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

    Background:
    Federal and state guidelines for parent involvement under Title I of the No Child Left Behind law require participation of parents in the design and evaluation of Title I programs. This report gives an overview of the Title I program in FCPS, highlights parent involvement programs and activities in schools, reports on survey findings, and makes recommendations for 2007-2008.

    Key Issues:
    Title I District Advisory Council members have made the following recommendations:
      Continue to provide guidance and support for parents on how to help their children practice reading, writing, and math skills at home.
      Increase the number of District Advisory Council meetings from four to five to provide greater opportunities for learning about Title I, the No Child Left Behind law, and best practices in family involvement.
      Purchase dual-language books for distribution to families through the Partners in Print family literacy programs so that parents can read to their children in either English or their home language.
      Continue high quality professional development for Title I teachers, classroom teachers, and administrators in collaboration with other offices in Instructional Services.
      Encourage and assist schools in linking the FCPS Title I web site with school web sites to provide parents easier access to information about the Title I program.
      Assist schools in updating their school Family Involvement Policies to meet federal requirements.
      Continue sponsoring and coordinating family field trips for Title I schools to provide opportunities for intergenerational learning.

    Accept the Advisory Committee Report

    Agenda Item:
    Office of Early Childhood and Family Services Early Head Start (EHS)/Family and Early Childhood Education Program (FECEP)/Head Start (HS) Annual Report

    Staff Contact:
    Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

    Background:
    Federal Head Start Program Performance Standards require programs to involve parents of Head Start children in the overall development and operation of the program at the local level. In order to facilitate this, agencies are expected to provide a variety of opportunities for parents that have the potential for strengthening their role as the prime or first educator of their children and have the potential for parents’ personal growth and enhance their personal experiences.

    The EHS/FECEP/HS Annual Report highlights program updates and information, a review of 2006-2007 EHS/FECEP/HS Policy Committee activities, parent involvement and training opportunities, a status report of the Policy Committee’s June 2006 recommendations, and the Policy Committee’s recommendations for 2007-2008.

    Key Issues:
    The EHS/FECEP/HS Policy Committee has made the following recommendations for 2007-2008:
      Expand the center-based option of the Early Head Start program.
      Acquire a permanent space for EHS parent and child socializations.
      Procure vehicles to provide transportation for EHS/FECEP/HS parent activities.
      Increase reimbursement for Policy Committee and sub-committee meeting travel expenses.
      Have more parent education and skill development opportunities.
      Pursue funding for expansion of FECEP/HS classrooms to serve wait-listed children.
      Explore options to increase attendance at the schools’ quarterly Parent Committee meetings.

    School Board Discussion/Decision:
    For School Board information.

    Staff Recommendation:
    Accept the Advisory Committee report.

    Agenda Item: HRAC 2006-2007 Annual Report

    Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent

    Background:

    The Fairfax County School Board is committed to preparing all students in our global community for the world of the future and recognizes that in order to accomplish this it must consider the unique needs of our diverse ethnic and religious communities. Hence, the School Board charged the HRAC in 2006-2007 to make suggestions the Board could take to engage and maintain a higher level of stakeholder’s involvement. The School Board asked the HRAC to:

      Identify a variety of stakeholders within our diverse ethnic and religious communities.
      Meet with them to solicit their suggestions for actions the School Board could take to engage and maintain a higher level of involvement.
      Identify “best practices” currently employed by our schools that engage stakeholders within our global community.

    Key Issues:

    All of the groups came to the consensus, agreeing that the parent liaisons are an essential tool for outreach and need additional funding and support for services to meet the growing demand. FCPS needs to expand translation services to reflect the diversity of the school. The community outreach group identified a number of organizations/site/groups which are potential locations for outreach to underrepresented groups.

    School Board Discussion/Decision:

    Information only

    Staff Recommendation:

    Accept the Advisory Committee report.

    Agenda Item: School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) Annual Report

    Staff Contact: Alice Farling

    Background:

    The School Health Advisory Committee is mandated by the Commonwealth of Virginia to assist with the development of health policy in the school division and the evaluation of the status of school health, health education, the school environment, and health services.

    Key Issues:

    SHAC supports and recommends:
        Consider requiring a minimum of two physical education class sessions per week (a minimum of 90 minutes per week) at the elementary level.
        Setting a strong recess policy.
        Raising awareness of the need for physical activity.
        Identifying successful programs currently used in FCPS and communicating the availability and value of these programs to all elementary school principals.

    School Board Discussion/Decision:

    Report is for information only.

    Staff Recommendation:

    Accept the Advisory Committee report.
