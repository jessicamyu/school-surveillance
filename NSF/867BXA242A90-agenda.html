SUBJECT: Updated Renovation Queue Consultant Report


TYPE: Discussion


BACKGROUND:
      In March 2008 the School Board directed staff to engage the services of a private firm to conduct a study of 63 specific schools to determine in an objective manner the priority listing of renovating these schools. The firm was to use the criteria approved by the Board for the study and present its findings in time for incorporation of the results in the FY 2010 – 2014 Capital Improvement Program (CIP).

      In May 2008 the firm of Samaha and Associates was engaged to conduct this study. The firm has completed its work and presented its impartial findings in the attached report.

KEY POINTS:
      Staff intends to use the results of this report in developing the FY 2010 – 2014 CIP.
      As originally noted to the School Board, the school system will only commit to conducting in the report’s priority sequence those projects that can be completed within five years. Every five years the school system will engage a firm to conduct an updated facility assessment and condition study that might result in changes to the priority sequence for those schools that cannot be renovated within the first five years of this renovation queue. It is possible and probable that the School Board will add schools to the scope of future studies.


RECOMMENDATION:
      Staff recommends that the School Board accept the results of the study and direct staff to incorporate it into future capital improvement plans.

Meeting

Category
21. Policy - 4:30 p.m.
Type






Staff Contact: Eileen Grattan, Hearing Officer for the Superintendent


Meeting Type: September 8, 2008 Work Session

SUBJECT: Policy 2611


TYPE: Discussion


BACKGROUND:
      Policy 2611 outlines the process for appealing certain student disciplinary decisions to the School Board.

      As presently in effect, Policy 2611.7 provides a hearing before a School Board committee for any decision issued by the Division Superintendent, following a school’s recommendation for expulsion, which forwards the expulsion recommendation or assigns the student to a different school or educational program.

      The revised Policy retains a School Board committee hearing for those appeals where the Division Superintendent has endorsed and forwarded the school’s recommended expulsion, but provides for School Board review only on the written record on those appeals where the Division Superintendent decided not to forward the school’s recommendation for expulsion and instead to assign the student to a different school or educational program while holding the school’s expulsion recommendation in abeyance.

KEY POINTS:
Under the Code of Virginia, only the School Board has the authority to expel a student. The Superintendent has the authority to assign a student to a different school or alternative program. If the Superintendent’s hearing officer determines that a sanction other than expulsion is appropriate, the case is not forwarded to the School Board for any further action. However, the current version of P2611 provides the parents with a process to appeal the Superintendent’s decision to assign a student to another regular or alternative school. If a parent chooses to appeal that decision, the School Board committee then convenes a hearing to make a decision on whether or not to expel the student.

Staff is recommending that Policy 2611 be revised to provide parents the opportunity to appeal a hearing officer’s decision, for those cases where the hearing officer has not forwarded the school’s expulsion recommendation, to the School Board for review only on the written record. This written record review will streamline the appeal process by reducing the number of School Board committee hearings, as well as reduce the number of days before a final decision is delivered and thereby hasten students’ receipt of educational services following disciplinary action. Legal fees for School Board committee hearings also should decrease.

This revision will make appeals of school assignments consistent with the appeal process currently in place for long term suspensions and for reassignments as a result of criminal charges/convictions based on actions in the community.


OPTIONS:
      1. Adopt the revised regulation

      2. No change in current version of 2611

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board accept the revision of Policy 2611.

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

Meeting Type: September 8, 2008, School Board Work Session No. 17


SUBJECT: Policy 8130

TYPE: New Business

BACKGROUND: Review Policy Clarifications on Administrative Boundary Changes.


KEY POINTS: Paragraph 4 of Policy 8130.5, Adjustment Procedures and Administrative Changes, sets forth three circumstances under which the Superintendent is authorized to make administrative boundary changes after consultation with affected School Board representatives. Staff is seeking clarification on two of these circumstances:
                  - an emergency or other overriding public need requires such a change
                  - less than five percent of the enrollment of each school will be affected, and analysis indicates the change will improve the operating efficiency
              Staff is seeking clarification on what constitutes overriding public need, e.g., overcrowding that impacts building function and instruction; overcrowding that can no longer be addressed by temporary facilities; transportation inefficiencies.

              Staff is also seeking clarification on 1) whether the intent of five percent of enrollment refers to projected or current enrollment and 2) whether that five percent limitation is on a per year basis or based on cumulative impact of an administrative change as it is phased over several years.

              The interpretation of these points will impact the effectiveness of the administrative boundary change policy as a tool to manage local school boundaries. Note: No clarification or change is sought for the accompanying Regulation 8130.5 which requires public notice and that a public meeting be held with the sending and receiving schools to explain the adjustment and to solicit public comments.

OPTIONS: Direct staff to revise policy for clarification
Make no change to existing policy

RECOMMENDATION: Provide clarification which would allow use of Administrative Boundary Change as a more effective and flexible tool to exercise the Board’s vested authority “to provide for the consolidation of schools or redistricting of school boundaries or adopt pupil assignment plans whenever such procedure will contribute to the efficiency of the school division.” [Code of Virginia: Section 22.1-79 (4)]

SUMMARY OF LINKAGE COMMITTEE RECOMMENDATION
ON COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT ON CHANGING SCHOOL START AND DISMISSAL TIMES

Background
    • The Board agreed to conduct a two-phased Linkage approach with regard to the TTF. The first phase (conducted over the summer) was the Study Circles. The plan for phase two was to conduct a number of community meetings (preferably over one weekend) to engage the broader community after the Board had received the report from staff providing detailed costs and an actual blueprint for the bell schedule.
    • At its August 19 meeting, the Linkage Committee reviewed Bell Schedule Report Iteration #2 presented by Dean Tistadt and members of his staff.

Recommendation
    If the School Board accepts Iteration #2 as the final response to the TTF report, recommend that the Board conduct a series of meetings with the community to present this report to the community. The objectives of these community meetings would be:
      1. To present to the general public our blueprint for moving to later start times (if the Board were to decide to move ahead in this direction)
      2. To gauge the reaction of the community of the anticipated changes in the bell schedule as outlined in Iteration #2 and to the associated costs
      3. To share concerns gathered from participants of the June 2008 Study Circles and engage the community in a solutions-oriented discussion around the concerns. The concerns that emerged included:
        • Child-care issues—with the proposed change, high schoolers and middle schoolers would be at home alone getting off to school; at the end of the day, elementary schoolers would be at home alone without older children to supervise
        • Time spent at school for younger children—elementary students who start early would have a longer day if they attend SACC; parents were also concerned about the cost of SACC increasing if more hours were needed to cover earlier start times
        • After-school activities—the later the dismissal time, the more concerned parents were with juggling after-school activities, homework, and dinner
    Due to the extensive time the School Board will be committing to community dialogue about the budget this fall, the Linkage Committee recommends that these community meetings on the bell schedule should take place in February 2009.
