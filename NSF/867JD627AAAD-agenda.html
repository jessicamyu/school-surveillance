    RESOLUTION RECOGNIZING
    VIRGINIA SCHOOL BULLYING AWARENESS MONTH
    JANUARY 2007

    WHEREAS, school bullying has become an increasingly significant problem in the United States and Virginia; and

    WHEREAS, almost thirty percent of the youth in United States are estimated to be involved in bullying each year, either as a bully or as a victim; and

    WHEREAS, an estimated one hundred sixty thousand students in kindergarten through twelfth grade miss school every day due to a fear of being bullied; and

    WHEREAS, bullying can take many forms, including verbal, physical, and most recently in cyberspace, and can happen in many places on and off school grounds; and

    WHEREAS, it is important for Virginia students, teachers, and school administrators to be aware of bullying, and to encourage discussion of the problem as a school community; and

    WHEREAS, the state school board association adopted a model policy on anti-bullying to encourage positive behaviors and to eliminate bullying behaviors; and

    NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board joins the Virginia School Boards Association in recognizing the month of January 2007, as Virginia School Bullying Awareness Month, with the intention that the issue of bullying and its prevention be discussed in Virginia during that time.

    National Mentoring Month - January 2007

    National Mentoring Month will mark its sixth year in 2007. By focusing national attention on the need for mentors, as well as how each of us – individuals, businesses, government agencies, schools, faith communities and nonprofits – can work together to increase the number of mentors, we can assure brighter futures for our young people.

    The future course of our nation rests on the shoulders of its youth, and educated, confident, and nurtured children will give rise to a stronger nation. Mentoring is a proven, effective strategy matching a caring, responsible adult with a student to provide guidance and build confidence, stability, and direction for that student. Research has shown that mentoring has a definitive impact on young people by increasing attendance at school, improving rates of high school graduation and college attendance, and decreasing involvement with drugs, alcohol, and violent behaviors.

    National Mentoring Month emphasizes the importance of mentoring and recognizes with praise and gratitude the many Americans that are involved in mentoring.

    Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

    SUBJECT: Award of Contract – Integrated Retirement Benefit and Payroll System for ERFC

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board authorize the Educational Employees' Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC) Board of Trustees, or its designee, to execute a contract with Levi, Ray & Shoup, Inc. (LRS) for the provision of an integrated Retirement Benefit Administration and Payroll computer system in the amount of $2,272,472; to execute a contract with L. R. Wechsler, Ltd. (“LRWL”) for the provision of associated consulting services in an amount not to exceed $420,000; to procure required system hardware/software in an amount not to exceed $241,895; and to obtain necessary maintenance and support agreements for a five-year period following system implementation. Funding will be provided from the ERFC Trust Fund.

    EXPLANATION:
        The FCPS Office of Procurement Services issued a Request for Proposal RFP06-861257-73 soliciting offers from qualified sources to provide an integrated Retirement Benefit Administration and Payroll computer system for ERFC. System requirements include the complete replacement of all current ERFC applications with a single integrated solution containing features that extend beyond ERFC’s current capabilities for benefit administration, retiree payroll, benefit calculation and workload monitoring. The contract also includes back-file conversion of member hardcopy files using LRS’ imaging solution and member web services to allow members to access account information and process requests on-line.
        The computer system RFP was publicly advertised with three (3) firms submitting proposals. A Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) comprised of the ERFC Executive Director and other senior administrators from ERFC and the FCPS Department of Information Technology evaluated the proposals in accordance with the criteria and procedures established in the RFP. Based upon the proposals that were reviewed, interviews and site visits conducted with the two top candidates, the SAC recommended contract award to LRS.

        The FCPS Office of Procurement Services also issued RFP06-828861-73 to solicit bids from qualified sources to provide consulting services related to project management and implementation of the new system. After reviewing four bids, a subset of the SAC recommended contract award to LRWL.

        Software maintenance and support costs for the five-year period following full implementation of the new system will average approximately $137,000 per year.
        At the Board meeting on November 21, 2006, members of the ERFC Board of Trustees voted unanimously to recommend the contract award.

      Meeting
      Dec 21, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 10
      Category
      6. New Business
      Type
      Information


      Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer
      SUBJECT: Memorandum of Understanding

      RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board approve and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute a Memorandum of Understanding between the School Board and the Town of Vienna to confirm the longstanding understandings between the parties regarding the use, maintenance, supervision, and management of Waters and Caffi Fields located adjacent to Vienna Elementary School.
      EXPLANATION:
          The School Board owns the approximately 6.74-acre property in the Town of Vienna on which Waters and Caffi Fields are located. The subject property is adjacent to Vienna Elementary School. For many years, this property has been operated, maintained and supervised as a recreational facility by the Town of Vienna.

          Over the years, Waters and Caffi Fields have become an integral community focal point for Vienna. The Town has made significant improvements to the fields and has maintained them, at the Town’s expense and with contributions from various user groups. The Town has maintained insurance coverage for use of the fields and has assumed all responsibility for the operation, maintenance and supervision of the facilities. Vienna Elementary School students do use these fields, as do students from surrounding schools, such as James Madison High School.

          The creation of the proposed Memorandum of Understanding was initiated by the Town of Vienna and will serve to memorialize the longstanding understandings and agreements between the Town and the School Board. Staff believes this action to be in the best interest of the School Board.
