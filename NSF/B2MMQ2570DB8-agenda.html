3.04 Recognition of 2019 School Board Character Award Recipient
Meeting
Jun 13, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board Chris Giovarelli and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. The Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation has generously funded the $500 award since 2007. The award is given to a junior or senior who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for students in Fairfax County Public Schools in day-to-day behavior.

 

The recipient of the 2019 School Board Character award is Camden Wiseman, a senior at TJHSST.

 

Camden is a powerful source of inspiration for the TJHSST community. As a survivor of childhood cancer and volunteer ambassador for the Childhood Brain Tumor Foundation, Camden has shared his story in hopes it will inspire others to find strength and power in their own lives. His positive words and actions encourage his peers to pursue their own unique passions in making the world a better place. According to one counselor, “Camden has grown into an amazing advocate for himself as well as others and enriches the lives of everyone around him. Simply put, I’m better for knowing him.”

 

Camden is an intrepid, buoyant thinker whose residual vision impairment has not deterred him from serving his community. Camden is the captain of his chess team, and in this leadership role, founded a chess outreach program at an FCPS Title I elementary school where he and his teammates provide free chess instruction to students on a weekly basis. He has also served on his school’s honor council for the past two years, been a certified soccer referee since 2013, and was selected to serve as his National Honor Society chapter’s Service Chair where he has helped coordinate events for causes such as safe teen driving, food for homeless shelters, bookmarks for kids at low-income elementary schools, a book drive at a nearby synagogue, and a pet toy drive for an animal shelter.

 

With great dedication and a courageous spirit, Camden eagerly anticipates broadening his exposure to economics, finance, and frontier oncology breakthroughs and aspires to a business career that encompasses cancer research advocacy. Camden’s high morals, strong integrity, and resilience epitomize the character traits we want to see in all of our students. Through his exceptional work ethic, he has developed an admirable profile in academics, athletics, and community service. Camden has earned and truly deserves the School Board Character Award.

Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

 

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

3.06 Recognition of School Resource Officers
Meeting
Jun 13, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type

Fairfax County Public Schools and the police departments of Fairfax County, Fairfax City and the Town of Herndon have enjoyed a mutually beneficial relationship for more than two decades. One of the hallmarks of this relationship is the School Resource Officer (SRO) program. SROs partner with school administrators to ensure safe, productive, and inviting school environments for students and staff by deterring crime, maintaining orderly schools, and preventing truancy.

In addition, SROs provide relevant safety education programs to FCPS students. Many SROs also volunteer as student mentors through the Mentorworks Program, serving as trusted adults that students can depend on to assist them in making healthy and safe choices. Furthermore, SROs play a critical role in ensuring the success of the Alternative Accountability Program, a collaborative and community-oriented response to juvenile offenses that utilizes the principles of restorative justice to hold students accountable for their actions, while at the same time preventing formal Court involvement and satisfying the needs of victims.

 

Simply put, in order for students to be successful, they require safe and productive learning environments. Our SROs help provide these positive environments and also establish valued and trusted relationships with students and staff.

3.08 Recognition of National Safety and Law Enforcement Officer Month
Meeting
Jun 13, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type

Observed annually in June, National Safety Month is a time to increase safety awareness with the goal to "Keep Each Other Safe". We are reminded that safety is no accident, it is a choice we need to make throughout our lives. We are all empowered to make safe decisions for ourselves and those we care about. Over 186,000 FCPS students depend on school employees to provide a safe environment in schools and centers. Prevention, identification, and abatement of hazards are integral parts of every employee’s job.

 

The FCPS Office of Safety and Security is a 24/7 operation that develops, deploys and monitors safety and security programs throughout the division that keep all of us safe. The office has established emergency plans and provided appropriate training for employees to cultivate safety and security throughout the school district.

 

Safety services are focused in the areas of; environmental health, chemistry classrooms, theatre arts, fire alarm systems, traffic operations, OSHA programs, and include a comprehensive annual inspection process at all schools.

 

Security services provide; a 24-hour security operations center for emergency communications, security patrols, response to calls, school inspections, central monitoring of the Security and Fire Alarm System for all FCPS facilities, training and assistance to all schools and centers in crisis management, facility assessments, emergency management, incident command, technology based and physical security initiatives and systems, the SRO program, and telephone, text, and email tip lines for safety.

 

During National Safety Month, we also honor Law Enforcement Officers of every rank and file who have chosen a profession that puts their lives on the line every day for the safety of their communities. They’ve answered a call to public service that is demanding and often unappreciated. On this day we have an opportunity to thank these dedicated men and women for the service and sacrifices they make for our communities. They selflessly confront danger to protect our families and defend our communities. These officers serve with courage, dedication, and strength, fearlessly enforcing our laws, even at the risk of personal peril, safeguarding our property, our liberty, and our lives. We owe them, and their families, our full and enduring support.

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

 

The FY 2020 employee calendar currently includes 12 holidays for twelve-month employees which was aligned with 11.5 holidays designated for Fairfax County Government employees.

 

For the 2019-2020 school year, the July 4th holiday falls on a Thursday and Friday, July 5th is currently scheduled as a work day.

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors recently authorized July 5th as an additional holiday for county employees. Additionally, the Virginia Governor authorized July 5th as an additional holiday for state employees earlier in May.

 

It is recommended that July 5, 2019, also be authorized as an additional holiday for all twelve-month Fairfax County Public Schools employees to remain in alignment with the county. This also provides the School Board with an opportunity to recognize the work of twelve-month employees and thank them for dedication with a much deserved four-day weekend.

 

 

Recommendation:  That the School Board approve Friday, July 5, 2019 as a paid holiday for all twelve-month Fairfax County Public Schools employees.

Summary/Background:

 

The Instructional Services Department recommends that the School Board award a contract to the University of Wisconsin for the provision of English language proficiency (ELP) assessments.  These assessments are mandated by the Virginia Department of Education for use by all public schools in Virginia.

 

The total estimated payment to the University of Wisconsin for this assessment for the 2018-2019 school year is expected to be $1.1M; the payment amount is based upon the total number of assessments submitted for scoring in a given school year.  Policy 5011 requires that the School Board approve “any contract or contract amendment awarded between the School Board and other governmental entity that exceeds $250,000.”  Policy 5011.3 (III)(A)(1).

 

These assessments also comply with federal legislation [Section 3113(b)(2) and Section 1111 (b) (7) of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 2015] and related guidance from the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE), which requires that all English learners (ELs):

 

  1. be assessed in English language proficiency upon entry into a Virginia public school; and
  2. be assessed annually in English language proficiency.  

 

This assessment suite from the University of Wisconsin, developed by the World-Class Instructional Design and Assessment (WIDA) Consortium, measures an English learner’s proficiency in English reading, writing, speaking, and listening skills for the purpose of establishing the student’s eligibility for English for Speakers of Other Languages (ESOL) services and monitoring the student’s progress in acquiring English proficiency.

 

 

Assessments within the WIDA suite are:

 

  • WIDA Assessing Comprehension and Communication in English State to State for English Language Learners® 2.0 (WIDA ACCESS for ELLs 2.0), used for annual assessment of most ELs
  • WIDA Alternate ACCESS for ELLs, used for annual assessment of certain ELs with significant cognitive abilities
  • WIDA-ACCESS Placement Test (W-APT), used for entering kindergarten ELs
  • WIDA Screener, used for ELs entering or re-entering FCPS from outside Virginia public schools
  • WIDA Measure of Developing English Language (MODEL), used for ELs entering in second semester of kindergarten or first semester of grade 1

 

The Virginia Board of Education adopted the WIDA ACCESS for ELLs 2.0 in 2007 and the WIDA Alternate ACCESS for ELLs in 2013 as the state-approved ELP annual assessments for EL students. 

 

In FCPS, these assessments are used to:

 

  • Determine students’ ELP level, eligibility for ESOL services, and eligibility for testing accommodations and exemptions;
  • Calculate EL progress as part of the combined rate for English Achievement and English Achievement Gap school quality indicators within the Standards of Accreditation (SOA);
  • Help teachers and families understand students’ current level of English language proficiency and progress toward proficiency along the developmental continuum;
  • Provide teachers with information they can use to plan for and support student learning;
  • Produce data necessary to calculate annual ESOL staffing allocations for each school; and
  • Generate information required for annual application for federal Title III, Part A funding.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (i) award the contract to the University of Wisconsin for English language proficiency assessments for the period of time required by the Virginia Department of Education, beginning with 2018-2019 school year, and (ii) authorize the Director of the Office of Procurement Services to execute, deliver and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

Policy 5011.3 requires that the School Board approve any contract or contract amendment between the School Board and another Government entity that exceeds $250,000.

 

The Pathway to the Baccalaureate is a multi-year, regional program supported by NVCC, George Mason University, and other participating local school divisions. The 2019-20 school year will be the 14th year of our partnership and participation in the Pathway program. Since 2005, this program has increased from four FCPS high schools with approximately 265 students to 16 high schools and approximately 1,300 students.     

 

The program uses a three-step, student-centered approach to increase access to higher education for selected, underrepresented students. Step one is to prepare students for a successful transition to college by providing one-on-one meetings with a Pathway counselor to develop individualized transition plans. Step two is to support students while in college through continued one-on-one counseling, academic advising, and academic and leadership skill building. Step three includes customized degree planning, assistance with the transfer process, and continued access to program supports at George Mason University.   

 

FCPS expenditures for this contract will not exceed $280,000 for the 2019-20 school year. Provisions have been included to adjust the funding if positions are not fully staffed during the term of the agreement.
