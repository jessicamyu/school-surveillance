Fire Prevention Week October 8 - 14

Fire Prevention Week was established to commemorate the Great Chicago Fire, the tragic 1871 conflgration that killed more that 250 people, left 100,000 homeless, destroyed more than 17,400 structures and burned more that 2,000 acres. The fire began on October 8, but continued into and did most of its damage on October 9, 1871.

"Prevent Cooking Fires: Watch What You Heat" - that's the message of this year's Fire Prevention Week. From October 8-14, the National Fire Protection Association will be spreading the word that more fires start in the kitchen than in any other part of the home - and teaching families and kids how to keep cooking fires from starting in the first place.

Head Start Awareness Month
October has been designated as Head Start Awareness Month. Since 1965 Head Start has succeeded by promoting school readiness in children and self-sufficiency for their families through: health services, social services, nutritious meals, parent involvement and early childhood education. Since its inception, Head Start has provided services to more than eight million children and their families in every state and territory.

RESOLUTION
NAMING THE FINE ARTS LIBRARY AT
THE INSTRUCTIONAL SUPPORT CENTER
“THE FARRIS FINE ARTS LIBRARY”

WHEREAS, John and Penny Farris have served Fairfax County Public Schools with distinction and commitment since 1956, when W.T. Woodson was Superintendent, and the school system served 33,000 students; and

WHEREAS, as music teachers in band, chorus, and general music, and at all levels of instruction, they have cumulatively served the schools of Annandale, Braddock, Bren Marr Park, Cameron, Crestwood, Edsall Park, Forestdale, Fort Hunt, Franconia, Garfield, Irving, Lorton, Luther Jackson, Lynbrook, Newington Forest, Rose Hill, Sleepy Hollow, Springfield Estates, and Stratford Landing; and

WHEREAS, since Penny’s first retirement in 1988, John and Penny have served faithfully as Central Music Librarians, and later Fine Arts Librarians, assisting all of the Fine Arts teachers of FCPS; and

WHEREAS, John and Penny Farris have served the community of Fairfax County as founding members of the Fairfax Symphony, where John played for 32 years, and Penny played for 25 years, and as founders of the Northern Virginia Youth Symphony, which later became the American Youth Philharmonic;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the Fine Arts Library at the Instructional Support Center “The Farris Fine Arts Library,” for the Farris’ unselfish dedication and 100 cumulative years of outstanding service to music education, providing quality resources to all fine arts teachers, and improving the quality of life in Fairfax County.

SUBJECT: W.T. Woodson High School Renovation Project – School Board’s Delegation of Procurement and Contract Authority

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to procure and contract in accordance with the Virginia Public Procurement Act (including the issuance and execution of all necessary and appropriate documents including, without limitation, contracts and contract change orders) on behalf of the School Board for the purchase of construction, goods and services specifically related to the W.T. Woodson High School renovation project, provided each such individual purchase transaction does not exceed $1,000,000.00 and does not, when aggregated with all prior purchase transactions, exceed the total funds budgeted by the School Board for this Project.
EXPLANATION:
      The School Board’s procurement of construction and design-related services is subject to the Virginia Public Procurement Act (the “Act”), which expressly permits delegation of procurement and contract authority to “its duly designated or authorized officers and employees.” Va. Code Ann. § 2.2-4302.

      The Woodson Renovation Project will be accomplished through a series of multiple-prime construction contracts between the School Board, as owner, and various trade contractors or vendors. In addition, in order to save the cost of sales and use taxes, the Project documents provide for certain materials and equipment to be purchased by the School Board directly or by others pursuant to agency agreements specifically pre-approved for this purpose by the Virginia Department of Taxation.

      The School Board will not employ the traditional owner-general contractor structure on this Project. Instead, the school system’s Office of Design & Construction will undertake many of the project responsibilities customarily performed by the general contractor, including coordination, scheduling, and supervision. In assuming this role, the school system will be required from time to time to procure and contract for certain items or services on an expedited basis in order to respond to events occurring at the job site. The delegation of procurement and contract award authority (including change orders to contracts previously awarded), as contemplated by this agenda item, will enable the school system to fulfill its responsibilities on a timely basis in this context.

      The successful completion of the project’s critical path milestones is dependent upon the ability of staff to conduct business in a timely manner. From time to time as the project progresses, the school system will be required to solicit bids and award contracts (and change orders) within timeframes that may be inconsistent with the School Board’s established meeting calendar. All contract awards and change orders will be made in accordance with the requirements of the Virginia Public Procurement Act and within the total project budget for this renovation.

      During the course of the Project, the School Board will receive a monthly summary of all purchase transactions effected pursuant to this agenda Item.

SUBJECT: Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee

RECOMMENDATION:
    That six School Board Members appoint individuals to serve on the Challenged Materials Interdepartmental Review Committee, as listed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:
    Regulation 3009.8, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents. Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee. Two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school. The level will be determined by a random selection. If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate.

    The student representative to the School Board appoints an individual to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.
    Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in Regulation 3009.8

      That the School Board award annual contracts for time and materials rates for facilities modifications and construction services to responsive and responsible bidders and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contracts on behalf of the School Board.

EXPLANATION:
      Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) annually seeks to establish contracts for the provision of facility modifications services at schools and administrative buildings on a ready, as-required basis.
