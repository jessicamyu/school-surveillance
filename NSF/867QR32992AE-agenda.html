    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    SUBJECT: FY 2005 Midyear Budget Review

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2005 Midyear Budget Review, as detailed in the agenda item.
      EXPLANATION:

          Major expenditure adjustments in this agenda provide $1.0 million that can be allocated by the School Board. It is recommended that these funds be added to the FY 2006 beginning balance to help offset the projected deficit.

          Adjustments include an additional $3.5 million in grant-related revenue that is partially offset by a corresponding $1.0 million increase in grant related expenditures.
          Other revenue adjustments include a $1.4 million reduction in state aid due to a revised ADM projection and a $0.6 million reduction to Fairfax City tuition. Major expenditure adjustments include:
      · $1.5 million and 24.0 instructional coaches to support schools in jeopardy of not making AYP in 2005
      · $1.8 million for compliance with statutory mandates
      · $2.3 million and 40.0 positions are not required due to lower general education membership
      · $1.7 million reduction related to the NCLB set aside funds

          Meeting
          Dec 2, 2004 - Regular Meeting No. 9
          Category
          6. New Business - 8 p.m.
          Type
          Information
          Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

          SUBJECT: District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education, Title I

          RECOMMENDATION:
            That the Board confirms the elected members of the Title I District Advisory Council (see attachment).

          EXPLANATION:
            Compensatory Education, Title I regulations require that the local education agency establish a District Advisory Council. Guidelines state that membership must be composed of elected members from each school and at least one teacher of children being served. Parents whose children are currently receiving Compensatory Education services must comprise at least 51 percent of the council. The proposed membership meets this requirement. Confirmation by the Board gives legal status to District Advisory Council members in the performance of their program planning and evaluation duties.
