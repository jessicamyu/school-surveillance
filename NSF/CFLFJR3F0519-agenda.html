RESOLUTION

HONORING

SOUTH COUNTY MIDDLE SCHOOL

                                                                             

WHEREAS, South County Middle School, proudly celebrated its 10th anniversary on May 10, 2022, and its foundation built on the mission of its first Principal, Marsha Manning, to produce “Capable, Connected, and Contributing” citizens for a global society; and

 

WHEREAS, for 10 years, South County Middle School has provided a positive learning environment by providing an academically challenging education to students through general education, an Advanced Academics Level IV Center Program, an Honors Program, and a Special Education Program; and

 

WHEREAS, South County Middle School works in partnership with faculty, parents, students, Parent Teacher Student Organization members, and the community to benefit student success and achievement in the classroom and on the school grounds, by providing students with access to a wide variety of elective offerings through Fine and Performing Arts, Career and Technical Education, and World Languages Programs; and through diverse and robust after school programs; and

 

WHEREAS, the dedicated faculty of South County Middle School continues to stay abreast of technological changes and use technology to support the 21st century learns, with each student receiving an FCPS-issued laptop to access dynamic resources and participate in learning tailored to the student’s individual needs; and

 

WHEREAS, South County Middle School continues its mission under the leadership of Principal Peter Kownacki, taking great pride in displaying the HORSE character traits of Honesty, Ownership, Respect, Service, and Effort;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED  that the Fairfax County School Board honors South County Middle School on the occasion of its 10th Anniversary, commends the school on its history of providing educational excellence and enrichment opportunities to the students and staff, and conveys its most heartfelt best wishes for a successful anniversary celebration.

NAMING OF THE CAFETERIA AT STRATFORD LANDING ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

“THE JOHN ADAMS CAFETERIA”

 

WHEREAS, John Adams has served Stratford Landing Elementary School as Building Supervisor and Custodian for 45 years, earning the 2010 Fairfax County Schools Outstanding Operational Employee award, collaborating with multiple departments through two renovations and one building addition, preparing the building for summer programs and the start of school, maintaining a safe environment for students and staff; and

 

WHEREAS, Mr. Adams responded to teacher needs in a timely manner, securing needed items, moving furniture, setting up for special events, reliably opening the building for teachers to have an early start to their teaching day; and

 

WHEREAS, Mr. Adams interacted positively and patiently with all students, using the proper blend of friendliness and authority, and was constantly aware of and responsive to students’ needs in the school and cafeteria; and

 

WHEREAS, Mr. Adams willingly volunteered to help before anyone requested his assistance, the suggestions he provided to help make after school activities flow more smoothly, and his daily presence in the cafeteria during breakfast and lunch helped to instill a sense of respect and responsibility in the students; and 

 

WHEREAS, Mr. Adams continued to keep the grounds of Stratford Landing well maintained and manicured so that the outside appearance of the school was as inviting as the inside.

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the School Board of Fairfax County, Virginia, honors John Adams with the naming of the Stratford Landing Elementary School Cafeteria as "The John Adams Cafeteria" in recognition of his service to the entire Stratford Landing community through the caring way he has maintained Stratford Landing and served the students, families, and community during his time as custodian and building supervisor at Stratford Landing Elementary School.

Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services 

 

Meeting Category:  July 14, 2022 Regular Meeting

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

All the FY 2022 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the nine funds. All comparisons are against the FY 2022 Revised Budget. In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for revenue and expenditure variances, School Board flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, and commitments and carryover, funds available for FY 2023 total $90.9 million.

 

Excluding School Operating Fund grants, the FY 2022 actual revenue variance totals $8.5 million, due to an increase in sales tax offset by lower Basic Aid caused by reduced enrollment; Federal revenue; offset by other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, Fairfax City tuition, and miscellaneous revenue.

 

Excluding School Operating Fund grants, the expenditure variance is $260.1 million less than budgeted. Funding available after accounting for reserves, commitments, and carryover is primarily comprised of salary and benefits fallout attributed to lower student enrollment caused by the pandemic, salary lapse, and a one-month health benefit premium holiday.

 

As a result of the revenue and expenditure variances, available funds for FY 2023 total $90.9 million. In addition, the General Assembly had not formally adopted the 2022-2024 biennial budget at the time of FCPS’ FY 2023 Approved Budget adoption. As compared to the budget passed by the General Assembly, FCPS will receive a net increase of approximately $25.3 million in additional state revenue. Of the additional revenue, $18.1 million is to support a one-time $1,000 bonus for Standards of Quality funded instructional and support positions.

 

This agenda item includes recommendations for FY 2023 expenditure adjustments, including an employee bonus and funding for staffing contingencies, stadium restrooms, major maintenance, FCPS cost share for electric buses, Young Scholars extracurricular enrichment, a shortfall in the Adult and Community Education Fund, a middle school start time study, a recruitment incentive for substitute teachers, increased electricity costs, paper inflation, a superintendent strategic reserve, food services equipment, Title IX softball field upgrades, a safety and security review, turf fields, and to maintain the beginning balance for FY 2024. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

The School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

Problem Statement: 

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to partner with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.  The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

 

Additional state funding for the regional school.  The estimate for FY 2023 is $4.2 million.  The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $5.7 million.

  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

 

Outcome: 

 

To continue to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2022-2023.

              and execute Proffer Statements

 

Staff Contact:  Jessica Gillis, acting assistant superintendent, Facilities and

                      Transportation Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Heather Diez, director, Design and Construction Services

 

Meeting Category:  July 14, 2022—Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points (Justice High School):

 

Facilities staff has filed, on behalf of the School Board, Rezoning Application No. RZ-2021-MA-00026 in order to permit two building additions and site improvements at Justice High School.

The current student enrollment at 2,182 exceeds the existing design capacity at 1,994 students.  In order to provide additional capacity for the school to accommodate current enrollment and allow for growth up to 2,500 students, facilities staff is proposing to increase the maximum permitted gross floor area for the property to allow a three-story classroom building addition and a one-story expansion of the cafeteria.  Construction funds for these building additions were approved in the 2019 Fairfax County School Bond referendum. 

The proposed building additions will add approximately 47,222 square feet, which will result in a total gross floor area of approximately 353,889 square feet.  However, the current R-3 District zoning for the Justice HS property permits a maximum of 0.30 FAR for public uses.  With the additions, the FAR will increase to 0.387 which exceeds the maximum permitted FAR.  The R-4 and R-5 Districts permit a maximum of only 0.35 FAR for public uses, which will not accommodate the proposed 0.387 FAR for the additions.  To address this, the Justice High School property needs to be rezoned to the R-8 District, which permits a maximum FAR of 0.55.

In keeping with County policy, the School Board must submit a signed Proffer Statement (a draft of which is attached) prior to the public hearing held by the Board of Supervisors.  Typically, such a Proffer Statement can require minor revisions as the Rezoning Application is reviewed by County staff and by the Planning Commission in preparation for the Board of Supervisors’ public hearing.  The final version of the Proffer Statement adopted by the Board of Supervisors will go into effect, superseding all prior drafts.  The hearing before the Planning Commission is currently scheduled for July 13, 2022, with a hearing before the Board of Supervisors on August 2, 2022.

Key Points (Mosaic Elementary School):

Facilities staff has filed, on behalf of the School Board, Rezoning Application No. RZ-2021-PR-00030 in order to permit two building additions and site improvements at Mosaic Elementary School.

The current student enrollment at 961 is less than the existing design capacity at 1,038 students.  However, the school is in need of significant improvements.  This project will renovate the school with new building systems and finishes along with several building additions.  Specifically, the renovation will include an expansion of the building consisting of approximately 55,365 square feet.  With the proposed renovations, the existing design capacity will increase by 22 students for a design capacity of 1,060 students.  Construction funds for the renovation of the building were approved in the 2021 Fairfax County School Bond referendum.

The current R-2 District zoning permits a maximum of 0.25 FAR for public uses.  The cumulative gross floor area for the school with the additions will be approximately 125,000 square feet.  With that total gross floor area, the FAR will be 0.29 which exceeds the maximum FAR in the R-2 District.  Therefore, the School Board is proposing to rezone the Property to the R-3 District.

The School Board needs to authorize the negotiation and signing of the Proffer Statement for the Mosaic Elementary School application as well.  The hearing before the Planning Commission is currently scheduled for September 28, 2022, with a hearing before the Board of Supervisors to be scheduled.

Recommendation:

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent and the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to take all actions necessary and appropriate, in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to pursue approval of the Rezoning Application Nos. RZ-2021-MA-00026 and RZ-2021-PR-00030, respectfully, including negotiating, signing, and submitting the final Proffer Statements and any other documents on behalf of the School Board.

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for revenue and expenditure variances, School Board flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, and commitments and carryover, funds available for FY 2023 total $90.9 million.

 

Excluding School Operating Fund grants, the FY 2022 actual revenue variance totals $8.5 million, due to an increase in sales tax offset by lower Basic Aid caused by reduced enrollment; Federal revenue; offset by other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, Fairfax City tuition, and miscellaneous revenue.

 

Excluding School Operating Fund grants, the expenditure variance is $260.1 million less than budgeted. Funding available after accounting for reserves, commitments, and carryover is primarily comprised of salary and benefits fallout attributed to lower student enrollment caused by the pandemic, salary lapse, and a one-month health benefit premium holiday.

In addition, the General Assembly had not formally adopted the 2022-2024 biennial budget at the time of FCPS’ FY 2023 Approved Budget adoption. As compared to the budget passed by the General Assembly, FCPS will receive a net increase of approximately $25.3 million in additional state revenue. Of the additional revenue, $18.1 million is to support a one-time $1,000 bonus for Standards of Quality funded instructional and support positions.

 

This agenda item includes recommendations for FY 2023 expenditure adjustments, including an employee bonus and funding for staffing contingencies, stadium restrooms, major maintenance, FCPS cost share for electric buses, Young Scholars extracurricular enrichment, a shortfall in the Adult and Community Education Fund, a middle school start time study, a recruitment incentive for substitute teachers, increased electricity costs, paper inflation, a superintendent strategic reserve, food services equipment, Title IX softball field upgrades, a safety and security review, turf fields, and to maintain the beginning balance for FY 2024.

Summary/Background:

 

Funding for this project will be provided from the Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief (ESSER) II Fund under the Coronavirus Response and Relief Supplemental Appropriations (CRRSA) Act, 2021, Public Law 116-260, enacted on December 27, 2020. Specifications have been prepared and the project has been advertised. An invitation for bid was issued and advertised. A bid tabulation and recommendation for award will be furnished prior to the August 25, 2022, School Board meeting.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the Ventilation System Upgrades at Quander Road School to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the acting Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.
