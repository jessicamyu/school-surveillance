National Red Ribbon Week, October 23-31, 2012

 

National Red Ribbon Week was established in memory of Enrique Camarena, a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration agent who was killed in the line of duty while investigating a major drug cartel in Mexico.  To honor his battle against illegal drugs, friends and neighbors began to wear red badges of satin and some parents began to form coalitions.  National Red Ribbon Week grew from these grassroots efforts to form a national movement against the use of illegal drugs.  Its annual campaign encourages students to take a visible stand against substance abuse and make a personal commitment to follow a drug-free lifestyle.

 

 

Native American Heritage Month - November 2012

 

Native American Heritage Month started in 1915 as American Indian Day by the Boy Scouts of America.  Over the years, individual states recognized Native American days, but it wasn’t until 1990 that President George H.W. Bush designated November as National American Indian Heritage Month.  Since 1994, a presidential proclamation has proclaimed the month to be Native American Heritage Month in honor of the significant contributions made by the first Americans to the establishment and growth of the U.S.

Fairfax County School Board Resolution

Federal Budget Sequestration

 

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County School Board believes that each student is entitled to an excellent education that meets his or her individual needs; that effective educators are essential to student success; and that a well-rounded education enables students to lead productive, fulfilling, creative and culturally rich lives; and

 

     WHEREAS, the federal Budget Control Act of 2011 includes a provision to impose $1.2 trillion in across-the-board budget cuts to almost all federal programs including education that would become effective January 2, 2013; and

 

     WHEREAS, these across-the-board budget cuts would become effective during the 2013-14 school year, with the exception that the potential reduction to the Impact Aid program (funding intended to assist school divisions that have high concentrations of federally connected students or tax-exempt Federal property) would occur in the current 2012-2013 school year; and

 

     WHEREAS, these across-the-board budget cuts, also known as sequestration, would impact education by a potential reduction in funds of 8.2 percent or more and could result in additional cuts to important educational programs already under pressure from recent state and local budget cuts; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Public Schools initial estimates are that such a flat rate cut could result in a loss of at least $6 million in federal funds in FY 2014 for programs such as Title I grants for disadvantaged students, the Individuals With Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), English Language Acquisition, Perkins Career and Technical Education funding, 21st Century Community Learning Centers, E-Rate and more; and

 

     WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Public Schools, as well as other public schools, would be impacted nationwide by an estimated $2.7 billion loss from just three programs alone – Title I grants, IDEA special education state grants and Head Start – that serve a combined 30.7 million of our most at-risk students; and

 

     WHEREAS, federal funding for K-12 programs was already reduced by more than $835 million in Fiscal Year 2011, and state and local funding for education continues to be impacted by state budget cuts; and

 

     WHEREAS, states and local governments have very limited capacity to absorb further education budget cuts without cuts to core programs, as the Fairfax County Schools have already implemented multiple rounds of reductions based on difficult state and local budget conditions; and

 

     WHEREAS, sequestration would disproportionately impact the underlying economy and local tax base in Fairfax County, compounding the negative impacts of education program funding cuts and eroding our ability to carry out our core mission to inspire and empower students to meet high academic standards, lead ethical lives, and be responsible and innovative global citizens;

 

     NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board urges Congress and the Administration to amend the Budget Control Act to mitigate the drastic cuts to education that would affect our students and communities, and to protect education as an investment critical to economic stability and American competitiveness.

Subject:                  Strategic Governance Manual Revisions

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related to:              Strategic Governance Manual

 

Key Points:

 

The Board reviewed the Strategic Governance Manual at their June 8-9 and July 20-21, 2012, retreats.  There were numerous Manual revisions proposed and discussed by Board members during the two retreats, and there was consensus to proceed with the revisions.  The order of some sections of the Manual was rearranged, and some information that had previously been part of a specific policy was incorporated into new appendices. 

 

The Governance Committee reviewed all the proposed revisions from the June and July retreats at the September 10, September 18, and October 1 committee meetings.  The Governance Committee did include some questions for further Board discussion related to three OEs (see details below).  The following highlights the major changes:

  • Section 2:  OEs—additions, edits, and rearrangement of order
    • Questions for further Board discussion are noted on OE 5 (Financial Planning), OE 7 (Asset Protection), and OE 9 (Instructional Program)
  • Section 3:  Board of Self-Governance Policies—additions, edits, and rearrangement of order
    • “Conflict of Interest” was added as a separate policy (had previously been part of “Code of Conduct”) after legal review
    • “Process for Addressing Violations”—revised after legal review
    • “Board Liaisons and Committees” were discussed at the September 10, 2012, work session; the discussion of whether or not any changes will be made will continue at a future meeting, so no changes are reflected in this version of the Manual except for the terms of members of the Family Life Education Curriculum Advisory Committee, which was changed from one to two years

At its June 11, 2012, work session, the School Board directed staff to issue a Request for Proposal (RFP) to hire a search firm to conduct the Division Superintendent Search.  The RFP was issued on July 3, 2012, and closed on August 7, 2012.  The Office of Procurement Services issued the Notice of Solicitation to 25 potential offerors and received five proposals.

Sandy Evans, Mason District
