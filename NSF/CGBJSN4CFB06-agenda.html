National Family Engagement Month

 

November is Family Engagement Month and during this month we have the opportunity to highlight and celebrate the important role of families in the education and well-being of their children. Almost four decades of research show when schools partner with families, and actively seek their engagement, students are more likely to experience improved outcomes. Family engagement is shared responsibility between the school, families and the community, so during this month, I encourage families, schools and our community to reflect upon how to promote and strengthen family-school partnerships in Fairfax County Public Schools. These strong partnerships will aid in developing a generation of successful, productive, and conscientious citizens in our county. My hope is that together we will foster a love of learning together in partnership with a focus of our students at the core.

Virginia is home to over 79,000 military-connected school age students. All our Fairfax County Public Schools proudly welcome and support the over 14,000 military connected youth and their families who are part of our school community.

Tonight, we are honoring 40 of our schools who have been awarded the Purple Star Designation for the first time or have renewed their Purple Star Designation. I would like to recognize the Fort Belvoir Garrison Commander Colonel Joseph Messina who is with us this evening.   

In 2018, the Virginia Department of Education established the Purple Star Designation to be awarded to military-friendly schools that have demonstrated a major commitment to students and families connected to our nation’s military. Criteria established for the award were designed to increase educator awareness of military connected youth and help schools structure their support to and for military connected youth and their families. In the inaugural year, 14 Fairfax County Public Schools received the Purple Star recognition.  In 2020 and 2021 the following schools either earned their first Purple Star designation or reapplied to keep their Purple Star Schools prestigious designation.

Citizens who want to address the Board at a Regular meeting, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. four (4) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. For more information, please visit the FCPS Website.

 

A total of 16 in-person, live virtual, and prerecorded video speaker slots are available per meeting. Thirteen slots will be reserved for in-person and live virtual speakers.  Three (3) slots will be reserved for students and three (3) slots will be reserved for those speakers who wish to submit a prerecorded video. The speakers list closes at 48 hours before the meeting. After the registration closes, speakers will be determined by a random lottery process.  After the random process, speakers will be notified if they have received a speaking or prerecorded video spot.   After the first 13 in-person or live virtual speakers are determined, the next ten (10) names as determined by the same random process will be chosen as the waitlist and notified that they are on the waitlist to speak.  Video submissions will not be permitted for the waitlist.  Waitlist speakers must be present at the meeting to speak in the event of a no show.  Speaker substitutions are not permitted.  A speaker may not yield their time to another individual before or during their remarks.

 

The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

Summary/Background:

 

School Board Policy 8220 was first adopted in July 1986.  It has been revised and reviewed routinely since that time.  The purpose of the policy is to establish procedures for selecting and compensating architects and engineers.  Staff reviewed the policy for effectiveness, efficiency, and alignment with guidelines, Capital Improvement Program, educational specifications, and current philosophies.  Staff drafted revisions to the policy to conform with the Virginia Public Procurement Act and Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, streamline the selection advisory committee process, and allow for more expedient use of professional services.  On October 19, 2022, the Governance Committee reviewed and recommended the proposed revisions be sent to the School Board for approval.

Subject: Revisions to Policy 2202, Eligibility for Enrollment

 

Staff Contact:  Michelle Boyd, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Meeting Category:  November 14, 2022--Regular Meeting

  

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

 

Policy 2202 establishes the eligibility requirements for student enrollment in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS). Revisions to Section III. J. 3. and Section III. L. are recommended to ensure consistency with the requirements of the Code of Virginia (§ 22.1-3;  § 22.1-4.1) and to streamline the student registration process in order to expedite enrollment of eligible students.

 

Summary of revisions to Policy 2202:

         

  • Remove current requirement for parents to provide notarized, independent supporting documentation for kinship care agreements and court ordered agreement and replace it with the Parent/Legal Guardian Attestation.

 

  • Remove the requirement for Power of Attorney and include language regarding educational decision making in the Parent/Legal Guardian Attestation.

 

  • Eliminate requirement for the coordinator of Student Registration to approve kinship care arrangements and add that the coordinator may verify information submitted by the parent on the signed Parent/Legal Guardian Attestation.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board review and approve proposed revisions to Policy 2202, Eligibility for Enrollment

The updated plan received feedback from stakeholders including the Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee and central office staff.

Subject: Issuance of School Bonds

 

 

Staff Contact: Charles W. Fanshaw, interim assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: November 14, 2022 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Related To: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Board of Supervisors plans to sell County general obligation bonds as well as school bonds in January 2023.  In connection with this undertaking, the School Board is expected to consider and vote at its scheduled, public meeting on December 1, 2022, on a resolution requesting that the Board of Supervisors include in the sale and, thus, issue school bonds that were authorized in previous referenda.  Prior to School Board action, the text of the proposed resolution and related documents (which are prepared initially by bond counsel for the County) will be reviewed and approved for the purpose of legal sufficiency by the Office of Division Counsel and will be provided to the Board. The proposed resolution will be provided prior to the decision vote on December 1, 2022.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (a) adopt the Resolution approving the 2023 sale of school bonds, and (b) authorize the Chair or Vice Chair and Clerk, as required, with the advice of legal counsel for the school division, to negotiate, execute, deliver, and administer the School Board Tax Certificate and all other documents as are deemed appropriate and advisable, and to do all other things necessary to complete the transaction contemplated by the Resolution, as detailed in the agenda item.
