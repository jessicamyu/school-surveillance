American Education Week (November 16 - 22)
      This year marks the 83rd annual observance of American Education Week; students will profit tomorrow from what is invested in education today; all citizens in our community are affected by the quality of education in Fairfax County Public Schools—whether or not they have children in school—because today’s students are tomorrow’s adult leaders, workforce, and community members, and the quality of their educational system directly affects the quality of life in our community for all citizens in the future
National Adoption Awareness Month - November 2004

The theme of National Adoption Month 2004 is "Answering the Call." This theme builds on the Collaboration to AdoptUSKids' national recruitment campaign. The purpose of National Adoption Month is to raise awareness about the 129,000 children in foster care nationwide waiting for permanent families. Recruitment efforts are highlighted throughout the National Adoption Month web site to encourage America's families to "answer the call" to ensure the safety, permanency, and well-being of our children.

      There are many ways to form a family, and over 8,000 families in Fairfax County have chosen adoption as the way to form their own family.  Fairfax County Public Schools recognizes the importance of adoption, and the School Board, Leadership Team, teachers, principals, and staff fully support Adoption Awareness Month.

Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    6.01 High School Course Offerings - Recommendation to approve changes to the list of high school course offerings for the 2005-2006 school year, as recommended by the High School Course Review Committee and as detailed in the agenda item (IS; Action 11/16/04)
    Meeting
    Nov 4, 2004 - Regular Meeting No. 7
    Category
    6. New Business - 7:55 p.m.
    Type
    Information
    Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services

    SUBJECT: High School Course Offerings

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve changes to the list of high school course offerings for the
      2005-2006 school year, as recommended by the High School Course Review Committee and as detailed in the agenda item.

    EXPLANATION:
      A. Standard and optional courses for high schools are reviewed each year, and new proposals are submitted to the High School Course Review Committee for review and recommendation to the Superintendent and School Board. This process is outlined in Regulation 3202.5, Approval of New Courses and Deletion and Consolidation of Existing Courses.
    B. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as additions to the list of standard course offerings for the 2005-2006 school year (see Attachment A for course description):
          1. Entrepreneurship
          Rationale:
            Entrepreneurship would provide students with an opportunity to learn small business ownership skills. It would be open to all students in grades 11 and 12 as a full-year course at the Marshall Academy.
          2. Network Administration II
          Rationale:
            Network Administration II is designed to continue teaching aspects of network administration focusing on management and support of network users and systems. This course would be available to students in grade 12 for two credits and would provide the necessary preparation for students to take certification exams. The course is approved by the Virginia Department of Education and would be added to the standard course offerings for all Professional Technical Studies’ academies.
    C. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as additions to the list of optional course offerings for the 2005-2006 school year (see Attachment B for course description):

          1. Advanced Oral Communication
            Rationale:
            Advanced Oral Communication provides a second level to students with a higher level of speech and debate, and would provide sequential elective option for students to meet graduation requirements.
          2. Technology Assessment and Technology Transfer
            Rationale:
            Technology Assessment and Technology Transfer provide a sequence for students in grades 10-12 who have completed the Design and Technology course in grade 9. These two courses, approved by the Virginia Department of Education, provide students with the skills to integrate current and emerging technologies with other subject areas, such as math and science.
          3. Teacher Cadet
            Rationale:
            Teacher Cadet introduces students in grades 11 and 12 to a career in teaching and education. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the job opportunities for teachers over the next 10 years should be excellent, attributable mostly to the large number of teachers expected to retire. Although employment of preschool, kindergarten, elementary, middle, and secondary school teachers is expected to increase about as fast as the average for all occupations, a large proportion will be eligible to retire by 2010, creating many vacancies, particularly at the secondary level. Intense competition for highly qualified teachers is already under way among employers in many locations, with schools luring teachers from other states and districts with bonuses and higher pay.
    D. The following courses were recommended by the High School Course Review Committee as pilots for the 2005-2006 school year (see Attachment C for course description):

          1. AP Computer Science A
            Rationale:
            The current Computer Science course includes all concepts of AP Computer Science A except for three. This would provide students with another AP course option that can be completed in one year instead of the two years required for AP Computer Science AB.
    2. Multicultural Literature
            Rationale:
            Multicultural Literature has been developed and is being recommended as a pilot.
    E. The following course was recommended for a deletion offering for the 2005-2006 school
      year (see Attachment D for course description):
            Introduction to Food Occupations
            Rationale:
            There has been no enrollment for the past two school years.
