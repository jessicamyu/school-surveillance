U. S. Constitution Day and Citizenship Day - September 17, 2011

 

Constitution Day and Citizenship Day is observed each year on September 17 to commemorate the signing of the Constitution on September 17, 1787, and “recognize all who, by coming of age or by naturalization, have become citizens.”  Recent legislation has renamed the day as Constitution Day and Citizenship Day and urges proper observance of the day. This year, the official observance day is Friday, September 16.

 

In observance of Constitution Day and Citizenship Day, FCPS social studies teachers will use a variety of curriculum resources to teach students about the importance of the Constitution and responsible citizenship.  This may relate to the Board’s Student Achievement Goal 3- Responsibility to the Community.  Throughout the school year, high school students will have an opportunity to earn a Civics Seal on their diplomas through service learning projects related to responsible citizenship.

 

 

National Hispanic Heritage Month - September 15-October 15, 2011

 

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-American community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.

 

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

 

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

Resolution
Naming the Athletic Field at
Mount Vernon High School
Melvin B. Landes Stadium
“The Jeffrey M. Todd Memorial Field”
 

Whereas, Jeffrey M. Todd was a businessman, community member, and supporter of Mount Vernon High School for many years, humbly embodying the school’s motto Esse Non Videri “To Be and Not to Seem” through his countless selfless acts of service to the school and community as a committed and proven advocate; and

 

Whereas, Mr. Todd had devoted himself to the athletic and academic programs at Mount Vernon High School; he was a mentor to many students and the honorary coach of the football team, eagerly and regularly volunteering on weekends to inventory and improve football equipment and facilities, working faithfully as the team’s statistician; he was the voice of the Mount Vernon Majors during basketball games; and he advocated and supported the Mount Vernon Men of Vision, a program designed to build leaders in the community; and

 

Whereas, he had made innumerable contributions of his time, talent, business services, and substantial monetary donations to Mount Vernon High School and the Mount Vernon community, including the Roy Rogers Bowl, which he initiated and sponsored to bring the Mount Vernon and Hayfield communities together through sportsmanship and competition; and

 

Whereas, he had displayed constant devotion to the advancement of the Mount Vernon community and special programs at Mount Vernon High School through his volunteerism and leadership in the Chamber of Commerce and other organizations, supporting programs reaching every neighborhood of the Mount Vernon community; and he had set a standard of service for all community members as he would accept no reward or accolades for his service other than more opportunities to serve; and

 

Whereas, the naming of the Mount Vernon High School athletic field at Melvin B. Landes Stadium in his honor will be an enduring remembrance of Mr. Todd’s love for life, and his relentless dedication to service for all those in the school and in the community who will use the facility for years to come;

 

Now, therefore, be it resolved that the Fairfax County School Board names the athletic field at Mount Vernon High School’s Melvin B. Landes Stadium “The Jeffrey M. Todd Memorial Field” to honor his valued and unparalleled service to Mount Vernon High School and to the surrounding community.

School Board Action Required:  Information                                  

 

Key Points:

            

Policy 1475.2, Approval Process for Research Studies and Data Collection Activities, provides guidance on  the review and approval of all research studies and data collection activities in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS).

 

The revised policy contains three significant changes:

  1. It provides guidance for the review of research or data collection by three different committees.
  2. It designates a school committee (designated by principal) to review projects conducted at a single school by community partners or FCPS staff; designates a division science committee to review student projects for Science Fairs or human subjects research; and designates the division research screening committee to review all other research or data collection.
  3. It establishes an application fee for division research requests and fees for certain types of data collection.

Subject:                    Local Plan for the Gifted 2011 - 2016         

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                                     

 

Related To:              Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

 

Key Points:

 

On May 27, 2010, the Virginia Board of Education adopted revised Regulations Governing Educational Services for Gifted Students (8 VAC 20-40-10 et. seq.).  These revisions were made in order to strengthen the regulations and to bring them into conformity with best practices in gifted education. The Regulations Governing Educational Services for Gifted Students became effective for school divisions beginning in the 2010-2011 school year.  The Regulations are available for use in planning and developing a local plan for the education of gifted students.

 

Among the new revisions is the requirement that local school boards must approve their division’s local plan for the education of gifted students.  Upon approval, the school board shall submit a copy of its plan to the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) for technical review on a schedule determined by the VDOE.

 

Fairfax County Public Schools’ Local Plan for the Gifted 2011-2016 outlines a continuum of gifted services that are offered in grades K-12.  The plan was updated and revised through the efforts of a committee that consisted of members of the Advanced Academic Advisory Committee, central office staff, elementary, middle, and high school administrators and teachers. 

 

The major changes to the plan are listed on the attached document.

 

Recommendation:
