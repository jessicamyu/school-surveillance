RESOLUTION
HONORING
JUDITH TESSIE WILSON

 

WHEREAS, Judith “Tessie” Wilson has served as a founding member of the Foundation for Fairfax County Public Schools’ board of directors, appointed first as the school board representative, and continuing as a volunteer, serving as Treasurer of that board for the past nine years; and

 

WHEREAS, Ms. Wilson was an elected member of the Fairfax County School Board for 12 years, representing the Braddock District from 2000 to 2011, serving as Vice Chairman and Budget Chairman; and

 

WHEREAS, her record of public service in the community led Ms. Wilson, in 2004, to found College Access Fairfax, where she continues to give generously of her time and knowledge to assist students in pursuing their post-secondary educational goals through free financial aid counseling; and

 

WHEREAS, Ms. Wilson owns CW Accounting Services, providing accounting and bookkeeping services to small businesses and non-profit associations, and in fact, has provided bookkeeping services to the Foundation for Fairfax County Public Schools at no charge for the past five years, and

 

WHEREAS, Ms. Wilson has been recognized and honored over the years in Fairfax County for her volunteerism, passion, and service to her community, particularly in the area of education and equal opportunity, and has been for over thirty years a steadfast supporter and true friend to Fairfax County Public Schools, its Foundation, and its students;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Judith Tessie Wilson, as she retires from the Foundation’s board of directors, for her dedicated service, her passion and commitment, and her many and varied contributions to Fairfax County Public Schools, its Foundation, and its students.

On behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, it gives me a great pleasure to honor and congratulate the South County High School girls softball team on winning the 6A state championship!

Subject:  FY 2019 Year End

 

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:     Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:  July 11, 2019 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 – Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

All of the FY19 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the nine funds. All comparisons are against the FY19 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the revenue variance and the FY20 budgeted beginning balance, flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, textbook reserve, and other commitments, the FY19 funds available total $35.0 million.

 

Revenue variances begin on page one of the agenda attachment, and FY19 actual total funds available are $1.4 million more, or 0.05 percent, of the FY19 Third Quarter Budget Review projection. State Aid for FY19 totals $470.0 million, which is $0.8 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts total $207.1 million, $1.6 million more than budgeted. Federal revenue totals $50.1 million, which is $0.7 million less than budgeted primarily as a net result of unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and re-appropriated in FY20. Other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, and miscellaneous revenue, are $1.3 million more than the third quarter estimate.

 

Expenditure variances on page two of the agenda attachment, total $33.7 million. The total expenditure variance of $33.7 million is 1.1 percent of the FY19 Third Quarter Budget total disbursements and is largely attributed to unanticipated lapse and turnover of $34.2 million from salaries and employee benefits offset by $1.5 million from other accounts. Multiyear unspent federal grant funds of $1.0 million is carried forward and re-appropriated for FY20.

 

As a result of the revenue and expenditure variances, the total funding available from FY19 totals $35.0 million. This agenda item includes recommendations for FY20 expenditure adjustments. Items identified as part of the FY20 Approved Budget include $0.7 million to support the first year of three-year plan to bring IA’s and PHTA’s salaries to 50 percent of teacher salaries on the BA lane, $0.3 million for professional development to address challenging behaviors of students and classroom management support, and $0.5 million to bring FCPS’ intranet to accessibility standards. Prior committed priorities and requirements include $3.6 million to continue FCPS’ annual commitment to allocate funding from year-end to major maintenance projects. Other funding to support strategic investments includes $0.6 million to support the equity plan for discipline policies and practices; $0.2 million for online discipline tool; $0.2 million for Trades for Tomorrow; $3.3 million for the staffing contingency; $0.4 million for modifications that are necessary to ensure two softball fields are Title IX compliant, and $0.4 million to expand the Global STEM Challenges program.

All the remaining funding available, $24.9 million, is recommended to be set aside for the FY21 budgeted beginning balance. The FY20 beginning balance is $25.5 million.

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Summary/Background {Key Points):

 

A committee was assembled for a second consecutive year to assist in the development of the calendar. The committee was composed of representatives from a variety of internal and external constituent groups which included community religious organization representatives, teachers, employees and principal association representatives, along with central office staff. The 2020 - 2021 Standard School Year Calendar was developed with a focus on instruction and learning time for students. While teaching and learning was the priority, a multitude of factors were considered and balanced during the development of the calendar.

 

A calendar survey was administered to families and employees from May 13 – 23, 2019. The survey collected feedback from participants on their overall preferences related to the student calendar and preference between Version 1 or Version 2 for the 2020-2021 calendar.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board adopt the 2020-2021 standard school year calendar.

To continue to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

All of the FY19 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the nine funds. All comparisons are against the FY19 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the revenue variance and the FY20 budgeted beginning balance, flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, textbook reserve, and other commitments, the FY19 funds available total $35.0 million.

 

Revenue variances begin on page one of the agenda attachment, and FY19 actual total funds available are $1.4 million more, or 0.05 percent, of the FY19 Third Quarter Budget Review projection. State Aid for FY19 totals $470.0 million, which is $0.8 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts total $207.1 million, $1.6 million more than budgeted. Federal revenue totals $50.1 million, which is $0.7 million less than budgeted primarily as a net result of unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and re-appropriated in FY20. Other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, and miscellaneous revenue, are $1.3 million more than the third quarter estimate.

 

Expenditure variances on page two of the agenda attachment, total $33.7 million. The total expenditure variance of $33.7 million is 1.1 percent of the FY19 Third Quarter Budget total disbursements and is largely attributed to unanticipated lapse and turnover of $34.2 million from salaries and employee benefits offset by $1.5 million from other accounts. Multiyear unspent federal grant funds of $1.0 million is carried forward and re-appropriated for FY20.

 

As a result of the revenue and expenditure variances, the total funding available from FY19 totals $35.0 million. This agenda item includes recommendations for FY20 expenditure adjustments. Items identified as part of the FY20 Approved Budget include $0.7 million to support the first year of three-year plan to bring IA’s and PHTA’s salaries to 50 percent of teacher salaries on the BA lane, $0.3 million for professional development to address challenging behaviors of students and classroom management support, and $0.5 million to bring FCPS’ intranet to accessibility standards. Prior committed priorities and requirements include $3.6 million to continue FCPS’ annual commitment to allocate funding from year-end to major maintenance projects. Other funding to support strategic investments includes $0.6 million to support the equity plan for discipline policies and practices; $0.2 million for online discipline tool; $0.2 million for Trades for Tomorrow; $3.3 million for the staffing contingency; $0.4 million for modifications that are necessary to ensure two softball fields are Title IX compliant, and $0.4 million to expand the Global STEM Challenges program.

All the remaining funding available, $24.9 million, is recommended to be set aside for the FY21 budgeted beginning balance. The FY20 beginning balance is $25.5 million.

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.
