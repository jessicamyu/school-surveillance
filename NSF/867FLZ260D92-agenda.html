Fire Prevention Week - October 7-13, 2007

Fire Prevention Week was established to commemorate the Great Chicago Fire, the tragic 1871 conflagration that killed more that 250 people, left 100,000 homeless, destroyed more than 17,400 structures, and burned more that 2,000 acres. The fire began on October 8, but continued into and did most of its damage on October 9, 1871.

"Practice Your Escape Plan!" is the theme of Fire Prevention Week 2007. It's not enough to have a home fire escape plan. To escape safely, you've got to make sure that everyone in the home has practiced the plan. According to a National Fire Prevention Association poll, the majority of Americans have a fire escape plan, but most haven't practiced it.


Head Start Awareness Month - October 2007
October has been designated as Head Start Awareness Month. Since 1965 Head Start has succeeded by promoting school readiness in children and self-sufficiency for their families through: health services, social services, nutritious meals, parent involvement and early childhood education. Since its inception, Head Start has provided services to more than eight million children and their families in every state and territory.

A Tribute to
Gary D. Chevalier

For 36 years, Gary D. Chevalier has contributed greatly to the success of Fairfax County Public Schools. He began his career as a young student of 17 at the William J. Burkholder Center’s print shop. Gary quickly advanced to the planning office and continued through all four planning level positions. He later became coordinator and, for the past seven years, has served as the director of Facilities Planning Services. His work has been invaluable in deciding future student accommodations and related capital requirements. Despite the many variables that ultimately influence student enrollment, Gary has maintained an excellent record in projecting student membership. His ability to provide solid methodologies and policy leadership in student membership forecasting and his ability to determine student and community needs for and best uses of school facilities have been extraordinary.

Gary’s excellent work ethic, his uncompromising integrity, the respect in which he is held by FCPS staff members and elected officials, and his unfailing pursuit of excellence all have made him a model for other FCPS employees to emulate.

FCPS employees—especially principals—know Gary as the numbers man and the boundaries man. At boundary meetings he always exudes a steady and calm presence. Anyone who has attended a boundary meeting can well appreciate the difficulty this represents. His thoroughness and accuracy as well as his commitment to always provide support have earned him the respect of not only members of our school system but the citizens of Fairfax County.

Gary is a true gentleman whose many accomplishments include laboring far beyond normal hours to produce an informed boundary process. He will always be known for providing accurate projections of school membership—which is the foundation for budgeting, staffing, temporary classroom allocations, capital needs, and boundary adjustments.

SUBJECT: Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board adopt the revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution.
      EXPLANATION:
          The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution on June 26, 2006. During the 2007 session of the General Assembly, 35 bills were introduced relating to procurement and/or contracts. Of this number, 14 bills passed; however, none modified a mandatory section of the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA). The following procedural changes are recommended.
              • The definition for an ‘independent contractor’ is added to make County terminology consistent with Internal Revenue Service definitions. The definition is added in correlation with the elimination of the term ‘personal services,’ thus establishing consistency.
              • The process for selection, negotiation, and approval of proposals for consultant services has been revised to establish consistency with other contracts awarded through competitive negotiations.

Other Committees (page 41), and confirm that all matters related to School Board advisory committees be governed by the current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board (SB; work session scheduled 10/15/07)
      Meeting
      Sep 27, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 4
      Category
      5. New Business
      Type
      Information

      This agenda item was added at the meeting.

      Contact: Steve Hunt, At Large Member
      SUBJECT: Strategic Governance Manual Revision

      RECOMMENDATION:
        That the Board remove from the School Board’s adopted Strategic Governance Manual, Section 4, Board Committees, the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.B. Legally Required Committees (page 37) and the “note” paragraph at the beginning of 7.C. Other Committees (page 41), and confirm that all matters related to School Board advisory committees be governed by the current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board.

      EXPLANATION:
          When the School Board adopted the Strategic Governance Manual in December 2006, the following notes were included as it related to School Board advisory committees:
               7.B.  Legally Required Committees (page 37):  NOTE:  Unless otherwise noted in the Charge, all legally-required committees are deemed to support the work of the Superintendent.  These committees will function for the remainder of the 2006-2007 school year as Board committees.  Beginning with the 2007-2008 year, the committees will interface with appropriate staff and will issue any required reports to the Board through the Superintendent.  The Superintendent will transmit the reports to the Board, along with the Superintendent’s response.  While the Board will appoint members of the committee, the Superintendent may appoint additional community members if their appointment will enhance the committee’s work.

              7.C.  Other Committees (page 41):  NOTE:  The following committees exist at the current time and are maintained for the balance of the year.  Following the conclusions of the current term, these committees will be continued at the discretion of the Board and/or the Superintendent.  Should the Board decide to continue any of the committees as Committees of the Board, the Board will carefully define their role and their purpose to assure that they meet the Board’s definition of Board Committees.

          As the Board is still deliberating about the roles and responsibilities of these advisory committees, the notes are no longer appropriate and should be removed from the Manual.  The current version of Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board, continues to govern all matters related to School Board citizen advisory committees.

      8.02 Certification of Closed Meeting Compliance
      Meeting
      Sep 27, 2007 - Regular Meeting No. 4
      Category
      8. Closed Meeting
      Type
      Information


      In order to comply with the Code of Virginia, it is necessary for the Board to certify that since the Fairfax County School Board convened a closed meeting on September 27, 2007, pursuant to an affirmative recorded vote and in accordance with the provisions of the Virginia Freedom of Information Act, and since Section 2.2.3712(D) of the Code of Virginia requires a certification by the School Board that such closed meeting was conducted in conformity with Virginia law, the Fairfax County School Board hereby certifies that, to the best of each member’s knowledge, 1) only public business matters lawfully exempted from open meeting requirements by Virginia law were discussed in the closed meeting, and 2) only such public business matters as were identified in the motion convening the closed meeting were heard, discussed, or considered. The foregoing also shall be deemed to include the certification of each member of this School Board who served on a student disciplinary committee, since the last certification, that the closed meetings of the student disciplinary committees on which the member served during that time period are lawfully exempted from open meeting requirements by §2.2-3711(A)(2) of the Code of Virginia, as stated in the annual organizational meeting of the School Board held in January, and that only student disciplinary matters were heard, discussed or considered in the closed committee meeting or meetings attended by the member for that purpose.
