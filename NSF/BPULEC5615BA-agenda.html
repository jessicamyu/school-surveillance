3.04 Resolution Honoring 2020 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship Award Recipient

Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

 
Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.
 
The School Board will recognize Beatrice Webster from George C. Marshall High School, the recipient of the 2020 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.

Fairfax County School Board

Resolution for Participation in Board Meetings by Means of Electronic Communications

 

The Fairfax County School Board may conduct public meetings in which one or more Board members is permitted to participate by means of electronic communication only in the event of: (1) a Qualifying Unavailability (as defined below), in accordance with Virginia Code § 2.2-3708.2, subject to the requirements and procedures in paragraphs 1 through 6 of this Resolution; or (2) a declaration of a state of emergency by the Governor of the Commonwealth of Virginia, in accordance with Virginia Code § 2.2-3708.2(A)(3), in which case a Qualifying Unavailability is not required.

 

As used in this Resolution, the term “Qualifying Unavailability” means: (1) a temporary or permanent disability or other medical condition that prevents the member’s physical attendance at a meeting (a “Medical Condition”); or (2) a personal matter that renders a member unable to attend a meeting in person (a “Personal Matter”).

 

The Board may conduct a public meeting in which one more Board members participates by means of electronic communication due to a Qualifying Unavailability only as follows:

 

  1. Request by Board Member. A Board member who wishes to participate in a Board meeting by means of electronic communication must request approval for such participation by delivering written notice of a Qualifying Unavailability to both the Chairman and Clerk of the Board on or before the date of the meeting. The request (the “Remote Participation Request”) must specify a Medical Condition or a Personal Matter as the basis for the request. If the reason is a Personal Matter, the Remote Participation Request must identify the nature of the Personal Matter with specificity.

 

  1. Prior Arrangements. After receiving a Remote Participation Request and before the meeting begins, the Clerk to the Board or the Clerk’s designee will make all necessary arrangements, if the request is approved, for the voice of the remote participant to be heard by all persons at the meeting.

 

  1. Consideration of Request. The Board may permit a Remote Participation Request only if a quorum of the Board is physically present at the meeting. The members who are physically present must consider and vote upon the Remote Participation Request as the first order of business after the meeting is called to order. The Remote Participation Request may be approved only by an affirmative vote of six or more members who are physically present at the meeting. The Board will disapprove any Remote Participation Request that would violate this Resolution.

 

  1. Remote Participation. If the Remote Participation Request is approved, the requesting member may thereafter participate in the meeting by means of electronic communication, provided the member can be heard by all persons who are physically present at the meeting.

 

  1. References to Remote Participation in Meeting Minutes.

 

    1. Approval by Board. If the Board approves a Remote Participation Request, the meeting minutes will identify the remote location from which the requesting member participated. The remote location itself need not be open to the public. If the approved request was based on a Medical Condition, the minutes will indicate that the requesting member participated through electronic communication means due to a temporary or permanent disability or other medical condition that prevented the member’s physical attendance. If the approved request was based on a Personal Matter, the minutes will set forth the specific nature of the personal matter cited by the requesting member in the Remote Participation Request.

 

    1. Disapproval by Board. If the Board disapproves a Remote Participation Request, the minutes will note such disapproval. If the Board disapproves the Remote Participation Request on the ground that allowing it would violate this Resolution, the minutes will set forth the basis for such disapproval with specificity.

 

  1. Further Requirements and Limitations. The conduct of a Board meeting in which a member is permitted to participate by electronic communication means is subject to the following rules:

 

    1. Quorum. A quorum of the Board must be physically assembled at the established meeting location for the entire meeting.

 

    1. Personal Matters. No member may participate remotely in more than two Board meetings during any calendar year due to Personal Matters.

 

    1. Application. This Resolution will be applied strictly and uniformly, without exception, to the entire Board and without regard to the identity of the member requesting remote participation or the matters to be considered or voted on at the meeting.

Summary/Background:

 

Staff recommends that the School Board continue to lease 13,593 square feet of office/maintenance space, which is strategically located in the Town of Herndon, from which staff from the Office of Facilities Management provides maintenance services to the 39 schools located in the northwest part of the school division. The Northwest Maintenance Support Center is the base for 49 employees who provide such services as HVAC, plumbing, electrical, mechanical, structural, generator and kitchen equipment repairs. This center is one of four decentralized maintenance facilities serving FCPS:

Location Type Facility Service Area of the County

Sideburn Center FCPS-owned Central/Southwest

Edison Center FCPS-owned Southeast

Herndon Center Leased Facility Northwest

Merrifield Center Leased Facility Northeast

This decentralized maintenance facility plan has resulted in productivity gains (less time in traffic means more time performing services in schools), faster service, and cost savings (reduced vehicle usage).

School system needs for this Northwest Maintenance Support Center are projected to remain constant. Neither FCPS nor Fairfax County facilities are available to meet this need. This maintenance facility has been under lease by FCPS since 2005.

AAPAC could research approaches to Honors coursework at the middle school level including: availability of and equitable access to Honors courses across FCPS middle schools, academic advising, student choice in accessing open enrollment to Honors coursework in self-determined areas of interest or strength, and teacher capacity to implement differentiation practices for increased rigor.

Staff Contact:  Fabio Zuluaga, Region 2 Assistant Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:    July 23, 2020 - Regular Meeting

                    

Problem Statement: 

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school. The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

 

Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2021 is $3.5 million. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $5.7 million.

  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

 

Outcome: 

 

To continue to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.

 

    

 

 

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2020-2021.

Staff Contact:  Leigh Burden, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:     Marty Smith, chief operating officer

Alice Wigington, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:  July 9, 2020 Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4 - Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

All the FY 2020 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the nine funds. All comparisons are against the FY 2020 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the revenue variance and the FY 2021 budgeted beginning balance, flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, textbook reserve, and other commitments, the FY 2020 funds available total $38.2 million.  

 

Revenue variances begin on page one of the agenda attachment. FY 2020 actual total funds available are $4.0 million less, or 0.1 percent, than the FY 2020 Third Quarter Budget Review. State Aid for FY 2020 totals $491.9 million, which is $1.8 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts total $214.7 million, $25,492 less than budgeted. Federal revenue totals $45.7 million, which is $0.8 million less than budgeted primarily due to the unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and re-appropriated in FY 2021. Other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, and miscellaneous revenue, are $1.4 million less than the third quarter estimate.

 

Expenditure variances on page two of the agenda attachment, total $42.2 million. Due to the changing fiscal landscape brought about by the COVID-19 pandemic, FCPS implemented spending guidance that deferred all non-critical expenditures including maintaining position vacancies and only hiring for positions that fulfill critical FCPS functions.

The total expenditure variance is 1.4 percent of the FY 2020 Third Quarter Budget total disbursements and is largely attributed to savings of $15.4 million from salaries and employee benefits primarily resulting from additional turnover and savings of $25.3 million primarily from utilities, fuel, and supplies as a result of the pandemic. Multiyear unspent federal grant funds of $1.6 million is carried forward and re-appropriated for FY 2021.

 

As a result of the revenue and expenditure variances, the total funding available from FY 2020 totals $38.2 million. This agenda item includes recommendations for FY 2021 expenditure adjustments including $9.7 million to support FCPS’ post COVID-19 response plan. Prior committed priorities and requirements include $3.6 million to continue FCPS’ annual commitment to allocate funding from year-end to major maintenance projects and one-time funding of $0.2 million to address a shortfall in Adult and Community Education Fund (ACE). Other funding includes $0.3 million to support the Global STEM Challenges program.

 

All the remaining funding available, $24.4 million, is recommended to be set aside for the FY 2022 budgeted beginning balance. This reflects no change from the FY 2021 beginning balance.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.
