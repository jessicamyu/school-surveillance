Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month - May 2009

Americans of Asian and Pacific ancestry have made significant contributions to the history and development of the United States in many fields of endeavor such as science, commerce, the arts, and the humanities. The Fairfax County School Board recognizes that an understanding of these contributions should be an integral part of the year-round education of all students.

The President of the United States proclaims May to be Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month and has chosen this month in recognition of the work done by Chinese immigrants on the transcontinental railroad, which was completed on May 10, 1869.

Pride in America Month - May 2009

Our country’s founders–more than 200 years ago–established a nation of unique, diverse individuals working toward the common goal of a free and open society–a nation that has accomplished much in two centuries and of which we all can be proud. Thomas Jefferson understood that we, as a people, do have a common heritage in which we can and should, as individuals, develop pride and discipline, and Jefferson envisioned what our nation’s common heritage truly is when he wrote, “All men are created equal and are endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable rights."

Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., understood and believed this when he proclaimed, “I have a dream. . .” recognizing that the principles about which our country's founders wrote were and are the cornerstone of equality and freedom and that these principles form our nation’s common heritage for its people.

The Fairfax County School Board recognizes May as Pride in America Month and everyone in Fairfax County Public Schools expresses strong support and deep gratitude to the men and women of our armed forces, and to the many Virginia-based loved ones of our military, including especially the children of military parents enrolled in Fairfax County Public Schools, for their courage, service, and sacrifice during this time.

School Nurse Day - May 6, 2009

School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.

School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health and safety, intervene with actual and potential health problems, and provide case management services and are dedicated individuals who work in cooperation with families, teachers, and school administrators.

The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognizes May 6 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

Family and Consumer Sciences Week in the Commonwealth of Virginia - April 19-25, 2009

Governor Kaine has proclaimed the week of April 19-25 as Family and Consumer Sciences Week in the Commonwealth of Virginia.

Fairfax County Public Schools employs 83 family and consumer sciences teachers who provide research-based practical knowledge regarding everyday life, including life span development, personal behavior, housing and the environment, food, nutrition, and health, apparel and textiles, and research management. The study of family and consumer sciences assists youth, adult and public decision-makers in making sound choices that result in healthy, productive, and fulfilling lives.

RESOLUTION SUPPORTING COUNTYWIDE INITIATIVE
TO REDUCE UNDERAGE DRINKING


WHEREAS, alcohol is the most commonly used illegal drug by Fairfax County students; and underage alcohol use is associated with traffic crashes and fatalities, other drug use, violence and gang involvement, rape, depression and suicide, and educational failure.

WHEREAS, the 2008 Fairfax County Youth Survey reports that 43 percent of high school seniors and almost 27 percent of tenth graders consumed alcohol in the last 30 days, and that the prevalence of binge drinking increases dramatically by grade with over one in every four seniors reporting binge drinking in the last two weeks.

WHEREAS, 65 percent of underage youth say they get alcohol from family and friends, even though it is illegal in Virginia for a person under 21 years of age to consume or possess alcohol, and it is illegal for an adult to provide alcohol to a person under 21 years of age.

WHEREAS, the Unified Prevention Coalition of Fairfax County Public Schools and its school-community coalitions have brought together concerned residents, school officials, county government agencies, and many community groups to address the underage drinking issue, and in spring 2007 launched an educational and public awareness campaign, known as “Parents Who Host, Lose the Most: Don’t be a party to teenage drinking” using a variety of media and print materials in multiple languages to educate parents and to increase awareness of and compliance with the most recent Virginia underage drinking laws.

WHEREAS, prevention efforts including parents talking to their teens and modeling appropriate behavior are critical, particularly during prom and graduation season.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board wholeheartedly supports the Unified Prevention Coalition’s Countywide Initiative to Reduce Underage Drinking Task Force and recognizes the month of May 2009 as “Parents Who Host, Lose The Most” month in Fairfax County.

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

Meeting Type: April 23, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 18

SUBJECT: Proposed 2009 School Bond Referendum
TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:

On January 22, 2009, the School Board adopted the FY 2010-2014 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the coming five-year period. Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period. Voter authorization to issue bonds is needed in November 2009 to provide construction funds for a new middle school, construction of several additions to existing schools, renovation of one high school, planning funds for the renovation of a middle school, and funding for relocation for modular building additions to meet critical capacity needs.
KEY POINTS:

In the past two years, school membership has grown by approximately 5,200 students and continued growth is projected. By 2013 approximately 50 schools will be over capacity by more than 10 percent and 31 schools will exceed capacity by more than 20 percent. The schools identified for capacity enhancements were selected based on the severity of the capacity deficit ratio which, for the schools selected, range from 1.24 to 1.45, and are limited to those schools where the capacity deficit can not be solved by other solutions such as program moves, boundary changes, planned renovations, or construction of new schools. For example, while Poe Middle School is severely overcrowded, we have proposed a boundary study that should provide relief to that school.
      Staff is confident that the schools selected will continue to need additional capacity based on the following factors: sustained high enrollment and/or growth experienced in the past several years; the continued need for temporary classrooms; the schools are located in high growth areas as defined by the County Comprehensive Plan; development in the pipeline (planned, approved and/or under construction); strong growth at the kindergarten and primary grades; and demographic characteristics. Based on an analysis of these factors, when reviewed together with the severity of the existing and projected capacity deficits, staff strongly believes that the high enrollments at these schools will continue and that permanent building capacity enhancements as identified in the attachment will be required for the long term needs of these schools.

      The proposed bond includes construction funding for capacity enhancements at eight elementary schools and one middle school to meet existing and growing enrollments. Funds are also included to support essential infrastructure, such as roof and HVAC replacements, athletic infrastructure, and upgrades to facilities to meet Americans with Disabilities (ADA) requirements.
RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 3, 2009, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $232,580,000.
BOARD MEMBER AMENDMENT:

Amend the main motion by deleting the $50 million for construction of a new south county middle school, and instead directing those funds to the following projects:

1. $10 million to construct an addition to South County Secondary School, to accommodate current and projected student enrollment.

2. $40 million to be applied toward funding the renovation of Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology. (Gibson)

      6.03 Award of Contract - Recommendation to approve the contract awards for providing IRS Section 403(b) Plan deferred compensation services for FCPS employees to Great West Retirement Services, TIAA-CREF, and VALIC effective September 1, 2009, and through the duration of the three (3) year contract as recommended by the Office of Benefit Services and endorsed by the Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) (HR; action scheduled 5/07/09)
      Meeting
      Apr 23, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 18
      Category
      6. New Business - 9:20 p.m.
      Type
      Information

      Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

      Meeting Type: April 23, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 18

      SUBJECT: Award of Contracts

      TYPE: New Business

      BACKGROUND:

      FCPS offers two deferred compensation programs, 403(b) and 457(b), that provide employees the opportunity to save for retirement and supplement the benefits provided under the Virginia Retirement System (VRS) and the Educational Employees Supplemental Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC), or the Fairfax County Employees Retirement System. These deferred compensation plans are funded completely with employee money – no employer contribution is involved.

      The contracts for the current 403(b) plan expire on June 30, 2009. On September 8, 2008, Fairfax County Public School’s Office of Procurement Services issued a Request for Proposal RFP09-995675-73, soliciting offers from qualified firms to provided 403(b) investment services. The 403(b) Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) has completed an extensive RFP process to arrive at award recommendations for new 403(b) contracts. Given the complexity of the transition to new providers and extensive counseling needed on new fund choices, staff is working with the current 403(b) vendors to obtain contract extensions until December 31, 2009. It is recommended that the contract period for the new contracts awarded as a result of this RFP begin on September 1, 2009.

      KEY POINTS:

      Previously, contracts executed under 403(b) plans were considered individual investment arrangements with no employer involvement other than the taking a payroll deduction. Over the last few years there has been increased scrutiny of these plans, which prompted the federal government to enact regulatory changes that now require the employer to provide increased oversight and compliance monitoring for 403(b) plans.

      In concert with these regulatory changes, most employers are substantially reducing the number of vendors which are available under the plan. Vendor reduction allows the employer to appropriately monitor compliance with plan limits and contractual terms, but more importantly, pools the employees’ investment dollars which can then be leveraged to secure lower investment management fees. The contract award recommendations made as a result of this RFP accomplish the above objectives for FCPS. Currently, the FCPS 403(b) plan consists of six active and two grandfathered vendors, which in total constitute thousands of individual contracts that employees have executed with plan providers.


      The Request for Proposal required that proposals be submitted based on offering ‘best in class’ fund selections and a variety of investment structures in order to meet the needs of FCPS employees. The Request for Proposal was sent to current 403(b) providers as well as those who are registered in the eVA (Virginia e-Procurement Solution). The SAC received proposals from eleven vendors/offerors, ten of whom were considered responsive. The responsive vendors/offerors were: Great West Retirement Services, Metropolitan Life Insurance Company, Variable Annuity Life Insurance Company of America (VALIC), ING Life Insurance and Annuity Company, Teachers Insurance and Annuity Association - College Retirement Equities Fund (TIAA-CREF), Lincoln Financial Group, AXA, Ameriprise, Horace Mann, and Lincoln Investment Planning. All proposals were evaluated based on the following defined criteria:
            • Company Overview
            • Organizational Structure
            • Account Management & Member Services
            • Investments, Plan Procedures and Recordkeeping
            • Regulatory Actions and Compliance
            • Information Systems and Reporting
            • Financial Stability and Insurance Coverage
            • Communications
            • Participant Education/Field Services Reps, Investment Advisory Services
            • Implementation
            • Investment Structure
            • Mutual Funds
            • Fixed Variable Annuities
            • Mutual Fund Window
            • Other Fees

      Upon completion of the final evaluations and negotiations, the Office of Benefit Services believes three of these vendors/offerors (Great West Retirement Services, TIAA-CREF, and VALIC) provide employees with the best array of investment options, competitive fees, and responsive member services, and is recommending contract award to these vendors. The Selection Advisory Committee concurs with this assessment. Of the three vendors/offerors recommended for contract award, only VALIC currently has a contract with FCPS for 403(b) plan services. Great West Retirement Services currently has a contract with FCPS for 457(b) plan services, and TIAA-CREF, a vendor nationally recognized in the education marketplace, is not currently offered. Contracts for five vendors that are currently in effect (ING Financial Advisors, Metropolitan Life Insurance Company, Lincoln Financial Group, Financial Directors, and Horace Mann) will expire effective December 31, 2009, however, new enrollments to these plans will not be permitted after August 2009.

      RECOMMENDATION:

      That the School Board approve the contract awards for providing IRS Section 403(b) Plan deferred compensation services for FCPS employees to Great West Retirement Services, TIAA-CREF and VALIC effective September 1, 2009, and through the duration of the three (3) year contract as recommended by the Office of Benefit Services and endorsed by the Selection Advisory Committee (SAC).
