RESOLUTION

HONORING

LINDA L. BURKE

 

 

 

WHEREAS, Linda L. Burke has served Fairfax County Public Schools for 42 years with excellence and dedication; throughout her career with FCPS, Ms. Burke has effectively and energetically represented and served the interest of students, teachers, schools, and diverse communities in a variety of positions, including teacher at Frost and Cooper Middle Schools, assistant principal at Kilmer Middle and Hayfield Secondary Schools, principal at Twain Middle School and Lake Braddock Secondary School; the Cluster 7 assistant superintendent and, most recently, the Associate Superintendent for Region Leadership; and

 

WHEREAS, during her tenure as principal of Lake Braddock Secondary School, Ms. Burke implemented a massive staff development plan that integrated advanced instructional technology into each classroom; introduced professional learning communities that supported rising test scores for SATs, Advanced Placement exams, and Standards of Learning tests at all levels; with a dramatic increase in participation in Advanced Placement courses by all students, accomplishments which led to multiple years of recognitions that ranked Lake Braddock among the nation’s top high schools; while principal at Lake Braddock Secondary, Ms. Burke served as the president of the High School Principals Association in 2007 and was the 2003 Principal of the Year finalist; and

 

WHEREAS, as the Cluster 7 assistant superintendent, Ms. Burke inspired excellence at 24 schools that served approximately 25,000 students; through her individual and collective efforts, Ms. Burke  supported leadership initiatives at all Cluster 7 schools, which included ongoing study and professional development, the monitoring of each school’s advances in the development of collaborative teams and assessment for learning; her work ensured that teachers and students in Cluster 7 schools increased their capacity with daily opportunities in both intervention and enrichment, resulting in higher student achievement; and

 

WHEREAS, during her tenure as Associate Superintendent for Region Leadership, Ms. Burke supported FCPS in successfully transitioning through a reorganization to a regional structure of support to schools; as the Associate Superintendent, she provided leadership and guidance to region assistant superintendents and executive principals, while sharing her systemic experiences and expertise as a member of the Superintendent’s cabinet; and

 

WHEREAS, in every leadership position in which Ms. Burke has served, she has modeled integrity, compassion, and genuineness; over the course of her FCPS career, Ms. Burke has mentored a multitude of teachers, administrators, and support staff; her ability to identify and hire talented leaders throughout the school division has positively impacted the lives of countless children, families, schools, and communities; the effect of her outstanding leadership will be evident for years to come;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Linda L. Burke upon her retirement and thanks her for her inspirational leadership, outstanding service, and unwavering dedication to the students, teachers, administrators, and communities of Fairfax County Public Schools.

Key Points: 

 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the “individual Trustee.”  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.

 

At a recent meeting of the ERFC Board of Trustees, Michael Burke was unanimously recommended for appointment as the “individual Trustee.”  Mr. Burke is a Managing Director in the Washington, D.C. Private Client Group of Stifel, a wealth management and investment firm.   If approved by the School Board, Mr. Burke will serve for his fourth year on the ERFC Board.  Mr. Burke meets all of the requirements of the position.

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)                                                                       

 

Other Staff Present: Alice Reilly, coordinator, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

                                                                       

Meeting Category:  June 30, 2016 - Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject:  Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee Members

 

School Board Action Required: Consent                      

 

Key Points:

 

New Social Studies Standards of Learning have been adopted by the Virginia Department of Education and textbook contracts expire June 2017. To address these changes, the Social Studies textbook adoption review process is scheduled to begin August 2016. The outcomes for this process are to review, recommend, and purchase social studies basal resources for the 2017-2018 school year. 

 

The Constitution of Virginia gives the Virginia Board of Education the authority to adopt instructional materials for use in the schools. Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Regulation 3004.3 Adopted Basal Instructional Materials incorporates state adoption guidelines and requirements and specifies procedures for establishing committees to review textbooks and other basal instructional materials and make recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board for local adoption. Each FCPS review committee is composed as follows:

 

• Teachers 50%

• School-based administrators 25%

• Community Members 25%

 

Central office staff members serve as nonvoting, ex-officio members to guide and assist the work of the committee.

 

• Each School Board member shall appoint one community member to the Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee.

• Participating staff members will be from the Instructional Services Department and the Department of Student Services.

 

In May, School Board members received a request to make a recommendation to appoint community members to the Social Studies basal instructional materials adoption committee.  Regional Assistant Superintendents and principal associations have also been requested to provide committee recommendations.  Social Studies teachers were selected to serve on the adoption committee via an application process.  The basal instructional materials review committee composed of community members, administrators, and teachers will meet in August to review and recommend new Social Studies instructional materials for use in grade K-12 social studies courses. These materials will be considered basal materials.

 

 

 

Basal instructional materials adoption committee meeting dates are scheduled for:

  • August 11, 18, and 25 from 4:00 to 9:00 pm
  • September 12 from 4:00 pm to 9:00 pm

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint committee members to the Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

 

Any additional names for committee members should be sent to Alice Reilly, Coordinator, K-12 Social Studies (alreilly@fcps.edu)

5.08 Suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure
Meeting
Jun 30, 2016 - Regular Meeting No. 22
Category
5. Consent Agenda 9:00 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period effective July 1, 2016, and ending
June 30, 2017; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations for the same period.

Staff Contact:  Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Other Staff Contact: Michael Draeger, director, Business Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 30, 2016 - Regular Meeting No. 22

 

Subject: Suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Key Points:                        

 

At the December 18, 2008, regular meeting, the School Board approved the suspension of Policy 4680 for the period beginning January 1, 2009, and ending June 30, 2011, due to budgetary reasons. At the February 3, 2011, regular meeting, the School Board approved continuing the suspension of the policy for the period beginning July 1, 2011, and ending

June 30, 2013. At the February 20, 2014, regular meeting, the School Board approved continuing the suspension of the policy for the period beginning March 1, 2014, to June 30, 2016.

 

The budget climate that resulted in the previous redline suspension continues to persist and if there are to be planned savings realized from reorganizations and budget reductions, it is necessary to suspend the policy again.

 

Failure to suspend the redlining policy will mean that very little, if any, funds will be saved in reorganization and in budget-related reductions that involve employees moving to shorter contract lengths or to lower-level positions.

 

Recommendation:            

 

That the School Board suspend Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period effective July 1, 2016, and ending

June 30, 2017; further direct the Superintendent to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations for the same period.

Resolution to Create a J.E.B. Stuart High School Renaming Working Group

 

 

Whereas, in 1958 the Fairfax County School Board voted to name a new high school in the Munson Hill/Seven Corners area of Fairfax County “J.E.B. Stuart High School,” in honor of a Confederate military officer.

 

Whereas, the opening of the school and the naming of the school came at a time when Fairfax County, the Commonwealth of Virginia and the nation as a whole were subject to the 1954 Supreme Court Brown vs. the Board of Education ruling requiring all public schools to desegregate and end the practice of educating white students and black students in separate schools.

 

Whereas, some elected Virginia leaders at the time advocated for refusing to comply with the Supreme Court’s ruling and started a “Massive Resistance” movement, which included the passage of state laws denying funding and threatening school closures in any school system that integrated.

 

Whereas, the first black students to attend J.E.B. Stuart High School were admitted in 1961, seven years after the Brown vs. the Board of Education ruling, two years after the school opened in 1959 and a year after the U.S. District Court ruled that Fairfax County must integrate its schools in accordance with the Brown vs. the Board of Education ruling.

 

Whereas, few records exist from the time to indicate the rationale of the Fairfax County School Board in naming the school, previously referred to as the Munson Hill High School, after J.E.B. Stuart, other than the decision to name new high schools for “some prominent American, now deceased.”

 

Whereas, the school was named for an individual prominent primarily in Virginia for his role in fighting for the Confederacy and all it stood for, including the despicable institution of slavery, and the school logo for many years was a horse and rider carrying the Confederate flag.

 

Whereas, while we cannot know what was in the hearts and minds of Fairfax County School Board members in the naming of J.E.B. Stuart High School, the current Fairfax County School Board, responding to concerns raised by some current students, alumni and community members, regards the name as inappropriate for a Fairfax County public school and not reflective of our diverse community.

 

Whereas, community engagement to date has shown a mix of views on the idea of renaming the school, with the greatest concern expressed in opposition being the potential cost to the school system and to booster organizations.

 

Therefore, be it RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board sets as a goal the renaming of J.E.B. Stuart High School to one more reflective of the community it serves.  

 

Be it further RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board hereby approves the creation of a J.E.B. Stuart Renaming Working Group to consist of students, parents, community members, alumni, and business and community leaders to be chosen by the Superintendent in consultation with the Board. The Superintendent and FCPS staff will facilitate the group’s work. The working group will explore public-private partnerships to finance a name change; ways to prevent added burdens on the school’s booster clubs, PTSA and Stuart school administration; the best method for transitioning from the old name to the new; proper record retention; and the best timing for such a change. The working group will also provide ideas for ways to honor and preserve the school’s history, traditions and past achievements and to memorialize its decades as J.E.B. Stuart High School. The working group will also solicit ideas for a new name to be considered as part of the established school naming process.  

 

Be it further resolved that the working group will report on its findings and recommendations to the School Board by June 2017.

 

I so move.

Subject:  Local Plan for the Gifted 2016 - 2021       

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                       

 

Key Points: 

 

According to Regulations Governing Educational Services for Gifted Students (8 VAC 20-40-10 et. seq.) local school boards must approve their division’s local plan for the education of gifted students. Upon approval, the school district shall submit a copy of its plan to the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) for technical review on a schedule determined by the VDOE.

 

Fairfax County Public Schools’ Local Plan for the Gifted 2016-2021 outlines a continuum of advanced academic services that are offered in grades K-12.  The plan was updated and revised through the efforts of the Advanced Academic Programs Advisory Committee, central office staff, elementary, middle, and high school administrators and teachers. 

 

The updates include a clarification of the appeals process for Level III, incorporation of the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate, and new goals in each of six major areas for the next five years.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the 2016-2021 Local Plan for the Education of the Gifted.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:
