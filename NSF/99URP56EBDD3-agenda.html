RESOLUTION

HONORING

DANIEL H. PARRIS

 

 

     WHEREAS, Daniel H. Parris has served Fairfax County Public Schools for 31 years with excellence and dedication; throughout his career with FCPS, Mr. Parris has effectively and energetically represented and served the interests of students, teachers, schools, and diverse communities in a variety of positions, including teacher and assistant principal at Holmes and Stone Middle Schools and London Towne Elementary School, director of Student Services at Stone Middle School, principal at Rocky Run Middle School, the Cluster 3 assistant superintendent and, most recently, the Interim Deputy Superintendent; and

 

     WHEREAS, during his tenure as principal of Rocky Run Middle School, Mr. Parris led the school to the highest combined math and reading achievement rate in the Commonwealth of Virginia; he was an early adopter of the practices, structures, and tenets of Professional Learning Communities and worked to establish collaborative teams to help students achieve their goals; Mr. Parris encouraged and supported the development of leadership among staff and embodied Rocky Run’s motto, Excellence in All Endeavors; and

 

     WHEREAS, as the Cluster 3 assistant superintendent, Mr. Parris mentored staff at 26 schools that served approximately 24,000 students; he promoted systemic growth of all Cluster 3 schools to improve student achievement and close achievement gaps, to develop Professional Learning Communities, and to implement best practices; he created strategic plans for data collection, intervention and improved student achievement; and

 

     WHEREAS, during his tenure as Interim Deputy Superintendent, Mr. Parris continued as the Priority School shepherd and worked diligently on a number of important initiatives with staff, the School Board, and community members including the Strategic Plan, Portrait of a Graduate, the Master Schedule, and Student Rights & Responsibilities; and 

 

     WHEREAS,  in every leadership position in which Mr. Parris has served, he challenged staff to embrace data rather than fear it, he worked to build strong system-wide relationships, he contributed significantly to the quality education of all students with his spirited leadership, caring manner, uncompromising integrity, and strong support and encouragement of administrators and teachers to continuously improve instructional programs and services to meet the needs of all students;  Mr. Parris remembered at all times that this is a human business and that people and family come first.

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Daniel H. Parris upon his retirement and thanks him for his inspirational leadership, outstanding service, and unwavering dedication to the students, teachers, administrators, and communities of Fairfax County Public Schools.

Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

 

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

 

The School Board will recognize Meghan Elizabeth Pollard from Centreville High School, the recipient of the 2014 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship

SCHOOL BOARD CHARACTER AWARD

Presented To

Kelly Margaret McConnell

Edison High School

 

 

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board Chris Giovarelli and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. The Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation has generously funded the $500 award since 2007.  The award is given to a junior or senior, who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for students in Fairfax County Public Schools in day-to-day behavior. 

 

Kelly Margaret McConnell, a senior at Edison High School, is the recipient of the 2014 School Board Character Award.

 

Kelly is an outstanding student and truly demonstrates the qualities required of the School Board Character Award. Kelly is a hardworking, dedicated, and service-oriented leader in her community and Kelly’s sense of commitment and responsibility to her obligations is noteworthy. In addition to being a Culinary Arts student and working part-time in a coffee & pastry shop, Kelly serves as Vice President of the Edison High School Chapter of the Best Buddies organization, she volunteers on a weekly basis with the Northern Virginia Therapeutic Riding program, and she participates in her church youth and bible study group. Kelly has actively participated in numerous service projects in her community, including helping with food drives at a local food pantry, preparing meals for those less fortunate in Fairfax County, volunteering at a local homeless shelter and at a summer camp for under-privileged youth, and going on a mission trip with her church youth group.

 

Kelly played on the Edison High School field hockey team for four years, and her determination and skill development led to her being recognized as a very valuable member of the field hockey program. Kelly participated in the Army JROTC program at Edison High School and developed leadership qualities and skills that she has carried over to academic, sports, and volunteer activities.

 

After losing her oldest sister to suicide when Kelly was nine years old, Kelly turned her loss and personal pain into a purpose and has organized events to promote awareness for suicide prevention. Kelly developed an inner strength and compassion that is extremely comforting and she has provided support and encouragement to others who have experienced similar tragedies.

 

Kelly hopes to pursue a career in the field of special education and certainly has the qualities and passion that will enable her to be a great educator. As one of her nominators noted, “Kelly lives to make the world a better place by making her small world better.”

2014 Virginia Index of Performance (VIP) Award
 
Fifty-two Fairfax County public schools have earned a 2014 Virginia Index of Performance (VIP) award for advanced learning and achievement from Governor Terry McAuliffe and the Virginia Board of Education. The awards recognize schools and divisions that exceed minimum state and federal accountability standards and achieve excellence goals established by the governor and the board.
 
Carson Middle School, Longfellow Middle School, and Rocky Run Middle School were among five schools statewide that received the Governor’s Award for Educational Excellence, the highest VIP award presented. This is the fifth consecutive Governor’s Award for Rocky Run. This award is a recognition of schools that have met all state and federal achievement benchmarks and achieve excellence goals for elementary reading, enrollment in Algebra I by the eighth grade, high school graduation, enrollment in college level programs and attainment of advanced diplomas, increased attainment of career and industry certifications, and participation in the Virginia Preschool Initiative. Schools and divisions can also earn bonus points for other performance measures, including the Governor’s Nutrition and Physical Activity Scorecard. 
 
Twenty-two Fairfax County public schools received the 2014 Board of Education Excellence Award, a recognition of schools and divisions that have met all state and federal achievement benchmarks and have made significant progress toward goals for increased student achievement and expanded educational opportunities. Schools receiving the Governor’s Award for Educational Excellence are: Canterbury Woods Elementary, Chesterbrook Elementary, Churchill Road Elementary, Colvin Run Elementary, Cooper Middle, Floris Elementary, Frost Middle, Greenbriar West Elementary, Haycock Elementary, Hunters Woods Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences, Keene Mill Elementary, Kilmer Middle, Langley High, Louise Archer Elementary, Madison High, Oak Hill Elementary, Oakton Elementary, Sangster Elementary, Spring Hill Elementary, Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, Thoreau Middle, and Wolftrap Elementary.
 
Twenty-seven Fairfax County public schools received the 2014 Board of Education Distinguished Achievement Award, a recognition of schools that have met all state and federal benchmarks and are making progress toward designated goals. Schools receiving the Distinguished Achievement Award include: Fairfax Villa Elementary, Flint Hill Elementary, Forestville Elementary, Franklin Middle, Franklin Sherman Elementary, Great Falls Elementary, Hunt Valley Elementary, Irving Middle, Kent Gardens Elementary, Lake Braddock Secondary, Mantua Elementary, McLean High, Mosby Woods Elementary, Oakton High, Poplar Tree Elementary, Robinson Secondary, South County High, Springfield Estates Elementary, Stratford Landing Elementary, Vienna Elementary, Wakefield Forest Elementary, West Springfield Elementary, West Springfield High, Westbriar Elementary, White Oaks Elementary, Willow Springs Elementary, and Woodson High.
 

Title I Distinguished Schools

 

Lorton Station Elementary School, a Fairfax County public school, has been named a National Title I Distinguished School by the Virginia Department of Education.  Lorton Station Elementary is one of 59 schools across the country that is being nationally recognized for exceptional student achievement in 2013, having exceeded annual measurable objectives (AMOs) on standardized testing for the current and previous accountability years.  Earlier this year, Lorton Station Elementary was one of 57 schools across Virginia—along with Belvedere Elementary School—named Title I Distinguished Schools by the Virginia Board of Education.

Staff Contact:          Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Meeting Category:  June 5, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 21

 

Subject:                  Gatehouse Administrative Center Refinancing

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision                                            

 

Related To:              Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The Board of Supervisors recently adopted a refinancing plan for the 2005 transaction by which the School Board arranged original financing of the lease/purchase and improvement of the Gatehouse Administrative Center and associated real estate (collectively, the “Gatehouse Project”).

 

In the 2005 transaction, the Fairfax County Economic Development Authority (“EDA”) issued facility revenue bonds to finance the acquisition and sold the Gatehouse Project to Fairfax County which, in turn, leased the Gatehouse Project to the School Board pursuant to the terms of the County/School Board lease agreement (the “Lease Agreement”). This Lease Agreement requires the School Board to make rental payments from its operating budget to the County in amounts that equal the payments due from the County to the EDA (for the principal and interest payable by EDA on the 2005 facility revenue bonds).

 

The County’s refinancing plan involves the EDA issuing refunding bonds, in all likelihood on or before June 26, 2014, in order to achieve debt service savings. This savings will reduce the rental payments due from the School Board to the County pursuant to the Lease Agreement, and the Lease Agreement will be amended accordingly to reflect the new, lower rental amount, following the issuance of the refunding bonds.

 

The Board of Supervisors and the EDA previously approved this refinancing on May 13, 2014, and May 20, 2014, respectively.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (i) adopt a Resolution approving the 2014 refinancing plan for the Gatehouse Project, as detailed in the agenda item; (ii) authorize the Chairman or Vice Chairman, with the advice of legal counsel for the school division, to negotiate, execute and administer a School Board Tax Certificate and all other documents as are deemed appropriate and advisable, and to do all things necessary to complete the transaction contemplated by the Resolution.

Subject:                     Student Rights and Responsibilities, Regulation 2601.28P

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:                 Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

Regulation 2601.28P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, has been reviewed and revised to better align with best practice, to reduce suspensions, and to include changes in the Code of Virginia. Major proposed revisions:

  1. Increase focus on school-based interventions
  2. Reduce number of offenses which carry mandated consequence
  3. Change “recommendation for reassignment” and “recommendation for expulsion” to “referral to the District Superintendent”
  4. Limit suspensions for violations which are not being referred to the District Superintendent to a maximum of five days
  5. Permit, but does not require suspension of up to ten days when there is a referral to the Division Superintendent
  6. Extend expedited review to first time use of marijuana which does not occur on school grounds during the school day, or during school sponsored activities
  7. Establish a cluster assistant superintendent records review process to address chronic misconduct
  8. Require records review for students in K-6 before referral to District Superintendent for chronic misconduct
  9. Add smoking devices such as electronic cigarettes and hookah pens under “tobacco products”
  10. Clarify need to have a note to leave school early
  11. Require principals to document investigation of bullying and to report all allegations of sexual harassment and discriminatory harassment to the office of Equity and Employee Relations
  12. Permit principals to take action other than referral to District Superintendent when a student self-reports and voluntarily turns in knife or other non-statutory weapon
  13. Revise process for letter of agreement from Hearings Office

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.28P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.

6. New Business

Staff Contact:          Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category:  June 5, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 21

 

Subject:                  Amendment 4 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:            Administrative Clarification

 

Key Points:                               

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending a technical amendment to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) for the purpose of deleting the reference to "GASB" in the requirement for amortization of the unfunded liability in ERFC's funding policy.  Under the new GASB Statements 67 and 68, the accounting and funding measures are disconnected and the funding policy should not reference GASB. The proposed change does not affect the benefits payable under the Plan.

 

Funding Policy - General Financial Objective and Employer Contributions:  The current Plan document contains a funding policy for the Plan which states that the objective shall be "to establish and receive contributions which will remain approximately level from generation to generation of citizens and which, when combined with other assets and investment return, will be sufficient to pay benefits when due, while providing a reasonable margin for adverse experience."  The policy further states that the Trustees and the actuary should determine reasonable assumptions regarding future experience.  The final criterion states that any unfunded accrued liabilities shall be amortized over a period not to exceed 30 years, in accordance with GASB requirements.

 

GASB has stated that "it is not within the scope of the Board's activities to set standards that establish specific methods of financing the benefits (that being a policy decision for government officials...)" Therefore, actuarial standards of practice that apply to public sector plans should govern the establishment of the amortization period for unfunded liabilities rather than GASB.  The ERFC Board of Trustees, in conjunction with its actuary, has established a remaining amortization period of 25 years in the December 31, 2013 valuation.  The Board intends to decrease the amortization period annually until the Plan is 100 percent funded.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve Amendment 4 to ERFC and ERFC 2001 for the purpose of deleting the reference to the GASB requirement for amortization of the unfunded liabilities.

Subject:                  Appointment of "individual Trustee" to the Board of Trustees of the Educational Employees' Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC)

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:             Legal Requirement

 

Key Points: 

 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the "individual Trustee."  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.

 

At a recent meeting of the ERFC Board of Trustees, Michael Burke, the Managing Director for the Wealth and Investment Management division of Barclays, was unanimously recommended for appointment as the "individual Trustee." If approved by the School Board, Mr. Burke will serve for his second year on the ERFC Board.  Mr. Burke meets all of the requirements of the position.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve the appointment of Michael Burke to the ERFC Board of Trustees for a one year term beginning July 1, 2014.
