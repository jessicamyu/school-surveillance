RESOLUTION NAMING THE OUTDOOR CLASSROOM AT
WOODBURN SCHOOL FOR THE FINE AND COMMUNICATIVE ARTS
“THE SARAH LAHR OUTDOOR CLASSROOM”


WHEREAS, Sarah Lahr has been a beloved resident and historian of Holmes Run Acres and an ardent supporter of Woodburn School since its construction in 1954; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Lahr was active in the original PTA at Woodburn School, served as a delegate to the Fairfax County Council of PTAs, and edited the Fairfax County Council of PTAs newsletter; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Lahr served on the Fairfax County School Board in 1962-63 and served as chair of the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors’ subcommittee on the mental health of children in the 1960s; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Lahr has chronicled the history of the area, the Holmes Run Acres community, Fairfax County Public Schools, and Woodburn School for more than 50 years by writing articles for the Holmes Run Acres Holmes Runner magazine and other publications; and

WHEREAS, Mrs. Lahr has established the Sarah Lahr Educational Foundation to provide funding for projects serving the area’s schools and children, including Woodburn’s outdoor classroom;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the outdoor classroom at Woodburn School for the Fine and Communicative Arts “The Sarah Lahr Outdoor Classroom” in recognition of Mrs. Lahr’s many years of dedication and service to the community, the schools, and the area’s children.

5.02 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution - Adopt the newly revised 2009 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the two conditions detailed in the agenda item (FS; presented as new business 9/24/09)
Meeting
Oct 8, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 5

Action (Consent)

Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

Other Staff Present: Anthony E. Crosby, director, Office of Procurement Services

Meeting Category: October 8, 2009 - Regular Meeting No. 5

Subject: Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

School Board Action Required: Decision

Related to: Legal Requirement

Key Points (Revised September 23, 2009)
    On June 22, 2009, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors (BOS) adopted the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, as a result of legislative changes enacted by the 2009 General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the BOS.

    The 2009 Purchasing Resolution, as adopted by the BOS, contains revisions resulting from both legislative and administrative changes, which are summarized below.

    2009 Changes to the Code of Virginia

    During the 2009 session of the General Assembly, fifteen (15) bills were approved relating to public procurement and/or governmental contracts. Three of these bills modified a mandatory section of the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA). The remaining bills affected state agencies only or were not otherwise applicable to Fairfax County or the Fairfax County School Board.
    • House Bill 2615, Code of Virginia §2.2-4303, increases the amount from $30,000 to $50,000 for single or term contracts for professional services not requiring competitive negotiation. This bill changes a mandatory section of the VPPA and, thus, requires revision of the Purchasing Resolution.
    • Senate Bill 1203, Code of Virginia §2.2-4301, requires offerors to be informed of any ranking criteria (in addition to professional competence) that will be used by the public body in evaluating professional services proposals. This bill changes a mandatory section of the VPPA and, thus, requires revision of the Purchasing Resolution.
    • Senate Bill 834, Code of Virginia §2.2-4301, provides that a contract for architectural or professional engineering services relating to construction projects negotiated by a local public body for multiple projects may be renewable for up to four (4) additional one-year terms at the option of the public body. Under current law such contracts are renewable for up to two (2) additional one-year terms. This bill changes a mandatory section of the VPPA and, thus, requires revision of the Purchasing Resolution.

    County’s Administrative Changes applicable to FCPS

    In addition to revisions based on changes to the VPPA, which became effective as of July 1, 2009, school system staff recommends two (2) administrative changes to the Purchasing Resolution:
    • Add an exception to the requirement for competitive procurement for the procurement of single or term contracts for certain goods and services that are not expected to exceed $50,000, as set forth in Fairfax County IPM 12-19, dated June 26, 2009.
    • Provide that the School Board may accept the donation of services as well as goods; approval of the procedures for accepting such donations are unchanged.

    Conditions to School Board’s Adoption of 2009 Purchasing Resolution
    School system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2009 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:
    1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:
        “The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.”
        [Note: The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority: “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts. Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]
    2. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Recommendation:
    That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2009 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the two conditions detailed in the agenda item.
