                                 Bettrys Huffman, coordinator, Title I, ISD

                                 Karin Aycox, family engagement resource teacher, Title I, ISD

 

Meeting Category:    June 4, 2013 – Work Session No. 123

 

Subject:                   Title I District Advisory Council Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                          

 

Related To:              Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points:

 

Federal and state guidelines for parent involvement under Title I, Part A of the No Child Left Behind law require participation of parents in the design and evaluation of Title I programs.  This report gives an overview of the 2012-13 Title I District Advisory Council and Executive Board’s efforts, highlights family engagement programs and activities in schools, reports on survey findings, and makes recommendations for 2013-14.

 

Title I District Advisory Council members have made the following recommendations:

  • Recommendation: Provide continued support for schools’ efforts to develop parents’ understanding and use of 21st Century digital literacy, digital fluency, and digital citizenship skills.
  • Recommendation: Identify and increase awareness of family engagement practices and strategies specifically in support of engaging fathers and other male role models.
  • Recommendation: Provide materials and supports to build school staff members’ capacity to utilize various forms of feedback to reflect upon family engagement practices implemented (e.g., surveys, focus groups, evaluations).
  • Recommendation: Increase dialogue and collaboration between FCPS and Fairfax County staff members regarding supports and initiatives offered by both systems for families and schools.
  • Recommendation: Provide professional development opportunities and resources for school staff members to support the use of a variety of methods of communication between home and school.

Subject:                    Human Relations Advisory Committee (HRAC) Annual Report

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Related to:    Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board
 

Key Points: 

 

The School Board charged the HRAC to “Examine the role of the Parent Liaison in impacting parental involvement as it relates to closing the student achievement gap.”

 

In response to the School Board’s charge, the role of the parent liaison was reviewed to define their impact on helping to reduce the minority student achievement gap. 

 

The committee took on the task to learn more about the parent liaison position through guest presentations, teacher research and other written materials. In addition, committee members interviewed principals and parent liaisons of 15 selected elementary schools.
