2021 Purple Star Schools Recognition

 

Virginia is home to over 75,000 military connected school age students, more than any other state in the nation. All our Fairfax County Public Schools proudly welcome and support the over 12,000 military connected youth and their families who are part of our school community.

 

Tonight, we are honoring 11 of our schools who have been awarded the Purple Star Designation. At this time, I would like to recognize the Fort Belvoir Garrison Commander Colonel Joshua P. SeGraves and invite him to please join me at the podium.

 

In 2018, the Virginia Department of Education established the Purple Star Designation to be awarded to military-friendly schools that have demonstrated a major commitment to students and families connected to our nation’s military. Criteria established for the award were designed to increase educator awareness of military connected youth and help schools structure their support to and for military connected youth and their families. In the inaugural year, 14 Fairfax County Public Schools received the Purple Star recognition.

 

Schools that earned this prestigious designation received a Resolution from the Virginia State Superintendent of Public Instruction and a special Purple Star recognition to display at their school.

 

The Purple Star Award serves to set each of these schools apart for the hard work of the faculty, students, parents, and community partners who stand together to ensure that our military connected students are supported academically, behaviorally, and emotionally during their transitions. We thank the FCPS school staff and students from these 11 schools earning the recognition this year for the warm welcome to their military connected students and we thank our military families for keeping our nation strong and safe.

 

The following four newly awarded FCPS schools have received the Virginia Purple Star Designation:

 

Cameron Elementary School

Fairview Elementary School

Island Creek Elementary School

Ravensworth Elementary School

 

 

The following seven schools have the unique distinction of being honored for a second time in recognition of their ongoing commitment to welcoming military students and families:

 

Clermont Elementary School

Flint Hill Elementary School

Fort Belvoir Primary

Fort Belvoir Upper Elementary

Hunt Valley Elementary School

Sangster Elementary School

Waynewood Elementary School

 

 

We would also like to recognize members of the Fairfax County Public Schools Military Connected Youth Process Action Team. This team’s charge is to identify unique academic, behavioral, and social/emotional needs of military-connected youth and their families and ensure faculty and staff are informed and prepared to meet the identified needs, partnering with stakeholders as appropriate.

 

I would like to invite the following members of the Military Connected Youth Process Action Team to come forward for a photo with our Board members, Purple Star Schools, and Garrison Commander Colonel Joshua P. SeGraves. Ft. Belvoir School Liaison Officers, Kristen Acquah and Ty Bovain, George Mason University Assistant Professor Dr. Jennifer Drake-Patrick, and Fairfax County Staff Members Dr. Renee LaHuffman-Jackson, Meredith Ayala and Joan Lee.

WHEREAS, Inclusive Schools Week is an annual event sponsored by the Inclusive Schools Network (ISN) and is held during the first full week of December; and

 

WHEREAS, this year’s theme, “Rebuilding our Inclusive Community Together" provides an important opportunity for educators, students, and parents to discuss what else needs to be done in order to ensure that our schools continue to improve their ability to successfully educate all children; and

WHEREAS, expanding inclusive communities requires a blueprint that guides our work as educators and is foundational to the continuous improvement of the division; and

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board reminds the entire FCPS community that education takes on a beautiful shape when all children are included.

Staff Contact:      Michael Molloy, Director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:        Final Action

 

School Board Action Required: Approval

 

Ignite Link:          Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options; sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2022 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) from the Board’s 2021 legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; to anticipate potential future actions from state and federal policymakers, and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board discussed recommended changes to the State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session on October 25th.  Additional edits to clarify both existing and newly proposed position language will also be made in the final document included with this agenda item.  The Board will also adopt both state and federal priority positions within the Legislative Program as part of Final Action on this agenda item.

Subject           Modification of Contract - Fairfax County Public Schools Special Education Comprehensive Review

 

Type               Action (Consent)

 

Recommended Action

That the School Board approve the recommended modification of the Special Education Comprehensive Review Contract between Fairfax County Public Schools and American Institutes for Research (AIR).
 

Staff Contact: Esther Ko, auditor general

 

Meeting Category: November 22, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To: Goal 2 – Caring Culture

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

At the October 27 Audit Committee meeting, the committee members concurred with the Special Education Comprehensive Review expansion over: (a) Stakeholder focus groups, (b) Classroom observations, (c) Extant data analysis, totaling $87,443. Audit Committee recommends, to the School Board, to move forward with the additional funding of $87,443 on the Year 2 Special Education Review. The original contract value was at $375,398.  Background: As a result of the September 21 SB work session re Year 1 Special Education Review report, AIR took the School Board suggestions and proposed modifications to the Year 2 work.

 

300 Individualized Education Program (IEP) scope expansion (from the original 300 to a total of 600 IEPs) - AIR pointed out that the margin of error will be reduced by 1.4% with this expansion (from 4.9% to 3.5%). The Audit Committee did not recommend proceeding with expanding the sample size for the IEP review due to diminishing returns to expanding the sample size and an additional cost of $43,487.

 

Instead, AIR proposed to analyze the original 300 IEPs data further, at no cost to FCPS. AIR further inspected the disability subgroup sample sizes and did not feel confident that this analysis will produce statistically significant results. However, AIR believed that this analysis will still yield important information for the School Board, and they agreed to do it at no cost to FCPS.

Attached is the full Year 2 work modification proposal.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the recommended modification of the Special Education Comprehensive Review Contract between Fairfax County Public Schools and American Institutes for Research (AIR).

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category:  November 22, 2021 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 4

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

The chiller, pumps, expansion tank and VFD (variable frequency drive) at Centre Ridge Elementary School need to be replaced.  Funding for this project will be provided from the Major Maintenance Funds.

 

The Office of Facilities Management (OFM) proposes to utilize Protec Construction, Inc. for this project; the contractor was selected and awarded per the competitively procured, HVAC and Plumbing Contract #4400007938. Protec Construction, Inc. has performed successfully on other HVAC and Plumbing projects for FCPS, including the 2021 Chiller Replacement at Hutchison Elementary School.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the Chiller Replacement at Centre Ridge Elementary School to Protec Construction, Inc. in the amount of $577,954.97, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.
