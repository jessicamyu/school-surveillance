Subject:                   Regulation 2601.29P, Student Rights and Responsibilities

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Related To:               Legal Requirement                             

 

Key Points: 

 

Regulation 2601 Student Rights and Responsibilities, has been reviewed and revised.

 

A. In V. Summer School Attendance, changed sentence from “A school principal may impose a short-term suspension and recommend a long-term suspension and/or expulsion in any situation involving prohibited conduct” to read “A school principal may impose a short-term suspension and/or make a referral to the Division Superintendent in any situation involving prohibited conduct.” In paragraph four, changed “A student who is expelled for a violation that occurred during a summer school session may also be expelled from all FCPS programs during the regular school term” to read “A student who is expelled, reassigned, or suspended for a violation that occurred during a summer school session may also be expelled, reassigned, or suspended during the regular school term.”

 

B. In chapter I.A.2., changed sentence from “An unexcused absence is one for which the parent does not have prior knowledge, consent, and/or legitimate reason” to read “An unexcused absence from school or from class is one for which the student does not have a legitimate reason in the opinion of the principal.”

 

C. In chapter I.A.3., changed “All-Day Absences” to read “Five or More Unexcused Days of Absences.”

 

D. In chapter I.I. changed “actions or inactions of the school administration” to read “actions or inactions of the school officials.”

 

E. In chapter II, changes have been made throughout this document to reflect synthetic marijuana is now considered a controlled substance.

 

F. In chapter II.A.2.a.(6), removed “unregistered.

 

G. In chapter I, footnote 4, removed “registration” and added “the use appropriate use.”

 

H. In chapter II.2.b.(2), removed “or harassment based on a person's race, color, religion, national origin, sex, disability, personal or physical attributes, or matters pertaining to sexuality, including sexual orientation, gender identity, or gender expression.”

 

I. In chapter II.A.2.b.(3), added “Discriminatory harassment (which is harassment based on a person's race, color, religion, national origin, gender, disability, personal or physical attributes, or matters pertaining to sexuality, including sexual orientation, gender identity, or gender expression).”

 

J. In chapter II.A.2.b.(4), added “Sexual harassment (which includes unwelcome sexual advances, regardless of sexual orientation; requests for sexual favors; and other inappropriate verbal, electronic, or physical conduct of a sexual nature that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive environment).”

 

K. ”In chapter II.A.2.b.(2). (3), and (4) footnote 5 change to footnote 5, 6, and 7, and changed from “The principal is responsible for investigating and documenting all reported incidents of bullying and harassment. In addition, the principal shall notify the Office of Equity and Employees Relations of any reports of such, where the misconduct is based upon one or more of the personal characteristics listed above” to read “The principal is responsible for investigating and documenting all reported incidents of bullying, discriminating harassment, and sexual harassment. All allegations and supporting information should be recorded in the Bullying and Harassment Management System (BHMS). See Regulation 4952 for procedural requirements.”

 

L. In chapter II.A.5.b.(1)(a), changed “of malicious intent” to read “that the student intended to use such weapon to threaten, intimidate, or harm another.”

 

M. In chapter II.A.5.c., added “to threaten, intimidate, or harm another.”

 

N. In chapter II.C.3.c.(3), removed “hand delivered” and added “by U.S. mail, and, if possible, by e-mail or facsimile.”.

 

O. In Definition of SR&R Terms Used Throughout Regulation, added definition for discriminatory harassment and sexual harassment.

 

P. Under “See also the current versions of” added Regulation 4952.

 

Options:  

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.29P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.

Meeting Category:    April 13, 2015 - Work Session No. 100

 

Subject:                    Project MOMENTUM: Schools on the Move (formerly Priority Schools Initiative)           

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                    

 

Related To:               Student Achievement Goal 1. Pursue Academic Excellence

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS will provide differentiated tiers of support for all schools to raise the bar and close gaps in student achievement. 

 

A small number of schools in FCPS have immediate and urgent concerns with state accountability measures, achievement gaps, and accreditation.  These schools will receive more intensive level of support.

 

Project MOMENTUM takes lessons learned from PSI schools and narrows the scope and focus to create a sustainable research-based improvement strategy; an accelerated school improvement timeline; dedicated school support team resource and differentiated staffing; and, specific expected outcomes for each school.

Key Points:

 

Update on Audit Committee Topics:

  1. Mathematics Curriculum Study

The study was presented to the School Board Audit Committee at their March 12, 2015 meeting.  The mathematics study is being presented to the School Board for discussion. The Superintendent’s staff plans to implement the recommendations as outlined in the Program Manager’s Response.

  1. Potential topics for the FY 2016 Audit Plan

The School Board Audit Committee began discussing the fiscal year 2016 audit plan at their February 25, 2015 meeting and will be requesting topics for consideration from the School Board.  The following information is being provided to assist the School Board in formulating ideas for the plan:

  1. “Audit Conducted in Other School Districts” is a chart that lists the types of audits that have been completed by other districts. 
  2. “FCPS Internal Audit Reports is a listing of audits that have been completed by the FCPS Office of Internal Audit.  The audits are listed by title and have been classified by type.  The full report for each of the audits listed may be found on the School Board Auditor website.
  3. “Potential Audits” provides a brief explanation of the types of audits that are completed and follows with potential audit topics delineated by department. 

The timeline for the FY 2016 audit plan development is:

  • February 25 past audits completed and potential audits discussed with the Audit Committee
  • March/April inquiries to School Board members and FCPS leadership regarding topics to be considered for inclusion in the 2016 audit plan
  • May 27 Audit Committee discussion of topics
  • June 24 DRAFT 2016 Audit Plan submitted to the Audit Committee
  • July 9 School Board information item (new business)
  • July 13 School Board work session
  • July 23 School Board action item (consent)
