Better Hearing and Speech Month - May

Every May, the nation's attention is focused on hearing loss during Better Hearing and Speech Month. This is the month that there is a major push to get more people screened for hearing loss. Hearing loss in young children imperils their ability to learn language. That is why it is so important to identify, as early as possible, the 2 in 100 children younger than 18 who have a hearing loss.

Better Hearing and Speech Month has been around since 1927, and continues to expand as more speech and hearing loss organizations join the Council for Better Hearing and Speech Month.

Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

SUBJECT: Annual Special Education Plan and Application for Federal Funds, 2005-2006

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board approve the 2005-2006 annual special education plan, Part B funding application and Section 619 Preschool Grant Application, and Sliver Grant application, as detailed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:
    The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 105-17, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, has completed the attached plan, for use by the Virginia Department of Education in preparing the state plan for special education.

    The funding application estimated revenue for Part B monies should yield approximately $32,429,261 to Fairfax County Public Schools. This year’s increase in funds, an estimated $2.9 million, will be used to support the increased cost of salaries and benefits for the 489.0 FTE budgeted within the grant.

    The Section 619, Preschool Grant Funds, actual entitlement is based on a count of students ages three through five taken on December 1, 2004. Funds ($842, 223) support Childfind activities, parenting and transition services, and social work services for preschool-age students.
    Sliver (Capacity Building) Grant funds in the amount of $232,409 will be used to support special education teacher preparation, parent participation, and research-based instructional staff development.

    The Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities reviewed the plan and funding application at its April 13, 2005, meeting.
