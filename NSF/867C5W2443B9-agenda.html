SUBJECT: Purchase and Sale Agreement for 8111 Gatehouse Road

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:
      On November 16, 2004, the School Board approved the consolidation of Fairfax County Public Schools administrative offices into a central campus in Merrifield. Once completed, this action will combine approximately 1,700 personnel from twelve School Board-owned properties and five leased offices into a single campus.

      Phase I of the consolidation was completed in May 2006 with the acquisition of the existing campus properties at Jefferson Park consisting of one building (Gatehouse Administration Center I) with approximately 208,000 square feet, that houses approximately 650 staff members. In addition, an adjacent 3.13-acre parcel was acquired that is currently undeveloped and used for overflow parking.

      The debt service costs for Phase I of the consolidation are fully supported through savings achieved from the expiration of leases; efficiencies, expense, and staff reductions; and the avoidance of future renovation and major maintenance costs for those owned properties that became surplus and were transferred to Fairfax County as a result of the consolidation. This transaction benefited the school system’s capital program through an increase in capital funds resulting from the transfer of surplus properties to the County. These increased funds for the capital program have been and will be used only for the construction and renovation of schools.

      The second and final phase of the consolidation can be achieved with the acquisition of an approximately 275,000 square foot facility that is located at the Jefferson Park complex. The negotiated purchase price of the facility is $52 million, plus an amount up to $5 million to reimburse the Seller for certain documented renovations made by the seller to the property. In addition to these acquisition costs, the School Board is expected to finance approximately $53.1 million to renovate and furnish the facility.

      It is anticipated that the acquisition of this property will be funded initially by Bond Anticipation Notes, and then permanently financed over a 26.5-year period by bonds issued by the Economic Development Authority. Under the anticipated financing structure, the School Board will not begin paying debt service until FY2013. The debt service and building operating costs will be fully funded from savings from lease expirations, position reductions, and operational efficiencies as delineated in the attached business model. These savings also will provide an additional $1 million per year, which can be used for instructional needs within the school system, or can be classified as overall expenditure savings.
      The Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA), upon execution, provides for a 60-day Due Diligence Period that will serve a variety of useful purposes. First of all, staff and technical specialists will have the opportunity to inspect and evaluate the physical property and all building systems. Equally important, this 60-day period will provide time for the public and other stakeholders to review and comment on the proposed transaction. Under the PSA, the School Board has the right to terminate the PSA for any reason during the 60-day Due Diligence Period and to receive a refund of the $500,000 deposit. Finally, the School Board’s obligations under the PSA are expressly contingent, among other things, upon action by the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors in connection with the financing.

KEY POINTS:
      Debt Service costs will continue to be supported through operational savings, thus there will be no direct or indirect negative impact to the capital program.

      The size of the 8111 Gatehouse Road facility will enable the School Board to elect to vacate the following administrative facilities, which may thereafter be used for other functions: Devonshire Center, Dunn Loring Center, and Lacey Center.

OPTIONS:

None recommended.

RESOLUTION

THE NEED FOR PARENTAL CONSENT IN THE PROVISION OF
EDUCATION AND RELATED SERVICES TO CHILDREN WITH SPECIAL NEEDS

WHEREAS, the Fairfax County School Board understands that the Regulations Governing Special Education Programs for Children With Disabilities in Virginia, effective March 27, 2002, are being revised to comply with the changes outlined in the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004 (IDEA), and its federal implementing regulations to consolidate and reduce the number of regulations, and for other reasons; and

WHEREAS, the Virginia Department of Education and local school boards have long been leaders in the provision of special education and related services to children with special needs pursuant to IDEA, including affording parents the right of consent throughout the special education process; and

WHEREAS, the Virginia Department of Education has afforded a public comment period and invited interested citizens, parents, school administrations, and other public officials, including the various local school boards across the Commonwealth to provide state officials with their views; the Virginia Department of Education is commended for inviting citizen participation; and

WHEREAS, the Virginia Department of Education proposes in their revised proposed regulations to remove the present parental consent requirement for partial or full termination of special education and related services and to remove the present parental consent requirement for implementing transfer Individual Education Plans (IEPs); and

WHEREAS, the Virginia Department of Education’s proposed changes would permit the termination of special education services to children all across the Commonwealth without parental consent; and

WHEREAS, the Governor of Virginia, the Honorable Timothy M. Kaine, has announced his opposition to the proposed changes to Virginia’s parental consent rules; and

WHEREAS, it is the intent of IDEA and generally accepted education practices to involve parents in the education of their children in the maximum extent feasible and to foster a partnership between parents and school officials in meeting the educational needs of children with special needs; and

THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Fairfax County School Board hereby opposes any and all changes to the present regulations that would remove the requirement for parental consent for the partial or full termination of special education and related services or the implementation of transfer IEPs, or otherwise diminish or dilute parental rights in the special education process established by IDEA, implemented by regulations of the Virginia Department of Education, and fulfilled by local school divisions.

The Clerk is directed to send a copy of this RESOLUTION to the Governor of Virginia, the Secretary of the Virginia Department of Education, the Speaker of the Virginia General Assembly, the President of the Virginia Senate, and the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors.

Staff Contact: Susan Quinn, Acting Chief Financial Officer, Department of Financial Services

SUBJECT: School Board Policy

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:
      Waiver to Policy 7325, Video Surveillance, is required to implement a closed circuit TV technology pilot in six high school serving areas and storerooms. The schools would be Annandale, Mount Vernon, and Westfield High Schools, and Lake Braddock, Robinson, and South County Secondary Schools.
KEY POINTS:
      The goal is to reduce the estimated $1.2 million loss due to student theft and mitigate food tampering. In a recent high school student survey 8.5 percent of 10,000 students admitted taking food without payment. In the past five years, Food and Nutrition Services has piloted a variety of activities to reduce losses. These included offering free meals to adults in return for lunch line supervision, limiting number of students entering service areas, and posters and public announcements designed by the Fairfax County Public Schools Student Advisory Council. Despite these efforts student theft continues to rise.

      Safety and Security have researched the closed circuit TV technology and the monitors would be placed on each serving line and snack bar. The monitors would be in plain view; students would see their image on the screen while moving through the lines. The images also would be recorded for review. Cameras would be placed in the storerooms to record presence of unauthorized personnel.

      The purchase of the equipment and installation would be coordinated with Safety and Security to ensure compliance with procurement and maintenance standards. The estimated $60,000 cost would be with Food and Nutrition Services funds.

      When cameras were installed in the school cafeterias in Brevard County, Florida and Duke University in North Carolina, the food service programs experienced an increase in revenue.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board amend Policy 7325, Video Surveillance, to permit a pilot for closed circuit TV technology and video recording at Annandale, Mount Vernon, and Westfield High Schools and Lake Braddock, Robinson, and South County Secondary Schools.

WHEREAS, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board have a history of working cooperatively together on budget and financial issues; and,

RESOLVED FURTHER, both Boards will engage in a dialogue with the public and employees to secure suggestions and comments regarding the budget opportunities and alternatives for adjustment throughout the development of the FY 2010 budget; and,

RESOLVED FURTHER, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board will review current joint and shared service functions and examine opportunities for further collaboration and consolidation for efficiencies; and

RESOLVED FURTHER, the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board are committed to sustaining our quality of life, managing the expectations of County residents and recognizing the economic realities of the foreseeable future; and

On July 14, 2008, staff presented the recommended fall 2008 Scope of Boundary Studies for the Coppermine and Laurel Hill elementary school sites. The Board concluded that 1) additional information was needed and that 2) the Board would take a formal vote on the scope for these studies at a regular Board meeting.

The Board requested that modular capacity information provided in the initial staff report be consolidated in the capacity analysis table; that more schools be added to the capacity analysis provided for schools outside staff’s recommended scope of study; and that a clearer articulation of the basis for staff’s recommendation be provided.


KEY POINTS:

The purpose of the fall studies is to establish elementary school attendance areas for these two elementary school sites that are under construction and scheduled to open in fall 2009. Staff has reviewed the surrounding elementary schools for both sites and provided an assessment of capacities, enrollments and projections.
