Meeting Category: October 19, 2009 - Work Session No. 29

Subject: 2008-2009 Accountability/Audit Operational Expectations Monitoring Report

School Board Action Required: Decision

Related To: Accountability/Audit Operational Expectations

Key Points:

The Accountability function has met and/or exceeded its operational expectations by, among other accomplishments: publishing an updated multi-year plan for evaluations; producing a Summary Exploratory Evaluation Report (SEER) for four programs; completing initial program evaluation designs for three programs; publishing an evaluation of the Foreign Language in the Elementary School (FLES) program; and producing Quality Standard Elements and Benchmarks to guide the assessment of program components. The Internal Audit function has also met and/or exceeded its operational expectations by, among other accomplishments: meeting with the Internal Audit Committee on July 23, 2009; securing the School Board’s approval of the Annual Audit Plan on July 23, 2009; presenting the Quarterly Audit Status Report to the Internal Audit Committee; and posting all completed audit reports to the Internal Audit web site and simultaneously making public announcements of the reports’ availability.

Challenges for Accountability Operational Expectation #1:

The Department of Professional Learning and Accountability is a primary source for research, instrument design and analysis, and technical assistance on Student Achievement Goals reporting, Project Management Oversight Committee projects, research screening, and other division projects. The Office of Program Evaluation is constantly faced with requests for technical assistance that far exceed the available man hours. How can the Office of Program Evaluation preserve sufficient time to conduct all evaluations in a timely and effective manner in light of competing requests?

Challenges for Audit Operational Expectations #2 and #3: Internal Audit has been charged by management and the School Board with the responsibility for continually examining and evaluating the adequacy and effectiveness of our systems of internal control and the quality of program performance. This responsibility only increases in tough economic times when the risk of impropriety increases. How can Audit assist management and the School Board to minimize this risk of impropriety while balancing the requests for assistance with the work outlined in the approved annual audit plan coupled with maintaining positive, cooperative working relationships with auditees and preserving the objectivity, integrity, and accuracy of reported audit results? How can Audit maintain its level of effectiveness when current requests for assistance far exceed the available man hours in the Audit Office?

Recommendation:

That the School Board accept the Accountability/Audit Monitoring Report and find that each indicator is in compliance.

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, chief operating officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Type: October 19, 2009 - School Board Work Session No. 32

Subject: Facilities Planning Advisory Council

School Board Action Required: Decision

Key Points:
      On January 22, 2009, the School Board established the Comprehensive Plan Development Committee (CPDC) with a charge to design a recommendation for a comprehensive planning process for Board approval. The Committee began monthly meetings in March 2009 and a two-step process was presented to the School Board in September 2009. During the meeting the CPDC presented a detailed Step 2 of the process, known as the Ad Hoc Community Engagement Committee. Step 2 was developed to respond to the timely enrollment and capacity concerns outlined by staff in the southwestern region of the county. It was purposely developed in such a manner so that it could be modified and used for future studies when deemed appropriate within an overall comprehensive planning process.

      The CPDC indicated to the Board that the creation of an “umbrella planning group” was envisioned which could provide comprehensive planning oversight and guidance for the entire school district and which could also recommend the development of Ad Hoc Committees, when needed, for the School Board’s approval. The establishment of such an umbrella planning group is envisioned as Step 1 of this process.
      To that end, the CPDC presents the attached recommendation which outlines the formation of a district-wide planning group; the function and process for comprehensive school facilities planning; criteria and expectations for appointees; and work deliverables for the Board’s consideration.

Options:
      This is a new comprehensive planning initiative and process; therefore, the CPDC seeks School Board comments and guidance on the recommendation in order to continue moving forward.

Recommendation:
      That the School Board direct staff to initiate a charter for the establishment of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council as generally outlined in Attachments A and B.

Subject: 2010 Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program

School Board Action Required: Information

Related To: Legislation

Key Points:
      The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

      The attached 2010 Fairfax County School Board Draft Legislative Program includes extensive additions, subtractions and formatting changes throughout. These changes are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate, to incorporate staff recommendations, and to update, simplify and streamline existing position language.

      A detailed list of recommended changes is included in the attached Summary of Staff Recommendations (Changes2010LPWS.doc)

      The Program was edited for clarity based on Board recommendations from the September 14 Work Session and a specific salary calculation example was added to the Education Funding – State position.
Options:

Modify as desired.

Recommendation:

That the School Board adopt the 2010 Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program as reviewed and discussed at the Legislation work session.
