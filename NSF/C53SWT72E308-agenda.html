Summary/Background:

 

The Membership Trends Report is updated annually by the Office of Facilities Planning Services. The report informs the student membership projection set and school capacity evaluations, both of which form the basis for additional capacity planning and recommendations that are part of the Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Capital Improvement Program (CIP).

 

The COVID-19 pandemic has had an impact on FCPS membership. In School Year (SY) 2021-22, FCPS saw a decline of 1,556 (1%) in the total student membership. Whether these trends are temporary or not will depend on many factors, including the future course of the pandemic and economic conditions.

 

FCPS is offering a virtual program in SY 2021-22 to provide virtual instruction to support students who have documented medical needs related to COVID-19. The FCPS Virtual Program includes students at elementary, middle, and high schools, centers/alternative programs, adult education, multi-agency, and home school and private school special education services. Students enrolled in the FCPS Virtual Program are concurrently enrolled at the school the student would be attending if the student was not enrolled in the virtual program. As the return by those students to in-person learning is anticipated, students will remain enrolled in the school while learning virtually and will be eligible for services and programs provided at the schools. For administrative purposes, the FCPS Virtual Program is assigned under Region 1.

 

Total membership includes all students who receive instruction within the school division, including general education, special education, Advanced Academic Program (AAP), FCPS Prekindergarten (PreK), preschool, special education centers, preschool resource centers, alternative school programs, alternative court programs, adult education, multi-agency, and home school and private school special education services. Home school students receiving special education services have been removed from school due to disciplinary or other reasons. Total membership is composed of the base sub-total added to student counts at centers/alternative programs and the other category. For this report, FCPS base membership (identified as “FCPS base sub-total” in the tables) includes traditional FCPS elementary, middle, and high schools, which include Bryant HS, Mountain View HS, and Thomas Jefferson HS. Centers/Alternative Programs membership includes special education centers, preschool resource centers, alternative school programs, and alternative court programs. Other membership includes adult education, multi-agency, and home school and private school special education services.

 

Highlights of membership for SY 2021-22 include:

  • Total membership decreased by 1,556 (1%) students
    • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 8,859 (5%) students, there has been a total decrease of 10,415 (6%) students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.
  • FCPS base sub-total membership decreased by 1,460 (1%) students:
    • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 8,338 (4%), there has been a total decrease of 9,798 (5%) students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.
    • In-person membership
      • ES membership decreased by 655 (1%) students.
        • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 7,729 (8%), there has been a total decrease of 8,384 (9%) ES students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.
      • MS membership decreased by 1,010 (3%) students.
        • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 217 (1%), there has been a total decrease of 1,227 (4%) MS students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.
      • HS membership decreased by 176 (0%) students.
        • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 392 (1%), there has been a total decrease of 568 (1%) HS students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.
    • Virtual Program membership (new SY 2021-22 program)
      • ES membership is 217 students.
      • MS membership is 66 students.
      • HS membership is 98 students.
  • Total Centers/Alternative Programs membership decreased by 151 (10%) students:
    • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 356 (19%), there has been a total decrease of 507 (27%) students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.
    • In-person membership decreased by 153 (10%) students.
    • Virtual Program membership is two students.
  • Total Other category membership increased by 55 (9%) students:
    • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 165 (21%), there has been a total decrease of 110 (14%) students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.  
    • In-person membership increased by 53 (9%) students.
    • Virtual Program membership is two students.

 

Traditionally, three fundamental factors affect the counts within student membership and projections from year-to-year: the birth to kindergarten ratio, net migration, and transfers. Student withdrawals over the summer have been included in the report’s appendix along with these three factors to aid in the evaluation of the change in total membership from SY 2019-20 to SY 2021-22.

 

Highlights of changes for these three factors in SY 2021-22 include:

  • The kindergarten membership increased by 1,186 students, resulting in an increase in the birth to kindergarten ratio from 75% in SY 2020-21 to 84% in SY 2021-22.
    • Combined with the SY 2020-21 decrease of 2,208 (17%), there has been a total decrease of 1,022 (8%) kindergarten students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 membership.
  • A total of 15,839 students migrated into the division and a total of 15,688 students migrated out of the division resulting in a net migration of 151 students.
    • Combined with the SY 2020-21 migration totals, there has been a net migration of -4,802 students since the pre-pandemic SY 2019-20 total.
  • A total of 11,243 students withdrew over the summer.
    • The top three withdrawal reasons provided are as follows: 3,682 (33%) to attend a public school in VA, but not FCPS, 3,601 (32%) to attend a non-VA US public school, and 936 (8%) to attend a school outside the US.
    • Withdrawals are less than the SY 2020-21 total of 12,317, but more than the last pre-pandemic total of 8,823 in SY 2019-20.
  • A total of 17,952 students transferred into a school other than their base (assigned) school and 17,332 students transferred out of their base (assigned) school, resulting in a net transfer of 620 students.
    • The top three transfer reasons provided are as follows: 4,481 (25%) to access AAP, 3,452 (19%) to enter special education programs, and 3,016 (17%) were related to reasons considered within the student transfer regulation (Regulation 2230).
    • Combined with the net transfer of 499 students in SY 2020-21, there has been a total net transfer of 1,119 students during the pandemic.

 

Summary data tables are included in the report to compare these factors in the current and prior years by school level (ES, MS, and HS), region, HS pyramid, and by school. It should be noted that data utilized each year is adjusted for accuracy at such times as actual numbers have been verified; this can result in a difference between counts from year-to-year for the same data point. Therefore, strict comparisons cannot be made between reports presented to the Fairfax County School Board in previous years.
