Subject:                     Strategic Governance Manual Revision

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related to:              Strategic Governance Manual

 

Key Points:

 

At the September 13, 2010, work session, School Board member Martina Hone recommended that the Governance committee consider a revision to the Strategic Governance Manual, Board Member Code of Conduct, to include specific language that Board members would not publicly disparage members of the general public.

 

The Governance Committee met on December 8, 2010, and agreed to recommend to the full Board that the Strategic Governance Manual be revised to include the following additional language (highlighted below) to Section 3.e. of the Board Member Code of Conduct (pg 26): 

 

       3. To build trust among members and to ensure an environment conducive to effective governance, members will:

 

              a.    focus on issues rather than personalities;

              b.    respect decisions of the full Board;

              c.    exercise honesty in all written and interpersonal interaction, never intentionally misleading or misinforming each other;

              d.   recognize the positive and negative aspects of dissent and shall refrain from public criticism of the motives, thinking, and

                    philosophy of other members but may own views clear when in disagreement with a publicly stated opinion of the Board;

              e.   not publicly disparage the superintendent, or staff, or members of the public;

              f.    make every reasonable effort to protect the integrity and promote the positive image of the system and one another;

              g.   never intentionally embarrass each other or the school system.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the proposed revision to Section 3.e., Board Member Code of Conduct, page 26, of the Strategic Governance Manual, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                        FY 2012-2016 Capital Improvement Program

 

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2012-2016 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five year enrollment projections, capacity calculations, and renovation and infrastructure requirements. Detailed background information supporting CIP recommendations are contained in the Cluster Data compact disc attached to the FY 2012-2016 Capital Improvement Program booklet and in the attachment to this agenda item.

 

The Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2012-2016 Capital Improvement Program Summary can be found in the attachment to the Capital Improvement Program booklet.

 

Discussion:

 

During FY 2007 and 2008 the CIP project costs were generally in-line with the estimates for the projects.  However, beginning FY 2009, construction costs declined rapidly.  This CIP proposes to apply the cost savings of $31 million, achieved as the result of very favorable market conditions and lower than estimated bids, towards the projects in the renovation queue.  Additional monies also are available as a result of the School Board’s decisions to close Clifton Elementary School and Pimmit Alternative High School. The application of these cost savings allows many projects to begin planning and construction sooner, ultimately resulting in the renovation start dates for many schools being moved up by 2-3 years.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2012-2016 Capital Improvement Program as described in the narrative and in the Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2012-2016 Capital Improvement Program Summary.

Staff Contact:           Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

Meeting Category:   January 10, 2011 – Work Session No. 65

Subject:                  Suspension of Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Background:

 

Policy 4680, Salary Redline Procedure, authorizes redlining of salaries for up to two years for those employees whose salaries have been reduced as the result of a change in position, if that change was made for the benefit of the school system, rather than a voluntary choice of the employee.  The policy provides that redlining may be modified by the Board if deemed necessary for budgetary reasons.

Key Points:

 

At the December 18, 2008, regular meeting, the School Board approved the suspension of the redlining policy for the period beginning January 1, 2009, and ending June 30, 2011. The redlining policy plays a valuable role in minimizing morale problems and easing the transition for employees to lower level jobs by insuring that their salary remains at a higher level.  However, this policy was envisioned to ease the transition to lower level positions during normal times.  The budget situation that resulted in the current redline suspension continues to persist and if we are to realize planned savings from reorganizations and budget reductions, the policy suspension needs to be continued.

 

Failure to suspend the redlining policy will mean that very little, if any, money is saved in reorganization and in budget-related reductions that involve people moving to lower level positions or shorter contract lengths. 

 

The suspension would not preclude the Superintendent or Assistant Superintendent for Human Resources to extend salary levels for short periods in order to settle legal disputes.

 

Options:

 

The School Board reinstate the redlining policy and make compensating budget reductions as required.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board continue the suspension of Policy 4680, Salary Redlining Procedure, as described in the agenda item, for budgetary reasons for the period of July 1, 2011, to June 30, 2013;  further direct the Superintendent to continue to suspend the redlining provisions of any implementing regulations for the same period; the School Board must approve the continued suspension of the policy and implementing regulations beyond June 30, 2013.

Staff Contact:              Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:     January 10, 2011 – Work Session No. 67

 

Subject:                      Ad Hoc Reports, Annandale Regional Planning Study Committee

 

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

On April 29, 2010, the School Board authorized the formation of an ad hoc committee for the Annandale Regional Planning study.  The mission of the ad hoc study group was to examine overcrowding at the middle and high school levels and review attendance area options for a new elementary school at the Lacey Site in the Annandale area of the county.  The specific focus areas are the current and projected overcrowding at Annandale High and Poe Middle Schools, among others, and overcrowding at the elementary level, which will be relieved with a new elementary school.

 

The committee members were appointed by PTA and PTO leadership and included parents and community members from 21 schools in the study area.  Beginning in July and working through December 2010, committee members met periodically at Annandale High School.  The schools currently included in the regional study are the following: Annandale, Edison, Falls Church, Lee, Stuart, West Springfield, and Woodson High Schools; Lake Braddock Secondary School; Frost, Glasgow, Holmes, Jackson, and Poe Middle Schools; and Annandale Terrace, Beech Tree, Belvedere, Braddock, Columbia, Sleepy Hollow, Westlawn, and Woodburn Elementary Schools.

 

Since the issues here are two-fold, two separate committees were formed 1) to focus on the high and middle schools and 2) to focus on the elementary schools.  The committees have conducted preliminary outreach to inform the affected communities about the process and issues.  Further community outreach is planned in January to get feedback on the various solutions identified by the ad hoc committee.  The attached reports contain the different options presented by both ad hoc committees.  Background information on the formation of this ad hoc study, their work and supporting staff materials may be found at http://www.fcps.edu/fts/planning/annandalestudy/index.htm

 

Discussion:

 

The middle and the high school committee confirms the current and future overcrowding at Annandale High, Poe Middle, and Frost Middle Schools.  The committee has looked at a number of options to relieve the overcrowding, including options that involve boundary and non-boundary changes.  The elementary school committee identified the overcrowding in the schools proximate to the Lacey Site.  The committee considered the different instructional models in these schools (K-5 and K-6).  The reports include various options that deal with these issues and highlight strengths and opportunities for each option. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the reports and request that the ad hoc committees conduct a series of community meetings in January to share their reports and to gain feedback regarding the various options.  The School Board also requests that the committee share the results of these meetings with the Board as soon as possible.

 

That based upon the work of the ad hoc committee, staff identify a regional scope for a boundary study to begin no later than April 2011, to relieve overcrowding at the middle and high school levels and to establish an attendance area for a new elementary school at the Lacey Site and that such study be complete before September 2011, so that implementation can occur in September 2012.
