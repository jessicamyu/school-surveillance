2.04 Recognition of 2018 School Board Character Award Recipient

The School Board Character Award was initiated by former Student Representative to the School Board, Chris Giovarelli, and established by the Student Advisory Council in 2001. The Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation has generously funded the $500 award since 2007. The award is given to a junior or senior who demonstrates a continuous record of high morals, strong integrity, and good character, and who is recognized as a role model for students in Fairfax County Public Schools in day-to-day behavior.

 

The recipient of the 2018 School Board Character award is Rabia Saad, a senior at Hayfield Secondary School.

 

Rabia is a tremendous source of hope and inspiration for the Hayfield community. She shares heartfelt compassion and empathy with her peers, serving as a listening ear, guiding them to available resources and counseling, and working to guarantee a safe place for students to come to. Her passion is making people feel acknowledged and understood. According to one teacher, “To put it simply, she cares. She is constantly there for anyone who needs emotional help…Her warm persona brightens the day for everyone she comes into contact with in our school.”

 

With great dedication and a caring heart, volunteer service is very important to Rabia. Since her freshman year, she has volunteered on weekends with GIVE Tutoring, helping children of all races, religions, and abilities on a variety of subjects and learning the impact of friendship in shaping future leaders. Rabia is very involved and engaged in Hayfield activities, encouraging STEM academics with the Technology Student Association, supporting the arts and creativity as a member of the National Art Honor Society, and advocating for her peers through the Student Government Association. 

 

Rabia is dedicated to promoting equity and acceptance. With the Muslim Youth Club, she has helped educate students and advance cultural awareness for Islam in today’s society. She is trustworthy and responsible, serving as emotional support for her peers and helping them overcome challenges. As one of her nominators said, “She inspires everyone around her to become a better person and to give back to the world.” Her strength and influence reveal character traits all of our students can be inspired by. Rabia has earned and is truly deserving of the School Board Character Award.

2.05 Resolution Honoring 2018 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship Award Recipient

Laura Ashley Piper served as student representative to the Fairfax County School Board from 1986 to 1987. This outstanding student, talented athlete, and exceptional leader graduated from Robinson Secondary School and the Air Force Academy and was commissioned a Second Lieutenant in the Air Force. She was killed in action over northern Iraq in April 1994. To honor Laura Ashley Piper’s strong commitment to student participation and to recognize her high standards of achievement and involvement and her willingness to continually accept responsibility throughout her lifetime, the School Board of Fairfax County has established the annual $1,000 Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship.  In 2007, Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation began its endowment of the Laura Ashley Piper Scholarship, providing $1,000 annually.  We appreciate their continued generosity.

Each year, every high school may nominate one senior student who exemplifies the “attributes of personal excellence and commitment to student government” of Lt. Piper. A committee from the Office of School Counseling Services, Department of Instructional Services, considers the nominees. There is an engraved plaque in the School Board Office with each winner’s name.

2.07 Recognition of School Resource Officers
Meeting
Jun 14, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
2. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.

Fairfax County Public Schools and the police departments of Fairfax County, Fairfax City and the Town of Herndon have enjoyed a mutually beneficial relationship for more than two decades. One of the hallmarks of this relationship is the School Resource Officer (SRO) program. SROs partner with school administrators to ensure safe, productive, and inviting school environments for students and staff by deterring crime, maintaining orderly schools, and preventing truancy.

In addition, SROs provide relevant safety education programs to FCPS students. Many SROs also volunteer as student mentors through the Mentorworks Program, serving as trusted adults that students can depend on to assist them in making healthy and safe choices. Furthermore, SROs play a critical role in ensuring the success of the Alternative Accountability Program, a collaborative and community-oriented response to juvenile offenses that utilizes the principles of restorative justice to hold students accountable for their actions, while at the same time preventing formal Court involvement and satisfying the needs of victims.

 

Simply put, in order for students to be successful, they require safe and productive learning environments. Our SROs help provide these positive environments and also establish valued and trusted relationships with students and staff.

On behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, it is an honor for me to recognize the students from Fairfax County Public Schools who won First Place Awards in 2018 Technology Student Association (TSA) Technosphere Competition!

 

The Technology Student Association (TSA) is open to students who have currently enrolled in technology education courses or have completed courses in the past. TSA’s membership includes over 150,000 middle and high school students in 2,000 schools spanning 48 states. TSA is supported by educators, parents and business leaders who believe in the need for a technologically literate society. Members learn through exciting competitive events, leadership opportunities and workshops.

 

Every spring, the Virginia Association of the Technology Student Association hosts its annual leadership conference, Technosphere. The Technosphere Conference is the highlight of the Virginia TSA year and provides an incredible opportunity for members to compete, lead, and learn through competitive events for middle and high school students, leadership workshops and other special events.

 

During Technosphere, members gain experience and insight through networking with other students, teachers, parents, and members of the business community. These interactions throughout Technosphere strengthen the goals of innovation and collaboration through science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education. Members exercise their technical knowledge, leadership skills, and professionalism as they engage in competitive events, projects, and seminars.  

 

This year, Fairfax County Public School students earned 20 first place awards at the TSA Technosphere competition! On behalf of the Fairfax County School Board, I would like to recognize the students and their teachers from Twain Middle School, Carson Middle School, Kilmer Middle School, West Potomac High School, Thomas Jefferson High School, Hayfield Secondary School, Chantilly High School, Oakton High School, and West Springfield High School for their outstanding work.

 

This achievement is a testament to your talent, dedication, and hard work. We wish you continued success and all the best in your future endeavors!

Problem Statement:  Regulation 2601 needs to be updated to reflect legislative changes, to clarify items, and to address concerns raised by principals and the community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

  1. Chapter I.A.1., removed sentence defining justifiable absences.

 

  1. Chapter I.A.2., added definition of chronic absence.

 

  1. Chapter I.A.3., changed definition of unexcused absences.

 

    IV. Chapter I.A.4., updated consequences of five or more unexcused days of absences.

 

  1. Chapter I.D., added “Social Media” parental guidance. 

 

    VI. Chapter I.J.1., removed “director, Office of Equity and Employee Relations,” and replaced with “Family and Student Ombudsman.”

 

  1. Chapter I.K.2., updated to refer discrimination issues to the Family and Student Ombudsman. Added Ombudsman phone number and e-mail address.

 

  1. Chapter II.A., added footnote stating “Students who are in kindergarten through third grade ordinarily are not subject to suspensions from school of more than three days.  Virginia law provides for exceptions where such students engage in dangerous misconduct. (See chapter II.C.3 of this regulation.)”

 

    IX. Chapter II.A.2.a.12., added "Possession or use of a smoking or vaping device (e.g., a vape, a JUUL, or a like device). To the extent the foregoing contains alcohol, nicotine, or an illegal drug, specific consequences are listed below in this regulation."

 

  1. Chapter II.A.3.a., clarified the term smoking device.

 

     XI. Chapter II.A.3.b.1.a., II.A.3.d.1.a., II.A.3.d.2.a.ii.b., II.A.5.b.1.a., added clarification of 'school-sponsored activities' to include "such as, but not limited to, homecoming, prom, and graduation ceremonies"

 

  1. Chapter II.C.3., added section regarding Suspension of Students in Grades K-3.

 

  1. Chapter II.C.4.d., added parenthetical " without either a referral to the Division Superintendent or a recommendation for reassignment.”

Summary/Background:

 

The Office of Professional Learning and Family Engagement recommends that the School Board award a contract to the University of Virginia (UVA) for provision of a customized education program for FCPS teachers and counselors who aspire to be future school administrators.

 

This educational program is known as the Accelerated Certification Cohort (ACC), which is designed to provide FCPS with a highly qualified pipeline of future school administrators (including assistant principals), while also offering an incentivized path for FCPS teachers who wish to become school administrators. Thus, the ACC also serves as a teacher recruitment tool for FCPS.

 

ACC is a one year program which is approved by the Virginia Department of Education for an alternative certificate in administration. At the conclusion of the program, participants receive a Pre K-12 Administration and Supervision endorsement on their Virginia teacher and educator’s license. Approximately, 14-20 FCPS teachers enroll in the ACC each year. The 2018-19 school year will be the 14th year of operation for the ACC program in FCPS.

 

During the course of the program, ACC participants are mentored by experienced school administrators, and develop a project focused on improving student achievement in FCPS schools. All coursework is instructed by UVA faculty. The FCPS Leadership Development Team and UVA integrate a united vision to support alignment and consistency to the program in FCPS, and collaborate to incorporate the foci of the School Board’s Strategic Plan. ACC participants also serve as administrative interns in the FCPS extended school year program.

 

Estimated contract value over the five (5) year term is not expected to exceed $325,000, or $65,000 per academic year. ACC participants are invoiced for, and obligated to pay, 50 percent of the established tuition rate, thereby reducing the amount to be paid by FCPS to UVA in connection with this contract.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve and award a contract to the University of Virginia for the provision of the Accelerated Certification Cohort educational program, effective May 1, 2018 through June 30, 2023, and authorize the Division Superintendent or Deputy Superintendent to execute and administer such contract on behalf of the School Board.
