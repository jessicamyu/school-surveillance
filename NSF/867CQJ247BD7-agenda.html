The Board will discuss charges for each advisory committee for 2008-2009. Here is a list of the charges given to the committees last year.

Adult and Community Education Advisory Committee
Analyze how Adult and Community Education programs and services align with and support the student achievement goals.

Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities
Develop recommendations on how the school division can build on current efforts and develop new ones that foster reading-skill development for elementary and secondary students with disabilities.

Advisory Committee for Gifted and Talented
Provide recommendations on how the school system can support and meet the unique needs of gifted students in achieving the School Board’s three student achievement goals.

Career and Technical Education Advisory Committee
Consider current and future global trends and implications for High Schools of the Future initiatives and related programs within the consortia.

District Advisory Council for the Fairfax County Compensatory Education Title I Program
Determine ways to strengthen family involvement in schools and focus on how schools can best support families in developing children's literacy skills.

FECEP/Headstart
Find and recommend ways to increase parental involvement.

Human Relations Advisory Committee
Determine how member organizations and other community groups can support students achieving Student Achievement Goal 2, Essential Life Skills.

Minority Student Achievement Oversight Committee
Determine how to establish a partnership between minority families and schools so that parents become informed advocates for their children to be successful in school.

School Health Advisory Committee

Determine the elements of a comprehensive school health policy, compare those elements to the current Wellness Policy and other FCPS health-related written policies, and determine if a comprehensive school health policy should be established for FCPS.

Contact: Kathy L. Smith, Chairman, Governance Committee, and Sully District Representative

SUBJECT: School Board Policy

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      On April 4, May 9, and May 19, 2008, the Governance Committee discussed possible revisions to Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board. Revisions include:
      1. The timeframe for appointing community members to the committees was changed to June to reflect current practice.
      2. The time period for providing each advisory committee with their annual charge was specified to be July, and those charges will be developed in consultation with the Superintendent.
      3. Language was added to require a minority report coordinator, in the event there is a minority report.

      At the May 12, 2008, work session, the Governance Committee discussed the recommended revisions with the School Board, and there was consensus to add the revised policy to the June 5 Board meeting agenda (new business), with action scheduled for June 19.

That the School Board adopt Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board.

Jun 9, 2008 - Work Session No. 116-121
Category
118. Human Resources - 1:30 p.m.
Type

Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

SUBJECT: ERFC Annual Report to the School Board

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:

ERFC will update the School Board members on the status of ERFC’s membership, financial status, investment results, and expenses. Staff will also be prepared to answer any questions related to Proposed Amendment 14 to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan, and Proposed Amendment 1 to ERFC’s Benefit Restoration Plan (BRP).
    KEY POINTS:
        • In FY 2008, active membership in the ERFC 2001 Plan surpassed active membership in the ERFC Legacy Plan for the first time since the newer, streamlined benefit plan structure was implemented on July 1, 2001.
        • During CY 2007, ERFC paid benefits totaling more than $130 million. 71 percent of ERFC benefits, or $92 million was paid to Virginia residents.
        • The average monthly benefit paid to current Legacy Plan members increased slightly from $1281 to $1303.
        • During CY 2007, ERFC expanded the System’s target asset allocation to include Global Asset Allocation and Absolute Return strategies.
        • As of December 31, 2007, ERFC’s 1-year, 3-year and 5-year returns all exceeded the Plan portfolio’s custom benchmark. The 10-year return lagged the custom benchmark by 0.2 percent.
        • ERFC’s Funding Ratio increased from 86 percent to 88 percent during CY 2007.
        • Beginning in 2010, FCPS Employer Contribution requirements will decrease from 3.37 percent of salary to 3.20 percent of salary.
        • ERFC’s expense ratio decreased from 0.59 percent to 0.53 percent in CY 2007. However, the System does anticipate that this ratio will increase to 0.57 percent in FY 2008, due primarily to the functional costs associated with ERFC’s move into alternative investment strategies, and “one time” expenses related to implementation of the new integrated payroll/administration system.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    Acceptance of ERFC’s Annual Report to the School Board as presented

    119.05 Discussion - Policy 2245, Home Instruction
    Meeting
    Jun 9, 2008 - Work Session No. 116-121
    Category
    119. Policy - 2 p.m.
    Type
    Information
    Staff Contact: Alice Farling, assistant superintendent, Special Services

    Meeting Type: June 9, 2008 - Work Session 119

    SUBJECT: School Board Policy

    TYPE: Discussion

    RELATED TO: Student Achievement Goals


    BACKGROUND:
        The two-year pilot program that introduced partial enrollment of home-instructed students in FCPS high schools ended with the 2006-07 school year. During the 2005-06 school year, five home-instructed students enrolled in classes that included algebra, chemistry, AP English literature, and Spanish. During the 2006-07 school year, nine students enrolled in a variety of science, math, foreign language, English, and social studies classes. For the current school year, eleven home-schooled students are enrolled in classes including AP world history, Latin, French, algebra, biology, Spanish, and English. No high school has ever had more than three home-instructed students during the same year. It is proposed that language in School Board Policy 2245 that refers to a pilot program be removed and that the partial enrollment program be continued in its current form.


    KEY POINTS:
        • The number of students involved continues to be small and does not constitute an undue burden on FCPS staff or resources.
        • Four of the nine students partially enrolled last year are partially enrolled again this year, and another became a full-time FCPS student this year.


    RECOMMENDATION:

    That the School Board approve the changes to Policy 2245

        120.02 South County Secondary School Options
        Meeting
        Jun 9, 2008 - Work Session No. 116-121
        Category
        120. Facilities - 3 p.m.
        Type
        Information

        Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities & Transportation Services

        Meeting Type: June 9, 2008 - Work Session No.

        SUBJECT: South County Secondary School

        TYPE: Discussion [Board action scheduled June 19, 2008]

        BACKGROUND:

            The South County Secondary School (SCSS) is operating on a 9-period day as a means to manage its overcrowding. While designed to be a high school with a capacity of 2,500 students, it has operated since it opened in 2005 as a secondary school. Its current enrollment is approximately 2,960 students. It is necessary to provide relief to the school either by enhancing capacity or by reducing enrollment, or some combination thereof.

        KEY POINTS:
                · Comparison of projected and actual student yield in the South County Secondary School attendance area.
                · Impacts of anticipated residential growth, by-right development and rezoning.
                · Impacts of BRAC (Base Realignment and Closure).
                · Projected enrollments for South County Middle School’s feeder schools.

        OPTIONS: 
            Solution options that were considered:
                  1.              Boundary changes.

                  2.              Build a new middle school to serve this area.

                  3.              Build an addition to the existing school.

                  4.              Relocate programs.

            Based upon the fact that we do not project significant increases in enrollment at SCSS, we recommend an addition to the existing school.  This option is less expensive than building a new middle school and will provide sufficient capacity to meet the enrollment needs of the school in the near term.  Assuming cooperation from the county and the absence of unusual circumstances, we believe the addition can be built in time for the opening of the 2010-2011 school year. 
         RECOMMENDATION:

          That the School Board approve an addition to South County Secondary School to meet enrollment needs of the school in the near term and as a less expensive option to building a new middle school.
