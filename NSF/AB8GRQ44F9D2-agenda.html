Subject:  Membership Analysis and Trends

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

 

Key Points:

 

Student enrollment in Fairfax County Public Schools showed moderate growth this year, in spite of the slight growth experienced last year. As has previously been communicated, various factors alter year to year membership changes and student enrollment projections. The two most important factors that influence the overall FCPS membership changes and student enrollment projections are the birth to kindergarten ratio and net migration.  A third factor that alters membership and the student enrollment projections for individual schools from year to year is student transfers due to program access needs and the student transfer regulation.   

 

Staff implemented and experienced success using a new student enrollment and demographics projection methodology in October 2015 for the five-year projections and in March 2016 for the September 30, 2016 projections. The methodology is based on best practices and includes the use of Geographic Information System (GIS) technology. GIS technology pulls Student Information System (SIS) data by student ID number. It allows for improved sorting, tracking, and mapping of students based on their residence and actual school of attendance. This precision in data analysis and the ability to account for each student in the student enrollment projection process resulted in better projections performance predicting September 30, 2016 membership, as compared to the performance predicting the September 30, 2015 membership. Staff is continuing to adjust and refine the projection methodology as new data sets are being considered and analyzed for possible future incorporation into the methodology. 

 

For today’s work session, the Facilities and Transportation Services staff have prepared detailed information and charts that show the current conditions as well as comparison information between this year’s and last year’s membership. Information is also presented about the performance of the new student enrollment projections methodology. The latest five-year projections, projected capacity balances, and projected capacity balance maps are provided for the Board’s consideration. These projected conditions will lead to the FY 2018-22 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) recommendations which will be proposed on December 15, 2016. A CIP public hearing is scheduled for January 10, 2017. A School Board work session is scheduled for January 23, 2017. The action to vote on the FY 2018-22 Capital Improvement Program is scheduled for January 26, 2017.

Subject:  Compensation Study

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS has been conducting a compensation study to identify and implement a compensation structure that attracts and retains qualified employees through an optimal mix of salary and benefits. The study is a key piece of the Strategic Plan’s goal 3 – Premier Workforce – which aims to recruit and retain high quality employees who are valued, respected, and rewarded for their efforts, and ensure that FCPS maintains a competitive compensation package. 

 

In March 2016, various options for teacher pay progression, which took into consideration findings from the market study of FCPS’ competitors as well as focus groups of its employees, were previously presented to the Board. In July 2016, teacher and school-based administrator pay scale recommendations were presented to the School Board. The recommended teacher pay scale places more emphasis on pay progression at earlier steps in the scale, allows teachers to progress through the scale more quickly and makes the Division’s scale also included a focus on market competitive salary ranges and faster pay progression. A new separate scale for school-based administrators has been developed. The scale emphasizes expedited pay progression toward the beginning of the scale, allows for quicker progression through the scale and the scale is more market competitive.

 

A recommended scale for classroom instructional support positions, which include public health training assistants, public health attendants, and instructional assistants, will be presented at the December 5 meeting. The Unified Scale conceptual framework will be discussed, and the presentation will include an introduction of a potential differentiated supplemental pay model for teachers. It is anticipated that a recommended Unified Scale will be presented in spring 2017.

Subject:  FY 2017 Midyear Budget Review      

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, funds available are decreasing by $5.1 million. The FY 2017 beginning balance is decreasing by $1.3 million due to final year-end adjustments made after the FY 2016 Final Budget Review. The adjustments include an increase of $0.7 million in sales tax revenue based on actual receipts and expenditure increases totaling $2.1 million. In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2017 revenue adjustments include a decrease in state aid of $4.4 million due to the reduction in the compensation supplement for pay increases, including the compensation supplement component for the Academic Year Governor’s School as a result of the state’s significant revenue shortfall for FY 2016; an increase of $0.5 million for City of Fairfax tuition based on actual expenditures for FY 2016; and a net increase of $0.1 million in federal grant revenue. 

 

Recommended FY 2017 expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $9.9 million resulting primarily from a reduction of $6.8 million in compensation accounts due to higher turnover experienced beyond the initial projection; a decrease of $2.5 million resulting from savings in health plan rates due to the new pharmacy benefit contract; a decrease of $1.3 million in outstanding encumbered obligations due to expenditures reported after the FY 2016 Final Budget Review, offset by an increase of $0.5 million for early hire teacher incentives and $0.1 million in federal grant expenditures.

 

Expenditure adjustments, combined with revenue adjustments, result in available funds totaling $4.8 million recommended to be set aside to increase the FY 2018 beginning balance from $22.2 million to $27.0 million.

Subject:  FY 2018 Budget  

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion                                                                                 

                          

Key Points:

 

This will continue the budget planning discussion that began when the FY 2018 fiscal forecast for the School Operating Fund was presented to the School Board on September 19, 2016. Since that time, two joint meetings of the School Board and the County Board of Supervisors were held on October 4, 2016, and November 22, 2016. In addition, the School Board held a work session on the budget on October 24, 2016.  

 

Without additional funding from the County, assuming FCPS’ projections for state and other revenue combined and with increasing required expenditures and recommended salary increases, FCPS is forecasting a significant deficit for FY 2018. 

 

Prior to any County transfer increase or employee salary increase, FCPS faces a shortfall of approximately $130 million. Without additional funding from the State or County in FY 2018, FCPS will need to eliminate or reduce programs, services, and/or salary increases to eliminate the projected shortfall.

 

FCPS has implemented significant budget reductions nearly each year since FY 2008. Reductions were required because recurring revenue has not kept pace with FCPS’ increases in expenditures resulting from student enrollment and demographic changes, employee salary increases, and other unavoidable costs. As a result of not being able to fund salary increases each year, FCPS has fallen behind the market average of surrounding school systems for teacher salaries. 

 

While FCPS was able to make an initial investment in teacher pay in FY 2017, without increasing revenue, FCPS will not be able to continue to close the teacher salary gap, address employee compensation, and sustain the current quality, nor the full range of academic programming we currently offer our students.

 

During the work session, the School Board will review the budget development calendar and begin the discussion of possible reductions by reviewing the recommendations of the Budget Task Force and any potential implementation timeline considerations.

Subject: ERFC Options

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Key Points: 

 

FCPS has hired Aon Hewitt, a provider of retirement consulting and actuarial services, to conduct a study which benchmarks FCPS' retirement benefit levels to surrounding school divisions, Fairfax County and the federal government.  The study also included the adequacy of income replacement at retirement provided by the retirement programs that FCPS offers.

 

On May 12, 2016, ERFC presented the history of the FCPS retirement programs along with the key provisions of the existing plans.  Aon Hewitt then provided a briefing on the results of its study, illustrating retirement benefit levels for FCPS employees at various age and service combinations with comparisons to the peer group.  Aon Hewitt also presented ERFC options for possible future consideration.

 

On July 21, 2016, as a follow-up to the May work session and as requested by the School Board, the benefit impact on new hires of the options for possible consideration were presented.  On September 19, 2016, the employer cost savings associated with those options for new hires was discussed.  On November 17, 2016, the employer cost savings associated with those options for new hires and non-vested employees was discussed.

 

At this work session, the employer cost savings associated with expanding those options to include all members except those currently eligible for full service retirement will be discussed.  Current retirees were excluded from all of the analysis.
