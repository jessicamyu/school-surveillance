Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Other Staff Present: Lee Ann Pender, director, Administrative Services

Meeting Category: April 5, 2010 - Work Session No. 100

Subject: Monopole Program Expansion, Implementation of Stealth Telecommunication Facilities (such as tree poles) at Elementary School Sites

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Related To: School Board Policy 8335, Wireless Telecommunications Facilities

Background:

Staff has been working with the Fairfax County Department of Planning and Zoning and the Planning Commission to coordinate use of school board-owned sites for telecommunication uses. Thus far, uses have primarily been at high schools, with a few non-high school exceptions where light poles existed previously. On January 21, 2010, FCPS staff met with the Fairfax County Planning Commission’s Policy and Procedures Committee to review a potential change in School Board policy that would allow “stealth” poles, such as tree poles, at FCPS locations to include middle and high school sites.
Key Points:

Public lands, such as school board-owned property, remain the preferred locations for new telecommunication facilities.

Telecommunication facilities should be constructed in areas to blend with an existing pattern of tall structures, and should be disguised and camouflaged so as to resemble other man-made structures, such as light poles, and natural features, such as trees;

The FCPS monopole program generates revenues that support communications safety initiatives, as well as local school programs/activities.

Recommendation:

That FCPS staff continue to coordinate with the Fairfax County Department of Planning and Zoning and the Planning Commission to expand the FCPS monopole program, including the introduction and implementation of stealth telecommunication facilities at elementary and middle school sites, to:
      (1) Increase revenues to the schools system;
      (2) Increase services to the community; and
      (3) Take a critical step towards providing Internet services to all students.

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Other Staff Present: Denise James, director, Office of Facilities Planning

Meeting Category: April 5, 2010 – Work Session No. 101
Subject: Facilities Planning Advisory Council

School Board Action Required: Decision at a future meeting

Key Points:

      In January, 2009, the School Board established the Comprehensive Plan Development Committee (CPDC) with a charge to design a recommendation to the School Board for a comprehensive facilities planning process. As a result of its deliberations and research, the CPDC presented the concept of a facilities advisory council in September, 2009. The concept envisioned the creation of an “umbrella planning group” to interact with staff, the community and the Board and which could provide district-wide comprehensive planning oversight and guidance. To that end, the CPDC now presents a recommendation for the establishment of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC). The draft proposal outlines the organization and function of a Board appointed citizen council, including mission, objectives, function and operations, work products, communications flow, community engagement, application criteria and process for council selection.

      If accepted, the FPAC would become a standing committee of the School Board, subject to the guidelines contained in School Board Policy 1710. It is further noted that modifications to Policy 1710 may be required to accommodate the unique features of the proposed advisory council, modification which will be brought forward for Board review and approval pending the outcome of the Board’s action on the FPAC proposal.
Options:
      Since we have never conducted such a process before, the School Board is asked to provide its thoughts about the proposal at the April 5, 2010, work session.

Recommendation:
      That the School Board approve the establishment of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council as proposed by the School Board’s Comprehensive Planning Development Committee and detailed in the attachment.

Subject: Regulation 2601, Student Responsibilities and Rights, Student Responsibilities and Rights Booklet

School Board Action Required: Discussion

Related To: Student Achievement Goal 2, Essential Life Skills

Key Points:
      Regulation 2601, Student Responsibilities and Rights (SR & R) and booklet are revised every year. This is a summary of the recommended changes:
          Throughout regulation the word “medication(s)” was changed to “drug(s).”

          A. In chapter I.K. updated address and phone number for Office of Design and Construction Services and Due Process and Eligibility to reflect current information.

          B. In chapter I.K.3. changed name of “Monitoring and Compliance Section” to “Due Process and Eligibility.”

          C. In chapter II.C.3.d.(1) removed “being” and added “arriving on school property, or to a school-sponsored activity.” Changed “illegal controlled substance” to read “illegal or illegally used controlled substance.” Removed “or for the illegal use of prescription drugs.”

          D. In chapter II.C.3.d.(3) added “Use or” to sentence for clarification.

          E. In chapter II.C.9. removed sentence “The decision of the Division Superintendent shall be final unless altered by the School Board upon timely written petition by the student or the parent or guardian for a review of the record by the School Board.” Added sentence “Following the hearing, the Division Superintendent shall promptly notify the parent or guardian and the principal of the decision and the basis for the decision, noting the parent’s or guardian’s right to appeal in writing to the full School Board. Appeals to the full School Board shall be conducted in accordance with the procedures established in the current version of Policy 2611.”

          F. In section “Acceptable Use Policy for Student Network Access” added “Students using privately-owned computing devices must follow the policy stated in this document while on school property, attending any school-sponsored activity, or using the FCPS network.”
Recommendation:

That the School Board approve Regulation 2601.24 P. Student Responsibilities and Rights
