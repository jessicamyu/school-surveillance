Staff Contact: Francisco Durán, chief equity officer

                       Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Special Services 

 

Other Staff Present: Lea Skurpski, director, Jacqueline Prentice, coordinator, Special Services 

 

Meeting Category: April 16, 2020 - Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 1 Student Success

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report for the 2020-2021 school year, as required by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent’s Memo #288-19

2020-2021 Special Education Annual Plan/Part B Flow-Through Application-This is a Word document. dated December 20, 2019. This report will be used by VDOE in preparing the state plan for special education. As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 8, 2020.

 

The 2020-2021 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $37,414,692. This amount is equal to the preliminary IDEA Section 611 funding allocation for Federal Fiscal Year (FFY) 2020, July 1, 2020 through September 30, 2022, as communicated in the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent’s Email dated March 18, 2020. Estimated Section 611 expenditures include costs associated with the voluntary 15 percent set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS); a mandatory proportionate set-aside, which ensures that FCPS spends a proportionate share of funds on special education services for parentally-placed private school children with disabilities; materials/supplies; and state restricted indirect costs of 1.1 percent. The IDEA Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 292.60 FTE positions.

 

The 2020-2021 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $836,008. This amount is equal to the preliminary IDEA Section 619 funding allocation for Federal Fiscal Year (FFY) 2020, July 1, 2020 through September 30, 2022, as communicated in the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent’s Email dated March 18, 2020. These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students. Estimated expenditures include costs associated with a mandatory proportionate set-aside and state restricted indirect costs of 1.1 percent. The IDEA Section 619 (Preschool), award will fund 6.0 FTE positions.

 

The revenue amounts above reflect a combined increase of $1.2 million, as compared to the 2019-2020 awards. The 2020-2021 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2019-2020 application. Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, materials/supplies, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.

 

The 2020-2021 Annual Plan was presented to the Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) at the March 11, 2020 meeting.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the 2020-2021 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool grant funding application, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:  High School Science Basal Instructional Resources Approval

 

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present:  Noel Klimenko, director, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

 

Meeting Category:  April 16, 2020 Regular Meeting
 

School Board Action Required: Consent

 

Key Points:

The Virginia Department of Education revised the Standards of Learning for Science in 2018.  The Science team in Instructional Services has been revising curriculum documents to align with these new standards and providing professional development for teachers and administrators about the revised SOLs which will be fully implemented in the 2021-2022 school year. 

 

The current high school science textbooks were purchased in 2005.  Schools have been utilizing these texts as well as print and digital materials available through various sources. To provide equitable resources for students in all high school science courses, Instructional Services began the process for new basal instructional materials as defined in FCPS Regulation 3004-Adopted Basal Instructional Materials in 2019.

 

Funding for these materials will be provided from the existing textbook reserve account; no new FY21 funding will be needed for these purchases.

 

A timeline of the process for the science basal instructional resource review can be found in Attachment A.  In October 2019, the School Board approved the committee members to review and recommend basal instructional resources for high school science. The High School Science Basal Instructional Resources Review Committee convened in November and December to review resources for high school science that were submitted by vendors in response to an RFP for consideration as basal instructional materials.  In accordance with the Code of Virginia, regulations of the Virginia Board of Education, and Fairfax County Public Schools regulations, the Basal Instructional Resources Review Committee in conjunction with the Selection Advisory Committee and the Technical Advisory Committee recommends the titles listed in Attachment B for adoption at the division level.  Additionally, a summary of the committee rationales for the recommended materials and community input received are provided in attachments C and D.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the titles listed for high school Science as recommended by the High School Science Basal Instructional Resources Review Committee and the Selection Advisory Committee.
