Custodian Appreciation Week - February 22-26, 2016

The School Board recognizes the hard work and unending dedication of the custodians who work for Fairfax County Public Schools. Our custodians are the first employees to arrive in the morning and the last employees to leave at night. By providing clean and safe school environments, the custodians at FCPS have a direct impact on the educational success of our students. Many custodians take additional training leading to certification as professional custodians.  The week of February 22-26 has been named Custodian Appreciation Week to recognize and thank our dedicated custodians whose attention to detail and high standards affect all students, employees, and many members of the community.

 

 

In addition, each worksite for Fairfax County Public Schools also acknowledges their great support for staff during Support Employee Appreciation Week, which is from April 18-22, 2016.

National Career and Technical Education Month - February 2016

 

 

Career and technical education prepares both youth and adults for a wide range of careers, from nurse to computer technician. These careers may require varying levels of education--from high school to postsecondary certificates to two- and four-year college degrees.
 


The subject areas most commonly associated with career and technical education are: Business and Information Technology (programming , business management, information technology); Trade and Industrial (skilled trades such as automotive technician, carpenter, cosmetologist, law enforcement); Health and Medical Sciences (nursing, dental, and emergency medical technicians); Family and Consumer Sciences (culinary arts, child development, education); Marketing (sales, entrepreneurship); and Technology Education (electronics, engineering). 

 

As part of the Career and Technical Education program FCPS has six High School Academies offering course work in CTE, Fine Arts and World Languages.  These academies offer high specialized and technical courses that lead to further study at a two or four year institution or immediate placement in the workforce.  Four of the six academies have designations as governor’s Academies in either STEM or Health Sciences.  Students enrolled in CTE program have the opportunity to earn industry credentials that provide valuable workforce credentials.

Career and technical education is offered in middle schools, high schools, two-year community and technical colleges and other postsecondary schools. According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, 18 of the 20 fastest growing occupations within the next decade will require career and technical education.

National School Counseling Week - February 2-6, 2016

 

National School Counseling Week 2016, "School Counseling: The recipe for Success” will be celebrated from February 1-5, 2016, to focus public attention on the unique contribution of professional school counselors within U.S.  school systems.  National School Counseling Week, sponsored by the American School Counseling Association, highlights the tremendous impact school counselors can have in helping students achieve school success and plan for a career.  National School Counseling Week is always celebrated the first full week in February.

 Fairfax County Public Schools employs over 600 school counseling professionals, including school counselors, directors of student services, and career center specialists.  These professionals are committed to preparing students to become lifelong learners and productive members in a global society.

RESOLUTION

ON

HUMAN TRAFFICKING AWARENESS MONTH

 

 

          WHEREAS, human trafficking, a heinous crime, is thought by many Americans to be confined to other parts of the globe but is instead the fastest-growing criminal enterprise in the world and is occurring so regularly here in Northern Virginia that our area is now one of the top hubs of teen trafficking in the United States; and

 

          WHEREAS, human traffickers prey on middle and high school teens but have also been known to hone their skills in targeting and exploiting psychologically and emotionally vulnerable men, women, and children as young as 9 years old at shopping malls, metro stations, bus stops, and via Facebook and other forms of social media; and

 

          WHEREAS, trafficking victims are from all ethnic and socio-economic backgrounds and often unaware that they are being carefully manipulated and coerced by their new found “friends” until they realize all too late that they have become trapped and are either threatened with or have become actual victims of violence; and

 

          WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools and the Just Ask Prevention Foundation have instituted a teen sex trafficking prevention curriculum in grades six through ten and twelve and developed effective awareness campaigns that have become models for other jurisdictions; and

 

          WHEREAS, student groups at Fairfax County high schools, educators, parents, medical professionals, law enforcement officials, civic organizations, and the business community understand the importance of bringing awareness about teen sex trafficking to young people and wish to mobilize students and others in the community in order to create an environment where this crime is not tolerated and its victims are not overlooked;

 

          NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board joins the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors in recognizing the month of January 2016 as Human Trafficking Awareness Month in Fairfax County and supports the efforts of all those who are seeking to eliminate human trafficking and its impact on our community.

                                                                                   FAIRFAX COUNTY SCHOOL BOARD RESOLUTION ON

                                                                              OPPOSING A CONSTITUTIONAL AMENDMENT TO DIVEST LOCAL

                                                                    SCHOOL BOARDS OF THE SOLE AUTHORITY TO APPROVE CHARTER SCHOOLS

 

 

      WHEREAS, in its 2016 session, the General Assembly will be considering a resolution seeking a constitutional amendment to divest local school boards of the sole authority to approve charter schools, vesting authority with a gubernatorial appointed board; and

 

      WHEREAS, local school boards, which are elected or appointed by their community and entrusted with the authority to supervise schools, are best equipped to assess the needs of their students and the locality; and

 

      WHEREAS, local school boards are responsible for the outcomes of all schools within the school system, including charter schools; and

 

      WHEREAS, research has shown that charter school outcomes are mixed, with the majority doing no better than the traditional public schools that feed them; and

 

      WHEREAS, state-authorized charter schools would result in a massive unfunded mandate to local school divisions, costing millions of dollars to local taxpayers;

 

      NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Fairfax County School Board hereby opposes the resolution seeking a constitutional amendment to divest local school boards from the sole authority to approve charter schools.

Category
5. New Business 8:30 p.m.
Type

 

Staff Contact:  Ilene Muhlberg, Clerk of the School Board

                                                                                     

Meeting Category:  February 4, 2016 – Regular Meeting No. 13

 

Subject: 5.01 Recommendation to revise Policy 1802  to move student representative election to April, rather than February, effective for the 2017-18 school year

 

School Board Action Required:  Action                                    

 

Key Points:

Consideration of revision to Policy 1802 (Student Representative to School Board), to move the election of the Student Representative to the School Board to April instead of the currently-prescribed February time frame.  Students have expressed concern that it is too early to attract candidates from the Student Advisory Council (SAC) and School Board leadership program.  The change would be effective with the 2017-18 school year. These proposed revisions were the topic of a Board forum on January 21 where consensus was reached to present this matter for the Board’s consideration as new business on February 4 and action on February 18.

                                  

Recommendation

Approve the proposed revisions to Policy 1802 to change the timing for the election of the Student Representative to the School Board by the (SAC) from February to April, effective in the 2017-18 school year

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
