Staff Contact:         Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  April 23, 2014 – Work Session No. 98

 

Subject:                  Blueprint for Change Update

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

On April 3, 2013, the School Board awarded a contract to Children’s National Medical Center (CNMC) to develop a Blueprint for Change for later high school start times for Fairfax County Public Schools.

 

The CNMC team has researched scientific literature; analyzed previous FCPS task reports, surveys, case studies and media articles; performed analysis of school districts across the country with successful school start time changes, and completed in-depth interviews. 

 

As part of its community engagement efforts, the CNMC team has created and maintained website and social media pages with educational materials, held over 45 individual community stakeholder meetings, and hosted four group stakeholder meetings to examine costs, logistics, feasibility and the potential impact of moving high school start times later.

 

The work resulted in a number of scenarios/ranges recommended for consideration to be discussed and subsequently presented to the broader public for input/feedback.  Once outreach is achieved in each cluster, final recommendation(s) will be provided to the School Board in July.

Meeting Category:  April 23, 2014 - Work Session No. 99

 

Subject:                  Capital Improvement Program (CIP) Prioritization

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

The development of the annual FCPS Capital Improvement Program has grown more difficult over the past 6 years as the significant student growth demands upon our facilities has diminished our ability to renovate our aging buildings and replace key infrastructure.

 

Attachment A displays the current status of enrollment and capacity status of each of our schools. The status of the schools is color coded to indicate the following:

  • Green - whether there has been a recent or ongoing capital project
  • Blue – if there are potential solutions which have previously been discussed in a public setting
  • Red – to be determined

Presently there is a surplus of capacity, most namely due to the capital projects. Unfortunately, this surplus is not necessarily where all of the overcrowding exists.

 

Attachment B provides similar information as attachment A except it displays the most recent 5 year projections. The schools are color coded in a similar manner to provide some indication of whether a potential solution has been publicly discussed at this point. It should be noted that many of the major capital projects will be underway during this time, but we do not include new capacity until the project has been completed. In fact, if the capacity of these capital projects is included we would have an overall net surplus of seats; though not directly in every area of growth.

 

Attachment C only contains schools which are within the renovation queue. It is organized in a similar manner to the preceding charts and sorted by the most crowded queue school to the least using the present enrollment and capacity status. Schools which have received funding (either planning or construction) are indicated in green. This chart indicates that the criteria used to evaluate and rank the schools for the queue was not solely based upon capacity, but instead included significant weighting to other factors such as quality of learning spaces or age and condition.

 

Attachment D provides a capacity analysis of the schools by cluster and recommends boundary solutions to balance enrollments between overcrowded and underutilized schools.  This also includes schools that may be included in the studies to establish attendance areas of the proposed new schools.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That capacity continues to be the priority of the CIP, regardless of whether it is gained through a renovation, addition, new facility or strategic programmatic placement.

Staff Contact:          Dan Parris, Interim Deputy Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  April 23, 2014 – Work Session No.100       

 

Subject:                  Strategic Planning - Update from ECRA Consultants

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion               

 

Key Points:

 

As previously described, the ECRA group was selected to partner with Fairfax County Public Schools in the development of a division-wide strategic plan. The proposal outlines a three-phase process that includes conducting research, developing the strategic plan and identifying steps for implementation. Phase I of the process provides a focus on documenting the current state of reality and desired future position of the school division.

 

During this work session, ECRA will engage and focus the School Board on the tasks described below:

  1. Work with School Board members and other stakeholders to assess and revise as needed the School Board Beliefs, Vision and Mission statements, Student Achievement Goals and operational objectives in order to assist the School Board in clarifying priorities and affirming a guiding strategic direction for the division.
  1. Conduct a thorough analysis of relevant division-level information to assist in assessing the current state of performance. Use these findings to identify key drivers for advancing student outcomes and operational efficiencies.

 

Specific guiding elements to be considered include:

 

Explore the position of the School Board in regards to the status of current beliefs, vision and mission statements.

 

Discuss the strategic plan survey instrument and provide input as to the areas of focus.

 

Affirm next steps and timelines in the strategic plan process.
