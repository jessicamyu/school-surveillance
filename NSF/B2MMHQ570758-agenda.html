Teacher Appreciation Week - May 6-10, 2019

 

Teachers are real life superheroes.  They educate, innovate, encourage, and support, touching the lives of millions of children.  Every day, over 15,000 teachers in Fairfax County Public Schools demonstrate their personal and professional dedication to providing an excellent education for the children in our community. Our teachers are committed to ensuring that all children develop their full potential for academic achievement. Teachers are influential in fostering in their students both healthful self-concepts and a sense of civic responsibility. Fairfax County Public Schools values the skills, creativity, and professionalism that characterize our teachers.

 

 

Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month - May 2019

 

Americans of Asian and Pacific ancestry have made significant contributions to the history and development of the United States in many fields of endeavor such as science, commerce, the arts, and the humanities. The Fairfax County School Board recognizes that an understanding of these contributions should be an integral part of the year-round education of all students.

Since 1992, the President of the United States has proclaimed May to be Asian- and Pacific-American Heritage Month and has chosen this month to pay tribute to the generations of Asian and Pacific Islanders who have enriched American’s history and are instrumental in its future success.

 

 

School Nurse Day - May 8, 2019

School Nurse Day was established in 1972 to foster a better understanding of the role of school nurses in the educational setting.  This annual event recognizes the contributions that school nurses make every day to improve the health and success of our nation’s children.  This year’s theme, “School Nurses:  Better Health. Better Learning,” reflects the significant roles that school nurses have in health care in their school communities, as well as in modeling health and resiliency.

School nursing is a specialized practice of professional nursing that advances the health and well-being of students and is an integral part of a quality school health program. School nurses promote health, safety, prevention, and wellness; intervene with actual and potential health problems; and provide case management services.  They are an invaluable bridge connecting health care providers, public health, and families.

The school system works closely with the Fairfax County Health Department to provide quality services to the children of Fairfax County Public Schools and recognized May 8 as School Nurse Day in Fairfax County.

Recognition of Better Hearing and Speech Month - May 2019

 

Since 1927, the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) has celebrated Better Hearing and Speech Month every year in May. The theme this year is “Communication Across the Lifespan”. This annual event provides opportunities to promote services that can improve the quality of life for those who experience problems with speaking and listening. An estimated 42 million people have communication disorders and they impact one in ten families.

 

Currently, Fairfax County Public Schools provides speech and language services to more than 10,000 students. Audiologists and special education teachers also serve more than 500 deaf and hard of hearing students in class-based programs, via base school itinerant services, and in early childhood programs. FCPS annually observes and celebrates Better Hearing and Speech Month and encourages all citizens in our school community to recognize the efforts, dedication, and skills of the Speech-Language pathologists, audiologists, and teachers of students who are deaf and hard of hearing.

Subject:  Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2019-2020

 

 

Staff Contact:  Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services (DSS)

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Lea Skurpski, director; Operations and Strategic Planning, DSS

           Jacqueline Prentice, coordinator, Financial Management, DSS

 

 

Meeting Category:  April 25, 2019 - Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 1: Student Success

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report for the 2019-2020 school year, as required by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent's Memo #336-18  dated December 7, 2018.  This report will be used by VDOE in preparing the state plan for special education.  As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 10, 2019.

 

The 2019-2020 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $36,241,914.  This amount is equal to the 2018–2019 award as stated in Superintendent's Memo #193-18, dated July 27, 2018.  Estimated expenditures include costs associated with the voluntary 15 percent set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS); a mandatory proportionate set-aside, which ensures that FCPS spends a proportionate share of funds on special education services for parentally-placed private school children with disabilities; materials/supplies; and state restricted indirect costs of 1.3 percent. The IDEA Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 381.6 FTE positions.

 

The 2019-2020 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $807,316.  This amount is equal to the 2018-2019 award as stated in Superintendent's Memo #193-18, dated July 27, 2018.  These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students.  Estimated expenditures include costs associated with a mandatory proportionate set-aside and state restricted indirect costs of 1.3 percent. IDEA Section 619 (Preschool) funds 6.0 FTE positions.

 

The revenue amounts above reflect no change from the 2018-2019 awards.  The 2019-2020 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2018-2019 application.  Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, materials/supplies, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.  

The 2019-2020 Annual Plan was presented to the Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) at the March 13, 2019 meeting.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the 2019-2020 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B Preschool grant funding application, as detailed in the agenda item.
