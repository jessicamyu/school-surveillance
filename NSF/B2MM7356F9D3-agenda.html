Crossing Guard Appreciation Day - February 13, 2019

 

The Virginia Safe Route to School program has designated the second Wednesday in February as Crossing Guard Appreciation Day. This is a statewide event to say thank you to the individuals who help students cross the street safely on their way to and from school and will be celebrated in Virginia on Wednesday, February 13.  Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) will observe the day by honoring the 80 crossing guards who manage 174 school-related crossings every day.  FCPS crossing guards are employees of the Fairfax County Police Department.

 

While the focus of Crossing Guard Appreciation Day is on crossing guards and the work that they do, it can also be a good time to remind students how to safely cross the street.

Custodian Appreciation Week - February 18-22, 2019

The School Board recognizes the hard work and unending dedication of the custodians who work for Fairfax County Public Schools. Our custodians are the first employees to arrive in the morning and the last employees to leave at night. By providing clean and safe school environments, the custodians at FCPS have a direct impact on the educational success of our students. Many custodians take additional training leading to certification as professional custodians.  The week of February 18-22 has been named Custodian Appreciation Week to recognize and thank our dedicated custodians whose attention to detail and high standards affect all students, employees, and many members of the community.

7. I move to amend the main motion by:

a. Adding 9 custodial positions at a cost of $0.4 million; and

b. Reducing the Staffing Initiatives placeholder by $0.4 million.(Palchik/Moon)

 

8. In order to make a first step toward achieving the recommended facilities trades management guidelines for educational facilities, to improve our ability to monitor and support online student safety, and to provide appropriate coordination and community engagement for the boundary process, I move to amend the main motion by:

b. Adding one security position at a cost of $0.1 million; and

d. Reducing the Staffing Initiatives placeholder by $1.0 million. (Palchik/McLaughlin)

Board member proposed follow-on motions:

 

1. I move that the School Board direct the Superintendent to provide analyses on the following subjects by April 8, 2019 in order for the School Board to develop budget guidelines for the FY21 budget:

a. A prioritized list and explanation of the factors that affect the recruitment and retention of highly qualified teachers, including, but not limited to, salary and benefits;

b. Proven strategies to recruit and retain highly qualified teachers; and,

c. The impact of providing above-market salary levels to teachers on the budget and the recruitment and retention of highly qualified teachers. (Moon/McElveen)

 

2. I move that the School Board direct the Superintendent to provide the School Board a multi-year plan for restoring IA salaries to align with the new teacher BA scales, ensuring that at a minimum IAs are paid a living wage and an average of ½ of the corresponding pay scale on the BA lanes. Additionally, the superintendent shall provide a plan for raising the entry level educational support personnel pay scales, currently paid hourly, up to a living wage and adjusting the higher steps accordingly. (Corbett Sanders)

 

 

3. I move that the School Board direct the Superintendent and staff to study and further engage with stakeholders on concerns regarding the living wage in Fairfax County, and to propose a multi-year plan to be implemented beginning with the FY21 budget. (Palchik)

 

 

4. I move that the School Board direct the Superintendent and staff continue to work to create a multi-year plan to be aligned with the Gibson report recommendations in our custodial and trades positions starting in FY21, and to include staffing ratios for these positions.(Palchik)

 

5. In line with the One Fairfax policy, I move to direct the Superintendent to provide options and recommendations to the Board, no later than May 1, 2019, on ways to improve equity of opportunity among Fairfax County schools in sports, field trips, after-school programs and other extracurricular activities. Considering the socioeconomic imbalance among schools in gate receipts and funds provided by support organizations such as PTAs and Booster clubs (as detailed in the following school-by-school breakdown found on p. 123-124 in Next Steps Responses), the superintendent’s report will provide a plan for supporting schools that have both high poverty and low levels of extra funds from these sources. In creating the plan, the superintendent will engage stakeholders such as the FCPS Foundation, FCCPTA and booster clubs to provide input. (Evans/McLaughlin)

 

6. I move that the School Board direct the Superintendent to provide a multiyear plan for Title I school funding by May 1, 2019. 

 

Specifically, I request an analysis of FY20 allocations for schools that may be impacted by the loss of federal funds in order to maintain positions; and enrichment and remedial programs that align with staffing from FY18, and FY19. In addition, project Title I school-based staffing costs for FY21. (Keys-Gamarra)

 

7.  In an effort to improve equity of opportunity for all FCPS students, the Board directs the Superintendent to provide the initial findings from FCPS’ review of its Current Practices as well as Best Practices related to School-Based Clinics. Along with these findings, the Superintendent will present any recommended changes and/or FY20 budget requests related to the School-Based Clinics delivery model. This information will be presented at a work session no later than April 30th, 2019, so that any changes to the FY20 Budget can be considered before the Board’s final vote on May 23, 2019. (McLaughlin)

 

8.  I move that the School Board direct the Superintendent to review the FY20 Elementary Staffing Standard and its impact on schools and provide an opportunity for the School Board to discuss the formula in greater detail at a work session by April 30, 2019. (Evans)

Subject: Award of Contract – HVAC Make-up Air Unit Replacements at Daniels Run Elementary School

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category: February 7, 2019 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Funding for this project will be provided by a fund transfer from Fairfax City Schools to Fairfax County Public School’s Major Maintenance Fund. The Office of Facilities Management staff will administer the contract and provide project management. Specifications have been prepared and the project has been advertised. An invitation for bid was issued and advertised; bids were received on February 7, 2019. A bid tabulation and recommendation for award will be furnished prior to the February 21, 2019, School Board meeting.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the HVAC make-up air unit replacements at Daniels Run Elementary School to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.
