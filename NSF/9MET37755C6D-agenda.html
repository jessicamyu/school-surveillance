National Red Ribbon Week, October 23-31, 2014

 

National Red Ribbon Week was established in memory of Enrique Camarena, a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration agent who was killed in the line of duty while investigating a major drug cartel in Mexico.  To honor his battle against illegal drugs, friends and neighbors began to wear red badges of satin and some parents began to form coalitions.  National Red Ribbon Week grew from these grassroots efforts to form a national movement against the use of illegal drugs.  Its annual campaign encourages students to take a visible stand against substance abuse and make a personal commitment to follow a drug-free lifestyle.

 

 

Native American Heritage Month - November 2014

 

Native American Heritage Month started in 1915 as American Indian Day by the Boy Scouts of America.  Over the years, individual states recognized Native American days, but it wasn’t until 1990 that President George H.W. Bush designated November as National American Indian Heritage Month.  Since 1994, a presidential proclamation has proclaimed the month to be Native American Heritage Month in honor of the significant contributions made by the first Americans to the establishment and growth of the U.S.

National Career Development Month - November 2014

 

November 2014 is National Career Development Month and, last week, Virginia celebrated Career Week. Career center specialists, school counselors, career experience specialists, employment and transition representatives play unique roles in career development for students. With a focus on readiness for postsecondary endeavors, these educators are employed to help students explore and develop their abilities, interests, strengths, and talents as they relate to further education and entry into the workforce.

 

These educators collaborate with parents to enhance academic and career planning for students. All educators in PreK-12 help students investigate skills, qualities, and academic opportunities that relate to career fields, encourage lifelong learning, and promote future readiness. Students explore careers and other postsecondary options through classroom instruction, the student learning plan, academic advising, and related activities. Through career exploration and development, students learn the skills and training necessary to be successful in future careers and achieve gainful employment and personal fulfillment.

Adopt the newly revised 2014 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item (FS; presented as new business 10/9/14)

Staff Contact:            Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Roger L. Ball, director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:     October 23, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 6

 

Subject:                    Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 1, 2014, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2014 session of the General Assembly.  Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

County’s Administrative Changes

  1. Formal solicitation threshold:  The General Assembly passed SB1107 in 2011, a non-mandatory bill that increased the small purchase threshold from $50,000 to $100,000 for goods and non-professional services.  The same bill increased the small purchase threshold from $50,000 to $60,000 for professional services.  Subsequent to this change, the County will have the same thresholds the state implemented in 2011.
  1. Public posting requirement (emergency and sole source contracts):  The public posting requirement for emergency and sole source contracts is increased to $100,000 in accordance with SB 1107.
  1. Drug-free workplace contract provision:  The threshold for requiring a drug-free workplace contract provision is increased from $50,000 to $100,000.
  1. State Corporation Commission (SCC) authorization:  The threshold for contractors to obtain a SCC authorization is increased from $50,000 to $100,000.
  1. Design-Build Contracts:  The requirement for post project review by the Design-Build/Construction Management Review Board is removed.  The Review Board was eliminated by the General Assembly in 2011.  All other references to the Review Board were previously removed from the Purchasing Resolution.
  1. Construction Prequalification:  A provision to limit the remedy available to a contractor denied prequalification is added to the Purchasing Resolution.  The change will limit relief to restoration of eligibility, consistent with the VPPA.
  1. Article 6, Supply Management:  Text changes to clarify the consumable and fixed assets, as well as, surplus property duties of the Purchasing Agent for Fairfax County Government and Fairfax County Public Schools.

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, and 2013 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2014 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

  1. The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

  1. The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2014 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                     2015 Fairfax County School Board Legislative Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Information          

 

Related To:            Legislation

 

Key Points:

 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program to inform public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy issues of importance to the Fairfax County Public Schools.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.  The attached proposed 2015 FCSB Legislative Program includes additions, subtractions and formatting changes. These changes are intended to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal government, to reflect the current policy climate, to incorporate staff recommendations, and to update and streamline existing position language. 

 

Major Changes to the proposed 2015 Legislative Program include:

  • Rephrased most paragraph-based position language to fit a bullet-point format
  • Added language supporting the use of high growth as a factor in state funding formulas (Education Funding – State)
  • Added support for expanding the ESL student subgroup to account for students who exit due to achieving language proficiency (English Language Learners)
  • Added language referencing “Portrait of a Graduate;” supporting multiple pathways to graduation, including changes to personal finance graduation requirement, identification of additional courses as fulfilling graduation requirements, and revisiting student verified credits; and supporting simplified mechanisms for submitting substitute assessment scores to VDOE (Standards of Accreditation and Standards of Learning)

NOTE that all changes to the Proposed 2015 Legislative Program are documented in the Change Summary document, as attached to the agenda item.

 

In addition, the following editing changes have been made to the proposed Program document since the Board’s October 6th work session:

  • Board Priority Position Language - based on existing Board Legislative Positions.  NOTE: Priority Position language will be provided to the Board prior to scheduled final action on this agenda item, currently scheduled for November 6, 2014.
  • Clarified language in Joint FCBS/FCSB Priority Position related to impact of special education services
  • Rephrased School Calendar position to better align new bulleted language format with previously adopted Board position

Options:

 

Modify as desired.

Subject:                     Policy 1450, Nondiscrimination

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

School Board Policy 1450, Nondiscrimination, was adopted July 1, 1986, to ensure conditions of nondiscrimination and equal opportunity in the school system.

Due to recent decisions by the US Supreme Court and the Fourth Circuit Court of Appeals, effective October 6, 2014, the Commonwealth of Virginia recognizes same-sex marriage. As a result, Virginia employers must provide benefits to same-sex couples. 

 

In keeping with the express intent of the School Board that every policy, practice, and procedure shall conform to all applicable requirements of federal and state law, it is imperative that Policy 1450 be revised to include protection for students, employees, and applicants against discrimination based on sexual orientation.

 

Recommendation:

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
