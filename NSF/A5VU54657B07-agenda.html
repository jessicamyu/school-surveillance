Staff Contact:     Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Financial Services

 

Meeting Category:  January 28, 2016 – Work Session No. 61

 

Subject:     FY 2017 Advertised Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

On January 7, 2016, the Superintendent presented the FY 2017 Proposed Budget.  The FY 2017 Proposed Budget presentation and document are available at http://www.fcps.edu/news/fy2017.shtml.

 

The FY 2017 Proposed Budget for the School Operating Fund totals $2.7 billion and is an increase of $121.4 million, or 4.8 percent, over the FY 2016 Approved Budget.

 

The two key priorities are focused on investment in the classroom.

1.    Employee Compensation

·         A step increase and 1.0% market scale adjustment for all employees.

·         An additional $40 million investment in teacher salary scales, a first step in a multiyear strategy to invest in our workforce.

 

2.    Elementary Class Size Reduction

·         $10 million to fund additional classroom teachers to reduce class size in every elementary classroom to below 30 students where space is available.

 

To balance the budget, a 6.7 percent increase in funding from the County is requested. Recently, the increase in funding from the County has not covered FCPS’ basic budget drivers: salary increases, enrollment increases and student demographic changes, rising health care costs, and increases in state retirement costs.

 

During the work session, questions regarding the FY 2017 Proposed Budget will be discussed.
