RESOLUTION

NAMING THE GOLD OFFICE AT

OAKTON HIGH SCHOOL

“THE ELWYN ‘CHIP’ COMSTOCK GOLD OFFICE”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Elwyn ‘Chip’ Comstock has served Fairfax County Public Schools for 33 years with excellence and dedication; he began his professional career as a teacher at Falls Church High School and, most recently, served for 14 years as an assistant principal at Oakton High School; and

 

     WHEREAS, Chip’s legacy at Oakton High School is one of a committed, selfless, and diligent leader; he often worked days, nights, and weekends to ensure the smooth operation of Oakton High School; Chip managed the extensive logistics involved with coordinating student and faculty parking, scheduling building use, managing maintenance and repairs, and monitoring large-scale building improvement projects; and

 

     WHEREAS, in addition to his responsibilities as supervisor of multiple departments, Chip efficiently managed Oakton’s financial resources; his sound financial management practices ensured that students, faculty, and staff had the opportunities, support, and resources they needed to succeed; and

           

     WHEREAS, Chip was an exceptional role model for Oakton’s faculty and staff; his leadership and support motivated them to create a collaborative and caring working environment and enabled them to focus on improving student achievement while cultivating an innovative learning environment; and

 

     WHEREAS, Chip was a champion of Oakton’s students and faculty and was continuously promoting their achievements; a constant presence at academic awards ceremonies, sporting events, and performing arts presentations, Chip was enthusiastic about sharing in students’ successes; Chip’s attention to detail while planning proms, graduations, and all-night graduation parties, ensured that students celebrated their high school milestones without a hitch;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board, in appreciation of Elwyn “Chip” Comstock’s dedicated service and contributions to Fairfax County Public Schools and the Oakton High School community, hereby names the Gold Office at Oakton High School “The Elwyn ‘Chip’ Comstock Gold Office” in his honor and in recognition of his many years of selfless support and commitment to the school.

Other Staff Present:  Carol Hurley, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:      July 9, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                      FY 2015 Final Budget Review     

 

School Board Action Required:  Information 

 

Key Points:

 

All of the FY 2015 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds. All comparisons are against the FY 2015 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the revenue variance and the FY 2016 budgeted beginning balance, the flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the FY 2015 funds available total $33.2 million.

 

Revenue variances begin on page 1 of the agenda attachment, and FY 2015 actual total funds available are $5.0 million lower, or total 99.8 percent of the FY 2015 Third Quarter Budget Review projection. State Aid for FY 2015 totals $399.8 million, which is $1.6 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts total $179.9 million, $3.0 million more than budgeted. A decrease of $9.4 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $8.3 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2016, combined with a net decrease in other federal funding. Other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, and miscellaneous revenue, are $3.0 million more than the third quarter estimate.

 

Expenditure variances begin on page 2 of the agenda attachment, and FY 2015 actual expenditures are $29.9 million less than projected after funding FY 2016 beginning balance adjustments, the School Flexibility Reserve, and accounting for other commitments and carryover, excluding grants. Of the $29.9 million variance, $18.9 million is from salaries, $8.9 million from employee benefits, and $2.1 million is from other accounts, primarily fuel. In addition, $8.3 million in multiyear unspent federal grant funds is carried forward and reappropriated for FY 2016.

 

This agenda item also includes recommendations for FY 2016 expenditure adjustments. Committed priorities and requirements include a change in the indirect expenditures charged to Food and Nutrition Services of $0.9 million based on the FY 2016 rate set by the Virginia Department of Education; $3.6 million to continue FCPS’ annual commitment to allocate funding from year-end for major maintenance; $1.5 million for the joint Board of Supervisors/School Board Synthetic Turf Initiative; and $0.2 million for the infrastructure sinking reserve fund in accordance with recommendations of the joint Board of Supervisors and School Board Infrastructure Financing Committee (IFC). Funding to support key strategic plan investments includes $1.5 million for a systemwide compensation study and to implement a website content management system; $0.2 million for the second year of the development of world language curriculum for elementary and secondary schools and to identify opportunities to develop global partnerships, and $1.5 million for the replacement of buses and equipment. 

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

6.07 Revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual related to Title I Advisory Council - Recommendation to approve the recommended revisions to the Title I District Advisory Committee, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly (SB; action scheduled 7/23/15)
Meeting
Jul 9, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 1
Category
6. New Business
Type

Staff Contact:          Pamela Goddard, Executive Assistant and Clerk of the School Board

 

Meeting Category:  July 9, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                   Revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual related to Title I Advisory Council

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points: 

 

At the June 15, 2015, Governance Committee work session, there was a discussion about the request from Instructional Services and the District Advisory Council (Title I Advisory Committee) related to proposed revisions to the Strategic Governance Manual to reflect recent by-law revisions adopted by the Title I Advisory Committee.  The revisions would ensure that the committee’s name appropriately represents the intent of the committee within FCPS and the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) guidance.  There was consensus among the Governance Committee members to recommend the changes to the full Board at a future meeting for approval.

 

Specifically, the following revisions were recommended:

  1. Change the committee name from “District Advisory Council Title I” to “Title I Parent Advisory Committee”. There is no required name for this committee within the current ESEA or Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) family engagement guidance, and there is no prohibition from changing the existing committee name.
    1. Removing “District” in the name will remove non-standard language usage within the Commonwealth of Virginia, which refers to Local Educational Agencies as divisions rather than districts;
    2. Replacing “Council” with “Committee” in the name will remove non-standard language usage within the School Board strategic governance system, which typically establishes citizen advisory committees;
    3. Adding “Parent” in the name will improve the descriptiveness of the name, making clear the intended membership and purpose under the ESEA.
  2. Add a middle school liaison to be appointed by the Title I coordinator along with the existing teacher liaison to enhance opportunities for vertical articulation between Title I schools and their feeder middle schools regarding engagement practices for families from high poverty communities.
  3. Change the required minimum percentage of membership composed of parents of children receiving Title I services from 51 to 75 percent to better represent the intended membership for this committee under ESEA guidance; 
  4. Change the membership appointment date to June 30 (from December 31) to align with other School Board citizen advisory committee terms. 

Policy 1710, School Board Citizen Advisory Committees, will also be revised at a future date to reflect the revised committee name and the change in the annual appointment from December to June.

 

RECOMMENDATION:

 

That the School Board approve the recommended revisions to the Title I District Advisory Committee, as detailed in the agenda item, and update the Strategic Governance Manual accordingly.

Jul 9, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 1
Category

Staff Contact:          Fabio Zuluaga, Region 2 Assistant Superintendent

 

Meeting Category:  July 9, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 1

 

Subject:                   Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

In accordance with its policy, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia State Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a regional school.  The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:

  • Additional state funding for the regional school.  The estimate for FY 2016 is $2,739,951.  The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $4,177,182.
  • Cooperation with participating school divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.
  • Status the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.
  • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

Options:

 

The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Virginia Department of Education designation of Fairfax County Public Schools as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia for 2015-2016.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
