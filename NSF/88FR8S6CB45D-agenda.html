Contact:                     Tessie Wilson, Budget Chairman and Braddock District Representative

 

Meeting Category:    November 8, 2010 - Work Session No. 39

 

Subject:                      Proposal for Budget Tool Kit

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

Fairfax County revised its outreach efforts around the FY 2012 budget.  However, there were only two sessions, and they were poorly attended (less than 25 people).  So it will be up to the School Board to get its message out.  This will be particularly important if employee compensation is to be a top priority.  Also, with the continuing work on the Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) project, the budget staff will be hard pressed to do the number of presentations that need to be done. 

 

Below is a draft proposal for a new outreach effort for FCPS.  This outreach effort builds on the County’s idea of preparing a “budget tool kit. ”  The County’s thought was to make information available so that interested citizens could use the kit to present to their civic associations or other groups.  This proposal provides a slightly different approach.

 

BUDGET TOOL KIT PROPOSAL:

 

  • The budget office will prepare a PowerPoint presentation – much like they did last year – for School Board members to use to present to the public.
  • Each slide will have a “fact sheet” for use by the presenter to augment the material on the slide. 
  • Additional materials will be made available by the budget office for either distribution or for reference material by the presenters.  For example, a copy of the County Executive's presentation would be a good “tool” for the presenters to have, as it answers some general questions about the County’s assumptions on the budget.
  • As Board members do presentations, any unanswered questions about the PowerPoint presentation should be sent to the budget office.  The Budget office will update the underlying “fact sheet” as necessary for the presenters.
  • Board members will be given a supply of “Stump the Board Member” cards.  When a question is asked that the Board member cannot answer (and which is outside the parameters of the PowerPoint presentation),  the public will be asked to complete the card.  Those cards will be returned to the budget office, and answers will be provided and posted to a web site.  (These will be priority three questions.)
  • The budget staff will also provide at least two training sessions for School Board members on the PowerPoint presentation.  This will not only help Board members with the presentation, but it will also give staff a good indication of the type of information which should be on the fact sheet.
  • Coordination will occur between the Budget chair and co-chair and the Public Engagement Committee.
  • Each School Board member will be expected to conduct a minimum number of presentations.
  • The Public Engagement Committee will prepare a list of organizations other than PTAs/PTOs that should be contacted about presentations.
  • A Board member may request that a budget staff member attend an event and assist with the presentation .

 

The budget tool kit should be available shortly after the Superintendent presents his budget in January 2011.  Attached is a draft of the contents for the proposed budget tool kit.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board accept the Budget Tool Kit proposal, as detailed in the agenda item.

Contact:                     Brad Center, Governance Chairman and Lee District Representative

 

Meeting Category:   November 8, 2010 – Work Session No. 41

 

Subject:                     Review of Departmental Operational Expectations

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

At the July 23-24, 2010, retreat, the School Board accepted the new Operational Expectations monitoring report format. The Board agreed to a review of all current departmental Operational Expectations to clarify the Board’s values and to ensure alignment with the new monitoring report format.

 

The Governance Committee reviewed all the departmental Operational Expectations on October 13, and recommended several revisions, including the following:

  • Adding a Global Operational Expectation
  • Revising language for Human Resources, Professional Learning and Training, Budget and Financial Management, Facilities and Transportation Services, Community Relations, and Relationship with the Board
  • Revising language for the Instructional Program and Treatment of Students Operational Expectations, resulting in the creation of two separate Operational Expectations—Instructional Program and Learning Environment
  • Incorporating Accountability Operational Expectations into the new Instructional Program Operational Expectations
  • Continuing the audit procedures currently included in the Accountability and Audit Operational Expectations as a function of the Board’s Audit Committee (which is comprised of two School Board members, the Superintendent, and staff) and continuing to provide public access to the information
  • Keeping the Technology Operational Expectations unchanged

The Governance Committee’s recommended revisions were discussed by the entire Board at the October 18 work sessions, and some additional revisions were suggested to Budget and Financial Management and Facilities and Transportation Services Operational Expectations.   All the recommended revisions have been incorporated into the Operational Expectations being recommended for final approval by the Board on November 15.  The Strategic Governance Manual will be updated after Board approval.

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve revisions to the Operational Expectations, as detailed in the agenda item.
