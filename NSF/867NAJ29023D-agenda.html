Fire Prevention Week (Oct. 9 -15)

The National Fire Protection Association has designated the week of October 9 through 15, 2005, as Fire Prevention Week; the causes of most fires are predictable, and numerous fire hazards have been identified and mitigated in the past by employees from all areas of Fairfax County Public Schools.

National School Lunch Week (Oct. 10-14)

The National School Lunch Program is celebrating 59 years of service to children.  In 1946, President Harry S. Truman signed the National School Lunch Act into law. Schools celebrate National School Lunch Week annually – this year during the week of October 10 – 14.  The theme this year is “School Lunch:  It’s Instrumental”.
 
Lunch History in FCPS.  In 1943, FCPS served its first lunches at Herndon High School.  Parents sent surplus garden produce to be used for lunch preparation.  Students furnished their own silver and china which they took home each day.  The lunch price was 10 cents.  Today, lunch is served in every school.
    Lights On Afterschool!

    On October 20, more than 7,500 communities will celebrate Lights On Afterschool, a nationwide event organized by the Afterschool Alliance to celebrate afterschool programs across the nation. The event was created to showcase the benefits of enriching afterschool activities, and call attention to the resources required to keep these vital programs going. Afterschool programs keep kids safe, help working families and inspire learning -- yet more than 14 million kids have no place to go when the school day ends.

    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 7:30 and 8 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.
