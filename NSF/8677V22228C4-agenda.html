School Board Action Required: Decision

Related To: Professional Learning and Training Operational Expectations


Key Points: The Department of Professional Learning and Training has met all of its Operational Expectations and has exceeded these expectations. It has done so by systemically designing a model for compliance training using only internal resources and by securing a large grant from the Apple Federal Credit Union Education Foundation during a bleak financial period in Fairfax County.

Challenges for Operations Expectation #1:
    One of the key findings in Professional Learning in the Learning Profession: A Status Report on Teacher Development in the United States and Aboard is that teachers must have time to plan, time to learn, and time to collaborate. In the present budget crisis, how can FCPS maintain (and expand) teacher time for planning, learning and collaboration?
      FCPS must determine which professional learning experiences are designed to raise student achievement and then ensure these offerings are aligned with Best Practices as they relate to:
          Offering the number of hours required over a longer duration
          Ensuring the learning is embedded into PLC activities
          Focusing on important content

      Other Staff Present: Denise James, director, Office of Facilities Planning

      Meeting Category: September 14, 2009 – Work Session No. 19
      Subject: Southwestern County Community Engagement Process and Ad Hoc Planning Studies, Process Guidelines for School Board Members

      School Board Action Required: Decision

      Key Points:
          Feasibility studies have been conducted by Facilities and Transportation Services on the Clifton Elementary School renovation and on overcrowding in several elementary schools in the southwestern portion of the county

          The scope and complexity of the issues suggest that a community based comprehensive planning study should be undertaken to address the renovation challenges at Clifton Elementary School and the capacity deficit challenges in this area of the county. The study process is designed to accomplish three primary objectives through community engagement:1) to review and validate/determine the issues; 2) to determine the most complete list of the possible solutions to the issue(s) identified; and 3) to document all advantages and disadvantages of each potential solution.

          The School Board’s Comprehensive Planning Development Committee (CPDC) drafted the community engagement process. As it is likely that the proposed process would begin in fall 2009 with a goal to conclude in spring 2010, it is important that the School Board give direction to staff at the conclusion of the September work session.

          To support the work of this proposed Southwestern County Ad Hoc Planning study, Process Guidelines for School Board Members have also been developed for review and discussion.
      Options:
          Since we have never conducted such a process before, the School Board is asked to provide its thoughts about the proposed process at the September 14, 2009, work session so that the guidance can be given to staff in order to allow the process to begin immediately.

      Recommendation:
          That the School Board direct staff to initiate the Southwestern County Community Engagement Process for Clifton Elementary School renovation and overcrowded schools as presented by the School Board’s Comprehensive Planning Development Committee.

          REVISED RECOMMENDATION AS APPROVED BY THE BOARD ON SEPTEMBER 14, 2009:

          That the School Board direct staff to initiate the Southwestern Community Engagement Process as presented by the School Board’s Comprehensive Planning Development Committee.

      Meeting
      Sep 14, 2009 - Work Session No. 16-22
      Category
      20. Policy - 4 p.m.
      Type
      Information

      Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer
      Susan Quinn, Chief Financial Officer

      Other Staff Present: Penny McConnell, director, Food and Nutrition Services
      Fred Ellis, director, Safety and Security

      Meeting Category: September 14, 2009 – Work Session No. 16-22

      Subject: Video Surveillance Pilot Project Update and Policy 8614, Video Surveillance

      School Board Action Required: Information

      Related To: N/A
      Key Points:
      On July 24, 2008, the School Board amended Policy 8614, Video Surveillance, to permit a pilot prevention strategy at Annandale, Mount Vernon, and Westfield High Schools, and Lake Braddock, Robinson, and South County Secondary Schools in an effort to mitigate theft, food tampering, and inappropriate behaviors. Security video systems were strategically placed in the serving lines combined with conspicuous video monitors depicting real time images of customers. The security video system provides video images for review by school security.

      Overall, the security video system made an impact on reducing theft and inappropriate behaviors in the pilot schools. The school administrators overwhelmingly believed the pilot deterred student theft, food tampering, and inappropriate behavior.

      School administrators support continuing the security video system in the service areas. There have been no negative comments from parents, administration, staff, or students. Food and Nutrition Services requests School Board approval to extend the pilot security video system to gather additional data regarding the cost benefits.

      Staff recommends extension of the video pilot and approval of changes to the video surveillance policy to codify existing practice regarding ongoing and temporary monitoring when there is reasonable suspicion that a violation of law or policy has been committed.

      Recommendation:

      That the School Board extend the pilot security video system and adopt the revisions to Policy 8614, Video Surveillance, as detailed in the agenda item.
