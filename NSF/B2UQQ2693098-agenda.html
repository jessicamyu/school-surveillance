Subject:  Review of Disproportionality in Discipline Outcomes Plan

 

Staff Contact:  Francisco Durán, chief equity officer

 

Other Staff Present: Teresa Johnson, assistant superintendent, Special Services

                                   Lidi Hruda, director, Office of Research and Strategic Improvement

 

Meeting Category: February 11, 2018 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

In May 2018, the School Board directed the Superintendent to work with the chief equity officer and the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement to plan for and conduct an internal and external review of race and income disproportionality in discipline outcomes in FCPS. The components of the plan (Phase II) were to be presented to the School Board in January 2019 (moved to February 2019). This work on student behavior, discipline, and disproportionality follows earlier reporting to the School Board in November 2018 (Phase I).

 

The primary focus of today’s presentation is on presentation of the plan for internal and external reviews. The selection of experts, content and reporting for the external review will be described, as will the design of ORSI’s study (internal review), which is already underway.

 

In addition, this presentation includes a review of the November analyses as context for the plan and highlights of new and ongoing efforts FCPS is implementing to reduce missed instructional time and disproportionality.

Meeting Category: February 11, 2019 – School Board Work Session

 

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan 2018-2020

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

FCPS implemented the new strategic plan, Ignite, during school year 2014-15. Since that time, refinements have been made to increase the clarity of the plan’s elements. During school year 2017-18, a variety of stakeholders (School Board, Leadership Team, principals, teachers, students, and the community) provided input to prioritize the outcomes in each of the four goal areas.

 

The result of this work is a streamlined Strategic Plan with eight areas of focus for school years 2018-2020. These areas of focus have specific strategic actions to move the Division toward its goals. Each strategic action has targeted metrics to show the Division’s current baseline data, an interim target to achieve by school year 2020, and an aspirational statement that describes what success would look like for students, families, employees, and the community.

 

At this work session, the Board will have an opportunity to review enhancements that have been made to the Strategic Plan since the November 19, 2018, School Board meeting.

 

Recommendation:

The School Board accept the proposed targets and metrics to the strategic plan.
