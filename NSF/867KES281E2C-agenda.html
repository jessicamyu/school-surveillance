    RESOLUTION
    HONORING
    CALANTHIA R. TUCKER

    WHEREAS, Calanthia Tucker served with distinction and dignity as a teacher, an administrator, and, most recently, a cluster superintendent in Fairfax County Public Schools for more than 30 years; and
    WHEREAS, her keen insight and educational leadership inspired administrators to strive for excellence in preparing students for the 21st century, to continue their pursuit of educational opportunities, to close the gap between minority and majority students, and to provide challenging programs for all students and acceleration for those who need it; and
    WHEREAS, having a strong appreciation for involving parents and community members in the quest for a quality education for future citizens, Dr. Tucker served on community boards and committees—always exhibiting great poise, intellect, and cooperation as she related to the citizens. She fostered collaborations with National Urban Alliance and Computer Learning Centers Partnership and was just and kind in serving the parents and students within all of Cluster IV, always governing by the policies and regulations established; and
    WHEREAS, she was dedicated to staff members and to community members, showing mutual respect, compassion, and understanding when dealing with challenging situations that required fair and honest judgment. She was committed to seeing all students succeed and to seeing their rights respected as she supported administrators in their daily interactions with parents and students. Her integrity, high expectations of herself and others, and positive spirit gained her the respect and admiration of the people she encountered;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its highest commendation and deepest appreciation to Dr. Calanthia R. Tucker for her vision, leadership, and services, for her contribution to the quality of education, and for her unwavering dedication to all the children of Fairfax County Public Schools, and the entire Fairfax community extends its very best wishes to her as she embarks on this new chapter in her life.

        RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board award contracts for university intern programs to George Washington University in the amounts of $339,000, $252,263, and $85,810; to George Mason University in the amounts of $130,162 and $9,600; and to Marymount University in the amount of $77,500.

        EXPLANATION:
          The George Mason University Professional Development School program, the Marymount Professional Development School program (formerly known as the Professional Development Academy), and the George Washington University Transition to Teaching program are ongoing programs that train selected teacher education candidates in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS). Two other George Washington University programs provide interns in special education.
          These university programs provide access to an especially qualified group of applicants who understand the needs of FCPS students. Costs for these programs are funded from the substitute teacher account by trading off two instructional assistant (IA) vacancies and by placement of interns in existing IA vacancies. The universities supervise all student interns.

          State prescribed procedures are being used to award the contract to George Mason University. In the cases of George Washington and Marymount Universities, the agreements will be approved as sole source contracts on the basis that no other entities have expressed interest in these programs or can provide such a highly talented pool of substitute teachers at this time.

        5.05 Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology - Continue in 2006-2007 to accept, for Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor's Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia (Cluster III; presented as new business 7/27/06)
        Meeting
        Jul 27, 2006 - Regular Meeting No. 2
        Category
        5. Consent Agenda
        Type
        Action (Consent)

        Staff Contact: Cecilia Krill, Assistant Superintendent, Cluster III

        SUBJECT: Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology

        RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board approve that Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) continue in 2006-2007 to accept the Virginia Department of Education designation as the Governor’s Regional School for Science and Technology in Northern Virginia.

        EXPLANATION:
            In accordance with Fairfax County School Board Policy 3355.2, High School for Science and Technology, and Regulation 3355.8, Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, the School Board decides on an annual basis whether or not to cooperate with the Virginia Department of Education to operate Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST) as a governor’s regional school. The recommendation to continue to operate TJHSST as a regional school is based on the following advantages:
                • Additional state funding for the regional school. The estimate for FY 2007 is for $1,575,681. The tuition for out-of-county students is estimated at $1,580,000.

                • Cooperating with participating schools divisions to provide their qualified students the opportunity to attend the specialized high school.

                • Status that the Governor’s School designation provides in attracting corporate sponsors throughout the Northern Virginia area.

                • Greater visibility throughout the region, the Commonwealth, and the nation under the umbrella of the state initiative.

        ALTERNATIVE:
            The disadvantages of not continuing as a regional school are loss of state funding and possible disruption of relationships with the Virginia Department of Education and the participating school divisions.

          Regulation 3009.8, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents. Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee. Two appointees will participate at each of the three levels of challenged materials: elementary, middle, and high school. The level will be determined by a random selection. If the deliberations involve multiple levels, a randomly selected School Board appointee from each involved level will participate.

          The student representative to the School Board appoints an individual to serve on the Interdepartmental Review Committee for challenges that involve high school materials.
          Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in Regulation 3009.8

          SUBJECT: Curriculum Advisory Committees

          RECOMMENDATION:
            That the School Board appoint individuals to serve on the English Language Arts, Family Life Education, Fine Arts, Foreign Language, Health and Physical Education, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies Curriculum Advisory Committees, and on the High School Course Review Committee, as listed in the agenda item.
          EXPLANATION:
            Regulation 1707.6, Curriculum Advisory Committees, establishes eight curriculum advisory committees for levels K through 12 to advise on Program of Studies development and implementation.
            Additionally, this agenda item covers appointments to the High School Course Review Committee, which annually identifies standard or optional course offerings for deletion, consolidation, or revision and makes recommendations for action by the Division Superintendent and the School Board (see the current version of Regulation 3202, Approval of New Courses and Deletion or Consolidation of Existing Courses).

            The term of service for all School Board member representatives on the above-named advisory committees is one year. These terms will be effective until June 30, 2007. Representatives may be reappointed. In addition, classroom teachers are elected to these committees and serve two-year terms, with elections being held during the spring of odd-numbered years. Other committee members serve terms as set forth in the appropriate regulation.
