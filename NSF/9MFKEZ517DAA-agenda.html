Autism Awareness Month - April 2015

 

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.


Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about autism. It is sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA). The theme is “A Better World for Autism.” Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

 

 

Mathematics Awareness Month - April 2015

Mathematics Awareness Month is held each year in April to increase public understanding of and appreciation for mathematics. Sponsored by the Joint Policy Board for Mathematics, the goal is to increase the visibility of mathematics as a field of study and to communicate the power and intrigue in mathematics to a larger audience.  The 2015 theme is “Math Drives Careers,” a focus on the different careers that rely on mathematics, including examples from individuals who use math in their everyday jobs.

Meeting Category:  March 26, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                  FY 2015 Third Quarter Budget Review

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2015 budget and to make revisions as necessary. The FY 2015 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 18, 2014.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund revenue adjustments reflect a decrease of $0.1 million in state revenue for National Board Certified Teacher (NBCT) pass-through payments based on the number of FCPS teachers eligible to receive a stipend from the state in FY 2015. This revenue adjustment is offset by a corresponding expenditure adjustment. While no adjustment is recommended at this time, state aid and sales tax revenue will continue to be monitored for the remainder of FY 2015. Sales tax receipts are currently trending higher than projected but are anticipated to be offset by state aid that is projected to be lower based primarily on the state’s enrollment estimates. All variances and funds available will be included in the FY 2015 Final Budget Review.

 

Recommended School Operating Fund expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $4.1 million and include the corresponding expenditure adjustment for NBCT; one-time savings of $2.8 million in placeholder funding set aside for full-day Mondays; and savings of $1.2 million for transportation fuel. 

 

Expenditure adjustments, combined with the revenue adjustment, result in available funds totaling $4.0 million. All of this is recommended to be set aside for the FY 2017 beginning balance.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure adjustments as reflected in the FY 2015 Third Quarter Budget Review and as detailed in the agenda item.

 

Proposed Amendment:

 

I move to amend the main motion by reducing the funding set aside to FY 2017 by $1.0 million and allocating $1.0 million in onetime funding to FY 2016 to increase the funding previously allocated to specifically reduce large elementary classes. (Jane Strauss)

School Board Action Required:  Decision

Key Points:

  • Greenbriar West Elementary School has more than 1,100 students, 265 students over capacity.
  • Greenbriar West has 600 students in the Advanced Academic Program (AAP) Center.
  • If no change is made, Greenbriar West will not be able to accept new students into the AAP Center.
  • Poplar Tree Elementary School has capacity for additional students.
  • If approved, the Poplar Tree AAP Center will serve students from Poplar Tree, Brookfield, and Cub Run elementary schools; the Greenbriar West Center will serve students from Greenbriar West, Greenbriar East, and Powell elementary schools; and the Bull Run Center will serve students from Bull Run, Virginia Run, Deer Park, London Towne and Centre Ridge elementary schools.

Recommendation: Level IV Advanced Academic Program Expansion

 

That the School Board authorize the establishment of a new advanced academic program (AAP) center in September 2015 at Poplar Tree Elementary School for the purpose of relieving severe overcrowding at Greenbriar West Elementary School; further move that the new center placements for AAP center eligible students at Poplar Tree ES begin at grade 3, with rising grade 4 students permitted to request a pupil transfer to the new Poplar Tree center, and with rising grade 5 and grade 6 students remaining at their current AAP centers (“grandfathered”); future center eligible siblings of students in the Greenbriar West center will not be granted pupil transfer consideration for center placement.

 

Proposed Amendment:

 

I move to amend the main motion by (a) inserting “and grade 4” after the phrase “begin at grade 3” and (b) deleting the phrase “with rising grade 4 students permitted to request a pupil transfer to the new Poplar Tree center” and inserting the phrase “that rising grade 4 students based at Cub Run ES who currently attend the Bull Run ES center be provided the option to remain at Bull Run or to request a pupil transfer to the new Poplar Tree center.”  The phrase would then read:  “further move that the new center placements for AAP center eligible students at Poplar Tree ES begin at grade 3 and grade 4, and that rising grade 4 students based at Cub Run ES who currently attend the Bull Run ES Center be provided the option to remain at Bull Run or to request a pupil transfer to the new Poplar Tree center.” (Kathy Smith)

6.01 School Board Member Salary - Recommendation that, effective January 1, 2016, the annual salary for each member of the Fairfax County School Board be set at $37,500, and that the School Board Chairman be paid an additional annual salary of $2,000 (SB; action scheduled 4/9/15)
Meeting
Mar 26, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 16
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

Contact:                 Ilryong Moon, At Large Board Member

 

Meeting Category:  March 26, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                 School Board Member Salary

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

According to the Code of Virginia, any change in the salary of School Board members is required to be approved by the School Board prior to July 1 in the year in which there is a School Board election and by a recorded vote of a majority of the School Board members present at a public meeting.  Moreover, such change only affects the salary of the School Board members elected in the new election (for example, the election is scheduled in November 2015, and those elected will take office effective January 1, 2016).  A sitting school board cannot raise the salary for its own members and, therefore, the salary increases being proposed are for the next School Board (2016-2019 term) and not the current School Board (2012-2015 term). 

 

By law, the salary of the members of the Fairfax County School Board shall not exceed the salary of the members of the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors.  Additionally, the School Board may vote to pay its chairman an additional salary not to exceed $2,000 per year. On March 3, 2015, the County Board of Supervisors voted to increase the supervisors' salary to $95,000 (an increase from $75,000) and its chairman's to $100,000 (an increase from $75,000), effective January 2016.   Salaries for School Board members were last increased in 2007 from $12,000 to $20,000 per year, and prior to that in 1999 from $8,000 to $12,000 per year.  If a salary increase is not approved by this School Board by June 30, 2015, the next opportunity will not be available for another four years.

 

Board members are responsible for setting general school policy, approving the annual budget, and hiring the Superintendent.  Board members spend many hours attending Board, school, PTA, and community meetings, visiting schools, reviewing meeting materials in preparation for Board meetings, attending required professional development programs, and communicating with students, parents, staff, and community members. 

 

Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) is the 10th largest school district in the nation and with over 185,000 students is the largest school district in Virginia, more than twice as large as the next largest Virginia district.  The School Board members are responsible for overseeing an annual budget of more than $2.5 billion and serving over 1.1 million constituents.  FCPS is also the second largest employer in the Commonwealth of Virginia.

 

Recommendation:

 

That, effective January 1, 2016, the annual salary for each member of the Fairfax County School Board be set at $37,500, and that the School Board Chairman be paid an additional annual salary of $2,000.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
