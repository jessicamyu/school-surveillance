Autism Awareness Month - April 2014

 

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.


Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about autism. It is sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA). The 2014 theme is “A Better World for Autism.” Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

 

 

Mathematics Awareness Month - April 2014

Mathematics Awareness Month is held each year in April to increase public understanding of and appreciation for mathematics. Sponsored by the Joint Policy Board for Mathematics, the goal is to increase the visibility of mathematics as a field of study and to communicate the power and intrigue in mathematics to a larger audience.  The 2014 theme is “Mathematics, Magic, and Mystery,” a celebration of the use of mathematics in everything from magic squares to Mobius bands to magical card tricks and illusions, engaging people in creative and rational thinking via brain-teasing challenges.

 

 

National Volunteer Week - April 6-13, 2014

 

Established in 1974, National Volunteer Week has grown exponentially in scope each year since, drawing the support and endorsement of all subsequent U.S. presidents, governors, mayors and other respected elected officials.

 

National Volunteer Week is about inspiring, recognizing, and encouraging people to seek out imaginative ways to engage in their communities. It’s about demonstrating to the nation that by working together, in unison, we have the fortitude to meet our challenges and accomplish our goals. National Volunteer Week is about taking action, encouraging individuals and their respective communities to be at the center of social change—discovering and actively demonstrating their collective power to foster positive transformation.

 

During the 2012-2013 school year, 195 schools, centers, and alternative high schools reported that 55,138 volunteers donated 807,251 hours of volunteer service to FCPS schools.  If those volunteers had been paid at the entry-level instructional assistant salary of $14.88 per hour, the bill for 2012-2013 would have totaled over $12 million dollars. The educational value of the work performed by volunteers is, of course, incalculable.

National Library Week/School Library Month - April 2014

 

National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association and libraries across the country each April.  It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate.  This year, National Library Week will be observed April 13-19 with the theme, "Lives Change @ your library.”

 

School Library Month is the American Association of School Librarians' celebration of school librarians and their programs. Every April school librarians are encouraged to create activities to help their school and local community celebrate the essential role that strong school library programs play in a student's educational career.

 

The School Board welcomes this opportunity to commend Fairfax County Public Schools’ librarians and to express its gratitude for the energy, knowledge, and vision that they tirelessly and unselfishly provide to the school system.

Staff Contact:         Terri Breeden, assistant superintendent, Professional Learning and Accountability

 

Meeting Category:  March 20, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                 Alternative Accreditation Plans Requests for Key and Kilmer Centers, Bryant and Mountain View Alternative High Schools, and Fairfax Adult High School

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:            Student Achievement Goal 1. Pursue Academic Excellence 

 

Key Points:

 

Every three years VDOE allows any “Special Purpose School” to submit an alternative accreditation plan.  Five schools in Fairfax County - Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School – once again are pursuing the approval of alternative accreditation plans in order to be able to be “Fully Accredited” in 2014-2015, 2015-2016, and 2016-2017.

 

Because the five schools listed above are classified as “Special Purpose” schools, serving unique populations of students for whom the measures of accreditation are inappropriate, staff in the Office of School Improvement at the Virginia Department of Education recommend that alternative accreditation plans be submitted to the Virginia Board of Education for approval.  This recommendation is based on the Regulations Establishing Standards for Accrediting Public Schools in Virginia, Section 8 VAC 20-131-280.C. The regulation states: 

 

"Subject to the provisions of 8 VAC 20-131-350, the governing school board of special purpose schools such as those provided for in § 22.1-26 of the Code of Virginia, Governor’s schools, special education schools, alternative schools, or career and technical schools that serve as the student's school of principal enrollment may seek approval of an alternative accreditation plan from the Board of Education. Schools offering alternative education programs and schools with a graduation cohort of 50 or fewer students as defined by the graduation rate formula adopted by the board may request that the board approve an alternative accreditation plan to meet the graduation and completion index benchmark. Special purpose schools with alternative accreditation plans shall be evaluated on standards appropriate to the programs offered in the school and approved by the board prior to August 1 of the school year for which approval is requested. Any student graduating from a special purpose school with a Standard, Advanced Studies, or Modified Standard Diploma must meet the requirements prescribed in 8 VAC 20-131-50."

 

Options:

 

 1. Approve the request for approval of the Alternative Accreditation Plans for Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School for submission to the Virginia Board of Education.

2. Continue to be evaluated under the current state accreditation regulations from the Virginia Department of Education and have Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School rated “Accredited with Warning” or “Provisionally Accredited-Graduation Rate” and undergo an academic review and prepare a school improvement plan in accordance with the guidelines adopted by the Virginia Board of Education, per Section 8 VAC 20-131-300.C of the Regulations Establishing Standards for Accrediting Public Schools in Virginia.   

 

Recommendation: 

 

That the School Board approve the Alternative Accreditation Plan requests for Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Fairfax Adult High School for submission to the Virginia Board of Education, as detailed in the agenda item.

6.02 Naming of School - Recommendation to name the campus at the Eastern Elementary School site "Baileys Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences" (Cluster 3; action scheduled 4/10/14)
Meeting
Mar 20, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 16
Category
6. New Business
Type
Information

[Posted 3/20/14]

Staff Contact:         Douglas A. Tyson, Assistant Superintendent Cluster 3

 

Meeting Category:  March 20, 2014 – Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                  Naming of School - Eastern Fairfax Elementary School site (Bailey's campus)

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Background:

 

On March 19, 2014, a meeting was held at Bailey’s Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences for the purpose of naming the elementary campus at the Eastern Fairfax Elementary School site.  Those present included Mason District School Board member Sandy Evans, Cluster 3 Assistant Superintendent Douglas Tyson, Cluster 3 Director Deirdre Lavery, Principal Marie Lemmon and representatives from the community and staff.  In accordance with Regulation 8170.6, public notice of the meeting included information on the FCPS website, e-mails and letters sent to parents in the affected communities. 

 

Key Points:

 

Parents, students, staff and community members attended the March 19 meeting to consider and recommend names for the new campus.  As residents checked in to vote, they were given six different-colored dots.  Three would be used in the first round using a weighted system to vote on the entire list of names recommended for the new campus.  The fourth and fifth dots would be used in a second round of weighted voting, if it was needed.  After presentations, questions and audience discussion, a total of eight names were recommended and the voting process was explained as follows:

  • Each voter would place their first three dots on their first, second, and third choices.
  • They could vote only once for any particular name.
  • Participants voted for their top three choices using a weighted point system (first choice = 3 points, second choice = 2 points, and third choice = 1 point). 

The three names that received the highest point total during the first round of voting were Bailey’s Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences, Bailey’s Rising Tigers Elementary School and Bailey’s Elementary – Thurgood Marshall School for the Arts and Sciences.  The complete set of data derived from the first round of voting is listed below.  Sixty three parents, staff, and community members registered to vote.

 

First Round of Voting

 

Bailey’s Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 49 points

Bailey’s Elementary School – West Campus – 63 points

Bailey’s Corners Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 45 points

Bailey’s Elementary Rock Stars – 39 points

Bailey’s Tigers School – 8 points

Bailey’s Rising Tigers Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 60 points

Bailey’s Elementary – Thurgood Marshall School for the Arts and Sciences – 90 points

Bailey’s Fort Buffalo School for the Arts and Sciences – 18 points

 

Using their fourth and fifth dots, each voter was asked to vote for one name.  Each dot represented one vote.  Votes were weighted.  First choice =3 points, second choice=2 points.

 

Second Round of Voting

 

Bailey’s Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 99 points

Bailey’s Rising Tigers Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 101 points

Bailey’s Elementary – Thurgood Marshall School for the Arts and Sciences – 103 points

 

Using their sixth and final dot, each voter was to vote for one name.  There was no weighting.

 

Third Round of Voting

 

Bailey’s Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 29 votes

Bailey’s Rising Tigers Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 7 votes

Bailey’s Elementary – Thurgood Marshall School for the Arts and Sciences – 23 votes

 

Because the vote was so close for the top two, the consensus in the room was to have a run off of the top two choices.  All voters in the room were recertified and each voter was given one more voting dot which they used to select their top choice.

 

Run Off

 

Bailey’s Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences – 38 votes

Bailey’s Elementary – Thurgood Marshall School for the Arts and Sciences – 25 votes

 

It should be noted that the name for the existing Bailey’s Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences will remain intact and will not be impacted by the name of the new campus.  The new campus is scheduled to open in September 2014.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board name the campus at the Eastern Elementary School site “Bailey’s Upper Elementary School for the Arts and Sciences.”

Subject:                   Award of Contract – West Potomac High School Turf Field Project

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:              School Board Policy 5011.2 Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

Funding for this project will be provided by the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the Fairfax County School Board.  Specifications have been prepared and the project has been advertised.  Bids will be received on March 25, 2014.  A bid tabulation and recommendation for award will be furnished prior to the April 10, 2014, School Board meeting.

That the School Board award a contract for the West Potomac High School Turf Field Project to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
