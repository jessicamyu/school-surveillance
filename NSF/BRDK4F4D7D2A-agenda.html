National School Lunch Week, October 12-16, 2020

 

National School Lunch Week is officially scheduled for October 12-16 and was started by President John F. Kennedy in 1962 to promote the importance of a healthy school lunch in a child's life and the ian act of Congress. Using the theme "Now Playing: School Lunch!", spotlights the wide variety of flavors, dishes, delivery options and tastes that can be customized in today’s school lunch.

 

National School Lunch Week promotes excitement about the benefits of students consuming wholesome, healthy school lunches of fresh fruits and veggies, whole grains and milk. The 2020 event is designed to help students and school nutrition professionals connect and share with parents, school officials, the media and the public what each loves most about school lunches. The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own, nutrition habits in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

 

 

 

Disability Employment and Awareness Month - October 2020

 

Did you know that October is National Disability Employment and Awareness Month (NDEAM)? This year’s theme is “Increasing Access and Opportunity, Celebrating 30 years of the American Disability Act and 75 years of National Disability Employment Awareness Month."

Each year, Fairfax County Public Schools participates in the NDEAM which promotes career development for individuals with disabilities through hands-on career exploration and job shadowing in the following fields: Administrative Support, Animal Care, Business Management, Communications, Information Technology, and Parks and Recreation.

 

NDEAM strives to encourage students to explore potential career or employment opportunities, to learn more about businesses in their community, and begin to plan to achieve more successful employment outcomes.

According to the National PTA, "negative attitudes toward lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender students puts them at an increased risk for violence and bullying. Negative attitudes also impact a student's ability to learn at school."

 

LGBT History Month is recognized in October nationwide and in FCPS to acknowledge and celebrate that LGBT students, staff, and families are an integral component of our nation, and of Fairfax County Public Schools.

 

This special month was first recognized in 1994; October was chosen to commemorate National Coming Out Day on October 14, and the First March on Washington for Gay and Lesbian Rights on October 14, 1979.

 

One of the first Gay-Straight Alliances in Virginia was founded at Hayfield High School in 2000, and now students are involved in clubs at many of the high schools in the county, welcoming not only LGBT students but their friends and those interested in creating safe schools.

 

More than a dozen Fairfax County schools have active Gender and Sexuality Alliances or Gay Straight Alliances through which students are able to network, socialize and educate their communities about inclusion of the LGBTQ community in our schools. Students do this through forums, posters, announcements and activities such as ally week. The existence of GSAs is an asset to our schools. Educational research indicates that schools which have GSAs have better mental health outcomes for all students. The equity liaisons in our schools also help to make our schools more welcoming for all students and staff.

 

In addition to student clubs, Fairfax County Public Schools boasts an organization for LGBTQ employees, parents of LGBTQ students, and LGBTQ family members. FCPS Pride holds monthly coffee houses and an annual picnic for the community and supports activities such as holiday celebrations and the LGBTQ prom.

 

LGBT History Month in October is an opportunity for Fairfax County Public Schools staff and students to celebrate, and to learn more about, these important constituents in our schools and neighborhoods.

National School Bus Safety Week, October 19-23, 2020

 

The week of October 19-23 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. Based on the theme “Red Lights Mean STOP!”, ​the week focuses on student safety when waiting for and boarding a school bus. School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 144,000 students in over 1,625 school buses on more than 6,800 separate routes traveling over 16.8 million operational miles each year.  

 

Motorists are encouraged to practice patience and drive gently around school buses in our neighborhoods and school communities.

 

Thank you to Francine Furby and all the staff from the Department of Facilities and Transportation for their continued hard work to keep our children safe.

Subject: 2021 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact: Michael Molloy, director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category: October 8, 2020 - Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement:

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options; sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

The School Board annually adopts a State and Federal Legislative Program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community. The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board annually considers additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) to the Board’s previous year legislative positions. These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate. Existing legislative positions have historically carried over from year to year, unless specifically changed by the Board.

 

The presentation will provide the Board with an overview of the existing Legislative Program structure and positions, highlight initial changes which can be made due to previous year policy actions, and preview legislative and policy issues the Board may wish address in Legislative Program position language.

 

The Board will discuss any proposed staff recommended changes as well as Board-directed changes to the FSCB 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for October 20, 2020. Final Action on the Legislative Program is scheduled for November 5, 2020.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board receive the Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program presentation.

Type

Subject:  2021 FCSB State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Staff Contact:  Michael Molloy, director of Government Relations

 

Meeting Category:  October 8, 2020 - Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4, Resource Stewardship

 

 

Problem Statement: 

Fairfax County Public Schools is both directly and indirectly impacted by policy and budget decisions made by local, state, and federal officials. As such, it is critical that FCPS provide input to policymakers as they debate various policy options; sharing the possible programmatic and financial impacts of legislative and policy decisions on Fairfax students, teachers, staff and the broader community.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

The School Board annually adopts a legislative program and legislative priorities to inform local, state and federal public officials and others of its positions on legislative policy and budget issues of potential importance to Fairfax County Public School students, teachers, staff, and community.  The program contains the School Board’s positions on state and federal issues of major and current significance.

 

The Fairfax County School Board 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program will include additions, deletions, and formatting changes (if applicable) from the Board’s 2020 legislative positions.  These changes and updates are intended to adjust position statements to reflect actions taken over the past year by state and federal governments; to reflect the Board’s current approaches to policy debates and issues; and to clarify and simplify existing position language where appropriate.

 

The Board will discuss proposed staff recommended changes as well as Board-directed changes to the FSCB 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program at a Legislation work session scheduled for October 20, 2020.  In addition, the Board will consider both state and federal legislative priorities.

 

Full draft text as well as a complete table of proposed changes to the draft 2021 State and Federal Legislative Program will be included as part of the Board’s October 20 Work Session agenda item.  Final Action on this agenda item is scheduled for November 5, 2020.

 

Recommendation:

That the Board adopt the 2021 Fairfax County School Board State and Federal Legislative Program

 

Attachments: Documents will be available after the Board’s October 20 Work Session to reflect the Work Session discussion and provided prior to Board Final Action on November 5.
