    RESOLUTION NAMING THE ANIMAL CARE LAB
    AT BURKE SCHOOL
    “THE SUSAN KNECHT ANIMAL CARE LAB”

    WHEREAS, Mrs. Susan Knecht served Fairfax County Public Schools with distinction for 20 years as a psychologist, coordinator of student services, and principal until her retirement effective December 2004; and

    WHEREAS, she demonstrated leadership, loyalty, insight, and a professional commitment with colleagues, staff members, and particularly special education students and their families, and, throughout her career, her enthusiastic personality supported them all and helped them grow; and

    WHEREAS, she ensured the best possible education for all her students through her unique ability to inspire high expectations and collaboration among her staff members, resulting in a high level of professionalism and a commitment to innovation to benefit special education students through her constant encouragement, support, and modeling that made her a role model for teachers who sought to emulate her devotion to children and to special education; and

    WHEREAS, she created a family within the school community, unifying children, parents, faculty members, and the local community to accomplish her goal-- reaching, touching, and teaching each and every child; and

    WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools has been served admirably through the tireless efforts, exemplary skills, creative talents, and pure dedication of Susan Knecht;

    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the Burke School animal care lab “The Susan Knecht Animal Care Lab” in honor of her endless dedication and commitment to special education children and their families, to her loyal staff members, and to all those whose lives she touched.

    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 7:30 and 8 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    4.03 Pohick School Site - Schedule and provide notice of a public hearing to be held on September 23, 2004, in connection with school system staff's recommendation to declare as surplus and thereafter to sell the Pohick school site, as detailed in the agenda item.
    Meeting
    Sep 9, 2004 - Regular Meeting No. 3
    Category
    4. Other Action Items
    Type
    Action

    Staff Contact: Thomas M. Brady, Chief Operating Officer

    SUBJECT: Pohick School Site

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board schedule and provide notice of a public hearing to be held on Thursday, September 23, 2004, at 8:00 p.m. at Luther Jackson Middle School, 3020 Gallows Road, Falls Church, Virginia, in connection with school system staff's recommendation to declare as surplus and thereafter to sell the Pohick school site, with the proceeds from such sale being retained and accrued to the school system's capital improvement fund for use for new school construction and school renovation projects in accordance with Section 22.1-129(A) of the Code of Virginia, as amended.
      EXPLANATION:

          The School Board is the owner of approximately 35 acres of undeveloped real property located in the Springfield Magisterial District near South Run District Park known as the Pohick School Site. The land was dedicated to the School Board in 1967 but, due to subsequent disputes between the parties, was not conveyed to the School Board until 1989. The deed of conveyance contained restrictions to the effect that if the site was sold or developed for a purpose other than a secondary or high school, title to the site would revert back to the party that conveyed it to the School Board. Recently, school system staff and the successor-in-interest to the original conveying party have had discussions about the possible sale of the site. Negotiations are ongoing.

          The sale of this residentially-zoned parcel would generate significant revenue, which would then be available to meet urgent school facilities needs. Under applicable law, a School Board property must be declared surplus, following a public hearing, in order to be sold.

          If the School Board members wish to do so, the School Board could vote at the October 7, 2004, regular School Board meeting to declare the property surplus and to authorize the sale of the site on such terms as the School Board approves.

      SUBJECT: Contract Amendment - Energy Performance Contracting Services (Bundle No. 4)

      RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board further amend the contract for the Energy Performance Services with Noresco, LLC, dated November 8, 2002, to include improvements at additional buildings; enter into a lease/purchase financing agreement for such services and improvements with a recognized commercial financing company, all on the terms negotiated by school officials, with the advice of the School Board attorney; and authorize Thomas M. Brady, chief operating officer; or Dean Tistadt, assistant superintendent of facilities and transportation services, to execute the agreements and related documents for these services/improvements and the lease/purchase financing on behalf of the School Board.

      EXPLANATION:
        On November 7, 2002, the School Board awarded a contract for energy performance services to Noresco, LLC for 14 schools, referred to as Bundle No. 1 (the “contract”). Work at those schools is proceeding satisfactorily. Subsequently, on May 22, 2003, the School Board approved the amendment of the contract to add improvements for 7 additional schools, which are referred to as Bundle No. 2; and on September 11, 2003, an additional 20 buildings, which are referred to as Bundle No. 3. The contract expressly contemplates that the scope of work may be modified to include additional schools upon terms mutually agreeable to the parties.

        The 19 school locations and 1 center location to be added at this time, as Bundle No. 4, are Bren Mar Park, Crossfield, Cub Run, Forestdale, Hollin Meadows, Lees Corner, Little Run, Mount Vernon Woods, Rose Hill, Silverbrook, Springfield Estates, Westgate, Weyanoke, White Oaks, and Woodley Hills Elementary Schools, Twain and Frost Middle Schools, Falls Church and Langley High Schools, and Sideburn Support Center. This contract amendment, like the previous bundles, will be based on a detailed energy audit for energy performance services, encompassing lighting retrofits and energy management controls. These improvements will be completed as part of the multiple bundles that will be self-funded from energy savings generated from energy conservation measures. Staff may recommend that additional schools be included in the future.
