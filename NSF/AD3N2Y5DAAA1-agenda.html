Subject:  ERFC Options

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS hired Aon Hewitt, a provider of retirement consulting and actuarial services, to conduct a study which benchmarked FCPS’ retirement benefit levels to surrounding school divisions, Fairfax County and the federal government.  The study also included the adequacy of income replacement at retirement provided by the retirement programs that FCPS offers.

 

On May 12, 2016, ERFC presented the history of the FCPS retirement programs along with the key provisions of the existing plans.  Aon Hewitt then provided a briefing on the results of its study, illustrating retirement benefit levels for FCPS employees at various age and service combinations with comparisons to the peer group.  Aon Hewitt also presented ERFC options for possible future consideration.

 

On July 21, 2016, as a follow-up to the May work session and as requested by the School Board, the benefit impact on new hires of the options for possible consideration were presented.  On September 19, 2016, the employer cost savings associated with those options for new hires was discussed.  On November 17, 2016, the employer cost savings associated with those options for new hires and non-vested employees was discussed.  On December 5, 2016, the employer cost savings associated with expanding those options to include all members except those currently eligible for full service retirement was discussed.

 

At this work session, the employer cost savings associated with those same options except increasing the minimum retirement age from age 55 to age 60 for new hires and non-vested employees will be discussed.
