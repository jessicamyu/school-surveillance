Key Points:  FCPS staff will present the revised FCPS Student and Staff Health and Wellness Policy (5008.2). This policy has been developed in close collaboration with the School Health Advisory Committee (SHAC) and is in compliance with Public Law 111.296, Healthy, Hunger-Free Kids Act of 2010. Subject:   Proposed Woodlawn, Fort Belvoir, and Woodley Hills

                Elementary Boundary Adjustments/Pupil Assignment Plans

 

School Board Action Required:   Decision

 

Recommendation #1 - From Woodlawn ES to Fort Belvoir ES

 

Background:

 

Students who live in Fort Belvoir base housing are assigned currently to both Fort Belvoir ES and Woodlawn ES because Fort Belvoir ES is not large enough to accommodate all students who reside in base housing. Woodlawn Village is the name of the base housing community that is assigned to Woodlawn ES. In SY 2015-16, 283 FCPS PreK-6th graders live in Woodlawn Village.

 

In 2013, FCPS was presented with the opportunity to apply for a federal grant for funding to construct a new school to serve students who reside on Fort Belvoir. The grant was approved and awarded in 2014. The new school, Fort Belvoir Upper Elementary School, will be an expansion of the existing school and will open for students in SY 2016-17. The new additional capacity will enable Fort Belvoir ES to accommodate all students who reside in base housing. 

 

Key Points:

 

The Division Superintendent recommends this proposal for school boundary adjustments and pupil assignment plans to reassign students living in Woodlawn Village from Woodlawn ES to Fort Belvoir Elementary School beginning with SY 2016-17. With this, all students who live in base housing will be accommodated in the two FCPS facilities located on the base: students in grades K-3 attend the current Fort Belvoir Elementary School; the newly completed building will house students in grades 4-6.

 

Discussion:

 

Region and Facilities staff hosted a community meeting at Fort Belvoir ES on February 5, 2016. The community is generally supportive of this proposed boundary adjustment. Some members of the community requested that FCPS allow students who attend Woodlawn ES in SY 2015-16 to remain at this school if their families have military transfer orders that will take place during SY 2016-17.  

 

FCPS transportation will be provided during SY 2016-17 from Woodlawn Village to Woodlawn ES in order to accommodate sixth grade students who may remain at Woodlawn ES in SY 2016-17 due to “boundary phase-in.” In addition, siblings of such 6th grade students also may choose to remain at Woodlawn ES for SY 2016-17 only. Lastly, students who reside in Woodlawn Village and have military orders to transfer during SY 2016-17 will be allowed to remain at Woodlawn ES and will be eligible for FCPS school bus transportation. All other PreK–5th grade students who reside in Woodlawn Village will be assigned to Fort Belvoir ES with FCPS transportation in SY 2016-17.

 

See Attachment B (phasing chart).

 

Recommendation:

 

That, following a public hearing, the School Board approve redistricting of school boundaries and adoption of pupil assignment plans to effect the reassignment of elementary students, who reside in Woodlawn Village, from Woodlawn ES to Fort Belvoir ES effective for the 2016-17 school year, in order to contribute to the efficiency of the school division and to increase instructional effectiveness by, among other things, balancing enrollment and utilization of school facilities; all pursuant to applicable law, School Board policies, and division regulations (including Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4); Policy 8130.7, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings; and Regulation 2230.12, Exceptions for Attendance at Other Than Base School and Procedure for Applying for Intracounty Exceptions).

 

 

Recommendation #2 - From Woodley Hills ES to Woodlawn ES

 

Background:

 

As a result of the foregoing school boundary adjustment and pupil assignment plan, reassigning  students from Woodlawn ES to Fort Belvoir ES, more than 200 seats will become available in the newly renovated Woodlawn ES in SY 2016-17.

 

Key Points:

 

The Division Superintendent recommends this proposal for school boundary adjustments and pupil assignment plans to reassign elementary students residing in Mallard Court Apartments from  Woodley Hills ES to Woodlawn ES. In SY 2015-16, 122 elementary aged students reside in Mallard Court Apartments, a complex which is in close proximity to Woodlawn ES.

 

Discussion:

 

Region and Facilities staff hosted a community meeting on April 19, 2016, and another is scheduled to be held on May 5, 2016, to continue to elicit feedback from the community about this boundary adjustment. In general, the community seems satisfied with this recommendation.

 

FCPS transportation will be provided during SY 2016-17 from Mallard Court Apartments to Woodley Hills ES in order to accommodate sixth grade students who may remain at Woodley Hills ES in SY 2016-17 due to “boundary phase-in.” In addition, siblings of these 6th grade students also may choose to remain at Woodley Hills ES for SY 2016-17 only. All other PreK–5th grade students who reside in Mallard Court Apartments will be assigned to Woodlawn ES with FCPS transportation in SY 2016-17. Like Woodley Hills ES, Woodlawn ES has a FECEP/HS program and SACC program.

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)                                                                       

 

Other Staff Present:   Alice Reilly, PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, ISD

                                                                       

Meeting Category:  May 26, 2016, Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:  Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee Members

 

School Board Action Required: Information                      

 

Key Points:

 

This committee will review and recommend instructional materials for use in grade K-12 social studies courses. These materials will be considered basal materials.

 

The Constitution of Virginia gives the Virginia Board of Education the authority to adopt instructional materials for use in the schools. Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Regulation 3004.3 Adopted Basal Instructional Materials incorporates state adoption guidelines and requirements and specifies procedures for establishing committees to review textbooks and other basal instructional materials and make recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board for local adoption. Each FCPS review committee is composed as follows:

 

• Teachers 50%

• School-based administrators 25%

• Community Members 25%

 

Central office staff members serve as nonvoting, ex-officio members to guide and assist the work of the committee.

 

• Each School Board member shall appoint one community member to the Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee by Friday, June 17, 2016.

• Participating staff members will be from the Instructional Services Department and the Department of Student Services.

• School based administrators will be nominated by Region Assistant Superintendents and principal associations.

• Teacher representatives will be selected from applications submitted to Instructional Services.

• Basal instructional materials adoption committee meeting dates are scheduled for:

  • August 11, 18, and 25 from 4:00 to 9:00 pm
  • September 12 from 4:00 pm to 9:00 pm

 

New Social Studies Standards of Learning have been adopted by the Virginia Department of Education and textbook contracts expire June 2017. To address these changes, the Social Studies textbook adoption review process will begin August 2016.

                         

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board appoint committee members to the Social Studies Basal Instructional Materials Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.

 

Names for committee members should be sent to Alice Reilly, Coordinator, K-12 Social Studies at alreilly@fcps.edu

Meeting Category:    May 26, 2016 – Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                     Award of Contract – Boilers Replacement at Bren Mar Park Elementary School

                              

School Board Action Required:   Information

 

Related To:                School Board Policy 5011.3 Authority to Contract

               

Key Points:

 

Funding for this project will be provided from the Fairfax County Infrastructure Replacement and Upgrade Funds. The Office of Facilities Management (OFM) has an urgent requirement to replace the boilers, associated pumps and expansion tanks (collectively, the “Boilers Replacement Project”) at Bren Mar Park Elementary School in order to maintain the reliability of the heating system in the school building.  The above-referenced system components have exceeded their useful life.  The reliability of the boilers is unpredictable, and the cost of continuing to maintain them can be expected to increase significantly if they remain in use.  Because the Boilers Replacement Project must be completed prior to the 2016-17 heating season, and because of the long lead time for ordering certain items, there is insufficient time to use the traditional design-bid-install methodology.

 

Therefore, through cooperative procurement as well as a joint procurement agreement with the County pursuant to the Code of Virginia and to the Joint Purchasing Resolution, OFM proposes to utilize the contractor, which was awarded the competitively procured, Fairfax County Job Order Contract #4400001417.  In accordance with Policy 5011.3, a specific recommendation for award will be furnished prior to the June 9, 2016, School Board meeting, when this item is scheduled for action.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the contract for the boilers replacement at Bren Mar Park Elementary School to the contractor and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

SCYPT members include high-level leaders from the County Government, Fairfax County Public Schools, and the community.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
