National Women's History Month - March 2009
          To honor the originality, beauty, imagination, and multiple dimensions of women's lives, the National Women's History Project has chosen "Women Taking the Lead to Save Our Planet" as the 2009 theme for National Women's History Month. Rachel Carson, the founder of the contemporary environmental movement, will serve as the iconic model of the theme.


Purchasing Month - March 2009
          Public procurement and fixed asset management professionals support and enhance the delivery of government services by promoting ethical purchasing practices. Public purchasing involves managing goods, establishing sources and shipping through inventory and warehousing, disposing of surplus items, and determining which product or service to buy to meet a specific need at the lowest possible price within the required deadline without sacrificing quality or service; and

          Public purchasing and materials management professionals in Fairfax County Public Schools, through professional development and certification from accredited programs, are dedicated to pursuing efficiency in school system operations and to providing the highest value for taxpayers’ dollars.

    Read Across America Day - March 3, 2009

    National Education Association's (NEA) Read Across America is an annual reading motivation and awareness program that calls for every child in every community to celebrate reading on or around Dr. Seuss's birthday.

    In cities and towns across the nation, teachers, teenagers, librarians, politicians, actors, athletes, parents, grandparents, and others develop NEA's Read Across America activities to bring reading excitement to children of all ages. Governors, mayors, and other elected officials recognize the role reading plays in their communities with proclamations and floor statements. Athletes and actors issue reading challenges to your readers. Teachers and principals seem to be more than happy to dye their hair green or be duct-taped to a wall if it boosts their students' reading.

    Music in Our Schools Month - March 2009

    March has been officially designated by the National Association for Music Education for the observance of Music In Our Schools Month, the time of year when music education becomes the focus of schools across the nation.

    The purpose of Music In Our Schools Month is to raise awareness of the importance of music education for all children - and to remind citizens that school is where all children should have access to music. It also is an opportunity for music teachers to bring their music programs to the attention of the school and the community and to display the benefits school music brings to students of all ages.
    Youth Art Month - March 2009
            Art education contributes powerful educational benefits to all elementary and secondary students. Art education develops students' creative problem-solving and critical thinking abilities; teaches sensitivity to beauty, order, and other expressive qualities; gives students a deeper understanding of multicultural values and beliefs; and brings to life what students learn in other subjects.

            The Fairfax County School Board believes that support should be given to art teachers as they attempt to strengthen art education in schools and communities, and all citizens are urged to take an interest in and give full support to quality school art programs for children and youth.

    SUBJECT:     Proposed 2009-2010 Program/Attendance Area Adjustments

    TYPE:     Action

    BACKGROUND:

    In September 2008, the School Board authorized and directed staff to undertake two boundary studies: 1) Adjustments to school attendance areas to establish school boundaries for a new elementary school at the Coppermine site which includes Floris, Herndon, Hutchison, McNair and Oak Hill Elementary Schools and, 2) Adjustments to school attendance areas to establish school boundaries for a new elementary school at the Laurel Hill site which includes Gunston, Halley, Lorton Station, Newington forest and Silverbrook Elementary Schools.

    KEY POINTS:

    The purpose of the studies is to establish attendance areas for new elementary schools at the Coppermine and Laurel Hill sites, both of which are under construction and are scheduled to open in fall 2009. The new elementary schools will provide additional capacity to address overcrowding at the elementary level.

    BOUNDARY ADJUSTMENT RECOMMENDATIONS

    In October 2002, the School Board adopted revised Policy 8130.5 governing the adjustment of special program center locations and school/program attendance areas. The policy requires that, under most circumstances, conditions necessitating program location or attendance area adjustments be evaluated jointly with the affected communities. Solutions emerging from the “Community Boundary Town Meeting” process are to be recommended to the School Board for public hearing and action. The recommendations contained in this agenda item reflect input from a series of community town meetings conducted to review adjustments to attendance areas of existing schools. Meetings were announced via press release, mail outs, “backpack” and “Keep in Touch” notices to parents, and web-site postings. Citizen comment from each meeting was captured in summaries posted to the FCPS web page after each meeting. Scenario details (maps and impact tables) were similarly posted.

    The following section summarizes the reviews undertaken with affected communities and resulting boundary change recommendations. Detailed outlines of these recommendations describing the geographic areas and numbers of projected students are presented in Appendices A and B.

    SCHOOL ATTENDANCE AREA REALIGNMENTS

    Appendix A Boundary adjustments between Floris, Herndon, Hutchison, McNair, and Oak Hill Elementary Schools

    Realign attendance areas to establish school boundaries for a new elementary school at the Coppermine site.

    Appendix B Boundary adjustments between Gunston, Halley, Lorton Station, Newington Forest, and Silverbrook Elementary Schools

    Realign attendance areas to establish boundaries for a new elementary school at the Laurel Hill site.

    RECOMMENDATION:

    1. Approve adjustments to school attendance areas, effective for the 2009-2010 school year, related to the opening of a new elementary school at the Coppermine site, and the reassignment of Gifted and Talented (GT) Center eligible students from McNair Elementary, Floris Elementary, and the Coppermine elementary site, currently assigned to the Oak Hill Elementary GT Center, to the McNair Elementary GT Center, which will be established as part of this attendance area adjustment, as detailed in the agenda item in Appendix A, and pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4), and Policy 8130.5, Local School Boundaries—Program Assignments and School Closings.

    2. Approve adjustments to school attendance areas, effective for the 2009-2010 school year, related to the opening of a new elementary school at the Laurel Hill site as shown in Appendix B, and pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4), and Policy 8130.5, Local School Boundaries-Program Assignments and School Closings.

    Board Amendment (Dan Storck)

    I move the following amendment: Revise the staff recommendation in Appendix B as follows: 1) the Evans subdivision, the senior housing development adjacent to South County Secondary School, and the future Laurel Hill Adaptive Reuse Program Site reassigned from Halley Elementary to the Laurel Hill elementary site; 2) the areas generally south of Lorton Road and west of I-95 (Lorton Valley North, Curtis, Gunston Commons, Shirley Acres, Village at Lorton Valley, and the Green Ridge subdivisions) reassigned from Lorton Station Elementary to Halley Elementary; and 3) areas generally west of Richmond Highway and east of I-95, south and west of Hagel Circle (Lorton Station South, Gunston Square, Cranford James Estates, Gunston Hill, and Lorton Valley subdivisions) reassigned from Lorton Station Elementary to Laurel Hill Elementary.
