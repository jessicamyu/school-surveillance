RESOLUTION
NAMING THE LIBRARY AT
BUSH HILL ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
“THE DAVID READYHOUGH LIBRARY”

WHEREAS, David Readyhough, who retired in 1999 and passed away in August 2004, had proudly served Fairfax County Public Schools with distinction, dedication, and honor for over 25 years as a classroom teacher and an assistant principal and, for his last nine years in Fairfax County Public Schools, as the principal of Bush Hill Elementary School; and
WHEREAS, through his commitment to children and his belief that “it has to be right for the children,” Bush Hill is a child-centered school in which every student, no matter what his and her ability or disability, is a valued member of the school; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Readyhough was instrumental in the planning and the first two years of Bush Hill’s renovation that resulted in a beautiful school in which learning and student achievement could flourish; and
WHEREAS, Mr. Readyhough planned—through the renovation process—that the library should be the central area of the school where the search for and attainment of knowledge would be daily achieved in a technology-rich environment;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the Bush Hill Elementary School library “The David Readyhough Library” in recognition of his love of children, his commitment to their education and love of reading, and his tireless devotion to the school staff and community.

4.02 CTEMS Annual Plan and 2005-2006 Application for Career and Technical Education Funds - Approve the Career and Technical Education Management System (CTEMS) annual local education plan and the 2005-2006 Application for State and Federal Career Technical Education Funds (IS; presented as new business 3/31/05)

Category

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department

SUBJECT: CTEMS Annual Plan and 2005-2006 Application for Career and Technical Education Funds

RECOMMENDATION:
    That the School Board approve the Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) annual local education plan and the 2005-2006 Application for State and Federal Career and Technical Education funds.

EXPLANATION:
    The Career and Technical Education Management Systems (CTEMS) document provides the Virginia Department of Education with each local education agency’s (LEA) plan for providing and implementing career and technical education instruction to both public school and adult students. The CTEMS document specifies all of the activities and programs available to students as a result of local, state, and federal funds. The CTEMS document is prepared in the preset uniform format (including assurances, charts, and schedules) as provided by the Virginia Department of Education. Career and technical education in Fairfax County Public Schools is called Professional Technical Studies (and will be referenced as such through the CTEMS document).

    Changes to the previous 2004-2005 CTEMS document have been bolded throughout.

    Federal funding from the Carl D. Perkins Vocational Technical Act of 1998 (Perkins III), which is a continuing grant provided to all local education agencies (LEA) in the Commonwealth of Virginia, is available to assist school districts in providing elective career and technical programs for middle and high school students. Formal application for federal moneys is one component of the annual CTEMS document to the Virginia Department of Education.

    Schedule 2-B, page 52, of the CTEMS document specifies the expenditures of the Perkins federal grant in improving career and technical education programs according to the “Appropriate Use of Federal Funds” on page 67 of the document.

    Focusing on current priority needs for this school division, the CTEMS local plan continues a cycle in identifying the goals, objectives, and strategies for locally providing professional technical studies programs.

    Category
    4. Consent Agenda - 8 p.m.
    Type
    Action (Consent)
    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

    SUBJECT: Consent to Right-of-Way


    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board (i) consent to the vacation and abandonment of a portion of the Chichester Lane right-of-way adjacent to Fairhill Elementary School and accept title to a portion of the vacated area, (ii) authorize the granting of a sidewalk and utility easements on a portion of the vacated area, and (iii) authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute, on behalf of the School Board, the deeds and any necessary related documents, all as approved by School Board counsel, to accomplish these items.
    EXPLANATION:
        Chichester Lane currently is a dead-end street along the western property boundary of Fairhill Elementary School. With redevelopment of neighboring properties there no longer is the possibility that Chichester Lane will be extended as a through street. Neighboring property owners have requested Fairfax County to vacate and abandon a portion of the right-of-way and return the vacated right-of-way to the abutting property owners. The School Board would receive approximately 802 square feet of land. In addition, a cul-de-sac will be constructed and the sidewalk will be extended around the cul-de-sac and to the community to the north of the school, both of which will benefit the school. The School Board will need to grant a sidewalk easement to Fairfax County, who will be responsible for maintaining the sidewalk, and certain utility easements for facilities that currently exist in the portion of the Chichester Lane right-of-way that will be vacated.

    6.04 School Bond Referendum – Recommendation to adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 8, 2005, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $244,326,000 (FTS; Action 4/28/05)
    Meeting
    Apr 14, 2005 - Regular Meeting No.17
    Category
    6. New Business - 8:10 p.m.
    Type
    Information
    Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services


    SUBJECT: Proposed 2005 School Bond Referendum


    RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board adopt the resolution requesting the Board of Supervisors to approve and take appropriate action to place on the ballot for the November 8, 2005, general election a School Bond Referendum in the amount of $244,326,000.

    EXPLANATION:
        On January 27, 2005, the School Board adopted the FY2006-2010 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that identified school facility needs anticipated during the coming five-year period. Capital improvement programming provides the foundation for cost-effective investment and construction project management of facility needs identified throughout the planning period.
          Voter authorization to issue bonds is needed in November 2005 to provide construction funds for one new elementary school, planning funds for two new elementary schools, modular additions to two existing schools, and site acquisition for future school needs.

          The recommended 2005 referendum would provide funds to renovate older general education facilities, including eight elementary schools and one high school. The proposed referendum also includes funds to plan the renovation of eight elementary schools, one middle school, and one high school. These planning funds will prepare the school system to accelerate the pace of renovations at such time as additional capital improvement cash flow is approved by the Board of Supervisors.
            The proposed referendum also includes funds to continue significant upgrades of technology networks in our school facilities. Additional allowances have been made in the referendum for the following: roof, boiler and air conditioning replacement; Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) improvements; asphalt repaving, and security enhancements. Finally, the proposed package includes funding for bonding expenses and the cost of directing the design and construction of projects authorized through the referendum.
              The scale and content of this referendum have been significantly influenced by two considerations: the requirement to address overcrowding, as well as, renovation and infrastructure needs countywide; and current limits on the county’s ability to cash-flow debt.
                To facilitate the orderly financing of identified capital improvement needs, it is recommended that the Proposed 2005 School Bond Referendum be offered for voter approval in the November 8, 2005, general election. It is believed that this proposal is a well-balanced presentation of needs countywide. The revised amount reflects the additional $1 million for site acquisition per the School Board’s direction at the April 4, 2005, Facilities and Operations work session.

                A School Board work session on this proposal was held on April 4, 2005; a public hearing and School Board action is scheduled for April 28, 2005.
