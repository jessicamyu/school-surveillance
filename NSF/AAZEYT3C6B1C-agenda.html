National Dyslexia Awareness Month, October 2016

 

October is National Dyslexia Awareness Month.  Dyslexia is a language-based learning disability that results in people having difficulties learning to read, spell, write, and pronounce words. Recognizing the markers of dyslexia and intervening early helps to provide opportunities for future success. Over the next several months, the Office of Special Education Instruction in collaboration with Instructional Services, will be offering several opportunities to help raise awareness about dyslexia. On Friday October 21, the Parent Resource Center will host a Dyslexia Open House from 10:00-1:00. On December 7, January 11, and January 25, there will be additional workshops about dyslexia at the Parent Resource Center.  These workshops will highlight strategies to support reading, writing and math at home and strategies to work with schools to support students with dyslexia. Please check our FCPS web site to find additional resources, including, “Clues to Dyslexia,” which highlights both the strengths and difficulties students with dyslexia exhibit at each stage of their education.

 

 

The School Board will formally recognize National Dyslexia Awareness Month at the October 20 Regular School board Meeting.

 

 

 

National School Lunch Week, October 10-14, 2016

National School Lunch Week is officially scheduled for October 10-14 by an act of Congress. Using the theme Show Your Spirit, National School Lunch Week promotes excitement about the benefits of students consuming wholesome, healthy school lunches of fresh fruits and veggies, whole grains and milk.  The 2016 event will encourage school food professionals to use social media to spread the word about healthy school lunches.  The National School Lunch program is dedicated to the health and well-being of our nation’s children, and has been joined through the years by many other excellent child nutrition programs. There is a continued need for nutrition education and awareness of the value of school nutrition programs and the School Board encourages all residents to become aware and concerned about their children's, and their own, nutrition habits in hopes of achieving a more healthful citizenry for today and the future.

 

 

Teen Read Week, October 9-15, 2016

 

 Teen Read Week encourages teens to go beyond the traditional book and explore poetry, audiobooks, books about music, and more at their school and local library.  The 2016 theme Read for the fun of it encourages teens to escape their daily routines and pick up something fun to read – fantasy novels, science fiction, beach reads, travel books, or tales of adventure.  Teen Read Week has been observed since 1998 in an effort to engage teens in reading for pleasure and fun and to use the services at the library.  Research shows that teens who read for fun have better test scores and are more likely to succeed in the workforce.  The School Board encourages all FCPS teenage students to take advantage of the resources in their school and public library and to become lifelong readers and learners.

 

 

Mental Health Awareness Week, October 2-8, 2016

 

 

The National Alliance on Mental Illness or NAMI and participants across the country are raising awareness of mental illness during Mental Illness Awareness Week which is October 2 - 8.  Each year NAMI works to fight stigma, provide support, educate the public and advocate for equal care.  NAMI believes that mental illness issues are important to address year round, but highlighting these issues during Mental Health Awareness Week provides a time for people to come together and display the passion and strength of those working to improve the lives of the tens of millions of Americans affected by mental illness. This year’s theme is I am stigma free. Being Stigma Free means learning about and educating others on mental illness, focusing on connecting with people to see each other as individuals and not a diagnosis, and most importantly, taking action on mental health issues.

National School Bus Safety Week, October 17-21, 2016

 

The week of October 17-21 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week. Based on the theme Be Smart—Be Seen, I wait in a safe place!, the week focuses on student safety when waiting for and boarding a school bus.  School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders.

The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 141,000 students in over 1,600 school buses on more than 6,500 separate routes traveling nearly 17 million operational miles each year.

Related To:  Strategic Plan; Centers for Disease Control recommendation; System of Care Behavioral Health Blueprint

 

Key Points: 

  1. This is a new policy to establish guidelines for suicide prevention efforts, individual suicide risk assessment, and response and supports for students and staff following a death by suicide.
  2. Prevention-FCPS will provide:
    1. Training for staff and students on signs of depression and suicidal thinking and how to refer for help
    2. Wellness screenings

    2. Determination of risk
    3. Parent notification
    4. Referral to appropriate resources
  4. Postvention-FCPS will provide:
    1. Procedures for providing response to school community
    2. Identification of youth at risk
    3. Provision of grief counseling
    4. Parent notification and referral to appropriate resources as needed

Staff Contact:  Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Roger L. Ball, director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:  October 6, 2016 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted, on June 23, 2016, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution (“Purchasing Resolution”), following legislative changes resulting from the 2016 session of the General Assembly. Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

Virginia Code Changes Relevant to FCPS

 

  • Virginia grown food. Purchases under $100,000 for Virginia grown food are exempt from competitive solicitation. Page 24.
  • Employment Services Organizations. The County’s Small and Minority Business Enterprise Program actively and diligently promotes the procurement of goods and services from businesses such as small businesses and minority-owned and woman-owned businesses. The code change defines and adds employment services organizations to this group of businesses. Pages 7 and 29.

 

County’s Administrative Changes Relevant to FCPS

 

  • Construction definition updated. The definition of construction has been revised. Pages 6 and 38.
  • Exceptions to Requirement for Competitive Procurement: Litigation/Regulatory Proceedings. Exceptions to requirement for competitive procurement for litigation/regulatory proceedings have been clarified. Page 23.
  • Exceptions to Requirement for Competitive Procurement: Document applicability of exception for real estate transactions. Exceptions to requirement for competitive procurement for real estate transactions have been established. Acquisition of the use of meeting room and lodging room space is considered short term rental of portions of real property, which effectively is a real estate transaction, and therefore, the competitive requirements of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution do not apply. Page 24.
  • Contract Audit: Requirement applies to all contracts in excess of $10,000 (small purchase threshold), regardless of the method of procurement. All contracts and amendments in excess of $10,000 shall include a provision permitting the County to have access to and the right to examine any documents and other material involving transactions related to the contract or compliance with any clauses thereunder, for a period of three (3) years after final payment. Page 34.  

 

FCPS Conditions to the Purchasing Resolution:

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, and 2015 Purchasing Resolutions, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2016 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

  • The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia. The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

  • The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopts the newly revised 2016 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the conditions detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
