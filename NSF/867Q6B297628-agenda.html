RESOLUTION
DEDICATING THE MAIN OFFICE SERVICE ROAD
AT MOUNT VERNON HIGH SCHOOL
“VICTOR KAZLAUSKY WAY”

WHEREAS, Victor Kazlausky served Fairfax County Public Schools with distinction as an athletic director and an assistant principal for 25 years until his retirement in 1979; and

WHEREAS, he gave tireless commitment to Mount Vernon High School and leadership within the community and endless support of “Major Pride” even to the present day; and

WHEREAS, Mr. Kazlausky served as a role model for several generations of students and fellow faculty members through his positive demeanor and respect for others; and

WHEREAS, the school’s faculty and community members join in requesting that his contributions to Mount Vernon High School be acknowledged in a lasting tribute;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the main office service road at Mount Vernon High School “Victor Kazlausky Way” in recognition of Victor Kazlausky’s unselfish dedication and service to the Mount Vernon community.

    Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
    Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list opens at 9 a.m. on the Monday prior to the meeting. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. Sign-ups are taken by phone only. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between7:30 and 8 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

    4.05 Land Conveyance - Approve the use of 13.81 acres of School Board property, known as Springfield Village, for use as the new residential construction site for Fairfax County Public Schools Professional Technical Studies construction technology program in collaboration with the Foundation for Applied Technical Education Inc. (FATE) (FTS; presented as new business 1/27/05)
    Meeting
    Feb 10, 2005 - Regular Meeting No. 13
    Category
    4. Consent Agenda - 8:45 p.m.
    Type
    Action
    Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Transportation and Facilities Services

    SUBJECT: FATE Land Conveyance

    RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve the use of 13.81 acres of School Board property, known as Springfield Village, for use as the new residential construction site for Fairfax County Public Schools Professional Technical Studies construction technology program in collaboration with the Foundation for Applied Technical Education Inc. (FATE).
    EXPLANATION:
      In 1970, Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) called on its business and professional community for assistance in providing a unique vocational education program that incorporates “on demand” skills and expertise with an entrepreneurial component to better prepare students for a successful transition into the workplace. The result was FATE (formerly known as FAXVO), an educational program that incorporates professional partnering and student participation in collaborative learning environments and work-based situations.

      In early 2006 the current residential construction site, Condon Manor, will be fully built with the sale of the seventeenth home constructed by FCPS students. For more than thirty years students in FCPS have been building homes and working on construction projects supported by FATE.

      The Springfield Village site is located at the intersection of Spring Village Drive and Franconia Springfield Parkway and was acquired by the School Board by dedication in 1966.

      FATE has applied for, and been granted, county approval to build eighteen houses on the Springfield Village site. Development will occur in two phases.

      Historically, FATE has taken about twenty-four months to complete construction of a house, the typical square footage of which is 6,000. Thus, the new site will meet the long-term needs of this program.

    6.02 Naming of School - Recommendation to name the new secondary school to be opened in September 2005 at the South County site "South County Secondary School" (Cluster V; action scheduled 2/24/05)
    Meeting
    Feb 10, 2005 - Regular Meeting No. 13
    Category
    6. New Business - 10 p.m.
    Type
    Information
    Staff Contact: Betsy Fenske, Assistant Superintendent, Cluster V

    SUBJECT: Naming of School


    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board name the new secondary school to be opened in September 2005 at the South County site “South County Secondary School.”


    EXPLANATION:
        On February 7, 2005, a meeting was held at Halley Elementary School for the purpose of naming the new secondary school at the South County site. Those present included Mount Vernon District School Board member Daniel Storck, At Large School Board members Stephen Hunt and Janet Oleszek, Cluster V Coordinator Judy Matlock, Principal Dale Rumberger, and representatives from the community. In accordance with Regulation 8170.3, public notice of the meeting included community newspaper articles, information on the FCPS website, and letters sent to parents in the affected communities.

        The parents, students, staff members, and community members who attended the meeting recommended and considered a total of 55 names for the new school. By consensus, the list was narrowed to 37 recommended names (see attachment). After presentations, questions and audience discussion, the participants voted for their top three choices using a weighted point system (first choice = 3 points, second choice = 2 points, and third choice = 1 point). The name “South County Secondary School” received the most support.

    ALTERNATIVE: None Recommended
