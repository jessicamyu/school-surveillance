National Red Ribbon Week, October 17-23, 2010

 

National Red Ribbon Week was established in memory of Enrique Camarena, a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration agent who was killed in the line of duty while investigating a major drug cartel in Mexico.  To honor his battle against illegal drugs, friends and neighbors began to wear red badges of satin and some parents began to form coalitions.  National Red Ribbon Week grew from these grassroots efforts to form a national movement against the use of illegal drugs.  Its annual campaign encourages students to take a visible stand against substance abuse and make a personal commitment to follow a drug-free lifestyle.

 

 

Native American Heritage Month - November 2010

 

Native American Heritage Month started in 1915 as American Indian Day by the Boy Scouts of America.  Over the years, individual states recognized Native American days, but it wasn’t until 1990 that President George H.W. Bush designated November as National American Indian Heritage Month.  Since 1994, a presidential proclamation has proclaimed the month to be Native American Heritage Month in honor of the significant contributions made by the first Americans to the establishment and growth of the U.S.

Career center specialists, school counselors, and educators are employed to help students reach their full potential and successfully transition from school to further education or work and they are actively committed to helping students explore their abilities, strengths, interests, and talents as these traits relate to career awareness and development and lifelong planning.

Staff Contact:             Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services    

 

Other Staff Present:   Ronald A. Hull, acting director, Office of Procurement Services

 

Meeting Category:      October 21, 2010 - Regular Meeting No.6

 

Subject:                      Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution

 

School Board Action Required:     Information

 

Key Points:

 

   

The Fairfax County Board of Supervisors adopted on July 13, 2010, the current version of the Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, as a result of legislative changes enacted by the 2010 General Assembly actions.  Historically, the Fairfax County School Board also has annually adopted the newly revised Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, following its adoption by the Board of Supervisors.

 

During the 2010 session of the General Assembly, eleven (11) bills were approved relating to governmental procurement and/or contracts. Three of these bills modified the Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA) in a manner which affects the operations of Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS).  The remaining bills affected state agencies only or were not otherwise applicable to FCPS.    

 

2010 Changes to the VPPA relevant to FCPS

  • House Bill 789, Code of Virginia §2.2-4303, increases the maximum amount from $1 million to $1.5 million for which the use of competitive negotiations for construction contracts is permissable.
  • Senate Bill 567, Code of Virginia §2.2-4305, increases the amount from $30,000 to $50,000 for the required use of competitive bidding or competitive negotiations for state-aid construction projects.
  • House Bill 831, Code of Virginia §2.2-4311.2, adds requirements to include in every contract over $50,000 a provision that the contractor must be authorized to transact business in Virginia as a domestic or foreign business entity as required by the State Corporation Commission.

 

County’s Administrative Changes applicable to FCPS

 

In addition to revisions based on changes to the VPPA, which became effective as of July 1, 2010, school system staff recommends one (1) administrative change to the Purchasing Resolution:

  • Add Personal Conflicts of Interest section to Article 5, Ethics in County Contracting.

 

As was done in connection with the School Board’s adoption of the 2009 Purchasing Resolution, school system staff recommends that the School Board adopt the 2010 Purchasing Resolution, subject to the following two conditions:

 

1.  The “FCPS Construction Exception,” which is set forth in Article 1, Section 3(A)(2.) and Article 3, Section 1(2.) of the Purchasing Resolution and which is by definition an exception to the Purchasing Resolution, shall be revised to read as follows:

 

“The Fairfax County School Board shall be responsible for construction, related architectural and engineering services, related consulting services, maintenance, repair, and related services in connection with building, furnishing, equipping, renovating, maintaining, and operating the buildings and property of the school division in accordance with §22.1-79 of the Code of Virginia.  The Fairfax County School Board’s procurement, award, and execution of contracts under this provision shall be in accordance with the policies and regulations of the school division and with the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA.” 

 

[Note:  The following text is presently contained in the above-referenced Articles of the Purchasing Resolution adopted by the BOS, but this text would be excluded from the School Board’s adoption because it inaccurately describes the Superintendent’s authority:  “The school division’s Superintendent or his designee shall have the same authority as the County Purchasing Agent to execute and administer contracts.  Execution of contracts under this section shall be conducted under the rules and regulations established by the Fairfax County School Board in accordance with the mandatory sections in the Code of Virginia.”]

 

2.  The Fairfax County School Board reserves the right to procure and award contracts for goods, services, and insurance pursuant to its own policies and regulations and pursuant to the Code of Virginia, including the VPPA, where such is in the best interest of the school division.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the newly revised 2010 Fairfax County Purchasing Resolution, previously adopted by the Board of Supervisors, subject to the two conditions detailed in the agenda item.
