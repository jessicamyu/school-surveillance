National Volunteer Week - April 15-21, 2018

 

Established in 1974, National Volunteer Week has grown exponentially in scope each year since, drawing the support and endorsement of all subsequent U.S. presidents, governors, mayors and other respected elected officials.

 

National Volunteer Week is about inspiring, recognizing, and encouraging people to seek out imaginative ways to engage in their communities. It’s about demonstrating to the nation that by working together, in unison, we have the fortitude to meet our challenges and accomplish our goals. National Volunteer Week is about taking action, encouraging individuals and their respective communities to be at the center of social change—discovering and actively demonstrating their collective power to foster positive transformation.

 

During the 2016-2017 school year, 183 schools, centers, and alternative high schools reported that 68,076 volunteers donated 650,271 hours of volunteer service to FCPS schools.

3.04 Month of the Military Child Recognition

Apr 12, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Meeting Opening - 7 p.m.
Type
Presentation

April is the Month of the Military Child. It is a time to recognize our military and veteran-connected students for their service and sacrifice. Military kids sacrifice and serve our country in their own way. Here in Fairfax County, we are extremely fortunate to have 8,066 students who are part of a military family as well as part of our Fairfax County Public Schools family.

 

Military-connected children and youth live with numerous challenges presented by frequent moves, parental deployments, and a host of life transitions that include reintegration and dealing with profoundly changed parents. The following are just a few of the challenges military children face: moving and starting over on an average of every 1-3 years, attending 6-9 different schools from kindergarten through 12th grade, and multiple deployments of a parent. Throughout all of this, military children are resilient and patriotic. They know first-hand what it feels like to be the new kid, so they are usually the first one to welcome a new student to school.

 

Celebrating the Month of the Military Child provides a powerful opportunity to raise awareness and build support to help these children meet the unique challenges they face! It is an opportunity to recognize and thank military children and youth for their heroism, character, courage, sacrifices and continued resilience.

National Library Week and School Library Month - April 2018

 

 

National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association and libraries across the country each April.  It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate.  National Library week is April 8 - 13 and the theme is "Libraries Lead.”

 

School Library Month is the American Association of School Librarians' celebration of school librarians and their programs. Strong school library programs play in an essential role in a student's educational career, and FCPS school librarians are dedicated to meeting the needs of all students through literacy development and relevant inquiry-based instruction that fosters curiosity, innovation, and life-long learning.

 

The School Board welcomes this opportunity to commend Fairfax County Public Schools’ librarians and to express its gratitude for the energy, knowledge, and vision that they tirelessly and unselfishly provide to the school system.

Autism Awareness Month is celebrated every year in April to provide an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness and acceptance of autism and the unique aspects and talents of all people. Subject: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Staff Contact: Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Other Staff Present: Francisco Durán, chief academic and equity officer

 

Meeting Category: April 12, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

As outlined in the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success, FCPS is committed to reaching, challenging, and preparing every student for success in school and life. This report highlights successes as well as important challenges facing the school system, including continued gaps in opportunity, access, and achievement among student sub-groups.

The report format includes separate chapters for each of the four strategies under Goal 1. Each chapter includes a strategy introduction, the related desired outcomes, student achievement data, and analysis to highlight the associated successes and challenges. The report’s appendix includes additional details on the division’s student achievement results.

 

Overarching Strategy 1: Enhance instructional practices to ensure that all students receive an education in a dynamic environment designed to foster life-long learning and support them in achieving their full potential.

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • The curriculum will reflect POG outcomes in all content areas, where students are appropriately challenged

  • Achievement gaps will be eliminated

  • Teachers, students, and parents will have access to contemporary and effective technology resources

  • All students will demonstrate digital citizenship skills

  • Centralized Support will be available for schools and school staff based on student achievement needs

     

Highlights

  • Gains in reading and mathematics SOL pass rates across all subgroups over multi-year timeframe

  • Increase in number of English Learners earning high school credits

  • Increase in available student, teacher, and parent resources to support Digital Citizenship

  • Increase in number of FCPS schools that are fully accredited

     

    Challenges

  • Closing gaps in opportunity, access, and achievement among student sub-groups

  • Providing equitable access to technology

     

    Overarching Strategy 2: All students will achieve their full potential through the use of assessment and data systems for decision making that support student attainment of the outcomes defined by the Portrait of a Graduate

     

    Desired Outcomes:

     

  • A fully-balanced assessment system will reflect POG outcomes in all content areas

  • Data tools to monitor progress

  • Grading will be an accurate reflection of learning

Highlights

  • Increased use of performance-based assessment for measuring content knowledge and Portrait of a Graduate skills

  • Expanded pilot of Capstone Experiences

  • Increased availability of data tools to teachers and school-based administrators

Challenges

  • Need for state policy flexibility for use of performance-based assessment to meet state accountability requirements

  • Supporting teacher data literacy skills to enhance data-based decision-making practices

 

Overarching Strategy 3: Provide quality Early Childhood experiences aligned with Portrait of a Graduate outcomes that are designed to prepare students to successfully enter Kindergarten.

 

Desired Outcomes:

 

  • All qualifying future FCPS students will have access to high quality early education programs

  • All agencies will coordinate their efforts to provide similar early childhood experiences

Highlights

  • Students that participate in PreK experiences out-perform students that do not have a PreK experience

  • Increased PreK opportunities for preschoolers and infants and toddlers in FCPS and County

  • Increased access to professional learning for early childhood community educators

  • Development of the Fairfax County’s Equitable School Readiness Strategic Plan

Challenges

  • Limited number of PreK opportunities for qualifying students

  • Neediest communities have higher numbers of students entering without a PreK experience

 

Overarching Strategy 4: Provide students with relevant opportunities to explore options and prepare for College and Career

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • A portfolio of school options will provide multiple pathways to a high school diploma

  • Students will be fluent in two or more languages

Highlights

  • Expansion of high school pathways that include coursework leading to in demand career preparation

  • Increased dual enrollment offerings

  • Increased “resume ready” credentials

  • Increased work-based learning experiences

Challenges

  • Division accountability systems for college and career indicators

  • Expansion of work-based learning opportunities

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success report as detailed in the agenda item.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report for the 2018-2019 school year, as required by the Virginia Department of Education (VDOE) Superintendent's memo #341-17 dated December 15, 2017. This report will be used by VDOE in preparing the state plan for special education. As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-prescribed abbreviated format on a date, no later than May 11, 2018.

The 2018-2019 Section 611, Part B application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $34,895,733. This amount is equal to the final 2017–2018 award as stated in Superintendent's memo #302-17, dated October 20, 2017. Estimated expenditures include costs associated with the voluntary 15 percent set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS); a mandatory proportionate set-aside, which ensures that FCPS spends a proportionate share of funds on special education services for parentally-placed private school children with disabilities; materials/supplies; and state restricted indirect costs of 1.3%. The IDEA Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 404.4 FTE positions.

The 2018-2019 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $774,964. This amount is equal to the final 2017-2018 award as stated in Superintendent's memo #214-17, dated July 21, 2017. These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for school-age students. Estimated expenditures include costs associated with state restricted indirect costs of 1.3%. IDEA Section 619 (Preschool) funds 6.0 FTE positions.

The revenue amounts above reflect no change from the 2017-2018 awards. 2018-2019 application reflects estimated costs for the current programs funded in the 2017-2018 application. Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, materials/supplies, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.

The 2018-2019 Annual Plan was presented to the Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) at the March 14, 2018, meeting.

That the School Board approve the 2018-2019 Annual Special Education plan, Section 611, Part B grant funding application and Section 619, Part B preschool grant funding application, as detailed in the agenda item.

7.05 Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success [ISD; WS 4/16; Action 4/26; approve the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success report as detailed in the agenda item.]

Subject: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Staff Contact: Sloan Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Other Staff Present: Francisco Durán, chief academic and equity officer

 

Meeting Category: April 12, 2018 – Regular Meeting

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Ignite Link: Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

As outlined in the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success, FCPS is committed to reaching, challenging, and preparing every student for success in school and life. This report highlights successes as well as important challenges facing the school system, including continued gaps in opportunity, access, and achievement among student sub-groups.

The report format includes separate chapters for each of the four strategies under Goal 1. Each chapter includes a strategy introduction, the related desired outcomes, student achievement data, and analysis to highlight the associated successes and challenges. The report’s appendix includes additional details on the division’s student achievement results.

 

Overarching Strategy 1: Enhance instructional practices to ensure that all students receive an education in a dynamic environment designed to foster life-long learning and support them in achieving their full potential.

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • The curriculum will reflect POG outcomes in all content areas, where students are appropriately challenged

  • Achievement gaps will be eliminated

  • Teachers, students, and parents will have access to contemporary and effective technology resources

  • All students will demonstrate digital citizenship skills

  • Centralized Support will be available for schools and school staff based on student achievement needs

     

Highlights

  • Gains in reading and mathematics SOL pass rates across all subgroups over multi-year timeframe

  • Increase in number of English Learners earning high school credits

  • Increase in available student, teacher, and parent resources to support Digital Citizenship

  • Increase in number of FCPS schools that are fully accredited

     

    Challenges

  • Closing gaps in opportunity, access, and achievement among student sub-groups

  • Providing equitable access to technology

     

    Overarching Strategy 2: All students will achieve their full potential through the use of assessment and data systems for decision making that support student attainment of the outcomes defined by the Portrait of a Graduate

     

    Desired Outcomes:

     

  • A fully-balanced assessment system will reflect POG outcomes in all content areas

  • Data tools to monitor progress

  • Grading will be an accurate reflection of learning

Highlights

  • Increased use of performance-based assessment for measuring content knowledge and Portrait of a Graduate skills

  • Expanded pilot of Capstone Experiences

  • Increased availability of data tools to teachers and school-based administrators

Challenges

  • Need for state policy flexibility for use of performance-based assessment to meet state accountability requirements

  • Supporting teacher data literacy skills to enhance data-based decision-making practices

 

Overarching Strategy 3: Provide quality Early Childhood experiences aligned with Portrait of a Graduate outcomes that are designed to prepare students to successfully enter Kindergarten.

 

Desired Outcomes:

 

  • All qualifying future FCPS students will have access to high quality early education programs

  • All agencies will coordinate their efforts to provide similar early childhood experiences

Highlights

  • Students that participate in PreK experiences out-perform students that do not have a PreK experience

  • Increased PreK opportunities for preschoolers and infants and toddlers in FCPS and County

  • Increased access to professional learning for early childhood community educators

  • Development of the Fairfax County’s Equitable School Readiness Strategic Plan

Challenges

  • Limited number of PreK opportunities for qualifying students

  • Neediest communities have higher numbers of students entering without a PreK experience

 

Overarching Strategy 4: Provide students with relevant opportunities to explore options and prepare for College and Career

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • A portfolio of school options will provide multiple pathways to a high school diploma

  • Students will be fluent in two or more languages

Highlights

  • Expansion of high school pathways that include coursework leading to in demand career preparation

  • Increased dual enrollment offerings

  • Increased “resume ready” credentials

  • Increased work-based learning experiences

Challenges

  • Division accountability systems for college and career indicators

  • Expansion of work-based learning opportunities

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success report as detailed in the agenda item.
