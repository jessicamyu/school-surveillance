Agenda Item: Student Information System Replacement: Business Case

Staff Contact: Maribeth Luftglass, Assistant Superintendent, Information Technology

Background: FCPS implemented its current student information system, SASI, more than 10 years ago. SASI is a legacy application built on what is now an aging and out-dated technology platform. As a result, SASI functionality is limited in its ability to support the FCPS school board Vision, Mission, and Strategic Goals.

FCPS needs to acquire a student information system that can:
      Measure, document, and track new performance criteria over time
      Provide secure anytime/anywhere access for an expanded set of users
      Provide near real-time data and reporting
      Respond to changing business needs and local, state, and federal mandates

Staff has conducted a business case analysis including conducting a market review, issuing an RFI with 11 vendor responses, and surveying large and neighboring school districts.

Key Issues
      Implementation is estimated to be 4-5 years in length.
      Preliminary license and implementation/customization costs estimated to be $15.25M, spread out over 5 years (true costs will result from formal RFP process).
      Lack of flexibility of SASI limits its ability to respond to current and emerging FCPS needs.
School Board Discussion/Decision
      Consensus for recommendation that FCPS move forward with inclusive requirements gathering process with key stakeholders (teachers, principals, students, parents, administrators) for replacement of its current student information system, with goal of developing an RFP (Release date anticipated prior to Jan 2008)
      Authorize $2.7M funding from the FY 2007 budget year-end balance for Year 1 anticipated expenditures
Staff Recommendation
That FCPS move forward with requirements gathering process and issue of an RFP to replace the current student information system.


Options

Retain legacy SASI system, with limited capabilities. Within the next four years, the existing 200 SASI servers and scanners will need to be replaced at an additional cost of approximately $2.7 million.

Agenda Item: Proposed Telecommunications Policy Revision

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

Background

On April 14, 2005, the School Board unanimously passed a motion to adopt Policy 8335, Wireless Telecommunication Facilities. On July 13, 2006, the Fairfax County Planning Commission (PC)approved an application to replace an existing athletic field light pole at Mount Vernon High School; action on this same application was presented to the School Board on October 19, 2006, which voted 6-5 in favor of indefinitely postponing the Mount Vernon monopole application.

At the same October 2006 meeting the School Board passed a motion to consider Policy 8335 at a future work session. The monopole policy was a topic at the March 22, 2007, School Board forum. At that time the School Board requested that staff amend Policy 8335 to clarify procedures for seeking approval of telecommunications facilities.

The proposed policy revision incorporates the philosophy of the existing policy, as well as clarifies the responsibilities of staff in determining the feasibility of school board-owned sites to house telecommunications facilities. The proposed policy also acknowledges that once the PC votes to approve a telecommunications facility application, the School Board can be assured that the proposed facility will be in compliance with requirements of the Federal Telecommunications Act of 1996, as stipulated in the Code of Virginia, as well as supportive of the county government’s efforts to provide adequate cellular telephone coverage for the citizens of the county.

Key Issues

The proposed policy has been revised in its entirety to clarify procedures for seeking approval of telecommunications facilities. as well as accept the Fairfax County Planning Commission’s approvals for the installation of telecommunications facilities on school board-owned property when such approval has been granted pursuant to a public hearing process. Furthermore, should an application for a telecommunications facility to the Fairfax County Planning Commission not require that body to hold a public hearing prior to approval due to its nature as a feature already shown on the comprehensive plan, the revised policy enables the School Board to hold a public hearing on the application even when not legally required should it determine that a public hearing is in the best interest of the School Board. The proposed policy maintains the current guidelines for monopole installations and ensures that no installations would occur inconsistent with the policy and without the express approvals of the school principal and the Department of Facilities and Transportation Services. Assuming these approvals, applications would be submitted to the PC. If the PC holds a public hearing, and if the PC approves the application, no further action would be required by the School Board. If the PC decides that a public hearing is not required and approves the application, the School Board would hold its own public hearing and make a decision on whether to allow installation of the monopole.

School Board Discussion/Decision

The School Board needs to decide on its level of involvement in considering applications for telecommunications towers on school property. As evidenced by the Mount Vernon applications, the current policy and regulation do not provide sufficient certainty of outcome to justify the time and effort required by applicants and staff. Staff is requesting guidance from the School Board on the level of involvement it desires to have in making decisions on monopole installation applications. Staff believes that the current policy and regulation provides for sufficient uncertainty in the application outcome that it is unlikely that the industry will submit applications for new monopoles.

Staff Recommendation

That the School Board adopt the revised policy that clarifies procedures for approving the installation of telecommunications facilities on School Board-owned property. Staff believes that monopoles serve a benevolent purpose for our communities and that the revenue generated for the schools and the school system is meaningful. For these reasons, staff believes that the school system should find a means to simplify the process and have a more predictable outcome.


Options

Continue with current policy and regulation processes. If the School Board makes the decision to maintain the current policy and regulation, it is unlikely that further applications for additional telecommunications towers will be processed by staff.
    1. Continue with current policy and regulation processes.
    2. Shift policy to have Superintendent responsible for decision-making
    3. Cease contracting with telecommunication companies seeking to install facilities on school properties.

Agenda Item: School Boundary Change Process Modifications

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer

Background
Some School Board members and staff have expressed an interest in modifying the current school boundary change process to better accommodate the public, lessen staff vilification, and gather greater input from the affected communities. Staff has developed a revised boundary change process that would utilize a Citizens Boundary Planning Committee (CBPC) to address the above concerns.

Key Issues
Volunteers for the CBPC would be sought from schools included in the boundary study. Final appointment to the committee would be made by cluster assistant superintendents using a lottery system. Boundary change recommendations would be developed by the CBPC working with the Facilities Planning Office as outlined in the attached working paper. The CBPC would hold community meetings to gather input on boundary issues and to review boundary proposals.

School Board Discussion/Decision

Staff Recommendation
That the School Board consider making changes to the current school boundary change process effective for the 2007-2008 school year.

Options
Modify the recommended process, continue with the current process or develop a new process different from the recommendation.

Agenda Item: Veterans’ Day and National Day of Mourning


Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services Department


Background:
Staff was asked to respond to School Board member concerns regarding the recognition and commemoration of Veterans’ Day and National Day of Mourning.

Currently there is a statement on the FCPS “Calendar of Religious and Cultural Observances” directing teachers to engage students in activities that “honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.” (See White Paper attached for additional background.)

Expectations regarding the acknowledgement of National Day of Mourning have not been established.

Key Issues:
Inconsistent practices in schools regarding observance of Veterans’ Day and National Day of Mourning.

School Board Discussion/Decision:
Staff will present a revision to current policy that clarifies expectations regarding Veterans’ Day and National Day of Mourning. Staff will also propose that expectations about Veterans’ Day be communicated to schools annually and that resources be provided to schools to support instructional activities. When a National Day of Mourning occurs, expectations regarding appropriate acknowledgement will be communicated and resources provided to schools as quickly as possible.

Staff Recommendation:
Revise Policy 3380.1; communicate expectations to schools; provide resources to schools.

Options:
Accept the staff recommendation.
Maintain current policy without revision.
