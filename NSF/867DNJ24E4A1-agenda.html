RESOLUTION
NAMING THE ORAL HISTORY LAB AT
ROCKY RUN MIDDLE SCHOOL
“THE MELVIN H. ROSEN ORAL HISTORY LAB”

WHEREAS, the new Oral History Lab at Rocky Run Middle School was created for the purpose of preserving live interviews with eyewitnesses to history through the efforts of Colonel Melvin H. Rosen, a World War II veteran who passed away earlier this year; and

WHEREAS, Colonel Rosen, while serving during World War II, survived the Bataan Death March and was held for more than three years as a Japanese prisoner of war and shared these painful experiences to inspire hundreds of Rocky Run students with his story of survival, perseverance, hope, and patriotism; and

WHEREAS, Colonel Rosen inspired Rocky Run Middle School teachers to use the human resources available in their community to capitalize on the instructional value of primary resources with their students; and

WHEREAS, Colonel Rosen demonstrated, as a young man, his absolute will to survive unfathomable prisoner of war conditions and used that experience later in life to help individuals in our community suffering through intolerable and often terminal health conditions; and

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board dedicates the Oral History Lab at Rocky Run Middle School “The Melvin H. Rosen Oral History Lab” in appreciation of the late Colonel Rosen’s contributions to the Rocky Run school community as well as recognition of his service to our country and outstanding leadership.

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Type: April 10, 2008 - Regular Meeting No.17

SUBJECT: South County Secondary School

TYPE: Action

BACKGROUND:
      At the March 10, 2008, School Board work session, the Board agreed to pursue discussions and negotiations with the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors to consider adding a wing to South County Secondary School to alleviate overcrowding as soon as possible with the understanding that this would not take the concept of a stand alone South County Middle School off the table. The County would provide $10 million over two years from their sources of funds and the School Board would provide $2 million from our Capital Improvement Program (CIP) that is currently directed to planning for the middle school. The Board also agreed that a motion on this matter as new business may be considered on March 27, 2008, for action on April 10, 2008. Staff was directed to report on progress to the Board during Forum on March 13.


KEY POINTS:
          · Due to present overcrowding and use of the building as a secondary school, a 9-period day was created to allow middle and high school students to have overlapping schedules to allow maximum use of shared space in the building
          · While the 9-period day has been effective, this schedule has placed a significant strain on school staff and cannot be continued indefinitely or considered a permanent solution.
OPTIONS:
          1. Build a new middle school which is currently shown in the CIP for FY2014-2018 and is unfunded.
          2. Conduct a boundary study in Fall 2008 to move additional students.
          3. Build an addition onto South County Secondary similar to that added to Westfield High School.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board approve a building addition to South County Secondary School using the $10 million provided by county funding and the $2 million in FCPS bond funding currently designated for the planning and design of a South County middle school, and direct staff to begin immediately to take steps to plan and complete the building addition.

SUBJECT: Award of Contract

TYPE: New Business

BACKGROUND:
      The existing renovation queue for FCPS schools was established in 2000 by the Office of Design and Construction Services (D&C) with information provided from a physical plant and capacity study conducted in 2000 by an objective third party expert. At that time, D&C ranked the renovation of thirty-eight elementary, seven middle, and four high schools. The report ranked these schools in order to determine the sequence in which they would be renovated. This objective third party expert evaluation process has served the school system well in determining the relative needs of these schools and in de-politicizing the decision process. The Office of Design & Construction Services seeks to engage a firm to conduct a new study for the purpose of creating an updated renovation queue. Schools that are in the 2000 queue and for which either planning or construction funding has been approved by Fairfax County voters will remain in their current sequence and will begin before the new queue is implemented. It is anticipated that the assessment will include forty-two elementary, seven middle, and six high schools. The Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) received proposals from fourteen architectural firms on March 24, 2008. The SAC is currently evaluating the proposals and will rank the firms in accordance with the criteria set forth in the current version of School Board Policy 8220, Architectural and Engineering Services. The SAC will then recommend top-rated firms for contract negotiations consideration; alternative firms will be identified in the event that successful negotiations cannot be completed with the primary nominees.

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board authorize staff to negotiate a professional services contract for the facility program and condition assessment with the selected architectural firm and authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to execute the contract on behalf of the School Board.

SUBJECT: Legislative Recommendations for the 2009 Policies and Resolutions of the Virginia School Boards Association (VSBA)

TYPE: New Business

BACKGROUND:
      Each spring the VSBA Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee seeks recommendations from local school boards concerning new or revised legislative policies and resolutions to be considered for VSBA approval. The Committee reviews the local school boards’ recommendations along with other suggestions for changes to the existing policies and resolutions. The VSBA takes action on the Committee’s report at the annual fall convention. Staff recently learned that position recommendations are due to VSBA May 7, 2008.

      This process allows the School Board to make recommendations for the VSBA legislative program and to receive statewide support for some of its own legislative positions. The Fairfax County School Board 2009 Legislative Program will be developed for adoption in the fall.

      Changes are proposed to VSBA positions regarding limited English proficient students, the federal Elementary and Secondary Education Act of 1965 (No Child Left Behind), and state funding of education.

      The recommended changes are all either directly or indirectly based on policy positions the School Board has already adopted in its own 2008 Legislative Program.
OPTIONS:

Modify as desired.

RECOMMENDATION:

That the School Board accept the legislative recommendations to be forwarded to the Virginia School Boards Association Legislative, Policies and Resolutions Committee for their consideration, as detailed in the agenda item.
