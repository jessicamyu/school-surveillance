5.03 Amendment 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County (ERFC) Defined Benefit Plan
Meeting
Nov 21, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 8
Category
5. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Approve Amendment 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan for the purpose of preserving ERFC’s status as a source of supplemental benefits only. (HR; presented as new business 11/10/11)

Staff Contact:        Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category: November 21, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 8

 

Subject:                 Amendment 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County (ERFC) Defined Benefit Plan

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related to:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

The ERFC Board of Trustees is recommending an amendment to the ERFC Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC) as the result of a VRS amendment that the Virginia Legislature adopted in the 2011 Session.  The proposed ERFC amendment would maintain ERFC’s status as a source only of supplemental benefits.

 

ERFC, the Legacy Plan, was designed to supplement benefits that members receive from VRS. The benefit formulas achieve this purpose by specifying a percentage of the member’s final average salary (the “total promised benefit”) and calculating the ERFC benefit as that total amount minus the amount that VRS is expected to pay based on the member’s creditable service under VRS (“the VRS benefit reduction”). 

 

When the benefit formulas were drafted, there was only one circumstance in which a member might forfeit his or her VRS service credit, with the result that the VRS benefit reduction would be zero.  That circumstance was the member’s voluntary withdrawal of accumulated VRS member contributions. In order to protect ERFC from paying the total promised benefit in such a situation, the ERFC Plan document provides that such forfeited VRS service credit continues to be counted in calculating the VRS benefit reduction. 

 

In the 2011 Legislative Session, the Virginia Legislature amended the VRS statute to add another way that a member’s VRS service credits can be forfeited.  An amendment to Virginia Code § 51.1-124.13 provides that a VRS member who commits a felony in connection with his or her VRS-covered employment after July 1, 2011, will lose the right to VRS benefits based on service previously accumulated.  All VRS service credit up to that date is considered to have been “relinquished.” 

 

In order to make certain that any such relinquishment of VRS service credit will not result in a VRS benefit reduction equal to zero, the Trustees recommend a clarifying amendment to the ERFC Plan document.  The recommended amendment would provide that any service credit relinquished pursuant to Virginia Code § 51.1-124.13 will be counted in calculating the VRS benefit reduction, in the same manner that service forfeited by withdrawal of VRS contributions is counted.

 

No similar amendment is needed for ERFC 2001 Plan document because  ERFC 2001 benefits are based on a different formula that does not include a VRS benefit reduction.

 

A proposed Amendment Number 2 is attached for review and approval.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve Amendment 2 to the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan for the purpose of preserving ERFC’s status as a source of supplemental benefits only.

Meeting Category:   November 21, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 8

 

Subject:                   FY 2012 Midyear Budget Review       

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                                                     

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, the FY 2012 beginning balance increased $0.5 million due to audit adjustments made after the FY 2011 Final Budget Review, including an increase of $0.7 million in sales tax revenue based on actual sales tax receipts and other revenue adjustments totaling $0.8 million offset by expenditure adjustments totaling $1.0 million. 

 

In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other revenue adjustments total $0.4 million.  This is a result of revenue from the City of Fairfax increasing by $0.9 million based on the final tuition bill for FY 2011 reflecting actual expenditures and grant revenue decreasing $0.5 million. 

 

Recommended expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $5.0 million; a reduction of $4.6 million in compensation accounts is due to the average salaries of new hires for this school year being less than budgeted; a reduction of $0.5 million in grant expenditures is primarily from a reduction in the federal award for the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act; and an increase of $0.1 million for 2.0 internal audit positions.  The recommended audit positions are requested to conduct local school activity fund (LSAF) audits.

 

Expenditure adjustments, combined with revenue adjustments, result in available funds totaling $5.9 million recommended to be set aside to increase the FY 2013 beginning balance from $51.6 million to $57.5 million.  This will bring the FY 2013 beginning balance slightly above the level projected in the FY 2013 fiscal forecast.  

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.
