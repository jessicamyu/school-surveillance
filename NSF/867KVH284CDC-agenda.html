Agenda Item: Policy 1810, Benefits for School Board Members


Staff Contact: Kevin North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources


Background

Policy 1810 is being revised. The Department of Human Resources suggests that as School Board members have access to FCPS health insurance that they be treated as similarly situated individuals with respect to access to coverage post long service employment.

Key Issues

School Board members with FCPS health coverage do not currently have the ability to continue coverage after long service to the system. This proposal would provide this option if they had at least three terms of service. It does not provide for monetary support of the coverage.

School Board Discussion/Decision

Appropriateness of treatment similar to employee retirement for long service School Board members.

Staff Recommendation

Adopt revised changes.

Options

None recommended

Agenda Item: Policy 4150.4 Administrative Staff—Promotions and Appointments

Staff Contact: Kevin L. North, Assistant Superintendent, Human Resources

Background:

Policy 4150.3 has been reviewed in an effort to reflect previous Board discussion. The current policy contains many details that are duplicated in the regulation. These revisions reflect a realignment the two documents to better differentiate policy versus process. As a result, there are numerous lines eliminated from the policy as they are process related and have been incorporated into the regulation. The proposed policy and regulation reflect current practice with administrative promotions and appointments.

Key Issues:

To keep language at the policy level, it is recommended that detail of the promotion and appointment process be included in a complementary regulation.

See key issues.

Agenda Item: Policy 7350.3, General Safety

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities & Transportation Services.

Background:

Policy is being revised to reflect updated Code requirements and current practice.

Key Issues:

Information on prioritizing sidewalk projects was deleted because this service is no longer required. Other responsibilities for safety, security, and health were added to reflect current programs and practices.

The statement implying school division has responsibility for students going to and from school was removed. The Code of Virginia does not hold school divisions responsible for portal to portal supervision of students.

Legal References – Corrected Code of Virginia reference for Section 22.1-78 “Bylaws and Regulations,” and added the Code of Virginia requirement for state safety audits and crisis and emergency management plans, Section 22.1-279.

School Board Discussion/Decision:

See key issues.

Staff Recommendation:

Approve revised policy.

Options:

Maintain existing policy, with or without changes.
