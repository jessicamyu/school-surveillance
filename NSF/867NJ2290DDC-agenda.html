The Hispanic population is the fastest growing ethnic group in the United States and also in the Fairfax County Public Schools community; and the Fairfax County School Board recognizes that developing an understanding of the contributions of Hispanic Americans is an integral part of the education of all students through implementation of a pluralistic curriculum.

1.03 Recognition of the 50th Anniversary of Adult and Community Education
Meeting
Sep 8, 2005 - Regular Meeting No. 3
Category
1. Meeting Opening - 8 p.m.
Type
Information

One of the hallmarks of Fairfax County is its reputation for educational excellence. We enjoy our strong economy largely because this area is a magnet for educated individuals for whom education is a very strong value. Even those residents who don’t have children in the school system value the strength of Fairfax County’s public education system.

And because of this, in September 1955, the School Board at that time had the vision to institute an adult education program to ensure that access to education was a reality for all residents. Since that small beginning with a little over 100 students, the adult and community education program has flourished and grown to its present stature, with annual enrollments exceeding 80,000. The magnitude of its offerings reaches into nearly every area of county life: bringing second-chance learners back into the schools; teaching newcomers the language and culture of America; helping adults to learn new job skills that keep our workforce strong; and developing and nurturing lifelong learning for all residents, from kindergarten through adulthood.

For 50 years, our adult and community education program has provided world-class educational offerings for the county’s lifelong learners. How very different this community would have been without this vital program which has so enhanced the quality of life for so many thousands of our neighbors! Tonight, on the occasion of its 50th Anniversary, it gives me great pleasure – on behalf of myself and my colleagues – to congratulate the Office of Adult & Community Education, and to convey best wishes for the next 50 years.

Summary of Sign-Up Procedures for Citizen Participation
Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so only online between 6 and 11 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting; after 11 a.m. on that Monday, citizens may sign up online or by calling the School Board office at 703-246-3646. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 7:30 and 8 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving cases under litigation, issues that have been scheduled for public hearings, or complaints regarding individual employees or students.

Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services

SUBJECT: Curriculum Advisory Committees

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board appoint individuals to serve on the English Language Arts, Family Life Education, Fine Arts, Foreign Language, Health and Physical Education, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies Curriculum Advisory Committees, and on the High School Course Review Committee, as listed in the agenda item.

EXPLANATION:
      Regulation 1707.6 establishes eight curriculum advisory committees for levels K through 12 to advise on Program of Studies development and implementation.
      Additionally, this agenda item covers appointments to the High School Course Review Committee, which annually identifies standard or optional course offerings for deletion, consolidation, or revision, and makes recommendations for action by the Division Superintendent and the School Board (see the current version of Regulation 3202).

      FCPS does not appoint Curriculum Advisory Committees in years when there is a textbook adoption taking place. However, in recent years, the adoption process in several subject areas has been scheduled for more than one year to decrease the impact on the schools and the budget. For example, last year, we adopted science textbooks for high school; in 05-06 we will adopt science textbooks for middle school, grades 6-8. Similarly, the textbook adoption for English/language arts scheduled for 05-06 is for high school only. Because we believe that we should not suspend Advisory Committees for more than one year and because 05-06 English/language arts and science adoptions are limited to one level, IS will convene Curriculum Advisory Committees in these subject areas next year. The work of the adoption committees will be completed by early September; therefore, it is very possible that School Board appointees may be willing to continue to serve on advisory committees.
      The term of service for all School Board member representatives on the above-named advisory committees is one year. These terms will be effective until June 30, 2006. Representatives may be reappointed. Classroom teachers are elected to these committees and serve two-year terms, with elections being held during the spring of odd-numbered years. These terms will be effective until June 30, 2007. Other committee members serve terms as set forth in the appropriate regulation.

Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

SUBJECT: Conveyance of Easements

RECOMMENDATION:
      That the School Board authorize (i) conveyance to the Commonwealth of Virginia, Department of Transportation (VDOT), of the easements described in the attached draft Deed, and (ii) payment of $15,000 to VDOT in exchange for VDOT constructing and installing a sanitary sewer extension to the School Board’s property on Lorton Road; and that the School Board authorize the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services to execute on behalf of the School Board a deed and any necessary related documents, all as approved by School Board counsel, to accomplish the conveyance.
EXPLANATION:
      The Department of Facilities and Transportation Services has negotiated with VDOT to grant to VDOT storm drainage and temporary construction easements on a portion of the School Board’s property on Lorton Road, in connection with VDOT’s improvements to Lorton Road. VDOT initially offered $10,013.00 as compensation for the easements. Staff conferred with an independent Appraiser to confirm that VDOT's initial offer of compensation was consistent with the fair market value of the affected property. Further, to address potential issues with the existing drainfield that serves the School Board property, Staff negotiated for VDOT to incorporate a sanitary sewer extension to the School Board property as part of the VDOT road project. The cost of installing the sewer line exceeded VDOT’s $10,013.00 offer of compensation by at least $15,000. VDOT agreed to modify its plans and install the sanitary sewer line as part of its project if the School Board would contribute $15,000 to cover the additional costs. This sewer extension is critical to the future use of the School Board property and would be provided much more efficiently and inexpensively as part of the VDOT road project, as opposed to the School Board undertaking such an extension as a stand alone project sometime in the future. The value of the sewer extension to the School Board exceeds the combined value of the easements being conveyed and the payment of $15,000 to VDOT.
