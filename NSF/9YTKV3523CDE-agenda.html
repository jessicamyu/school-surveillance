The Budget Task Force has met eight times and has continued its thoughtful deliberation. Two additional meetings have been added to their schedule to allow time for further discussion and completion of their work. The additional meeting dates are October 20 and November 4. Meeting Category:  October 19, 2015 – Work Session No. 26

 

Subject: Audit: Program Evaluation - Reading

 

School Board Action Required: Information                    

 

Key Points:

 

Two audits were conducted related to the teaching and learning of reading in FCPS:

  • Literacy Audit by Marjorie Lipson who reviewed and made recommendations regarding a) the curriculum and instruction, b) division assessment practices, and 3) professional development

 

  • Dyslexia Audit by Deborah Reed who reviewed and made recommendations regarding a) the current assessment plan for students with dyslexia and b) the division plan to support students with dyslexia

 

This presentation to the School Board combines the two audit’s findings, recommendations  and staff responses into a single discussion organized around a) curriculum and instruction,    b) assessment and c) professional development

 

 

Recommendation:

 

          Although the audits provided a number of recommendations to staff, this presentation provides Implications for the School Board: Alignment and Funding in the following areas: Request ISD/DSS staff to:

  • Identify specific links between responses (i.e. actions) to the literacy and dyslexia audits and the strategic plan.
  • Ensure buy-in from schools by engaging selected school-based staff in the planning and review of ISD/DSS actions.
  • Identify specific outcomes anticipated from the actions and a timeframe for reporting to the School Board
  • Identify funding implications for each action.

Staff Contact: Steve Lockard, Deputy Superintendent    

                                                                                                     

Other Staff Present:   Terry Dade, assistant superintendent, Region 3

             Mark Greenfelder, executive principal, Region 3

 

Meeting Category:    October 19, 2015 – School Board Work Session No. 29

 

Subject: Project Momentum

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion                                                     

 

Related To:  Student Achievement Goal 1. Pursue Academic Excellence

 

Key Points:

 

FCPS will provide differentiated tiers of support for all schools to raise the bar and close gaps in student achievement. 

 

Update on the status of Project Momentum Schools.  Intensive and Targeted schools will be reviewed along with supports that were in place during the first year of Project Momentum.

 

Included will be metrics to show the positive growth of all Intensive schools in Project Momentum along with additional supports provided for the 2015-2016 school year.

 

Project Momentum was initiated in 2014-2015 taking lessons learned from FCPS and National school improvement initiatives and comprising them into a unified and target approach for our schools most at risk of VDOE State Accreditation.  Project Momentum narrows the scope and focus to create a sustainable research-based improvement strategy; an accelerated school improvement timeline; dedicated school support team resource and differentiated staffing; and, specific expected outcomes for each school.
