Subject:  FCPS Equity Profile

 

Staff Contact: Francisco Durán, chief equity officer

 

Other Staff Present:  Maribeth Luftglass, assistant superintendent, Information Technology

 

Meeting Category:  March 11, 2019 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link:  Goals 1-4:  Student Success, Caring Culture, Premiere Workforce, and Resource Stewardship

 

Outcome:

 

School Board preview of the FCPS Equity Profile prior to public release.  The Equity Profile will be available on March 15 on the FCPS public website.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

The Equity Profile is a part of the work outlined in One Fairfax—a joint policy of the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors and the School Board.  The policy states, "Fairfax County Public Schools will incorporate data and publish performance measures that can be analyzed, quantified, and disaggregated to evaluate the extent to which our systems are achieving goals."

 

The Equity Profile was developed to provide data and publish performance measures to show progress FCPS is making on achieving specific goals related to the Strategic Plan: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship.  The data shows division-wide desired outcomes and metrics that will be monitored to ensure gaps in achievement, opportunity, access, and attainment are being addressed.  The display of data in a variety of charts is based on the best visualization for the type of data analysis (e.g. comparison vs. distribution).  The data can be analyzed, quantified, and disaggregated to view trends in division and school performance.

 

The student demographics data provides information about all students, various demographic groups, and specific student populations.  The Equity Profile is interactive and mobile responsive and allows users to download a PDF file for printing or JPEG for use in a PowerPoint.  The profile will be updated annually.

 

The Equity Profile will include program assessment data on the School Resource Officer Program when that data is made available by the Fairfax County Policy Department.

Subject: Provide guidance on goals for boundary policy and prioritize factors to include in boundary policy

 

 

Staff Contact: Jeffrey Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Other Staff Present: Marty Smith, chief operating officer

                                 Jessica Gillis, director, Facilities Planning Services, Facilities and Transportation Services

                                 Lidi Hruda, director, Research and Strategic Improvement

 

 

Meeting Category: March 11, 2019 – Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Ignite Link: Goal 4: Resource Stewardship, as well as Goals 1(Student Success) and 2 (Caring Culture)

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): On October 15, 2018, the Office of Facilities Planning Services, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services presented information about a review of Policy 8130 and boundary policies and practices of other school divisions. This presentation introduced 16 factors that are commonly considered by school divisions in making boundary changes. As a next step, the School Board requested Work Sessions to cover a review of the educational research related to boundary changes and these 16 factors and to address factors around boundaries.

 

On February 25, 2019, the Office of Research and Strategic Improvement presented findings from their review of the educational research on factors considered in boundary decision-making. The Office of Facilities Planning Services, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services also described how FCPS practice relates to the areas identified in research along with information about current FCPS boundaries and school capacity.

 

The intent of the discussion is to continue to provide information about current FCPS boundaries, programs, and school capacity and for the School Board to provide guidance on goals for boundary policy and prioritize factors to include in boundary policy.

Subject:  Advanced Academic Programs

 

Staff Contact:  Sloan J. Presidio, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department (ISD)

 

Other Staff Present:  Noel Klimenko, director, PreK-12 Curriculum and     Instruction, ISD

Kirsten Maloney, coordinator, Advanced Academics        Programs, ISD

 

Meeting Category:  March 11, 2019, School Board Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

This work session is a result of the Forum held on December 20, 2018.

 

There are existing Virginia Department of Education regulations governing Services for Gifted students, which include guidance on processes for screening and identification.

 

FCPS submits a Local Plan for the Gifted to VDOE every five years.  The current plan, which includes several goals related to equity, may be accessed on the FCPS website https://www.fcps.edu/node/34510.

 

Though many efforts to increase participation of historically underrepresented student groups of Advanced Academic Programs have improved access, gaps in subgroup data remain.

 

An external review of current FCPS program practices and enrollment data, research-based best practices, and feedback around AAP Identification practices from multiple stakeholder groups may inform future actions and bring about improved outcomes. 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the proposed scope and timeline for an external review of the FCPS Level IV Advanced Academics Program.

Subject: 2020-2021 Standard School Year Calendar

 

 

Staff Contact: Helen Nixon, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

 

 

Meeting Category: March 11, 2019 – All Day Work Session

 

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

 

Related To: Legal Requirement

 

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): A calendar committee was assembled for a second consecutive year to assist in the development of the calendar. The committee was composed of representatives from a variety of internal and external constituent groups which included community religious organization representatives, teachers, employees and principal association representatives, along with central office staff. The 2020 – 2021 Standard School Year Calendar was developed with a focus on instruction and learning time for students. While teaching and learning was the priority, a multitude of factors, including past community survey data, were considered and balanced during the development of the calendar.

 

 

Recommendation: That the School Board adopt the 2020 – 2021 standard school year calendar.
