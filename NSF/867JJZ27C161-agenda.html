    Recommended Action
    Approve the Strategic Governance Manual, as reviewed and discussed by the Strategic Governance Committee



    Staff Contact: Phillip Niedzielski-Eichner, Providence District

    SUBJECT: Strategic Governance Manual

    RECOMMENDATION:

        That the School Board adopt the Strategic Governance Manual, as reviewed and discussed by the Strategic Governance Committee, effective January 1, 2007.

    EXPLANATION:

        Over the past year, the School Board has launched a new strategic governance initiative which includes new beliefs, vision and mission statements and student achievement goals to provide a more concentrated focus on student achievement and to establish clearer accountability. The School Board held a series of community meetings from February to May 2006 to solicit input and feedback from members of the community on the student achievement goals. The School Board’s new governing process includes operational expectations for every department, a framework within which the Superintendent and staff members should work, and the student achievement goals, which include academics, essential life skills, and responsibility to the community.

        The Strategic Governance Manual is a compilation of expectations for students and Fairfax County Public Schools. Information in the Manual includes beliefs, vision, and mission statements, student achievement goals, operational expectations for departments, and Board member roles and responsibilities. The By-Laws section incorporates information from existing School Board policies regarding operations of the School Board, including salary and travel reimbursement, duties of the student representative, clerk and deputy clerks, types of meetings, meeting procedures, citizen participation, and the communication process between the School Board and the Superintendent and staff.

      Staff Contact: Alan Belstock, ERFC Executive Director

      SUBJECT: Award of Contract – Integrated Retirement Benefit and Payroll System for ERFC

      RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board authorize the Educational Employees' Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC) Board of Trustees, or its designee, to execute a contract with Levi, Ray & Shoup, Inc. (LRS) for the provision of an integrated Retirement Benefit Administration and Payroll computer system in the amount of $2,272,472; to execute a contract with L. R. Wechsler, Ltd. (“LRWL”) for the provision of associated consulting services in an amount not to exceed $420,000; to procure required system hardware/software in an amount not to exceed $241,895; and to obtain necessary maintenance and support agreements for a five-year period following system implementation. Funding will be provided from the ERFC Trust Fund.

      EXPLANATION:
          The FCPS Office of Procurement Services issued a Request for Proposal RFP06-861257-73 soliciting offers from qualified sources to provide an integrated Retirement Benefit Administration and Payroll computer system for ERFC. System requirements include the complete replacement of all current ERFC applications with a single integrated solution containing features that extend beyond ERFC’s current capabilities for benefit administration, retiree payroll, benefit calculation and workload monitoring. The contract also includes back-file conversion of member hardcopy files using LRS’ imaging solution and member web services to allow members to access account information and process requests on-line.
          The computer system RFP was publicly advertised with three (3) firms submitting proposals. A Selection Advisory Committee (SAC) comprised of the ERFC Executive Director and other senior administrators from ERFC and the FCPS Department of Information Technology evaluated the proposals in accordance with the criteria and procedures established in the RFP. Based upon the proposals that were reviewed, interviews and site visits conducted with the two top candidates, the SAC recommended contract award to LRS.

          The FCPS Office of Procurement Services also issued RFP06-828861-73 to solicit bids from qualified sources to provide consulting services related to project management and implementation of the new system. After reviewing four bids, a subset of the SAC recommended contract award to LRWL.

          Software maintenance and support costs for the five-year period following full implementation of the new system will average approximately $137,000 per year.
          At the Board meeting on November 21, 2006, members of the ERFC Board of Trustees voted unanimously to recommend the contract award.
