Childhood Immunization Month - April 2011

 

April is being recognized as Childhood Immunization Month by the County of Fairfax to raise awareness of the importance of immunizations in protecting the health and well-being of the community.   Fairfax County Public Schools continues to work collaboratively with the Fairfax County Health Department to highlight the importance of immunizations and to ensure that students are adequately immunized before enrolling in school.  

 

Autism Awareness Month - April 2011

 

Autism is a complex developmental disability that affects an individual's social interaction and communication. It is known as a spectrum disorder, because it affects each individual in different ways and to varying degrees.


Autism Awareness Month, celebrated every year in April, provides an opportunity for families, friends, and local communities to raise public awareness about autism. It is sponsored by the Autism Society of America (ASA). Individuals and communities can get involved by participating in activities sponsored by their local ASA chapter or by promoting services related to autism.

 

Mathematics Awareness Month - April 2011

The definition of mathematics is associated with the investigation of patterns of structure, change, and space. It is often referred to as the study of “figures and numbers.” Although not considered a natural science, mathematics is the vehicle used to investigate and add order to the natural sciences. Mathematics as an art form rather than as a practical or applied science lends aesthetics and order to the arts and architecture. Mathematics as a literacy allows all citizens full participation in a global society. In our lives, mathematics structures our time, patterns of change, and our space. In our daily lives, “math matters.”

Mathematics Awareness Month is held each year in April. Its goal is to increase public understanding of and appreciation for mathematics. Mathematics Awareness Month began in 1986 as Mathematics Awareness Week with a proclamation by President Ronald Reagan. In 1999, Mathematics Awareness Week became Mathematics Awareness Month.

National Library Week/School Library Month - April 2011

 

National Library Week is a national observance sponsored by the American Library Association and libraries across the country each April.  It is a time to celebrate the contributions of our nation's libraries and librarians and to promote library use and support. All types of libraries - school, public, academic and special - participate.  This year, National Library Week will be observed April 10-16 with the theme, "Create your own story @ your library®."

 

School Library Month is the American Association of School Librarians' celebration of school librarians and their programs.  Every April school librarians are encouraged to create activities to help their school and local community celebrate the essential role that strong school library programs play in a student's educational career.

 

The School Board welcomes this opportunity to commend Fairfax County Public Schools’ librarians and to express its gratitude for the energy, knowledge, and vision that they tirelessly and unselfishly provide to the school system.

Staff Contact:                        Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:              Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:                March 24, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                                FY 2011 Third Quarter Budget Review

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Key Points:

 

This agenda item is provided to review the current FY 2011 budget and to make revisions as necessary.  The FY 2011 Third Quarter Budget Review reports on activity and requests that have been made since the School Board’s Midyear Budget Review that was approved on December 2, 2010.

 

This agenda recognizes increased revenue totaling $5.0 million and zero net change in expenditures in the School Operating Fund.  This results in $5.0 million available to set aside towards the FY 2013 beginning balance.  Revenue adjustments include sales tax, state funding for school nurses and the National Board Certified Teacher (NBCT) program, and the elimination of revenue from student fees for Advanced Placement (AP) and International Baccalaureate (IB) tests. 

 

Based on current receipts, it is anticipated that sales tax revenue will exceed the updated projection included in the FY 2011 Midyear Budget Review by $3.0 million.  As specified in the state’s FY 2011-FY 2012 adopted budget, FCPS will be required to expend to Fairfax County funding totaling $3.9 million for school nursing services.  The state’s budget included language which required school divisions to spend these funds for licensed school nurse positions employed by the school division or contracted by the school division to provide school health services.  Previously, school divisions received funding for school nurses through the support cost calculation in Basic Aid and were only required to allocate these funds for such purposes.  In effect, providing this funding to the county will result in a reduction of available funds for FCPS.  As a result, the County Executive and Superintendent approved a proposal designed to share the benefit of the funding by implementing a long overdue staffing increase and recognizing the support of FCPS of the program in terms of facility utilization and integration into the school community.  As a result, half of the total funding of $3.9 million will support salaries, benefits, and operating costs associated with the new county positions and other Health Department support for the School Health program, and half will be made available to FCPS for services provided in support of school health functions.

 

The remaining revenue adjustments include an increase in the number of teachers receiving an NBCT stipend from the state, which will result in an increase of $0.1 million in revenue received for the state-funded portion of the stipend that will be paid to teachers by FCPS on behalf of the state and a decrease in student fees of $2.0 million.  Kenneth T. CuccineIli, II, the Attorney General for Virginia, issued an opinion stating, “It is my opinion that a local school board cannot impose a mandatory fee on students taking advanced placement courses for the required taking of the Advanced Placement Examination.”  The Superintendent’s recommendation is to continue to mandate test participation for AP/IB courses and eliminate the related test fees.  Payments received by FCPS for this school year will be refunded.

 

Expenditure increases for school nurses and NBCT result as a condition of the revenue increases and are offset by compensation decreases resulting in zero change to expenditures. Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure adjustments as reflected in the FY 2011 Third Quarter Budget Review and as detailed in the agenda item.

4.02 Alternative Accreditation Plan Requests
Meeting
Mar 24, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 16
Category
4. Action Items - 9 p.m.
Type
Action
Recommended Action
Approve the Alternative Accreditation Plan requests for five schools for submission to the Virginia Board of Education, as detailed in the agenda item (PLA; presented as new business 3/10/11)

Staff Contact:           Terri Breeden, assistant superintendent, Professional Learning and Accountability                                                                                 

 

Meeting Category:    March 24, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                    Alternative Accreditation Plans Requests for Key and Kilmer Centers, Bryant and Mountain View Alternative High Schools, and Woodson Adult High School

 

School Board Action Required:     Decision                                              

 

Related To:               Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

 

Background:

 

Five schools in Fairfax County are pursuing the approval of alternative accreditation plans in order to be able to be “Fully Accredited” in 2011-2012, 2012-2013, and 2013-2014. Those schools are Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Woodson Adult High School.

 

Because the five schools listed above are classified as “Special Purpose” schools, serving unique populations of students for whom the measures of accreditation are inappropriate, staff in the Office of School Improvement at the Virginia Department of Education recommend that alternative accreditation plans be submitted to the Virginia Board of Education for approval.  This recommendation is based on the Regulations Establishing Standards for Accrediting Public Schools in Virginia, Section 8 VAC 20-131-280.C. The regulation states: 

 

"Subject to the provisions of 8 VAC 20-131-350, the governing school board of special purpose schools such as those provided for in § 22.1-26 of the Code of Virginia, Governor’s schools, special education schools, alternative schools, or career and technical schools that serve as the student's school of principal enrollment may seek approval of an alternative accreditation plan from the Board of Education. Schools offering alternative education programs and schools with a graduation cohort of 50 or fewer students as defined by the graduation rate formula adopted by the board may request that the board approve an alternative accreditation plan to meet the graduation and completion index benchmark. Special purpose schools with alternative accreditation plans shall be evaluated on standards appropriate to the programs offered in the school and approved by the board prior to August 1 of the school year for which approval is requested. Any student graduating from a special purpose school with a Standard, Advanced Studies, or Modified Standard Diploma must meet the requirements prescribed in 8 VAC 20-131-50."

 

Key Points:

 

Two of the schools (Key Center and Kilmer Center) are requesting alternative measures for determining student achievement, as specified in Section 8 VAC 20-131-280.B.1. All five schools (Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Woodson Adult High School) are requesting alternative measures for calculating the graduation and completion index (GCI), which goes into effect this year.  Section 8 VAC 20-131-280.B.2, states that achievement will be based on certain criteria including:

 

"The percentage of students graduating from or completing high school based on a graduation and completion index prescribed by the Board of Education. The accreditation rating of any school with a twelfth grade shall be determined based on achievement of required SOL pass rates and percentage points on the board’s graduation and completion index. School accreditation shall be determined by the school’s current year index points or a trailing three-year average of index points that includes the current year and the two most recent years, whichever is higher. The Board of Education’s graduation and completion index shall include weighted points for diploma graduates (100 points), GED recipients (75 points), students not graduating but still in school (70 points), and students earning certificates of program completion (25 points). The Board of Education's graduation and completion index shall account for all students in the graduating class’s ninth-grade cohort, plus students transferring in, minus students transferring out and deceased students. Those students who are not included in one of the preceding categories will also be included in the index."

 

All five schools are submitting separate applications for alternative accreditation plans.  Each plan describes the mission and purpose of the school, the population of the students the school serves, the instructional programs offered by the school, and the strategies and instruments used by the schools to evaluate student progress.  In addition to this, all plans provide detailed explanations as to why portions of Standard 8 VAC 20-131-280—Expectations for School Accountability—are not appropriate measures for school and the populations they serve.  

 

Key and Kilmer Centers serve students with severe disabilities who are assessed under the Virginia Alternate Assessment Program (VAAP).  They are submitting separate, but similar, plans that include the following:

  • If VAAP results meet the required accreditation benchmarks for performance, no alternative measure will be used.
  • If VAAP results do not meet the required accreditation benchmarks, points will be assigned to the various performance levels for each student in order to obtain a composite score by content area.  An average composite score of 70 points must be achieved to meet the benchmark.
  • Students who receive a Special Diploma will count for 100 points in GCI calculations; dropouts will count for 0 points.  Students who leave the program due to extreme medical reasons will not be considered dropouts but will be removed from the cohort.

 

Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Woodson Adult High School serve students who are already behind their cohort for graduation due to life circumstances.  Each school provides programs that meet the needs of this “at-risk” population where students usually take 6 or more years to graduate.  These three schools are submitting separate, but similar, plans that include the following:

  • Extra bonus points applied to the GCI for maintaining a dropout rate below 25%.
  • Extra bonus points applied to the GCI if 50% of the students who reach the maximum age for educational services continue with their education.
  • Extra bonus points applied to the GCI if 75% of the graduate class enrolls in post-secondary education, joins the military, or obtains a full-time job.
  • Extra bonus points applied to the GCI if 75% of the students with a reduced course load due to family obligations meet the goals of their educational plans.

In addition, each school is asking that the following subgroups be removed from the cohort prior to GCI calculations:

  • Students who enroll from outside the county and do not complete two full semesters of classes
  • Students who enroll from outside the U.S. without six years of education from their home country
  • Students 18 years of age or older who enroll in FCPS as their first VA public school
  • Students 18 years of age or older who move back to their home country before obtaining a diploma
  • LEP students who have not been enrolled in a VA public school for at least 11 semesters

 

Options:        

  1. Approve the request for approval of the Alternative Accreditation Plans for Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Woodson Adult High School for submission to the Virginia Board of Education.
  2. Continue to be evaluated under the current state accreditation regulations from the Virginia Department of Education and have Key Center, Kilmer Center, Mountain View Alternative High School, Bryant Alternative High School, and Woodson Adult High School rated “Accredited with Warning” or “Provisionally Accredited-Graduation Rate” and undergo an academic review and prepare a school improvement plan in accordance with the guidelines adopted by the Virginia Board of Education, per Section 8 VAC 20-131-300.C of the Regulations Establishing Standards for Accrediting Public Schools in Virginia.

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the Alternative Accreditation Plan requests for five schools for submission to the Virginia Board of Education, as detailed in the agenda item.

6.01 Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee - Recommendation to appoint committee members to the Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item (IS; action scheduled 5/26/11)

Mar 24, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 16

6. New Business

Information

Staff Contact:          Peter J. Noonan, assistant superintendent, Instructional Services Department

 

Meeting Category:   March 24, 2011 - Regular Meeting No. 16

 

Subject:                   Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee Members

           

School Board Action Required:  Information                                

 

Related To:               Student Achievement Goal 1. Academics

 

Key Points:

 

The last Mathematics Textbook Adoption was done in 2001. The books currently being used for instruction were published in 1999 through 2001.

 

This committee will review and recommend books for use in K-12 mathematics courses.  These materials will be considered basal materials. 

 

The Constitution of Virginia gives the Virginia Board of Education the authority to adopt textbooks for use in the schools.  Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) Regulation 3004.2, Adopted Basal Instructional Materials, incorporates state adoption guidelines and requirements and specifies procedures for establishing committees to review textbooks and other basal instructional materials and make recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board for local adoption.  Each FCPS textbook review committee is composed as follows:

 

                        Teachers 50 percent

                        School-based administrators 25 percent

                        Community members 25 percent

 

Central office staff members serve as nonvoting, ex-officio members to guide and assist the work of   the committee.  All recommended books will be reviewed for content accuracy by  qualified content experts prior to public review.

 

Each School Board member shall appoint one community member to the Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee.  School-based administrators and teachers will be nominated by cluster assistant superintendents.  Participating staff members will be from the Instructional Services Department, Department of Special Services, and schools. 

 

The School Board will appoint members to review and recommend basal instructional materials to be used in Fairfax County Public Schools. 

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board appoint committee members to the Mathematics Textbook Adoption Committee, as detailed in the agenda item.
