National Safety Month - June 2014

Each June, the National Safety Council sponsors National Safety Month to promote safe and healthy employee wellness and the President’s Active Lifestyle Award+ challenge, ergonomics, preventing slips, trips, and falls, and driving safety.  This year’s theme, Safety:  It Takes All of Us, was inspired by the idea of continuous risk reduction and encourages employees to spot hazards early, evaluate their risk, and remove or control them before harm is done.

Over 184,000 FCPS students depend on school employees to provide a safe environment in schools.  Prevention, identification, and abatement of hazards are integral parts of every employee’s job. The FCPS Office of Safety and Security has established emergency plans and provided appropriate training for employees to cultivate safety throughout the school district.

Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Week - May 19-23, 2014

The Adult and Community Education (ACE) program of Fairfax County Public Schools began with an action by the School Board in 1955 and, in the 59 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills and completed their high school educations.

Through the years, adults have learned new languages, acquired job skills, transitioned to new careers, and grown as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to ACE to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education has made major contributions to the school system as a laboratory of innovation for the piloting of new programs and services in support of PreK-12 education through family literacy, summer programs, extended day programs, enrichment courses, and driver education.

Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner, as well as providing enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day, ACE has helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

[Updated 5/27/14 - Final Action]

 

Staff Contact:            Susan Quinn, chief financial officer, Department of Financial Services

 

Other Staff Present:  Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:     May 22, 2014 – Work Session No. 115

 

Subject:                      FY 2015 Approved Budget     

 

School Board Action Required: Decision                                                       

 

Key Points:

 

On February 6, 2014, the School Board adopted the FY 2015 Advertised Budget.  The advertised budget was presented to and discussed by the School Board and County Board of Supervisors at a joint budget work session on March 18, 2014. 

 

On April 29, 2014, the County Board of Supervisors adopted their FY 2015 budget that included an increase in the transfer to the School Operating Fund of 3.0 percent above the FY 2014 Approved Budget.  The increase of $51.5 million is $46.6 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget.  While the State has not yet adopted their budget, FCPS is projecting an increase of $30.0 million over the estimate included in FCPS’ FY 2015 Advertised Budget based on the current state budget proposals available. It appears very possible that the State will not adopt their budget prior to FCPS’ approved budget adoption.

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue reflects a net reduction of $19.5 million as compared to the FY 2015 Advertised Budget.  Funding from the county is $46.6 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by a projected increase in state revenue of $30.0 million and a projected increase in sales tax revenue of $2.1 million.  Tuition revenue from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $1.0 million, and other revenue will decrease by $4.0 million based on the recommendation to eliminate fees for Advanced Placement and International Baccalaureate exams.

 

Expenditures:

In total, expenditures reflect a net reduction of $19.5 million as compared to the FY 2015 Advertised Budget and include the following reductions: $6.3 million in the cost of enrollment growth and changes in student demographics; $3.9 million in employee benefit adjustments; $1.8 million in projected energy conservation savings; $10.4 million from delaying the implementation of step until November, $0.2 million for out of school support, and $0.3 million for debt service requirements.  These reductions are offset by an increase of $1.1 million for support for small schools, $1.9 million for in-school suspension support, $0.1 million for the Early Literacy Program and Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters, and $0.2 million for the staffing reserve.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt the FY 2015 Approved Budget as detailed in the FY 2015 Advertised Budget and modified with the adjustments included in this agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
