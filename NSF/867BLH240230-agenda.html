Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Type: October 6, 2008 - Work Session No. 31

SUBJECT: School Board Policy

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:

The School Board proposes the authorization of naming rights to portions of school facilities, such as fields, scoreboards, gymnasiums, or other buildings on school campuses by corporate entities to provide recognition for significant contributions to FCPS. This would require modification of Policy 8170, Naming School Facilities and Dedicating Areas of School Facilities or Grounds.
KEY POINTS:

Although schools are a public use, the implementation of naming rights for school facilities and the resultant signage on portions of school buildings or other facilities would be subject to Fairfax County Zoning Ordinance, Article 12, Signs, and may require sign permits. Regulations vary by zoning districts and are typically more restrictive in residential areas where many school facilities are located.

Article 20 of the Zoning Ordinance defines a sign as follows: “used to announce, direct attention to, identify, advertise or otherwise make anything known; and is visible from the public right of way or from adjoining property”.

Any policy modification which would recognize private or corporate benefactors to FCPS through naming rights should limit such signage so that sign location, size and lighting are not visible from adjacent residential properties or public rights of way or as may otherwise be permitted by County regulation.

OPTIONS: 1. Direct staff to revise policy to allow naming rights for portions of school facilities.
2. Make no change to existing policy.

RECOMMENDATION: That the School Board adopt Policy 8170 as set forth and modified.

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Department of Facilities and Transportation

Meeting Type: September 8, 2008, School Board Work Session No. 17


SUBJECT: Policy 8130

TYPE: New Business

BACKGROUND: Review Policy Clarifications on Administrative Boundary Changes.


KEY POINTS: Paragraph 4 of Policy 8130.5, Adjustment Procedures and Administrative Changes, sets forth three circumstances under which the Superintendent is authorized to make administrative boundary changes after consultation with affected School Board representatives. Staff is seeking clarification on two of these circumstances:
                  - an emergency or other overriding public need requires such a change
                  - less than five percent of the enrollment of each school will be affected, and analysis indicates the change will improve the operating efficiency
              Staff is seeking clarification on what constitutes overriding public need, e.g., overcrowding that impacts building function and instruction; overcrowding that can no longer be addressed by temporary facilities; transportation inefficiencies.

              Staff is also seeking clarification on 1) whether the intent of five percent of enrollment refers to projected or current enrollment and 2) whether that five percent limitation is on a per year basis or based on cumulative impact of an administrative change as it is phased over several years.

              The interpretation of these points will impact the effectiveness of the administrative boundary change policy as a tool to manage local school boundaries. Note: No clarification or change is sought for the accompanying Regulation 8130.5 which requires public notice and that a public meeting be held with the sending and receiving schools to explain the adjustment and to solicit public comments.

OPTIONS: Direct staff to revise policy for clarification
Make no change to existing policy

RECOMMENDATION: Provide clarification which would allow use of Administrative Boundary Change as a more effective and flexible tool to exercise the Board’s vested authority “to provide for the consolidation of schools or redistricting of school boundaries or adopt pupil assignment plans whenever such procedure will contribute to the efficiency of the school division.” [Code of Virginia: Section 22.1-79 (4)]

Staff Contact: Dean Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation

SUBJECT: Scope of Renovation Projects

TYPE: Discussion

BACKGROUND:
      The Capital Improvement Plan for the years 2009 – 2013 identifies $745 million in capital spending dedicated to new school facilities, renovations and facility infrastructure improvements. In addition to these requirements, there is an additional requirement to renovate the sixty-three schools that comprise the revised renovation queue. The estimated present value cost to renovate these schools is approximately $1.8 billion. Their ultimate cost will be significantly higher if existing inflationary trends continue.

      The large amount of capital required to renovate our facilities coupled with the limited amount of capital funding available from the county will result in the renovation queue being completed around 2042 and possibly longer if additional funds have to be directed to capacity enhancement projects. This means that some schools will have over 50 years elapse between renovations.
KEY POINTS:
      Considering that continuing our current program commits us to failure in terms of meeting the School Board’s goal of renovating schools on a 25 – 30 year cycle, it may be the appropriate time to consider alternatives to the current renovation methods and scope of work.

      There would be significant issues with changing the current scope of renovation projects. While it would accelerate the timing of getting to schools waiting for renovation, it does have negative implications. It would not be well received at schools near the top of the renovation queue that have been waiting for the renovation of their schools with the expectation that it would be a comprehensive renovation. It would mean that we would have to go back to each school multiple times to address all facilities needs which means overall greater disruption to the instructional program. It also would mean higher total project costs as we would lose some of the economies from contractors who now only have to mobilize once

OPTIONS:

1. Continue renovations scopes as currently defined.
2. Evaluate scope to determine options and the advantages and disadvantages of each.

RECOMMENDATION:

The School Board needs to give direction to staff about its interest in pursuing a revised renovation scope program.
