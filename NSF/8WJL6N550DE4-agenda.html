National Safety Month - June 2013

Each June, the National Safety Council sponsors National Safety Month to promote safe and healthy employee wellness and the President’s Active Lifestyle Award+ challenge, ergonomics, preventing slips, trips, and falls, and driving safety.  This year’s theme, Safety Starts With Me, encourages organizations to create a culture where people feel a personal responsibility for their safety as well as that of their coworkers, family, and friends.

Over 184,000 FCPS students depend on school employees to provide a safe environment in schools.  Prevention, identification, and abatement of hazards are integral parts of every employee’s job. The FCPS Office of Safety and Security has established emergency plans and provided appropriate training for employees to cultivate safety throughout the school district.

Recognition of National Adult and Continuing Education Week - May 20-24, 2013

The adult and community education program of Fairfax County Public Schools began with an action of the School Board in 1955, and, in the 58 years of the program’s existence, thousands of adults have learned essential skills and completed their high school educations.

Through the years,  adults have learned new languages, acquired job skills, transitioned to new careers, and grown as workers, citizens, and parents. The Fairfax County community has looked to Adult & Community Education to help develop our workforce, provide solutions to community problems through education, and create a culture of lifelong learning for residents of all ages.

Adult and Community Education has made major contributions to the school system as a laboratory of innovation for the piloting of new programs and services in support of pre-K-12 education through family literacy, summer programs, extended day programs, enrichment courses, and driver education.

Through creative programming and targeted advocacy for the adult learner – as well as provision of remediation and enrichment opportunities for youth beyond the school day – ACE has helped to create a dynamic learning community for all generations in Fairfax County.

6.01 Student Rights and Responsibilities

 


Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. three (3) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. (For information on submitting video testimony, please visit: http://www.fcps.edu/schlbd/meetings/requestspeak.shtml )  A maximum of ten (10) speaker slots are available, first come, first served. The speakers list closes at 4:30 p.m. on the last business day prior to the meeting date.  Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

Key Points:

 

On February 7, 2013, the School Board adopted the FY 2014 Advertised Budget.  The County Board of Supervisors subsequently adopted their FY 2014 budget on April 30, 2013, and included funding that was $61.7 million less than requested in FCPS’ advertised budget.  The Virginia General Assembly adopted its 2012-2014 budget, as amended, on April 3, 2013. 

 

Revenue:

In total, revenue reflects a net reduction of $44.7 $38.4 million as compared to the FY 2014 Advertised Budget.  Funding from the county is $61.7 million less than requested, and this reduction is offset by an increase in state revenue of $2.6 $8.9 million and a $14.4 million increase in the budgeted beginning balance resulting from savings realized during the FY 2013 Third Quarter Budget Review.  Revenue from Fairfax City is projected to decrease by $0.7 million offset by a $0.7 million increase in tuition, fees, and other revenue.

 

Expenditures:

In total, expenditures reflect a net reduction of $44.7 $38.4 million as compared to the FY 2014 Advertised Budget and include the following reductions: $3.7 million in salary adjustments; $7.0  $10.7 million in employee benefit compensation adjustments; $0.2 million in the cost of membership growth and changes in student demographics; $1.5 million from a freeze in the growth of school-based clerical positions; and $2.0 million in utilities.  These reductions are offset by an increase of $0.2 million for EpiPen replacement. In addition to these required adjustments, the School Board will need to identify reductions of $30.5 million prior to the budget being adopted on May 23, 2013. included the adjustments detailed in the main motion and as modified by several amendments that passed which are as follows:

Approve one additional paid nonworkday on July 5, 2013, for 12-month personnel for the 2013-2014 school year (HR; presented as new business 5/9/13)

Staff Contact:         Phyllis C. Pajardo, assistant superintendent, Human Resources

 

Meeting Category:  May 23, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                 Employee Calendar - July 5 Holiday (Paid Nonworkday for 12-Month Employees)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Background:

 

In 2001-2002, the School Board approved the reduction of two instructional school days due to the lack of inclement weather. Those two days were also granted for less than 12-month personnel. Recognizing that July 4, 2002 fell on a Thursday and that 12-month employees did not get the benefit of the two-day reduction, the Board approved Friday, July 5, 2002, as a designated holiday.

 

Also, in 2005-2006 the Board approved July 3, 2006, as a paid holiday for 12-month employees when July 4, 2006, fell on a Tuesday.  The same key points below were referenced.

 

Key Points:

 

For the 2013-2014 school year, July 4, 2013, falls on a Thursday. To recognize that workload has increased for all employees while salaries have only slightly increased, declare Friday, July 5, 2013, a paid nonworkday for 12-month employees. This would also provide some energy efficiencies as all the buildings will be closed and air-conditioning minimized for a four-day weekend. There will be a very small cost associated with overtime for security and a few other critical employees.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve one additional paid nonworkday on July 5, 2013, for 12-month personnel for the 2013-2014 school year.

[Updated 5/23/13 - major revisions highlighted]

 

Staff Contact:           Kim P. Dockery, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Other Staff Present:  MaryAnn Panarelli, director, Office of Intervention and Prevention

 

Meeting Category:     May 23, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 20

 

Subject:                     Student Rights and Responsibilities, Regulation 2601.27P

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:                Legal Requirement 

 

Key Points:

 

Regulation 2601.27P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, has been rewritten and reflects consideration of the Ad Hoc Community Committee on Student Rights and Responsibilities recommendations to the Fairfax County School Board. Major proposed revisions to the SR&R include:

 

1. Preamble with overarching philosophy

2. Reduced number of offenses which carry mandated consequence 

3. Differentiated range of consequences for elementary and secondary students 

4. Emphasis on positive behavior approach

5. Highlighted commitment to collaboration with parents to prevent and address behavior concerns

6. Provide option to recommend for reassignment to an alternative program, when appropriate, instead of recommend for expulsion. This allows principal to accurately reflect their recommendation, and avoids creating anxiety for parent that student may be removed without services.

7. Authorized use of restorative conferences, alcohol and other drug intervention seminar, and restorative behavior intervention seminar and other interventions as possible alternatives to suspension

8. Provides paper review of first time possession of marijuana, shortening process and avoiding hearing for students for whom there is clear evidence of special circumstances. Provides for hearing to consider additional input to determine if there are special circumstances 

9. Reflects change in state law reducing number of weapons require mandatory recommendation for expulsion 

10.  Informs parents of limits of role of SRO in investigating disciplinary infractions

11. Includes section explaining procedural protections for students with disabilities. 

12. Includes notice that written statement is voluntary.

 

Recommendation:  

 

That the School Board adopt Regulation 2601.27P, Student Rights and Responsibilities, as detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
