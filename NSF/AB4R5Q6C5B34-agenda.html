School Board Action Required: INFORMATION

 

Related To:   N/A

 

Key Points: 

 

1.  As of 12/31/2015, the ERFC membership included 21,585 active members, among which 75% were covered under the ERFC 2001 benefit structure (The plan

    provided for members hired on or after 7/1/2001) and 25% were covered under the ERFC Legacy structure (The plan provided for members hired prior to

    7/1/2001).

 

2.  During CY 2015, ERFC paid $161 million to its retirees and beneficiaries, 72% of whom are Virginia residents.

 

3.  For the five-year period ending March 2016, the ERFC Fund had an annualized investment return of 5.6%.

 

4.  ERFC’s asset market value was $2.1 billion at the end of the 2015 calendar year.

 

5.  ERFC’s ratio of funding value of assets to liabilities decreased to 76.0% as of 12/31/2015 from 77.7% at the prior year-end.

 

6.  ERFC’s administrative expense ratio is lower than the average fund in the $1-$5 billion range as measured in the survey conducted by the investment

     consulting firm RV Kuhns and Associates.

Meeting Category:  July 21, 2016 – Work Session

 

Subject:   Retirement Study Follow-Up

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  N/A

 

Key Points:                   

 

FCPS has hired Aon Hewitt, a provider of retirement consulting and actuarial services, to conduct a study which benchmarks FCPS’ retirement benefit levels to surrounding school divisions, Fairfax County and the federal government.  The study also included the adequacy of income replacement at retirement provided by the retirement programs that FCPS offers. 

 

On May 12, 2016, ERFC presented the history of the FCPS retirement programs along with the key provisions of the existing plans.  Aon Hewitt then provided a briefing on the results of the study, illustrating retirement benefit levels for FCPS employees at various age and service combinations with comparisons to the peer group.  Aon Hewitt also presented ERFC options for possible future consideration.

 

This is a follow-up to the May 12, 2016 work session where the School Board requested additional information on the benefit and cost impact of the proposed options, to be presented at this meeting.
