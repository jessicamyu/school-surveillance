Subject:  FY 2018 Advertised Budget

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Key Points:

 

On January 12, 2017, the Interim Superintendent presented the FY 2018 Proposed Budget. The FY 2018 Proposed Budget presentation and document are available at https://www.fcps.edu/about-fcps/budget/budget-documents.

 

The FY 2018 Proposed Budget for the School Operating Fund totals $2.8 billion and is an increase of $130.4 million, or 4.9 percent, over the FY 2017 Approved Budget. To balance the budget, a 5.7 percent increase in funding from the County is requested.

 

The two key priorities include investments in the Strategic Plan.

 

Employee Salaries (in support of Ignite: Premier Workforce, Goal 3)

  • A step increase for all eligible employees.
  • An additional $33.2 million investment to implement one-fourth of the new teacher salary scale, including a market scale adjustment. With this level of investment each year, full implementation of the new scale is anticipated by FY 2021.
  • Placeholder funding of $14.9 million to implement new nonteacher salary scales ($7.0 million), including an average 1% market scale adjustment ($7.9 million).

 Classroom Resources (in support of Ignite: Student Success, Goal 1)

  • $0.5 million to support investment in early childhood education, the local match requirement necessary to receive federal and state funding.
  • $2.7 million new investment is needed for the adoption of social studies textbooks. Funding of $5.1 million will be provided from the textbook reserve, bringing the total for new textbooks to $7.8 million.
  • $2.4 million is needed for an assessment tool, Universal Screener, to help identify remediation and acceleration needs for students in literacy and numeracy.

 

During the work session, questions regarding the FY 2018 Proposed Budget and potential amendments to the main motion for the advertised budget will be discussed. The draft main motion states that the School Board adopt the FY 2018 Advertised Budget as detailed in the Superintendent’s Proposed Budget.
