Meeting Category:    June 6, 2011 – Work Session No. 124

 

Subject:                     Adult and Community Education  (ACE) Advisory Committee Annual Report

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Related To:                Policy 1710, Citizen Advisory Committees to the School Board

 

Key Points:

 

School Board Charge:

1. Examine the organizational and curriculum alignment between K-12 and ACE programs, and identify opportunities for increased collaboration and synergy in supporting the School Board’s student achievement goals.

2. Assess current community advertising and outreach methods as they relate to course enrollment, and explore methods to promote enrollment in ACE programs.

 

Response:

1. The Committee identified numerous examples of vertical articulation and coordination with dynamic links to K-12 programs at the elementary school level via Pre K-12 ESOL and the K-12 World Languages and at the high school level via career and technical education and ACE certification and Adult High School Completion opportunities.

2. ACE expanded its outreach and marketing strategies, and launched well-received new courses in response to perceived demand.  Enrollments have rebounded and increased above those of a year ago.

 

Committee Observations:

In the second fiscal year of the reorganization of ACE and AHSC into different departments, the Committee continues its oversight responsibility.  The Committee reports that representatives of the three plus one sections in two departments have participated in the Advisory Committee meetings and coordinated successfully.  Presentations, both staff suggested and Committee requested and follow-up information were provided, and the vital team approach has been a winning symbiosis for ACE.  The Committee looks forward to continued expansion of curriculum linkages in support of the School Board’s student achievement goals.  The Committee looks forward to additional outreach innovations to attract and inform the community of the benefits of lifelong learning and to continuing enrollment increases.

Staff Contact:               Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:       June 6, 2011 – Work Session No. 124

 

Subject:                          Facilities Planning Advisory Council 2010-11 Annual Report to the School Board

 

School Board Action Required:  Discussion

 

Background:

 

On September 23, 2010, the School Board approved the formation of a Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) as a new advisory council of the Board. The mission of the advisory council is to advise and inform the staff and School Board in the development of comprehensive, long term plans for facilities needs in the most effective and efficient way.  FPAC is made up of 13 citizens – one from each Fairfax County magisterial district, one from the City of Fairfax, and three at-large members.  Facilities-related issues that may be considered by the advisory council may include, but are not limited to:

 

  • school program capacity
  • enrollment and projections
  • transportation and operating efficiencies related to facilities planning
  • Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) prioritization
  • creative financing and construction strategies
  • scope of renovations
  • school closures and new schools
  • student accommodation planning (building additions/modular relocations/ review of school boundaries)

 

 Key Points:

 

Consistent with the School Board guidelines for advisory committees, FPAC submits its annual report to the School Board, including an executive summary and recommendations, list of meeting dates, attendance reports, and voting records.
