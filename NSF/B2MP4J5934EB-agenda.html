Meeting Category:  July 15, 2019 - Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Goal 2: Caring Culture

 

Outcome:  To understand and discuss the results of the first division-wide family engagement survey    

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

Research tells us that families have a major influence on their children’s achievements through school and through life. When schools build partnerships with families, respond to their concerns, and honor their contributions, they promote a range of benefits for students, including improved school readiness, higher student achievement, better social skills and behavior, and increased likelihood of high school graduation. In spring 2019, FCPS, in collaboration with K12 Insight, developed and administered the first family engagement survey across the division. The survey was made available in 9 languages, including English, and more than 29,000 responses were received. The survey questions were focused on the topics of welcoming environment, communication, student success, advocacy, family and school partnerships, and collaboration with the community.

 

The survey was open from March 24 to April 14. Email invitations with unique survey links were sent to parents. Parents could also participate via a public link on the division’s website. Paper surveys were available on request. Parents with children attending more than one school could take the survey for each school.

 

A draft Family Engagement policy is included for preview and consideration.

Summary/Background (Key Points):

All of the FY19 accounts have been closed subject to the annual audit. A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the nine funds. All comparisons are against the FY19 Third Quarter Budget Review.

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the revenue variance and the FY20 budgeted beginning balance, flexibility reserve, fuel contingency, textbook reserve, and other commitments, the FY19 funds available total $35.0 million.

 

Revenue variances begin on page one of the agenda attachment, and FY19 actual total funds available are $1.4 million more, or 0.05 percent, of the FY19 Third Quarter Budget Review projection. State Aid for FY19 totals $470.0 million, which is $0.8 million less than budgeted, and sales tax receipts total $207.1 million, $1.6 million more than budgeted. Federal revenue totals $50.1 million, which is $0.7 million less than budgeted primarily as a net result of unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and re-appropriated in FY20. Other revenue receipts, including tuition, fees, other service charges, and miscellaneous revenue, are $1.3 million more than the third quarter estimate.

 

Expenditure variances on page two of the agenda attachment, total $33.7 million. The total expenditure variance of $33.7 million is 1.1 percent of the FY19 Third Quarter Budget total disbursements and is largely attributed to unanticipated lapse and turnover of $34.2 million from salaries and employee benefits offset by $1.5 million from other accounts. Multiyear unspent federal grant funds of $1.0 million is carried forward and re-appropriated for FY20.

 

As a result of the revenue and expenditure variances, the total funding available from FY19 totals $35.0 million. This agenda item includes recommendations for FY20 expenditure adjustments. Items identified as part of the FY20 Approved Budget include $0.7 million to support the first year of three-year plan to bring IA’s and PHTA’s salaries to 50 percent of teacher salaries on the BA lane, $0.3 million for professional development to address challenging behaviors of students and classroom management support, and $0.5 million to bring FCPS’ intranet to accessibility standards. Prior committed priorities and requirements include $3.6 million to continue FCPS’ annual commitment to allocate funding from year-end to major maintenance projects. Other funding to support strategic investments includes $0.6 million to support the equity plan for discipline policies and practices; $0.2 million for online discipline tool; $0.2 million for Trades for Tomorrow; $3.3 million for the staffing contingency; $0.4 million for modifications that are necessary to ensure two softball fields are Title IX compliant, and $0.4 million to expand the Global STEM Challenges program.

All the remaining funding available, $24.9 million, is recommended to be set aside for the FY21 budgeted beginning balance. The FY20 beginning balance is $25.5 million.

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject: Procedures for Naming School Facilities and Dedicating Areas of School Facilities or Grounds

 

Staff Contact: John Torre, executive director, Office of Communication and Community Relations

 

Other Staff Present: John Foster, division counsel

 

Meeting Category: July 15, 2019 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link: Caring Culture

 

Problem Statement: As discussed at the February 7, 2019 Forum, the Superintendent will share proposed changes to the working process for Regulation 8170.7

 

Outcome: Superintendent will implement the proposed new Regulation 8170.7.

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

At the February 7, 2019 Forum, the School Board considered the following topic request:

To modify our school naming/renaming policy in an effort to ensure an inclusive, respectful learning environment as outlined in our adopted One Fairfax Policy.

 

After discussion, the Board approved the referral to a work session the following objective:

 

We must examine in a transparent and comprehensive way the criteria by which FCPS can name or rename schools appropriately. This discussion should have an intentional examination of schools that are named after Confederate soldiers or named after individuals with Confederate ties -- because we have recognized that Confederate values are ones that do not align with our community.

 

Direct the Superintendent to provide to the Board via a work session information on:

- schools named after Confederate Soldiers

- schools named after individuals with Confederate ties

- date that each school was named or renamed

- reasoning/history for name choice

 

In addition, direct the Superintendent to develop a working process for action by the Board, informed by best practices, including community wide engagement, that the School Board will follow once they determine what names should be considered for change.

 

Staff research has determined there are two schools named after Confederate soldiers and several additional schools with Confederate ties.

 

The two schools named after soldiers are:

  • Robert E. Lee High School
  • Mosby Woods Elementary School
  • There is a third facility owned by the City of Fairfax School Board and where FCPS students attend.  Any decision to rename this facility rests with the City of Fairfax School Board.

 

 

Schools named after individuals with Confederate ties:

  • Haycock Elementary School
  • Lees Corner Elementary School
  • Sangster Elementary School
  • Shrevewood Elementary School
