Fire Prevention Week - October 5–11, 2014

 

Fire Prevention Week 2014 focuses on the importance of smoke alarms.  The official theme, Working Smoke Alarms Save Lives:  Test Yours Every Month! encourages citizens to ensure they have smoke alarms in their homes and that those smoke alarms are in working condition.  Working smoke alarms cut the risk of dying in home fires in half.

 

During Fire Prevention Week, October 5-11, fire safety educators around the country will work with citizens to teach them about fire safety tips, encourage them to regularly test their smoke alarms, and provide activities featuring Sparky the Fire Dog.

Approve the installation of tobacco-free signage, in cooperation with the Fairfax County Park Authority and the Live Healthy Fairfax Transformation Grant, on all Fairfax County Public Schools fields and playgrounds (S; presented as new business 9/4/14)

Staff Contact:         Division Counsel, John E. Foster

 

Meeting Category:  September 18, 2014 - Regular Meeting No. 4

 

Subject:                  Tobacco-free Signage for all FCPS Fields and Playgrounds

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related to:              School Board Policy 4419, Smoke-Free Environment

 

Key Points:

 

School Board Policy 4419, Smoke-Free Environment, was adopted January 13, 1994, to prohibit smoking and the use of tobacco products in all Fairfax County Public schools, administrative offices, facilities, and FCPS vehicles. This policy authorizes the Superintendent to post signs and otherwise advise employees and the community of this policy.

 

The School Board further supports tobacco-free FCPS fields and playgrounds. Funding is available to FCPS from the Live Healthy Fairfax Transformation grant through late September 2014, which would pay for the creation and installation of tobacco-free signage for all FCPS-owned fields and playgrounds. The signs would read "Tobacco-Free Play Zone. Thank you for your cooperation. If you need help to quit smoking, call 1-800-QUIT-NOW or visit www.smokefree.gov "

 

Numerous scientific and medical studies, including those by the Centers for Disease Control, have concluded that smoking harms nearly every organ in the human body, and causes many diseases, including heart disease, lung cancer, and stroke.

 

Consensus on this issue was obtained by the School Board on July 24, 2014, at the forum work session to proceed with this effort to place signs on FCPS fields and playgrounds.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the installation of tobacco-free signage, in cooperation with the Fairfax County Park Authority and the Live Healthy Fairfax Transformation Grant, on all Fairfax County Public Schools fields and playgrounds.

Key Points:

 

The current version of Regulation 3009, Challenged Library and Instructional Materials, establishes an Interdepartmental Review Committee to review challenges of library and instructional materials by residents.  Six School Board members (on a rotating basis) will each select an individual to serve a one-year term on the Interdepartmental Review Committee.  For 2014-2015 the Board members making appointments are Tamara Derenak Kaufax (Lee), Sandy Evans (Mason), Elizabeth Schultz (Springfield), Ilryong Moon (At Large), Ryan McElveen (At Large), and Ted Velkoff (At Large). Other committee members are appointed to serve one-year terms as set forth in the current version of Regulation 3009.

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points: 

 

At the July 24, 2014, School Board meeting, the Board approved the following 2014- 2015 charge for Human Relations Advisory Committee: 

 

Currently approved 2014-15 HRAC charge:

 

"The HRAC will work with the Office of Equity and Employee Relations to provided oversight and feedback related to FCPS’s efforts to monitor, address, and provide education related to peer-on-peer behavior including bullying and sexual misconduct. This will include ensuring consistent definitions of conduct as well as consistent reporting requirements of FCPS staff."

 

HRAC is recommending that the Board approve the following revised 2014 - 2015 charge:

 

Revised 2014-15 HRAC charge

 

"The HRAC will work with the Office of Equity and Employee Relations in providing evaluation of and feedback of FCPS efforts related to peer-to-peer behaviors including bullying, harassment, and sexual misconduct.

The HRAC recommendation is based on the following rationale:

  • The proposed charge clarifies the role o f the HRAC.
  • The proposed charge supports the spirit of the previously approved charge.
  • The HRAC co-chairs wished to ensue the members were focused on the review of the FCPS efforts to report, record, and characterize incidents of student mistreatment.
  • With the proposed charge, the HRAC will serve as a body to endure the fidelity of the process and provide input related to community concerns.

Key Points:

 

Staff recommends that the School Board continue to lease 47,000 square feet of warehouse space, located in Springfield near the Capitol Beltway and known as the Instructional Programs Support Center (IPSC). This warehouse primarily serves the Department of Instructional Services (ISD) and, to a lesser extent, the Department of Special Services (DSS), Department of Information Technology (DIT), Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (FTS), and the Department of Financial Services (FS), including:

 

 

  • ISD-Library Support Services
  • ISD-Musical Instruments
  • ISD-ESOL Library
  • DSS-Early Childhood; Special Programs
  • ISD-Title I Library
  • DIT-Forms and Records Storage
  • ISD-Fine Arts Library
  • FTS-Modular Furniture Parts
  • ISD-ACE Text Books

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
