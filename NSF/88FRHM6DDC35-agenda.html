FACILITIES AND TRANSPORTATION SERVICES

 

A.    Facilities:  The Superintendent will provide the necessary infrastructure to support instructional programs and operations.  The Superintendent will:

 

1.    Ensure facilities are clean and safe.

2.    Develop and annual report on a master plan required to implement an effective preventive maintenance program.

3.    Provide for public use of facilities at a reasonable net cost to the school system, as long as student safety, student functions, and the instructional program are not compromised and use guidelines are administered consistently.

4.    Establish and regularly update a Facilities Comprehensive Plan, which reflects School Board policy and guides decision making to ensure sound instructional practices; a reasonable balance of capacity utilization; and wise, efficient, and environmentally-responsible use of resources.

5.    Establish an objectively prioritized Capital Improvement Program that consists of major maintenance, renovation, and new construction which ensures that classroom capacity and infrastructure meet instructional program and community needs and which endeavors to be equitable across the county.

 

B.   Transportation:   The Superintendent will assure the transportation of all students in a safe and timely manner, to instructional programs for which they are eligible or that meet their needs, in an efficient, effective manner.

 

C.   Safety and Security and Emergency Preparedness:  The Superintendent will provide physical security and safety programs that ensure the protection of students, staff members, visitors, and FCPS property.

 

1.  Ensure that updated emergency management plans are in place, that key personnel receive training as appropriate, and that a collaborative and effective working relationship is maintained with local, state, and federal emergency management representatives.

Staff Contact:             Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:     July 18, 2011 – Work Session No. 6

 

Subject:                     Proposed Program/Attendance Area Adjustment Annandale Regional Study        

 

School Board Action Required:   Discussion

 

Background:

 

On April 28, 2011, the School Board authorized a regional boundary study for elementary, middle, and high schools in the Annandale area in order to create an attendance area for a new elementary school at the Lacey site and to relieve overcrowding at Annandale High School.  The scope of study included the following schools:

 

Elementary:  Annandale Terrace, Beech Tree, Belvedere, Braddock, Bren Mar Park, Columbia, North Springfield, Pine Spring, Sleepy Hollow, Westlawn, and Woodburn

 

Middle:  Frost, Glasgow, Holmes, Jackson, Key, Lake Braddock Secondary (7thand 8th), Poe, and Twain

 

High: Annandale, Edison, Falls Church, Lake Braddock Secondary (9th– 12th), Lee, Stuart, and Woodson

 

In addition to approving a scope of study, the School Board also adopted the following criteria.  To the extent feasible and practicable: help balance and accommodate current and projected enrollments; have geographic proximity to the school site; provide socio-economic and ESOL balance; adhere to core capacity; respect man-made and natural barriers; minimize split neighborhoods and split feeder assignments; maximize program stability; minimize additional capital/operating costs; and consider not only these criteria, but also any other factors that will contribute to the improved operational efficiency and instructional effectiveness of the school system, pursuant to Policy 8130, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings.

 

The authorization of this study continued the work of an ad hoc community-based review of these issues initiated by the School Board in 2010. The ad hoc study committees presented their reports to the School Board in January 2011.  The presentation included solutions requiring boundary changes as well as non-boundary solutions. These reports are the foundation for this regional study, and may be found along with other background information at http://www.fcps.edu/fts/planning/annandalestudy/index.htm

 

Key Points:

 

Staff conducted multiple community dialogue meetings in which four initial options, including a non-boundary solution were presented for review and feedback.  The non-boundary solution involved changing grade configurations at Holmes and Poe Middle schools and at Annandale High School.  Three of the options provided coordinated boundary changes from elementary through high school.  The non-boundary solution (Option 4) only addressed grade level configuration changes at the middle and high school levels.

In the three boundary change options initially presented to the community, it should be noted that:

  • the new elementary school at the Lacey site was proposed to include grades K-5 and feed to Poe Middle and Falls Church High school; AAP students would attend centers at Belvedere Elementary and Glasgow Middle schools;
  • enrollment relief at the middle school level was provided through a combination of boundary adjustments and re-locating the AAP Center from Glasgow Middle to either Poe or Holmes Middle School;
  • enrollment relief for Annandale High School was provided through boundary adjustments with Woodson High, Edison High, Stuart High, or Lake Braddock Secondary;
  • overcrowding at Jackson Middle School could not be addressed with the current scope of study and is the reason the new elementary school only fed to Poe Middle School;
  • sufficient enrollment buffer was retained at the new elementary school to accommodate a future sixth grade which would provide an opportunity for the school to feed to Jackson Middle, at such time as capacity becomes available;
  • a need for more center-based special education classes in the region was identified by the Department of Student Services (DSS), some capacity was reserved at Lacey for that purpose;
  • a target maximum enrollment of 2,350 students was identified for Annandale High School in order to minimize the number of students re-assigned through a boundary change, and to provide flexibility in the school’s capacity allowing for the removal of most trailers and a portion of the modular addition over the next several years. 

The most prominent community comments, including the on-line feedback, focused on key elements  of the proposed options as summarized below:

  • The proposed relocation of the AAP Center from Glasgow Middle School
  • The proposed elimination of the middle school split feeder at Wakefield Forest Elementary School
  • The extent of impacts on Belvedere and Bren Mar Park Elementary schools and their feeder middle and high schools
  • Cost implications related to providing additional capacity at Frost Middle School
  • Busing, cost, and instructional implications in Option 4, which proposed new grade configurations for Annandale High (10-12), Poe Middle (8-9), and Holmes Middle (6-7)
  • Grandfathering provisions and the pace of relief for Annandale High School
  • The proposed reassignment of Wakefield Forest Elementary attendance area to Lake Braddock Secondary School 

The preliminary options generated a wide range of comments and preferences that are reflected on the feedback forms collected from community dialogue sessions and the web-based surveys.  Moving the fewest number of students (Option 1) appeared to be strongly favored by most respondents.  The logic of eliminating split feeders and attendance islands (Option 2) also appeared to be strongly favored.  In the broadest of terms, changing existing K-5 schools to K-6 (Option 2 and 3) did not garner much support.  The non-boundary solution (Option 4), which would change the grade configuration for Holmes and Poe Middle and Annandale High resulted in very similar percentages of communities and/or individuals who ranked the option either highest or lowest.  Based on a scale of one to four, with one being the lowest and four being the highest, respondents were asked to rank the options by preference.  According to the comments received from the online community dialogue feedback form, the respondents ranked Option 1 highest (33%), followed by Option 4 (31.5%), then Option 2 (29.6%).  The least preferred option was Option 3 (10.7%).  It is noted that out of 1,136 respondents, only half (547) indicated their preference to the options.

 

Although the non boundary option (Option 4) would have retained all of the current Annandale High School attendance area, it would result in the greatest movement of students (1,520 students in the 2012-13 and 1,750 students in the 2016-17 school year), resulting in overcrowding at Holmes and Poe Middle, and as identified by staff, would have resulted in significant facilities and instructional impacts requiring:

  • Building modifications at both Holmes and Poe Middle
  • Relocation of some existing programs
  • Addressing logistical demands of instructional programs at grades nine and ten that support accelerated course options and intervention efforts for students
  • Consideration of change to clubs, athletics, fine arts, and athletic programs
  • Change in bell schedule at Poe Middle
  • Additional 12 buses to transport students who currently walk to all three schools
  • Additional solutions to accommodate students currently assigned to Poe Middle that feed to Falls Church High and Wakefield Forest Elementary students assigned to Poe Middle
  • Additional time to implement

Creating another new and unique school model further constrains the School Board’s flexibility to accommodate boundary and/or program changes in the future, a problem evidenced by the current mix of K-5 and K-6 elementary schools in this region.

 

Given the community comments and the School Board’s objectives and policies, a combination of boundary changes are recommended for the elementary, middle, and high school levels that draw upon elements of the initial boundary options.  Consistent with other middle and high school studies, staff believes that a more gradual transition to implement the boundary changes should be accommodated for the middle and high school levels.  Implementation would generally begin with rising 9th grade students at the high school level and rising 6th or 7th grade students at the middle school level (depending upon the grade model).  Elementary school changes would begin with grades K-4 or K-5 (depending upon the grade model) allowing 5th grade (or 6th grade) students to remain back at their current elementary school assignment.

 

Detailed Staff Recommendations:

 

New Elementary at Lacey Site (K-5)

 

From Annandale Terrace Elementary

  • The area between Hummer Road and Annandale Road, north of Royce Street, generally including Woolfenden, Galanis Woods, Annawood, Ramblewood, Moore & Keith, and Trinity Estates subdivisions

From Beech Tree Elementary

  • The area south of Homes Run including Broyhill Crest, Kenwood, Rolf Heights, and Annandale Mews subdivisions

From Belvedere Elementary

  • The area west of Sleepy Hollow Road including Sleepy Hollow Woods, Overlook Knolls, and Columbia Pines subdivisions

From Woodburn Elementary

  • Parliament Village Apartments and the area between Annandale Road and Gallows Road including, Kenwood, Beverly Manor, Chatelain Village, Annandale Gardens, and Annandale subdivisions; also areas Southeast of Annandale Road including Kimberly Hill, Masonville Heights, State Hill, State Crest Woods, Annandale Woods, Round Tree Park, and State Hill Woods subdivisions

Other Elementary Level Changes:

 

From Annandale Terrace Elementary To Woodburn Elementary

  • The area between Hummer Road and Annandale Road, south of Royce Street including Marquerite and Perrel, Rainbow Glen, Hummer Woods Estates, Woodland Place, and Fairmont Gardens subdivisions

From Pine Spring Elementary To Beech Tree Elementary

  • The area west of Tripps Run and East of Marshall Street, including City Park Homes subdivision

 

The attendance area recommendation for the new elementary school at the Lacey site is the same as featured in initial Option 2.  This proposed boundary eliminates the attendance area islands for Woodburn and Pine Spring Elementary schools and eliminates the current middle and high school split feeders for Belvedere and Beech Tree Elementary schools.  However, the proposal does establish the new elementary school at the Lacey site as a split feeder sending approximately 85% of its students to Poe Middle School and Falls Church High School and approximately 15% to Glasgow Middle School and Stuart High School.  The new elementary school is projected to open with 475 students in the 2012-13 school year, growing to 557 by school year 2016-17.  The estimated ESOL and Free and Reduced Lunch (FRL) percentages are 45% and 45.7%, respectively.  Leaving an enrollment buffer for this school provides an opportunity for the addition of center-based special needs programs, a FECEP program, and potentially, Level IV school-based AAP services.  Depending upon future programming, having some enrollment buffer also provides an opportunity to add 6th grade at the school.  This would allow the school to ultimately feed to Jackson Middle, at such time as the middle school has available capacity.  Although the proposed boundary for the new elementary school provides significant enrollment relief for Annandale Terrace and Woodburn Elementary schools, and to a lesser extent, relief to Pine Spring Elementary, these schools are projected to remain in excess of 110% building utilization.  No changes are proposed at the elementary level for Bren Mar Park Elementary.  The new elementary school at the Lacey site is not sufficiently proximate to provide relief for current and projected overcrowding at that school.  Staff will work towards facility and program changes to effect relief at Bren Mar Park Elementary in the near future. 

 

Middle School:

 

From Poe to Frost

  • That portion of the Wakefield Forest Elementary attendance area that is west of I-495 and generally east of Wakefield Chapel Road

From Poe to Holmes

  • All of the Bren Mar Park Elementary attendance area

From Poe to Jackson

  • The area between Hummer Road and Annandale Road, south of Royce Street including Marquerite and Perrel, Rainbow Glen, Hummer Woods Estates, Woodland Place, and Fairmont Gardens subdivisions (Grades 7 and 8 only, Grade 6 will be assigned to Woodburn Elementary School). This corresponds with Annandale Terrace Elementary School attendance area being reassigned to Woodburn Elementary School.

From Jackson to Glasgow

  • The area west of Tripps Run and east of Marshall Street including City Park Homes (includes 6thgrade from Pine Spring Elementary).  This corresponds with the Pine Spring Elementary attendance island being reassigned to Beech Tree Elementary School.

From Jackson to Poe

  • Parliament Village Apartments plus the area between Annandale Road and Gallows Road, including Kenwood, Beverly Manor, Chatelain Village, Annandale Gardens, and Annandale subdivisions; also areas Southeast of Annandale Road including Kimberly Hill, Masonville Heights, State Hill, State Crest Woods, Annandale Woods, Round Tree Park and State Hill Woods subdivisions (includes Grade 6 from Woodburn Elementary School).  This corresponds with the Woodburn Elementary School attendance area being reassigned to the new elementary school at the Lacey site.

 

The recommended attendance area changes at the middle school level eliminate the current split feeder for Wakefield Forest Elementary (which currently sends half of its 6th grade students to Poe Middle at 7th grade) and the non-contiguous boundary for Holmes Middle School.  To accommodate the proposed changes, staff recommends placement of a modular unit at Frost Middle until permanent capacity may be constructed in conjunction with the planned renovation at Frost in 6-7 years.  A temporary modular addition (6-8 rooms) at a cost of approximately $3 million would address the needed capacity.  It should be noted that only one third (approximately) of that cost would be attributable to the re-assignment of Wakefield Forest Elementary to Frost Middle from Poe Middle.  No relocation of the AAP Center at Glasgow Middle is proposed at this time; however, capacity enhancements and/or programs changes may need to be considered for Glasgow and Holmes Middle in the future.  Middle school enrollments will be closely monitored, especially at Glasgow, since two of its elementary feeders (Bailey’s and Glen Forest Elementary schools) have experienced significant growth in the primary grades and are projected to grow substantially. 

 

High School:

 

From Annandale to Woodson

  • The eastern half of the Wakefield Forest Elementary attendance area, generally west of I-495 and east of Wakefield Chapel Road.

From Annandale to Edison

  • The Bren Mar Park Elementary attendance area

 From Falls Church to Stuart

  • The area west of Tripps Run and east of Marshall Street including City Park Homes.  This corresponds with the Pine Spring Elementary attendance island being reassigned to Beech Tree Elementary School.

The recommended attendance area changes at the high school level alleviate overcrowding at Annandale High School and have minimal impact on the ESOL and FRL demographics.  This was a paramount concern expressed in the ad hoc community study.  The proposed boundary change would re-assign approximately 121 students out of Annandale High School in the 2012-13 school year, increasing to 441 students in the 2016-17 school year.  This reflects a gradual phase-in of the boundary change beginning with incoming 9th grade students in the school year 2012-13.  The re-assignment of Bren Mar Park attendance area students to Edison High School creates a split feeder for Holmes Middle School.  However, Edison is presently the most underutilized high school in the study and absent any boundary change will remain the most underutilized high school in the study.  The proposed change from Annandale to Edison High School will reduce travel times by 2 to 12 minutes.  This information is based on studies conducted by the transportation office in which times from current bus stops were measured to Annandale High and Edison High.  In each case the travel time to Edison was less than the time needed to reach Annandale High.  The recommended boundary change from Annandale High School to Woodson High School eliminates the Wakefield Forest split feeder and utilizes available capacity at Woodson.  The recommended reassignment from Falls Church High School to Stuart High School corresponds with the change from Pine Spring Elementary School to Beech Tree Elementary School and Jackson Middle School to Glasgow Middle School.  This change avoids creating a split feeder for Beech Tree at the high school level.

The recommended boundary changes reflect the key community feedback comments and the School Board objectives and policies:

  • Boundary changes can be accommodated with incoming classes at both middle and high schools;
  • No relocation of the AAP Center at Glasgow Middle is proposed;
  • No new K-6 grade configurations are proposed at existing K-5 schools, but the option is preserved for the new elementary school;
  • Attendance islands and non-contiguous school boundaries are addressed at elementary and middle school levels;
  • Adequate relief is provided for Annandale High, Poe Middle, Annandale Terrace, and Woodburn Elementary schools; and
  • Minimal ESOL and FRL demographic impacts result from the recommended boundary changes.

Advanced Academic Program (AAP) Assignments

 

In addition to boundary changes that balance enrollments and create an attendance area for the new elementary school at the Lacey site, several AAP center assignment changes are proposed.  The proposed AAP center assignment changes serve to align school boundary changes with AAP center assignments.  Annandale Terrace and Woodburn Elementary students assigned to the new elementary school at the Lacey site would attend AAP centers at Belvedere Elementary and Glasgow Middle schools.   Students reassigned from Annandale Terrace to Woodburn Elementary School would attend AAP centers at Mantua Elementary and Jackson Middle schools.  Students reassigned from Pine Spring Elementary to Beech Tree Elementary would attend AAP centers at Belvedere Elementary and Glasgow Middle schools.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve adjustments to school attendance areas in order to establish an attendance area for the new elementary school at the Lacey site and to contribute to the efficiency of the school division, pursuant to Virginia Code Section 22.1-79(4), and Policy 8130.6, Local School Boundaries, Program Assignments, and School Closings, as shown in Appendix A to be effective for the 2012-13 school year for the following schools:

 

Annandale Terrace, Beech Tree, Belvedere, Pine Spring, and Woodburn Elementary Schools; Frost, Glasgow, Holmes, Jackson, and Poe Middle Schools; Annandale, Edison, Falls Church, Stuart, and Woodson High Schools;

 

And, further, that the School Board authorize staff to use existing bond authority within the construction reserve account; to design and construct a modular addition at Frost Middle School; and to otherwise implement the Board’s action, as detailed in the agenda item.

Key Points:

 

Some Fairfax County high school communities are discussing the topic of honors, Advanced Placement (AP), and International Baccalaureate (IB) course offerings.  FCPS’ goal is to ensure all of our students are college and career ready.  The outcome we seek is to ensure all students are college ready and workforce ready. Therefore:

 

  • FCPS offers both rigorous college preparatory and advanced academic courses.
  • Offering two levels of courses (standard and advanced) provides greater access to advanced coursework for traditionally underrepresented populations.
  • A curriculum cross-walk and alignment study between former honors courses and AP courses revealed no significant difference in content or rigor. There is no room between the standard course offering and the AP course to insert an honors course.
  • Research shows that students are more likely to be accepted to college, be more successful while in college, and earn a degree if they take AP or IB courses in high school.
  • Advanced Placement courses and the International Baccalaureate Diploma Program offer distinct rigorous course offerings that are in line with their unique educational missions and were not designed to be compared.

All of the FY 2011 accounts have been closed, subject to the annual audit.  A summary of the revenue and expenditure variances is provided for each of the ten funds.  All comparisons are against the FY 2011 Third Quarter Estimate. 

 

In the School Operating Fund, after accounting for the FY 2012 and FY 2013 budgeted beginning balances as included in the FY 2012 Approved Budget, flexibility reserve, and other commitments, the funds available are $55.4 million.

 

FY 2011 actual revenue was $1.5 million lower than the estimate or 99.9 percent of the FY 2011 Third Quarter Estimate.  State aid is $0.7 million and sales tax projections are $3.4 more than projected.  A decrease of $11.7 million in federal revenue is due primarily to $13.5 million in unspent multiyear grant awards that will be carried forward and reappropriated in FY 2012,offset by a net increase in other federal funding comprised primarily of an increase of $1.2 million in Impact Aid.  The decreases in federal revenue are offset by increases of $6.2 million in tuition, fees, and other revenue.

 

After accounting for the flexibility reserve, unexpended grants, and other commitments, FY 2011 actuaI expenditures were $56.9 million, or 2.5 percent, lower than the FY 2011 Third Quarter Estimate.  Greater vacancy and turnover and continued efforts to conserve resources and achieve efficiencies resulted in these savings.  The FY 2012 budget has incorporated a recognition of higher turnover through a decrease in the salary base and includes an increase in budgeted lapse.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the revenue and expenditure adjustments as detailed in the agenda item, including setting aside $4.4 million for a centralized textbook fund and allocating the remaining FY 2011 ending balance from the school operating fund of $51.0 million for the FY 2013 beginning balance.
