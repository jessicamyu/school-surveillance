National Hispanic American Heritage Month - September 15-October 15

America's cultural diversity has always been a great strength of our Nation. The Hispanic-american community has a long and important history of commitment to our Nation's core values, and the contributions of this community have helped make our country great. During National Hispanic American Heritage Month, we celebrate the many achievements of Hispanic Americans and recognize their contributions to our country.

In 1968, the Congress authorized President Lyndon Johnson to proclaim National Hispanic Heritage Week, and this observance was expanded in 1988 to a month-long celebration to honor our Nation's Hispanic heritage. During this month, America celebrates the traditions, ancestry, and unique experiences of those who trace their roots to Spain, Mexico, the countries of Central and South America, and the Caribbean.

Throughout our history, Hispanic Americans have enriched the American way of life, and we recognize the millions of Hispanic Americans whose love of family, hard work, and community have helped unite us as a people and sustain us as a Nation.

Resolution Naming the College and Career Center
at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology
“The Elizabeth V. Lodal College and Career Center”

WHEREAS, Elizabeth V. Lodal has distinguished herself at the highest professional level serving Fairfax County students as assistant principal, associate principal, middle school principal, and high school principal, most recently at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST), and supporting the mission of this great school system for 33 years with a passion for educating our youth; and
WHEREAS, she used her outstanding leadership skills to create a community of collaboration and shared vision as TJHSST reexamined its mission and academic focus to more truly reflect the original mandate as the Northern Virginia Regional Governor’s School for Mathematics, Science, and Technology; and
WHEREAS, she purposefully set about putting TJHSST in the public eye to encourage a broad commitment to the school’s mission, encouraging the business community to re-affirm its commitment to and support of this school and advancing the dream of establishing a facility that provided the optimal learning environment for the work of its nearly 2,000 motivated students; and
WHEREAS, she diligently fought for increasing the enrollment of underrepresented minorities at TJHSST, demonstrated respect for all people, and emphasized the education of the whole child to guide the leaders of tomorrow in all areas of life; and
WHEREAS, she has modeled creativity, thinking big and outside the box, openness and respect for new ideas, knowledge sharing with the greater community and across the nation through a variety of venues, and has set a very high standard for taking on difficult issues with dignity and integrity with students as the main focus as she moved this renowned school firmly into the 21st century;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the College and Career Center at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology “The Elizabeth V. Lodal College and Career Center” in recognition of her deep caring for this learning community and all school communities where she devoted her time, talents, and boundless energy, and her exemplary leadership as an educator who has worked tirelessly to make this world a better place for children.

      Staff Contact: Dean A. Tistadt, Chief Operating Officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

      SUBJECT: W.T. Woodson High School Renovation Project – School Board’s Delegation of Procurement and Contract Authority

      RECOMMENDATION:
          That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent, or his designee, to procure and contract in accordance with the Virginia Public Procurement Act (including the issuance and execution of all necessary and appropriate documents including, without limitation, contracts and contract change orders) on behalf of the School Board for the purchase of construction, goods and services specifically related to the W.T. Woodson High School renovation project, provided each such individual purchase transaction does not exceed $1,000,000.00 and does not, when aggregated with all prior purchase transactions, exceed the total funds budgeted by the School Board for this Project.
      EXPLANATION:
          The School Board’s procurement of construction and design-related services is subject to the Virginia Public Procurement Act (the “Act”), which expressly permits delegation of procurement and contract authority to “its duly designated or authorized officers and employees.” Va. Code Ann. § 2.2-4302.

          The Woodson Renovation Project will be accomplished through a series of multiple-prime construction contracts between the School Board, as owner, and various trade contractors or vendors. In addition, in order to save the cost of sales and use taxes, the Project documents provide for certain materials and equipment to be purchased by the School Board directly or by others pursuant to agency agreements specifically pre-approved for this purpose by the Virginia Department of Taxation.

          The School Board will not employ the traditional owner-general contractor structure on this Project. Instead, the school system’s Office of Design & Construction will undertake many of the project responsibilities customarily performed by the general contractor, including coordination, scheduling, and supervision. In assuming this role, the school system will be required from time to time to procure and contract for certain items or services on an expedited basis in order to respond to events occurring at the job site. The delegation of procurement and contract award authority (including change orders to contracts previously awarded), as contemplated by this agenda item, will enable the school system to fulfill its responsibilities on a timely basis in this context.

          The successful completion of the project’s critical path milestones is dependent upon the ability of staff to conduct business in a timely manner. From time to time as the project progresses, the school system will be required to solicit bids and award contracts (and change orders) within timeframes that may be inconsistent with the School Board’s established meeting calendar. All contract awards and change orders will be made in accordance with the requirements of the Virginia Public Procurement Act and within the total project budget for this renovation.

          During the course of the Project, the School Board will receive a monthly summary of all purchase transactions effected pursuant to this agenda Item.
