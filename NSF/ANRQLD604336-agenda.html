Staff Contact: R Chace Ramey, assistant superintendent, Department of Human Resources

 

Meeting Category: November 13, 2017 – All day Work Session

 

Subject: Recommendations to address impact of the new BA teacher salary lanes

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related To: Goal 3: Premier Workforce

 

Key Points: Beginning in the winter of 2015, FCPS engaged in a compensation and classification review that had a goal of developing pay scales that were market competitive for all employees. Teacher compensation was at the center of this work. In July 2016, a recommended new Teacher Pay Scale was presented to the School Board. The scale was recommended to be implemented over several years beginning in Fiscal Year 2018. In spring of 2017, the implementation of the new scale was approved as part of the FY2018 budget process.

 

The new scale is designed to provide teachers a salary that is market competitive, which is defined as career earnings between 95 to 105 percent of the market average. While the majority of lanes on the former scale were below market, FCPS was above market for the BA lanes. To maintain a market competitive scale, the new teacher scale capped the BA lanes at 19 steps.

 

In order to address teachers who were impacted by the new structure, FCPS is recommending a program to provide a 3% bonus to teachers for 3 years on the BA lanes from step 19 up to the last step on the FY 2017 teacher salary scale. This is accompanied by a tuition-sharing Masters Cohort Program. The cohort program will allow teachers to take an expedited path to a Master’s Degree that will allow teachers to move from a BA lane to a Master’s lane in as little as two years. The cohort program would be a 50 – 50 cost share between the employee and FCPS. As part of this program, FCPS would also reinstate tuition reimbursement, which would be available to all employees.

 

The three year bonus program would be available to approximately 420 employees and would cost approximately $1.1 million per year. For the master’s cohort program and tuition reimbursement, the recommendation is to start in spring of 2018. This recommendation would require an allocation of $250,000 for FY 2018 and $1.0 million for FY 2019 and beyond.

 

Recommendation:  School Board consensus to initiate the programs as outlined above to mitigate the impact of the new BA teacher salary lanes.

Meeting Category:  November 13, 2017 – Work Session

 

Subject:  FCPSOn Expansion

 

School Board Action Required: Information                          

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan Goal 1: Student Success

 

Key Points:

 

Full implementation of FCPSOn would increase equitable access to technology and instructional practices that lead to personalized, meaningful learning experiences in order to provide opportunities for deeper understanding and development of Portrait of a Graduate attributes by all students.

 

The technology implementation would phase in over the next four years, beginning at the high school level(FY19), then middle school (FY20), and then moving to the elementary level (FY20 and FY21).  Beginning with high school, capitalizes on the six high schools that are already part of FCPSOn and will allow us to prepare students for college and careers since these students are the group that is closest to graduation.

 

The basal resource identification and purchase would align with the adoption of new VDOE Standards of Learning. These resources take many forms including eBooks, leveled readers, online/hardback textbooks, and online student and teacher learning objects.  The School Board will receive the mathematics titles for the current adoption as new business at the regular meeting on November 20th and therefore this presentation will include a brief overview of the materials recommended by the committee.  Future adoptions/purchases are scheduled as follows:  FY 20 HS Language Arts, FY21 MS Language Arts and HS Science, FY22 ES Language Arts and MS Science, and FY 23 ES Science.

 

FCPS has developed a financial model for this plan including all aspects of expenditures: devices, basal resources, home internet access, infrastructure, professional development, evaluation, and positions based on experience from Phase I. The model would establish sustainable funding and a replacement plan that includes shared cost between schools and central office as well as a student fee.
