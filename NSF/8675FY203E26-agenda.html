National Volunteer Week - April 18-24, 2010

National Volunteer Week, which takes place April 18-24, 2010, began in 1974 when President Nixon signed an executive order establishing the week as an annual celebration of volunteering. Since then, every U.S. President has signed a proclamation promoting National Volunteer Week.

During the 2008-2009 school year, 186 schools, centers, academies, and central offices reported that 43,412 volunteers donated 738,543 hours of volunteer service to FCPS schools.

Over the past 24 years, 1,384,650 volunteers have provided a reported total of 27,318,974 hours of service to our school. The monetary value of this contribution is almost $300 million.

If our volunteers had been paid at the entry-level instructional assistant salary of $13.95 per hour, the bill for 2008-2009, alone, would have totaled over $10 million. The educational value of the work performed by our volunteers is, of course, incalculable.

Resolution
Naming the Track at
West Springfield High School
"The Leslie Sherman Memorial Track”



Whereas, Leslie Sherman, a member of the West Springfield High School graduating class of 2005, was an outstanding scholar and an outstanding athlete during her high school years as a member of the West Springfield High School cross country team, indoor track team, outdoor track team, and basketball team and as an active member of many extracurricular activities including the National Honor Society, the History Honor Society—of which she was president—and as a recipient of the Senior Department Award for Social Studies; and

Whereas, outside school, Leslie Sherman was driven by the need to help those who were less fortunate than she, often giving her personal time to volunteer at a retirement home, to coach for the Special Olympics, and to tutor others and share her enthusiasm for learning and life; and

Whereas, Leslie Sherman was known as a caring, intelligent, positive, genuine, honest, generous, humorous, thoughtful, focused, loyal, enthusiastic, and encouraging friend, student, and teammate who was truly well-rounded and liked by all who met and worked with her; and

Whereas, Leslie Sherman’s promise, which was obvious in so many facets of high school life, was also evident in her college experiences at Virginia Tech, but ended on April 16, 2007, when her life was tragically taken on the Virginia Tech Campus, depriving the West Springfield community and the world of a truly exceptional young woman; and

Whereas, the naming of the West Springfield High School track in her honor will be an enduring remembrance of Leslie Sherman’s life for all those in the school and in the community who will use the facility for years to come;

Now, therefore, be it resolved that the Fairfax County School Board names the track at West Springfield High School “The Leslie Sherman Memorial Track” to honor Leslie Sherman’s life and to remind everyone of the exemplary standard that she set for her peers and the staff at West Springfield High School in all facets of her life.

Suspension Data
Speakers have three minutes to address the Board.


Citizens who want to address either a new business or an action item, as listed on the School Board regular meeting agenda, may do so online starting at 6 a.m. on the Monday preceding the meeting or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. The list consists of a maximum of ten speakers, first come, first served. The list closes when the maximum number is reached or at 4:30 p.m. on the day before the regular meeting. Unfilled spaces may be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis between 6:30 and 7 p.m. before the meeting is called to order. Requests should be made to the deputy clerk of the School Board. Citizens signing up for these unfilled spaces may address any school-related topic. The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.
