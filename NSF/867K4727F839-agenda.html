    RESOLUTION NAMING
    THE WESTFIELD HIGH SCHOOL BAND ROOM
    “THE LAURA MCBRIDE BAND ROOM”

    WHEREAS, Laura McBride, as Westfield High School’s director of bands, served the school with devotion for six years from 2000 until her untimely death in January 2006; and
    WHEREAS, she built the Westfield band program from its inception and led the school to national recognition as having one of the band community’s premier high school programs; her bands consistently received superior ratings at the Virginia Band and Orchestra Directors Association festival and earned recognition by performances at the Virginia Music Educators Association state convention, the festivals of music awards ceremony in Boston, and the Bands of America Festival in Indianapolis; and
    WHEREAS, her first teaching position in the Northern Virginia area was as assistant band director at Woodbridge High School from 1991-92. After which she spent eight years as the director of bands at Stuart High School in Falls Church and then helped open the newly built Westfield High School in 2000; Laura McBride touched the lives of many young people and will be fondly remembered for her great talent and skill as a teacher and band director, her commitment to quality music education, and her dedication to her students and their dreams for the future; and
    WHEREAS, the faculty and community join in requesting that her contribution to Westfield High School be acknowledged in a lasting tribute and, in her honor, the Westfield High School band booster organization has established the Laura McBride Music Education Scholarship for seniors who plan to attend a four-year, two-year, or technical institution to study music education, as well as the Laura McBride Spirit Award;
    NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board names the band room at Westfield High School “The Laura McBride Band Room” to recognize Laura McBride’s exceptional leadership skills and to leave her legacy in this very special room.

    Category

    Type

    Staff Contact: Ann Monday, Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Services

    SUBJECT: Curriculum Advisory Committees

    RECOMMENDATION:
        That the School Board appoint individuals to serve on the English Language Arts, Family Life Education, Fine Arts, Foreign Language, Health and Physical Education, Mathematics, Science, and Social Studies Curriculum Advisory Committees, and on the High School Course Review Committee, as listed in the agenda item.

    EXPLANATION:
        Regulation 1707.6 establishes eight curriculum advisory committees for levels K through 12 to advise on Program of Studies development and implementation.
        Additionally, this agenda item covers appointments to the High School Course Review Committee, which annually identifies standard or optional course offerings for deletion, consolidation, or revision and makes recommendations for action by the Division Superintendent and the School Board (see the current version of Regulation 3202).

        The term of service for all School Board member representatives on the above-named advisory committees is one year. Representatives may be reappointed. Classroom teachers are elected to these committees and serve two-year terms, with elections being held during the spring of odd-numbered years. These terms will be effective until June 30, 2007. Other committee members serve terms as set forth in the appropriate regulation.

    SUBJECT: Award of Contract

    RECOMMENDATION:

        That the School Board approve the award of contract for providing center-based model HMO benefits for Fairfax County Public Schools employees to Kaiser Permanente, effective January 1, 2007, as recommended by the Office of Benefit Services.

    EXPLANATION:
        Kaiser Permanente remains the only health plan in the Washington, DC, metro area that provides health care through a medical center based delivery system.
