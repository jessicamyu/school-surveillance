National Volunteer Week - April 15-21, 2012

 

Established in 1974, National Volunteer Week has grown exponentially in scope each year since, drawing the support and endorsement of all subsequent U.S. presidents, governors, mayors and other respected elected officials.

 

National Volunteer Week is about inspiring, recognizing, and encouraging people to seek out imaginative ways to engage in their communities. It’s about demonstrating to the nation that by working together, in unison, we have the fortitude to meet our challenges and accomplish our goals. National Volunteer Week is about taking action, encouraging individuals and their respective communities to be at the center of social change—discovering and actively demonstrating their collective power to foster positive transformation.

 

During the 2010 -2011 school year, 140 schools, centers, and alternative high schools reported that 38,669 volunteers donated 570,196 hours of volunteer service to FCPS schools.  If our volunteers had been paid at the entry-level instructional assistant salary of $13.95 per hour, the bill for 2010-2011 alone would have totaled almost $7.9 million dollars. The educational value of the work performed by our volunteers is, of course, incalculable.

NAMING THE NEW ADDITION AT

WOLFTRAP ELEMENTARY SCHOOL

“THE CANDACE LEYTON INNOVATION LEARNING LAB”

 

 

     WHEREAS, Candace Leyton taught multiple grade levels at Wolftrap Elementary School from 1971 to 1994, and at Cooper Middle School from 1994 until her retirement in 2002 from Fairfax County Public Schools, after over 30 years of dedicated service; and

 

     WHEREAS, Mrs. Leyton nurtured her students and encouraged them to reach their full potential to accomplish individual goals both in school and in life; she was an extraordinary teacher, who set high expectations and delighted in observing her students gain self confidence as they developed into thinkers and problem solvers; and  

 

     WHEREAS, she valued the connection between the teacher and student and worked hard to find ways to know all her students, establishing a strong relationship that she believed was key to motivating even the most challenged learner;  and

 

     WHEREAS, Mrs. Leyton was a visionary leader, expanding the scope of the elementary curriculum to include the creation of a mathematics lab and the infusion and integration of music into the curriculum, and achieving stronger relationships between home and school with curriculum events, such as Family Math Night; and

 

     WHEREAS, the naming of the new innovation learning lab in her honor will be an enduring remembrance of Candace Leyton’s life for all those in the school who will use the facility for years to come;

 

     NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, in recognition of Candace Leyton’s dedicated service to the students, faculty, and families of the Wolftrap community and her contributions to Fairfax County Public Schools, hereby names in her memory the new addition at Wolftrap Elementary School, “The Candace Leyton Innovation Learning Lab.”

[Updated 4/13/12 - Motion and Voting Below]

 

At the Forum work session held on March 9, 2012, the Board discussed the proposed adoption of a resolution on the goal to start high schools after 8 a.m. and to direct the Superintendent to identify and report on school divisions with later starting high schools at the June 11, 2012, work session.  There was Board consensus to add the resolution to the March 29, 2012, regular meeting agenda as new business, with action scheduled April 12, 2012.

 

 

RESOLUTION ON THE GOAL OF THE FAIRFAX COUNTY SCHOOL BOARD

TO START HIGH SCHOOLS AFTER 8 A.M. AND TO DIRECT THE SUPERINTENDENT TO IDENTIFY AND REPORT ON SCHOOL DIVISIONS WITH LATER STARTING HIGH SCHOOLS

 

     WHEREAS, the National Institutes of Health (NIH), the Centers for Disease Control (CDC), and the National Sleep Foundation (NSF) all conclude that adolescents on average need about nine hours of sleep per night for optimal performance, health, and brain development; and

 

     WHEREAS, most teens experience delayed sleep onset during puberty, and the hormones that help regulate sleep make it difficult for most teens to fall asleep before 11 p.m. or to be alert before 8 a.m.; and

 

     WHEREAS,  Fairfax County Public School (FCPS) buses begin picking students up at 5:45 a.m. and drop students off at high schools between 6:45 and 7 a.m., at a time when teen bodies typically demand sleep; and

 

     WHEREAS, the 2010 Fairfax County Youth Survey found that two-thirds of FCPS 8th, 10th, and 12th grade students report sleeping seven hours or less on an average school night, resulting in a large nightly sleep deficit; and the survey further found that nearly one-third of teen students showed signs of depression; and

 

     WHEREAS, sleep plays an important role in learning and memory; and sleep debt affects teens’ ability to think, perform, and react appropriately; and

 

     WHEREAS, there is ample evidence that later high school start times result in adolescent students getting more sleep on school nights; and,

 

     WHEREAS, research shows there are many benefits from later high school start times, including  decreases in teen depression, car crashes, behavior problems, discipline referrals, absenteeism, tardiness, and dropout rates ; and,

 

     WHEREAS, these benefits serve the Board's Student Achievement Goals that students will “achieve their full academic potential in the core disciplines” (Goal 1.1) and “make healthy and safe life choices” (Goal 2.8), as well as Operational Expectation 7 that “The Superintendent shall establish and maintain a learning environment that is safe, healthful, nondiscriminatory, respectful, and conducive to effective learning";

 

     THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that, because of the benefits to student health, well-being, and performance, it is a goal of the Fairfax County School Board to achieve high school start times after 8 a.m.so that students are able to obtain a healthy and sufficient amount of sleep, as defined by sleep research and medical experts; and

 

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board direct the Superintendent to identify and provide a list of practices in school divisions that start high schools after 8 a.m. -- including successful approaches in school districts that have changed from before to after 8 a.m. and in school districts that have traditionally had later morning start times (including, but not limited to: Arlington County and Loudoun County, VA; Minneapolis, MN; Wilton, CT; and Brevard, FL) -- to determine and report to the School Board by June 2012 on how such school districts:

  • Scheduled elementary, middle and high schools
  • Arranged transportation for elementary, middle and high school students to and from school
  • Scheduled sports and after-school activities for high school students around their start and end times
  • Used successful change management techniques to involve and prepare parents, teachers, students and other stakeholders and the school division for a different schedule, in cases where a shift to later start times has occurred.

Proposed Amendment

 

I move to amend the main motion by (1) revising paragraph #9 by striking the words “to achieve high school start times after 8 a.m. so that students are able to obtain a healthy and sufficient amount of sleep, as defined by sleep research and medical experts” and inserting the words “that students obtain a healthy and sufficient amount of sleep;” and (2) inserting a new paragraph #10 as follows:  “BE IT ALSO RESOLVED, that the Fairfax County School Board direct the Superintendent to assess potential actions in support of this goal, including, but not limited to, high school start times after 8 a.m. and bus pick-up times after 6:45 a.m.; and to assess the potential impact of such actions on the budget, students, parents, employees, and the community.” (Ted Velkoff) (failed)

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Background:

 

Schools will have completed 180 days of school as of Friday, June 15, 2012.  This provides the School Board an opportunity to schedule the last day of school on this date and release students from school on the following Monday and Tuesday, June 18 and 19, 2012.  The early release of students will provide teachers two additional workdays to complete end-of-year assignments, school improvement planning, teacher planning, and collaborative team planning, and any other workload relief that can be accomplished in the schools.

Recommended Action
Approve the accelerated renovation of high, middle and elementary school group bathrooms; (2) delegate authority to the Division Superintendent or the Chief Operating Officer to allocate funding for the bathroom renovation projects from the construction reserve; and (3) authorize the Division Superintendent or Chief Operating Officer (either of whom may act without the other) to solicit bids, negotiate, execute and administer on behalf of the School Board the design and construction contracts necessary to renovate the group bathroom facilities (FTS;presented as new business 3/29/12)

Staff Contact:        Dean A. Tistadt, chief operating officer, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category: April 12, 2012 - Regular Meeting No. 17

 

Subject:                 Accelerated Renovation of High, Middle and Elementary School Group Bathrooms

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:           School Board Policy 5011.2 Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

The Department of Facilities and Transportation (FTS) recommends the accelerated renovation of group bathrooms at schools currently in the renovation queue whose renovations are not scheduled to begin within the next five years.

 

There are currently 44 schools in the Renovation Queue which will not begin their renovations within the next five years.  The total estimated to renovate these group bathrooms is approximately $8.35M. The scope of the bathroom facility renovations will include the replacement of all fixtures, partitions, bathroom hardware, and finishes within the space.  In addition, each group bathroom will be brought into alignment with all prevailing codes to include ADA requirements.

 

Funding for these projects will be provided from the construction reserve.  Staff further recommends that the projects be prioritized starting with the three “legacy” high schools, then middle schools, and then elementary schools (to include Centreville High School). The initial projects to be performed will be the three legacy high schools in their current queue order: Herndon, Oakton, and Falls Church; followed by the middle schools: Rocky Run, Hughes, Cooper, Frost, and Twain; lastly, the elementary school and Centreville High School bathrooms will be renovated in the order of their queue position.

 

This work can begin in the near future with the intent of completing the three legacy high and five middle schools (a total of approximately 92 bathrooms) by the end of the 2012-2013 school year. It is anticipated that the entire initiative will be completed throughout an approximate six year period to minimize its impact upon the current renovation schedule.

 

Staff believes that the completion of this work will provide a substantive improvement to these schools for students, staff, and community users while reducing the overall maintenance and utility costs at each building.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the accelerated renovation of high, middle and elementary school group bathrooms; (2) delegate authority to the Division Superintendent or the Chief Operating Officer to allocate funding for the bathroom renovation projects from the construction reserve; and (3) authorize the Division Superintendent or Chief Operating Officer (either of whom may act without the other) to solicit bids, negotiate, execute and administer on behalf of the School Board the design and construction contracts necessary to renovate the group bathroom facilities.

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Related To:   Legal Requirement  

 

Key Points: 

 

The Department of Special Services, as required by Public Law 108-446, the Individuals with Disabilities Education Improvement Act of 2004, has completed the Local Special Education Annual Plan and Report 2012-2013, as required by VDOE Superintendent’s memo #324-11, dated December 16, 2011.  This report will be used by the Virginia Department of Education in preparing the state plan for special education.  As required by VDOE, this report has been prepared and will be submitted entirely online in a VDOE-proscribed abbreviated format on a date no later than May 14, 2012.  

 

New this year is an inclusion of an ‘excess cost’ calculation, used to determine that IDEA funds are used to support only those costs in excess of the average cost of a general education student, as well as a comparison of FY 2011 actual and FY 2012 budgeted special education costs as a test of FCPS’ eligibility to receive federal funds under the IDEA Maintenance of Effort rules.

 

The 2012-2013 Section 611, Part B (grades K-12) application estimates revenue to Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS) of $32,557,475. This amount is equal to the final 2011-2012 award as revised by Superintendent’s memo #052-12 dated February 24, 2012. Estimated expenditures include cost associated with maintaining a mandatory 15% set-aside for Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS).  Section 611, Part B, award will fund a total of 459.5 FTE positions.

 

The 2012-2013 Section 619, Part B Preschool application estimates revenue to FCPS of $789,151. This amount is equal to 2011-2012 award.  These funds support Child Find activities as well as parenting and transition services for preschool-age students.  Section 619 funds seven FTE positions.

 

The amounts above reflect no change from the 2011-2012 award.  The 2012-2013 application reflects funding of the same programs funded in the 2011-2012 application.  Small adjustments in both sections 611 and 619 have been made for incumbent salaries, employee benefit costs, and the state restricted indirect cost rate.  Section 611 also includes costs for instructional materials for Responsive Instruction, as part of the CEIS set-aside.  None of the above represent new or additional operating resources.

 

The Fairfax County Advisory Committee for Students with Disabilities (ACSD) reviewed the plan and funding application at its March 14, 2012 meeting.

  1. Approve the plan and application as detailed in the agenda item.
  2. Amend or reject the plan and application and provide direction to staff for further action.
