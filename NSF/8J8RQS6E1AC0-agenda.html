National Inclusive School Week - December 5 - 9, 2011

 

Inclusive Schools Week is an annual event sponsored by the Inclusive Schools Network (ISN) at Education Development Center, Inc. (EDC), which is held each year during the first week in December.  Since its inception in 2001, Inclusive Schools Week has celebrated the progress that schools have made in providing a supportive and quality education to an increasingly diverse student population, including students who are marginalized due to disability, gender, socio-economic status, cultural heritage, language preference, and other factors.  The Week also provides an important opportunity for educators, students, and parents to discuss what else needs to be done in order to ensure that their schools continue to improve their ability to successfully educate all children. Inclusive Schools Week, celebrated annually the first week in December, highlights and celebrates the progress of our nation's schools in employing inclusive practices to ensure a quality education for an increasingly diverse student population. The Week encourages educators, students, and parents to strive for improved academic achievement for all children.

Other Staff Present: Kristen Michael, director, Office of Budget Services

 

Meeting Category:   December 1, 2011 – Regular Meeting No. 9

 

Subject:                   FY 2012 Midyear Budget Review       

 

School Board Action Required:  Information                                                     

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, the FY 2012 beginning balance increased $0.5 million due to audit adjustments made after the FY 2011 Final Budget Review, including an increase of $0.7 million in sales tax revenue based on actual sales tax receipts and other revenue adjustments totaling $0.8 million offset by expenditure adjustments totaling $1.0 million. 

 

In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other revenue adjustments total $0.4 million.  This is a result of revenue from the City of Fairfax increasing by $0.9 million based on the final tuition bill for FY 2011 reflecting actual expenditures and grant revenue decreasing $0.5 million. 

 

Recommended expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $5.0 million; a reduction of $4.6 million in compensation accounts is due to the average salaries of new hires for this school year being less than budgeted; a reduction of $0.5 million in grant expenditures is primarily from a reduction in the federal award for the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act; and an increase of $0.1 million for 2.0 internal audit positions.  The recommended audit positions are requested to conduct local school activity fund (LSAF) audits.

 

Expenditure adjustments, combined with revenue adjustments, result in available funds totaling $5.9 million recommended to be set aside to increase the FY 2013 beginning balance from $51.6 million to $57.5 million.  This will bring the FY 2013 beginning balance slightly above the level projected in the FY 2013 fiscal forecast.  

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2012 Midyear Budget Review, as detailed in the agenda item.

Subject:                 Falls Church-McLean Children's Center Agreement

 

School Board Action Required:  Information           

 

Related To:             School Board Policy 5011.2 Authority to Contract

 

Key Points:

 

Since 2003, the Falls Church-McLean Children’s Center (the “Children’s Center”) has been utilizing approximately 8,000 square feet of space in the building at Lemon Road Elementary School.  During the renovation of Lemon Road Elementary School in 2001-2002, Fairfax County provided FCPS with $1,959,000 in funding for the construction of this 8,000 square foot children’s care center space (the “Improvements”), allowing the Children’s Center to relocate its operations from a nearby church to the elementary school in 2003. 

 

The Improvements have a capacity to serve 80 children and are operated on a year round basis. The Children’s Center pays FCPS monthly for all operating expenses, including utilities, cleaning services and supplies. 

 

An agreement has been drafted to memorialize the use arrangement, to recognize the County’s original funding of the Improvements, and to obligate the School Board to reimburse the County (or its designee) for the fair market value of the Improvements should the School Board elect to terminate the use arrangement (without cause)  prior to September 1, 2028.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent or Chief Operating Officer, in consultation with legal counsel for the school system, to negotiate, execute, and administer an agreement on behalf of the School Board with the Falls Church-McLean Children’s Center and with the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors for the Children’s Center’s use of a portion of the Lemon Road Elementary School building.

Subject:                  Facilities Management Northeast Satellite Office Lease

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

In order to provide more efficient services to schools, the Office of Facilities Management (OFM) is implementing the final phase of a decentralized maintenance facility plan, to be operated through four satellite office/warehouse facilities located throughout the county:

 

                                                     Type Facility                              Service Area of the County

 

Sideburn Center

FCPS-Owned

 Central/Southwest

Edison Center

FCPS-Owned

 Southeast

Herndon Center

Leased Facility

Northeast Center

New Lease Request

 Northeast

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

The final phase originally proposed an FCPS-owned maintenance satellite at the former Pimmit Hills High School facility; however, instructional needs and community concerns precluded that initiative. No other FCPS-owned facilities are available for this purpose.  As such, the final phase now involves a request to lease a commercial facility strategically located to support schools in the northeast area of the County. 

 

The proposed Northeast Center site was selected after a 9-month search, which included collaboration with the Economic Development Authorities of both Fairfax County and the City of Falls Church.  The proposed site offers the best location in terms of accessibility, parcel size, appropriate zoning (industrial classification), community impact and costs.   

 

The proposed site will provide more responsive maintenance services to (52) schools and other FCPS facilities located in the northeast part of Fairfax County, primarily through savings in OFM staff travel time.  Currently, these service runs originate from the Sideburn Center in Fairfax, resulting in the addition of as much as an hour in travel time to the first service calls.  

 

This proposed site affords other benefits from its large parking lot and warehouse component.  Specifically, the parking lot can accommodate 20-25 school buses.  The current plan is to use the warehouse space for school-wide storage and distribution of HVAC filters as well as other building materials and equipment for the northeast service area.

 

Offsets to leasing costs are expected from:

  • Productivity Gains (less time in traffic means more time performing services in schools)
  • Cost Savings (reduced vehicle usage)
  • Cost Avoidance (proposed site also would accommodate parking 20-25 school buses)

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board authorize the Division Superintendent or Chief Operating Officer, in consultation with legal counsel for the school system, to negotiate, execute, and administer a commercial lease agreement for the Northeast Satellite Maintenance facility and including any and all related documentation, on behalf of the School Board.

Subject:                     School Board Policy 8614, Video Surveillance

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

At work sessions held on September 12, 2011 and November 14, 2011, the Board discussed a recommendation by staff to revise Policy 8614, Video Surveillance, to authorize the installation in high schools of surveillance cameras for cafeterias and other high traffic areas.  The revised policy would not require the installation of such cameras, but rather would allow principals, with the support of their respective communities, to install surveillance cameras only in those areas of the school that are consistent with the revised policy.

 

This policy revision also includes requirements for establishing regulations to ensure a community engagement process to obtain feedback prior to installing surveillance cameras in a particular school. 

 

Annual reports will be provided to the School Board regarding the effectiveness of surveillance cameras.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 8614.5, Video Surveillance, as detailed in the agenda item.
