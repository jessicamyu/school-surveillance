RESOLUTION HONORING
LAURA CHU

 

WHEREAS,  Laura Chu, a newly graduated member of the 2017 senior class at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology (TJHSST), has enthusiastically served as the 46th student representative to the Fairfax County School Board for the 2016-2017 school year; and

RESOLUTION SUPPORTING CONTINUED FEDERAL FUNDING FOR THE NATIONAL ENDOWMENTS FOR THE HUMANITIES AND THE ARTS AND ASSOCIATED SUMMER EDUCATION PROGRAMS FOR SCHOOL TEACHERS

 

WHEREAS, the National Endowment for the Humanities (NEH) and the National Endowment for the Arts (NEA) are independent federal agencies created in 1965 and are dedicated to strengthening teaching, learning and the arts in our nation’s schools and communities; and

 

WHEREAS, the NEH is one of the largest funders of humanities programs in the United States and a major funder of summer education programs utilized by Fairfax County elementary and secondary educators; and

 

WHEREAS, NEH summer education programs (Teacher Institutes and Seminars) have enabled Fairfax County history and social studies teachers to participate in summer professional development programs that benefit their students; and NEH summer education programs allow FCPS teachers to earn professional development points toward recertification as required by the Commonwealth of Virginia; and 

 

WHEREAS, the NEA recommends more than 2,400 grants for every Congressional District in the country; a significant percentage of which go to those who have fewer opportunities to participate in the arts, including high-poverty neighborhoods and underserved populations such as people with disabilities

and veterans; and

 

WHEREAS, Congress has demonstrated bipartisan support for both the NEA and NEH in recent years, taking modest steps to increase their funding; and

 

WHEREAS, the Budget Request for FY 2018 sent to Congress by the administration proposes to eliminate the NEA, NEH and the summer professional development programs for K-12 educators they support, thus removing a significant source of grant-funded professional development and teacher recertification programs for FCPS teachers;

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board supports the NEA, the NEH and associated summer education programs for K-12 teachers; and calls upon the members of Fairfax County’s congressional delegation to support at least level funding in Congress’s annual appropriation for these organizations in FY 2018.

5.02 Proposed Restatement of the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001) [ERFC, NB 6/8/17]
Meeting
Jun 22, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Consent Agenda - 9 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
approve proposed restated plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001.

Staff Contact:  Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category:  June 22, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Proposed Restatement of the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

On April 27, the School Board approved ERFC Plan modifications effective July 1, 2017.  The School Board directed the ERFC Board of Trustees to initiate the process to implement the ERFC Plan modifications with the amended Plan documents submitted to the School Board for approval by June 2017.  The Plan amendments are summarized below: 


ERFC and ERFC 2001 Plan Documents

Proposed amendments to the ERFC and ERFC 2001 Plans provide for a 4 percent interest rate on accumulated contributions for periods after June 30, 2017. The rate is reduced from the 5 percent currently credited on accumulated contributions.    
 

Proposed administrative amendments to the ERFC and ERFC 2001 Plans clarify that the cost-of-living adjustment is calculated without regard to any temporary adjustments to a member’s benefit and update the Internal Revenue Code compensation limit. 

 

ERFC 2001 Plan Document

 

Proposed amendments that are applicable to members who first enter eligible employment on or after July 1, 2017, include:  instituting retirement eligibility requirements that age and service equal 90 or attainment of full Social Security retirement age with at least five years of service; increasing the period for calculating the final average salary from 3 years to 5 years; and changing the cost-of-living adjustment to equal 100 percent of the Consumer Price Index (CPI-U) for the Standard Metropolitan Statistical Area that includes Fairfax County with a cap of 4 percent.  In circumstances where the ERFC Board of Trustees is allowed to suspend cost of living adjustments (COLAs), the Board may not reduce the COLAs that were previously granted below 3 percent for members hired prior to July 1, 2017, or the lesser of 4 percent or the increase in the CPI-U for members hired on or after July 1, 2017.    

 

Proposed administrative amendments to the ERFC 2001 Plan document include:

clarifications that a member may nominate a beneficiary consistent with administrative procedures adopted by the ERFC Board of Trustees and that a spouse who is eligible for a 100 percent and a 50 percent survivor option (Options A and B) will receive benefits under the 100 percent option (Option A) in the event of an active member death.  Other administrative amendments provide that a member who leaves eligible employment and returns to work without receiving a refund of contributions will be treated as hired before July 1, 2017, for all purposes under the Plan; conversely, a member who leaves eligible employment, receives a refund and returns to work will be treated as hired on or after July 1, 2017, even if the member redeposits the withdrawn contributions.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve proposed restated plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001.

Meeting Category:  June 22, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Appointment of “Individual Trustee” to the Board of Trustees of the Educational Employees’ Supplementary Retirement System of Fairfax County (ERFC)

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Related To:  Legal Requirement

 

Key Points:

 

According to the Fairfax County Ordinance, Chapter 3, Section 3-4-3, the ERFC Board of Trustees consist of seven (7) persons, one of whom is referred to as the “individual Trustee.”  The individual Trustee may not be affiliated with or employed by Fairfax County, the Fairfax County School Board, or any union or similar organization which represents teachers or other employees in Fairfax County.  The individual Trustee is chosen by unanimous vote or consent of the other six trustees subject to the approval of the Fairfax County School Board, on the basis of the fact that such individual Trustee possesses competent financial and/or investment counseling skills.

 

At a recent meeting of the ERFC Board of Trustees, Michael Burke was unanimously recommended for appointment as the “individual Trustee.”  Mr. Burke is a Managing Director in the Washington, D.C. Private Client Group of Stifel, a wealth management and investment firm.   If approved by the School Board, Mr. Burke will serve for his fifth year on the ERFC Board.  Mr. Burke meets all of the requirements of the position.    

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the appointment of Michael Burke to the ERFC Board of Trustees for a one year term beginning July 1, 2017.

By policy, committee members “shall be residents of Fairfax County or employees of Fairfax County Public Schools,” and “to the maximum extent possible, appointments made by School Board members should reflect the religious, cultural, and ethnic diversity of the areas they represent.” Additional committee members are selected as provided for in the current version of Regulation 1710. All appointments shall be approved by the School Board.
  • Members of the citizen advisory committees serve one-year terms, as do the student representative’s appointees. All terms begin in July and end in June. 
  • Members of the FECEP-Head Start Parent Policy committee are selected according to federal mandates and are confirmed by the School Board each year in November.
  • Title I District Advisory Committee members are selected according to federal mandates and are confirmed by the School Board each year in June.
  • Membership in the Facilities Planning Advisory Council (FPAC) is handled differently, as the terms vary.
  • Nominees to be appointed or reappointed by this action will be listed by committee below.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
