Staff Contact: Kristen Michael, assistant superintendent, Department of Financial Services                                                            [Updated 4/29/16]

                                                                           

Meeting Category:  May 2, 2016 – Work Session No. 104 Budget

 

Subject:    FY 2017 Approved Budget    

 

School Board Action Required:   Discussion                                   

 

Key Points:

 

Overview

On February 4, 2016, the School Board adopted the FY 2017 Advertised Budget that includes a requested increase in the county transfer of $122.7 million, or 6.7 percent. Expenditure adjustments total $149.2 million and 127.0 positions. The FY 2017 Advertised Budget increase includes funding to address large class sizes in elementary schools and to eliminate elementary class sizes of 30 and above, where classroom space is available. The budget also includes funding to provide a step increase for eligible employees and a 1.0 percent market scale adjustment (MSA) for all employees. Funding is provided for an initial investment in the teacher salary scales to begin to address the findings from the external compensation study that is currently underway that showed FCPS is significantly below market on teacher salaries. Other expenditure increases in the FY 2017 Advertised Budget include a living wage adjustment, retirement rate increases, and health insurance increases. Collectively, these are offset by expenditure reductions totaling $27.7 million.

 

County Executive’s Advertised Budget

The County Executive presented his FY 2017 Advertised Budget Plan on February 16, 2016, which included a School Operating Fund transfer increase of $54.75 million, or 3.0 percent, which leaves a gap of $67.8 million.

 

Impact of State Budget to FCPS

The final state budget results in an increase in state funding of $16.8 million as compared to FCPS’ FY 2017 Advertised Budget.

 

In addition to the increase in revenue, FCPS’ expenditures will increase by $1.2 million due to higher rates set by the state for mandatory group life insurance.

 

Discussion

The County’s Markup Package increases County support for Schools beyond the levels included in the Advertised Budget, adding an additional $33.6 million, for a total increase in School Operating support of $88.4 million.

 

Remaining Shortfall

To address the gap of $15.7 million that remains, at the April 18 work session, we recommended adjustments for additional revenue and divisionwide reductions. The total of these adjustments is $6.3 million which reduces the gap to $9.4 million.
