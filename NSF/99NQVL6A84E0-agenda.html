Veterans Day - November 11, 2013

 

Veterans' Day (formerly Armistice Day), is the anniversary of the Armistice which was signed in the Forest of Compiegne by the Allies and the Germans in 1918, ending World War I, after four years of conflict.

At 5 a.m., on Monday, November 11, 1918, the Germans signed the Armistice, an order was issued for all firing to cease; so the hostilities of the First World War ended. This day began with the laying down of arms, blowing of whistles, impromptu parades, and closing of places of business. All over the globe there were many demonstrations; no doubt the world had never before witnessed such rejoicing.

 

Veterans' Day is observed annually in the United States in honor of all those, living and dead, who served with the U. S. armed forces.

 

The School Board directs school personnel to provide students with instructional activities that honor veterans and recognize the role of the military in American democracy.  Many schools have special events, to which they invite veterans and members of the school community on active duty military.

 

 

American Education Week - November 18-22, 2013

 

American Education Week was established in 1921 by the National Education Association (NEA) to raise public awareness of the importance of public education in America.  The organization’s annual tagline, Great Public Schools:  A Basic Right and Our Responsibility, focuses on the critical role parents play, the invaluable services provided by education support professionals, and the importance of substitute teachers.  As part of the observance, community leaders are invited to serve as educators for a day in order to get a glimpse at a day in the life of a school employee.  American Education Week features a special observance on each day of the week, including veterans, parents, education support professionals, and substitute educators. 

 

 

Geography Awareness Week - November 18-22, 2013

 

Geography Awareness Week is an opportunity to focus on geography as a critical component of a 21st century education.  The 2013 theme, The New Age of Exploration, focuses on how geography enables everyone to serve as intrepid explorers. 

 

Geography Awareness Week was established by presidential proclamation in 1987 to underscore the importance of geographic understanding to ensure our nation’s economic competitiveness, national security, environmental sustainability, and livability of our communities in the 21st century.

School Psychology Awareness Week - November 11 –15, 2013

 

The theme of this year’s School Psychology Awareness Week, "We Are All In!  Teams Work!” focuses on helping students and colleagues recognize, employ, and enhance team-related skills.  The program involves resources and activities that school psychologists can use to encourage fellow staff members, students, and parents to identify and use skills that are critical to successful teams and success in school and life.

 

School psychologists work with students in early childhood settings and elementary and secondary schools.  They collaborate with teachers, parents, and school personnel to create safe, healthy, and supportive learning environments for all students.  School psychologists address students' learning and behavioral problems, suggest improvements to classroom management strategies or parenting techniques, and evaluate students with disabilities and advanced academic students to help determine the best way to educate them.

Subject:                 Policy 2158, Student Employment

                             Policy 2418, Promotion, Retention, Grading, and Graduation Requirements

                             Policy 3201, Program of Studies

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

                            

Policy 2158 has been changed to reflect Fairfax County Public Schools will no longer have an active role in Virginia’s Child Labor Permit process in accordance with Commonwealth of Virginia’s establishing a new database that issues work permit certificates electronically. 

 

A student seeking a work permit will no longer need to visit their local school to have an issuing officer sign the forms.  Documents required to process a child labor permit is now available on line at:  www.doli.virginia.gov.

 

Policy 2418 has been updated to include wording that encourages establishing a strong partnership between parent or guardian and teacher to ensure student success.  This would include informal and ongoing communication.

 

Policy 3201 has been updated to clarify the alignment of the FCPS Program of Studies (POS) and the Virginia Standards of Learning (SOL).  The POS defines the comprehensive, rigorous curriculum to be taught and contains benchmarks outlined in the SOL, as well as additional objectives that offer students opportunities to extend their learning based on their aptitudes and interests.  The POS is to be used by schools as the basis for meeting the Virginia Standards for Accrediting Public Schools in Virginia as well as meeting high expectations outlines by the FCPS Student Achievement Goals that will prepare students for college and workplace readiness.

 

Recommendation:      

 

That the School Board adopt Policy 2158, Student Employment; Policy 2418, Promotion, Retention, Grading, and Graduation Requirements; and Policy 3201, Program of Studies, as detailed in the agenda item.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
