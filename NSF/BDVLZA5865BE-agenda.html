Subject: School Readiness Resources Panel Recommendations

 

Staff Contacts:  Francisco Durán, chief equity officer, Fairfax County Public Schools

                          Anne-Marie Twohie, director, Fairfax County Office for Children

 

Meeting Category:  October 28, 2019 – Work Session

 

School Board Action Required: Discussion

 

Ignite Link:  Goal 1: Student Success

 

Summary/Background:

 

At its meeting on October 4, 2017, the Successful Children and Youth Policy Team (SCYPT) endorsed the Equitable School Readiness Strategic Plan.  Implementation of the plan began in FY 2019.  Key initiatives have included:

  • Implementing and expanding parent-child playgroups and parent workshops that use the Mind in the Making framework to support the development of executive function skills;
  • Piloting the Early Development Instrument in Fairfax County public schools;
  • Increasing slots in early childhood programs;
  • Expanding the Nurse-Family Partnership program; and
  • Increasing the Child Care Assistance and Referral provider maximum reimbursable rate.

In spring 2019, the Board of Supervisors directed a School Readiness Resources Panel to identify expansion goals and long-term funding strategies to support an ongoing priority to expand early childhood programs.  The panel identified the following goal:

Goal:  All families with children under age 5 living in households with income below 300 percent of the Federal Poverty Level (FPL) have access to publicly funded early childhood programs in the public and private sectors.

In addition, the panel developed 16 recommendations, which are included in the attached presentation document.  The recommendations are intended to support a comprehensive approach to advancing and expanding the system and cover six key areas:

  • Legislative and state actions
  • Land use and development
  • Dedicated early childhood fund
  • Public-private partnerships
  • Community engagement
  • Ongoing reporting

At its meeting on September 25, 2019, SCYPT endorsed the School Readiness Resources Panel Recommendations.

Ignite Link:  Strategic Plan Goal 4 Resource Stewardship

 

Summary/Background (Key Points):

 

On March 7, 2019, the School Board approved a streamlined Strategic Plan with eight areas of focus for school years 2018-2020. These areas of focus have specific strategic actions to move the Division toward its goals. Each strategic action has targeted metrics to show the Division’s current baseline data, an interim target to achieve by school year 2020, and an aspirational statement that describes what success would look like for students, families, employees, and the community.

 

The current report describes actions and outcomes undertaken in FY19 related to Goal 4 of the Strategic Plan, Resource Stewardship, which details FCPS’ commitment to championing the needs of our school communities and being responsible stewards of the public’s investment. It represents one of the four goals (one for each goal area) that will be presented this fall.

 

All goal reports provide information about actions FCPS undertook during the year to support achievement of its aspirations, outcome data on School Board approved metrics, ROI analyses couched within a Strategic Decision-Making Framework and Future Considerations related to the results from the reporting year. The Goal 4 Resource Stewardship report addresses performance on indicators of Efficient Budgeting and Allocation, specifically, those related to the following four aspirations:

  • FCPS will maintain a cost per pupil ± $1,000 of the average cost per pupil of the Washington Area Boards of Education (WABE) average;
  • All spending will be allocated using the Strategic Decision-Making Framework;
  • All schools will have adequate classroom space for students, eliminating the need for trailers;
  • FCPS will maintain schools that all students, families, and staff consider safe.

 

Recommendation:

That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan Goal 4: Resource Stewardship FY19 report as detailed in the agenda item.
