Regular Meeting of the Board of Trustees
6:00 pm

Northside Activity Center

This Regular Meeting of the Board of Trustees is authorized in accordance with the Texas Government Code, Sections 551.001-551.146. Verification of Notice of Meeting and Agenda are on file in the Office of the Superintendent. An Executive Session, if required, is authorized by the statute and will be conducted prior to the conclusion of the meeting.
Anyone wishing to address the Board regarding Citizens to be Heard or an agenda item at the meeting must complete a sign-up sheet prior to the start of the meeting. Sign-up sheets will be provided at the entrance of the meeting room.
Meeting will be livestreamed at www.nisd.net/live. An electronic copy of the agenda may be reviewed at www.nisd.net/board/agendas. A recording of the meeting will be available for viewing at www.nisd.net/board.
Northside facilities are wheelchair accessible. Accessible parking spaces are available and designated by signage. Interpreters for the deaf must be requested at least 24 hours prior to the meeting by calling 210-397-8770.

6. Recognitions
Subject
A. William Lee, Clark HS, Texas Foreign Language Association (TFLA) Teacher of the Year
Meeting
Dec 14, 2021 - Regular Meeting of the Board of Trustees
Category
6. Recognitions
Type
Discussion

William Lee, a Latin teacher at Clark HS, was named Teacher of the Year by the Texas Foreign Language Association (TFLA) for 2021. He now represents Texas at the Southwest Region Conference.

Each year, the TFLA recognizes world language teachers for their Excellence in Teaching in the different languages. 

File Attachments
Subject
B. Fatimah Fotouh Aboueisha, Brandeis HS, 2021 Presidential Awards for Excellence in Mathematics and Science Teaching (PAEMST) State Finalist
Meeting
Dec 14, 2021 - Regular Meeting of the Board of Trustees
Category
6. Recognitions
Type
Discussion

Fatimah Fotouh Aboueisha, a science teacher at Brandeis HS, was selected as the the 2021 Presidential Awards for Excellence in Mathematics and Science Teaching state finalist in the Science secondary teacher division.

 

She is one of three finalists from the state who will be recognized at the national level, which includes a trip to Washington, D.C., a $10,000 award, and a signed certificate from the President of the United States.

 

Aboueisha has been teaching for six years, and the 2021-2022 school year will be her third year at Brandeis where she also serves as Science Department Coordinator in addition to teaching biology and chemistry.
