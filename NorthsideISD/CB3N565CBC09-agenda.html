A. The Superintendent will report about current events, announce achievements or special recognitions, and share other information that may be of interest to Trustees or citizens. No action is required.
Meeting
Apr 26, 2022 - Regular Meeting of the Board of Trustees
Category
4. Superintendent's Report
Type
Information

In the last month cabinet members and I have done hundreds of bond information presentations to staff, parents and community groups. As a reminder, early voting has begun and runs through May 3rd with election day May 7th.

 

Speaking of bond projects, we recently had the ribbon cutting ceremony for the new Law and Medical Services magnet high school building. Thank you to LMS principal Margaret Bray and Marshall principal Susan Clevelend for a really nice celebration.

 

Also in the last month I have been fortunate to attend many robotics, eSports, and Fine Arts events. The highlight for me is always Los Leones, our partnership with the city of Leon Valley that showcases all of the Fine Arts that our students participate in. Similar was last weekend's ArtFest which gives the other 1/2 of our schools a chance to showcase their talented students. 

 

We also enjoyed our elementary and secondary EOY events that highlighted our campus educators of the year.

 

I also attended a meeting w/ Speaker Phelan's staff to discuss some issues related to public schools and the upcoming 88th legislative session.

 

Erika Olivares, principal of the soon to open CAST Teach magnet and I had a chance to present our ideas to Commissioner Morath. His comment was something like, "We just need 100 of these across the state."

 

Finally, last Saturday we successfully ran our solar car event. It was really nice to be racing again and to have such a large number of teams and supports in the audience. Thanks again to our Science group in C&I, the US Air Force and, of course, Mr. Blount for their work on this program.

 

We don't have a video moment tonight as we have one associated w/ our recognition.

6. Recognitions
Subject
A. Recognition of Brandeis HS Chamber Orchestra
Meeting
Apr 26, 2022 - Regular Meeting of the Board of Trustees
Category
6. Recognitions
Type

 

The Brandeis High School Chamber and Concert Orchestras performed and competed in New York City at the National Orchestra Cup at Alice Tully Hall in Lincoln Center.

Competing against some of the best orchestra programs in the country and being judged by the assistant conductor for the New York Philharmonic, the Chamber Orchestra brought home the top award and are Grand Champions.
 

They are the first orchestra in San Antonio to win the National Orchestra Cup. In addition, two Brandeis students won best soloist awards. They are Vincent Garcia-Hettinger, best low strings soloist, and Ray Zhang, best high strings soloist. Kevin Garcia Hettinger is head orchestra director, and Olga De Leon is assistant orchestra director.
