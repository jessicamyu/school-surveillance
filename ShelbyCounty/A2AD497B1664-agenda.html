Recommended Action
It is recommended that the Shelby County Board of Education approves the award to Northwest Evaluation Association (NWEA) in the amount of $1,204,234.50 to provide student assessment, benchmark assessment, and a universal screener for the 2015-2016 school year. The total amount of $1,204,234.50 consists of a base contract award of $975,148.50, and a license/assessment contingency amount of $229,086.00. The cost of the student assessment system includes licenses for online access for 86,719 students, professional development, webinars, and workshops. The assessment/universal screener will help identify specific individual student’s skill-level needs at all grade levels, identify any deficiencies, and identify root causes contributing to a student’s low performance. The license/assessment contingency will be used, if needed, to cover future increases in the number of licenses/assessments required.
The agreement shall continue for a term of one (1) year and reserve SCS the unilateral option of extending the term of the agreement for two (2) additional terms of one (1) year each provided that the maximum duration of the agreement will not exceed three (3) years.

Responsible Department: Dr. Heidi Ramirez, Academics

Recommended Action
It is recommended that the Shelby County Board of Education approves the award to Houghton Mifflin Harcourt Publishing Co, in the amount of $298,433.00 to provide an RTI2 Progress Monitoring System. The total amount of $298,433.00 consists of a base contract award of $235,587.00 and a license/assessment contingency amount of $62,846.00. Under the contract award Houghton Mifflin Harcourt will provide the RTI2 Progress Monitoring component of the districtwide Student Assessment System and progress monitoring for the 2015-2016 school year. The cost includes licenses for online access for 58,682 students, Professional Development, Webinars, and Work Shops. The progress monitoring will help in strengthening early literacy through phoneme segmenting, word reading fluency, comprehension, phonics, vocabulary, reading comprehension and math assessment. The license/assessment contingency will be used, if needed, to cover future increases in the number of licenses/assessments required.
The agreement shall continue for a term of one (1) year and reserve SCS the unilateral option of extending the term of the agreement for two (2) additional terms of one (1) year each provided that the maximum duration of the agreement will not exceed three (3) years.

Responsible Department: Dr. Heidi Ramirez, Academics
