7.1 Plan Approval: Return of 3 Achievement School District (ASD) schools to Memphis-Shelby County Schools (MSCS)
Meeting
Apr 19, 2022 - April Work Session
Category
7. Staff Action Items
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
It is recommended that the Shelby County Board of Education approves the following actions to aid with the transition of Frayser Achievement Elementary School, Corning Achievement Elementary School, Georgian Hills Achievement Elementary, and Whitney Achievement School from the Achievement School District (ASD) to Memphis-Shelby County Schools (MSCS) for the 2022-2023 school year: (1) MSCS will apply to open three schools with these names: Frayser-Corning Elementary; Georgian Hills Elementary; and Whitney Elementary. (2) MSCS will maintain the attendance zones already established by the ASD for these three schools. (3) The Board waives requirements of Policy 1007 and 6009 regarding community meetings and public input for the purposes of naming and attendance zones although community meetings will still be held to assist parents with the transition.
Goals
Initiative 2: Recruit and retain the best district leaders and teachers in the nation, immerse them in professional development to embrace and teach foundational literacy skill concepts, and entrench them in the community and classroom;
Initiative 3: Create relevant and equitable academic choices and learning environments to ensure scholars are prepared for the global workforce

Responsible Party: Dr. Angela Whitelaw, Deputy Superintendent of Schools and Academic Support
