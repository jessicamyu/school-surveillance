Meeting
Aug 18, 2020 - August Work Session
Category
7. Staff Action Items
Type
Action (Consent)
Dollar Amount
$135,000.00
Recommended Action
It is recommended that the Shelby County Board of Education approves the request for a one-year contract for professional services and coaching from the National Center for Urban School Transformation (NCUST), an organization that has identified, studied, and learned from over 160 urban elementary, middle, and high schools where every racial/ethnic/income group served outperforms state averages on multiple indicators. NCUST will develop the capacity of middle school principals by tailoring coaching support to the specific context, strengths, and needs of each individual schools. Services will be provided to build capacity and support in seven (7) middle schools (American Way, Craigmont, Georgian Hills, Grandview Heights, Raleigh Egypt, Riverview K-8, and Woodstock) with school based equity audits, network meetings, monthly on-site meetings at each middle school, participation in the America’s Best Urban Schools Symposium, and a book study for Teaching Practices from America’s Best Urban Schools. The total amount of the contract is $135,000 for the 2020-21 school year.
Goals
Priority 2: College and Career Readiness
Priority 3: Develop Teachers, Leaders, & Central Office

Responsible Party: Dr. Antonio Burt, Chief Academic Officer
