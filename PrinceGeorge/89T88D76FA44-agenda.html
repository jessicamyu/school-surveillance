Notes from the Chair

Good Evening,

Student Board Member Edward Burroughs, III will not be here tonight because he is attending the Maryland Student Council Conference in Ocean City, Maryland.

The Prince George's County Public Schools (PGCPS) Howard B. Owens Science
Center has started its spring schedule of public programs. Held in the planetarium, the
programs are designed for families to join their children for a fun learning experience
about the night sky.

Throughout the year, Howard B. Owens Science Center hosts a wide variety of
science education and enrichment programs for public school students using a
constructivist, hands-on approach. Working in partnership with its neighbor NASA and
other community partners, the Center also hosts special events and opportunities for
families and community members to learn more about science. The facility includes a
planetarium, a Challenger Learning Center, classroom laboratories, a nature trail, and a reptile center.

Planetarium programs are generally held the second Friday of each month. Doors
open at 7:15 p.m., and programs begin at 7:30 p.m. For more information please visit our website at www.pgcps.org.

On February 20, 2010 the Prince George's County Parent Teacher Association Council, Inc. (PGCPTA) was established to serve the Prince George's County local PTAs. We are very excited about this new organization and look forward to working with them and their assistance with our PTA’s.

Read Across America takes place on March 1 - 5. The program provides children with motivation to read. Motivating children to read is an important factor in student achievement and creating lifelong and successful readers. It calls on every community to celebrate Dr. Seuss’s birthday through reading motivation and awareness. I am happy to say that our Board Members will also participate in this program by going to schools and reading to our students.

KUDOS FOR students

Antawn Jamison of the Washington Wizards came to New Carrollton Elementary School to visit four students who won more than $80,000 worth of Hewlett-Packard equipment for their school in the HP Digital Assist Education Grant competition.

Eighth-graders Raynal Bell and Amber Booth, both 13, and seventh-graders Phyllis Arthur-Williams and Marquette Freeman, both 12, produced and filmed their video, "Science and Basketball," to demonstrate the concept of kinetic energy, and specifically whether the transfer of kinetic energy is related to the mass of the object. (Kinetic energy is the extra energy an object has because of motion).

Oxon Hill High School was featured on ABC World News Tonight for their efforts to not only raise $3,000, but up to $4,155. They will also donate mounds of clothes and food to the Red Cross.
