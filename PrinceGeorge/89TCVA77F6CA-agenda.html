Mrs. Jacobs read the following report:

    Notes from the Chair

Good Evening Everyone,

I am pleased to announce that on May 20th at the County Administration Building the County Council awarded Student Board Member Mr. Haywood Perry and our former Student Member Mr. Leslie Hall a Student Scholarship Award. This will continue as an incentive for our Student Board Members.

It was indeed an honor for my colleagues and I to attend the 2008 commencement exercises acknowledging so many young adults graduating from the Prince George's County Public School System. The Board of Education salutes the Class of 2008. Congratulations and best of luck in all of their future endeavors.

On June 17th, my colleagues Dr. Watson, Board Vice-Chair, Ms. Waller, At-Large Member, and I along with Dr. Deasy were joined by officials from the Michael & Susan Dell Foundation and the Eli and Edythe Broad Foundation to announce the award of grants totaling $6 million that will support the transformation to a “Performance Management” culture in Prince George's County Public Schools. The Board of Education truly appreciates this investment in the future of our students, and we are extremely proud to have the confidence and support of two nationally respected foundations.

At the request of my colleagues and I, an assessment was done by Parsons 3D/I of the condition of 184 Prince George's County public schools, and was recently completed and presented in a public meeting on June 17th. The Board will use this new information when identifying critical needs and prioritizing funding for capital improvements. The presentation and the complete facility condition assessment are available online for public consumption.

A reminder to parents that Early bird registration for new students, students who changed residence, and students who failed to properly register in the prior school year begins Monday, July 7, 2008. Immunizations are part of school registration as well. Register students before school starts to be sure schedules are ready on the first day. For more information visit our school system’s website.

Congratulations to the Prince George’s County Public Schools Department of Television Resources and Web Services, the Department of Curriculum and Instruction, and the 2007-2008 Student School Board Member Haywood Perry for their television work being recognized as excellent by the 29th Annual Telly Awards. Prince George’s County Public Schools TV 96 on Comcast and 38 on Verizon received six Telly Awards. For those of you that are not familiar with the Telly Awards, it is the Oscar or Emmy of the Cable industry. The television staff also did a tremendous job working with several PGCPS students under their training in the TV 96/38 Internship Program including the Student School Board Member that worked on programs that were honored for excellence.
    Kudos for Students
The U.S. Achievement Academy announced that Kelvin Jens M. Moncera of Fort Washington has been recognized for academic achievement as a U.S. National Honor Student and U.S. National Leadership and Service Award winner. Moncera, a sophomore at Oxon Hill High School for Science and Technology, has a 4.13 GPA and will appear in the U.S. Achievement Academy’s Official Yearbook which is published nationally.

Eight Prince George's County Public Schools students will receive the prestigious Robert C. Byrd college scholarship. The students were among 109 winners announced on June 18th by the Maryland State Department of Education (MSDE).
Each student will receive a four-year scholarship worth up to $1,500 per year, to be used for undergraduate study at any institution of higher education based on cost of attendance. Congratulations Deanna Gonzalez of Central HS, Kevin Guerrero of Parkdale HS, Morgan McKee of Gwynn Park HS, Quiana Montgomery of Friendly HS, Cameron Posey of Surrattsville HS, Tiffany Redfield of Laurel HS, Lisa Rosales of Parkdale HS, and Vinh Tran of Frederick Douglas HS.

I would like to wish the Oxon Hill High School Band and the Eleanor Roosevelt Wind Ensemble a safe trip, and good luck. They were selected to participate in the 2008 Beijing China Olympics on June 19th through June 30th to present a special band salute. Congratulations.
    Moment of silence
Kenyatta Boodram, Junior at Surrattsville High School

Quenton Rose, Sophomore at Bowie High School

Johnsie Hatcherson, Teacher at Dwight D. Eisenhower MS

Jasmine Clark, Para-Professional at Arrowhead Elementary School

Willie Harris, Cafeteria Assistant at Walker Mill Middle School

Before I yield the floor to Dr. Deasy for his report, I would like to take this time to bid farewell to Mr. Haywood Perry, III our Student Member. Would my colleagues please join me front and center for a presentation?

      Superintendent’s Report (FINAL)
      Board of Education Meeting
      June 26, 2008

      Thank you, Madame Chair:

      And Good Evening Board Members!
      I, too, would like to acknowledge Student Board Member Haywood Perry and congratulate him on his excellent service while on the Board. We wish him well as he prepares to move on to college and suggest he wait, at least a semester, before he tackles service on the university’s Board of Governors. I am, once again, honored to also congratulate Mr. Edward Burroughs, III on his election as the Student Member of the Board of Education for the 2008-09 academic year. We look forward to the “Passing the Torch” celebration on July 9th!

      I am delighted to report that our Schools closed on a positive note and our first Summer School session began on June 23rd . I encourage the public to go to our website at www.pgcps.org and review our Guide of all the scheduled Extended Learning Summer Programs.

      I am pleased to inform the Board that on June 3rd, Chief Operating Officer, Lawrence Fryer, met with the Mayor of Laurel, Mayor Moe, who has agreed to invoke HIS authority and reduce the fees for the Laurel High School project by 25%. This is a savings of approximately $35,000. As the Board will recall, Laurel High School is undergoing a major renovation project including a new auditorium and classroom addition. During this process, the site has received additional temporary classrooms. By regulation, the school system had to pay an additional $141,000 in permit fees to the City of Laurel. Hence, Mayor Moe’s demonstrated commitment to our school system and the students is greatly appreciated.

      On June 9th, Our Business Partners, Carl Werner, Chief Executive Officer of ASRC Federal and Debbie Crum, the Customer Advocate for Deltek, presented Prince George’s County Board of Education with a check for $40,000 to be used to build a new playground at the Judy Hoyer Family Learning Center. Business partnerships, such as the one with ASRC Federal and Deltek, have become more critical to public schools than ever before. Our Business Partnerships Office has expanded its efforts to match potential business partners with our schools’ needs.


      On June 17th, the Prince George’s County Board of Education received two grants, totaling $6 million, from Michael and Susan Dell and The Broad Foundations. This funding will support our New Performance Management System intended to raise student achievement, drive academic improvement and move the system from a ‘compliance culture’ to a true ‘performance culture.” Specifically, the New Performance Management System will allow the school system to regularly assess our organizational effectiveness and progress and to reward or discontinue certain practices or strategies based on quantifiable outcomes.



      On June 19, 2008, eight (8) Prince George’s County Public School students received the prestigious Robert C. Byrd College Scholarship. The students were among 109 winners announced by the Maryland State Department of Education. The selected students are:

      Deanna Gonzalez – Central High School
      Kevin Guerrero – Parkdale High School
      Morgan McKee – Gwynn Park High School
      Quiana Montgomery – Friendly High School
      Cameron Posey – Surrattsville High School;
      Tiffany Redfield – Laurel High School
      Lisa Rosales – Parkdale High School
      Vinh Tran – Frederick Douglas High School

      Each student received a four-year scholarship worth up to $1,500 per year to be used for undergraduate study at any institution of higher education based on cost of attendance. Our eight (8) scholars were chosen from more than 500 nominees made by public and private high school principals across the state.


      On this past Tuesday, June 24th, I was proud to announce that Prince George’s County Public Schools’ Enrichment and Specialty Programs Office will launch its Montessori Contextual Learning Environment to a third site this fall at the Judith P. Hoyer Early Childhood Center in Cheverly. Currently, the Montessori Program is offered at two sites – Robert Goddard Montessori School in Seabrook and John Hanson Montessori School in Oxon Hill. Our Montessori program is in exceptionally high demand by our community, and has a proven track record for academic success!


      Please Mark Your Calendars:

      Early Bird Registration begins Monday, July 7, 2008, for New Students, students who have changed their residence, and students who did not register during the prior school year.

        We encourage parents to register your children before school starts to be sure class schedules are ready on the first day! As Immunizations are also a part of school registration, we are encouraging parents to go to our website and click on the 2008-09 State School Immunization Schedule and plan to have your children vaccinated.

      In closing, I wish everyone a wonderful and safe summer!
