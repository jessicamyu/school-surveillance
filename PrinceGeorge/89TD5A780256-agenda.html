Notes from the Chair

Good Evening Everyone,

Dr. Watson, Ms. Waller and Student Board Member Mr. Haywood Perry will not be in attendance this evening.

On May 1st, Dr. Deasy and I attended the Washington Post “Agnes Meyer Outstanding Teacher Award” celebration for PGCPS teacher Natalie Stephenson from Kenmoor Middle School in Landover, Maryland. Also, on May 2nd I attended Princeton Elementary School as a guest speaker for the “Pearl of Wisdom” Career Day; it was beyond awesome to watch the students explore the “law” in their own unique way. Believe me they asked some very challenging questions – some that I really pondered such as “how old are you” and how much money does lawyers make.”

On Tuesday, May 6th the Prince George’s County Board of Education hosted the Board Awards Program at High Point High School in Beltsville, Maryland. This event honored teachers and students throughout various schools in the Prince George's County Public School System for their outstanding achievements.

Prince George’s County Public Schools (PGCPS) partnering with the Harlem Renaissance Foundation for the ninth consecutive year celebrated the Harlem Renaissance era by hosting the 9th Annual Harlem Renaissance Festival on Saturday, May 3rd at the Kentland/Columbia Park Community Center in Landover, Maryland. Parents had the opportunity to learn more about PGCPS programs and initiatives located in the Academic tent. PGCPS students showcased their talents on stage with musical and theatrical performances, and also hosted a Tech Showcase to display students’ artwork. In addition, students from Thomas G. Pullen Middle School dressed as characters of the Harlem Renaissance period which allowed visitors to “activate” the impersonators and hear about the person’s life.

In partnership with the national Special Olympics Organization, Prince George's County Public Schools (PGCPS) hosted the Special Olympics Spring Games on Wednesday, May 7th at the Prince George’s Sports and Learning Complex in Landover, Maryland. More than 500 student athletes participated in this year’s event in such sports as bowling, volleyball, running relays, softball, football, roller skating, baton relays, and aquatics.



Kudos for Students

Bowie resident Christian Zelenka, 17, was sworn in as Chief of the Amangemek Wipit Lodge 470 of the Order of the Arrow, an honor society of the Boy Scouts of America, on April 15. Zelenka is an Eagle Scout in Troop 1688 and a junior at Eleanor Roosevelt High School. As the lodge chief, he serves as the executive officer and oversees the largest lodge in the nation with more than 4,700 dues-paying members. Christian Zelenka ran for the office in March and has been an active participant in the scouting organization for 10 years.

Nearly 250 Laurel High School students participated in the Third Annual Interclass Olympics April 18, in which the grades competed against one another in basketball, track, flag football and kickball. The class with the greatest number of total wins overall, won. This year, it was the junior class, which won last year as the sophomore class. Its prize is the Interclass Olympics trophy, which goes each year to the winning grade. Principal Dwayne Jones’ goal is to improve morale.
