Jan-Michael Archer

 

Jan-Michael Archer is a doctoral student at the University of Maryland's School of Public Health and UMD-NSF Global STEWARD Fellow. Frustrated with the general lack of diversity and inclusion in conservation groups, Jan left outdoor education to pursue a career in research, policy, and action. As a member of the Center for Community Engagement, Environmental Justice, & Health (CEEJH), Jan has worked across public, private, and non-profit sectors to develop mapping tools and low-cost sensor networks that empower community members to collect, visualize, and interpret environmental data in their own neighborhoods.

 

 

Abel Olivo

 

Abel Olivo is the Executive Director of Defensores de la Cuenca, which translates to Watershed Defenders, a non-profit dedicated to engaging Latinos and Spanish-speakers in watershed-related issues and experiences. A community activist in Prince George’s County, MD, Olivo uses his insights and perspectives as a former lobbyist & advocate to connect resources and relationships through coalition building and strategic partnerships for the community. Olivo has extensive experience in developing and implementing policy and engagement campaigns to maximize reach, impact, and message.

 

Prior to founding Defensores de la Cuenca, Olivo was the Director of Community Outreach & Partnerships at Corazón Latino. He worked for 13 years in government affairs at the federal level. He worked with the highest levels of government on issues from large infrastructure projects and environmental impacts to banking and housing finance to education policy.

 

The son of migrant farm workers and the first in his family to graduate from college, Olivo earned his BA in Political Science from The Ohio State University and is a proud Buckeye. He now lives in Cheverly with his wife, Aimee, and their two sons, ages 15 and 11. Abel is very active in his community, teaching English as a Second Language at his church, serving as a member of the Cheverly Planning Board, a member of the Board of Directors for the Cheverly Swim and Racquet Club, and a member of the Board of Directors for Audubon Naturalist Society. He was recognized as a Regional Environmental Champion in 2020 by the Naturally Latino Conference planning committee for his outstanding work to engage the Latino and Spanish-speaking communities in the Washington, D.C. metropolitan region.

 

 

Staci Hartwell

 

As an Energy Advisor, Staci has been responsible for the sales and installation of over  40 MW of residential, commercial, and industrial solar in Washington, DC, and the  State of Maryland. Her clients include major property management companies, State  and Local Utility, and national retail brands including, but not limited to, DC Water  and Home Depot. Staci’s work includes helping homeowners adopt over 3MW of  solar as a part of the DMV’s residential energy mix. She has managed solar projects  from design to interconnection.  

 

Staci formed her energy expertise, while employed with energy giant and innovator,  Tesla. In doing so, she mounted “top-down, bottom-up” strategies for increasing market share for solar.  She developed a grass-roots and tops approach to advancing the adoption of solar through faith-based,  social and community groups, employment retraining organizations, and institutes of higher education.  She has lectured to realtors in the Maryland Realtors Association’s continuing education program to help  agents understand how to market solar. As a community volunteer/advocate, Staci was appointed as Co chair for Environmental and Climate Justice with the NAACP Maryland State Conference in November 2019. Since then, she has orchestrated the MD Solar Equity Initiative, a solar awareness, and jobs  campaign; the “Earth Day Solar Awareness” project; and “Climate Justice is Racial Justice” awareness  campaign, to name a few. 

 

Staci brings a unique mix of proven technical/industry knowledge, planning, and project management  skills to the table. Since graduating from Al Gore’s Climate Reality Project, she became heavily involved in  increasing community awareness for renewable energy. She was asked to join the NAACP Prince George’s  County Branch as the Environment and Health Chair, working with members, county constituents, local  and state government, and environmental coalition partners on “green initiatives.” She was recently  appointed to the Board to the League of Conservation Voters and consults regularly with leading  environmental foundations and institutes of higher learning. 

 

Her passion is the environment and for accelerating the adoption of renewable energy to, literally, help  save the planet. 

 

 

Emily Frias

 

Emily Frias has worked for Chesapeake Climate Action Network since 2018, as the Maryland Grassroots Coordinator. A Baltimore native, Emily is especially interested in environmental justice issues, and studied Economics and Geography at UMBC to focus in on these intersections. Prior to CCAN, Emily served as a Legislative Secretary in the Maryland General Assembly and as a Field Canvasser for Clean Water Action, fundraising and mobilizing grassroots energy on a range of campaigns. She also worked as the manager of UMD's Phonathon. In her free time, Emily works closely with the Prince George's DSA Branch on the Ecosocialist working group and goes on hikes with her neighbors in Greenbelt.
