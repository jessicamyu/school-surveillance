Subject
3.4 Climate Change Action Plan Focus Work Group Report
Meeting
Jan 24, 2022 - Academic Achievement Committee Meeting 5:30 P.M.
Category
3. Discussion Items
Type
Climate Change Action Plan Focus Work Group
Statements from Student Participants:
Breanna Malcolm, Eleanor Roosevelt HS and Nithin Gudderra, Oxon Hill HS
 
Breanna: Growing up in the Caribbean I experienced climate change first-hand with the loss of a beach close to my hometown. Since then I’ve had a passion for making a change in my community. The Climate Action Focus Work Group has been a great experience for me because I have the opportunity to combat climate change within my county. The meetings have been very insightful and I’ve been able to learn about the internal issues our community faces while also providing my feedback as a student on these challenges. I do believe that to mitigate climate change as students we can continue to demand action and work with legislators to make a change in government policy and laws. 
 
Nithin: My experience in the workgroup has been a good one. I’ve had the opportunity to listen and learn potential solutions for the issues in our communities and the world, which has been very insightful. I believe that we are responsible for protecting our world and ensuring that there is a safe future for the billions of people that come tomorrow. I can't pass laws and policies, but I can and will continue to use my voice to advocate and fight for a better and brighter future for all because I care. Students should and definitely can help with the implementation of the plan since we are the future. 
 
Both: As student members, we believe that motivation, education, and outreach are ways in which students can lead the implementation of this plan. There are already motivated students, which is one reason why many schools have their own green teams. Green teams generally serve as a way to educate students about issues in their communities and ways they can help. For example, at Eleanor Roosevelt HS our Environmental Defense Club does weekly recycling which is open to all students at Roosevelt. We often make outreach materials like posters and social media challenges to promote a cleaner environment. We have written testimonies and letters to our local officials ensuring that our perspectives are heard. Through initiatives like recycling and composting, student action and interest in making a change are shown. Our potential and perspective are what we can offer. Advocating and displaying how we are working towards change as students and a school community serve as a direct way to make our voices heard.
