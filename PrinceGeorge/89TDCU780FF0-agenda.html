Mrs. Jacobs read the following Chairs Report:
    Good Afternoon Everyone,

    Dr. Watson is attending a Leadership Maryland Orientation this afternoon, therefore, he will not be in attendance.

    The Maryland School Assessment (MSA)’s were administered this week to grades 5 – 8 this is a test of reading, math, and science achievement. The reading and mathematics tests are administered annually to students in grades 3 through 8. The science test is administered annually in grades 5 and 8. I would like to commend our students for their efforts during this very stressful week.


    Kudos for Students

    Musicians from Dr. Henry A. Wise Jr. High School in Upper Marlboro traveled to Florida in March to compete in Festival Disney. The school sent about 90 students from its concert, marching and jazz bands as well as the concert and gospel choirs and the drum line. The competition took place March 14 and 15 at the Walt Disney World Resort in Lake Buena Vista, Fla., which included both ranking competitions and rating competitions.

    Christopher Stevenson a senior at DuVal HS, was invited by the University Presidential Inaugural Conference to take part in the festivities surrounding the 56th Annual Presidential Inauguration on Jan. 20, 2009 where he will have a front row seat. He was selected to attend because he participated in a 10-day National Youth Leadership Forum on Medicine at Washington, D.C.’s Georgetown University in 2006.

    Nearly 60 student athletes and alumni from the Prince George’s County Public
    School special education program had the opportunity to compete in a swim meet sponsored by Special Olympics. Special Olympics provides year-round sports training and athletic competitions in a variety of Olympic-type sports for students and community members with disabilities, and celebrates ability, dedication, accomplishment, and teamwork.

    Moment of Silence
      Calvin Ford, at senior at Dr. Henry A. Wise, Jr., HS
      Gerda Lenz, grandmother of Birgitt Brevard, Creative Supervisor for the Office of Communications
      Guillermo Medina, a sophomore at Parkdale HS
      Virginia Simms, who worked for over thirty-five years in our school district as a Teacher, Administrator and Vocal Music Supervisor.
    Superintendent’s report

    I will now yield the floor to Dr. Deasy for the Superintendent’s report.

    Dr. Deasy, you may proceed.

    Thank you Dr. Deasy.
