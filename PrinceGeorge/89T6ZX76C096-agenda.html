June 17, 2010 Board Meeting


Good Evening,

Board Members Patricia Fletcher and Donna Hathaway Beck were in attendance at the 2010 Healthy Schools Recognition Program in New York City on June 15th, when three Prince George’s County Public Schools were recognized by the Alliance for a Healthier Generation for their efforts to provide a healthy environment and encourage students and staff to adopt a healthy lifestyle. Bradbury Heights Elementary School, Thurgood Marshall Middle School, and Charles H. Flowers High School are Bronze National Recognition Award winners. To earn this award, all three schools created healthy eating and physical activity programs that met or exceeded stringent standards set by the Alliance for a Healthier Generation’s Healthy Schools program. We would like to send a special thanks to Ms. Briana Webster-Patterson, Relationship Manager, Alliance for a Healthier Generations and also the principals and support staff of the recognized schools, their input was greatly appreciated.
KUDOS FOR students
Students at Arrowhead Elementary School participated in a school-wide Field Day on May 28th. However this was no ordinary Field Day, the students raised funds for the American Cancer Society. The Field Day was part of the school’s yearlong fundraising effort to aid the American Cancer Society. The school has already raised $2,200 through other events this year. Congratulations, job well done.

Seven Prince George’s County Public School students from Eleanor Roosevelt High School were winners in the 2010 National Achievement and National Merit Scholarship Programs. Reese Fuller, Fayola Fears, Benjamin Shaibu and Blossom Tewelde were among 800 who earned the $2,500 National Achievement Scholarship. Blossom Tewelde was also selected for the National Merit Scholarship. Thomas Senecal was selected for the corporate sponsored National Merit Mary E. Beyerle Scholarship. Laura Jones and Jeffrey Wang were selected for the College sponsored National Merit University of Maryland Scholarship. This award provides between $500 and $2,000 annually for up to four years. Congratulations.

Laurel Elementary School’s chorus traveled to Hershey, PA., on June 4th to participate in its first music festival and brought home a first place winner. The Music in the Park Festival held at Hershey Park contains competitions for elementary, middle and high school groups from Maryland, Virginia, Pennsylvania and the District of Columbia. Laurel Elementary School’s chorus, which is made up of third through sixth graders, won first place in the treble elementary choir category with a rating of “excellent” the highest rating in that category. Congratulations to the Laurel Elementary School chorus.
KUDOS FOR TEACHERS

Katherine Rodeffer, a Beltsville resident and instrumental music supervisor for Prince George's County Public Schools, was honored for her work April 17 by the Maryland Music Educators Association. Rodeffer received the association's Corwin Taylor Music Education Leadership Award, given annually to an educator who makes significant contributions to music education in the state. The award — established in 1993 — is named after the late Corwin Taylor, who taught instrumental music in Baltimore from 1945 to 1968, and at the University of Maryland, College Park, from 1968 to 1976. He died in 1992. In 10 years as the county's instrumental music supervisor, Rodeffer has organized programs to find more performance opportunities for students, provide instruments for at-risk and low-income students and train county music teachers.
