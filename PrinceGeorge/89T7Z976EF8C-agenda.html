Mrs. Jacobs read the following Chair’s Report:

Good morning and welcome to the February 27, 2010 Board Meeting of the Prince George’s County Board of Education. Before we begin, I would like to ask everyone to turn off any wireless communication devices as they interfere with the microphone and taping of the meeting.

Pursuant to the Education Article of the Annotated Code of Maryland, the Superintendent of Schools has proposed an Operating Budget for the school system covering the period July 1, 2010 through June 30, 2011, totaling $1,629,028,173. The amount is $82.2 million or 4.8% less than the FY 2010 Operating Budget originally approved by the Board of Education for the current fiscal year ending June 30, 2010.

The process for developing and adopting the budget includes significant public input. The FY 2011 Proposed Operating Budget development process included one public budget hearing held in October and one in November to receive community input on budget priorities. The Board of Education held three public budget hearings in January and February, and held public work sessions on the budget in January and February, as it considered adoption and submission of its budget request to the County Executive by March 1, 2010.

The Bridge to Excellence Master Plan, a dynamic strategic plan, guides the implementation of programs, services and resources necessary to achieve the mission of the school system. Performance against the strategies, objectives and goals of the strategic plan is measured and assessed regularly with adjustments made to programs and services where needed to improve performance. The Master Plan sets the direction for the annual operating budget.

The Superintendent of Schools originally proposed a FY 2011 Operating Budget totaling $1,668,679,898. Subsequent changes in State and County revenue estimates, and program and services priorities discussed by the Board of Education during recent budget work sessions, required a combination of reductions that amended the Superintendent’s initial Proposed Budget by $39,651,725. Accordingly, the proposed budget (as amended) totals $1,629,028,173. The resolution declares certain actions shall be used to eliminate and redirect existing budgeted resources in order to fund mandatory cost of doing business changes. Such actions include not funding cost-of-living adjustments, step increases, and staff differentials and stipends and implementing staff furloughs and reductions-in-force/layoffs.

The Board of Education, after considering public input from hearings on the proposed budget and discussions during public work sessions, must approve and transmit its FY 2011 Operating Budget Request to the County Executive by March 1, 2010.
