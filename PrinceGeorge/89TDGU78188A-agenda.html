Superintendent’s Report

Board of Education Work Session

March 26, 2008

Thank you, Mrs. Jacobs and Good Afternoon Board Members

Congratulations to Natalie Stephenson, a Language Arts teacher at Kenmoor Middle School, who has been selected to receive The Washington Post’s 2008 Agnes Meyer Outstanding Teacher Award for Prince George’s County.  The Agnes Meyer Award is presented annually to one teacher from each Washington metropolitan area school district.  The winners will be honored during a reception at The Washington Post on May 1st.   Ms. Stephenson has been working for Prince George’s County Public Schools since 2002.  She has contributed to her position as a Language Arts Instructor, teaching in summer school, writing curriculum, and acting as a Teacher Mentor in the International Teachers and Job-Alike Mentors’ Program.  She is also an English Department Adjunct instructor at Montgomery College in Rockville.  Stephenson has a Bachelor’s of Arts and Masters of Education in English from State University of New York at Buffalo.  She is attending Trinity University in Washington, D.C., working on a second Masters of Education in Administration, and expects to receive her degree this spring.  Again, Congratulations, Ms. Stephensen. 

     Congratulations to our Teacher of the Month, Mr. William Lynch.  Mr. Lynch has taught for 23 years – 20 of which has been in Prince George’s County Public Schools.  He graduated from the University of Washingtonand earned a Master’s degree from The Catholic University of America.  Mr. Lynch counts among his accomplishments efforts made in collaboration with colleagues at two schools – Kingsford Elementary and Rosa L. Parks Elementary School where he is currently teaching.  Over the years, he has co-sponsored one or more enrichment activities, including a Video Club, Photography Club, student newspaper, Morning Show, Discover Club, summer camps and plays benefitting hundreds of students.  Congratulations, Mr. Lynch.

I am proud to announce that we received a reading grant from the Comcast Foundation last calendar year for $90,000, for over three years, to implement the Reading Network program in select elementary schools.  The purpose of the program is to bring in volunteers to tutor 2nd grade students who are reading below grade level.  So far the program has been very successful in our elementary schools.  The program is a joint partnership between Prince George’s County Public Schools, Comcast, the State’s Attorney’s Office and the Prince George’s Community Foundation.  As part of the grant, we are also partnering with a public relations effort featured in Today’s Newsbreak.  I would like to publically thank our partners who are here this evening.  WILL YOU PLEASE STAND:  Comcast, the State’s Attorney’s Office and the Prince George’s Community Foundation.

Congratulations to Ugonna Ohiri, a senior at DuVal High School, who was accepted into the University of Maryland-Baltimore County, Meyerhoff Scholar Program.  Ugonna is a member of the Student Government Association and the National Honor society.

 

Please note the following dates:

Tuesday, April 1st and Wednesday, April 2nd, the Maryland School Assessment in Reading will be administered at all Elementary and Middle Schools;

Also on the April 1st is the 10th Annual Career Expo, “Tune in to Transition,” hosted by PGCPS Department of Special Education.  The event will be held at PG Community College for 10th and 11th grade students in the Special Education Program to provide life skills and workshops related to careers.

Thursday, April 3, 11:30 a.m. - Connect with Kids “Student Summit” at Camelot

Friday, April 4, 11:30 a.m. - Connect with Kids “Community Conversations” at Camelot

Friday, April 4th is the deadline to apply for the International Baccalaureate Program.  The IB Program is offered in 4 of the high schools:  Central, Suitland, Parkdale and Laurel;

On Monday, April 7th, the Dr. Henry A. Wise, Jr. High School Simple Elements Jazz Band will perform at Blues Alley Supper Club.

Also on Monday, April 7th, the public Hearing on Laurel-Beltsville Area Boundary Changes will be held at 7:00 pm at Beltsville Elementary School.  The Prince George’s County Board of Education will hold a public hearing on the Boundary changes being proposed in the Laurel-Beltsville area to accommodate the opening of the new Laurel-Beltsville area Elementary school in previous public hearing some community members were in favor of “Option B” as presented by the Board for the proposed Boundary changes and possible grade structure changes for the elementary and middle schools in the area.  As another option for the community to consider, the Board will present an “Option Z” for further consideration.  All parents, students, and citizens are welcome to attend and will have the opportunity to offer comments for consideration by the Board of Education.  To register to speak at the hearing, call the Region 5 Office at (240) 264-1700 before 4 p.m. on the date of the hearing.

  The Maryland School Assessment in Math will be administered on Tuesday, April 8th and Wednesday, April 9th, at all Elementary and Middle Schools.

Please join me in a moment of silence on behalf of a PreK student at John Bayne, Egypt Hussan Thomas, who drowned in the bathtub on Monday afternoon, March 24th.

Also, on behalf of another PreK-student at Chapel Forge Early Childcare Center, Fernando Amaya, who drowned in a friend of a family’s swimming pool.  Both students were 4 years old.
