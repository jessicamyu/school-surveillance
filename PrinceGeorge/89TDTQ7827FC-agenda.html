Mrs. Jacobs read the following Chair's Report:

Good Evening Everyone, I would like to bring to your attention the following changes to the Board Calendar. The Budget Work Session on February 13th, has been cancelled, Board Work Session scheduled for Thursday, March 6th has been changed to Thursday, March 13th. This meeting will convene at 1:00 PM. The Board Meeting scheduled for Thursday, March 27th has been changed to Wednesday, March 26th and the Board Work Session for May 8th has been changed to an evening meeting and will convene at 7:05 PM.

Colleagues, please confirm by motion that the Board will not receive Public Comment at the Budget Work Session on February 11, 2008. The Board has received information and public input on the proposed budget during the budget public hearing process. All in Favor.

The Board Members along with Dr. Deasy will attend an RGA Site Visit on March 4th and 5th at the Comfort Inn Hotel in Bowie, Maryland. This meeting is open to the public, however, public comment is not allowed.

Governor O’Malley and County Executive Jack Johnson toured Bladensburg HS on Monday, January 28th, to announce school construction funds. Funds were committed to the following projects, most of which pays back the county for forward funding: Rosa Parks ES, Bladensburg HS, DuVal HS, Laurel-Beltsville ES, and Northview ES.
Kudos for Students

Congratulations to the Technical Academy students from Suitland High School who had the opportunity to showcase their electric car at the Washington Auto Show in January. Digital images are available on the website.

Superintendent’s Report

Board of Education Meeting

February 7, 2008

Thank you, Madame Chair:

We are live on the Web today.  Prince George’s County Public Schools is happy to announce that we are now able to provide 24 hour live webcasting of our TV stations 96 and 38.  Our various publics can now watch our Board of Education meetings on the web.

I would like to recognize the International Student Guidance and ESOL offices for their outstanding efforts in organizing the Hispanic College Fair on Saturday, February 2nd.  There were 900-1000 participants (mostly parents

We are also excited to announce that the number of schools making significant academic progress is increasing!!   Forty-nine (49) Prince George’s County Public Schools have been honored as part of the Maryland School Performance Recognitions Program for the 2006-07 school year.  The number of schools making significant progress increased nearly 45% from last year’s total of 34!  Forty-eight (48) elementary schools and one high school received the State’s School improvement Award for Achievement – or the School Improvement Award for Improvement Among Subgroups, or both.  Each year, the state releases information on how well schools have done in meeting state performance standards.  All states were required to set high standards for school performance based on student learning and attendance.  The number of schools making significant academic progress continues to grow each year, with much thanks due to the combined efforts of principals, teachers, students, and parents!

The Center of the Visual and Performing Arts located at Suitland High School continues to accept applications for the 2007-08 school year.  Current 8th and 9th grade students who have a strong desire to pursue college-level training for a career in the arts are strongly encouraged to apply.  Transportation will be provided to all selected students regardless of home address provided they reside in Prince George’s County.  The application deadline is extended to February 10, 2008.  Applications are available through all of the creating and performing arts teachers at each school.  All auditions will be held on March 1, 2008.

Our students are using the electric car which provides a unique learning opportunity on how to apply their electrical skills to automotive technology.  The electric car was featured at the Washington Auto Show in Washington, DC from January 23-27.  These students are also repairing motorcycles through a special internship program at the Old Glory Harley-Davidson motorcycle dealership in Laurel, Maryland.  The electrical class is also currently working with licensed contractors to wire the Technical Academy’s student built house in Clinton, Maryland.

We hosted a press conference on January 30 to kick off our Litter Free Offices initiative, which focuses on expanding our recycling efforts from the school level to central offices. I would like to thank representatives from the County Executive’s Office, Keep Prince George’s County Beautiful, the student recycling team from Patuxent Elementary, and Gwynn Park HS junior Dennis Harvey (our Paper Retriever mascot) for partnering in this event. I encourage all Sasscer employees to support this effort and set an example for students by recycling their clean paper waste in the recycling boxes distributed throughout the building.

This week is National School Counseling Week. Sponsored by the American School Counselor Association (ASCA), the event was established to focus public attention on the important contributions of professional school counselors, and celebrate and highlight the critical role they play in helping students achieve academic success and prepare for their future. I encourage students and families to acknowledge and thank their professional school counselor for their important contribution towards student achievement.

Nominations for the Maryland Teacher of the Year are due to the Department of Publicity and Publications by February 29th.  Please consider nominating an outstanding teacher from your school.

Over 140 of our school sites will be used as polling places for the upcoming primary election on February 12th.  On that date, our schools and offices will be closed.

Thank you!
