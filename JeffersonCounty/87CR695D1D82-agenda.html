Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

    Title I, Part A (Improving the Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged) $9,878,857
    Funds are used to close the achievement gap of disadvantaged students by accelerating learning in literacy and math. The district will provide professional development opportunities to all Title I building administrators, teachers and instructional paraprofessionals on aligning instruction to the district’s prioritized benchmarks, essential learnings, assessments and site-based resources for all students within the core in reading, math, science, and social studies. With the assistance of School Improvement Specialists, all Title I schools will create meaningful school improvement plans based on data. Teachers in Title I schools will increase their capacity for delivering quality instruction as a result of the Specialist supervising the Instructional Coaches in Title schools. Title I funds will increase the number of teachers who are trained in ESL (English as a Second Language) strategies. Additional staff will supplement services for the district ELLs (English Language Learners) to enhance and expand the frequency and availability of high-quality professional development opportunities for teachers and administrators. This program aligns with strategic plan business and finance section, objective 1, which ensures the alignment of resources to include student achievement and organizational effectiveness, as well as community section, objectives 1 and 2, ensuring the district reaches out to the community and involves parents and families in increasing student achievement. Linda Reyes-Quinonez is the Title I director.

    Title II, Part A (Preparing, Training and Recruiting High Quality Teachers and Principals) $2,469,514
    Funds increase student academic achievement by increasing the number of highly qualified teachers and principals. The allocation will provide the ten Title I schools with the highest percentage of free and reduced lunch rates and the highest percentages of minority students an additional teacher to reduce class size. 35 Instructional Coaches will provide ongoing site-based staff development to support teachers to be highly skilled in the teaching of reading, writing, and math. Curriculum coordinators will support teacher training or curriculum development that provides teachers with increased knowledge, skills, and strategies for working with low-performing students. The district will implement EMII (Early Mathematics Instruction and Intervention) to assist identified students in grades K-2 for additional support. The funds will provide science workshops for teachers to increase content knowledge for specific science units. They will also provide pre-advanced placement training and staff development for teachers in grades 7-10 to increase the number of students enrolled and successful in AP classes. The district will provide technical assistance by conducting school reviews to those schools on academic watch, on probation, or rated low on the SAR (School Accountability Report). Title II funds will provide assistance to schools and teachers on implementing best strategies for gifted students. A plan will be developed to assess the highly qualified status of special education teachers. This program aligns with strategic plan business and finance section, objective 3, which ensures a workforce that is diverse, qualified, and skilled; strategic plan instruction section, objective 2, which ensures professional development of all staff that results in improved skills to increase student achievement and instructional leadership capacity; and strategic plan schools section, objective 2, which ensures instructional staff development for teachers and administrators results in improved skills to increase student achievement. Cindy Hernandez is the program manager.

    Title II, Part D (Enhancing Education Through Technology) $92,950
    Funds support student achievement through use of technology in schools. These funds will be allocated directly to schools/articulation areas to support site based professional development. Technology leadership teams will submit plans for how they will provide site based activities and resources to support all students being technologically literate as defined by district technology benchmarks, support teachers in their skill levels and knowledge of best instructional strategies with technology, and support teachers with effective strategies for using core district technology tools. The district will develop new online courses that support the needs identified in articulation proposals and provide facilitation of these online courses to support teacher learning. Teams of teachers and librarians will work to identify and develop effective resources for teachers to use in the implementation of Education Technology/Information Literacy (ET-IL) benchmarks within the district curriculum. The program aligns with strategic plan instruction section, objective 1, which ensures curricula, assessments, and tools of technology support schools in increasing academic achievement. John Canuel manages these funds.
    Title III (Language Instruction for Limited English Proficient and Immigrant Students) $505,110 and Title III Immigrant Set-Aside $98,275
    Funds focus existing programs on teaching English to limited English proficient (LEP) children, including immigrant children and youth, and holding states accountable for their LEP students attaining English. With Title III funds, Jeffco will provide additional staff to improve, supplement and expand the quality and availability of multilingual parent communication between schools, families and district departments. High quality, research-based instructional materials and curriculum will be purchased in order to appropriately and equitably serve the ELL population in language instruction educational programs. Summer school programs will provide ELLs extended learning opportunities and extra tutorial services that have a positive impact on student achievement. The programs are designed for “acceleration” not “remediation.” Funds will be used to build capacity of secondary administrators, counselors, content teachers, and ESL teachers to help immigrant students meet graduation requirements, support these students and their families with the culture of U.S. schools, U.S. and Colorado history, U.S. and district expectations of parental involvement in their children’s education, graduation requirements, and post-secondary opportunities. The district will use technology to promote student engagement. The program aligns with the strategic plan schools section, objective 1, which ensures that student achievement is increasing in every school regardless of gender, race/ethnicity, socioeconomic status, or special needs. Susan Miller and Catherine Baldwin are the Program Co-Directors for the Title III Funds.
    Title IV (Safe and Drug Free Schools) $200,286
    These funds supports programs that prevent violence in and around schools and prevent the illegal use of alcohol, tobacco, and drugs in order to foster a safe and drug- free learning environment that supports student academic achievement. Title IV funds will provide a team of qualified, knowledgeable staff to support the multi-faceted and comprehensive approach in order to maintain a safe school environment. Staff will collaborate with other departments and agencies to secure the necessary resources to support youth in healthy lifestyle development, setting forth clear behavioral expectations through district policy, advocacy for parental involvement, adopting evidence-based practices, planning for active youth participation, and support of interagency collaboration in ways that can measure the outcomes of our efforts. The program aligns with the strategic plan schools section, objective 3, which ensures a safe, welcoming, caring, and collaborative community. Betsy Thompson is the program manager.
