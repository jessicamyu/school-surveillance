2.02 Supplemental Funds - CDE to DLEA (EL-6)
Meeting
Oct 22, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A, B and C.
    1. The Department of Learning and Educational Achievement (DLEA) received third year funds of a three year grant of $206,321 from the Colorado Department of Education’s math and science partnership. The program aligns with the strategic plan community objective 1, which ensures the district reaches out to the community and continuously builds support for Jeffco Schools and instruction objective 2, which ensures professional development of all staff results in improved skills to increase student achievement and instructional leadership capacity.
    2. The Northeast Front Range Mathematics/Science Partnership to Increase Teacher Competence in Content (MSP) is a collaboration of 10 school districts (Adams 12, Adams 14, Brighton, Elizabeth, Englewood, Jefferson County, Mapleton, Platte Canyon, St. Vrain, and Weld County Re-8) and four universities (University of Colorado at Boulder, University of Colorado at Denver, University of Denver, and Metropolitan State College of Denver) who commit to working collaboratively to provide professional development to high need teachers. Jeffco serves as the fiscal agent of this grant. The partnerships will provide four summer institutes where teachers engage in learning mathematics or science content delivered by university faculty.
    3. The project will be evaluated by an outside evaluator to determine if student achievement in math and science improves as a result of their teachers participating in project-sponsored professional development.
    4. The district is not required to continue this project once funding ends.
    5. Tammy Weatherly, science director, is the project manager.
    6. Please open the attachment below to review additional background material.

2.03 Contract Renewal: Oracle Maintenance Agreement (EL-7)
Meeting
Oct 22, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.The district had a contract with DLT Solutions to procure Oracle maintenance for PeopleSoft modules, including Human Resources, Financials and Database Management. The 2008/2009 contract was for $622,559.88.
2.The Oracle quote for maintenance renewal for the 2009/2010 term is $634,764.17, approximately one percent less than the DLT quote.
3.Due to Colorado State law regarding vendors who contracted with undocumented workers, Oracle previously was not able to enter into agreements with Colorado public entities. This law was amended late in 2008 to exempt technology vendors from the requirements. Because of this exemption, Oracle was able to provide pricing for the upcoming renewals.
4. Oracle is the provider of all maintenance services, whether the services are purchased through a reseller or from them directly. Purchasing directly from Oracle will provide for a deeper discount on their services.
5.The contract with Oracle will be for an initial one year term, with the option to renew up to four additional years subject to annual review and approval.

2.04 Contract Award: ISC (EL-7)
Meeting
Oct 22, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1. The district disk storage system contains all mission critical data (application databases for student grades & attendance as well as financials and HR data). The Storage Array System was acquired in August, 2000 and has been fully depreciated. It is approaching product end of life and end of support from the vendor. The decision was made to replace current storage array with a more cost effective, scalable “green” system that would provide capacity and reliability for district data.
2.In August of 2009, a Request for Proposal (RFP) for an EMC NS-480 Storage Array System was issued on the Rocky Mountain E-Purchasing System (RMEPS) to 114 vendors and emailed to five additional vendors and the manufacturer.
3.Three responses were received of which two met the specifications. The three responses were received from: Dell, ISC, and MSN Communications, Inc.
4.District staff members thoroughly evaluated the submitted proposals. Dell’s response did not meet the specifications. ISC and MSN quoted the correct systems, however, ISC’s pricing was substantially lower than MSN. Reference checks were performed as provided.
5.The contract will be for a one time purchase of equipment and training with ISC. All training and services will be provided by the manufacturer, EMC directly. EMC will also provide three year warranty service. The cost of the equipment is $656,256.06. Training for five employees is $49,477.50.
6.The award also includes file synchronization services which will provide an offsite replication of data. Cost of this service is $101,753.46. Approximately $100,000.00 of the current maintenance fees paid for the old disk storage system will be credited to the district as part of this purchase to offset this cost.

To authorize the director of Purchasing to negotiate a contract with ISC in the amount of $807,487.02.

Motion by Richard G Rush, second by Dave J Thomas.

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.
