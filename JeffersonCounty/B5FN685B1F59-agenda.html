Meeting
Dec 13, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category

estimated time: 6 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 minutes) 

The Board of Education is pleased to recognize Kendrick Lakes Elementary School students, community and leaders for being awarded the 2018 National Blue Ribbon award.  Principal Sarah Roberts and David Singh, former principal, will be joining the Board with members of the school community. 
 

The National Blue Ribbon Award is an annual designation presented to public and private elementary, middle, and high schools throughout the United States. Schools are nominated for the award by the state department of education, and then complete a comprehensive application about school practices. The program began in 1982 with the goal of recognizing schools for their academic excellence and accomplishments in closing achievement gaps among student subgroups. See the full press release from the U.S. Department of Education here: https://www.ed.gov/news/press-releases/us-department-education-announces-2018-national-blue-ribbon-schools.
 

Only 349 schools nationwide received National Blue Ribbon School honors this year, five are in Colorado. The U.S. Department of Education honored the award-winning schools at a recognition ceremony November 7 and 8 in Washington, D.C.

6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Dec 13, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation, EL-7, Asset Protection, "the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000."
     
  2. The federal statute Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) requires that students must have access to related services that have been deemed necessary in order to help the students benefit from their special education services and from the general education classroom so that they may achieve academic growth.  Speech language therapy and occupational therapy are considered to be related services.
     
  3. Speech language and occupational therapy services must be provided in order to stay in compliance with current student Individual Education Plans (IEP) in the district.  The district has a need for these therapy services to meet the needs of students that are on an IEP or a 504 Plan.  The district has been unable to hire enough staff to provide the required services to our students.
     
  4. According to IDEA, compensatory services must be provided if a student has not received the number of service minutes on the IEP.
     
  5. All students are required to receive a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE). This principle provides that special education and related services are provided to eligible children with disabilities at public expense, and without charge to the parent or guardian.  
     
  6. It is imperative that related service providers are hired for students that have an IEP or a 504 Plan.  Despite continued job postings for speech language and occupational therapy providers since May 2018, Jeffco Public Schools continues to have positions unfilled.
     
  7. Jeffco Public Schools will continue to search for related service providers to be employees of the district.  Although the shortage of speech language pathologists and occupational therapists are a state and national issue, Jeffco Public Schools will actively recruit and hire employees to serve our students.
     
  8. Jeffco Public Schools has entered into an agreement for services with Advanced Medical Personnel Staffing for the current 2018/2019 school year in the amount of $466,000.  It is estimated that an additional $320,000 will be needed to serve the needs of Jeffco students.  The ongoing need for providing speech language pathologists and occupational therapists in addition to the resignation of current Jeffco service providers requires an additional increase in the contract for the remainder of the 2018/2019 school year to stay in compliance with IDEA.
     
  9. Staffing firms are pre-qualified and rates established to provide special education staffing services.  As needs arise, pre-qualified firms are requested to submit resumes of qualified, available staff for district interview.  The most qualified contracted staff are selected to temporarily fill open positions.  Those companies with selected staff are awarded agreements to provide services as needed throughout the year.
