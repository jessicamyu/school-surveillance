Katie Winner, task force chair

 

PURPOSE:

The Start Time Task Force will provide information to the Board of Education about their work researching/investigating the importance of changing school start times for middle and high school students and present the task force recommendations to the Board of Education for acceptance.

 

BACKGROUND:

The task force was commissioned by the superintendent in February 2018 and since then has been meeting approximately every six weeks. The charge of the task force by the superintendent was to research the issue of secondary school start times and present a set of recommendations to the superintendent and Board of Education in early spring, 2019.

The Board of Education is proud to honor the Jeffco school communities and their parent, teacher and principal leadership for achievement of the 2018 Governor's Distinguished Improvement Awards, John Irwin Awards, Center of Excellence Awards and High School Academic Growth Award.  Thirty-eight schools will be recognized for 45 academic awards - and increase from 35 schools last year.
 

Governor's Distinguished Improvement Award schools demonstrated exceptional rates of student growth. These schools received an Exceeds expectations rating on the Academic Growth indicator of the School Performance Frameworks, reflecting consistently high rates of student growth in English Language Arts, Math and English Language proficiency.

 

John Irwin Award schools demonstrated the highest achievement in the state (top 8 percent), as measured by performance on the Colorado Measures of Academic Success (CMAS) assessments. These schools received an Exceeds expectations rating on the Academic Achievement indicator of the School Performance Frameworks reflecting exceptional performance in Math, English Language Arts, and Science.

Center of Excellence schools engaged a student population, of which at least 75 percent are at-risk students, and performed at the highest rates of student longitudinal growth as measured by the Colorado Growth Model.
 

Receiving the High School Academic Growth Award indicates a high school that demonstrated the highest levels of student academic growth in reading, writing and math, within each classification used by the Colorado High School Activities Association (CHSAA) for the sport of football.

estimated time: 6:36 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

6.06 Limited Waiver of District Policy: Localworks Ridge at 38 Criterium and Brewfest and Ridgefest Community Events (EL-7)
Meeting
Feb 7, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the request by Localworks for a limited waiver of district policy allowing for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages at the 2019 Ridge at 38 Criterium and Brewfest on Sunday, June 9, and the 2019 Ridgefest event on Saturday, September 7, in accordance with the MOU between Jeffco Public Schools and the City of Wheat Ridge, on the south lawn and parking lot at the Stevens Elementary school site.
Goals
EL-7, Asset Protection

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, “the superintendent shall not allow assets of the district to be unprotected, inadequately maintained, inappropriately used or unnecessarily risked…."
     
  2. Localworks is presenting the Ridge on 38 Criterium and Brewfest, in its sixth year and growing, is a state championship level Rocky Mountain Road Cup race sanctioned by the Bicycle Racing Association of Colorado (BRAC) and USA Cycling. The event is one of the few races in Colorado that awards equal prize money to both men and women. A Criterium is a short, fast, 6 corner, 1.15-mile course that takes place on city streets and ensures a lot of action and excitement for spectators and athletes. The brewfest and vendor market were added four years ago to this family friendly event that includes music, food trucks and a children’s area with face painters and kids crafts. The event will be held on Sunday, June 9, 2019. The racing event begins at 9 a.m., the children's area runs from 11 a.m. to 6 p.m., and the vendor area and Brewfest run from noon to 6 p.m.
     
  3. The Localworks Ridge at 38 Ridgefest event on Saturday, September 7, 2019, will be held on the south lawn and parking lot at the Stevens Elementary school site. The free, family-friendly event is an opportunity for the community to celebrate the heritage of Wheat Ridge and Ridge at 38. The event aims to satisfy enthusiasts of all kinds through diverse offerings like a classic car show, an Artisan Marketplace, the Chalk Art Festival, live bluegrass music, a kids area and other community activities.
     
  4. Both events are intended to promote and encourage community spirit and citizen participation within Wheat Ridge. Both are non-district sponsored events. The Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) allows the City of Wheat Ridge to serve, sell, possess and distribute alcoholic beverages on the premises subject to Board of Education approval.
     
  5. In 2013, Jeffco Public Schools and the City of Wheat Ridge agreed to an MOU that allows for the use of the south lawn and the south parking lot of the Stevens Elementary school site (formerly Wheat Ridge 5-8 School), subject to mutually agreed to terms and conditions for community events and district policy.
     
  6. In accordance with the MOU, Localworks is requesting a limited waiver of district policy and regulation GBEC, Drug Free Workplace, and KF-R, Community Use of School Facilities, to allow for the sale and consumption of alcohol on school grounds during both events.
     
  7. Localworks, the event sponsoring organization, under the auspices of the City of Wheat Ridge, is a community-based non-profit corporation formerly known as Wheat Ridge 2020, with a mission of advancing Wheat Ridge as a vibrant and sustainable community. The City of Wheat Ridge's "Repositioning Wheat Ridge" Plan informs the work of Localworks to encourage appropriate activity and development necessary to retain and attract strong Jefferson County and Denver metro area households to create a community that will fill an important niche in the community.
     
  8. Localworks staff and volunteers are Colorado TIPS Alcohol trained on awareness and prevention of intoxication, identification of fake IDs and development of people skills for customer intervention. Localworks staff and volunteers have also participated in training through the Wheat Ridge Police Department with respect to public drunkenness. The Wheat Ridge Police Department will be at both events, as well as additional security personnel at the Criterium and Brewfest, to monitor exits and entrances. Localworks works closely with Wheat Ridge Liquor Enforcement to ensure compliance with laws for both events.
     
  9. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant the limited waiver of district policy GBEC with respect only to the prohibited use and possession of alcoholic beverages for these events. The policy prohibits the unlawful possession and use of alcohol on district premises. It also states that, “...an employee knowingly in possession of, or under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance shall be suspended immediately by his principal or supervisor if such use or possession is on any district property at any time.” Approval of the limited waiver at this time will allow Localworks to finalize details of the event and to begin promoting these events to the community.
     
  10. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policy regulation KF- R with respect only to the prohibited use and possession alcoholic beverages for this event. This policy states that, “...the use and possession of alcoholic beverages or drugs by any person or group on district property is strictly prohibited.”
     
  11. Localworks will have a Special Events liability insurance policy covering the district and other entities supporting this event, and the City of Wheat Ridge will provide police/fire protection for the event.
     
  12. Building and Facility Use forms have been completed and approved and a copy of the approved permit will be posted during the event. The Board of Education has approved similar limited waiver of district policy to allow for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages on district property 13 times since 2009.

Meeting
Feb 7, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category

Recommended Action
to be informed of supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for February 2019.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The Gill Foundation awarded Choice Programming $275,000, via Jeffco Schools Foundation, for developing K-12 progressive STEM pathways in the North Arvada articulation area. The grant will support building Creative Learning Systems SMART (STEM) labs at Swanson and Thomson Elementary Schools. Future labs are planned for Secrest, Hackberry Hill, North Arvada Middle and Arvada High. The project addresses the Conditions for Learning Tactic: Commitment to Equity through a focus on increasing underrepresented students pursuing STEM pathways. STEM Pathway Designer Heather Waldron and Choice Programming Director Marna Messer oversee the project.
     
  2. Jeffco Schools Foundation awarded Educational Research & Designs Visual Arts Department $15,000 to continue four district-wide Art Shows (Equity and Excellence, Elementary School, Middle School and High School). Exhibits provide a public venue for recognizing achievements of student artists. Funds cover substitutes and/or additional pay, allowing art teachers to prepare for the shows. Curriculum coordinator Shannon May, from Choice Programming, organizes the events.
     
  3. Rose Community Foundation awarded the Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters (HIPPY) program $20,000 for the home-based, peer-delivered, early intervention program that helps parents provide educational support for their children. Fifty-percent of enrolled families speak a primary language other English. HIPPY aligns with Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning Tactic: Meaningful Family Engagement. Coordinator Lisa Schell, from the Early Learning Department, manages the program.
     
  4. The City of Golden awarded Shelton Elementary $10,000 for a new robotics and coding makerspace in the library. At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
