2.04 Student Based Budgeting (SBB) Project Update
Meeting
Oct 12, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 5:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes)

PRESENTING STAFF:

Brenna Copeland, chief, Finance

Dave Weiss, chief of schools

Dan Bock, principal, Lakewood High School

Ryan Lucas, principal, Governor’s Ranch Elementary School



PURPOSE:
To update the Board of Education on the project to analyze and redesign the Student Based Budgeting formula and process so that schools have equitable resources to meet the needs of their students.

 

BACKGROUND:

The district adopted a student-based budgeting approach to resource schools for the 2015-16 school year. This approach provides funding to school budgets based on the number of students they serve. The district also employees a staff-allocation approach for certain school-based resources (e.g., SPED teachers, supports to English learners, custodial, food service, etc.). After several years of this blended approach, the district has undertaken a detailed review of what’s working and what needs improvement in the way resources are distributed to schools.

At the June 8 meeting, the board received an update about the work. The project is supported by Education Resource Strategies, an external group with deep expertise in student based budgeting and the ability to both provide analysis and consultative services as the district undertakes revisions to its budgeting approach.

Since that update, the district and ERS have engaged in the analysis phase of the project, which is nearing completion. This presentation aims to provide a brief update on the analysis work and a deeper discussion of the next steps in the design phase of work to change and improve the SBB formula and process.

2.05 Programs and Student Supports Funded With ESSER III
Meeting
Oct 12, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 5 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dr. Kym LeBlanc-Esparza, deputy superintendent

Brenna Copeland, chief, Finance

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will receive an update on the programming and student supports available through the ESSER III grant from the federal government, as administered by the Colorado Department of Education. Board members will hear about changes to the original programming plans that have been adopted since their last Board update in May 2022.

 

BACKGROUND:

ESSER III is the final round of funding available to Jeffco as part of the American Rescue Plan. Approximately $68.2 million was supplied to the district. $6.9 M has been distributed to charter schools. The remaining $61.3 M is available to support a wide array of district programs over a three year period from 2021-22 through 2023-24. 

 

The district submitted a preliminary grant budget, inclusive of plans from charter schools, in December of 2021. The district then received comments and questions from the Colorado Department of Education. The district submitted revisions in response to these comments and questions multiple times through April 2022, obtaining provisional approval in that timeframe. 

 

This presentation will focus on revisions to the plan that have been adopted since the last board update, item 2.02 at the June 8, 2022 board study session.

To provide the Board of Education with a brief presentation on the district's DPF and growth information.

2.08 Updates to Discipline and Restraint Policies
Meeting
Oct 12, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session

estimated time:  7:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 15 minutes)

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Julie Tolleson, chief, Legal Officer

David Weiss, chief, School Leadership

Matt Palaoro, chief, Student Success

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board to review and discuss the proposed revisions of policies JK, JKD/JKE, JKA and JKG.  These policies both reflect changes in legislation (including HB-22-1376 and HB 19-1194) and some language and format changes aligned to model policies from the Colorado Association of School Boards.  The proposed policies also including a new policy JKBA regarding the framework for teachers to remove students from classrooms pursuant to CRS 22-32-109.1(2)(a)(I)(B).  Final drafts will be presented for a final vote at the October 20, 2022, regular Board Meeting.

 

BACKGROUND:

Policies JK, JKD/JKE, and JKD were last reviewed and revised more than nine years ago.  Colorado statutes require that certain district policies be subject to approval by the Board of Education. Policies related to the district's conduct and discipline code, including regarding suspension and expulsion, are among those that require board action.  Policy JKA was reviewed more recently but amendments to the restraint statute require some adjustments.

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education annually reviews the proposed resolutions collated by the Colorado Association of School Boards (CASB) which provide direction to the efforts of the CASB during the 2022 legislative session.
 

BACKGROUND:

The Board's discussion will provide guidance to the Board's delegate, Stephanie Schooley, who will attend the Fall Conference and Delegate Assembly on October 23 in order to finalize the guidance to the statewide organization.
 

The Colorado Association of School Boards (CASB) holds its annual Fall Conference and Delegate Assembly in October in preparation for legislative work in Colorado on public education. There are proposed resolutions for the 2022 legislative session as well as standing resolutions on local government, finance and student achievement. 
 

At the Delegate Assembly, positions are taken on resolutions which affect state school districts. As one of the largest school districts in the state, the work of CASB may impact the district's work.
