2.01 Public Comment: Bond Issue and Mill Levy Override Ballot Questions
Meeting
Aug 2, 2016 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Public Agenda
Type
Information

estimated time: 5:30 p.m.  (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in a Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Thursday).  
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on the topic of a potential bond and/or mill levy override question on the November 8, 2016 election ballot related to this evening's agenda for up to one hour. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings. Thank you.

Recommended Action
to adopt the resolutions calling an election on November 8, 2016 to authorize additional local property tax revenues for the general fund of the district and general obligation indebtedness and determine the ballot title and text of the ballot issue to be submitted at such election.

estimated time:  6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 
 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. The Board of Education received presentations on facilities needs and budget priorities throughout the spring in the development of both the Facilities Master Plan and the 2016/2017 Adopted Budget.
     
  2. Public comment and feedback on these topics was received by the Board of Education at public meetings on April 7, 21 (from the Capital Asset Advisory Committee), May 5, June 2 and 16; and, at 14 community forums/tele-town halls held throughout the school district in May and June.
     
  3. Through the development of the 2016/2017 Adopted Budget and the negotiations process with district employees, the Board of Education knows of general operational needs unmet by the level of state funding expected for Jeffco Public Schools in the immediate and future years. Board members believe taking Jeffco Schools’ funding future ‘into our own hands’ with a bond and a mill levy is essential to Jeffco students maintaining an edge while state funding dwindles due to the impact of the negative factor. Jeffco Public Schools has not received approximately $490 million which otherwise would have gone to educate and support Jeffco students since the implementation of the negative factor in 2009.
     
  4. The district’s $2.7 billion real estate portfolio, with schools at a median age of 45 years, has not had a significant investment of capital since 2004 (in 2012 voters approved a minimal $99 million to ensure a ‘warm, safe and dry’ learning environment). From the early 1990s, Jeffco has been successful at passing capital improvement bond issues on a four-year cycle. Those bonds addressed the growth and capital improvement needs of the school district. The failure, in 2008, of the bond issue has left a funding gap in the four-year development and maintenance cycle for school facilities. The district currently has in excess of $800 million in building deficiencies and life cycle needs.
     
  5. Mill levy funds will address classroom needs for student mental health supports and interventions to increase school security, competitive compensation to attract and retain quality educators, and school needs to meet individualized student learning objectives for success in college and the workforce.
     
  6. On July 28, the Board of Education directed the superintendent to prepare ballot language for Board action on August 2 for a $33 million mill levy override and a $535 million bond for the November 8, 2016 general election in Jefferson and Broomfield counties.
     
  7. The last mill levy override and bond issue supported by Jefferson County voters occurred in 2012.
