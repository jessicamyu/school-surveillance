2.02 ASBO Meritorious and GFOA Distinguished Budget Awards - Financial Services
Meeting
Mar 19, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Information
The Board of Education is pleased to recognize the staff of the district's Financial Services Department. Jeffco Public Schools is honored to receive both the national and international budget awards.

The first recognition is for achievement of the Meritorious Budget Award for excellence in the preparation and issuance of its school system budget for the 2008-2009 annual budget from the Association of School Business Officials (ASBO) International. This award represents a significant achievement reflecting a commitment to meeting the highest standards of school budgeting. In order to receive the award, Jeffco Public Schools had to satisfy specific guidelines for effective budget presentation recognized by school business officials throughout North America.

The district received the Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA) Distinguished Budget Presentation award. This recognition represents a commitment by the governing body and staff to an effective budget presentation as a policy document, financial plan, operations guide and communications device. Award recipients have pioneered efforts to improve the quality of budgeting for governments. The GFOA's Distinguished Budget Presentation awards program is the only national awards program in governmental budgeting.

Financial Services staff being recognized include: Lorie Gillis, Kay Bridges, T.J. McGrath, Marci Jasek, and Lorri Dugan.

3.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Mar 19, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
3. Public Agenda
Type
Information
At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.
