At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

7.01 Appealed Decision on Learning Resource Challenge (EL-11)
Meeting
Mar 25, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
7. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.On June 11, 2009, the Board of Education affirmed the superintendent’s decision to remove the library book It, by Stephen King, from middle school library shelves.
2.On August 3, 2009, the same parent challenged this resource, It, by Stephen King, at the high school level as a library book. Following district policy, KEC, Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources, the Division of Instruction began the process to convene a Challenge Review Committee.
3.Between August 3, 2009 and December 16, 2009, several meetings were held between administrators and/or the appellant to review procedures and questions since this challenge was initiated at the district level rather than at the school level.
4.On December 16, 2009, a Challenge Review Committee was convened. The purpose of this meeting was to provide both an overview of the process and copies of the book for each member to read. As the book is quite lengthy, committee members were given an extended period of time to read the book.
5.On February 3, 2010, a Challenge Hearing was held in accordance with district regulation, KEC-R, Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources, to address the high school level challenge.
6.Mary Beth Bazzanella, library services coordinator, served as chairperson for the Challenge Review Committee. On February 4, 2010, Ms. Bazzanella submitted the committee’s recommendations to Superintendent, Dr. Cynthia Stevenson.
7.On March 11, 2010, the Superintendent shared her decision by letter with committee members, the appellant, and school staff.
8.In accordance with district policy KEC, the Superintendent’s decision was appealed to the Board of Education on March 12, 2010.
9.On March 25, the appellant will have 10 minutes to present his position to the Board of Education, as will district administration, with a decision to be rendered by the Board on April 29, 2010.
Please open the links provided to review the superintendent's decision or the committee recommendation. To review the appellant's letter to the Board, please contact the Board office, 303-982-6801, board@jeffco.k12.co.us.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Item A and C.
    1. Jeffco Public Schools received $473,923 from the Colorado Department of Education’s Plan to Alternatively Compensate Teachers (ACT) grant. The program aligns with strategic plan business and finance objectives 1, 3 and 4, community objectives 1 and 2, instruction objectives 1, 2, and 3, leadership objectives 1, 2, and 3, and schools objectives 1, 2, 3 and 4.

    2. The grant will allow Jeffco Public Schools to continue work on designing a compensation system for teachers that meet new state and federal guidelines for recommended compensation changes that are aligned with student learning measures and evaluations. The goals of this project are to increase teacher effectiveness through the use of alternative compensation for student learning, teacher learning and teacher leadership that will increase student achievement, lower drop-out rates, help close identified achievement gaps and increase the number of post secondary degrees and certificates awarded to Colorado students. The funds will be used to hire a project manager and administrative support, as well as provide additional earnings for certified staff to participate in focus groups and on Internal Review Panels. The major activities of the project will be 1) design an alternative compensation system with all stakeholders, 2) develop a communications plan to provide information and get feedback on the program with a goal of implementing a pilot program in fall of 2011, 3) develop a cost analysis of the program for sustainability, and 4) evaluate the program.
    3. We will hire recognized experts to help create an evaluation method that uses measurable outcomes and focuses on the goals of the project.
    4. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.
    5. Warren Blair, Hackberry Hill Elementary principal, is the compensation steering committee project manager.
