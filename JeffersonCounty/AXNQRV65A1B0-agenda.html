PRESENTING STAFF:

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

Jef Fugita, executive director, Curriculum & Instruction

Scott Bain, principal, Jeffco Open School

Michael James, principal, Jefferson High School

Wesley Paxton, principal, Conifer High School

Andy Geise, principal, Pomona High School

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on the process for meeting the state graduation guidelines.

 

BACKGROUND:

Board Ends 2 states, "every school and the district will ensure that every student has the opportunity to work towards being connected to career, college and/or life aspirations, with the expectation that:

  • Every student will demonstrate a year or more of growth on their way to mastery of Colorado Academic Standards (Content Mastery competency).
  • Every student will be able to apply and transfer learning across disciplines and real world contexts (Critical and Creative Thinking) competency).
  • Every student will have the opportunity and expectation to demonstrate leadership attributes.
  • Every student will communicate effectively in a variety of formats and situations (Communication competency).

In September 2015 the Colorado State Board of Education approved the Colorado graduation guidelines.  Each district is to ensure that the local graduation requirements meet or exceed the Colorado graduation guidelines.

The Board of Education annually reviews a draft Board of Education meeting schedule for the next school year prior to formal approval.

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to continue its budget discussion of May 3 in order to provide final direction for adoption of the 2018/19 Budget on June 7.

 

BACKGROUND:

On May 3, staff presented an overview of the 2018/2019 Proposed Budget.
 

Staff presented information on the district's budget at the Board meetings of November 16, February 1, March 1 and April 5 that included a financial update on the current year; revenue assumptions per the state's fiscal outlook and estimated funding for Jeffco Public Schools; expenditure assumptions; and, budget priorities as identified by school accountability committees through the District Accountability Committee, Cabinet, staff and district leadership.
 

Input on budget priorities from the community, district leadership and other stakeholders was shared with the Board of Education at the March 5 and April 5 Board meetings.
