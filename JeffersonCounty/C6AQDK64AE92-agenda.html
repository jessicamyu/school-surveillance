2.02 Current State of Career Pathways (CTE, work-based learning, college opportunities) and Draft Resolutions (EL-11)
Meeting
Oct 14, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session 4:50 pm
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 4:50 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

Marna Messer, director, Choice Programs

Heather Keeton, principal, Warren Tech

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will receive an update on current programming and future aspirations of Jeffco Public Schools' career pathways programming.  Staff leading this effort will present information on the wide variety of programming available for our students at all of our secondary schools, including Warren Tech campuses. We will explore the connections between career pathways, college, and industry. Future aspirations to ensure career readiness for all Jeffco Public Schools students will also be shared as part of this presentation.  

 

Following the presentation and dialogue, board members will have time to discuss a draft resolution for career pathways expansion. Board Director Brad Rupert will lead this board discussion and share work that has been completed to date. Staff will be available to answer questions.  

 

BACKGROUND:


In 2018, Jefferson County voters passed the 5A mill levy. A portion on these funds were identified to support CTE programming in our schools. Since that time, we have expanded CTE programming across our district, including all of our middle schools and high schools, to allow more students access to CTE programming, work-based learning, and college opportunities.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Due to COVID safety and the number of presenters and honorees this evening, audience seating is limited to 50 attendees and public comment will be taken virtually via Zoom (no in-person public comment will be offered). Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education  (the form will be open 7 am Sunday, closed 5:30 pm Wednesday).  
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

 

Additional directions for participating via Zoom for public comment will be emailed to public comment participants before the meeting.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for October 2021.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The Colorado Department of Health Care Policy & Financing (HCPF) and the Colorado Department of Education (CDE) reimbursed Health Services $6,025,482 in Medicaid funding for the 2021/21 school year. HCPF reimburses school districts with the federal share of Medicaid dollars for providing Individual Education Plan (IEP) services to Medicaid eligible students. In turn, the district uses the funds to enhance health services for all students. Covered costs include salaries and benefits for staff, vision and hearing screenings, transportation, immunizations, professional development, and outreach and enrollment. As a reimbursement program, the 2021/22 allocation is calculated from previous year expenses. Services support Readiness for Learning, Schools as Community Hubs. School Medicaid Program Coordinator Michael McCoy from Health Services oversees the program.
  2. The district received a CDE Empowering Action for School Improvement (EASI) continuation award of $577,852. The Cohort 3 (year three) and Cohort 4 (year two) state and federal funds include initial and expansion funds for the District Designed and Led Improvement Strategies, participation in CDE’s Turnaround Network for Emory Elementary and Swanson Elementary, and exploration support for Great Work Montessori. Chief of Schools - Elementary Renee Nicothodes directs the project.
  3. The CDE awarded $229,816 to the Office of Student Engagement for Expelled and At-Risk Student Services (EARSS) in year four of a four-year grant. Funds help promote Restorative Practices as specialists engage in side-by-side coaching of school staff, as well as providing individualized support for attendance and discipline concerns. The initiative aligns with Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning, Social Emotional Supports. Student Engagement Director Dave Kollar oversees the services.
  4. Gifted & Talented (GT) received $90,357 through CDE’s Universal Screening and Qualified Personnel grant for the 2021/2022 school year. Funds pay for universal screening for gifted identification, programming and partially offset the cost of the GT director’s salary. GT Director Roger Dowd supervises the program. 
  5. New America School received a $150,000 Local Accountability System Grant from CDE to continue the development of an accountability system that supplements state measures. Finding additional indicators may lead to a more comprehensive understanding of success. The exploration of supplemental measures include non-academic student outcomes such as engagement, attitudes, and dispositions toward learning. Participating schools include Brady Exploration and nine other schools around the state. Principal Jon Berninzoni oversees the project.
  6. The Gill Foundation awarded a fifth grant, via Jeffco Schools Foundation, for STEM labs in the North Arvada articulation area. The $118,750 award will support a SMART Lab at Arvada K-8. The project addresses the Conditions for Learning Tactic: Commitment to Equity through a focus on increasing underrepresented students pursuing STEM pathways. STEM Pathway Designer Heather Waldron and Choice Programs Director Marna Messer manage the project.
  7. The Colorado Restaurant Foundation, in partnership with the Colorado Department of Labor and Employment, awarded $10,000 to Warren Tech South. Funds will support restaurant site visits allowing students to understand and practice front and back of house operations, proper meal coursing and dining etiquette. Resort & Event Management Instructor, Laura Seymour, will administer the funds with Associate Principal Eric Stout.

7.15 Districtwide Trash & Recycling Services (EL-7)
Meeting
Oct 14, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to execute a one-year renewal agreement with Republic Services, Inc. DBA Allied Waste Transportation, Inc. for Districtwide Trash & Recycling Services, with an estimated annual expense of $550,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board Executive Limitation policy EL-7 Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000.”
     
  2. District trash and recycling services support academic achievement for our students by fostering a solid-waste free environment through trash collection and disposal, while educating students about the positive impact recycling has on protecting the environment and saving energy.
     
  3. On June 29, 2018, Request for Proposal 24750, Districtwide Trash & Recycling Services, was posted on BidNet Direct to more than 1,457 companies.
     
  4. Republic Services, Inc. DBA Allied Waste Transportation, Inc. was awarded a contract not to exceed $475,000.
     
  5. The contract included language allowing for the initial contract term with the potential to renew the contract for four additional one-year periods.
     
  6. Republic Services, Inc. DBA Allied Waste Transportation, Inc. has successfully fulfilled its third year commitment to the district.
     

  7. As the district’s need for service has increased due to the addition of several schools needing three not two days/week trash pick-up (especially in mountain schools due to wildlife), Republic Services, Inc. DBA Allied Waste Transportation, Inc. requested a 4% increase to its renewal agreement, increasing this year’s estimated annual spend to $550,000.
     

  8. Due to the open market for these services, going out to bid could result in an increase of 7-12% over the district’s current agreement with Republic Services, Inc. DBA Alllied Waste Transportation, Inc. The Chief Operating Officer and Purchasing department determined it best to proceed without a re-bidding process.

Subject
8.01 District Unified Improvement Plan, School Priority Improvement and Turnaround School Unified Improvement Plans (UIP) (Ends 2-3)
Meeting
Oct 14, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
8. Ends Monitoring
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the District's Unified Improvement Plan (UIP) and the UIP's for the 11 schools in Jeffco Public Schools with School Performance Framework ratings of "Priority Improvement" or "Turnaround" for submission to the Colorado Department of Education (CDE) to be posted on CDE's website, School-View.org.
Goals
Ends 3 - Student Achievement
Ends 2 - Student Achievement

estimated time: 7:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) requires all public school districts and schools to complete a Unified Improvement Plan (UIP). These improvement plans are designed to identify priority challenges and develop major improvement strategies to meet these needs. While Jeffco is a high performing school district that consistently meets state accreditation expectations, the CDE expects the district's UIP (DUIP) to focus on areas for improvement. The Board of Education discussed the DUIP on September 9, 2021.
     
  2. Within an expectation of continuous improvement, all schools in Jeffco complete an annual UIP that focuses on priority challenge areas for improvement.
     
  3. Schools with the state’s defined School Performance Framework ratings of ‘Priority Improvement’ or ‘Turnaround’ have completed a comprehensive UIP to focus on system improvements. These identified schools include Arvada High School, Arvada K-8, Brady Exploration School, Connections Learning Center, Edgewater Elementary, Emory Elementary, Great Work Montessori, Jefferson Junior/Senior High School, Moore Middle School, Swanson Elementary, and Welchester Elementary. School leaders, with their support team, review CDE feedback on their UIP's and revise the UIP's as appropriate.
     
  4. The Board of Education will dialogue with the district’s Turnaround Support Team and representative principals for the 11 schools in ‘Priority Improvement,’ ‘Turnaround’ or ESSA identified status to learn of the ongoing efforts, supports in place, and the challenges faced by school communities to meet the state’s desired outcome through the UIP process. The Turnaround Support Team will outline strategies used to support school improvement, and progress monitoring of the schools state or federally so-identified.
     
  5. The district has worked closely with the District Accountability Committee (DAC) to develop the 2021-22 District Unified Improvement Plan. Members of the DAC DUIP subcommittee include: Jill Fellman, vice co-chair, Lorri Avery, vice co-chair, Matt Flores, Carol Eaton, Eun Jung Decker, JoAnn Euler, Vanessa Hoy, Elizabeth Kantner, and Maegen Tracy.
     
  6. The improvement planning process included a comprehensive analysis of district level data, the root cause analysis to focus on system improvements, and development of an action plan aligned to the priority student performance challenges.
     
  7. Throughout the school year, Jeffco's District Accountability Committee will continue to partner with the district to provide input and feedback on the implementation of the district's Unified Improvement Plan.
     
  8. After being approved by the Board of Education, the district will submit the District Unified Improvement Plan and the UIP's for Arvada High School, Arvada K-8, Brady Exploration School, Connections Learning Center, Edgewater Elementary, Emory Elementary, Great Work Montessori, Jefferson Junior/Senior High School, Moore Middle School, Swanson Elementary, and Welchester Elementary to the Colorado Department of Education by October 15, 2021.

Information

estimated time: 10 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)
 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Due to COVID safety and the number of presenters and honorees this evening, audience seating is limited to 50 attendees and public comment will be taken virtually via Zoom (no in-person public comment will be offered). Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education  (the form will be open 7 am Sunday, closed 5:30 pm Wednesday).  

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

Additional directions for participating via Zoom for public comment will be emailed to public comment participants before the meeting.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

 

 

Hora estimada: 10 p.m. (duración estimada: hasta 1 hora)

 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de inquietud de la comunidad en dos secciones de Comentarios del Público en su agenda. Debido a la seguridad contra COVID y al número de presentadores y homenajeados esta tarde, los asientos de la audiencia están limitados a 50 asistentes y los comentarios públicos se comunicarán de modo virtual a través de Zoom (no se ofrecerá ningún comentario público en persona). Inscríbase aquí para hablar con la Junta Directiva de Educación (el formulario estará abierto a las 7 a.m. del domingo y se cerrará a las 5:30 p.m. del miércoles).

 

En este momento, en la segunda parte del Comentario Público, la Junta Directiva escucha las preocupaciones sobre temas no relacionados con los temas de la agenda de la reunión de esta tarde. Los miembros o el personal de la Junta Directiva responderán durante esta reunión o posteriormente. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que no haya aplausos y que todos se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente o miembro del personal del distrito que haya completado la información solicitada en la lista de comentarios públicos puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por persona. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público.

 

Si esta tarde hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. De esta manera, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro o más personas que desean dirigirse a la Junta Directiva en el mismo lado del mismo tema, la Junta Directiva entonces asignará 10 minutos para que el punto de vista de dicho grupo sea escuchado. Todos los que se han inscrito deben acompañar al orador al frente de la sala de la Junta Directiva. Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema.

Instrucciones adicionales para participar a través de Zoom en cuanto a los comentarios públicos se enviarán por correo electrónico a los participantes con comentarios públicos antes de la reunión.

La Junta Directiva escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.
