Meeting
Apr 29, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A, B and C.
    1. The Colorado Division of Vocational Rehabilitation has awarded Jefferson County School District’s School to Work Alliance Program (SWAP) with 16th year funding in the amount of $353,491.24. SWAP is a program designed to work with youth with disabilities, ages 16-25. The plan aligns with strategic plan instruction objective 3, which ensures students have access to alternative pathways and interventions to improve achievement, close achievement gaps, and increase graduation rates.
    2. The Division of Vocational Rehabilitation (DVR) funds cover salary and operating expenses. The district provides office space, equipment, and in-kind services from special educators.
    3. The funds are used to (1) increase employment opportunities for this group of youth, (2) enhance employability of participants, and (3) involve participants in their community in a meaningful way. The strategies of this project include helping students develop employability skills, find appropriate, entry-level career path employment, provide students with job development and placement, and conduct one-year follow-up services after employment.
    4. The success of the SWAP program is measured by how many youth are employed under DVR guidelines.
    5. Donna Brovsky, assistant director, Special Education, Department of Diverse Learners, is the program manager.
    6. Please open the attachment below to review additional background material.

2.Workshops were held with key district stakeholders to get input for current and future Disaster Recovery (DR) needs and to deliver a DR framework and contingency plan. The workshops applied industry best practices and standards balancing cost vs. benefit and real threat vs. sensationalism. The goal was to ensure adequate recovery of district data to continue the business of the district’s mission critical services and systems for staff and students.
3.Several sites were explored for serving as the district’s secondary data center and it was ultimately determined that 809 Quail would the best site due to its proximity and cost savings.
4.The costs for the Disaster Recovery project would be approximately $2.2M which would include disk storage and services, contracted services, site construction, and procurement of additional network equipment.

PERTINENT FACTS:

5.The district will receive additional interest savings the sooner the bus COP's are paid off. The next payoff date is June 15, 2010. Notification is required by May 15, 2010 to ensure completion of the transaction.
