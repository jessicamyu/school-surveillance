2.02 Jeffco Online Enrollment Project: EnrollJeffco (EL-11)
Meeting
Nov 7, 2018 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 9:50 a.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kristopher Schuh, chief of schools secondary

Shannon Fitzgerald, enrollment consultant

Michele DeAndrea-Austin, principal, Bell Middle School

Josh Shapiro, principal, Foothills Elementary

Tammy Weatherly, director, Student Data Privacy & Reporting

Diana Wilson, executive director, Communications Services

 

PURPOSE:

To provide the Board of Education with information on the implementation of Jeffco’s online enrollment project, EnrollJeffco.

 

BACKGROUND:

At the Board of Education meeting on November 16, 2017, Board members discussed a variety of challenges Jeffco currently faces with regard to multiple application and enrollment processes, the vast majority of which are paper-based. Board members concluded that solutions must be identified and implemented. Since that time, Superintendent Dr. Glass has facilitated numerous conversations with a diverse group of stakeholders and summoned together a steering committee comprised of cross-functional district leaders to spearhead this work. Steering committee members have identified a set of solutions to implement in the 2018/19 school year and have contracted with an enrollment consultant to lead the initiative. In support of the district’s Commitment to Equity and Meaningful Parent & Community Engagement, the Rose Community Foundation and the Gates Family Foundation have contributed additional funding for the implementation of these new tools. At the Board of Education meeting on September 6, 2018, a contract with Acumen for a new enrollment system was approved.

2.05 Budget Development Status Update (EL-5)
Meeting
Nov 7, 2018 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session

Goals
EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting

estimated time: Noon (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:
Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Nicole Stewart, director-budget and treasury


PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive an update on the budget process, timeline and community engagement plan and to provide initial direction for development of the 2019/2020 budget. 

 

BACKGROUND:

The governor's proposed budget was released in early November. Funding estimates through the School Finance Act are based on estimated October enrollment counts. The potential for new funds is pending the outcome of several ballot issues, and staff is proposing two timeline scenarios based on whether the district will receive any new funding outside the potential funding per the Governor's proposed budget. Staff continues to work with schools and district leadership on funding requests to support the district's strategic plan through the processes for school Student Based Budgeting (SBB) and department Budgeting for Outcomes (BFO) processes. 

 

The Financial Oversight Committee reviewed the SBB and BFO budgeting models and concur that these methods support the strategic mission of the district and ensure solid fiscal management per the recommendations in the attached reports.
