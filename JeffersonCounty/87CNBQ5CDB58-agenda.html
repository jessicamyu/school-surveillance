Subject
3.02 Voice Communication Infrastructure, Disaster Recovery Projects
Meeting
Apr 22, 2010 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session, 5 p.m.
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Information
Estimated Time: 8 p.m.
Location: 4th floor, Seminar Room, 1829 Denver West Drive, Bldg. 27, Golden


PRESENTING STAFF:
Cindy Stevenson, superintendent
Lorie Gillis, chief financial officer
Brett Miller, executive director of Information Technology


PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive and review information on the Voice Communication Infrastructure and Disaster Recovery projects pending Board approval on April 29, 2010.

BACKGROUND:

The current phone system was purchased in 1994 and subsequent software and hardware upgrades have extended the life of the system. Life expectancy of the current system is 18-21 years. Future support, however, will require refurbished parts and reliability and availability of this legacy system will be a challenge. Similar systems are encountering unreliable service and unexpected outages. This project would build the infrastructure that simplifies and integrates all forms of communications to better serve students and staff.

The primary data center located at the Education Center is currently at 78 percent electrical capacity. Workshops were held with key district stakeholders to get comprehensive input for current and future disaster recovery needs and to deliver a framework and a contingency plan. This project would ensure adequate recovery of district data to continue the business of the district’s mission critical services and systems for staff and student.

Additional background information, timelines and costs associated with the projects will be provided to the Board of Education. The presentation is available here. The Consent Agenda executive summaries are here: disaster recovery and infrastructure project.
