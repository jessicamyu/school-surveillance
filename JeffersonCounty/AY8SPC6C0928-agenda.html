The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.     
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

7.07 Resolution: Authorizing the Use of CSIP for Investment of Bond Redemption Funds (EL-7)
Meeting
Sep 6, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to adopt the resolution authorizing the use of Colorado Statewide Investment Pool (CSIP) for investment of bond redemption funds.
Goals
EL-7, Asset Protection

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, "the superintendent shall not allow assets of the district to be unprotected, inadequately maintained, inappropriately used or unnecessarily risked; and accordingly, may not invest funds in securities where contrary to state law;" and, in compliance with district policy DFA/DFAA, Revenues from Investments/Use of Surplus Funds.
     
  2. In Colorado, there are three authorized local government pools, CSafe - Colorado Surplus Asset Fund, ColoTrust - Colorado Local Government Investment Trust, and CSIP - Colorado Statewide Investment Pool. All three of these government pools are in full compliance with state statute and district investment policy, DFA.
     
  3. Of the three local government pools, CSIP is the only pool authorized to use a Liquid and Term Portfolio strategy allowing districts and municipalities to take advantage of longer term investment instruments. The use of a Term Portfolio is well suited for bond redemption funds because the payment schedule is defined allowing for defined fixed-term investment opportunities.
     
  4. At this time, Douglas County School District, Cherry Creek School District and Denver Public Schools have monies invested in the CSIP pool. 
     
  5. Jeffco Public Schools currently invests most of district operating cash with CSafe and Insight Investment and have had previous experience with ColoTrust.
     
  6. Staff is recommending approval of the use of CSIP as a strategic investment for the district's bond redemption fund in order to utilize the Liquid and Term portfolio options to maximize investment earnings. 
     
  7. The Financial Oversight Committee has reviewed and discussed the investment differentiation offered by CSIP at their meeting on August 7, 2018. The FOC recommendation for approval of the resolution authorizing use of CSIP is attached below.

    • A cumbersome application process for families (i.e., driving to multiple schools to submit multiple paper applications), resulting in inequities of access;
    • Drain on front office productivity;
    • Lack of consistency between schools and their processes;
    • No system-wide transparency (i.e., no one can explain why any given student gets into a school or does not);
    • Lack of visibility for school leaders to know their enrollment numbers early enough to plan and staff;
    • Lack of accurate data about school and programmatic demand that would allow Jeffco leaders to make data-driven decisions about offerings;
    • No source of information to discover school options and offerings, requiring families to conduct their own research.
       
  3. At the June 7, 2018 meeting, the Board of Education approved funding for a tool to address these enrollment process issues.
     
  4. The district will be implementing two new tools for families in preparation for the 2019/20 enrollment cycle. The first is an online school finder that will inform families of their neighborhood school, as well as allow for a dynamic search function that will highlight various schools and programs that might best fit the needs of their child(ren). The second is an online application and enrollment system that will allow families the ability to apply to multiple Jeffco schools, manage their child’s applications, monitor waitlist statuses, and accept or decline offers, all from one user-friendly site.
     
  5. A formal Request for Proposal (RFP) was issued this spring and Acumen Solutions was selected through a competitive process which included formal proposals, vendor demonstrations, and reference checks. Members of the Information Technology, Gifted and Talented, and Purchasing departments, school-based staff, and parents participated in the process. Based on complete evaluations, the evaluation committee recommended to award Acumen Solutions, which has the capability to deliver a world-class online school finder tool and online application/enrollment system in time for the 2019/20 enrollment cycle.
     
  6. Acumen Solutions offers Jeffco Public Schools the opportunity to build a school finder and enrollment system to meet the needs of Jefferson county families, including interfaces that make their experience intuitive, seamless, and transparent. Both tools will be translated into multiple languages and be available via web and smartphone. Acumen will work with Jeffco staff to integrate with our existing systems to ensure data privacy and maximize efficiency.
     
  7. The district’s strategic tactics of Commitment to Equity and Meaningful Parent & Community Engagement (in alignment with Jeffco Generations Strategic Plan) is at the forefront of this initiative and the desire to provide meaningful information and tools to all families, regardless of where they live, their income, or language spoken. Additionally, the idea of utilizing ‘Technology to Transform Learning’ aligns well with these new tools, as Jeffco will be exemplifying the use of technology to simplify processes and enable data-driven decision making at the highest level.
     
  8. In support of the district’s Commitment to Equity and Meaningful Parent & Community Engagement, the Rose Community Foundation ($100,000) and the Gates Family Foundation ($40,000) have contributed additional funding for the implementation of these new tools.
  9. The first year cost for these tools is $552,960. This cost includes the school finder implementation, enrollment system implementation, training, and support. For year 2 through year 5, the annual ongoing cost will be funded by the approved $320,000 ongoing budget. The annual ongoing cost includes license fees ($30,800), support ($40,000), and any additional customization the district requires in Jeffco Public Schools (i.e., preschool enrollment, SAE, etc.).

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. This agenda item is brought to the Board of Education in accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board; and governance process policy, GP-4, Board Job Description.
     
  2. Through the development of the 2018/19 Adopted Budget and the negotiations process with district employees, the Board of Education knows of general operational needs unmet by the level of state funding expected for Jeffco Public Schools in the immediate and future years. Board members believe taking Jeffco Public Schools’ funding future ‘into our own hands’ with a bond and a mill levy is essential to Jeffco students maintaining an edge while state funding dwindles due to the impact of the budget stabilization factor. Jeffco Public Schools has not received approximately $703 million which otherwise would have gone to educate and support Jeffco students since the implementation of the negative factor in 2009.
     
  3. The district’s $2.7 billion real estate portfolio, with schools at a median age of nearly 50 years, has not had a significant investment of capital since 2004 (in 2012 voters approved a minimal $99 million to ensure a ‘warm, safe and dry’ learning environment). From the early 1990's, Jeffco Public Schools has been successful at passing capital improvement bond issues on a four-year cycle. Those bonds addressed the growth and capital improvement needs of the school district. The failure, in 2008 and 2016, of the bond issue has left a funding gap in the four-year development and maintenance cycle for school facilities. The district currently has in excess of $1.2 billion in building deficiencies and life cycle needs.
     
  4. Mill levy funds will address classroom needs for student mental health supports and interventions to increase school security, competitive compensation to attract and retain quality educators, and school needs to meet individualized student learning objectives for success in college and the workforce.
     
  5. As part of the development of the 2018/19 budget, the Board of Education (BOE) discussed educational funding, capital needs and budget priorities throughout the spring. With adoption of the 2018/19 budget on June 7, Board members discussed continued needs in Jeffco Public Schools that were not able to be addressed even with a state funding boost through the budget stabilization factor (aka negative factor).
     
  6. The Board of Education discussed the value and impact of educational funding on January 11, March 5 and June 11, 2018 and adopted resolutions of support (on February 1 and on April 5) for statewide efforts to improve the level of public education funding across Colorado to ensure all students receive an education to meet their potential regardless of zip code.
     
  7. Principals addressed the Board on March 5 regarding facilities needs to ensure an ideal learning environment for students at schools.
     
  8. On April 5, the Capital Asset Advisory Committee (CAAC) presented the 2018 Capital Improvement Plan (CIP) and provided input to the Board on capital needs. The CAAC was established in 2010 for citizen oversight of facility conditions, long range facility planning and oversight of capital programs. The CIP addresses the condition of our schools related to future readiness, parity, programmatic needs, growth areas/reinvestment in established areas, and specialties (technology and safety/security).
     
  9. Funding options and community input on the direction of the school district were discussed with the Board on June 11 and August 8.
     
  10. On August 8, the Board directed the superintendent to prepare a recommendation on a mill levy override question and a bond issue question with options for Board discussion and action at the August 23 special meeting.
     
  11. On August 23, the Board of Education directed the superintendent to prepare ballot language for Board action on September 6 for a $33 million mill levy override question and a $567 million bond issue question for the November 6, 2018, general election in Jefferson and Broomfield counties.
     
  12. Public comment and feedback on these topics was received by the Board of Education at public meetings on December 7, January 11, February 1, March 1 (including priorities from the District Accountability Committee), May 3, June 7, and August 23.
     
  13. The last mill levy override and bond issue supported by Jefferson County voters occurred in 2012.
