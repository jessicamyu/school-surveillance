Subject
2.02 Update: Start Time Task Force (EL-11)
Meeting
Apr 14, 2021 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information
Goals
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 9 a.m.  (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dan Cohan, interim chief of schools secondary, School Leadership

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools elementary, School Leadership

Katie Winner, parent, co-chair Start Time Task Force

Laura Johnson, parent, co-chair Start Time Task Force


PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on and provide direction for next steps on the work of the Start Time Task Force.
 

BACKGROUND:

Following the work completed at the direction of the Board of Education after the February 5, 2020 Reimagining Time presentation, leaders of the Start Time Task Force requested further direction from the Board. Start Time Task Force members were invited to share an update, including the executive summary report from School Bus Logistics, the consultant hired to review Jeffco Public Schools start times and transportation options in 2019.


The efforts of the Start Time Task Force continue to be focused on later start times for middle and high school students.


The Start Time Task Force was commissioned by the superintendent in February 2018 and meets approximately every two months. Most recently the task force was part of the “Reimagining Time” presentation to the BOE on February, 5, 2020. Before this the task force had been researching secondary school start times and presented to the BOE on February 7, 2019.


The Board of Education directed the hire in 2019 of an implementation firm/consultant (School Bus Logistics) to create a plan for adjusting school start times so middle schools start on or after 8 a.m. and high schools start on or after 8:30 a.m. This action was one of the Start Time Task Force recommendations.


As a result of the February 5, 2020 presentation, the BOE asked the Start Times Task Force and the district to further engage School Bus Logistics to explore more cost-effective options for starting school later for middle and high school students.
 

Board members were provided a written update in January with the following requests:


Requests of Task Force co-chairs:

  • Jeffco Public Schools composes and sends the survey to gather community feedback on the August 2020 School Bus Logistics Report.
  • Present a final report to the Interim Superintendent and Board of Education that has been subject to feedback and surveying from the Jeffco community.
  • Meet with the Board in January/February 2021 to present final recommendations after community feedback is gathered in the event that the Board wishes to discuss a potential Fall 2021 implementation. The Task Force co-chairs are open to a March/April 2021 meeting pending the results of the community survey.

 

Requests of District Cabinet:

  • Budget office analysis of costs and estimates provided in the consultant’s executive summary.
  • No district-wide implementation of a change to start times for Fall 2021 due to the many uncertainties being managed from COVID-19 and the $54 million in potential budget reductions for the 2021/22 school year.
  • Schedule a Board study session discussion involving the Task Force co-chairs in March/April 2021 once all data is collected and provided to the Board for guidance on next steps.

2.05 Public Comment on Advisement and Staff Decision on Allendale Elementary School (EL-11)

Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Information

estimated time: 11:15 a.m.  (estimated duration: 30 minutes)

 

 

To ensure that community members have an opportunity to communicate with the board, the public comment portion of the Wednesday, April 14, 2021 special meeting will allow up to 30 minutes of public comment on the advisement and staff decision on Allendale Elementary.

 

Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available beginning 10 am Saturday, closes 5 p.m. Tuesday)  
 

At this time, the Board listens to comments related to the Advisement and Staff Decision on Allendale for up to 30 minutes.  You may submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 and fewer than 30 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board. Over 30 individuals will have 1 minute and groups will have 3 minutes to address the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment on the advisement and staff decision on Allendale for up to 30 minutes if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kristopher Schuh, interim superintendent

Marcia Anker, interim superintendent deputy

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools - elementary

Brenda Carlson, community superintendent

Lee Cooper, community superintendent

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will be advised of the decision and rationale regarding the future of Allendale Elementary.

 

BACKGROUND:

Our district vision calls for us to “deliver engaging learning experiences for all students.” In order to do this, four conditions for learning must be in place and sustained over time. Those conditions are:

  • a commitment to equity
  • high expectations
  • a professional model of teaching
  • educator learning

These conditions allow students equitable opportunities; access to high level, standards aligned instruction; highly qualified teachers; and staff that are knowledgeable and prepared to deliver current best practices in instruction. The decrease in enrollment at Allendale over the past five years has limited our ability to deliver on these promises for the students and staff at Allendale.

2.08 Appointment of Superintendent, Approval of Contract and Transition of Leadership (B/SL 1-4)
Meeting
Apr 14, 2021 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To appoint Ms. Tracy Dorland as superintendent of schools effective April 19, 2021; and, to approve the contract of employment with Ms. Dorland as presented.

estimated time: 1 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)
 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. On July 30, the Board of Education shared its intention to begin a national search for a superintendent which includes the process of identifying and gathering information about qualified candidates. The search process is summarized in C.R.S. §24-6-402(3.5), and includes deadlines for applications, requirements for applicants, selection procedures, and the time frame for making a selection. August 11, 2020, the Board announced an interim superintendent. A superintendent search process typically begins no later than January to take full advantage of a national candidate pool.
     
  2. On November 5, David Bell, chief human resource officer, reviewed with the Board of Education the process underway to meet required deadlines.
     
  3. On December 9, Mr. Bell, reviewed with the Board of Education the Request for Qualifications and Informal Quote (RFQ) for hiring a superintendent search firm, and selection of a single search firm for the winter and spring search. A district search team selected Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates as the firm with which to contract on management of the superintendent search.
     
  4. On December 16, Mr. Bell and Hank Gmitro and Lisa Flores, representatives of Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates, began the process of defining the parameters for the superintendent search, including community engagement planning, community survey planning, search calendar.
     
  5. On January 7, Mr. Bell, Mr. Gmitro and Ms. Flores continued discussion of key decisions related to the superintendent search parameters and other related needs of the Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates consultants.
     
  6. In February, the Board finalized the Leadership Profile report and Desired Characteristics summary developed through Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates’ gathering of feedback from community engagement sessions and online survey responses. Applicants were accepted through the deadline of February 15, 2021.
     
  7. At the meetings of March 9-11, 16, 22, 23, 2021, the Board of Education narrowed its search and on March 25, 2021, the Board made public the name of the sole finalist, Tracy Dorland, fourteen days prior to her appointment or employment.
