PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. On July 30, the Board of Education shared its intention to begin a national search for a superintendent which includes the process of identifying and gathering information about qualified candidates. The search process is summarized in C.R.S. §24-6-402(3.5), and includes deadlines for applications, requirements for applicants, selection procedures, and the time frame for making a selection. August 11, 2020, the Board announced an interim superintendent. A superintendent search process typically begins no later than January to take full advantage of a national candidate pool.
     
  2. On November 5, David Bell, chief human resource officer, reviewed with the Board of Education the process underway to meet required deadlines.
     
  3. On December 9, Mr. Bell, reviewed with the Board of Education the Request for Qualifications and Informal Quote (RFQ) for hiring a superintendent search firm, and selection of a single search firm for the winter and spring search. A district search team selected Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates as the firm with which to contract on management of the superintendent search.
     
  4. On December 16, Mr. Bell and Hank Gmitro and Lisa Flores, representatives of Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates, began the process of defining the parameters for the superintendent search, including community engagement planning, community survey planning, search calendar.
     
  5. On January 7, Mr. Bell, Mr. Gmitro and Ms. Flores continued discussion of key decisions related to the superintendent search parameters and other related needs of the Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates consultants.
     
  6. In February, the Board finalized the Leadership Profile report and Desired Characteristics summary developed through Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates’ gathering of feedback from community engagement sessions and online survey responses. Applicants were accepted through the deadline of February 15, 2021.
     
  7. At the meetings of March 9-11, 16, 22, 23, 2021, the Board of Education narrowed its search. The law requires that the finalist(s) be made public fourteen days prior to appointing or employing one of the finalists.
