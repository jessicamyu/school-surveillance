PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting, “Financial planning for any fiscal year shall not deviate materially from the Board’s Ends policies, risk fiscal jeopardy or fail to be derived from a multi-year plan.”
     
  2. State law requires the local Board of Education to adopt and appropriate a budget for all district funds prior to the beginning of the fiscal year Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 22-44-103(1). After adoption of the budget, the Board may review and change the budget, with respect to both revenues and expenditures, at any time prior to January 31 of the following year C.R.S. 22-44-110(5).
     
  3. The 2021/2022 Budget for the July 1, 2021, through June 30, 2022, fiscal year was adopted by the Board of Education on June 10, 2021 and revised on September 2, 2021. 
     
  4. The September 2, 2021 revisions to the 2021/2022 Adopted Budget adjusted for compensation changes per the final negotiated agreement with the JESPA including an additional estimate of $3.2 million to increase the minimum hourly wage to $15 per hour.
  5. On November 10, 2021 staff presented updated revenue and expense figures that impact the 2021/2022 Revised Budget. 
  6. Pursuant to C.R.S. 22-44-103 and 22-44-107, school districts must pass a resolution to adopt and appropriate the budget and per C.R.S. 22-44-105(1.5) must also pass a resolution authorizing the use of a portion of the beginning fund balances in the district's budget.
  7. The recommendation is to approve both the resolution for adoption and appropriation of the fiscal year 2021/2022 Revised Budget and the resolution authorizing the use of a portion of the beginning fund balance as presented.
