PRESENTING STAFF:

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

Carol Eaton, Ph.D., executive director, Instructional Data Services


PRESENTING GUESTS:
Jim Earley, chair, District Accountability Ccommittee (DAC)
Deb Guiducci, chair, DUIP subcommittee, DAC

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive the District Accountability Committee (DAC) recommendation and information on the 2017-2018 District Unified Improvement Plan, which will be submitted to the Colorado Department of Education (CDE).

 

BACKGROUND:

Board Ends 3 states, "Every school and the district will have effective learning systems and shared leadership that:

  • Utilize continuous improvement processes that incorporate problem-solving approaches to reduce or eliminate root causes of student performance challenges."

The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) requires all public school districts and schools to complete an annual Unified Improvement Plan (UIP). These improvement plans are designed to identify priority challenges and develop major improvement strategies to meet these needs.  The improvement planning process includes a comprehensive analysis of district level data, the root cause analysis to focus on system improvements and development of an action plan aligned to the priority student performance challenges.

The DAC reviewed information over the last several months to reach the recommendation on February 20 to present to the Board of Education.  Members of the DUIP subcommittee include Lori Avery, Thomas Dougherty, Jill Fellman, Hi Howard, Margaret Lessenger, Melissa Monoski and Susan Vermeer Lopez.

6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Mar 1, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:40 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Recommended Action
to approve the request for a limited waiver of district policy to allow for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages at a charity race event to benefit Two Angels Foundation on May 20, 2018, at the North Area Athletic Complex Stadium.
Goals
EL-7, Asset Protection

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, “the superintendent shall not allow assets of the district to be unprotected, inadequately maintained, inappropriately used or unnecessarily risked…."
     
  2. Right Start Events manages a charity 5K run/walk race for the non-profit Two Angels Foundation that was founded by Dawn and Roger Mohatt in memory of their daughters who were both diagnosed with a rare form of muscular dystrophy that ultimately took their lives. The foundation helps families with the financial burden of acquiring adaptive equipment for home and school thus facilitating a more normal life for special needs children.
     

  3. The event, in its 12th year and formerly held in Westminster, is a fundraiser for the Foundation that includes a 5K run/walk followed by a 50-yard Adaptive Dash where children will use adaptive equipment to walk, roll or ride to the finish line. The event starts at 9 a.m. and ends at 11:30 a.m. with an estimated 250 participants.
     

  4. For the past several years, the organization has received donated beer that has been sold to raise funds at the event. They would like to continue offering beer at the event.
     

  5. To allow for such activity on district property, district administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policies GBEC, Drug Free Workplace and KF-R, Community Use of School Facilities with respect to the prohibited use and possession of alcoholic beverages.
     

  6. Use of the North Area Athletic Complex (NAAC) Stadium and surrounding Jeffco facilities has been requested and approved through the Jeffco Facility Use Permit process.
     

  7. The event is contingent upon Right Start Events management acquiring all necessary permits and licenses from City of Arvada and appropriate insurance per district risk management. A copy of approved permits will be posted during the event.
     

  8. Approval of the limited waiver of these district policies at this time will allow the Right Start Events to finalize details of the event and to begin promoting this event to the community. 
     

  9. As per district policy KF, Jeffco Public Schools, events such as this promote community and business partnerships with stakeholders by making district buildings and facilities available to the community when not in use for school activities. The use of the NAAC Stadium and granting of a limited waiver to district policy allows for a potential annual community event that celebrates and promotes community spirit and citizen engagement.
     
  10. The Board of Education has approved similar limited waiver of district policy to allow for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages on district property 12 times since 2009.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education. 

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
