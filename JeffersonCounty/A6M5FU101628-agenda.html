2.01 Community Engagement: JCBEA (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 18, 2016 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 5:30 p.m.  (estimated duration: 30 minutes)

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dan McMinimee, superintendent

Joni Inman, executive director, Jefferson County Business Education Alliance (JCBEA)

Sean Kaiser, chairman, Jefferson County Business Education Alliance (JCBEA)

Scott Fast, lead member, JCBEA Employer Roundtable

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to meet members of the JCBEA and to learn of the outcomes and recommendations from the business community that resulted from a series of Employer Roundtable discussions around needed career ready skills.

 

BACKGROUND:

The Jefferson County Business Education Alliance is a non-profit 501(c)(3) organization led by a board of directors that includes both members of the business community as well as educators.  Its purpose is to ensure that students have skills and opportunities that lead to successful employability, no matter their career aspirations, and that employers have access to a well educated and well trained workforce in order to grow and prosper.

 

The JCBEA's cornerstone program provides a Career Ready Workshop at no cost to students in Jefferson County high schools.  Students do not have to have a minimum grade point average (GPA) to attend, nor do they have to be on a specific career pathway. The workshop sessions are all taught by members of the business community who volunteer their time to give students exposure to a variety of professionals and real-world examples of workplace expectations.

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive information specific to teacher compensation to provide guidance for employee negotiations.
 

BACKGROUND:

Under Colorado Law, school board discussions regarding employee contracts must be conducted in open session (except under specific circumstances). This session will provide Board members with information on teacher compensation issues. JCEA will provide information, followed by the District team. Following that, the Board will engage in a discussion about teacher compensation focused on providing direction to the District negotiating process.
