Subject
1.04 Contract Award: Oracle Hyperion Budget & Asset Lifecycle Management Modules (EL-8)
Meeting
Feb 25, 2010 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session, 5 p.m.
Category
1. Preliminary
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.The district has a master agreement with Oracle that allows the district to negotiate directly with Oracle rather than resellers on the purchase of Oracle products, services and maintenance.
2.The district would like to purchase Hyperion Workforce Planning and Hyperion Planning Plus which would replace the current manual budgeting applications. Asset Lifecycle Management (ALM), ALM Portal Pack, and PeopleSoft Enterprise Inventory Applications are new applications and will supplement and consolidate various asset tracking methods into a single asset repository.
3.Program licenses and 12 months of technical support have been negotiated as a part of the Oracle proposal for the Applications.
4.Funding for the combined Hyperion Workforce Planning Applications and the Asset Lifecycle Management applications has been planned for in the Information Technology Operating Budget. The cost, including licensing and 12 months of technical support/maintenance, is estimated to not exceed $575,240.28.

Action
Time: 6 p.m.

PERTINENT FACTS:
1.For the past two years, the Jeffco Public Schools Board of Education has supported the efforts of the Colorado school finance litigation, known as Lobato v. State of Colorado.
2.In October 2009, the Colorado Supreme Court remanded the case to district court. School districts are being asked by the Colorado Association of School Boards (CASB) and the Colorado Association of School Executives (CASE) to help pay legal expenses as the litigation continues through its next level of action.
3.Success with this litigation could lead to positive changes in how Colorado schools are funded to meet constitutionally and legislatively mandated levels of student achievement.

Cindy Stevenson, superintendent
Ed Bowditch, contract lobbyist, Tomlinson & Associates

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to discuss pending legislation and to provide direction to the contract lobbyist for the 2010 legislative session.

BACKGROUND:
Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, requires that the Board's positions on pending legislation and regulations be communicated to members of the Colorado General Assembly and the Executive Branch of state government. It has been the Board's practice to schedule special meetings to discuss pending legislation during the legislative session.

The 2010 legislative session will produce a number of bills of interest to Jefferson County Public Schools. The district contract lobbyist will bring legislation to the Board of Education for positions and direction. The district’s contract lobbyist provides state legislators with the Board’s positions on legislation.
