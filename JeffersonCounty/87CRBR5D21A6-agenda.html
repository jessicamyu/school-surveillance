At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

7.01 District Calendar for 2009-2010 (EL-11)
Meeting
Oct 16, 2008 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
7. Discussion Agenda
Type
Discussion
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.In accord with Board executive limitation policy, EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, “…the superintendent may not fail to advise the Board in a timely manner of relevant trends, facts and information, including significant organizational changes… provide to the Board the results of districtwide surveys and polls… notify the Board of the resolution of significant grievances and complaints.”
2.As outlined in district policy, IC/ICA, School Year/School Calendar, “The standard school calendar for the district shall be prepared by the superintendent and/or designee and staff within the Board limitations. The number of hours and days scheduled for students shall meet or exceed the requirements of state law. From the standard school calendar, individual schools shall schedule a minimum of 175 days for students.”
3.After working through the Spring, the Calendar Committee presented a three-year calendar in May 2008 to the Board of Education and superintendent. Community members and staff subsequently commented on the new three-year district calendar as well as individual school calendars for 2008-2009.
