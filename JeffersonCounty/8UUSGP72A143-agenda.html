3.02 Teacher Compensation
Meeting
Jun 14, 2012 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 5:04 pm (estimated duration: 1 hour)

PRESENTING STAFF:

Cindy Stevenson, superintendent

Pati Montgomery, principal on special assignment

Heather Beck, chief academic officer

Allen Taggart, executive director, Employee Relations and Legal Counsel

Lisa Elliott, executive director, Jefferson County Education Association (JCEA)
Amy Weber, executive director, Human Resources

Stephanie Rossi, high school teacher

PURPOSE:
The Board of Education will receive an update on the Strategic Compensation Project, a presentation on the research that exists on variables that appear to make a difference in student achievement, a presentation on the new Memorandum of Understanding, and a view from an administrator and from a teacher about alternative compensation.

 

BACKGROUND:
The Board of Education, district leadership, and JCEA have all expressed an interest in collaborating to develop new ways to pay teachers in Jeffco.  On June 7, 2012 the Board of Education voted to accept the Memorandum of Understanding from the negotiations process that outlines the parameters for this collaborative work.

 

Jeffco leadership and JCEA have worked together for the past three years to study different ways of compensating teachers.  The years of collaboration resulted in a grant application to the Federal Teacher Incentive Fund.  In September 2011, Jeffco was awarded approximately $39 million to implement a new “Strategic Compensation” system.

 

The Strategic Compensation project includes action research on teacher effectiveness, teacher compensation, staff development, and evaluation.  Two research projects are being conducted:  one to assess the impacts of money and one to assess the impacts of teacher training, evaluation, and support for teachers.

3.03 Decision on Appeal of Superintendent's Decision: Cut (EL-1)
Meeting
Jun 14, 2012 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to affirm, reverse or amend the superintendent's decision that Cut by Patricia McCormick remain on the middle school library shelves.

estimated time: 6:04 pm (estimated duration: 25 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with district policy KEC, Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources, appeals concerning learning resources may be challenged by any Jefferson County citizen, student, parent or guardian of district student, or district employee.  Challenges are heard by a committee of district parents, teachers and administrators, which submits a recommendation to the superintendent for his/her decision.  The superintendent will forward a copy of the decision to members of the Board of Education.
     
  2. On October 12, 2011, a parent challenged the resource Cut by Patricia McCormick at the middle school level as a library book.  Following district policy, the Division of Instruction began the process to convene a challenge review committee.
     
  3. On March 6, 2012, a challenge review committee was convened.  The purpose of this meeting was to provide both an overview of the process and copies of the book for each member to read. 
     
  4. On April 4, 2012, a Challenge Hearing was held in accordance with district regulation KEC-R, Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources, to address the middle school level challenge.
     
  5. Mary Beth Bazzanella, Library Services coordinator, served as chairperson for the Challenge Review Committee.  On April 12, 2012, Ms. Bazzanella submitted the committee’s recommendations to Superintendent, Dr. Cynthia Stevenson.
     
  6. On April 25, 2012, the superintendent shared her decision by letter with committee members, the appellant, and school staff.
     
  7. In accordance with policy, the superintendent’s decision was appealed to the Board of Education on April 30, 2012.
     
  8. On June 7, 2012, the Board of Education reviewed the record and took the appeal of the superintendent's decision on Cut by Patricia McCormick under consideration to render a decision on June 14, 2012.
