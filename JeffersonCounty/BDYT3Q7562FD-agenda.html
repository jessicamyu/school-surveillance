PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will hear the appeal and ask questions of the superintendent’s decision regarding the enrollment pattern between Maple Grove Elementary and The Manning School.

 

BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education received an appeal of the superintendent’s decision related to the Manning School enrollment process and Maple Grove Elementary by email on May 24, 2019 from Maple Grove Elementary parents. The Board of Education replied on June 6, 2019 informing the Maple Grove Elementary parents an appeal hearing would be held in the fall during a study session and a decision to be rendered at the next regular business meeting.

On March 22, 2018, a community forum consisting of families from eight elementary schools and Manning, along with Manning staff members, met to review and discuss open enrollment procedures at Manning. The outcome of the meeting was sent to Superintendent Dr. Glass and as a result sibling priority was extended to outgoing 8th graders for the 2019/20 enrollment lottery.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) awarded a $2,000,000 Building Excellent Schools Today (BEST) grant for the construction of a fiber network built in collaboration with Jefferson County municipalities and anchor institutions (e.g., Colorado School of Mines). The district’s long-term goal is to be independent of commercial service, saving millions of dollars. To uphold Strategic Plan Technology Tactic: Infrastructure to Support Student Learning, the network capacity will sustain ongoing bandwidth increases for educational needs in schools. Chief Information Officer Brett Miller directs the project.
     
  2. The CDE distributed Reading to Ensure Academic Development (READ) Act Funds of $1,877,347. The allocation is based on the 2,936 K-3rd graders with reading deficiencies reported last spring. READ Act funds provide resources to support effective reading instruction and intervention. READ Act Assistant Director Kim Ballantyne from Curriculum and Instruction oversees the use of funds.
     
  3. The CDE allocated $2,031,351 as a formula-based grant per HB19-1055 to support the implementation of full-day kindergarten. Funds are for acquiring furniture, fixtures or equipment needed to conduct full-day kindergarten or preschool educational programs. Chief of Schools, Elementary Renee Nicothodes will direct the spending of these funds.
     
  4. The CDE released the district’s 2019/20 allocation for the Individuals with Disabilities Act (IDEA) – Part B of $14,717,162. Funds provide services to students with disabilities such as hearing, vision, speech or language impairment; emotional or intellectual disabilities; or autism spectrum disorder. An additional allocation of $344,068 is specifically for identifying, screening and serving preschool children. Services align with Strategic Plan objective Conditions for Learning, Commitment to Equity. Chief Student Success Officer Susan Leach oversees the program.
     
  5. The Colorado Department of Health Care Policy & Financing (HCPF) and the CDE reimbursed Health Services $3,062,650 in Medicaid funding for the 2019/20 school year. HCPF reimburses school districts for the federal share of Medicaid dollars for providing Individual Education Plan (IEP) services to Medicaid eligible students. In turn, the district uses the funds to enhance health services to all students. Covered costs include salaries and benefits for staff, vision and hearing screenings, transportation, immunizations, professional development and outreach and enrollment. As a reimbursement program, the 2019/20 allocation is based on expenses from the previous year. Services support Readiness for Learning, Schools as Community Hubs. School Medicaid Program Coordinator Kathryn Hetzel from Health Services oversees the program.
     
  6. Gifted & Talented (GT) received its 2019/20 allocation of $808,145 from the CDE. Funds cover professional development, identification of gifted students, development and support for advanced learning plans, direct services to students, consultants and coaching. Students have the option and choice to attend a GT Center school for intensive programming if they qualify with a body of evidence that suggests this is an appropriate learning environment. For students who do not choose to attend a GT Center school, an Advanced Learning Plan (ALP) is developed to bolster their area(s) of strength. Services target the Strategic Plan Objective Conditions for Learning, Commitment to Equity. GT Director Roger Dowd supervises the program.
     
  7. Gifted & Talented (GT) received $99,724 through CDE’s Universal Screening and Qualified Personnel grant for the 2019/20 school year. Funds pay for universal screening for gifted identification, programming and partially offset the cost of the GT director’s salary. GT Director Roger Dowd manages the screenings.   
     
  8. Student Services received a $50,000 CDE’s School Counselor Corps planning grant to begin a new cohort of participating schools. Funds will bring counseling support to four schools: Arvada K-8, Bear Creek K-8, Foster K-7 and Long View High School. In alignment with the Strategic Plan’s Conditions for Learning, High Expectations, services are designed to positively affect graduation rates and postsecondary matriculation in articulation areas. Director of Student Services Terry Walderman oversees the program.
     
  9. Student Services received a year 2 award of $240,000 from the CDE’s School Counselor Corps grant to serve Belmar, South Lakewood and Westgate Elementary Schools.
