2.01 Update on the Review of Academic Functions (EL-11)
Meeting
Nov 10, 2021 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 8 a.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Tracy Dorland, superintendent

Jaime Aquino, Ph.D., consultant

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to have a conversation with the superintendent about her work on the review of academic functions supporting our schools to provide a high quality education for our Jeffco students.

 

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel with the Board, this item is brought forward to the Board.

The quality of a school district is a key factor when families are looking for a place to live. Research has shown that quality public schools are the bedrock of a strong economy and democracy. Jeffco Public Schools has historically been committed to increasing a child’s chances of future success by providing an education that is relevant and meaningful to each student. Jeffco is proud of its students and staff for their achievements both inside and outside the classroom.

 

Superintendent Tracy Dorland began work as Jeffco’s superintendent April 19, 2021. Her key areas of focus for district work include:

  • Focus on relationships, collaboration, and a positive climate and culture with stakeholders in our district, schools, and communities.
  • Explore how our district is organized to support schools with improved student achievement and social emotional well-being.
  • Find the just-right balance between system coherence and school-based autonomy.
  • Maximize the efficiency of our resources, aligned to the goal of improved student achievement - particularly focused on closing achievement gaps.
  • Support students with additional learning opportunities to make up for lost instructional time due to the pandemic.

 

To begin her work in the 2021/22 school year, she hired Dr. Jaime Aquino as a consultant to work with her on a districtwide review of how our central supports are organized so that our schools have the resources needed to ensure an excellent education for every student and a top-quality work environment for every staff member.

 

This is the third monthly update on this work.

2.03 2018 Capital Improvement Mid-Program Assessment (EL-8, 11)
Meeting
Nov 10, 2021 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information, Procedural

estimated time: 10 a.m. (estimated duration: one hour)

 

PRESENTING:

Steve Bacchetti, director, Moss Adams, LLP

Tammy Lohr, manager, Moss Adams, LLP

Jordan Wahl, senior auditor, Moss Adams, LLP

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

Tim Reed, executive director, Facilities and Construction Management

Tammy Schiff, chief communications officer

Brenna Copeland, chief financial officer

Kathy Hodgson, member, Capital Asset Advisory Committee (CAAC)

Tom Murray, member, CAAC

Megan Castle, member, CAAC

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board to receive and discuss the results of the 2018 Capital Improvement Mid-Program Assessment.

 

BACKGROUND:

Superintendent Tracy Dorland, in her fourth month leading Jeffco Public Schools, committed to learn more about Jeffco’s six year $829M Capital Improvement Program, beginning with an external, chronological mid-cycle assessment of the program. The report’s goal is in support of continual improvement for systems and structures aligned to best practices that will inform the community and enhance confidence and trust in Jeffco Public Schools as the district enters the second half of its six-year Capital Improvement Program.

 

In June 2021, the district issued a Request for Proposal to prepare a mid-program assessment on the 2018 Capital Improvement Program. Moss Adams, LLP of Seattle, Washington was selected among several candidates to prepare the report.

 

Since July 2021, Moss Adams, LLP has conducted interviews, met with staff, received and reviewed documentation from the district in the preparation of the report. Representatives of Moss Adams, LLP will present the findings to the Board.

 

In fall of 2018, the district successfully passed a bond ballot measure providing the district with $567M to invest in its Capital Improvement Program. As of June 2021, approximately $461M had been invested or committed through near term contracts in school improvement projects.  

 

The $567M bond issue represents the largest portion of the estimated $829M Capital Improvement Program.  Other revenue sources include bond premium, accrued interest and six years of capital transfer from the general fund.

 

This information is shared in accordance with Board executive limitation policies E L-8, Facilities, the superintendent may not “permit capital dollars to be spent unnecessarily or unwisely…” and E L-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, “the superintendent may not fail to advise the Board in a timely manner of relevant trends, facts and information…”.
