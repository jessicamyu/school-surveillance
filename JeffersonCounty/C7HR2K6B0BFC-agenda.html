PRESENTING STAFF:

Dr. Susan Leach, chief student success officer

Jen Gallegos, director, Student Discipline

Dave Kollar, director, Student Engagement

John McDonald, executive director, Safety, Security and Emergency Management

Allison Meier, coordinator, Restorative Practices

Maryann Peratt, director, Judicial Services

Jeff Pierson, director, Safe School Environment

Terry Walderman, director, Student Services

Dr. Erin Sullivan, coordinator, Social Emotional Learning

Mishele Carroll, coordinator, Social Emotional Learning Integration

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will receive an update and dialogue on the current state of student discipline, restorative practices, safety and security, judicial services and the wide array of community partnerships that exist to support students, as well as an overview of behavior management within a multi-tiered system of supports.

 

BACKGROUND:

Jeffco Public Schools employs a broad continuum of interventions to create and maintain positive school climates across the district. Structured within a multi-tiered system of supports, interventions range from universal in nature (designed to support all students) to very targeted, and at times requiring supports that extend outside the district through collaboration with community partners.   

 

This work is subsidized through a variety of mechanisms, including the 5A mill levy funds, other grant funding, and general fund dollars.

Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education (opens 7 am Sunday, closes 5:30 pm Wednesday).   
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may address the Board. Public Comment will be taken virtually and in-person. For those who would like to address the Board of Education from home without wearing a mask, comments will be taken virtually via Zoom.  For those who are present in the audience, comments will be taken in-person and masks are required. Costume masks are not considered an adequate COVID-safe face covering (mask).

 

There is a three* minute time limit per individual speaker. *Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10* minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

Recommended Action
To adopt the resolution for Expanding Career Readiness in Jeffco Public Schools as presented.
Goals
Ends 3 - Student Achievement
Ends 2 - Student Achievement

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board,  ". . . the superintendent shall not fail to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed . . .", and GP-01, Governance Commitment, "Our community requires that the Board . . . set high standards and ensure that students can be successful in the next phase of their lives whether they go to college, attend technical school, join the military, begin an entry-level job, and/or serve as community members", the following resolution is brought forward."
     
  2. The Board of Education received a presentation on October 14 regarding career/technical education with an opportunity to discuss the proposed resolution. This board work and resolution is to provide more equitable outcomes for Jeffco Public Schools students.  Ensuring students participate in programming that results in achieving college credit, earning an industry recognized credential, and learning the skills to be successful in a future career is fundamental for student future success.
     
  3. Career and Technical Education programs have been proven to support student career success (Colorado CTE Fact Sheet 2021).
     

  4. According to the University of Denver College Evaluation and Action Lab report, Does Concurrent Enrollment Improve College Access, Success, Time-To-Degree and Earnings? A Quasi-Experimental Analysis of Colorado Students, students who participate in Concurrent Enrollment are more likely to complete postsecondary programs and earn higher wages after 5 years.
     

  5. Costs associated with Work-Based Learning:

    • Career Centers will require an ongoing cost of $2,000,000 (funded through a collaboration between industry, workforce and community partners, and Jeffco Public Schools) to support staffing, career exploration, internships and apprenticeships, and training for school-based staff;

    • Continued funding for Concurrent Enrollment opportunities within Career Pathways for students;

    • Continued use of Perkins funds to support the development of in-demand career pathways in Jeffco Public Schools.

To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for November 2021

PERTINENT FACTS:


In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.   

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) distributed Reading to Ensure Academic Development (READ) Act Funds of $1,905,816. The funds provide resources to support effective reading instruction and intervention to K-3rd grade students identified with reading deficiencies. Per pupil funding is $496 this year, as compared to last year’s $640 per pupil. READ Act Assistant Director Kim Ballantyne from Curriculum and Instruction oversees the initiative.
  2. The district will submit an Empowering Action for School Improvement (EASI) grant application to CDE for resources to support schools identified for improvement through the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) and the state’s Educational Accountability Act (HB 18-1355). Approximately $700,000 will be requested for five schools to join or continue participation in CDE’s Turnaround Network, and for training for principals and other school leaders in identified schools. Awards will be announced in January. Chief of Schools – Elementary, Renee Nicothodes supervises the program.
  3. The City of Lakewood awarded a Neighborhood Participation Program grant of $60,000 to Green Gables Elementary. Funds grant will help pay for the design and construction of a walking trail on the school grounds. Principal Suanne Hawley manages the project.
  4. The City of Lakewood awarded a Neighborhood Participation Program grant to Stober Elementary. The $50,240 will help fund a community garden and outdoor classroom. Principal Anne DiCola administers the project.
  5. Kaiser Foundation Health Plan of Colorado awarded a Thriving Schools grant of $24,500 to support physical activity kits for Title I schools, classroom movement kits, and materials for rejuvenation rooms. Healthy Schools Coordinators Kristina Kain and Fran Taffer direct the project.
