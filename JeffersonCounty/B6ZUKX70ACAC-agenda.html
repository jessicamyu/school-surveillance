4. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Subject
4.01 Board of Education Appreciation
Meeting
Jan 10, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

estimated time: 6 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 minutes) 
 

January is School Board Recognition Month, and mid-school year is a great time to honor the work of Jeffco Public Schools’ five hard-working school board members (Susan Harmon, Ali Lasell, Ron Mitchell, Brad Rupert, Amanda Stevens), sharing thanks for their contributions to our schools and community, and educating the public about the important role that a school board plays, not only in Jeffco Public Schools, but in Colorado, a local control state.

Jeffco’s current five member school board has been in place since 2015 when two members (Ali Lasell and Amanda Stevens) won election to two open seats and three members (Susan Harmon, Ron Mitchell and Brad Rupert) won seats through the successful recall of three school board members, mid-term. Ms. Harmon, Mr. Mitchell and Mr. Rupert have since won re-election in 2017 to four-year terms.
 

Colorado school board members are volunteers, carving time out of their work and family life to provide the community’s perspective on legal and leadership decisions affecting students in Jefferson County. Board member activities include visits to Jeffco’s 155 schools; participating in school celebrations of state championships, academic honors, and groundbreakings; speaking on behalf of the district at community town halls, parent meetings, service clubs, city council meetings, and other social engagements. All that plus preparing and participating in board meetings! As the second largest school district in Colorado with more than 86,000 students, school board members also take a leadership role statewide through the Colorado Association of School Boards, dialogue with state and federal legislators, and other school board members across the state in an effort to improve and promote the value of public education to the success of Colorado and our nation.
 

Most of all, school board members work with district staff to represent the community’s focus on strategic goals; to close opportunity gaps and ensure all students find engagement and success in each day of their learning in Jeffco Public Schools.

Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:22 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Meeting
Jan 10, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
8. Ends Discussion
Type
Discussion
Goals
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 7:50 p.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 

 

Pertinent Facts:

  1. In accordance with Board Executive Limitation 11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, this agenda item is to share with the Board a proposal from Dennison Elementary School and D’Evelyn Jr./Sr. High School with the intent to transition sixth graders from Dennison and to D’Evelyn over time.
  1. In 2017, the Board approved the transition of sixth graders from neighborhood elementary schools to middle schools.
  1. To align with this model, a task force made up of district, school and community leaders in support of the Dennison and D’Evelyn communities has worked with their respective school governance structures and parent communities to consider different options regarding the placement of sixth graders.
  1. Following multiple meetings with stakeholders, the task force wrote a proposal to transition sixth graders from Dennison Elementary to D’Evelyn Jr./Sr. High School over a period of two years.
  1. The proposal will change each school’s governing documents to reflect the grade changes: Dennison Elementary K-5 aligns with districtwide elementary schools; and, D’Evelyn Jr./Sr. High becomes the only 6-12 grade configuration in the district.
  1. Both a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to D’Evelyn’s Founding Document and a revision to the Dennison Governing Document are anticipated to be brought to the Board of Education on February 7 for action following tonight’s discussion.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education. (will be available 10 am Monday)

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
