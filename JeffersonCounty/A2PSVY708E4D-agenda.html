1.05 Recognition: JSF Parent Child Home Program
Meeting
Oct 15, 2015 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
1. Preliminary
Type
Recognition

estimated time: 5:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 3 minutes)

The Board of Education is pleased to recognize the efforts of community volunteers supporting the Jeffco Schools Foundation Parent-Child Home Program. Representatives of the PCHP Jeffco present for the recognition include former Lakewood Mayor Steve and Anne Burkholder, former Colorado State Senators Norma Anderson and Moe Keller; Robin Weikel, program coordinator; and, Denise Delgado, executive director of Jeffco Schools Foundation.

The PCHP Jeffco positively impacts school readiness and long-term learning for two- and three-year olds from high risk families in Jefferson County. Taking in a weekly gift of a new book or an educational toy, trained home visitors from the community model how to create a constant flow of words in the home. Words are key to literacy. The parent gains confidence to become the child's first teacher, and the connection sticks. PCHP Jeffco changes the life of the entire family.

Research has shown this two-year transformation has produced preschoolers ready to learn, prepared to read, and poised to succeed.  As the final year of this three-year pilot project begins, PCHP Jeffco continues to have a dedicated community advisory board, outstanding staff and enthusiastic families.

3.02 2015 ICAP Update (Ends 1-5)
Meeting
Oct 15, 2015 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 7:00 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Bryan Krause, director, Student Services/Security and Emergency Management

Jon Widmier, specialist, Student Services/Security and Emergency Management

 

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive an update on Jeffco's ICAP program.

BACKGROUND:

Individual Career and Academic Plans (ICAP) is a multi-year process that intentionally guides students and families in the exploration of career, academic and post-secondary opportunities. With the support of adults, students develop the awareness, knowledge, attitudes, and skills to create their own meaningful and pathways to be career and college ready. 

 

Since the release of ICAP legislation in winter 2009, students in Jeffco have been building ICAPs with the support and guidance of educators, industry partners and pre-collegiate groups. Beginning in the seventh grade, students have been learning how to discover interests, plan intentionally and build pathways to success for life after high school.
