PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to hear from staff on the districtwide financial condition through the third quarter of the 2019/2020 school year in order to monitor district finances.

 

BACKGROUND:

Each quarter, in compliance with Board executive limitation policies EL-5, Financial Planning and Budgeting, and EL-6, Financial Administration, and as outlined in Board governance process policy GP-13, Committee Structure, the Board meets to review and monitor quarterly financial reports. Staff and an independent auditor from CliftonLarsonAllen, LLP,  identify the funds, departments and/or schools to monitor closely throughout the year. Members of the Financial Oversight Committee attend quarterly financial updates for the opportunity to provide a report to and dialogue with the Board of Education.

2.02 Budget Development 2020/2021: Reduction Scenarios (EL-5, 6)
Meeting
May 13, 2020 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 9:00 a.m. (estimated duration: 2 hours)

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Nicole Stewart, director-budget and treasury

 

PURPOSE:

To continue discussion with the Board regarding development of the 2020/2021 budget and potential revenue adjustments, reductions and use of reserves. 

BACKGROUND:

Discussion of the 2020/21 proposed budget to date has covered revenue assumptions per the state's fiscal outlook; impacts and updates at the state and district level on funding estimates for Jeffco; revenue and expenditure assumptions; budget priorities and potential reductions and use of reserves as identified by Cabinet, district leadership and the community including recommendations for funding requests and potential reductions for development of the 2020/21 proposed budget.

 

Due to the financial climate as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic, the Board will continue discussions to address potential revenue reductions due to changes at the state and local level as well as examples of changes to district budget assumptions to ensure continued financial stability for the district. 

Budget updates were presented to the Board of Education at the September 5, October 10 and December 12, 2019, and the February 5, March 5, March 11, April 2 and April 8, April 29, and May 7, 2020, meetings.

2.03 Restart Plan Modeling (EL-11)
Meeting
May 13, 2020 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Information

estimated time: 11 a.m. (estimated duration: 2 hours) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kristopher Schuh, deputy superintendent/chief of schools secondary

Dr. Tom McDermott, chief strategy officer

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will receive a presentation outlining the process, planning, and feedback structures to develop a plan to engage students in learning to begin the fall semester. The presentation focuses on preparations in progress and potential models for the fall semester of the 2020-2021 school year following the remote learning model initiated due to the COVID-19 pandemic on March 13, 2020.

 

BACKGROUND:

Remote learning for students was implemented by Jeffco Public Schools beginning on March 17, 2020 in response to the Coronavirus COVID-19 pandemic. On April 3, 2020 Jeffco Public Schools and 14 Denver-Metro School Districts decided to close schools for in-person instruction for the remainder of the school year. On April 22, 2020 Governor Polis issued an executive order closing all Colorado Public Schools to in-person instruction for the remainder of the school year.
