estimated time: 9:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Brett Miller, chief information officer

Chris Paschke, executive director, Data Security

 

PURPOSE:

To provide an update to the Board of Education on data privacy in the district and receive direction from the Board on the priorities for the Technology and Data Privacy Advisory Committee (TDPAC) for the 2018/19 school year.

 

BACKGROUND:

The Information Security department has been protecting district data and providing oversight for the past 12 years. The Technology and Data Privacy Advisory Committee (TDPAC) is a 23-member Board of Education advisory committee consisting of eleven community members and twelve Jeffco central staff members. The committee was created to advise the Board of Education on district technology strategies, systems and overall data governance and encourages continuous improvement of, and fosters adherence to, district policies, procedures and practices at all levels.

PURPOSE:

To provide the Board of Education with information on the 2017/18 application process, the innovations selected through that process, and recommendations for the Innovation Acceleration Fund in the future.

 

BACKGROUND:

On January 11, 2018 the Board of Education approved $1 million to fund the Jeffco Innovation Acceleration Fund. Following that approval, staff facilitated an application process which resulted in the March 2018 identification and funding of 10 innovative ideas from Jeffco staff.

 

The Board of Education will hear a report on the processes, outcomes, lessons-learned and recommended next steps for the Jeffco Innovation Acceleration Fund including comments from recipient innovators in this first cycle.
