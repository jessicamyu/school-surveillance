Meeting
Jan 7, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
1. Study/Dialogue Session 5 pm
Type
Information

estimated time: 5:30 p.m. (estimated duration:20 minutes)
 

PRESENTING:

Stephanie Schooley, secretary, Board of Education

Helen Neal, chief of staff for Board and Superintendent

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive and review changes to the board correspondence process.

 

BACKGROUND:

Over the past several years, there has been a notable shift in emails sent directly to board member email addresses and they have responded directly to constituents. The superintendent and chief of staff support this direct constituent communication. The Board President and Board Secretary have reviewed and contributed to the new process plan.

 

Key points of change:

  • Board members respond directly to their emails
  • Correspondence is no longer logged and published
  • Board Secretary reports at the monthly regular meeting on topics received in the past month (Public Correspondence agenda item)
  • Board member emails will be highlighted as contact point for constituents and board@jeffco.k12.co.us will be available as needed and responded to as necessary (i.e., student issues directed to community superintendent and/or school principal).
  • New system begins January 2021.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.    
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for January 2021

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. Jefferson County awarded $1,782,423 to the district from their CARES COVID Relief funds. Of the award, $1,420,700 is for district PPE expenses and $234,000 goes to Food & Nutrition Services. Two charter schools received portions of the award for COVID related expenses - Mountain Phoenix ($40,433) and Rocky Mountain Academy of Evergreen ($87,292). Grants Accounting Manager Joshua Shoemaker will oversee the awards.
  2. Colorado Department of Education (CDE) Safe Schools Reopening Grants were awarded to the district and five charter schools. Of the $650,201 award, the district received $491,861 for PPE and related supplies. Charter school awards include Rocky Mountain Academy of Evergreen ($59,401), Collegiate Academy ($41,405), Mountain Phoenix ($21,367), Montessori Peaks ($19,467), Great Work Montessori ($10,000) and New America Schools ($6,700). Grants Accounting Manager Joshua Shoemaker administers the funds.
  3. Round two of Child Care Sustainability Grants were awarded to 14 school-based licensed child care sites (including charters) from the Colorado Department of Human Services. Grants totaling $45,000, and averaging $3,125 per site, will cover costs such as salaries and safety/cleaning supplies. Jeffco School Age Enrichment Coordination Cathy Lytle, McClain Community High School Child Care Director Holly Davidson and charter preschool directors manage the funds.
  4. CDE’s Remote Learning for Charters funds were awarded to Doral Academy ($35,000), Great Work Montessori ($34,919), Jefferson Academy ($54,164) and Rocky Mountain Academy of Evergreen ($35,000). These one-time grants will cover costs of resources for remote learning such as hardware and software, professional development and materials for student emotional support. Charter school leaders will oversee each award.
  5. Food and Nutrition Services received $50,000 through CDE’s USDA Equipment Grant. Funds will purchase a walk-in freezer for Thomson Elementary School. Food and Nutrition Services Facilities/Equipment Manager Jerry Jones leads the project.
  6. City of Lakewood, via Jeffco Schools Foundation, awarded $15,000 of CARES Act resources to Emory, Lasley and Molholm Elementary schools. Each school will receive $5,000 for site-based food pantries. School principals will manage the grants.

Recommended Action
to authorize the director of purchasing to execute the first renewal on the second year of a five year contract with Hospital Shared Services Inc (HSS) for school video surveillance equipment, installation and licensing for an estimated cost of $3 million over the possible five year life of the contract.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with the following Board executive limitation policies this agenda item is brought forward for action:
  •  EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000…;”
  •  EL-8, Facilities, “…facilities shall not be unsafe, inadequate or unnecessary…;” and, in compliance with district regulation FBC-R, Prioritization of Facility Improvements, Group 2A-Security, "...projects that decrease security risks and enhance overall safety of a building or site, e.g. increase visibility, physical or technological improvements."
  1. In September 2019, the Purchasing Department, in conjunction with the Information Technology (IT) and Facilities departments, issued Request for Proposal (RFP)# 25247 for Avigilon Cameras and services. Five vendors responded to the RFP and the Intent to Award this RFP went to Hospital Shared Services, Inc. (HSS) This will be the second year of the contract with three 1-year optional renewals remaining for a total contract life not to exceed five years.
     
  2. Jeffco Public Schools’ priority to increase school safety is strengthened through this work to enable schools and security personnel better visibility and record of activities at school sites, historical data and evidence of incidents, if needed. Avigilon Cameras and services enhance building and site security. All schools in Jeffco Public Schools have at least one camera for video surveillance through a longstanding (8-10 year) program. The work under this contract will provide upgrades to video surveillance software, license purchases for sites, video surveillance camera purchases, installation of new cameras at sites, and repair of existing broken, damaged or non-functioning cameras. Cameras may be added at school locations as needed when incidents arise or schools have a need for a record of activities in areas of the building and within budgeted resources.
     
  3. Funding for the Video Management and Security Camera program is provided by the general fund, capital transfer, and 2018 bond proceeds. To date, from the estimated $3 million contract with HSS we have spent $86,000 from the general fund and $467,550 has been spent from the bond.

Recommended Action
to amend the award of contract to Hord Coplan Macht in the amount of $173,250 for a total of $1,165,000 for the Marshdale Elementary School Replacement project; and, to authorize the executive director, Facilities and Construction Management, to execute the contract documents.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000.”
  2. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-8, Facilities, “…facilities shall not be unsafe, inadequate or unnecessary.”
  3. The contract award is for consultant services necessary for the design of the Marshdale ES replacement and includes associated site development, demolition and utility infrastructure.
  4. September 23, 2020 a purchase order in the amount of $173,250 was issued to Hord Coplan Macht for Design Advisory Group (DAG) and Schematic Design consultant services.
  5. On October 27, 2020, the Board approved a fee proposal of $991,750 from Hord Coplan Macht for the balance of consulting services. The DAG and Schematic Design services’ fee was inadvertently not included on that agenda item. The revised contract amount is $1,165,000.
  6. Funding for the work is the 2018 Capital Improvement Program. This is a one-time capital investment without ongoing costs. Hord Coplan Macht is a Denver based company.
  7. The project work needs to begin as soon as the contract is fully executed with project completion scheduled for August 2022.
