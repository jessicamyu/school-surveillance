0.02 Public Engagement: Citizens Without Children in School (Ends 1), 6 p.m.
Meeting
Sep 11, 2008 - Special Meeting - Study/Dialogue Session, 5 p.m., Seminar Room
Category
0. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion
PRESENTING STAFF:
None

PURPOSE:
To discuss the Board’s Ends 1 policy, “All students explore and develop their potential as productive global citizens in a 21st century environment,“ as it’s goal for the students of Jefferson County Public Schools and to receive feedback from constituents on this goal.


BACKGROUND:
As the elected representatives of the Jeffco community for public kindergarten – 12th grade education, the Board schedules community engagement sessions to review the direction of the district with various constituent groups. After revising it’s Ends policies in the Spring of 2008, the Board of Education determined groups of Jeffco citizens with which to discuss the Board’s long-term goals for the district and its students.

The following questions will begin the discussion:
• What skills will students need for jobs we don’t know will exist?
• Why should someone be a life-long learner? Who can we tell if someone is a continual learner?
• What does a global perspective mean to you? Why is it important to have a global perspective?
• What else should Board members be asking that they aren’t?

0. Study/Dialogue Session
Type

PRESENTING STAFF:
Cindy Stevenson, superintendent
Allen Taggart, legal counsel
Helen Neal, manager

PURPOSE:
To discuss the process the Board will follow in filling the vacancy for the Board seat in director district 5.


BACKGROUND:
The Board declared a vacancy in director district 5 on August 21 with a deadline of September 3 for eligible applicants to apply to fill the Board seat. By law, the Board has 60 days from the declared vacancy date of August 21, 2008 to appoint a resident of Jefferson County’s school board director district 5. The Board will interview applicants on September 18 prior to the regular business meeting.

On August 21, the Board requested this time to discuss how interviews would be conducted, what questions would be asked of applicants and who would be interviewed. Legal counsel is present to respond to any questions and remind Board members of the legal requirements to appointment, if necessary.

The following individuals applied to fill the Board seat (their letter of application/resume are available through the links): Jamie Adler Rodriguez, Larry Eigel, Mary Everson, Tina Francone, Spencer Larkin, Charles Marquez, Paula Noonan, Alonzo Rodriguez, Richard Rush, and Susan Settanni.
