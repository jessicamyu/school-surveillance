PRESENTING STAFF:

Karen Quanbeck, chief of schools elementary (DAC)
Kristopher Schuh, chief of schools secondary (DAC)

Helen Neal, chief of staff, Board of Education and Superintendent (DAC)

Brett Miller, chief information officer (TDPAC)
Tim Reed, executive director, Facilities Planning (CAAC)

Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer (FOC)

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to review the membership recommendations and applications for openings in four Board advisory committees: Capital Asset Advisory Committee (CAAC), District Accountability Committee (DAC), Financial Oversight Committee (FOC) and Technology Data Privacy Advisory Committee (TDPAC) in order to make appointments.

 

BACKGROUND:

The role of Board of Education advisory committees is outlined in Board governance process policies GP-12, Board Committee Principles, and GP-13, Committee Structure.

 

Four committees generated vacancies in the 2018/19 school year. In order to be prepared for committee work beginning in August of the 2019/20 school year, an online application process was available and advertised this Spring.

 

Board members received membership applications in May for review prior to the Board’s June 6 discussion and appointments. Each applicant was invited to meet with individual board members on May 29. 

There is one vacancy open in the TDPAC, two appointments for the FOC and CAAC, and 23 appointments to make to the DAC (four of which are direct Board appointments).

The Board of Education is pleased to honor the advisory board and staff of Jeffco’s Parent Child Home Program for their work over the past six years, in partnership with funding through the Jeffco Schools Foundation and Jeffco's Early Childhood Education department, connecting young families and learners with literacy resources to support school readiness at home. Present for the recognition include PCHP Advisory Board Members Anne Burkholder, former Lakewood Mayor Steve Burkholder, former Board of Education member Hereford Percy, and Senator Norma Anderson and PCHP staff Amy Carlton, Andrea Hernandez, Yolanda Luna, Celia Mendoza and Suzie Reichert.


The success of the Parent Child Home Program is shown through extensive scientific research that has consistently found that this model significantly improves parent-child interactions, increases school readiness and later school success, and reduces risks of child abuse and neglect.

All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business.
