2.01 Classroom Dashboard System
Meeting
Aug 22, 2013 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 5:00 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 hours 15 minutes) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dr. Cynthia Stevenson, Superintendent

Panel of Invited Guests including,

 

Colorado:  Jill Hawley, associate commissioner of Achievement and Strategy; and, Tony Dyl, senior assistant attorney general

EPIC:  Kaliah Barnes, administrative law counsel

Parent Concerns:  Rachael Stickland and Barmak Nassirian, director of federal relations and policy analysis, American Association of State Colleges and Universities

inBloom:  Sharren Bates, chief product officer; and, Reg Leichty, partner, EducationCounsel, Nelson Mullins Riley and Scarborough

Data Management Advisory Council:  Marty Reames

District Perspective:  Matt Cormier, executive director, Educational Research and Design; and, David Millard, 5th grade teacher, Weber Elementary School

 

PURPOSE:
To provide an overview of Jeffco’s plans, the concerns that have been raised, the role of the state, information about inBloom, and the work of the Data Management Advisory Council which was formed to study the issues and make recommendations to Jeffco.  In the first hour, the panel will provide a variety of perspectives on the work of Jeffco Schools and on inBloom.
 

BACKGROUND:

Jeffco continues to be a leader in innovation to increase student achievement.  Our community understands that to succeed in today’s global economy, students need learning experiences that meet their individual needs, engage them deeply and let them learn at their own pace. This requires teachers to have an up-to-date picture of a student’s progress; an understanding of where he or she needs extra attention; and, access to materials that will support their students’ learning.
 

Jeffco is creating a classroom dashboard system for teachers that transforms student academic data into information for teachers that will allow them to more easily tailor education to the developmental needs, skill levels and learning pace of each individual student and to engage parents more deeply in the learning process.  This classroom dashboard also has the potential to save teacher time and school funding.  The system will also allow Jeffco to house student information more safely and securely than it would be otherwise.
 

The plan to use inBloom, a 501c3, as the “middleware” in the classroom dashboard system has resulted in questions related to student privacy and data security.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. The Board of Education is required by statute, C.R.S. 22-31-125, to declare a vacancy for the director seat when the duly elected director has submitted his or her written resignation. 
     
  2. On August 12, Robin Johnson submitted her written resignation, effective August 12, 2013, to the Jefferson County Board of Education due to her family moving out of director district 1.
     
  3. C.R.S. 22-31-129(2) requires the Board of Education to appoint a person to fill the vacancy within 60 days after the vacancy has occurred, which is October 10, 2013.
     
  4. The Board will accept applications from eligible Jefferson County residents before 4 p.m., Friday, August 30, 2013 in the Board office as outlined in the attachment below.
     
  5. The Board will discuss applicants and appoint a replacement at a special meeting on Thursday, September 12 prior to the Strategic Planning and Advisory Council (SPAC) meeting.  The oath of office will take place Thursday, September 19 at 6 p.m.
