PURPOSE:  

In accordance with Board Executive Limitations Policy 11, "...the superintendent shall not fail to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed and supported.  Board Ends 3 states, "Every school and the district will have effective learning systems and shared leadership that:
 

  • Utilize continuous improvement processes that incorporate problem-solving approaches to reduce or eliminate root causes of student performance challenges.
     

The Board of Education will receive the District Accountability Committee, DAC, recommendation and information on the 2022-23 District Unified Improvement Plan, which will be submitted to the Colorado Department of Education (CDE).

 

BACKGROUND:

The Colorado Department of Education, CDE, requires all public school districts and schools complete a Unified Improvement Plan, UIP. These improvement plans are designed to identify priority challenges and develop major improvement strategies to meet these needs.  The district has worked closely with the District Accountability Committee (DAC) to develop the 2022-23 District Unified Improvement Plan. 

 

The improvement planning process included a comprehensive analysis of district level data, the root cause analysis to focus on system improvements, and development of an action plan. The district's UIP aligns with the Jeffco Thrives 2025 strategies, Culture of Academic Excellence and Extraordinary Student Experience.

PURPOSE:

To provide the Board with an update on Phase 1 of Regional Opportunities for Thriving Schools in order to be well informed for the November 10, 2022 vote.  In accordance with Board Executive Limitations Policy 11, "...the superintendent shall not fail to give the Board as much information as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed and supported".  

 

BACKGROUND:

Jeffco has capacity to serve 96,000 students in its traditional district-managed schools and we currently only serve 69,000 students. The impact of Jeffco’s excess capacity is felt in nearly every corner of our District as we stretch our resources to sustain the number of schools we have with declining enrollment. On April 7, 2022, the Board of Education adopted a resolution to guide the work of regional opportunities for thriving schools in Jeffco Public Schools.  On August 25, 2022, Regional Opportunities for Thriving Schools, Phase 1, focusing on thriving elementary schools, was brought forward to the Board of Education. The plan included the staff's recommendation of a consolidation plan.
