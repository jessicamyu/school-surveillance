4.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Dec 6, 2007 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
4. Public Agenda
Type
Information
At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

5.09 Supplemental Funds - Medicaid Program (EL-7)
Meeting
Dec 6, 2007 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
5. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board policy EL-7, Financial Administration, the Administration will bring grants to the Board for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the District (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the District is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A and C.
      1. Jeffco’s Health Services Department received approximately $816,315 for the 2007-08 school year from the Medicaid Extended School Health Program (MESH). MESH reimburses school districts for the federal share of Medicaid dollars when they provide health and medical services to children who are Medicaid clients. MESH dollars are funneled through the Colorado Department of Health Care Policy and Financing who develops guidelines for submitting claims. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) is responsible for providing technical assistance to districts in meeting administrative requirements, such as the annual report and developing service plans. Colorado’s statute is unique among states with this program in that it stipulates that the money given back to districts be used to provide additional medical and health services for all students in a district rather than just Medicaid students. Colorado sees this money as a way to improve the health and well-being of all students in the state. The program aligns with Strategic Plan Objective 2, which ensures all students with special needs demonstrate achievement of academic content standards through appropriate services, instruction, and assessment.
      2. CDE assists each participating school district in developing a Local Services Plan to provide direct services to children and then bills Medicaid for these services. Because this is a reimbursement program, the 2007-08 allocation of $816,315 is based on expenses submitted for reimbursement in 2006-07.
      3. Linda Buzard, director of Health Services, manages the project.
