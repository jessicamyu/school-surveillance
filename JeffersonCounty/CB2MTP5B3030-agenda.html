PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on the new Graduation Requirements for students and the current status of Jeffco seniors on meeting the new requirements. 

 

BACKGROUND:

Colorado Department of Education adopted new graduation guidelines in May 2013 in line with House Bill 07-1118. Due to delays from the pandemic, the class of 2022 will be the first students required to demonstrate knowledge in mathematics and reading, writing, and communicating in accordance with the bill. (CDE Background and History)

 

The Jeffco Public Schools Board of Education adopted the entire Colorado Department of Education menu of options to be used for graduation requirements along with completing the previously required 23 credits. These requirements are found in Board Policy IKF.  In addition to the new requirements, this policy was updated in October 2020 to reflect students being able to earn high school credit for high school courses taken in middle school math and world language. 

 

The Jeffco Public Schools graduation requirements are posted on the Jeffco Schools homepage for students and families. New graduation requirements have been communicated with families through both district and school communication pathways.

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may address the Board. Public Comment will be taken virtually and in-person. Costume masks are not considered an adequate COVID-safe face covering (mask).

 

There is a three* minute time limit per individual speaker. *Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10* minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for March 2022

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information. 

 

Formula grants are noncompetitive awards for which the allocation is derived from a predetermined formula (e.g., number of students receiving a specific instructional program). Competitive grant awards are based on selection after a review of all applications.    

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

 

  1. The Colorado Office of Early Childhood (OEC), Department of Human Services, awarded one-time Child Care Operations Stabilization and Workforce Sustainability grants to the district’s 27 School Age Enrichment Program (SAE) sites ($3,249,693) and 41 district preschools ($1,629,801). The following charter schools also received grants: Addenbrooke Classical ($57,231), Compass Montessori ($240,714), Free Horizon ($141,191), Great Works ($125,478), Montessori Peaks ($142,191), Mountain Phoenix ($142,191), and Woodrow Wilson ($30,393). As part of OEC’s Child Care Stimulus Grants Program (passing through federal funds), grant amounts are determined based on childcare center license capacity and distributed over a nine-month period. All licensed childcare programs in the state are eligible to apply. These unplanned funds are for personnel, facility costs, equipment, supplies or mental health services for children and educators. SAE Coordinator Cathy Lytle will manage the funds for district SAE sites. Early Learning Executive Director Dawn Odean will supervise funds for preschools. Each charter school manages its grant funds.

  2. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) awarded Arvada K-8 and Thomson Elementary a $300,000 21st Century Community Learning Center renewal grant (year 4 of 5 years). Federal funds, passed through CDE, support non-school activities to boost academic achievement, social-emotional health, physical health, and family engagement/education. Performance criteria are based on student reading scores, student knowledge of essential skills, attendance rates and parent participation rates. Grant Manager Weston Scott leads the program.
     
  3. CDE awarded a 21st Century Community Learning Center renewal grant of $150,000 (year 4 of 5 years) to Alameda International Jr/Sr High. Federal funds, passed through CDE, support non-school activities including STEM, math courses, 7th and 9th grade transition courses and family enrichment activities. Performance measures are based on student math and reading scores, attendance rates and family participation rates. Principal Susie Van Scoyk and Grant Manager Kevin Schoenfeld oversee the program.
     
  4. CDE awarded a Local Accountability Systems grant of $50,000 to the district’s Research and Development team (year 1 of 2 years). This project will augment School Insights™ - a public dashboard of holistic school data to drive school improvement. Funds will be used for external consultants and annual software fees. This support, called District Insights™, will create a more coherent, holistic, and data-informed improvement process. Deliverables include metrics for identifying strengths and needs such as risk ratio calculations, quartile thresholds for publicly available student, family and educator surveys, and data models to help understand expected versus actual performance. Director of Research and Assessment Dr. Heather MacGillivary supervises the initiative.

Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to execute contract documents reflecting the increase of the not to exceed amount of the contract with DHE for audio video equipment to $2,000,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:
 

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent "may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000."
     
  2. As a result of a partial award of Bid 25848, on August 1, 2021, the Purchasing department signed a contract not to exceed $500,000 with DHE for the purchase of Audio Video Equipment for a one year period. 
     
  3. The current agreement expires on July 31, 2022.  If the request for additional contract funds is not approved, district departments and schools will no longer be able to purchase projectors or interactive displays using 2021/2022 funds.
     
  4. The district has experienced a recent increase in school purchases of projectors and interactive displays due to funding available through Title I. This sudden increase in purchases has resulted in the district nearing the $500,000 limit of the current contract with DHE.
     
  5. Many schools are replacing old projectors and end of life Smartboards with new interactive displays and projectors. These projectors and interactive displays are a core technology used in classrooms across the district.
     
  6. The technology requirements to efficiently use multimedia applications - graphics and video - in learning and non-learning environments has required an enhancement in projection device specifications.  These more robust displays have resulted in a higher per unit cost.
     
  7. The narrative in the Federal Consolidated Application and approved by CDE offers: Fully integrate mobile technology in all Title I schools (will be used) to create a digital learning environment inside and outside of the classroom that will improve student achievement. The objective is to provide the framework for a blended digital approach to innovative teaching and learning. Students will be able to create, distribute, access and collaborate using the highest level of technology-going beyond mere substitution of tasks to true redefinition.  Through a Comprehensive Needs Assessment this year, the district’s Title I office gathered school data and realized the need to replace outdated audio-visual equipment in district Title I schools. Through a collaborative partnership with Information Technology and Educational Technology, Jeffco schools had the opportunity to learn about the latest available equipment and make informed decisions to meet the needs of their school communities which has resulted in the increased purchase and need for the contract amendment.
     
  8. The estimated fund extension for the remaining five months of the contract is estimated to be $1,500,000.  Schools and departments will purchase the equipment from their budgets.  Orders will be placed centrally.

6.10 Contract Renewal: Dirsec Web Filtering System Securly (EL-7)
Meeting
Mar 3, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To authorize the director of purchasing to renew a contract with DirSec for the purchase of Securly Web Filtering and Classroom Management for an estimated total of $642,039.30

PERTINENT FACTS:
 

  1. In accordance with Board of Education executive limitation policy, EL-7, Asset Protection, the following is brought forward for approval.
     
  2. This renewal includes and aligns contracts of at home filtering and digital classroom management products. The district entered into the first three year contract March 13, 2019 to lock in stable pricing as TechforEd deployment grew. This will be the first renewal.
     
  3. Initial purchase of the products was made through a district competitive solicitation led by the Purchasing department.  Renewal pricing is under a cooperative purchasing contract (through PEPPM #528897-236, an approved cooperative for Public Education and Entity technology contracts reviewed/approved by JPS Purchasing for our schools/departments). The selected vendor, DirSec, will provide the licensure to increase filtering and digital classroom management capabilities in relation to TechForEd device growth. The components will be purchased with Information Technology and Ed Tech operational budgets.
     
  4. Entering into a three year agreement saves money and simplifies budgeting as both Jeffco device count and nationwide inflation grow rapidly.
     
  5. The at home filtering licensure includes the following features:
    1. Cloud based centralized management and filtering policies – limiting the amount of time needed by Jeffco engineers to manage the service.
    2. Simple and reliable user authentication – reinforcing digital citizenship principals and ensuring that students are accountable for activity associated with their device.
    3. Centralized logging – assisting in quickly responding to incidents ranging from simple misuse to formal threat assessments.
    4. Parental monitoring and control of student devices – building trust with our community.
       
  6. The classroom licensure includes the following features for teachers only while student devices are in their classroom:
    1. Pushing content to devices – limiting manual repetitive work and saving instruction time.
    2. Screen sharing – allowing collaboration or reinforcing instruction.
    3. Screen lock – giving teachers the ability to focus student attention on in person instruction.
    4. Custom filtering lists – to limit classroom distractions or focus on specific content.

The MEOELS Master Plan recommended $425/Square Foot (SF) to replace the Hall, the construction budget cost bid is $718/SF. The bids ranged from the low of $4,812,251 to a high of $6,907,400.  Construction Management believes these are contributing reasons for the overrun: 

 

  • The proposed building is wood frame construction.  The price of plywood and lumber has declined from peak pricing during the summer of 2021; since 2017 plywood has increased 37% and lumber 46%.
  • The Hall has a new kitchen with all new equipment which is a higher cost per SF than other spaces.  Equipment is subject to long lead times, delivery delays and manufacturer cost increases make it difficult to estimate the cost of outfitted commercial kitchens. 
  • The building is equipped with modern restrooms. 
  • The fire authority required non-combustible siding and roofing; an automatic fire sprinkler system with an independent water source (cistern). 
  • Other factors facing the construction industry in general, include a labor shortage, general inflation and the state of the economy. 
  • Due to location and unpredictability of the weather, Mt. Evans OELS tends to have a very short construction season, and can be difficult to transport labor, materials and supplies.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.
