Recommended Action
to be informed of the grant award of $60,000 (BA-09-057) from the Colorado Parent & Child Foundation to Jeffco's Family Literacy HIPPY program.
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Item C.
    1. Family Literacy received $60,000 from the Colorado Parent & Child Foundation. The program aligns with strategic plan community objectives 1 and 2, instruction objectives 1, 3 and 3, and schools objectives 2 and 3.

    2. The funds are used to support the general operating costs for the HIPPY (Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters) Program.     4. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.
    5. Gwen Welch, Family Literacy director, is the project manager.

4. Public Agenda
Type
Information
At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

5.02 District Calendars: 2010/2011 and 2011/2012 (EL-14)
Meeting
Nov 5, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
5. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the 2010/2011 and 2011/2012 district calendars as presented by Dr. Stevenson.
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.Every three years, the district’s calendar committee convenes to develop a three-year cycle of district calendars. The calendar committee is composed of parents, staff, students and community members as approved by the Board of Education.
2.The calendar committee reached consensus on a three-year cycle of district calendars for the 2008/2009, 2009/2010 and 2010/2011 school years which were approved by the Board of Education on May 31, 2007.
3.Due to the overwhelming amount of community feedback received by Superintendent Dr. Cynthia Stevenson opposing the early start dates, she reconvened the calendar committee on October 15, 2008 to discuss a later start date for the 2009/2010 calendar. As a result of that meeting, the start date for the 2009/2010 school year was changed from August 12, 2009 to August 24, 2009.
4.On September 25, 2009, Chief Academic Officer Deborah Backus reconvened the calendar committee to determine calendars for the 2010/2011 and 2011/2012 school years.
5.On October 9, 2009, consensus was reached and the calendar committee submitted two district calendars to Dr. Stevenson. The committee requested that a 2011/2012 district calendar be released that would replicate the parameters of the 2010/2011 district calendar.
6.The committee’s recommendation resumes the multiple-year calendar cycle, a practice of sequential district calendars available for planning purposes for parents, staff and community.
7.Two school year district calendars for 2010/2011 and 2011/2012, are presented by Dr. Stevenson for Board of Education approval. To review the draft calendars, please open the links provided in the previous sentence.

5.03 Budget Request: Mountain Phoenix Community School (EL-13)
Meeting
Nov 5, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
5. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve a one year postponement of the first installment of the loan provided to MPCS; and further, that the loan be renegotiated to be paid over five years beginning with the 2010-11 school year.
PERTINENT FACTS:
1. Board executive limitation policy EL-13, Charter Schools Application and Monitoring, states "In regard to existing charter schools, the superintendent may not: (9) Fail to require charter schools to submit revised budgets to the charter school administrator when their official October 1 count of students is more than 5% less than the projected student enrollment that has been submitted to the district."
2.Mountain Phoenix Community School (MPCS) has experienced a sharp decline in their expected enrollment from 41 FTE to 28 FTE. Although not certified as official by the state, 28 FTE is the student count on October 1, 2009.
3.MPCS has submitted a new budget in which the school indicates they can produce a balanced budget for the 2009-10 school year on the condition that the school district will allow a one year extension on the repayment of the loan to the school. The loan was used to create a parking lot for the school. MPCS had been expected to repay one third of the $93,000 this year and one third in each of the next two years.
4.MPCS requests that the initial installment of the loan be postponed one year to allow them to establish a balanced budget for this year. MPCS believes that they have addressed, and are addressing, shortcomings identified in a report from a Colorado Department of Education (CDE) site visit and that they will be more stable and able to repay the loan in the future.
5.MPCS indicates that they will now have a stable program that will be attractive to prospective parents and students and that they have had renewed interest in their school. They believe that their enrollment will increase during this school year and in the future based on conversations with prospective parents.
6.MPCS further requests that the loan be renegotiated to be paid over five years rather than three beginning with the 2010-11 school year. This will allow them to more easily absorb the cost of repayment in their budget if the enrollment does not increase as expected.
