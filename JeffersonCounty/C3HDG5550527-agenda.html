2.01 Public Comment (Agenda Related) IN PERSON / Asunto de la agenda - Comentarios del público (Relacionados a la agenda)
Meeting
Jun 10, 2021 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Public Agenda
Type
Information

estimated time: 5 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.     
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

Category
3. Consent Agenda

Action
Recommended Action
to appoint members to the District Accountability Committee as discussed, directed to the Superintendent in the earlier study session, and provided with this agenda item.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board governance process policy GP-13, Committee Structure, the Board of Education approves membership to the District Accountability Committee (DAC).
     
  2. Through March 16-April 25, 2021, an online application form was available to individuals interested in serving on the DAC and applicants were provided an opportunity to meet individually with board members on May 25.  The Board received 37 applications to serve on the DAC which were discussed at the earlier study session when direction was given for appointments confirmed through this agenda item.
     
  3. Appointments are made for two year terms, unless directed otherwise by the Board of Education.
     
  4. The DAC has 33 vacancies to fill for the 2021/22 school year due to term limits and resignations. Following policy, the superintendent recommends appointments for the articulation/school parent representatives and employee association categories. Board members have three parent members to appoint. DAC membership was last set on September 3, 2020.
     
  5. Continued efforts should be made to have DAC membership comply with C.R.S. 22-11-301, representing student demographics across the district in non-Caucasian, free/reduced, English language learners, migrant, gifted and challenged parent populations.

Recommended Action
to adopt the resolutions for adoption and appropriation of the 2021/2022 budget including authorizing the use of fund balance and interfund borrowing; and for a supplemental expenditure appropriation adjusting the funds as provided in the resolution detail for the 2020/2021 budget.
Goals
EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting

estimated time: 6 p.m. (estimated duration: 15 minutes)

 

PERTINENT FACTS:  /  DATOS PERTINENTES:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting, “Financial planning for any fiscal year shall not deviate materially from the Board’s Ends policies, risk fiscal jeopardy or fail to be derived from a multi-year plan.”
     
  2. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, "With respect to the actual, ongoing financial condition and activities of the district, the superintendent shall not cause nor allow fiscal jeopardy or any fiscal condition that is inconsistent with achieving the priorities established in Board's Ends policies."
     
  3. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communictaion and Counsel to the Board, the superintendent shall not fail to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed and supported.
     
  4. The proposed budget for the July 1, 2021, through June 30, 2022, fiscal year was submitted to the Board of Education for study in compliance with Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 22-44.108 on May 4, 2021.
     
  5. The district complied with C.R.S. 22-44-109 by providing legal notice of the proposed budget to the citizens of Jefferson County.
     
  6. A public hearing on the proposed budget was held at the regular Board of Education meeting on May 6, 2021, to provide an opportunity for citizens to address the Board regarding the proposed budget in compliance with C.R.S. 22-44-110. 
     
  7. The Board continued discussion of the district's proposed 2021/2022 Budget at the Board meetings on May 6,  May 14, and June 3, with further discussion planned for the June 9 and 10, 2021, meetings.
     
  8. Pursuant to C.R.S. 22-44-103, the Board of Education of each school district shall adopt a budget and an appropriation resolution for each fiscal year, prior to the beginning of the fiscal year.
     

  9. Pursuant to C.R.S. 22-44-105, the budget presents a complete financial plan for the ensuing fiscal year by fund; and school districts must pass a resolution specifically authorizing the use of a portion of the beginning fund balance in the district's budget.
     

  10. C.R.S. 22-44-113 requires that the Board of Education pass a resolution to approve interfund borrowing in advance of borrowing.
     

  11. The Board of Education has been informed of required proposed supplemental appropriations throughout the year via staff presentations, monthly, and/or quarterly reports.
     

  12. To ensure adequate appropriations at the end of the fiscal year, supplemental appropriations are proposed for the specific funds as outlined in the resolution.
