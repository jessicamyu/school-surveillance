Recommended Action
To approve the district calendar changes for the 2021/2022 school year to reflect conversion of instructional days to non-instructional days.

estimated time: 4:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 5 minutes) 
 

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. In accordance with Colorado law (C.R.S. 22-32-109(1)(n)), board executive limitation policy, EL-14, School Year Calendar, and district policy IC/ICA, School Year/School Calendar, recommended changes to the 2021/2022 district calendar must be presented to the Board of Education for approval more than 30 days in advance of the first proposed change. 
     
  2. On February 10, 2022 the superintendent recognized a memorandum of understanding with the Jefferson County Educators Association (JCAA), which includes the conversion of two student contact (instructional) days to non-student-contact days – Monday, March 28, 2022 and Friday, April 22, 2022.
     
  3. Educators will use these additional non-student-contact days for planning in order to best prepare for student learning during the remainder of the student-contact days.
     
  4. District families and staff will be provided with timely notification of the district calendar changes.
     
  5. Accordingly, the superintendent recommends the Board of Education take action to approve the recommended changes to the 2021/2022 district calendar.

3.01 Board Review of District Vision, Values and Mission (Ends 1-3)
Meeting
Feb 16, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 4:45 p.m. (estimated duration: 3 hours)

PRESENTING STAFF:
Tracy Dorland, superintendent

Jaime Aquino, Ed.D., facilitator

Lisa Relou, chief of strategy and communications


PURPOSE:

Board members will continue their work from February 5 to develop a draft of the district's vision, mission and values statements based on the earlier discussions which will set the foundation for the Jeffco Public Schools strategic plan.

 

BACKGROUND:

Jeffco Public Schools has begun the process of updating its mission, vision and values to launch a 2025 (3-year) strategic plan.

 

Previous presentations on the superintendent’s academic review in the prior semester (September 9, October 20, November 10, December 15, 2021) previewed the need for revising Jeffco Generations. Superintendent Dorland shared with the Board who articulated an appetite to move quickly on a consistent strategic direction to which the organization can align.
 

On January 12, the board began the process of working toward the next iteration of a strategic plan for Jeffco Public Schools.
