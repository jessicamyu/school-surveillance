Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education (opens 7 am Sunday, closes 5:30 pm Wednesday).   
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may address the Board. Public Comment will be taken virtually and in-person. For those who would like to address the Board of Education from home without wearing a mask, comments will be taken virtually via Zoom.  For those who are present in the audience, comments will be taken in-person and masks are required. Costume masks are not considered an adequate COVID-safe face covering (mask).

 

There is a three* minute time limit per individual speaker. *Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10* minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

Director of Choice Programs Marna Messer oversees the funds.

  4. Choice Programs received a Jeffco Schools Foundation award of $13,400 to continue four district wide art shows. Exhibits provide a public venue for recognizing achievements of elementary, middle and high school student artists. Funds cover professional development for arts staff coordinating each exhibit. Director of Choice Programs Marna Messer and Visual Arts Director Shannon May oversee the initiative.

7.12 Certification: 2021 mill levies to be collected in 2022 (EL-6)
Meeting
Dec 9, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to adopt the mill levy certification resolution as prepared in advance of the December 15, 2021 deadline.

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-6, Financial Administration, and pursuant to Colorado Statute C.R.S. 22-40-102(6), 39-5-128(1) and 39-10-114(I)(a)(I)(b), the Board of Education must certify its mill levies to the Board of County Commissioners of Jefferson and Broomfield by December 15, 2021.
     
  2. The required Certification Resolution for the general fund and bond redemption fund levies are based on the assessed valuation as certified by the Jefferson County and Broomfield County Assessors.
     
  3. As presented to the Board of Education per the attached documents, the general operating mill levy revenues will be levied against net valuation of assessment for all taxable property located within the boundaries of the school district. The levy consists of mills pursuant to the Public School Finance Act of 1994 for abatements and refunds and for voter approved mill levy overrides. Revenues will flow to the district general fund for the fiscal period beginning July 1, 2021, and ending June 30, 2022.
     
  4. As presented to the Board of Education per the attached document, in compliance with Colorado Statute section 22-54-106 of HB 20-1418; the district will set the total program mill for 2021 property taxes, collected in 2022, at 27 mills.
     
  5. As presented to the Board of Education per the attached documents, the bond redemption fund mill levy for 2021 taxes paid in 2022 will be levied against net valuation of assessment for all taxable property located within the boundaries of the school district.
     
  6. The counties have until December 10, 2021, to release final assessed valuation numbers. Preparing final documentation may require changes up until the Board of Education certification and approval on December 9, 2021.
     
  7. As additional context, for 2021, the counties and the district are responsible for accurately computing an additional tax exemption available on business personal property through HB21-1312. The tax revenue lost due to this exempted value will be reimbursed to the district by the County Treasurer in accordance with 39-3-119.5(3), C.R.S., whereby the state effectively reimburses the county and district for these lost receipts. To ensure the district correctly levies the proper millage, this exempted value must be added back to the gross assessed value when computing the number of mills necessary to reach voter approved mill levy override amounts and necessary abatements. Broomfield’s certification of valuation included the addback but Jefferson County’s did not due to system constraints. The district confirmed with Jefferson County that it is correct and appropriate to add back this exempted value when computing the required millage, the manual adjustment for which has been applied in the provided resolution..

Documents provided by the district’s legal bond counsel Kutak Rock on December 9, 2021 are the operative documents for approval relative to the November 4 approval of vendor Glacier Bank.

A feasibility study for a new pool was shared on September 9, completing the examination of the projected operational costs, staffing structure, market opportunities, and user preferences including amenities and site for a new pool. Preliminary cost estimates for the project are reported at $33-35 million. Final project costs will be determined once the facility has been designed.

Meeting

Recommended Action
to approve continuation of the annual bus purchase and authorize the director of Purchasing to enter into a contract with the awarded vendor(s).

estimated time: 8:25 p.m. (estimated duration: 15 minutes) 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7 Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000.”
     
  2. The budget for bus purchases is $1,980,000 and 5 type A buses have already been purchased leaving $1,739,000 remaining in the budget. The estimated amount for an additional 16 buses is $1,700,000.
     
  3. The department implemented an ongoing annual purchase program to update the fleet to best practice of an average age life of 11 years. Historically the fleet had an average life of 18-19 years. Beginning in 2011/12 the department purchased 19 buses per year from current budgets, and grants supplemented purchase of an additional 2-5 buses/year. In 2004, 150 buses were purchased through the bond program. There are 81 buses remaining with an age of 18 years.
     
  4. Current fleet is 407 buses, 382 route school buses and 25 activity school buses. Transportation currently has 15-20 buses ready for replacement due to high operating and maintenance costs. 
     
  5. Continue ongoing program to update fleet to best practice average age of 11 years. Current fleet has average life of 12+ years. Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMVCSA) and Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) recommends average life of student transportation fleet of 5-8 years.
     
  6. By continuing to delay the purchase of school buses, we will experience the following impacts to the budget:
  • Must repair the retired buses identified for auction and put them back in service. The availability of parts to repair these buses is a challenge; and, if able to locate parts, they will be purchased at a higher cost.
  • Older buses reduce safety for students (increased breakdowns and delays in transporting students – routes, activities and athletic events).
  • Current shortage of Bendix brake parts for new build school buses extends the delivery time of new buses. The Bendix brake shortage is currently effecting replacement repairs on existing buses.
  • Blue Bird (one of the awarded manufacturers) will be placing a 90-day freeze on new school bus orders, starting as soon as next week (December 13). This will extend the delivery of this asset even more than previous estimated delivery date to September/October 2022.
  • The supply chain factor has already influenced the automotive industry for parts, in rising cost and availability.
  • Purchasing newer buses will decrease operational costs. These vehicles will fall under warranty, we will see a 90% reduction in maintenance costs.

Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education (opens 7 a.m. Sunday, closes 5:30 p.m. Wednesday).

At this time, in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to the operation of our schools that are not on this evening's agenda. Public Comment will be taken virtually and in-person. For those who would like to address the Board of Education from home without wearing a mask, comments will be taken virtually via Zoom (additional direction for virtual participation will be emailed prior to the meeting). For those who are present in the audience, comments will be taken in-person and masks are required. Costume masks are not considered an adequate COVID-safe face covering (mask).

The Board listens with respect and asks those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any eligible individual who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up as a group must accompany the speaker. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
