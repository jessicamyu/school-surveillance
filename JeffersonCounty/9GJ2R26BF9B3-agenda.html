Subject
2.01 Executive Search Services Agreement: Ray & Associates
Meeting
Feb 27, 2014 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve and execute an agreement with Ray and Associates, Inc. to provide executive search services for an estimated not to exceed cost of $44,580 based on the scope of work provided by the firm.

estimated time: 7:00 p.m.

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board/Staff Linkage policy, BSL-04, Monitoring Superintendent Performance, "All employment decisions regarding the superintendent remain within the sole and continuing discretion of the Board."
     
  2. Due to the retirement of the current superintendent, the Board of Education is planning a recruitment process for a new superintendent; staff was directed to provide information regarding the process for hiring an executive search firm to facilitate the search.
     
  3. On December 18, 2013, staff communicated information regarding the district Request for Proposal (RFP) process and purchasing and contracting processes to ensure objectivity, transparency and credibility.  A tentative schedule for RFP activities for executive search services was also provided.
     
  4. On January 13, 2014, an RFP for executive search services was issued through Rocky Mountain e-Purchasing bid posting service.  Through this service, 345 firms were notified of the RFP.
     
  5. Four proposals were received for executive search services.  The Board received copies of the proposals and evaluation forms for review and evaluation.
     
  6. The Board met in a special study session and based on the RFP evaluation criteria selected two firms as finalists to be invited in for interviews based on evaluation of the proposals received. 
     
  7. On February 18, 2014, Ray and Associates, Inc. and PROACT Search presented to the Board of Education.  The Board provided rankings of the two finalist firms based on review of proposals, reference checks, presentation and discussion. The firm with the majority of votes was selected for award.

3. Study/Dialogue Session
Subject
3.01 Budget Review
Meeting
Feb 27, 2014 - Special Meeting
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 7:01 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

PRESENTING STAFF:
Lorie Gillis, chief financial officer

Lorri Dugan, director of Budget

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to gain a greater understanding of the district's budget and to identify specific budget priorities for the 2014-2015 budget development process.

 

BACKGROUND:

This study/dialogue session topic was initially scheduled for February 8, 2014.

On January 16, the Board of Education scheduled a February Saturday work session to allow for an in-depth presentation and discussion of the district's budget following briefer budget presentations on December 12, 2013 and January 16, 2014. 

On January 16, board members also expressed interest in expending $7 million in planned reserves for 1) fee reductions; 2) one time bonus for some staff; 3) parity per pupil funding for charter schools.  The Board agreed to add these initiatives to the 2014-2015 budget development process.

Board budget priorities for 2014-2015 will be will be specifically identified from the goals in Ends 1, Ends 2, Ends 3.
