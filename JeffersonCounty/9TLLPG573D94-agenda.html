2.02 Contract Award: District WAN Bandwidth (EL-6)
Meeting
Mar 19, 2015 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to enter into a new agreement with CenturyLink for the continued provision of services to the Education Center, 809 Quail, all district high schools and the “high priority locations” (as listed in the RFP) for an annual reoccurring cost of $648,782.00.
Goals
EL-6, Financial Administration

estimated time: 6:00 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-6, Financial Administration “…the superintendent shall not cause nor allow fiscal jeopardy or any fiscal condition that is inconsistent with achieving the priorities established in Board's Ends policies.”
     
  2. The current contract for E-rate WAN Bandwidth will expire on March 31, 2018.  This new comprehensive RFP was completed in 2014/2015 to increase bandwidth to the Education Center and 809 Quail as well as at high schools and high priority locations.  The high priority locations were identified by presently over subscribing their allocated bandwidth or not having modern delivery methods capable of handling high (100 Mbps or more) bandwidth.  The district needs to prepare for the rapidly increasing demand for network connectivity and simultaneously increase network capability while modernizing delivery methods to some sites.  
     
  3. The increased use of individual privately owned devices (Bring Your Own Device), district owned devices and Mobile Device Management (MDM) all mandate a faster more robust network and are aligned with the key objectives in the Call to Action and District Technology Plan.
     
  4. The total annual contract amount is estimated at $648,782.00.  This contract would change transport media to six district sites and increase bandwidth to 32 sites.
     
  5. Projected costs are $1,628.00 more per year compared to present costs for the same services at lower bandwidths.
     
  6. Board of Education approval of these funds will be one step in a three step process to complete the E-rate submissions.  All contracts issued through purchasing with the service providers must be signed prior to form 471 submissions (deadline 23 March 2015).  Purchasing and information technology need a few business days to complete contracts and submit the E-rate forms.
     
  7. There will be one time installation costs at some of the sites totaling $6,600.00. This is due to CenturyLink having to build their network to these sites presently not served by Optical Ethernet.
