2.02 Contract Award: Storm-Damaged Roof Replacements (EL-7, -8)
Meeting
Mar 4, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.On July 20, 2009, a storm with high winds and hail caused widespread and significant damage to roofing systems at numerous sites.
2.Temporary repairs were made by Facilities Maintenance staff to protect facilities from further damage.
3.District staff, insurance carrier representatives, and a third-party roofing consultant have assessed damage and scope of necessary replacement/repair.
4.On January 6, 2010, the Purchasing department issued a Request for Proposal (RFP) for roof replacement/repair of hail damaged roofs at Dennison Elementary, Lakewood High School, Miller Special, Prospect Valley Elementary and 809 Quail Street, Building #1.
5.Respondents to the RFP were: Alpine Roofing, B&M Roofing, CEI Roofing, Central States Roofing, D&D Roofing, Weathercraft Co., and Weathersure Systems.
6.Weathersure Systems was low bidder at $587,515.
7.The insurance deductible for the storm is $100,000 and the insurance policy will cover replacement/repair expenses in excess of that amount.
8.A previous Board agenda item for disaster restoration (Belfor Property Restoration) related to this weather event was approved on September 3, 2009.
9.Background information, available in the attachment below, shows the remaining storm-damaged projects projected to be completed by the end of the calendar year.

3.02 Public Comment

Mar 4, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
3. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

Mar 4, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
4. Ends Development and Monitoring Agenda
Type
Discussion
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.The state of Colorado is proposing substantial reductions in school funding for the 2010-2011 school year and for the 2011-2012 school year. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, with respect to providing information and counsel to the Board, the superintendent shall not fail to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed and supported.
2.Jeffco Public Schools has conducted an extensive budget process presented to the Board of Education on November 5, 2009 that has involved over a thousand community and staff members. 3.While decision making will continue over the next three weeks, members of the Board of Education will have a dialogue about the current recommendations and what else might be considered to balance the budget for the next two school years.
4.Staff will present the current recommendations for consideration, not for decision making.
5.The Board of Education will receive the most current recommendations on budget reductions and have a dialogue on the members' most current thinking.
