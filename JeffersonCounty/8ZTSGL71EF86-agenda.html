6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Dec 13, 2012 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:20 p.m.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda only. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Meeting
Dec 13, 2012 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Superintendent Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize administrative staff to negotiate and the chief operating officer to execute a motor fuel agreement in an amount not to exceed the approved budget.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In compliance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, "the superintendent may not fail to use a competitive bidding procedure for all contracted services and purchases of supplies, materials and equipment in the amount of $25,000 or more."
     
  2. Jeffco Public Schools has used forward contracting fuel for three years.  Forward contracting locks the price per gallon of diesel fuel for budget certainty.  It is then insulated from the extreme volatility of the fuel market and serves as risk mitigation.
     
  3. Historically, fuel prices decline each November and December.  If this holds true, it would allow an excellent lock-in price for three, six or nine months.
     
  4. Transportation and Fleet Maintenance projects at least $3.1 million will be required for diesel and gasoline fuels for the 2013-2014 school year with a proposed budget of $3.4 million.
     
  5. With approval, administrative staff will monitor market prices and price movement with the intent to lock in the lowest estimated pricing for future months as possible.

  5.
