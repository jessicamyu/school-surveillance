PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to be informed of the changes under development for the Jefferson articulation area schools for the 2015/2016 school year to support increased student achievement and opportunities.

 

BACKGROUND:

Meeting
Jan 15, 2015 - Regular Meeting
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session 5:30 pm

PRESENTING STAFF:

Terry Elliott, chief school effectiveness officer

Susie Van Scoyk, principal, Alameda International High School

Jennifer Kirksey, principal, O'Connell Middle School

Samantha Salazar, principal, Stein Elementary School

 

PURPOSE:

Provide the Board of Education an overview of a proposed solution to the facilities needs in the Alameda articulation area.

 

BACKGROUND:

In order to meet the growing enrollment pressures in the Alameda articulation area, which have been reviewed by the Board, a proposal was developed by staff to create an additional elementary campus.  This move would utilize the current Alameda International High School campus to become a 7-12 campus with three wings (7-8, 9-10, 11-12) enabling the creation of a new elementary school on the site of the current O'Connell Middle School.  This would alleviate the over capacity issues at Stein, Deane, and Lasley elementary schools and provide additional room for future growth in the area.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday). 
 

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda and any Part Two comments for up to one hour. After one hour, any remaining Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

6.09 Resolution: Authorizing the District to Enter into a Line of Credit (EL-6)
Meeting
Jan 15, 2015 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to adopt the resolution authorizing the district to enter into a short term line of credit in an amount not to exceed $15 million.
Goals
EL-6, Financial Administration

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, states that, "With respect to the actual, ongoing financial condition and activities of the district, the superintendent shall not cause nor allow fiscal jeopardy or any fiscal condition that is inconsistent with achieving the priorities established in Board's Ends policies."
     
  2. As outlined in a memo dated October 16, 2014, that was distributed in the October 24, 2014, Weekly Update, the district has cash obligations that must be met throughout the fiscal year. However, the majority of property tax revenues are received in the last four months of the fiscal year (March-June).
     
  3. Due to the mismatch of timing between ongoing expenses and revenue collections (cash outflows and inflows), the district requires a contingency source of cash to ensure the district’s ability to meet operating obligations, including employee salaries.
     
  4. Staff is recommending a line of credit (LOC) because it is the lowest cost, temporary (short term) instrument available.
     
  5. The district is requesting authorization to establish a LOC in order to provide temporary (short-term) cash coverage for district operations prior to the receipt of property tax revenue in the spring of 2015.
     
  6. Depending on actual cash activity over the next few weeks, use of the established LOC may not be necessary. If any amount is drawn against the LOC, the full amount will be repaid following the receipt of property tax revenue in March 2015. The LOC will be closed prior to June 30, 2015.
