The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online is available 7 am Sunday and closes 1 pm Wednesday or the day before the Board Meeting.  During sign up hours, click here to sign up to speak to the Board of Education at the Board Meeting.   

 

The Board listens to concerns on any topic for up to one hour. Public comment can also be submitted to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of the presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board,  they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker. We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  
 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de interés de la comunidad en los Comentarios del Público de su agenda. Registrarse en línea aquí para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva de Educación. (estará disponible a las 10 a.m. del lunes)

Por el momento, la Junta atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados a items on the agenda hasta por una hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que ha completado su información en la lista para Comentarios del público y tiene el equipo e internet necesarios para participar de una reunión virtual mediante Zoom puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por orador individual. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público. Se enviarán instrucciones adicionales por correo electrónico a aquellos que se registraron para los Comentarios del público. (ej.: enlace para la participar de la reunión) antes de la reunión.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. Por esta razón, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas que como grupo se registraron para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, ellos tendrán 10 minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se registran más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo hacia la Junta.

La Junta escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

 

Rogamos abrir el anexo siguiente el día 11 de agosto después de las 8 a.m., para conocer el orden de oradores invitados a la reunión para el comentario público.

5.06 Supplemental Funds Notification: September 2022
Meeting
Oct 20, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (consent)
Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for October 2022.

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

The grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information. 

 

Formula grants are noncompetitive awards for which the allocation is derived from a predetermined formula (e.g., number of students receiving a specific instructional program). Competitive grant awards are based on selection after a review of all applications.    

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

 

1.  Bloomberg Foundation via Rose Community Foundation awarded $810,000 for high dosage tutoring. Funds will support tutoring in eight schools with students whose median MAP achievement scores are between the 50th and 55th percentile (schools whose students scored below the 50th percentile receive ESSER III funds for tutoring). Schools will offer small group tutoring, 2-3 times per week for 30-60 minute sessions. Outcomes will be measured with benchmark NWEA MAP assessment data for elementary English language arts and secondary math. Deputy Superintendent Kym LeBlanc-Esparza supervises the initiative.

 

2.  Colorado Department of Education’s (CDE) Expanded Learning Opportunities grant was awarded to the Ninth Grade Transitions Program at Arvada and Wheat Ridge High and Jefferson Success Academy at Edgewater Elementary. Both programs involve partnerships with community agencies – Stand for Children Colorado’s Center for High School Success and Edgewater Collective. The award of $703,812 (ESSER III State set-aside funds) will support summer and after school programs to address learning loss. Outcome goals include accelerated student achievement, increased credit accrual, decreased course failure and increased attendance. Chief Academic Officer Renee Nicothodes and Student Engagement Director Dave Kollar oversee the project.

 

3.  With remaining funds from the state’s ESSER I allocation, CDE opened a Rapid Request grant opportunity for districts with expenses exceeding allocations. Jeffco IT received $44,000 to cover licensing costs for software loaded onto student 1:1 devices. Grants Accounting Manager Andy Flinn and Executive Director of Enterprise Client Technology Shawn Rhoades manage the initiative.  

 

4.  The district received a CDE Empowering Action for School Improvement (EASI) continuation award of $484,730. The Cohort 4 (year three) and Cohort 5 (year two) state and federal funds pay for District Designed and Led Improvement Strategies, participation in CDE’s Turnaround Network for Parr and Weber Elementary. Chief Academic Officer Renee Nicothodes, Chief of Schools Dave Weiss, and Turnaround Support Team Director Renae Schreiner lead the project.

 

5.  Community First Foundation awarded Food and Nutrition Services $500,000 to fund expanded no cost meals for students. Over 1,000 students were identified whose families do not qualify for Free or Reduced Price Meals by a minimal amount. With this grant, meals will be free to this group of students for the remainder of the 2022/23 school year. Food and Nutrition Services Executive Director Beth Wallace oversees the initiative.

 

6.  The CDHS, Office of Behavioral Health awarded an additional $38,571 Opioid Response Grant to Student Engagement, Indian Education. Funds are for prevention activities focusing on reducing substance abuse and suicide attempts. A Prevention Specialist and Indian Education Liaison implement the program. Student Engagement Manager Jason Firestone supervises the project.

 

7.  Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters (HIPPY) received a year three award from the Tony Grampsas Youth Services funds, via intermediary organization Parent Possible. The $25,000 grant will support the home-based, peer-delivered, early intervention program that fosters parents’ skills to teach their children. Grant outcomes include increased positive discipline, parental interactions related to reading and educational behaviors, and social connections. Coordinator Katherine Harrison from the Early Learning Department manages the program.

5.08 2022/2023 Bus Purchase
Meeting
Oct 20, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Consent Agenda
Type
Action (consent)
Recommended Action
to authorize the Director of Purchasing to enter into an agreement to proceed with award purchase to approved vendors.

PERTINENT INFORMATION:

 

  • To inform the Board of an expenditure greater than $500,000.
  • We are executing the annual bus purchase of 16 buses which is approximately $1,739,000 and would not exceed remaining budget of $1,980,000.
  • The department implemented an ongoing annual purchase program to update the fleet to industry best practice of an average age life of 11 years.  Historically the fleet had an average life of 18-19 years.  Beginning in 2011-12 the department purchased 19 buses per year from current budgets and grants supplemented purchase of an additional 2-5/year.  In 2004, 150 buses were purchased with Bond program.  64 remaining with an age of 19 years. 
  • Current fleet is 440 school buses, 379 route school buses and 41 activity school buses.  We currently have 20 high cost to operate and maintain buses ready for replacement – 16 school buses that need to be retired (3 school buses were purchased in 2007 & 2008 and 13 school buses were purchased in 2004). The 2004 school buses are operating better than the three younger buses (2007 & 2008).
  • Continue ongoing program to update fleet to best practice average age of 11 years.  Current fleet has average life of 12+ years.  Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) and Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) recommends average life of student transportation fleet of 5-8 years.
  • By not purchasing new school buses, we will experience the following impacts to the budget:
    • We would be forced to repair the retired buses identified for auction buses and put them back in service. The availability of these parts to repair these buses are a challenge; and if we are able to locate the parts, we will be forced to purchase the parts at a higher cost.
    • Older buses reduce safety for our students (increased breakdowns and delays in transporting students – routes, activities and athletic events).
    • There is a current shortage of Bendix brake parts for new build school buses, which will extend the delivery time of new buses. The Bendix brake shortage is currently affecting our current replacement repairs on our existing buses.
    • IC Bus (one of the awarded manufacturers) will extend the delivery of this asset even more than previous estimated delivery date to September/October 2023.
    • The supply chain factor has already influenced the automotive industry for parts, in rising cost and availability.
    • Purchasing newer buses will decrease operational cost. Since these vehicles will fall under warranty, we will see a reduction in maintenance costs.

estimated time: 6:45 p.m. (estimated duration: 60 minutes) 

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online is available 7 am Sunday and closes 1 pm Wednesday or the day before the Board Meeting.  During sign up hours, click here to sign up to speak to the Board of Education at the Board Meeting.   

 

The Board listens to concerns on any topic for up to one hour. Public comment can also be submitted to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of the presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board,  they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker. We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  
 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de interés de la comunidad en los Comentarios del Público de su agenda. Registrarse en línea aquí para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva de Educación. (estará disponible a las 10 a.m. del lunes)

Por el momento, la Junta atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados a items on the agenda hasta por una hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que ha completado su información en la lista para Comentarios del público y tiene el equipo e internet necesarios para participar de una reunión virtual mediante Zoom puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por orador individual. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público. Se enviarán instrucciones adicionales por correo electrónico a aquellos que se registraron para los Comentarios del público. (ej.: enlace para la participar de la reunión) antes de la reunión.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. Por esta razón, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas que como grupo se registraron para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, ellos tendrán 10 minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se registran más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo hacia la Junta.

La Junta escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

 

Rogamos abrir el anexo siguiente el día 11 de agosto después de las 8 a.m., para conocer el orden de oradores invitados a la reunión para el comentario público.
