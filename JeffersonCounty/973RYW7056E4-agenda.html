3.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Jun 6, 2013 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:25 p.m. (estimated duration: 35 minutes)

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda only. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

4.06 District Policy Waiver: Wheat Fest 2013 (EL-8)
Meeting
Jun 6, 2013 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Superintendent Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the use of Wheat Ridge 5-8 School as the site for the "Wheat Fest Celebration" in accordance with the Memorandum of Understanding between Jeffco Schools and the City of Wheat Ridge.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-8 Facilities, “the Superintendent may not permit capital dollars to be spent unnecessarily or unwisely...”
     
  2. The City of Wheat Ridge is proposing a “Wheat Fest” celebration to be held on the south lawn and parking lot at the Wheat Ridge 5-8 School.  The celebration will feature food, beverages, and community activities and is intended to promote and encourage community spirit and citizen participation within Wheat Ridge.
     
  3. In 2013, Jeffco Schools and the City of Wheat Ridge agreed to a Memorandum of Understanding that would periodically allow the use of the south lawn and the south parking lot of the Wheat Ridge 5-8 School, subject to mutually agreed to terms and conditions for community events and district policy.
     
  4. Wheat Ridge 2020, the event sponsoring organization, under the auspices of the City of Wheat Ridge, has requested that the event take place on Saturday, July 20, 2013 from 10:00 a.m. to approximately 10:00 p.m. and has requested use of the south lawn and parking lot for the “Wheat Fest Celebration.”  The event would include the sale and consumption of alcohol on school grounds at the Wheat Ridge 5-8 School on July 20, 2013.
     
  5. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policy GBEC, Drug Free Workplace, with respect only to the prohibited use and possession of alcoholic beverages, for this one-time event.  The policy prohibits the unlawful possession and use of alcohol on district premises.  It also states that “an employee knowingly in possession of, or under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance shall be suspended immediately by his principal or supervisor if such use or possession is on any district property at any time.”
     
  6. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policy KF-R, Community Use of School Facilities, with respect only to the prohibited use and possession alcoholic beverages, for this one-time event.  This policy states that “the use and possession of alcoholic beverages or drugs by any person or group on district property is strictly prohibited.”
     
  7. Wheat Ridge 2020 will have a Special Events liability insurance policy covering the district and other entities supporting this effort and the City of Wheat Ridge will provide police/fire protection for the event.
