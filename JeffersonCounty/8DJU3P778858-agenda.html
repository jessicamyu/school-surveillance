3.02 O'Rourke Award: Kay Newcomer
Meeting
Mar 3, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

The Board of Education is pleased to recognize Bear Creek High School Instructional Coach Kay Newcomer as the 2010-2011 recipient of the O'Rourke Prize for Distinguished Achievement in Staff Development for the state of Colorado from the Colorado Staff Development Council.  Ms. Newcomer has worked relentlessly to lead the educational advancement of over 100 teachers and administrators in the implementation of research-based instructional strategies, the design of quality assessments, differentiation to meet the needs of all learners, and the use of technology in the 21st century classroom.

The O’Rourke Prize, sponsored by the Colorado Staff Development Council (CSDC) and Mrs. Anne O’Rourke, honors an exemplary leader of professional learning and collaborative practices who has significantly impacted the learning of educators. This annual award is named for the late Dr. Bill O’Rourke, a distinguished Colorado educator.
 

Vision, commitment, leadership and effectiveness are attributes that are addressed in the nomination of the awardee. The recipient’s ability to 1) support the systemic process of change, 2) ensure content-focused learning opportunities, 3) establish learning communities, and 4) create job-embedded learning opportunities are key considerations in the selection process. These are based on the National Staff Development Council Standards and the CSDC’s “Framework for School-Focused Professional Development,”

4.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Mar 3, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

The ESL/Dual Language department will purchase the book packs for all ESL students in non-Title I schools.

4. Funding for the Summer Take Home Book Project will primarily come from Title I Regular and Title I ARRA funds. The ESL/Dual Language department and Non-Title I schools participating in the project will use their General Funds.
