PRESENTING STAFF:

Ed Bowditch, district lobbyist

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on the 2015 legislative session from the district’s lobbyist.

 

BACKGROUND:

It has been the Board of Education practice to receive periodic updates at board meetings on legislative activities of interest to and impacting k-12 education in Colorado and Jefferson County Public Schools, in particular.  The district’s lobbyist provides weekly reports and other timely communication as needed related to pending legislation.  The 2015 legislative session began on January 7, 2015.  In accordance with Board governance process policy 4, Board Job Description, the Board of Education may take positions on pending legislation to provide direction to Mr. Bowditch.

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dr. Syna Morgan, chief academic officer

Dr. Blanche Kapushion, director of Gifted Programming


PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive an update on the Gifted and Talented (GT) funding for the 2014/2015 school year.

 

BACKGROUND:

At the January 15, 2015 Board of Education meeting, the Board of Education requested an update on the Gifted and Talented funding for the current school year.

Board Ends policy 2 states, “every student will achieve at least one year's growth, or more as needed to 'catch up,' in every year of school and be ready for the next level.”  With the Board of Education's continued focus on student growth and achievement, time was requested to provide an update on the Gifted and Talented funding toward meeting the Board of Education goal in Ends 2 Student Achievement: the percentage of ALP students who have growth measures over 50 will increase: in writing from 54 to 56; in math from 60 to 62; and in reading from 57 to 59.
