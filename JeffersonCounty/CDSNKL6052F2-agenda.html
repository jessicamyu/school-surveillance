If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10* minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

To approve the donation from the Outdoor Lab Foundation for $195,998 to support student engagement at the Outdoor Lab Schools.

PERTINENT FACTS:
 

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, and district policy KCD and KCD-R, Public Gifts/Donations, gifts of money, real or personal property and personal services from organizations, community groups and/or individuals which will benefit the district shall be encouraged by district administration.
     
  1. On May 10, 2022 Jefferson County Public Schools received a donation of $195,998 from the Outdoor Lab Foundation to support student engagement at Windy Peak and Mt. Evans Outdoor Education Lab Schools.  The check presentation will occur at the June 2, 2022 Board of Education meeting.
     
  1. The Outdoor Lab Foundation donated $195,998 to Jefferson County Public Schools to support the Outdoor Lab Program. The funds were distributed to 36 community schools across the district to help with deficits each school may be experiencing because of their participation in Outdoor Lab this school year. The Foundation particularly allocated funds based on each school’s free and reduced lunch populations and the associated tuition gaps each school faced as a result. Moreover, the donation includes a $20,000 contribution to support intern stipends for both the Mount Evans and Windy Peak sites.
