1.02 Meyers Pool Replacement Project (EL-11)
Meeting
Mar 4, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
1. Study/Dialogue Session 5 pm
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 5 p.m.  (estimated duration: 15 minutes)

PRESENTING STAFF:

Steve Bell, chief operating officer
Nicole Stewart, interim chief financial officer
Tim Reed, executive director, Facilities and Construction Management

 

PURPOSE:

 

For the Board of Education to determine next steps regarding replacement of the existing Meyers Pool through a partnership with Jeffco Public Schools, the City of Arvada and Apex Park and Recreation District. 

 

BACKGROUND:

 

In accord with Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel with the Board, this topic is brought forward. The George J. Meyers Swimming Center (Meyers Pool), owned by the City of Arvada and operated by the Apex Park and Recreation District, is almost 50 years old and in need of replacement. A plan had been developed to keep the pool in operation until a new pool opens.  Estimated end of the pool's useful life is 2023.  
 

Elected officials and representatives from three agencies have discussed development of an intergovernmental partnership to replace Meyers Pool:  Jeffco Public Schools, Apex Park and Recreation District and the City of Arvada. 
 

A feasibility study for a new pool has been completed which examines the projected operational costs, staffing structure, market opportunities; and, user preferences including amenities and site for the new pool has been completed.  Preliminary cost estimates for the project are estimated at $33-35 million.  Final project costs will be determined once the facility has been designed.
 

A partnership agreement currently drafted provides for Jeffco Public Schools and the City of Arvada to jointly finance construction of the aquatics complex, with Apex Park and Recreation District providing provide ongoing management and operations of the site.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Friday)  
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

6.05 Supplemental Funds Notification: March 2021 (EL-11)
Meeting
Mar 4, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for March 2021.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The district is submitting an application to receive federal CARES Act funds via the Colorado Department of Education (CDE). The district’s Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief Fund (ESSER II) preliminary allocation (based on the district Title I formula) is $30,375,421. Funds address coordination of COVID preparedness and response, learning loss, other activities necessary to maintain education services, and school facility repairs related to health hazards. Grants Accounting Manager Joshua Shoemaker will manage the funds.
     
  2. Supplemental ESSER I funds were set aside by CDE for Title VI Indian Education grantees. Based on the American Indian/Alaskan Native/Native Hawaiian student count, the district’s Indian Education program received $37,800. Funds will be used to meet the unique educational and culturally-related academic needs of Indian students. Student Engagement Manager Jason Firestone oversees the initiative.
     
  3. Colorado Department of Human Services awarded $488,700 in Child Care Relief Grants to district School Age Enrichment (SAE) Programs. Each of the district’s 27 SAE sites received $18,100. Funds cover costs associated with COVID such as accommodating smaller groups, purchasing safety and cleaning supplies, and staff salaries. School Age Enrichment Coordinator Cathy Lytle administers the funds.
     
  4. The CDE’s Colorado State Library awarded $20,274 to Digital Teacher Librarians for the purchase of educational resources. The district-level, non-competitive allocation is calculated based on student enrollment. The grant is used to increase teacher and student access to information through books, periodicals, subscriptions, ebooks and device applications. Resources support Strategic Plan Learning tactics: Transforming the Student Task, Responsive Teaching and Technology to Transform Learning. Digital Teacher Librarian Coordinator Heidi Floyd from Educational Technology manages the project.
     
  5. Jeffco Schools Foundation awarded $28,000 to support the Department of Curriculum and Instruction, Language Arts Department, Jeffco Summer of Early Learning (JSEL) Program. JSEL serves kindergarten through 3rd grade students for six weeks focusing on literacy and math skills. Students who are not currently at end-of-year expectations or on a READ plan who have been recommended by their teacher are eligible. Assistant Director Kim Ballantyne manages the program.
     
  6. Choice Programs received a Jeffco Schools Foundation award of $15,000 to continue four districtwide art shows. Exhibits provide a virtual, public venue for recognizing achievements of elementary, middle and high school student artists. Funds cover professional development for arts staff coordinating each exhibit. Director of Choice Programs Marna Messer and Visual Arts Director Shannon May oversee the initiative.
