2.04 Foster PK-8 Dual Language Option School (EL-11)
Meeting
Oct 16, 2019 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information
Goals
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 11 a.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Leigh Hiester, principal, Foster Elementary School

Catherine Baldwin-Johnson, director, ESL/Dual Language Department

Renee Nichothodes, chief of schools elementary

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will review the Foster Dual Language PK-8 option school proposal with an anticipated vote on the proposal at the November 7, 2019 regular board meeting.

 

BACKGROUND:

After two years of extensive conversations, community meetings, data gathering, and launching a pilot Dual Language Middle School this school year, Foster is ready to proceed with finalizing the steps toward establishing the first Two-Way Dual Language PK-8 school in Jeffco Public Schools.

 

On December 13, 2018, Foster students and parents presented the Foster Dual Language PK-8 pilot proposal to the Board of Education. Board members asked questions and offered suggestions on how to proceed. Foster staff followed up with all of the suggestions and are now prepared for the final decision on formally changing Foster from a PK-6 to a PK-8 Dual Language option school.

 

Over the past 2 years with the intent of determining the need, interest, support and possibility of implementation, Foster staff has met with parents, community members, students, superintendent, and Jeffco Public Schools administrators from Transportation, Food Service, Choice Enrollment, Facilities, Budgets, ESL/Dual Language, Student Data Privacy, Human Resources, Curriculum and Instruction, Communications, Chief Academic Office, Chief Strategy Office, Chief of Schools Elementary, and nearby Arvada area principals. All have contributed to and are in support of the proposal in moving forward as a PK-8 Dual Language option school.

 

Since Foster would be changing from a neighborhood school to an option school (with priority enrollment reserved for Foster students in the historic Foster neighborhood boundary), the Jeffco Board of Education needs to vote on the proposal.
