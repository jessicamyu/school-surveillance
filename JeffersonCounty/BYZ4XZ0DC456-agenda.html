Meeting

Category
4. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

 

Hora estimada: 6:30 p.m. (duración estimada: hasta 1 hora)
 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de interés de la comunidad en los Comentarios del Público de su agenda. Registrarse en línea aquí para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva de Educación. (estará disponible a las 10 a.m. del lunes)

Por el momento, la Junta atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados a items on the agenda hasta por una hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que ha completado su información en la lista para Comentarios del público y tiene el equipo e internet necesarios para participar de una reunión virtual mediante Zoom puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por orador individual. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público. Se enviarán instrucciones adicionales por correo electrónico a aquellos que se registraron para los Comentarios del público. (ej.: enlace para la participar de la reunión) antes de la reunión.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. Por esta razón, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas que como grupo se registraron para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, ellos tendrán 10 minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se registran más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo hacia la Junta.

La Junta escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

 

Rogamos abrir el anexo siguiente el día 11 de agosto después de las 8 a.m., para conocer el orden de oradores invitados a la reunión para el comentario público.

To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for April 2021

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. Colorado Department of Human Services, Office of Early Childhood awarded $1,030,103 in Child Care Relief grants to district preschools and School Age Enrichment programs (House Bill 20B-1002 set aside $45 million from the state’s general fund). Ninety-two Jeffco sites/programs received grants ranging from $3,150 to $29,800 depending on license capacity and Colorado Shines Rating (a measure of quality) for each site. Funds can be used to accommodate smaller group sizes, increase availability of child care, purchase safety and cleaning supplies and salaries for staff who provide child care. SAE Coordinator, Cathy Lytle, and school-based preschool directors oversee the funds.
     
  2. The Gill Foundation awarded another grant via Jeffco Schools Foundation for STEM labs in the North Arvada articulation area. The $334,400 award will provide SMART Labs at Fitzmorris, Lawrence and Peck elementary schools. The project addresses the Conditions for Learning Tactic: Commitment to Equity through a focus on increasing underrepresented students pursuing STEM pathways. STEM Pathway Designer Heather Waldron and Choice Programs Director Marna Messer manage the project.
     
  3. Tides Center awarded a Next Generation Learning Challenges grant of $10,000 to be used to address a Problem of Practice by applying Transformation Design principles, practices and resources. Funds will be used for continued participation in the Deeper Learning Dozen, facilitated by the Harvard Graduate School of Education. Through a community of practice, participants support equitable access to deeper learning experiences and outcomes for all students and adults, through changes in leadership, school district systems, adult learning and pedagogy. Districts learn from their collective experiences and share with the field. Chief Academic Officer Matt Flores will oversee the project along with partners Chief Student Success Officer Dr. Susan Leach and Chief Strategy Officer Dr. Tom McDermott.

August 11, 2020, the Board announced an interim superintendent. A superintendent search process typically begins no later than January to take full advantage of a national candidate pool.

  4. On December 16, Mr. Bell and Hank Gmitro and Lisa Flores, representatives of Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates, began the process of defining the parameters for the superintendent search, including community engagement planning, community survey planning, search calendar.
     
  5. On January 7, Mr. Bell, Mr. Gmitro and Ms. Flores continued discussion of key decisions related to the superintendent search parameters and other related needs of the Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates consultants.
     
  6. In February, the Board finalized the Leadership Profile report and Desired Characteristics summary developed through Hazard, Young, Attea & Associates’ gathering of feedback from community engagement sessions and online survey responses. Applicants were accepted through the deadline of February 15, 2021.
     
  7. At the meetings of March 9-11, 16, 22, 23, 2021, the Board of Education narrowed its search. The law requires that the finalist(s) be made public fourteen days prior to appointing or employing one of the finalists.
     
  8. At the special meeting of March 25, 2021, the Board of Education appointed Tracy A. Dorland as the sole finalist for the position of superintendent, and authorized the district’s chief human resources officer and legal counsel to begin discussions with Ms. Dorland about the terms of employment.
