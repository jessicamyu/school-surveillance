4.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related) via Zoom / Asunto de la agenda - Comentarios del público (Relacionados a la agenda)

Information

estimated time: 6:20 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

 

Hora estimada: (time) (duración estimada: hasta 1 hora)
 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de interés de la comunidad en los Comentarios del Público de su agenda. Registrarse en línea aquí para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva de Educación. (estará disponible a las 10 a.m. del lunes)

Por el momento, la Junta atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados a items on the agenda hasta por una hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que ha completado su información en la lista para Comentarios del público y tiene el equipo e internet necesarios para participar de una reunión virtual mediante Zoom puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por orador individual. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público. Se enviarán instrucciones adicionales por correo electrónico a aquellos que se registraron para los Comentarios del público. (ej.: enlace para la participar de la reunión) antes de la reunión.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. Por esta razón, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas que como grupo se registraron para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, ellos tendrán 10 minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se registran más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo hacia la Junta.

La Junta escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

 

Rogamos abrir el anexo siguiente el día 11 de agosto después de las 8 a.m., para conocer el orden de oradores invitados a la reunión para el comentario público.

6.08 Donation: Jeffco Public Schools (EL-11)
Meeting
Jun 3, 2021 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the donation from the Outdoor Lab Foundation for $235,000 to support student engagement at the Outdoor Lab Schools.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, and district policy KCD and KCD-R, Public Gifts/Donations, gifts of money, real or personal property and personal services from organizations, community groups and/or individuals which will benefit the district shall be encouraged by district administration.
  1. On May 6, 2021 Jefferson County Public Schools received a donation of $235,000 from the Outdoor Lab Foundation to support student engagement at Windy Peak and Mt. Evans Outdoor Education Lab Schools.  The check presentation occurred at the Board of Education meeting.
  1. The Outdoor Lab Foundation donated the $235,000 to Jefferson County Public Schools to support the Outdoor Lab Program. The funds were distributed to 36 community schools across the district to help with deficits each school may be experiencing because of their participation in Outdoor Lab this school year. The Foundation particularly allocated funds based on each school’s free and reduced lunch populations and the associated tuition gaps each school faced as a result. Moreover, the donation includes a $20,000 contribution to support intern stipends for both the Mount Evans and Windy Peak sites. Lastly, the Foundation provided three $2,000 scholarships to high school leaders and interns who are launching their post-secondary education this fall.
  1. The Foundation supports Outdoor Lab by investing in four strategic initiatives. First, the Foundation’s Tuition Assistant Program which makes up the bulk of it’s philanthropy as touched on above. Second, the Intern Stipend Program. Third, the High School Leaders Program which is made up of the scholarship awards and other incentives to thank our young adult mentors for their volunteerism on site. Finally, fourth, the Site Support Program which is currently focused on ensuring the teachers have the materials and supplies to implement fun and impactful programming, and that nurses have the outerwear and resources they need to keep every student warm, dry, safe, and feeling welcome on site.
  1. The monetary donation was received from the Outdoor Lab Foundation.

Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for June 2021

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The district's American Rescue Plan Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief Fund (ARP ESSER III) preliminary allocation awarded through the Colorado Department of Education (CDE) is $68,218,881 (based on the Title I formula). The district's chief student success officer will lead a diverse team of stakeholders to create a plan based on the most pressing needs related to COVID. Plan components include summer learning opportunities, mental health, school year opportunities, operational investments due to ongoing health needs and community engagement/family supports. The plan will address barriers to students, teachers and other beneficiaries with special needs. Grants Accounting Manager Joshua Shoemaker will manage the funds.
     
  2. CDE awarded State Set Aside Funds (10% of the state's ESSER II allocation) to support Native American students and students with special needs. The district's award of $151,200 will provide support and services for Native American students. Student Engagement Office Manager Jason Firestone will manage the funds. An allocation of $683,792 will fund services for special education students. Matt Palaoro, director of Special Education, will oversee the funds.
     
  3. CDE awarded a 21st Century Community Learning Center (CCLC) Expanded Learning Opportunities grant of $48,325 to Alameda International Junior/Senior High School's 21st CCLC program. Funds are available through the ESSER I allocation for out-of-school time programs. Principal Susie Van Scoyk and 21st CCCLS Program Director Lisa Vega-Fields manage the grant.

  6. Infinite Campus is widely considered to be a very strong player in the SIS marketplace. The state has not yet indicated a direction as a result of this work. The foreseeable challenges in supporting Infinite Campus are the optimization of its architecture to improve its performance, managing the necessary software upgrades, and the support of customization requests.
     
  7. The effective period of the renewed one-year contract would be from July 1, 2021, through June 30, 2022.

estimated time: 9:40 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)
 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education. (will be available 10 am Monday)

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
