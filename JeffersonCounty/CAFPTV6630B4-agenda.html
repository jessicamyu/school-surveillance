Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education (opens 7 am Sunday, closes 5:30 pm Wednesday).   
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may address the Board. Public Comment will be taken virtually and in-person. For those who would like to address the Board of Education from home without wearing a mask, comments will be taken virtually via Zoom.  For those who are present in the audience, comments will be taken in-person and masks are required. Costume masks are not considered an adequate COVID-safe face covering (mask).

 

There is a three* minute time limit per individual speaker. *Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10* minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

Inscríbase aquí para hablar con la Junta Directiva de Educación (el formulario estará abierto a las 7 a.m. del domingo y se cerrará a las 5:30 p.m. del miércoles).

Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que haya completado la información solicitada en la lista de comentarios públicos puede dirigirse a la Junta Directiva. Para aquellos que deseen dirigirse a la Junta de Educación desde casa sin usar una mascarilla, los comentarios se recibirán virtualmente a través de Zoom. Las mascarillas de disfraz no se consideran una cubierta facial adecuada de seguridad ante la COVID (mascarilla).

De esta manera, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se inscriben más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo a la Junta.

La Junta Directiva escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for February 2022.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.   

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

 

  1. Community First Foundation awarded a $100,000 grant toward the cost of a standalone health clinic building at Alameda International Jr/Sr High. Previous clinic space was lost through the building remodel. STRIDE Community Health Center will continue to collaborate with the district to provide health care services once the building is complete. Community Superintendent David Weiss directs the project.
     
  2. Community First Foundation provided a $20,000 Community Impact grant to Student Engagement. Funds will cover vouchers for credit recovery and student transportation to after-school work/career related activities. Student Engagement Director David Kollar supervises the initiative.
     
  3. Supplemental ESSER III funds were set aside by CDE for supports and services to Special Education Students. An allocation of $537,250 will go to the Special Education Department. Matt Palaoro, Director of Special Education, will manage the funds.   
     
  4. Supplemental ESSER III funds were set aside by CDE for Title VI Indian Education grantees. Based on the American Indian/Alaskan Native/Native Hawaiian student count, the district’s Indian Education program received $226,845. Funds will support the unique educational and culturally related academic needs of Native American students. Student Engagement Manager Jason Firestone oversees the initiative.
     
  5. Food and Nutrition Services received $65,108 through a USDA Food Service Equipment grant via CDE. Funds will purchase a new walk-in freezer at Weber Elementary. Food and Nutrition Services Manager of School Operations Jennifer Mazey leads the project.
     
  6. The Institute of Museum and Library Services via CDE’s State Library awarded $44,462 from the American Rescue Plan to help communities respond to the pandemic and related needs through equitable approaches in digital inclusion and library services. Digital Teacher Librarian Coordinator Heidi Floyd from Educational Technology manages the project.
     
  7. The CDE’s Colorado State Library awarded $23,038 to Digital Teacher Librarians for the purchase of educational resources. The allocation is based on student enrollment. The grant is used to increase teacher and student access to information through books, periodicals, subscriptions, ebooks and device applications. Resources support Strategic Plan Learning tactics: Transforming the Student Task, Responsive Teaching and Technology to Transform Learning. Digital Teacher Librarian Coordinator Heidi Floyd from Educational Technology administers the project.
     
  8. Student Services received $24,015 through a Counselor Corps supplemental grant from CDE. Funds will support Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) resources for all schools and professional development related to college and career success for 15 counselors. Director of Student Services Terry Walderman supervises the program.

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7 Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000."
  2. In compliance with the Strategic Plan of Operations: create systems to maximize operational efficiencies in support of the learning environment, Tactic 1: Safe and On-Time Transportation.
  3. In November 2020, and in accordance with district polices DJE, Bidding Procedures, and DJB, Purchasing Procedures, RFQ 25651 process was issued and an award made to HopSkipDrive Inc. 
  4. In July 2021, a contract was executed in an amount not to exceed $450,000.
  5. Due to the quality of HopSkipDrive’s services, and the bus driver shortage impacting all transportation service providers, the district has increased its usage of HopSkipDrive services more than anticipated in July.
  6. In planning for the 2021/22 school year, the Transportation Services department contracted for the number of students who used the service in 2020/21 with a 15% budget increase for unexpected student needs over the prior school year. Year-to-date, Transportation has seen a 30% increase in new requests for student transportation needs to meet the mandates of McKinney Vento, homeless and foster care students.
  7. Currently, the district contracts with three transportation service companies (HopSkipDrive, ALC, and Noah Care) to support these student transportation services required by federal and state mandates. Of the three service providers, only HopSkipDrive has reached a service level that will exceed the $500,000 contract amount (requiring board approval by district policy) due to the company being able to service more of the transportation requests than ALC and Noah Care.
