PRESENTING STAFF:
Daniel M. McMinimee, superintendent

 

PURPOSE:

In accordance with Board governance process policy 4, Board Job Description, the Board of Education will consider performance-based salary payment outlined in the superintendent’s contract, item 3B, aligned with the Ends policies in place for the 2015/16 school year prior to April 7, 2016 Ends policy revisions.

 

BACKGROUND:

The superintendent’s evaluation and performance-based pay are based on direction provided in Board/Staff Linkage policy 4, Monitoring Superintendent Performance, and a year’s progress toward Board determined goals.
 

The superintendent’s contract calls for a plan for performance-based salary payments to be developed by mid-October of each year which has historically aligned with the district’s receipt of student achievement data from the state (superintendent contract item 3B).
 

The determination to pay performance-based pay to the superintendent, separate from the development of the plan for the next school year, has been made each October.  The Board of Education followed this same schedule approving the superintendent’s 2014/2015 performance pay component at the November 5, 2015 board meeting.
 

A separate item from the performance-based pay component in the superintendent’s contract is the annual evaluation (contract item 8A) which was discussed on January 14, 2016 following the Board/Staff Linkage policy 4, Monitoring Superintendent Performance. Monitoring reports approved by the Board of Education providing the superintendent's reasonable interpretation of executive limitations is available, here.
 

The Board revised its Ends (district goals) policies on April 7, 2016. The Ends policies direct the work of the district. As the Board’s sole employee, Ends policies guide the superintendent’s work.  The goals for performance-based pay were kept as developed in October 2015, aligned with the previous Ends policies at the point of adopting new Ends policies in April.

 

An addendum to the contract will be created by the chief human resources officer for Board action on the November 3 consent agenda. (see attached model from last year: ContractAddendum_1 PerformancePay 20142015McMinimee)

Renee Nicothodes, achievement director

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive information regarding our work to support our District Unified Improvement Plan (DUIP) improvement strategies.

 

BACKGROUND:

Board Ends 3 states, "Every school and the district will have effective learning systems and shared leadership that:

  • Utilize continuous improvement processes that incorporate problem-solving approaches to reduce or eliminate root causes of student performance challenges."

Since July, leaders from throughout the Jeffco Schools system (School Innovation and Effectiveness Team, Student Success Office, and Educational Research and Design) have been working to use continuous improvement processes to address the major improvement strategies in the DUIP.  Representatives from each team will share the work that has been done to impact improvement in the areas of Algebraic Thinking, Early Literacy, Student Plans, Continuous Improvement, and Learning Pathways.
