Subject
2.03 Jeffco Innovation Acceleration Fund 2.0 (EL-11)
Meeting
Sep 12, 2018 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 10:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Lisa Summitt, specialist, Educational Technology

Tom McDermott, Ed.L.D., chief strategy officer

 

PURPOSE:

To provide the Board of Education with information on the revised 2018/19 Jeffco Innovation Acceleration Fund application process.

 

BACKGROUND:

On January 11, 2018 the Board of Education approved $1 million to fund the first Jeffco Innovation Acceleration Fund. Following that approval, staff facilitated an application process which resulted in the March 2018 identification and funding of 10 innovative ideas from Jeffco staff. Staff collected feedback on the application process and made design changes in response to feedback.

 

The Board of Education will hear a report on revisions to the application process based on feedback and lessons learned from the first cycle of the Jeffco Innovation Acceleration Fund.

Recommended Action
To acknowledge receipt of the Superintendent's recommendation for dismissal of the teacher, ratify the teacher's suspension with pay subject to the limitation of the Teacher Employment, Compensation, and Dismissal Act; and, to authorize the Secretary of the Board and legal counsel to send the teacher written notice of intent to dismiss together with the other matters required by the Act.

PERTINENT FACTS:  

  1. Board Executive Limitation policy EL-3, Staff Treatment, provides that the Superintendent shall not fail to make recommendations as appropriate for termination of employment.  
     
  2. Following a thorough review of the matter, the Superintendent has submitted his recommendation for dismissal of a teacher, pursuant to the provisions of the Teacher Employment, Compensation, and Dismissal Act, C.R.S., Section 22-64-101 et. seq.
     
  3. The grounds for the recommendation is insubordination, immorality, and other good or just cause as provided in C.R.S., Section 22-63-301 and as detailed in the Superintendent's written charges.  Upon receipt of the recommendation, the procedures for dismissal as specified in C.RS., Section 22-63-302 will commence.
