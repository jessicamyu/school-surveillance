PRESENTING STAFF:

Marcia Anker, sixth grade transition coordinator, School Leadership

Wendy Doran, principal, Carmody Middle School

Monica Elzinga and Sofia Goshea, parent and 7th grade student, Moore Middle School

Brenda Fletcher, principal, Moore Middle School

Kristina Hernandez and Tristen Hernandez, parent and 6th grade student, Carmody Middle School

Christie Hurt, principal, Ken Caryl Middle School

Carrie Sargent and Nick Sargent, parent and 7th grade student (who was a 6th grade student in the GT program at Ken Caryl Middle School last school year), Ken Caryl Middle School

John Schalk, principal, Mandalay Middle School

Kristopher Schuh, chief of schools secondary, School Leadership

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to converse with representative district staff, middle school principals, parents, and students about the transition of sixth grade students to the middle schools in Jeffco Public Schools.

 

BACKGROUND:

In Spring 2017, district leaders made the decision to transition 6th grade students to the middle schools in articulation areas where the middle schools were 7th-8th grades.

 

On May 4, 2017, the Board of Education committed to a systemic K-5/6-8 model and approved funds to provide additional space to accommodate 6th grade students at Drake and Dunstan Middle Schools. The process began on July 1 to transition 6th grade students to twelve middle schools for the 2018/2019 school year.

 

On October 19, 2017, district staff and principals provided the Board of Education with an interim report on the sixth grade transition.

 

On January 11, 2018, the Board of Education approved funds to provide additional space to accommodate 6th grade students at Creighton, Ken Caryl, and Summit Ridge Middle Schools.

 

August 2018 twelve district middle schools welcomed 6th grade students.

 

August 2019 three additional middle schools are on track to welcome 6th grade students to complete the systemic K-5/6-8 model district-wide.

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) and the external audit for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2018.   


BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education monitors the districtwide conditions as outlined in Board executive limitation policies EL-6, Financial Administration and EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting. 

 

Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 29-1-606 (1)(b) requires that the CAFR be delivered to the Board of Education by November 30, 2018.  The Board received a copy on November 16, 2018. The report and audit results were reviewed with the Financial Oversight Committee on December 4, 2018, and the Audit Committee on December 5, 2018.

 

Colorado revised Statute 22-32-109 requires the district to prepare a CAFR. The report consists of financial information prepared by the district and audited by an independent firm and indicates the financial status of the district at the end of the reporting period. The CAFR also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

2.05 Budget Update: 2017/2018 Increase Results, 5A Integration into Budget Process (EL-11)
Meeting
Dec 19, 2018 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 11:50 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:
Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Nicole Stewart, director-budget and treasury


PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive an update on use and results of prior year funding increases and the timeline for and integration of new funding of the 5A mill levy override into the budget planning process. 

 

BACKGROUND:

Budget increase requests are submitted as part of the budget development process each year through the Student Based Budgeting (SBB) for schools and Budgeting for Outcomes (BFO) for departments processes. The requests, which include performance measures and objectives, are prioritized based on the current state of the district, input from stakeholders, and consideration of how the requests align with the district's strategic plan. Funded requests are evaluated against stated objectives and the outcomes and results are communicated to the Board to assure that the funding directly contributed to the success of district priorities.

 

Voters approved two ballot issues on November 6, 2018, including 5A for a $33 million mill levy override and 5B for $567 in bond funding. New funding for 5A will be integrated into the budget for current year and planning process for development of the 2019/2020 budget. A 2018/2019 supplemental budget request for use of 5A funds is anticipated for February 2019.

 

The governor's proposed budget was released in early November; a December forecast from the state is due to be released in mid- to late-December. Funding estimates through the School Finance Act continue to be based on estimated October enrollment counts and school finance assumptions per the governor's proposal. Staff continues to work with schools and district leadership on funding requests for 2019/2020 through the Student Based Budgeting (SBB) for schools and Budgeting for Outcomes (BFO) for departments processes to support the district's strategic plan.

 

The Board received an update on timelines and initial planning for development of the 2019/2020 budget, recommendations for community engagement and funding estimates based on the governor's proposed budget at the Board of Education meeting on November 7, 2018.
