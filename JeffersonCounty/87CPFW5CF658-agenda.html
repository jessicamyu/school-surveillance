2.09 Supplemental Funds - Health Services (Medicaid Extended School Health) (EL-6)
Meeting
Sep 17, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A and C.
    1. Health Services Department received $689,605.98 for the 2009-10 school year from the Medicaid Extended School Health Program (MESH). MESH reimburses school districts for the federal share of Medicaid dollars when they provide health and medical services to children who are Medicaid clients. MESH dollars are funneled through the Colorado Department of Health Care Policy and Financing who develops guidelines for submitting claims. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) is responsible for providing technical assistance to districts in meeting administrative requirements, such as the annual report and developing service plans. Colorado’s statute is unique among states with this program in that it stipulates that the money given back to districts be used to provide additional medical and health services for all students in a district rather than just Medicaid students. Colorado sees this money as a way to improve the health and well-being of all students in the state. The program aligns with strategic plan school objective 1, which ensures that student achievement is increasing in every school regardless of gender, race/ethnicity, socioeconomic status, or special needs.
    2. CDE assists each participating school district in developing a local services plan to provide direct services to children and then bills Medicaid for these services. Because this is a reimbursement program, the 2009-10 allocation is based on expenses submitted for reimbursement in 2008-09.
    3. Linda Buzard, director of Health Services, manages the project.

The Board of Education is pleased to recognize our 40 schools which have earned the distinction of participating in the Power Libraries Project, a program administered by the Colorado Department of Education (CDE). There are two levels of recognition, Developing and High Performance power libraries. Developing libraries are usually new and/or are recognized as having many of the necessary components of a strong library program. High Performance libraries meet criteria set by CDE for having an exemplary library and agree to mentor other schools to increase teacher and librarian collaboration resulting in increased student achievement.

The Power Library model represents the foundation of the visition that the Teacher-Librarians presented to the Board two years ago.

In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.
