6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Apr 4, 2013 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 7 p.m. (estimated duration: 15 minutes)

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda only. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

to authorize the director of Purchasing to execute a renewal agreement with Panorama for the high school athletic trainer program for a base amount of $780,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7 Asset Protection, “the superintendent may not fail to use a competitive bidding procedure for all contracted services and purchases of supplies, materials and equipment in the amount of $25,000 or more . . . “
     
  2. To ensure the safety of student athletes, Jeffco Public Schools has contracted for athletic trainer services since 2005.  In 2010, the Central Athletics staff revised the proposal specification to ensure that the services covered in the agreement more completely meet the needs of students and staff.
     
  3. A Request for Proposal was issued on April 7, 2010.  Notification was sent to 188 vendors through email and the Rocky Mountain E-Purchasing System. Proposals were received from three qualified vendors.  Panorama was awarded an agreement for the 2010/2011 and 2011/2012 school years.  Based on past performance, no cost increase, and added value programs, administrative staff recommends renewal of the agreement with Panorama for high school athletic training services for an additional year at an estimated cost of $780,000.
     
  4. Jeffco Public Schools was able to keep the base agreement cost at $780,000 with no increases for the 2011/2012 and 2012/2013 school years. However, for the 2012/2013 school year, Panorama proposed minimal changes to the specification with no increase cost to the District while absorbing the cost of providing their staff with a minimal cost of living increase.

Meeting

Recommended Action
to approve the movement of the Mitchell Elementary sixth graders to Bell Middle School beginning in the 2013-2014 school year.

estimated time: 8:25 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Mitchell Elementary has around 625 neighborhood students enrolled this school year and currently has around 640 enrolled for 2013-2014 (as of late February).  Mitchell has not accepted choice enrollment for additional students.  The Gym was built for 400 students and the cafeteria was built for 240 students.  The parking lot and acreage are under-sized due to the topography.  Developments within the attendance area will provide continued growth.
     
  2. Mitchell is currently at 110% capacity/utilization.  Neighboring north Golden/Arvada Fairmount Elementary is at 93% capacity/utilization with 614 students.  Bell Middle School is at 56% capacity/utilization with 512 students. 
     
  3. During the 2012-2013 school year one kindergarten spent half of the year having class in the library because of the lack of classroom space.  A temporary classroom was moved onto the Mitchell site in January of 2013.  Any additional temporary classrooms will have to be placed on the playground and will be “dry” temps. 
     
  4. In a student survey, 86% of the fifth graders supported moving to Bell Middle School. 
     
  5. Three meetings have been held with staff and community.  In February a parent advisory committee considered options for the crowding at Mitchell.  On March 4, 2013 a community meeting was held to answer questions and again examine the options for the overcrowding.  On March 12, 2013 the advisory committee met again and invited those members of the community to join them to determine a recommendation to the superintendent. 
     
  6. The options considered included the following:
  • Land in the Table Rock subdivision was set aside for a new school, but a bond is needed to build an approximate $15M elementary school
  • Moving boundary lines for enrollment would require changing neighborhood school lines to the north
  • Additional building at Mitchell would mean bringing in another Temporary Bldg (Dry Temp- no water/sewer) on the Upper Playground (by the bike cages) and no other facility enhancements would be made
  • 6th Grade moving to Bell MS for Mitchell only for next school-year and/or subsequent school years
  1. Building a new school was eliminated from the options because of the necessity of passing a bond election to provide funding for building a new school.  Boundary changes were not a viable option because the schools along the Highway 93 corridor are all experiencing growth and/or overcrowding with the new developments.  The two options considered at the final meeting were bringing in one more “dry” temp or moving the sixth grade to Bell Middle.
