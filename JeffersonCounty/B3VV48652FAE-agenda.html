6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Oct 4, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:32 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   

 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Recommended Action
To be informed of supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for October 2018.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.   

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) awarded year 4 of a 4 year EARSS grant to Student Engagement. The $102,498 will address goals related to preventing repeat behavior (suspension/ expulsion) and improving attendance and academic standing of identified youth. Case managers will provide intensive support to students and families, assisting them in navigating school and community resources, including substance abuse assessments and counseling services. Student Engagement Director Dave Kollar is the project manager.
     
  2. Student Engagement received $420,475 from the CDE Student Re-Engagement Grant Program. This is an additional year of funding of a multi-year grant. Funds support activities and strategies focused on student engagement and student re-engagement leading to improved postsecondary and workforce readiness. Participating schools are Jeffco Virtual Academy, Alameda International Junior/Senior High, Brady Alternative High School, Jefferson Junior/Senior High, and Sobesky Academy. Efforts include targeted attendance and behavioral interventions, social-emotional programming, increased opportunities for credit recovery and community/family engagement programming. This initiative ties to Strategic Plan Objective Learning. Student Engagement Director Dave Kollar oversees services.
     
  3. Colorado Creative Industries awarded $25,000 to the Title VI Indian Education Program in the Office of Student Engagement. The annual award supports a multi-disciplinary, afterschool and summer arts program with professional artists. Field trips for students and families are incorporated. The intent of the initiative, tied to Learning and Readiness for Learning, is to engage students in their education and build a connection with their traditional culture through visual and performing arts. Indian Education Coordinator LeRoy Saiz from the Student Engagement Office oversees the program.
     
  4. Student Services received $825,164 from the CDEโs School Health Professional Grant Program. Eleven elementary schools and three high schools, located in Jefferson, Lakewood, and Wheat Ridge articulation areas will receive education to address, prevent and decrease substance abuse, specifically related to the legalization of marijuana. Students with substance abuse and other behavioral needs will receive coordinated referral care. Funds will pay salaries of a Social Emotional Learning Specialist (half time) at each elementary school and a full-time nurse at each high school. The project supports Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning, Social-Emotional Support. Student Services Director Adric Arndt manages the program.
     
  5. The Jay and Rose Phillips Family Foundation of Colorado awarded $50,000 to the Early Learning Department for the Jeffco Bright Futures Roadmap planning work. This collective plan will leverage community-wide assets to ensure more children have access to preschool opportunities and the support they need to engage in the learning process. The initiative aligns with Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning, Expand Early Childhood Quantity and Quality. Director of Early Learning Dawn Odean oversees the project.
     
  6. The CDE awarded a $30,000 Comprehensive Health Education grant to Healthy Schools. This final award of a three-year grant will allow a health education content specialist, partially funded by this grant, to work with curriculum teams on curriculum development, implementation, professional development and support in addressing the Comprehensive Health Education Standards for health-specific units. The initiative addresses Strategic Plan Objectives Conditions for Learning through support of school-based health and wellness teams, and Readiness for Learning because healthy choices increase student access to learning. Healthy Schools Coordinator Emily OโWinter is the project manager.
     
  7. Kaiser Permanente funds will support employee wellness. A staff wellness retreat and up to 10 water bottle filling stations at select schools will be funded with $38,000. Healthy Schools Coordinator Emily OโWinter will oversee the projects.
     
  8. The Colorado Health Foundation awarded a capital grant of $237,690 to Health Services to fund the build-out of space at Golden High School for health clinic. Staff from Metro Community Provider Network (MCPN) will provide health care services. A provision of the funding is that the clinic will serve low-income, vulnerable individuals (birth to age 21) for at least five years after opening. School health services support Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning. Health Services Director Julie Wilken will manage the project.

7.11 Contract Award: Bus and Vehicle Purchases (EL-7)
Meeting
Oct 4, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to enter into agreement(s) with awarded vendors to acquire school buses, Type A buses and support vehicles in an amount not to exceed the approved budget including previous yearโs carryover.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7 Asset Protection, “...the superintendent may not fail to use a competitive bidding procedure for all contracted services and purchases of supplies, materials and equipment in the amount of $100,000 or more..."
     
  2. The purchase of school buses, Type A passenger buses and support vehicles occur as marked for replacement based on age, mileage, maintenance history costs and as needed for additional services. From time to time, additional buses are purchased to support student transportation at the school level or student special needs. The Transportation department annually replaces a percentage of school buses scheduled for disposal at end of life. These units are auctioned in accordance with district policy.
     
  3. The buses are purchased from a bid or Request For Proposal (RFP) award and the support vehicles are purchased from Colorado State vehicle awards or various bids/quotes.
     
  4. Purchases do not exceed budget or available funds for this annual capital investment. The fleet replacement schedule is a component of the Transportation department’s long-term plan and, depending on factory demand, bus orders will be filled within three to nine months. Upon approval, the order is adjusted (number, size, equipment options, quantity, etc.) to maximize the spend value and meet district transportation needs. Ordering buses at the beginning of the school year allows delivery mid-year which optimizes the number of buses for end of school year academic trips, sports events and event shuttles.
     
  5. District savings are gained through maintenance cost avoidance, lower fuel costs through the use of propane powered buses and more efficient diesel engine technologies. Student safety is enhanced when the bus fleet is consistently replaced with new buses due to updated safety regulations.
