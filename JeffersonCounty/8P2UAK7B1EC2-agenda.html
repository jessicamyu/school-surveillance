PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive information from Lorie Gillis, Kathleen Askelson and Clifton Gunderson on the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2011 in compliance with Board executive policy EL-6, Financial Administration.  

 

BACKGROUND:

Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 29-1-606 (1)(b) requires that the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report be delivered to the Board of Education by November 30, 2011. The Board received a copy prior to November 30, 2011.  Colorado Revised Statute 22-32-109 requires the district prepare a comprehensive audited financial report. The financial report consists of financial information prepared by the district and audited by an independent firm and indicates the financial status of the district at the end of the reporting period. It also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

PRESENTING STAFF / COMMUNITY:

Cindy Stevenson, superintendent

Dan Cohan, community superintendent

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

 

OELS Community Task Force Members

David Epp, principal, Mt. Evans OELS

Robb Gneiser, principal, Windy Peak OELS

Ryan Lucas, OELS alumni and member, OELS Foundation Board

Darl Hobson, Alameda West Kiwanis

Kelly Johnson, parent and member, PTA

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to understand the history of the outdoor lab education program in Jeffco schools, the educational services and experience offered to Jeffco students, as well as the financial and sustainability plan of the program so that the Board can work in partnership with the Outdoor Lab Foundation to support the continued existence of the outdoor lab educational experience for Jeffco students.

 

BACKGROUND:

In recent years, as the Jefferson County School District Board of Education has been faced with tough budget decisions, the Outdoor Lab Foundation has been tasked with making up the shortfall to ensure the Outdoor Lab program continues to serve all of the Jefferson County School District sixth grade students.  Last March, this task became even larger, requiring the Outdoor Lab Foundation to raise $450,000 by December of 2011.   To date the Outdoor Lab Foundation, in partnership with the Jefferson Foundation, has met its commitment.   In support of the district, the Task Force and Foundation are committed to raising funds to assist with the capital needs of the Outdoor Lab Schools as identified in the recently completed needs assessments.  This update will share more about the business and sustainability plan for the Outdoor Lab Schools.

Becky Feuerstein, director of Early Childhood
Catherine Baldwin-Johnson, director of ESL and Dual Language

 

PURPOSE: 
For the Board of Education to receive an update on Colorado State Standards in Jeffco schools.

 

BACKGROUND: 
As required by CAP4K, also known as Senate Bill 08-212 or the Colorado Achievement Plan for Kids, Colorado’s academic standards have been revised and adopted by the State Board of Education.  Jeffco’s Division of Instruction staff have reviewed and revised, as necessary, the Jeffco standards prior to the December 15, 2011 deadline in order for Jeffco’s curriculum to meet or exceed state standards.

 

The Board President requested that the superintendent provide a report to the Board on this work in order to show compliance with CAP4K requirements.
