2.01 Retreat: School Panel on Innovative Practices within Jeffco Schools (EL-11)
Meeting
Apr 16, 2015 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 5:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 hours) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Terry Elliott, chief school effectiveness officer

Rhonda Hatch-Rivera, principal, Lumberg Elementary

Kristen McCann, teacher librarian, Lumberg

Jennifer McCabe, 1st grade teacher, Lumberg

Amy Sward, 2nd grade teacher, Lumberg

Stephanie Woodhouse, 5th grade teacher, Lumberg

Sandy Craig, principal, Pennington Elementary

Kym O'Donnell, Expanded Learning Tim coordinator, Pennington

Amy McDaniel, parent liaison, Pennington

Ryan West, principal, Falcon Bluffs Middle

Allyn Atadero, PE teacher, FBMS

Rob Longbrake, counselor, FBMS

Bridget Jones, principal, Bell Middle

Rob Hoover, principal, Deer Creek Middle

Matt Flores, executive director, Education Research and Design

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to explore innovations in public education occurring within Jeffco Schools.  Programs of innovation will be shared from Lumberg Elementary on their Technology innovation, Pennington Elementary on their Time innovation, Falcon Bluffs Middle School on their SPARK program, and Bell/Deer Creek Middle schools on their STEM programs.

 

BACKGROUND:

The Board discussed having a retreat focused on innovation and creativity in the delivery of public education.  The school panel is the first of a four hour, two-panel discussion set.

 

Each school will provide a 15-minute overview of its innovation leaving an hour of discussion with the Board of Education.

PRESENTING STAFF:

Helen Neal, chief of staff for the Board and Superintendent

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education annually reviews a draft Board of Education meeting schedule for the next school year prior to formal approval.

BACKGROUND:

A draft schedule is prepared taking into consideration the following when possible: 1st/3rd Thursdays, first meeting after school begins, every other Thursday, avoiding known conflicts (holidays religious and otherwise, school breaks, conferences).

The Board of Education will adopt a schedule of regular and special meetings for the next school year at the regular meeting of May 7, 2015.
