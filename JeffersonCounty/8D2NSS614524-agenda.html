PRESENTING STAFF:
Cindy Stevenson, superintendent
Ed Bowditch, contract lobbyist, Tomlinson & Associates

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to discuss its legislative platform to provide direction to the contract lobbyist for the 2011 legislative session.

BACKGROUND:
Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, requires that the Board's positions on pending legislation and regulations be communicated to members of the Colorado General Assembly and the Executive Branch of state government. It has been the Board's practice to schedule special meetings to discuss pending legislation during the legislative session.  On September 17, those dates were set for January 20, February 24, March 17, April 7 and 21, 2011.

The 2011 legislative session will produce a number of bills of interest to Jefferson County Public Schools. The district contract lobbyist will bring legislation to the Board of Education for positions and direction. The district’s contract lobbyist provides state legislators with the Board’s positions on legislation.

Peg Kastberg, community superintendent

Jose Martinez, community superintendent

Priscilla Straughn, executive director, Department for Learning and Educational Achievement

Carol Eaton, executive director, Instructional Data Services



PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive the monitoring information requested to determine district performance toward meeting the Board’s Ends or goals 1 through 6, listed below.


BACKGROUND:
On October 7 and November 4, 2010, the Board of Education discussed with staff what information would be useful to board members to be able to monitor district performance. That information will be shared tonight.
 

  • Every student will master the Colorado Content Standards at grade level.
  • Every student will achieve one year’s growth or more as needed to ‘catch up’ in every year of school and be ready for the next level.
  • Every student will graduate career and workforce and/or post secondary ready.
  • Every student will learn in a caring, safe, and engaging school environment that maximizes parental involvement and encourages community support.
  • Every student will become a responsible citizen.
  • Every student will be taught by an effective teacher in a school led by an effective principal.

Cindy Stevenson, superintendent
Cabinet
Cheryl Humann, executive director, Facilities Planning and Construction
 

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to discuss 2011 Non-Bond recommendations of the preliminary facilities master plan.

BACKGROUND:
The Board of Education received the preliminary facilities master plan on January 6, 2011.
 

On January 13, the Board agreed to discuss the 2011 Non-Bond recommendations of $3.5 million prior to holding community meetings on the plan’s recommendations.  Board members agree to provide their questions on the 2011 non-bond recommendation to the superintendent by Tuesday, January 18.


The plan provides facilities recommendations ranging from general renovation projects to phased replacements to consolidations and closures. The Plan will be used in preparation for the next Capital Improvement Plan as well as short term facilities changes that will provide for the most efficient and effective use of its buildings.
