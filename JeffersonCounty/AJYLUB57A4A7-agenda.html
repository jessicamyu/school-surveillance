PRESENTING STAFF:

Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Nicole Stewart, manager-budget & analysis, Financial Services

Suzi Thompson, manager-treasury & analysis, Financial Services

 

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on the state fiscal outlook, school finance and Jeffco funding that will include budget priorities as identified by Cabinet, district leadership, community and staff engagement; and, to provide final direction on priorities and placeholders to direct preparation of the 2017/2018 proposed budget for the planned public hearing on April 20.

 

BACKGROUND:

Discussion on development of the 2017/2018 budget began in October 2016 with additional discussions at board meetings on November 17, 2016, December 1, 2016, January 26, 2017 and February 9, 2017.

On February 9, the Board of Education provided initial direction on the recommendations from district leadership for reductions and reallocations that were presented at the January 26 board meeting to address funding needs. A summary of that direction, which has been revised to separate out items that the Board directed be deferred and/or removed from consideration for reduction or reallocation, is attached below.

The Board has received input on budget priorities from the community through engagement at the Now Boarding Board of Education forums, public comment at board meetings, correspondence to the Board, results from the community online interactive budget tool exercise and four telephone town hall sessions, as well as information on priorities identified by school-level accountability committees (SAC) that was reviewed by the budget subcommittee of the District Accountability Committee (DAC) and presented to the Board by the DAC. 

Board budget priorities for 2017/2018 will be in line with the Board of Education goals in Ends 1, Ends 2, Ends 3.

Ed Bowditch, district lobbyist

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive and discuss an update on legislative activities in the 2017 Colorado Legislative Session and finalize its revision to the 2017 Legislative Platform.
 

BACKGROUND:

On March 9, the Board suggested a revision to its statement of principles and priorities, the 2017 Legislative Platform.  This document provides direction to the district lobbyist in the absence of direct Board discussion on any legislative matter during the Legislative Session.

 

Board governance process policy GP-4, Board Job Description, outlines how "the Board may, by motion or resolution, take a position of advocacy on national, statewide or local ballot issues, referred measures, legislation or rules and regulations" and further that, "the Board may, by motion or resolution, initiate action which leads to preparation and introduction of legislation deemed appropriate and important to the education mission of the Jefferson County School District."


This is the Board's third discussion of legislation this session.

Recommended Action
approve the lease agreement between Doral Academy Building Corporation and Jefferson County School District R-1 for the 2017-18 school year; and, to authorize the executive director of Facilities and Construction Management to execute the lease.

estimated time: 6:50 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. The district was approached by Doral Academy Building Corporation regarding a one-year lease of the Zerger Elementary School, 9050 Field Street, Westminster, CO 80020, for the 2017-18 school year. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, and district policy DN, School Properties Disposition, 'Surplus real property shall be sold or conveyed by the Board of Education upon such terms and conditions as it may approve.' The lease agreement is brought forward for Board conveyance of the property pursuant to the terms of the lease.
     
  2. The district and Doral Academy Building Corporation negotiated specific terms and conditions of the lease as follows:
    1. Maintenance, operations and utility costs incurred by the district will be reimbursed by Doral Academy Building Corporation.
    2. Maintenance, operations and utility costs are $4.50 per SF, or an annual cost to Doral Academy Building Corporation of $197,442.
    3. District will provide a facility manager and custodian which will be reimbursed by Doral Academy Building Corporation consistent with item a. above.
    4. Using district staff, the district will provide general maintenance services, security, fire alarm services, site maintenance and pay utilities consisting of electricity, natural gas, water, sewer and stormwater.  Costs will be reimbursed by Doral Academy Building Corporation consistent with item a. above.
    5. Doral Academy Building Corporation will sublease the building to Doral Academy of Colorado.  Doral Academy Building Corporation assumes responsibility for meeting the terms and conditions of the lease.
       
  3. Doral Academy Building Corporation will be entitled to the use of the building for one-year provided they are in compliance with the terms and conditions of the lease.
     
  4. Doral Academy Building Corporation understands that the building is for sale and listed by a commercial real estate broker and agrees not to subject the building to conditions that could jeopardize an eventual sale.
