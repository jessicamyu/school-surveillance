4.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Oct 4, 2012 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda only. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Oct 4, 2012 - Regular Meeting
Category

Recommended Action
to adopt the proclamation honoring Jefferson County school principals in declaring October as National Principals Month.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Parents may realize the complexity of the job of a school principal, however, fewer members of the larger Jefferson County community have reason to know the vital responsibilities carried out each school year by Jeffco’s elementary, middle level and senior high principals.  
     
  2. To highlight the tireless work of school principals, several national associations worked together to declare October as National Principal Month across the United States.
     
  3. Governor John Hickenlooper has proclaimed October in Colorado as National Principal Month.
     
  4. Principals lead Jeffco schools in order to meet the Board of Education goal, every student will be taught by an effective teacher in a school led by an effective principal so that they are prepared for continuous learning and the world of work in the changing environment of the 21st century.
     
  5. The Board of Education will honor the work of its school principals on behalf of the more than 85,000 students (more than 10% of the state’s population), their parents, grandparents, neighbors and employers by adopting the proclamation attached below.

estimated time: 7:52 p.m.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.

At this time, in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
