Category

estimated time: 8:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:
Ed Bowditch, district legislative liaison, Bowditch & Cassell Public Affairs

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive its regularly scheduled update on legislative activities as the 2021 legislative session begins later today.

BACKGROUND:
 

In accordance with Board governance process policy GP-4, Board Job Description, the Board of Education, “may, by motion or resolution, take a position of advocacy on national, statewide or local ballot issues, referred measures, legislation or rules and regulations. The Board may, by motion or resolution, initiate action which leads to preparation and introduction of legislation deemed appropriate and important to the education mission of the Jefferson County School District.”
 

As the 2021 Colorado Legislative Session convenes today, this is the Board's first discussion of legislative activity this session.

Subject
2.02 TeamUp Jeffco/Learning Communities Support (EL-11)
Meeting
Jan 13, 2021 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time:  9:00 a.m. (estimated duration:  45 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dan Cohan, interim chief of schools – secondary, School Leadership

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools - elementary, School Leadership

Jean Boylan, co-founder and program director, TeamUp Jeffco

Katie Winner, co-founder, TeamUp Jeffco

Brenda Fletcher, principal, Moore Middle School

Wendy Doran, community superintendent, School Leadership

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will receive an update and have the opportunity to ask questions about the status of the TeamUp Jeffco program as well as other ways schools and communities are working together to re-engage students.

 

BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education has been informed of the TeamUp Jeffco program and has asked about the status of this district/community partnership.

 

At the December 16 Board of Education study session, the Board indicated its interest in being apprised of the status of student re-engagement and TeamUp Jeffco efforts in January 2021.

 

On January 7, the Board of Education received a presentation on the Jeffco Restart Evaluation Plan.

 

TeamUp Jeffco is an example of community/school district partnership to re-engage students in remote and hybrid learning.
