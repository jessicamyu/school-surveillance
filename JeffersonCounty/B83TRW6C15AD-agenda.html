The Colorado Department of Education provides the following overview of the Healthy Kids Colorado Survey:

The Healthy Kids Colorado Survey is the state’s only comprehensive survey on the health and well-being of young people. The purpose of this survey is to better understand youth health and the factors that help young people make healthy choices. The Survey is widely used by schools, districts and communities in their efforts to grow the healthiest youth in the country. The results of the survey can help:

     • Provide direction for schools and communities to address health issues.

     • Inform parents on relevant topics to help them talk to their children about their health and well-being.

     • Secure youth health program funding for schools, community organizations, and local and state government agencies.

2.04 One-to-One Device Initiative (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 13, 2019 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session

estimated time: 9:50 a.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

PRESENTING STAFF:
Brett T. Miller, chief information officer

Matt Flores, chief academic offier

Shawn Rhoades, director, Enterprise Client Technology Services

Mary Beth Bazzanella, director, Educational Technology

PURPOSE:
As a follow-up to discussion at the January 16, 2019 Board of Education study session, staff is providing the Board of Education with additional information on the 1-1 Initiative and a high level plan of how it will roll out.

 

BACKGROUND:

The Jeffco Strategic Plan calls for a Learning-Centered Vision for our schools focused on providing authentic, relevant, and engaging learning experiences for all students. In support of our strategic plan, the 1:1 Device and Technology to Transform Learning tactics outline the following theories of action.

  • Technology to Transform Learning Theory of Action: IF schools provide integrated technology instruction aligned to the Jeffco Generation skills THEN students will experience expanded, flexible learning opportunities that prepare them to thrive in a connected digital world.
  • 1:1 Device Theory of Action: IF we provide equitable and sustainable options for school technology THEN all schools can provide access to technology for all 5th through 12th grade students.

High Level plan:

  • The District 1:1 initiative will cover grades 5-12 and will take 4 years to implement for all students
  • We will use a combination of 5A funding and student fees and general fund transfers to make the program sustainable
  • The 5A funding covers the cost for all Free and Reduced status students
  • Students that receive a device will pay a yearly $50.00 "Technology Usage" fee that covers the Device, Online tools, Support, PD, internet filtering, and bandwidth.
  • Schools will have device choice between iPad and Touch Screen Chromebook
  • The program will begin in August of 2019 and will be rolled out in 3 phases. Phase 1 in August, Phase 2 in November and Phase 3 in January. Phases will be determined by school readiness.Schools will receive devices based on their 5th and 9th grade student count. We will use previous year October count and true up based on actual enrollment utilizing the +5% devices we order.

 

Articulation Area:

  • Articulation Area leadership will meet as a group to determine how devices are distributed within the articulation area
    • Discussions will include current 1-1 implementations, current fee structure, school need, articulation needs, teacher readiness, school readiness, community readiness, and how to utilize existing technology.
  • Articulation areas will determine and agree on a consistent device type within articulation area for students that will transition between school
  • All articulation areas must submit a 4 year plan that gets to a sustainable model that aligns district 1:1 model where all students have a device within 4 years.
