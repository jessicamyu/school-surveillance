6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Dec 10, 2015 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 7:10 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   
 

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour. Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

7.08 Resolution: Authorizing the District to Enter into a Line of Credit (EL-6)
Meeting
Dec 10, 2015 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to adopt the resolution authorizing the district to enter into a short term line of credit in an amount not to exceed $15 million.
Goals
EL-6, Financial Administration

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, states that, "With respect to the actual, ongoing financial condition and activities of the district, the superintendent shall not cause nor allow fiscal jeopardy or any fiscal condition that is inconsistent with achieving the priorities established in Board's Ends policies."
     
  2. As outlined in a memo that was distributed to the Board of Education in the November 20, 2015, Weekly Update, the district has cash obligations that must be met throughout the fiscal year. However, the majority of property tax revenues are received in the last four months of the fiscal year (March-June).
     
  3. Due to the mismatch of timing between ongoing expenses and revenue collections (cash outflows and inflows), the district requires a contingency source of cash to ensure the district’s ability to meet operating obligations, including employee salaries.
     
  4. Staff is recommending a line of credit (LOC) because it is the lowest cost, temporary (short term) instrument available. The district utilized a LOC as a contingency source of cash in 2014/2015; however, the LOC was never drawn against and was closed on March 12, 2015, after receipt of property tax revenue.
     
  5. The district is requesting authorization to establish a LOC in order to provide temporary (short-term) cash coverage for district operations prior to the receipt of property tax revenue in the spring of 2016.
     
  6. Depending on actual cash activity over the next few months, use of the established LOC may not be necessary. If any amount is drawn against the LOC, the full amount will be repaid following the receipt of property tax revenue. The LOC will be closed prior to June 1, 2016.
     
  7. The district will leverage the current banking relationship with U.S. Bank to establish a short-term line of credit.

8.01 Bradford K-8 School Configuration (Ends 1-3)
Meeting

8. Discussion Agenda

Recommended Action
to approve the establishment of the Bradford K-8 school for the Chatfield Articulation Area by allowing the current Bradford K-6 program to expand to include 7th grade programming beginning with the 2016-17 school year and 8th grade programming beginning with the 2017-18 school year.

estimated time: 8:10 p.m.  (estimated duration: 10 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In 2003, the Bradford parent community researched and proposed a Bradford K-8 program for consideration by Jeffco Public Schools.  While the program was not implemented, interest in the concept has continued and today the administration, district staff, and parent community are all in support of adding 7th and 8th grade programming to Bradford Elementary School.
     
  2. Sixth grade programming was added to Falcon Bluffs Middle School in 2005 for all Schaffer Elementary School students and has expanded over the years to include additional 6th grade students outside of the Schaffer boundary.
     
  3. Sixth grade STEM programming was added to Deer Creek Middle School in 2014 for interested students.
     
  4. Today, the Chatfield articulation area has a fragmented matriculation pathway for elementary students as they move from 5th grade to 6th grade.
     
  5. Area principals are working to develop a comprehensive plan to bring stability and multiple pathways to students in the area.
     
  6. A key element of this plan is the creation of the Bradford K-8 School.
     
  7. Bradford Primary and Intermediate would be reconfigured into Bradford K-8, an experiential learning program to serve the Bradford boundary area with additional seats provided for interested families through the choice enrollment process. 
     
  8. Bradford boundary area students would be able to choice enroll into other middle school options if desired.  Excess 'seats' would be made available to students who reside outside the Bradford boundary interested in the program.
     
  9. Enrollment would be limited to ensure no expansion to the current facilities is required (approximately 90 students per grade level).
     
  10. The proposed Bradford K-8 enables efficient use of current resources and does not require an investment in physical plants nor long-term ongoing funding.
     
  11. The school has actively engaged, and been driven by, parent community input.  Including a town hall meeting in September, 5th and 6th grade parent meeting in October, and a comprehensive survey, drafted by the School Accountability Committee and constructed by the Jeffco Assessment department was conducted in the fall.
     
  12. Additional discussions have occurred with a subcommittee of parents, administrators, and staff during the Spring and Fall of 2015 as part of the program development.
     
  13. School staff has participated in the development and design of the proposed program.
     
  14. The proposed changes are aligned to the Jeffco 2020 Vision, Strategic Plan, and in support of Board Ends policies.
