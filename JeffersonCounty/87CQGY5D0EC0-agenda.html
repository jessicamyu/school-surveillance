At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

7.01 Green Mountain High School Academies for 2009/2010 (EL-11)
Meeting
Mar 5, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
7. Discussion Agenda
Type
Discussion
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.In November of 2007, a Future Search was conducted at Green Mountain Senior High School to identify a long range vision for Green Mountain Senior. Over 75 people attended including staff, administrators, parents, students, community members, elected officials, Board of Education members, and district leadership.
2.Following that meeting, action teams were formed with the focus to increase student achievement and graduation rates by developing rigor, relevance, and relationships in the delivery of Jeffco curriculum to the students at Green Mountain. Two Board members have been involved in the process. The end result of the community’s work was the concept of “academies” with a focus on career paths.
3.Academic instruction at Green Mountain Senior will continue to be based on the district curriculum and instructional program. Students will not be in academies for all classes but select electives that are aligned with a career cluster (Health and Human Services, Business and Global Studies, Arts, Humanities and Performance, Math, Science and Technology, Renaissance). Students are not “separated” into academies. Classes in the core curriculum will provide opportunities to pursue and explore projects that align with a career focus.
4.On February 12 during discussion of the Board of Education work calendar and superintendent’s weekly updates, the Board requested that the superintendent provide information on the structure of academies, as the concept will be used at Green Mountain High School beginning in fall 2009.
5.Peg Kastberg, community superintendent for the Green Mountain area, will present information on the selection of this career oriented design to deliver Jeffco curriculum and instruction at Green Mountain High School.
