PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to discuss aspects of the district’s finances with members of the Financial Oversight Committee and to hear from staff on the districtwide financial condition through the first quarter of the 2017/2018 school year in order to monitor district finances.

 

BACKGROUND:

Each quarter, in compliance with Board executive limitation policies EL-5, Financial Planning and Budgeting and EL-6, Financial Administration and as outlined in Board governance process policy GP-13, Committee Structure, the Board meets to review and monitor quarterly financial reports. Staff identifies the funds, departments and/or schools to monitor closely throughout the year. Members of the Financial Oversight Committee attend quarterly financial updates for the opportunity to provide a report to and dialogue with the Board of Education.

EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting

estimated time: 5:20 p.m. (estimated duration: 50 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:
Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Nicole Stewart, director-budget and treasury


PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive an update on the state outlook/governor's proposed budget, school finance and use of prior year funding increases and to provide initial direction on budget development for the 2018/2019 school year. 

 

BACKGROUND:

The governor's proposed budget was released in early November. Funding estimates through the School Finance Act are based on estimated October enrollment counts. Staff continues to work with schools and district leadership on funding requests through the processes of Student Based Budgeting (SBB) for schools and Budgeting for Outcomes (BFO) for departments to support the district's strategic plan. 

 

A timeline for development of the 2018/2019 budget and recommendation for community engagement were provided to the Board of Education on October 6, 2017. The Financial Oversight Committee reviewed the SBB and BFO budgeting models and concur that these methods support the strategic mission of the district and ensure solid fiscal management per the recommendations in the attached reports.

2.03 General Obligation Bond Refunding (EL-5, 6)
Meeting
Nov 16, 2017 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 6:10 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)


PRESENTING STAFF:

Brian Kelso, managing director of Public Finance, Robert Baird & Co.

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive market information on ratings and interest rates for refunding existing general obligations bonds that would yield a net present value savings for the school district in order to provide direction to staff regarding a request for action by the Board of Education on December 7, 2017.  

 

BACKGROUND:

The district's finance team monitors interest rate conditions in an ongoing basis to evaluate opportunities for refinancing all or a portion of the district's outstanding general obligation bonds.  A favorable interest rate environment was exhibited in 2017 which initiated discussion among the finance team and investment bankers. After analysis, it was determined that a portion of the district's maturities of the Series 2012 Bonds can be refinanced to realize a significant savings for the outstanding term of the bonds.
