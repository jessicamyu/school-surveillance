Apr 5, 2018 - Regular Meeting

6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:26 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Recommended Action
to approve the final version of the District's Unified Improvement Plan, the Gifted Program Addendum, and three priority improvement or turnaround school Unified Improvement Plans for submission to the Colorado Department of Education (CDE) to be posted on the CDE SchoolView.org website.
Goals
Ends 3 - Student Achievement
Ends 2 - Student Achievement

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) requires all public school districts and schools to complete a Unified Improvement Plan (UIP). These improvement plans are designed to identify priority challenges and develop major improvement strategies to meet these needs.  While Jeffco is a high performing school district that consistently meets state accreditation expectations, the CDE expects the district's UIP to focus on areas for improvement.
     
  2. To that end, the district has worked closely with the District Accountability Committee (DAC) to develop the 2017-2018 District Unified Improvement Plan.  Members of the DAC UIP subcommittee include: Deb Guiducci, deputy chair; Margaret Lessenger, vice chair; Jim Earley, Matt Flores, Carol Eaton, Melissa Martini, Jill Fellman, Thomas Dougherty, Lorri Avery, Hi Howard, Susan Lopez, Jef Fugita, Cecily Klein, Marna Messer, and Jennifer Steele.
     
  3. The improvement planning process included a comprehensive analysis of district level data, the root cause analysis to focus on system improvements and development of an action plan aligned to the priority student performance challenges.
     
  4. Throughout the school year, Jeffco's District Accountability Committee will continue to partner with the district to provide input and feedback on the implementation of the district's Unified Improvement Plan.
     
  5. The Unified Improvement Plans for the three schools in Jeffco identified as priority improvement or turnaround:  Arvada K-8, Jefferson Jr./Sr., and Moore Middle School, were provided to the Board of Education prior to the April 5 Board of Education Meeting.
     
  6. After being approved by the Board of Education, the district will submit the District Unified Improvement Plan and the aditional documents identified above to the Colorado Department of Education by April 2018, as required by CDE.

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the superintendent and board of education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. 1. Jefferson Junior/Senior High School received $18,000 from Morgridge Family Foundation and mindSpark Learning (mSL) to deliver a learning experience for educators in the Jefferson High articulation area including: Jefferson Jr/Sr High; Lumberg ES; Edgewater ES; and Molholm ES. Professional learning and training for all certified staff members in Project Based Learning (PBL) classroom practice. Principal Michael James will oversee the project.

 

2. Jefferson Junior/Senior High School received $21,275 from Morgridge Family Foundation and mindSpark Learning (mSL) to work in collaboration towards creating a professional development opportunity, and offer a STEM Summer Institute for certified staff members for the Jefferson High articulation area. Principal Michael James will oversee the project.

 

3. Two Roads Charter received $20,000 from the Colorado Department of Education’s Connect for Success Grant Program as the first year’s allocation of a grant that will total $180,000 over the next three years. The grant will offer Two Roads an opportunity to build an action/implementation turnaround plan, address high student mobility, and insure data-driven instruction supported by an implementation coach and staff development. Principal Wendy Noel will oversee the project. 

 

4. The School Innovation and Effectiveness Team received $204,000 from the Colorado Department of Education’s School Turnaround Leaders Development Grant Program. Leading a building out of turnaround status (priority improvement or turnaround (PITA) as indicated on the School Performance Framework, or SPF) takes a special set of skills and expertise. School and district leaders will complete CDE identified leadership development programs that support turnaround efforts. Karen Quanbeck, chief of schools-elementary, will oversee the project.

 

5. The School Innovation and Effectiveness Team received $672,568 from the Colorado Department of Education’s District Design and Led Improvement Strategies Grant Program. Funds will allow the district to expand district-designed supports for schools in need of improvement using multiple strategies focused on rapid school improvement (instructional coaches, induction coordinator, signing bonuses, revised hiring processes, staff development). Karen Quanbeck, chief of schools-elementary, will oversee the project.
