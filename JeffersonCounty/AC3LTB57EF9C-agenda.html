estimated time: 5:30 p.m.  (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in a Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.     
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on the topic of a potential bond and/or mill levy override question on the November 8, 2016 election ballot related to this evening's agenda for up to one hour. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings. Thank you.

Subject
3.01 Update on Lead in Water at Jeffco Schools (EL-7, 11)
Meeting
Jul 28, 2016 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
3. Discussion Agenda
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Daniel M. McMinimee, superintendent

Tim Reed, executive director, Facilities and Planning

Karen Minteer, environmental health specialist, Environmental Services

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on the work being done over the summer to ensure safe drinking water sources in each Jeffco school.

 

BACKGROUND:

Jeffco’s Environmental Services Department felt it would be prudent to test Jeffco Public Schools’ drinking water sources due to heightened awareness of this issue in older public buildings and its priority for a healthy, safe environment for students and staff.

 

The median age of Jeffco schools is 45 years and all schools' testing began with the oldest schools.

 

Before school starts on August 18, samples will be collected from all Jeffco elementary schools built before 1990. Any drinking source found to be elevated above acceptable limits will not be used as a source of drinking water until the lead level is corrected. Bottled water will be provided to the school as needed.

3.02 Review of 2016 Bond Issue and Mill Levy Override: Finance Modeling and Content (EL-11)
Meeting
Jul 28, 2016 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
3. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to direct the superintendent to prepare ballot language for approval on August 2 as discussed for bond issue and mill levy override questions on the November 8, 2016 election ballot.

estimated time: 6:40 p.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. On June 16, the Board of Education reviewed proposed bond issue packages and a mill levy override proposal, settling on a $535 million bond amount to address identified facilities needs and a $33 million mill levy override to address general operational needs unmet through the level of state funding expected for Jeffco Public Schools in the immediate and future years.
     
  2. Specifically, Jeffco has not received approximately $490 million dollars which otherwise would have gone to educate and support Jeffco students since the implementation in 2009 of the negative factor in school financing.
     
  3. To move forward as directed by the Board in June, Chief Operating Officer Steve Bell and Chief Financial Officer Kathleen Askelson will review debt modeling options to meet the requirements of the Taxpayers Bill of Rights (TABOR) to confirm with the Board its direction on financing of bonds and the content of both the bond issue and mill levy override prior to preparing ballot language.
