Meeting
Sep 18, 2008 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m., Board Room
Category

Type
Information
LOCATION: Seminar Room, 4th floor

PRESENTING STAFF:
None

PURPOSE:
To conduct a screening interview with the 10 applicants for the open Board of Education director district 5 seat.


BACKGROUND:
The Board declared a vacancy in director district 5 on August 21 with a deadline of September 3 for eligible applicants to apply to fill the Board seat. By law, the Board has 60 days from the declared vacancy date of August 21, 2008 to appoint a resident of Jefferson County’s school board director district 5.

On September 11, the Board determined it would interview applicants on September 18 prior to and following this evening's regular business meeting.

The Board will conduct 15 minute screening interviews with all applicants, afterwhich the Board will determine individuals to participate in final interviews. Final interviews will take place at a special meeting on October 2, 4-6 p.m. The Board will make it's appointment at the regular meeting of October 2.

The following individuals applied to fill the Board seat (their letter of application/resume are available through the links): Jamie Adler Rodriguez, Larry Eigel, Mary Everson, Tina Francone, Charles Marquez, Paula Noonan, Alonzo Rodriguez, Richard Rush, and Susan Settanni.

As of September 14, 2008, Mr. Spencer Larkin rescinded his letter of application. On September 18, Charles Marquez and Tina Francone withdrew their applications.

Category
5. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

6.02 Loan Request - Mountain Phoenix Community School (EL-13)
Meeting
Sep 18, 2008 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.According to EL-13, Charter School Application and Monitoring, the superintendent may not fail to inform the Board of Education and place on the consent agenda any loans to charter schools.
2.Mountain Phoenix Community School was granted a charter on November 2, 2006 and their contract with the school district was approved on April 26, 2007.
3.Mountain Phoenix Community School (MPCS) requests a loan of $95,000.00 from the Board of Education for the purpose of creating a fire lane and expanded off street parking to be paid back over a four year period commencing with the 2009/10 school year.
4.The creation of the fire lane will satisfy the request of the Fire Marshall for an access to the school for emergency equipment. The school is currently operating under a temporary agreement that is in effect until the access is constructed. There is an urgency to complete this project.
5.Off street parking will respond to concerns from neighbors that at certain times of the day the road is congested with parked vehicles and will alow the school to address those concerns.
6.MPCS has submitted budgets that include a repayment plan over the next five years and the plan has been reviewed by our accounting department. The budget is satisfactory, but as always is subject to the school meeting enrollment projections. This year the school is above projected enrollment and the school indicates a growing interest in the Waldorf program they offer.
7.MPCS indicates that they have applied for a grant that they feel will be awarded that will cover the cost of the fire lane and parking area. Their intention to complete the fire lane in a timely fashion for safety purposes precludes waiting for approval of the grant. If the grant is awarded, it will be used to offset the loan from the school district.
8.If approved, an addendum to the MPCS contract will be drafted that will outline the repayment plan and become a part of their contract with the district.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board Executive Limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A, B and C.

1. The U.S. Department of Education identified Jeffco Public Schools as a recipient of a Fund for the Improvement of Education (FIE) program as part of the Appropriations Act of 2008. The USDE awarded the district’s Department for Learning and Educational Achievement $310,480. The program aligns with strategic plan objective 3, which ensures students and instructional personnel use curricula, assessments, and the tools of technology aligned with academic content standards.
    2. These funds will allow the district to use 21st century digital learning environments, along with rigorous, quality curriculum aligned with content standards and highly qualified instructional practice, to provide quality credit recovery options to students in 21 high schools. District-developed and formally selected third party curriculum will be delivered through the district Blackboard online learning system to schools providing school-based and central recovery programs. Students with special needs that cannot attend the credit recovery programs in the schools will receive the same curriculum through full time online learning courses. The grant will serve grades 9-12 in 17 high schools and four option schools. It will impact teachers at these schools through staff development and instructional support.
    3. The project will be evaluated by collecting credit recovery rates, drop out data, data from administration, staff and students on program perception and effectiveness.
    4. The district is not required to continue this program.
    5. John Canuel, executive director of Educational Technology Services, is the project manager.
