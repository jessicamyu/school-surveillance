Cindy Stevenson, superintendent

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to revisit its student advisor program, discuss how student advisors would be chosen and integrated into the work of the County Student Council and Board of Education and provide direction to the superintendent.

BACKGROUND:
In the spring of 2008, the Board of Education discussed starting a student advisor program. Applications were accepted from eight students representing Board director districts 1 – 4. Student interviews were postponed due to the Board of Education vacancy in director district 5.

Now that the five-member Board of Education is in place, the Board president requested that Board members discuss how to move forward with the student advisor program.

PERTINENT FACTS:

1.In accordance with Board executive limitation policies EL-3, Staff Treatment, EL-10, Treatment of Students, and EL-12, School Safety, the followign is brought forward to the Board of Education.
2.On November 6, 2008, the superintendent submitted to the Board of Education a recommendation for dismissal of a teacher, pursuant to the provisions of the Teacher Employment, Compensation, and Dismissal Act, C.R.S., Section 22-63-101 et seq. ("The Act").
3.A hearing on the superintendent's recommendation was held before an independent hearing officer and the hearing officer has issued written findings of fact and a recommendation.
4.Pursuant to the provisions of The Act, the Board of Education must review the hearing officer's findings of fact and recommendation and enter a written order taking one of the following three actions: that the teacher be dismissed, the teacher be retained, or the teacher be placed on a one-year probation.
