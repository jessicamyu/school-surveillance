Recommended Action
To move into executive session as authorized by the following provisions of the Colorado Open Meetings Law: C.R.S. § 24-6-402(4)(c) – to meet with applicants for the position of superintendent whose identity is required to be kept confidential by C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3)(a)(XI); C.R.S. § 24-6-402(4)(g) – consideration of documents submitted by applicants for the position of superintendent which are protected by the mandatory nondisclosure provisions of the Colorado Open Records Act, C.R.S. § 24-72-204(3)(a)(XI)(A); C.R.S. § 24-6-402(4)(e) – to determine positions relative to matters that may be subject to negotiations with finalists for the position of superintendent and to discuss negotiations with district employee associations; and, C.R.S. § 24-6-402(4)(b) – to confer with legal counsel for the purpose of receiving legal advice regarding compliance with the Colorado Open Meetings Law and the Colorado Open Records Act during the superintendent selection process.

After the vote to move into executive session, this portion of the meeting is closed to the public.  The Board will reconvene in open session at approximately 3:30 p.m. in the Seminar Room.


At the conclusion of the executive session, the Board of Education will hold a public session to determine and announce the finalist(s) for the position of superintendent of Jeffco Public Schools.  The law requires that the list of finalists be made public 14 days prior to appointing or employing one of the finalists.  The announcement of finalists at this public meeting will start the 14 day period. 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dr. Bill Newman, Ray & Associates

Amy Weber, executive director, Human Resources

Brad Miller, Board attorney

Jim Branum, district legal counsel

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to interview candidates for the position of superintendent, including discussion of documents submitted by the candidates.

 

BACKGROUND:

Ray & Associates was hired by the Board of Education to provide professional Superintendent Search services to Jeffco Schools. On May 5, the Board of Education reviewed candidate materials and selected candidates to be interviewed in executive session. The Board conducted the first interview session on May 9 and continues its process at this time.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. On January 9, 2014, the Board of Education began its process to find a new superintendent for Jeffco Public Schools with the announced retirement of Dr. Cynthia Stevenson.
     
  2. On February 27, the Board secured the services of Ray and Associates to conduct the executive search which began with community input on the characteristics sought in a superintendent through an online survey and community meetings during the month of March.
     
  3. In April, the Board finalized the superintendent profile and accepted applicants through the deadline of April 25, 2014.
     
  4. At the meetings of May 5, 9 and 10, 2014, the Board of Education narrowed its search to a list of finalists. The law requires that the list of finalists be made public fourteen days prior to appointing or employing one of the finalists.
