PRESENTING STAFF:

Kristopher Schuh, chief of schools secondary

Shannon Fitzgerald, director, Enrollment Services

Jeremy Felker, executive director, Student Data Privacy & Instructional Data Reporting

Wendy Chi, research analyst, Research & Assessment

Dawn Odean, executive director, Early Learning

Mackenzie Nickum, assistant principal, Lakewood High School

 

PURPOSE:

To provide the Board of Education with an update on year one implementation of EnrollJeffco and what lies ahead for year two.

 

BACKGROUND:

At the September 6, 2018, meeting, the Board of Education approved the implementation of a new enrollment services/system for Jeffco Public Schools. EnrollJeffco was implemented to help make enrollment processes across the district more transparent, equitable, and efficient.  EnrollJeffco had two primary technology components - the online School Finder and the online enrollment application system.  Even though the implementation timeline was compressed, the EnrollJeffco team went live with the School Finder in December 2019 and then with the online enrollment and application system the following month.  Since that time, there have been many lessons learned that help to inform future enhancements and improvements to the systems, protocols, and policies, many of which are underway for the next enrollment cycle (2020/21).

5. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:20 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000.   1. Curriculum and Instruction office was awarded $11,000 by Jeffco Schools Foundation to support Graham Fletcher professional development training on math instruction. The training aligns with strategic plan objective Conditions for Learning, Professional Model of Teaching. Director of Curriculum and Instruction, Mathematics, Science and STEM Cecily Klein organized the training.
     
  2. Choice Programs received a Jeffco Schools Foundation award of $15,000 to continue four districtwide art shows (Equity and Excellence, Elementary, Middle, and High schools). Exhibits provide a public venue for recognizing achievements of student artists. Funds cover substitutes and/or additional pay, allowing art teachers to prepare for the shows. Director of Choice Programs Marna Messer and Visual Arts Director Shannon May oversee the initiative.
     
  3. The district will submit an Empowering Action for School Improvement (EASI) Grant application to the Colorado Department of Education (CDE) for resources to support schools identified for improvement through the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) and the state’s Educational Accountability Act (HB 18-1355). In the first year, approximately $750,000 will be requested for district led improvement strategies, school participation in the Turnaround Network, accountability planning, diagnostic reviews/planning and intensive professional development. Funds will allow for expansion of the district’s current EASI grant activities. Awards for the 2019/20 grant cycle are announced in January. Chief of Schools–Elementary Renee Nicothodes oversees the initiative.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
