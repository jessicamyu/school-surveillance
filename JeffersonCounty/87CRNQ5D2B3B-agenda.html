Subject
2.01 November 4, 2008 - Authorize Election for Voter Approval of Additional Local Property Tax Revenues for the District General Fund (Mill Levy Override Election) and Approve Ballot Question (GP-4)
Meeting
Aug 21, 2008 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Statutory Requirements Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.In accordance with Board policy Governance Process 4, Board Job Description, it is the job of the Board of Education to represent and lead the district by determining and demanding appropriate and excellent organizational performance.
2.The Board of Education and superintendent have strategically planned the spend-down of mill levy funds as promised to voters. Through the Budgeting for Results process, the district has redirected funds to target programs for increased student achievement. Additionally, budget reductions of over $16 million since the 2004-2005 school year have stretched the community’s mill levy funds supporting our schools.
3.The Financial Oversight Committee, a group of Jeffco citizen business leaders, and the Board’s external auditor consistently report to the Board of Education that the district’s financial administration is responsive, addresses identified concerns, and plans appropriately to manage the district’s resources. The Colorado State Treasurer has reported that funding for Colorado school districts (per the cost of living index) does not reflect the typical operational costs of school districts.
4.Conservative budget projections show that in four years, by 2012, Jeffco Public Schools will have reduced its budget by $35 million. Staff has projected where reductions will occur without additional, maintenance funding for existing programs which are closing achievement gaps among students and offering a 21st century learning environment to prepare students for a world economy. Among reductions will be increased class sizes, and fewer supports for new graduation requirements which begin with next year’s 9th graders.
5.The Board of Education gave direction to the superintendent to begin to plan for a possible mill levy override election. Data shared with the Board of Education has shown that the Jeffco community could support a $32 million mill levy override request at $2.92/month for every $100,000 in property valuation or $35/year.
6.A group of community members, Citizens for Jeffco’s Schools, will be presenting information to the Board of Education regarding their work to inform the greater Jefferson County community on the successes and needs of the school district.
7.Given this information, the administration and district legal counsel have prepared the attached proposed resolution which includes a proposed mill levy override ballot question. The ballot content is consistent with the requirements of Article X, Section 20 or the Colorado Constitution (TABOR).
8.The proposed resolution authorizes the mill levy override election and the content of the ballot question.
9.In accordance with state law, the ballot content must be certified to the Jefferson County and Broomfield City and County clerks by September 8, 2004.

Category
4. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.
