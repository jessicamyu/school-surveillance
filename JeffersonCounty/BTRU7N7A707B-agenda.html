PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In compliance with EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, “to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed and supported” and with EL-6, Financial Administration, “with respect to the actual, ongoing financial condition and activities of the district, the superintendent shall not cause nor allow fiscal jeopardy or any fiscal condition that is inconsistent with achieving the priorities established in Board's Ends policies,” the following applies.
     
  2. Voters of Jefferson and Broomfield counties in Colorado approved a $567 million bond ballot issue at the November 6, 2018, election. The first issuance of $326.49 million in proceeds for projects and to fund cost of issuance was issued in December 2018.
     
  3. Robert W. Baird & Co., the District’s investment bankers, has been monitoring the market and the interest rate available for general obligation bonds.
     
  4. Due to IRS spenddown requirements, construction costs, project schedule estimates and favorable market conditions, the district is recommending a second issuance for the remaining 2018 authorization to generate no less than $240.51 million in proceeds for capital projects and to fund cost of issuance.
     
  5. Staff, with support of district's investment bankers Robert W. Baird & Co., will meet with rating agencies, Moody’s Investors Services and Standard & Poor’s, to affirm the district's ratings. 
     
  6. Representatives from Robert W. Baird & Co., met with the Financial Oversight Committee on November 3, 2020, with the Capital Asset Advisory Committee on November 5, 2020, and with the Board of Education in study session on November 5, 2020 to provide market information, discuss the bond structure financing and a possible refinancing of two maturities in the Series 2012 refunding bond. 
     
  7. Both committees concur with the recommendation of staff to proceed with the bond sale for the second issuance and a potential taxable advance refunding of two maturities in the Series 2012 refunding bond contingent on market conditions.

Category

estimated time: 9:05 a.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes)

PRESENTING STAFF:
Kristopher Schuh, interim superintendent

members, District Cabinet

Julie Wilken, director, Health Services



PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive and discuss the plans to support students, staff, families and the greater Jeffco community as Jefferson County moves within the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment (CDPHE) COVID19 Dial Dashboard framework.

 

BACKGROUND:

Following feedback from the Board of Education during discussion of the November 5 presentation, and taking other sources of feedback and public health information into consideration, district leadership will review logistics for Jeffco Public Schools in preparation for changes to learning models.

 

Aligned with its adoption of the June 17 resolution, Affirming Fundamental Responsibilities in Response to COVID-19, the Board of Education has been kept informed as Jeffco Public Schools has moved forward to provide instruction and engagement with students. Following presentations on May 13, June 4, June 10, August 11, September 3 and November 5 with the direction provided on June 17 to maximize in-person learning to the extent possible given public health guidance, Jeffco Public Schools has had elementary PK-5 schools in 100% in-person learning and 6-12 grade secondary (middle and high) schools in a hybrid (50% in-person/remote) with K-12 options for 100% remote learning. / De acuerdo con la adopción de la resolución del 17 de junio, Afirmación de responsabilidades fundamentales en respuesta a COVID-19 , la Junta Directiva de Educación se ha mantenido informada a la vez que las Escuelas Públicas de Jeffco procedieron a proporcionar instrucción e interacción con los estudiantes. Luego de las presentaciones del 13 de mayo, 4 de junio, 10 de junio, 11 de agosto, 3 de septiembre y con la directriz presentada el 17 de junio para maximizar en lo posible la instrucción en persona dadas las pautas de salud pública, las escuelas Públicas de Jeffco tuvieron las escuelas primarias PK-5 con el 100% de la instrucción en persona y las escuelas secundarias con los grados 6-12 (intermedia y preparatoria) con una opción híbrida (50% en-persona/a distancia) con opciones de 100% de instrucción a distancia para K-12.

 

The RestartJeffco webpage provides the Jeffco Public Schools Covid-19 Dashboard, the full plan and its highlights plus resources for parents, students and staff, real-time updates of frequently asked questions for students, families and staff. / La página web RestartJeffco proporciona el Tablero de Covid-19 (Dashboard), el plan completo y sus puntos más destacados, además de recursos para padres, estudiantes y personal, novedades en tiempo real de las preguntas más frecuentes para los estudiantes, las familias y el personal.

estimated time: 9:50 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

David Bell, chief human resources officer

 

PURPOSE:

To provide the Board of Education with a review of the general planning timeline for hiring a superintendent of schools.

 

BACKGROUND:

On July 30, 2020, the Board of Education announced it would launch a national search for a superintendent. This effort includes the process of identifying and gathering information about qualified candidates. The search process is summarized in C.R.S. 24-6-402(3.5), and includes deadlines for applications, requirements for applicants, selection procedures, and the time frame for making a selection.

 

For a potential July 2021 appointment, a search typically begins no later than January. The Jeffco Public Schools search began in November in order to take full advantage of a national candidate pool.

  3. C.R.S. 22-31-129(2) requires the Board of Education to appoint a person to fill the vacancy within 60 days after the vacancy has occurred, which is January 10, 2021.
