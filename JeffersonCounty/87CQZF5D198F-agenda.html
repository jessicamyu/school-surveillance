PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive information from Lorie Gillis and Kathleen Askelson on the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2008 in compliance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration.

BACKGROUND:

Colorado Revised Statute, C.R.S. 29-1-606 (1)(b) requires that the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report be delivered to the Board of Education by November 30, 2008. Colorado Revised Statute 22-32-109 requires the district prepare a comprehensive audited financial report. The financial report consists of financial information prepared by the district and audited by an independent firm and indicates the financial status of the district at the end of the reporting period. It also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

Subject
2.02 Award of Contract: Mondo Publishing (EL8)
Meeting
Dec 18, 2008 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session, 5:30 p.m.
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-8, Asset Protection, and district policy DJB, Fiscal Management, expenditures of $250,000 and above must be approved by the Board of Education.
2.The district is adopting Mondo’s Bookshop Reading Program for grades K-5 in ten pilot schools across the district. A professional development plan has been developed to support this adoption by the district beginning in the fall of 2008 and continuing through June, 2009. The emphasis of this professional development will be on the implementation of all aspects of a comprehensive literacy program and in particular the effective use of formative assessment data.
3.Mondo’s professional development team will support the district elementary schools in the K-5 adoption of the Bookshop Reading Program at a cost of $28,400 per school, which includes six leadership days, six coach days, and 4 site visits per school. This model gives maximum support to assist with the systemic implementation of Book Shop and give strong support at the school level.
4.Funding for this professional development will come from the district’s Title I allocation.
