3.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Sep 1, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

Action
Recommended Action
to authorize administrative staff to negotiate a motor fuel agreement in an amount not to exceed the approved budget.
Goals
EL-7, Asset Protection

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In compliance with EL-7 Asset Protection, “the superintendent may not fail to use a competitive bidding procedure for all contracted services and purchases of supplies, materials and equipment in the amount of $25,000 or more."
     
  2. Transportation and Fleet Maintenance projects at least $3 million will be required for diesel and gasoline fuels for the 2011-2012 school year with an adopted budget amount of $3.4 million.  The projected usage is 650,000 gallons of diesel fuel and 150,000 gallons of unleaded fuel per year.
     
  3. Jeffco Public Schools has used forward contracting fuel for two years. Forward contracting locks the price per gallon of diesel fuel for budget certainty. It then is insulated from the extreme volatility of the fuel market and serves as risk mitigation.
     
  4. Locking the fuel price allows for better budget planning without the looming consequences of skyrocketing fuel prices.
     
  5. Recent crude oil prices have declined from over $100 a barrel to $80 per barrel.  Immediate action will improve the opportunity to obtain favorable fuel pricing.
     

  6. Jeffco Schools will use a cooperative purchasing agreement/piggyback provision to the City of Colorado Springs with four pre-qualified vendors who will provide formal binding quotes.

Goals
EL-6, Financial Administration

estimated time: 10:05 pm (estimated duration: 5 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Board executive limitation policy 6, Financial Administration,  states that, “With respect to the actual, ongoing financial condition and activities of the district, the superintendent shall not cause nor allow fiscal jeopardy or any fiscal condition that is inconsistent with achieving the priorities established in Board's Ends policies."
     
  2. On August 25, the Board of Education received information in Study Session on the district’s pending Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs) issuance.
     
  3. The Tax Anticipation Notes (TANs) are issued to provide funds to the General Fund to pay certain short-term expenses of the District.  Most expenses of the District are incurred during the traditional school year of September through May and most revenue is not received until the months of March through June.  The district has forecasted that a cash flow shortage will occur.
     
  4. Disclosure counsel has provided a Preliminary Official Statement to the Board.
     
  5. The District’s investment bankers, Robert W. Baird & Co., have been monitoring the ratings from Moody’s Investors’ Services and Standard and Poor’s and also the interest rates available.
