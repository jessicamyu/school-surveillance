Subject
2.03 Contract Agreement: Executive Search Services for Next Superintendent
Meeting
Feb 18, 2014 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Discussion Agenda
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 5:50 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 hours) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with district policy DJ/DJA, Purchasing/Purchasing Authority, "the purpose of the Board of Education policies related to purchasing is to provide for the fair and equitable treatment of all persons involved in purchasing by the district."
     
  2. On January 9, 2014, district staff met with the Board of Education in study session to address questions and receive direction regarding a Request for Proposal (RFP) for executive search services.  The Board provided direction on the scope of work, evaluation criteria and the calendar for the RFP. 
     
  3. On January 13, 2014, an RFP for executive search services was issued through Rocky Mountain e-Purchasing bid posting service.  Three hundred and forty-five firms were notified on the RFP.  On January 29, 2014 four proposals were received for executive search services.
     
  4. On January 30, 2014, the Board of Education received copies of the proposals and evaluation forms for review and evaluation.  They met in for a special study session meeting on February 5, 2014 and selected two firms to be invited in for interviews.
     
  5. On February 18, 2014, the firms Ray and Associates, Inc. and PROACT Search will present information to the Board of Education regarding the executive search services that they provide and their proposal.  This will be an opportunity for the Board to ask questions, have discussion, and determine the executive search services company that would be awarded the negotiated agreement.

3.02 Budget Community Engagement
Meeting
Feb 18, 2014 - Special Meeting
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 8:10 p.m.  (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Cindy Stevenson, superintendent

Lorie Gillis, chief financial officer

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to provide direction for its budget community engagement sessions on March 3, 5 and 8 related to the agenda and format for the sessions, the length of the sessions, questions for participants, common message for board members to convey, handouts for participants and feedback forms to be used.  Staff will prepare draft materials for final board approval before the March meetings.

 

BACKGROUND:

In January, the Board scheduled budget forums to gather public input for the 2014-2015 budget development process.  Due to the canceling of the February 8 budget study session, the Board has not had the opportunity to discuss the content, format or materials to be used at the budget community forums.
