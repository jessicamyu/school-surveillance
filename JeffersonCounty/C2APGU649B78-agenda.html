PRESENTING STAFF:

Nicole Stewart, interim chief financial officer

Jason Hendricks, director of finance

Paul Niedermuller, independent auditor, CliftonLarsonAllen

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to hear from staff on the districtwide financial condition through the third quarter of the 2020/2021 school year in order to monitor district finances and to receive information on the Single Audit for the 2019/2020 fiscal year ending June 30, 2020.

 

BACKGROUND:

Each quarter, in compliance with district policy BEDB, Board executive limitation policies EL-5, Financial Planning and Budgeting, and EL-6, Financial Administration, and as outlined in Board governance process policy GP-13, Committee Structure, the Board meets to review and monitor quarterly financial reports. Staff and an independent auditor from CliftonLarsonAllen, LLP, (CLA) identify the funds, departments and/or schools to monitor closely throughout the year. 

 

The district's Annual Comprehensive Financial Report for the 2019/2020 fiscal year was presented to the Board of Education on December 16, 2020. The single audit was not included within the report due to delays in release of audit guidance by the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) for the Coronavirus Relief Funds (CRF). The Board was made aware that the single audit would be published as a stand-alone document by the federal deadline that was extended to June 30, 2021. The district completed the audit and filed the report by the original deadline of March 31, 2021. The report was submitted to the Board of Education and posted online on March 26, 2021 and presented to the Financial Oversight Committee on April 6, 2021. The Board will hear from an independent auditor from CLA on the report.

 

For additional information, please open the documents below; the transmittal and third quarter financial report for the period ending March 31, 2021, may be viewed online from the Quarterly Financial Reports webpage of the district's website; the single audit report for fiscal year 2019/2020 may be viewed on the Annual Comprehensive Financial webpage of the district's website.

2.04 Elementary Literacy Update and Contract Award: Into Reading/Arriba la Lectura (EL-7, 11)
Meeting
May 12, 2021 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Action, Discussion, Information
Recommended Action
To authorize the director of Purchasing to negotiate and enter into an agreement with Houghton Mifflin Harcourt as Literacy vendor for K-5 for FY 22 with an estimated cost not to exceed $1,000,000.
Goals
Ends 3 - Student Achievement
EL-7, Asset Protection

estimated time: 10:45 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Tracy Dorland, superintendent

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools-elementary/charters

 

PURPOSE: 

The Board of Education will be given an update on the plan for elementary literacy efforts with the outcome of approval to move forward with the phase two plan as outlined.

 

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Board Executive Limitation, EL-7, Asset Protection, ". . . the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000".
 

On February 4, 2021, the Board was updated on literacy in Jeffco Public Schools, providing them with an overview of efforts to support elementary literacy across the district.  Board Ends 3 states, "Every school and the district will have effective learning systems and shared leadership that: provide high quality core instructional practices, interventions and enrichments with the expectation that:

Every student will receive the skills, support and educational opportunities needed to achieve his or her full potential."
 

A formal Request for K-5 Literacy Proposal was issued this spring and 146 companies were solicited through RMEPS (Rocky Mountain e-Purchasing System).  Houghton Mifflin Harcourt was selected through a competitive process which included seven companies including formal proposals and vendor demonstrations.  Jeffco staff members of Curriculum and Instruction, the Purchasing Department, and school-based teachers participated in the process.
 

The estimated cost for FY22 will not exceed $1,000,000.  Estimated costs include teacher materials and resources, student materials and resources, professional development, digital licenses, and shipping and handling fees.  Upon successful research and evaluation of this tool more schools could be invited to participate.  Jeffco Public Schools retains the ability to lock in pricing for six years for up to 76 schools.  If 76 schools decided to participate, the total investment would not exceed $9,000,000 over six years.

PRESENTING STAFF:

Tracy Dorland, superintendent

Dr. Susan Leach, chief student success officer

 

PURPOSE: The Board of Education will gain an understanding of initial work on the Pandemic Recovery Plan as outlined for how Jeffco Public Schools will address and monitor key areas of operation to ensure students are positioned to thrive in the years to come.

 

BACKGROUND: On March 11, 2021 President Joe Biden signed the American Rescue Plan Act of 2021 into law. The $1.9 trillion economic stimulus bill was enacted to recover from the economic and health impacts of COVID-19 and builds upon facets of the CARES Act of March 2020 and the Consolidated Appropriations Act of December 2020. The most recent aid package earmarked $130 billion for K-12 schools to improve operational infrastructure and address any gaps in learning that may have occurred for students who missed school during the pandemic. Dr. Leach has been selected to lead this initiative in Jeffco Public Schools that will rely on broad stakeholder engagement.
