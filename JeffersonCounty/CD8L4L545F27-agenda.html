PURPOSE:
For the Board to visit Alameda International Jr/Sr. High School to tour possible sites and learn of the project for a Stride community health clinic at Alameda Int’l Jr/Sr HS prior to the Board’s June discussion of Capital Improvement Program (CIP) priority projects list.

 

BACKGROUND:

With voter approval of the 2018 bond program, Alameda Int’l Jr/Sr HS received a large investment in a new building and several property improvements including the new structure, asbestos abatement and property improvements such as a turf field, new outdoor tennis and basketball courts and new parking lot. 

 

As the project took shape with the school’s Design Advisory Group, the Stride Clinic on campus was ultimately deemed cost prohibitive in the remodel plans.  Since that time, the community health clinic has become an important need of the school community and a critical way to serve some of our most underserved students and families in the broader Alameda community.  One grant for $100,000 from Community First Foundation has been received. One other grant application has been submitted to the Colorado Health Foundation for $400,000. The City of Lakewood has been approached to reduce the cost of accessing city utilities, depending on the site location. 

The goal of this visit is to provide the Board of Education with as much information as needed for their decision-making in June on priority projects for Capital Improvement Program funds, to garner support for the project, and to understand the needs for the best possible location.

Board members will receive a brief presentation before leaving for a walking tour of the sites.
