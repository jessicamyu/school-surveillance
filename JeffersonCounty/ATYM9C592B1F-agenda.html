2.03 Overview of the Charter Authorizer Statutes and Jeffco Public Schools Processes (EL-13)
Meeting
Jan 25, 2018 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 6:45 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kristin Edgar, Esq., Caplan & Earnest

Karen Quanbeck, chief school effectiveness officer, elementary

Tim Matlick, achievement director/charter schools, School Innovation and Effectiveness Office

                       

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will be informed of the statutory guidelines related to authorizing charter schools and the process employed by Jeffco Public Schools for reviewing charter school applications, renewals and oversight.

 

BACKGROUND:

Board of Education reviews and takes action on charter school renewals annually as contracts are completed and applications when received. Understanding the statutory requirements and guidelines associated with the district's authorizer obligation is essential to supporting effective decision making on all renewals and applications. Legal counsel and district staff will provide information for the Board of Education on the statutory guidelines related to authorizing charter schools and the process employed by Jeffco Public Schools for reviewing charter school renewals and applications. Information will also be presented regarding charter school oversight and reporting obligations.  Time will be provided for the Board of Education to ask questions related to their responsibilities as charter school authorizers.

2.06 Contract Extension: Chrome Device (EL-7)
Meeting
Jan 25, 2018 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to recommend director of Purchasing to appropriate an additional $250,000 to the existing Chrome Device contract with CTL, DHE, and GovConnection to increase the current contract for the purchase of Chrome Devices from $3,000,000 to $3,250,000.

estimated time: 8:45 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 minute)   

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000.
     
  2. RFP 24158, Chrome OS Devices and the Chrome Management Licenses was issued on October 27, 2016. Notification was sent to 4 vendors via email and 1546 vendors through Rocky Mountain E‐Purchasing System.
  3. Many teachers and students prefer Chromebooks over other mobile devices because they are easy to use, cost effective and extremely reliable.
  4. Chromebooks are compatible for use in all Jeffco standardized assessments, including PARCC/CMAS.
     
  5. On February 9, 2017 the BOE approved a twelve-month contract amount of $3,000,000, for schools and departments to purchase Chrome devices, licenses and accessories from their budgets. The current contract expires on March 1, 2018.
     
  6. In response to the district’s 1:1 technology strategy, during 2017 schools have accelerated the adoption of Chromebooks in learning environments. This surge in demand has exhausted the available funds in the existing contract.
     
  7. It is estimated that the district will need an additional $250,000 to be added to the current Chrome device contract to enable purchases to continue through March 1, 2018.
     
  8. In response to RFP 24516, the BOE approved a new contract for Chrome devices on January 11, 2018. This contract will replace the existing contract on March 2, 2018.
