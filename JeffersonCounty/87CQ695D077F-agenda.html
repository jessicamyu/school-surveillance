2.02 Dissertation of the Year-American Education Research Assn: Dr. Janet Filbin
Meeting
Apr 30, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Information
The Board of Education is pleased to recognize Dr. Janet Filbin, director of Jeffco's Instructional Data Services, for earning the Dissertation of the Yearaward from the American Educational Research Association's (AERA) Leadership for School Improvement division. The award showcases research on how the leadership of teachers, principals, and superintendents influence instructional capacity resulting in improved student outcomes and how policy guides this collaborative effort.

Dr. Filbin's dissertation was based on a program evaluation of Jeffco's I2a initiative. The results of her research found that strong teacher beliefs and use of data for making instructional decisions combined with strong principal leadership focused on data conversations and monitoring school goals resulted in higher achievement outcomes for students.

3.04 Supplemental Funds - CDVR, SWAP (EL-6)
Meeting
Apr 30, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A, B and C.
    1. The Colorado Division of Vocational Rehabilitation has awarded Jefferson County School District’s School to Work Alliance Program (SWAP) with 15th year funding in the amount of $354,584. SWAP is a program designed to work with students having mild/moderate disabilities as they transition from school to the workplace. The plan aligns with strategic plan instruction objective 3, which ensures students have access to alternative pathways and interventions to improve achievement, close achievement gaps, and increase graduation rates.
    2. The Division of Vocational Rehabilitation (DVR) funds cover salary and operating expenses. The District will provide a cash match of $354,584 at the beginning of the fiscal year. DVR returns $354,584 throughout the year in incremental payments. The district provides office space and equipment.
    3. The funds are used to (1) increase employment opportunities for this group of youth, (2) enhance employability of participants, and (3) involve participants in their community in a meaningful way. The strategies of this project include helping students develop employability skills, find appropriate, entry-level career path employment, provide students with job development and placement, and conduct one-year follow-up services after employment.
    4. The success of the SWAP program is measured by how many students are employed under DVR guidelines. That target is for at least 80 students to be successfully employed.
    5. Donna Brovsky, SWAP coordinator, is the program manager.

4.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Apr 30, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
4. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.
