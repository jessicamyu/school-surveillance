Recommended Action
To consider participation in school district mass litigation against Juul and affiliates.

estimated time:  6:20 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)

PRESENTING STAFF:

 

Julie Tolleson, Chief Legal Counsel 


 

BACKGROUND:


A group of law firms representing hundreds of school districts in claims against JUUL has approached Jeffco Public Schools about joining the case, a multi-district litigation (MDL) set up in San Francisco.  All school district cases against e-cigarette manufacturer Juul are consolidated there under the supervision of a single federal judge.  Participating districts must complete a Plaintiff questionnaire, but external counsel does not anticipate being subject to formal discovery. The agreement proposes a contingent fee structure in which the district will bear no out-of-pocket costs.  Rather, attorneys' fees and costs would be deducted from any award or settlement.  Nationwide, over 1200 school districts have joined. 

 

Regionally, plaintiff districts include:

 

  1. Arizona:  Mesa, Tucson and Chandler have joined (their three largest districts)
  2. Utah:  36 of their school districts have joined, who represent 98% of their students
  3. Idaho:  67 of their school districts have joined, who represent 84% of their students
  4. New Mexico:  6 of their school districts have joined, and Albuquerque has informed plaintiff counsel they intend to join.
  5. Nebraska:  4 of their school districts have joined, with Lincoln Public Schools being the largest.
  6. Kansas:  15 of their school districts have joined, with Shawnee Mission being the largest.
  7. California:  Over 100 of their school districts have joined, including Los Angeles, San Francisco, and San Diego
  8. Oregon:  Portland decided to join last week, making them the first district to join in Oregon
  9. Washington:  over 75 of their districts have joined, representing roughly 40% of their students, including Seattle
  10. Colorado:  7 school districts have joined, with Boulder being the largest.  

 

Certain designated "bellweather" cases are headed to trial this fall. If the jury returns a verdict in favor of the schools, counsel for the districts anticipate that the remaining cases will resolve via a negotiated settlement.  

 

The proposed participation agreement and plaintiff questionnaire are attached.

Recommended Action
To appoint members to the District Accountability Committee as presented.

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 5 minutes) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS:
 

  1. In accordance with Board governance process policy GP-13, Committee Structure, the Board of Education approves membership to the District Accountability Committee (DAC).
  2. When the Board approved appointments as a consent agenda item on June 8, the attachment did not clearly identify the final appointees. A "yes" vote approves the corrected attachment and final appointees to the District Accountability Committee. 
  3. Through March 11-April 11, 2022, an online application form was available to individuals interested in serving on the DAC and applicants were provided an opportunity to meet individually with board members on May 25.  The Board received 29 applications to serve on the DAC which were discussed at the earlier study session when direction was given for appointments confirmed through this agenda item.
  4. Appointments are made for two year terms, unless directed otherwise by the Board of Education.
  5. The DAC has 33 vacancies to fill for the 2022/23 school year due to term limits and resignations. Board policy GP-13 establishes a membership structure that includes one business community representative, up to 10 at large representatives plus:
    1. Two parent representatives from a school-level accountability committee (SAC) from each of 17 articulation areas
    2. Charter school SAC parent representatives (3)
    3. Option school SAC parent representatives (2)
    4. Teacher(s) employed by Jeffco Public Schools (one per grade band:  Elem, MS, HS)
    5. Building level administrator(s) (ideally one per grade band: Elem, MS, HS)
    6. Jeffco PTA member
    7. Classified staff member
    8. Superintendent  designee

 

6. DAC membership was last set on June 10, 2021.

7. Continued efforts should be made to have DAC membership comply with C.R.S. 22-11-301, representing student demographics across the district in non-Caucasian, free/reduced, English language learners, migrant, gifted and challenged parent populations.

PURPOSE:
The main goal of the agenda topic is to publicly review and discuss the 85 Elementary School Report that was provided in compliance with policy FCB. This is an opportunity for the District to reinforce the information that has been provided to the board since March of 2022, for the Board members to speak publicly about their insights from review of the dashboard, and for the District to share the timeline including the Superintendent’s recommendation, public hearing dates, and date of the Board vote. 

BACKGROUND:
On April 7, 2022, the Board of Education adopted a resolution to guide the work of regional opportunities for thriving schools in Jeffco Public Schools.

Jeffco has capacity to serve 96,000 students in traditional schools and we currently serve 69,000 students in traditional schools.  Our regions are widely viewed through the lens of articulation areas. Difficult and untimely spring decisions to close two elementary schools in the last two years have been required due to minimal student enrollment (Allendale and Fitzmorris).


Regional Opportunities for Thriving Schools is an initiative that was devised in the Spring of 2022 to respond to the Board’s request for a comprehensive plan to ensure thriving schools with enrollment to support extraordinary student experiences across our district.
