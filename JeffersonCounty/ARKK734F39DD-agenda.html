6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Nov 2, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.    
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Recommended Action
To approve the membership for the 2019-2022 Calendar Committee as presented.
Goals
EL-14, School Year Calendar

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. As stated in Board executive limitation policy EL-14, School Year Calendar, the superintendent may not fail to adopt a school year calendar in three or more year increments and may not fail to have the Board of Education approve the committee membership. The approval of the calendar committee must happen before the district can begin creating future district calendars.
     
  2. The calendar committee determines the three-year calendar cycle to present to the superintendent for adoption. This will include the 2019/2020, 2020/2021, and 2021/2022 school year calendars. 
     
  3. To collect the names for the calendar committee, leaders from each of the groups represented on the attached list were asked to provide names of members who could work through a collaborative process taking into consideration the minutes students need to be in the classroom, holidays, and other aspects that are important to our community.  Members were selected to represent different areas within our district. Students, parents, and a variety of staff groups will be participating in this year’s calendar committee. Moreover, feedback has been collected throughout the year from community members and staff who have reached out to the Chief Academic Officer's office directly or submitted questions or concerns through board correspondence.
     
  4. The calendar committee is composed of individuals representing the three employee associations, Jeffco PTA, District Accountability Committee, Strategic Planning and Advisory Council, Student Voice, parent representatives from the elementary, middle and high school levels, a high school college counselor, a representative from athletics, a school principal and assistant principal, and an achievement director.
     
  5. After the calender committee has been approved by the Board of Education, they will convene in spring 2018 for three meetings to come to a consensus on a three-year calendar to be submitted to the superintendent in May 2018 for adoption.  The calendar committee may be reconvened throughout the three-year cycle, if needed.

7.12 Contract Award: Drake MS Classroom Addition Construction Manager/General Contractor Services Phase I (EL-8)
Meeting
Nov 2, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the award of contract to GH Phipps for the Drake MS Classroom Addition; and, to authorize the executive director of Facilities and Construction Management to execute a Phase I contract with the CM/GC in an amount of $2,106,277.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-8 Facilities, “. . . fail to make business decisions based on a cost/benefit analysis for . . . facility projects of $250,000 or more . . .”
     
  2. The contract provides construction management/general contractor (CM/GC) preconstruction and construction services for the design of an approximate 25,000 square foot (SF), sixteen classroom addition to Drake Middle School (MS), 12550 W. 52nd Avenue, Arvada, Colorado 80002.
  3. The Board of Education has concluded that additional classroom space is required at Drake MS to accommodate the sixth grade move in the Arvada West Articulation Area.
  4. Following district policy FEB, Architect/Engineer/Construction Manager, staff reviewed 20 pre-qualified general contractors and/or construction managers/general contractors qualifications to shortlist for this project.
  5. Based on qualifications, three pre-qualified CM/GCs were contacted and sent an invitation to submit fees and general conditions for the project.
  6. On September 13, 2017 proposals were received from GH Phipps, Saunders, and WE O’Neil construction companies.  The proposals were reviewed by Facilities and Construction Management staff for conformance to the Request for Proposal (RFP), qualifications, experience with projects of this nature and alignment with typical fees for a project of this size and complexity.
  7. The selection team recommends that GH Phipps be awarded the CM/GC contract.

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
