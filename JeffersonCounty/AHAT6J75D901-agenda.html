PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to discuss its legislative priorities for the 2017 Colorado Legislative Session and receive an update on the legislative session to-date.
 

BACKGROUND:

Annually, the Board prepares a statement of principles and priorities, its Legislative Platform.  This document provides direction to the district lobbyist in the absence of direct Board discussion on any legislative matter during the Legislative Session.

 

Board governance process policy GP-4, Board Job Description, outlines how "the Board may, by motion or resolution, take a position of advocacy on national, statewide or local ballot issues, referred measures, legislation or rules and regulations" and further that, "the Board may, by motion or resolution, initiate action which leads to preparation and introduction of legislation deemed appropriate and important to the education mission of the Jefferson County School District."

2.05 Finance and 2017/18 Budget Overview: Cabinet Recommendations (EL-11)
Meeting
Jan 26, 2017 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion
Goals
EL-6, Financial Administration

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Nicole Stewart, manager-budget & analysis

Suzi Thompson, manager-treasury & analysis

 

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on the December budget forecast from the State; updates on proposed legislation, the Governor's budget request, Jeffco's fiscal year (FY) 2016/17 budget; and, information on development of Jeffco's FY 2017/18 budget including reductions, Cabinet recommendations, use of one-time funds, status of reserves, timeline and next steps.    


BACKGROUND:

Discussions on development of the 2017/18 budget began at the Board of Education study session on October 14, 2016. Dialog continued at the November 17, 2016, Board of Education study session with information on the state outlook, school finance, district funding and the concept for the community engagement interactive budget tool. Also at the November 17 meeting, given the defeat of November's ballot measures 3A and 3B for additional general fund (operating) and bond (facility) dollars, a panel of Cabinet members assisted the Board of Education with setting initial short and long-term direction to chart the next steps for the district and superintendent. At the December 1, 2016 Board of Education meeting, the Board provided direction on the community engagement online interactive budget tool.

 

Staff continues to work with district and school leadership through the Budgeting for Outcomes process and student based budgeting (SBB) to assess and evaluate budget efficiencies to ensure dollars are focused on areas with the most impact to student achievement.  School accountability committees are reviewing school-level budgets and will be submitting budget priority information through the District Accountability Committee (DAC) and the work of the DAC budget subcommittee. The online budget tool will open to the public for three weeks beginning Saturday, January 21, 2017.

Terry Elliott, chief school effectiveness officer

Tim Reed, executive director of Facilities and Construction Management

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive the staff recommendations on the approach to be taken related to facilities in Jeffco Schools.

 

BACKGROUND:

As a result of the failure of 3A/3B on last November’s ballot, it is necessary for the district to reevaluate the allocation of general fund dollars and facilities maintenance and operation funds.

 

The approach presented will involve recommendations for moving sixth grade, implementing limited capital improvements to middle schools, school closures and boundary adjustments.  These measures will support the goals of the Jeffco 2020 vision, implementing long term budget reductions and the efficient operation of existing facilities.

Recommended Action
to adopt a resolution conditionally approving a charter to Great Work Montessori School.

estimated time: 9:25 p.m. (estimated duration: 5 minutes)
 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board governance process policy GP-2, Governing Style and Philosophies, and executive limitation policy EL-13, Charter Schools Application and Monitoring, the following is brought to the Board of Education for action.
     
  2. On September 8, 2016, the Board received a charter school application from Great Work Montessori School for study in accordance with district policies LBD, LBD-E, and LBD-R.
     
  3. On October 6, 2016, the Board held a public meeting at which representatives of Great Work Montessori School presented information about the application and received and responded to questions from the Board. 
     
  4. On November 3, 2016, the Board adopted a resolution denying the application because the Board believed that such denial was in the best interests of the district, its students, and the community, for the reasons set forth in the resolution.
     
  5. On November 7, 2016, Great Work Montessori School appealed the Board’s denial of the application to the Colorado State Board of Education, which remanded the matter to the Board for reconsideration following a hearing on December 14, 2016.  
     
  6. At the Board’s January 12, 2017 meeting, a resolution was introduced to conditionally approve the application, but such resolution failed for lack of a majority.
     
  7. Following the January 12, 2017 meeting, district and Great Work Montessori School representatives continued to discuss the matters set forth in the State Board’s remand order and have provided the information generated in such discussions to the Board for consideration.
