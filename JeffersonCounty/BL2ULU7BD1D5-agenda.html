2.03 Report: Food and Nutrition Services Master Plan (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 5, 2020 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 9:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Beth Wallace, executive director, Food and Nutrition Services

Steve Bell, chief operating office, Jeffco Public Schools

Paul Mackesey, president, Mackesey & Associates, LLC

Anne Weber, senior vice president, Canon Design

Rebecca Rogers, senior associate, Canon Design

 

PURPOSE:

To provide and discuss an overview on the districtwide feasibility study to develop a long-term roadmap to provide quality meal service to all Jeffco Public Schools students.  

 

BACKGROUND:

In January 2019, Jeffco Public Schools Food and Nutrition Services (FNS) department contracted with Canon Design Architecture and Mackesey & Associates to study the district’s existing facilities and equipment to develop a long range master plan.  The study participants include Food and Nutrition Services staff, Jeffco Facilities staff and the Jeffco Finance department.

 

Jeffco FNS is a self-sustaining fund within Jeffco Public Schools; therefore, it must operate as a business to meet the needs of its customers. To successfully do so, Jeffco FNS needs a business plan overhaul – one that addresses both current needs and long-term sustainability. This study provides a detailed explanation of the current realities within Jeffco FNS, a comprehensive facilities and equipment master plan, options for fresh food preparation, and estimated project costs.

 

As the Child Nutrition industry continues to evolve, influenced heavily by regulations and student and family desires, it is critical that the Jeffco FNS department determines a plan of action to meet existing and future needs in Jeffco schools. One of the main goals of the master plan was to study options that would allow for greater use of fresh and non-processed ingredients in either a decentralized or a centralized model. The final recommendations, with the support of adequate facilities, will provide FNS the ability to meet current goals and proactively prepare for the future of Child Nutrition programs. 

 

Going forward, a clear, actionable plan is needed to provide FNS professionals with the resources necessary to provide quality, healthy meals to the students of Jeffco Public Schools. This document provides that plan.

2.04 Reimagining Time Task Force (Start Time, Late Start/Early Release, and Food/Nutrition) (EL-11)

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kristopher Schuh, deputy superintendent, School Leadership

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools elementary, School Leadership

Tom McDermott, chief strategy officer

Dan Cohan, community superintendent, School Leadership

Robert Jacobus, consultant, School Bus Logistics

Greg Jackson, executive director, Transportation and Fleet Services

Katie Winner, parent, co-chair Start Time Task Force

Laura Johnson, parent, co-chair Start Time Task Force

Jeremy Felker, executive director, Student Data Privacy and Reporting

Todd Engels, executive director, School Leadership

 

PURPOSE:

To provide an update to and receive direction on next steps from the Board of Education (BOE) in relation to the work of three task forces that have been meeting. These task forces, Start Time, Late Start/Early Release, and Food and Nutrition, are each unique in their scope, but are being presented together under the umbrella topic of ‘time.’ 

 

BACKGROUND:

The Reimagining Time Task Force began in the fall of 2019.  Members of this task force include participants from Start Time, Late Start/Early Release, and Food and Nutrition task forces.  The Reimagining Time Task Force will present information for the first time to the BOE on February 5.

  • The Start Time Task Force was commissioned by the superintendent in February 2018 and meets approximately every six weeks.  The task force researched secondary school start times and presented to the BOE on February 7, 2019. One of the task force’s recommendations was to hire an implementation firm/consultant. School Bus Logistics was hired in the spring of 2019 to create a plan for adjusting school start times so middle schools start on or after 8 a.m. and high schools start on or after 8:30 a.m.
  • The Late Start/Early Release Task Force was created through negotiations in a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between Jeffco Public Schools and the Jefferson County Education Association (JCEA) in the spring of 2018.  The MOU designated a joint task force explore the viability of a late start or early release with the purpose of increased planning time for educators across the district.  The task force including teachers, principals, parents, transportation staff and central administrators met consistently during the 2018/2019 school year beginning in November 2018.  A survey, which closed on March 6, 2019, was administered to the Jeffco community to assess family and educator thoughts on late starts and early release time.  The task force presented to the BOE on March 7 and June 12, 2019.  Survey results and additional information are available at the BOE presentation links.
  • Food and Nutrition Task Force began in November 2018 to examine current and best/next practices in providing healthy and nutritious meals for students.  Task force membership includes community members, parents, students and staff.  The task force presented to the BOE on December 12, 2019.  One of the recommendations to the Board was to institute a 30-minute standard minimum time for students to eat lunch.
