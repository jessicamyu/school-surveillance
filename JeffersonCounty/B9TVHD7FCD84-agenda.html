The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Goals
Ends 2 - Student Achievement
Ends 3 - Student Achievement

Pertinent Facts:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) requires all public school districts and schools to complete a Unified Improvement Plan (UIP). These improvement plans are designed to identify priority challenges and develop major improvement strategies to meet these needs. While Jeffco is a high performing school district that consistently meets state accreditation expectations, the CDE expects the district's UIP to focus on areas for improvement.
  1. To that end, the district has worked closely with the District Accountability Committee (DAC) to develop the 2018/19 District Unified Improvement Plan. Members of the DAC DUIP subcommittee include: Deb Guiducci, deputy chair; Margaret Lessenger, vice chair; Lorri Avery, Kevin Carroll, Thomas Dougherty, Roger Dowd, Jim Earley, Carol Eaton, Jill Fellman, Matt Flores, Jef Fugita, Jack Gallar, Hi Howard, Cecily Klein, Susan Lopez, Heather MacGillivary, Melissa Martini, Tom McDermott, Marna Messer, Ben Robertson, Stephanie Schooley, and Jennifer Steele.
  1. The improvement planning process included a comprehensive analysis of district level data, the root cause analysis to focus on system improvements and development of an action plan aligned to the priority student performance challenges.
  1. Throughout the school year, Jeffco's District Accountability Committee will continue to partner with the district to provide input and feedback on the implementation of the district's Unified Improvement Plan.
  1. Within an expectation of continuous improvement, all schools in Jeffco complete an annual UIP that focuses on priority challenge areas for improvement.
  1. Schools with School Performance Framework ratings of "Priority Improvement" or "Turnaround" have completed a comprehensive UIP to focus on system improvements. As required by CDE, the drafts of these schools' UIPs were submitted to the CDE for state feedback in January 2019.
  1. These identified schools reviewed CDE feedback on their UIPs in March 2019 and revised the UIPs as appropriate.
  1. After being approved by the Board of Education, the district will submit the District Unified Improvement Plan and the UIPs for Arvada K-8, Emory Elementary, Fitzmorris Elementary, North Arvada Middle, Peak Expeditionary, and Stevens Elementary to the Colorado Department of Education by April 15, 2019, as required by CDE.

Meeting
Apr 4, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to execute a twelve month renewal of the Apple contract for purchase of all Apple products at an estimated cost not to exceed $1,000,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation's policy, EL-07, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not "...contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000."
     
  2. Apple sets standard educational pricing for all educational customers and pricing is non-negotiable. Jeffco Public Schools requires all Apple devices to be enrolled in Apple’s Device Enrollment Program (DEP). Apple is the only vendor that can provide access to this program. Due to these constraints, Apple products qualify as a Sole Source Justification. The 12-month purchase contract is May 1 to April 30 each year.
     
  3. Teachers and students use Apple technologies to increase efficiency and effectiveness while enriching classroom instruction.
    1. The use of iPads is a popular instructional device for some of Jeffco's learning environments. Apple has an extensive application catalog, many that are customized for education, that are only compatible with Apple devices. Their devices are known for their quality, durability, and a user interface that students find easy to navigate.
    2. A limited number of staff maintain a consistent demand for Apple laptops. Apple laptops deliver a premium experience for end users with appealing proprietary interface, low maintenance, and features that are easy to integrate into business and education environments
    3. Apple desktop computers remain a popular choice for school multimedia lab environments. Classes such as Graphic Design, Yearbook, Photography, and Video Production require enhanced computing hardware and industry standard software that is optimized for Apple's macOS environment.
       
  4. The estimated annual contract amount is not to exceed $1,000,000 for school year 2019/20.
    1. Schools and central departments have replaced fewer iPads in the last 12 months than anticipated.
    2. Schools and central departments continue to purchase small quantities, 100-200 devices annually, of Apple Macbooks (laptops) and iMacs (desktops).
    3. School purchasing trends at Jeffco Public Schools continue to shift toward adopting Chromebooks as the primary student device. Chromebooks provide a more cost-effective instructional solution that is easier to manage.
    4. About 4,000 iPads will be removed from Jeffco’s environment at the end of the 2018/19 school year. These are devices that are no longer eligible to receive Apple security updates and therefore represent a potential threat to our schools and departments.
       
  5. Apple devices align to the following tactics in the Strategic Plan:
    1. Technology to Transform Learning - If schools provide integrated technology instruction aligned to the Jeffco Generations skills and it is supported by high-quality devices, then students will experience expanded, flexible learning opportunities that prepare them to thrive in a connected digital world.
    2. Student-Directed Learning - Students leverage technology to take an active role in choosing, achieving and demonstrating competency in their learning goals. Students use technology to seek feedback that informs and improves their practice and to demonstrate their learning in a variety of ways.
    3. Conditions for Learning - The commitment to equity providing cost-effective 1:1 devices provides equitable access to digital learning tools for all students. Apple devices provide learning experiences across a wide spectrum of tools for content mastery, collaboration, critical and creative thinking, communication, and adaptability for all types of learners.
    4. Technology of 1:1 devices if we provide equitable and sustainable options for school technology then all schools will provide access to technology for all students. iPads are an option for schools to select as their preferred one-to-one (1:1) device.
       
  6. Schools and departments will purchase the equipment, applications and accessories from their budgets. Orders will be placed centrally.

7.23 Contract Renewal: Microsoft Licensing (EL-7)
Meeting
Apr 4, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to renew the MicroSoft licensing contract for a three-year period to expire in June 2022 in an estimated amount not to exceed $2,300,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not "...contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000."
     
  2. In June 2018, the district extended its contract with Microsoft Enrollment for Education Solutions (EES) agreement. The agreement provides software licenses, maintenance and upgrades for District Enterprise computing systems - Active Directory, Server Operating Systems, SQL databases, Microsoft Office Suite (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, etc), Antivirus, SCCM, Microsoft Operating Systems for End Points (Computers and Laptops) and other core license products used by staff and students. Microsoft is an enterprise application tool used by all staff and students in the district. In addition to the Microsoft productivity tools used in the classroom, applications such as Hyperion for the budget office and PeopleSoft Financials are critical applications that rely on the Microsoft Suite of products. Many of the district’s core systems for endpoint provision rely on the suite of application covered under this agreement. All user accounts are provisioned via Microsoft Active Directory and the applications that students and staff access on a daily basis are built on central servers that all run on a Microsoft operating system. Students and Teachers rely on devices with the Microsoft toolset for classroom learning as well as to complete their annual state testing.
     
  3. The current extension expires June 30, 2019, and the Information Technology (IT) department is seeking approval to renew the three-year EES subscription. Microsoft's new model licenses all employees including substitute teachers, bus drivers and maintenance crews; however, Jeffco Public Schools was able to negotiate licensing without including these worker groups.
     
  4. The EES agreement has simplified the management of licensure as it allows for unlimited installations of the Microsoft Office Suite as well as core system licenses for classroom computers and central servers. Over the past three years, the EES agreement has simplified the license distribution, tracking and reduced overhead costs. More importantly, the agreement has provided for continuing compliance to copyright and software licensing laws.
     
  5. Endpoints (laptops and computers) can continue to be upgraded to the same versions across the install base insuring security compliance against threats and attacks.
     
  6. By continuing with the license strategy, the EES agreement provides a cost-effective way to administer license distribution and tracking while keeping the install based compliant with copyright and licensing laws.
     
  7. The EES subscription is billed and trued-up on an annual basis based on Microsoft's licensing count for an organization.  The renewal of a new three-year agreement will ensure that the district locks in the current price over the contract term. 
  8. The estimated cost of the renewal will not exceed $2,300,000 for the three-year contract.  The amount will be paid from the Information Technology budget.  Savings can be estimated in “cost avoidance” of the staff hours required to inventory the district's install base. Cost avoidance includes the cost to true-up licenses, since the EES agreement allows for unlimited license installs.

7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To adopt the resolution for the supplemental appropriation of 5A mill levy override funds and budget adjustments for fiscal year 2018/19 as provided in the resolution and pertinent facts.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting, "Financial planning for any fiscal year shall not deviate materially from the Board's Ends policies, risk fiscal jeopardy or fail to be derived from a multi-year plan."
  2. The 2018/2019 Jeffco Public Schools budget was adopted by the Board of Education (BOE) on June 7, 2018.
  3. Subsequent to the adoption of the 2018/19 budget, additional revenues and/or expenditures have occurred within the funds which could not have been reasonably foreseen at the time of the budget adoption pursuant to C.R.S. 22-44-110(5).
  4. In November 2018, Jeffco voters approved a $33 million mill levy override (MLO). The district will receive these unplanned MLO property tax revenues in the spring of 2019. No revenues or expenditures associated with the proposed mill levy override were included in the 2018/19 Adopted Budget; therefore, these funds need to be appropriated so the district does not exceed BOE authorized spending levels for the 2018/19 school year.
  5. To ensure adequate appropriations at the end of the fiscal year, supplemental appropriations are proposed for the General Fund. A request for supplemental funding was not included in the previous supplemental budget requests approved on February 7 and March 7, 2019.
  6. On September 12, 2018, the Board of Education heard the initial proposal for the 1:1 initiative during monitoring of the progress of its strategic plan tactic. On January 16, the Board gave direction to prepare to roll out the deployment beginning in August 2019 instead the following year; and, on February 13, 2019, the Board received a project update on the 1:1 initiative.
  7. The original 1:1 initiative proposed deploying devices in August 2020. Based on feedback from the Board of Education and community, the timeline to begin the 1:1 initiative deployment plan was modified to begin during the 2019/20 school year. This change accelerated a 17 month project to a five month project.
  8. Funds being requested are for the purchase of district 1:1 initiative devices for 5th and 9th grades at schools participating in the August Phase I deployment. Devices for deployment in Phases II (November 2019) and III (January 2020) will be purchased after July 1. A full plan on the 1:1 initiative will be presented to the BOE on May 2 with the proposed 2019/20 budget agenda item.
  9. The 1:1 initiative project team has participated in over 30 school and district planning meetings to share and collect feedback to develop the best possible 1:1 initiative deployment model.
  10. All schools that will receive devices next school year have identified Chromebooks as the preferred device.
  11. Based on the majority of requests being for Phase I deployment, Information Technology will work to increase the number of devices available for the start of school in August 2019. The ability to order, acquire, configure and deploy a maximum of 6,600 devices can be achieved providing a purchase order can be submitted by April 22.
  12. Phase I is designed for schools that are currently 1:1 and not having devices at the start of school would be detrimental to staff and students.
  13. Based on feedback received so far, 75 percent of schools are requesting to participate in Phase I deployment in August 2019.
  14. Devices must be procured as soon as possible in order for schools to receive them by the start of the 2019/20 school year.
