2.01 Update: Start Time Task Force (EL-11)
Meeting
Jan 12, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information
Goals
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 3 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dan Cohan, chief of schools secondary, School Leadership

Katie Winner, parent, co-chair, Start Time Task Force

Laura Johnson, parent, co-chair, Start Time Task Force

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools elementary, School Leadership

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

Greg Jackson, executive director, Transportation & Fleet Services

Robert Jacobus, president and CEO, School Bus Logistics

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on and provide direction for next steps regarding district school start times. 

 

BACKGROUND:

Following the work completed at the direction of the Board of Education on April 14, 2021, members of the Board of Education asked leaders of the Start Time Task Force and district staff to work on additional options for the West area, to continue the support of the consultant on these efforts; and, to work with Superintendent Dorland on a timeline to present options moving forward for the Board to consider.

 

During the February, 5, 2020 Reimagining Time presentation, Start Time Task Force members shared an update, including the executive summary report from School Bus Logistics, the consultant hired to review Jeffco Public Schools start times and transportation options in 2019.  

 

The efforts of the Start Time Task Force continue to be focused on later start times for middle and high school students.

The Start Time Task Force was commissioned by the superintendent in February 2018 and met approximately every two months. Before February 2020, the task force had been researching secondary school start times and presented information to the Board of Education on February 7, 2019. 

In 2019, the Board of Education directed the hire of an implementation firm/consultant (School Bus Logistics) to create a plan for adjusting school start times so middle schools start on or after 8 a.m. and high schools start on or after 8:30 a.m. This action was one of the Start Time Task Force recommendations.

Jan 12, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session

2. Study/Dialogue Session

PRESENTING STAFF:

Brenna Copeland, chief financial officer 

Lisa Anderson, controller 

Paul Niedermuller, independent auditor 

 

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on the Annual Comprehensive Financial Report (ACFR) (formerly known as CAFR) and Annual External Audit for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2021. 

 

BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education monitors the districtwide financial condition as outlined in Board executive limitation policies EL-6, Financial Administration and EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting.

 

Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 29-1-606 (1)(b) requires that the Annual Comprehensive Financial Report (ACFR) be delivered to the Board of Education by November 30, 2021.  The Board received a draft copy of the ACFR on November 30, 2021.

 

Colorado revised Statute 22-32-109 requires the district to prepare an annual comprehensive financial report. The financial report consists of financial information prepared by the district and audited by an independent firm and indicates the financial status of the district at the end of the reporting period. The ACFR also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

 

For the 2020/2021 fiscal year, the single audit is not included within this report. Due to delays in release of audit guidance by the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) for the Coronavirus Relief Funds (CRF), the single audit was not completed at the time of publishing the ACFR and will be published as a stand-alone document.

Ed Bowditch, district legislative liaison, Bowditch & Cassell Public Affairs

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive its regularly scheduled update on legislative activities as the 2022 legislative session begins.

BACKGROUND:
In accordance with Board governance process policy GP-4, Board Job Description, the Board of Education, “may, by motion or resolution, take a position of advocacy on national, statewide or local ballot issues, referred measures, legislation or rules and regulations. The Board may, by motion or resolution, initiate action which leads to preparation and introduction of legislation deemed appropriate and important to the education mission of the Jefferson County School District.” Additionally, "with respect to providing information and counsel to the Board, the superintendent shall not fail to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed and supported..." as noted in executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board.
 

As the 2022 Colorado Legislative Session convenes today, this is the Board's first discussion of legislative activity this session.
