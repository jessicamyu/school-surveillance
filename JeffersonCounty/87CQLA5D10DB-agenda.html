5.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Feb 26, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
5. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

6.04 Supplemental Funds - CDE to Arvada Middle School (EL-6)
Meeting
Feb 26, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.
    1. This grant falls under Items C.
    2. Arvada Middle School received $30,935 from the Colorado Department of Education’s Title I School Improvement Grant. The program aligns with strategic plan school objective 4, which ensures the alignment of resources to increase student achievement and organizational effectiveness.
    3. A school support review team from CDE will examine all facets of Arvada Middle School’s operation, including curriculum, assessment, instruction, school culture, parent involvement, professional development strategies, school organization, and comprehensive planning. Part of the funds will be used to pay for the review team who will issue a report on their findings. The remaining funds will be used to contract with a consultant who will facilitate the debriefing of the report and provide time for extended and in-depth collaborative planning with staff.
    4. The project will be evaluated on accomplishing objectives.
    5. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.
    6. Rod Pugnetti, principal, will manage the project.

7.01 Appealed Decision on Learning Resource Challenges: Carrie (EL-1)
Meeting
Feb 26, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
7. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1. In accordance with district policy KEC, Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources, appeals concerning learning resources may be challenged by any Jefferson County citizen, student, parent or guardian of district student, or district employee. As stated in policy, “Challenges are heard by a committee of district parents, teachers and administrators, which submits a recommendation to the superintendent for his/her decision. The Superintendent will forward a copy of the decision to members of the Board of Education.”
2.Throughout September 2008, a parent challenged four middle level library books by Stephen King: The Girl Who Loved Tom Gordon, Christine, Carrie, and Cujo. Following policy, in October, The Division of Instruction began the process to convene four Challenge Review Committees.
3.On December 11, 2008 four Challenge Review Committees convened in accordance with district regulation, KEC-R, Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources.
4.Rick Hammans, Library Services curriculum coordinator, served as chairperson for the Challenge Review Committees. On December 16, 2008, Mr. Hammans submitted the committee’s recommendations to Superintendent, Dr. Cynthia Stevenson.
5.On January 23, 2009, the Superintendent shared her decision by letter with committee members, the appellant, and school staff.
6.On January 26, 2009, the Superintendent’s decision was appealed to the Board of Education in accordance with district regulation KEC-R, Public Concerns/Complaints about Instructional Resources.
7.On February 12, 2009 the Board of Education reviewed of the record, heard from the appellant and other interested parties, and took the appeal of the Superintendent's decision on Cujo, Carrie, Christine, and The Girl Who Loved Tom Gordon under consideration.
Please open the links provided here to review the committees' recommendation and Superintendent's decision, and the appeal of the Superintendent's decision.

7.06 Facilities Usage Committee Work (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 26, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
7. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to receive an update on the work-to-date for the Facilities Usage Committee.
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.In November 2008 during the budget development process, the Board of Education directed the superintendent to create a Facilities Usage Committee to study current facilities based on capacity, enrollment, future enrollment, needs of the district and our unique communities, special programs, future direction, educational specifications and financial stewardship. The committee will make a December 2009 recommendation to the Board of Education on any changes in facility usage, including but not limited to school closures, boundary changes, and/or re-purposing of buildings.
2.Beginning in December, Board members submitted their committee appointees. Additional opportunities for community members to volunteer are provided through an application on the Jeffco website. The committee will also have members from the three employee associations (4), PTA (1), Strategic Planning Advisory Council (1), Capital and Financial Oversight committees (1) and administration.
3.The project plan and timeline were shared with Board members on January 15, 2009. The superintendent has targeted mid-March to convene the committee and set future meeting dates. Contracting with a facilitator and data gathering for the committee is underway.

7.07 Guidelines for Staff/Students Observances of Religious Holidays (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 26, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
7. Discussion Agenda
Type
Discussion
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.Each year, the superintendent directs leadership staff to ensure no district events are scheduled in conflict with major religious holidays observed by the Jefferson County community. The district has four policies/regulations in place related to religious observances: IMDCA, Religion in the Schools; IMD, School Ceremonies and Observances; JH/JH-R, Student Absences and Excuses.
2.In September 2007, the Board of Education adopted a resolution urging the Colorado Association of School Boards (CASB) to act on observance of religious holidays.
3.The superintendent tasked the Community Superintendent’s Office to bring heightened awareness to leadership staff and support in providing work processes for administrators to ensure student and staff religious observances are honored within the Jeffco Public Schools community.
4.A committee of community members and staff met October through December 2008 and developed Religious Holiday Scheduling Guidelines to address observance of the primary holidays of our community’s three major religions: Christianity, Judaism, and Islam.
5.On February 12, the Board of Education requested the superintendent schedule time for the Board members to receive and discuss the guidelines developed.
