4.01 Wayne Carle Awards
Meeting
Oct 10, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category

Type
Recognition

estimated time: 6 p.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 

The Board of Education is pleased to have the Wayne Carle Award Recipients honored this evening and presented by the Student Engagement Office. Recipients were nominated by Jeffco staff, students, and community members. The Wayne Carle Awards are given to individuals for outstanding contributions to diversity and equity in Jeffco Public Schools. The awards are named for retired Jeffco Superintendent, Dr. Wayne Carle, and reflect Jeffco's desire to promote cultural understanding and respect.
 

2019 Wayne Carle Legacy Award recipients:

 

Administrator: Samantha Hollman, Principal

For her unwavering dedication to Diversity and Equity. Sam has always supported and encouraged growth toward representation and inclusion at Mitchell Elementary.

 

Licensed Staff: Jessica Post, High School Teacher

Jessica Post’s passion for diversity and equality has manifested in several areas of our community. Ms. Post created a multicultural perspective class which allows students to learn about other cultures, social issues, and issues within the school that students try to resolve.

 

Educational Support Professional: Andrea Syko, Family Engagement Liaison

Striving to make the world more language inclusive, Andrea Syko was instrumental in making the Jefferson County Family Leadership Training Institute accessible to participants in both English and Spanish.

 

Student: Gabrielle Davis, High School Student

A champion for social justice and inclusion. Gabrielle Davis has become a dynamic and vocal leader of the Student Voice Leadership Committee. She shows up tirelessly and always brings innovative ideas to the Student Voice planning process.

 

Community Group: Jeffco Safe Schools Coalition, Community Group
Since 2017 Jeffco Safe Schools Coalition (JSSCo) works to foster safe and inclusive learning spaces by elevating the voices and experiences of LGBTQ+ youth, families and educators while acknowledging the many different identities within that community.

Type
Information

estimated time: 6:40p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.    
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board. When more than 30 individuals sign up to speak, individuals will have one minute and groups will have three minutes to address the Board. We have more than 30 individuals who have signed up to address the Board of Education.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

    2. Centrally store log information that tracks important events such as: system logins, privileged system use, data access, system errors, network traffic trends, user rights assignments, and system configuration changes

    1. The student data reporting group when investigating FERPA concerns
    2. Achievement directors when responding to incidents such as cyber bullying and technology abuse by students

    4. HR and Benefits when responding to phishing threats
    5. Safety and Security when responding to physical threats against our schools
       
  6. Log management is an essential component of well-designed IT systems. Log management is also required for medical records (HIPAA) compliance, credit card processing (PCI) compliance, Children’s Internet Protection Act (CIPA) compliance, and for the district to maintain Cyber Liability Coverage.

There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
