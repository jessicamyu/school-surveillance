7.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Jan 11, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Category
8. Consent Agenda

Recommended Action
to authorize the director of purchasing to execute a twelve-month agreement, with three optional annual renewal periods with DHE.  The contract  will start on March 2, 2018 and will be for the purchase of Lenovo Chrome Devices with an estimated annual cost of $4,500,000. 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000. 
     
  2. RFI 24448, Chrome Devices, was issued on September 12, 2017.  The intent of the RFI was to select a manufacturers to proceed to the RFP phase.  Six manufacturers responded to the RFI - Dell, Asus, Acer, Lenovo, HP and Samsung.  The manufacturers attended a Chrome Fair on November 1, 2017 at the Ed Center to provide demonstrations of their devices to a cross section of Jeffco's school and central staff.  The manufacturers were ranked based on their RFI proposal and the scores from staff who attended the Chrome Fair.  Lenovo was the successful manufacturer.
     
  3. RFP 24516, Lenovo Chromebooks and Google licenses, was issued on November 13, 2017.  Notification was sent to vendors via email and through the Rocky Mountain E-purchasing System.  Four vendors responded - Virtucom, Trinity 2 Technology, DHE and Counter Trade.  Based on various criteria such as price, support, and warranty, the evaluation committee selected DHE as the winning proposal.
     
  4. Many teachers and students prefer Chromebooks over other mobile devices because they are easy to use, cost effective and extremely reliable.
     
  5. Chromebooks are compatible for use in all Jeffco standardized assessments, including PARCC/CMAS.
     
  6. Jeffco currently has about 40,000 Chromebook devices being used by staff and students.  In support of the 1:1 device initiative announced by the Superintendent in October 2017, it is anticipated that many of Jeffco schools will accelerate their acquisition of Chrome Devices in 2018.
     
  7. The estimated twelve-month contract amount is $4,500,000. The schools and departments will purchase the equipment, licenses and accessories from their respective budgets; student based budgets, school campus activity budgets or department budgets.  Orders will be placed centrally.

8.07 Policy Review: Section G
Meeting
Jan 11, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
8. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To approve revisions to district policy GBEB, Staff Conduct.

PERTINENT FACTS:
​

  1. In accordance with Colorado Revised Statute § 22-32-109.1, the district Board of Education should approve district policies regarding conduct and discipline codes.
     
  2. District cabinet established a task force to review district policies and regulations in order to reduce redundancies and ensure legal compliance. The task force consists of individuals from each key subject area in the district.
     
  3. Representatives of this group met with Colorado Association of School Board (CASB) staff members entrusted with overseeing policy development for school districts to evaluate Jeffco's policies and regulations.
     
  4. Resulting from this meeting was an action plan to assess policies for consistency, redundancy and legal compliance. As a part of this plan, each policy will be reviewed to determine if the policy is required by law and/or highly recommended by CASB. If so, the policy will be cross-referenced to pertinent state statutes, board and district policies and provided to the district subject-matter expert for review and update. When appropriate, community input will be solicited.
     
  5. As a part of this process, the Staff Conduct policy was recently reviewed in its entirety and updated for state law consistency and to provide staff transparency. District subject-matter experts were engaged to review this policy, compare it to the CASB models, solicit stakeholder and community input and provide policy revision recommendations.
     
  6. Proposed revisions have been reviewed by the policy review task force and all three employee association groups. Input received was incorporated, when appropriate, and the draft policy was provided to cabinet for study, input and approval.
     
  7. If adopted by the Board, this policy will be updated, posted and covered with all impacted staff members.

In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.
