2.01 Supplemental Funds - Colorado Parent & Child Foundation, JPS Family Literacy HIPPY (EL-6)
Meeting
Nov 19, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the Administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Item C.
    1. Family Literacy received $17,500 from the Colorado Parent & Child Foundation. The program aligns with strategic plan community objectives 1 and 2, instruction objectives 1, 3 and 3, and schools objectives 2 and 3.

    2. The funds are used to support the general operating costs for the HIPPY (Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters) Program. Supporting the basic tenet from the National Center for Family Literacy, the program increases the basic skills and attitudes towards education, parenting skills, children’s literacy and school readiness, and the overall quality of the parent-child relationship. Funds will allow HIPPY to provide a continuum of services for 270 children living in Title I attendance areas, schools that are considered the most educationally at-risk.
    3. Jeffco HIPPY measures success via a variety of methods, including feedback and testimonials from participants, a state-wide collaborative study with the Colorado Parent & Child Foundation, and portfolio samples for each child to monitor progress.
    4. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.
    5. Gwen Welch, Family Literacy coordinator, is the project manager.

    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A and C.
    1. The department of Diverse Learners received American Reinvestment and Recovery Act (ARRA) funds from the Colorado Department of Education, Special Education Services Unit in the amount of $265,411 to be used for the IDEA Preschool Project. These funds, authorized by the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), are supplemental to the regular formula award that is based on the number of eligible students with disabilities. The program aligns with strategic plan school objective 1, which ensures that student achievement is increasing in every school regardless of gender, race/ethnicity, socioeconomic status, or special needs.
    2. The funds will be used to expand the current Child Find team to a district-wide Child Find team serving children birth to age 21. The expansion will allow for development of a common intake, referral, screening and assessment process for children who are not currently enrolled in a Jefferson County school. Child Find screens and assesses children for significant delays or disabilities. The funds will also provide professional development regarding the appropriate use of data to drive instruction, research-based instructional interventions and progress monitoring for all children, especially those identified with special needs. In addition, professional development will address how to effectively support preschool families during the transition to kindergarten.
    3. Laura Munro, executive director of the Diverse Learners department, is the project manager.

3.01 2009 Title I Distinguished School: Stein Elementary School
Meeting
Nov 19, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
3. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Information
The Board of Education is please to honor Stein Elementary School Principal Socorro Alarcon, her staff, students and community for its achievement of the 2009 Title I Distinguished School for Closing the Achievement Gap. Stein will receive a check for $10,000, a banner and plaque to hang in the school, and the principal and one other person are invited to attend the National Title I Conference in Washington, D.C. in January 2010.

Although other Title I schools had greater decreases in their achievement gap, none in Colorado were as high-scoring as Stein Elementary School. Stein increased the performance of its Free and Reduced population from 80.58 percent in 2008 to 88.89 percent in 2009, for an increase of 7.39 percent. The non-Free and Reduced population increased student performance from 94.19 percent in 2008 to 96.88 percent in 2009, for an increase of 2.69 percent. The achievement gap was decreased by 4.70 percent.

3.02 JPS Educational Technology Awards
Meeting
Nov 19, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
3. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Information
The Board of Education is pleased to share that in October, Jeffco Public Schools had the distinction of being recognized by two national organizations for the exemplary use of technology to support teaching, learning and operations. The two organizations, the National School Boards Association's Technology + Learning conference and the Center for Digital Education, noted the district's work as follows:

The Center for Digital Education singled out Jeffco as the #1 district in the nation for their leadership and use of:
• green technology practices
• proactive communication tools, such as Messenger, to parents and staff
• integration of technology into instructional programs
• professional development
• District technology plan and its relationship to the district technology plan
• Organizational alignment of technology and instructional departments to meet the needs of a digital school district

According to the NSBA, Jeffco “was selected for developing an approach that ensures instructional needs drive every technology decision. The district’s comprehensive Digital Professional Development initiative blends the best research on professional development with new technology tools that provide educators with online courses, site-based support from teacher-librarians, and district wide digital Professional Learning Communities (PLC’s). All staff are active in either their grade level PLC for elementary educators, or a specialized content area PLC for the upper grades, plus many have elected to participate in one or more optional PLCs.

5.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Nov 19, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
5. Public Agenda
Type
Information
At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.
