3.01 Elite 8 World Status: Lakewood High School Debate Team
Meeting
Apr 5, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

(estimated duration: 5 minutes)

The Board of Education is pleased to recognize the Lakewood High School debate team of Allexandra 'Alli' Nilles, William Crimmins and Alexander Fan, and their coach Gregory Davis, for advancing to the finals in the International Public Policy Forum (IPPF) debate tournament in New York City.  From among more than three hundred teams around the world, the LHS team defeated The International School of Geneva, Switzerland and The American School of Sofia, Bulgaria to advance as one of the top eight teams in the world.

The IPPF, sponsored by the Bickel and Brewer law firm and New York University, is the only international debate tournament that combines persuasive essay writing with oral argument.  This challenging format is increasingly important now that the SAT includes an essay section.  The IPPF provides students with the opportunity to compete before a panel of some of the world's foremost experts in debate, business, journalism, law and politics.

Category

Type
Information

estimated time:  6:10 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a two minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 5 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

Recommended Action
to approve, contingent upon proof of Special Event Insurance being provided by May 31, 2011, the use of Jeffco Stadium and Miller Special parking lot for Lakewood on Parade; and, to grant a waiver to policy and regulation GBEC and KF-R for July 3, 2011.

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. Since 2009 the City of Lakewood has held a Fourth of July Festival at the Jeffco Stadium and Miller Special parking lot. The festival, which includes a fireworks program, promotes and encourages community spirit and citizen participation within Lakewood.
     
  2. Once again the Lakewood on Parade Organization has requested that the event take place on July 3, 2011 from 3:00 p.m. to approximately 10:30 p.m. and has requested use of the Jeffco Sports Stadium and Miller Special parking lot for the festival and fireworks display. The event would include the sale and consumption of alcohol on school grounds at the Stadium on July 3, 2011.
     
  3. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policy GBEC, Drug Free Workplace, with respect only to the prohibited use and possession of alcoholic beverages for this one-time event. The policy prohibits the unlawful possession and use of alcohol on district premises. It also state that “an employee knowingly in possession of, or under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance shall be suspended immediately by his principal or supervisor if such use or possession is on any district property at any time.”
     
  4. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policy KF-R, Community Use of School Facilities , with respect only to the prohibited use and possession alcoholic beverages for this one-time event. This policy states that “the use and possession of alcoholic beverages or drugs by any person or group on district property is strictly prohibited."
     
  5. Lakewood on Parade will have a Special Events liability insurance policy covering the district and other entities supporting this effort.  The City of Lakewood will provide police/fire protection for the event.
