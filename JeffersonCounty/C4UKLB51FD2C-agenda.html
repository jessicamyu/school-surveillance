2.01 North Area Aquatics Facility Presentation (EL-11)
Meeting
Sep 9, 2021 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 8:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

PRESENTING STAFF:

Steve Bell, chief operating officer
Nicole Stewart, interim chief financial officer
Tim Reed, executive director, Facilities and Construction Management

Mark Deven, city manager, City of Arvada

Gordon Reusink, director, Vibrant Community and Neighborhoods, City of Arvada 

Hillary Roemersberger, director of Recreations Services, APEX Park and Recreation District
Katie Groke, director of Marketing and Communications, APEX Park and Recreation District

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to determine next steps regarding replacement of the existing Meyers Pool through a partnership with Jeffco Public Schools, the City of Arvada and Apex Park and Recreation District. 

 

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel with the Board, this topic was brought forward at the March 4, 2021 study/dialogue session.

 

The George J. Meyers Swimming Center (Meyers Pool), owned by the City of Arvada and operated by the Apex Park and Recreation District, is almost 50 years old and in need of replacement. A plan has been developed to keep the pool in operation until a new pool opens. The estimated end of the pool's useful life is 2023.  
 

Over the course of several years, elected officials and representatives from three agencies have discussed development of an intergovernmental partnership to replace Meyers Pool: Jeffco Public Schools, Apex Park and Recreation District and the City of Arvada. 
 

A feasibility study for a new pool has been completed which examines the projected operational costs, staffing structure, market opportunities, and user preferences including amenities and site for a new pool. Preliminary cost estimates for the project are reported at $33-35 million. Final project costs will be determined once the facility has been designed.
 

A partnership agreement currently drafted provides for Jeffco Public Schools and the City of Arvada to jointly finance construction of an aquatics complex, with Apex Park and Recreation District providing ongoing management and operations of the site.

PRESENTING STAFF:

Susan Leach, chief student success officer

Matt Palaoro, executive director, Special Education

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive information regarding a proposal to implement a day treatment program for students with disabilities at the former Allendale Elementary School.

 

BACKGROUND:

 

In accordance with Board executive limitation policies EL-8, Facilities, and EL-11, Communication and Counsel with the Board, this topic was brought forward to the Board for discussion.

All students are entitled to a free and appropriate education in the least restrictive environment. For some students with disabilities, the least restrictive environment is characterized by intensive supports and services that allow them to access education. A small subset of students require supports and services that exceed that which has historically been available in the district. In such cases, the district contracts with vendors who offer specialized services for students with significant disabilities. More recently, some issues have complicated the district's ability to acquire these services for Jeffco students. First and foremost, some vendors have had to discontinue services because of staffing shortages. Further, stipulations in the Family First Act may result in reduced availability of placements for students with the most significant disabilities. The closing of Allendale Elementary presents an opportunity for the district to offer a program tailored to meet the needs of students with disabilities who have historically been served outside the district.

Subject
2.04 Update on the Review of Academic Functions (EL-11)
Meeting
Sep 9, 2021 - Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 10:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Tracy Dorland, superintendent

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to have a conversation with the superintendent about her initial work with the review of academic functions project.

 

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel with the Board, this item is brought forward to the Board.

The quality of a school district is a key factor when families are looking for a place to live. Research has shown that quality public schools are the bedrock of a strong economy and democracy. Jeffco Public Schools has historically been committed to increasing a child’s chances of future success by providing an education that is relevant and meaningful to each student. Jeffco is proud of its students and staff for their achievements both inside and outside the classroom.

 

Superintendent Tracy Dorland began work as Jeffco’s superintendent April 19, 2021. After approximately four months on the job, her key areas of focus for district work include:

  • Focus on relationships, collaboration, and a positive climate and culture with stakeholders in our district, schools, and communities.
  • Explore how our district is organized to support schools with improved student achievement and social emotional well-being.
  • Find the just-right balance between system coherence and school-based autonomy.
  • Maximize the efficiency of our resources, aligned to the goal of improved student achievement - particularly focused on closing achievement gaps.
  • Support students with additional learning opportunities to make up for lost instructional time due to the pandemic.

 

To begin her work in the 2021/22 school year, she has hired Dr. Jaime Aquino as a consultant to work with her on a districtwide review of how our central supports are organized so that our schools ensure an excellent education for every student and a top-quality work environment for every staff member.

 

This is the first of monthly updates through December on her work.
