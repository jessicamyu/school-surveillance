4.04 Superintendent Contract Amendment: Superintendent Dr. Jason E. Glass (EL-3, 4)
Meeting
May 7, 2020 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the contract amendment modifying the superintendents compensation for fiscal year 2021.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. On May 16, 2017, the Board of Education appointed Jason E. Glass, Ed.D as superintendent of schools effective July 1 and approved his superintendent’s contract. Acknowledging the community support and accomplishments of Dr. Glass, the Board of Education approved the extension of the superintendent’s employment contract on March 7, 2019 to June 30, 2025.
     
  2. Due to the recent turmoil caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, the fiscal forecast for Jeffco Public Schools is uncertain as the economic impacts of the disease continue to reverberate throughout the nation.
     
  3. This uncertainty has caused the Board of Education to consider limiting pay increases for employees and may cause additional cost-saving measures during the 2020/2021 budget year.
     
  4. As originally set forth in the superintendent contract, Dr. Glass’s compensation is indexed to inflation and would result in a pay increase for the 2020/2021 budget year.
     
  5. Dr. Glass contacted the Board on April 8, 2020, requesting his 2020/2021 compensation be amended to reflect any changes that may be incurred by Jeffco Public Schools staff and waiving his right to receive an inflationary increase.
     
  6. The Board of Education accepted Dr. Glass’s offer and moves forward with an amendment to the May 16, 2017 contract as presented.

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. The Colorado Department of Public Health & Environment awarded $68,194 to Choice Programs to develop and implement a comprehensive human sexuality education curriculum for students with severe cognitive delays and emotional disabilities. The two-year project aligns with Strategic Plan Learning Tactic: Embracing the Full Range of the Human Experience. Health Education Content Specialist Jaime Grimm-Rice and Director of Choice Programs Marna Messer oversee the initiative.

6.02 Contract Award: Partial Replacement Alameda International Jr./Sr. High School Construction Agreement (EL-7, 8)
Meeting
May 7, 2020 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the award of a contract to GH Phipps Construction Companies in the amount of $25,941,546 for the Partial Replacement of Alameda International Jr./Sr. High School; and, to authorize the executive director, Facilities & Construction Management, to execute the contract documents.

estimated time: 7 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000.”
  2. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-8, Facilities, “…facilities shall not be unsafe, inadequate or unnecessary.”  Information on Jeffco Public Schools construction projects is available online with a review of the work.
  3. The Construction Manager/General Contractor (CMGC) contract award is to construct a partial replacement of Alameda International Jr./Sr. High School, renovate portions of the existing building and site redevelopment. The project consists of:
    1. Demolishing the west 39 classrooms and the library consisting of 95,300 square feet (SF).
    2. Constructing a southeast addition of 64,135 SF consisting of 20 classrooms, relocated main entrance, secure entry, student commons, library/media center, music suite with band/orchestra and choral, theatrical support, administrative suite, career/counseling suite, staff commons and work area.
    3. Renovating existing spaces to include career and technical education space, art, cafeteria, business and graphic arts, west performance/athletic entrance, finish upgrades, fire sprinkler system throughout, mechanical system upgrades, roofing, plumbing and electrical upgrades, and LED lighting.
    4. Site improvements include entry drive, queue space from West Louisiana Avenue and additional parking at new southeast entry, new soccer field at the northwest portion of site, four relocated tennis courts, improved site drainage and stormwater detention, additional hardscape and outdoor learning areas and landscaping.
  4. The original design included classroom space for 6th grade students. In March 2020 it was determined that those students would remain in their assigned elementary schools. The architect and contractor removed six classrooms from the addition reducing the square footage from 69,735 SF to 64,135 SF.
  1. The Facilities and Construction Management office maintains a list of pre-qualified CM/GC and general contractors to provide construction services.  Following district policy FEG, Construction Contracts Bidding and Awards, and DJ/DJA, Purchasing/Purchasing Authority, relating to the selection criteria for capital projects, staff contacted 20 pre-qualified general contractors.  Six pre-qualified contractors were selected to submit qualifications and fees for the project.
  2. GH Phipps Construction Companies was selected and hired February 8, 2019 to provide Design Phase Services and construction manager/general contractor (CM/GC) services.  The district entered into an agreement with GH Phipps Construction Companies of $35,000.
  3. On February 14, 2020 a change order was issued to GH Phipps Construction Companies in the amount of $29,232 for exploratory underground testing to confirm soil conditions.
  4. April 10, 2020 GH Phipps Construction Companies submitted a Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP) that includes the aforementioned contracts and services in the amount of $25,941,546. The GMP includes contingencies of $1,343,360 for estimating and construction.
  5. The COVID-19 pandemic is a factor on projects under construction or soon to be. Contractor partners are working within local, state and federal guidelines for personal protection equipment (PPE), social distancing and other recommendations.
  6. Funding for the work is the 2018 Capital Improvement Program. This is a one-time capital investment without ongoing costs. GH Phipps Construction Companies is a Greenwood Village, Colorado based company.
  7. The work is scheduled to begin in May 2020 with project completion in August 2021. As essential work, district construction projects in the 2018 Capital Improvement Program and are moving ahead following county public health directives on social distancing, PPE and other measures. Though schedules may shift from original timelines, work will be completed within budget.
