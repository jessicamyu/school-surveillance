The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda only. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. 10.03 Contract Award: Storage Infrastructure Upgrade (EL-7)
Meeting
Oct 3, 2013 - Regular Meeting
Category
10. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to negotiate and execute an agreement with EMC for the purchase of the next generation of storage infrastructure.
Goals
EL-7, Asset Protection

estimated time: 9:02 p.m. (estimated duration: 5 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation, EL-7, Asset Protection, "the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $250,000."
     
  2. The district must upgrade its current data storage environment to improve application performance.
     
  3. Low storage capacity is currently impacting system performance.
     
  4. By purchasing updated storage infrastructure, the district will be able to resume 100 percent uptime with normal application delivery as well as support the demand for increased capacity and performance.
     
  5. The district needs to purchase new technology for the data storage environment that will be under warranty and reduce projected increased maintenance costs (approximately $250,000 annually) on the current platform.
     
  6. Increasing demands driven by growth in classroom technology utilization has accelerated district storage needs. The district has seen a substantial increase in computing this school year which had a profound impact on the core equipment including storage.
     
  7. This is a direct purchase from the manufacturer EMC. The original decision to use the EMC storage system was made in 2009 through a competitive purchase process.
     
  8. The proposed pricing for the EMC storage infrastructure upgrade totals $1,197,979 and includes hardware, software, licensing and EMC support for the Denver West and Quail data centers.
     
  9. System capacity and stability are critical in support of classroom instruction. Awarding a contract the first week of October will allow the district to meet the demands of the organization and avoid issues based on anticipated demand which typically occurs at the end of semesters.
