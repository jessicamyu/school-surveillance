4.01 2011 Institution Award-Office of Diversity and Inclusion
Meeting
Dec 1, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

The Board of Education is pleased to honor Jeffco's Office of Diversity and Inclusion which was nationally recognized with the 2011 Institution Award from the National Association for Multicultural Education (NAME) in November.  NAME is a nonprofit organization that recognizes innovative, outstanding work making major contributions in the field of multicultural education.  Dr. Irene Griego, director, and Dr. Angelique Acevedo-Barron, assistant director, accepted the award at the 21st annual international conference.  

By promoting understanding, respect and appreciation of diversity, the office works to address the achievement gap in Jeffco.  Success lies in improving school climates, mediating issues of difference and improving connections to schools.  School principals receive monthly diversity training sessions and more than 80 students from every Jeffco high school make up Student Voices, a group to develop student leadership.
 

Past recipients of the Institution Award include the Southern Poverty Law Center (1996), The Smithsonian Institution-Center for Folklife and Cultural Studies (1998), Children's Book Press (1999), and Purdue Cooperative Extension Service (2005).

5.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Dec 1, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  Thank you.

6.02 Employee Benefit Cafeteria Plan Changes and HIPAA Certification (EL-3)
Meeting
Dec 1, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the plan document changes as presented.
Goals
EL-3, Staff Treatment

PERTINENT FACTS: 

  1. The following employee benefit documents are brought forward for the Board of Education review and approval following Board Policy EL-3, Staff Treatment, in order “ . . . . to provide staff with an opportunity to become familiar with their right under this policy . . . “ and in support of the Board’s Ends Policy 7, “Every employee will work in a positive workplace environment."
     
  2. The district maintains a Cafeteria Plan for the benefit of the district and its employees.  The Plan document provides the formal guidance for how the district manages the provision of benefits that are tax-advantaged for employees and the district.  IRS rules require that the district have a formal written document.
     
  3. The Flexible Spending Account (FSA) portion of the plans operate on a calendar year basis and thus do not align with the district’s other health and welfare plan years.  The Benefits Advisory Committee has recommended that the FSA Plan align with the fiscal year, beginning with a short, transitional plan year of 6 months (January 1, 2012 through June 30, 2012) and then operate on an ongoing basis on a 12-month fiscal-year basis. 
     
  4. Effective July 1, 2011, the district implemented a high-deductible health plan with United Healthcare.  This plan qualifies participants to contribute towards a health savings account (HSA). Due to the implementation of the PeopleSoft Human Resources Management System (PSHRMS) upgrade in July, limited programming changes were possible at that time. Further, the district requested, and has now received, clarification from PERA with regards to how HSA contributions are treated from a PERA, includible salary, standpoint.   This has allowed us to move forward with establishing a before-tax opportunity for participants to save towards their health care expenses.  In order to offer this before-tax contribution opportunity, the Cafeteria Plan document must be amended. 
     
  5. The Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) Certification has been updated and reviewed by the district’s benefits attorney for corresponding changes and must be approved prior to December 31, 2011.

Recommended Action
to approve the district calendar for 2012/2013 as presented.

estimated time: 7:00 pm (estimated duration: 15 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. During 2010-2011 a Calendar Committee was approved by the Board of Education.  That committee met beginning in February of 2011.  The list of members is attached.
     
  2. The committee had two charges.  Their charges are listed here. 
  • Recommend the district calendars for the school years 2012-2013, 2013-2014, and 2014-2015 according to district policies IC/ICA, district regulations IC/ICA-R1 and IC/ICA-R2, and Board Policy EL-14, School Year Calendar. 
  • Study and make recommendations related to the amount of time in the school year and the use of time for students and for staff.  Those recommendations could include but are not limited to the following:  the length and structure of the school year, the use of time for teacher staff development and planning, the use and structure of the school day. 
  • The committee may make the recommendation that other committees or task forces be charged with specific recommendations related to time. 
  1. The committee developed three years of recommended calendars but did, in fact, recommend that a different group consider alternatives in the use of teacher and student time. 
     
  2. The committee fulfilled its charge with distinction.  However, they did not include the reduced teacher work year in their proposal.  Nor did they recommend days for early release.
     
  3. The superintendent modified the teacher and student year to meet the needs of the reduced work year.  The early release days and snow days were also scheduled so that families can plan around those days.
     
  4. EL-14, School Year Calendar, states that the calendar will be approved in three year increments.  At this point, the superintendent is recommending that only one year be approved for the calendar for these reasons.
  • The calendar is changed from the recommendations of the committee.
  • Furlough days must be negotiated and are not certain this year or in the future.
  • The work year has been permanently reduced along with compensation and predicting the work year after 2012-2013 is very difficult. 
  • As part of our Call to Action, Jeffco will be considering expanded time for learning and for staff development.
