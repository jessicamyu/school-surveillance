John D'Orazio, principal, Molholm Elementary

 

PURPOSE:

To provide an update on the first year outcomes of the Jefferson Area Plan as well as to note key foci for the 2016-17 school year in support of school effectiveness and student achievement.

 

BACKGROUND:

Approved in the spring of 2015 for implementation during the 2015-16 school year, the Jefferson Area Plan is a framework developed to leverage enhancements in school support systems, teacher efficacy, and articulation coherence to ensure significant gains in student academic and post-secondary readiness within three years.  The framework consists of Three Pillars: Culture and Engagement, Professional Learning Communities, and Language for Achievement.  An update was provided to the Board of Education on October 1, 2015.

John Fleck, training director, Colorado Building and Trades

Marna Messer, director, Choice Programing Jeffco

Gina Yacovetto-Rivas, principal, Arvada High School

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on pre-apprenticeship program in Jeffco Public Schools.
 

BACKGROUND:

Jeffco Public Schools supports many opportunities for students to access internships and apprenticeships while in high school.  This presentation will highlight one example of a pre-apprenticeship program that is growing at Arvada High School in partnership with Warren Tech.  Presenters will represent the public and private partnership that will help Jeffco students gain access to skilled trade opportunities through this pre-apprenticeship program.

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to review the resolutions directing the efforts of the CASB during the 2017 legislative session and which provide guidance to the Board's selected delegate to the Fall Conference and Delegate Assembly on October 14-15, 2016.

BACKGROUND:
The Colorado Association of School Boards (CASB) holds its annual Fall Conference and Delegate Assembly in October in preparation for legislative work in Colorado on public education.

At the Delegate Assembly, positions are taken on resolutions which affect state school districts.  As one of the largest school districts in the state, the work of CASB may impact the district's work.
