At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

6.09 District Option Program: Home Options (EL-11)
Meeting
Apr 10, 2008 - Regular Meeting 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1. As directed in Board executive limitation policy 11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, the superintendent shall, "...advise the Board in a timely manner of relevant trends, facts and information, including significant organizational changes."
2. In 1996 the HOPE Home Option program (not to be confused with HOPE On-Line Learning Academy), began as a district home school option supplemental program under the direction of the principal at D'Evelyn High School. After several changes, it is currently under the supervision of Excel Academy Charter School and serves approximately 320 home schooled students.
3.To avoid confusion with HOPE On-Line Learning Academy, the new district option program will be know as Home Options program.
4.In August 2007, Home Options submitted an application to become a charter school. Upon advice of legal counsel the application was withdrawn because Home Options is not a complete educational program and therefore does not fit the definitions contained in law for charter school status.
5.Home Options expressed a desire to move under the direct supervision of the school district as an Option program within the district Choice program. Discussions between Home Options and district representatives have reached an agreement for this move to take place beginning with the 2008-09 school year.
6.The district will request two waivers from the Colorado Department of Education: 1) licensure requirement for all part time teaching staff; 2) non-licensed program managers to evaluate teaching staff in the program. These two waivers facilitate staffing the Home Options program.
7.The waiver regarding part time teaching staff will not change the requirement that staff will meet the "highly qualified" standard, only not be required to be licensed. This is the district standard currently applied to charter school employees. Part time teachers will obtain a Colorado Substitute Teacher's license as a condition of employment.
8.The district will also waive its requirement that the program manager of the Home Options program possess a Colorado Administrator's license; state requirements will be applied. This is the standard currently in use at district charter schools.

To be informed of the change in status of the Home Options program from a charter school supplemental program to a district Choice program; and, to begin the process of requesting waivers from the Colorado Department of Education regarding the licensure of teachers and to allow non-licensed program managers to evaluate staff in this program; and, to approve the waiver from district policy requiring licensure for the school's program manager.

Motion by Sue A Marinelli, second by Dave J Thomas.

Apr 10, 2008 - Regular Meeting 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.On February 19, 2008, bids for the Sierra Elementary School Phase I replacement project were received from four qualified bidders for the above reference work.
2.All four bids were significantly over budget, therefore; on March 1, 2008 all bids were rejected.
3.On March 4, 2008, staff interviewed two Construction Manager/General Contractors (CM/GC) for the project.
4.The target Guaranteed Maximum Price contract value exceeds the construction budget. The budget/target contract value has been discussed with the Capital Improvement Oversight Committee and is funded by the 2005-2010 Capital Improvement Program with the overage being managed by the CIP contingency fund.

Motion & Voting

To approve the award of contract to Himmelman Construction for the Sierra Elementary School Phase I replacement project; and, to authorize the executive director of Construction Management to execute a contract with the CM/GC for a Guaranteed Maximum Price of $3,500,000.
