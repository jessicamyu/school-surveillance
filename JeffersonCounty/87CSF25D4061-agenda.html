PERTINENT FACTS:

1.The Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, requires that the Board's positions on pending legislation and regulations be communicated to members of the Colorado General Assembly and the Executive Branch of state government. In establishing its work schedule for the 2007-2008 school year, the Board of Education directed the Superintendent to schedule special meetings in February, March and April, 8-9 a.m. in the Seminar room to discuss pending legislation in the 2008 legislative session. These special meetings take the place of legislative discussions at regular Board meetings.
2.The Board of Education will discuss its priorities and focus for discussions of pending legislation for the 2008 General Session dealing with education issues.
3.The 2008 legislative session will produce a number of bills of interest to Jefferson County Public Schools. The district contract lobbyist will bring legislation to the Board of Education for positions and direction.
4.The district’s contract lobbyist provides state legislators with the Board’s positions on legislation.

2.01 Community Engagement - Strategic Planning and Advisory Council (SPAC)
Meeting
Mar 11, 2008 - Special Meeting-Community Engagement 3 p.m.
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session 5:30 p.m.
Type
Discussion
PRESENTING STAFF:
none

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to engage the members of the Strategic Planning and Advisory Council (SPAC) in a discussion of the work of the Board of Education to meet the needs of the students of Jefferson County. The discussion will be framed through the Board’s Ends 1 policy, “All students explore and develop their potential as productive global citizens in a 21st century environment.”

The following questions will be posed to members of SPAC:
  1. What skills will students need for jobs we don't know will exist?
  2. How can we tell if a child is a continual learner?
  3. What does a global perspective mean to you?

BACKGROUND:
In setting its meeting agenda, the Steering Committee of the SPAC requested time with the Board of Education to determine how best the SPAC may assist and advise the Board of Education in its work.
