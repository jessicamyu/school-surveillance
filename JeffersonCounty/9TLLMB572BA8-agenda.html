5.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Mar 5, 2015 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:40 p.m.  (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.    
 

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda and any Part Two comments for up to one hour. After one hour, any remaining Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

The final list of speakers will be posted below prior to the meeting and after 4:30 p.m.

The group developed the Jefferson Area Plan and presented the framework of the plan to their staffs on Wednesday, December 3, 2014.   The plan was shared with Jefferson area families and announced to local media on Friday, December 5, 2014.   Information was shared with Jeffco Schools employees via the Messenger and Chalk Talk the following week.
     

  3. The framework addresses the social-emotional needs of highly-impacted students residing in the Jefferson area and provides structures to establish an environment that supports high expectations for all students to achieve at a high level.
     
  4. The framework focuses on strategies that can be implemented in a timely and cost effective manner.  The practices contained in the framework are supported by research and experiences connected to the student learners of the Jefferson area.  The key tenants of the framework are focused on student and staff needs and they provide the systemic supports the area principals know can make a significant impact on achievement and options for post-secondary endeavors.   
     
  5. The Jefferson Area Plan framework was created as the culmination of this process. The three key elements of the plan provide strategies related to: 1) people, 2) structures, and 3) programming.   
     
  6. In January 2015, meetings with staff and community were held to gather input and provide additional direction on the proposed Jefferson Area Plan.  Community meetings were presented in English and Spanish utilizing the parent liaisons and dual language translators.  The Office of Student Engagement supported the meetings and parent outreach efforts.
     
  7. Details of the Jefferson Area Plan were shared with the Board of Education on January 5, and February 19, 2015 as input was received and reflected in the developing proposal.
     
  8. A Jefferson Area Staff Advisory Committee, comprised of teachers, classified staff, and administrators from each of the schools in the Jefferson area, was formed to provide guidance and direction on the final plan constructed from the original framework to be implemented for the 2015-16 school year.  The committee has met twice and will continue to meet through the spring of 2015 to finalize specific strategies/programs for implementation in the 2015-16 school year and beyond.  The committee will also develop, with support from the Assessment Department, the metrics to assess and progress monitor the key elements of the final plan.
     
  9. The director of gifted and talented programming facilitated the migration of the GT Center program serving grades 7 and 8 from WR 5-8 to Everitt Middle School.  This move enables identified gifted learners the ability to select center programming from 1st grade through 12th grade within the Wheat Ridge High School articulation area.
     
  10. Additional community meetings were held in February 2015 with both the Jefferson Area School-level Accountability Committee chairs as well as the community served by Jefferson area schools.
     
  11. The plan does not include:

·         a request for innovation status.

·         a MOU with the employee association(s).

·         a change in boundary for any school in the Jefferson articulation area.

·         a change in Wheat Ridge articulation schools or school boundaries.

EL-7, Asset Protection

Estimated time: 9:10 pm (Estimated duration:  15 minutes)

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $250,000.
     
  2. RFP 23077, Slate (tablets) Devices, was issued through Rocky Mountain Online Bid System vendors and 14 additional vendors in February 2012. 
     
  3. The RFP and the resulting contracts contain language that allows the district to review additional new slate technology as it becomes available during the term of this contract. Based on the review, additional vendors or products may be added to the list of approved and supported devices therefor allowing the district to continue to bring the latest technology to students and staff.
     
  4. On April 5, 2012, the Board of Education authorized the director of purchasing to negotiate and execute contracts with multiple vendors for a one-year period with four renewal options. 
     
  5. This is the third renewal requested of the original 2012 awards.
     
  6. Current contracts are with Apple Inc. and GovConnection.
     
  7. Slate devices are aligned with key objectives of the Call to Action.
     
  8. Teachers and students use this technology to increase efficiency and effectiveness while enriching classroom instruction. Many schools and departments have been utilizing slate technologies for over three years and have participated in training presented by Educational Research and Design. 
     
  9. The estimated annual renewal contract amount is $3,000,000, and the schools and departments will purchase the equipment, applications, and accessories from their budgets. Orders will be placed centrally.
     
  10. The current contract for procuring Slate devices (iPads and Galaxy tablets) will expire on March 31, 2015.  This is the third of 4 renewals from a comprehensive RFP that was completed in 2012 to ensure that the district receives the best possible devices for student use.
     
  11. This contract is in addition to the Chromebook contract that was approved by the Board of Education in August, 2014.
     
  12. The use of Slate devices continues to increase in the instructional environment and is aligned with the key objectives in the Call to Action and district technology plan.
     
  13. The total annual contract amount is estimated at $3,000,000 which equates to approximately 6,000 devices.
     
  14. Schools and departments will purchase the devices and accessories from their budgets.  The district purchasing department will place all orders centrally.
     
  15. Current school acquisition for mobile devices over the past 18 months is outlined below.  There are over 21,000 mobile devices utilized for instruction at school sites.  Of that, approximately 12,000 are iPad/Galaxy tablets and approximately 8,000 are Chromebooks.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.    Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.  

 

At this time, in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
