Mar 5, 2018 - Special Meeting: Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 9 a.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

PRESENTING STAFF:
Jason E. Glass, superintendent
Susan Meek, communication director, Great Education Colorado
Ken Delay, executive director, Colorado Association of School Boards

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on the Great Schools, Thriving Communities ballot initiative currently endorsed by AFT-Colorado, Colorado Association of School Boards, Colorado Association of School Executives, Colorado PTA, Colorado Rural Schools Alliance, Colorado School Finance Project, Great Education Colorado, NAACP, Support Jeffco Kids and Teach Plus.
 

BACKGROUND:

The Great Schools, Thriving Communities campaign was inspired by a diverse and inclusive coalition of education-connected organizations working to advance better and more equitable funding of public schools through a ballot initiative in 2018. Great Schools, Thriving Communities believes:

  • Every student needs the opportunity to reach his or her full potential and to participate meaningfully in the civic and economic life of the community.
  • The Colorado way of life should be about every student having the chance to succeed regardless of their zip code or their learning needs.
  • A strong economy requires quality public education as it develops a quality workforce that will drive a vibrant Colorado economy for decades to come.

One of the endorsing organizations, Great Education Colorado, will present on the initiative.The mission of Great Education Colorado is to harness the power of grassroots activism and stimulate wise investment in Colorado’s public schools, colleges and universities. Its vision is for all Colorado children – regardless of where they live or how they learn – graduate prepared to lead their best lives.

estimated time: 10:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Nicole Stewart, budget and treasury director

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will provide direction on priorities and placeholders for development of the 2018/2019 proposed budget.

 

BACKGROUND:

Development of the 2018/2019 budget was discussed at the November 16 and February 1 Board of Education meetings. At the March 1 Board of Education meeting, the Board received information for consideration on budget priorities to direct development of the 2018/2019 budget that included a financial update on the current year; revenue assumptions per the state's fiscal outlook and estimated funding for Jeffco; expenditure assumptions; and, budget priorities as identified by Cabinet, staff and district leadership, and the community.

 

Direction from the Board on priorities and placeholders at this time will enable staff to begin building the 2018/2019 proposed budget.
