Recommended Action
to provide direction on the membership structure of the DAC and feedback on the revised draft DAC bylaws.

estimated time: 5:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)


PRESENTING STAFF:

Terry Elliott, chief school effectiveness officer

Julie Oxenford O’Brian, DAC Chair

Orin Levy, DAC Chair-elect

 

PURPOSE:

To gain support from the Board of Education on the establishment of a district accountability committee (DAC), direction on the membership structure of the DAC, and feedback on the revised draft DAC bylaws reflecting the Board suggestions from September 3 discussion.

 

BACKGROUND:

On August 27, the Board of Education reviewed draft bylaws for a DAC and provided suggestions to staff for the next discussion.

 

On September 15, a transitional DAC held a meeting to begin the work in support of Jeffco Schools.  The group reviewed the DAC revised draft bylaws and during a productive conversation identified several items to adjust and revise in order to enhance the DAC’s work in support of school-level accountability committees (SACs), operating practices, decision making model, and proper communication to the Board of Education.  The group committed to continuing to work towards a comprehensive set of bylaws ensuring a parent centered committee that provides meaningful insight to the Board of Education and enables two-way communication with the SACs.
 

With requested direction from the Board, the DAC will meet in October to continue efforts to support Jeffco Schools and finalize draft bylaws for review and acceptance by the Board in November 2015.

2.03 Teacher Dismissal Matter: Act on Hearing Officer's Findings of Fact and Recommendation (EL-3, 10, 12)
Meeting
Sep 24, 2015 - Special Meeting-S/D Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To review the hearing officer's findings of fact and recommendation and enter a written order taking one of the following three actions: that the teacher be dismissed, the teacher be retained, or the teacher be placed on a one-year probation.

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 5 minutes) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS: 

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policies EL-3, Staff Treatment, EL-10, Treatment of Students, and EL-12, School Safety, the following is brought forward to the Board of Education. 
     
  2. On November 6, 2014, the superintendent submitted to the Board of Education a recommendation for dismissal of a teacher, pursuant to the provisions of the Teacher Employment, Compensation, and Dismissal Act of 1990, C.R.S., Section 22-63-101 et seq. ("The Act").
     
  3. A hearing on the superintendent's recommendation to dismiss a teacher was held before an independent hearing officer.  The hearing officer at the conclusion of a four-day hearing issued written findings of fact and a recommendation that the teacher be dismissed. 
     
  4. Pursuant to the provisions of The Act, the Board of Education must review the hearing officer's findings of fact and recommendation and enter a written order taking one of the following three actions:  that the teacher be dismissed, the teacher be retained, or the teacher be placed on a one-year probation.  The Board will receive the hearing officer's decision prior to the meeting.
