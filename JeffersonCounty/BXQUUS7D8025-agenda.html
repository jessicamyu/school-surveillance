Subject
2.01 Return of Secondary Students to 100% In-Person Learning (EL-11) / Regreso de los estudiantes de educación secundaria (intermedia y preparatoria) a la instrucción en persona al 100% (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 23, 2021 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Information

estimated time: 1 p.m. (estimated duration: 15 minutes) / Hora estimada: 1 p.m. (duración estimada: 15 minutos)

PRESENTING STAFF / PERSONAL EXPOSITOR:

Kristopher Schuh, interim superintendent / superintendente interino

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board to receive a report on the announcement of the date secondary students will return to 100% in-person learning. / Que la Junta de Educación reciba un informe sobre el anuncio de la fecha en que los estudiantes de educación secundaria (intermedia y preparatoria) regresarán a la instrucción en persona al 100%.

 

BACKGROUND:

Following feedback from stakeholders including the Board of Education on February 10, secondary principals, and public health guidance and modeling, district leadership has worked on logistics for Jeffco Public Schools to change from hybrid to 100% in-person learning in second semester. / De acuerdo a la información obtenida de las partes interesadas, incluida la Junta Directiva de Educación el 10 de febrero, de los directores/as de escuelas secundarias, y orientación y modelado de la salud pública, el liderazgo del distrito ha trabajado en la logística para que las Escuelas Públicas de Jeffco cambien de instrucción híbrida a la instrucción en persona al 100% en el segundo semestre.

 

After Winter Break, grades K-5 returned to in-person learning on January 19 and grades 6-12 returned to hybrid learning on January 25. / Después de las vacaciones de invierno, los estudiantes de kínder a 5º grado volvieron a la instrucción en persona el 19 de enero y los de 6º a 12º grado volvieron a la instrucción híbrida el 25 de enero.

 

Jeffco Public Schools had been in a full remote learning model, with the exception of preschool, significantly disabled students and career/technical students, November 16 (secondary students) and November 30 (elementary students) through Winter Break due to the community spread of COVID-19 within Jefferson County. / Las Escuelas Públicas de Jeffco habían participado en un modelo de instrucción a distancia total, con la excepción de los estudiantes de preescolar, los significativamente discapacitados o en educación profesional/técnica, desde el 16 de noviembre (estudiantes de educación secundaria) y desde el 30 de noviembre (estudiantes de primaria) hasta las vacaciones de invierno, debido a la propagación comunitaria del COVID-19 en el condado de Jefferson.

 

Aligned with its adoption of the June 17 resolution, Affirming Fundamental Responsibilities in Response to COVID-19, the Board of Education has been kept informed as Jeffco Public Schools has moved forward to provide instruction and engagement with students. Presentations have been shared on May 13, June 4, June 10, August 11, September 3, November 5, November 11 and December 16, 2020 and January 7, and 13, and February 10, 2021. / De acuerdo con la adopción de la resolución del 17 de junio, Afirmación de responsabilidades fundamentales en respuesta a COVID-19, la Junta Directiva de Educación se ha mantenido informada a la vez que las Escuelas Públicas de Jeffco procedieron a proporcionar instrucción e interacción con los estudiantes. Las presentaciones se han compartido el 13 de mayo, el 4 de junio, el 10 de junio, el 11 de agosto, el 3 de septiembre, el 5 de noviembre, el 11 de noviembre y el 16 de diciembre de 2020 y el 7 de enero y el 13 y el 10 de febrero de 2021.

 

Following the direction provided by the Board of Education on June 17 to maximize in-person learning to the extent possible given public health guidance, except for the period prior to Winter Break, Jeffco Public Schools had elementary PK-5 schools in 100% in-person and grades 6-12 in a hybrid (50% in-person/remote) learning model with K-12 options for 100% remote learning. / Siguiendo la dirección proporcionada por la Junta de Educación el 17 de junio para maximizar la instrucción en persona en la medida de lo posible, dada la directriz de salud pública, excepto para el período previo a las vacaciones de invierno, las Escuelas Públicas de Jeffco tuvieron el modelo de instrucción 100% en persona en escuelas primarias PK-5 e híbrida para los grados 6 a 12 (50% en persona/a distancia) con opciones de instrucción 100% a distancia para K a12º grado.

 

The RestartJeffco webpage provides the Jeffco Public Schools Covid-19 Dashboard – now including active quarantines, vaccinations offered and doses administered – and the full plan and its highlights plus resources for parents, students and staff, real-time updates of frequently asked questions for students, families and staff. / La Página web de RestartJeffco proporciona el Tablero de Covid-19 (Dashboard) de las Escuelas Públicas de Jeffco, que ahora incluye las cuarentenas activas, las vacunas ofrecidas y dosis administradas, y el plan completo y sus puntos más destacados, además de recursos para padres, estudiantes y personal, actualizaciones en tiempo real de las preguntas para los estudiantes, las familias y el personal.

2.03 Update on State Assessments During COVID19 (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 23, 2021 - Special Meeting
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information
Goals
Ends 2 - Student Achievement
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 2:15 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

Carol Eaton, executive director, Instructional Data Services

 

PURPOSE:

To engage in a discussion with the Board of Education about state assessments during the COVID19 pandemic through a series of guided questions.  In accordance with Board Executive Limitations Policy 11, "...the superintendent shall not fail to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow Board members to be adequately informed and supported".

 

BACKGROUND:

As a district, Jeffco Schools is committed to timely progress monitoring of student achievement and growth through our district assessments of NWEA MAP and Acadience.  Board Ends Policy 2 states, "Every school and the district will ensure that every student has the opportunity to work towards being connected to

career, college and/or life aspirations...with the expectation that:

  • Every student will demonstrate a year or more of growth on their way to mastery of Colorado Academic Standards (Content Mastery competency".

State assessments serve a different purpose and use, in that they provide a reflection on end of year summative performance across schools, districts, and the state.  Last spring, the Colorado General Assembly called for the creation of a stakeholder group of Colorado education leaders to advise policymakers on what, if any, changes should be made to state law around testing and accountability because of the COVID-19 crisis. This fall, the Colorado Department of Education convened a COVID-19 Policy Implications Stakeholder Group with representatives from a wide range of stakeholders across Colorado, including Jeffco Public Schools. Their final recommendations have been posted on the CDE website.
