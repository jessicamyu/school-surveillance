estimated time:  5:30 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 2 hours)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online is available 7 am Sunday and closes 1 pm Wednesday or the day before the Board Meeting.  During sign up hours, click here to sign up to speak to the Board of Education at the Board Meeting.   

 

The Board listens to concerns on any topic for up to two hours. Public comment can also be submitted to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of the presentation. We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board,  they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker.

 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de interés de la comunidad en los Comentarios del Público de su agenda. Registrarse en línea aquí para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva de Educación. (estará disponible a las 10 a.m. del lunes)

Por el momento, la Junta atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados a items on the agenda hasta por dos hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que ha completado su información en la lista para Comentarios del público y tiene el equipo e internet necesarios para participar de una reunión virtual mediante Zoom puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por orador individual. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público. Se enviarán instrucciones adicionales por correo electrónico a aquellos que se registraron para los Comentarios del público. (ej.: enlace para la participar de la reunión) antes de la reunión.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. Por esta razón, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas que como grupo se registraron para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, ellos tendrán 10 minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se registran más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo hacia la Junta.

La Junta escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

 

Rogamos abrir el anexo siguiente el día 11 de agosto después de las 8 a.m., para conocer el orden de oradores invitados a la reunión para el comentario público.

To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for November 2022

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

Formula grants are noncompetitive awards for which the allocation is derived from a predetermined formula (e.g., number of students receiving a specific instructional program). Competitive grant awards are based on selection after a review of all applications.    

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

 

1.  Career and Technical Education (CTE) received the annual Perkins Federal Grant allocation via the Colorado Community College System. The grant of $456,180 will cover salaries, teacher professional development, equipment and instructional supplies. CTE Coordinator Shelley Goerdt from Choice Programming manages the grant.

 

2.  The City of Lakewood awarded $93,133 to Green Gables Elementary through the Neighborhood Participation Grant Program. Funds will be used to construct a walking trail at Green Gables. The trail will be accessible to the public after school hours or when the building is not in use. Green Gables Principal Jennifer Withee oversees the project.

 

3.  The Colorado Office of Early Childhood (OEC) awarded round two of one-time Child Care Operations Stabilization and Workforce Sustainability grant. Irwin Preschool at Warren Tech North received $30,393 as part of OEC’s Child Care Stimulus Grants Program (passing through federal funds). Grant amounts are determined based on childcare center license capacity and distributed over a nine-month period. These unplanned funds are for personnel, facility costs, equipment, supplies or mental health services for children and educators. Principal Matthew Walsh administers the program.

 

4.  Community First Foundation awarded $18,500 to support the Regional Opportunities for Thriving Schools initiative. Funds will be used for events bringing together consolidating school communities with the aim of a seamless transition for students, families and staff. Chief of Strategy & Communications Lisa Relou manages the work.

3.19 Contract Approval: Brainfuse, Inc.
Meeting
Nov 10, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To authorize the director of Purchasing to execute a contract with Brainfuse, Inc. for the acquisition of high dosage tutoring services at an estimated amount of $560,000 for 12 months of service.

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. To inform the Board of an expenditure greater than $500,000.

 

  1. The district completed a Request for Proposal (RFP) process for Core Instruction Intervention Resources. RFP 26140 was reviewed by over 70 vendors; 24 vendors provided proposals. One (1) vendor, Brainfuse, was selected to provide direct tutoring support services for students.

 

  1. Throughout the COVID-19 pandemic, schools, teachers, students, and families have strived to keep students safe and healthy, and to keep their learning moving forward. Early data indicates that school closures and disruptions in SY19-20 and SY20-21 are likely to result in unfinished learning for many students statewide, making multi-year recovery and acceleration supports even more crucial. 

 

  1. In order to ensure additional supports are provided to best facilitate learning acceleration, Jeffco Public Schools is implementing a high-dosage tutoring program focused on early literacy development in elementary grades and secondary math for sixth through ninth grades. High-dosage tutoring is a proven, effective strategy for increasing student outcomes, with a strong research base (Frey, Fisher, and Almarode, 2022). This program is an approved use of the 20% of ESSER III dollars that must be used to address the academic impact of lost instructional time.

3.20 Consent Agenda: Jeffco/CEPC Audio Visual Equipment
Meeting
Nov 10, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To authorize the director of purchasing to execute a on-year contract agreement with Bluum USA, for Audio Visual equipment with an estimated expense not to exceed 2,000,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. To inform the Board of an expenditure greater than $500,000.

 

  1. On August 26, 2022, BID# 26112, Jeffco/CEPC Audio Visual Equipment 2022-2023 was posted on BidNet Direct with 38 companies receiving the opportunity to bid on this solicitation. 

 

  1. In an effort to create a district-wide systemic approach to instructional and technological AV supports, the number of AV options available to schools on the bid has been reduced.

 

  1. As a result of a partial award of Bid 26112, the Purchasing department signed a contract estimated annual expense of $500,000 with Bluum for the purchase of Audio Video Equipment for a one year period.

 

  1. Many schools are replacing old projectors and end of life Smartboards with new interactive displays and projectors.  Projectors and interactive displays are a core technology used in classrooms across the district.  New construction projects also include this technology in planning to equip schools with future-ready technology.

BACKGROUND

 

Regional Opportunities for Thriving Schools is Jeffco Public Schools’ response to the Board of Education’s (BoE) request for a comprehensive plan to ensure thriving schools and extraordinary student experiences across our district.  The 2020 Census shows a population increase in Jefferson County of an additional 55,854 residents from 2000 to 2020. However, during this time, the population of school-aged children (5-19) decreased by 29,918. In addition, the population of children five and under has decreased. In 2020 the lowest number of births in 15 years was recorded. There are significantly fewer school-aged children in Jeffco today than 20 years ago.

Jeffco has capacity to serve 96,000 students in its traditional district-managed schools and we currently only serve 69,000 students. Thirty-nine percent of the District’s excess capacity is at the elementary school level. The impact of Jeffco’s excess capacity is felt in nearly every corner of our District as we stretch our resources to sustain the number of schools we have, with declining enrollment. It is hard on all school communities when we do not efficiently utilize our resources and take advantage of economies of scale. Most importantly, it creates inequities in the educational opportunities that schools are able to provide to students due to budget limitations. In the past two years, the District has been required to make reactive decisions to close two small schools in the spring, after the choice enrollment period, due to unsustainable enrollment projections for the upcoming school year.

Regional Opportunities for Thriving Schools calls for a comprehensive, proactive plan, and encourages all of us to consider the stewarding of our resources in ways that provide extraordinary student experiences in support of collaborative and data driven team cultures. A recommendation for Phase I Regional Opportunities for Thriving Schools was presented to the Board of Education and the public on August 25, 2022.

On November 10, 2022 the Jeffco Board of Education will take one vote on the following package: The closure of 16 elementary schools in 12 articulation areas. This includes redrawing boundaries for 15 of the schools and redrawing boundaries for formerly closed schools – Allendale and Fitzmorris.
