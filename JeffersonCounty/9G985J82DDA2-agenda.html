5.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)

Apr 3, 2014 - Regular Meeting

5. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 7:10 p.m.  (estimated duration: 45 minutes)


The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.  

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda and any Part Two comments for up to 45 minutes. After 45 minutes any remaining Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to 45 minutes if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to purchase licenses, professional development and supplemental materials under the current software license and service agreement with Amplify in an amount not to exceed $600,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy 7, Asset Protection, “the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to expenditures greater than $250,000.”
     
  2. The Colorado READ Act requires that school districts provide three components of reading assessment and intervention to students in grades K-3: An interim assessment to be given to all students, a diagnostic assessment for those students that have been identified with a significant reading deficiency, and an intervention resource designed to address those students’ specific, diagnosed reading skill deficiencies.
     
  3. Jeffco’s participation in the CDE Early Literacy Assessment Tool (ELAT) Project provides access to DIBELS Next, a CDE approved interim measure; and, the Burst: Assessment Measures, a CDE-approved diagnostic measure at no cost to the district.  It does not provide an intervention resource.
     
  4. Between February and April 2013, district staff reviewed intervention resources from the Colorado Department of Education advisory list of instructional programming and selected Amplify’s Burst: Reading Early Literacy Intervention and mCLASS: Reading 3D for purchase.
     
  5. The district implemented Burst: Reading Early Literacy Intervention in 2013-2014. Implementation of mCLASS: Reading 3D will occur during the 2014-2015 school year as phase two of the district’s Colorado READ Act implementation.  In addition, eleven schools purchased additional licenses of the ELAT tools to implement with students in grades 4-6.
     
  6. In the 2014-2015 school year, all Jeffco schools will continue to use the Amplify mCLASS: DIBELS Next and Burst: Reading Assessment Measures assessment and progress monitoring systems.

In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed.
