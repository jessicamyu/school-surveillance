For the Board of Education to provide guidance on any work for next year's DAC and to receive the District Accountability Committee recommendations on:

On February 18, after months of work in subcommittees and the full committee, the District Accountability Committee (DAC) reached agreement on its 2020/21 recommendations to the Board of Education.

The DAC Budget Subcommittee (Dave Johnson, Jeff Baucum, Rebecca Christensen, Tom Dougherty, Caitlin Fitzpatrick, Nancy McCanless, Geneva Moore, Mark Stutz, Laura Velapoldi, Naomi Xayavangsa) worked with district staff and information collected through the school accountability committee survey, DUIP budget recommendations, and FSCP budget recommendations to provide the budget recommendations through the DAC.

On April 2, it is anticipated the Board of Education will vote to approve the District Unified Improvement Plan before submission to the Colorado Department of Education.

The DAC Family School Community Partnership Subcommittee (Carsten Engebretsen, Evie Hudak, Amy Born, Susan Chapla, Becky DeLong, Juan Carlos Galvan, Amanda Garner, Toni Nicholas, Megan Picket, Ruben Rodriguez, Jackie Siebel, Lauren Simpson).

The DAC Charter Review Subcommittee (Sally Kate Tinch, Dave Wells, Jeff Baucum, Rebecca Christensen, Erica Gradwell, Nancy Hardesty, Evie Hudak, Jill Mark, Kay Slater).

The DAC Executive Committee (Jim Earley, Sally Kate Tinch, Carsten Engebretsen, Deb Guiducci, Evie Hudak, Dave Johnson, Margaret Lessenger, Jackie Siebel, Dave Wells) welcomes any direction from the Board for future DAC work.

5. Public Agenda Part One
Type

estimated time: 6:02 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Recommended Action
to be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for March 2020.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. Choice Programs received $95,377 from CareerWise Colorado to support youth apprenticeships and concurrent enrollment related to work experience. This initiative aligns with the Strategic Plan Learning Tactic: Customized Pathways. Jeffco Career Links Administrative Coordinator Sheri Bryant and Director of Choice Programs Marna Messer oversee the program.
  2. The Colorado Department of Education’s (CDE) Colorado State Library awarded $23,781 to Digital Teacher Librarians for the purchase of educational resources. As a district-level, non-competitive allocation, funding is calculated based on student enrollment. The grant is used to increase teacher and student access to information through books, periodicals, audiovisuals, subscriptions, ebooks and applications for tablets and computers. Resources support the Strategic Plan Tactics under Learning: Transforming the Student Task, Responsive Teaching and Technology to Transform Learning. Digital Teacher Librarian Coordinator Heidi Floyd from Educational Technology manages the project.
  3. Warren Tech was awarded $17,500 from AGC Construction to implement a construction trade skills and OSHA safety education program. Sponsored by Careers in Construction Colorado and using the Home Builders Institute curriculum and certifications, funds will cover staff training and implementation costs. This project upholds the Strategic Plan Learning Tactic: Customized Pathways. Principal Heather Keeton supervises the program.
  4. Career and Technical Education (CTE) received the annual Perkins Federal Grant allocation via the Colorado Community College System. This year’s grant of $409,151 will be used for salaries, teacher professional development, equipment and instructional supplies to bring programs up to industry standards. CTE focuses on the Strategic Plan Learning Tactics: Customized Pathways and Student-Directed Learning. CTE Coordinator Shelley Brunjak-Goerdt from Choice Programs administers the grant.
  5. McLain Community High School received notification that they are a qualified provider of the Workforce Diploma Pilot Program through CDE. Established in 2019, the program supports adults without a high school credential by encouraging providers who re-engage adults to obtain a high school diploma and other credentials. One of six providers statewide, McLain will be reimbursed up to $51,750 for half-credits and high school diplomas earned by students 21 year old and older. Students attend McLain’s daytime or evening adult education program. This program aligns with the Strategic Plan Learning Tactic: Customized Pathways. Principal Gavin Goodrich and Student Engagement Specialist Tim Ebendick manage the program.
  6. Rose Community Foundation via Parent Possible awarded the Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters (HIPPY) program $20,000 for the home-based, peer-delivered, early intervention program to assist parents in providing educational support for their children. HIPPY aligns with the Strategic Plan Readiness for Learning Tactic: Meaningful Family Engagement. Coordinator Katherine Harrison from the Early Learning Department manages the program.

to authorize the director of Purchasing to negotiate and enter into a multi-year contract with Advance Network Management (ANM) to provide new handsets for an estimated amount of $1.4 million.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. This work aligns with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection.
     
  2. Through a district competitive solicitation led by the Purchasing department, Advance Network Management (ANM) has been selected to provide the phone handsets for all school sites in the district. Charter schools are typically responsible for their own telecommunications and infrastructure improvements. They decide how and if they wish to upgrade their phone handsets based on their individual school needs and budgets.
     
  3. Funding for this work will be through the Information Technology budget.
     
  4. The phone handsets will allow the district to be compliant with the following components:
  • Compliant with UC Software version 14.xx and higher
  • Support for Cisco Emergency Responder (CER), Contact Center, Unity
  • Future proofs district phones for additional software updates to the central communication system
  • Bluetooth capable for Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) compliance
  • Will allow for additional lines per set for flexibility and programing
  1. The work is to replace the existing phone handset installation base.  The current handsets are approaching their end of sale, support and useful life and are in need of replacement.  Firmware updates are no longer available.  Current phones will only allow for dial tone as Cisco Unified Communication software is upgraded and effects conferencing, transferring, multi-line and 911 reporting.

12. Public Agenda Part Two

12.01 Public Comment (Not On Agenda)

Mar 5, 2020 - Regular Meeting
Category
12. Public Agenda Part Two
Type
Information

estimated time: 11:20 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)
 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education. (will be available 10 am Monday)

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
