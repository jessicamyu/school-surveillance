The Board of Education is pleased to honor the Wayne Carle Diversity Award recipients for 2009:

Pat D. Rojo Kennedy - began her career in Jeffco as a classroom aide, flourished as an elementary teacher at Bear Creek, Kendallvue and Stein elementary schools. Her work to teach English acquisition skills to the Spanish speaking community grew and merged with the "Learning Source" organization with additional classes for parenting skills with a sensitivity to cultural understanding, citizenship acquisition, and electoral choices. Ms. Kennedy retired after 20 years of service from Jeffco Public Schools (JPS).

Shermita West - came to Jefferson County in 1969 and helped integration efforts in predominately white Jeffco. She served as president of the Jefferson County Education Association (JCEA), retiring after 30 years of service from Jeffco Public Schools. She continues her work through the Jeffco Action Center and the Colorado History Museum.

Jason Firestone - of the Kaw Nation and first generation Mexican-American, he grew up in the Leadville and Buena Vista areas before joining Indian Education efforts in Jeffco Public Schools as a community liaison and coordinator. Mr. Firestone supports over 1,000 Native students who attend Jeffco schools.

Mile High United Way - has successfully tapped into the power of collective generosity to provide creative, long-term solutions for the most pressing needs in the Denver Metro area. By partnering with Jeffco Public Schools and the Jefferson Foundation, the Lights On After School program serves nearly 1200 students at 20 Jeffco schools to provide a safe, nurturing environment after school.

Aimee Pickar - began teaching in Jeffco Public Schools in 1995 with the past 12 years at Swanson Elementary School. Ms. Pickar advances that only with an appreciation of diversity can a deeper understanding of art truly occur. She began and has spearheaded the JPS Equity and Diversity Art Exhibition for the past nine years, among her wider community art involvement.

Phyllis Bubeck - came to Jeffco Public Schools in 1991 as a special education teacher and focused her cultural connections to enhance student learning at Fitzmorris Elementary School. In 1995 she became an art teacher and currently works at Wayne Carle Middle School, has worked as a gifted/talented liaison on the writing team for twice exceptional students, the multicultural visual arts writing team, and the Curriculum Alignment Project (CAP) documents for middle school visual arts team and CAP visual arts vertical teams, among other community arts involvements.

Be the Change Group - at Arvada High School, this student group works on keeping the spirit of acceptance, tolerance and understanding integral to the school's annual Challenge Dayexperience alive year-round. The students challenge others to exhibit positive behaviors around sharing and accepting those who are different. Students identified "acts of kindness, acceptance, helping other students, and compassion" as positive behaviors for this school year to raise awareness and improve the school climate.

Peace Jam Group - at Arvada West High School, this student group works to raise money and heighten awareness on world issues including atrocities at Darfur resulting in genocide, children who have lost their parents to AIDS in Africa, the Invisible Children, child soldiers in Uganda, and addressing hunger in Colorado through support of the Rocky Mountain Food Bank. "Ending Racism and Hate in the World Begins with Us... How We Treat Each Other was the pledge to treat every person in the school with respect and dignity, no matter how different they are from me."

PERTINENT FACTS:
1.The City of Lakewood will celebrate its 40th anniversary this year with a Fourth of July “40 and Fireworks” sponsored by “Lakewood on Parade.”
2.The event will take place on July 3, 2009 from 4:00 p.m. to 10:30 p.m. The Lakewood on Parade Board has requested use of the Jeffco Stadium and Miller Special parking lot for the fireworks display, which is located at 6th & Kipling. 4.District administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policy KF-R, Community Use of School Facilities, with respect only to the prohibited use and possession of alcoholic beverages for this one time event. This policy states that “the use and possession of alcoholic beverages or drugs by any person or group on district property is strictly prohibited.”
5.The Lakewood on Parade organization will provide insurance and the City of Lakewood will provide police/fire protection for the event. During this event, Lakewood on Parade will have a Special Events liability insurance policy covering the district and other entities supporting this effort.
6.A Building and Facility Use form has been completed and approved and a copy of the approved permit will be posted during the event.

3.14 Contract Renewal: Acuity, Yearly Progress Pro (EL-7)
Meeting
May 7, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1. On March 3, 2006, a Request for Proposal (RFP) was issued to eight vendors for a pilot program web based Benchmark Assessment & Progress Monitoring System.
2.On March 23, 2006, five responses were received. Thorough evaluations were performed by a team of representatives from central departments and school based personnel. Based upon higher overall technical ratings and lower overall cost, the RFP was awarded to CTB/McGraw Hill. The total contract price was $150,000 to develop the reading and math assessments for grades 3-10 based on an estimate of 10,000 students.
3.In May 2007, the district began negotiations with CTB/McGraw Hill to extend the contract for four additional one year terms. Pricing was quoted as fixed for the first and second renewals, while allowing for nominal price increases for the third and fourth renewals.
4.During the third year of the contract, the program was extended to an additional 50 schools. It is anticipated that for the 2009/2010 school year, the program will expand to the remaining 30,000 students from grades 3-10 not currently taking Acuity and Yearly Progress Pro (YPP) assessments. In addition, 1,000 students in grade 11 participating in required English and Algebra course, and grade 1-2 students will have progress monitoring for reading, language arts, and math as requested by the school.
5.Ninety-nine Jeffco schools are currently using the Acuity and Yearly Progress Pro (YPP) system for benchmark and progress monitoring systems. Program evaluation completed over the past two years has shown strong correlations between teachers’ and administrators’ use of the data to guide instruction and increased student achievement.
6.The 2009/2010 school year will be the final year of rollout of the Acuity system across all Jeffco schools. The remaining schools will be provided with the system and the supports to administer the assessment and interpret the results.

3.18 Contract Renewal: Property/Casualty Insurance Portfolio (EL-7)
Meeting
May 7, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.The property insurance market has not risen to the extent previously anticipated even after the third significant district fire which took place on April 22, 2008. The 2009-2010 property insurance market is still in negotiations to finalize terms and conditions yet we already have premium indications yielding an approximate two percent reduction in cost. This reduction has been achieved while at the same time limits of liability have been increased from $150 million to $200 million. The policy will be subject to a deductible of $250,000 on fire loss and a $100,000 deductible for other perils. Current premiums are quoted at $1,033,500 with increased limits compared to $1,054,216 expiring premium. The district broker continues to work to improve policy structure, terms and conditions from the expiring policy prior to the June 1, 2009 renewal date.
2.The general liability/auto/educator’s legal liability, with the incumbent carrier, Genesis, offered expiring coverage at a two percent increase in premium at $284.848. Limits of $5 million/$8 million are provided subject to a $500,000 retention. Competing carriers have offered potential reductions of as much as five percent and eight percent and the Risk Management department is working with the brokers reviewing terms, conditions and carrier history, before a final recommendation is made.
3.The incumbent excess workers’ compensation carrier, Mid-West Employer’s Casualty, offered the same coverage as expiring, with a 10 percent increase in premium, due in part to an increase in payroll for a premium of $152,314. The increase is also based on loss experience which in three cases retentions will likely be met. Statutory limits are provided subject to $500,000 retention. Reduced premium is available with Mid-West given an increase in the retention level and this is being considered. Competing excess workers’ compensation carriers have offered placements with increases between one percent and 15 percent of expiring premium but with some reductions in retention levels. Additional quotes are still coming in. Given these indications, the Risk Management department is working to reduce the retention while at the same time absorbing increased premiums of no more than the 10% offered by the incumbent.
4.Travelers’ is providing renew of the $1 million crime coverage (including bonding) for the second year of a three year rate guarantee of $6,628. The fiduciary liability coverage with $1 million limits, $10,000 deductible was quoted with Traveler’s at $8,715, a three percent increase in premium.
5.Special Risk, providing emergency assistance for employees traveling overseas, is provided prepaid to 2011 at an annual cost of $4,624.
6.International travel liability for employees, students and chaperones can be renewed with ACE as expiring with a one percent decrease in premium at $5,000.
