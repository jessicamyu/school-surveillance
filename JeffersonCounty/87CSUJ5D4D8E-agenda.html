Tom MacDonnell, interim executive director, Facilities Management
Bob Smith, interim chief operating officer

PURPOSE:
The Board of Education approved the members of the Capital Improvement Program (CIP) Oversight Committee in February 2005. The Oversight Committee is required to give semi-annual reports to the Board of Education.

BACKGROUND:
The Oversight Committee last met with the Board of Education on May 11, 2007. The committee members include: Dick Burczyk, Gordon Calahan, Andy Dmohowski, Helga Grunerud, Marv Kay, Paul Konrath, Tom Murray, and M.L. Richardson.

The charter of the Oversight Committee consists of: 1) Ensure that the program completed with these funds is consistent with the promises made to the community. 2) Monitor procedures relating to the implementation of the program: development of specifications, selection process for consultants and contractors, financing issues, and project scheduling. 3) Review of all projects; especially when significant changes in scope, budget or scheduling occur. 4) Ensure the program represents sound, long-term decisions consistent with the needs of the District. 5) Assist in safeguarding public trust and confidence through active involvement in communications related to the Bond projects. 6) Provide annual written reports to the Board of Education on the quality of the program management, quality of the financial management, quality of completed projects and alignment with the overall plan.

The report to the Board of Education will have detail information in each of the six areas listed above.

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to ask any questions they have on the revision of graduation requirements for Jeffco Public Schools students beginning with the seventh graders of 2007-2008.

BACKGROUND:
After a year’s work with the graduation committee, comment on several drafts of the graduation policy, IKF/-R, and feedback from Board members at October and November 2007 study sessions, the Board directed the superintendent to provide an update on implementation plans prior to final Board of Education approval scheduled for January 24, 2008. That update was provided on November 20.

This study session is designed to respond to any remaining questions as a final Board discussion before taking formal Board action at the January 24, 2008 regular business meeting.
