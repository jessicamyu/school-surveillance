PURPOSE:

To provide an update to the Board of Education on data privacy in the district and receive direction from the Board on the priorities for the Technology and Data Privacy Advisory Committee (TDPAC) for the 2017/18 school year.

 

BACKGROUND:

The information security department has been protecting district data and providing oversight for the past 11 years. The Technology and Data Privacy Advisory Committee (TDPAC) is a 23-member committee consisting of both community members and Jeffco staff, and was created to advise the Board of Education on district technology strategies, systems and overall data governance.  The committee encourages continuous improvement of, and fosters adherence to, district policies, procedures and practices at all levels. Reference: Board Policy GP-13

4.04 Evergreen Middle School Artwork: Pentagonal Pangea
Meeting
Apr 6, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

estimated time: 6:09 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 minutes)
 

The Board of Education is pleased to honor last year's 8th grade art class of Evergreen Middle School and art teacher Ann Simpson, technical education teacher Brendan Hager, and the school’s guest artist David Cuin for the artwork Pentagonal Pangea purchased last year as a special exhibit for the Education Center by the superintendent.

 

Student artists of the 2015/16 school year Justin Arndt, Elizabeth Baker, Holly Beato, Krista Choate, Lily Claus, Leah Clayton, Anna Clubb, Grace Dunlap, Charlotte Hawkins, Ashlee Kerber, Felicia Lademan, Abrianna Mangiamele, Nick Manson, Rachel Mosch, Ruth Mosch, Lauren Oeser, Samantha Schleef, Katelin Schneider, Hayden Schreiber, Kimber Taylor, Bri Teegarden, Rachel Urban, Alma Venta, Emily Young created the 3-D glass fused artwork which focuses on motifs of five cultures: Japanese, Egyptian, Aboriginal, Scandinavian, and Southwest Native American.

 

"Pangaea/Pangea" from Ancient Greek, pan (all, entire, whole) and Gaia (mother earth, land). The theory of Pangea is that millions of years ago all the continents were joined together in one enormous land mass known as Pangea and about 200 to 300 million years ago the continents broke apart and began to drift in opposite directions.

 

After study of the art of 24 cultures, five were chosen (Japanese, Egyptian, Aboriginal, Scandinavian, SW Native American) to display typical motifs related to that culture involving tiles of fused glass, wire and metal. All tiles relate to each other, conform to design principles, and each team produced one facet of the pentagon pyramid. The concept was to create a contemporary composition in fused glass with metal inclusions and reverse embossing.

 

Ms. Simpson is providing a book on the project for the Education Center, compiled for the students from the proceeds of the purchase.

5.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Apr 6, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:15 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) requires all public school districts and schools to complete an annual Unified Improvement Plan (UIP).  These improvement plans are designed to identify priority challenges and develop major improvement strategies to meet these needs.  While Jeffco is a high performing school district that consistently meets state accreditation expectations, the CDE expects the district's UIP to focus on areas for improvement.
     
  2. To that end, the district has worked closely with the District Accountability Committee (DAC) to develop the 2016-2017 District Unified Improvement Plan. Members of the DAC UIP subcommittee include Julie Oxenford O'Brian, chair; Jim Earley, vice chair; Deb Guiducci, Sed Keller, Margaret Lessenger, Tom Coyne, Tonya Coulter, Cindy Walker, Melissa Martini, Nikki Fitterer, Beth Knudsen, and Suzanne Covington.
     
  3. The improvement planning process included a comprehensive analysis of district level data, the root cause analysis to focus on system improvements and development of an action plan aligned to the priority student performance challenges.
     
  4. Throughout the school year, Jeffco's District Accountability Committee will continue to partner with the district to provide input and feedback on the implementation of the district's Unified Improvement Plan.
     
  5. Following presentation of the DAC UIP report to the Board of Education by Deb Guiducci, DAC chair, Julie Oxenford O'Brian, DAC UIP subcommittee chair; and, Jim Earley, DAC UIP subcommittee vice-chair, the district will submit the District Unified Improvement Plan to the Colorado Department of Education by April 15, 2017, as required by CDE.

9. Public Agenda Part Two
Subject
9.01 Public Comment (Not On Agenda)
Meeting
Apr 6, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
9. Public Agenda Part Two
Type
Information

estimated time: 7:55 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)
 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education. (will be available 10 am Monday)

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Following the work calendar review, the Board of Education will review a draft Board of Education meeting schedule for the next school year. A draft schedule is prepared annually prior to formal approval to receive feedback.
     
  2. The draft schedule is prepared taking into consideration the following when possible: 1st/3rd Thursdays, first meeting after school begins, every other Thursday, avoiding known conflicts (religious and secular holidays, school breaks, conferences).
     
  3. It is anticipated that the Board of Education will adopt a schedule of regular meetings and study sessions for the next school year at the regular meeting of May 4, 2017.
