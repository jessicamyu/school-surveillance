Helen Neal, chief of staff, Board of Education and Superintendent (DAC)

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools elementary (DAC)
 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to review the membership recommendations and applications for openings in four Board advisory committees: District Accountability Committee (DAC), Financial Oversight Committee (FOC), Performance Evaluation Council (PEC), and Technology Data Privacy Advisory Committee (TDPAC) in order to make appointments.

 

BACKGROUND:

The role of Board of Education advisory committees is outlined in Board governance process policies GP-12, Board Committee Principles, and GP-13, Committee Structure.

 

Four committees generated vacancies in the 2019/20 school year. In order to be prepared for committee work for the 2020/21 school year, an online application process was available mid-March to July 26 (extended in May due to the COVID-19 pandemic).

 

Board members received membership applications in August for review prior to the Board’s September 3 discussion and appointments and had an opportunity to meet interested applicants on August 31. 

There is one vacancy open in the TDPAC and FOC, four appointments for PEC, and twelve appointments to make to the DAC (three of which are direct Board appointments).

Students have the option and choice to attend a GT Center school for intensive programming if they qualify with a body of evidence that suggests this is an appropriate learning environment. For students who do not choose to attend a GT Center school, an Advanced Learning Plan (ALP) is developed to bolster their areas of strength. Services target the Strategic Plan Objective Conditions for Learning, Commitment to Equity. GT Director Roger Dowd supervises the program.

  • An improved user experience for both families and staff as all of this information will be in one application;
  • Significant reduction in manual workload for registrars facilitated by the automated upload of documents such as immunization records, birth certificates, utility bills for address verification, and transcripts by families; and
  • Reduced security risks and minimized errors as the amount of data passing between systems is reduced.

In 2019, the district began conversations with other K12 school districts and the state for general awareness of competing student information systems and to develop strategies for implementing the next generation of SIS, given the direction in the industry toward cloud computing solutions. Through those conversations, it was clear that Infinite Campus is widely considered to be a very strong player in the SIS marketplace. 
     
  2. Utilizing the OLR module instead of Jeffco Connect for registration activities will streamline district internal and external processes, reduce a large amount of manual effort, reduce contracted support costs and unnecessary paper production, and strengthen the overall technical environment allowing district staff to better meet strategic goals surrounding data management, integration, and exchange.

Category
8. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the award of a contract to Fransen Pittman Construction, Incorporated, in the amount of $ 5,159,616 for the Manning School Addition & General Upgrades Project; and, to authorize the executive director, Facilities and Construction Management, to execute the contract documents.

estimated time: 10:50 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes) / Hora estimada: 10:50 p.m. (duración estimada: 10 minutos)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000.”
  2. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-8, Facilities, “…facilities shall not be unsafe, inadequate or unnecessary.”
  3. The contract award is to construct a seven classroom addition to the Manning School, reconfigure the north student drop off, replacement of the parking lot and driveways, stormwater management infrastructure improvements, secure entry at the main entrance, door hardware upgrades, renovation of the boy's locker room to create a single use staff bathroom, installation of resilient flooring in the auxiliary gym, removal and relocation of lockers, replacement of existing boiler plant and water heater.
  4. The Facilities and Construction Management office maintains a list of pre-qualified general contractors to provide construction services.  Following district policies FEB, Architect/Engineer/Construction Manager, and FEG, Construction Contracts Bidding and Awards, and DJ/DJA, Purchasing/Purchasing Authority, relating to the selection criteria for capital projects, staff contacted eight pre-qualified general contractors and seven participated in the mandatory pre-bid walk. Six pre-qualified general contractors submitted bids.
  5. On August 13, 2020, competitive bids and pricing of alternates were received and publicly read from the six pre-qualified bidders for the Manning School Addition & General Upgrades Project.
  6. The low bidder is Fransen Pittman Construction, Incorporated in the amount of $5,159,616 including Alternates 1 and 2, a staff toilet in the boy's locker room and flooring in the auxiliary gymnasium respectfully.
  7. Funding for the work is the 2018 Capital Improvement Program.  This is a one-time capital investment without ongoing costs.  Fransen Pittman Construction, Incorporated, is an Englewood, Colorado based construction company.
  8. The project work will begin as soon as the contract is fully executed with project completion scheduled for August 2021.

Recommended Action
to approve the award of contract to JHL Constructors, in the amount of $9,217,083 for the new building, Warren Tech South; and, to authorize the executive director, Facilities & Construction Management, to execute the contract documents.

estimated time: 11 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes) / Hora estimada: 11 p.m. (duración estimada: 10 minutos)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not “contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $500,000.”
  2. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-8, Facilities, “…facilities shall not be unsafe, inadequate or unnecessary.”
  3. The contract award is to construct a 25,867 SF career and technical education facility located on the southern portion of the Dakota Ridge High School property. The building will consist of learning facilities for live sound and event production and lighting design, audio-visual, classrooms for hospitality and healthcare, auditorium, administration, commons, and an addition dedicated to aviation and associated site development and infrastructure.
  4. The Facilities and Construction Management office maintains a list of pre-qualified general contractors to provide construction services. Following district policies FEG, Construction Contracts Bidding and Awards, and DJ/DJA, Purchasing/Purchasing Authority, relating to the selection criteria for capital projects, based on qualifications, seven (7) pre-qualified general contractors submitted bids.
  5. On August 20, 2020, competitive bids and pricing of alternates were received and publicly read from the seven pre-qualified bidders for the construction of Warren Tech South New Building.
  6. The low bidder is JHL Constructors, in the amount of $9,217,083 including base bid and Alternates: aviation addition, additional parking lot, replacement of existing parking lot, audio suite floating slab, ceiling treatment in commons, projector at platform, window treatment at commons, gravel base at slab on grade, and wall graphics.
  7. Alternate One, the aviation addition, consists of approximately 4,800 square feet (SF) that will support a program dedicated to repair and maintenance, avionics, and general aviation study.  Funding for this alternate will be through one-time funds for Warren Tech South Building and not the Capital Improvement Program.
  8. Funding for the base building and alternates 2-9 is the 2018 Capital Improvement Program. This is a one-time capital investment without ongoing costs.  JHL Constructors is a Centennial, Colorado based company.
  9. The project work will begin as soon as the contract is fully executed with project completion scheduled for August 2021.
