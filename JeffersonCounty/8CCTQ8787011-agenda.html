6.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Feb 3, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

to approve the continuation of the agreement with EDUSS Learning, which is estimated to exceed $250,000, as outlined below and in the background material.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board Executive Limitation Policy 7, Asset Protection, the Superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $250,000.
     
  2. As part of the NCLB federal mandates, Jeffco’s Title I program must offer Supplemental Educational Services (tutoring) to any school that is in its second year of School Improvement, on Corrective Action or is in Restructuring. These classifications are assigned to schools when they do not reach the assigned Federal Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) targets.
     
  3. The selection of vendors is performed by CDE and Jeffco is provided a list of approved vendors at the beginning of each school year.
     
  4. EDUSS Learning was on the approved vendor list for the 2010-2011 school year. Jeffco entered into an agreement with EDUSS in July 2010 to provide tutoring to eligible students.
     
  5. Parents have their choice of vendors; Jeffco does not assign vendors to students. Based on Fall Semester enrollment, Jeffco is estimating that the services provided by EDUSS will total approximately $350,000 for the 2010-2011 school year.
     
  6. Funding for Supplemental Education Services comes from the Title I mandated Set-Aside funds.

PERTINENT FACTS:


Please open the attachment below for additional background information.

  1. On March 3, 2006, a Request for Proposal (RFP) was issued to eight vendors for a pilot program, web based Benchmark Assessment and Progress Monitoring System.
     
  2. On March 23, 2006, five responses were received. Thorough evaluations were performed by a team of representatives from central departments and school based personnel. Based upon higher overall technical ratings and lower overall cost, the RFP was awarded to CTB/McGraw Hill. The total contract price was $150,000 to develop the reading and math assessments for grades 3-10 based on an estimate of 10,000 students.
     
  3. In May 2007, the district began negotiations with CTB/McGraw Hill to extend the contract for four additional one-year terms. Pricing was quoted as fixed for the first and second renewals, while allowing for nominal price increases for the third and fourth renewals.
     
  4. During the third year of the contract, the program was extended to an additional 50 schools. The 2009-2010 school year was the final year of rollout of the Acuity system across all Jeffco schools.
     
  5. In 2009-2010, all Jeffco schools have implemented the Acuity assessment system. Program evaluation completed over the past three years has shown strong correlations between teachers’ and administrators’ use of this data to guide instruction and increased student achievement.
     
  6. In 2010-2011, all Jeffco schools continue to use the Acuity/ Yearly Progress Pro assessment and progress monitoring systems.
