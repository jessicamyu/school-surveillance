2.02 Student Achievement: CMAS English Language Arts and Math (Ends 1, 2)
Meeting
Dec 17, 2015 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion
Goals
Ends 1 - Student Achievement
Ends 2 - Student Achievement
estimated time: 7:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dr. Syna Morgan, chief academic officer

Dr. Carol Eaton, executive director, Instructional Data, Educational Research and Design

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive information regarding the results of the Colorado Measures of Academic Success (CMAS) testing on English Language Arts and Math.

 

BACKGROUND:

 

Board Ends policy 1 states, “every student will master the Colorado Content Standards at grade level.”  Board Ends policy 2 states, “every student will achieve at least one year's growth, or more as needed to 'catch up,' in every year of school and be ready for the next level.” 

 

The new Colorado Measures of Academic Success (CMAS) tests in English Language Arts and Math measure student performance in a new way on the more rigorous Colorado Academic Standards and set a baseline for Jeffco students.

 

The new tests measure performance against higher expectations for students and are given online for the first time.

2.03 2015/2016 Budget - $15M Underspend Discussion (EL-5)
Meeting
Dec 17, 2015 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion
Goals
EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting

estimated time: 8:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:
Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer


PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on ideas for use of the $15M in underspend that was identified by staff at the end of fiscal year 2015 prior to adoption of the 2015/2016 budget. Staff is recommending that the funds be repurposed to the General Fund and that certificates of participation (COPs) be issued to fund capital needs. 

 

BACKGROUND:

Staff identified $15M in underspend at the end of FY 2015. At the May 7, 2015, Board of Education meeting, staff recommended that the $15M be retained in the General Fund and identified several possible uses for the one-time funds. In addition, staff, along with the support from the Financial Oversight Committee and Capital Advisory Committee, recommended the use of COPs to fund the construction of a new north area school. On June 11, 2015, the Board of Education approved a resolution to use the $15M in underspend to fund the new school construction from the Capital Reserve Fund.
