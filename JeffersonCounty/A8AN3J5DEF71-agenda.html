COMMITTEE MEMBERS:

Gordon Calahan, owner, Calahan Construction

Kathy Hodgson, city manager, City of Lakewood

Marv Kay, former Golden mayor/professor emeritus Colorado School of Mines

Vicki Ray, owner, Wild About Plants Landscaping

M.L. Richardson, regional manager, Asphalt Paving Company
Jeff Wilhite, pending 2016 member
Dawn Williams, business analyst

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive the annual report from the Capital Asset Advisory Committee prior to the Board's discussion of the Facilities Master Plan.

 

BACKGROUND:

In 2010, the Board of Education authorized the establishment of the Capital Asset Advisory Committee in accordance with policies EL-8, Facilities, and FB, Facilities Planning, both of which deal with facility condition and long range facility planning.  The Committee was formed from members of the 2005 Capital Improvement Program Oversight Committee and the 2009 Facilities Usage Committee.  The purpose of the Committee is to monitor the planning of capital needs and the implementation of capital programs, which may include future bond programs.
 

Members have a working familiarity with facility design and/or construction practices; business management expertise with organization(s) of comparable size to the district, and are independent and free from any relationship that would interfere with independent judgment.  Preference is given to Jefferson County residents. The committee meets monthly throughout the year.

PRESENTING STAFF:
Kevin Carroll, chief student success officer
Dr. Polly Ortiz Lutz, director, special education
Linda Buzard, director, health services

PURPOSE:
To inform the Board of Education of the findings of the review completed by WestEd Center for Prevention and Early Intervention regarding Jeffco Public Schools' Special Education department.

BACKGROUND:
In April 2015, Jeffco Schools initiated a request for a comprehensive program evaluation review to be conducted by the WestEd Center for Prevention and Early Intervention.  The review explored all elements of Jeffco programming related to Special Education and Health Services.  The review leverages comparative data with other surrounding school districts, in addition to research based best practices, making recommendations for program enhancement.
