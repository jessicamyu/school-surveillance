In accordance with Board Executive Limitation 11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, the Board of Education will receive information regarding the student-based budgeting process for the 2019/20 school year, including changes as a result of the free Full Day Kindergarten funding, as well as parts of SBB the team will be analyzing for future budgeting.

Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session

EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 11:40 a.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Todd Engels, executive director, School Leadership

Karen Quanbeck, chief of schools elementary, School Leadership

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will receive an update on the late start/early release work from task force representatives.

 

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Board Executive Limitation 11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, the Board of Education will receive updated information gained through the continued work of the Late Start/Early Release Task Force.

 

As a reminder, during negotiations between JCEA and district leadership in spring 2018, a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) was signed designating a joint task force to explore the viability of a late start or early release with the purpose of increasing planning time for educators across the district.

 

The task force met starting in November 2018 and included teachers, principals, parents, transportation, and central administrators.

 

The task force administered a survey to the Jeffco community to assess family and educator thoughts on late starts and early release time.

 

Survey results as well as other information regarding potential late start/early release schedules and impacts were presented to the Board of Education on March 7, 2019.
