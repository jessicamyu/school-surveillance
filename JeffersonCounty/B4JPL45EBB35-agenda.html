Meeting

Category

Ends 3 - Student Achievement

estimated time: 5 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

Ana Blanch, art teacher, Dunstan Middle School

Amy Laydon, art teacher, Arvada K-8
Scot Odendahl, graphic design/digital photography instructor, Warren Tech

Shannon May, coordinator, Visual Arts

Marna Messer, director, Choice Programs

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on the planning for arts in Jeffco Public Schools, including the Jeffco School of Art & Design.

 

BACKGROUND:

Board Ends 3 states, "Every school and the district will have effective learning systems and shared leadership that: provide high quality core instructional practices, interventions and enrichments, with the expectation that: Every student will develop life skills and a continuous learning mindset to succeed in post-secondary aspirations (Self-Direction and Personal Responsibility competency).

In partnership with our arts teachers, our community, and our central staff between December 2017 and June 2018, a strategic plan for the arts in Jeffco Public Schools was developed.  This plan includes supports for music, theater, and visual arts across our entire system. Because of this work, there is an addition of a theater coordinator and additional marketing for Jeffco Public Schools arts programs, and more to support students throughout our district.

Another component of this plan is the consideration of a Jeffco School of Art and Design focusing on visual arts and core content through project based learning. Since August, a group of visual arts teachers have helped to plan for the opening of this school. Tonight, the Board of Education will hear from representatives of this group and their recommendations for the Jeffco School of Art & Design.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

7.05 Intergovernmental Agreement: Jefferson Academy
Meeting
Nov 1, 2018 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorized the Jeffco Public Schools Board President to sign the intergovernmental agreement as updated between the City and County of Broomfield, Jefferson Academy and Jeffco Public Schools.
Goals
EL-13, Charter Schools Application and Monitoring

PERTINENT FACTS

  1. In accordance with district policy KF, Community Use of School Facilities, “...it is district policy to make district buildings and facilities available to the community when not in use for school activities.”
  1. The City and County of Broomfield has partnered with Jeffco Public Schools and Jefferson Academy (JA) since 2014 via an intergovernmental agreement (IGA) to allow for Broomfield community use of property owned by Jefferson Academy. An IGA has been in place since 2014 for this purpose.
  1. This year, Broomfield has again partnered with Jefferson Academy in helping to fund a new performing arts addition to the JA Secondary campus. Broomfield has provided this partial funding to JA in exchange for use of the new facility during non-school hours without disrupting school or school activities via an updated IGA.
  1. Jefferson Academy Secondary resides in a narrow piece of land that sits in both Jefferson County and the City and County of Broomfield. The City and County of Broomfield has Service Expansion Fee (SEF) funds designated to be used on educational facilities in specific zones to enhance the services offered to students and local residents. In this particular case, JA Secondary is the only school eligible for these funds.
  1. Several years ago, JA and Broomfield, with the approval of Jeffco Public Schools, entered into an IGA where Broomfield provided JA with $750,000 to help construct its soccer field. In return, Broomfield has been allowed use of JA's athletic facilities as long it does not interfere with JA programming.
  1. In September of this year, the Broomfield City Council unanimously approved allocating $1 million to JA Secondary for the construction of an auditorium, art display gallery, and other spaces. In return, Broomfield will be allowed to use these spaces after school hours when it does not interfere with JA programming.
  1. On November 1, the IGA to memorialize this new agreement presents updates to the previous IGA to include the new spaces being constructed. The IGA only pertains to the space at JA Secondary and does not include or impact any other Jeffco Public Schools facility. The IGA does not encumber the facility but rather outlines the partnership use of the space. As per the previous agreement, the funds will be provided to JA Secondary which will deposit the funds in their Jeffco account to be used solely for construction costs.

Meeting

Recommended Action
To be informed of supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Schools for November 2018.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

 

1.  The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) awarded a new Expelled and At-Risk Student Services Grant (EARSS Program) to Student Engagement. The year 1 award of $245,922 will support schools in school wide implementation of Restorative Practices. Three specialists will engage in side-by-side coaching of school staff as well as individualized student support related to attendance and discipline concerns. This initiative aligns with Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning, Social-Emotional Supports. Student Engagement Director Dave Kollar will oversee services.

 

2.  The Colorado Department of Human Services awarded a Refugee Services Program Grant extension of $60,375 to Adult English as a Second Language (ESL) and Literacy. Funds are for English instruction for recently arrived adult refugees especially related to employability. English skills will promote parents’ engagement in their children’s education. These services align with the Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning by addressing English skills in the family. Adult ESL and ABE Coordinator, Karen Caddoo, from the ESL/Dual Language Department will oversee the program.

 

3.  CDE’s Colorado State Library awarded $21,246 to Digital Teacher Librarians for the purchase of educational resources. This grant, a district-level, non-competitive allocation is calculated based on student enrollment. Funds are to increase teacher and student access to information through books, periodicals, audiovisuals, subscriptions, ebooks and applications for tablets and computers. This initiative aligns with Strategic Plan Tactics under Learning: Transforming the Student Task, Customized Pathways, Responsive Teaching, Technology to Transform Learning and Embracing the Full Range of the Human Experience. Digital Teacher Librarian Coordinator, Heidi Floyd, from Educational Technology manages the project.

 

4.  The School Leadership Team received a year 2 allocation of $503,586 from the CDE District Design and Led Improvement Strategies Grant Program. Funds will allow the continuation of district-designed supports for schools in need of improvement using multiple strategies focused on rapid school improvement (instructional coaches, induction coordinator and staff development). Chief of Schools Elementary, Karen Quanbeck oversees the project.

 

5.  Two Roads Charter received $80,000 from the CDE Connect for Success Grant Program. This year 2 allocation will allow Two Roads to continue to address high student mobility, and ensure data-driven instruction supported by an implementation coach and staff development. Principal Wendy Noel manages the project. 

6.  The district will submit an Empowering Action for School Improvement (EASI) Grant application to CDE for resources to support schools identified for improvement through the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA). Approximately $721,500 will be requested for services including support from the Turnaround Team, participation in the Turnaround Network and/or intensive professional development. Funds will allow for expansion of the district’s current EASI grant activities. Awards for the 2018/19 grant cycle will be announced in January. Chief of Schools – Elementary, Karen Quanbeck oversees the initiative.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education. 

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
