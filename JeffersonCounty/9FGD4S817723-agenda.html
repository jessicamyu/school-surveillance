Meeting
Jan 30, 2014 - Special Meeting
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Cindy Stevenson, superintendent

Ed Bowditch, lobbyist

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on the 2014 legislative session, to discuss a draft legislative platform and an evaluation of the work provided by the district’s lobbyist.

 

BACKGROUND:

It has been the Board of Education practice to receive periodic updates at board meetings on legislative activities of interest to and impacting k-12 education in Colorado and Jefferson County Public Schools, in particular.  The district’s lobbyist provides weekly reports and other timely communication as needed related to pending legislation.  The 2014 legislative session began on January 8, 2014.  In accordance with Board governance process policy 4, Board Job Description, the Board of Education may take positions on pending legislation to provide direction to Mr. Bowditch.

 

The Board of Education also prepares a Legislative Platform to assist in providing direction to the district’s contract lobbyist.  The document also provides information for state legislators and the Jefferson County community regarding the Board’s goals for the legislative session.

 

Ms. Williams requested an opportunity to evaluate the work of the district’s lobbyist.

Subject
3.03 Cornerstone Academy Charter Application Process Possibilities (EL-13)
Meeting
Jan 30, 2014 - Special Meeting
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 7:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Cindy Stevenson, superintendent

John Peery, charter school liaison

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to discuss the Cornerstone Academy charter school application.

 

BACKGROUND:

At the regular business meeting of October 3, 2013 a public hearing on the Cornerstone Academy charter school application was held by the Board of Education as outlined in Board executive limitation policy EL-13, Charter Schools Application and Monitoring.
 

At the regular business meeting of November 7, the Board of Education adopted a resolution to deny the application of Cornerstone Academy.
 

At the special meeting of November 21, the new Board of Education requested information on the possible next steps for the Cornerstone Academy charter application.  On December 5, the Board received information from the superintendent regarding a charter school appeal process to the state Board of Education and the alternative, a facilitation process, in lieu of an appeal, between the district and Cornerstone Academy to work toward an application satisfactory to the Board of Education.
 

At the special meeting of January 9, 2014, the Board requested information on the cost differences between the two possible next step processes for the charter school application of Cornerstone Academy.
