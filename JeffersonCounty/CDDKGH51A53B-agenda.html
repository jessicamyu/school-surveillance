PRESENTING STAFF:

Brenna Copeland, chief financial officer

David Bell, chief human resources officer

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education and the public to receive and consider the 2022/2023 proposed budget

 

BACKGROUND:

Board executive limitation policy, EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting, states that, “Financial planning for any fiscal year shall not deviate materially from the Board’s Ends policies, risk fiscal jeopardy or fail to be derived from a multi-year plan.” 

 

Budget updates were presented to the Board of Education at the November 10, 2021, and January 26, March 9, and April 7, 2022, meetings that included financial updates for the current year as well as preliminary budget information for the coming year. In April, staff shared recommendations on increases for the departmental budgets.

 

The District Accountability Committee met with the board at the April 13, 2022 study session to share their budget advice to the board. 

 

The public is invited to view the 2022-23 Proposed Budget attached below, or available on the Finance & Budget webpage at www.jeffcopublicschools.org/about/finance. Any person paying school taxes towards Jefferson County Public Schools may file or register (via email or via mail to the address below) comment or objection to this budget prior to its adoption. Additionally, members of the public may address the board in person at the June 2, 2022 regular board meeting by signing up for public comment. Currently, the board plans to adopt the 2022-23 Budget at the June 2, 2022 meeting, though the date may shift later in the month if necessary.

 

In addition to the 2022-23 Proposed Budget, the district has attached the maximum fee amounts that any school may charge for consumables or school related activities. This fee schedule will be posted on the Jeffco website.

3.06 Colorado Family and Medical Leave Insurance (FAMLI) Program Update (EL-11)
Meeting
May 11, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 6:35-6:55 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes) 

PRESENTING STAFF:

David Bell, chief human resources officer

PURPOSE:
To present an overview of the Colorado Family and Medical Leave Insurance (FAMLI) Program, which goes into effect in January 2023, and to prepare the board of education for opt in/opt out decision, as required.


BACKGROUND:

The Colorado FAMLI leave program will provide income protection for personal or family medical leaves, as allowed by new state statute, and as approved by Colorado voters in November 2020. The program has participation opt in/opt out requirement for local governments, including school districts. Local governments should consider current income replacement and protection programs in their opt in/opt out decision.

 

The opt in/opt out must be declared six months prior to the first contribution period in January 2023.
