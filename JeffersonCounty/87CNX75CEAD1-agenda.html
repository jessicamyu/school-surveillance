It also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:

    1. Foster and Russell elementary schools were awarded $227,390 from the Colorado Department of Education’s 21st Century Community Learning Centers to continue Project Prepare. This is the first year of a new five year grant. The program aligns with strategic plan community objective 2, which ensures staff, community, and parent/family involvement focuses on increased student achievement.
    2. Project Prepare is a program that provides academic tutoring, educational enrichment activities after school and during the summer, licensed child care when school is not in session, and opportunities to promote the health of our families. The funds will be used to provide tutoring, enrichment, family education, and mental health support. Foster and Russell elementary schools have partnered with PeaceJam Foundation, Jefferson Center for Mental Health, Jefferson County Library’s Knott’s Kids Program, Arvada Community Food Bank, the Arvada Police Department, Share Our Strength’s Operation Frontline Program, Denver Area Council Boy Scouts of America, and McLain Community High School’s adult education to design and deliver many of the program’s activities.
    3. Project Prepare was established nearly five years ago, also funded by a 21st Century Community Learning Centers grant. Each year, attendance numbers have increased. Data shows that students who attended Project Prepare thirty (30) or more days in the 2008-09 school year outperformed their school peers on CSAP in both reading and math. The project will be evaluated by monitoring student test scores, attendance rates, and increased parental involvement. Teachers and staff will also be informally surveyed and asked their opinions of student behavior and attitude changes.

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

Meeting
Dec 10, 2009 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
4. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action

PERTINENT FACTS:

1. Jeffco Public Schools has been asked to take a leadership role in Colorado’s efforts to obtain federal ‘Race to the Top’ funds. As the largest school district in the state, Jeffco’s work impacts over 10 percent of the state’s students. Additionally, because of Jeffco’s current work and/or systems in place implementing several of the performance strands targeted for the federal funds, including pay for performance, ‘turn around’ efforts, assessments, and standards, Dr. Stevenson has been approached for Jeffco Public Schools to take a leadership role in this work.
2. Nina Lopez, co-chair of Colorado’s Race to the Top efforts, will present an overview of Colorado’s Race to the Top work for the Board of Education. Ms. Lopez also serves as director of the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) at the Colorado Department of Education. Prior to coming to CDE in May 2009, Ms. Lopez served as Vice President of Strategy and Operations for the Colorado Children's Campaign, providing overall organizational oversight as well as direct supervision of the policy team. She has worked in K-12 education policy since 2003, first as director of external affairs for the Colorado League of Charter Schools and more recently as policy director for the Donnell-Kay Foundation. Ms. Lopez also co-chaired the education committee for Governor Bill Ritter's transition team.
3. The focus of the federal initiative, Race to the Top, is divided into four areas: ensuring that quality teachers are spread equitably through the state; better use of data to track students’, schools’ and educators’ improvement; creating international benchmark standards and assessments; and strategies for improving low-performing schools.
4. A draft Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) which outlines the responsibilities of the various parties involved in Colorado’s application for Race to the Top funds is presented for Board of Education approval.
