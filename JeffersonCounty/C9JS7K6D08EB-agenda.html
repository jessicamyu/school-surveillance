estimated time: 6:08 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education (opens 7 am Sunday, closes 5:30 pm Wednesday).   
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may address the Board. Public Comment will be taken virtually and in-person. For those who would like to address the Board of Education from home without wearing a mask, comments will be taken virtually via Zoom.  For those who are present in the audience, comments will be taken in-person and masks are required. Costume masks are not considered an adequate COVID-safe face covering (mask).

 

There is a three* minute time limit per individual speaker. *Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10* minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

 

hora estimada:  p.m. (duración estimada: hasta 1 hora)

 

Inscríbase aquí para hablar con la Junta Directiva de Educación (el formulario estará abierto a las 7 a.m. del domingo y se cerrará a las 5:30 p.m. del miércoles).

Por el momento, la Junta Directiva atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados con asuntos de la agenda durante incluso una hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que haya completado la información solicitada en la lista de comentarios públicos puede dirigirse a la Junta Directiva. El comentario público se recibirá de forma virtual y en persona. Para aquellos que deseen dirigirse a la Junta de Educación desde casa sin usar una mascarilla, los comentarios se recibirán virtualmente a través de Zoom. Para aquellos que están presentes en la audiencia, los comentarios se recibirán en persona y las mascarillas son requeridas. Las mascarillas de disfraz no se consideran una cubierta facial adecuada de seguridad ante la COVID (mascarilla).

 

Hay un límite de tres* minutos por orador individual. *Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. De esta manera, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas o más que como grupo se inscriben para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, tendrán 10* minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se inscriben más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo a la Junta.

La Junta Directiva escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

6.04 Supplemental Funds Notification: January 2022 (EL-11)
Meeting
Jan 6, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type

Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for January 2022.

PERTINENT FACTS:


In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

 

  1. Career and Technical Education (CTE) received the annual Perkins Federal Grant allocation via the Colorado Community College System. The grant of $510,901 will cover salaries, teacher professional development, equipment and instructional supplies. CTE focuses on Strategic Plan Learning Tactics: Customized Pathways and Student-Directed Learning. CTE Coordinator Shelley Goerdt from Choice Programming manages the grant.
     
  2. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) awarded $463,978 for High Impact Tutoring. Targeting students in grades 4-7 at seven high needs schools, tutoring will focus on building math fluency skills to complement core math instruction. Paraprofessionals will receive training from math TOSAs, and work with small student groups four times per week. Curriculum and Instruction Executive Director Jef Fugita and Math/Science Director Cecily Klein oversee the initiative.
     
  3. Student Services received $23,500 through a Counselor Corps supplemental grant from CDE. Funds will support 11 schools with family engagement events, Social Emotional Learning family toolkits, add pay for afterschool clubs/groups, and additional mental health support. Director of Student Services Terry Walderman oversees the program.
     
  4. CDE granted $29,999 for Computer Science Teacher Education allowing K-12 teachers to participate in professional learning in computer science and robotics. This training aligns with Strategic Plan Tactic for Learning: Technology to Transform Learning. Computer Science Specialist Marnie Roush and Educational Technology Director Kelly Sain will manage the training.
     
  5. The Buell Foundation announced an award to the HIPPY program (Home Instruction for Parents of Preschoolers). Funds totaling $45,236 will support home visitors in evidence-based work with parents to provide instruction, books, activities, and the skills to prepare their children for school. The HIPPY program supports Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning. HIPPY Coordinator Katherine Harrison from the Early Learning Department directs the program.
