6.06 Attendance Area Alignment and Boundary Change: Ralston Valley and Standley Lake Articulation Areas (EL-17)
Meeting
Oct 25, 2007 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.EL-17 and district policy JC establish that: “The Board endorses the philosophy of an articulation area comprised of neighborhood elementary schools feeding into a common middle school which in turn feeds a single senior high school. Whenever possible, the boundaries of individual schools and the high school articulation area shall be established with the objective of reinforcing this concept.”
2.Administrative staff reviewed current enrollment capacities and future development impacts to establish the proposed boundary changes.
3.A community task force was assembled to provide recommendation to district leadership and the Board of Education regarding preferred boundaries and matriculation patterns. The group's recommendation is consistent with the enrollment projections provided by Facilities Planning and Design.
4.Due to increased student enrollment with new housing developments, Meiklejohn students are currently attending Drake, Oberon, or Wayne Carle Middle schools and either Ralston Valley or Standley Lake high schools.
5.To facilitate clearer coordination of programming, events and instructional alignment from pre-kindergarten through high school, students will matriculate to one middle school, Wayne Carle, and one high school, Ralston Valley.
6.Current Meiklejohn Elementary students in grades five and six will have the choice of either Wayne Carle Middle or Oberon Middle schools. Transportation will be provided to both middle schools through the 2009-2010 school year.
7.2007-2008 seventh grade students in the Meiklejohn attendance area may choose to continue at their current middle school for 8th grade and attend Ralston Valley High School.
8.Beginning with the current fourth grade, Wayne Carle Middle School will be the single home middle school for Meiklejohn Elementary School and transportation will no longer be provided to Oberon or Drake Middle schools.
9.All Meiklejohn area students will have Ralston Valley High School as their only designated home school and will no longer have the option of Standley Lake High School as their home school. Students currently enrolled at Standley Lake High School from the Meiklejohn attendance area may continue to attend Standley Lake High School.
10.The choice enrollment process is available to families desiring additional alternatives.

6.08 Supplemental Funds - NCLB 2007-08 allocation from CDE to Jeffco (EL-7)
Meeting
Oct 25, 2007 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board policy EL-7, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the District (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the District is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A and C.
      1. The Board is hereby notified the District received its share of federal and state NCLB (No Child Left Behind) funds from The Colorado Department of Education in the amount of $11,247,259. The district will use these funds to provide best developmental and instructional programs in compliance with NBLB mandates. The program aligns with Strategic Plan Objective 1, which ensures that all students demonstrate achievement of academic content standards.
      2. No Child Left Behind Act of 2001 Programs include:
    § Title I, Part A (Improving the Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged)
    Allocation: $7,776,484
    A 49% and above free lunch rate qualifies an elementary or middle school to be a Title I school. High schools qualify when they reach 75% or above free lunch count. Over $5,000,000 will be distributed directly to schools to create a “double dose” of instruction in literacy and math. The remaining funds will provide technical coaching and assistance for teachers and principals in reading and math instruction and strategies to analyze data; provide for a .5 School Improvement Specialist to assist Title I schools in creating school improvement plans. Classroom teachers will enroll in graduate-level English as Second Language endorsement courses to assist an increasingly diverse student population. Funds will help support Jeffco’s Family Literacy program. A .5 Parent Involvement Coordinator will be hired to increase parental awareness of Title I and its importance to the success of students and the schools. Funds will also be set aside for the coordination of services to homeless students in both non-Title I and Title I schools. Pat Sudduth is the Title I Program Director.
    § Title II, Part A (Preparing, Training and Recruiting High Quality Teachers and Principals)
    Allocation: $2,405,050
    These funds will be used to provide an additional teacher to reduce class sizes at the ten Title I schools with the highest percentage of free lunch rates and the highest percentages of minority students. Thirty-five (35) .5 Instructional Coaches in non-title I schools and in schools on improvement status will provide ongoing site-based staff development to support teachers to be highly skilled in the teaching of reading, writing, and math. Staff development will also provide teachers with instructional strategies that support the learning of low performing groups. EMII (Early Mathematics Instruction and Intervention) classes for teachers will increase content knowledge, skills, and strategies and follow-up support for teachers through ongoing professional learning communities. The funds will help provide science workshops for teachers to increase content knowledge for specific science units. Secondary students will be trained in Pre-AP (Advanced Placement) strategies to use with all students in core content classes in Language Arts, Social Studies, Science, and Mathematics. A plan will be developed to assess the highly qualified status of Special Education teachers. Teachers at Wheat Ridge Middle School, a Title I school identified as on “School Improvement” because they did not meet their 2006 AYP targets in Math, will receive ongoing, sustained staff development in math strategies. School Reviews will be conducted in those schools on Academic Watch, on Probation, who rated LOW on the SAR, or on School Improvement status. A retired consultant will be hired to help non-Title schools to meet AYP targets. Charter schools and private schools receive services or allocations to provide for their professional development. Cindy Hernandez is the Program Manager for the Title II, Part A Funds.

    § Title II, Part D (Enhancing Education Through Technology)
    Allocation: $78,475
    Title II, Part D technology funds will be used to extend district-wide knowledge and strategies on effective implementation of technology to support student learning; develop on-line courses; develop an extended support base in the district with staff that can support Smart Boards and other technologies to support teaching and learning. The funds will be used to support the district I2A (Instruction, Intervention & Assessment) project with educational technology skills for teachers and support teachers in a district-wide rollout of Blackboard online learning. A team of key district leaders will attend the national and/or state educational technology conference to learn about new directions in technology use and then plan for implications in the district. The district is also defining, developing and will implement CDE’s required 8th grade technological literacy assessment. Charters and private schools will be extended invitations to certain programs. John Canuel manages these funds.
    § Title III (Language Instruction for Limited English Proficient and Immigrant Students)
    Allocation: $636,437
    Title III funds assist limited English proficient (LEP) children and immigrant students attain English proficiency and meet State academic standards. ESL (English as a Second Language) resource teachers will continue to assist classroom teachers with ESL strategies and ESL tutors will be assigned to work with ESL students. The funds will also provide ongoing staff development for classroom teachers designed to improve the instruction and assessments of ELLs. Teachers will enroll in graduate-level ESL endorsement courses. To accommodate the increase of 300 ELL students to Jeffco’s secondary schools, new ESL teachers were hired. Title III funds will provide explicit new ESL teacher induction training. The funds will assist in providing ongoing staff development to all bilingual teachers at Edgewater, Eiber, Foster, Lumberg, and Stein Elementary Schools, which have bilingual programs. Bilingual teachers will participate in a year-long training in Spanish Literacy Curriculum, Standards and Assessments. Additional staff to supplement services, such as a Vietnamese Community Liaison and Translation/Interpretation Services technicians, will be hired. The funds will provide a supplemental 2008 summer school program for ELLs. To administer the 2008 CELA (Colorado English Language Assessment), Title III will hire and train additional staff. Services are also provided to immigrant students. Lisa Conway and Catherine Baldwin are the Program Co-Directors for the Title III Funds.
    § Title IV (Safe and Drug Free Schools)
    Allocation: $238,225
    Funds will be used to maintain services currently provided to reduce violence and drug abuse and increase safety and civility in order to support increased student achievement and healthy lifestyle development among school-age youth. Services include district-wide training sessions on conflict mediation, bully-prevention, teen pregnancy, healthy lifestyle choices, asset building, suicide prevention/intervention models, gang culture-mentality and activities, tobacco prevention and cessation. Private and charter schools will be invited to take advantage of these workshops.
    Betsy Thompson is the Program Manager.
    § Title V (Innovative Programs)
    Allocation: $112,588
    Title V funds will provide strategies to improve Brady Exploration’s reading and math CSAP scores; provide for a .5 School Improvement Specialist to assist schools on School Improvement status to create meaningful school improvement plans based on data. Math teachers at Wheat Ridge Middle School will receive ongoing, sustained staff development in math strategies. Private and charter schools will receive a per pupil allocation. Cindy Hernandez is the Program Manager for the Title V Funds.
      3. Each Federal Program Manager will evaluate the success of their program using a variety of methods, including surveys, test scores, etc.

To address the issue of Mountain Phoenix Community School’s site development plan in a manner consistent with C.R.S. § 22-32-124(1.5)(a); and WHEREAS, the Jefferson County Planning Commission has requested, pursuant to COLO. REV. STAT. § 22-
32-124(1.5)(a), a hearing before the Board of Education of Jefferson County School District No. R-1 regarding the
Planning Commission’s concerns about the Mountain Phoenix Community School site development in connection
with its location at 11398 Ranch Elsie Road in Coal Creek Canyon; and
WHEREAS, the Board of Education has reviewed and considered the comments of the Planning
Commission regarding the site development plan, dated September 5, 2007, other information provided by the
Jefferson County Planning Commission, and the response of Mountain Phoenix Community School to the Planning
Commission’s comments; and
WHEREAS, the Board of Education held a public hearing on October 11, 2007, in accordance with COLO.
REV. STAT. § 22-32-124(1.5)(a), and considered the presentations of the Jefferson County Planning Commission and
Mountain Phoenix Community School, as well as input from District staff and the public;
NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Board of Education of the Jefferson County School District
No. R-1, County of Jefferson, State of Colorado, that Mountain Phoenix Community School is prohibited from
proceeding with its site development plan in connection with the location of the school at 11398 Ranch Elsie Road in
Coal Creek Canyon for the following reasons:
1. The Planning Commission recommends that schools be located in designated Community Centers
where public infrastructure has been established. This site is outside of a Community Center. The school will be
operational within an existing single family dwelling. According to Planning Commission staff, the proposed land use
and intensity of the proposed development of the school are not compatible with the surrounding residential mountain
environment and will result in a significant increase in traffic in comparison to a household.
2. Even though the school, on an interim basis, is providing bottled drinking water and following the
protocol set forth in the Bottled Water Advisory issued by the State of Colorado, it has not yet obtained the requisite
Public Water Supply Identification Number from the state in order to establish, install, and monitor the non-community
drinking water supply.
3. Even though the school has worked cooperatively with the Coal Creek Canyon Fire Protection
District to develop an interim fire emergency plan and obtained approval from the Fire Marshall to open the school,
construction of a fire apparatus access drive has not been completed.
4. On-site parking for the faculty and parents is inadequate and the school has indicated that funds
are not available for construction of on-site parking within the current budget.
