Subject
1.03 Resolution: Refunding of General Obligation Bonds-Series 2009 (EL-7)
Meeting
Jan 7, 2010 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session, 5 p.m.
Category
1. Preliminary
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.On October 22, the Board of Education adopted a resolution authorizing the issuance of General Obligation Series 2009 refunding bonds in a principal amount not to exceed $245,000,000 and related required agreements, for a net present value savings of at least $5.71 million.
2.Stifel Nicolaus, Jeffco Public Schools' investment bankers, have been monitoring the ratings and the interest rate available for refunding existing general obligation bonds. Time has elapsed and the October 22 resolution needs to be renewed for action to be taken by the investment bankers.
3.On October 8, 2009, Stifel Nicolaus met with the Financial Oversight Committee to provide market information and discuss the refunding opportunity. They received concurrence from the committee with the financial diligence in capturing the savings opportunities.
4.The refunding will yield a net present value savings of greater than 2.25 percent.
5.Ratings from Moody's Investors Services and Standard and Poor's should remain unchanged. More rating information will be provided by Stifel Nicolaus.

2.01 Legislative Issues
Meeting
Jan 7, 2010 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session, 5 p.m.
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion
Location: 4th floor, Seminar Room, 1829 Denver West Drive, Bldg. 27, Golden

PRESENTING STAFF:
Cindy Stevenson, superintendent
Ed Bowditch, contract lobbyist, Tomlinson & Associates

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to discuss its legislative platform to provide direction to the contract lobbyist for the 2010 legislative session.

BACKGROUND:
Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, requires that the Board's positions on pending legislation and regulations be communicated to members of the Colorado General Assembly and the Executive Branch of state government. It has been the Board's practice to schedule special meetings to discuss pending legislation during the legislative session.

The 2010 legislative session will produce a number of bills of interest to Jefferson County Public Schools. The district contract lobbyist will bring legislation to the Board of Education for positions and direction. The district’s contract lobbyist provides state legislators with the Board’s positions on legislation.

Jan 7, 2010 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session, 5 p.m.
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Report
PRESENTING STAFF:
Rick Myles, community superintendent
Deborah Gard, principal, McLain Community High School
Dave Kollar, director, Dropout Prevention and Recovery
Lisa Mumma, principal, Johnson Program (soon to be Connections Learning Center)
Debbie Staten, administrator, Student Outreach
Kenlyn Newman, assistant director, Dropout Prevention and Recovery

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information from staff on what is known about engaging students in their schooling and an update on this ongoing work in Jeffco Public Schools.

BACKGROUND:
In August 2009, the Board identified topics related to student achievement that members wished to learn more about related to the development of their goals for the school district. One topic identified for future Board discussion was, “What motivates students to learn and stay engaged in their learning?” This discussion will be focused on dropout prevention and retrieval.

After hearing from staff, the Board will discuss this topic with community members and students at a future meeting.
