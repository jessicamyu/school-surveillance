2.01 Renaming: Pennington Elementary School (EL-11)
Meeting
Jun 28, 2018 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the suggested name change of Pennington Elementary to Peak Expeditionary School at Pennington.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Pennington Elementary is moving to an Expeditionary Learning school model beginning this fall for the 2018/19 school year and continuing into the future.
     
  2. The school community, led by Principal Tim Carlin, has requested and supports a school name revision to better communicate that Pennington is now utilizing Expeditionary Learning as an instructional model.
     
  3. The school community followed district policy FF, Naming of School Facilities to consider a name change, including working with Chief Operating Officer Steve Bell. Principal Tim Carlin facilitated stakeholder meetings and sent out Google surveys to solicit community input. A stakeholder summit included parents, families, students, and community members. The community discussed the strengths and challenges of a name change. The school's PTA also weighed in on the name change and supports it.
     
  4. The requested name change is to Peak Expeditionary School at Pennington. It does not require a Colorado Department of Education (CDE) code change.
     
  5. The primary reasons for the change: 1) Peak because of the five "peaks" in metal out in front of the school; and, 2) the adventure education aspect of an Expeditionary Learning (EL) school will attract enrollment.
     
  6. A school name change requires Cabinet approval through the chief operating officer, which occurred in June, as well as this request for Board of Education approval.

For the Board of Education to meet with the mayor and city council members of the City of Arvada to discuss items of mutual interest including but not limited to school funding, Meyers Pool and the city bond.

In the past, Jeffco Public Schools boards of education have scheduled time to meet with the elected officials of Jefferson County municipalities to discuss issues related to the populations each body mutually serves.
