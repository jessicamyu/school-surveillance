Subject
2.01 Monitoring: Standards-Based Grading (Ends 2)
Meeting
Aug 19, 2010 - Special Meeting 5:00 pm
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Report

TIME: 5:00 p.m.

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Priscilla Straughn, executive director of Department for Learning and Educational Achievement (DLEA)

Heather Beck, executive director of School Management

Linda Rice, director of Middle Level Curriculum and Instruction for DLEA

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will deepen its understanding of the importance of standards-based grading to ensure a guaranteed and viable curriculum resulting in an increase in student achievement for all Jeffco students.

 

BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education continues to study middle level education and the various essential components of middle level instructional programs.  Standards-Based grading is one of the essential components of school reform focusing on the importance of clear feedback for students and parents.
 

To review the presentation for the Board and to read the article, The Challenges of Standards-Based Grading, by Thomas Guskey and Lee Ann Jung, please open the attachments below:

PURPOSE: 

The Board will continue its review of governance process policies.

 

BACKGROUND:

Governance Process policies outline Board of Education norms of behavior. On August 5, 2010, the Board of Education began its review of governance process (GP) policies.
 

The Board discussed revisions to GP-1, Governance Commitment, and GP-2, Governing Style & Philosophies.  The Board will take all policy revisions under study before adopting revisions.

 

To review governance process (GP) policies of the Board of Education, please find them in the Policies tab above, under Active>Board Policies>Governance Process.
