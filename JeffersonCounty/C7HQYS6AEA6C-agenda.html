 estimated time: 9 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)

PRESENTING STAFF:

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive information on a potential financing model for the Meyers Pool Replacement.   

 

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policies EL-7, Asset Protection, and EL-11, Communication and Counsel with the Board this item is brought forward to the Board.  

 

A partnership agreement (IGA) provides for Jeffco Public Schools and the City of Arvada to jointly finance construction of an aquatic complex, with Apex Park and Recreation District providing ongoing management and operations of the site.

 

Financing structure initial consideration includes:

  • Initial lender interest
  • Term; 20-25 years, fixed rate
  • Level repayment, semi-annual or annual principal
    • Right of first refusal
  • Joint and Several consideration for City of Arvada and Jeffco Public Schools
  • Pre-payment options; optional and purchase
  • Integrate with IGA

The Board of Education annually reviews the proposed resolutions collated by the Colorado Association of School Boards (CASB) which provide direction to the efforts of the CASB during the 2022 legislative session.
 

BACKGROUND:

The Board's discussion will provide guidance to the Board's delegate, Stephanie Schooley, who will attend the Fall Conference and Delegate Assembly on October 23 in order to finalize the guidance to the statewide organization.

2.05 Update on the Review of Academic Functions (EL-11)
Meeting
Oct 20, 2021 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 10:30 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Tracy Dorland, superintendent

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to have a conversation with the superintendent about her work on the review of academic functions supporting our schools to provide a high quality education for our Jeffco students.

 

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel with the Board, this item is brought forward to the Board.

The quality of a school district is a key factor when families are looking for a place to live. Research has shown that quality public schools are the bedrock of a strong economy and democracy. Jeffco Public Schools has historically been committed to increasing a child’s chances of future success by providing an education that is relevant and meaningful to each student. Jeffco is proud of its students and staff for their achievements both inside and outside the classroom.

 

Superintendent Tracy Dorland began work as Jeffco’s superintendent April 19, 2021. Her key areas of focus for district work include:

  • Focus on relationships, collaboration, and a positive climate and culture with stakeholders in our district, schools, and communities.
  • Explore how our district is organized to support schools with improved student achievement and social emotional well-being.
  • Find the just-right balance between system coherence and school-based autonomy.
  • Maximize the efficiency of our resources, aligned to the goal of improved student achievement - particularly focused on closing achievement gaps.
  • Support students with additional learning opportunities to make up for lost instructional time due to the pandemic.

 

To begin her work in the 2021/22 school year, she hired Dr. Jaime Aquino as a consultant to work with her on a districtwide review of how our central supports are organized so that our schools have the resources needed to ensure an excellent education for every student and a top-quality work environment for every staff member.

 

This is the second monthly update on this work.
