3.07 Jeffco Public Library Summer Reading
Meeting
May 5, 2016 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

estimated time: 6:44 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 minutes) 
 

The Board of Education is pleased to remind everyone that kids need to read and keep their minds active over the summer or they stand to lose up to two months of learning progress. This is called the summer slide. Think about this: research shows that a child who reads only one minute a day outside of school will learn 8,000 words by the end of sixth grade, where a student who reads 20 minutes a day outside of school will learn 1,800,000 words!

 

Jeffco’s Public Library's Summer Reading program helps prevent the summer slide because it’s easy, fun, and there are motivational prizes that keep kids reading!

 

Last year, 6,281 Jeffco Public School students participated in the Library’s summer reading program, reading and logging more than 1.4 million minutes.

 

Help Jefferson County read a marathon 26.2 million minutes and your school could win $300.  Jefferson County Public Library is awarding $300 to one school in each category (preschool, elementary, middle, high, charter/option) with the highest percentage of finishers based on school enrollment. Winning schools will each receive a Visa card presented at a school assembly in September 2016.

 

Pam Nissler, executive director of Jeffco Public Libraries, and other members of JPL management team will be present.

estimated time: 6:55 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.  
 

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Goals
EL-7, Asset Protection

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-7, Asset Protection, the superintendent shall not fail to obtain insurance coverage against theft and casualty losses and liability coverage for Board members, staff or the district itself in amounts reasonable for school districts of like size and in accordance with state law to the extent that cost and insurance market conditions permit.
     
  2. District insurance programs and policies involve: (a) School Entity Liability insurance designed to protect district employees, officials, volunteers and the district against liabilities and related costs while carrying out official district duties, including Automobile Liability and Professional Liability; (b) Excess Workers Compensation insurance required for self-insured statutory workers compensation programs designed to cover catastrophic benefit programs; (c) Property insurance to protect districts physical assets, content and property valued in excess of $2.37 billion dollars; and (d) other insurance policies such as Commercial Crime/Employee Dishonesty coverage, Fiduciary, Foreign Travel Liability and Network Security/Privacy Liability insurance.
     
  3. Working with the district insurance broker, Arthur J. Gallagher Company, marketing submissions were made to 23 insurance markets including incumbent markets. This resulted in 18 recommended placements and renewals. 
     
  4. Risk Management continues to balance insurance premium budgets with self-insured layers while loss exposures continue to increase. A strategy of working collaboratively with markets while continuously requesting coverage enhancements, effectively and efficiently protects the district from many types of catastrophic financial loss.
     
  5. The core property insurance program costs have increased $43,111 or 2.2 percent over the last renewal year. At the same time, some insurance limits have been increased, strengthening the district's position when the markets do harden on future renewals. Because of nearly flat renewals and the extended property insurance rate guarantee, costs have stabilized.
     
  6. The districts overall risk financing goals are met by executing this recommendation. All recommended insurance programs, services and premiums fall within 2016/2017 budget projections.
     
  7. Most insurance policies are to be bound with coverage effective June 1, 2016. Authorization for these expenditures should be secured no later than May 6, 2016, to carry forward coverage binding orders.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.    Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   

 

At this time, in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
