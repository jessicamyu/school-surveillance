The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online is available 7 am Sunday and closes 1 pm Wednesday or the day before the Board Meeting.  During sign up hours, click here to sign up to speak to the Board of Education at the Board Meeting.   

 

The Board listens to concerns on any topic for up to one hour. Public comment can also be submitted to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of the presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board,  they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker. We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  
 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de interés de la comunidad en los Comentarios del Público de su agenda. Registrarse en línea aquí para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva de Educación. (estará disponible a las 10 a.m. del lunes)

Por el momento, la Junta atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados a items on the agenda hasta por una hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que ha completado su información en la lista para Comentarios del público y tiene el equipo e internet necesarios para participar de una reunión virtual mediante Zoom puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por orador individual. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público. Se enviarán instrucciones adicionales por correo electrónico a aquellos que se registraron para los Comentarios del público. (ej.: enlace para la participar de la reunión) antes de la reunión.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. Por esta razón, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas que como grupo se registraron para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, ellos tendrán 10 minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se registran más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo hacia la Junta.

La Junta escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

 

Rogamos abrir el anexo siguiente el día 11 de agosto después de las 8 a.m., para conocer el orden de oradores invitados a la reunión para el comentario público.

7.13 Supplemental Funds Notification: September 2022
Meeting
Sep 15, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda 6:25 p.m.
Type
Action (consent)
Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for September 2022

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information. 

 

Formula grants are noncompetitive awards for which the allocation is derived from a predetermined formula (e.g., number of students receiving a specific instructional program). Competitive grant awards are based on selection after a review of all applications.    

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

 

1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE) released the district’s 2022/23 preliminary allocation for the Individuals with Disabilities Act (IDEA) – Part B of $15,402,225. Funds provide services to students with disabilities such as hearing, vision, speech or language impairment; emotional or intellectual disabilities; or autism spectrum disorder. An additional allocation of $411,604 is specifically for identifying, screening and serving preschool children. Chief Student Success Officer Matt Palaoro administers the program.

 

2. Gifted & Talented (GT) received a 2022/23 allocation of $770,637 from CDE. Funds cover professional development, identification of gifted students, development and support for advanced learning plans, direct services to students, consultants and coaching. Students have the option and choice to attend a GT Center school for intensive programming if they qualify with a body of evidence that suggests this is an appropriate learning environment. For students who do not choose to attend a GT Center school, an Advanced Learning Plan (ALP) is developed to bolster their areas of strength. GT Director Dr. Allen Golden supervises the program.

 

3. Gifted & Talented (GT) received $108,366 through CDE’s Universal Screening and Qualified Personnel grant for the 2022/2023 school year. Funds pay for universal screening for gifted identification, programming and partially offset the cost of the GT director’s salary. GT Director Dr. Allen Golden supervises the program. 

 

4. CDE awarded a $613,533 Mentor Program grant to Teacher Learning. The ESSER III state set-aside funds will expand the district’s teacher mentor program by increasing opportunities for veteran teachers to serve as mentors/coaches and providing more support for preservice and beginning teachers. New teacher surveys will gage adequacy of support.  Director of Professional Learning Stacy Paulson and New Educator Induction Coordinator Leann Chalupa manage the initiative.

 

5. Curriculum and Instruction received $499,262 through a CDE grant called ESSER K-8 Mathematics Curricula & K-3 READ Act Instructional Program. Funds, from CDE’s ESSER III state set-aside, will cover the purchase of the curriculum and training for Illustrative Math for 19 schools (primarily middle schools). For evaluation, staff will track academic outcomes for students benefiting from curricular resources. Math and Science Teaching and Learning Director Cecily Klein leads the project.

 

6. Excel Academy received an ESSER K-8 Mathematics Curricula & K-3 READ Act Instructional Program grant. The goal of this ESSER state reserve grant is to accelerate learning. The $47,332 award will purchase READ Act approved curriculum for English Learners. Principal Dr. Susan-Marie Farmen oversees the initiative.

 

7. Free Horizon Montessori received a CDE Education Workforce Program grant of $100,000. The ESSER III state set-aside funds will pay assistant teachers during the 2022/23 school year. A goal for this funding is to reduce staff turnover and increase or maintain current enrollment levels. Principal Donnetta MacDonald supervises the initiative.

 

8. Alameda International Jr/Sr High accepted CDE’s Education Workforce Program grant of $83,966. The ESSER III state set-aside funds will provide stipends for students participating in the school’s Pathways2Teaching program. Program activities include fieldwork in elementary schools and earning college credit through concurrent enrollment toward a paraprofessional certificate. Students complete a pre and post survey about the teaching profession. Principal Susie Van Scoyk and Teachers Genevieve Bassett and Eva Quezada manage the program.

 

9. Strategy and Communications received $150,000 from CDE’s ESSER state set-aside to support participation in a CDE strategic planning pilot. Funds will cover staff time, travel, and partner facilitation costs. Chief of Strategy and Communications Lisa Relou leads the project.

 

10. CDE awarded an additional $11,759 to the district’s previously awarded $463,978 for High Impact Tutoring. Targeting students in grades 4-7 at seven high needs schools, tutoring will focus on building math fluency skills to complement core math instruction. Paraprofessionals will receive training from math TOSAs, and work with small student groups four times per week. Math and Science Teaching and Learning Director Cecily Klein oversees the initiative.

 

11. CDE awarded Family & Community Connections, in the Title I Office, an additional $84,706 through the American Rescue Plan Homeless Children and Youth Formula Grant (this supplements the grant of $386,820 reported in June). Funds will support additional outreach events to identify and enroll homeless students, tutoring, school-based mentors, a parent learning series, and social/emotional support. Evaluation activities include at least one monitoring visit from CDE, an annual program report addressing spending in alignment with priority areas and proposed activities. Title I Director Linda Reyes-Quinonez and Family & Community Connections Coordinator Doria Barajas administer the project.

 

12. Jefferson Jr/Sr High, Lumberg and Stevens Elementary received a 21st Century Community Learning Center grant for $450,000 (year two of a five-year grant). Non-school activities include STEM, cultural and physical-movement activities. Monitoring, evaluation and reporting for these federal funds involve site visits from CDE staff; completion of an implementation rubric; and, tracking student academic achievement. Grant Manager Weston Scott oversees services.

 

13. Arvada K-8 and Thomson Elementary received a $300,000 21st Century Community Learning Center grant (year four). Funds support non-school activities to boost academic achievement, social-emotional health, physical health, and family engagement/education. Monitoring, evaluation and reporting for these federal funds involve site visits from CDE staff; completion of an implementation rubric; and, tracking student academic achievement. 8.01 Proposition FF: Healthy School Meals For All Public School Students
Meeting
Sep 15, 2022 - Regular Meeting
Category
8. Discussion & Action
Type
Action, Action (consent), Discussion, Information
Recommended Action
To support the passage of Proposition FF - Healthy School Meals For All Public School Students.

estimated time:  6:35 p.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. This initiative will provide access to free meals for all public school students.  Currently the state and federal government pay for students who qualify for free and reduced price lunches, and this measure would make all students eligible for reimbursement, includes funds for districts to purchase local, healthy foods, and provides funding for employee wages or stipends.
     
  2. Proponents believe that providing free meals will lead to more students eating healthy meals on campus. 
     
  3. The measure would be paid for by limiting the state tax deductions claimed by individuals who make more than $300,000 per year. Capping the deductions for individuals making over $300,000 is estimated to raise $100.7 million per year in additional General Fund revenue.  There is also an estimated increase of $15 million in federal funds.  

 

  1. To recognize the additional cost to districts and to handle the increased workload, participating districts can use $0.12 per lunch as employee wages or stipends for employees who serve school meals.  The statewide cost for this provision is $7.6 million.  

 

  1. Prior to the pandemic, a combination of federal and state sources paid for school meals for students who qualified for free or reduced price lunches. The federal government paid for all school meals for the last two years during the pandemic.  Jeffco saw a 30% increase in the number of meals served during this period.  If the federal government reinstated federal funds for all meals, the legislature could eliminate this program and the tax policy change. 
     
  2. Districts could opt into the program.  There will be some implementation issues for districts once this program begins in FY 2023-24 (if approved), but these are not significant.
     
  3. Current supporters include AFT Colorado, Children’s Hospital, Children’s Campaign, CEA, Colorado PTA, and many other policy organizations (see schoolmealsforall.org).
