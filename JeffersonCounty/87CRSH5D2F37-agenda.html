At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

Meeting
Jun 5, 2008 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.According to EL-13, Charter Schools Application and Monitoring, the superintendent may not fail to inform the Board and place on the consent agenda any loans to charter schools.
2.Rocky Mountain Deaf School has recently submitted its finalized excess costs calculations to the State Board of Education for approval. The dollar figure submitted by the school, after negotiation with the Colorado Department of Education, was reduced from $104.43 to $88.72. Along with other issues, this will result in a deficit of $118,000 for the current school year.
3.Rocky Mountain Deaf School is requesting a short-term loan of no more than $118,000 to cover the expected deficit. The loan will be repaid from the district's initial allocation to the school on July 1, 2008. School district staff are working cooperatively with the RMDS to finalize a balanced budget for the 2008/09 school year. A balanced budget is predicated on achieving enrollment goals and significant fundraising. At this time, a budget for next year has not been submitted that includes a repayment plan for the short term loan that is a balanced budget.
4.Rocky Mountain Deaf School will also show a deficit of $190,816 due to the late approval of the excess cost calcuation by the State Board. This deficit reflects uncollected excess cost billings for students from other school districts who are enrolled in the RMDS. This portion of the deficit should be eliminated when other school districts pay the excess costs for their students.
