3.11 Supplemental Funds - AmeriCorps Program to Family Literacy (EL-7)
Meeting
Oct 11, 2007 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Policy EL-7, Financial Administration, the Administration will bring grants to the Board for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the District (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the District is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.
This grant falls under Items A and C.
      1. The Board is hereby notified that Jeffco’s Family Literacy Department received $60,000 from the AmeriCorps Program, through the Colorado Parent and Child Foundation. The program aligns with Strategic Plan Objective 1, which ensures all students demonstrate achievement of academic content standards, and Objective 6, which ensures staff, community, and parent involvement, focuses on increased student achievement.
      2. The funds are used to support the general operating costs for the HIPPY (Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters) Program. Supporting the basic tenet from the National Center for Family Literacy, the program increases the basic skills and attitudes towards education, parenting skills, children’s literacy and school readiness, and the overall quality of the parent-child relationship
      3. The effectiveness of the program is evaluated by parent evaluation surveys, pre and post child observations, the HOME and Woodcock Johnston assessments. In addition to these national standardized tests, continuous observations and reports will be used to track growth and needs.
      4. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.

Gwen Welch, Family Literacy director, will serve as the project manager

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

9.01 Loan Request – Mountain Phoenix Community School (EL-19)
Meeting
Oct 11, 2007 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
9. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.According to EL-19, Charter School Application and Monitoring, the Superintendent may not fail to inform the Board and place on the consent agenda any loans to charter schools.
2.On April 26, 2007 the Board of Education approved a contract for Mountain Phoenix Community School (MPCS) to begin operation in the 2007-08 school year.
3.The resulting start-up costs for creation of an arts infused K-8 program led to significant up front costs that caused MPCS to overdraw its budget for the first quarter by approximately $21,000.00. This is a first and second quarter cash flow issue that results from the necessary purchase of materials, supplies, furniture, etc. in the start-up of the school.
4.The school has provided a written memo explaining the causes of the short-term cash flow.
5.The school requests that the district provide a short-term loan, the end of first quarter to the start of the second quarter, and the end of the second quarter to the start of the third quarter, in the amount of $21,000.00 which will be repaid from expected income based on FTE, school construction funds from the state, and SPED reimbursements. The school’s enrollment is near projection and the budget is projected to be balanced by the end of the school year. Due to the size of the school, budgets will need to be watched closely.
6.A member of the Board of Education requested this item be moved to the Discussion Agenda.

File Attachments

Motion & Voting

To approve the short-term loan of $21,000.00 for Mountain Phoenix Community School to cover cash flow issues related to the start-up costs of the PreK-6 program, as presented in BA-07-228.

Motion by Scott D Benefield, second by Hereford Percy.
Final Resolution: Motion Carries
Yea: Jane M Barnes, Vince Chowdhury, Sue A Marinelli, Scott D Benefield, Hereford Percy
