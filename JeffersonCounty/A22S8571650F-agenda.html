5.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Oct 1, 2015 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:40 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.  
 

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda and any Part Two comments for up to one hour. After one hour, any remaining Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

  6. In January 2015, meetings with staff and community were held to gather input and provide additional direction on the proposed Alameda Area Plan.  Community meetings were presented in English, Spanish and Vietnamese, utilizing the parent liaisons and dual language translators.  The Office of Student Engagement supported the meetings and parent outreach efforts.  Additionally, Stein Elementary School held coffee and conversation events with the families of Stein. Alameda High School students participated in conversations with school leadership to provide insight on the proposal.
     
  8. Details of the Alameda Area Plan were shared with the Board of Education on January 5, and February 19, 2015 as input was received and reflected in the developing proposal.
     
  9. The plan was revised to include the full relocation of Stein Elementary School following staff and community input.
     
  10. Additional community meetings were held in February 2015 and included revisions as suggested from previous community and staff meetings.
     
  11. The Board approved the plan during the March Regular meeting.
     
  12. The plan did not include changes to current school boundaries in the Alameda area.  Conversations with facilities, district leadership, and community members will be continue in the Fall of 2015 to discuss the development of a new elementary school to serve the central Lakewood area.
     
  13. The Board of Education requested an update on the Alameda plan for the October meeting.

7.03 Jefferson Area Plan Update

Oct 1, 2015 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Ends Discussion
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 8:40 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In September 2014, the Jefferson Articulation Area principals met with members of the School Effectiveness and Innovation Team (SIET) to review student and school performance data. Highlights of the review of the state data included one school entering year 4 of being “on the clock” as a Priority Improvement/Turnaround school; several schools have been Priority Improvement and/or Turnaround schools over the past four years; most schools are performing below district averages; and low performance in post-secondary readiness indicators.  Following the review of the school performance data, school leaders embarked on a planning process to identify key elements for school improvement aligned to the unique needs of the Jefferson articulation area.
     
  2. The group developed the Jefferson Area Plan and presented the framework of the plan to their staffs on Wednesday, December 3, 2014.   The plan was shared with Jefferson area families and announced to local media on Friday, December 5, 2014.   Information was shared with Jeffco Schools employees via the Messenger and Chalk Talk the following week.
     
  3. The framework addresses the social-emotional needs of highly-impacted students residing in the Jefferson area and provides structures to establish an environment that supports high expectations for all students to achieve at a high level.
     
  4. The framework focuses on strategies that can be implemented in a timely and cost effective manner.  The practices contained in the framework are supported by research and experiences connected to the student learners of the Jefferson area.  The key tenants of the framework are focused on student and staff needs and they provide the systemic supports the area principals know can make a significant impact on achievement and options for post-secondary endeavors. 
     
  5. The Jefferson Area Plan framework was created as the culmination of this process. The three key elements of the plan provide strategies related to: 1) people, 2) structures, and 3) programming.   
     
  6. In January 2015, meetings with staff and community were held to gather input and provide additional direction on the proposed Jefferson Area Plan.  Community meetings were presented in English and Spanish utilizing the parent liaisons and dual language translators.  The Office of Student Engagement supported the meetings and parent outreach efforts.
     
  7. Details of the Jefferson Area Plan were shared with the Board of Education on January 5, and February 19, 2015 as input was received and reflected in the developing proposal.
     
  8. A Jefferson Area Staff Advisory Committee, comprised of teachers, classified staff, and administrators from each of the schools in the Jefferson area, was formed to provide guidance and direction on the final plan constructed from the original framework to be implemented for the 2015-16 school year.  The committee met through the spring of 2015 to finalize specific strategies/programs for implementation in the 2015-16 school year and beyond.
     
  9. Additional community meetings were held in February 2015 with both the Jefferson Area School-level Accountability Committee chairs as well as the community served by Jefferson area schools.
     
  10. The plan was approved by the BOE during the April 2015 regular meeting.
     
  11. The plan was formally implemented during the first week of August 2015 with staff returning one week early for four days of professional learning and planning related to the Three Pillars.
     
  12. The Board of Education requested an update on the plan during the October regular meeting.

Recommended Action
to direct the superintendent to continue meeting with the SPAC committee as a district level committee; that the Board update policy GP-13 to accurately reflect that the SPAC is a district committee; and, that the Board adopt the District Accountability Committee resolution as amended and read into the record.
WHEREAS, the Board of Education recognizes that in an increasingly competitive and uncertain world it is critical that students graduate from Jefferson County schools ready for college and careers;
WHEREAS, the Board of Education has concluded that the percentage of Jefferson County students meeting the college and career ready standards on the ACT test taken by all students in Colorado is not acceptable;
WHEREAS, Jefferson County Schools is in the process of implementing a new strategy, which will require considerable support to District management from the members of the District’s Strategic Planning Advisory Council, which was originally established for this purpose;
WHEREAS, in 2009 the state of Colorado established District Accountability Committees to advise boards of education (in coordination with School Accountability Committees) on issues related to improved achievement, including the annual diagnosis of root causes, formulation of major improvement initiatives, alignment of budgets with these initiatives, and related matters, which together require the investment of a considerable amount of time and effort by dedicated volunteers;
WHEREAS, it is clear that District management requires sound advice from the Strategic Planning Advisory Council, and the Board of Education requires sound advice from the District Accountability Committee;
WHEREAS, a subcommittee of the Strategic Planning Advisory Council has recommended the separation of the Strategic Planning Advisory Council from the District Accountability Committee;
WHEREAS, the 2014/2015 Unified Improvement Plan (UIP) submitted by Jefferson County R-1 to the Colorado Department of Education specifically identified the need to revise the roles and responsibilities of the District Accountability Committee to ensure alignment with state guidelines.
BE IT THEREFORE RESOLVED, that a new District Accountability Committee will be established, comprised of members as described in state law (C.R.S. 22-­‐11-­‐301);
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Board of Education shall appoint members to the District Accountability Committee for a term of two years, and shall direct the administration to solicit for Board review no later than October 10, applications from members of the public who meet the requirements for membership under state law;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, the Board of Education shall vote to appoint eleven members to the District Accountability Committee, which in compliance with C.R.S. 22-11-301, shall include parents and at least one member of the business community (per C.R.S. 22-11-301(2)(c), a parent shall not be eligible to serve if he or she is employed by, or is a relative of a person who is employed by, the school district, including being employed at a public school of the school district). The Superintendent shall appoint at least one and up to three teachers and at least one and up to three administrators. The District Accountability Committee also shall have as members one School Accountability Committee representative from each of the 17 parent articulation areas, three charter school SAC parent representatives, one option school SAC representative and the Superintendent of the District or the Superintendent’s designee. No more than one concurrently serving member of the Strategic Planning Advisory Council (SPAC) shall serve on the District Accountability Committee;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that given the urgency of the need to improve student achievement results, as well as the need to meet the timetable for the delivery of District Accountability Committee outputs that is set by the state, the appointment of new members of the District Accountability Committee will take place at the October 15 meeting of the Board of Education;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that in accordance with state law, the members of the District Accountability Committee shall select from among the parent members a member to serve as Chair of the District Accountability Committee;
BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that in addition to the documents required by state law, each year, by no later than August 31, the District Accountability Committee shall deliver to the Board of Education a report on the percentage of students who are college and career ready, how Jefferson County compares to other districts in this area, critical obstacles hindering more rapid improvement of student achievement results, and recommendations for what should be done to overcome those obstacles.

estimated time: 9:10 p.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes)
