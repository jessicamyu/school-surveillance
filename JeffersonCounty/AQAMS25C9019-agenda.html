6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Sep 7, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:30 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.    
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

7.10 Supplemental Funds Notification: September 2017
Meeting
Sep 7, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
To be informed of the supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools as presented.
Goals
Ends 1 - Student Achievement

PERTINENT FACTS:


In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information shall be included in the board agenda item.

 

The Board is hereby notified that:

  1. CDE released 2017-18 allocations for the Individuals with Disabilities Act (IDEA)–Part B.  Jeffco’s allocation is $14,201,013, which will be used for the 2017-18 school year to provide a variety of support services to students with disabilities, such as hearing impairment, emotional disabilities, intellectual disabilities, speech or language impairment, vision impairment, autism spectrum disorder, or other categories of disability or impairment.  These funds provide services for approximately 8,000 eligible students.  Chief Student Success Officer Kevin Carroll oversees the program.    
  1. CDE released 2017-18 allocations of $325,764 for IDEA – Preschool. These funds are to be used for 2017-18 school year to assure that preschool-aged children with disabilities are identified, screened, and appropriately served.  Jeffco staff will continue to work with families, agencies, and schools to facilitate successful transitions into preschool services and into kindergarten for eligible students, using data driven, best practices approaches to intervention services and case management.  Chief Student Success Officer Kevin Carroll oversees the program. 
  1. The U.S. Department of Education released the Title VI 2017-18 allocations for the Indian Education program.  Jeffco’s portion of $98,381 is based on a per pupil allocation for each of the 514 eligible American Indian and Alaska Native students enrolled in Jeffco Schools with a U.S.D.E. Title VI Student Eligibility Certification (ED 506) form on file.  The funds will be used from July 1, 2017 to June 30, 2018 to assist eligible students and their families in meeting the challenging state and district standards by engaging them in activities that help them excel in their learning while at the same time providing opportunities to explore and maintain their cultural heritage.  Coordinator LeRoy Saiz oversees the program.    
  1. The State of Colorado Department of Health Care Policy & Financing (HCPF) and the Department of Education reimbursed Health Services School Medicaid $2,271,420 to be used for the 2017-18 school year. HCPF reimburses school districts for the federal share of Medicaid dollars for providing Individual Education Plan (IEP) services to Medicaid eligible students.  In turn, the district uses the funds to enhance health services to all students, which includes salaries and benefits for staff to carry out the goals of the program, vision and hearing screenings, transportation, mileage, physical services, such as immunizations, professional development around health education, and outreach and enrollment.  Because this is a reimbursement program, the 2017-18 allocation is based on expenses submitted for reimbursement in 2016-17.  Grants Accounting Manager Joshua Shoemaker oversees the program.
  1. CDE awarded Pennington Elementary School a 21st Century Community Learning Centers Grant.  $217,133 represents year three of the five year grant and is to be used for the 2017-18 school year.  These funds provide opportunities for academic enrichment and a broad array of additional services, programs, and activities.  To better meet the needs of Pennington’s community, a comprehensive expanded learning time (ELT) school model will offer enrichment opportunities through daily 45 minute blocks in five major categories:  health/nutrition, fitness, and technology, academics, arts/drama, and life skills for all students K-6.   Pennington ES Principal Sandy Craig is the project director.
  1. CDE awarded Brady High School a 21st Century Community Learning Centers Grant.  $222,040 represents year three of a five year grant and is to be used for the 2017-18 school year.  Of the ~500 students attending Brady, 99% are deemed “highly at-risk,” as defined by CDE.  Brady High School will partner with Jefferson Center for Mental Health to provide addiction counseling.  The program will also offer intramural sports, a competency-based diploma for family members age 21 and over, tutoring, and transportation for students.  Brady HS Principal Troy Braley is the project director.
  1. Student Engagement will receive $153,746 to be used for the 2017-18 school year from CDE’s Expelled and At-Risk Student Services (EARSS) grant.  This is the 3rd year of a four year grant.  The goal of the project is to prevent repeat behavior (suspension/ expulsion) and improve attendance and academic standing of identified youth.  Case managers will identify youth and provide intensive support to students and families, assisting them in navigating school and community resources, including substance abuse assessments and counseling services.  Director Dave Kollar is the project manager.
  1. Student Services received $162,800 from CDE’s School Counselor Corps grant to be used from July 1, 2017 through June 30, 2018. This is the fourth year of a four year grant and provides additional in-depth support to students moving from Mandalay Middle School to Standley Lake High School.  The transition from eighth to ninth grade is particularly one of the most pivotal times in a student’s education.  Counselors with expertise in college planning, dropout prevention and cultural competency provide comprehensive guidance and counseling to these at-risk students.   Director Jon Widmier oversees the program.
  1. Career and Technical Education (CTE) received its continuing allocation of $393,979 from the Colorado Community College System’s Carl Perkins Basic Formula Grant to be used for the 2017-18 school year.  The funds provide program support by offering CTE curriculum, academic integration, program materials and teacher professional development.   CTE includes skills and knowledge training in each of the six Colorado Career Clusters including: Business, Marketing and Public Administration; Agriculture, Natural Resources and Energy; STEM, Arts, Design, and Information Technology; Skilled Trades and Technical Sciences; Health Science, Criminal Justice and Public Safety; Hospitality, Human Services and Education.  CTE specialist Shelley Brunjak-Goerdt oversees the program.
  1. Gifted & Talented (GT) will receive $101,222.53 from CDE’s Universal Screening and Qualified Personnel Grant to be used for the 2017-18 school year.  Jeffco believes students should be challenged in their learning.  Students have the option and choice of attending a GT Center school for intensive programming if they qualify with a body of evidence that suggests this is an appropriate learning environment for them.  For learners who do not choose to attend a GT Center classroom, an Advanced Learning Plan (ALP) will be developed to support their area(s) of strength.  These funds provide universal screening for gifted identification and programming and partially offset the cost of the GT Director’s salary.  Director Roger Dowd is the project manager. 
  1. The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
