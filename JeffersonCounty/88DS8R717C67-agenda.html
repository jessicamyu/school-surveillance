4.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Sep 2, 2010 - Regular Meeting 6:00 pm
Category
4. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

America’s Choice products are research-based resources and designed to deliver targeted reading and math instruction. Ramp Up to Algebra and Ramp Up to Literacy are designed to replace the core. Navigator is a math intervention and will be provided outside the general education classroom. Intensive professional development will be included for teachers. Also included will be on site technical assistance; America’s Choice consultants will be working side by side in classrooms providing one on one modeling, coaching, and feedback.
     
  2. The following schools have been identified to participate in Professional Development and Technical Assistance provided by America’s Choice in 2010/2011: Wheat Ridge Middle School, Jefferson High School, Arvada K-8 School, O’Connell Middle School, and Alameda High School. America’s Choice will also provide intervention materials. In addition, the program may expand if additional sites are identified for these interventions.
     
  3. America’s Choice has researched and developed the professional development programs to increase student achievement in the areas of math and literacy interventions. They will facilitate and assist with the implementation of the instructional resource, professional development and technical assistance for the schools.
     
  4. The contract amount for the professional development, technical assistance and materials will not exceed $500,000. The contract will be funded through General Funds, Title I regular and Title I ARRA funds in the amounts of $173,000; $235,000 and $92,000 respectively.
