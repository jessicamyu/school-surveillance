Subject
2.01 District Membership Patterns and Trends (EL-11)
Meeting
Feb 9, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information
Goals
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 3 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

 

presenting staff        

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

Jeremy Felker, executive director, Student Data Privacy and Reporting

Bruce Huxley, director, Planning & Property

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to be aware of district membership (enrollment) patterns and trends.

BACKGROUND:
Enrollment and "membership" are terms sometimes used interchangeably. Membership focuses on the actual number of students in seats at our facilities, and "enrollment" is often used for budgeting purposes. Membership is data utilized by the Facilities Department to track student populations in school buildings in order to plan for program upgrades, capacity increases, building additions, and possibly new facilities. This information is also used to track underutilized schools in the district.

Data will be shared on membership enrollment patterns and trends over the past five years, including a focus on the last and current years’ data (2020/21, 2021/22): membership enrollment, choice enrollment, students leaving the district, free and reduced-lunch, home-schooling increases, and membership comparisons with other metro Denver school districts.

This information is shared in accordance with board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, and district policy FBB, Enrollment Projections.

Type
Discussion

PRESENTING STAFF:
Jennifer Withee, principal, Fitzmorris Elementary School
Celeste Sultze, community superintendent, Arvada area elementary schools
Sarah Branstiter, instructional coach, Fitzmorris ES
Stephanie Dill, learning specialist, Fitzmorris ES
Christine Wiggins, former Fitzmorris parent and president of PTA

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to engage in discussion with Fitzmorris Elementary School leaders, parents and staff on the topics brought forward to the Board during public comment on January 6, 2022.

BACKGROUND:
On January 6, 2022, members of the Fitzmorris Elementary School community signed up to address the Board of Education on the challenges and opportunities at Fitzmorris Elementary.

The Board of Education schedules time to accomplish its goals to increase student achievement and engage with parent and constituent groups to discuss issues related to the work of the district in accordance with Board governance process policy GP-17, Community Engagement.

The superintendent supports this engagement as outlined in Board executive limitation policy, EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, “with respect to providing information and counsel to the Board, the superintendent may not fail to give the Board as much information as necessary to allow board members to be adequately informed and supported…” and “…provide for the Board as many staff and external points of view and opinions as needed for fully informed Board decisions as decided and agreed upon by the majority of the Board.”

Feb 9, 2022 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session

Discussion, Information

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

Tim Reed, executive director, Facilities & Construction Management

 

PRESENTING GUESTS:

Brittany Warga, member, Capital Asset Advisory Committee; project manager, Adolfson & Peterson Construction

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive a fourth quarter report on the 2018 Capital Improvement Program with a member of the Board’s citizen advisory Capital Asset Advisory Committee (CAAC) to ensure accountability and transparency in the current status of the program by project.  The CAAC receives monthly reviews of the activities and status of the 2018 Capital Improvement Program.
 

BACKGROUND:

The quarterly reports to the Board of Education focus on the current status of the 2018 Capital Improvement Program, projects anticipated to be completed by the start of the next school year (2022/23) or beyond, the financial status including contract awards and disbursed funds; and, for this fourth quarter report, projects begun in the 2021 calendar year.
