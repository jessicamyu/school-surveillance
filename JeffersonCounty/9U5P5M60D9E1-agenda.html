estimated time: 5:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 50 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dr. Syna Morgan, chief academic officer, Educational Research and Design

Michael Maffoni, executive director, Strategic Compensation

Kristy Parsons, TOSA Strategic Compensation

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to receive an update on the Strategic Compensation project.

 

BACKGROUND:
Jeffco was awarded a Teacher Incentive Fund Grant to conduct action research on the impact of variables on teacher performance and student achievement.  This project was titled “Strategic Compensation.”  

Twenty schools are participating in the Strategic Compensation project.  Ten schools are “design” schools in which all teachers and principals have access to increases in compensation as a result of changes in student achievement and distinguished ratings on evaluations.  Ten schools are “control” schools in which the staff members do not have access to compensation but are automatically paid an additional one percent of their compensation.  All schools have the same support structures including Master Teachers and Peer Evaluators. 

The project is being studied to analyze the impacts of the variables on student achievement.  Climate and culture in the schools have also been examined.

Preliminary research results will be shared as part of an update on the learning that has occurred over the past four years of implementation.  The project is in its final year.

The Board will hear from school based staff and from the executive director of the project, Michael Maffoni.

estimated time: 6:45 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.     
 

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda and any Part Two comments for up to one hour. After one hour, any remaining Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room.
