Meeting

Category
5. Honors, Recognition and School Reports

Recognition

The Board of Education is pleased to recognize the school communities of Stein Elementary and Deane Elementary schools, named  2010 Center of Excellence Schools awarded by the Colorado Department of Education in December 2010.  Only 32 schools in the state received the prestigious honor.  This is Stein's second year to receive the award. 


Established by the Colorado State Legislature, the award recognizes schools that demonstrate the highest rates of student longitudinal growth as measured by the Colorado Growth Model among those that have at least 75 percent at-risk pupils in their student population.

Deane Elementary received a $25,000 congratulations grant and Stein Elementary received a $30,000 congratulations grant from The Partnership for Great Schools, which includes The Anschutz Foundation, Fox Family Foundation, Gates Family Foundation and Piton Foundation.   

Stein Principal Socorro Alaracon and Deane Principal Charlene White will be present, along with members of the school staff, students and community.

6.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Jan 6, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

Contract Award: District Bandwidth (EL-7)
Meeting
Jan 6, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize the director of Purchasing to negotiate and enter into a 7 year contract with Qwest at a yearly cost not to exceed $455,520.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board Executive Limitation Policy 7, Asset Protection, the superintendent may not contractually obligate the Board to an expenditure greater than $250,000.
     
  2.  The District utilizes the Qwest Metropolitan Ethernet fiber for delivering technology to the schools and central offices. The District currently has two service agreements that deliver the fiber infrastructure for the District.
     
  3. The original agreement expires this school year and a Request for Proposal #22755 was issued October 27, 2010 for voice and data circuits to service selected schools. The RFP closed November 29, 2010. Four responses were received. One was a “no bid” and one was deemed non-responsive and not considered for evaluation. The remaining two responses were evaluated by a team comprised of District personnel from Telecom, WAN, Network Security and Information Technology.

     
  4. Evaluation found that one of the vendors could not meet the project deadline. Best and Final pricing was requested from Qwest and the District is accepting their Best and Final Offer (BAFO) for a seven year term.

     
  5. The seven year contract with Qwest will reduce the monthly costs of this service by 26%. The new adjusted pricing agreement for this service is effective July 1, 2011 and will be approximately $455,520 per year.

8. Ends and Monitoring Agenda
Subject
8.01 Preliminary Facilities Master Plan (EL-8)
Meeting
Jan 6, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
8. Ends and Monitoring Agenda
Type
Discussion, Report

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

  1. In compliance with Board executive limitation EL-8, Facilities, ". . . the superintendent may not build or renovate without well-documented needs." 
     
  2. In March 2009, Jeffco Public Schools authorized Jacobs, Magellan Consulting, DeJONG and MOA Architecture to provide a comprehensive district-wide assessment of facilities. The assessment consisted of a capacity analysis, an educational adequacy assessment, a building condition assessment, and a review of all site and building systems with a life cycle renewal forecast.
     
  3. All of the collected data is housed in an assessment database maintained by the Facilities Planning staff.
     
  4. The teams visited each facility and completed data collection assessment forms identifying current deficiencies at each location, along with building systems and subsystems for each building with the estimated remaining life.
     
  5. A final report, the Facilities Conditions Assessment Summary of Findings dated September 2010 provides an overview of the assessment work completed, the results and the Facility Condition Index for each building.
     
  6. As a result of the Summary of Findings, a Preliminary Facilities Master Plan (The Plan) has been developed.  The Plan provides facilities recommendations ranging from general renovation projects to phased replacements to consolidations and closure.  The Plan will be used in preparation for the next Capital Improvement Plan as well as short term facilities changes that will provide for the most efficient and effective use of its buildings.
