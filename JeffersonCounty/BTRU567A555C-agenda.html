PURPOSE / PROPÓSITO: 
For the Board of Education to receive an update on the current bond market, timeline and structure for issuance and refinance of bonds. / Para que la Junta Directiva de Educación reciba un reporte sobre el mercado actual de bonos, el calendario y la estructura de la emisión y la refinanciación de los bonos.

 

BACKGROUND / ANTECEDENTES:

On November 6, 2018, voters of Jefferson and Broomfield counties in Colorado approved a $567 million dollar bond package for school and district capital construction projects. Due to IRS spenddown requirements and the feasibility of work that could be completed, the district made a decision to do two issuances. The first issuance for approximately $326,490,000 million in proceeds was completed in December 2018 with plans to issue the remaining 2018 authorization 2-3 years later. Robert W. Baird & Co., the district’s investment bankers, has been monitoring the market and the interest rate available for general obligation bonds. District staff has been working with the underwriting team on timeframe for the structure and sale of the bonds for the new issuance as well refinancing two maturities in the 2012 refunding bond.

 

El 6 de noviembre de 2018, los votantes de los condados de Jefferson y Broomfield en Colorado aprobaron un paquete de bonos de 567 millones de dólares para proyectos de construcción de activos fijos escolares y distritales. Debido a los requisitos de reducción de gastos del IRS y a la viabilidad de los trabajos que podrían ser completados, el distrito tomó la decisión de hacer dos emisiones de bonos. La primera emisión por un monto aproximado de ingresos de 326.490.000 millones de dólares se completó en diciembre de 2018 con la intención de emitir la autorización restante del 2018 dos o tres años más tarde. Robert W. Baird & Co., los banqueros de inversión del distrito, ha estado monitoreando el mercado y la tasa de interés disponible para los bonos de obligación general. El personal del distrito ha estado trabajando con el equipo de suscripción en el calendario para la estructura y venta de los bonos para la nueva emisión, así como en la refinanciación de dos vencimientos del bono de reembolso de 2012.

estimated time: 6:10 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in Public Comment on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Friday)  
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on the agenda for up to one hour.  If your topic is unrelated to the meeting's agenda please submit written comments to board@jeffco.k12.co.us. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident, staff member or parent of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster and has the necessary equipment and internet access to join a virtual meeting via Zoom may speak. There is a three minute time limit per individual speaker. Additional guidelines for public comment.  Additional direction will be emailed to those who sign up for public comment (i.e., link to join the board meeting) prior to the meeting.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts be shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who sign up to address the Board they will have 10 minutes for that group's view to be presented. All those who sign up as a group must be able to accompany the speaker virtually (see additional emailed direction). We respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.

 

Hora estimada: (time) (duración estimada: hasta 1 hora)
 

La Junta Directiva escucha los temas de interés de la comunidad en los Comentarios del Público de su agenda. Registrarse en línea aquí para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva de Educación. (estará disponible a las 10 a.m. del viernes)

Por el momento, la Junta atiende inquietudes sobre temas relacionados a items on the agenda hasta por una hora. Si su tema no está relacionado con la agenda de esta reunión, rogamos enviar sus comentarios por escrito a board@jeffco.k12.co.us. Escuchamos con respeto y pedimos lo mismo a los que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva. A fin de respetar a todas las personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva, pedimos que se enfoquen en los asuntos más que en las personalidades. Cualquier residente, miembro del personal o padre de familia del distrito que ha completado su información en la lista para Comentarios del público y tiene el equipo e internet necesarios para participar de una reunión virtual mediante Zoom puede hablar. Hay un límite de tres minutos por orador individual. Directrices adicionales para comentarios del público. Se enviarán instrucciones adicionales por correo electrónico a aquellos que se registraron para los Comentarios del público. (ej.: enlace para la participar de la reunión) antes de la reunión.

 

Si hay varios oradores con comentarios sobre el mismo tema, pedimos que se consoliden las observaciones y que se compartan nuevas ideas o conceptos. No es el número de personas que se dirigen a la Junta Directiva lo que guía en la mejor toma de decisiones, sino el contenido de su presentación. Por esta razón, pedimos respetuosamente no repetir el mismo mensaje de un orador anterior. Si hay cuatro personas que como grupo se registraron para dirigirse a la Junta Directiva, ellos tendrán 10 minutos para presentar su punto de vista. Todos aquellos que se registran como grupo deben acompañar de manera virtual al orador (ver instrucciones adicionales enviadas por correo electrónico). Respetuosamente pedimos que no repitan el mismo mensaje, más bien presenten conceptos distintos sobre el mismo tema. Si se registran más de 20 individuos para dirigirse a la Junta, los individuos tendrán dos minutos y los grupos tendrán cinco minutos para dar un mensaje colectivo hacia la Junta.

La Junta escuchará los comentarios del público hasta por una hora si es necesario y, por mayoría de votos, puede extender el tiempo para reuniones de alta participación. Gracias.

 

Rogamos abrir el anexo siguiente el día 11 de agosto después de las 8 a.m., para conocer el orden de oradores invitados a la reunión para el comentario público.

Goals
EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting, “Financial planning for any fiscal year shall not deviate materially from the Board’s Ends policies, risk fiscal jeopardy or fail to be derived from a multi-year plan.”
     
  2. State law requires the local Board of Education to adopt and appropriate a budget for all district funds prior to the beginning of the fiscal year Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 22-44-103(1). After adoption of the budget, the Board may review and change the budget, with respect to both revenues and expenditures, at any time prior to January 31 of the following year C.R.S. 22-44-110(5).
     
  3. The 2020/2021 Budget for the July 1, 2020, through June 30, 2021, fiscal year was adopted by the Board of Education on June 17, 2020.
     
  4. At the time of adoption, negotiations with the Jefferson County Education Association (JCEA) was not complete. The JCEA Negotiated Agreement was ratified on September 30, 2020, and approved by the Board on October 1, 2020, that included a one-time pay increase for eligible educators equal to 3 percent of the educator's annual salary payable in two installments occurring on the November 2020 and February 2021 paychecks and lane advancements per Article 17-7 payable beginning in October 2020 with retroactive pay for September 2020.
     
  5. Proposed revisions to the 2020/2021 Adopted Budget to adjust for compensation changes per the final negotiated agreement include a placeholder for other bargaining group with a total placeholder equaling a one-time 3 percent stipend for all staff. 
     

  6. Pursuant to C.R.S. 22-44-103 and 22-44-107, school districts must pass a resolution to adopt and appropriate the budget and per C.R.S. 22-44-105(1.5) must also pass a resolution authorizing the use of a portion of the beginning fund balances in the district's budget.
     

  7. The recommendation is to approve both the resolution for adoption and appropriation of the fiscal year 2020/2021 Revised Budget and the resolution authorizing the use of a portion of the beginning fund balance as presented.

Student Services received a year 4 award of $400,000 from the CDE’s School Counselor Corps grant (Cohort 7) to serve Jeffco Virtual Academy, Brady High School, Jefferson Junior/Senior High School, Alameda International Junior/Senior High School and Jeffco Open School. Counselors with expertise in college planning, dropout prevention and cultural competency provide comprehensive guidance and counseling for at-risk students. Director of Student Services Terry Walderman oversees the program.
  4. The Colorado Department of Human Services, Office of Behavioral Health awarded a second Opioid Response Grant to Student Engagement, Indian Education. Funds of $98,131 are for prevention activities through Native H.O.P.E. and Circle of Courage models with focus on reducing substance abuse and suicide attempts. A Prevention Specialist and Indian Education Liaison implement the program. This initiative aligns with Strategic Plan Objective Readiness for Learning, Social-Emotional Supports. Manager of the Student Engagement Office Jason Firestone supervises the project.
  5. Career and Technical Education (CTE) received the annual Perkins Federal Grant allocation via the Colorado Community College System. The grant of $409,303 will be used for salaries, teacher professional development, equipment and instructional supplies to bring programs up to industry standards. CTE focuses on the Strategic Plan Learning Tactics: Customized Pathways and Student-Directed Learning. CTE Coordinator Shelley Brunjak-Goerdt from Choice Programs administers the grant.
  6. The CDE Office of Adult Education awarded $238,780 to the Adult English as a Second Language (ESL) and Literacy Program. Funds support English language instruction to prepare adults (ages 18 and older) for employment and further education. More than 275 adults participate in year round daytime and evening classes at multiple school sites. Adult classes promote Readiness for Learning by preparing adults to better support their children’s education. Coordinator Karen Caddoo, from the ESL/Dual Language Team, oversees the program.
  7. The district will submit an Empowering Action for School Improvement (EASI) Grant application to CDE for resources to support schools identified for improvement through the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) and the state’s Educational Accountability Act (HB 18-1355). In the first year, approximately $650,000 will be requested for district led improvement strategies, as well as school-based efforts. Funds will allow for expansion of the district’s current EASI grant activities. Awards for the 2020/21 grant cycle are announced in January. Chief of Schools – Elementary, Renee Nicothodes oversees the initiative.

Meeting
Nov 5, 2020 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to accept for study district policy revisions to IKF, Graduation Requirements, as presented.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. District policies, regulations and exhibits are assessed for consistency, redundancy and legal compliance by the Office of Employee Relations and Legal Counsel before approval by Cabinet. As a part of this review, it is determined if law requires that a policy receive Board of Education review and approval prior to revisions being adopted.
  2. Jeffco Public Schools has a significant number of middle school students enrolled in and passing high school level courses, and currently they do not receive high school graduation credit for this work.
  3. Adding “Health” to physical education corrects an oversight from the prior time this policy was revised.
