estimated time: 5:30 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 
 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in the Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday).  
 

At this time, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. On April 21, the Board of Education received the proposed Facilities Master Plan after Board discussions regarding urgent facilities needs, enrollment/utilization issues and parameters in December and January.
     

  2. The Board directed the superintendent to host public presentations on the Facilities Master Plan to collect feedback and survey responses prior to the Board’s approval of the plan.
     

  3. On June 14, the Board received the comments from the public presentations and provided initial direction on a Jeffco Facilities Master Plan.
     

  4. With approval of a Jeffco Facilities Master Plan by the Board of Education, the plan is still reviewed annually and updated as needed.

estimated time: 7:45 p.m. (estimated duration: 45 minutes)
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Dan McMinimee, superintendent

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to discuss funding of educational programs in Jeffco Public Schools given anticipated future funding from the state.  

 

BACKGROUND:

Through its development of the 2016/2017 Adopted Budget and the negotiations process with employees, the Board of Education knows of general operational needs unmet by the level of state funding expected for Jeffco Public Schools in the immediate and future years.

 

The Board of Education approved a budget for 2016/2017 that worked to address employee compensation, clinic aides in schools, additional mental health supports, school security, funds to support the District’s Unified Improvement Plan goals (early literacy, algebraic thinking, multiple pathways to graduation), English Language Proficiency Act training, hard to staff schools and reserves (page 66 of Proposed Budget 2016/2017).
 

On June 14, the superintendent provided the Board of Education with a list of prioritized unmet operational needs.
