3.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Nov 7, 2013 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:05 p.m. (estimated duration: 15 minutes)

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda only. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

4.04 Supplemental Retirement Pension Plan-Amendments Number 9, 10
Meeting
Nov 7, 2013 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Superintendent Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve Amendments Number 9 and 10 to the Plan to terminate the Plan effective June 30, 2014 and reduce the number of voting representatives from each employee association.
Goals
EL-4, Staff Compensation

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy (EL) 4, Staff Compensation, with respect to employment compensation and benefits for employees, consultants and contract workers, the superintendent shall not cause or allow jeopardy to the fiscal integrity or public image of the district. Accordingly, the superintendent may not: establish or change pension/retirement benefits levels.
     
  2. The Supplemental Retirement Plan was established subject to Board approval and became effective September 1, 1999. The original funding commitment from the Board of Education was that the Plan and associated retirement related programs would not exceed an annual budget of $9 million for 10 years ($90 million total).

  3. The Supplemental Retirement Committee and the Board of Education have taken many steps over the course of the last nine years to best match the assets and liabilities of the Plan.

  4. Based on the draft of the actuarial valuation for the year ending August 31, 2013, the Plan is funded at 48.9%, and has a remaining unfunded accrued liability of $4.5 million.  The Board has previously determined that it is unable to add additional funds to the Plan since it has already exceeded its original financial commitment.  Plan assets are $4.3 million as of August 31, 2013 but actuarial liabilities remain at $8.8 million.  This August 31,2013 actuarial valuation report also indicates that in order to fund the Plan, an annual contribution amount of about $440,000 would be required. 
     

  5.  The Supplemental Retirement Plan Committee is now requesting that the Board move forward with formal action to terminate the Plan effective as of June 30, 2014 (the last day of the current fiscal year).

  6. Additionally, we are asking that the Board approve an amendment to the Plan that will reduce the number of voting representatives from each employee association from two to one. 
     
  7. The associations have agreed in writing, to the Plan amendments.

2. On August 15, 2013, Cornerstone Academy Charter School submitted an application to the district for approval by the Board of Education to become a district charter school. The Board of Education accepted the application for study on September 19, 2013.
3. A public hearing was held on October 3, 2013.  The Board of Education requested additional information of the applicant.
4. According to Colorado Statute, the Board of Education must respond to the proposal within 90 days after receiving the application.
  1.
