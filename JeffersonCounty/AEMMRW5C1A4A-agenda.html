Meeting

Category

Type
Information

estimated time: 5 p.m. (estimated duration: no one signed up to address the Board of Education)
 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in the Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.     
 

At this time, the Board will listen to comments on the topic of agenda item 2.01, Public Comment: Northwest Arvada School Boundaries for up to one hour.  We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker.

If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

Thank you.

Recommended Action
to approve the Coal Creek K-8, Meiklejohn Elementary, Sierra, Elementary, Three Creeks K-8, Van Arsdale Elementary, and West Woods Elementary designated school boundaries as recommended; and, to authorize the Planning and Property Department to prepare the necessary documentation to enact the change.
Goals
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 5 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS

  1. On October 6, in accordance with district policy JC, School Attendance Area, district staff presented recommendations to set new boundaries to designate student enrollment into six Ralston Valley Articulation area elementary schools.
     
  2. Administrative staff reviewed current and future enrollment patterns to develop the proposed boundary changes.
     
  3. Community meetings were held at Meiklejohn Elementary School, West Woods Elementary School, and Wayne Carle Middle School to solicit feedback on the recommendations for the school boundaries in support of opening the new Three Creeks K-8 School.
     
  4. â€‹Failure of the bond measure (3B) results in a reduction of 200 classroom seats being added to Three Creeks K-8 School.
     
  5. Failure of the bond measure (3B) results in the inability of Jeffco Public Schools to construct an additional K-5 elementary school in the Leyden Rock neighborhood.
     
  6. Continued growth of new homes in the Leyden Rock and Candelas subdivisions is not expected to slow or be reduced. Therefore, planning for anticipated student enrollment must be taken into consideration in the school boundary decisions.
     
  7. The final district recommendations for elementary and middle school boundaries for Coal Creek K-8, Meiklejohn Elementary, Sierra Elementary, Three Creeks K-8, Van Arsdale Elementary and West Woods Elementary schools are shown on the attached maps.
     
  8. For the 2017-18 school year, Three Creeks Elementary will enroll students in grades Kindergarten through 5th grade or 6th grade.
     
  9. For the 2018-19 school year, Three Creeks Elementary will enroll students in grades Kindergarten through 7th grade.
     
  10. For the 2019-18 school year, Three Creeks Elementary will enroll students in grades Kindergarten through 8th grade.
     
  11. Students attending Meiklejohn Elementary School will matriculate from 6th grade to Wayne Carle Middle School for 7th grade.
     
  12. All students residing in the elementary boundary areas for West Woods, Meiklejohn, and Three Creeks will matriculate to Ralston Valley High School.
     
  13. For the 2017-18 and the 2018-19 school years, current Jeffco students will be allowed to attend their current school or their newly designated school as determined by the new boundary map.  This two year transition period is limited to current students selecting between their current school and the newly designated neighborhood school.  Once a selection has been made by students, they may attend the school and its feeder school pattern as long as they remain continuously enrolled in the selected school (if outside their designated school boundary area).
     
  14. For the 2017-18 and the 2018-19 school years, transportation will be provided to students choosing to remain in their current school as well as for students attending the new designated school.
     
  15. For the 2017-18 and the 2018-19 school years, siblings will enjoy the same “grandfather” clause as older, current student siblings, as outlined above in item 6, for the two years of transition to the designated school.
     
  16. All new students will need to enroll using the revised boundary maps or the choice process to enroll in a school other than the designated neighborhood school.  The choice enrollment process is available for all families desiring to attend a school other than their current or newly designated school.  Families are responsible for transportation to a choice school per district policy.
