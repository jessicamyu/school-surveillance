PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2017.   


BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education monitors the districtwide condition as outlined in Board executive limitation policies EL-6, Financial Administration and EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting. 

 

Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 29-1-606 (1)(b) requires that the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report be delivered to the Board of Education by November 30, 2017.  The Board received a copy on November 16, 2017. 

 

Colorado revised Statute 22-32-109 requires the district to prepare a comprehensive audited financial report. The financial report consists of financial information prepared by the district and audited by an independent firm and indicates the financial status of the district at the end of the reporting period. The CAFR also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

Meeting
Dec 7, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Honors, Recognition and School Reports

Recognition

estimated time: 6:02 pm (estimated duration: 2 minutes) 

The Board of Education is pleased to honor the Dennison Elementary School community for being one of five Colorado schools awarded the national 2017 Blue Ribbon School Award by U.S. Secretary of Education Betsy DeVos.  The Dennison Elementary community has been recognized with this award three times. Principal Pamela Yoder will be present with leaders of her school community for this recognition having just returned from being honored at the awards ceremony in Washington, D.C. in November. 

 

The National Blue Ribbon Award is an annual designation presented to public and private elementary, middle, and high schools throughout the United States. Schools are nominated for the award by the state department of education, and then complete a comprehensive application about school practices. The program was initiated in 1982 with the goal of recognizing schools for their academic excellence and accomplishments in closing achievement gaps among student subgroups (though no Colorado schools were honored for closing gaps this year). Only 324 schools nationwide were recognized as National Blue Ribbon Schools in 2017.

6.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Dec 7, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:40 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

HIPPY provides high quality home visits to families at risk including those who are English Language Learners, low-income, recent immigrants or have low educational attainment.
