PRESENTING STAFF:

Lorie B. Gillis, chief financial officer

Betty Standley, director of Purchasing

 

 

PURPOSE:

The Board of Education will further its knowledge of the Purchasing department’s policies and procedures in order to work toward meeting its goals outlined in Ends 8 policy.

 

Ends  8 reads, the Jeffco Board will be effective and responsible and will function as a highly effective team and makes the following commitments to the community.

  • Effective and transparent oversight of the fiscal condition of the district and of the allocation of funds to meet Ends policies.
  • Communication about the constantly evolving and innovative work of Jeffco Schools.
  • High expectations for staff and student performance.

 

BACKGROUND:

Throughout the year, the Board of Education will schedule overviews of the work of departments within the school district.  These overviews will inform the Board on the various areas within the district contributing to or in support of student achievement in Jefferson County.

 

The Board will receive information from the Purchasing department and its role in insuring the ‘effective and transparent oversight of the fiscal condition of the district.’

Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

  1. The District seeks to implement a Tier II, targeted reading intervention to support struggling readers in grades K-2. The Leveled Literacy Intervention system (LLI) is a research-based resource designed to deliver targeted reading instruction and will be provided outside the general education classroom, in addition to core reading instruction.   2. Seventeen schools will implement this targeted intervention during the 2010-2011 school year.
     
  3. Irene Fountas and Gay Sue Pinnell developed the Leveled Literacy Intervention program for Heinemann Publishers. Heinemann will assist teachers with the implementation of the LLI resource and will provide customized professional development through the course of the school year.
     
  4. The purchase amount for materials for 17 schools plus professional development will not exceed $300,000.
     
  5. The funding for this initiative is through IDEA ARRA funds.

6.02 Board Policy for Adoption: Ends 1-9
Meeting
Aug 26, 2010 - Regular Meeting 6:00 pm
Category
6. Ends and Monitoring Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to adopt revisions to Board Ends policies 1 through 9, as presented.

PERTINENT FACTS:
 

1. In policy governance, the adoption of Ends policies is an essential Board of Education function.

2. Ends policies provide a global view on the desired outcomes for students, staff, and members of the Board of Education.

3. The following Ends policies were taken under study on August 5 and are brought forward for adoption:

 

Global Ends: All students are prepared for continuous learning and the world of work in the changing environment of the 21st century. Therefore,
 

Ends 1. (Student Achievement End) Every student will master the Colorado Content Standards at grade level.
Ends 2. (Student Achievement End) Every student will achieve one year's growth or more as needed to 'catch up' in every year of school and be ready for the next level.
Ends 3. (Student Achievement End) Every student will graduate career and workforce and/or post-secondary ready.
