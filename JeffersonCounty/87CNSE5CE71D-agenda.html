Subject
2.01 Supplemental Funds - CDE Counselor Corp to Student Services/Alameda (EL-6)
Meeting
Jan 14, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Item A and C.
    1. The board is hereby notified that student services received supplemental funds of $17,706 from the Colorado Department of Education’s school counselor grant program. The program aligns with strategic plan instruction objective 3, which ensures students have access to alternative pathways and interventions to improve achievement, close achievement gaps, and increase graduation rates.

    2. Alameda High School and Arvada High School have been identified as being at higher risk of students dropping out of school. These funds will provide stipends for staff to assist students in their six year graduation planning process, including completion of college and scholarship applications, FAFSA forms, arranging college visitations and interviews, etc. The funds will also provide students and parents with relevant materials, activities and supportive adults to guide them to identify, then take concrete steps to begin to actualize their college and career goals. These funds supplement a grant awarded earlier in the amount of $106,363, which was used to hire two counselors charged with specific responsibilities of decreasing the dropout rate, increasing the graduation rate and the percentage of students who appropriately prepare for, apply to and continue into post secondary educational options.

    3. The project will be evaluated on accomplishing objectives which include increasing enrollment in postsecondary educational institutions and promoting skill development and readiness to plan for college and career planning.
    4. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.
    5. Betsy Thompson, director of Student Services, will manage the project.

5.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Jan 14, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
5. Public Agenda
Type
Information
At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

6.01.010 Facilities Usage Decisions: Temporary Buildings (Ends 1)
Meeting
Jan 14, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
6. Ends Development and Monitoring Agenda
Type
Action
Pertinent Facts:
1.In November 2008 during the budget development process, the Board of Education directed the superintendent to create a Facilities Usage Committee to study current facilities based on capacity, enrollment, future enrollment, needs of the district and our unique communities, special programs, future direction, educational specifications and financial stewardship.
2.A project plan and timeline were shared with the Board of Education in January 2009 with committee recommendations expected to include, but not be limited to, school closures, boundary changes, and/or re-purposing of buildings. The Board discussed the plan and timeline with committee leadership, facilitators and staff in March 2009.
3.In September and November 2009, the Board met with the Facility Usage Committee leadership, facilitators, and staff to review the September interim report, community forum process, and November draft recommended options for public feedback. The Facility Usage Committee prepared its final recommendations for consideration by the Board of Education in December 2009 to be provided to the Board in a final report on January 14, 2010.
4.The Board of Education discussed its decision making process on the work of the Facilities Usage Committee on December 12, 2009 and January 9, 2010. The Board agreed to evaluate the recommended options based on the following five values, in no particular order: reasonable implementation by fall 2010, educational opportunities for students, financial implications, impact on community, and finalizing decisions for the community by mid-January 2010.
5.The Board of Education will be taking action on January 14, 2010 on the following: 1) the preschools at Arvada West into Fitzmorris Elementary and Swanson into Secrest Elementary; 2) moving current occupants of cottages to the main building at Allendale Elementary; 3) closure of O’Connell Middle School and Pleasant View Elementary School; 4) closure of Arvada Middle School or Russell Elementary; and, sale or disposal of temporary buildings at school sites where utilization is less than 92 percent. Until additional districtwide study of the issue occurs, there will be no movement of 6th graders from current grade configurations.
6.Additional options to be considered by the Board of Education on January 14, 2010 will be: closure of Wheat Ridge Middle School to create a 7-12 program at Jefferson High School; and, the closure of Russell Elementary School to create a K-8 program at Arvada Middle School.
To review additional information on the Jeffco Public Schools Facilities Usage process, please go to the district's main webpage: www.jeffcopublicschools.org
