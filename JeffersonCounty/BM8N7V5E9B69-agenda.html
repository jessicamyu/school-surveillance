The district wishes to renew the maintenance contracts with Oracle for the Oracle/PeopleSoft Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) applications, which includes human resources, financials, Hyperion budgeting, database management, asset lifecycle management, and others. Maintenance contracts entitle the district to software updates and technical support from Oracle.
     
  3. Oracle/PeopleSoft applications are core, mission critical tools that assist with the efficient and effective administration of the financial, human resources, budgeting, payroll and asset management processes at the district. It is imperative that the district has access to the latest software updates and support in order to successfully administer these systems.
     
  4. The PeopleSoft ERP provides high service reliability and sustainability of district business practices, procedures and compliance, facilitating communication between business units and systems, which are essential in providing high-quality support, promoting longevity and health of any organization.
     
  5. By renewing the maintenance contract with Oracle, the district will continue to receive key software updates that improve system stability, enhance the security of data, mitigate software "bugs," and enhance end-user functionality. In addition, renewing these contracts will allow uninterrupted access to Oracle technical support.
     
  6. Purchasing maintenance contracts directly from Oracle is the least expensive option, as the district receives the same wholesale pricing that is made available to Oracle resellers.
     
  7. The current maintenance contracts expire on June 30, 2020. If the district chooses not to renew these contracts, technical support and software updates will no longer be made available to the district. Purchasing maintenance contracts reduces the risk of incurring much higher costs associated with unsupported, outdated enterprise software. The renewal cost for all Oracle maintenance contracts will not exceed $1,038,286.96 and is budgeted as a planned expenditure in the Information Technology Fund.
     
  8. The contract renewal cost represents a four percent (4%) increase from the previous year. Over 11 business areas within Jeffco Public Schools utilize PeopleSoft including financial/supply chain, database licenses, human resources, asset lifecycle management, budgeting, payroll, imaging applications, training applications, customer relationship management, database tuning and diagnostic tools, and enterprise portal. Users include 15,440 employees and persons of interest (10,860 FTE).

3.10 Contract Award: Upgrade Palo Alto Firewall Components (EL-7)
Meeting
Apr 2, 2020 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize the director of purchasing to enter into an agreement with DirSec to purchase components to upgrade the existing Palo Alto next generation firewalls for a not to exceed amount of $693,000.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. This item is brought forward for Board of Education approval in accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-7, Asset Protection.
     
  2. Through a district competitive solicitation led by the Purchasing Department, the selected vendor, DirSec, will provide the components to increase internet capacity from 10 Gigabytes per second to 35 Gigabytes per second for the Education Center and Quail buildings. The components will be purchased with Information Technology Department capital budget.
     
  3. Upgrading the firewall hardware increases capacity and allows the district to take full advantage of the next release of the Palo Alto firewall’s operating system, which includes the following benefits:
  • Keeps a single security platform that combines multiple security functions including firewall, web-filtering, threat-prevention, and anti-malware;
  • Integrates with existing infrastructure to identify users on both wired and wireless networks;
  • Improves phishing site detection;
  • Uses new DNS Security subscription predictive analytics to disrupt attacks using DNS for command and control or data theft; and
  • Integrates with advanced artificial intelligence and Data Lake to increase visibility into malware/data breaches.
  1. Upgrading the firewall hardware will not increase the subscription costs for threat detection, web filtering, and other firewall features. The licensure model continues to ensure that there is consistent, predictable pricing as the district’s internet usage continues to grow.
     
  2. The firewalls are essential components of well-designed IT systems.
