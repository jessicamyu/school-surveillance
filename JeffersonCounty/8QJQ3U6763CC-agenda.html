5.02 Public Comment
Meeting
Feb 2, 2012 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Public Agenda
Type
Information

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

8. Ends Development and Monitoring Agenda
Subject
8.01 Board Budget Priorities
Meeting
Feb 2, 2012 - Regular Meeting
Category
8. Ends Development and Monitoring Agenda
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 7:50 pm (estimated duration: 1 hour)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. Throughout fall 2011, community members joined district staff in learning of and reviewing district work in order to present a list of suggested budget reductions for the 2012/2013 and 2013/2014 school years.  Early projections estimated a need for $70 million in reductions over the next two years.
     
  2. With the Governor’s January budget estimates, the district’s conservative reduction estimate shifted to a need for up to $55 million in reductions over the next two years to achieve a balanced budget.
     
  3. The Citizens Budget Advisory Council prioritized a list of suggested reductions generated with the involvement of over 3,000 Jeffco individuals.  The prioritized list has been available to the Board of Education and Jefferson County community since December 2011. 
     
  4. After the new board was established, members determined a schedule of meeting discussion topics in December and January which led Board members to a discussion on February 2 of the Board’s Budget Priorities.  Identified board priorities will direct the superintendent in the drafting of a proposed budget for the 2012/2013 school year.
     
  5. The budget is final upon its adoption which is scheduled for June 7, 2012 after two public hearings with the Board on May 3 and June 7, 2012.
