6. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:40 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the superintendent and board of education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information shall be included in the board agenda item. 

 

The board is hereby notified that:

  1. The Colorado Department of Education (CDE)  awarded Jefferson 7-12, in partnership with Stevens K-6 and Lumberg Elementary, a 21st Community Learning Centers Grant.  $666,120 represents year three of a five year grant and is to be used for the 2017-18 school year.  The program, titled the Jefferson Consortium, will provide non-school day activities and connects students more closely to their schools through extra academic, cultural, and sports experiences.  Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) programming will be the focus of activities.  21st CCLC project director Scott Weston oversees the program.
     
  2. Jeffco received its Reading to Ensure Academic Development (READ) Act allocation from CDE of $2,059,380.29 to be used for the 2017-18 school year. READ Act funds, appropriated through the Colorado early literacy fund, are calculated based on the number of K-3rd grade students identified as having a significant reading deficiency at the end of each school year.  Last year Jeffco identified 2,530 eligible students as having reading deficiencies.  The funds are used to support these students with strategies to help them succeed.  Administrative intern Kim Ballantyne oversees the project.
     
  3. Community & Family Connections (CFC) was awarded $37,000 from the CDE McKinney-Vento Education for Homeless Children & Youth Grant to be used for the 2017-18 school year. These funds represent year two of a three year grant which provides supportive services to the 2,950 identified students experiencing homelessness. The CFC program seeks to address the safety and educational needs of these students by attending to their direct basic needs as well as access to academic supports, with the ultimate goal of seeing each of these students graduate career and college ready. These funds will provide supplies, such as school supplies, hygiene, and other basic necessities for homeless families. They will also offset credit accrual, recovery or other educational fees, transportation to schools of origin for special circumstances, printing and outreach materials, and parent and family outreach evenings. Selected staff will attend the National Association for the Education of Homeless Children & Youth (NAEHCY) Conference and the Institute for Children, Poverty and Homelessness national convention. Coordinator Rebecca Dunn oversees the program.
     
  4. Healthy Schools received $30,000 from CDEโs Comprehensive Health Education Grant. These funds represent year two of a three year grant and will be used for the 2017-18 school year. Jeffco Schools will continue working to integrate health education into its schools, using Bridge To Curriculum to provide schools with a guaranteed, viable, standards-based curriculum. The health education content specialist, partially funded by this grant, has been instrumental in increasing the capacity for health education in Jeffco preK-12 by working with the curriculum teams and Bridge to Curriculum. The main activities of this project are curriculum development, implementation, professional development, and support in addressing the Comprehensive Health Education Standards for health-specific units, classrooms and teachers. Healthy schools coordinator Emily OโWinter is the project manager.
     
  5. Indian Education received $25,000 from Colorado Creative Industries to be used from September, 2017 through June 30, 2018.  The funds, which are awarded annually, will be used to support a Native American Afterschool Arts program, with the intent at keeping Native American students engaged in their education and to build or enhance a connection with their traditional culture through visual and performing arts experiences with professional artists.  This program includes a multi-session, multi-disciplinary afterschool arts program focusing on students at Bear Creek K-8, Arvada K-8, Thomson ES, South Lakewood ES, and Edgewater ES, working with teaching artists and mentored by high school students.  Coordinator LeRoy Saiz oversees the project.
     
  6. Arvada K-8 received $80,000 from CDEโs Connect for Success Grant.  These funds represent year two of a three year grant, to be used from January 1, 2017 through June 30, 2018.  The Connect for Success grant is made available to Title I schools with a Priority Improvement or Turnaround plan type in order to strengthen their Title I programs by implementing effective structures and strategies, and providing quality instruction to meet needs of minority students, students experiencing poverty, students with disabilities, and English learners. The grant offers Arvada K-8 an opportunity to improve their practices, create a collaborative culture, and improve academic achievement.  Principal Scott Simon will oversee the project.
     
  7. Healthy Schools and Student Success received $73,803 from CDEโs School Health Professional Grant to be used from July 1, 2017 through June 30, 2018 to provide social/emotional and drug prevention education to students at Wheat Ridge High School and Everitt Middle School.  These funds represent year two of a three year grant, the goal of which is to increase prevention education to address, prevent, and decrease substance abuse, specifically related to the legalization of marijuana, and coordinate referral care for students with substance abuse and other behavioral needs.  One full-time school prevention counselor will implement school-level prevention initiatives, including delivering education at a universal level.  Director Jon Widmier is the program manager.
     
  8. Student Engagement received $420,475 from CDEโs Student Re-Engagement Grant Program to support activities and strategies focused on student engagement and student re-engagement and improvement of postsecondary and workforce readiness performance indicators.  Each of the five participating schools, Jeffcoโs 21st Century Virtual Academy, Alameda International Junior/Senior High, Brady Alternative High School, Jefferson Junior/Senior High, and Sobesky Academy, use a slightly different strategy to engage their students.  The support includes targeted attendance and behavioral interventions, social-emotional programming, increased opportunities for credit recovery as well as community and family engagement programming.  The grant period is from July 1, 2017 through June 30, 2018 and is the final year of a three year grant.  Future funding is contingent on future appropriations.  Director Dave Kollar oversees the program.

Subject
8.01 Physical and Emotional Well-Being of Students (Ends 1)
Meeting
Oct 5, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
8. Ends Discussion
Type
Discussion
Goals
Ends 1 - Student Achievement

estimated time: 7:40 p.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

 

1. Board Ends 1 states, "Every school and the district will have an engaging climate and culture that:

  • Ensures a safe, caring and engaging environment for students, staff and families.
  • Values the diversity of all students, staff and families.
  • Supports the social, emotional and physical wellness for students and staff."
