Meeting
Jun 18, 2015 - Special Meeting
Category
3. Discussion Agenda

Recommended Action
to approve the memorandum of understanding regarding compensation agreed to by the bargaining teams for the district and JCEA.

estimated time: 5:31 p.m. (estimated duration: 15 minutes) 
 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In March of 2015 the Board of Education approved the implementation of a new hire salary placement plan that is competitive with other school districts in the Denver metro area and aligned with the Board-approved teacher compensation plan. 
     
  2. However, as a consequence of multiple years of pay freezes for Jeffco staff the new hire salary plan resulted in new hires being paid more than Jeffco staff with similar credentials and years of experience.  
     
  3. On May 1, JCEA filed a complaint in the Jefferson County District Court requesting that the court enter an injunction to stop the district from hiring new teachers under the approved pay plan. In a ruling issued on May 12, the court recognized the authority of the school board to adopt and implement a compensation plan that rewards effective teachers and ruled favorably on the district's initial efforts to hire new employees pursuant to the new hire salary placement plan. However, the court granted the injunction with respect to additional hiring under the new salary plan and instructed the district and JCEA to negotiate the issue of new hire salary placement. 
     
  4. The negotiating teams have successfully negotiated the attached Memorandum of Understanding which is presented to the Board of Education for review and approval.  With the approval of this MOU, the district can proceed to make competitive salary offers. 
     
  5. Within five days of approval of this MOU by the Board of Education, the district and JCEA will enter a joint stipulated motion to dismiss the cause of action currently pending before the Jefferson Country District Court.

3.06 Acuity Results (Ends 1, 2)
Meeting
Jun 18, 2015 - Special Meeting
Category
3. Discussion Agenda
Type
Discussion

estimated time: 6:46 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 
 

PERTINENT FACTS

  1. On June 11, 2015, the Board of Education reviewed Acuity results for the 2014/2015 school year. At that meeting, the Board was informed of the administration of Acuity Next, the major Acuity redesign to align with the Colorado Academic Standards, in Jeffco.  The administration of the assessment allowed for up to three assessments over the course of the year. Most schools administered Acuity A (beginning of year) and Acuity C (end of year) in the 2014/2015 school year. As a predictive assessment for students achieving a year’s growth in a year, each Acuity administration is more challenging for students than the previously administered assessment.
     
  2. Board discussion focused on English Language Arts and mathematics scores. Scale scores increased in all grades in both subjects.
     
  3. The Board President has requested additional discussion on disaggregated data by school and related to special education, gifted/talented and free/reduced student cohorts that were not available to share with the Board of Education for the June 11 presentation and discussion.
     
  4. Board Ends policy 1 states, ‘Every student will master the Colorado Content Standards at grade level.’ Board Ends policy 2 states, ‘Every student will achieve at least one year’s growth, or more as needed to ‘catch up,’ in each year of school and be ready for the next level.’
