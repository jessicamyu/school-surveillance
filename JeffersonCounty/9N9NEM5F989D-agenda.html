3.02 PTA Schools of Excellence: Elk Creek, Pennington elementary schools
Meeting
Sep 4, 2014 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

estimated time: 6:33 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 minutes) 
 

The Board of Education is pleased to honor Elk Creek and Pennington elementary schools as National PTA Schools of Excellence.  Elk Creek Principal Ryan Lucas and Pennington Principal Sandy Craig will be present for this recognition with members of their school communities.

 

The Schools of Excellence certification program started in 2003 and was revamped for the 2013/2014 school year.  When PTAs, parents, and schools have great partnerships, the educational experience and overall well-being for all students is deeply enriched.  

 

A School of Excellence certification both recognizes and celebrates these partnerships.

 

Families at a School of Excellence feel welcomed and empowered to support student success and acknowledge that PTA is a key partner for continuous school improvement.

 

PTAs and schools who participate in the School of Excellence program learn new ways to engage families in school decision-making, such as improvements to programs, practices, and policies related to education, health, safety, and the arts.  All this supports PTA's deep and long-held belief that when parents are involved in their children's school and education, children do better.

4.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Sep 4, 2014 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:35 p.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour)


The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday).  
 

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda and any Part Two comments for up to one hour. After one hour, any remaining Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, each person must sign up online and then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.
