3.06 Warren Tech: NASA HUNCH Project
Meeting
Jun 2, 2011 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

estimated time: 6:43 p.m. (estimated duration: 10 minutes)

The Board of Education is pleased to receive an update on the NASA HUNCH project at Warren Tech and recognize the two student scholarship award winners Garret Lipkor/Arvada West High School and Sarah Fellar from Lakewood High School, who each received $500 scholarships toward their continuing education.  Alex Domain/McLain High School will also be recognized for his winning work in Precision Machining in the Transport Flight Cases contest.
 

The NASA HUNCH (High Schools United with NASA to Create Hardware) project is an extension of the NASA mission supporting the International Space Station (ISS) and the eventual mission to Mars.  Teachers and students involved in this project are also considered employees of NASA and work to develop various projects to support astronaut training for the ISS.  During the past year, students have worked on growth experiments and transport case hardware.  In March, several students along with their teachers tested the growth experiments on the zero gravity plane at the Johnson Space Center.

 

In early May, students presented their findings to a group of NASA scientists at the Johnson Space Center.  They will  share a overview tonight.  Working closely with the NASA scientists, students gained valuable insight to the inner workings of the ISS and how to work as a team.  Additionally, the NASA scientists paid the highest compliment to our student who produced flight ready transport cases.  In their opinion, the cases Alex Domain produced were as good as or better than the ones Boeing of Lockheed Martin produced at a much higher cost.

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.
