It also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has signed up on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

6.10 Bandwidth Expansion to All Schools (EL-6, 7)
Meeting
Jan 10, 2008 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.The Districts Wide Area Network (WAN) is comprised of over 150 sites.
2.Legacy equipment in use today cannot keep up with anticipated demands for 21st technology (Web 2.0, Online/Distance learning, “Discovery Learning” - video streaming, Web-Based testing, Video Conferencing, IP television distribution, Video Security, and VoIP).
3.The expanded use of web based systems such as Blackboard, PeopleSoft, United Streaming, Infinite Campus, School Center, Encore, has contributed to the increasing demand for bandwidth between schools, Ed Center and/or the internet.
4. IT conducted a District wide bandwidth survey at the start of school year and it indicated that 15% of the schools were currently high or exceeding bandwidth utilization thresholds.
5The 2006 National study (Greaves Group) predicts that more than half of all student computing devices will be mobile by the year 2011 and online learning will grow at a compound annual rate of 26% over the next five years increasing the demand for bandwidth.
6The District has drafted a three year technology plan that highlights the need for additional bandwidth expansion to all sites.
7.Based on current bandwidth usage, the national study, the technology plan, current trends, and increasing bandwidth demand across the District a long term bandwidth expansion solution was required.
8.An RFP process was initiated to expand bandwidth to all schools. The RFP process and contract negotiations are tied to the national E-rate process deadlines requiring Board of Education action on the winning contract by January 31st.
9.Funding for this contract is via a BFR proposal that was submitted to expand bandwidth for all schools. The long term solution (optical Ethernet) will cost an additional $1,280,905 per year. The existing bandwidth contract with Qwest in the amount of $480,000.00 will be retired when the optical Ethernet contract is fully implemented allowing for full funding of the new contract.
10.To meet E-rate deadlines and insure these additional costs qualify for e-rate reimbursement a signed contract must be in place before January 31, 2007. The contract will includes terms that state the contract is only valid if the Board of Education approves funding of the Bandwidth Expansion BFR proposal.

6.11 Internet Expansion (EL-6, 7)
Meeting
Jan 10, 2008 - Regular Business Meeting 6 p.m.
Category
6. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

1.District Wide Area Network (WAN) is comprised of ~150 sites
2.In the mid 90’s the District focused on bringing together the school LANs to provide the needed mainframe connectivity to these LANs and to network all of the district’s telecommunications service into a common hub. Computer Labs were utilized for student learning
3.High bandwidth applications and data (united streaming, video conferencing, webcasts, video security, and web-based testing) have increased Internet access requirements. Students are now able to access real time developing information rather than static closed information. Teachers are able to look not just at a single book or source, but provide a variety of avenues for students to understand concepts.
4.The 2006 National study (Greaves Group) predicts that more than half of all student computing devices will be mobile by the year 2011 and online learning will grow at a compound annual rate of 26% over the next five years.
5.Based on this national study, current usage, and anticipated usage the District’s current Internet link (100Mbps) will be saturated by 2011.
6.Funding will be provided via IT Operating Budget.
