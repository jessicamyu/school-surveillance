3.01 Budget Update: Finalizing FY 2019/2020, Updates on Current Year Budget, and Community Engagement and Reduction Planning for 2021/2022
Meeting
Sep 9, 2020 - Special Study/Dialogue Session Meeting
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion
Goals
EL-6, Financial Administration
EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting

estimated time: 9 a.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Nicole Stewart, interim chief financial officer

Jason Hendricks, acting director-budget and treasury

 

PURPOSE:

The Board will receive information on the status of finalizing fiscal year (FY) 2019/2020, the budget status and enrollment for current year, and the community engagement and reduction planning process for FY 2021/2022.

BACKGROUND:

Due to the financial climate as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic, the Board will receive an update on the work in progress for finalizing FY 2019/2020 including an analysis of Coronavirus Relief Funds (CRF), preparation for the year-end audit, and status of outstanding tax revenues and interest.

 

The presentation will include an update on the current year budget that will cover revenue and expenditure assumptions and potential impacts with regard to CARES funding, the compensation agreement with employee associations, possible mid-year rescissions from the state, potential restart costs, and enrollment as well as estimates for spend down of reserves and use of CRF funds.

 

In addition, staff will present preliminary ideas for community engagement opportunities and the budget reduction planning process for FY 2021/2022 to start in fall of 2020.

Meeting
Sep 9, 2020 - Special Study/Dialogue Session Meeting
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information

estimated time: 10 a.m. (estimated duration: 1 hour) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

Dr. Carol Eaton, executive director, Instructional Data Services

Dr. Heather MacGillivary, director, Research and Assessment

 

PURPOSE:

To review and discuss with the Board of Education an overview of the survey findings from the 2019/2020 surveys administered in Jeffco Public Schools: Family School Partnership (FSP) survey, Make Your Voice Heard (MYVH) student survey, and Teaching Learning Conditions Colorado (TLCC) staff survey.

 

BACKGROUND:
Jeffco Public Schools conducts stakeholder surveys to gather feedback from Jeffco Public Schools students, parents, and employees. Each of these surveys inform the district and schools about implementation of Board Ends, the Strategic Plan, District and School Unified Improvement Plans, and other areas of continuous improvement. The Board received Friday Board Updates about each of the surveys in the spring 2020. The most recent Board of Education presentation about climate surveys occurred in June 2018. An interactive dashboard of the student and family surveys can be found here. The interactive dashboard of the educators survey is available from the Colorado Department of Education here.

Subject
3.03 Name Change: New Classical Academy at Vivian (EL-11)
Meeting
Sep 9, 2020 - Special Study/Dialogue Session Meeting
Category
3. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion, Information
Goals
EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board

estimated time: 11 a.m. (estimated duration: 20 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Michael Zweifel, principal, Vivian Elementary School

Brenda Carlson, community superintendent

Renee Nicothodes, chief of schools-elementary

Matt Flores, chief academic officer

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to review the name change proposal for Vivian Elementary prior to action on the name change to Classical Academy at Vivian, in October.  

 

BACKGROUND:

Early fall of 2019, Superintendent Dr. Jason Glass encouraged district administration to review the classical academy model as a possible alternative for elementary schools who were declining in enrollment.

 

After one year of extensive conversations, community meetings, and data gathering, Vivian Elementary is ready to proceed with finalizing the steps toward establishing a classical academy: name change approval by the Board of Education.

 

Over the past year with the intent of determining the need, interest, support and possibility of implementation, Vivian staff has met with parents, community members, students, superintendent, and administrators from Transportation, Food Service, Choice Enrollment, Facilities, Budgets, ESL/Dual Language, Student Data Privacy, Human Resources, Curriculum and Instruction, Communications, Chief Academic Office, Chief Strategy Office & Chief of School Elementary. All have contributed to and are in support of the proposal in moving forward as a classical academy.
