PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to provide guidance on any work for next year's DAC and to receive the District Accountability Committee recommendations on:

  • spending priorities for district funds in the development of the 2019/2020 budget;
  • the District Unified Improvement Plan including priority challenges, major improvement strategies and action plans;
  • engagement supports with family, school, and community members;
  • charter school application DAC rubric; and,
  • an expansion of articulation area representatives in the membership of the DAC.

BACKGROUND:
On February 19, after months of work in subcommittees and the full committee, the District Accountability Committee (DAC) reached agreement on its 2019/20 recommendations to the Board of Education.

The DAC Budget Subcommittee (Dave Johnson, Dave Wells, Jeff Baucum, Joan Chavez-Lee, Alex Dorotik, Tom Dougherty, Susan Lopez, Nancy McCanless, Geneva Moore, Mark Stutz) worked with district staff and information collected through the school accountability committee survey, cabinet budget recommendations, DUIP budget recommendations, and FSCP budget recommendations to provide the budget recommendations through the DAC.

The DAC DUIP Subcommittee (Deb Guiducci, Margaret Lessenger, Lorri Avery, Jim Earley, Jill Fellman, Hi Howard, Ben Robertson, Jackie Seibel, Stephanie Schooley) worked with district staff to provide the DUIP recommendations through the DAC.

The DAC Family School Community Partnership Subcommittee (Carsten Engebretsen, Susan Chapla, Caitlin Fitzpatrick, Mary Jo Gentleman, Molly Given, Evie Hudak, Tim Mills, Mercedes Ramos, Lisa Vega-Fields) worked with district staff to provide the community engagement recommendations through the DAC.

The DAC Charter Review Subcommittee (Sally Kate Tinch, Dave Wells, Jeff Baucum, Nancy Hardesty, Evie Hudak, Maggie Ruddy, Katie Van Guilder) worked with district staff to provide the charter application rubric recommendation through the DAC.

The DAC Executive Committee (Jim Earley, Sally Kate Tinch, Carsten Engebretsen, Deb Guiducci, Dave Johnson, Margaret Lessenger, Lisa Vega-Fields, Dave Wells) submits a recommendation to expand DAC membership at the articulation area level to support engagement efforts.

5.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Mar 7, 2019 - Regular Meeting
Category
5. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:20 p.m. (estimated duration: up to 1 hour)

 

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

Recommended Action
To be informed of supplemental funds awarded to Jeffco Public Schools for March 2019

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with district policy regulation DD-R, Funding Proposal, Grants and Special Projects, the grant acquisition department shall inform the Superintendent and Board of Education of all grant awards greater than $10,000. Included in notification is background information, amounts awarded, identification of the grant manager, evaluation criteria, and any other pertinent information.  

 

The Board of Education is hereby notified that:

  1. The Jeffco-DeAngelis Foundation awarded $140,000 for the Frank DeAngelis Center for Community Safety in support of technology and infrastructure to enhance first responder training for Colorado schools and law enforcement agencies to better prepare, train and respond to incidents of violence. Funds will purchase video/audio technology and IT infrastructure support. The Center’s work supports the Strategic Plan Tactic for Operations: Safe and Secure Facilities to Support Learning. John McDonald, executive director of Security and Emergency Management, will oversee the project.
     
  2. The Colorado Division of Homeland Security & Emergency Management awarded $712,387 though a School Security Disbursement Grant. Funds will be used to improve school safety and security. The project aligns with Operations Tactic: Safe and Secure Facilities to Support Learning. Executive Director of Security and Emergency Management John McDonald will manage the funds.
     
  3. The Colorado Department of Education awarded a Computer Science Education Grant of $29,980. Funds will pay for professional development for a computer science K-12 pathway. MindSpark or Colorado School of Mines training will be available to elementary, middle and high schools teachers, focusing on schools without a trained teacher or digital teacher librarian. This professional development addresses the Strategic Plan Tactic for Learning: Technology to Transform Learning. Computer Science Specialist Marnie Roush and Educational Technology Director MaryBeth Bazzanella oversee the initiative.
