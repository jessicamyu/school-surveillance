PRESENTING STAFF:

Esther Valdez, principal, Rose Stein International

Susie Van Scoyk, principal, Alameda International Jr./Sr.

Terry Elliott, chief school effectiveness officer

 

PURPOSE:

To provide an update on the progress of the implementation of the Alameda Articulation Area Plan.

             

BACKGROUND:

Approved in the spring of 2015 for implementation beginning with the 2015-16 school year, the Alameda Area Plan utilizes the International Baccalaureate (IB) Middle Years Program (MYP) for all 7th through 10th grade students, the IB Career and Diploma program as an option for 11th and 12th grade students.  Additionally, the articulation area offers a two-way dual language program at Emory Elementary and the IB Primary Years Program (PYP) at both Patterson Elementary and Rose Stein Elementary (to open Fall 2017).   

The Board of Education requested an update on the progress of student achievement and development for Alameda International Jr./Sr. High School as well as progress on the re-opening of the Rose Stein International Elementary program.

PRESENTING STAFF:

Kathleen Askelson, chief financial officer

Stephanie Corbo, director of finance

Independent Auditor, CliftonAllenLarson, LLP

 

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to receive information on the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR) and external audit for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2016.   


BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education monitors the districtwide condition as outlined in Board executive limitation policies EL-6, Financial Administration and as monitoring for EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting. 

 

Colorado Revised Statute (C.R.S.) 29-1-606 (1)(b) requires that the Comprehensive Annual Financial Report be delivered to the Board of Education by November 30, 2016.  The Board received a copy prior to November 30, 2016. 

 

Colorado revised Statute 22-32-109 requires the district prepare a comprehensive audited financial report.  The financial report consists of financial information prepared by the district and audited by an independent firm and indicates the financial status of the district at the end of the reporting period.  It also provides a starting point for the annual budget preparation process.

Recommended Action
to take under study the proposed five year renewal contracts with Collegiate Academy of Colorado and Mountain Phoenix Community School including the requests for district and state waivers of law and policy.

estimated time: 7:20 p.m. (estimated duration: 5 minutes)

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-13, Charter Schools Application and Monitoring, the superintendent shall not allow charter school applications to be recommended if fiscal jeopardy or failure to make consistent progress towards their stated objectives is a likely outcome or is evident.
     
  2. The charter school contracts for Collegiate Academy of Colorado and Mountain Phoenix Community School expire on June 30, 2017.
     
  3. District regulation, LBD-R, Charter Schools, states charter schools must submit applications for renewal. Collegiate Academy of Colorado and Mountain Phoenix Community School each submitted renewal applications by December 1, 2016.                                                                                                                   
  4. In accordance with district regulation, LBD-R, Charter Schools, the Board of Education must rule on applications to renew no later than February 1, or a mutually agreed upon alternative date.  The Board of Education is scheduled to make a decision on the renewal applications at its February 9, 2017 regular business meeting.
     
  5. ​Materials regarding the evaluation of the renewal applications by district staff will be provided to the Board of Education prior to the February 9, 2017 meeting.
