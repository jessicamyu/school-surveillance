Information

estimated time:  6:35 pm  (estimated duration:  45 minutes)

 

 

To ensure that community members have an opportunity to communicate with the board, the public comment portion of the Tuesday, May 27 special board meeting will have 45 minutes of public comment, and the time limit for individual speakers and groups of speakers will be extended to align with what is stated on the Jeffco Public Schools website.  If more than 25 people sign up to speak to the Board under public comment, we will limit individual speakers to two minutes and groups to five minutes so that the Board can hear from as many citizens as possible.  Sign-up will be on a first-come, first-served basis. The sign-up form will open at 8 a.m. on Friday, May 23 and will remain open until 3:30 p.m. on Tuesday, May 27.

 

It is best to plan your presentation in advance, have copies of any written materials to distribute to the Board, and make your presentation as clear and concise as possible.  

 

Sign up online here after 8:00 am on Friday, May 23 to speak to the Board of Education.   

 

 

Thank you.

Recommended Action
To hire Daniel McMinimee to be superintendent of Jeffco Public Schools.

estimated time 7:25 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)

 

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. On January 9, 2014, the Board of Education began its process to find a new superintendent for Jeffco Public Schools with the announced retirement of Dr. Cynthia Stevenson.
     
  2. On February 27, the Board secured the services of Ray and Associates to conduct the executive search with began with community input on the characteristics sought in a superintendent through an online survey and community meetings during the month of March.
     
  3. In April, the Board finalized the superintendent profile and accepted applicants through the deadline of April 25, 2014.
     
  4. At the meetings of May 5, 9 and 10, 2014 the Board of Education narrowed its search to one finalist, Daniel McMinimee.  Mr. McMinimee was announced publicly on May 10, 2014.
     
  5. The law requires that the finalist or finalists be made public fourteen days prior to appointing or employing one of the finalists.
     
  6. On May 7, 2014 the preliminary Terms of Offer were communicated to the Board.
     
  7. District staff and district legal counsel have drafted a proposed contract for review and discussion.
     
  8. The finalized contract is planned to be on the June 5 regular Board of Education meeting agenda for approval.
