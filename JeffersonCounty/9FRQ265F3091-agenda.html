3.02 Public Comment (Agenda Related)
Meeting
Mar 6, 2014 - Regular Meeting
Category
3. Public Agenda Part One
Type
Information

estimated time: 6:35 p.m.  (estimated duration: 45 minutes)

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.  

At this time, in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda and any Part Two comments for up to 45 minutes. After 45 minutes any remaining Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to 45 minutes if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

4.09 Benefit Plan Renewals and Contract Approvals for 2014/2015 (EL-3)
Meeting
Mar 6, 2014 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to authorize district staff to finalize the benefit programs for 2014/2015; and, to authorize the chief financial officer to sign and approve agreements.
Goals
EL-3, Staff Treatment

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy, EL-3, Staff Treatment, "With respect to employment, compensation and benefits for employees, consultants and contract workers, the superintendent shall not cause or allow jeopardy to the fiscal integrity or public image of the district..."
     
  2. Proposals for the Employee Benefit Plans for 2014/2015 were evaluated by the Benefits Advisory Committee. The Committee recommends the following medical, dental, vision, life, and disability contracts, vendors, and plans. 
    • Negotiations with Kaiser and United Health Care (both existing vendors) have resulted in the following recommendations for the 2014/2015 plan offerings and budget:
      • Kaiser High-Deductible Health Plan will experience a 3.92% increase and the Kaiser Deductible HMO Plan a 4.99% increase.  Expected annual cost of total premiums $53,997,450.*
      • Plan design changes due to the Affordable Care Act will now require that the deductible and co-payment amounts (other than prescription drug co-payments in this next plan year) be included in the out-of-pocket maximum.  Therefore, the out-of-pocket maximum will be increased to accommodate this change.  There will be little net effect on most participants as a result of this change. 
      • The deductible carry-over provision will be eliminated from the Kaiser Deductible HMO Plan.
      • United Healthcare will experience an increase of 5.95%.  Expected annual cost of total premiums $5,810,450.*
      • In order to hold down the amount of the premium increase, the co-insurance on the plan will be raised from 10% to 15%.
      • It is recommended that we continue with the same medical plan providers, rather than spend dollars performing an expensive vendor search that is not likely to result in cost savings and may, in fact, disrupt a portion of the employee population if the current, non-Kaiser vendor was to determine they no longer want to operate alongside the substantial population covered by the Kaiser contract.
                  
  3. Delta Dental is the third-party administrator responsible for adjudicating and paying dental claims.  Per the last accepted RFP agreement, the district will see an expected increase in administrative fees of .20 per employee per month.  The expected annual contract amount for admin fees alone is $248,150.*
    • The Benefits Advisory Committee (BAC) has recommended increasing the annual maximum and life-time maximum for orthodontia treatment for both plans by $250 each; to add coverage for dental implants at the major category payment percentage and to cover "other" basic services at the basic category payment percentage. These changes are recommended in order to better match other district's benefits, recognizing that such limits need to be reviewed on a periodic basis to ensure that coverage remains competitive in the marketplace.
    • Benefits premiums for dental coverage will be held at the current level. The dental plan has sufficient reserves to accommodate the enhanced benefits; a spend-down of some of the reserves is anticipated and appropriate.
       
  4. EyeMed is the third-party administrator responsible for adjudicating and paying vision claims.  We are approaching the last year of a 3-year rate guarantee.  With no increase in administrative fees the expected annual contract amount for admin fees alone is $43,200.* 
    • There are no proposed design changes to the vision plan. 
       
  5. No changes are proposed to the current district contribution toward the cost of benefits for the 2014/2015 plan year. This amount has not increased since 2003. 
     
  6. Standard is the insurance carrier for basic life, accidental death and dismemberment, voluntary life insurances (paid by employees), short and long-term disability insurance and a grandfathered minimal retiree life insurance benefit.  The vendor is holding the rates for the 2014/2015 plan year.  Total expected contract cost for the insured benefits and administrative fees, is $3,454,650.*
    • Estimated claims cost for the $2,000 retiree life insurance policy are estimated at $150,000 and are in addition to the amount stated above.  These claims are a straight pass-through to the district.
    • There are no design changes currently proposed for the life or disability benefits. 

 

*Annual costs are projected based on current enrollment.  Actual costs may vary based on enrollment shifts which may occur during the annual enrollment period; or increased or decreases in staffing levels, or for salary-based benefits, a salary increase.

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In 2008 the Board of Education approved a loan of $93,000 to Mountain Phoenix Community School to be paid back in three payments beginning in 2009.  In 2009 Mountain Phoenix Community School asked that the first payment be delayed by one year and that the loan be paid over five years.  The Board agreed to those changes.  Currently the school still owes the school district $19,800 that is due this year to complete payment of the original loan.

  3.   4. Mountain Phoenix requests that the Board of Education delay the final payment of $19,800 due this year from the previous loan and extend their loan by $230,200 to meet obligations to Himmelman Construction and to cover the projected shortfall in operating funds for a total loan of $250,000.  The school has submitted a repayment plan for five years that is attached below.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment section on its agenda.    Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.

At this time, in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person.

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
