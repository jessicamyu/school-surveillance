4.03 Board of Education Appreciation
Meeting
Jan 9, 2020 - Regular Meeting
Category
4. Honors, Recognition and School Reports
Type
Recognition

estimated time: 6:04 p.m. (estimated duration: 2 minutes) 
 

January is School Board Recognition Month, and mid-school year is a great time to honor the work of Jeffco Public Schools’ five school board members (Susan Harmon, Brad Rupert, Ron Mitchell, Susan Miller and Stephanie Schooley), sharing thanks for their contributions to our schools and community, and educating the public about the important role that a school board plays, not only in Jeffco Public Schools, but in Colorado, a local control state.

Jeffco’s current five member school board has been in place since December when two members, Stephanie Schooley/District 3 and Susan Miller/District 4, took their oath of office for a four year term. 
 

Colorado school board members are volunteers, carving time out of their work and family life to provide the community’s perspective on legal and leadership decisions affecting students in Jefferson County. Board member activities include visits to Jeffco’s 155 schools; participating in school celebrations of state championships, academic honors, and groundbreakings; speaking on behalf of the district at community town halls, parent meetings, service clubs, city council meetings, and other social engagements. All that plus preparing and participating in board meetings! As the second largest school district in Colorado with 9% of all Colorado students, school board members also take a leadership role statewide through the Colorado Association of School Boards, dialogue with state and federal legislators, and other school board members across the state in an effort to improve and promote the value of public education to the success of Colorado and our nation.
 

Most of all, school board members work with district staff to represent the community’s focus on strategic goals; to close opportunity gaps and ensure all students find engagement and success in each day of their learning in Jeffco Public Schools.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda. Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education.   (will be available 10 am Monday)  
 

At this time in Part One of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics related to items on this evening's agenda for up to one hour.  Part Two comments will be heard at the end of the meeting under Part Two Public Comment.  Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.  If more than 20 individuals sign up to speak to the Board, individuals will have two minutes and groups will have five minutes for a collective message to the Board.

The Board will listen to Part One Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Board members welcome Part Two Public Comments after the business items are completed.  Part Two Public Comment addresses topics unrelated to this evening's regular business. Thank you.

7.05 Limited Waiver of Policy: Localworks Ridge at 38 Criterium, Brewfest, and Ridgefest (EL-7)
Meeting
Jan 9, 2020 - Regular Meeting
Category
7. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
Recommended Action
to approve the request by Localworks for a limited waiver of district policy allowing for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages at the 2020 Ridge at 38 Criterium and Brewfest on Sunday, June 7; and, the 2020 Ridgefest on Saturday, September 12, in accordance with the MOU between Jeffco Public Schools and the City of Wheat Ridge, on the south lawn and parking lot at Stevens Elementary School.
Goals
EL-7, Asset Protection

PERTINENT FACTS:

  1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, "the superintendent shall not allow assets of the district to be unprotected, inadequately maintained, inappropriately used or unnecessarily risked…."
     
  2. Localworks is presenting the Ridge on 38 Criterium and Brewfest, in its seventh year and growing, is a state championship level Rocky Mountain Road Cup race sanctioned by the Bicycle Racing Association of Colorado (BRAC) and USA Cycling. The event is one of the few races in Colorado that awards equal prize money to both men and women. A criterium is a short, fast, 6-corner, 1-mile course that takes place on city streets and ensures a lot of action and excitement for spectators and athletes. The Brewfest and vendor market were added five years ago to this family friendly event that includes music, food trucks and a children’s area with face painters and kids crafts. The event will be held on Sunday, June 7, 2020. The racing event begins at 9 a.m., the children's area runs from 11 a.m. to 5 p.m., and the vendor area and Brewfest run from noon to 6 p.m.
     
  3. The Localworks Ridge at 38 Ridgefest event on Saturday, September 12, 2020, will be held on the south lawn and parking lot at the Stevens Elementary school site. The free, family-friendly event is an opportunity for the community to celebrate the heritage of Wheat Ridge and Ridge at 38. The event aims to satisfy enthusiasts of all kinds through diverse offerings like a classic car show, an Artisan Marketplace, the Chalk Art Festival, live bluegrass music, a children’s area with field day games and other community activities.
     
  4. Both events are intended to promote and encourage community spirit and citizen participation within Wheat Ridge. Both are non-district sponsored events. 
     
  5. In 2013, Jeffco Public Schools and the City of Wheat Ridge agreed in a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to allow for the use of the south lawn and the south parking lot of the Stevens Elementary school site. The MOU allows the City of Wheat Ridge to serve, sell, possess and distribute alcoholic beverages on the premises subject to Board of Education (BOE) approval and mutually agreed to terms and conditions for community events and district policy.
     
  6. In accordance with the MOU, Localworks is requesting a limited waiver of district policy and regulation GBEC, Drug Free Workplace and KF-R, Community Use of School Facilities, to allow for the sale and consumption of alcohol on school grounds during both events.
     
  7. Localworks, the event sponsoring organization, under the auspices of the City of Wheat Ridge, is a community-based non-profit corporation with a mission of advancing Wheat Ridge as a vibrant and sustainable community. The City's "Repositioning Wheat Ridge" Plan informs the work of Localworks to encourage appropriate activity and development necessary to retain and attract strong Jefferson County and Denver metro area households to create a community that will fill an important niche in the community.
     
  8. Localworks staff and volunteers are Colorado TIPS Alcohol trained in awareness and prevention of intoxication, identification of fake IDs and development of people skills for customer intervention. They have also participated in training through the Wheat Ridge Police Department (WRPD) with respect to public drunkenness. The WRPD will be at both events with additional security personnel at the Criterium and Brewfest, to monitor exits and entrances. Localworks works closely with Wheat Ridge Liquor Enforcement to ensure compliance with laws for both events.
     
  9. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant the limited waiver of district policy GBEC with respect only to the prohibited use and possession of alcoholic beverages for these events. The policy prohibits the unlawful possession and use of alcohol on district premises. It also states that, “...an employee knowingly in possession of, or under the influence of alcohol or any controlled substance shall be suspended immediately by his principal or supervisor if such use or possession is on any district property at any time.” Approval of the limited waiver at this time will allow Localworks to finalize details of the event and to begin promoting these events to the community.
     
  10. District administration requests that the Board of Education grant a limited waiver of district policy regulation KF-R with respect only to the prohibited use and possession alcoholic beverages for this event. This policy states that, “...the use and possession of alcoholic beverages or drugs by any person or group on district property is strictly prohibited.”
     
  11. Localworks will have a Special Events liability insurance policy covering the district and other entities supporting this event, and the City of Wheat Ridge will provide police/fire protection for the event.
     
  12. Building and Facility Use forms have been completed and approved and a copy of the approved permit will be posted during the event. The Board of Education has approved similar limited waiver of district policy to allow for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages on district property 13 times since 2009.

  3. Use of the Jeffco Stadium and surrounding Jeffco facilities has been requested and approved through the Jeffco Facility Use Permit process, and a copy of the approved permit will be posted during the event.
     
  4. The Board of Education approved a similar request for limited waiver of district policy to allow for aerial fireworks and the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages on district property for this same event beginning in 2018.

  6. Jeffco Public Schools has a history of working with the City of Lakewood on similar events; however, this is a non-district sponsored event.
     
  7. The proposed free July 4, 2020, fireworks evening event includes an area for kids’ lawn games, beer garden featuring local Lakewood breweries managed by a nonprofit organization and poured by TIPS certified servers, food trucks on the perimeter (on asphalt around east bleachers), DJ and a 20-minute fireworks show choreographed to music.
     
  8. City of Lakewood will acquire all necessary permits, including Jeffco Health and West Metro Fire Special Event and Fireworks permits. The beer garden will be part of a larger program used at all Lakewood community events whereby the nonprofit organization will pull the permits and sell the tickets, and brewery vendor staff will pour the alcohol.

City of Lakewood will provide staffing, security, entrance monitors, parking attendants, crowd control, additional portable restrooms, additional water stations, cleaning staff for trash collection, dumpsters, two jersey barriers at the north end of the stadium, temporary fencing and security for crowd safety around the fireworks launch vicinity, set up and tear down the day before and after the event, and general event management. West Metro Fire will provide brush truck with three firefighters, four medics and an ambulance. Lakewood Police will provide security and traffic control; at a minimum of 12 officers (at least one posted at each event entrance), including undercover staff, traffic control and sergeants. Firestorm Pyrotechnics, Inc. will be hired through the City of Lakewood (details of the fireworks show and proposed launch sites are available).
     
  10. As per district policy KF, Community Use of School Facilities, this event promotes community and business partnerships with stakeholders by making district buildings and facilities available to the community when not in use for school activities. The use of the Jeffco Stadium and granting of a limited waiver to district policy allows for a potential annual community event that celebrates and promotes community spirit and citizen engagement.
     
  11. District Risk Management works with the City of Lakewood to assure appropriate insurance is in place for the event. Lakewood will have a Special Events liability insurance policy covering the district and other entities supporting this effort. Lakewood vendors supporting the event will also have properly structured insurance coverage protecting the district.

The Board listens to the community’s issues of concern in two Public Comment sections on its agenda.  Sign up online here to speak to the Board of Education. (will be available 10 am Monday)

 

At this time in Part Two of Public Comment, the Board listens to concerns on topics unrelated to items on this evening's agenda. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. We listen with respect and ask those who address the Board to do the same. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. Any resident or staff member of the district who has completed the requested information on the public comment roster may speak. There is a three minute time limit per person. Additional guidelines for public comment. 

 

If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic then the Board will allow 10 minutes for that group's view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme.

The Board will listen to Part Two Public Comment for up to one hour if necessary and, by a majority vote, may extend the time for high volume meetings.  Thank you.
