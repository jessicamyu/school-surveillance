PRESENTING STAFF:
Cindy Stevenson, superintendent
Ed Bowditch, contract lobbyist, Tomlinson & Associates

PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to discuss pending legislation and to provide direction to the contract lobbyist for the 2010 legislative session.

BACKGROUND:
Board executive limitation policy EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, requires that the Board's positions on pending legislation and regulations be communicated to members of the Colorado General Assembly and the Executive Branch of state government. It has been the Board's practice to schedule special meetings to discuss pending legislation during the legislative session.

The 2010 legislative session will produce a number of bills of interest to Jefferson County Public Schools. The district contract lobbyist will bring legislation to the Board of Education for positions and direction. The district’s contract lobbyist provides state legislators with the Board’s positions on legislation.

2.04 Supplemental Funds - Colorado Health Foundation to School Medicaid (EL-6)
Meeting
Feb 4, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
2. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A and C.
    1. School Medicaid Program received $147,621 from the Colorado Health Foundation to be used over a two-year period. This program aligns with strategic plan community objective 2, which ensures staff, community, and parent/family involvement focuses on increased student achievement.
    2. School Medicaid’s funds from the Colorado Department of Health Care Policy and Finance (HCPF), awarded in September 2009, were cut through state budget reductions. These funds will replace the HCPF funds for a period of two years. These funds are used to provide staff for application assistance and eligibility determinations for Medicaid or Child Health Plan Plus (CHP+) applicants who have children attending Jeffco Schools. The goal is to provide Medicaid/CHP+ healthcare coverage to as many uninsured children as possible. School Medicaid will partner with Food Services to obtain names of families who qualify for free and reduced lunch rates. These families will be contacted to offer Medicaid/CHP+ application assistance.
    3. The program will be measured by collecting data such as number of applications taken, number of children qualified, number of families contacted, number accepting application assistance, number of families who denied assistance.
    4. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.
    5. Linda Buzard, director of Health Services, will manage the project.

PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A and C.
    1. Family Literacy received $48,184 from the Rose Community Foundation. The program aligns with strategic plan community objective 2, which ensures staff, community, and parent/family involvement focuses on increased student achievement.

    2. The funds are used to support the HIPPY (Home Instruction for Parents of Preschool Youngsters) Program. HIPPY receives grant funds from a variety of funders, including Colorado Parent & Child Foundation, AmeriCorp, Daniels Funds, Buell Foundation, and Title I to support their total operating budget of $370,502. Supporting the basic tenet from the National Center for Family Literacy, the program increases the basic skills and attitudes of low-income parents towards education, parenting skills, children’s literacy, school readiness, and the overall quality of the parent-child relationship. The funds provide salaries and mileage for HIPPY assistants to make weekly home visits to 250 families/271 children. Families also attend monthly family meetings held at Title I schools to provide enrichment activities to support student academic success.
    3. The effectiveness of the program is evaluated by weekly conversations with parents during weekly home visits, home visitation reports/time logs, pre and post parent surveys, parent sign-in sheets for family meetings at parent functions, and anecdotal reports from participating kindergarten teachers.
    4. The district will not be required to continue this project after funding ends.
    5. Gwen Welch, Family Literacy director, is the project manager.

The Board of Education is pleased to honor Michelle Garland, secondary science curriculum specialist in the Department for Learning and Educational Achievement, for her work on Kendall Hunt's innovative middle school science program, Middle School Life Science textbook which won the People's Choice Award at the recent 2009 Chicago Book & Media Show. The annual event honors excellence in the planning, supervision, and execution of the physical and visual aspects of publishing media, encouraging excellence in publishing by providing a platform for educational, social and professional interaction.

Middle School Life Science is a full-year middle school program emphasizing depth rather than breadth, concentrating on fundamental concepts in greater depth for better understanding. Organized around a three-step learning cycle, concepts are introduced with hands-on learning experiences, and then developed through discussions, mini-lectures, and/or readings.

At this time the Board listens to the community’s issues of concern. Board members or staff will respond during this meeting or at a later time. In order to respect all persons addressing the Board, we request that the audience not applaud individual speakers and that issues, rather than personalities, be addressed. If there are a number of speakers commenting on the same topic this evening, we ask that remarks be consolidated and new thoughts or concepts shared. It is not the number of people who address the Board that leads to better decision-making, rather the content of your presentation. So, we respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message of an earlier speaker. If there are four or more people who wish to address the Board on the same side of the same topic, the Board will allow 10 minutes for that view to be heard. All those who sign up must accompany the speaker to the front of the Board room. We respectfully ask that you do not repeat the same message, but rather present varying concepts on the same theme. Thank you.

5.01 Purchase Agreement: Solar Energy Partnership (EL-8)
Meeting
Feb 4, 2010 - Regular Business Meeting, 6 p.m., Board Room
Category
5. Discussion Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1.Jeffco Public Schools currently purchases electrical power from Xcel Energy and Intermountain Rural Electric Association. For 2008-2009, the districtwide electrical bill was $9.3 million.
2.Various options have been evaluated for renewable energy opportunities but most required a large financial investment.
3.In October 2009, a Request for Proposal (RFP) was issued soliciting responses for any renewable energy alternative that would be cost neutral or better for Jeffco Public Schools, i.e., it would cost less than current or future Xcel or IREA rates for electrical service.
4.A mandatory pre-proposal conference was attended by 33 companies and six responses were received for solar energy panels.
5.Four eligible bids were received: 1) Bella-Conergy; 2) CEI Roofing LLC; 3) Oak Leaf Energy Partners; and, 4) Solar Power Partners.
6.The bid evaluation team included representatives from the departments of Construction Management, Facilities Planning and Design, Facilities Management, Financial Services, and Purchasing.
7.Administrative staff received and evaluated four solar energy proposals that met the minimal mandatory and submittal requirements.
8.The proposals required solar panels be installed on nearly 30 school building roofs and a Purchase Power Agreement for up to 20 years.
9.Each proposal was evaluated based on their management summary; supplier profile; pricing; technical summary; equipment offering; and, schedule and implementation plan.
10.Administrative staff recommends the establishment of a partnership with one vendor: CEI Roofing, LLC.
11.Funding to purchase the solar-generated electricity is included in the annual operating budget.
12.The project is projected to save approximately $1 million over the 20-year term.
