PURPOSE:
For the Board of Education to review the resolutions directing the efforts of the CASB during the 2018 legislative session and which provide guidance to the Board's selected delegate to the Fall Conference and Delegate Assembly on October 13-14, 2017 at the Denver Marriott South at Park Meadows.

BACKGROUND:
The Colorado Association of School Boards (CASB) holds its annual Fall Conference and Delegate Assembly in October in preparation for legislative work in Colorado on public education. There are 24 proposed resolutions for the 2018 legislative session as well as standing resolutions on local government, finance and student achievement. 

At the Delegate Assembly, positions are taken on resolutions which affect state school districts.  As one of the largest school districts in the state, the work of CASB may impact the district's work.

2.02 Briefing on DeAngelis Center (EL-11)
Meeting
Sep 21, 2017 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Information

estimated time: 5:45 p.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes)
 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Jason E. Glass, superintendent

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

John McDonald, executive director, Safety, Security and Emergency Management

 

PURPOSE:

To provide the Board of Education with an update on the status of the Frank DeAngelis Center for Community Safety.

 

BACKGROUND:

In June 2011, the Board of Education closed Wheat Ridge’s Martensen Elementary as part of the 2011 Facilities Master Plan. The school site has since been transformed into the first of its kind in the country: a training facility for local law enforcement and other first responders preparing for active shooter situations and crisis prevention in a school environment.

The district currently offers the site at no cost to its local law enforcement partners and wants to share plans to enhance the center’s financial situation with the Board of Education.
