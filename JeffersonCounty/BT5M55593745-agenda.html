PRESENTING STAFF

Steve Bell, chief operating officer

Tim Reed, executive director, Facilities & Construction Management

Gordon Callahan, member, Capital Asset Advisory Committee and president, Callahan Construction

Jeff Wilhite, member, CAAC

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board to receive the quarterly report on the 2018 Capital Improvement Program to ensure accountability and transparency in the current status of the program by project covering financial status including contract awards and disbursed funds; and, projects planned to begin in the 2021 calendar year.

 

BACKGROUND:

In accord with Board executive limitation policies EL-8, Facilities, the superintendent may not “permit capital dollars to be spent unnecessarily or unwisely…” and EL-11, Communication and Counsel to the Board, "the superintendent may not fail to advise the Board in a timely manner of relevant trends, facts and information…”

 

The Board of Education’s citizen advisory Capital Asset Advisory Committee receives monthly reviews of the activities and status of the 2018 Capital Improvement Program. One or more members of the CAAC will join staff in reporting to the Board. The third quarter report to the Board of Education will focus on the current status of Capital Improvement Program projects anticipated to be completed by the start of the next school year (2020/21) or beyond, the financial status including contract awards and disbursed funds; and, projects planned to begin in the 2021 calendar year.

2.03 Fourth Quarter 2019/2020 Financial Report Review and Dialogue with Financial Oversight Committee (EL-5, 6)
Meeting
Oct 7, 2020 - Special Meeting-Study/Dialogue Session
Category
2. Study/Dialogue Session
Type
Discussion
Goals
EL-6, Financial Administration
EL-5, Financial Planning/Budgeting

estimated time: 9:00 a.m. (estimated duration: 30 minutes) 

 

PRESENTING STAFF:

Nicole Stewart, interim chief financial officer

Mary Cooper, director of finance

Charlotte Franson, member, Financial Oversight Committee (FOC)

 

PURPOSE:

For the Board of Education to hear from staff on the districtwide financial condition through the fourth quarter of the 2019/2020 school year in order to monitor district finances.

 

BACKGROUND:

Each quarter, in compliance with Board executive limitation policies EL-5, Financial Planning and Budgeting, and EL-6, Financial Administration, and as outlined in Board governance process policy GP-13, Committee Structure, the Board meets to review and monitor quarterly financial reports. Staff and an independent auditor from CliftonLarsonAllen, LLP,  identify the funds, departments and/or schools to monitor closely throughout the year. Members of the Financial Oversight Committee attend quarterly financial updates for the opportunity to provide a report to and dialogue with the Board of Education.
