3.10 Supplemental Funds: NCLB/ARRA Plan to CDE (EL-6)
Meeting
Jun 11, 2009 - Special Meeting, 5:30 p.m.
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:

In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-6, Financial Administration, the administration will bring grants to the Board of Education for approval when:
    A. Large amounts of funds are coming to the district (i.e., Title I, VI, etc.)
    B. Obligations are long term (i.e., funding for multiple years, or the district is asked to continue the program after funding ends), or
    C. Grants are for a dollar amount over $10,000.

This grant falls under Items A and C.
    1. The Board is hereby notified that the district is submitting the consolidated application to receive its share of federal and state NCLB (No Child Left Behind) funds from The Colorado Department of Education. Preliminary federal allocations total $15,232,882. American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) allocations total $9,587,301. The district will use these funds to provide best developmental and instructional programs in compliance with NCLB mandates. The programs align with strategic plan business and finance objective 1, which ensures the alignment of resources to increase student achievement and organizational effectiveness.

No Child Left Behind Act of 2001 Programs include:
    § Title I, Part A (Improving the Academic Achievement of the Disadvantaged) $11,783,060 plus ARRA funds of $9,309,832 = $21,092,892
    Funds are used to close the achievement gap of disadvantaged students by accelerating learning in literacy and math. The district will provide professional development opportunities to all Title I building administrators, teachers and instructional paraprofessionals on aligning instruction to the district’s prioritized benchmarks, essential learning, assessments and site-based resources for all students within the core in reading, math, science, and social studies. With the assistance of School Improvement Specialists, all Title I schools will create meaningful school improvement plans based on data. Teachers in Title I schools will increase their capacity for delivering quality instruction as a result of the Specialist supervising the Instructional Coaches in Title schools. Title I funds will increase the number of teachers who are trained in ESL (English as a Second Language) strategies. Additional staff will supplement services for the district ELLs (English Language Learners) to enhance and expand the frequency and availability of high-quality professional development opportunities for teachers and administrators. To be in compliance with federal guidelines/regulations defining Parent Involvement, a .5 Parent Involvement Coordinator will assist schools to increase awareness of Title I and its importance to the success of students and the school. Funds will be set aside for the supervision and coordination of educational services to homeless students in non-Title I and Title I schools. Funds will be distributed directly to Title I schools to increase the academic performance of all students of color and poverty in reading and math at all grade levels.

    § Title II, Part A (Preparing, Training and Recruiting High Quality Teachers and Principals) $2,545,329
    Funds are used to increase student academic achievement by increasing the number of highly qualified teachers and principals. Instructional Coaches will provide ongoing site-based staff development to support teachers to be highly skilled in the teaching of reading, writing, and math. The district will provide technical assistance by conducting school reviews to those schools on academic watch, on probation, or rated low on the SAR (School Accountability Report). Title II funds will provide assistance to schools and teachers on implementing best strategies for gifted students. A plan will be developed to assess the highly qualified status of special education teachers. A portion of these funds will be distributed to private and charter schools to meet their needs.

    § Title II, Part D (Enhancing Education Through Technology) $109,674 plus ARRA funds of $277,469 = $387,143
    Funds support student achievement through use of technology in schools. These funds will be used for salaries for two professional development specialists to facilitate on-going and sustained professional development to build capacity for Jeffco’s Education Technology/Information Literacy Plan. Funds will provide substitutes, additional pay and registration fees for staff to participate in the professional development.
    § Title III (Language Instruction for Limited English Proficient and Immigrant Students) $582,209
    Funds focus existing programs on teaching English to English Language Learners (ELL) children, including immigrant children and youth, and holding states accountable for their ELL students attaining English. With Title III funds, Jeffco will provide professional development for all K-12 English as a Second Language (ESL) teachers, instructional coaches, ESL resource teachers and administration. Summer school programs will provide ELLs extended learning opportunities and extra tutorial services that have a positive impact on student achievement. The programs are designed for “acceleration” not “remediation.” Funds will be used to provide translation services to schools. A minimum of 200 classroom teachers will be able to enroll in graduate-level ESL endorsement courses developed through a partnership with the University of Colorado at Denver. Title III funds are used to expand and supplement this partnership by adding additional instructors/courses, which allow teachers to pay reduced tuition costs.
    § Title IV (Safe and Drug Free Schools) $212,610
    These funds supports programs that prevent violence in and around schools and prevent the illegal use of alcohol, tobacco, and drugs in order to foster a safe and drug- free learning environment that supports student academic achievement. Title IV funds will provide a team of qualified, knowledgeable staff to support the multi-faceted and comprehensive approach in order to maintain a safe school environment. Staff will collaborate with other departments and agencies to secure the necessary resources to support youth in healthy lifestyle development, setting forth clear behavioral expectations through district policy, advocacy for parental involvement, adopting evidence-based practices, planning for active youth participation, and support of interagency collaboration in ways that can measure the outcomes of our efforts.
    2. Each federal program manager will evaluate the success of their program using a variety of methods, including surveys, test scores, etc.

3.11 Supplemental Materials: Scholastic Read 180 Secondary Reading Intervention Program (EL-7)
Meeting
Jun 11, 2009 - Special Meeting, 5:30 p.m.
Category
3. Consent Agenda
Type
Action
PERTINENT FACTS:
1. In accordance with Board executive limitation policy EL-7, Asset Protection, and district policy DJB, Purchasing Procedures, expenditures of $250,000 and above must be approved by the Board of Education.
2.The Division of Instruction Secondary Literacy Committee conducted a two year study of supplemental reading materials for struggling secondary students who were reading two years or more below their grade level. Scholastic Read 180 Program was selected.
3.During the 2008/2009 school year, four middle schools and three high schools implemented the Read 180 Reading Program. The implementation included professional development for teachers and instructional coaches and involvement with principals through classroom walkthroughs and collegial conversations.
4. Mid-year data indicated that 45% of the students had over one year of reading growth. Of those students 18% had over two years of reading growth. All schools had a mean growth rate of approximately one year of reading growth by January 2009.
5.Growth data in June 2009 indicated that 78% of the students had 1 or more years of reading growth and 52% had two or more years of reading growth. Twenty-one students achieved reading gains of 3½ - 4 years in one year.
6.These promising results led district leadership to the decision to provide Read 180 in all but two Jeffco secondary schools at an approximate cost of $1,094,000. Funding will come from the district’s general fund.
