Student Pledge Leader: Boy Scout Troop 70

 

 

 

Character Trait: Community Service

 

Tonight, we have Boy Scout Troop 70 in attendance. The troop was initiated in 1989 by Bill Tyler, and is led by Scout Master Ronnie Murphy. To date, they have awarded 55 Eagle Scouts! The two newest Eagle Scouts are here tonight with their parents: Christian Kent and Kelvin Rattley, Jr.

 

Kelvin will lead us in our Pledge of Allegiance. He is a 10th-grader at Phillip O. Berry Academy where he is a member of the marching band and jazz band, and engineering academy. Kelvin was recently selected as next year’s drum major for the Force of Phillip Marching Band. He is a member of Greenville Memorial AME Zion Church and works part-time as a lifeguard. Kelvin is joined tonight by his parents, Kelvin and Kondra Rattley, and Phillip O. Berry principal, Mrs. Terra Kennedy.

Due to several technical issues in Lawson and payroll, the Early College Calendar cannot begin prior to Aug. 1. The original calendar approved by the Board of Education Feb. 12. 2019, had the Early College beginning the year with teacher workdays on July 30 and July 31. The original calendar has been modified to remove the teacher workdays in July and place them at the end of the school year in May 2020. The revision will provide the Early College with more makeup days, if needed.

Recommend approval of a resolution authorizing the Director of Warehouse Operations to dispose of Surplus Property by way of an online bid in auction format.

 

The Board will be asked to approve the adoption of a resolution for the disposal of surplus school property by way of an online bid in auction format.  The online auction period will encompass June 6-June 25, 2019 period.  GovDeals, www.govdeals.com, an experienced and proven online government surplus sales services provider will facilitate the process.

 

Recommend approval authorizing the Director of Warehouse Operations to dispose of Electronics/AV Surplus School property through "secured disposal" by way of private negotiation and sale using Powerhouse Recycling, Inc. certified recycling vendor.  The process will occur during the period of June 6-June 25, 2019.

 

Action necessary, under N.C.G.S. 160-A226-270, to dispose of surplus property.

 

Action necessary, under 160A-274, sale lease, exchange and joint governmental property.

 

These auctions usually generate $500 to $15,000 in revenue.

 

Recommend approval of the adoption of the resolution.
