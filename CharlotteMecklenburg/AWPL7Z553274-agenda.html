3. PUBLIC HEARINGS
Subject
A. First Public Hearing on Policy GCOA, "Teacher Contracts"
Meeting
Apr 11, 2018 - Regular Board Meeting
Category
3. PUBLIC HEARINGS
Type
Discussion

This new policy is in response to a change in state law that goes into effect with the 2018-2019 school year. Starting with the upcoming school year, school districts across the state will be allowed to offer one, two and four-year contracts to teachers who do not possess career status.

 

This new policy creates the guidelines that CMS will follow to determine how and why teachers will be eligible for the differing contract lengths.

B. Second Public Hearing on Policy BB, "Board Legal Status"
Meeting
Apr 11, 2018 - Regular Board Meeting
Category
3. PUBLIC HEARINGS
Type
Discussion

The recommended change is the elimination of this policy in its entirety. CMS is formulated by state statute and is bound to the rules therein. Hence, we don’t believe it is necessary to have a policy defining our legal status as our legal status is defined in state statute anyway.  

 

This change was considered by the CMS Policy Committee on Feb. 8 and passed by a 4-0 vote.

Subject
H. Recommend Approval to Name the New Albemarle Road Elementary Relief School the Charlotte East Language Academy
Meeting
Apr 11, 2018 - Regular Board Meeting
Category
4. CONSENT ITEMS
Type
Action (Consent)
Preferred Date
Apr 11, 2018

Main Points:

The principal at the Albemarle Road Elementary Relief School has requested that the Board of Education name the new school the Charlotte East Language Academy.

 

The principal convened a school naming committee, as required by CMS Policy FF, "Naming Facilities," and its accompanying regulation, to develop a list of three names for the school, which are now submitted to the Board in order of preference.

 

1. Charlotte East Language Academy

 

2. Esperanza Language Academy

 

3. Gonzalo Mendez Language Academy

 

The Charlotte East Language Academy name honors the area and location of the school. As Charlotte rapidly grows and changes, the city’s east side is quickly becoming an area of excitement and progress. With its location close to downtown, beautiful established neighborhoods, and international and cosmopolitan flair, Charlotte East is a vibrant part of our city. The new school is located in this area and the school hopes it will become an integral part of this community where we can all unite to make our children’s futures better.
