Goodwill Industries of the Southern Piedmont, Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools (CMS), Central Piedmont Community College (CPCC), and The ROC (Rebuilding Opportunities in Construction) have joined together to establish a construction skills training center. The facility will be used to support high school youth during the day in building skillsets related to the construction industry and provide training at night for individuals in our community looking to begin or advance their careers in construction. The training center will be a valuable asset to our community by providing promising career pathways, creating opportunities to advance economic mobility through family sustaining wages, and delivering a much needed solution to the industry.

Q. Recommend Approval of Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) Accountability Insufficient Data Options for Alternative Schools
Meeting
Jun 5, 2018 - Regular Board Meeting
Category
4. CONSENT ITEMS
Type
Action (Consent)
Preferred Date
Jun 05, 2018
Absolute Date
Jun 05, 2018
Fiscal Impact
No
Budgeted
No

Under new Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) requirements, the former Alternative School Accountability Model will no longer exist and all traditional public schools across the state will receive a School Performance Grade.  However, if a school has insufficient data to award a grade, an alternative school can either return their scores to the sending school or use three years’ worth of data to determine a grade. 

 

We recommend that all three CMS alternative schools - Metro School, Lincoln Heights Academy and Turning Point - use three years of data to determine a school performance grade in the event that insufficient data is available.
