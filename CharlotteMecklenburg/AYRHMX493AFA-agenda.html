Town Hall on HB 514 At Huntersville Elementary
In order to share information and respond to questions related to HB514, the Board of Education will host a second town hall on Tuesday, May 15 from 7:00 – 8:30 pm at Huntersville Elementary. Please find a media advisory and invitation letter below and attached – the invitation letter is being sent today to families in the North Mecklenburg community via Connect e-mail. While this town hall is focused on North Meck, anyone from across the county is invited to attend to learn more about HB514.

Dear North Mecklenburg CMS families,


We invite you to join members of the Charlotte-Mecklenburg Board of Education for a discussion of the implications and impact of HB514. As you may know, HB514 is an NCGA legislative proposal to create municipal charter schools funded by local taxing authority. We want to hear directly from you – your thoughts on HB514, on the CMS schools that serve you and any concerns you have about your public schools. It is important that you be able to ask questions, learn more and understand the possible impacts of HB514 for you, your students, our community and public education in North Carolina. We believe that HB514 is not in the best interests of the students and families in Mecklenburg County. 
 

Please join us on Tuesday, May 15, at Huntersville Elementary School, located at 200 Gilead Road, Huntersville NC 28078, for a town hall meeting to discuss HB514. The meeting will begin at 7 p.m. and last until 8:30 p.m. No RSVP is necessary and informational materials will be provided.


We are committed to serving families and students in North Mecklenburg by providing the best schools possible. We value your input and look forward to hearing your opinions and concerns.


Sincerely,
Mary McCray
Chair, Charlotte-Mecklenburg Board of Education
