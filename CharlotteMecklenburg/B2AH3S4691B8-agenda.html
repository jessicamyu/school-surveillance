Budget Source

Recommended Action
Recommend approval of 2018-2019 employee salary increases

 

  • The General Assembly and Governor have approved the 2018-2019 state budget which includes all salary increases effective July 1, 2018.

     

  • Approval of salary increases will provide salary increases as follows:

     

    • Teachers

      • Teachers and certified instructional support receive a step increase plus varying amounts to the steps to be in line with the new 2018-2019 state teacher schedules. Teachers and certified instructional support will also receive an approximately 1% increase to the local supplement percentage. The average increase amount 6.5%.

         

    • School-based administrators

      • Principals receive a 6.9% increase on their state pay as determined by the new principal schedules. Their pay will be reviewed for changes due to EVAAS school growth and ADM in January 2019.

      • Assistant principals receive a step increase on their state and local pay. The new state tables are 19% above the bachelor’s schedule. The average increase is 8.44%.

         

    • All non-certified school and central office staff

      • A 3% increase. This includes bus drivers.

         

    • CMS will raise its minimum hiring to $11.78 per hour to align with the living wage calculator from MIT.

M. Grant Superintendent Authority to Negotiate Lease or Purchase of Davidson IB Property
Meeting
Jul 20, 2018 - Regular Board Meeting
Category
3. CONSENT ITEMS
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
The Charlotte-Mecklenburg Board of Education (Board) authorizes the Superintendent and such staff as he designates, to negotiate an agreement with the Town of Davidson (Davidson) for either the purchase or 40 year lease of the Davidson IB property (Property), subject to the following conditions:
1. The amount paid by Davidson for lease or purchase must be equal to or exceed the fair market value of the Property as determined by a qualified appraiser.
2. The Mecklenburg County Board of Commissioners declines to exercise its right of first refusal.
3. Davidson is able to provide proof that it is able to sufficiently finance the lease or purchase of the Property, as evidenced by the passage of bonds or other means.
4. The Board and Davidson each approves a final agreement at their first regular meeting in August.
5. All other aspects of the lease or purchase of the Property are in accordance with all applicable laws and, except as provided in this motion, Board policy.
