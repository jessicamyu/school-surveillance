Camille Harvell

 

 

 

Camille Harvell is a senior at Butler High School. She serves as the service project coordinator of the Student Government Association, president of the Black Student Union, community service chair for Butler’s DREAM Team, a varsity volleyball player, and a proud board member of NHS, NTHS, Key Club, and HOSA. Over this past summer, Camille volunteered at Mercy hospital as part of Butler’s Academy of Health Sciences. She grew up in the CMS school system, attending Elizabeth Traditional Elementary School and Crestdale Middle School before joining Team Butler. Thanks to encouragement from some amazing teachers at the CMS schools she’s attended, Camille has grown into a well-rounded student that is currently achieving the very high goals she sets for herself. Her teachers have also taught her the importance of respecting others and keeping an open mind about everyone’s opinions. Through the guidance of Camille’s teachers and coaches, she has developed many leadership qualities is grateful for every person that has helped her become a very successful student.

 

 

Camille is accompanied by her Mother Charlena Harvell-Carter, her Principal John LeGrand, Southeast Learning Community Superintendant, Dr. Kondra Rattley and Tracy Hayes, Executive Director of the SoutheastLearning Community.
