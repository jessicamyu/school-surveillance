Recognizing School Social Workers Week-3 13 2018

 

 

 

 

  • Last week, March 4-10, 2018, was National School Social Work Week. We want to take a moment to acknowledge our school social workers.

     

  • Children today are increasingly victims of many social forces that negatively affect them and can hinder academic performance. Many families and communities are in a state of change.

  • Until families are stable, children’s unmet physical and emotional needs can continue to interfere with their ability to learn and succeed in school.

     

  • School social work is a specialized area of practice within the broad field of the social work profession

     

  • School social workers are trained mental health professionals. Here are some of the areas where their work is invaluable: mental health concerns, behavioral issues, positive behavioral

  • support, academic and classroom support. The also consult with teachers, parents and administrators and provide individual and group counseling. They support students experiencing homelessness, neglect and attendance challenges. School social workers bring unique knowledge and skills to the school system and are instrumental in furthering the mission of the schools.

  • Thank you to all CMS social workers!

  • Now we would like to recognize one social worker in particular: Ms. Charlene Davidson.

  • On March 5, Ms. Davidson was named the CMS School Social Worker of the Year. She has been a CMS school social worker since 2001 and serves as one of three lead school social workers.

  • She has recently completed her term as the North Carolina School Social Work Association’s president and continues to serve as a member of that state board.

  • She is a positive, knowledgeable force in her field and a staunch advocate for children, families and her profession.

  • Ms. Davidson, please come forward. [Present certificate]

  • Thank you, Ms. Davidson, and thank you to your colleagues. Your work is helping CMS students every day!
