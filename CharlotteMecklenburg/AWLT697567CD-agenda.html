Approval of Grant of Easement for Sanitary Sewer and Access for Doby Creek Sanitary Sewer Impacting Vance High School and Governor's Village property

The City of Charlotte is requesting a sanitary sewer easement for the purpose of constructing a new parallel replacement gravity sewer line to support the growth in the University area of Charlotte.  Ingress and egress to the schools will not be impeded due to the construction activities. The gravity sewer will be installed underneath University Pointe Blvd and School Access Road via trenchless construction. Those roads are anticipated to remain open through the entirety of construction. Per the City of Charlotte, the majority of the construction on the CMS parcels will take place in the summer of 2018 while school is out.  No work will be performed during final exams. If the gravity isn’t installed while school is out, the contractor will have to finish construction during nights and weekends when school is in session. The entire project is expected to be completed in the summer of 2019, with a 1-year warranty period extending beyond the project completion date.  No financial consideration will be requested from the City.

Recommended Action
Approve the submitted summer program for the summer school in 2017-18, thus allowing the administration of End-of-
Grade (EOG) and End-of-Course (EOC) exams at the conclusion of the summer program.

For the 2017–18 school year, districts may administer End-of-Grade (EOG) and End-of-Course (EOC) tests during summer school. As in previous years, these scores will not be included in growth analyses for school accountability or for educator effectiveness. However, the higher score will be included in proficiency calculations for school accountability within the current accountability year. The 2017–18 accountability year ends on July 6, 2018. Summer school EOG and EOC test scores uploaded to the North Carolina Department of Public Instruction (NCDPI) by July 6, 2018, will be included in 2017–18 accountability calculations. The end of the accountability year is being extended to allow for additional summer program instructional days.

 

As a reminder, no State Board of Education (SBE) policy exists that allows retesting. The previous retesting policy was eliminated in 2010 when the SBE rescinded the Student Gateways at Grades 3, 5, and 8 and the high school Graduation Exit Requirements policies, and there has not been any action to reinstate retesting. Therefore, the inclusion of these test scores in the 2017–18 accountability reports is permitted by the State Board in recognition that summer school is an additional learning opportunity that occurs outside of the regular academic calendar.

 

Student participation is summer school is voluntary. Parents or guardians must make the final decision regarding student’s summer school attendance. Students must be held harmless if they do not participate.  

 

For a school or school district to offer a summer school program that includes an End-of-Grade (EOG) or End-of-Course (EOC) testing component, that summer school program must be approved by the School Board.  We ask that Board review and approve our summer school program so that we may proceed in offering this option to students and families.
