The Request for Qualifications (RFQ) for program management and staff extension services was publicly advertised via Charlotte Observer, NC Interactive Purchasing System, and courtesy emails were sent to firms that expressed an interest.  The interview panel consisted of the following team members:  Gary Adams (Architecture), Greg Bame (Procurement Services), Dennis Dreyer (Bond Oversight Committee - Wells Fargo), Joel Gilland (Bond Oversight Committee - TSP Group, Inc.) Chiquitha Lloyd (Diversity & Inclusion), Dennis Rorie (Facilities Planning & Management) and Carol Stamper (COO).  Five program management firms submitted qualifications.  Three firms were interviewed - CBRE/Heery, CPM, Inc. and LeChase/Jacobs.  Based on the qualification submittal and interviews, the committee recommends LeChase/Jacobs be offered the contract.  The capital program management services contract shall cover individual projects identified in but not limited to the 2017 bond funded capital improvement program as well as any other projects mutually agreed upon by Charlotte-Mecklenburg Board of Education and LeChase/Jacobs.

As part of the 2013 Bond, the Rea Farms Relief School will open for the 2020-21 school year.  The Board of Education will determine, with community and staff input, the school's attendance boundary.  Additionally, the current Lincoln Heights facility will be vacant in August 2019 as the school currently in that facility will move to a new facility at that time.  Allowing a year for renovation, the Lincoln Heights facility will be available for a new program beginning with the 2020-21 school year.
