                                  Kevin Sneed, special projects administrator, Facilities and Transportation Services

                                  Jessica Gillis, director, Facilities Planning Services

 

Meeting Category:  December 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Membership Analysis and Trends

 

School Board Action Required: Information

 

Key Points:

 

Student membership in Fairfax County Public Schools showed a moderate growth this year although slightly smaller than last year. As has previously been communicated, various factors alter year to year membership changes and student membership projections. The two most important factors that influence the overall FCPS membership changes and student membership projections are the birth to kindergarten ratio and net migration. A third factor that alters membership and the student membership projections for individual schools from year to year is student transfers due to program access needs and the student transfer regulation.   

 

Staff has continued to adjust and refine the student membership projection methodology as new data sets are being considered for possible future incorporation. The methodology is based on best practices and includes the use of Geographic Information System (GIS) technology. GIS technology pulls Student Information System (SIS) data by student ID number. It allows for improved sorting, tracking, and mapping of students based on their residence and actual school of attendance. This precision in data analysis and the ability to account for each student in the student membership projection process resulted in better projections performance predicting September 30, 2017 membership, as compared to the performance predicting of September 30, 2016 membership.

 

The Facilities and Transportation Services’ staff have prepared detailed information and tables that show the current conditions as well as comparison information between this year’s and last year’s membership. Information is also presented about the performance of the new student membership projections methodology. The latest five-year projections and projected capacity balances are provided for the Board’s consideration. These projected conditions will lead to the FY 2019-23 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) recommendations which will be proposed on December 14, 2017. A CIP public hearing is scheduled for January 9, 2018. A School Board work session is scheduled for January 22, 2018. The action to vote on the FY 2019-23 Capital Improvement Program is scheduled for January 25, 2018.

Meeting Category:  December 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  FY 2019-2023 Capital Improvement Program

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2019-2023 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five-year student membership projections and capacity calculations, as well as the Capital Construction Cash Flow which identifies plans for new construction, capacity enhancements, renovations, and site acquisition to accommodate future capacity needs.

 

Discussion:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools continues to face a challenging situation whereby student membership (in certain locations) is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities, provide capacity enhancement projects and it will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions and boundary adjustments to accommodate growing enrollment needs.

 

New School Construction: The CIP proposes three new elementary schools: one in the North West County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and the Dulles Airport access road; one to relieve overcrowding in the Fairfax/Oakton area; one near the new Silver Line Metro Innovation Center station. It also proposes construction of a new high school in the Western end of the Silver Line metro vicinity to alleviate capacity concerns at multiple high schools. Also proposed for needs beyond the five year horizon are five new and /or repurposed schools.

 

Capacity Enhancements: Funding for additions at West Potomac High School, Justice High School, and Madison High School are proposed. Funds are also proposed for the relocation of three modular additions.

 

Renovations: Funding for renovations at five named high schools are proposed in the CIP. Additionally, funds for renovations at 21 named elementary schools and 12 unnamed elementary schools are included. Lastly, funds for the renovation of four named middle schools and one unnamed middle school are proposed. Many of these renovation projects will include capacity enhancements.

 

Site Acquisition: Funds have been provided as a placeholder to acquire a school site in the future.

 

The FY 2019–23 CIP also recommends possible solutions for future consideration to resolve capacity deficits. These options include interior modifications, temporary classrooms, program location suggestions, and boundary adjustments. Any boundary adjustment or program change would be discussed and decided through a transparent process that engages the community in accordance with School Board Policies and Regulations.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2019-2023 Capital Improvement Program.

6.02 Grant of Perpetual Street Easement to Virginia Department of Transportation for Public Street Purposes at George C. Marshall High School [FTS; NB 12/7]
Meeting
Dec 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting
Category
6. Consent Agenda - 8:45 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  December 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Grant of Perpetual Street Easement to Virginia Department of Transportation for Public Street Purposes at George C. Marshall High School

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 8330.3 

 

 

Key Points:

 

The Office of Design & Construction has reviewed and found acceptable a proposal for the Virginia Department of Transportation (“VDOT”) to maintain the entirety of George C. Marshall Drive (the “Street”), which runs along the eastern portion of the Marshall High School (the “School”) site.

 

Currently, VDOT maintains most of the Street, beginning at its intersection with Route 7; a portion of the Street (at its opposite terminus) is presently maintained by the adjacent property owner, a condominium association. VDOT’s maintenance of the entire Street as a public roadway would improve its reliability and be advantageous to the School, which uses the Street for additional ingress/egress, student parking, and bus turn-arounds.

 

In order for VDOT to accept the remaining portion of the Street, and thereby become solely responsible for maintenance of the entire Street, the condominium association must perform certain roadway improvements to its portion of the Street to meet current standards. The School Board would grant a Perpetual Street Easement in favor of VDOT for this remaining portion of the Street to accomplish the transfer of future maintenance obligations to VDOT.

 

The Office of Design & Construction has determined that the School Board’s granting the above-referenced Easement is of direct benefit to the School and to the operation of the school system. Per Policy 8330.3(v). no charge would be made by the school system for granting this easement.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board (i) approve the granting of a Perpetual Street Easement to VDOT for the above described property at George C. Marshall High School, subject to VDOT’s approval of roadway improvements to be performed by others; and (ii) authorize the Division Superintendent or the Assistant Superintendent, Department of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other), to take all actions necessary and appropriate in consultation with legal counsel for the school division, to negotiate, execute, deliver and administer the Deed of Easement and any other related documents on behalf of the School Board.

Staff Contact:  Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  December 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  Award of Contract – Carpet and Floor Tile Installation and Replacement Contract

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

 

Related To:  School Board Policy 5011.3 Authority to Contract  

 

 

Key Points:

 

The Office of Facilities Management (OFM) seeks to establish a job order contract, as authorized by the Virginia Public Procurement Act (the “Act”), to provide for small-scale carpet and floor tile installations in FCPS buildings as the need arises. Work would be performed on the basis of unit prices established through the bidding process. The typical, individual task order under this contract would be less than $20,000, and this work is performed during non-school hours, including weekends.

 

Because the aggregate dollar amount paid for services performed in a given year under such a contract is expected to exceed $250,000, Policy 5011.3 requires the School Board to award the contract.

 

Funding for the carpet and floor tile installation and replacement contract is provided from the Major Maintenance Fund, as well as school and department Operating Funds.

 

Four bids for this contract were received on November 21, 2017.  OFM reviewed and evaluated the bids on the basis of unit prices submitted and responsiveness as well as bidder responsibility, as required by the Act.  The low bidder, Carpet Experts, Inc. has performed satisfactorily on previous FCPS contracts, and staff recommends that company for the primary contract award.  Staff also conducted a due diligence review of the second low bidder, Mida, Inc. dba Abbey Commercial Flooring, and staff recommends that company for the secondary contract award.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board award the primary contract for the carpet and floor tile installation and replacement contract to Carpet Experts, Inc. and the secondary contract to Mida, Inc. dba Abbey Commercial Flooring, and authorize the Division Superintendent, or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services, to execute and administer both of the above contracts on behalf of the School Board.

  • Lanier MS has committed leadership, and the school is well-prepared to offer a high quality Level IV AAP center program.
  • Seventy people attended the community meeting on October 19, 2017. 
  • There were 407 respondents to the electronic survey.
    • 77% of survey participants reported having an AAP Level IV eligible student
    • 350 participants selected a preference for one of the two options offered:  
      • 53% preferred option 1 for SY 2018-19 to be a choice year for rising grades 7 & grade 8 (185 people)
      • 47% preferred option 2 to begin reassignment of rising 7th grade students without choice in SY 2018-19  (165 people)
  • Prior to voting on the establishment of the Lanier Level IV Center and the adoption of a new pupil assignment plan, the School Board will hold a public hearing for the purpose of obtaining additional public comment as a part of its scheduled meeting of January 11, 2018, which public hearing will be the subject of at least 10 days advance notice to the public (including notice published in a newspaper of general circulation in the school division).

 

 

Recommendation:    

That, in order to contribute to the efficiency of the school division, the School Board approve the establishment of a new Level IV advanced academic center program in fall 2018 at Lanier Middle School, and also adopt the following pupil assignment plan: in fall 2018 Lanier based rising 7th and 8th grade center-eligible students currently assigned to Rocky Run MS would be afforded the option to attend the new Lanier MS Level IV center or their current Level IV center at Rocky Run MS.  In fall 2019, Lanier based rising 7th grade students currently assigned to Rocky Run will be required to attend Lanier’s Level IV center. Rising 8th grade students in fall 2019 would be afforded the option to attend Lanier Level IV center or their current Level IV center at Rocky Run MS.  In fall 2020 Lanier based rising 7th and 8th grade center-eligible students will be required to attend the Lanier Level IV center.

Meeting Category:  December 14, 2017 - Regular Meeting

 

Subject:  FY 2019-2023 Capital Improvement Program

                                                                        

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2019-2023 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five-year student membership projections and capacity calculations, as well as the Capital Construction Cash Flow which identifies plans for new construction, capacity enhancements, renovations, and site acquisition to accommodate future capacity needs.

 

Discussion:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools continues to face a challenging situation whereby student membership (in certain locations) is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities, provide capacity enhancement projects and it will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions and boundary adjustments to accommodate growing enrollment needs.

 

New School Construction: The CIP proposes three new elementary schools: one in the North West County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and the Dulles Airport access road; one to relieve overcrowding in the Fairfax/Oakton area; one near the new Silver Line Metro Innovation Center station. It also proposes construction of a new high school in the Western end of the Silver Line metro vicinity to alleviate capacity concerns at multiple high schools. Also proposed for needs beyond the five year horizon are five new and /or repurposed schools.

 

Capacity Enhancements: Funding for additions at West Potomac High School, Justice High School, and Madison High School are proposed. Funds are also proposed for the relocation of three modular additions.

 

Renovations: Funding for renovations at five named high schools are proposed in the CIP. Additionally, funds for renovations at 21 named elementary schools and 12 unnamed elementary schools are included. Lastly, funds for the renovation of four named middle schools and one unnamed middle school are proposed. Many of these renovation projects will include capacity enhancements.

 

Site Acquisition: Funds have been provided as a placeholder to acquire a school site in the future.

 

The FY 2019–23 CIP also recommends possible solutions for future consideration to resolve capacity deficits. These options include interior modifications, temporary classrooms, program location suggestions, and boundary adjustments. Any boundary adjustment or program change would be discussed and decided through a transparent process that engages the community in accordance with School Board Policies and Regulations.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2019-2023 Capital Improvement Program.
