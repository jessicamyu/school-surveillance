National Mentoring Month - January 2014

 

National Mentoring Month highlights mentoring and the positive impact it can have on young lives. This month-long outreach campaign focuses national attention on the need for mentors, as well as how each of us—individuals, businesses, government agencies, schools, faith communities and nonprofits—can work together to increase the number of mentors and assure brighter futures for our young people.  Positive relationships between mentors and their mentees have been shown to encourage young people to stay in school, believe in themselves, and live up to their potential.  National Mentoring Month marks its 13th anniversary in 2014 with the theme “Mentoring Works!” encouraging citizens to step up and serve as mentors.

RESOLUTION
HONORING
RICHARD A. MONIUSZKO

 

 

     WHEREAS, Richard A. Moniuszko was appointed Deputy Superintendent of Fairfax County Public Schools on May 25, 2006, and served with distinction for almost eight years; and

 

     WHEREAS, he worked diligently with the academic leaders in Fairfax County Public Schools to fulfill and implement the School Board’s three Student Achievement Goals to Pursue Academic Excellence, Develop Essential Life Skills, and Demonstrate Responsibility to the Community and the World so that together we are “Building the future….child by child”; and

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Moniuszko led major Fairfax County Public Schools initiatives, including the Academic Learning Group, the Project Management Oversight Committee, the Priority Schools Initiative, the Charter School Review Committee, the Student Rights and Responsibilities Ad Hoc Committee, and the new Teacher Evaluation System while keeping the focus on our teachers, administrators, students, and families; and

 

     WHEREAS, he was a key leader in promoting twenty-first century skills or the 4Cs: Critical Thinking, Communication, Collaboration and Creativity, and worked at the national level to create measureable outcomes for these essential skills; and

 

     WHEREAS, Dr. Moniuszko kept a compassionate, kind and caring approach at the center of his leadership and honored each employee as a valued staff member and colleague;

 

     NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board extends its sincerest commendation and deepest appreciation to Richard A. Moniuszko, upon his retirement, for his leadership, his commitment to public education in Fairfax, and his dedication to the staff, students, and families in Fairfax County Public Schools; and

 

     BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Fairfax County School Board, on behalf of the entire Fairfax community, extends best wishes to Dr. Moniuszko, his wife Linda, and his family as they embark on their new journey.

Subject:                     FY 2014 Midyear Budget Review

 

School Board Action Required:  Decision

 

Key Points:

 

In the School Operating Fund, the FY 2014 beginning balance increased by $3.5 million due to final year-end and audit adjustments made after the FY 2013 Final Budget Review primarily due to an increase of $2.5 million in sales tax revenue based on actual sales tax receipts and expenditure decreases of $1.0 million. In addition to the beginning balance adjustment, other FY 2014 revenue adjustments reflect a net decrease in grant revenue totaling $18,131.

 

Recommended expenditure adjustments reflect a net decrease of $18,131 in grant expenditures primarily from a decrease in the federal award for the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act offset by an increase in funding from the Carl D. Perkins grant.

 

Expenditure adjustments, combined with revenue adjustments, result in available funds totaling $3.5 million recommended to be set aside to increase the FY 2015 beginning balance from $45.0 million to $48.5 million.

 

Changes to other School Board funds are detailed in the attachment.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve revenue and expenditure changes reflected in the FY 2014 Midyear Budget Review as detailed in the agenda item.

Staff Contact:         Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

Meeting Category:  December 19, 2013 - Regular Meeting No. 10

 

Subject:                  FY 2015-2019 Capital Improvement Program

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2015-2019 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five year enrollment projections and capacity calculations, as well as identification of new construction, capacity enhancement, renovation, special program, site acquisition and infrastructure requirements.

 

The Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2015-2019 Capital Improvement Program Summary can be found in the Capital Program Recommendations chapter of the Proposed Capital Improvement Program booklet.

 

Discussion:

 

The school system continues to face a challenging situation in which student enrollment is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities and capacity enhancement projects and will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions, as well as boundary adjustments.

 

The CIP includes funding for five new elementary schools and a new high school.  The new elementary school needs are identified as follows:  one in the Eastern Fairfax area to relieve overcrowding in the Bailey’s Crossroads area; one in the Richmond Highway Corridor (Route 1 area) to relieve overcrowding in the Groveton/Hybla Valley area; one in the Northwest County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and Dulles Airport Access Road; one to relieve overcrowding in the Fairfax/Oakton area; and one on the army base to relieve overcrowding at Fort Belvoir Elementary School. 

 

It is also anticipated that beyond fiscal year 2019 an additional new high school will be needed to address the future growth in the western Fairfax County area. Funding for additions at South Lakes High School and two elementary schools to help relieve overcrowding in the Tysons/Vienna area (Westbriar and Marshall Road) are also identified. Capacity enhancements are proposed at both Herndon and Oakton High Schools as part of their renovations. Furthermore, the CIP includes construction funds for renovation at nine elementary and one middle school, plus a renovation at one high school, as well as planning funds for renovations at eight elementary, one middle, and two high schools. Many of the renovation projects will also include capacity enhancements.

 

Allocating funds for new schools will result in delays for some future renovation projects. Favorable construction pricing has helped mitigate these delays; however, they will occur if the school system does not receive additional capital funding authorization from the Board of Supervisors.

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2015-2019 Capital Improvement Program as described in the narrative and in the Superintendent’s Proposed FY 2015-2019 Capital Improvement Program Summary.

DEBRIEFING FORMATS

Three simple questions:

  1. What did the Board do well in our meeting?
  2. What did not work well for us?
  3. What do we want to do to improve?

Quick Whip Technique--around the room, back, and around again:

  1. What I contributed to the meeting to help us as a board.
  2. What I did that detracted from good board accountability and what I intend to do to not let that happen again.


One member takes the lead:
One board member monitors the meeting and gives feedback to the board. Rotate the responsibility. Other members respond to the leader's assessment.
