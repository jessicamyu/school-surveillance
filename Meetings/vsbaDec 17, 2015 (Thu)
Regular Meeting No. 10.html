National Mentoring Month highlights mentoring and the positive impact it can have on young lives. 3.07 Strategic Plan - Goal 1, Student Success
Meeting
Dec 17, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 10
Category
3. Presentations to the School Board 7:30 p.m.
Type

Staff Contact:   Francisco Durán, Chief Academic Officer

 

 

Other Staff Present:  Noel Klimenko, director of PreK-12 Curriculum and Instruction, Instructional Services

                                    Kathy Oliver, director of Student Testing, Chief Academic Office

 

 

Meeting Category:     December 17, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 10

 

 

 

Subject:     Strategic Plan Goal 1 (Strategies 1 & 2)—Student Success

               

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan –Goal 1

 

 

 

Key Points:   

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. All of the goals contain overarching strategies, desired outcomes, actions, and monitoring metrics. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan. The purpose of this report is to provide an update on the current work that supports Goal 1, Student Success.

 

The following are two of the four overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 1 Student Success.  The remaining two strategies will be presented on January 21, 2016

 

Overarching Strategy 1:

Enhance instructional practices to ensure that all students receive an education in a dynamic environment designed to foster life-long learning and support them in achieving their full potential.

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • The curriculum will reflect Portrait of a Graduate outcomes in all content areas, where students are appropriately challenged
  • Achievement gaps will be eliminated
  • Teachers, students and parents will have access to contemporary and effective technology resources
  • Centralized support will be available for schools and school staff based on student achievement needs.

 

Overarching Strategy 2:

All students will achieve their full potential through the use of assessment and data systems for decision making that support student attainment of the outcomes defined by the Portrait of a Graduate

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • A fully-developed balanced assessment system will reflect Portrait of a Graduate outcomes in all content areas
  • Data tools will be available to schools, school teams, and individual teachers to diagnose and monitor individual student progress
  • Grading will be an accurate reflection of learning 

 

Recommendation: That the School Board approve the Strategic Plan - Goal 1 Student Success Strategies 1 and 2 as detailed in the agenda item.

3.08 FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program
Meeting
Dec 17, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 10
Category

Staff Contact:              Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services                                   

 

Meeting Category:       December 17, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 10

 

Subject:                       FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program

 

School Board Action Required:   Information

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five-year student enrollment projections and capacity calculations, as well as the Capital Construction Cash Flow which identifies plans for new construction, capacity enhancements, renovations, and site acquisition to accommodate future capacity needs.

 

Discussion:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools continues to face a challenging situation whereby student enrollment in certain locations is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities, provide capacity enhancement projects, and it will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions, and boundary adjustments to accommodate growing enrollment needs.

 

New School Construction: The CIP proposes four new elementary schools: one on the army base to relieve overcrowding at Fort Belvoir Elementary School which is jointly funded with the Department of Defense; one in the Richmond Highway Corridor (Route 1 area) to relieve overcrowding in the Groveton/Hybla Valley area; one in the North West County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and the Dulles Airport access road; and one to relieve overcrowding in the Fairfax/Oakton area.  It proposes funds for a gymnasium addition at Bailey’s Upper ES. It also proposes construction of a new high school in the Western end of the Silver Line metro vicinity to alleviate capacity concerns at multiple high schools.   

 

Capacity Enhancements: Funding for additions at Westbriar Elementary School and South Lakes High School are identified, as well as for potential additions at two high schools (one in the eastern part of the county) and a middle school. Funds are also proposed for the relocation of four modular additions.

 

Renovations: Funding for renovations at 6 named high schools and one unnamed high school are proposed in the CIP.  Additionally, funds for renovations at 16 named elementary schools and 12 unnamed elementary schools are included. Lastly, funds for the renovation of five named middle schools and one unnamed middle school are proposed. Many of these renovation projects will also include capacity enhancements.

 

Site Acquisition: Funds have been proposed for a site acquisition in the future.

 

Like last year, the FY 2017–21 CIP also recommends possible solutions for future consideration to resolve capacity deficits. These options include interior modifications, temporary classrooms, program location suggestions, and boundary adjustments. Any boundary adjustment or program change would be discussed and decided through a transparent process that engagements the community, in accordance with School Board Policy and Regulations.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program.

Approve proposed restated plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001 for submission to the Internal Revenue Service with the application for a current determination letter presented as new business on December 3.

Staff Contact:   Jeanne M. Carr, ERFC Executive Director/CIO

 

Meeting Category: December 17, 2015 – Regular Meeting No. 10

 

Subject: Proposed Restatements of the Educational Employees of Fairfax County Defined Benefit Plan (ERFC and ERFC 2001)

 

School Board Action Required: Decision

 

Related To:   LEGAL REQUIREMENT

 

 

Key Points: 

The ERFC Trustees have periodically submitted the plan documents to the Internal Revenue Service for a determination that the Plan continues to be qualified for favorable tax treatment under the Internal Revenue Code.  By obtaining such a determination, the Trustees receive assurance that the Trust Fund does not have to pay income tax on its investment earnings and that the members do not have to pay tax on the benefits they are earning until they actually retire and begin to receive benefits.  The Internal Revenue Service has set January 31, 2016, as the deadline for governmental plans to submit plans for review in Cycle E.   Fairfax County Public Schools may submit the plan for review by the IRS in Cycle E by filing a determination letter application for the plan by January 31, 2016.

 

The IRS requires, for purposes of such a submission, that the plan documents be “restated” to incorporate all amendments that have been adopted since the last time that the plan documents were submitted to the IRS.  TheERFC and ERFC 2001 plan documents were last submitted to the IRS in 2011.  Since then, the School Board has approved 4 separate sets of amendments.   

 

Restated plan documents have been prepared.  These proposed restatements, which are attached, incorporate amendments 1 through 4.

 

 

 

Recommendation:  That the School Board approve proposed restated plan documents for ERFC and ERFC 2001 for submission to the Internal Revenue Service with the application for a current determination letter.

6.01 FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program [PH 1/5/16, WS 1/11/16, Action 1/21/16]

Dec 17, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 10

Staff Contact:              Jeffrey K. Platenberg, assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services                                   

 

Meeting Category:       December 17, 2015 - Regular Meeting No. 10

 

Subject:                       FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program

 

School Board Action Required:   Information

 

Key Points:

 

The FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) provides five-year student enrollment projections and capacity calculations, as well as the Capital Construction Cash Flow which identifies plans for new construction, capacity enhancements, renovations, and site acquisition to accommodate future capacity needs.

 

Discussion:

 

Fairfax County Public Schools continues to face a challenging situation whereby student enrollment in certain locations is projected to exceed the school system’s ability to accommodate students within existing school buildings. This will necessitate an increase in allocation of capital dollars to build new facilities, provide capacity enhancement projects, and it will require continued use of temporary classrooms, modular additions, and boundary adjustments to accommodate growing enrollment needs.

 

New School Construction: The CIP proposes four new elementary schools: one on the army base to relieve overcrowding at Fort Belvoir Elementary School which is jointly funded with the Department of Defense; one in the Richmond Highway Corridor (Route 1 area) to relieve overcrowding in the Groveton/Hybla Valley area; one in the North West County area in the vicinity of Route 28 and the Dulles Airport access road; and one to relieve overcrowding in the Fairfax/Oakton area.  It proposes funds for a gymnasium addition at Bailey’s Upper ES. It also proposes construction of a new high school in the Western end of the Silver Line metro vicinity to alleviate capacity concerns at multiple high schools.   

 

Capacity Enhancements: Funding for additions at Westbriar Elementary School and South Lakes High School are identified, as well as for potential additions at two high schools (one in the eastern part of the county) and a middle school. Funds are also proposed for the relocation of four modular additions.

 

Renovations: Funding for renovations at 6 named high schools and one unnamed high school are proposed in the CIP.  Additionally, funds for renovations at 16 named elementary schools and 12 unnamed elementary schools are included. Lastly, funds for the renovation of five named middle schools and one unnamed middle school are proposed. Many of these renovation projects will also include capacity enhancements.

 

Site Acquisition: Funds have been proposed for a site acquisition in the future.

 

Like last year, the FY 2017–21 CIP also recommends possible solutions for future consideration to resolve capacity deficits. These options include interior modifications, temporary classrooms, program location suggestions, and boundary adjustments. Any boundary adjustment or program change would be discussed and decided through a transparent process that engagements the community, in accordance with School Board Policy and Regulations.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board approve the FY 2017-2021 Capital Improvement Program.

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Related To:  Strategic Plan –Goal 1

 

 

 

Key Points:   

In July 2015, the School Board approved Ignite, the strategic plan for Fairfax County Public Schools, which represents the cooperative work of the School Board and Leadership Team, with significant community input, to create a long term plan for continuous improvement. At the core of this plan is the FCPS Portrait of a Graduate. The Ignite Plan contains four strategic goals: Student Success, Caring Culture, Premier Workforce, and Resource Stewardship. All of the goals contain overarching strategies, desired outcomes, actions, and monitoring metrics. This school year, 2015-2016, marks the first year of implementation of the Strategic Plan. The purpose of this report is to provide an update on the current work that supports Goal 1, Student Success.

 

The following are two of the four overarching strategies and the desired outcomes for Goal 1 Student Success.  The remaining two strategies will be presented on January 21, 2016.

 

Overarching Strategy 1:

Enhance instructional practices to ensure that all students receive an education in a dynamic environment designed to foster life-long learning and support them in achieving their full potential.

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • The curriculum will reflect Portrait of a Graduate outcomes in all content areas, where students are appropriately challenged
  • Achievement gaps will be eliminated
  • Teachers, students and parents will have access to contemporary and effective technology resources
  • Centralized support will be available for schools and school staff based on student achievement needs.

 

Overarching Strategy 2:

All students will achieve their full potential through the use of assessment and data systems for decision making that support student attainment of the outcomes defined by the Portrait of a Graduate

 

Desired Outcomes:

  • A fully-developed balanced assessment system will reflect Portrait of a Graduate outcomes in all content areas
  • Data tools will be available to schools, school teams, and individual teachers to diagnose and monitor individual student progress
  • Grading will be an accurate reflection of learning
