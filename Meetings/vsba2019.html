National Red Ribbon Week - October 23-31, 2022

 

National Red Ribbon Week is celebrated each year from October 23-31.  It was established in 1985 in memory of Enrique Camarena, a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration agent who was killed in the line of duty while investigating a major drug cartel in Mexico. To honor his battle against illegal drugs, friends and neighbors began to wear red badges of satin and some parents began to form coalitions. National Red Ribbon Week grew from these grassroots efforts to form a national movement against the use of illegal drugs. Its annual campaign encourages students to take a visible stand against substance abuse and make a personal commitment to follow a drug-free lifestyle. The 2022 Red Ribbon Theme is "Celebrate Life. Live Drug Free” is a reminder that every day Americans across the county make significant daily contributions to their communities by being the best they can be because they live Drug-Free!

RESOLUTION HONORING INTERNATIONAL AUGMENTATIVE AND ALTERNATIVE COMMUNICATION (AAC) AWARENESS MONTH

 

WHEREAS, October 2022 is International AAC Awareness Month, and the theme is “Show Your Voice;” and

 

WHEREAS, the United States Society for Augmentative and Alternative Communication (USSAAC) defines Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) as including “all forms of communication (other than oral speech) that are used to express thoughts, needs, wants, and ideas; and

 

WHEREAS, communication – in all its forms - is a basic human right and is specifically protected under the Americans with Disabilities Act; and

 

WHEREAS, AAC includes, but is not limited to, gestures and facial expressions; writing; drawing; spelling words by pointing to letters; pointing to photos, pictures, or written words; using an app on an iPad or tablet to communicate, or using a computer-based speech-generating device; and

 

WHEREAS, “AAC strategies, tools, and technologies enable more than 2 million children and adults in the United States” to access their fundamental right to communication; and

 

WHEREAS, research supports that there are no prerequisites required in order for individuals to benefit from AAC access; that AAC users are presumed competent and given access to “the tools and instruction they need to learn, regardless of their speech impediment, diagnosis or degree of difference;” and

 

WHEREAS, Fairfax County Public Schools is in the process of implementing a division-wide literacy plan that ensures comprehensive, PreK-12 research-based reading instruction that is aligned with the Science of Reading to provide all students - including AAC users - the foundational skills upon which literacy is built; and

 

WHEREAS, students, educators, and families will benefit from increased awareness about AAC users and technology, and the combination of strengths and difficulties that AAC users can have; and

 

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Fairfax County School Board recognizes October 2022 as AAC Awareness Month and encourages all residents and FCPS staff to learn more about Augmentative & Alternative Communication, including identifying students that can benefit from AAC; understanding the core of “presuming competence,” knowing and making available supports for use by educators and families; and highlighting the success that AAC users can have in their careers and life.

National School Bus Safety Week Recognition

 

The week of October 17-21 has been designated as National School Bus Safety Week and the theme this year is “1 Bus + 1 Driver = a BIG Impact on Education”. Throughout the years, the school bus and driver remain a strong symbol for our education community; a profession that is as important to our education system as it is honorable.  The week focuses on student safety when waiting for and boarding a school bus. School bus safety is an issue for all citizens of Fairfax County, who need to be aware of laws requiring them to stop and wait behind a school bus when it is loading and unloading student riders. Motorists are encouraged to practice patience and drive gently around school buses in our neighborhoods and school communities.


The major responsibility for school bus safety in Fairfax County rests firmly in the hands of our school bus drivers and attendants, who daily transport more than 129,800 students in 1,625 school buses on more than 6,500 separate routes traveling over 18.3 million operational miles each year.  

 

Thank you to all the staff from the Department of Facilities and Transportation for their continued hard work to keep our children safe.

Resolution of Support for Teachers and Administrators regarding Inclusive Curriculum and Instruction  

  

Whereas, Fairfax County Public Schools are committed to providing an academically rigorous education that promotes a responsive, caring, and inclusive culture where all feel valued, supported, and hopeful; 

  

Whereas, essential to that inclusive classroom culture is an education that reflects all voices, and respects the need for robust instruction regarding historic and systemic injustice;  

  

Whereas, FCPS teachers, administrators, and central office staff are developing curriculum and classroom resources, along with incorporating more representative literature,  to strengthen student understanding of our complex national and global histories; 

   

Whereas, it is the role of the School Board and the Superintendent to support educators in carrying out FCPS expectations that all students will become critical thinkers and effective collaborators who respect the diverse perspectives, cultures, religions, ethnicities, abilities, sexual orientation, and gender identity of our community;

  

Whereas, FCPS’ Controversial Issues Policy and Regulation (P3280/R3280) provide essential guidance and support for teachers and administrators as they engage students in more inclusive curriculum related to racism, bias, intolerance, and injustice. 

  

Now, therefore, be it resolved that the School Board commits to protect and support teachers and administrators as they deliver FCPS-approved curriculum and classroom resources that are inclusive, and meet the high aspirations of our students, families, and the Fairfax County community. Citizens who want to address the Board at a Regular meeting, may sign up online beginning at 6 a.m. four (4) business days prior to the meeting, or by calling the School Board office at 571-423-1075 during normal business hours. For more information, please visit the FCPS Website.

 

A total of 16 in-person, live virtual, and prerecorded video speaker slots are available per meeting. Thirteen slots will be reserved for in-person and live virtual speakers.  Three (3) slots will be reserved for students and three (3) slots will be reserved for those speakers who wish to submit a prerecorded video. The speakers list closes at 48 hours before the meeting. After the registration closes, speakers will be determined by a random lottery process.  After the random process, speakers will be notified if they have received a speaking or prerecorded video spot.   After the first 13 in-person or live virtual speakers are determined, the next ten (10) names as determined by the same random process will be chosen as the waitlist and notified that they are on the waitlist to speak.  Video submissions will not be permitted for the waitlist.  Waitlist speakers must be present at the meeting to speak in the event of a no show.  Speaker substitutions are not permitted.  A speaker may not yield their time to another individual before or during their remarks.

 

The School Board will not hear statements involving issues that have been scheduled for public hearings (such as Capital Improvement Program, budget, and boundaries), or personal attacks on any person. Complaints regarding individual students or school-based employees should not be raised at public meetings. Any such concerns should be directed to the appropriate school principal or other school official. Citizens are encouraged to write the School Board on any school-related topic.

Subject:  Solar Power Purchase Agreement for Annandale High School

 

 

Staff Contact:  Charles Fanshaw, interim assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

Meeting Category:  October 20, 2022 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

Summary/Background:

 

On March 4, 2021, the School Board approved participation by FCPS in the solar power purchase program that was procured by Fairfax County Government on behalf of itself, certain County authorities, and FCPS.  Since that time, the County terminated one of the three contracts awarded for solar power purchase services (PPA Service Contracts), leaving two solar contractors eligible to perform these services on behalf of the participating entities.  Thereafter, the County amended its contracts with the two remaining solar contractors effective January 14, 2022, to, among other things, amend the fee schedule and eliminate the primary, secondary and tertiary contractor rankings.

 

Following the January 2022 contract amendment, FCPS’s Office of Facilities Management (OFM) requested proposals from both remaining solar contractors for FCPS’s first rooftop solar power purchase project at Annandale High School.  In the months since that request, one of the two remaining solar contractors responded. 

 

On August 12, 2022, Ipsun Power Inc. d/b/a Ipsun Solar (Ipsun) submitted its proposal for Annandale High School pursuant to Fairfax County Contract No. 4400009517, as amended.  Ipsun’s proposal has since been reviewed by the Energy Solutions Division of CMTA, Inc. (CMTA), a third-party engineering firm that has evaluated and advised OFM regarding projected energy savings and other aspects of Ipsun’s proposal. 

 

 

In order to proceed with the implementation of Ipsun’s proposal for Annandale High School, FCPS and Ipsun will be required to enter into a Solar Power Purchase Agreement (Solar PPA) in the form attached to this agenda item.  The Solar PPA sets forth the process for finalizing the design of the solar facilities to the satisfaction of FCPS, and provides for installation, commissioning, and operation of the facilities by Ipsun.  Ipsun will retain ownership of the solar facilities throughout the term, subject to exercise of FCPS’s purchase option. The Solar PPA further memorializes Ipsun’s commitment to sell (and FCPS’s obligation to purchase) all electricity generated by the rooftop solar facilities for a period of 25 years at a rate of $0.102 per kilowatt hour, subject to an annual escalation of 2% after the 5th year of operation.  Ipsun’s rooftop solar facilities will be connected to the electrical distribution system maintained by FCPS’s utility provider so as to permit transfer of solar-generated electricity that is not immediately usable at the site to the utility under a net metering arrangement to be agreed upon by FCPS and the utility.  Under that net metering arrangement, FCPS expects to receive credits for all electricity transferred to the utility’s distribution system.

 

Total payments to Ipsun over the course of the Solar PPA’s 25-year term are estimated at $2,153,088.71, an amount representing energy cost savings estimated by Ipsun and CMTA as ranging between $11,000 and $22,000 per year, on average, for this site.  In addition to the anticipated energy cost savings, the solar power purchase program proposed for Annandale High School will further FCPS’s efforts in responding to the renewable energy recommendations of the Joint

That the School Board approve the implementation of a rooftop solar power purchase program at Annandale High School and authorize the Division Superintendent and the interim Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services (either of whom may act without the other) to execute the Solar PPA and administer the contract on behalf of the School Board.

6.05 Peace Valley Lane - Acquisition to confirm the School Board’s title and ownership of a 25-foot strip of land along the southern property boundary of Justice High School [FTS; Action 11/3/22; That the School Board schedule and hold a public hearing to (i) determine that the School Board needs to acquire clear, indefeasible title to the 25-Foot Strip (hereinafter defined) to provide for necessary public school uses; (ii) authorize the acquisition of the;25-Foot Strip (hereinafter defined) by condemnation ;other means and (iii) authorize the Division Superintendent or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services to execute on behalf of the School Board all necessary documents to accomplish such acquisition, including, without limitation, a Certificate of Taking and Petition for Condemnation, all as approved by School Board counsel]

Subject:  Peace Valley Lane - Acquisition to confirm the School Board’s title and ownership of a 25-foot strip of land along the southern property boundary of Justice High School

 

 

Staff Contact:  Charles Fanshaw, interim assistant superintendent, Facilities and Transportation Services

 

 

Meeting Category:  October 20, 2022 – Regular Meeting

 

 

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

 

Related To:  Goal 4- Resource Stewardship

 

 

Summary/Background:

 

The Fairfax County School Board (the "School Board") previously approved much-needed improvements at Justice High School, consisting of two building additions to provide new classrooms, science labs, special education spaces and a cafeteria expansion, along with related site improvements (the “Project”). The Project required the School Board to seek and obtain a rezoning of Justice High School from the R-2 District to the R-8 District (the “Rezoning”) to increase the permitted gross floor area of the site to accommodate the various building additions and site improvements to be constructed. 

 

In connection with the Project, School Board Staff discovered the existence of a long abandoned outlet road comprising a twenty-five foot wide strip of land along the southern property boundary of Justice High School ("the 25-Foot Strip") that was believed to be part of the Justice High School property. The 25-Foot Strip is immediately between the southern boundary of Justice High School along a portion of Peace Valley Lane (which is owned by the School Board and identified as Tax Map Parcel 061-1-((1))-13A) and the northernmost boundary of Justice Park (which is owned by the Fairfax County Park Authority (the “Park Authority”) and identified as Tax Map Parcel 61-1-((1))-15).

 

The 25-Foot Strip was likely intended to be included in the property acquired by the School Board in the mid-1950s, but the chain of title does not contain clear language conveying the strip to the School Board.  The 25-Foot Strip, however, has been used continuously by school faculty, staff and students for educational purposes for over half a century.  Among other things, the 25-Foot Strip overlaps a part of the south side of Peace Valley Lane, including the portion of Peace Valley Lane between Justice High School and Justice Park owned and maintained by the School Board since the high school opened in 1959.  That portion of Peace Valley Lane is integral for access to and from Justice High School, as well as to provide much-needed parking along the north side of the road.  The remainder of the 25-Foot Strip to the south of Peace Valley Lane consists of unimproved lands which are maintained by the School Board’s Facilities staff and have been used continuously by school faculty, staff and students to provide access to and from tennis courts, a softball field, an amphitheater and other facilities in Justice Park that are used for school sports and other activities.  In addition, acquisition of the 25-Foot Strip will facilitate the Project and future building expansions at the already-undersized school site.  As noted above, the Rezoning was necessary because the gross floor area of the site was insufficient to accommodate the various building additions and site improvements to be constructed.  By acquiring clear, indefeasible title to the 25-Foot Strip, the School Board will ensure that it has sufficient lands to satisfy the gross floor area requirements for the Project as well as for future additions and improvements that will likely be needed in the near future due to the comparatively small size of the Justice High School property.  Moreover, by clearing the School Board’s title to the 25-Foot Strip, the County will be able to construct a sidewalk along the south side of Peace Valley Lane which will significantly improve pedestrian access for students, faculty, staff and the public to and from Justice HS as well as to and from the ball fields and other facilities in Justice Park used for school sports and other activities.

 

A title report (the “Title Report”) prepared by Fidelity National Title Insurance Company (“Fidelity”) is of the opinion that in the absence of clear conveyance language in the deeds and instruments recorded in the land records of Fairfax County, the ownership of the 25-Foot Strip would be vested in the heirs of Avon M. Nevius (“Nevius”).  The Title Report, however, is based solely on a review of land records, and does not consider whether the School Board may have acquired ownership of the 25-Foot Strip through the doctrine of adverse possession.  Because of the School Board’s continuous occupation and use of the 25-Foot Strip for much longer than 15-years, including its construction, maintenance, and use of the portion of Peace Valley Lane between Justice High School and Justice Park that the strip overlaps, staff and outside counsel have concluded that that the School Board has likely acquired title by adverse possession to all or significant portion of the 25-Foot Strip.

 

Counsel for the School Board has made several diligent attempts to locate possible heirs of Nevius with no success. Therefore, the true owner of the 25-Foot Strip cannot with reasonable diligence be found within the Commonwealth of Virginia.  It is on that basis that the School Board can proceed with condemnation without attempting to purchase the Outlet Road through negotiations.  Pursuant to Va. Code Ann. § 25.1-204 and 25.1-303, no effort to purchase is required where the owner “cannot with reasonable diligence be found within this Commonwealth.”

 

The condemnation process will enable the School Board to obtain clear, indefeasible title to the 25-Foot Strip, thereby eliminating the cloud on its legal ownership and control of portion of Peace Valley Lane adjacent to Justice High School and provide the additional land area needed to facilitate the Project and other school uses presently and in the future as described above.  The old outlet road that once ran through the 25-Foot Strip has long since been abandoned, and the heirs of Nevius have never possessed or used that land to our knowledge at any time since the School Board’s acquisition of the Justice High School site in the mid-1950s.  The 25-Foot Strip is also not a legal lot and cannot be developed or built on as a stand-alone parcel due to its size, shape, topography, as well as its landlocked status, being sandwiched entirely between lands owned by the School Board, the Park Authority and Fairfax County or the Commonwealth of Virginia.  And as previously noted, the School Board has likely acquired title to all or a significant portion of the 25-Foot Strip under the doctrine of adverse possession.  Accordingly, staff  concludes that the 25-Foot Strip has no appreciable market value, and is worth less than $10,000.00. As part of the condemnation process, a sum will be deposited into the Circuit Court of Fairfax County, or to the Clerk thereof as required by law, the sum of money estimated by staff to be at or above the fair market value of the 25-Foot Strip of up to $10,000.  This compensation will be held by the Court on behalf of the heirs of Nevius if and when they may ever come forward.  We propose using the School Board’s “quick take” condemnation authority, which will allow it to occupy and use the 25-Foot Strip immediately upon filing and recording a Certificate of Taking with the Circuit Court and depositing with the Court the sums representing the above-described estimated value of the land.  Proceeding in that manner will eliminate any potential for delays of the Project or construction of the sidewalk along the south side of Peace Valley Lane which will improve the pedestrian access for students, faculty and staff to and from Justice High School and the Park facilities to the south.  Formal condemnation proceedings, which include a trial to determine the just compensation required for the lands, will be initiated shortly after the filing and recordation of the Certificate of Taking.

 

The School Board has the authority to condemn this property pursuant to Va. Code § 22.1-127. It is required by Va. Code Ann. § 15.2-1903(B) to hold a public hearing to approve the condemnation prior to initiating condemnation proceedings.

 

 

Recommendation:

 

That the School Board schedule and hold a public hearing to (i) determine that the School Board needs to acquire clear, indefeasible title to the 25-Foot Strip (hereinafter defined) to provide for necessary public school uses; (ii) authorize the acquisition of the 25-Foot Strip (hereinafter defined) by condemnation or other means and (iii) authorize the Division Superintendent or the Assistant Superintendent of Facilities and Transportation Services to execute on behalf of the School Board all necessary documents to accomplish such acquisition, including, without limitation, a Certificate of Taking and Petition for Condemnation, all as approved by School Board counsel.

Subject: Revisions to Policy 2202, Eligibility for Enrollment

 

Staff Contact:  Michelle Boyd, assistant superintendent, Department of Special Services

 

Meeting Category:  October 20, 2022--Regular Meeting

  

School Board Action Required:  Information

 

Summary/Background (Key Points): 

 

Policy 2202 establishes the eligibility requirements for student enrollment in Fairfax County Public Schools (FCPS). Revisions to Section III. J. 3. and Section III. L. are recommended to ensure consistency with the requirements of the Code of Virginia (§ 22.1-3;  § 22.1-4.1) and to streamline the student registration process in order to expedite enrollment of eligible students.

 

Summary of revisions to Policy 2202:

         

  • Remove current requirement for parents to provide notarized, independent supporting documentation for kinship care agreements and court ordered agreement and replace it with the Parent/Legal Guardian Attestation.

 

  • Remove the requirement for Power of Attorney and include language regarding educational decision making in the Parent/Legal Guardian Attestation.

 

  • Eliminate requirement for the coordinator of Student Registration to approve kinship care arrangements and add that the coordinator may verify information submitted by the parent on the signed Parent/Legal Guardian Attestation.

 

Recommendation: That the School Board review and approve proposed revisions to Policy 2202.
