Subject

Meeting
Mar 1, 2016 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
I. OLD BUSINESS - SOUTHWEST AREA ELEMENTARY SCHOOL BOUNDARY STUDY RECOMMENDATION (third reading) (Ms. Miller/Dr. Wheatley-Phillip)
Type
Action

On February 2, 2016, the Board of Education of Baltimore County (Board) received for consideration and approval, a report from the Southwest Area Boundary Committee recommending boundary changes to schools in the region to help relieve overcrowding. The recommendation, known as Option 3.2b (revised) affects the boundaries of Arbutus, Catonsville, Halethorpe, Hillcrest, Relay and Westchester elementary schools. The Board held a public hearing on the recommended boundary change on February 17, 2016.  The recommendation Option 3.2b (revised) is being forwarded for Board approval.

N. NEW BUSINESS - APPROVAL OF THE INITIATION OF THE DESIGN AND CONSTRUCTION FOR A NEW ELEMENTARY SCHOOL IN THE NORTHEAST (Mr. Smith/Mr. Dixit)
Subject
1. Approval of the Initiation of the Design and Construction for a New Elementary School in the Northeast
Meeting
Mar 1, 2016 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
N. NEW BUSINESS - APPROVAL OF THE INITIATION OF THE DESIGN AND CONSTRUCTION FOR A NEW ELEMENTARY SCHOOL IN THE NORTHEAST (Mr. Smith/Mr. Dixit)
Type
Action

As a community and school planning region, northeast Baltimore County has experienced substantial demographic change over the past decade and the growth is projected to continue.  On October 6, 2015, the Board approved the State Capital Improvement Program, and on January 5, 2016, the Board approved the County Capital Improvement Program.  Both included a request for planning a new elementary school site in the northeast area.  There are two BCPS-owned sites in the northeast area which are Nottingham and Ridge Road; however, the site recommended for the northeast school is the Joppa Road site which is owned by Baltimore County Government. 

 

The 20.43 acres parcel is located north of East Joppa Road and south of the intersection of East Joppa Road and Chapel Hill Road.  The parcel is currently owned by the county, and is the most viable site to relieve the overcrowding in the northeast area.  This site is of sufficient size to accommodate an elementary school and the necessary parking spaces, drop-off area, and bus loop.  All athletic fields on an adjacent parcel will be constructed and owned by Baltimore County. 

 

This exhibit is to request Board approval to move forward with the design and construction of a new elementary school on the Joppa Road site.
