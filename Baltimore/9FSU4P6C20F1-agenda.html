L. OLD BUSINESS
Subject
1. Consideration of the Proposed FY 2015 Operating Budget (Ms. Burnopp)
Meeting
Feb 11, 2014 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
L. OLD BUSINESS
Type
Action (Consent)

The superintendent's proposed FY2015 budget was introduced to the Board on January 14, 2014.  A public hearing was held on January 23, 2014.  The Board held a budget work session on January 28, 2014.  Once approved, the budget will be forwarded to the county executive before March 1, 2014.  The superintendent's proposed budget is based on a projected 3.7% increase of approximately $21.2 million in state funding.  Local county revenues are proposed to increase 5.7%  over Maintenance of Effort.  Following the Board's budget work session, the superintendent has requested that 4.0 FTE positions, a building operations supervisor and three building service workers, be added to the FY2015 operating budget to provide for the operations and maintenance of the reconfigured charter school.  Funds that are budgeted for contractual services are adequate to provide for this cost; however, the total proposed general fund FTEs will increase from 13,449.7 to 13,453.7.

 

The total budget for all funds is $1,771,929,823.  It includes $945,905,352 in local funding from Baltimore County, $697,995,463 in state funds, $92,158,926 in federal grants and contributions, and $35,870,082 in funds from service charges and other sources.

2. Consideration of the FY14 Capital Budget Supplement (Ms. Burnopp)
Meeting
Feb 11, 2014 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
L. OLD BUSINESS
Type
Action (Consent)

That the Board of Education approves a revised FY 2014 supplemental appropriation to the capital budget of $17,244,721 from Baltimore County government and $11,715,000 from the state of Maryland.  The funds will be used to install air conditioning at Parkville Middle School, and Featherbed Lane, Scotts Branch, Wellwood International, and Hawthorne elementary schools.  Baltimore County funds are derived from a debt premium generated from a December 12, 2012, bond sale and designed for air conditioning improvements to the aforementioned Baltimore County Public Schools on September 3, 2013, by the county council in Bill No. 47-13.  State of Maryland funds are derived from the Air Conditioning Initiative of the FY 2014 Capital Improvement Program and approved by the Board of Public Works on August 21, 2013, for air conditioning projects at the same five county schools.

In accordance with Superintendent’s Rule 7330, Pikesville High School is proposing the installation of a new marquee and electronic sign be approved.  This marquee and electronic sign is being constructed with donations from private donations made to the BCPS Education Foundation and funds from the Pikesville High School account.  The installation will be completed by Stewart Signs
