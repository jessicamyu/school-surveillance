K. NEW BUSINESS - FY2021 SPECIAL REVENUE SUPPLEMENTAL APPROPRIATION OF GRANT AWARDS (Dr. Scriven and Mr. Sarris) (8:15 - 8:30 p.m.)
Subject
1. Consideration of the FY2021 Special Revenue Supplemental Appropriation of Grant Awards
Meeting
May 18, 2021 - HYBRID WORK SESSION OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
K. NEW BUSINESS - FY2021 SPECIAL REVENUE SUPPLEMENTAL APPROPRIATION OF GRANT AWARDS (Dr. Scriven and Mr. Sarris) (8:15 - 8:30 p.m.)
Type
Action

Each year, the budget is amended when funds are transferred between MSDE activities. The categories established by MSDE each represent an appropriation of funds for specific purposes. Legislative bodies must appropriate, or authorize, the use of funds by law. Infrequently, unanticipated revenues are provided, often by the federal government, to local education agencies. This occurred during the 2008 financial crisis when ARRA funds were provided to sustain LEAs when tax revenues eroded. Another such event, the global pandemic, brought about extraordinary federal action by two administrations, to provide four separate grants of additional funds to state and local agencies. As we have discussed throughout the year, CARES Act revenues to date total over $150 million. These funds were not known or anticipated on March 1, 2020, when the FY2021 budget was forwarded to the County Executive. The first grant award was made June 26, 2020, and three successive grants followed through the summer and fall of 2020. The FY2021 budget must now be amended to reflect the availability and use of these funds.

 

The special revenue budget has always included an allowance of approximately $5 million for undesignated grants that may be awarded during the upcoming fiscal year. This process has worked well. During FY2021, the above-referenced emergent federal grants awarded in response to the global pandemic exceeded the value of undesignated grant awards.

 

To comply with accepted governmental accounting principles and state and local regulations, it is necessary to amend the FY2021 adopted special revenue budget to reflect these grant awards. The last special revenue supplemental budget appropriation was presented in 2015 to appropriate a larger than anticipated Title I grant award.

 

An operating budget supplemental appropriation of special revenue grant funds in the amount of $54,529,564 is attached for approval by the Board of Education. This supplement includes funds for the following grants awarded after the FY2021 budget was finalized.

  • $19,245,933 is from the Elementary and Secondary Schools Emergency Relief (ESSER) Fund.
  • $10,530,577 is from the Coronavirus Relief Fund – Technology.
  • $12,812,697 is from the Coronavirus Relief Fund – Tutoring.
  • $1,288,489 is from the Governor's Emergency Relief Fund (GEER).
  • $750,000 is from the Governor's Office of Rural Broadband.
  • $667,629 is related to carryover of the Blueprint Concentration of Poverty (COP) grant.
  • $375,000 is from the Baltimore County Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act grant (WIOA).
  • $275,601 is related to the Infants and Toddlers Blueprint grant.
  • $1,995,078 is related to the Special Education Passthrough grant.
  • $6,500,000 is related to Title I carry over funds.
  • $88,560 is from the MABE Risk Management grant.
