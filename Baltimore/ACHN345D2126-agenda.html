J. NEW BUSINESS - OBSERVANCE OF EID AL-ADHA AND EID AL-FITR (Ms. Williams)
Subject
1. Consideration of Eid Al-Adha and Eid Al-Fitr
Meeting
Aug 23, 2016 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
J. NEW BUSINESS - OBSERVANCE OF EID AL-ADHA AND EID AL-FITR (Ms. Williams)
Type
Action

The Policy Review Committee’s (PRC) July 2015 Report on the School Calendar, recommended that, whenever possible, a professional development day should be scheduled and schools should be closed to students when Eid Al-Adha or Eid Al-Fitr occurred on school days.  That recommendation was adopted by the Board.  PRC has reconsidered that recommendation and is now asking the Board to adopt the following:  Baltimore County Public Schools and Offices will be closed on Eid Al-Adha and Eid Al-Fitr.

L. NEW BUSINESS - FY2017 CAPITAL BUDGET SUPPLEMENTAL APPROPRIATION (Mr. Sarris)
Subject
1. FY2017 Capital Budget Supplemental Appropriation
Meeting
Aug 23, 2016 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
L. NEW BUSINESS - FY2017 CAPITAL BUDGET SUPPLEMENTAL APPROPRIATION (Mr. Sarris)
Type
Action

That the Board of Education approves the FY2017 supplemental appropriation to the capital budget of $83,512,500 from Baltimore County government.  The total is a combination of $38,940,000 in county general funds and $44,572,500 in anticipated state aid for School Air Conditioning Projects that are being “forward-funded” by Baltimore County.  The $38,940,000 is comprised of $20,000,000 of unassigned reserve funds from BCPS fund balance and $18,940,000 from Baltimore County general fund unappropriated balance.  The $20,000,000 transfer from BCPS fund balance to Baltimore County was approved by the Board on July 12, 2016. 

 

The funds will be used to install air conditioning at Battle Grove, Charlesmont, Orems, and Reisterstown elementary schools; Church Lane Elementary Technology; Arbutus, Golden Ring, Middle River, and Stemmers Run middle schools; Southwest Academy; and Franklin and Kenwood high schools.  Baltimore County funds are derived from a combination of county general funds and forward-funded state funds.  The Baltimore County Council approved this supplemental appropriation on August 1, 2016, in Bill No. 47-16.
