J. UNFINISHED BUSINESS - CONSIDERATION OF THE 2019-2020 SCHOOL CALENDAR (Dr. Mayo, Mr. Duque) (7:55 - 8:15 p.m.)
Subject
1. Consideration of the 2019-2020 Revised School Calendar
Meeting
Jun 11, 2019 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
J. UNFINISHED BUSINESS - CONSIDERATION OF THE 2019-2020 SCHOOL CALENDAR (Dr. Mayo, Mr. Duque) (7:55 - 8:15 p.m.)
Type
Action (Consent)

After reviewing the calendars for the pre and post-Labor Day starts, BCPS was able to make alterations to the post-Labor Day calendar that would be more aligned to the calendar that was previously approved by the Board in March 2019.  With the revisions, some of the slated emergency/inclement weather closure days will have to be approved by the Maryland State Board of Education. The inclement weather days are built into the calendar in the following manner:

 

  • Emergency/Inclement Weather Closure #1 – President’s Day (February 17, 2020)

    • Requires approval from the Maryland State Board of Education since a state-mandated holiday

  • Emergency/Inclement Weather Closure #2 – April 13, 2020

    • Requires approval from the Maryland State Board of Education since a state-mandated holiday

  • Emergency/Inclement Weather Closure #3 – June 17, 2020

  • Emergency/Inclement Weather Closure #4 – June 18, 2020

  • Emergency/Inclement Weather Closure #5 – June 19, 2020

     

    The revised calendar equates to a 16.5 hours balance at the high school level for delayed openings and/or early releases due to emergencies and/or inclement weather when factoring in the two emergency closures in February and April being approved by the Maryland State Board of Education.

     

    As a result, the board will have three different options to consider for the 2019-2020 school year – two post-Labor Day calendars (one with a week-long spring break, when counting weekends equates to ten calendar days, and one with six calendar days for spring break) and one pre-Labor Day calendar with a full spring break equating to ten calendar days.

On May 7, 2019, the Board of Education of Baltimore County (Board) received for consideration recommendations from the Dogwood Relief Study and Johnnycake Relief Study Committees for boundary changes at four southwest elementary schools. The Dogwood Relief Study Committee recommended Option 2, which affects the boundaries for Dogwood and Featherbed Lane elementary schools.

 

The Johnnycake Relief Study Committee recommended Option 4, which affects the boundaries for Johnnycake and Edmondson Heights elementary schools.

 

The Board public hearing on the recommended boundary changes was held on May 15, 2019, at Woodlawn High School. The Board will vote on the recommendations at the June 11, 2019, Board meeting.

The November 6, 2018, bond referendum was approved by the electorate for the construction of elementary and middle schools to continue Baltimore County's aggressive Schools for Our Future program. The funding represents the county’s capital contribution for approved projects and allows BCPS to issue the necessary purchase orders to construction contractors prior to July 2019. The specific capital project and schools impacted by the supplemental appropriation include the construction of a new middle school on the Nottingham site; the Pine Grove Middle School addition/renovation project; the replacement of Berkshire, Chadwick, Colgate, and Dundalk elementary schools; and the construction of a new elementary school on the Ridge Road site.

 

The Baltimore County Council approved this supplemental appropriation on November 19, 2018, in Bill No. 83-18.

2. Consideration of the Privately Funded Capital Project - Towson High School Bleachers
Meeting
Jun 11, 2019 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
S. NEW BUSINESS - PRIVATELY FUNDED CAPITAL PROJECTS (Ms. Byers) (9:15 - 9:25 p.m.)

The Towson High School Sports Boosters Club received a donation from the Towson High School Class of 1954, as well as additional funds from a Capital Projects Grant from the State of Maryland General Assembly, to purchase and install bleachers.  Bleacher seating is currently limited in the Towson High School Stadium, and crowded conditions do not provide an optimal or safe seating environment.  The current seating does not accommodate the Towson High Marching Band and supporters of visiting teams.  The additional bleachers would be installed on a concrete bed adjacent to the current bleachers.  The new bleachers would add up to 165 new seats and will be ADA compliant. This expansion would have a useful life of several decades and would serve residents of the entire county beyond the Towson area.
