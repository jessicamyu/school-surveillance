Subject
1. Consideration of the Privately Funded Capital Project - Glyndon Elementary School Butterfly Garden
Meeting
Nov 10, 2020 - VIRTUAL MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
O. NEW BUSINESS - PRIVATELY FUNDED CAPITAL PROJECT - GLYNDON ELEMENTARY SCHOOL (Ms. Byers) (9:45 - 9:50 p.m.)

A Glyndon Elementary School parent/volunteer has donated funds for the creation of a butterfly garden.  The butterfly garden will offer students and the community the opportunity to experience nature on school grounds and to expand the classroom outdoors.  Glyndon Elementary School will join the Baltimore County Public Schools Outdoor Science Program.

P. NEW BUSINESS - PRIVATELY FUNDED CAPITAL PROJECT - PLEASANT PLAINS ELEMENTARY SCHOOL (Ms. Byers) (9:50 - 9:55 p.m.)
Subject
1. Consideration of the Privately Funded Capital Project - Pleasant Plains Elementary School - Tree Planting
Meeting
Nov 10, 2020 - VIRTUAL MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
P. NEW BUSINESS - PRIVATELY FUNDED CAPITAL PROJECT - PLEASANT PLAINS ELEMENTARY SCHOOL (Ms. Byers) (9:50 - 9:55 p.m.)
Type
Action

Blue Water Baltimore is being paid by the Baltimore County Department of Environmental Protection and Sustainability (EPS) to plant 37 trees at Pleasant Plains Elementary School. Trees will be planted around the school to reduce the environmental impact of the school to the watershed, reduce heating and cooling costs, and filter runoff.  The trees will provide students with shade and quieter places to play, add beauty to the school campus, improve wildlife habitat, and engage students in the planting process.
