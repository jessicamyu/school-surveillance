O. NEW BUSINESS - REVISED SCHOOL CALENDAR FOR 2016-2017 (Dr. Mayo/Mr. Duque)
Subject
1. Consideration of Revised School Calendar for 2016-2017
Meeting
Jun 14, 2016 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
O. NEW BUSINESS - REVISED SCHOOL CALENDAR FOR 2016-2017 (Dr. Mayo/Mr. Duque)
Type
Action (Consent)

A revised calendar for the 2016-2017 school year (Option 1) is submitted for Board review and approval.  The revision was prompted by MSDE changes to the 2016-2017 student testing calendar.  The new MSDE calendar now lists the observance of the Muslim holiday Eid-al Adha as Monday, September 12, 2016.  In keeping with Board recommendations on the school calendar, September 12, 2016, will now become a Professional Development day for teachers with all schools closed for students. Other minor adjustments were also made, and all adjustments are highlighted.

 

Also, in anticipation of the Board’s new policy on heat and air conditioning and the possible lowering of the heat index used to determine school closures, an Option 2 proposed calendar is provided for the Board's consideration. 

 

Based on a review of the past three academic years, it was determined that 3 to 4 additional closure days would have been needed if the proposed new heat index had been applied.   Option 2 provides a means to recuperate student days should heat related closures occur at the beginning of the academic year. 

 

The contingency in Option 2 calls for delaying the start of spring break by two (2) days should four (4) or more emergency closure days be used by March 1, 2017.  Should it be necessary to implement this contingency and BCPS uses all six (6) emergency closure days built into the calendar, Option 2 would: (1) recoup two (2) student days; (2) fulfill MSDE’s requirement to demonstrate that we have made sufficient effort in providing instruction through calendar planning and modifications; and, if needed, (3) allow BCPS to apply for and be favorably considered for a waiver of the 180-day year requirement in accordance with COMAR 13A.02.01.04.

Blueprint 2.0: Performance Report 2016

Executive Summary

 

The Blueprint 2.0: Performance Report 2016 highlights BCPS’ progress against the system strategic plan, Blueprint 2.0: Our Way Forward from 2012 through 2015. An overview of and status update on systemic initiatives to date in the goal areas of Academics, Safety and Security, Communication, and Organizational Effectiveness is provided. Systemic successes will be highlighted and opportunities for continuous improvement will be shared.

 

In the goal area of Academics, our two theories of action — S.T.A.T. (Students and Teachers Accessing Tomorrow) and Passport Program (second language acquisition)–are transforming teaching and learning in classrooms across BCPS. During this past year, BCPS’ graduation rates have continued to increase. The 2015 graduation rate of 87.8% represents an increase of 4 percentage points since 2012.

 

Safety and Security enhancements continue across schools and offices. These efforts are designed to support our World-Class Teaching and Learning Environments and include a robust safety planning and practice schedule, and a building access and monitoring plan that is nearing completion. Currently, 100% of schools have emergency plans and 100% of buildings have buzzer systems and closed-circuit television systems.

 

In the goal area of Communication, BCPS continues to engage a wide range of stakeholders through its ongoing engagement efforts. The office of Family and Community Engagement (FACE) continues to provide critical information to stakeholders. In 2015, 60% of community members agreed that communication is open, timely, and clear, an increase from 49% in 2014. In addition, in 2015, 81% of parents agreed that information and results data are available and easily accessible.

 

Organizational Effectiveness also continues to improve. The core work in this goal area is based in two areas: performance management and organizational efficiencies (e.g., fiscal resource management and transportation efficiencies). BCPS’ efforts are evident among stakeholders and their perceptions of system effectiveness. Parental perception of BCPS’ effectiveness has increased from 80% in 2014 to 87% in 2015.
