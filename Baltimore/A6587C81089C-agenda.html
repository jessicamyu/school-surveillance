M. OLD BUSINESS - PROPOSED FY17 OPERATING BUDGET (Mr. Sarris)
Subject
1. Consideration of the Proposed FY17 Operating Budget
Meeting
Feb 2, 2016 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
M. OLD BUSINESS - PROPOSED FY17 OPERATING BUDGET (Mr. Sarris)
Type
Action

The superintendent’s proposed FY2017 operating budget was introduced to the Board on January 5, 2016.  A public hearing was held on January 12, 2016.  The Board held a public budget work session on January 19, 2016.  Once approved by the Board, the budget will be forwarded to the county executive on or before March 1, 2016. 

 

Preliminary calculations for major state aid programs had not yet been released by the Maryland State Department of Education at the time the proposed FY2017 budget was developed.  However, state aid to education is projected to increase $23.1 million over FY2016.  The superintendent’s proposed general fund budget is proposed to increase 4.3% over FY2016.  The budget includes $10,000,000 for school air conditioning and $182,214 for an additional Board of Education internal auditor and administrative assistant, all of which were added to the proposed budget by the Board at the January 19, 2016, public work session.  The proposed budget for all funds increases 3.3% over FY2016.  Local county revenues are proposed to increase 2.2% over Maintenance of Effort.

 

The total budget for all funds is $1,827,045,667.  It includes $949,708,327 in local funding from Baltimore County, $713,307,622 in state funds, $114,395,116 in federal grants and contributions, and $49,634,602 in funds from service charges and other sources.

Q. NEW BUSINESS - REPORT ON SOUTHWEST AREA ELEMENTARY SCHOOL BOUNDARY STUDY RECOMMENDATION (first reading) (Ms. Miller/Dr. Wheatley-Phillip/Mr. Cropper, Cropper GIS)
Subject
1. Report on Southwest Area Elementary School Boundary Study Recommendation
Meeting
Feb 2, 2016 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
Q. NEW BUSINESS - REPORT ON SOUTHWEST AREA ELEMENTARY SCHOOL BOUNDARY STUDY RECOMMENDATION (first reading) (Ms. Miller/Dr. Wheatley-Phillip/Mr. Cropper, Cropper GIS)
Type
Report

That the Board reviews the scenario 3.2b (revised) which was recommended by the Boundary Study Committee.  While the capital projects in the Southwest Area provide an additional 954 seats, there is still a need for seats in the future. 

  • Although relief is provided for Hillcrest Elementary School, with the recommended scenario, Hillcrest Elementary School is at 100% capacity.  It is anticipated that in approximately three to five years, the boundary for Hillcrest may need to be reviewed. 
  • The recommended scenario provides no relief for Johnnycake Elementary School.  We will be working with our funding authorities to address the capacity needs of that school and surrounding schools.
