J. OLD BUSINESS - CLOSING OF SCHOLARS K-8 (Ms. Blannard)
Subject
1. Consideration of the Proposed Closing of Scholars K-8
Meeting
Oct 6, 2015 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
J. OLD BUSINESS - CLOSING OF SCHOLARS K-8 (Ms. Blannard)
Type
Action

In accordance with Board Policy 1290, at the September 1, 2015, Board of Education meeting, Ms. Blannard presented the superintendent’s recommendation to close Scholars K-8 School at the conclusion of the 2015-2016 school year.  Attached is Ms. Blannard's September 1, 2015, report to the Board.

 

As required by COMAR 13A.02.09.01 and Board Policy 1290, the impact of the proposed closing was evaluated using the following factors: student enrollment trends; age or condition of the school building; student transportation; educational programs; racial composition of the student body; financial considerations; student relocation; and the impact on the community in the geographic attendance area for the school proposed to be closed, and for schools to which the students will be relocated.

 

As required by Superintendent’s Rule 1290, the staff, PTA and Scholars community were informed on August 18, 2015, of the superintendent’s recommendation and of this proposal.

 

On September 1, 2015, the first reading of the superintendent’s recommendation to close Scholars K-8 was presented to the Board of Education.  The Board of Education convened a public hearing on September 21, 2015, to permit public testimony by interested citizens.

 

Having reviewed all of the previous requisite factors and having carefully examined the educational benefit to students, the superintendent hereby recommends that the Board of Education close Scholars K-8 at the end of the 2015-2016 school year.

This special project request is for the replacement of the old kiln with a new kiln at Rodgers Forge Elementary School.  Replacing this kiln will benefit students by expediting the time that they are able to paint their sculptures, which have been fired.  The current kiln is too small to fit the work of one class.
