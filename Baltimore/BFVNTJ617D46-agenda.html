Subject
1. Consideration of the Privately Funded Capital Project - George Washington Carver Center for Arts and Technology - Bleachers
Meeting
Oct 8, 2019 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
M. NEW BUSINESS - PRIVATELY FUNDED CAPITAL PROJECT (Ms. Byers) (8:15 - 8:20 p.m.)
Type
Action

The Carver Foundation donated funds to purchase and install bleachers at the Carver Center Stadium to provide seating for spectators at athletic events. The remaining funds will come from the school Athletics account and additional donations made by previous graduating classes. When the stadium was built, a space was designated and a concrete pad was installed for the future installation of bleachers. The existing concrete pad can accommodate two sets of bleachers and provide the required ADA spaces adjacent to the bleachers. The addition of bleachers at the stadium will provide seating for spectators during school athletic events. The community at large will also benefit with improved facilities for recreation programs.

P. NEW BUSINESS - REPORT ON WATER TESTING (Mr. Smith, Mr. Dixit) (8:55 - 9:05 p.m.)
Subject
1. Report on Water Testing
Meeting
Oct 8, 2019 - MEETING OF THE BOARD OF EDUCATION OF BALTIMORE COUNTY, MARYLAND
Category
P. NEW BUSINESS - REPORT ON WATER TESTING (Mr. Smith, Mr. Dixit) (8:55 - 9:05 p.m.)
Type
Discussion

The Clean Water Act of 1974 requires municipal water supply systems to meet federal quality standards. In 2018, the state of Maryland issued regulations requiring sampling for lead in water from all potential drinking sources in schools served by municipal water supply systems.

 

BCPS developed a communication's protocol which explained the process and posted results on the BCPS Web site. Communities were notified at the beginning of school year 2018-2019 that sampling was being conducted. Five-gallon water bottles on dispensers have been provided in all schools and are free for students and staff.

 

Sampling and testing at schools have now been completed. Of those tested, five (5) percent or 380 devices needed to be replaced. To date, 330 devices have been replaced with the remaining 50 to be replaced by the end of September 2019.  Resampling of the replacement devices will commence in October 2019 and is expected to be complete by the end of February 2020 with the results by the end of April 2020.

 

The Lead In Water Update includes details on individual schools.
