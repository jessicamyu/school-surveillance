Executive Summary

Phase III BCPS Course Requests – Deletion of Courses

 

Mathematics

 

Approval is requested to terminate Algebraic Functions course and associated course number.  Algebraic Functions (20-21900) is the second half of the Algebra I course that was aligned to the Core Learning Goals for the High School Assessment (HSA).  Students now take a course that is aligned to the Algebra I Maryland College and Career Ready Standards.

 

Social Studies

 

Approval is requested to terminate Global Literacy Mag MS6 and Economic Literacy Mag MS7 courses and associated course numbers.  Both Global and Economic Literacy were developed to support the magnet program at Deep Creek Middle School, which was the only school to have offered or implemented the course. These courses are no longer offered at Deep Creek Middle School. The principal reports that these courses have been replaced by course titles offered through the Office of Career and Technology.

 

Approval is requested to terminate Human Geography course and associated course number.  Human Geography was never offered in more than one school since 2000.  It was last implemented in 2007-2008.  Any school that offered this course in the past now offers Advanced Placement (AP) Human Geography instead.  Offerings and enrollment in AP Human Geography is increasing, and more schools have expressed interest in implementation for 2015-2016. There is no demand for this course and students’ interest in geography is being satisfied by the AP option.

 

Approval is requested to terminate Government and Public Issues course and associated course number.  Government and Public Issues was once a graduation requirement.  It became an elective course when American Government was offered as a full-credit course in Grade 9, and Economics and Public Issues in Grade 12 was implemented to satisfy the graduation requirement for economics.  This course has not been offered in any school for 15 years and there is no interested in offering it in the future.  A significant portion of content within this course is available through American Government, Economics and Public Issues, Civil Rights and Civil Liberties, and International Relations.

 

Language Arts

 

Approval is requested to terminate the Language! course and associated course numbers.  Language! is a reading intervention program that will no longer be an approved program in 2015-16.   Schools will be able to offer reading at each grade level in middle school.  The following Reading course numbers are available:

            10-06100 – Reading MS6

            10-06105 – Reading GT MS6

            10-07100 – Reading MS7

            10-08100 – Reading MS8

Reading intervention services and research-based, approved programs may be used within all Reading courses.  A reading course should not specify a program name in the title.  The RTI (Response to Intervention) plan includes the use of a variety of research-based programs that meet the specific reading needs of our students.

 

Career and Technology Education

 

Approval is requested to terminate the Career and Technology Education program titled Engineering Technician program (CIP 14.0101) and all associated course numbers.  The program is offered exclusively at Sollers Point Technical High School.  The current Engineering Technician facilities will be used for the Project Lead The Way (PLTW) Engineering program at Dundalk High School.  PLTW Engineering has been implemented at Dundalk High to allow for vertical integration from the K-8 STEM program at Norwood-Holabird to Dundalk High to Community College of Baltimore County (CCBC) Dundalk campus.  Although no additional students are being accepted in the program, the students who are currently enrolled will be able to complete the program.  The last cohort of students will graduate in 2017-18; and the program will no longer be offered, starting in 2018-19.

Report on Blueprint 2.0

Executive Summary

 

The Report on Blueprint 2.0 highlights BCPS’ progress against the system strategic plan, Blueprint 2.0: Our Way Forward. An overview of and status update on systemic initiatives to date in the goal areas of Academics, Safety and Security, Communication, and Organizational Effectiveness is provided. In addition, goal area data which highlight systemic successes and opportunities for continuous improvement will be shared.

 

In the goal area of Academics, our two theories of action — S.T.A.T. (Students and Teachers Accessing Tomorrow) and ¡Todos Adelante! (second language acquisition)–are transforming teaching and learning in classrooms across BCPS. During this past year, BCPS’ graduation rates have continued to increase. The current BCPS’ graduation rate of 87.6% is an increase of 1.3 percentage points since last year and 5.8 percentage points since 2011.

 

Safety and Security enhancements continue across schools and offices. BCPS has a number of ongoing initiatives designed to support our World-Class Teaching and Learning Environments including a robust safety planning and practice schedule, and a building access and monitoring plan that is nearing completion. Currently, 100% of buildings have buzzer systems and closed-circuit television systems.

 

In the goal area of Communication, BCPS continues to engage a wide range of stakeholders. For example, last year, BCPS successfully launched Parent University. Currently, 60% of community members agree that communication is open, timely, and clear, an increase from 40% in 2014.

 

Organizational Effectiveness continues to improve. The core of the work in this focal area is based in two areas: performance management and organizational efficiencies and BCPS’ efforts are evident among stakeholders. Parental perception of BCPS’ effectiveness has increased from 80% in 2014 to 87% in 2015, even as the number of parent respondents has doubled.
