G.01. Public Hearing and Resolution in the Matter of Certification of Sufficient Textbooks and Instructional Materials [THIS ITEM WILL NOT BE HEARD BY THE BOARD BEFORE 5:30 P.M.] (Board Governance Policy: OE-10)

Oct 27, 2009 - 10/27/2009

Budget Source
State Instructional Materials Funds
RECOMMENDATION: Conduct Public Hearing and approve the attached Resolution in the Matter of Certification of Sufficient Textbooks or Instructional Materials indicating the district has sufficient and available textbooks and instructional materials as defined in Education Code Section 60119 for the 2009-10 school year.

FISCAL IMPACT: In order to be eligible to receive funds for the purpose of providing sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials, the governing board of a school district must hold a public hearing and make a determination that each pupil in each school in the district has sufficient textbooks or instructional materials on or before the eighth week of school pursuant to Education Code Section 60119. The district uses funding from various sources, including state Instructional Materials Funding Realignment Program (IMFRP) funds and state lottery funds to purchase textbooks and instructional materials according to the state adoption cycle. Newly adopted materials are provided based on actual enrollments the first year of an adoption. Schools are responsible for maintaining sufficiency during the remaining years of the adoption and are provided funding to support the purchase of instructional materials needed due to growth in student numbers or replacement of lost and/or damaged materials.

BACKGROUND: In accordance with Education Code 60119, each school district’s governing board must hold a public hearing no later than the eighth week of the school year to determine, through a resolution, whether each pupil in the district has sufficient textbooks or instructional materials, or both in the following subjects: mathematics, science, history-social science, and English/language arts. The governing board must also determine whether each student enrolled in a foreign language or health course has been provided sufficient textbooks or instructional materials. According to Williams Legislation, sufficient textbooks or instructional materials means that each pupil, including English learners, must have a district-adopted textbook or instructional materials, or both, to use in class and to take home. Access to two sets of instructional materials is not required.

Current Williams legislation also requires that science classrooms have available basic science laboratory equipment for students in science laboratory courses offered in Grades 9 to 12. The Science Department developed basic core instructional materials lists for Biology, Chemistry, Earth Science, and Physics classrooms. All schools conducted inventories and principals signed and submitted Science Equipment Sufficiency Certifications.

Williams Settlement Legislation forced districts and schools to closely examine student access to textbooks and instructional materials and gather evidence to use as the basis of our annual resolution of sufficiency. Administrative Circulars 19, 20, and 21, dated September 11, 2009, were posted notifying school sites of the need to conduct a school site inventory and complete a “Principal Certification of Textbook Sufficiency.” Teacher Certification of Textbook Sufficiency forms were included for completion by classroom teachers and returned to principals by September 18, 2009. After completing the inventory and taking action to remedy insufficiencies, each principal signed the Principal Certification of Textbook Sufficiency form and returned it to the Instructional Materials Office by September 25, 2009. In the event of insufficiencies, an addendum was faxed to principals for signature, verifying the textbooks ordered to achieve sufficiency had been received and the school was indeed sufficient. Also, between the 2008-09 through the 2012-13 fiscal years, sufficient textbooks or instructional materials means that all students enrolled in the same course within the district have standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials from the same adoption cycle.

Additionally, 21 elementary schools, four K-8 schools, five middle schools, and 17 senior high schools were visited by a team from San Diego County Office of Education. All 47 schools were found to have sufficient instructional materials. All 17 senior high schools were found to have adequate laboratory equipment for the science laboratory courses offered.

Staff and Public Support and Engagement: Pursuant to Education Code Section 60422, ten days notice of the public hearing was posted containing the time, place, and purpose of the hearing.

RECOMMENDATION: Conduct Public Hearing and approve the attached Resolution in the Matter of Certification of Provision of Standards-Aligned Instructional Materials indicating the district has provided each pupil with a standards-aligned textbook or basic instructional materials in the four core academic areas.

FISCAL IMPACT: In order to be eligible to receive funds for the purpose of providing sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials, the governing board of a school district must hold a public hearing and make a determination that each pupil in each school in the district has sufficient textbooks or instructional materials on or before the eighth week of school pursuant to Education Code Section 60119. The district uses funding from various sources, including state funds from the Instructional Materials Funding Realignment Program (IMFRP) and state lottery funds to purchase textbooks and instructional materials according to the state and local adoption cycles. Once the provision of standards-aligned instructional materials in the four core academic subject areas is determined through this resolution, IMFRP funding typically may be spent on other instructional materials pursuant to Education Code 60242. Education Code 42605 provides further flexibility with the use of these funds for any educational purpose for the 2008-09 fiscal year to the 2012-13 fiscal year, inclusive.

BACKGROUND: AB 1781 established the Instructional Materials Funding Realignment Program (IMFRP) that, starting with the 2002-03 fiscal year, combined three previous funding sources for instructional materials. The program requires that districts must use funds to ensure each pupil, K-12, is provided with a standards-aligned textbook or basic instructional materials in the four core subject areas: English language arts, mathematics; science; and history/social science. Additionally, in accordance with state regulations, the governing board must also certify the standards-aligned basic instructional materials are provided by the beginning of the school term that starts no later than 24 months after those materials were adopted. Education Code 60422.1 waives this 24 month requirement for the 2008-09 to the 2012-13 fiscal years, inclusively. The governing board of a school district is not required to provide pupils with instructional materials by a specified period of time following adoption of those materials by the state board. This does not relieve school districts of their obligations to provide every pupil with standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials, per Education Code 1240.3. Once the governing board certifies it has provided each pupil with standards-aligned instructional materials, the district may use 100 per cent of any remaining IMFRP funds to purchase other instructional materials consistent with the content and cycles of the curriculum frameworks. Education Code 42605 provides further flexibility with the use of these funds for any educational purpose for the 2008-09 fiscal year to the 2012-13 fiscal year, inclusive.

For students in Kindergarten through Grade 8, the instructional materials were purchased from an approved standards-aligned state adoption list as required by CCR, Title 5, Section 9531. In addition, on May 12, 2009, the board adopted new standards-aligned instructional materials for Grades 9-12 in English language arts, mathematics, science and world languages and these instructional materials were provided to each pupil in these classes by September 8, 2009, which is the start of the first school term.

Staff and Public Support and Engagement: Pursuant to Education Code Section 60422, ten days notice of the public hearing was posted containing the time, place, and purpose of the hearing.
