RENEWAL VERSUS REVISION

 

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.

 

Within a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, special education status, expansion of grade levels, or other significant revision.

 

CONCLUSION

 

Staff recommends the board grant the proposed revision to the charter of empower Charter School to change the name of the school to Empower Language Academy, effective July 1, 2020.

Financial Review

 

District staff reviewed and analyzed the 2019-20 budget submitted by HSHMC.  Revenues presented are within range of district’s staff calculations with the exception of budgeted local revenue which was conservatively budgeted compared to prior years’ revenues received.  HSHMC’s budgeted expenditures have also been examined and adjustments were made for expenses that were understated as compared to its three-year actual average.  The leased space located at 4823 El Cajon Boulevard has a monthly base rent of $5,540 ($66,481 annually).

 

Based on the adjusted budget and expenses, HSHMC is financially viable.  The school meets the district’s minimum reserve requirement of 3% of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater.  At the end of 2018-19, per the school’s unaudited actuals report, the ending fund balance was approximately $5,378,777, which exceeded the reserve requirement.

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.

 

Within a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, special education status, expansion of grade levels, or other significant revision.
