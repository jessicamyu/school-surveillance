1. 2020-21 School Plans for Student Achievement (SPSAs) and Categorical Budget Summaries for 160 Schools; BUDGETED: N/A

Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the proposed 2020-21 School Plans for Student Achievement (SPSAs) and Categorical Budget Summaries for 160 schools.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Each school's School Site Council (SSC) worked with its site instructional leadership team and district staff to develop, implement, monitor, and evaluate its SPSA. Site principals, SSC chairpersons, SSC members, and the District Advisory Council (DAC) for Compensatory Education received various training regarding specific plan requirements. Specifically, the DAC received information and provided feedback on the timeline and components of developing the SPSA beginning with their January and February 2020 meetings: January 29 (Take to Your SSCs), February 19 (SPSA Update).

 

BACKGROUND: In accordance with California Ed Code 64001, the state holds each district school accountable for developing and implementing a School Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA). The SPSA identifies the instructional needs of students and specifies how school funding, including categorical funds, provided through the Consolidated Application, will be used to accomplish the goals outlined in the plan. The SPSA is developed or modified through an annual planning cycle. SPSAs are created under this part may serve as school improvement plans required under federal law for schools identified for targeted support as described in Section 1003(e)(1)(A) of the federal Every Student Succeed Act (Public Law 114-95) or comprehensive support as described in Section 1003(e)(1)(B) of the federal Every Student Succeeds Act.

 

Additionally, Strategic Planning for Student Achievement Department staff provided individual support to principals and site teams to discuss expenditures and the SPSA development process. Sites then developed their SPSAs, approved by their SSCs, and submitted them to their area superintendent for review. Area superintendents reviewed plans to ensure schools identified student needs and aligned their goals with appropriate courses of action to address those needs. Once approved by the area superintendents, these schools' SPSAs were forwarded to the Strategic Planning for Student Achievement Department where staff reviewed each plan and provided individual assistance to schools to ensure that their SPSAs met state requirements and that the plans and associated categorical budget allocations were in alignment. The local governing board must review and approve each school's SPSA. This report brings forward for board approval the 2020-21 SPSAs and categorical budget summaries for 160 schools.

 

Area superintendents review site plans during visits to schools and discuss progress with principals as determined by the measurable outcomes. In addition, Strategic Planning for Student Achievement Department resource teachers monitor progress made, and work with area superintendents and principals to evaluate the plans.

 

Electronic copies of each 2020-21 SPSA, which include the budget summaries and required, signed Recommendations and Assurances pages are attached to this agenda item; hard copies are available in the Board Services Offices, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231 San Diego, CA 92103.

  • Discover the what, why, and how of proficiency scale development. 
  • Investigate how to create, refine, or be a consumer of quality classroom-based assessments.
  • Explore ways to figure meaningful grades.
  • Examine approaches for grading exceptional learners.
  • Discuss implementation at school sites and district.
