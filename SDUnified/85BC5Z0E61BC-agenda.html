D. DISTRICT OPERATIONS MONITORING
Subject
D.01. Informational Budget Briefing and Proposed Monthly Financial Managment Report Template (Board Governance Policy: OE-5; OE-6)
Meeting
Nov 19, 2008 - Special Meeting
Type
Information
The Chief Financial Officer will provide an update on the state adopted budget signed by the Governor on September, 23, 2008. This will include the impacts for the district’s 2008-2009 budgets.

For the district, these changes include $4.1 million for COLA on the revenue limit, $2.6 million for class size reduction, and $9.6 million for categorical programs (eliminating the 6.5% reduction). Special Education remained flat with no COLA adjustment.

Our revenue limit amount could change based on our small increase in ADA for the start of the new school year. The district’s June, 2008, adopted budget included $3 million in mandated cost revenue, which was based on the Governor’s original proposal to fund at $150 million. However, the mandate funding was ultimately reduced to $1,000 per mandate for the entire state system, resulting in an unanticipated loss to the district.

For our categorical programs the district will receive approximately $9 million more over its adopted budget. This includes an additional amount of approximately $4 million for the Targeted Instructional Improvement Block Grant, $1 million more for Economic Impact Aid and an additional $527 thousand for the Master Plan for Instructional Materials. These amounts could change slightly if the state applies the negative growth percentage (-0.52%), based on the overall decline in state enrollment.

The goal of the Financial Operations Division is to provide a financial management report on a monthly basis. This will provide a more transparent organization and an improved understanding of the district's financial condition.

D. DISTRICT OPERATIONS MONITORING
Subject
D.02. Contract Processes (Board Governance Policy: OE-6; OE-7; OE-13)
Meeting
Nov 19, 2008 - Special Meeting
Type
Information
The purpose of this item is to brief executive management and the board of education on the district contract processes.

The district serves 135,053 students and 9,000 teachers in 221 schools. The day to day operation of these schools requires the purchase and delivery of goods and services of all types. The operation repair and maintenance of these school facilities requires specialized contracting because of the rules and regulations found primarily in the Field Act (Title 24) and other related legislation such as the Public Contract and Education Codes which were enacted to ensure safe facilities for students and teachers. This function is provided by the Facilities Contracts and Construction Management (FCCM) Department, which is under Facilities Planning and Construction Department under the Chief Logistics Officer.

The purpose of the Procurement and Contracts (P&C) Department, which is under the Chief Financial Officer, is to provide districtwide acquisition of goods and services for a wide array of educational needs for many of the district departments, which support the teaching and learning process. This is accomplished using various conventionally accepted techniques and processes including competitive bids, request for proposals, professional services agreements, and formal and informal quotations.

These two departments accomplish their mission tasking by following many distinctly different laws and regulations. [Originator/Contact: Bill Kowba, Chief Logistics Officer, 619.725.8191, wkowba@sandi.net]
