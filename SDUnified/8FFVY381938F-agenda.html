RECOMMENDATION: Approve the material revision to the charter of Harriet Tubman Village Charter School (Tubman Charter School) to transition from its current status as an “arm-of-the-district” charter school to be operated by a nonprofit public benefit corporation, the Harriet Tubman Village Charter School, Inc., effective July 1, 2011 to June 30, 2014. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Tubman Charter School currently operates a K-8 educational program serving approximately 300 students.  Tubman Charter School is located on a district facility at 6880 Mohawk Street, San Diego, California 92115.

 

The district granted a charter to Tubman Charter School and recently renewed it on April 21, 2009.  Since that time, Tubman Charter School has taken steps to be operated by the Harriet Tubman Village Charter School, Inc., a nonprofit public benefit corporation.

 

The revised charter and related documents are attached as exhibits.

 

Pursuant to Education Code section 47604(c), the district, as the authority that granted a charter to the Tubman Charter School, shall not be liable for the charter school’s debts or obligations, or for claims arising from the charter school’s performance of acts, errors, or omissions, if the charter school is operated by, or as, a nonprofit public benefit corporation and the district complies with all oversight responsibilities required by law. 

 

Staff recommends the board approve the material revision to the charter of Tubman Charter School to transition from “arm-of-the-district” status to being operated by a nonprofit public benefit corporation, the Harriet Tubman Village Charter School, Inc., effective July 1, 2011 to June 30, 2014, the remainder of the term of its current charter.  Academic performance will be summarized and reviewed when Tubman Charter School seeks renewal of its charter in 2014.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve and implement those recommendations included in the Action Plan to Accelerate Achievement of African-American and African Students that are no cost items or cost neutral.  Staff will continue to explore options for funding of the cost related items and will report back to the Board when funding has been identified.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Implementation of the proposed action plan will require the services of teachers, counselors, administrators, Area Superintendents, Instructional Support Services, Race/Human Relations and Advocacy, Parent Involvement, Physical Education, Health, and Athletics, Special Education, the Office of Accountability, and Human Resource Services.

BACKGROUND:  On May 25, 2010, the San Diego County Association of African American Educators (AAAE) presented its Blueprint to Accelerate the Achievement of African American and African Students to the Board of Education. The board directed the Superintendent to convene a Task Force to review and provide input on the Blueprint.  The Blueprint included four Focus Areas:  Policies and Procedures, Graduation Rate, Gender-Specific Needs, and Parent/Family Support. Members of the Task force included district staff and community representatives.  A roster of the Task Force members is included as Attachment 2.

 

On November 9, 2010, the Board of Education was updated on the progress of the Task Force and approved the Blueprint in concept.  The Task Force continued to meet and develop recommendations that are included in the proposed Action Plan’s scope, actions related to the four Focus Areas, budgetary implications, prioritized implementation plan, and timeline to address the needs of our African American and African students.

 

The Action Plan to implement the Blueprint beginning in 2011-2012 includes strategies that focus specifically on activities designed for African-American and African students and their parents/guardians.  The attached documents delineate the work of the Task Force.  The recommendations make operational the concepts within each of the Focus Areas from the Blueprint to Accelerate the Achievement of African-American and African Students.

2. Direction to Staff to Expedite Recall of Staff Allowed by Changes in State Funding and Attrition (Evans)
Meeting
Jul 5, 2011 - REVISED Regular Charter and Prop S Update Meeting, 5:00 P.M. & Cultural Proficiency Workshop: 2:00 P.M.
Type
Action

 RECOMMENDATION: Direct staff to expedite recall of staff allowed by changes in state funding and attrition.

 

 

 At the 6/21/2011 Board of Education meeting a contingency plan was approved that would allow for the Superintendent to restore staff positions up to a value of $36 million.  It was contingent on a budget approved by the legislature and signed by the Governor that includes an increase in funding of $36 million above the amount calculated for the District budget approved on 6/28/2011.

 

It is recognized that the actual amount of funds that the District will receive is yet to be determined.  While there is an increase of $330 per pupil, there are changes and/or cuts in the areas of mental health, transportation and child care.  These reductions need to be calculated as soon as possible in order to determine the actual net increase in revenue.

 

Once the calculation is determined the staff needs to perform the recall of employees in restored positions as soon as possible, according to the Board action of 06/21/2011.  In the event that the funds are less than $36 million, the Superintendent needs to prepare a lesser plan of restoration that is consistent with the priorities set by the Board and the mission of the District.

 

It is the Board’s intention that recalls be performed as soon as possible for the following reasons:

  1. To inform those who are recalled, so that they are still able to return to their original positions before they make alternative plans.
  2. To allow employees who are not recalled to proceed with alternative plans, since it is quite clear that funds restored will not allow for recall of all employees.
  3. To allow families to make enrollment plans based on accurate information on class size and academic programs at their schools.

 

In addition to recalls related to positions restored, employees must also be recalled based upon separations (resignations or retirements) of employees in categories that have been reduced.  If there is a teacher who resigns for whom there is no available replacement (by credential) within the District, those positions should be assigned to a well-publicized list. This will ensure that no internal employee qualified for that slot is overlooked before there is a new hire.  It is the expectation of the Board that all recalls will be performed within the necessary time frame, so that recalled teachers will be able to return to their original position at their original site before they are replaced. Similarly, classified positions must be recalled in an expeditious manner. 

 

The intended result is that recalled teachers return to their original classrooms and that any additional teachers hired will only be due to the fact that no credentialed replacement exists within the ranks of the laid off teachers.  The Board recognizes that this is a complex and crucially important task for the Department of Human Resources and expects the Superintendent to provide the resources needed by the Interim Chief Human Resources Officer to carry out this task.
