4. Proposal for 2011-12 Administration of Small Schools and Update on 2011-12 Area Superintendent Recruitment
Meeting
Jun 2, 2011 - Special Budget Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action, Information

RECOMMENDATION: Review of proposal for 2011-12 administration of small schools:  The attached proposed operational plans for administration of Lincoln High School and the Crawford, San Diego, and Kearny High School Educational Complexes, were developed by school leadership, faculty, staff, and the parent communities with the goal of focusing educational programs to increase student achievement and operational efficiency while achieving budget savings.

 

Update on 2011-12 Area Superintendent Recruitment:   As a result of the continuing fiscal challenges facing the district, the board has proposed a reduction in the number of Area Superintendents overseeing the supervision of schools in the preliminary 2011-12 budget. Proposed organizational overviews and descriptions of duties were reviewed with board trustees on April 12, 2011 along with corresponding budget impacts.

 

Area superintendent positions have been posted and the recruitment process has begun. Community involvement will be solicited to paper-screen applicants.  A review committee comprised of representatives from San Diego State University (SDSU), San Diego County Office of Education (SDCOE), Administrators Association San Diego (AASD), San Diego Education Association (SDEA), and elementary and secondary principals will review qualified candidates.

 

The top candidates will be identified and six will be recommended to the board for appointment by July 1, 2011. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Small Schools - see attachment. 

 

Area Superintendents - the board-approved preliminary 2011-12 budget includes savings of approximately $2.5 million vs. the 2010-12 configuration of Area Superintendents and assistants. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: Small Schools - see attachment.

 

Area Superintendents - the board-approved preliminary 2011-12 budget includes savings of approximately $2.5 million vs. the 2010-11 configuration of Area Superintendents and assistants. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Small Schools - see attachment.

 

Area Superintendents - the board-approved preliminary 2011-12 budget includes six Area Superintendent positions vs. nine Area Superintendents and one Assistant Area Superintendent position in 2010-11.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  Small Schools - Plans for small schools at the San Diego and Crawford Complexes were presented to the board for information on May 25, 2010 (Item A.4.). 

 

Area Superintendents - The Area Superintendent organizational overview and description of duties were reviewed with the board on April 12, 2011. The overview included various organizational structures and budget impacts.
