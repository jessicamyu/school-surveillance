F.01. 2008 Accountability Progress Report (APR) for San Diego Unified School District (Board Governance Policy: OE-8; OE-9)
Meeting
Sep 23, 2008 - 09/23/2008
Type
Information
This report brings forward results from the 2008 Accountability Progress Reports (APR), that were publicly released on September 4, 2008, by the California Department of Education (CDE), for each school and district in the state.  The APR includes three components: Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) report, Academic Performance Index (API) Growth report, and Program Improvement report.  The AYP report provides data on the achievement of the district and schools with respect to federal No Child Left Behind (NCLB) targets. The API Growth report provides district and school results on 2008 API growth scores and attainment of 2008 state targets. The Program Improvement reports, applicable only to Title I schools and districts, provide information on schools that have not made AYP targets for two or more consecutive years and the federally required interventions. Eighth Amendment to Agreement with URS Corporation Americas (URS) effective July 10, 2001 through December 31, 2008 (C-6191).

    Pursuant to California Government Code §53060, on July 10, 2001, the district contracted with URS to provide a complete range of professional services on an “as required” basis related to implementation of the Proposition MM bond measure as an extension of district staff. To protect the district’s interest, it was determined that the agreement would be funded on a year-to-year basis in connection with planned projects over a five-year period. The total amount of the initial contract was $7 million with a term of one year and an option to extend four additional years at district’s election. On June 25, 2002, an amendment was approved to increase the consideration to a maximum of $16.5 million and extend the term through July 8, 2003. The board approved a second amendment on July 8, 2003, to increase the consideration to a maximum of $25.5 million and extend the term of the agreement through July 7, 2004, and on June 22, 2004, a third amendment was approved to extend the term through July 7, 2005, and provide for a maximum consideration of $34.3 million. A fourth amendment was authorized on February 22, 2005, which specifically increased the consideration to a maximum of $35.1 million. Subsequently, on June 21, 2005, the board approved a fifth amendment to bring the total consideration to a maximum of $44 million and extend the term through July 7, 2006. At the end of this five-year period, the district determined that the overall best value for the district would be to extend URS’ services beyond the initial five-year term to complete Proposition MM and transition into new projects rather than initiate a new solicitation for these services. Therefore, a sixth amendment was authorized on June 27, 2006, to increase the consideration to a maximum of $50.5 million and extend the term through July 7, 2007, and on December 12, 2006, a seventh amendment was approved to increase the consideration to a maximum of $56 million and extend the term through December 31, 2008.

    All funds have been committed, and it is requested that an additional $900,000 be authorized by the Board of Education to allow for completion of projects through the end of 2008. This final amendment is necessary to complete tasks in progress and provide the necessary project planning, in part, for the new general obligation bond (Proposition S) approved for placement on the November 2008 ballot. Attached hereto and made a part hereof by reference is a memorandum dated September 2, 2008, describing the tasks which will be completed pursuant to this amendment. No additional funds are related to Proposition MM, since tasks pursuant to this bond measure have been completed almost in full. The district is planning to advertise a new solicitation for these services in September 2008.
