RECOMMENDATION: Approve revised Propositions S, Z and Measure YY Bond Program Plan and adopt resolution approving revised Propositions S, Z and Measure YY project plan and projects undertaken pursuant to Propositions S, Z and Measure YY as shown on Attachment 1.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Ongoing. The Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee met on November 19, 2020

 

BACKGROUND: This update addresses project milestones and provides an overview of the current program. On November 4, 2008, November 6, 2012, and November 6, 2018, voters approved, respectively, Propositions S, Z and Measure YY general obligation bond measures to improve every school in the San Diego Unified School District. On May 14, 2013, the Board of Education (Board) approved the Proposition Z Two-Year Project Plan for implementing projects identified in the Bond Project List and included estimated project schedules. On December 1, 2013, the Board approved an update to the Proposition Z Two-Year Project Plan which also incorporated Proposition S projects and schedules. On June 3, 2014, Propositions S and Z Project Plan expanded beyond its two-year time horizon and has come to be known as the multiple-year project plan. The multiple-year project plan has subsequently been updated at each staff presentation to the Board, most recently on July 14, 2020. In response to Board priorities and direction, staff is presenting its latest update to the multiple-year project plan for Board approval which includes references to the voter guides for Proposition S, Proposition Z, and Measure YY projects.

RECOMMENDATION: Receive report from the Propositions S, Z and Measure YY Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee (ICOC). ICOC Vice Chair, Mahogany Taylor, will present the Committee's semi-annual update to the Board of Education addressing these topics:

  • ICOC Responsibilities & Activities
  • Review Expenditures
  • Inspection of Bond Funded Projects
  • Financial and Performance Audit Review

BACKGROUND: Proposition S, a $2.1 billion general obligation bond measure, was approved by the voters on November 4, 2008. On November 6, 2012, Proposition Z, a $2.8 billion general obligation bond measure was approved by voters. On November 6, 2018, Measure YY, a $3.5 billion general obligation bond measure was approved by voters

 

The Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee was established to ensure Propositions S, Z and Measure YY funds are properly expended; review Propositions S, Z amd Measure YY financial audits; review district deferred maintenance plans; and review district efforts to maximize Propositions S, Z and Measure YY revenues.

D. OPERATIONAL MATTERS RESERVED FOR THE BOARD
Subject
7. Resolution in the Matter of Authorizing The Issuance And Negotiated Sale of Not to Exceed $125,000,000 of Proposition S Current Interest Bonds of San Diego Unified School District, Prescribing the Terms of Sale of Said Bonds, Authorizing The Board of Supervisors Of The County Of San Diego to Sell Said Bonds By a Negotiated Sale Pursuant To One or More Bond Purchase Contracts, Authorizing the Preparation and Execution and Delivery of Paying Agent Agreements, Investment Management Agreements, And Disclosure Dissemination Agreements for Said Bonds, And Authorizing The Execution Of Necessary Documents Relating To Said Bonds
Meeting
Dec 1, 2020 - Regular Charter and Propositions S & Z and Measure YY Updates Meeting, 4:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution authorizing the issuance of not to exceed $125,000,000 Proposition S Bonds of San Diego Unified School District, requesting the Board of Supervisors of the County of San Diego to sell said bonds by a negotiated sale pursuant to one or more bond purchase contracts, approving the form of and authorizing the execution and delivery of bond purchase contracts, paying agent agreements, investment management agreements, disclosure dissemination agreements, and other documents for said bonds and delegating to district officers authorization to take necessary actions and prepare, execute, and deliver necessary documents relating to said bonds. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: An election was held for the San Diego Unified School District, County of San Diego, California on November 4, 2008, at which electors of the district approved, by more than 55 percent of the votes cast, Proposition S authorizing the district to issue up to $2.1 billion in general obligation bonds pursuant to California Constitution Article XIII (A), to be used for the specific school facilities projects set forth in Proposition S.

 

Pursuant to Proposition S, the district shall only issue bonds if it projects that the tax rate necessary to pay debt service al the proposed bonds, when combined with the tax rate necessary to pay debt service on presently outstanding bonds of the district in each year, will not exceed the rate levied in 2007-08 (being $66.70 per $100,000 of assessed valuation) to pay debt service on the presently outstanding bonds alone, referring to bonds issued under Propositions MM and S.

As part of the current plan of finance for the Proposition S Bond Program, the district anticipates issuing one or more series of bonds, as needed, every other year, until the full authorization has been issued. The plan proposes the sale of bonds that allow for the compounding of interest but the current issue will consist of current interest bonds only.

8. Albert Einstein Academies: Action on Renewal of Charter Petition, Action on "Merger" of Elementary School into Middle School, and Action on Material Revision to Expand to Serve Grades 9-12

Dec 1, 2020 - Regular Charter and Propositions S & Z and Measure YY Updates Meeting, 4:00 P.M.

RECOMMENDATION: Staff recommends that the board take the following three separate actions:

 

  1. Approve the renewal of the charter for Albert Einstein Academy Charter Middle School, including a change of name to “Albert Einstein Academies,” for a six year term beginning July 1, 2021, and ending June 30, 2027, with the following clarifications:
    1. Clarification noted below for page 65 regarding School Governance.
    2. Clarification noted below for page 68 regarding Teacher Requirements.
    3. Clarification noted below for page 71 regarding Health and Safety.
    4. Clarification noted below for page 72 regarding Racial and Ethnic Balance.
    5. Clarification noted below for pages 78 and 81 regarding Student Discipline.
    6. Clarification noted below for page 102 regarding Oversight.
  2. Approve a material revision to add grades K-5 to the Albert Einstein Academies charter petition, effective July 1, 2021.
  3. Approve a material revision to add grades 9-12 to the Albert Einstein Academies charter petition, with the first year of high school enrollment beginning July 1, 2024.

BACKGROUND: Staff recommends that the board take the following three separate actions:

 

  1. Approve the renewal of the charter for Albert Einstein Academy Charter Middle School, including a change of name to “Albert Einstein Academies,” for a six year term beginning July 1, 2021, and ending June 30, 2027, with the following clarifications:
    1. Clarification noted below for page 65 regarding School Governance.
    2. Clarification noted below for page 68 regarding Teacher Requirements.
    3. Clarification noted below for page 71 regarding Health and Safety.
    4. Clarification noted below for page 72 regarding Racial and Ethnic Balance.
    5. Clarification noted below for pages 78 and 81 regarding Student Discipline.
    6. Clarification noted below for page 102 regarding Oversight.
  2. Approve a material revision to add grades K-5 to the Albert Einstein Academies charter petition, effective July 1, 2021.
  3. Approve a material revision to add grades 9-12 to the Albert Einstein Academies charter petition, with the first year of high school enrollment beginning July 1, 2024.

Albert Einstein Academy Charter Middle School (“Einstein Middle”) is operated by the nonprofit public benefit corporation, Albert Einstein Academies (“AEA”), pursuant to Education Code section 47604. AEA also operates Albert Einstein Academy Charter Elementary School. In addition to the renewal, AEA has submitted two material revision requests concurrently with the renewal. AEA is seeking to “merge” its current elementary school into its middle school, and change the name of the charter school to Albert Einstein Academies, effective July 1, 2021. AEA is also seeking a material revision to add grades 9-12 to the charter, with high school enrollment beginning July 1, 2024. The district is recommending a renewal of the charter for a six-year term beginning July 1, 2021, and ending June 30, 2027. The public hearing to consider the level of support for the school was held on November 3, 2020.

 

The attached Staff Report includes a comprehensive analysis of the renewal, as well as the two material revisions to "merge" the elementary school into the middle school, and add grades 9-12.
