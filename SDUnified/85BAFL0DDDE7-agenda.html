Background Information:
On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that will provide $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified School District facilities during the next ten years.

On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11 member Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) for Proposition S. As part of the Proposition and as required by law, the board will appoint the ICOC to monitor how the district spends these tax dollars.

The membership for the Proposition S ICOC will be selected from the following criteria:
  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district (Jackson)
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens’ organization (Nakamura)
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association (Evans)
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district (Barrera)
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization (de Beck)

Board members will each choose one appointee from the above criteria;
Board members will each select an individual of their own choosing; one current Proposition MM ICOC committee member will continue to serve on the Proposition S ICOC to provide background to new committee members.

On February 14, 2009, the Board appointed nine members to the Proposition S ICOC.

Terms for membership on the Proposition S ICOC will be for two years, and there will be a two term maximum. No employee or official of the district shall be appointed to the citizens’ oversight committee, nor any vendor, contractor or consultant of the district.

This report is for information only and represents the enrollment forecast used by the district for planning purposes for the 2009-10 school year. Each year, the Instructional Facilities Planning Department (IFPD) prepares district and school-by-school enrollment forecasts for the following school year for all San Diego Unified School District (SDUSD) schools, for use in district planning and budgeting. The enrollment forecast for 2009-10, is presented in this report. At the board meeting on October 14, 2008, the board received an analysis of 2008-09 student enrollment trends.

The districtwide enrollment forecast for October 2, 2009, is 133,309 students, including students enrolled in both district-run and charter schools. This represents an increase of 611 students (0.5 percent) from the official fall 2008 enrollment of 132,698. We are projecting a third year of moderate growth in enrollment for 2009-10.

As the planning process continues through the spring, changes will be made to the forecasts to reflect up-to-date information about issues that will affect enrollment at specific schools. These include possible program changes, charter school openings or closures, and grade reconfigurations.

Approval to use Proposition S funds to provide food carts at district high schools

The scope of food service improvements in Proposition S, which was approved by voters in November, 2008, includes the design and construction of food kiosks at district high schools and middle schools to increase service efficiency and provide healthier food choices. Only 30 percent of senior high school students in the district are participating in the complete lunch program; others are purchasing ala carte, single-items and many are not eating at all. This proposal is the initial phase of improving accessibility of complete meals to students under Proposition S by extending Points-of-Service throughout the campus grounds with food service-themed carts. These carts will be provided in advance of the construction of food kiosks, which will be phased in with modernization and other capital improvements at district high schools under the bond.

The bond resolution allows for bond proceeds to pay for the furnishing and equipping of new and existing facilities. These carts will ultimately be operated as extensions of the kiosks. The food carts will be equipped with point-of-service terminals allowing for all students to access meals without the stigma many high school students associate with the free and reduced-meal program. This system also allows the Food Services Department to continue moving toward a cashless environment.

The estimated cost of this initial phase is $2 million and can be in place by the beginning of the 2009-10 school year. These new food carts are capable of serving an additional 11,500 students per day and generating a gross funding increase of $5.2 million and a net funding increase of $1.2 million dollars per year.
