RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Special Education Memorandum of Understanding with the following Charter Schools: McGill Charter School, Iftin High School Charter School, Iftin Charter School, Magnolia Science Academy San Diego, Magnolia Science Academy San Diego 2, and San Diego Global Vision Academy Charter School.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None

BACKGROUND: On December 1, 2009, the Board of Education approved a template Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) setting forth the terms and conditions under which the district and district authorized charter schools will interact with each other regarding Special Education.  In collaboration with charter schools, the Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) template to outline the relationship between the District and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services.  Under the MOU that was collaboratively developed, Charter Schools will have three options for the delivery of Special Education services at charter schools.  The options are the following:

 

  1. Charter Schools elect to be considered as a public school of the District for the purposes of special education services and funding.  The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the District, and the District will provide special education services.
  2. Charter Schools are designated as schools within the District Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA).  With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the District, and the District will reimburse the Charter School for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the District for equivalent service providers.
  3. Charter Schools are designated as schools of another SELPA.  This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified.  Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served.  The District retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.

 

Staff recommends that the Board of Education approve these Special Education MOU agreements, with the understanding that each individually signed Special Education MOU will return for Board approval as a consent item when schools sign the agreement and when individual charters are renewed.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the Fiscal Year 2009-10 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report in Standardized Account Code Structure (SACS) Series Form. In addition, approve the revised 2010-11 Adopted Budget.  Approve a Resolution identifying the reduction to address the deficits for 2011-12 and 2012-13.
 

FISCAL IMPACT:The Fiscal Year 2009-10 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report shows a total General Fund Ending Balance of $97.0 million.  Compared to the 2009-10 Adjusted Beginning Balance of $164.0 million, this reflects a deficit spending of $67.0 million.
 

The 2009-10 Ending Balance of $97.0 million rolls into the Beginning Balance for the Adopted 2010-11 Budget.  The 2010-11 Budget was adopted with a projected Beginning Balance of $102.0 million. Adopting the revised 2010-11 Budget results in a reduction to our Reserve for Economic Uncertainty previously required at 2% down to .93%.


PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: The Fiscal Year 2008-09 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report showed a total General Fund Ending Balance of $160.6 million. Compared to the 2008-09 Adjusted Beginning Balance of $119.8 million, this reflected surplus spending of $40.8 million.
 

The 2008-09 Ending Balance of $160.6 million rolled into the Beginning Balance for the Adopted 2009-10 Budget. The 2009-10 Budget was adopted with a projected Beginning Balance of $134.1 million.
 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None
 

BACKGROUND: On May 20, 2003, the board approved the Single Budget Adoption Cycle for the district. The board adopted the 2009-10 Budget on June 23, 2009. Under the Single Budget Adoption Cycle, California Education Code, Section 42100 states that on or before September 15, the governing board of each school district shall approve, in the format prescribed by the Superintendent of Public Instruction, the Unaudited Actual Financial Report of the district for the preceding fiscal year.
