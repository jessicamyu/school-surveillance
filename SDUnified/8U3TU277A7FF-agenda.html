Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

BACKGROUND: This report brings forward results from the 2012 Accountability Progress Reports (APR), which were released on October 11, 2012, by the California Department of Education (CDE) for each school and district in the state. The APR includes three components: Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) report, Academic Performance Index (API) Growth report, and Program Improvement report. The AYP report provides data on the achievement of the district and schools with respect to federal No Child Left Behind (NCLB) targets. The API Growth report provides district and school results on 2012 API growth scores and attainment of 2012 state targets. The Program Improvement reports, applicable only to Title I schools and districts, provide information on schools that have not made AYP targets for two or more consecutive years and the federally required interventions. These reports, in addition to the previously discussed California Standards Test and California High School Exit Exam results, provide additional information that informs district evaluation of the strategies and programs included in the Local Educational Agency (LEA) Plan.

On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that will provide $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified School District facilities.  On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11-member Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) for Proposition S. As part of the Proposition and as required by law, the board appointed members to the ICOC to monitor how the district spends these tax dollars.

 

The membership for the Proposition S ICOC is selected from the following criteria:

  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens’ organization 
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district 
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization

Terms for membership on the Proposition S ICOC are for two years, and there is a two-term maximum. No employee or official of the district shall be appointed to the citizens’ oversight committee, nor any vendor, contractor or consultant of the district.

4. Attendance Boundary Changes Between Birney and Garfield Elementary Schools and Florence and Washington Elementary Schools; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-9)

RECOMMENDATION: Approve attendance boundary changes between Birney and Garfield elementary schools and an associated high school boundary change to assist the Health Sciences High School and Middle College (HSHSMC) in an application to the state for the Charter School Facility Grant Program (SB 740), and a boundary change between Florence and Washington elementary schools for the same reason to assist the Museum School with its application for the grant . 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:    In order to assist the Health Sciences High School and Middle College (HSHSMC) and the Museum School with their applications to the state for the Charter School Facility Grant Program (SB 740), boundary changes transferring two uninhabited, non-residential blocks from one elementary school attendance boundary to a neighboring elementary school are being recommended. 

 

In the case of HSHSMC, it is recommended that an uninhabited block be transferred from the Birney Elementary School attendance area to the Garfield Elementary School attendance area; for Museum School, a transfer of an uninhabited block from Florence Elementary School to Washington Elementary School is being recommended.  By changing the block between Birney and Garfield, it is also necessary to change the high school boundary between San Diego High School Complex and the optional area between the San Diego High School Complex and Hoover High School, which follows the elementary boundary.  Attachment 1 is a map of the Birney-Garfield change, and Attachment 2 is a map of the Florence-Washington change.  No students are impacted by these changes.  It should be noted that if future requests of this nature are received, the district will not entertain making boundary changes which impact students’ schools of residence. 

 

Per the California Department of Education’s website, "The Charter School Facility Grant Program is a noncompetitive program that provides assistance with facilities rent and lease expenditures for charter schools that meet specific eligibility criteria. The grant program was enacted in 2002 to reimburse charter schools for rental and lease costs in low-income areas. This program is targeted toward schools and communities with high proportions of economically disadvantaged students. Eligible applicants must have at least 70 percent of students enrolled at the charter school who are eligible for free or reduced-price meals or the charter school must be physically located in an elementary school attendance area where at least 70 percent of students enrolled are eligible for free or reduced-price meals. The charter school must also give a preference in admissions to students who reside in the elementary school attendance area.”

 

By placing these uninhabited non-residential blocks in the Garfield Elementary School boundary and the Washington Elementary boundary, the likelihood that HSHSMC and the Museum School will need to apply for facilities space from the district through the Proposition 39 process will be reduced if they are successful with their grant application.
