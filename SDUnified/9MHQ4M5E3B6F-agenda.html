INFORMATIONAL REPORT: Each month, Superintendent Marten provides an LCAP Implementation Report that includes the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting Year 1 LCAP actions, services, and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's LCAP Implementation Report each month, cluster and advisory groups have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report, and provide input and feedback on current implementation and suggested next steps.

 

This month, the Superintendent will provide information to the board on the implementation of Vision 2020 Quality Indicators:  Quality Teaching, Quality Leadership, and Valuing Diversity, with a focus on the implementation of the Teacher Pipeline Task Force recommendations and supporting baseline data.

 

Included within this item are two recommendations for board action which support quality teaching, quality leadership, and value diversity: 

 

RECOMMENDATION 1: Approve the district's participation in the California Education Leadership Professional Learning Initiative, for the period of January 2015 through September 2016, and authorize the Superintendent to accept a subaward from the University of San Diego on behalf of the district in the amount of $498,916 from its California Department of Education Improving Teacher Quality Grant.

 

RECOMMENDATION 2: Approve the district's intent to participate in the California Teacher Pathway (CTP) Initiative via the Letter of Intent and Memorandum of Understanding with the San Diego County Office of Education, community colleges, California State Universities, after school employers and the Workforce Investment Board

 

Quality Teaching

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination:  Effective teaching requires careful crafting of learning communities built on trust and respect, as well as routines, expectations, resources, and strategies that support diverse students' intellectual engagement in learning.  Effective teaching happens through thoughtful planning focused on defined outcomes for student learning and leveraging of teachable moments.  Effective teaching is revealed in the ways in which teachers respond to the strengths and needs of individual students while engaging and supporting all students in their learning.  Effective teaching is a form of inquiry wherein teachers use evidence and analysis of students' strengths and struggles, and their own performance, to guide their practice in support of student learning.  Effective teaching relies on engagement within a professional learning community.  Teachers in California have a professional responsibility to provide students with safe and caring environments, where students' differences are celebrated and supported, and they acquire the knowledge, skills, strategies, and concepts they will need for successful participation in an increasingly technological and global society. Teachers are personally aware of the role race plays in perpetuating a system of bias, prejudice, and inequity.  

 

LCAP Goal:  Students have access to a highly effective teacher in every course and in every year.

 

LCAP Actions and Services - Year 1:

  1. Provide for effective teacher hiring, recruitment (including partnerships with local universities), and retention practices (including Special Education, Bilingual, and other hard to staff positions) and assure that teachers are properly assigned and supported.
  2. Provide district support for beginning teachers, interns, and peer assistance to effectively teach diverse student groups.
  3. Provide supports and training for special and general education staff who teach and support students with special needs.
  4. Provide systems of support at the school and district level to enhance instructional practices, professional learning, attendance, and success.
  5. Design visiting teacher academies to build capacity of visiting teachers and develop a  cohort of quality teacher candidates.
  6. Cluster support teachers work alongside principals, teachers, and school staffs to build capacity around the Common Core and English Language Development (ELD) State Standards, adult learning structures (Professional Learning Communities [PLC], Instructional Leadership Teams [ILT]), technology integration, and effective teacher practice.
  7. The Special Education Division works in concert with the Office of Leadership Development, area superintendents, and principals to support schools in providing quality professional learning and instructional supports for students with disabilities. 

Quality Leadership

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination:  Schools have a clear rationale, vision, and high expectations for achievement and immediacy for change in a positive and productive learning environment.  School leaders collaborate with and motivate the school community providing support, sharing leadership, paying attention to the work and magnifying best practices using the appreciative inquiry lens.  Schools know students' learning needs and can recognize if instruction is improving student learning through classroom visits and analyzing data focused on student achievement.  School leaders monitor belief systems and teach the importance of embracing diversity and respecting all cultures.  The school community understands why change is important and collectively acts to ensure that all students have a quality education.  Leaders positively influence the attitudes of students, staff, parents, and the community in setting high expectations for all students.  Leaders bring systemic change towards a culture that embraces diversity, respects all cultures, and ensures the development and implementation of educational programs that maximize the academic achievement of all students.  Leaders are personally aware of the role race plays in perpetuating a system of bias, prejudice, and inequity.

 

LCAP Goal:  Quality leadership is evident at all levels of the system with a unifying vision that motivates, inspires, and empowers stakeholders to provide optimal student educational opportunities.

 

LCAP Actions and Services - Year 1:

  1. Area superintendents and school leaders participate in a comprehensive professional development plan to support instructional leadership directed at improved teaching and learning in the classroom (e.g., the 5 Dimensions of Teaching and Learning, Learning Cycles, Leadership Practices).
  2. School leaders participate in regular Professional Learning Communities (PLC) to plan, design lessons, examine student data, and engage in collaborative learning structures and processes.
  3. The content of professional development supports the effective implementation of the English Language Development (ELD) State Standards and reflects school, district and state priorities including the integration of digital tools and relevant technology platforms.
  4. Supports are provided at multiple levels of leadership to foster positive and collaborative student and staff relationships, positive school climate, and students' academic success.

Supportive Environments that Value Diversity in the Service of Students

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination:  All employees have the knowledge and skills necessary to make equity a reality as they support and serve the learning needs of diverse student populations.  Students demonstrate the knowledge and skills, and act thoughtfully and respectfully in order to learn collaboratively with, and value the contributions of, students of different backgrounds.  Multiple cultural perspectives and experiences are valued and people feel respected and welcomed.  Schools challenge and eliminate practices that encourage bias.  Leaders are personally aware of the role race plays in perpetuating a system of bias, prejudice, and inequity.

 

LCAP Goal:  Staffs and faculties provide for learning environments and instructional practices that are culturally responsive and challenge bias.

 

LCAP Actions and Services - Year 1:

  1. Provide cultural proficiency training that recognizes personal bias and discriminatory practices.
  2. Monitor district and school policies, practices, and services for evidence of bias or discriminatory practices and take appropriate corrective action.
  3. Provide for school-based systems and supports that reflect varied student needs and interests.

3. Proclamation Recognizing Tom Hom (McQuary/Beiser)
Meeting
Feb 24, 2015 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve proclamation recognizing Tom Hom for being a positive force for racial equality in San Diego communities.

 

BACKGROUND: The son of Chinese immigrants, Tom Hom was born and raised in San Diego in the 1920s, when minorities were denied certain jobs and lived in certain neighborhoods.  At the age of 12, while accompanying his dad on a delivery to City Hall, Mr. Hom was inspired to “one day be on the inside, passing the laws.”

 

Twenty-four years later, Mr. Hom was the first minority elected to the San Diego City Council stating, “I didn’t run because I wanted to break that barrier, I ran because I thought I could do a good job.”

 

Mr. Hom was instrumental in the establishment of San Diego’s Historic Gaslamp District, the Asian Pacific Historical Thematic District, and the San Diego Chinese Historical Museum.

 

Mr. Hom recently published his autobiography “Rabbit on a Bumpy Road” which follows his life as a boy from San Diego’s Chinatown who couldn’t speak English when he started kindergarten, who then grew up to be a community leader and a key player in significant social and economic change.

The ROP courses and related services began on July 1, 2014.  However, because the San Diego County Superintendent of Schools does not receive its funding to support this program until much later in the school year, this Agreement is not submitted to the district for its approval until the funding has been received.

BACKGROUND:  As a result of changes in federal privacy law and the requirements of the County’s agreements with the State of California, the County is required to amend current Article 14 (Business Associate Agreement) and replace it with a new Article 14, Information Privacy and Security Provisions.  This Amendment revises and adds language to all sections of the existing contract regarding Article 14: Information Privacy and Security Provisions.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve authorization to continue using The Cooperative Purchasing Network (TCPN) agreement with Office Depot, Inc. (GD-15-0840-19).  The term of this authorization is March 1, 2015 through February 28, 2018.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Total not to exceed amount for the three-year term of this authorization: $11,500,000.00.  Budget string: Various site identified funds.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  For the period July 1, 2010 through January 13, 2015: $13,232,189.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  TCPN is a nationally recognized cooperative purchasing consortium that has awarded Office Depot a five-year contract resulting from competitive Bid No.14-17.

 

On December 13, 2011, the board authorized the continued use of The Cooperative Purchasing Network (TCPN) agreement with Office Depot, Inc. through February 28, 2014. This recommendation allows schools and departments to continue purchasing office supplies from Office Depot, Inc. through February 28, 2015.

 

The purchase of office supplies significantly affects the operational budgets of schools and departments.  The TCPN agreement with Office Depot, Inc. is a strategic agreement that is necessary in order to minimize the district's spending for office supplies, while providing a broad spectrum of items from which schools and department may choose in order to meet their respective needs for office supplies.

 

This district utilizes various regional and nationwide cooperative contracts awarded by other governmental entities in order to purchase the goods, supplies, materials, furniture and equipment that are required to support school and district operations.  Cooperative contracts aggregate the total purchases from many governmental entities resulting in lower costs.  In addition to achieving additional cost savings when compared to the prices that would be received if this district were to solicit its own bids, only cooperative contracts provide greater volume discounts and rebates which this district uses to further reduce the costs to schools and departments at the time of each purchase.  For example, in 2007, this district began purchasing office supplies from Office Depot utilizing a nationwide cooperative contract vs. the district’s contract with Office Depot at that time which was based upon the district’s own bid in 2006.  The use of a nationwide cooperative contract at that time provided this district with an additional savings of approximately $125,000 annually.

 

In addition, with the approval of this authorization, the district will then continue to be positioned for schools and departments to have a choice in both the selection and the costs when purchasing office supplies.  While the prices in this agreement are similar to the previously approved authorization to use an agreement with Independent Stationers, Inc., the prices offered by each office supplier are not identical.  By comparing both prices and the products offered by both Office Depot, Inc. and Independent Stationers, Inc., then schools and departments can purchase the appropriate office supplies at the lower cost at the time of each purchase thereby spending less of their budgeted funds.  The Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department continues to work with both suppliers to further reduce the costs of the district's office supplies.

 

Under Government Code section 6500 et seq, a school district may purchase goods under a cooperative, consortium, or joint powers agreement for which it is a participating public agency.  A school district may only order products specified in the contract at the prices specified in the contract.
