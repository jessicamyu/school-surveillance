RECOMMENDATION: Reconfiguration of iHigh Virtual High School beginning in the 2021-22 school year to better serve the needs of students districtwide by offering a comprehensive K-12 online learning program. This recommendation establishes a K-12 online Virtual Academy magnet school with the intention of maintaining the current iHigh Virtual High School and expanding to serve K-8 students.

 

Upon Board approval of the recommended reconfiguration, next steps include the following:

 

July 2021:

  • Hire administrative staff to oversee Virtual Academy.
  • Use the family survey results and e-mail interest for Virtual Academy to staff all bargaining units.
  • Confirm and enroll students into the K-12 Virtual Academy.

 

August 2021:

  • Schedule students into classes.
  • Communicate class assignments, school schedules, and other general information to enrolled students and families.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Total cost estimated at $11 million.  ESSER II Funds will be used, when appropriate, to help support the new Virtual Academy  

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: The Virtual Academy will be provided with the necessary number and types of staff required to meet both instructional and operational needs. Bargaining units were engaged in preliminary discussions for development of Virtual Academy. Tentative agreement has been reached with SDEA on implementation of Virtual Academy.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: An input session was conducted on May 10, 2021 with members of district advisory committees via Zoom to collect feedback related to Fall 2021 onsite and online instructional programs.

 

 

DISTRICT DESIGN TEAM: The roles and responsibilities of each department are outlined below.

 

  • Leadership and Learning - Provide guidance and support to site administration throughout the reconfiguration. Provide an instructional model framework.
  • Facilities, Planning and Construction - Ensure Virtual Academy staff have access to workspaces and facilities for instructional and extracurricular programming needs.
  • Family and Community Engagement - Provide support to site administrators in communicating with parents and community how to enroll in the Virtual Academy.
  • Human Resource Services - Recruit and hire qualified staff to serve K-12 students.
  • Labor Relations - Ensure provisions of collective bargaining agreements are honored in staffing the Virtual Academy.
  • Neighborhood Schools and Enrollment Options - Provide assistance to families who request to have their students return to onsite instruction throughout the year. Students can return to their neighborhood school at any time during the school year. Choice students can return to their school of Choice at the end of a grading term and/or semester. Plan for and prepare for emerging needs in Transportation Services.
  • Physical Plant Operations - Plan and prepare for emerging needs in Food and Nutrition, Custodial, and Landscape Services.
  • Public Information Office - Develop Virtual Academy marketing and communications plan. 

BACKGROUND: In order to meet the needs of students who are unable to attend their homeschool or choice school for onsite instruction due to medical or family needs, a K-12 Virtual Academy will be established for students opting for online learning as their primary source for instruction. The Virtual Academy will serve grade levels K-12 and will be a magnet school housed under the existing iHigh school code.

 

The Virtual Academy will open for the 2021-22 school year and will offer a comprehensive educational program that is substantially equivalent to onsite, in-person instruction. Online curriculum will be provided through Learning Management Systems and will utilize educational technology tools to deliver content and materials. The goal is to provide a rigorous, online school program that meets the needs of 21st Century learners, expands post-secondary education options and prepares graduates for successful careers in a competitive global marketplace. In addition to the Virtual Academy, iHigh will continue to serve students through their existing independent study and/or credit recovery programs. All enrolled students will be provided a digital device and access to broadband connectivity.

BACKGROUND: Marzano Resources, LLC (Marzano) will provide participating CSI schools' leadership professional development and coaching to support their improvement on the following California Dashboard indicators: chronic absenteeism, graduation rate, suspension rate, and academic achievement, through the implementation of Dr. Robert Marzano’s High-Reliability Schools (HRS) framework. Based on fifty years of research, the HRS framework serves as a sustainable long-term strategic planning guide for schools and districts. The framework encourages educators to concentrate their efforts on five key areas of operation: 1. Safe, Supportive, and Collaborative Culture; 2. Effective Teaching in Every Classroom; 3. Guaranteed and Viable Curriculum; 4. Standards-Referenced Reporting; and 5. Competency-Based Education. The focus will be on the following:

 

1. Leading and sustaining a High-Reliability School.

2. Level 1 - Building a safe, supportive, and collaborative school-wide culture.

3. Level 2 - 4 - Decreasing absenteeism by improving classroom instruction and the classroom environment. Increase student learning in math and language arts by establishing a guaranteed and viable curriculum and an instructional model.

 

Marzano will also provide professional development focused on increasing academic achievement and providing a more equitable learning environment through the implementation of Standards-Based learning practices to participating secondary schools’ leadership teams and school sites. Standards-Based learning requires a dramatic shift in the existing curriculum, assessment, and reporting practices. 

 

Marzano will provide virtual and onsite professional developments, webinars, school surveys and analyses, virtual coaching, and professional development materials.

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The asphalt repair project at Toler Elementary School (Proposed Project) is subject to CEQA, however, the project is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Section 15301 – Existing Facilities (Class 1). A Class 1 exemption consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of existing or former use. The key consideration is whether the project involves negligible or no expansion of use.

 

As described above, the Proposed Project consists of existing asphalt repairs at Toler Elementary School. The Proposed Project would not result in the expansion of existing asphalt areas. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway.   5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.

RECOMMENDATION: Determine that asphalt repair project at Hearst Elementary School is Exempt from further environmental review pursuant to California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Guidelines section 15301 – Existing Facilities; and Approve Agreement with Kirk Paving, Inc., for Hearst Elementary School Asphalt and Concrete Repairs (CP22-0039-39). The term of the Agreement is July 16, 2021 through completion of the project.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not-to-exceed amount of $439,600.00. Budget String: 9143-90504-00-5751-8500-0000-21400-8534.

BACKGROUND: At Hearst Elementary School the project consists of making asphalt/concrete repairs throughout the site, including removal and replacement of asphalt/concrete, Trupave, patch, overlay, pave, skim patch, raise or replace meter boxes, fixing tripping hazards, seal coating all asphalt on site and re-striping all asphalt parking lots. The alternate work includes removing and replacing asphalt on parking lot, saw cutting around handicap parking spaces and removing and re-installing all wheel stops.

 

Pursuant to Public Contract Code section 20111(b), the district must publicly competitively bid any public project involving an expenditure of $15,000.00 or more. The notice calling for bids was published once a week for two successive weeks in accordance with Public Contract Code section 20112. Bids were due and opened on June 3, 2021. The district received a total of five bids from A.M. Ortega Construction, Inc., Frank and Son Paving, Inc., Kirk Paving, Inc., Palm Engineering Construction Company, Inc., and RAP Engineering, Inc. After evaluating the bids, staff determined Kirk Paving, Inc. to be the lowest responsive and responsible bidder.

 

Consistent with the May 10, 2011, board-approved resolution in support of service-disabled veteran-owned businesses (SDVOB) and disabled veteran business enterprises (DVBE) which established a three percent required participation of DVE’s for all district construction contracting opportunities. The recommended contractor, Kirk Paving, Inc., submitted a three percent DVBE participation in its bid.

 

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The asphalt repair project at Hearst Elementary School (Proposed Project) is subject to CEQA, however, the project is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Section 15301 – Existing Facilities (Class 1). A Class 1 exemption consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of existing or former use. The key consideration is whether the project involves negligible or no expansion of use.

As described above, the Proposed Project consists of existing asphalt repairs at Hearst Elementary School. The Proposed Project would not result in the expansion of existing asphalt areas. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. Construction activities associated with the Proposed Project would be contained to an existing district school site and the existing site use will not change as a result of the implementation of the Proposed Project. The Proposed Project will not impact environmentally sensitive areas or an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern because these resources are not located on any district property. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Proposed Project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. The Proposed Project is generally considered repair and maintenance of existing facilities, which is consistent with ongoing operations of district sites. Ongoing operations would not be considered a cumulative impact. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Proposed Project.
  3. Significant Effect. The Proposed Project is not considered an unusual circumstance as these repair and maintenance activities are common activities that occur throughout all district properties. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project would not result in the demolition of existing structures or the construction of new facilities that would directly or indirectly affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the Department of Toxic Substance Control, no district properties are included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. Because the Proposed Project does not require the demolition of existing historical era buildings or structures, the significance of a potentially historical resource will not be materially impaired, and no substantial adverse changes to the significance of a historical resource or significant impacts to the environment at the project site is anticipated. Additionally, the likelihood of impacting either prehistoric or historic archeological resources is negligible for the current undertaking due because grading and trenching into native soils is not proposed. This exception does not apply to the project.

Approval of this item approves the Proposed Project as described, authorizes and directs staff to file the attached Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk, and awards the agreement to Kirk Paving, Inc., to provide asphalt repairs at Hearst Elementary School.
