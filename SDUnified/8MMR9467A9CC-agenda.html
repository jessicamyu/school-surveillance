Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve First Amendment to Agreement with American Institutes for Research (AIR) to provide evaluation services for the Check & Connect programs at Clairemont, Crawford, Kearny, Lincoln, Madison, Mission Bay, Morse Point Loma, San Diego, and Serra high schools (PS-11-1106-08). This First Amendment to Agreement extends the agreement through February 28, 2013 for a total term of March 1, 2011 through February 28, 2013.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: This agreement will provide $975,269.00 in total revenue to the district in support of the Check & Connect program through the February 28, 2015 end date of the grant. However, under the terms of this agreement, AIR provides the district with funding to hire a lead mentor, ten school-based mentors, and a stipend for the collection and provision of related data by funding year (March 1 through February 28).  The grant is incrementally funded on an annual basis as follows:

 

Year One:    $251,476.00 March 1, 2011 - February 28, 2012

Year Two:      $297,500.00 March 1, 2012 - February 28, 2013

Year Three:  $297,500.00 March 1, 2013 - February 28, 2014

Year Four:     $128,793.00 March 1, 2014 - February 28, 2015

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: For the period March 1, 2011 through February 28, 2012: $251,476.00

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: The grant will fund a Check & Connect Lead Mentor and ten school-based mentors.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: American Institutes for Research (AIR) partnered with the district to demonstrate the effects of the Check & Connect program on general education students at risk of dropping out of high school. A sample of at-risk ninth graders in ten district high schools - approximately 300 students, 30 from each of the ten identified high schools - were randomly assigned (by lottery) to participate in the Check & Connect program from ninth grade through their scheduled graduation from high school to receive personal mentoring. Data will be used to evaluate the effects of these support services on student engagement, attendance, academic persistence and performance, and dropout and graduation rates.

 

Because the grant is only funded annually by AIR for a single year, it is necessary to amend the agreement annually at the beginning of each new funding year for the revenue to the district for the respective funding year.

Subject
5. Resolution in the Matter of the Reduction in Certificated Staff Due to Reduction or Elimination of Particular Kinds of Services (PKS)--First Reading (OE-5)
Meeting
Feb 28, 2012 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
First Reading

RECOMMENDATION:  Conduct First Reading on the Resolution Reducing or Eliminating Certain Certificated Services for the 2012-13 school year.  This item will be agendized for action on March 6, 2012.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Approval and full implementation of this Resolution would result in a reduction in currently performed certificated services, and a corresponding reduction in permanent and probationary certificated employees, sufficient to realize necessary budget reductions/savings.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  The Resolution provides for a reduction of certificated services totaling 1608.57 full-time equivalents (FTEs)

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The district is continuing to face a fiscal crisis.  Approximately 90 percent of the district’s unrestricted budget is dedicated to staff (including salary and health and welfare benefit costs).  In order to maintain fiscal solvency, and in conjunction with the March 15 approval of a second interim report, the district must take action to allow for significant reductions in certificated staffing consistent with prior board actions and the plan/projections specified in the second interim report, absent negotiated concessions by the San Diego Education Association (SDEA).

 

For permanent certificated employees, the Education Code requires that the board identify the particular kinds of services or programs that may be reduced or eliminated in the upcoming fiscal year.  For probationary certificated employees, Article 19 of the collective bargaining agreement between the district and SDEA allows the board to determine that reductions are necessary due to lack of funds.  Permanent certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a statutory layoff notice on or before March 15, 2012.  Probationary certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a contractual layoff notice on or before April 15, 2012.  Noticed employees will be able to challenge the action through a request for hearing (permanent) or the filing of a grievance (probationary).  Temporary certificated employees will receive a separate reminder notice that their services will end at the close of the 2011-12 school year.

 

In order to provide the necessary flexibility and still meet statutory and contractual deadlines, which are jurisdictional, this Resolution lists a large number of programs, many of which may ultimately not be reduced or eliminated if fiscal circumstances improve.  Additionally, based on the fact that the service reductions in the Resolution are FTEs, rather than a number of employees, and other factors related to potentially disputed issues regarding seniority (and deviation therefrom), certificates, and qualifications for certain positions to displace less senior employees, the number of certificated employees receiving these notices will exceed the FTE service reductions listed in the Resolution.
