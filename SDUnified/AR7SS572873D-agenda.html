BACKGROUND: The Board of Trustees recognizes the importance of environmental sustainability for our school facilities, students, staff and community. District sustainability actions provide students with valuable learning and leadership opportunities, while helping to control district operating costs. For the fourth consecutive year, the district’s contract with EDCO Disposal Corporation for districtwide waste and recyclables collection services provides an opportunity to reward schools for their commitment to sustainability through participation in the annual EDCO Recycling Challenge. The goal of the Challenge is to reduce waste and increase recycling in our schools, to encourage environmental stewardship, curb costs, and facilitate compliance with State and local waste diversion laws.

 

Representatives from EDCO and the district will present a Recycling and Waste Diversion Certificate of Achievement and monetary award to the following schools:

Under our current contract EDCO conducts bin fullness surveys to help schools determine the most cost effective trash and recycling service for their site, assists with presentations and assemblies as part of education and outreach efforts, and provides collection carts to schools at no charge to expand their recycling efforts.

 

Congratulations to our top winners on their recycling and waste diversion achievements, and please recognize their efforts!

VISION 2020 Quality Indicator Destination: Every school is committed to maintaining high expectations for all students and to eliminating the persistent disparities in achievement and performance among student groups based on race, ethnicity, language or disability.  These disparities are understood as unacceptable and a focused plan of support is in place to eliminate the significant achievement gaps in order to realize academic proficiency for all students.  All relevant policies and practices reflect the goals of eliminating achievement disparities and attaining academic proficiency, explicitly and emphatically.  A districtwide focus and effort supports the advancement of high levels of student achievement by ensuring that every school is culturally competent, ensures uniformly high expectations, promotes rigorous curricula, differentiates instruction, and maximizes access for all students to high-level educational opportunities.  The district actively seeks to hire a diverse cadre of educators and administrators, as well as ensure proper emphasis on culturally responsive practices and regularly examines and monitors student achievement, policies, programs, tools and written documents to ensure that achievement gap goals are implemented. All staff members understand how their respective positions contribute, directly or indirectly, to the implementation of the achievement gap goals.  Staff members understand and actively seek to learn the necessary knowledge and skills to serve diverse students and families.  Staff members assume responsibility for implementing plans that will address achievement disparities.  District and community stakeholders engage students and families, the private sector, faith-based groups, and higher education institutions to support district students in achieving academic proficiency.

 

LCAP Goal 1: All levels of the organization work to improve student achievement and close the achievement gap for all underperforming student groups.

 

 

BACKGROUND:  On January 9, 2018, the board approved a partnership with the University of San Diego (USD) Institute for Entrepreneurship in Education (IEE), the Lincoln High School Community, and the San Diego Unified School District to create a transformative vision for Lincoln High School.  The purpose of this partnership is to facilitate the development of a coherent, transformative, and shared vision with anticipated concrete outcomes for students, and a clearly defined strategic plan for Lincoln High School, including stakeholder expectations, resource requirements, and interim goals for determining progress.  The partnership also established a Lincoln High School Transformation Leadership Team to to develop capacity to lead the work of school transformation.  This report is an update to the board and the public on the Listening Phase: Understanding Lincoln's Context (Phase 0), which occurred from January-March 2018.

 

At regular, public board meetings Superintendent Marten provides reports that include the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting LCAP actions, services and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's Vision 2020/LCAP report, cluster and advisory groups, employee groups, and members of the public have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report and provide input and feedback on current LCAP implementation.  The complete LCAP document is available on the district website at https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve proclamation recognizing and celebrating Reverend George Walker Smith. The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District is honored to wish Reverend Smith a very Happy 90th Birthday and recognizes him for his lifelong commitment to provide equal educational opportunities for all students in San Diego County and beyond.

 

BACKGROUND: When Reverend George Walker Smith arrived in San Diego in 1956, he founded the Golden Hill United Presbyterian Church, which eventually became the Christ United Presbyterian Church of San Diego, where he served as pastor until his retirement in 2000.

 

Soon after moving to San Diego, Reverend Smith became one of the City’s foremost community leaders, and in 1963, he became the first African American to hold political office in San Diego with his election to the San Diego Unified School District’s Board of Education.

 

During Reverend Smith’s four consecutive terms on the San Diego Unified board he served as President and advocated for a change in the district’s employment practices which led to more inclusive staffing and the employment of almost 800 African American and Latino teachers by the end of his fourth term in 1979.

 

With the intervention of the United States Office of Education and the Superior Court, Reverend Smith was able to overcome board opposition and establish the Voluntary Ethnic Enrollment (VEEP) and Magnet programs to help eliminate racial imbalance in San Diego schools.

 

On a national level, Reverend Smith served as president of the Council of Great City Schools (CGCS) in 1972, and president of the National School Board Association (NSBA) in 1976.

 

In 1989, Reverend Smith co-founded the Innovative Preschool Project, subsequently establishing the School of Success, now known as the McGill School of Success Charter.

Discussion of Proposed Project

 

The Proposed Project involves the demolition of the existing theater building, administration building, counseling building, and 20 modular classrooms and the construction of a new three-story administration/classroom building (approximately 47,565 sf) and a new performing arts center (approximately 25,704 sf). The new administration/classroom building will also include the construction of a new five-story tower in the style of the previous five-story tower located on the campus that was once considered the signature defining aspect of the campus but was demolished in the 1970s. The new three-story building will be located on the portion of the school site currently occupied by Buildings 100 (Counseling) and 1700 (Administration).

The first floor of the three-story building will accommodate the school's administrative staff, its counselors, and the support staff. The second and third floors will house ten classrooms each. Those 20 new classrooms will replace the 20 modular classrooms currently located in the school's annex area.

The Performing Arts Center will be located on a portion of the school site currently occupied by the existing theater, to the proposed three-story building. The theater building is located to enable access for the public from El Cajon Boulevard, student access from within the secure campus perimeter and delivery access from Highland Avenue via the existing driveway located to the south of Building 200. The building is also configured to facilitate daily access between the drama classrooms and the performance/back-of-house areas.

The facility will provide an approximate 500 seat theater, theater support space, and instructional space for theater arts. The theater will fully support musical and theatrical events, feature a 43-foot-high flight loft, a 19-foot-high proscenium, and an orchestra pit. Theatrical support space includes a public lobby for access to and exiting from the auditorium (ground and mezzanine levels); a control room, sound room, and lighting booth (mezzanine level); a stage; a green room; dressing rooms; storage areas; a costume workshop; and a scenery workshop. The enclosed lobby space will provide a ticket office and restrooms. The lobby space is also paired with an adjacent courtyard that can be used for pre-function activities and intermission overflow space.

Included in the project is the reconfiguration of the main parking lot, a student drop-off area and various improvements in the front and interior courtyards intended to provide safe, convenient and functional use of the site and to provide an aesthetically pleasing appearance to the prominently located new buildings. The reconfiguration of the parking lot would include the addition of approximately 50 new parking spaces.

The new building’s design inspiration is inspired by the 1930 architecture of the original Hoover High School campus and will combine an old-fashioned 'schoolhouse' concept with an energy-efficient modern design. This project is scheduled to start construction in the second quarter of 2018 and be completed by the third quarter of 2020.

CEQA Analysis

 

The proposed project is subject to the requirements of the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), Public Resources Code (PRC) section 21000 et. seq., and its implementing Guidelines (California Code of Regulations, Title 14, Division 6, Chapter 3, sections 15000 et. seq., as amended). However, it is exempt from further environmental review under the following CEQA categorical exemption classes:

 

Replacement or Reconstruction (Class 2)

Class 2 consists of replacement or reconstruction of existing structures and facilities where the new structure will be located on the same site as the structure replaced and will have substantially the same purpose and capacity as the structure replaced (CEQA Guidelines §15302).

 

The Proposed Project would demolish existing administration, temporary classrooms, and theater facilities on the campus and replace them with buildings and structures that are substantially the same size, purpose, and capacity as the existing structures. The proposed tennis courts would be located within an area of the school previously occupied by portable classrooms and would replace tennis courts that were removed as part of previous project. Therefore, the application of a Class 2 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

Minor Additions to Schools (Class 14)

Class 14 consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25% or ten classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption (CEQA Guidelines §15314).

 

The Proposed Project would result in minor additions to an existing school campus; however, the improvements would not change the existing school use and operation, and would not increase student capacity. Therefore, the application of a Class 14 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Section 15300.2 of the CEQA Guidelines specifies several exceptions to the use of a categorical exemption. However, staff has determined that no exception applies to defeat application of the categorical exemptions. Approval of this item approves the Project as described, authorizes and directs staff to file a Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk and awards the lease-leaseback contract for construction of the project.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the use of short-term classified employees for the upcoming June 5, 2018 and November 6, 2018 elections at various district schools and facilities to assist with directing voters and facilitating voter access to polling locations.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: $7,634.00. Budget String: 5600-04003-00-2251-8100-0000-40003-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. The purpose of using short-term employees is to ensure voters can access the polling locations. Without the use of these short-term employees, school staff would have to provide support to voters. Short-term employees will only be used on two days: June 5, 2018 and November 6, 2018. The work performed by the short-term employees who will work at the polling locations does not belong to any of the bargaining units. This item does not provide for the broad use of short-term employees on a regular basis.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The District has received a request from the County of San Diego Registrar of Voters to utilize its facilities as polling places in the upcoming 2018 elections. In order to support the county, the District needs to employ short-term employees to assist voters to access the specific locations on the school campuses where the voting will be held. "Short-term employee" means a person who is employed to perform a service for the school district, upon the completion of which, the service required or similar services will not be extended or needed on a continuing basis. Before employing a short-term employee, the governing board of a school district, at a regularly scheduled board meeting, shall specify the service required to be performed by the employee.
