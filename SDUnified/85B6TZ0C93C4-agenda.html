The resultant agreement would provide professional services support to the Independent Citizens Oversight Committee (ICOC) on an as-required basis. The term of the agreement will be negotiated.

FISCAL IMPACT: Although there is no financial impact at this time, there are no budgeted funds for a future agreement in the Property Management fund.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: In November 2008, the voters of San Diego County passed Proposition S, a general obligation bond measure, by a margin of more than 55 percent in compliance with the guidelines established by Proposition 39. In accordance with state law, the district established an Independent Citizens Oversight Committee (ICOC) to review and report to the public on the proper expenditure of taxpayers' money for work performed using Proposition S funds, and to advise the public as to whether the district is in compliance with state law in the use of Proposition S funds. On October 13, 2009, the board approved the Bylaws of the ICOC. In order to fulfill its requirements to the public, the ICOC desires for the district to employ a firm to assist in the conduct of its work. Some of the tasks required by the ICOC include: (1) assistance with preparation of financial and independent performance audits, (2) site reviews of Prop S construction projects in progress, and (3) annual reports on the Prop S Business Outreach Program, Labor Compliance Program, and joint use facilities projects and property acquisitions financed by Prop S. (Education Code section 15278 et. seq.)

Education Code section 15280 states that the governing board of a school district shall, without expending bond funds, provide the ICOC with any necessary technical assistance and administrative assistance in furtherance of its purpose and sufficient resources to publicize the conclusions of the ICOC.

Government Code section 53060 allows a school district to contract with and employ any persons for the furnishing of special services if such persons are specially trained, experienced and competent to perform the special services required.

Although not required to do so, the district advertised Request for Proposals (RFP) No. PS-90-520-02 on September 21, 2009, seeking competitive proposals from interested firms to ensure best value and maximize competition. Thirteen firms requested a copy of the RFP; however, only two firms responded by the closing date of October 12, 2009.

Based upon the submittals, both firms were invited to interview with the district on October 26, 2009. Each firm responded to questions from a five-member interview panel based upon the evaluation criteria established in the RFP. The interview panel consisted of:

    • Glenn Hillegas, Member, ICOC
    • Leonard Pinson, Member, ICOC
    • John Stump, Member, ICOC
    • Stuart MacDonald, former member, ICOC for Proposition MM
    • Jim Watts, Director of Facilities Planning

Colbi Technologies, Inc. unanimously received the higher ranking by the interview panel and is being recommended for approval to provide the desired services. The firm has met all qualifications of the district's RFP and Government Code 53060.

BACKGROUND: Government Code section 53060 allows a school district to contract with and employ any persons for the furnishing of special services and advice if such persons are specially trained and experienced and competent to perform the special services required.

The district is seeking to apply to ULI for a panel review of our property portfolio. The panel will work together with district staff, the ACUESP and ultimately the board to evaluate the potential for reconstruction and redevelopment of the EBEC and evaluate the highest and best use the school district's real property assets in order to accomplish the task. The ULI panel will define the requirements for future usage of the property, which will include a discussion of the potential financing options to move forward with the project.

The district has 28 properties that have been declared excess, which are not currently used as active district school sites. On September 24, the ACUESP approved a motion for district staff to take the application forward for board review and approval. The ACUESP has categorized and made recommendations for the use of the 28 properties to the board. The EBEC is one of the excess locations. The EBEC is the primary administrative head quarters of the district and is the site most immediately interesting to the district. The site is comprised of 20 buildings with approximately 172,868 square feet on 11.43 acres.

The district is interested in redeveloping the 50 plus year old, deteriorating building and adjacent temporary buildings that have outlived their useful lives. Several studies have been completed for the redevelopment of the building but none of these have been implemented. The EBEC currently is the district’s main office location, with many administrative programs housed in the facility. The district needs an appropriate facility to house these services and potentially others while supporting the district for another 50 plus years. ULI will only review and recommend the other 27 sites as appropriate to accomplish the task.

Approval of this agenda item will authorize the Executive Director, Auxiliary Services, or his designee, to execute any documents in connection therewith.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve agreement with WestEd to provide a comprehensive professional development Quality Teaching for English Learners (QTEL) apprenticeship process that will enhance district capacity to design, implement, and support quality instruction for secondary-level English learners. Services delivered will provide QTEL professional development for approximately 240 district teachers as well as develop QTEL certified district teacher leaders. Agreement is effective December 1, 2009, through December 31, 2011.

FISCAL IMPACT: The total amount of this Agreement with WestEd is $650,000, to be paid from Title III funds, which must be used to support English learners, and will be allocated proportionally over three fiscal years: 2009-10 ($210,000); 2010-11 ($210,000); and 2011-12 ($230,000). Title III funds must be spent to prevent delay of subsequent funds. Significant future costs savings will be realized as a result of shifting responsibilities from QTEL consultants to QTEL certified district staff.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: The district has identified a need to improve achievement of English learners (ELs). The district is now in program improvement, in part due to the achievement levels of our EL students. At the secondary level in particular, a key area of need is in building teacher capacity to provide effective instruction across all content areas for ELs.

Of the nearly 32,000 current ELs enrolled in district schools, approximately 10,000–or almost one-third–are enrolled in Grades 6-12 at the middle and senior high schools. This is a very diverse group representing students at all levels of English language proficiency and academic preparation. Most ELs who began school in the primary grades have made timely progress in learning English and were reclassified in the elementary years. However, the majority (approaching 70 percent) of students currently identified as ELs at the secondary level are considered “long term” ELs who have attended school for seven or more years. While fluent in English, many of these students lack the academic skills, as measured on California Standards Tests (CST), needed to be reclassified as Reclassified Fluent English Proficient (RFEP). Conversely, fewer than 5 percent of secondary EL students are 'New Arrivals' – students who are new to the United States and new to English. The remaining secondary EL students are those who arrived in the U.S. at the secondary level and are in the process of developing English proficiency.

Effectively teaching this diverse group of secondary ELs is a challenge. Teachers need support in meeting this challenge. Currently, there is district staff that holds expertise in aspects of effective instruction for ELs. The district does not, however, have sufficient capacity to provide the breadth and depth of professional development that is required. There is a need to develop an intensive, comprehensive professional development plan that will develop district capacity in providing effective instruction to English learners, and all students. QTEL’s apprenticeship process for professional development and capacity building is designed to meet this need.

QTEL is a research-based, proven process that has already shown promise in the district. Since fall of 2007, QTEL staff has provided ten QTEL institutes to cohorts of district middle and senior high teachers as well as intensive QTEL institutes and on-site support for English language arts teachers at eight middle schools. Evaluations have been overwhelmingly positive by both teachers and principals in promoting changes in instructional practice which promotes achievement of all students. Teachers note that through QTEL, they develop the approaches and strategies they need to be more effective in their work. The Office of Language Acquisition regularly receives calls from teachers and principals requesting QTEL professional development. The QTEL apprenticeship process has also been successfully implemented in other large, urban school districts including New York City (winner of the Broad Prize in 2007) and Austin, Texas. Both of these districts credit QTEL as a key factor in improved student achievement.

This agreement establishes a three-year collaboration with WestEd’s QTEL staff to provide an apprenticeship process that will provide training for principals, involve approximately 240 secondary teachers in QTEL professional development, and will result in a cadre of district teachers who are QTEL certified to provide professional development and on-site teacher support. This district teacher cadre will continue to provide professional development and site-based instructional support for district staff, using the resources and supports of QTEL. This professional development will begin with secondary teachers and expand into the upper elementary grades.

The apprenticeship process will be implemented in three phases, over a 24-month period. Phase one begins in December 2009, with a full-day principal conference with Aida Walqui (requested by the Secondary School Improvement Officers), director of QTEL and a nationally recognized expert on issues related to adolescent English learners. Professional development institutes will begin in January 2010, and continue through December 2011. Intensive institutes will be scheduled during the summer months. Teachers will be compensated for their time during summer institutes or other non-contract hours. Staff met with middle and senior high school principals who support the QTEL professional development plan.

The QTEL apprenticeship process will be a coordinated effort, implemented with cross-district collaboration and support. Over time, responsibility shifts from QTEL consultants to district certified lead teacher/presenters resulting in increased district capacity and significant future cost savings. The district will receive immediate and long-term benefits as it more efficiently and effectively uses district, human and fiscal resources resulting in the improved achievement of its English learners.

Other components include reducing risk factors and enhancing protective factors, including reducing stigma around seeking help and promoting education about mental health.

Helping students feel connected to their school, which has proven to be the number one protective factor against suicidal behavior, will be a priority.
