RECOMMENDATION: Approve recommendation of the Interdivisional Curriculum Committee (ICC) for a waiver request for online science courses. This request seeks a one-time waiver of the district's approved science course graduation requirements for iHigh Virtual Academy students who complete specific online science courses taken in spring 2009 and duringthe 2009-10 school year. The district's current, board-approved high school graduation requirement for science specifies that courses must be approved by the University of California (UC) as meeting its laboratory science or elective subject-area admission requirements. Except for courses offered through the University of California College Prep (UCCP) online program, the UC does not presently recognize online science courses as meeting subject-area admission requirements. As a result, the online science courses being offered at the iHigh Virtual Academy do not currently meet district science graduation requirements. Approval of this waiver request will allow students at iHigh Virtual Academy to complete UC-accepted meet District science graduation requirements. iHigh Virtual Academy is undergoing the Western Association of Schools and Colleges (WASC) accreditation process beginning in February 2010. A list of courses affected by this waiver is attached.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: The board cedes to the ICC the responsibility to assess and recommend requests pertaining to the curriculum included in the district Course of Study. The ICC reviews and recommends to the board new courses, deletion of courses, and changes to approved courses.

The board approved the creation of the iHigh Virtual Academy in the 2008-09 school year. iHigh provides students an individualized opportunity to earn a high school diploma while working with online courses. In addition to teacher interaction through daily online lessons, students use physical lesson materials, offline tools, and may meet face to face with their instructors at Mt. Everest Academy or the Fremont Training Center.

RECOMMMENDATION: Approve the use of Prop S funds for the design and construction of a new 500 seat auditorium at Patrick Henry High School.

FISCAL IMPACT: Total project cost is estimated to be $6,300,000 from Prop. S program funds allocated for the Patrick Henry School project.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: There is no impact to district staffing levels.

PREVIOUS YEAR EXPENDITURE: None.

BACKGROUND: Patrick Henry High School is one of two comprehensive high schools that do not have an auditorium. Patrick Henry High school was constructed in 1969 with a small, 7,000 square foot multipurpose “cafetorium” that has a small stage and a folding partition that divided the seating area and dining area. The seating on the stage side can accommodate 150 with an additional seating capacity of 175 on the kitchen side when the partition is opened. After completing a collaborative, site-based master planning and design task force process with the school staff, parents, students and the community the need for a dedicated auditorium became evident.

Scope—$36,103,208 of the Proposition S budget is currently allocated for Patrick Henry High School. A 500 seat, 16, 466 square foot auditorium is being proposed at a total estimated cost of $6,300,000. The $6,300,000 budget required for the auditorium would come from projected savings and scope reductions of other program elements of the Patrick Henry whole site modernization project.

The district has also submitted a Career Technical Education (CTE) matching grant application to the California Department of Education (CDE) to provide an additional 9, 327 square feet of instruction space adjacent to the auditorium. The district should be notified in Mid- February of the project ranking to determine if the project will be eligible for a state matching CTE grant. An additional $1,835, 000 in district matching funds would be required if the district elects to accept the grant. This item will be brought back to the board, along with other CTE project grants that have been submitted in the third round of applications after the CDE ranking process is completed.

Schedule—The Patrick Henry, whole site modernization project is scheduled to begin construction in 2015. The current projected revenues from annual bond sales and planned Prop S expenditures do not permit an acceleration of the schedule at this time.

Recommendation—Approve the 500 seat auditorium for Patrick Henry High School at a estimated cost of $6,300,000 from Prop S program funds allocated for the Patrick Henry High School project.
