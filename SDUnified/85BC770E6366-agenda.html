The Superintendent recommends 1) the implementation of the Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) program at 15 Title I schools in 2008-09; 2) the adoption of a related consultant services contract with Safe & Civil Schools for 2008-09 in the amount of $44,150; 3) authorization of the purchase of Safe & Civil Schools instructional materials for 2008-09 in the amount of $27,800; and 4) authorization to send eight district staff members to a "training of trainers" institute in the summer of 2009 at a cost of $32,000. (With board approval, review of results, and school feedback, there is the possibility of amending the current consultant contract for fewer consultant days in 2009-10 and 2010-11.)

The Board of Education reviewed a "Positive Behavioral Supports Pilot Proposal" on August 18, 2008.  At that time, the board requested that staff revise/clarify aspects of the proposal and bring a plan back to the board for action.  This agenda item brings forward a proposal for a "Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) Program.”  The revised proposal addresses board concerns and requests for clarification.

This proposal presents a plan for the Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) school-wide program that includes targeted interventions for "at risk" students at up to 15 Title I volunteer schools. The overarching goals are to lower suspensions, expulsions, and dropout rates. The basis for the program is research-based behavioral analysis methodology that provides a framework to support schools in creating a more positive climate by developing a comprehensive discipline plan. This proposal includes training and support for classroom teachers and other certificated staff, school leaders, and classified school staff. The end goal is the establishment, and independent support of a district program of positive behavior support and interventions.

Based on Assembly Bill 1908, Education Code 15278A and current Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee (ICOC) guidelines, board member Shelia Jackson is requesting that the ICOC for Proposition S be established using the following criteria:

According to Proposition 39, seven is the minimum number of members required. The Committee must include:
  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located with the district
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens' organization
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district
