BACKGROUND: The Proposed Project includes the installation of a 5’x9’ illuminated full-color light-emitting diode (LED), dimmable and timer-controlled marquee sign at Longfellow Elementary School, Rolando Park Elementary School, Webster Elementary School, Wegeforth Elementary School and Patrick Henry High School. The proposed marquee signs will have the capability to be programed to display animation and flash, and would be used to present school-related information to school attendees and the general public regarding the schools’ affairs. The proposed marquee signs would be mounted onto the face of the existing exterior buildings or on a double pole mount. Figure 1 through Figure 11 included within the attached document Appendix_E_with_Attachment_A_Marquee_NOE_4Sept18 provides the general location, existing conditions, and a visual simulation of each marquee at each of the five project sites. The marquee signs that would be wall mounted to existing school buildings would not require any electrical trenching. The double pole mount marquee at Longfellow Elementary School would require trenching to the nearest building on campus, or approximately 70 feet. Trench dimensions would be approximately 2 feet deep by 1 foot wide.

 

The outdoor display would use full-color LED lighting to create text and graphics. The text could be displayed using a variety of display options such as sparkle, flash, blink, and scroll to create a message. Transitions between messages would occur every 1 to 4 seconds, with individual messages each being displayed between approximately 4 and 10 seconds. San Diego Unified (District) would pre-program each marquee sign to automatically turn off or dim from 10:00 pm to 7:00 am, consistent with District Site Operations Circular No. 1053, to avoid nighttime light impacts and unnecessary energy consumption.

 

Installation of each marquee would take less than one week and would not require large construction equipment such as cranes, graders, or excavators.

Reasons Why Project Is Exempt: The proposed activity is a project subject to California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA). However, it is exempt from further environmental review under the following CEQA categorical exemption classes:

Accessory Structures (Class 11) - Class 11 consists of construction, or placement of minor structures accessory to existing commercial, industrial, or institutional facilities including, but not limited to, on-premise signs (CEQA Guidelines §15311[a]).

The Proposed Project involves the installation of full-color LED message display marquee signs for school related announcements at existing schools. The proposed marquee signs would either be mounted onto the face of an existing building on the campus or on a double pole mount. In the case of Rolando Park Elementary, the marquee would replace an existing manual changeable letter (non-digital) marquee sign. Therefore, the application of a Class 11 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

Minor Additions to Schools (Class 14) - Class 14 consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or 10 classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption (CEQA Guidelines §15314).

 

The Proposed Project would install new marquee signs on various school campuses and would not result in the addition of new classrooms or other facilities that would increase the number of classrooms or seating capacity. Therefore, the application of a Class 14 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): Section 15300.2 of the State CEQA Guidelines specifies several exceptions to the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources including, but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified Environmental Impact Report (EIR).
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Analysis: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. The proposed marquee signs would be installed on existing school campuses in urbanized communities. All areas on the schools where the marquees would be installed are either paved, disturbed, or comprised of non-native landscaping. No trees will be removed to accommodate any of the marquee installations. Therefore, none of the project sites are considered environmentally sensitive. Additionally, the proposed marquee signs would either be mounted onto the face of an existing building or, in the case of Longfellow Elementary School, on a double pole mount, and would require minimal ground disturbance. Ground disturbance would not occur within native soils and would take place on existing disturbed areas. As such, the Proposed Project would not impact areas that have environmental resources of hazardous or critical concern. This exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. The proposed improvements that would be implemented by the District at each project site concurrent with other projects are described below:
  • Longfellow Elementary School: The proposed marquee would be installed between September 2018 and the end of October 2018. The Whole Site Modernization Project is being completed this summer and is currently beneficially occupied.
  • Rolando Park Elementary School: The proposed marquee would be installed between September 2018 and the end of October 2018. There are no other known projects proposed for construction concurrent with the proposed marquee.
  • Webster Elementary School: The proposed marquee would be installed between September 2018 and the end of October 2018. There are no other known projects proposed for construction concurrent with the proposed marquee. Environmental mitigation completed.
  • Wegeforth Elementary School: The proposed marquee would be installed between September 2018 and the end of October 2018. The HVAC Phase II project is scheduled to be completed this summer.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): Section 15300.2 of the State CEQA Guidelines specifies several exceptions to the use of a categorical exemption. These exceptions will also be used to test the use of the general rule exemption for the Proposed Project to determine, with certainty, that there is no possibility that the Proposed Project will have a significant effect on the environment.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified Environmental Impact Report.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): Section 15300.2 of the State CEQA Guidelines specifies several exceptions to the use of a categorical exemption.

 

(a) Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may, in a particularly sensitive environment, be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.

(b) Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.

(c) Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.

(d) Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources including, but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings or similar resources within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.

(e) Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.

(f) Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

 

Environmental Analysis: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the proposed project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project. A more detailed analysis is provided in the attached Mira Mesa HS NOE with Attachment A.

 

Reasons Why Project Is Exempt: The proposed activity is a project subject to the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA). However, it is exempt from further environmental analysis under a Class 14, Minor Additions to Schools categorical exemption, which consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or ten classrooms, whichever is less (CEQA Guidelines 15314).

 

The proposed project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines 15300.2. Consequently, as documented below, the proposed project is exempt from further CEQA review.

(a) Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment, in a particularly sensitive environment may be significant. The proposed project falls under a Class 14 categorical exemption and not within a class that requires additional consideration due to its location. As described in Existing Conditions, the project site is not environmentally sensitive. This exception does not apply to the subject Notice of Exemption.

(b) Cumulative Impact. The project site is within an urbanized area consisting primarily of single-family homes, commercial development, and recreational space. It is not anticipated that there would be a substantial number of other concurrent projects in the immediate vicinity of the proposed project such that construction of the proposed project would contribute to a temporary cumulative impact. In addition to the proposed project at Mira Mesa High School, the District has completed, and is proposing, modernization projects at other District schools; however, these individual projects are all separate from the proposed project. Future proposed projects at District schools would include implementation of standard operating procedures and/or contractor specifications to avoid potential environmental impacts. As such, the proposed projects at other District school sites would not result in impacts that are individually limited, but cumulatively considerable, when viewed in connection with the proposed project. Regarding operation, the proposed project would replace existing classroom facilities. Therefore, the project would not increase student capacity or affect existing academic-related operations at the school because temporary classrooms would be replaced with permanent classrooms. Because no project-level impacts would occur with implementation of the proposed project, there would be no cumulative impacts once the project is constructed. The proposed project would not result in a cumulatively considerable impact, and this exception does not apply to the Notice of Exception.

(c) Significant Effect. Construction of the project would last an estimated 19 months, with some of the process occurring during the summer months when school is not in session to avoid disruptions to students and faculty. All construction would occur within the existing boundaries of the school property. Some construction activities would occur when school is in session. If construction must occur when school is in session, the hours for construction activities may be limited to between 2 p.m. and 7 p.m. to avoid disruptions to students and faculty. The District would implement a number of standard operating procedures or contractor specifications to ensure compliance with federal and state environmental regulations. Short-term construction-related impacts would not generate a significant effect. Operation of the new buildings and various improvements would be similar to existing conditions at the school. The improvements would not result in an increase in the number of students or require expanded operational procedures. Therefore, operation of the project would not generate a significant effect and this exception does not apply to the Notice of Exemption.

(d) Scenic Highways. There are no scenic highways in the vicinity of the proposed project, nor is the project site visible from a scenic highway. Officially Designated State Scenic Highways within the City and County of San Diego include portions of State Route (SR-) 75, SR-78, SR-125, and SR-163, none of which are in the vicinity of the project site (California Department of Transportation 2015). The nearest designated scenic highway, SR-163, is approximately 4 miles to the south. Therefore, no impact to scenic highways would occur, and this exception does not apply to the Notice of Exemption.

(e) Hazardous Waste Sites. The proposed project would occur entirely within the existing school property where there are no active hazardous materials/wastes sites or open cases. This exception does not apply to the Notice of Exemption.

(f) Historical Resources. Mira Mesa High School was constructed in 1976, and therefore does not meet the 50-year age threshold for consideration as a potential historical resource. Additionally, the District does not consider the school site to have historical significance. This exception does not apply to the Notice of Exemption.

The draft position descriptions were prepared using the data collected from employees through an online survey tool, as well as management feedback. The goal of this system-wide review of classified and classified management position descriptions is to ensure that our position descriptions are more than a list of tasks and duties. A well-written position description gives the reader an understanding of the basic and essential functions as well as the requisite knowledge, skills, and abilities required to perform successfully in the position. By updating our position descriptions, we are able to clearly define our roles, our interactions, and enhance the efficiency of our organization.

 

The bargaining units received the first set of draft job descriptions on April 7, 2017, the next set on September 28, 2017, and the last group of recommended changes in January 2018. As job description revisions are a mandatory subject of bargaining, the parties have scheduled times to meet to negotiate the recommendations. The recommendation presented today has been negotiated with the AASD unit and is presented to the Board of Education for approval. As negotiated agreements are reached with the other employee groups, those recommendations will be brought forward at future board meetings.
