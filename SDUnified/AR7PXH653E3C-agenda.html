The mission of PTA is to make every child’s potential a reality by engaging and empowering families and communities to advocate for all children.

BACKGROUND:

 

As part of California's new school accountability system aligned with the Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF) and the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP), accountability results will be reported via the California School Dashboard (Dashboard). In addition to several state indicators (e.g., student scores from the Smarter Balanced assessments, English learner progress, graduation rates), Local Educational Agencies (LEAs) are required to report outcomes on four local indicators that apply to LEAs only, not schools:

 

  1. Appropriately Assigned Teachers, Access to Curriculum-Aligned Instructional Materials, and Safe, Clean and Functional School Facilities (State Priority 1)
    • Standard: LEA annually measures its progress in meeting the Williams Settlement requirements at all of its school sites, as applicable, and promptly addresses any complaints or other deficiencies identified throughout the academic year, as applicable.
  2. Implementation of State Academic Standards (State Priority 2)
    • Standard: LEA annually measures its progress implementing state academic standards.
  3. Parent Engagement (State Priority 3)
    • Standard: LEA annually measures its progress in (1) seeking input from parents in decision making and (2) promoting parental participation in programs.
  4. School Climate (State Priority 6)
    • Standard: LEA administers a local climate survey at least every other year that provides a valid measure of perceptions of school safety and connectedness, such as the California Healthy Kids Survey, to students in at least one grade within the grade span(s) that the LEA serves (e.g., K-5, 6-8, 9-12).

Prior to finalizing information reported via the Dashboard, LEAs are required to have their governing boards review the information at a regularly scheduled public meeting. The information below and in the attached exhibits, present presents some new information and also supplement supplements information that was included in the 2017–18 LCAP that was adopted by the Board of Education on June 27, 2017. 

 

The three performance levels for the local indicators are: Met, Not Met, and Not Met for Two or More Years. To report that the standard is met on a local indicator, LEAs must demonstrate that they have collected and reported performance data for each local indicator. This agenda item fulfills the requirements and therefore, the district has met the standard for each of the local indicators.

BACKGROUND:

The California Department of Education (CDE) requires a school board to adopt a resolution that the acquisition of property for a new school site has met certain requirements. Specifically, Education Code Section 17213 prohibits a school district from purchasing a school site, and Public Resources Code Section 21151.8 prohibits a school district from approving an Environmental Impact Report (EIR), unless the governing board of the school district finds that the proposed school site is not:

  1. The site of a current or former hazardous waste disposal or solid waste disposal site (unless the wastes have been removed);
  2. A hazardous substance release site;
  3. A site that contains one or more pipelines, situated underground or aboveground, that carries hazardous substances, extremely hazardous substances, or hazardous wastes, unless the pipeline is a natural gas line which is used only to supply natural gas to that school or neighborhood or other nearby schools;

On July 25, 2017, the Board of Education (Board) approved a California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) Addendum to the Program EIR for the Quarry Falls (Civita) Specific Plan Project, to evaluate the acquisition, construction, and operation of the proposed Civita Elementary School. The CEQA Addendum found that the proposed project would not result in new significant impacts beyond those already identified and mitigated for in the previously approved EIR, or result in substantially more severe impacts than disclosed in the previously approved EIR. The CEQA addendum also evaluated the proposed Civita Elementary School site pursuant to the Education Code and CDE’s school site selection standards. A summary of the evaluation used as the basis for the required resolution is provided below. All referenced documents can be found at: https://www.sandiegounified.org/environmental-reviews

 

  1. The site of a current or former hazardous waste disposal or solid waste disposal site. As noted in Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA) report prepared for the Civita Elementary School Project, the site is not considered a ‘hazardous waste property,’ as defined by Section 25221 of the California Health and Safety Code. Additionally, the site is not considered a current or former ‘hazardous waste disposal site’ or ‘solid waste disposal site’ in accordance with Section 17213 of the California Education Code. Therefore, the proposed Civita Elementary School site is not the site of a current or former hazardous waste disposal or solid waste disposal site.
  2. A hazardous substance release site. According to the California Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) EnviroStor database, the state Department of Health has identified no hazardous substance release sites on or near the site of the proposed school project. Furthermore, DTSC issued a letter on March 24, 2017, and determined that the proposed Civita Elementary School site does not pose a significant risk to students and staff and is suitable for use as a school. Therefore, the proposed Civita Elementary School site is not on a hazardous substance release site.
  3. A site with hazardous pipelines. The Phase I ESA prepared for the proposed Civita Elementary School Project identified portions of a natural gas pipeline within 1,500 feet of the proposed school site. A pipeline risk analysis was prepared and found that adjacent natural gas pipeline segments would result in an individual fatality risk of 4.3E-9, which is substantially below the CDE maximum individual fatality risk criterion of 1.0E-06. Therefore, the proposed Civita Elementary School site is not a site that contains one or more pipelines, situated underground or aboveground, that carries hazardous substances, extremely hazardous substances, or hazardous wastes.
  4. A site within 500 feet of a major roadway. The proposed school site is three-quarters of a mile from Interstate 805, the nearest major highway and outside of the identified 500 foot threshold. Therefore, the proposed Civita Elementary School site is not a site within 500 feet of the edge of the closest traffic lane of a freeway or other busy traffic corridor as defined in Education Code Section 17213(d)(9) and Public Resources Code 21151.8(c)(9).
  5. Hazardous air emissions and facilities within 0.25 mile of the school site. The District consulted with the San Diego Air Pollution Control District (APCD) to identify hazardous air emitters within one-quarter mile of the proposed school site. The APCD identified an existing concrete batch plant, which has since been relocated approximately 2,500 feet east of the project site. Additionally, the APCD identified an emergency generator at the now closed Sports Authority store, located 0.25 miles southeast of the project site at 8550 Rio San Diego Drive. A formal health risk assessment to evaluate the potential health risk from locating a school facility within 0.25 miles of this diesel‐powered standby generator was prepared. The results of the assessment, which modeled for cancer health risk, found that the 25‐year worker and 9‐year student exposure at the Civita Elementary School site would be 0.000059 in 1,000,000 (5.19e‐05) and 0.000419 in 1,000,000 (4.19e‐04) respectively, both of which are below the public notification level of 10 in 1,000,000. Therefore, the proposed Civita Elementary School site is not located within 0.25 miles of hazardous air emitters and/or emissions and facilities.
