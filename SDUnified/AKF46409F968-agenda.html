The founding of the United States is intertwined with the historical settlement and development of the Southwest by descendants of Spanish settlers and indigenous peoples.

 

The Latino population comprises 57 million residents of the United States of America and continues to contribute significantly to the political, economic, and social development of the nation.

 

San Diego County has a population of over 991,000 Latinos and San Diego Unified School District serves over 59,000 Latino students within its educational system.

 

The San Diego Unified School District recognizes the diversity reflected within the City of San Diego as well as its students and staff and respects the contributions, culture, and heritage of Latinos in this community.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution recognizing September 2017 as Attendance Awareness Month in San Diego Unified.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education recognizes that good attendance is essential to student achievement and graduation, and is committed to enhancing the connection between school attendance and academic achievement.

 

Children who are chronically absent in preschool, kindergarten, and first grade are much less likely to read at grade level by the third grade, and students who cannot read at grade level by the end of third grade are four times more likely than proficient readers to drop out of high school.

           

Chronic absence becomes, by sixth grade, a leading indicator that a student will drop out of high school.

 

Since 2015, San Diego Unified attendance rates have been above 95 percent, with the 2017 attendance rate at 95.2 percent and the 2016 drop-out rate at 3.4 percent.

 

Closing the achievement gap requires a serious and sustained focus on chronically absent students due to the correlation between absenteeism and test scores, graduation, and dropout rates.

 

San Diego Unified is committed to supporting positive school attendance, as well as monitoring and addressing attendance at the first signs of chronic absence.

 

San Diego Unified has partnered with several organizations to promote regular attendance such as the Attendance Works 2017 Attendance Summit, United Way of San Diego’s Every Student Every Day Program, and California School-Based Health Alliance & District School-Based Health Centers.

 

The district recognizes the importance of partnering with schools, families and communities to create and promote a positive culture of daily attendance for all students and significantly, thus reducing chronic absenteeism by understanding and removing barriers that keep children in grades kindergarten through twelve from getting to school.

Pursuant to Proposition S, the district shall only issue bonds if it projects that the tax rate necessary to pay debt service on the proposed bonds, when combined with the tax rate necessary to pay debt service on presently outstanding bonds of the district in each year, will not exceed the rate levied in 2007-08 (being $66.70 per $100,000 of assessed valuation) to pay debt service on the presently outstanding bonds alone, referring to bonds issued under Propositions MM and S. The bond issue is being structured to be in compliance with AB 182 with a term of 25 years, call feature after year 10, and a projected repayment ratio of less than 4:1 for all series of bonds.

As part of the current plan of finance for the Proposition S Bond Program, the district anticipates issuing one or more series of bonds, as needed, every other year, until the full authorization has been issued.  The plan proposes the sale of bonds that allow for the compounding of interest.  The district's financial advisor and underwriting team will discuss the bond financing model and available options for upcoming Proposition S new monies transactions and recent Proposition S issuances.
