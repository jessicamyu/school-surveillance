3. Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing June 2022 as LGBTQIA+ Pride Month
Meeting

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of recognizing June 2022, as LGBTQIA+ Pride Month, and use this opportunity to increase awareness and visibility around the LGBTQIA+ community and renew our ongoing commitment and education plan to promote safe and affirming school spaces for students, families, and educators that focuses on  intersectional diversity, acceptance, and unconditional support for our LGBTQIA+ students, families, and  educators. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer, Intersex, Asexual, plus (LGBTQIA+) Pride Month is celebrated in June each year to honor the 1969 Stonewall Uprising in Manhattan. The Stonewall Uprising was a tipping point for the Gay Liberation Movement in the United States and was led by two transgender women of color, Marsha P. Johnson and Sylvia Rivera. President Bill Clinton declared June "Gay and Lesbian Pride Month" in 1999 and 2000; and from 2009 to 2016, each year he was in office, President Barack Obama declared June LGBT Pride Month, and on May 28, 2010 issued Proclamation 8529, saying, “I call upon all Americans to observe this month by fighting prejudice and discrimination in their own lives and everywhere it exists.” In 2020, during the 50th anniversary of Pride, presidential candidate and current President Joe Biden stated the following, “Pride has come to be recognized as a global movement of love, self-expression, and community - resilient in the face of oppression and fear and hopeful for a better future. This month, let us recommit to those principles of Pride and remain steadfast in the fight for justice and equality.” The San Diego Unified School District is committed to safety, inclusion, and affirmation for all students through developing and coordinating activities, training, and opportunities for the SDUSD community including: 

 

  • Creating visibility and awareness exhibits at the Eugene Brucker Education Center and on district  campuses to honor the month and all students, staff, and families who identify as LGBTQIA+, such as flying the LGBTQIA+ Pride Rainbow flag and Transgender Pride flag; and  
  • Implementing history and social studies curriculum that highlights the contributions of members of the  LGBTQIA+ community and allows students to see themselves accurately reflected, as prescribed by the FAIR Education Act, SB 48, which was signed into law on July 14, 2011; and 
  • Offering ongoing and student-centered opportunities for education, advocacy, and advancement for  LGBTQIA+ students, families, and staff; and 
  • The adoption and implementation of comprehensive bullying/harassment policies that specifically enumerate sexual orientation, gender identity, and gender expression with clear and effective systems for reporting and addressing incidents that students experience. 

 

A hostile school climate affects students’ academic success and mental health. LGBTQIA+ students who experience victimization and discrimination at school have poorer educational outcomes and psychological well-being. The availability and the utility of LGBTQIA+-related school resources and supports help offset the negative effects of a hostile school climate and promote a positive learning experience. A month of observance is one critical component of our ongoing collective resolution to continue dismantling racist, homophobic, transphobic, ablist dynamics and other forms of oppression within our school system in order to maximize the accessibility of our educational spaces and facilities for ALL students.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of recognizing June 19, 2022, as Juneteenth, 

a day of celebration of the past, present, and future of Black resilience, culture, and liberation. We acknowledge that “Black Lives Matter” and work to center students in our efforts to “build local power to intervene in violence inflicted on Black communities … by combating and countering acts of violence, creating space for Black imagination and innovation, and centering Black joy” (Black Lives Matter Global Network Foundation, Inc.). We call on educators to teach the importance of Juneteenth and movements such as “Black Lives Matter” that advance liberation efforts for the Black community with classroom lessons throughout the year; just as we fight for equity, justice, and basic human rights through each interaction with youth, the principles of the Black Lives Matter movement and the promise of liberation that Juneteenth celebrates should be explicitly talked about and discussed year-round. We encourage staff, students, and community members to use this day to reflect on the historical oppression of the Black community and engage in individual and collective action to break the cycle of systemic racism that exists today through commitment to education, self-improvement, advocacy, and activism.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Juneteenth is the oldest celebration of the emancipation of African American slaves. Juneteenth celebrates the final emancipation of slaves in the United States and the persistent drive and determination to seek freedom, liberty, and equality in accordance with the Emancipation Proclamation issued January 1, 1863, and the 13th Amendment to the Constitution which abolished slavery, except section 1, ratified by Congress on December 6, 1865. President Abraham Lincoln first issued the Emancipation Proclamation effective January 1, 1863, freeing the slaves in the South. On June 19th, 1865, Union soldiers arrived in Galveston, Texas and enforced the president’s order, freeing the final African American slaves two and a half years after it was first decreed. Through other systems of oppression, such as sharecropping, Jim Crow, redlining, Black Codes, mass incarceration, exclusionary policies, police violence, and segregation, the exploitation of the Black community continued past slavery and persists to this day, affecting the physical and mental health, safety, and education of the Black community. Liberation from these systems of oppression must include a commitment to dismantle policies and practices that perpetuate systemic racism and replace them with anti-racist educational systems that center BIPOC voices, stories, healing, and joy and create new structures that require critical self-awareness, deconstructs white supremacy culture, and interrupts anti-blackness. The accurate representation of the contributions and experiences of the African American community were, for generations, and continue to be, systematically excluded from and misrepresented in history textbooks, mainstream media, and classroom discussions teaching San Diego Unified students about Black history and contemporary events is a critical affirmation of the fact Black history is American history. The San Diego Unified School District opposes and rejects any form of oppression and pledges to support the Black community and their intersecting identities by working to achieve genuine equity, access, and success for our students, employees, and communities which we serve.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of support for voter engagement, and in advance of upcoming elections, the San Diego Unified School District shall provide high school students with resources regarding voter registration, including how to pre-register to vote and where to vote; and shall prominently display instructions for students on how to register to vote in the State of California. The San Diego Unified School District hereby encourages 16 and 17-year-olds to actively participate in the voting process. The San Diego Unified School District hereby encourages schools and students to accept the Dr. Shirley N. Weber Award Youth Voter Registration Challenge and register to vote, get out the vote, and vote. The San Diego Unified School District will work to educate extended community families through multilingual communications to help increase the number of underrepresented community members and students to register and vote.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: While many young people ages 18-21 are active in social movements and hold a strong belief that their generation can achieve change, this activism does not automatically translate to increased likelihood to engage in the voting process. The San Diego Unified School District believes voting is a core tenant of the country, and is essential to the operation of a thriving global democracy. In recent years a resurgence of youth activism has led to increased youth participation in key social movements including those related to gun violence, climate change, racial justice, and abortion rights. When students are properly educated on the importance of voting and given the opportunity to vote they will be significantly more likely to cast a ballot. According to the American Journal of Political Science voting in one election can increase the probability that a person will vote in the next election by 25 percent. Required history, government, and civics courses in 11th grade and 12th grade offer ideal environments for students to learn about the voting process and register to vote. The state of California allows youth ages 16 and 17 to pre-register to vote in order to ensure automatic registration upon the age of 18. The San Diego Unified School District promotes civically engaged learning content and curriculum meant to spark student engagement in an increasingly interconnected and global world. The San Diego Unified School District currently awards the California State Seal of Civic Engagement recognizing youth striving to make transformational change in their schools and communities. The state of California’s 2022 primary election will be on June 7, 2022, with the midterm election on November 8, 2022. Eighteen school sites currently serve as polling stations for the 2022 election season. High school students have served as election poll workers or observers during previous elections. Students ages 18 and over may be excused from class to vote on election day. San Diego City Mayor Todd Gloria has established the Dr. Shirley N. Weber Award for Youth Voter Registration to recognize students who have registered to vote. The San Diego Unified School District strives to ensure all students of voting age have the knowledge of how to access the necessary resources to cast a vote for candidates they feel represent their values.

6. Resolution in the Matter of Support for Senate Bill 1236 to Amend Section 35012 of the Education Code (Glazer/Bogh) (Patterson/Barrera)
Meeting
May 24, 2022 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Fiscal Impact
No

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of support for Senate Bill 1236 to amend Section 35012 of the Education Code (Glazer/Bogh). Senate Bill 1236 would authorize local school boards to grant their pupil board members expanded rights up to and including:

 

1) full-voting rights

2) the right to make and second motions on open session matters, including those related to employer-employee relations

3) the right to receive closed session materials and to attend and cast a preferential vote on matters subject to closed session discussion.

 

Senate Bill 1236 would, further:

 

1) require a governing board with six members as a result of adding a full voting pupil member to establish an affirmative vote and quorum at four members

2) prohibit a governing board from abolishing the pupil board member position once established

3) permit a governing board, when adopting a resolution authorizing the change of the preferential voting pupil member position to the full voting member position, approve a motion restricting the pupil member from voting on pupil disciplinary matters if the pupil member attends the same school as the pupil under consideration or shares a direct association to the pupil in some capacity, negative personnel matters if the pupil member shares a direct association to a classified or certificated employee under consideration in the matter and other matters specifically called out in the motion

4) require a unanimous vote of the governing board to approve subsequent action to further restrict the pupil member from voting on certain matters

5) permit the governing board to pay a stipend to the pupil board member. 


 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District believe students are the primary stakeholders of the education system and deserve representation. According to the most recent data from the California Department of Education School Climate survey only 30% of  San Diego Unified secondary students reported meaningful participation in the creation of school activities and rules. The San Diego Unified School District believes that steps should be taken to expand the formal role of diverse and equitable student voices in shaping district policy. The San Diego Unified School District solicits student feedback regarding District initiatives from student organizations including, but not limited to, its Student Advisory Board, Council of Associated Student Body (ASB) Presidents, Student Equity Ambassadors, and Student Wellness Education and Resources Committee. The San Diego Unified School District currently has students sit on a number of its advisory committees including, but not limited to, the Independent Citizens Oversight Committee on Racial Justice and Equity, the Community Schools Advisory Committee, the Sexual Health Education Advisory Committee, the District Wellness Council, and the District’s Calendar Committee. The San Diego Unified School District believes the Student Board Member position offers a critical connection between students and the school board and that allowing students formal representation on the governing board is essential to the democratic process. The San Diego Unified School District strives to support legislation expanding the fundamental right of representation of District students. The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District supports Senate Bill 1236 and urges the State Legislature to approve the bill and the Governor to sign the bill into law.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve postponing the implementation of the student vaccine mandate as a condition of attending in-person learning and participation in extracurricular activities, for students within the designated grade levels or age groups as set forth by the California Department of Public Health, until the state proceeds but no earlier than July 2023.

 

Approve reinstating a student vaccination mandate for July 2023 or later implementation based on the data and conditions at the time, which will be brought to the Board for review and action prior to implementation.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District is working to ensure the highest-quality instruction in the safest environment possible for all students and employees. On September 28, 2021 the board took action to mandate a vaccine for all district employees and students, with the student vaccination requirement applying to student age groups after full FDA approval of a vaccination for that age group. The action and future actions were and are guided by the interests of promoting and protecting the health and safety of students, promoting a safer overall educational environment and community by increasing and maximizing vaccination rates, promoting academic progress through educationally-sound and logical implementation dates of the vaccination requirement, and complying with legal obligations including federal and state law and collective bargaining agreements. Mandate implementation was set for January 2022, but was delayed due to operational concerns and considerations prohibiting feasible implementation of the mandate on the schedule originally adopted by the Board. A trial court ruling did not delay later implementation, because a stay of that ruling was granted by the Court of Appeal on February 1, 2022, allowing us to proceed.

 

On February 22, 2022 and March 8, 2022 the Board approved revisions to the Student Vaccine Mandate Plan including but not limited to implementation dates for summer 2022 programs, fall 2023 extracurricular activities, and fall 2023 in-person instruction. The mandate continued to apply to all students unless medically-exempted, but to ensure student safety immediate implementation remained applicable to students 16 and older because full FDA approval of a COVID-19 vaccine has not yet occurred for younger age groups.

 

Since the March 8, 2022 Board action, changed circumstances including the current scientific data and current pandemic conditions have prompted delays in the implementation of other state and local student COVID vaccination requirements.  Those changed circumstances, and the success of the District’s efforts to increase vaccination rates in our communities and other mitigation efforts, result in District staff recommending that the vaccination plan be postponed to at least July, 2023. Furthermore, because we do not know what the circumstances will be a year from now, if a vaccine mandate plan is implemented in July 2023 or after, the parameters of the plan will be based on the science and conditions at that time.

RECOMMENDATIONS: Approve the use of California Education Code §44258.3 as the local assignment option for the use of an alternative means for completion of the prescribed course of study for high school graduation in Physical Education per Subsection (b) of Education Code §51225.3.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Approval of this local assignment option provides an alternative means for completion of the prescribed course of study for high school graduation in Physical Education.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: As identified in the District’s Course of Study, certain Basic Military Drill (BMD) and Junior Reserve Officer Training Corps (JROTC) courses provide Physical Education credit. Teachers are deemed qualified by the credential held. All Basic Military Drill (“BMD”)/ Junior Reserve Officer Training Corps (“JROTC”) teachers hold a Designated Subjects Special Subjects Teaching Credential (DSSS); they must add the Teaching Assignment Option or a Special Teaching Assignment (“STA”) allow these individuals to grant credit in PE.  

 

Approval of amendments to Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations (CCR) section 80037 pertaining to Designated Subjects Special Subjects Teaching Credentials established a Special Teaching Authorization (STA) in Physical Education effective January 1, 2015. Holders of a DSSS credential in BMD and JROTC may elect to add the STA in Physical Education upon completion of specific requirements.  The STA in Physical Education added to a DSSS credential in BMD or ROTC will provide the holder with a distinct physical education authorization limited to the areas of basic military drill and physical fitness training. The STA will not authorize instruction in any physical education courses that are offered outside of BMD and JROTC programs.

 

Holders of DSSS credentials in BMD and JROTC who do not have the STA listed on their documents will still be authorized, by school board action, to teach Physical Education in the context of BMD or JROTC courses that have been approved to carry high school graduation credit for Physical Education per Education Code section 44258.3. Education Code section 44258.3 allows local school districts to assign any credential holder to teach departmentalized classes, with their consent, in Grades K-12 irrespective of the designations on their teaching credentials. The teacher's competence must be verified according to policies and procedures established by the governing board consistent with the language of the statute; Board Policy 4113 and the accompanying AR: Assignment, along with Administrative Regulation 6142.7: Physical Education and Activity fulfill this requirement.

 

For teachers who have not yet earned the STA, the Teaching Assignment Option is used. The teacher agrees to teach the BMD/ JROTC course for PE credit. Then, the Assignment Review Panel verifies the teacher's qualifications.  The Assignment Review Panel's criteria includes, but need not be limited to, evidence of the candidate's knowledge of the subject matter to be taught, including demonstrated knowledge of the curriculum framework for the subject to be taught and the specific content of the course of study in the school district for the subject, at the grade level to be taught. The approval is for one year and may be extended for additional time if the teacher and district consent.
