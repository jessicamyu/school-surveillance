The Office of School Choice recommends the Board of Education grant the charter petition for King Chavez Community High School for a five-year school term beginning on July 1, 2008 and expiring on June 30, 2013. The Office of School Choice recommends the adoption of the staff report as findings in support of the Board’s decision to grant the charter petition. The potential fiscal impact to SDUSD is an estimated loss of $580,000 generated in average daily attendance in the first year because SDUSD does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools. King Chavez Community High School (KCCHS) petitioners submitted their petition for review to the Office of School Choice on December 14, 2007. A public hearing for the petition was held on February 12, 2008. The Office of School Choice has been working with the petitioners to ensure that the petition meets legal and District requirements. Based on a departmental and legal staff review the petitioners were provided with substantive feedback on February 21, 2008. The petitioners were asked to address deficiencies in 13 of the 16 elements, as well as concerns with the addendum portions (elements 17-28). KCCHS petitioners included a charter term that is invalid since it indicates that the term would begin on July 1, 2009. The approval of the charter petition shall become effective July 1, 2008, and shall expire on June 30, 2013. [Originator-Office of School Choice]

F.01. Board Policy Changes to the Facilities Major Repair and Replacement Program
Meeting
Apr 8, 2008 - 04/08/2008
Type
Action (Consent)
Fiscal Impact

Budgeted
No
Budget Source
Not Applicable

On February 12, 2008, the Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) presented to the Board policy recommendations to update the Board policies affecting the facilities Major repair and replacement (MRR) program. The policy changes and additions attached provide improved understanding of facility status and program direction, and a more integrated approach to managing the District’s largest physical assets. Ensuring proper evaluation regarding the District facilities provides the Board the requisite resource management information to make informed decisions.

These policy changes seek the coordinated and comprehensive planning needed to achieve effective long-range strategies that use multiple funding sources appropriately; i.e. annually recurring funds for maintenance and short-lived repairs, and long term debt for repairs that are still within their useful service life after that debt has been paid off. In addition, these policies will ensure that: 1) actual funding needs are effectively communicated to the community, and 2) budget decisions are informed decisions. [Originator: Office of the Chief Administrative Officer]

Meeting

Type

Fiscal Impact

Dollar Amount

Budgeted
Yes
Budget Source
Risk Management Insurance Funding
On March 18, 2008, electrical distribution cables were cut and removed/stolen from a section of underground vaults which feed electrical power to the south end of the Correia Middle School campus, including Buildings 100, 200, 300 700, 800 900 and all portable classrooms, resulting in the loss of electrical power, fire alarm, security, phone, data and heating/cooling systems.

Public Contract Code Section 20113(a) states, In an emergency when any repairs, alterations, work, or improvement is necessary to any facility of public schools to permit the continuance of existing school classes, or to avoid danger to life or property, the board may, by unanimous vote, with the approval of the county superintendent of schools, do either of the following: (1) Make a contract in writing or otherwise on behalf of the district for the performance of labor and furnishing of materials or supplies for the purpose without advertising for or inviting bids. (2) Notwithstanding Section 20114, authorize the use of day labor or force account for the purpose. (3) Nothing in this section shall eliminate the need for any bonds or security otherwise required by law.

District legal staff has written an opinion on emergency declaration, shared with the interim chief facilities officer and the Board of Education, and concurs that this action is justified and within the stipulations of the Public Contract Code. The District secured Southland Electric, Inc. to perform the work of repairing and replacing the missing electrical distribution cabling. It is anticipated that the installation of the new cabling will be completed prior to the students and staff returning from Spring break on March 24, 2008.

Costs to repair and restore power to the buildings will not exceed $30,000. [Originator-Interim Executive Director, Facilities/Funding Source-Risk Management funds] This item requires a unanimous (5/0) vote by the Board.
