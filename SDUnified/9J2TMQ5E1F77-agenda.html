RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution in the matter of recognizing September as Attendance Awareness Month.

 

The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education recognizes the connection between school attendance and academic achievement and acknowledges the importance of working together with schools, families, and communities to promote a positive culture of daily attendance for all students and significantly reduce chronic absenteeism by addressing barriers that keep children from getting to school.

 

Improving school climate, reducing instruction missed due to discipline issues, and implementing school wide positive behavior intervention and support systems and restorative justice practices can have a significant impact on improving attendance and schools and school districts must do more to track, calculate, and share data on how many and which pupils are chronically absent so that schools and communities can work to deliver the right interventions for the right pupils.

 

The district’s primary source of revenue is based on average daily attendance (ADA). The district loses $44.57 per day for every absent student and given the ongoing budget crisis, it is crucial to note that a 1 percent increase in attendance would translate to an additional $6 million in revenue for the district.

 

San Diego Unified School District is committed to monitoring and addressing attendance at the first signs of chronic absence.

 

Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District declares September as Attendance Awareness Month and the Superintendent and other appropriate staff will continue to provide assistance to raise public awareness on the importance of improved attendance.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve Third Amendment to Agreement with the California Department of Education to provide Early Childhood Education Child Development Program services (PS-14-0713-26; CSPP 3445).  The term of the Agreement is unchanged and remains July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2014.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: For the period July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2014: $14,483,812.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: None.

 

BACKGROUND:  On December 10, 2013, the board approved an agreement with the California Department of Education to provide funding in the amount of $18,019,341.00 to provide services for district Early Education State Pre-School Programs for the term July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2014.

 

On June 24, 2014, the board approved a First Amendment to Agreement that added an additional $264,471.00 for a new total revenue amount of $18,283,812.00 and increased the minimum Child Days of Enrollment (CDE) from 537,570 to 545,460.  The term remained unchanged and there were no other changes.

 

On July 8, 2014, the board approved a Second Amendment that decreased the revenue amount by $3,800,000.00 from $18,283,812.00 for a new total revenue amount of $14,483,812.00 and reduced the minimum CDE from 545,460 to 432,095. The term remained unchanged and there were no other changes.

 

This Third Amendment, in consideration of changes in the district calendar made when furlough days were restored to teachers, increases the number of Minimum Days of Operation (MDO) from 237 to 241. The term remains unchanged and there are no other changes.
