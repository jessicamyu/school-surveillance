1. Resolution in Support of the Board's Commitment to Preserve and Repurpose Certain Properties on the Normal Street Campus (Barrera/Beiser)

Jul 24, 2018 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Board Trustees Richard Barrera and Kevin Beiser will discuss and request the Board adopt the Resolution in Support of the Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District Commitment to Preserve and Repurpose Certain Properties on the Normal Street Campus which provides,

 

To the extent permitted by all relevant laws, and to the extent made feasible by voter approval of the “San Diego Neighborhood School Repair and Student Safety Measure,” the Board hereby affirms the District’s commitment to work collaboratively with stakeholders in the University Heights Community, the City of San Diego, and all other relevant public agencies, to successfully complete the following projects:

  1. Adaptively reuse Teachers Training Annex 1 as a teacher training facility and potential joint-use library or community education facility to support students and neighborhood families;
  2. Restore and install the original University Heights Library mural inside Annex 1;
  3. Adaptively reuse the Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2 as a community resource facility for joint-use to support students and neighborhood families;
  4. Install wireless facilities and solar panels on the Teachers Training Annex 1 and the Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2 to offset maintenance and energy costs;
  5. Explore the feasibility of a paid parking structure with electric vehicle charging stations and solar panels for joint-use to support students and neighborhood families;
  6. Create a collaborative outdoor learning space and green space on the lawn in front of Annex 1 for joint-use to support students and neighborhood families.

The Superintendent will establish a task force made up of District and community representatives to plan and carry out actions necessary to complete the projects listed above.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None at this time.  Future costs are unknown at this time

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: San Diego Normal School was built at 4100 Normal Street in University Heights by the State of California in 1897 as a training facility for elementary school teachers, renamed the San Diego State Teachers College in 1921, moved to Montezuma Mesa in 1930, and renamed San Diego State University in 1974.  The Teachers Training Annex 1 was built in 1910 by the State of California adjacent to the San Diego Normal School as a training laboratory for student teachers who were observed as they provided instruction to school-age children.  The Teachers Training Annex 1 was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1999 through the efforts of the University Heights Historical Society.

 

The University Heights community has always had a deep and abiding connection to its early beginnings as a historic educational district.  A community financed historic survey conducted by an architectural historian in 2009 identified the following buildings on the Education Center site at 4100 Normal Street as historic or potentially historic: Teachers Training Annex 1, Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2, San Diego Normal School Drafting Classroom & Men’s Locker Room, Horace Mann Jr. High School Domestic Science Hall & Band Room, Horace Mann Jr. High School Girl’s Showers & Locker Room, Birney School/Horace Mann Jr. High School Teachers Cafeteria Building, Alice Birney Elementary School, and San Diego City Schools Eugene Brucker Education Center. 

 

A University Heights Task Force was formed in 2001 to explore possibilities with the San Diego Unified School District for joint use of the Teachers Training Annex 1 and the Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2.  $250,000 was set aside by the San Diego City Council in 2002 at the behest of local organizations for a Feasibility Study of the Teachers Training Annex 1.  Community members worked with the San Diego Unified School District leadership in 2009 and 2011 to engage its support for retaining and preserving the historic Teachers Training Annex 1 and the Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2 as community resources.  The Community Coalition of University Heights and the University Heights Community Association strongly support preservation and adaptive reuse of all historic buildings on the Education Center site, and preservation or expansion of the public green space in front of the Teachers Training Annex.  San Diego Unified School District supports the University Heights Community’s past and present endeavors for the preservation and future uses of the Teachers Training Annex 1 and Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2, and preservation or expansion of the public green space in front of the Teachers Training Annex to benefit the needs of neighborhoods south of Interstate 8.

 

On July 10, 2018, the Board of Education (Board) of the San Diego Unified School District (District), approved a Resolution to place the “San Diego Neighborhood School Repair and Student Safety Measure” on the general election ballot of November 6, 2018.

 

To the extent permitted by all relevant laws, and to the extent made feasible by voter approval of the “San Diego Neighborhood School Repair and Student Safety Measure,” that the Board hereby affirms the District’s commitment to work collaboratively with stakeholders in the University Heights Community, the City of San Diego, and all other relevant public agencies, to successfully complete the following projects:

  1. Adaptively reuse Teachers Training Annex 1 as a teacher training facility and potential joint-use library or community education facility to support students and neighborhood families;
  2. Restore and install the original University Heights Library mural inside Annex 1;
  3. Adaptively reuse the Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2 as a community resource facility for joint-use to support students and neighborhood families;
  4. Install wireless facilities and solar panels on the Teachers Training Annex 1 and the Alice Birney Kindergarten Building Annex 2 to offset maintenance and energy costs;
  5. Explore the feasibility of a paid parking structure with electric vehicle charging stations and solar panels for joint-use to support students and neighborhood families;
  6. Create a collaborative outdoor learning space and green space on the lawn in front of Annex 1 for joint-use to support students and neighborhood families.

Further, the Superintendent will establish a task force made up of District and community representatives to plan and carry out actions necessary to complete the projects listed above.

RECOMMENDATION: In accordance with Education Code Section 76004, conduct a public hearing, second reading and adoption of the 2018-19 College and Career Readiness Pathways (CCAP) Agreements with the San Diego Community College District for college classes to be conducted on San Diego Unified School District high school campuses. Community college and K-12 districts offering college courses on high school campuses are required to enter into CCAP partnerships that include specific language regarding the targeted number of high school students to be served and the total number of full-time equivalent students (FTES) projected to be claimed by the community college district for those students.

 

  1. SV-19-0077-99 San Diego City College - for various classes at Crawford, Garfield, Hoover, Lincoln, Morse and San Diego High Schools and the School of Creative and Performing Arts (SCPA)
  2. SV-19-0078-99 Mesa College Accelerated College Program (ACP) - for Mathematics and Political Science classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Morse, Point Loma and University City High Schools
  3. SV-19-0079-99 Mesa College - for various classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Mission Bay, Point Loma and University City High Schools
  4. SV-19-0080-99 Miramar College - for various classes at Mira Mesa, Scripps Ranch, Serra and University City High Schools

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. The San Diego Unified School District is eligible for average daily attendance (ADA) revenue for the periods each day that students are enrolled in San Diego Unified courses, and the San Diego Community College District is eligible for apportionment for the period that students are enrolled in City, Mesa, or Miramar College classes.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. As stated in the agreements, each entity agrees to adhere to its respective collective bargaining agreements and no San Diego Community College District or San Diego Unified School District teachers will be displaced by the implementation of these agreements.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Education Code Section 76004 authorizes community college and K-12 districts to enter into College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) partnerships with the goal of developing pathways from high school to community college for career technical or preparation for transfer, improving high school graduation rates, or helping high school students achieve college and career readiness.  Through dual enrollment partnerships, school districts and community college districts can create clear pathways of aligned, sequenced coursework that allow students to more easily and successfully transition to for-credit, college-level coursework leading to an associate degree, transfer to the University of California (UC) of California State University (CSU) systems, or to a program leading to a career technical education certificate.  The 2018-19 CCAP agreements were presented to the San Diego Community College District Board of Trustees for first reading on June 7, 2018, and adopted on July 19, 2018.  The 2018-19 CCAP Agreements were presented to the San Diego Unified Board of Education on July 10, 2018, and are submitted for final reading and adoption.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): Section 15300.2 of the State CEQA Guidelines specifies several exceptions to the use of a categorical exemption.

(a)  Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.

(b)  Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.

(c)  Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.

(d)  Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.

(e)  Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.

(f)  Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

The main objectives of the Agreement are to close race and income participation gaps in AP/IB, raise AP/IB performance, and develop systems and structures for the district to sustain and improve upon these results in later years.

 

The participating schools are: Clairemont, Crawford, Henry, Hoover, La Jolla, Lincoln, Madison, Mira Mesa, Mission Bay, Morse, Point Loma, San Diego, Scripps Ranch, Serra, and University City High Schools. The term of this Agreement extends to September 15, 2021. The continued provision of services under this agreement for subsequent fiscal years is dependent upon the identification of sufficient funds.
