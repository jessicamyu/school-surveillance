CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The District Advisory Council (DAC) on Compensatory Education discussed the Title I Ranking process and allocations on December 15, 2021, and will revisit the allocations at their January 19, 2022, meeting. District staff also reviewed the Title I Ranking Report with the DAC Executive Board on January 5, 2022.

 

BACKGROUND: The Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) of 1965, was reauthorized as of December 10, 2015, as the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA). Title I, which is one of several programs under ESSA, provides funding to schools to be used to help disadvantaged children meet challenging academic standards. The law intends that the funds go to the districts and schools that have the highest concentrations of poverty. Title I requires funding to be allocated to a district based on the number of students who qualify for Title I funding who reside within each district’s boundary. The federal census is used to identify these children, ages 5 through 17, who are living in poverty who are included in the calculation of Title I entitlement.

 

The proposal of the 2022-23 Title I Ranking report allocates Title I funding to all district schools with a 2021-22 LCFF percentage of 40 percent or higher. The per student amount was increased from $502.00 to $558.00 per eligible student and we continue to use the formula to decrease the per student amount by $5.50 for every 1 percent drop in percentage count. This report represents the historical total Title I base allocation to sites of $15.5 million.

 

The total 2022-23 Title I entitlement is approximately $38,000,000.00.

BACKGROUND: Assembly Bill No. 361 (“AB 361”) was enacted by the California Legislature and is intended to “improve and enhance public access to . . . local agency meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic and future applicable emergencies, by allowing broader access through teleconferencing options.” AB 361 permits local legislative bodies subject to the Brown Act and/or the Badgley Keene Act to hold virtual meetings, without needing to publish the physical locations of Board members or make those locations open to the public, if there is a proclaimed state of emergency (pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act) and either: (i) state or local officials have imposed or recommended measures to promote social distancing, or (ii) there is a finding by the local legislative body that, as a result of the emergency, meeting in person “would present imminent risks to the health or safety of attendees.” Governor has proclaimed a state of emergency pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act. Since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, in compliance with public health orders and for the protection all involved persons, the Board of Education (“Board”) has held its meetings telephonically via Zoom and provided for public comments to be provided via teleconferencing rather than allowing in-person attendance by the public at the Education Center. The COVID-19 pandemic continues to present an imminent health risk to the public which require certain specific health and safety measures be maintained. The District’s central office facilities present specific challenges in maintaining COVID-19-related safety protocols required for the health and safety of attendees, which include, but are not limited to, adequate ventilation and security, enforcement of masking requirements, and maintenance of recommended social distancing. The health and safety risks are presented by providing public access at the remote locations from which Board members currently attend Board meetings remotely. The Board hereby finds that conducting Board and/or committee meetings in person in light of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic presents an imminent risk to the health and safety of prospective attendees.

  1. Approve establishment of a new attendance boundary for new high school at Logan Memorial Educational Campus.
  2. Approve establishment of new Logan Memorial Educational Campus high school cluster and feeder pattern.
  3. Approve modification of existing attendance boundaries for San Diego High School, Roosevelt Middle School, Logan Memorial Educational Campus middle, Perkins K-8, Burbank Elementary, Emerson Elementary, Kimbrough Elementary, Rodriguez Elementary and Sherman Elementary Schools.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: Logan Memorial Educational Campus (LMEC) currently serves children from neo-natal care and preschool to Transitional Kindergarten through 8th grades. Its 9th through 12th grade high school facilities are under construction and scheduled to begin serving students in August 2022.

 

Staff recommend attendance area, feeder pattern, and cluster changes for the 2022-23 school year, the first year high school grades will open at Logan Memorial Educational Campus (LMEC). The attendance area modifications described in the attached report are at the elementary, middle and high school levels in order to optimize neighborhood school assignments.

 

The new LMEC high school and feeder pattern establish the district’s 17th high school cluster. The entire recommended high school attendance area for LMEC is currently assigned to San Diego High School (SDHS). The current SDHS cluster feeder pattern includes 12 elementary schools, three K-8 schools, and two middle schools, making it the largest among the existing 16 clusters.

 

Proposed Modifications

 

Staff recommend a modification to the SDHS attendance area, and the creation of a new attendance area for LMEC high school. The new high school attendance area boundary map would take effect as LMEC opens each high school grade. LMEC 9th grade will open in fall 2022, 10th grade in 2023, 11th grade in 2024, and 12th grade in 2025. No currently enrolled high school students would be required to change from their current school.

 

Schools reassigned from SDHS to the LMEC feeder pattern are those geographically closest to LMEC: Burbank Elementary, Emerson Elementary, Kimbrough Elementary, Logan Memorial Educational Campus, Perkins TK-8, Rodriguez Elementary, and Sherman Elementary.

 

The new middle school attendance area boundary map is congruent with the high school map. The middle school modification is proposed to take effect in Fall 2022. No currently enrolled students would be required to change from their current school.

 

The elementary school attendance area map modifications are proposed to take effect in Fall 2022. No currently enrolled students would be required to change from their current school. Rebalancing the size of these elementary boundaries assigns more students to their nearest elementary school.

 

Legacy Enrollment Proposal

 

Staff recommend granting to current students the legacy enrollment policy (formerly called “grandfathering”) described in the attached report in conjunction with attendance area changes. 2021-22 legacy students from these schools may choose SDHS for 9th grade:

  • Burbank Elementary
  • Emerson Elementary
  • Kimbrough Elementary
  • Logan Memorial Educational Campus
  • Perkins TK-8
  • Rodriguez Elementary
  • Sherman Elementary

 

Younger siblings who wish to enroll at the same school concurrently with their older sibling, who is a 2021-22 legacy student, will have the same legacy status until the younger sibling selects their 9th grade school. Implementation of the legacy enrollment policy is a digital process within the PowerSchool enrollment system. No Choice application will be required unless the student moves to a home address outside of the LMEC attendance area.

 

Boundary Study Process

 

The Boundary study process commenced in January 2021 and continued through December 2021, as described in the attached report. A multi-departmental boundary study team completed a five-phase process. The efforts focused on equity, which is supported on several levels. First, the campus and buildings were designed in collaboration with the community. The buildings are of the highest quality and every space is utilized to create learning opportunities. The architectural influence is Mexican Modernist style to honor the rich cultural history of the area. Second, students who are assigned are not constricted only to the assigned school. The Choice enrollment option provides high acceptance rates to many other schools. Third, opportunity is extended to the entire LMEC attendance area for Voluntary Enrollment Exchange Program (VEEP) bus transportation to other middle and high schools when minimum ridership and program guidelines are met.

 

The boundary study process was guided by Board Policy 5116, its 11 factors for boundary review, and by the best practices of digital mapping tools, as presented to the board on April 7, 2021.

2. Proposed Reorganization of San Diego High School, Effective Fall 2022
Meeting
Jan 11, 2022 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Information, First Reading

RECOMMENDATION: Reorganize San Diego High School from three small schools to one comprehensive high school. The school would be named San Diego High School and have one state CDS code. This reorganization will provide more opportunities and equitable access for all students to programs and pathways while the site will be able to maximize resources.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: When San Diego High School became a small school complex in 2004, there were initially six small schools supported by the Bill Gates Foundation. In 2009, Gates funding expired. Between the years of 2011-2013, the six schools were closed and reorganized to three small schools. In 2019, the need to provide opportunities for equity and access for all students across the complex was identified, including access to programs such as the International Baccalaureate, Pathways, including California Partnership Academies and California Career and Technical Education (i.e., Engineering, Medical, Journalism, Finance, Culinary, Computer Science). School demographic data illustrate that the majority of our students with disabilities and English learners were enrolled in only one of the small schools. Data also indicated that opportunities for advanced coursework existed primarily at one of the other small schools. This reorganization will provide more opportunities and equitable access for all students to programs and pathways while the site will be able to maximize resources.

BACKGROUND: The Furnish and Install Shade Shelters Project consists of the installation of a shade shelter structure on playground areas at 10 district sites. Other features include concrete and asphalt patching. The ten sites are as follows: Chavez Elementary, Chollas Mead Elementary, Dailard Elementary, Encanto Elementary, Green Elementary, Hearst Elementary, Horton Elementary, Jerabek Elementary, Miramar Ranch Elementary, and Nye Elementary Schools.

 

Pursuant to Public Contract Code section 20111(b), the district must publicly competitively bid any public project involving an expenditure of $15,000.00 or more. The notice calling for bids was published once a week for two successive weeks in accordance with Public Contract Code section 20112. Bids were due and opened on November 4, 2021. The district received a total of four bids from De La Fuente Construction, Inc., R.E. Schultz Construction, Inc., Straight Line General Contractors, Inc., and Team C Construction. After evaluating the bids, staff determined De La Fuente Construction, Inc., to be the lowest responsive and responsible bidder.

 

Additionally, consistent with the May 10, 2011, board-approved resolution in support of service-disabled veteran-owned businesses (SDVOB) and disabled veteran business enterprises (DVBE) which established a three percent required participation of DVE’s for all district construction contracting opportunities. The recommended contractor, De La Fuente Construction, Inc., submitted a three percent DVBE participation in its bid.

 

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The approval of the Proposed Project is subject to CEQA; however, it is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Sections 15303 (Class 3) – New Construction or Conversion of Small Structures. Class 3 consists of construction and location of limited numbers of new, small facilities or structures; installation of small new equipment and facilities in small structures; and the conversion of existing small structures from one use to another where only minor modifications are made in the exterior of the structure. The Proposed Project involves the installation of a limited number of shade shelters on existing school campuses. Therefore, the application of a Class 3 exemption is appropriate.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of existing school campuses. The Proposed Project is consistent with the existing use on each project site. Furthermore, the Proposed Project would not extend into any offsite or open space areas that may be classified as an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed on each site or in the project vicinity that, when combined with the construction timeframe of the Proposed Project, would result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. No work is proposed offsite that could result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. There are no district school facilities visible from any designated scenic highway in San Diego County. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the California State Water Resources Control Board Geotracker database, the none of the project sites are located on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. The Proposed Project would not result in the modification to an existing historical resource at any of the project sites. Installation of the shade structures would not require modification to existing structures. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Project.

Approval of this item approves the Proposed Project as described, authorizes and directs staff to file the attached Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk, and approves the proposed Agreement with De La Fuente Construction, Inc., to furnish and install shade shelters at 10 district sites.
