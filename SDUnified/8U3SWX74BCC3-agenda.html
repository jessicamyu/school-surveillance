RECOMMENDATION:  Ratification of Agreements with Colorado Boys Ranch d/b/a CBR Boys Ranch, Deveruex Cleo Wallace, Devereux Treatment Network, Excelsior Youth Center, Fred Finch Youth Center, Heritage School, Mental Health Systems Eastwood, New Haven Youth and Family Services, Inc., New Alternatives, Oak Grove Institute, Provo Canyon School, San Diego Center for Children, Victor Treatment Centers-Reading North Valley School, Yellowstone Boys & Girls Ranch, and Montcalm School to provide room and board and mental health treatment services for district students at residential treatment facilities.  The term of these agreements is July 1, 2012 through June 30, 2013.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not to exceed amount: $2,500,00.00 for all agreements.  Budget string: 5419-65120-00-5108/5808-1180-5750-01000-4697/4699.

  

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  For the period July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2012: $2,500,889.00.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  District staff provides educational and mental health oversight to these students whether they are placed in a d istrict program or in a non-district program. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: AB3632 became effective in 1984, requiring local County Mental Health departments to assume responsibility for mental health services for Special Education students who needed them in order to benefit from their education.  The governor eliminated the funding for this program through budget reductions on October 8, 2010.  Subsequent legislation repealed AB3632 and returned responsibility for these services to local education agencies.  Because of the repeal of AB3632, the district has had to assume responsibility for the cost, placement and oversight of all mental health related services, including residential treatment; and has reduced the number of students in residential from a July 1, 2011 high of 37 students to a June 30, 2012 low of 14 students.  These reductions provide a significant cost savings to the district by maintaining students in local and district programs, and by reducing costs for 24-hour care and for travel for parents and staff.

On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that will provide $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified School District facilities.  On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11-member Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) for Proposition S. As part of the Proposition and as required by law, the board appointed members to the ICOC to monitor how the district spends these tax dollars.

 

The membership for the Proposition S ICOC is selected from the following criteria:

  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens’ organization 
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district 
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization

Terms for membership on the Proposition S ICOC are for two years, and there is a two-term maximum. No employee or official of the district shall be appointed to the citizens’ oversight committee, nor any vendor, contractor or consultant of the district.

RECOMMENDATION:  Board Trustee Beiser will submit for board action a proclamation recognizing Thursday, November 15, 2012, as National Parent Involvement Day.

 

BACKGROUND:  The San Diego Unified School District and the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs are partnering to implement the first National Parent Involvement Day at district schools.

 

San Diego Unified School District and San Diego Unified Council of PTAs both agree that parent engagement focused on supporting the learning process, both at school and at home, is critical to increasing overall student achievement.  Over thirty years of research indicates that when parents and caregivers are strategically involved in children's learning at school and at home, student achievement significantly improves and schools are more effective.  

 

Schools will host an event on National Parent Involvement Day, Thursday, November 15, 2012, to honor the powerful contributions that parents and caregivers make to support the success of students and to reinforce awareness of the invaluable role that parents and caregivers play in supporting student learning and public education, and honor their contributions to student success. In addition to recognizing the role parents and caregivers play as invaluable allies in student success, secondary goals of this event include identifying parent and community volunteers whose efforts will help schools increase parent engagement, building strong partnerships with parent and community linked to student learning, laying the foundation for strategic family engagement, and strengthening the partnership between schools and PTA.


The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education recognizes the importance of National Parent Involvement Day and its goal of increasing overall student achievement through parent engagement.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve a no-cost reorganization of the Legal Services Division which involves 1) reductions in the budgeted amount for outside legal services; and 2) elimination of certain positions and creation and/or reclassification of others to account for previous organizational changes and to accurately reflect current assigned duties.

 

The salaries of 16 positions and outside counsel budgeted for 2012-13 is $1,459,228 from the general fund and $559,924 from other funds for a total of $2,019,152.  If the reorganization is approved, the salaries of 15 positions and outside counsel will be $1,434,844 from the general fund and $584,308 from other funds for a total of $2,019,152.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  This is a no-cost reorganization, which shall be accomplished through a combination of reduction in outside counsel expenditures, one-time vacancy savings and elimination or downgrading of certain positions.  If the reorganization is approved, the general fund will realize a $24,384 savings.  The salaries of 16 positions and outside counsel budgeted for 2012-13 is $1,459,228 from the general fund and $559,924 from other funds for a total of $2,019,152.  If the reorganization is approved, the salaries of 15 positions and outside counsel will be $1,434,844 from the general fund and $584,308 from other funds for a total of $2,019,152.

 

The district has realized an ongoing savings of approximately $500 K annually by in-house handling of work which previously went to outside counsel.  (In-house attorney salaries are approximately one-fourth the rates charged by outside counsel.)  A further benefit of maintaining an in-house legal department is the ability to identify revenue claims which would otherwise be unnoticed.  The Legal Services Division has recovered or secured almost $1 million in general fund unrestricted funds by pursuing claims for unpaid redevelopment and special education funds.  

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Approval to establish new job classifications in the Legal Services Office will allow for the reclassification of eight incumbents and the recruitment and appointment to fill one management vacancy.  In the Risk Management Department, two incumbents will be reclassified.  A single incumbent will be reclassified to Administrative Claims Assistant effective November 6, 2012.  A single incumbent will be reclassified to Workers’ Compensation/Light Duty Coordinator in accordance with the Employment Regulations for the Classified Service and district administrative procedures. 

 

BACKGROUND:  With the intent of expanding in-house capacity to handle complex legal matters, the Legal Services Office was expanded in 2009 to add two attorney positions.  In 2010, Risk Management was reassigned from the Finance Division and placed under Legal Services.  Most recently, in the summer of 2012, responsibility for oversight of the Labor Relations Department was assigned to Legal Services.  The Legal Services Division now encompasses the Legal Services Office, Risk Management, and Labor Relations Departments.  Additionally, the professional development of personnel in all three departments has increased both the level and quality of services provided to the district. 

 

The expansion of the Legal Services Office has resulted in an annual cost savings to the district of approximately $500,000 in outside counsel costs.  This reorganization further reduces the department’s budget for contracted legal services.  However, the assigned salaries for in-house staff are extremely low based on internal and external comparisons.  This jeopardizes the ability of the Legal Services Office to maintain these cost savings.  Approval of the recommendation to establish new job classes will allow for the reclassification of eight incumbents that provides appropriate compensation levels to better recognize the scope and complexity of legal work performed.

 

The proposal for the Risk Management Department is to flatten the organization such that all personnel report directly to the Manager, Insurance and Risk Services.  As a result, the current Workers’ Compensation Supervisor incumbent and position is recommended to be reclassified downward to account for the elimination of supervisory responsibilities.  Approval to establish a new job class of Workers’ Compensation/Light Duty Coordinator will allow for reclassification of a single incumbent to continue to perform the essential functions of those domains but in a non-supervisory capacity.  Additionally, the Legal Services Division proposes eliminating the current vacant Risk Management Specialist position. 

 

The Administrative Aide incumbent staffed in the Risk Management Department has received extensive training to perform specific duties relating to processing of liability claims.  Approval to establish an Administrative Claims Assistant job class will more accurately reflect the specialized skills and responsibilities of the position and will allow for reclassification of a single incumbent.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve the Agreement with Teach For America (TFA) for recruitment, selection and hiring of teachers effective November 6, 2012 through April 30, 2017.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  District costs to cover TFA fees of $5,000 per teacher per year.  If all 25 teachers are placed in each cohort year into vacant budgeted positions, the district costs will not exceed $125,000 in fiscal year 2013-14 and $250,000 in each subsequent year under the agreement. Budget string: 5508 00000 00 5100 1000 1110 01000 0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  This agreement with Teach For America (TFA) would provide the district with teaching staff for budgeted positions over the next three years. 

 

The district seeks to recruit new teachers who are trained to lead students to academic achievement and to equip new teachers with ongoing professional development and support to further develop and sustain their professional practice.  Each year, the district has a need to recruit new teachers to fill open budgeted positions in key subject areas and to fill positions in high need schools.  Under this agreement, TFA would recruit up to 25 teachers per year who would only be hired should the district have unfilled budget positions. 

 

The Agreement provides that TFA will work to recruit students from local universities to fill these positions.  The district will also give priority in selection to graduates of San Diego Unified schools and local universities.  The district will also seek to place TFA teachers in high need schools in the district.  The district agrees to place TFA teachers for a minimum of two years.  To cover recruitment and professional development and support costs, the district will pay TFA an administrative fee of $5,000 per teacher per year for an annual fiscal impact not to exceed $125,000 in year one, if all 25 teachers under this agreement are placed in positions.

 

TFA is a national leader in recruiting, selecting, training and providing ongoing professional development to individuals committed to closing the achievement gap by serving as effective classroom teachers specifically equipped to enhance student achievement in under-resourced school systems.  TFA works in 46 communities nationwide where corps members, alumni, and staff members work in partnership with local leaders and community members to close the achievement gap.  TFA goal is to develop a movement of leaders who will help drive change at every level of our education system toward the goal of closing the achievement gap.  These leaders start their paths as corps members; helping students in high-need communities make the academic progress that expands their opportunities.  Deeply affected by their teaching experience, TFA alumni have continued to advocate for students and build lasting change in many different roles and fields.

 

The term of this Agreement will cover the 2013 cohort of Teachers for the 2013-14 and 2014-15 academic years; the 2014 cohort of Teachers for the 2014-15 and 2015-16 academic years and the 2015 cohort of Teachers for the 2015-16 and 2016-17 academic years. This Agreement will expire on April 30, 2017, but all provisions applicable to the 2015 cohort of Teachers will remain in effect through the conclusion of the 2017 academic year. This Agreement may be renewed at the end of the term on the same or substantially similar terms by mutual agreement of the parties, and subject to approval by the district’s governing board.
