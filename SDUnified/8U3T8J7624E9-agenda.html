Our goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective deputy or chief, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

FISCAL IMPACT:  $1,500.00. Budget string: 0253 90965 00 4301 1000 1110 01000 0000. Services and materials for the program will be provided at no cost to the district.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.  Services will be provided by AMETEK Foundation, Inc.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The AMETEK Foundation, the charitable arm of AMETEK INC., invests in community partnerships with a goal of developing and supporting literacy in the San Diego area. A $33 billion dollar company that funds over $5 million in literacy projects, AMETEK identifies schools for ACTION 100 Book Challenge implementation that are racially and economically diverse Title I schools located within a 15-minute drive of AMETEK sites because staff is provided to facilitate reading sessions with students. Sequoia Elementary school has been identified as meeting the requirements for implementation of the ACTION 100 program. Volunteers from the local AMETEK site, Programmable Power, will donate time once each week to read to, and with, struggling readers.

 

The grant includes all materials, professional development, and coordination services to implement ACTION 100, as well as all materials supplied to the five K-3 classrooms at Sequoia Elementary. Each classroom will receive reading materials including at least ten baskets of leveled text collections (300+ new books per classroom) and supporting consumable materials. A consultant will be assigned to Sequoia to provide ten days of on-site, in-classroom training sessions.Costs for all materials and services will be purchased from the American Reading Company (ARC) using grant funds that will be disbursed by AMETEK directly to ARC.  The school site coordinator will be paid directly from ARC.

 

Funds will be paid to the school to host a kick-off ceremony, end-of-year celebrations and academic achievement awards for students who meet reading practice goals.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve First Amendment to Agreement with AECOM Technical Services, Inc. to continue providing mechanical and network engineering and planning services, and to continue performing facility assessments (PS-11-0453-02),   The term of this Amendment will be extended by this First Amendment through September 14, 2014, for a revised total term of September 15, 2010 through September 14, 2014. No additional funds are being requested in this amendment to the contract.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  For the period September 15, 2010 to July 20, 2012:  $466,080.50.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  On September 12, 2006, the board approved an Agreement with DMJM+Harris Inc. (now known as AECOM Technical Services, Inc.) to complete a Facilities Condition Assessment (FCA) study as well as to outline a Major Repair and Replacement (MRR) ten-year plan.  Pursuant to Policy G-3250, the board requires all district-owned facilities to be assessed on a recurring cycle not to exceed five years. This requires regular FCAs to be completed at a rate of about one-fifth of the district’s sites annually.   In addition to the FCA study, the necessity of a MRR ten-year plan provides for an estimate of total facility needs and includes any building systems which need to be fixed, repaired or replaced within the subsequent ten-year, Proposition S bond period.  This Agreement was extended on August 5, 2008, and on July 28, 2009, respectively, to increase the consideration of the Agreement and to update various provisions of the Agreement in order to complete the necessary work.

 

Subsequently, on September 14, 2010, the board approved a new Agreement with AECOM in order to continue the FCAs at district sites for implementation of the Major Repair and Replacement portion of the Proposition S plan.  Additional tasks included design of a new Proposition S Data Center and comprehensive mechanical engineering master planning to assure cost-effective expenditure of Proposition S funds.  To date, 58 assessments have been completed, and it is necessary to accelerate assessments at selected district sites in order to adequately develop scope for Whole Site Modernization work.  Also, the Mechanical Master Plan is 30 percent complete and is necessary to allow for Whole Site Modernization project implementation.  Additional investigation is required to realize the goal of minimizing the impact of capital bond-funded improvements on long-term General Fund obligations (i.e. operation and maintenance and energy cost of operation of new HVAC facilities).

 

Staff recommends the Agreement be extended for an additional two years to preserve the progress the firm has made to date and to allow for design and planning continuity for all these critical FCA and mechanical engineering projects.  The rates currently in effect in this Agreement will remain for the duration of the term of this Agreement upon approval of this First Amendment.  The previously approved contract amount will remain unchanged.

 

California Government Code section 53060 allows a school district to contract with and employ any persons for the furnishing of special services if such persons are specially trained, experienced and competent to perform the special services required.

Section 4.1 provides,

Assure the timely flow of information, appropriate input, and strategic, twoway dialog between the district and the community that informs, shapes, and builds understanding and support for district and school efforts.

 

Status Report:

Since the board added this as a focus area in March, the Superintendent and staff have continued to work toward increased communications internally and externally on major issues and programs. 

 

During the last quarter, the major issue has been the adoption of the 2012-13 budget and the collective bargaining agreement with SDEA.  The Superintendent made communications about the SDEA agreement a priority by sending several, 'all staff' communications about the budget and the SDEA agreement.  The media was continually updated about negotiations progress and the announcement of the SDEA agreement was highlighted by a joint news conference with SDEA.  Overall the communications on these important issues were effective in keeping staff and the community well informed about the budget adoption and labor agreements.

During the summer, the Superintendent and staff are planning another Back-to-School event for principals and department managers on August 23.  This annual event is an important opportunity to communicate with district leadership the goals for the new school year.  At this event, the Chief of Staff will highlight and promote communications initiatives related to the website, Principal News, and targeted communications efforts related to the November ballot measures.

RECOMMENDATION:  Board direction regarding the restrictions on the use of the baseball field at Dana Middle School in the current joint use agreement between the district and the City of San Diego for the fields at Dana Middle School.  This would leave the agreement for the recreational area and the softball field within the joint use agreement unchanged.  The joint use agreement expires 2019.  The restrictions would preclude the use of the fields by non-school affiliated, adult baseball teams. 


FISCAL IMPACT:  None.


PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

BACKGROUND: The City and the district currently have a joint use agreement for the fields at Dana Middle School, including the softball, baseball, and recreational fields.  Point Loma High School uses the baseball field for its teams.  In addition, the baseball field is used by Little League teams, Dana Middle School students, and adult baseball teams from within the community.  The City grants usage upon request.

David Wells, a former professional baseball player and alumnus of Point Loma High School wants to completely rebuild the baseball field and maintain it at his own cost (thus relieving the City from its maintenance).  The renovated field would be used by Point Loma High School teams.  In addition, the field could be used by local Little Leagues, the Dana Middle School students, and occasional high school baseball tournaments.  The restrictions would preclude the use of the field by adult teams.

Discussions have been held between the San Diego Parks and Recreation Department and district staff in this regard.  The City has requested that the district initiate the request for revision of the agreement after having received input from the Point Loma Community regarding the same.  The San Diego Parks and Recreation Department will then consider the request for whether or not it will pass it on to the City Council for approval.  This would not eliminate the joint use agreement, nor would it remove the baseball field from the agreement.  It would basically eliminate the use of the field by adult baseball league usage.  The district real estate team would work out the actual verbiage with the San Diego Parks and Recreation Department.  Both staffs would then bring the revised agreement to their respective governing bodies for approval.

District staff recommends that the Board of Trustees, upon hearing the input from the community, approves moving forward with negotiations with the City for the amendment of the current joint use agreement.
