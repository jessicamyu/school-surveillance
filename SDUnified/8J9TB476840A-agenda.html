D. Student Instructional Matters
Subject
1. Memorandum of Understanding Regarding Conditions for Suspending Revocation Proceedings by and between San Diego Unified School District and Promise Charter School (6:00 P.M., AUDITORIUM)
Meeting
Jul 19, 2011 - REVISED Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M., Room 2226 & 6:00 P.M., AUDITORIUM
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve Memorandum of Understanding Regarding Conditions for Suspending Revocation Proceedings (MOU) with Promise Charter School, without waiving or otherwise constraining the district’s ability to pursue revocation of Promise Charter School, in accordance with the MOU, in the district’s sole discretion.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  On April 12, 2011, the board approved the issuance of a Notice to Remedy to Promise Charter School, in conformity with Education Code section 47607(c) and (d). The Notice to Remedy was delivered to Promise Charter School on April 13, 2011, and provided notice to the charter school of its obligation to refute or remedy the violations identified therein by May 20, 2011. Promise Charter School responded to the Notice to Remedy on May 20, 2011. 

 

After thorough review of Promise Charter School’s response to the Notice to Remedy and all supporting documents submitted therewith, the district concluded that the Charter School failed to refute or remedy all of the violations set forth in the Notice to Remedy.  As such, at a June 7, 2011 meeting, the board approved the issuance of a Notice of Intent to Revoke and Notice of Facts in Support of Revocation (Ed. Code, section 47607(e)) to Promise Charter School.  A draft of the Notice of Intent to Revoke was brought to and approved by the board at the June 7, 2011 meeting, setting forth the grounds, facts, and evidence upon which the revocation proceedings were based, as well as a schedule for the proceedings.  The Notice of Intent to Revoke and all supporting evidence were provided to Promise Charter School on June 8, 2011. In conformity with Education Code section 47607(e), the public hearing was to take place at the board meeting scheduled for June 28, 2011 and the board was to issue a final decision on the revocation at its meeting scheduled for July 5, 2011.

 

Shortly after the Notice of Intent to Revoke was issued by the board, Promise Charter School requested for the district to stay revocation proceedings to allow the parties to participate in mediation, in an effort to avoid revocation of the Promise Charter School Charter.  The board approved postponing the public hearing and revocation decision to allow the parties to participate in mediation. 

 

During mediation, the parties agreed to a Memorandum of Understanding Regarding Conditions for Suspending Revocation Proceedings (MOU) which is subject to approval of both parties’ respective governing boards.  The MOU requires the charter school to, among other things, reconstitute its governing board, change employment assignments, undergo extensive training of the staff and board, and provide various reports to the district demonstrating compliance with the requirements of the MOU.

 

If Promise Charter School’s and the district’s boards both approve the MOU, the Notice of Intent to Revoke issued at the June 7, 2011 meeting will not proceed, except under the terms of the MOU.

D. Student Instructional Matters
Subject
2. Public Hearing: Promise Charter School (6:00 P.M., AUDITORIUM)
Meeting
Jul 19, 2011 - REVISED Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M., Room 2226 & 6:00 P.M., AUDITORIUM
Type
Public Hearing

RECOMMENDATION: In conformity with Education Code section 47607(e), the Board of Education shall hold a public hearing, in the normal course of business, on the issue of whether evidence exists to revoke the Promise Charter School Charter.  During the public hearing, the board will receive information and comment from district staff, legal counsel, Promise Charter School, and the public on the issue of whether evidence exists to revoke the Promise Charter School Charter. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  On April 12, 2011, the board approved the issuance of a Notice to Remedy to Promise Charter School, in conformity with Education Code section 47607(c) and (d). The Notice to Remedy was delivered to Promise Charter School on April 13, 2011, and provided notice to the charter school of its obligation to refute or remedy the violations identified therein by May 20, 2011. Promise Charter School responded to the Notice to Remedy on May 20, 2011. 

 

After thorough review of Promise Charter School’s response to the Notice to Remedy and all supporting documents submitted therewith, the district concluded that the charter school failed to refute or remedy all of the violations set forth in the Notice to Remedy.  At the June 7, 2011 board meeting, the board reviewed the staff reports and supporting information, and the draft Notice of Intent to Revoke setting forth the grounds, facts, and evidence upon which the revocation proceedings were based, as well as a schedule for the proceedings, and approved the issuance of the Notice of Intent to Revoke and Notice of Facts in Support of Revocation (Ed. Code, section 47607(e)) to Promise Charter School.  Promise was provided with the Notice of Intent to Revoke and all supporting evidence on June 8, 2011. In conformity with Education Code section 47607(e), the public hearing was to take place at the June 28, 2011 board meeting and the board was to issue a final decision on the revocation at its meeting scheduled for July 5, 2011.

 

Shortly after the Notice of Intent to Revoke was issued by the board, Promise Charter School requested for the district stay revocation proceedings to allow the parties to participate in mediation, in an effort to avoid revocation of the Promise Charter School Charter.  The board approved postponing the public hearing and revocation decision to allow the parties to participate in mediation.

 

The parties agreed to a Memorandum of Understanding Regarding Conditions for Suspending Revocation Proceedings (MOU) during mediation, which is subject to approval of both parties’ respective governing boards.  The MOU requires the charter school to, among other things, reconstitute its governing board, change employment assignments, undergo extensive training of the staff and board, and provide various reports to the district demonstrating compliance with the requirements of the MOU.

 

If either the Promise Charter School Board or the district’s board does not approve the MOU, the stay on revocation agreed to in the existing MOU will end, and the board will reinstate revocation proceedings by holding a public hearing in accordance with Education Code section 47607(e).

 

The public hearing will be held in the normal course of business on the issue of whether evidence exists to revoke the Promise Charter School Charter.  During the public hearing, the board will receive information and comment from district staff, legal counsel, Promise Charter School, and the public on the issue of whether evidence exists to revoke the Promise Charter School Charter. The district will be afforded 15 minutes to present to the board on the issue of revocation; the charter school will be afforded 15 minutes to respond; and the district will have five minutes to reply. During the public hearing, the board will have the opportunity to pose any questions they may have and the public will be afforded the opportunity to comment on the issue of whether evidence exists to revoke the Promise Charter School Charter subject to any time limitations that may be designated by the board.
