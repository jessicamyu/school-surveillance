BACKGROUND: This update addresses program and project milestones and provides an overview of the current program. On November 4, 2008, November 6, 2012, and November 6, 2018, voters approved, respectively, Propositions S, Z and Measure YY general obligation bond measures to improve every school in the San Diego Unified. On May 14, 2013, the Board of Education (Board) approved the Proposition Z Two-Year Project Plan for implementing projects identified in the Bond Project List and included estimated project schedules. On December 1, 2013, the Board approved an update to the Proposition Z Two-Year Project Plan which also incorporated Proposition S projects and schedules. On June 3, 2014, Propositions S and Z Project Plan expanded beyond its two-year time horizon and has come to be known as the multiple-year project plan. The multiple-year project plan has subsequently been updated at each staff presentation to the Board, most recently on July 9, 2019. In response to Board priorities and direction, staff is presenting its latest update to the multiple-year project plan for Board approval which includes references to the voter guides for Proposition S, Proposition Z, and Measure YY projects.

FISCAL IMPACT: Receive up to $179,144.00 in grant funds. Revenue Budget String: 0000-95419-00-8699-0000-01000-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: Up to $168,896.00.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Continues funding for the Lead Mental Health Clinician position at Monroe Clark Middle School.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district Mental Health Resource Center applied for and was awarded a grant from Price Philanthropies Foundation to fund the Lead Mental Health Clinician at Clark Middle School. This funding comprises one part of the generous support that Price Philanthropies Foundation provides to the schools in the City Heights neighborhood.
