3. School Realignment and Consolidation Workshop
Meeting
Jul 10, 2012 - Special Meeting, 10:00 A.M.
Type
Information

RECOMMENDATION:  Board direction on whether to implement one or more of the following processes: 

  • Initiate a process to close or consolidate eight schools
  • Continue the current process of strategic changes over time
  • Create standards for school size and configuration for the district

Staff would like board input on what criteria should be used and should any be weighted, what weight should be given to geographical equity, composition of community advisory committee, and process and timeline recommendations.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  $4,000,000


PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Some certificated and classified employees may not be in the same positions in future years due to post and bid or priority consideration. 
 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.


BACKGROUND:  The board has approved a budget solutions list for 2013-14 that includes the closure of eight schools to save $4,000,000.  School closures/consolidations will have to be approved in January 2013, to provide time to implement the decision.  The process must begin this summer and continue consistently through the fall with a board recommendation in December.  School closure exploration is a controversial and disruptive process for all parties involved in it.  Staff is seeking board direction on whether to begin a formal process for community input and decision making. Staff can continue with the same process the board directed back in December 2011 – continue looking for opportunity to consolidate schools where it makes good sense from a facility and educational perspective.  At the same time or as an alternative, staff will work to design standards for school size and grade level configurations across the district.
