RECOMMENDATION: Approve restructuring proposals for eight district schools in Program Improvement Year 4 status: Carson; Horton, Language Academy; Rosa Parks; Wangenheim; Riley/New Dawn; San Diego Communications, Investigations in a Multicultural Atmosphere; and San Diego Media, Visual and Performing Arts.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Each school received regular Title I funding based on their school's ranking.  In addition, each PI Year 4 school will receive $170 per student per board action on January 26, 2010.


PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

BACKGROUND:   On July 27, 2010, staff presented to the board a First Reading of the restructuring proposals for Program Improvement Year 4 status schools, Carson; Horton, Language Academy; Rosa Parks; Wangenheim; Riley/New Dawn; San Diego Communications, Investigations in a Multicultural Atmosphere; and San Diego Media, Visual and Performing Arts.

 

General Information:  Program Improvement (PI) Status:  Under the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001 (NCLB), a Title I school that, for two consecutive years, does not meet or exceed a series of annual academic performance goals known as Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) is designated Program Improvement. Such schools must provide specified types of services and/or interventions during each year that they are identified as PI.  A school is eligible to exit PI if it makes AYP for two consecutive years.

 

District Schools in PI Year 4, 2009–10:  School Restructuring:  A school that enters its fourth consecutive year in PI status is required to develop a plan to restructure that will be implemented the following year, in this case in 2010–11. The restructuring plan must describe in detail the changes in governance, instructional practice and staffing that will be required to improve students’ academic achievement and enable the school to meet its AYP goals.  District schools submitting a PI Year 4 restructuring plan are:  Carson; Horton; Language Academy and Rosa Parks elementary schools; Wangenheim Middle; Riley/New Dawn; and San Diego Communications, Investigations in a Multicultural Atmosphere; and San Diego Media, Visual and Performing Arts.    Encanto Elementary School entered PI Year 4 status in 2008-09 with the board approving their restructuring plan in June 2009.  Encanto met AYP for the 2008-09 year and is on its second year of PI Year 4 status with the possibility of exiting PI if AYP targets are met this year.

 

The restructuring proposals were developed by School Program Improvement Leadership Teams composed of teachers, administrators, classified staff, and parents, in collaboration with the Office of Federal and Special Programs.

 

District Schools in PI Year 5, 2009–10:  Federal and Special Programs met with the 24 PI Year 5+ schools to review their existing restructuring plans.  All stakeholders engaged in discussion and reviewed student data to analyze the success of the strategies outlined in the plans.  The instructional teams made data driven tentative decisions about what strategies/programs improved student achievement.  These decisions will be reflected in their 2010-11 Single Plan for Student Achievement.

Through this agreement, the parties will work toward a full Workload Model to be implemented in the 2011-12 school year.

  2. the Initial Study and Mitigated Negative Declaration (Attachment A) were presented to the Board of Education, which reviewed and considered the information contained in the record prior to approving the project; and

  6. Authorize staff to file the Notice of Determination for the Clairemont High School Athletic Facilities Upgrades Project (SCH No. 2010051085) with the Recorder/County Clerk of San Diego County, California (Attachment C). 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Adoption of the Initial Study and Mitigated Negative Declaration by the Board of Education (BOE) will require the district to file a Notice of Determination with the County Clerk of San Diego County in the amount of $50.00.  Costs associated with preparation and filing the Mitigated Negative Declaration are included in the Proposition S general obligation bond.  The Board of Education will have an opportunity to approve the Clairemont High School Stadium ADA Improvements Phase 2 and the Track and Field IDIQ Projects and award construction contracts at a subsequent board meeting, currently scheduled for November 9, 2010.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:   None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  There is no impact to district staffing levels.

 

BACKGROUND: The Clairemont HS Athletic Facilities Upgrades Project, which will upgrade the existing athletic facility to improve the school’s existing athletic program and provide Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)-compliant facilities.  Primary project elements include a new synthetic turf football field; an all-weather running track; replacing and adding football stadium bleachers, football/track field lighting, upgraded public address system; new restrooms and snack bars; new baseball turf field; new baseball field bleachers and dugouts; and replacing the tennis courts with a new softball field and associated facilities.  Events at the existing athletic facility were only possible during daylight hours or in the evening with the use of temporary lights.  The District anticipates that approximately 15 evening events would occur on an annual basis with the implementation of this project.  The project will also add ADA-accessible bleachers, announcer’s booth, listening devices, restrooms, snack bars, ticket booths, drinking fountains, parking spaces, and path of travel.

 

A Mitigated Negative Declaration/Initial Study (MND/IS) was prepared for the Clairemont High School Athletic Facilities Upgrade Project (SCH No. 2010051085) in compliance with the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) and the Guidelines for Implementation of CEQA.  The MND/IS was made available for public review between May 28, 2010 and June 28, 2010 (Attachment C).   A Notice of Intent (NOI) to Adopt an MND for the proposed project was filed with the San Diego County Clerk’s office and was published in the San Diego Union-Tribune and La Prensa.  Copies of the Draft MND/IS were sent to the State Clearinghouse; federal, state, and local agencies; and community entities/organizations.  The Draft MND/IS was also made available for review at the district’s Physical Plant Operations Annex; at the City of San Diego Library, Clairemont Branch; and at Clairemont High School Main Office. 

 

The Initial Study included an analysis of the following environmental issues: aesthetics, agriculture and forestry resources, air quality biological resources, cultural resources, geology/soils, greenhouse gas emissions, hazards and hazardous materials, hydrology/water quality, land use/planning, mineral resources, noise, population/housing, public services, recreation, transportation/traffic, and utilities/service systems.  The MND concluded that the proposed project will not result in any potentially significant environmental impacts with implementation of the mitigation measures included in the Mitigation Monitoring and Reporting Plan (Attachment B).  The district received two comment letters in response to the issuance of the Draft MND/IS.  The first emphasized the importance of this project in providing accessibility for students and others with mobility related disabilities and the second was from the State Clearinghouse indicating that no state agency submitted comments on the proposed project.

 

The Final MND was prepared in accordance with CEQA and the Guidelines for Implementation of CEQA.  A copy of the Final MND is located in Board Services Office, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231.

UDL is based on the concept that the primary barrier to achievement of many students is the inflexible, "one-size-fits-all" curricula that raise unintentional barriers to learning.  Typically, UDL is based on the assumption that it is the curricula not the student that fail to provide all individuals with fair and equal opportunities to learn.  UDL assesses curricula (goals, methods, materials and assessments) rather than students for their strengths and challenges and the curricula are designed or redesigned more broadly to meet the needs of a diverse group of students. CAST's professional development will: introduce the key concepts of UDL in order to lay a foundation for understanding UDL; apply UDL to lesson planning to in order to improve learning outcomes for all students; and align with the i21 Interactive Classroom plan.

BACKGROUND:  Government Code section 4217.10 et seq. allows a school district to enter into an energy management agreement on terms that are determined by its governing board to be in its best interest.  Six companies, AMSOLAR Corporation, Barnhart Baker Solar JV, Main Street Power, Pathway Trans-Global Systems, Sigma Solar Energy, Inc., and Solar Power Partners, Inc., responded to the district’s Request for Proposals (RFP).  A team consisting of representation from the district’s energy, legal, and finance offices, and an outside third-party solar expert was formed to evaluate submitted proposals.  AMSOLAR Corporation was rated best overall for methodology, technical and economic viability, and industry and institutional standards.  The board ratified a non-binding Letter of Intent (LOI), at the April 13, 2010 board meeting, that allowed AMSOLAR Corporation to secure rebates through the California Solar Initiative, and it approved entering into negotiations with AMSOLAR Corporation that led to this proposed Solar Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with the district.  AMSOLAR Corporation then formed another entity called AMSOLAR SDUSD I, LLC for financing purposes, which is common in the industry. 

 

The PPA allows AMSOLAR SDUSD I, LLC to own, install, and maintain solar panels on roofs and car ports of designated district property, and then to sell the energy back to the district at a discounted rate when compared to SDG&E’s rate.  At the end of the 22 year agreement, unless otherwise agreed by the parties, AMSOLAR SDUSD I, LLC will remove the solar panels at its own expense.

 

Under the PPA, the district has an option to purchase the solar panels at the 10th anniversary of the Commencement Date with a price that is the greater of $12.3 million or the fair market value.  The PPA also provides for remedies if either party terminates the agreement early.  If AMSOLAR SDUSD I, LLC terminates early because of district’s default or breach, then district must pay a “termination value” according to a published schedule and the reasonable removal costs of the solar panels plus any sums currently owed for energy.  If district terminates early due to default or breach by AMSOLAR SDUSD I, LLC, then it must pay the district for the difference in SDGE’s price over the remaining term of the PPA and it will remove the solar panels at its own expense.  The PPA also has a dispute resolution process.

 

This PPA is for installing and maintaining solar panels at the following 20, district sites:

With this PPA the number of district sites that generate electricity with onsite solar systems will increase from 28 to 48 and from 4.17 Megawatts to 9.37 Megawatts.  This project will make San Diego Unified School District one of the top solar powered school districts in the country. This project will also result in the district increasing its total electricity generated by means of onsite, clean, renewable, solar generation from approximately 10 to 20 percent.

 

In concert with the PPA, the Lease is a companion document that gives AMSOLAR SDUSD I, LLC access to designated district property to install and maintain the solar panels.  An additional Letter Agreement requires AMSOLAR SDUSD I, LLC to educate the community about solar power (i.e., provide two demonstration parking lot solar systems, hold a solar awareness event, conduct teacher workshops, and provide demonstration wind energy systems) and offers the district an opportunity to purchase Renewable Energy

Certificates every five years of the PPA.  Also attached are renderings of a roof top system and carport.
