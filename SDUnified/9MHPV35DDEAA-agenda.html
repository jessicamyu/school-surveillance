Meeting
May 12, 2015 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution recognizing Harvey Milk Day on May 22, 2015.

 

BACKGROUND: Harvey Milk was born May 22, 1930 in Woodmere, New York. Harvey served in the United States Navy during the Korean War as a diving officer aboard the submarine rescue ship USS Kittiwake and was later transferred to Naval Station, San Diego to serve as a diving instructor with the rank of Lieutenant. Harvey was a tireless advocate of fairness for all Americans, both as a candidate for office and as a San Francisco Supervisor.

 

In 1977, Harvey was elected to the San Francisco Board of Supervisors, becoming the first openly gay man to be elected to public office in California. This milestone achievement gave hope to millions of gays and lesbians across the country that a day would come when they could live their lives openly and honestly without fear of discrimination. As a Supervisor, Harvey worked with others to secure passage of San Francisco’s landmark Gay Rights Ordinance. This ordinance extended employment protections to gays and lesbians in San Francisco, and it became a model for anti-discrimination legislation throughout California and the nation.

 

In 1978, Harvey traversed the state to campaign against Proposition 6, which was known as the Briggs Initiative. Had it passed, Proposition 6 would have required California school districts to fire openly gay and lesbian teachers solely because of their sexual orientation. The proposition was defeated in the November 1978 election in part because Harvey successfully appealed to Californians’ basic sense of fairness.

 

Although Harvey’s life was cut short, he was a hero and role model, and his legacy of hope, tolerance, and equality lives on.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve support of Assembly Bill 1461, California New Motor Voter Program. 

 

BACKGROUND:  At the April 28, 2015 Board of Education meeting the board adopted a resolution, item F.4.,  to support AB 1461 to Extend Definition of "Residence" for Voters to Include Place of Business or Public Space for Voting Purposes.  Following the publication of the board’s April 28 meeting agenda, AB 1461 was amended and completely changed.  The amended version of AB 1461 provides that eligible residents would be automatically registered to vote anytime they apply for or renew their driver’s license at the Department of Motor Vehicles.  The bill further provides individuals with the ability to opt out.  The bill is now referred to as the California New Motor Voter Program.  After determining a resident to be eligible to vote, the Secretary of State would provide the information to the County Registrar of Voters.

 

According to the Secretary of State an estimated 7 million Californians are eligible to vote but unregistered. The board’s intention in supporting AB 1461 is to promote democracy and the civic engagement of individuals by increasing opportunities for eligible residents to participate in elections.

BACKGROUND: GEAR UP is a federal program funded by the U. S. Department of Education, which provides federal funding for 213 grant partnerships serving 748,000 low income students in 48 states and six U. S. regions.

 

The California GEAR UP program, sponsored by the California Education Round Table at the Governor's request, is administered by the University of California.  Funded for its third six-year cycle on October 1, 2011, the program goal is to: Develop and sustain the organizational capacity of middle schools to prepare ALL students for high school and higher education through a statewide network of support for adults—counselors, faculty, school leaders and families—who influence middle school students. Services are provided to a cohort of 48 middle schools throughout California, focused on building a sustainable, college-going culture throughout the school community.  Marston, Montgomery, and Taft middle schools were approved for participation in the program.  All program services are geared towards sustainability, such that school change can be successful beyond the life of the grant cycle.

 

California GEAR UP beliefs:

  • That ALL students deserve an equitable education – one that provides the knowledge and skills to choose and be successful in postsecondary education pursuits;
  • That students must master rigorous academic standards to successfully progress along the pipeline from middle school to high school and into and through college;
  • That in order for students to plan for college they need to see themselves in college;
  • That overcoming the challenges faced by low-income, first-generation college-bound students requires the continued engagement of school leaders, families and communities.

Each middle school will receive $7,500 from the GEAR UP grant.  In addition, the district will submit a claim for reimbursement to the University of San Diego for up to $20,270.00 for Montgomery Middle School, and up to $24,330.00 for Taft Middle School for hourly and substitute teacher reimbursement, and for travel reimbursement in support of these services.

 

Marston Middle School will receive $7,500 from the GEAR UP grant.  In addition, the district will submit a claim for reimbursement to the University of San Diego up to $23,973.00 for hourly and substitute teacher reimbursement, and for travel reimbursement in support of these services.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve Agreement with San Diego County Health and Human Services Agency to provide CalWIN Data Extract services for the district Food Services Department (PS-15-1084-26).  The term of this Agreement is May 13, 2015 through May 12, 2020.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The County of San Diego Health and Human Services Agency (HHSA) and the district are entering into this System Data Agreement (SDA) for California Work [Opportunity and Responsibility to Kids] Information Network (CalWIN).

 

CalWIN Data extract of confidential files will meet the provisions of the federal Child Nutrition and WIC (Women, Infant, and Children) Reauthorization Act of 1989, §202(b) (1). This act allows a designated school food authority to certify a student as eligible for a free or reduced price school lunch or breakfast without further application by directly communicating with the appropriate local agency to obtain documentation of student status as a member of a CalFresh household. CalFresh is a state nutrition program and is federally known as SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program).

 

This Agreement will allow certification of students for free or reduced meals without requiring that a Free and Reduced Meal Application be completed by the parent.  The district is currently in compliance with the State of California requirement that districts certify students using the statewide California Longitudinal Pupil Achievement Data System (CALPADS) system.  Under this SDA, the district will now work with the local County Health and Human Services Agency to provide a monthly match of district students from qualifying households. 

 

Free meal status is also used by school districts for various purposes such as Local Control Funding Funding (LCFF), E-rate and grant funding and as such, the district will benefit from this agreement by having the ability to directly certify more students, specifically previously unidentified students, thereby increasing the number of students eligible to receive meals at no cost and provide more funding opportunities for the district with less paperwork for parents.
