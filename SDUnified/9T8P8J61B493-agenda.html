Meeting
Dec 8, 2015 - Regular Meeting, 4:30 P.M.
Type

RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the board on LCAP Goal 5:  Parent and Community Engagement.

 

LCAP GOAL 5: Parents and community members are engaged and work within and across schools to support student learning.  Neighborhood schools are highly regarded for their quality as well as their service as centers for extended learning and enrichment opportunities, and academic and social services.

 

Actions and Services:

  • 5.1.b. - Provide additional opportunities for parent leadership training to enhance meaningful engagement and participation.
  • 5.1.c. - The district and all schools, including those receiving Title I funds, will plan and provide family engagement opportunities responsive to the needs of their parents and families, and supportive of neighborhood culture and circumstance.
  • 5.1.d. - Provide opportunities and resources for parents/guardians to support their children's education at home and their role in preparing their children for college and career.  Include guidance on navigating the school system and finding resources to support education and learning at home.
  • 5.1.f. - Provide a consistent venue for parent communications and information by enhancing the functionality and accessibility of Parent Portal (through PowerSchool), and provide training and support to effectively use this resource.
  • 5.1.g. - Schools will be provided with resources and materials to create a welcoming environment for all families and staff.
  • 5.1.j. - Establish a Parent Engagement Task Force comprised of district staff and parents from across the district and make recommendations for increasing meaningful parent engagement and identifying and/or developing appropriate metrics to measure engagement.
  • 5.6.b. - Create, sustain, and expand community partnerships to support student and family engagement and learning.
  • 5.6.c. - Solicit input from the community to assist in the identification of needed learning and social services.

Vision 2020, Quality Indicator Destination:  All schools reach out to parents as partners in their children's education and provide parents with the information necessary to know whether their sons and daughters are on-track to success.  Entire communities provide the support and resources to raise expectations and encourage students to graduate ready for success in college and careers.

 

BACKGROUND:  Each month, Superintendent Marten provides a District Accountability Report that includes the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting LCAP actions, services, and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's District Accountability Report each month, cluster and advisory groups have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report, and provide input and feedback on current LCAP implementation and suggested next steps.  The complete LCAP document is available at https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

FISCAL IMPACT: None at this time.  The U.S. Department of Education awards three-year MSAP grants of up to $4,000,000.00 per year for a total of up to $12,000,000.00 over the three years.  Funding supports the development of innovative academic programs with the goal of accelerating student achievement and promoting diversity.  Funded supports include staff development, curriculum writing, key personnel for academic support, and supplies and equipment costs related to development of the academic program, raising achievement for all students, and promoting diversity.  

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: San Diego Unified received three-year MSAP grants in 1995, 1998, 2001, 2007, and 2010, and is in the third year of the 2013 grant.  The 2013 grant provided $3,853,939.00 in Year 1 (2013-14), $3,469,323.00 in Year 2 (2014-15), and $3,302,209.00 in Year 3 (2015-16).  At the close of the 2013 MSAP grant cycle in October 2016, the district will have received a total of $10,625,471.00 in restricted allocations.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: The MSAP grant provides opportunities for district certificated staff as student enrollment increases at the MSAP funded sites.  Additionally, the MSAP grant supports the academic growth of grant sites by funding up to two certificated positions with specific credentials and or skills related to the academic focus.  The Office of School Innovation will be staffed with a Director, Program Manager, and two Resource Teachers with specific credentials and/or skills related to the academic foci of the MSAP supported sites.  These positions are fully funded by the MSAP grant and have an end date that follows the grant timeline.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The Office of School Innovation is preparing a grant proposal for the 2016 Magnet Schools Assistance Program grant, a competitive federally funded grant providing support to districts to significantly revise existing or develop new magnet schools for the purpose of supporting integration and closing the achievement gap.

 

Sites proposed for inclusion in the grant have been identified based on specific grant criterion, the district’s Vision 2020 goals, and the interest of community members and families, site administrators, teachers, and the Area Superintendent.  Input from the Executive Director, Facility Planning and Design, specific to the development of academic patterns within targeted clusters was critical in narrowing the selection of sites.  Exhibit 1 outlines federal site selection criterion, the proposed sites, and provides an overview of program design. On October 6, 2015, staff presented a board workshop to review the role of the district's magnet program as it relates to federal law, and the role magnets are able to play as a critical support for Vision 202.

 

The MSAP request for proposals (RFP) is due to be released at any point from this date forward through May 2016.  Upon release of the RFP, the Office of School Innovation will have approximately 45 days to prepare and submit the district’s proposal.  Award notification has historically been received in the September following the submission.
