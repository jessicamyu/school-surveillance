RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the Board of Education on the implementation of Vision 2020 Quality Indicator 3, Professional Learning for All Staff, and Quality Indicator 8, Valuing Diversity to Make Equity a Reality.  The report will include 2013-14 principal and area superintendent professional development and cultural proficiency training provided through the Leadership and Learning Division, and other specialized professional learning for staff provided through various departments as a baseline, along with a districtwide plan to scale professional learning to all staff.  

 

VISION 2020 Quality Indicator 3, Professional Learning for All, Destination: Schools hold professional learning among their top priorities for the school community. They recognize that universal high expectations for all students require ambitious and continual improvements in curriculum, instruction, assessment, leadership practices and support systems. These improvements require effective professional learning to expand educators' knowledge, skills, practices and dispositions. Schools demand effective professional learning focused on substantive results for themselves, their colleagues, and their students. Schools artfully combine deep understanding of and cultural responsiveness to the community they serve with high expectations and support for adult learning in order to achieve school and school system goals. They embed professional learning into the organization's vision by communicating that it is a core function for improvement and by establishing and maintaining a public and persistent focus on educator professional learning.

 

LCAP Goal:  All staff members engage in ongoing professional study directed at standards-aligned teaching and learning.

 

LCAP Actions and Services - Year 1:

  1. Support site-based professional learning through Professional Learning Communities.
  2. Provide cluster-level support and learning opportunities through Common Core Cluster support teachers and other district staff.
  3. Schools develop, monitor, and refine a Site Professional Development Plan with clear emphasis on the successful implementation of the Common Core and ELD State Standards and high levels of student achievement.
  4. Establish visiting teacher academies to develop a pool of well-trained visiting teachers.

VISION 2020 Quality Indicator 8, Valuing Diversity to Make Equity a Reality, Destination:  All employees have knowledge and skills necessary to make equity a reality as they support and serve the learning needs of diverse student populations.  Students demonstrate the knowledge and skills, and act throughtfully and respectfully in order to learn collaboratively with, and value the contributions of, studens of different backgrounds.  Multiple cultural perspectives and experiences are valued and people feel respected and welcomed.  Schools challenge and eliminate practices that encourage bias.  Leaders are personally aware of the role race plays in perpetuating a system of bias, prejudice, and inequity. 

 

LCAP Goal:  Staffs and faculties provide learning environments and instructional practices that are culturally responsive and challenge bias.

 

LCAP Actions and Services - Year 1:

  1. Provide cultural proficiency training that recognizes personal bias and discriminatory practices.
  2. Monitor district school policies, practices, and services for evidence of bias or discriminatory practices and take appropriate corrective action.
  3. Provide for school-based systems and supports that reflect varied student needs and interests.

BACKGROUND:  Each month, Superintendent Marten provides an LCAP Implementation Report that includes the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting Year 1 LCAP actions, services, and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's LCAP Implementation Report each month, cluster and advisory groups have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report, and provide input and feedback on current implementation and suggested next steps.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Members of the District Advisory Council (DAC) for Compensatory Education, at their November 19, 2014 meeting, were invited to review the draft LEA Plan revision. The revised draft was posted on the District Advisory Council (DAC) for Compensatory Education webpage and discussed at its November 19, 2014 DAC meeting. In addition, DAC members participated in Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) discussions during several meetings during the 2013–14 school year (e.g., November 20, 2013; February 19, 2014; May 21, 2014; and June 11, 2014). The feedback generated during these meetings, and also via a survey of DAC and schools’ SSC members, was used in the development of the LCAP, and the strategies and actions therein were incorporated into the LEA Plan revision so that district planning documents are aligned.

                          

BACKGROUND:   The California Department of Education (CDE) requires that districts annually review their LEA Plans and update them as necessary.  The district LEA Plan was last updated and approved by the Board of Education on October 8, 2013.  This updated LEA plan (attached) was generated from the state's California Accountability and Improvement System (CAIS) that allows us to enter data electronically to monitor implementation. It is important to note that the district's LEA Plan was the first to be given approval to depart from the state's template and instead, use the district's 12 indicators of quality schools as the structure for the plan.

 

During the 2013-14 school year, San Diego Unified collaborated with stakeholders across the district to create the state-required Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP). The resulting LCAP, like the LEA Plan, provides an overview of key actions and services to promote the achievement of all students, while also addressing the needs of identified student groups (e.g., low income, English learners). In addition, both the LEA Plan and the LCAP use the district's 12 indicators of quality schools as the structure for the plans. It is intentional that these two plans should align and provide a coherent and cohesive structure and plan for improvement that are grounded in, and reflect, the district’s Vision 2020 approach to ensuring Quality Schools in Every Neighborhood. Although the CDE is working to align, or combine in some way, the LEA Plan and LCAP, there is no near-term resolution. The district has, however, begun this work through this initial revision/alignment of the LEA Plan.

 

Following the review by subject matter experts, most of the LEA Plan remains substantially unchanged – but reflects the alignment of key LCAP actions and services, as well as adjustments for details such as time, duplicate tasks, dates, and names of responsible parties.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the resolution in support of the Parent Teacher Association (PTA) Climate Change Initiative.

 

BACKGROUND: Parent Teacher Association (PTA) has, for over 100 years, served as the voice for children and families at local, state, and national levels and works to promote the welfare of children and youth in home, school, community, and place of worship.

 

The PTA strives to raise the standards of home life and to secure adequate laws for the care and protection of children and youth and seeks to bring into closer relation the home and the school, that parents and teachers may cooperate intelligently in the education of children and youth.

 

The PTA works to develop between educators and the general public, such united efforts as will secure for all children and youth, the highest advantages in physical, mental, social, and spiritual education.

 

The San Diego Unified Council of PTAs which represents over 80 PTAs and 11,000 members in the San Diego Unified School District, has identified climate change as a children’s issue and seeks to pass a state PTA resolution.

 

This resolution will support the efforts of the San Diego Unified Environmental Sustainability Advisory Committee which seeks to develop a climate action plan, implement a solar energy plan, join the city and county efforts regarding a community choice, aggregation study, embed sustainability education into the fabric of teaching and learning, and rescue wasted food.

 

The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education supports the efforts of the Council PTA in seeking a California State PTA resolution that climate change is a children’s issue, and will work with the PTA to serve as a role model for practices that promote energy conservation, alternative energy sources, reducing dependency on automobile travel, and encouraging sustainable practices (i.e. the use of resources by the current generation to meet current needs while ensuring that future generations will be able to meet their needs).

 

San Diego Unified will work with the PTA and parents to support programs and strategies to make schools more climate-safe, energy-efficient living laboratories to prepare children for climate changes already underway.
