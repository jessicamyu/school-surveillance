RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the board and the public on Family and Community Engagement (Vision 2020 Quality Indicator and corresponding LCAP Goal 5).

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination:  All schools reach out to parents as partners in their children's education and provide parents with the information necessary to know whether their sons and daughters are on track to success.  Entire communities provide the support and resources to raise expectations and encourage students to graduate ready for success in college and careers.

 

LCAP Goal 5:  Parents and community members are engaged and work within and across schools to support student learning.  Neighborhood schools are highly regarded for their quality as well as their service as centers for extended learning and enrichment opportunities, and academic and social services.

 

Family and Community Engagement Related Actions and Services Identified in LCAP:  

  • 1.2.b./c.6.; 1.10.1.; 5.4.4. - Communication and recruitment strategies will be provided to families to ensure all eligible students participate in Pre-K and Transitional Kindergarten (TK).  
  • 1.9.4. - The district will develop a plan to encourage foster and homeless youth and their parents and guardians to participate as stakeholders in school and district-level initiatives, such as the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP).  
  • 1.10.10. - The district will provide support for parents and families of underachieving African American and Latino students.
  • 5.1.2. - An annual district-wide sub-group, school and cluster family engagement survey will be conducted to identify strengths and areas for improvement in family engagement.
  • 5.1.3. -  Schools/parents/guardians will create a family engagement plan based on survey results, student academic needs, and/or identified priorities, and aligned to district goals and federal and state requirements.  The Design Thinking method for practical, creative resolution of problems and creation of solutions with the intent of an improved future result will be used.  By considering both present and future conditions and parameters of a problem, alternative solutions may be explored simultaneously.
  • 5.1.4. - Parents/guardians/community members will be provided with leadership training opportunities to enhance meaningful engagement, participation in shared decision making and to assume leadership roles.
  • 5.1.5. - The district and all schools, including those receiving Title I funds, will plan and provide family engagement opportunities (e.g., workshops, print, video, online/on-demand, etc.) responsive to the needs of parents and families, and supportive of neighborhood culture and circumstance.
  • 5.1.6. - The district will provide parents/guardians with opportunities (i.e., workshops, college fairs, etc.), guidance on navigating the school system and resources to support their student's preparation for college, career and community.
  • 5.1.7. - The district will develop and provide support and resources (e.g., print, video, online/on-demand, etc.) for parents and families to support student learning on relevant topics such as Common Core and other new content standards, 'a-g' and graduation requirements, district goals, etc.
  • 5.1.8. - The district will develop a plan and timeline to train school staffs, community partners and family leaders to empower each and every family with effective home-based strategies to support their student's learning.
  • 5.1.9. - A consistent venue for parent communications and information will be provided by enhancing the functionality and accessibility of Parent Portal (through PowerSchool), and providing training and support to effectively use this resource.
  • 5.1.10. - Resources and materials (e.g., print, video, online/on-demand, etc.) will be provided to create a welcoming environment for all families and staff.  
  • 5.1.11. - The district will engage the community in a broader dialog and examination of issues related to students, such as Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Questioning (LGTBQIA), race, language, and culture.
  • 5.1.12. - The district will collaborate with the San Diego Education Association (SDEA) to build on and expand the Home Visit Project to increase parent engagement and build positive relations between staff, students, parents, and the community.
  • 5.1.13. - Workshops will be provided on parent-suggested topics such as parent rights, English as a Second Language (ESL), the local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP), Individual Education Plans (IEP), how to navigate the school system, and how to support students with effective home-based strategies.
  • 5.1.14. - The Family and Community Engagement Department will provide support to five clusters each year to provide opportunities to engage parents in Design Thinking sessions and provide training on High Impact Home Strategies.
  • 5.5.1. - The district will develop and implement strategies customized for each cluster by establishing a committee composed of parents and community members to work collaboratively with district staff on initiatives such as communication and information, the implementation of the Framework for Systemic Family Engagement, and establishing criteria to measure family engagement.
  • 5.6.2. - Community partnerships to support student and family engagement and learning will be created, sustained, and expanded.    

Family and Community Engagement Related Metrics Identified in LCAP:  Parent opportunities for input and participation to engage in Design Thinking sessions and training on High Impact Home Strategies:  Establish Baseline.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution and declare October 2016, as National Bullying Prevention Month.  The Superintendent and other appropriate staff will initiate anti-bullying activities at all schools, among all students to be planned, coordinated, and implemented by the individual school site students and staff.  The board encourages parents and the community to support the work of the students and staff.

 

BACKGROUND: The National Bullying Prevention Month campaign was founded in 2006.

 

San Diego Unified School District Vision 2020 calls for all schools to be “safe, attractive neighborhood learning centers,” and each quality neighborhood school to provide a “supportive environment that values diversity in the service of students.

 

School district leadership is committed to actively addressing bullying by utilizing updated and revised protocols and procedures, participating in innovative training, and effectively mitigating incidents of bullying and/or intimidation for all involved.

 

The San Diego Unified Council of ASB Presidents fully supports October as National Bullying Prevention Month in which the schools may conduct anti-bullying activities within the month and throughout the year. Students’ participation in anti-bullying activities provide opportunities for the following to occur:

  • Allow students to speak honestly about their experiences with bullying and find solace in the knowledge that others have been in similar situations.
  • Allow students to learn of the various forms of bullying including, but not limited to, cyber, verbal, physical, exclusion, Islamaphobia, and lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, queer/questioning, intersex, and asexual (LGBTQIA) bullying.
  • Help students become aware of bullying on campus and the consequences of these actions.
  • Support students to come up with solutions to stop bullying and enforce positive interactions between students.
  • Engage teachers and parents in raising awareness if a student is being bullied.
  • Connect with larger community and civic efforts to confront bullying.
  • Help empower students in the recognition of the role everyone plays in addressing and reducing bullying with the goal to eradicate bullying on all school campuses.

All elementary, middle, and high schools shall coordinate anti-bullying activities at the beginning of each new school year to be held in the month of October, during a week or days of their choosing.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve and declare October 2016, as Filipino American Heritage Month, and recognize that this is a time of reflection and remembrance, and provides an opportunity for all people in the United States to learn and appreciate Filipino Americans’ historic contributions to the Nation.

 

BACKGROUND: In 1988, Filipino American History Month was first proclaimed by the 103rd Congress of the United States through House Resolution 155.

 

The Filipino American community is the second largest Asian American group in the United States with a population of approximately 3,100,000 people and over 7,000 students in the San Diego Unified School District.

 

Filipino Americans have contributed greatly to the fine arts, music, dance, literature, business, journalism, education, science, technology, government, politics, fashion, health care, the armed forces, and other fields in the United States that enrich the landscape of the country.

 

Efforts should continue to promote Filipino American history and culture, because the roles of Filipino Americans and other people of color have been overlooked in the writing, teaching, and learning of United States history.

 

In September 1988, Bell Junior High School was the largest junior high school west of the Mississippi, and had the largest concentration of Filipino students in the San Diego Unified School District.

 

In 1989, the Filipino community rallied to advocate the offering of Filipino language classes at Morse High School and Bell Junior High School.

 

Filipino language classes are now offered at Challenger Middle School and Mira Mesa High School as well.

 

When No Child Left Behind Legislation was signed into law in 2002, the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing required that a California Subject Matter Examination for Filipino be written so that teachers, who are credentialed in other subject matter other than Filipino, be required to pass the Filipino examination.

 

Filipino Heritage Month is a time of reflection and remembrance, and provides an opportunity for all people in the United States to learn and appreciate Filipino Americans’ historic contributions to the Nation.

Proposed Project
The proposed project involves the implementation of various improvements that would provide Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) access to the existing stadium, softball field, and baseball field at Mira Mesa High School.  ADA access would be provided from the various parking areas around the campus to the stadium and baseball/softball field areas.   The proposed project includes various upgrades to the existing stadium facilities, softball field, and baseball field. The following is a list of the specific improvements included under the proposed project (See Attachment A for Figures 1 and 2—Site Location and Conceptual Site Plan):

  • Renovation of the existing home and visitors’ bleachers, including ADA improvements and increased seating capacity
  • Renovation of the existing press box
  • Renovation of existing concessions buildings including home and visitors’ restrooms with ADA upgrades
  • Installation of a new modular elevator at the renovated home bleachers to provide ADA press box access
  • Replace existing discus and shot put field
  • Construction of new home and visitors’ ticket booth and access gates
  • Installation of two new dugouts, scorers booth, bullpen, and five-tier aluminum bleacher at the existing softball field
  • Installation of new replacement light-emitting diode (LED) scoreboard at the stadium and new LED scoreboards at the softball field and baseball field
  • Replacement of four, existing 75-foot stadium high-intensity discharge (HID) light poles with four, new 90-foot stadium LED light poles
  • Replacement of the existing public address system at the stadium
  • Installation of new hardscape and landscape around the existing all-weather track
  • Installation of a new upper level artificial turf all-purpose play field
  • Construction of new ADA accessible upgrades to paths of travel and parking lots
  • Installation of new emergency egress lighting along the paths of travel

The proposed, new emergency egress lighting would be installed along the paths of travel from the stadium to various parking lots around the campus to improve safety and security.  All new proposed lighting elements, including the new replacement stadium lights, would be down-shielded to prevent offsite light spillover in accordance with City of San Diego regulations (Municipal Code sections 142.0730 and 142.0740).

 

Construction activities would be limited to between 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m. to comply with the City of San Diego’s noise ordinance.  Construction of the proposed project is anticipated to commence in the fall of 2016, with an approximate duration of 11 months. All construction equipment staging would occur within the boundaries of the northwest portion of the school site.  The project includes best management practices that will be implemented during construction of the project to minimize stormwater impacts.

 

Approval of this item approves the project as described and authorizes and directs staff to file a Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk.

The purpose of the Final IS/MND was to provide the decision-making body (Board of Education), responsible agencies, and the public with information regarding the environmental impact of the proposed project.  The Board of Education adopted the Final IS/MND on November 19, 2013.  The notice of determination for the project was filed with the County Clerk’s Office on December 9, 2013 (Attachment 1).

 

Since adoption of the Final IS/MND, construction of the approved project has begun. During construction, it was determined that soils that were previously identified for export from the site would need to be reused on site because of soil contamination (i.e., arsenic and organochlorine pesticides (OCP) gamma chlordane and dichlorodiphenyldichloroethylene [DDE]).  Although it was determined that the human health risk from these contaminants is less than significant, the soil could not be exported from the site without restriction.  It was determined, therefore, that the soil was more suitable for onsite reuse than offsite disposal.

 

The proposed changes to the approved project analyzed in the Addendum (referred to as the “proposed” project), would include reuse of the soils on site, which would result in the elevation of the existing grade of the playfields by 1 to 2 feet, construction of a berm on the north side of the project site, and raising the elevation of a gravel area to match the 61st Street elevation at the northwest corner.  With these proposed changes to the approved project, site grading would now be balanced on site, and the exportation of soils, as originally analyzed in the 2013 Final IS/MND for the previously approved project, would no longer occur.  Given the final design of the new high school, it was determined that bioswales rather than stormwater retention ponds would be used to address the hydrology and water quality impacts of the project.  Changes regarding the use and design of the playfields are now being proposed.  The project now proposes to include a football field, softball field, and running track.  The playfields would no longer involve a joint-use venture with the City of San Diego (City).

 

Environmental Review

The district has prepared this CEQA Addendum to address potential environmental impacts related to implementing the revisions to the proposed project on the project site.  The Final IS/MND and this Addendum, together with the documents that are incorporated by reference, serve as the environmental review of the proposed project.

 

As described in Attachment 2, the proposed changes to the O’Farrell Community School Modernization and New High School Facility Project would involve the following:

  • Reusing soils excavated from the new high school site at the playfields site (it was assumed in the Final IS/MND that soils excavated from the new high school site would be exported off site);
  • Adding a berm along the northern portion of the project site and elevating the playfields by 1 to 2 feet to accommodate reuse of the excavated soils;
  • Increasing the size of the playfields from 2 acres to 5 acres and adding a football field and track in addition to the originally proposed softball field;
  • Using bioswales instead of retention ponds to address hydrology and water quality issues; and
  • Removing consideration of the joint-use agreement with the City for use of the playfields.

Incorporation of these changes into the proposed project would result in minor changes related to the conclusions for geology and soils in the Final IS/MND.  Given the conclusions of the geotechnical report included in Attachment 2 to the Addendum, reuse of the soils excavated from the new high school site for the playfields would require implementation of a lime mixture to reduce the expansive potential of the soil.  All other conclusions from the Final IS/MND remained applicable to the proposed project.  Soils excavated from the high school were determined to be safe for reuse at the playfields and did not require any further mitigation not already identified in the Final IS/MND.

 

The use of bioswales would allow the same level of stormwater retention and filtration as the proposed retention ponds and result in no changes to the conclusions regarding hydrology and water quality impacts in the Final IS/MND.

 

The joint-use agreement, which would have increased the availability of recreational resources for surrounding residents, would not eliminate an existing resource because a joint-use agreement was not in place prior to the proposed project. Therefore, elimination of the joint-use agreement would not increase use at neighboring recreational facilities.

 

There would be no increase in noise or traffic related to the minor expansion of the playfield because daytime use, including sporting events and games, of the facilities by school students was assumed in the Final IS/MND.

 

As concluded in the Final IS/MND, all impacts associated with the proposed project would be less than significant or less than significant with mitigation incorporated. Impacts resulting from the proposed changes would be less than significant, and the proposed changes would not result in new significant impacts or substantially increase the severity of previously identified impacts.  The changes would not require new mitigation measures that were not already considered in the Final IS/MND.
