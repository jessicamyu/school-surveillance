RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution in the matter of support for federal gun control action.

 

BACKGROUND: Senseless and tragic mass shootings at places such as schools, colleges, and universities; movie theatres; music festivals; hospitals and medical clinics; shopping malls; and religious institutions have highlighted the dangers of and clearly demonstrates that gun-related violence can occur any place at any time.

 

The crisis of gun violence in our country necessitates a coordinated and collaborative effort involving entire communities, elected officials at every level of government, law enforcement, and the entire criminal justice system.

 

Elected officials must commit to closing gaps in the current patchwork of regulation, including those gaps that enable felons, people convicted of domestic violence, children, those found to be a danger to themselves or others, and other prohibited persons to access firearms, and those that allow the trafficking of illegal guns.

 

Since the Columbine High School shooting in 1999 and the Sandy Hook Massacre in 2012, there have been more than 200 school shootings nationwide shattering communities and leaving emotional scars.

 

The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District demands action from our State and Federal Representatives to reinstate the assault weapon ban and adopt stricter controls governing the sale, transfer, possession, manufacturing, and distribution of all firearms, dangerous weapons, and ammunition.

 

And that those stricter controls include not only comprehensive and thorough background checks of all purchases of firearms, including curios and relics; dangerous weapons; and ammunition; and include the purchase of those items facilitated through the internet and at gun shows; but also an outright ban on semi-automatic firearms, high-capacity magazines, armor-piercing ammunition, bump stocks, and any other equipment, alteration, or modification that would increase a firearm’s capacity for ammunition or rate of fire; as well as reasonable waiting periods and mandated training in the safe use of guns.

BACKGROUND:

Proposed Project

In alignment with the recommendations of the San Diego Unified School District's (District) Environmental Sustainability Advisory Committee, Photovoltaic System (PV) Installation projects have been initiated to create an energy efficient and renewable power source, delivering a significant cost savings to the District.

 

The District, as the lead agency under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), has prepared the attached Solar Photovoltaic Installations Initial Study to evaluate the potential environmental consequences associated with solar PV installations (Proposed Project) at eight District schools, including Chollas-Mead Elementary School, DePortola Middle School, Knox Middle School, CPMA at Kroc Middle School, Kearny High School, Madison High School, University City High School, and SCPA, which serves grades 6-12. Specifically, the Proposed Project involves the installation of a fixed-tilt canopy and/or a ground-mount array system at each of the existing school sites. Implementation of the Proposed Project would require approval by the Board of Education. As part of the District’s discretionary review process, the Proposed Project is required to undergo an environmental review in accordance with CEQA.

 

The Proposed Project involves the installation of a solar power generation system on the existing school sites. The solar power generation system would consist of PV arrays mounted onto canopy structures and/or a ground-mount array system in existing graded and disturbed areas within the campus, such as parking lots. All proposed work would occur entirely within the boundaries of the existing school property. The solar PV arrays would generally be supported by metal cantilever frames anchored in concrete pier foundations. The individual solar PV panels would be constructed of dark-colored (usually blue or black) materials and covered with anti-reflective coatings. The Proposed Project would be designed to ensure that all existing Americans with Disabilities Act accessible paths of travel are maintained or reconfigured as prescribed by code. Electrical transmission lines would be installed in an underground conduit and would extend from the combiner to the main electrical service in one of the existing school buildings. All affected hardscape and/or landscape areas would be restored to pre-project conditions once construction is complete.

 

New lighting would be installed under the proposed canopies to provide a similar amount and quality of lighting as the existing lighting on the campus. All new lighting would be down-shielded to prevent any light spillover off-site in accordance with City of San Diego regulations on glare and outdoor lighting (Municipal Code Sections 142.0730 and 142.0740).

 

Environmental Review

Public Resources Code Section 21080.35 provides a statutory exemption that applies to the installation of solar photovoltaic (PV) arrays within existing parking lots. Each of the eight project sites include the installation of PV arrays within existing parking lots and those installations are therefore statutorily exempt under Public Resources Code Section 21080.35. However, PV installations at CPMA, University City High School, and SCPA would also occur in areas outside of parking lots. These locations were separately analyzed to determine whether the proposed project(s) qualified for a categorical exemption. After completing an Initial Study, the following exemptions were identified as applicable to the proposed project(s). 

 

15311. Accessory Structures (Class 11)

Class 11 consists of construction, or placement of minor structure accessory to (appurtenant to) existing commercial, industrial, or institutional facilities.

 

The proposed canopy structures would be located in the existing developed or previously disturbed areas of the campus, and would serve as carports/shade structures if installed in the existing parking lots on the campus.

 

15314. Minor Additions to Schools (Class 14)

Class 14 consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or 10 classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption.

 

The Proposed Project would not add new classrooms or other facilities on the existing campus and would therefore not increase the existing capacity of the school site.

 

Categorical exemptions may not be relied upon if the application of the exemption triggers any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2. Based on the initial study, staff has determined that no exception applies to prohibit application of the statutory and/or categorical exemptions. Approval of this item authorizes and directs staff to file a NOE, as set forth in the attached Appendix E and Attachment A, with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk and California Office of Planning and Research State Clearinghouse.

RECOMMENDATIONS: Authorization to continue utilizing the Agreement with Computers 2 SD Kids, a 501(c)(3) corporation, to provide recycling services of retired district technology assets (SV-16-1155-19). Authorize the execution of any documents necessary to effectuate these services. This authorization extends the term through June 27, 2021, for a revised term of June 28, 2016 through June 27, 2021.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: This authorization will allow the district to continue responsibly recycling retired district technology assets, providing logistical efficiencies in the disposal of such equipment, and providing computers to low income students and families who otherwise cannot afford them. Computers 2 SD Kids (C2SDK) accepts all working and non-working electronics. C2SDK either refurbishes the technology and donates to local families in need or submits the hardware elements for recycling.

 

From August 3, 2016 -September 1, 2017 SDUSD technology donations included 28,565 devices, of which 23,694 were able to be refurbished back to a factory delivered state and then redistributed to the community. The remaining 4,871 devices were used for spare parts and/or recycled netting over 13,000 pounds in reduced landfill and hazardous materials disposal. 
 
C2SDK handles all logistics necessary for the pickup and sanitation of the retired assets including:
1. Picking up the assets directly from locations where assets are retired, or from a central location specifically assigned by the district.
2. If necessary, providing a pallet to stage assets until pickup is requested.
3. Securely transporting and storing assets until they can be sanitized.
4. Removing all district licensed software and license key stickers (if applicable).
5. Sanitizing all data storage media pursuant to Department of Defense 5220.22-M data destruction methods.
 
When assets are picked up from the district by C2SDK, a receipt of assets that contains a list of all assets with serial numbers will be provided to the district. 
 
Once data destruction is completed on data storage media, C2SDK provides a receipt of sanitation to the district.

 

According to C2SDK, official San Diego County estimates suggest that 41.8 percent of public school students are from low-income families, 28 percent of San Diego families do not have a computer in their home, and nearly 60 percent of low-income households do not have a working computer in the home. Children of low-income families rely primarily on their school computers and have no technological access at home. 

 

This authorization to the current Agreement is beneficial because it continues to allow the district to narrow the technology gap for low income students and families and efficiently recycle/dispose of surplus equipment which has exceeded its useful life for the district.
