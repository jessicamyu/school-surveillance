RECOMMENDATION: Adopt proclamation in honor of Morris Casuto and call on all San Diegans to honor Morris Casuto's legacy by standing up for the vulnerable and by building bridges between diverse communities based on honesty and respect.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: San Diego lost a huge champion for equity when Morris Casuto passed away at age 79 on February 11, 2022 at UCSD Medical Center. Casuto dedicated 37 years of service to the Anti-Defamation League, founded the local chapter and led the San Diego branch from 1978 to 2010. Casuto was known for his wit, humor, intellect and passion for justice, serving as a powerful local voice against all intolerance. Casuto was also known for his courage and was often targeted by White supremacists for his commitment to equal justice, personally threatened, forced to wear a protective vest, his home telephone number and address published by extremist websites, and had to install monitors, locks and other defensive devices at his home and his offices. Casuto served an important role in training law enforcement on how to support the victims of hate crimes, explaining, “We realized that police are trained to look at the seriousness of a crime by injury, theft or damage, but very often with hate crimes, the injury is internal. … Police have to know that there is a way to deal with this type of incident so that the victim will be given some type of comfort, that they are not alone.” Casuto also provided important service to the education community, serving on the Integration Task Force to reform San Diego Unified and helping to begin the “World of Difference” diversity program in schools. ADL San Diego Director Tammy Gillies has said, “Morris was a leader in safety and security for the Jewish community, and was a force to be reckoned with. He was a warrior for the Jewish people and a voice for anyone who was a target of hate. We are grateful for the foundation that Morris laid and proud to continue his work.” We call on all San Diegans to honor the legacy left to us by Morris Casuto by standing up for the vulnerable and by building bridges between diverse communities based on honesty and respect.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing March 2022, as Women's History Month in San Diego Unified and encourage student participation in appropriate programs and school activities.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The origins of Women’s History Month began in 1978 with a weeklong celebration planned by the Education Task Force of the Sonoma County (California) Commission on the Status of Women; and, in 1980, President Jimmy Carter issued the first Presidential Proclamation, declaring the Week of March 8th, 1980 Nation Women’s History Week. In 1987, Congress passed Public Law 100-9, designating March as Women’s History Month and, since 1995, each President has issued an annual proclamation re-designating March as Women’s History Month. Women fought fiercely for over 100 years for their voices to be heard in American politics and policy before they were given the right to vote with the ratification of the 19th Amendment in 1920 and even then, women of all races were not able to vote. Women of every race, class, and ethnic background have made historic contributions to the growth and strength of our Nation in countless recorded and unrecorded ways. Women, especially women of color, are still underrepresented in state and federal government, executive positions in major corporations, and continue to be paid less than their male counterparts. Women have played and continue to play a critical economic, cultural, and social role in every sphere of the life of the Nation by constituting a significant portion of the labor force working inside and outside of the home despite persistent and enduring wage gaps. Women have played a unique role throughout the history of the Nation by providing the majority of the volunteer labor force of the Nation. Women were particularly important in the establishment of early charitable, philanthropic, and cultural institutions in our Nation. Women of every race, class, and ethnic background served as early leaders in the forefront of every major progressive social change movement. Women have been leaders, not only in securing their own rights of suffrage and equal opportunity, but also in the abolitionist movement, the emancipation movement, the industrial labor movement, the civil rights movement, and other movements, especially the peace movement, which create a more fair and just society for all. The history and contributions of women have consistently been overlooked, misinterpreted, and undervalued in the curriculum of public education institutions and still today. San Diego Unified is committed to recognizing and honoring women for their many contributions not only to their communities but to the students and families in our district schools.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing March 2022, as California Arts Education Month in San Diego Unified and recognize the efforts and dedication of arts educators and advocates around the state. 

 

The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District supports the ‘Arts ARE Education’ campaign that calls for continued funding of arts education programs, supporting arts educators, and availability of resources that are critical to successful arts programs, and facilities built and furnished for arts-based activities. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District is committed to supporting and advancing projects and collaborations that bring high-quality, standards-based arts education into the lives of students in public schools throughout the District. California Arts Education Month celebrates arts education which includes dance, music, theatre, various visual arts, and media arts. These experiences encourage creative learning in children and young adults. A well-rounded education should include equitable access to the stimulation and creative thinking that the arts help to develop, and supports social and emotional development, health, and well-being. Learning in and through the arts enables students to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, imagination and creativity, discipline, alternative ways to communicate and express feelings and ideas, and cross-cultural understanding, which supports academic success across the curriculum as well as personal growth beyond the classroom. The arts can transform our schools into havens of creativity and exploration, where students want to learn, teachers want to teach, and all members of the learning community are more engaged and motivated. High quality school-based arts education involves a wide range of partners, including school boards, district administrators, educators, parents, teaching artists, arts organizations, community members, and local businesses, whose collective endeavors towards equitable provision of arts learning for all students we celebrate and promote. San Diego Unified recognizes and celebrates the essential role of arts education in our District schools.

BACKGROUND: This License Agreement would allow Jewish Family Service (JFS) to temporarily utilize 100 of approximately 400 existing parking spaces at George Walker Smith Education Center, 9330 Balboa Avenue, San Diego, CA, for 18 months or until construction commences at the site. JFS requested this for staff overflow parking for their nearby Balboa Avenue facility. JFS previously rented parking from Coleman College which is no longer available due to construction.

 

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The approval of the Proposed Project is subject to CEQA, however, it is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Sections 15301 (Class 1) – Existing Facilities. Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of existing or former use. The Proposed Project consists of a license agreement for temporary parking on an existing district owned site. The proposed agreement would not displace existing parking because less than 100 of the existing 400 spaces are being used by the district. Additionally, the proposed agreement would not result in the construction of new parking spaces or parking related facilities. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 exemption is appropriate.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

 

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of an existing district property, specifically an existing parking lot. The Proposed Project is consistent with the existing use on the project site. Furthermore, the Proposed Project would not extend into any offsite or open space areas that may be classified as an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed at the project site or in the project vicinity that, when combined with operation of the Proposed Project, would result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. There is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances because no new construction or change in use is proposed. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project does not require the construction of new facilities and would not directly, or indirectly, affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. The Proposed Project does not require the construction of new facilities and would not directly, or indirectly, affect an existing hazardous waste site. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. The Proposed Project does not require the construction of new facilities and would not directly, or indirectly, affect an existing historic or prehistoric resource. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.

 

Approval of this item approves the Proposed Project as described, authorizes and directs staff to file the attached Notice of Exemption (Attachment 2) with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk, and approves the License Agreement with Jewish Family Service of San Diego for Temporary Parking at George Walker Smith Education Center, 9330 Balboa Avenue, San Diego, CA.

The term of this Agreement remains July 1, 2018 through June 30, 2023. Child Youth and Transition will bring forward a new Agreement in June 2023 to capture Fiscal Year 2023/2024 services.

Budget Strings: 5582-32120-00-5853-2495-0000-01000-3814, 5582-00006-00-5853-2495-0000-01000-0000, 5660-90566-00-5853-1000-1110-01000-0000 and Various Site Identified Funds.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On July 24, 2018, the board approved the Agreement with Alliance for African Assistance in the amount of $75,000.00 to provide translation and interpreting services in languages other than Spanish and Vietnamese for students and families from July 25, 2018 through June 30, 2023.  

 

On May 12, 2020, the board approved the First Amendment to add $95,000.00 to the Agreement to continue to allow for all districtwide translation and interpreting services, in languages other than Spanish and Vietnamese, that are requested in support of distance learning, including during the COVID-19 Pandemic. The Alliance for African Assistance (AKA Global Village Language Bank) will allow the district to provide translation/interpretation services for 100-plus additional languages. 

 

On December 15, 2020, the board approved the Second Amendment to add $250,000.00 to continue to facilitate increased communications with parents, families, and community members to facilitate and share information.

 

This Third Amendment will allow for additional families, that require translation services, to have access to these services to ensure equitable educational opportunities. 

 

  • Alliance for African Assistance / Global Village is a non-profit organization that has served refugee communities since 1989. Alliance for African Assistance was founded by Walter Lam, a refugee from Uganda, and has focused on refugee resettlement and community services, including interpretation/translation services.
  • Alliance for African Assistance / Global Village translators/interpreters are independent contractors that are paid competitive market rates and mileage if they are traveling to a site.
  • Alliance for African Assistance / Global Village can provide 120+ languages that are available nationwide through three-way phone interpretation 24/7.
  • The district has had a contract with Alliance for African Assistance / Global Village for at least the last 10 years, first with the Special Ed Translation Unit and for the last five years with the Translation Department.
  • Alliance for African Assistance / Global Village has provided competitive rates that also considers the independent contractors that are part of their team.

 

Allowable under Elementary and Secondary Emergency Relief (ESSER) through support of activities to address the unique needs of students (low-income children or students, children with disabilities, English learners, racial and ethnic minorities, students experiencing homelessness, and foster care youth) which includes outreach and service delivery.
