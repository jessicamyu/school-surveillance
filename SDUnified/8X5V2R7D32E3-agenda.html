BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue our improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting district wide, we are implementing a new process streamlining our Independent Contractor Agreements. Our goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective deputy or chief, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

The law intends that the funds go to the districts and schools that have the highest concentrations of poverty. Title I requires funding to be allocated to a district based on the number of “children in poverty” residing within the district boundary. The federal census is used to identify children ages five through seventeen living in poverty who are included in the calculation of Title I entitlement.

District leadership is charged with designing a plan that meets legislative requirements while producing sustainable support that will positively impact student learning and significantly raise achievement over time during an era of diminishing unrestricted resources.   • Impact of the district continuing in Program Improvement

BACKGROUND:  The CDE permits districts to claim Average Daily Attendance (ADA) and instructional time credits for days on which schools are forced to close.  Approval of the request allows the school agency to receive credit for the material decrease in ADA for the temporary school closure and for the missed instructional time.  Once the credit is approved, the district is no longer required to make up the day, and receives the ADA and instructional minutes as though the emergency did not occur.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the First Phase of the Human Resources reorganization as presented in the accompanying documents.

FISCAL IMPACT:  First Phase is $437,435 in salaries, plus $170,350 in variable and fixed benefits, General Fund Unrestricted.  Total annual cost this year is $145,811 plus $56,783 in variable and fixed benefits.  Cost will go down with subsequent phases.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Increase of five positions in first phase.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Reviewed plan several times with Labor Coalition Group.

BACKGROUND:

In Phase 1:

  • Director positions over each of these key areas (Certificated and Classified) will be created.  These are positions that were eliminated in the various budget reductions over the past five years.  They are essential to the effective functioning of the department.  Recruitment for these positions will be both internal and external.
  • The chief will personally work with administrator hiring, accountability and discipline to insure consistency and fairness across the district. The reorganization creates an appeal structure from HRO – Director – Chief for disputes related to administrative action related to employees.
  • One additional Human Resource Officer position will be created on the certificated side.  Recruitment will be internal and external.
  • Three Confidential I positions will be created to support the administrators.  One of these is a downgrade from a II to a I (a vacant position) and two are new.
  • The existing HR Manager position (vacant) will be downgraded to a Supervisor position and filled.
  • Already budgeted positions that are or become vacant will be filled to support the department’s functioning.
  • The role of HR within the overall district function and accountability will be clarified and codified.  The focus will be clarifying the role of the Chief HR Officer in the overall responsibility for hiring, discipline, and staffing of the district.

In the future, once the director positions are filled and further discussions are held with the union coalition, a second phase will be brought forward to the board for consideration. This will come later on in the spring of 2013.

 

This Phase 1 reorganization effort will provide the beginning structure and resources needed to move the department forward in its functioning, but it will not solve all the challenges, some of which are procedural and organizational across the district.  To accomplish that, the following will also have to be considered in the future:

  • The integration and roles of finance, HR, legal, and labor relations need to be further clarified.  The result then needs to be refined and communicated to all district stakeholders.  Funding, staffing and position control need tightly integrated with all stakeholders aware of different department’s role in the eventual outcome. 
  • Specific policy needs refined related to the role of HR in hiring, disciplinary and accountability policies and procedures.  This may involve negotiations and/or changes in current policy.  Labor groups are frustrated that HR is not more active in the ultimate decisions related to these issues.  HR is unsure about its role in the same.   This needs clarified in the near-term.

Staff has met, listened to and worked with labor groups to refine this reorganization on several different occasions, as recently as Friday, December 21.  Labor had created a Coalition to meet with staff to provide input on the reorganization.  The Coalition provided a document to staff with recommendations, concerns and demands.  In response to this collective input staff has amended the reorganization plan three times from the original.  In Phase 1, these changes have saved the district over $250,000 $337,416 in cost from the original design.  The cost will continue to decline in subsequent phases.  Once the directors are in place, the HR leadership team will meet with the Labor Coalition to further refine the reorganization to save further costs.

 

The Phase 1 of the reorganization adds a net total of 5 FTEs and incurs a GFU net annual additional cost of $437,435 $607,785 (includes benefits) down from the original cost of approximately $680,000 $945,201 (includes benefits).  Impact on the remaining 2012-13 GFU budget is a maximum of $145,811 $202,594 (maximum projected cost this year), assuming the positions are promptly filled.  This cost will lessen through subsequent phases.

 

Staff asks the board to continue its support of HR by approving the reorganization and to direct the continued coordination of a districtwide effort to clarify policy, procedures and roles in the staffing, hiring, allocation and accountability functions.
