BACKGROUND: At the January 28, 2014 board meeting, the superintendent was directed to scale a plan that promotes and implements positive behavior strategies and supports as the preferred discipline method across San Diego Unified.  The proposed plan would be an alternative to traditional discipline approaches as well as address the disproportionate suspension and expulsion rates for African American and Latino students.

 

The board's critical role in reshaping discipline policies to create safe and supportive learning environments is a hallmark of the Vision 2020 Quality Schools in Every Neighborhood initiative.  With this in mind, historical and current discipline data, positive behavior strategies, as well as district policies and practices related to such topics as Zero Tolerance, Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS), and Restorative Justice will be introduced for discussion purposes and board action.

 

Agreement with the San Diego County Superintendent of Schools to Implement the PASS AmeriCorps Program: The PASS (Promoting Achievement and Student Success) AmeriCorps program provides mentoring and prevention/intervention services to students in designated school sites who are identified as at-risk for violence.  AmeriCorps is a civilian corps of individuals whose interests are to serve communities in need by giving one year of service to realize the five AmeriCorps Promises to Youth: (1) safe places, (2) a healthy start for mind and body, (3) caring relationships with adults, (4) marketable skills and (5) volunteer opportunities for youth in their schools and communities.  PASS AmeriCorps mentors are trained to provide services with the assistance of a visiting on-site PASS support supervisor to ensure program objectives are maintained and that the experience is a meaningful and productive one.

 

The San Diego County Office of Education will provide outreach, hire, train, support and supervise five full-time PASS AmeriCorps staff to provide gang and violence prevention services, including support services for students/families, at the above mentioned district school sites.  PASS AmeriCorps volunteers will provide support to students to help them to develop decision-making skills and strategies for making healthy tobacco, drug, gang, and violence-free choices

RECOMMENDATION: Approve revisions to Administrative Procedures 9229 (related to use of athletic fields and stadiums) and 9205 (related to Civic Center use of district facilities), and adopt Field Use Policy for Point Loma High School. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: This is a second reading of this item.  The first reading on this item occurred on June 24, 2014.  District staff have been working with sites and community groups to design specific site-based field use policies which ensures maximized benefits to the school; considers the size, location and available facilities at the site; and addresses the needs and concerns of the surrounding community. 

 

Following the first reading, staff met again with the Point Loma High School community to discuss their reaction to the proposed draft.  Based upon that discussion and in consultation with the site and the Superintendent, the proposed Field Use Policy has been revised to provide  a cap of eighteen nighttime lighted events, not including playoff games or the use of lights to complete games or practices during Pacific Standard Time.  Use of the lighted stadium shall be generally reserved for PLHS use.  Any other school or group wishing to use the stadium may appeal to the Superintendent; who may grant the appeal only after ensuring the proposed event can be properly managed and will not cause the total nighttime events to be held at the lighted stadium to exceed eighteen.  Finally, the Cluster will hold semi-annual meetings at which the larger community shall be invited, for the specific purposes of reviewing the use of the stadium and its impact on the community.

 

The proposed Field Use Policy for Point Loma High School is intended to be the first of several site-specific field use policies which will be brought forward by staff as athletic fields are upgraded and stadium lighting is constructed.  It is intended that each field use policy will operate under the umbrella of a districtwide field use policy which sets forth districtwide policies and procedures for the use of athletic fields and lighted stadiums, including prioritization for use, guidelines for times and manner of use, and measures to mitigate negative impacts to the community.  The districtwide policies are provided in the revisions to Administrative Procedure 9229, related to the use of athletic fields and lighted stadiums.  The proposed revisions to Administrative Procedure 9205, related to Civic Center use of district facilities, incorporate by reference the proposed districtwide and site-specific policies and restates that Civic Center use of district facilities shall be managed in a manner which maximizes benefits to the school and its students, and minimizes negative impacts to the surrounding community.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the Superintendent's proposed concept for expenditure of $1.5 million in Title I funding for supplemental performing arts programs at schools with 75 percent or greater Title I eligibility (84 sites are eligible in the 2014-15 school year), and direct the Superintendent to develop program details and parameters for board approval in September 2014. 

 

The Superintendent proposes offering two-year supplemental performing arts programs, inspired by and developed around the Turnaround Arts program model, for which eligible schools can apply to augment their educational programs.  Eligible schools will apply via a mini-grant process for these programs and will be required to demonstrate how a selected program addresses specific site needs.

 

As identified in the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP), targeted areas for district-wide improvement include increased student achievement and attendance, decreased disciplinary issues, and improved school climate.  The effectiveness of participation in supplemental arts programs at the eligible schools on these targeted areas will be measured over the two years, using baseline data developed for the LCAP. 

 

Founded in 2012, Turnaround Arts is a national program by the President's Committee, in coordination with the White House, the U.S. Department of Education, and several foundations to bring arts education programming and resources to high-poverty, elementary and middle schools across the country.  It is based on the premise that high-quality and integrated arts education can strengthen school reform efforts, boost academic achievement, and increase student engagement in schools facing some of the toughest educational challenges in the country.  First Lady Michelle Obama, Honorary Chair of the President's Committee, invited the San Diego Unified School District to attend the Turnaround Arts White House Talent Show in Washington, D.C. in May 2014.  Burbank Elementary has been selected to participate in the Turnaround Arts program for the 2014-15 and 2015-16 school years. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: $1.5 million.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.   

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The District Advisory Committee on Compensatory Education (DAC) will be included in the development of the program details and parameters.  

 

BACKGROUND:  The Board of Education created this Title I grant program on November 29, 2011, as a pilot project targeting high-poverty schools to improve student achievement.  During the remainder of the 2011-12 school year, staff began developing an application and scoring process for board approval.  The project was placed on hold during the 2012-13 and 2013-14 school years due to concerns about potential Title I reductions, and continuing state budget issues.  The district is now prepared to resume the project during the 2014-15 school year.

BACKGROUND: For the first time in several years, the San Diego Unified School District would like to consider sponsoring or co-sponsoring legislation to add or modify existing law.  At the April 8, 2014 regular board meeting, the board directed staff to conduct research and develop a process for San Diego Unified to be more proactive in sponsoring legislation in the future.  In response to the board request, this Legislative Sponsorship Request Form is a template that contains information needed to consider any legislative proposals.  The appropriate department and its staff with interest in modifying the education code or other state statutes will be asked to justify the need for the bill, and the proposal will need to be aligned with the Superintendent's and board's stated objectives for the district.

Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Since the middle of the 1990's, the district has used the eSchool Solution absence management software to staff classrooms or school site front office staff with substitutes. The original software was DOS based and called Substitute Employee Management System (SEMS) and in the early 2000’s was upgraded to Windows. In 2006, the district upgraded to the new web version of the product called SmartFind Express (SFE). As part of that upgrade, the district replaced the server hardware, increased phone line capacity, and consolidated all of the equipment into the Data Center and is now the substitute employee system for both Food Service and Custodial Operations. In addition, the system has seamless integration with two key systems:

  • The PeopleSoft Payroll system that automates sending hourly (substitute/visiting teacher) time to Payroll for processing.
  • The Electronic Registrar On-Line (ERO) system that allows teachers and non-teaching staff to automatically request a substitute when registering for training and/or professional development and has been in use since 2009. 

The original server hardware is no longer eligible for support and must be replaced. An increase to the phone line capacity is required  in order to accommodate the addition of Food Services and Custodial Operations and the anticipated increase in professional development requiring  a substitute teacher for the classroom as the district prepares to deploy Common Core. As an option to the costly replacement of the server hardware, Information Technology Support Services staff explored the eSchool Solutions cloud based solution (also called Software as a Service (SaaS)) to determine its ability to accommodate the District's volume of substitute assignments (which typically handles 1500+ assignments/day) and continue to be cost effective.

 

After a thorough evaluation of  cloud-based absence management software solution, ITSS staff solicited input from the Human Resources and Finance departments. Senior leadership in both divisions agree with ITSS staff and recommends approval of an Agreement with eSchool Solutions to provide a cloud based substitute employee management system  that allows for teaching and non-teaching staff to report and fill their absences without additional training. This solution is cost effective as it no longer requires district staff to maintain servers, communication/phone lines, and provide support of the current, on premise solution. Finally, the integration to the PeopleSoft Payroll system and ERO will remain intact and migration to  the cloud-based solution will be seamless to the end user.

BACKGROUND: The district and SDEA bargained and reached agreement for an amendment to the February 24, 2014 agreement to the implementation of a 2014-15 SERP, upon Board of Education and San Diego County Office of Education approvals.  At the April 29, 2014 board meeting, the board extended the deadline for eligible participants until May 23, 2014.  Effective May 5, 2014, all resignations submitted as part of the SERP were non-revocable.  Upon board action at the May 27, 2014 board meeting, the SERP was approved for 472 eligible employees and enrollment was closed.  On June 9, 2014, the district announced new staffing allocation guidelines, prompting a small number of additional requests from SDEA members to enroll in the SERP.  The board took action on June 24, 2014, authorizing an extension of the SERP deadline to June 27, 2014.  This information reflects the final analysis based on actual enrollment of 477 employees as of July 9, 2014.
