2. District Accountability Report: Focus on High School (LCAP/Vision 2020 Indicator 5: Closing the Achievement Gap with High Expectations for All)

RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the board on the implementation of LCAP/Vision 2020 Quality Indicator 5: Closing the Achievement Gap with High Expectations for All, with a focus on high school.

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator 5, Closing the Achievement Gap with High Expectations for All, Destination:  Every school is strongly committed to maintaining high expectations for all students and to eliminating persistent disparities in achievement and performance among student groups based on race, ethnicity, language, or disability. These disparities are understood as unacceptable and a focused plan of support is in place to eliminate significant achievement gaps in order to realize academic proficiency for all students.  All relevant policies and practices reflect the goals of eliminating achievement disparities and attaining academic proficiency, explicitly and emphatically.  A districtwide focus and effort supports the advancement of high levels of student achievement by ensuring that every school is culturally competent, ensures uniformly high expectations, promotes rigorous curricula, differentiates instruction, and maximizes access for all students to high-level educational opportunities.  The district actively seeks to hire a diverse cadre of educators and administrators, as well as ensure proper emphasis on culturally responsive practices and regularly examines and monitors student achievement, policies, programs, tools and written documents to ensure that achievement gap goals are implemented.  All staff members understand how their respective positions contribute, directly or indirectly, to the implementation of the achievement gap goals.  Staff members understand and actively seek to learn the necessary knowledge and skills to serve diverse students and families.  Staff members assume responsibility for implementing plans that will address achievement disparities.  District and community stakeholders engage students and families, the private sector, faith-based groups, and higher education institutions to support all district students in achieving academic proficiency.

 

LCAP Goal:  All levels of the organization work to improve student achievement and close the achievement gap for all underperforming student groups.

 

LCAP Actions and Services - Year 1:

  1. Schools use multiple assessment measures to gauge student understanding and application of learning.
  2. Schools set and monitor progress goals for all students and student groups.
  3. High schools participate in Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) processes and will develop strategic plans to improve both school climate and student behavior outcomes.
  4. Early learning programs will define, develop, and assure the implementation of a well-articulated pathway from pre-school through Grade 1.
  5. Provide strategic supports at the earliest levels by lowering class size in Grades TK-3; provide targeted intervention and supports for at-risk learners; and, provide professional development for teachers directed at best first teaching, intervention, and support.
  6. Improve outcomes for TK and Kindergarten students with disabilities by providing access to age-appropriate educational opportunities with non-disabled peers.
  7. Counselors and support staff monitor student access to advanced pathways.
  8. Central office staff supports area superintendents, principals, and school staffs to access data and build a culture of data analysis across all schools.
  9. Create and monitor the implementation of appropriate programs and services for students with disabilities.
  10. All middle and high school students have a six- or four-year plan, respectively, that clearly articulates the courses students must complete to meet the 'a-g' requirements, including World Language.
  11. Central office and secondary school staffs work collaboratively to identify and implement best practices that improve outcomes for Grade 9 students at every high school.
  12. Improve the outcome for students with disabilities.  Specifically:  (a) increase the percentage of students who earn high school diplomas, and (b) increase the percentage of students participating in functional curricula, who by the age of 22, transition into the community as contributing members. 

BACKGROUND:  Each month, Superintendent Marten provides a District Accountability Report that includes the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting Year 1 LCAP actions, services, and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's District Accountability Report each month, cluster and advisory groups have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report, and provide input and feedback on current LCAP implementation and suggested next steps.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve a community-based Summer Readers-Future Leaders camp program for 90 second and third grade students at Chollas-Mead Elementary to be held June 22-July 24, 2015, and approve related agreement PS-15-1114-22 with Jacobs Center for Neighborhood Innovation (JCNI) in the amount of $41,150.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The District Advisory Committee for Compensatory Education (DAC) was consulted on the expenditure of Title I funds for this program on May 20, 2015.

 

BACKGROUND:  On March 26, 2013, the board adopted a resolution to partner with the Diamond Educational Excellence Partnership (DEEP) with a focus on improving educational outcomes for children living in a cluster of southeastern San Diego neighborhoods known as the Diamond Community and to offer a pilot Summer Readers-Future Leaders camp program for Chollas-Mead Elementary third grade students to increase reading motivation, reduce summer reading loss, improve social-emotional development and physical health, and engage in experiential inquiry-based educational and service-learning activities linked to the restoration of natural resources. Additional community partners that participated in the program included the UCSD California Reading and Literature Project, Young Audiences of San Diego, Groundwork San Diego, UCSD Science Project, Malcolm X Library, READ/San Diego, and the Jackie Robinson YMCA. Services were coordinated by the Jacobs Center for Neighborhood Innovation (JCNI).  

 

On April 29, 2014, the board approved the district's participation in a second year of the Summer Readers-Future Leaders pilot at Chollas-Mead Elementary. The Jacobs Center for Neighborhood Innovation coordinated services of the partner agencies, including Young Audiences of San Diego, Groundwork San Diego, the Malcolm X Library, READ/San Diego, and the Jackie Robinson YMCA.   The Evaluation Report of the 2014 program, Executive Summary, and comparison of the 2013 and 2014 programs prepared by the Diamond Educational Excellence Partnership (DEEP) are attached. 

 

The proposed 2015 Summer Readers-Future Leaders camp program will continue its five-week, full-day format for 90 rising second and third graders attending Chollas-Mead Elementary School to engage students in learning experiences designed to reduce summer reading loss, fuel motivation to read, enhance physical and social-emotional growth, and increase exposure to inquiry-based science activities.

 

The Jacobs Center for Neighborhood Innovation will provide program coordination with partner agencies including the Jackie Robinson YMCA, Groundwork San Diego, Young Audiences of San Diego, and the California Reading and Literature Project.  The cost of the partner agencies services is $41,150.  The Malcolm X Library is providing $2,000 worth of services (in-kind), READ/SD is providing $10,000 worth of books (in-kind), and JCNI is providing program coordination at no cost ($20,000 value).

 

The Summer Readers-Future Leaders program may be expanded to Johnson and/or Horton elementary schools, contingent upon securing partner funds from the City of San Diego.  Proposed program dates for Johnson and/or Horton are July 27, 2015 through August 28, 2015. A separate action item and any related contract agreement will be brought forward for board approval.

BACKGROUND: Since 2009, San Diego Unified has been committed to community-based school reform under Vision 2020.  In 2012, Governor Jerry Brown introduced a Local Control (and Equity) Funding Formula (LCFF) that included a Local Control (Community) Accountability Plan (LCAP) that changes the accountability system.  Rather than reporting only to the state and federal officials, the LCAP calls for the district to develop a set of priorities and achievement goals in collaboration with the community.  Then we report back to the community on the results of the district plan each year.  The LCAP is a living document, which is revised each year.

 

San Diego Unified held several Vision 2020 forums to initiate a community input process ahead of the LCAP.  Since then, the district has engaged a diverse group of stakeholders, including representatives from advisory groups, clusters, and professional staff.

 

The state has given flexibility to rely on local input and we are also doing this across the district with the diverse communities.

 

Over the past six years, we have developed vibrant cluster councils across the 16 clusters of the district (feeding into 16 comprehensive high schools).

 

MOTION: That each cluster elect one official representative (and possibly one alternate) to the LCAP planning committee each year at the first cluster meeting. This person would serve in addition to the members already selected that represent the diverse group of stakeholders from advisory committees, community groups, and professional staff.  This person would be elected by each cluster no later than September of each year.  This official appointment from each cluster would serve the following purposes:

 

1) To ensure that each cluster's point of view and priorities are officially represented on the LCAP planning committee

2) To allow an opportunity for each cluster to be informed on an ongoing basis throughout the year as to the plans of the committee and the subsequent results (accountability)

3) To ensure consistent attendance at all of the LCAP planning meetings throughout the year by the same individuals

4) To allow the collective voice of the clusters to be heard in the forum in which they can have the greatest impact on improving educational outcomes in San Diego Unified, through the LCAP.

 

The above will solidify the board's commitment in San Diego Unified to community-based reform.  In addition to the LCAP process, each cluster is also able to advocate to their elected board trustee on specific policy issues.
