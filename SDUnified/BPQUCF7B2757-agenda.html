BACKGROUND: The California Government Code and Education Code recognize the complexities in the day-to-day operation of school districts and authorize boards of education to delegate authority and responsibility to the superintendent and staff members to perform the many routine functions of a school district. Annually, the Board of Education delegates authority and assigns administrative responsibility to staff members by adopting a series of resolutions before July 1, specifying the responsibility and authority authorized by the California Government and Education Codes.

 

These resolutions cover such items as signing contracts, acceptance and recording of deeds, certification of board meeting actions and minutes, rejection of claims against the district, employee mileage allowances, execution of personnel action forms, travel allowances within the amount budgeted by the board, execution of loyalty oaths, financial report documents, etc. The resolutions delegating authority are attached.  Changes made to the resolutions include names and titles to reflect organizational structure and position title changes and increased financial settlement authority amounts delegated to staff, made effective for the 2020-21 year.

BACKGROUND:  At an election held on November 6, 2012, the district sought authorization under Proposition Z to issue general obligation bonds in an amount of up to $2.8 billion. Proposition Z was approved by more than 55 percent of the district's eligible voters voting on the proposition. Proceeds from the sale of bonds are to be used by the district for the specific school facilities projects set forth in Proposition Z. In addition, the district board adopted a resolution on September 4, 2012, which stipulates that any bonds issued under the Proposition Z authorization would not be issued as Capital Appreciation Bonds (CABs).

 

A Memorandum of Understanding between the District and County of San Diego, dated June 1, 2019, was executed with respect to establishing an annual reserve for bonds issued under Proposition Z for the purpose of avoiding a fluctuating tax levy in accordance with Section 15250 of the Education Code. BACKGROUND: The Board of Education has annually adopted a resolution for issuance of TRANs for the upcoming fiscal year, most recently at the June 25, 2019 meeting for the 2019-20 fiscal year. Budget string 5528-00006-00-5840-9100-0000-01000-0000.

 

The district receives a substantial portion of its annual income from property taxes, which are traditionally paid by taxpayers on or before December 10 or April 10. As a result, the district must borrow funds each year in advance of these dates to pay operational expenses in the July to December timeframe. Thus, funds are borrowed at the beginning of the fiscal year using TRANs notes and repaid within an eight to twelve month period. Short-term borrowing using TRANs is necessary to ensure the district meets its financial obligations on a cash basis.

 

On January 22, 2019, the Board approved a pool of firms to provide financial underwriting services to support the sale of Propositions S, Z and Measure YY bonds and TRANs issuances. The district has annually utilized this approved pool of firms from which to select the Underwriters that will purchase and sell the district's annual TRANs. On April 1, 2020, the district solicited proposals from the firms in the approved pool of providers for the 2020-21 TRANs issuance. The proposals were reviewed by the district’s financial advisor, KNN Public Finance, LLC, as well as members of the district’s Finance Division. Upon completion of the review of the proposals submitted, UBS Financial Services and Bank of America were elected to provide underwriting services for the 2020-21 TRANs and will be written into the Resolution.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Proposition 30, the Schools and Local Public Safety Protection Act of 2012, approved by the voters on November 6, 2012, temporarily increases the state's sales tax rate for all taxpayers and the personal income tax rates for upper-income taxpayers.

 

The new revenues generated from Proposition 30 are deposited into a newly-created state account called the Education Protection Account (EPA). School districts, county offices of education, and charter schools (Local Education Agencies or LEAs) will receive funds from the EPA based on their proportionate share of the statewide revenue limit amount, which includes charter school general purpose funding.

 

A corresponding reduction was made to an LEA's revenue limit or charter school general purpose state aid equal to the amount of their EPA entitlement. For 2012-13, LEAs received EPA payments at the end of June 2013. Beginning with the 2013-14 fiscal year, LEAs received these EPA payments on a quarterly basis which commenced in September 2013.

 

Proposition 30 requires all districts, counties, and charter schools to report on their websites an accounting of how much money was received from the EPA, how that money was spent, and that the board acknowledge that the EPA funds will not be spent on administrators or other administrative costs. Proposition 30 also requires that the use of EPA funds be determined by the governing board at an open public meeting prior to any expenditures being recorded for 2019-20.

This is a partnership Agreement between RCHSD and the district to support the FACES Program with students in the Health Science and Medical Technology Pathway studies at Hoover, Crawford and Kearny high schools. The FACES internship program introduces underrepresented high school students to the health professions, assists them to get into educational programs of their choice, and equips them with the necessary personal skills to succeed in these and other demanding pursuits. The programs will continue to bring rigorous and meaningful academic and career technical experiences to district high school students.

 

Instructors are already aligned for the full cohort of students in introductory, intermediate and advanced level courses for the health pathways that support this program. Career technical education (CTE) instructors are scheduled to provide internship activities as part of the established advanced level CTE course of study. As part of the delivery of the CTE program, schools will provide counseling support for FACES for the Future students and teachers for core content. Schools will designate the FACES Program Manager or his or her designee, as its Academic Coordinator of Clinical Education (ACCE) to work with an employee of RCHSD designated by RCHSD as its Coordinator of Clinical Education (CCE) to coordinate the academic and clinical aspects of each student's education.
