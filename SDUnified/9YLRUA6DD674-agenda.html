2. Proposed Additions to Board Governance Policies, First Reading (Evans/Barrera)
Meeting
Feb 23, 2016 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Information, First Reading

Board Trustees John Lee Evans and Richard Barrera will discuss Board Governance Policies, specifically the Governance Culture (GC) and Board/Superintendent Relationship (B/SR) policies.  Trustees Evans and Barrera will propose additions to the Governance Culture policy statements.  The board discussion today will be a first reading, and action on the item will be agendized for the board's regular meeting on March 8, 2016.

 

Board Governance Policy B/SR-1, Single Point of Direction, remains unchanged and in effect as follows: “The superintendent is the board's sole point of direction to the operational organization.  The board will set the direction for the operational organization only through the superintendent, functioning as the Chief Executive Officer.  However, nothing herein shall prohibit a constructive, two-way dialogue with students, staff, parents, and the community as a means to engage all stakeholders in the work of the board and the district.”

 

The suggested revision to Board Governance Policy GC-7, Board Members' Code of Conduct, includes language to address a future circumstance where a new member may have a student currently attending a district school.  The revision to GC-8, Board Member Conflict of Interest, creates a new requirement for all board members to participate in a training session on conflict of interest laws and ethics whenever a new board member is seated.

 

More expansive revisions are suggested for GC-9, Process for Addressing Board Member Violations.  The revisions would create additional responsibilities for the superintendent in the event that a board member attempts to direct district staff on an operational matter or displays unprofessional behavior to a staff member.

 Violation:

 

A board member attempts to direct staff on operational matter or displays unprofessional behavior to a staff member

 

 Action:

  1. Superintendent will discuss the problem with the board member and the staff member(s) and will issue a memorandum to all board members reporting the issue.
     
  2. If the matter is not satisfactorily resolved or if the offending behavior recurs, the superintendent will place an item on an open board meeting agenda for discussion by the full board with the superintendent.
     
  3. The superintendent shall establish and maintain a procedure for employees to report the above violations in a safe, non-retaliatory manner, so that she/he may report alleged violations to the full board.

CONFLICT OF INTEREST LAWS AND ETHICS

 

Within 60 days of seating a new member on the Board of Education, the full board will participate in a training session, to be presented by experts, on conflict of interest laws and ethics.

 

DUAL ROLE AS TRUSTEE AND PARENT OF STUDENT(S)

 

Within 60 days of seating a member on the Board of Education, who also has a child who attends a San Diego Unified School District school, the superintendent and staff of the school will meet with the member to address the manner in which all parties will discuss the means to ensure that the role as a parent and the role as a board member are clearly distinguished

RECOMMENDATION: Authorize staff to continue negotiations with Magnolia Educational and Research Foundation, doing business as Magnolia Public Schools, for partial use of ground at De Anza School Site, located at 6575 Estrella Avenue, 92120; continue environmental review process; and develop one or more future agreements to implement the proposed project.  Proposed term of a future agreement is five years.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The district will receive income of $20,000/month, which is $240,000/year. Total revenue to district over the five-year term of the agreement is $1,200,000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Magnolia Educational and Research Foundation, doing business as Magnolia Public Schools (Magnolia), has operated on the former Cleveland Elementary site (Cleveland property) under a Multiyear Facilities Use Permit Agreement (MFUPA) approved by the board on September 9, 2014.

 

The Cleveland property was identified for sale as part of the short-term real estate utilization strategy, requiring the relocation of Magnolia from the Cleveland property. On April 14, 2015, the board approved a Termination Notice for Magnolia’s occupancy of the Cleveland property, effective June 30, 2016, and approved a First Amendment to MFUPA to clarify the dates of the modified agreement, pending the property sale.  On June 2, 2015, escrow closed on the sale of the Cleveland property.

 

Since March 2015, district staff has been working diligently and collaboratively with Magnolia to transition its occupancy to a new location. The proposed Term Sheet (Term Sheet) for the partial use of De Anza School site, located at 6525 Estrella Avenue, San Diego, CA 92120, is a direct result of ongoing good faith efforts by the district, and collaboration with Magnolia.

 

The Term Sheet demonstrates the cooperation between district and Magnolia in the future use of the De Anza site.  It serves as a good faith agreement for the preparation of a definitive agreement and future actions between the district and Magnolia for development of the proposed project, subject to all environmental considerations. The Term Sheet includes review under California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) as well as the securing of appropriate governmental permits, approvals, and entitlements for the proposed project.

 

Under the Term Sheet, the district will deliver the ground, consisting of approximately 3.57 acres (which is 155,470 square feet) in good condition.  Magnolia, at its sole expense, will be responsible for demolition of existing portables, the cost of moving the Child Development Center (CDC) inventory currently located on the De Anza site to another location within the district (with costs to be shared by the district), and the cost and construction of its own improvements to the premises related to the installation and furnishing of 16 portables approved by the Division of State Architect and in compliance with applicable requirements for an educational occupancy, testing, inspection, and associated costs.  

 

As consideration, the Term Sheet states that Magnolia will pay the district $20,000/per month, which is $240,000/per year, over the five-year term, contingent upon Magnolia’s “in good standing” status with the district, Magnolia’s operation of a public charter school in the district’s geographical boundaries consistent with its charter petition approved by the district, and for no other purpose.

 

If the Term Sheet is approved by the board, staff will develop one or more definitive agreements, which may include a License Agreement or a Ground Lease Agreement, that sets forth the terms and conditions upon which Magnolia may occupy and use the De Anza site consistent with the Term Sheet.  Any future agreement remains subject to approval in the board's sole and absolute discretion. 

 

Staff has met with the Foster Elementary School Principal and communicated that Magnolia will be occupying the lower De Anza site adjacent to Foster Elementary School. However, this will not be a co-location and Magnolia will not be sharing any services or playground space.  Staff will continue to work with Foster Elementary School regarding any operational and safety issues that may arise from Magnolia occupancy at the De Anza site.

 

Staff has met with Neighborhood House Association (Neighborhood House), who currently occupies a portion of the De Anza site on a use permit that expires June 30, 2016.  District staff has informed Neighborhood House that the permit will not be renewed, pending approval of this Agreement.  Neighborhood House is currently looking for a site in Tierrasanta to relocate as most of its students live in that area.  Staff will continue to work collaborative with Neighborhood House to ensure a smooth transition.
