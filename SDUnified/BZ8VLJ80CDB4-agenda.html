RECOMMENDATION: As a service-providing organization, the San Diego Unified School District's employees are its most valuable resource. Today's recognition includes recognition of employees with 40, 30, 20, 15, 10 and 5 years of service. When the district returns to in-person work, each employee will receive a certificate and pin. The Superintendent and the Board of Education extend their most sincere gratitude to these individuals. 

 

Today, we celebrate milestones of 54 employees.

 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: As San Diego Unified celebrates progress towards meeting the inspirational goals contained in Vision 2021, we are taking time to celebrate those employees who have helped us achieve this progress. The number one predictor of student success is the presence of an experienced, high quality teacher in the classroom, according to the Learning Policy Institute. (Source: “Report: Teacher qualifications the best predictor of student success,” By Amelia Harper, May 16, 2019, Education Dive). That study, which was written last year and co-authored by the Chair of the State Board of Education, found high quality teachers have helped San Diego schools do better than the rest of the state. Given the clear association between the health and success of our students and the well-being and effectiveness of our workforce, we publicly recognize employees for their continuous years of service to the students, families and communities of San Diego Unified.

BACKGROUND: On April 14, 2020, the board approved $600,000.00 to be allocated for this effort under the Instructional Materials/Lottery Funds; Budget String: 5458-63000-00-4101-1000-1110-01000-6172.  San Diego Unified District provides instructional materials for district high school students enrolled in community college courses with SDCCD.

 

On July 28, 2020, the board approved an Educational Partnership Agreement (SV21-0290-92) on behalf of Scripps Ranch High School, Canyon Hills (formerly Serra) High School, Mira Mesa High School, University City High School, and Twain-Mesa High School between San Diego Miramar College to Participate in College-Level Courses Taught on the College Campus.

 

On July 28, 2020, the board approved a Memorandum of Understanding (SV21-0276-92), with San Diego Community College District that is used as a Master Agreement for programs in which SDUSD high school students participate in classes on SDCCD campuses, and by which all individual educational partnerships are authorized. This Memorandum of Understanding is incorporated as Exhibit A into this Agreement.

 

This new Educational Partnership Agreement between Miramar College and select district high schools: Scripps Ranch, Canyon Hills (formerly Serra), Mira Mesa, and Mark Twain-Mesa campuses, will provide up to 40 high academically prepared junior and senior year students from the high schools the opportunity to experience high-quality teaching and learning in select college-level courses (i.e., Business 115, Political Science 101, 102). Completion of these courses will enhance the academic preparation of potential incoming Miramar College students and assist with transferring to four-year colleges/universities. This new Agreement accounts for new in-person and online/virtual classes and removes University City High School. 

 

Education Code Section 76004 authorizes community college and K-12 districts to enter into College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) partnerships with the goal of developing pathways from high school to community college for career technical or preparation for transfer, improving high school graduation rates, or helping high school students achieve college and career readiness. Through dual enrollment partnerships, school districts and community college districts can create clear pathways of aligned, sequenced coursework that allow students to more easily and successfully transition to for-credit, college-level coursework leading to an associate degree, transfer to the University of California (UC) or California State University (CSU) systems, or to a program leading to a career technical education certificate.

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: This Agreement will allow the district to continue responsible up-cycling of retired district technology assets, providing logistical efficiencies in the disposal of such equipment, and providing computers to low-income students and families who otherwise cannot afford them. Computers 2 SD Kids (C2SDK) accepts all working and non-working electronics. C2SDK either refurbishes the technology and donates it to local families in need or submits the hardware elements for recycling.

 

In addition, this Agreement will allow the district to serve as the proxy for Computer 2 SD Kids to received State of California Department of General Services retired I.T. equipment to also be refurbished and re-distributed to low-income students and families in San Diego who otherwise cannot afford them.


C2SDK handles all logistics necessary for the pickup and sanitation of the retired assets including:
1. Picking up the assets directly from locations where assets are retired, or from a central location specifically assigned by the district.
2. If necessary, providing a pallet to stage assets until pickup is requested.
3. Securely transporting and storing assets until they can be sanitized.
4. Removing all district licensed software and license key stickers (if applicable).
5. Sanitizing all data storage media pursuant to Department of Defense 5220.22-M data destruction methods.
 
When assets are picked up from the district by C2SDK, a receipt of assets that contains a list of all assets with serial numbers will be provided to the district. 
 
Once data destruction is completed on data storage media, C2SDK provides a receipt of sanitation to the district.

 

According to C2SDK, official San Diego County estimates suggest that 41.8 percent of public school students are from low-income families, 28 percent of San Diego families do not have a computer in their home, and nearly 60 percent of low-income households do not have a working computer in the home. Children of low-income families rely primarily on their school computers and have no technological access at home. 

 

This Agreement is beneficial because it continues to allow the district to narrow the technology gap for low-income students and families and efficiently recycle/dispose of surplus equipment that has exceeded its useful life for the district.
