 BACKGROUND:

  1. Board approval is recommended to transfer excess insurance funds into General Fund to help address the projected deficit.  Recent actuarial studies have demonstrated that funds in certain self-insurance accounts are in excess to the required and safe reserve levels.

  2. Board approval is recommended to approve the use of flexible Tier III resources.  These reductions will be taken out of supplemental funds at the schools and central office programs.  All basic school site allocations will be sustained.  Reductions include a 10 percent reduction in magnet funds, a reduction that will be allocated by magnet and academic staff with input from school sites.  The following programs will be excluded from the reductions: Off Campus Integrated Learning Experience (OCILE), International Baccalaureate (IB), Gifted and Talented Education (GATE) certificated staff.  Please see the attached Magnet Summary spreadsheet for specifics regarding this reduction.

  3. Board approval is recommended to use Title I resources for specified programs.  Staff has contacted subject area and legal experts to assure that the use of Title 1 funds for the recommended reductions are legal and appropriate.  Consultation with the District Advisory Committee (DAC) for Compensatory Education on the use of these funds is planned for the June 16, 2010, DAC meeting.  Please see attached documents for supporting information.

  4. Board approval is recommended to reduce the special education budget by $1 million dollars in summer school/inter-session costs, $3.2 million dollars from the elimination of vacant/unused positions and a $600 thousand dollar reduction in supplies.  The summer school/intersession budget may be reduced by $1 million dollars while maintaining the integrity of the program.

  5. Board approval is recommended to transfer funds for instructional materials from unrestricted to restricted resources.  Adult Education fund ending balance will be transferred to General Fund Unrestricted.

  6. Board approval is recommended to reduce the budget of the summer school program due to smaller anticipated attendance.  The program itself will be kept intact, with the addition of one or two schools as summer school sites.

  7. This will be a $5 million reduction to non-personnel resources within the central office.

RECOMMENDATION: Board discussion and First Reading regarding adoption of the resolutions in the matter of the elimination of vacant classified positions, effective end of day June 30, 2010.


FISCAL IMPACT: The Finance Division has calculated an estimated savings of $ 3.7 million for salary and benefits.


IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: These resolutions eliminate vacant positions that were identified by school sites and departments. The positions are as follows:

BACKGROUND: Resolutions in the matter of elimination of vacant classified positions: Office-Technical and Business Services (OTBS) Bargaining Unit; Operational Support Services (OSS) Bargaining Unit; Paraeducators (PARA) Bargaining Unit; School Police Services (POA) Bargaining Unit; Administrators Association of San Diego Classified Supervisors (SUPER) and Management (MGRS)Bargaining Unit; and Confidentials (CONF) are the result of budget reductions throughout the district.

 

Like other school districts and public agencies in the state, the district is faced with addressing reductions in financial resources and increasing costs. It follows that schools and central offices are impacted by dwindling revenue streams and have to make budget and staffing adjustments as necessary. It is extremely difficult for schools and central offices to make the necessary hard decisions concerning eliminating vacant positions. If the board does not approve these eliminations, approximately $3.7 million will need to be reinstated and these vacancies will be filled by employees that were affected on the April 27, 2010 resolution to eliminate filled positions.

5. A.5 Proposal to Rescind Notices Sent to Probationary Certificated Employees (Resolution in the Matter of the Reduction of Certificated Staff Due to Reduction or Elimination of Particular Kinds of Services) (Evans)

RECOMMENDATION:  Board Member Evans will move to rescind the notices to 112 certificated probationary employees. 


FISCAL IMPACT: $5.8 million


PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:


IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Teachers that receive rescission of certificated layoff notices will return to their 2009-10 position unless their position is reduced or eliminated for the upcoming school year due to a change in allocations, and/or program changes. If there is no allocation for the teacher, they will be added to the excess list of teachers needing an assignment for the 2010 school year. In the event a vacant budgeted position is not identified by the start of school, these teachers will be temporarily placed to assist at schools sites until a vacancy becomes available.


BACKGROUND:  On June 1, 2010 Board Member Evans proposed that the board rescind the notices to 112 certificated probationary employees.  The motion did not pass, with members Evans and Barrera voting yes, Nakamura voting no and requesting additional information, and member de Beck abstained from the vote.  The board requested that the item be re-agendized for further information and proposed action at the special board meeting on Thursday, June 3, 2010.
