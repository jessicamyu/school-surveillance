BACKGROUND: The first Earth Day on April 22, 1970, activated 20 million Americans from all walks of life and is widely credited with launching the modern environmental movement.

 

Growing out of the first Earth Day, Earth Day Network (EDN) works with over 22,000 partners in 192 countries to broaden, diversify, and mobilize the environmental movement.

 

In partnership with the U.S. Green Building Council and The Clinton Foundation, EDN’s Green Schools Campaign aims to green all of America’s K-12 schools within a generation. Green schools save money, conserve energy and water, and foster better-performing, healthier students.

 

EDN’s Educators' Network, used by over 30,000 teachers and administrators nationwide, provides innovative tools and resources to promote civic participation and to develop a sense of environmental responsibility among students of all ages.

 

Together, EDN’s Education and Policy Teams organize federal and state legislative campaigns to green school facilities, improve school food, and enhance environmental education and civic engagement.  These include the Healthy Schools Act and No Child Left Inside.

 

In 2013, San Diego Unified formed the Environmental Sustainability Advisory Committee (ESAC) which developed many recommendations to conserve energy and water, green schools, and preserve the environment.

 

San Diego Unified has implemented many of the ESAC recommendations and has established Garden to Café Programs at many schools; over half of district schools have native and vegetable gardens and Green Teams focusing on recycling and conservation projects at their sites; over 200 district teachers have aquaponic systems in their classrooms, teaching students how to grow leafy greens in a sustainable way, to name just a few.

 

San Diego Unified’s California Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS) Early Implementers Team will take a stand for the community, health, economy, and San Diego’s future by participating in the March for Science on Saturday, April 22, 2017. The 27th Annual Earth Fair will be celebrated in San Diego Balboa Park on Sunday, April 23, 2017.

RENEWAL VERSUS REVISION

 

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.

Within a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, reassignment of special education responsibilities, expansion of grade levels, or other significant change.

BACKGROUND: San Diego Unified School District and CSEA/OSS have met and negotiated in good faith and agreed on an MOU for a Supplemental Retirement Program for 2016-17. The SERP will only be available to CSEA/OSS bargaining unit members in accordance with the terms contained in the attached MOU.

 

In order to be eligible for the SERP, CSEA/OSS members must meet all six criteria as set forth in the MOU, summarized below:

 

(1) Must be a CSEA/OSS Unit Member;

(2) Must be a permanent classified employee of the district as of February 21, 2017;

(3) Must have five years of district service as a permanent classified employee as of June 30, 2017, for traditional school or central office employees, or as of July 21, 2017, for year-round employees;

(4) Must be eligible to retire under CalPERS or CalSTRS as of June 30, 2017, for traditional school or central office employees; or as of July 21, 2017, for year-round school employees;

(5) Must submit all required SERP enrollment materials on or before May 5, 2017; and

(6) Must have resigned from district employment on the last workday for the 2016-17 school year, effective on or before June 30, 2017, for traditional school or central office employees, or on or before July 21, 2017, for year-round employees.

 

The district and CSEA/OSS agree that the SERP must have sufficient plan participation in order to meet the district's fiscal and operational objectives by the enrollment deadline of May 5, 2017, in order for the SERP to go into effect for CSEA/OSS. These objectives include that the combination of: a) the SERP, and b) the Collective Bargaining Agreement changes contained in the SERP Memorandum of Understanding between the parties will generate net savings, or no net cost, to the district's General Fund-Unrestricted, cumulatively over five years. Further, the district reserves the right to determine which positions vacated by retirement shall be filled.  

 

CSEA/OSS must present the attached MOU to its membership for ratification.  Due to the condensed timelines for OSS members to decide to participate in the SERP, the parties will be working now to get the information out to eligible CSEA/OSS employees, and the Board will approve this MOU subject to written notification from CSEA/OSS of ratification by union membership.

BACKGROUND: Proposition 39 (Education Code section 47614) requires the district to offer reasonably equivalent facilities to charter schools that show that they are eligible to receive such facilities.  At its February 7, 2017 meeting, the board approved district staff recommendations of the preliminary offers of facilities to eligible charter schools that made appropriate applications for facilities under the provisions of California Education Code section 47614 and California Code of Regulations, Title 5, sections 11969 et seq.  Education Code section 47614 and the California Code of Regulations, Title 5 sections 11969 et seq. (Regulations), require 1) that districts must submit a final offer of facilities to eligible charter schools on or before April 3, and 2) that the charter school must respond to the final offer by May 1, if they intend to receive district facilities for the upcoming year.

 

Subsequently, staff analyzed each of the requests in conformity with the requirements of Education Code section 47614 and the implementing regulations and developed recommendations for the final offers of facilities for eligible charter schools.  Staff is submitting this report and its recommendations for board approval and requests for the board to adopt the report as its statement of reasons.

 

The allocation of district facilities to charter schools pursuant to Proposition 39 constitutes a project for purposes of the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), Public Resources Code section 21000 et seq.  However, the project is exempt from further environmental analysis under the General Rule clause and two categorical exemption classes as set forth below and as further described in the attached Notice of Exemption (NOE):

 

15061(b)(3). GENERAL RULE

 

The activity is covered by the general rule that CEQA applies only to projects which have the potential for causing a significant effect on the environment. Where it can be seen with certainty that there is no possibility that the activity in question may have a significant effect on the environment, the activity is not subject to CEQA.

 

The proposed project would not result in any physical changes to the affected school sites. The continued co-occupancy of the charter school(s) and district-operated school would not exceed the capacity of that school site.  Therefore, no physical changes are required and it can be seen with certainty that there is no possibility that the continued co-location may have a significant effect on the environment.

 

15314. MINOR ADDITIONS TO SCHOOLS

 

Class 14 consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or ten classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption.

 

The project would not add new classrooms or other facilities on the existing campuses. Additionally, the co-occupancy of the charter school and district-operated school would not exceed the existing capacity of that school site.

 

15322. EDUCATIONAL OR TRAINING PROGRAMS INVOLVING NO PHYSICAL CHANGES

 

Class 22 consists of the adoption, alteration, or termination of educational or training programs, which involve no physical alteration in the area affected or which involve physical changes only in the interior of existing school or training structures. 

 

The proposed activity would result in either the continued operation of two education programs on an existing school site or the introduction of a new charter school program at an existing district-operating school. The combined programs on the campuses would not require any physical changes to any of the sites.

 

Categorical exemptions may be not be relied upon if the application of the exemption triggers any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2.  Staff has reviewed the sites on which the charter schools will be located and determined that no exception applies to defeat application of the categorical exemptions.  Approval of this item authorizes and directs staff to file a Notice of Exemption in substantial conformance with the Notice of Exemption Attachment with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk.

Environmental Review: The proposed project is a project under CEQA, Public Resources Code (PRC) section 21000 et. seq., and its implementing guidelines (California Code of Regulations, Title 14, Division 6, Chapter 3, sections 15000 et. seq., as amended). The proposed project, however, is exempt from expanded environmental review under the following Categorical Exemption Classes:

 

Existing Facilities (Class 1) - Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of use beyond that existing at the time of the lead agency‘s determination. The key consideration is whether the project involves negligible or no expansion of an existing use (CEQA Guidelines §15301).

 

The proposed project would result in the repair of existing facilities. While there would be an expansion of the number of HVAC units, the use of HVAC is consistent with other buildings on-site. The improvements would not change the existing school uses and operations. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 categorical exemption is appropriate for the proposed project.

 

Accessory Structures (Class 11) - Class 11 consists of construction, or placement of minor structures accessory to existing commercial, industrial, or institutional facilities, including but not limited to on-premise signs (CEQA Guidelines §15311[a]).

 

The proposed project involves the replacement of the existing roof and HVAC units with a new roof and new HVAC units. The new HVAC units are accessory to the existing use of the site as a school and do not expand use of the school site. Therefore, the application of a Class 11 categorical exemption is appropriate for the proposed project.

 

Minor Additions to Schools (Class 14) - Class 14 consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or ten classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption (CEQA Guidelines §15314).

 

The proposed project would not result in the addition of new classrooms or other facilities that would increase the number of classrooms or seating capacity. Therefore, the application of a Class 14 categorical exemption is appropriate for the proposed project.

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not-to-exceed amount:  $9,000,000.00.  Budget string:  Various Site Identified Funds.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: For the period July 1, 2015 through June 30, 2016: $3,290,902.00.   

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: This authorization will provide the district with a competitive solicitation to purchase next-day office products and supplies for three years through the utilization of the E&I Cooperative Services, Inc. agreement with Staples Contract & Commercial.

 

On October 21, 2014, E&I Cooperative Services, Inc., along with the California State University System, released a competitive solicitation for office products, supplies, and solutions. E&I received responses from Office Max/Depot, Staples Contract & Commercial, and Independent Stationers. After evaluation of all proposals, a ten-year contract award was made to Staples Contract & Commercial.

 

On July 8, 2014, the district’s board of education received the final report from the Environmental Sustainability Advisory Committee.  Included in this report as a "Dream Big" idea was a recommended action to join or create larger purchasing cooperatives.  A purchasing cooperative establishes various contracts which aggregate the total purchases from many government entities resulting in lower costs to governmental entities who then utilize these contracts.
