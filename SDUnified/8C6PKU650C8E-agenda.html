RECOMMENDATION:  Review and approve Interpretations and Indicators for Sections 2.3.4 (Social Studies) and 2.1 (High Expectations); review work to date on implementation of the overarching goal.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  A workgroup consisting of approximately 30 people has been working since spring 2010 on developing the GSA-2 Interpretations and Indicators. This group consists of teachers, administrators, area superintendents, instructional support services staff and community members.  Meetings are generally held after school hours to ensure that classroom staff and community members have the opportunity to participate.  Substitutes are provided for any meetings/workshops held during school hours.  A roster of the workgroup members and schedule of remaining meetings for the 2010-11 school year is included as Attachment 8.

 

BACKGROUND:  In fall 2009, under the guidance of the Aspen Group, a team comprised of central office leaders was created to address the Interpretations of the board's academic goals and develop Indicators that show the district's progress in meeting the goals.  In spring 2010, as a result of board feedback on the proposed Interpretations and Indicators, a new workgroup was created that began building upon and expanding the work of the original team.

 

The new workgroup identified four components in the Overarching Goal:  (1) how to encourage and measure critical thinking; (2) how to help students reach their full academic potential; (3) how to measure one year of academic growth; and (4) development of social skills for success in academics and the workplace.  Interpretations and Indicators of the Overarching Goal were presented to the board and approved in summer 2010.  The board then directed the workgroup to develop Interpretations and Indicators for English/Language Arts (Section 2.3).  A subgroup met during fall 2010 to develop the English/Language Arts Interpretations and Indicators, which were presented to the board and approved in November 2010.  Two subgroups worked during winter 2010-11 on the Interpretations and Indicators for Social Studies (Section 2.3.4) and High Expectations (2.1), which are being presented to the board for review and approval today.

4. Career Ready and College Preparatory (CRCP) Task Force Report and Recommendations; BUDGETED: NO; (GSA-2, OE-2)

Feb 22, 2011 - Special Meeting, 9:00 A.M.

Information

RECOMMENDATION:  The Career Ready and College Preparatory (CRCP) Task Force recommends the following Action Steps in relation to the board's June 2009 resolution to provide UC 'a-g' access for all students.  Recommendations 1-14 are in alignment with the recommendations provided by the Education Trust West (ETW) in their September 2010 Educational Opportunity Audit Report.  Recommendations 15 and 16 were developed by the CRCP Task Force.  Details of each Action Step and a Yearly Progression (timeline) are included as Attachments 10, 11, 13 and 14. 

 

Recommendation 1:  Reform the Master Schedule Process 

Recommendation 2:  Examine Grading Policy

Recommendation 3:  Review Use of "D" Grade Across the District

Recommendation 4:  Open Access to AP and IB Courses

Recommendation 5:  Evaluate and Implement Professional Development throughout the District, including determining needed professional development based on district initiatives and successful practices to date, increasing K-12 AVID strategies districtwide, and creating intervention programs at transition grades.

Recommendation 6:  Examine use of Parent Connect and School Websites, including establishing information sessions for parents about career and college requirements, and providing consistent school website designs with common components and formats. 

Recommendation 7:  Coordinate (align) Elementary, Middle, and High School Curriculum

Recommendation 8:  Create Districtwide Bridge Programs

Recommendation 9:  Examine Ways to Meet the Needs of Special Populations

Recommendation 10:  Develop a College-Going Culture

Recommendation 11:  Evaluate Intervention and Support Programs, including level of successful interventions and supports, modifying as needed to implement best practices, exploring and establishing foundational districtwide grading policies, and developing a communication system of support.

Recommendation 12:  Evaluate Current Staffing

Recommendation 13:  Create a Districtwide Course Catalog Format

Recommendation 14:  Prioritize Resource Allocation for Implementation, including projected numbers of students needing supports and interventions, and providing two science laboratory rooms at Twain High School.

Recommendation 15:  Determine Policies for Waivers, Exemptions, "Testing Out" and Specific Courses

Recommendation 16:  Determine Waivers/Modifications for Diploma Requirements that may Involve Developing Individual Graduation Plans for some Students.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Preliminary estimates range from cost-neutral/no fiscal impact to $16 million, depending upon board direction for implementation.  The CRCP Task Force recommends that implementation begin with the Class of 2015 or later.  To ensure high quality, successful implementation, and ease financial impact on the district, the CRCP Task Force recommends extending implementation over a longer period of time.  By beginning with cost-neutral action steps and maintaining current funding levels needed to support the initiative, larger cost items may be implemented over multiple future years when budget projections may be more optimistic.  A detailed Budget Summary and Implications report is included as Attachment 12.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  The 'a-g' initiative has districtwide impact on school counselors, teachers, principals, and central office departments (e.g., Academics, Instructional Support, Student Services, Information Technology, Human Resources, Accountability, Facilities, etc.).  

 

BACKGROUND:  In June 2009, the board passed a resolution directing the superintendent to develop an implementation plan for UC 'a-g' for all students, which included the direction to establish a steering committee to review district graduation requirements and make recommendations for changes and/or modifications, and report its recommendations to the board and superintendent. In September 2009, the board approved an agreement with the Education Trust West (ETW) to conduct an Educational Opportunity Audit to provide the district with data needed to make recommendations to implement 'a-g' for all students.  The audit included analysis of a random sampling of student transcripts and other artifacts from the class of 2009, conducting focus groups at five high schools with students, teachers, and school counselors; and conducting a community conversation about college and career readiness with parents and community members.  In February 2010, ETW made a presentation to the board members on its work to date and provided an overview of the audit process.  Staff provided the board with updates on the progress of the audit via memoranda in December 2009, April 2010, June 2010, and July 2010.  ETW presented its audit findings to the board, Superintendent's Cabinet, Career Ready and College Preparatory (CRCP) Task Force and the general public in September 2010.  The CRCP Task Force met in September, October, November, and December 2010, and January 2011, to review the audit findings, and develop proposed action steps and timelines for implementation of the ETW recommendations.  Staff provided the board with updates on the progress of the Task Force via memoranda in November and December 2010, and held a meeting in January 2011, to update new board members on the 'a-g' initiative.  All documentation related to the 'a-g' initiative and the CRCP Task Force is available on the district website at the following link: http://www.sandi.net/20451072011455933/blank/browse.asp?a=383&BMDRN=2000&BCOB=0&c=73809&20451072011455933Nav=|6720|&NodeID=6720 or under Departments/Instructional Support Services.
