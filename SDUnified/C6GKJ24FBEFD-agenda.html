FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: Assembly Bill No. 361 (“AB 361”) was enacted by the California Legislature and is intended to “improve and enhance public access to . . . local agency meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic and future applicable emergencies, by allowing broader access through teleconferencing options.” AB 361 permits local legislative bodies subject to the Brown Act and/or the Badgley Keene Act to hold virtual meetings, without needing to publish the physical locations of Board members or make those locations open to the public, if there is a proclaimed state of emergency (pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act) and either: (i) state or local officials have imposed or recommended measures to promote social distancing, or (ii) there is a finding by the local legislative body that, as a result of the emergency, meeting in person “would present imminent risks to the health or safety of attendees.” Governor has proclaimed a state of emergency pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act. Since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, in compliance with public health orders and for the protection all involved persons, the Board of Education (“Board”) has held its meetings telephonically via Zoom and provided for public comments to be provided via teleconferencing rather than allowing in-person attendance by the public at the Education Center. The COVID-19 pandemic continues to present an imminent health risk to the public which require certain specific health and safety measures be maintained. The District’s central office facilities present specific challenges in maintaining COVID-19-related safety protocols required for the health and safety of attendees, which include, but are not limited to, adequate ventilation and security, enforcement of masking requirements, and maintenance of recommended social distancing. The health and safety risks are presented by providing public access at the remote locations from which Board members currently attend Board meetings remotely. The Board hereby finds that conducting Board and/or committee meetings in person in light of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic presents an imminent risk to the health and safety of prospective attendees.

BACKGROUND: This easement (Attachment 2) is being requested and is necessary for San Diego Gas and Electric to upgrade the existing overhead circuit located along the rear of Clay Elementary School.  Upgrades involve replacing crossarms on the existing pole and replacing segments of the overhead wires.

 

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: Approval of the subject resolution of intent to grant easement and right-of-way to San Diego Gas and Electric Company for Electric Distribution Facilities upgrades adjacent to Clay Elementary (Proposed Project) is subject to environmental review under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), however, it is exempt from further environmental review consistent with CEQA guidelines section 15301 - Existing Facilities (Class 1). Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features. Existing facilities of both investor and publicly-owned utilities used to provide electric power, natural gas, sewerage, or other public utility services; (CEQA Guidelines §15301[b]). The Proposed Project involves the approval of an easement on District Property to allow San Diego Gas and Electric to provide electrical distribution facilities upgrades adjacent to Clay Elementary School.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2). Section 15300.2 of the State CEQA Guidelines specifies several exceptions to the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time, is significant. 
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. 
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified Environmental Impact Report. 
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. 
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

 

The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the proposed project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. All adjacent on-site and off-site areas are either paved, developed, disturbed, or comprised of non-native landscaping.  Therefore, none of the project sites are considered environmentally sensitive and this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. The proposed construction and/or maintenance activities associated with the implementation of the proposed easement are minor in scope, individually limited, and consistent with the maintenance of existing utility infrastructure. Consequently, the associated environmental effects would not be significant. Combined, the implementation of the Proposed Project and any other unknown projects that may occur on-or off- site, would not be significant, and impacts would not be cumulatively considerable. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. The Proposed Project is not considered an unusual circumstance as this type of easement and maintenance activity is common on District properties on within developed areas served by public utilities. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project would not result in the demolition of existing structures or the construction of new facilities that would directly or indirectly affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project. 
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the Department of Toxic Substance Control, no district properties are included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. Because the Proposed Project does not require the demolition of existing historical era buildings or structures, the significance of a potentially historical resource will not be materially impaired, and no substantial adverse changes to the significance of a historical resource or significant impacts to the environment at the project site is anticipated. Additionally, the likelihood of impacting either prehistoric or historic archeological resources is negligible for the current undertaking due because grading and trenching into native soils is not proposed. This exception does not apply to the project.

 

Approval of this item approves the Proposed Project as described, authorizes and directs staff to file the attached Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk, and approves Resolution of Intent to Grant Easement and Right-of-Way for to San Diego Gas and Electric Company on Portion of District-Owned Real Property at Clay Elementary.
