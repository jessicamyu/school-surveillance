Subject
B.01. K-2 Class Size Reduction and Cohort Study
Meeting
Jul 22, 2008 - Board Retreat
Type
Information
The K-2 Class Size Reduction (CSR) and Cohort Study will assess the impact on achievement of reducing class size to an average ratio of 15:1 and/or keeping cohorts of randomly assigned students together as they progress from kindergarten through Grade 2. The study will be conducted in 60 schools starting in September 2008 and continue through the 2010-11 school year.

Sixty schools with the highest percentages of students eligible for free or reduced-price meals and with required classroom space available to accommodate CSR at 15:1 were selected for participation. In order to achieve valid research results, the schools were randomly assigned to one of four treatment groups: 1) Control-does not have 15:1 class size reduction and does not keep cohort together (KCT); 2) CSR at an average of 15:1 but does not keep cohort together; 3) KCT- does keep cohort together but does not have 15:1 CSR; and 4) CSR & KCT-does have 15:1 class size reduction and does keep cohort together.

Additional information about the study is contained in the following
documents:
1. K-2 CSR/KCT Study Summary
2. K-2 CSR/KCT Study List of Schools
3. K-2 CSR/KCT Study Design
