The rates for CDE-certified nonpublic schools and agencies are negotiated by the San Diego County Office of Education (COE) and the Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA) representatives.  In 1977, all school districts and county offices of education were required to form geographical regions (SELPAs) of sufficient size and scope to provide for all of the special education service needs of children residing within the region boundaries.  Each SELPA region developed a local plan describing how it would provide special education services.  The six SELPAs include special education directors who represent 42 San Diego County school districts consisting of the San Diego Unified School District, Poway Unified School District, North Coastal Consortium (14 districts), North Inland (10 districts), East County (10 districts), and South County (6 districts).  Each SELPA must have an Administrative Unit (AU)--an entity that receives funds and is responsible for ensuring that every eligible child receives appropriate services.  The COE is the AU for North Coastal, North Inland, East County and South County SELPAs; San Diego Unified and Poway Unified are the AUs for their districts.

 

In addition to rate negotiation, a "master contract" is developed and approved by the SELPAs for use by each school district. The master contract is then distributed to the 42 school districts for approval by their respective boards.  Because the SELPA group often finalizes its master contract after the fiscal year-end, and because the CDE certifies its nonpublics for a calendar year (January through December) rather than a fiscal or school year (July through June), the district submits master contracts for board approval twice yearly—once within the first quarter of the fiscal year for services provided from July through December; and once within the first quarter of the calendar year for services provided from January through June after expiration of the previous year's CDE certification documents. Due to timing issues, these contracts are submitted as ratifications.

 

Nonpublic school placements and nonpublic agency educational support services are dictated by each student's IEP, due process or mediation agreement. In addition, students may be placed in an NPS by County Mental Health or other County agencies; or nonpublic students move into district boundaries requiring the district to provide special education student services which includes NPS placement or NPA services if mandated by the student's IEP.  Therefore, student enrollment count can change or new nonpublic schools and agencies may be identified at any time during the year requiring ratification of additional NPS/NPA agreements.

 

On February 23, 2010, the board approved master contracts for nonpublic schools and agencies to provide educational and related services to district students with special needs for the term January 1, 2010 through June 30, 2010. In July 2010, the 2010-11 master contract was distributed to all school districts by the COE with direction for each school board to approve individual agreements with each nonpublic school and agency. On August 3, 2010, NPS and NPA master contracts were approved by the board for the term July 1, 2010 through December 31, 2010.

 

However, shortly after August 3, 2010, a dispute arose between the nonpublics and the SELPAs concerning master contract language, terms, conditions, and costs; and the nonpublics refused to sign their respective agreements. Therefore, with no fully-executed agreements in place, the terms, conditions, and costs of previous agreements (January 2010 through June 30, 2010) then governed the provision of services to those students receiving NPS/NPA services. Accordingly, the district has continued to make payments under these agreements to the nonpublics.

 

The SELPAs then began continuous negotiation with the nonpublics. Ultimately, the SDCOE Superintendent requested intervention by the Division of Administrative Hearings in order to facilitate finalizing of an agreement with the nonpublics.

 

At this time, the hearing officer still has not rendered a decision. Therefore, in accordance with the January 2010 through June 2010 agreements, the district is still obligated to pay for services for 355 students receiving educational and related services from NPS providers and 248 students receiving educational support services from NPA providers. When the hearing officer renders a decision, and when the terms, conditions, and costs are agreed upon with NPS and NPA providers for the terms July 2010 through December 2010 and also January 2011 through June 2011, then the finalized agreements will be submitted for board approval.

 

Approval of this item authorizes payment for NPS placements and NPA services to eligible students based upon the terms and conditions of the last fully executed agreements.
