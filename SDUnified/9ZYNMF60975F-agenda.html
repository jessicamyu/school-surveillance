3. Re-Contextualizing Magnet Sites within Goals of Vision 2020, Looking Ahead to 2016 Magnet School Assistance Program (MSAP) Grant
Meeting
Oct 6, 2015 - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Information

RECOMMENDATION: Information only. On April 24, 2015, there was a board workshop on the “Logistics and Implementation of Quality Schools in Every Neighborhood”.  During the workshop eleven concepts to action were presented as strategies to increase neighborhood student enrollment.  The third concept to action presented was to review the district’s Choice Programs. The purpose for this board workshop is to review the role of the district’s Magnet Program as it relates to federal law, and the role magnets are able to play as a critical support for Vision 2020. 

 

Conversations regarding magnet sites generally focus on compliance.  Often missing is discussion of the academic and social benefits of diverse learning environments.  This workshop will open with conversation about the power of developing learners who value diversity, and learn to draw from diverse perspectives as a critical toll for college and career success.  This conversation frames the potential of magnet schools to support the alignment of academic programs within clusters (concept to action 1) as well as increase student enrollment.

 

An overview of the district’s history with magnet schools will be provided.  Alignment and revision of current magnet sites to support processes to strengthen clusters under Vision 2020 will be proposed.  A motion for approval to compete for the 2016 Magnet School Assistance Program grant will be placed on the December 8, 2015, board meeting agenda, allowing time for collaborative conversation with proposed site’s Area Superintendent, school, and community leadership.
