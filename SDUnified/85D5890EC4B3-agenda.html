RECOMMENDATION: Approve the extension of schools’ current (2009-10) Single Plans for Student Achievement (SPSAs) until updated plans are submitted in December 2010.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: The district adopted the new Site Based Budgeting (SBB) online tool for the 2010-11 school year with the intention to incorporate the budget development, school site plan, and staffing development for the fiscal year into a single online resource.  Due to unexpected difficulties in configuring the school site plan template into the SBB application, principals were not able to include their Single Plans for Student Achievement (SPSAs).

 

Staff in the Finance Division has been working with Proxient, the company contracted to provide the SBB application, to resolve the issues of adding the school site plans to the SBB tool. All issues are expected to be resolved to permit full implementation for the 2011-12 school year.

 

2010-11 Single Plan for Student Achievement

 

Principals received notice by email on April 16, 2010 that the SPSA online version would not be available and were instructed to follow the SPSA planning process (below) in developing a budget aligned with their goals for student achievement. 

 

  1. Conduct a needs assessment.
  2. Use student achievement data to determine site goals and student group targets.
  3. Categorical funds must be allowable expenditures aligned with goals based on school’s needs.
  4. Complete all required goals — ELA, Math, Graduation Rate, and Parent Involvement in the SPSA.
  5. Schools that receive ARRA stimulus funds for class size reduction and 10-to-Succeed  leadership programs must include these programs in the SPSA.
  6. Involvement of the SSC in the process and approval of the expenditures.

 

Principals were asked to review student summary data with their School Site Councils (SSCs) and decide on the strategies and activities to use for the upcoming school year, and then develop the 2010-11 budget and staffing based on those decisions. The SPSA template was provided for sites to use in the development of the budget with the expectation that it would be completed in the fall. 

 

An administrative circular, to be posted in September, will provide principals and SSCs with instructions for completing the SPSA template and the due date.  The  SPSAs will be submitted for board approval at the December meeting.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the revision to District Policy K-6500, Advertising Sponsorships Policy and direct the Chief District Relations Officer to proceed with an RFP to select a vendor to assist the district in implementing a district advertising sponsorship program to generate revenue from district websites and school sites consistent with the provisions of the new policy.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  No negative fiscal impact.  Possible positive impact from advertising revenue from district websites and site advertising.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.  The project will use existing staff.

 

BACKGROUND: As the district continues to face significant budget deficits, the Board of Education asked that a detailed policy be developed that would provide guidelines for the generation of revenue from advertising sponsorships.  During the last six months, the Chief District Relations Officer has researched this topic and has developed a draft Advertising Sponsorships Policy for board consideration.  This policy was drafted using several other policies in use in other school districts around the nation as a starting point.  The Chief District Relations Officer also sought input on the policy draft from Area Superintendents and Principals and believes that the draft before the board for consideration can serve as an effective guide to implement a program that can generate significant revenue for the district in the current and subsequent fiscal years.

 

Respecting the board's Governance Policy OE-3 which states that  "The superintendent shall establish and maintain a learning environment that is safe, disciplined, respectful and conducive to effective learning."  This draft policy specifically prohibits any advertising that is not consistent with the mission of the San Diego Unified School District (Section III b. and c.).  The policy also ensures that the district retains all approval rights for any ads placed on district property or web sites and delegates approval responsibility to the Superintendent and the Chief District Relations Officer.  Under the policy, advertising would only be permitted on middle school and high school sites and on district managed web sites.  The implementation of this policy and the selection of any locations for advertising sponsorships would also respect OE-9 which states that:  "The superintendent shall assure that physical facilities, consistent with the board’s vision, support the accomplishment of the board’s Goals for Student Achievement policies, are safe and properly maintained, and serve the broader needs of the communities in which they exist.

 

Following the approval of this policy, the district would issue an RFP to identify a vendor who could work with the district to implement a revenue generating sponsorship program that would include both web advertising and school site signage.  This service would be provided at no cost to the district through a revenue sharing agreement with the vendor (s). To date, five companies have approached the district to express interest in providing this service.  The RFP would be issued in September with the goal of presenting an agreement to the board for approval in November.  This timeline would allow the program to begin in early 2011 to generate revenues for the district in the current fiscal year.  The Chief District Relations Officer anticipates that the first advertising under this policy would be on the district website.  It is estimated that the district website, which has significant traffic of 1.4 million visitors each month, could generate more than $100,000 in annual advertising revenues for the district.

 

Other provisions of the draft policy includes the formation of a committee to work with the Superintendent and the Chief District Relations Officer to identify acceptable locations for school site advertising with input from school principals and others.  The policy also states that revenues from school site advertising generated through this program will be shared with 80 percent of site advertising revenues returned to school site budgets.

 

In addition to the draft policy for San Diego Unified, several other similar policies from other school districts are attached as background information.

FISCAL IMPACT:  Under this agreement and through its 20-year term, the district will purchase approximately $33 million of solar generated electricity from Main Street Power Company in lieu of purchasing the electricity from San Diego Gas and Electric Company (SDG&E).

 

The projected savings is estimated to be $2 million to $6 million over the term of the 20-year agreement.  Projected savings are based upon a flat rate for the generated electricity of $0.175 per kilowatt-hour for 21 sites or 1.9 megawatts, and $0.165 per kilowatt-hour for 15 sites or 2.5 megawatts, both will be adjusted at 4 percent annually.  Our current electricity provider, SDG&E, is estimated to increase rates by 3-4 percent annually for the cost of electricity during the term of this agreement.  Currently we pay SDG&E an average of $0.19 per kilowatt-hour districtwide. Budget string is 5537 00013 00 5502 8100 0000 01000 0000.

BACKGROUND:  Government Code section 4217.10 et seq. allows a school district to enter into an energy management agreement on terms that are determined by its governing board to be in its best interest.  Six companies responded to the districts Request for Proposals (RFP).  A team consisting of representation from the districts Energy, Legal, Strategic Sourcing, and Finance departments, and an outside third party solar expert was formed to evaluate submitted proposals.
