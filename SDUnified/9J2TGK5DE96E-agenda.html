Regarding the educational program, the petition for Ingenuity Charter School describes a nonclassroom-based, independent study program.  The petition states that Ingenuity Charter School will “endeavor to place students wishing to return to a traditional school setting back to their neighborhood school whether it is a traditional or charter school.”  The instructional program will focus on serving the following:

  • Students who desire or are in need of a standardized, flexible, and personalized learning approach to education.
  • Students of all ability levels, including but not limited to special education (SPED), Gifted and Talented (GATE), and English Language Learners (ELL).
  • Students who wish to combine career and technical training with their core academic subjects.
  • Students who are in need of an alternative approach to the traditional school setting.
  • Students who are in need of another academic setting due to disciplinary actions such as suspension or expulsion.
  • Students who are wards of the court to include Foster Youth.
  • Students who are academically at-risk of dropping out and/or recovered dropouts.
  • Students who are pregnant and/or parenting.
  • Students who have been considered habitually truant.
  • Students who were retained more than once in kindergarten through grade eight. 

With respect to facilities, it is proposed that Ingenuity Charter School will operate on the O’Farrell Charter School campus, a district-owned site, at 6130 Skyline Drive, San Diego, 92114.

 

If approved, both Ingenuity Charter School and O’Farrell Charter School would be governed by the same nonprofit public benefit corporation, which is known as “The O’Farrell Charter Schools.” 

 

Staff is reviewing the attached Ingenuity Charter School documents, including financial information, and will bring back this agenda item to the board at a future date for action to grant or deny the petition.

Academics

A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  • Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school.
  • Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
  • Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
  • The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.
  • Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(h).

SDGVA Elementary is eligible for renewal based on their 2013 statewide rank of 8 in the prior year.  The elementary school’s most recent (2012-13) API was 869, an increase of 46 points from the prior year.  The school has consistently exceeded the statewide performance target of 800. 

 

SDGVA Middle School is eligible for renewal based on their statewide rank of 7 in the prior year.  The middle school’s most recent (2012-13) API was 848.  The school began operating in fall 2012, and their one year of API data exceeded the statewide performance target of 800.

Governance and Operations

 

SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle are governed by a five-member board that meets in the evenings.  The charter governing board has ranged from two to three parent members. 

 

Based on the co-location with Adams Elementary, the school safety plans for SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle include district information. 

 

Uniform complaint procedures were adopted by the charter governing board on February 21, 2013, and are regularly reviewed for updates as needed.

 

Financials

 

The budgets, locations, grade levels, programs offered, and the enrollment projection information submitted by SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle have been evaluated and appear to be within normal range compared to other charter schools and district schools.  The estimated California Department of Education (CDE) funding rates and Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) factor, provided by School Services of California’s Financial Projection Dartboard, have been used to estimate revenue for SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle based on their respective projected Average Daily Attendance (ADA).  These revenue projections have been compared to the projected revenue included in SDGVA Elementary’s and SDGVA Middle’s respective budgets and no significant variances have been found.  Other expenses unique to charter schools have also been examined and found to be comprehensive and appropriate.  Over the past few years, SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle have addressed all required annual financial audit and reporting to the state and the district.  The schools’ actual expenses for every year of operation in the current term were all less than budgeted amounts.  Based on the projected enrollment, revised projected budget, expenses, and cash flow submitted in the renewal charter documents, SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle appear financially sound.  In addition, staff has examined whether SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle maintain an adequate Reserve for Economic Uncertainty greater than 3 percent of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater.  At the end of 2013-14, per SDGVA Elementary’s and SDGVA Middle’s unaudited reports, the ending fund balances were $892,910.00 and $374,714.00, respectively, which exceeded the reserve requirements. 

 

Conclusion

 

The renewal documents submitted by SDGVA Elementary and SDGVA Middle are attached.  Staff recommends approval of the renewal of the charters of San Diego Global Vision Academy Elementary Charter School, San Diego Global Vision Academy Middle Charter School, and special education agreements for five-year terms beginning July 1, 2015, and ending June 30, 2020.

RECOMMENDATION: Authorize the Chief Human Resources or his/her designee to file applications for H-1B visas for up to two visiting teachers from China, so that they may be employed by the district to teach at Barnard Asian Pacific Language Academy.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Not to exceed $5,000, inclusive of legal fees in the amount of $750 per application and filing fees of $1875.00.  Budget string: 0017-09800-00-5853-7200-0000-01000-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:Two additional employees for a period of three years.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Two teachers were recruited by the principal at Barnard to come and teach at the district in the Barnard Mandarin Chinese language immersion program.  In order for them to be legally employed by the district, the district must file applications with the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) for H-1B visas.  An H-1B visa is a non-immigrant visa which allows a U.S. employer to temporarily employ foreign workers in specialty occupations.  The application must be filed by the sponsoring employer and must be accompanied by a labor certification from the California Department of Labor.  In filing the visa application, the district must commit to maintaining the employer-employee relationship for the duration of the term of the visa, and becomes obligated to pay for the employee's return travel to China, if the district decides to sever the relationship.

 

Immigration law is a specialized field and it will be necessary to retain outside counsel to handle the applications.  We have located a local attorney who has agreed to handle the work at a discounted rate of $750 per application.  The site has identified funds to cover the legal and filing fees.

 

This is a one-time request.
