RECOMMENDATION: Staff recommends the board non-renew the renewal charter petition for Nubia Leadership Academy Charter School (Nubia).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Nubia opened in 1997, and currently serves 223 students in Grades K-6.  Nubia is located on a private facility at 6134 Benson Ave., San Diego, California 92114.

Academic Performance Summary: Prior to being granted a renewal, a charter school must meet at least one of the following criteria, as established in the Education Code:

  1. Attain its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years, or in the aggregate for the prior three years.
  2. Attain a statewide rank of 4 or higher in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
  3. Attain a similar school rank of 4 or higher in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
  4. The academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the district schools in which the charter school is located.
  5. Has qualified for an alternative accountability system.

 

Nubia did not meet the academic performance criteria required for renewal.

 

In 2010-11, Nubia did not meet its API growth target with a 24-point decline in its API from a Base API of 695 to a Growth API of 671.

 

Additionally, in the previous year (2009-10), Nubia did not meet its API growth target with a 25 point decline in its API from a Base API of 720 to a Growth API of 695. 

A more detailed description of the school’s academic performance is included in the attached board report.

 

Financial Analysis: Based on the Nubia budget, location, grade levels, and programs offered, the enrollment projections have been evaluated and appear to be within normal range compared to other charter schools and district schools.  The estimated California Department of Education (CDE) funding rates and Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) factor, provided by School Services of California’s Financial Projection Dartboard, have been used to estimate revenue for Nubia based on the projected Average Daily Attendance (ADA).  This revenue projection has been compared to the projected revenue included in the charter school petition budget and no significant variances have been found.  Other expenses unique to charter schools have also been examined and found to be comprehensive and appropriate.  Over the last four years, the petitioners have addressed all required annual financial audit and reporting to the state and the district.  

 

Based on the projected enrollment, projected budget, expenses, and cash flow submitted in the renewal petition, Nubia has been found to be financially viable.  In addition, as another consideration of the school’s financial viability, the district has examined whether Nubia maintains an adequate Reserve for Economic Uncertainty that is 3 percent of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater.  According to the financial documentation provided in its financial audit, Nubia has maintained an adequate reserve for economic uncertainty.

 

In summary, the school is financially sound.  At the end of 2010-11, the school’s ending fund balance of $535,915, exceeded the reserve requirement of 3 percent of expenses.
