RECOMMENDATION: Approve revision of the charter of Urban Discovery Academy to add Grades 9-12, effective July 1, 2016.  Accept Urban Discovery Academy request to withdraw agenda item. See attached letter from Urban Discovery.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: Staff recommends the board approve the request to revise the charter of Urban Discovery Academy to serve Grades 9-12, beginning in the fall of 2016. As part of the proposed revision, effective July 1, 2016, Urban Discovery Academy will transition to becoming independently reporting for California Longitudinal Pupil Achievement Data System (CALPADS) and will acquire and implement an independent student information system to perform operational functions, including but not limited to attendance, transcripts, master schedules, reclassification, and special education. Urban Discovery Academy will continue to regularly report student enrollment data in the method requested by the district.

 

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing and (ii) action to grant/deny.

 

During a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, reassignment of special education responsibilities, expansion of grade levels, or other significant change to the program.

 

Urban Discovery Academy is a charter school that is operated by a nonprofit public benefit corporation by the same name. Urban Discovery Academy operates at a non-district site at 840 14th Street, San Diego, 92101, which is located in the San Diego High cluster. The current charter term ends on June 30, 2018.

As reflected above, over a four-year period UDA intends to add one grade level per school year. To distinguish itself from the K-8 program, the high school will be called “IDEATE High Academy,” but it will be governed by a single charter governing board. 

 

The educational program for IDEATE High Academy includes:

  • Opportunities for applied projects and internship experiences.
  • Project-based learning in a small high school environment.
  • A focus on partnerships with business and universities, starting with the UC Extension Program at UCSD.
  • A core approach based on the five elements of Design Thinking: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test.

Urban Discovery Academy has identified a non-district site at 525 Hawthorn Street (92101), which is in the San Diego High cluster, as the location for IDEATE High Academy. Urban Discovery Academy has informed the district that Balboa City School is a private school that is currently occupying the site under a month-to-month lease and Balboa City School will vacate the site at the end of the 2015-16 school year.

 

District staff has visited Urban Discovery Academy annually. The annual site visit includes document review, classroom observations, and interviews of charter board member(s), teacher(s), administrator(s), financial manager(s), parent(s), and/or student(s). During the annual site visit, as well as throughout the year, the areas of academics, finances, and operations are monitored as part of the district’s oversight duties. At this time, Urban Discovery Academy presents a sound academic and operational program. 

 

Urban Discovery Academy has met or exceeded the minimum financial reserve requirement per its fiscal year 2014-15 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report and First Interim Financial Report. Urban Discovery Academy submitted financial reports by the required deadlines and appears fiscally sound at this time.

 

Based on the documents submitted and reviewed, staff recommends the board approve the request to revise the charter of Urban Discovery Academy to serve Grades 9-12, effective July 1, 2016, to begin serving students in the fall of 2016.
