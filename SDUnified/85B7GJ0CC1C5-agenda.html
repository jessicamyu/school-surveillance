C. STUDENT INSTRUCTIONAL MATTERS
Subject
C.02. Adoption of the Amended Charter Petition for Promise Charter School (Board Governance Policy: OE-10)
Meeting
Oct 6, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
No
RECOMMENDATION: Adopt amendments to Charter Petition for Promise Charter School (PCS) with material changes, effective upon Board of Education adoption and expiring with the original term of the charter petition, June 30, 2012.

FISCAL IMPACT: There is no fiscal impact to the district, as the school is currently in operation.

BACKGROUND: Promise Charter School is currently operating a charter petition which was granted by the Board of Education on February 27, 2007. On July 22, 2009, the Promise Charter School petitioners submitted amendments to their charter petition in order to change various components of the present charter. These changes include the school increasing the number of students currently enrolled from 160 to 190 (Element One, Academic Program), inserting greater flexibility into measuring students outcomes (Element Two, Measurable Pupil Outcomes), updating measurement methods to reflect current practice (Element Three, Methods to Measure Pupil Outcomes), making modifications to the description of staff at the school (Element Five, Employee Qualifications), changing the number of board members to align with the schools’ bylaws and instituting an English Language Learners Advisory Council (Element Four, Governance), and modifying the charter to indicate that the school may follow the district salary schedule (Element Thirteen, Employee Rights). PCS currently does not follow the district salary schedule.

The charter term is not impacted by these amendments. The amendments shall become effective upon board adoption and will expire with the original term of the charter petition, June 30, 2012. The PCS petitioners submitted their amendments to the district for review and consideration of approval in accordance with Education Code requirements. Staff has reviewed the amendments and found them to meet academic, legal, fiscal, and operational requirements. Staff recommends the adoption of this report as findings in support of the board’s decision to adopt the amendments to the PCS charter petition.

Subject
C.03. Multiyear Agreements for Charter Schools Housed on District Property (Board Governance Policy: OE-13)
Meeting
Oct 6, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
No
Budgeted
No

RECOMMENDATIONS: The superintendent recommends that the Board of Education approve one of the following two options:

OPTION 1:

1. Adopt the criteria, as listed below, to determine eligibility of charter schools beginning with the 2010-11 multiyear agreements, as opposed to making an annual application for facilities space under Proposition 39. This action would exclude Albert Einstein Academy Charter Middle School, Gompers Preparatory Academy, and King/Chavez Academy of Excellence from being eligible for a multiyear facilities agreement beginning in 2010-11:

    • Stability Measure: Charter school will have had its charter approved for renewal at least
    • Charter school is one of the “conversion” charters approved by the Board in 2005-06.
    • Fiscal Solvency Measure: Charter school will have received an acceptable, annual performance review by district staff in the last two years of operation, including:

      o A rating of “Yes” in Criterion 5 of the review (Financial Stability).

      o A rating of “Meets Standards” in Criterion 6 of the review (Financial Management).


  • A multiyear agreement will not be offered to a charter school that shares a site with a district-run school.


2. Approve the draft template of the multiyear agreement, as shown in Attachment 4 of the Board report.

OPTION 2:

 

1. Allow all charter schools currently occupying district facilities to be eligible for a multiyear facilities agreement.

 

2. Adopt the criteria, as listed below, to determine eligibility of charter schools beginning with the 2010-11 multiyear agreements, as opposed to making an annual application for facilities space under Proposition 39, and to apply the criteria prospectively to future applicants:

    • Stability Measure: Charter school will have had its charter approved for renewal at least once before being eligible for a multiyear agreement; or
    • Charter school is one of the “conversion” charters approved by the Board in 2005-06.
    • Fiscal Solvency Measure: Charter school will have received an acceptable, annual performance review by district staff in the last two years of operation, including:

      o A rating of “Yes” in Criterion 5 of the review (Financial Stability).

      o A rating of “Meets Standards” in Criterion 6 of the review (Financial Management).


  • A multiyear agreement will not be offered to a charter school that shares a site with a district-run school.


3. Approve the draft template of the multiyear agreement, as shown in Attachment 4 of the Board report.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

BACKGROUND: Following the board’s award of facilities to charter schools under Proposition 39 for the 2009-10 school year, staff was instructed to research whether it was permissible to offer multiyear facilities use agreements to charter schools on district property. The district’s Legal Services Office sent a memorandum to the board and superintendent on March 17, 2009, concluding that it was permissible to offer multiyear agreements to charter schools outside of the state-mandated Proposition 39 regulations. At its April 21, 2009 meeting, the board instructed staff to proceed with developing multiyear agreements for charter schools, including criteria under which the agreements would be granted. One suggestion from the board included a multiyear agreement coinciding in term with the school’s charter. District focus on district budget issues at the end of the 2008-09 school year precluded staff from progressing on this issue until this time.

Criteria under which multiyear agreements would be offered were developed with the input of charter principals. Applied to the current set of charter schools using district facilities, three of the sixteen schools would not be eligible for a multiyear agreement, but can still apply for facilities space under the annual Proposition 39 cycle.

District staff reviewed the results of applying the criteria, and is proposing as a second option that all charter schools currently occupying district facilities be eligible to enter into multiyear facilities agreements with the district, and that the criteria be applied prospectively to future applicants.

C.04. Resolution in the Matter of Evangeline Roberts Institute of Learning Establishing a Separate Fund in the San Diego County Treasury (Board Governance Policy: OE-5)
Meeting
Oct 6, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action (Consent)
Fiscal Impact
No
RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution to establish a separate fund in the San Diego County Treasury for Evangeline Roberts Institute of Learning.

FISCAL IMPACT: None

BACKGROUND: Education Code section 47651(a) provides that a charter school funded pursuant to Education Code 47630 et. seq. may receive the state aid portion of the charter school's total general purpose entitlement and categorical block grant directly, or through the local educational agency that granted its charter.

Evangeline Roberts Institute of Learning was granted a charter by the San Diego Unified School District and has now elected to receive the state funding directly from the state.

After board approval, a separate fund will be established in the San Diego County Treasury for funds received by the San Diego Unified School District for Evangeline Roberts Institute of Learning.
