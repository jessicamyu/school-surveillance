At the October 3, 2017 meeting, board members requested that future board items include additional information regarding demographic data, facilities, what unique or innovative features the charter school adds to the educational system, and/or how the charter school impacts the overall ecosystem of district and charter schools. Clarification 1. Approve SELPA Affiliation on page 64 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

UDA operates as an independent Local Education Agency (LEA) member in the EDCOE SELPA for purposes of special education, pursuant to Education Code Section 47641(a). UDA reserves its right to operate as a public school of SDUSD for purposes of special education, pursuant to Education Code Section 47641(b). A change in LEA status or SELPA membership shall not require a material revision of this charter.

 

Clarification 2. Remove Amendments on page 88 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

Material revisions to the charter must be approved by the SDUSD’s Board of Trustees. However, any proposed revisions to the charter will be presented to the District for a determination as to whether it is a material revision that must be approved by the SDUSD’s Board of Trustees. The District will make its determination and, if required, the SDUSD’s Board of Trustees will consider the revision for approval within a time mutually agreed to.

 

Clarification 3. Approve Financial Audits on page 117 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

Urban discovery Academy will continue to transmit a copy of the audit to the District – as well as to the County Superintendent of Schools, the State controller and the State Department of Education – by December 15 of each year. Any Audit disputes with the District will be remedied using the Dispute Resolution process describe in the charter. Audits over the last five years have been implemented within legal timelines, meeting procedural and implementation requirements. In addition, UDA complies with ongoing public financial disclosure obligations as required by the bond issues used to fund its East Village K-8 facility. UDA timely posts annual, quarterly and other financial and operational information as documentation of its continuing compliance with bond covenants, including is disclosure obligations under Securities and Exchange Commission Rule 15c-2.

The Director or designee has the authority to determine whether or not to impose a suspension or recommend an expulsion. Clarification 6. Approve Dispute Procedures on pages 129 and 130 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

Urban Discovery Academy and the District will negotiate in good faith on an annual basis to develop a Memorandum of Understanding (“MOU”) separate from this charter, one that establishes more specifically the financial and service relationship.

 

In the event that the District believes that the dispute relates to an issue that could lead to issuance of a notice of violation supporting revocation of the charter, UDA requests that this shall be specifically noted in the written dispute statement the District provides the School, although it recognizes is cannot legally bind the District to do so. However, participation in the dispute resolution procedures outlined in this section shall not be interpreted to impede or act as a prerequisite to the District’s ability to proceed with revocation in accordance with Education Code Section 47607. Within 30 business days, or longer if both parties agree, or at the earliest mutually convenient date, of sending written correspondence, an Academy representative and a District representative shall meet and confer in an attempt to resolve the dispute. In the event that this informal meeting fails to resolve the dispute, both parties shall identify two representatives from their respective organizations who shall jointly meet with the Superintendent and the CEO of the Charter School, or their respective designees, at the earliest mutually convenient date and attempt to resolve the dispute.

 

If this joint meeting fails to resolve the dispute, an Academy representative and a District representative shall meet again within 10 business days or longer if both parties agree at the earliest mutually convenient date to jointly identify a neutral, third-party mediator to assist in dispute resolution. The format of the third-party aspect of the dispute resolution process mediation shall be developed jointly by the representatives and shall incorporate informal rules of evidence and procedure unless both parties agree otherwise. The finding or recommendation of any arbiter shall be non-binding, unless the governing board of the Academy and the District jointly agree to bind themselves. Unless jointly agreed, the process involving the assistance of a third-party participant shall conclude within 30 business days of identifying the mutually agreed to third-party. Mediation shall be held at the earliest mutually convenient date. The Academy and the District shall share all meditation and/or arbitration costs and all other the cost of the mediator associated with dispute resolution equally. The school shall be solely responsible for its attorney’s fees and any other costs or expenses the school incurs arising from a dispute associated with resolving a dispute with the District.

 

In the event that the above process does not result in an agreement over the dispute, both parties agree to continue negotiations in good faith toward a resolution of the dispute. If the matter cannot be mutually resolved, the District may proceed to issue a Notice of violation per 5 Cal. Code Regs section 11968.5.2 under which the charter school shall be given a reasonable period of time to correct the violation, unless the District determines, in writing, that the violation constitutes a severe and imminent threat to the health a safety of the School’s pupil. In such event, the District may proceed in accordance with 5 Cal. Code regs. Section 11968.5.3. In addition, the dispute is not required to be referred to mediation in those cases where the District determines the violation constitute a severe and imminent threat to the health and safety of the School’s pupils. If mediation does not resolve the dispute, either party may pursue any other actions available under California statutes or regulations, and any remedy available under the law.

 

UDA will not refer complaints to the district. The District agrees to refer all complaints regarding operations of Urban Discovery Academy to the Academy’s CEO for resolution in accordance with the Academy’s adopted policies. In the event that the Academy’s adopted policies and processes fail to resolve the dispute, Tthe District agrees not to intervene in the dispute without the consent of the Academy unless the matter has given the District reasonable cause to believe that a violation of this charter or related agreements, or a violation of laws has occurred. directly relates to one of the reasons specified in law for which a charter may be revoked. Notwithstanding the above, the District Board of Trustees shall have the ability to intervene in and respond to complaints about the operation of Urban Discovery Academy as is required by law.

ACADEMICS

 

As the charter granting authority, pursuant to Education Code section 47607(a)(3)(A), the district shall consider increases in student academic achievement for all groups of students served by the charter school as the most important factor in determining whether to grant a charter renewal.

 

A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school.
  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.
  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(g).

Assembly Bill (“AB”) 484 amended Education Code sections 52052(e)(2)(F) and 52052(e)(4) to allow schools that do not have an API calculated in 2013–14 through 2016-17 to use one of the following criteria to meet legislative and/or programmatic requirements:

  1. The most recent API calculation;

  2. An average of the three most recent annual API calculations; or

  3. Alternative measures that show increases in pupil academic achievement for all groups of pupils schoolwide and among significant groups.

    The decision to use one of these criteria may be made on a program by program basis and is a local decision.

FINANCES

 

Urban Discovery Academy (“UDA”) was formed as a California nonprofit public benefit corporation on February 28, 2008. Since 2008, UDA has operated Urban Discovery Academy as a public charter school.

 

Fourteenth Street Holdings LLC (“LLC”) is a California limited liability company that was formed on April 21, 2014, and whose sole member is Urban. The LLC was created for the purpose of acquiring educational facilities for Urban. In October 2014 the California Municipal Finance Authority loaned proceeds from the sale of bonds totaling $10,500,000 to the LLC for the acquisition, construction, and renovation of facilities located at 840 14th Street. In December 2015, an additional $925,000 was loaned to LLC for the project.

 

Urban leases the facilities from the LLC. Annual lease costs in fiscal year 2016-17 totaled $610,054. Urban is eligible for and receives assistance with lease expenditures through the Charter School Facility Grant Program (SB740). Charter schools that meet certain eligibility criteria are awarded the lesser of $1,117 per ADA or 75% of rent and lease costs. In fiscal year 2016-17, Urban was awarded a total of $353,040.00. The net lease costs to the school totaled $257,014.

 

The 2015-16 Independent Auditor’s Report is a consolidated audit comprised of Urban and the LLC. The following table represents UDA’s net assets for the year ended June 30, 2016. While Urban had an increase in net assets of $161,195, the LLC had a decrease in net assets of $481,111. The organization as a whole had a loss of $319,916 in net assets.

 

 

Urban’s 2016-17 unaudited actuals report, submitted to the district on September 15, 2017, shows the school had a decrease in net position of $307,664 and ending net assets of $1,047,585. Financial information for the LLC will not be available until the 2016-17 annual audit report is due to the district on December 15, 2017. If the LLC had a decrease in net position for fiscal year 2016-17, the total net assets of the UDA organization as a whole will be less than $566,879. Staff will continue to monitor the fiscal condition of the organization as a whole.

 

District staff reviewed and analyzed the four-year budget and four-year cash flow submitted by the school, as well as previously submitted financial reports including, the 2015-16 independent audit report, the 2016-17 unaudited actuals report, and the 2017-18 preliminary budget.

 

LCFF revenues presented in the budget are within range of district staff’s calculations. Expenditures are consistent with prior years. UDA is projecting operational surpluses of a minimum of $121,364 per year during the term of this renewal. However, if enrollment projections are not met, the school could face significant operational losses as they did in fiscal year 2016-17. The projected ending net position for each year meets the district’s minimum reserve requirement of the greater of 3% of expenditures or $50,000. As of June 30, 2017, available cash of $726,165 was sufficient to satisfy current liabilities of $688,420.  Over the past five-year term, UDA submitted all required financial reports to the district in a timely manner.

 

The charter renewal documents submitted by Urban are attached.

 

District staff recommends that the board grant the renewal of the charter petition of Urban Discovery Academy, with the noted clarifications, for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2018, and ending June 30, 2023.
