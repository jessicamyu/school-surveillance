RECOMMENDATIONS:

  1. Approve Superintendent’s Recommended Preliminary 2011-12 Budget
  2. Approve Recommended Alternative Budget with Restorations
  3. Direct Human Resources Division to proceed with preparation of staff reduction resolutions - Particular Kinds of Service (PKS)
    – Finalization of staff reduction numbers
    – Board consideration on February 22, 2011
  4. Direct Finance Division to proceed with completion of Second Interim Report for submittal on March 15, 2011, to San Diego County Office of Education (COE)
    – Board consideration on March 8, 2011

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Alternative 1 = Approximately $120.6 million budget reduction based on the Governor’s January 10, 2011 proposed budget; Alternative 2 = Approximately $63.6 million based on passage of a June 2011 ballot measure for K-12 educational funding.

 

BACKGROUND:  The Superintendent has prepared a recommended preliminary budget for 2011-12 for consideration by the Board of Education.  This recommended budget is based on the fiscal assumptions required by the COE and is based on the Governor’s proposed budget released on January 10, 2011.  The recommended budget reflects the school site budgets that were prepared by all schools during the last month with extensive input from their parent and school communities.  The recommended budget also reflects the significant reductions to central office departments which have been updated to reflect most current projected reductions.

 

Two budget alternatives are recommended for approval, the first alternative includes a budget reduction of approximately $120.60 million based on the Governor’s January budget proposal.  A second alternative budget includes a budget reduction of approximately $63.6 million which is based on the estimated impact of the additional funds the district would receive if the voters approve a ballot measure in June that would provide additional funds for K-12 education.  The passage of this ballot measure would allow the restoration of $57 million in programs that are specifically identified in the recommended restoration budget alternative.

 

With board approval of the two alternative budgets, staff will need to operationalize the budget beginning with the preparation of staff reduction notices and the completion of the Second Interim Report for submittal to the COE.
