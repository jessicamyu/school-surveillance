1. Adoption of Charter School Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with KIPP Adelante, Health Sciences High and Middle College, Tubman, Einstein, Arroyo Paseo, Evangeline Roberts, Gompers Preparatory Academy, and Darnall Charter Schools; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-2) (3:00 P.M.)

Oct 4, 2011 - REVISED Regular Charter Meeting, 11:00 A.M. & 3:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the following charter schools: KIPP Adelante, Health Sciences High and Middle College, Tubman, Einstein, Arroyo Paseo, Evangeline Roberts, Gompers Preparatory Academy, and Darnall.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.


BACKGROUND: On December 1, 2009, the Board of Education approved a template Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) setting forth the terms and conditions under which the district and district authorized charter schools will interact with each other regarding Special Education.  In collaboration with charter schools, the Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education MOU template to outline the relationship between the district and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services.  Under the MOU that was collaboratively developed, charter schools will have three options for the delivery of Special Education services at charter schools.  The options are the following:

  1. Charter schools elect to be considered as a public school of the district for the purposes of special education services and funding.  The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the district, and the district will provide special education services.
  2. Charter schools are designated as schools within the district Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA).  With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the district, and the district will reimburse the charter school for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the district for equivalent service providers.
  3. Charter schools are designated as schools of another SELPA.  This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified.  Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served.  The district retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.

Staff recommends that the Board of Education approve these Special Education MOU agreements, with the understanding that each individually signed Special Education MOU will return for board approval when individual charters are renewed.

BACKGROUND: O’Farrell currently operates a charter school serving approximately 900 students in grades 6-9.  In the next few years, O’Farrell plans to expand its education program to serve Grades K-12.  The school is located on a district facility at 6310 Skyline Drive, San Diego, California 92114.

 

The district granted a charter to O’Farrell in 1994 to operate a middle school and recently renewed it in 2009.  During the 2010-11 school year, O’Farrell added Grade 9.  O’Farrell has provided a summary of its ninth grade test scores which is attached as an exhibit. 

 

O’Farrell is currently in Year 5 of Program Improvement.  O’Farrell has developed a Single School District (SSD) Plan which is a multi-year plan that includes hiring a math consultant for professional development, implementing intervention programs in the form of individualized tutoring, and a summer school program for English Learner (EL) students.  The O’Farrell SSD Plan is attached as an exhibit.

BACKGROUND: Public hearing pursuant to Education Code section 47605 to consider the level of district teacher, other district employee, and parent/guardian support for the charter petition submitted by the San Diego Global Vision Academy (SDGVA) Middle School. 

 

SDGVA Middle plans to enroll students from the existing SDGVA Elementary.  Both schools would be operated by the same nonprofit, public benefit corporation and will have the same governing board.  Two of the seats of the governing board will be parent representatives who are democratically elected.

 

SDGVA Middle proposes to operate at the 3430 School Street, formerly the Adams Annex, which is where SDGVA Elementary is currently located.  SDGVA Middle proposes to serve Grades 6-8 as summarized in the table below.
