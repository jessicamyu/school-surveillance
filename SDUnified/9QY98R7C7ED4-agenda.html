RECOMMENDATION: Staff will present the Phase 1 Sustainability Implementation Feasiblity Study.  Direct staff to develop Phase 2 detailed sustainability implementation plans including analysis of costs and benefits for each of the 13 big idea areas for board consideration and action as they are developed.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Potential future savings $1.3 million per year starting in 18 months increasing over time.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The board established the Environmental Sustainability Advisory Committee (ESAC) to consider opportunities for improving environmental sustainability. On July 8, 2014, the Committee presented a report to the board encompassing 13 "Dream Big" ideas, including:

  1. Develop a San Diego Unified Climate Action Plan
  2. Complete a San Diego Unified Community Choice Aggregation Feasibility Study
  3. Go Off-Grid with Solar
  4. Adopt Net Zero Energy
  5. Flip the Switch to LED Lighting
  6. Maximize Water Conservation
  7. Buy Local! Source Produce and Other Food Locally
  8. Adopt Zero Waste
  9. Establish or Join Large Purchasing Cooperatives
  10. Rescue Uneaten Whole Fruit and Unused Packaged Foods
  11. Support School Gardens to Grow Produce for Food Services
  12. Embed Sustainability into the Fabric of Leadership and Learning
  13. Improve Transportation Sustainability

On July 8, 2014, the board requested staff develop a preliminary feasibility study.  The Phase 1 Sustainability Implementation Study titled "Dream Big Solutions for a Sustainable Future" examines these ideas on the following premises:

  • San Diego Unified Sustainability Achievements
  • Committee and Additional Recommendations
  • Prioritization in accordance with guiding principles:
    • ​Potential to Reduce the District's Environmental Footprint
    • Opportunities for Student Leadership and Learning
    • Potential for Revenue Generation, Cost Containment, or Reduction
    • Cost to Implement (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
  • Implementation Plan
  • Stakeholder Involvement Process
  • Timeline for Development and Execution
  • Dashboards and Metrics Required

The Phase 1 Sustainability implementation feasibility study titled "Dream Big Solutions for a Sustainable Future" shall provide the foundation for Phase 2 Sustainability Action Plans in each of the 13 big idea areas.  Staff will bring individual detailed sustainability solution implementation plans including analysis of costs and benefits for each of the 13 big idea areas to the board for consideration and action as they are developed.

5. Site Safety and Security Review; BUDGETED: N/A
Meeting
Jan 20, 2015 - Special Meeting, 4:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Staff will present the results of the Safety and Security Review and recommends the establishment of a cross-functional implementation team to adjudicate and resolve the recommendations identified through this review.  The team will be charged with overseeing the implementation of the operational and infrastructure recommendations.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Up to $200 million, from various funding sources, predominantly bond and facility maintenance funds.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

  

BACKGROUND: In January 2013, the board directed a review of site safety plans.  In addition, the board asked staff to provide general fund and Capital Improvement Fund recommendations to prevent physical harm being threatened to students, staff, and campus visitors, and to provide protection and response measures from potential natural disasters and other emergencies.  The review consists of three components: operations, small projects resulting from addressing current issues presented by school sites, and major infrastructure improvements.

 

It should be pointed out that students and staff are safe in district schools.  The review is intended to assist us in making school sites safer and more secure by looking at operational and infrastructure improvements.

 

A positive school climate can only exist if students feel safe and secure.  Physical security, through operations and infrastructure, contributes to the students’ safe and secure feeling.  Although not addressed in this report, it is acknowledged that this is only one component, and there are other factors such as a student’s mental, social, and emotional well-being that are also necessary for the student to feel safe.  In a similar vein, one can see how safety and security improvements complement our efforts as we continue to develop as a Restorative Justice school district.

 

As part of the operations review, this presentation provides a vision for the district’s School Police Department, integrating the support provided to schools by Police Officers, Community Service Officers, and Campus Security Assistants into a school cluster focused team.  This provides a more consistent level of community based policing across the district and aligns the support with the needs of the broader cluster as opposed to individual school sites.

 

In addition to the School Police vision mentioned above, the operations review also identified proposed changes in operational practices, training needs and policy/administrative procedure changes and updates.

 

Small projects were generated from safety and security review requests by school sites.  This fast paced review by district staff was intended to identify and remedy smaller scope safety and security issues, ahead of the major infrastructure review.  The small project team responded to more than 70 school sites and identified more than 270 projects to perform.  Examples of these projects include installing a short section of fence, adding exterior lighting or installing a security camera at a critical location.  It should also be noted that during this same time frame (January 2013 to the present) the district’s Physical Plant Operations (PPO) staff completed 4,446 work orders valued at $2.4 million making safety and security repairs, such as repairing an existing fence or resolving a tripping hazard, as part of regular maintenance activities.

 

The districtwide infrastructure review was conducted by an outside consult, AECOM.  AECOM’s multi-discipline team of security and facility experts visited each district school site, reviewing the main infrastructure elements of access control, doors and locks, CCTV, camera systems, and, emergency communications.  The AECOM recommendations are being presented by staff without edits or modifications.  AECOM developed a priority mechanism to describe their findings:

  • Correct—Take action as soon as practical.
  • Budget—Take action as soon as funding is available.
  • Plan—Take action within the normal maintenance or project development cycle.
  • Monitor—Review on an annual basis (no action necessary at this time).

The fiscal impact of implementing the Small Projects and Infrastructure review recommendations is:

 

Safety and Security as a part of on-going maintenance

$2.4 million

Small Projects

$11 million

Total Preliminary Cost Assessment of AECOM recommendations

$180.8 million

  Correct

$97.3 million

  Budget

$82.8 million

  Plan

$0.7 million

  Monitor

$0

 

In addition to the infrastructure costs shown above, there are likely to be additional operational costs that will result from this review.  An example of this would be additional staffing required to monitor (police dispatch) and maintain (PPO) an increased number of security cameras.  Staff believes the total cost of all recommendations is approximately $200 million, realizing this number would be refined as this work continues.

 

In summary, district schools are safe and secure places to learn.  The infrastructure and operational improvements recommended will make our schools safer.  Common themes for infrastructure improvement encountered throughout the district are:

  • Perimeter Fencing and Single Points of Entry
  • Classroom Door Locks
  • Emergency Communications

Staff recommends the establishment of a cross-functional implementation team to adjudicate and resolve the recommendations identified through this review.  The team will be comprised of representatives from Physical Plant Operations (PPO), Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC), Facilities Planning and Design, School Police, and Student Services.  The team will be charged with overseeing the implementation of the operational and infrastructure recommendations.  The team will:

  • Focus on operational issues and the three common themes of perimeter fencing and single point of entry, classroom door locks and emergency communications.
  • Coordinate information gathered from the safety and security review with school sites.
  • Align within the broader district context of Vision 2020, site, cluster, and community needs, and facilities programs (FPC and PPO).
  • Make periodic reporting to the Board of Education, updating information and seeking further input as necessary.

BACKGROUND: In January of every year, the Governor submits the Governor’s Proposed Budget to the Legislature, which is revised in May for the fiscal year beginning July 1. The Proposed Budget Summary includes the Governor’s goals and objectives for the forthcoming year and highlights significant issues, policies and initiatives of the Administration reflected in the Governor’s Budget, including K thru 12 education. The district provides the board with an update on the Governor’s Proposed budget as it relates to K-12 education. This update initiates the budget development process with schools and departments and is subject to change based on legislative activities.  The state legislature is required to approve a balanced budget by June 15.
