RECOMMENDATION: Approve the 2009-14 Local Education Agency (LEA) Plan and Addendum. The LEA First Reading was presented to the board on December 1, 2009.

FISCAL IMPACT: $3.9 million. NCLB regulations specify the district is required to set aside 10 percent of its Title I allocation for high quality professional development

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: LEA Plan -Each LEA must develop a single, coordinated, and comprehensive Plan that describes the educational services for all students that can be used to guide implementation of federal and state-funded programs, the allocation of resources, and reporting requirements. The development of such a plan involves a continuous cycle of assessment, parent and community involvement, planning, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation. The duration of the Plan should be five years, 2009-14. The Plan should be periodically reviewed and updated as needed, but at least once each year.

LEA Addendum – A school district that does not make AYP in the same content area (English language arts or mathematics) and does not meet AYP criteria in the same content area in each grade span noted for two consecutive years is identified for program improvement. Accordingly, in July 2009, the San Diego Unified School District was identified as Title I Program Improvement Year 1. NCLB regulations specify the district is required to set aside 10 percent of its Title I allocation for high quality professional development.

LEAs identified as Program Improvement shall develop or revise an LEA Plan in consultation with parents, school staff and others. This requirement is met by writing an addendum to the LEA Plan which addresses the strategies and actions the district will implement to meet the needs of its students, primarily the English learner and students with disabilities subgroups.

The approval of the LEA Plan and Addendum will enable the district to meet the NCLB requirements and implement the specific strategies designed to meet the identified needs of the district’s students. The proposed strategies and actions spelled out in the Addendum are the result of reviewing student data, analyzing the three state-developed surveys taken by staff and careful planning by committees consisting of parents, central office and site staff.

School enrollment forms will be revised prior to the 2010-11 school year to fully clarify the question of "opting in" or "opting out" of student information being provided to military recruiters, while at the same time remaining in compliance with NCLB requirements regarding student data.
