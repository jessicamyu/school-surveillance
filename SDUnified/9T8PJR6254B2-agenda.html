RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution recognizing October as LGBTQ History Month.  The Superintendent and other appropriate staff will initiate activities to celebrate this month in district schools accordingly.

 

BACKGROUND: Since 1998, the month of October is nationally recognized as Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender (LGBTQ) History Month, and the San Diego Unified School District is committed to maintaining safe, inclusive environments wherein each and every one of the students can thrive.

 

October is also designated as Bullying Prevention Month, staff members and students are developing and coordinating activities that reiterate commitment to diversity, inclusiveness, and safety, including:

  • Allowing students to learn of the various forms of bullying including cyber, verbal, physical, exclusion, and lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, questioning (LGBTQ) bullying
  • Creating vibrant exhibits at the Eugene Brucker Education Center and on district campuses to celebrate the month
  • Implementing history and social studies lessons that highlight the contributions of members of the LGBTQ community, as prescribed by the FAIR Education Act (SB 28)
  • Providing OUT for Safe Schools badges for employees to visibly show their support for safe and inclusive schools

The LGBTQ+ Community Advisory Committee will meet regularly to provide the district leadership with on-going input through the year on plans to implement inclusive curricula, maintain safe environments, and respond to the needs of students.

BACKGROUND: The district proposes to modernize the existing Grant K-8 campus within the Mission Hills portion of City of San Diego.  The proposed project includes the following components:

Grant K-8 School Whole Site Modernization Improvements

The proposed Whole Site Modernizations (WSM) would be implemented in four phases over a span of approximately 20 years. The following describes each phase of the project:

  • Phase 1A of the proposed project will include construction of a two-story elementary school building with eight classrooms, restrooms, a staff lounge, materials storage, a kitchen, a cafe/multi-purpose room, an outdoor covered lunch shelter, and an elevator. In addition, a turf field will be installed upon completion of the construction of Phase 1A.  The eight new permanent classrooms will replace eight portable classrooms.   
  • Phase 1B will include construction of elementary play courts and gecko gardens. 
  • Phase 2A will include construction of a middle school and kindergarten classroom facilities, restrooms, and an elevator.  Phase 2A will include the demolition of two permanent classrooms and eight relocatable classrooms and replace them with 14 new permanent classrooms.  
  • Phase 2B will include construction of middle school hard courts and greens.
  • Phase 3A will include construction of administration, instructional support, special education, elementary science, music, art, and digital media facilities, P.E. offices and lockers, an amphitheater, and a quad. Additionally, a marquee sign will be installed upon completion of the construction of Phase 3A. The proposed marquee sign will include on/off/dimming controls provided by photocells, time clocks, and/or computer controls, and will be generally turned off by 10:00 pm. Phase 3A will include the demolition of eight existing permanent classrooms and the construction of one new permanent classroom.  
  • Phase 3B will include construction of sidewalk improvements, a field, an ADA ramp and seating to Pioneer Park.
  • Phase 4 will include construction of a gym, storage, community rooms, and a stage.  Phase 4 will include the demolition of two portable classrooms and the construction of five new permanent classrooms. 

The proposed WSM of the existing campus would replace existing classrooms with new modernized classrooms and would not result in a net change in the number of classrooms.  Upon completion of the WSM, the enrollment capacity of the school would be approximately 770 students with 35 classrooms. While the estimated enrollment capacity would minimally increase (less than 1 percent from existing), projected future enrollment of the school would remain below 770 students.  Due to the minimal increase in the estimated enrollment capacity of the school (less than 1 percent from existing), it is stated throughout this document that the proposed project would not result in an increase in enrollment capacity. As such, the proposed project would not increase the existing school’s enrollment capacity or the net number of classrooms.

 

On July 24, 2015, the district filed a Draft Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration dated July 2015, out for 30-day public review period.  A Notice of Intent to adopt the Negative Declaration was published in the Union-Tribune and La Prensa and filed at the County of San Diego and California State Clearinghouse.  Copies of the Draft Negative Declaration were sent to the State Clearinghouse, state and local agencies, and community entities/organizations.  The Draft Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration was made available at the district Physical Plant Operations Annex; Mission Hills Branch Library; and online at the Environmental Review/Studies page at www.sandiegounified.org/environmental-reviews.
