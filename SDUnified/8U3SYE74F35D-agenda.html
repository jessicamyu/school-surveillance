8. Petition to Establish the San Diego Cooperative Charter School 2; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-2)
Meeting
Dec 4, 2012 - Regular Charter Meeting and Construction Bonds Update (Propositions S and Z), 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Approve, with conditions, the petition to establish the San Diego Cooperative Charter School 2 (SDCCS2), a charter school, for a five-year term beginning on July 1, 2013, and expiring on June 30, 2018.  Staff recommends board action to conditionally approve the SDCCS2 petition, contingent upon the lead petitioner’s agreement to revise the petition according to the factual findings below and submit the revised petition and the Conditional Approval MOU Agreement (as approved by the board) to the Office of Charter Schools by March 29, 2013.  Alternatively, the board may approve the petition without conditions or adopt this report as making written factual findings in support the board’s denial.

 

Factual Findings: The SDCCS2 petition was circulated for review by multiple district departments.  The petition was analyzed according to the criteria established in the Education Code.  Below are the factual findings by staff reviewers of the petition.

 

Reasonably Comprehensive Description: The SDCCS2 petition does not contain reasonably comprehensive descriptions of all of the required elements set forth in Education Code.

  1. Academic Program (Element 1): English Language Learner (ELL) program description lacks a comprehensive description of the program that will be implemented.  The petition contains a brief paragraph description of the ELL support process on page 12, but this description needs to include greater detail to be reasonably comprehensive.  For example, there is no mention of how progress and developing proficiency in ELD will be assessed and monitored.  It was noted that the petition did not include world language in the middle school curriculum that was described; this is part of the UC/CSU “e” language requirement beginning in seventh and eighth grade.
  1. Reflecting District Demographics (Element 7): The petition contains a description of the outreach efforts on page 29, however, there are no funds identified in the budget for outreach.
  1. Audits (Element 9):  Page 32 of the petition needs to be revised to indicate the final, “unaudited report for the full year.” The language in place is not currently accurate.
  1. Retirement Systems (Element 11): If the school offers CalSTRS; all qualifying employees must be enrolled.
  1. Budget: The SDCCS2 combined both the budget and cash flows in one document, this needs to be revised.  The budget assumptions do not clearly show the number of employees or FTE; this needs to be revised.  The budget does not show a cumulative ending fund balance for each of the budgeted years. However, district staff has calculated the school will meet the minimum reserve requirement at the end of the third year of operation.  SDCCS2 submitted a first year budget with negative net income.  
  1. Cash Flow: Cash flow is inaccurate for the following reasons:   
    1. The school will not receive lottery cash in the first year of operation.  
    2. Cash receipts of State Aid funding do not show the correct funding percentages with the corresponding months of funding distributions.
    3. The school will not receive funds entitled all in one year.  Funds entitled but not received must be accrued at the end of the year.
    4. The cash flow cannot be negative at the end of the first year of operation.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Cooperative Charter School 2 (SDCCS2) petitioners submitted a charter petition to establish a new K-8 charter school.  The public hearing for the establishment of this charter school took place on November 6, 2012.

 

Based on the review of the charter petition and the experience of the petitioners in operating the San Diego Cooperative Charter School, the Office of Charter Schools is making the recommendation to approve, with conditions, the SDCCS2 charter petition to establish the SDCCS2 program to operate a K-8 grade program.  The charter school indicates it plans to begin operating in the fall of 2013.
