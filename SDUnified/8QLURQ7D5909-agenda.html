Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

Under these agreements, AMSOLAR must design, install, operate, and maintain, at their expense, solar systems on designated roofs and carports at the district in exchange for electricity payments.  Specifically, these agreements require AMSOLAR to design and install solar systems that are predominantly “self-ballasted and non-penetrating” on our roofs to minimize holes, penetrations, and possible leaks, ensuring that district roofs last through their expected life cycle.  AMSOLAR is unable to accomplish this contractual requirement for solar panels on district roofs.  As such, no work was started on district roofs.

 

Because the non-penetrating design was not an issue for solar systems on carports, AMSOLAR completed solar system installation on carports at Kearny, Madison, Morse, and Scripps Ranch.  The carport at University City will be complete when the district finishes the main electrical upgrade.  The solar systems on carports at these five sites will be turned on simultaneously, and we expect them to be fully operational by December 31, 2012.  Thus, the parties have agreed to revise the contractual Commercial Operation Date to December 31, 2012, for the photovoltaic carports.  At that time, the district will begin to pay the AMSOLAR $0.175 per kilowatt-hour, which is adjusted at two percent annually, for electricity generated by those solar panels on the carports. The original price of $0.17 per kilowatt-hour is increased by half of one cent to cover the cost of a smaller system as well as the cost of the carport structure.

 

The carport at Clairemont is pending approval by a regulatory agency due to newly identified burn ash.  If the environmental issue is remediated, the carport at Clairemont will be completed and operational sometime after the other five carports are functional.

 

The agreements also allow any party to terminate, without triggering liability, if AMSOLAR misses the Commercial Operation Date of December 31, 2011.  AMSOLAR failed to meet this deadline for solar systems on roofs.  Consequently, the parties have decided to mutually terminate the solar power purchase agreements, amendments, and leases for roofs only.  

 

Approval of this board agenda item will authorize district staff to execute the following documents:  (1) amended power purchase agreements and leases with AMSOLAR SDUSD I, II, and III, LLC’s to consolidate solar systems on carports  and amended power purchase agreement and lease with AMSOLAR SDUSD IV, LLC to consolidate all solar systems on roofs; (2) consents and acknowledgements with AMSOLAR SDUSD V, VI, VII, and VIII, LLC’s on moving projects out; (3) termination agreement with AMSOLAR SDUSD I, IV, V, VI, VII, VIII, LLC’s for solar systems on roofs; and (4) memoranda of leases for solar agreements with AMSOLAR SDUSD I, II, and III, LLC’s relating to carports.   As such, the District will have solar generated electricity from carports at six high school sites (Clairemont, Kearny, Madison, Morse, Scripps Ranch, and University City).

RECOMMENDATION:  Information only.  

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  $2 million from Title I funds.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  District workgroup charged with establishing the criteria for awarding grants under the Title I $2 million grant program included representatives from the District Advisory Committee (DAC) for Compensatory Education and the Title I Tiger Team. The grant program was included on the May 16, 2012 DAC agenda; however, little new or specific information was available.

 

BACKGROUND:  At the board meeting on November 29, 2011, the board created a Title I $2 million grant program for the 2012-13 fiscal year by reserving $1 million from central office allocations and $1 million from school site allocations (schools in the 40-74 percent threshold range).  These funds are intended to support research-based, effective and sustainable school improvement activities that increase the likelihood that all students learn challenging academic content and achieve proficiency on state assessments in English language arts and mathematics. The board also directed staff to convene a group to establish criteria for awarding grants to eligible schools.  The workgroup included representatives from parents (DAC and Title I Tiger Team), principals, area superintendents, and the Office of Accountability.  

 

Title I schools with populations of students eligible for free or reduced-price meals at or above the 75 percent threshold are eligible to apply for funding from this grant program.  To receive grant funds, a school must submit an application to the district that complies with the provisions in the grant application.  Schools meeting the eligibility criteria may submit applications for funding not to exceed $100,000. School applications will be judged against the scoring rubric for this grant.  Grants will be awarded to schools with the highest scoring applications until available funding is exhausted, irrespective of school level (elementary, middle, or high) or school geographic location.  

 

To ensure the district meets program accountability requirements associated with Title I funding, the board prescribed that a condition of acceptance of grant funds is that schools participate in a fiscal monitoring pilot, whereby schools will be required to use the four-digit extended code to provide additional detail about expenditures.  The district will rely on appropriate fiscal and program information contained in district data systems to fulfill its monitoring responsibilities.  In addition, district representatives may conduct site visits to validate information submitted by schools and gather additional information from interviews and observations for technical assistance, monitoring, and evaluation purposes.

 

The Office of Accountability will be responsible for posting notifications of grant awards on its webpage within five days of decisions on award recipients.  All applications, whether approved or not, will be posted in their entirety on the Office of Accountability webpage. The Office of Accountability will also post a summary of the grant awards. 

 

At their last meeting, workgroup members discussed the likely reduction in Title I funding for 2012-13, and how this should be considered given board discussion that the $2 million reserved for the grant program shall be used to offset any reduction in Title I funding, the outcome of which is tied to the federal fiscal year.  Thus, available funding may not be known until October of this year at the earliest.  The grant timeline in the draft application document was also discussed at the last work group meeting and subsequently modified based on feedback from members of the board and the Superintendent (see page 3 in the draft application).  Any decision about the timing of the grant program should likely include overall budget constraints and district plans to continue to support other programs that have been supported with Title I funds in the past (e.g., limited summer school).

After consulting with both Adams Elementary and San Diego Global Visions Academy, it is proposed that Room B-24 be returned for use by Adams Elementary School, and that Room B-26 be allocated to San Diego Global Vision Academy.  All other terms of the agreement are unchanged (see attachment, Exhibit A, page 2).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: At its May 1, 2012 meeting, the board approved a Facilities Use Permit Agreement with the SDGVA for space on the Adams Elementary site.  Adams Elementary had objections to the agreement and representations were made to the board and staff to alter the agreement before it was signed.  Staff has met with both schools and listened to the concerns expressed, and is presenting a revised agreement, from the May 1, 2012 agreement which changes one room assignment.
