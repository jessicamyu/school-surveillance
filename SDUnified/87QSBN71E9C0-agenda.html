3. Board Discussion and Direction on Whether to Place a Ballot Measure, Emergency Parcel Tax, on the November 2, 2010 Ballot
Meeting
Jul 27, 2010 - Special Meeting, 4:30 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION:  Board members will discuss whether to go forward with the request to order an election to authorize a qualified special tax, Emergency Parcel Tax, on the November 2, 2010 ballot.

 

If the decision of the board is to rescind the July 13, 2010 action to place a ballot measure on the November 2, 2010 ballot, adopt the attached resolution entitled, Resolution of the Board of the San Diego Unified School District Withdrawing the Election Order for An Election on November 2, 2010  (see attached resolution).

­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­

On July 13, 2010, the board adopted a resolution (Option B) of the Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District Ordering an Election to Authorize a Qualified Special Tax, Establishing Specifications of the Election Order, and Requesting Consolidation with Other Elections Occurring on November 2, 2010.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  The estimated cost to place a proposed tax measure on the November 2010 General Election Ballot is $230,000, payable to the County of San Diego, Registrar in October 2010.  Budget source: Board of Education Election Expenses 5510 00000 00 5858 7100 0000 01000 0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  In 2008-09, the cost to the district for the Proposition S ballot measure was $897,909. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

BACKGROUND:  On July 13, 2010, the board adopted (Option B) Resolution of the Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District Ordering an Election to Authorize a Qualified Special Tax, Establishing Specifications of the Election Order, and Requesting Consolidation with Other Elections Occurring on November 2, 2010.

 

Additional information has become available indicating that the City of San Diego may place a ballot proposition on the November 2, 2010 ballot which would increase the sales tax in the City of San Diego.

 

The deadline to request that the measure be removed from the November 2, 2010 ballot is August 11, 2010.  Under state law, a parcel tax measure must be approved by two-thirds of the voters. 

 

A parcel tax measure has been considered because of the more than $400 million in budget cuts that have been imposed on the district over the last four years.  In order to maintain educational programs and reasonable class sizes, it is critical that the district explore all available revenue sources. 

 

A temporary 5-Year Emergency Education Tax, parcel tax, is the only local funding option available to school districts to provide funds for operational proposes.  As proposed the measure would generate an estimated $58 million annually for the district.
