Background Information:
On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that will provide $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified School District facilities during the next ten years.

On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11 member Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) for Proposition S. As part of the Proposition and as required by law, the board will appoint the ICOC to monitor how the district spends these tax dollars.

The membership for the Proposition S ICOC will be selected from the following criteria:
  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district (Jackson)
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens’ organization (Nakamura)
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association (Evans)
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district (Barrera)
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization (de Beck)

Board members will each choose one appointee from the above criteria;
Board members will each select an individual of their own choosing; one current Proposition MM ICOC committee member will continue to serve on the Proposition S ICOC to provide background to new committee members.

Terms for membership on the Proposition S ICOC will be for two years, and there will be a two term maximum. No employee or official of the district shall be appointed to the citizens’ oversight committee, nor any vendor, contractor or consultant of the district.
