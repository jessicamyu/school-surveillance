BACKGROUND: Kids for Peace is a global, nonprofit organization founded in 2006, that provides a platform for young people to actively engage in socially conscious leadership, community service, arts, environmental stewardship and global friendship. It is the mission and vision of Kids for Peace to create peace through youth leadership, community service, global friendships, and thoughtful acts of kindness, and that we all live in a safe and peaceful world where all people respect and care for each other and the planet. The Kids for Peace pledge states, “I pledge to use my words to speak in a kind way. I pledge to help others as I go throughout my day. I pledge to care for our earth with my healing heart and hands. I pledge to respect people in each and every land. I pledge to join together as we unite the big and small. I pledge to do my part to create peace for one and all!” Kids for Peace motto is “Kindness Matters” and encourages kids to do their part to create peace by simply being kind to each other and to the earth. The Great Kindness Challenge helps schools create a culture of connection and kindness especially important as we continue to develop positive school environments during COVID-19 pandemic. The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education believes all kids deserve to learn in a safe, supportive, and dynamic environment. The Great Kindness Challenge provides a powerful tool that actively engages students, teachers, administration, families, and community in creating a school culture of acceptance, tolerance, unity, and respect. The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education is designating the week of January 25-29, 2021, Great Kindness Challenge Week. Information on the challenge is available at https://thegreatkindnesschallenge.com.

BACKGROUND: The Reading Writing Project Network, LLC, will provide Mason Elementary School staff with professional development focusing on reading/phonics instruction to meet the needs of all students.

 

Mason Elementary School has used the Reading Units of Study Program for the past five years, however, the staff was not trained in the curriculum.  This Agreement will ensure all teachers are provided the professional development to include high-quality instruction so all students will have access to the Guaranteed Viable Curriculum (GVC).

 

The Reading Writing Project Network staff developers will work virtually with the principal and teachers to refine and review effective practices that promote the schoolwide deployment of the classroom strategies that ensure systemic instruction and schoolwide academic achievement.

 

This staff development will be presented virtually by providing classroom demonstrations in pre-established classroom lab sites at each grade level to include a debriefing with those same participants outside the classroom setting.  Staff development will also have a set time to meet with the principal and teachers to ensure the program's follow-through and success.  The staff developers will also be available for parent workshops, subject to scheduling and as permitted by the district.

 

Consultant/Professional Staff Developers will focus on:

 

1) Best practices in reading instruction

2) Units of study across the year in the reading workshop

3) Methods of holding students accountable for doing their best work

4) Assessing and planning for work with individuals and small groups

5) Using literature to help students craft their writing from what they have read

6) Classroom structures that support inquiry and collaboration

7) Creating mini-lessons based on assessment

8) Methods for differentiating instruction to respond to the diverse needs of all students and

teachers

9) Assistance in planning units of study in reading

10) Assistance developing mini-lessons, workshop teaching, and the debriefing exploration of work

11) Assistance in setting up structures for the principal to turn-key staff development

12) Management techniques to support independence in reading workshops

13) Aligning curriculum to standards

14) Assisting teachers to work with parents as literacy partners

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The proposed license agreement to install a roof camera at the Education Center (Proposed Project) is a subject to CEQA, however, the project is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Section 15311 – Accessory Structures. Class 11 consists of the construction, or placement of minor structures accessory to (appurtenant to) existing commercial, industrial, or institutional facilities. The LMC would temporarily install a minor structure, consisting of a non-penetrating, solar-powered camera the Education Center for the purpose of documenting construction at their adjacent property located at 4354 Park Boulevard. Therefore, the application of a Class 11 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2, and summarized below, prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Analysis: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

 

  1. Location: The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundary of an existing developed site used by the district. The Proposed Project is consistent with the existing operations on the project site as there is existing office space and parking used by the district. The Proposed Project would not extend into any off-site or open space areas that may be classified as an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect: The Proposed Project includes the execution of a license agreement for the temporary use of space for the installation of a roof camera by LMC on district property. No new construction or operational activity is proposed on- or off-site, not already contemplated, that would result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas as a result of the Proposed Project. As such, there is nothing unusual about the project site or surrounding area. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways: The Proposed Project does not require the construction of new permanent facilities that would directly, or indirectly, affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. Therefore, implementation of the Proposed Project would not substantially damage scenic resources within a state scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.

  6. Historical Resources: The Proposed Project does not require the construction of new permanent facilities and therefore, would not result in a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource as described in CEQA Guidelines Section 15064.5(b)).
