BACKGROUND: The board reviewed these plans as a first reading on May 26, 2009.

General Information: Program Improvement (PI) Status: Under the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001 (NCLB), a Title I school that, for two consecutive years, does not meet or exceed a series of annual academic performance goals known as Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) is designated PI. Such schools must provide specified types of services and/or interventions during each year that they are identified as PI. A school is eligible to exit PI if it makes AYP for two consecutive years.

District Schools in PI Year 3, 2009-10: Corrective Action: A school that enters its third consecutive year in PI status must be identified for corrective action. Corrective action is a significant intervention in a school that is designed to remedy the school's persistent inability to make adequate yearly progress toward all students’ becoming proficient in English language arts and mathematics.

District schools in PI Year 3 status: Carson, Edison, Horton, and Rosa Parks elementary schools; Language Academy; Lewis, Pershing, and Wangenheim middle schools; San Diego Communications, Investigations in a Multicultural Atmosphere (CIMA) and San Diego Media, Visual, and Performing Arts (MVPA) high schools. The school corrective action plans were developed by school teams in collaboration with the Office of Federal and Special Programs.
Fiscal implications: Existing district Title I funds (approximately $20,000 per school) were used to support the costs of web-based reading interventions and related professional development; and "push-in" teachers.
District Schools in PI Year 4, 2009–10: School Restructuring: A school that enters its fourth consecutive year in PI status is required to prepare a plan to restructure that will be implemented the following year, in this case in 2009–10. The restructuring plan must describe in detail the changes in governance, instructional practice and staffing that will be required to improve students’ academic achievement and enable the school to meet its AYP goals.

District schools in PI Year 4 status: Encanto Elementary School; Creative, Performing and Media Arts (CPMA) Middle School; and Clairemont High School. The restructuring proposals were developed by School Program Improvement Leadership Teams composed of teachers, administrators, classified staff and parents, in collaboration with the Office of Federal and Special Programs.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the revised or newly developed Single Plans for Student Achievement (SPSA) plans and categorical budget summaries for 176 schools.

FISCAL IMPACT: There is no cost associated with this item.

BACKGROUND: Through SB 374, the state holds each school accountable for developing and implementing a Single Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA). The SPSA identifies and addresses the instructional needs of students and specifies how school funding including categorical funds provided through the Consolidated Application will be used to accomplish the goals outlined in the plan.

The SPSA is developed or modified through an annual planning cycle. The School Site Council (SSC) in collaboration with the site instructional leadership team is responsible for developing, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating the SPSA. Site principals, SSC chairpersons, SSC members and the District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education (DAC) received training on February 2, 4, 10, and 12, regarding specific plan requirements.

Additionally, Federal and Special Programs staff attended elementary, middle and high schools principals’ meetings to discuss Title I expenditures and SPSA plan development process. Following these meetings, certificated staff with extensive knowledge and experience reviewed each plan and provided individual assistance to schools to ensure that each SPSA meets state requirements and that the plan and associated categorical budget allocations are in alignment. A final review was conducted by Chief/School Improvement Officers to ensure schools have identified student needs, aligned smart goals with an appropriate course of action to address those needs.

The local governing board must review and approve each school’s SPSA plan before it may be implemented. This report will bring forward the 2009-10 SPSA plans and categorical budget summaries for 176 schools for board approval.

Copies of each SPSA, budget summary, and required, signed assurances page are available in the Board Services Office, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231, San Diego, CA 92103.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve amendment to existing District Policy F-5800 (Resource Persons/Volunteers), to specifically call for a volunteer screening procedure.

Staff recommends amending District Policy F-5800 as follows:

The Board of Education encourages the participation of volunteers in district programs in order to enrich instruction, to provide an opportunity for interested community members to participate in district programs, and to strengthen school/community relations. (Volunteers cannot will not displace a bargaining unit member or be placed in a position where an employee was laid off.)

The Board of Education further acknowledges its obligation to the health, welfare, and safety of schools and the children they serve. Therefore, volunteers will be required to undergo screening, as determined by the Superintendent, to ensure student, staff, and overall school safety.

(Approved 3-88. Revised 5-22-2001 TBD. Related Procedures 4595, 5001, 7065, 9300, 9301, 9302.)

FISCAL IMPACT: There is no additional cost associated with the screening of Category A, B and C volunteers. Category D volunteers require fingerprinting. Annual cost to fingerprint Category D volunteers is estimated to be $29,375. This is a new requirement for the School Police Services. The additional funds will cover both the California and National records.

BACKGROUND: On March 24, 2009, the Board of Education endorsed the Superintendent’s effort to review and update Administrative Procedure 4595 as deemed appropriate and promulgation of a uniform policy.

Staff convened a small working group, with representatives from the logistics, school police, community relations, communications, and legal departments, to review and revise Administrative Procedure 4595 – School Volunteer Programs (AP 4595 attachment 1). A couple of very important components of the revision process was sharing the proposed changes with various stakeholder groups and requesting their feedback, as well as a national inquiry by School Police Services requesting information from other districts about their volunteer background and fingerprint requirements (attachment 2).

The feedback received identified a variety of concerns related to the process of screening potential volunteers. These were dealt with individually and have been addressed by revising the volunteer application and the development of a new “Request for Volunteer Screening” form attached to AP 4595. In addition, all principals and site volunteer coordinators will be required to attend training conducted by the Community Relations Department at the onset of the 2009-10 school year.

Proposed revisions to the procedure include four categories of volunteers and the different degrees of scrutiny and clearance levied on them. Key components of the revised procedure include the following:

CATEGORY A

Volunteers or guests who enter a school for a one-time event. This person has no unsupervised exposure or contact with children. Typical examples could include Read Across America guest reader, Principal for a Day participant, guest/resource speaker, senior exhibition panel member or other day or guest-type activity.

Screening requirements:

  • Be sponsored or approved by a school site or district employee
  • Be able to present some form of current government-issued photo identification (driver’s license, passport, military ID, US or other government identification)
  • Sign in on the district approved “Volunteer Sign-in Sheet” in the main office, which includes a criminal disclosure
  • Upon approval, be required to display a volunteer/visitor identification badge which they will surrender following the event or activity.

CATEGORY B

Volunteers with group exposure who have little or no direct unsupervised exposure or contact with children. Typical examples could include non-classroom volunteers such as office helpers, community speakers or panel members, non-classroom assistance, organized functions associated with school organizations such as PTA, school foundation, Site Council. Volunteer conditions are typically public settings, staff or other adults can observe at all times, no solitary time with children, and always within unobstructed view.

Screening requirements:
  • Be sponsored or approved by a school site or district employee
  • Be able to present some form of current government-issued photo identification (driver’s license, passport, military ID, US or other government identification)
  • Complete and submit for approval the district School Volunteer Application
  • Be checked by designated site personnel against the California Department of Justice, Sexual Offender (Megan’s Law) website (http://www.meganslaw.ca.gov)
  • Present a Tuberculosis clearance card
  • Sign in on the district approved “Volunteer Sign-in Sheet” in the main office, which includes a criminal disclosure
  • Upon approval, be required to display a volunteer identification badge to be surrendered at the conclusion of the day’s volunteer activity

CATEGORY C

Volunteers with classroom exposure, who work with children and are generally supervised by district staff. This could include direct contact with supervision. Typical examples could include tutoring, classroom reading, classroom assistance, before and after school programs depending upon the conditions, and field trip drivers. Conditions typically are classrooms or areas where staff or other adults can observe at most times. These may occasionally include short solitary time with children and short duration of obstructed view.

Screening requirements:
  • Be sponsored or approved by a school site or district employee
  • Be able to present some form of current government-issued photo identification (driver’s license, passport, military ID, US or other government identification)
  • Volunteer drivers for field trips must have a current driver’s license and provide documentation of automobile insurance coverage. (Refer to Administrative Procedures 4585 and 4587 regarding field trip requirements)
  • Complete and submit for approval the district School Volunteer Application
  • Submit to a criminal background check conducted by district School Police Services to determine whether the applicant is precluded from volunteering as specified in California Education Code section 35021 et seq. In rare cases, depending upon information returned, volunteer applicants may require fingerprinting.
  • Present a Tuberculosis clearance card
  • Sign in on the district approved “Volunteer Sign-in Sheet” in the main office, which includes a criminal disclosure
  • Upon approval, be required to display a volunteer identification badge to be surrendered at the conclusion of the day’s volunteer activity

CATEGORY D

Volunteers with unrestricted exposure, who work with children and may be unsupervised by district staff. This volunteer will likely have direct and unsupervised interaction with children. Typical examples could include volunteer walk-on coaches, senior high support personnel for athletics, off-site tutoring, mentoring, and overnight chaperones. Conditions typically could include an off-campus setting and unsupervised, solitary time with students.

Screening requirements:
  • Be sponsored or approved by a school site or district employee
  • Be able to present some form of current government-issued photo identification (driver’s license, passport, military ID, US or other government identification)
  • Complete and submit for approval the district School Volunteer Application
  • Submit fingerprints to both State and National databases conducted by district School Police Services
  • Present a Tuberculosis clearance card
  • Sign in on the district approved “Volunteer Sign-in Sheet” in the main office, which includes a criminal disclosure
  • Upon approval, be required to display a volunteer identification badge to be surrendered at the conclusion of the day’s volunteer activity

District K-12 students who volunteer at schools other than their assigned school, are only required to complete and submit the district School Volunteer Application.

Staff has presented the Superintendent with a draft of proposed changes to the procedure, and if the revision to the policy is approved by the board, staff will recommend that a districtwide implementation directive be generated to implement the revised volunteer procedure beginning with the 2009-10 school year.
