A.02. Approval of the Fiscal Year 2009-10 First Interim Financial Report in Compliance with Education Code Section 42130; BUDGETED: (OE-6)
Meeting
Dec 11, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the Fiscal Year 2009-10 First Interim Report and certify that the district will be able to meet its financial obligations for the current fiscal year and two subsequent fiscal years (positive certification).

FISCAL IMPACT: The district’s Fiscal Year 2009-10 Unrestricted General Fund, First Interim Report depicts a projected positive balance of $37,102,927.00 with surplus spending at $53,257,354.00. This financial report was developed using various assumptions and projections as highlighted in the exhibit. The 2009-10 General Fund Restricted and Unrestricted ending balance is $72,794,467.00.

Fiscal Year 2010-11, indicates a positive ending balance of $50,812,323.00 with surplus spending at $126,638,222.00. The 2010-11 projected ending balance includes an estimated $84,196,289.00 of budget reductions. In accordance with the County Office of Education requirements, the district must have a plan for resolution of the budget shortfall by the time of the Second Interim Report submittal on March 17, 2010.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:

The district's Fiscal Year 2008-09 unrestricted General Fund, First Interim Report depicted a projected positive balance of $52,906,802 with surplus spending at $2,464,971. The 2008-09 General Fund Restricted and Unrestricted ending balance is $106,828,695.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: On June 23, 2009, the Board of Education adopted the Fiscal Year 2009-10 budget, under the single budget adoption cycle. On September 8, 2009, the board approved the 2008-09 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report.

California Education Code Section 42130 requires the Board of Education approve the First Interim Report of the district’s budgetary status as of October 31, on or before December 15. The board is also required to certify to the state that the district’s projected financial condition at this point in time is one of the following:

a) Positive Certification - will be able to meet its financial obligations
b) Qualified Certification - may not meet its financial obligations
c) Negative Certification - will be unable to meet its financial obligations.

The projected decline in the ending balance and continued deficit spending indicate the need for budget actions to end deficit spending and re-establish the district's ending balance at a sustainable level.

All other district funds outside of the General Fund are projected to meet their financial obligations for the current year.
