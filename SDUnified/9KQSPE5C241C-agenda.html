4. Real Estate Utilization Strategy: Resolution of Intent to Consider Joint Occupancy Proposals at Various District Locations; BUDGETED: N/A
Meeting
Jul 22, 2014 - Special Meeting, 4:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION:  Adopt the resolution to seek proposals for joint occupancy projects at five district sites.

  1. Ballard/Fremont Center, located at 2375 Congress St., San Diego, CA 92110
  2. Scripps Mesa Conference Center, located at 10380 Spring Canyon Rd., San Diego, CA 92131
  3. Transportation Services Center, located at 4710 Cardin St., San Diego, CA 92111
  4. Barnard Asian Pacific Language Academy at 2445 Fogg St., San Diego, CA 92109
  5. Revere Center, located at 6735 Gifford Way, San Diego, CA 92111

FISCAL IMPACT:  None at this time.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Work to be completed by staff, primarily from Special Projects, Real Estate, Facilities Planning and Construction, and Legal Services. Additional professional services to supplement staff is needed to complete this effort (cost to be determined).

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: As some district sites being considered are declared excess, the concept of joint occupancy has been discussed at various meetings of the Advisory Committee on the Utilization of Excess Property (ACUESP), including June 28, 2012, January 24, 2013, February 28, 2014, and April 25, 2014.

 

BACKGROUND: Under Education Code sections 17515-17526, school districts may enter into joint occupancy agreements, which allow the district to maintain ownership of a property while jointly developing uses through agreements with development entities.

 

On September 11, 2012, the board adopted a resolution of intent to consider proposals to enter into agreements to develop certain district real properties for joint occupancy, and to consider interest in certain district real properties for sale, lease or joint use.  Through this resolution, the board expressed interest in focusing on the concept of "joint occupancy" projects.

 

As part of the 2012 resolution, the board directed that all appropriate community planning and stakeholder organizations be contacted to provide input and obtain feedback.

 

In February 2014, a board workshop on real estate was held to discuss the development of the district's real estate utilization strategy, including long-term joint occupancy and building additional capacity to generate new, ongoing revenue through the lease and permitted use of district properties.

 

At the workshop, the board expressed consensus on a set of guiding principles for the real estate utilization strategy, including:

  • Maintain ownership of district property where appropriate
  • Generate ongoing revenue
  • Develop properties as neighborhood centers providing neighborhood services (in alignment with Vision 2020 goals)
  • Provide for community engagement and input in the development process

Education Code section 17521 requires the Board to adopt a resolution for the purposes of receiving proposals for joint occupancy.  The resolution must identify the specific properties and the intended joint occupancy use.  This item is intended to fulfill statutory requirements for the joint occupancy of district property.  The resolution to consider proposals for joint occupancy identifies the following district properties:

  1. Ballard/Fremont Center, located at 2375 Congress St., San Diego, CA 92110
  2. Scripps Mesa Conference Center, located at 10380 Spring Canyon Rd., San Diego, CA 92131
  3. Transportation Services Center, located at 4710 Cardin St., San Diego, CA 92111
  4. Barnard Asian Pacific Language Academy at 2445 Fogg St., San Diego, CA 92109
  5. Revere Center, located at 6735 Gifford Way, San Diego, CA 92111

This list of sites is intended to serve as a beginning point for the joint occupancy development process. Additional sites have been identified that could be the focus of future efforts, including the Education Center.

 

Following adoption of the resolution, staff will release a Request for Proposals (RFP) to solicit qualified development teams to submit proposals to jointly develop one or more of the identified properties.  The intent of the RFP is to identify highly qualified development teams who are interested in jointly developing properties with the district. During the evaluation process, staff will identify the most qualified development teams.  Once identified, staff will coordinate with the development teams to conduct a community engagement process to further identify ideas for the development of each site. Subsequently, further refinement or addendum to the RFP will take place and proposers will be required to incorporate community feedback into their proposals. Staff would also engage with affected bargaining units regarding changes prior to submitting the proposals to the board for approval. After the board selects the proposal (s), staff must forward the proposal(s) to the state board of education for approval.

 

A timeline of these activities is attached for board consideration.
