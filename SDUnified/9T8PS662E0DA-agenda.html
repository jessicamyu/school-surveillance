Regarding the educational program, the petition for City Farming Academy of San Diego describes a classroom-based charter school.  The petition states:

  1. City Farming Academy of San Diego will use an agricultural based curriculum from both the national and California Foundations for Ag in the Classroom and 4-H, that is rigorous and standards-based.  (Petition at page 29.)
  1. Students will participate in farming activities on a daily basis, adjusting their responsibilities based on age-appropriate work.  For example, students in Grades K-1 will take on the task of feeding the chickens, while students in Grades 2-3 will take on the additional task of collecting eggs from the chickens.  As students get older, they will be given responsibility to take of larger animals, such as goats.  (Petition at page 30.)
  1. The mission of City Farming Academy of San Diego is to cultivate an environment that fosters the whole child with collaborative project-based learning and with a curriculum rooted in urban farming principals and methods.  CFASD will prepare children to be successful 21st Century thinkers, self-sufficient problem solvers, and stewards of a diverse community, their family, and our Earth.  (Petition at page 16.)
  1. The goal of City Farming Academy of San Diego is to provide a rich opportunity for local youth to become familiar with urban farming and agriculture so they can contribute to a healthier lifestyle for themselves and their community.  It will allow students to literally get their hands dirty for the benefit of themselves and their community, as they learn to grow, eat, and sell healthier food items.  (Petition at page 23.)
     
  2. City Farming Academy of San Diego intends to focus its recruitment efforts on the southeast area of the city, which includes parts of San Diego Unified Sub-District D and Sub-District E.  The target area will be regions south of Interstate 8, North of Interstate 54, east of Interstate 805, and west of Interstate 125; primarily including zip codes 92102, 92104, 92114, 92115, and 92139, which include schools in the Crawford, Hoover, Lincoln, and Morse clusters.  (Petition at pages 19 and 24.)

  1. A freestanding office building with room on site for additional portable classrooms if necessary at 3544 Euclid Avenue, San Diego 92105.
     
  2. A commercial building already designated as a school with a tenant improvement allowance for renovations at 2520 Camino del Rio South, San Diego 92108.

BACKGROUND:  Old Town Academy is charter school serving Grades K-8.  Old Town Academy is seeking a renewal of its charter for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2016, and ending June 30, 2021.  The public hearing is to consider the level of support for Old Town Academy.

 

CONCERNS

 

Over the past few months, district staff has expressed numerous concerns to Old Town Academy regarding the following developments.

  • During the summer of 2014, Old Town Academy sought to become a member of the Tri-Valley Learning Corporation.  The Tri-Valley Learning Corporation is located in Livermore, California, and operates four charter schools located in Alameda County (Livermore) and San Joaquin County (Stockton).  Old Town Academy failed to notify the district of its proposed revision to the school's governance structure. 
     
  • On May 7, 2015, district staff conducted its annual site visit of Old Town Academy and was misinformed by charter staff, a charter board member, and an employee of the Tri-Valley Learning Corporation, that the Tri-Valley Learning Corporation was a third-party vendor to the school that was hired to provide back-office services. 
     
  • In June of 2015, district staff received numerous correspondence from parents and employees expressing concern and confusion that their charter school, Old Town Academy, had been "sold" to Tri-Valley Learning Corporation, the doors to the school were locked, and, as a result, a board meeting had to be conducted on the sidewalk in front of the school.  Multiple law firms contacted district staff claiming to be representing Old Town Academy in litigation filed with the San Diego Superior Court.
     
  • On June 17, 2015, the district sent Old Town Academy the attached letter notifying the school that it was not in good standing.
     
  • On July 27, 2015, the Old Town Academy board passed a resolution stating the Old Town Academy board “retains full and complete authority over the operations and management of the charter school,” and that the Tri-Valley Learning Corporation would serve as “an administrative services consultant” to the school until the district made its decision on the charter renewal.  However, documents reviewed by district staff during a school site visit on September 30, 2015, show that the Tri-Valley Learning Corporation is identified as the employer of several new employees at Old Town Academy. 
     
  • While total enrollment has increased from 187 to 252 students over the past five years, the enrollment of students with IEPs has declined.  In 2012-13, Old Town Academy reported having 23 students with IEPs.  In 2013-14, that enrollment dropped to 12 students.  At the site visit in 2014-15, Old Town Academy reported having only 9 students with IEPs. 

Old Town Academy is located on a non-district site at 2120 San Diego Avenue, San Diego, 92110, which falls within the San Diego High/Point Loma High optional cluster.  The tables below summarize academic and enrollment information about the school.

RECOMMENDATION: Hold a public hearing regarding the renewal of Audeo Charter School.  Audeo is seeking renewal of its charter for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2016, and ending June 30, 2021.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.
 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

BACKGROUND: Audeo Charter School is operated by the nonprofit public benefit corporation by the same name. Audeo is described as part of the Altus network of charter schools. Audeo is seeking renewal of its charter for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2016, and ending June 30, 2021. The public hearing is to consider the level of support for Audeo.  Audeo operates what it considers to be a non-classroom based “independent study” program for students in Grades K-12. Classroom instruction is included as part of the program.  The table below provides an overview of Audeo.

Audeo, Charter School of San Diego, and Laurel Preparatory Academy are separately authorized by San Diego Unified School District. These three entities refer to themselves as being part of the “Altus” network.  Altus is described as a private corporation that is a charitable nonprofit organization established to oversee the mission, marketing strategies, and training, for its affiliated charter schools.

 

CONCERNS:

  • In July of 2015, the district sent Audeo a letter to initiate discussions about a plan to transition sites operated by Audeo outside of district boundaries into compliance with the Charter Schools Act.  A copy of the letter is attached as an exhibit.
     
  • In September and October of 2015, the district provided Audeo with a written site visit report that, among other items, noted that Mary Bixby is a board member of Altus, Audeo, Charter School of San Diego, and Laurel, while concurrently, Ms. Bixby is an employee of the Charter School of San Diego and also “on loan” to Altus to provide support services to Audeo and Laurel. 
     
  • Also noted in the site visit report, the Audeo governing board has not met in closed session for at least the past three years. 
     
  • Altus, a private corporation, is the sole statutory member of Audeo, a public school.  Altus has the right to vote on the disposition of all or substantially all of Audeo’s assets, resulting in a private corporation having decision-making authority over a public school.  As a private corporation, the district does not have oversight over Altus.
