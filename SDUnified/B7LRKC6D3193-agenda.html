RECOMMENDATION: Approve the 2019-20 proposed English Learner Reclassification Criteria for SDUSD to reflect the changes of the State Board of Education (SBE) overall performance level (PL) 4 as the statewide standardized English Language Proficiency (ELP) (Criterion 1) for Reclassification. Criterion 1 is now standardized and will no longer be locally determined. Criteria 2, 3, and 4 continue to be locally determined.

 

San Diego Unified School District will continue using the following four criteria to establish Reclassification policies and procedures. 

  1. Assessment of ELP, using an objective assessment instrument, including, but not limited to, the state test of English Language Proficiency Assessments for California (ELPAC Overall PL 4 only); and
  2. Demonstration of student performance in basic skills in English compared against an empirically established range of performance in basic skill based on the performance of English proficient students of the same age (ELA SBA, FAST, aReading, Benchmark Advance Interim Assessment 3, Inspect ELA Interim Assessment, or DRA); and
  3. Teacher evaluation, including but not limited to, a review of the student's curriculum mastery ("C" or above for secondary students (6-12) and a "2" or above in the Elementary Language Portion of the Report Card (TK-5); and
    1. Protocol for Teacher of English Learners Observation (PTEL-O), an observation protocol for language designed to evaluate the student's English language proficiency; and
  4. Parent opinion and consultation.

The proposed 2019-20 revised Reclassification Criteria was discussed and voted on at the Executive DELAC meeting on September 19, 2019. 

 

BACKGROUND: On January 18, 2019, on the basis of the new 2018-19 Summative ELPAC threshold scores, the SBE approved the use of ELPAC overall performance level (PL) 4 as the statewide ELP criterion (Criterion 1) for Reclassification beginning with the 2018-19 Summative ELPAC administration for Kindergarten through Grade 12.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution in the matter of recognizing November 21, 2019, as National Family/Parent Involvement Day. The District recognizes the importance of National Family/Parent Involvement Day and its goal of increasing overall student achievement through family engagement.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: In 2012, the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs introduced the first National Parent Involvement day for San Diego Unified School District.

 

The San Diego Unified School District, the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs, and the broader education field recognize there are many significant caregivers in children’s lives not adequately represented by the term “parent,” therefore the term “family” is also used to celebrate this day.

 

The San Diego Unified District’s Family Engagement Department and the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs jointly support the district’s Local Control Accountability Plan (LCAP) Goal 5: Family and Community Engagement with Highly Regarded Neighborhood Schools that Serve Students, Families and Communities.

 

The San Diego Unified School District and the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs partner to recognize National Parent/Family Involvement Day at district schools and all of the existing avenues for family engagement.

 

The San Diego Unified School District and San Diego Unified Council of PTAs are catalyzed by more than 40 years of research confirmed by experience that strong family, school, and community partnerships to support student learning, both at school and at home, increase overall student achievement, attendance, credit acquisition, graduation rates, life outcomes, and school effectiveness.

 

The mission of PTA is to make every child’s potential a reality by engaging and empowering families and communities to advocate for all children.

 

Schools will host an event during the week of November 18-22, 2019, to celebrate National Parent/Family Involvement Day, to honor the powerful contributions that families make to support the success of students and to reinforce awareness of the invaluable role that families play in supporting student learning and public education, and honor their contributions to student success.

 

In addition to recognizing the role families play as invaluable allies in student success, secondary goals of National Parent/Family Involvement Day include identifying family and community leaders whose efforts will help schools increase family engagement, building strong partnerships linked to student learning.

  • Health Care and Life Sciences WBL and Certification Initiative: This project will sustain a model health careers pathway program that prepares at-risk high school students for good careers in the field. FACES San Diego operates in partnership with Rady Children’s Hospital and provides intensive support for high school students in the patient care pathway at two City Heights schools. This project will sustain the FACES program at those schools, provide healthcare rotations at two others, and provide health career information workshops at four other schools. 

These programs shall create, support, and expand high-quality career technical education programs at the K-12 level.

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The approval of the Proposed Project is subject to CEQA, however, it is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Sections 15301 (Class 1) – Existing Facilities and 15314 (Class 14) – Minor Additions to Schools.

 

Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of existing or former use. The key consideration is whether the project involves negligible or no expansion of an existing use. Examples cited by the CEQA guidelines relevant to the Proposed Project include interior or exterior alterations including interior partitions, plumbing, and electrical conveyances. The larger modernization elements associated with the Proposed Project that do not fit within a Class 1 Exemption would be exempt under the Class 14 exemption, as described below.

 

A Class 14 exemption consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or ten classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption (CEQA Guidelines §15314). The Proposed Project consists of school modernization, minor expansion, and upgrades that would not result in any increase in enrollment or classroom capacity beyond the criteria set forth in CEQA Guidelines Section 15314. The existing and projected number of students and classrooms is more fully discussed in Attachment A. Therefore, the application of a Class 14 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project. This analysis and conclusions is based on the Initial Study Checklist that was prepared for the Proposed Project by the district.

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of the existing school campus. The proposed modernization is consistent with the existing use on the project site. Furthermore, the Proposed Project would not extend into any offsite or open space areas that may be classified as an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed at the project site or in the project vicinity that, when combined with the construction timeframe of the Proposed Project, would result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. The Proposed Project includes modernization to an existing school. No work is proposed offsite that could result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas as a result of the Proposed Project. As such, there is nothing unusual about existing school site or surrounding area. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project would not result in the construction of new facilities that would directly, or indirectly, affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. The closest designated state scenic highway is the portion of State Route 125 between State Route 94 and Interstate 8, located approximately 4.5 miles northeast of the project site. Therefore, implementation of the Proposed Project would not substantially damage scenic resources within a state scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the California State Water Resources Control Board Geotracker database, the project site is not located on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. According to the CEQA Guidelines regarding historical resources, a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource amounts to a significant impact on the environment (Guidelines § 15064.5(b)). Accordingly, a substantial adverse change means demolition, destruction, relocation, or alteration of the resource or its immediate surroundings resulting in the significance of the resource being materially impaired. Furthermore, according to the National Register of Historic Places (NRHP) guidelines, properties completed at least 50 years ago must be considered for eligibility for designation in the NRHP. Morse High School was evaluated against the NRHP criteria to determine eligibility. It was concluded that Morse High School did not meet any of the eligibility criteria for designation in either the NRHP or the California Register of Historic Places, which uses the same criteria. Moreover, Morse High School did not meet any of the local eligibility criteria for designation in the City of San Diego Historic Resources Inventory. Finally, Morse High School does not maintain enough integrity to be eligible for listing at the national, state, or local levels. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Project.

BACKGROUND: This project consists of HVAC controls integration at nine sites: Curie, Holmes, Johnson, Kumeyaay, Nye, and Zamorano Elementary Schools; DePortola Middle School, and Harriet Tubman Village and Gompers Preparatory Academy charter schools (Proposed Project).

 

Pursuant to Public Contract Code section 20111(b), the district must publicly competitively bid any public project involving an expenditure of $15,000 or more. The notice calling for bids was published once a week for two successive weeks in accordance with Public Contract Code section 20112. Bids were due and opened on September 5, 2019. The district received a total of five bids from Climatec, LLC, Greater San Diego Air Conditioning Company, Inc., Metro Builders & Engineers Group, Ltd., My Electrician, Inc., Pacific West Industries, Inc., and Performance Automation Solutions, Inc. After evaluating the bids, staff determined My Electrician, Inc., to be the lowest responsive and responsible bidder.

 

Additionally, consistent with the May 10, 2011, board-approved resolution in support of service-disabled, veteran-owned businesses (SDVOB) and disabled veteran business enterprises (DVBE) which established a three percent required participation of DVBEs for all district construction contracting opportunities. The recommended contractor, My Electrician, Inc., submitted a 100 percent DVBE participation in its bid.

 

CEQA ANALYSIS: The Proposed Project is consistent with CEQA Guidelines Section 15301 (Class 1 Categorical Exemption), which exempts projects that include the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of use beyond that existing at the time of the lead agency's determination. The Proposed Project consists of modifications to existing electrical systems and would not result in the expansion of use. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 Categorical Exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.  

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2, and summarized below, prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of existing school sites. Installation of HVAC controls is consistent with existing uses at each of the existing school sites and implementation of the Proposed Project would not extend into any offsite or open space areas. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed at each school site or in the project vicinity that, when combined with the construction of the Proposed Project, would result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Once the Proposed Project has been constructed, school operations at each site would remain unchanged compared with existing conditions. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. No work associated with the Proposed Project is proposed offsite that could result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project would not result in the demolition of existing structures or the construction of new facilities that would directly or indirectly affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the California State Water Resources Control Board Geotracker database, there are no district school facilities located on any list compiled pursuant to section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. Historical Resources. According to the CEQA Guidelines regarding historical resources, a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource amounts to a significant impact on the environment (Guidelines § 15064.5(b)). Accordingly, a substantial adverse change means demolition, destruction, relocation, or alteration of the resource or its immediate surroundings resulting in the significance of the resource being materially impaired. Because the Proposed Project does not require the demolition of existing historical era buildings or structures and the HVAC controls would replace existing control units, the significance of a potentially historical resource will not be materially impaired, and no substantial adverse changes to the significance of a historical resource or significant impacts to the environment at each of the nine sites is anticipated. Additionally, the likelihood of impacting either prehistoric or historic archeological resources is negligible for the current undertaking because grading and trenching into non-native soils is not proposed. This exception does not apply to the project.

Approval of this item approves the Project as described, and authorizes and directs staff to file the attached Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk, and awards the contract to My Electrician, Inc.
