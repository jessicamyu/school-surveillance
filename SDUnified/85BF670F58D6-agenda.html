The Office of School Choice recommends the Board of Education deny the charter petition for Urban Discovery Academy (UDA). The Office of School Choice recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the Board’s decision to deny the charter petition. Urban Discovery petitioners submitted their petition for review to the Office of School Choice on March 4, 2008. A public hearing for the petition was held on April 8, 2008. Based on a departmental and legal staff review the petitioners were informed that staff would recommend denial of this petition based on outstanding concerns. The Urban Discovery Academy petition contains the following flaws, which support denial: 1) Petitioners are demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program set forth in the petition [E.C. 47605(b)(2)]; 2) The budget presented by petitioners would make the school insolvent, and 3) This petition would be an indirect conversion of a private school, Harborside, to a charter school, which is not allowed by law [E.C. 47602(b)]. The academic program does not indicate a comprehensive scope regarding the curriculum and educational program that the school would implement. The denial of this petition shall become effective immediately. Should the Board decide to approve the petition, the potential fiscal impact to SDUSD is an estimated loss of $840,021 generated in average daily attendance in the first year because SDUSD does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools. Agreement, effective May 14, 2008 through May 14, 2010, with Transworld Systems, Inc.(TSI) for collection of salary overpayments made to former District employees. Under this agreement the District may assign to TSI its rights to recover salary overpayments from former District employees. TSI may then take the actions described below to recover the overpayments. These collection actions may include the filing of a suit by TSI as plaintiff against the former employee debtor.

Through inquiry and analysis, the district’s 2006/07 external auditors identified overpayments as an area of internal control requiring the need for the district to put into place a proven recovery process. Following a discussion at the December 11, 2007 board meeting, board members requested information regarding the 2007 external audit findings on payroll overpayments. An in-depth report was prepared and delivered to board members on April 18. 2008.

Over the past year, Financial Operations staff have taken steps to address the causes of payroll overpayments, as well as recovery efforts and met with a handful of collection agencies and TSI was selected to provide this service. TSI provides a web-based program that allows district staff to monitor and manage collection activity. The district will be able to view, add, and make changes to collection accounts as needed. As well as be able to produce reports to track accounts receivable activity.

TSI provides Phase I, Phase II, and Phase III collections. In Phase I, TSI contacts the overpaid former employee via U.S. Mail. Five attempts are made over a 60-day period prior to moving the account to Phase II. Once in Phase II, TSI will pursue collections with verbal contact to the overpaid party. Upon the district’s recommendation, the account will be forwarded to Phase III collections. It is at this point in the process that legal action is pursued and managed by TSI.

TSI will manage 250 accounts for a total cost of $2,875. Additional accounts can be purchased for $11.50 per account. TSI guarantees they will collect a minimum of two times the total cost. During Phase I, the district receives 100 percent of the funds collected. During Phase II, the district will receive 50 percent of the funds collected. When an account is referred to the agency’s legal department, the district 50 percent portion will be reduced by any court-associated costs. Any account that does not generate a modest return to the district will not be referred for legal action. Court costs constitute 1 – 2 percent of collected amounts.
