BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue our improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting districtwide, we are implementing a new process streamlining our Independent Contractor Agreements. The goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective deputy or chief, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve agreement with American Institutes for Research (AIR) to evaluate the effects of Check & Connect on high school graduation. (PS-11-1106-08).  Site-based mentors will be assigned at ten district high schools, to provide personalized support to identified ninth graders and increase their engagement in school. Participating high schools include, Clairemont, Crawford, Kearny, Lincoln, Madison, Mission Bay, Morse, Point Loma, San Diego, and Serra high schools. Agreement effective from March 1, 2011 to February 28, 2015.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Proposed Spend Fiscal Year 2011-12 $251,476.00; Proposed Spend Fiscal Year 2012-13 $297,500.00; Proposed Spend Fiscal Year 2013-14 $297,500.00; Proposed Spend Fiscal Year 2014-15 $128,793.00; Total Spending Authorization: $975,269.00.  Budget string: TBD


This subcontract is a part of AIR’s Prime Contract R305A110252, issued by U. S. Department of Education, National Center for Education Research. PL 107-279 Education Science Reform Act of 2002.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT:  Program Manager, Dropout Prevention will assist with the implementation of this grant. The grant will pay for .5 FTE for a Lead Mentor and ten .5 FTE mentor positions to serve each of the ten high schools as per the study specifications

 

BACKGROUND:  The American institutes for Research, along with IMPAQ International and University of Minnesota, will partner with the district on a study to demonstrate the effects of the Check & Connect program on general education students at risk of dropping out of high school. The district, in conjunction with AIR, IMPAQ International and the University of Minnesota, will identify a sample of at-risk ninth graders in ten district high schools. Approximately 300 students, 30 from each high school, will be randomly assigned (by lottery) to participate in the Check & Connect program from ninth grade through their scheduled graduation from high school to receive personal mentoring. Data will be used to evaluate the effects of these supports on student engagement, attendance, academic persistence and performance, and rates of dropping out and graduating from high school. The ten high schools identified to receive services are Clairemont, Crawford, Kearny, Lincoln, Madison, Mission Bay, Morse, Point Loma, San Diego, and Serra high schools.

Any staff member that observes, overhears or otherwise witnesses bullying (including cyber-bullying), harassment, or intimidation, or to whom such actions have been reported must take prompt and appropriate action to stop the behavior and to prevent its reoccurrence as detailed in the applicable Administrative Procedure.  Students who observe, overhear, or otherwise witness such actions must, and parents/district visitors are encouraged to report the behaviors to a staff member.  At each school, the principal or principal’s designee is responsible for receiving and promptly investigating complaints alleging violations of this policy.  Any form of retaliation in response to a report of such acts is prohibited. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  No additional district staff will be needed to implement this policy; existing staff members will be held accountable for its enforcement.

A Safe Schools Task Force that includes district staff and community members (Attachment 2) was formed to develop the policy and procedure. The Safe Schools Task Force met on January 5, January 19, February 2, February 16, March 2, and March 16, 2011. An update on the Safe Schools Task Force work was presented to the board on January 25, 2011. Information on the Safe Schools Task Force is posted on the district website.

 

Upon board approval of the proposed policy language, the Safe Schools Task Force will begin developing the associated procedure.  It is intended that the associated Administrative Procedure will be developed and presented to the board for approval by June 30, 2011, for implementation in the 2011-12 school year, along with a proposal for a cultural sensitivity training program for all counseling, certificated and classified staff as called for in the resolution.
