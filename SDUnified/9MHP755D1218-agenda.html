Academics

 

A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school.
     
  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
     
  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
     
  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.
     
  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(h).

King-Chavez Arts Academy serves Grades 3-5. King-Chavez Athletics Academy serves Grades 3-5. King-Chavez Primary Academy serves Grades K-2. All three charter schools are located together at 415 31st Street (92102), which is a district site within the San Diego High cluster.

The King-Chavez governing board regularly meets 4-5 times per year on the third Wednesday of the month at 4:30 p.m. The location of the meeting rotates among the King-Chavez charter schools.

As described in the respective charter renewal documents opportunities for parent involvement include the following:

  • The King-Chavez parent groups representing all of six charter schools select one parent to sit on the charter governing board.
     
  • Membership in the Parent-Teacher Organization (PTO), which meets weekly. The PTO provides opportunities for parents and charter employees to communicate about ongoing issues, plan school-wide events, fundraise, and provide classroom support as needed. Parent volunteers are led by the Parent Volunteer Coordinator who organizes a variety of volunteer opportunities, including assembling materials, assisting in the classroom, participating in the parent security team, or participating in school events.
     
  • A group of parents have been elected to participate in School Site Council (SSC) and English Language Advisory Committee (ELAC). The SSC focuses on school financials and academics and works alongside administration in the decision-making process of King-Chavez. The ELAC committee works alongside the principal, staff, and SSC on programs, services, and goals for English Learners. The ELAC committee also supports with the development of each school’s needs assessment, and ways to promote high attendance rates.

Finances

 

King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy offer eligible employees to participate in California State Teachers’ Retirement System (CalSTRS), California Public Employees' Retirement System (CalPERS), and federal social security.

 

King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy have been timely in all CalSTRS and CalPERS payments and reports due to the district. 

Based on locations, respective budgets, grade levels, and programs offered, the enrollment projections of King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy have been evaluated and appear to be within normal range compared to other charter schools and District schools. The estimated California Department of Education (CDE) funding rates and Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) factor, provided by School Services of California’s Financial Projection Dartboard, have been used to estimate revenue for King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy based on the projected Average Daily Attendance (ADA). This revenue projection has been compared to the projected revenue included in each charter school’s projected budget and no significant variances have been found. Other expenses unique to charter schools have also been examined and found to be comprehensive and appropriate. Over the past four years, King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy have addressed all required annual financial audit and reporting to the district.

 

Based on the respective projected enrollment, budget, expenses, and cash flow submitted in the charter renewal documents, King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy have been found to be financially viable. In addition, the district has examined whether King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy maintain an adequate reserve for economic uncertainty greater than 3% of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater, as another consideration of its financial viability. According to the financial information provided in the charter renewal documents, King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy have maintained adequate reserve for economic uncertainty.

 

Attached are documents that compare the charter schools’ budgets to actual expenses. The actual expenses for 2010/2011, 2011/2012, 2012/2013 and 2013/2014 were all greater than budgeted amounts for King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy.

 

In summary, King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy appear financially sound. At the end of 2013/14, per the unaudited reports for these charter schools, the ending fund balances (EFB) were $204,760 / $243,160 / $1,030,741 which exceeded the minimum reserve amounts.

 

Conclusion

 

The charter renewal documents and special education agreements submitted by King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy School are attached. Staff recommends the board approve the renewal of charter petitions and special education agreements for King-Chavez Arts Academy, King-Chavez Athletics Academy and King-Chavez Primary Academy for five-year terms beginning July 1, 2015, and ending June 30, 2020.

BACKGROUND: On the November 4, 2014 general election ballot, the ballot included two trustee areas for the Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District, sub-districts B and C.  The County Registrar of Voters has certified the official election results of the November 4, 2014 election and those results are attached to this agenda item.  The board must accept the official results of the November 4, 2014 General Election.  While the district's Board Services Office was informed via email that the Registrar of Voters Office had certified the election results, the official results were not received by the district in time to agendize for the board's December 8, 2014 meeting.
