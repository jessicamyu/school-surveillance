RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and approve submittal of a general waiver request to the State Board of Education (SBE) for Learning Choice Academy, and adopt a resolution approving submittal of the waiver.

 

Pursuant to Education Code section 33050, a school district, after a public hearing, may request on behalf of one of its charter schools, that the State Board of Education waive the application of the Education Code and/or the California Code of Regulations.  LCA missed the February 1, 2015, deadline to submit its determination of funding request to the State Board of Education, and is seeking the approval of the Governing Board of the San Diego Unified School District to request a general waiver from the State Board of Education.  LCA is seeking to waive the second sentence of California Code of Regulations Section 11963.6(c) regarding the timeline for submitting a funding determination request, as shown below.

 

11963.6 (c)  Any determination of funding request approved by the State Board of Education for an existing nonclassroom-based charter school from the 2006-07 fiscal year forward shall be prospective (not for the current year), in increments of a minimum of two years and a maximum of five years in length.  Beginning with the 2007-08 fiscal year, nonclassroom-based charter schools that had a funding determination in the prior year must submit a funding determination request by February 1 of the fiscal year prior to the year the funding determination will be effective, when a new request is required under these regulations.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The Charter Schools Facility Committee has reviewed and approved these charter school facility projects.

 

BACKGROUND: On September 4, 2012, the board passed a resolution establishing the San Diego Unified School District Charter School Facility Committee (CSFC) for the purpose of providing recommendations on the allocation of $350 million of Proposition Z funds set aside for charter school capital facility projects.  Such projects may include acquisition, construction, reconstruction, rehabilitation, or replacement of charter school facilities, including the furnishing and equipping of charter school facilities.

 

Charter school applications are reviewed by district staff for conformity to the ranking rubric established by the CSFC, and those found to be compliant are then rated according to the priorities established by the committee.  The CSFC subsequently conducts an open discussion on the merits of each project at its regularly scheduled meetings.  These discussion are open to the public. 

 

On February 4, 2014, the board adopted a policy requiring district staff to provide input as well as comment on the spending recommendations from the Charter School Facility Committee prior to any board action. 

 

On February 4, 2014, the board approved CSFC recommendations to establish project budgets for twelve charter schools totaling $41,750,000.

 

On June 3, 2014, the board approved CSFC recommendations to establish project budgets for four charter schools totaling $2,650,000.

 

On July 29, 2014, the board approved CSFC recommendation to establish project budget for one charter school totaling $50,000.

 

On February 3, 2015, the board approved the CSFC recommendation to increase the project budget of a charter school in the amount of $4,600,000.

 

On January 21, 2015 and April 30, 2015, the CSFC conducted open discussions on the merits of each project listed above at its regularly scheduled meetings.  The meetings were publicly noticed in accordance with the Ralph Brown Act.  The discussions were open to the public whose comment/input was invited.  At the conclusion of each of these meetings, the CSFC voted to recommend these projects to the board for review and approval.  A tabular summary of the staff recommendation, consistency with the board's charter policy, estimate, and description for each project is attached as Attachment A.  Attachment B includes a narrative overview of each proposed project, including a summary of staff participation in the project's development, and staff comment regarding the project's feasibility.  Attachment C is a listing of board-approved projects to date.
