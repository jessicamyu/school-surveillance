RECOMMENDATION:  Approve the proposed 2017-18 Title I Eligibility Ranking - Scenario 1. This report presents the 2017-18 Title I Eligibility Ranking and school participation. There are fourteen schools that no longer meet the forty percent eligibility threshold. Staff recommends these fourteen schools be grandfathered for the 2017-18 school year.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  The District Advisory Council (DAC) discussed the Title I ranking at their November 16, 2016, December 14, 2016, and January 18, 2017 General Membership meetings. The DAC recommends Scenario 2, as shared in the December 2016 DAC Chairperson report.

 

BACKGROUND: The Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) of 1965 was reauthorized as of December 10, 2015 as the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA).  Title I, one of several programs under ESSA, provides funds to schools to help disadvantaged children meet challenging academic standards.  The law intends that the funds go to the district and schools that have the highest concentrations of poverty.  Title I requires funding to be allocated to a district based on the number of "children in poverty" residing within the district boundary.  The federal census is used to identify children ages 5 through 17 living in poverty who are included in the calculation of Title I entitlement.

 

The 2017-18 Title I Ranking Report includes a few changes from prior years.

  • Previously there were Provision 2 and Non-Provision 2 schools. Now there are provision 2, non-provision 2 and CEP schools.

There are two Federal programs where all students at a school eat for free, with no meal application required. These programs are: Provision 2 and CEP*. The data collection for both of these programs for Title I allocation is the same. The forms of data collected are: direct certification**, alternate meal eligibility forms, and categorical (homeless, foster, and migrant) eligibility to determine percentage of meal eligibility.

 

All other schools follow traditional meal application procedure and are referred to as Non-Provision 2 Schools. The data collection for Title I allocation is meal applications, direct certification, and categorical (homeless, foster, and migrant) eligibility to determine percentage of meal eligibility.

 

The meal eligibility data collected through these three programs is a factor in determining the students in poverty component of the LCFF State allocation to our District.

 

*Community Eligibility Provision (CEP): This District began a pilot with 34 schools in Fall of 2016. These schools were previously Provision 2 Schools. With this group of schools, there is no visible change for the school community. The only difference is a potential increase in funding and a streamlined process for administering the program.

 

**Direct Certification: The District receives data from the State and County for families identified as receiving support from program such as; Supplemental Nutrition Assistance program (SNAP) and Temporary Assistance for needy Families (TANF).

 

The proposed 2017-18 Title I Ranking report allocates Title I funding to all district schools with a 2016-17 meal eligibility percentage of 40 percent or higher and the fourteen additional schools recommended to be grandfathered.  The list of sites recommended to be grandfathered are as follows: Barnard, Salk, McKinley, Hickman, Loma Portal, Green, Dana, Doyle, Hage, elementary schools; Correia, Standley, Pershing middle schools; Point Loma, and University City high schools. The total allocation for these fourteen schools is: $246,302 for Scenario I and $400,659 for Scenario 2.

 

The total 2016-17 Title I Entitlement is $37,937,634. Due to changes in ESSA guidelines and poverty decline based on 2015 California census data, the 2017-18 Title I Entitlement for San Diego Unified could potentially decrease. The projected 2017-18 Entitlement should be known in March 2017.

LID is an innovative storm water management approach with a basic principle that is modeled after nature: manage rainfall at the source using uniformly distributed decentralized micro-scale controls. LID's goal is to mimic a site's predevelopment hydrology by using design techniques that infiltrate, filter, store, evaporate, and detain runoff close to its source. Techniques are based on the premise that storm water management should not be seen as storm water disposal. The storm water, exhibit design construction improvements will feed the Chollas Creek watershed. The physical improvements augment bond-funded improvements planned at each of these sites. In addition, students will be provided related STEM opportunities in the Earthlab at each schools. The education and outreach initiatives are funded through the Drought Response Outreach Program at Schools (DROPS) Grant Funds.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve Fourth Amendment to Agreement with Pacific Bell Telephone Company, doing business as AT&T DataComm (AT&T), to continue providing electronic components and pathways for Local Area Network (LAN) upgrades at selected district schools (P89-020-GC).  Authorize the execution of any documents necessary to effectuate this Fourth Amendment.  The Fourth Amendment extends the term of the Agreement two years and increases the funding by an additional $25 million. The term of the Agreement is extended from February 10, 2009 through February 9, 2019.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not-to-exceed amount: $25,000,000.00 for a new total not-to-exceed Agreement amount of $53.2 million.  Budget string: 9xxx-90403-00-6200-8500-0000-21400-86xx and 9xxx-9011x-00-6200-8500-0000-21312-86xx Propositions S and Z

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  On May 7, 1997, the federal government implemented the Telecommunications Act of 1996. This Program (often referred to as E-rate) was designed to ensure that eligible schools have access to modern telecommunication services, which includes replacing infrastructure and LAN electronic components. Subsequently, district staff filed the necessary paperwork to enroll in the E-rate Program and take advantage of the various discounts.

 

In accordance with the Agreement provisions, the services were to be furnished in accordance with the California Multiple Award Schedule (CMAS) and California Public Contract Code section 12100.7, et seq.

 

On February 10, 2009, the district entered into an Agreement with AT&T, Inc. in order to qualify for the available funds and provide the technology services required under the Proposition S bond measure.  The Agreement specified a maximum consideration of $13,200,000 and initial three-year term of February 10, 2009 through February 9, 2012, with an option to extend up to two years at district’s discretion. The Agreement also stated that the contract could be renewed at the end of the five-year term for an additional negotiated term.

 

In November 2009, the district was awarded $40 million in E-rate funds to provide technology upgrades at 76 district schools that qualified under the E-rate Program.

 

The First Amendment was approved by the board on July 12, 2011, to incorporate a fourth CMAS contract to the Agreement which allowed the district flexibility in purchasing Cisco products.  There was no cost associated with the First Amendment.

 

In the fall 2011, the district was awarded $7.7 million in E-rate funds for an additional 11 district schools that qualified under the E-rate Program.  A Second Amendment was approved by the board on December 13, 2011, to extend the Agreement through February 9, 2014, increase the consideration by an additional $3 million and update the contract provisions to current standards.

 

On November 6, 2012, Proposition Z was approved by the voters of San Diego, which provided the district with an opportunity to leverage Proposition Z funds to qualify for and accept an additional $52.3 million in E-rate funds for specified projects.  On June 18, 2013, the board authorized the use of $19.2 million from Proposition Z bonds sales for these projects.

 

In the spring 2013, the district was awarded $43.5 million in E-rate funds for 88 district schools to receive technology upgrades.  Thereafter, a Third Amendment was approved by the board on December 10, 2013, to extend the Agreement an additional three years with an option to extend up to two years in order to take advantage of the additional E-rate funds available, increase the consideration of the Agreement by an additional $12 million and update the contract provisions to current standards.

 

Currently, the district desires to exercise the option to extend the Agreement an additional two years through February 9, 2019,  in order to take advantage of the additional E-rate funds available.  The consideration of the Agreement needs to be increased by an additional $25 million and various administrative provisions need to be updated in order to support the planned technology work to be completed during the term of the Agreement, as amended.

 

The technology upgrades associated with the initial 76 schools are 100 percent complete, and for the next group of 11 schools the upgrades are 90 percent complete, and 5 percent complete for the subsequent 88 schools.  Many district schools have received more than one E-rate funded technology upgrade.   Recently, the district received an additional E-rate award of $11 million for technology upgrades at 176 district schools.  This Fourth Amendment will allow the district to qualify for E-rate available discounts estimated at $54 million for technology upgrades at the 176 district schools listed in the attachment.

 

Schools will remain in operation while construction work is being performed.  Construction phasing and other strategies are employed to minimize disruption of school operations so that teaching and learning are not compromised.

BACKGROUND: On August 15, 2012, the district entered into a Ground Lease with COMM22 Senior Housing, LP (COMM22) for the Commercial Street project.  To date, two amendments were approved by the board:

  • November 18, 2014 – First Amendment (SV-15-0708-06) acknowledged the prepayment of rent and updated the legal description of the property.
  • May 24, 2016 – Second Amendment (SV-15-0708-06) affirmed the rent payments made by COMM22 and application of rent credit allowable under the original agreement.

The Ground Lease anticipated that at various stages of development, COMM22 would refinance the project.   • Sets out the process for the lender to take over Ground Lease in case of a breach by COMM22.
  • Places restrictions on the use of the property – limited to affordable senior housing.

The Commercial Street project is consistent with the district’s Real Estate Utilization Strategy and guiding principles adopted by the board in 2014.  The district’s fee interest (ownership) of the developed neighborhood center generates long-term revenue for the district.

BACKGROUND:  On May 7, 1997, the federal government implemented the Telecommunications Act of 1996. This Program (often referred to as E-rate) was designed to ensure that eligible schools have access to modern telecommunication services, which includes replacing infrastructure and LAN electronic components.  Subsequently, district staff filed the necessary paperwork to enroll in the E-rate Program and take advantage of the various discounts.

​

In accordance with the Agreement provisions, the services were to be furnished in accordance with the California Multiple Award Schedule (CMAS) and California Public Contract Code section 12100.7, et seq.

 

On February 10, 2009, the district entered into an Agreement with Vector Resources, Inc. in order to qualify for the available funds and provide the technology services required under the Proposition S bond measure.  The Agreement specified a maximum consideration of $35,900,000 and initial three-year term commencing on February 10, 2009, and terminating on February 9, 2012, with an option to extend up to two years at district’s election.  The Agreement also stated that the contract could be renewed at the end of the five-year term for an additional negotiated term.

 

In November 2009, the district was awarded $40 million in E-rate funds to provide technology upgrades at 76 district schools that qualified under the E-rate Program. 

 

The First Amendment was approved by the board on July 12, 2011, to replace two CMAS contracts to the Agreement that had expired to allow the Contractor to provide LAN services required.  There was no cost associated with the First Amendment.

 

In the fall 2011, the district was awarded $7.7 million in E-rate funds for an additional 11 district schools that qualified under the E-rate Program.  A Second Amendment was approved by the board on December 13, 2011, to extend the Agreement through February 9, 2014, and update the Contractor’s address listed in Article 33 entitled “Notices”. 

 

On November 6, 2012, Proposition Z was approved by the voters of San Diego, which provided the district with an opportunity to leverage Proposition Z funds to qualify for and accept an additional $52.3 million in E-rate funds for specified projects.  On June 18, 2013, the board authorized the use of $19.2 million from Proposition Z bonds sales for these projects.

 

In the spring 2013, the district was awarded $43.5 million for 88 schools.  Thereafter, a Third Amendment was approved by the board on November 19, 2013, to extend the Agreement an additional three years with an option to extend up to two years in order to take advantage of the additional E-rate funds available, increase the consideration of the Agreement by an addition $12 million and update various administrative sections of the Agreement to current standards.

 

Currently, the district desires to exercise the option to extend the Agreement an additional two years through February 9, 2019, in order to take advantage of the additional E-rate funds available.  The consideration of the Agreement  needs to be increased by an additional $36,999,990 and various administrative provisions need to be updated in order to support the planned technology work to be completed during the term of the Agreement, as amended.

 

The technology upgrades associated with the initial 76 schools are 100% complete, and for the next group of 11 schools the upgrades are 90 percent complete, and 5 percent complete for the subsequent 88 schools.  Many district schools have received more than one E-rate funded technology upgrade.  Recently, the district received an additional E-rate award of $11 million for technology upgrades at 176 district schools.  This Fourth Amendment will allow the district to qualify for E-rate available discounts estimated at $54 million for technology upgrades at the 176 district schools listed in the attachment.

 

Schools will remain in operation while construction work is being performed.  Construction phasing and other strategies are employed to minimize disruption of school operations so that teaching and learning are not compromised.
