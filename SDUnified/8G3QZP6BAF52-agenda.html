4. Presentation of Jones Lang LaSalle Americas, Inc., Consultant's Report on Real Estate Portfolio Assessment Review; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-9; OE-10)
Meeting
May 10, 2011 - Special Meeting, 9:30 A.M.
Type
Action, Information

RECOMMENDATION: Accept the Jones Lang LaSalle Americas, Inc., Portfolio Assessment Review Report.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None at this time.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The Advisory Committee on Utilization of Excess School Property (ACUESP) meets on a regular basis to review and recommend to the board regarding the disposition of school district property.  The board adopted the Property Inventory and Property Management Plan, as recommended by the ACUESP, on March 10. 2011.

 

BACKGROUND: Jones Lang LaSalle was engaged to conduct a Portfolio Assessment of the San Diego Unified School District’s surplus properties. Twenty-seven properties were deemed surplus by the district. The properties included remnant parcels (located adjacent to currently operating schools), administrative facilities, leased and ground leased properties, and entitled and unentitled raw land. Jones Lang LaSalle was tasked with providing valuations and recommendations for each property.

 

Methodology--The Jones Lang LaSalle team collaborated with district representatives to conduct preliminary due diligence on each site. This effort included the following:

  • Site visits of each property;
  • Review of district documentation;
  • Evaluation of income and expenses; and
  • Identification of appropriate comparable properties.

The results of this review were consolidated in a concise, two-page Property Overview Report for each property. The reports for the 27 properties are contained in this document.

 

The recommendations for each property were arrived at after consultation and review with district representatives. The following preferences were used in determining recommendations:

 

1. Short-term disposition potential;

2. Long-term revenue generation; and

3. Reduction of ongoing expenses.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve Agreement of Purchase and Sale with the San Diego Community College District (SDCCD) of .99 acres of district-owned, unimproved land located at 3900 Armstrong Street, San Diego, CA, APN 427-020-30.  

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Upon close of escrow, income will be received by the district in the amount of $1.6 million, or the amount determined by an updated appraisal, whichever is greater.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Prior to the board's May 26, 2009 action to adopt the resolution of intention to sell the property, the Advisory Committee on Utilization of Excess School Property (ACUESP) was consulted.  

 

BACKGROUND:  On May 26, 2009, the board approved a resolution of intention to sell a .99 acre parcel of land located at 3900 Armstrong Street, currently a parking lot at Mesa College, and located within land owned by SDCCD.

 

Previously, the board had directed staff to secure a long-term lease (20-25 years) of SDCCD property for the MET Program prior to closing escrow on the .99 acre parcel of property.  The board has now removed the condition to execute a long-term lease with the SDCCD prior to the sale of the .99 acre parcel.
