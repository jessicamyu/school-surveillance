RECOMMENDATIONS:  UPDATED 5/10/17 5:55 PM-THE DISTRICT RECEIVED NOTIFICATION FROM THE PARAEDUCATOR UNIT FOLLOWING THIS AGENDA BEING PUBLISHED THAT THEIR MEMBERS RATIFIED THE AGREEMENT REGARDING A FURLOUGH, INCLUDED AT ITEM B2, THEREFORE NO ACTION IS REQUIRED ON THIS ITEM

 

Approve the reduction in work year for Paraeducators. Effective August 1, 2017, a permanent reduction in work year shall be implemented as follows:

A. Traditional Work Year Calendars

  • 207-day calendars shall be reduced by 11 days, consisting of 6 days during winter break and 5 days during spring break. Should winter break exceed 6 days, the parties will continue to follow the provisions of Article 12. Effective August 1, 2017, the work year shall be 196 days.
     
  • 204-day calendars shall be reduced by 11 days, consisting of 6 days during winter break and 5 days during spring break. Should winter break exceed 6 days, the parties will continue to follow the provisions of Article 12. Effective August 1, 2017, the work year shall be 193 days.
     
  • 163-day calendars with either Wednesdays or Fridays off shall be reduced by 8 days, consisting of 4 days during winter break and 4 days during spring break. The parties will continue to follow the provisions of Article 12 for any additional days during winter break. Effective August 1, 2017, the work year shall be 155 days.
     
  • 239-day work year calendars shall be reduced by 13 days, consisting of 6 days during winter break, 5 days during spring break, and 2 days to be determined by the district based on student need.  Effective August 1, 2017, the work year shall be 226 days.

B. Year-Round Work Year Calendars

  • 204-day calendars shall be reduced by 11 days, consisting of 6 days during winter break and 5 days during spring break. Should winter break exceed 6 days, the parties will continue to follow the provisions of Article 12. Effective August 1, 2017, the work year shall be 193 days.
     
  • 163-day calendars with either Wednesdays or Fridays off shall be reduced by 8 days, consisting of 4 days during winter break and 4 days during spring break. The parties will continue to follow the provisions of Article 12 for any additional days during winter break. Effective August 1, 2017, the work year shall be 155 days.
     
  • 239-day work year calendars shall be reduced by 13 days, consisting of 6 days during winter break, the first 5 days during spring break, and 2 additional days during the spring break period.  Effective August 1, 2017, the work year shall be 226 days.

C. 12-Month Work Year Calendars

  • 12-month work year calendars shall be reduced by 14 days, consisting of 6 days during winter break, 5 days during spring break, and 3 days during the week of Thanksgiving. Should winter break exceed 6 days, no additional adjustments will be made.
    - Unit members working a 12-month, work year calendar at a child development center, shall be reduced by 6 days during winter break.
     
  • 12-month modified work year calendars shall be reduced by 8 days, consisting of 5 days during spring break and 3 days during the week of Thanksgiving.

The parties will continue to negotiate the impacts and effects of this reduction in work year.

 

The reduction in work year shall be permanent and the salary schedules updated accordingly.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The proposed solutions total $4.3 million. The approval of the reduced work year is the implementation of one of the budget solutions approved by the board on February 28, 2017, and is taken in order to prepare a balanced budget for 2017-18.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: The work year for Paraeducator bargaining unit employees will be reduced. The student instructional year of 180 days, as well as the board-approved instructional calendars, are not changed by the reduction in work year for these employee groups.  

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district has been grappling with a variety of structural challenges including declining enrollment, pension contribution increases, labor market pressures, and mandates from the state and federal governments. Stability can be achieved but it requires efficiently using available resources in order to focus on student achievement. As reported at the Second Interim Budget Update, the projected budget deficit for 2017-18 is $124.4 million.

 

Approximately 95 percent of the district’s unrestricted budget is dedicated to staff, including salary and benefit costs. In order to balance the 2017-18 budget and reduce the district’s structural deficit, the district must take action to allow for necessary reductions in the work year.

RECOMMENDATIONS: 

  1. Adopt as the final decision of the Board of Education the attached decision of the Administrative Law Judge, in the matter of the reduction in force of certificated employees (Exhibit A); and 
     
  2. Approve issuance of final notice of the termination, effective commencing the 2017-2018 school year, of the services of those permanent certificated employees listed in Exhibit B, who requested a hearing in regard to said termination and are referenced in said final decision of the board; and to those permanent certificated employees listed in Exhibit C, who did not request a hearing on the issue of cause for termination of their services and are, therefore, not referenced in said final decision.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  As the board and public are aware, the district is facing a significant budget challenge this year. The district spends approximately 93 percent of its budget in personnel. As a result, on February 28, 2017, the Board of Education approved a resolution eliminating and reducing various certificated services within the district.

 

Certificated employees whose services were recommended for termination were notified of this recommendation and were notified of their right to a hearing to determine if cause existed for termination of their services. For permanent certificated employees who requested a hearing, the hearing on the matter was held on April 10, 11, and 12, 2017, before a state administrative law judge. On May 5, 2017, the administrative law judge issued his proposed decision on the matter. Pursuant to Education Code section 44949, the attached decision (Exhibit A) is recommended to be adopted as the final decision of the board in this matter.

 

Based upon and consistent with the aforementioned decision, following application of tie-breaking and skip criteria, and offsets for based upon credential match, pursuant to Education Code 44955, staff recommends the board approve giving final notice of the termination, effective commencing the 2017-2018 school year, of the services of the permanent certificated employees listed in Exhibits B and C.
