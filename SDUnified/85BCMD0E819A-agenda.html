On December 17, 2007, the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) released revenue procedure 2007-71 covering comprehensive requirements under IRS Code section 403(b). These regulations go into effect January 1, 2009, and are the first comprehensive section 403(b) regulations since 1964.

The Internal Revenue Service provided model plan language that any public school employer may use to comply with the written plan requirement of the regulations. If the public school employer adopts the model language on a word-for-word basis or adopts an amendment that is substantially similar in all material respects, it can rely on the model language as if it were a favorable private letter ruling.

The district is using the model plan for its formal written plan document. As such, there is no need to apply for a favorable letter ruling. The IRS allows the removal of a section without affecting the favorable ruling status. The district has removed the section of the model plan that allows loans. The new compliance requirements on tracking payments and defaults cannot be met by our third-party administrators or district staff. The removal of the loan provision in the 403(b) plan document makes the document consistent with the 457(b) plan document which also prohibits loans. This amendment is necessary to: (1) extend the term of the agreement an additional year, (2) increase the consideration to a maximum of $3.5 million. Approval of this amendment will allow the district to complete architectural plans and specifications for three projects that will replace aging portable classrooms with new state-of-the-art, two-story, permanent classroom buildings and relieve overcrowded campuses. The State of California Office of Public School Construction (OPSC) is providing matching fund grants for these projects with the stipulation that the designs are completed and approved by the Division of State Architect in a short time frame. Approval of this contract extension will help to enable district to claim its share of state school facilities bond funded grants that extend the purchasing power of local facilities funds.

Under Government Code section 4525, et seq.,architectural selection is based on best value/qualifications. District architect agreements are set up as a form of Indefinite Delivery Indefinite Quantity (IDIQ) contract in which tasks are issued against the agreement on a project-by-project basis as the need arises. This contract vehicle ensures flexibility and cost savings to the district.

The first three tasked projects to be awarded under this IDIQ contract extension will allow the district to obtain state matching funds for projects to be constructed pursuant to the Overcrowded Relief Grant (“ORG”) including: (1) a new, 20-classroom building at Hoover High School; (2) a new, 8-classroom building at Point Loma High School; and (3) a new, 16-classroom building at San Diego High School.

The original contract agreement does not have sufficient capacity to complete the projects. Several other architectural firms the district works with were contacted but all of them were too busy to take on the projects and complete them within the timeframe required. GKK has the capability and requisite expertise to handle projects of this scope. The design fees proposed are fair and reasonable and well within district parameters of approximately 10 percent of expected project construction cost. Timing is crucial as the district needs to qualify for the matching funds, otherwise the funds will be committed to other school districts. With construction costs of the three projects approximating $24 million, the district estimates receipt of matching funds from the OPSC in the amount of $9.3 million.

Currently, the district has limited in-house architectural capacity and is unable to assume design efforts of this magnitude.
