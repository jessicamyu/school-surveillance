RECOMMENDATION: Approve the renewal charter petition for Audeo Charter School (Audeo) for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2011, and expiring on June 30, 2016, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

 

BACKGROUND: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education approve the renewal charter petition for Audeo Charter School (Audeo) for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2011, and expiring on June 30, 2016, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition.  The Office of Charter Schools recommends the adoption of the staff report as findings in support of the board’s determination of the renewal of the charter petition.  Audeo was originally approved to be a charter school on June 12, 2001, by the board and has been operating as a charter school that offers Grades 6-12 in the district for nine years.  The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective on July 1, 2011, and will expire on June 30, 2016.  District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met requirements to continue performing as a charter school in the district.    

 

If the board desires additional options beyond approval, these options could be:

  • Approve the charter petition with conditions. In essence, the petition is approved with conditions set by the board.
  • Deny the charter petition.  The board would need to identify written findings to support a decision to deny the charter renewal. 

Academic Performance Summary:  Audeo has met the academic performance requirements required for renewal by charter law.  The school received a Similar Schools Rank of 10 during the 2009-10 school year and a Similar Schools Rank of 9 for both the 2008-09 and 2007-08 school years.  Student academic achievement at Audeo is at least equal to district schools where students would have otherwise attended.  Audeo has met the state required academic performance requirements to be eligible for charter renewal.

RECOMMENDATION: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District approve with conditions the petition to establish the Old Town Charter School (OTA) for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2011 and expiring June 30, 2016.  A public hearing for the petition was held on June 1, 2010.  Staff is recommending the approval of this charter petition with the following conditions:

  • Element 2 (Measurable Pupil Outcomes): Charter needs to be modified to include clearly measurable, quantifiable pupil outcomes;
  • Element 3 (Method Used to Measure Student Outcomes): Charter needs to clearly identify the accommodations that will be made for English Language Learner (ELL) and Special Education (SPED) students for testing purposes.
  • Element 4 (Governance): OTA revise the bylaws to state that all meetings will be held within the jurisdictional boundaries of the district, subject to applicable exceptions set forth in the Brown Act;
  • Element 8 (Admissions): OTA charter must be revised to state that in the event OTA applies for the California Public Charter School Grant Program (PCSGP), the 10% exemption will apply to children of founders and “faculty” (rather than “employees”);

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

BACKGROUND: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District approve the petition to establish the Old Town Academy (OTA) Charter School, with the following conditions, for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2011 and expiring June 30, 2016:

  • Element 2 (Measurable Pupil Outcomes): Charter needs to be modified to include clearly measurable, quantifiable pupil outcomes;
  • Element 3 (Method Used to Measure Student Outcomes): Charter needs to clearly identify the accommodations that will be made for English Language Learner (ELL) and Special Education (SPED) students for testing purposes;
  • Element 4 (Governance): OTA revise the bylaws to state that all meetings will be held within the jurisdictional boundaries of the district, subject to applicable exceptions set forth in the Brown Act;
  • Element 8 (Admissions): OTA charter must be revised to state that in the event OTA applies for the California Public Charter School Grant Program (PCSGP), the 10 percent exemption will apply to children of founders and “faculty” (rather than “employees”);

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the following items:

 

1.  Approve the Independent Citizen Oversight Committee (ICOC) recommendations regarding charitable gifting. The ICOC developed a general template to organize data and measure the impact to the bond program and the district from donations and donations in-kind made by individuals or organizations.  This proposal is attached for reference. The district recommends adoption of the ICOC's proposal.  

 

2.  Approve Prop. S matching funding for five Career Technical Education remodel projects:  Hoover High School Academy of Information Technology, Mira Mesa High School Broadcast Journalism, Mira Mesa Screen Printing and Computer Graphics, Sci-Tech @ San Diego High School - Biomedical, School of Creative and Performing Arts - Live Broadcast. The district currently has active Career academic programs in each of these areas at the above sites. 

The total cost of these five projects is $11,921,096 (The state match is $4,849,463 and the required Prop. S match is $7,127,633.  $2,161,800 of this funding will come from the current Proposition S budget for these schools, and is based on a $100 per square foot allocation credit from major repair and replacement work in these areas that would be done under the whole site modernization budget, and an estimated $4,967,833 will come from a program assessment on all future projects. (Final assessments will be based on construction bid results. Currently, the district is experiencing 20 to 25 percent lower bid results over a historical bid environment. Further program efficiencies will be realized through conducting value engineering during design development). The district has determined that these projects are permitted under Proposition S. There will be minimal impact to the Proposition S five-year construction schedule.     

 

3.  Provide direction on Proposition S matching funds for two, Career Technical Education new construction projects:  Clairemont High School - Academy of Business and Technology, Henry High School Production Studios.

 

The total cost of these two projects is $9,571,019.  The state match is $4,388,897, and the required Proposition S match is $5,182,122. This funding will come from Proposition S reserves. Of the two projects, the Clairemont Academy of Business and Technology is an existing academic program housed in the main academic site. The Henry High School Production studio would be built to support a future auditorium with performing arts attributes. There are not sufficient funds in the whole site modernization budgets at either site to fund these initiatives within the budget. There is minimal impact on the five year schedule due to the construction of these facilities.   

 

Provide direction for school site issues in Items 3 through 8:

 

4. Approve the schedule for Mission Bay High School stadium improvements and whole site modernization per the proposed five-year plan (See exhibit Proposition S Proposed 5-Year Plan Comparison 11-2-10.pdf). The proposed schedule of the stadium improvements with a July 9, 2013, construction start reflects a three-year and four month acceleration of the stadium project, and the April 14, 2015, construction start for the whole site modernization reflects a 3-month acceleration of the whole site modernization project based on the increase in bond revenues from the October 2009 plan. Further acceleration would place these projects ahead of other projects based on the board approved prioritization matrix.  Staff does not recommend acceleration of this project. If accelerated, this project will come out of sequence and before San Diego High School whole site modernization, Bell and Pershing middle schools whole site modernizations, Memorial Preparatory Academy whole site modernization, O'Farrell Community Charter School and Gompers Preparatory Academy. 

 

5.  Approve the schedule for Scripps Ranch High School whole site modernization project per the proposed five year plan (attachment). District facilities staff have evaluated the site for the provision of air conditioning apart from the whole site modernization project. Installation of air conditioning is very intrusive work and requires construction work throughout the facility.  As background, natural ventilation is provided in site classrooms including portable buildings via operable windows. These windows will need to be replaced with dual glazed windows to comply with State Title 24 energy and green code requirements when air conditioning is provided.  There are common areas in the kitchen, gym, library, and theatre that have limited or no operable windows.  These spaces were provisioned with inefficient or ineffective evaporative cooling systems.  Duct and plenum chases are required to condition lower floors.  Installation of roof top units requires structural changes throughout the site. The proposed schedule with a construction start of September 2015 reflects a one-year and four month acceleration of the whole site modernization project based on the increase in bond revenues from the October 2009 plan. While some new schools built during Prop MM were designed to accommodate future air conditioning retrofit Scripps Ranch High was built during Proposition Y that provided for only heating. To fully install air conditioning at this site warrants moving the entire whole site modernization schedule forward due to the scope of work. Acceleration would place this site ahead of six other high schools including Clairemont High, University City High School, Crawford High, Serra High, Mission Bay and Mira Mesa High School. District staff does not recommend performing this work apart from the whole site modernization project.  There is no impact to the budget due to the proposed schedule change. The district also proposes that a study be conducted and recommendations made to the Board of Education be completed concerning district wide operational policies pertaining to high heat conditions at school sites.   

 

6. Approve $1 million in additional Proposition S funding from program reserves to convert Knox K-8 School to a 6-8 middle school with a capacity of 700 students. This project is permissible under the provision in Proposition S.

 

7. Approve $4.0 million in additional Proposition S funding from program reserves to increase the current capacity at Millennial Tech Middle School to 700 students. This change in scope at Millennial Tech provides for the construction of six additional classrooms.   The cost of this increase in scope is permissible under the provisions of Proposition S.  Staff has determined that there will be minimum impact to the Five-Year Proposition S execution schedule. The board may re-direct $4.0 million dollars of the $9.5 million dollars in whole site modernization budget or elect to use $4.0 million dollars in program reserves. State School Facilities funds could also be allocated for this expense, however, this would require reprogramming of currently authorized commitments and reserves.  There will be no impact to the completion of other scheduled Prop S projects and minimal financial impact to the bond program.  

 

8. Approve the Data Center project at Serra High School. This project is now ready to be submitted to the Division of the State Architect (DSA) for construction document approval and needs to move forward. The district conducted a district wide site selection study in May 2009, completed a Business Case Study in February 2010, and completed a value engineering study in June 2010.  Project plans will be submitted to the DSA no later than October 25, 2010.  The district has determined that the current lack of a redundant data center has created a significant threat to district operations.  Further, the district has determined that operating the data center in house over a 20 year period would cost $25.3 million and outsourcing the data center would cost $62 million over that same period. All relevant expenses were taken into consideration including cost of construction, equipment costs, equipment replacement costs, depreciation, operator salaries, and other expenses. Outsourcing data center services are estimated to cost between $3.1 million to $12.3 per year depending on the vendor and if the district provides the equipment.  Operational expenses are carried by the General Fund. There is no provision in the General Fund budget to carry these annual outsourcing expenses.
