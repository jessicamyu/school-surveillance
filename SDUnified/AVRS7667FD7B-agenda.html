RECOMMENDATION: Approve Amended East Village High School Educational Partnership Agreement with San Diego City College (SV-17-0619-99) for the 2018-19 school year. This Amended Agreement establishes dates for each semester by which East Village High School (EVHS) will provide course requests and student names to San Diego City College, allows EVHS students to enroll in lab classes as needed, allows all EVHS students to participate in courses on the City College campus, and eliminates the need for EVHS to pay San Diego City College for a Physical Eduction (PE) instructor as PE will be provided by the San Diego Unified School District.   

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: $32,000.00 (cost of a Physical Education instructor).

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On February 14, 2017, the board approved ratification of the East Village High School Educational Partnership with San Diego City College (SV-17-0619-99) for the 2016-17 school year. The Educational Partnership calls for joint evaluation of the program in the Spring of each academic year. Review of the partnership has resulted in the proposed amendments to the agreement. This partnership is developed in accordance with the overarching terms and conditions of the Second Amended Memorandum of Understanding (SV-14-0291-13) between the San Diego Unified School District and the San Diego Community College District executed by the Chancellor of the San Diego Community College District on April 4, 2017, and the San Diego Unified School District Board of Eduction on May 16, 2017.

2. 2018-19 Alignment of East Village High School Academic Year with San Diego City College; BUDGETED: N/A

RECOMMENDATION: Approve proposed 2018-19 East Village High School academic year calendar to align with San Diego City College, with a school start date of August 7, 2018 and end date of May 30, 2019 in support of the middle college design, and to attract students to this unique academic program. On July 25, 2017, the Board of Education approved the districtwide 2018-19 academic calendar with a start date of August 27, 2018 and end date of June 11, 2019 for schools on traditional schedule. This proposal seeks exception from the districtwide calendar for East Village High School.  This proposal can be adopted as a one-year test pilot for 2018-19 with authorization to continue in subsequent years if successful. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH DISTRICT REPRESENTATIVES AND IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Aligning the East Village High School calendar with San Diego City College's academic calendar will result in staff working a schedule that is not aligned with the work year of other district schools.  Under San Diego Unified's current districtwide academic calendar which does not align with the community college district's instructional calendar, EVHS staff must be paid to work off-contract to assist with college enrollment, obtain required college textbooks and instructional materials, and prepare students for the first day of college classes. This proposal would eliminate the need for off-contract days, as individual schedules will be developed for each EVHS employee as per collective bargaining agreements.

 

Representatives from Integrated Technology, Pupil Accounting, Human Resource Services, Labor Relations and Payroll/Benefits have been included in the development of this proposal. The East Village High School Site Governance Team (SGT) and School Site Council (SSC) have approved this proposed calendar change, along with a change to a 4x4 schedule.  

 

BACKGROUND:  East Village High School (EVHS) is a middle college in partnership with San Diego City College (SDCC).  It is the only middle college in San Diego County and a member of the National Middle College Consortium.  EVHS students access courses through an Educational Partnership with SDCC and take full advantage of dual enrollment opportunities throughout the year.  EVHS can schedule students into two college classes per semester as well as summer and weekend classes, resulting in college credit potential in excess of 40 units upon graduation from high school.  

 

In an effort to establish best practice and support the school's vision, EVHS staff have researched calendars and scheduling practices at effective middle colleges throughout the country.  An example close to home is Early College High School in Costa Mesa, CA, where the school's calendar is aligned to the partner college.  School begins in the second week of August, and staff schedules are developed to ensure effective student scheduling and preparation for the first day of classes.  Courses conclude at the end of May to support students through the end of the Spring semester with finals and projects.  Throughout the school year, student vacations and holidays are aligned with the college calendar to increase the level of connectivity to the college and maximize student success in college courses and exams.
