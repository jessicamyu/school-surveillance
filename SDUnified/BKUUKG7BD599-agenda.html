2. Resolution Recognizing September 15 through October 15, 2020, as National Hispanic Heritage Month
Meeting
Sep 8, 2020 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Fiscal Impact
No

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing September 15, through October 15, 2020, as National Hispanic Heritage Month and encourage teachers, other staff members, students, and the community to use this opportunity to honor the contributions of the Latinx population through student led activities and inclusive curriculum.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: In 1968, President Lyndon B. Johnson proclaimed the week of September 15, 1968, as the first National Hispanic Heritage Week.

 

In 1988, President Ronald Reagan declared September 15 through October 15 as National Hispanic Heritage Month.

 

Each year, Americans observe National Hispanic Heritage Month by celebrating the histories, cultures and contributions of Americans citizens whose ancestors represent rich and vibrant Mesoamerican civilizations beginning over 3,000 years ago.

 

Pre-Columbian civilizations included the Olmect, Maya, Zapotec, Teotihuacan, Mixtec, and Aztec, demonstrating kingdoms, empires, sophisticated cities, intellectual accomplishments and vibrant indigenous cultures developed in parts of Mexico, Central and South America, prior to Spanish conquest.

 

The United States history includes indigenous cultures such as Latinx communities that express and demonstrate incredibly diverse cultures, represented by dialects, languages and richly historical cultures.

 

The United States Latinx population comprises 52 million people or 16.7 percent of the population, 34.1 percent of the population of San Diego County, and 44 percent of the San Diego Unified student body, and continues to contribute significantly to the political, economic, and social development of the nation.

 

The San Diego Unified School District will continue its commitment of eliminating barriers that prevent our Latinx students from receiving equitable access and educational outcomes, including implementation of restorative school communities, access to equitable grading practices and access to ethnic studies instruction. 

 

The San Diego Unified School District recognizes the diversity reflected within the City of San Diego as well as its students and staff and respects the Latinx community’s contributions, culture, and heritage.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing September 11, 2020, as Patriot Day in Honor of Those We Lost on September 11, 2001, and the many who rose in service in response to the attacks.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: People of all ages and walks of life, across America, and around the world, collectively witnessed an event of immense tragedy on September 11, 2001.

 

The events of that day instantly transformed our lives, the lives of those personally affected by the loss of friends and loved ones, and many others through an unfamiliar sense of individual and national vulnerability.

 

An unprecedented, historic bonding of Americans arose from the collective sorrow, unifying the country in an outpouring of national spirit, pride, selflessness, generosity, courage and service.

 

Countless brave people heroically, tirelessly, and courageously participated in an extraordinarily difficult and dangerous rescue and recovery effort, in some cases voluntarily putting their own well-being at risk.

 

September 11 will never, and should never, be observed as just another day in the hearts and minds of all Americans.

 

We honor those who we lost and embrace those who survive in a continuous journey of humanization and appreciation for our collective resilient human spirit.

 

On March 19, 2009, the United States Congress passed bipartisan legislation authorizing the establishment of September 11 as a federally recognized National Day of Service and Remembrance, which President Barack Obama signed into law on April 21, 2009.

 

The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education calls upon school leaders, educators, students, and classified staff to participate in this observance through lesson plans ad activities that teach students the importance of understanding this historical event, oral history, and observe moments of silence, the flying of the flag of the United States at half-staff, and/or activities that pay tribute and provide opportunities for students for solemn remembrance.

 

Resources for teaching and learning about Patriot Day are available at https://www.911memorial.org/learn/students-and-teachers/lesson-plans/lesson-plans/local-heroes, https://www.911memorial.org, and https://www.911memorial.org/learn/resources.

3. Public Hearing and First Reading of the Proposed 2020-21 Learning Continuity and Attendance Plan (LCP)
Meeting
Sep 8, 2020 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Information, First Reading, Public Hearing

RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and first reading of the district's proposed 2020-21 Learning Continuity Attendance Plan (LCP).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The District Advisory Council (DAC) and the District English Learner Advisory Committee (DELAC) serve as the district's LCP advisory committees. The DAC Executive Committee was consulted on September 2, 2020, and the DELAC General Meeting was conducted on September 3, 2020 but did not reach quorum. The DAC General Meeting is scheduled for September 16, 2020 and an additional DELAC General Meeting will be scheduled before second reading and adoption. Additional stakeholder feedback sessions with advisory groups are also being conducted before second reading and adoption on September 22, 2020.

 

BACKGROUND:
 

Senate Bill (SB) 98 established that the Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) and an annual update to the LCAP are not required for the 2020–21 school year and that the California Department of Education (CDE) shall not publish the California School Dashboard in December 2020 based on performance data on the state and local indicators. On December 10, 2019, the board approved the First Amendment to Master Facilities Lease, increasing the total not to exceed value for preconstruction services (found on Exhibit C of the Master Facilities Lease) by an additional $250,000.00, for a revised total of $600,700.00.

 

Under the RFP, the duration for preconstruction services was from February 26, 2019, through December 2019, with the Phase 1 of Construction starting in January 2020. This is also stated as such in Exhibit F of the Master Facilities Lease and was the basis of the preconstruction services schedule provided in Swinerton Builder's RFP Submission. Since then, the preconstruction duration has been extended due to City of San Diego requiring changes to the driveway apron onto Balboa Ave., and the addition of the signage and graphics scope to the design. The district and Swinerton Builders now desire to include the Guaranteed Maximum Price for the Project.  The parties also desire to make certain administrative and substantive changes to process payments more efficiently, for the benefit of the Contractor, which is further described on page 2 of the attached Revised and Restated Master Facilities Lease.

 

The district’s capital improvements are funded by three General Obligation bond measures: Proposition S and Z, and Measure YY. In an effort to provide a quality school in every neighborhood, the district is using these funds to repair, renovate and revitalize district schools  Lindbergh/Schweitzer Elementary School was originally built in the 1960’s. The prior CEQA approval for the Standley Middle School and Spreckels Elementary School Joint Use Facilities Project did not contemplate the Spreckels Elementary School Whole Site Modernization Project (WSM Project However, the Spreckels Elementary School Whole Site Modernization Project is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Section 15314 – Minor Additions to Schools (Class 14). A Class 14 exemption consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or ten classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption (CEQA Guidelines §15314).

 

As described above, the WSM Project would not result in the addition of 10 or more classrooms or result in a 25 percent increase in student capacity. Therefore, the application of a Class 14 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

 

a) Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.

b) Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.

c) Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.

d) Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.

e) Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.

f) Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

 

Environmental Review: The following provides a review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project. See the attached Environmental Checklist (Attachment 2) for additional detail.

 

a) Location. Construction and operation of the Proposed Project would be contained to the existing Spreckels Elementary School campus. Land use designations adjacent to the Proposed Project site include residential uses to the north, east, south, and west with an open space area to the southeast of the campus beyond the playfield area. The Proposed Project site and adjacent areas are heavily disturbed and not considered as environmentally sensitive. Additionally, existing site use will not change as a result of the Proposed Project. The Proposed Project will not impact environmentally sensitive areas or an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern. This exception does not apply to the Proposed Project.

b) Cumulative Impact. Construction of the Proposed Project would occur simultaneously with the Standley Middle School and Spreckels Elementary School Joint Use Field Project. However, all construction activities would occur within the existing boundaries of each respective school campus and all residual impacts, in consideration of cumulative projects, were found to be less than significant. The Proposed Project would not result in any significant cumulative impacts. This exception does not apply to the Proposed Project.

c) Significant Effect. The Proposed Project is not considered as an unusual circumstance as these activities fulfill the demands of the existing school and include standard maintenance of building facilities. The WSM alterations and improvements of the existing building are necessary to meet current standards for public health and safety. Such activities are not considered as an unusual circumstance as these activities do not include specifications that would be considered out of the ordinary. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.

d) Scenic Highways. There are several designated and eligible state scenic highways throughout San Diego County; however, the nearest scenic highway is SR-52, between Santo Road and Mast Boulevard, and is located approximately 6.5 miles to the east and is too far to provide views of the project site. The Proposed Project will not involve activities resulting in directly, or indirectly impacting an officially designated highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway as the activities associated with the Proposed Project will occur within the existing school campus. Scenic highways deemed eligible or officially designated are not located in the immediate vicinity of the Spreckels Elementary School. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.

e) Hazardous Waste Sites. The Proposed Project will occur within an existing, operational school. A review of federal and State standard and supplemental databases indicated that the Proposed Project site is not located within any identified hazardous material site pursuant to Government Code Section 65962.5. In addition, the alteration and improvement activities will remain within the property and will not involve heavy trenching or excavation. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.

f) Historical Resources. Based on a review of available historic research and the results of the field survey, the Spreckels Elementary School campus does meet the eligibility criteria for inclusion on the California Register of Historical Resources. Accordingly, Spreckels Elementary School is not a historical resource under CEQA; therefore, implementation of the Proposed Project will not result in an adverse change in the significance of a historical resource. This exception does not apply to the Project.
