Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC) uses a design, bid, build process to accomplish the majority of planned capital projects.

Facilities Planning and Construction does not use in house architects, electrical engineers, mechanical engineers, structural engineers, landscape architects, interior designers, acoustic engineers, or environmental consultants to accomplish this work. The work is accomplished by firms who enter into professional agreements on a cost not to exceed basis over a three-year period of time. During the height of Proposition MM, more than 18 architectural firms were employed by the district to perform the work.

Implementation of Proposition S requires an aggressive schedule for the planning and construction of $100 million to $150 million per year in project work over approximately a 16 year period. The scheduled scope of work includes 165 modernization projects, two school demolition/reconstruction projects, two new schools, and 180 technology projects.

Facilities Planning and Construction prefers to have a wide variety of architectural firms of different size and capacities for Prop S projects. Four of the first increment of architects can be considered small firms, three firms are considered medium sized firms and three are large firms. All firms have local offices with in house staff. Eight of the ten firms in the first increment have recent FPC work history and five have current contracts that will expire in May 2009.

Selection Process
On October 23, 2008, the district publicly advertised a Request for Qualifications. Fifty-two firms responded by the closing date of November 18, 2008. Of the 52 firms, four submitted their qualifications to perform landscape work for the district.

The firms’ statements of qualification were reviewed and ranked by a qualified panel of five district employees in accordance with the RFQ, based on the following criteria: (1) staff qualifications; (2) design experience; (3) references; (4) quality assurance; (5) computer/CAD capabilities; (6) responsiveness to RFQ; and (7) cost. One firm was deemed non-responsive by the rating panel. Four qualified district employees rated the landscape architect firms.

Based on the final ratings of the panel, ten firms are recommended to be awarded contracts: These contracts represent only a portion of the total master agreements to be awarded as a result of the selection process, however. Specifically, 22 firms are recommended for contract award; e.g., an initial ten firms are recommended for approval on February 24, 2009, and an additional 12 firms will be presented for board approval on March 10, 2009, at the completion of negotiations. There is a critical need to complete design projects in progress, and with insufficient funds remaining on the current contracts, it is imperative for the district to enter into new agreements as soon as possible.

The initial ten firms recommended for contract award include the following:

 

 

  • Galvin Cristilli Parshalle Architects: $1.5M
  • Gkkworks: $2M
  • HB&A Architects, Inc.: $1.5M
  • Mesquita & Associates, Inc. $3M
  • R.J. Maus, Architects: $1.5M
  • Platt/Whitelaw Architects, Inc.: $3M
  • Roger Eliot Kerr – Architect: $1.5M
  • Tucker Sadler Architects, Inc.: $2M
  • Wolff/Lang/Christopher Architects, Inc.: $1.5M
  • Zagrodnik + Thomas Architects: $3M


The agreements provide for an initial three-year term with an option to extend for two additional years. Primary funding for these contracts is available in the Proposition S fund.

California Government Code section 4525, et seq.,grants the district the authority to contract with and employ persons for the furnishing of architectural and landscape architectural services if such persons are specially trained, experienced, and competent to perform the special services requested.

Without employment of these firms, the district would have to hire a significant number of professionals in-house to perform the multitude of projects needing completion for the program.

Employment of the selected firms will provide the best value overall for the district because: (1) it ensures a fair selection process; (2) meets the requirements mandated for architectural selection; (3) ensures available resources on a multitude of projects in a timely manner; (4) avoids a lengthy selection and approval process on each project; (5) provides cost controls and tracking continuity; and (6) avoids the necessity of in-house layoffs of a significant work force.

The district will be contracting with firms ranging in size from small to large to provide the variety of resources necessary that can be accessed at district discretion. Specifically, the district has a preference for local firms in order to provide work to the community, and even with large firm contract awards, there is considerable small business participation because of subconsultant association with the primes. Overall, this results in good will in the community and lower costs to the district.

The two lowest bidders, Bekins A-1 Movers, Inc. and Oddrose Studio, will each receive contracts with the district because of the necessity to have available staff ready for work on very short notice. Task orders will be issued to each firm on a project-by-project basis; however, the firm offering the lowest hourly rates for a specific task will get first call for the work. This agreement with Bekins A-1 Movers, Inc. provides for a maximum consideration of $493,400, and a term commencing on February 25, 2009 and terminating on February 24, 2010.

There is a significant need for school move services related to bond and Physical Plant Operations associated work. At present, there are classroom assignments to be performed at Memorial Area Middle School (15 classrooms) and Star Academy at Horace Mann MS (10 classrooms). Due to the nature of school moves, it is necessary to respond on very short notice, or construction will be delayed with resultant site problems. Assignments are on an intermittent on-call basis and include the furnishing of all labor, materials, transportation, equipment and services for the work. In essence, the work effort is tailored to ensure timely performance of services only as critically required within very short timeframes.

Pursuant to Administrative Procedure 5475, district truck driver assistance is restricted to moving items from one site location to a specified delivery point at an additional site. Services do not extend to packing, providing boxes, or moving boxes internally within a particular site, which are key to school moves. There are, also, scheduling problems; e.g., in order to schedule moves when school is not in session on Saturdays, Sundays and evenings, staff must be available on an hourly basis to complete the tasks, and with decreased staff there are limited resources available to perform the moves. To provide maximum opportunity to district staff, however, district personnel will have the right of first refusal on any specific task on a project-by-project basis as each assignment is scheduled. Specifically, when there is a need for Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC) to perform move services at district sites, FPC will contact Distribution Services (DS) to determine if its staff can perform the specified task in a timely manner. If it is agreed DS staff can perform the task in a timely manner, the task will be assigned to DS. However, if DS staff cannot perform the particular task in a timely manner, FPC will complete the task with other resources. This collaboration has worked well in the past and will continue in the future.

It is important to note that this contract for move services shall not result in the layoff or reduction of bargaining unit members or positions, nor shall it diminish the rights provided to laid-off unit members under the provision of Layoff and Reemployment. Operations-Support Services Bargaining Unit Contract, Article 19, section 3, page 99.

The total for the lowest responsive and responsible base bid is considered fair and reasonable and within the district’s budget. In fact, the average rates for the services to be performed by Consultant are less than the rates paid in 2008-09 for the same services, resulting in potential savings to the district. Since award of this Agreement is in the best interest of the district, it is recommended that the contract be approved in the total base bid amount of $493,400.
