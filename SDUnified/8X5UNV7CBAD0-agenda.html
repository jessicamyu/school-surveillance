BACKGROUND: Future Educators Scholarships are offered to provide the San Diego Unified School District with an ongoing source of qualified teachers from under-represented groups.  Since 1989, the district has provided five-year scholarships to more than 135 students who want to become teachers.  The San Diego Education Fund  provided up to $13,000 for four years of undergraduate study and a fifth year of funding for the credential year.  Most of the scholars are the first in their families to attend college.  Students who will graduate in June 2013 are eligible to apply.  Funds are paid directly to students in the form of a stipend that may be used to defray expenses related to their education.  The district has a 100 percent graduation rate for scholars who attend four year universities.   And, several past scholarship recipients are now principals and vice principals.

 

Mashin Scholarships in Mathematics and Science is administered by the San Diego Education Fund. The awards are funded from the estate of educators Virginia and Jack Mashin.  The Mashins left a gift of $688,000 for the principle to be invested and the income used to issue perpetual scholarships. Virginia was a district Math Specialist and a calculus teacher at Kearny High School.  After her retirement she tutored students in math.  She received numerous awards from the San Diego Mathematics Council.  Jack Mashin was a coach for over 60 years and is a member of the Coaches Hall of Fame.  The Mashins did not have children and lived a frugal life, so they could leave a legacy for district students.

 

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) Scholarships will be awarded for the first time to students who will major in science, technology, engineering or mathematics and attend a college or university in San Diego County.

 

In addition to the scholarship funds, scholarship recipients will receive counseling and mentoring from the San Diego Education Fund.

BACKGROUND: National Board Certification is a symbol of professional teaching excellence and is achieved through a rigorous program of professional growth and development. It includes an intense assessment of a teacher’s knowledge and the actual demonstration of skills and professional judgment as applied daily in the classroom. Achieving certification is a demonstration of a teacher’s practice as measured by the highest and most rigorous standards, and improves the quality of education by demonstrating a high level of leadership to their peers, and having higher expectations of their students. National Board Certification is a tremendous achievement for any teacher.

 

In 2012, one teacher from San Diego Unified rose to the challenge and received either National Board Certification or recertification.

 

National Board Certification is valid for 10 years, and renewal candidates must begin the renewal process during their eighth or ninth years as National Board Certified Teachers (NBCTs).  National Board Certification is available nationwide for most pre-K–12 teachers.
