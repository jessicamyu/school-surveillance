In previous years, the Budget Act required each county office of education and each county mental health agency to enter into a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) for the purposes of transferring Federal IDEA funds to the county mental health agency for educationally-related mental health services (ERMHS) as set forth in AB 3632 that were provided to students with disabilities as determined by an IEP team.

BACKGROUND: On October 8, 2010, the Governor of the State of California vetoed all state funding for ERMHS provided pursuant to AB 3632 and unilaterally declared the mandate on county mental health to provide these services suspended. The Legislature through Assembly Bill 101 (AB 100) and Senate Bill 87 (SB 87) also known as the 2011-12 Budget Act, appropriated $98.586 million payable from the Mental Health Services Act Fund (Proposition 63) to counties to provide handicapped and disabled students, seriously emotionally disturbed students, and students in out-of-state mental health services facilities with ERMHS. SB 87 appropriated $1.229 billion from the Federal Trust Fund (IDEA funds) for special education programs for eligible students with special needs; of which $69 million has been made available to, and will be allocated by the Superintendent of Public Instruction to special education local plan areas (SELPAS). The Legislature, through Assembly Bill 114 (AB 114), shifted the responsibility for providing ERMHS from county offices of mental health to local school districts by amending and repealing various provisions in the Education, Government, Family and Welfare and Institution Codes, thereby repealing AB  3632. As a result of AB 114, the Local Interagency Agreement between the County of San Diego Health and Human Services Agency (CMH), public school districts in San Diego county and the Superintendent of Schools, San Diego County Office of Education (COE) is no longer valid. The Legislature, through SB 87 authorizes a county mental health agency and local educational agency to enter into a MOU or contract to address interagency service responsibilities for the provision and transition of ERMHS identified on a student’s IEP during the 2011-2012 fiscal year in order for a local educational agency to access AB 100 funds. COE entered into a MOU with CMH to facilitate access to AB 100 funds which shall be used exclusively for the purpose of providing ERMHS for which expenses have been or will be incurred in the 2011-2012 fiscal year and to provide continuity of the provision of ERMHS to students with disabilities who are the responsibility of San Diego County school districts. Pursuant to the MOU, the CMH also agreed to utilize funding from the Medi-Cal, Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnosis and Treatment (EPSDT) and apply its portion of the AB 100 funds towards ERMHS it provides to students of the district between July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2012, or until the AB 100 funds are exhausted, whichever occurs first. CMH will provide the COE and the school districts with a report when it projects the AB 100 funds will be exhausted on or about March 1, 2012. COE, on behalf of the district, will reimburse CMH for the provision of ERMHS that exceed the amount of AB 100 funds allocated to CMH. The accounting of expenditures for the provision of ERMHS will occur two times during the term of this Agreement. The first accounting of expenditures will cover the period of July 1, 2011 through December 31, 2011, and occur on or before March 1, 2012. The second accounting of expenditures will cover the period January 1, 2012 through June 30, 2012, and will be provided on or before August 15, 2012.   1. The Final Environmental Impact Report (EIR) for the construction and operation of the Jonas Salk Area Elementary School (SCH No. 2010011021) has been prepared in compliance with applicable laws and regulations to evaluate the potential for significant adverse environmental impacts; and

BACKGROUND: The district proposes to construct and operate the Jonas Salk Area Elementary School (including a park and joint-use facilities) within the Mira Mesa Community of the City of San Diego. The proposed project includes the following components:

  • Construction and operation of the new school;
     
  • Construction and operation of a park and joint-use facilities (to be owned and operated by the City of San Diego (City); and
     
  • Implementation of a Vernal Pool Restoration Plan (mitigation) within the City of San Diego’s McAuliffe Park site (restoration site to be owned and maintained by the District); and
     
  • Implementation of 0.76-acres of vernal pool mitigation within the City of San Diego’s Carroll Canyon Preserve site.

The proposed new school would support the enrollment needs in the Mira Mesa Community and help the district achieve the enrollment standards set forth in its Long Range Facilities Master Plan. As part of the project, the district would convey 6.1 acres of the school site to the City (4.1 acres of park use and 2 acres of joint-use play field) in exchange for allowing the district to implement vernal pool mitigation on approximately 12.7 acres of the project mitigation site at McAuliffe Park.

 

On January 11, 2010, the district filed a Notice of Preparation at the San Diego Clerk’s Office for the Draft EIR for the proposed Jonas Salk Area Elementary School. The Notice was distributed to city, county, and state agencies, other public agencies, and various interested private organizations and individuals and was published in the Union Tribune and La Prensa. A public scoping meeting was held on January 27, 2010. Subsequently, a Draft EIR (SCH No. 2010011021) was issued for a 75-day public review on July 30, 2010. Copies of the Draft EIR were sent to the State Clearinghouse; federal, state, and local agencies; and community entities/organizations.

 

The Draft EIR evaluated the following environmental issues; aesthetics, air quality, greenhouse gas emissions, biological resources, cultural resources, geology and soils, hazards and hazardous materials, hydrology and water quality, land use and planning, transportation/circulation, noise, and public services and utilities. The Draft EIR also addressed cumulative impacts; other required considerations, including growth inducing impacts and effects found not to be significant during the initial study process; and alternatives that would reduce or avoid significant impacts of the proposed project. In response to the Draft EIR, 19 public comments were received. Responses to these comment letters were prepared and are included in the Final EIR.

 

Subsequent to the release of the Draft EIR for public review, the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service (USFWS) deemed all vernal pools on the school site to be potentially occupied by the San Diego fairy shrimp. Previous surveys only identified 1.31 acres of vernal pools as occupied. As such, the total fairy shrimp assumed occupied vernal pool impact acreage for the proposed project has been increased from 1.31 acres to 1.66 acres. Furthermore, the mitigation requirement has been revised from 2.62 acres to 3.32 acres. This information was added to the Final EIR.

 

The district has been working with USFWS since the beginning of this year (2011) to identify a location to fulfill the additional 0.76-acre mitigation requirement. The USFWS suggested that the district determine the availability of the use of the City of San Diego’s Carroll Canyon Preserve or another location within the project vicinity for this additional mitigation. The district has been in discussions with the City of San Diego for use of the Carroll Canyon Preserve site. At this time, the Carroll Canyon Preserve site appears to be a viable mitigation option for the district; however, the district does not have a City Right of Entry Permit. The additional mitigation will be performed using a similar vernal pool restoration plan as the McAuliffe Park vernal pool mitigation site. If the district is unable to negotiate a Right of Entry Permit with the City for use of the Carroll Canyon Preserve site, the district will be required to pursue other options acceptable to the USFWS.

 

An analysis of the environmental impacts associated with the use of the Carroll Canyon Preserve vernal pool mitigation site has been added to each section of the Final EIR. No new significant and unmitigable environmental impacts were identified for the proposed project with the addition of the Carroll Canyon Preserve vernal pool mitigation site. The addition to the Final EIR of the information describing impacts to vernal pools that are now assumed to be occupied, and the addition to the Final EIR of the Carroll Canyon Preserve site to mitigate those impacts, do not constitute significant new information as defined by the State CEQA Guidelines (Section 15088.5). Assuming the pools are occupied is not a new impact since the project was already impacting 1.31 acres of occupied vernal pools. It is only assumed the additional pools are occupied; therefore, development of those pools would not increase the severity of the impact to fairy shrimp. The project is also fully mitigating the impacts to the additional 0.35 acre of pools as if they are occupied. Use of the Carroll Canyon site would have similar biological resources, paleontological resources, and noise impacts to the use of the McAuliffe Park site; therefore, these impacts would not be more severe. The same mitigation measures required for the McAuliffe Park site would be required for the Carroll Canyon Preserve site. These mitigation measures would reduce the significant biological resources, paleontological resources, and noise impacts at the Carroll Canyon site to less than significant. The alternatives considered in the Draft EIR already addressed impacts to fairy shrimp and assuming all pools on-site are occupied does not change the project alternatives or the mitigation measures required for the Jonas Salk Area Elementary School site or McAuliffe Park site.

 

The Final EIR was prepared in accordance with CEQA and the Guidelines for Implementation of CEQA. A copy of the Final EIR and technical appendices for the Jonas Salk Area Elementary School has been provided to the board prior to the public meeting and is available for review in the Board Servicers Office, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231. Additional copies of the Final EIR are available for review in the Physical Plan Operations Annex, Room 5, 4860 Ruffner Street, Room 5, San Diego, CA, 92111 and on the Facilities Planning and Construction web page at: http://www.sandi.net/page/948.

 

As described above, the district proposes to modernize and construct classroom facilities (“Project”) at Jonas Salk Area Elementary School (“School Site”) for educational purposes.  

 

Government Code section 53094 authorizes a governing board of a school district, by a vote of two-thirds of its members, to render city zoning ordinances inapplicable to its proposed use of property for classroom facilities.  Passage of this resolution will render City of San Diego zoning ordinances inapplicable to proposed Project and School Site and directs the Superintendent, or his designee, to provide written notice to the City within ten days of this action.
