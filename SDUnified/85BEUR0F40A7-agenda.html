F.02. Proposed restructuring plans for eight District schools in Program Improvement Year 4 status and revised restructuring plan for one school in Program Improvement Year 7

Under the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001, all Title I schools that do not make Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) for two consecutive years are identified as Program Improvement (PI). There are required services and/or interventions that schools must implement during each year they are in PI status. A requirement for schools in PI Year 4 is to prepare a Restructuring Plan to be implemented in Year 5.
This item brings forward proposed Restructuring Plans for eight District schools in Program Improvement Year 4 Status: 1) Alternative Learning for Behavior and Attitude (ALBA), 2) Correia Middle, 3) Emerson Bandini Elementary, 4) Garfield High, 5) Logan Elementary, 6) Montgomery Middle, 7) Madison High, and 8) Twain. The full proposals for each school are provided as exhibits. These proposals are the result of ongoing meetings with the schools in PI 4 status and their restructuring teams. In concert with the schools’ recommendations, the Office of the Deputy Superintendent has met on multiple occasions with District financial officers and has analyzed the fiscal impact of the schools’ recommendations. The Finance Office has approved the funding allocations that are presented in the schools’ proposed restructuring plans. Funding sources include Title I (as approved by the Board on February 12, 2008 as part of the 2008-09 plan for Title I Eligibility Ranking and Participation), Quality Education Investment Act (QEIA) as awarded to designated schools by the California State Department of Education, and Economic Impact Aid (EIA) funding that is available through the Office of Language Acquisition. Also included with this item are individual schools summaries of the key elements for each of the Restructuring Plans. Lastly, budget line item descriptions for the proposals are provided as a separate exhibit.

In addition to the Restructuring Plans for schools in Program Improvement Year 4 Status, this item brings forward revisions to the restructuring proposal for the Mann complex, currently in Program Improvement Year 7. The focus of these revisions, effective with the 2008-09 school year, is the transition of the school calendar from year round to traditional and the consolidation of the School of Expedition, the School of Exploration, and the School of Expression into one school – Mann Middle School. Upon Board approval, this action would result in the closure of the School of Exploration and School of Expression. Costs associated with this proposal include $330,000 for the calendar transition and $332,000 for four CPUs. These expenditures would be funded through Targeted Instructional Improvement Grant (TIIG) funds. [Originator/Contact: Geno Flores, Office of the Deputy Superintendent, 619.725.7104]

All portable and relocatable buildings (units) used by the district that house either students and/or faculty are required to be approved through the Division of the State Architect (DSA). On January 14, 2003, the Board of Education directed the Chief Operating Officer, Facilities Management, to prepare a Portable Certification Program to fund and implement the required certification based on findings from the districtwide examination of the condition of all such units not currently DSA certified. Including units certified and/or demolished prior to that decision, that districtwide survey, coupled with current inventory information, identified approximately 2703 units that were required to meet technical compliance with the Field Act and allow district staff to obtain DSA certification. Work efforts involved with achieving such compliance include one or more of the following categories: structural foundation upgrades, fire and life safety system improvements, and providing ramps and other modifications to properly provide for Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) access.

Since inception on approximately May 27, 2003 of this formal certification program, the district has DSA certified 513 units. Additionally, 294 DSA certifications occurred prior to commencement of the program, for a combined current total of 807 units being certified to date. Further, and again since inception of the certification program, the district has demolished 321 older/under-utilized units. Additionally, 56 units were demolished prior to the certification program, for a combined total of 377 units being demolished/removed from the district inventory.

On April 10, 2007, the Board of Education approved the allocation of $10 million for the Fourth Phase of the Portable Certification Program in addition to the $24 million allocated since May 27, 2003, for a current total of $34 million in certification funding. Currently, approximately 1519 units remain to be DSA certified on district sites.

Additional funding of $10 million will be required to fund this Fifth Phase of Portable Certification through the 2008/2009 fiscal year.
