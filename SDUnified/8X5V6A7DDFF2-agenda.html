CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The mission of the Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID) Program is to close the achievement gap by preparing all students for college readiness and success in a global society.  Implementation of the AVID Program in California is undergoing transition from a regional level to a district level implementation model. The district level implementation model is used nationally. California’s regional system was a unique model.  Prior to January 1, 2013, AVID was implemented via a California Regional System Model.  Under this model, a Regional Director and Regional Coordinators located at the San Diego County Office of Education coordinated support for AVID programs in county schools with the support of the AVID Center. The California Regional System Model was terminated on December 31, 2012.  The AVID Center provided service to California AVID districts without a signed Implementation Agreement between January 1 and June 30, 2013.

 

During these months, San Diego Unified completed Early Commitment transitional steps to continue AVID site programs by designating one district level leader and five teacher leaders as District Directors, by committing to AVID District Leadership Training for these designees, and by including AVID annual site membership fees during the development of the 2013-14 district budget. AVID annual site membership fees are determined on a sliding scale based upon the number of AVID sites in the district.

 

AVID District Directors will work with AVID Center Program Managers to provide professional development, support and coach site level AVID staff, and oversee the data collection/certification process in the district.  AVID annual site fees provide limited use of AVID logo and intellectual property, ongoing consultation, AVID newsletter and other publications, licensing for reproduction of student worksheets, site certification, comparative data collection and analysis, curriculum resources and training, technology support, professional development, and access to members only website.

BACKGROUND:  With the intent of getting as many teachers GATE certified as possible, and despite the budget crisis, the Advanced Studies Department was able to procure a very favorable agreement with San Diego State University Research Foundation (SDSURF) to provide this service.  They will be able to provide three semesters of Tier I certification experience for 300 K-12 teachers at the very reasonable cost of $30,000.00.  That includes the instructor time, any materials, and the classroom on the SDSU campus.  The rest of the funding is coming from a co-pay from teachers of $200.00 and SDSU’s revision of the delivery model.  The use of these funds will directly benefit students in 300 classrooms and will instill additional strategies to aid each teacher in raising achievement, levels of rigor, and provide enhanced engagement for all students.


Due to the fact that the vast majority of schools have chosen the Model B or Diversity Model* for their GATE Cluster classes, almost every class at the elementary level is considered a GATE class.  It must be taught by a trained teacher who can differentiate for all students.  Tier I GATE teacher certification helps to make that happen. 

San Diego Unified has always offered free GATE teacher certification during the school year, the cost of which comes directly out of the GATE discretionary funds allotted to the department.  It was our largest, yet most important, expense—one that is the most beneficial to all students.  This compromise method of support between SDSU, San Diego Unified, and the teachers will allow GATE Teacher Certification to continue.
 

*Model B – The Diversity cluster model provides that a minimum of 25 percent of the students in a class are identified as GATE. The remaining students reflect the diversity of the school, thus placing all other students to complete the class roster.
