BACKGROUND: The Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) of 1965, was reauthorized as of December 10, 2015, as the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA). Title I, one of several programs under ESSA, provides funds to schools to help disadvantaged children meet challenging academic standards. The law intends that the funds go to the districts and schools that have the highest concentrations of poverty. Title I requires funding to be allocated to a district based on the number of “children in poverty” residing within the district boundary. The federal census is used to identify children ages 5 through 17 living in poverty who are included in the calculation of Title I entitlement.
 

The Proposed 2016-17 Title I Ranking Report allocates Title I funding to all district schools with a 2015-16 Free and Reduced Lunch percentage of forty percent or higher.  The total projected 2016-17 Title I Entitlement is $36,507,059.00. Of the total projected entitlement, the proposal recommends allocating $15,515,369.00. to schools. 

 

The Proposed 2016-17 Title I Ranking Report was updated on January 22, due to discrepancies with Emerson/Bandini allocations.

African American history reflects a determined spirit of perseverance and cultural pride through struggles to equally share in the opportunities of a nation founded upon the principles of freedom and liberty. The history and contributions of African American citizens were consistently overlooked, misinterpreted, and undervalued in the curriculum of public education institutions prior to the Civil Rights Act of 1964. San Diego Unified strives to accelerate the achievements of all students and recognizes the diversity reflected within the San Diego community as well as its students and staff and respects the contributions, culture, and heritage of African Americans.

BACKGROUND:

 

Overview of Real Estate Utilization Strategy and Request for Proposals (RFP)

The board has developed a real estate utilization strategy to maximize the use of district-owned sites, including pursuing opportunities to develop properties, build capacity to generate ongoing revenue, and create partnerships to help maintain real estate assets to a high standard and serve as a community resource.

 

On September 11, 2012, the board unanimously adopted a resolution directing staff to seek opportunities to develop district properties, especially joint occupancy. The Scripps Mesa site was included in the list of sites staff were directed to pursue.

 

On February 18, 2014, the board held a public workshop on real estate. During this meeting, the board unanimously developed a set of guiding principles for the real estate utilization strategy. The board discussed several sites, including the Scripps Mesa site, for possible inclusion in an RFP for joint occupancy. Staff was directed to prepare an RFP for approval.

 

On July 22, 2014, the board unanimously adopted a resolution authorizing the release of a Request for Proposals (RFP) for joint occupancy.

 

On August 1, 2014, the RFP was released via Demand Star and the district Real Estate department website. On August 29, 2014, a mandatory pre-submittal conference was held to answer questions and receive initial qualifications statements from interested proposers.

 

On November 12, 2014, district staff hosted an initial community meeting to share information on RFP, including the concept of joint occupancy and potential to develop the site. During this meeting, the current zoning of the site was shared and community ideas were sought on potential uses. Attendance at these meetings was mandatory for all interested proposers in order to incorporate community feedback into their RFP response. The minutes from this meeting were posted with the RFP information online, and are also attached to this item for reference. Proposals were due to the district on February 2, 2015 (six months from opening date). The district received only one proposal for this site.

 

Along with proposals received for other sites, this proposal was reviewed and evaluated according to the criteria listed in the RFP by a committee composed of district staff from Facilities Planning and Construction, Special Projects, Real Estate, and Finance Division, as well as a faculty member from the University of San Diego graduate school of real estate. Out of multiple proposals received on four sites, the committee recommended moving forward only with the proposal from Monarch for the Scripps Mesa site.

 

On September 29, 2015, the board unanimously approved the selection of the Monarch proposal and authorized staff to negotiate a joint occupancy agreement with Monarch for the Scripps Mesa site.

 

Pursuant to Education Code section 17524 (a), staff submitted the proposal on September 30, 2015, to the State Board of Education (SBE) for approval. SBE approved the proposal on November 5, 2015; the motion was passed by a vote of 10-0.

Project Components and Benefits

Consistent with the RFP, the proposed Term Sheet and future Joint Occupancy Agreement envision the future of the Scripps Mesa site as a high-quality, shared development with the following primary features:

 

District STEAM Lab Facility, Community Meeting Space and Garden

  • Innovative district facility featuring STEAM lab and makerspace (modeled from successful district partnership with Qualcomm Thinkabit lab)
  • Meeting and conference room spaces for district and community use through Civic Center Act rentals

Multi-family Residential Community

  • 264 units of Class A, multifamily for-rent housing and resident amenities
  • Of those units, 22 units designated affordable at 50 percent Area Median Income (AMI)
  • Affirmative marketing of units to district employees
  • Community garden, an amenity requested during initial outreach with cluster and neighborhood members

This mixed use represents both the highest and best use of the land as well as added district and community benefit. The proposal is also consistent with the board-approved guiding principles for the real estate utilization strategy as follows:

BACKGROUND: Education Code 47614 and the California Code of Regulations, Title Sections 11969 et seq. (Regulations) require:

  • Districts offer facilities, to the extent required by law, to a charter school that submits a legally complete application;
     
  • A charter school wishing to occupy district facilities in the 2016-17 year must submit a complete application for facilities by November 1, 2015;
     
  • Districts analyze each charter school's request in conformity with the law and raise concerns to the charter school regarding the charter school's stated in-district classroom ADA projections, prior to developing the district's preliminary proposal in response to the application;
     
  • On or before February 1, districts must submit preliminary proposals to eligible charter schools, and on or before March 1, charter schools must respond with any concerns regarding the preliminary proposals;
     
  • On or before April 1, districts must submit final offers of facilities to eligible charter schools, and on or before May 1, charter schools must respond to the final offers if they are to receive district facilities for the upcoming year.
     
  • In the case of America's Finest Charter School, the district determined that it was only possible to offer two sites as a preliminary proposal, rather than one contiguous site.  The regulations (11969.2(d)) require that the board make a finding that a charter school could not be accommodated at a single site.  The reasons for this finding are included in Section E of the attached report.

Please note that the preliminary proposals are only the first step in the process of developing final offers of facilities to charter schools.  At this point, it is unknown whether the preliminary proposals will be accepted by the charter school applicants.  Board approval of these preliminary proposals is required in order to continue this process, including discussion of preliminary proposals with the charter schools, district schools impacted by the proposals, and to meet the statutory requirements to present these proposals to charter schools by February 1, 2016.
