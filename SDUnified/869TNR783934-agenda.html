RECOMMENDATION: The Monitoring, Accountability, and Compliance Committee and Superintendent recommend adoption of the proposed 2010-11 management and organization structure of the Accountability Office that includes the following:

  1. Establish the Accountability Office. The existing positions and the operating budget in the current Federal and Special Programs Division and the Research and Evaluation (RED) Division will support this Office.

  2. Change the reporting line of the Federal and Special Programs Division and the Research and Evaluation Division from the Office of the Deputy Superintendent to the Accountability Office.

  3. Change the title of the Federal and Special Programs Division and the Research and Evaluation Division to the Accountability Office.

  4. Establish, effective June 29, 2010, a new management job classification, Executive Director, Accountability Office, at salary grade 038 on the Management Salary Schedule.

FISCAL IMPACT: There are no new costs associated with the Accountability Office. The established positions and operating budgets in the current Federal and Special Programs Division and the Research and Evaluation Division are sufficient to support this Office.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Approval of this structure and placement of employees within the department will displace through layoffs three (3) employees:  Admin Asst I, Secretary III and Planning Analyst.  In addition, the vacant position of Secretary I would be eliminated.


BACKGROUND: On March 16, 2010 and April 20, 2010, the Board of Education discussed the district’s organization and management structure. The board approved a structure for executive management positions to include a Deputy Superintendent of Academics and a Deputy Superintendent of Business that were designed, in part, to create greater efficiencies while providing long-term stability within the organization. In addition, the board requested clarity on the composition and responsibilities of the proposed Accountability Office with corollary consideration given to the role(s) of RED and its intersection with the Integrative Technology Support Services (ITSS) Division. The proposed Accountability Office described in this report is based upon the findings of a planning committee, the board-approved organizational concept, board input, and is designed to enable the monitoring of departments across the district.

9. Resolution Declaring Board Commitment to Develop Budget Restoration Plan
Meeting
Jun 29, 2010 - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
No

RECOMMENDATION:  It is recommended that the board adopt the resolution to demonstrate its commitment to the commuity that the district will work to restore important educational programs as funding is restored to the district in future budget years.


FISCAL IMPACT: None.


PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  None.


IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  The resolution itself has no impact to district staffing.


BACKGROUND:  The San Diego Unifed School District has faced four consecutive years of budget cuts from the State of California.  As a result, the district has had to dramatically cut educational programs and support services for schools.  These reductions and future reductions that may be necessary in 2011-12 have had a negative impact on the ability of the district to provide quality educational programs and on the level of services for schools.

 

The purpose of this resolution is for the Board of Education to clearly state their intention to restore educational programs as soon as increased funding returns to the district.  The resolution directs the Superintendent to prepare a restoration plan based on board priorities and the Vision 2020 adopted by the board.  This restoration plan will be prepared by the Superintendent during the next budget year and considered by the board as part of the 2011-12 budget adoption process.  The board also states in this resolution its intent to continue a strong advocacy program to push for adequate funding from the state for K-12 education.
