A.03. A-G Audit Update; BUDGETED: YES, GENERAL FUNDS (OE-2)
Meeting
Feb 9, 2010 - - Special Meeting, 8:30 A.M.
Type
Information
RECOMMENDATION: Education Trust-West will present its timeline for conducting an Educational Opportunity (A-G) Audit, will provide the board with an update on its work to date, and an overview of the work scheduled throughout spring 2010.

FISCAL IMPACT: In 2009-10, the district contracted with Ed Trust West in the amount of $300,000.00.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Minimal thus far; central office staff have provided necessary data and artifacts to Ed Trust West to facilitate the audit process. Future impact to district staffing unknown at this time, but depending upon the outcome of the audit, could necessitate increases in the number of teachers required to provide instruction in specific content areas and staff to develop courses that meet A-G requirements.

BACKGROUND: On April 28, 2009, the board passed a resolution adopting the University of California (UC) A-G course sequence as part of high school graduation requirements. The board's direction to staff was to implement necessary learning supports to ensure that all students are prepared to successfully complete the A-G course sequence.

On September 29, 2009, the board approved an agreement with Education Trust-West for consulting services to assist the district with establishing a college and career ready A-G course sequence for high school students. The scope of work includes Education Trust-West meeting with district high school principals to discuss the strategies for A-G implementation; conducting an Educational Opportunity Audit and preparing an analysis of student transcripts and other artifacts; conducting voluntary focus groups with students, teachers, counselors, parents, and community members; and developing an implementation plan.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC) staffing actions:

1. Reduce contracted staff by 30 full-time equivalents (FTE)

2. Convert 10 of the 30 positions to district positions which will consist of 4 new job titles (staffing 6 positions); Human Resources will bring forward for board approval at a future date; (Senior Building Systems Project Manager (2 FTE), Building systems Project Manager (0 FTE), Building systems Project Coordinator (3 FTE), and Senior Labor Compliance Coordinator (1 FTE)

3. Fill 4 existing approved and budgeted (vacant) positions; Senior Specification Writer (1 FTE), Facilities Development Project Manager III (1 FTE), Senior Facilities Development Project Manager (1 FTE), Civil/Environmental Engineering Coordinator (1 FTE),

4. Eliminate 12 existing budgeted (vacant) district positions; Facilities Development Project Coordinator (1 FTE), Project Management Supervisor (2 FTE), Director, Construction (1 FTE), Class I Inspector (1 FTE),Class II Inspector (1 FTE), Class III Inspector (1 FTE), Administrative Aide (1 FTE), Senior Clerk (3 FTE), and Clerk Typist II (1 FTE).

Should the FPC staffing actions be approved, staff will, immediately, proceed to implement the plan, including the establishment of new position titles which will be brought to the Board Of Education for approval at a future public meeting. Substantiating documentation will clearly indicate that a substantial savings will be realized for each new position, which will be replacing a contractor augmentee function.

FISCAL IMPACT: The overall changes to the FPC organization, planned through mid 2011 will result in a yearly savings of approximately $6.8 million, consisting of $5.4 million (28 percent) in Prop. S funds and $ 1.4 million (34 percent) in non-Prop. S expenditures, as well as allow for a more efficient implementation of Prop. S. The total anticipated yearly expenditures by the end of fiscal year 2011 will be $17,006,588.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE: FPC staff budget for 2009-10 was $23,925,720.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: If board approved, FPC plans to reduce this contracted staffing level from 75 to 45 by the end of FY 2010-11, with further reductions planned in the years ahead. Of the 30 FTE reductions in contracted positions, 10 will be converted to district positions. Sixteen currently budgeted (vacant) positions will be eliminated and no currently filled district positions will be eliminated.

BACKGROUND: The combined district and contracted professional staff performs contract administration, program control, budgeting, environmental compliance, facilities planning, state facilities funding grant administration and project management from inception to completion.

At the October 6, 2009 board workshop, staff presented the revised implementation plan for Prop. S. This plan reflected a drop in anticipated revenues over the next five years of $172 million from the first comprehensive plan developed in March 2009. As a result of this cash flow reduction, a proposal to adjust the staffing of FPC has been developed in response to this change.

FPC currently employs a total of 140 individuals - 65 district staff members and 75 staff augmentation contractors. Of the total FPC staff, 133 individuals are assigned to Prop. S. Of the total FPC staff, 107 FTE are assigned to Prop S - 41 district employees and 66 contracted augmentees. A total of 33 staff members (24 district employees and 9 contracted augmentees) are dedicated to non-Prop S duties.

The proposed staffing actions have been discussed with and formally received by the Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC), and are being vetted through the Office-Technical and Business Services (OTBS) bargaining unit via the Human Resources Division .
