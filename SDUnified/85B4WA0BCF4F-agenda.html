RECOMMENDATION: Approve proposed organizational restructuring of the Research and Evaluation Department (RED) into the Integrated Technology Division.

FISCAL IMPACT: None, the Research and Evaluation Department remains as it is currently organized. There are no staffing reductions or increases proposed in this restructuring.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None, the Research and Evaluation Department remains as it is currently organized. There are no staffing reductions or increased proposed in this restructuring.

BACKGROUND: On March 16, 2010, the board discussed and approved with modifications the organizational structure for the district effective July 1, 2010. Part of the board's discussion centered around the belief that the Research and Evaluation department needed to be independent of the Deputy Superintendent for Academics. The attached memo further outlines the realignment and addresses concerns about direct line of reporting and safeguarding the staffing and focus of the RED team.

RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent recommends adoption of the proposed 2010-11 districtwide organization and management structure that includes the following:
  1. Change the reporting line of Risk Management from Auxiliary Services to Legal Services.
  2. Change the reporting line of the Research and Evaluation Division from the Office of the Deputy Superintendent to the Integrated Technology Support Services Division.
  3. Establishment of the Office of the Deputy Superintendent of Business Services together with the job description for the Deputy and an Administrator, Business Services. (Funding to support this office will be identified from budgetary resources within the business-related functions of the district, i.e., Cafeteria Funds, Property Management Funds, etc.).
  4. Change the reporting line of the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Office from the Finance and Budget Division to the Office of the Deputy Superintendent of Business Services.
  5. Change the title of the Office of the Deputy Superintendent to the Office of the Deputy Superintendent of Academic Services and approve the job description for the same. (The existing positions and operating budget in the current Office of the Deputy Superintendent will support this office).
  6. Change the reporting line of NCLB Private School Support from the Federal and Special Programs Division to the Charter School Office.
  7. Change the reporting line of Supplemental Educational Services (SES) from the Federal and Special Programs Division to the Prime Time (formerly 6-to-6 program) Office under Student Services.
  8. Change the reporting line of Race/Human Relations from District Relations to Instructional Support Services Division (which includes Off Campus Integrated Learning Experiences [OCILE] programs).
  9. Change the reporting line of Early Childhood Education Programs from the Federal and Special Programs Division to the Instructional Support Services Division.
  10. Change the title of the Federal and Special Programs Division to the Monitoring, Accountability, and Compliance (MAC) Office. (Funding to support staffing and operating costs will be identified among existing mandated and special program funding resources as appropriate.)
  11. Change the reporting line of Program Monitoring from the Federal and Special Programs Division to the Monitoring, Accountability, and Compliance Office.
  12. Change the reporting line of Parent Outreach and Engagement Programs from the Federal and Special Programs Division to the District Relations Office (which includes Communications and Community Relations).

Upon board approval, staff will take the necessary steps to implement the above-noted recommended actions.


FISCAL IMPACT: Recommendation 3: The proposed 2010-11 budget for the Office of the Deputy Superintendent of Business Services is $561,215. Funding will be identified from budgetary resources within the business-related functions of the district, i.e., Cafeteria Funds, Property Management Funds, etc. Existing staff and services will not be impacted by this action.

Recommendation 5: There are no new costs associated with the proposed 2010-11 budget for the Office of the Deputy Superintendent of Academic Services. The established positions and operating budget in the current Office of the Deputy Superintendent (Cost Center 5635) are sufficient to support this office.

Recommendation 10: Any funding needs of the Monitoring, Accountability and Compliance Office will be covered by existing mandated and special program funding resources as appropriate.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: There is no negative impact to district staffing.

BACKGROUND: On March 16, 2010, the Board of Education discussed the district's organization and management structure. The board approved a structure for executive management positions to include a Deputy Superintendent of Academic Services and a Deputy Superintendent of Business Services.

The proposed districtwide organization as described in this report is based upon the board-approved structure, and is designed to enable shared authority among the Deputy positions (with the Superintendent continuing to serve as the ultimate authority), create greater efficiencies, and provide long-term stability within the organization.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the renewal of charter petition for Keiller Leadership Academy Charter School.

FISCAL IMPACT: $3,194,000.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education approve the renewal charter petition for Keiller Leadership Academy (KLA) for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2010, and expiring on June 30, 2015, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition. The Office of Charter Schools recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the board’s decision to renew the charter petition. KLA was originally approved to be a charter school on March 1, 2005, by the Board of Education and has been operating as a charter school that offers Grades 6-8 in the San Diego Unified School District for five years. For the renewal charter petition, the charter indicates it will add an additional grade to offer a 6-9 grade configuration. The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective immediately, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition and will expire on June 30, 2015. The potential fiscal impact to the district is an estimated loss of $3,194,000 generated in average daily attendance in 2010-11 because the district does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools. District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met requirements to continue performing as a charter school in San Diego Unified.

If the Board of Education desires additional options beyond approval, these options could be:

  • Approve the charter petition with conditions. In essence, the petition is approved with conditions set by the Board of Education.
  • Deny the charter petition. The Board of Education would need to identify written findings to support a decision to deny the charter renewal.


Academic Performance Summary: KLA has met the academic performance requirements required for renewal by charter law. The school received a Growth Academic Performance Index (API) of 710 for the 2008-09 school year, which represents a 28 point increase over the 2007-08 API score of 682, and student academic achievement at KLA is at least equal to district schools where students would have otherwise attended. Thus, KLA has met the state required academic performance requirements to be eligible for charter renewal.

Financial Analysis: Based on a thorough fiscal analysis, the school has been found to be in sound fiscal health and deemed to be financially viable.

District Review of the School: A district review team has visited KLA in the 2007-08, 2008-09, 2009-10 school years. The review team examined academic performance, financial management, financial solvency, governance and operations. The review determined that the school met criteria requirements to be eligible for charter renewal. The school was found to have demonstrated academic success and to have demonstrated operational viability. Overall, the school was found to have: met state requirements to be eligible for renewal; submitted all required financial reports on time; demonstrated effective governance and organizational leadership; and demonstrated effective management of operations.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Special Education Memorandums of Understanding with the following charter schools:
  1. Iftin Charter School.

2. Promise Charter School.

3. O'Farrell Community School Charter School.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: On December 1, 2009, the Board of Education approved a template Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) setting forth the terms and conditions under which the district and district authorized charter schools will interact with each other regarding Special Education. In collaboration with charter schools, the district Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) template to outline the relationship between the district and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services. Iftin Charter School has selected Option 1, which means that the school will be considered a public school of the district for the purposes of delivery of special education services and funding. Under the collaboratively-developed MOU, charter schools will have the following three options for the delivery of Special Education services:
  1. Charter schools elect to be considered as a public school of the district for the purposes of special education services and funding. The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the district, in return, the district will provide special education services.
  2. Charter Schools are designated as schools within the district Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA). With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the district, and the district will reimburse the charter school for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the district for equivalent service providers.
  3. Charter schools are designated as schools of another SELPA. This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified. Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served. The district retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.
