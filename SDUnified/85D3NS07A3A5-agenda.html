RECOMMENDATION: Conditionally approve the charter renewal for King Chavez Arts Academy Charter School for a five-year term beginning on July 1, 2010, and expiring on June 30, 2015, subject to King Chavez Arts Academy fulfilling the terms stated in the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between the District, King Chavez Arts, and King Chavez Public Schools; approve Memorandum of Understanding with King Chavez Arts, and King Chavez Public Schools.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: To be determined.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

BACKGROUND: The Board of Education tabled this item at the June 1, 2010, Board Meeting and continued consideration of it to the July 6, 2010 Board meeting. At the June 1, 2010, Board meeting, the Board indicated that it needed additional information regarding the staff recommendation to renew the charter petition for the King Chavez Arts Academy (KC Arts).

 

The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education conditionally approve the renewal charter petition for KC Arts for a five-year term beginning on July 1, 2010, and expiring on June 30, 2015, subject to KC Arts Academy fulfilling the terms stated in the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) between the District, KC Arts, and King Chavez Public Schools. The MOU is brought to the Board for approval simultaneously with this item. KC Arts was originally authorized by the Board of Education on March 1, 2005 and has been operating as a charter school that offers grades 3-5 in the San Diego Unified School District (SDUSD) for five years. The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective immediately and will expire on June 30, 2015 if KC Arts Academy meets the conditions stated herein and in the MOU between the parties.

 

There is the potential ADA funding impact for any students leaving district operated schools to charters. With that said, there is also the potential for reduction in costs. The students not attending a district operated school could reduce the allocation of staff and services and therefore reduce cost.

This analysis would require detailed student information including school of residence for each student attending the charter school. With this information in the future, staff can analyze the financial impact based on allocation of funds and staffing at the school and district level.

 

Upon conducting a full review of the Renewal Petition, staff determined that although the Renewal Petition could be granted by the Board, there were a number of areas in which some demonstration of improvement and ongoing attention was necessary to instill confidence in the district that KC Arts is able to operate in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations and standards for the conduct of a public charter school over its renewal term.

 

Therefore, district staff recommends the approval of the renewal of the KC Arts for a period of July 1, 2010 through June 30, 2015, with the following conditions:

 

1. KC Arts Academy has revised its petition since initial submission and agrees to further revise its Renewal Petition to ensure it is compliant with the following requirements:

 

a. Description of core courses and curriculum and demonstration of full integration of state standards and assessments in the instructional program at each grade level, including a description of how KC Arts Academy will close the achievement gap using specific interventions proactively and remove itself from the CDE’s list of persistently low achieving schools, as well as the use of formative assessments to inform instruction.

 

b. Description of measurable student outcomes and their methods of assessment, as well as a demonstration of the Charter School’s success over the prior term in implementing assessment and programming to achieve those outcomes.

 

c. Description of a comprehensive program for English Learners that is compliant with federal and state requirements.

 

d. Demonstration of a full understanding of the unique needs of, and requirements for serving, students eligible for special education as an LEA member of a geographically distant Special Education Local Plan Area (“SELPA”).

 

e. Description of the qualification of school employees, including demonstration of the KC Arts Academy’s compliance with NCLB requirements for highly qualified teachers, as well as a listing of all the positions the school will have and the qualifications for such.

 

f. Description of Health and Safety procedures the Charter School intends to follow.

 

g. Description of process(es) for monitoring the school’s racial and ethnic balance.

h. Description of the procedure through which the school will ensure compliance with admission preferences for in-district students, as required for conversion charter schools.

 

i. Description of procedures for suspension/expulsions that meets due process requirements, including procedures for discipline of students with disabilities.

 

j. Description of the retirement systems which reflects KC Arts Academy’s current practice.

 

k. Description of employee rights which includes a recognition that any leave of absence from the District must comply with the Collective Bargaining Agreement, and the Charter School must comply with the Educational Employment Relations Act (EERA) of 1976.

 

l. Description of the governance of KC Arts Academy, including a conflict of interest policy, articles of incorporation and bylaws of the nonprofit public benefit corporation operating KC Arts Academy, and KC Arts Academy’s process for compliance with the Political Reform Act and achievement of such during its last term.

 

m. Commitment to providing a quarterly financial report that provides complete financial information, including any information related to the operation of any Charter Management Organization or consultants providing service to KC Arts Academy.

 

n. Commitment to providing access to all Charter School information including information maintained by any Charter School Organization or consultant providing services to KC Arts Academy.

 

2. KC Arts agrees to voluntarily surrender its charter petition after the third year of operation (June 30, 2013) if it has not met the required API growth rates and requirements for three consecutive years: 2010/11, 2011/12, and 2012/13.

 

3. KC Arts agrees to provide the District with quarterly financial reports commencing in the 2010/11 school year, for the duration of the School Improvement Grant (SIG).

 

4. KC Arts agrees to allow access to its records by the District Internal Auditor to perform a comprehensive audit of the finances for the KC Arts, as well, as the King Chavez Charter Management Organization. Both KC Arts and the King Chavez Management Organization will cooperate with the audit process and provide all documentation necessary for the completion of such audit.


5. KC Arts Academy agrees to provide a conflict of interest policy and articles of incorporation and by laws for King/Chavez Public Schools, the non-profit public benefit corporation the Renewal Petition indicates will operate the KC Arts Academy, with the State seal stamp demonstrating filing therewith.

 

6. The Renewal Petition will be revised to reference the obligation to provide the District any and all documents including documents regarding the Charter Management Organization and non-profit public benefit corporation who is ultimately responsible for KC Arts Academy’s operations.

 

7. KC Arts Academy agrees to execute this Agreement as well as the District’s Memorandum of Understanding pertaining to KC Arts Academy operations, special education and oversight.

 

In the event that any of these conditions is not met, as determined in the sole discretion of the Board, KC Arts charter school has agreed to surrender its charter and complete the statutorily required closure proceedings, without legal action or appeal.

 

The approval of the Petition is further based upon acceptance of KC Arts Academy’s offer that the charter be renewed on the condition that KC Arts Academy will voluntarily surrender its charter at the end of the 2012-2013 school year (based upon the KC Arts Academy’s calendar), and that KC Arts Academy will proceed with closure in accordance with the terms of the charter and applicable law, without the right to appeal, if the District Board determines that KC Arts Academy does not meet the conditions stated herein.

 

All of the above conditions are to be set forth in written agreement which must be executed by KC Arts Academy and the non-profit public benefit corporation operating KC Arts Academy (currently King Chavez Public Schools) on or before July 15, 2010 in order for the renewal to take effect. The charter to be granted based upon KC Arts Academy’s successful completion of all conditions stated herein, including timely execution of the attached agreement.

 

However, if the Board of Education desires additional options beyond conditional approval, it could:


• Approve the charter petition without conditions. The charter would be authorized to continue to operate and bound by the Petition it submitted for the standard five year term.


• Deny the renewal of the charter petition. If the Board denies the charter petition it must adopt written findings. In the case of the non-renewal of KC Arts, the following are the findings in support of non-renewal [according to Education Code Section 47605(b)(1), (2), (3), and (5)].


• The finding with respect to section 47605(b)(1) is that the charter school presents an unsound educational program for students to be enrolled in the charter school.


• The finding with respect to section 47605(b)(2) is that the petitioners are demonstrably unlikely to implement a successfully implement the program set forth in the charter petition.

 

The following facts reflect and support the Board’s finding that KC Arts presents an unsound educational program for students:


• The school has ranked a State Decile Rank of 1 for the past 3 years (Table 3 of Board Report).


• The school has oscillated between a 1 and 5 Similar Schools Rank, without any consistency (Table 3 of Board Report).


• The school achieved a 25 point decline in the API for the 2008/09 school year (Table 4 of Board Report).


• The school has not met AYP in the prior 4 years of data that is available (Table 5 of Board Report).


• In 2007/08, KC Arts students did not demonstrate a higher percentage of students proficient in ELA than any of the nine SDUSD schools where KC Arts students would have attended if not enrolled in the Charter School (see Graph 1 of Board Report).


• Overall, the ELA academic performance tended to be much lower performing than District schools where students would have otherwise attended, and Math academic performance tended to be lower than District schools where students would have otherwise attended. Taken as a whole, the school was found to not have performed at least as well as the schools where students would have otherwise attended (Graphs 1 and 2 of Board Report).

 

The following facts are in support of the Board finding that the petitioners are demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program set forth in the charter petition:


• The school was listed by the California Department of Education as being a “Consistently Lowest-Achieving” school.


• The school operators have tried to implement several different interventions required when a school is identified as consistently low performing: during its first term, the school dismissed a large number of teaching staff and also school dismissed the previous director, and appointed a new director.


• None of the actions taken to this point have demonstrated success; rather, as reflected by the API Growth score, the school experienced a 25 point decline after the restructuring was accomplished.


• A more in-depth review of the bylaws presented for the KC Arts school indicates that the school does not have a set of bylaws for the KC Arts schools or King Chavez Public Schools, the non-profit public benefit corporation listed as operating the KC Arts Academy in the Renewal Petition. The articles of incorporation and bylaws presented were for the King Chavez KC Excellence school, not KC Arts Academy or the non-profit public benefit corporation which the petition indicates operates the charter school.


• According to the California Secretary of State’s website, he entity referred to as, “King Chavez Public Schools” does not legally exist, and it appears from the Petition that the charter school is operated by the King Chavez Academy of Excellence and not King Chavez Public Schools.


• The KC Arts Academy does not appear to have a Board of Directors that represents their interest, nor any advisory body to ensure that there is a representation of the school’s best interest before the King Chavez Academy of Excellence Board.


• Lastly, after four years of operation, the school has not met Element 7 [Education Code Section 47605(b)(5)(g)], which indicates the means by which the school will achieve a racial and ethnic balance among its pupils that is reflective of the general population residing within the territorial jurisdiction of the school district to which the charter petition is submitted (e.g. San Diego Unified School District). For reference, see Table 1.

 

If the Board elects to non-renew the charter for KC Arts, the preceding findings may be adopted in support of their determination. Further, the preceding facts also support the Board findings to non-renew the charter for KC Arts. If the Board elects to non-renew the charter petition for KC Arts, then the conditional renewal agreement shall not be adopted and shall have no force and effect.

RECOMMENDATION: Conditionally approve the renewal charter for Magnolia Science Academy Charter School for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2010, and expiring on June 30, 2015, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: To be determined.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

BACKGROUND: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education conditionally approve the renewal charter petition for Magnolia Science Academy San Diego (MSA SD) for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2010, and expiring on June 30, 2015, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition. The Office of Charter Schools recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the board’s determination of the conditional renewal of the charter petition. MSA SD was originally approved under the name, “Momentum Charter School” to be a charter school on March 1, 2005, by the Board of Education and has been operating as a charter school that offers Grades 6-9 in the district for five years. The conditional renewal of the charter petition shall become effective immediately, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition and will expire on June 30, 2015.  There is the potential ADA funding impact for any students leaving the district operated schools to charter schools.  With that said, there is also the potential for reduction in costs.  The students not attending a district operated school could reduce the allocation of staff and services and therefore, reduce cost.  This analysis would require detailed student information including school of residence for each student attending the charter school.  With this information in the future, staff can analyze the financial impact based on allocation of funds and staffing at the school and district level. District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met the minimum requirements to continue performing as a charter school in San Diego Unified.

 

Staff is recommending the following conditions be part of the renewal for the charter of MSA SD Charter School:

  • The petitioners shall submit to the district by September 1, 2010, a comprehensive professional development plan for school administrators regarding bullying and discipline.
  • The petitioners shall submit to the district by September 1, 2010, documentary evidence that the Magnolia foundation has established a local board, based in San Diego, this board will advise the Magnolia Foundation and be a part of the accountability of the school’s administration and academic performance. The charter already contains a Site Advisory Board, but documentation must be submitted that reflects that this is implemented by September 1, 2010.
  • The petitioners shall develop a more robust discipline policy. The policy must provide clarity of the steps that the school will take regarding bullying complaints. This policy must be clearly communicated to all stakeholders, including, but not limited to parents, teachers, administrators, and other staff at the school.

 

If the Board of Education desires additional options beyond conditional approval, these options could be: 

  • Approve the charter petition without conditions.
  • Deny the charter petition. The Board of Education would need to adopt written facts to support findings in support of a decision to deny the charter renewal.
