RECOMMENDATION: Staff will present an update on the Proposition S and Proposition MM Financial Model that includes issuance of Capital Appreciation Bonds and Current Interest Bonds.

 

On January 7, 2014, bond resolutions authorizing issuance of Proposition S and Proposition MM will be brought forward for board approval.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Independent Citizens Oversight Executive Subcommittee - December 4, 2013.

 

BACKGROUND:  An election was held for the San Diego Unified School District, County of San Diego, California on November 4, 2008, at which a bond proposition ("Proposition S") was passed by electors of the district authorizing the sale of $2.1 billion in general obligation bonds pursuant to California Constitution Article XIII (A), to be used for the construction, reconstruction and/or rehabilitation of school facilities.  Proposition S provides that the funds shall be used for items described in the November 4, 2008, County of San Diego Registrar of Voters Sample Ballot and Voter Information Pamphlet. The Voter's Information Pamphlet permits work to be accomplished at school sites throughout the district.

 

As part of the current plan of finance for the Proposition S Bond Program, the district anticipates issuing one or more series of bonds, as needed, every year, until the full authorization has been issued.  The plan proposes the sale of bonds that allow for the compounding of interest.  The district's financial advisor and underwriting team will discuss the bond financing model and available options for upcoming Proposition S, new monies transaction, and Proposition MM refunding.

 

On January 7, 2014, bond resolutions authorizing issuance of Proposition S and Proposition MM will be brought forward for board approval.
