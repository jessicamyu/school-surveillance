Subject
1. Proposal to Eliminate Referral of Unpaid Transportation Fees to a Collection Agency (Evans/Whitehurst-Payne/Beiser)
Meeting
Feb 13, 2018 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes

RECOMMENDATION:  Trustees John Lee Evans, Sharon Whitehurst-Payne and Kevin Beiser will discuss transportation fees for parent paid transportation services and propose that the Board direct staff to discontinue the referral of unpaid transportation bills to a collection agency.  All transportation bills, including delinquent ones, will be handled by District staff.  If the financial situation of a family changes and the student becomes eligible for free or reduced priced meals, no further attempts to collect the fees will be made.

 

Trustee Beiser will introduce a proposed resolution in support of Assembly Bill 1974, the Primary Education Fair Debt Collection Act.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  The  financial impact is unknown at this time.

 

BACKGROUND:  Free transportation mandated by law has been significantly reduced due to the expiration of No Child Left Behind, Program Improvement Program and other factors.  Students being transported as a required service of their Individualized Education Program (IEP) continue to receive free transportation as required by law.  State funding is not sufficient for the District to provide free transportation for every student who attends a magnet school or other special program.

 

The District’s Fee for Transportation Program is available for eligible riders who choose to participate in the program, but any student whose family meets the eligibility criteria for free or reduced priced meals is provided with free transportation as required by law.

 

During the school year no student who is currently receiving transportation services will be denied transportation due to their parents being behind in their payments.

 

The Transportation department will continue to provide various payment options to parents and if requested by a parent, will make reasonable payment plan accommodations.

 

It is our hope that the voluntary paid transportation program may be converted to free transportation for all once the state provides full and adequate funding for our schools.

 

 

Assembly Bill 1974, the Primary Education Fair Debt Collection Act

Assemblymember Lorena Gonzalez, representing much of our district and a strong advocate for public education, has proposed the Primary Education Fair Debt Collection Act, or AB 1974.  The proposed legislation aligns generally with the belief of San Diego Unified that family income should never interfere with a student’s access to public education.  The proposed legislation would bring into alignment all school districts in the state in supporting a commitment to maintain access to education services for all students regardless of family income.

AB 1974 would prohibit the use of debt collection agencies to collect any fees or other financial obligations owed by any current or former students or their families.  The district has previously relied on collections agencies but has now determined such work can be handled internally by district staff.  The use of district staff to seek payment of unpaid fees will avoid the appearance of detrimental information appearing on any of our families’ credit reports.

The legislature has yet to consider AB 1974, and some elements of the bill which create unlimited liability issues for school districts across the state will hopefully be improved during the legislative process.  The Board endorses passage of the Primary Education Fair Debt Collection Act, or AB 1974, subject to the elimination of those provisions creating unlimited liability for school districts.
