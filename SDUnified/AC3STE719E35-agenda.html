BACKGROUND:  Education Code 47614 and the California Code of Regulations, Title Sections 11969 et seq. (Regulations) require:

  • Districts offer facilities, to the extent required by law, to a charter school that submits a legally complete application;
  • A charter school wishing to occupy district facilities in the 2017-18 school year must submit a complete application for facilities by November 1, 2016;
  • Districts analyze each charter school's request in conformity with the law and raise concerns to the charter school regarding the charter school's stated in-district classroom ADA projections, prior to developing the district's preliminary proposal in response to the application;
  • On or before February 1, districts must submit preliminary proposals to eligible charter schools and, on or before March 1, charter schools must respond with any concerns regarding the preliminary proposals; this year, all ten charter schools applying agreed to postpone receipt of their preliminary proposals until February 7, and will respond by March 7.
  • On or before April 1 (a Saturday this year), districts must submit final offers of facilities to eligible charter schools and, on or before May 1, charter schools must respond to the final offers if they are to receive district facilities for the upcoming year.
  • In the case of Old Town Academy, the district determined that it was only possible to offer two sites as a preliminary proposal, rather than one contiguous site.  The regulations (11969.2(d)) require that the board make a finding that a charter school could not be accommodated at a single site.  The reasons for this finding are included in Section E of the attached report.  Thrive Public Schools is also proposed to be located on multiple sites, but they requested multiple sites in their application.

Please note that the preliminary proposals are only the first step in the process of developing final offers of facilities to charter schools.  At this point, it is unknown whether the preliminary proposals will be accepted by the charter school applicants. Board approval of these preliminary proposals is required in order to continue this process, including discussion of preliminary proposals with the charter schools, district schools impacted by the proposals, and to meet the statutory requirements to present these proposals to charter schools by February 1, 2017.
