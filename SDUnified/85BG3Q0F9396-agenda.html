Review and accept the School Assistance and Intervention Team (SAIT) Report of Findings and Recommended Corrective Actions for Wilson Middle School as required by the Public Schools Accountability Act (PSAA) of 1999. The PSAA established the High Priority Schools Grant Program (HPSGP) for low-performing schools, regardless of their relative progress. HSPGP schools that do not meet acceptable progress criteria are identified as state-monitored.

Wilson Middle School did not meet the criteria for exiting HPSGP. Wilson met all growth targets in 2003-04; in 2004-05 the school made growth but did not meet its targets. No growth information or targets were available for 2005-06 or 2006-07 because Wilson Middle School did not have a valid 2006 API Base. When a school does not have API Base data, the California Department of Education (CDE) uses an alternative method to determine if the school is making adequate progress. Using alternative growth criteria, the CDE determined that Wilson Middle School did not make adequate progress during 2005-06 and 2006-07. As a result the school was identified as state monitored in November 2007.

As required by state legislation, the District contracted with a state-approved provider (Perry and Associates) to deploy a School Assistance and Intervention Team (SAIT) to visit Wilson Middle School to develop site-specific corrective action plans. The SAIT’s Report of Findings and Recommended Corrective Actions for Wilson Middle School is being submitted for Board review and acceptance. Approval to submit to the State Board of Education a waiver to allow the District to maintain a 1 percent reserve for economic uncertainties.
Currently, San Diego Unified School District (SDUSD) is operating under a requirement to maintain a 2% general fund reserve. However, SDUSD previously operated under a 1% budget reserve requirement pursuant to the provisions of SB 2023 (Alpert/1998), which expired in 2003. Subsequent to the law’s expiration, the California State Board approved a SDUSD waiver for 2005-2006 (CDSIS-108-4-2004-W-1) allowing SDUSD to maintain a 1% reserve. SDUSD operated in a sound financial manner under the 1% budget reserve. The waiver request for 2008-09 would allow SDUSD to operate once again under the 1% reserve requirement. The 1% reserve requirement would provide the District additional flexibility during a time of state budgetary cuts, while still maintaining $11 million in budgetary reserves. Failure to obtain this waiver would require San Diego to make $11 million more in cuts to instructional programs in order to maintain the higher budget reserve.
