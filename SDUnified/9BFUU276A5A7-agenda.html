The percentage of district students reclassified to English proficient has remained at approximately 10 percent from 2009-2010 to 2012-2013; and the percentage of students being reclassified on an annual basis at the individual school site level ranges from 4 percent to 20 percent or more.

 

Schools will support, monitor achievement of Long Term English Learners, and establish yearly progress targets for reclassification.

 

The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District is committed to improving outcomes for English Language Learners as a district strategy to close the achievement gap.

3. Comprehensive Plan for Integrating Opening of Salk Elementary into Mira Mesa Cluster in September 2015 (Evans/Foster)
Meeting
May 27, 2014 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes

Background: After more than two decades of delay, Salk Elementary will open in September 2015, in the Mira Mesa Cluster.  The school was originally planned in the 1980's since the schools in Mira Mesa were filled beyond capacity and had adopted rotating year round schedules to accommodate four classes in three classrooms.  Portables were also added and Mira Mesa has many that are beyond their reasonable life span. 

 

Currently, there is a major development on the east end along I-15 and in future years a large new development is planned on the west end.  Salk is located in a neighborhood in the southwest quadrant of Mira Mesa.  The neighborhood is separated from the rest of Mira Mesa by two major thoroughfares, south of Mira Mesa Blvd and west of Camino Ruiz.  The construction of the school began in September 2013, and it is scheduled to open as a K-5 school in September 2015.

 

The Mira Mesa Cluster has been involved in an intensive planning process during the 2013-14 school year with input from parents, staff, and community members at several meetings.  Mira Mesa has identified itself as a comprehensive college and career ready, focused cluster.  It has extensive arts programs and career technical education programs and a high level of focus on college preparation.  The integration of the new school offers the opportunity to reinforce a smooth K-12 articulation across the cluster.

 

MOTION -- Board Trustees Evans and Foster will ask the board to approve the following:

  • The opening of Salk requires establishment of attendance boundaries for the new school, as well as adjustment of the attendance boundaries of the other schools in the interests of balancing the enrollments, but with particular attention to the safety of students and the preservation of logical neighborhood boundaries.  See new attendance boundary map and chart, which is based on community consensus, attached.
  • The plan developed by community and staff also includes provisions for grandfathering to allow students currently attending one elementary school to stay at that school if they do not wish to change to the school in the new boundaries.
  • Additionally, to facilitate higher attendance at neighborhood schools and less choicing of students to other schools (often due to calendar conflicts), it is the intent of the board that the three year-round schools (Ericson, Mason, Walker) would convert to traditional calendars to align the cluster schools at all levels and to ensure balanced enrollment in the neighborhood schools.  Changes to school calendars would occur in the 2015-16 school year.  While the cluster council, including parents and staff, has reached consensus on this issue, this action directs district staff to engage with affected bargaining units on this change prior to implementation.

  • To facilitate the orderly articulation of Hage Elementary students through middle and high school, the Mira Mesa Cluster has come to a consensus on directly aligning it with Mira Mesa High School, rather than the current optional enrollment with Scripps Ranch.  This is consistent with Vision 2020 Vision 2: Schools as Neighborhood Learning Centers, Section C: "Clusters will ensure that there is continuity for the neighborhood students in the K-12 program."  This action will direct staff to enter into further discussions regarding this change prior to implementation with all of the families affected at Hage Elementary and Wangenheim Middle School, as well as consultation with Scripps Ranch High School and the Scripps Ranch Cluster.

  • With all of the above changes, facilities staff will conduct a review of all aging portables in the cluster to determine whether they need to be replaced or eliminated due to enrollment shifts, as well as other campus facilities needs.  Staff will also undertake a review of the need for any increased capacity on the campuses of the middle schools and high school due to the ongoing growth of the Mira Mesa community.

FISCAL IMPACT:  There are no budget costs for 2013-14.  The estimated cost for 2015-16 due to the potential change of some teachers from a year round to traditional school calendar at three schools (Ericson, Mason, Walker) is approximately $737,305 (148 FTE).  This amount includes site based positions only, such as principal, vice principal, counseling and nursing, special education, clerical, campus security, and noon duty. It does not include food services, transportation, support services positions budgeted in the central office departments.  Note: some teachers who might be excessed from a school due to a shift in students to Salk Elementary, would most likely move to a traditional calendar anyway.

BACKGROUND: The district requires waste and recyclables collection on an ongoing basis.  These waste and recycling services are necessary to maintain clean and healthy facilities, while providing recycling and other waste diversion services that will save the district money on disposal costs and contribute to the state’s 75 percent waste reduction goal by 2020.

 

On July 9, 2013, the board approved an agreement with EDCO Disposal Corporation to continue providing waste and recycling collection through August 14, 2014.  The current agreement is intended to bridge the time between the expiration of the previous agreement and this new broadened RFP for a broad base of trash, recycling, etc. efforts.

 

On February 26, 2014, the district received three proposals in response to the dstrict’s RFP from the following vendors: EDCO Disposal Corporation, Republic Services, and Waste Management.  A five-member Evaluation Committee was formed and charged with finding the best proposed Waste and Recyclables Collection and Management Services solution(s) for the district and recommend the vendor with the best overall proposal.  The Committee included representation from various positions/roles across the district including central office staff, Physical Plant Operations management, and waste and recycling technical staff.  The committee also included one member from the City of San Diego Environmental Services Department.

 

All proposers were required to submit a Baseline Service Fixed Price.  Republic Services was the only proposer to submit an alternate pricing option, which was allowable under this RFP.  Their option, or Service Improvement Plan, was based on using six cubic yard trash and recyclables collection dumpsters at certain sites, instead of the standard three cubic yard dumpsters. This is a valid consideration for reducing truck traffic to school sites and decreasing costs; however, there is no guarantee that the number of larger dumpsters proposed could actually be located at the sites listed in the alternate pricing proposal without further review and site approval.  In order to give consideration to the effort put into this option, Strategic Sourcing and Contracts completed a cost analysis using pricing provided by the other two vendors for three and six cubic yard dumpsters at the level proposed by Republic Services.  In this scenario, Waste Management had the lower pricing.

 

The committee's evaluation of the written proposals determined that all three proposers should be further evaluated utilizing an oral interview.  The interviews consisted of a series of questions that were developed in order to evaluate, in further detail, the proposer’s experience, implementation plan, customer service approach, education and outreach commitment, waste reduction plans, reporting, website development, incentives, and pricing.  The proposer with the highest overall scoring of both the written proposals and the oral interviews was EDCO Disposal Corporation. 

 

EDCO Disposal Corporation will be providing the district with containers to collect waste and recyclable materials, and provide haul-away service/recycling service as applicable.  This new contract provides the district with an opportunity to create a partnership with a waste and recycling company, rather than a strict service agreement.  In a side-by-side comparison of costs from our current contract pricing there is an annual savings of just over $30,000.

 

In addition, this new contract provides significant value-added services that will assist the district in reducing overall costs, educating staff and students, and providing reporting tools to facilitate program planning.  Under the new contact, EDCO will:

 

1) Provide $5,000 annually as an incentive to reward the best recycling schools in the district (1st, 2nd, 3rd, and honorable mentions).

 

2) Create a dedicated web-page (on their existing company website) for district schools and sites to access information on recycling competitions, school waste and recycling rates (by site), and other information we deem important/appropriate.  Sites would have a specific code to access the website.

 

3) Create a dedicated Environmental Times newsletter specific to our schools to be distributed twice per year (content to be approved by the district).

 

4)  Conduct waste and recyclable composition studies to provide information and  detailed breakdowns of the materials in each stream, as well as waste quantities (tonnage) to determine generation and diversion rates.

 

5)  Conduct up to six waste composition analysis annually for specific 40-yard roll-off container loads to identify material that can be diverted from disposal and assist in educating the sites on diversion alternatives/strategies.

 

6)  Assist student groups in conducting on-site waste audits in order to provide visual feedback on the types and amounts of waste disposed by students at their school and to tailor specific diversion programs for the school.

 

7)  Provide a quarterly report that would overview the district’s diversion efforts, including program participation and compliance, quantity estimates for all waste streams, and projected results looking forward.

 

8)  Exchange all existing trash and recycling bins with new bins prior to the start of the Agreement.

 

9)  Visit all sites within the first three months of the contract to assess service needs and levels (right-size service) and identity potential to increase recyclables diversion by site.

 

10) Adjust service levels, based on right-sizing evaluations with the goal to decrease collection of non-recyclables by 15 percent within the first three months of the contract. On an annual basis this is equal to a $138,000 cost savings.

 

11) Instruct drivers to conduct bin-fullness surveys (visual survey to determine ¼, ½, ¾, or full status of each bin) when servicing district sites to continually provide feedback on appropriate service levels or trends.

 

12) Provide a detailed follow-up plan for each site to increase diversion levels.

 

13) Prepare an initial education plan and meet with the district to review the plan shortly after contract award.

 

14) Actively seek qualified environmental education organizations to assist and reach out to student clubs and organization.

It ensures that all courses are rigorous and provide students with multiple pathways to graduation, college, and career.  For 2013-14, the Committee is comprised of representatives from the following divisions and departments: Office of Teaching and Learning, Office of Accountability, Office of Language Acquisition; Special Education; Counseling and Guidance; Instructional Materials; College, Career, and Technical Education; Advanced Studies; Education Technology; Research and Reporting; Dropout Prevention, one middle school principal, and one high school principal who represents the high school academic council.
