Superintendent Marten will discuss Vision 2020 which provides quality schools in every neighborhood.  Schools are organized into clusters to ensure there is a K-12 continuity for neighborhood students.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District has adopted Vision 2020 as the cornerstone vision for guiding and focusing our work.  As part of Vision 2020, schools will serve as “neighborhood learning centers” with the overarching goal to establish and maintain a strong bond between a community and its schools.

 

Three main objectives support the creation of neighborhood schools as learning centers:

  1. Schools will be safe, attractive learning centers.
  2. Schools will extend beyond the school site.
  3. Schools will be organized into clusters for greater community cohesion.

The goals and structure for clusters include:

  • Clusters will consist of a high school and middle school and elementary schools that feed into it.
  • Clusters will ensure there is continuity for the neighborhood students in the K-12 program.
  • Cluster Councils will promote the schools in their communities.
  • Cluster Councils will work with schools, community and district staff to improve the quality of neighborhood schools.
  • Cluster Councils will be a democratic representation of the school community including teachers, administrators, support staff, students, parents and community members.
