RECOMMENDATION: Ratification of Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with San Diego Education Association (SDEA) for the 2016-2017 Supplemental Early Retirement Program (SERP) in accordance with section 3547.5 of the California Government Code and section 1031 of the Bylaws of the Board of Education.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Various funds.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  San Diego Unified School District and San Diego Education Association have met and conferred in good faith and agreed on an MOU for a Supplemental Early Retirement Program for 2016-2017.  The SERP will only be available to SDEA bargaining unit members in accordance with the terms contained in the attached MOU. 

 

In order to be eligible for the SERP, SDEA members must meet all six criteria as set forth in the MOU, summarized below: 

 

(1) Must be an SDEA unit member;

(2) Must be a permanent contract employee of the district or College, Career, Technical Education teacher as of February 21, 2017;

(3) Must have five years of contracted district service as of June 30, 2017, for traditional school or central office employees, or as of July 21, 2017, for year-round school employees;

(4) Must be eligible to retire under STRS or PERS as of June 30, 2017, for traditional school or central office employees, or as of July 21, 2017, for year-round school employees;

(5) Must submit all required SERP enrollment materials by April 21, 2017; and

(6) Must resign from the district as of June 30, 2017, for the traditional school or central office calendar or July 21, 2017, for year-round employees.  

 

The district and SDEA agreed that the SERP must have sufficient plan participation in order to meet the district’s fiscal and operational objectives by the enrollment deadline of April 21, 2017, in order for the SERP to go into effect for SDEA.  These objectives include that the SERP will generate net savings, or no net cost, to the district’s General Fund-Unrestricted, in the initial year of implementation and cumulatively over five years.  Further, the district reserves the right to determine which positions vacated by retirement shall be filled, provided that any net savings to the General Fund-Unrestricted from the 2016-2017 SERP in Fiscal Year 2017-2018, will only be used to fill positions that are in needed credential areas and/or are needed to meet contractual obligations.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve proposed solutions including specific 2017-18 budget solutions and resolutions to reduce and/or eliminate certificated and classified services;  approve the revision and abolishment of specific position descriptions; and approve the 2017-18 organizational structure, in preparation for approving the Second Interim Financial Report on March 14, 2017, and adopting the 2017-18 district budget on June 27, 2017. Resolutions and exhibits include:

  • Certain Certificated Services (PKS)
  • Non-Represented Managers
  • Confidentials
  • Administrators Association of San Diego (AASD) Classified Supervisors
  • Operational Support Services (OSS)

  • San Diego City Schools Police Officers’ Association (POA)

FISCAL IMPACT: These budget and staffing decisions will have significant impact to the district. All actions taken by the board will be to prepare a balanced budget for 2017-18. The proposed solutions total $124.4 million. Some of the solutions are subject to negotiations with the district’s bargaining units. The specific solutions are attached.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.


IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Impacts to district staffing are reflected in the attachments which include:

  • Resolution in the Matter of Reducing or Eliminating Certain Certificated Services for the 2017-18 school year.
  • Resolutions in the Matter of the Reduction or Elimination of Classified Services: Non- Represented Managers; Confidentials; Administrators Association of San Diego (AASD); Operational Support Services (OSS); Office Technical and Business Services (OTBS); Paraeducators (PARA); San Diego City Schools Police Officers’ Association (POA).

Approval and full implementation of the personnel resolutions will result in a reduction in currently performed certificated and classified services, and a corresponding reduction in permanent and probationary employees (as appropriate) as well as vacant positions.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district has been grappling with a variety of structural challenges including declining enrollment, pension contribution increases, labor market pressures, and mandates from the state and federal governments. Stability can be achieved but it requires efficiently using available resources in order to focus on student achievement. The proposed solutions to the projected deficit in the 2017-18 budget will enable the district to meet its mandates and requirements, as well as the goals of Vision 2020. At this time the projected budget deficit for 2017-18 is $124.4 million.

 

Approximately 92 percent of the district’s unrestricted budget is dedicated to staff, including salary and benefit costs. In order to balance the 2017-18 budget and reduce the district’s structural deficit, and in conjunction with the March 14 approval of a second interim report, the district must take action to allow for necessary reductions in staffing.

 

Certificated staff

For permanent certificated employees, the Education Code requires that the board identify the particular kinds of services or programs that may be reduced or eliminated in the upcoming fiscal year. For probationary certificated employees, Article 19 of the collective bargaining agreement between the district and SDEA allows the board to determine that reductions are necessary due to lack of funds.

  • Permanent certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a statutory layoff notice on or before March 15, 2017.
  • Probationary certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a contractual layoff notice on or before April 15, 2017.
  • Noticed employees’ due process rights include either a request for hearing (permanent) or the filing of a grievance (probationary).
  • Temporary certificated employees will receive a separate reminder notice that their services will end at the close of the 2016-2017 school year.

The list of services to be reduced are stated as a full-time equivalent (FTE) rather than a number of employees. It is determined based on factors related to potentially disputed issues regarding seniority (and deviation therefrom), certificates, and qualifications for certain positions to displace less senior employees, the number of certificated employees receiving these notices will exceed the FTE service reductions listed in the resolutions.

 

Classified staff

Resolutions in the matter of the elimination of the classified services are the result of the budget reductions.  Overall, these eliminations are the result of decreased state funding for public education.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution of Intent to Consider Joint Occupancy Proposals at Revere Center located at 6735 Gifford Way, San Diego CA 92111 (Revere Center).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Facilities Planning and Construction, Real Estate, and Legal Services staff support this effort.  Additional professional service providers, such as an appraiser, will supplement staff, as needed, to complete this effort (costs to be determined).

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Under Education Code sections 17515-17526, school districts may enter into joint occupancy agreements relating to real property and buildings. In February 2014, the board established guiding principals for its real estate utilization strategy which included long-term, joint occupancy of district property to generate new, ongoing revenue all while retaining property ownership and leveraging improvements to educational facilities.

 

In July 2014, the board adopted a resolution of intent to consider proposals to enter into joint occupancy agreements to develop certain district sites for joint occupancy; the Revere Center was one of these sites.  A request for proposal (RFP) was released on August 1, 2014, with final proposals due February 2, 2015.  At that time, all proposals received for Revere Center were rejected for various reasons with no further action at the site.

 

In consideration of the board’s real estate utilization strategy, the district is again requesting proposals for joint occupancy at Revere Center. As such, pursuant to Education Code section 17521, the board must adopt a resolution of intent (resolution) for the purposes of receiving proposals. Following board adoption of this resolution, staff will release an RFP to solicit highly qualified development teams to submit proposals.  The RFP will also provide development objectives, to include improved facilities for educational purposes, and guidelines for proposal submission.

 

In accordance with the RFP, a pre-qualification process will be conducted by district staff to determine the best qualified development teams (teams). The pre-qualification process includes mandatory attendance at a pre-submittal conference by all interested parties. From the pre-qualification process, the best qualified teams will be given the opportunity to submit final proposals which must include community input and concerns.

 

A timeline of these activities is attached for board consideration.
