RECOMMENDATION:  Information only.   Staff will report on San Diego Unified support for our military families. 

 

BACKGROUND: San Diego Unified services the largest number of military connected students in the state of California. Overall, we have over 13,000 military children and service 6 military bases within our district boundaries. Almost every school within our district services a child whose parent or guardian is serving or has served in our armed forces. San Diego Unified School District honors their commitment by assuring our military families that their children will receive the best public education possible and by providing them with a variety of resources and services catered to their unique needs.

 

Military children move from school to school an average of nine times during their K-12 years. As their parents serve our country, it is our duty to focus on effective strategies and essential resources that provide the best educational outcomes for their children and all our students. It is our promise to strengthen our military families as they have strengthened our country.

 

Because of our strong connection to the military community we are proud to support our military families with one on one supports in a variety of ways. This includes welcoming new families to their neighborhood school, offering services that help match them with similar educational programs they were enrolled in previously, as well as introductions to local military liaisons that can help with any issues or resources they may need.

 

All of our schools have military-friendly environments with programs, teachers and counselors trained to aid in the transition and adaptation of our incoming and outgoing military families.

 

Military Family Resource

  • Communication between sending and receiving schools
  • Individual military liaison support
  • Transition services
  • Counseling services
  • Social and emotional support services
  • Academic support
  • Tutoring support
  • Credit recovery support
  • 1:1 school choice counseling
  • Kindergarten readiness workshops
  • Enrollment workshops
  • Before and after school programs
  • Deployment support

This is the highest opinion that can be bestowed on the financial statements of a school bond program.

 

In its performance audit, Crowe Horwath found that "in all significant respects, San Diego Unified School District met the objectives listed . . . and expended Proposition S and Z General Obligation Bond funds for the year ended June 30, 2017 only for the specific projects developed by the District's Governing Board and approved by the voters, in accordance with the requirements of Proposition 39, as specified by Section 1(b)(3)(C) of Article XIIIA of the California Constitution." This is the highest opinion that can be bestowed on an operational performance of a school bond program.

BACKGROUND: San Diego State University (SDSU) Precollege Institute Upward Bound program provided tutoring, test-taking development, strategies for SAT and ACT, financial literacy and college application workshops, college advising, summer component, core curriculum, residential program, recreational activities, and field experiences from June 16, 2017 through July 21, 2017. The program was led by SDSU teachers in collaboration with district teachers. The Upward Bound program was offered to 120 students from Crawford, Hoover, Lincoln, Morse, and San Diego High Schools.
