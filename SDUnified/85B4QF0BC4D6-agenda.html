BACKGROUND: The district's goal is to accelerate student achievement and promote diversity, integration, and school choice by increasing elementary and secondary parent/student magnet school options by offering the International Baccalaureate (IB) World School Middle Years Program through the Mission Bay High

Many district and interdistrict students attend Mission Bay High School cluster schools through School Choice programs including Magnet, Voluntary Enrollment Exchange Program (VEEP), School Choice, and Open Enrollment. Parent options will be enhanced by Pacific Beach Middle School conversion from VEEP to Magnet.

There are two district K-12 IB Program continuums open to families, one through the San Diego High School cluster and the second through the Mission Bay High School cluster. San Diego International Studies School and Roosevelt Middle, as well as, Mission Bay High School are magnet schools providing equitable access to the IB Programme through magnet transportation. At this time, Pacific Beach Middle School’s IB Programme is limited by VEEP constraints, limiting access to the Mission Bay High cluster IB continuum. The current VEEP articulation pattern includes Johnson, Kimbrough, Rodriguez, Sherman, and Webster elementary schools. By granting Pacific Beach Middle School magnet status families across the district will have equal access to either the San Diego High or Mission Bay High IB cluster continuum. This will provide neighboring resident schools to the VEEP schools access to the IB Programme as well. In addition, by providing students districtwide access to Pacific Beach Middle School through magnet transportation, there will be an increase in the number of students better prepared to articulate directly into the IB Diploma Programme at Mission Bay High School.

The magnet articulation pattern, supported by transportation, will begin in 2010-11 following transportation procedures as approved by the Board of Education. Pacific Beach Middle School will continue transportation for students attending the school through VEEP as magnet students. They do not need to reapply to the school.

Discussion of Instructional International Baccalaureate K-12 Articulation - Pacific Beach Middle School was authorized as an International BaccalaureateWorld School by the Geneva based International Baccalaureate Organization in 2007. The reason for the district- initiated move towards establishing IB programs at both Mission Bay High and Pacific Beach Middle was to address concerns expressed by Pacific Beach families. The parent’s primary concern was the “lack of a quality educational program” at the secondary level in Pacific Beach. Many families in Pacific Beach and Mission Beach choose to send their children to schools in La Jolla rather than have them attend their neighborhood schools. With a feasibility study beginning in 2002-03 and authorization in 2007, Pacific Beach Middle School and Mission Bay High school began to implement the prestigious International Baccalaureate Programs.

The International Baccalaureate Middle Years Program is based on the philosophy and framework of the International Baccalaureate Organization with a focus on providing quality programs of international education to a worldwide community of schools. There are more than 794,000 IB students at 2,870 schools in 138 countries. The IB Middle Years Program, for students aged 11 to 16, provides a framework of academic challenge that encourages students to embrace and understand the connections between traditional subjects and the real world, and become critical and reflective thinkers. The program consists of eight subject groups integrated through five areas of interaction that provide a framework for learning within and across the subjects. Students are required to study their mother tongue, a second language, humanities, sciences, mathematics, arts, physical education and technology. In the final year of the program, students also engage in a personal or culminating project, which allows them to demonstrate the understandings and skills they have developed throughout the program.

The IB MYP allows the schools to design their own curriculum based on the framework fundamentals of the program and the California State Standards. Students are encouraged to develop and maintain proficiency in English and at least one other language. Spanish is offered as the Language B World Language offering.

The Mission Bay High School cluster IB continuum begins at the kindergarten level at Sessions Elementary and continues through Pacific Beach Middle School and on to Misison Bay High School’s Diploma Program. Certificated staff have been hired and trained to design and deliver the program to all students at Pacific Beach Middle School as this program is designed as a whole-school framework and philosophy.

As stated previously, reasons for increasing access to Pacific Beach Middle School through Magnet Schools program is twofold. First, is to strengthen academic success in the Mission Bay High cluster IB continuum. Second, is to provide students equitable access to the programme through a broader transportation network. It is the intention of the cluster to prepare students to achieve in the challenging IB Diploma high school coursework and ultimately increase the number of students earning the full IB Diploma at Mission Bay High School. There is also the potential to increase the number of students enrolled in AP and/or IB courses during the student’s high school career. As a result of the rigor of the IB, more students in the Pacific Beach area are choosing to stay and attend the local middle school. In addition, under the leadership of the principals, the Spanish and mathematics departments from Pacific Beach Middle and Mission Bay High School have begun meeting and collaborating with a focus on preparing all students for the rigors of high school. Both schools are working together to address the needs of the significant numbers of English Learners in our schools. By providing Pacific Beach Middle School magnet status students from across the district and out of district will have access to the IB programs.

Resident parents not wishing their student to participate in the IB MYP program will continue to have alternative options through the Choice Program. It is the school’s focus to continue recruiting both resident students as well as increased marketing to private, new-to-the-district and out-of-district students who may otherwise be choosing to attend schools outside SDUSD. Magnet marketing and recruitment strategies were put into place during the 2009-10 school year. A marked increase in the number of families attending monthly parent tours and expressing interest in attending the Pacific Beach Middle Years Programme has been noted. Families interested in a balanced program of international education that leads to increased achievement and student success in the IB Diploma Program will lead to the continued increase in enrollment of resident students and the increase in enrollment of families who have expressed interest in from areas such as Clairemont, Kearny Mesa, Morse, Serra, and University City.

The Mission Bay High School and Sessions Elementary School have embraced the IB philosophy. During the last four years, the schools have revised curriculum to meet both the local and regional requirements as well as the IB objectives and program standards for each of the respective programs. While it would benefit the cluster to be able to offer more primary or elementary IB programs, due to budget cutbacks the cluster understands that this is not a possibility at this time. Access to the IB continuum continues to be a popular choice for many families.

IB MYP and Diploma Program are supported through school site budgets at the secondary level. Pacific Beach Middle and Mission Bay High School have received school allocations to offer the program. Pacific Beach Middle School is requesting continued district support for the 2010-11 school year and inclusion into the Magnet Program to increase access to families through transportation as outlined by district procedures. Both schools are committed in future years to support the IB through their school site budgets and additional funding as provided by the district.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve Lease Agreement (Lease) with the City of San Diego (City) for two consecutive floors intended for use as a public, charter school in the new central downtown library (Library).

FISCAL IMPACT: The district will enter into a 40-year, $20 million prepaid Lease to acquire use of the sixth and seventh floors of the Library for a public, charter school to support downtown educational requirements. The $20 million will be paid to the City in installments during the course of construction of the shell building. On March 30, 2010, in open session, the board approved the use of Centre City Development Corporation (CCDC) redevelopment funds for an initial portion of the prepaid lease of two floors of the Library; CCDC funds will later be used to reimburse $20 million back into Proposition S for use on other downtown projects. On November 4, 2008, the voters approved the use of bond revenues “to construct classrooms and schools in the downtown area to meet education needs of the district.” District legal counsel has confirmed that expenditure on the Library is an appropriate use of Proposition S funds.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT: District staff will not be required to perform services under the Lease.

BACKGROUND: On June 9, 2009, in closed session, the Board of Education agreed to pursue a 40-year, prepaid $20 million lease with the City for the district to lease two contiguous floors (sixth and seventh) of approximately 71,800 rentable square feet (Premises) to be used as a charter school educational facility. On June 23, 2009 in open session, the board approved a non-binding letter of intent with the City for a proposed charter school in the Library, which formed the basis for the Lease. With the City’s prior written approval, the district may also use the Premises for other purposes, i.e., a private school or administrative or general business offices.

The Library building is located at 11th Street, Park Boulevard, J Street, and K Street in downtown San Diego (APN 535-362-16). The Lease term is for 40 years, with the one option to extend the term of the Lease for an additional ten (10) years at the then prevailing market rent. The district also has an option to purchase the premises at the end of the 10th, 20th, 30th, and 40th years of the lease term. The purchase price for the Premises shall be the greater of: (1) the fair market value as of the date district exercises its purchase option; or the district proportional share of the actual building shell construction costs plus $13,964,000. This amount represents the land value of the Library, calculated by multiplying the 69,820 square feet of land comprising the Library multiplied by $200 per square foot.

While Commencement of Construction is expected to be August 1, 2010, if Commencement of Construction fails to occur on or before August 1, 2011, the district may thereafter at any time deliver to City a notice of intent to terminate the Lease. The City, in its sole discretion and with notice to district, may decide not to commence construction of the library building shell. The Lease also provides for other options to terminate by either side.

The City shall provide a first trust deed on non-Library property with a minimum value of $30 million until the end of the 10th Lease year, with the minimum value of $25 million during the next ten years, and thereafter the minimum value would be 125 percent of the unearned prepaid rent.

The Lease states that the City may not begin construction of the building shell, and the district is not required to pay any rent until all of the following conditions are met: 1) City must set aside sufficient funds; 2) City must enter into a signed contract with a reputable, experienced construction manager at risk; 3) City must retain a general contractor who shall provide payment and performance bonds; 4) the City must purchase title insurance, at its own expense; 5) the Memorandum of Lease must be recorded; and 6) the first deed of trust on non-Library City property valued at must be recorded with the values specified in Lease.

District will prepay the Initial Term (40 years) Rent in installments as follows:

  1. $5 million shall be paid on the Commencement of Construction;
  2. $5 million shall be paid when the Library is 25 percent complete, as certified by City’s project architect;
  3. $5 million shall be paid when the Library is 50 percent complete, as certified by City’s project architect;
  4. $3 million shall be paid when the Library is 75 percent complete, as certified by City’s project architect;
  5. $2 million shall be paid upon Completion of Construction.

District will be responsible for the cost of design and construction of its tenant improvements to its Premises, estimated at $10 million to be paid out of CCDC funds.
