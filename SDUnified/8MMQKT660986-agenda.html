Meeting

Action

RECOMMENDATION: Grant, with conditions, the petition to establish the Village Academy of Cognitive Development (VACD), a charter school, for a three-year term beginning on July 1, 2012, and expiring on June 30, 2015. Staff recommends board action to conditionally approve the VACD petition, contingent upon the lead petitioner’s agreement to revise the petition according to the factual findings below and submit the revised petition and the Conditional Approval MOU Agreement to the Office of Charter Schools by April 6, 2012.  Additionally, the petitioners shall meet the following conditions:

  • By August 15, 2012, the petitioners shall submit to the Office of Charter School a copy of a fully executed lease agreement for its facilities and demonstrate compliance with California Building Code.  If the fully executed lease agreement is not submitted or compliance with California Building Code is not demonstrated, VACD agrees to voluntarily surrender the charter petition by August 22, 2012; and
  • The Charter School agrees to voluntarily surrender its charter by July 2, 2013, if it has not enrolled 90 students in Grades K-3 by September 28, 2012.

Alternatively, the board may approve the petition without conditions or adopt this report as making written factual findings in support the board’s denial.

 

Education Code section 47605 states that the governing board of the school district shall grant a charter for the operation of a school under this part if it is satisfied that granting the charter is consistent with sound educational practice. The governing board shall not deny a petition for the establishment of a charter school unless it makes written factual findings, specific to the particular petition, setting forth specific facts to support one or more of the following findings:

  1. The petition does not contain the required number of signatures.
  1. The petition does not contain an affirmation of each of the conditions described in Education Code section 47605(d).
  1. The petition does not contain reasonably comprehensive descriptions of all of the required elements set forth in Education Code sections 47605(b)(A)-(P).
  1. The charter school presents an unsound educational program for the pupils to be enrolled in the charter school.
  1. The petitioners are demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program set forth in the petition.

7. Material Revision to Downtown Charter High School; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-2)
Meeting
Mar 6, 2012 - Regular Charter Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the material revisions to the charter of Downtown Charter High School (DCHS), a charter school serving Grades 9-12, that is scheduled to open in the fall 2013, as follows:

  • Pages 8, 18, and 22, have been revised to reflect a projected enrollment of 500; previously the enrollment was projected at 400.
  • Page 68 has been revised to reflect a new board member residency provision such that the majority of board members will reside in the San Diego area.
  • Page 69 has been revised to include two democratically-elected board members, to be elected by parents and DCHS staff.
  • Page 72 has been revised to reflect updated principal qualifications, including a proven track record operating a school that has achieved API target growth in the past four years or demonstrated an equivalent measure of success.
  • Pages 80-83 have been revised to bring the admissions element into alignment with the Public Charter School Grant Program (PCSGP), including the school will conduct a single public lottery with all applicants receiving equal weight; children of faculty will be exempted from the lottery (not to exceed 10 percent); siblings of existing students will be exempted from the lottery; and students seeking to change schools as part of public school choice provisions of Title I, part of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) will receive a preference for admissions.
  • Page 107 contains an updated budget projection, including a projected enrollment of 500 students.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Downtown Charter High is requesting to revise the charter petition to reflect the application for facilities that was submitted earlier. In addition, the school is amending its admissions process in the charter petition.  The petition is also being modified as indicated in the above-noted changes.  The material revisions are identified in the redlined (“track changes”) version of the attached charter exhibit.  A clean (“changes accepted”) version of the charter that incorporates the revisions is also attached as an exhibit.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve Resolution Reducing or Eliminating Certain Certificated Services for the 2012-13 school year.  First Reading was conducted on February 28, 2012.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Approval and full implementation of this Resolution would result in a reduction in currently performed certificated services, and a corresponding reduction in permanent and probationary certificated employees, sufficient to realize necessary budget reductions/savings.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: The Resolution provides for a reduction of certificated services totaling 1656 full time equivalents (FTE).

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: For permanent certificated employees, the Education Code requires that the board pass a resolution to identify the particular kinds of services or programs that may be reduced or eliminated in the upcoming fiscal year.  For probationary certificated employees, Article 19 of the collective bargaining agreement between the district and San Diego Education Association (SDEA) allows the board to determine that reductions are necessary due to lack of funds.  Permanent certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a statutory layoff notice on March 15, 2012. Probationary certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a contractual layoff notice on or before April 15, 2012.  Noticed employees will be able to challenge the action through a request for hearing (permanent) or the filing of a grievance (probationary).  Temporary certificated employees will receive a separate reminder notice that their services will end at the close of the 2011-12 school year.
