  1. Operational Plan: The Thrive petitioners indicated that its operational plan is to begin at a District facility during its first two years and then move to a private facility by its third year.   The Thrive petition states that the charter school will focus its outreach efforts on students residing in the Crawford and Hoover clusters.  Of the fourteen charter schools that have opened and operated in the Crawford or Hoover clusters in the past ten years, none of these charter schools have moved from a district facility to a private facility.  Historical data shows that seven of the fourteen charter schools that opened in a private facility in the Crawford or Hoover clusters subsequently moved to a district facility.  Currently, eight out of the twelve charter schools located in the Crawford or Hoover clusters operate on a district facility.

Employment Plan: After submitting a charter petition to San Diego Unified School District, on or about September 16, 2013, the petitioners submitted a second charter petition to the La Mesa-Spring Valley School District to establish “Thrive Public School #2.”  Both charter petitions include signatures of teachers; however, eight of the signatures appear to be duplicates of the same teachers, calling into question which charter school the individuals are meaningfully interested in being employed at for the same proposed first year of operation in 2014-15. 
  1. The Petition does not contain a reasonably comprehensive description of all the elements set forth in Education Code section 47605(b)(5).  Specifically, the Petition does not contain a reasonably comprehensive description of the governance structure pursuant to Education Code section 47605(b)(5)(D).  As noted above, two charter petitions with same name, “Thrive Public School,” were submitted to the San Diego Unified School District and La Mesa-Spring Valley School District with the same opening year of 2014-15.  The governance structure among the two charter schools is not described in the Petition.  The budget document included in the Petition to San Diego Unified School District includes a CMO fee, but this fee does not begin until the school’s third year of operation in 2016-17. 

The Board of Education may deny a charter petition by making written factual findings, specific to the particular petition, which supports at least one of the following findings:

  1. The charter school presents an unsound educational program.
  2. The petitioners are demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program set forth in the petition.
  3. The petition does not contain the required number of signatures.
  4. The petition does not contain an affirmation of the conditions set forth in Education Code section 47605(d).    
  5. The petition does not contain a reasonably comprehensive description of all the elements set forth in Education Code section 47605(b).

RECOMMENDATION: Public noticing and First Reading of the Tentative Agreement with San Diego Schools Police Officers’ Association (POA) for a successor Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) effective July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2015, in accordance with Section 3547.5 of the California Government Code and Section 1031 of the Bylaws of the Board of Education.  Ratification for board action will be brought forward on October 8, 2013. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Impact to 2013-14 General Fund Unrestricted is $32,235.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: No prior year impact.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: No change to district staffing levels.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The Tentative Agreement extends the current MOU with negotiated changes for a two-year term from July 1, 2013 through June 30, 2015, with an optional extension for 2015-16.  The Tentative Agreement also implements the restoration of the deferred compensation set forth in 2012 Tentative Agreement and provides for changes to Article 3 – Employee Organizational Rights, Article 5 – Definitions, Article 6 – Negotiation Procedures, Article 7 – Wages, Article 8 – Hours of Employment, Article 9 – Health and Welfare Benefits, Article 10 – Safety Conditions of Employment, Article 11 – Shift Changes, Transfers and Reassignments, Article 12 – Leave Policies, Article 17 – Layoff and Reemployment, Article 20 – Tuition Reimbursement Program, Appendix A – Salary Schedule and Rules, Appendix B – Administrative Procedure and language cleanup. 

 

These changes to the Memorandum of Understanding were necessary for the district to fulfill its obligations and commitment to serving students while maintaining fiscal responsibility in approaching total compensation provided to district employees by making changes that are consistent with the district’s values, and local, state, and federal economic realities.
