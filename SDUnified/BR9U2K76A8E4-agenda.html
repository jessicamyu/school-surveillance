PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: On April 14, 2020, the board approved Administrative Regulation (AR) 5121.1, Grades/Evaluation of Student Achievement During Emergency School Closures, that includes a "hold harmless" grading practice in the event of an emergency school closure.  The proposed revisions include definitions for short-term closure and long-term closure as they apply to emergencies that warrant schools shifting to an online format.  For short-term closures, a "hold harmless" practice will continue, however, for a long-term closure that extends past one grading period, a grade validation practice will be enacted for secondary schools that allows a second semester grade of "D" or higher to validate a first semester grade of "F" with a "D."  This applies only to secondary schools, as elementary grading is cumulative.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer, Intersex, Asexual, plus (LGBTQIA+) Pride Month is currently celebrated in June each year to honor the 1969 Stonewall Uprising in Manhattan. The Stonewall Uprising was a tipping point for the Gay Liberation Movement in the United States and led by two transgender women of color, Marsha P. Johnson and Sylvia Rivera. President Bill Clinton declared June "Gay and Lesbian Pride Month" in 1999 and 2000; and from 2009 to 2016, each year he was in office, President Barack Obama declared June LGBT Pride Month, and on May 28, 2010 issued Proclamation 8529, saying, “I call upon all Americans to observe this month by fighting prejudice and discrimination in their own lives and everywhere it exists.” In 2020, during the 50th anniversary of Pride, presidential candidate and current President Joe Biden stated the following, “Pride has come to be recognized as a global movement of love, self-expression, and community - resilient in the face of oppression and fear and hopeful for a better future. This month, let us recommit to those principles of Pride and remain steadfast in the fight for justice and equality.” The San Diego Unified School District is committed to safety, inclusion, and affirmation for all students through developing and coordinating activities, training, and opportunities for the SDUSD community including:

 

  • Creating visibility and awareness exhibits at the Eugene Brucker Education Center and on district campuses to honor the month and all students, staff, and families who identify as LGBTQIA+, such as flying the LGBTQIA+ Pride Rainbow flag and Transgender Pride flag.
  • Implementing history and social studies curriculum that highlights the contributions of members of the LGBTQIA+ community and allows students to see themselves accurately reflected, as prescribed by the FAIR Education Act, SB 48, which was signed into law on July 14, 2011.
  • Offering ongoing and student-centered opportunities for education, advocacy, and advancement for LGBTQIA+ students, families, and staff.
  • The adoption and implementation of comprehensive bullying/harassment policies that specifically enumerate sexual orientation, gender identity, and gender expression with clear and effective systems for reporting and addressing incidents that students experience; and

 

Nationally, 59.1% of LGBTQIA+ students felt unsafe at school because of their sexual orientation, 42.5% because of their gender expression, and 37.4% because of their gender according to the 2019 GLSEN National School Climate Survey. A hostile school climate affects students’ academic success and mental health. LGBTQIA+ students who experience victimization and discrimination at school have poorer educational outcomes and psychological well-being. The availability and the utility of LGBTQIA+-related school resources and supports help offset the negative effects of a hostile school climate and promote a positive learning experience. A month of observance is one critical component of our ongoing collective resolution to continue dismantling racist, homophobic, transphobic, ablist dynamics and other forms of oppression within our school system in order to maximize the accessibility of our educational spaces and facilities for ALL students.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of support for HR 1280, the George Floyd Justice in Policing Act, and urge the United States Congress and the President to approve and sign HR 1280 into law.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On May 25, 2020, George Floyd was brutally murdered on a Minneapolis, Minnesota street by Minneapolis police officer Derek Chauvin. The murder of George Floyd, an unarmed Black man, at the hands of police, and the events surrounding his death captured and highlighted the fear and deep distrust that has historically existed in American society and exacerbated racial inequities and disparities. George Floyd’s murder was a catalyst for a movement of people across the world demanding an end to police brutality, systemic racism and calling for substantive reforms. To address the deep-rooted reforms needed within law enforcement, shortly after the murder in 2020 Representative Karen Bass of California, Representative Jerrold Nadler of New York, Senator Cory Booker of New Jersey and then-Senator Kamala Harris of California introduced HR 7120, the George Floyd Justice in Policing Act. CEQA BACKGROUND

 

In consultation with the City, the District has prepared a Draft Environmental Impact Report (DEIR) for the Proposed Project in accordance with the requirements of CEQA (Public Resources Code Section 21000 et seq.) and CEQA Guidelines (California Code of Regulations Sections 15000 et seq.). The District is the CEQA Lead Agency for the DEIR and, as such, has the primary responsibility for evaluating environmental effects of the Proposed Project and considering whether to approve or disapprove the Proposed Project in consideration of these effects. As the Project includes the execution of a lease agreement between the City and the District, the City is a CEQA Responsible Agency, which means that they also have approval authority of certain aspects of the Project and must consider the environmental effects of the Proposed Project when considering their Project-related actions. As such, the DEIR was prepared in close coordination with the City of San Diego Planning Department.

 

ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT REPORT PROJECT DESCRIPTION

 

The Proposed Project evaluated in the DEIR includes the following: (1) execution of a ground lease agreement with the City authorizing the District’s use of the Project site for 99-years; (2) implementation of near-term whole site modernization (WSM) elements, and (3) implementation of Long-Range Facilities Master Plan (LRFMP) Elements. As described above, the exchange of district-owned property as consideration for the proposed lease was previously evaluated under CEQA and not included in the DEIR analysis.

 

Specifically, the WSM improvements would consist of campus-wide upgrades to the existing school site, general improvements to existing buildings, and the addition of approximately 700 square feet (sf) onto Building 100. The WSM improvements also include upgrades to the athletic fields, replacement and reconfiguration of the student quad, and reconfiguration of the east and north parking lot.

 

The LRFMP improvements include the demolition of three school buildings (buildings 400, 600, and 700) and the construction of seven school buildings (the performing arts building, parking structure, auxiliary gymnasium, new classroom building 400, food service and custodial building 700, field house, and aquatic center). Other improvements include upgrades at Balboa Stadium, new campus entrances/exits via Park Boulevard and Russ Boulevard, and site enhancements consisting of landscape and hardscape improvements west of building 100 near the corner of Russ Boulevard and Park Boulevard.

 

Construction of the WSM improvements is anticipated to begin in Summer of 2021 and be completed by the end of 2023 (approximately 36 months). Construction timing for the LRFMP improvements is uncertain; however, they would occur after the WSM improvements, between 2024 and 2035.
