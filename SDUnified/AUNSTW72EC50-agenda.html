Meeting
Jul 10, 2018 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
First Reading, Public Hearing

RECOMMENDATION: In accordance with Education Code Section 76004, conduct a public hearing and first reading of the 2018-19 College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Agreements with the San Diego Community College District for college classes to be conducted on San Diego Unified School District high school campuses.   Community college and K-12 districts offering college courses on high school campuses are required to enter into CCAP partnerships that include specific language regarding the targeted number of high school students to be served and the total number of full-time equivalent students (FTES) projected to be claimed by the community college district for those students.  

 

  1. SV-19-0077-99 San Diego City College - for various classes at Crawford, Garfield, Hoover, Lincoln, Morse and San Diego High Schools and the School of Creative and Performing Arts (SCPA)
  2. SV-19-0078-99 Mesa College Accelerated College Program (ACP) - for Mathematics and Political Science classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Morse, Point Loma and University City High Schools
  3. SV-19-0079-99 Mesa College - for various classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Mission Bay, Point Loma, and University City High Schools
  4. SV-19-0080-99 Miramar College - for various classes at Mira Mesa, Scripps Ranch, Serra and University City High Schools

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. The San Diego Unified School District is eligible for average daily attendance (ADA) revenue for the periods each day that students are enrolled in San Diego Unified courses, and the San Diego Community College District is eligible for apportionment for the period that students are enrolled in City, Mesa, or Miramar College Classes.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. As stated in the agreements, each entity agrees to adhere to its respective collective bargaining agreements and no San Diego Community College District or San Diego Unified School District teachers will be displaced by the implementation of these agreements.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  Education Code Section 76004 authorizes community college and K-12 districts to enter into College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) partnerships with the goal of developing pathways from high school to community college for career technical or preparation for transfer, improving high school graduation rates, or helping high school students achieve college and career readiness.  Through dual enrollment partnerships, school districts and community college districts can create clear pathways of aligned, sequenced coursework that allow students to more easily and successfully transition to for-credit, college-level coursework leading to an associate degree, transfer to the University of California (UC) or California State University (CSU) systems, or to a program leading to a career technical education certificate.  The 2018-19 CCAP agreements were presented to the San Diego Community College District Board of Trustees for first reading on June 7, 2018, and will be agendized for final reading and adoption on July 19, 2018.  The 2018-19 CCAP Agreements will be brought forward for San Diego Unified School District Board of Education final reading and adoption on July 24, 2018.

BACKGROUND: On February 27, 2018, the Board of Education (Board) approved the Purchase Sale Agreement with CSS (Attachment 2) to commence escrow for the purchase of 3910 University Avenue, San Diego CA for the intended occupancy by HSHMC.

 

During the course of due diligence, District staff discovered items of deferred maintenance that will require repair after the sale transaction is complete. CSS was unwilling to resolve the repair issues and therefore escrow was canceled on May 25, 2018.

 

On June 15, 2018, upon renegotiation with CSS, a reduction in the purchase price from $31,000,000 to $29,900,000 was agreed.

 

Upon execution of this Amendment, escrow will be reinstated and the transaction will proceed. Close of escrow is now anticipated by the end of July 2018, contingent upon factors, including but not limited to, the expiration of the existing tenant, Workforce Strategies (Workforce). ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The HSHMC Building Improvements Project (Proposed Project) involves the acquisition of the property by the District and eventual reconfiguration of the existing four-story building with parking structure for exclusive use by HSHMC. This would require interior tenant improvements to convert existing commercial/office space into educational space (including reconfiguration of the space and interior painting), security and access improvements to meet educational standards, as well as upgrades to heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) units located on the roof of the building.

 

Per an existing easement agreement between the City of San Diego (City), the current property owner, and adjacent residential uses, the existing parking structure would continue to be shared with adjacent multi-family residences. Upon purchase of the building by the District, the existing 296 parking spaces allocated for the existing educational, commercial, and office uses would be utilized solely by the charter school. The 296 parking spaces allocated to the charter school would satisfy the 138 parking spaces required under the City’s Municipal Code. No changes to the existing 195 spaces for the multi-family residences are proposed. The parking structure would be improved to provide separate code-compliant exit stairwells at either end of the structure to serve the school and residences and also with security improvements to provide a physical separation of the two uses.

 

As a result of the Proposed Project, student enrollment is expected to increase from approximately 750 students to approximately 1,000 students; an increase of about 250 students. Also, the existing commercial and office uses would be eliminated, along with the existing 70 employees associated with these uses. Upon acquisition of the building, the school would occupy all four stories, totaling about 82,200 sqft; an increase of approximately 41,800 sqft.

 

Construction is anticipated to begin in October 2018 and end in October 2019, for an estimated construction duration of approximately one year.

 

The existing HSHMC Charter School is currently permitted to operate under City's Conditional Use Permit (CUP) No. 583145, Project No. 163094, approved by the City on October 8, 2015. Expansion of the charter school use on the site, as proposed, would not be consistent with the existing CUP. However, the District Board adopted a resolution at its July 10, 2018 meeting, to exempt the project site from city zoning requirements, as allowed under California Government Code Section 53094.   

Reasons Why Project Is Exempt:

Section 15061 (b)(3) of the CEQA Guidelines states that an activity “…is covered by the general rule that CEQA applies only to projects which have the potential for causing a significant effect on the environment. Where it can be seen with certainty that there is no possibility that the activity in question may have a significant effect on the environment, the activity is not subject to CEQA.” An initial study, included as Attachment 3, was prepared to determine its eligibility for an exemption from additional environmental review under CEQA.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2):

Section 15300.2 of the State CEQA Guidelines specifies several exceptions to the use of a categorical exemption. These exceptions will also be used to test the use of the general rule exemption for the Proposed Project to determine, with certainty, that there is no possibility that the Proposed Project will have a significant effect on the environment.

 

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified Environmental Impact Report.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of an existing developed site within an urbanized community. The site has been developed with its current use since 2003. Surrounding land uses consist of residential or commercial uses. However, the Proposed Project would not extend into any offsite areas. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. The project site is within an urbanized area consisting primarily of residential and commercial land uses. It is not anticipated that there would be a substantial number of other concurrent projects in the immediate vicinity of the Proposed Project such that construction of the Proposed Project would contribute to a temporary cumulative impact. Construction of the proposed project does not involve substantial grading and the majority of construction activity would be within the interior of the existing building. In regard to operation, the project would increase student capacity but would not result in an overall increase in capacity of the building. Traffic associated with the increase in student enrollment was determined to be less than existing conditions based on the analysis within the Initial Study Checklist (Attachment 3). Other operational impacts such as noise and air quality emissions are expected to remain the same as existing conditions based on the analysis within the Initial Study. Lastly, because no significant project level impacts would occur with implementation of the Proposed Project, there would be no cumulative impacts once the project is constructed. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. The Proposed Project site is located within an urbanized area, and no work is proposed offsite that could result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas. As analyzed in the attached Initial Study Checklist (Attachment 3), there is nothing unusual about the project site or surrounding area. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project would not result in the demolition of existing structures or the construction of new facilities that would directly, or indirectly, affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. The portion of I-15 in the vicinity of the project site is not designated as a scenic highway and there are no designated scenic resources or highways within ¼ mile of the project site. Lastly, the site is not visible from any designated scenic highways. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. There are no District school facilities located on a hazardous waste site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. In addition, A Phase 1 Environmental Site Assessment prepared for the project site (See Attachment 3) and review of available online regulatory databases found that no sites of hazardous concern are located on the project site. The Phase 1 identified past presence of petroleum and lead impacted soils on a portion of the site. However, the past release of these hazardous substances is considered to be a Controlled Recognized Environmental Condition (CREC), which has been addressed to the satisfaction of the applicable regulatory authority (see Attachment 3).  As a result, impacts related to creating a significant hazard to the public or the environment are less than significant. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. Generally, historical resources are considered to be resources that are eligible for listing in the California Register of Historical Resources if they are greater than 50 years in age, or if the lead agency (e.g., the District) considers the buildings to be historically significant. The existing four story building and parking structure in which the proposed improvements would take place were built between 2002 and 2003 and do not meet the 50-year age threshold for consideration as potential historical resources. Additionally, the existing structures are not considered historical by the District or any other local agency. Therefore, the proposed project would not cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource as defined in Section 15064.5 of the State CEQA Guidelines, and no impacts would occur. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.

 

Approval of this item approves the Project as described and authorizes and directs staff to file a Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk (Attachment 4).  Approval of this item also allows escrow to be reinstated and the transaction to proceed. .
