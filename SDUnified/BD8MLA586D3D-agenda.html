C. Board Workshop on Re-Opening
Subject
1. Staff Presentation and Board Discussion on Re-Opening Schools
Meeting
Jun 16, 2020 - Special Workshop, 3:30 p.m.
Type
Action, Information

RECOMMENDATION: Approve plans for the safe reopening of school facilities ahead of the 2020-21 academic year based on state public health guidelines issued on June 5, 2020, and by the California Department of Education on June 8, 2020.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The costs of reopening schools following the Covid-19 crisis will depend on the emergency relief programs created by the state and federal governments.

 

BACKGROUND: The purpose of this item is to provide for the reopening of school facilities with a focus on the health and safety of all students, staff, families and community members. Staff will review plans to implement the revised public health guidelines provided by the California Department of Health and the California Department of Education on June 5, 2020, and 8, 2020, respectively.

 

Although safety remains the primary planning consideration, staff will also outline proposals for multiple instructional models based on individual family needs, student learning styles and the different ways in which the Covid-19 crisis has impacted different communities.

 

District schools were physically closed on March 13, 2020, due to the COVID-19 Pandemic. Since that time:

 

  • Students and teachers have logged 69 million Zoom meeting minutes

  • More than 50,000 Chromebook computers have been distributed to students

  • Nearly 98 percent of all students have logged in to access their school work online

  • More than 4,830,000 copies of instructional materials have been printed for families

  • Teachers have taken part in more than 400 professional development trainings

  • They have benefitted from more than 14,000 hours of training

  • More than 1,000 parents took part in trainings to help support their students

  • School workers have provided more than 2 million meals to local families
