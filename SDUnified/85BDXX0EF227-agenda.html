This report presents the district's facilities that need to be fixed, replaced, or repaired within the next ten years as part of a coordinated and comprehensive effort to achieve effective long-term planning. The term "facilities" includes district-owned property and equipment, consisting of buildings and building systems, underground systems, grounds and site improvements.

The findings are listed in the Facilities Condition Assessment (FCA) Study, and resulting Major Repair and Replacement (MRR) Ten-Year Plan and Major/Repair and Replacement (MRR/RR) fiscal year 2008/2009 Implementation Plan. Part I of the FCA study summarizes the longer term planning view, and Part II presents the fiscal year 2008/2009 MRR/RR Implementation Plan. The FCA and MRR/RR allow the District to protect and effectively manage its facilities.

Specifically, the FCA and MRR/RR groups the repairs and replacements into the following five categories:

1. Capital Improvement (CI)-- programmatic improvement upgrades to meet educational needs, district programs and curriculum, and significant change of systems technology (i.e., replacement of heating units with package air condition systems, and associated new energy efficient windows and building insulation)
2. Major Repair & Replacement (MRR) -- replacement and upgrades of systems, such as roof system, building enclosures often considered part of a modernization of a building, generally with life expectancy of 20 or more years
3. Repair & Replacement (RR) -- repair and replacement of items generally with life expectancy of less than 20 years
4. Regular Maintenance (RM) -- minor repairs, as reported from sites, from normal wear and tear on buildings such as toilets, locks, and light switches
5. Preventive Maintenance (PM) -- maintenance such as inspections and lubrications of selective building systems designed to prevent deterioration into disrepair

Recommendations
The FCA and MRR/RR are consistent with the changes to the facilities policy that the board approved at its April 8, 2008 public meeting.
District staff recommends that the Board of Education approve the following:

1. Accept the Facility Condition Assessment.
2. Approve the Independent Citizens Oversight Committee recommended Facility Condition Index of 5 percent to 7 percent as a ten-year goal and the corresponding escalated Major Repair and Replacement expenditure of $358 million during that time period be accepted.
3. Approve the Major Repair and Replacement/Repair and Replacement 2008/09 Plan as presented.

Subject
G.02. Ad Hoc Task Force on the Bond Committee Report to the Board
Meeting
Jul 8, 2008 - 07/08/2008
Type
Information
On February 26, 2008, the Board of Education voted 5-0 to affirm that the district intends to place a capital facilities general obligation bond on the November 2008 ballot and established an ad hoc task force towards that end.

The task force's charter is to review the District's facility needs and and the components of a proposed facilities bond, and to provide the Board of Education with advice regarding both. The task force comprised 22 members, appointed by the Board. It is not a political or deliberative body, but rather a conduit to provide the best information available to the Board. The members of the task force represent a cross-section of community groups including, Business Roundtable on Education, California Charter Schools Association, San Diego Unified Council PTA, and San Diego County Taxpayers Association. Between May 15, 2008 and June 23, 2008, the task force has met six times at different locations throughout the district.

This informational report describes the process and provides recommendations for the Board’s deliberations in adopting a resolution to place a general obligation bond measure on the November 4, 2008 general election ballot. Specifically, the task force identified the following four themes:

          1) Schools are and should be the “heart and soul” of the community.
          2) Parity of the physical structures in ALL schools, including charter schools where appropriate, should be addressed and considered.
          3) Facilities improvements that clearly improve student learning should be a priority.
          4) The District should continue to review and reassess lessons learned from Prop. MM, including oversight of bond implementation and construction of capital improvements under the bond

Under the four themes above, the task force also preliminarily prioritized the facilities needs as follows:
(1) critical projects required for code and safety;
(2) committed projects; and
(3) other needs such as technology, K-8 conversion, site discretionary funds, performing arts, and air conditioning.

Members of the task force will be at the July 8, 2008 public meeting to present this item and respond to questions. While the task force represented a diverse range of perspectives, the task force did not communicate any serious concerns, collectively or individually, about the need or appropriateness of the proposed bond.
