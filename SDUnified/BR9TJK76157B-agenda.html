RECOMMENDATION: As a service-providing organization, the San Diego Unified School District's employees are its most valuable resource. Today's recognition includes recognition of employees with 40, 30, 20, 15, 10 and 5 years of service. When the district returns to in-person work, each employee will receive a certificate and pin. The Superintendent and the Board of Education extend their most sincere gratitude to these individuals. 

 

Today, we celebrate milestones of 116 employees.

 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: As San Diego Unified celebrates progress towards meeting the inspirational goals contained in Vision 2020, we are taking time to celebrate those employees who have helped us achieve this progress. The number one predictor of student success is the presence of an experienced, high quality teacher in the classroom, according to the Learning Policy Institute. (Source: “Report: Teacher qualifications best predictor of student success,” By Amelia Harper, May 16, 2019, Education Dive). That study, co-authored by the Chair of the State Board of Education, found high quality teachers have helped San Diego schools do better than the rest of the state. (Source: “California’s Positive Outliers: Districts Beating the Odds,” by Anne Podolsky, Linda Darling-Hammond, Christopher Doss, and Sean Reardon, May 16, 2019, Learning Policy Institute). Educators also provide crucial services in addition to teaching. They are critical to keeping students safe at school. School staff also provide healthy, nutritious meals to children who do not have access to food at home. Nearly 100,000 schools/institutions serve school lunches to 29.8 million students each day, including 20.2 million free lunches. (Source: School Nutrition Association) Given the clear association between the health and success of our students and the well-being and effectiveness of our workforce, we publicly recognize employees for their continuous years of service to the students, families and communities of San Diego Unified.  

 

*This service recognition award is not used to calculate or verify the seniority date of an employee nor is the recognition part of the formal personnel processes that calculate the date for longevity pay. Both longevity and seniority dates are dictated by the Education Code as well as the applicable collective bargaining agreement. The date used to determine this service award is based on the individual's permanent status as an employee, temporary or hourly work is not used in the determination of the service award recognition. Service recognition awards will be awarded on a monthly basis. If you believe an error has been made, you may contact the Human Resource Service Division by emailing jobs@sandi.net.

RECOMMENDATION: Recognize Guadalupe “Pita” Birkbeck, 11th grader from Point Loma High School, the winner of the “Abuse and Harassment: Suspect It, Report It" poster contest. The poster contest was one of several board-approved recommendations of the Multi-Agency Task Force to facilitate a student-led campaign aimed at increasing knowledge of reporting procedures.

 

In collaboration with School Police and other district departments, the Communications Department held a student poster contest to help create a culture of reporting among students and staff.

 

Criteria for poster entries:

  • Include the theme, "ABUSE AND HARASSMENT: SUSPECT IT, REPORT IT"
  • Encourage students and staff to take action and report suspected
    child abuse and sexual harassment
  • Embrace a culture of reporting
  • Include the Child Abuse Hotline number 858-560-2191
  • Target audience should be students and district staff
  • Poster should be age appropriate for grades 9 through 12

 

Retired School Police Chief Michael Marquez collaborated with the Communications Department to promote the poster contest at meetings with three student organizations: ASB Presidents on October 21, 2020, Student Advisory Board on October 22, 2020, and Student Equity Ambassadors on October 29, 2020. There was positive student feedback shared during the presentations, including the suggestion of promoting the contest via a YouTube video. The Communications Department created a YouTube video that was shared on the district’s YouTube channel and via social media.

 

The district promoted the contest during the months of October and November 2020 via social media and other platforms including Facebook, Twitter, YouTube, the weekly E-blast, the weekly principals' update, and the district website.

 

A panel to review the poster entries included Interim School Police Chief Joseph Florentino; Timothy Benson, VAPA Program Director; Dr. Noemi Villegas, Instructional Support Officer; Zachary Patterson, Student Board Member; and Esha Walia, President, Student Equity Ambassadors.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of recognizing March 2021 as Women's History Month in San Diego Unified School District and encourage student participation in appropriate programs and school activities.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The origins of Women’s History Month began in 1978 with a week-long celebration planned by the Education Task Force of the Sonoma County (California) Commission on the Status of Women; and, in 1980, President Jimmy Carter issued the first Presidential Proclamation, declaring the Week of March 8th, 1980 Nation Women’s History Week. In 1987, Congress passed Public Law 100-9, designating March as Women’s History Month and, since 1995, each President has issued an annual proclamation re-designating March as Women’s History Month. Women fought fiercely for over 100 years for their voices to be heard in American politics and policy before they were given the right to vote with the ratification of the 19th Amendment in 1920 and even then, women of all races were not able to vote. Women of every race, class, and ethnic background have made historic contributions to the growth and strength of our Nation in countless recorded and unrecorded ways. Women, especially women of color, are still underrepresented in state and federal government, top jobs in major corporations, and continue to be paid less than their male counterparts. Women have played and continue to play a critical economic, cultural, and social role in every sphere of the life of the Nation by constituting a significant portion of the labor force working inside and outside of the home despite persistent and enduring wage gaps. Women have played a unique role throughout the history of the Nation by providing the majority of the volunteer labor force of the Nation. Women were particularly important in the establishment of early charitable, philanthropic, and cultural institutions in our Nation. Women of every race, class, and ethnic background served as early leaders in the forefront of every major progressive social change movement. Women have been leaders, not only in securing their own rights of suffrage and equal opportunity, but also in the abolitionist movement, the emancipation movement, the industrial labor movement, the civil rights movement, and other movements, especially the peace movement, which create a more fair and just society for all. The history and contributions of women have consistently been overlooked, misinterpreted, and undervalued in the curriculum of public education institutions and still today. San Diego Unified is committed to recognizing and honoring women for their many contributions not only to their communities but to the students and families in our district schools.

BACKGROUND: On February 2, 2021, the board approved a process for the recruitment and selection of a permanent Superintendent upon the resignation of Superintendent Cindy Marten who has been recommended for appointment to a cabinet position in the Biden administration. The approved plan involves an open and transparent, inclusive search process.

 

The selection process shall be supported by an organization tasked with leading town halls, listening sessions and community engagement meetings. The Advisory Committee will draft a Request for Proposals (“RFP”) which will be used to solicit proposals from interested organizations by the end of March 2021. These proposals will be reviewed by the Advisory Committee that will make the recommendation to the board for the organization that will lead town halls, listening sessions and community engagement meetings. Town Halls will be held in late Spring and early Summer. This will lead to the creation of a vision for the future of the district, and the identification of the qualities of a Superintendent to lead that vision.

 

The Advisory Committee will utilize the information gathered during the town halls, listening sessions and community engagement meetings to develop the job posting.

 

The position will then be posted by the end of July 2021.  The recruitment will open to qualified candidates from around the globe.

 

The Advisory Committee will screen the candidate application packages and will recommend up to ten (10) candidates to be interviewed by the Board in September 2021.

 

The Board will select up to three (3) finalists by October 2021.

 

Community forums will be held in early November 2021 for the public to meet the finalists and provide feedback.

 

A permanent Superintendent will be selected by the beginning of December 2021 to allow for a month-long transition into the position.
