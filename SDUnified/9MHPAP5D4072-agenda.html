Academics

A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school.
     
  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
     
  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
     
  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.
     
  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(h).

Keiller is eligible for renewal based on attaining its API growth target in the prior year, and its decile ranking of 4 for a demographically comparable school.

Finances

Based on its location, budget, grade levels, and programs offered, the enrollment projections of Keiller have been evaluated and appear to be within normal range compared to other charter schools and district schools. The estimated California Department of Education (CDE) funding rates and Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) factor, provided by School Services of California’s Financial Projection Dartboard, have been used to estimate revenue for Keiller based on the projected average daily attendance rate. This revenue projection has been compared to the projected revenue included in the charter school’s budget and no significant variances have been found. Other expenses unique to charter schools have also been examined and found to be comprehensive and appropriate. Over the past four years, Keiller has addressed all required annual financial audit and reporting to the district.

 

Based on the projected enrollment, budget, expenses, and cash flow submitted in the charter renewal documents, Keiller has been found to be financially viable. The school’s actual expenses for 2010-11 were less than the budgeted amounts. The actual expenses for 2011-12, 2012-13, and 2013-14 were all greater than the budgeted amounts.

 

In addition, the district has examined whether Keiller maintains an adequate reserve for economic uncertainty which is 3 percent of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater, as another consideration of its financial viability.

 

At the end of 2013-14, per Keiller’s audit report, the ending fund balance (EFB) was $1,107818, which exceeds the minimum reserve amount. In summary, Keiller appears financially sound.

 

Conclusion

The charter renewal documents submitted by Keiller are attached.

 

Staff recommends the board approve the renewal of the charter and special education agreement for Keiller for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2015, and ending June 30, 2020.

Finances

Based on its location, budget, grade levels, and programs offered, the enrollment projections of Coleman Tech Charter High School have been evaluated and appear to be within normal range compared to other charter schools and District schools. The estimated California Department of Education (CDE) funding rates and Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) factor, provided by School Services of California’s Financial Projection Dartboard, have been used to estimate revenue for Coleman Tech Charter High School based on the projected average daily attendance rate. This revenue projection has been compared to the projected revenue included in the charter school’s budget and no significant variances have been found. Other expenses unique to charter schools have also been examined and found to be comprehensive and appropriate. Over the past three years, Coleman Tech Charter High School has addressed all required annual financial audit and reporting to the district. Based on the projected enrollment, budget, expenses, and cash flow submitted in the charter renewal documents, Coleman Tech Charter High School has been found to be financially viable. The school’s actual expenses for 2010-11, 2011-12, 2012-13, and 2013-14 were all greater than budgeted amounts.
