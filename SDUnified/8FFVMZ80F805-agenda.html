RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the following charter schools: Innovations Academy Charter School and Urban Discovery Academy.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On December 1, 2009, the Board of Education approved a template Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) setting forth the terms and conditions under which the district and district authorized charter schools will interact with each other regarding Special Education.  In collaboration with charter schools, the Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education MOU template to outline the relationship between the district and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services.  Under the MOU that was collaboratively developed, charter schools will have three options for the delivery of Special Education services at charter schools.  The options are the following:

  1. Charter schools elect to be considered as a public school of the district for the purposes of special education services and funding.  The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the district, and the district will provide special education services.
  2. Charter schools are designated as schools within the district Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA).  With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the district, and the district will reimburse the charter school for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the district for equivalent service providers.
  3. Charter schools are designated as schools of another SELPA.  This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified.  Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served.  The district retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.

Staff recommends that the board approve these Special Education MOU agreements, with the understanding that each individually signed Special Education MOU will return for board approval when individual charters are renewed or there is a change is status.

2. Resolution to Direct Staff to Develop a Plan for Use of Biodiesel or Alternative Fuel Conversion in San Diego Unified School District Diesel Fueled Vehicles (Beiser) (5:00 P.M., AUDITORIUM)
Meeting
Dec 6, 2011 - Regular Charter Meeting, 2:00 P.M. & 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION:  Adopt resolution to direct staff to develop a plan for use of biodiesel or alternative fuel conversion in San Diego Unified School District diesel-fueled vehicles by 2014-15.

 

RECOMMENDATION:    The district currently uses diesel fuel in district vehicles.  Biodiesel is a clean burning alternative fuel produced from renewable resources, is better for the environment and has lower emissions compared to petroleum diesel.   Biodiesel has been shown to reduce asthma and cancer in children due to the reduction in particulate matter emissions and biodiesel is less toxic than table salt and biodegrades as fast as sugar. 

 

Biodiesel is simple to use, biodegradable, nontoxic and essentially free of sulfur and aromatics and is made in the USA from renewable resources such as soybeans or waste vegetable oil.  Biodiesel is the only alternative fuel to have fully completed the health effects testing requirements of the 1990 Clean Air Act Amendments.
 

The board directs the superintendent to develop a plan to use biodiesel (industry standard fuel mix of 20 percent biodiesel and 80 percent diesel) in all diesel fueled vehicles, by the 2014-15 school year.


The board further directs that the superintendent will have flexibility in the day-to-day purchase and use of biodiesel fuel if market prices are significantly higher than diesel or there are supply challenges.
