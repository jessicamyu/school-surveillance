IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: The recommendation to terminate this agreement, effective June 30, 2011, does not impact district staffing during the remainder of the 2010-11 school year.

 

BACKGROUND:  The district’s Off-Campus Integrated Learning Experience (OCILE) Program includes a Race/Human Relations Outdoor Education Program at the Palomar Mountain Outdoor School, located in the Palomar Mountain State Park, approximately 70 miles from San Diego. In order for the board to have maximum flexibility during the establishment of its final budget for the 2011-12 fiscal year, it is in the best interest of the district to provide the required notice to the COE to terminate the existing agreement at this time. This action would not preclude the board from entering into a new agreement for the Palomar Mountain Outdoor School Educational Program at a later date, after it has established its budget for the 2011-12 fiscal year, should the 2011-12 budget contain funds to use the Palomar Mountain Outdoor School.  If a new agreement were to be negotiated with the COE for the 2011-12 school year, then this new agreement would be written to provide the maximum value to the district based upon the available funding approved in the 2011-12 budget.

FISCAL IMPACT:  Approval and full implementation of this Resolution would result in a reduction in currently performed certificated services, and a corresponding reduction in permanent and probationary certificated employees, sufficient to realize necessary budget reductions/savings.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  The Resolution provides for a reduction of certificated services totaling 910.1 full-time equivalents (FTEs).

 

BACKGROUND:  The district is continuing to face a fiscal crisis.  Approximately 80 percent of the district's unrestricted budget is dedicated to staff (including salary and health and welfare benefit costs).  In order to maintain fiscal solvency, and in conjunction with the March 15 approval of a second interim report, the district must take action to allow for significant reductions in certificated staffing consistent with prior board actions and the plan/projections specified in the second interim report.

 

For permanent certificated employees, the Education Code requires that the board pass a resolution to identify the particular kinds of services or programs that may be reduced or eliminated in the upcoming fiscal year.  For probationary certificated employees, Article 19 of the collective bargaining agreement between the district and San Diego Education Association (SDEA) allows the board to determine that reductions are necessary due to lack of funds.  Permanent certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a statutory layoff notice on March 15, 2011.  Probationary certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a contractual layoff notice on or before April 15, 2011.  Noticed employees will be able to challenge the action through a request for hearing (permanent) or the filing of a grievance (probationary).  Temporary certificated employees will receive a separate reminder notice that their services will end at the close of the 2010-11 school year.

 

In order to provide the necessary flexibility and still meet statutory and contractual deadlines, which are jurisdictional, this Resolution lists a large number of programs, many of which may ultimately not be reduced or eliminated if fiscal circumstances improve.  Additionally, based on the fact that the service reductions in the Resolution are FTEs, rather than a number of employees, and other factors related to potentially disputed issues regarding seniority, certificated, and qualifications for certain positions, the number of certificated employees receiving these notices will exceed the FTE service reductions listed in the Resolution.
