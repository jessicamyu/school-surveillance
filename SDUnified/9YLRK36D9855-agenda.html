RECOMMENDATION:  This action will direct staff to continue to collaborate with Innovations Academy Charter School to locate a new facility.   In the event that Innovations Academy Charter School is not able to move into a new facility by June 30, 2017 (the original Fifth Amendment to Multiyear Facilities Use Permit Agreement termination date), this action today authorizes staff to offer an additional year of occupancy on the current site, former E.B. Scripps Elementary School site and also known as the Scripps Mesa Conference Center at Spring Canyon, until July 1, 2018, in accordance with the terms negotiated with Monarch Scripps Mesa, LLC.

 

BACKGROUND OF THE FORMER E.B. SCRIPPS ELEMENTARY SCHOOL SITE, ALSO KNOWN AS SCRIPPS MESA CONFERENCE CENTER SITE: Originally acquired as a temporary location due to the immediate needs of the Scripps Ranch community (former E.B. Scripps Elementary School site); the site was never intended for a permanent school (too close to another school(s), was used as a conference center (Scripps Mesa Conference Center at Spring Canyon), and was then offered on a temporary basis to Innovations Academy Charter School.  Now, the site is part of the new board policy to leverage district properties with joint occupancy and development agreements instead of an outright sale to provide ongoing income for classroom expenses.

 

INNOVATIONS ACADEMY CHARTER SCHOOL: Founded in 2008, and was originally located at private facilities, 2008-09 in the Rolando Park area, 2009-10 in the Mission Valley area, and in 2010-11 in the Allied Gardens area.  In 2011, the district offered Innovations temporary housing at the Scripps Mesa facility until a decision was made regarding the property.  Innovations has been located at the Scripps Mesa facility since July 2011.   Innovations had been looking at another private property to purchase, but that site recently fell through and has necessitated more time for planning.

 

The board approved a joint occupancy development plan as described in the Term Sheet for Monarch Scripps Mesa, LLC to build among other things a STEAM Lab/MakerSpace, which is one in a series of STEAM labs being built across the district.  In addition, the development will provide annual income for district educational programs.

 

All avenues for a new facility for Innovations need to be explored in cooperation with the school: 1) Proposition 39 district facility allocation of a suitable facility if available, 2) commercial property or 3) Proposition Z facility grant funds.  Consideration of all of these options require the continued close collaboration between Innovations and district staff.

BACKGROUND:  On February 2, 2016, Trustee Marne Foster submitted a letter of resignation to be effective February 7, 2016. Pursuant to Education Code section 5090, the letter of resignation was transmitted to the San Diego County Superintendent of Schools.  Due to the timing of her resignation and the upcoming regular election, a special election is neither required nor possible.  Therefore, the board may opt to leave the seat vacant until it is filled in the upcoming election, or it can fill the vacancy by way of provisional appointment.  Staff recommends the board make a provisional appointment to ensure Subdistrict E has representation for the remainder of the current term.

 

The San Diego Municipal Code, which governs district elections, provides that the board may elect to follow the process specified in the Municipal Code or adopt its own process for provisional appointment pursuant to other law.  Staff recommends the board adopt the attached resolution establishing its own process pursuant to Education Code section 5090, et. seq.  The recommended process is similar in substance to what is specified in the Municipal Code, but allows the board to establish its own timelines.  Attached to the resolution is an Application for Board Appointment, which tracks as closely as possible with what is required to be submitted to the Registrar of Voters by any person seeking to run for election to the board. 

 

Staff, further recommends the board establish a schedule for the submission and review of applications, the selection of finalists to be interviewed by the board, and the selection of a candidate for provisional appointment.  Pursuant to the Brown Act, these actions are required to be taken in open session.  Also provided herein is a proposed timeline for this process, which contemplates special meetings be held on February 16, 2016, and either February 23, 2016, or March 1, 2016, for the review of applications and selection of finalists, and final interview and appointment, respectively.  The board-determined dates will be inserted into the Resolution when adopted.

BACKGROUND: An election was held for the district on November 4, 2008, at which a bond proposition (Proposition S) was passed by electors of the district authorizing the sale of $2.1 billion in district general obligation bonds pursuant to California Constitution Article XIII (A), to be used for the construction, reconstruction and/or rehabilitation of school facilities, including the furnishing and equipping of school facilities.  Proposition S provides that the funds shall be used for items described in the November 4, 2008, County of San Diego Registrar of Voters Sample Ballot and Voter Information Pamphlet. The Voter’s Information Pamphlet permits work to be accomplished at school sites throughout the district.

 

Pursuant to Proposition S, the district shall only issue bonds if it projects that the tax rate necessary to pay debt service on the proposed bonds, when combined with the tax rate necessary to pay debt service on presently outstanding bonds of the district in each year, will not exceed the rate levied in 2007-08 (being $66.70 per $100,000 of assessed valuation) to pay debt service on the presently outstanding bonds alone, referring to bonds issued under Propositions MM and S. Proposition Z, which was subsequently passed by electors of the district on November 6, 2012, has a separate tax rate. 

 

Due to prepayment features on the previously issued Proposition MM bonds and current low interest rates, some of the Proposition MM bonds may be refunded for savings.  The savings have been provided throughout the years prior to 2030, and by utilizing certain reserve funds to defease other Proposition MM bonds that are not callable, the district can issue the Proposition S Bonds as Current Interest Bonds instead of Capital Appreciation Bonds and project that the tax rate necessary to pay the Proposition MM and S bonds after the issuance will not exceed $66.70/$100,000.  As a result, the district will receive money at lower interest rates to provide infrastructure under Proposition S and refund Proposition MM bonds with lower cost debt.
