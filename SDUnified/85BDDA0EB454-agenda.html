E.01. Approval of the Declaration of Need for Fully Qualified Educators
Meeting
Aug 19, 2008 - Special Meeting
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes
The Declaration of Need for Fully Qualified Educators is the annual form submitted to the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CCTC) by county offices of education, public school districts, state-wide agencies or non-public schools/agencies. Title Five California Code of Regulations, adopted by the CCTC, requires each public school district anticipating the use of Provisional Internship Permits, Short Term Staff Permits, Limited Assignment Permits and Internship Credentials during the school year to file a Declaration of Need for Fully Qualified Educators. The regulation requires the Board of Education take action to adopt the Declaration, and that the Declaration be an open agenda item and not on the Consent Calendar.

Due to the continuing staffing needs in severe shortage areas such as Math, Science, and Special Education, it is necessary to file the Declaration of Need for the 2008/2009 school year. [Originator/Contact: Sam Wong, Chief Human Resources Officer, 619.725.7132, swong@sandi.net]

This proposal presents a Positive Behavioral Supports (PBS) pilot school program designed to lower suspensions, expulsions, and dropout rates, through creating a more positive climate in participating district schools.  The basis for the pilot program is research-based methodology that provides a framework to support schools in creating a climate in which appropriate behavior is the norm.
 
The plan would be to begin the PBS pilot at 15 district schools in 2008-09, through the support of expert consultants (Safe & Civil Schools).  In the summers of 2009 and 2010, district staff members would receive "training for trainers" inservice offered by the consultant entity.  Once a cadre of qualified district trainers is established, the pilot would be expanded to include additional schools in 2009-10 and 2010-11, using our internal resources.  The goal is to sustain and build on the program entirely without consultant support.
 
The program cost of the PBS pilot proposal over a three-year period is approximately $160,000. The funding source is Title I:
