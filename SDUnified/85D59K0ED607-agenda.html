1. Presentation and Report by Education Trust West on Findings of Educational Opportunity (A-G) Audit; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-2)

RECOMMENDATION: Based on the audit findings and recommendations, district staff will develop an implementation plan and timeline for adopting the UC A-G course sequence as part of high school graduation requirements.  The Education Trust West will provide guidance and technical assistance to district staff in the development of the implementation plan. (Some preliminary recommendations provided by the Education Trust West for Grade 9 students began in fall 2010). 

 

Major audit findings include the following, and are detailed in the attached report:

 

(1) SDUSD graduation requirements are not aligned with the A-G requirements.

(2) Significant gaps in A-G success appear by ethnicity.

(3) Significant gaps in access to A-G courses appear by ethnicity.

(4) Significant gaps exist in access to A-G courses for English learners, students with disabilities, and economically disadvantaged students.

(5) Wide disparities in A-G success exist among SDUSD high schools.

(6) Wide gaps exist in access to AP and IB courses.

(7) Course grades of D disproportionately affected A-G success.

 

Recommendations, which are detailed in the attached report, include:

 

(1) Reform the master schedule process.

(2) Examine grading policies.

(3) Review the use of the D grade across the district.

(4) Open access to AP and IB courses.

(5) Evaluate and implement districtwide professional development.

(6) Expand use of parent connection and individual school websites.

(7) Coordinate middle school and high school curricula.

(8) Create districtwide "bridge" programs.

(9) Examine how to meet the needs of special populations.

(10) Develop a college-going culture districtwide.

(11) Evaluate intervention and support programs.

(12) Evaluate current staffing.

(13) Create a districtwide format for course catalogues.

(14) Prioritize resource allocation for A-G implementation.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The district contracted with Education Trust West from October 1, 2009 through December 31, 2010, in the amount of $300,000.  Future fiscal impact may include the need to purchase additional textbooks and materials to support increased numbers of students taking courses in specific content areas.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: To date, the district has paid $200,000 to the Education Trust West, with remaining payments to be made in September and December 2010 ($50,000 each).

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: District staff that were involved in the audit included the Deputy Superintendent of Academics;  Assistant Superintendent, Instructional Support Services; School Improvement Officers; Executive Director, Accountability Office; Chief Information and Technology Officer; Human Resource Services; central office support staff assigned to the preceding departments; and high school principals, counselors and teachers.  Future impact to district staffing may include the need to hire additional teachers in specific content areas (i.e., World Languages, Mathematics).

 

BACKGROUND:  On April 28, 2009, the board passed a resolution adopting the UC A-G course sequence as part of high school graduation requirements.  On September 29, 2009, the board approved an agreement with the Education Trust West to conduct an Educational Opportunity Audit that included audit and analysis of a random sampling of student transcripts and other artifacts (e.g., master schedules) from district high schools; conducting focus groups at five high schools with students, teachers, and counselors; and conducting a Community Conversation with parents and community members.  On February 9, 2010, the Education Trust West made a presentation to the board to report on its work to date and provide board members with an overview of the audit process.  Staff provided the board with updates on the progress of the audit via memorandums on December 9, 2009, April 27, 2010, June 21, 2010, and July 14, 2010.

BACKGROUND: The Serra High School Data Center Project includes the construction and operation of a new 10,992-square-foot data center building on the existing Serra High School campus.  The proposed project would include a data center facility and Information Technology (IT) classrooms for the high school students.  The data center portion of the building will supplement the District’s existing data center with additional active capacity for storage and retrieval of the district’s network and computer–based applications including mission critical information.  The proposed IT classrooms will include a lab classroom environment with equipment for certification programs and a server room separate from the main server room where students can simulate the function of a server room in an environment that is completely disconnected from the data center equipment.

 

A Mitigated Negative Declaration/Initial Study (MND/IS) was prepared for the Serra High School Data Center Project (SCH No. 2010071022) in compliance with the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) and the Guidelines for Implementation of CEQA.  The MND/IS was made available for public review between July 9, 2010 and August 9, 2010 (Attachment A).   A Notice of Intent (NOI) to Adopt an MND for the proposed project was filed with the San Diego County Clerk’s office and was published in the San Diego Union-Tribune and La Prensa.  Copies of the Draft MND/IS were sent to the State Clearinghouse; federal, state, and local agencies; and community entities/organizations.  The Draft MND/IS was also made available for review at the District’s Physical Plant Operations Annex; at the City of San Diego Library, Tierrasanta Branch and at the Serra High School Main Office. 

 

The Initial Study included an analysis of the following environmental issues: aesthetics, agriculture and forestry resources, air quality, biological resources, cultural resources, geology/soils, greenhouse gas emissions, hazards and hazardous materials, hydrology/water quality, land use/planning, mineral resources, noise, population/housing, public services, recreation, transportation/traffic, and utilities/service systems.  The MND concluded that the proposed project will not result in any potentially significant environmental impacts with implementation of the mitigation measures included in the Mitigation Monitoring and Reporting Plan (Attachment B).  The district did not receive any comment letters as a result of the issuance of the Draft MND/IS.  However, the district did receive a letter from the State Clearinghouse stating that the district complied with the State Clearinghouse and that no state agencies commented on the MND.  The letter is included in the Final MND and did not require revision of the document. 

 

The Final MND was prepared in accordance with CEQA and the Guidelines for Implementation of CEQA.  A copy of the Final MND is located in Board Services Office, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with Pearson Learning Teams (LT) for Bell Middle School to be a Pearson LT "Partnership Site".  The effective term of this agreement is September 29, 2010 through June 30, 2011.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The district will receive services from Pearson LT at a cost estimated to be $60,000.00.  The cost to the district, in accordance with this Agreement, is $3,600.00 for substitutes. Budget string: 0302-30100-00-1192-0100-02000-0100-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE: None.  This is a new agreement.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Services will be provided by Pearson Learning Teams. 

 

BACKGROUND: Pearson Learning Teams is a unique service model distinguished by a rigorous evidence base that leverages teacher collaboration as the central mechanism for improving student achievement and overall school culture. The core components of Pearson Learning Teams are teams (teacher work groups), distributed leadership, tested protocols, stable settings, and assistance and training. The purpose of this MOU is to establish a framework of cooperation between Pearson Learning Teams and Bell Middle School for its Learning Teams services during the 2010­-11 school year.

 

Bell Middle School is one of a few schools in the United States to be selected by Pearson Education to become a Pearson LT “partnership site” for 2010­-11. The benefits to Bell Middle School resulting from this selection include receiving full Pearson LT implementation services for 1 academic year (approximate value of $60,000). This partnership will also provide the opportunity for other schools to observe how Pearson Learning Teams might be relevant and useful to its schools.  The Pearson Leadership Teams will implement tested protocols with the goal of systematically improving teaching and learning at Bell Middle School.  Leadership training for administrators and teacher facilitators will guide the site instructional leadership team as they analyze data, modify teaching practices, and develop targeted formative assessments. Pearson coaches will provide on-site support to guide and sustain the implementation of Learning Teams.  As a result, Bell Middle School staff will provide valuable feedback to the district about the leadership team process and serve as future trainer-of-trainers for other district schools.  Ultimately, this project can improve and expand the leadership capacity within all schools in the district.
