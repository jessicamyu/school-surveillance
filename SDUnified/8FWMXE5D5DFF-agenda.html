RECOMMENDATION:  This conference will provide cluster leaders with the opportunity to share ideas with board members and each other about their ongoing development in San Diego's diverse communities.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  As part of the Vision 2020 for Student Excellence, the board adopted a cluster-aligned framework as a unified, streamlined method for leading the K-12 experience by connecting all elementary, middle and high schools within an established cluster with a more integrated team structure, identity and goals.  As part of the cluster-aligned framework, in 2010-11, the district adopted a Community-Based Reform Model to improve student achievement by empowering teachers, site leaders, students, and parents within each cluster to identify specific cluster needs and develop strategies to address those needs.  Area Superintendents are charged with providing oversight and guidance to schools within a cluster to facilitate articulation of students up the K-12 ladder, and work with cluster leaders and community members to identify ways to best serve students, build strong relationships, and develop a joint sense of ownership.  The 16 established clusters are: Clairemont, Crawford, Henry, Hoover, Kearny, La Jolla, Lincoln, Madison, Mira Mesa, Mission Bay, Morse, Point Loma, San Diego, Scripps Ranch, Serra, and University City.

 

Presentations on work-to-date within each cluster were made to the Board of Education on November 30, 2010, and January 11, 2011.  Since January 2011, a cluster meeting schedule has been distributed to the board monthly and information on each cluster is posted on the district website.  On March 10, 2011, the board directed the Superintendent to convene a conference of cluster leaders to give board members the opportunity to hear about the progress and challenges facing the cluster development in each community.  In preparation for the Cluster Leaders Conference, the Deputy Superintendent Academics met with cluster leaders and Area Superintendents on April 15, 2011, and May 19, 2011, to develop the conference agenda and goals.  The conference will use the World Cafe® model to facilitate a structured conversation (www.theworldcafe.com).
