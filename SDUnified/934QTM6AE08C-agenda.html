RECOMMENDATION: Staff will present an update on the Proposition Z Financial Model.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  At an election held on November 6, 2012, the district sought authorization under Proposition Z to issue general obligation bonds in an amount of up to $2.8 billion.  Proposition Z was approved by 61.8 percent of the district’s eligible voters.  Proceeds from the sale of bonds are to be used by the district for (i) repairing deteriorating 60-year-old classrooms, libraries, wiring, plumbing, bathrooms, and leaky roofs, (ii) removing hazardous mold, asbestos, and lead, (iii) upgrading fire safety systems/doors, and (iv) upgrading classroom instructional technology, labs, and vocational education classrooms.

 

In addition, the board adopted a resolution on September 4, 2012, which stipulates that any bonds issued under the Proposition Z authorization would not be issued as Capital Appreciation Bonds (CABs).

 

As part of the current plan of finance for the Proposition Z Bond Program, the district anticipates issuing one or more series of bonds, as needed, every other year, until the full authorization has been issued. The district's financial advisor and underwriting team will discuss the bond financing model and available options for upcoming Prop Z new monies transaction.

 

On February 26, 2013, a bond resolution authorizing issuance of Proposition Z will be brought forward for board for approval.
