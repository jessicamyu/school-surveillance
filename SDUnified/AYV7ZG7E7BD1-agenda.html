RECOMMENDATION: In accordance with Education Code Section 76004, conduct a public hearing and first reading of the 2019-20 College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Agreements with the San Diego Community College District for college classes to be conducted on San Diego Unified School District high school campuses. Community college and K-12 districts offering college courses on high school campuses are required to enter into CCAP partnerships that include specific language regarding the targeted number of high school students to be served and the total number of full-time equivalent students (FTES) projected to be claimed by the commuinty college district for those students.

  1. San Diego City College (SV20-0195-99) - for various classes at Crawford, East Village, Garfield, Hoover, Lincoln, Morse and San Diego High Schools and the School of Creative and Performing Arts (SCPA)
  2. Mesa College Accelerated College Program (ACP) (SV20-0196-99) - for Mathematics and Political Science classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Point Loma and University City High Schools
  3. Mesa College (SV20-0197-99) - for various classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Mission Bay, Point Loma, and University City High Schools
  4. Miramar College (SV20-0198-99) - for various classes at Mira Mesa, Scripps Ranch, Serra ad University City High Schools

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. The San Diego Unified School District is eligible for average daily attendance (ADA) revenue for the periods each day that students are enrolled in San Diego Unified courses, and the San Diego Community College District is eligible for apportionment for the period that students are enrolled in City, Mesa, or Miramar College classes.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. As stated in the agreements, each entity agrees to adhere to its respective collective bargaining agreements and no San Diego Community College District or San Diego Unified School District teachers will be displaced by the implementation of these agreements.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Education Code Section 76004 authorizes community college and K-12 districts to enter into College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) partnerships with the goal of developing pathways from high school to community college for career technical or preparation for transfer, improving high school graduation rates, or helping high school students achieve college and career readiness. Through dual enrollment partnerships, school districts and community college districts can create clear pathways of aligned, sequenced coursework that allow students to more easily and successfully transition to for-credit, college-level coursework leading to an associate degree, transfer to the University of California (UC) or California State University (CSU) systems, or to a program leading to a career technical education certificate. The 2019-20 CCAP agreements were presented to the San Diego Community College Board of Trustees for first reading on June 13, 2019, and will be agendized for final reading and adoption on July 18, 2019. The 2019-20 CCAP Agreements will be brought forward for the San Diego Unified School District Board of Education final reading and adoption on Juy 30, 2019.

The Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) is an essential element of California's Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF). It is a three-year, district-level plan that is updated annually. The 2019-20 LCAP is the third year of the three-year plan. The plan describes the school district's key goals for students as well as the actions (with expenditures) the district will take to achieve the goals and the measures used to indicate progress. The LCAP also includes an Annual Update, which reflects the implementation of the current year's LCAP (2018-19). The San Diego Unified School District has aligned its six LCAP goals and supporting actions and expenditures to the board-adopted Vision 2020 Quality Schools in Every Neighborhood. A budget overview for parents is included in this years LCAP. 

 

RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and adopt the district's proposed 2019-20 Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP). The 2019-20 content was presented to the Board of Education and the public on June 12, 2019, along with specific implementation plans for the 2019-20 school year. The 2019-20 LCAP document was presented to the Board of Education at a public hearing and first reading on June 18, 2019. This is the second and final reading of the district's proposed 2019-20 LCAP. 

 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The proposed 2019-20 LCAP is submitted for adoption in conjunction with the adoption of the proposed 2019-20 budget. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: The Annual Update section of the proposed 2019-20 LCAP includes 2018-19 actions, services, and estimated actual expenditures.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The District Advisory Council (DAC) and the District English Learner Advisory Committee (DELAC) serve as the district's LCAP advisory committees. During 2018-19, updates on LCAP implementation were presented to the board and public at regular board meetings, and the presentations were available on the district's LCAP webpage for dissemination to all stakeholders. The early draft 2019-20 LCAP was presented to the DELAC on May 9, 2019, and to the DAC on May 15, 2019, for review and comment. The district received questions and comments from the DELAC and DAC, responses to the DAC's and DELAC's questions and comments will be provided by the end of July. The full LCAP document is posted on the district website  at https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

 

BACKGROUND: The LCAP is intended to be a comprehensive planning tool. For school districts, pursuant to Education Code Section 52060, the LCAP must describe, for the school district and each school within the district, goals and specific actions to achieve those goals for all students and each subgroup of students in Education Code Section 52052, including students with disabilities, for each of the state priorities and any locally identified priorities.

 

The district's local priorities (LCAP Goals are):

 

1. Closing the Achievement Gap with High Expectations for All: All levels of the organization work to improve student achievement and close the achievement gap for all underperforming student groups.

2. Access to Broad and Challenging Curriculum: Students have equitable access to rigorous, well-rounded, standards-aligned curricula and access to and skill in applying technologies to leverage learning, assuring readiness for a full range of post-graduation options.

3. Quality Leadership, Teaching and Learning: Effective leadership and teaching is evident system-wide with a unifying vision that equips and empowers all stakeholders to provide optimal student learning opportunities and outcomes.

4. Positive School Environment, Climate and Culture: Schools provide safe and well-maintained facilities and positive learning climates and instructional practices that are culturally responsive, challenge bias, and support the academic, social, emotional, and physical needs of students.

5. Parent and Community Engagement with Highly Regarded Neighborhood Schools that Serve Students, Families, and Communities: Parents and community members are engaged and work within and across schools to support student learning. Neighborhood schools are highly regarded for their quality as well as their service as centers for extended learning and enrichment opportunities, and academic and social services.

6. Well-Orchestrated Districtwide Support Services and Communications: All parts of the organization - personnel and infrastructure - work together to support student success through quality schools in every neighborhood.

 

The State priorities are:

 

1. Basic: The degree to which teachers are appropriately assigned and fully credentialed in the subject areas and for the students they are teaching; students in the school district have sufficient access to the standards-aligned instructional materials; and school facilities are maintained in good repair.

2. Implementation of State Standards: The implementation of state board adopted academic content and performance standards for all students, which are:

  • English Language Arts - Common Core State Standards (CCSS) for English Language Arts 
  • Mathematics - CCSS for Mathematics
  • English Language Development (ELD)
  • Career Technical Education
  • Health Education Content Standards
  • History-Social Science
  • Model School Library Standards
  • Physical Education Model Content Standards
  • Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)
  • Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA)

The Committee’s role is to accept, review, and make recommendations to the Superintendent for requests for classification review. The committee consists of one representative from each classified bargaining unit and an equal number of district representatives Reclassification Committee.

 

The classified bargaining units received the first set of draft job descriptions on April 7, 2017, the next set on September 28, 2017, and the parties have been in ongoing negotiations since that time. Approved position descriptions are brought before the Board of Education for approval  on an ongoing basis as the parties reach agreement.

BACKGROUND: The Proposed Project includes the installation of new security fencing at the existing Clay Elementary School (ES) joint use field (6506 Solita Ave, San Diego, CA 92115) and within a City of San Diego (City) owned parcel, jointly operated as a joint use field by the City and the district, adjacent to E.B. A ten-foot-high security fence will be installed around the perimeter of the existing joint use field at each school to improve student and staff safety and security at the schools. Construction would begin in early July 2019 and is scheduled to be completed by the end of September 2019. These projects are anticipated to be constructed using the previously authorized JOC Fencing Contract.

 

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The installation of new security fencing at Clay Elementary School and E.B. Scripps Elementary School Joint Use Fields is  subject to the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), however, the project is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Section 15303 – New Construction or Conversion of Existing Structures (Class 3). Class 3 consists of construction and location of limited numbers of new, small facilities or structures; installation of small new equipment and facilities in small structures; and the conversion of existing small structures from one use to another where only minor modifications are made in the exterior of the structure. The numbers of structures described in this section are the maximum allowable on any legal parcel. Examples of this exemption include accessory (appurtenant) structures including fences. Therefore, the application of a Class 3 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2, and summarized below, prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve First Amendment to Agreement with Vision To Learn, Inc., to continue providing vision screenings, examinations, prescriptions for lenses, and free glasses to district students (PS19-0786-68).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On February 26, 2019, the board ratified an Agreement with Vision to Learn, Inc. to provide vision screenings, examinations, prescriptions for lenses, and free glasses to district students. This First Amendment modifies the scope of work to eliminate the "opt-out" process and replace with an "opt-in" process. All other provisions in the Agreement remain.
