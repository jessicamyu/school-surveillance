Approval of agreement with Pitsco, Inc. for a three year period, effective August 5, 2008 through August 5, 2011, for the Star Academy Program.

On May 27, 2008, Board of Education acted on a new district Promotion/ Retention Plan: A System to Support Student Learning and approved the allocation for materials, supplies, curriculum, and staff for a specialized accelerated program for retained eighth grade students. To implement this program, the district desires to contract with Pitsco, Inc. for the Star Academy Program, including comprehensive, innovative and instructional curriculum, professional development as well as equipment, management system, and enhancements.

California Government Code Section 53060 allows the district to contract with and employ persons for special services if such persons are specially trained, experienced, and competent to perform the special services requested. Specifically, Pitsco, Inc. will provide five days of initial onsite training for six teachers, an administrator, and a counselor in the Star Academy research-based Synergistic curriculum for 120 retained eighth grade students. They will provide professional services, including but not limited to site visits for bimonthly program evaluations, liaison evaluations, and ongoing technical and professional support. Pitsco, Inc. will also furnish and install modular furniture and equipment that is unique in that students are seated in pairs with innovative, hands-on learning experiences at their work stations. Each module session provides hands-on cooperative curriculum delivered in a unique, team-based learning environment by delivering science and math content so that students are interactive and collaborative. In addition, Pitsco, Inc. will provide the needed software and computers for this program, and ongoing technical support for teachers and students.

In one academic year, students will take courses to recover credits in subjects that they failed in the eighth grade and complete ninth grade coursework in an accelerated block schedule. The objective of the program is to end social promotion, decrease the drop-out rate, and increase graduation rates. The Star Academy will be located on the Mann Middle School campus.

The SPSA identifies and addresses the instructional needs of students and specifies how categorical funds provided through the Consolidated Application will be used to accomplish the goals outlined in the plan.

The SPSA is developed or modified through an annual planning cycle. The School Site Council (SSC) in collaboration with the site instructional leadership team is responsible for developing, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating the SPSA. Site principals, SSC chairpersons, SSC members and the district Advisory Council for Compensatory Education (DAC) received training on January 29 and 31, 2008, and on February 5, 7, 11, 12 , and 19, 2008, regarding specific plan requirements. In addition, certificated staff with extensive knowledge and experience reviewed each plan and provided individual assistance to schools to ensure that each SPSA meets state requirements and that the plan and associated categorical budget allocations are in alignment.

The local governing board must review and approve each school’s SPSA before it may be implemented. This report brings forward, for board approval, the 2008-09 SPSAs and categorical budget summaries for nine schools. Categorical funding levels for each school were determined by the Budget Operations Department in June 2008, as part of the adoption of the 2008-09 district budget. Unanimous board approval/ratification of this item allows the disrict to declare an emergency to provide for the continuation of classes or to avoid danger to life or property such that competititve bidding is waived to perform the necessary restoration work. (See accompanying board item on the contract award to Southland, who performed the repair work, item K.15.).

On June 19, 23, 24 and 25, 2008, the main circuit breaker shut off at Rosa Parks Elementary School, resulting in the administration building being without power. Power was restored after each event by resetting the main breaker, but it was clear that the problem needed to be assessed and corrected to prevent the main breaker from tripping unnecessarily.

The district has secured Southland Electric, Inc. to analyze the problem and to perform the work to restore power and secure voltage. Southland has performed preliminary investigations and reset the administration building sub-breaker, lowering the tripping threshold to have it shut off before causing the main breaker to shut off. BSE Engineering, the consulting electrical engineer to Southland, has opined that the main breaker is the cause of the power outages, in part due to a problem with the cable to the administration building. Physical Plant Operations (PPO) staff located a replacement breaker installation after business hours on July 3, 2008, requiring SDG&E support. At that time, cable feeding the administration building was also tested, and replaced as needed.

Southland returned each day to serve as a stand-by electrician to reset any breakers that may have shut off until the problem was resolved. Southland also recommended that it temporarily install a power supply specific to the main breakers that powers down during low load periods. The breaker now efficiently supplies power through evenings, which is the time of concern, given that the outages have not been during normal business hours. PPO staff also arranged for electrical generators, if needed, to provide same day service for immediate power to the site.

District legal staff has written an opinion on emergency declaration, shared with the chief logistics officer and the Board of Education, and concurs that this action is justified and within the stipulations of the Public Contract Code.

The costs to repair and restore power to the buildings will not exceed $50,000.

PCC 20113 (a) In an emergency when any repairs, alterations, work, or improvement is necessary to any facility of public schools to permit the continuance of existing school classes, or to avoid danger to life or property, the board may, by unanimous vote, with the approval of the county superintendent of schools, do either of the following:
(1) Make a contract in writing or otherwise on behalf of the district for the performance of labor and furnishing of materials or supplies for the purpose without advertising for or inviting bids.
(2) Notwithstanding Section 20114, authorize the use of day labor or force account for the purpose.
(3) Nothing in this section shall eliminate the need for any bonds or security otherwise required by law.
