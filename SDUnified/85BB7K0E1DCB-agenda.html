I.08. Direction to Superintendent on Implementation of New Title I Funding Guidelines (Evans)

The board intends to introduce planning and accountability to Title l programs which are funded by the federal government to improve academic achievement in schools that are highly impacted by poverty. The Title 1 program does not entitle schools to automatically add dollars to a school budget and the board wants a careful accounting as to how schools spend Title 1 funds. The board has designated schools with over 60 percent of students in poverty to make these schools a priority, but that their programs will not be automatically funded, but rather will be funded for those programs that are clearly in line with the goals of Title 1 and will produce results for students. Changes need to be implemented for schools in the 40-60 percent category in a fair and reasonable manner that does not cause students at these schools to suffer in their academic achievement. This is especially true for schools in Program Improvement or for schools that would face other serious consequences due to the loss of these funds.

There are two possible means of Title 1 funding for the 40-60 schools: 1) funds that are not approved for specific programs of the 60-100 group; 2) potential additional funds from the federal stimulus package, which would allow us to broaden the program to more schools. For those current programs in the 40-60 schools that are producing achievement gains, every effort will be made to fund those programs from other funds.

The board requests democratically elected Site Governance Teams review these programs and their usefulness that district staff, particularly the Director of Title 1 programs, to review the various program and plan for the 2009-10 school year based upon the above criteria and that board members will meet with community members at the cluster level for further input. The board will exercise its oversight responsibility to ensure that no local, state, or federal tax dollars are wasted, but are serving the needs of San Diego Unified students.

The Superintendent and staff will develop a plan and timeline for the above goals and will report back to the board.
