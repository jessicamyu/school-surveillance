BACKGROUND:  Pearson DBA NCS Pearson, Inc. will provide professional development training titled: "Transitioning to the Common Core State Standards (in Mathematics: Common Core Connections I" for 1,260, district K-8 mathematics teachers).  Trainings will be held on April 4 and 5, 2013, for traditional year teachers and on April 10 and 11, 2013, for year-round teachers. There will be nine concurrent grade level specific workshops per level over four days for a total of 36 workshops with 35 teachers attending each workshop.  The training will afford participants with an opportunity to learn about the use of both teacher and student transitional materials which will be made available at each grade level training session.

 

The training is designed to help teachers make connections between current best practice, Common Core content expectations and Math Practice Standards for Students (Habits of Mind that students must be able to use and apply mathematically.)  Participants in this professional development will explore all components of the Common Core State Standards for Mathematics with an emphasis on the framework for learning provided for college and career readiness; focus on the impact of Mathematical Practice Standards for developing students' critical thinking and reasoning skills by exploring processes, proficiencies, and problem solving, and explore at specific grade levels, the enVision and middle school Pearson Math CCSS transition material designed to supplement California standards based texts and support materials.

 

The California Common Core State Standards (CCSS) are slated for full implementation in the 2013-14 school year.  Grade level workshops are specifically designed to familiarize teachers with the Common Cores State Standards, the shifts in instruction that will be necessary to meet CCSS expectations for student learning and prepare teachers with the knowledge and understanding needed for successful full implementation of the new standards.

RECOMMENDATION: Board Trustees Richard Barrera and Kevin Beiser will discuss and propose Board action to adopt the resolution in support of immigration reform.

 

BACKGROUND:  The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District recognizes that immigration reform must protect the rights of all students and their families, regardless of immigration status, family structure, sexual orientation, gender identity, or marital status, and provide sufficient family-based channels for migration in the future.  Family separation caused by immigration enforcement endangers children and their family members, many of whom attend San Diego Unified schools.  A number of children who attend San Diego Unified schools have been separated from one or both parents as a result of an immigration raid, and some children have lost a parent at the hands of border agent brutality.

 

The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District maintains that immigration reform must seek to restore trust between immigrant and refugee communities and local police by reducing racial profiling and increasing transparency and accountability for the involvement of federal immigration authorities in local law enforcement. The Board supports a reasonable and wide-ranging approach to reforming the now broken immigration system that protects children, youth, and their families.  Civil and human rights are deeply rooted in the fabric of democratic and principled societies, and must be instilled in all elements of our enforcement apparatus to ensure every individual’s dignity and humanity is upheld, recognized and respected.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve resolution to declare 2012-13 Second Interim qualified certification, including 2013-14 and 2014-15 budget solution strategies for submission with 2012-13 Second Interim Financial Report to the San Diego County Office of Education (SDCOE).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Budget reductions/savings of approximately $92.63 million in Fiscal Year 2013-14 and $52.7 million in Fiscal Year 2014-15.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: $120 million

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. No layoffs.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The district is preparing for the development of its 2013-14 budget.  Fiscal assumptions with regard to 2013-14 revenues and expenditures are made based on historical trends, future commitments, and direction from the SDCOE.

 

On January 10, 2013, Governor Edmund G. Brown, Jr. released his proposed budget for the 2013-14 fiscal year.

 

Overview:  The 2013-14 Governor's Budget reflects a significant improvement in the state's finances due to the economic recovery and voter approval in the November 2012 election of temporary increases in sales and use tax and personal income tax (Proposition 30) as well as increased corporate income taxes for multi-state businesses (Proposition 39).  The Governor's budget summary document indicates that economic recovery has begun and provides a plan to reduce the "wall of debt" the state has accumulated after years of deficits.

 

For the first time since 2008-09, the state does not face a multi-billion-dollar deficit.  The Governor's budget projects General Fund (GF) revenues and transfers of $98.5 billion and proposed $97.7 billion in GF expenditures. 

 

Major proposed components of the increased funding to Proposition 98 for K-12 schools in 2013-14 include:

  • Paydown of deferrals: $1.8 billion
  • Implementation of a new Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF): $1.6 billion
  • Implementation of Proposition 39, the California Clean Energy Jobs Act: $400.5 million
  • Increase in funding for the mandates block grant to reflect the addition of two programs: $100 million

San Diego Unified will follow the traditional Base Revenue Limit model until approved by the state legislature.

 

What's Next:  The Governor's Budget proposal contains significant education reform proposals that will undoubtedly be the subject of debate and discussions throughout the spring.  The deadline for final legislative action on the budget is June 15.  The budget takes effect on July 1, 2013.

 

Based on current fiscal assumptions, and the 2013-14 Budget Solutions presented and implemented, we project a $52.7 million, balanced budget gap for 2014-15.

 

The district must demonstrate its commitment to take all steps necessary to meet its fiduciary responsibility and balance district budgets for a three-year period as required by AB1200.  The attached resolution outlines this commitment and the attached Potential Solutions delineate the reductions recommended to the board.
