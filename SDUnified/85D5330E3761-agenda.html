RECOMMENDATION:   Approve of Title I, Part A, Local Education Agency (LEA) Waiver Application to the California Department of Education (CDE) and the State Board of Education (SBE)

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  This action impacts the district's ability to use Title I, Part A funding.


PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  N/A


IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: N/A


BACKGROUND:  As part of the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA), the amount of Title I funding provided to most local education agencies nearly doubled in 2009-10.  Although ARRA Title I funding is managed under a separate resource code, its use must follow the same guidelines regular Title I funding.  This includes the ways in which the funding is spent, requirements for specific reservations, and limits on carryover.  The U.S. Department of Education recognized that because of the large amount of funding provided, it may not be practical for LEAs to follow all of the rules regarding the reservations and carryover limits.


The California Department of Education (CDE) and State Board of Education (SBE) were granted waiver requests pertaining to Title I, Part A of the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) funding for programs provided by the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) allocations and the proposal to repeal provision 34 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) Section 200.47(b)(1)(iv)(A),(B). 


LEAs seeking approval for these waivers are expected to meet all application criteria and may be subjected to increased SBE accountability requirements in addition to specific requirements related to the waiver.


The approval of these waivers will allow the LEA:

  • Exemption from the 14-day school choice parent notification requirement for students enrolled in newly identified program improvement schools for 2009-10 or schools that anticipated exiting PI during the 2009-10 school year but did not (Elementary and Secondary Education Act [ESEA] Section 1116[b][1][E][i] and 34 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) Section 200.43[b][4][iv]).
  • To exclude some or all of Title I ARRA funding from the calculation of the LEA’s 20 percent obligation for SES and/or choice-related transportation (ESEA Section 1116[b][10] and 34 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) Section 200.48[a][2]).
  • To exclude some or all of Title I ARRA funding from the calculation of the LEA’s 10 percent obligation for professional development (ESEA Section 1116[c][7][A][iii]).
  • To exclude some or all of Title I ARRA funding from the calculation of the LEA’s 10 percent obligation for professional development for schools in PI (ESEA Section 1116[b][3][A][iii]).
  • To exclude some or all of Title I ARRA funding from the calculation of per-pupil amount for SES (ESEA Section 1116[e][6][A] and34 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) Section 200.48[c][1]).
  • To carryover more than 15 percent of its Title I, Part A, 2009 and 2010 allocations due to ARRA funding (ESEA Section 1127[a][b]).

The CDE will review the application and schedule the waivers for SBE approval at the next regularly scheduled SBE meeting.  LEAs will be notified of the waiver approval status after consideration by the SBE.

BACKGROUND: Strategic Technology Plan - Districts applying for state and federal technology funding such as e-Rate or EETT grants must develop a multi-year coordinated and comprehensive plan that describes the core and strategic elements along with technologies and services necessary to guide implementation of program, the allocation of resources, and monitoring of the plan. The plan:

  • Focuses on student achievement
  • Incorporates and maximizes the board approved i21 initiative
  • Accounts for increased levels of funding for sustainability
  • Involves collaborative process for developing monitoring and key outcomes metrics
  • Involves leadership at all levels to ensure a successful implementation
  • Includes monitoring and reporting at regular intervals
  • Includes periodic review and modification to keep pace with changes in technology and desired outcomes

The approval of the Strategic Technology Plan and e-Rate Addendum will enable the district to meet the federal and state requirements and implement the specific strategies designed to meet the instructional and operational technology needs of the district.
