BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue our improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting district wide, we are implementing a new process streamlining our Independent Contractor Agreements. Our goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective deputy or chief, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

The School Names Committee has reviewed the request from the PLHS Instrumental Music Boosters, along with the required documentation, and unanimously supports this recommendation.

He was a positive force for music education in the Pt. Loma community and the region.

 

Dr. Nelson died in 2003, after a courageous battle with Parkinson's Disease and ALS, commonly known as Lou Gehrig's disease. According to PLHS Principal Barbara Samilson, "Prof. Nelson was always there for our students:  when he said, "I'll make it happen," he did. PLHS owes a great debt to Prof. Nelson and hopes to honor him perpituity for his selfless service."

RECOMMENDATION:  Conduct public hearing and approve the district's general waiver request to waive the class size penalty should the district exceed the 1964 district average (35.6) number of pupils per teacher in Grades 4-8. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Undetermined; contingent on class size calculations.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The district is requesting a waiver from the State Board of Education (SBE) to waive any Grades 4-8 class size penalty should the district class size exceed the allowed limit.  The district faces severe fiscal challenges from multiple years of state funding reductions and has done everything possible to maintain reasonable class sizes, but due to the lack of funding, the district has been forced to increase class size.    

 

On March 13, 2012, board trustees approved an increase in the funded allocation for Grades 4-8: from 32:1 to 34.6:1 at Grades 4-6; and from 28.7:1 to 35:1 at middle schools (Grades 6-8). The waiver the district is seeking would waive the penalty should the district exceed the 1964 district average (35.6) number of pupils per teacher in Grades 4-8. The penalty equates to a reduction in Average Daily Allowance (ADA) funding. The waiver is being sought only as a contingency should unforeseen circumstances arise that could push the districtwide average slightly over the allocation by some fraction (e.g., unanticipated changes in student enrollment). Therefore, the waiver will protect district funding. Without the waiver, the district could receive less funding from the state (ADA), thereby creating the possibility of having to reduce staffing further.

 

School Site Councils (SSCs) at district schools with Grades 4-8 were asked to discuss the class size penalty waiver request at their March meetings and respond to the Office of Accountability with a position (in favor, opposed to, or neutral) and any comments. SSC meetings were held at all 116 elementary, ten K-8, six atypical, and 24 middle schools during the month of March (156 schools total).  The San Diego Education Association (SDEA) was also notified and consulted for input but has not yet submitted a written response.

RECOMMENDATION: Direct staff to proceed with creating Resolutions of Intent to Sell for various excess properties owned by San Diego Unified School District as described below, and return to the board in May with the Resolutions for action.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Potential additional revenue of approximately $24.9 million, if all items enacted by the end of 2012-13.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The Advisory Committee on the Utilization of Excess School Property (ACUESP) is aware of the proposals.  Their position regarding the sale of property has been that it should occur when ownership does not benefit the district now or in the near future, and it would not cost more in the future to to recover for educational purposes.  While there is no immediate need for these sites to meet district educational needs, the district may need to consider a limited program of home-to-school transportation to nearby schools with space in the case of the Barnard property if redevelopment and/or population change causes growth in the student population in the vicinity of the site.

 

BACKGROUND: On November 1, 2011, the board approved the investigation for the sale of the Barnard Elementary Site, Bay Terraces #11, and Mission Beach Center.  As part of these investigations, staff has now obtained appraisals for each  of these properties. 

 

In addition, staff has researched other sale possibilities to increase revenue, including the sale of three, single-family lots on the Revere Center site and Camp Elliott #3 (see attached map of all sites in question, and detail plan for the Revere site).  Staff has also been working with the state to recoup unused site fees at Bay Terraces #6, and additional unused sites fees would be saved in the future if Bay Terraces #11 and Camp Elliott #3 were sold (no unused site fees are paid at Barnard, Revere and Mission Beach).  In total, the appraisal values received for the three properties staff were originally directed to investigate was in line with the estimate of likely revenue that would need to be generated from their sale in 2012-13 budget documents presented to the board.

 

The three lots at the Revere Center and the Camp Elliott #3 sites were estimated to be worth approximately $4.9 million according to the Jones Lang LaSalle study of excess property done for the board in May 2011.

 

Approximately $130,000 could be saved in state-mandated unused site fees if Bay Terraces #6, Bay Terraces #11 and Camp Elliott #3 were sold.

 

It should be noted that the timeline (see attached) to sell the properties by the end of 2012-13 will require action on Resolutions of Intent to Sell no later than May.  Selling the additional properties at Revere and Camp Elliott #3 would give increased latitude to realize revenue in case issues arise during escrow for any of the properties.
