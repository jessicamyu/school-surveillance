RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and approve a Resolution certifying that the district has provided sufficient standards-aligned textbooks, instructional materials, or both. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  The district may use funding from various sources, including state Local Control Funding Formula funds and state lottery funds to purchase standards-aligned textbooks and instructional materials. Newly adopted materials are provided based on actual enrollments the first year of an adoption. Schools are responsible for maintaining sufficiency during the remaining years of core adoptions by ordering from the district's Instructional Materials Office, using an Instructional Materials Order Form or by ordering textbooks and instructional materials using the Destiny Textbook Management System.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  In accordance with Education Code 60119, each school district’s governing board must hold a noticed public hearing, no later than the eighth week of the school year, to determine whether each pupil in the district has sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in the following subjects: mathematics, science, history-social science, and English language arts. The governing board must also determine whether each student enrolled in a foreign language or health course has been provided sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials. According to Williams Legislation, sufficient textbooks or instructional materials means that each pupil, including English language learners, must have a district-adopted textbook or instructional materials, or both, to use in class and to take home. However, the district does not have to provide each pupil with two sets of instructional materials.  The board is required to adopt a resolution setting forth its determination that there are sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials as applicable. 

 

Current Williams legislation also requires that science classrooms have available basic science laboratory equipment for students in science laboratory courses offered in Grades 9 to 12.  The district Science Department and the San Diego County Office of Education developed a science laboratory equipment worksheet to identify basic core, lab equipment for science laboratory courses.  All schools conducted inventories and principals verified and submitted a “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency.”

 

Williams Settlement Legislation requires districts and schools to closely examine student access to textbooks and instructional materials and gather evidence to use as the basis of the district's annual resolution of sufficiency.  Administrative Circulars 11, 12, and 13, dated August 28, 2014, were posted notifying school sites (not subject to a Williams site visit) of the need to conduct a school site inventory and complete a “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency.”  In September 2014, instructional materials inventory data and current PowerSchool enrollment for September 5, 2014, was compiled and entered into the “Instructional Materials Sufficiency Form” and provided to each elementary school by the Instructional Materials Office.  Sites were directed to verify their instructional materials inventory and take action to remedy any insufficiencies by ordering from the Instructional Materials Office.  Principals confirmed instructional materials sufficiency and returned the “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency” to the Instructional Materials Office by September 19, 2014. Secondary school principals, after completing their inventories and taking action to remedy any insufficiencies, also returned the “Principal Verification of Instructional Materials Sufficiency." 

 

Twenty-seven elementary schools, five K-8 schools, eight middle schools, and eight senior high schools were visited by a team from San Diego County Office of Education.  All 48 schools were found to have sufficient instructional materials.  Additionally, all eight senior high schools were found to have adequate laboratory equipment for the science laboratory courses offered.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution in the matter of recognizing October 2014, Filipino American Heritage Month.

 

BACKGROUND: Filipino American History Month was first proclaimed by the 103rd Congress of the United States through House Resolution 155.

 

The Filipino American community is the second largest Asian American group in the United States with a population of approximately 3,100,000 people.

 

Filipino Americans have contributed greatly to the fine arts, music, dance, literature, business, journalism, education, science, technology, government, politics, fashion, health care, the armed forces and other fields in the United States that enrich the landscape of the country.

 

Efforts should continue to promote the of Filipino American history and culture, because the roles of Filipino Americans and other people of color have been overlooked in the writing, teaching and learning of United States history.

 

In September 1988, Bell Jr. High School was the largest junior high school west of the Mississippi and had the largest concentration of Filipino students in the San Diego Unified School District.

 

Under the leadership of Counselor Rolando Cruz and teacher Evangeline Lopez, a proposal was made to Bell Principal Joene Bruhn to pilot Filipino 1-2.

 

In 1989, the Filipino community rallied to advocate the offering of Filipino language classes at Morse High School and Bell Junior High School.

 

In September 1989, the very first Filipino language class was offered at Morse High School at 6:30am (0 period) and was taught by Rosalina (Sally) Idos.

 

When No Child Left Behind Legislation was signed into law in 2002, the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing required that a California Subject Matter Examination for Filipino be written so that teachers who are credentialed in other subject matter other than Filipino be required to pass the Filipino examination.

 

Filipino Heritage Month is a time of reflection and remembrance, and provides an opportunity for all people in the United States to learn and appreciate more about Filipino Americans’ historic contributions to the Nation.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution designating October Anti-Bullying Awareness Month for all schools and students in the San Diego Unified School District.

 

BACKGROUND: San Diego Unified’s Vision 2020 suggests “schools will be safe, attractive neighborhood learning centers,” and indicates quality neighborhood schools provide a “supportive environment that values diversity in the service of students.”

 

The San Diego community is committed to supporting the young people in district schools as they make positive choices that lead to successful futures.

 

The Council of ASB Presidents fully supports an Anti-Bullying Awareness Month in which the schools will have flexibility to carry out anti-bullying activities within that month. The students’ participation in anti-bullying activities would provide the opportunity for the following to occur:

  • Allow students to speak honestly about their experiences with bullying and find solace in the knowledge that others have been in similar situations.
  • Allow students to learn of the various forms of bullying including cyber, verbal, physical, exclusion, and lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, questioning (LGBTQ) bullying.
  • Help students become aware of bullying on campus and the consequences of their actions.
  • Support students to come up with solutions to stop bullying and enforce positive interactions between students.
  • Engage teachers and parents in raising awareness if a student is being bullied.
  • Invite the media to further emphasize the importance of taking a stand against bullying.
  • Help empower students in the recognition of the role everyone plays in addressing and reducing bullying with the goal to eradicate bullying on all school campuses.

All elementary, middle, and high schools will coordinate anti-bullying activities at the beginning of each new school year in the month of October, during a week or days of their choosing.

 

The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District declares October as Anti-Bullying Awareness Month and the Superintendent and other appropriate staff will initiate anti-bullying activities at all schools, among all students to be planned, coordinated, and implemented by the individual school site students and staff.

 

The board encourages parents and the community to support the work of the students and staff
