2. Special Education Memorandum of Understanding Agreements with America's Finest Charter School and Innovations Academy Charter School; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-2)
Meeting
Sep 4, 2012 - Regular Charter Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with America’s Finest Charter School and Innovations Academy Charter School

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.


BACKGROUND: On December 1, 2009, the Board of Education approved a template Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) setting forth the terms and conditions under which the district and district authorized charter schools will interact with each other regarding Special Education.  In collaboration with charter schools, the Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education MOU template to outline the relationship between the district and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services.  Under the MOU that was collaboratively developed, charter schools will have three options for the delivery of Special Education services at charter schools.  The options are the following:

  1. Charter schools elect to be considered as a public school of the district for the purposes of special education services and funding.  The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the district, and the district will provide special education services.
  2. Charter schools are designated as schools within the district Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA).  With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the district, and the district will reimburse the charter school for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the district for equivalent service providers.
  3. Charter schools are designated as schools of another SELPA.  This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified.  Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served.  The district retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.

Staff recommends the board approve the Special Education MOU agreements with the understanding the Special Education MOU will return for board approval when the charter petition is renewed or there is a change is status.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve restructuring proposals for six district schools in Program Improvement Year 4 status:  Adams, Alcott, Marshall, and Porter elementary schools, and Audubon and Carver K-8 schools.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Each school has been allocated regular Title I funding based on the school's ranking, according to the sliding scale distribution approved by the board on November 29, 2011.  Specifically, each of the six schools will receive the following amount per funded student:  Adams--$319; Alcott--$179; Audubon--$374; Carver--$374; Marshall--$411; and Porter--$392.  Additionally, each school will receive supplemental funding for Program Improvement (PI) Year 4 and 5 schools of $65.46 per student.


PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  General Information--Program Improvement (PI) Status:  Under the No Child Left Behind (NCLB) Act of 2001, a Title I school that, for two consecutive years, does not meet or exceed a series of annual academic performance goals known as Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) is designated Program Improvement. Such schools must provide specified types of services and/or interventions during each year that they are identified as PI.  A school exits PI if it makes AYP for two consecutive years.

 

District Schools in PI Year 4, 2011–12--School Restructuring:  A school that enters its fourth consecutive year in PI status is required to develop a plan to restructure that will be implemented the following year, in this case in 2012–13. The restructuring plan must describe in detail the changes in governance, instructional practice, and staffing that will be required to improve students’ academic achievement and enable the school to meet its AYP goals.  District schools submitting a PI Year 4 restructuring plan are:  Adams, Alcott, Marshall, and Porter elementary schools, and Audubon and Carver K-8 schools.  

 

The restructuring proposals were developed by School Program Improvement Leadership Teams composed of teachers, administrators, classified staff, and parents, in collaboration with the Office of Accountability.

 

Each school's School Site Council (SSC) was involved in the development and approval of its restructuring plan.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approval of revisions to the 2012-13 year-round student instructional calendar:

  • Thursday, April 25, 2013, from a Teacher Preparation Day to a Furlough Day
  • Friday, April 26, 2013, from an instructional day to a non-instructional day
  • Friday, June 14, 2013, from a Furlough Day to an instructional day

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

San Diego County Taxpayers

 

On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that will provide $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified School District facilities.  On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11-member Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) for Proposition S. As part of the Proposition and as required by law, the board appointed members to the ICOC to monitor how the district spends these tax dollars.

 

The membership for the Proposition S ICOC is selected from the following criteria:

  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens’ organization 
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district 
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization

Terms for membership on the Proposition S ICOC are for two years, and there is a two-term maximum. No employee or official of the district shall be appointed to the citizens’ oversight committee, nor any vendor, contractor or consultant of the district.
