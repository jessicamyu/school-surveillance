5. Resolution in Support of a November 2020 Schools and Communities First Funding Act Ballot Initiative (Evans/Barrera)
Meeting
Dec 17, 2019 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.

Fiscal Impact

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in Support of a November 2020 Schools and Communities First Funding Act Ballot Measure.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Since the passage of Proposition 13 in 1978, school funding in California has experienced severe limitations in what was once the main source of funding for schools, the property tax. While the intent of Proposition 13 is to help homeowners, a loophole in the system permits major commercial and industrial properties to avoid regular reassessment, providing a windfall to commercial property owners at the expense of vital school funding. The share of the property tax burden has shifted away from commercial property and toward residential property throughout the state and in virtually every county. Per-pupil support, which relies on state funding, has declined from the top 10 in the nation to the bottom quarter. School funding in California is $2,400 per pupil less than the national average and $10,000 below the top-funded states, while California's cost of living is among the highest in the nation. Staffing ratios for teachers, guidance counselors, librarians, and administrators in California rank at the bottom of the nation. Public schools in California face challenges in providing an equitable and fair education for a student population with vast differences in language, income, parental education level, and other social, educational, and economic factors. Research has proven that investments in high needs students raises achievement levels, lowers poverty, and increases the productivity of the workforce. Estimates by academic researchers at the University of Southern California (USC) have identified that reassessing commercial property will raise $12 billion in property tax revenue every year for local schools, cities and counties. USC has identified that nearly 80 percent of the revenue will come from just eight percent of large properties. USC research shows that a majority of commercial property owners already are assessed at close to market value, making the current system inequitable among businesses, benefiting a relatively small number of properties owned by the largest corporations and wealthy owners who have held land for a long period of time. The current failure to close the commercial property loophole has led to poor land use and inflated land values, particularly limiting the ability to provide adequate high-density housing and land use. The Schools and Local Communities Funding Act is on the November 2020 ballot. The measure provides about $4.5 billion annually for K-14 schools. The measure will provide funding to all school districts, over and above Proposition 98 funding, and following the Local Control Funding Formula to all students in need in all districts. The Schools and Local Communities Funding Act will also provide billions in funding yearly for cities, counties, and special districts in locally controlled revenues for affordable housing, parks, libraries, emergency responders, health and human services, libraries, and public infrastructure. The Schools and Local Communities Funding Act will improve land use, provide a direct tax break to small businesses, and level the playing field between neighboring commercial property owners.

The draft position descriptions were prepared using the data collected from employees through an online survey tool as well as management feedback. The goal of this system-wide review of classified and classified management position descriptions was to ensure that position description were more than a list of tasks and duties. A well-written position description gives the reader an understanding of the basic and essential functions as well as the requisite knowledge, skills, and abilities required to perform successfully in the position. By updating position descriptions, we are able to clearly define the roles and interactions, and enhance the efficiency of the organization.

 

In addition to the Position Description Review Project, in the Summer of 2016, the Human Resource Services Division, Administrators Association of San Diego, Police Officers' Association, and all three chapters of the California School Employees Association collaborated to create a joint labor-management Reclassification Committee. The Committee’s role is to accept, review, and make recommendations to the Superintendent for requests for classification review. The committee consists of one representative from each classified bargaining unit and an equal number of district representatives Reclassification Committee.

 

The classified bargaining units received the first set of draft job descriptions on April 7, 2017, the next set on September 28, 2017, and the parties have been in ongoing negotiations since that time. Approved position descriptions are brought before the Board of Education for approval on an ongoing basis as the parties reach agreement.

The notice calling for bids was published once a week for two successive weeks in accordance with Public Contract Code section 20112. Bids were due and opened on October 30, 2019. CEQA ANALYSIS: Approval and implementation of the Proposed Project is subject to CEQA, however, it is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines section 15301 (Class 1) – Existing Facilities. Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of existing or former use. The key consideration is whether the project involves negligible or no expansion of an existing use. Examples cited by the CEQA guidelines relevant to the Proposed Project include interior or exterior alterations involving such things as interior partitions, plumbing, and electrical conveyances. The Proposed Project consists of the maintenance, replacement, and repair of existing facilities at the Kearny High School stadium that would not result in the expansion of existing use. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of the existing school campus. The Proposed Project is consistent with the existing use on the project site. Furthermore, the Proposed Project would not extend into any offsite or open space areas that may be classified as an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed at the project site or in the project vicinity that, when combined with the construction timeframe of the Proposed Project, would result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Additionally, construction activities, including staging, would be contained to the project site. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. The Proposed Project includes maintenance, replacement, and repair of existing facilities at the Kearny High School stadium. No work is proposed offsite that could result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas as a result of the Proposed Project. As such, there is nothing unusual about existing school site or surrounding area. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project would not result in the construction of new facilities that would directly, or indirectly, affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. The closest designated state scenic highway is the portion of State Route 163 through Balboa Park, located approximately four miles south of the project site and the project site is not visible from this area. Therefore, implementation of the Proposed Project would not substantially damage scenic resources within a state scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the California State Water Resources Control Board Geotracker database, the project site is not located on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. According to the CEQA Guidelines regarding historical resources, a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource amounts to a significant impact on the environment (Guidelines § 15064.5(b)). Accordingly, a substantial adverse change means demolition, destruction, relocation, or alteration of the resource or its immediate surroundings resulting in the significance of the resource being materially impaired. Furthermore, according to the National Register of Historic Places (NRHP) guidelines, properties completed at least 50 years ago must be considered for eligibility for designation in the NRHP. Construction of the Kearny High School stadium was completed in 2016, and is not considered a historic property. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Project.

RECOMMENDATIONS: Approve Agreement with VectorUSA to provide, install and integrate software for a districtwide Emergency Communication System (ECS) (SV20-0647-13).  Authorize the execution of any documents necessary to effectuate this Agreement. The term of this Agreement is December 18, 2019 through December 17, 2020, with delegation of authority to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Officer to exercise an additional Option Term 1 and Option Term 2 provided for in the contract documents to extend the Agreement for up to two additional one-year terms, subject to the identification of budgeted funding sources.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not-to-exceed amount: $1,518,000.00. Budget String: 9XXX-90502-00-6214-8500-0000-21500-8612.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Improving Emergency Communication Systems (ECS) is one of the initiatives to enhance physical security at schools.  This project implements ECS districtwide in an effective and efficient manner. The district specified the following requirements for a robust ECS:

  • Native integration with the district’s existing proprietary Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) telephone system, known as Cisco Unified Communications Manager (CUCM). 
  • Limit modifications to existing CUCM software and servers installed at district Data Centers.
  • Provision for local survivability in the case of network failure.
  • Availability for integration with mobile devices, enabling the ability to launch emergency communications away from desk phones.  This feature also provides survivability in the case of a local school power failure in an affordable manner.
  • Integration with the district’s existing site PA, Intercom, Bell, Clock and messaging systems, allowing for “single-pane-of-glass” management of existing systems as one comprehensive Emergency Communication System.
  • Capability for future integration with the district’s access control, surveillance, fire protection, smoke control, and building control systems.

On November 20, 2019, the district received three Proposals from Providers for the licensing, installation and integration of an enterprise-level InformaCast Fusion software subscription service which will provide software licensing for San Diego Unified’s emergency mass notification system throughout its 175 school sites using a single, centrally managed and accessible interface.  The objective of the emergency communication system is to equip each school site with the full breadth of features and functions of a two-way emergency communication system. Providers addressed how they will implement the requested services identified in the district's Scope of Work.  An evaluation committee of five members from Facilities, Planning and Construction, and Integrated Technology Services reviewed submittals from AT&T Business, CDW-G, and Vector Resources, Inc. dba VectorUSA. It is the recommendation of the evaluation committee that it is in the best interest of the district to award this project to VectorUSA.

 

Public Contract Code 20118.2 states, due to the highly specialized and unique nature of technology, telecommunications, related equipment, software, and services, because products and materials of that nature are undergoing rapid technological changes, and in order to allow for the introduction of new technological changes into the operations of the school district, it is in the public’s best interest to allow a school district to consider, in addition to price, factors such as vendor financing, performance reliability, standardization, life-cycle costs, delivery timetables, support logistics, the broadest possible range of competing products and materials available, fitness of purchase, manufacturer’s warranties, and similar factors in the award of contracts for technology, telecommunications, related equipment, software, and services.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The National Center for Montessori in the Public Sector (NCMPS) will prepare a fully implemented Montessori program for Logan Memorial to train teachers in the Montessori theory and practice, facilities requirements, and professional development. NCMPS will work with district staff to provide a three-year program of intensive support followed by ongoing professional development.

 

The intent of the program is to train district teachers and staff on theory and practices, facilities requirements and professional development related to the implementation of the NCMPS Montessori program. The services shall provide the preparation and training on the following components:

  1. Program design
  2. Financial modeling
  3. School site development
  4. Communications
  5. Procurement of instructional resources
  6. Professional development

NCMPS will collaborate with district staff to create and deliver a comprehensive program that will include the following components:

  1. Critical Design Decisions – Consult with district design team to determine implications (needs and benefits) of creating a comprehensive prenatal to high school program and make critical design decisions regarding facilities, enrollment trajectory/growth, staffing, community engagement, etc.
  2. School Site Development – With district staff, spend one day onsite consulting on appropriate interior design. Consult with Montessori principles for indoor and outdoor environments, including possible onsite visit, and customer prepared report on recommendations for classroom spaces and adjacencies in order to provide an optimal Montessori prepared environment.
  3. Orientation – Provide an orientation session on Montessori theory and practice to all members of the district, learning community, and school staff who will be involved in establishing the Montessori program.
  4. Standards Alignment – Support in understanding and communicating the alignment of Montessori and state standards.
  5. Implementation Timeline, Enrollment Scenarios and Daily Schedules – NCMPS will prepare a report recommending implementation timeline and enrollment scenarios to build up from Planning Year through full enrollment. Prepare recommendations on class configuration and daily schedule that will align with district needs and incorporate Montessori protocols needed to sustain a strong practice (see below under Professional Development).
  6. Financial model and five-year budget projections – NCMPS will prepare model inputs and structures, determine and test key assumptions based in specifics of Logan Memorial Complex design, Solicit questions and feedback, and Partner review.

NCMPS will work with the district's Communications Department and staff to create a communication plan that consists of providing resources and information to share with parents and community members, assistance with parent and community meetings and Q&A sessions, partner with the administration to develop new family orientation and support the preparation of school handbooks and documents.
