D.01. Renewal Charter Petition for O'Farrell Community School (Board Governance Policy: OE-10)
Meeting
Mar 3, 2009 - - Special Meeting
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes
Dollar Amount
$6,000,000.00
The Office of Chief School Innovation and Choice Officer recommends the Board of Education approve the renewal charter petition for O’Farrell Community School Center for Advanced Academic Studies for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2009, and expiring on June 30, 2014. The Office of School Innovation and Choice recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the board’s decision to renew the charter petition. The board originally approved the O'Farrell charter conversion on January 11, 1994; O'Farrell has been operating as a charter school in the San Diego Unified School District for 15 years. The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective on July 1, 2009. The potential fiscal impact to the district is an estimated loss of $6,000,000 generated in average daily attendance in 2009-10, because the district does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools. District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met requirements to continue performing as a charter school in this district.

D.10. Adoption of Amended Charter Petition for Momentum Middle School Charter School (Board Governance Policy: OE-10)
Meeting

Action
Fiscal Impact
No

The Office of School Innovation and Choice (OSIC) recommends the Board of Education (BOE) approve the amendments to the charter for Momentum Middle School (Momentum). OSIC received amendments for Momentum on January 28, 2009. The amendments shall become effective March 4, 2009 and will expire with the original term of the charter petition, June 30, 2010. The Momentum petitioners submitted their amendments to the San Diego Unified School District (SDUSD) for review and consideration of approval in accordance with Education Code requirements. Staff has reviewed the amendments and found them to meet legal, fiscal, and operational requirements. Staff recommends the adoption of this report as findings in support of the BOE’s decision to approve the amendments to the Momentum charter petition.

The original Momentum charter petition was granted by the Board of Education on February 22, 2005. The charter term is effective through June 30, 2010. The charter term is not impacted by these amendments. The Momentum petitioner’s primary request to amend the charter is to include a ninth grade to their academic program in the 2009-10 school year. The school’s intent is for the 2009-10 school year to serve as a transition year. The school intends to propose an expanded academic program from sixth to twelfth grade when the charter is due for renewal in 2009-10. In addition, the school is proposing amendments to change their official name from Momentum Middle School to Magnolia Science Academy San Diego and to unify the school with the Magnolia Foundation nonprofit board.

The proposed amendments are:

  • to add ninth grade to the academic program in the 2009-10 school year, the school currently offers a 6-8 grade program;
  • to change the school name from Momentum Middle School to Magnolia Science Academy San Diego;
  • to change the Articles of Incorporation, with the state, under the new foundation name, the Magnolia Foundation; the original foundation name, as evidenced on the school’s original 501 c3 corporation, is the Dialog Foundation;
  • to include language in the revised charter that gives the school the option of joining another Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA);
  • to reflect the school’s location in the San Carlos area of San Diego, the original petition included language that the school would be included in Clairemont Mesa or Mira Mesa;
  • to provide greater detail regarding lottery admissions preferences that are within regulatory guidance provided by the California Department of Education (CDE);
  • to provide more detail regarding the process that will be used if students are recommended for expulsion;
  • to contain new state regulation regarding the procedures to be implemented if the school were to close.
