RECOMMENDATION: Approve proclamation recognizing Harvey Milk Day on May 22, 2014.

 

BACKGROUND: Harvey Milk was born May 22, 1930 in Woodmere, New York. Harvey served in the United States Navy during the Korean War as a diving officer aboard the submarine rescue ship USS Kittiwake and was later transferred to Naval Station, San Diego to serve as a diving instructor with the rank of Lieutenant. Harvey was a tireless advocate of fairness for all Americans, both as a candidate for office and as a San Francisco Supervisor; and

 

In 1977, Harvey was elected to the San Francisco Board of Supervisors, becoming the first openly gay man to be elected to public office in California. This milestone achievement gave hope to millions of gays and lesbians across the country that a day would come when they could live their lives openly and honestly without fear of discrimination. As a Supervisor, Harvey worked with others to secure passage of San Francisco’s landmark Gay Rights Ordinance. This ordinance extended employment protections to gays and lesbians in San Francisco, and it became a model for anti-discrimination legislation throughout California and the nation.

 

In 1978, Harvey traversed the state to campaign against Proposition 6, which was known as the Briggs Initiative. Had it passed, Proposition 6 would have required California school districts to fire openly gay and lesbian teachers solely because of their sexual orientation. The proposition was defeated in the November 1978 election in part because Harvey successfully appealed to Californians’ basic sense of fairness.

 

Although Harvey’s life was cut short he was a hero and role model, and his legacy of hope, tolerance, and equality lives on.

On April 8, 2003, the board established the CTEAC. The committee's purpose is to develop recommendations on the programs and services of the Office of College, Career, and Technical Education (CCTE). [California State Education Code Sections 8006-8008, 8070, 8080, 51224, 51228, 51225.3, 17070, 17070.52, 17070.955, 17078.7, 17079.72; State Allocation Board (SAB) Regulation 1859.196].

15. The Director of CCTE

The CTEAC must be presented to the board through a scheduled presentation on at least an annual basis.

Governance and Operations

Staff has visited Darnall Charter School annually. The annual site visit includes document review, classroom observations, and interviews of the charter faculty and leadership. During the annual site visit, as well as throughout the year, the areas of academics, governance and operations, and finance are monitored as part of the district’s oversight duties.

 

Finances

Based on the Darnall budget, location, grade levels, and programs offered, the enrollment projections have been evaluated and appear to be within normal range compared to other charter schools and district schools.  The estimated CDE funding rates and Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) factor, provided by School Services of California’s Financial Projection Dartboard, have been used to estimate revenue for Darnall based on the projected Average Daily Attendance (ADA).  This revenue projection has been compared to the projected revenue included in the charter school petition budget and no significant variances have been found.  Other expenses unique to charter schools have also been examined and found to be comprehensive and appropriate.  Over the last five years, the petitioners have addressed all required annual financial audits and reporting to the state and the district.  

 

Based on the projected enrollment, revised projected budget, expenses, and cash flow submitted in the renewal petition, Darnall has been found to be financially viable.  In addition, the district has examined whether Darnall maintains an adequate Reserve for Economic Uncertainty greater than 3 percent of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater, as another consideration of their financial viability.  According to the financial documentation provided in its charter, Darnall has maintained an adequate reserve for economic uncertainty.

 

Attached to this report is a table that compares the budget to actual expenses for each year of the charter.  The school’s actual expenses for each of the four years of operation (2009-10, 2010-11, 2011-12, and 2012-13) were all greater than budgeted amounts.  In summary, the school is financially sound.  At the end of 2012-13, pursuant to the school’s unaudited actuals report, the ending fund balance of $1,666,706.04 exceeded the reserve requirement of 3 percent or $50,000.

 

Staff recommends approval of the renewal of the charter of Darnall Charter School for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019.
