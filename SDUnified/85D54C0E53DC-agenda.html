BACKGROUND:  In response to board direction, staff is presenting proposed ballot measure options that would ask voters of the district to approve a parcel tax to provide funds for district educational programs.  Under state law, a parcel tax measure must be approved by two-thirds of the voters.  This measure would be placed on the November 2, 2010 General Election ballot.


Since September of 2009, staff has worked with a consultant to develop this proposal.  In October, 2009, the district completed a voter survey to explore voter opinions about several alternative approaches to a parcel tax.  The results of the survey were presented to the board in an update report on January 26, 2010.  At that time, staff indicated that a ballot measure would be drafted for board consideration prior to the August 3 deadline to place measures on the November ballot.   Staff has consulted with district academic and finance leadership and has met with several external stakeholder groups over the past several months to develop the draft measure before the board for consideration.  Staff is bringing this measure to the board at this meeting so the board has adequate time before the August deadline to make any revisions or to direct staff to move forward with placing the measure on the ballot.


As the board is aware, a parcel tax measure is being considered because of the more than $400 million in budget cuts that have been imposed on the district over the last four years.  In order to maintain educational programs and reasonable class sizes, it is critical that the district explore all available revenue sources.  A parcel tax is the only local funding option available to school districts to provide funds for operational proposes.


Measure Summary

The Resolution provides for a temporary parcel tax of five years that would be used for specific educational purposes and programs outlined in the measure.  State law requires that all similar property categories must be taxed uniformly so this measure provides for a separate tax level for residential parcels, commercial industrial parcels and parcels with multi-family units.  As proposed the measure would generate an estimated $58 million annually for the district. 

 

The measure specifies the following uses for the parcel tax funding:

  • Category 1: Direct Per-Pupil Budget Allocation.  To ensure that funds from this measure benefit every student and classroom in the district, a $150 per-pupil allocation of funds (a projected $20 million annually) will be granted to every district school, based upon the number of students attending said school who reside within the boundaries of the district.  This per-pupil allocation shall only be used for academic programs at the school site, including teachers, librarians, and counselors or for educational supplies to support classroom instruction, including, without limitation, class size reduction; math, science, and English education; career technical and vocational education; music and art programs that enhance the academic program; and classroom instructional technology.
  • Category 2: Class Size Reduction.  Of the funds remaining after the per-pupil allocation described above, the district will dedicate approximately 54 percent of remaining funds (a projected $20 million annually) to district programs to prevent any further erosion of class sizes in K-3 below their current level of 24:1, to restore K-3 class sizes back to a 20-1 ratio as soon as possible, and to help finance a reduction in class sizes to as small as possible within five to seven years.  
  • Category 3: Math and Science Education; English Proficiency.  To maintain and enhance education programs to prepare students for Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) careers and to insure students achieve proficiency in English, approximately 33 percent of the remaining funds (a projected $13 million annually) will be dedicated solely for enhancing curriculum, reducing class sizes and providing professional development to improve teacher skills in Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) and English Proficiency in district middle schools and high schools.
  • Category 4: Classroom Instructional Technology.  The district has become a national model for the use of technology in classroom instruction.  Funded by Proposition S, the district has purchased student netbook computers, and placed high speed internet, wireless networks and interactive teaching tools in every district classroom. Approximately 13 percent (a projected $5 million annually) will be allocated to maintain and upgrade the district’s classroom instructional technology, provide on-going professional development to improve classroom instruction utilizing the latest technology.

Funds may only be used to support direct classroom instruction, and no funds may be expended for central office or site or district administrators.

 

District-sponsored charter schools existing as of November 2, 2010 are entitled to receive a pro-rata share of tax revenue for use by each recipient charter school in its discretion for purposes described in the categories above.  Charter schools may spend the revenues on some or all of the categories described above, as needed; provided, however, that expenditure restrictions described within a selected category must be observed.

To ensure that all parcel tax funds are spent as directed by the voters in this measure the district will be required to arrange for an annual, independent audit of all revenues and expenditures of funds generated by this measure and cause that audit to be published on the district’s website.  The audit shall be completed and published within 90 days of the end of each Fiscal Year that this measure shall be in effect.

Summary


The superintendent recommends that the board approve the placement of the parcel tax measure on the November ballot.  This measure will give the voters of the district the opportunity to consider the fiscal position of the district and to make a decision to provide emergency funds that are necessary to partially replace state cuts to education that impact all students in the district.

3. Resolution for Transfer of Proceeds of Sale of Surplus Property to the District General Fund Pursuant to Education Code Section 17463.7 (OE-7)
Meeting
Jul 13, 2010 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution certifying that the district has no major deferred maintenance needs that cannot be funded with the district’s existing capital outlay resources, as required by Education Code section 17463.7, prior to transferring the proceeds from the sale of surplus real property into the general fund; the sale of the Beale Property did not violate the provisions of any local bond act; and the property is not suitable to meet the district’s projected school construction needs for the next ten years.


FISCAL IMPACT: $6,000,000.00


PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE: None.


IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.


BACKGROUND: On November 22, 2005, the district sold to the San Diego Community College District certain real property known as the former Beale Elementary School Site (“Property”) in exchange for payment in the amount of $14,650,000.  The district now proposes to transfer a portion of the proceeds of the sale of the property, in the amount of $6,000,000 into the general fund pursuant to Education Code section 17463.7.  The funds will be used to pay for Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes (TRANs) and American Recovery and Reinvestment Act (ARRA) interest expenses of $5,100,000 and non-capitalized technology purchases in the amount of 900,000.


Section 17463.7 provides that a school district may deposit the proceeds of the sale of surplus real property into the general fund to be used for any one-time general fund purpose, provided the property was purchased by the district using entirely local funds.  If local bond funds were used towards the purchase, Section 17463.7 provides a formula for determining the amount of proceeds which may be transferred pursuant to this section.  Legislative Counsel has issued an opinion that this section permits the district to transfer the proceeds of surplus property sales which occurred prior to the effective date of the statute. 


Prior to exercising its authority under this section, the board must certify to the State Allocation Board that 1) it has no major deferred maintenance needs that cannot be funded by the district’s existing capital outlay resources; 2) the sale of the property does not violate the provisions of any local bond act; and 3) the district has determined that the property is not suitable to meet the district’s projected school construction needs for the next ten years.  The board must also present a plan at a regularly scheduled meeting which identifies the source and intended use of the funds and describes the reasons why the expenditure will not result in ongoing fiscal obligations for the school district.


District staff has confirmed that the property was purchased entirely with local funds, and that no bond proceeds were used towards the purchase.  District staff has further confirmed that the district has no major deferred maintenance needs that cannot be funded by the district’s existing capital resources; the sale of the property did not violate the provisions of any local bond act; and the property is not suitable to meet the district’s projected school construction needs for the next ten years. 


This resolution makes the required certifications to be sent to the State Allocation Board.  Staff has prepared the required plan to be presented in conjunction with this item.

    RECOMMENDATION: Approve additional spending authorization in the amount of $650,000.00 for the purchase of classroom and school furniture for Hoover High School and other various school sites from Agreement No. 08-44-685 between Wichita Unified School District No. 259, County of Sedgwick, State of Kansas and Virco Manufacturing Company. Agreement effective for the period from January 1, 2010 through December 31, 2010.


    FISCAL IMPACT: This additional spending authorization of $650,000 will provide for a total amount of spending authorization in the amount of $1,000,000.00 for the term of the agreement.


    PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: Since January 1, 2010, purchases of classroom and school furniture from Virco Manufacturing Corporation total $188,399.91.


    IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.


    BACKGROUND:  Since 1956, this district has standardized its classroom and school furniture to be furniture manufactured by the Virco Manufacturing Corporation.  Virco's corporate headquarters and major manufacturing facility is located in Torrance, California.  Virco manufactures quality furniture that serves this district for years beyond the product's warranty period, withstanding years of daily use in the district's classrooms.  It also provides exceptional service in meeting the district's needs.  The classrooms built during Prop MM are furnished with Virco products.  By using the Wichita Unified School District's agreement with Virco as a strategically sourced contract, the district further reduces its cost for Virco classroom and school furniture by leveraging its purchases as part of a  nationwide contract used by other school districts and governmental agencies. This agreement provides the lowest cost, net to buyer, that Virco will sell its products to any public agency. In addition, this agreement has multiple delivery options which reduces the district's costs, and it includes increasing discounts for larger orders when the district orders large quantities of classroom and school furniture.  Since Virco is both the manufacturer and the distributor, then the district's costs are further reduced since the district is dealing with only one company.


    On November 10, 2009, the board approved the purchase of classroom and school furniture from Agreement No. 08-44-685 between Wichita Unified School District No. 259 and Virco Manufacturing Company for the period from January 1, 2010 through December 31, 2010. The amount of spending authorization approved at that time was $350,000.  This additional spending authorization is necessary at this time for the immediate purchase of Virco classroom and school furniture for the 20 classroom addition under construction at Hoover High School, as well as for the purchase of Virco classroom and school furniture for schools utilizing their Proposition S site discretionary funds and for schools who have identified other funding for Virco classroom and school furniture.


    Under Public Contract Code section 20118, the governing board has determined that it is in the best interests of the district to utilize this "piggyback" contract to purchase office and classroom supplies. This statute provides that the district may purchase personal property from contracts already competitively bid by another public agency. The district may only order products specified in the contract at the prices specified in the contract.
