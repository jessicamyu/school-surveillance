3. Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing October 2021 as Filipino American History Month
Meeting
Sep 28, 2021 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of recognizing October 2021 as Filipino American History Month for all schools and students in San Diego Unified.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The celebration of Filipino American History Month in October of each year commemorates the first recorded presence of Filipinos in the continental United States, which occurred on October 18, 1587, when “Luzones Indios” came ashore from the Spanish galleon Nuestra Senora de Esperanza and landed at what is now Morro Bay, California. In 1992, the late Dr. Fred Cordova, along with his wife, Filipino American National Historical Society (FANHS) Founder Dr. Dorothy Laigo Cordova, first introduced October as Filipino American History Month with a resolution from the FANHS National Board of Trustees. In 2009, U.S. Congress recognized October as Filipino American History Month in the United States. Filipino Americans are the second largest Asian American group in the nation and the third largest ethnic group in California, after Latinx and African Americans. 4,716 students in the San Diego Unified School District are Filipino-American. In September 1988, Bell Junior High School was the largest junior high school west of the Mississippi, and had the largest concentration of Filipino students in the San Diego Unified School District. In 1989, the Filipino community rallied to advocate the offering of Filipino language classes at Morse High School and Bell Junior High School. Filipino language classes are currently offered at Challenger Middle School and Mira Mesa High School as well. Filipino Americans have contributed greatly to the fine arts, music, dance, literature, business, journalism, education, science, technology, government, politics, fashion, health care, the armed forces, and many other fields that enrich the landscape of the country. Efforts should continue to promote Filipino American history and culture, because the roles of Filipino Americans and other people of color have been overlooked in the writing, teaching, and learning of United States history. Filipino History Month is a time of reflection and remembrance, and provides an opportunity for all people to learn and appreciate Filipino Americans’ historic contributions with various activities and events to bring awareness of the significant role Filipinos have played in American history. Resources for teaching and learning about Filipino American History are available at http://fanhs-national.org/filam/.

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: The month of October is nationally recognized as Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer/Questioning, Intersex, and Asexual Plus (LGBTQIA+) History Month. The San Diego Unified School District is committed to maintaining safe, inclusive environments wherein each and every one of its students can thrive; including:

  • Implementing practices that support LGBTQIA+ students as prescribed by the School Success and Opportunity Act, AB 1266, which went into effect on January 1, 2014.
  • Options for name and gender marker changes on all unofficial school records to reflect the student’s name and gender marker that is consistent with the student’s gender identity irrespective of the gender listed on the pupil’s records.
  • Addressing students by the name and pronouns consistent with their gender identity, without the necessity of legal documentation or a change to the student’s official district record.
  • Options for the use of facilities consistent with their gender identity, irrespective of the gender listed on the pupil’s records.

We, San Diego Unified School District, acknowledge the importance of teaching our youth about the historical and present oppression and discrimination that impacts the LGBTQIA+ community, the positive and accurate contributions of the LGBTQIA+ community, and the ways in which we can proactively build advocacy and allyship with the LGBTQIA+ community. The Department of Youth Advocacy provides learning opportunities, professional development and workshop opportunities to all SDUSD schools, educators, and administrators; and the Department of Youth Advocacy develops leadership opportunities and pathways for the youth themselves to engage their schoolmates in year-round dialogue that preventatively mitigates for harm and humanizes ALL students for being nuanced individuals who bring value to the school community. The San Diego Unified School District offers lesson plans, media, and print resources to support schools in observing LGBTQIA+ History Month through the Digital Cultural Awareness Center at culturalawarenesscenter.weebly.com and information/resources for students, families, educators at youthadvocacy.us. October is also designated as National Bullying Prevention Month, staff members and students are developing and coordinating activities that proactively reiterate their commitment to diversity, inclusion, affirmation, and safety, including:

  • Promoting student awareness of the various forms of bullying including cyber, verbal, physical, and social exclusion on the basis of actual or perceived race, religion, national origin, disability, neurodiversity, socio-economic status, language, immigration status, sexual orientation, gender identity and/or gender expression;
  • Implementing history and social science (HSS) curriculum that highlights the contributions of members of the LGBTQIA+ community and allows students to see accurate and respectful reflections of themselves [and their classmates] in the curriculum as prescribed by the FAIR Education Act, SB 48, which was signed into law on July 14, 2011.
  • Attending professional development to ensure that all staff and administrators are updated on the most recent policies, protocol, and best practices to support and guide LGBTQIA+ students to greater access, affirmation, and opportunities for success.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing October 2021 as National Bullying Prevention Month in San Diego Unified and use this opportunity to highlight our ongoing commitment to promote an improved school environment, enforce positive interactions between students, and raise awareness for teachers if a student is being bullied.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The National Bullying Prevention Month campaign was founded in 2006. The San Diego Unified School District calls for all schools to be safe, inclusive neighborhood learning centers, and each quality neighborhood school to provide a “supportive environment that values diversity in the service of students.” School district leadership is committed to actively addressing bullying by utilizing updated and revised protocols and procedures, participating in innovative training, and effectively mitigating incidents of bullying and/or intimidation for all involved. San Diego Unified has partnered with the Anti-Defamation League (ADL) and their No Place for Hate program which provides participating schools with anti-bias and anti-bullying resources to help create sustainable change that leads to an improved school environment. San Diego Unified has implemented programming to support schools in building positive school climates and a culture of upstanders through "Ally Action,” bullying prevention efforts, and student-led capacity building for peers as well as school site staff. The San Diego Unified Council of ASB Presidents fully supports October as National Bullying Prevention Month in which the schools may conduct anti-bullying activities within the month and throughout the year. Students’ participation in anti-bullying activities provides opportunities for the following to occur:

  • Allow students to speak honestly about their experiences with bullying and learning skills to be aware of and speak of injustices, learning to be allies and upstanders, as they advocate for positive school environments.
  • Allow students to learn of the various forms of bullying which people may experience including, but not limited to, cyber, verbal, physical, and exclusion;
  • Help students become aware of bullying on campus, the consequences of bullying and the many ways in which they can activate their voice to promote social change.
  • Support students to determine solutions to stop bullying and enforce positive interactions between students.
  • Engage educators and families in raising awareness about the many factors that affect bullying and work to mitigate them, giving highlighted importance to our LGBTQIA+ students.
  • Connect with larger community and civic efforts to confront bullying, advocate for inclusive and positive school communities while raising awareness about the complexities of bullying.
  • Work toward addressing and eradicating incidences of bullying on all school campuses.

 

The San Diego Unified School District offers lesson plans, video resources, and print resources through the Digital Cultural Awareness Center at culturalawarenesscenter.weebly.com and information about Ally Action Programming, bullying prevention, and bullying intervention at youthadvocacy.us. All elementary, middle, and high schools coordinate anti-bullying activities at the beginning of each new school year to be held in the month of October, during a week or days of their choosing.

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The proposed license agreement is a subject to CEQA, however, the project is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines section 15301 Existing Facilities (Class 1). Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of existing or former use. The key consideration is whether the project involves negligible or no expansion of an existing use. The Proposed Project involves the execution of a license agreement between the district and the Friends of La Jolla Elementary to use a portion of the existing school campus lot for an Open Aire Market. No construction or other improvements would be required as part of the license agreement, and no permanent expansion of use is proposed beyond existing operations. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2, and summarized below, prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

 

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

 

Environmental Analysis: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

 

  1. Location: The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundary of an existing developed school site owned by the district. The Proposed Project is consistent with the existing use of the site as a market. The Proposed Project would not extend into any off-site or open space areas that may be classified as an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact: The Proposed Project would not result in new construction activities or result in a change in existing operations and would not result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect: The Proposed Project includes the execution of a license agreement for the use of existing district facilities for an open aire market. No new construction or operational activity is proposed on- or off-site that would result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas as a result of the Proposed Project. As such, there is nothing unusual about the project site or surrounding area. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways: The Proposed Project does not require the construction of new facilities and therefore, would not directly, or indirectly, affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. Therefore, implementation of the Proposed Project would not substantially damage scenic resources within a state scenic highway. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites: According to the California State Water Resources Control Board Geotracker database, the project site is not located on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources: The Proposed Project does not require the construction of new facilities and therefore, would not result in a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource as described in CEQA Guidelines Section 15064.5(b). Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Project.
  7. On December 17, 2020, 16 contractors submitted Statements of Qualifications (SOQs) to the districts RFQ.
