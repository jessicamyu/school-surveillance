RECOMMENDATION: In accordance with Education Code Section 76004, conduct a public hearing, second reading and adoption of the 2019-20 College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) Agreements with the San Diego Community College District for college classes to be conducted on San Diego Unified School District high school campuses. Community college and K-12 districts offering college courses on high school campuses are required to enter into CCAP partnerships that include specific language regarding the targeted number of high school students to be served and the total number of full-time equivalent students (FTES) projected to be claimed by the community college district for those students.

  1. San Diego City College (SV20-0195-99) - for various classes at Crawford, East Village, Garfield, Hoover, Lincoln, Morse and San Diego High Schools and the School of Creative and Performing Arts (SCPA)
  2. Mesa College Accelerated College Program (ACP) (SV20-0196-99) - for Mathematics and Political Science classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Point Loma and University City High Schools
  3. Mesa College (SV20-0197-99) - for various classes at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Mission Bay, Point Loma, and University City High Schools
  4. Miramar College (SV20-0198-99) - for various classes at Mira Mesa, Scripps Ranch, Serra and University City High Schools

FISCAL IMPACT: None. The San Diego Unified School district is eligible for average daily attendance (ADA) revenue for the periods each day that students are enrolled in San Diego Unified courses, and the San Diego Community College District is eligible for apportionment for the period that students are enrolled in City, Mesa, or Miramar College classes.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. As stated in the agreements, each entity agrees to adhere to its respective collective bargaining agreements and no San Diego Community College District or San Diego Unified School District teachers will be displaced by the implementation of these agreements.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Education Code Section 76004 authorizes community college and K-12 districts to enter into College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) partnerships with the goal of developing pathways from high school to community college for career technical or preparation for transfer, improving high school graduation rates, or helping high school students achieve college and career readiness. Through dual enrollment partnerships, school districts and community college districts can create clear pathways of aligned, sequenced coursework that allow students to more easily and successfully transition to for-credit, college-level coursework leading to an associate degree, transfer to the University of California (UC) or California State University (CSU) systems, or to a program leading to a career technical education certificate. The 2019-20 CCAP agreements were presented to the San Diego Community College Board of Trustees for first reading on June 13, 2019, and adopted on July 18, 2019. The 2019-20 CCAP Agreements were presented to the San Diego Unified School District Board of Education for first reading on June 25, 2019, and are hereby submitted for final adoption.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution in the matter of support to amend City of San Diego City Charter Article XVI to include board members of the San Diego Unified School District and strongly urge the City Council Rules Committee to place the City Charter amendment before the voters in March 2020 and ensure the City's Municipal Code includes provisions for removal of San Diego Unified School Board members.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: In November 2016, nearly 88 percent of San Diego voters approved Measure E which amended the process by which City elected officials are removed from office.

 

Prior to Measure E, City elected officials could only be removed through resignations or recall.

 

Article XVI of the City Charter which currently only applies to the Mayor, Council members, and the City Attorney, now includes a process by which the City Council can initiate a special removal election for the following causes:

  • by conviction of a felony or other serious crime;
  • if determined to be physically or mentally incapacitated;
  • and/or for dereliction of official duties.

Election rules for the San Diego Unified School District are defined in Article VI, Section 66 of the San Diego City Charter.

 

Article XI, Section 16(a) of the California State Constitution grants charter cities the authority to determine not only election rules for boards of education, but the process of removal for board members.

 

To ensure consistency within the City Charter, an amendment has been proposed by City Council members Chris Cate and Vivian Moreno to broaden the application of Article XVI to include San Diego Unified School Board members.

  • Providing daily formative assessment opportunities and tools for teachers to monitor and track student progress across the Emerging, Expanding, and Bridging proficiency levels.

Springboard's instructional materials in print and digital are fully aligned to state standards, Advanced Placement (AP) coursework, and the SAT Suite of Assessments. FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable

 

BACKGROUND: As identified in the Course of Study, certain BMB and JROTC courses provide for Physical Education credit. Teachers are deemed qualified by the credential they hold. All BMD/ JROTC teachers hold a Designated Subjects Special Subjects Teaching Credential (DSSS), they must add the Teaching Assignment Option or the STA allow them to grant credit in PE.  

 

Approval of amendments to Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations (CCR) §80037 pertaining to Designated Subjects Special Subjects Teaching Credentials established a Special Teaching Authorization (STA) in Physical Education effective January 1, 2015. Holders of a DSSS credential in BMD and ROTC may elect to add the STA in Physical Education upon completion of specific requirements.  The STA in Physical Education added to a DSSS credential in BMD or ROTC will provide the holder with a distinct physical education authorization limited to the areas of basic military drill and physical fitness training. The STA will not authorize instruction in any physical education courses that are offered outside of BMD and ROTC programs.

 

Holders of DSSS credentials in BMD and ROTC who do not have the STA listed on their documents will still be authorized, by school board action, to teach Physical Education in the context of BMD or ROTC courses that have been approved to carry high school graduation credit for Physical Education per Education Code §44258.3. Education Code §44258.3 allows local school districts to assign any credential holder to teach departmentalized classes, with their consent, in Grades K-12 irrespective of the designations on their teaching credentials. The teacher's competence must be verified according to policies and procedures established by the governing board consistent with the language of the statute; Board Policy 4113 and the accompanying AR: Assignment, along with Board Policy 6142.7 /Administrative Regulation: Physical Education and Activity fulfill this requirement.

 

For teachers who have not yet earned the STA, the Teaching Assignment Option is used. The teacher agrees to teach the BMD/ JROTC course for PE credit. Then, the Assignment Review Panel verifies the teacher's qualifications.  The Assignment Review Panel's criteria includes, but need not be limited to, evidence of the candidate's knowledge of the subject matter to be taught, including demonstrated knowledge of the curriculum framework for the subject to be taught and the specific content of the course of study in the school district for the subject, at the grade level to be taught. The approval is for one year and may be extended for additional time if the teacher and district consent.

BACKGROUND: This license allows APCD to install a temporary, mobile monitoring station (Proposed Project) to collect ambient air quality data in the Barrio Logan/Logan Heights/National City/Port District areas to meet requirements of AB 617, which requires the California Air Resources Board and air districts to develop and implement additional emissions reporting, monitoring, reduction plans, and measures in an effort to reduce air pollution exposure in disadvantaged communities. The equipment will be installed outside the perimeter security fence surrounding Burbank Elementary.

 

This license commences on August 1, 2019, and shall be for five years. In consideration for this license, APCD will pay San Diego Unified $3,000.00.

 

CEQA Environmental Review: The Proposed Project is subject to CEQA, however, it is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines section 15306 - Information Collection (Class 6). Class 6 consists of basic data collection, research, experimental management, and resource evaluation activities which do not result in a serious or major disturbance to an environmental resource. The APCD operates several monitoring stations where air quality is routinely measured for air pollutants to provide the public with information on the status of the air quality. The data is used by health researchers, business interests, environmental groups, and others. The Proposed Project would add another monitoring station to the APCD’s existing network for data collection. Therefore, the application of a Class 6 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2, and summarized below, prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern were designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. Construction activities associated with the Proposed Project would be contained to an existing district campus and the existing site use will not change as a result of the implementation of the Proposed Project. The Proposed Project will not impact environmentally sensitive areas or an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern because implementation of the Proposed Project will be contained to existing developed areas on Burbank Elementary. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Proposed Project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed at Burbank Elementary School that would be implemented concurrently with installation of the proposed mobile air quality monitoring station that would result in a significant cumulative impact. Once operational, the monitoring station would passively monitor ambient air quality and would not result in a cumulative operational impact. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the Proposed Project.
  3. Significant Effect. The Proposed Project includes the construction of a temporary ambient air quality monitoring station. No work is proposed outside the boundaries of the existing school campus that would result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas as a result of the Proposed Project. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project, which includes a fenced off area with the monitoring equipment, that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. The Proposed Project would not result in the demolition of existing structures or the construction of new facilities that would directly or indirectly affect an officially designated scenic highway or scenic resources near a scenic highway. The nearest designated scenic highway is over two miles to the north of the project site and the proposed improvements would not be visible at this distance. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the Department of Toxic Substance Control, no district properties are included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. According to the CEQA Guidelines regarding historical resources, a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource amounts to a significant impact on the environment (Guidelines § 15064.5(b)). Accordingly, a substantial adverse change means demolition, destruction, relocation, or alteration of the resource or its immediate surroundings resulting in the significance of the resource being materially impaired. Because the Proposed Project does not require the demolition of existing historical era buildings or structures, the significance of a potentially historical resource will not be materially impaired, and no substantial adverse changes to the significance of a historical resource or significant impacts to the environment at any district site is anticipated. Additionally, the likelihood of impacting either prehistoric or historic archeological resources is negligible for the current undertaking due because grading and trenching into non-native soils is not proposed. This exception does not apply to the project.
