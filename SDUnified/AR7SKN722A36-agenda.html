1. Vision 2020 Quality Schools in Every Neighborhood - Report on LCAP Goal 4: Positive School Environment, Climate and Culture, including Counseling and Guidance, Youth in Transition, and School Safety
Meeting

Type
Action, Information

RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the board and the public on Positive School Environment, Climate and Culture, with a focus on the comprehensive school counseling and guidance plan, youth in transition (homeless, foster, military and refugees), and school safety (Vision 2020 and Corresponding LCAP Goal 4). 

 

The Superintendent recommends the Board adopt an Options Based Response to an active threat and/or violent incident at a school site or support facility. This recommendation also includes starting a train-the-trainer model of capacity building on the Options Based Response approach, starting with principals and additional leaders.

 

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination: All schools are clean, safe, and well-organized and have a positive climate that supports the academic, emotional and physical needs of students through appropriate supervision, positive behavior intervention and support and customer service.

 

LCAP Goal 4: Schools provide save and well-maintained facilities and positive learning climates and instructional practices that are culturally responsive, challenge bias, and support the academic, social, emotional and physical needs of students.

 

Related LCAP Actions and Services:

  • 4.1.a.7. The district will create a comprehensive K-12 counseling model that increases academic, social emotional and college, career and community readiness that includes utilizing a research-based social emotional curriculum across the district, providing an Integrated Multi-Tiered System of Support (I-MTSS) and increased support with school climate approaches.
  • 4.1.b.1. The district will provide additional intervention counselors for high need high schools with high percentages of English learners, foster/homeless youth and low income students (i.e., Crawford, Hoover, Lincoln, Morse and San Diego High Schools).
  • 4.1.b.2. The district will continue to provide counseling, psychological and guidance services that address student social and emotional needs. 
  • 4.1.b.5. Continued refinements will be made to the elementary counseling program to increase student academic, social emotional and college, career and community readiness for high school.
  • 4.3.a./b.1. The district will continue to provide coordinated and collaborative support services to youth in transition (homeless, foster, probation, refugee, military) and will monitor academic progress, attendance rates and behavioral data.
  • 4.3.a/b.4. The district will continue to provide staff with professional development on laws and topics specific to homeless and foster youth (e.g., AB 490, McKinney Vento Act, graduation requirements).
  • 4.3.a./b.5. Area Superintendents will work with the Children and Youth in Transition (CYT) staff to develop a comprehensive plan for school sites to ensure access and equity for foster, homeless, military, refugee, and students participating in the Home/Hospital program.  The plan will include development of a site-level resource binder to assist with enrollment, academic, mentor and partnership resources for supporting students in transition.  A pilot process will be used to build capacity and principal leadership focused on students in transition, including professional development for school staffs in developing site-specific strategies to support youth in transition.
  • 4.8.1. The district will ensure that school safety and security continues to be a priority that is monitored by school staff with the support of School Police Services, including the annual update of School Safety Plans and Emergency Response Plans and inspection of Emergency Response Boxes; student supervision; and School Safety Committees.  Support will include summer training for all principals on safe school planning, along with tutorial videos for district staff posted on the School Police website.

BACKGROUND:  At regular, public board meetings Superintendent Marten provides reports that include the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting LCAP actions, services and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's Vision 2020/LCAP report, cluster and advisory groups, employee groups, and members of the public have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report and provide input and feedback on current LCAP implementation.  The complete LCAP document is available on the district website at https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

The successful leadership development of César E. Chavez Service Clubs, organized by San Diego Unified and Urban Corps of San Diego, are now being emulated in California’s most populated cities throughout the state.

 

San Diego Unified dedicated César E. Chavez Elementary School, in September 1997, to provide an education program for more than 700 students.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the District's Guiding Principles: Financial Planning for Student Success.

 

Our Principles

 

Ensure each financial decision is rooted in district supports, structures, and capacity development that accomplish Vision 2020, Local Control Accountability Plan, and future plans to improve outcomes for students.

 

Develop, maintain and communicate long-term strategies that ensure strong fiscal health for the District.  

 

Use a disciplined approach to make financial decisions and hold ourselves accountable for each transaction as it relates to student outcomes and sound economic strategies aligned with Vision 2020 (i.e. decisions made with consideration to projected revenue and long-term obligations).

 

Regularly and openly have conversations with the public about fiscal projections, activities, accomplishments and engage key groups and stakeholders in an input process. (i.e.board meetings, LCAP input process, yearly school site budgeting process, yearly review of the organization, etc.).

 

(New) Utilize creative and entrepreneurial approaches to seek funds through legislative advocacy at the local, state, and federal levels along with grants, partnerships and/or other funding mechanisms to support each and every student.  

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable

 

BACKGROUND:

The Board of Education completed a first reading of the Guiding Principles on September 12, 2017 at the open meeting. The Board requested the Superintendent to gather additional feedback prior to formal adoption. The Superintendent and staff gathered input from different stakeholders including labor partners, parent groups, board members, district committees, along with other groups and individuals. The Guiding Principles have been revised based on the feedback received and the updates include an additional emphasis on entrepreneurial approaches to increase revenues.

 

Prior Background Provided for the First Reading:

 

With careful attention to student outcomes and equity, the District has set forth new fiscal and operational models to ensure the organization is financially healthy and situated to serve current and future generations of students while remaining responsible fiscal stewards of taxpayer funds. The Board of Education and District staff are continuously evaluating current practices and forecasting based on all data available while paying special attention to variables expected to create fiscal impact. These forecasts and projections indicate significant opportunities for student achievement coupled with laws and policies out of the District’s control that will impact the District’s future operations. The Board of Education has accounted for these variables, risks, and opportunities and is taking strong action.

Therefore, the Board of Education is establishing the following guiding principles to achieve Vision 2020 through the implementation of LCAP goals, create meaningful learning experiences for all students and maintain long-term fiscal stability. The guiding principles serve as the essential ideas and ideological anchors for all financial processes and decisions system-wide. The principles are listed below along with practical examples of their application to specific processes intended to improve student outcomes and preserve current and future fiscal resources.

 

Our Principles:

We will:

 

1. Ensure each financial decision is rooted in district supports, structures, and capacity development that accomplish Vision 2020, Local Control Accountability Plan, and future plans to improve outcomes for students.

 

2. Develop, maintain and communicate long-term strategies that ensure strong fiscal health for the District.

 

3. Use a disciplined approach to make financial decisions and hold ourselves accountable for each transaction as it relates to student outcomes and sound economic strategies aligned with Vision 2020 (i.e. decisions made with consideration to projected revenue and long-term obligations).

 

4. Regularly and openly have conversations with the public about fiscal projections, activities, accomplishments and engage key groups and stakeholders in an input process. (i.e.board meetings, LCAP input process, yearly school site budgeting process, yearly review of the organization, etc.).

 

5. (New) Utilize creative and entrepreneurial approaches to seek funds through legislative advocacy at the local, state, and federal levels along with grants, partnerships and/or other funding mechanisms to support each and every student.

 

We are committed to implement processes and controls that ensure long-term fiscal stability and focus on achievement for all learners (example applications of the principles below)

  • Controls and Audits
    • Vigilant adherence to internal controls such as a fraud hotline and account auditing
    • Consistent use of professional experts and external auditors to ensure compliance and maintenance of proper controls
  • Spending Freeze:
    • Evaluation of discretionary expenses at all levels to confirm support for student success and the responsible stewardship of taxpayer dollars
    • Reduction or suspension of all non-essential travel and purchases
  • Position Management / Position Review Committee
    • Prioritize positions that fill critical needs for both school sites and the required business of the district
    • Ensure all positions are properly funded
    • Consistent evaluation of all positions to ensure they are optimized and providing meaningful support to the District’s mission

These processes serve as initial examples, and the Board of Education’s adoption will result in continual addition, refinement and application of processes to ensure the District can continue to unlock the genius for current and future generations of students.

Alternatively, if there is an existing contract between a public corporation or agency and a vendor for the lease or purchase of the personal property, a school district may authorize the lease or purchase of personal property directly from the vendor by contract, lease, requisition, or purchase order and make payment to the vendor under the same terms that are available to the public corporation or agency under the contract. Approval of this item reflects the Board's determination that it is in the best interest of the district to use the contract awarded by LAUSD for the purchase of the identified materials.
