1. Title I Eligibility Ranking for San Diego Unified School District, 2021-22 (First Reading) BUDGETED: YES, TITLE I, PART A ENTITLEMENT
Meeting
Feb 2, 2021 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Information, First Reading

RECOMMENDATION: This is an informational Report of the Title I Eligibility Ranking for the district. The report includes the preliminary 2021-2022 Title I Eligibility Ranking and school participation. We are bringing this forward at this time to initiate public feedback on the formula used for distributing funds; however, the report is subject to significant changes due to the extended deadline for CALPADS reporting received on January 28, 2021, and updated federal information received on January 29, 2021.  

 

The district’s recommendation is to provide an allocation to schools that closely matches the current year allocations.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Staff recommends schools remain funded at a minimum of $15,500,000, total allocation for schools. With the identification of additional eligible students, the total allocations will increase.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: $15,500,000.00.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The District Advisory Council (DAC) on Compensatory Education discussed the Title I Ranking process on November 18, 2020, and December 16, 2020.  District staff will review the revised Title I Ranking Report with the DAC Executive Board on February 3, 2021.

 

BACKGROUND: The Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) of 1965, was reauthorized as of December 10, 2015, as the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA). Title I, which is one of several programs under ESSA, provides funding to schools to be used to help disadvantaged children meet challenging academic standards. The law intends that the funds go to the districts and schools that have the highest concentrations of poverty. Title I requires funding to be allocated to a district based on the number of students who qualify for Title I funding who reside within each district’s boundary. The federal census is used to identify these children, ages 5 through 17, who are living in poverty who are included in the calculation of Title I entitlement.

 

This Preliminary Title I Ranking report allocates Title I funding to all district schools using the 2020-21 National School Lunch Program calculation with a percentage of qualifying students of 40 percent or higher. This report was prepared using the current (status quo) formula that was used to allocate the 2020-21 Title I funds to eligible schools.

 

Due to the challenges school districts are experiencing in collecting data from eligible students, the Federal Education Department and the California Department of Education recently issued guidance that provided some flexibility in the data that may be used in the school district’s Title I Ranking formula. Additionally, the State, on January 28, 2021, extended the CALPADS deadline from January 29th to February 4th. Staff will take advantage of these flexibilities to arrive at an allocation that would provide funds to schools that more closely match the current year allocations.  

 

A revised Title I Ranking Report will be submitted to the BOE for approval on February 9, 2021.

These new laws, particularly AB 1505, represent some of the most significant and comprehensive changes to charter school legislation since the original Charter Schools Act of 1992.

 

In response to these legislative changes, on July 28, 2020, the San Diego Unified School District Board of Education adopted a resolution requesting that the Superintendent bring forward an AB 1505 Implementation Plan within 90 days.  The draft of the AB 1505 Implementation Plan was reviewed by the San Diego Unified Board of Education at its November 3, 2020, meeting.  The following documents are attached for review and/or approval:

 

  • A “redline” version of the Implementation Plan which shows changes made from the original draft

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the proposed process for recruitment and selection of a permanent Superintendent upon the resignation of Superintendent Cindy Marten.

 

The proposed plan involves an open and transparent, inclusive search process.

 

An Advisory Committee will be formed to guide the process including, but not limited to, representatives from:

 

  • Administrators Association of San Diego (AASD)

  • California Charter Schools Association (CCSA)

  • California School Employees Association (CSEA)

  • Community Advisory Committee (CAC) for Special Education

  • District Advisory Council (DAC) on Compensatory Education

  • District English Learner Advisory Committee (DELAC)

  • Parent Teacher Association (PTA)

  • San Diego Community College District (SDCCD)

  • San Diego County Board of Supervisors

  • San Diego Educators Association (SDEA)

  • San Diego Mayor’s Office

  • San Diego Schools Police Officers Association (POA)

  • San Diego State University (SDSU)

  • San Diego Unified Subdistricts A, B, C, D, and E

  • San Diego Workforce Partnership

  • Student Advisory Board/Associated Student Body (ASB)

  • University of California San Diego (UCSD)

  • Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA) Foundation

The Advisory Committee members shall reflect the diversity of our students and the San Diego Community.  

 

The selection process shall be supported by an organization tasked with leading town halls, listening sessions and community engagement meetings. The Advisory Committee will draft a Request for Proposals (“RFP”) which will be used to solicit proposals from interested organizations by the end of March 2021. These proposals will be reviewed by the Advisory Committee that will make the recommendation to the Board for the organization that will lead town halls, listening sessions and community engagement meetings. Town Halls will be held in late Spring and early Summer. This will lead to the creation of a vision for the future of the district, and the identification of the qualities of a Superintendent to lead that vision.

 

The Advisory Committee will utilize the information gathered during the town halls, listening sessions and community engagement meetings to develop the job posting.

 

The position will then be posted by the end of July 2021.  The recruitment will open to qualified candidates from around the globe.

 

The Advisory Committee will screen the candidate application packages and will recommend up to ten (10) candidates to be interviewed by the Board in September 2021.

 

The Board will select up to three (3) finalists by October 2021.

 

Community forums will be held in early November 2021 for the public to meet the finalists and provide feedback.

 

A permanent Superintendent will be selected by the beginning of December 2021 to allow for a month-long transition into the position.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Any costs associated with the Superintendent search process will be brought to the Board for approval as needed.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: District Advisory Committees and community stakeholder groups will be an important part of the search process.

 

BACKGROUND: Superintendent Cindy Marten is being considered for a cabinet level post in the Biden administration. Upon the resignation of Ms. Marten, Dr. Lamont Jackson has been appointed as Interim Superintendent through December 31, 2021. The district will enter into a search process for a permanent Superintendent in Spring 2021. Public testimony on this item will be heard during Open Session only.
