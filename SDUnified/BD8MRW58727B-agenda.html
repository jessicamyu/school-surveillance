3. Resolution in Support of SB 874: Community College Districts: Statewide Baccalaureate Degree Pilot Program (Barrera/Whitehurst-Payne)
Meeting
Apr 28, 2020 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Fiscal Impact
No

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in Support of SB 874 Community College Districts: Statewide Baccalaureate Degree Pilot Program, which would make permanent and expand the state's baccalaureate pilot program, thereby supporting continued student recruitment and participation.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District Board of Education recognizes that workforce education is one of the most important missions of K-12 and community college districts. A growing number of workforce fields and employers now require a bachelor's degree in their job qualifications. California's public universities either do not offer or lack the capacity for providing adequate baccalaureate instruction in these high-demand workforce fields. Students who need programs that are not provided by California's public universities in these workforce fields are compelled to leave the state in order to obtain their degrees or to enroll in high-cost nonprofit institutions, or inexpensive for-profit institutions that have questionable success rates and result in significant student debt. Community colleges are able to provide high-quality and accessible, local programs at the baccalaureate level, at the affordable cost of $10,560 for all four years. The San Diego Unified School District supported SB 850 in 2015, which created a pilot project in California for 15 colleges, including San Diego Mesa College, to offer a bachelor's degree in a specific career/technical field, with all programs already operating successfully and serving a highly diverse student population, thereby delivering on the promise of creating greater access to four-year degrees for the state's residents. The San Diego Unified School District also supported SB 1406 in 2018, which extended the "sunset" date for completion of the degree to 2026, meaning that students cannot enroll after 2022 and be assured time to complete their studies. SB 874 has been introduced by Senator Jerry Hill, with Senators Ben Hueso and Scott Wilk as co-authors, as successor legislation to SB 850 and SB 1406 that proposes to make the existing baccalaureate pilot programs permanent, and expand the opportunity of offering baccalaureate programs to all community colleges. The Board of Education encourages the Superintendent to provide this Resolution to members of the legislature, statewide, community and business organizations and leaders, and others who can assist in implementing this important expansion of opportunity for students in California higher education.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing May 2020 as National Military Appreciation Month. San Diego Unified recognizes and celebrates the essential role and tremendous sacrifice of military personnel and families to our community and encourages students, parents, and staff to recognize and honor military families throughout San Diego.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Nearly eight percent of the US active duty military population, including 17 percent of all Navy personnel and 30 percent of all Marine personnel are stationed on San Diego's seven major military bases. Military children make up a very special part of our nation's population and over eight and a half percent of students enrolled in the San Diego Unified School District are connected to military families. These brave sons and daughters stand in steadfast support of their military parents through moves and deployments. April is designated as Month of the Military Child, and April 22 is designated as Day for the Military Child to recognize military children for their sacrifice on the homefront. May is designated as National Military Appreciation Month to encourage Americans to honor current and former members of the US Armed Forces, including those who have died in the pursuit of freedom. Six national observances are held during National Military Appreciation Month including Loyalty Day, to affirm loyalty to the United States and to reflect on the proud heritage of our American Freedom (May 1), Public Service Recognition Week, to honor men and women who serve our nation as federal, state, county and local government employees (May 3-9), V-E Day, marking the end of World War II in Europe (May 8), Military Spouse Appreciation Day, to recognize the important role of our military families in keeping our Armed Forces strong and our country safe (May 8), Armed Forces Day, to honor everyone serving in the US Military branches (May 16), and Memorial Day, in remembrance of men and women who died while in military service (May 25).

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Logan K-8 is located at 2875 Ocean View Boulevard, San Diego, and Memorial Preparatory for Scholars and Athletes is located at 2850 Logan Avenue, San Diego. These neighboring schools are currently in the midst of a major rebuild, merging the two campuses into a comprehensive educational facility in the heart of Logan Heights. The facility will feature individual areas for infants, toddlers and pre-K; TK-6; 7-8; and 9-12, bringing the first public high school to Logan Heights. Additional features include athletic and recreation fields, hardcourts, and a wellness center that will support the community with health education, legal representation access, dental and counseling services. Led by a new director and supported by two principals, the new campus will host a first of its kind Prenatal to Career Montessori-based program.

 

The project was the culmination of close collaboration among the district, architects, and stakeholders of Logan K-8 and Memorial Preparatory, bringing to fruition a vision that centers on supporting a child from birth to career, and one that is based on developmental stages according to scientific brain architecture and Montessori methods.

 

During the planning and stakeholder input process, stakeholders expressed interest in a new facility name that pays homage to the rich history of both schools, while easily identifying the new comprehensive campus as a center of education in the Logan Heights community. Reflecting the merger of the two schools, Logan Memorial Educational Campus has been proposed.  

 

Pursuant to Administrative Procedure 3300, Naming of Schools and School Facilities, this is a request to rename Logan K-8 and Memorial Preparatory for Scholars and athletes to Logan Memorial Educational Campus. The school community has engaged in the required outreach efforts and has provided supporting documentation. The School Names Committee has reviewed the renaming request and supporting documents submitted by the school community. With six votes supporting and one abstention, the committee has voted to support the name change request. There were no votes in opposition.

RECOMMENDATION:  [NOTE: The district will be able to offer continued employment to every employee impacted by this board action; although the position eliminations will initially impact employees, they have the opportunity to remain employed, however, it may be in another capacity.]  

 

In order to provide all classified employees reemployment in current classifications, former classifications, tentatively approved classifications or vacant positions and ensure a balanced budget, approve the Resolutions to Eliminate Classified Services for the 2020-21 school year pursuant to Education Codes 45114, 45115, 45117, 45298 and 45308. 

 

Resolutions and exhibits include:

  • Administrators Association of San Diego (AASD) Classified Supervisors

  • Confidential Employees

  • Operational Support Services (OSS)

  • Office Technical and Business Services (OTBS)

  • Paraeducators (PARA)

 

FISCAL IMPACT: All actions taken by the board will be to prepare a balanced budget for 2020-21.  On March 10, 2020, the Board of Education approved the Fiscal Year 2019-20 Second Interim Financial Report and Resolution to certify that the district’s fiscal status as qualified, which identified the amount of budget solutions needed for fiscal years 2020-21 and 2021-22. The impacts and effects of these position eliminations may be subject to negotiations with the district’s bargaining units. The district's Fiscal Year 2019-20 Unrestricted General Fund, Second Interim Report depicted a projected positive ending fund balance of $71.8 million, with a net decrease in fund balance of $17.8 million. This financial report was developed using various assumptions and projections as highlighted in the exhibits. The 2019-20 total General Fund Restricted and Unrestricted ending balance is $101.3 million.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: N/A

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Approval and full implementation of the personnel resolutions will result in:

  • The reassignment and transfer of Child Development Permit holders pursuant to the current collective bargaining agreement; and

  • A reduction in currently performed classified services and a corresponding reduction in permanent and probationary employees (as appropriate) as well as vacant positions; and

  • The opportunity to continue to engage the respective bargaining units in order to offer employees reemployment opportunities in their current classifications, former classifications, tentatively approved classifications or vacant positions.

The bargaining units affected by this action have been apprised of the contemplated action in order to afford the opportunity to exercise their rights under the Educational Employment Relations Act, the applicable collective bargaining agreements and/or Employment Regulations for the Classified Service.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: While the State of California continues to inadequately fund public education, school districts across the state must continue to produce balanced budgets despite increased costs; the COVID-19 pandemic has only exacerbated the district’s ability to do so. The March 10, 2020, Second Interim Report detailed the increased costs generated by state and federal mandates and the lack of adequate revenue. On an annual basis, schools and departments within San Diego Unified School District must make difficult decisions to be in compliance with these mandates. Reflecting the commitment to put students’ needs first, the staffing changes outlined in this document are based on the district’s continued commitment to improve instruction to our earliest learners as well as the site-based student-focused budgeting completed by all schools within the district. This means student need and student equity are the primary elements considered when making these decisions.

 

Despite these challenges and pending continued negotiations and ratification by those units, the district will be able to offer continued employment to every employee impacted by this board action; although the position eliminations will initially impact employees, they have the opportunity to remain employed, however, it may be in another capacity. 

 

Since 2017-18, the district has utilized a targeted approach to future budget reductions, as opposed to across the board cuts. For that reason, the position eliminations outlined in this item are focused and limited. It is important to note, the district normally would have taken this action in March, alongside the certificated position eliminations. However, the district delayed this action in order to work with the respective collective bargaining units in order to ensure all employees would have an opportunity to be reemployed in their current classifications, former classifications, tentatively approved classifications or vacant positions. 

 

The approval of the board action to eliminate positions enables the district to ensure a balanced budget while providing employees with seniority based reemployment rights as well as any additional rights negotiated through the impacts and effects bargaining process. 

 

Of the 458 positions listed in the attached resolutions and exhibits, 130 are vacant; the remaining 328 employees will be able to remain in their current classification, return to a former classification, or accept a tentatively approved new classification or vacant position.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Oral health is a fundamental component of overall health and wellness. Children with oral health issues are more likely to experience dental pain that leads to school absence and poor academic performance.

 

San Ysidro Health will provide comprehensive school-based mobile/portable dental services to schools in underserved areas of the district and will perform mandated kindergarten oral health assessments, and dental screenings, fluoride varnish, and oral health education. Services will be provided at no cost to the students.

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not-to-exceed amount: $14,000.00. Budget String: 5451-63882-00-5853-1000-3800-01000-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  Larry B. Ainsworth is an educational consultant specializing in timeless educational practices. He provided a two-day training workshop for Career and Technical Education (CTE) and Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA) instructors focused on:

  • Clarifying learning targets, success criteria and learning progressions
  • Developing common formative assessments
  • Designing authentic performance tasks
  • Creating rigorous curricular units of study in all content areas

The workshop provided instructors with the necessary tools to prepare, plan and deliver competency-based instruction to students. Workshop participants unwrapped and analyzed their content area standards, represented the standards in a graphic organizer and matched concepts and skills to corresponding levels of rigor. The workshop provided a foundation for directly aligning classroom instruction and assessments with the unwrapped concepts, skills, and rigor of standards to produce evidence of student learning.  The workshop strengthened educators' ability to better motivate and engage their students in co-creating specific learning targets, thus increasing students' ownership and engagement in the learning process.

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The proposed license agreement to provide health care services at a district site (Proposed Project) is a subject to CEQA, however, the project is exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Section 15301 – Existing Facilities (Class 1). Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public or private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of existing or former use. The key consideration is whether the project involves negligible or no expansion of an existing use. The Proposed Project involves the execution of a license agreement between Centro De Salud De La Comunidad De San Ysidro, Inc., d/b/a San Ysidro Health Center and the District to continue to operate an existing community health clinic on a portion of the District owned property at Lincoln High School. No construction or other improvements would be required as part of the license agreement, and no expansion of use is proposed beyond existing operations at the health center. Therefore, the application of a Class 1 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

 

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines Section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines §15300.2 prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location: Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact: All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect: A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4.
