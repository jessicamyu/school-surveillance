A.03. Board Discussion of School District Leadership Models
Meeting
Jan 5, 2010 - - Special Meeting, 2:00 P.M.
Type
Information
RECOMMENDATION: Board members will discuss and review information on alternative school district leadership models.

FISCAL IMPACT: None

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None at this time. This meeting is for information and discussion purposes and will begin the dialog on this subject. It is unknown at this time what the potential impact on staffing might be in the future depending upon the ultimate decision of the board.

BACKGROUND: As the board begins their discussion and planning for next steps in the process and on the future leadership for the school district, the board will review and discuss alternative models of school district leadership currently being used in other districts. In addition, the board is interested in discussing and reviewing other models which may not currently be in existence elsewhere in the country and would like to hear from the community on their views and suggestions.

Subject
A.04. Board Discussion of Coherent Governance and Schedule
Meeting
Jan 5, 2010 - - Special Meeting, 2:00 P.M.
Type
Action, Information
RECOMMENDATION: Board members will discuss and determine next steps relative to the engagement of governance consultant, The Aspen Group, and the board and/or superintendent assessments.

FISCAL IMPACT: Unknown at this time, depending upon the decision of the board, there could be a financial impact.

PRIOR YEARS EXPENSE HISTORY: April 2008 through February 28, 2010 - $325,000 ($69,000 April 2008-July 2008; $160,000--August 11, 2008-August 31, 2009; $96,000--September 1, 2009-February 28, 2010)

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None

BACKGROUND: On April 22, 2008, the board approved an agreement with Aspen Group International, LLC (Aspen) to initiate a Coherent Governance model and training program. Aspen conducted regular training sessions for the board and executive staff and Aspen has provided assistance by reviewing and critiquing Operational Expectations monitoring reports to assure consistent and effective implementation. On September 8, 2009, the board approved an agreement with Aspen through February 28, 2010, to complete the first yearly cycle of operational expectation monitoring reports and prepare for the two-step process to be implemented in 2010. Step 1 will be to provide proposed interpretations and indicators for board review and approval and Step 2 will be the presentation of the monitoring reports. In addition, in 2010 the board will begin the review and monitoring of the Goals for Student Achievement, GSA-2 Academics. Access Board Goverance Policies at http://www.sandi.net/21832072015468430/site/default.asp.
