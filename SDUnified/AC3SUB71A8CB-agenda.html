When considering a petition for renewal, pursuant to Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations section 11966.4(b)(1), the district governing board shall consider the past performance of the school’s academics, finances, and operations in evaluating the likelihood of future success, along with future plans for improvement if any.

 

Staff has visited San Diego Cooperative annually. The annual site visit includes document review, classroom observations, and interviews of charter board members, teachers, administrators, financial managers, parents, and/or students. During the annual site visit, as well as throughout the year, the areas of academics, finances, and operations are monitored as part of the district’s oversight duties.

 

ACADEMICS

 

As the charter granting authority, pursuant to Education Code section 47607(a)(3)(A), the district shall consider increases in student academic achievement for all groups of students served by the charter school as the most important factor in determining whether to grant a charter renewal.

 

A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school. 
  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years. 
  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.  
  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.  
  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(h).

Assembly Bill (AB) 484 amended Education Code sections 52052(e)(2)(F) and 52052(e)(4) to allow schools that do not have an API calculated in 2013–14 and 2014–15 to use one of the following criteria to meet legislative and/or programmatic requirements:

  1. The most recent API calculation;
  2. An average of the three most recent annual API calculations; or
  3. Alternative measures that show increases in pupil academic achievement for all groups of pupils schoolwide and among significant groups. 

The decision to use one of these criteria may be made on a program by program basis and is a local decision.

FINANCES

 

Financial plan documents reviewed by the San Diego Cooperative governing board on February 8, 2017, included fundraising and foundation revenue for the two charter schools operated by the organization for the amounts summarized above in Table 1 for $200,000 and $250,000 in 2017-18 and 2018-19, respectively.  However, there is no prior history of the organization receiving these “soft” revenues. San Diego Cooperative school is projecting operational deficits in 2016-17, 2017-18, and 2018-19.

 

Staff concludes that San Diego Cooperative has ending fund balances that meet the district’s minimum reserve requirement of the greater of 3 percent of expenditures or $50,000 in each year. However, the district notes that deficit spending is an area of concern for this charter school.

 

Over the past five-year term, San Diego Cooperative has submitted all required financial reports and payments to the district in a timely manner.

 

CONCLUSION

RECOMMENDATION: Deny the renewal of the charter of Arroyo Paseo Charter High School (Arroyo Paseo), with the proposed term beginning July 1, 2017, and ending June 30, 2022, and adopt the following written factual findings that are specific to the renewal charter petition (Petition):

  1. Arroyo Paseo presents an unsound educational program for the pupils to be enrolled in the charter school as set forth by the following specific facts:
  1. Lowest Math Assessment Result: At 4 percent, Arroyo Paseo reported the lowest total percentage of scores that met or exceeded the standard based on the Mathematics Smarter Balanced Assessment (SBAC) results of all district-operated high schools and district-authorized charter high schools in 2016. The prior year score was also just 4 percent.
     
  2. Lowest English Language Arts Assessment Result: At 21 percent, Arroyo Paseo reported the lowest total percentage of scores that met or exceeded the standard based on the English Language Arts/Literacy SBAC results of all district-operated high schools and district-authorized charter high schools in 2016.
     
  3. Lowest Attendance Rate: At 82 percent, Arroyo Paseo’s 2015-16 attendance rate was far below that of the public schools the majority of its students would have otherwise been required to attend, specifically Hoover, Crawford, and Lincoln high schools.
     
  4. Lowest Graduation Rate: At 53.7 percent, Arroyo Paseo’s 2014-15 graduation rate was far below that of the public schools the majority of its students would have otherwise been required to attend, specifically Hoover, Crawford, and Lincoln high schools.
  1. Highest Dropout Rate: At 12.2 percent Arroyo Paseo’s 2014-15 dropout rate was significantly higher than the public schools the majority of its students would have otherwise been required to attend, specifically Hoover, Crawford, and Lincoln high schools.
  1. Highest Suspension Rate: At 10.4 percent Arroyo Paseo’s 2015-16 suspension rate was significantly higher than the public schools the majority of its students would have otherwise been required to attend, specifically Hoover, Crawford, and Lincoln high schools.
  1. Arroyo Paseo is demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program described in the Petition as set forth by the following specific facts:
  1. Outdated Plan to Use CAHSEE and CST Assessments: In its Petition, Arroyo Paseo identifies outdated assessments, specifically the CAHSEE and CSTs for biology and chemistry, as the methods by which pupil outcomes will be measured as part of its future plans. Due to changes in academic standards by the State, these assessments have not been administered in California public schools for several years. The Petition demonstrates Arroyo Paseo’s lack of understanding and/or awareness of California’s current academic standards and assessments.
  1. Parent Involvement Goal Eliminated from LCAP: Arroyo Paseo’s 2014-15 LCAP goal to increase the number of parents who are involved in policy, educational, and long-term planning decisions and who attend input sessions was deleted from the 2015-16 LCAP with no update or replacement.
  1. Historical Enrollment Decline Does Not Support Future Projected Enrollment Increase: Arroyo Paseo’s enrollment declined from 2013-14 to 2016-17, from 146 to 111 students. Despite a 24 percent enrollment drop, the Petition shows Arroyo Paseo has based its financial plan on a 22 percent projected growth rate. Arroyo Paseo’s projected enrollment of 135 students in 2017-18 is unrealistic.
  1. Historical Attendance Rates Do Not Support Future Projected Average Daily Attendance: The Petition shows Arroyo Paseo has based its financial plan on a 90 percent average daily attendance to enrollment ratio. This rate is unrealistic based on Arroyo Paseo’s low historical attendance rates of 81 percent for 2014-15 and 82 percent for 2015-16.
  1. Frequent Untimely Payments: Arroyo Paseo’s payments to the district for fees and services were frequently late with approximately nine untimely payments over a two-year period.

Denying the renewal of the charter would allow Arroyo Paseo to continue operating through June 30, 2017, the end date of the current charter term.

When considering a petition for renewal, pursuant to Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations section 11966.4(b)(1), the district governing board shall consider the past performance of the school’s academics, finances, and operations in evaluating the likelihood of future success, along with future plans for improvement, if any.

 

As the charter granting authority, pursuant to Education Code section 47607(a)(3)(A), the district shall consider increases in student academic achievement for all groups of students served by the charter school as the most important factor in determining whether to grant a charter renewal. A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school. 
  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years. 
  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.  
  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.
  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(h).

Assembly Bill (AB) 484 amended Education Code sections 52052(e)(2)(F) and 52052(e)(4) to allow schools that do not have an API calculated in 2013–14 and 2014–15 to use one of the following criteria to meet legislative and/or programmatic requirements:

  1. The most recent API calculation;
  2. An average of the three most recent annual API calculations; or
  3. Alternative measures that show increases in pupil academic achievement for all groups of pupils schoolwide and among significant groups. 

The decision to use one of these criteria may be made on a program by program basis and is a local decision.

FINANCES

 

Overall, the budget and multi-year projections appear to be built on unrealistic and unsupported projections, as well as incomplete information. Based on Arroyo Paseo’s historical data from the official fall enrollment and attendance reports, the ratio of average daily attendance to enrollment appears to be overstated for 2017-18 through 2019-20.  Arroyo Paseo has projected an ADA to enrollment ratio of 90 percent for each school year. RENEWAL VERSUS REVISION

 

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.

 

Within a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, special education status, expansion of grade levels, or other significant revision.
