F.05. Recommendation that San Diego Unified School District Join the "No Child Left Inside" Coalition (Barrera/Jackson)
Meeting
Mar 10, 2009 - 03/10/2009
Type
Action, Information
Board members Richard Barrera and Shelia Jackson recommend the San Diego Unified School District join the "No Child Left Inside Coalition" (NCLI) a broad-based organization made up of more than 1,000 member groups from across the United States. NCLI membership includes environmental, educational, business, public health, outdoor recreation and conservation groups. The NCLI Coalition speaks for a diverse group of Americans who believe young people should receive a strong education about their natural world.

The board directs the Superintendent to report to the board on existing outdoor educational programs currently provided to students in the district and make recommendations for expanding and improving those programs. For additional information regarding the "No Child Left Inside" Coalition see, http://www.cbf.org/site/PageServer?pagename=act_sub_actioncenter_federal_NCLB.

The superintendent recommends the Board approve the Resolution in the Matter of Intent to Balance the District 2008-2009 Budget and Certify Fiscal Solvency for the Current and Subsequent Two Fiscal Years.

California Education Code Section 42130 states that the Board of Education must approve the district's Second Interim Financial Report on the district's budgetary status as of January 31, on or before March 17. The board is also required to certify to the state that the district's projected financial condition at this period is one of the following:

a) Positive Certification - will be able to meet its financial obligations

b) Qualified Certification - may not meet its financial obligations

c) Negative Certification - will be unable to meet its financial obligations

Firms recommended for contract award for a three-year period with option to extend for two additional years:
  • Delawie Wilkes Rodrigues Barker: $3M
  • Dominy + Associates Architects, LLP: $1.5M
  • IBI Group: $1.5M
  • JCJ Architecture, Inc.: $2M

  • SGPA Planning & Architecture San Diego: $1M
  • Westberg + White, Inc.: $2M

On February 24, 2009, the Board of Education approved ten contract awards to architect firms in support of Proposition S and the Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC) department. Both the selection process and the background of these firms were detailed in the attached Memorandum to the Board dated February 10, 2009. Initially, 22 firms were proposed for contract award; however, since negotiations had not been completed prior to the February 24 board date, ten firms were recommended for Board award on February 24, 2009, and the remaining 12 planned for Board approval on March 10, 2009. Two firms have decided not to contract with the district; therefore, ten firms are currently recommended for approval.

As noted in the attached memorandum, it is necessary to augment the Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC) staff due to insufficient in-house resources. Most of the funds have been expended on the design contracts, and the agreements will expire in May 2009. It is, therefore, imperative for the district to enter into new agreements as soon as possible.

FPC has selected a wide variety of architectural firms of different size and capacities for Prop S projects with a preference for local firms to perform the work. Of the ten firms recommended for current award, five of the firms are new to the district, and three have recent FPC work history. They range in size from small (3) and medium (5) to large (2). All firms have local offices with in-house staff.

The agreements provide for an initial three-year term with an option to extend for two additional years. Primary funding for these contracts is available in the Proposition S fund.

California Government Code section 4525, et seq., grants the district the authority to contract with and employ persons for the furnishing of architectural and landscape architectural services if such persons are specially trained, experienced, and competent to perform the special services requested.

Employment of the selected firms will provide the best value overall for the district because: (1) the firms were selected based on a fair selection process; (2) the requirements mandated for architectural selection have been met; (3) available resources on a multitude of projects is assured in a timely manner; (4) a lengthy selection and approval process on each project is not required; (5) cost controls and tracking continuity is ensured; and (5) the necessity of in-house layoffs of a significant work force is avoided.

Approve award of contracts C89-044 to Bureau Veritas North America Inc., and C89-045 to Ninyo & Moore to provide construction testing and inspection services at various district sites on an as-needed basis. Contract duration shall be one year, with option to renew for an additional two, one-year terms (for a total of three years) with the initial term completion of March 14, 2010. The district shall have the sole option of renewing or re-bidding this contract based on market conditions at time of renewal.

The district has a requirement to provide specialized testing of materials to be installed in school facilities in keeping with the construction of safe schools to be occupied by children and teaching staff. This is mandated by Title 24 of the California Code of Regulations, also known as the California Buildings Standard Code.  These tests are highly technical and measure the strength and durability of specified materials to be used in construction projects. The tests, by law, must be conducted in state certified laboratories by qualified personnel. Representative materials are concrete, structural steel and masonry. In addition, this code requires field testing and inspection of on site grading, excavation, and placement of soils by qualified personnel under the general supervision of a structural engineer.

These services are provided by state licensed testing and inspection laboratories who have the equipment, laboratories and trained and certified staff to perform the required tests and inspections.

This report presents the results of the final selection of five geotechnical/soils inspection, construction materials testing and inspection consultants, to provide construction testing and inspection consulting services on an as-needed basis for the Proposition S, Physical Plant Operations, and Facilities Planning and Construction Programs. These particular services are required during the construction phase to insure that construction materials and inspection practices are being provided and performed according to contract specifications and state codes as described above.

On December 16, 2008, Request for Qualifications (RFQ) were issued for as-needed professional services for geotechnical/soils inspection, construction materials testing and inspection. The deadline for receipt of Statement of Qualifications (SOQ) was January 9, 2009.

SOQ’s were submitted by 16 firms for construction testing and inspection services. Each SOQ was screened and ranked, based on criteria outlined in the RFQ, by a five-person panel comprised of representation from Facilities Planning and Construction. The five, top ranked firms were previously qualified; therefore, their selection did not require an interview process. The district intends to contract with these five firms beginning with the two firms listed here and with the other firms later as the need for their services develops over the next few months.

Approve award of contracts C89-044 to Bureau Veritas North America, Inc. and C89-045 to Ninyo & Moore, to provide construction testing and inspection services at various district sites on an as-needed basis. The total not-to-exceed amount for each contract shall be $1,000,000 for the one-year term period.
