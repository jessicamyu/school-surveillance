BACKGROUND: The public hearing to consider the level of support for Charter School of San Diego (“CSSD”) occurred on September 12, 2017. Pursuant to Education Code 47604, the school is operated by the nonprofit public benefit corporation Student Success Programs, which also operates Audeo Charter School and Laurel Preparatory Academy. CSSD is seeking renewal of its charter for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2018, and ending June 30, 2023.

 

CSSD currently operates a non-classroom based “independent study” program for students in Grades 7-12. As part of this renewal process, CSSD is seeking to expand its grade levels to 6-12 beginning July 1, 2018, and the charter for Laurel Preparatory Academy (“Laurel Prep”) will expire on June 30, 2018.

 

CSSD’s charter petition was initially approved in April 1993 for a term beginning July 1, 1994. Since that time, the charter has been renewed three times. The current term will expire on June 30, 2018. Staff recommends that the board grant the charter renewal of CSSD for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2018, and ending June 30, 2023, to serve Grades 6-12, with the following four clarifications:

 

Clarification 1:  Approve Dispute Resolution Procedures on page 161 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

In the event of a dispute between CSSD and the District regarding the terms of this charter or any other issue regarding CSSD and the District’s relationship, CSSD staff, employees and Board members and the District agree to first frame the issue in written format (“dispute statement”) and to refer the issue to the District’s Superintendent or designee and the CSSD Executive Director. In the event that the District believes that the dispute relates to an issue that could lead to revocation of the charter in accordance with Education Code Section 47607, CSSD requests that this shall be noted in the written dispute statement, although it recognizes it cannot legally bind the District to do so. However, participation in the dispute resolution procedures outlined in this section shall not be interpreted to impede or act as a pre-requisite to the District’s ability to proceed with revocation in accordance with Education Code Section 47607.

 

The CSSD Executive Director or designee and District Superintendent or designee shall informally meet and confer in a timely fashion to attempt to resolve the dispute, not later than five (5) business days at the earliest mutually convenient date from receipt of the dispute statement. In the event that this informal meeting fails to resolve the dispute, both parties shall identify two Board members representatives from their respective boards organizations who shall jointly meet with the District Superintendent or Designee and the CSSD Executive Director at the earliest mutually convenient date and attempt to resolve the dispute. within fifteen (15) business days from receipt of the dispute statement.

 

If this joint meeting fails to resolve the dispute, the District Superintendent or designee and CSSD Executive Director or their designees shall meet to jointly identify a neutral third party mediator to engage the parties in a mediation session designed to facilitate resolution of the dispute. The format of the mediation session shall be developed jointly by the District Superintendent or designee and the CSSD Executive Director. Mediation shall be held within sixty (60) business days of receipt of the dispute statement at the earliest mutually convenient date. The cost of the mediator shall be split equally between the district and CSSD. CSSD shall be solely responsible for its attorney’s fees, if any, and any other costs or expenses arriving from a dispute. If mediation does not resolve the dispute, either party may pursue any other remedy available under the law. All timelines and procedures in this section may be revised upon mutual written agreement of the District and CSSD.

 

Clarification 2: Approve School Closure Procedures on page 164 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

On closure of CSSD, all assets of CSSD, including but not limited to all leaseholds (except any leaseholds over district-owned property), personal property, intellectual property and all ADA apportionments and other revenues generated by students attending CSSD, remain the sole property of Student Success Programs and, upon the dissolution of the Student Success Programs non-profit public benefit corporation, shall be distributed in accordance with the Articles of Incorporation. Any assets, including but not limited to any leaseholds over district-owned property, acquired from the District or District property will be promptly returned upon school closure to the District. The distribution shall include return of any grant funds and restricted categorical funds to their source in accordance with the terms of the grant or state and federal law, as appropriate, which may include submission of final expenditure reports for entitlement grants and the filing of any required Final Expenditure Reports and Final Performance Reports, as well as the return of any donated materials and property in accordance with any conditions established when the donation of such materials or property was accepted.

 

Clarification 3: Approve Insurance and Indemnification Language on page 166 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

Self-Insured Workers’ Compensation coverage with a $100,000 Self Insured Retention and limits of $900,000 with Am Trust/WESCO. Excess workers’ compensation with statutory limits is underwritten by Safety National Insurance Company. The coverage is endorsed with a waiver of subrogation in favor of the district.

 

The District is named as an additional insured on all policies of CSSD except workers’ compensation. Evidence of the above insurance coverage shall be provided to the District any time upon request.

 

Clarification 4: Approve Oversight Provisions on page 169 of the charter petition renewal document as clarified below:

 

Pursuant to California law, the District shall be required to provide oversight

and performance monitoring services, including monitoring school and student performance data, reviewing the school’s audit reports, performing annual visits, engaging in any necessary dispute resolution processes, and considering charter amendment and renewal requests, including the following:

(1) Identify at least one staff member as a contact person for the charter school.

(2) Visit each charter school at least annually.

(3) Ensure that each charter school under its authority complies with all reports required of charter schools by law, including the local control and accountability plan and annual update to the local control and accountability plan required pursuant to Section 47606.5.

(4) Monitor the fiscal condition of each charter school under its authority.

(5) Provide timely notification to the department if any of the following circumstances occur or will occur with regard to a charter school for which it is the chartering authority: (A) A renewal of the charter is granted or denied. (B) The charter is revoked. (C) The charter school will cease operation for any reason.

ACADEMICS

 

As the charter granting authority, pursuant to Education Code section 47607(a)(3)(A), the district shall consider increases in student academic achievement for all groups of students served by the charter school as the most important factor in determining whether to grant a charter renewal.

 

A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school. 
  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years. 
  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.  
  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.  
  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(h).

Assembly Bill (“AB”) 484 amended Education Code sections 52052(e)(2)(F) and 52052(e)(4) to allow schools that do not have an API calculated in 2013–14 and 2014–15 to use one of the following criteria to meet legislative and/or programmatic requirements:

  1. The most recent API calculation;
  2. An average of the three most recent annual API calculations; or
  3. Alternative measures that show increases in pupil academic achievement for all groups of pupils schoolwide and among significant groups. 

The decision to use one of these criteria may be made on a program by program basis and is a local decision.

FINANCES

 

District staff reviewed and analyzed the four-year budget and four-year cash flow submitted by CSSD, as well as previously submitted financial reports including, the 2015-16 independent audit report, the 2016-17 second interim report, and the 2017-18 preliminary budget.

 

LCFF revenues presented in the budget are within range of district staff’s calculations. Expenditures are consistent with prior years. CSSD is projecting operational surpluses of a minimum of $1,082,960 per year during the term of this renewal. Based on the fiscal year 2016-17 second interim report, the school projected total net assets of $34,878,064. As of January 31, 2017, available cash of $20,624,893 was sufficient to satisfy current liabilities of $213,181. The ending net position meets the district’s minimum reserve requirement of the greater of 3% of expenditures or $50,000.

 

Over the past five-year term, CSSD has submitted all required financial reports and payments to the district in a timely manner.
