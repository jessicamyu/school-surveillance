RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the board and the public on Closing the Achievement Gap with High Expectations for All (Vision 2020 Quality Indicator and corresponding LCAP Goal 1.  This report will focus on students with disabilities.

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination:  Every school is strongly committed to maintaining high expectations for all students and to eliminating persistent disparities in achievement and performance among student groups based on race, ethnicity, language or disability.  These disparities are understood as unacceptable and a focused plan of support is in place to eliminate the significant achievement gaps in order to realize academic proficiency for all students.  All relevant policies and practices reflect the goals of eliminating achievement disparities and attaining academic proficiency, explicitly and emphatically.  A districtwide focus and effort supports the advancement of high levels of student achievement by ensuring that every school is culturally competent, ensures uniformly high expectations, promotes rigorous curricula, differentiates instruction, and maximizes access for all students to high-level educational opportunities.  The district actively seeks to hire a diverse cadre of educators and administrators, as well as ensure proper emphasis on culturally responsive practices and regularly examines and monitors student achievement, policies, programs, tools and written documents to ensure that achievement gap goals are implemented.  All staff members understand how their respective positions contribute, directly or indirectly to the implementation of the achievement gap goals.  Staff members understand and actively seek to learn the necessary knowledge and skills to serve diverse students and families.  Staff members assume responsibility for implementing plans that will address achievement disparities.  District and community stakeholders engage students and families, the private sector, faith-based groups, and higher education institutions to support all district students in achieving academic proficiency.  

 

LCAP Goal 1:  All levels of the organization work to improve student achievement and close the achievement gap for all underperforming student groups. 

 

Related LCAP Actions and Services:  

  • 1.1.a.2. Central office will support area superintendents, principals, and school staffs to design site-based assessment systems that promote a culture of revision and continuous improvement of student learning based on individual site needs.  
  • 1.7.7. and 1.7.8. To provide for improved outcomes and close the achievement gap for students with disabilities, the Special Education Division will coordinate, design, and provide for specialized and age-appropriate programs, services, and supports for students, birth to age 5, TK - Grade 12, and ages 18-22.  Programs and services will include developing, implementing, and monitoring quality student-centered Individualized Education Plans (IEP), including documentation and adherence to requirements and timelines; and using the specific recommendations of the Special Education Division's WestEd study for the purposes of improvement (i.e., appropriate staffing supports, improved service delivery models, training, etc.). 

BACKGROUND:  At regular, public board meetings Superintendent Marten provides a report that includes the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting LCAP actions and services.  Following the Superintendent's Vision 2020/LCAP report, cluster and advisory groups have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report and provide input and feedback on current LCAP implementation.  The complete LCAP document is available on the district website at https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

BACKGROUND: The California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CCTC) requires that under Education Code Section 44300, local boards of education annually adopt a Declaration of Need for Fully Qualified Educators and an authorizing resolution in the form of a motion, prior to employing teachers on emergency permits. The Declaration requires the district estimate the number, type, and subject of emergency permits that may be needed for the ensuing school year and to provide information about recruitment activities that have been conducted in the effort to employ or train fully-credentialed teachers.

 

The district utilizes emergency permits in designated subject fields such as special education and bilingual Mandarin or French. Emergency permits are only utilized after a diligent search to recruit a fully-prepared teacher is made, and a suitable fully-prepared teacher is not available to the school district.

 

The Declaration of Need must be adopted by the governing board of the school district before filing documents with the CCTC; therefore this declaration is submitted for approval.

BACKGROUND:  At its April 4, 2017 meeting, staff presented its report on final offers of facilities space under Proposition 39 to charter schools.  The board directed district staff to continue discussions with three of the charter schools - Kavod Elementary Charter School, KIPP Adelante Preparatory Academy, Thrive Public Schools - regarding the final offers that were presented to the board on that day. The charter schools agreed to continue the discussion outside the statutory timeline.

 

Staff have held a series of meetings with representatives of the three charter schools and have also met with principals and representatives of district schools where changes were being considered.  As a result of these meetings, staff are making the following recommendations:

 

KAVOD ELEMENTARY CHARTER SCHOOL:  Staff met with the Director of Kavod on April 19.  The charter school expressed its request to remain on the Cubberley campus, with an additional two classroom spaces allocated, and discussed safety concerns regarding location on the Lindbergh/Schweitzer campus under the original final offer (which allocated Kavod its existing space at Cubberley, plus five additional spaces at Lindbergh/Schweitzer).  They also reiterated that they did not wish to relocate to Kimbrough Elementary School, which had been informally discussed before the April 4 board meeting, and the charter school sent a letter to the Superintendent and board on April 9, summarizing these concerns.  Staff has also met with the Cubberley principal and parents, who have expressed their concerns regarding allocating two additional classrooms to Kavod.  Further negotiations with Kavod and Cubberley have occurred.  Kavod is assigned one additional classroom space relative to 2016-17, and will have exclusive use of the resource space in Room 10, as well as continuing shared access of the multipurpose space.

 

KIPP ADELANTE PREPARATORY ACADEMY:  Staff met with the Executive Director of KIPP Adelante on April 24, and discussed alternatives to the final offer of space at Bell Middle School that had been presented at the April 4 board meeting, including space on a high school campus, and KIPP exploring a longer-term facilities solution via the Charter School Facilities Committee.  Staff reiterated its concern regarding mixing a wide span of grade levels (KIPP serves 5th-8th grade) on a high school site.  There are no other middle school sites with the amount of space KIPP Adelante would require under Proposition 39.  Accordingly, staff is recommending to continue with the final offer of space at Bell Middle School, as presented at the April 4 board meeting.

 

THRIVE PUBLIC SCHOOLS:  Staff met with the Chief Executive Officer of Thrive on April 20.  The charter school agreed to forgo the original final offer's allocation of space at Cherokee Point Elementary, Marston Middle School, and San Diego High School, and the original offer of space at Carver Elementary is being increased to 13 classroom spaces, plus a space for office/administrative functions.  Staff met with the Carver principal to confirm the availability of the additional space.  Additionally, staff agreed to develop a project to provide air conditioning for the portable classrooms occupied by Thrive, along with associated electrical upgrades.  Over the course of the next few months, staff will also work with Thrive to develop options to address secondary space needs for the 2018-19 school year and beyond.

 

Because discussion of final offers for these schools was extended beyond the statutory Proposition 39 timeline, these charter schools will need to respond to the district indicating that they intend to occupy the space offered no later than Friday, June 16, in order for staff to have time to prepare the spaces for occupancy for the 2017-18 school year.

 

The allocation of district facilities to charter schools pursuant to Proposition 39 constitutes a project for purposes of the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), Public Resources Code section 21000 et seq.  However, the project is exempt from further environmental analysis under the General Rule clause and two categorical exemption classes as set forth below and as further described in the attached Notice of Exemption (NOE):

 

15061(b)(3). GENERAL RULE

 

The activity is covered by the general rule that CEQA applies only to projects which have the potential for causing a significant effect on the environment. Where it can be seen with certainty that there is no possibility that the activity in question may have a significant effect on the environment, the activity is not subject to CEQA.

 

The proposed project would not result in any physical changes to the affected school sites. The continued co-occupancy of the charter school(s) and district-operated school would not exceed the capacity of that school site.  Therefore, no physical changes are required and it can be seen with certainty that there is no possibility that the continued co-location may have a significant effect on the environment.

 

15314. MINOR ADDITIONS TO SCHOOLS

 

Class 14 consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25 percent or ten classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption.

 

The project would not add new classrooms or other facilities on the existing campuses. Additionally, the co-occupancy of the charter school and district-operated school would not exceed the existing capacity of that school site.

 

15322. EDUCATIONAL OR TRAINING PROGRAMS INVOLVING NO PHYSICAL CHANGES

 

Class 22 consists of the adoption, alteration, or termination of educational or training programs, which involve no physical alteration in the area affected or which involve physical changes only in the interior of existing school or training structures. 

 

The proposed activity would result in either the continued operation of two education programs on an existing school site or the introduction of a new charter school program at an existing district-operating school. The combined programs on the campuses would not require any physical changes to any of the sites.

 

Categorical exemptions may be not be relied upon if the application of the exemption triggers any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2.  Staff has reviewed the sites on which the charter schools will be located and determined that no exception applies to defeat application of the categorical exemptions.  Approval of this item authorizes and directs staff to file a Notice of Exemption in substantial conformance with the Notice of Exemption Attachment with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk.
