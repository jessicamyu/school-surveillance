1. Resolution in Support of Civic Learning for All Students (McQuary/Beiser)

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the resolution of civic learning for all students and lead by example on what good participatory citizenship looks like by actively reaching out to constituents, including students; carefully considering the needs and wishes of parents and students; thoroughly deliberating issues that come before us, working collaboratively, and appropriately delegating and supporting the Superintendent of Public Education in carrying out the vision of the district so that the students’ interests are best served.

 

BACKGROUND: It is the Mission of San Diego Unified School District that All San Diego students will Graduate with the Skills, Motivation, Curiosity and resilience to Succeed in their choice of College and Career in order to Lead and Participate in the society of Tomorrow. We are currently preparing our students by offering them community service hours to promote the importance of civic duty.

 

Schools are a critical place for students to develop the civic knowledge, skills and values needed to effectively contribute to and participate in our democracy.  There has never been a better – or more crucial time – to revitalize civic learning in our schools; as we implement Common Core State Standards, we cannot leave civic learning behind, and the success of the nation and state depends on educated, informed and engaged citizens and residents, and California currently ranks 38th of 50 states in civic engagement; and

 

The education system has a major role in ensuring students have equitable access to learning to participate in our democracy.  Revitalizing civic learning opportunities can contribute to meeting these goals, and we have much to gain by revitalizing high quality civic learning that encourages students to think critically, collaborate, develop research skills, assess and synthesize information, and present coherent arguments based on data. High quality civic learning also helps teach our students skills needed for the 21st century workplace including creativity, initiative and innovation; and

 

The National Council for Social Studies framework providing guidance to enhance the rigor of K-12 civics education has identified Six Proven Practices in Civic Learning:

  • Classroom instruction in government, history, geography, law, democracy, and economics;
  • Discussion of current events and controversial issues;
  • Service learning experiences that are directly linked to curriculum and instruction;
  • Extracurricular activities;
  • Student participation in school governance; and
  • Simulations of democratic processes; and

The district currently makes it possible for students to be part of student government through Associated Student Bodies (ASB) where students run for officer positions and have a voice in student government.

 

The Common Core standards in History/Social Science work require that students learn to analyze multiple perspectives and be able to determine the central ideas or information of a primary and secondary source, and students in San Diego Unified are being offered multiple opportunities to test their knowledge of analyzing primary and secondary sources through document based questioning and assessments that focus on primary and secondary sources.

 

The Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District requests that the Superintendent and staff make a commitment to work towards developing civic literacy of all its students by integrating the Six Proven Practices into instructional practice and school life and that the district establishes a goal to make schools aware of and encourage participation in the application for the annual Civic Learning Award. 

 

A civic learning plan will be developed to include, but not be limited to, integrating civic learning into English Language Arts/English Language Development for all students across grade levels.

BACKGROUND: A first reading of the revisions to District Policy H-7700 Comprehensive School Wellness, and Resolution in the Matter of Supporting a Collaborative Partnership with the County of San Diego's Live Well San Diego Initiative, were presented to the board on March 10, 2015.

 

Live Well San Diego is the County of San Diego's roadmap to achieve its vision of a safe, healthy, and thriving county.  Adoption of this resolution will incorporate San Diego Unified School District into the Live Well San Diego Initiative.

 

The Child Nutrition and WIC Reauthorization Act of 2004, mandated that all local educational agencies participating in the federal school meals program have an established Local Wellness Policy. These Wellness policies are unique to each district and include goals for nutrition education, physical activity and nutrition guidelines for all foods available on campus.

 

The Healthy, Hunger-Free Kids Act of 2010

On December 13, 2010, President Obama signed the Healthy, Hunger-Free Kids Act of 2010 (HHFKA) reauthorizing the Child Nutrition Programs. Section 204 added Section 9A to the Richard B. Russell National School Lunch Act (Title 42, U.S. Code 1758b), Local School Wellness Policy Implementation. The provisions enhanced the previous Local School Wellness Policy requirements, strengthening requirements for ongoing implementation, assessment, and public reporting of wellness policies and expanding the team of collaborators participating in the wellness policy development to include more members from the community. The HHFKA now requires that the local school wellness policy, at a minimum, include:

  • Goals for nutrition promotion* and education, physical activity, and other school based-activities that promote student wellness.
  • Nutrition guidelines for all foods and beverages available on school campus during the school day.
  • Requirements that Stakeholders be provided opportunities to participate in the development, implementation*, and periodic review and update* of the wellness policy.
  • A plan for measuring effectiveness, that is measured periodically and that the assessment is made available to the public.*
  • Public notification informing and updating the public (parents, students, and others in the community) periodically about the content and implementation of the local school wellness policy.*
  • Local designation must include one or more local education agency officials or school officials to ensure that each school complies* with the local school wellness policies.

*These are new requirements.

 

It is the Wellness Committee's belief that healthy students not only excel academically but are more likely to be positively engaged in school. According to the National School Board Association, research shows that when school districts and schools have effective policies and practices that support the health of their students and staff; student and staff absenteeism decrease, student concentration improves, student behavior problems are reduced, and children and adolescents establish lifelong health promoting behaviors. The Wellness Committee believes that implementing this comprehensive Wellness Policy and Framework is in alignment with current research and is a natural first step towards improving districtwide wellness.   Additionally, this policy and framework support the district's goals as stated in Vision 2020 and in the Local Control Accountability Plan (LCAP).

BACKGROUND:  On June 23, 2009, the board approved a three-year agreement with Apex Learning, Inc. to provide online coursework for the district’s, site-based, online Credit Recovery program and the district’s iHigh Virtual Academy program.  The iHigh Virtual Academy provides a dual enrollment program that serves students at district schools and atypical sites as well as a full-time accredited diploma program through independent study.  On April 10, 2012, the contract was amended to a term of June 24, 2009 through July 13, 2015.

 

The APEX learning system has provided the majority of online courses for district students from 2009 to the present.  However, in order to provide a full complement of necessary courses for credit recovery and high school graduation, the district requires the use of additional online course providers.  With the advent of Common Core State Standards and increased sophistication of online course availability, the district sought to find a provider that could meet the multiple needs described herewith.

After this evaluation committee evaluated all six proposals, the evaluation committee recommended that it is in the best interest of the district to enter into agreements for UC ‘a-g” aligned Common Core online learning systems with Apex Learning, Inc. and Edgenuity, Inc.

 

These Agreements will continue to provide students with enhanced opportunities to access a broad and challenging curriculum.  They will provide all school sites expanded access to UC ‘a-g’ approved courses and opportunities for students to recover credits toward graduation.

 

It is anticipated that the online program will continue to contribute toward on-time promotion of students to the next grade level as well as higher graduation rates with a commensurate reduction in the number of dropouts.

Eleven (11) firms responded by the closing date of February 11, 2015, and were evaluated by an Evaluation Committee on the basis of best value, including technical ability and price structure.  The members of the Evaluation Committee consisted of Ronald Lintz, Construction Management Supervisor; Kevin Ohlin, Assistant District Architect; Tony Raso, Project Manager; and Don Webb, Director of Construction Management.  Based on the final ratings of the panel, four firms are proposed for award in order to balance the projected workloads in a timely manner. 

 

All of the firms being recommended have presented rates which are consistent with industry standards and will be cost effective over the length of the Agreements. 

 

California Government Code §§ 53060 and 4525, respectively, grant the district the authority to contract with and employ persons for the furnishing of special services if such persons have the necessary training, experience, and competency to perform the special services requested.
