    FISCAL IMPACT: There is no fiscal impact associated with conducting a public hearing or submitting the waiver request.

    PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

    IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

    BACKGROUND: Roosevelt Middle School has been in Program Improvement (PI) for six years. Roosevelt's Program Improvement Plan included reduced class sizes in key content areas. In 2006-07, the district provided additional teachers to the school, in addition to those allocated by the district's formula, to help the school implement the class size component of their Program Improvement Plan. The goals align with those in the Office of Dropout Prevention and in the ones established in the 2007, report on special education prepared by Dr. Thomas Hehir. It addresses Response to Intervention (RTI) for both academics and behavior by providing a tiered, problem solving structure for schools. This is supported by research indicating behavior issues as one of the main reasons "at risk" students drop out.

    On November 18, 2008, the Board of Education approved a Positive Behavioral Interventions and Supports (PBIS) Pilot Proposal for 15 volunteer Title I schools. The purpose of the program is to assist schools in addressing issues relating to discipline, school climate, and student behaviors. The expected results are lower suspension rates, more safe and secure campuses, and fewer distractions from instructional time.

    The program includes guiding school teams in setting high school-wide behavioral expectations, lesson plans for teaching behavioral expectations to all students, a continuum of consequences for violating behavioral expectations, school wide and classroom reinforcement system for acknowledging appropriate behavior, and a system for reviewing and monitoring the ongoing use of data for decision-making in regards to school procedures and student interventions. In summary, the program is designed to support staff in successfully addressing the behavioral and discipline systems needed for successful learning and social development of students.

    The PBIS strategy is not a packaged program, but a behavior-based system approach that assists a school staff, with feedback from students and families, to develop an effective school wide behavioral support and discipline plan. The school is guided step-by-step in the development of the plan to ensure a common understanding and mutual responsibility regarding behavioral expectations, rules, positive reinforcement, and consequences.
