RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and approve resolution certifying that the district has provided sufficient standards-aligned textbooks, instructional materials, or both. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  The district may use funding from various sources, including state Local Control Funding Formula funds and state lottery funds to purchase standards-aligned textbooks and instructional materials. Newly adopted materials are provided based on actual enrollments the first year of an adoption. Schools are responsible for maintaining sufficiency during the remaining years of core adoptions by ordering from the district's Instructional Materials Office, using an Instructional Materials Order Form or by ordering textbooks and instructional materials using the Destiny Textbook Management System.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  In accordance with Education Code 60119, each school district’s governing board must hold a noticed public hearing, no later than the eighth week of the school year, to determine whether each pupil in the district has sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in the following subjects: mathematics, science, history-social science, and English language arts. The governing board must also determine whether each student enrolled in a foreign language or health course has been provided sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials. According to Williams Legislation, sufficient textbooks or instructional materials means that each pupil, including English language learners, must have a district-adopted textbook or instructional materials, or both, to use in class and to take home. However, the district does not have to provide each pupil with two sets of instructional materials.  The board is required to adopt a resolution setting forth its determination that there are sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials as applicable. 

 

Current Williams legislation also requires that science classrooms have available basic science laboratory equipment for students in science laboratory courses offered in Grades 9 to 12.  The San Diego County Office of Education developed a science laboratory equipment worksheet to identify basic core, lab equipment for science laboratory courses.  All schools conducted inventories and principals verified and submitted a “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency.”

 

Williams Settlement Legislation requires districts and schools to closely examine student access to textbooks and instructional materials and gather evidence to use as the basis of the district's annual resolution of sufficiency.  Administrative Circulars 3, 4, and 5, dated August 26, 2015, were posted notifying school sites (not subject to a Williams site visit) of the need to conduct a school site inventory and complete a “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency.”  In September 2015, instructional materials inventory data and current PowerSchool enrollment for September 11, 2015, was compiled and entered into the “Instructional Materials Sufficiency Form” and provided to each elementary school by the Instructional Materials Office.  Sites were directed to verify their instructional materials inventory and take action to remedy any insufficiencies by ordering from the Instructional Materials Office.  Principals confirmed instructional materials sufficiency and returned the “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency” to the Instructional Materials Office by September 25, 2015. Secondary school principals, after completing their inventories and taking action to remedy any insufficiencies, also returned the “Principal Verification of Instructional Materials Sufficiency." 

 

Twenty-five elementary schools, five K-8 schools, seven middle schools, and six senior high schools were visited by a team from San Diego County Office of Education.  All 43 schools were found to have sufficient instructional materials.  Additionally, all six senior high schools were found to have adequate laboratory equipment for the science laboratory courses offered.

The contributions of Native American Indians to this nation’s government, history, and culture are as numerous and varied as the tribes themselves.

 

The President authorized and requested to call upon federal, state, and local governments, groups and organizations, and the people of the United States to observe November with appropriate programs, ceremonies, and activities.

The commemorative month aims to provide a platform for Native American Indian’s to share their culture, traditions, music, crafts, dance, and ways and concepts of life, and the Indian Education Program, in collaboration with local Native American Indian Organizations and the Ballard Parent Center, will acknowledge the month of November by focusing on heritage with Culture Awareness Workshops on Wednesday evenings during this month.

BACKGROUND: The proposed Project at Audubon K–8 School includes modernization improvements at student drop-off and pick-up areas, the installation of an emergency communication system, expansion of existing parking facilities, creation of a joint-use field, site fencing and security lighting, installation of air-conditioning, accessibility improvements, renovation of restrooms, replacement of kitchen fixtures and general site infrastructure, additions and modifications to the existing administration building, and performance space upgrades. The proposed modernization improvements at Audubon K–8 School would include general upgrades to existing school facilities to create a better learning environment. No buildings would be demolished or removed; however, additions and modifications would occur to the existing administration building.

 

The Project is funded Propositions S and Z.

 

Details of Proposed Improvements:

 

Student Health, Safety, and Security. An emergency communications system would be installed, in addition to 10-foot-high security fencing and lighting around a proposed courtyard, to secure the site against vandals and intruders. As part of the project, student drop-off and pick-up areas for school buses and automobiles along the northern part of the campus would be improved to increase student safety. Improvements would involve cutting sidewalks and striping. The west elevation of the administration building would be modified to eliminate the existing open arcade that leads into the school campus to enhance security and safety.

 

School Accessibility and Code Compliance. Proposition S includes provisions to upgrade school sites to achieve compliance with ADA Titles I and II. Non-compliant ADA ramps would be replaced, and ADA signage and a path would be installed at the proposed parking and joint-use field as part of the project. The existing parking lot on the southwestern corner of the school would be renovated to accommodate an additional 21 spaces for visitors to the joint-use field. Also, additions and modifications to the existing administration building would occur to secure the campus by eliminating the open layout and requiring visitors to check in prior to accessing the campus.

 

Interior Remodeling. The project would include remodeling restrooms and adding sink and grease traps to the kitchen area. New HVAC equipment would be installed in classrooms and some portables, along with new rooftop mechanical units. Electrical upgrades would also occur as part of the proposed project.

BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue the improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting district wide, a new process will be implemented for streamlining Independent Contractor Agreements. The goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

 

Executive Leadership should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective Executive Leadership/Instructional Cabinet team member, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

BACKGROUND:  Human Trafficking is a serious threat facing the health and safety of all children.  "Pimps" and traffickers use every opportunity to recruit male and female children.  Poor and underprivileged youth are often the most vulnerable, easily falling prey to subversive recruitment tactics.  These children are then forced into sex slavery, and are often kidnapped and transferred to other cities, states, and other countries. 

 

The San Diego County Human Trafficking Task Force was formed in 2014, to specifically combat this growing threat to children in the area.  Participating agencies include the Federal Bureau of Investigation; Immigration and Customs Enforcement/Enforcement and Removal Operations; Immigrations and Customs Enforcement/Homeland Security Investigations; United States Attorney's office, Southern District of California; California Department of Justice, Attorney General's office; California Highway Patrol; San Diego County District Attorney's office; San Diego Sheriff's Department; San Diego City Attorney's office; San Diego Police Department and Oceanside Police Department.  Associate Members include the U.S. Customs and Border Protection, San Diego Sector Border Patrol; San Diego County Probation Department; Coronado Police Department; Escondido Police Department; and La Mesa Police Department.  There are two levels of task force participation:

There is an estimated 28, full-time detectives/investigators currently assigned to the Task Force from full Participating Agencies.

The primary mission of School Police is protecting children and keeping them in school; combating the tragedy of human trafficking is one of the foremost priorities.

Parts of this project have been mandated by Congress and the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) directive to implement a nationwide interoperable public safety/first responder emergency communications network.  As part of the upgrade, the San Diego Police Department is currently in the process of replacing their Computer Aided Dispatch (CAD) system; School Police will be purchasing the same system.  Failure to upgrade the district CAD system will pose serious safety risks to students/staff and includes limiting district's ability to effectively communicate with City police during school emergencies. 

 

A CAD system, is the most mission-critical system in any police department.  The CAD is the repository for capturing, prioritizing, and managing large amounts of voice, video, and other data information; all of the “who, what, when, and where data.”  The CAD disseminates information quickly and accurately, maximizing personnel and productivity.  The CAD allows communications center personnel to manage a large amount of information—incident locations, unit locations, unit statuses, information files, pending and active calls, and other critical data—while serving as a voice of reassurance to callers and providing vital information that links police officers, firefighters, and paramedics.

 

The Emergency Communication Systems consist of five components, which are companion board items I.35. through I.39. 

 

For item I.39, the district is purchasing Computer Aided Dispatch (CAD) hardware and software

  • The existing Computer Aided Dispatch system was installed in 1999, and was end-of-life in 2009.  It cannot be updated or maintained.  It operates on Windows XP and, therefore, all workstations also operate on Windows XP, thereby preventing the workstations to be upgraded to handle current software programs that require a newer operating system.
  • The existing CAD system is very cumbersome and labor intensive when extracting data for reports: it currently takes up to 16 hours to prepare a single report; it takes 6 months to prepare an annual crimes report
  • The new CAD system will provide a centralized and consolidated database capable of generating actionable, real-time information about school incidents.

Because of the antiquated and unsupported technology, the dispatch function must be managed using several separate systems that are not interfaced and do not communicate with each other.  The dispatcher must operate the phones, the CAD system, geographical mapping, district databases (PowerSchool, PeopleSoft) and various law enforcement systems, sometimes simultaneously, all independently of each other.  This extensive “system jumping” and multiple operating systems creates a labor intensive dispatch function, negatively impacting student, staff and officer safety.

 

The CAD is also used for processing the fire and intrusion alarms for almost 12,000 alarm lines throughout the district, providing comprehensive reports for analysis.  The majority of all safe school data (mandated by California law) is extracted from CAD; fire drills, evacuations, lockdowns, etc.  However, the current process for extracting and compiling the data is cumbersome and labor intensive, with some reports taking upwards of 16 hours for a Crime Analyst to prepare.  Updating the CAD and report process will make compiling the data more efficient and user-friendly as well as provide additional and customized options for collecting, standardizing, managing, and analyzing information flowing through the district.

 

The new CAD will have several enhanced features:

  • Information files – Once the location has been verified, CAD links the incident to any of its information databases, including previous incidents at that same location, address alerts, free-form text information, telephone numbers, personnel lists, emergency medical dispatch procedures, fire pre-plans, HAZMAT info, hazards, site maps, etc.
  • Time stamping – whenever the dispatcher takes an action (enter a new incident, dispatch a unit, unit arrives on-scene, etc.), the computer records that time and links it to the incident and unit records for later review.
  • Intelligent unit recommendations based on resources to help maximize personnel and productivity.
  • Mapping System –interfaced with the CAD geofile to display units and incident locations.
  • Enhanced ability to retrieve information and eliminate multiple search requirements.
  • Improved ability to disseminate crime information, including crime maps, to district leadership.
  • Interface of CAD and officer computer workstations with other external local, state, and federal information systems.
  • Elimination of redundant data entry between integrated and non-integrated systems.

School Police operates completely within the geographical boundaries of the San Diego Police Department.  It is necessary for the departments to work together, often times sharing resources to maintain the safety of students and staff.  This collaborative effort requires compatible equipment and resources.   Current CAD systems for San Diego Police and School Police do not communicate, a problem solved by these new CAD systems.  Responding officers from either department would have access to vital information needed to ensure student/staff safety. 

 

Sensitive information (e.g. a victim’s name or medical condition) could be sent through the CAD system.  School Police dispatchers could also send real-time updates to the San Diego police officers responding to schools, and vice versa, to include live-feed security camera footage.  School Police officers would also have access to information on incidents occurring around schools that impact student safety and the educational environment. 

 

Operating within the same CAD system as San Diego Police Department provides for redundancy and an additional layer of support.  In the event an emergency at School Police headquarters requires evacuation, School Police dispatchers could respond to the San Diego Police Communications Center, access the district's School Police CAD system from that location with minimal interruption in service, resuming School Police operations.

 

The district cannot continue to operate on its obsolete and unsupported CAD network infrastructures; the result of component failure would be catastrophic.  Without redundancy to mitigate impacts and no ongoing support, the probability of serious impacts continue to escalate.  As the existing “end of life” equipment ages, associated risks only increase over time.  Additionally, with the move towards the wide-spread use of personal, social, and multimedia communication devices, it is imperative that the district's CAD system be upgraded to include the capability of interfacing with these next generation communication devices. 

 

The City of San Diego is finalizing its contract for its CAD hardware and software.  Approval of this recommendation will provide for the district to utilize the City's contract for the identical CAD hardware and software upon its award.  By purchasing from the same supplier as the City of San Diego, coordination for this hardware and software will result in better overall project efficiency and protection of the district's investment.
