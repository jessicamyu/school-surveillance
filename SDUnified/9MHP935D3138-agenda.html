Academics

 

A charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school.
     
  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
     
  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.
     
  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.
     
  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(h).

Finances

 

Based on its location, budget, grade levels, and programs offered, the enrollment projections of Iftin Charter School have been evaluated and appear to be within normal range compared to other charter schools and District schools. The estimated California Department of Education (CDE) funding rates and Cost of Living Allowance (COLA) factor, provided by School Services of California’s Financial Projection Dartboard, have been used to estimate revenue for Iftin Charter School based on the projected average daily attendance rate. This revenue projection has been compared to the projected revenue included in the charter school’s budget and no significant variances have been found. Other expenses unique to charter schools have also been examined and found to be comprehensive and appropriate. Over the past four years, Iftin Charter School has addressed all required annual financial audit and reporting to the district. Based on the projected enrollment, budget, expenses, and cash flow submitted in the charter renewal documents, Iftin Charter School has been found to be financially viable. The school’s actual expenses for 2010-11, 2011-12, 2012-13, and 2013-14 were all greater than budgeted amounts. In addition, the district has examined whether Iftin Charter School maintains an adequate reserve for economic uncertainty which is 3 percent of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater, as another consideration of its financial viability. At the end of 2013-14, per the Iftin Charter School’s audit report, the ending fund balance (EFB) was $1,870,063, which exceeds the minimum reserve amount. In summary, Iftin Charter School appears financially sound.

 

Conclusion

BACKGROUND: On September 4, 2012, San Diego Unified School District Board of Education passed a resolution establishing the San Diego Unified Charter Schools Facility Committee (CSFC), for the purpose of providing recommendations concerning acquisition, construction, reconstruction, rehabilitation, or replacement of charter school facilities, including furnishing and equipping of charter school facilities.

 

On Feburary 4, 2014, the board approved recommendations of the Charter School Facility Committee (CSFC) to expend the Charter School allocation of Proposition Z funds for various Charter School projects.  Among the approved projects was acquisition of the Riverbank Plaza office building by the district for $5,599,625.  Pursuant to the purchase, the district entered into negotiations on a long term lease with Audeo Charter School for the purpose of operating a charter school.

 

Subsequent to board approval, staff in coordination with Audeo Charter School conducted a feasibility study to convert the purchased property for school use. The study identified building modifications and furniture and equipment required to meet Audeo Charter School's operational needs.  The total cost of the required modifications is estimated to be $4.6 million.  This project will serve approximately 667 public charter school students.

 

On October 20, 2014, the CSFC reviewed the request by Audeo Charter School for additional funding from the Charter School allocation of Proposition Z funds.  The CSFC conducted an open discussion on the merits of the project at its regularly scheduled meeting.  This discussion was open to the public, whose comment/input was invited.  The CSFC  unanimously approved this project.

 

This proposed project is in compliance with the board charter school policy for Proposition Z funding, adopted February 4, 2014.  Audeo Charter School has been in operation for more than five years.  Its current charter expires on June 30, 2016.  A list of all previously approved Prop. Z charter school projects is attached for reference as Exhibit B.
