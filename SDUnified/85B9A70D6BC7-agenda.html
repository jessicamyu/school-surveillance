RECOMMENDATION: As the new fiscal/school year of 2009-10 approaches and the budget crisis continues, the district staff requests board direction with regard to further examination of small schools and the development of recommendations for use beyond September 2010.

FISCAL IMPACT: On average, the complete closure of an elementary school campus generates about $450,000 in savings. This stems, in part, from the elimination of the principal and administrative support positions and significant reductions in maintenance, landscaping, custodial, and utility costs. Such costs can be applied to offset a General Fund budget deficit.

BACKGROUND: In the past nine months, small elementary schools with declining enrollment (under 400) have been evaluated for closure and/or reconfiguration in response to the evolving and deepening district budget deficit. In late January 2009, Small Schools Committee of Board/Superintendent-appointed community members provided recommendations for the closure of six campuses. Subsequently in May 2009, the staff updated the recommendations. To date, the board has decided to close one school, North Park Elementary School, to free up campus facilities for the relocation of the ALBA Program.

An examination of small schools facilitates a discussion of the district vision for the use of school property, application of district resources, and the deployment of educational programs. Today, there is a broad-based imbalance between campus capacities and resident student populations. This is especially relevant for older, smaller campuses located in the central and southern sections of the district. The attached commentary and spreadsheet provide specifics. The lack of capacity in certain areas drives some amount of busing of students out of their neighborhoods to campuses further away with capacity to receive them. At the high school level, this lack of capacity is magnified. Case in point, the San Diego, Hoover, and Lincoln high school facilities simply cannot accommodate all of the eligible students within their enrollment boundaries.

Of note, at the June 2, 2009 board workshop, staff was directed to review and revise Administrative Procedure 3113, School Closure: Staff and Community Process, to reflect a 12 vice 18 month process. The revision will be presented at the board session on July 28, 2009.
