RECOMMENDATION:  Ratification of Agreement with the San Diego County Superintendent of Schools for participation in the Science Outreach Programs (SV-13-0267-13).  The date of this Agreement is July 1, 2012 through June 30, 2013.  

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Individual schools are responsible for the cost of participation in this program.  In order for a school to participate in this program, the schools must make arrangements directly with the San Diego County Office of Education.  Funding may come from school site budgets, Parent-Teacher-Student Association funds, grants, donations, etc.  The cost of this program is:

  • Green Machine:  $305.00 per trip, serving four one-hour elementary classes (Grades K-4) of up to 30 students each
  • Splash Science Mobile Lab:  $550.00 per trip, serving four one-hour elementary classes (Grades 4-6) of up to 36 students each
  • Salk Institute Mobile Genetics Lab:  No charge, serving 90-160 middle-level students in four class sessions

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  Individual schools that participated in these programs during 2011-12, paid for the trips from site funds, PTSA funds, grants, donations, etc.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Sites are responsible for making arrangements directly with the San Diego County Office of Education to participate in these programs.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: This Agreement authorizes district schools to participate in the Green Machine, Splash Science Mobile Lab, and Salk Institute Genetics Lab programs available to district elementary and middle-level schools during the 2012-13 school year.  The description of each program is as follows:

  • The Green Machine curriculum aligns with the California Science Content Standards for Grades K-4.  The presentation describes the journey from seed to dinner table through hands-on learning stations that enable students to learn where their food comes from.  A musical and dramatic wrap-up reinforces the learning.
  • The Splash Science Mobile Lab curriculum aligns with the California Science Content Standards for Grades 4-6.  The presentation offers hands-on learning opportunities for students at multiple learning stations (i.e., watershed/storm drain, water conservation, San Diego Estuary, and microscope).
  • The Salk Institute Mobile Genetics Lab provides a unique opportunity for middle-level students to learn about genetics and DNA from real Salk Institute scientists.

These are all field trips or mobile activities that come to the school site. These opportunities are provided to district schools through an agreement with San Diego County Superintendent of Schools. Many schools participate and each site arranges their own field trips since it is a site cost.

FISCAL IMPACT: Potential savings due to not having to pay fees under Labor Code sections 1771.3 and 1771.7.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.


CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On July 28, 2009, the board adopted a Project Stabilization Agreement (PSA) and Addendum One for Proposition S School Construction, Repairs, and Renovation for projects that exceed $1 million.  On December 13, 2011, the board unanimously adopted the PSA resolution to extend the PSA for the duration of the Prop S program and expand its scope to cover all projects paid for in whole or in part with state bond funds.  A copy of the board-adopted resolution is attached as an exhibit.  Since the adoption of the PSA, a third party study has confirmed that the PSA has met the intent of the agreement to promote the use of local workers and provide quality training for the local workforce. 

 

Public Contract Code 2500-2502 (effective January 1, 2012) allowed the district to use project labor agreements that meet minimum criteria designed to protect the taxpayers, which include provisions that prohibit discrimination on the basis of race, national origin, religion, sex, sexual orientation, political affiliation, or membership in a labor organization; permit all qualified contractors to bid for and be awarded work regardless of whether otherwise parties to a collective bargaining agreement;  provide an agreed-upon protocol for substance abuse testing of employees working on site; guarantees against strikes, work stoppages, lockouts, and similar disruptions; and provides that disputes between parties will be resolved by a neutral arbitrator.

 

Labor Code sections 1771.3, 1771.7 and 1771.75 (effective January 1, 2012) created a state compliance monitoring unit to ensure payment of prevailing wage to all employees working on projects paid for in whole or in part with state bond funds.  School districts are exempt from paying a fee to the state enforcement agency for enforcement of prevailing wage laws if the projects are covered by a collective bargaining agreement, such as the PSA, which binds all of the contractors performing work on the project and that includes a mechanism for resolving disputes about the payment of wages.

 

On December 13, 2011, the board made the required findings under Public Contract Code section 2500 et seq. that the PSA met the minimum criteria described above, and authorized ratification of a side letter agreement (Addendum Two) with the signatories to the PSA that extends the term of the PSA for the duration of the Prop S program and extends its scope to cover projects paid for by state bond funds.  The latter enabled the district to shift prevailing wage enforcement back to the state without having to pay a fee under Labor Code sections 1771.3 and 1771.7.

 

The attached resolution continues to make the required findings of taxpayer protection under Public Contract Code section 2500 -2502 and approves the attached Addendum Three to expand the PSA to all district projects paid in whole or in part with future local bond funds, whose contract award exceeds $1 million; the district will continue to shift enforcement of prevailing wage back to the state for applicable projects.

 

Approval of this item will authorize district staff to execute the attached resolution and Addendum Three to the PSA with the signatories to the PSA to expand the PSA to cover projects paid for by future local bonds that exceed $1 million.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the attendance boundary changes for the 2012-13 year included in the attached report, as listed below:

  • Give the Franklin Elementary School attendance boundary the option of feeding to Lewis Middle School and Henry High School, in addition to its current feeder pattern of Wilson Middle School and Hoover High School.
  • Create an optional area for the four Navy housing units at the Naval Medical Center San Diego (NMCSD) to Jefferson or Silver Gate elementary schools, Roosevelt Middle School or Dana and Correia middle schools, and the San Diego High School Complex or Point Loma High School.
  • Reassign the current Barnard Elementary attendance boundary to Ocean Beach, Loma Portal and Dewey elementary schools.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Three different attendance boundary change reports are attached.  The first pertains to giving the Franklin Elementary School attendance boundary, which currently feeds to Wilson Middle School then Hoover High School, the additional option of feeding to Lewis Middle School and Henry High School.  This change was requested following a meeting between the Deputy Superintendent, Business, and the Kensington/Talmadge Community Association.  The community believes neighborhood enrollment will increase at Franklin if their feeder pattern is optional to the Henry Cluster.  Presently, only 16 percent of residents choose to attend Wilson Middle School, compared with 13 percent for Lewis or Pershing middle schools; 35 percent of residents choose Hoover High versus 10 percent who attend Henry High.

 

Second, following a meeting with Navy leadership, the Superintendent is requesting that four naval housing units at NMCSD in Balboa Park be made optional between Jefferson or Silver Gate elementary schools, Roosevelt Middle School or Dana and Correia middle schools, and the San Diego High School Complex or Point Loma High School .

 

Finally, with the transition of Barnard Elementary School to a dedicated magnet school without an attendance area, plus the recent board decision to relocate the Mandarin program to Bayview Terrace Elementary, the attendance area for Barnard Elementary needs to be reassigned to the neighboring schools of Loma Portal, Ocean Beach, and Dewey.  District staff met with the Area 6 Superintendent and the principals of Loma Portal and Ocean Beach (no students presently reside in the portion of Barnard proposed to be assigned to Dewey), and they are in agreement with the proposed changes.  Note that no students currently attending district schools are impacted by this boundary change; only new residents who move into the newly established attendance areas will be affected.  All existing students will continue at the schools they currently attend, and 'concurrent siblings' may also attend with them.

 

For the Board's information, boundaries for Millennial Tech Middle School and Knox Middle School as described in the Area 1 Superintendent's report of December 14, 2010 titled "Establishing Feeder Middle School for Lincoln High School" (Item E.2) will be fully in effect in 2012-13.
