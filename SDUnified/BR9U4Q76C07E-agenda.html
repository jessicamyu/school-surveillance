3. Suncoast STEAM Academy: Acceptance of the Voluntary Surrender of the Charter Petition Effective July 1, 2021
Meeting
Jun 8, 2021 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)

RECOMMENDATION: Accept the voluntary surrender of the charter petition for Suncoast STEAM Academy effective July 1, 2021, and approve the resolution in support of the surrender.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: National University Academy (“NUA”) was a nonclassroom-based charter school that was affiliated with National University, and authorized by Lakeside Union School District (“LUSD”).  After the Anderson v. Shasta court ruling, charter schools that operated sites outside of their authorizer’s geographic boundaries were required to conform with geographic restrictions.  At that time, NUA was operating one site within district boundaries, located at 3678 Aero Court.  The operators of NUA submitted the National University Academy 1001 STEAM (“NUA 1001”) charter petition to the district as part of a transition plan to make the site compliant with the geographic restrictions.

 

On May 16, 2017, the Board of Education denied the NUA 1001 charter petition based on multiple findings in the following areas:

 

  1. The petitioners are demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program set forth in the charter petition
  2. The charter petition does not contain a reasonably comprehensive description of the required elements.
  3. The petition presents an unsound education program for the pupils to be enrolled in the charter school.

 

After addressing the noted concerns in the previously submitted charter petition, NUA submitted a revised petition to serve grades 6-12, and on April 3, 2018, the Board of Education approved the charter petition to operate NUA 1001 for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2018, and ending June 30, 2023. The charter petition detailed an ongoing relationship with National University, and indicated that NUA 1001 would continue to operate out of a rent-free site at 3678 Aero Court that was owned by National University.

 

On July 28, 2020, the Board of Education approved a material revision to change the charter school’s name from National University Academy 1001 STEAM to Suncoast STEAM Academy (“Suncoast”).

 

California Education Code requires charter authorizers to provide oversight of their authorized charter schools. Conducting an annual site visit is part of the required oversight. Annual site visits have been conducted at Suncoast in each school year since the charter was authorized. In each year, the district identified governance, operations, and/or academic concerns. The concerns were shared with the school’s leadership both verbally and in written site visit reports, and during multiple meetings that were held with the school.

 

Several of the concerns identified were related to “splitting” the original charter that was approved by LUSD, and the transition to a new authorizer. Suncoast appeared to have issues with getting the operational and governance structures in place to operate as a single charter school that was wholly independent from a larger entity. Without this foundation in place the school continued to struggle in its transition to oversight by the district, along with other issues.

 

On March 4, 2020, National University sent NUA 1001 a written notice of its intent to terminate its affiliation with NUA 1001 effective September 30, 2020, and requested that NUA 1001 change its name to eliminate any reference to National University. As part of the termination of the relationship, NUA 1001 was required to vacate the location at 3678 Aero Court as of June 30, 2020.

 

On July 7, 2020, the district notified NUA 1001 that it was being placed in “not in good standing” status based on ongoing and unresolved issues related to governance and operations.

 

At the 2020-21 site visit held on February 9, 2021, additional concerns were identified by district staff. On March 31, 2021, the Suncoast governing board voted to approve the voluntary surrender of the charter petition and closure of the school, effective June 30, 2021.

 

After twelfth grade students graduate in June, there will be approximately 67 students enrolled at Suncoast. The school notified families of the closure, and has held several meetings and information sessions with families to support them in finding their next school placement. Suncoast has also maintained communication with the district regarding the transition of students to new placements for the 2021-22 school year.

 

CONCLUSION

 

Staff recommends the board accept the voluntary surrender of the charter petition for Suncoast STEAM Academy effective July 1, 2021, and approve the resolution in support of the surrender.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing June 19, 2021, as Juneteenth in San Diego Unified, a day of celebration of the past, present, and future of Black resilience, culture, and liberation.

 

We acknowledge that “Black Lives Matter” and work to center students in our efforts to “build local power to intervene in violence inflicted on Black communities … by combating and countering acts of violence, creating space for Black imagination and innovation, and centering Black joy” (Black Lives Matter Global Network Foundation, Inc.).

 

We call on educators to teach the importance of Juneteenth and current movements such as “Black Lives Matter” that advance liberation efforts for the Black community with classroom lessons throughout the year; just as we fight for equity, justice, and basic human rights through each interaction with youth, the principles of the Black Lives Matter movement and the promise of liberation that Juneteenth celebrates should be explicitly talked and discussed year round.

 

We encourage staff, students, and community members to use this day to reflect on the historical oppression of the Black community and engage in individual and collective action to break the cycle of systemic racism that exists today through: commitment to education, self-improvement, advocacy, and activism.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Juneteenth is the oldest celebration of the emancipation of African-American slaves. Juneteenth celebrates the final emancipation of slaves in the United States and the persistent drive and determination to seek freedom, liberty, and equality in accordance with the Emancipation Proclamation issued January 1, 1863 and the 13th Amendment to the Constitution which abolished slavery, except section 1, ratified by Congress on December 6, 1865. President Abraham Lincoln first issued the Emancipation Proclamation effective January 1, 1863, freeing the slaves in the South. On June 19th, 1865, Union soldiers arrived in Galveston, Texas and enforced the president’s order, freeing the final African-American slaves two and a half years after it was first decreed. Through other systems of oppression, such as sharecropping, Jim Crow, redlining, Black Codes, mass incarceration, exclusionary policies, police violence, and segregation, the exploitation of the Black community continued past slavery and persists to this day, affecting the physical and mental health, safety, and education of the Black community. Liberation from these systems of oppression must include a commitment to dismantle policies and practices that perpetuate systemic racism and replace them with anti-racist educational systems that center BIPOC voices, stories, healing, and joy and create new structures that require critical self-awareness, deconstructs white supremacy culture, and interrupts anti-blackness. The accurate representation of the contributions and experiences of the African American community were, for generations, and continue to be, systematically excluded from and misrepresented in history textbooks, mainstream media, and classroom discussions teaching San Diego Unified students about Black history and contemporary events is a critical affirmation of the fact Black history is American history. The San Diego Unified School District opposes and rejects any form of oppression and pledges to support the Black community and their intersecting identities by working to achieve genuine equity, access, and success for our students, employees, and communities which we serve.

  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to Section 65962.5 of the Government Code.

Contemporaneously, on or about October 27, 2020, TLVC and Old Republic Surety Company (Old Republic) provided a Performance Bond No. WCN5936201 in the penal sum of $1,734,000 (Bond). TLVC and Old Republic are jointly and severely liable under the Bond to promptly and faithfully perform the covenants, conditions, and agreements of the Contract and to promptly remedy any default under the Contract.

 

TLVC received a timely Notice to Proceed and, according to the Contract, was required to complete the Project by April 18, 2021.  TLVC has performed no construction work on the Project whatsoever.

Under Article 16.3.1 of the General Conditions of the Contract, district may terminate the Contract upon one or more of the defaults by TLVC, including but not limited to failure or refusal to prosecute the Work with diligence as to insure Substantial Completion of the work within the contract time; failure to substantially complete the work within the contract time; failure to supply sufficient skilled Workmen or suitable materials or equipment to complete the work; disregard for laws, ordinances, rules, codes, regulations, orders applicable to the Work; disregard of proper directives of the Architect or the Construction Manager under the Contract documents; and material violations of any Contract requirements.

The Cure Notice advised that the District’s remedies for failure to cure might include termination of the Contract due to damages sustained to the District arising out of TLVC’s failure to make timely progress. In response, TLVC submitted certain requested items, but they largely did not comport with Contract requirements and did not comply with the District’s demands in the Cure Notice.

 

On May 25, 2021 in closed session, the board authorized staff to declare TLVC in default of the Contract, terminate the Contract, send notice of termination, and demand that Old Republic takeover the Project under the Bond with a replacement contractor. TLVC’s last day on the Project and Old Republic’s deadline for a response to the district is June 15, 2021, which is 20 days from the notice of termination and takeover demand. If Old Republic accepts the district’s tender, district staff will bring the takeover agreement to the board for approval at a future meeting.
