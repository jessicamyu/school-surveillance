RECOMMENDATION:  Approve the Redistricting Advisory Committee recommended board sub district boundary map approved at the RAC meeting of September 9, 2011 and adopt resolution.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Consultation with Redistricting Advisory Committee on July 22, August 16 and 23, and September 1, 2011.

 

BACKGROUND: Every ten years after the National Census, every public agency must re-balance electoral districts.  Under Article VI, Section 66 of the City Charter, the district is required to divide its five sub districts as nearly as possible into equal populations.  The Supreme Court has ruled that the deviation in population between sub districts cannot exceed 10 percent.  Education Code section 5019.5 requires that the district readjust its boundaries following each decennial census to ensure we maintain roughly equivalent populations.

 

The current sub districts exceed the 10 percent deviation guidelines.  The current boundaries and population data are shown in Attachment 1.

 

On June 21, 2011, the Board of Education created a five-member Redistricting Advisory Committee and directed staff to issue an RFP to secure the services of a mapping consultant.  On August 2, 2011, the Board of Education approved a contract with ArcBridge to provide mapping and data service to support the Redistricting Advisory Committee in the preparation of a recommended sub district boundary map that balanced population to meet the above requirements.

 

The San Diego County Registrar of Voters has asked that the district submit its new sub district boundary map before October 1 in order for the County to complete a final map to be used in the June 2012 primary election.

 

Recommended Map - The recommended map balances the population between trustee sub districts to below a .9 percent maximum deviation well within the 10 percent permitted.  The recommended map also includes a summary of the ethnic population data for each sub district.  The recommended map makes the following changes to the current sub district boundaries: 

  1. Mission Valley West goes from C to B
  2. Mission Hills  and Midtown go from C to D
  3. Azalea/Hollywood Park goes from D to E
  4. Rolando Park goes from B to E
  5. Part of Normal Heights go from D to B
  6. Part of Cortez, Core-Columbia and East Village go from C to D
  7. Hoover High School moves from B to D.  Includes a part of Talmadge neighborhood

These boundary adjustments impact the board representation of three schools; Hoover High School, Grant and Clay elementary schools.  The RAC received public comment in support of this recommended map and made adjustments to reflect public comment received on the website and in public meetings.  The Committee recommends that the Board of Education adopt this recommended map with no changes.

BACKGROUND:  Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC) staff has been approached by Barbara A. Carpenter, President and CEO for Brighten A Life, and members of her staff, on the possibility of developing a Green Schoolhouse project on the Kearny High campus.

 

The Green Schoolhouse Series is the signature project for Brighten A Life, a 501(c)(3) charitable organization, founded in 1996 as Blanket of Hope, Inc., based in Scottsdale, Arizona.  As a non-profit organization, Brighten A Life seeks donors and grantors to provide financial support for the Green Schoolhouse Series.

 

The Green Schoolhouse Series seeks to build healthy, environmentally friendly and energy efficient schoolhouses that will enhance student learning experiences.  Each project positively impacts the local community and environment through the use of green products, materials and processes.  The Green Schoolhouse Series is currently active in Phoenix, Arizona; Los Angeles, Sacramento, San Diego, and San Francisco in California, with plans to expand to a national platform starting in 2013.

 

During the academic year 2011-12, four Green Schoolhouses will be built in Phoenix, Arizona.  During the last 18 months, multiple planning sessions and design charrettes have been held with The Green Schoolhouse team; the Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) consultant; the architects, contractors and engineers; school personnel; and the community to ensure that the final building design will meet the needs of the recipient school.  Architects and contractors, such as EMC2 and Weitz, are from the local area as well as such nationally known and respected companies as Turner Construction and Hensel Phelps. The Green Schoolhouse Series is expanding to California with plans to build four Schoolhouses during the academic year 2012-13. The locations for the Schoolhouses are Los Angeles, Sacramento, San Diego, and the San Francisco Bay Area.  The construction partner contacts for the district project are Harley Ellis Devereaux Architects, and Reno Construction.  Both firms have local offices in San Diego.

 

The Kearny Campus is being proposed as the initial project from brighten a life after site visits to several campuses with the district’s educational and  facilities staff. The basis for the selection included the following criteria:

·         Low income K-12 public school with high percentage of Title 1 students.

·         Strong interest in participation from district faculty, staff and students.

·         Current Construction Tech Academy (CTA) curriculum associated with sustainability and green jobs in the future.

·         Ability of CTA students to work with the project architect.

·         Ability to augment the academic programs at the four Kearny schools with the 21st century learning environment provided by the Green Schoolhouse.

·         Availability of space on campus for the Green Schoolhouse project.

·         Easy access to the freeway.

·         Central to the district.

·         Location with good community access.

 

The Green Schoolhouse at Kearny will also replace several of portable classrooms with permanent construction that are planned as a part of the first phase of Prop S scope of work for the whole site modernization of the campus.

 

Once this project is complete, Brighten-a-Life would like to do other green school house facilities at other campuses in the district.

Brighten A Life and its contractors will also coordinate the planning, design and construction of the furnished and equipped schoolhouse with representatives from the district’s FPC department.  Representatives of Brighten A Life will design, construct, furnish and equip the Schoolhouse at no cost to the district.  The value of these donations is between $2.4 million to $2.9 million.
