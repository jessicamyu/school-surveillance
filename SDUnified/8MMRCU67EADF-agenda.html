BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue our improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting districtwide, we are implementing a new process streamlining our Independent Contractor Agreements. Our goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective deputy or chief, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

BACKGROUND:  The State of California Environmental Protection Agency, Department of Toxic Substance Control (DTSC) is the state regulatory agency responsible for oversight investigation and response actions with respect to public school sites contaminated with hazardous materials.  Requirements include review of environmental documents for any new site acquisitions to determine if hazardous materials could be present and oversight of any proposed remediation.  (Education Code sections 17210.1, 17213.1 and 17213.2, and Health and Safety Code section 25355.5(a)(1)(c)).

On March 12, 2002, the school board approved a Master Oversight Agreement with the DTSC to oversee the district’s investigation and response actions for potential new school sites to be constructed under Proposition MM.  This Master Oversight Agreement included a list of proposed new schools to be developed, which was revised to include additional sites subsequently found to contain hazardous materials on November 16, 2006. The Master Oversight Agreement also provided for reimbursement by the district to the DTSC for oversight and review costs.  

 

This First Amendment adds Clairemont High School to the listing of sites that require environmental assessments and response actions.  Such action became necessary on July 21, 2011, after a contractor excavating utility trenches in a paved parking lot at the southern end of the site along Ute Street encountered a deposit of burn ash consisting of burned trash and debris buried beneath the parking lot.  DTSC and the City of San Diego’s Local Enforcement Agency were notified on July 22, 2011.  DTSC then requested a work plan by the district, which was provided and approved on August 31, 2011. 

 

Approval of this Amendment will allow the DTSC to work with the district to develop a mitigation plan for the Clairemont site.  The cost of DTSC review of the mitigation plan should not exceed $30,000 based on past experience with similar sites. However, this is only an initial estimate, and the amount needed to reimburse the DTSC may be much more since it is often a lengthy process.  Moreover, it is necessary to provide for work at future sites as they arise, which may require up to $1 million in additional environmental assessment and response actions by the DTSC in order to complete construction during the Proposition S program.  This First Amendment is required because the district must have the capacity to support the work.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The District Calendar Committee, comprised of employee bargaining units, parents, teachers and central office representatives, developed 2012-13 instructional calendar recommendations.  The calendar recommendations are based on a 180-day instructional year, and provide sufficient periods of instructional time, including scheduled breaks, prior to the end of the fall and spring semesters.  The recommended calendars include Spring Break as the first week in April, per board direction to align the instructional calendar with the San Diego Community College District and other adjacent K-12 districts in San Diego County. The calendars accommodate the California Standards Testing schedule and the timeline for the California High School Exit Exam.  A September 2012 start provides slightly less instructional time prior to the Advanced Placement testing period in May 2013.

 

*Staff recommend the approval of the 2012-13 student instructional calendar as developed by the District Calendar Committee, with the exception that Friday, March 29, 2013 (the Friday before Easter) be an instructional day; the last day of the traditional school year be Monday, June 17, 2013; and the last instructional day of the year-round school year be Thursday, July 18, 2013.  Rationale for the exception to the District Calendar Committee's recommendation:  Establishing Spring Break as the first week in April on a permanent basis results in some average daily attendance (ADA) and staff time loss when Easter is not concurrent with these dates.  However, other instructional dates that fall adjacent to holidays also result in ADA loss, including the Friday before Winter Break. Designating the Friday before Easter as a non-instructional day has the potential to create a de facto holiday in the years that Easter falls later in April and creates a break in the academic schedule.
