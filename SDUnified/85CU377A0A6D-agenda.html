The Office of Charter Schools recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the board’s determination of the renewal of the charter petition. • Approve the charter petition with conditions. In essence, the petition is approved with conditions set by the board.

The school received a Growth Academic Performance Index (API) of 657 for the 2008-09 school year, represents a 25 point decline over the 2007-08 API score of 682. In 2007-08, the school received a Growth API of 674, which was a 34 point increase over the 2006-07 API score of 640. In the 2006-07, the school received a Growth API of 641, which was an 11 point increase over the 2005-06 API score of 630. Although the school did not meet the API growth target in the 2008-09 school year, in two out of the past three years the school did meet the API growth targets. In the 2008-09 school year, student academic achievement at KC Arts was not at least equal to district schools where students would have otherwise attended. KC Arts has met the state required academic performance requirements for API in order to be eligible for charter renewal.


Financial Analysis: Based on a thorough fiscal analysis, the school has been found to be in sound fiscal health and deemed to be financially viable.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the renewal charter for Iftin Charter School.

FISCAL IMPACT: $1,600,000

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

BACKGROUND: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education approve the renewal charter petition for Iftin Charter School K-8 (ICS) for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2010, and expiring on June 30, 2015, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition.  The Office of Charter Schools recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the board’s decision. 

 

The charter for ICS came before the board for the renewal of the charter on April 20, 2010.  At that time, the Office of Charter Schools recommended the board approve the renewal with four conditions that had to be met within 30 days.  The board directed staff to work with petitioners in order to address the recommended conditions.  The conditions that were made at that time were the following:

  • Submit within 30 days, the Measurable Pupil Outcomes under Element 2 of the charter that clearly provide the goal and the metrics by which the school will measure pupil outcomes.  What is currently provided is regarding assessment; there is no unique aspect to the charter.

  • Submit a plan to the district within 30 days of renewal to address concerns regarding diversity and recruitment.  Specifically, the school needs to make a financial commitment to address concerns with Element 7 of the charter petition, which reflects Education Code Section 47605(b)(5)(G); which requires the charter petition to describe the means by which the school will achieve a racial and ethnic balance among its pupils that is reflective of the general population residing within the school district (demographic information is found on table 1 of this report).

  • Submit a plan within 30 days of renewal to diversify the board to address concerns that the board is an insular group that does not include different stakeholders.

  • Within 30 days, provide an updated charter petition that clearly states that staff has a right to unionize to address Element O (15).

 

On April 27, 2010, the charter petitioners met with staff to address the concerns that were identified earlier.  Below are the responses:

  • The charter was modified under Element 2 to clearly provide goals and metrics by which the school will measure pupil outcomes.  The prior charter did not provide the stated outcomes for the next five years of the charter; it only identified the outcomes in the previous five years.  Please see the modified charter petition that was submitted, beginning with page 30.

  • The school has submitted a charter and budget for the next five years that makes a concrete financial commitment of $3,000 per year towards addressing concerns with Element 7 of the charter petition, which requires the charter petition to describe the means by which the school will achieve racial and ethnic balances that reflects district demographics.

  • The charter was modified in two concrete ways: 1) The charter now clearly differentiates between the board members and people who serve in a staff/support capacity, which provide clarification regarding interested parties on the board, and 2) the school amended the charter to clearly indicate that as board positions become available, there will be an effort to bring diversity to the board at Iftin.

  • The charter was amended to address the final concern.  The charter now clearly states that staff at the school has the right to unionize.

 

The responses that were provided by the Iftin K-8 Charter School petitioners have addressed the concerns that staff had regarding the points outlined above.  The Office of Charter Schools will provide follow up to these areas during the annual site visit process wherein the district reviews the academic, governance, and finances of charter schools.

 

The board originally approved ICS as a charter school on March 1, 2005, and has been operating and offering Grades K-8 in the district for five years.  The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective immediately, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition and will expire on June 30, 2015.  The potential fiscal impact to the district is an estimated loss of $1,600,000 generated in average daily attendance in 2010-11 because SDUSD does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools.  District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met requirements to continue performing as a charter school in the district, but the petition needs to meet the additional conditions. 

 

If the board desires additional options beyond approval, these options could be:

  • Approve the charter petition with conditions as the board may decide upon during a public meeting to vote on the charter petition renewal.
  • Deny the charter petition.  The board would need to adopt the findings to support a decision to deny the charter renewal. 

 

Academic Performance Summary:  ICS has met the academic performance requirements required for renewal by charter law.  The school received a Growth Academic Performance Index (API) of 689 for the 2007-08 school year, which represents a 114 point increase over the 2006-07 API score of 575, and student academic achievement at ICS is at least equal to district schools where students would have otherwise attended.  Thus, ICS has met the state required academic performance requirements to be eligible for charter renewal. 

 

Financial Analysis:  Based on a thorough fiscal analysis, the school has been found to be in sound fiscal health and deemed to be financially viable.
