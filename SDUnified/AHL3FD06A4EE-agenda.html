C. DISTRICT OPERATIONS
Subject
1. REVISED 2-19-17: Development of 2017-18 District Budget, First Reading; BUDGETED: YES
Meeting
Feb 21, 2017 - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Information, First Reading

RECOMMENDATION: Approve proposed solutions including specific budget solutions and resolutions to reduce and/ or eliminate certificated and classified services in preparation for approving the Second Interim Financial Report on March 14, 2017, and adopting the 2017-18 district budget on June 27, 2017.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: These budget and staffing decisions will have significant impact to the district. All actions taken by the board will be to prepare a balanced budget for 2017-18. The proposed solutions total $124.4 million. Some of the solutions are subject to negotiations with the district’s bargaining units. The specific solutions are attached.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.


IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Impacts to district staffing are reflected in the attachments which include:

  • Resolution in the Matter of Reducing or Eliminating Certain Certificated Services for the 2017-18 school year (first reading).
  • Resolutions in the Matter of the Reduction or Elimination of Classified Services: Non-Represented Managers; Confidential staff, Administrators Association of San Diego (AASD); Operational Support Services (OSS), Office Technical and Business Services (OTBS), Paraeducators (PARA); San Diego City Schools Police Officers’ Association (POA) (first reading).

Approval and full implementation of the personnel resolutions will result in a reduction in currently performed certificated and classified services, and a corresponding reduction in permanent and probationary employees (as appropriate) as well as vacant positions.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district has been grappling with a variety of structural challenges including declining enrollment, pension contribution increases, labor market pressures, and mandates from the state and federal governments. Stability can be achieved but it requires efficiently using available resources in order to focus on student achievement. The proposed solutions to the projected deficit in the 2017-18 budget will enable the district to meet its mandates and requirements, as well as the goals of Vision 2020. At this time the projected budget deficit for 2017-18 is $124.4 million.

 

Approximately 92 percent of the district’s unrestricted budget is dedicated to staff, including salary and benefit costs. In order to balance the 2017-18 budget and reduce the district’s structural deficit, and in conjunction with the March 14 approval of a second interim report, the district must take action to allow for necessary reductions in staffing.

 

Certificated staff

For permanent certificated employees, the Education Code requires that the board identify the particular kinds of services or programs that may be reduced or eliminated in the upcoming fiscal year. For probationary certificated employees, Article 19 of the collective bargaining agreement between the district and SDEA allows the board to determine that reductions are necessary due to lack of funds.

  • Permanent certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a statutory layoff notice on or before March 15, 2017.
  • Probationary certificated staff potentially impacted by this action will be served with a contractual layoff notice on or before April 15, 2017.
  • Noticed employees’ due process rights include either a request for hearing (permanent) or the filing of a grievance (probationary).
  • Temporary certificated employees will receive a separate reminder notice that their services will end at the close of the 2016-2017 school year.

The list of services to be reduced are stated as a full-time equivalent (FTE) rather than a number of employees. It is determined based on factors related to potentially disputed issues regarding seniority (and deviation therefrom), certificates, and qualifications for certain positions to displace less senior employees, the number of certificated employees receiving these notices will exceed the FTE service reductions listed in the Resolutions.

 

Classified staff

Resolutions in the matter of the elimination of the classified services are the result of the budget reductions.  Overall, these eliminations are the result of decreased state funding for public education.

RECOMMENDATION: The district proposes to negotiate with each bargaining unit a potential retirement incentive program in accordance with Section 3547 of the California Government Code and Section 1031 of the Bylaws of the Board of Education.

  1. Adopt resolution to approve a Public Agency Retirement Services (PARS) Supplementary Early Retirement Plan (SERP) for eligible employees (AASD, Confidential, Non-Represented Management, OSS, OTBS, POA, PARA, SDEA)
  2. Approve Agreement with Phase II Systems dba Public Agency Retirement Services (PARS) to provide administrative management services for the district's Supplementary Early Retirement Plan (SERP) (PS-17-0912-99).  The term of this Agreement is February 22, 2017 through January 22, 2022.

FISCAL IMPACT: To be determined when SERP window closes.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: In order for the district to reach stated fiscal goals, a sufficient number of eligible employees must enroll in a retirement incentive plan.  If sufficient participation by eligible employees does not generate needed savings, the district may withdraw the retirement incentive. The review of applications submitted will be presented to the Board of Education for final review and action on the retirement plan by May 11, 2017.  If the district withdraws the retirement incentive, resignations may be rescinded. District staff will support efforts with SERP workshops and orientation.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district agrees to move forward on a SERP for eligible employees in order to mitigate the effect of the layoffs for 2017-18 fiscal year.  The parties acknowledge there are numerous factors that make the offering of a SERP advantageous, including continued declining enrollment and changing shifts in courses needed due to enactment of Vision 2020, Common Core, and "a-g" requirements, as well as increased district STRS and PERS contribution rates.

 

Prior to implementation, this plan and any corresponding MOU’s must be approved by the County Office of Education. 

 

Agreement with Phase II Systems dba Public Agency Retirement Services (PARS):  Upon implementation of the Plan associated with this Agreement, the district will, on the first business day of July, of each year of this Agreement, deposit sufficient funds to fund the required annual Plan contributions.  The district agrees to pay an ongoing administration fee equal to three and one-half percent (3.50 percent) of all premiums made by the district on behalf of Participants in the subject Plan. Fees will be billed to the Custodian as contributions are made by the district, and it will be the responsibility of the Custodian to pay those fees from the custodial assets of the Plan. Said payment of fees by the Custodian shall be made after July 1 of each year of this Agreement. 

 

If the district decides to proceed with the formal offering of the Plan to potential enrollees after the Notice of Intention process, but decides to cancel the Plan after all orientations, workshops, and post analyses have been completed, then it is the responsibility of the district to pay Consultant a one-time fee equal to $75,000.00, plus applicable postage costs. The fee will be billed to the district upon notice of cancelation of the Plan and it will be the responsibility of the district to pay this fee.

 

Fees for services beyond the scope of this Agreement will be charged at Consultant’s hourly rate at the time services are performed. Before any such services are performed, Consultant will provide the district with written notice of the subject services, terms, and an estimate of the fees therefore.  The Agreement provides for discounted hourly rates for any additional services.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND:  In order for the district to maintain fiscal responsibility while fulfilling its obligation and commitment to serving students, the board has determined to implement a fourteen-day work-year reduction with a commensurate 5.4 percent reduction in their annual compensation.  Board members are currently paid $1,500 per month for their service, and by this action will reduce their compensation by $81 per month, with a total savings to the district general fund of $4,860 per year.
