RENEWAL VERSUS REVISION

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.

 

Within a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, reassignment of special education responsibilities, expansion of grade levels, or other significant change.

BACKGROUND:  On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that provided $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified district facilities. As part of the Proposition S, and as required by law, the board appointed the Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee (ICOC).

 

On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11-member, Proposition S ICOC that monitored how the district spent Proposition S dollars.

 

On November 6, 2012, San Diego voters approved Proposition Z, a school bond measure that provided $2.8 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified district facilities.

 

On February 12, 2013, the board took action to amend the Proposition S ICOC bylaws to include oversight of Proposition Z.  Under the amended bylaws, the consolidated committee consists of 13 members; no member may serve more than three terms.  The bylaws also provide for the board to declare vacancies and remove ICOC members in its sole discretion.

 

On February 10, 2015, the board amended the bylaws to reduce the number of committee members to 11.  While all ICOC members are selected by a vote of the board, eight members will be nominated from the following stakeholder organizations:

  • San Diego Regional Chamber of Commerce representative who is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district
  • Middle Class Taxpayers Association representative who is active in a bona fide taxpayers’ organization
  • San Diego Unified School District Cluster Governance Committee representative who is a parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district
  • San Diego Unified Council of PTAs representative who is a parent or guardian of child enrolled in the district and active in a parent/teacher organization
  • San Diego Unified School District Charter School Facility Committee representative
  • American Society of Civil Engineers San Diego Section representative
  • San Diego Building and Construction Trades Council representative
  • Construction industry organization representative

As provided in the bylaws, the remaining three members shall be appointed by the board, in its sole discretion, from a list of candidates who submit applications to the district. Applications and evaluation criteria are posted on the ICOC webpage. The qualifications of all applicant are reviewed according to the evaluation criteria. The Superintendent or designee can provide input to the board on applicant qualifications.

  • One member who is active in a senior citizens' organization
  • Two members from the community at large

BACKGROUND: On September 4, 2012, San Diego Unified School District Board of Education passed a resolution establishing the San Diego Unified Charter Schools Facility Committee (CSFC), for the purpose of providing recommendations concerning acquisition, construction, reconstruction, rehabilitation, or replacement of charter school facilities, including furnishing and equipping of charter school facilities. The CSFC meetings are publicly noticed in accordance with the Brown Act.  The meetings are open to the public, and comment/input is invited.

 

Charter school applications are reviewed by district staff for conformity to the ranking rubric established by the CSFC, and those found to be compliant are then rated according to the priorities established by the committee.  The CSFC subsequently conducts an open discussion on the merits of each project at its regularly scheduled meetings.  These discussion are open to the public. 

 

On February 4, 2014, the board adopted a policy requiring district staff to provide input as well as comment on the spending recommendations from the Charter School Facility Committee prior to any board action. 

 

On February 4, 2014, the board approved CSFC recommendations to establish project budgets for twelve charter schools totaling $41,750,000.

 

On June 3, 2014, the board approved CSFC recommendations to establish project budgets for four charter schools totaling $2,650,000.

 

On July 29, 2014, the board approved CSFC recommendation to establish project budget for one charter school totaling $50,000.

 

On February 3, 2015, the board approved the CSFC recommendation to increase the project budget of a charter school in the amount of $4,600,000.

 

On July 7, 2015, the board approved the CSFC recommendation to establish project budgets for twelve charter schools totaling $188,043,555. 

 

On January 28, 2016, February 25, 2016 and March 24, 2016, at its regularly scheduled meetings, the CSFC conducted open discussions on the merits of each of the projects listed above.  On January 28, 2016, at the conclusion of the discussion regarding the Urban Discovery Charter School project, the CSFC considered the project for action.  At its meeting on April 21, 2016, the CSFC conducted an open discussion on the merits of the remaining projects listed above.  At the conclusion of these meetings, the CSFC voted to recommend these projects for approval. Attachment A includes a narrative overview of each proposed project, including a summary of staff participation in the project's development, and staff comment regarding the project's feasibility.

 

The proposed projects are in compliance with the board charter school policy for Proposition Z funding, adopted February 4, 2014.  A tabular summary of the staff recommendation, consistency with the board's charter policy, estimate, and description for each project is attached as Attachment B.  Attachment C is a listing of prior board-approved Prop Z Charter projects to date.

 

If the board approves this item, the remaining, unallocated Proposition Z charter facilities funds will total $29,935,217.
