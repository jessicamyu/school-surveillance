BACKGROUND: This grant will serve 15 elementary schools over a four-year period with integrated arts/English Language Arts (ELA)/English Language Learners (ELL) training for over 60 teachers.  Teachers co-teach 27 arts lessons with teaching artists to give hands-on experience in the classroom with Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA) content integrated with ELA and ELL strategies and have culminating instructional videos to support teaching.  This project will translate into standards-based arts instruction and ELA/ELL strategies to improve student access to dance, theatre and visual art and will improve student achievement at participating sites.

 

Professional experts are required for year 1 of each round of the grant.  That is, every year for three-four years, five schools will receive teaching artists in the classroom.  After that first year, teachers will be supported by the VAPA office resource teachers until there is a full release of responsibility to the classroom teachers for arts integrated instruction.

BACKGROUND:   District practice has been for sites to develop their Single Plans for Student Achievement (SPSAs) during the spring preceding the year of implementation.  Problems last spring with the newly adopted Site-Based Budgeting (SBB) tool, that includes a function to develop SPSAs, required delaying SPSA development until this fall. 

 

As a result of SB 374, the state holds each school accountable for developing and implementing a Single Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA). The SPSA identifies the instructional needs of students and specifies how school funding, including categorical funds provided through the Consolidated Application, will be used to accomplish the goals outlined in the plan.

 

The SPSA is developed or modified through an annual planning cycle.  The School Site Council (SSC), in collaboration with the site instructional leadership team, is responsible for developing, implementing, monitoring, and evaluation the SPSA.  Site principals, SSC chairpersons, SSC members, and the District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education (DAC) received training regarding specific plan requirements. 

 

Additionally, the Monitoring and Accountability Reporting department staff provided individual support to principals and site teams to discuss Title I expenditures and the SPSA development process.  Sites then developed their SPSAs, had them approved by their SSCs, and submitted them to the respective area superintendents for review.  Area superintendents reviewed plans to ensure schools identified student needs and aligned their smart goals with appropriate courses of action to address those needs.  Once approved by area superintendents, the plans were forwarded to the Monitoring and Accountability Reporting department where staff reviewed each plan and provided individual assistance to schools to ensure that their SPSAs met state requirements and that the plans and associated categorical budget allocations were in alignment.

 

Prior to implementation, the local governing board must review and approve each school's SPSA.  This report brings forward for board approval the 2010-11 SPSAs and categorical budget summaries for 50 schools.

 

Copies of each SPSA, budget summary, and required, signed assurance pages are available in the Board Services Office, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231, San Diego, CA 92103.

Newly elected Board of Education Members Scott Barnett and Kevin Beiser are heading up the new Operations and Efficiency Committee.  The goal is to generate efficiencies and savings minimizing administrative costs so that more district resources can go directly to the classroom.

 

Trustees Barnett and Beiser are looking to recruit action-oriented "Tiger Teams," who will spend 30 to 45 days looking at individual functions within the district.  Each team will include district employees and citizen volunteers with private sector expertise in the areas being analyzed.  The Tiger Team will review a department’s purpose, how it operates and collect input from district employees and citizens on alternatives. Results will be reported back to the Board of Education and Superintendent for action.  Noticed public hearings will be held on recommendations prior to being sent to the board.

  • Human Resources

  • Transportation/Fleet Management, Including bussing schedules and operational costs

  • IT -- Information and Technology

  • Special Education

It should not be inferred that a review of an area indicates any specific concern over the current operation.

 

Any interested employee, parent or citizen is invited to participate in a “Tiger Team."

It's estimated the review of all district operations will take 12-18 months.

 

Members of the public with suggestions or who would like to become a member of a Tiger Team can send an email to efficiency@sandi.net. In addition, a website has been established for the effort at www.sandi.net/efficiency.
