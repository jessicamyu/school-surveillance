BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue our improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting district wide the goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Officers and Area Superintendents should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective site administrator, program director, area superintendent, deputy superintendent or district officer,  the form  with all necessary approved  attachments, will be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA form will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

BACKGROUND: District practice has been for sites to develop their Single Plans for Student Achievement (SPSAs) during the spring preceding the year of implementation.  Problems last spring with the newly adopted Site-Based Budgeting (SBB) tool, that includes a function to develop SPSAs, required delaying SPSA development until this past fall. 

 

As a result of SB 374, the state holds each school accountable for developing and implementing a Single Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA). The SPSA identifies the instructional needs of students and specifies how school funding, including categorical funds provided through the Consolidated Application, will be used to accomplish the goals outlined in the plan.

 

The SPSA is developed or modified through an annual planning cycle.  The School Site Council (SSC), in collaboration with the site instructional leadership team, is responsible for developing, implementing, monitoring, and evaluation the SPSA.  Site principals, SSC chairpersons, SSC members, and the District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education (DAC) received training regarding specific plan requirements. 

 

Additionally, the Monitoring and Accountability Reporting department staff provided individual support to principals and site teams to discuss Title I expenditures and the SPSA development process.  Sites then developed their SPSAs, had them approved by their SSCs, and submitted them to the respective area superintendents for review.  Area superintendents reviewed plans to ensure schools identified student needs and aligned their smart goals with appropriate courses of action to address those needs.  Once approved by area superintendents, the plans were forwarded to the Monitoring and Accountability Reporting department where staff reviewed each plan and provided individual assistance to schools to ensure that their SPSAs met state requirements and that the plans and associated categorical budget allocations were in alignment.

 

Prior to implementation, the local governing board must review and approve each school's SPSA.  This report brings forward for board approval the 2010-11 SPSAs and categorical budget summaries for 24 schools.

 

Copies of each SPSA, budget summary, and required, signed assurance pages are available in the Board Services Office, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231, San Diego, CA 92103.

RECOMMENDATION:  Adopt resolution to support placing a revenue extension measure on the June 2011 ballot.

 

BACKGROUND: Governor Brown's 2011-12 budget proposal is a balanced approach between revenues and new cuts to solving the deficit and provides for an extension of temporary revenues to support programs our students need and deserve.  Over the last several years, K-12 education funding has taken a disproportionate amount of budget cuts.  State and local funding for schools has been cut by more than $18 billion, or about $1,900 per student in the last three years.  The San Diego Unified School District will have cut $490 million from its budget for the past five years as a result of ongoing statewide cuts to education funding.  The loss of $7 billion in one-time federal funding further reduces school budgets.

 

To begin to reverse this downward spiral, Californians must retain the revenues that enable us to invest in our schools and students.  The governor's budget proposal to limit further cuts to schools in 2011-12, is dependent on voter approval of an extension of existing temporary tax increases.  A ballot measure to extend temporary revenues will help prevent further cuts to schools, and without this extension the Legislative Analyst's Office (LAO) reports that funding for schools would fall by at least $2 billion, or more than $335 per student.  The San Diego Unified School District expects our local legislators to work with the governor to protect schools from further cuts and to ensure the continued investment our students deserve. 

 

The San Diego Unified School District opposes a cuts-only budget and supports a budget that is balanced with a combination of cuts and revenue extensions.  The district supports placing a measure on the June 2011 ballot calling for a five-year revenue extension to protect our schools and students by making education a priority in our state.
