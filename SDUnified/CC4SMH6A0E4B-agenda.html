San Diego Unified’s Family Engagement Department and the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs jointly support the district’s Local Control Accountability Plan (LCAP), especially LCAP Goal 5: Family and Community Engagement with Highly Regarded Neighborhood Schools that Serve Students, Families, and Communities. RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution in the matter of recognizing November 2022, as National Homeless Youth Awareness Month and requests that elected officials in the County of San Diego and all of its citizens recognize the unique needs of children and youth experiencing homelessness in order to increase both awareness of the issue and their commitment to helping these young people succeed. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Nearly 1.3 million children and youth were identified as homeless in the United States of America during the last school year. Over 18,000 students in San Diego County were identified by their school as experiencing homelessness last year. Children and youth experiencing homelessness have the least access to essential opportunities and supports; and a disproportionate number of these youth are gay, lesbian, bisexual or transgender. Runaway and homeless youth who live on the street are victims of physical abuse, sexual exploitation and crime in untold numbers. Runaway and homeless youth who flee conflict, abuse, neglect, or, increasingly, poverty in their homes, become disconnected from educational systems and the workforce and do not have the skills and financial resources to live on their own. Youth who leave school without a high school diploma or equivalent are four and a half times more likely to experience homelessness as a young adult. The COVID-19 pandemic negatively affected youth experiencing homelessness as evidenced by increased demand for youth-centered care and mental health services, technology, hygiene, food, shelter, and safety. The district’s Office of Children and Youth in Transition provides services and support for youth experiencing homelessness, to ensure equal access to educational opportunities to enable homeless youth to acquire the knowledge, skills, and abilities needed to develop into healthy and productive adults. Organizations nationwide have declared November as Runaway and Homeless Youth Month to increase awareness of the urgent needs of children and youth experiencing homelessness.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of support for closure of the San Diego Police Department gun range in the Mount Hope community. The Board of Education expresses appreciation and gratitude for the decision to prioritize the health and safety of students by closing the gun range, and offers ongoing collaboration and partnership on any future planning such as hosting public meetings in conjunction with the City of San Diego and community partners.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The students, families and residents of the Fairmount Park neighborhood in the Mt. Hope community of San Diego have been negatively impacted for many years by the presence of the San Diego Police Department’s (SDPD) outdoor gun range at 4008 Federal Blvd. Residents who live near the gun range have been subjected to the sound of gunshots sometimes late into the evening, as families are having dinner and children are studying. The residents of the Mt. Hope community are entitled to lead lives free from trauma and should no longer be the only neighborhood in San Diego to endure the entire burden of adequate police firearms training. San Diego Unified is dedicated to forging strong relationships with the communities it serves, and community input must be the foundation of all strong relationships between neighborhoods and the leaders elected to represent them. Mt. Hope residents were denied adequate input into the 2019 decision to invest $17.8 million in taxpayer money on the outdoor gun range in their community, despite being significantly impacted by this decision. The Board of Education has expressed concern regarding the presence of an outdoor gun range in an historic community of color at multiple board meetings, including but not limited to the remarks by Board President Dr. Whitehurst-Payne at the meeting on February 25, 2020. The City of San Diego under the current Administration has made equity a foundation for city planning, spending and community engagement, and this emphasis aligns closely with the priorities of San Diego Unified, allowing for continued close collaboration on joint-use parks and other initiatives. San Diego Mayor Todd Gloria has played a productive role in addressing concerns around the SDPD gun range, meeting with school leaders on multiple occasions and dedicating significant staff time and energy into resolving the challenges of the Mt. Hope community. Council Member Monica Montgomery and Council President Sean Elo-Rivera have also played an important role, and we appreciate their efforts. Community leaders have been similarly engaged in working with the Board President to end the trauma caused by the outdoor gun range, including Charles “3X” Alexander, Pastor Dale Huntington at City Life Church, Bishop Cornelius Bowser of Charity Apostolic Church, and the Environmental Health Coalition. On November 1, 2022, the City of San Diego confirmed its intention to permanently close the outdoor gun range at 4008 Federal Blvd. San Diego Unified students and all San Diego residents benefit from a well-trained police force, including training in the safe operation and handling of deadly weapons, and training requires sufficient practice opportunities.
