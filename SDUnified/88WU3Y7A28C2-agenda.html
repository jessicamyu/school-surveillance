3. Board Action and Direction to Staff on the 2011-12 Budget Deficit Solutions
Meeting
Nov 16, 2010 - Special Budget Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve the list of budget solutions in the amount of $146.2 $167.8 million to close the 2011-12 projected budget deficit and direct staff to prepare the 2010-11 First Interim Financial Report for board action on December 2, 2010, to include these recommended budget solutions, to the extent necessary, in its multiyear projections to achieve required general fund reserve levels in 2011-12 and 2012-13; and direct staff to take all appropriate actions required to implement the 2011-12 budget deficit solutions. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Based on approval, the 2011-12 projected budget will be reduced by up to $146.2 $167.8 million, dependent upon specific financial variables, including 2010-11 first interim impacts.  Staff will implement Cabinet recommended reductions in its projections prior to implementing other alternative options.

 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  By approving the list of budget solutions to close the 2011-12 budget deficit, the board directs staff to begin the implementation process required to realize these reductions, such as the development of seniority lists; identifying the particular kinds of services (PKS) to be reduced; the determination of district staff impacted through district wide and site-based reduction decisions.

 

BACKGROUND:   AB 1200 provides for a distinct financial oversight role for the County Office of Education in relation to the district and AB 2756 strengthens that oversight function.  The County Office of Education issued a conditional approval of the district’s 2010-11 budget that required the board to submit a list of planned reductions and a timetable to implement those reductions.  The board must affirm to the County that it will continue to take all actions necessary to meet its fiduciary responsibility and provide the district with balanced budgets that achieve required reserve levels for the current fiscal year (2010-11) and two subsequent years (2011-12 and 2012-13) in order to receive a positive certification from the County Office of Education.
