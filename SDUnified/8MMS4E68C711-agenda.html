RECOMMENDATION:  Approve Agreement with San Diego State University Research Foundation (SDSURF) to continue providing Advanced Studies/Gifted and Talented Education (GATE) Certification classes to district teachers (SV-13-0181-15).   The term of this Agreement is June 27, 2012 through June 30, 2013.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  30,000.00.  Budget string: 53700-00000-00-5853-2100-0000-01000-0000.

  

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  $20,000.00.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  With the intent of getting as many advanced studies teachers certified as possible, and despite the budget crisis, the Advanced Studies Department was able to establish a very favorable cost with SDSURF to provide this certification.  San Diego State University Research Foundation will provide three semesters of Tier I certification experience for 300 K-12 teachers at a cost of $30,000.  This cost includes the instructor time, any materials, and the classroom on the SDSU campus.  The rest of the cost for certification will be provided by a co-pay from teachers at a cost of $200.00, and from SDSURF’s revision of the delivery model.  This certification will directly benefit students in 300 classrooms and will instill additional strategies to aid each teacher in raising achievement, levels of rigor, and provide enhanced engagement for all students. 

 

Due to the fact that the vast majority of schools have chosen the Model B or Diversity Model* for their Advanced Studies/GATE cluster classes, almost every class at the elementary level is considered an Advanced Studies class which must be taught by a trained teacher who can differentiate for all students.  Tier I GATE teacher certification supports this process. 

 

In addition, the district has always offered free Advanced Studies/GATE teacher certification during the school year, with the cost being funded from the Advanced Studies Department's discretionary budget.  It was our largest, yet most important, expense—one that is the most beneficial to all students.  This compromise method of support between SDSURF, the district, and teachers will allow Advanced Studies/GATE teacher certification to continue.

 

* Model B--The Diversity Cluster model provides that a minimum of 25 percent of the students in a class are identified as Advanced Studies students.  The remaining students reflect the diversity of the school, thus placing all other students to complete the class roster.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve panel removal agreement with TIFD-SIT, LLC to authorize the removal of solar panels and related equipment at 24 district sites and terminate existing solar purchase power agreements for those 24 district sites.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: $8,000 for district maintenance costs.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: $6,000 for Solar System Management.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Small increase in maintenance work orders.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: This current panel removal agreement is necessary for two reasons: 1) to deal with the recent bankruptcy of a solar provider with its lender; and 2) address recently identified fire and shock hazards in the solar panels.

 

The district currently has 24 solar purchase power agreements with Solar Power Electric I, LLC (SPE), which is owned by Solar Integrated Technologies (SIT), that began in 2005-06 for 24 district sites. Under these agreements, the district not only received sustainable power from the solar panels, but also received new roofs on the buildings during the solar system installation.  The fundamental benefit of these agreements for the district was the new roofs that would belong to the district upon the termination or expiration of the 20-year agreements.

 

TIFD-SIT, LLC (owned by General Electric GE) owns the solar panels which are leased to SPE.   TIFD-SIT, LLC received an assignment to the accounts receivables under the 24 agreements between the district and SPE. 

 

In February 2012, SIT filed for bankruptcy which affects SPE’s ability to perform under the agreements with the district.  As owner of the solar panels, TFID-SIT, LLC, has been very cooperative with the district on management of the SIT bankruptcy and the appropriate resolution to the solar power agreements.  As part of this process, TIFD-SIT, LLC retained a professional solar engineering company to inspect the district sites and to evaluate the viability of the solar system operation into the future. 

 

During the inspection of six sites, it was discovered that an electrical short in the panels and connecting wiring was causing a burn in the area of non-combustible materials.  (Prior to this engineering inspection, some damage had previously occurred and was repaired by SPE’s vendor.)  The inspection revealed that in some cases water was under the panels, causing a short circuit in the panels.  With the safety of students and staff being paramount, it was determined that the best corrective action by TIFD-SIT, LLC, was to roll up the effected panels and turn off the connection to the electrical system.

 

This step was performed in April and May and included shutting down systems and securing any panels that have burn spots.  The district appreciates the decisive action taken by TIFD-SIT, LLC which reflects their concern for the best interest of the students, staff, and district facilities regardless of the costs it incurred related to such action.  After extensive review and collaboration with TIFD-SIT, LLC, and consulting professionals, the district determined that the next step is to remove the approximately 5,000 solar panels from all 24 district sites, along with the related equipment and terminate the 24 solar power agreements.  Under this panel removal agreement, TIFD-SIT, LLC, will bear all the costs associated with the removal of the solar panels, and the roofs received during their installation will become property of the district.

 

The district’s goals of sustainability and energy independence continue to move forward.  This event is a technical issue with the solar system with safety concerns taking precedence.  The system shut down will not affect power to district sites; it will simply revert back to San Diego Gas & Electric providing 100 percent of the power rather than a portion being provided by solar.  District staff continues to coordinate these activities and future actions will be planned to minimize impacts to school operations.  Subject to limited exceptions, this panel removal agreement provides that TIFD-SIT, LLC will completely remove the solar panels and related equipment during this current summer break period.

  1. Revenue Funds
  2. Expenditures;
    1. Administrative costs of the plan
    2. Special education services to pupils with severe disabilities and low incidence disabilities

    4. Supplemental aids and services to meet the individual needs of pupils placed in regular education classrooms and environments

Pursuant to Education Code Section 56205(b)(2), these services descriptors demonstrate that all individuals, age birth through 21 years, with exceptional needs have access to services and instruction appropriate to meet their needs as currently specified in their Individualized Education Programs (IEP).

BACKGROUND: GASB 54 seeks to improve financial reporting by establishing fund balance classifications that are more user-friendly.  Essentially, it establishes a hierarchy of fund classifications based largely on the extent to which the government agency is restricted in the use of the funds.   GASB 54 establishes the following classifications of ending fund balances:

  • non-spendable
  • restricted
  • committed
  • assigned
  • unassigned
