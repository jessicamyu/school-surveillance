2. Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing May 22, 2020, as Harvey Milk Day
Meeting
May 12, 2020 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m. - Revised 5/9/2020 (Item G.3. Added)

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing May 22, 2020, as Harvey Milk Day. The board encourages students and staff to virtually recognize Harvey Milk’s contributions by flying the rainbow flag and reading and discussing Milk’s speech “The Hope,” delivered in 1978 at a public celebration of the California Gay Freedom Day, that encouraged the LGBTQ+ community and all other discriminated minorities to fight for their rights as a people. Resources for teaching and learning about Harvey Milk and the LGBTQIA+ community’s positive contributions are available at http://culturalawarenesscenter.weebly.com/harvey-milk.html.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Harvey Milk was born May 22, 1930, in Woodmere, New York. Harvey Milk served in the United States Navy during the Korean War as a diving officer aboard the submarine rescue ship USS Kittiwake and was later transferred to Naval Station, San Diego to serve as a diving instructor with the rank of Lieutenant. Harvey Milk was a tireless advocate of fairness for all Americans, both as a candidate for office and as a San Francisco Supervisor. In 1977, Harvey Milk was elected to the San Francisco Board of Supervisors, becoming the first openly gay man to be elected to public office in California. This milestone achievement gave hope to millions of gays and lesbians across the country that a day would come when they could live their lives openly and honestly without fear of discrimination. As a Supervisor, Harvey Milk worked with others to secure passage of San Francisco’s landmark Gay Rights Ordinance. This ordinance extended employment protections to gays and lesbians in San Francisco, and it became a model for anti-discrimination legislation throughout California and the nation. In 1978, Harvey Milk traversed the state to campaign against Proposition 6, which was known as the Briggs Initiative. Had it passed, Proposition 6 would have required California school districts to fire openly gay and lesbian teachers solely because of their sexual orientation. The proposition was defeated in the November 1978 election in part because Harvey Milk successfully appealed to Californians’ basic sense of fairness. Although Harvey Milk’s life was cut short, he was a hero and role model, and his legacy of hope, tolerance, and equality lives on.

Established in 1987, the Peace Run is celebrating 33 years of passing a torch from hand to hand through more than 150 nations around the globe as a way to promote international friendship and understanding from people of all walks of life. BACKGROUND: The California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CCTC) requires that under Education Code section 44300, local boards of education annually adopt a Declaration of Need for Fully Qualified Educators and an authorizing resolution in the form of a motion, prior to employing teachers on emergency permits. The Declaration requires the district estimate the number, type, and subject of emergency permits that may be needed for the ensuing school year and to provide information about recruitment activities that have been conducted in the effort to employ or train fully-credentialed teachers.

 

The district utilizes emergency permits in designated subject fields such as special education and bilingual Mandarin or French. Emergency permits are only utilized after a diligent search to recruit a fully-prepared teacher is made, and a suitable fully-prepared teacher is not available to the school district.

 

The Declaration of Need must be adopted by the governing board of the school district before filing documents with the CCTC; therefore, this declaration is submitted for approval.

These First Amendments will extend the term of each Agreement for an additional year from July 1, 2020 through June 30, 2021, for a revised term of July 1, 2017, through June 30, 2021; and will increase the total amount of all the Agreements by $487,400.00 for a new total not-to-exceed amount of $1,987,400.00.  These First Amendments will allow for the continuation of Vision Assessment and Vision Therapy Services for students with Individualized Education Programs (IEPs). All district vision therapy contractors are certified COVD optometrists who provide developmental vision assessment and/or vision therapy services to children with identified disabilities through the IEP process.

 

Vision Therapy is an Individual Education Program (IEP) related service as defined by section 3051.75 of the California Education Code. A student must receive Special Education Services in order for Vision Therapy to be considered as a related service. If an Individualized Education Plan (IEP) team determines that a student’s vision skills (eye teaming, accommodation, convergence/divergence) is impacting that student’s ability to participate in their educational program, then the IEP team is obligated to obtain a developmental vision assessment to determine how vision impacts educational performance. A developmental vision assessment is performed by a developmental optometrist in order for the IEP team to consider if vision therapy would be required for that student to make progress on his/her educational program. As a related service, vision therapy is unique in that it is provided in the office of a developmental Ootometrist (an optometrist with certification from the College of Vision Development or COVD). The vision therapist providing vision therapy must be directly supervised by a developmental optometrist. An optometrist’s office is required because specialized optometric equipment is utilized when providing vision Therapy services.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the use of Education Code section 44258.3 as the local assignment option for the use of an alternative means for completion of the prescribed course of study for high school graduation in Physical Education per Subsection (b) of Education Code section 51225.3.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.


BACKGROUND: As identified in the Course of Study, certain BMD and JROTC courses provide for Physical Education credit. Teachers are deemed qualified by the credential they hold. All BMD/ JROTC teachers hold a Designated Subjects Special Subjects Teaching Credential (DSSS), they must add the Teaching Assignment Option or the STA allow them to grant credit in PE.  

 

Approval of amendments to Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations (CCR) section 80037 pertaining to Designated Subjects Special Subjects Teaching Credentials established a Special Teaching Authorization (STA) in Physical Education effective January 1, 2015. Holders of a DSSS credential in BMD and ROTC may elect to add the STA in Physical Education upon completion of specific requirements.  The STA in Physical Education added to a DSSS credential in BMD or ROTC will provide the holder with a distinct physical education authorization limited to the areas of basic military drill and physical fitness training. The STA will not authorize instruction in any physical education courses that are offered outside of BMD and ROTC programs.

 

Holders of DSSS credentials in BMD and ROTC who do not have the STA listed on their documents will still be authorized, by school board action, to teach Physical Education in the context of BMD or ROTC courses that have been approved to carry high school graduation credit for Physical Education per Education Code section 44258.3. Education Code section 44258.3 allows local school districts to assign any credential holder to teach departmentalized classes, with their consent, in Grades K-12 irrespective of the designations on their teaching credentials. The teacher's competence must be verified according to policies and procedures established by the governing board consistent with the language of the statute; Board Policy 4113 and the accompanying AR: Assignment, along with Board Policy 6142.7 /Administrative Regulation: Physical Education and Activity fulfill this requirement.

 

For teachers who have not yet earned the STA, the Teaching Assignment Option is used. The teacher agrees to teach the BMD/ JROTC course for PE credit. Then, the Assignment Review Panel verifies the teacher's qualifications.  The Assignment Review Panel's criteria includes, but need not be limited to, evidence of the candidate's knowledge of the subject matter to be taught, including demonstrated knowledge of the curriculum framework for the subject to be taught and the specific content of the course of study in the school district for the subject, at the grade level to be taught. The approval is for one year and may be extended for additional time if the teacher and district consent.
