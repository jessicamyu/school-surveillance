3. Proposition S Bond Finance Overview (OE-6, OE-9)
Meeting
Oct 19, 2010 - Special Meeting, 9:00 A.M.
Type
Information

Background:  Gardner, Underwood & Bacon LLC (“GUB”), the district’s Financial Advisor, has prepared an analysis of the benefits associated with incorporating a bond structure with 40 year maturities into the Proposition S Bond Issuance Plan.  As a point of reference, the original plan as developed for the ballot measure contemplated a phased issuance of bonds over 10 years.  The growth in assessed valuation was assumed to be 5.0 percent annually, the average borrowing rate was assumed to be 5.25 percent and the issuances were planned for every two years, beginning in 2009.  The final maturity for the program was estimated at 2044, with total debt service over the life of the program estimated at $5.9 billion.

 

Since 2009, the financing model has been revised to reflect the following major changes: declining growth rate in assessed valuations, increased interest rates and state regulation giving districts the authority to issue general obligations bonds with a maximum maturity of 40 years. 

 

GUB has been asked by the district to prepare two analyses, the first incorporating 40 year issuances and the second incorporating 25 year issuances.
