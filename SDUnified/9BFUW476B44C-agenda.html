CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The District Advisory Council (DAC) for Compensatory Education discussed the proposed Title I Budget Plan at its meetings on December 18, 2013, and January 15, 2014. 

 

BACKGROUND: The Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) of 1965, was reauthorized by the No Child Left Behind (NCLB) Act of 2001. Title I, one of several programs under NCLB, provides funds to schools to help disadvantaged children meet challenging academic standards. The law intends that the funds go to the districts and schools that have the highest concentrations of poverty. Title I requires funding to be allocated to a district based on the number of “children in poverty” residing within the district boundary. The federal census is used to identify children ages five through seventeen living in poverty who are included in the calculation of Title I entitlement.

District leadership is charged with designing a plan that meets legislative requirements while producing sustainable support that will positively impact student learning and significantly raise achievement over time. To that end, the board previously approved, at its January 21, 2014 meeting, the school allocation method and amounts (the Title I Ranking report) for 2014-15, that allocates the same overall amount to schools in 2014-15 that they collectively received in 2013-14.

 

The district has provided opportunities for consultation with parents as required by Title I legislation, NCLB Section 1118(a). At the December 18, 2013 meeting, the DAC discussed the proposed 2014-15 Title I Budget Plan. An action at the meeting established a subcommittee to review the district's proposed spending plan. The subcommittee met on January 8, 2014, to develop recommendations for the board and for the DAC general body. The DAC again reviewed the proposed 2014-15 Title I Budget Plan at its January 15, 2014 meeting.

 

The proposed 2014-15 Title I budget plan presented for approval is unchanged from that presented for discussion on January 21, 2014.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The Board of Education has annually adopted a resolution for issuance of TRANS for the upcoming fiscal year, most recently at the June 18, 2013 meeting for the 2013-14 fiscal year.

 

The district receives a substantial portion of its annual income from property taxes, which are traditionally paid by taxpayers on or before December 10 or April 10.  As a result, the district must borrow funds each year in advance of these dates to pay operational expenses in the July to December timeframe.  Thus, funds are borrowed at the beginning of the fiscal year using TRANS notes and repaid within an eight- to twelve–month period.  Short-term borrowing using TRANS is necessary to ensure the district meets its financial obligations on a cash basis.

 

On March 27, 2012, the board approved a pool of firms to provide financial underwriting services to support the sale of Proposition S and Proposition Z bonds and TRANS issuances.  The district has annually utilized this approved pool of firms from which to select the Underwriters that will purchase and sell the district's annual Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes.  The district sought proposals from the firms in this approved pool of providers. The proposals were reviewed by the district’s financial advisor, Fieldman, Rolapp, and Associates, as well as a selection committee comprised of members of the district’s Finance Division.  Upon completion of the review of the proposals submitted, J.P. Morgan, Stifel, Nicolaus & Company, Incorporated, and Goldman, Sachs & Co., were elected by this committee to provide underwriting services for the 2014-15 Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes and will be written into the Resolution.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: Pursuant to Administrative Procedure 3300, Naming of Schools and School Facilities, this is a request to approve the naming of the softball/hockey field at Morse High School after Martha McCarthy. The School Names Committee has reviewed this request and unanimously concurs with this recommendation.

 

Martha McCarthy was a beloved teacher/coach grade teacher at Morse High School from 1965-2003, when she retired from the district. She spent her entire career at Morse High School, coaching eight different sports, including gymnastics, swimming, badminton, basketball, volleyball, and softball. She coached Boys' Water Polo when there was no one else willing to coach. Since her retirement, she continues to coach the Morse Girls' softball and field hockey teams with her twin sister, Mary, who had an equally long tenure at Hoover High School.

 

McCarthy said she thoroughly enjoyed her teaching and coaching experiences at Morse. "I have always found these kids to be hard workers with a spirit that drives them no matter who their opponent is. Even if they are underdogs, they always give all they have and play for the love of the game. I have been blessed with great kids and wonderful giving parents who have been very supportive of their kids and the program. I can't even begin to tell you how many of these kids and their parents have touch my life in such a positive way. It is my hope that the players I have coached found their experience to be a rewarding one that has left them with fond memories of their playing days."

 

Colleagues, current and former students, parents, and community members agree that naming the softball/hockey field for Martha McCarthy is an appropriate way to honor a teacher/coach who has been devoted to Morse students for nearly a century.

FISCAL IMPACT: Each purchase order has sufficient funds from available budgets for the purchase.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: For the period May 1, 2013 through May 31, 2013:  $1,717,589.90.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: California Education Code section 17604 allows the Board of Education to delegate certain powers to a designee. To allow for efficiency in the administration of a large urban school district, California Education Code section 17605 specifically allows a school district to delegate limited purchasing power, i.e., for purchases no greater than $84,100 (for the year 2014) for supplies, materials, apparatus, equipment and non-construction services; for purchases less than $15,000 for construction services; and for contracts, leases or other documents no greater than $84,100 (for the year 2014) for supplies, materials, apparatus, equipment and non-construction services as long as each transaction is below the competitive bidding threshold under Public Contract Code section 20111. The purchase orders, contracts, leases, or other documents are binding on the district once the employee delegated by the board has entered into those purchase orders, contracts, leases or other documents. For this reason, these types of transactions must be reviewed by the board within 60 days of the date of the transaction under Education Code section 17605. Pursuant to the authority delegated to the district's Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Officer on June 18, 2013, these purchase orders have been released to the respective vendors and are being submitted for review by the board at this time.

 

Thus, the district has met the requirements of Education Code section 17605 with this process of reporting out the specified purchase orders under $84,100 (for the year 2014) that are not associated with any previously-approved board contracts.
