3. Administrative Law Judge Proposed Decision to be Adopted, as Final Decision of Board of Education
Meeting
May 22, 2012 - Special Meeting, 7:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes

 RECOMMENDATION: Adopt proposed decision of administrative law judge, as presented or amended, as final decision of Board of Education and authorize the superintendent to issue appropriate notices to affected certificated employees.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Final fiscal impact will be determined once we have received the decision and determine how many FTEs are affected.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Adoption of the proposed decision will result in the layoff of certificated employees.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On March 6, 2012, the Board of Education approved a resolution eliminating and reducing various certificated services within the district.  Certificated employees whose services were recommended for termination were notified of this recommendation and were notified of their right to a hearing to determine if cause existed for termination of their services.  For permanent certificated employees who requested a hearing, the hearing on the matter was held on April 18, 19, 20 and May 9, 2012, before a state administrative law judge.  The administrative law judge is required to issue his proposed decision on the matter by May 16, 2012.  Pursuant to Education Code section 44949, the attached decision (Exhibit) is recommended to be adopted as the final decision of the board in this matter, unless staff proposes amendments to it. The board further authorizes the Superintendent to issue notice before May 25, 2012, in conformance with the order of the administrative law judge, to the certificated employees listed in Appendix A that their services are no longer required by the district.

4. Resolution in the Matter of District Budget for 2012-13 and Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes (TRANS)
Meeting
May 22, 2012 - Special Meeting, 7:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve Resolution in the Matter of District Budget for 2012-13 and Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes (TRANS).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district will be seeking in June 2012, a Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes (TRANS) for the fiscal year of 2012-13, as it has done for a number of past years.  These TRANS assist the district in meeting its financial obligations during the fiscal year since tax and other revenues are not received in even amounts over the course of the fiscal year.  Recently, concerns were raised that the district was allegedly not in sound fiscal condition.  Staff worked diligently to provide a plan to continue our sound fiscal condition with an excellent credit rating.  As noted in the Resolution, the Board of Education has accepted and is implementing that plan to adopt a balanced budget for fiscal year 2012-13.  This Resolution supports the issuance of the TRANS as part of prudent fiscal management of the balanced budget and assists in providing timely payments to our employees and vendors.  Finally, the Resolution states the intention by the Board of Education to maintain a sound fiscal policy and not to seek any emergency apportionment from the State of California.
