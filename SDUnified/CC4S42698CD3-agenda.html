1. Conversion of the Community Schools Advisory Committee (CSAC) from a Board-Level Advisory Committee to a Staff-Level Steering Committee
Meeting
Jul 26, 2022 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Convert the Community Schools Advisory Committee from a board-level advisory committee to a staff-level steering committee.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Each member of the Community Schools Advisory Committee received information about this potential conversion via e-mail on July 6, 2022.

 

BACKGROUND: On July 28, 2020, the Board of Education created the Community Schools Advisory Committee (CSAC) as part of its Resolution in the Matter of Supporting the San Diego Community Schools Coalition.  As a board-level advisory committee, the CSAC is required to comply with the Brown Act.  The Brown Act requires, among other things, that a quorum of the committee be present at its meetings in order to conduct business and that members who appear telephonically be at publicly noticed and accessible locations.  The CSAC had to cancel several meetings during 2021-22 due to lack of a quorum.  This has caused delays in implementing the community schools initiative. Committee chairpersons Kyle Weinberg and Isabella Millard support the request for the CSAC to be converted from a board-level advisory committee to a steering committee which reports to staff.  This will allow for greater flexibility in allowing CSAC members to participate in meetings electronically.  All other aspects of the CSAC remain unchanged as described in the July 28, 2020 Resolution.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the Memorandum of Understanding with the San Diego Education Association (SDEA) for a one-time off-schedule hiring and retention bonus and hiring incentives for hard to staff positions in special education and school nurse assignments. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT: No budget adjustments are required for this agreement as the existing budget covers any fiscal impact.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district and SDEA recognize the importance of hiring and retaining high quality educators to avoid staffing shortages.  The district is experiencing an acute shortage of staff in certain areas that has been exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic, with Special Education and school nursing experiencing a higher rate of staffing shortages.  To hire and retain educators, the district and SDEA met to discuss a one-time, off-schedule hiring and retention bonus and incentive for Special Education and school nurse assignments. After bargaining in good faith, the parties reached an agreement. 

 

The collective bargaining agreements for PARA, OSS, OTBS, POA, AASD Certificated, and AASD Classified units each contain an equity clause that requires the same one-time, off-schedule hiring and retention bonus for their respective members which are provided to another bargaining unit. 

 

Consistent with district practice, Confidential and Non-Represented employees will receive off-schedule bonuses equivalent to those provided to represented employees.

BACKGROUND:  The district is seeking to acquire property adjacent to Perkins Elementary School to expand the school for a new playfield, for students and residents of the greater Barrio Logan community. In November 2016, the district prepared a draft Site Acquisition Summary Report to analyze alternatives to expand the school, consistent with the California Department of Education (CDE) standards for school site selection (Education Code Section 17251 and California Code of Regulations, Title 5, commencing with Section 14001 through 14012). Based on the findings of this report, staff determined that the three parcels of land southwest of the existing school site, located at 1709 and 1779 Main Street and a portion of City of San Diego owned right-of-way (ROW) on Main Street between Sigsbee Street and Beardsley Street, were the preferred location to expand the school. All three parcels total approximately 2.4 acres. The subject properties are developed with a single-story commercial warehouse building with associated loading dock, asphalt-paved parking lot, landscaped areas, and a paved public right-of-way.

 

In July 2014, a Phase I Environmental Site Assessment (ESA) was prepared for a portion of the subject property by Ninyo & Moore, which identified Recognized Environmental Conditions (RECs) that may have contaminated soil, groundwater, and/or soil gas at the site.

A Phase II ESA was performed on a portion of the subject property and was finalized in May 2017. The Phase II ESA evaluated environmental site conditions related to the former fuel aboveground storage tank (AST) near Sigsbee Street, the former junk yard in the central portion of the site, and the bulk fuel facility in the southern portion of the site (near Beardsley Street). Due to access issues and the need to obtain court order for property access, RECs within the existing building footprint at 1709 Main Street were not evaluated during this initial Phase II ESA. Additional follow up field work and testing was performed in two phases between January and July 2021, as private property access to 1709 and 1779 Main Street, and permit approvals for encroachment and traffic control for testing within the Main Street ROW did not coincide.

The results of the Phase I and II ESA were included within a draft Preliminary Environmental Assessment (PEA) and submitted in December of 2021 to the State of California Environmental Protection Agency, Department of Toxic Substances Control (DTSC) in December of 2021. The DTSC is the state regulatory agency responsible for oversight investigation and response actions with respect to public school sites potentially contaminated with hazardous materials. The role of the DTSC in the acquisition process includes review of environmental documents for any new site acquisitions to determine if hazardous materials could be present and oversight of any proposed remediation (Education Code sections 17210, 17210.1, and 17213).

The initial findings of the Draft PEA presented to the DTSC indicate that the proposed use of the subject property as a playfield would not present an excessive human health risk for soil and soil gas exposure by users of the site. However, the DTSC may recommend additional human health risk assessment using site-specific parameters to make an informed risk management decision regarding whether remedial action is warranted before the site would be approved for development as a playfield. Therefore, the District must further consult with the DTSC to further define any remedial action that may be required prior to use of the site as a playfield. This consultation can only occur with approval of the proposed environmental oversight agreement.

The DTSC approval of the PEA and other related findings will then be sent to the CDE as part of their site approval process, consistent with Education Code Section 17251 and the California Code of Regulations (CCR), Title 5, sections 14001 through 14012, which outline the powers and duties of the Department regarding school sites and the construction of school buildings. Other documentation required as part of the CDE approval process, including California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA) compliance, is being prepared concurrently with the PEA.

CEQA ENVIRONMENAL REVIEW: The district has initiated the CEQA environmental review process for the Perkins Elementary School Whole Site Modernization and Land Acquisition Project (Proposed Project). In addition to the site acquisition, the scope of the whole site modernization of the campus may include the construction of a new 6,500-square-foot (sf), Early Childhood Special Education building; a 12,000 sf two-story classroom building; a 7,000 sf gymnasium/PE building with a physical education office, activity room, and lockers; and a 2,000 sf lunch shelter with 200 sf food kiosk. In addition to new classroom facilities, numerous improvements will be provided on the campus, including development of a Joint Use playfield (2.4 acres) adjacent to the existing campus; modernization of the existing two-story classroom building; modernization of existing Multipurpose and kitchen building; modernization of existing administration building; modernization of existing library building, conversion of the existing transitional kindergarten/kindergarten building into a community service building; and development of a drop off area for students and staff parking at Main Street.

 

A CEQA Notice of Preparation (NOP) was released for public review on January 28, 2022 through March 1, 2022.  Comments on the NOP were received from the Barrio Station Community Group, The City of San Diego, The San Diego Metropolitan Transit System (MTS), and the Native American Heritage Commission. Responses to these comments will be addressed in the Draft and Final Environmental Impact Report that is scheduled to be released in the Winter of 2022-23. The EIR schedule is dependent on the approval of the subject EOA with the DTSC and the timing of subsequent consultation between the District, DTSC and the CDE.

Approval of this agreement will allow the DTSC to work with the district to oversee the district’s preparation of a final PEA for the subject site and other related activities, as necessary. The cost of DTSC review of the PEA should not exceed $20,462.00.

BACKGROUND: On July 24, 2018, the board ratified the Agreement with Alliance for African Assistance in the amount of $75,000.00 to provide translation and interpreting services in languages other than Spanish and Vietnamese for students and families from July 1, 2018 through June 30, 2023.  

 

On May 12, 2020, the board approved the First Amendment to add $95,000.00 to the Agreement to continue to allow for all districtwide translation and interpreting services, in languages other than Spanish and Vietnamese, that are requested in support of distance learning, including during the COVID-19 pandemic. The Agreement with Alliance for African Assistance allows the district to provide translation/interpretation services for 100-plus additional languages. 

 

On December 15, 2020, the board approved the Second Amendment to the Agreement to add $250,000.00 to continue to facilitate increased communications with parents, families, and community members to facilitate and share information.

 

On February 22, 2022, the board approved the Third Amendment to the Agreement with Alliance for African Assistance to add $322,000.00 to allow additional families, including refugees from Afghanistan, access translation and interpreting services to ensure equitable educational opportunities. 

 

This Fourth Amendment adds $1,781,000.00 to continue providing district students and families access to translation and interpreting services, as identified in Exhibit A1 Scope of Work/Services and Payment Terms. These services have been instrumental in supporting refugee and immigrant newcomer youth students and families transition and acclimate to U.S. schools and communities.   

 

  • Alliance for African Assistance is a non-profit organization that has served refugee communities since 1989. Alliance for African Assistance was founded by Walter Lam, a refugee from Uganda, and has focused on refugee resettlement and community services, including interpretation/translation services.
  • Alliance for African Assistance translators/interpreters are independent contractors that are paid competitive market rates and mileage if they are traveling to a site.
  • Alliance for African Assistance can provide 120+ languages that are available nationwide through three-way phone interpretation 24/7.
  • The district has had a contract with Alliance for African Assistance for at least the last 10 years, first with the Special Ed Translation Unit and for the last five years with the Translation Department.
  • Alliance for African Assistance has provided competitive rates that also considers the independent contractors that are part of their team.
