6. Information Regarding Status of Harriet Tubman Village Charter School; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-2)
Meeting
Feb 7, 2012 - Regular Charter Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Information

RECOMMENDATION: This is an information item, no action required.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.
 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.
 

BACKGROUND: The district is the charter-granting agency for Tubman, which authorizes the district to close the school by revoking its charter in cases where violations of law are unresolved.  On April 13, 2011, the district issued a Notice to Remedy as a preliminary step in revocation process.  On June 7, 2011, the Board of Education approved a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) Regarding Conditions for Continuing the Remedy Period which gave Tubman additional time to remedy the violations. 

 

Status:

Over the past few months, district staff has monitored Tubman’s efforts, which included document review, a visit to the school, and interviews with the principal, teachers, and a Tubman board member.  Below is a summary of actions recently taken by Tubman:

  • Tubman transitioned from “arm-of-district” status and is now operated by a
    nonprofit corporation.
  • Tubman partially reconstituted its governing board, which is currently composed of two parents, two community members, and one employee.
  • Tubman entered into a collective bargaining agreement and provided documentation that the Unfair Practice Charge against the school was withdrawn.
  • Tubman board members participated in trainings covering the areas of the Brown Act and the Educational Employment Relations Act. 
  • Tubman adopted formal, written fiscal policies and complaint procedures. 

The Office of Charter Schools will continue its standard oversight and monitoring of Tubman.  Overall, Tubman has demonstrated compliance with the conditions set forth in the MOU.

Action

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt and approve the Charter School Ad Hoc Committee Policy Recommendations.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.


PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Charter School Ad Hoc Committee.

BACKGROUND: On January 10, 2012, the board conducted a First Reading of the proposed policy recommendations by the Charter School Ad Hoc Committee. After the First Reading, the policy was further refined to respond to public comment.  This policy addresses three main subjects, as follows:

  • Participation in a capacity interview for new petitioners;
  • Review meaningful complaints from students, parents, or staff at charter schools;
  • Ongoing review of charter policies.

The Charter School Ad Hoc Committee will continue to meet to develop a comprehensive policy regarding charter schools and present it to the board for adoption by June 30, 2012.
