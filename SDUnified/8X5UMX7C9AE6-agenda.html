RECOMMENDATION: Approve, with conditions 1-7 described below, the Petition to establish the International High School of Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts and Mathematics (IHS STEAM), a charter school, for a five-year term beginning on July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019. 

 

Staff further recommends the board set a deadline of May 6, 2013, for the International High School of Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics to meet the above-described conditions by submitting a revised petition to the Office of Charter School, and any failure to meet the conditions shall rescind the board’s approval of the Petition and constitute a denial of the Petition pursuant to Education Code section 47605(b)(2) and (5).

 

Condition No. 1: Educational Program. 

  1. The Petition needs to clarify that a change in LEA status or SELPA membership will require a material revision of its charter. 
  2. The Petition needs to specify that the charter school will be solely responsible, at its own expense, for compliance with Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 with respect to eligible students, including the initial determination of eligibility. 
  3. The Petition needs to specify that the charter school will be solely responsible, at its own expense, for compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act with respect to eligible students, including the initial determination of eligibility.
  4. The Petition needs to include a more comprehensive description of the charter school’s internship program.

Condition No. 2: Governance.

  1. The Petition needs to clarify in the bylaws whether the meetings of the charter school governing board will occur within the boundaries of San Diego Unified.
  2. The Petition needs to include a more comprehensive description of the charter school’s affiliations with Ying Royal and Ma Sha.

Condition No. 3: Health and Safety Procedures.  The Petition needs to state that the charter school will annually submit its emergency and safety plan to the San Diego Unified School Police Department. 

 

Condition No. 4: Student Discipline Procedures.  The Petition needs to include a more comprehensive description of the charter school’s expulsion procedures, including identification of who is authorized to make a recommendation to expel a student, identification of who is authorized to determine if the recommendation will be adopted, and how parents/guardians of the student will be notified of the right to a hearing.

 

Condition No. 5: Retirement Systems.  The Petition needs to include a more comprehensive description of the manner by which staff members will be covered by the State Teachers’ Retirement System (STRS), the Public Employees’ Retirement System (PERS), or federal social security.  The petition needs to specify that all qualifying employees will be enrolled in STRS and clarify whether the charter school will offer PERS. 

 

Condition No. 6: Dispute Resolution Procedures. The Petition at page 93 needs to amend its dispute resolution procedures as follows:

  1. Reference to the district Superintendent needs to be replaced with “district representative.”
  2. Reference to “two governing board members from SDUSD” needs to be replaced with “district representative.”
  3. The sentence in the Petition regarding the equal sharing mediation costs needs to specify that the charter school will be solely responsible for its attorney’s fees and costs, if any.
  4. The following language needs to be deleted, “If no resolution is reached, the SDUSD Board of Trustees shall vote to resolve the issue.” 
  5. The following language needs to be included in the Petition, “In the event the district determines there is a severe and imminent threat to the health and safety of students, the district reserves the right to take any action it deems appropriate.”

Condition No. 7: Finances and Operations.  The Petition needs to amend its financials as follows:

  1. While newly operational charter schools do not receive their first payment of state lottery cash until the second year of operation, it would be appropriate to record the revenue when earned and not when the cash is received.
  2. Incorrect percentages are used for state aid (should be 35 percent not  85 percent) and Property Tax payments (should be 65 percent not 15 percent) on the Cash Flow worksheet.
  3. Regarding cash flow, in lieu of property tax is not distributed in July.
  4. The Petition needs to include a more comprehensive description of the terms and interest rate related to the $750,000 loan from Ying Royal, and the interest payment needs to be reflected in the budget and cash flow worksheet.
  5. The Petition at page 51 needs to be revised to reflect a five-year charter term beginning July 1, 2014, and ending June 30, 2019, so that the language is consistent with the dates on page 5.
  6. The Petition at page 98 needs to be amended to reflect that the district does not contract with charter schools to provide financial services. 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) Agreement between Magnolia Education and Research Foundation on behalf of Magnolia Science Academy- San Diego (a charter school), the State of California, State Allocation Board and California School Finance Authority, and the San Diego Unified School District (San Diego Unified).  This recommendation is made under the understanding the MOU Agreement is applicable only to the portion of the campus (located at 6365 Lake Atlin, San Diego, CA 92119) on which this project is to be constructed.  The project involves the addition of nine Department of State Architect (DSA) approved, portable buildings containing six classrooms, one science lab, one computer lab, and boys/girls restrooms.  It is proposed to be constructed on a portion of the site which is currently vacant. See attached proposed project map.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None, but if Magnolia has its charter revoked, or it is not renewed, and there is any remaining amount owed on the loan provided by the state loan, San Diego Unified will be required to repay the remaining loan. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  Magnolia Education and Research Foundation (Magnolia) has applied for funding through a Charter School Facilities Program (CSFP) administered by the State Allocation Board and California School Finance Academy.   The CSFP provides as a grant 50 percent funding for eligible charter school facilities.  The charter may also borrow its 50 percent matching share through the program; which may be paid in lump sum, or over time in the form of lease payments.   Magnolia proposes to build a project of approximately $3 million, for which it will receive approximately $1.5 million as a state grant.  The remaining half will be provided by state as a loan, and repaid over a 30-year period. 

 

The project involves the addition of nine Department of State Architect (DSA) approved, portable buildings containing six classrooms, one science lab, one computer lab, and boys/girls restrooms.  It is proposed to be constructed on a portion of the site which is currently vacant.   

 

The statutes under which the program was created require Magnolia to simultaneously enter into the attached MOU with San Diego Unified which obligates San Diego Unified to comply Education Code 17078.62.  That section provides that the funded project shall be made available to Magnolia on an ongoing basis, as a first priority.  If Magnolia has its charter revoked, or it is not renewed, there is a six-month waiting period before any other entity may occupy the facility; during which time Magnolia can appeal the revocation or non-renewal.   Thereafter, as a second priority, the facility must be made available to another qualifying charter school under the same terms as it was made available Magnolia.   Only at such time as the facility is no longer needed for charter school facilities may it be re-occupied by San Diego Unified.   If there is any remaining amount owed on the loan provided by the state loan, San Diego Unified will be required to repay the remaining loan.
