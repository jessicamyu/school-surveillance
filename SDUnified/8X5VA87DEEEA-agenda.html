Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

The proposed agreements will follow the same model as those currently in effect under Proposition S. Because of the level of expertise and volume of services required, four firms will be required to provide support on an “as-required” basis.  Services include program analysis, fiscal management, environmental planning (including CEQA), design and construction management and other related services for planned and ongoing projects.

 

With passage of Proposition Z on November 6, 2012, and subsequent authorization by the Board of Education on February 26, 2013 to sell $530 million of general obligation bonds, implementation of Proposition Z will require an aggressive schedule for the planning and construction of identified projects over a 15-year period. The scheduled scope of work includes building and classroom modernizations, fire safety system improvements, classroom technology upgrades, energy efficiency projects, and Charter School facilities renovations.

 

Each proposed agreement has a 32-month term with an option to renew.  Primary funding for these agreements is available via the Proposition Z Fund.  Over the initial period, the district’s bond program expects to put in place over $800 million in new school facilities and facility school renovation projects.  While the contract values may appear significant, they actually represent a very small percentage of the overall program and are well below industry standards for such a large program. In fact, over the 32-month term of each agreement, the four firms average only a 3 percent increase in hourly rates as compared to the rates presently in effect. Moreover, it is anticipated the overall cost of the agreements will encourage efficiency because of enhanced competition among them for the district's business.

 

The agreements require each firm to conduct employment practices that offer opportunities to local, small, emerging, disadvantaged business contractors, consultants, and suppliers in a manner that maximizes participation without regard to race, creed, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, national origin, age, marital status, physical or mental disability, or veteran status, within the limits imposed by law.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve revised ethical practices for all San Diego Unified managers and board members.

  1. Public Trust.  As public servants, we will act to earn, promote and maintain the public’s trust and confidence in the San Diego Unified School District by adhering to the highest standards of transparency, accountability, and ethical practices.
  2. District Contracts.  All district contracts will be awarded after an open and transparent process, in accordance with the applicable requirements governing the respective contracting process.
  3. Gifts.  District managers and board members shall not (a) receive any gift if receipt of the gift gives the impression of seeking partiality, favoritism, or improper influence; or (b) receive gifts, presents, or articles of value from students or classes, or give such gifts; or (c) receive gifts from any company doing, or attempting to do, business with the school district. See attached AP 9300, page 1, Section B.2.  District managers and board members will not accept gifts, services, travel, entertainment, jobs for immediate family members or other special considerations that may give the appearance that the benefit could improperly influence district decisions.  When any gift is received, the manager or Board member should follow all appropriate protocol for reporting the same related to Form 700.  Not all district managers are required to fill out Form 700; however those who are so required should exercise due diligence in complying with all the requirements therein. 
  4. Personal gain from position or resources.  No district employee or board member will use their district position for personal or private gain.  We will not use public resources for private, non-district purposes or for purposes other than for which they were intended.  We will not solicit or accept contributions for non-district purposes from companies who may do business with the district.
  5. Conflicts of interest.  We will not hold financial interests that conflict with the performance of our duties to the district.  We will not engage in outside employment or activities that conflict with our official district duties.
  6. Waste, fraud, abuse, corruption. We will immediately disclose possible waste, fraud, abuse and corruption to the appropriate personnel (fraud hotline 1(800) 539-6621) at the district.
  7. Use of authority.  We will not use our authority to intimidate, threaten or retaliate against a person to attempt to interfere with the disclosure of potentially improper governmental activity.
  8. Non-interference.  Board members will not interfere in the process of staff recommending contracts and contractors to the board.  The board’s role should begin at the point of reviewing and responding to staff recommendations.
  9. Observe Cooling Periods.  A “Cooling Period” is a mechanism used by public agencies and private organizations across the country to ensure that no unfair competitive advantage is extended due to the hiring of current or former employees.  Allowing for some time to pass before a former official works on matters related to their prior agency or a new official works on matters related to their prior employer helps to mitigate concerns about the appearance of a “revolving door” where public offices are sometimes seen to be used for personal or private gain.

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Comes as recommendation from Procurement Practices Committee

 

BACKGROUND:  These ethic codes outline the guiding principles and expected behaviors for all San Diego Unified managers and board members, especially when relating to vendors or contractors who either serve or are seeking to serve the district.

 

The board requested staff to set up a Procurement Practices Committee to review district procurement practices, especially as related to construction purchases.  The Committee was asked to develop a voluntary Ethical Practices Statement for all managers and board members.  The Committee has done so and has voted unanimously to recommend the attached Ethics Statement to the board for its consideration.  It is not the intent of the Committee that this statement replaces or supersedes any existing district policy or administrative procedure.  It is designed to set the bar high for personal conduct for all district managers, including board members.

 

On March 26, 2013, a first reading was presented to the board for review and discussion.  Based on board member input, the ethical practices statement has been revised.
