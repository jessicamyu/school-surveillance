FISCAL IMPACT: Amount of funding not to exceed $80,000.00; funded by adult education state apportionment funding; Budget string: 5462-03091-5853-2700-4100-11000-0000.  This contract comes to the board on an annual basis.  Because instructional mentors employed through this contract are crucial to the successful operation of the Adult Education Program, mentor contract expense is given budget priority in budget development each year.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: This contract has enabled the Adult Education Program to utilize the services of Instructional Mentors for many years.  Because the Adult Education Program is an open-entry/open-exit program, the number of students enrolled in the program changes on a daily basis.  Instructional Mentors are college students hired throughout the school year to meet the changing needs of the program.  Therefore, the cost of the contract varies from year to year.  The total cost of the mentor contract for the 2008-09 school year was $101,590.00. Contract cost for the 2009-10 school year totaled 124,841.00.  It is projected that the cost of the contract for the 2010-11 school year will be approximately $76,000.00. 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: There is no impact to district staffing.  The position of Instructional Mentor is not a job classification authorized in the district. Instructional Mentors are employees of the San Diego Community College Auxiliary Organization.  The position is a temporary, hourly position.  

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: None.

 

BACKGROUND: The district awards the Adult Education High School Diploma jointly with the San Diego Community College District Continuing Education (Adult Education) Program as the result of a Delineation of Function Agreement entered into by the districts in 1979.  Due to the success of the Delineation of Function Agreement, this contract relationship has worked well over the years.  Mentors will be assigned to five Adult Education site locations throughout the San Diego Unified School District which will include the Crawford, Garfield, Hoover, Madison, and Mira Mesa Adult Education sites.  Instructional Mentors will provide assistance to adult education teachers and one-on-one tutoring to adult education students during the adult education instructional day.  Instructional Mentors have assisted thousands of students, over the years, to obtain the Adult Education High School Diploma.

BACKGROUND: In June 2010, the San Diego Unified School District Board of Education voted to “Phase Out” the district sponsored BTSA Induction Program. Since the inception of SB2042 legislation in 1998, our program has supported over 7,200 new teachers in earning a California Clear Teaching Credential.

Although our district has chosen to redirect these funds, the Education Code Section 42605 and Senate Bill SB242 remain the same; beginning teachers are still required to clear preliminary teaching credentials during the initial years of teaching.

On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that will provide $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified School District facilities.  On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11-member Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) for Proposition S. As part of the Proposition and as required by law, the board appointed members to the ICOC to monitor how the district spends these tax dollars.

The membership for the Proposition S ICOC is selected from the following criteria:

  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens’ organization 
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district 
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization

Board members each choose one appointee from the above criteria and each select an individual of their own choosing, "at-large."  The superintendent provides one appointee to serve "at-large."

 

Terms for membership on the Proposition S ICOC are for two years, and there is a two-term maximum. No employee or official of the district shall be appointed to the citizens’ oversight committee, nor any vendor, contractor or consultant of the district.

Meeting
May 24, 2011 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Information

RECOMMENDATION: This item is information only. The Chief Financial Officer will provide additional information, including proposed Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) Statement 54 policies and procedures, to the board at subsequent meetings.  The First Reading of these will be presented as information at the June 14, 2011 board meeting.  The Second Reading will be presented for action at the June 28, 2011 board meeting.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFF: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: None.

 

BACKGROUND: The information below is excerpted directly from the Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) Statement 54 clarification memo, dated March 2009.

 

Fund balance refers to the difference between assets and liabilities in the governmental fund balance sheet.  This information is one of the most widely used elements of state and local government financial statements.  It is of central importance to credit agencies, taxpayer associations, research organizations and oversight bodies, as well as to the general public.

 

GASB has determined that there are many inconsistencies and misunderstandings with respect to how reporting agencies interpret fund balance definitions and descriptions.  These diminish the value of audit report information to the end users of those reports.

 

GASB 54 seeks to improve financial reporting by establishing fund balance classifications that are easier to understand and apply.  Essentially, it establishes a hierarchy based largely on the extent to which a government is bound to observe spending constraints that govern how it can use amounts reported in the governmental funds balance sheet.

 

GASB 54 establishes the following classifications of ending fund balances:

  • non-spendable
  • restricted
  • committed
  • assigned
  • unassigned

Governmental agencies—including school districts— are required to adopt policies and protocols to establish the processes that shall determine amounts relegated to these classifications, e.g.- through board action.  Agencies are required to implement Statement 54 for fiscal years first ending June 30, 2011.
