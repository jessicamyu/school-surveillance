BACKGROUND: In 2012, the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs introduced the first National Parent Involvement day for San Diego Unified School District. The San Diego Unified School District, the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs, and the broader education field recognize there are many significant caregivers in children’s lives not adequately represented by the term “parent,” therefore the term “family” is also used to celebrate this day. San Diego Unified’s Family Engagement Department and the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs jointly support the district’s Local Control Accountability Plan (LCAP), especially LCAP Goal 5: Family and Community Engagement with Highly Regarded Neighborhood Schools that Serve Students, Families, and Communities. The San Diego Unified School District and the San Diego Unified Council of PTAs partner to recognize National Parent/Family Involvement Day at district schools and all of the existing avenues for family engagement. The San Diego Unified School District and San Diego Unified Council of PTAs are catalyzed by more than 40 years of research confirmed by experience that strong family, school, and community partnerships to support student learning, both at school and at home, increase overall student achievement, attendance, credit acquisition, graduation rates, life outcomes, and school effectiveness. The mission of PTA is to make every child’s potential a reality by engaging and empowering families and communities to advocate for all children. Schools will host an event during the week of November 15-19, 2021, to celebrate National Parent/Family Involvement Day, to honor the powerful contributions that families make to support the success of students and to reinforce awareness of the invaluable role that families play in supporting student learning and public education, and honor their contributions to student success. In addition to recognizing the role families play as invaluable allies in student success, secondary goals of National Parent/Family Involvement Day include identifying family and community leaders whose efforts will help schools increase family engagement, building strong partnerships linked to student learning.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of imminent risk of harm to attendees of public meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic which will be effective for 30 days, commencing November 10, 2021.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Assembly Bill No. 361 (“AB 361”) was enacted by the California Legislature and is intended to “improve and enhance public access to . . . local agency meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic and future applicable emergencies, by allowing broader access through teleconferencing options.” AB 361 permits local legislative bodies subject to the Brown Act and/or the Badgley Keene Act to hold virtual meetings, without needing to publish the physical locations of Board members or make those locations open to the public, if there is a proclaimed state of emergency (pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act) and either: (i) state or local officials have imposed or recommended measures to promote social distancing, or (ii) there is a finding by the local legislative body that, as a result of the emergency, meeting in person “would present imminent risks to the health or safety of attendees.” Governor has proclaimed a state of emergency pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act. Since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, in compliance with public health orders and for the protection all involved persons, the Board of Education (“Board”) has held its meetings telephonically via Zoom and provided for public comments to be provided via teleconferencing rather than allowing in-person attendance by the public at the Education Center. The COVID-19 pandemic continues to present an imminent health risk to the public which require certain specific health and safety measures be maintained. The District’s central office facilities present specific challenges in maintaining COVID-19-related safety protocols required for the health and safety of attendees, which include, but are not limited to, adequate ventilation and security, enforcement of masking requirements, and maintenance of recommended social distancing. The health and safety risks are presented by providing public access at the remote locations from which Board members currently attend Board meetings remotely. The Board hereby finds that conducting Board and/or committee meetings in person in light of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic presents an imminent risk to the health and safety of prospective attendees.

RECOMMENDATION: Trustees Barrera and Whitehurst-Payne will propose revision of the Superintendent Search timeline, to allow the Superintendent Search Advisory Committee adequate time to review applicant packages and recommend up to 10 candidates to the Board, and to allow sufficient time for the three finalists selected by the Board to be presented to the public. The proposed revised timeline is below, and results in the appointment of a permanent Superintendent in January 2022, instead of December 2021, as originally planned.

 

NOVEMBER 2021 

  • Nov 11: Superintendent position application deadline (unchanged) 

  • Nov 17: Advisory Committee Closed Session Meeting (HR to provide training on scoring applications)

  • Nov 17-19: Applications reviewed and scored by Advisory Committee for recommendation of up to 10 candidates

 

DECEMBER 2021 

  • Dec 1: Advisory Committee Closed Session Meeting (to finalize recommendation of up to 10 candidates to the Board)

  • Dec 7-8: Presentation of up to 10 candidates to Board of Education (Closed Sessions)

  • Dec 9-10: Board selects 3 Finalists (Closed Sessions)

  • Dec 14: Board of Education Meeting (Announcement of 3 Finalists)

 

JANUARY 2022

  • Jan 6, 7, 10, 11: Public vetting of 3 Finalists

  • Jan 12-14: Board selects Superintendent (Closed Sessions)

  • Jan 18: Board announces permanent Superintendent at State of the District Address

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The Superintendent Search Advisory Committee has been notified of the proposed timeline change. 

 

BACKGROUND: The district is conducting a search process for its next permanent Superintendent. Former Superintendent Cindy Marten was appointed on May 13, 2021 as Deputy Secretary of the U.S. Department of Education. Dr. Lamont Jackson has been appointed as Interim Superintendent through Dec. 31, 2021. 

 

In February 2021, the board adopted a Superintendent Recruitment and Selection Process that included establishment of an Advisory Committee, reflecting the diversity of the district's students and the San Diego Community, and called for community engagement to identify what is most important to stakeholders in selecting the district's next permanent Superintendent.

 

The National Center for Education and the Economy (NCEE) was contracted to facilitate the community input process and gather and compile the data. The process included 34 community input sessions, held from July through September 2021. As noted in NCEE's report, the timing of this work fell largely during the summer, when many families and community members take vacations and typically pay less attention to school-related matters. In addition, a rise in COVID cases throughout the region compelled the district to cancel many in-person activities at district facilities and may have dampened the willingness of some to attend the community input sessions that were held in person. Some community members also were not comfortable sharing their feedback via an online digital platform. Despite lower than hoped for participation, the findings indicate that Equity, A Focus on the Whole Child, and Organizational Structures and Processes are the three main themes most important to district stakeholders.

 

The board approved the Job Posting on October 12, 2021, and the district will receive applications from qualified candidates through November 11, 2021. The Advisory Committee will then screen the candidate application packages and will recommend up to 10 candidates to be interviewed by the board. In December 2021, the board will select up to three final candidates. Community forums will be held for the public to meet the finalists and provide feedback. The board will appoint a permanent Superintendent. 

 

Information about the Superintendent Search is available on the district's webpage at https://sites.google.com/sandi.net/sdusd-superintendent-search/home. Questions or comments may be sent to superintendentsearch@sandi.net.

BACKGROUND: In June 2021, the School Names Committee began the process of selecting a name for the new elementary school in Mission Valley.  With input from the San Diego Unified Ethnic Studies Department and Youth Advocacy team, the committee generated a list of eligible names, published a webpage and a developed stakeholder survey using Google Forms.

A nexus for expansive trade routes, this village was an important gathering point of cultural exchange and a thriving venue for exchange of goods. One small step toward restorative justice, this name recognizes and celebrates the long history of indigenous peoples.
