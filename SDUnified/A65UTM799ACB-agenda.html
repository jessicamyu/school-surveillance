RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the board on the implementation of specific elements of Access to Broad and Challenging Curriculum (Vision 2020 Quality Indicator and corresponding LCAP Goal 2).  The Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA) Plan is being brought forward for formal board adoption.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Funds to support the implementation of the VAPA Plan and the purchase of musical instruments and 1:1 devices has been included in the 2016-17 district budget.

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination:  Schools have a clear rationale, vision, and high expectations for achievement and immediacy for change in a positive and productive learning environment.  School leaders collaborate with and motivate the school community providing support, sharing leadership, paying attention to the work and magnifying best practices using the appreciative inquiry lens.  Schools know students' learning needs and can recognize if instruction is improving student learning through classroom visits and analyzing data focused on student achievement.  School leaders monitor belief systems and teach the importance of embracing diversity and respecting all cultures.  The school community understands why change is important and collectively acts to ensure that all students have a quality education.  Leaders positively influence the attitudes of students, staff, parents, and the community in setting high expectations for all students.  Leaders bring systemic change towards a culture that embraces diversity, respects all cultures, and ensures the development and implementation of educational programs that maximize academic achievement of all students.  Leaders are personally aware of the role race plays in perpetuating a system of bias, prejudice, and inequity.

 

LCAP Goal 2:  Students have equitable access to rigorous, well-rounded, standards-aligned curricula and access to and skill in applying technologies to leverage learning, assuring readiness for a full range of post-graduation options.  

 

Related Actions and Services Identified in LCAP:  

  • 2.2.2. - In addition to the core content areas of math, science, English/Language Arts, social studies, and physical education, the district will provide enhanced and extended learning opportunities including:  Linked Learning; College, Career, and Technical Education (CCTE); Junior Reserve Officers Training Corps (JROTC); Gifted and Talented Education (GATE); Advanced Placement (AP); International Baccalaureate (IB); biliteracy, dual language, and world language programs; Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA); instrumental music; Advancement Via Individual Determination (AVID); Science Technology Engineering Arts Math (STEM/STEAM); Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS); Civics Education; Interscholastic Athletics; and outdoor learning opportunities.
  • 2.2.5. - Additional Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA) opportunities will be provided to selected Title I schools.
  • 2.2.6. - Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA) will be enhanced by beginning a yearly band uniform replacement cycle, providing additional musical instruments and repairs, and support in vocal music, dance, and theater.  
  • 2.3.1. - The district will develop and refine standards aligned, core content and course curriculum maps, tools, and guides, including access to quality digital tools and reputable online resources in service of effective teaching and learning for diverse groups.  
  • 2.4.1. - Digital resources will be expanded including timely replenishing of teacher and student devices, as well as maintaining and upgrading technology infrastructures.  
  • 2.4.4. - The district will increase/expand 1-to-1 access for digital tools.
  • 5.5.1. - The district will develop and implement strategies customized for each cluster, by identifying, reviewing, and expanding successful programs in targeted clusters (e.g., dual language; Visual and Performing Arts [VAPA]; Science, Technology Engineering, Arts, and Math [STEAM]). 

BACKGROUND: At each regular, public board meeting, Superintendent Marten provides a report that includes the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting LCAP actions, services and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's Vision 2020/LCAP report, cluster and advisory groups have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report, and provide input and feedback on current LCAP implementation. The complete LCAP document is available on the district website at https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the resolution declaring October 2016, Civil Discourse and Civic Engagement Month.  The board requests that this message be disseminated to teachers, staff, parents, and students.  The board supports meaningful and polite civil dialogue in the classroom and on school campuses that analyzes all points of view with a critical lens in a non-partisan fashion.  The board will continue to encourage civil discourse among themselves when there are disagreements and ask the public to do the same.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District promotes civil discourse and civic engagement based on respect for all.

 

The level of national discourse in recent years has bombarded the public and students with hateful and demeaning language that would not be tolerated in any district classrooms, and district students have been involuntarily exposed to unacceptable behavior and language from adults in the public eye who show little to no regard for their audience and are setting a negative example for future leaders.

 

While this has been happening on the national scene, most notably during the 2016 Presidential Campaign, some local groups, such as former San Diego Unified School Board Member, Reverend George Walker Smith’s Catfish Club of San Diego, holds an Annual Restoring Civility to Civic Dialogue Conference where they support the community initiative to promote greater civility in San Diego’s public dialogue.

 

In San Diego Unified, programs have been implemented districtwide to endorse mutual respect, tolerance, and acceptance of others, and help combating bullying in schools based on race, religion, creed, color, marital status, parental status, veteran status, sex, sexual orientation, gender expression or identity, ancestry, national origin, ethnic group identification, age, mental or physical disability or any other distinguishing characteristic.

 

The board supports meaningful and polite civil dialogue in the classroom and on school campuses that analyzes all points of view with a critical lens in a non-partisan fashion.

 

Staff will increase efforts to address all forms of bullying that occur in schools and continue to publicize the systems already in place in the district to report any form of bullying or discrimination in schools.

 

The Associated Student Body (ASB) leaders will continue to provide leadership that models civil civic engagement.

RECOMMENDATION: Ratification of Agreement with Donald D. Diffenbaugh to repair or replace roofing on portable and permanent buildings on an as-needed basis (CP-17-0242-23). The term of this Agreement is October 1, 2016 through September 30, 2017; with delegation of authority to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Officer to exercise an additional Option Year 1 and Option Year 2 provided for in the Agreement to extend up to two additional one-year terms, subject to the identification of budgeted funding sources.
 

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not-to-exceed amount of the initial term: $995,000.00.  Budget strings: 9xxx-90404-00-6200-8500-0000-21400-8xxx and 5686-81500-00-5853-8100-0000-01000-0000.

 

Total not-to-exceed amount for Option Year 1: $995,000.00 for the term October 1, 2017 through September 30, 2018.  Budget string to be determined for Option Year 1 in the event the district exercises this option at a later date.

 

Total not-to-exceed amount for Option Year 2: $995,000.00 for the term October 1, 2018 through September 30, 2019.  Budget string to be determined for Option Year 2 in the event the district exercises this option at a later date.

 

Grand total not-to-exceed amount: $2,985,000.00 if the initial term and both Option Year 1 and Option Year 2 are exercised.  

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: For the period of October 1, 2015 through September 16, 2016: $992,059.00.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  Bids were solicited on August 30, 2016.  Bids were received from Best Contracting Services, Inc., Commercial & Industrial Roofing Company, Inc., Donald D. Diffenbaugh Construction, Inc., Eberhard Benton Roofing, Good-Men Roofing & Construction, Inc. dba Good-Men Roofing, Danny Letner Inc. dba Letner Roofing Company, A. Preman Roofing, Inc., Premier Roofing CA, Inc., and Sylvester Roofing Company, Inc.  Donald D. Diffenbaugh Construction, Inc. has been determined to be the lowest responsive and responsible bidder.

 

This agreement provides for the repair and replacement of roofing systems on portable and permanent buildings on an as-needed basis.  This agreement is only utilized to repair roofs when Physical Plant Operations roofers have a full workload.  The district has two full-time roofers who perform small routine roof repairs.  This agreement is also used during the rainy season when many roof repairs are required to keep facilities operational and to support student instruction.  In addition, this agreement will be used to proactively address maintenance issues at a number of sites that have been identified by the purchase agreement for solar installations that have roofs which are currently in need of repair.

 

On May 10, 2011, the board approved a resolution in support of service-disabled, veterans-owned businesses (SDVOB) and disabled, veterans business enterprises (DVBE) which established a 3 percent required participation of DVBE's for all district construction contracting opportunities.  The recommended contractor, Donald D. Diffenbaugh submitted a 4 percent DVBE participation in its bid.
