G.01. Food Services Proposed Meal Price Increase
Meeting
Jun 20, 2008 - 06/20/2008
Type
Action
Budgeted
Yes
The Food Services Department is requesting a 25-cent increase in school breakfast and lunch meals beginning with the 2008-09 school year in order to maintain current programs due to increased costs. The price increase will involve students who purchase full-price meals only. This will result in an elementary lunch meal price of $1.75 and a secondary meal price of $2.25, and breakfast meal price of $1.00. Students who qualify for free and reduced-meal prices will not be affected by the meal price increases as recommended.

Since the last price increase of $.25, approved at the June 26, 2007 board meeting, costs have increased substantially in the last year. In order for the Food Services Department to continue to offer a healthy-choice lunch program and provide nutritious foods, continual product development is necessary. The $.25 meal price increase would continue to provide the necessary revenue to maintain current programs and avoid encroachment of the general fund. The Food Services Department is finacially self-sufficient and historically nondependent on the District's general fund. [Originator/Contact: Gary Petill, Director, Food Services, 858.627.7301]

The fuel management system is 17 years old and lacks the necessary technology to manage the District's fleet. There is an imminent need to replace these systems prior to the start of the 2008-2009 school year in order to meet upcoming deadlines by agencies having jurisdiction over the operations of fueling facilities, and also to provide Student Transportation with reliable, state-of-the-art systems.

Authorizes Agreement C89-002 with WESTERN PUMP, INC. for the installation of a fuel dispensing and automated fuel management system at a total cost of $1,107,490. Work is expected to be completed by August 23, 2008.

Bids were due and opened on May 29, 2008. The bid submitted by Western Pump, Inc. has been determined to be the lowest responsive and responsible bid, and is within the District's budget for completion of this work. Prior to bidding, it was determined by certified fuel systems specialists that the existing fuel dispensing system, due to its age, could not be either certified or modified to meet the new regulatory requirements. In addition, a review of the continued increases in maintenance costs, lack of availability of parts, frequency of repairs and limitations on the throughput of fuel during peak fueling periods confirmed the need to replace these systems at this time.
The regulatory agencies that have jurisdiction over the operation of the District's fuel storage and dispensing facility are the Air Pollution Control District (APCD), California Air Resources Board (CARB), Department of Environmental Health (DEH) and the San Diego Fire Department (SDFD). New rules associated with unleaded gasoline dispensing, enhanced vapor recovery and operation were established under executive order VR- 201 and 202 respectively by the APCD. The executive order requires all operators to comply by established deadlines with the implementation and certification of the enhanced vapor recovery (EVR2) systems by April 1, 2009. The application deadline to submit an application for authority to construct and permit to operate a certified phase 2, EVR system is October 1, 2008.

The District must comply with these deadlines and meet all regulatory requirements. This facility is mission critical due to its daily support of school buses, food service transport trucks, maintenance trucks and school police cars. Construction activities will completely disable the existing system; therefore, work must begin in early July and be completed by late August in order to minimize the impact on school operations by having the new systems installed, tested and certified prior to the start of school in September.

Alternate fueling methods for use during the construction activities have been evaluated and will be in place prior to the start of construction. [Originator/Contact: Pete Iverson, Interim Executive Director, Facilities/Funding Source-State Facilities funds, 858.637.3603]
