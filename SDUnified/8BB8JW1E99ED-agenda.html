4. Board Governance Policies and Annual Work Plan Calendar
Meeting
Mar 1, 2011 - Regular Charter & Prop S Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
No

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve revised Board Governance Policy statements and the annual work plan calendar for 2011.

 

BACKGROUND:  The board met on January 4, February 15, and February 19, 2011 to discuss governance issues.  Based on the board's work at their governance retreats, the governance policy statements have been revised to add policy statements to the Goals for Student Achievement, GSA-2, Academics, Operational Expectations, OE-4 Democratic Engagement of Stakeholders and OE-5 Personnel. 

 

The Board added at GSA-2 Academics that all schools will achieve their academic potential by exhibiting one year's growth.  The Board added as an indicator of achievement for the new policy statement that 100 percent of district schools will meet their API growth targets each year with accountability measures.

 

At OE-4 Democratic Engagement of Stakeholders the board added new number 6, direction to the superintendent to develop a cluster management strategy that will be supportive of democratic clusters in the improvement of schools.  Management will encourage development of lead principals chosen by the cluster to work in close cooperation with the community leaders.  Clusters will be held accountable for proving democratic process.  Cluster administrative support leaders will facilitate and support, not dictate from the top. 

 

At OE-5 Personnel, the board added new number 16, the superintendent will maintain accurate information on employee positions and overall statistics and have the ability to effect staffing changes on short notice due to accurate, real time numbers and information; and new number 17, the superintendent will have the Human Resources staff work with the Deputy Superintendent Academics and Deputy Superintendent Business to develop recruitment and retention plans for their respective areas that set high standards, develop internships, residency-type programs, and other means to develop new staff, work towards having staff match the diversity of the students and effective means to retain and professionally develop all employees.

 

The board has established priority areas for 2011 which include OE-2, Instructional Program, Sections 2.2 and 2.4; OE-4 Democratic Engagement of Stakeholders, Section 4.6; OE-5 Personnel, Sections 5.16 and 5.17; OE-6 Financial Planning, Sections 6.1, 6.2, and 6.3; and OE-7 Financial Administration, Section 7.8.  These priority areas will be monitored yearly at the month identified in the annual work plan calendar and brief updates will also be provided mid-year in June and a year-end report in December.

Subject
5. Direct Superintendent to Develop a Five-Year Community-Based Reform Model Master Plan Which Implements the Board's Vision 2020 for Neighborhood Schools
Meeting
Mar 1, 2011 - Regular Charter & Prop S Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

The board met on February 19, 2011, in a retreat session to discuss governance and board values.  As a result of the board's conversation at the retreat the following direction to the superintendent is recommended for action:

 

Direct Superintendent to develop a master plan that includes details on how to implement Vision 2020 and community-based schools model that includes a quality school for each neighborhood, a full range of programs in each cluster, accountability measures, a zero based review of all functions.     

 

The Superintendent is to develop a five year Community Based Reform Model Master Plan which implements the board’s Vision 2020 for Neighborhood Schools.  The plan will focus on:

 

A)    A quality school available for every student in the neighborhood in which he/she resides

B)    A full range of academic programs offered in each cluster.

 

The strategic plan will require a zero based review of all functions.  This focus on community-based neighborhood schools will also include a system of district-wide accountability measures.

 

Various factors, including, but not limited to, the following should be considered:

  • Transportation adjustments based upon attendance at a quality neighborhood school being the highest priority.
  • Facilities adjustments (expansion, closure, etc.) to meet the needs of the residential students of each neighborhood.
  • Adjustment of special academic programs to include them within each cluster unless logistically impossible for certain one-of-a-kind schools in the district.
  • Retention of school choice, but the priority of the district is to provide a quality school that meets the needs of each neighborhood.
  • Ongoing democratic engagement in each cluster to ensure greater parent, staff and community participation.
  • Emphasis on K-12 alignment in each cluster.
  • Development of plans to address integration/diversity issues as school attendance patterns change.

The Superintendent will bring this plan to the board by September 2011, but will also keep in mind the board’s long-term priorities in the development of the current budget.
