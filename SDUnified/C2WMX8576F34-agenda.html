BACKGROUND: Our vision for summer programming is to accelerate student learning and reimagine enrichment opportunities. San Diego Unified is dedicated to supporting the development of wellbeing, knowledge, skills, and abilities through this summer and beyond.

 

Onsite Morning Academic Program: The onsite morning academic program will meet students where they are, with the purpose of re-engaging and re-igniting a passion for learning.

  • TK-5th students will be grouped by grade level, avoiding combination classes when possible. Learning outcomes will be aligned to individualized foundational literacy and numeracy skills, as well as an integrated approach to social emotional learning.
  • 6th-12th grade students will participate in interdisciplinary hands-on, minds-on projects (English, HSS, Math, and Science) connected to key industry and community partners in San Diego. Student learning outcomes will be personalized first to the students’ credit recovery needs and students interests.
  • Session dates/times: TBD (elementary/middle/high)
    • High School - 6 weeks (two three 3-week sessions), 4 hours/day. Program dates are June 21 - July 29, 2022
    • Middle School- 5 weeks, 4 hours/day (one 1-session). Program dates are June 21 – July 22, 2022.
    • Elementary School- 5 weeks, 4 hours/day (one 1-session). Program dates are June 21 – July 22, 2022.

 

iHigh Virtual Academy Online Program: Our iHigh Virtual Academy Online program will offer online learning academic coursework for grades K-12. Students who choose to participate in the online program will be enrolled in iHigh Virtual Academy for the summer session only. Summer session dates will mirror the same dates as the onsite morning academic program.

 

Proposed 2022 Extended School Year (ESY) Program for Students with Disabilities

The California Code of Regulations Section 3043(f) requires that Extended School Year (ESY) services be provided for each individual with exceptional needs who has unique needs and requires special education and related services in excess of the regular academic year. Such individuals shall have disabilities which are likely to continue indefinitely or for a prolonged period, and interruption of the pupil’s educational programming may cause regression, when coupled with limited recoupment capacity, rendering it impossible or unlikely that the pupil will attain the level of self-sufficiency and independence that would otherwise be expected in view of his or her disabling condition. The lack of clear evidence of such factors may not be used to deny an individual an ESY program if the student’s Individual Education Program (IEP) team determines the need for such a program and includes ESY in the IEP. The proposed program for High School students is June 20, 2022 – July 29, 2022 and for Elementary and Middle School students is from June 20, 2022 – July 22, 2022. Transportation services will be provided for ESY.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of imminent risk of harm to attendees of public meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic which will be effective for 30 days, commencing December 15, 2021.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: Assembly Bill No. 361 (“AB 361”) was enacted by the California Legislature and is intended to “improve and enhance public access to . . . local agency meetings during the COVID-19 pandemic and future applicable emergencies, by allowing broader access through teleconferencing options.” AB 361 permits local legislative bodies subject to the Brown Act and/or the Badgley Keene Act to hold virtual meetings, without needing to publish the physical locations of Board members or make those locations open to the public, if there is a proclaimed state of emergency (pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act) and either: (i) state or local officials have imposed or recommended measures to promote social distancing, or (ii) there is a finding by the local legislative body that, as a result of the emergency, meeting in person “would present imminent risks to the health or safety of attendees.” Governor has proclaimed a state of emergency pursuant to Section 8625 of the California Emergency Services Act. Since the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, in compliance with public health orders and for the protection all involved persons, the Board of Education (“Board”) has held its meetings telephonically via Zoom and provided for public comments to be provided via teleconferencing rather than allowing in-person attendance by the public at the Education Center. The COVID-19 pandemic continues to present an imminent health risk to the public which require certain specific health and safety measures be maintained. The District’s central office facilities present specific challenges in maintaining COVID-19-related safety protocols required for the health and safety of attendees, which include, but are not limited to, adequate ventilation and security, enforcement of masking requirements, and maintenance of recommended social distancing. The health and safety risks are presented by providing public access at the remote locations from which Board members currently attend Board meetings remotely. The Board hereby finds that conducting Board and/or committee meetings in person in light of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic presents an imminent risk to the health and safety of prospective attendees.

BACKGROUND: San Diego Unified School District (“District”) is committed to providing a quality education to our students and preparing them to succeed in today’s economy. Replacing, upgrading and/or building new classrooms and facilities is expensive and state school bond funds provide the opportunity to build safe and modern classrooms that offer the learning opportunities required for a high-quality 21st century education. AB 75, as currently written, authorizes the Kindergarten-Community Colleges Public Education Facilities Bond Act of 2022 (Bond Act of 2022), which provides funding for renovation and improvements of existing classrooms, construction of new classrooms and school facilities, upgrades for HVAC and clean air filters, expansion Career Technical Education facilities to improve job and career training, assistance for small school districts; and interim housing to help Districts impacted by natural disasters. The District a facility program that improves access and increases funding for school construction projects. Investments in modern school facilities are investments in our youth, our communities, our economy, and our shared future. The State of California has committed funds from all previous bond measures and is currently facing an increasing backlog of unfunded needs, currently at over $3 billion dollars. AB 75, the Bond Act of 2022, will not raise individual local property taxes, while at the same time reduces the need for additional local property taxes for school facilities.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: The district is conducting a search process for its next permanent Superintendent. Former Superintendent Cindy Marten was appointed on May 13, 2021 as Deputy Secretary of the U.S. Department of Education. Dr. Lamont Jackson has been appointed as Interim Superintendent through January 31, 2022. 

 

In February 2021, the board adopted a Superintendent Recruitment and Selection Process that included establishment of an Advisory Committee, reflecting the diversity of the district's students and the San Diego Community, and called for community engagement to identify what is most important to stakeholders in selecting the district's next permanent Superintendent.

 

The National Center for Education and the Economy (NCEE) was contracted to facilitate the community input process and gather and compile the data. The process included 34 community input sessions, held from July through September 2021. As noted in NCEE's report, the timing of this work fell largely during the summer, when many families and community members take vacations and typically pay less attention to school-related matters. In addition, a rise in COVID cases throughout the region compelled the district to cancel many in-person activities at district facilities and may have dampened the willingness of some to attend the community input sessions that were held in person. Some community members also were not comfortable sharing their feedback via an online digital platform. Despite lower than hoped for participation, the findings indicate that Equity, A Focus on the Whole Child, and Organizational Structures and Processes are the three main themes most important to district stakeholders.

 

The board approved the Job Posting on October 12, 2021, and the district received applications from qualified candidates through November 11, 2021. The Advisory Committee screened the candidate application packages and recommended up to 10 candidates to be interviewed by the board. Interviews were conducted on December 9, 2021, and the board selected finalists on December 10, 2021. Finalists will be subject to a public engagement process on January 10, 2022. The board will appoint a permanent Superintendent in January 2022. 

 

Information about the Superintendent Search is available on the district's webpage at https://sites.google.com/sandi.net/sdusd-superintendent-search/home. Questions or comments may be sent to superintendentsearch@sandi.net.

The passage of Proposition 39 in November 2000, amended the California Constitution to include accountability provisions. Specifically, the district must conduct an annual independent performance audit to ensure that funds have been expended only on the specific projects listed in the bond project list, as well as an annual independent financial audit of the proceeds from the sale of the bond until all proceeds have been expended for facilities projects.

In its performance audit, Crowe LLP found that "in all significant respects, San Diego Unified School District met the objectives listed . . . and expended Propositions S and Z and Measure YY General Obligation Bond funds for the year ended June 30, 2021, only for the specific projects developed by the district's Governing Board and approved by the voters, in accordance with the requirements of Proposition 39, as specified by Section 1(b)(3)(C) of Article XIIIA of the California Constitution."  This is the highest opinion that can be bestowed on an operational performance of a school bond program.

 

For the thirteenth consecutive year the district's capital bond program has received the highest opinion auditors can offer for both its financial and performance audits.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve Corrected Agreement with Central Dispatch, Inc., to provide comprehensive after-hours fire and intrusion alarm monitoring throughout the district (SV22-0455-36). The term of this Agreement is January 1, 2022 through December 31, 2026.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Total not-to-exceed amount: $900,000.00. Budget String: 5605-00005-00-5100-8300-0000-01000-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: For the period of July 1, 2020 through June 30, 2021: $111,941.68.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The District Police Department deploys officers Monday-Friday, 0700-1700 hours, however, the fire and intrusion alarms require round the clock monitoring. The dispatch center has inadequate staff to be able to monitor the fire and intrusion alarms without significant overtime costs to the district.

 

On July 22, 2021, the district advertised RFI SV21-0395-40 in the Daily Transcript and via PlanetBids™ to 102 vendors to solicit specifications, literature, and samples for After-Hours Alarm Monitoring Services and Future Security Management Systems.

On June 24, 2021, two RFI responses were received, from Central Dispatch, Inc., and Rancho Santa Fe Security Systems, Inc., with information on alarm monitoring and security management that helped determine and develop the scope for RFP SV22-0478-36.

On October 21, 2021, the district advertised RFP SV22-0478-36 in the Daily Transcript and via PlanetBids™ to 115 vendors for District Alarm Monitoring Services.  One proposal was received in response to the RFP from Central Dispatch, Inc. 

After a review of the proposal by district Police and IT staff, it was determined that Central Dispatch, Inc., is capable in providing the necessary district alarm monitoring services and that the rates presented are in line with industry standards and will be cost-effective over the term of the Agreement.

Central Dispatch, Inc., will share, with district School Police Services, in the monitoring of all district fire and intrusion alarms, dispatch on active fire and intrusion alarms, and monitor security surveillance cameras districtwide, during the evenings of Monday through Saturday, from 2200 to 0600 hours, and all day Sundays.
