REVENUE-Low Performing Students Block Grant Funds

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the proposed plan for the use of Low Performing Block Grant funds to increase the academic performance of 1,500 identified students who are not otherwise identified for supplemental grant funding under the Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF), or eligible for special education services. The plan will allow the district  to continue to nurture student academic success by enhancing a culture where students feel safe, valued, cared for, respected, and engaged. Specifically, funds will be used to increase student access to academic supports by nurturing social-emotional learning and behavioral competencies, increase educator capacity by providing tools, skills and resources that build inclusionary multi-tiered systems of support pedagogy, improve student agency and academic skills, and expand collaboration with families to enhance student social-emotional competencies and behavioral and academic outcomes in the home environment.    

 

Plan effectiveness will be measured by student attendance rates, student assessment data in Formative Assessment System for Teachers (FAST), behavioral outcomes including referral, suspension and expulsion data, and Smarter Balanced Assessment results. 

 

The term of the grant is February 26, 2019, to November 1, 2021. Terms of the grant include the requirement that applicant districts provide a board-adopted plan of action for use of the funds by March 1, 2019.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Potential Revenue to the District $2,987,749 over three years

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: 6 FTE Certificated SDEA Resource Teachers and 1 FTE Classified Administrative Aide will be employed during durations of funding terms.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The Low Performing Students Block Grant provides funding for Local Education Agencies (LEA) serving students identified as low performing on State English Language Arts or Math assessments, who are not otherwise identified for supplemental grant funding under the Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF), or eligible for special education services. The proposed plan aligns to the district's Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP). 

 

The plan includes continued support of the important relationship of students' academic achievement and agency development. A strong body of research demonstrates the importance of having safe, collaborative and inclusive school cultures to increase student academic outcomes. Funds will be used to address the persistent achievement gap by providing evidence-based practices that initiate and sustain authentic systemic change.

3. Public Hearing on District's Initial Proposal to Negotiate Special Education Changes with San Diego Education Association (SDEA)

RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and approve the San Diego Unified School District’s initial proposal to negotiate with San Diego Education Association (SDEA) in accordance with section 3457 of the California Government Code and section 1031 of the bylaws of the Board of Education.


The provisions of California Education Code 3457 require that public school employers present proposals which relate to matters within the scope of the representation at a public meeting of the public school employer prior to commencing negotiations.


This constitutes the initial proposal of the District for the purposes of negotiating Special Education changes to the Resource Specialists and Education Specialists-Mild/Moderate positions job descriptions and caseloads to better support student success.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Fiscal impact is not known at this time. The full monetary impact will be known at the conclusion of bargaining when agreements are reached with each of the bargaining units.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Unknown until the completion of bargaining.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: SDEA and its unit members have expressed concerns over the staffing of Special Education and believe there are insufficient resources for our Special Education students to succeed.  The District hears these concerns and, as fellow educators, takes these concerns extremely seriously.  The District is committed to collaborating with SDEA through focused and sustained efforts by both parties to improve our processes and serve our Special Education population.

 

Based on feedback from SDEA members -- and to allow for sincere collaboration, the District halted all changes to the allocations for Resource Specialists and Education Specialists-Mild/Moderate during the Site-Based Budgeting process. As a concrete next step to improve our service to our Special Education students, the District requested mutual agreement from SDEA to come to the bargaining table to negotiate changes to the Resource Specialists and Education Specialists-Mild/Moderate positions regarding job descriptions and caseloads.

 

Upon mutual agreement to bargain, the District will provide a detailed redlined proposal to SDEA at the first bargaining session, however, the following is an overview of the District’s initial proposal:

  • One job description for Resource Specialists/Education Specialists-Mild/Moderate teachers to give our educators the flexibility required to serve our students.
  • A caseload hard cap of 22:1. This caseload change will not result in the layoff of any special education teachers.
  • Caseload overages and monitoring:
    • Beginning seven weeks after the start of the semester:
      • A teacher with a caseload of 23 to 24 students for more than 30 consecutive days will receive a maximum of one $1,000 stipend per semester;
      • If a teacher’s caseload reaches 25 students, a proportional staffing allocation will be provided to the school.
      • Caseloads will be monitored on a regular basis consistent with general education class size monitoring to ensure compliance with the collective bargaining agreement.
      • SDEA will have 24/7 access to the electronic system like they do with the general education class size data reporting system. (The Special Education caseload monitoring system is under development with the goal to have it ready for the beginning of the 2019-2020 school year.)

Additionally, the District makes the following commitments to our Special Education students and educators:

  • The District will commit to providing enhanced opportunities over the next three years for summer professional development designed around the needs of both general and special education where staff can be paid or earn salary credit.
  • The District is committed to strengthening our partnership with SDEA in the Joint Special Education Committee created in Article 29.2 of the collective bargaining agreement by working with SDEA to formalize the collaborative process and communicate decisions to both SDEA membership and site leadership.  

The District is prepared to immediately begin bargaining regarding Resource Specialists and Education Specialists-Mild/Moderate caseloads and job descriptions.  Upon conclusion of this Special Education bargaining, the District is committed to promptly beginning Reopener Negotiations per Section 36.6 of the SDEA Collective Bargaining Agreement.

 

The District is hopeful that the parties will work diligently together to conclude bargaining and reach a timely resolution, ideally by March 18, 2019, so that sites can be provided with their allocations and to ensure that we are able to include accurate positions during the May Post and Bid process.

The Proposed Project also includes additional improvements to the existing campus, including Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and security improvements to the parking lots, facilities, and school perimeter, the replacement of all building windows; the replacement of aging wiring and upgrades to electrical systems, perimeter fencing, as well as adding additional receptacles throughout the campus. CEQA Environmental Review Process:

 

The District has prepared a Draft Environmental Impact Report (DEIR) for the Proposed Project (Project) in accordance with the requirements of CEQA (Public Resources Code Section 21000 et seq.) and CEQA Guidelines (California Code of Regulations Sections 15000 et seq.) The District issued a Notice of Preparation (NOP) to prepare a DEIR on October 5, 2018 and the NOP was available for public review and comment through November 5, 2018. A Notice of Availability (NOA) of the DEIR was published in the San Diego Union-Tribune and the La Prensa Spanish language newspaper. All identified mitigation measures and the analysis of the impacts after mitigation are discussed in the Mitigation Monitoring and Reporting Program (MMRP-Attachment 3).

 

Written Findings (Attachment 4) have been prepared for each of the significant effects.
