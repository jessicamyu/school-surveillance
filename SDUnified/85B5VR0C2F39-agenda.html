C.01. Consideration of the Renewal Petition and Charter for McGill School of Success Charter; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-2)

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the renewal of McGill School of Success Charter School.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education approve the renewal charter petition for Chancellor William McGill School of Success Charter School (McGill) for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2010, and expiring on June 30, 2015, retroactive to the expiration of the current charter petition. The Office of Charter Schools recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the Board’s decision to renew the charter petition. McGill was originally approved to be a charter school on September 12, 1995, by the Board of Education and has been operating as a charter school that offers Grades K-2 in San Diego Unified since the fall of 1996. For the renewal charter petition, the charter indicates it can offer a K-3 grade configuration. The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective immediately and will expire on June 30, 2015. The potential fiscal impact to the district is an estimated loss of $720,000 generated in average daily attendance (ADA) in 2010-11 because the district does not receive any ADA funds for students enrolled at charter schools. District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met requirements to continue performing as a charter school in the district.

If the Board of Education desires additional options beyond approval, these options could be:
  • Approve the charter petition with conditions. In essence, the petition is approved with conditions set by the Board of Education.
  • Deny the charter petition. The Board of Education would need to identify written findings to support a decision to deny the charter renewal.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Special Education Memoranda of Understanding with the following Charter Schools:
    1. King Chavez Arts Academy
    2. King Chavez Athletics Academy
    3. King Chavez Community High School
    4. King Chavez Excellence
    5. King Chavez Preparatory Academy
    6. King Chavez Primary
    7. Museum Charter School
    8. San Diego Cooperative Charter School
    9. Harriet Tubman Village Charter School

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: On December 1, 2009, the Board of Education approved a template Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) setting forth the terms and conditions under which the district and district authorized charter schools will interact with each other regarding Special Education. In collaboration with charter schools, the district Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) template to outline the relationship between the district and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services. Under the collaboratively-developed MOU, charter schools will have the following three options for the delivery of Special Education services.
    1. Charter schools elect to be considered as a public school of the district for the purposes of special education services and funding. The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the district, in return, the district will provide special education services.
    2. Charter Schools are designated as schools within the district Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA). With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the district, and the district will reimburse the charter school for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the district for equivalent service providers.
    3. Charter schools are designated as schools of another SELPA. This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified. Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served. The district retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.
