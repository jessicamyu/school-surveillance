RENEWAL VERSUS REVISION

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.

 

Within a charter term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, reassignment of special education responsibilities, expansion of grade levels, or other significant change.

 

CONCLUSION

Staff recommends the board approve the material revision to the KIPP Adelante Preparatory Academy charter petition to amend the bylaws and add an administrative space located at 1933 S. Broadway, #1144, Los Angeles, CA 90007, effective July 1, 2022.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve First Amendments to Agreements with College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) and Accelerated College Program (ACP) with the San Diego Community College District for college courses for district high school students; San Diego City College (SV22-0027-37), San Diego Mesa College (SV22-0028-37), San Diego Miramar College (SV22-0029-37), and San Diego Mesa College ACP (SV22-0030-37). Authorize the execution of any documents necessary to effectuate these Agreements. The revised term of these Agreements is July 1, 2021 to June 30, 2023.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On April 14, 2020, the board approved $600,000.00 to be allocated for this effort under the Instructional Materials/Lottery Funds. The district provides instructional materials for district high school students enrolled in community college courses with SDCCD.


On July 28, 2020, the board approved the CCAP Partnership Agreement (SV21-0263-92) between SDCCD and the district allowing district high school students to receive college units and take college courses.


On July 28, 2020, the board approved the Memorandum of Understanding (SV21-0276-92), with San Diego Community College District that is used as a Master Agreement for programs in which district high school students participate in classes on district campuses, and by which all individual educational partnerships are authorized.


On July 13, 2021, the board ratified four Agreements, San Diego City College (SV22-0027-37), San Diego Mesa College (SV22-0028-37), San Diego Miramar College (SV22-0029-37), and San Diego Mesa College ACP (SV22-0030-37), with College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) and Accelerated College Program (ACP) with the San Diego Community College District for college courses.

 

These First Amendments will continue to allow district high school students to take college-level courses and accelerated college-level programs for an additional one year through June 30, 2023.
 

CCAP will continue to be offered at San Diego Unified School District high schools as identified below:

 

San Diego City College will provide college courses at Crawford, East Village, Garfield, Hoover, Lincoln, Morse, School of Creative and Performing Arts (SCPA), and San Diego High Schools.


San Diego Mesa College will provide college courses at Clairemont, Hoover, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Mission Bay, Henry, and Point Loma High Schools.


San Diego Miramar College will provide college courses at Mira Mesa, Scripps Ranch, Canyon Hills (formerly Serra), and University City high schools.


San Diego Mesa College Accelerated College Program (ACP) for Mathematics and Political Science college courses will be provided at Clairemont, Henry, Kearny, La Jolla, Madison, Morse, Point Loma, and University City High Schools.
 

Education Code Section 76004 authorizes community college and K-12 Districts to enter into College and Career Access Pathways (CCAP) partnerships with the goal of developing pathways from high school to community college for career technical or preparation for transfer, improving high school graduation rates, or helping high school students achieve college and career readiness. Through dual enrollment partnerships, school districts and community college districts can create clear pathways of aligned, sequenced coursework that allow students to more easily and successfully transition to for-credit, college-level coursework leading to an associate degree, transfer to the University of California (UC) or California State University (CSU) systems, or to a program leading to a career technical education certificate.

BACKGROUND: On March 16, 2022, the district's Mental Health Services Department responded to a Request for Proposal advertised by the County of San Diego Health and Human Services Department to provide Prevention and Early Intervention (PEI) services.  The district Mental Health Services Department was awarded Lot 1 (County Contract No. 566987) with an estimated revenue of $5,555,090.00 and Lot 2  (County Contract No. 5669980) with an estimated revenue of $6,261,770.00 for a total possible revenue of $11,816,860.00.

 

 

The Prevention and Early Intervention (PEI) program is one component of the Mental Health Services Act (MHSA) approved by California voters in November 2004.  The County of San Diego Health and Human Services Agency (HHSA) through the MHSA expanded outpatient and school based programs to Seriously Emotionally Disturbed (SED) children, youth, and their families that do not have Medi-Cal or private insurance.  PEI services promote wellness and healthy living choices that foster resiliency for the broader community.  PEI targets children and families at risk of developing issues and those that do not meet threshold criteria for receiving mental health services.

 

This social-emotional mental health evidence-based PEI contract shall target identified district school campuses for children in grades preschool through third grade in the San Diego County Central Southeastern regional area and associated communities.  This program will be delivered by Mental Health Resource clinicians in conjunction with school teachers to promote positive behaviors, conflict resolution, positive peer relations and will contain a structured parenting component.
