RECOMMENDATION:  Approve collaboration with Panasonic Foundation and Perry & Associates in the Lincoln and Morse clusters to help accelerate the district efforts to improve student achievement;

 

Approve agreement with the Panasonic Foundation and Perry & Associates, effective October 15, 2010 through September 30, 2013, to provide assistance to the district in its efforts to improve student achievement at up to eight district schools in the Lincoln and Morse clusters.  

 

FISCAL IMPACT: There is no cost to the district.  Panasonic Foundation/Perry & Associates services will be funded through the Panasonic Foundation with resources provided by the Sidney E. Frank Foundation.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  The Panasonic Foundation and Perry & Associates will work with the Superintendent, Deputy Superintendent Academics, Assistant Superintendent of Instructional Support Services, Area 1 Superintendent and Assistant Superintendent, and the principals/teachers at up to eight district schools in the Lincoln and Morse clusters.  

 

BACKGROUND: The partnership work will support implementation of the district's mission, the board-adopted Goals for Student Achievement in Academics, and other district academic initiatives. Selection of up to eight schools in the Lincoln and Morse clusters will be based upon the expressed interest of the principal and area superintendent.  The work in these schools will include:

  1. Supporting principals in strengthening instruction so that all students complete A-G requirements and are prepared for success in college and career, in areas of community-based school reform including the effective use of professional learning communities, data driven instruction, parent engagement and in integration of multiple initiatives and resources into an aligned and articulated school improvement strategy (using the school's Single Plan for Student Achievement as a starting point);
     
  2. Coaching and direct instructional support to teacher leaders, particularly in the areas of English Language Arts and Mathematics, and in the design and delivery of professional development;
     
  3. Facilitating cross-school learning in cohort groups; and
     
  4. Assisting Area Superintendents and central office leaders in identifying key indicators of student success consistent with board-adopted goals and best practices within schools, and in sharing best practices with schools both within and across clusters.

To measure the impact/added value of the partnership, the Panasonic Foundation/Perry & Associates will work with the Superintendent and central office leaders to:

  1. Jointly develop specific annual achievable results, which clearly identify the strategies, actions, indicators of success and deliverables for each of the major areas of the partnership work.  The achievable results include action plans, impact on student achievement, impact on climate and customer satisfaction surveys that determine the partnership's value to district and school work;
     
  2. Jointly agree to a year-to-year continuation based upon annual review of the progress in reaching the indicators of success for the major areas of work; and
     
  3. Jointly participate in a formal annual partnership review to determine attainment of achievable results and indicators of success.  Formal review will be completed by August 30 of each partnership year with results to be submitted to the Panasonic Foundation, Superintendent, and Board of Education in September of each partnership year.
