Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to each contractor.

Established in 1919, the JROTC program has benefited tens of thousands of our district students, helping them achieve academically, socially, and personally. Oversight and accountability for JROTC in the district is the responsibility of the Office of College, Career & Technical Education (CCTE).

  • To appreciate the ethical values and principles that underlie good citizenship.
  • To develop leadership potential, while living and working cooperatively with others.
  • To be able to think logically and to communicate effectively with others, both orally and in writing.
  • To appreciate the importance of physical fitness in maintaining good health.
  • To understand the importance of high school graduation for a successful future, and learn about college and other advanced educational and employment opportunities.
  • To develop mental management abilities.
  • To become familiar with military history as it relates to America's culture, and understand the history, purpose, and structure of military services.
  • To develop the skills necessary to work effectively as a member of a team.

The district’s JROTC programs delivered through the Office of College, Career & Technical Education provide outstanding opportunities for student development and achievement, offer a significant return on capital investment, and are a net positive revenue resource. It is a tested and proven program that increases student key performance indicators and post-secondary options. It is a leadership development program that mentors and develops students. It is administered by men and women that honorably served our country for more than twenty years and have continued their service through the education profession; preparing students for their future—college and career ready.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On December 1, 2009, the Board of Education approved a template Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) setting forth the terms and conditions under which the district and district authorized charter schools will interact with each other regarding Special Education.  In collaboration with charter schools, the Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education MOU template to outline the relationship between the district and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services.  Under the MOU that was collaboratively developed, charter schools will have three options for the delivery of Special Education services at charter schools.  The options are the following:

  1. Charter schools elect to be considered as a public school of the district for the purposes of special education services and funding.  The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the district, and the district will provide special education services.
  2. Charter schools are designated as schools within the district Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA).  With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the district, and the district will reimburse the charter school for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the district for equivalent service providers.
  3. Charter schools are designated as schools of another SELPA.  This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified.  Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served.  The district retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.

Staff recommends that the board approve the Special Education MOU with America’s Finest Charter School, with the understanding that each individually signed Special Education MOU will return for board approval when the charter school is renewed or there is a change in status.
