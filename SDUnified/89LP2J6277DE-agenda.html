FISCAL IMPACT: Agreement amount is not to exceed $12,200.00. Budget string: 5579-00000-00-5853-7100-0000-01000-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Services will be provided by Malachied, Inc. dba as The Breakthrough Coach.  The Area 9 Superintendent will provide ongoing coaching and support regarding the content of the two-day, research-based professional development seminar.

 

BACKGROUND: The Breakthrough Coach (TBC) is a two-day, research-based professional development seminar for teams of administrators and their secretaries.

 

Area 9 administrators have collectively articulated a desire to deepen their knowledge of management tools and processes to maintain a sensible work week by increasing personal productivity and efficiency.  Data from two surveys distributed this academic year (i.e., Area 9 Administrators Survey and Area 9 School and Community Survey) suggest that administrators endeavor to spend more time in classrooms and better manage their day-to-day schedules and paperwork in order to make a difference in student learning and achievement.  Faculty, staff, and students who responded to the School and Community Survey, reflect that many of their administrators arrive to school at or before dawn, remain well after the staff leaves for the day, or come to the office on the weekends so they can get the work done.

 

Administrators who participate in the two-day, research-based professional development seminar will develop (or refine) their school-based practices for:

  • Employing best practices for using administrative support (secretaries/administrative assistants);
  • Organizing their schools to produce breakthroughs in student achievement.
  • Observing classroom instruction multiple times each week;

The ultimate goal of the two-day, research-based professional development seminar is an emphasis on re-casting the role of the principal as the instructional leader of a school.  For those who focus on instruction, the impact on student achievement is overwhelmingly positive:

  • Administrators are able to head off issues before they arise;
  • Administrators interact effectively with parents/guardians, who were thrilled that the administrators actually know their children;
  • Administrators are able to build respect and trust because they are in the classrooms several times each week, they know what was going on, and know all their students and teachers.
  • Student achievement increases because administrators are actively monitoring the work.

Malachied, Inc. dba as The Breakthrough Coach agrees to perform the following services:

  • Facilitate a two-day, research-based professional development seminar for school administrators and their secretaries. The seminar will include lecture, small group discussion, questions-and-responses, and hands-on implementation.
  • Provide course support materials for each participant team.

BACKGROUND: California Education Code Section 44253.1 requires all teachers to have an active and valid English Learner authorization if assigned to a classroom that has one or more students designated as an English Learner. Because of the Williams Settlement legislation, a review of this Education Code has determined that the need for an English Learner authorization also applies to all Career Technical Education (CTE) and Junior Reserve Officers' Training Corps (JROTC) certificated staff employed by the district. Teachers who hold a bachelor’s degree and completed an approved teacher preparation program leading to the issuance of a regular education credential or special education credential, but did not hold an English Learner authorization, qualify for an Emergency Crosscultural Language Academic Development (CLAD) Permit to cover them in their assignments while completing the requirements to obtain a Clear CLAD Certificate.
 

Due to the fact that Career Technical Education and JROTC teachers are not required to hold a bachelor’s degree or complete an approved teacher preparation program to be issued a Designated Subjects Teaching Credential by the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing, this group of district teachers do not qualify for an Emergency CLAD Permit. On March 19, 2008, the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing announced its approval of SB 1292 giving all holders of Designated Subjects Teaching Credentials an option to obtain an English Learner authorization. Prior to this approval, there was no legal option available to the holder of a Designated Subjects Teaching Credential to be issued any form of English Learner authorization. On September 26, 2008, the San Diego County Office of Education (COE) began the first session of training for qualifying teachers employed in the county.

After working with the COE, the district has been advised that all Career Technical Education and JROTC teachers will need to apply for CLAD Waivers with the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing in order not to be considered mis-assigned for the 2010-11 academic school year. These waivers will be valid for the 2010-11 school year, but can be renewed for an additional year in the event the COE cannot accommodate the county’s entire population of teachers who need to complete the training.

 

Pursuant to CTC leaflet CL-622 (9/10), Emergency CLAD permits may be reissued to an applicant four times authorizing a maximum of five years of service to complete the requirements for the English Learner/CLAD Certificate.

 

CTC has not determined a similar strategy for reissuing the CLAD waiver while an applicant waits to be accepted into a CTC-approved staff development program to complete the requirements for the Certificate of Completion of Staff Development (Specially Designed Academic Instruction in English--SDAIE).

 

The new CTE program standards, which went into effect September 1, 2010, include training for the instruction of English Learners that is limited to providing SDAIE services.  The implementation process has begun and will conclude September 1, 2013.  Current Designated Subjects Credential holders will have until June 30, 2013 to complete the English Learner requirement.  Current applications requiring the Certificate of Staff Completion (SDAIE) may need to apply for a CLAD waiver as many as three times before the implementation process has been completed depending on the availability of a CTC-approved staff development program.  CCTE projects the number of applicants who will need to apply for an annual CLAD waiver during the implementation period to be 25 and decreasing each year until the implementation application process closes June 30, 2013.

  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located with the district
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens' organization
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization.

The current Bylaws include the above requirements and additionally require that one ICOC member have previously been a member of the Prop MM ICOC.  The requirement for previous service on the Prop MM ICOC will be eliminated under the proposed amendment to the Bylaws. 

 

Under the proposed amendment, while meeting the above statutory requirements, district staff recommends that eight of the ICOC members be selected from the following labor organizations, professional organizations, trade associations and public sector organizations whose members perform their duties in the commercial construction industry or organizations in the public sector that execute large scale capital programs.  Inclusion of individuals having these skills will contribute to the efficient and effective execution of program. 

  • Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers 
  • American Institute of Architects
  • Association of General Contractors
  • The American Institute of Certified Public Accounting/CalCPA
  • Construction Managers Association of America
  • San Diego Regional Chamber of Commerce
  • San Diego and Imperial County Building and Construction Trades Council
  • City of San Diego, County of San Diego, State of California, or Federal Agency Representatives

The Resolution Clarifying the Statutory Duties of the ICOC is intended to provide further clarity to the ICOC members, the board, district staff and members of the community regarding the powers and duties assigned to the ICOC under Proposition 39; thereby increasing the efficiency and effectiveness of the ICOC.  In specific, the document includes language that details duties of the Committee, authorized activities of the Committee, and authority specifically reserved by the Board of Education.  While in general, these items are outlined in the governing statutes in the California Constitution (Article XIIIA), and are required under Proposition 39, the district believes it is productive to clarify these duties and activities in a manner consistent with applicable laws.

BACKGROUND:  On September 9, 2008, the board adopted, and on September 29, 2009, re-adopted, revised Board Governance Policies which include Operational Expectations.  Beginning in 2010, a two-step process was implemented for monitoring of compliance with Board Governance Policies.  Step 1 is for staff to provide proposed interpretations and indicators for board review and approval.  Step 2 is the presentation of a monitoring report for each Operational Expectation, with compliance measured based on the approved interpretations and indicators. 

 

On March 16, 2010, the Board of Education approved the interpretations and indicators for OE-1, and directed staff to come back to the board with a monitoring report based thereon.  OE-1 is the overarching expectation that the Superintendent take reasonable measures to ensure that all practices, activities, decisions and organizational conditions are lawful, ethical, safe, respectful, prudent, consistent with board policy and protect the organization's public image and credibility.  Since OE-1 is the global expectation for the district within the Coherent Governance framework, the primary indicator of compliance with OE-1 is the degree of compliance with all of the board’s other Operational Expectations, as determined through the OE Monitoring Reports and board decisions regarding those reports.  Therefore, the OE-1 report is the final report in the series and incorporates the results of each of the preceding reports. 

 

It is the board's responsibility to consider the monitoring report as evidence of district performance in this area of operations.  The board policy provides two criteria against which they are to judge the report:  --

  1. Whether the board is satisfied that the district and the superintendent are in compliance with the provisions of the policy or have disclosed procedures to establish compliance; and
  2. Whether the board is satisfied that the information is sufficient to allow the board to decide.
