BACKGROUND: Staff recommends granting the charter revision for Ingenuity Charter School to add a resource center at 8300 Paradise Valley Road, Suite 104, effective July 1, 2017, and operate under the state’s ASAM designation as set forth by the California Department of Education’s criteria.

 

The charter petition for Ingenuity Charter School was originally approved for a five-year term beginning July 1, 2015, and ending June 30, 2020.  The charter school currently serves Grades 6-12 at the following locations.

  • 6130 Skyline Drive, 92114 (located on O’Farrell campus) (Lincoln boundaries)
  • 342 Euclid Avenue, #401, 92114 (Lincoln boundaries)
  • 6530 El Cajon Boulevard #3, 92115 (Crawford/Henry boundaries)

The new site, located at 8300 Paradise Valley Road, is located within the attendance boundaries of Freese Elementary, Bell Middle, and Morse High.  The proposed Paradise Valley location would be Ingenuity’s fourth resource center.  Ingenuity plans to operate a maximum of five resources centers.

California's 1999 Public Schools Accountability Act (PSAA) requires that all schools be held accountable under systems developed by the California Department of Education (CDE).  Accordingly, California Education Code section 52052[g] requires that “[t]he Superintendent, with the approval of the state board, shall develop an alternative accountability system for schools under the jurisdiction of a county board of education or a county superintendent of schools,…and alternative schools serving high-risk pupils, including continuation high schools and opportunity schools…”

 

The CDE designates schools as ASAM if the school meets the established criteria.  A school is eligible to apply for the ASAM if it is explicitly defined in the Education Code as an Alternative School, or if it is an Other Alternative School that serves high-risk students, but is not explicitly required to so.  This latter category includes charter schools.  To be eligible for ASAM, a charter school must have at least 70 percent of the school’s total enrollment comprised of high-risk groups, including the following:

  • Expelled (including situations in which the expulsion order was suspended)
  • Suspended more than 10 days in a school year
  • Wards of the Court (WIC Section 601 or 602) or dependents of the court (WIC Section 300 or 654)
  • Pregnant and/or Parenting
  • Recovered Dropouts
  • Habitually Truant (EC Section 48262) or Habitually Insubordinate and Disorderly whose attendance at the school is directed by a school attendance review board or probation officer (EC Section 48263)
  • Retained More Than Once in kindergarten through grade eight

RENEWAL VERSUS REVISION

 

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.  Within a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, reassignment of special education responsibilities, expansion of grade levels, or other significant change.
