BACKGROUND:  On November 6, 2012, residents of San Diego voted to approve Proposition Z to fund capital improvement projects at district owned schools.  In an effort to provide a quality school in every neighborhood, the district is using Proposition  Z funds to repair, renovate and revitalize district schools.  Bond projects include classroom technology; safety and security upgrades; Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) upgrades; new/renovated facilities for College, Career and Technical Education facilities; temporary classrooms replaced by permanent classrooms; air conditioning; ADA upgrades and improvements to athletic facilities; turf fields; and other capital improvements at district and charter schools throughout the district.  

 

Currently, the district has only one swimming pool (on La Jolla High School) which was built with private funds.  There is a significant need for swimming pool facilities to serve district sports teams as well as for neighborhood recreational uses.  In recent years there have been significant cutbacks in the operational hours of municipal pools.  Pool operations and maintenance require significant ongoing funds, which San Diego Unified does not have nor is likely to have in the foreseeable future.  

 

On May 28, 2013, at a publicly noticed meeting, the Board of Education unanimously adopted an action directing staff to issue a Request for Proposals with interested organizations for the construction, operations and maintenance of up to 10 swimming pool facilities. 

 

On May 1, 2014, the district publicly advertised Request for Qualifications/Proposals SV-15-0040-08  for Funding, Design, Construction, Maintenance and/or Operations of Swimming Pool Facilities at Various Sites within San Diego Unified School District.  The advertisement appeared in news publications of general circulation, and was posted on DemandStar.

 

Two firms responded by the closing date of May 29, 2014, the YMCA of San Diego County and Ruhnau Ruhnau Clarke and Associates, and were evaluated by an Evaluation Committee on the basis of best value, including technical ability, benefit to district students and overall ability to perform.  On the basis of its work, the Evaluation Committee recommends that the district enter into a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the YMCA of San Diego County. Opportunities for pool and athletic facilities partnerships with the YMCA, are detailed in Exhibit A to the MOU.

 

The MOU establishes the framework for implementing the development of pool and recreational facilities at the identified sites/school clusters: Clairemont/Madison, Crawford/Morse, Hoover, Kearny, Lincoln, Mira Mesa, Patrick Henry, Point Loma, San Diego, and Scripps Ranch/Serra.  The district and the YMCA anticipate conducting site visits within 90 days of executing the MOU.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve Resolution of Intent to enter into Agreement with the YMCA of San Diego County for construction, operation, maintenance, and lease of joint occupancy facilities at Pacific Beach Middle School (SV-15-0750-02).  The Resolution provides the Agreement itself will be effective without further action of the board upon approval by the State Board of Education.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Estimated cost of $2 million for construction of synthetic turf field and parking lot (Propositions S and Z Funds). No cost to the district for construction of the pool and community recreational facilities.   

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

BACKGROUND: District staff has been in discussion with the Peninsula Family YMCA (YMCA) since 2009 to develop a joint occupancy facility on the Pacific Beach Middle School campus. On August 2, 2011, the board adopted a “Resolution of Intention to Consider Proposals to Lease Property for Joint Occupancy for Development, Operation, and Maintenance of Recreation Facilities, and a Community Center at Pacific Beach Middle School.” On December 13, 2011, the board received and accepted a single proposal from the YMCA and district staff thereafter entered into discussion with the YMCA regarding the terms and conditions pursuant to which the facilities will be financed, constructed, operated and maintained. 

 

Under the original proposal accepted by the board, it was contemplated that the YMCA would construct, at its expense, a synthetic turf field and parking lot and would remodel an existing building on the campus to be converted into a community recreational facility. In subsequent conversations with YMCA, it was determined that the facilities will be constructed in two phases. In Phase I,  the district will construct at its expense the synthetic turf field, with a surrounding track, and the parking lot. This work will be part of the Whole Site Modernization completed for Propositions S and Z. In Phase II, YMCA will construct, at its expense, the community recreational center ("Recreation Center"); which will include a new building (rather than converting the existing building) and, additionally, a pool. 

 

Under the proposed Agreement, the district will lease the portion of the Pacific Beach MS site on which the Recreation Center will be constructed, to the YMCA and the YMCA, at its expense, will construct, operate and maintain the Recreation Center for the benefit of district students and the community. YMCA will also have use of the field during non-school hours. The Agreement provides a framework for the joint occupancy of the facilities and will be further developed once the district's program needs are more fully identified. The district will have use of the joint facilities other than the community center during the school’s hours of operation and the YMCA will have use of the joint facilities during periods when the school is not in session. The Recreation Center will be separated from the school campus by a physical barrier and locked gate.

 

The term of the initial Agreement is 40 calendar years effective on the Commencement Date.  The Agreement provides for an option to extend the term an additional 25 years with terms and conditions and subject to board approval at the time the option is exercised. Either party may terminate the Agreement with 90 days written notice to the other party to be effective at the end of the fiscal year in which the agreement to terminate is made.

 

Pursuant to California Education Code section 17518(b), the building housing the Recreation Center, together with its contents, will be the property of the YMCA for the term of the Agreement. Upon termination of the Agreement for any reason whatsoever, title to the Recreation Center as well as the fixtures and furnishings, without compensation to YMCA, will automatically vest to district free and clear.

 

The State Board of Education, pursuant to California Education Code 17524(a) approved the 2011 Joint Occupancy Agreement with the Peninsula Family YMCA at its March 2012 meeting. However, since the project has now changed, staff believes the project must be resubmitted to the State Board of Education for approval.  The Agreement will be taken back to the State Board of Education for approval in spring 2015.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the revision to the charter petition of Elevate Elementary Charter School to move to 2285 Murray Ridge Road, San Diego, California 92123.  Elevate seeks to begin instructing students at the permanent location beginning January 5, 2015, through the end of its current charter term on June 30, 2019.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Staff recommends approval of a revision to the charter of Elevate Elementary Charter School to move from its current temporary location at 6845 University Avenue, San Diego, California 92115 (Crawford High cluster) to its proposed permanent location at 2285 Murray Ridge Road, San Diego, California 92123 (Kearny High cluster).  Both are non-district sites. 

 

Elevate began serving Grades K-5 at its temporary location on September 2, 2014.  Elevate seeks to begin instructing students at the permanent location beginning January 5, 2015, through the end of its current charter term on June 30, 2019. 

 

Supporting documents from Elevate, including the Certificate of Occupancy, are attached.

 

California Education Code section 47605(a)(4) requires a charter school that proposes to establish operations at one or more additional sites to request a revision to its charter and notify the authority that granted its charter of those additional locations.  The authority that granted its charter shall consider whether to approve those additional locations at an open, public meeting.  If the additional location is approved, it shall be a revision to the charter schools charter.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve Lease Agreement with Little Italy Association of San Diego (LIA) for the surplus portion of Amici Park (SV-15-0743-02).  ITEM REQUIRES 4 VOTES.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Estimated $78,077.00 in annual savings to the district. LIA will be responsible for the Maintenance Assessment District (MAD) fees, park utility costs, and maintenance costs at Amici Park and Washington Elementary School park grounds. The City of San Diego annually invoices for the Little Italy Economic Development MAD which is currently billed at $8,907, and increases each year per the agreement.  The estimate of annual utility costs is $11,170.00.  The estimate for the annual park maintenance is $58,000.00.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  On July 29, 2014, the Advisory Committee on the Utilization of Excess School Property (ACUESP) recommended that a 0.69 acre portion of Amici Park be declared as surplus.  At the September 26, 2014 and October 24, 2014 meetings, in its review of the Property Management Plan, the ACUESP indicated that they preferred the property be leased.

 

BACKGROUND:  On July 29, 2014, the Board of Education approved the declaration of 0.69 acre portion of Amici Park as surplus and recommended that staff commence the process for disposition. District staff first offered the property to public agencies and other entities as required by Education Code Section 17464.  On September 15, 2014, LIA, a non-profit, public benefit corporation, submitted a letter of interest in leasing the site. District staff has negotiated the attached lease agreement, which has a 66-year term.

 

On January 14, 2014, the district entered into a license agreement with LIA to bifurcate Amici Park; one portion of the park will continue to be used by Washington Elementary School and other Civic Center permitted use, and the second portion will be used by LIA for its events/activities (off-lease dog park and bocce ball court). The license agreement allows for LIA to construct restrooms, a concession stand, maintenance building and other public improvements.

 

On May 23, 2014, the district entered into an amendment to the license agreement authorizing LIA to move an historic house onto the leased property. The amendment also addressed several other legal and logistical conditions related to the placement of the house.  he house will contain the restrooms called out in the license agreement.

 

On October 27, 2014, the district entered into an agreement with the Fenton Little Italy, LLC which allowed the temporary placement of the historical house on the surplus portion of Amici Park until permanent construction improvements can be made. The historical house will be placed on temporary footings, while waiting for building permits to be processed. The lease agreement will formalize the terms under which the district agrees to construction of permanent improvements. 

 

The Washington Elementary School Principal and LIA have met to discuss the in-kind details for the lease. Approval of this item authorizes the Director, Real Estate, to conduct further negotiations, to modify non-material terms of the lease with said modifications being subject to approval as to form and legality by General Counsel and to execute the Agreement on behalf of the district.
