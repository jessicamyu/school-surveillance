RECOMMENDATION: Approval of the revision of the charter of Ingenuity Charter School to operate an additional site at 342 Euclid Avenue, San Diego, 92102, beginning with the 2015-16 academic year.


FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: Beginning with the 2015-16 academic year, Ingenuity Charter School, a new charter school serving grades 6-12 through an independent study, non-classroom based program, is seeking to locate an additional site at a privately-owned facility at 342 Euclid Avenue, San Diego, 92102.  As stated in the charter petition, Ingenuity Charter School will also operate a site on the O’Farrell Charter School campus at 6130 Skyline Drive, San Diego, 92114.  Both sites are located in the Lincoln cluster.  The charter was approved by the Board of Education on November 18, 2014.  Supporting documentation, including the completed Charter Revision Form, and the Charter Facility Confirmation Form are attached.

 

Legal Authority: California Education Code section 47605(a)(4) requires a charter school that proposes to establish operations at one or more additional sites to request a material revision to its charter and notify the authority that granted its charter of those additional locations.  The authority that granted its charter shall consider whether to approve those additional locations at an open, public meeting.  If the additional locations are approved, they shall be a material revision to the charter school’s charter.

 

Staff Analysis: Staff has reviewed the documentation submitted by Ingenuity Charter School.  Ingenuity Charter School intends to enroll approximately 20 students at the Euclid Avenue site during the 2015-16 year, and the remainder of its students at the Skyline Drive site.  The table below summarizes the total projected enrollment for both sites.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve revised Construction Bond Project Plan under Propositions S and Z, and adopt resolution approving revised Propositions S and Z project plan and projects undertaken pursuant to Propositions S and Z as shown on Attachments 1 and 2.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  $195 million for FY 2013-14.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Ongoing - The Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee last met on May 21, 2015.

 

BACKGROUND:  This update addresses program and project milestones and provides an overview of the current program.  Propositions S and Z, general obligation bond measures to improve every school in the district, were approved by the voters on November 4, 2008 and November 6, 2012.  On December 3, 2013, the board approved the Proposition Z Two-Year Plan for implementing projects identified in the Bond Project List and included estimated project schedules.  At that same meeting, the board also reviewed a proposed Proposition S two-year plan.  The board approved subsequent updates to the Propositions S and Z Bond Project Plan on June 3, 2014 and December 2, 2014.  This agenda item requests board action to approve updates to the Propositions S and Z Bond Project Plan, which includes reference to the voter guide for projects.

Type
Information

RECOMMENDATION: Receive report from Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee (ICOC).  Chair, Andy Berg, will present the Committee's semi-annual update to the board and speak to the following topics:

  • Update on ICOC Committee Activities
  • District School Facilities Condition
  • Swimming Pools MOU
  • Athletic Facilities
  • Legal Questions

BACKGROUND: Proposition S, a $2.1 billion general obligation bond measure, was approved by the voters on November 4, 2008. On November 6, 2012, Proposition Z, a $2.8 billion general obligation bond measure was approved by voters.

 

The Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee was established to ensure Propositions S and Z funds are properly expended; review Propositions S and Z financial audits; review district deferred maintenance plans; and review district efforts to maximize Propositions S and Z revenues.
