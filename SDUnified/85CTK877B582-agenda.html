4. Plans for Small High Schools--San Diego and Crawford High School Complexes
Meeting
May 25, 2010 - Special Meeting, 9:00 A.M.
Type
Information

RECOMMENDATION: Approve new operational plans for the San Diego and Crawford Educational Complexes as developed by the school leadership, faculty, staff, and parent community with the goal of refocusing educational programs to increase student achievement and creating operational efficiencies to achieve budget savings of $295,728. 

 

Today's workshop session is for information only.  Any proposed actions related to this item will be considered for action by the board on Thursday, May 27, 2010.


FISCAL IMPACT: On February 23, 2010, the board approved targeted savings of $295,728 from the consolidation of ten small high schools at the San Diego and Crawford Educational Complexes. On March 9, 2010, the board clarified its position and approved targeted savings of $295,728 from new operational plans to be developed by each Educational Complex.  This recommendation retains that projected budget savings be included in budget projections for the 2010-11 budget.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: The two Educational Complexes have developed new operational plans.  Additional plans have been developed by district staff.  Some plans include staffing adjustments.

BACKGROUND:  On June 16, 2009, the board discussed the district’s small high schools. Topics discussed by individual board members included:

  • Refrain from making decisions about structure of small high schools for the 2009-2010 academic year.
  • Permit minor administrative adjustments that do not impact the integrity of the school.
  • Avoid pitting small high schools against each other.
  • Improve the quality of unsuccessful small high schools.
  • Leave successful small high schools alone.
  • Consider converting/combining small high schools; for example moving from four schools to three or two on a complex.

The board reached consensus and offered direction to the superintendent to critically examine small high schools without making drastic changes for the 2009-2010 academic year.

 

Following the Budget Workshop on February 9, 2010, district staff were directed to brainstorm ideas for small high school consolidation. On February 18 and 22, 2010, the Interim Superintendent and district staff met with the principals of the San Diego and Crawford Educational Complexes, respectively, to discuss the idea of each community developing a plan to address each Educational Complex’s academic performance needs and effective/efficient operations. 


On March 3 and 15, 2010, respectively, Board Members Barrera and Jackson, together with the Interim Superintendent and district staff, held meetings with the faculty, staff, and administration of the Crawford and San Diego Educational Complexes.  The goal of these meetings was to articulate the board’s intent that each complex would be charged with developing a new operational plan to achieve budget savings of $295,728 (i.e., $29,572.80 per small high school) and strengthen overall focus on increasing student achievement levels.

 

The San Diego and Crawford Educational Complexes established project teams and sub-teams to examine each small high school at the Educational Complex utilizing various data sources to determine options that are more efficient and/or more effective; consider best instructional practices to inform possible scenarios; retool/revamp bell schedules, support services, administrative structures, course offerings, etc. and explore other school elements/ideas to improve student engagement, performance, and achievement.  Each project team was asked to research, design, and develop at least one plan/scenario in response to the board’s direction to develop strategies for improving campus-wide academic performance while realizing $295,728 in savings.  Additionally, the Office of Small School Innovation launched www.sandi.net/smallhighschoolsas a webspace to warehouse key information about the work of each Educational Complex and as a means of promoting transparency and openness with the process.  The webspace includes a review of board discussions on small high schools, surveys and results, artifacts from meetings, and research.

6. Report on District Assessments and Proposal for 2010-11 School Year; BUDGETED: YES (OE-2)
Meeting
May 25, 2010 - Special Meeting, 9:00 A.M.
Type
Information

RECOMMENDATION:   Today's workshop item is for discussion and information only.  Actions related to this item will be considered by the board on Thursday, May 27, 2010.

 

Approve the continued use of selected district assessments in 2010-2011, including benchmark exams in English, language arts, and mathematics in Grades 1-8; end-of-course exams in Grades 6-12; and Writing and Reading Assessment Profile (WRAP) or Developmental Reading Assessment (DRA) in Grades K-2.  This recommendation will provide the district with the continued ability to support the measurement of student achievement as identified in the Goals for Student Achievement [GSA], Operational Expectations [OE], the Local Educational Agency [LEA] Plan, Single Plans for Student Achievement [SPSA], School Assistance and Intervention Team [SAIT] Progress Reporting, and the district's Promotion/Retention Plan.  

FISCAL IMPACT:  The estimated cost for the continued use of selected district assessments in the 2010-2011 school year is $500,000.00.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  In 2009-10, the district budgeted $1,178,604 for district assessments.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  It is estimated that 3.0 FTE will be required to continue the use of selected district assessments in the 2010-11 school year.

BACKGROUND:  A number of district assessments are used to inform student course placements, monitor student growth, determine need for intervention programs, and help teachers adjust their instruction according to student need.  Among these are benchmark exams that are district-developed, standards-based assessments in the content areas of English, language arts, mathematics, and science.  Schools assess students in English, language arts, and mathematics at Grades 1-8, and in algebra and geometry three times during the school year to determine students' understanding of content taught during each interval of instruction.  Science benchmark exams are also administered at Grades 3, 4, and 5.  Benchmark assessments provide teachers with information about student performance in relation to standards and are used to inform instructional and programmatic decisions.  End-of-course exams are also district-developed, standards-based assessments that are intended to serve some of the same purposes as benchmark exams.  Other district assessments, such as the Writing and Reading Assessment Profile (WRAP), Gates-MacGinitie Reading Test, and Practice California High School Exit Exam (CAHSEE) are used as screening and diagnostic tools to inform student program placement and interventions appropriate for individual students or groups of students.

On February 23, 2010, as part of the budget reduction strategy, the board approved the elimination of  all central office funding for non-mandated assessments in the 2010-11 school year.  This action eliminated funding for test booklets, materials, processing of non-mandated assessments, and 5.0 FTE from the Assessment Services Department.  The total amount of the reduction was $1,178,604.
