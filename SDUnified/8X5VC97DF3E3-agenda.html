In its performance audit, CWA found that the Proposition S program "complied, in all material respects, with California Proposition 39, as incorporated in Article 13A of the California Constitution and Education Code Section 15264 et seq. during the fiscal year that ended on June 30, 2012."  This is the highest opinion that can be bestowed on operational performance of a school bond program.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve the Resolution Certifying the Final Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration Report for the Bell Middle School Modernization Project and certify that:

  1. an Initial Study (IS) was conducted and it was determined that the proposed project would not result in a significant effect on the environment and that an Environmental Impact Report is not required; and
  2. the Final Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration Report (Final IS/MND) for the modernization of the Bell Middle School (SCH #2012111026) has been prepared in compliance with applicable laws and regulations to evaluate the potential for significant adverse environmental impacts; and
  3. the Final IS/MND (Attachment A) was presented to the Board of Education, which reviewed and considered the information contained in the record prior to approving the project; and
  4. the Final IS/MND reflects the district’s independent judgment and analysis.  A copy is available at the Physical Plant Operations Annex, Room 5, 4860 Ruffner Street as well as on the district website; and
  5. Authorize staff to file the Notice of Determination for the Bell Middle School Modernization Project (SCH No. 2012111026) with the Recorder/County Clerk of San Diego County, California (Attachment B). 
  6. Authorize that the project be carried out as defined in the Final IS/MND for the proposed Bell Middle School Modernization Project.

FISCAL IMPACT:  Certification of the Final Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration Report (Final IS/MND) by the board will require San Diego Unified to file a Notice of Determination with the County Clerk of San Diego County with associated filing and processing fees totaling of $50.00.  The filing and processing fee will be paid out of the Prop S Fund; budget string: 9302-90112-00-6215-8500-0000-21312-8670

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:   None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  There is no impact to district staffing levels associated with certification of the Final IS/MND.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The district proposes to modernize Bell Middle School campus within the Paradise Hills community of the City of San Diego.  The proposed project includes the following components:

  • Modernization providing school improvements for health, safety and security
  • Projects to improve school accessibility, code compliance upgrades
  • Major building systems repair/replacement

On November 9, 2012, the district filed a Draft Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration dated October, 2012, out for 30-day public review period.  A Notice of Intent to adopt the Mitigated Negative Declaration was published in the Union-Tribune and La Prensa and filed at the County of San Diego and California State Clearinghouse.  Copies of the Draft Mitigated Negative Declaration were sent to the State Clearinghouse, state and local agencies, and community entities/organizations.  The Draft Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration was made available at the district’s Physical Plant Operations Annex, the City of San Diego Library, the district website, Tierrasanta Branch of the San Diego City Public Library, and online at the Environmental Review/Studies page at: www.sandi.net/facilities. 

 

The Draft Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration evaluated the following environmental issues: aesthetics, agriculture and forestry resources, air quality, greenhouse gas emissions, biological resources, cultural resources, geology and soils, hazards and hazardous materials, hydrology and water quality, land use and planning, mineral resources, transportation/circulation, noise, population and housing, recreation, public services and utilities and service systems. 

 

Based on review of the comments received during public review it was determined that no revisions to the Draft Negative Declaration needed to be made in the Final Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration in response to comments received.

 

The Final Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration was prepared in accordance with CEQA and the Guidelines for Implementation of CEQA.  A copy of the Final Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration and Technical Appendices for the Bell Middle School Modernization Project has been provided to the board for review prior to the public meeting and is located in Bell Middle School, 620 Briarwood Road, San Diego, CA 92139.  Additional copies of the Final IS/MND are available for review in the Physical Plant Operations Annex, Room 5, the district website, and the Paradise Hills Branch Library.  The Final Initial Study/Mitigated Negative Declaration, with Technical Appendices, is also available online at the Environmental Reviews/Studies page at www.sandi.net/facilities.

RECOMMENDATION: First reading of ethical practices for all San Diego Unified employees and board members.  Recommend review and discussion by board.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Comes as Recommendation from Procurement Practices Committee

 

BACKGROUND:  These ethic codes outline the guiding principles and expected behaviors for all San Diego Unified employees and board members, especially when relating to vendors or contractors who either serve or are seeking to serve the district.

 

The board requested staff to set up a Procurement Practices Committee to review district procurement practices, especially as related to construction purchases.  The Committee was asked to develop a voluntary Ethical Practices Statement for all employees and board members.  The Committee has done so and has voted unanimously to recommend the attached Ethics Statement to the board for its consideration.  It is not the intent of the Committee that this statement replaces or supersedes any existing district policy or administrative procedure.  It is designed to set the bar high for personal conduct for all district employees, including board members.  This is being presented today as a first reading to enable the board to review and discuss prior to adoption at a subsequent meeting.

6. Three-Year Plan to Eliminate Budget Deficit and Rebuild the Reserves Inclusive of Property Sales as Bridging Mechanism, Including Resolutions of Intent to Sell MacMullen and Stevenson Elementary Sites—REQUIRES 4 VOTES; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-10)

RECOMMENDATION: Discuss the elimination of the budget deficit and rebuild reserves in three years and approve resolutions of intent to Sell MacMullen and Stevenson elementary sites.  Details for each property are included in Attachment "A."  Item requires 4 votes.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Upon close of escrow, income will be received by the district in the amount of $27,900,000.00, less title and escrow expenses.  Details for each property are included in Attachment "A."  Under normal circumstances the use of funds from the sale would be limited to capital outlay or maintenance costs; however, the proceeds from the sale may be deposited in the general fund through January 1, 2014 (Ed Code § 17463.7). Funds will be used as part of an overall strategy to eliminate the structural deficit by the end of the 2015-16 school year.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  Details for each property are included in Attachment "A."

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The Advisory Committee on Utilization of Excess School Property was consulted on May 10, 2012, and supports their recommendations for each property as delineated in the Property Management Plan (PMP) submitted and approved by the board on May 29, 2012.  A copy of the PMP is attached.

 

BACKGROUND:  The district needs to use the sale of real estate as a bridge mechanism to eliminating the structural budget deficit by the end of the 2015-16 school year.  This, together with the attrition strategy, additional revenue from Prop 30 and disciplined, budget decision making will enable the budget to eliminate the repeated annual cycle of deficits.  Details for each property are included in Attachment "A."  As specified in each Purchase and Sale Agreement, district staff will first offer the property to the City and County and other public and non-profit entities as required by Education Code § 17464.  If there is no interest or if the district and public entity cannot agree on terms for the sale after the statutorily required time, district staff will advertise the property for sale, select the bidder with the highest bid and terms most beneficial to the district, enter into a negotiation/option period with the selected bidder, and return to the board for approval of the sale of the property to the selected bidder.

 

Approval of this agenda item will authorize the Deputy Superintendent Business, or his designee, to execute any documents in connection therewith.
