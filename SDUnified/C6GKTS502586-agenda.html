RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of support and advocacy for legislation in furtherance of the shared goals of the San Diego Unified School District and the San Diego Community College District and urge the State Legislature to approve and the Governor to sign into law the package of legislative proposals. This legislation was discussed by both boards at the Joint Meeting held on March 16, 2022.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District and the San Diego Community College District meet annually to discuss shared goals, status reports and focus areas.  Their shared areas of focus include accountability, data and research, outreach and parent engagement, collaboration and communication, career pathways and early college credit, the San Diego Promise program, legislation and advocacy, and racial justice and equity. With respect to legislation and advocacy, the San Diego Unified School District and the San Diego Community College District desire to collaborate on legislation that impacts both transitional kindergarten to 12th grades and the community colleges individually, and collectively. In furtherance of their shared goals related to legislation and advocacy, the San Diego Unified School Districts supports the following legislative proposals pending in the California State Legislature: AB 102 (Holden), AB 1187 (Irwin), AB 1719 (Ward) and AB 1746 (Medina). AB 102 (Holden) would extend Career and College Pathways Program partnership provisions indefinitely and authorizes a county office of education to enter into a Career and College Pathways Program with a community college. AB 1187 (Irwin) would provide that supervised tutoring for basic skills, and for degree-applicable and transfer-level courses, as authorized pursuant to regulations adopted by the Board of Governors by July 31, 2022, is eligible for state apportionment funding. AB 1719 (Ward) would permit community colleges to build affordable housing for faculty and classified employees, and would specify that a school district or community college district may allow foster youth or former foster youth to occupy housing created through the program. AB 1746 (Medina) would reform the States Cal Grant system by matching eligibility requirements with Pell Grants, and make it easier for non-traditional students to qualify for financial aid and simplifying the process in which students apply for Cal Grants.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution in the matter of support for the Arts and Music in School Funding Guarantee and Accountability Act to increase funding in the arts and music education in transitional kindergarten to 12th grades in the state of California.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: San Diego Unified promotes artistic literacy, innovation, creativity, and excellence in the arts and music, and advocates for high quality arts and music experiences for all students. Increasing access to high quality arts and music education for students in San Diego Unified is a mission of the VAPA Foundation. Access to arts and music education at school is a critical part of a transitional kindergarten to 12th grade education, which is supported by studies on educational achievement which demonstrate that arts and music education improve student learning. For example, music education improves cognitive development and spatial reasoning, while the dramatic arts improve reading comprehension. Arts and music education also improves school attendance and individual student self-confidence and motivation to learn, particularly among low-income and other at-risk students. An arts and music education also provides a viable pathway to a job in California’s creative economy, while developing and expanding culture. Emerging from the COVID-19 pandemic stronger than before requires ongoing investments in arts and music education to positively impact students’ mental health and well-being. The Arts and Music in Schools Funding Guarantee and Accountability Act would provide dedicated annual source of additional funding for arts and music education. The Arts and Music in Schools Funding Guarantee and Accountability Act would result in additional funding likely in the range of $800 million to $1 billion annually for arts and music and schools, and such funding would increase access to the arts and music in low-income communities.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2, and summarized below, prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

 

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located – a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

 

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of the existing school sites. Installation of security fencing and other security infrastructure is consistent with the operation of a school. Generally, the surrounding land uses at the project sites consist of urban built up areas and the Proposed Project would not extend beyond each project site into open space areas. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed at the project site or in the project vicinity that, when combined with the construction of the Proposed Project, would result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Once the Proposed Project has been constructed, existing school operations would remain unchanged compared with existing conditions. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. The Proposed Project includes the construction of security fencing and infrastructure at existing school sites. No work is proposed offsite that could result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas as a result of the Proposed Project. Removal of ornamental landscaping and trees may be required however, would be removed consistent with District Capital Improvement Program Environmental Impact Report standard mitigation Measure BIO-1, which requires nesting surveys for any vegetation removal that occurs between January 15 and August 31. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. There are no designated scenic highways visible from any district school site. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the California State Water Resources Control Board Geotracker database, the project site is not included on any list compiled pursuant to section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. According to the CEQA Guidelines regarding historical resources, a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource amounts to a significant impact on the environment (Guidelines section 15064.5(b)). Accordingly, a substantial adverse change means demolition, destruction, relocation, or alteration of the resource or its immediate surroundings resulting in the significance of the resource being materially impaired. Because the Proposed Project does not require the demolition of existing historical era buildings or structures, the significance of a potentially historical resource will not be materially impaired, and no substantial adverse changes to the significance of a historical resource or significant impacts to the environment is anticipated. Additionally, the likelihood of impacting either prehistoric or historic archaeological resources is negligible for the current undertaking because major grading and trenching into native soils is not proposed. This exception does not apply to the project.

CEQA ENVIRONMENTAL REVIEW: The Synthetic Grass Replacement Projects at Edison and Jefferson Elementary Schools and Hoover and Scripps Ranch High Schools (Proposed Project) are subject to the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), however, they are exempt from further environmental review under CEQA Guidelines Section 15302 Replacement or Reconstruction (Class 2). Class 2 consists of replacement or reconstruction of existing structures and facilities where the new structure will be located on the same site as the structure replaced and will have substantially the same purpose and capacity as the structure replaced, including replacement or reconstruction of facilities involving negligible or no expansion of capacity. The Proposed Project entails the replacement of existing synthetic grass at existing school facilities with no change in use. Therefore, the application of a Class 2 categorical exemption is appropriate for the Proposed Project.

Exceptions to Use of a Categorical Exemption (CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2): The Proposed Project does not have the potential to trigger any of the exceptions identified in CEQA Guidelines section 15300.2, and summarized below, prohibiting the use of a categorical exemption.

  1. Location. Classes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 11 are qualified by consideration of where the project is to be located a project that is ordinarily insignificant in its impact on the environment may in a particularly sensitive environment be significant. Therefore, these classes are considered to apply in all instances, except where the project may impact on an environmental resource of hazardous or critical concern where designated, precisely mapped, and officially adopted pursuant to law by federal, state, or local agencies.
  2. Cumulative Impact. All exemptions for these classes are inapplicable when the cumulative impact of successive projects of the same type in the same place, over time is significant.
  3. Significant Effect. A categorical exemption shall not be used for an activity where there is a reasonable possibility that the activity will have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances.
  4. Scenic Highways. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project which may result in damage to scenic resources, including but not limited to, trees, historic buildings, rock outcroppings, or similar resources, within a highway officially designated as a state scenic highway. This does not apply to improvements that are required as mitigation by an adopted negative declaration or certified EIR.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project located on a site that is included on any list compiled pursuant to section 65962.5 of the Government Code.
  6. Historical Resources. A categorical exemption shall not be used for a project that may cause a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource.

Environmental Review: The following provides review of the potential environmental issues associated with the Proposed Project to ensure that the above exceptions are not relevant to the project.

  1. Location. The Proposed Project would occur entirely within the boundaries of existing school sites and would be consistent with existing uses. Generally, the surrounding land uses at the project site consists of built up urban areas and would not extend beyond the project site into open space areas. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  2. Cumulative Impact. There are no known future projects proposed at the project site or in the project vicinity that, when combined with the construction of the Proposed Project, would result in a cumulatively considerable effect on the environment. Once the Proposed Project has been constructed, existing school operations would remain unchanged compared with existing conditions. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  3. Significant Effect. No work is proposed offsite that could result in impacts to sensitive vegetation or other environmentally sensitive areas as a result of the Proposed Project. Furthermore, there is nothing unusual about the design of the Proposed Project that could result in a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances. Therefore, there is no reasonable possibility that the Proposed Project would have a significant effect on the environment due to unusual circumstances, and this exception does not apply to the project.
  4. Scenic Highways. There are no designated scenic highways visible from any District school site. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  5. Hazardous Waste Sites. According to the California State Water Resources Control Board Geotracker database, the project site is not included on any list compiled pursuant to section 65962.5 of the Government Code. Therefore, this exception does not apply to the project.
  6. Historical Resources. According to the CEQA Guidelines regarding historical resources, a substantial adverse change in the significance of a historical resource amounts to a significant impact on the environment (Guidelines section 15064.5(b)). Accordingly, a substantial adverse change means demolition, destruction, relocation, or alteration of the resource or its immediate surroundings resulting in the significance of the resource being materially impaired. Because the Proposed Project does not require the demolition of existing historical era buildings or structures, the significance of a potentially historical resource will not be materially impaired, and no substantial adverse changes to the significance of a historical resource or significant impacts to the environment is anticipated. Additionally, the likelihood of impacting either prehistoric or historic archaeological resources is negligible for the current undertaking because major grading and trenching into native soils is not proposed. This exception does not apply to the project.

Approval of this item approves the project as described above, authorizes and directs staff to file the attached Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk, and awards the agreement to FieldTurf USA, Inc., to replace synthetic grass surfacing at four sites, as described above.
