RECOMMENDATION: Approve multiple agreements with Pearson Education, Inc. for the Implementation of Learning Teams Services during the 2011-12 school year at nine district schools as listed below.  The term of these Agreements is September 28, 2011 through June 30, 2012.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The cost is $58,860.00 per school site, for a total amount of $529,740.00. All sites will be funded by Title I American Reinvestment Recovery Act (ARRA) funds, budget string: 5635-30110-00-5801-2490-0000-01000-0000, with the exception of Burbank Elementary School which will be funded by SIG funds, budget string: 0039-31812-00-5150-2700-0000-01000-0000.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None: All services will be provided by Pearson Educational Inc., Learning Teams.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  Bell Middle School was one of a few schools in the United States to be selected by Pearson Education to become a Pearson LT “partnership site” for 2010-­11. The benefits to Bell Middle School resulting from this selection, included receiving full Pearson LT implementation services for one academic year (approximate value of $60,000). This partnership also provided the opportunity for other schools to observe how Pearson Learning Teams might be relevant and useful to its schools.  Based on the success of this program Area Superintendents made recommendation of nine other school sites to participate in this program.

 

Pearson Learning Teams is a unique service model distinguished by a rigorous evidence base that leverages teacher collaboration as the central mechanism for improving student achievement and overall school culture. The core components of Pearson Learning Teams are teams (teacher work groups), distributed leadership, tested protocols, stable settings, and assistance and training.

 

The Pearson Leadership Teams will implement tested protocols with the goal of systematically improving teaching and learning at nine district schools.  Leadership training for administrators and teacher facilitators will guide the site instructional leadership team as they analyze data, modify teaching practices, and develop targeted formative assessments. Pearson coaches will provide on-site support to guide and sustain the implementation of Learning Teams.  As a result, school staff will provide valuable feedback to the district about the leadership team process and serve as future trainer-of-trainers for other district schools.  Ultimately, this service can improve and expand the leadership capacity within all schools in the district.

 

The nine participating schools are: Burbank (SV-12-0459-13) and Valencia Park (SV-12-0276-13) elementary schools; Bell (SV-12-0278-13), De Portola (SV-12-0295-13), Knox (SV-12-0277-13) and Standley (SV-12-0458-13) middle schools; Clairemont (SV-12-0287-13), Crawford (SV-12-0457-13), and San Diego (SV-12-0290-13) high educational complexes.

BACKGROUND:  Pursuant to state requirements and implementation of Proposition S (Prop S), an Independent Citizens Oversight Committee (ICOC) was established to review and report to the public on the proper expenditure of taxpayers’ money in the use of Prop S funds for school construction. (California Education Code section 15278, et seq.)  The members of the Prop S ICOC appointed by the district’s board members have undertaken their responsibilities in accordance with the ICOC Bylaws approved by the board on October 13, 2009.  Currently, the ICOC has established four subcommittees: Audit; Construction; Finance, Planning and Controls; and Executive/Governance.

 

Education Code section 15280 (part of Proposition 39) states that the governing board of a school district shall, without expending bond funds, provide the ICOC with any necessary technical and administrative assistance in furtherance of its purpose and sufficient resources to publicize its conclusions.

 

The district advertised a Request for Proposals (RFP) No. PS-12-0076-02 for competitive proposals on June 21, 2011.    The RFP was advertised in the (1) San Diego Daily Transcript, (2) La Prensa San Diego, (3) The San Diego Voice and Viewpoint, and (4) the Union-Tribune.  In addition, the RFP was advertised on DemandStar to 291 suppliers, and forwarded to interested firms in the district’s Facilities Outreach database and community.

 

Three proposals were received by the July 21, 2011 deadline and evaluated for best value by an evaluation committee.  The submitting firms were Colbi Technologies, Inc., SGI Construction Management, and Total School Solutions.  Colbi Technologies, Inc. was unanimously recommended by the evaluation committee for an award recommendation.  The members of the Evaluation Committee were: Andrew Berg, ICOC; Lee Dulgeroff, Executive Director of Facilities Planning and Construction (FPC); John Gordon, ICOC; Cynthia Reed-Porter, Facilities Communications Supervisor; and Jim Watts, Director of FPC Special Projects. 

 

Colbi will be requested to attend multiple meetings, coordinate ICOC financial and performance audits, perform on-site project reviews and field reports of Prop S construction projects in progress, and perform professional and administrative support for ICOC related functions as required and directed by the district.  Services under Prop S have been expanded to include various reviews and reports with respect to district responsibilities.  Such reviews encompass quarterly reports, evaluations of the Project Stabilization Agreement (PSA) and Labor Compliance Program, review of the three-year construction plan, and, if required, an additional annual report.

 

Because of the nature of the activities and the uncertainty of how many hours will be required per task, Colbi will bill only for those hours actually expended and authorized by the district.

 

Government Code section 53060 allows a school district to contract with and employ any persons for the furnishing of special services if such persons are specially trained, experienced and competent to perform the special services required.

I. District Operations
Subject
2. Declaration of Need for Fully Qualified Educators; BUDGETED: N/A (OE-5)
Meeting
Sep 27, 2011 - REVISED Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action

RECOMMENDATION: Approval of Declaration of Need for fully Qualified Educators, effective July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2012, submitted to the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CCTC) by county offices of education, public school districts, state-wide agencies or non-public schools/agencies.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: this item is approved annually by the Board of Education and has no fiscal impact.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Support staffing in areas of critical need.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  Title V California Code of Regulations, adopted by the CCTC, requires each public school district anticipating the use of Provisional Internship Permits, Short Term Staff Permits, Limited Assignment Permits and Internship Credentials during the school year to file a Declaration of Need for Fully Qualified Educators.  The regulation requires the Board of Education take action to adopt the Declaration, and that the Declaration be an open agenda item and not on the Consent Calendar. Due to the continuing staffing needs in sever shortage areas such as Special Education Mild/Moderate, it is necessary to file the Declaration of Need for the 2011-12 school year.
