CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  The District Advisory Council (DAC) received training regarding specific plan requirements.

 

BACKGROUND: As a result of SB 374, the state holds each district school accountable for developing and implementing a Single Plan for Student Achievement (SPSA).  The SPSA identifies the instructional needs of students and specifies how school funding, including categorical funds provided through the Consolidated Application, will be used to accomplish the goals outlined in the plan.  The SPSA is developed or modified through an annual planning cycle.

 

The School Site Council (SSC), in collaboration with the site instructional leadership team, is responsible for developing, implementing, monitoring, and evaluating the SPSA.  Site principals, SSC chairpersons, SSC members, and the District Advisory Council for Compensatory Education (DAC) received training regarding specific plan requirements.  Each school's School Site Council (SSC) works with its site instructional leadership team and district staff to develop, implement, monitor, and evaluate its SPSA.

 

Additionally, the Financial Planning, Monitoring, and Accountability staff provided individual support to principals and site teams to discuss Title I expenditures and the SPSA development process.  Sites then developed their SPSAs, had them approved by their SSCs, and submitted them to their area superintendents for review.  Area superintendents reviewed plans to ensure schools identified student needs and aligned their goals with appropriate courses of action to address those needs.

 

Once approved by area superintendents, the plans were forwarded to the Financial Planning, Monitoring, and Accountability Department where staff reviewed each plan and provided individual assistance to schools to ensure that their SPSAs met state requirements and that the plans and associated categorical budget allocations were in alignment.  The local governing board must review and approve each school's SPSA.  This report brings forward for board approval the 2017-18 SPSAs and categorical budget summaries for 176 schools.

 

A copy of each 2017-18 SPSA, which includes the budget summaries and required signed Assurances page is available in the Board Services Office, Eugene Brucker Education Center, 4100 Normal Street, Room 2231, San Diego, CA 92103.

The Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) is an essential element of California's Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF).  It is a three-year, district-level plan that is updated annually.  The plan describes the school district's key goals for students as well as the actions (with expenditures) the district will take to achieve the goals and measures used to indicate progress.  The LCAP also includes an Annual Update, which reflects on implementation of the current year's LCAP (2016-17).  The San Diego Unified School District has aligned its six LCAP goals and supporting actions and expenditures to the board-adopted Vision 2020 Quality Schools in Every Neighborhood. 

 

RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and adopt the district's proposed 2017-20 Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP).  The 2017-20 LCAP content was presented to the Board of Education and the public on June 6, 2017, along with specific implementation plans for the 2017-18 school year. The 2017-20 LCAP document was presented to the Board of Education as a public hearing and first reading on June 13, 2017.  This is the second and final reading of the district's proposed 2017-20 LCAP.

 

SUMMARY OF CHANGES TO LCAP SINCE FIRST READING: The following changes to narrative which are included in the attached final documents, have been made to the LCAP since the First Reading on June 13, 2017:

BACKGROUND: The LCAP is intended to be a comprehensive planning tool.  For school districts, pursuant to Education Code Section 52060, the LCAP must describe, for the school district and each school within the district, goals and specific actions to achieve those goals for all students and each subgroup of students in Education Code Section 52052, including students with disabilities, for each of the state priorities and any locally identified priorities.  The district's local priorities (LCAP Goals) are:  

 

1. Closing the Achievement Gap with High Expectations for All:  All levels of the organization work to improve student achievement and close the achievement gap for all underperforming student groups.  

2.  Access to Board and Challenging Curriculum:  Students have equitable access to rigorous, well-rounded standards-aligned curricula and access to and skill in applying technologies to leverage learning, assuring readiness for a full range of post-graduation options.  

3. Quality Leadership, Teaching and Learning: Effective leadership and teaching is evident system-wide with a unifying vision that equips and empowers all stakeholders to provide optimal student learning opportunities and outcomes.  

4. Positive School Environment, Climate and Culture:  Schools provide safe and well-maintained facilities and positive learning climates and instructional practices that are culturally responsive, challenge bias, and support the academic, social, emotional, and physical needs of students.  

5. Family and Community Engagement with Highly Regarded Neighborhood Schools that Service Students, Families, and Communities:  Parents and community members are engaged and work within and across schools to support student learning.  Neighborhood schools are highly regarded for their quality as well as their service as centers for extended learning and enrichment opportunities, and academic and social services.  

6. Well-Orchestrated Districtwide Support Services and Communications:  All parts of the organization - personnel and infrastructure - work together to support student success through quality schools in every neighborhood.

2. Implementation  of State Standards:  The implementation of state board adopted academic content and performance standards for all students, which are:  

  • English Language Arts - Common Core State Standards (CCSS) for English Language Arts  
  • Mathematics - CCSS for Mathematics  
  • English Language Development (ELD)  
  • Career Technical Education  
  • Health Education Content Standards
  • History-Social Science
