Subject
C.02. Renewal Charter Petition for Holly Drive Leadership Academy (Board Governance Policy: OE-10)
Meeting
Jun 2, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes
Holly Drive Leadership Academy has existed as a charter school in the San Diego Unified School District for ten years. Holly Drive’s charter term expires at the end of the 2008-09 school year. Holly Drive is seeking a five-year renewal of its charter term. Holly Drive submitted its renewal charter petition to the Office of School Innovation and Choice on March 31, 2009. The Board of Education has three options:

1. Charter School Non-Renewal;

2. Approve Charter School Renewal with Conditions; or

3. Approve Charter School Renewal without Conditions.

The Office of School Innovation and Choice recommends the board select Option One: Charter School Non-Renewal. An accompanying board report gives a complete analysis.

Meeting
Jun 2, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes
Dollar Amount
$2,163,000.00
The Office of School Innovation and Choice (OSIC) recommends the board approve the renewal charter petition for Explorer Elementary Charter School for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2009, and expiring on June 30, 2014. OSIC recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the board’s decision to renew the charter petition. Explorer was originally approved to be a charter school on September 1, 1999, by the board and has been operating as a charter school in the San Diego Unified School District for ten years. The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective on July 1, 2009. The potential fiscal impact to the district is an estimated loss of $2,163,000 generated in average daily attendance in 2009-10 because the district does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools. District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met requirements to continue performing as a charter school in the district.

C. STUDENT INSTRUCTIONAL MATTERS
Subject
C.10. Adoption of Amended Charter Petition for Urban Discovery Academy (Board Governance Policy: OE-10)
Meeting
Jun 2, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes
Dollar Amount
$170,000.00
The Office of School Innovation and Choice (OSIC) recommends the Board of Education adopt the amendments to the charter for Urban Discovery Academy (UDA) Charter School. The amendments include a request to increase the grade offerings to include a 7th and 8th grade academic program. UDA currently has nine 6th graders enrolled and this amendment would allow them to maintain this cohort in 7th grade in 2009-10. The potential fiscal impact to the district is an estimated loss of $170,000 generated in average daily attendance because the district does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools. OSIC received amendments for the UDA on May 5, 2009. The amendments shall become effective June 3, 2009, and will expire with the original term of the charter petition, June 30, 2013. The UDA petitioners submitted their amendments to the district for review and consideration of approval in accordance with education code requirements. Staff has reviewed the amendments and found them to meet legal, fiscal, and operational requirements. Staff recommends the adoption of this report as findings in support of the board’s decision to approve the amendments to the UDA charter petition.

[Originator/Contact: Dr. Rich Cansdale, Chief School Innovation and Choice Officer, 858.627.7408, rcansdale@sandi.net]
