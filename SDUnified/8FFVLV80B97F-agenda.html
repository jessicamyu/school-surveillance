RECOMMENDATION:  Ratify Agreement with the County of San Diego Health and Human Services Agency, effective July 1, 2011, through September 30, 2011, to provide Tdap vaccination clinics to support implementation of AB 354, passed into California law and effective July 1, 2011.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:    School nurses will assist with dates and locations of clinics.  

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  In support of the implementation of AB 354, passed into California law and effective July 1, 2011, the County of San Diego received a limited supply of free Tdap vaccines to assist school districts in obtaining this vaccine booster for its students. This law requires that students entering the 7th through 12th grades in the 2011-12 school year must be immunized with a pertussis (whooping cough) vaccine. The County of San Diego needed to start the clinics to provide this vaccine in August to ensure that students would be properly immunized and not be prohibited from attending school, in accordance with the provisions of AB 354.  Therefore, this agreement is being submitted for ratification at this time.

 

Pertussis (Whooping Cough) is a very contagious respiratory disease that can be severe and last for months.  The immunity received from either early childhood immunization or pertussis disease wears off over time, leaving older students and adults susceptible again to pertussis.  Immunization with Tdap can protect students, schools, and communities against pertussis.
