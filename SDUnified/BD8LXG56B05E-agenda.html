BACKGROUND: As San Diego Unified celebrates progress towards meeting the inspirational goals contained in Vision 2020, we are taking time to celebrate those employees who have helped us achieve this progress. The number one predictor of student success is the presence of an experienced, high quality teacher in the classroom, according to the Learning Policy Institute. (Source: “Report: Teacher qualifications best predictor of student success,” By Amelia Harper, May 16, 2019, Education Dive). That study, which was written last year and co-authored by the Chair of the State Board of Education, found high quality teachers have helped San Diego schools do better than the rest of the state. (Source: “California’s Positive Outliers: Districts Beating the Odds,” by Anne Podolsky, Linda Darling-Hammond, Christopher Doss, and Sean Reardon, May 16, 2019, Learning Policy Institute). Educators also provide crucial services in addition to teaching. They are critical to keeping students safe at school. School staff also provide healthy, nutritious meals to children who do not have access to food at home. Nearly 100,000 schools/institutions serve school lunches to 29.8 million students each day, including 20.2 million free lunches. (Source: School Nutrition Association.) Given the clear association between the health and success of our students and the well-being and effectiveness of our workforce, we will begin publicly recognizing employees for their continuous years of service to the students, families and communities of San Diego Unified.  Additional years of service recognitions will be scheduled throughout the remainder of the 2019-20 school year, and will be held monthly beginning with the 2020-21 school year. 

 

*This service recognition award is not used to calculate or verify the seniority date of an employee nor is the recognition part of the formal personnel processes that calculate the date for longevity pay. Both longevity and seniority dates are dictated by the Education Code as well as the applicable collective bargaining agreement. If you believe an error has been made, you may contact the Human Resource Service Division by emailing jobs@sandi.net.

Financial Review

 

District staff reviewed and analyzed the most recent financial documents available for the AEA schools.  The monthly rent of approximately $1,629 for the new office space has a minimal impact on the overall AEA budget.  AEA meets the district’s minimum reserve requirement of 3 percent of expenses or $50,000, whichever is greater.  At the end of 2018-19, per the schools’ unaudited actuals report, the ending fund balance for the elementary school was approximately $4,382,904, and for the middle school was approximately $1,807,941.

 

RENEWAL VERSUS REVISION

 

A petition to open a new charter school can be approved by the district for up to a five-year term. Near the end of the term, a charter school applies for “renewal” of its charter to continue operating for another five-year term. Education Code section 47605 requires the review and approval process for new and renewing charter petitions to take place over two board meetings: (i) public hearing, and (ii) action to grant/deny.

 

Within a five-year term, a charter school may request the district’s approval to revise its existing charter pursuant to Education Code section 47607. Proposed revisions may include a change in the school’s location, special education status, expansion of grade levels, or other significant revision.
