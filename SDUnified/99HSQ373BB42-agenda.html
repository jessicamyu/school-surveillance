Implementation of Initial Restoration between San Diego Unified and San Diego Education Association (SDEA), Administrators Association of San Diego (AASD) Certificated, Confidential Employees and Non-Represented Employees; BUDGETED.

 

RECOMMENDATION:  For the 2013-14 fiscal year, approve restoration of three instructional days, restoration of partial raises for SDEA, AASD Certificated, Confidential Employees and Non-Represented Employees and three furlough days for SDEA, Confidential, Non-Represented Employees and Board of Education Trustees.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  Total fiscal impact is $24,821,399.  Public disclosure of confidential and non-represented management employee attached.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:   Yes.  As a result of the concession agreements, the district realized savings in 2012-13 from the deferred salary increases and continued furloughs.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  In the 2012-13 school year, the district scheduled five furlough (non-instructional) days, equating to providing students 175 instructional days. Based on the adoption of the final district budget on July 30, 2013, the district recommends restoration of three instructional days which increases student instructional days to 178.  Revised calendars reflecting the increase to 178 instructional days of student attendance for both traditional and year-round schools are attached here and posted on the district web page.

 

The board has previously committed to the principle that each employee group, including those not represented by an employee organization, should contribute equally to addressing the district’s fiscal circumstances. During 2012, the district asked all employees, through their bargaining units, or individually, to take salary concessions to address fiscal challenges.  SDEA and AASD Certificated units agreed to defer previously negotiated salary schedule increases and to continue furlough days as a joint effort to meet the district financial obligations, contingent upon their restoration upon new revenues.

 

The board also imposed salary concessions and furlough days equivalent to what SDEA and AASD-Certificated Units agreed to, upon confidential employees and non-represented employees. The board has remained committed to restoring salary concessions to all employees upon similar restoration of SDEA and AASD-Certificated units.  Based on 2013-14 budget projections, the district has agreed to the restorations as set forth below.

 

SDEA: Consistent with the Tentative Agreement restoration schedule, implement a 2 percent increase on the salary schedule retroactive to January 1, 2013. The increase in instructional calendar will result in only two remaining furlough days for the 2013-14 school year. Total fiscal impact of restoration is $22,535,221.

 

AASD Certificated: Consistent with the Tentative Agreement restoration schedule implement negotiated 4.83 5 percent salary increase effective July 1, 2013.Total fiscal impact of restoration is $1,784,127.

 

Confidential Employees: Board previously adopted the Tentative Supplement to MOU restoring the 5 percent degree of difference between similar positions in the OTBS bargaining unit. The increase in instructional calendar will result in three less furlough days for the 2013/14 school year.  Total fiscal impact of restoration is $201,056.

 

Non-Represented Employees: Consistent with restoration to SDEA, 2 percent increase on salary schedule.  Similarly the increase in instructional calendar will result in three less furlough days for the 2013-14 school year. Total fiscal impact of restoration is $299,987.  

 

Board Members: The increase in instructional calendar days will result in the equivalent of three fewer furlough days for the 2013-14 school year.  Total fiscal impact of restoration is $1,008.

 

With the passage of Proposition 30, the state has provided additional funding for education.  With these new funds, the district is able to restore three instructional days for a total of $7,923,960.  The cost to restore each instructional day is $2,641,320.

RECOMMENDATION: Board Trustees Evans and Foster will ask that the board take action to direct the Superintendent to develop a Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF) Accountability Proposal for the California Department of Education (CDE).

 

CDE is developing accountability plans for LCFF that they will finalize by January 2014.  San Diego Unified needs to make sure our advocates have a clear message on what we believe will be a robust accountability plan.  We are directing staff to develop a proposal that places an emphasis on two things: 1) local control and decision making about allocation of resources and 2) measurable results for the district and for Free/Reduced Lunch students (F/RL), English Language Learners (ELL), and foster children.  Title I allocates funds, per school, without accountability measures.  NCLB has unrealistic progress measures and a punitive approach.  We do not need the state micro-managing how funds are spent nor mandating the exact procedures for engaging stakeholder groups.  The district needs to develop, however, a proposal for the measures of student achievement, including the common core tests, as well as other measures.  Staff should bring a plan or a timeline for a plan at the next board meeting that will ensure that San Diego Unified, the second largest school district in the state, has a strong influence on the development of these plans by the state.

BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue the improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting districtwide, a new process will be implemented for streamlining Independent Contractor Agreements. The goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective deputy or chief, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.
