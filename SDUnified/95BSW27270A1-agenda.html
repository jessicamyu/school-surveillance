RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a first reading of the Common Core State Standards (CCSS) Spending Plan. Action requesting approval of the plan will be sought at the December 10, 2013 board meeting.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: Revenue to the district of $22,498,000

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: Resource staff for CCSS implementation.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The district CCSS Leadership Team and CCSS Advisory/Working group (comprised of SDEA and AASD representation) collaborated in the development of this plan.

 

BACKGROUND: California, along with 45 additional states, has adopted the Common Core State Standards (CCSS).   California Assembly Bill 86 provides one-time funds to local educational agencies to support activities required to implement the CCSS. These new standards are required to be in place for the 2014-15 school year.  This new funding is targeted to CCSS implementation and must be used to support the following:

  • Professional Development to build the capacity of teachers, leaders, and support staff involved in the direct instruction of students that is aligned to the CCSS.
  • Instructional materials and supplemental materials aligned to Common Core State Standards.
  • Technology support needed to implement CCSS, including computer-based student assessments.

The funds must be spent by July 1, 2015.  As a condition of receiving the funds, a spending plan must be presented at a public meeting of the governing board and then approved at a subsequent meeting. This item is a first reading of the proposed spending plan for the Common Core State Standards funding that we will receive.

RECOMMENDATION: Denial of the petition to establish Elevate Elementary Charter School, based on the following findings.

  1. The petitioners are unfamiliar with the content of the Petition with respect to English Learners.  Page 61 of the Petition indicates that Elevate anticipates serving an average of approximately 8.4 percent English Learners, which is 22 students, based on a first-year project enrollment of 266 students.  This statement does not align with the charter school’s budget which projects an English Learner enrollment of zero students. 
  1. The Petition presents an unsound educational program with respect to the proposed instructional techniques and curriculum plan.
  1. Pages 56-60 describes two sample projects to reflect the petitioners’ proposal to incorporate Project Based Learning (PBL) as a primary instructional technique.  These sample projects are not in complete alignment with PBL, rather they are more indicative of projects with connected activities.  The sample projects lack the larger global perspective and authentic tasks connected to them. They are thematic in nature rather than PBL.
  1. Regarding English Learners and Students with Disabilities, the Petition proposes an approach of learning skills in isolation, especially vocabulary, which will not allow students to connect instruction to the context of the text and produce strong student outcomes.
  1. The curricular resources identified in the Petition, beginning on page 23, are not reflective of the expectations and demands of the Common Core State Standards.  The Petition contains a lack of continuity of instruction across the grade levels and the continuous build that is required with the Common Core State Standards (“CCSS”) due to an educational structure that allows each individual teacher to choose from a list of curricular resources.  The resources identified in the Petition do not appear sufficient to create a comprehensive academic program in alignment with the CCSS and for students to be successful with the new Smarter Balanced Assessments.  Many of the resources involve “fill in the blank” tasks that do not require complex thinking, reading, writing and communication connected to the text or high levels of mathematical reasoning. As a leveled reader, “Reading A-Z” is not CCSS aligned and will not provide the text complexity for students in Grades 3-5 who will be expected to read complex text and integrate knowledge from multiple sources.
  1. The petitioners are demonstrably unlikely to successfully implement the program set forth in the petition regarding its governance structure, enrollment projections, and plan for serving high-achieving students. 
  1. There is a concerning amount of intermingling between the petitioners and the proposed landlord, Newsbreak Church, which will also serve as the lender of start-up loans up to $400,000 to the charter school (Exhibits 1-2).  Five of the founding team members, including a charter board member, are either a direct employee of the landlord/lender, or are married to employees of the landlord/lender. 
  1. The Petition presents an overly optimistic projection, in the district’s judgment and based on historical data, for its first year enrollment.  The petitioners indicated that they plan to enroll 266 students during their first year of operation.
