RECOMMENDATION: Adopt resolution requesting waiver of Education Code section 37202 related to the equity length of time of school day for transitional kindergarten (TK) programs and authorize submittal of waiver to the California Department of Education.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None specific to waiver.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: The district consulted with the District Advisory Committee (DAC) on Compensatory Education on February 19, 2014.  

 

BACKGROUND: Transitional kindergarten is a relatively new educational opportunity for children who turn five between September 2 and December 2. It is the first of a two-year kindergarten program that uses an age-appropriate curricula aligned to the Kindergarten Common Core Standards. Transitional kindergarten acts as a bridge between preschool and kindergarten and is designed to provide the youngest learners with the pivotal skills, experiences, and habits of mind needed for success in kindergarten and beyond.
 

San Diego Unified is intent on leveraging early learning programs (preschool, transitional kindergarten, kindergarten) to eliminate the achievement gap that exists among groups of students. To this end, we have targeted the following key leverage points:

  1. Establish an academic staircase for the youngest learners:
    1. A three-hour academic program in preschool aligned to the California Preschool Learning Foundations
    2. A modified day academic program (3.5 - 4 hours) in transitional kindergarten in which students are introduced to the California Common Core State Standards for kindergarten
    3. A full day academic program in kindergarten in which students master the California Common Core State Standards.
       
  2. Establish a districtwide commitment to transitional kindergarten. Currently, half the district schools provide this “extra” year of instruction for TK eligible students. In order to assure a quality neighborhood school for every child, we are prepared to extend this program to all district schools beginning in the 2014-15 instructional year.
     
  3. Embed professional development within the fabric of the early learning structures to build teacher capacity. The intent is to assure that every child has access to best first teaching and, as needed, high-quality, research-based interventions. Building instructional expertise at every school site is essential in assuring that every child learns to read well and on time.

San Diego Unified is requesting to waive EC Section 37202, the equity length of time requirement for transitional kindergarten and kindergarten programs. EC Section 37202 requires that all students at a given grade level in a district receive “an equal length” of instructional time. The district currently offers an extended-day kindergarten program which substantively exceeds the maximum four hour school day (EC 46110). San Diego Unified is requesting flexibility in determining the length of the transitional kindergarten program in order to provide a clear and appropriate academic staircase for our youngest students.

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: On January 30, 2014, the audits were presented to the Independent Citizen's Oversight Finance Audit subcommittee, and on February 20, 2014, to the full Independent Citizen's Oversight committee.

 

BACKGROUND: On November 4, 2008, the voters of San Diego County approved, by more than the required 55 percent favorable vote, Proposition S, authorizing the issuance and sale of general obligation bonds, not to exceed $2,100,000,000.  On November 6, 2012, the voters of San Diego County approved, by more than the required 55 percent favorable vote, Proposition Z, authorizing the issuance and sale of general obligation bonds, not to exceed $2,800,000,000.

 

The passage of Proposition 39 in November 2000, amended the California Constitution to include accountability provisions.  Specifically, the district must conduct an annual

independent performance audit to ensure that funds have been expended only on the specific projects listed in the bond project list, as well as an annual, independent financial audit of the proceeds from the sale of the bond until all of the proceeds have been expended for facilities projects.

 

The fiscal year 2012-13 Performance and Financial Audits presented to the Audit Subcommittee of the Propositions S and Z Independent Citizens Oversight Committee on January 30, 2014.  The final audits, incorporating the suggestions of the committee and district staff were delivered by Christy White Accounting (CWA) to the district on February 10, 2014.  On February 20, 2014, the audits were presented to the full Independent Citizens Oversight Committee.

 

In its financial audit, CWA found that the districts financial statements "present fairly, in all material respects, the financial position of the Propositions S and Z Bond Building Fund as of June 30, 2013, and the results of its operations for the fiscal year ended June 30, 2013, in conformity with accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America."  This is the highest opinion that can be bestowed on the financial statements of a school bond program.

 

In its performance audit, CWA found that the Propositions S and Z bond program "complied, in all material respects, with California Proposition 39, as incorporated in Article 13A of the California Constitution and Education Code Section 15264 et seq. during the fiscal year that ended on June 30, 2012."  This is the highest opinion that can be bestowed on operational performance of a school bond program.

BACKGROUND:  In alignment with the district’s Vision 2020 and Quality Schools in Every Neighborhood, an Executive Director, Human Resources and Director, Human Resources is needed to ensure effective, efficient, timely and accuracy management of staffing processes and procedure that will positively impact service to schools and programs.

 

The work of the Executive Director, Human Resources will focus on:

  • planning, organizing, and managing the daily operations of assigned functions within Human Resources.
  • directing the process of implementing classified staffing.
  • streamlining processes and procedures to provide timely delivery of high quality services to the district.
  • assisting with long-range strategic planning of human resources goals and objectives and the development of measures of achievement and effectiveness.
  • developing, implementing, and evaluating support programs and interventions.

The work of the Director, Human Resources will focus on:

  • monitoring certificated and classified staffing assignments to determine vacant and excess positions according to staffing formulas.
  • directing the evaluation of credentials, experience and education of teaching applicants and teachers returning from leaves of absence in relation to job requirements of vacant positions.
  • developing personnel policies, procedures and contract language related to assigned functions and implement, as appropriate.
  • directing the district’s response to claims of harassment.
  • selecting, training, and evaluating the performance of assigned staff; develop work schedules; interview and select employees and recommend transfers, reassignment, termination and disciplinary actions.
