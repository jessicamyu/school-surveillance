2. Resolution In Support of SB 30, Prohibiting Public Contracting with Any Person Who Is Providing Goods or Services to Federal Government for Construction of Federally-Funded Wall, Fence, or Other Barrier Along California’s Southern Border (Barrera/Beiser)
Meeting
Apr 25, 2017 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
No

RECOMMENDATION: Trustees Richard Barrera and Kevin Beiser will ask that the board adopt a resolution in support of proposed Senate Bill (SB) 30 which, if enacted, would prohibit public agencies, commencing January 1, 2018, from awarding any contract with any person that, at the time of bid or proposal for a new contract or renewal of an existing contract, is providing or has provided goods or services to the federal government for the construction of a federally-funded wall, fence, or other barrier along California's southern border.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  The United States has begun contractor selection for construction of a wall on the US-Mexico border, aimed at preventing illegal migration.   SB 30, proposed by Senator Lara, provides that the proposed border wall will do serious economic, social and environmental harm; and cites opposition to construction of this wall based upon the level of commerce and cultural history in the border region, the presence of endangered and threatened likely to be harmed, and its effect on a recent binational treaty between the U.S. and Mexico to address pollution in the border region.  

 

The proposed bill, as amended, would prohibit the State of California from awarding any contract with a person who is providing services to the federal government for construction of any wall, fence, or barrier along California's southern border or who has done so in the past.  If enacted, the bill will also amend the definition of a "responsible bidder" in California Public Contract Code section 1103, to exclude any person who has or is providing goods and services to the federal government for such a project.  This effectively prevents the district and other local public agencies with awarding any contract for goods and services (except construction services ) in excess of $88,300 or any contract for construction services in excess of $15,000 with such a person because, Public Contract Code section 20111 requires that the district and other public agencies must award such contracts to the "lowest responsible bidder."  Contracts not governed by section 20111 would be unaffected.  

 

"Person" is defined broadly to include an individual, partnership, joint venture, association, or any other organization or any combination thereof, whether or not located within the state.  The proposed bill is silent as to what it means to "provide goods and services."  Therefore, it is unclear if this would prohibit purchasing from suppliers or subcontractors to federal contractors, for example, or it refers to those persons directly contracting with the federal government.

BACKGROUND:  On Tuesday, April 4, the City of San Diego’s Public Utilities Department began testing district schools and schools operating on district property for possible lead contamination in water.  Testing is being done as a result of a December 20, 2016, permit action by the State Water Resources Control Board (SWRCB) Division of Drinking Water, directing all community water providers, including the City of San Diego, to test for lead in drinking water at K-12 schools, if requested by school districts.  Lead is a naturally occurring element which has historically been used in plumbing, paint, and other building materials.  There are many potential sources of lead exposure, such as old deteriorated paint, lead in the air from industrial emissions, lead in the soil from cars using leaded gasoline, consumer products (imported candy, medicines, toys, dishes, etc.), and plumbing components such as faucets and valves made of brass or lead-containing solder.  Although plumbing components installed prior to the 1990's contained more lead than new materials, the amount of lead in the drinking water cannot be predicted by the age of building.  During the testing period, the district has initiated a flushing protocol at all schools to reduce the risk of exposure to any possible lead in potable drinking water at San Diego Unified.

 

Information on the district's water sampling effort, and additional resources, can be found at: www.sandiegounified.org/watersampling

BACKGROUND:

Proposed Project
The proposed project would occur on a portion of the CPMA Middle School campus, located at 5050 Conrad Avenue within the city of San Diego, California.  Specifically, the proposed project includes improvements to the northeastern portion of the campus, where an existing decomposed granite (DG) field is located.  The proposed project would remove the DG and install a natural turf field and a stabilized walking track that would provide a new recreational space for District students and nearby residents.  It is envisioned that the joint use field can be used as two separate playing fields, the larger field would be 165 feet by 300 feet and the smaller field would be 105 feet by 150 feet.  The proposed project also includes the installation of 10-foot-high replacement fencing, gates, a drinking fountain and an ADA crosswalk from the parking lot the joint use fields.  Please refer to Attachment 1 for a detailed site plan.

 

Students at CPMA Middle School would have exclusive use of the joint use fields from 30 minutes before the campus opens until 30 minutes after the dismissal bell on Monday through Friday.  The community would have use of the field 30 minutes after school until 30 minutes before school, Monday through Thursday, and 30 minutes after school on Friday until 30 minutes before school on Monday.  The proposed project does not include the installation of lighting; accordingly, the fields would generally be closed at dusk and not opened until dawn.  On the weekdays when school is in session, district custodians would open and close the pedestrian gates leading to the joint use fields.  The City will contract with a security company to open and close the gates during periods when school is not in session.

 

Each joint use field may be used for organized sports activities.  The size of a joint use field typically dictates what type of organized sports activity could occur, for example, the larger field is suitable for age 11 and younger junior soccer league play and practices. The smaller field is suitable for age 8 and younger junior soccer league play and practices. It should be noted, however, that each field could be used for a variety of sports.

 

Parking for the proposed project would generally be accommodated within existing parking areas at the site.  Although the district is not subject to City parking requirements or other development regulations, the district would comply with these regulations and would provide adequate parking.  Parking requirements for the site were determined based on the City’s Municipal Code (Section 142.050 et seq.).  The Municipal Code does not specify parking requirements for parks or sports fields; however, the land use for a health club requires one additional space per the maximum number of authorized players per court.  The fields would require 46 parking spaces (assuming two teams per field with 11 players per team, plus one additional space per field).  As CPMA Middle School currently has 96 off-street parking spaces, the required parking for the project would be accommodated.

 

Environmental Review
The project is subject to CEQA, Public Resources Code (PRC) section 21000 et. seq., and its implementing Guidelines (California Code of Regulations, Title 14, Division 6, Chapter 3, sections 15000 et. seq., as amended). The project, however, is exempt from expanded environmental review under as further described below and in the attached NOE (Attachment 2) and environmental checklist (Attachment 3):

 

Existing Facilities (Class 1)

Class 1 consists of the operation, repair, maintenance, permitting, leasing, licensing, or minor alteration of existing public private structures, facilities, mechanical equipment, or topographical features, involving negligible or no expansion of use beyond that existing at the time of the lead agency’s determination (CEQA Guidelines §15301).

 

The proposed project includes alterations to existing areas that are currently used for recreation at the school, including the DG fields. Minor alterations include converting the existing DG fields to natural turf fields, a walking track, and the replacement of fencing. The usage of the joint-use fields outside of school hours by residents would be similar to the usage of the fields that currently occur during school hours, and therefore would be considered a negligible expansion.

 

Minor Additions to Schools (Class 14)

Class 14 consists of minor additions to existing schools within existing school grounds where the addition does not increase original student capacity by more than 25% or ten classrooms, whichever is less. The addition of portable classrooms is included in this exemption (CEQA Guidelines §15314).

 

The proposed project entails the installation of a joint use field, a running track, a drinking fountain and replacement fencing within the existing CMPA Middle School Campus. The proposed project would not induce any increase in student enrollment. In addition, the proposed project does not include the construction of any new classrooms or other facilities that would increase the student capacity of the campus by more than 25 percent or ten classrooms.

 

Normal Operations of Facilities for Public Gatherings (Class 23)

Class 23 consists of the normal operations of existing facilities for public gatherings for which the facilities were designed, where there is a past history of the facility being used for the same or similar kind of purpose.  Past history is defined as a similar kind of activity that has been occurring for at least three years and that there is a reasonable expectation that the future occurrence of the activity would not represent a change in the operation of the facility (CEQA Guidelines §15323).

 

The proposed project entails the installation of a joint use field, a walking track, and replacement fencing within the existing CMPA Middle School campus.  These uses would replace the existing DG field in an area adjacent to existing play courts.  These areas have been used for recess for several decades, since the school began operation.

 

Recess typically involves students playing several types of games and typically includes recreational activities, which can include basketball, soccer, or other sporting activities. The future occurrence of the use of these areas for recreation by the general public would be substantially similar to that which currently occurs at the campus.

 

Furthermore, the project would not represent a change in the operation of the facility as it would still operate as a school and would be available for the public to use for recreational purposes outside of school hours.  Therefore, there is a reasonable expectation that the future occurrence of the activity would not represent a change in the operation of the facility. 

 

As set forth in Attachments 1 through 3, staff has determined that no exception applies to defeat application of the categorical exemption, and approval of the project would not compromise the environmental quality of the site and surrounding area. Approval of this item approves the Project as described and authorizes and directs staff to file a Notice of Exemption with the San Diego County Recorder/Clerk.

FISCAL IMPACT: This Second Amendment adds an additional $300,000.00 to the Agreement, for a new total not-to-exceed revenue amount of $1,430,000.00.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: Revenue for the Period May 25, 2016 through May 24, 2017:  $185,000.00.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:  On April 28, 2015, the board approved an Agreement for the district to provide rental of transportation equipment (buses) to the 22nd Agricultural District to transport patrons attending the San Diego County Fair (Fair) from various locations.

 

On April 26, 2016, the board approved the First Amendment to the Agreement for the district to continue providing the same services to the 22nd Agricultural District to transport patrons attending the San Diego County Fair (Fair) from various locations.

 

This Second Amendment with 22nd District Agricultural Association (DAA) will allow district to continue providing services. Equipment operators will be employees of the 22nd Agricultural District, with proper licensing and documentation provided to the district in prior to using of district buses.  The 22nd Agricultural District will provide adequate insurance and release the district from liability associated with the transportation of Fair patrons.  The buses pursuant to this Agreement are otherwise idle.  Therefore, use of the buses from June 2, 2017 to July 6, 2017, will not impact district operations.
