RECOMMENDATION:  Approve in concept the San Diego County Association of African American Educator's Blueprint to Accelerate Achievement of African American and African Students presented to the board on May 25, 2010, subject to the review by the Task Force and Focus Area Groups, and other appropriate staff.  Specific Superintendent recommendations will be presented to the board upon completion of the staff, Focus Area Groups and Task Force reviews.    All recommendations will be developed to complement and comply with federal mandates, California Education Code, district policy, collective bargaining agreements, and other authoritative directives.  The recommendations will also be built to support the staffing and budgeting realities of the district during this prolonged state-wide fiscal crisis.  

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None at this time. As part of the review process, finance staff will evaluate the financial implications of the recommendations contained in the Blueprint document.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY:  None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None at this time.  As part of the review process, Human Resources and the Deputy Superintendent Academics staff will evaluate the staffing implications of the recommendations contained in the Blueprint document.

 

BACKGROUND:  On May 25, 2010, the San Diego County Association of African American Educator’s (AAAE) presented the Blueprint to Accelerate the Achievement of African American and African Students (Blueprint) to the Board of Education of the San Diego Unified School District.  The board referred the Blueprint to the Superintendent to convene a Task Force for review and input of the Blueprint document and directed the Superintendent to report back to the board. 

 

Following the summer break, the Superintendent convened a Blueprint Task Force comprised of AAAE members, district staff, union leaders, and community organization representatives.  The Blueprint Task Force membership roster is attached.  The Blueprint Task Force has met on ­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­­September 9, 2010, September 30, 2010, and October 25, 2010, and is scheduled to meet again on December 6, 2010.  Four Focus Area groups have been established and the membership for each of the Focus Area groups is indicated on the Task Force membership roster.  The Focus Areas include,

 

1.  Policies and Procedures

2.  Graduation Rate

3.  Gender-Specific Needs

4.  Parent/Family Support

 

The Focus Area groups will begin meeting in November to fully review the recommendations of AAAE and will provide comments and recommendations to the Superintendent.  The Superintendent will meet with union representatives to discuss those areas of the Blueprint recommendations related to employee contractual issues. In addition, staff will review and evaluate the policy implications and consistency with the board's 2020 Vision, Goals for Student Achievement, and the Community-Based Reform Model.  Finance staff will provide the fiscal implications and the Human Resources and Deputy Superintendent Academics will provide staffing impacts of the proposed recommendations.  The Superintendent's recommendations to the board will include a recommended scope, proposed actions related to each of the four Focus Areas, budgetary implications, a prioritized implementation plan and a proposed timeline to address the needs of our African American and African students.

FISCAL IMPACT:  Increase in 2009-10 P-2 revenue of approximately $87,727.00.

 

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

BACKGROUND:  California state law permits a school district to file a claim for Average Daily Attendance (ADA) when certain emergency conditions occur (such as an epidemic) which can cause a district to experience a material decrease in ADA. During the 2009-10 school year, San Diego Unified School District had 120 schools reporting a total of 2,217 absences that were directly traced to the H1N1 Influenza epidemic. Since the district did experience a decrease in attendance due to the H1N1 epidemic, we can apply to the California Department of Education (CDE) for a credit of the lost days of attendance.

 

Attached is the J-13A waiver “Material Decrease” form including the governing board’s affidavit which needs to be signed by all of the board members. Once the signatures are obtained, the original signed forms will then be submitted to the San Diego County Superintendent of Schools for approval.  The county office will then submit the forms to the CDE.  We request that the board members sign three original documents.

 

Once written approval is obtained from the CDE, the credited days of student attendance will be substituted for the actual (decreased) days by submitting a revised P-2 Attendance Report for the 2009-10 school year.
