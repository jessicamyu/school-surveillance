Subject
A.04. Title I: Clarification of Direction to Superintendent on Implementation of New Title I Funding Guidelines (Board Governance Policy: OE-10) -- 5:00 P.M., AUDITORIUM
Meeting
Feb 17, 2009 - Special Meeting
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
No
At the January 27, 2009, regular meeting of the Board of Education, the board took action on the 2009-10 Title I Eligibility Ranking and Participation, to distribute 2009-10 Title I funds to schools based on “Scenario 4” in the staff report: 1) Elementary, middle and high schools with poverty levels of 60 to 100 percent will receive Title I funding; and 2) There will be no funding tiers – per pupil allocation of $432 for all percentage levels.

At the February 10, 2009, regular meeting the Board of Education, the board reconsidered the prior action, and a majority of the board supported changing to a 2009-10 distribution of Title I funds to elementary, middle and high schools with poverty levels of 40 percent and above, rather than 60 percent and above. However, based on uncertainty during the motion, amendment and voting process, it was not clear whether a majority of the board endorsed a per pupil allocation formula based on a tiered structure or a flat-rate structure. Specifically, it is currently not clear whether a majority the board endorses the tiered structure in “Scenario 1” of the staff report ($447/pupil for 85-100 percent, $296/pupil for 60-84 percent, $196/pupil for 40-59 percent), or the flat-rate structure in “Scenario 2” in the staff report $343/pupil for all percentage levels).

This agenda item is to allow the board to resolve this uncertainty.
