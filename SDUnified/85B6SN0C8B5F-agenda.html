RECOMMENDATION: Approve the amended charter petition for Gompers Preparatory Academy.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: The Office of Charter Schools recommends the Board of Education approve the amendments to the charter for Gompers Preparatory Academy (GPA). The amendments include a request to offer a 6 - 12 grade program and an update on measureable student outcomes. GPA would merge the Gompers Charter Middle School (GCMS) 6 - 8 grade school into the GPA school and become a comprehensive 6 - 12 grade school. If the board desires additional options beyond approval, these options could be:
  • Approve the amended petition with conditions. In essence, the amendments are approved with the conditions set by the Board of Education.
  • Deny the amended petition. As the result of choosing this option the current Gompers schools (GCMS and GPA) both continue as separate entities. The existing GCMS charter slated to expire this school year would have to be renewed; the current GPA charter remains in effect through 2014.


Again, the aforementioned are cited solely to inform the board of its existing options.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Special Education Memorandum of Understanding template with charter spchools.

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: In collaboration with charter schools, the Office of Charter Schools developed a Special Education Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) template to outline the relationship between the district and charter schools for the delivery of Special Education services. Under the MOU that was collaboratively developed, Charter schools will have three options for the delivery of Special Education services at charter schools. The options are the following:
  1. Charter schools elect to be considered as a public school of the district for the purposes of special education services and funding. The school agrees to pay a local contribution to the district, and the district will provide special education services.
  2. Charter schools are designated as schools within the district's Special Education Local Plan Area (SELPA). With this option, charter schools may hire their own special education staff, independent of the district, and the district will reimburse the charter school for the actual costs of providing special education services, not to exceed the average annual salary and benefits of the district for equivalent service providers.
  3. Charter schools are designated as schools of another SELPA. This option allows charter schools to join a SELPA other than San Diego Unified. Under this option, the charter schools will meet the requirements of the SELPA under which they will be served. The district retains general oversight responsibilities over the charter schools exercising this option.


Staff recommends that the Board of Education approve this MOU template, with the understanding that each individually signed Special Education MOU will return for board approval as a consent item when schools sign the agreement and when individual charters are renewed.

D.05. First Reading of the Local Educational Agency (LEA) 2009-10 Plan, including Addendum, as District in Program Improvement Year 1; BUDGETED: YES, TITLE I (OE-2)
Meeting
Dec 1, 2009 - - Special Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Information, First Reading
RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a First Reading of the 2009-10 Local Education Agency (LEA) Plan and Addendum. The LEA will be submitted to the board for action on December 15, 2009.

FISCAL IMPACT: $3.9 million. NCLB regulations specify the district is required to set aside 10 percent of its Title I allocation for high quality professional development

PRIOR YEAR EXPENSE HISTORY: None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

BACKGROUND: LEA Plan -Each LEA must develop a single, coordinated, and comprehensive Plan that describes the educational services for all students that can be used to guide implementation of federal and state-funded programs, the allocation of resources, and reporting requirements. The development of such a plan involves a continuous cycle of assessment, parent and community involvement, planning, implementation, monitoring, and evaluation. The duration of the Plan should be five years. The Plan should be periodically reviewed and updated as needed, but at least once each year.

LEA Addendum – A school district that does not make AYP in the same content area (English language arts or mathematics) and does not meet AYP criteria in the same content area in each grade span noted for two consecutive years is identified for program improvement. Accordingly, in July 2009, the San Diego Unified School District was identified as Title I Program Improvement Year 1. NCLB regulations specify the district is required to set aside 10 percent of its Title I allocation for high quality professional development.

LEAs identified as Program Improvement shall develop or revise an LEA Plan in consultation with parents, school staff and others. This requirement is met by writing an addendum to the LEA Plan which addresses the strategies and actions the district will implement to meet the needs of its students, primarily the English learner and students with disabilities subgroups.

The approval of the LEA Plan and Addendum will enable the district to meet the NCLB requirements and implement the specific strategies designed to meet the identified needs of the district’s students. The proposed strategies and actions spelled out in the Addendum are the result of reviewing student data, analyzing the three state-developed surveys taken by staff and careful planning by committees consisting of parents, central office and site staff.
