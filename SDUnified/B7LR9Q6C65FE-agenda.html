Each year, Americans observe National Hispanic Heritage Month by celebrating the histories, cultures and contributions of American citizens whose ancestors came from Spain, Mexico, the Caribbean and Central and South America and 2019 marks the 51st anniversary of the national celebration of National Hispanic Heritage Month.

San Diego County has a population of over 1.4 million Latinos and San Diego Unified School District serves over 45,000 Latino students within its educational system.

 

The San Diego Unified School District recognizes the diversity reflected within the City of San Diego as well as its students and staff and respects the contributions, culture, and heritage of Latinx in this community.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution Establishing a Multi-Agency Task Force to Recommend Comprehensive Strategies and Protocols for Inter-Agency Cooperation for the Protection of District Students. The Board of Education directs the Superintendent to convene a task force charged with:

 

  • Identifying best practices among child-serving institutions locally, nationally, and internationally which have developed comprehensive inter-agency protocols and practices designed to protect children from sexual harassment and abuse.
  • Reviewing the district's external relationships with other agencies involved in the protection of children from sexual harassment and abuse, and comparing those efforts to the aforementioned practices.
  • Recommending to the board necessary actions to create a comprehensive multi-agency cooperation protocol for the district regarding the reporting, response, and prevention of sexual harassment and abuse of district students.

 

By the second board meeting in September 2019, the Superintendent will select and recommend to the board for approval members of the task force, which shall include:

 

  • One or more community advocates with expertise in institutional child abuse protection
  • Two district students
  • Two parents of current district students
  • A representative from the County of San Diego Child Welfare Services
  • A representative of the Chadwick Center at Rady Children's Hospital
  • A representative from the San Diego County District Attorney's Office
  • A representative from the City of San Diego Police Department
  • A representative from the San Diego City Attorney's Office
  • A representative from the San Diego County Office of Education
  • A representative from the San Diego Psychological Association
  • A representative from the San Diego Unified School District Police Department
  • One or more academics with expertise on research-based best practices in the institutional protection of child sexual harassment and abuse
  • All relevant district leadership, including, as needed, representatives from the district's Legal Services, Title IX Office, Human Resources, Counseling and Instructional Departments
  • One representative each from the San Diego Education Association (SDEA), the California School Employees Association (CSEA), and the Administrators Association of San Diego (AASD) certificated unit who have expertise in the area of child abuse and harassment

 

In addition, two current members of the Board of Education will be appointed to serve on the task force.

 

The task force will present its preliminary findings and recommendations to the board by the end of January 2020.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

In recent years the Superintendent and General Counsel have conducted a comprehensive review of district policies, procedures and regulations to improve the district's capacity to prevent and respond to incidences of student abuse. Protecting district students from sexual harassment and abuse requires the district to work effectively with a variety of public agencies and community-based organizations. The San Diego community is rich with leaders, organizations and resources that are committed to partnering with the district to protect students. Students, parents, district staff, and the public must be clear and informed about the district's efforts to protect students from sexual harassment and abuse, including the district's external relationships with all relevant community-based organizations and public agencies that are involved in the protection of students.

FISCAL IMPACT: None

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  N/A

 

BACKGROUND: Ninety-seven percent of publishing climate scientists along with organizations including the US National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), National Association for the Advancement of Science, National Academy of Sciences, and the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change affirm that anthropogenic climate change is driving dire consequences for the planet. The current federal administration undermines the validity of climate change and suppressed findings by the US Geological Survey that predicts over $100 billion in climate damages to California by 2100. The current administration intends to pull the United States, the second largest emissions contributor in the world, out of the Paris Climate Agreement as soon as the option is available in 2020.

 

Congress is considering emerging legislation like the Green New Deal, which addresses the climate crisis at the scale of the problem and would ensure that the US reenters the Paris Climate Agreement. The 2019 United National Climate Action Summit is to be held on September 23, 2019, in New York with potential to create US climate action. The San Diego Unified Board of Education has previously recognized the threat from global warming by calling on the California State Teachers' Retirement System (STRS) and the California Public Employees' Retirement System (PERS) to divest their investment portfolios of stocks in fossil fuel companies. The Board of Education has adopted Resolutions in Support of Community Choice Aggregation, PTA-Climate Change is a Children's Issue, and Supporting Climate Science Literacy. 

 

The San Diego Unified School District's "Dream Big Solutions for a Sustainable Future" plan ensures district climate action and climate consciousness and its Climate Action Plan commits to 100% renewable energy by 2035. San Diego Unified is taking steps toward meeting its goals by retrofitting schools with more efficient air conditioning and lighting systems; and more than 29,000 lighting fixtures have been converted to LED, offsetting 1,300 metric tons of CO2 gas emissions; and projected district solar energy production by the end of 2020 will offset 8,100 tons of CO2. San Diego Unified has funded the construction of hundreds of acres of parks and green spaces through the joint-use partnership with the City of San Diego; and reduced litter and plastic waste by nearly 4.7 million pieces through the replacement of utensil packets with individual forks, spoons, and napkins. Students from San Diego Unified have taken a leadership role in confronting global warming through participation in "Green Teams," recycling clubs, "Planet Protectors," and other student groups; and the "Love Food Not Waste" Program has prevented 202,775 pounds of prepared food from ending up in landfills, provided 169,120 meals to the hungry, reduced food costs, and eliminated 110,107 pounds of CO2 gas emissions. 

 

There will be a Global Climate Strike and Walkout on September 20, 2019, led by Greta Thunberg and other youth climate leaders to demonstrate to world leaders at all levels of government that the people, especially the youth, are not satisfied with the insufficient response to climate change.  The San Diego Unified School District supports and is proud of students' leadership in calling for aggressive climate action at the local, national and global levels and their participation in the Global Climate Walkout. The San Diego Unified School District encourages all professional educators to support student Global Climate Walkout events provided that educational outcomes are demonstrated (e.g., preparing an essay or video on the experience, documenting planning time). The San Diego Unified School District supports the free speech rights of every student, and will ensure students are able to help plan and participate in Global Climate Walkout events in support of the Global Climate Walkout on September 20, 2019.
