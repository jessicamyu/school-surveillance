RECOMMENDATION: Approve revisions to Administrative Regulation 5121: Grades/ Evaluation of Student Achievement as called for in the July 21, 2020, Freedom Summer Workshop.  Approve staff recommendations for the Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee on Racial Justice and Equity composition and community representatives. FISCAL IMPACT: None

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

BACKGROUND: On June 23, 2020, the board adopted a Resolution in the Matter of Supporting a Call for Freedom Summer 2020 and focus on eliminating those barriers that prevent our students of color from receiving an equitable education. On July 21, 2020, selected student leaders co-presented a workshop focused on the issue of racial justice and equity. Students shared their lived experiences in our district. The board approved staff recommendations to strengthening the district's key strategies in achieving racial justice and equity, with an emphasis on student voice as we build anti-racist and restorative school communities. Each month staff will provide with an update on the implementation of the approved recommendations in the following areas: 

  • Staff Diversity

  • Implementation of Ethnic Studies for All Students

  • Interrupting Discriminatory Grading Practices

  • Dismantling Discriminatory Discipline Policies and Practices

  • Building Anti-Racist and Restorative School Communities

Staff will also provide an update on access to advanced studies coursework that provide early college credit opportunities for students in grades 9-12. 

 

Staff will also present recommendations for the composition of the Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee on Racial Justice and Equity and community representatives for approval and have been advised by Andra Greene not to discuss specific racial preferences and, instead, discuss membership preferences in terms of "those students most affected by racial justice issues."  Racial preferences remain illegal in the state of CA and specific quotas are prohibited under both state and federal law.

RECOMMENDATION: Conduct a public hearing and approve a Resolution certifying that the district has provided sufficient standards-aligned textbooks, instructional materials, or both. 

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  The district may use funding from various sources, including state Local Control Funding Formula funds and state lottery funds to purchase standards-aligned textbooks and instructional materials. Newly adopted materials are provided based on actual enrollments the first year of an adoption. Schools are responsible for maintaining sufficiency during the remaining years of core adoptions by ordering from the district's Instructional Materials Office, using an Instructional Materials Order Form or by ordering textbooks and instructional materials using the Destiny Textbook Management System, and/or providing student access to digital versions of Board approved instructional materials using a computer.
 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: In accordance with Education Code 60119, each school district’s governing board must hold a noticed public hearing, no later than the eighth week of the school year, to determine whether each pupil in the district has sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials, or both, in the following subjects: mathematics, science, history-social science, and English language arts. The governing board must also determine whether each student enrolled in a foreign language or health course has been provided sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials. According to Williams Legislation, sufficient textbooks or instructional materials means that each pupil, including English language learners, must be provided access to a district-adopted textbook or instructional materials, or both, to use in class and to take home. However, the district does not have to provide each pupil with two sets of instructional materials. The materials may be in a digital format as long as each pupil, at a minimum, has and can access the same materials in the class and to take home, as all other pupils in the same class or course in the school district and has the ability to use and access them at home.  The board is required to adopt a resolution setting forth its determination that there are sufficient standards-aligned textbooks or instructional materials as applicable.  
 
Current Williams legislation also requires that science classrooms have available basic science laboratory equipment for students in science laboratory courses offered in Grades 9 to 12.  The San Diego County Office of Education developed a science laboratory equipment worksheet to identify basic core, lab equipment for science laboratory courses.  All schools conducted inventories and principals verified and submitted a “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency.”
 
Williams Settlement Legislation requires districts and schools to closely examine student access to textbooks and instructional materials and gather evidence to use as the basis of the district's annual resolution of sufficiency.  Administrative Circulars 52, 53, and 54, dated September 2, 2020, were posted notifying school sites (not subject to a Williams site visit) of the need to conduct a school site inventory and complete a “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency.”  Principals were directed to complete an instructional materials inventory, remedy any insufficiencies, and have confirmed instructional materials sufficiency by completing and returning the “Principal Verification Statement of Instructional Materials Sufficiency” to the Instructional Materials Office by September 18, 2020. 
 
Twenty-five elementary schools, four K-8 schools, six middle schools, and six senior high schools were also subject to an instructional materials audit performed by the San Diego County Office of Education (SDCOE).  All 41 schools submitted the necessary instructional materials survey documentation required by the SDCOE to the Instructional Materials Office no later than September 25, 2020.  These documents were reviewed by district staff and provided to SDCOE by the Instructional Materials Office. Each of the 41 schools were found to have sufficient instructional materials

Meeting
Oct 13, 2020 - Regular Meeting, 5:00 p.m.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt Resolution in the Matter of Recognizing Indigenous People's Day.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None. 

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Unified School District recognizes that the Indigenous Peoples of the lands that would later become known as the Americas have occupied these lands since time immemorial and the fact that San Diego is built upon the homelands and villages of the Indigenous Peoples of this region, without whom the building of the City would not have been possible. The district values the many contributions made to our community through Indigenous Peoples’ knowledge, labor, technology, science, philosophy, arts and the deep cultural contribution that has substantially shaped the charter of the City of San Diego. The district has a responsibility to oppose the systematic racism towards Indigenous people in the United States, which perpetuates high rates of poverty and income inequality, exacerbating disproportionate health, education, and social crises, and must seek to combat prejudice and eliminate discrimination stemming from colonization and to promote awareness, understanding, and good relations among indigenous peoples and all other segments of our district. The district promotes the closing of the equity gap for Indigenous Peoples through policies and practices that reflect the experiences of Indigenous Peoples, ensure greater access and opportunity, and honor our nation’s indigenous roots, history, and contributions. California State Assembly Bill 738, approved by the Governor on October 9, 2017, requires the state to develop a model curriculum that encourages school districts to offer a standards-based Native American studies curriculum. The district seeks to join the hundreds of other municipalities, school districts, and universities that recognize the second Monday of October as Indigenous Peoples’ Day to be observed as a day in solidarity with indigenous peoples and shall encourage staff to utilize this day as an opportunity to reflect upon the ongoing struggles of Indigenous People of this land, to celebrate the thriving cultures and values of the Indigenous Peoples of our region, and to stand in solidarity with Indigenous Peoples elsewhere.
