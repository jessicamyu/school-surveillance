BACKGROUND: In an effort to continue our improvement of procedures and processes for centralized procurement and contracting district wide, we are implementing a new process streamlining our Independent Contractor Agreements. Our goal is to limit and reduce the number of contracted service items that are included in this item for approval via the ratification process. Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

Chiefs should work with requestors to reduce and eliminate the need for ratified ICA's. Effective immediately, after an ICA is submitted to and approved by the respective deputy or chief, it will then be sent to the Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Department. The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

RECOMMENDATION:  Approve the resolution to seek offers in concept for sale, lease, joint use,  or other instruments of development and for proposals of joint occupancy between the district and developers for certain district properties identified by the Real Estate Department and the Ad Hoc Real Estate Committee. (Regular majority 3-2 vote required)


FISCAL IMPACT:  None in the immediate future.  The board has already authorized the funding for a consultant to assist in the development of this effort.


PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  Real Estate Department staff members will be engaged in supporting this effort and in evaluating, with the consultant the responses.


CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:

 

BACKGROUND: The board has previously approved the “sale and/or lease” of seven district excess properties to provide support to the 2012-13 budget deficit in the amount of $20,000,000.  The purpose of this resolution is to expand the opportunity for revenue that may not entail the sale of district assets.    

 

The district’s goals are as follows:

  • Maximize revenues to district;
  • Maintain ownership and control of district property; and
  • Provide flexibility over use of district property

Through this effort, the district will be especially focusing on the concept of “joint occupancy ” agreements with developers.  Joint occupancy allows the district to maintain ownership of all or a significant portion of a property in exchange for a portion of the property to be developed being identified for public use through a request for proposal process.  This allows the district to receive revenue without losing control or ownership while maintaining flexibility over its use.  There may be certain situations where through this effort the district may lose the functionality of a particular piece of property.  In that case, the developer would be required as part of the contract, to replace that functionality, at its expense, to the satisfaction of the district to ensure that students continue to be served. 

 

This resolution is also for the purpose of seeking any other opportunities for revenue generation for the properties identified herein that might be suggested by a prospective developer.  In every case, if a particular property is identified as a good candidate for revenue potential all appropriate community planning and stakeholder organizations will be contacted to provide input and obtain their feedback. 

 

The district is facing significant budget deficits in the near term future.  We are, through this resolution, seeking to utilize our real estate assets to assist in this effort through local revenue generation that cannot be taken away from us, without the concurrent loss of ownership of property. 

 

The Ad-Hoc Real Estate Committee that was created by the Board of Trustees has led this effort.  The Committee voted unanimously to recommend this resolution for board approval.
