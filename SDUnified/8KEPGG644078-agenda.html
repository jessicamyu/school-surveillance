RECOMMENDATION: Approve the Fiscal Year 2010-11 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report in Standardized Account Code Structure (SACS) Series Form.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: The Fiscal Year 2010-11 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report shows a total General Fund Ending Balance of $116.8 million.  Compared to the 2010-11 Adjusted Beginning Balance of $97.0 million, this reflects a surplus of $19.7 million. 

 

The 2010-11 Ending Balance of $116.8 million rolls into the Beginning Balance for the Adopted 2011-12 budget.  The 2011-12 budget was adopted with a projected Beginning Balance of $117.3 million.


PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: The Fiscal Year 2009-10 Unaudited Actuals Financial Report showed a total General Fund Ending Balance of $97.0 million. Compared to the 2009-10 Adjusted Beginning Balance of $164.0 million, this reflected deficit spending of $67.0 million.

 

The 2009-10 Ending Balance of $97.0 million rolled into the Beginning Balance for the Adopted 2010-11 budget. The 2010-11 budget was adopted with a projected Beginning Balance of $102.0 million.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: On May 20, 2003, the board approved the Single Budget Adoption Cycle for the district. The board adopted the 2010-11 budget on June 29, 2010. Under the Single Budget Adoption Cycle, California Education Code, Section 42100 states that on or before September 15, the governing board of each school district shall approve, in the format prescribed by the Superintendent of Public Instruction, the Unaudited Actual Financial Report of the district for the preceding fiscal year.

On November 4, 2008, San Diego voters approved Proposition S, a school bond measure that will provide $2.1 billion to improve and modernize San Diego Unified School District facilities.  On November 18, 2008, the board took action to establish an 11-member Independent Citizens’ Oversight Committee (ICOC) for Proposition S. As part of the Proposition and as required by law, the board appointed members to the ICOC to monitor how the district spends these tax dollars.

The membership for the Proposition S ICOC is selected from the following criteria:

  • One member that is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district
  • One member who is active in a senior citizens’ organization 
  • One member of a bona fide taxpayers’ association
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district 
  • One member who is the parent or guardian of a child enrolled in the district and is active in a parent/teacher organization

Board members each choose one appointee from the above criteria and each select an individual of their own choosing, "at-large."  The superintendent provides one appointee to serve "at-large."

 

Terms for membership on the Proposition S ICOC are for two years, and there is a two-term maximum. No employee or official of the district shall be appointed to the citizens’ oversight committee, nor any vendor, contractor or consultant of the district.
