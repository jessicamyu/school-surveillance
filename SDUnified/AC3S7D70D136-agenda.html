RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide information to the board and the public on Access to Broad and Challenging Curriculum (Vision 2020 Quality Indicator and corresponding LCAP Goal 2).  This report will focus on English Learners and includes an update to the report that was provided to the board and the public on January 24, 2017.

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination: All students have equitable access to a broad and challenging curriculum, aligned to California/common core state standards, that engages them in inquiry, critical thinking and creativity and provides them with a strong foundation in the academic disciplines, broad exposure to the liberal arts and opportunities to pursue individual interests.  Teachers are prepared to provide a broad and challenging curriculum.

 

LCAP Goal 2: Students have equitable access to rigorous, well-rounded, standards-aligned curricula and access to and skill in applying technologies to leverage learning, assuring readiness for a full range of post-graduation options.

 

Related LCAP Actions and Services:

  • 2.5.1.:  The district will provide, refine, and monitor Grades K-12 English Learner (EL) course placement and pathways.  
  • 2.5.2.:  Well-articulated English Learner programs that include access to advanced courses and learning opportunities that lead to college, career and community preparedness will be provided.  
  • 2.5.3.:  A coherent, sequential course of study from elementary to secondary for biliteracy, dual language, and world language programs will be provided.  
  • 2.5.4.:  The Academic Language Development (ALD) course will be expanded to more middle and high schools with Long-Term English Learners (LTEL).

Related LCAP Metrics: English Learner reclassification rates and numbers at school and district level; English Learners making at lease one year's progress in learning English and achieving proficiency in English as measured by the CELDT.

 

BACKGROUND:  At regular, public board meetings, Superintendent Marten provides a report that includes the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 Quality Indicators, and supporting LCAP actions, services, and baseline data.  Following the Superintendent's Vision 2020/LCAP report, cluster and advisory groups have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report and provide input and feedback on current LCAP implementation.  The complete LCAP document is available on the district website at https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

Sites proposed for inclusion in the grant have been identified based on specific grant criterion, San Diego Unified’s Vision 2020 goals, and the interest of community members and families, site administrators, teachers, and Area Superintendents.  Input from the Executive Director, Facilities Planning and Design, specific to the development of academic patterns within targeted clusters was critical in narrowing the selection of sites.  Exhibit 1 outlines federal site selection criterion, the proposed sites, and provides an overview of program design.

 

The MSAP grant application deadline is April 11, 2017.  Award notification is expected prior to or during September 2017.

RECOMMENDATION: Adopt the resolution recognizing Friday, March 31, 2017, as Cesar E. Chavez Day of Service and Learning, and encourage student participation in authorized school community service honoring the life and work of César E. Chavez.

 

BACKGROUND: The son of migrant farm workers, Cesar E. Chavez learned about the oppression of field laborers firsthand and directed his life goals and aspirations toward educating his fellow farm workers to understand their rights to a safe workplace and a just wage.

 

In 1962, Chavez founded the United Farm Workers of America. From his studies in civic action and his study of the work of Gandhi and Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr., Chavez developed and adhered to principles of non-violence in his crusade for peace and justice. César B. Chavez said, “Real education should consist of drawing the goodness and the best out of our own students. What better book can there be than the book of humanity.”

 

César E. Chavez and legions of loyal supporters dedicated themselves to the education of migrant workers and their children through the establishment of better schools, fighting to empower farm workers through voter registration, and teaching community members how to organize themselves to effectively represent their interests with government, school, and financial institutions.

 

California schools are authorized to commemorate César E. Chavez with student community service projects on Friday, March 31, and are directed to provide instruction on the farm labor movement in the United States and the role of César E. Chavez.

 

The successful leadership development of César E. Chavez Service Clubs, organized by San Diego Unified and Urban Corps of San Diego, are now being emulated in California’s most populated cities throughout the state.

 

San Diego Unified dedicated César E. Chavez Elementary School, in September 1997, to provide an education program for more than 700 students who will commemorate his birthday with special activities including a community march on Wednesday, March 15, 2017.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the Seventh Amendment to Option Agreement (PS-90-563-64) extending the option period for an additional 18 months to September 30, 2018, for the final remaining option to develop loft/commercial/retail space on Lot 1.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: $25,000 revenue to district.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Not Applicable

 

BACKGROUND: In 2005, an Option Agreement (Agreement) was granted to COMM 22, LLC (Comm22) to lease and develop excess property on the district’s former Maintenance and Operations Center site located on Commercial Street and 22nd Street in the Logan Heights neighborhood. The Agreement consisted of four options:

  1.  Affordable family housing (ground lease);
  2.  Affordable senior housing (ground lease);
  3.  For sale townhomes on Lot 7 (property conveyance); and,
  4.  Lofts/commercial development on Lot 1 (ground lease).

In 2012, Comm22 exercised two of the ground lease options, affordable senior and affordable family housing.  Comm22’s construction of these developments were completed in 2015.

 

In 2016, Comm22 exercised the conveyance option of Lot 7 (for sale townhomes). Comm22 has completed the required environmental clean-up of Lot 7 and is in escrow with Habitat for Humanity to build for sale townhomes starting in early 2017. The district will receive $150,000 from the sale of these townhomes.

 

The remaining option under the Agreement is the ground lease of Lot 1 (lofts/commercial development).Comm22 has requested an 18-month extension to allow for additional time to continue the design and finance of loft/commercial/retail space on Lot 1 and agreed to pay the district $25,000.00 as consideration for this extension.

 

Comm22’s proposed development plan for Lot 1 consists of 57 market-rate, urban apartments with a shared courtyard between the two buildings and a rooftop deck for residents with views of downtown and the Coronado Bridge. This development requires City of San Diego planning approval (estimated to take 9 months). Once the City grants approval, Comm22 will be in a position to exercise this final option for Lot 1.   Under the Agreement, once a ground lease is executed for Lot 1, lease payments will be approximately $40,000 annually with consumer price index (CPI) escalation.
