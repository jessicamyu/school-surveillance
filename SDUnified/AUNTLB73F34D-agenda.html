Subject
2. Resolution Calling on Gompers Preparatory Academy (GPA) Board and Staff Leadership to Respect Educators' Organizing Rights and Process
Meeting
Dec 4, 2018 - Regular Charter and Propositions S, Z and YY Updates Meeting, 5:00 P.M.
Type
Action (Consent)

RECOMMENDATION: Approve resolution calling on Gompers Preparatory Academy (GPA) board and staff leadership to respect educators' organizing rights and process.

 

BACKGROUND: The San Diego Education Association has formally petitioned the California Public Employee Relations Board to be certified as the association representing non-management certificated educators at GPA.  The petition attests to enjoying support from a majority of non-management certificated educators at GPA. The GPA board and staff leadership have been made reasonably aware of the petition made by the non-management certificated educators at GPA. The California Educational Employee Relations Act ("EERA") explicitly protects the right of public sector workers to organize and associate with organizations like the San Diego Education Association. GPA's own Charter and Charter Renewal Agreement expressly declare GPA to be the exclusive public school employer within the meaning of EERA. EERA prohibits public school employers from interfering in employees' decision to join organizations like the San Diego Education Association.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve revised Propositions S, Z and YY Bond Program Plan and adopt resolution approving revised Propositions S, Z and YY project plan and projects undertaken pursuant to Propositions S, Z and YY as shown on Attachment 1.

 

FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  $315 million for FY 2018-19.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING:  None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE:  Ongoing - The Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee last met on November 15, 2018.

 

BACKGROUND:  This update addresses program and project milestones and provides an overview of the current program.  On November 4, 2008, November 6, 2012 and November 6, 2018, voters approved Propositions S, Z and YY, general obligation bond measures to improve every school in the district. On May 14, 2013, the board approved the Proposition Z Two-Year Project Plan for implementing projects identified in the Bond Project List and included estimated project schedules.  On December 1, 2013, the board approved an update to the Proposition Z Two-Year Project Plan which also incorporated Proposition S projects and schedules.  On June 3, 2014, Propositions S and Z Project Plan expanded beyond its two-year time horizon and has come to be known as the multiple-year project plan.  The multiple-year project plan has subsequently been updated at each staff presentation to the board, most recently on November 7, 2017.  With the passage of Proposition YY, and in response to board priorities and direction, staff is presenting its latest update to the multiple-year project plan for board approval which includes reference to the voter guide for projects.
