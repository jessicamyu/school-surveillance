RECOMMENDATION: This is a First Reading of the proposed 2018-19 Title I Eligibility Ranking for the district. This report presents 2018-19 Title I Eligibility Ranking and school participation. There are two schools, Ericson Elementary and Patrick Henry High School, that no longer meet the forty percent eligibility threshold. BACKGROUND: The Elementary and Secondary Education Act (ESEA) of 1965 was reauthorized as of December 10, 2015, as the Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA). Title I, one of several programs under ESSA, provides funds to schools to help disadvantaged children meet challenging academic standards. The law intends that the funds go to the district and schools that have the highest concentrations of poverty. Title I requires funding to be allocated to a district based on the number of "children in poverty" residing within the district boundary. The federal census is used to identify children ages 5 through 17 living in poverty who are included in the calculation of Title I entitlement.

 

The proposed 2018-19 Title I Ranking report allocates Title I funding to all district schools with a 2017-18 meal eligibility percentage of 40 percent or higher and the two additional schools recommended to be grandfathered. There are also 14 schools which were grandfathered last year and are no longer eligible to be grandfathered. These schools include Barnard Elementary, Doyle Elementary, Green Elementary, Hage Elementary, Hickman Elementary, Loma Portal Elementary, McKinley Elementary, Salk Elementary, Correia Middle, Dana Middle, Pershing Middle, Standley Middle, Point Loma High and University City High Schools. The total 2017-18 Title I Entitlement is $38,541,900.00.

In its charter petition, KC High states that the school seeks excellence in academics, arts and athletics from the foundation of love. The school is structured based on a liberal arts college model with the first two years consisting mainly of core and required classes, and the last two years students have the ability to select courses that align with their future transition goals. The school provides opportunities for students to participate in internships and work-study programs.

 

The school utilizes a robust Alumni College Assistance Program to help fill the voids of financial, academic and personal support that students attending college need. The program’s three-pronged approach includes preparing students for the rigors of college, informing and engaging parents about the opportunities for college, and providing resources for graduates to sustain themselves as college students. The school has designated one of its sites as a “Freshman Academy” to support students with the transition from middle school to high school. Having the ninth graders on a single site provides a smaller setting and opportunities for intense social-emotional support.

As the charter granting authority, pursuant to Education Code section 47607(a)(3)(A), the district shall consider increases in pupil academic achievement for all groups of pupils served by the charter school as the most important factor in determining whether to grant a charter renewal.

 

Per Education Code section 47607(b), a charter school shall meet at least one of the following criteria before receiving a renewal:

  1. Attained its Academic Performance Index (API) growth target in the prior year or in two of the last three years both schoolwide and for all groups of pupils served by the charter school.

  2. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API in the prior year or in two of the last three years.

  3. Ranked in deciles 4 to 10, inclusive, on the API for a demographically comparable school in the prior year or in two of the last three years.

  4. The entity that granted the charter determines that the academic performance of the charter school is at least equal to the academic performance of the public schools that the charter school pupils would otherwise have been required to attend, as well as the academic performance of the schools in the school district in which the charter school is located, taking into account the composition of the pupil population that is served at the charter school.

  5. Qualified for an alternative accountability system pursuant to Education Code section 52052(g).

Assembly Bill (“AB”) 484 amended Education Code sections 52052(e)(2)(F) and 52052(e)(4) to allow schools that do not have an API calculated in 2013–14 through 2016-17 to use one of the following criteria to meet legislative and/or programmatic requirements:

  1. The most recent API calculation;

  2. An average of the three most recent annual API calculations; or

  3. Alternative measures that show increases in pupil academic achievement for all groups of pupils schoolwide and among significant groups.  The decision to use one of these criteria may be made on a program by program basis and is a local decision.

  7. Should elected Board members be paid full-time salaries and be allocated paid staff?
