RECOMMENDATION: Information only.  Health Sciences High School and Middle College Charter School will present on successful innovative practices and implementation in district schools

 

BACKGROUND: On November 19, 2013, the board highlighted that a primary reason for the existence of charter schools is for them to serve as incubators of innovation in a more flexible environment, which can then be shared with traditional district schools.  In keeping with the district’s ongoing collaboration with charter schools, valuing of community-based educational programs, and paying attention to home-grown, successful innovations, the board directed the superintendent to identify and develop presentations on successful innovative practices at charter schools and their implementation in district schools. 

 

Health Sciences High School and Middle College is a charter school authorized by the district.  The organization serves Grades 6-12 at 3910 University Avenue, in the Hoover cluster. 

 

Dr. Douglas Fisher is the charter school’s Dean of Faculty Affairs as well as a Professor of Educational Leadership at San Diego State University.  Dr. Fisher will provide an overview of the charter school’s accomplishments and describe the strategic approach with leadership meetings, cross school teacher-leader workshops, and job alike groups. 

 

Dr. Ian Pumpian is the charter school’s President/Chief Educational Officer as well as a Professor of Educational Leadership at San Diego State University.  Dr. Ian Pumpian will detail the 2013-14 collaboration, including the Charter School Dissemination Grant, professional development and learning communities, instructional approaches, core curriculum techniques including close reading, and restorative practices.

BACKGROUND:  Parents and staff have been meeting for over the past several years to improve the schools in the Madison cluster and to find ways to work together.  The cluster council meets monthly with representatives from each school, as well as from community organizations, to work on the implementation of Vision 2020 in their schools. The Madison Cluster in Clairemont has had about one-half of their students attending school outside of the cluster and this group is working to reverse that trend.  In working to promote quality schools in their neighborhood and higher enrollment of neighborhood students (Quality School Indicator 9), the cluster has indicated an articulation gap at the middle school level. 

 

Madison’s elementary schools are Grades K-6 and the neighborhood middle school, Innovation Middle School (STEM focus) is Grades 7-8.  This results in some of the students leaving the cluster at sixth grade to attend a comprehensive middle school outside the cluster.  A few students attend CPMA (6-8), a districtwide magnet, just a couple of blocks away. All of this currently disrupts the K-12 continuity (Vision 2020 Vision 2c: “Clusters will ensure that there is continuity in the neighborhood students in the K-12 program.”)

 

The cluster has come to a consensus on a STEAM focus (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts and Math).  This fulfills the Quality Neighborhood School Indicator: Access to a broad and challenging curriculum.  Students would be able to take advantage of the STEM focus at Innovation Middle or the arts focus at CPMA.  The elementary schools would align themselves with preparing their students for the middle school experience and the comprehensive Madison High School would provide further opportunities for STEM and arts programs.

 

MOTION:

  • To move sixth grade from elementary schools to the middle school level.
  • To give Madison Cluster students priority enrollment at CPMA Magnet Middle School.  It is not anticipated that this would have a significant effect on the opportunity for students outside the cluster to attend CPMA, as the school has additional capacity at its facility to incorporate more neighborhood students.
  • To more fully align the calendar across the cluster and to increase neighborhood participation, Sequoia Elementary would switch from year-round to a traditional calendar.  Sequoia is the only year-round school in the cluster.  Changes to the school calendar would occur in the 2015-16 school year.  While the cluster council, including parents and staff, has reached consensus on this issue, this action directs district staff to engage with affected bargaining units on this change prior to implementation.
  • To direct staff to plan facilities according to the above changes, particularly at Innovation Middle School to accommodate the addition of sixth grade to its campus and to make sure that the elementary and high school facilities are compatible with the STEAM academic focus, as well as the health and safety of the students.
  • To make all of the above changes for the 2015-16 school year to facilitate proper planning and an orderly transition.
  • To request that the Superintendent and staff provide the support needed for the sixth grade classes in the Madison cluster to differentiate Common Core math instruction in the 2014-15 year when those students will still be in elementary school.

It does not include food services, transportation, support services positions budgeted in the central office departments.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the second amended and restated Bylaws of the Proposition S and Proposition Z Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee (ICOC).

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None. 

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: With the passage of Proposition Z on November 6, 2012, San Diego Unified is required by state law to establish an Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee (ICOC) to monitor expenditures of voter approved funds.  At the time, the district had already established an ICOC to monitor expenditures of Proposition S, a bond measure approved by voters on November 4, 2008. 

 

To fulfill the district’s desire to concurrently implement projects under Proposition S and Proposition Z, on February 12, 2013, the board adopted Amended and Restated Bylaws (“Bylaws”) which establish a single Independent Citizens' Oversight Committee to monitor bond expenditures for both Proposition S and Proposition Z.  In addition to consolidating oversight, the Bylaws also modified the member nomination process to provide greater representation of San Diego Unified citizens to ensure that the perspectives of parents, community members, business and labor organizations were represented.

 

Under the terms of the bylaws, organizations were identified to provide nominations to the ICOC.  If an organization failed to nominate a representative, the district may request nominees from similar organizations as determined by the board.  The San Diego Regional Economic Development Corporation, was identified to nominate a representative “who is active in a business organization representing the business community located within the district.”  On March 7, 2013, the San Diego Regional Economic Development Corporation informed staff that it does not intend to nominate a representative.  In consultation with the ICOC, staff has identified the San Diego Regional Chamber of Commerce as a similar organization and recommends that the Bylaws be amended to replace the San Diego Regional Economic Development Corporation with the San Diego Regional Chamber of Commerce.
