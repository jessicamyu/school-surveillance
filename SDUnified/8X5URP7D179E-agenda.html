Ratification is an after-the-fact request for the board to approve services already provided or being provided at the time the Independent Contractors Agreement (ICA) is presented for approval.

The ICA will include a requisition number that pre-encumbers the funding for payment of the ICA after services are completed. After board approval, a purchase order will be created and sent to the contractor.

  • Marine Science Floating Laboratory:  $660.00 per half-day trip, serving a maximum of 36 students and 4 adults per trip
  • Green Machine:  $400.00 per trip, serving four one-hour elementary classes (Grades K-4) of up to 30 students each
  • Splash Science Mobile Lab:  $600.00 per trip, serving four one-hour elementary classes (Grades 4-6) of up to 36 students each

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  Individual schools that participated in these programs during 2012-13 paid for the trips from site funds, PTSA funds, grants, donations, etc.

BACKGROUND: These Agreements authorize schools to participate in the Marine Science Floating Laboratory, Green Machine and Splash Science Mobile Lab programs available to elementary and middle-level schools during the 2013-14 school year.  The description of each program is as follows:

  • The Marine Science Floating Laboratory program consists of a half-day field trip on San Diego Bay with hands-on science instructional and materials.  It provides students with the opportunity to study marine biology, oceanography, history, and economics in a "real world" environment.
  • The Green Machine curriculum aligns with the California Science Content Standards for Grades K-4.  The presentation describes the journey from seed to dinner table through hands-on learning stations that enable students to learn where their food comes from.  A musical and dramatic wrap-up reinforces the learning.
  • The Splash Science Mobile Lab curriculum aligns with the California Science Content Standards for Grades 4-6.  The presentation offers hands-on learning opportunities for students at multiple learning stations (i.e., watershed/storm drain, water conservation, San Diego Estuary, and microscope).

These are all field trips or mobile activities that come to the school site. These opportunities are provided to schools through the respective agreement with San Diego County Superintendent of Schools.  Many schools participate and each site arranges their own field trips since it is a site cost.

 BACKGROUND: The passage of Proposition Z on November 6, 2012, included voter approval for support of teaching and learning in the classrooms.  This has provided the district with an opportunity to leverage Proposition Z funds to qualify for and accept additional funding available under the federal governments E-rate program for the federal funding years 14 and 15. This also allows completion of six schools associated with federal funding year 12.

 

On May 7, 1997, the federal government implemented the Telecommunications Act of 1996.  This program (referred to as E-rate) is designed to ensure that eligible schools have access to modern telecommunication services, which includes replacing infrastructure (conduits, cabling, cabinets, etc.) and Local Area Network (LAN) electronic components (routers, core switches, edge switches) and wireless systems etc.

 

This funding opportunity will allow the district to receive an estimated $52.3 million in federal funds to use together with an estimated $19.2 million in Proposition Z funds to install technology needed to support the 21st century learning environment as outlined in the districts technology plan. While the districts current i21 interactive classroom initiative allows for the installation of interactive whiteboards and distribution of computers to teachers and students, the E-rate funding allows for the replacement/installation of technology infrastructure and components required to support i12 classrooms connectivity. The technology improvements covered by this funding would benefit 89 district schools that have qualified based on their Free and Reduced Lunch program participation levels.

 

District staff previously filed the necessary applications to request approximately $52.3 million dollars in funding from the E-rate program. To date we have received approvals for $16.9 million dollars and expect the remainder to be approved soon. To be eligible to receive these funds, the district must pay a portion of the cost of these projects. 

 

This request to pay the districts portion of an estimated $19.2 million from Proposition Z funds over the next 12 to 36 months will support the initiative to ensure eligible schools have access to classroom technology.

 

The schools will remain in operation while construction work is being performed. Construction phasing and other strategies will be employed to minimize disruption so that teaching and learning are not compromised.

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT:  None.

BACKGROUND:  The Board of Education has annually adopted a resolution for issuance of TRANS for the upcoming fiscal year, most recently at the June 22, 2012 meeting for the 2012-13 fiscal year.

 

The district receives a substantial portion of its annual income from property taxes, which are traditionally paid by taxpayers on or before December 10 or April 10.  As a result, the district must borrow funds each year in advance of this date to pay operational expenses in the July to December timeframe.  Thus, funds are borrowed at the beginning of the fiscal year using TRANS notes and repaid within an eight- to twelve – month period.  Short-term borrowing using TRANS is necessary to ensure the district meets its financial obligations on a cash basis.

 

On March 27, 2012, the board approved a pool of firms to provide financial underwriting services to support the sale of Proposition S and Proposition Z bonds and TRANS issuances.  The district has annually utilized this approved pool of firms from which to select the Underwriters to be the district’s sellers for its annual Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes.  Therefore, the district sought proposals from the firms in this approved pool of providers.  The proposals were reviewed by the district’s financial advisor, Fieldman, Rolapp, and Associates, as well as a selection committee comprised of members of the district’s Finance Division.  Upon completion of the review of the proposals submitted, then Citi Global Markets, Inc., Loop Capital Markets, LLC, and Stifel, Nicolaus, and Co., were elected by this committee to provide underwriting services for the 2013-14 Tax and Revenue Anticipation Notes and be written into the Resolution.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the updated Property Inventory and Property Management Plan (PMP) for Selected School Sites (Exhibits A, B, and C) dated May 2013.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None. Currently the lease of excess district properties provides approximately $7.1 million of unrestricted funds to the district. Additional income may be obtained through leases or the sale/exchange of property.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: On February 28, 2013, April 25, 2013, and May 23, 2013, the Advisory Committee on the Utilization of Excess School Property (ACUESP) reviewed the PMP and Summary of Comments (SOC). Due to the pending sales, the district's Barnard Elementary site and Mission Beach Center site will be removed from the SOC next year. ACUESP advises the board to approve the new PMP.

 

BACKGROUND: In accordance with Administrative Procedure 3105 and the board directive on February 24, 2004, the Property Inventory and Property Management Plan (PMP) for Selected School Sites is reviewed annually by district staff and the ACUESP to determine if any revisions are necessary.

 

The PMP was last amended and approved by the board on May 29, 2012. The proposed PMP for Selected School Sites is provided as Exhibit A. Exhibit B is a map showing the Board of Education districts and the excess school properties within each district. Exhibit C is a summary of ACUESP's thoughts and comments gained from committee discussions and previous site visits.

 

An overview of the updates made to the May 29, 2012 PMP are included below, in order by exhibit:

  • Exhibit A: Changes from Exhibit C are incorporated into Exhibit A.
     
  • Exhibit B: A new property category (Ground Lease) is added to the map’s legend.  MacMullen, Porterfield, and Commercial Street are in the Ground Lease category.   MacMullen and Porterfield had previously been noted as unimproved sites.  Commercial Street was formerly noted as a support facility.  Serra Elementary was changed from a remnant parcel to an unimproved site.  The Fairbrook property, which closed escrow last year, was removed from the map.
     
  • Exhibit C:  The approximate dates that the properties were declared excess were added to the Comments & Recommendations column.  The amounts of Unused Site Fees (for those properties for which we pay the fees) were included in the Site column.  Camp Elliot #2 and Camp Elliot #3 had “sell” removed from the Potential Use column and to their current designation.  Mission Beach Center had “sell” added to its current designation.

RECOMMENDATION: Direct staff to conduct an in-depth review of  moving the district to a modified common calendar.  Expenses and savings would both be reviewed in this analysis. 

 

The recommendation is for the board to direct staff to conduct an in-depth review of moving the district to a modified common calendar, often known as a traditional calendar for the 2014-15 school year or later depending on the availability of funding to do so. In this review, staff would assess the impact on students, schools, staff, and stakeholders.
