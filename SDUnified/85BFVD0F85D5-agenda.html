Subject
E.04. Final Offers for the Allocation of Facilities for Charter Schools Under Proposition 39 (Education Code Section 47614) for the 2008-09 School Year
Meeting
Mar 25, 2008 - 03/25/2008
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes
Dollar Amount
$150,000.00
Budget Source
State School Facilities Fund
At its February 26, 2008 meeting, the Board approved District staff recommendations for preliminary offers of facilities to eligible charter schools that made appropriate applications for facilities under the provisions of California Education Code section 47614 and California Code of Regulations Title 5, sections 11969 et seq.

District staff have met with the eligible charter schools to discuss and refine the preliminary offers, and the final offers of facilities space are contained in this report.

Proposition 39 (Education Code section 47614) requires the District to offer reasonably equivalent facilities to charter schools that show that they are eligible to receive such facilities. Staff has analyzed each of the requests in conformity with the requirements of Education Code section 47614 and the implementing regulations and has developed recommendations for responding to each of the requests, as well as for the final offers of facilities for eligible charter schools. Staff is submitting this report and its recommendations for Board approval and requests the Board adopt the report as its statement of reasons.
    Under the provisions of Education Code section 47614 and the implementing regulations found at California Code of Regulations, Title sections 11969 et seq. (“Regulations”), it is required that:

    • Districts provide eligible schools with facilities as outlined in the law to the degree the charter school makes an application that complies with the statutory and regulatory requirements;

    • A charter school meeting the requirements of the statute and Regulations and wishing to occupy district facilities in the 2008-09 school year submit a complete application for facilities by October 1, 2007;

    • Districts analyze each charter school’s request in conformity with the law and raise concerns regarding the charter school’s stated in-district classroom ADA projections to the charter school prior to developing its preliminary offer in response to the application;

    • By April 1, districts make final offers to eligible charter schools and that districts provide in advance of the final offer, a preliminary offer for the eligible charter schools.

    • By May 1, charter schools are obligated to respond to the District’s final offers if they are to receive facilities for the 2008-09 school year.

    Recommendations:

    The Superintendent recommends that:

    1. The Board approve the final offers of facilities for charter schools under Proposition 39 for the 2008-09 school year as described in this report, in order for the District to fulfill its obligation to make final offers to charter schools by April 1, 2008.

    2. The Board approve this report as the Board’s additional statement of reasons in support of the final offers.[Originator - Director, Facilities Planning]

    Request for allowance of attendance at San Diego Unified School District from October 22 through October 29, 2007, because of emergency conditions—San Diego County Fires.

        In accordance with the provisions of Education Code Section 46392, the Superintendent recommends approval of submission of a “Request for Allowance of Attendance Because of Emergency Conditions” (Form J-13A and Affidavit of Governing Board Members) to the California Department of Education (CDE) for a substitution of estimated days of attendance from October 31, 2007 through November, 16, 2007, during which school attendance was materially decreased as a consequence of the wildfires.
        On November 13, 2007, the Board approved submission of a waiver that allowed the District to receive credit for lost average daily attendance (ADA) and instructional time for the week of October 22 to October 29, 2007, when the schools were closed.
        The CDE permits districts to claim ADA credit for material decrease when attendance is below normal for days on which schools were kept open during, or immediately following an emergency. The October fires constitute that emergency. The District has determined that ADA was measurably lower after the schools re-opened for the period October 31, 2007 through November 16, 2007, than it was in the timeframe immediately prior to the school closures. As a result, a waiver request is warranted.
        Form J-13A: (pps.1-5)         Request for Allowance of Attendance Because of Emergency Conditions is attached, along with the actual apportionable attendance for days of material decrease calculator.
        The requested 1,431,338.44 days of attendance will result in an additional 10,446.69 days of apportionment which is approximately $543,000 in revenue to the District.
