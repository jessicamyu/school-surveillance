Dollar Amount

The Office of School Innovation and Choice recommends the Board of Education approve the renewal charter petition for Darnall Charter School for a five-year charter term beginning on July 1, 2009, and expiring on June 30, 2014. The Office of School Innovation and Choice recommends the adoption of this staff report as findings in support of the board’s decision to renew the charter petition. The Board of Education originally granted Darnall a five-year charter term on July 27, 1993, and has been operating as a charter school in the San Diego Unified School District (SDUSD) for 16 years. The renewal of the charter petition shall become effective on July 1, 2009. The potential fiscal impact to SDUSD is an estimated loss of $4,051,000 generated in average daily attendance in 2009/10 because SDUSD does not receive any average daily attendance funds for students enrolled at charter schools. District staff has analyzed the school’s academic performance, financial viability, charter petition, and results from a staff review and found that the school has met requirements to continue performing as a charter school in this district.

D. DISTRICT OPERATIONS MONITORING
Subject
D.01. Board Discussion and Action Regarding Multi-Year Leases with District Charter Schools (Board Governance Policy: OE-13)
Meeting
Apr 21, 2009 - - 5:00 P.M. Special Meeting
Type
Action
Board discussion and action regarding multi-year leases with district charter schools.

At the January 27, 2009, meeting the board directed charter school and legal staff to explore the possibility of: 1) offering multi-year leases to charter schools that are currently allocated facilities under Proposition 39; and 2) giving control to charter schools over the Civic Center Act use of their facilities, rather than keeping that process at the district level.

Based on feedback from board members at the January 27 meeting, and subsequent analysis conducted by staff confirming that multi-year leases are a legally permissible option, it is the staff's recommendation that:

  1. At the April 21 meeting, the board discuss the issue, and take action giving further direction to staff to offer multi-year leases to charter schools, with lease terms serving the interests of the district in areas such as lease payment amounts, term of agreement, termination provisions, responsibility for maintenance and capital repairs, fee for use/lease of facility, tenant rights, and condition of property; and
  2. Existing Civic Center Act procedures continue to be applied to board-approved charter schools, because charter schools are already on the same “playing field” as non-charter schools and the risks associated with delegating Civic Center Act responsibilities to another entity outweigh any benefit to the district.
