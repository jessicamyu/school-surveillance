This report presents five scenarios for Title I eligibility ranking and school participation and an overall plan for the 2009-10 Title I program for the San Diego Unified School District (SDUSD).

The educational and administrative leadership of the district are challenged with designing a plan that meets strict legislative requirements while producing sustainable support that will positively impact student learning and significantly raise achievement over time during an era of diminishing resources. Information regarding the 2009-10 entitlement is not available at this time and will not be known until later in the year. However, total Title I funding for 2009-10 is anticipated to be the same level of funding budgeted for 2008-09, approximately $44.3 million. In addition, staff is projecting $4 million in carry over Title I funds, for a total of $48.3 million in available Title I funding in 2009-10.

Key factors that affect the projected reduction in school allocations are:
· Projected addition of 39 schools to Program Improvement status
· Leveling off of enrollment
· Free and Reduced lunch students increased over prior year by 2,237 students
· Impact of becoming a Program Improvement district

District staff know that significant and important changes in funding are occurring with the Title I resource. Over the past months, district leaders have worked to develop funding scenarios and shared possible funding scenarios with community stakeholders and district staff. Key district leaders have also consulted with members of the District Advisory Committee (DAC) on three occasions.

Education Code section 47614 and the California Code of Regulations, Title 5 sections 11969 et seq. (“Regulations”), require:
  • Districts offer facilities, to the extent required by law, to a charter school that submits a legally complete application;
  • A charter school wishing to occupy district facilities in the 2009-10 school year must submit a complete application for facilities by November 1, 2008;
  • Districts analyze each charter school’s request in conformity with the law and raise concerns regarding the charter school’s stated in district classroom ADA projections to the charter school prior to developing its preliminary proposal in response to the application;
  • On or before February 1, districts must submit preliminary proposals to eligible charter schools, and on or before March 1, charter schools must respond with any concerns regarding the preliminary proposals;
  • On or before April 1, districts must submit final offers of facilities to eligible charter schools, and on or before May 1, charter schools must respond to the final offers if they are to receive district facilities for the upcoming year.

The preliminary proposals are only the first step in the process of developing final offers of facilities to charter schools. Board approval of the preliminary proposals is required in order to continue this process and to meet the statutory requirements to present these proposals to charter schools by February 1, 2009.

Recommendations:

The superintendent recommends that:

1. The board approve the preliminary proposals of facilities (Attachment 1) to eligible charter schools that have made appropriate applications for facilities, without waiver of the deficiencies in each of their requests. The preliminary proposals are subject to the terms and conditions set forth in this report.

2. The board approve the pro rata charge for the use of the square footage allocated to the eligible charter schools under Education Code section 47614 and the associated Regulations for 2009-10 to be $1.00 per square foot annually, as provided in the Regulations. This is the same rate charged for the 2008-09 school year.

3. The board approve offering charter schools the opportunity to occupy additional space at the annual rate of $2.75 per square foot on campuses where such space is available and subject to the terms as required by the district. This is the same rate charged for the 2008-09 school year.

4. The board adopt this report as the supporting statement of reasons for its decisions.

Agreements with Harris Turner (P89-019-JP), Arcadis-US, Inc. dba PinnacleOne, Inc. (P89-018-JP), and URS Corporation Americas (P-89-017-JP) effective January 28, 2009 through January 27, 2012.
On September 10, 2008, the district publicly advertised a Request for Qualifications and Proposal (RFQ/P) for Proposition “S” Staff Support Services. Eleven firms responded on the due date of October 8, 2008; one firm was deemed non-responsive because of a late submittal. The firms’ qualifications and proposals were initially reviewed and ranked by a qualified panel of seven district employees in accordance with established criteria stated in the RFQ/P. Based on the results of this ranking, eight firms were selected for interviews. These firms were AECOM, Barnhart-Heery, Gafcon, Harris Turner, Parsons-Brinckerhoff, PinnacleOne, URS Corporation, and Vanir.

A separate qualified panel of five district employees and one member of the Independent Citizens Oversight Committee participated in the interview process and ranked the firms on the basis of their response to seven questions posed by the panel members together with a 20-minute presentation by each firm of their qualifications and experience.

At the completion of the paper screening and interview process, the district totaled both scores and gave equal weight to each; e.g., the paper screening results were 50 percent and the interviews were 50 percent of the total score. Based on the final ratings of both panels, the following firms are recommended to be awarded contracts: (1) URS Corporation; (2) Pinnacle One; and (3) Harris Turner.

Passage of Proposition S requires an aggressive schedule for the planning and construction of 150 million per year, in work, over approximately a ten-year period. The scheduled scope of work includes 165 modernization projects, two school demolition/reconstruction projects, two new schools, and 180 technology projects.

To successfully meet the requirements, qualified professionals in a number of fields are required. The selected consultants will provide subject matter expert scientists and engineers in the fields of environmental, technology, mechanical systems, specification writing, database software, and Geographic Information Services (GIS) to support these ongoing tasks. Services will include program analysis, fiscal management, environmental planning (including CEQA), design and construction management and other related services for planned and ongoing projects.

The proposed contract follows the same model as the original contract used to support the implementation of Proposition MM, in which the district contracted with a single large professional services consultant to provide support on an “as-required” basis as an extension of district staff. However, a significant difference between Proposition MM and Proposition S is Facilities Planning and Construction’s (FPC’s) recommendation to contract with three firms versus a single provider. This recommendation will create competition among the pool of providers, reduce dependency on one firm and will improve timeliness and quality of services provided.

Each consultant agreement will be for an initial term of three-years with an option to renew. Primary funding for these contracts is available via the Proposition S Fund. While the contract values may appear significant, they actually represent a very small percentage of the overall program and are well below industry standards for a large program. With the anticipated competition among the three support contractors, the overall cost of the three contracts should be less than the one existing contract. Competition will encourage efficiency.

The contracts will request these consultants to conduct employment practices and offer opportunities to local, small, emerging, disadvantaged business contractors, consultants, and suppliers in a manner that maximizes participation and treats each on the basis of merit and experience without regard to race, creed, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation or preference, national origin, citizenship, age, height, weight, marital status, medical condition, physical or mental handicap, disability, or veteran status, within the limits imposed by law.

California Government Code §§4525, et seq. and §53060, grant the district the authority to contract with and employ persons for the furnishing of special services if such persons have the necessary training, experience, and competency to perform the special services requested.

It is requested that these contracts be authorized by the Board of Education in the following amounts for an initial three-year period:
  • Harris Turner $6M
  • PinnacleOne, Inc. $6M
  • URS Corporation $9M

Attached hereto and made a part hereof by reference is a memorandum dated January 13, 2009, providing additional details.
