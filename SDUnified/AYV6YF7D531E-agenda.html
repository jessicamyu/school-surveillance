RECOMMENDATION: The Superintendent will provide an update to the board and the public on LCAP Goal 1: Closing the Achievement Gap with High Expectations for All, with emphasis on Hoover High School.

 

Vision 2020 Quality Indicator Destination: Every school is committed to maintaining high expectations for all students and to eliminating the persistent disparities in achievement and performance among student groups based on race, ethnicity, language or disability.  These disparities are understood as unacceptable and a focused plan of support is in place to eliminate the significant achievement gaps in order to realize academic proficiency for all students.  All relevant policies and practices reflect the goals of eliminating achievement disparities and attaining academic proficiency, explicitly and emphatically. A districtwide focus and effort supports the advancement of high levels of student achievement by ensuring that every school is culturally competent, ensures uniformly high expectations, promotes rigorous curricula, differentiates instruction, and maximizes access for all students to high-level educational opportunities. The district actively seeks to hire a diverse cadre of educators and administrators, as well as ensure proper emphasis on culturally responsive practices and regularly examines and monitors student achievement, policies, programs, tools and written documents to ensure that achievement gap goals are implemented. All staff members understand how their respective positions contribute, directly or indirectly, to the implementation of the achievement gap goals. Staff members understand and actively seek to learn the necessary knowledge and skills to serve diverse students and families. Staff members assume responsibility for implementing plans that will address achievement disparities. District and community stakeholders engage students and families, the private sector, faith-based groups, and higher education institutions to support district students in achieving academic proficiency.

 

LCAP Goal 1: All levels of the organization work to improve student achievement and close the achievement gap for all underperforming student groups.

 

Related LCAP Actions and Services:

  • 1.3.a.3. Integrated Youth Services, Area Superintendents, and site counselors will develop a rubric to standardize districtwide expectations in identifying at-risk students. The Early Warning System (EWS) that was piloted by the Children and Youth in Transition (CYT) Department in 2016-17 for middle and high schools will continue. It is designed to identify the students at risk within the areas of attendance, D and F grades, Grade Point Average (GPA) by grading period, citizenship, chronic tardiness, chronic absence, suspensions, referrals, and high school credits earned. Students identified as at-risk are designated to receive Tier 2 and Tier 3 services and supports as needed. The Online Student Profile System (OSPS) that assesses students at risk of not meeting district graduation requirements will continue.
  • 1.5.b.1. All high school students will have the opportunity to participate in UC 'a-g' approved online courses including Advanced Placement (AP), first time, and credit recovery courses.
  • 1.10.a.3. The district will continue to increase enrollment of males of color in advanced courses (e.g., Advanced Placement [AP], International Baccalaureate [IB], college classes, and College, Career and Technical Education [CCTE] capstone courses [the last course in a planned sequence necessary for employment in an identified occupation]).
  • 1.10.b.2.The district will improve graduation rates for African American and Latino students.

 

BACKGROUND: At regular, public board meetings Superintendent Marten provides reports that include the alignment of Vision 2020, the 12 quality Indicators, and supporting LCAP actions, services and baseline data. Following the Superintendent's Vision 2020/LCAP report, cluster and advisory groups, employee groups and members of the public have the opportunity to engage in dialogue on the report and provide input and feedback on LCAP implementation. The complete LCAP document is available on the district website at  https://www.sandiegounified.org/what-lcap.

RECOMMENDATION: Approve the phase-out of the La Jolla High - University City High optional attendance area and the alignment of middle school boundaries in Henry Cluster, beginning in 2019-20.

 

FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: None.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND:

 

Optional Area Phase-Out

At the April 21, 2015 board workshop (Logistics and Implementation of Quality Neighborhood Schools in Every Neighborhood), one of the "Concepts to Action" presented involved analyzing the district's optional attendance areas through the lens of providing clear articulation patterns within clusters to strengthen neighborhood schools. In most cases, the district's neighborhoods are assigned to one elementary school, one middle school, and one high school. However, there are a number of small areas which have more than one school assignment at one, two, or three school levels; these areas are called "Optional Areas." Some of these optional areas were created decades ago, and the reasons for their creation are sometimes unclear.


The map in the attached report shows the location of optional areas in the district. The phase out of optional areas is an ongoing process: in 2012-13, there were 25 optional areas (counting each level - elementary, middle and high - as a separate option), and as of this year, there are ten. This report proposes phasing out one of the remaining optional areas, La Jolla High - University City High, beginning in 2019-20. Remaining optional areas will be analyzed for potential phase-out in future years, depending on enrollment trends in those areas and capacity considerations, among other factors.

 

The optional area being proposed for phase out has one option being chosen overwhelmingly (La Jolla High).  Phasing out the less popular option (University City High) will therefore result in a very small impact on school enrollments.  It is also proposed that any existing students in the optional area may continue to attend the option school they have chosen, as can any upcoming siblings who would attend school with the older child concurrently.

 

Staff attended the scheduled cluster meeting of the school affected by the phase-out, University City High, on October 15, 2018, and the proposal was supported by those in attendance.

 

Middle School Boundaries Alignment

 

Best practices for attendance boundaries dictate that boundaries between levels - elementary, middle, and high - should align or "nest" in order to ensure clear articulation for students as they move up through the grades. This report proposes to correct two small areas where middle school boundaries do not align with elementary boundaries.

 

Henry cluster is served by two middle schools. Foster, Hearst, Hardy, and Marvin Elementary schools articulate to Lewis Middle. Benchley/Weinberger, Dailard, Gage, and Green Elementary schools articulate to Pershing Middle. However, two small geographic portions of Dailard and Green’s attendance boundaries currently are within Lewis Middle, instead of Pershing. This results in 5th grade classes exiting Dailard and Green being split for 6th grade. 

 

In order to streamline the articulation path for students, it is proposed to align middle school attendance boundaries to coincide fully with Dailard and Green’s elementary school boundaries. This change will be effective in the 2019-20 school year and is projected to apply to fewer than 20 students annually. No current students will be required to change schools. Grandfathering will be offered as is the standard district practice with attendance boundary changes; specifically, current 5th grade students in the areas of change may choose either Lewis or Pershing for 6th grade, as can any upcoming siblings who would attend school with the older child concurrently. All other students will follow the new feeder pattern.

 

Staff attended the scheduled Henry cluster meeting on October 11, 2018, and the proposal was supported by those in attendance. In addition, a letter was mailed to current families residing in the proposed area of change.

RECOMMENDATION:  Ratify the Authorization to utilize the North County Educational Purchasing Consortium (NCEPC) Cooperative Agreement with Kelly Paper (GD19-0794-19) and Liberty Paper (GD19-0795-19) for the purchase of various sizes and colors of xerographic copy paper. The term of this authorization is January 1, 2019, through December 31, 2019.

 

BUDGET STRING: Total not-to-exceed amount: $123,000.00. Budget Strings: Various site identified funds.

 

PRIOR YEAR FISCAL IMPACT: For the period of February 14, 2018 through December 19, 2018:  $378,026.00.

 

IMPACT TO DISTRICT STAFFING: None.

 

CONSULTATION WITH BOARD ADVISORY COMMITTEE: Not applicable.

 

BACKGROUND: The district purchases various sizes and colors of xerographic copy paper throughout the year for school sites and departments. The district is an Associate Member of the North County Educational Purchasing Consortium (NCEPC), therefore is eligible to use NCEPC competitively awarded contracts. Under the Public Contract Code (PCC) Section 20118, the governing board has determined that it is in the best interest of the district to utilize this "piggyback" contract to purchase xerographic copy paper. PCC 20118 provides that the district may purchase personal property from contracts already competitively bid by another public agency. The district may only order products specified in the contract at the prices specified in the contract. The district will utilize line 6C of the bid for the purchase of carload paper at $26.82 per case. 

 

On July 8, 2014, the board received the final report from the Environmental Sustainability Advisory Committee. Included in this report as a "Dream Big" idea was a recommended action to join or create larger purchasing cooperatives. A purchasing cooperative establishes various contracts which aggregate the total purchases from many government entities resulting in lower costs to governmental entities who then utilize these contracts.

BACKGROUND: These transactions are submitted to the board for review pursuant to Education Code sections 17604 and 17605 and under its delegation of authority to the district Strategic Sourcing and Contracts Officer. To allow for efficiency in the administration of a large urban school district, California Education Code section 17605 specifically allows a school district to delegate limited purchasing power, i.e., for purchases no greater than $90,200 (for the year 2018) for supplies, materials, apparatus, equipment, non-construction services not greater than $90,200, and construction services less than $15,000. For this reason, these types of transactions must be reviewed by the board within 60 days of the date of the transaction under Education Code section 17605. Additionally, the attached agreements were signed pursuant to the delegation of authority and, as required by Education Code section 17604, must be approved or ratified by the Board of Education.

 

The district has met the requirements of Education Code section 17605 with this process of reporting out the specified purchase orders under $90,200 (for the year 2018) that are not associated with any previously-approved board contracts.
