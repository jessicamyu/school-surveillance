Thursday, August 15, 2013
Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on August 15, 2013, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

August 15, 2013 Monthly Work Session Topics:

 

Quality-Plus Leader Academy, Aspiring Leader Program Graduation - The 4th Cohort of the Aspiring Leader Program, implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, presented 53 graduates to the Board for recognition of their completion of the 6-month long course of study.

 

Summary of CRCT and EOCT Results - Staff updated the Board on achievement results from Criterion Referenced Competency Test (CRCT), and the End of Course Testing (EOCT). CRCT is administered to students in grades 3 through 8. EOCT applies to nine (9) high school level courses. 

 

Summer Staff Development - Staff updated the Board on summer staff development. Teacher and/or administrator training continues to remain critical preparation for each school year. As new programs, initiatives, and updated methods are developed, summer break provides opportunities for extended training.

 

Final Discipline Report for School Year 2013 - Staff reported to the Board data related to the number of student disciplinary hearings and suspensions that occurred in 2012-13.  The report highlighted system-wide initiatives implemented to improve student discipline in GCPS schools.

 

Title IX Gender Equity Report - Staff provided to the Board figures regarding programmatic expenditures and the number of male and female students in GCPS who participated in Georgia High School Association sponsored activities for the 2012-2013 school year.

 

Summer School Report - Staff presented the Board with a report of the 2013 Summer School session. The report included number of participants, pass rates following summer school, as well as online course participation.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with the current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools

The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry-level assistant principal and administrative intern positions. The fourth cohort of 53 graduates is presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of the 6-month long course of study concluding with a summer school residency. To date, 35 members of this cohort have been appointed as assistant principals or administrative interns. A total of 122 participants have been appointed to leadership positions from the first four cohorts of the Aspiring Leader Program.
