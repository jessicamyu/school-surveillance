2.01 Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

 

Board members receive supporting documentation for items on the Monthly Business Meeting agenda seven days prior to the meeting.  Staff is available during this time to answer questions or provide additional information, if needed.

Recognition:

Best in Region, Innovative App Challenge

 

Awarded by:   Verizon

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Executive Director of Academies and Career & Technical Education

 

Background:

The Verizon Innovative App Challenge is a collaborative competition for middle and high school students, which aims to ignite interest in STEM through an empowering learning experience. Students are required to develop an original app concept that addresses a need or problem in their community and aligns with one of Verizon’s three areas of focus: Education, Healthcare, or Sustainability. Students from Meadowcreek put their skills to the test to develop “Teen Fitness,” an interactive mobile application designed with teenagers in mind. The app offers both an individualized and interpersonal experience, in hopes of motivating teens to establish healthier lifestyle habits. In recognition of their efforts, these students were awarded “Best in the Region,” and advanced to the national level. As a result of participating in this competition, the team members have expanded their knowledge in STEM and mobile app development.

11.02 Intergovernmental Agreement for the Upgrade of Public Safety Notification and Response System for Emergencies at Public Schools

 

Prepared by:  Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer

 

Presented by:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent     

 

Background:  The Gwinnett County Board of Commissioners, in conjunction with Gwinnett County Public Schools and Buford City Schools, agreed to use public safety proceeds from the county's 2014 Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax to fund an upgrade of the public safety emergency notification and response system for schools.

 

The project will provide: an automatic alert system at each school, to include notification buttons, direct links to the 911 center, and live-feed video capabilities; automatic locking systems on certain doors at specified schools; and automatic notifications and radio amplification as appropriate.

 

The estimated total cost of the project is $3,914,578.00, with all costs being reimbursed by the county.

 

Financial Impact:  Project to be funded with Gwinnett County’s 2014 Special Purpose Local Option Sales Tax.

 

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to approve the Intergovernmental Agreement between Gwinnett County Public Schools and Gwinnett County.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
