Thursday, November 15, 2012
Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on November 15, 2012, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

November 15, 2012, Monthly Work Session Topics:

 

Human Resources Oversight Workshop - In conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing the management and operation of the school system, the Board held a workshop on Human Resources management. Staff provided an overview of key issues and topics relevant to recruitment, employment, and development of the Gwinnett County Public Schools' workforce.

 

STEP Academy Program Update - Staff provided an update on the STEM Targeted Education Program (STEP) at Moore MS, Sweetwater MS, and Berkmar HS.

 

K-8 Language Arts Supplemental Materials Review - Staff provided the Board with an overview of instructional materials in view of adoption.

 

Update on Implementing the AKS Aligned to the Common Core - Mathematics - Staff shared an update on the status of Georgia Common Core Performance Standards in the area of mathematics.

 

 

Staff Reports:

 

Staffing Update -  A monthly report on personnel matters was presented to the Board.

 

2013 Legislative Program - Staff presented a draft of the 2013 Legislative Program for the Board's consideration.

 

First Quarter Discipline Report - Staff provided the First Quarter Discipline Report for the 2012-13 school year.

 

Budget Update for FY2013 - Staff updated the Board on the current status of the FY2013 budget.

Nov 15, 2012 - Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Category
1. Discussion Topics and Presentations

Dr. Gale Hey, Associate Superintendent for Teaching and Learning, and Mary Elizabeth Davis, Assistant Superintendent for Curriculum and Instruction, will provide the Board with an overview of instructional materials in view of adoption. In compliance with Gwinnett County Public Schools Policy IFAA and the Georgia Department of Education Rule 160-4-1.12, the instructional materials review was conducted for non-fiction language arts texts for grades K-8 and language arts teacher resources for grades K-5 to align to Common Core, AP Latin, and AP Spanish.

 

Supplemental materials were approved by the Community Health Advisory Committee (CHEAC) as required.
