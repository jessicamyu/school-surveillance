  • Equipment
  • Industry/Community Involvement
  • Work-Based Learning Opportunities
  • Career Technical Student Organizations (CTSOs)
  • Instructor Qualifications
  • Program Safety

The Georgia State Legislature and the Georgia Department of Education are committed to the industry certification process as a part of its efforts to strengthen technical and academic standards for all CTAE programs.  Industry certification is designed to promote high quality instruction for students wishing to pursue post-secondary education and qualify for entry-level positions in industry.

5.03 Gwinnett Retirement System - Review of Claim
Meeting
May 17, 2012 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
5. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

Prepared by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by:  Rick Cost

 

Background:  Within 60 days after notice of denial of a claim for Long Term Disability (LTD) benefits under the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan, a participant may request a review of the denial by filing written notice with the Plan Administrative Committee.  The Committee received such a request on April 11, 2012. 

 

The claim involves a second denial of the participant’s appeal for continuation of LTD benefits by the GRS Director, based on the definition of “Disability” contained in the GRS Plan Document and medical documentation from the participant’s treating physicians.

 

The procedures outlined in Article 8 of the GRS Plan Document call for the Committee to promptly notify the Board of Education of a request for a review.  The Board then decides whether it or the Committee shall handle the review, and notifies the Committee and the claimant.  Should the Board decide to allow the Committee to decide the claim, all such decisions are final, conclusive, and binding upon all parties.  The claimant is advised of the disposition of the claim within 45 days after receipt of the request for review.

 

Financial impact:  None.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to allow the Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee to handle review of the appeal received by the Committee on April 11, 2012 regarding denial of LTD benefits claim.

Category
11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
