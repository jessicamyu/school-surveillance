6.11 Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee - Annual Plan Contribution Rates and Fund Transfer
Meeting
Mar 18, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

Prepared by: Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

Presented by: Rick Cost

Background: Contributions to the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) are calculated using accepted actuarial methods by the Plan’s Actuary. These calculations may be relied upon by the employer to establish contributions rates to the Plan going forward. The GRS Administrative Committee receives the results of the Actuary’s calculations and transmits them to the Board along with recommendations as to contributions. At its February 2010 meeting the Committee received the Actuary’s report regarding contributions for the remainder of FY 2010 and all of FY 2011. The report indicated that employer contributions of 4.88% of compensation for the fiscal years ending June 30, 2010 and June 30, 2011 are required to support the benefits of the System. The Actuary also recommended that in accordance with Section 9.03 of the GRS Plan Document, $70,500,000 be transferred from the Disability Fund to the Retirement Fund to internally rebalance assets within the Plan. In accordance with the GRS Plan Document, the Committee recommends that employee contributions of 1% of compensation remain unchanged.

Financial impact: No change to the GRS Plan benefits.

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to set the employer rate of contributions to GRS at 4.88% of compensation for the fiscal years ending June 30, 2010 and June 30, 2011, and transfer $70,500,000 from the GRS Disability Fund to the GRS Retirement Fund.

Subject
6.12 Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Document Revisions
Meeting
Mar 18, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

Prepared by: Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

Presented by: Rick Cost

Background: The provisions and operational procedures of the Gwinnett County Board of Education’s Retirement System (GRS) are described in the GRS Plan Document. Since 1983, the Board has amended and restated the Plan Document from time to time to reflect changes in legislation and also to institute desired changes to benefits. Currently the Plan calls for the annual calculation of a Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) for GRS retirement and disability benefits. Under current methodology this calculation could cause benefits to be decreased if deflation (as measured by the U.S. Department of Labor’s Consumer Price Index (CPI)) occurs in the U.S. economy. Based on the Plan actuary’s confirmation that this provision could be eliminated from the GRS Plan Document without impacting contribution rates, and the opinion of the Plan’s legal advisor, the GRS Plan Administrative Committee voted to approve a recommendation to the Board to adopt a revised and restated Plan Document. The recommended change is contained in Section 4.05 of the GRS Plan Document submitted for the Board’s approval.

Financial impact: None.

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to approve a revised and restated GRS Plan Document with an effective date of July 1, 2010.

Prepared by: Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

Background: The Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) is directed by a nine-person committee. The Chief Financial Officer and the Chief Human Resources Officer serve as permanent members of this committee. The other seven members serve seven-year rotating terms. The committee consists of three teachers, one local school administrator, one classified employee, one retiree, and one position of any classification covered by the GRS plan. The two vacancies to be filled are designated to be one local school administrator and one teacher. The GRS Plan Administrative Committee serves as a screening committee for nominations and provides the Superintendent at least two nominations for each vacancy. At its February 2010 meeting, the Committee approved these nominations for the Superintendent’s consideration. Category
11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
