    Subject
    7.07 Elementary and Middle School Gateway Review
    Meeting
    Jun 21, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
    Category
    7. SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORTS
    Type
    Information

    Prepared by: Dr. Cindy Loe, Associate Superintendent for Teaching and Learning

    Presented by: Dr. Cindy Loe

    Background: Of the 19,601 Gwinnett fourth and seventh graders required to pass the 2007 Gateway test administered in April, the vast majority earned passing scores on the first try and were promoted to the next grade level. For those students who did not do as well on the Gateway as would be expected based on their classroom grades, the school system’s Assessment Review Procedure was immediately initiated and acted upon by the Gwinnett County Board of Education.

    According to the Assessment Review Procedure, any student whose report cards in 2006-07 included no grade below a “C” in Gateway subjects qualified for an automatic review if he or she failed any part of the Gateway test. Each review is conducted by an impartial committee of teachers, principals, and central office administrators. Two such committees operate simultaneously in order to complete the reviews before the June 21, 2007 Monthly Business Meeting.

    In addition to reviewing each student’s preliminary Gateway scores, the committee examines each student’s classroom performance as documented by the child’s teacher(s). Members also study each student’s standardized test data, other assessment information, and evidence provided by the parents of extenuating circumstances (illness, death in the family, etc.) that could have caused the student to perform poorly on the test. The committee then recommends one of the following three actions for each student:
    - waive passage of this year’s Gateway and promote to the next grade
    - waive passage of this year’s Gateway, promote to the next grade, but recommend summer school
    - do not waive passage of this year’s Gateway, recommend summer school, and require Gateway retake and passage for promotion.

    The committee’s recommendations and the Board’s final decisions will be reviewed in this report.

    Financial impact: N/A

    Recommendation: N/A
