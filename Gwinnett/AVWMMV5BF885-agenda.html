4.03  VEX Robotics - State Champions

 

Honorees:        Five Forks Middle School BroncoBots

 

Recognition:     State Champions

 

Awarded by:     Robotics Education and Competition Foundation

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Executive Director Academies and Career and Technical Education

 

Background:  The VEX Robotics Competition, presented by the Robotics Education and Competition Foundation, is the ultimate STEM activity for middle school and high school students. Each year, an engineering challenge is presented in the form of a game. Students, with guidance from their teachers and mentors, use the VEX Robotics Design System to build innovative robots designed to score the most points possible in qualification matches, elimination matches, and skills challenges. Through their participation in the VEX Robotics Competition and their work within their team, students will learn many academic and life skills. Middle School VEX robotics teams from across the state were invited to compete in the VEX Robotics 2018 Middle School State Championship at West Forsyth High School in Cumming on February 24, 2018. Five Forks Middle School BroncoBots Team A won the Build Award, the Maze Award, and were named the Overall State Champions. They will advance to compete at the World Championship in Louisville, KY in April. Five Forks Middle School BroncoBots Team B won the Georgia Sportsmanship Award. This award is given to a team that takes the time to help their fellow competitors by lending tools, motors, and support throughout the day, regardless of potentially helping a team beat them during the competition.

 

Accompanying Honorees:

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
