4.08 Gwinnett County Public Schools - The Clean Air Campaign PACE Award Winner
Meeting
Sep 15, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITIONS
Type
Information

 

Honoree:  Gwinnett County Public Schools

 

Recognition:  PACE Award - 2011 "Clean Air Schools" District of the Year

 

Awarded by:  The Clean Air Campaign

 

Introduced by:  Berney Kirkland, Chief of Staff

 

Background:  On August 24th the Clean Air Campaign announced its PACE Award winners for 2011, celebrating the success of employers, property managers, schools, and individuals in advancing the campaign's goals of cleaner air and a reduction in traffic through commute option programs. Gwinnett County Public Schools was named the 2011 "Clean Air Schools" District of the Year in Recognition of GCPS' efforts to create a healthier environment and to educate students about conservation, clean air, and environmental stewardship. The school district partners with Gwinnett Clean & Beautiful in the Green & Healthy School Program, which uses information from the Clean Air Campaign's Clean Air Schools program to teach students about reducing smog and cleaning the air.  In addition, no-idling initiatives on school campuses; promoting alternative modes of transportation such as walking or riding the school bus; and environmental lesson plans integrated in the Academic Knowledge and Skills curriculum are part of the school district's comprehensive approach to promoting a healthier environment. Last year, Gwinnett schools reduced idling by an average of nearly 50 percent, decreasing significantly students' exposure to such pollutants as carbon monoxide and particulate matter, which contribute to smog formation. Almost half of the district's schools earned the distinction of "Clean Air School" in 2010-2011.

Subject
5.07 Ratification of Settlement Agreement
Meeting
Sep 15, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
5. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

 

Prepared by:  Victoria Sweeny, Board Attorney

 

Presented by:  Victoria Sweeny

 

Background:  Omega US Insurance (“Omega”) named the Gwinnett County Board of Education as a Defendant in an insurance coverage and declaratory judgment action filed in US District Court for the Northern District of Georgia.  Omega sought an order from the Court that it was not required to pay for losses resulting from the over spray of paint onto data cables during the construction of the Gwinnett School of Mathematics, Science, and Technology.  Omega insured a subcontractor on a painting project as well as a third-tier painting sub contractor who are allegedly responsible for the over spray of paint onto data cables, which resulted in the loss of the warranty for the cables.  Prior to this action the general contractor for the project, Carroll Daniel, made the Gwinnett County Board of Education whole by replacing the warranty on the data cables with a bond that would pay for any repair or replacement that might be required.  Since the Gwinnett County Board of Education was made whole by its general contractor it does not seek any proceeds from Omega or Omega’s insured.  Omega agreed to dismiss the Gwinnett County Board of Education, with prejudice, from the action in exchange for an agreement that the Board would not later challenge the determinations made by the trial court regarding Omega’s responsibilities as an insurer in the case. 

 

Financial impact:  None

 

Recommendation: That the Board ratify the agreement reached by the CEO/Superintendent and Omega US Insurance resulting in dismissal of the Board from the declaratory judgment in exchange for an agreement that the Board will not challenge the decision of the trial court regarding insurance coverage.
