4.03 Mill Creek High School, Parkview High School, and Mountain View High School - Georgia Industry Certifications

 

Honorees: Mill Creek High School, Mountain View High School, and Parkview High School 

 

Recognition: Georgia Industry Certification

 

Awarded by: Georgia Department of Education and International Technology and Engineering Educators Association, and Georgia Department of Education and Media Education Foundation of Georgia 

 

Introduced by Tim Hemans, Executive Director of Academies and Career and Technical Education

 

Background: An “Industry Certified” program represents the apex of program quality. Only those programs that have successfully undergone rigorous reviews by leaders from business and industry are recognized with this distinction. This program is a joint effort between the Georgia Department of Education and the following partners:

  • International Technology and Engineering Educators Association (Engineering and Technology)
  • Media Education Foundation of Georgia (Audio, Video, Technology, and Film)

Industry Certification provides many benefits for the student, the school, and the community. Most importantly, certification shows that a program is contributing to the development of a highly-skilled future workforce. Schools that pursue Industry Certification receive a special grant provided by the Georgia Legislature and are certified for a five-year period.

7.13 Radloff MS: Easement Agreement

 

Prepared By: Division of Facilities and Operations

 

Presented By: Walt Martin, Chief Operations Officer

 

Background: Radloff Middle School’s property was originally developed by the Trackman Corporation as part of the Hampton Green Business Park. The school property shares an entrance driveway, access road, and stormwater drainage system with three (3) other undeveloped parcels. CRP/TP Solis Gwinnett Owner, LLC of Atlanta, Georgia is developing a new housing development on the three (3) contiguous adjacent parcels and an agreement is needed to establish the perpetual obligations of the Gwinnett County Board of Education and the developer. The proposed agreement consists of easements for main driveway and traffic markings, drainage and stormwater runoff, and construction and maintenance activities. The agreement permanently governs the maintenance and access requirements of the parties to share in the common ownership and operation of the driveway, access road, and the shared stormwater runoff system.

 

Financial Impact: None   

 

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to approve this agreement.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned OR Executive Session is Resumed

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and/or student discipline matters.
