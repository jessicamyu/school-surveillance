Subject
6.04 Gwinnett Retirement System - Review of Claim
Meeting
Sep 14, 2006 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

Prepared by: Bobby Crowson, Chief of Staff

Presented by: Bobby Crowson, Chief of Staff

Background: Within 60 days after notice of denial of a claim for benefits under the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan, a participant may request a review of the denial by filing written notice with the Plan Administrative Committee. The Committee received such a request on August 16, 2006. The claim involves a request for the Plan to pay out a lump sum benefit that exceeds the maximum amount allowable under the provisions of the Plan. The procedures outlined in Article 13 of the GRS Plan Document calls for the Committee to promptly notify the Board of Education of a request for a review. The Board then decides whether it or the Committee shall handle the review and notifies the Committee and the claimant. Should the Board decide to allow the Committee to decide the claim, all such decisions are final, conclusive and binding upon all parties. The claimant is advised of the disposition of the claim within 60 days after receipt of the request for review or within 120 days, if a hearing is held.

Financial impact: None

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to allow the GRS Plan Administrative Committee to handle review of the request received by the Committee on August 16, 2006 regarding payment of a lump sum benefit.

11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
