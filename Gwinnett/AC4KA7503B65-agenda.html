Monthly Work Session 2:30 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on August 18, 2016, will begin at 2:30 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

August 18, 2016 Monthly Work Session Topics

 

Aspiring Principal Program Cohort 10 Graduation Recognition - The Aspiring Principal Program (APP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares Assistant Principals to move into principal positions. The 10th Cohort of 15 graduates was presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of a year-long course of study. 

 

Summer Professional Learning Opportunities Review - Staff reviewed the summer professional learning opportunities including the Teaching and Learning Conference and New Teacher Orientation.

 

Georgia Milestones Assessment System End of Grade (EOG) and End of Course (EOC) Results Summary - Staff provided a summary to the Board of the Georgia Milestones Assessment System End of Grade (EOG) and End of Course (EOC) results.

 

Title IX Gender Equity Report - Staff shared the Title IX Gender Equity Report for the 2015-2016 school year.

 

Final Discipline Report for 2015-2016 - Staff reviewed with the Board the Final Discipline Report for the 2015-2016 school year.

 

Back to School Update - Staff shared a Back to School update with the Board.

 

Strategic Waiver School System (SWSS) Update - Staff updated the Board information on the Strategic Waiver School System contract, which included the district's process for adding schools to the waiver.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

The Aspiring Principal Program (APP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares Assistant Principals to move into principal positions. The 10th Cohort of 15 graduates is presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of a year-long course of study. To date, eight members of this cohort have been appointed as principal. A total of 166 participants have been appointed to the principal/district leadership positions from the first ten cohorts of the Aspiring Principal Program.
