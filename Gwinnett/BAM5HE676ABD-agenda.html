The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry level assistant principal, administrative intern, and community school director positions. The tenth cohort of 52 graduates is presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of a six month long course of study that incorporated an in-house residency in the form of individualized leadership experiences. To date, 28 (54%) members of this cohort have been appointed as assistant principals or administrative interns. A total of 411 (89%) graduates have been appointed to leadership positions from the ten cohorts of the Aspiring Leader Program.

Apr 18, 2019 - Monthly Work Session 2:00 p.m.
Category
1. Discussion Topics and Presentations
Type
Information

Dr. Glenn Pethel, Assistant Superintendent for Leadership Development, will share information from the Rand Corporation report on Principal Pipelines. This study examined the efforts of six urban school districts to put in place systematic processes for the strategic management of school leaders. The Wallace Foundation funded and provided technical assistance to the six districts, including Gwinnett County Public Schools, as a part of its Principal Pipeline Initiative (PPI) from 2011 through 2018. The report summarizes the key findings. The main analysis found positive effects of the Principal Pipeline Initiative on student achievement, principal retention, and affordability.
