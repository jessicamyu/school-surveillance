Awarded by:   WordMasters Challenge™

 

Introduced by:   Kimberly Lipe, Language Arts Director

 

Background:  The WordMasters Challenge™ is an exercise in critical thinking that first encourages students to become familiar with a set of interesting new words (considerably harder than grade level), and then challenges them to use those words to complete analogies expressing various kinds of logical relationships.  Working to solve the analogies helps students learn to think both analytically and metaphorically.

 

The sixth grade team representing Sweetwater Middle School achieved Highest Honors in the 2012-13 WordMasters Challenge™—a national vocabulary competition involving nearly 150,000 students annually. The sixth grade team scored a perfect 200 out of a possible 200 points in the last of three meets this year, placing first in the nation.  In addition, the sixth grade team finished the overall competition in first place with a cumulative score of 600 points, the only team in the country to achieve a perfect score. 

 

Accompanying Honoree(s):  Georgann Eaton, Principal of Sweetwater Middle School, and Lois Bean, WordMaster Team Coach, will accompany the honorees.

7.07 Summerour Middle School Relocation

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations     

 

Presented by:  Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer     

 

Background:   On Tuesday, July 30, 2013 at 3:00 pm, proposals were received from thirteen General Contracting firms for Construction Management at Risk Services for the upcoming Summerour Middle School Relocation project. These proposals were solicited in a Request for Qualifications (RFQ) process where each firm submitted its qualifications demonstrating the ability to successfully complete a project like this on time and within budget.  Each proposal included the project team, previous project experience on similar projects, and the fee on the total construction cost of the project. 

 

The selection committee of professionals independently reviewed and scored each firm according to an outlined scoring system in the RFQ.  The six firms scoring the highest were invited to present their credentials, project teams, and approach to the committee.  After these presentations the committee scored all firms and is ready to recommend the most qualified Construction Manager at Risk to the Board for approval.

 

Financial Impact:   The recommended Construction Manager at Risk will work with the school district's design and construction team to complete this project for a fee on a Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP).  The total cost will be within the current budget of the 2012-2017 Building Program.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to award the contract to the most qualified firm as recommended.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
