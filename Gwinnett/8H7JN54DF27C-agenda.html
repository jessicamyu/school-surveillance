Subject
7.05 Alliance to Reform Education Leadership
Meeting
Jul 21, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORTS
Type
Information

 

Prepared by:  Berney Kirkland, Chief of Staff

 

Presented by:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  On June 20, 2011, Gwinnett County Public Schools signed an agreement with The Bush Institute to participate as one of a handful of "innovation sites" for the Institute's Alliance to Reform Education Leadership (AREL).  Former First Lady Laura Bush was on hand for the signing and a round table discussion with district representatives.  The mission of AREL is "to help change the way in which school principals throughout the U.S. are recruited, selected, prepared, certified, empowered, compensated, supported, and evaluated." AREL seeks to achieve its mission by forming a network of training sites with shared goals and values. Gwinnett County Public Schools was invited to join AREL based on the success of the district's Quality-Plus Leader Academy, established in 2007 to prepare the principals and leaders the school district needs now and in the future.

 

Recommendation:  Information item

 

Financial impact:  None

Jul 21, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
11. ADJOURNMENT
Type
Information

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
