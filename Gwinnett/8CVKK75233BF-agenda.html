Honorees:  30 GCPS Schools That Earned the SSAS Award

                  (List attached)

 

Recognition:  2010 Single Statewide Accountability System (SSAS) Award

 

Awarded by:  Georgia Department of Education and Governor’s Office of Student Achievement

 

Introduced by:  Dr. Steve Flynt, Associate Superintendent for School Leadership and Operational Support

 

Background:  The Governor’s Office of Student Achievement (GOSA) and the Georgia Department of Education (DOE) recently announced that 30 Gwinnett County schools earned the 2010 Single Statewide Accountability System Award during the 2009-2010 school year. This award recognizes schools for their continuous improvement in raising student achievement and closing achievement gaps on state curriculum tests and Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) status.

 

The schools were recognized in two categories:

1) Highest Performance, indicating the percentage of students meeting and exceeding academic standards; and

2) Greatest Gains, indicating the greatest gains in the percentage of students meeting and exceeding academic standards.

 

In each category, the state recognizes four school award levels: Platinum, Gold, Silver, and Bronze.

Subject
6.03 Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) - Actuary's Recommendations for Retirement and Disability Funds
Meeting
Feb 10, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

 

Prepared by:  Tom Daymude, Director, Gwinnett Retirement System

 

Presented by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background:  In accordance with Article 9.02 of the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan Document, each year the Plan’s actuary calculates the amount of contributions which are sufficient to fund the benefits provided by the Retirement and Disability Funds of the Plan using accepted actuarial methods. The Board may rely upon the estimates made by the actuary of the amount of contributions needed to carry out the Plan. The actuary delivers the calculated rates to the GRS Plan Administrative Committee which in turn transmits the results of the calculations to the Board together with recommendations as to contributions.

 

In accordance with Article 9.03 of the GRS Plan Document, the Board may, on the recommendation of the actuary, cause assets to be transferred from one Fund to the other, except as prohibited by the Internal Revenue Code or other applicable law.

 

The Committee received the actuary’s recommendations to fund the Plan for the following fiscal year at its November 2010 meeting and unanimously voted to forward the recommendations shown below to the Board for approval.  These recommendations represent contributions to be made to the Retirement and Disability Funds of the Plan for the period July 1, 2011 through June 30, 2012.   

 

Financial impact: The recommended employer contribution rate represents an increase of .21% over the current year rate.  No change to GRS Plan benefits. 

 

Recommendation:  That the Board approve the actuary’s recommendations for funding the Retirement and Disability Funds of GRS for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2012 as follows:

 

·         Employer Contributions: 5.09% of compensation

·         Employee Contributions: 1.00% of compensation

·         Transfer $31,700,811 from the Disability Fund to the Retirement Fund
