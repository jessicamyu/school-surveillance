4.02 Parkview HS Marine Corps JROTC - Naval Honor School Award
Meeting
Oct 21, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7:00 p.m.
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITIONS
Type
Information

Honoree:  Parkview HS Marine Corps JROTC

 

Recognition:  Naval Honor School Award

 

Awarded by:  Commanding General, Marine Corps Training and Education Command, Quantico, VA

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Director, Technical Education, Computer Science, and Work-Based Learning

 

 Background:  JROTC units are selected by the Area Commander for attaining the highest overall rating during the school year.  Subsequently, these units are designated Naval Honor Schools by the Commanding General Training and Education Command, MCCDC Quantico, VA.

 

This designation is given to 20% of the JROTC units in the nation in recognition of a unit’s exemplary performance during the Area Commander’s inspection, and the unit shows exemplary performance in drill competitions, community service, and marksmanship competitions. The JROTC unit also must demonstrate exemplary performance in unit administration, training, supply, facilities, armory management, cadet citizenship, school and community involvement, scholastic achievement, and extracurricular events according to Training and Education Command guidelines. 

 

Parkview HS' unit was designated as national champions in Orienteering and state champions in Marine Corps Drill Competitions.  The unit also received two appointments to the U.S. Naval Academy, received five ROTC scholarships, and the Rifle Team qualified for state and national competitions... all while averaging 40 hours per cadet in community service hours.

 

 

Accompanying the honorees: David Smith, principal, Parkview HS, Captain Modesto Silverio, USMC retired,

Gunnery Sergeant and Marine Instructor Dave Erwin, USMC retired, Parkview Marine JROTC Officers

7.07 Fitnessgram Health Reporting Tool
Meeting
Oct 21, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7:00 p.m.
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORTS
Type
Information

Prepared by:  Dale Robbins, Associate Superintendent for Teaching and Learning Support

 

Presented by: Dale Robbins

 

Background:  GCPS has used FITNESSGRAM and collected data regarding student fitness levels for a number of years. Last year, the Georgia legislature passed HB 229 which is referred to as the SHAPE (Student Health and Physical Education) bill.  It calls for this type of activity to be done statewide. The assessment includes a variety of health-related physical fitness tests that are used to determine a students' overall physical fitness and the results will be provided to parents. Gwinnett County Public Schools is one of five districts in the state serving as a pilot for the program.

 

Financial Impact: The SHAPE program is supported by the Governor's Office, the Health Department, Children's Healthcare of Atlanta and the Arthur Blank Foundation.
