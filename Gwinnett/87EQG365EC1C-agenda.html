7.07 2010-11 School Calendar
Meeting
Feb 18, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORTS
Type
Information

The 2010-11 school calendar includes three planned furlough days for all staff as the district has opted not to "furlough" teachers and other staff an additional three days this spring as recently proposed by the Governor. Instead, Gwinnett County Public Schools will reduce work days for next year as we did in the fall of 2009. This will lessen the impact on our employees for this school year, allow us to protect the integrity of the instructional program 2nd semester, and will provide employees time to plan accordingly. The planned furlough days for school personnel, noted with purple boxes, will be August 2, 3, and October 11. The planned furlough days for 12-month staff, noted with circled dates, will be November 24, and December 20 and 21.

Taking the furlough days into account, teachers will return to work on Wednesday, August 4, and we will hold the first day of school on Monday, August 9.

Another change in next year's calendar is the change to a one-week break for students and teachers the week of Thanksgiving. Traditionally, Gwinnett's school calendar has included two school days during the week of Thanksgiving and a three-day break. Feedback we received on the one-week break option suggested that it could address the higher absenteeism we typically have during this time of year and would be less disruptive instructionally than a two-day school week.

11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
