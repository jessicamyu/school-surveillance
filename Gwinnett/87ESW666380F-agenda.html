Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting.

The monthly Work Session on November 13, 2008, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, GA.

November 13, 2008, Monthly Work Session Topics:

The following topics were on the agenda of the Board's afternoon Work Session, which began at 3:00 p.m.:

Human Resources Business Oversight Workshop - An overview of key topics relevant to the Human Resources Division.

Gwinnett School of Mathematics, Science, and Technology - Staff will provide a program update on GSMST.

Curriculum Update: Technical Education - Staff will provide an update for the Techical Education and JROTC Programs.

JROTC Program Update - Staff will provide an update of Curriculum and Instruction's JROTC program in GCPS.

2009 Legislative Program - Staff will review the Board's requests in the area of State Funding Improvements, as well as the Board's K-12 Continuing Positions.






Staffing Update - Monthly report on personnel matters (applications, new hires, vacancies).

First Quarter Discipline Report for 2008-2009 - Staff will provide a report on discipline for the first quarter 2008-2009.

Process/Timeline for Naming New Schools - Staff will provide an update on the process/timeline for naming new schools.

1.05 2009 Legislative Program
Meeting
Nov 13, 2008 - Monthly Work Session
Category
1. Work Session Discussion Topics and Presentations
Type
Information

Each year the Gwinnett County Board of Education adopts its program of legislative priorities to be shared with members of the Gwinnett Legislative Delegation in the upcoming session of the Georgia General Assembly. The 2009 Legislative Program outlines the Board’s requests in the area of State Funding Improvements, as well as the Board’s K-12 Continuing Positions on matters related to local control of public education, school safety and security, contracts for classified employees, and vouchers/tuition tax credits.
