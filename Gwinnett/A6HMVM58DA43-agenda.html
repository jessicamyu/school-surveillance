2.01 Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

 

Board members receive supporting documentation for items on the Monthly Business Meeting agenda seven days prior to the meeting.  Staff is available during this time to answer questions or provide additional information, if needed.

4.03 J. Alvin Wilbanks, Gwinnett County Public Schools - Friend of Music Award, Georgia Music Educators Association

 

Honoree:        J. Alvin Wilbanks 

 

Recognition:   Friend of Music Award

 

Awarded by:   Georgia Music Educators Association

 

Introduced by:  David DuBose, Director of Fine Arts

 

Background:  This award is given by the Georgia Music Educators Association to an individual who is not a current or retired member of the organization and has significantly contributed to the advancement of music education in Georgia. The concept behind the GMEA "Friend of Music Education" award is to recognize individuals who have provided a tremendous service to or greatly assisted the group's membership in a variety of ways. Alvin Wilbanks, superintendent of Gwinnett County Public Schools, was nominated for this award due to his unwavering patronage of music teachers, even through the toughest of financial times, as well as his commitment to offering students a wide array of music courses taught by highly qualified teachers. In an era when school systems cut both employees and elective offerings to balance budgets, Mr. Wilbanks was a tremendous force in keeping Gwinnett County's fine arts programs intact. Mr. Wilbanks does not simply say he supports music instruction for young people, he demonstrated his valuation of this instruction in the most visible, and sometimes difficult, manner possible.   

 

Attending with Honoree:  Dr. Bernadette Scruggs, GMEA past President's Representative, with Cecil Wilder, Executive Director of the Georgia Music Educators Association

7.10 New Norcross Cluster STEM HS: Construction Manager At Risk Request for Qualifications/Request for Proposal (RFQ/RFP) Evaluation and Selection Approval

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations      

 

Presented by: Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer      

 

Background:   On Monday, February 1, 2016 at 3:00 pm, proposals were received from ­­­­­­­­­­­­six Construction Management at Risk firms for Construction Management at Risk Services for the upcoming New Norcross Cluster STEM High School project. These proposals were solicited in a Request for Qualifications/Request for Proposal (RFQ/RFP) process where each firm submitted their qualifications demonstrating their ability to successfully complete a project like this on time and within budget.  Their proposals included their project team, previous project experience on similar projects, and their fee on the total construction cost of the project. 

 

Five submitting firms were invited to present their credentials, project team and approach to the selection committee.  After these presentations the committee scored all firms and is ready to recommend Bowen & Watson, Inc. of Toccoa, Georgia, as the most qualified Construction Manager at Risk to the Board for approval.

 

Financial Impact:   The recommended Construction Manager at Risk will work with the design and construction team to complete this project for a fee on a Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP).  The total cost will be within the current budget of the 2017 Building Program.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to award the contract to the most qualified firm as recommended.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
