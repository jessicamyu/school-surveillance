4.01 Gwinnett School of Mathematics, Science, and Technology Math Team - First Place, Georgia Mathematics Championship

 

Honorees: GSMST Math Team

Timothy Gieseking 

Julius Tao

Jason Fan

Albert Kim

 

Recognition:  First Place, Georgia Mathematics Championship

 

Awarded by:   Georgia Council of Teachers of Mathematics

 

Introduced by:   Bonnie Brush, Director of Mathematics 

 

Background:  The Georgia Council of Teachers of Mathematics (GCTM) sponsors the annual state math championship.  Schools are invited to send four of their best math team students to compete.  Schools earn an invitation to the state math championship based on their performance at other tournaments throughout the state.  Schools from all Georgia High School Association (GHSA) and Georgia Independent School Association (GISA) classifications compete together. At the state competition, competitors individually complete a 50-question multiple-choice test and a speed round followed by teams working together to complete a set of a dozen challenging problems.  The questions come from topics included in the standard high school curriculum up to and including calculus, as well as topics not traditionally found in the curriculum, such as number theory and combinatorics. 

 

A team of four students from the Gwinnett School of Math, Science, and Technology (GSMST) won first place in the 40th annual state mathematics tournament.  The team placed first in the state over all classifications. 

 

Team members were Timothy Gieseking, who was the individual state math tournament winner, Julius Tao, Jason Fan, and Albert Kim.  GSMST’s team is coached by teacher Robin Miles.

 

 

Accompanying Honorees:  Mr. IV Bray, Principal of GSMST and Ms. Robin Miles, Teacher at GSMST will accompany the honorees.

11.01 Strategic Waiver School System (SWSS) Letter of Intent and Contract

 

Prepared by:    Dr. Steve Flynt, Associate Superintendent of School Improvement & Operations

 

Presented by:  Mr. J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO / Superintendent

 

Background:  Gwinnett’s SWSS Partnership Contract (formerly known as IE² Partnership Contract), which was approved in October 2014, provides local schools and the school district with flexibility from fourteen laws and the accompanying State Board rules.  This flexibility is coupled with accountability targets for the five years of the contract. Since the approval of this contract, Gwinnett has added three new schools:  Discovery High School, Coleman Middle School, and Baldwin Elementary School.  Additionally, Gwinnett Online Campus and Gwinnett School of Mathematics, Science and Technology no longer operate as charter schools as of July 1, 2016.  These five schools will need to be added to our SWSS Partnership Contract through a request for an amendment which will be submitted to the GaDOE to be approved by the State Board of Education.

 

Financial impact:  n/a

 

Recommendation: That the Board submit a resolution to the GaDOE, accompanied by a letter of intent from CEO/Superintendent Wilbanks, supporting the desire to amend the existing contract.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
