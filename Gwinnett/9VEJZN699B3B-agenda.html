4.02 Nick Pelletier, Duluth HS - Selected Member of 2015 National Youth Orchestra

 

Honoree: Nick Pelletier, Duluth High School student violist

 

Recognition:  Member of the 2015  NYO-USA (National Youth Orchestra-USA)  

 

Awarded by:   National Youth Orchestra-USA  

 

Introduced by:   David DuBose, Director of Fine Arts

 

Background:  Nick will join 120 other young musicians age 16-19 in a concert tour of China as well as a Carnegie Hall performance which will be broadcast live worldwide.  All expenses will be paid by Carnegie Hall's Weill Music Institute.  One of the missions of the NYO-USA is to foster cultural discovery through performance and meetings with local young musicians from China.  The Orchestra will perform nine concerts on the two-week tour, July 10-26, preceded by a two-week residency at Purchase College, SUNY.  Members of the New York Philharmonic will coach the students. The tour will take them to seven cities: Beijing, Shanghai, Suzhou, Xi'an, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, and Hong Kong. The Orchestra is in its third year and is the first national youth orchestra of this kind since Leopold Stokowski established one in 1940 which was then suspended by the advent of WWII.  The first two seasons the orchestra toured Russia and the USA.

7.09 Gwinnett Retirement System - Claim Review  

 

Prepared by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by:  Rick Cost

 

Background:  Within 60 days after notice of a second denial of a claim for benefits under the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan, a participant may request a review of the denial by filing written notice with the Plan Administrative Committee.  The Committee received such a request on March 22, 2015.   The claim involves an application for GRS Long Term Disability benefits.  The procedures outlined in Article 8 of the GRS Plan Document call for the Committee to promptly notify the Board of Education of a request for a review.  The Board then decides whether it or the Committee shall handle the review, and notifies the Committee and the claimant.  Should the Board decide to allow the Committee to decide the claim, all such decisions are final, conclusive and binding upon all parties.  The claimant is advised of the disposition of the claim within 60 days after receipt of the request for review or within 120 days if a hearing is held.

 

Financial Impact:  None

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to allow the Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee to handle review of the request received by the Committee on March 22, 2015 regarding payment of Long Term Disability benefits.

11.01 Adoption of FY2016 Budget

 

Prepared by:  Joe Heffron, Executive Director of Business and Finance

 

Presented by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background:  CEO/Superintendent Wilbanks provided his FY2016 budget recommendations to the members of the Gwinnett County Board of Education the week of March 23, 2015.  The Board met in work sessions to discuss the budget on March 28 and April 14, 2015.  School Board members tentatively adopted the FY2016 budget and then held public hearings on May 14, 2015 and May 28, 2015.  As part of the budget process, the GCPS Board of Education is required by the state to adopt a tentative budget, to advertise it and to hold a public hearing.

 

Financial impact: 

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt the FY2016 budget in the expenditure amount of $1,858,289,327 as presented by the superintendent and tentatively adopted by the Board on April 14, 2015.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned 

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
