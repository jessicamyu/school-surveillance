 Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting.

 

The monthly Work Session on July 18, 2013, began at 3:00 p.m. with a tour of the new GIVE West facility, 5550 Peachtree Industrial Blvd., Norcross, followed by the regular Work Session at 4:30 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools' Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

 

 

July 18, 2013 Monthly Work Session Topics:

 

Board Business Oversight Workshop - Construction and Facilities Management - Staff from the Department of Construction and Facilities Management presented a Board Business Oversight Workshop in conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing management of the school system's business and operational systems.

 

 

Staff Reports:

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with the current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

Opening Schools for 2013-14 - Staff updated the Board on the opening of schools for 2013-14.

 

The 5-R Project - Staff provided the Board with a report on the 5-R Project, a proposed model for providing educational opportunities in an alternative school setting for dropouts and students who are at-risk of dropping out. A brief description was provided for each component with special attention directed toward Project Reconnect.

 

5th Grade Writing Assessment Results - Staff presented the Board with the results on the 5th Grade Writing Assessment.

2.03 The 5-R Project
Meeting
Jul 18, 2013 - Monthly Work Session - 3:00 p.m. Tour of New GIVE West Facility; 4:30 p.m. Work Session
Category
2. Staff Reports
Type
Information, Discussion

The 5-R Project is a proposed model for providing educational opportunities in an alternative school setting for dropouts and students who are at-risk of dropping out. The five components of the model are Rebound, Recovery, Redirect, Rescue, and Reconnect. Dr. James Taylor, Executive Director for Academic Support, will provide a brief description of each component with special attention directed at Project Reconnect, which involves encouraging identified dropouts to continue pursuit of their high school diploma.
