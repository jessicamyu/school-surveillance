Thursday, May 16, 2013
Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on May 16, 2013, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

May 16, 2013 Monthly Work Session Topics:    

 

 

Athletic Safety Report - Staff reported on athletic safety and well-being measures.

 

Gwinnett Teacher Effectiveness Evaluation System Update - Staff updated the Board on the Gwinnett Teacher Effectiveness Evaluation System.

 

CLASSe Pilot and Demonstration - Staff updated the Board on the status of the eCLASS Pilot and demonstrated some of the features of the initiative. 

 

Summer 2013 - Staff presented the 2013 summer schedule information.

 

Alternative School Update - Staff updated the Board on Alternative Schools.

  

 

Staff Reports:       

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with the current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

Legislative Update - Staff reviewed with the Board the status of education-related legislation in the current session of the Georgia General Assembly.

 

Quarterly Discipline Report - Staff updated the Board with the third quarter discipline report for the 2012-2013 school year.

 

Title I Update - Staff presented an update on Title I to the Board.

 

Intergovernmental Agreement - City of Sugar Hill / Quit Claim Deed - Staff briefed the Board on a proposed Intergovernmental Agreement with the City of Sugar Hill / Quit Claim Deed that is to be presented for action in tonight's Business Meeting.

 

Partnership Gwinnett Public Funding Entity, Inc. - CEO/Superintendent J. Alvin Wilbanks will review with the Board a proposed agreement between Gwinnett County Public Schools and Partnership Gwinnett Public Funding Entity, Inc., a Georgia non-profit corporation charged with the development of a long-term strategic community and economic development plan for Gwinnett County.
