Monthly Work Session
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on August 18, 2011, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

August 18, 2011, Monthly Work Session Topics:

 

Board Business Oversight Workshop - Construction and Facilities Management - In conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing management of the school system's business and operational systems, staff will present a workshop on construction and facilities management.

 

XFAT Team: Proposal on Classroom Safety - A report on the team's review of current safety standards regarding classroom instruction, chemical storage, and facilities and operations.   

 

XFAT Team: Proposal on Issuing Carnegie Units in Middle School - A report on the team's recommendations as to whether Carnegie units should be issued to students in middle school who are taking high school courses.

 

Title IX Gender Equity Report - The Title IX Gender Equity Report for 2010-11 school year will be presented. 

 

Summer School Report - An update on the academic success of students in this year's summer school at the elementary, middle, and high school levels will be presented.

 

 

 

Staff Reports:

 

Staffing Update - A monthly report on personnel matters (applications, new hires, vacancies).

 

Final Discipline Report - The final discipline report for the 2011 school year will be given.

 

Public Education Leadership Project (PELP) Report -  A report on this executive training program and the GCPS team's recent participation.

Information

 

In July a team of Gwinnett County Public Schools leaders participated in an executive training program conducted jointly by the Harvard Business School and the Harvard Graduate School of Education. The Public Education Leadership Project (PELP) was established at Harvard in 2003 to identify effective leadership and management practices that support large-scale organizational change in urban school districts, with the intent to improve achievement for all students. Staff will brief the Board on the five-day PELP experience and the GCPS team's work on the specific local challenge it identified to address during the daily team time on the PELP schedule.
