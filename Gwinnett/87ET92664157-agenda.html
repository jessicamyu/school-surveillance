Recognition: Outstanding Service Recognition for First Responder Training in CPR and First Aid

Awarded by: Gwinnett County Public Schools

Introduced by: Chuck Truett, Director of Health and Physical Education

Background: In 1994 Fire Chief Steve Rolader, in cooperation with Gwinnett County Public Schools (GCPS), designed a CPR and First Aid Course specifically for GCPS employees called First Responders. Over the last 14 years, Chief Rolader and his staff have conducted this course, which has become an extremely popular staff development opportunity for GCPS employees. Chief Rolader was so committed to this effort that, on several occasions, he used his vacation time to provide this extremely important service. In the summer of 2007, the Gwinnett County Health and Physical Education Department worked with Chief Rolader to modify the program to provide an opportunity for even more GCPS employees to be certified in CPR and First Aid. Each year, the Gwinnett County Fire Department typically trains about 75-100 GCPS employees as part of GCPS' staff development, but since July 2007, 340 GCPS employees have been trained. James Mason, a training technician for the Gwinnett County Fire Department, has been instrumental in providing the training since August 2007. This recognition provides a thank you for the continued support of Chief Rolader and the instruction provided by James Mason during the 2007-2008 school year.
