Subject
4.03 Schools Highlighted for Closing the Achievement Gap
Meeting
Jan 11, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION
Type
Information

Honorees: Centerville ES, Chattahoochee ES, Dacula ES, B.B. Harris ES, McKendree ES, Rebecca Minor ES, Shiloh ES, K.E. Taylor ES, Alton C. Crews MS, Peachtree Ridge HS, Peachtree ES, and J.E. Richards MS

Recognition: Schools Recognized for Reducing the Student Achievement Gap

Awarded by: Standard & Poor's School Evaluation Services

Introduced by: Dr. Cindy Loe, Associate Superintendent, Division of Teaching and Learning

Background: Twelve Gwinnett County schools were recognized nationally for significantly narrowing the student achievement gap between higher- and lower-performing student groups during the 2003-04 and 2004-05 school years. The recognition is based on the evaluation performed by Standard & Poor's School Evaluation Services. Gwinnett County Public Schools is one of only three Georgia school systems in which more than five schools have significantly narrowed at least one achievement gap between student groups.

Nine Gwinnett elementary schools, two middle schools, and one high school have reduced the gap in at least one of three categories: Black and White student achievement, Hispanic and White student achievement, and economically disadvantaged and non-disadvantaged student achievement.

According to SchoolMatters, a service of Standard and Poor's, "These schools are recognized here in the hopes that they may serve as benchmarks and sources of promising practices for educators elsewhere in the state who are trying to close achievement gaps while improving the achievement of all students."

11. ADJOURNMENT
Subject
11.01 Meeting Adjourned
Meeting
Jan 11, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
11. ADJOURNMENT
Type
Information

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
