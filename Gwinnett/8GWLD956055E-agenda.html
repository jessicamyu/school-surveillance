Raymond "Geoff" Richardson, Brookwood HS

Recognition: Perfect Papers on National Latin Exam

Mr. Richardson: Perfect Paper, Level 1 National Latin Exam

Mr. Cunningham: Perfect Paper, Level 3 National Latin Exam

Mr. O'Donnell:  Perfect Paper, Latin 4 Prose, National Latin Exam

 

Awarded by:  National Latin Exam (sponsored by the American Classical Association and National Junior Classical League)

 

Introduced by:  David Jahner, Director of Foreign Language

 

Background:  Each year more than 130,000 students around the world participate in the National Latin Exam.  Fewer than 1% of those students achieve a perfect score on the exam, which assesses student's understanding of the Latin language.  On the level 1 exam there were fewer than 250 perfect papers among the more than 50,000 exams administers, which illustrates Mr. Richardson's accomplishment.  Mr. Cunningham is one of only 14 students to achieve a perfect paper on the Level 3 exam.  Nearly 15,000 students took the test.  Mr. O'Donnell's achievement is similarly impressive with only 6 of 3,100 students around the globe obtaining a perfect paper on the Level 4 Prose exam.

 

 

 

Accompanying the honorees:  Debra Dees, Principal, Brookwood HS; David T. Smith, Principal, Parkview HS; and Dr. Jim Markham, Principal, Mill Creek HS.

Meeting
Jun 16, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORTS
Type
Action

 

 

Prepared by:  Delores Hendrix, Executive Director of School Operations and Support

 

Presented by:  Calvin Watts, Dr. Gale Hey, and Dr. John Green, Area Superintendents

 

Background:  Of the 46,082 Gwinnett fourth, fifth, seventh, and eighth graders required to pass the 2011 Gateway tests, the vast majority earned passing scores on the first try and were promoted to the next grade level.  For those students who did not do as well on the Gateway, as would be expected based on their classroom grades, the school system's Assessment Review Procedure was immediately initiated and acted upon by the Gwinnett County Board of Education.

    

According to the Assessment Review Procedure, any student whose report cards in 2010-11 included no grade average below a "C" in Gateway subjects qualified for an automatic review if he or she failed any part of the Gateway test.  Each review is conducted by an impartial committee of teachers, principals, and central office administrators.  Three such committees operate simultaneously in order to complete the reviews before the June 16, 2011, Monthly Business Meeting.

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open). If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
