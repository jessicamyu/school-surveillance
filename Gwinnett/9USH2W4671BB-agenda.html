Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on April 16, 2015, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

April 16, 2015 Monthly Work Session Topics

 

XFAT Final Report: Becoming a Culturally Competent Organization  - Staff presented the findings and recommendations of the Cross Functional Action Team's (XFAT) review of Cultural Competence of the district.

 

XFAT Final Report: Mobile Applications - Staff presented the findings and recommendations of the Cross Functional Action Team's (XFAT) review of mobile applications.

 

Standardized Testing Data: Milestones Update - Staff provided the Board with information regarding the administration of Georgia Milestones Assessment Systems (Georgia Milestones). The Georgia Milestones spans grade 3 through high school and measures how well students have learned the knowledge and skills outlined in the state-adopted content standards in language arts, mathematics, science and social studies.

 

Changes in Math Courses for 2015-2016 - Staff updated the Board on changes coming to the district's mathematics program in preparation for the 2015-2016 school year (instructional expectations, alignment with Georgia Standards for Excellence, and using formative assessments to guide instruction).

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

Legislative Update - Staff reviewed with the Board the status of education-related legislation in the current session of the Georgia General Assembly.

Staff will present the findings and recommendations of the Cross Functional Action Team's (XFAT) review of mobile applications. As the eCLASSS initiative moves forward, and as mobile apps become more abundant in society as a whole, more interest has been generated in providing apps to GCPS stakeholders: students, staff, community, and parents. The team was charged with developing recommendations for a district protocol and reviewing the benefits/concerns of mobile app technologies.
