7.06 Restatements of the Gwinnett County Board of Education's 403(b) Plan and 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan

 

Prepared by:  Joe Heffron, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by:  Joe Heffron

 

Background:  As a result of a competitive Request for Proposal (RFP) issued in 2015, GCPS entered into a single provider service agreement with VALIC Retirement Services Company (VALIC) to become the sole service provider for the Board’s 403(b) Plan and 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan effective January 1, 2016.   Under the service agreement, VALIC now provides Plan Document compliance services at no extra charge to GCPS, thus allowing the Plans to forego the added administrative expense of separate legal review and preparation of Plan Document changes.  The proposed restatements of the Plan Documents provided by VALIC are based on their review of our current Plan Documents and operations and are summarized here.

 

GCPS 403(b) Plan:  The proposed document was prepared using VALIC’s template for 403(b) Plans and contains no substantive changes to the current operation of the Board’s Plan.  It clarifies the ineligible status of Part-time, Seasonal and Temporary (PST) employees as ineligible to participate in the 403(b) Plan based on their automatic participation in the Board’s 401(a) FICA Alternative Plan as allowed under US Treasury Regulations.  Since July 1, 2011, any PST employee transferring from temporary to permanent employment status has automatically ceased participation in the 401(a) Plan and been automatically enrolled as an eligible employee in the 403(b) Plan.  The document also removes references to service providers who are no longer active in the Plan.

 

GCPS 457(b) Plan:  The proposed document was prepared using VALIC’s template for 457(b) Plans and contains no substantive changes to the current operation of the Board’s Plan.

 

Financial impact: None.  All Plans are 100% funded by employee voluntary contributions. 

 

Recommendation:  That the Board adopt resolutions to restate the Gwinnett County Board of Education’s 403(b) Plan and 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan, and to authorize the Director of Retirement Services to execute the attached restated Plan Documents.

11.03 GCPS Enterprise Data Governance - IBM PCR 1705

 

Prepared by:  Executive Data Governance Steering Committee composed of Executive Cabinet Members

 

Presented by:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  In February 2016, Gwinnett County Public Schools (GCPS) and IBM signed a formal agreement extending a strategic partnership to meet and advance strategic goals of the District. This partnership is driven by a need for GCPS to have a comprehensive partner, with a broad variety of technology and services projects, who is an industry leader in technology and integration, who is fully capable of providing consulting, solution architecture design, project management, integration, and implementation services along with any other resources necessary to address GCPS' instructional, administrative, and operational technology needs. This agreement provided a five-year extension of the GCPS/IBM Technology Integration Partnership and represents GCPS' desire to provide the consistent integrated implementation of critical enterprise-wide, multi-year initiatives. One such initiative focuses on education data governance. In September 2016, CEO/Superintendent J. Alvin Wilbanks launched an Enterprise Data Governance Program which will develop a framework for supporting GCPS' culture of accountability, collaboration, and standardization around data access and information security.

 

IBM will assist GCPS with this program by providing strategic technologies and supporting services. IBM will engage consultants with data governance, data management, and information cybersecurity expertise to support GCPS' Enterprise Data Governance efforts. In addition, IBM will provide required support, technology, implementation, and consulting services that will include providing recommendations for underlying infrastructure and software component technology improvements. IBM will specifically identify gaps between existing support and software/infrastructure components, and those that may be necessary to deliver on the goals and objectives for the Enterprise Data Governance program. As part of the Enterprise Data Governance effort, IBM also will suggest areas where existing technologies may be repurposed and/or leveraged to provide a more efficient and effective technology environment.


Financial impact:  $4,600,046.00

 

Recommendation:  That the Board approve the addition of IBM Project Change Request 1705, GCPS Enterprise Data Governance, to the existing IBM partnership contract approved in February 2016.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
