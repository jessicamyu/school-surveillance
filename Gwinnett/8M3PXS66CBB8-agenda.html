Subject
4.01 Parkview HS Marine Corps JROTC - Naval Honor School Award
Meeting
Nov 17, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITIONS
Type
Information

 

Honoree:  Parkview HS Marine Corps JROTC

 

Recognition:  Naval Honor School Award

 

Awarded by:  Commanding General, Marine Corps Training and Education Command, Quantico, VA

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Director of Technical Education, Computer Science and Work-Based Learning

 

Background:  JROTC units are selected by the area Commander for attaining the highest overall rating during the school year.  Subsequently, these units are designated Naval Honor Schools by the Commanding General Training and Education Command, MCCDC in Quantico, Virginia.  This designation is given to 20% of the JROTC units in the nation in recognition of a unit's exemplary performance during the area Commander's inspection, and the unit shows exemplary performance in drill competitions, community service, and marksmanship competitions.  The JROTC unit must also demonstrate exemplary performance in unit administration, training, supply, facilities, armory management, cadet citizenship, school and community involvement, scholastic achievement, and extracurricular events according to Training and Education Command guidelines. 

 

Parkview HS Marine Corps JROTC accomplishments for the past year include: Naval Honor School for the fourth consecutive year; first place in State Drill Competition; second place nationally in Orienteering (placed in top three for the past three years); state semifinals in Rifle competition; doubled enrollment in the past three years; includes cadets from six schools (Parkview, Brookwood, Grayson, Archer, Meadowcreek, and Berkmar); over $60,000 in cash and toys collected for Toys-for-Tots last year; averaged 40 hours per cadet in Community Service hours; two appointments to the United States Naval Academy; one appointment to West Point; five ROTC scholarships; and numerous academic scholarships.

 

 

Accompanying the honorees:  David Smith, principal, Parkview HS; Major Ronald Tootle, USMC retired; Gunnery Sergeant and Marine Instructor Dave Erwin, USMC retired; Parkview Marine JROTC Officers

4.02 GCPS Social Studies Department - Program of Excellence
Meeting
Nov 17, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITIONS
Type
Information

 

Honoree:  GCPS Social Studies Department

 

Recognition:  Program of Excellence

 

Awarded by:  Georgia Council for the Social Studies (GCSS)

 

Introduced by:  Dale Robbins, Associate Superintendent for Teaching and Learning Support

 

Background:  Each year, the Georgia Council for the Social Studies identifies exemplary social studies programs throughout the state.  The purpose is to identify and provide recognition for outstanding social studies programs currently being implemented in Georgia and to encourage the growth and development of effective programs in the social studies.  This year, GCSS recognized GCPS Social Studies Department for the "Building Literacy in Social Studies" initiative.  Over the past two years, the Social Studies Department has trained over 300 K-12 teachers using the "train the trainer" model for re-delivery to their local schools.  The program uses research-based strategies that align with our Quality Plus Teaching Strategies on the reading process, vocabulary development, fostering engaged learning, textbook literacy, gender-differences in reading instruction, primary and secondary documents, critical thinking about social studies issues and promotion of civic engagement in the community.

4.03 Oswaldo Terrones Suarez - 1st Place Winner, Jimmy Carter Mini-Poster Contest
Meeting
Nov 17, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITIONS
Type
Information

 

Honoree:  Oswaldo Terrones Suarez, Dacula ES

 

Recognition:  1st Place Winner, Jimmy Carter Mini-Poster Contest

 

Awarded by:  Jimmy Carter National Historic Site Education Program

 

Introduced by:  Dr. Debbie Daniell, Director of Social Studies

 

Background:  The Jimmy Carter National Historic Site Education Program is a curriculum-based program for organized school groups visiting the historic sites in Plains, Georgia.  Students have the opportunity to explore history through interactive activities at the Plains High School Museum, the Depot and the Jimmy Carter Boyhood Farm.  Each year, K-12 grade students are invited to participate in the "Plains, Peanuts, and a President" Mini-Poster Art Contest, where entries are judged by their creativity in work and neatness in drawing an image that represents Jimmy Carter, Plains, and Peanuts.  This year there were 9,566 K-12 entries statewide.  Oswaldo Terrones Suarez, a third grader at Dacula ES was the 1st place winner.  Oswaldo's entry will be on display at the Peanut Festival September 24, and in the Plains High School Museum until next summer, and featured on the Jimmy Carter website.  He will also receive an autographed copy of one of President Carter's books.

 

Accompanying the honoree: Mary Lou Enright, principal, Dacula ES; Pam Tuggle, assistant principal, Dacula ES; Edriel Wilkerson, 3rd grade teacher, Dacula, ES.
