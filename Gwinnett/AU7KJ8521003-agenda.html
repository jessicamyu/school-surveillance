7.06 Gwinnett Retirement System - Review of Claim

 

Prepared by: Joe Heffron, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by: Joe Heffron

 

Background: Within 60 days after notice of the denial of a claim, any person claiming retirement benefits under the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan may request a review of such denial by filing notice in writing with the GRS Plan Administrative Committee. The Committee shall promptly notify the Board of Education of a request for a review. The Board shall decide whether it or the Committee shall handle the review and shall so notify the Committee and the claimant. The Committee (or the Board, as the case may be), in its discretion, may hold a full hearing of the issues presented by the claimant if a majority of the Committee (or Board) members vote for such a hearing. Within 60 days after receipt of a request for review, or within 120 days if a hearing is held, the claimant shall be advised of the disposition of his claim. All such decisions by the Committee (or the Board) will be final, conclusive, and binding upon the Employer and the claimant and his beneficiaries.

 

Financial impact: None.

 

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to allow the Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee to handle review of the request received by the Committee on January 5, 2018, regarding payment of retroactive retirement benefits.

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
