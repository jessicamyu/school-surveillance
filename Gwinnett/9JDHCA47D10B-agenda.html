2.01 Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

 

Board members receive supporting documentation for items on the Monthly Business Meeting agenda seven days prior to the meeting.  Staff is available during this time to answer questions or provide additional information, if needed.

4.02 Maxwell HS of Technology - Technical Education - Industry Certification

 

Honorees:  Maxwell HS of Technology

 

Recognition:   Technical Education - Industry Certification

 

Awarded by:   The American Culinary Federation and the Georgia Department of Education

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Executive Director of Academies, Career and Technical Education

 

Background:  When a program becomes industry certified, it receives a "stamp of excellence", which represents the apex of program quality. Only those programs that have successfully undergone rigorous reviews by leaders from business and industry are recognized with this distinction.

 

This formal process strengthens all program components, including:

• Classrooms and labs which are equipped with state-of-the-art equipment and technology

• CTE curriculum standards and academic knowledge and skills that are aligned to national standards

• In-depth, project-based instruction and assessments in all curriculum areas

• Career and Technical Student Organizations (CTSOs) which offer co-curricular competitive events on the local, state and national level and provide leadership development skills for personal and professional growth

• Business, industry and community involvement in all aspects of the program 

 

The student benefits from participation in industry certified programs are tremendous. The students are ensured participation in programs that have been carefully interfaced with current industry standards, thus helping to increase their qualifications toward successful employment.  Schools which pursue this distinction are committed to improving student preparation and ensuring that Georgia’s workforce meets the demands of the 21st century.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
