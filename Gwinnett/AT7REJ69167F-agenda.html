Subject
11.03 Authorization to Begin G.O. Bond Referendum Process
Meeting
Dec 21, 2017 - Monthly Business Meeting 7 p.m.
Category
11. NEW BUSINESS
Type
Action

Prepared by: Joe Heffron, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by: J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background: Gwinnett County Public Schools has been developing the next version of “The Plan” (Phase 4) to address the future facility needs of the district and allow it to continue a proactive approach to managing its ongoing enrollment growth. The funding for capital improvements that is generated by the education special-purpose local option sales tax (E-SPLOST) has been depressed over the last several years, with revenue from the sales tax running well below the level needed to address the school district’s need for classrooms and other facilities, technology improvements, and systemwide infrastructure upgrades. To augment the E-SPLOST V funds, staff is recommending that a General Obligation bond referendum be held in conjunction with the General Primary Election on May 22, 2018. The bond referendum as proposed would not require an increase in the current debt service millage rate.

 

Financial impact: Future debt service payments (semi-annually) depending on market conditions at time of issuance. This General Obligation bond referendum would not require an increase in the current debt service millage rate.

 

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to authorize the CEO/Superintendent and staff to work with Thompson & Sweeny (Board attorney) and K-12 Capital Advisors (financial advisor) to begin the process and procedures necessary for placing a General Obligation bond referendum question on the ballot for the May 22, 2018, General Primary Election.

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
