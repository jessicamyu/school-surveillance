2.01  Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting. Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised. The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

 

Board members receive supporting documentation for items on the Monthly Business Meeting agenda seven days prior to the meeting. Staff is available during this time to answer questions or provide additional information, if needed.

7.03  Restatement of the Gwinnett County Board of Education's 403(b) Plan

 

Prepared by: David Harris, Director Retirement Services

 

Presented by: Joe Heffron, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background: As a result of a competitive Request for Proposal (RFP) issued in 2015, GCPS entered into a single provider service agreement with VALIC Retirement Services Company (VALIC) to become the sole service provider for the Board’s 403(b) Plan and 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan effective January 1, 2016.  Under the service agreement, VALIC now provides Plan Document compliance services at no extra charge to GCPS, thus allowing the Plans to forego the added administrative expense of separate legal review and preparation of Plan Document changes.  

     The plan document for the District's 403(b) Plan has been updated to remain in compliance with appropriate laws and contains no substantive changes to the current operation of the Board’s Plan. 

 

Financial impact: None.  The Plan is 100% funded by employee voluntary contributions. 

 

Recommendation: That the Board adopt the resolution to restate the Gwinnett County Board of Education’s 403(b) Plan, and to authorize the Director of Retirement Services to execute the restated Plan Document.

8.05  Tribunal Training Course Outline

 

Prepared by:  Dr. Steve Flynt, Associate Superintendent for School Improvement and Operations

 

Presented by:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  Due to the passage of Senate Bill 367 the Georgia Department of Education promulgated Rule 160-4-8-.15 which became effective August 3, 2016. This Rule requires the LEA to do the following:

  • Annually approve a Training Course Outline to be followed by the Tribunal Training provider(s).
  • This Outline requires a minimum of five (5) hours of instruction for all qualified Student Discipline Hearing Officers and/or Panel Members in the areas of 1) Student disciplinary provisions in Title 20, 2) Due process requirement under federal and state law, 3) Applicable rules of evidence, 4) Federal and state judicial decisions and 5) Applicable standards and the role of the Hearing Officer as an independent, neutral arbitrator.

 

Financial impact:  None

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education approve the attached Tribunal Training Course Outline.

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
