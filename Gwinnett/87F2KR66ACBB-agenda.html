Prior to each monthly business meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a work session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The work session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled monthly business meeting.

The monthly work session on February 8, 2007, will begin at 2:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, GA.

1.01 SACS CASI District Accreditation Report and Reception
Meeting
Feb 8, 2007 - Monthly Work Session
Category
1. Work Session Discussion Topics and Presentations
Type
Information

Gwinnett County Public Schools is concluding the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Council on Accreditation and School Improvement district accreditation visit. District accreditation supports a systemic approach to achieving results through improvement; is an integrated process that supports accountability requirements and serves to strengthen efforts to meet such requirements; and provides recognition for the system and its schools, students, teachers, and the community. Dr. Eddie Krenson, Chair, Senior Vice President - East Region, AdvancED / SACS CASI, will review Gwinnett County Public Schools SACS CASI District Accreditation visit with commendations and recommendations. Immediately following the presentation there will be a reception (2:30-3:00 p.m.) for the SACS CASI Team, Board members, and staff.

Subject
1.02 Core Instructional Materials Adoption
Meeting
Feb 8, 2007 - Monthly Work Session
Category
1. Work Session Discussion Topics and Presentations
Type
Information

In compliance with Gwinnett County Public Schools Policy IFAA and the Georgia Department of Education Rule 160-4-1.12, the periodic program and instructional materials review was conducted in the area of Social Studies, grades 6-12. All materials were piloted in classrooms during Fall 2006. The attached recommendations included in this document are based on the comprehensive social studies program review, an update of materials for two high school courses, Language Arts (AP Language and Composition) and Language Arts/Mathematics (SAT Preparation), and health materials for middle and high school use. Health materials were approved by the Community Health Advisory Committee (CHEAC).
