4.02 Lanier HS FIRST Robotics Team - Georgia 2013 FIRST Robotics State Champions

 

Honorees:         Lanier HS FIRST Robotics Team

 

Recognition:  Georgia 2013 FIRST Robotics State Champions

 

Awarded by:   FIRST Robotics

 

Introduced by:   Jody Reeves, Director of Career and Technical Education

 

Background:

FIRST Robotics Competitions (FRC) are considered the varsity sport for the mind.  FRC combines the excitement of sport with the rigors of science and technology. Under strict rules, limited resources, and time limits, teams of 25 students or more are challenged to raise funds, design a team "brand," hone teamwork skills, and build and program robots to perform prescribed tasks against a field of competitors.  It’s as close to "real-world engineering" as a student can get. Volunteer professional mentors lend their time and talents to guide each team.  On Saturday, October 5th, 2013 at the Georgia World Congress Center, 25 teams from Georgia competed for the 2013 State Title. The 120-lb. robots compete in alliances and score by shooting frisbees and climbing pyramids.  The Lanier High School Mechanical Bulls were strong competitors and emerged as a member of the winning alliance. 

 

Attending with Honorees:

Kerensa Wing, principal, Lanier HS

Michael Reilly, sponsor, Lanier HS

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
