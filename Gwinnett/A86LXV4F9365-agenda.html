Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on April 21, 2016, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

April 21, 2016 Monthly Work Session Topics

 

XFAT on Classified Staff Development - Staff provided a report on this Cross-Functional Action Team (XFAT) that focused on identifying best practices to support the selection, training, and ongoing development of non-certified staff members. It reviewed the current state of practices already in place within the district and discussed what steps need to be taken in the future so that the district's staff members who support those who teach are highly qualified. 

 

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) in GCPS - Staff provided an update on the implementation of Science, Technology, Engineering and Math (STEM) in Gwinnett County Public Schools. In addition, an update was provided to the board on the 2016 Science and Engineering Fair and the Governor's Office of Student Achievement (GOSA) STEM Innovation grant.

 

Update on Georgia Milestones End of Grade and End of Course Assessments - Staff provided an update on the Georgia Milestones End of Grade (EOG) and End of Course (EOC) assessments that will be given in April and May. 

 

Summer School 2016 - Staff presented information about upcoming Summer School dates and events for 2016.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

Legislative Update - Staff reviewed with the Board the status of education-related legislation in the current session of the Georgia General Assembly.

As a component of the GCPS commitment to continuous quality improvement, this Cross-Functional Action team (XFAT) focused on identifying best practices to support the selection, training, and ongoing development of non-certified staff members. It reviewed the current state of practices already in place within the district and discussed what steps need to be taken in the future so that the district's staff members who support those who teach are highly qualified and have the ongoing training and support to be successful in their roles.
