Honorees: Gwinnett County Public Schools' Title I Distinguished Schools

Recognition: 2007 Georgia Title I Distinguished Schools

Awarded by: Georgia Department of Education

Introduced by: Carol Grady, Director of Federal and Special Programs

Background: The Title I Distinguished Schools Program, authorized in Section 1117 of the No Child Left Behind Act of 2001, recognizes Title I schools that have made Adequate Yearly Progress (AYP) for three or more consecutive years. The program was established by the Georgia Department of Education to recognize schools that meet the state's academic content standards and student academic acheivement standards. State Superintendent of Schools Kathy Cox will host a banquet honoring the Georgia Title I Distinguished Schools on November 2 at the Georgia World Congress Center.

Honoree: Gary Petmecky, Parkview HS

Recognition: 2007 Georgia Social Studies Teacher of the Year

Awarded by: Georgia Council for the Social Studies

Introduced by: Debbie Daniell, Social Studies Director

Background: Mr. Petmecky was selected the 2007 Social Studies Teacher of the Year at the Georgia Council of Social Studies' annual conference on October 19, 2007. He is currently social studies department chair at Parkview HS and formerly named an Economics Teacher of the Year. He has been a statewide winner of the Georgia Stock Market Game, and serves as president of the Georgia Association of Economic Educators, teaches Advanced Placement Micro and Macro Economics courses for the College Board, and has served on Gwinnett County Public Schools' Social Studies Curriculum Review Committee this past year. In addition, Mr. Petmecky has assisted in training more than 400 Gwinnett County middle and high school teachers in the use of the Virtual Economics CD from the National Council for Economic Education. He was employed in the business world before coming to Gwinnett County Public Schools where he has taught for more than 15 years.

Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION

Information

Honoree: Sweetwater MS

Recognition: Jeff L. Townsend Award

Awarded by: International Committee for Children

Introduced by: Linda Mitchell, Executive Director of Student Accountability, Assessment, and Advisement

Background: Sweetwater MS has received national recognition for its exemplary use of the "Second Step" advisement program that promotes a safe and drug-free environment at school. As part of classroom instruction, the advisement program helps children develop empathy, interpersonal problem-solving, and cooperation skills. When applied, these skills help create a safe and drug-free learning environment.

Sweetwater MS implemented the "Second Step" program three years ago and is the only Georgia school receiving this year's award. The school will receive a $1,000 grant toward expansion of the program.


Mark Cheshire, ISS teacher, and Mary Vaughn, lead counselor, work with the program.

    11. ADJOURNMENT
    Subject
    11.01 Meeting Adjourned
    Meeting
    Nov 8, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
    Category
    11. ADJOURNMENT
    Type

    The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

    Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
