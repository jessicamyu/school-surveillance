6.16 Gwinnett Retirement System – Review of Claim
Meeting
Nov 18, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7:00 p.m.
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action

Prepared by:  Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by:  Rick Cost

 

Background:  Within 60 days after notice of denial of a claim for Long-Term Disability (LTD) benefits under the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan, a participant may request a review of the denial by filing written notice with the Plan Administrative Committee.  The Committee received such a request on October 28, 2010.

 

The claim involves a second denial of the Participant’s application for LTD benefits by the Director, based on the information contained in the original application and a subsequent review of the Participant’s condition by an independent physician. 

 

The procedures outlined in Article 8 of the GRS Plan Document call for the Committee to promptly notify the Board of Education of a request for a review.  The Board then decides whether it or the Committee shall handle the review, and notifies the Committee and the claimant.  Should the Board decide to allow the Committee to decide the claim, all such decisions are final, conclusive, and binding upon all parties.  The claimant is advised of the disposition of the claim within 45 days after receipt of the request for review.

 

Financial impact:  None.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to allow the Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee to handle review of the request received by the Committee on October 28, 2010 regarding denial of LTD benefits claim.

7.04 2010 Digital School Districts Survey
Meeting
Nov 18, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7:00 p.m.
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORTS
Type
Information

Prepared by:  Berney Kirkland, Chief of Staff

 

Presented by:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background: The Center for Digital Education, the National School Boards Association, and Converge Online conduct an annual "Digital School Districts Survey" to showcase exemplary school boards and districts' use of technology to govern the district; communicate with students, parents, and the community, and to improve district operations.  In the category for large school districts (15,000 students or more), Gwinnett County Public Schools was ranked sixth in the nation on the 2010 survey.  Districts ranking in the top ten in each size category are the ones judged to have demonstrated the fullest implementation of technology benchmarks in the evolution of digital education, as presented in the survey questions.

 

Recommendation: The Board will be presented with a certificate acknowledging Gwinnett County Public Schools' ranking among the top ten large districts in the 2010 Digital School Districts Survey.
