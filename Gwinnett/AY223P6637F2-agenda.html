Monthly Work Session 2:00 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on May 17, 2018, will begin at 2:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

May 17, 2018, Monthly Work Session Topics

 

Aspiring Leader Program Cohort 9 Graduation Recognition - The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry level assistant principal, administrative intern, and community school director positions. The ninth cohort of 75 graduates was presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of a four month long course of study that incorporated an in-house residency in the form of individualized leadership experiences. 

 

Board Business Oversight Workshop: Transportation - In conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing management of the school system's business and operational systems, the Board held a workshop on Transportation as part of its May 17, 2018, Monthly Work Session. CEO/Superintendent J. Alvin Wilbanks and Chief Operations Officer Walt Martin provided an overview of key issues and topics relevant to the sound operation of the school system's transportation department.

 

Quality-Plus Teaching Strategies Update - Staff presented the recently refreshed Quality-Plus Teaching Strategies. 

 

Cross-Functional Action Team Report: Grading Practices Across Curriculum -  Staff shared a report from the team's work that considered how the transition to a new gradebook program (Synergy) would impact how grading is configured at the district and local level.  

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

Legislative Update - Staff reviewed with the Board the status of education-related legislation in the current session of the Georgia General Assembly.

 

3rd Quarter Discipline Report for SY2018 - Staff presented the discipline report for the 3rd Quarter of SY2017-2018.

1.02 Aspiring Leader Program Cohort 9 Graduation Recognition

Category
1. Discussion Topics and Presentations
Type

The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry level assistant principal, administrative intern, and community school director positions. The ninth cohort of 75 graduates will be recognized for their completion of a four month long course of study that incorporated an in-house residency in the form of individualized leadership experiences. To personalize the learning, the cohort was divided into two groups, with a fall and spring session. To date, 42 (56%) members of this cohort have been appointed as assistant principals or administrative interns. A total of 385 (83%) graduates have been appointed to leadership positions from the nine cohorts of the Aspiring Leader Program.
