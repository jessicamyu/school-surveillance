Gwinnett County Public Schools has established a tradition of success in Science Olympiad over the last eleven years, and the Science Olympiad competition prepares students for success in college and careers.

Prepared by: LaWanda Hankins, Director of Financial Reporting and Accounting

 

Presented by: Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background: The Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) Statement, Fund Balance Reporting (statement number 54) was issued to provide clearer Fund Balance Classifications that can be more consistently applied.  As a result, some funds classified as Special Revenue Funds, must have their sources of funding formally "committed" by resolution at the highest level of decision making authority (Board of Education).

 

Fund 567 - This revenue source would be committed for the purpose of providing evaluation services of Gwinnett County students and strengthen the pipeline of these students into post-secondary STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematic) education.

 

 

Financial impact: None.  The revenue source for the fund listed above is currently being used for the purposes for which they are being "committed."

 

Recommendation: The Gwinnett County Public School Board of Education adopt a resolution to "commit" the revenue sources for Fund 567 for the purposes outlined above.  This revenue source would be reported as a Special Revenue Fund iin compliance with GASB statement 54.

10.02 Sale of Buice School Facility

 

Prepared by:  Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer      

 

Presented by: J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  The Buice School facility in Sugar Hill houses a special program for a small number of pre-kindergarten children. The long-term use of this school facility is not in the future plans for Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

In August 2015, the pre-kindergarten students at Buice School will be assigned to attend their respective home schools.  The vacating of Buice School designates this facility as surplus property for the school district and makes it available for sale to the City of Sugar Hill.

 

Financial impact:  The Buice School facility and property will be sold to the City of Sugar Hill for its negotiated value as determined by multiple independent appraisals.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education approve and execute the contract to sell the identified property to the City of Sugar Hill and approve a short-term lease for GCPS to occupy the property until the facility is vacated.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
