Thursday, August 16, 2012
Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on August 16, 2012, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

August 16, 2012 Monthly Work Session Topics:

 

The following topics were on the agenda of the Board's afternoon Work Session which began at 3:00 p.m.

 

Recognition and Graduation of Cohort 3, Aspiring Leader Program - The third cohort of 42 participants of the Aspiring Leader Program was presented to the Board for recognition of completion of the 6-month long course of study.

 

eCLASS Transformation Update - Staff updated the Board on the current status of eCLASS Development and implementation.

 

Curriculum and Instruction Summer Staff Development - Staff updated the Board on summer staff development and Common Core aligned AKS implementation.

 

2011- 2012 End Of Course Test and Criterion-Referenced Competency Tests Update - Staff updated the Board on the 2011-2012 EOCT and CRCT results.

 

Title IX Gender Equity Report - Staff presented the Title IX Gender Equity Report for the 2011-2012 school year.

 

Summer School Report - Staff briefed the Board on the academic success of students involved in this year's summer school at the elementary, middle, and high school levels.

 

Staff Reports:

 

Staffing Update - A monthly report on personnel matters was presented to the Board.

 

SY2012 Final Discipline Report - Staff provided the Board with the final discipline report for school year 2012.

 

Constitutional Amendment Update - Staff updated the Board with information related to the proposed amendment of Georgia's Constitution in regard to state approval of charter schools, as called for in House Resolution 1162 that was passed in the 2012 session of the Georgia General Assembly.

 

State Education Finance Study Commission Update - Staff presented background information on the recommendations of the State Education Finance Study Commission and reported out on the current status of Subcommittee #1: Foundational Funding Formula.

Category

Information

The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry-level assistant principal and administrative intern positions. The third cohort of 42 participants is presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of the 6-month long course of study concluding with a summer school residency. To date, 25 members of this cohort have been appointed as assistant principals or administrative interns. A total of 81 participants have been appointed to leadership positions from the first three cohorts of the Aspiring Leader Program.
