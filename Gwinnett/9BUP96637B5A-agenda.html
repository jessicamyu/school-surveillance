 2.01 Adoption of Agenda

7.03 GCPS 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan Document

 

Prepared by:  Tom Daymude, Director, Retirement and Risk Management Services

 

Presented by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background:  The GCPS 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan Document requires amending and restatement in order to reflect changes in federal laws impacting the operation of the Plan and to clarify certain definitions within the Plan.  The purpose of this proposed amendment and restatement is to: 

 

1.  clarify the definition of "Beneficiary" to streamline the process of providing death benefits if no Beneficiary is designated in the deferred Compensation Agreement or if no designated Beneficiary survives the Participant;

 

2.  clarify the definition of "Surviving Spouse", by incorporating the definitions found in Georgia statues, replacing references to the Federal Defense of Marriage Act, a part of which was declared unconstitutional by the U.S. Supreme Court in June 2013; and

 

3.  comply in good faith with the Heroes Earnings Assistance and Relief Act of 2008 ("HEART"), which provides for beneficial tax qualification for survivors of participants who die while performing qualified military service.

 

Financial impact:  None.  The GCPS 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan is 100% funded by employee voluntary contributions.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board approve and execute the proposed amendment and restatement of the GCPS 457(b) Deferred Compensation Plan Document to be effective November 1, 2013.

 14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
