2.01 Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

 

Board members receive supporting documentation for items on the Monthly Business Meeting agenda seven days prior to the meeting.  Staff is available during this time to answer questions or provide additional information, if needed.

4.01 Osborne MS Odyssey of the Mind Team - 2013 World Finals, 1st Place, Problem 1 - Pet Project - Division 2

 

Honorees:  Osborne Middle Odyssey of the Mind Team

Emma Beguhn, Benjamin Breer, Caroline Littleton, Bailey Martinez, Kyle Patel, Richard Perry, and Kayleigh Prusak

 

Recognition:  2013 World Finals, 1st Place, Problem 1-Pet Project-Division 2

 

Awarded by:   Odyssey of the Mind

 

Introduced by:   Julia Osborne, Accelerated Programs and Gifted Education Director

 

Background:  Odyssey of the Mind is an international educational program that provides creative problem solving opportunities for students from kindergarten through college.  Teams apply their creativity to compete on the local, state and World level.  Thousands of teams from throughout the US and from about 25 other countries participate in the program.  This creative extravaganza was held May 22-25 at Michigan State University, where more than 825 teams from all around the world came together to showcase talented students' creative minds.

The team chose to compete in the "Pet Project" vehicle category.  Working throughout the school year, the team designed, built, and ran three vehicles (each with a unique propulsion system) that delivered parts to a designated area; designed a pet animal which was assembled from the delivered parts and which later performed a "trick"; designed and built a signal that let the audience know which vehicle was about to travel and deliver a part. 

While scoring was largely based on creativity, engineering and risk-taking behind the propulsion systems, vehicle designs, pet animal and signal, the overall performance captured the attention of tournament competitors, judges, and spectators alike.

 

Accompanying Honorees:  Osborne MS Principal, John Campbell and Parent Coaches Janie Martinez and Ben Martinez

4.02 Victoria Martinez and Elias Gomez - ACT-SO New Brunswick Culinary Arts Competition Winners

 

Honorees:

 

Victoria Martinez, Meadowcreek HS      ACT-SO New Brunswick Culinary Arts Competition, 1st Place in State

Elias Gomez, Meadowcreek HS              ACT-SO New Brunswick Culinary Arts Competition, 2nd Place in State

          

                  

Recognition:  ACT-SO New Brunswick Culinary Arts Competition Winners

 

Awarded by: NAACP Afro-Academic Cultural, Technological and Scientific Olympics

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Technical Education, Computer Science, and Work-Based Learning Director

 

Background:

ACT-SO, a program sponsored by the NAACP, is an acronym that stands for Afro-Academic, Cultural, Technological and Scientific Olympics. It is a student enrichment program that culminates in a local and national competition where students compete for awards, and prizes.

 

The National NAACP ACT-SO Office, in conjunction with the 2012 Culinary Arts partners (Hyatt Hotels and Sodexo), has recently expanded the Entrepreneurship category by introducing Culinary Arts as a separate competition. Culinary Arts is offered to high school students who have formal culinary training and attend an institution that offers Culinary Arts as a Curriculum.

 

All Culinary Arts participants are required to complete a menu plan consisting of an appetizer and entrée. Also, students are required to do a five minute presentation on their culinary background, how they have come up with their dishes and about their mentorship with Sodexo. The guidelines include the community storeroom which houses all of the ingredients for the Competition, a list of spices and the identified protein. All participants must adhere to the food items available in the community storeroom. The mentoring chef assists students with developing and preparing their menu.

 

Additional Staff Attending with Honorees: 

 

Tommy Welch, principal, Meadowcreek High School, and Chef Simone Byron, instructor, Meadowcreek High School

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
