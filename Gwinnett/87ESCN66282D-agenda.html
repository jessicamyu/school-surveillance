Subject
4.01 GCPS Department of Special Education and Psychological Services, Excellence in the Education of Students with Disabilities
Meeting
Feb 12, 2009 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION
Type
Information

Honoree: GCPS Department of Special Education and Psychological Services
Accepting award: Susan White, Executive Director of Special Education and Psychological Services

Recognition: Excellence in the Education of Students with Disabilities – Certificate for Meeting State Target

Awarded by: Georgia Department of Education and Georgia Council for Administrators of Special Education (GCASE)

Introduced by: Dale Robbins, Associate Superintendent for Teaching and Learning

Background: At the annual fall conference of the Georgia Council for Administrators of Special Education (GCASE), Gwinnett County Public Schools was recognized as a leader in the state of Georgia for outstanding efforts to improve academic performance of students with disabilities. Data from the 2007-2008 school year reflected the accomplishments of Gwinnett. This recognition for excellence in the education of students with disabilities is achieved by meeting the state target:
    • for educating young children in the least-restrictive environment;
    • for reducing the students who drop out of school;
    • for graduates with regular education diplomas;
    • for students who meet or exceed state achievement standards for math; and,
    • for students who meet or exceed state achievement standards for reading/English language arts.

This recognition honors the entire Department of Special Education and Psychological Services, which includes GCPS Special Education, Collaborative, and General Education Teachers; GCPS local school leaders; Special Education directors, coordinators, and support personnel - ISC staff; Curriculum and Instruction directors and support staff – ISC; as well as the parents of students with disabilities.

4.03 North Gwinnett HS Air Force JROTC Program, "Exceeds Standards" Rating
Meeting
Feb 12, 2009 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION
Type
Information

Honoree: North Gwinnett HS Air Force JROTC Program (Unit GA-935)
Accepting Award: CM Sgt. Mike Shannon, Major Bruce Bonds, Cadet Corps Commander Colonel Chloe Babcock, and Ed Shaddix, Principal

Recognition: Air Force JROTC Unit Evaluation Rating of “Exceeds Standards”

Awarded by: Air Force Junior Reserve Officer Training Corps

Introduced by: Dr. John Green, Area Superintendent

Background: In December 2008, the North Gwinnett HS Air Force JROTC Program (Unit GA-935) underwent an overall unit assessment. The purpose of the assessment was to evaluate how effectively, efficiently, and safely Unit GA-935 accomplished its mission, as well as to determine the school’s compliance with Public Law, Dept. of Defense and Air Force Instructions, and the contract between the school and the Air Force. The AFJROTC Unit earned an overall unit assessment score of "Exceeds Standards," the highest rating attainable.
The Unit received the highest rating based on the following determinations: the excellent facilities provided by GCPS contribute greatly to the success of the Unit; the instructors are extremely talented and dedicated and create a dynamic and supportive learning environment in and out of the classroom; cadets display exemplary pride; and their extensive participation in numerous community service projects is very impressive. As a result, North Gwinnett HS Air Force JROTC Program (Unit GA-935) is making a positive impact on the school, the school district, and surrounding community, by developing citizens of character dedicated to serving their nation and community.

11.01 Meeting Adjourned
Meeting
Feb 12, 2009 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
