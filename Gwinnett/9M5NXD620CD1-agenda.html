Recognition:  1st Place Winners, Georgia Mathematics Tournament

 

Awarded by:   Georgia Council of Teachers of Mathematics

 

Introduced by:   Bonnie Driscoll, Director of Mathematics

 

Background:  The Georgia Council of Teachers of Mathematics (GCTM) sponsors the annual state math championship.  Schools are invited to send four of their best math team students to compete.  Schools earn an invitation to the state math tournament based on their performance at other tournaments throughout the state.  Schools from all Georgia High School Association (GHSA) and Georgia Independent School Association (GISA) classifications compete together.

At the state competition, competitors individually complete a 50-question multiple-choice test and a speed round followed by teams working together to complete a set of a dozen challenging problems.  The questions come from topics included in the standard high school curriculum up to and including calculus, as well as topics not traditionally found in the curriculum, such as number theory and combinatorics. 

 

A team of four students from the Gwinnett School of Math, Science, and Technology (GSMST) won first place in the 38th annual state mathematics tournament on Saturday, April 26, in Macon.  The team placed first in the state over all classifications.  Team members were Josh Brunner, Jason Fan, Timothy Gieseking, and Mark Wang.  GSMST’s team is coached by teacher Robin Miles.

7.08 Gwinnett Retirement System - Review of Claim

 

Prepared by:  Tom Daymude, Director, Retirement and Risk Management Services

 

Presented by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background:  Within 60 days after notice of denial of a claim for Long Term Disability (LTD) benefits under the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan, a participant may request a review of the denial by filing written notice with the Plan Administrative Committee.  The Committee received such a request on July 29, 2014.

 

The claim involves a second denial of the Participant’s application for LTD benefits by the Director, based on the information contained in the original application and documentation subsequently provided by the Participant and treating physicians.

 

The procedures outlined in Article 8 of the GRS Plan Document call for the Committee to promptly notify the Board of Education of a request for a review.  The Board then decides whether it or the Committee shall handle the review, and notifies the Committee and the claimant.  Should the Board decide to allow the Committee to decide the claim, all such decisions are final, conclusive, and binding upon all parties.  The claimant is advised of the disposition of the claim within 45 days after receipt of the request for review.

 

Financial impact: None

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to allow the Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee to handle review of the request received by the Committee on July 29, 2014 and make a final decision regarding the LTD benefits claim.

11.03 Intergovernmental Agreement By and Between the City of Norcross and Gwinnett County Board of Education for the Lease of Real Estate

 

Prepared by:   Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer

 

Presented by:   J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent     

 

Background:   For several months discussions have taken place between officials with the city of Norcross and Gwinnett County Public Schools regarding each entity's specific needs for certain properties located in the city limits. An intergovernmental agreement was negotiated that would authorize each entity to lease property it owns to the other entity.

According to the intergovernmental agreement, the City of Norcross would lease to the Gwinnett County Board of Education property near the intersection of Everglade Trail and Mitchell Road (Land Lot 225, District 6). The Board would lease to Norcross its property near the intersection of Price Road and Mitchell Road (Land Lot 242, District 6).

 

Financial Impact:   None

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education approve and execute the Intergovernmental Agreement for the Lease of Real Estate with the City of Norcross.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
