4.01 Nancy Chrisman, Shiloh HS - 28th Annual Leaveys Award for Excellence in Private Enterprise Education

 

Honoree: Nancy Chrisman, special education teacher, Shiloh High School

 

Recognition: 38th Annual Leaveys Award for Excellence in Private Enterprise Education

 

Awarded by: Freedoms Foundation at Valley Forge
 

Introduced by: Dr. Debbie Daniell, Director of Social Studies 

 

Background: Given annually at the National Council for the Social Studies, the Leaveys Award honors outstanding teachers who create and conduct programs in entrepreneurship.  Nancy Chrisman earned this national honor in recognition of her “Silver Lining Boutique” program. The Silver Lining is a school-based fashion boutique operated by Shiloh High School students. The Silver Lining’s mission is to provide an ongoing workplace environment for students to learn hands-on job skills, work ethics, and entrepreneurial skills. Students start a business from the ground up, developing an entrepreneurial inclination and learning problem-solving and team-building skills along the way.  Ms. Chrisman is one of only 12 educators in the country to be honored

 

Freedoms Foundation at Valley Forge was founded in 1949 and has been conducting educational programs for students and teachers since 1965. The mission of Freedoms Foundation is to educate the American people about their rights and responsibilities as citizens, honor acts of civic virtue, and challenge all people to reject apathy and get involved. Since its founding, 2 million students and teachers have been impacted by its citizenship programs. The Leaveys awards were established by the co-founder of the Farmers Insurance Group, the late Thomas Leavey and his philanthropic wife Dorothy, in partnership with the Freedoms Foundation at Valley Forge.

 

Additional Staff Attending with Honoree:  Dr. Danyel Dollard, principal, Shiloh High School

6.01 Georgia Power Commercial Energy Rebate Program

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations

 

Presented by:  Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer

                       

Background:  Georgia Power partners with the Gwinnett County School district to serve 48 schools and 12 support locations with electricity. As a part of their service to the district, they help us save money by helping us use energy efficiently while protecting the environment.

 

Through the Commercial Energy Efficiency Rebate Program, Georgia Power reinforces its commitment to helping customers save money and energy.  For each project that meets eligibility requirements (such as lower wattage lighting, more efficient heating and cooling, the addition of occupancy sensors, etc.)  at a Georgia Power served school, dollars are returned to our district through the rebate program that are in turn used to fund additional energy saving initiatives at other facilities. GCPS has earned a total of $64,484.51 in rebates. Doug Jenkins and Georgia Power will present to the Board a check in the amount of $64,484.51 for our participation in this rebate program.

       

Financial Impact:  None

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
