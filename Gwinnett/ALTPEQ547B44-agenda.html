Monthly Work Session 2:30 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting.

1.01  May 18, 2017 Work Session Topics

 

May 18, 2017, Monthly Work Session Topics

 

Assisting Developing Adults with Productive Transitions (ADAPT) and Supported Training and Rehabilitative Instruction in Vocational Education (STRIVE) Report -  Representatives of ADAPT/STRIVE presented a report to the Board on the school's accomplishments, areas needing attention, and general student experience. The report is one in a series that will be presented by the school district's "special entity" schools that serve students district wide rather than in specific clusters. 

 

Building Relationships in Discipline, Goals, and Education (The BRIDGE) and Oakland Meadow School Reports -  Similar to the ADAPT/STRIVE report, teachers, parents, and students of The BRIDGE and Oakland Meadow School presented reports to the Board on their respective schools' accomplishments, areas needing attention, and general student experience.  

 

GCPS' Balanced Assessment System, Part II - Staff provided information on norm-referenced and locally created assessments.  

 

Implementation of New Academic Knowledge and Skills Curriculum Standards in Science and Social Studies -  Staff provided an update on the new Academic Knowledge and Skills curriculum standards in K-12 science and social studies and the instructional resources available to support their successful implementation.

 

3rd Quarter Discipline Report - Staff shared the 3rd Quarter Discipline Report for SY2017 with the Board.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

Legislative Update - Staff reviewed the status of education-related legislation passed in the 2017 session of the Georgia General Assembly with the Board.
