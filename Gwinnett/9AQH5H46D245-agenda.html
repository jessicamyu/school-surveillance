Monthly Work Session 2 p.m. Tour of New Online Campus Facility; 3:00 p.m. Work Session
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting.

The Monthly Work Session on September 19, 2013, will begin at 2:00 p.m. with a tour of the new Online Campus located at 713 Hi Hope Road, Lawrenceville, followed by the regular Work Session at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools' Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business meeting.

 

The monthly Work Session on September 19, 2013, began at 2:00 p.m. with a tour of the new Online Campus, 713 Hi Hope Road, Lawrenceville, followed by the regular Work Session at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools' Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

 

September 19, 2013 Monthly Work Session Topics:

 

Aspiring Principal Program (APP) Graduation - The Aspiring Principal Program (APP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares assistant principals to move into principal positions. The 7th cohort of 16 graduates were presented to the Board in recognition of completion of a year-long course of study.

 

Business Oversight Workshop - Information Management and Technology Division - Staff from the Division of Information Management and Technology presented a Board Business Oversight Workshop in conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing management of the school system's business and operational systems.

 

 Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with the current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

2014 Legislative Issues - Staff updated the Board on the timeline for the presentation of the 2014 Legislative Program.

 

School Nutrition Program SY13 Report - Staff provided the Board with an annual report on all school nutrition programs. This report reflects the sound position of the School Nutrition Program and is provided to keep the Board information of participation and the financial status of each school.

 

GoSTEM 2012/2013 Summary Report - Staff provided the Board with an overview of the GoSTEM partnership with Georgia Institute of Technology, and progress being made on this integration of science, technology education, and math.

 

Teacher Connections Update - Staff provided the Board with an update on Teacher Connections, a digital library resource of lesson plans created to model quality teacher stratgegies.

The Aspiring Principal Program (APP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares Assistant Principals to move into Principal positions. The seventh cohort of 16 graduates is presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of a year-long course of study. To date, 7 members of this cohort have been appointed as principal. A total of 94 participants have been appointed to principal positions from the first seven cohorts of the Aspiring Principal Program.
