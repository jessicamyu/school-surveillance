5.01 Gwinnett County Public Schools Public Service Award -- Connie Wiggins (during the Business Meeting the agenda was amended to move this item up to be renumbered 4.01)

 

Honoree:  Connie Wiggins, Executive Director of Gwinnett Clean & Beautiful

 
Recognition:  Gwinnett County Public Schools Public Service Award
 
Introduced by:  CEO/Superintendent J. Alvin Wilbanks
 
Background: Connie Wiggins, who retires in April as Executive Director of Gwinnett Clean & Beautiful since 1985, will be recognized with the Board's highest honor, the Gwinnett County Public Schools Public Service Award. The honor is bestowed in recognition of Mrs. Wiggins' three decades of partnering with the school district to educate students, teachers, parents, and citizens about the importance of being good environmental stewards. Under Mrs. Wiggins' guidance, Gwinnett Clean & Beautiful has developed and provided instructional resources that align with the district's science curriculum standards. She has been instrumental in building students' and adults' awareness of and engagement around citizens' responsibility to care for the cleanliness, beauty, and environmental health of the community in which they live. Through her leadership at GC&B and by her personal example, Mrs. Wiggins has made a positive impact on the quality of life for all who live in Gwinnett County and leaves a legacy of outstanding community service. She is only the fifth recipient of the GCPS Public Service Award.

8.06 Resolution for Title 50 Waiver

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations      

 

Presented by: Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer      

 

Background:  Gwinnett County Public Schools seeks the Georgia Board of Education’s approval of a request for a waiver from compliance with the specific vehicle provisions of “Georgia School Bus Specifications” (GADOE 2010). The district wishes to conduct a pilot using third-party transportation services in low-density (nine or fewer students) transport situations where requiring use of traditional school buses is not possible, practical, or cost effective. Use of third-party services in specific instances will provide for safe student transport and help mitigate the significant, rising costs associated with the growing requirements for this type of student transportation.

 

Financial impact:  None.  Any pilot costs within existing budgets.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a resolution requesting a waiver of State Board Rule 160-5-3-.01 in order to conduct a pilot program of alternative vehicle student transportation.

11.01 Board of Education's Core Beliefs and Commitments

 

Prepared by:  Berney Kirkland, Chief of Staff

 

Presented by:  J. Alvin Wilbanks,CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  In 2006, the Gwinnett County Board of Education adopted a statement of its Core Beliefs and Commitments regarding education in Gwinnett County Public Schools. For almost a decade the beliefs and commitments have been a prominent document among the "foundational components" of the school district's strategic direction. In recent years the Board has revised several of these foundational components, including the district's vision, strategic goals, and Strategic Priorities for 2010-2020, as well as the district's definition and characteristics of world-class schools. In the fall of 2015, the Board began work on revisions to its core beliefs and commitments, building on the original statement but enhancing it to reflect the Board's collective views about the value of public education in America. A draft document was produced and shared with district leaders for feedback, resulting in a final update to the foundational component that will be formally adopted by the Board.

 
Financial impact: None
 
Recommendation:  That the Board adopt the proposed revisions to its Core Beliefs and Commitments

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
