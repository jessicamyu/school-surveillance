4.01 Leo Xu and Yasmeen Ahmed - 2012-2013 Continental Math League National Student Leaders

 

Honorees: Leo Xu, Arcado Elementary School, and Yasmeen Ahmed, Fort Daniel Elementary School

 

Recognition:  2012-2013 Continental Math League National Student Leaders

 

Awarded by:   Continental Math League

 

Introduced by:   Bonnie Driscoll, Director of Mathematics  

 

Background:  Continental Math League is a high-level problem-solving competition which allows students to compete with other students on the same grade level from schools all over the United States. This national competition included 5 tests given during the school day at various times throughout the year. Each test is 30 minutes in length and consists of 6 questions. All students are eligible to participate. The top 6 scores from each school for each test are submitted to the Continental Math League Office in New York. Continental Math League recognizes outstanding student performance at the national and regional levels.

 

Leo Xu from Arcado Elementary School was recognized for achievement in Euclidean Math, 4th Grade level and Yasmeen Ahmed from Fort Daniel Elementary School for achievement in Euclidean Math, 5th Grade level.  Both have been recognized as National Student Leaders based on their outstanding performance in the Continental Math League Competition during the 2012- 2013 school year. 

 

Accompanying Honorees:  Penny Palmer Young, Principal, Arcado Elementary and Paul Willis, Principal, Fort Daniel Elementary School will accompany the honorees.

4.06 Parkview HS Marine Corps JROTC - Naval Honor School Award

 

Honorees:    Parkview HS Marine Corps Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps

 

Recognition:    Naval Honor School Award

 

Awarded by:  Commanding General, Marine Corps Training and Education Command, Quantico, VA

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Director, Career and Technical Education

 

Background:

Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (JROTC) units are selected by the Area Commander for attaining the highest overall rating during the school year. Subsequently, these units are designated Naval Honor School by the Commanding General Training and Education Command, MCCDC Quantico, VA. This designation is given to 20% of the JROTC units in the nation in recognition of a unit’s exemplary performance during the area Commander’s inspection, and the unit shows exemplary performance in drill competitions, community service, and marksmanship competitions. The JROTC unit must also demonstrate exemplary performance in unit administration, training, supply, facilities, armory management, cadet citizenship, school and community involvement, scholastic achievement, and extra curricular events according to Training and Education Command guidelines. For the sixth consecutive year, Parkview HS has been awarded the Naval Honor School Award.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
