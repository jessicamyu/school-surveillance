Thursday, October 20, 2016
Monthly Work Session 2:30 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on October 20, 2016, will begin at 2:30 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

October 20, 2016 Monthly Work Session Topics

 

Board Business Oversight Workshop - Division of Information Management and Technology - In conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing management of the school system's business and operational systems, the Board held a workshop on Information Management and Technology as part of its October 20, 2016, Monthly Work Session. Chief Information Officer Frank Elmore provided an overview of key issues and topics relevant to the sound operation of the school system.

 

Junior Achievement (JA) Academy Update - Mr. Jack Harris, President/CEO Junior Achievement of Georgia and staff presented an update of the Junior Achievement Academy to the Board of Education.

 

National Assessments Summary - Staff shared with the Board a summary of district and school level results on the SAT, ACT, and Advanced Placement examinations.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

Benefits Open Enrollment - Staff reviewed changes to the 2017 Open Enrollment Program which will begin October 17, 2016, and close November 4, 2016.

 

Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) - Revised Standards - Staff reviewed revised standards for the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA).

 

2017 Legislative Program - Staff presented a draft of the 2017 Legislative Program for the Board's consideration.

 

Teacher Compensation System - Mr. Wilbanks briefed the Board on progress to date in the development of a new Teacher Compensation System model based in part on performance.
