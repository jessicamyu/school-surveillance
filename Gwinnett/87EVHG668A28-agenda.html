4.01 Peachtree Ridge HS, High Schools That Work Gold Improvement Award
Meeting
Aug 9, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION
Type
Information

Honoree: Peachtree Ridge HS

Recognition: High Schools That Work Gold Improvement Award

Awarded by: Southern Regional Education Board

Introduced by: Dale Robbins, Area Superintendent

Background: The High Schools That Work Gold Improvement Award is given to schools that had significant improvement in reading, mathematics, and science from 2004 to 2006 on the High Schools That Work (HSTW) Assessment. Included in the criteria for the award, schools must have had 50 or more students complete the 2006 assessment; participate in two assessments (2004 and 2006); not have greater than a ten-point change in the percentage of minority students or the percentage of students with a low parent education level from 2004 to 2006; met state Adequate Yearly Progress requirements; and had significant gains in achievement in all three subject areas from 2004 to 2006. Only 19 high schools in the HSTW network received this award at the 2007 HSTW Summer Staff Development Conference in New Orleans, Louisiana.

Dr. Steven Flynt is principal at Peachtree Ridge HS, and Gail Adkinson is assistant principal for curriculum and instruction.

11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
