4.08 Phu Nguyen, Norcross HS; Savana Ogburn, Grayson HS; Jenna Still, Brookwood HS; Cadie Crow, Mill Creek HS - National Gold and Silver Medalists, Scholastic Art and Writing Awards

 

Honorees:  Phu Nguyen, Norcross High School, Gold Medal Winner, Savana Ogburn, Grayson High School - Gold Medal Winner, Jenna Still, Brookwood High School - Silver Medal Winner, and Cadie Crow, Mill Creek High School - Silver Medal Winner    

 

Recognition:  Scholastic Art & Writing Awards (Gold and Silver Medalists)

 

Awarded by: Alliance for Young Artists and Writers

 

Introduced by:   Robert Andrews, Director of Fine Arts 

 

Background: The Scholastic Art & Writing Awards began as a small writing contest with a $5 prize and six winning applicants in 1923. Today, more than 90,000 teens in grades 7 through 12 from around the nation annually submitted more than 185,000 works of art and writing in 28 categories.  The works were judged by artistic professionals who named four national winners from Gwinnett County Public Schools.  These students have been invited to New York on June 5-7 as their artwork will be displayed nationally.

 

Phu Nguyen, art student at Norcross HS, is a Gold Medal winner for his drawing entitled, “City of Atlantis.” Phu’s art teacher is Angela Cronon and William Bishop is the school’s principal.

 

Savana Ogburn, art student at Grayson HS, is a Gold Medal winner for her photographic work entitled, “Tulle Expansions.”  Savana’s art teacher is Carisa Griffin and Dana Pugh is the school’s principal.

 

Jenna Still, art student at Brookwood HS, is a Silver Medal winner for her ceramics and glass work entitled, “Fundens Ad Angulum.”  Jenna’s art teacher is Chris Vigardt and Bo Ford is the school’s principal.

 

Cadie Crow, art student at Mill Creek HS, is a Silver Medal winner for her artwork entitled, “Clowning Around.” Cadie’s art teacher is Mike Lasseter and Jason Lane is the school’s principal.

11.02 Investing in Educational Excellence (IE2) Partnership Contract Amendment

 

Prepared by:    Dr. Steve Flynt, Chief Strategy & Performance Officer

 

Presented by:  Dr. Steve Flynt

 

Background:  Gwinnett’s IE² Partnership Contract which was approved in 2009 provides local schools and the school district with flexibility from thirteen laws and the accompanying State Board rules.  This flexibility is coupled with accountability targets for the five years of the contract. Since the contract was approved, a number of factors have changed, including the shift to a new accountability system – The College and Career Ready Performance Index, and changes in curriculum and testing at the state level.  GCPS has developed a new amendment to the initial contract which will be submitted to the State Board of Education for approval.  The amendment ensures that each school’s contract is relevant based on the unforeseen changes listed previously.

 

A Public Hearing on the proposed IE2 Partnership Contract Amendment will be held at 6:00 p.m. on April 17 in the Board Room at the Instructional Support Center, 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia. The purpose of the public hearing is to review and gather input on the proposed amendment.

 

Financial impact:  n/a

 

Recommendation:  That the Board approve Gwinnett’s IE² Partnership Contract Amendment as presented.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
