2.01 Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

 

Board members receive supporting documentation for items on the Monthly Business Meeting agenda seven days prior to the meeting.  Staff is available during this time to answer questions or provide additional information, if needed.

4.01 Grace Kim, Brookwood HS, Allison Park and Connor O'Kelley, North Gwinnett HS - National Latin Exam, Perfect Papers and Book Awards

 

Honorees:  

Grace Kim, Perfect Papers - Brookwood HS

Allison Park, Perfect Papers - North Gwinnett HS

Connor O’Kelley, Maureen O'Donnell Book Award - North Gwinnett HS

 

Recognition:  National Latin Exam, Perfect Papers and Book Awards

 

Awarded by:   American Classical Association

 

Introduced by:   Jon Valentine, Director of Foreign Languages

 

Background:  Each year,  Latin students from across the nation participate in the National Latin Exam. GCPS students compete against both private and public school peers and are scored on their knowledge of Latin grammar, comprehension, derivatives, literature, use of oral Latin, and Roman life and history, by the American Classical Association. In 2015, nearly 141,000 students from all over the United States and 20 other countries took this exam but only 1,558 students were awarded Perfect Papers. Additionally, only 459 test takers won the Maureen O’Donnell Book Award for earning four gold medals in a row.  Gwinnett is home to four recipients of Perfect Papers and six recipients of the Book Award. Of our ten winners, three students remain in GCPS -- the rest have moved on to excel in college. Congratulations to our Latin students and to their teachers on this truly outstanding achievement. 

 

Additional Staff Attending with Honorees:

Nathan Ballantine, Principal, North Gwinnett High School

Jeremy Martin, Teacher, North Gwinnett High School

4.02 Oscar Cruz-Nataren, Summerour MS - 1st Place MS Chopin in Art Contest, The Chopin Society of Atlanta

 

Honoree:  Oscar Cruz-Nataren, Summerour Middle School

 

Recognition:  1st Place Middle School Division, Chopin in Art Contest 

 

Awarded by: The Chopin Society of Atlanta 

 

Introduced by:  David DuBose, Director of Fine Arts 

 

Background:  The Chopin Society of Atlanta has sponsored a Chopin in Art contest for school students K-12 in the State of Georgia. The purpose of the contest is to give participants the opportunity to express their appreciation of Chopin's life and music through visual media. Each participant presents an original work of art representing Chopin's life and/or music in the form of a drawing or a painting. The Chopin Society of Atlanta awards 1st through 3rd place prizes to participants in elementary, middle school, and high school categories throughout Georgia. 

 

The Chopin Society of Atlanta is a non-profit organization devoted to promoting Fryderyk Chopin's music, advancing the understanding and appreciation of his legacy and promoting the arts in the Metro Atlanta area. 

 

Additional Staff Attending with Honoree: Linn Zamora, Art Teacher, and Dorothy Parker-Jarrett, Principal.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
