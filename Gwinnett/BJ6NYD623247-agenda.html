This award has been offered through the German Central Office of Schools Abroad since 1974 with over 75,000 students from 65 countries sitting for this examination each year.

11.01  Gwinnett Retirement System

 

Prepared By;  Joe Heffron, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented By: Joe Heffron    

 

Background:  Annually the Gwinnett Retirement System has an actuarial study conducted to determine the annual employer contribution rate needed to fund the plan. This employer rate is included in the budget adopted by the Board each year. The current rate for the Fiscal Year 2020 budget is 5.53%. The actuarial study has calculated the annual employer rate required for Fiscal year 2021 budget will be 6.41%.  In order to fund the plan at the highest level possible and mitigate potential future funding increases, staff is recommending that the district begin funding the plan at the new rate effective January 1, 2020.  

 

Financial Impact:  $4.7 million in additional contributions to the plan for the remainder of fiscal year 2020.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education approve the actuarial determined employer contribution rate of 6.41% of payroll for the Gwinnett Retirement System effective January 1, 2020.

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
