11.02  Gwinnett Retirement System - Claim Review

 

Prepared By: Joe Heffron, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented By: Joe Heffron

 

Background: Within 60 days after notice of a second denial of a claim for benefits under the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan, a participant may request a review of the denial by filing written notice with the Plan Administrative Committee.  The Committee received such a request on January 21, 2020.  The claim involves an application for GRS Long Term Disability benefits.  The procedure outlined in Article 8 of the GRS Plan document calls for the Committee to promptly notify the Board of Education of a request for a review.  The Board then decides whether it or the Committee shall handle the review, and notifies the Committee and the claimant.  Should the Board decide to allow the Committee to decide the claim, all such decisions are final, conclusive and binding upon all parties.  The claimant is advised of the disposition of the claim within 60 days after receipt of the request for review or within 120 days if a hearing is held.

 

Financial Impact: None

 

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to allow the Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee to handle review of the request received by the Committee on January 21, 2020, regarding payment of Long Term Disability benefits.

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
