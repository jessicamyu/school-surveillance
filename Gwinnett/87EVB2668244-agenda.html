Meeting
Sep 13, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION
Type
Information

Honorees: Gwinnett County Public Schools and Information Management Division

Recognition: 2007 Laureate Award in the Education and Academics

Awarded by: The Computerworld Honors Program

Introduced by: Scott Futrell, Chief Information Officer, and Randall Seabolt, IBM

Background: Men, women, organizations, and institutions from around the world are creating innovative ways to utilize technology to be leveraged to benefit society worldwide. Some of the byproducts of these efforts are the records of what has been accomplished and how it is being used to make a difference. In its 19th year, The Computerworld Honors Program acknowledges these achievements as well as carves a place in history for these priceless materials to be retained.

Gwinnett County Public Schools and the Division of Information Management are now part of this history after being recognized by Computerworld for their extraordinary use of information technology (IT) in providing access to information. This contribution has been archived for future generations and is now part of the international best practices in leading the world’s ongoing IT revolution. The award was accepted by Scott Futrell, Chief Information Officer, on behalf of CEO/Superintendent J. Alvin Wilbanks at a global ceremony held in Washington, D.C. in June 2007.

10.03 Naming Grayson HS Football Field
Meeting
Sep 13, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
10. NEW BUSINESS
Type
Action

Prepared by: Delores Hendrix, Area Superintendent

Presented by: J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

Background: Acting on behalf of the school and community, Keith Chaney, principal of Grayson HS, and the Grayson HS Local School Council, have requested that the football field on the campus of Grayson HS be named for two very important members of its community, Jack Britt and Dr. Mike Moody, and be known as “Britt-Moody Field.”

Jack Britt: Jack Britt graduated from Grayson HS in 1946 and played basketball on the Grayson HS team the year the school won the county championship. He is a World War II veteran. Mr. Britt started the football program at Tucker HS and Lawrenceville HS, as well as taught at South Gwinnett HS, Parkview HS, and Bethesda ES. Mr. Britt built several trophy cases for Grayson HS and lockers for the softball, basketball, and soccer programs. He also contributed to the building of Grayson HS’s stadium, concession stand, restrooms, and coaching offices. He continues to be an active member of the Grayson Alumni Association.

Mike Moody: Dr. Mike Moody was the first principal of Grayson HS from 2000 to 2002. He created the vision of “First Comes Learning" at Grayson HS. Dr. Moody led the fundraising efforts to build Grayson HS's football stadium, and the baseball and softball facilities. Because of his diligence, Grayson HS was the first new high school to open with a stadium built by the community. He developed a strong working relationship between Grayson HS and the Grayson Alumni Association. Dr. Moody was an educator for 32 years.

The dedication of the football field would be held before the game against South Gwinnett HS on Friday, October 5, 2007.

Financial impact: None

Recommendation: That the Board of Education name the football field on the campus of Grayson HS in honor of Jack Britt and Dr. Mike Moody and be known as “Britt-Moody Field.”

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session.
