Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on August 20, 2015, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

August 20, 2015 Monthly Work Session Topics

 

Aspiring Leader Program Cohort 6 Graduation Recognition - The sixth cohort of 44 graduates was presented to the Board of Education for recognition of the completion of the six-month long course of study concluding with a summer residency. 

 

PELP Team 2015 Presentation - Members of the 2015 team that attended the Public Education Leadership Project training in July presented an overview of the team's strategic problem of practice that was addressed during the week-long professional development program.

 

2015 Summer Leadership Conference Report - Staff presented a report on the 2015 Summer Leadership Conference held June 3-5, 2015 at the J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center. The report included information on participants, distinguished speakers, and choice sessions. Conference evaluation data was also shared.

 

Title IX Gender Equity Report - Staff presented the 2014-15 Title IX Gender Equity Report.  This report provided figures regarding programmatic expenditures and the number of male and female students in GCPS who participated in Georgia High School Association sponsored activities for the 2014-15 school year.

 

Final Discipline Report for School Year 2014-2015 - Staff presented to the Board the 2014-15 Final Discipline Report. The report provided data related to the number of student disciplinary hearings and suspensions that occurred in 2014-15.  In addition, the report highlighted system-wide initiatives that were implemented to improve student discipline in our schools.

 

5 R's Update - Staff provided the Board with an update of the 5 R's project.  5 R's represents the following project initiatives - Rebound, Recovery, Redirect, Reconnect, and Rescue.

 

Summer 2015 Update - Staff presented to the Board a report of the 2015 Summer School session and an update of the district AdvancED accreditation.

 

State Assessments Update - Staff presented an update to the Board on the Milestones Assessment System, as well as promotion and retention requirements for 2015-16.

 

Summer Staff Development -Staff updated the Board on summer staff development, New Teacher Orientation, and the inaugural Teaching and Learning Conference.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

1.02 Aspiring Leader Program Cohort 6 Graduation Recognition
Meeting
Aug 20, 2015 - Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Category
1. Discussion Topics and Presentations
Type

The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry level assistant principal and administrative intern positions. The sixth cohort of 44 graduates is presented to the Board of Education for the recognition of completion of the six-month long course of study concluding with a summer residency. To date, 35 (80%) members of this cohort have been appointed as assistant principal or administrative interns. A total of 282 (82%) participants have been appointed to leadership positions from the six cohorts of the Aspiring Leader Program. Staff will introduce a few of Aspiring Leader Program Cohort 6 graduates to the Board.
