Meeting
Dec 16, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7:00 p.m.

Information

Honoree:  Kelly Stopp

 

Recognition:  Georgia’s 2010 National Milken Educator of the Year

 

Awarded by:  Milken Family Foundation

 

Introduced by:   Calvin Watts, Area Superintendent

 

Background: The Milken Educator Awards program was conceived by Lowell Milken to recognize the importance of outstanding educators, and encourage talented young people to enter the teaching profession. The award comes with a no strings attached cash prize of $25,000. Kelly Stopp, Meadowcreek ES, was recently surprised with the news of receiving this award.  Working with her Ph.D. in reading, Kelly goes to great lengths to help her students gain literacy skills.  More than 90 percent of her students (68% of whom are English as a Second Language speakers) passed the second grade reading portion of the CRCT.  As second grade reading specialist, Kelly extends her impact by sharing her professional development in literacy techniques.  She also created a grammar lesson booklet which is now used countywide, and organized a team to transform two reading rooms.

 

 

Accompanying the honoree: Kelli McCain, Principal, Meadowcreek ES

4.02 Mindy Lewis, 2010 Georgia Outstanding Social Studies Educator of the Year
Meeting
Dec 16, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7:00 p.m.
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITIONS
Type
Information

Honoree:  Mindy Lewis 

 

Recognition: 2010 Georgia Outstanding Social Studies Educator of the Year

 

Awarded by:  Georgia Council for Social Studies

 

Introduced by: Debbie Daniell, Director of Social Studies

 

Background: The Georgia Council for Social Studies was established in 1981 and seeks to recognize educators who have made an outstanding contribution in the teaching of social studies.  The selection of Educator of the Year is based on the nominee's promotion of social studies education that includes activities that demonstrate significant service and foster the development of democratic values and citizenship in the classroom, school and community.  Other standards for selection include professional involvement beyond the normal job requirements, efforts toward the improvement of student learning, use of multiple sources to enhance social studies instruction, and professional publication and/or conference or workshop presentations.

 

Ms. Lewis currently serves as a Content Assessment Specialist.  Prior to this position, she was the social studies instructional coach and an eighth grade social studies teacher at Crews MS.  She has been active in many of the county social studies initiatives such as building literacy in social studies, textbook adoption, Project Citizen, the Georgia Economic History Project, curriculum review and sequential lesson plans.  She also has been trained as a History Alive! instructional coach and serves as a trainer for the Center for Civic Education.  Recognized as a Georgia Department of Education Master Teacher, Ms. Lewis has presented workshops for GCPS, the Georgia Council for Social Studies, and the National Council for Social Studies.

Honoree:  North Gwinnett MS

 

Recognition:  GASSP Distinguished Breakthrough Middle School

 

Awarded by:  Georgia Association of Secondary School Principals

 

Introduced by:  Dr. John Green, Area Superintendent

 

Background: The Georgia Association of Secondary School Principals chose to recognize middle schools in Georgia that are high achieving or dramatically improving student achievement. This award is modeled after the NASSP Breakthrough School Award. One school from each GASSP district was nominated for a total of 13. North Gwinnett MS was the nominee from District K, which is Gwinnett County. Nominees sent in an application to be considered as a Distinguished Breakthrough Middle School. The selection criteria was based on collaborative leadership among the staff, personalization and attention to students, and access to a rigorous curriculum for all students with differentiated instruction, multiple assessments and data-based decision making. North Gwinnett MS was selected as one of the 5 Distinguished Breakthrough Schools.
