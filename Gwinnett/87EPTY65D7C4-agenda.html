Subject
7.05 Elementary and Middle School Gateway Review
Meeting
Jun 17, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORTS
Type
Information

Prepared by: Delores Hendrix, Assistant Superintendent of School Operations and Support

Presented by: Calvin Watts, Dr. Gale Hey, and Dr. John Green, Area Superintendents

Background: Of the 43,740 Gwinnett fourth, fifth, seventh, and eighth graders required to pass the 2010 Gateway tests, the vast majority earned passing scores on the first try and were promoted to the next grade level. For those students who did not do as well on the Gateway as would be expected based on their classroom grades, the school system’s Assessment Review Procedure was immediately initiated and acted upon by the Gwinnett County Board of Education.

According to the Assessment Review Procedure, any student whose report cards in 2009-10 included no grade below a “C” in Gateway subjects qualified for an automatic review if he or she failed any part of the Gateway test. Each review is conducted by an impartial committee of teachers, principals, and central office administrators. Three such committees operate simultaneously in order to complete the reviews before the June 17, 2010, Monthly Business Meeting.

In addition to reviewing each student’s preliminary Gateway scores, the committee examines each student’s classroom performance as documented by the child’s teacher. Members also study each student’s standardized test data, other assessment information, and evidence provided by the parents of extenuating circumstances (illness, death in the family, etc.) that could have caused the student to perform poorly on the test. The committee then recommends one of the following three actions for each student:
    • waive passage of this year’s Gateway and promote to the next grade;
    • waive passage of this year’s Gateway, promote to the next grade, but recommend summer school;
    • do not waive passage of this year’s Gateway, recommend summer school, and require Gateway retake and passage for promotion.
The committee’s recommendations and the Board’s final decisions will be reviewed in this report.

Financial impact: N/A

Recommendation: N/A

Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
