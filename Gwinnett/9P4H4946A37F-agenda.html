4.01 Parkview HS Marine Corps Junior Reserve Officers' Training Corps - Naval Honor School Award

 

Honorees: Parkview HS Marine Corps Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps

 

Recognition:    Naval Honor School Award

 

Awarded by:  Commanding General, Marine Corps Training and Education Command, Quantico, VA

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Executive Director, Academies and  Career & Technical Education

 

Background:

Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (JROTC) units are selected by the Area Commander for attaining the highest overall rating during the school year. Subsequently, these units are designated Naval Honor School by the Commanding General Training and Education Command, MCCDC Quantico, VA. This designation is given to 20% of the JROTC units in the nation in recognition of a unit’s exemplary performance during the area Commander’s inspection, and the unit shows exemplary performance in drill competitions, community service, and marksmanship competitions. The JROTC unit must also demonstrate exemplary performance in unit administration, training, supply, facilities, armory management, cadet citizenship, school and community involvement, scholastic achievement, and extra curricular events according to Training and Education Command guidelines. For the seventh consecutive year, Parkview HS has been awarded the Naval Honor School Award.

 

Accompanying Honorees:  

David T. Smith, principal, Parkview HS,

Major Ronald Tootle, USMC retired, Gunnery Sergeant and Marine Instructor Dave Erwin, USMC retired,

7.07 Inter-Governmental Agreement (IGA) with Gwinnett County to Initiate Stop Arm Camera Program for Gwinnett County Public Schools

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities & Operations

 

Presented by:  Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer

 

Background: At its July 18, 2013 meeting, the Board approved the selection of Redflex to install cameras on approximately 300 Gwinnett school buses to catch motorists putting children at risk by illegal passing of the bus while its stop arm is out.

 

Based on joint discussions between and among Gwinnett County, Redflex and GCPS, responsibilities and procedural details have now been agreed upon and the IGA is ready for signing.

 

The Gwinnett County Board of Commissioners approved the IGA at their meeting on October 7, 2014.

 

Upon approval, the program will commence during the 2014-2015 school year and be in effect for 5 years, subject to annual review and renewal.

 

Financial Impact:  None.  Program is funded solely from citation proceeds.

 

Recommendation: That the Board of Education adopt a motion to approve the IGA between GCPS and the County.

11.03 eCLASS Program Services Continuation

 

Prepared by:  Scott Futrell, Chief Information and Technology Officer, and Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  In February 2013, IBM was selected as GCPS' lead systems integrator and program manager for Gwinnett County Public Schools' eCLASS initiative. As of August 2014, components implemented under eCLASS include tools for curriculum and instruction, teacher collaboration, professional development, data analytics, and teacher and leader evaluation. Moving forward with eCLASS, IBM will place increased emphasis on the acceleration of systems delivery, continued implementation of existing components, and the effective rollout of a new student information system (SIS) component. Additional focus also will be put into the acquisition and integration of quality digital content and the development of advanced data analytics and eCLASS performance metrics.

 

As the eCLASS systems integrator and program manager, IBM was tasked with evaluating the student information system needs of the district and recommending an appropriate product to replace the current, non-sustainable SASI system. An RFP was issued to identify a replacement SIS. Staff is recommending continuation of the systems integrator's eCLASS services, including implementation of the new student information system, Edupoint.

 

Financial Impact:  PCR 1317D - Not to exceed $10,209,176 (Funding included in the current E-SPLOST budget)

                              PCR 1317E - Not to exceed $8,251,610 (Funding included in the current E-SPLOST budget)

 

Recommendation:  That the Board approve the two Project Change Requests (1317D and 1317E) as submitted.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
