2.01 Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

 

Board members receive supporting documentation for items on the Monthly Business Meeting agenda seven days prior to the meeting.  Staff is available during this time to answer questions or provide additional information, if needed.

4.01 AATG/Pedagogical Exchange Service Study Trip Award

 

Honoree:  Sergio Haab, Parkview High School

 

Recognition: AATG/Pedagogical Exchange Service Study Trip Award

 

Awarded by:   American Association of Teachers of German (AATG)

 

Introduced by:   Dr. Jon Valentine, Director of Foreign Language

 

Background:  For over 50 years, the Federal Republic of Germany, through the German Foreign Office (Auswärtiges Amt) and its Pedagogical Exchange Service (Pädagogischer Austauschdienst), has provided the AATG/PAD Study Trip Awards, a 3-4 week trip to Germany in the summer following the Exam. Students attend classes at an academic high school, stay in homestays, and participate in various excursions to places of cultural significance.

Students must score in the 90th percentile or higher on the National German Exam and have a minimum of a B average to be eligible to receive the award. 

 

Additional Staff Attending with Honoree(s)

Sanja Knezovic, Teacher, Parkview High School

11.01 Maxwell HS - Firefighter Training Tower

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations      

 

Presented by: Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer      

 

Background:  Gwinnett County and Gwinnett County School District have been in discussion on a joint venture for providing a firefighter training tower on the Maxwell High School campus, intended for joint usage by Gwinnett County Fire Services and the Fire and Emergency Services program of Maxwell High School.  Gwinnett County and Gwinnett County School District propose to enter into an Intergovernmental Agreement for the design, construction, use and maintenance of this firefighter training tower.

 

Financial impact:  The total project cost for the design, purchase and construction of the firefighter training tower is $600,000.00. Gwinnett County will be responsible for all costs up to $530,000.00, and Gwinnett County School District will be responsible for any remaining costs over that amount up to the total project cost.  The school district’s portion of the costs will be funded from the 2012-2017 Building Program budget.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to approve the Intergovernmental Agreement for the firefighter training tower at Maxwell High School.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
