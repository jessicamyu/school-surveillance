Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting.

The monthly Work Session on October 8, 2009, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, GA.

October 8, 2009, Monthly Work Session Topics:

The following topics were on the agenda of the Board's afternoon Work Session, which began at 3:00 p.m.:


Vision for Public Education in Georgia Project - A report on the Vision for Public Education in Georgia Project will be given.

Response to Intervention (RTI) Update - An update on the Response to Intervention will be given.

Curriculum Update: Special Education - An update on GCPS' special education program will be presented.

Staff Development Policy Update - An update will be given regarding GCPS' Staff Development Policy.

District Key Performance Indicators (KPI) Update - An update on the progress of the accountability system that establishes desired outcomes identified in KPIs.

Conversion Charter School Application: Maxwell HS of Technology - An update on the conversion charter school application will be presented.

Redistricting Process Update - An update on the redistricting process for the 2010-2011 school year.




Staffing Update - Monthly report on personnel matters (applications, new hires, vacancies).

Employee Benefits/Open Enrollment - A review of the changes in the upcoming Benefits/Open Enrollment for employees.

Legislative Priorities - Board members will be briefed on the district's 2010 Legislative Priorities.

Mr. Wilbanks will report to the Board on "A Vision for Public Education in Georgia." This joint project of the Georgia School Boards Association and the Georgia School Superintendents Association was established to engage stakeholders across the State in the important work of creating a vision for the future of public education in Georgia that will focus on the intellectual development of all students, prepare them to be contributing members of a democratic society, embrace world-class standards, promote 21st century skills, gain the support of State and local policy makers, and be supported by the citizens of the State.
