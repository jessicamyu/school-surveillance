Background:  The Horizon Educational Drone Competition (HEDC) is a STEM program in which students learn the history and current uses of drones in society. Students also build, modify, and improve their own battery-powered drones to compete in three separate challenges: drone racing, package delivery, and First Person View (FPV) Surveillance Mission. A team of students from Central Gwinnett High School traveled to Atlanta on March 17 to compete at the National Science Teachers’ Association Annual Conference. During the competition, the students had to design and create a device that would attach to the drone, pick up an item, and deliver it to a check point.  Secondly, they had to race their drone through an obstacle course. Finally, teams had to fly a “recon mission” over several buildings to identify the letters or symbols located on the roof of the buildings. The team from Central Gwinnett placed first in the “recon mission,” second in the package delivery competition, and were awarded an overall second place finish.

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
