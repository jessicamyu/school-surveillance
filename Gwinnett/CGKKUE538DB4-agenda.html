4.03 1st Place at the 2022 Anne Smedinghoff Academic WorldQuest HS Global Affairs Challenge

 

Honoree(s): Four Gwinnett School of Mathematics, Science, and Technology students

 

Recognition: 1st Place at the 2022 Anne Smedinghoff Academic WorldQuest HS Global Affairs Challenge

 

Awarded by: Tennessee World Affairs Council (TNWAC)

 

Introduced by: Shirelle Tubbs, Director of Middle School and High School Social Studies

 

Background: The Anne Smedinghoff Academic WorldQuest is the flagship youth education program of the Tennessee World Affairs Council and the national network of World Affairs Councils of America (WACA). High school students from across Tennessee are invited to play. TNWAC also welcomes teams from other states who do not have a nearby Academic WorldQuest program available.

 

The TNWAC Academic WorldQuest program is named in honor of Anne Smedinghoff, a United States Foreign Service Officer posted at the U.S. Embassy in Kabul, Afghanistan. She was killed in the line of duty in 2013 by a car bomb while an American team was delivering books to an Afghan school.

 

WorldQuest is a team game, held at Belmont University, and tests high school students’ knowledge of international affairs. In the game, four-person teams compete by answering multiple-choice questions divided into ten engaging thematic categories.

 

Accompanying Honorees:

Logan Malm, Principal, Gwinnett School of Mathematics, Science, and Technology

6.05 Tribunal Training Course Outline

 

Prepared by: Dr. Al Taylor, Chief of Schools

 

Presented by: Dr. Calvin J. Watts, Superintendent

 

Background: Due to the passage of Senate Bill 367, the Georgia Department of Education promulgated Rule 160-4-8-.15 which became effective August 3, 2016. This Rule requires the Local Education Agency to do the following:

  • Annually approve a Training Course Outline to be followed by the Tribunal Training provider(s).
  • This Outline requires a minimum of five (5) hours of instruction for all qualified Student Discipline Hearing Officers and/or Panel Members in the areas of 1) Student disciplinary provisions in Title 20, 2) Due process requirements under federal and state law, 3) Applicable rules of evidence, 4) Federal and state judicial decisions, and 5) Applicable standards and the role of the Hearing Officer as an independent, neutral arbitrator.

 

Financial impact: None

 

Recommendation: That the Board of Education approve the attached Tribunal Training Course Outline.

12.01 Meeting Adjourned OR Executive Session is Resumed

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session). If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and/or student discipline matters; and cybersecurity plans.
