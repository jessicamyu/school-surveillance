4.04  Discovery High School Army JROTC and Grayson High School Army JROTC -Army Honor Unit with Distinction

 

Honorees: Discovery High School Army JROTC and Grayson High School Army JROTC

 

Recognition: Army Honor Unit with Distinction

 

Awarded by: United States Army Cadet Command

 

Introduced by: Tim Hemans, Executive Director of Academies and Career and Technical Education

 

Background: Army JROTC programs may earn the Honor Unit with Distinction award as the result of their regularly scheduled JROTC Program for Accreditation evaluation. The inspection is conducted by U.S. Army Cadet Command officials who are accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools. The JROTC cadets are evaluated in the following areas: cadet leadership presentations, service learning projects, drill and ceremonies, color guard, and cadet portfolios. JROTC instructors are also inspected for pedagogy, professional development, and community involvement. A score of at least 95% is required to earn the Honor Unit with Distinction and is the highest possible unit achievement.

4.05  Parkview High School Marine Corps JROTC - Naval Honor School

 

Honorees: Parkview High School Marine Corps JROTC

 

Recognition: Naval Honor School

 

Awarded by: Commanding General, Marine Corps Training and Education Command Quantico

 

Introduced by: Tim Hemans, Executive Director of Academies and Career and Technical Education

 

Background: Junior Reserve Officers’ Training Corps (JROTC) units are selected by the Area Commander for attaining the highest overall rating during the school year. Subsequently, these units are designated Naval Honor School by the Commanding General Training and Education Command Quantico. This designation is given to 20% of the JROTC units in the nation in recognition of a unit’s exemplary performance during the area Commander’s inspection. The unit shows exemplary performance in drill competitions, community service, and marksmanship competitions. They must also demonstrate exemplary performance in unit administration, training, supply, facilities, armory management, cadet citizenship, school and community involvement, scholastic achievement, and extra-curricular events according to Training and Education Command guidelines. For the eleventh consecutive year, Parkview High School Marine JROTC has been awarded Naval Honor School.

7.06  Seckinger HS: Construction Manager-At-Risk for Qualifications/Request for Proposal (RFQ/RFP) Evaluation and Selection Approval

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations

 

Presented by: Walt Martin, Chief Operations Officer

 

Background:  On Monday, November 4, 2019, at 3:00 pm, proposals were received from five Construction Management at Risk firms for Construction Management at Risk Services for the upcoming new Seckinger High School project. These proposals were solicited in a Request for Qualifications/Request for Proposal (RFQ/RFP) process where each firm submitted their qualifications demonstrating their ability to successfully complete a project like this on time and within budget. Their proposals included their project team, previous project experience on similar projects, and their fee on the total construction cost of the project. 

 

All five submitting firms were invited to present their credentials, project team and approach to the selection committee. After these presentations the committee scored all firms and is ready to recommend Carroll Daniel Construction of Gainesville, Georgia, as the most qualified Construction Manager at Risk to the Board for approval.

 

Financial impact:  The recommended Construction Manager at Risk will work with the design and construction team to complete this project for a fixed fee on a Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP). The total cost will be within the current budget of the 2019 General Obligation Bond program.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to award the contract to the most qualified firm as recommended.

14.01  Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
