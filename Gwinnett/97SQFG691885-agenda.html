The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

Grayson High School Electrathon Team                                1st Place in Georgia

Mill Creek High School Electrathon Team                              2nd Place in Georgia

 

Recognition:  Electrathon Competition

 

Awarded by:   Georgia Electrathon Association

 

Introduced by:   Jody Reeves, Director of Technical Education, Computer Science, and Work-Based Learning

 

Background:

The mission of the Georgia Electrathon Association is to promote the education of alternative energy within the community and school system. The Georgia Electrathon Association is an organized group of teachers, students and promoters that sponsors and organizes battery powered technology competition events. On May 11, 2013, several GCPS students participated in the 3rd Annual Run for the Peaches Electrathon series competition. The objective of the Electrathon series is to see how far a student team designed car can travel in 60 minutes on one Kilowatt Hour of energy. Students have to design the car based on weight and aerodynamic chassis. During the competition, students are using teamwork and communication skills to make changes to the vehicle speed, based on different variables of the competition.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
