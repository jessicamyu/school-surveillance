Subject
5.05 North Gwinnett High School Board Resolution
Meeting
Jan 19, 2012 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
5. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations

 

Presented by:  L. J. Steele, Chief Operations Officer

                       

Background:  Georgia Department of Education requires a Board Resolution to approve the demolition of Buildings 5010, 5050, and 5051 which are inadequate for the needs of the school today.  A new classroom addition will be built to replace these buildings.

 

Due to the age of the buildings planned for demolition (Buildings 5010, 1958; Building 5050, 1962; Building 5051, 1966), it would not be practical to efficiently renovate/expand these to meet the needs of the school today.  The classrooms in these buildings are undersized to meet the current Gwinnett County Public Schools Educational Specifications. The buildings' HVAC does not meet current standards, resulting in energy inefficiencies, and accessibility improvements are needed.

 

It is not feasible to add on to the current one-story buildings (vertically) due to the structural constraints of the existing old facilities. The fact that there is no room on site to expand prevents an expansion horizontally.  Therefore, in order to add classrooms, the only option is to build a multi-story addition over the footprint of the proposed demolition area (Buildings 5010, 5050, and 5051).

        

Financial Impact:  None

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to approve the Board Resolution.

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
