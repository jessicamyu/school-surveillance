2.01 Adoption of Agenda

 

The agenda for the Monthly Business Meeting is tentative and subject to change until it is adopted by the Board at this point in the meeting.  Before the agenda is adopted, the Superintendent may recommend to the Board that items be added, deleted, or revised.  The Board will vote on the Superintendent's recommendation(s) in adopting the evening's official agenda.

11.04 Sale of Buice School Facility (During the Business Meeting the agenda was amended to move this item up to be renumbered 3.02)

 

Subject:  Sale of Buice School Facility

 

Prepared By:  Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer

 

Presented By:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  The Buice School facility in Sugar Hill houses a special program for a small number of pre-kindergarten children. The long-term use of this school facility is not in the future plans for Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

In August 2015, the pre-kindergarten students at Buice School will be assigned to attend their respective home schools.  The vacating of Buice School designates this facility as surplus property for the school district and makes it available for sale to the City of Sugar Hill.

 

Financial Impact:  The Buice School facility and property will be sold to the City of Sugar Hill for its negotiated value as determined by multiple independent appraisals.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education approve and execute the contract to sell the identified property to the City of Sugar Hill and approve a short-term lease for GCPS to occupy the property until the facility is vacated.

7.06 Norcross Cluster ES New Facility

 

Prepared by:  Division of Facilities and Operations      

 

Presented by: Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer      

 

Background:   On Monday, March 2, 2015 at 3:00 pm, proposals were received from three Construction Management at Risk firms for Construction Management at Risk Services for the upcoming New Norcross Cluster Elementary School project. These proposals were solicited in a Request for Qualifications (RFQ) process where each firm submitted its qualifications, demonstrating its ability to successfully complete a project like this on time and within budget.  Their proposals included their project team, previous project experience on similar projects, and their fee on the total construction cost of the project. 

 

All three submitting firms were invited to present their credentials, project team and approach to the selection committee.  After these presentations the committee scored all firms and is ready to recommend Carroll Daniel Construction Company of Gainesville, Georgia, as the most qualified Construction Manager at Risk to the Board for approval.

 

Financial Impact:   The recommended Construction Manager at Risk will work with the design and construction team to complete this project for a fee on a Guaranteed Maximum Price (GMP).  The total cost will be within the current budget of the 2012 Building Program.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a motion to award the contract to the most qualified firm as recommended.

11.03 Sale of Hooper Renwick School Facility

 

Subject:  Sale of Hooper Renwick School Facility

 

Prepared By:   Daniel W. Jardine, Chief Operations Officer

 

Presented By:  J. Alvin Wilbanks, CEO/Superintendent

 

Background:  The Hooper Renwick School facility houses a program for special needs students operated by North Metro. The facility and its site will become surplus property for the school district when the Hooper Renwick program moves to its new location at Northbrook Center in July 2015. The City of Lawrenceville desires to purchase the property, which no longer will be needed for GCPS purposes.

 

The Hooper Renwick School property is located at 56 Neal Boulevard in Lawrenceville and consists of parcels of land on both sides of Green Street. These parcels total approximately 11.39 acres with 72,300 square feet of buildings. In addition to the school, the property has provided a parking lot for a large number of GCPS school buses. That parking lot could be relocated to an available city-owned parcel of approximately 9.7 acres on Stone Mountain Street near Gwinnett Drive in Lawrenceville.

 

Financial Impact:  The Hooper Renwick School property will be sold to the City of Lawrenceville for its negotiated value. Gwinnett County Public Schools will purchase the city’s property located on Stone Mountain Street near Gwinnett Drive for its negotiated value. The value of the properties was determined by multiple independent appraisals.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education approve and execute the contracts to sell the identified property to the City of Lawrenceville, purchase the identified property from the City of Lawrenceville, and approve a short-term lease for GCPS to occupy the Hooper Renwick School facility until the school is vacated.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
