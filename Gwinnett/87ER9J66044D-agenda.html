Honorees: Shiloh HS Air Force Junior ROTC Unit GA-871

Awarded by: U.S. Department of the Air Force/Air University (AETC)

Introduced by: Jody Reeves, Director of Technical Education, Computer Science, and Work-Based Learning

Background: The Shiloh HS Air Force Junior ROTC (AFJROTC) unit was selected to receive the Air Force Junior ROTC Distinguished Unit Award for FY2009 from the U.S. Department of the Air Force. Shiloh High is one of 393 units nationwide to receive this award. The objectives of the AFJROTC program are to educate and train high school cadets in citizenship and life skills, promote community service, instill responsibility, character, and self-discipline through character education, and provide instruction in air and space fundamentals.

Honorees:

Katie Fosse, First Place Winner, Senior Individual Exhibits, Collins Hill HS

Brittany Partridge, Aisha Ellis, Kevin Lee, Trent Keatley, and O-Mayra Rimpel, First Place Winners, Senior Group Exhibits, Collins Hill HS
Aniqa Alam, Divya Desai, Jumana Bambot, and Sonali Shah, Second Place Winners, Senior Group Exhibits, Collins Hill HS

Christopher Willis, Christian King, Alexandria Smith, and Kyungmin Kim, First Place Winners, Senior Websites, Collins Hill HS

Bailee Kitchens and Julie Tremblay, First Place Winners, Senior Group Performance, Norcross HS

Recognition: National History Day State Winners

Awarded by: Georgia Humanities Council

Introduced by: Debbie Daniell, Director of Social Studies

Background: National History Day in Georgia is a history education program for students in grades 6-12, sponsored by the Georgia Humanities Council. In conjunction with a national theme, students research topics and present their findings in papers, performances, exhibits, or documentaries. Students can work individually, or in all categories except historical paper. They can collaborate in teams of 2-5 students. National History Day gets students out of the textbook, and into primary sources such as buildings, artifacts, records, interviews, or archaeological sites. Students can research any topic they would like, but they must also remember to look at available primary sources on their topic. Those who place first or second at state are eligible to participate at nationals. The 2009 national contest took place June 14-18th in Maryland.


Accompanying the honorees: Glenn McFall, Principal; Terrae Fogarty and Teresa Nichols, History teachers, Collins Hill HS

Subject
7.05 SACS CASI AdvancED Report
Meeting
Sep 10, 2009 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORTS
Type
Information

Prepared by: Dale Robbins, Associate Superintendent for Teaching & Learning

Presented by: Dale Robbins

Background: In February, 2007, a Quality Assurance Review Team visited GCPS as part of the SACS CASI (AdvancED) accreditation process to evaluate adherence to quality standards and assess the effectiveness of the school system's improvement efforts. GCPS is required to submit a progress report two years after the team visit to describe district actions to address the team's recommendations. The recommendations were to: 1) continue to close the achievement gap at all levels; 2) recruit and retain high-quality staff with a particular emphasis on increasing the percentage who reflect the diversity of students served; and 3) continue to study the long-range impact of increased student growth upon the system with a particular emphasis on system organization, systemwide services, and system. funding. This update is a summary of the information included in the progress report submitted to AdvancED in May, 2009.

Financial impact: None

Recommendation: For information only

11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
