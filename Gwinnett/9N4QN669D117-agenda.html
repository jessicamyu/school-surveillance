Thursday, September 18, 2014
Monthly Work Session 3 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on September 18, 2014, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

September 18, 2014 Monthly Work Session Topics

 

JA BizTown/Finance Park Updates - Superintendent Wilbanks and Jack Harris, President of Junior Achievement (JA) of Georgia, reviewed with the Board the status of the development of Gwinnett's JA program at the new high school in Lawrenceville that will open August of 2015.

 

Aspiring Principal Program Cohort 8 Graduation - The 8th cohort of the Aspiring Principal Program, implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, presented 21 graduates to the Board for recognition of their completion of the year-long course of study.

 

Naming New Schools - Dr. Flynt presented name recommendations for new schools opening in August 2015.

 

Redistricting Update - Staff provided a review of the plans for redistricting that will take effect in August 2015.

 

eCLASS C & I Full Implementation Update - Staff updated the Board on the full implementation of eCLASS. All schools are now participating in this initiative.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

 

2015 Legislative Issues - Staff updated the Board on the timeline for the presentation of the 2015 Legislative Program.

 

Update on the IE2 Partnership Contract Renewal - Staff reviewed the proposed IE2 Partnership Contract recommended for Board approval.

In August 2015, a new high school will open in Lawrenceville to relieve the Berkmar and Central Gwinnett clusters. The facility will host a traditional high school program as well as a number of innovative instructional programs, including Junior Achievement's BizTown and Finance Park, which provide financial literacy instruction tailored to middle school students. Jack Harris, president of Junior Achievement (JA) of Georgia, will review with the Board the status of the development of Gwinnett's JA program at the new high school.

The Aspiring Principal Program (APP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares Assistant Principals to move into Principal positions.  The eighth cohort of 21 graduates is presented to the Board of Education for recognition of completion of a year-long course of study.  To date, 8 members of this cohort have been appointed as principal.  A total of 104 participants have been appointed to principal positions from the first eight cohorts of the Aspiring Principal Program.
