Honorees: Parkview HS Marine Corps JROTC Unit

Recognition: Naval Honors School

Awarded by: Commanding General, Marine Corps Training and Education Command, Quantico, VA

Introduced by: Jody Reeves, Director of Technical Education, Computer Science, and Work-Based Learning

Background: JROTC units are selected by the area Commander for attaining the highest overall rating during the school year. Subsequently, these units are designated a Naval Honors School by the Commanding General Training and Education Command, MCCDC Quantico, VA. This designation is given to 20% of the JROTC units in the nation in recognition of a unit’s exemplary performance during the area Commander’s inspection, and the unit shows exemplary performance in drill competitions, community service, and marksmanship competitions. The JROTC unit must also demonstrate exemplary performance in unit administration, training, supply, facilities, armory management, cadet citizenship, school and community involvement, scholastic achievement, and extra curricular events according to Training and Education Command guidelines.



Accompanying the honorees: David Smith, principal, Parkview HS; Captain Modesto Silverio, USMC retired; Gunnery Sergeant and Marine Instructor Dave Erwin, USMC retired;

4.03 Dreana Trent, GCPS Special Needs Bus Driver
Meeting
Oct 8, 2009 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION
Type
Information

Honoree: Dreana Trent, Special Needs Bus Driver, Duluth Transportation Zone

Recognition: American Red Cross Certificate of Merit, and Georgia Association for Pupil Transportation Valor Award

Awarded by: American Red Cross, and Georgia Association for Pupil Transportation

Introduced by: Don Moore, Assistant Director of Transportation

Background: Ms. Trent was recognized recently by two organizations, the American Red Cross in September, and the Georgia Association for Pupil Transportation in June, with their highest honors for saving the life of a 9-year-old passenger on December 15, 2008.

Ms. Trent was driving students home when she noticed a boy was choking. She pulled the bus to the side of the road, asked her backup-trained student to call dispatch over the radio while she rushed to help. When abdominal thrusts did not dislodge the object and the passenger stopped breathing, she laid the boy on the floor of the bus and started chest compressions until the obstruction dislodged and breathing was restored.

Ms. Trent, who had renewed her Red Cross CPR certification as part of her GCPS Transportation Department annual training just seven days before the incident, was also honored earlier today for her actions by the Georgia Board of Education. Ms. Trent has been a GCPS employee since 2005.

11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
