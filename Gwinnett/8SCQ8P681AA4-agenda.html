Subject
5.03 Gwinnett Retirement System Plan Administrative Committee - Plan Document Revisions
Meeting
Apr 19, 2012 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
5. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

 

Prepared by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by:  Rick Cost 

 

Background:  The provisions and operational procedures of the Gwinnett County Board of Education’s Retirement System (GRS) are described in the GRS Plan Document.  Since 1983, the Board has amended and restated the Plan Document from time to time to reflect changes in legislation and also to institute desired changes to benefits.  The Plan’s actuary recently concluded an experience study of the Plan to validate the assumptions used to determine contributions necessary to maintain a funding level adequate to fund all benefits as they become due.  As a result of the study, the actuary has recommend changes to assumptions and Plan provisions affecting vesting requirements for active employee participants and optional reductions in future retirement benefits to reflect the actual cost of providing survivors’ benefits. Based on the Plan actuary’s recommendations, the GRS Plan Administrative Committee voted to approve a recommendation to the Board to adopt a revised and restated Plan Document.

 

Financial Impact:   Based on the Plan's actuary projections, a "Normal" employer contribution rate of 4.26% would be required to fully fund the Plan per the amended GRS Plan Document.  The recommended employer contribution rate for FY2013 is less than the "Normal" rate due to the Plan's current funding status.

Subject
5.04 Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Recommendations for Retirement and Disability Funds Contribution Rates for FY2013
Meeting
Apr 19, 2012 - Monthly Business Meeting - 7 p.m.
Category
5. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

 

Prepared by:  Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by:  Rick Cost

 

Background:   In accordance with Article 9.02 of the Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan Document, each year the Plan's actuary calculates the amount of contributions which are sufficient to fund the benefits provided by the Retirement and Disability Funds of the Plan using accepted actuarial methods.  The Board may rely upon the estimates made by the actuary of the amount of contributions needed to carry out the Plan.  The actuary delivers the calculated rates to the GRS Plan Administrative Committee which in turn transmits the results of the calculations to the Board together with recommendations as to contributions.

 

In accordance with Article 9.03 of the GRS Plan Document, the Board may, on the recommendation of the actuary, cause assets to be transferred from one Fund to the other, except as prohibited by the Internal Revenue Code or other applicable law.

 

The Committee received the actuary's recommendations to fund the Plan for the following fiscal year at its February 2012 meeting and unanimously voted to forward the recommendations shown below to the Board for approval.  These recommendations represent contributions to be made to the Retirement and Disability Funds of the Plan for the period July 1, 2012 through June 30, 2013.

 

Financial Impact:  The recommended employer contribution rate represents a decrease of 2.52% over the current year rate.  

 

Recommendation:  That the Board approve the actuary's recommendations for funding the Retirement and Disability Funds of GRS for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2013 as follows:

 

  • Employer contributions:   2.57% of compensation
  • Employee contributions:  1.00% of compensation
  • Transfer $40 million from the Disability Fund to the Retirement Fund

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
