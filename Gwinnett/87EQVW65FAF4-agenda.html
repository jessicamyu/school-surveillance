Honorees: North Gwinnett HS Air Force JROTC Unit

Recognition: 2008-2009 Air Force JROTC Distinguished Unit Award with Merit

Awarded by: Department of the Air Force

Introduced by: Jody Reeves, Director of Technical Education, Computer Science, and Work-Based Learning

Background: The North Gwinnett HS Air Force JROTC Unit was selected for a 2008-2009 Air Force JROTC Distinguished Unit Award with Merit from the Department of the Air Force. This award honors Air Force JROTC units that perform above and beyond normal expectations, and have distinguished themselves through outstanding service to their school and community, while meeting the AFJROTC mission of producing better citizens for America. North Gwinnett HS is one of 96 units nationwide to receive this award. The objectives of the Air Force JROTC program are to educate and train high school cadets in citizenship and life skills, promote community service, instill responsibility, character, and self-discipline through character education, and provide instruction in air and space fundamentals.


Accompanying the honorees: Ed Shaddix, Principal, North Gwinnett HS; Major Bruce Bonds, Senior Aerospace Science Instructor; Chief Master Sergeant Michael Shannon, Aerospace Science Instructor; and North Gwinnett HS Air Force JROTC Officers.

Meeting
Nov 12, 2009 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
10. NEW BUSINESS
Type
Action

Prepared by: Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

Presented by: Rick Cost

Background: Effective 2009, the IRS issued new regulations regarding plan sponsors (employers) under section 403(b) of the Internal Revenue Code. These regulations greatly increased the plan sponsor’s compliance requirements and plan administration responsibilities. This being the case, a comprehensive RFP process for plan investment provider(s) and plan administrator was initiated in June, 2009. The process was facilitated by Iron Capital Advisors and the recommendation below reflects the final evaluation and result of that process.

Financial impact: Estimated annual savings of approximately $3.75 million to Plan participants.

Recommendation: That the Board of Education authorize VALIC and Lincoln Financial Group as the Plan investment providers effective July 1, 2010, and also designate VALIC as the sole recordkeeper and plan administrator.

11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
