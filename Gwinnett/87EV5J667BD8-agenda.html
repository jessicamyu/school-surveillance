Subject
4.01 Maxwell HS of Technology Auto Collision Repair Program, Industry Certification by The National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation and The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence
Meeting
Oct 11, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
4. BOARD RECOGNITION
Type
Information

Honoree: Maxwell HS of Technology Auto Collision Repair Program

Recognition: Industry Certification

Awarded by: The National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation and the National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence

Introduced by: Jody Reeves, Director of Technical Education, Computer Science, and Apprenticeships

Background: The Auto Collision Repair Program at Maxwell HS has received industry certification by The National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation (NATEF) and The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE). To achieve this recognition, the school's automotive training program underwent rigorous evaluation by NATEF. Nationally accepted standards of excellence in areas such as instruction, facilities, and equipment were used as benchmarks.

The Auto Collision Repair Program facilitates cooperation between local education and industry leaders and provides added assurance that Maxwell HS graduates will have received training for entry-level technician jobs. Upon completion of the evaluation, NATEF recommended that Maxwell HS be certified by ASE. ASE is a national nonprofit organization which certifies automotive training programs as well as tests and certifies repair technicians.

Don Henderson and Butch Luther are the Auto Service Repair instructors for Maxwell HS.

11.01 Meeting Adjourned
Meeting
Oct 11, 2007 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
