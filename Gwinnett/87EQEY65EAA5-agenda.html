Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting.

The monthly Work Session on March 18, 2010, will begin at 2:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, GA.

March 18, 2010, Monthly Work Session Topics:

The following topics were on the agenda of the Board's afternoon Work Session, which began at 2:00 p.m.:

Board Business Oversight Workshop - Business and Finance - Staff will provide an overview of key financial issues and topics relevant to the sound fiscal operations of the school system.

Board Members' Meeting with Teachers' Advisory Council (TAC) - Following their meetings with TAC representatives from schools in their districts, Board members and TAC representatives will gather in the Board Room where Board members will report-out on conversations just held with those representatives.



Staff Reports

Instructional Materials Adoption - Staff will provide an update on the Instructional Materials Adoption process and recommendations.

Staffing Update - Monthly report on personnel matters (applications, new hires, vacancies).

Legislative Update - Staff will review the status of education-related legislation in the current session of the Georgia General Assembly.

Maxwell HS Charter Amendment - Staff will provide an update on the Maxwell HS Charter Amendment.

In conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing management of the school system’s business and operational systems, the Board will hold a workshop on Finance as part of its March 18, 2010 Monthly Work Session. CEO/Superintendent J. Alvin Wilbanks and Chief Financial Officer Rick A. Cost will provide an overview of key financial issues and topics relevant to the sound fiscal operations of the school system. The overview will be focused on key financial information included in the June 30, 2009 Comprehensive Annual Financial Report (CAFR), the Year 2 SPLOST III Audit and Review Report and the Annual Investment Oversight Committee Report.

Each year the Board of Education members meet with members of the Teachers' Advisory Council (TAC) from the schools in their Board districts. The Board and TAC representatives will meet, by district, from 3:00 p.m. until 3:45 p.m. At 3:50 p.m. all TAC members and the Board will gather in the Board Room for a report-out by each Board member on the conversations with the TAC representatives.
