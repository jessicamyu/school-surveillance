Background: Brookwood HS has been named a Georgia School of Excellence. Brookwood HS is among just 26 schools in the state, one of only four high schools, to earn the prestigious honor of being named a Georgia School of Excellence.

Only 26 Schools of Excellence are chosen each year and they are honored in two categories. Qualifying schools are chosen from each Congressional District in the following categories:
  • Top 10 Percent: Schools that are in the top 10 percent of schools in Georgia as measured by assessments in reading and mathematics
  • Greatest Gains: Schools that demonstrated greatest continuous gains in student achievement for the past three years as measured by assessments in reading and mathematics.

6.03 403(b) Plan Document
Meeting
Dec 11, 2008 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

Prepared by: Tom Daymude, Director, Gwinnett Retirement System

Presented by: Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

Background: The Board has traditionally sponsored a tax-deferred retirement savings plan for employees as authorized under Section 403(b) of the Internal Revenue Code. Historically, a written plan document was not a requirement for school systems that sponsored this type of plan. Effective with final 403(b) regulations released by the Internal Revenue Service and Department of the Treasury in 2007, a written plan document is required effective January 1, 2009. The purpose of the 403(b) plan document is to document basic provisions of the Board’s current 403(b) plan in accordance with the final IRS regulations.

Financial impact: None.

Recommendation: That the Board approve the new 403(b) Plan Document as written.

6.04 Gwinnett Retirement System (GRS) Plan Document
Meeting
Dec 11, 2008 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

Prepared by: Tom Daymude, Director, Gwinnett Retirement System

Presented by: Rick A. Cost, Chief Financial Officer

Background: The GRS Plan Document was originally adopted by the Board and became effective January 1, 1983. It has been periodically amended and restated throughout its history in order to maintain retirement and long term disability benefits for participants in accordance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements. The purpose of the current amendment and restatement is to reflect recent changes in Federal legislation, including the Pension Protection Act of 2006, the final Section 401(a)(9) Regulations (regarding required minimum distributions) released in June 2004, the final Section 415 Regulations (regarding limitations on maximum annual benefits payable) released in April 2007, to remove the requirement to honor domestic relations orders and to make additional technical changes.

Financial impact: None.

Recommendation: That the Board approve the revised GRS Plan Document as written.

11. ADJOURNMENT
Type

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Open Session. If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
