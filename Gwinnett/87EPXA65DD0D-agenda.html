7.06 Gwinnett Online Campus - Local Education Agency Start-Up Charter School Application
Meeting
May 20, 2010 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
7. SUPERINTENDENT’S REPORTS
Type
Action

Prepared by: Tricia Kennedy, Executive Director of Curriculum and Instruction

Presented by: Dr. Steve Flynt, Associate Superintendent for the Division of School Leadership and Operational Support

Background: The Gwinnett County Online Campus has been a successful online supplemental program for GCPS students since the year 2000 and currently enrolls more than 4,000 students within a school year. In its current form, Online Campus serves middle and high school students enrolled in GCPS through extended-day and summer-school offerings. High schools students may also take Online Campus courses as a part of their regular class schedule while concurrently enrolled in their home/neighborhood high school. Students and parents have expressed an interest in a full-time online option. The Gwinnett County Online Campus Charter Application proposes a fourth through twelfth grade, diploma-granting full-time charter school as an option for any student within the Gwinnett County Public School attendance zone. Built upon the tradition of excellence in the existing Online Campus program, and the world-class education offered through Gwinnett County Public Schools, the Gwinnett County Online Campus Charter School proposes to provide a flexible alternative for students and parents. District leadership staff reviewed the application for compliance with Georgia law and Georgia Board of Education rule and presented findings to inform the CEO/Superintendent’s recommendation for approval or denial of the application.

Financial impact: The Gwinnett County Online Campus funding would remain at its current level for the extended-day and summer-school programs. Online Campus would also earn state funding for Full-Time Equivalent (FTE) segments, and local funds would be allotted based on FTE earnings at the current rate of funding.

Recommendation: That the Board of Education accept the Superintendent’s recommendation regarding this application.
