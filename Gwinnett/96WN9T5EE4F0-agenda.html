Background:  Trickum Middle School’s Odyssey of the Mind team is competing at the World Finals at Michigan State University from May 22 – May 25, 2013.  In order to qualify, the team had to place either first or second at Georgia’s State Tournament that was held April 4, at Columbus State University.  The problem, “ARTchitecture: The Musical” required that teams research and choose a building that was built between 1000 AD and 1600 AD and build a model to resemble it.   “Throw in three art pieces that disappear that have to be retrieved, include a couple of songs and dances… and perform it all within an 8 minute time limit.”  The team of seven middle school students wrote their play, created their own props and costumes, made puppets and a 10 foot dragon, choreographed a ballet and dragon dance and performed this play in front of a panel of judges.  Students began working on their problem/solution in September.

Honorees:  Duluth HS Engineering & Technology Education

Recognition:   Technical Education - Industry Certification

 

Awarded by:   

  • Engineering and Technology- The Georgia Department of Education
  • Marketing- American Marketing Association and the Georgia Department of Education
  • Culinary Arts- The American Culinary Federation and the Georgia Department of Education

 

Introduced by:  Jody Reeves, Technical Education, Computer Science, and Work-Based Learning Director

 

Background:  When a program becomes industry certified, it receives a "stamp of excellence", which represents the apex of program quality. Only those programs that have successfully undergone rigorous reviews by leaders from business and industry are recognized with this distinction.

 

This formal process strengthens all program components, including:

•  Classrooms and labs which are equipped with state-of-the-art equipment and technology

•  CTE curriculum standards and academic knowledge and skills that are aligned to national standards

•  In-depth, project-based instruction and assessments in all curriculum areas

•  Career and Technical Student Organizations (CTSOs) which offer

    co-curricular competitive events on the local, state and national level and provide leadership development skills for personal and 

    professional growth

•  Business, industry and community involvement in all aspects of the program 

 

The student benefits from participation in industry certified programs are tremendous. The students are ensured participation in programs that have been carefully interfaced with current industry standards, thus helping to increase their qualifications toward successful employment. Schools which pursue this distinction are committed to improving student preparation and ensuring that Georgia’s workforce meets the demands of the 21st century.

11.05 Partnership Gwinnett Public Funding Entity, Inc. Agreement

 

Prepared by:  Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Presented by: CEO/Superintendent J. Alvin Wilbanks

 

Background: Partnership Gwinnett Public Funding Entity, Inc. (Partnership Gwinnett PFE) is being formed as a Georgia non-profit corporation charged with the development of a long-term strategic community and economic development plan for Gwinnett County. In support of this charge, which benefits the tax-payer supported Gwinnett County Public Schools (GCPS), the school district proposes to provide professional services funding for the staffing of two "business development project manager" positions to assist Partnership Gwinnett PFE in its functions and objectives. The funding for the two positions for one year would not exceed a total of $150,000. The terms and conditions associated with the funding of the positions are specified in the "Agreement Between Gwinnett County Public Schools and Partnership Gwinnett Public Funding Entity, Inc." The proposed bylaws for Partnership Gwinnett PFE call for the Gwinnett County Board of Education to hold one seat on the organization's Board of Directors, and that representative will be appointed by the Board of Education.

 

Financial impact:

 

Recommendation: That the Board designate Chief Financial Officer Rick Cost as the Board of Education's representative on the Board of Directors of Partnership Gwinnett Public Funding Entity, Inc.

14.01 Meeting Adjourned

 

 

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:15 p.m. Public Forum (open session) and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open session).  If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
