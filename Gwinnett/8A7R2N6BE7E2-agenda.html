Monthly Work Session - 3:00 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on November 18, 2010, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting.

The monthly Work Session on November 18, 2010, will begin at 3:00 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, GA.


November 18, 2010, Work Session Topics:

 

Human Resources Business Oversight Workshop - An overview of key issues and topics relevant to recruitment, employment, and development of the Gwinnett County Public Schools' workforce.

 

Academic Achievement Update: CRCT and ITBS - An update on CRCT and ITBS testing results.

 

 

Staff Reports:

 

Staffing Update - Monthly report on personnel matters (applications, new hires, vacancies).

 

First Quarter Discipline Report for 2010-2011 School Year - The discipline report for the first quarter of the 2010-2011 school year will be presented.

In conjunction with its responsibility for overseeing the management and operation of the school system, the Board will hold a Workshop on Human Resources Management as part of its November 18, 2010, Monthly Work Session. Alvin Wilbanks and Chief Human Resources Officer, Dr. Frances Davis will provide an overview of key issues and topics relevant to recruitment, employment, and development of the Gwinnett County Public Schools' workforce.
