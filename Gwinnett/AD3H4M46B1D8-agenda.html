Monthly Work Session 2:30 p.m.
Prior to each Monthly Business Meeting, the Gwinnett County Board of Education holds a Work Session for the purpose of discussing a variety of topics related to student achievement and effective operations within the school system. The Work Session typically is held in the afternoon on the day of the School Board's regularly scheduled Monthly Business Meeting. The Monthly Work Session on September 15, 2016, will begin at 2:30 p.m. in the Executive Board Room at Gwinnett County Public Schools J. Alvin Wilbanks Instructional Support Center (ISC), 437 Old Peachtree Road, NW, Suwanee, Georgia.

September 15, 2016 Monthly Work Session Topics

 

Aspiring Leader Program Cohort 7 Graduation Recognition - The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry level assistant principal and administrative intern positions. The 7th Cohort of 43 graduates were presented to the Board for recognition of the six month long course of study which concluded with a summer residency. 

 

2016 Public Education Leadership Project Team Report - Team members who participated in the Public Education Leadership Project (PELP) this summer reviewed the team's work on the "strategic problem of practice" that was the focus of the week-long training program.

 

2015 Innovation and Transformation Award Winners Report - Dacula Cluster and Lanier Cluster - In May 2015, the "Innovation and Transformation Award" was instituted in the school district. The winners of the inaugural Innovation and Transformation Award, the Dacula Cluster and the Lanier Cluster, briefed the Board on their clusters' innovative practices funded by the award grant and the impact those practices are having on student achievement in their schools.

 

2016 Summer Leadership Conference Report - Staff presented a report on the 2016 Summer Leadership Conference held July 27-29, 2016 at the Classic Center in Athens, Georgia.

 

Staffing Update - Staff updated the Board with current information related to the staffing needs of Gwinnett County Public Schools.

The Aspiring Leader Program (ALP), implemented through the Quality-Plus Leader Academy, prepares teachers to move into entry level assistant principal and administrative intern positions. The seventh cohort of 43 graduates is presented to the Board of Education for the recognition of completion of the six month long course of study concluding with a summer residency.  To date, 35 (81%) members of this cohort have been appointed as assistant principals or administrative interns.  A total of 271 (83%) participants have been appointed to leadership positions from the seven cohorts of the Aspiring Leader Program.

1.04 2015 Innovation and Transformation Award Winners Report - Dacula Cluster and Lanier Cluster
Meeting
Sep 15, 2016 - Monthly Work Session 2:30 p.m.
Category
1. Discussion Topics and Presentations
Type

In May 2015, the "Innovation and Transformation Award" was instituted in the school district. The intent of the award is to formally recognize and financially reward a cluster or a group of schools for their exceptional efforts to transform teaching and learning through a systemic focus on GCPS' Strategic Initiatives and Priorities. The winners of the inaugural Innovation and Transformation Award, the Dacula Cluster and the Lanier Cluster, will brief the Board on their clusters' innovative practices funded by the award grant and the impact those practices are having on student achievement in their schools.
