Subject
6.04 Plan Amendments to Gwinnett Retirement System Plan
Meeting
May 19, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

 

Prepared by:  Tom Daymude, Director, Gwinnett Retirement System

 

Presented by:  Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background:  On March 8, 2011 the Internal Revenue Service ("IRS") issued a favorable determination letter with regard to the Gwinnett County Board of Education’s Retirement System (GRS) Plan. During the IRS' review of the Plan in making its determination, the IRS asked for a change to one of the sections of the Plan with regard to the definition of Leased Employees. Section 1.26 of the Plan defined a Leased Employee as "an individual who is a 'leased employee' of the Employer within the meaning of Sections 414(n) and (o) of the Code." While this language was previously permitted in qualified plans, as a result of the passage of the Small Business Job Protection Act, the definition of a "leased employee" may not incorporate the definition by reference to the section(s) of the Internal Revenue Code. Instead, the IRS has asked that the Plan be amended, revising Section 1.26, Leased Employee, to provide a more detailed description as set forth in the enclosed First Amendment. The First Amendment is a retroactive amendment which changes the definition effective January 1, 1997, in accordance with the Small Business Job Protection Act. However, in order to retain the favorable determination on the Plan's qualification issued on March 8, 2011, the First Amendment must be adopted by the Plan no later than June 6, 2011, 90 days from the date of the favorable determination.

 

Financial impact:  None.  The Amendment has no substantive effect on the Plan; it simply includes the language from the IRS Code section instead of referencing the Code Section.

 

Recommendation:  That the Board approve the IRS-requested changes to the Plan and the Board Chairman sign and date the attached First Amendment to the Gwinnett County Board Of Education’s Retirement System Plan Document.

6.07 GASB 54 Financial Statement
Meeting
May 19, 2011 - Monthly Business Meeting
Category
6. CONSENT AGENDA
Type
Action (Consent)

 

Prepared by:  Joe Heffron, Director of Budgets and Financial Reporting

 

Presented by:  Rick Cost, Chief Financial Officer

 

Background:  A new Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) Statement, Fund Balance Reporting (statement number 54), is effective for the current year (FY2011) Financial Reporting.  The GASB statement objective is to enhance the usefulness of Fund Balance information by providing clearer Fund Balance Classifications that can be more consistently applied.  As a result, some funds classified as Special Revenue Funds in the past must now have their sources of funding formally “committed” by resolution at the highest level of decision making authority (Board of Education). We currently have two existing Special Revenue funds that fall into this category:    

 

Fund 582 - This revenue source would be committed to fund Pcard administration and processing, financial reporting, and professional development. 

 

Fund 500 – This revenue source would be committed as Local School Activity Funds.

 

Financial Impact:  None.  The revenue sources for the funds listed above are currently already being used for the purposes for which they are being “committed.” 

 

Recommendation:  That the Board of Education adopt a resolution to “commit” the revenue sources for Fund 582 and Fund 500 for the purposes outlined above.  This revenue source would be reported as a Special Revenue Fund in compliance with GASB statement 54.

The Board meets in Executive (closed) Session prior to the 6:30 p.m. Public Forum and 7:00 p.m. Monthly Business Meeting (open). If Executive Session business remains to be conducted, the Board recesses Executive Session until the conclusion of the 7:00 p.m. meeting. The Board then reconvenes in the Executive Board Room to continue in Executive Session.

 

Executive Session may be held to discuss pending or threatened litigation with the Board attorney; the acquisition of real estate; the appointment, employment, compensation, hiring, disciplinary action, or periodic evaluation of personnel; and student discipline appeals.
