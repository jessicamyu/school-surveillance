-January 24th, 2022: Second reading (adoption hearing) Board will take official action during the board meeting

9. 2:30PM-2:45PM 2022-23 Workforce Education Continuation Grant Awards
Meeting
Oct 25, 2022 - Work Session 12:30PM
Category
A. Superintendent's Report
Type
Information

 

DESCRIPTION:

 

The Workforce Education Department has been awarded the continuation grants listed below from the Florida Department of Education. Additional information for each grant award is included in the attached executive summaries and budget impact analysis. The department's updated organizational chart and new job descriptions are also attached. We have one additional job description attached for review that is included on the updated org chart; TRST, Adult Ed Professional Development. 

 

 

Carl D. Perkins Secondary Grant - $1,486,006

 

The Carl D. Perkins Secondary Grant will continue to support the development of career academies in Polk County high schools and to further integrate academic achievement, career area certifications, and technical competencies. 

 

  • New positions: Senior Coordinator, Workforce Grants; Senior Coordinator, Career Academies & Community Liaison

 

 

Carl D. Perkins Postsecondary Grant - $227,715

 

The Carl D. Perkins Postsecondary Grant will continue to support the development of career and technical education in Polk County technical colleges and to further integrate academic achievement, career area certifications, and technical competencies. 

 

 

Perkins V Rural Innovation Grant - $25,000

 

The Perkins V Rural Innovation Grant will continue to support the development of career academies at Fort Meade and Frostproof Middle/Senior High Schools and Dundee Ridge Middle and to further integrate academic achievement, career area certifications, and technical competencies. 

 

 

Adult General Education Grant - $1,357,381

 

The Adult General Education Grant will continue to support the development of education services provided to English language learners who are adults, including professionals with degrees and credentials earned in their native countries, that enable such adults to achieve competency in the English language and acquire the basic and more advanced skills needed to function effectively as parents, workers, and citizens in the United States. 

 

  • New positions: Coordinator, Adult School Testing; Adult Testing Proctor

 

 

Integrated English Literacy and Civics Education Grant - $300,201

 

The Civics Education Grant will continue to support the development of educational programs to assist adults to become literate and obtain the knowledge and skills necessary for employment and economic self-sufficiency.

 

 

Corrections Education Grant - $100,000

 

The Corrections Education Grant will continue to support the development of educational programs for criminal offenders in correctional institutions and for other institutionalized individuals, including academic programs for adult education and literacy activities; integrated education and training; career pathways; and concurrent enrollment.

DESCRIPTION:

 

The Office of Acceleration & Innovation has been awarded a five-year Magnet Schools Assistance Program (MSAP) Grant valued $14,499,698.00. The grant provides support to create and/or significantly revise five magnet schools including:

 

  • Bethune Academy (transition to a Primary Cambridge program magnet school)
  • Daniel Jenkins Academy (transition to a Lower Cambridge program magnet school)
  • Rosabelle W. Blake Academy (transition from a school of choice to a Primary and Lower Cambridge program magnet school)
  • Fred G. Garner Elementary (creation of Fred G. Garner Academy as a technology/STEM magnet school)
  • James E. Stephens Elementary (creation of James E. Stephens Academy as a Primary Years Program [PYP] magnet school)

 

The MSAD project includes grant funded positions at both the district level as well as within each of the MASP magnet schools to fulfill the objectives of the grant and ensure successful implementation, reporting, and external evaluation. 

 

The submission of the MSAD application was presented at the March 8, 2022 Work Session and approval to submit the application was granted at the March 8, 2022 Board Meeting.

DESCRIPTION:

 

State Statute 6A-5.071 requires that the School Board annually approve the Professional Learning Catalog (formerly Master Inservice Plan - MIP).  The Professional Learning Catalog outlines high-quality professional learning components for awarding in-service points and prescribes record maintenance and data reporting requirements.

 

The Professional Learning Catalog will go through the 5-year revision process in March of 2023.  The description of the Professional Learning Catalog update provided with this summary does not include the entire catalog due to it being more than one thousand one hundred pages for over two hundred two specific in-service components.  The Professional Learning Management Suite is housed in the NetConnect System in the HUB.  The Professional Development Department annually reviews the components in the spring to meet the district and statutory requirements of the FLDOE.
