DESCRIPTION:

 

To review the charter contract negotiated between the Polk County School Board and the following charter school:

 

  • Achievement Academy, 15 year term

The contract used is referred to as the “Florida Standard Charter Renewal Contract”. It is statutorily required and provided by FLDOE. As a district, few changes were made to the contract unless otherwise requested by the board or superintendent. Below are the changes made to the contract for Achievement Academy:

 

 

Section 1

P. 3

  • B.  The term of this charter shall be:  15 years commencing on July 1, 2022 and ending on June 30, 2037 

 

 

Section 2: Academic Accountability

 

P. 11

  • C.  Student Promotion and Graduation: The School's student promotion policy shall be consistent with the provisions of the Application and applicable Florida law. The School will adopt the Sponsor's student progression plan.

 

Section 3: Students

P. 11

  • A. The School is authorized to serve students in grades ESE Pre-K.

P. 12

 

  • The School will participate in its Sponsor’s plan for the mental health assistance allocation.

 

P. 14 F.3.

 

 

 

 

P. 18

 

  • I.  ESE administrative services covered by the administrative fee, pursuant to section 1002.33(20), Florida Statutes, includes professional development related to IEP development; access to any electronic IEP system or forms; initial evaluation for ESE placement *and for the re-evaluation of ESE students; and other supports and services as agreed to by the School and the District.  *same language of previous contract

 

 

 

Section 4: Financial Accountability

 

P. 21

  • A.4.i.       The Sponsor shall calculate and submit eleven (11) monthly payments

to the account specified by the School. Each payment will be one-eleventh (1/11) of the funds described in Section 4.A.1., above, less the administrative fee set forth in Section 4.A.3, above. The first payment will be made by July 15 of the School’s first year of operation. Subsequent payments will be made no later than the 15th of each month beginning with August 15.  **PCPS standard practice to pay over 11 months.

Section 5: Facilities

P. 29

 

  • A. The School shall be located at 716 E. Bella Vista St. Lakeland, FL 33805, 695 E. Summerlin St. Bartow, FL 33830 and 2211 28th Street NW Winter Haven, FL 33880.

 

Section 6: Transportation

P.30

  • B. Reasonable Distance [for purposes of this Charter]: The School and Sponsor shall cooperate in making arrangements that ensure that transportation is not a barrier to equal access for all students residing within 5 miles.

Section 6: Insurance & Indemnification

 

P.32

  • F.          No later than 30 days prior to the opening of school, the School shall furnish the District with fully completed certificates of all insurance policies, signed by an authorized representative of the insurer(s) confirming the coverage begins by July 1. The certificates shall name the Sponsor as an additional insured. Until such time as the insurance is no longer required to be maintained by the School, the School shall provide the School Board evidence of the renewal or replacement of the insurance no less than thirty (30) days before expiration or termination of the required insurance for which evidence was provided.  Should any of the above described policies (A-E) be cancelled before the expiration date, written notice to the Sponsor shall be delivered in accordance with the policy provisions or within 10 days of cancellation, whichever is sooner. **This usually says “shall be issued to the sponsor and name the sponsor as an additional insured”  The school deleted “shall be issued to the sponsor”.

 

Section 9: Governance

P. 35

  • A. The general direction and management of the affairs of the School shall be vested in the Governing Board with a minimum of 3 members at least one of whom shall reside in Polk County.
  • A. The Board shall have at least two public meetings per school year within the District of Polk County.  A quorum of the Board must *be present.  *School changed this from “A quorum of the Board must be physically present in Polk County.
  • A. The School will be a private employer and will not participate in the Florida Retirement System.

p. 38

Stat. § 1002.33(7) (a) (18):
