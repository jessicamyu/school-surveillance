FLDOE VIP Dropout Recovery Program with Graduation Alliance:

A partnership with Graduation Alliance provides a last safety net for high school students who have dropped out or are disengaged with their education. Utilizing 360 degrees of support that is student-centered, technology enabled, and provides real-time teachers, coaches, and local advocates gives your most at-risk students the resources to get back on track and graduate with a diploma from Polk County Schools. As a FLDOE VIP provider this program has minimal impact on district resources. This district embedded, pay-for-performance model is based on defined academic goals with absolutely no startup costs, virtually eliminating the financial risk of launching a new program.

5. 2:15PM-2:30PM School Improvement Plan for Janie Howard Wilson Elementary Charter School

Nov 15, 2022 - Work Session 12:30PM
Category
A. Superintendent's Report
Type
Superintendent's Report

DESCRIPTION:

 

Section 1002.33(9), Florida Statutes, and State Board of Education Rule 6A-1.099827, F.A.C., requires low performing charter schools to develop and implement strategies to raise student achievement. A charter school that receives a school grade of “D” or “F” must develop and implement a school improvement plan. The plan must be approved by the charter school’s sponsor. State Board Rule requires the director and a representative of the governing board to appear before the sponsor at a publicly noticed meeting to present a school improvement plan that should include, at a minimum, the following components:

 


          • Mission statement of school;

• Academic data for most recent three (3) years, if available:

• Student achievement objectives included in the charter contract or most recent

sponsor approved school improvement plan;

• Analysis of student performance data including academic performance by each

subgroup;

• Detailed plan for addressing each identified deficiency in student performance,

including specific actions, person responsible, resources needed, and timeline;

• Identification of each component of school’s approved educational program

that has not been implemented as described in the school’s approved charter

application or charter contract;

• Detailed plan for addressing each identified deficiency noted in subparagraph

(4)(a)6. Of this rule, including specific actions, person responsible, resources

needed, and timeline;

• Identification of other barriers to student success, with a detailed plan for

addressing each barrier including specific actions, person responsible, resources

needed, and timeline; and

• Specific student achievement outcomes to be achieved.
