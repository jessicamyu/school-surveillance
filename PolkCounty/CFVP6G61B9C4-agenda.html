Presentation of Presidential Scholars district finalists. We have 30 students from 20 high schools. As part of the United States Department of Education Presidential Scholars Program, each state department of education is asked to select a limited number of current high school seniors from that state to receive this national recognition. In September, the Florida Department of Education sends us a memorandum of the guidelines describing the state process for selecting the Presidential Scholars from Florida. According to the guidelines each district was to select only two seniors to be the state finalists from that district. Along with several other criteria, one student was to be selected based largely on high academic achievement (including SAT or ACT assessments), and one student was to be selected on the basis of academic achievement in the area of Career Technical Education. In Polk County, we asked all the high school leadership staff to select the students from their school they felt were most worthy of consideration. High schools submitted names to the district in late September.

In accordance with Policy 0169.1:

 

 

The School Board recognizes the value to school governance of public comment on educational issues and the importance of allowing members of the public to express themselves on school matters of community interest.

     
Members of the public shall be given a reasonable opportunity to be heard on a proposition before the Board.

          

The opportunity to be heard is subject to policies adopted by the Board as follows:

  1. Guidelines regarding the amount of time an individual has to address the Board:
     
    1. Each statement made by a member of the public shall be limited to three (3) minutes duration. Persons will be recognized in the order in which the requests were received.
       
    2. The time period may be extended by the presiding officer.
       
  2. Procedures for allowing representatives of groups or factions on a proposition to address the Board:
    ​
    Rather than all members of groups or factions for or against a proposition addressing the Board at meetings in which a large number of individuals wish to be heard, the Board encourages representatives of such groups or factions to address the proposition in their representative capacity.
     
  3. Procedures or forms for an individual to use in order to inform the Board of a desire to be heard:

    Attendees must register their intention to participate in the public portion of the meeting upon their arrival at the meeting by completing a speaker's form at the entry of the meeting hall indicating his/her support, opposition, or neutrality on a proposition; and indicating his/her designation of a representative to speak for him/her or his/her group on a proposition if s/he so chooses.
     
  4. Designated period of time for public comment:

    The portion of the meeting during which the participation of the public is invited shall be limited to thirty (30) minutes, unless extended by a vote of the Board, except that all speakers who signed up to speak and who were outside of the thirty (30) minute time limit may address the Board at the end of the meeting.     

The presiding officer of each Board meeting at which public participation is permitted shall administer the rules of the Board for its conduct.

      
The presiding officer shall be guided by the following rules:

  1. Public participation shall be permitted as indicated on the agenda and before the Board takes official position on any action item under consideration.
     
  2. Participants must be recognized by the presiding officer and will be requested to preface their comments by an announcement of their name, address, and group affiliation.
     
  3. All statements shall be directed to the presiding officer; no person may address or question Board members individually. Staff members shall not be expected to answer questions from the audience unless called upon by the Board Chair or the Superintendent.
     
  4. Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:
     
    1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
       
    2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
       
    3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
       
  5. The presiding officer may:
     
    1. interrupt, warn, or terminate a participant's statement when the statement is too lengthy, personally directed, abusive, obscene, or irrelevant;
       
    2. request any individual to leave the meeting when that person does not observe reasonable decorum;
       
    3. request the assistance of law enforcement officers in the removal of a disorderly person when that person's conduct interferes with the orderly progress of the meeting; or
       
    4. call for a recess or an adjournment to another time when the lack of public decorum so interferes with the orderly conduct of the meeting as to warrant such action.     

No Board action shall be taken on requests made during public discussion unless the Board declares the matter to be an emergency in accordance with current law. Any other requests that the Board chooses to take action on shall be placed on an agenda for a subsequent meeting.

DESCRIPTION:

 

The District's self-funded health plan includes stop loss insurance which the District purchases to reduce the risks associated with an unexpected catastrophic illness or accident that can result in a large individual claim. With this insurance, the district self-insures up to a set dollar amount per individual, per plan year. This set dollar amount is referred to as the Individual Specific Deductible. 

 

The current stop loss carrier, Symetra Life Insurance Company, was awarded a contract effective 1/1/2019 pursuant to ITB #024-PLA-1119. The stop loss contract runs January-December each year and includes coverage for medical and prescription drug claims that accumulate towards the Individual Specific and Aggregating Specific Deductibles.

Ellevation also provides sustained, job-embedded, results-oriented professional development that helps every educator develop the capacity and skills to advance the language acquisition and content mastery of multilingual learners. Through a diverse offering of research-based professional learning modules and instructional activities, educators have access to curated tools to help students acquire language and master academic content. The professional learning modules in Ellevation Strategies will advance an educator’s knowledge of critical pedagogy, core concepts, and key vocabulary related to multilingual learners. The easy-to-understand dashboards and teacher-friendly descriptors guide educators to understand and apply data to help them better differentiate instruction. In accordance with Policy 0169.1:

 

 

The School Board recognizes the value to school governance of public comment on educational issues and the importance of allowing members of the public to express themselves on school matters of community interest.

     
Members of the public shall be given a reasonable opportunity to be heard on a proposition before the Board.

          

The opportunity to be heard is subject to policies adopted by the Board as follows:

  1. Guidelines regarding the amount of time an individual has to address the Board:
     
    1. Each statement made by a member of the public shall be limited to three (3) minutes duration. Persons will be recognized in the order in which the requests were received.
       
    2. The time period may be extended by the presiding officer.
       
  2. Procedures for allowing representatives of groups or factions on a proposition to address the Board:
    ​
    Rather than all members of groups or factions for or against a proposition addressing the Board at meetings in which a large number of individuals wish to be heard, the Board encourages representatives of such groups or factions to address the proposition in their representative capacity.
     
  3. Procedures or forms for an individual to use in order to inform the Board of a desire to be heard:

    Attendees must register their intention to participate in the public portion of the meeting upon their arrival at the meeting by completing a speaker's form at the entry of the meeting hall indicating his/her support, opposition, or neutrality on a proposition; and indicating his/her designation of a representative to speak for him/her or his/her group on a proposition if s/he so chooses.
     
  4. Designated period of time for public comment:

    The portion of the meeting during which the participation of the public is invited shall be limited to thirty (30) minutes, unless extended by a vote of the Board, except that all speakers who signed up to speak and who were outside of the thirty (30) minute time limit may address the Board at the end of the meeting.     

The presiding officer of each Board meeting at which public participation is permitted shall administer the rules of the Board for its conduct.

      
The presiding officer shall be guided by the following rules:

  1. Public participation shall be permitted as indicated on the agenda and before the Board takes official position on any action item under consideration.
     
  2. Participants must be recognized by the presiding officer and will be requested to preface their comments by an announcement of their name, address, and group affiliation.
     
  3. All statements shall be directed to the presiding officer; no person may address or question Board members individually. Staff members shall not be expected to answer questions from the audience unless called upon by the Board Chair or the Superintendent.
     
  4. Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:
     
    1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
       
    2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
       
    3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
       
  5. The presiding officer may:
     
    1. interrupt, warn, or terminate a participant's statement when the statement is too lengthy, personally directed, abusive, obscene, or irrelevant;
       
    2. request any individual to leave the meeting when that person does not observe reasonable decorum;
       
    3. request the assistance of law enforcement officers in the removal of a disorderly person when that person's conduct interferes with the orderly progress of the meeting; or
       
    4. call for a recess or an adjournment to another time when the lack of public decorum so interferes with the orderly conduct of the meeting as to warrant such action.     

No Board action shall be taken on requests made during public discussion unless the Board declares the matter to be an emergency in accordance with current law. Any other requests that the Board chooses to take action on shall be placed on an agenda for a subsequent meeting.
