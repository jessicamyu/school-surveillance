DESCRIPTION:

 

To review the charter contract negotiated between the Polk County School Board and the following charter school:

 

  • Cypress Junction Montessori, 5-year term

The contract used is referred to as the “Florida Standard Charter Renewal Contract”.  It is statutorily required and provided by FLDOE. As a district, few changes were made to the contract unless otherwise requested by the board or superintendent.  Below are the changes made to the Cypress Junction Montessori contract:

 

 

Section 1

P. 3

  • Term:  The term of this Charter shall be [5] years commencing on

July 1, 2021 and ending on June 30, 2026

 

 

Section 2: Academic Accountability

 

P. 11

  • C.  Student Promotion and Graduation: The School's student promotion policy shall be consistent with the provisions of the Application and applicable Florida law. The School will adopt the Sponsor's student progression plan.

 

Section 3: Students

P. 11

  • A. The School will serve students in grades K through 8.

P. 12

  • The School will participate in its Sponsor’s plan for the mental health assistance allocation. If the School develops its own plan, it must submit that plan to its governing board for approval. After the plan is approved by the governing board, it must be provided to the School’s Sponsor.

P. 14

  • F. Annual Enrollment table was inserted.

 

Section 4: Financial Accountability

 

P. 21

  • 4. i. The Sponsor shall calculate and submit eleven (11) monthly to the account specified by the School. Each payment will be one-eleventh (1/11) of the funds described in Section 4.A.1., above, less the administrative fee set forth in Section 4.A.3, above. The first payment will be made by July 15 of the School’s first year of operation. Subsequent payments will be made no later than the 15th of each month beginning with August 15.

 

Section 5: Facilities

P. 29

 

  • A. The School shall be located at 220 5th Street SW, Winter Haven, FL 33880.

 

Section 6: Transportation

P.30

  • B. Reasonable Distance [for purposes of this Charter]: The School and Sponsor shall cooperate in making arrangements that ensure that transportation is not a barrier to equal access for all students residing within 5 miles of the school.

 

Section 9: Governance

P. 34

  • A. Governance of the School will be in accordance with the Bylaws or other organizational documents of the School and as described in the Application. The general direction and management of the affairs of the School shall be vested in the Governing Board with a minimum of 3 members at least one of whom shall reside in Polk County.

p. 35

  • The Board shall have at least two public meetings per school year within the District of Polk County. A quorum of the Board must be physically present in Polk County for each of these meetings in Polk County.
  • The School will be a [private employer] and will not participate in the Florida Retirement System.

5. 1:40 - 1:50PM Recommend Name for the combination of Ben Hill Griffin and Frostproof Elementary
Meeting
May 11, 2021 - Work Session 12:30 pm
Category
A. Discussion Items
Type
Discussion

 

DESCRIPTION:

 

A naming committee was convened on March 11, 2021 for the purpose of choosing a name to recommend to the Board for the naming of the combining of Ben Hill Griffin, Jr. Elementary and Frostproof Elementary into a single school. There is an addition under construction at the current Ben Hill Griffin, Jr. Elementary school which allows for additional capacity. The combined school will open in August of 2021.

 

The naming committee members are:

 

Johnathan Albert - Frostproof Mayor

Moses Anderson, Jr. - Pastor, House of Praise Ministries

Barbara Aponte - Frostproof Elementary SAC Chair

Shannon Benton - Frostproof Middle/Senior Teacher

 

Victor Duncan - Regional Assistant Superintendent

Michelle Hutto - Ben Hill Griffin, Jr. Elementary SAC Chair and Naming Committee Chair

Alice Johnson - Ben Hill Griffin, Jr. Elementary Network Manager

Nicole McDowell - Frostproof City Manager

Dart Myers - Principal, Frostproof Elementary and Ben Hill Griffin, Jr. Elementary

Bea Reifeis - Frostproof Historian

Donna Respress - Ben Hill Griffin, Inc.

Lynn Wilson - District Board Member

Debbie Wrye - Frostproof Elementary Library Media Specialist

Raul Zamora - Babcock Store Manager

Angela Usher - Assistant Superintendent, Facilities and Operations PCPS

 

 

The Committee chose 3 potential names:

 

Frostproof Ben Hill Griffin, Jr.

Frostproof B.H. Griffin, Jr.

Frostproof BHG, Jr.

 

The possible names were sent out on social media and a weblink was provided to the community for their response. A paper ballot was also provided for the portion of the community who may not have access to technology.

 

61% chose Frostproof Ben Hill Griffin, Jr.

7% chose Frostproof B.H. Griffin, Jr.

31% chose Frostproof BHG, Jr.

 

There were also several comments requesting the word elementary be added.

 

The Committee reconvened on April 19, 2021 to review the community survey. The Committee moved unanimously to recommend the name Frostproof Ben Hill Griffin, Jr. Elementary to the Superintendent and the Board.
