Superintendent's Report

DESCRIPTION:

The District's self-funded health plan currently includes a comprehensive preferred provider network contracted through Florida Blue. The network includes a wide range of physicians, facilities, and other health care providers that meet the contracting standards set forth by Florida Blue. Health plan participants are encouraged to use preferred providers through reduced out-of-pocket costs when using in-network providers. 

 

Presentation by:

Dr. Matthew Resnick, Chief Medical Officer

Linda King, Director of Risk Management & Employee Benefits

 

Recommendation

It is the recommendation of staff to amend the current administrative agreement with Everside Health to include Embold Health services as a direct pass-through cost to the District. The estimated cost in Year 1, effective March 1, 2022, and based on an estimated 18,000 members, is approximately $450,000. Actual cost will fluctuate based on monthly enrollment. Embold Health is guaranteeing a 1:1 Return on Investment in 2022 and 2:1 ROI in 2023 with a penalty of 50% of the licensing fee for failure to meet the guarantee.

DESCRIPTION:

 

To review the charter contract negotiated between the Polk County School Board and the following charter school:

 

  • Mater Academy Davenport, 5-year term

The contract used is referred to as the “Florida Standard Charter Renewal Contract”. It is statutorily required and provided by FLDOE. As a district, few changes were made to the contract unless otherwise requested by the board or superintendent. Below are the changes made to the Mater Academy Davenport contract:

 

Section 1

P. 3

  • Term:  The term of this Charter shall be [5] years commencing on July 1, 2022 and ending on June 30, 2027.

 

Section 2: Academic Accountability

P. 11

  • C.  Student Promotion and Graduation: The School's student promotion policy shall be consistent with the provisions of the Application and applicable Florida law. The School [will/will not] adopt the Sponsor's student progression plan.

 

Section 3: Students

P. 11

  • A. The School will serve students in grades K through 5.

 

P. 12

  • In year one, the School will participate in its Sponsor’s plan for the mental health assistance allocation. On an annual basis thereafter, the School will notify the Sponsor on or before March 1 of each year to indicate whether the school will participate in its sponsor’s plan or if the school will develop its own plan for the following academic year. If the school develops its own plan, it must submit that plan to its Governing Board for approval. After the plan is approved by the Governing Board, it must be provided to the school’s Sponsor.

 

Section 4: Financial Accountability

P. 21

  • 4. The Sponsor shall calculate and submit eleven (11) monthly to the account specified by the School. Each payment will be one-eleventh (1/11) of the funds described in Section 4.A.1., above, less the administrative fee set forth in Section 4.A.3, above. The first payment will be made by July 15 of the school’s first year of operation. Subsequent payments will be made no later than the 15th of each month beginning with August 15.

 

Section 5: Facilities

P. 29

  • The school shall be located at 318 Ronald Reagan Parkway, Davenport, FL 33896.

 

Section 6: Transportation

P.30

  • B. Reasonable Distance [for purposes of this Charter]: The school and sponsor shall cooperate in making arrangements that ensure that transportation is not a barrier to equal access for all students residing within two to four miles distance from the school.

 

Section 9: Governance

p. 35

  • The school will be a private employer and will not participate in the Florida Retirement System.
