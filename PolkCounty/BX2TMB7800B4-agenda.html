Meeting
Mar 9, 2021 - Work Session 12:30 PM
Category
A. Discussion Items
Type

DESCRIPTION:

 

This is the annual financial review of the District's self-insured health plan.  The District is required to provide to the State of Florida Office of Insurance Regulation (OIR) an annual report on the Group Health Plan. The report is due to the State of Florida 90 days after the Plan Year ends. The District’s health plan year is January 1 – December 31; the PCPS Group Health Plan’s 2020 report is due to the State by March 31, 2021.

 

The 112.08 is required to include certification from an actuary on the soundness of the plan that certifies the net assets of the plan at the end of the plan year are adequate to fully fund the outstanding claim liabilities, the rate structure of the Plan appears adequate to support the projected claims and other expenses of the Plan for the upcoming plan year and the actuary considers the School Board of Polk County Self-funded Employee Health Plan is actuarially sound.

Mar 9, 2021 - Work Session 12:30 PM

DESCRIPTION:

This presentation allows Mater Academy, Inc. the opportunity to provide the Polk County School Board with an initial overview of their charter proposals received at the beginning of the 2021 charter application review cycle. 

Florida Statute 1002.33(6) states that a sponsor shall receive and consider charter school applications received on or before February 1 of each calendar year for charter schools to be opened 18 months later. 

 

OBJECTIVES 

  1. A brief introduction to the number, type, communities and targeted populations of the proposed charter schools. 

  1. An overview of the academic design, governance structure, founding board members and financial structure of the proposed charter schools. 

  1. An opportunity for school board members to ask questions based on their presentation. 

The charter review team will be in attendance to hear this presentation, or view the recording of this presentation, which will assist in the review process. 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

CONTACT: Candy Amato, Director of Charter Schools (863) 534-0625

 

 

 

FINANCIAL IMPACT:

 

Budgetary projections over a 5-year period is reflected in the charter application submitted by each charter applicant.   

 

The charter applicants will be eligible, if approved, for the FTE and categorical funds that 

students at the charter school generate based on current and future charter law. Applicants will also be eligible to apply for competitive Federal Start Up grant funds during their initial and second year of operation.

Category
B. Information Items

DESCRIPTION:

 

The purpose of this grant is to provide consultative services to assist the District to focus on additional progress monitoring due to the pandemic.

 

Polk County Public Schools will utilize consultants to provide training and Professional Development in data driven instructional practices and work with District staff to assist in developing and implementing Data Dashboards and disseminating data to State and Local stakeholder groups. Polk County has earned a district-wide grade of a “B” for the last two years. It is imperative the district dedicates additional resources to progress monitoring due to the pandemic impacting the learning format for all students and there being an absence of assessments in 2019-2020. This will allow extra efforts to be focused on district-wide progress monitoring
