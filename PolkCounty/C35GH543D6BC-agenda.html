DESCRIPTION:

Head Start Administration Grant application for FY January 1 through December 31, 2022, is funded by the Agency for Children and Families (ACF) via the Office of Head Start (OHS) in Washington, DC.  The Office of Head Start provides grants to public and private non-profit and for-profit agencies to provide comprehensive child development services to economically disadvantaged children and families, with a special focus on helping children to develop the early reading and math skills they need to be successful in school. Intended primarily for preschoolers from low-income families, Head Start promotes school readiness by enhancing the social and cognitive development of children through the provision of educational, health, nutritional, social and other services to enrolled children and families. Head Start programs engage parents in their children's learning and help them in making progress toward their educational, literacy and employment goals. The Head Start Program also emphasizes the significant involvement of parents in the administration of local Head Start programs. Each Head Start program must provide at least 10 percent of its enrollment opportunities available to children with disabilities. The Head Start grantee, Polk County Public Schools, must contribute 20 percent of the total cost of the program from non-federal funds. The annual application process is required by the federal oversight agency to provide current budgetary information and to realign costs. The district’s grant application is required to be submitted to HHS/ACF Region VI, Atlanta by October 1, 2021. The Program is funded by the Agency for Children and Families via the Office of Head Start. The federal Head Start grant portion of $8,572,969 for the prescribed number of children (942) requires a local match of $2,143,242. Including the USDA/Child Care Food Program, total funding amounts to $769,802 for a total funding of $11,486,013.

DESCRIPTION:

 

 

Title III is a federal entitlement through ESSA that focuses on supplemental services for English Language Learners (ELLs) and recently arrived immigrants. The purpose of Title III funding is to provide student support for language acquisition and student achievement. Similar to other ESSA entitlement grants, the funding allocations are based on district student population. 

 

 

Polk County Public Schools continues to experience a growth in the number of English learners (ELLs). During the 2020-2021 school year Polk County Public Schools served 14,391 English learners. Currently, 24% of our ELs are recent arrivals to our district. Regarding English language proficiency, 32% are recent arrivals and in need of intensive support, 14% are developing in need of moderate support.  Overall, ELLs represent 107 different languages in Polk County Schools.  The data highlights opportunities for growth in the number of students achieving proficiency and academic success. The ESOL Department in Polk County Public Schools has identified the following categories to address and meet the unique needs of English learners.  We increased the licenses for Rosetta Stone used in the successful in the last three years and hiring two ESOL Teachers Resources.  The two additional positions will increase the support that is provided to schools.  All activities are supplemental and provided above and beyond the services and resources available to all the students in the district.  Furthermore, the activities are evidence-based and compliant with the state and federal rules as well as the district's strategic plan.  The number of students attaining proficiency in the acquisition of the English Language and academic achievement will increase after the implementation of these activities.
