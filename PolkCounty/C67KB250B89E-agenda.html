In accordance with Policy 0169.1:

 

 

The School Board recognizes the value to school governance of public comment on educational issues and the importance of allowing members of the public to express themselves on school matters of community interest.

     
Members of the public shall be given a reasonable opportunity to be heard on a proposition before the Board.

          

The opportunity to be heard is subject to policies adopted by the Board as follows:

  1. Guidelines regarding the amount of time an individual has to address the Board:
     
    1. Each statement made by a member of the public shall be limited to three (3) minutes duration. Persons will be recognized in the order in which the requests were received.
       
    2. The time period may be extended by the presiding officer.
       
  2. Procedures for allowing representatives of groups or factions on a proposition to address the Board:
    ​
    Rather than all members of groups or factions for or against a proposition addressing the Board at meetings in which a large number of individuals wish to be heard, the Board encourages representatives of such groups or factions to address the proposition in their representative capacity.
     
  3. Procedures or forms for an individual to use in order to inform the Board of a desire to be heard:

    Attendees must register their intention to participate in the public portion of the meeting upon their arrival at the meeting by completing a speaker's form at the entry of the meeting hall indicating his/her support, opposition, or neutrality on a proposition; and indicating his/her designation of a representative to speak for him/her or his/her group on a proposition if s/he so chooses.
     
  4. Designated period of time for public comment:

    The portion of the meeting during which the participation of the public is invited shall be limited to thirty (30) minutes, unless extended by a vote of the Board, except that all speakers who signed up to speak and who were outside of the thirty (30) minute time limit may address the Board at the end of the meeting.     

The presiding officer of each Board meeting at which public participation is permitted shall administer the rules of the Board for its conduct.

      
The presiding officer shall be guided by the following rules:

  1. Public participation shall be permitted as indicated on the agenda and before the Board takes official position on any action item under consideration.
     
  2. Participants must be recognized by the presiding officer and will be requested to preface their comments by an announcement of their name, address, and group affiliation.
     
  3. All statements shall be directed to the presiding officer; no person may address or question Board members individually. Staff members shall not be expected to answer questions from the audience unless called upon by the Board Chair or the Superintendent.
     
  4. Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:
     
    1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
       
    2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
       
    3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
       
  5. The presiding officer may:
     
    1. interrupt, warn, or terminate a participant's statement when the statement is too lengthy, personally directed, abusive, obscene, or irrelevant;
       
    2. request any individual to leave the meeting when that person does not observe reasonable decorum;
       
    3. request the assistance of law enforcement officers in the removal of a disorderly person when that person's conduct interferes with the orderly progress of the meeting; or
       
    4. call for a recess or an adjournment to another time when the lack of public decorum so interferes with the orderly conduct of the meeting as to warrant such action.     

No Board action shall be taken on requests made during public discussion unless the Board declares the matter to be an emergency in accordance with current law. Any other requests that the Board chooses to take action on shall be placed on an agenda for a subsequent meeting.

Title III is a federal entitlement through ESSA that focuses on supplemental services for English Language Learners
(ELLs) and recently arrived immigrants. The purpose of Title III funding is to provide student support for language
acquisition and student achievement. Similar to other ESSA entitlement grants, the funding allocations are based on
district student population.

2021 school year Polk County Public Schools served 14,391 English learners. Currently, 24% of our ELs are recent arrivals
to our district. Regarding English language proficiency, 32% are recent arrivals and in need of intensive support, 14% are
developing in need of moderate support.Overall, ELLs represent 107 different languages in Polk County Schools. The data
highlights opportunities for growth in the number of students achieving proficiency and academic success. The ESOL
Department in Polk County Public Schools has identified the following categories to address and meet the unique needs of
English learners.We increased the licenses for Rosetta Stone used in the successful in the last three years and hiring two
ESOL Teachers Resources.The two additional positions will increase the support that is provided to schools. All activities
are supplemental and provided above and beyond the services and resources available to all the students in the district.
Furthermore, the activities are evidence-based and compliant with the state and federal rules as well as the district's
strategic plan. The number of students attaining proficiency in the acquisition of the English Language and academic

DESCRIPTION:

 

Polk County Schools utilizes the claims’ administration services of Johns Eastern Company Inc. to properly track and adjudicate workers' compensation, general liability, and auto liability under its self-insurance program. Johns Eastern Company Inc. has effectively served as the district’s third party administrator since January 1, 2015.  The services Johns Eastern Company Inc. provides to Polk County Schools include:

  • Claims management and information services for employee workers’ compensation and liability incidents, bill processing, and state reporting
  • A team of dedicated workers compensation and liability adjusters (one onsite litigated workers' compensation and liability adjuster)
  • Medical management services, including one onsite registered nurse and network discounts
  • Excess insurance reporting management
  • Attendance at hearings, mediations and client service claim review meetings;
  • Subrogation recoveries, special disability trust fund recoveries; and
  • Standard and custom loss reporting

Johns Eastern Company Inc. has done an excellent job in managing and reducing workers’ compensation claim costs.  Their liability team works closely with the School Board Attorney’s Office and Risk Management Department to manage liability claims and litigation.

 

In the past, Polk County Schools piggybacked on Duval County Schools bid, but due to the differences between our two Districts and the success of our program in the past, staff decided to directly negotiate with the assistance of Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, the district’s broker consulting firm for property and casualty insurance.  Gallagher staff reviewed terms and assisted with direct negotiations to ensure competitive pricing.  The direct result was lowering our proposed renewal cost, in addition to upgrading from a 3/4 time liability adjuster to full-time.   

 

The fees for November 1, 2021 – October 31, 2024 will be $775,000 per year; with a 3% increase each year beginning November 1, 2024. The fee is an increase of $83,189 over our expiring fee which is a 12% increase; however, this contract includes a dedicated Liability adjuster versus 3/4 time in the prior contract period. Johns Eastern has not had an increase for the last two years.

2. Renewal of Blue Cross Blue Shield of Florida Administrative Services Agreement = NTX $5,033,000 per year for three years
Meeting
Oct 26, 2021 - Board Meeting 5:00PM
Category
N. Consent Agenda - Risk
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Motion to Approve the renewal of Blue Cross Blue Shield of Florida Administrative Services Agreement.

DESCRIPTION:

 

Florida Blue (FB) has provided a renewal offer to extend the Administrative Services Only (ASO) agreement effective January 1, 2022 through December 31, 2024.  It is the recommendation of staff to renew the contract based on the following: 

  • Florida Blue will continue to provide all onsite resources and programs currently in effect for the duration of the contract with no annual cost escalators for the three-year renewal period.  Resources include:
    • Staff costs for Health Center: One (1) full-time Nurse Practitioner and one (1) Medical Assistant
    • Onsite Customer Service Representative
    • Funding for a Pharmacy Technician at the Ft. Meade Medical Program. 
  • Florida Blue will continue to provide a $100,000 wellness fund annually for the duration of the contract
  • Florida Blue will continue to provide a capitated payment arrangement with Quest for members using our Employee Health & Wellness Centers.  This means there are no additional costs for most laboratory services provided at the health centers.  The capitated lab arrangement can also be accessed through other community physician providers.  The approximate savings is $1,200,000 per year that would need to be paid out of the health plan on a fee-for-service basis if no capitation arrangement were in place.  No other carrier offers a capitation payment arrangement for laboratory services.
  • Prime Therapeutics (pharmacy benefits provider) is enhancing overall discounts, rebates, and dispensing fee guarantees by approximately 14% over current pharmacy pricing.  Based on previous prescription drug utilization, this could dampen the escalation of pharmacy expenses by more than $2,500,000 when compared to current pricing. 
  • Florida Blue will continue to provide an annual administrative credit of $2.00 per employee per month, approximately $300,000 per year, to offset risk management expenses such as open enrollment, etc. 
  • Florida Administrative Code does not require the District to conduct an RFP for third party administration.  With multiple activities and long-term strategies underway that would be impacted by a change in administrator (e.g., benefit enrollment system implementation, interface with employee health centers, the wellness tracking platform, and the $1.2 million additional claims costs we would incur for laboratory services), we believe it is in our best interest to renew with FB for ASO services. 

 

The proposed ASO fee effective January 1, 2022 is $35.25 Per Employee Per Month over the three-year renewal term, or approximately $5,033,000 per year.   The current fee is $31.80, which was reduced from $33.80 in 2019 with the change to the Blue Cross Blue Shield of Alabama platform.  This resulted in a three-year savings of approximately $850,000.  

 

The financial impact of the 2022 proposed fee is approximately an increase of $507,150.

W. Public Comments Addressing Items Not on Today's Agenda

In accordance with Policy 0169.1:

 

 

The School Board recognizes the value to school governance of public comment on educational issues and the importance of allowing members of the public to express themselves on school matters of community interest.

     
Members of the public shall be given a reasonable opportunity to be heard on a proposition before the Board.

          

The opportunity to be heard is subject to policies adopted by the Board as follows:

  1. Guidelines regarding the amount of time an individual has to address the Board:
     
    1. Each statement made by a member of the public shall be limited to three (3) minutes duration. Persons will be recognized in the order in which the requests were received.
       
    2. The time period may be extended by the presiding officer.
       
  2. Procedures for allowing representatives of groups or factions on a proposition to address the Board:
    ​
    Rather than all members of groups or factions for or against a proposition addressing the Board at meetings in which a large number of individuals wish to be heard, the Board encourages representatives of such groups or factions to address the proposition in their representative capacity.
     
  3. Procedures or forms for an individual to use in order to inform the Board of a desire to be heard:

    Attendees must register their intention to participate in the public portion of the meeting upon their arrival at the meeting by completing a speaker's form at the entry of the meeting hall indicating his/her support, opposition, or neutrality on a proposition; and indicating his/her designation of a representative to speak for him/her or his/her group on a proposition if s/he so chooses.
     
  4. Designated period of time for public comment:

    The portion of the meeting during which the participation of the public is invited shall be limited to thirty (30) minutes, unless extended by a vote of the Board, except that all speakers who signed up to speak and who were outside of the thirty (30) minute time limit may address the Board at the end of the meeting.     

The presiding officer of each Board meeting at which public participation is permitted shall administer the rules of the Board for its conduct.

      
The presiding officer shall be guided by the following rules:

  1. Public participation shall be permitted as indicated on the agenda and before the Board takes official position on any action item under consideration.
     
  2. Participants must be recognized by the presiding officer and will be requested to preface their comments by an announcement of their name, address, and group affiliation.
     
  3. All statements shall be directed to the presiding officer; no person may address or question Board members individually. Staff members shall not be expected to answer questions from the audience unless called upon by the Board Chair or the Superintendent.
     
  4. Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:
     
    1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
       
    2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
       
    3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
       
  5. The presiding officer may:
     
    1. interrupt, warn, or terminate a participant's statement when the statement is too lengthy, personally directed, abusive, obscene, or irrelevant;
       
    2. request any individual to leave the meeting when that person does not observe reasonable decorum;
       
    3. request the assistance of law enforcement officers in the removal of a disorderly person when that person's conduct interferes with the orderly progress of the meeting; or
       
    4. call for a recess or an adjournment to another time when the lack of public decorum so interferes with the orderly conduct of the meeting as to warrant such action.     

No Board action shall be taken on requests made during public discussion unless the Board declares the matter to be an emergency in accordance with current law. Any other requests that the Board chooses to take action on shall be placed on an agenda for a subsequent meeting.
