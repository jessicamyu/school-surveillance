6. 2:25PM - 2:40PM Proposed Poinciana High School Site
Meeting
Jun 8, 2021 - Work Session 12:30 pm
Category
A. Discussion Items
Type
Discussion

 

DESCRIPTION:

 

Ridge Community Senior and Haines City Senior are still experiencing increased enrollment from development surrounding the schools, almost reaching full capacity.  The new davenport High School helped with relief but due to rapid growth school capacity at the high school level remains a concern.

 

There are over 1300 students currently bused from the Poinciana area to Haines City Senior. The Poinciana area is also growing very quickly. Construction of a high school in the Poinciana area would help to relieve both Haines City Senior and Ridge Community Senior and provide a high school for the Poinciana Community.

 

An attached preliminary analysis demonstrates that within the next 5 years, all 3 schools would be at or over capacity due to approved development in the area. 

 

Poinciana Parkway SPE is offering approximately 85 acres of land in the Poinciana area for the price of $75,000 per acre. The site is 1 mile from Laurel Elementary, 1.5 miles from Lake Marion Creek Middle School and 5 miles from Palmetto Elementary.

Jun 8, 2021 - Work Session 12:30 pm
Category
A. Discussion Items
Type
Discussion

 

DESCRIPTION:

 

 

Student Code of Conduct outlines the Policy on Face Coverings and Exceptions

 

Face Covering – Grades K-12 (Only applicable when the wearing of a face covering is required by the District): Students must wear a face covering at all times required by the District. It must cover the nose, mouth, and fit snugly against the sides of a person’s face with little or no gaps to ensure their own safety and the safety of other students and staff. Students must wear the face covering when on school grounds or district provided transportation unless the student is involved in an approved activity when a face covering would not be appropriate. All face coverings must comply with general dress code and school uniform requirements.*** Grades K-1 are in developmental stages upon the first 9 weeks of entering schools.

 

Exceptions: Exceptions to the uniform dress code shall be permitted when:

1) A student wears a uniform of a nationally recognized youth organization, such as the Boy Scouts or Girl Scouts, on regular meeting days.

2) A student wears a button, armband, or other accouterment to exercise the right of free speech guaranteed by the United States and Florida Constitutions, unless the button, armband or other accouterment signifies or is related to gangs, gang membership or gang activity or would otherwise violate the dress code policy.

3) A student wears a costume or special clothing necessary for a school play or other school-sponsored activity as allowed by the principal.

4) The wearing of clothing in compliance with the uniform dress code violates a student’s held religious belief or by reason of a student’s disability or medical condition
