BACKGROUND:

Thomas B. Dobies was born and raised in Tarpon Springs. He has been serving the community as a funeral director since 1975. He opened his first funeral home in Holiday, FL in 1979, and later expanded his services to four additional locations in Pasco, Hernando and Pinellas counties. Mr. Dobies is very involved in the community and has supported hundreds of local organizations through the years with his time and treasure.

 

Thomas Dobies is also very well known for his generosity and support of local law enforcement. In the past 15 years, he has donated a substantial amount of money towards the purchase of police dogs to support police departments throughout the Tampa Bay area. The cost for purchasing high-quality police dogs is over $10,000 per dog and without Mr. Dobies generosity, police departments would limit their access to this valuable resource. Pinellas County Schools’ Police was one of those local police departments to benefit from Mr. Dobies donation and receive their police dog, Dobies, earlier this year. Officer Barbara Baugher and her dog Dobies are a nationally certified K-9 team and work throughout our school district to protect and serve Pinellas County Schools' students and staff.

 

The district would like to thank Thomas Dobies for his commitment to the safety and well-being of our students and recognize him for his overwhelming generosity.

BACKGROUND:

First Home Bank is based out of St. Petersburg with a mission to engage and support the community they serve. Their commitment to volunteerism is part of the First Home Bank difference. To expand on this commitment to the community, First Home Bank implemented a Quarterly Community Partnership initiative that started in 2021. This plan was created to help increase volunteerism throughout the First Home Bank family, so their staff could continue to make a difference in their community.

 

First Home Bank has identified four causes that they will be supporting each quarter through donations and volunteering. Each cause was chosen specifically with their strategic focus areas in mind, Community and Human Services, Education and Financial Literacy, Arts and Culture and Animal Services and Programs. First Home Bank selected the Helping Educate All in Transition (HEAT) Program as their final Quarterly Community Partnership for 2021. The HEAT Program will receive a $5,000 donation to support the students experiencing homelessness in Pinellas County. In addition to this generous donation, 23 volunteers from the First Home Bank team spent an entire day at Mount Vernon Elementary School working on a landscaping project to improve the curb appeal at this school. The district recognizes and thanks First Home Bank for their donation of time and treasure.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

BACKGROUND:

Each year, the district reviews policies in alignment with legislative updates as well as district and state practices. The proposed amendments to the policies below align with the legislative updates that were enacted into law on July 1, 2021.

 

Policy 2410 - Special Services

  • Added language to align with HB 590 requiring the Superintendent to annually report to the Department of Education the number of involuntary examinations initiated at schools, on school buses or at school-sponsored activities.

Policy 1140 - Discipline of Administrators, Policy 3140 - Discipline of Instructional Staff and Policy 4140 - Discipline of Support Staff

Policy 5630.01 - Use of Seclusion and Restraint on Students with Disabilities

  • Removed language to align with HB 149 that prohibits the use of seclusion or mechanical restraint on students with disabilities.

Policy 9800 – Charter Schools

  • Adjusted language to align with HB 1028 which removes the February 1st deadline to submit a charter school application and allows the charter school to determine the opening date of the school. Additional language was added in alignment with legislation that states if a charter school changes its curriculum focus, the change is automatically assumed accepted if the curriculum aligns with state standards.

RATIONALE:

The proposed amendments to these policies ensure alignment with legislative updates, as well as state and district procedures.

BACKGROUND:

Florida law requires that district school boards annually submit to the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) proposed procedures for the provision of special instruction and services for exceptional students. This document also serves as the basis for the identification, evaluation, eligibility determination and placement of students in special programs for exceptional students and is a component of the district’s application for supplemental student funds available under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Rule 6A-6.03411, Florida Administrative Code, requires the approval of this document by the FDOE as a prerequisite for districts’ use of weighted cost factors under the Florida Education Finance Program.

 

This State Policies and Procedures for Exceptional Student Education (SP&P) is typically written in the fall of the school year it serves and is completely reviewed and revised every three years. In between full revisions, the SP&P is revised as needed based upon changes in case law, federal guidance or state interpretation of IDEA. FDOE typically approves the district’s SP&P in the spring of each year. This year because of legal review, the SP&P for the DOE approval of the 2020-21 school year was not released until July 2021 after the school year was complete. The 2020-21 school year was a triennial complete review/revision year. Following FDOE approval, the new ESE SP&P is brought to the school board for approval prior to signature by the superintendent. The new ESE SP&P will be in effect through the end of the 2020-21 school year.

 

Updates include:

 

Section I A- Restraint and Seclusion

  • Changes were made to how incidents are reported to parents by schools and the monitoring/ training that will be provided by district-level specialists and committees.
  • Changes were made to the reporting and monitoring of restraint and seclusion to a new Restraint and Seclusion Team and the frequency of reporting to district stakeholders. Additional training programs and initiatives were added for school-based teams.
  • Current data was updated to include the 2019-20 school year.

Section I- B3-Collaboration of Public Private Partnership

  • We have contractual agreements for other counties to send their students to Pinellas County DJJ. The number of staff changed at some of our EAS sites and the names of the EAS schools changed and are reflected in the updates.

Section I C I- Exceptional Student Education Procedural Safeguards

  • Minor changes were made to meet federal guidelines.

Section I- G: General Education Intervention Procedures

  • Updates to this section reflect changes to the MTSS Manual for PCS.

Section II C- IEP

  • The language for ESY was changed to align general statutes.
  • The section on continuum of services was completely changed to be aligned with current language and practice federally and locally.

Section II- B.12: Exceptional Education Eligibility for Students with Specific Learning Disabilities

  • This section has increased detail concerning the process used to determine eligibility that includes multiple reviews of gap analysis, percentage of growth over time via response to intervention.

Section II E- Participation in State and District Assessments

  • The data for percentage of students on FSAA by subgroup in the 2019-20 school year were updated. A statement was added explaining the steps the district will take to reduce the percentages in totality and by individual sub-groups. New processes, training and monitoring were added to decrease participation.

Section II- D: Discipline

  • Changes were made to this section to include the current training that is occurring in the district on social emotional learning, restorative practices and trauma informed care. Language was added on who serves in various roles in discipline reviews and the steps that need to be taken during alternative placements. Language was added as to where alternative placements may occur. Language about CPI levels and who gets the training was changed as it was in the restraint and seclusion section.

Appendix C: District Plan to Increase the Participation of Underrepresented Students in the Program for Students who are Gifted

  • Current data was updated.

Appendix D: District Policies Regarding the Allowable Use or Prohibition of Physical Restraint and Seclusion

  • The Board policy on mechanical restraint was removed because we no longer allow the use of mechanical restraint. The Board policy on Seclusion was uploaded.

New Appendix- School Closures Due to National or Local Emergency

The Florida Department of Education added an appendix that deals with PCS response during times of national or local emergency. PCS was required to provide supporting documentation used to support and educate staff and families.  The documents provided are:

  • PCS ESE Instructional Continuity Guide
  • FERPA statement for therapies
  • Parent letter concerning the Digital Learning Plan
  • ESE Staff Roles Description Guidance Document
  • Process for IEP Meetings Guidance Document

RATIONALE:
The Department of Education has required that school boards adopt the new document with additions specific to Pinellas County Schools through 2019 as evidenced by signature of the superintendent of the school district with the date of the school board approval on the signature page (attached).

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Each year the district reviews policies in alignment with district practices. The proposed amendments to the policies below relate to the Student Progression Plan.

 

Policy 5420.03 – Reporting Student Progress and Class Ranking

  • The proposed amendment adds clarifying language for parents and students as to the original intended purpose of the early exam process and further outlines acceptable reasons to request an early exam. This includes students who are moving out of the district prior to final exams or for students who are experiencing an unforeseen hardship such as a medical emergency or the death of a family member, etc.

Policy 5430 – Class Rank

  • The proposed amendment adds the HOPE course to the list of courses eligible to be deleted from the class ranking computation as it relates to the Valedictorian and Salutatorian designations. The HOPE course is an unweighted course, and in recent years, there are growing numbers of students who complete the HOPE course prior to their 9th grade year. Those students, if they are in contention for the Valedictorian/Salutatorian designations after the second semester of their senior year, have a mathematical grade point advantage over students who take the HOPE course after they enter high school.

RATIONALE:

The proposed amendments to these policies ensure alignment with district practices.

 

Policy 5420.03 – Reporting Student Progress and Class Ranking

  • Our schools are experiencing a significant uptick in requests for early exams for leisure travel which is not a qualifying reason for an early exam. This recommended change in policy allows for parents and students to better understand the intent of this policy in order to avoid submitting a request that does not meet the original intent of this policy.

Policy 5430 – Class Rank

  • Up until now, including the HOPE course has not impacted the computations and selections for Valedictorian/Salutatorian in our high schools, but the upward trend of HOPE completions prior to high school inevitably will cause an issue in the years to come. This recommended change will ensure we continue to have a level playing field for all students, regarding the inclusion of unweighted classes that are required for graduation, when it comes to the computation that identifies the Valedictorian and Salutatorian designations each school year.

Recommended Action
Approve the renewal of the Interlocal Agreement with Pinellas County for a temporary emergency County Staging Area (CSA).
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 3. Equity with Excellence for All
BACKGROUND:

As part of its Comprehensive Emergency Management Plan, Pinellas County Emergency Management desires the use of district-owned property located between the campus of High Point Elementary School and the campus of Pinellas Technical College – Clearwater as a temporary CSA on an as-needed basis. The purpose of this agreement is to provide for cooperation and coordination between Pinellas County Emergency Management and the district in carrying out joint responsibility to serve the citizens of Pinellas County during disasters.

 
RATIONALE:

A CSA is a temporary facility set up and operated by a county emergency management agency and is activated when the resource requirements of a response to and recovery from a disaster exceed what can be provided by normal supply chains. Resources shipped into the county from external sources may be shipped to a CSA as an initial delivery point for bulk loads of items for distribution and for equipment that will later be sent to points of use. A CSA may also be used as a temporary receiving location for incoming personnel teams to assemble before being assigned.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
