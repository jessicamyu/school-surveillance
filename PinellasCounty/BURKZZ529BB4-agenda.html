4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.01 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating March 1–5, 2021, as “Newspaper in Education” Week
Meeting
Feb 23, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action, Presentation, Proclamation
BACKGROUND:

Annually, during the first full school week in March, Newspaper in Education Week is celebrated.

 

Newspaper in Education (NIE) is an international program that encourages the use of the daily newspaper as an instructional classroom tool. Through NIE, newspapers are used with all classroom subjects as a progressive teaching resource for students from elementary through adult education levels.

 

The Tampa Bay Times Newspaper in Education program is a cooperative effort between schools and the Tampa Bay Times to encourage the use of newspapers as an educational resource. This informational text resource serves as a living textbook for all students. NIE serves educators and students throughout Tampa Bay by providing print and digital newspapers, curriculum supplements, teacher guides, educator workshops and many more resources to classrooms at no cost to schools, teachers or students.

 

The newspaper also provides teachers with contemporary informational texts that engage students and relate academic learning to real-world experiences. Students who learn using the newspaper as an educational resource become lifelong readers and grow up to become more informed and engaged citizens.

5.01 Meeting Procedures
Meeting
Feb 23, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Meeting

Recommended Action
Approve the Florida Inventory of School Houses Certification of Facilities Data.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:

Each year, school districts across the state are required to verify the usage of all district owned properties and report such use to the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) via the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH). The verification and reporting of FISH helps ensure that school districts are using spaces as authorized by the FDOE.

 

Between December 2020 and February 5, 2021, all Pinellas County Schools' principals were required to review, verify and submit any changes or corrections to their current FISH report. 

 

Facilities Planning staff was available to visit schools to assist in this process.  All schools have submitted their verification forms to the Coordinator, School Planning. 

 

RATIONALE:

The State Requirements for Educational Facilities (SREF) 2014, Chapter 6, Section 6.1(5)(c) requires that “Prior to April 1 of each year, each district shall review Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) and shall certify to the Office of Educational Facilities (OEF), the inventory is current and accurate.”

A public hearing is scheduled for instructional materials for the Florida English Language Arts (ELA) and Reading Standards. New materials for grades K-12 ELA and Reading were reviewed and considered for adoption during school year 2020-21. These materials are in consideration for adoption to align to the Florida B.E.S.T. (Benchmarks for Excellent Student Thinking) standards which will be implemented according to a graduated schedule over the next two school years (2021-22 and 2022-23). The courses for which materials were reviewed are grades K-5 ELA, 6-8 ELA and Intensive Reading, and 9-12 ELA and Intensive Reading, including Advanced Placement ELA courses. The review, adoption and subsequent purchase of new materials is pursuant to Pinellas County Schools (PCS) Policy 2510- Instructional Materials, Including Textbooks.

 

The adoption review committees were formed in September 2020 and comprised of teachers, school and district administrators and community members. These committees reviewed the submitted ELA and Intensive Reading digital and print materials from October 2020 to December 2020.  The reviewers utilized a rubric which focused on the alignment to the B.E.S.T. standards, Exceptional Student Education (ESE) and English Learners (EL) supports, cultural relevance, teacher support and supplemental materials including family and community engagement.  After a thorough review process, the adoption committees narrowed the selections to two finalists whose sample materials (digital and print) were then sent to all district schools for review from January to early-February 2021.  In mid-February, the individual schools each provided a recommendation of materials for adoption to their applicable District Content Specialist.  Each District Content Specialist compiled the recommendations and made a recommended adoption selection.

The School Board will consider the approval of the recommended materials at the April 13, 2021, School Board meeting.

Procedural

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
