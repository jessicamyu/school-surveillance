4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.1 Presentation of the Distinction of “Master Board” to the Pinellas County School Board
Meeting
Aug 25, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Presentation

The Florida School Boards Association (FSBA) strives to meet the needs of its membership by offering several types of support to its members.  One very important area of support is professional development. FSBA Leadership Services Team provides learning/growth opportunities for new school board members and those members wishing to continue to strengthen their service.  Training is available to equip the board members with the necessary tools and the ability to focus on student achievement.

 

The FSBA Master Board Program is a governance team training for the School Board and Superintendent.  Its purpose is to strengthen the leadership team’s ability to work effectively, efficiently and collectively as a governance body while maintaining a focus on student achievement and factors contributing to the success of schools.  Opportunities are provided for the team to discuss, improve or enhance those important interpersonal skills that are essential for a high-functioning team.

 

The leadership curriculum offers meaningful learning sessions concentrating on student achievement through the five components of the FSBA Governance Model.  Some of the skills, knowledge, processes and concepts addressed in the leadership curriculum include  supporting student achievement, self-assessment, consensus building, establishing trust, conflict resolution, leadership and communication styles, team development, core beliefs, roles and responsibilities, productive meetings, systems thinking, district culture, problem solving and strategic planning.

 

During the past year, the Pinellas County School Board and Dr. Grego have availed themselves of the program.  April Griffin, Leadership Development Consultant for the Florida School Boards Association, will present the board with the distinction of Master Board.

William P. Corbett, Ed.D., Deputy Superintendent

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), Part B, K-12 Entitlement Project ensures that all children with disabilities, ages three through twenty-one, have access to a free appropriate public education (FAPE). Programs and services are provided to meet each child's unique needs to, in turn, prepare them for further education, employment and independent living. IDEA funds are used to support the District Strategic Plan and the Exceptional Student Education Department goals of increasing high school graduation rates, academic success and post high school outcomes for students with disabilities (SWD), as well as reducing discipline disproportionality and identification as having emotional and behavioral disabilities for Black students. IDEA funds will be allocated for personnel to provide educational services to SWD, contracts with staff for specialized services, instructional materials and supplies, travel required for students and staff and professional development.

 

A portion of IDEA, Part B funds will also be used to provide Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CEIS).  These services are provided to students who are not currently identified as needing special education or related services, but who need additional academic and behavioral supports to succeed in a general education environment.  The US Department of Education requires 15% of IDEA funds be allocated for CEIS when there is a significant disproportionality in identification of students based on race or ethnicity.  An allocation for CEIS in the amount of $4,239,635 will be used to provide additional personnel, materials, and professional development that benefit non-disabled students. 

 

Federal guidelines also required a proportionate share of IDEA funds to be allocated for services to Parentally Placed Private School Students with Disabilities (PPPSSD).  For 2020-2021, the allocation is $2,257,273.24 of the IDEA funds.  The private school proportionate share provides the required personnel, training and materials to serve students with disabilities attending private schools.

 

Based on the most current available data, student achievement outcomes for students with disabilities have shown a trend of improvement in several areas. The graduation rate for students with disabilities increased from 67.1% for 2016-2017 to 72.5% for 2017-2018 and to 75.5% for 2018-2019.  The risk ratio for Black students identified as EBD was decreased over the same three-year period—3.92 in 2016-2017; 3.84 in 2017-2018; and 3.34 in 2018-2019. Additionally, there was a 2.3% increase in English Language Arts proficiency and a 3.2% increase in Math proficiency on the 2019 Florida Standards Assessment.

The district entered into a five-year contract with Cenergistic, Inc. in March 2015 to provide energy conservation consulting services to the district with the goal of reducing overall energy consumption and, in turn, overall energy expenditures.  To date, the district has been able to realize over $32 million in cost avoidance on its energy bills due to the joint efforts of the district and Cenergistic under the contract.  The original contract called for the district to equally share these savings with Cenergistic.  Cenergistic’s average annual share of these savings is approximately $3.26 million.  The contract also required Cenergistic to supply a team of Energy Specialists to run the program and provided other staff to serve as consultants to district personnel.  The current five-year contract expires on October 31, 2020.

 

This contract extension will ensure the continuation of the agreement with Cenergistic for a period of 36 months beginning on November 1, 2020. The extension continues to require Cenergistic to supply a team of Energy Specialists to run the program and provides the district access to other Cenergistic staff such as their engineering team. The extension includes two major amendments to the original contract; 1) Cenergistic has changed their fee structure from that of equally sharing the savings to that of a flat fee of $74,417 per month, and 2) the extension includes a guarantee that pays the district a calculated sum if the district’s energy use intensity increases during the contract provided the district is substantially adhering to the energy conservation program guidelines.

 
RATIONALE:

District staff has calculated that the expected cost to the district to self-manage an energy conservation program to be approximately $946,656 per year.  This extension with Cenergistic will cost approximately $899,051 per year.  Extension of the contract with Cenergistic will result in continued high levels of energy savings with no degradation in the gains made in energy consumption at a lower cost to the district than a self-managed program.

BACKGROUND:

During the 2016-2017 school year, Pinellas County Schools (PCS) committed to the implementation of Restorative Practices as a key strategy to become a more equitable district. Restorative Practices emphasizes the importance of positive relationships as central to building community and involves processes that repair relationships when harm has occurred. When consistently implemented, Restorative Practices will promote and strengthen positive school culture. In the 2016-2017 school year, PCS contracted with the International Institute for Restorative Practices (IIRP) to support and provide expertise in our implementation of restorative practices.

 

The IIRP is a leading provider of restorative practices professional development, coaching, consulting, graduate education and educational resources. IIRP instructors have extensive backgrounds across multiple sectors including education, criminal justice, youth development, leadership development, family counseling and youth counseling. During this initial year, IIRP provided training on essential elements of Restorative Practices to approximately 45 school and district staff.

 

The IIRP provides a 'training of trainers' (TOT) program, which trains and licenses local stakeholders to provide their own ongoing professional development services. In 2017-2018, IIRP provided TOT training to a select team of district staff in order for them to begin training school teams. In 2018-2019 and 2019-2020, IIRP continued to provide TOT training which has resulted in the district’s ability to have at least one IIRP licensed certified trainer in every school to lead our work in restorative practices. IIRP has also provided every trainer with the research-based materials necessary to support their role.

 

This contract will build on the work from 2019-2020 as the district continues to grow the depth of knowledge and practice of restorative practices. IIRP will continue to support all 120 Pinellas County schools through a series of online educational webinars covering the restorative practices’ essential elements. In addition, a cohort of twenty-schools will work toward becoming high-quality programs receiving online or in-person coaching, targeted professional development training designed to support school administrators and their restorative leadership teams and formal restorative justice conferencing. This will help them effectively champion restorative practices throughout their buildings.

 

Description of Services

 

Restorative Justice Conferencing

Bring together those impacted to resolve conflict

Effective responses to incidents of conflict, wrongdoing, and harm are the hallmark of restorative practices. The IIRP dedicates two (2) full days of in-person or an online experience — tailored for teachers, administrators and other school staff interested in facilitating conferences in their building. This includes how to facilitate a formal, scripted circle process to address racial incidents, bullying, vandalism, and other serious infractions. Participants will learn all the steps required to conduct a restorative justice conference, from determining the readiness of all stakeholders to participate to running the formal meeting itself.

 

Putting Theory into Practice for School Administrators

Build school climate with a network of motivated school leaders

Putting Theory into Practice for School Administrators is a six-week intensive online experience designed for principals, superintendents and other school administrators with leadership responsibility over school culture and climate. Led by an experienced IIRP Instructor, this event is designed to help put into practice what was learned during Basic Restorative Practices professional development. Throughout the six (6) weeks, participants will practice the full range of restorative practices and gain support for transforming relationships between students, teachers and other administrators. Readings, videos and lectures will be provided as supplementary material.

 

Restorative Practices Coaching

Moving from theory into practice proficiency

IIRP coaches will provide on-site or online learning experiences for each school according to gaps and needs. Each coaching session includes lesson plans with activities designed to fit within a class schedule. This coaching model is based upon a Multi-Tiered System of Supports (MTSS) framework, which differentiates staff by their roles with students. This Tier-3 level coaching is designed for a few targeted staff receiving support to evaluate and reinforce the full continuum of restorative practices throughout the school building. This was mostly designed for building Restorative Leadership Teams committed to deepening and sustaining restorative practices within the culture of the school. A cohort of 20 schools will each receive 48 hours of coaching throughout the year. This is a total of 960 hours or 120 days of coaching to be delivered to the entire 20-school cohort. Each session is an hour in duration and engages school leaders in building proficiency and the skills needed for supporting others in implementation fidelity.

 

Basic Restorative Practices Webinar Series

Professional Learning Through a Restorative Lens

Led by IIRP Instructors. This webinar series is designed for school-based staff to look closely at each essential element of restorative practices. The series will be offered throughout the school year with each session scheduled for an hour through the IIRP webinar platform.

 

Restorative Justice Conferencing Handbook

Restorative Justice Conferencing: Real Justice® & The Conferencing Handbook are two books in one volume. Restorative Justice Conferencing combines (1) the official training manual that provides a step- by-step guide to setting up and conducting conferences and (2) actual conference stories to show how conferencing works and how it can change the way our society responds to wrongdoing in schools, criminal justice, the workplace and elsewhere. Each of the 25 participants will receive their own copy of the book.

 

RATIONALE:

The IIRP has a long-demonstrated ability to deliver high-quality restorative practices professional development training in diverse settings across multiple sectors. Capacity building and leadership development are integrated into all the IIRP's services.

 

As the district is committed to grow the depth of knowledge and practice of restorative practices from 2019-2020, IIRP will continue to support all 120 Pinellas County schools through a series of online educational webinars covering the restorative practices’ essential elements. In addition, a cohort of twenty-schools will work toward becoming high-quality programs receiving online or in-person coaching, targeted professional development training designed to support school administrators and their restorative leadership teams and formal restorative justice conferencing. This will help them effectively champion restorative practices throughout their buildings.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
