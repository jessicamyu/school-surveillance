4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Recognizing May 2022 as Better Hearing and Speech Month
Meeting
Apr 12, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating May 2022 as Better Hearing and Speech Month.

BACKGROUND:

Every May the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association celebrates Better Hearing and Speech Month. This is an opportunity to raise awareness about communication disorders and the role that speech-language pathologists and audiologists have in schools. This year the theme is ‘Connecting People’.

 

Speech Language Pathologists (SLPs) make connections with people every day. They have integral roles in education and are essential members of school faculties. SLPs in Pinellas County Schools provide a continuum of educationally relevant services for students with speech and/or language disorders across all age levels. They provide language therapy for children so they may access their education by targeting areas of need such as interpersonal communication, augmentative communication, oral language and literacy skills. Speech therapy addresses speech sound disorders, stuttering and voice disorders. 

 

Audiologists assess and diagnose hearing loss. They may connect students and families to resources or equipment to meet a variety of needs. They recommend the appropriate device to accommodate for the degree of hearing loss so that the children may hear for communication and learning. Our audiologists are a community resource as well as a school resource; they provide hearing assessments for children from birth through graduation who reside in Pinellas County.

 

Each and every day, SLPs and audiologists work with the shared goal of helping children to achieve, communicate and connect with the world around them. The district and the departments of Exceptional Student Education and Specialized Services recognize May as Better Hearing and Speech Month. We recognize the Pinellas County speech-language pathologists and audiologists who provide support to students with exceptional needs related to speech-language and hearing, and we thank them for serving our students.  

 

SUBMITTED BY:

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

4.2 Recognition of 2021 Energy Star Certified Buildings
Meeting
Apr 12, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Recognition
Goals

BACKGROUND:

ENERGY STAR is a United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) program that helps businesses and individuals save money and protect our environment through superior energy efficiency. Using the latest in scientific research and statistical analysis, the ENERGY STAR building certification program allows owners to compare the energy efficiency of their buildings with similar buildings across the United States and Canada via a certified ENERGY STAR score. A building or school campus earning ENERGY STAR Certification has met strict energy performance standards, uses less energy, and is less expensive to operate while emitting fewer greenhouse emissions.

 

To qualify for ENERGY STAR Certification, these buildings and school campuses must score in the top 25 percent of similar buildings nationwide, with no sacrifice to comfort. The ENERGY STAR Building Certification is recognition and affirmation at the highest level of Pinellas County Schools' commitment to energy conservation and protection of our natural resources. In 2021, the district was awarded 59 ENERGY STAR building certifications. Each of these schools will receive an award suitable for display.  

 

District staff wishes to thank our energy conservation partner, Cenergistic, for their support in achieving this recognition, and also thank the Board for their support of the district energy conservation program.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Meeting

Type

Budget Source
The premium for the Property Insurance Program is paid through Risk Management and Insurance Department's annual operating budget and subsequently booked as a capital outlay expense.
Recommended Action
Approve the 2022-2023 Property Insurance Program, including Boiler and Machinery Insurance coverage effective April 15, 2022, through April 15, 2023.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:
Pinellas County Schools is required to carry property insurance, including contents and boiler and machinery coverage pursuant to Florida Statute Section 1001.42(11)(b)(8)(d).
 
The district's new property insurance broker, Gallagher Risk Services (Gallagher), markets the district's property insurance program, assists with property valuation, coordinates property insurance claims and provides loss control services. Our current property coverage is multi-layered with over 17 insurance carriers.
 
With a complete redesign of our multi-layered program through Gallagher, the 2022-2023 property premium is projected to not exceed $6.52M, which is a decrease of $740,000 (10%) from the district's 2021-2022 property premium.  
 
The Risk Management Insurance Review Committee met with Gallagher in December 2021 and in February 2022 to review the program, the district's insurance and premium constraints and the current and future property insurance market. The committee requested a fresh look at the layered model structure, the carrier selections, our schedule of values and any new policy options available in the marketplace. The committee was interested in solutions to reduce any anticipated premium increase, as the market had resulted in three (3) consecutive years of property rate increases, with an industry average 8.8% increase for employers during fourth quarter 2021. 
 
Employers continue to be faced with rising property insurance premiums. Between wildfires, civil commotion and major freeze events, carriers responded to 2020 and 2021 with increased deductibles, reduced capacity and reduction in coverages. The district's incumbent carriers requested rate increases between 9-15%. However, as a result of a redesign of our layered property coverage and utilizing Gallagher's direct relationships with domestic, retail and international carriers, the market competition for our property program was enhanced and the overall premium was reduced. In addition, the terms of our property policies were improved and multi-year options were offered by a primary layer insurer.
 
The 2022-2023 Property and Boiler and Machinery insurance proposal is as follows:

 

2022-2023 Proposed Property Coverage  $100 Million
Deductible; Property, all perils   $100 Thousand
Deductible; Windstorm/Hail/Storm Surge  5%
Total Insured Property Value as of 1/1/22 - (3% increase vs. last year)  $3.210 Billion
Percentage of Total Insured Value  3.2%
250 Year Probable Maximum Loss*  $204 Million
Percentage of Probable Maximum Loss Insured  49%
Cost of Insurance - Not to Exceed $6.52 Million

*250 Year Probable Maximum Loss Projection is based upon the average of two standard storm models (RMS-AIR)

 
RATIONALE:
Pinellas County Schools is required, under Florida Statute, to insure the district's property. In declared disasters, Federal Emergency Management Act (FMEA) funding will become available for our insurance deductibles and property loss exceeding our insurance limits, provided the district is appropriately insured.  
 
The Risk Management Insurance Review Committee comprised of the Associate Superintendent, Finance and Business Services; Associate Superintendent, Human Resources Services; Executive Director, Budget; Director, Risk Management and Insurance; and Safety and Loss Prevention Specialist, in consultation with Gallagher Risk Services recommends approval of the 2022-2023 Property Insurance Program. The committee reviewed the proposal provided to assure the coverage is competitive and appropriate for FEMA reimbursement.
 
Attached is the 2022-2023 Property, Boiler and Machinery Insurance program structure with required applications and the PCS Historical Overview of coverage and premiums during the last ten (10) years.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
