Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Section 1003.4205, Florida Statutes, entitled Disability History and Awareness Instruction, was signed into law in 2008.  It requires school districts to designate the first two weeks of October as Disability History and Awareness Weeks and also promotes providing instruction for students in all public schools to expand student knowledge, understanding and awareness of individuals with disabilities, disability history and the disability rights movement.

 

Throughout these two weeks, schools should support activities that:

  • Promote positive attitudes toward the successful inclusion of students with disabilities;
  • Increase respect and acceptance of individual differences;
  • Increase understanding of disabilities and related social issues; and
  • Facilitate the development of literacy and language arts skills through activities about disability awareness.

Disability History and Awareness Weeks are an opportunity to celebrate and focus on the abilities and positive contributions of students and adults with disabilities. It is also a time to thank individuals for the positive difference they make in the Pinellas school district and in the local community.

Meeting
Sep 28, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.

Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating October 2021 as Dyslexia Awareness Month
BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools recognizes the importance of addressing the unique educational needs of children with specific learning disabilities resulting from dyslexia. According to the International Dyslexia Association, dyslexia is the most common learning disability and affects 80 to 90 percent of all individuals with a learning disability. Early diagnosis of dyslexia is critical for ensuring that individuals with dyslexia receive focused, evidence-based intervention that leads to the promotion of self-awareness and self-empowerment and the provision of necessary accommodations to ensure school and life success. In accordance with the U.S. Department of Education and U.S. Senate, the Exceptional Student Education (ESE) department recognizes Dyslexia Awareness Month during the month of October in our schools.

 

The district has assembled a team of general and special education leaders, along with Student Services team members to address dyslexia needs.  The team trains staff, researches and strengthens curricula and strategies provided to students in order to meet the needs of students with dyslexia across the district.  This school year, the district celebrates the second successful year of the Center for Literacy Innovation at Elisa Nelson Elementary and the addition of two new centers at Midtown Academy and Palm Harbor Middle School. This 1st-6th grade program provides students with a systematic, multi-sensory, structured approach to literacy instruction for students with dyslexia and other reading difficulties. Continued legislation has brought a greater focus on dyslexia and provided additional resources to support students with dyslexia. The ESE department as well as our general education colleagues are proud to support our students in overcoming dyslexia.

BACKGROUND:

October is National Physical Therapy Month, sponsored by the American Physical Therapy Association (APTA). Physical therapists in public school systems assess and treat students between the ages of three and twenty-two who have conditions that limit their abilities to move and perform functional activities throughout their school day. Physical therapists develop a plan for each student using treatment techniques to promote the ability to move and/or restore function and collaborate with school-based staff throughout the process to increase functional performance across the entire school day.

 

Pinellas County Schools currently employs 21 physical therapists and physical therapist assistants to serve 500 students in all grade levels at 99 schools, including extended transition programs and Pinellas Virtual School.

 

The district recognizes the important role physical therapists and physical therapist assistants play in the success of students accessing the curriculum, learning daily and promoting success in transition programs. National Physical Therapy Month affords the district an opportunity to acknowledge a sincere appreciation and thanks for the efforts and work of each physical therapist and physical therapist assistant within Pinellas County Schools.

BACKGROUND:

The Florida Association of School Administrators, the National Association of Secondary School Principals and the National Association for Elementary School Principals have declared the month of October as 'National Principals Month'.

 

Research continues to demonstrate that school leadership is second only to teaching among school-related factors in its impact on student learning. Pinellas County recognizes the importance of our principals in leading our schools toward 100% student success. Over the years, the increased demands for accountability have led researchers to examine the relationship between principal quality and student outcome. Based on findings, these researchers declared that principals are second only to teachers as the most influential school-level factor in student achievement.

 

Effective principals influence a variety of school outcomes through their recruitment and motivation of quality teachers, ability to identify and articulate school vision and goals, effective allocation of resources and development of organizational structures to support instruction and learning. They are situational leaders who are called upon to serve in a variety of functions and do so ably and successfully.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Approve the 2021-2022 Uniform Student Assessment Calendars
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Florida State law, Section 1008.22(7), F.S., requires the Florida Department of Education to publish a uniform calendar each year that includes statewide assessment information. Using this calendar, each school district must establish schedules for the administration of all statewide, standardized assessments and district-required assessments. The districtwide assessment calendars must be approved by the School Board, included in parent notifications and published on the district website, per the statute.

 

  1. districtwide assessment calendars have been established for elementary, middle and high school levels and have been converted to display in the Uniform Statewide Assessment Calendar format, as required by law. Assessment calendars are posted on the Assessment, Accountability and Research webpage. The 2021-2022 calendars can be viewed here: https://www.pcsb.org/Page/15479

 

Each year the Executive Directors of Elementary, Middle and High School Education, along with other district assessment staff, review the calendars for adherence to all local and state requirements. Local assessments are chosen based on input from district and school staff in order to progress monitor and best support students in achieving state standards. These include final exams in middle and high school and the Measures of Academic Progress (MAP) in elementary schools.

 

RATIONALE:

Statute requires that no student have more than 5% of their total school hours in a year scheduled for required state and local assessments. The proposed calendars meet the requirements of state statute and ensure students are assessed on required statewide assessments, while not exceeding the 5% threshold for assessments. The range of required assessments is as low as 0.6% in kindergarten and no grade level exceeds 3.5% of required time.

 

Local assessments support students by providing actionable data for teachers through individual student and schoolwide progress monitoring.

4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:

School districts in Florida must receive approval from the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) prior to the commencement of any project which involves the construction of a facility or a change in the use or function of a facility.  FDOE approval is granted after a review of the needs of the school district via the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) reporting process.

 

In regard to the potential razing of a building, the FDOE’s Office of Educational Facilities (OEF) requires that school districts conduct a financial study known as a Castaldi Analysis to compare the costs of razing an educational facility versus the costs of rehabilitating an educational facility. School districts are permitted to raze a structure if the Castaldi Analysis shows it would be costlier to rehabilitate the structure as opposed to razing it and replacing it with new construction.

 

As part of the FISH approval process, the OEF requires districts to submit a Room Condition Change Building Replacement/Raze (RCC-BRR) form to certify that there is no debt on the property being reviewed and that the School Board is aware that the district is considering the razing of a building. Upon receipt of the RCC-BRR, the OEF will confirm the districts’ Castaldi Analysis prior to extending approval to raze the building.

 

District staff performed a Castaldi Analysis on Building 5 at Mildred Helms Elementary School and found it to be less costly to replace/raze than it would be to rehabilitate the whole building.  Staff also confirmed that there is no outstanding dept on this real property.

 

This request to submit the RCC-BRR form and conceptual permissions, if approved, will allow staff to move forward with the process of submitting the RCC-BRR form to the OEF.

 

RATIONALE:

By approving, certifying and submitting the attached form, RCC-BRR, to the Florida Department of Education’s OEF, the district will comply with requirements for standardized procedures and processes as established by OEF.

On October 13, 2020, the school board approved the district’s Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan for 2020-2021 through 2024-2025 and adopted the corresponding Five-Year Facilities Work Program, in accordance with 1013.41 (3), 1013.61 and 1013.35, Florida Statutes.

 

The Capital Outlay Committee and support staff developed this Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan, using the July 2019 Educational Plant Survey, the District’s Capital Outlay Guidelines and Prioritization System and broad-based input from school and district staff.

 

The Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan maintains balanced capital priorities and projected revenues by year, over the five-year period. The Tentative Facilities Work Program incorporates the Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan and related data on permanent and relocatable facilities into a specific format, as prescribed by the Florida Department of Education. The board-adopted Facilities Work Program must be submitted to the Florida Department of Education by October 1, 2021.

 

A summary of major changes in the Proposed Five-Year Plan is provided in the support materials and includes the following:

  1. Revenue Projections: Revenue projections have been updated to reflect the 2021 Legislative Session and other more recent revenue estimates. An increase in the tax roll has resulted in a projected increase of local capital outlay (1.5 Mil) revenue. No PECO maintenance dollars were allocated to traditional schools in fiscal year 2021-2022, as such, no PECO revenue is included in this Five-Year Plan. CO & DS and Interest Earning revenue was updated from the prior year estimates.
  1. “New” Year 5 (2025-2026): Consistent with statutory requirements, “New” Year 5 (2025-2026) estimated revenues and project allocations have been incorporated into the Five-Year Plan.
  1. Adjustments to other Capital Projects significant changes are:
    1. The plan reflects the addition of new capital projects as presented on the district’s Facilities “Five Year Capital Outlay Plan”. The five-year total is projected to be $199.5 million and is funded through local capital outlay proceeds (1.5 Mill) and CO & DS.
    1. The plan updates the principal and interest payments associated with the COPs issuance. The projected debt service, including principal and interest, is estimated at a five-year total of $41.1 million.

The Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan and Tentative Facilities Work Program, along with the related support materials, are attached.

 

RATIONALE:

Florida Statutes require the school district to annually prepare a Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan and Tentative Facilities Work Program containing specific data elements. The school board must also provide the opportunity for public comment and then adopt the Tentative Facilities Work Program prior to adopting its annual capital outlay budget. Once approved, the board is required to adopt any amendments to the Tentative Five-Year Facilities Work Program, after providing proper notice and opportunity for public comments on the proposed changes, and to annually update the Five-Year Facilities Work Program.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
