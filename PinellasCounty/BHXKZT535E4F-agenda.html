4.1 Presentation of Proclamation Acknowledging the Contribution of Our Teachers During Teacher Appreciation Week Presented by Paula Texel, Associate Superintendent, Human Resources Services
Meeting
Apr 28, 2020 - Virtual - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Presentation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources

BACKGROUND:

Since 1984, National PTA has designated the first full week in May as a special time to honor the men and women who lend their passion and skills to educating our children. May 4-8, 2020, is designated as “Teacher Appreciation Week” with “Teacher Appreciation Day” celebrated May 5, 2020.

 

Teachers affect the lives of students in so many ways. School is not only a place where children learn subject matter, but social learning as well. This can have a tremendous impact on how students interact with others now, and in the future. Teachers inspire, challenge and encourage his/her students to be all that they can be. Whether teaching kindergarten, biology, English or physical education, a teacher is someone who students look up to, and aspire to be like.

 

The events of early 2020 have required traditional schools around the nation to change their instructional model to a distance learning format. Teachers became students themselves, quickly learning the tools necessary to provide lessons remotely. Our teachers stepped up to the challenge with great success and continue to be there to support our students, parents and families.

 

We request the School Board of Pinellas County adopt the attached proclamation designating the week of May 4-8, 2020, as "Pinellas County Schools Teacher Appreciation Week" and May 5, 2020, as “Pinellas County Schools Teacher Appreciation Day.” The School Board of Pinellas County recognizes and thanks all of our dedicated teachers for their commitment to the highest academic standards, safe schools, and preparing students for life.

Recommended Action
Approve the RCC-BRR form and conceptual permission to replace/raze permanent Building 35 at Clearwater High School.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:

The Department of Education’s Office of Educational Facilities (OEF) requires that school districts conduct a financial study known as a Castaldi Analysis to compare the costs of razing an educational facility versus the costs of rehabilitating an educational facility.

 

Districts are permitted to raze a structure if the Castaldi Analysis shows it would be more costly to rehabilitate the structure as opposed to razing it and replacing it with new construction. The OEF requires districts to submit a Room Condition Change Building Replacement/Raze (RCC-BRR) form and conceptual permission to replace/raze permanent buildings as part of the permission process.

 

District staff performed a Castaldi Analysis on Building 35 at Clearwater High School and found it to be less costly to replace/raze then it would be to rehabilitate each building.

 

This request to approve the RCC-BRR form and conceptual permissions, if approved, will allow staff to move forward with the process of submitting the RCC-BRR form to OEF.

 
RATIONALE:

By approving, certifying and submitting the attached RCC-BRR form to the Department of Education, OEF, the district will comply with requirements for standardized procedures and processes as established by OEF.

Recommended Action
Approve the request for a fifteen-year, high-performing charter modification for Discovery Academy of Science Charter School.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Discovery Academy of Science, initially Pinellas School of Math and Science in the original charter application, was approved as a K-12 charter school by the Pinellas County School Board (PCSB) in 2011.  The PCSB approved a five-year charter renewal for Discovery Academy of Science (nonprofit: Galaxy Education, Inc.) on July 1, 2016, expiring June 30, 2021.  Florida statute states that a high-performing charter school can modify its existing contract to a fifteen-year term or renewal.  The school received high-performing status from the Florida Department of Education in 2017 and continues to be a high-performing charter school.  Discovery Academy of Science serves 500 students between its two Dunedin locations: north campus grades 2-8, south campus grades K-1.  The K-8 school plans to add grade 9 in August 2022, adding one high school grade each following year until reaching full capacity of 870 students in grades K-12.

 
RATIONALE:

Pursuant to s. 1002.331(2) F.S., the school earned high-performing charter school status by:

  • Receiving a school grade of A for at least two years and no grade below a B during each of the previous three years.
  • Receiving yearly audits that did not reveal any financial concerns for the last three years.

Obtaining high-performing status entitles the charter school several unique provisions including:

  • A fifteen-year modification to their charter term. This term may be modified in the event that the school receives a C or lower on the state accountability system any two years during the term of the charter.
  • Lowering the sponsor’s administrative fee to withholding two percent instead of the usual five percent.

Discovery Academy of Science has been receiving the lowered two percent administrative fee since attaining high-performing status in 2017. There is no additional financial impact to the district until the school begins to add high school grade levels in 2022.  The financial impact at that time would be loss of FTE funding based on the number of PCSB high school students who enroll in the charter high school.

Meeting

Recommended Action
Approve the request to deny the charter application for King Charter School.
BACKGROUND:
An application for King Charter School was submitted on February 3, 2020.  This is the third application submitted by the applicant.  The two prior applications were denied by the Pinellas County School Board due to numerous "Does Not Meet the Standards" ratings. 
 
The application is for the same type of charter school presented in the two prior applications.  The application is for a K-5 charter school that would open with 108 students in grades K-2 in the first year, adding a grade level each following year until reaching full capacity of 360 students in five years. "The mission of King Charter School is to bring the best of public education to the Pinellas community by providing a trilingual education in an eco-friendly atmosphere so that each child recognizes the benefit of global sustainability." Although a location is not required during this phase, the application states the school will be in the Pinellas Park area, or nearby vicinity.
 
RATIONALE:
Charter school applications are subject to review by the sponsor in accordance with specific requirements spelled out in 1002.33(6) F.S. In response to these statutory requirements, district staff reviewed the application based on the twenty-two (22) Standards and Addendum section of the Florida Charter School Application Evaluation Instrument.  There was no improvement between the first and second application rating results, the first having 11 Does Not Meet the Standards and the second having 12 Does Not Meet the Standards.  The results of the third evaluation are listed below:
 
0 - Meets the Standard
8 - Partially Meets the Standard
11 - Does Not Meet the Standard
3 - Not Applicable
 
A capacity interview to determine the applicant's "capacity to open and maintain a high-quality charter school" was held on April 8, 2020.  The applicant team's interview responses were taken into consideration in the overall application rating. The application fails, and has failed in all three submissions, to demonstrate the capacity to effectively operate a public charter school in the state of Florida, provide appropriate services to students, ensure academic accountability measures are in place that function to improve student academic progress and achievement and demonstrate the school can sustain itself with a viable financial and staffing plan while being fiscally responsible with taxpayer dollars.  The district's evaluation instrument is included in this packet.
