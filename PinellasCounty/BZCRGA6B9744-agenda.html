Subject
4.1 Presentation of Proclamation Recognizing November 2021 as Family Engagement in Education Month
Meeting
Nov 9, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approval of proclamation designating November 2021 as Family Engagement in Education Month.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

November is designated as Family Engagement in Education Month, a time to celebrate the vital role parents and families play in their children's education. When schools and families work together to support learning, students are more motivated to succeed-not just in school, but throughout life.

 

The pandemic has placed a laser-focus on the importance of families in student learning. The American Rescue Plan offers a variety of opportunities for the district to expand family engagement strategies and practices to make them more equitable for the long term. Pinellas County Schools is committed to partnering with families for the successful academic, social and emotional development of children through education, empowerment and support. The Office of Strategic Partnerships has worked diligently to facilitate a new way of work by establishing a district Family Engagement Team, with representatives from many district departments, to guide and facilitate family engagement across the district.  The district team is focusing on a pilot program with our Reading Recovery students and their families, to both support these 1st grade students to meet and maintain grade-level expectations in literacy and, at the same time, gain valuable information about how to successfully implement family-school partnerships as an instructional strategy.

 

Additionally, schools across Pinellas County have developed Family Engagement Action Teams to encourage and maximize family engagement opportunities. These opportunities, provided in a variety of formats, include face-to-face workshops, webinars, social media, home visits and videos on key topics. At each interaction, parents learn and practice new skills and strategies to support learning at home, while also sharing and incorporating knowledge about their child. Furthermore, the Office of Strategic Partnerships provides training to school leaders, teachers, support staff and parents on effective strategies to foster positive communication and authentic collaboration between families and school to increase student achievement.

 

Family Engagement in Education Month is a time to celebrate the accomplishments of our successful family and school partnerships, which are built and sustained throughout the year.

 

SUBMITTED BY:
Lori Matway, Associate Superintendent, Student and Community Support Services

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Budget Source
The $600,000 annual cost for services will be paid by insurance carriers directly to the broker/consultant through commissions generated through placement of insurance policies.
Recommended Action
Approve to appoint Gallagher as the district's risk management and insurance broker/consultant for property and casualty insurance effective January 1, 2022, through December 31, 2025, with an option to renew for four additional years.

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools currently utilizes USI Services for our property and casualty broker/consultant. Their current agreement expires December 31, 2021.

 

In accordance with Florida Statute, Risk Management and Insurance maintains a program that combines self-insurance and procured insurance to protect the assets of Pinellas County Schools. Our broker/consultant assists the district to formulate a cost-effective risk strategy and structure based upon current market conditions. They market, negotiate and recommend coverage to include, but not limited to:

 

  • Property insurance for the district's total insured values of $3.1 billion of real property
  • Boiler and Machinery
  • Cyber and Privacy Liability
  • Builders Risk (as necessary)
  • Auto and General Liability
  • Educators Legal Liability
  • Law Enforcement Liability
  • Petroleum Liability
  • Crime

 

The broker/consultant will provide district assistance with analysis of loss trends, recommendations and strategies for loss prevention, and furnish the annual actuarial report for budget and auditing purposes.

 

On August 18, 2021, our purchasing department, at the request of the risk management and insurance department, released Intent to Negotiate ITN#22-946-017 for Property and Casualty Insurance Broker and Consulting Services. The district received seven responses from leading insurance brokers. The selection committee evaluated the responses on September 7, 2021, and heard oral presentations on September 17, 2021, from three finalists: USI Services, Marsh USA, Inc., and Arthur J. Gallagher Risk Management Services, Inc. dba Gallagher. After the oral presentations, USI Services and Gallagher were selected for further negotiations. On October 8, 2021, the selection committee met, and recommended Gallagher based upon the scoring, presentations and further negotiations and discussions with the finalists.

 

RATIONALE:

Due to the complexities of the property insurance market, the districts' risk of many varied exposures, and the high cost of insurance premium, it is essential the district have a broker/consulting firm with a broad range of resources, leverage and licensed experts in this industry.

 

The following factors were considered in the recommendation of Gallagher:

 

  • A global leading broker/consulting firm, with a strong presence in the Florida market representing over 37 Florida School districts.
  • Dedicated team of risk management professionals to provide all required service including insurance brokerage, loss prevention, loss analytics, catastrophic loss modeling, claims advocacy and experience with large disaster recovery claims in coastal Florida School districts.
  • Relationship with worldwide insurance marketplace to market and place complicated coverages with less reliance on intermediaries.
  • Specific expertise in creating rolling, multiyear property programs to insulate the district from significant premium changes following market changing events, such as a hurricane or terrorist attack.
  • Competitive pricing.

 

The proposed property and casualty consulting firms' fees are $600,000 annually, including the renewal period. This is approximately $117,000 less than the amount we are paying for similar services this year. The fees will be paid by insurers through commissions built into our property insurance policies.

Recommended Action
Approve the agreement with the School Board of Putnam County on behalf of the North East Florida Educational Consortium, October 1, 2021, through September 30, 2022, and authorize district administration to finalize, implement and amend the grant as needed.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

In 2017, the U.S. Department of Education awarded a State Personnel Development Grant (SPDG) to the state of Florida and charged the North East Florida Educational Consortium (NEFEC) with implementing the Strategic Instruction Model Project portion of the grant.  The purpose of the grant is to support evidence-based strategies that will lead to increased graduation and proficiency rates for students with disabilities (SWD).

 

During 2021-2022, Pinellas County Schools will be in its fifth year of implementation for this five-year grant.  As one of 14 participating SPDG districts, PCS receives a portion of SPDG funds to coordinate and facilitate professional development for teachers in participating districts on the Strategic Instruction Model™ (SIM).  This project aligns with our districtwide focus on improving learning outcomes and narrowing achievement gaps for SWD.  Other districts served by this project are Baker, Bradford, Brevard, Charlotte, Citrus, DeSoto, Escambia, Gadsden, Hendry, Hernando, Holmes, Polk and Volusia, with Putnam County serving as fiscal agent.

 

BUDGET:  
Personnel (Salaries & Fringe) $    354,678

   ESE Specialist

  
   Instructional Staff Developer (2)  

   Secretary II

  
Materials and Supplies $      41,902
Travel & Registrations $      11,200
Other Purchased Services $      76,650
   
Indirect Costs $      23,094
   
TOTAL: $    507,524

 

RATIONALE:

The SIM™ appears in Evidence for Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) as an evidence-based instructional technique that increases the proficiency of all students, including students with disabilities, when implemented with fidelity.  Results from participating schools implementing SIM™ Xtreme Reading Learning Strategies showed that 70% of students enrolled in Xtreme sections were assessed at Level 1 on the Florida Standards Assessment in English Language Arts during the 2018-2019, with 30% assessed at Level 2.  These students received Xtreme Reading intervention during the 2020-2021 school year.  On the 2021 FSA ELA assessment, 47% scored at a Level 1, 41% scored at a Level 2, 10% scored at a Level 3 and 2% scored at a Level 4.

BACKGROUND:

The Schools Police Communications Center is located within the Pinellas County Schools Police command station and operates twenty-four hours a day, seven days a week. Staffing consists of seven dispatchers and one supervisor; all of whom are certified 911 Public Safety Telecommunicators.

 

The Schools Police Communications Center is in direct connection with Schools Police Officers; Pinellas County Sheriff's Deputies; police officers from St. Petersburg, Gulfport, Pinellas Park, Largo, Clearwater and Tarpon Springs; and each of our school facilities in both emergency and non-emergency situations. In addition, they are responsible for the continual monitoring of all district security cameras, tracking of the Global Positioning System (GPS) on Schools Police vehicles and school buses, card access control and intrusion, active assailant and fire alarms.

 

The communications center is also responsible for the testing of various alarms, monitoring classroom intercom button activations; making weather notifications; processing and coordinating FortifyFL and Sandy Hook See Something, Say Something anonymous reporting apps; and coordinating and responding to mobile activations of the “Alyssa’s Alert” SaferWatch app.

 

The communications center currently has one command console to control and monitor all of these functions.

 

Motorola Solutions operates under Florida State Contract No.# 43190000-18-NASPO-ACS-1 and is the sole provider of the proposed equipment needed to upgrade the communications center. The Pinellas County Sheriff's Office, the Pinellas County 911 System and the St. Petersburg Police Department utilizes this equipment for countywide compatibility.

 

Motorola Solutions will:

  • Upgrade the existing radio console hardware in the amount $15,310
  • Install, configure and connect two radio consoles in the amount $164,297.34
  • Install an antenna between the county’s Public Safety Complex site (911 Center) and the Pinellas County Schools Police dispatch center in the amount $9,252
  • Provide labor services for the two radio consoles in the amount $119,688; the existing radio console in the amount $4,400; and an antenna in the amount $83,330
  • Confirm the functionality and performance of installed equipment in addition to training staff

Pinellas County Schools maintenance department will:

  • Demolish the existing communications center and install new electrical wiring in the amount of $50,000
RATIONALE:

The Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School Public Safety Act and other requirements as enacted by the legislature and the Office of Safe Schools have necessitated the expansion of our communications center to better and more efficiently accomplish the tasks required of the Schools Police dispatchers. The Schools Police communications center is currently equipped with a single Motorola dispatch radio console which allows a single dispatcher to communicate, dispatch and coordinate personnel and resources to various situations. Three dispatchers are on duty in the communications center during the school day.

 

The addition of the two new radio dispatch consoles and the necessary space modifications will allow for more efficiency and decrease the likelihood for errors while increasing the ability to maintain safety and allow students to learn in a safe learning environment. These upgrades will allow each dispatcher to have his/her own console.

 

In the case of an emergency, the new communications center equipment and configuration will allow for a dispatcher to handle the emergency while the other dispatchers can handle other tasks and address other needs of the district or a second emergency if one were to present itself.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
