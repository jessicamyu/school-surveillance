Subject
4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation of National African American History Month, February 2021
Meeting
Jan 26, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Presentation, Proclamation
BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools celebrates the many achievements and contributions of local, state, and national African American figures throughout the year and particularly during the month of February, National African American History Month. This is a time to highlight the struggles, successes and contributions of the African American and Black community in the United States. The theme for African American History Month 2021 is, “The Black Family: Representation, Identity and Diversity." Additional African American History curriculum resources and professional development opportunities tied to this year’s theme are provided by the Social Studies and Equity teams to all schools through the support of many community partnerships.

 

Since 2015, the Commissioner of Education’s African American History Task Force has recognized Pinellas County Schools as an Exemplary School District for our work to maximize the integration of African American History into our curriculum. To date, the approved exemplary status has only been awarded to ten school districts in the state of Florida. This approval is based on evidence of structured professional development for teachers, students, and school staff in strategies for teaching African American History, integration of African American History into required curriculum frameworks, and strong community partnerships, among other factors.

 

Throughout the year, teachers and other district staff have received increased support as they develop and implement more equitable instructional practices and resources related to African American History. Our Principals Multicultural Advisory Committee (PMAC) students are challenged to take the lead at the school level each February.  Examples of student led activities include promoting African American History Month through informational posters set up in public spaces, cultural celebrations, and morning announcements that highlight the significant contributions of African Americans.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

BACKGROUND:
February has been designated as Student Services Month, a time to recognize and celebrate the critical contribution of Student Services Professionals in our schools and the role they play in helping to mold and educate our children.  Student Services Month is one way of highlighting how school counselors, nurses, social workers, psychologists, educational diagnosticians and prevention staff and crisis response members who serve as liaisons between the home, school and community. 
 
The hard work, commitment and invaluable contributions made by the Student Services staff have been vital to our students' academic and emotional success particularly during these difficult times.  Student Services Professionals facilitate conditions for enhanced teaching and learning by providing supports that strengthen the bond between families and schools. They play an integral part in the success and education of all our children by increasing enrollment in advanced placement, dual enrollment and other rigorous courses; aligning pathway with the student's aptitude and ability; and providing social-emotional support throughout the developmental process. 
 
We appreciate the Student Services staff each and every day for their invaluable contribution and will formally recognize them during the month of February.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools has been using the Marzano Focused Teacher Evaluation Model since 2018 and was approved by the Pinellas County School Board at that time. The Focused Teacher Evaluation Model is a revised version of the research-validated Marzano Teacher Evaluation Model created by a partnership between Robert J. Marzano and Learning Sciences International. Florida school districts are required to submit substantial changes of their Instructional Personnel Evaluation System to the Florida Department of Education for review and approval.

 

RATIONALE:

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, teachers are now providing instruction through a variety of teaching methods, including virtual learning and traditional in-person learning. Adjustments needed to be made to our evaluation plan for the 2020-2021 school year to provide the necessary support for both teachers and administrators.

 

The revised evaluation plan for Pinellas County Schools was reviewed and approved by FDOE for use in the 2020-2021 school year and includes the following adjustments:

  • The number of required formal observations has been decreased by one.
  • Deliberate Practice Plans are not required and are not part of the final evaluation score.
  • The Instructional Practice Score weight has been increased to 66.7%.
  • The student data to be used for the mid-year evaluation of a newly hired teacher is agreed upon by the evaluator and teacher at the beginning of the year, or prior to the first formal observation.
  • Final Evaluation Scores are electronically acknowledged through iObservation

The recommended adjustments were based on input provided by members of the Appraisal Advisory Committee. This group has representation from a variety of stakeholders including Pinellas Classroom Teachers Association (PCTA); Assessment, Accountability, and Research (AA&R); Exceptional Student Education; Professional Development; Student Services; Instructional Staff; Teaching and Learning Services; and Human Resources Services.

Approve the first amendment to the agreement with Naviance, Inc. for the 2021-2023 school years.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness
BACKGROUND:

Naviance is a college- and career-readiness platform for middle and high school students that utilizes postsecondary planning to assist students and families to reach their college and career goals.  A major component of the Naviance implementation plan is to integrate the Naviance scope and sequence curriculum into the year-long school counseling comprehensive plan for middle and high schools. This consists of counselor seminars and in-class lessons which include opportunities for students to explore Naviance, establish their postsecondary and career goals, and learn about themselves, potential careers, college majors and scholarships.  The 6-12th grade scope and sequence curriculum outlines grade specific Naviance tasks that provide opportunities for student self-discovery and career and college exploration. School counselors introduce the Naviance curriculum through English/Language Arts classes and extends access independently to staff, students and families to explore the curriculum at home.

 

RATIONALE:

Pinellas County staff, students and families received access to the Naviance curriculum for a full year at no cost.  During this trial period, staff determined that this component of the Naviance suite was essential for student success; therefore, they recommended an amendment to the current Naviance contract to include unlimited access to the Naviance curriculum for middle and high school students and staff for the remaining two years of the existing Naviance contract. The use of this curriculum has resulted in increased number of students completing the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA), applying to college, and other career pathways activities.

4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

College Board officially launched the Pre-Advanced Placement Program (Pre-AP) nationally in 2018-19. Pre-AP courses provide grade-level appropriate instruction through focused course frameworks, engaging instructional resources and learning checkpoints. Pre-AP courses challenge students to build relevant critical thinking skills through close observation and analysis, evidence-based writing, higher-order thinking and academic conversation. The approaches to learning that are implemented in the courses are closely aligned to our strategic goals for delivering culturally relevant instruction in every classroom for every student and increasing the diversity of all advanced, honors and accelerated courses to mirror that of the school.

 

When a school commits to Pre-AP courses, they receive focused course frameworks that have the space for our curriculum leaders to ensure Florida standards are embedded in their entirety; engaging instructional resources that include model lessons with activities, engaging texts, and challenging practice; formative learning checkpoint assessments and an optional final exam; course-specific professional learning for teachers to review the course frameworks and best practices for implementation; leadership modules to support leaders in understanding the instruction design and program requirements; and a series of free webinars that are updated regularly to provide relevant information related to updates and/or additional model lessons/best practices.

 

Pre-AP courses will further our mission to build the academic confidence of our students so they feel comfortable engaging in an Advanced Placement course to potentially earn college credit while still in high school. When schools adopt Pre-AP courses, they must attest that they will provide Pre-AP courses for ALL students so that all are challenged at the same rigorous level, helping us overcome the issue that the level of rigor some students receive in instruction is not as strong as what other students receive. By offering Pre-AP World History and Geography and Pre-AP Visual Arts in middle school we are diversifying the population of students who will opt into courses in these areas early in their high school years. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
