4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating April 2021 as National Occupational Therapy Month
Meeting
Apr 13, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Presentation, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating April 2021 as National Occupational Therapy Month.

BACKGROUND:

Every April, the American Occupational Therapy Association (AOTA) celebrates National Occupational Therapy Month. Occupational therapy is a unique profession in that it helps people across their lifespan to do the things they want and need to do through the therapeutic use of daily activities (occupations). Occupational therapy practitioners enable people of all ages to live life to its fullest by helping them promote health and prevent, or live better with, injury, illness or disability.

 

In our schools, Occupational Therapists (OTs) assess and treat students to improve their fine motor, visual motor, daily living and organizational skills to promote access to and engagement in their curriculum and performance of daily activities. Just as important, the therapists collaborate with and provide strategies to teachers and behavior specialists to assist with the varying sensory needs of the students. Occupational Therapist Assistants (OTAs) support all treatment techniques as identified by the Occupational Therapist.

 

Pinellas County Schools currently employs sixty-five (65) Occupational Therapists (OT) and Occupational Therapist Assistants, serving approximately 2,200 students in all grade levels.

 

Each year, Occupational Therapy staff find creative ways to educate coworkers, administrators, parents and students about strategies which impact so many aspects of a student’s daily life. Below is just a sampling of ways staff will celebrate Occupational Therapy month in their schools this April:

  • Distribute flyers about Occupational Therapy, with information from the American Occupational Therapy Association, highlighting the amazing things occupational therapy can do to support our students.
  • Oversee quizzes and fun activities that allow staff and students to learn about their Occupational Therapy staff and more about OT in the school setting.
  • The OTs work with classes to introduce sensory-based activities for all the students, like S'Cool Moves® and Brain Gym®, that teachers can incorporate throughout the school day to keep students focused for learning.
  • Include information about OT and ideas for teachers in the school’s weekly newsletter.

The district recognizes the important role Occupational Therapists and Occupational Therapist Assistants play in the success of students accessing their curriculum and performing daily activities required in their school environment. National Occupational Therapy Month affords the district an opportunity to acknowledge a sincere appreciation and thanks for the efforts and work of each Occupational Therapist and Occupational Therapist Assistant within Pinellas County Schools.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Approve the proclamation designating April 2021 as National Autism Awareness Month.

BACKGROUND:

Since 1970, April has been designated as National Autism Awareness Month by the National Autism Society in order to highlight the growing need for awareness about autism. The United States recognizes April as a special opportunity to educate the public about autism, issues of the autism community, as well as to spotlight the needs of individuals and families impacted by autism.

 

Autism is a complex spectrum disorder that impacts an individual's ability to communicate and interact with others. The prevalence of autism continues to grow, making awareness and acceptance ever more important. The Center for Disease Control and Prevention reported currently the incidence of autism has risen to approximately one in 59 births in the United States and boys are four (4) times more likely to be diagnosed than girls. Pinellas County Schools serves approximately 1,600 students identified on the autism spectrum. The district serves students with autism across a continuum of supports in center schools, self-contained classrooms and inclusive classrooms. This year, District Behavior Analysts have initiated a new process to support the more intensive behavioral needs of students with autism and other disabilities called Practical Functional Assessment and Skill-based Treatment using a student/family-focused and trauma-informed approach. To learn more, please visit the article links below:

  • Practical Functional Assessment for Parents
  • A Perspective on Today’s ABA from Dr. Greg Hanley

Pinellas County Schools will continue to develop and improve quality programs and services to meet the individual needs of students with autism and their families. Real change for awareness and acceptance comes through uniting all members of a community. In order to celebrate Autism Awareness Month, the district has provided suggestions to schools on ways to unite communities and schools in spreading awareness, promoting acceptance and igniting change. Some of these activities include:

  • All students in the school to learn about and take the “commitment to kindness” pledge. This National Autism Month, it's time to light up the world with kindness!
  • Light It Up Blue Day – Commitment of the entire school to wear blue in recognition of Autism Awareness Month.
  • Offer volunteer opportunities for students to become a Sensory Savvy Advocate and create sensory bins for their classmates.
  • Autism Internet modules for professional development are provided to teachers and administrators to help them understand the underlying characteristics of ASD and effective practices that will support their student’s needs.
  • Parent Training Opportunities to families by the district Low Prevalence team, on how to work with their children to promote positive behaviors within the home aligned to their behavior plan. Access to the Microsoft Teams April 14, 2021, (6–8 p.m.) training link is located on the PCS webpage.
  • Take advantage of the multiple resources available through the use of www.autismspeaks.org.

BACKGROUND:

April 2021 is designated the 35th annual National School Library Month.

 

National School Library Month is the American Association of School Librarians' (AASL) celebration of school librarians and their programs. First sponsored in 1958, National School Library Month is sponsored by the American Library Association (ALA) and observed in libraries across the country each April. National School Library Month is also a time to recognize the contributions of our nation's libraries and library workers and to promote library use and support.

 

During this month, school library media specialists host activities to help their school and local community celebrate the essential role that strong school library programs play in transforming learning. The 2021 theme, ‘Welcome to Your Library’ promotes the idea that libraries extend far beyond the four walls of a building and that everyone is welcome to use their services.

 

This year, our libraries have also served as a hub of PCS Connects activities, with library media specialists assisting students and families access high-quality online resources. We are continuing to transform media centers into 21st Century facilities by providing hands-on support and informative decision-making with style and enthusiasm.

 

Pinellas County Schools celebrates our library media specialists and their commitment to students and families. Through their promotion of literacy, technology and research skills through collaboration, creativity and engagement, library media specialists truly make a positive impact on student achievement each and every day.

ENERGY STAR is a United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) program that helps businesses and individuals save money and protect our environment through superior energy efficiency. Today, the EPA is recognizing Pinellas County Schools with the 2021 ENERGY STAR Partner of the Year Award, for demonstrating exemplary commitment and dedication to leadership in energy efficiency and the ENERGY STAR program. This is the third consecutive year that Pinellas County Schools has been recognized by the EPA as an ENERGY STAR Partner of the Year. Pinellas County Schools is one of only six (6) school districts across the nation to receive these accolades for their continued commitment to energy efficiency.

 

District staff wishes to thank our energy conservation partner, Cenergistic, for their support in achieving this recognition, and also thank our Board for their support of the district energy conservation program.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Pinellas County Schools is required to carry property insurance, including contents, and boiler and machinery coverage pursuant to Florida Statute Section 1001.42(11)(b)(8)(d).
 
USI Insurance Services, the district's property insurance broker, and their property wholesale partner, AmWINS Group, Inc., annually markets the district's property insurance program, assists with property valuation, coordinates property insurance claims and provides loss control services.   $100 thousand

Recommended Action
Approve the School Resource Officer Agreements with the Sheriff of Pinellas County, the City of Pinellas Park, the City of Largo, the City of Gulfport, the City of St. Petersburg, the City of Tarpon Springs and the City of Clearwater.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment

BACKGROUND:

The School Resource Officer (SRO) Agreements entered into between the School Board and the seven agencies serving the middle and high schools within the District expired on June 30, 2020, but provided that the parties could automatically continue the Agreements until replacement Agreements were approved. The SRO Agreements also provided that if the Board approves an increase to the salary of the Pinellas County Schools Police during the term of the Agreement, the same percentage increase would apply to the amount payable for each officer under the respective SRO Agreements. On October 27, 2020, the School Board approved a 3.25% increase in the salary for Schools Police resulting in the amount payable for each SRO increasing from $61,412 to $63,407.96. The new agreements contain substantially the same provisions but provide for three-year terms expiring on June 30, 2023.

 

The Sheriff of Pinellas County will assign a total of twenty-two (22) deputies, two (2) to serve at Dunedin, East Lake, Hollins, Osceola, Palm Harbor University and Seminole high schools. Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
