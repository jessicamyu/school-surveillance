Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak during the public hearing and regarding the topic of the public hearing.

 

In each case, you

  1. Must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below;
  2. Must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers for that speaking opportunity or before the last speaker for that opportunity concludes their comments, whichever is later;
  3. Will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee;
  4. Will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson or a majority vote of the Board; and
  5. May not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.

 

The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. If a speaker wants to supplement their three (3) minute presentation, the speaker should provide the Board office with written materials prior to the meeting. Board members will make every effort to review these materials. Board members may not be able to review materials received less than two (2) business days prior to the Board meeting.
  2. The Board will not act on any matter raised by a speaker prior to the next regular meeting. If a majority of the Board desires to further consider a matter raised by a speaker, they will request the Superintendent to bring a recommendation at a future workshop or meeting or otherwise address the issue. It is generally the Board's practice not to respond to the issues brought forward by speakers except to correct inaccuracies or clarify issues.
  3. Speakers may not use any of the speaking opportunities to advertise or otherwise promote the interests of any commercial or other non-school organizations, except when recognizing school or district partners or when one of the exceptions in Policy 9700.01 applies. Further, no speaker may advertise or otherwise promote a political candidate, question or party.

 

Regulation of Disruptive Speech

 

Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.

 

To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive or otherwise interferes with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, as stated in s. 1001.372(3), Florida Statutes; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

 

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.

 

Recording of Board Meetings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), Part B Entitlement Project ensures that all children with disabilities, ages three through twenty-one, have access to a free appropriate public education (FAPE) to meet each child’s unique needs.  This, in turn, will prepare each child for further education, employment and independent living. The IDEA funds are used to support the district strategic plan and the Exceptional Student Education (ESE) department goals of increasing the high school graduation rate, increasing academic success, improving post-high school outcomes and reducing discipline and Emotional/Behavioral Disability (EBD) eligibility disproportionality for students with disabilities and Black students. Use of the IDEA funds will include personnel to provide services to students with disabilities, contracted services for specialized services, instructional materials and supplies, travel required for students and staff and professional development for staff. 

 

The US Department of Education requires 15% of the IDEA funds be allocated for Comprehensive Coordinated Early Intervening Services (CCEIS) to students not identified with disabilities to prevent disproportionality in identification of students from one race compared to the remaining population of students in Pinellas County. The CCEIS portion is $4,313,748.75 of the total IDEA budget. This allocation funds personnel, materials and professional development that benefit non-disabled students.

 

The district is also required to allocate a proportionate share for services to Parentally Placed Private School Students with Disabilities (PPPSSD) based on the survey 2 count Full-Time Equivalent (FTE) of Students with Disabilities (SWD). For 2022-2023, the allocation is $2,344,877.55 of the IDEA funds. The private school proportionate share provides the required personnel, training and materials to serve students with disabilities in private schools.

 

RATIONALE:

The IDEA, Part B K-12 Entitlement grant funds are used to supplement the excess costs associated with providing special education and related services to students with disabilities. Without this grant the school district would incur these costs.

Recommended Action
Approve the request to submit the RCC-BRR form to the Florida Department of Education for a raze of Buildings 1 and 2 at the Center for Professional Education and certify that there are no outstanding debts on said buildings.
Goals
BACKGROUND:

School districts in Florida must receive approval from the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) prior to the commencement of any project which involves the construction of a facility or a change in the use or function of a facility.  FDOE approval is granted after a review of the needs of the school district via the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) reporting process.

 

In regard to the potential razing of a building, the FDOE’s Office of Educational Facilities (OEF) requires that school districts conduct a financial study known as a Castaldi Analysis to compare the costs of razing an educational facility versus the costs of rehabilitating an educational facility. School districts are permitted to raze a structure if the Castaldi Analysis shows it would be costlier to rehabilitate the structure as opposed to razing it and replacing it with new construction.

 

As part of the FISH approval process, the OEF requires districts to submit a Room Condition Change Building Replacement/Raze (RCC-BRR) form to certify that there is no debt on the property being reviewed and that the School Board is aware that the district is considering the razing of a building. Upon receipt of the RCC-BRR, the OEF will confirm the districts’ Castaldi Analysis prior to extending approval to raze the building.

 

This request to submit the RCC-BRR form, if approved, will allow staff and the OEF to work together to secure the necessary approval to raze the Center for Professional Education.

 
RATIONALE:

The Center for Professional Education building has been the primary location of professional development trainings and seminars for many years. The buildings' size and configuration limit the types of trainings that can be held onsite which often forces the professional development staff to find alternate locations for training.  Scheduling these alternate locations for training has become challenging as schools continue to expand the number of after-hour programs and learning opportunities for their students and the community.  It is the desire of the district to create a new training center for all employees on the site of the current building which could host large group trainings for up to 250 individuals or host multiple smaller trainings all under one roof. 

 

The Florida legislature included a $5 million appropriation to Pinellas County Schools for construction of a new professional development center in the 2022 state budget which was approved by the governor in May 2022.  A raze of the current Center for Professional Education is necessary in order to proceed with the construction of the new center.

Recommended Action
Approve the First Addendum to Agreement with 3DE Florida, LLC and Junior Achievement of Tampa Bay, Inc.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools is expanding its partnership with Junior Achievement and their career academy model, 3DE, by adding two additional 3DE school-based programs over the next two years. The joint venture and this expansion aim to further the district's mission of ensuring all students are prepared for college, career and life beyond the achievement of earning their high school diploma. The 3DE program helps to ensure high school education is more relevant, experiential and authentically connected, beyond the classroom walls, to the complexities that exist in the local community workplaces. It is a school-within-a-school model that, with local businesses support, brings relevant case study curriculum to students, ensuring students who complete the program exit with at least one industry certification. 

 

The expansion plan includes Gibbs High School which has completed the planning phase for the 3DE Academy and will open a 3DE program in the 2022-2023 school year. Lakewood High School will enter the planning phase during the 2022-2023 school year and will open their respective program during the 2023-2024 school year. All new 3DE programs begin by enrolling approximately 150 freshmen students who are within the zones of the two high schools. The program enrollment mirrors the demographics of the zoned populations and compliments existing programs in the two schools.

 

RATIONALE:

This partnership expansion with Junior Achievement and 3DE allows us to extend our reach to more students in additional school communities to provide a more explicit framework and system for deeper, more sustainable and scalable connections between the high school curriculum that students experience while in school and the preparation needed for success in college, technical school and employment in local businesses, professions and the workforce at large. By targeting zoned school populations, the 3DE model is meeting the needs of groups of students not currently accessing career and technical education.

 

The annual expenses per 3DE site are approximately $330,000. With this addendum, the district is agreeing to fund $40,000 annually for each expansion site to assist with curriculum development and resources for the next two years.

BACKGROUND:

The citizens of Pinellas County voted to increase the ad valorem millage one-half mill for necessary operating expenses on November 2, 2004, January 29, 2008, November 6, 2012, November 1, 2016, and then again on November 3, 2020.  These funds must be used to recruit and retain quality teachers, preserve reading programs and music and art classes and provide classroom libraries, technology and other curriculum resources.

 

The board established specific areas for the expenditure of these funds and a process for monitoring purchases and reporting to the public on the allocation of referendum dollars. An additional requirement is in the establishment of an oversight committee of community members to monitor these expenditures. 

 

In addition to sharing funds with charter schools, proportionate to student enrollment, as required by law, School Board Policy 2140 states that eighty (80) percent of the Pinellas County Schools (PCS) proceeds shall be allocated exclusively to salary/benefits which totals approximately $43.5 million for school year 2022-2023.  The remaining 20% (approximately $15.5 million) is to be dedicated to the preservation of reading programs and music and art classes and to provide up-to-date classroom libraries, technology and other curriculum resources. The 2022-2023 distribution will be as follows:

 

  • $43,478,238 for teacher salaries/benefits
  • $  4,347,824 for reading
  • $  2,173,912 for performing arts
  • $  2,173,912 for visual arts
  • $  2,173,912 for technology
  • $  4,610,385 for charter schools

 

Specialists in art, music, reading and technology scheduled meetings with teachers, members of the business community, parents and representatives of various cultural organizations to plan how the dollars will be spent.

 

RATIONALE:

Acceptance of the work group recommendations enables district staff to implement a purchasing plan, create necessary professional development activities for teachers and develop a communication process with the oversight committee. Acceptance of the work group recommendations will also allow the public to have evidence of the use of the referendum funds early in the 2022-2023 school year as monitored quarterly by the Independent Citizens Referendum Oversight Committee (ICROC).

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak during the public hearing and regarding the topic of the public hearing.

 

In each case, you

  1. Must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below;
  2. Must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers for that speaking opportunity or before the last speaker for that opportunity concludes their comments, whichever is later;
  3. Will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee;
  4. Will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson or a majority vote of the Board; and
  5. May not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.

 

The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. If a speaker wants to supplement their three (3) minute presentation, the speaker should provide the Board office with written materials prior to the meeting. Board members will make every effort to review these materials. Board members may not be able to review materials received less than two (2) business days prior to the Board meeting.
  2. The Board will not act on any matter raised by a speaker prior to the next regular meeting. If a majority of the Board desires to further consider a matter raised by a speaker, they will request the Superintendent to bring a recommendation at a future workshop or meeting or otherwise address the issue. It is generally the Board's practice not to respond to the issues brought forward by speakers except to correct inaccuracies or clarify issues.
  3. Speakers may not use any of the speaking opportunities to advertise or otherwise promote the interests of any commercial or other non-school organizations, except when recognizing school or district partners or when one of the exceptions in Policy 9700.01 applies. Further, no speaker may advertise or otherwise promote a political candidate, question or party.

 

Regulation of Disruptive Speech

 

Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.

 

To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive or otherwise interferes with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, as stated in s. 1001.372(3), Florida Statutes; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

 

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.

 

Recording of Board Meetings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
