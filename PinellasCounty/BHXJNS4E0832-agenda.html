4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation of National African-American History Month, February 2020
Meeting
Jan 28, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Presentation, Proclamation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools (PCS) celebrate the many achievements and contributions of local, state, and national African-American figures throughout the year and particularly during the month of February, National African-American History Month. This is a time to highlight the struggles, successes and contributions of the African American and Black community in the United States. The theme for African-American History Month 2020 is, “African Americans and the Vote.” Additional African-American History curriculum resources and professional development opportunities tied to this year’s theme are provided by the Social Studies and Equity teams to all schools through the support of many community partnerships.

 

Since 2015, the Commissioner of Education’s African-American History Task Force has recognized Pinellas County Schools as an Exemplary School District for our work to maximize the integration of African-American History into our curriculum. To date, the approved exemplary status has only been awarded to ten school districts in the state of Florida. This approval is based on evidence of structured professional development for teachers, students, and school staff in strategies for teaching African-American History, integration of African-American History into required curriculum frameworks, and strong community partnerships, among other factors.

 

Throughout the year, teachers and other district staff have received increased support as they develop and implement more equitable instructional practices and resources related to African-American History. In addition to curriculum implementation, our Principals Multicultural Advisory Committee (PMAC) students take the lead at the school level each February, by promoting African-American History Month through informational posters set up in public spaces, cultural celebrations, and morning announcements that highlight the significant contributions of African Americans.

Meeting

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

7.6 Request Approval of the Membership Application and Continued Representation on the Pinellas Continuum of Care Board Relating to the Pinellas Homeless Leadership Board
Meeting
Jan 28, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Budget Source
There is no financial impact to the district.
Recommended Action
Approve the membership application and continued representation on the Pinellas Continuum of Care Board relating to the Pinellas Homeless Leadership Board.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 6. Communication and Stakeholder Engagement

BACKGROUND:

For over a decade, a coalition of public and private entities has examined the problems and potential solutions relating the issue of homelessness in Pinellas County. This coalition has been known most recently as the Pinellas County Homeless Leadership Board (“HLB”), but was previously named the Pinellas County Homeless Leadership Network. The School Board has always been represented on the coalition, which has as its stated goal to “work together to end homelessness in Pinellas County.”

 

The HLB is funded primarily through federal and state grants. Recently, a large coalition funder, the federal Department of Housing and Urban Development, has indicated that the structure of the HLB should be changed to better align with federal guidelines and best practices, including a separation of policy and administrative functions. Consequently, the HLB has undertaken steps to bifurcate itself into a policy-making board known as the Pinellas Continuum of Care Board (“COC”), and an administrative private, nonprofit lead agency body known as the Homeless Leadership Alliance of Pinellas. While the former HLB will cease to exist, some may still generally refer to one or both entities as the HLB.

 

All previous governmental and community organizational members of the HLB are encouraged to join the new COC, which will perform similar policy-making functions that the HLB performed, but will not oversee day-to-day operations of the administration of the programs.  Membership on the COC will not incur any cost by the School Board, whether as an application fee, annual dues, contributions toward programs, or in any other manner.  If this item is approved, the short online membership application will be completed in the name of the School Board and the School Board will continue to annually designate one member as the representative on the COC, and another as an alternate.

 

RATIONALE:

Approval of the membership application will reinforce the collaboration and coordination between the School Board and other public agencies in the county regarding the examination and improvement of the condition of homelessness in the county, which condition is experienced by many of our students and their families. Further, continued membership in the coalition will provide valuable endorsement of the coalition as it seeks continued and increased funding from federal, state and private sources.

Recommended Action
Approve request to recycle, cannibalize or scrap district surplus and obsolete fixed assets.
Goals
Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:

The warehouse receives requests from schools and district sites to pick up surplus and obsolete technology equipment. Computer equipment that is over 5 years old and unrepairable is sent to the surplus warehouse to be physically checked in by onsite district support staff. Any computers found to be useful by onsite technical support staff may get reassigned to schools or district sites. Schools may keep obsolete technology equipment that meets their educational needs. Computers that are deemed no longer useful to the district are sent to a recycling company.

 
RATIONALE:

The school board authorizes the disposal of surplus property. Florida Statutes 274.05, 274.06 and 274.07 provides direction for the disposition and recording of surplus assets which are obsolete and no longer serve a useful function within the district.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
