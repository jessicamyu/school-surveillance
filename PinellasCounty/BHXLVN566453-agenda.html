Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Every four years beginning in 2004, and most recently in November 2016, the voters of Pinellas County have expressed their commitment to support public education in Pinellas County by voting to approve and continue the one-half mill property tax. The most recent approval will end on June 30, 2021. In order to maintain the benefits of the one-half mill property revenue, it is necessary for voters in the county to approve a continuation of the one-half mill property tax at a duly-called election. A new law effective July 1, 2019, requires that proceeds from the referendum must be shared with charter schools in proportion to their student enrollment, and that the ballot question note this.

 

On April 28, 2020, the school board adopted a referendum resolution and authorized the district to work with the Pinellas County administration to obtain Board of County Commissioners' approval to place the question on the ballot for the November 3, 2020, general election. Due to a communication error, the County adopted an inappropriate resolution. However, this agenda item and subsequent Board of County Commissioners action that will occur on or about August 11, 2020, will cure the error. The referendum question will remain on the ballot for the November 3, 2020, general election and all prior information regarding the referendum remains the same.

 
RATIONALE:
The School Board is placing the referendum question on the ballot to seek approval of a renewal of the tax referendum in order to continue to provide additional revenue with which the district may recruit and retain quality teachers and maintain quality education programs in academic areas and in the areas of visual arts, performing arts, reading and technology.

The UniSIG funds will be used to support and help students gain a high-quality education and the skills to master the Florida Standards.  These supplemental funds are used to support school improvement plans and other components that will impact student achievement in the current school year and correlate with a specific Area of Focus identified in the eligible school’s School Improvement Plan. These activities include: school reform strategies; instruction and coaching by highly-qualified staff; high-quality, ongoing professional development; teacher participation in making assessment decisions; timely assistance to students not meeting standards; and coordination and integration of resources. 

 

Approval of the grant includes renewal of the contract with Achievement Network (ANet).  Content Area Teachers will receive full partnership coaching, professional development training in English/language arts and mathematics instruction and support regarding specific strategies that will result in complex thinking of scholars.  Instructional practices will be aligned for task and standard alignment, scholar-centered lesson development and improved best practices.

BACKGROUND:
The Arts Conservatory for Teens (ACT) was founded in 2009 in St. Petersburg, Florida.  The purpose of this unique, community-based program is to encourage positive self-expression, develop talent and instill character education principles, while allowing students to explore career opportunities in the Arts.  Collectively the two founders, Alex Harris and Herbert Murphy, have over 37 years of organizational and artistic experience.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:

School districts in Florida must receive approval from the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) prior to the commencement of any project which involves the construction or demolition of a facility or a change in the use or function of a facility.  FDOE approval is granted after a review of the needs of the school district via the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) reporting process.

 

In regard to the potential razing of a building, the FDOE’s Office of Educational Facilities (OEF) requires that school districts conduct a financial study known as a Castaldi Analysis to compare the costs of razing an educational facility versus the costs of rehabilitating an educational facility. School districts are permitted to raze a structure if the Castaldi Analysis shows it would be costlier to rehabilitate the structure as opposed to razing it and replacing it with new construction.

 

As part of the FISH approval process, the OEF requires districts to submit a Room Condition Change Building Replacement/Raze (RCC-BRR) form to certify that there is no debt on the property being reviewed and that the School Board is aware that the district is considering the razing of a building. Upon receipt of the RCC-BRR, the OEF will confirm the districts’ Castaldi Analysis prior to extending approval to raze the building.

 

District staff performed a Castaldi Analysis on Buildings 3, 4, 5, 6, and 7 at Seventy-Fourth Street Elementary School and found it to be less costly to replace/raze than it would be to rehabilitate each building. Staff has also confirmed that there is no outstanding debt on this real property.

 

This request to submit the RCC-BRR form and conceptual permissions, if approved, will allow staff to move forward with the process of submitting the RCC-BRR form to OEF.

 
RATIONALE:

By approving, certifying and submitting the attached form, RCC-BRR, to the Florida Department of Education, OEF, the district will comply with requirements for standardized procedures and processes as established by OEF.

The current ending date for students is Thursday, May 27, 2021, and for instructional staff on Friday, May 28, 2021.

 

Given the need to consider additional time to address the unique issues posed by the pandemic, the calendar committee was reconvened to discuss possible amendments to the Calendar, and specifically to the dates surrounding the beginning of school. For the reasons stated in the Rationale below, amendments to the Calendar are being recommended. Attached are (1) the Calendar approved in October 2019, and (2) the proposed amended Calendar. No amendments are being recommended relating to the Special Observance Days approved on October 22, 2019.

 

The proposed amended calendar includes the following:

  • A school start date for 10-month instructional staff on Thursday, August 13, 2020, and for students on Monday, August 24, 2020.
  • 88 days of instruction during the first semester and 92 days second semester with administration of first semester exams prior to winter break.
  • Seven days of preplanning for 10-month teachers.
  • Shift in non-student days for instructional personnel to coincide with the adjusted end of each quarter to now be on October 26, 2020, January 19, 2021, and April 1, 2021.
  • Potential hurricane make-up days on October 26, 2020, January 19, 2021, and April 1, 2021. If four (4) hurricane make-up days are needed those will be held on October 26, 2020, November 23, 2020, November 24, 2020, and January 19, 2021.
  • A Thanksgiving break from Saturday, November 21, 2020, through Sunday, November 29, 2020, for students and 10-month instructional staff.
  • A winter break from Saturday, December 19, 2020, through Sunday, January 3, 2021, for students and 10-month instructional staff.
  • A spring break from Saturday, March 13, 2021, through Sunday, March 21, 2021, for students and 10-month instructional staff.
  • A school ending date for students on Wednesday, June 9, 2021, and for 10-month instructional staff on Thursday, June 10, 2021.

 

RATIONALE:

The proposed amended Instructional Calendar is recommended based on input from representatives of a multitude of stakeholder groups. The changes provide additional time to plan for impacts of reopening Pinellas County Schools including scheduling changes that result from the learning options selected by students and families.

 

The calendar takes into consideration a number of non-negotiables:

  • That the Pinellas County School District makes all decisions based on what is in the best interest of teachers and students.
  • Contractual obligations with employees.

 

The Calendar Committee met on Tuesday, July 21, 2020, to discuss, review and gather input to inform the proposed amendments.

 

DATA SOURCES:

Calendar Committee comprised of Area Superintendents; Career, Technical and Adult Education; Communications; Community; Facilities; Finance; Food and Nutrition; Human Resources Services; Information and Technology Services; Parents; PCTA; PESPA; Principals; Professional Development; School Board; FPSU/SEIU; Students; Teachers; Teaching and Learning Services; Testing; and Transportation
