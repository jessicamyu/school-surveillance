Subject
4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation for Career and Technical Education Month
Meeting
Feb 8, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Adopt the proclamation for Career and Technical Education Month.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness

BACKGROUND: 

Career and Technical Education (CTE) enjoys a long history in Pinellas County Schools. There are approximately 28,000 high school students in the district with as many as 20,000 enrolled in at least one course under the umbrella of career technical education, while Pinellas Technical College boasts an enrollment of 3,260 students in certificate and apprenticeship programs including 656 dual-enrolled high school students.

 

The district offers 116 programs of study in our secondary and postsecondary schools, which offer students career pathway opportunities leading to high-skill, high-wage, high-demand careers. There are 48 registered high school Career and Professional Education (CAPE) academies and 18 registered middle school CAPE academies in the district.

 

In 2020-21, elementary, middle and high school students earned 10,244 industry certifications. Many of these certifications articulate to postsecondary education earning college credit for students who achieve them. Seventy-four percent of postsecondary students earned industry certifications.

 

Student performance data show that students engaged in high rigor interest-based programs such as career technical courses and career academies have higher attendance rates, higher grade point averages and higher graduation rates than their peers.

 

In Pinellas County, CTE programs feature high school and postsecondary partnerships that provide clear pathways for students and allow them to earn industry certifications that articulate to degree programs throughout the state. Most importantly, CTE programs prepare students to be career- and college-ready by providing core academic skills, employability skills and technical job-specific skills.

 

Pinellas County Schools joins the state of Florida and Governor Ron DeSantis in declaring February 2022 as Career and Technical Education month. 

 

SUBMITTED BY:

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Recommended Action
Adopt the proclamation recognizing February 7-11, 2022, as Student Services Week.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Student Services week is celebrated during the first week of February; a time to recognize and celebrate the critical contribution of Student Services professionals in our schools and the role they play in helping to mold and educate our children. Student Services week is one way of highlighting how school counselors, nurses, social workers, psychologists, educational diagnosticians, prevention staff and crisis response team members serve as liaisons between the home, school and community. 

 The hard work, commitment and invaluable contributions made by the Student Services staff facilitate conditions for enhanced teaching and learning by providing supports that strengthen the bond between families and schools.  They play an integral part in the success and education of all our children by: increasing enrollment in advanced placement, dual enrollment and other rigorous courses; aligning a pathway with the student’s aptitude and ability; and providing social-emotional support throughout the developmental process.  They also provide excellent leadership and guidance for students who are facing obstacles to their academic and social-emotional growth such as chronic absenteeism, poverty, disability, discrimination, abuse, addiction, bullying, divorce or the loss of a loved one.

 

We appreciate Student Services staff each and every day for their invaluable contribution and will formally recognize them during the first week in February.

Marissa is a true marigold-she enhances the lives of everyone around her.

BACKGROUND:

Kids Tag Art Pinellas (KTA) is a community program made possible by partnerships between the Pinellas County Tax Collector's Office, Pinellas County Schools, and generous community sponsors. Since its inception in 2015, KTA has raised more than $200,000 for elementary visual arts programs in Pinellas County Schools. KTA has a Florida Standards-aligned curriculum where district 5th-grade students design their own unique vanity tags (front license plates) during art class. These metal tags can be purchased and displayed on the front of vehicles in Florida and many other states. Proceeds from tag sales and community donations go to participating elementary art programs to purchase art supplies, materials and equipment.

 

Each year the Tax Collectors Office holds a ceremony to honor three students from each elementary school chosen as the school winners for their tag design. This year's ceremony was held virtually on January 20. The Tax Collector's Office is pleased to present a donation to the district in recognition of the annual Kids Tag Art event.

Meeting

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Recommended Action
Approve the Head Start Child Care Partnership Agreement with Lutheran Services Florida, Inc.

BACKGROUND:

Head Start is a federal program that promotes school readiness for children ages three to five from low-income families by supporting the development of the whole child. Head Start programs support growth and development in a positive learning environment through a variety of services which include early academic learning, social skills and emotional well-being, physical development, nutrition, health screenings, and family well-being. Head Start programs must give priority to children who are homeless, or whose family income is below the poverty level as defined by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, or who are otherwise eligible for public assistance described under Head Start rules.

 

Lutheran Services Florida, Inc., is the lead agency for Head Start programs for Pinellas County. Through this agreement, district will become one of the Head Start program providers for Lutheran Services Florida, Inc., allowing the district to serve 108 economically disadvantaged three- and four-year-old students at no charge to families. In partnership with Lutheran Services, district-hired certified teachers and credentialed child development associates (CDA) will deliver high-quality, comprehensive educational services within all Head Start classrooms in the district. This agreement supports six (6) classrooms within the district with two (2) Head Start classes at each of the following school locations, High Point, Lealman Avenue and Woodlawn elementary schools.

 

RATIONALE:

This agreement increases the number of high-quality early learning seats in Pinellas County and continues a partnership with Lutheran Services Florida, Inc.

 

This agreement will also allow the district to maintain the rights to all employment opportunities for Head Start classrooms within the district. The district will continue to hire highly-qualified educators, including a certified teacher and credentialed CDA, to work in each classroom.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
