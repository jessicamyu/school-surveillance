4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating September 15 - October 15, 2022, as Hispanic Heritage Month
Meeting
Sep 13, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating September 15 - October 15, 2022, as Hispanic Heritage Month.
BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools proudly supports Hispanic Heritage Month which is recognized from September 15 through October 15 each year. This is a time to highlight the successes and contributions of the Hispanic community in the United States. Schools are encouraged to avail themselves of district, community and public resources for lesson plans and multimedia activities to foster a deeper understanding of the Hispanic culture among our student population. Approximately 19 percent of Pinellas County Schools' students identify themselves as Hispanic and Pinellas County Schools recognizes the importance of supporting our students in feeling accepted, understood and valued.

 

Throughout the year, teachers are encouraged to share the contributions of all ethnic groups in our country as we tell the story of our nation and our world. This month, additional Hispanic Heritage resources from the National Education Association and the National Hispanic Heritage website (a subpage of the Library of Congress) will be disseminated to teachers through a variety of methods to honor these contributions.

Meeting
Sep 13, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action, Presentation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating the Month of September 2022 as American Founders‚Äô Month; September 17 as Constitution Day; September 17-23 as Constitution Week and the Last Full Week of September as Celebrate Freedom Week.
BACKGROUND:
In alignment with Florida Statutes, Pinellas County Schools proudly supports American Founders’ Month in September and Constitution Day (September 17) and Constitution Week (September 17–23) as well as Celebrate Freedom Week during the last full week in September. This is a time to educate our students and community about the sacrifices made for freedom in the founding of this country and the values on which this country was founded. Constitution Week and Celebrate Freedom Week help to reaffirm the American ideals of individual liberty. While social studies teachers, in particular, are encouraged to emphasize the teaching of the country’s origins with a focus on the founding documents, additional American Founders’ Month, Constitution Day and Week and Celebrate Freedom Week resources have been provided to all schools to encourage effective instruction throughout the month.
 
Throughout September, the resources provided to teachers and schools can be used as a series of morning announcements that include the history of the American flag, trivia questions about our founding fathers and documents and as a time to recite words from the Declaration of Independence. Social Studies classes around the county engage in taking a deeper look at founding documents and how they continue to shape and influence our country today. The Principal’s Multicultural Advisory Committees (PMAC) and social studies teachers continue to work to bring schools together throughout the year by utilizing guest speakers like local lawmakers, university professors and veterans to talk with students about civil service, experiences with the law and our role as citizens.

5.1 Meeting Procedures
Meeting

Type

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Recommended Action
Approve the 2022-2024 School Health Services Plan for Pinellas County.
BACKGROUND:
Florida State Statute 381.0056(3), F.S., mandates that the Department of Health, in cooperation with the Department of Education, shall supervise the administration of the School Health Services program.  Every two years, the Department of Health-Pinellas County (DOH-Pinellas) and the Pinellas County School Board must submit a joint school health plan.  The School Health Services Plan is a document that describes the health services to be provided to students, the responsible party for provision of the services and the implementation strategies and activities for service delivery.  The plan outlines how the district will meet the state mandates related to school and student health.  For the 2022-2024 plan, additions were made related to parental consent for healthcare services and the care of students with epilepsy or seizure disorder.   
 
Over the past few years, the strong partnership between DOH-Pinellas and the district has allowed both entities to navigate a changing and challenging public health landscape.  The clear roles and responsibilities set forth in this document allow for seamless continuity of care, and ensures students and staff learn and work in a healthy environment.  
 
RATIONALE:
This plan is required by the Florida Department of Health in keeping with Florida State Statute. This plan is in alignment with all requirements of House Bill 1557.  The specifics of the plan will allow the Registered Nurses, Licensed Practical Nurses and Unlicensed Assistive Personnel in the schools to deliver high-quality care while adhering to all laws and rules that govern school health services.

Recommended Action
Approve the Restriction of Use Agreement for Starting Right, Now with Pinellas County for improvements at the former Harris/TIPS site.
Goals
BACKGROUND:

On January 15, 2015, the board approved a Lease, Management and Operation agreement with Starting Right, Now (SRN) Inc. to lease the facility formally known as Harris/TIPS. The lease agreement was for a period of four (4) years and was renewed for an additional four (4) years in January 2019.

 

In January 2018, the board entered into a Restriction of Use Agreement for SRN with Pinellas County.  The agreement gave SRN access to funding through Pinellas County’s participation in the Community Block Development Grant (CBDG) program.  The 2018 CDBG funding allowed SRN to make improvements to the board-owned facilities leased, managed and operated by SRN under the 2015 agreement.

 

CDBG funding can only be used on certain eligible projects and a restriction of use must be in place for the property receiving the funding to ensure the future use of the property continues to meet the eligibility requirements of the CDBG program.   As property owner, the board must agree that the property will continue to be used in a manner that meets CDBG eligibility requirements for the length of time defined in the Restriction of Use Agreement.

 

This Restriction of Use Agreement will allow SRN to acquire funding to install solar panels on board-owned property to offset the electrical costs necessary to run the facility.  The funding source will be a CDBG through Pinellas County.  The two CDBG’s and the two Restriction of Use Agreements are not inclusive.

RATIONALE:

Board approval of the Restriction of Use Agreement is necessary for Pinellas County to receive CDBG funds and release those funds to SRN to acquire and install solar panels on certain buildings at the former Harris/TIPS facility.

4 - Strategic Direction 3. Equity with Excellence for All
4 - Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
4 - Strategic Direction 6. Communication and Stakeholder Engagement

BACKGROUND:

The District Strategic Plan (DSP) is grounded in the vision, mission, values, strategic directions and goals for Pinellas County Schools. Each year, the district reviews the DSP in order to engage in a cycle of improvement that puts student success at the center of the work. The annual update to the DSP is informed by:

  • student and district performance;
  • requirements of state and federal mandates; and
  • input and feedback received from students, parents, community members, partners and staff members throughout the year.

 

As the driving force for improvement across the district, the DSP is developed each year alongside the budget to ensure resources are prioritized and aligned with the programs and initiatives that support the district goals. The district, upon approval of this plan, will continue to progress with the following strategic directions:

  • Student Achievement
  • Learning in a Rewarding, Safe and Healthy Environment
  • Equity with Excellence for All
  • Career- and College-Readiness
  • Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
  • Communication and Stakeholder Engagement

 

The DSP is structured to delineate long-term direction and annual action goals. These high-leverage areas set forth the long-term path for continuous improvement. The action goals, aligned to each Strategic Goal, demonstrate the prioritized goals of the district for the 2022-23 school year. Each Action Goal has a district administrator who works collaboratively with teams to develop implementation plans and monitor progress throughout the year.

 

In support of the DSP, each school engages in a continuous improvement process through School Improvement Plans (SIPs). As schools build their SIPs, site-based teams review current progress, develop strategies, set goals and create action plans to support growth. Alignment between the DSP and SIPs is vital to ensure the entire district is working together to meet our Strategic Goals.

 

RATIONALE:

Pursuant to Policy 2110, and in conjunction with the development, approval and adoption of the district budget, the superintendent annually submits for school board approval a District Strategic Plan (DSP) containing the vision, mission, core values, strategic directions and strategic goals for the forthcoming school year. The school board reviewed the DSP at the board workshop on June 28 and July 19, 2022. Changes were made based on board discussions and recommendations.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
