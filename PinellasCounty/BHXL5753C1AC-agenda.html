4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Recognizing May 2020 as Better Hearing and Speech Month
Meeting
May 12, 2020 - Virtual - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Adopt the proclamation recognizing May 2020 as Better Hearing and Speech Month.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Every May the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association celebrates Better Hearing and Speech Month. This is an opportunity to raise awareness about communication disorders and the role that speech-language pathologists (SLP) and audiologists have in schools. This year the theme is “Communication at Work.”

 

Speech, language, and hearing disorders are among the most common disabilities in the United States. The following information is from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, National Center for Health Statistics, 2012.

 

  • Nearly 1 in 12 U.S. children ages 3-17 has had a disorder related to voice, speech, language, or swallowing in the past 12 months
  • Boys ages 3-17 are more likely than girls to have a communication or swallowing disorder (9.6 percent compared to 5.7 percent)
  • The prevalence of communication or swallowing disorders is highest amongst children ages 3-6 (11 percent) compared to children ages 7-10 (9.3 percent) and children ages 11-17 (4.9 percent)

 

In our schools, Speech-Language Pathologists (SLPs) evaluate, diagnose and serve students with speech and/or language impairments. Audiologists evaluate and diagnose hearing loss and recommend the appropriate device to accommodate for the degree of hearing loss. Each day SLPs, audiologists and Teachers of the Deaf and Hard of Hearing work tirelessly on behalf of students to facilitate access to the curriculum through identification of communication barriers which inhibit effective communication in the academic setting and daily life. The Speech-Language Pathologists and Audiologists in Pinellas County Schools collaboratively and creatively work with their co-workers, administrators and students to recognize speech and/or language impairments and support accommodations for hearing loss with the goal of increasing student success.

 

The district and the departments of Exceptional Student Education and Communication Disorders recognize May 2020 as Better Hearing and Speech Month. We recognize the Pinellas County Speech-Language Pathologists and Audiologists who provide support to students with exceptional needs related to speech-language and hearing and thank them for serving our students.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

7.14 Request Approval of BENCOR to Administer the Pinellas County Schools' FICA Alternative and Special Pay Plan
Meeting
May 12, 2020 - Virtual - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Approval of BENCOR Agreement effective July 1, 2020, through June 30, 2025, with a 5-year renewal option.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:
Pinellas County Schools has established two 401(a) Qualified Retirement Plans. The Federal Insurance Contributions Act (FICA) Alternative Plan is for part-time, temporary, and substitute employees who do not contribute to Social Security.  The Special Pay Plan allows district employees who are terminating with accumulated terminal pay of $2,500 or greater and eligible retiring employees, including DROP participants, to defer federal income taxes and avoid FICA taxes on terminal sick pay and annual leave. 
 
On January 27, 2020, the Purchasing Department, in conjunction with Risk Management and Insurance, issued an Invitation to Negotiate#20-946-148 (ITN) for administrative services of our 401(a) Qualified Retirement Plans. After review, the evaluation committee selected BENCOR to continue to provide this administrative service.  
 
The initial term of this contract will be for a 5-year period, with one 5-year renewal option.
 
RATIONALE:
BENCOR has been providing FICA Alternative and Special Pay Plan services to the district since 1996. The plan allows employees to eliminate FICA payroll taxes and defer federal income taxes on their terminal sick pay and annual leave. In addition, the district saves paying FICA payroll taxes. There is no cost to the board for this program.  Program administrative fees are paid by plan participants.
 
The Risk Management and Insurance Review Committee, comprised of the Associate Superintendent, Finance and Business Services; Director, Risk Management and Insurance; Managing Officer, Health, Benefits, and Workers' Compensation; Managing Officer, Payroll Operations; and Retirement Specialist reviewed the responses of the ITN and recommends BENCOR continue administrative services of our 401(a) plans.
