4.1 Presentation of Proclamation Recognizing School Bus Safety Week
Meeting
Oct 12, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment
BACKGROUND:
Florida joins other states across the country to observe National School Bus Safety Week, October 18-22, 2021. The National campaign this year is "Be Safe - Know the Danger Zone".
 
Each year our school bus drivers travel over 9 million miles and transport approximately 30,000 students safely to and from school each day. This also includes driving nearly 3,000 extracurricular trips each year. Our drivers carry up to 65 students on a ten-ton vehicle down some of Florida's most congested roads. The school bus is considered the safest way for students to travel both to and from school each day. Bus safety is a critical component of our overall transportation plan, and we are proud to celebrate National School Bus Safety Week in an effort to share bus safety issues with the public. 
 
Please join the 15,000 school bus operators on the roads in Florida in observance of National School Bus Safety Week as we all work together to ensure every student has a safe ride to and from school.

4.3 Presentation of Proclamation Designating October 2021 as LGBTQ History Month
Meeting
Oct 12, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type

Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating October 2021 as LGBTQ History Month.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools values diversity and, therefore, has dedicated staff in the district’s Prevention Office who provide guidance, monitoring, professional development, coaching and other supports which promote a safe learning environment and highest student achievement for all learners. Staff across the district work to identify and address barriers for student learning and achievement that are related to gender/sexual orientation and other factors. Pinellas County Schools proudly supports LGBTQ History Month which is recognized yearly during the month of October. This is a time to highlight the successes and contributions of the LGBTQ community throughout the United States.

 

Each year during the month of October, Gay Straight Alliance (GSA) advisors throughout our district are encouraged to share resources and activities to foster a deeper understanding and appreciation of the LGBTQ community for both students and educators. GSA advisors will continue to disseminate information to educators and students through a variety of methods to honor the contributions of the LGBTQ community. Additionally, Equality Florida consistently provides resources and supports to students, families and staff.  The resources provided by these partners, align with the district’s core value of Cultural Competence and strategic direction of Equity with Excellence for All.

 

The recognition of supports for LGBTQ History Month is one way Pinellas County Schools seeks to foster diversity and inclusion leading to the success of all learners.

 

SUBMITTED BY:

Lori Matway, Associate Superintendent, Student and Community Support Services

Meeting

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Budget Source
The proceeds of a TAN are used to fund district expenditures until revenue from local millage taxes are received starting in mid-to-late November. Once local millage revenue exceeds the amount of the TAN, the proceeds become surplus and are invested short-term to offset and, optimally, exceed the cost of issuing the TAN.
Recommended Action
Adopt the Corporate Resolution Authorizing the Issuance of a Tax Anticipation Note Not to Exceed $90,000,000
BACKGROUND:
A Tax Anticipation Note (TAN) is a short-term debt issued by governmental entities and secured by future tax revenue.  In Florida, section 1011.13 of the state statutes authorizes the issuance of debt.  Repayment of any note issued by the district would be secured by local millage tax revenue.  The district has issued a TAN fifteen (15) times since 1999 in amounts ranging from $40,000,000 to $95,000,000.  The notes are issued prior to receiving local millage tax revenue, typically at the end of October, and mature by the last day of the fiscal year.
 
RATIONALE:
Proceeds from the TAN allow the district to better manage cash resources from the start of the fiscal year to the first receipt of local millage tax collections in mid-to-late November.  Adopting the Corporate Resolution authorizes staff, the district's bond counsel and financial advisor to proceed with finalizing documentation, bidding and issuance of a TAN.

BACKGROUND:

Florida Statute 1001.42(16)(a) requires annual school board approval and implementation of a new, amended or continued Schoolwide Improvement Plan (SIP) for each school in the district.

 

Each year, in alignment with the District Strategic Plan (DSP), schools engage in a continuous improvement process through the development and implementation of their SIP. Alignment between the DSP and SIPs is vital to ensure the entire district is working together to meet the needs of students.

 

Each SIP is structured to delineate current progress, strategies, goals, action plans, professional development and monitoring to support growth. These high-leverage areas set forth a path for continuous improvement. In addition to the areas of focus (academic subject areas, sub-group, Family Community Engagement, etc.) included in previous SIPs, the 2021-2022 plans include explicit goals, strategies and action plans for equity at each school. District staff provided training, support and a formal review process to assist in the development of the SIP for each school.  Implementation, progress monitoring and review for effectiveness will be ongoing and completed by both school and district teams. As working documents, the SIP should be iterative and adjusted, as needed, to support school improvement. Please note that some data points within the SIPs are populated by the Florida Department of Education and as of October 5, 2021, these have not been updated by the state, so some data reflects the 2018-19 school year.

Florida law requires that district school boards annually submit to the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) proposed procedures for the provision of special instruction and services for exceptional students. This document also serves as the basis for the identification, evaluation, eligibility determination and placement of students in special programs for exceptional students and is a component of the district’s application for supplemental student funds available under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Rule 6A-6.03411, Florida Administrative Code, requires the approval of this document by the FDOE as a prerequisite for districts’ use of weighted cost factors under the Florida Education Finance Program.

 

This State Policies and Procedures for Exceptional Student Education (SP&P) is typically written in the fall of the school year it serves and is completely reviewed and revised every three years. In between full revisions, the SP&P is revised as needed based upon changes in case law, federal guidance or state interpretation of IDEA. FDOE typically approves the District’s SP&P in the spring of each year. This year because of legal review, the SP&P for the DOE approval of the 20/21 school year was not released until July 2021 after the school year was complete.  The 2020-21 school year was a triennial complete review/revision year. Following FDOE approval, the new ESE SP&P is brought to the School Board for approval prior to signature by the Superintendent. The new ESE SP&P will be in effect through the end of the 2020-21 school year.

 

Updates include:

 

Section I A- Restraint and Seclusion

  • Changes were made to how incidents are reported to parents by schools and the monitoring/ training that will be provided by district level specialists and committees.
  • Changes were made to the reporting and monitoring of restraint and seclusion to a new Restraint and Seclusion Team and the frequency of the reporting to district stakeholders. Additional training programs and initiatives were added for school-based teams
  • Current data was updated to include the 2019-20 school year.

Section I- B3-Collaboration of Public Private Partnership

  • Minor changes were made to meet federal guidelines

Section I- G: General Education Intervention Procedures

  • Updates to this section reflect changes to the MTSS Manual for PCS.

Section II C- IEP

  • The section on continuum of services was completely changed to be aligned with current language and practice federally and locally.

Section II- B.12: Exceptional Education Eligibility for Students with Specific Learning Disabilities

  • This section has increased detail concerning the process used to determine eligibility that includes multiple reviews of gap analysis, percentage of growth over time via response to intervention.

Section II E- Participation in State and District Assessments

  • The data for percentage of students on FSAA by subgroup in the 2019-20 school year were updated. A statement was added explaining the steps the district will take to reduce the percentages in totality and by individual sub-groups. New processes, training and monitoring were added to decrease participation.

Section II- D: Discipline

  • Changes were made to this section to include the current training that is occurring in the district on social emotional learning, restorative practices and trauma informed care. Language was added on who serves in various roles in discipline reviews and the steps that need to be taken during alternative placements. Language was added as to where alternative placements may occur. Language about CPI levels and who gets the training was changed as it was in the restraint and seclusion section.

Appendix C: District Plan to Increase the Participation of Underrepresented Students in the Program for Students who are Gifted

  • Current data was updated

Appendix D: District Policies Regarding the Allowable Use or Prohibition of Physical Restraint and Seclusion

  • The Board policy on mechanical restraint was removed because we no longer allow the use of mechanical restraint. The Board policy on Seclusion was uploaded.

New Appendix- School closures Due to National or Local Emergency

The Florida Department of Education added an appendix that deals with PCS response during times of national or local emergency. PCS was required to provide supporting documentation used to support and educate staff and families.  The documents provided are:

  • PCS ESE Instructional Continuity Guide.
  • FERPA statement for therapies
  • Parent letter concerning the Digital Learning Plan
  • ESE Staff Roles Description Guidance Document
  • Process for IEP Meetings Guidance Document

BACKGROUND:

Each year the district reviews policies in alignment with district practices. The proposed amendments to the policies below relate to the Student Progression Plan.

 

Policy 5420.03 – Reporting Student Progress and Class Ranking

  • The proposed amendment adds clarifying language for parents and students as to the original intended purpose of the early exam process and further outlines acceptable reasons to request an early exam. This includes students who are moving out of the district prior to final exams or for students who are experiencing an unforeseen hardship such as a medical emergency or the death of a family member, etc.

Policy 5430 – Class Rank

  • The proposed amendment adds the HOPE course to the list of courses eligible to be deleted from the class ranking computation as it relates to the Valedictorian and Salutatorian designations. The HOPE course is an unweighted course, and in recent years, there are growing numbers of students who complete the HOPE course prior to their 9th grade year.  Those students, if they are in contention for the Val/Sal designations after the 2nd semester of their senior year, have a mathematical grade point advantage over students who take the HOPE course after they enter high school. 
RATIONALE:

The proposed amendments to these policies ensure alignment with district practices.

 

Policy 5420.03 – Reporting Student Progress and Class Ranking

  • Our schools are experiencing a significant uptick in requests for early exams for leisure travel which is not a qualifying reason for an early exam. This recommended change in policy allows for parents and students to better understand the intent of this policy so as to avoid requests to school principals that do not meet the original intent of this policy.

 

Policy 5430 – Class Rank

  • Up until now, including the HOPE course has not impacted the computations and selections for Val/Sal in our high schools, but the upward trend of HOPE completions prior to high school inevitably will cause an issue in the years to come. This recommended change will ensure we continue to have a level playing field for all students, regarding the inclusion of unweighted classes that are required for graduation, when it comes to the computation that identifies the Valedictorian and Salutatorian designations each school year.

Approve the negotiated MOU for all active employees in these bargaining units and approve COVID Sick Leave Pay for all non-represented staff.
BACKGROUND:

Since March 2020, the Family First Coronavirus Relief Act (FFCRA) provided paid time off for employees who were unable to work due to reasons related to COVID. This was extended to September 30, 2021, by the authority granted under the American Rescue Plan. The district and SEIU/FPSU and FOP bargaining teams recognized the need to ensure that employees who are required to quarantine due to COVID continue to be paid after this date, and agreed on specific terms and conditions for which employees could be paid. Vaccinated employees required to quarantine will be paid up to ten (10) days provided they have not already used ten (10) days of COVID sick leave since March 2020. Employees who are not eligible for this COVID sick leave may access the sick leave bank which allows for up to ten (10) days if required to quarantine. This agreement will be in effect until December 17, 2021.  As with other previous agreements reached with the bargaining units, non-represented employees will follow the same terms and conditions.

 

RATIONALE:

The respective bargaining teams worked collaboratively on this agreement and believe this agreement provides employees with the ability to get paid when they are required to quarantine.  In remaining consistent with the other bargaining groups who worked with the district, this agreement also provides non-represented employees with the same ability to get paid when they are required to quarantine.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
