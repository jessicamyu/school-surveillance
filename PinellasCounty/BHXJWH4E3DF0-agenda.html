4.1 Presentation of a Proclamation for Career and Technical Education Month, February 2020
Meeting
Feb 11, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Proclamation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness
BACKGROUND:

Governor Ron DeSantis recognizes the state of Florida’s observance of Career and Technical Education month for February 2020.

 

Career and Technical Education (CTE) enjoys a long history in Pinellas County Schools. There are approximately 23,000 high school students in the district with as many as 20,000 enrolled in at least one course under the umbrella of career technical education. The technical colleges boast an enrollment of 3,044 students in certificate and apprenticeship programs. Of this total, 220 are dual enrolled high school students.

 

The district offers 116 programs of study in our secondary and postsecondary schools, which offer students career pathway opportunities leading to high-skill, high-wage, high-demand careers. There are 41 registered high school Career and Professional Education (CAPE) academies and 15 registered middle school CAPE academies in the district.

 

In 2018-19, elementary, middle and high school students earned 13,939 industry certifications. Many of these certifications articulate to postsecondary education earning college credit for students who achieve them. Seventy-four percent of postsecondary students earned industry certifications.

 

Student performance data show that students engaged in high-rigor interested-based programs such as career technical courses and career academies have higher attendance rates, higher grade point averages and higher graduation rates than their peers.

 

In Pinellas County, CTE programs feature high school and postsecondary partnerships that provide clear pathways for students and allow them to earn industry certifications that articulate to degree programs throughout the state. Most importantly, CTE programs prepare students to be career- and college-ready by providing core academic skills, employability skills and technical, job-specific skills.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Meeting
Feb 11, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.

4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

February has been designated as Student Services month, a time to recognize and celebrate the critical contribution of Student Services professionals in our schools and the critical role they play in helping to mold and educate our children.  Student Services Month is one way of highlighting how school counselors, nurses, social workers, psychologists, educational diagnosticians, attendance specialists and crisis response members serve as liaisons between the home, school and community.

 

Student Services professionals facilitate conditions for enhanced teaching and learning by providing supports that strengthen the bond between families and schools.  They play an integral part in the success and education of all our children by increasing enrollment in advanced placement, dual enrollment and other rigorous courses; aligning a pathway with the student’s aptitude and ability and providing social-emotional support throughout the developmental process.  They also provide excellent leadership and guidance for students who are facing obstacles to their academic and social-emotional growth such as chronic absenteeism, poverty, disabilities, discrimination, abuse, addiction, bullying, divorce or the loss of a loved one.

 

Student Services staff play a vital role in the academic and emotional success for all students.  We appreciate them each and every day for their invaluable contribution and will formally recognize them during the month of February.

4.3 Recognition of the Helios Education Foundation and Pinellas Education Foundation for Supporting the Elevating Excellence Initiative
Meeting
Feb 11, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Recognition
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness
BACKGROUND:

In spring 2018 we launched Elevating Excellence as our district commitment to support all high-achieving students, with targeted outreach for low-income and minority students, to aim high, know their options both academically and financially and have the knowledge and support to navigate what can be a difficult process. Through Elevating Excellence, students receive school-embedded and enrichment services within the 6 pillars of the initiative:

    • Academic Counseling and Targeted Support
    • Pathway Course Progression
    • Parent and Student Engagement
    • College Entrance Test Preparation
    • College and Scholarship Planning
    • Peer Collaboration Experiences

The district vision could not have come to life without the partnership and support of the Pinellas Education Foundation and LEAP Tampa Bay.  Through collaborative efforts the Helios Education Foundation has generously provided $1.3 million, through the Pinellas Education Foundation, to accelerate the progress of Elevating Excellence.

 

In spring 2019, we opened our first College and Career Center at Largo High School with four additional centers opening this school year at: Boca Ciega, Gibbs, Clearwater and Pinellas Park high schools. Through the support of the Helios Education Foundation over the next several years, every high school will have a fully operational College and Career Center to serve as the hub of information for the college- and career-going process. The support has also allowed the district to expand college entrance exam preparation services for students both within the school day and through enrichment opportunities. Such supports are critical for students as a high SAT or ACT score is often the biggest barrier for college admission or scholarship opportunities.

 

Elevating Excellence has been seen locally and nationally as an innovative and needed program and is changing both the reality and the perception of the services Pinellas County Schools provides our students.

 

With the investment by the Helios Education Foundation, the district will continue to intensify the tools and services we provide through Elevating Excellence and our College and Career Centers so our students and their families believe in and realize their dreams as they navigate the college- and career-going processes.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

There is no financial impact to the district. The attached agreement specifies the details of the funding provided by the Juvenile Welfare Board of Pinellas County for data deliverables.
Recommended Action
Approve the renewal agreement with Juvenile Welfare Board of Pinellas County for data deliverables.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Approximately 5,000 Pinellas County students are served in multiple at-risk behavior prevention, intervention, and treatment programs funded by the Juvenile Welfare Board of Pinellas County (JWB) every year. Enrollment in these programs requires examination of students’ school records including demographics, student achievement, attendance, and discipline data.

As program intakes occur throughout the school year for JWB-funded programs, continuous access to school data is crucial in the identification of potential participants and is necessary to track the progress of students served.

 

JWB staff request data from Pinellas County Schools (PCS) on a recurring basis in order to evaluate their funded programs and to identify student populations potentially in need of services that support school readiness, graduation rates, and overall school success. A collaborative agreement for data allows for the determination of appropriate services and programs to support PCS students in successfully completing their education.

 

In partnership with PCS, JWB provides the delivery of structured and well-supervised educational programs supplemented by a coordinated overlay of other appropriate services designed to address students’ needs as they relate to academics and school-related and personal behaviors with programs like tutoring and mentoring. PCS provides data deliverables in accordance with the agreement and within the scope of Section 1022.221(2)(c) F.S.

 

RATIONALE:

This is a renewal of an agreement approved by the School Board on June 27, 2017, and serves as the continuation of a contract with JWB to provide funding to Pinellas County Schools for data deliverables for school years 2019-2020 and 2020-2021. JWB funds numerous programs focused on at-risk students as well as before- and after-care programs and early learning initiatives that serve students at various schools within the district. JWB-funded programs provide a comprehensive range of services to elementary, middle and high school students and their families to promote school readiness, overall school success and reduce early risk behaviors, including truancy.

 

JWB program managers routinely use performance measures of school data to assess the efficacy of these programs. Furthermore, data are reported to the JWB Board of Directors for use in decision-making regarding funding and placement of these programs. A collaborative agreement is necessary to assist JWB in obtaining and analyzing school data.

Recommended Action
Approve the renewal of the interagency agreement for student data sharing with the Office of the State Attorney - 6th Circuit Court and Juvenile Welfare Board of Pinellas County.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

In accordance with Section 1002.221(2)(c) F.S., the School Board may, without consent of the student or the student’s parent, release personally identifiable records or reports of a student to parties to an interagency agreement among the Department of Juvenile Justice, school and law enforcement authorities, and other signatory agencies for the purposes set forth in the rationale below.

 

Pinellas County Schools, the Office of the State Attorney – 6th Judicial Circuit and the Juvenile Welfare Board of Pinellas County agree to share student data consistent with the restrictions imposed by state and federal laws and regulations. The purposes of this sharing shall be to implement Section 1002.221(2)(c) F.S. and this interagency agreement, to inform students of educational opportunities, monitor academic achievement, measure program effectiveness and facilitate ongoing research. Each organization agrees to treat such shared-student information as confidential, and not to release personally identifiable information to third parties except as permitted by law.

 

RATIONALE:

This is a renewal of the interagency agreement previously approved by the School Board on June 27, 2017. The interagency agreement will facilitate the board’s continuing work with the State Attorney and the Juvenile Welfare Board to work in a collaborative manner to determine the appropriate programs and services for juveniles and their families, particularly at-risk students; and provide structured and well-supervised educational programs supplemented by a coordinated overlay of services designed to correct behaviors that lead to truancy, suspensions, and expulsions, and support students in successfully completing their education including truancy prevention at its earliest; all within the meaning and scope of Section 1002.221(2)(c) F.S.

Approve request of setting a public hearing and authorizing the advertisement of the public hearing to consider amendment of Policy 5500.08 - CODE OF STUDENT CONDUCT - MISCONDUCT THAT REQUIRES SPECIFIC CONSEQUENCES.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment

BACKGROUND:

The proposed policy updates reflect the need to explicitly address vaping within the Code of Student Conduct. The language expands the previously Tobacco and Nicotine section to include: Tobacco, Nicotine and Vaping.  The policy takes a preventative approach while addressing consequences for the use of such products at school, at any school-sponsored activity, at a bus stop, or on the bus.  The proposed updates include educational interventions, opportunities for diversion programs, along with progressive consequences for each offense.

 

Additional proposed updates within the policy are being made to school safety provisions in alignment with recent legislative sessions. The district remains in compliance with practices. The recommended policy updates codify practices and are in alignment with statutory requirements regarding threats to school safety and threat assessment team consultation with law enforcement.

 

RATIONALE:

Proposed amendments to policies address vaping within the Code of Student Conduct and align with legislative updates for school safety requirements.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
