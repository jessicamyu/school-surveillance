Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak during the public hearing and regarding the topic of the public hearing.

 

In each case, you

  1. Must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below;
  2. Must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers for that speaking opportunity or before the last speaker for that opportunity concludes their comments, whichever is later;
  3. Will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee;
  4. Will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson or a majority vote of the Board; and
  5. May not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.

 

The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. If a speaker wants to supplement their three (3) minute presentation, the speaker should provide the Board office with written materials prior to the meeting. Board members will make every effort to review these materials. Board members may not be able to review materials received less than two (2) business days prior to the Board meeting.
  2. The Board will not act on any matter raised by a speaker prior to the next regular meeting. If a majority of the Board desires to further consider a matter raised by a speaker, they will request the Superintendent to bring a recommendation at a future workshop or meeting or otherwise address the issue. It is generally the Board's practice not to respond to the issues brought forward by speakers except to correct inaccuracies or clarify issues.
  3. Speakers may not use any of the speaking opportunities to advertise or otherwise promote the interests of any commercial or other non-school organizations, except when recognizing school or district partners or when one of the exceptions in Policy 9700.01 applies. Further, no speaker may advertise or otherwise promote a political candidate, question or party.

 

Regulation of Disruptive Speech

 

Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.

 

To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive or otherwise interferes with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, as stated in s. 1001.372(3), Florida Statutes; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

 

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.

 

Recording of Board Meetings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND: 

The Professional Learning Catalog (PLC) is a state-required planning document for professional education inservice training that must be updated annually.  The PLC aligns with our District Strategic Plan and lists all the professional development opportunities that are offered to Pinellas County Schools' employees.

 

Professional goals guide the selection of courses employees select within the training system.  At the school level, the School Improvement Planning process includes the use of student data and teacher evaluation data to determine training needs for instructional staff at the school sites.  During the 2021-2022 school year, there were 102,847 courses completed throughout Pinellas County Schools by all employee groups.  Courses in Instructional Strategies, Reading, ESE Classroom Management, and Laws, Rules and Policies were listed in the top 10 most attended professional development courses.  This increase is likely attributed to our transition towards technology integration within instructional practice.  Aspiring administrators participated in various assessments to determine areas of focus for a personalized pathway towards their professional growth. 

 

Support employees identify goals while working toward their Certificate of Distinction (COD), thus providing direction toward a choice of courses taken to achieve their goals. The 2021-2022 Support Catalog of Choice day was offered to support employees in furthering their professional growth.

 

Additionally, 375 newly hired teachers attended Embrace Pinellas (our 4-day new teacher orientation) in the summer of 2022 as a kickoff for the 2022-2023 school year.

 

The Professional Learning Catalog for 2022-2023 was developed by a cross-functional team representing all levels of the school system.  The cross-functional team utilized a variety of 2021-2022 school and district data to plan for the 2022-2023 school year. As a result, Pinellas County Schools began the school year with 481 courses available for our employees to consider. This is up from 432 courses available at the start of the 2021-2022 school year.

 

RATIONALE:

The Florida Department of Education (FDOE) requires each district to develop a comprehensive Professional Learning Catalog that is revised annually, board-approved and submitted to the FDOE by October 1.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

In Section 1008.365, Florida Statutes, the Reading Achievement Initiative for Scholastic Excellence (RAISE) was established within the Florida Department of Education to provide instructional supports in implementing evidence-based reading instruction and intervention in order to improve student reading achievement. The goal of the High-Impact Reading Interventions and Targeted Supports (HIITS) grant will provide supplemental support to promote high-quality reading strategies and literacy professional development based on the science of reading, with a focus on providing intensive interventions at schools with 50 percent or more of students in Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) through grade three scoring below grade-level in reading as measured by Florida Assessment for Student Thinking (FAST) in English Language Arts. This goal will be reached through a comprehensive program that includes evidence-based instructional materials as well as high-quality professional development provided by the University of Florida Lastinger Center to staff in identified schools.  The HIITS grant is funded under the American Rescue Act, Elementary and Secondary School Emergency Relief Fund.

 

 BUDGET:

Stipends for Teachers $   255,030
Materials and Supplies $   331,796
Contract Services - Lastinger Center $   510,025
Charter School Allocation $     85,236             
Indirect Costs $     51,775
   

TOTAL:  

 $1,233,862
 
RATIONALE:

Funding will provide additional classroom curriculum resources for 467 classrooms:  58 PreK, 96 Kindergarten and 104 first, 105 second and 104 third grade classrooms in 24 targeted schools which will impact approximately 10,000 students. The funds will allow teacher access to evidence-based interventions grounded in the science of reading for Tier 2 and 3 students. Through this two-year partnership, teachers and literacy coaches in Pinellas County Schools (PCS) will receive support in implementing evidence-based practices that lead to improved outcomes in student achievement.

 

Additionally, the partnership ensures that PCS will build its capacity to create scalable and sustainable high-quality professional learning experiences for its teachers. PCS literacy coaches will gain expertise alongside University of Florida Lastinger Center associates and participate in a gradual release model to ensure implementation fidelity and sustainability of the professional learning experiences in the district.  The district trainers will then be able to provide high-quality professional learning around bringing the sciences of reading into the classroom during the grant period and beyond. 

 

This project will replicate the high-yield training provided by Lastinger for the Pinellas Early Literacy Initiative (PELI) and provides it to non PELI schools. This is part of the gradual release effort to train content specialists and instructional coaches in this model so as to begin a process of owning it at the district, and not being reliant on Lastinger as we move forward.

Budget Source
The proceeds of a TAN are used to fund district expenditures until revenue from local millage taxes are received starting in mid-to-late November. Once local millage revenue exceeds the amount of the TAN, the proceeds become surplus and are invested short-term to offset and, optimally, exceed the cost of issuing the TAN.
Recommended Action
Approve the request to adopt the Corporate Resolution authorizing the issuance of a Tax Anticipation Note not to exceed $110,000,000.
BACKGROUND:
A Tax Anticipation Note (TAN) is a short-term debt issued by governmental entities and secured by future tax revenue.  In Florida, section 1011.13 of the state statutes authorizes the issuance of debt.  Repayment of any note issued by the district would be secured by local millage tax revenue.  The district has issued a TAN sixteen (16) times since 1999 in amounts ranging from $40,000,000 to $95,000,000.  The notes are issued prior to receiving local millage tax revenue, typically at the end of October, and mature by the last day of the fiscal year.
 
RATIONALE:
The amount of the TAN is determined by projecting what the maximum General Funds deficit will be prior to receiving the first millage tax distribution.  The Internal Revenue Service also allows issuers to add up to 5% of prior year expenditures to that amount for a buffer.  Proceeds from the TAN allow the district to better manage cash resources from the start of the fiscal year to the first receipt of local millage tax collections in mid-to-late November.  Adopting the Corporate Resolution authorizes staff, the district's bond counsel and financial advisor to proceed with finalizing documentation, bidding and issuance of a TAN.

BACKGROUND: 

Florida State law, Section 1008.22(7), F.S., requires the Florida Department of Education to publish a uniform calendar each year that includes statewide assessment information. Using this calendar, each school district must establish schedules for the administration of all statewide, standardized assessments and district-required assessments. The districtwide assessment calendars must be approved by the School Board, included in parent notifications and published on the district website, per the statute.

 

  1. Districtwide assessment calendars have been established for elementary, middle and high school levels and have been converted to display in the Uniform Statewide Assessment Calendar format, as required by law. Assessment calendars are posted on the Assessment, Accountability and Research webpage. The 2022-2023 calendars can be viewed here: https://www.pcsb.org/Page/15479

 

Each year the Executive Directors of Elementary, Middle and High School Education, along with other district assessment staff, review the calendars for adherence to all local and state requirements. Local assessments are chosen based on input from district and school staff in order to progress monitor and best support students in achieving state standards. New state-required progress monitoring occurs three times in the school year with the third serving as the end-of-year Florida Assessment for Student Thinking (FAST) assessment.

 

RATIONALE:

Statute requires that no student have more than 5% of their total school hours in a year scheduled for required state and local assessments. The proposed calendars meet the requirements of state statute and ensure students are assessed on required statewide assessments, while not exceeding the 5% threshold for assessments. The range of required assessments is as low as 0.56% in kindergarten with no grade level exceeding 3.89% of required time.

School Improvement Plans (SIPs) have an associated budget to support implementation. State lottery funds provide for School Recognition awards and Discretionary Lottery funds. Florida Statute 1001.42(16)(a) requires annual school board approval and implementation of a new, amended or continued Schoolwide Improvement Plan (SIP) for each school in the district.

 

Each year, in alignment with the District Strategic Plan (DSP), schools engage in a continuous improvement process through the development and implementation of their SIPs. Alignment between the DSP and SIPs is vital to ensure the entire district is working together to meet the needs of students.

 

Each SIP is structured to delineate current progress, strategies, goals, action plans, professional development and monitoring to support growth. These high-leverage areas set forth a path for continuous improvement. In addition to the areas of focus (academic subject areas, student sub-groups, etc.) included in previous SIPs, the 2022-2023 plans include explicit goals, strategies and action plans for positive culture and environment at each school.   • Employee groups covered by the SEIU/FPSU bargaining unit will receive a raise equal to 4.25% or increased to $15.00 per hour, whichever is greater, retroactive to July 1, 2022.

  • Classification of the Plant Operations Specialists working at elementary schools with the title, duties and responsibilities of a Night Foreman at middle and high schools, the classification is changed from a D07 to a D08 and the job title is changed to Night Foreman (Elementary/Middle/or High). 

 

Article 14 - Terminal Pay

  • Adjustments made to the percentages of unused accrued sick leave paid out upon retirement or death. Employees will now be paid 100% of unused sick leave at year 25 or after, upon retirement or death. Other percentages were brought into alignment with statute guidelines.

 

RATIONALE:

The respective bargaining teams worked collaboratively to examine healthcare payroll options, financial information impacting the availability of funds for a salary increase and contract language. Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
