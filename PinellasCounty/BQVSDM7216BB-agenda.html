4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating the Month of September 2020 as American Founders’ Month; Constitution Week From September 17-23, 2020; and Celebrate Freedom Week During the Last Full Week of September
Meeting
Sep 8, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Presentation, Proclamation

BACKGROUND:

In alignment with Florida Statutes, Pinellas County Schools proudly supports American Founders’ Month in September and Constitution Day (September 17) and Week (September 17 – 23) as well as Celebrate Freedom Week during the last full week in September. This is a time to educate our students and community about the sacrifices made for freedom in the founding of this country and the values on which this country was founded. Constitution Week and Celebrate Freedom Week help to reaffirm the American ideals of individual liberty. While social studies teachers, in particular, are encouraged to emphasize the teaching of the country’s origins with a focus on the founding documents, additional American Founders’ Month, Constitution Week and Celebrate Freedom Week resources have been provided to all schools to encourage effective instruction throughout the month.

 

Throughout September, the resources provided to teachers and schools can be used as a series of morning announcements that include the history of the American flag, trivia questions about our founding fathers and documents, and as a time to recite words from the Declaration of Independence. Social Studies classes around the county engage in taking a deeper look at founding documents and how they continue to shape and influence our country today. The Principal’s Multicultural Advisory Committee’s (PMAC) and social studies teachers continue to work to bring schools together throughout the year by utilizing guest speakers like local lawmakers, university professors and veterans to talk with students about civil service, experiences with the law and our role as citizens. This year the Principal’s Multicultural Advisory Committee’s (PMAC) and social studies teachers are working to bring schools together throughout the year by utilizing virtual meetings and pre-recorded videos to hear from guest speakers like local lawmakers, university professors, veterans, and supervisor of elections officials' to learn about civil service and our role as citizens.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Meeting
Sep 8, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Presentation, Proclamation
BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools proudly supports Hispanic Heritage Month which is recognized from September 15 through October 15 each year. This is a time to highlight the successes and contributions of the Hispanic community in the United States. Schools are encouraged to avail themselves of district, community and public resources for lesson plans and multimedia activities to foster a deeper understanding of the Hispanic culture among our student population. Approximately 17% of Pinellas County Schools' students identify themselves as Hispanic and Pinellas County Schools recognizes the importance of supporting our students in feeling accepted, understood and valued.

 

Throughout the year, teachers are encouraged to share the contributions of all ethnic groups in our country as we tell the story of our nation and our world. This month, additional Hispanic Heritage resources from Teaching Tolerance, the National Education Association, and the National Hispanic Heritage website (a subpage of the Library of Congress) will be disseminated to teachers through a variety of methods to honor these contributions.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Approve the Professional Learning Catalog.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

The Professional Learning Catalog (PLC) is a state-required planning document for professional education inservice training that must be updated annually. The Professional Learning Catalog for 2020-2021 was developed by a cross-functional team representing all levels of the school system. The cross-functional team utilized a variety of 2019-2020 school and district data to plan for the 2020-2021 school year. At the school level, the School Improvement Planning process includes the use of student data and teacher iObservation data to determine training needs at the school sites. The Professional Learning Catalog aligns with our District Strategic Plan and summarizes inservice trainings for the school year.

 

The Professional Learning Catalog lists all the trainings that are offered to Pinellas County Schools' employees. The Deliberate Practice Plans for instructional staff identify areas of focus aligned to their teaching assignment. Professional goals guide the selection of courses to take within the training system. Support employees identify goals while working toward their Certificate of Distinction, thus providing direction toward a choice of courses taken to achieve their goals. 

 

In the 2019-2020 school year, courses in Standards-based Planning and Instruction, Classroom Management, Cultural Competency/Diversity and High-Yield Instructional Strategies were listed in the top 10 areas of professional development. Three hundred and one (301) newly hired teachers attended our 4-day new teacher orientation (Embrace Pinellas) in the summer of 2020. One hundred forty-two (142) first year teachers participated in the Foundations series of courses throughout the 2019-2020 school year.

 

During the summer of 2020, the district offered two hundred eighty-six (286) professional development courses with eight hundred thirty-nine (839) sessions. More than 7,000 teachers, support staff and administrators attended at least one (1) day of Professional Development during this timeframe.

 

RATIONALE:

The Florida Department of Education (FDOE) requires each district to develop a comprehensive Professional Learning Catalog that is revised annually, board-approved, and submitted to the FDOE by October 1.

Approve to apply for the School Safety Grant, July 1, 2020 – June 30, 2021, for the Alert Logic Threat Management Platform in the amount of $2,600,000 and authorize district administration to finalize and implement the grant as written.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment
BACKGROUND:
The purpose of the School Safety Grant is to help education systems revamp, rebuild and reinforce their school safety and integrate existing technology seamlessly with first responders. The overarching goal is to implement technological solutions that work to accelerate response times in an active shooter situation, providing law enforcement with the actionable intelligence and enhanced controls statistically proven to save lives. This grant will provide a security platform through the use of Alert Logic Threat Management with a direct link from a school to law enforcement without having to access 911 or other forms of communication.
  • Provides a dashboard of safety and security systems that can be accessed by the district and outside law enforcement giving law enforcement immediate notification of campus emergencies and the ability to view live cameras, unlock electronic doors, access communication systems and silence fire alarms.
  • Provides interactive maps of each school tied into the video management system to allow first responders real-time access to cameras in the event of an emergency.
  • Provides a mobile alert feature that is in compliance with Alyssa’s Law (SB 70, 2020).
RATIONALE:

The Alert Logic Threat Management Platform is designed to improve emergency response times and facilitate better collaboration between area agencies. This platform will allow the sharing of valuable information and provide site-level mapping, which will direct first responders to the specific areas of immediate need. The most important function of this security platform is that it provides a direct link from a school to law enforcement without having to access 911 or other forms of communication. If approved, grant funds will be utilized for the installation of the ALERT security platform at all Pinellas schools during the 2020-2021 school year.

Recommended Action
Approve the 2020-2021 Uniform Student Assessment Calendars.
BACKGROUND:

Florida State law, Section 1008.22(7), F.S., requires the Florida Department of Education to publish a uniform calendar each year that includes statewide assessment information.  Using this calendar, each school district must establish schedules for the administration of all statewide, standardized assessments and district-required assessments.  The districtwide assessment calendars must be approved by the School Board, included in parent notifications and published on the district website, per the statute.

 

User-friendly districtwide assessment calendars have been established for elementary, middle and high school levels and have been converted to display in the Uniform Statewide Assessment Calendar format, as required by law.   Assessment calendars are posted on the Assessment, Accountability and Research webpage.  The 2020-2021 calendars can be viewed here: https://www.pcsb.org/Page/15479  

 

Each year the Executive Directors of Elementary, Middle and High School Education, along with other district assessment staff, review the calendars for adherence to all local and state requirements.  Local assessments are chosen based on input from district and school staff in order to progress monitor and best support students in achieving state standards.  These include final exams in middle and high school and the Measures of Academic Progress (MAP) in elementary schools.

 
RATIONALE:

Statute requires that no student have more than 5% of their total school hours in a year scheduled for required state and local assessments. The proposed calendars meet the requirements of state statute and ensure students are assessed on required statewide assessments, while not exceeding the 5% threshold for assessments. The range of required assessments is as low as 0.6% in kindergarten and no grade level exceeds 3.5% of required time.  

 

Local assessments will support students and provide actionable data for teachers through individual student and schoolwide progress monitoring.

School Improvement Plans (SIPs) have an associated budget to support implementation. State lottery funds provide for School Recognition awards and Discretionary Lottery funds. Discretionary Lottery funds are only available to districts when state lottery funds exceed the amount required for School Recognition awards. Districts receive Discretionary Lottery funds, when available, which are then provided to schools for support of school improvement plans. When available, schools are allocated up to $5 per Unweighted Full-Time Equivalent (UFTE) student, to be used at the discretion of the School Advisory Council (SAC), pursuant to F.S. 24.121(5). These funds are commonly referred to as “school improvement funds.” Historically, the district has provided local funds for this purpose when Discretionary Lottery funds have been unavailable.
Recommended Action
Approve the 2020-2021 School Improvement Plans.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment
4 - Strategic Direction 3. Equity with Excellence for All
4 - Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
4 - Strategic Direction 6. Communication and Stakeholder Engagement

BACKGROUND:

Florida Statute 1001.42(16)(a) requires annual school board approval and implementation of a new, amended or continued Schoolwide Improvement Plan (SIP) for each school in the district.

 

Each year, in alignment with the District Strategic Plan (DSP), schools engage in a continuous improvement process through the development and implementation of their SIP. Alignment between the DSP and SIPs is vital to ensure the entire district is working together to meet the needs of students.

 

In alignment with the federally-approved Florida Department of Education Every Student Succeeds Act (ESSA) plan, the district transitioned to the state CIMS platform for the 2020-2021 SIPs.

 

Each SIP is structured to delineate current progress, strategies, goals, action plans, professional development and monitoring to support growth. These high-leverage areas set forth a path for continuous improvement. In addition to the areas of focus (academic subject areas, sub-group, Bridging the Gap, Conditions for Learning, Family Community Engagement, etc.) included in previous SIPs, the 2020-2021 plans include explicit goals, strategies and action plans for equity at each school. District staff provided training, support and a formal review process to assist in the development of the SIP for each school.  Implementation, progress monitoring and review for effectiveness will be ongoing and completed by both school and district teams. As working documents, the SIP should be iterative and adjusted, as needed, to support school improvement.

 

The 2020-2021 SIPs can be viewed online at https://www.pcsb.org/Page/16901. Following approval of the plans, all SIPs will be made available on each school website. Following the submission and approval process within the state CIMs platform, they will also be available for viewing online at http://www.floridacims.org.

 

RATIONALE:

All schools are required to have a board-approved SIP each year in accordance with state statute and district Policy 2120-School Improvement and Academic Accountability.

  • student and district performance;
  • requirements of state and federal mandates; and
  • input and feedback received from students, parents, community members, partners and staff members throughout the year.

BACKGROUND:
Florida law requires that district school boards annually submit to the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) proposed procedures for the provision of special instruction and services for exceptional students. This document also serves as the basis for the identification, evaluation, eligibility determination and placement of students in special programs for exceptional students and is a component of the district’s application for supplemental student funds available under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA). Rule 6A-6.03411, Florida Administrative Code, requires the approval of this document by the FDOE as a prerequisite for districts’ use of weighted cost factors under the Florida Education Finance Program. This State Policies and Procedures for Exceptional Student Education is typically written in the fall of the school year it serves. It is completely reviewed and revised every three years. In between full revisions, the SP&P is revised as needed based upon changes in case law, federal guidance or state interpretation of IDEA. FDOE typically approves the District’s SP&P in the spring of each year. This year because of legal review and the COVID 19 closures of FDOE, the SP&P for the 19/20 school year was not released until May 2020. A complete revision will occur in the 20/21 SP&P. Following FDOE approval, the new ESE SP&P is brought to the School Board for approval prior to signature by the Superintendent. The new ESE SP&P will be in effect through the end of the 2019-2020 school year unless additional legislative changes are enacted with an earlier effective date.
 
Updates include:
 

Section I A- Restraint and Seclusion. Changes were made to how incidents are reported by schools and the monitoring/training that will be provided by district-level specialists and committees. Restraint and seclusion data comparisons were included for the 17/18 school year and 18/19 school years. Language about the structure and requirements for site-based Crisis Teams was changed to a determined number of members based on best practices and the CPI II training required for these members. In the district’s plan to reduce usage of restraint, language about Positive Behavior Interventions and Supports and Multi-Tiered System of Supports was added. Information specific to FBA data and implementation fidelity of the PBIP including the addition of the district’s Safety Plan and a Crisis De-escalation Plan was written into the SP&P. In the supports section, the addition of site-based and district behavior specialists was added.

 
Section I B.3- Collaboration of Public Private Partnership. We no longer have contractual agreements with Educational Alternative Services (EAS) in other districts for PCS students. We have contractual agreements for other counties to send their students to Pinellas County DJJ. The number of staff changed at some of our EAS and the names of the EAS schools changed and is reflected in the updates.
 
Section I C.1- Exceptional Student Education Procedural Safeguards. Minor changes were made to meet federal guidelines.
 
Section I G- General Education Intervention Procedures. Updates to this section reflect changes to the MTSS Manual for PCS.
 
Section II C- IEP. The language for ESY was changed to align general statutes. The section on continuum of services was completely changed to be aligned with current language and practice federally and locally.
 
Section II B.12- Exceptional Education Eligibility for Students with Specific Learning Disabilities. This section has increased detail concerning the process used to determine eligibility that includes multiple reviews of gap analysis, percentage of growth over time via response to intervention.
 
Section II E- Participation in State and District Assessments. Changes were made at each level to submit the districtwide assessments currently being given. The data for percentage of students on FSAA in the 18/19 school year were updated and a statement was added explaining the steps the district has taken to reduce the percentages and will be taking to increase professional development.
 
Section II D- Discipline. Changes were made to this section to include the current training that is occurring in the district on social emotional learning, restorative practices and trauma informed care. Language was added on who serves in various roles in discipline reviews and the steps that need to be taken during alternative placements. Language about CPI levels and who gets the training was changed as it was in the restraint and seclusion section.
 
Appendix C: District Plan to Increase the Participation of Underrepresented Students in the Program for Students who are Gifted. Current data was updated.
 
Appendix D: District Policies Regarding the Allowable Use or Prohibition of Physical Restraint and Seclusion. The Board policy on mechanical restraint was removed because we no longer have contractual agreements with residential placement facilities that would be allowed to use mechanical restraint. The Board policy on Seclusion was uploaded.
 
New Appendix- School closures Due to National or Local Emergency. The Florida Department of Education added an appendix that deals with PCS response during times of national or local emergency. PCS was required to provide supporting documentation used to support and educate staff and families. The documents provided are:
  • PCS ESE Instructional Continuity Guide

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
