4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.1 Presentation of Proclamation Designating January 2021 as National Mentoring Month
Meeting
Jan 12, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Proclamation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:
January is National Mentoring Month.  Pinellas County School District is grateful for all the mentors who pivoted to virtual mentoring to continue meeting the needs of students. Mentoring can create a transformational experience for so many of our students.   Mentors help guide positive choices, develop critical thinkers, support academic achievement and enhance social emotional well-being.  The National Mentoring Month public awareness campaign is led by the National Mentoring Partnership, the Corporation for National and Community Service, the Harvard School of Public Health, the Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention and United Way Worldwide.

 

Research depicts that students who are mentored increase their attendance, perform better academically, graduate from high school, attend college or trade school and are less likely to use drugs and/or alcohol.  Currently, there are over 1,500 students who are attending virtual mentoring sessions using Zoom, Microsoft Teams and Take Stock in Children’s Chat APP.  As a spinoff, Career Conversations and Mentor Coffee Hour are virtual meetings that permit students to discuss careers with keynote speakers, and allow mentors to engage and network with each other, share their experiences and discuss best practices. These initiatives were developed by Ms. Roberge, Volunteer Coordinator, who has been recognized nationally for developing and implementing the Pinellas County School virtual mentoring program.

 

We are proud of Pinellas County Schools’ mentoring programs that include general mentors and tutors, Lunch Pals, Take Stock in Children, 5000 Role Models of Excellence, Girlfriends, St. Petersburg Promise, Big Brothers Big Sisters and Peer to Peer Mentoring.

Lori Matway, Associate Superintendent, Student and Community Support Services

5.1 Meeting Procedures
Meeting
Jan 12, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Information

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

BACKGROUND: 

Seeking to clarify the PCS Policy for determining the graduation honors of summa, magna, cum and with distinction, that are awarded to students at the time of graduation each year.  The change in policy being proposed adds specificity by providing GPA ranges to two decimal places that clearly represents how students qualify for each of those honors categories.

The current wording in policy has been confusing for new high school assistant principals and data management technicians.  The language around GPAs “not being rounded” is unclear and specificity would assist all personnel, especially those new to these positions. Additionally, parents see student GPAs reported to two decimal places within FOCUS/Parent Portal. This proposed amendment will reduce confusion for senior parents as well.  Lastly, schools are adept at ensuring diplomas have all the stickers and designations that our students earn so the need to mail honors seals home so that language has been removed.

Subject
6.2 Public Hearing to Consider the Amendment to Policy 5630.01- Use of Seclusion and Restraint on Students with Disabilities (This is the second reading and there are no changes.)
Meeting
Jan 12, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
No
Recommended Action
Approve the Amendment to Policy 5630.01- Use of Seclusion and Restraint on Students with Disabilities.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment

BACKGROUND

With the Board approval of the ESE SP&P at the October 13, 2020 board meeting, updates  to Section I A- Restraint and Seclusion were made to how incidents are reported by schools and the monitoring/training that will be provided by district-level specialists and committees. The updates to Policy 5630.01- Use of Seclusion and Restraint on Students with Disabilities further clarifies that:

  • A minimum of two staff members should be observing the student being isolated within the seclusion area for the entirety of the seclusion. One staff member will take data on student’s condition and readiness to return to the educational environment. Student should be checked for safety and level of escalation/ de-escalation in two-minute intervals.
  • Any restriction of free movement of hand, wrist, arm, shoulder or back during a physical escort is considered a restraint.
  • A minimum of two trained staff members must be present at all times during the restraint and if the restraint is being used as part of transport, there must be a minimum of three trained staff members. One of the trained staff members must be available for data collection and monitoring of the student’s physical condition.
  • A student with a disability shall not be subjected to mechanical restraint except as specifically authorized in this policy regarding Contracted Residential Facilities.

 

RATIONALE 

Proposed amendments align with changes to the Pinellas County Schools approved State Policies and Procedures (SP&P) for Exceptional Student Education as outlined in Policy 2460 – EXCEPTIONAL STUDENT EDUCATION.

Subject
7.15 Request Approval of the Agreement with the Sutton Place Homeowners Association, Inc. for the Maintenance of the Common Wall Between the Association Property and the Highland Lakes Elementary School Site
Meeting
Jan 12, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action (Consent)
Recommended Action
Approve the Agreement with the Sutton Place Homeowners Association, Inc. for the Maintenance of the Common Wall Between the Association Property and the Highland Lakes Elementary School Site.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:
In 1995 the School Board approved the initial agreement with the Sutton Place Homeowners Association (Association) for the painting of the common wall between the Association property and the Highland Lakes Elementary School site. The Association provides the painting supplies and school district staff paints the top surface and the side of the wall facing Highland Lakes Elementary once every six (6) years. The initial agreement provided for one six (6) year renewal by letter in 2001 which expired in 2007. In 2007 the agreement was approved under the original terms with one six (6) year renewal by letter in 2013, but with the understanding that the agreement must be reviewed and approved by the board before it could be renewed again. The term of this agreement is for a period of six (6) years beginning on January 24, 2021 and ending January 27, 2027.
 
RATIONALE:

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
