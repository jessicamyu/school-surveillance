4.2 Presentation of Proclamation Designating November 2020 as Family Engagement in Education Month
Meeting
Nov 10, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating November 2020 as Family Engagement in Education Month.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

November is designated at Family Engagement in Education Month, a time to celebrate the vital role parents and families play in their children's education.  When schools and families work together to support learning, students are more motivated to succeed-not just in school, but throughout life. During the last eight months, education has pivoted across our country, districts and schools to ensure learning continues for students during the pandemic. We are grateful for all the work families are doing to support schools during a time of adaptation, a time of navigating this new reality. We see these challenging moments as great opportunities to make an impact together.

 

To optimize the engagement experiences, the Office of Strategic Partnerships has developed opportunities for parents to be engaged virtually. Every school has received a Zoom account to connect with their parents and families virtually, webinars on various topics have been facilitated and recordings have been shared with over 2,000 parents and families. A parent support forum, Parent-Guardian Connection, has been created to provide pertinent information to parents in real-time, as well as opportunities to collaborate and support other parents and a Parent Buddy system has been created to provide support to parents and families who have students who are learning virtually.

 

The district has worked diligently to facilitate a new way of work to enhance family engagement events to be results-oriented and ensure parental engagement is linked to learning. Parents can find activities, tools and resources on Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, Instagram and the PCS Family Engagement website. Information is communicated via Peachjar, Zoom, Microsoft Teams, School Messenger, newsletters, emails and texts.

 

Family Engagement in Education Month is a time to celebrate, but efforts to stay connected with our families, hear their voices and support their efforts are continuous. We believe working together as partners will get us to the next level.

Recognize Jeanne Reynolds, PK-12 Performing Arts Specialist, as the recipient of the Dr. Mary Palmer Legacy Trailblazer Award.
BACKGROUND:
The Florida Alliance for Arts Education is a statewide organization whose mission is to improve, enhance and promote arts education in Florida.  Established in 1981, the Alliance performs a wide variety of services including advocacy, arts integration education, standards-based curriculum development and art teacher in residence programs.  Each year, the Alliance recognizes outstanding arts educators and arts advocates through a series of statewide awards.  In 2018, the Alliance established the Dr. Mary Palmer Trailblazer Award, named after one of the organizations founders, former Professor of Arts Education and Dean of the College of Education at the University of Central Florida.  The Dr. Mary Palmer Legacy Trailblazer Award is an award given to honor individuals who have elevated arts education as their core mission over their career.
 
The Pinellas County School Board recognizes and congratulates Jeanne Reynolds for receiving the Dr. Mary Palmer Legacy Trailblazer Award.  Mrs. Reynolds received the award from the Florida Alliance for Arts Education at a virtual ceremony on October 21, 2020.  With thirty-five years as an educator and champion for the arts in Pinellas County Schools, Mrs. Reynolds is, indeed, a trailblazer.  Serving as the PK-12 Performing Arts Specialist for the last twenty-three years, Mrs. Reynolds has dramatically grown PCS performing arts programs through galvanizing community support, recruiting and retaining high-performing arts educators, and modeling a standard of excellence for others to emulate.  
 
Mrs. Reynolds has served as past president of the Florida School Music Supervision Association, the Florida Music Educators Association (FMEA), and served as the Government Relations Chair for FMEA on multiple occasions.  She has received a Distinguished Alumni Award for the University of South Florida and numerous awards from local arts education groups.  Mrs. Reynolds has been a "trailblazer" for Pinellas County students and teachers for decades and certainly deserving of this statewide recognition.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Type

Budgeted

Budget Source
The costs for benefits broker/consulting services and benefit communication will be paid through commissions on select voluntary benefits and through the district operating budget.
Recommended Action
Approve Gallagher Benefit Services for the district's insurance broker/consultant and benefit communications effective January 1, 2021, through December 31, 2024, with an additional 3-year renewal option.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources

BACKGROUND:
Pinellas County Schools currently utilizes Aon for our benefits insurance broker/consultant and benefits communications. Their current agreement expires December 31, 2020. 

 

Our benefit consultant assists with the analysis of our claims, actuary reporting for our self-funded health plan, benefit design consulting, renewal negotiations, contract review and legal compliance with federal, state and insurance rules. They specialize in benefit communication material for our new hires, retirees and annual enrollment. 

 

The district's purchasing department issued an Intent to Negotiate (ITN) for benefits broker/consulting and benefit communication services on May 26, 2020.  The selection committee evaluated the responses and heard oral presentations from the two viable vendors, Aon and Gallagher Benefit Services.  On October 15, 2020, the committee met and recommended Gallagher Benefit Services based upon the scoring, presentations and further discussions with the two finalists.  

 

RATIONALE:

Due to the complexities of benefits and insurance, legal liabilities and the high cost of benefits, it is essential that the district have a benefit consulting firm with a broad range of resources, leverage and experts in this industry, including actuaries who specialize in self-funded health plans. 


The following factors were considered in the recommendation of Gallagher Benefit Services:

  • A global leading broker/consulting firm, with 640 Florida-based clients and 14 Florida school districts
  • Data analytics employer dashboard, providing on-demand reporting and plan design financial projections
  • Self-funded health plan actuary services, including Governmental Accounting Standards Board (GASB) reporting
  • Pharmacy consulting
  • Legal Counsel for benefit-related issues, with $10,000 additional funding for outside legal services, if needed
  • Communications include a full Micro-site, in addition to a benefit mobile-ready app, improving education and engagement   
  • Two onsite representatives to facilitate disability and life insurance applications and claims and to assist with strategic communications for our benefits, retirement and wellness teams throughout the year
  • Dedicated account management support 
  • Competitive pricing 

The proposed benefit consulting and communication fees are $535,000 annually, with a 3% increase in the optional renewal. This is the current amount we are paying for similar services this year.  Approximately $400,000 will be paid through commissions built into our voluntary insurance plans (subject to enrollment) and the balance will be paid through the district operating account.

4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

The Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) Endorsement Program is designed to increase the knowledge, expertise and understanding of teachers working with students diagnosed with Autism Spectrum Disorder.  The endorsement is intended to raise the awareness of the unique characteristics, emotional/social needs, and other challenges and issues related to students with Autism Spectrum Disorder and its range of pervasive developmental disorders that adversely affect functioning and result in the need for specially designed instruction and related services.  It is designed to foster an understanding of the policies and guidelines to ensure implementation of appropriate curriculum, differentiation, strategies, educational intervention and support for this diverse population.

 

The ASD Endorsement Program provides an alternative training program to reduce the number of out-of-field teachers in the area of K-12 students with Autism Spectrum Disorder and to develop a pool of qualified and fully endorsed teachers with this specialized focus.  The option of completing the ASD Endorsement Program is availability for teachers taking online courses made available through limited university options. 

 

The Florida Diagnostic and Learning Resource System has developed a framework for the courses, facilitated by an ASD-endorsed staff member in order to provide the program free of charge for teachers in Pinellas County Schools.  This also offers a greater level of flexibility in ensuring that teachers are able to obtain their endorsement without delays created by limited course offerings through a university program.

 

RATIONALE:
Approval of this program and partnership allows teachers obtaining their ASD endorsement additional options and flexibility at no cost.  This will develop more endorsed and qualified teachers, thereby increasing student achievement.

Approve the Request to Set a Public Hearing and Authorizing the Advertisement of the Public Hearing to Consider the Amendment to Policy 5430- Class Rank.

BACKGROUND:

This proposed amendment seeks to clarify the PCS Policy for determining the graduation honors of summa, magna, cum and with distinction, that are awarded to students at the time of graduation each year. The change in policy being proposed adds specificity by providing GPA ranges to two decimal places that clearly represents how students qualify for each of those honors categories.

 

RATIONALE:

The current wording in policy has been confusing for new high school assistant principals and data management technicians. The language around GPAs “not being rounded” is unclear and specificity would assist all personnel, especially those new to these positions. Additionally, parents see student GPAs reported to two decimal places within FOCUS/Parent Portal. This proposed amendment will reduce confusion for senior parents as well. Lastly, schools are adept at ensuring diplomas have all the stickers and designations that our students earn so the need to mail honors seals home so that language has been removed.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
