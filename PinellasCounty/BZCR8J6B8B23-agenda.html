Subject
4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating the Month of September 2021 as American Founders’ Month; Constitution Day September 17 and Week From September 17-23, 2021; and Celebrate Freedom Week During the Last Full Week of September
Meeting
Sep 14, 2021 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating the month of September 2021 as American Founders’ Month; Constitution Day September 17 and Week From September 17-23, 2021; and Celebrate Freedom Week during the last full week of September.
BACKGROUND:

In alignment with Florida Statutes, Pinellas County Schools proudly supports American Founders’ Month in September and Constitution Day (September 17) and Week (September 17 – 23) as well as Celebrate Freedom Week during the last full week in September. This is a time to educate our students and community about the sacrifices made for freedom in the founding of this country and the values on which this country was founded. Constitution Week and Celebrate Freedom Week help to reaffirm the American ideals of individual liberty. While social studies teachers, in particular, are encouraged to emphasize the teaching of the country’s origins with a focus on the founding documents, additional American Founders’ Month, Constitution Day and Week and Celebrate Freedom Week resources have been provided to all schools to encourage effective instruction throughout the month.

 

Throughout September, the resources provided to teachers and schools can be used as a series of morning announcements that include the history of the American flag, trivia questions about our founding fathers and documents and as a time to recite words from the Declaration of Independence. Social Studies classes around the county engage in taking a deeper look at founding documents and how they continue to shape and influence our country today. The Principal’s Multicultural Advisory Committee’s (PMAC) and social studies teachers continue to work to bring schools together throughout the year by utilizing guest speakers like local lawmakers, university professors and veterans to talk with students about civil service, experiences with the law and our role as citizens.

Meeting

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Recommended Action
Approve the the Amendments to Policy 9270 - Home Education
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

The district is responsible for evaluating and identifying home school students who may need special education or gifted services; however, the law does not require and the district does not provide these services to home school students unless the student enrolls in a district school. The Florida Department of Education’s Bureau of Exceptional Education has recently recommended that each district’s policy should clearly state the special education and gifted services provisions for home school students. 

 

The proposed amendment to this policy adds two sections: one clarifying eligibility provisions for home school students to receive ESE services, and one clarifying the provisions for home school students to qualify for gifted services.

 

RATIONALE:

The amendment, as recommended by the Florida Department of Education, clarifies district responsibilities for the evaluation, identification and possible provision of services for students who may need special education or gifted services as defined in Florida Statute 1002.41(10) and Florida Administrative Code 6A-6.0331.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

The Professional Learning Catalog (PLC) is a state-required planning document for professional education inservice training that must be updated annually.  The PLC aligns with our District Strategic Plan and lists all the professional development opportunities that are offered to Pinellas County Schools' employees.

 

Professional goals guide the selection of courses employees select within the training system.  At the school level, the School Improvement Planning process includes the use of student data and teacher evaluation data to determine training needs for instructional staff at the school sites.  During the 2020-2021 school year, there were 102,847 courses completed throughout Pinellas County Schools by all employee groups. Courses in Instructional Strategies, ESE Classroom Management, Reading and Laws, Rules and Policies were listed in the top 10 most attended professional development courses.  Participation in courses focused on instructional strategies increased by 9.35%.  This increase is likely attributed to our transition towards technology integration within instructional practice.  Aspiring administrators participated in various assessments to determine areas of focus for a personalized pathway towards their professional growth. 

 

Support employees identify goals while working toward their Certificate of Distinction (COD), thus providing direction toward a choice of courses taken to achieve their goals.  The 2020-2021 Support Catalog of Choice day was offered to support employees in furthering their professional growth and featured 44 virtual courses serving 1,359 participants.  In total 4,080 support employees participated in professional development during the 2020-2021 school year.

 

During the summer of 2021, the district offered 410 professional development courses with 1,181 sessions. More than 7,000 teachers, support staff, and administrators attended at least one day of Professional Development during this timeframe.  Additionally, 252 newly hired teachers attended Embrace Pinellas (our 4-day new teacher orientation) in the summer of 2021 as a kickoff for the 2021-2022 school year.

 

The Professional Learning Catalog for 2021-2022 was developed by a cross-functional team representing all levels of the school system.  The cross-functional team utilized a variety of 2020-2021 school and district data to plan for the 2021-2022 school year.  As a result, Pinellas County Schools began the school year with 432 courses available for our employees to consider.  This is up from 291 courses available during the 2020-2021 school year.

 
RATIONALE:
The Florida Department of Education (FDOE) requires each district to develop a comprehensive Professional Learning Catalog that is revised annually, board-approved, and submitted to the FDOE by October 1.

Budget Source
The cost for PrudentRx Solutions (PrudentRx) is 25% of specialty drug savings, which is estimated at $525,000 annually, paid against the self-insured health fund.
Recommended Action
Approve amendments to Aetna's Agreement with updated Pharmacy terms and conditions, including PrudentRx Solutions as a Specialty Prescription Management Program.
BACKGROUND:
In 2020, Pinellas County Schools' overall pharmacy costs were approximately $27.8M.  Specialty drugs were a leading driver of the overall pharmacy spend, accounting for 57.3% of the overall pharmacy costs.  Specialty drugs are Tier 4 high-cost drugs on the health plan formulary and require authorization, purchase through CVS Specialty Pharmacy and are prescribed for very serious health conditions. 

 

To further control our costs and manage our pharmacy program, we requested our benefits' broker/consultant, Gallagher Benefit Services, perform a Pharmacy Market Price Check and review Pharmacy Copay Assistance Programs, available in the marketplace for self-insured employers.  

 

Pharmacy Market Price Check 

The check resulted in Aetna offering a more competitive pharmacy renewal agreement, retroactive to 1/1/21, and a cost avoidance of $2.2M.  Aetna will remain as our third-party administrator for the medical and pharmacy plan and have an exclusive specialty drug arrangement through December 31, 2024.

 
Pharmacy Copay Assistance Program Review
Gallagher recommended PrudentRx Solutions based on estimated savings, fee structures, guarantees and rebate guarantee impact. Their program was the least disruptive and seamless option for our members. This recommendation was supported by the Employee Wellbeing and Satisfaction Committee and Bargaining Leadership Team.
 

PrudentRX Copay Optimization Program Effective January 1, 2022

Additional details of the PrudentRx Copay Assistance Program are as follows:

  • Program integrates with CVS Specialty Pharmacy.
  • Approximately 490 members were identified as utilizers of specialty drugs. 
  • Coinsurance for all specialty drugs on PCS formulary will be designated as 30% coinsurance allowing PrudentRx to secure the maximum manufacturer's rebates. 
  • Upon activation/enrollment with the program, members will have $0 out-of-pocket costs for exclusive specialty drugs.
  • High-touch extensive communication plan is offered in a proactive multichannel outreach.
  • PrudentRx fee is 25% of savings, paid through the health insurance fund.
  • Net savings of the program is expected to generate $2.1M (in addition to the Market Check savings).

RATIONALE:

Approval of the amendment to Aetna's third-party administrative services and pharmacy benefit, including the addition of PrudentRx, will result in an overall annual plan savings of approximately $4.3M. The PrudentRx copay optimization program provides a positive financial impact for the district's health plan and members. These savings have been reflected in the overall health insurance rates proposed for 2022, and confirmed through our benefit consultant's actuarial department.

  • student and district performance;
  • requirements of state and federal mandates; and
  • input and feedback received from students, parents, community members, partners and staff members throughout the year.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
