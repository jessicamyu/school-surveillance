4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

January is National Mentoring Month and Pinellas County Schools celebrates over 3,600 active mentors. Research has shown that when matched through a quality mentoring program, mentors can play a powerful role in providing young people with the tools and strategies to make responsible decisions, stay focused and engaged in school. The National Mentoring Month public awareness campaign is led by the National Mentoring Partnership, the Corporation for National and Community Service, the Harvard School of Public Health, the Office of Juvenile Justice and Delinquency Prevention, and United Way Worldwide.

 

Pinellas County Schools’ mentoring programs include general mentors, Lunch Pals, Take Stock in Children, 5000 Role Models of Excellence, Girlfriends, Cohort of Champions (City of St. Petersburg), Big Brothers Big Sisters, and Peer-to-Peer Mentoring. We also have mentors supporting students in our afterschool programs. The "Men of Yesterday, Today, and Future" is a new mentoring program that supports male students in the 21st Century program at Campbell Park, Midtown, and Fairmount Park elementary schools. They provide guidance, wisdom, experience and friendship to these students.

Subject
4.2 Presentation of the 2018-2019 Independent Citizens Referendum Oversight Committee (ICROC) Annual Report to the Pinellas County School Board and Citizens of Pinellas County, Presented by, Reagan Miller, Chairperson, Independent Citizens Referendum Oversight Committee
Meeting
Jan 14, 2020 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Presentation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

On November 2, 2004, January 29, 2008, November 6, 2012, and again on November 8, 2016, the citizens of Pinellas County voted to increase the ad valorem millage one-half mill for necessary operating expenses including funds to recruit and retain quality teachers, supplement reading programs, enhance music and art programs, and provide up-to-date materials and technology in Pinellas County schools. The School Board established specific target areas for the expenditure of these funds and a process for allocating the referendum dollars, outlining clear parameters for both monitoring and reporting expenditures to the public.

 

The Independent Citizens Referendum Oversight Committee (ICROC) functions as an advisory body to the School Board. The committee’s purpose is to monitor the expenditure of referendum dollars over the four-year implementation period. The committee reviews quarterly expenditure reports that detail the items purchased by the specific program areas of teacher compensation, reading, visual arts, performing arts, and technology. In addition, the committee further examines the detailed narratives specifying the use of the items in each program area and emphasizes how schools benefit from the expenditure of these funds.

 

This time of year allows the ICROC Chairperson to present the 2018-2019 Annual Report summarizing the expenditures for this year. It is important to note that while these funds follow the intent of the referendum guidelines to recruit and retain quality teachers, enhance program initiatives, update equipment and instructional materials to positively impact student learning, these funds are not used as replacement dollars for existing department budgets.

 

Pinellas County is proud of the community support achieved over this fourteen year period and that our staff and students have benefited greatly from the thoughtful expenditures of the funds.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment

BACKGROUND:

Senate Bill 2500 (Chapter 2019-115, Laws of Florida, General Appropriations Act) appropriated $50,000,000 in non-recurring capital outlay funds to the Florida Department of Education for school districts to improve the physical security of school buildings based on the security risk assessment conducted in consultation with local law enforcement. District staff works closely with Schools Police and all local law enforcement agencies which serve district schools to conduct comprehensive security risk assessments each summer. The results of these assessments are analyzed and used to shape the districts’ security plan which is shared with Schools Police and partner law enforcement agencies before adoption. All proceeds of this grant will be used to further enhance the security of school sites as identified during the security risk assessments and defined by the district security plan. School districts are required to provide charter schools their proportionate share of funds upon receipt of the charter school’s security risk assessment.

RATIONALE:

If awarded, the district will use their allocation to install IP speakers as part of an integrated system to allow instant communication both districtwide and with partner agencies. Safer classrooms, offices, and ancillary spaces will be created by expanding the use of keyless access control systems that allows for quick lockdown of facilities.

 

Charter Schools will use their allocation to install signage and IP speakers and panic stations as part of an integrated system to allow instant communication. Safer classrooms, offices, and ancillary spaces will be created by installing security cameras, access control systems, and upgrading existing doors, hardware and lighting fixtures. Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

For many years the Pinellas County School’s Visual Arts office has utilized long-standing partnerships with local arts institutions to create and re-create two traveling mini-museums called “Art Mobiles." After a museum has agreed to be the partner for one of the art mobiles, they select and furnish reproductions of the works of art as well as design the interior and exterior for these themed exhibitions. The museums also produce an educational film that is viewed by students in the art mobile before they explore the works. District art teachers work with each institution to ensure that the works are suitable to support their curriculum. The art institutions complete all of this at no charge to the district.

 

Over the span of three years, each portable mini-museum travels across the county visiting the Pinellas County public elementary schools making it possible for every K-5 elementary student to experience the look and feel of an art museum without leaving their school grounds, resulting in almost 14,000 students attending each year.  

 

These partnerships with local art institutions give students the opportunity to benefit from a museum-like experience tailored specifically to their grade level. During this experience, students learn about the artists, the history and meaning of the artworks and get to have an up close interactive experience with the works, while teachers address several art learning goals, such as museum etiquette, in a more relevant setting.  In many cases, these mini-museum visits give students who wouldn’t otherwise have the opportunity a chance to experience the scale and details of the artwork they are studying in a more authentic environment.

 

Through Referendum funding, upgrades and upkeep to the mobile classrooms used for the mini-museums has been possible. Referendum also supports the Art Mobile program by providing funding for the teachers doing the curriculum work to prepare the lessons.

 

RATIONALE:

By providing a formal agreement between the district and a museum partner, the expectations for both parties are outlined to maintain a quality educational experience for students. This Memorandum of Understanding with The Salvador Dali Museum continues a partnership with the museum to bring the works of Dali directly to our students.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
