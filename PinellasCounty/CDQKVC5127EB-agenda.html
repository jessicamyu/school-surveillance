4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating November 2022 as Family Engagement in Education Month
Meeting
Nov 15, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating November 2022 as Family Engagement in Education Month.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

November is designated as Family Engagement in Education Month, a time to celebrate the vital role parents and families play in their children’s education. When schools and families work together to support learning, students are more motivated to succeed - not just in school, but throughout life.

 

Pinellas County Schools is committed to partnering with families for the successful academic, social and emotional development of children through education, empowerment and support. The Office of Strategic Partnerships helps support the efforts of family engagement through Parent Advocacy resources, Hispanic Outreach efforts, Parent Academy Power Hour webinars and Family Engagement trainings. With each activity, parents have an opportunity to learn and practice new skills and strategies to support learning at home, while also sharing and incorporating knowledge about their child.

 

Additionally, the Office of Strategic Partnerships provides continuous training to the schools’ Family and Community Liaisons to improve and maximize family engagement resources and opportunities at the school site. Furthermore, they provide training to school leaders, teachers, support staff and parents on effective strategies to foster positive communication and authentic collaboration between families and schools to increase student achievement.

 

Family Engagement in Education Month is a time to celebrate the accomplishments of our successful family and school partnerships, which are built and sustained throughout the year.

 
SUBMITTED BY:
Lori Matway, Associate Superintendent, Student and Community Support Services

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak during the public hearing and regarding the topic of the public hearing.

 

In each case, you

  1. Must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below;
  2. Must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers for that speaking opportunity or before the last speaker for that opportunity concludes their comments, whichever is later;
  3. Will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee;
  4. Will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson or a majority vote of the Board; and
  5. May not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.

 

The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. If a speaker wants to supplement their three (3) minute presentation, the speaker should provide the Board office with written materials prior to the meeting. Board members will make every effort to review these materials. Board members may not be able to review materials received less than two (2) business days prior to the Board meeting.
  2. The Board will not act on any matter raised by a speaker prior to the next regular meeting. If a majority of the Board desires to further consider a matter raised by a speaker, they will request the Superintendent to bring a recommendation at a future workshop or meeting or otherwise address the issue. It is generally the Board's practice not to respond to the issues brought forward by speakers except to correct inaccuracies or clarify issues.
  3. Speakers may not use any of the speaking opportunities to advertise or otherwise promote the interests of any commercial or other non-school organizations, except when recognizing school or district partners or when one of the exceptions in Policy 9700.01 applies. Further, no speaker may advertise or otherwise promote a political candidate, question or party.

 

Regulation of Disruptive Speech

 

Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.

 

To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive or otherwise interferes with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, as stated in s. 1001.372(3), Florida Statutes; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

 

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.

 

Recording of Board Meetings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Recommended Action
Approval of two-year renewal for Aetna Resource for Living Employee Assistance Plan Agreement effective January 1, 2023, through December 31, 2024, and the Aetna letter of understanding.

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools offers an Employee Assistance Plan (EAP) through Aetna Resource for Living (RFL) to employees and their family members living in the household. The current agreement expires December 31, 2022, although it allows for a two-year renewal option to extend services. The services include:

 

  • Up to 8 free face-to-face or telehealth counseling sessions per member per issue
  • Unlimited Telephonic counseling 24/7 for real-time support
  • Talkspace Chat and video therapy counseling
  • Worklife services, including legal and financial referral services
  • Website with tools and resources
  • Onsite Trainer allocated for 26 hours per week

 

Due to increased utilization in 2021 and projections for continued high utilization, RFL is requesting a premium increase of 18% in 2023 and 15% in 2024.  In 2021, Pinellas County Schools' utilization was 18.3%, compared to RFL's book of business of 5.3%.

 

RATIONALE:

Renewal of this agreement allows our employees and their household family members to continue to receive emotional well-being support and counseling, as needed. The district's current 2022 annual premium for EAP is projected at $405,960 ($1.99 per employee per month) for approximately 17,000 full time, part-time and substitute employees. BACKGROUND:

Each year, the district reviews policies in alignment with state legislation and Florida State Board of Education Rule updates, as well as district and state practices. The proposed amendments to the policies below align with state updates noted below.

 

Policies regarding instructional materials include Policy 2240 – Controversial Issues; Policy 2510 – Instructional Materials, Including Textbooks; and Policy 2540 – Use of Multi-Media. The amendments to these policies update current procedures to better align with the requirements of HB 1467 and State Board Rule 6A-7.0713, which state School Boards shall:

  • Adopt procedures for library media center collections and post the procedures on the website, and
  • Adopt policy for a parent or a resident of the county to object to the use of specific material, including a description of the process for addressing and resolving objections.

In alignment with State Board Rule 6A.-10.085, the amendment for Policy 2340 – Field Trips and Other District-sponsored events adds a new bullet regarding room sharing on overnight trips. The policy and associated field trip form specify that the room-sharing is assigned by biological sex at birth unless parents of minor students otherwise provide consent in writing. Additionally, the last section of the policy has been broadened to address any competitive team’s overnight trips, where it previously specified cheerleading.

 

Proposed amendments to Policy 5455 - Florida Bright Futures Scholarship reflect the revised state requirements, which now allows for work hours to be counted in addition to service hours. Student hours are no longer required to address a social issue. This policy was broadened to allow for more students to attain the hours required to qualify for the scholarships through a variety of methods.

 

Updates to Policy 9800 - Charter Schools address SB 758 and HB 225, which provide charter schools the opportunity to apply directly to the Florida Charter School Review Commission. Additionally, the amendments clearly articulate the Pinellas County School Board expectations to organizations applying for a Pinellas County Charter School. These updates are timely, the district has two renewals coming this year.

 

RATIONALE:

The proposed amendments to these policies ensure alignment with updated state requirements as well as district procedures.

  • 4.25% average salary increase for all instructional staff.
  • Increased teacher starting salary to $50,568.
  • The School Board will pay 100% (approximately $5.5 million) of the increase in healthcare costs for all employees.
  • The School Board will pay approximately $5.2 million for the increased Florida Retirement System contribution for all employees with no additional cost to employees.
  • Two additional paid days of planning for all instructional employees and an additional two days of paid planning for ESE teachers, paid at a teacher's hourly rate. 
  • District contributed $3,000,000 to be used for a non-recurring bonus to be distributed at the sole discretion of PCTA and described on the attached document.
  • District contributed an additional $257,000 to increase Advanced Degree Supplements.
  • District contributed an additional $108,000 to increase supplements to teachers who perform supplementary duties for athletics, art and curricular activities outside the school day.

 

   Article 31 - Teacher Evaluation

  • Changed language to state that only teachers with three or fewer years of experience or who are currently on a success plan will collaborate with their administrator to identify targeted elements on their Deliberate Practice.
  • For probationary teachers, adjusted the number of required observations from three to two and added an optional targeted observation.
  • For annual, professional service contract and continuing contract teachers, adjusted the number of required observations from two to one.

 

  Article 38 - Specialized Clinical Services

  • School Counselors are added to the groups covered in this article.
  • Added language specific to Specialized Clinical Services staff to reflect:
    • Schedules will be collaboratively developed and reviewed with Principal.
    • A private space will be provided for individual or small group confidential sessions.
    • Staff members who are itinerant or have "split" schedules will be excluded from the school's duty schedule during the student day.
    • An alternative performance evaluation form will be developed by the professional development department with input from the Appraisal Advisory Committee.

RATIONALE:

The respective bargaining teams worked collaboratively to examine healthcare options and financial information impacting the availability of funds for salary increases and also revised contract language and believe that the tentative agreement reached maximizes the benefits for instructional employees.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
