4.1 Recognition of Energy Star Partner of the Year Award
Meeting
May 10, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Recognition
Goals

BACKGROUND:

ENERGY STAR is a program run jointly by the United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and the United State Department of Energy to promote energy efficiency across all sectors of the economy.  ENERGY STAR’s certification of household products and appliances gives consumers valuable information to make purchasing decisions that will lead to lower utility bills through energy efficiency.  Likewise, building owners are provided insight into how to operate their buildings efficiently through the ENERGY STAR building certification program.  In short, the ENERGY STAR program gives consumers and property owners the tools and technology to follow the science of energy conservation.

 

This month, the EPA will present an award to Pinellas County Schools as the 2022 ENERGY STAR Partner of the Year for demonstrating exemplary commitment and dedication to leadership in energy efficiency.  This marks the fourth consecutive year that Pinellas County Schools has been named an ENERGY STAR Partner of the Year.  In addition, Pinellas County Schools is the only school district in the country to receive the Partner of the Year Award for Energy Management each of those years.

 

District staff wishes to thank our energy conservation partner, Cenergistic, for their support in achieving this recognition and thank the Board for their support of the district energy conservation program.

Clinton Herbic, Associate Superintendent, Operational Services

4.2 Recognition of Two Student Scholarship Winners for the Florida Prepaid College Scholarship
Meeting
May 10, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

The Florida Prepaid College Board has entered into partnerships with several school districts throughout the state of Florida to promote open enrollment for their Prepaid College Plan. As part of the partnership, the Florida Prepaid College Board will provide college scholarships (valued at $8,000 each) to the district to award to graduating seniors.   

 

Graduating seniors complete an application through the Pinellas Education Foundation Scholarship program. A committee reviews the applications and selects students with an interest in pursuing an education career.  The plan is designed to be used at a Florida college or a state university and prorated accordingly. Plans can also be used at in-state, out-of-state, public or private schools around the country; the value paid to other schools is the same as Florida Prepaid would have paid to a Florida school based on the student’s plan type. The student will have up to six years to use the plan.

 

This year’s recipients are Zoe Webster and Annie Ho. 

  • Zoe Webster is a senior from Boca Ciega High with a GPA of 4.4236. She plans to attend the University of South Florida.
  • Annie Ho is also a senior from Boca Ciega High. She has a GPA of 4.4514 and plans to attend the University of Florida.

 

Congratulations to our two scholarship recipients!

4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.3 Recognition of Superintendent Michael A. Grego's Leadership, Contributions and Service to Juvenile Welfare Board
Meeting
May 10, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Recognition

BACKGROUND:

In Pinellas County, children are 16% of the population and 100% of our future. The Juvenile Welfare Board (JWB) understands this and has been investing in the futures of children for 75 years.

 

Today, we are here to shine a light on that partnership, and more importantly, on Superintendent Dr. Michael A. Grego, whose strong leadership, guidance and contributions have served us well on the JWB Board over the past ten years.

Throughout his tenure and service on the JWB Board, Dr. Grego has been a steadfast leader and staunch advocate for quality early learning, universal PreK and VPK, out-of-school time and summer programming, intensive literacy instruction and mental health interventions and support.

 

Under his leadership, JWB has one of the most robust data sharing agreements in place with the district. This provides our organization with the ability to evaluate JWB school readiness and school success program portfolios more effectively. Plus, these data are the lynchpin to our Pinellas Child Opportunity Index, a composite index designed to measure child opportunity and gauge the health of our neighborhoods at the census tract level – of which Education is one of the top three domains.

 

JWB partners with the district at every level, from how JWB-funded school success interventions are structured, to which students and which schools need services most; to how JWB collects and reports data. JWB and the district are committed to continuous quality improvement. Together, we consult regularly regarding which students and schools most need JWB’s deeper-end interventions, including intensive literacy instruction and Middle School Academies and work to identify and fill gaps.

 

JWB has worked closely with Dr. Grego and his team to better align JWB-funded out-of-school time and summer programs to meet the district's needs. Examples include:

 

  • Promise Time is comprised of three main components: out-of-school time (OST) programming, online learning, and small group tutoring. Promise Time targets students who are low-performing or struggling and is a partnership between 47 Title One elementary schools, JWB and four OST providers: YMCA Suncoast, YMCA of Greater St. Petersburg, City of Dunedin, and R’Club Child Care.

 

  • Summer Bridge Wrap offers before- and after-care for students enrolled in the district’s Summer Bridge program, an educational program targeting low-performing students to help mitigate summer learning loss. JWB’s funding of wrap support extends the learning day, as well as the number of weeks during the summer to give working parents peace of mind that their children are safe and well.

 

  • Middle School Academies are education-based out-of-school time programs that extend the student’s learning day, providing safety and supervision to youth of working parents while enhancing student academic achievement and improving social skills. These out-of-school time programs exist in 13 middle schools in our county.

 

Dr. Grego has been a strong advocate for on-time grade-level reading and enhanced literacy skills for students.

 

  • Literacy Programming: Dr. Grego was an integral force behind JWB’s investments in intensive literacy instruction and maintenance programming, as well as the expansion of these programs in FY22. JWB consulted with several district literacy personnel to best understand and structure school- and community-based interventions for students. This resulted in three providers offering literacy instruction and maintenance programming at school and community sites: Boys and Girls Clubs of the Suncoast, YMCA of Greater St. Petersburg, and Shirley Proctor Puller Foundation. JWB also funds literacy outreach through R’Club Child Care and its ReadStrong Pinellas initiative.

 

  • Pinellas Grade-Level Reading Campaign: Dr. Grego and his team were key in the launch of the Pinellas Grade-Level Reading Campaign: Early Readers, Future Leaders. The Campaign’s vision is that all children will be proficient readers by third grade and focuses on five core factors associated with on-time reading proficiency: School Readiness, School Attendance, Summer Learning, Parent and Family Engagement and Healthy Readers.

 

The Juvenile Welfare Board would like to take this opportunity to publicly acknowledge and thank Pinellas County Schools Superintendent Dr. Michael A. Grego for his steadfast leadership, countless contributions, and decade of service to the children and families of Pinellas County, as a member of the JWB Board.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Approval of this grant will provide supplemental funding in the amount of $2,400,000 to support four community learning center sites which includes Lakewood, Melrose, High Point and Gulfport elementary schools.
Recommended Action
Approve to apply for Nita M. Lowey 21st Century Community Learning Centers grants, August 1, 2022, through July 31, 2026, requesting funding in the amount of $2,400,000 and authorize district administration to finalize, implement and amend the grant as needed.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

On May 20, 2022, the district may submit two Nita M. Lowey 21st Century Community Learning Centers (CCLC) grant proposals to the Florida Department of Education (FDOE). Each proposal may serve a maximum of two community learning center sites; the first proposal would serve Lakewood and Melrose elementary schools and the second proposal would serve High Point and Gulfport elementary schools. Each site will serve 60 students beginning August 1, 2022, to July 31, 2026. The grant total for four years is approximately 2.4 million dollars. The purpose of the 21st CCLC program is to enhance student academic achievement and personal enrichment through high-quality after-school activities and enhanced family engagement programming. A few of our 21st Century Learning Centers program highlights include CPR Training, Gardening, the Edible Peace Patch Project, STEM, Snapology (STEM-related activities) and field trips to local vendors such as the Alligator and Wildlife Discovery Center.

 

RATIONALE:

The Nita M. Lowey 21st CCLC grant is an innovative extended learning model focused on improving the academic performance of students with the greatest need through a holistic, community-based approach. This project is an expansion of a successful model that is yielding positive results in seven (7) other schools in the district. The program provides students with engaging project-based activities in reading, math and science as well as personal enrichment activities to support character development, health and wellness and life skills. Additionally, family engagement activities focused on mobilizing parents as problem-solvers and empowering them to become successful advocates for their children.

The process leads to an intervention plan and steps that one can test to improve the student's situation. The FBA informs a teaching plan that can develop a more acceptable alternative behavior for the student that will not interfere with the student's education.  The development of a targeted FBA is challenging and extremely time consuming. RATIONALE:
Insights to Behavior is a web-based application that empowers users to quickly create a legally defensible Behavior Intervention Plan with research-based strategies in less than an hour. The program allows users to track, manage and modify student behaviors and includes an extensive professional development training library including components on behavior interventions that match the function of the determined behavior. In addition with this program, school-based and district administration will have access to a single database of all behavior plans across the district.
 
Staff report that the program has decreased the amount of time it takes to complete these assessments and plans. Staff also report improved access to evidence-based interventions that resulted in improved student behavior. After utilizing the program for a full year, it has proven to be extremely helpful and a multi-year contract is recommended. By entering into a 3-year contract, there will be a savings of $31,650 over the life of the agreement.

Subject
8.2 Request Approval of Issuance of a Ninety (90) Day Notice to Terminate the Charter Agreement With NorthStar Academy, Inc., and Authorize Appropriate District Staff to Prepare and Transmit the Notice
Meeting
May 10, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action
Recommended Action
Approve the Issuance of a Ninety (90) Day Notice to terminate the Charter Agreement with NorthStar Academy, Inc., and authorize appropriate district staff to prepare and transmit the notice.
BACKGROUND:
On January 14, 2019, the board approved a five-year charter agreement for NorthStar Academy, Inc (NorthStar Academy of Pinellas). The school serves grades 7th - 12th and currently has 96 students registered with less than 70% in attendance. The Governing Board of NorthStar Academy, Inc. has been issued three notices of deficiency. These notices were issued on March 8, 2021, December 15, 2021, and February 14, 2022. The notices of deficiency outlined complaint information filed against NorthStar Academy of Pinellas with the Office of Inspector General - Florida Department of Education and placed the school on corrective action for a deteriorating financial condition. In response and to stabilize the school's finances, the management company, Edison Learning extended a promissory note for $683,863 that would be payable over 36 months commencing on July 1, 2022. However, a review of the school's financials proved the operating costs would exceed the amount of the promissory note. In an email dated May 4, 2022, Edison Learning issued a notice to the Governing Board of NorthStar Academy of Pinellas that it will no longer provide financial support to the school.
 
RATIONALE:
The Pinellas County Schools found NorthStar Academy of Pinellas in default of the contractual agreement between NorthStar Academy, Inc. and the School Board of Pinellas County. The default includes failure to provide Career Academy courses, failure to meet  F.S. 1002.33(7)(2)(a) which requires that reading is a primary focus of the curriculum, failure to implement appropriate services to students with disabilities and failure of the school to meet generally accepted standards of fiscal management. The school continues to be in a deteriorating financial condition and without the financial support of the management company and an enrollment that is less than 70% of the projected enrollment, the school has no ability to generate enough income to sustain financially viability.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
