As a result of the public health emergency that exists due to the COVID-19 VIRUS, and upon the authority granted by Executive Orders issued by Governor Ron DeSantis, including Executive Order 20-69, the School Board of Pinellas County, Florida, hereby gives notice that it will conduct a virtual public meeting using telephonic technology on June 23, 2020, at 10 a.m.

 

The public can attend this virtual meeting by listening to the meeting live by calling one of the following phone numbers: 1-929-205-6099 or 1-888-788-0099 (toll-free) and entering meeting ID Number 843 0758 0735, or visiting www.pcsb.org/boardvideo.

The public may submit comments to the School Board by: (1) sending them in writing to the following email address: publiccomment@pcsb.org, or (2) calling 727-588-6122 and orally providing the comments, which will be transcribed.

Recommended Action
Approval of the district's Casualty Insurance Program effective July 1, 2020, through June 30, 2021.
BACKGROUND:
USI Insurance Services, the district's property/casualty insurance broker, completed the marketing and negotiations of our casualty insurance benefit program, for coverage effective July 1, 2020. Our district's proposed insurance coverage includes the following:
 
1.  General, Automotive, and Employment Liability Coverage: This policy, offered through Safety National, covers the district for general liability occurrences, automobile liability, employment practices liability, sexual abuse liability, and employee benefits liability.
 
2.  Travelers Crime Coverage: This policy, offered through Travelers, covers the district for all financial losses associated with theft of funds.   
 
3.  Storage Tank Liability Coverage: These policies, offered by Commerce & Industry Insurance Company and Iron Shore/Liberty Mutual, are for on-site storage tanks. The policies cover pollution liability and the limits and deductibles vary with the age of the storage tanks.  
 
4.  Catastrophic Student Accident Coverage: This policy, offered by ACE American Insurance Company, provides students with secondary insurance if they suffer an injury in excess of $25,000 associated with athletic events, band, cheerleading, intramural sports and non-sport extra-curricular activities. This policy is in compliance with Florida High School Athletic Association (FHSAA) requirements. 
 
5.  Cyber Liability Coverage: This policy, offered by Lloyd's of London, provides coverage, services and expense reimbursements surrounding network and security breaches and data recovery expenses should an incident or breach of our computer systems' security occur affecting private student and employee information. 
 
RATIONALE:
Pinellas County Schools' casualty insurance program provides insurance coverage, subject to deductibles, for losses that may occur during operations. 
 

USI Insurance Services marketed the general, automobile and employment-related liability coverages to 14 separate carriers. Safety National is recommended as our new liability insurer for coverage effective July 1, 2020.  Safety National offered the best rates and coverage. They are a strong A+ financially-rated company, with a large presence providing insurance coverage for Florida school districts. There are no Coronavirus exclusions in this policy.     

 
The total premium cost for all lines of coverage is estimated not to exceed $454,628, an overall reduction of $51,710 from our 2019/2020 expiring policies. Please see the attached 2020 Casualty proposal for coverage limits and self-insured retention amounts.

Budget Source
VPK programs are not funded as part of the state’s K-12 Florida Education Finance Program (FEFP). Families must apply for a Certificate of Eligibility (voucher) for the school year or summer program through the Early Learning Coalition of Pinellas County. The district is then reimbursed by the Early Learning Coalition through attendance verification for the VPK hours, up to $2,442 per child for the school year program and up to $2,085 per child for the summer program. During the school year, families who opt to extend the program to a full school day pay a weekly tuition fee of $65. For the 2020-2021 school year, six schools will be supported with Title I funding so qualifying students can attend for the full school day without having to pay tuition.
Recommended Action
Approve the State of Florida Voluntary Prekindergarten Education Program Statewide Provider Contract with the Early Learning Coalition of Pinellas County.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Pursuant to the Florida Constitution Article IX, Section 1, the district offers a 540-hour instructional Voluntary Prekindergarten program (VPK). The program is a free, three-hour a day program with families having the option to extend the day to a full school day program through tuition. In Pinellas County Schools, the VPK program is currently implemented in 110 classrooms at 68 elementary schools and two (2) high schools during the school year. Most classrooms are blended with Exceptional Student Education (ESE) prekindergarten students to provide inclusion opportunities. Each year, programs are added at selected locations as space permits. Required in the implementing legislation is the obligation of all school districts to administer a 300-hour summer program in the public schools. The district uses available elementary sites to offer the summer VPK program to students who will be entering kindergarten in the fall. The program is approximately eight and a half hours a day for students depending on the number of summer days available for programming.

 

The district has been a VPK provider since 2005. In order to continue offering the VPK program and receive reimbursement from the Early Learning Coalition of Pinellas County, the district must enter into an agreement with the Early Learning Coalition of Pinellas County each program year. This agreement defines provider eligibility, program requirements, child eligibility, enrollment and attendance, and compensation and funding.

 

RATIONALE:

The district wants all children to enter kindergarten ready to learn. By offering a comprehensive VPK program, students are able to participate in a curriculum that builds a solid foundation in the areas of social/emotional development, approaches to learning, physical development, language and literacy, mathematical thinking, scientific inquiry, social studies, and creative expression through the arts. This foundation prepares students to be successful in kindergarten. As space in schools has allowed, the district has been able to increase the capacity of VPK programs from 306 children during the 2006-2007 school year to over 1,700 available seats for VPK children for the 2020-2021 school year. This increase in capacity allows more children to enter kindergarten ready to learn.

SCAP will equip students with work-ready skills to bolster job opportunities, as well as, provide the skills and experiences to impact success after high school. With the transition to remote learning, the students and teachers of Pinellas County Schools successfully shifted to digital, at-home learning during the final quarter of the 2019-20 school year. As a district, students participated at high-rates, engaged in small group and individual support sessions and independently accessed online instructional programs. Students logged-in to district digital resources over a million times each week. To continue learning, teachers developed live and recorded lessons and worked with families and support staff to meet individual student needs. To do this, the district distributed over 26,000 computer devices and, through partnerships with Spectrum, the Pinellas Education Foundation, the Juvenile Welfare Board and other community agencies, ensured that families in need had access to the internet.

PCS Connects is a new, bold strategic initiative to ensure every student in PCS has a computer device, internet access and regularly accesses digital learning supports so that, as a district, we leverage this moment to accelerate student achievement.  

 

RATIONALE: 

To accomplish the bold vision of PCS Connects, and eliminate the digital divide for Pinellas County Schools’, the district will strategically utilize funding to enhance how students engage, connect, extend and enrich the daily instructional practices within our classrooms and at-home.  The initiative will phase in over the next several years, with current devices in the district inventory being utilized until full implementation in 2023.   

 

During the initial year of implementation, in the 2020-21 school year, PCS Connects will provide new, up-to-date computer devices to all students in grades 4-9, beginning the transition to a full one-to-one school district.  Students in need of a device at home in grades K-3 and 10-12 will be provided a device currently in the PCS inventory for their use.
