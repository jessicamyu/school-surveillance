4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation of National African American History Month, February 2022
Meeting
Jan 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Adopt the proclamation of National African American History Month, February 2022.
BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools (PCS) celebrate the many achievements and contributions of local, state, and national African American figures throughout the year and particularly during the month of February, National African American History Month. This is a time to highlight the struggles, successes and contributions of the African American and Black community in the United States. The theme for African American History month 2022 is, “Black Health and Wellness.”

 

Since 2015, the Commissioner of Education’s African American History Task Force recognized Pinellas County Schools as an Exemplary School District for our work to maximize the integration of African American History into our curriculum. To date, the approved exemplary status has only been awarded to ten school districts in the state of Florida. This approval is based on evidence of structured professional development around African American History, integration of African American History into required curriculum frameworks using approved state standards, and strong community partnerships, among other factors.

 

Throughout the year, teachers and other district staff receive support and resources as they implement instruction related to African American History. Our Principals Multicultural Advisory Committee (PMAC) students are challenged to take the lead at the school level each February by promoting African American History month through informational posters set up in public spaces, cultural celebrations, and morning announcements that highlight the contributions of African Americans.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Subject
4.2 Presentation of the Distinction of “Master Board” to the Pinellas County School Board
Meeting
Jan 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Presentation

The Florida School Boards Association (FSBA) strives to meet the needs of its membership by offering several types of support to its members.  One very important area of support is professional development. FSBA Leadership Services Team provides learning/growth opportunities for new school board members and those members wishing to continue to strengthen their service.  Training is available to equip the board members with the necessary tools and the ability to focus on student achievement.

 

The FSBA Master Board Program is a governance team training for the School Board and Superintendent.  Its purpose is to strengthen the leadership team’s ability to work effectively, efficiently and collectively as a governance body while maintaining a focus on student achievement and factors contributing to the success of schools.  Opportunities are provided for the team to discuss, improve or enhance those important interpersonal skills that are essential for a high-functioning team.

 

The leadership curriculum offers meaningful learning sessions concentrating on student achievement through the five components of the FSBA Governance Model.  Some of the skills, knowledge, processes and concepts addressed in the leadership curriculum include supporting student achievement, self-assessment, consensus building, establishing trust, conflict resolution, leadership and communication styles, team development, core beliefs, roles and responsibilities, productive meetings, systems thinking, district culture, problem solving and strategic planning.

 

During the past year, the Pinellas County School Board and the Superintendent have availed themselves of the program.  Leadership Development Consultant for the Florida School Boards Association, April Griffin, will present the board with the distinction of Master Board.

4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.4 Presentation of the 2020-2021 Independent Citizens Referendum Oversight Committee (ICROC) Annual Report to the Pinellas County School Board and Citizens of Pinellas County, Presented by, Mitch Lee, Chairperson, Independent Citizens Referendum Oversight Committee
Meeting
Jan 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Presentation

BACKGROUND:

On November 2, 2004, January 29, 2008, November 6, 2012, November 8, 2016, and again on November 3, 2020, the citizens of Pinellas County voted to increase the ad valorem millage one-half mill for necessary operating expenses including funds to recruit and retain quality teachers, supplement reading programs, enhance music and art programs, and provide up-to-date materials and technology in Pinellas County Schools. The school board established specific target areas for the expenditure of these funds and a process for allocating the referendum dollars, outlining clear parameters for both monitoring and reporting expenditures to the public.

 

The Independent Citizens Referendum Oversight Committee (ICROC) was created to function as an advisory body to the school board. The committee’s purpose is to monitor the expenditure of referendum dollars over the four-year implementation period. The committee reviews quarterly expenditure reports that detail teacher compensation and the items purchased by the specific program areas of reading, visual arts, performing arts and technology. In addition, the committee further examines the detailed narratives specifying the use of the items in each program area and emphasizes how schools’ benefit from the expenditure of these funds.

 

Each year, the ICROC Chairperson presents the Annual Report summarizing the expenditures for the year. It is important to note that while these funds follow the intent of the referendum guidelines to recruit and retain quality teachers, enhance program initiatives, update equipment and instructional materials to positively impact student learning, these funds are not used as replacement dollars for existing department budgets.  As the ICROC Chairperson, Mitch Lee will present the 2020-21 Annual Report.

 

Pinellas County Schools is proud of the community support achieved over this sixteen-year period and that our staff and students have benefited greatly from the thoughtful expenditures of the funds.

Subject
5.1 Meeting Procedures
Meeting
Jan 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Information

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment
BACKGROUND:

By Florida law, school districts are required to update the needs of their facilities every five (5) years through an educational plant survey. The district completed a five-year survey in July 2019 covering the years 2019-2024. During the five-year survey period, districts may submit an interim or supplemental survey for additional new construction, renovation, remodeling or demolishing buildings for replacement. 

 

This supplemental survey is being submitted to gain Florida Department of Education approval for changes to four sites:

  • Pinellas Central Elementary - replace dismountable classroom walls and open media center with permanent floor to ceiling walls and remodel the administration suite.
  • Seminole High - demolish part of the existing cafeteria and remodel the remainder into art rooms and demolish buildings 11, 12, and 13 to create a new cafeteria and courtyard. 
  • Seminole Middle - construct a wall between the cafeteria and an inside circulation corridor to reduce noise pollution inside the building.

 

RATIONALE:

Florida Department of Education approval of Supplemental Educational Plant Survey 7.8 is required to proceed with the capital improvements described above.  Board approval of the survey is required before the survey can be officially submitted to the Department of Education for their review.

Approve waiving the attendance portion within Policy 5420.03 - Reporting Student Progress and Class Ranking - High School for second semester, 2021-2022

4 - Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment
BACKGROUND:
During the second semester of the 2019-20 school year, both semesters of the 2020-21 school year, and again in the first semester of the 2021-22 school year, the attendance requirement regarding exemption of final exams in high schools within Policy 5420.03 - Reporting Student Progress and Class Ranking - High School was waived to address health, wellness and safety concerns which arose due to the pandemic. Meeting

Type
Action
Fiscal Impact
Yes
Budgeted
Yes
Budget Source
Funding will be provided through ESSER funds.
Recommended Action
Approve the negotiated COVID Sick Leave Pay Memorandums of Understanding (MOUs) with Pinellas County Teachers Association (PCTA), Pinellas Educational Support Professionals Association (PESPA), SEIU/Florida Public Services Union (FPSU) and Fraternal Order of Police (FOP) for all active employees in these bargaining units and approve COVID Sick Leave Pay for all non-represented staff.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources

BACKGROUND:

Since March 2020, the Family First Coronavirus Relief Act (FFCRA) provided paid time off for employees who were unable to work due to reasons related to COVID. This was extended to September 30, 2021, by the authority granted under the American Rescue Plan. The district and the PCTA, PESPA and FOP bargaining teams recognized the need to ensure that employees who were required to quarantine due to COVID continued to be paid after this date and agreed on specific terms and conditions for which employees could be paid. Vaccinated employees required to quarantine were paid up to ten (10) days provided they had not already used ten (10) days of COVID sick leave since March 2020. Employees who were not eligible for this COVID sick leave could access the sick leave bank which allowed for up to ten (10) days if required to quarantine. Those agreements were in effect until December 17, 2021.

 

Due to the ongoing need for employees to quarantine due to COVID, the district and PCTA, PESPA, FPSU and FOP bargaining teams agreed on specific terms and conditions for which employees could be paid.  These terms and conditions provide for the same allowances that were offered first semester and are in effect until March 31, 2022. As with other previous agreements reached with the bargaining units, non-represented employees will follow the same terms and conditions.

 

RATIONALE:

The respective bargaining teams worked collaboratively on these agreements and believe these agreements provide employees with the ability to get paid when they are required to quarantine.  In remaining consistent with the other bargaining groups who worked with the district, this agreement also provides non-represented employees with the same ability to get paid when they are required to quarantine.

Procedural

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
