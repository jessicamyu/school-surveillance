4.1 Presentation of Proclamation Observing Disability History and Awareness Weeks During the First Two Weeks in October 2019
Meeting
Sep 24, 2019 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation observing Disability History and Awareness Weeks during the first two weeks in October 2019.
Goals
Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Section 1003.4205, Florida Statutes, entitled Disability History and Awareness Instruction, was signed into law in 2008. It requires school districts to designate the first two weeks of October as Disability History and Awareness Weeks and also promotes providing instruction for students in all public schools to expand student knowledge, understanding, and awareness of individuals with disabilities, disability history, and the disability rights movement.

 

Throughout these two weeks, schools should support activities that:

  • Promote positive attitudes toward the successful inclusion of students with disabilities;
  • Increase respect and acceptance of individual differences;
  • Increase understanding of disabilities and related social issues; and
  • Facilitate the development of literacy and language arts skills through activities about disability awareness.

 

Disability History and Awareness Weeks are an opportunity to celebrate and focus on the abilities and positive contributions of students and adults with disabilities. It is also a time to thank individuals for the positive difference they make in the Pinellas school district and in the local community.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Subject
4.2 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating October 2019 as Dyslexia Awareness Month
Meeting
Sep 24, 2019 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating October 2019 as Dyslexia Awareness Month.
Goals
Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools recognizes the importance of addressing the unique educational needs of children with specific learning disabilities resulting from dyslexia. Dyslexia is the most common learning disability and affects 80 to 90 percent of all individuals with a learning disability. Early diagnosis of dyslexia is critical for ensuring that individuals with dyslexia receive focused, evidence-based intervention that leads to the promotion of self-awareness and self-empowerment and the provision of necessary accommodations to ensure school and life success. In accordance with the U.S. Department of Education and U.S. Senate, the Exceptional Student Education (ESE) department recognizes Dyslexia Awareness Month during the month of October in our schools.

 

The district has assembled a team of general and special education leaders, along with Student Services team members to address dyslexia needs. The team trains staff, researches and strengthens curricula and strategies provided to students in order to meet the needs of students with dyslexia across the district.  This school year, the district opened the Center for Literacy Innovation at Elisa Nelson Elementary. This 1st-4th grade program provides a systematic, multi-sensory, structured approach to literacy instruction for students with dyslexia and other reading difficulties. This program will be extended to 1st-5th grade during the 2020-21 school year. Recent state legislation has brought a greater focus to dyslexia and provided additional resources to support students with dyslexia. The ESE department, as well as our general education colleagues, are proud to support our students in overcoming dyslexia.

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

Physical therapists serve qualifying students and play an important role in supporting student success. October is National Physical Therapy Month, sponsored by the American Physical Therapy Association (APTA). Physical therapists in public school systems assess and treat students between the ages of three and twenty-two who have conditions that limit their abilities to move and perform functional activities throughout their school day. Physical therapists develop a plan for each student using treatment techniques to promote the ability to move and/or restore function.

 

Pinellas County Schools currently employs 22 physical therapists and physical therapist assistants to serve 575 students in all grade levels at 92 schools.

 

The district recognizes the important role physical therapists and physical therapist assistants play in the success of students accessing the curriculum, learning daily and promoting success in transition programs. National Physical Therapy Month affords the district an opportunity to acknowledge a sincere appreciation and thanks for the efforts and work of each physical therapist and physical therapist assistant within Pinellas County Schools.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:
A Tax Anticipation Note (TAN) is short-term debt issued by governmental entities and secured by future tax revenue. In Florida, section 1011.13 of the state statutes authorizes the issuance of debt. Repayment of any note issued by the district would be secured by local millage tax revenue. The district has issued a TAN fourteen (14) times since 1999 in amounts ranging from $40,000,000 to $95,000,000. Goals
Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Florida State law, Section 1008.22(7), F.S., requires the Florida Department of Education to publish a uniform calendar each year that includes statewide assessment information.  Using this calendar, each school district must establish schedules for the administration of all statewide, standardized assessments and district-required assessments.  The districtwide assessment calendars must be approved by the School Board, included in parent notifications and published on the district website, per the statute.

 

User-friendly districtwide assessment calendars have been established for elementary, middle and high school levels and have been converted to display in the Uniform Statewide Assessment Calendar format, as required by law.   Assessment calendars are posted on the Assessment, Accountability and Research webpage.  The 2019-2020 calendars can be viewed here: https://www.pcsb.org/domain/10272.

 

Each year the Executive Directors of Elementary, Middle and High School Education, along with other district assessment staff, review the calendars for adherence to all local and state requirements.  Local assessments are chosen based on input from district and school staff in order to progress monitor and best support students in achieving state standards.  These include final exams in middle and high school and the Measures of Academic Progress (MAP) in elementary schools.

 
RATIONALE:

Statute requires that no student have more than 5% of their total school hours in a year scheduled for required state and local assessments.  The proposed calendars meet the requirements of state statute and ensure students are assessed on required statewide assessments, while not exceeding the 5% threshold for assessments.  The range of required assessments is as low as 0.6% in kindergarten and no grade level exceeds 3.7% of required time.  

 

Local assessments will support students and provide actionable data for teachers through individual student and schoolwide progress monitoring.  In the last two years, overall time spent on assessments in grades K-12 has been reduced by nearly 20 hours.

Recommended Action
Approve the request of Setting a Public Hearing and Authorizing the Advertisement of the Public Hearing to Consider Amendment of Policies 2410 - SPECIAL SERVICES and 8410 - CRISIS EVENT INTERVENTION AND RESPONSE.

BACKGROUND:

The 2019 Florida Legislature passed Senate Bill (SB) 1418 and Governor Ron DeSantis signed the bill into law on June 25, 2019.  SB 1418 requires the Florida Department of Education (FDOE) to develop a list of approved suicide screening instruments and establish additional criteria and posting requirements for “Suicide Prevention Certified Schools.” The FDOE, in collaboration with the Statewide Office for Suicide Prevention and suicide prevention experts, identified two suicide risk screening instruments appropriate for assessing suicide risk in school-age youth:

  • Columbia-Suicide Severity Rating Scale (C-SSRS)
  • Suicide Assessment Five-Step Evaluation and Triage (SAFE-T)

SB 1418 also established additional criteria for the “Suicide Prevention Certified School” designation which include the following:

  • Incorporate two hours of suicide awareness and prevention training for all instructional personnel;
  • Adopt a policy mandating the use of an approved suicide risk assessment instrument prior to an involuntary examination; and
  • Identify at least two school-based staff members certified to administer the C-SSRS and SAFE-T risk assessment instruments.

Therefore, additional language is proposed to be included within Policies 2410 and 8410 that reflects the utilization of an approved suicide risk assessment instrument prior to an involuntary examination due to concerns about the student’s suicide risk.

 

RATIONALE:

Proposed updates are aligned with best practices regarding suicide risk assessment.

Goals
Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources

BACKGROUND:

During the 2019 Legislative Session, Senate Bill 7070 amended s. 1012.731 F.S. (known as the Florida Best and Brightest Teacher Program), to provide for eligible instructional employees to receive a recognition award based on performance criteria and policies adopted by the School Board. This policy is recommended to comply with the statutory requirement. 

 

RATIONALE:

Pinellas County Schools is expected to receive 9.7 million dollars of the funds appropriated this year by the legislature to fund the Best and Brightest Teacher and Principal Programs.  Under the revised statute, a recognition award is available for instructional employees as defined by F.S. 1012.01 (2)(a)-(e) which includes paraprofessionals. This award is payable to instructional employees who received a highly effective or effective rating on their evaluation and is payable from funds remaining after payment of the allocation available for the recruitment and retention awards available under these programs.  The statute provides that the principal is to select the employee based on performance criteria and policies adopted by the School Board.

 

The district’s bargaining team met with representatives from the Pinellas Classroom Teachers Association (PCTA) and the Pinellas Educational Support Professionals Association to discuss the implementation of the statutory changes and distribution of the funds and agreed that the recognition award should be distributed equally to all “instructional employees” as defined by statute. With respect to paraprofessionals who do not receive an “effective” or “highly effective” rating on their evaluation because the evaluation instrument differs, it was agreed that they would be eligible so long as they have not received three or more ratings less than satisfactory on their evaluation.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
