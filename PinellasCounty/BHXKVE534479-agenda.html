4.2 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating April as National Occupational Therapy Month
Meeting
Apr 14, 2020 - Virtual - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Presentation, Proclamation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Every April, the American Occupational Therapy Association (AOTA) celebrates National Occupational Therapy Month. In our schools, Occupational Therapists (OTs) assess and treat students to improve a student’s fine motor, visual motor, daily living and organizational skills to assist in access to the curriculum and performance of daily activities. Just as important, the therapists provide strategies to teachers and behavior specialists to assist with the varying sensory needs of the students. Occupational Therapist Assistants (OTAs) support treatment techniques as identified by the Occupational Therapist.

 

Pinellas County Schools currently employs 65 Occupational Therapists and Occupational Therapist Assistants serving approximately 2,400 students in all grade levels.

 

Each year, Occupational Therapy staff find creative ways to educate co-workers, administrators, parents and students about strategies which impact so many aspects of a student’s daily life. Below is just a sampling of ways staff will celebrate OT month in their schools this April:

  • Set up disability simulation activities for administrators, teachers and other school staff allowing them to experience some of the difficulties many of their students face.
  • Oversee quizzes and fun activities, with prizes awarded, that allow staff and students to learn about their Occupational Therapy staff and more about OT in the school setting.
  • Distribute printed information about OT, the importance of movement and provide samples of adaptive paper for students struggling with writing.
  • The OT will work with classes to introduce sensory-based activities for all the students, like Brain Gym®, that the teacher can incorporate into the day.
  • Include information about OT and ideas for teachers in school’s weekly newsletter.

The district recognizes the important role Occupational Therapists and Occupational Therapist Assistants play in the success of students accessing their curriculum and performing daily activities required in their school environment. National Occupational Therapy Month affords the district an opportunity to acknowledge a sincere appreciation and thanks for the efforts and work of each Occupational Therapist and Occupational Therapist Assistant within Pinellas County Schools.

Meeting
Apr 14, 2020 - Virtual - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.

Presentation, Proclamation
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND:

Since 1970, April has been designated as National Autism Awareness Month by the National Autism Society in order to highlight the growing need for awareness about autism. The United States recognizes April as a special opportunity to educate the public about autism and issues for the autism community, as well as to spotlight the needs of individuals and families impacted by autism.

 

Autism is a complex spectrum disorder that impacts an individual’s ability to communicate and interact with others. The prevalence of autism continues to grow, making awareness ever more important. The Center for Disease Control and Prevention reported currently the incidence of autism has risen to approximately one in 59 births in the United States and boys are four times more likely to be diagnosed than girls.

 

In order to celebrate Autism Awareness Month, the district has provided suggestions to schools on ways to engage their communities to raise awareness. Some of these activities might include:

  • Light It Up Blue Day–Commit the entire school to wear blue in recognition of this celebration.
  • Hold an Autism Awareness book study at school site.
  • Set up a sensory exhibit to experience tunnels, swings, weighted vests, and body sock, all ways that have been successful for children on the autism spectrum.
  • Take advantage of the multiple resources available through the use of www.autismspeaks.org.
  • OAR, Organization for Autism Research has “kit for kids” with a free download for schools to use for Autism Awareness https://researchautism.org/kit-for-kids-promotes-autism-awareness-2.

Pinellas County serves approximately 1,600 students identified on the autism spectrum. The district serves students with autism across a continuum of supports in center schools, self-contained classrooms and inclusive classrooms. Pinellas County Schools will continue to develop and improve quality programs and services to meet the individual needs of students with autism and their families.

Recommended Action
Approve the 2020-2021 Property Insurance Program, including Boiler and Machinery Insurance coverage effective April 15, 2020, through April 15, 2021.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources
BACKGROUND:
Pinellas County Schools is required to carry property insurance, including contents, and boiler and machinery coverage pursuant to Florida Statute Section 1001.42(11)(b)(8)(d). 
 

USI Insurance Services, the district’s property insurance broker, and their property wholesaler partner, AmWINS Group, Inc., annually markets the district’s property insurance program, assists with property valuation, coordinates property insurance claims, and provides loss control services. Our current property coverage is multi-layered with over 20 insurance carriers rated A+ by A.M. Best Company. A historical review of our property insurance renewals is attached. 

 

In January 2020, the Risk Management Insurance Review Committee met with USI Insurance Services, AmWins Group, Inc., and underwriters from Lloyds of London. They confirmed this year's insurance premium will not exceed $6.795 million. 

 

After evaluating alternative marketing strategies for our insurance coverage, including the limits of property coverage, deductibles, and policy provisions, the district's insurance committee elected to maintain our current property coverage level of $100 million. 

 

The district has a deductible for the first $100,000 per occurrence for All Perils, except for Named Windstorm/Hail/Storm Surge, which has a deductible of 5%.  After a disaster declaration from the federal government, Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) funding becomes available for our insurance deductibles and property loss exceeding our insurance limits, provided the district is appropriately insured.

 

The boiler and machinery policy covers damage and mechanical and electrical breakdown to boilers, pressure vessels, refrigeration systems, piping and other vital equipment. These are not covered by standard property policies.

 

RATIONALE:  

The 2020-2021 property insurance premium is projected to not exceed $6.795 million. 
 

The proposed property insurance program will be submitted to Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) for approval, which would allow the district to apply for federal funds should a catastrophic loss occur. In addition, the Committee will be evaluating independent Disaster Recovery firms, should a large incident occur in the future to maximize and expedite reimbursements through our carriers and FEMA.  

 

The Risk Management Insurance Review Committee, comprised of the Associate Superintendent, Finance and Business Services; Associate Superintendent, Human Resources Services; Executive Director, Budget and Resource Allocation; Director, Risk Management and Insurance; and Safety and Loss Prevention Coordinator, reviewed the proposal provided by our property and insurance broker, USI Insurance Services and our wholesale insurance broker, AmWins Group, Inc., to assure the pricing and coverage is competitive and appropriate.
