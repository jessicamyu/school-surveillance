4.1 Information on the Summer Career Acceleration Program (SCAP) Success
Meeting
Aug 27, 2019 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.
Type
Information
Goals
Strategic Direction 4. Career- and College-Readiness
BACKGROUND:
The Summer Career Acceleration Program (SCAP) provided high school students the opportunity to earn an industry certification and internship with local business hosts. The culminating internships took place over seven weeks this summer, but the SCAP program began in January 2019 with recruiting students and business hosts.

 

Students not currently enrolled in a career technical program, or those who had not already earned an industry certification were actively recruited for SCAP. Interested students engaged in an orientation session, completed a job interview course sponsored by Junior Achievement, and interviewed with at least six business hosts during an internship interview fair prior to being placed for the internship. Once placed, interns and their parents met with the intern host to finalize the process and procedures of the program, including setting up a savings account with Achieva Credit Union for the direct deposit of their internship stipend provided through the Pinellas Education Foundation.

 

The inaugural year of SCAP brought the School District, Pinellas Education Foundation, Junior Achievement, Achieva Credit Union and over 100 businesses together to provide real-world experiences to 112 students further preparing them for entry into careers and college.  Representatives from participant organizations will be recognized for their contributions to meeting the district mission of educating and preparing each student for college, career and life.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Approve Aetna Resources for Living to provide an Employee Assistance Plan (EAP) from September 1, 2019, through December 31, 2022, with a two (2) year renewal option through December 31, 2024.
Goals
Strategic Direction 2. Learning in a Safe Environment
Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools offers an Employee Assistance Plan (EAP), which provides employees and their family members living in the household up to 8 free confidential counseling sessions for each issue through licensed professional counselors to assist with life’s challenges. Short-term counseling is available through telephone, face-to-face, or video conferencing. The plan provides assistance with issues such as stress, depression, relationship issues, and alcohol and/or drug abuse. In addition, they offer work-life resources to assist with eldercare, childcare, identity theft, and legal and financial concerns.

 

The EAP includes communication materials, management consultation, reporting and evaluation. Our EAP provider will respond to districtwide critical incidents to ensure our employees have information regarding the EAP plan and the benefits available to them and provide any debriefing counseling sessions, as needed. Employer services include a part-time onsite representative to offer employee education and orientation, supervisory training, and wellness seminars on a variety of topics; including, but not limited to an overview of our EAP benefits, Cultivating Optimism, Caring for the Caregiver, Managing Stress, Resiliency and Balancing Work and Family.

 

On October 13, 2015, Health Advocate was approved to continue their prior services as the district’s EAP provider. Their agreement was approved through December 31, 2020. In May 2019, Health Advocate provided notice of their intent to terminate our agreement for EAP services, prior to the end of their agreement based on increased costs due to higher utilization than expected in their financial assumptions. Although satisfied with their prior services, we were not in a position to increase their rates based upon the formal bid process, in which they initially received their agreement. We accepted the termination of agreement and asked Aon, our health benefits consultant, to request and negotiate new pricing and information from Health Advocate, BayCare Behavioral Health EAP and Aetna Resources for Living.  Our current agreement with Health Advocate will expire on August 31, 2019.

 

There are approximately 17,000 employees covered by our EAP. Employees and members living in the household, and children up to age 26 regardless of their residence, may receive services.  In 2018, there were 1,703 unique cases.

 

RATIONALE:

All three responses that were received resulted in a significant increase over our current costs. Our 2018 annual costs were $198,720. Health Advocate, our incumbent, proposed annual costs of $475,320, with a potential rate change if utilization changed 10% or more. BayCare provided a first-year rate of $367,200, but with a potential rate increase each year if the utilization increased by 5%. Aetna Resources for Living quoted a three (3) year annual rate guarantee of $401,880, with a potential rate increase if utilization increased more than 30%.

 

After reviewing the responses, we recommend Aetna Resources for Living. We are excited that they can provide:

  • a large national network of behavioral health providers that are also contracted as EAP providers;

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
