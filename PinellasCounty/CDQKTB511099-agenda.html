4.1 Presentation of Proclamation Designating October 31-November 4, 2022, as Gifted and Talented Awareness Week
Meeting
Oct 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action (Consent), Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating October 31-November 4, 2022, as Gifted and Talented Awareness Week.
BACKGROUND:

In alignment with the state of Florida, Pinellas County Schools proudly supports observing Gifted and Talented Awareness Week October 31, 2022 - November 4, 2022. This is a time to celebrate our district’s continued commitment to transformative gifted education to reflect on how we identify gifted students to provide all with opportunities for an equitable education, and to recognize the continued impact that gifted and talented education has had in our community. While gifted and talented teachers are well versed in gifted and talented education, additional exposure by other stakeholders is needed to build better community understanding of gifted and talented students and our program’s primary goals and our progress.

 

Throughout Gifted and Talented Awareness Week, we ask that schools, teachers and district staff use the resources provided to support the National Association of Gifted Children’s Campaign called “Giftedness Knows No Boundaries” which recognizes four goals in the identification and support of gifted children:              

  • See Me - All children deserve fair identification strategies.
  • Understand Me - Gifted and talented children need informed counselors, teachers and parents who can help them overcome social, emotional and psychological challenges involved in growing up.
  • Teach Me - Gifted and talented children have unique learning needs that set them apart from their peers and benefit most when they are taught by teachers trained to support them.
  • Challenge Me - Gifted and talented children need opportunities and curriculum that provide both support and challenge so that they can grow as learners.

 

Resources to support these goals include digital posters for each goal of the campaign and a flyer showing how Pinellas County Schools is currently addressing each of these goals as a district. Schools are encouraged to personalize the resources by adding in student anecdotes, program highlights and teacher spotlights to promote awareness on their school’s website and social media accounts.

 
SUBMITTED BY:
Daniel J. Evans, Ed.D., Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

4.2 Presentation of Proclamation Recognizing Lights On Afterschool Day on October 20, 2022
Meeting
Oct 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action, Proclamation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation recognizing Lights On Afterschool day on October 20, 2022.
BACKGROUND:
Launched in October 2000, Lights On Afterschool is a nationwide event celebrating afterschool programs and their important role in the lives of children, families and communities. Quality afterschool programs help children and youth discover their talents and passions while providing an opportunity to learn in a different kind of environment than they experience during the regular school day. These programs are essential to helping students succeed and give working parents the peace of mind that comes with knowing their children are safe and supervised with opportunities to learn.
 
October 20, 2022, marked the 23rd annual Lights On Afterschool day. More than 9,000 local and national events were planned and held, sending a powerful message that millions of students need quality afterschool programs. Many afterschool programs are facing funding shortfalls so severe that they are being forced to close their doors and turn off their lights.
 
Pinellas County Schools' afterschool programs support students by offering tutoring, academic support and enrichment activities both before and after the school day. In addition to Summer Bridge programs, 21st Century programs and STEM clubs, the district partners with the Juvenile Welfare Board (JWB) of Pinellas to support the Promise Time program. The Promise Time program is a collaborative initiative that pools the resources of the district and the community to provide extended learning at many of our elementary school sites. Acknowledging the proclamation recognizing Lights On Afterschool day provides an opportunity to recognize and thank our contributing partners.

BACKGROUND:

The following recognitions highlight many, if not all, of the District’s Core Values. These awards are emblematic of our core values of Respectful and Caring Relationships, Responsibility, Connectedness and a Commitment to Children, Families and the Community. Today, we are acknowledging the most deserving individuals during the last school term 2021-2022 with the following awards: the Florida Association of School Resource Officers (FASRO) Lifetime Achievement Award, the School Resource Officer/Deputy of the Year Award, the School Safety Officer/Guardian Award, the Campus Activities Monitor of the Year Award and the Inaugural Jesse Dorsey - Campus Activities Monitor of the Year Award. We are also recognizing K-9 Roo for his nine years of service to the students in Pinellas County.

 

School Resource Officer (SRO) Barbara Baugher was recently honored by the Florida Association of School Resource Officers (FASRO) with a Lifetime Achievement Award for her 34 years of service.  Officer Baugher was hired in September 1988 and is the longest serving Pinellas County Schools SRO. She is quick to volunteer for any task and is easily recognized as the face of the Pinellas County Schools Police Department.  

 

Grace Albritton, Gibbs High School, of the St. Petersburg Police Department, is being recognized as the School Resource Officer (SRO) of the Year for the 2021-2022 school year.  Officer Albritton has demonstrated through her actions and the relationships she has built with the students and staff the importance of trust and communication.

 

Archange "Paul" Leveille, Bay Point Elementary School, is recognized as the School Safety Officer (SSO) of the Year for the 2021-2022 school year.  Archange "Paul" Leveille has become an integral part of the Bay Point Elementary School family. His most important task is to ensure the safety of the students and staff and not be distracted from this duty.  

 

Jesse Dorsey was the first Campus Activities Monitor hired by Pinellas County Schools. He served the staff and students at Northeast High School and in Pinellas County for almost 24 years.  He was an approachable and important part of the staff at Northeast High School, loved and respected by students and staff alike.  He is an example of what a Campus Activities Monitor should be and was often called upon to train new Campus Activities Monitors.  In honor of his work and dedication to the position, the Campus Activities Monitor of the Year award will be named the Jesse Dorsey - Campus Activities Monitor of the Year. 

 

Lora McIntosh, Osceola Middle School, is the first recipient of the Jesse Dorsey - Campus Activities Monitor of the Year for the 2021-2022 school year. She is affectionately known as "Mrs. Mac."  She has best demonstrated the skills and attributes associated with our Campus Activities Monitors. 

 

K-9 Roo recently retired after nine years of service to the students and staff of Pinellas County Schools.  Roo is known for partnering with her human handler, SRO Dave Harrison, and formed the Reading with Roo Program. Roo intently listened to students with reading deficiencies with an encouraging demeanor and assisted the students with their reading proficiency.  Roo will now become ‘Roo the pet’ and undoubtedly will make a few appearances with her students after retirement.

5.1 Meeting Procedures
Meeting
Oct 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak during the public hearing and regarding the topic of the public hearing.

 

In each case, you

  1. Must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below;
  2. Must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers for that speaking opportunity or before the last speaker for that opportunity concludes their comments, whichever is later;
  3. Will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee;
  4. Will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson or a majority vote of the Board; and
  5. May not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.

 

The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. If a speaker wants to supplement their three (3) minute presentation, the speaker should provide the Board office with written materials prior to the meeting. Board members will make every effort to review these materials. Board members may not be able to review materials received less than two (2) business days prior to the Board meeting.
  2. The Board will not act on any matter raised by a speaker prior to the next regular meeting. If a majority of the Board desires to further consider a matter raised by a speaker, they will request the Superintendent to bring a recommendation at a future workshop or meeting or otherwise address the issue. It is generally the Board's practice not to respond to the issues brought forward by speakers except to correct inaccuracies or clarify issues.
  3. Speakers may not use any of the speaking opportunities to advertise or otherwise promote the interests of any commercial or other non-school organizations, except when recognizing school or district partners or when one of the exceptions in Policy 9700.01 applies. Further, no speaker may advertise or otherwise promote a political candidate, question or party.

 

Regulation of Disruptive Speech

 

Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.

 

To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive or otherwise interferes with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, as stated in s. 1001.372(3), Florida Statutes; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

 

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.

 

Recording of Board Meetings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

RATIONALE:
The August 30, 1999, Amended Order of the court granted unitary status to the district in the areas of facilities and resources, transportation and administrative staff assignment, and also created DMAC.  The School Board is listed as one of the six (6) appointing authorities to DMAC. BACKGROUND:

On September 28, 2021, the board approved the district’s Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan (COP) for 2021-2022 through 2025-2026 and adopted the corresponding Five-Year Facilities Work Program, in accordance with 1013.41 (3), 1013.61 and 1013.35, Florida Statutes.

 

The Capital Outlay Committee and support staff developed this Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan, using the September 2020 Educational Plant Survey, the District’s Capital Outlay Guidelines and Prioritization System and broad-based input from school and district staff.

 

The Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan maintains balanced capital priorities and projected revenues by year, over the five-year period. The Tentative Facilities Work Program incorporates the Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan and related data on permanent and relocatable facilities into a specific format, as prescribed by the Florida Department of Education. The board-adopted Facilities Work Program must be submitted to the Florida Department of Education.

 

A summary of major changes in the Proposed Five-Year Plan is provided in the support materials and includes the following:

 

  1. Revenue Projections: Revenue projections have been updated to reflect the 2022 Legislative Session and other more recent revenue estimates. An increase in the tax roll has resulted in a projected increase of local capital outlay (1.5 Mil) revenue. No PECO maintenance dollars were allocated to traditional schools in fiscal year 2022-2023, as such, no PECO revenue is included in this Five-Year Plan. CO & DS and Interest Earning revenue was updated from the prior year estimates.
  1. “New” Year 5 (2026-2027): Consistent with statutory requirements, “New” Year 5 (2026-2027) estimated revenues and project allocations have been incorporated into the Five-Year Plan.
  1. Adjustments to other Capital Projects significant changes are:
  1. The plan reflects the addition of new capital projects as presented on the district’s Facilities “Five Year Capital Outlay Plan”. The five-year total is projected to be $252.3 million and is funded through local capital outlay proceeds (1.5 Mill) and CO & DS.
  2. The plan updates the principal and interest payments associated with the COPs issuance. The projected debt service, including principal and interest, is estimated at a five-year total of $42.5 million.

 

The Proposed Five-Year Capital Outlay Plan and Tentative Facilities Work Program, along with the related support materials, are attached.

10.2 Public Participation
Meeting
Oct 25, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Procedural

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
