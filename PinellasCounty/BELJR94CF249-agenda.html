Subject
4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating the Month of September 2019 as American Founders’ Month and Celebrate Freedom Week, During the Last Full Week of September
Meeting
Sep 10, 2019 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Action, Presentation
Recommended Action
Approve the proclamation designating the month of September 2019 as American Founders Month and Celebrate Freedom Week during the last full week of September.
Goals
Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

In alignment with Florida Statutes, Pinellas County Schools proudly supports American Founders’ Month in September and Celebrate Freedom Week during the last full week in September. This is a time to educate our students and community about the sacrifices made for freedom in the founding of this country and the values on which this country was founded. Celebrate Freedom Week helps to reaffirm the American ideals of individual liberty. While social studies teachers, in particular, are encouraged to emphasize the teaching of the country’s origins with a focus on the founding documents, additional American Founders’ Month and Celebrate Freedom Week resources have been provided to all schools to encourage effective instruction throughout the month.

 

Throughout American Founders’ Month and during Celebrate Freedom Week, the resources provided to teachers and schools are used as a series of morning announcements that include the history of the American flag, trivia questions about our founding fathers and documents and as a time to recite words from the Declaration of Independence. Social Studies classes around the county engage in taking a deeper look at founding documents and how they continue to shape and influence our country today. The Principal’s Multicultural Advisory Committees (PMAC) continue to work to bring schools together throughout the year by utilizing guest speakers like local lawmakers, university professors and veterans to talk with students about civil service, experiences with the law and our role as citizens. PMAC groups also help coordinate schoolwide activities like voter registration drives, a symbolic signing of the Constitution or character meet and greets at lunch to hear the history of those who helped shape the Constitution and Bill of Rights.

 

SUBMITTED BY:

Kevin Hendrick, Associate Superintendent, Teaching and Learning Services

Goals
Strategic Direction 3. Equity with Excellence for All

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools proudly supports Hispanic Heritage Month which is recognized from September 15 through October 15 each year. This is a time to highlight the successes and contributions of the Hispanic community in the United States. Schools are encouraged to avail themselves of district, community, and public resources for lesson plans and multimedia activities to foster a deeper understanding of the Hispanic culture among our student population. Approximately 18% of Pinellas County Schools' students identify themselves as Hispanic and Pinellas County Schools recognizes the importance of supporting our students in feeling accepted, understood and valued.

 

Throughout the year, teachers are encouraged to share the contributions of all ethnic groups in our country as we tell the story of our nation and our world. This month, additional Hispanic Heritage resources from Teaching Tolerance, the National Education Association, and the National Hispanic Heritage website (a subpage of the Library of Congress) will be disseminated to teachers through a variety of methods to honor these contributions.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Approval of The Standard will allow employees to elect voluntary disability benefits through pretax payroll deductions. Employees who do not have health insurance may elect this benefit through board credits. The rates are approximately 20% lower than our current vendor's rates.
Recommended Action
Approve The Standard as the vendor for the district's voluntary disability insurance, effective January 1, 2020, through December 31, 2024.
Goals
Strategic Direction 5. Effective and Efficient Use of Resources

 

BACKGROUND:

The voluntary disability plan is a valuable benefit which provides income replacement in the event an employee is unable to work due to an illness or injury. Employees may elect to enroll in this plan through payroll deductions or through the cafeteria plan flex credits, if they do not elect health insurance.

 

The current carrier providing employee's a voluntary disability plan is Assurant (parent company Sun Life Financial) and is administered by Disability RMS. Although the carrier has changed over the years, the plan design has remained virtually the same. The plan was originally designed and underwritten for an educational employer, not routinely available in the marketplace, as most disability plans are based upon a percentage of salary.   

 

The current plan designs are as follows:  

  • Short-Term Disability (STD): benefit period is up to two years for illness/five years accident. Employees purchase flat monthly amounts, based upon their income, with three optional waiting periods (15, 30, 60 days). Participation:  4,328 employees.
  • Long-Term Disability (LTD):  benefit commences after short-term disability benefits are exhausted. Benefit period is up to age 65, with offsets. Participation:  2,788 employees.

In 2018, Pinellas County Schools was notified by Assurant that they were phasing out their Educator Disability plan and system's platform.  As a result of this information and continued service issues, we requested our Purchasing Department, in conjunction with Aon, our Broker/Consultant, conduct an Intent to Negotiate (ITN) in April 2019.  Only two of the eight carriers who reviewed the ITN returned quotes. The ITN results were reviewed by the committee and were considered unacceptable.  

 

A new plan design was subsequently marketed and directly negotiated through Aon. Optional levels of coverage were requested to provide more affordable deductions for our employees and alternative plan designs would be considered.  Four disability carriers submitted new quotes; including The Standard, Hartford, MetLife and Prudential.  The incumbent carrier, Assurant, did not quote.  After reviewing all options, The Standard was selected to offer their Educator LTD Plan. 

 

All employees affected will receive personalized communication to their email and home, in addition to the annual enrollment material that is provided to every benefit-eligible employee regarding this benefit change.  

 

RATIONALE:

The Standard was selected to offer their Educator LTD Plan because it provides a similar plan design with a reduction in premium for our employees currently enrolled in disability coverage. The Educator LTD plan provides monthly benefit amounts ranging from $400 per month to $5,000 per month, up to 60% of base salary.  

Two plans will be offered:

  • Plan 1 offers monthly benefits up to two years, with three optional waiting periods (14, 30, 60 days)
  • Plan 2 offers monthly benefits through Social Security Retirement age, with three optional waiting periods (14, 30, 60 days)*

*Benefits will be offset by other sources of disability income and employee must be unable to work in any occupation after two years.

 

Default Coverage:  During this year's annual enrollment, Risk Management will default all employees with short-term disability coverage to The Standard Plan 1, which closely matches their current election amount and waiting period.  Employees will not be defaulted to Plan 2, as many employees carry less coverage for long-term disability.  Employees who want a different plan, level of coverage, or waiting period, must go through Annual Enrollment and make their selection.  

 

The Standard will allow employees to select a disability plan during annual enrollment, without any medical underwriting required.  Although a preexisting condition clause will apply for new coverage, it will not affect current policyholders who carried the same coverage amount for at least 12 months with the prior carrier.  

The Standard received excellent references from Hillsborough County School District and Marion County Schools regarding their service, claims payment, and rates.  The Standard currently administers our life insurance and has provided exceptional service to date.

The Standard has also offered an additional one-year extension on our life insurance rates, by combining life and disability insurance through the same carrier.

Meeting
Sep 10, 2019 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type

Budget Source
There is no financial impact to the district.
Recommended Action
Approve the 2019-2020 Professional Learning Catalog
Goals
Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

The Professional Learning Catalog, previously known as Master Inservice Plan (MIP), is a state-required planning document, for professional education inservice training that must be updated annually. In the 1994-1995 school year, Pinellas County was a pilot for a new state process to develop this plan. Since 1995, the pilot process has been standardized and used annually to develop the district plan. The Professional Learning Catalog for 2019-2020 was developed by a cross-functional team representing all levels of the school system. The cross-functional team utilized a variety of 2018-2019 school and district data to plan for the 2019-2020 school year. At the school level, the School Improvement Planning process includes the use of student data and teacher evaluation data to evaluate training needs at the school sites. The Professional Learning Catalog aligns with our District Strategic Plan and summarizes inservice for the school year.

 

The Professional Learning Catalog lists the trainings that are offered to instructional staff and administrators. Trainings are and will continue to be held in specific content areas such as math, reading and science, as well as other areas such as computer literacy, exceptional student education, classroom management and diversity. Instructional staff are provided stipends or trade days for attending professional development that is aligned to their deliberate practice plans. Trainings are also provided to support various groups of employees, such as data management technicians, bookkeepers and paraprofessionals.

 

In the 2018-2019 school year, courses in Instructional Strategies, Behavior Intervention, ESE Instructional Strategies, ELA and Math were listed in the top ten areas of professional development. Two hundred (200) newly hired teachers participated in our 4-day new teacher orientation (Embrace Pinellas) in the summer of 2018. Forty-seven (47) first year teachers completed a year-long Foundations course throughout the 2018-2019 school year.

 

During the summer of 2019, the district offered over 260 professional development courses with 898 sessions. Just under 6,000 teachers, support staff and administrators attended at least one day of professional development during this timeframe.

 

RATIONALE:

The Florida Department of Education (FDOE) requires each district to develop a comprehensive Professional Learning Catalog that is revised annually and submitted to the FDOE by October 1.

BACKGROUND:
The School Board annually adopts a Legislative Program to direct the efforts of staff and consultants in their work with the Legislative Delegation. The 2020 Legislative Session begins in January and is scheduled to adjourn on March 2020. The proposed 2020 Legislative Program has been developed for consideration based on the 2019 program and new proposals presented by staff and school board members. The School Board participated in the development of the draft of the Legislative Program at a workshop on August 20, 2019.
 
Issues within the platform are classified as:
  • Sufficiently and Sustainably Fund Schools to Meet the Educational Needs of Students
  • Career Technical Education and Public Technical Colleges 
  • School and Student Safety 
  • World-Class Facilities
RATIONALE:
The Legislative Program is a compilation of legislative concerns. The adoption of a legislative program facilitates our efforts in working with the Florida Legislature and assists us to shape the educational environment that we desire for the students of Pinellas County.

BACKGROUND:

The following districts are members of the Greater Florida Consortium of School Boards:  Broward, Charlotte, Collier, Lee, Miami-Dade, Martin, Monroe, Pinellas, Sarasota, and St. Lucie.  The Consortium combines members with common interests that are normally not addressed through the Florida School Boards Association, such as the distribution of dollars during the legislative session. The Pinellas County School Board joined the Consortium in 2007 to strengthen its efforts in Tallahassee.

 

The Consortium provides legislative liaison assistance in Tallahassee before and during the legislative session, and provides administrative support services. Members of the Consortium meet with key legislators during the session as a united front on common issues.  Legislative liaisons set up meetings as needed.

 

The 2020 proposed legislative program for the Consortium is a document that must be unanimously supported by its member school boards.  Any item in the attached proposed program can be eliminated if Pinellas or any other member district objects.  Items may only be approved or deleted; they may not be amended.  Once the document is finalized, it is distributed electronically to member districts.

 

RATIONALE:
School districts that are members of the Greater Florida Consortium of School Boards are closely aligned with Pinellas on a majority of issues.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
