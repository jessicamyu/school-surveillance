Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement

BACKGROUND:

The Board agrees to provide educational services through Educational Alternative Services programs at the Department of Juvenile Justice (DJJ), Department of Corrections and various community agency facilities. The Board also agrees to contract for teen parent childcare services and to accept counseling and related services from various community agencies. The Board agrees to comply with state statutes for the operation of the DJJ programs and services within the district and to share information with the appropriate 6th Judicial Circuit staff as required by statute. These agreements are essentially renewals with appropriate updates in verbiage, dates and legislative requirements.

 

The value of each agreement is determined by the services provided by the Board and the agencies. Title I, Neglected and Delinquent funding is generated for eligible programs.

 

  • Teen Parenting Program

The state Department of Education Rule 6A-6.0525 F.A.C., and Florida Statute 1003.54 require that each district school board establish and implement a teenage parent program which is defined as an educational program designed to provide a specialized curriculum and other services to meet the needs of students who are either pregnant or are already teen mothers or fathers and the children of these students.

 

The board agrees to contract for teen parent childcare services and to accept counseling and related services from various community agencies through a partnership between Pinellas County Schools (PCS) and the Early Learning Coalition (ELC). The terms of the partnership are defined annually through a cooperative agreement between PCS and the ELC. Once the agreement has been approved by the board, oversight and accountability is monitored by the administration of Educational Alternative Services.


The Early Learning Coalition has provided services to approximately twenty-six (26) children, birth through three (3) years old, of teen parents through licensed daycare centers and homes during the 2021-2022 school year. PCS and ELC have a contractual agreement that is valid through June 30, 2022.  The contract outlines the costs for direct (childcare) and support services (administrative) provided through the ELC. The children receive a student ID number and the FTE generated is a funding source for daycare services.

 

In 2021-2022, seventy (70) teen parents received services from the Teen Parent Social Workers. Forty-one (41) babies were registered.  The ELC provided daycare service for approximately seven (7) babies at private daycares throughout the county.  Nineteen (19) babies received services at a school-based daycare at Pinellas Technical College (PTC) Clearwater.  Twenty-nine (29) teen parents graduated this school year as of May 2022. 

 

This agreement is an annual agreement with the Early Learning Coalition and is updated to reflect the new budget and estimated number of children to be served.   

 

  • PACE Center for Girls, Inc.

PACE Center for Girls, Inc. is a 230-day Department of Juvenile Justice, day treatment program with a capacity of sixty (60) students. In a day treatment site, many students' length of stay is forty-five (45) days or less. Some students have unanticipated departures due to judicial decisions, DJJ requirements, behavioral issues and/or parent removals. Additionally, serious attendance issues often make academic growth comparisons unavailable or inconsistent.

 

During the 2021-2022 school year to date, PACE has served eighty-eight (88) Pinellas County students. At the end of the 2021-2022 school year, of the fifty-five (55) high school students enrolled at PACE Center for Girls, fifty-one (51) earned credits.  The average number of credits earned was 2.64, due to generally short terms of enrollment in the program. As of May 2022, five (5) students graduated from PACE Center for Girls this school year.

 

The school district contracts for educational services through ninety-five (95) percent of generated FTE funds not to exceed $390,000.

 

While we’ve had a long-standing agreement with PACE Center for Girls; this agreement has been updated to comply with state rule 6A-6.05281 to reflect a change in the amount of shared FTE funds. PACE Center for Girls will receive ninety-five (95) percent of funds instead of ninety (90) percent and PCS will receive five (5) percent instead of ten (10) percent to cover itinerant educational services and administrative costs.

 

  • Operation PAR, Inc.

Operation PAR is a Department of Children and Families contracted residential treatment center for male and female students 13-17 years of age who exhibit substance abuse disorders and who are court-ordered or referred to the program. The program capacity is twenty-three (23) students and the normal stay is approximately three (3) months.

 

At the end of 2021-2022 school year, of the twenty-two (22) high school students enrolled at Operation PAR, sixteen (16) earned credits. The average number of credits earned was 2.81, due to short stays of less than three months in the program.

 

While we’ve had a long-standing agreement with Operation PAR; this agreement has been updated to reflect new verbiage related to providing services to students exhibiting “substance use disorders” instead of those exhibiting “substance abuse.”

 

RATIONALE:

These agreements facilitate compliance with state requirements for establishing and implementing a teenage parent program to meet the needs of students who are either pregnant or are already teen mothers or fathers, and the children of these students; for female students in a DJJ day treatment program at PACE Center for Girls; and for male and female students exhibiting substance use disorders at Operation PAR, Inc.

As prescribed by Chapter 1013 of Florida Statutes, SREF, and the Florida Administrative Code Chapter 69A58, Florida School Boards must approve the annual fire, health and safety inspections conducted during the current fiscal year. Inspections identify deficiencies which are addressed through repairs made at the school level or through planned budgets for that purpose.

Budget Source
Voluntary Prekindergarten (VPK) programs are not funded as part of the state’s K-12 Florida Education Finance Program (FEFP). Families must apply for a Certificate of Eligibility (voucher) for the school year or summer program through the Early Learning Coalition of Pinellas County. The district is then reimbursed by the Early Learning Coalition through attendance verification for the VPK hours, up to $2,803 per child for the school year program and up to $2,393 per child for the summer program. During the school year, families who opt to extend the program to a full school day pay a weekly tuition fee of $65. For the 2022-2023 school year, twenty-six (26) Title I schools will be supported with Title I funding and/or Elementary and Secondary Schools Emergency Relief (ESSER) funding. These funding sources will allow students to attend for the full school day without having to pay tuition. Selection of the Title I schools for the free, full day program were based on those with the highest percentages of students receiving free and reduced lunch.
Recommended Action
Approve the state of Florida Voluntary Prekindergarten Education Program Statewide Provider Contract with the Early Learning Coalition of Pinellas County.
Goals
4 - Strategic Direction 1. Student Achievement
BACKGROUND: 

Pursuant to Florida Law, the district offers a 540-hour instructional Voluntary Prekindergarten program (VPK). The program is a free, three-hour per day program with families having the option to extend the day to a full school day program through tuition. In Pinellas County Schools, the VPK program is currently implemented in 160 classrooms at 70 elementary schools and two high schools during the school year. Most classrooms are blended with Exceptional Student Education (ESE) prekindergarten students to provide inclusion opportunities. Each year, programs are added at selected locations as space permits.

 

RATIONALE:

The district wants all children to enter kindergarten ready to learn. By offering a comprehensive VPK program, students are able to participate in a curriculum that builds a solid foundation in the areas of social/emotional development, approaches to learning, physical development, language and literacy, mathematical thinking, scientific inquiry, social studies and creative expression through the arts. This foundation prepares students to be successful in kindergarten. As space in schools has allowed, the district has been able to increase the capacity of VPK programs from 306 children during the 2006-2007 school year to over 3,000 available seats for VPK children for the 2022-2023 school year. This increase in capacity allows more children to enter kindergarten ready to learn.

 

By blending the program with ESE, the district is also able to provide inclusive opportunities for PreK students with disabilities. Florida’s Part B State Performance Plan sets a target of 54% of 3-5 year old students receiving special education and related services with typically developing peers. In 2019-2020, the district was able to exceed this target with 77% of ESE 3-5 year old students receiving special education and related services with typically developing peers.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
