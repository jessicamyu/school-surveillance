4.1 Presentation of the Proclamation Designating April 2022 as National Occupational Therapy Month
Meeting
Mar 22, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 10 a.m.

Recommended Action
Adopt the proclamation designating April 2022 as National Occupational Therapy Month.
BACKGROUND:

Every April, the American Occupational Therapy Association (AOTA) celebrates National Occupational Therapy Month. Occupational therapy is a science-driven, evidence-based profession that enables people of all ages to participate in daily living or live better with injury, illness, or disability. This is accomplished through designing strategies for everyday living and customizing environments to develop and maximize potential. In the school setting, the Occupational Therapist’s holistic and customized approach to assessments, interventions, and outcomes helps a child with disabilities participate in school and in social situations.

Pinellas County Schools currently employs sixty-three (63) Occupational Therapists (OT) and Occupational Therapist Assistants (OTA), serving approximately 2,300 students ages 3-22. In our schools, Occupational Therapists assess and treat students to improve their fine motor, visual motor, daily living, and organizational skills to promote access to and engagement in their curriculum and performance of daily activities. The therapists collaborate with and provide strategies to teachers and behavior specialists to assist with the varying sensory needs of the students. Occupational Therapist Assistants support all treatment techniques as identified by the Occupational Therapist.

Each year during OT month, Occupational Therapy staff find creative ways to educate coworkers, administrators, parents, and students about strategies which impact so many aspects of a student’s daily life. They provide interactive trainings and write clever, informative newsletters. They also introduce sensory and movement-based activities into classrooms that teachers can incorporate throughout the school day to keep students focused and engaged in their learning.

The district recognizes the important role Occupational Therapists and Occupational Therapist Assistants play in the success of students accessing their curriculum and performing daily activities required in their school environment. National Occupational Therapy Month affords the district an opportunity to acknowledge a sincere appreciation for the efforts of each Occupational Therapist and Occupational Therapist Assistant within Pinellas County Schools.

Approve the proclamation designating April 2022 as National Autism Acceptance Month.
BACKGROUND:

Since 1970, April has been designated as National Autism Awareness Month by the National Autism Society in order to highlight the growing need for awareness about autism. In April of 2021, the National Autism Society shifted from awareness to acceptance. The United States recognizes April as a special opportunity to educate the public about autism and issues in the autism community, as well as to spotlight the needs of individuals and families impacted by autism.

Autism is a complex spectrum disorder that impacts an individual's ability to communicate and interact with others. The prevalence of autism continues to grow, making awareness and acceptance ever more important. The Center for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) reported currently the incidence of autism has risen to approximately one in 54 births in the United States. Boys are four (4) times more likely to be diagnosed than girls. Pinellas County Schools serves approximately 1,600 students identified on the autism spectrum. The district serves students with autism across a continuum of supports in center schools, self-contained classrooms, and inclusive classrooms. Our practical functional assessment initiative has expanded to include behavior technicians and district behavior analysts in an effort to support the more intensive behavioral needs of students with autism and other disabilities both at home and in school. Practical Functional Assessment and Skill-based Treatment is a student/family-focused and trauma-informed approach.

Pinellas County Schools will continue to develop and improve quality programs and services to meet the individual needs of students with autism and their families. Real change for awareness and acceptance comes through uniting all members of a community. In order to celebrate Autism Acceptance Month, the district has provided suggestions to schools for ways to unite communities and schools in spreading awareness, promoting acceptance and igniting change. Some of these activities include:

  • April 2, 2022 is World Autism Acceptance Day. CDC statistics revealed that autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is 4.3 times more prevalent in boys than it is in girls. Girls “often go undiagnosed because they do not fit autism stereotypes and they mask symptoms better than boys do,” according to the Child Mind Institute. There is no better way to celebrate this day than by becoming aware of the characteristics of people with this condition and how we can do better to increase our own understanding and promote kindness.
  • Autism Acceptance Month Self Advocate Panel: Kick-off Autism Acceptance Month on April 1, 2022 at 4:00 p.m. EST with a Facebook Live episode featuring autistic self-advocates, Anthony Ianni, Chloe Rothschild and Kris Guin who will discuss the importance of acceptance and building an inclusive society.
  • Virtual Panel with Cast of Everything’s Gonna Be Okay: Celebrate World Autism Acceptance Day on April 2, 2022 at 5:00 p.m. EST with the cast of Freeform’s “Everything’s Gonna Be Okay.” The panelists will offer a sneak peek of season 2 and discuss how they celebrate differences for Autism Acceptance Month.
  • Offer volunteer opportunities for students to become a Sensory Savvy Advocate and create sensory bins for their classmates.
  • Take advantage of free, online professional development at https://autisminternetmodules.org.
    •  Understanding the underlying characteristics of ASD and effective practices that support their needs is essential. Designed for those who support, instruct, work with, or live with someone with autism, the Autism Internet Modules (AIM) guide users through case studies, instructional videos, pre- and post-assessments, discussion questions, activities, and more.
  • Take advantage of the multiple resources available at https://www.autismacceptance.org/resources.

April 2022 is designated the 36th annual National School Library Month.

 

National School Library Month is an opportunity for the American Association of School Librarians' (AASL) to celebrate school librarians and their programs. First celebrated in 1958, National School Library Month is sponsored by the American Library Association (ALA) and observed in libraries across the country each April. National School Library Month is also a time to recognize the contributions of our nation's libraries and library workers and to promote literacy, library use and support.

 

During this month, school library media specialists host activities to help their school and local community celebrate the essential role that strong school library programs play in transforming learning.

 

Pinellas County Schools celebrates our library media technology specialists and their commitment to students and families. Through their promotion of literacy, technology and research skills through collaboration, creativity and engagement, library media technology specialists truly make a positive impact on student achievement each and every day.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

BACKGROUND:

This policy has been discussed at multiple board workshops, including most recently at the January 18, 2022 workshop.  While major areas of the policy, such as time limits for speakers and deadlines to sign up to speak, are not being changed from the current policy, amendments are recommended to clarify and streamline certain areas noted throughout the document.

 

RATIONALE:

The amendment restates the order of speaker presentations to the board, specifies that any speech that interferes with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting may be interrupted by the Chairperson, and clarifies other issues regarding public participation. It also explicitly states that public comment is not for commercial promotion of products, goods or services. Lastly, the amendment reorganizes the rules that apply to all opportunities for public participation into a new section, titled, “D. Other Rules Applicable to All Speaking Opportunities.”

BACKGROUND:

School districts in Florida must receive approval from the Florida Department of Education (DOE) prior to the commencement of any project which involves the construction of a facility or a change in the use or function of a facility.  FDOE approval is granted after a review of the needs of the school district via the Florida Inventory of School Houses (FISH) reporting process.

 

In regard to the potential razing of a building, the FDOE’s Office of Educational Facilities (OEF) requires that school districts conduct a financial study known as a Castaldi Analysis to compare the costs of razing an educational facility versus the costs of rehabilitating an educational facility. School districts are permitted to raze a structure if the Castaldi Analysis shows it would be costlier to rehabilitate the structure as opposed to razing it and replacing it with new construction.

 

As part of the FISH approval process, the OEF requires districts to submit a Room Condition Change Building Replacement/Raze (RCC-BRR) form to certify that there is no debt on the property being reviewed and that the School Board is aware that the district is considering the razing of a building. Upon receipt of the RCC-BRR, the OEF will confirm the districts’ Castaldi Analysis prior to extending approval to raze the building.

 

This request to submit the RCC-BRR form, if approved, will allow staff and the OEF to work together to secure the necessary approval to do a partial raze of Building 5 at Pinellas Secondary School.

Pinellas Secondary School, Building 5 includes a small garage/storage area adjacent to a loading dock.  Staff is exploring the possibility of razing this area to construct cold storage space for the Food and Nutrition department or to provide parking in the event that Building 5 is used for other purposes.  Neither option is possible without the FDOE granting approval to raze the building.

BACKGROUND:

The district bargaining team and PESPA reached a tentative agreement on the 2021-2024 Collective Bargaining Agreement. A summary of the tentative agreement includes:

 

Salary Increase and Healthcare

  • Employee groups covered by the PESPA bargaining unit will receive a raise equal to 3.25%, retroactive to July 1, 2021.
  • A one-time payment of $350 to all full-time and/or benefits-eligible employees in the PESPA bargaining unit actively working when the agreement is approved by the School Board.
  • Level E on the salary schedule is eliminated.
  • The School Board will pay 100% (approximately $9 million) of the increase in healthcare costs for all employees.
  • The School Board will pay approximately $4 million for the increased Florida Retirement System contribution for all employees with no additional cost to employees.
  • The average total compensation increase, factoring in the above components, totals approximately 5.2%.

Article 10 - Employee Work Schedule

  • Added language that employee work schedules will be made available to all employees, instead of posting them at worksites.
  • Added language to state that employees may ask for flexibility regarding break time.

Article 13 - Evaluation Procedure

  • Added language that all support staff will receive an annual evaluation which will include a conference between the employee and their appropriate administrator/supervisor and that the employee will receive a copy of the evaluation within five workdays.
  • Added language stating that if additional training is required, the employee must be on the clock for the training and if the required training occurs after hours the employee will be paid.  

Article 14 - Protection of Employees

  • Added Section G regarding Sexual Harassment to state that no person shall on the basis of age, sex, race, color, national origin, religion, disability, sexual orientation, marital status or any other characteristic protected by federal or state law or Board policy, be excluded from participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to unlawful discrimination or harassment with respect to employment.  Employees are encouraged to report any allegations of discrimination or sexual harassment to their administrators or the applicable district-level departments for prompt investigation in accordance with Board policies.  

Article 16 – Extended Leaves of Absence

  • Revised Section A - Association to state that the Board agrees that no more than one (1) employee designated by the Association shall, upon request, be granted a leave of absence with pay for up to three (3) years for the purpose of engaging in activities of the Association or its affiliates. The three-year term shall be renewable. The employee will retain all benefits allowable under law and the Association will reimburse the Board for all costs incurred.

Article 18 – Fringe Benefits

  • Deleted Section J, as the waiver language was an option under a former healthcare system, which is no longer an option today.
  • Revised Section M (new L) regarding Retiree Insurance 

Article 19 – Terminal Pay

  • Adjustments made to the percentages of unused accrued sick leave paid out upon retirement or death.  Employees will now be paid 100% of unused sick leave at year 25 or after, upon retirement or death.  Other percentages were brought into alignment with statute guidelines.

Article 20 – Deductions from Salary

  • Added language in Section A (Authorization to Deduct) to clarify that the Association will be granted one payroll slot for non-district sponsored benefits, and while not required to be granted, will be given careful consideration by the district.  In all cases, the vendor must utilize electronic files and not cause any undue hardship on the district.   

 

Other adjustments and enhancements to contract language throughout the contract were also agreed upon.

 

RATIONALE:

The respective bargaining teams worked collaboratively to examine healthcare payroll options, financial information impacting the availability of funds for salary increase and contract language. We believe the tentative agreement reached maximizes the amount of the total compensation package for support employees within the PESPA bargaining unit and clarifies rights and obligations under the contract.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
