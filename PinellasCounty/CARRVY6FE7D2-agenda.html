4. Special Order Agenda
Subject
4.2 Recognition of Superintendent Michael A. Grego's Leadership and Service to Pinellas County Schools
Meeting
Jun 14, 2022 - Regular School Board Meeting - 5 p.m.
Type
Recognition
BACKGROUND:

On January 6, 2022, Dr. Michael Grego announced his intent to retire at the end of the 2021-2022 school year.  Dr. Grego has served in public education in the state of Florida for 42 years, with the last 10 years as superintendent of Pinellas County Schools.

 

Over the past 10 years, Dr. Grego has worked tirelessly to elevate the educational opportunities for all students.  From graduation rates to school grades, to summer learning and early childhood education, under Dr. Grego’s leadership, Pinellas County Schools have seen remarkable improvement in all areas.

 

With a focus on educating the whole child, one of Dr. Grego’s top priorities has been the expansion of all performing and visual arts programs in the district. Pinellas County Schools’ arts programs are known and respected throughout the nation.

 

The school board has commissioned a piece of student artwork to commemorate Dr. Grego’s retirement. Created by Ms. Luna Garcia, a 2022 graduate of East Lake High School, the pen and ink drawing is titled The Next Chapter in Life and depicts Dr. Grego’s attendance at graduation.

 

Ms. Garcia has received numerous awards for her artwork, including the 2022 Scholastic Art Gold Key and American Visions Award, a first place award, at the 2022 Walker’s Rising Stars annual scholarship competition.

 

The entire school board would like to take this opportunity to publicly acknowledge and thank Dr. Grego for his commitment to the students, teachers and staff of Pinellas County Schools and for his many years of service to education.   

 

SUBMITTED BY:

Eileen M. Long, School Board Chair

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.

BACKGROUND:

Pinellas County Schools engages stakeholders to inform the Code of Student Conduct on an annual basis. Concurrently, the district reviews the policies within the Code of Student Conduct for alignment with legislative updates, as well as state and district practices.

 

The policies within the Code of Student Conduct provide guidance and descriptions for the conduct of students. The proposed amendments are intended to provide greater clarity to all stakeholders, and inform their decisions about engaging in education, behaving appropriately and reporting misbehavior and related consequences.

 

The proposed amendments to the policies within the Code of Student Conduct are reflective of legislative updates, district practices and stakeholder input.

 

For the 2022-23 school year, the following policy changes are recommended.

  • Policy 5500.04 - Dress Code
    • The requirements are rewritten to describe applicability to all clothing instead of specific articles of clothing. The requirements, while unchanged, are also reorganized to group similar guidelines together for clarity and ease of reference for stakeholders.
  • Policy 5500.07 - Discipline
    • Added language stating that reassignment or referral to mental health services will follow the conclusion of a threat assessment.
  • Policy 5500.08 – Misconduct that Requires Specific Consequences
    • Added language that a threat assessment will be conducted for firearms, guns and dangerous objects.
  • Policy 5500.10 - Disciplinary Actions and Policy 5500.12 – Suspension/Expulsion
    • Updated to consistently specify five (5) school days for appeal of suspension or reassignment.
  • Policy 5500.11 - Discipline for Students with Disabilities
    • Added language clarifying that a student being evaluated for consideration of ESE eligibility is considered a student with a disability, and will be disciplined according to this policy.
    • Clarified that manifestation determination reviews can identify a pattern of behavior.
  • Policy 5500.13 - Grievance Procedures
    • Updated to consistently specify five (5) school days for resolving problems with the principal or Area Superintendent

 

Additionally, Policies 5500.01, 5500.02 and 5500.06 were updated to reflect gender-neutral language, as were the seven policies with substantive revisions outlined above. These are technical edits and do not require board approval; however, are included as part of the Code of Student Conduct policy updates. Policy 5500.015 - Parental Rights is added to the group of policies within the Code of Student Conduct, but remains unchanged since Board approval at the March 22, 2022, public Board meeting.

 

RATIONALE:

The proposed amendments to these policies ensure alignment with legislative updates, as well as state and district procedures.

Recommended Action
Approve the request to authorize the superintendent to allow payment of the current bills and salaries beginning July 1, 2022, until the approval of the final 2022-2023 budget.
Goals
BACKGROUND:
The purpose of this item is to provide the authority for the district to continue operations during the period between July 1, 2022, (the beginning of the 2022-2023 fiscal year) and the final approval of the 2022-2023 budget during the Budget Public Hearing on September 13, 2022.
 

Florida Statute 1011.09 reads: “During the period from July 1 to the date the tentative budget becomes official, school boards are authorized to approve ordinary expenditures, including salary payments, which are necessary for the approved school program."

 

Florida Statute 200.065(2)(g) further states: "Notwithstanding other provisions of law to the contrary, a taxing authority may: 1. Expend moneys based on its tentative budget after adoption...and until such time as its final budget is adopted...in the case of a school district, if the fall term begins prior to adoption of the final budget." The Department of Revenue has included the following statement in its Manual of Instructions for Truth-In-Millage (TRIM) Compliance: "If the fall term for the school district begins before adoption of the final budget, the school district may expend in accordance with the adopted tentative budget, until such time as a final budget is adopted. (s.200.065(2)(g), F.S.)."

 

On July 26, 2022, the board will hold a Public Hearing on the 2022-2023 budget and millage rates. At that meeting, the board will adopt the tentative budget. Final budget approval is scheduled for September 13, 2022, after the beginning of the fall term.

 

Extraordinary requests for advance funding may be brought to the board for specific approval, if necessary, prior to the adoption of the final budget.

 
RATIONALE:
The budget process, as defined by the state of Florida Truth-In-Millage (TRIM) requirements, will not be completed until after the beginning of the fall school term. There are identified district needs which, in order to be met in a timely manner, require expenditures prior to the final adoption of the district's budget in September.

Goals

BACKGROUND:

This project is for the new construction of the Pinellas County Schools (PCS) Leadership Center. District leadership worked with Pinellas legislative leaders and requested state funding to help offset the cost of constructing a new professional development center, known as the Leadership Center, that would focus on the professional development of teachers and on enhancing the leadership skills of support and professional staff and school and district leaders. The Florida Legislature included $5,000,000 to help fund the Pinellas County Schools Leadership Center under HB4669 in the 2022 state budget. The district will match the legislature's funding towards the design and construction of a new $10,000,000 facility dedicated to the education profession and professional growth of all employees. The district intends to offer the space for lease to municipal partners and private groups for their leadership development as long as those uses are not in conflict with the needs of the PCS professional development department.

 

The project’s goal is to construct a new building that will provide a world-class learning environment for the professional development of PCS educators, support staff and school and district leaders as well as for community partners.

 

On June 22, 2021, the board approved an agreement with Rowe Architects, Inc. for architectural and contract administration services in connection with the conceptual design of the PCS Leadership Center in the amount of $55,000.

 

Amendment to Agreement No. 1 is for the full design service to create the PCS Leadership Incubator.  A copy of the agreement is attached.

 

RATIONALE:

The Discovery building has been the primary location of professional development trainings and seminars for many years. The building's size and configuration limits the types of trainings that can be held onsite which often forces the professional development staff to find alternate locations for training. Scheduling these alternate locations for training has become challenging as schools continue to expand the number of after-hour programs and learning opportunities for their students and the community. It is the desire of the district to create a new training center for all employees on the site of the current Discovery building which could host large group trainings for up to 250 individuals or host multiple smaller trainings all under one roof.

Meeting Procedures:

 

The Board welcomes public comment. For those who wish to speak to the Board, please note the following:

 

You are given three separate opportunities to speak. The first is during "Public Comment" on a topic not included in the agenda but pertaining to the general business of the district, and this opportunity occurs after adjournment of regular meetings. The second is on one or more agenda items upon which the Board will vote, and this opportunity occurs after the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings. The third is at public hearings, such as budget hearings, and second readings on Board policy, when you are able to speak to the Board before it takes action.


In each case, (1) you must register to speak with the Superintendent’s designee at the entrance to the meeting room as described below; (2) you will be called in the order you registered with the Superintendent's designee; (3) you will be allotted three (3) minutes, which may only be extended with the approval of the chairperson; and (4) you may not yield your time to any other person. To avoid repetition, speakers supporting or opposing the same issue are encouraged to designate a spokesperson and have the spokesperson request that members of the audience supporting the position stand during the presentation.


If you wish to speak to agenda items, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker on agenda items concludes his or her comments whichever is later. If you wish to speak during the Public Comment period, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker concludes his or her comments, whichever is later. If you wish to speak during a Public Hearing, you must register prior to the time the Chairperson calls for speakers or before the last speaker during the Public Hearing concludes his or her comments, whichever is later.


The following additional procedures apply depending upon the specific speaking opportunity:

 

  1. Public Comment. The Board will hear public comment on the general business of the district after adjournment of regular meetings. The Board will not act on any matter you may raise during your presentation.
  2. Numbered Agenda Items. Time will be set aside immediately following the special order agenda at regular meetings and as appropriate at special meetings for you to address numbered agenda items upon which the Board will vote. You may address as many agenda items as you wish during your three (3) minute period, other than items pertaining to employee or student discipline, or any other matter listed in Section 286.0114(3), F.S., such as ministerial acts or emergencies.
  3. Public Hearings. Time will be set aside at each public hearing for you to speak on the agenda item. You must confine your comments to the agenda item.


Regulation of Disruptive Speech
Board meetings are a limited open forum for First Amendment purposes, and your exercise of your First Amendment right of free speech in that context will be recognized and protected, subject to reasonable restrictions as to time, place, and manner. Applause is permitted only when awards are granted.
To ensure the expeditious and orderly process of Board meetings, the Chairperson may:

 

  1. Interrupt or terminate a speaker when his/her statement is not relevant, exceeds the time allotted, or is abusive, threatening, defamatory, obscene, profane, loud, interruptive, or otherwise of a disruptive or disorderly nature; and
  2. Order the removal of any person interfering with the expeditious or orderly process of the meeting, provided the Chairperson has first issued a warning that continued interference with the orderly processes of the meeting will result in removal.

Nothing herein is intended, nor shall anything be construed, to limit or restrain negative, positive, or neutral comments about the manner in which Board employees, agents, the Superintendent, and Board members carry out their duties in public employment or office.


Recording of Board Meetings
Recordings are permitted under the following conditions:

 

  1. No obstructions are created between the Board and the audience.
  2. No interviews are conducted in the meeting room while the Board is in session.
  3. No commentary, adjustment of equipment, or positioning of operators is made that would distract either the Board or members of the audience while the Board is in session.
