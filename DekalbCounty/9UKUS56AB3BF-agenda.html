5. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Subject
A. Recognition - Enharmonic Fusion
Meeting
May 5, 2015 - DeKalb CUSD 428 Workshop Board Meeting
Category
5. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

The DeKalb High School A Cappella ensemble “Enharmonic Fusion” recently competed in the International Championships of High School A Cappella national finals in New York City, NY. They performed and competed against the 11 other top high school groups in the country on April 17th, 2015 at the Peter Norton Symphony Space in Manhattan.

 

Formed in 2010, this is Enharmonic Fusion’s third appearance in five years and the fifth time overall that a DHS A Cappella group has competed in the national finals.  This year’s ensemble earned a spot in the national finals by winning first place at the ICHSA Midwest Semifinal held on March 6th, 2015. This is first time Enharmonic Fusion has advanced the finals by placing first in the ICHSA Midwest Championships.

 

Enharmonic Fusion also competed and placed second in the Sing Strong Chicago A Cappella Festival at Niles West High School on April 11, 2015.  Senior Evan Taylor received the award for Outstanding Arrangement at both the ICHSA and Sing Strong Chicago competitions for his arrangement of the group’s first piece: “This is Gospel.”

 

Enharmonic Fusion performs for many organizations and events in the community.  They were very honored to sing for their peers in an all school assembly just before departing for the national finals.  Students in the group are also involved in many other musical groups, athletics, and activities at DeKalb High School including: Marching Band, Varsity Choir, Concert Choir, Madrigal Singers, Steel Band, Jazz Ensemble, Orchestra, Student Council, Black Student Union, Football, Tennis, Softball, Track, Golf, New Pennies, Spring Musical, Color Guard

 

This year’s DHS Enharmonic Fusion Members include Matthew Callahan, Josiah Fagerstrom, Destinee Feliciano, Katie Hagaman, Eli Hamilton, Jesse Heinsohn, Rebecca Helmeid, Jimmy Henry, Rachel Hunter-Rosene, Gabe Ihm, Hope Jesmer, Mark Jesmer, Sarah Jesmer, Olivia Johnson-Smith, Ashley Kelly, Grace Klonoski, Emili Maag, Jaycee Mattis, Avery Maxwell, Nailah Mobley, Jordan Nguyen, Matt Petersen, Andrea Ruiz, Dylan Schweitzer, Richie Spahn, Evan Taylor, Nyari Turner, Ben Vance, Grace Verbic, Lizzy Warner, Khadijah Williams, Michael Williams, and Adam Wilson.

 

Enharmonic Fusion would like to thank the DeKalb High School staff and students, the District #428 Administration, the District #428 Music Staff, and community for all their tremendous support. They extend their heartfelt gratitude and are indebted to all who made their travel to the national finals possible.  The would especially like to thank sponsors such as the Irving Family, the Proesel Family, DeKalb Music Boosters, DeKalb Athletic Boosters, Kish Health Services, Hayes Body Shop, the DeKalb Elks Lodge, as well as all who supported their vigorous fundraising efforts!

B. Recognition - DHS High School French Team
Meeting
May 5, 2015 - DeKalb CUSD 428 Workshop Board Meeting
Category
5. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION

Recognition

On Saturday, April 25, 2015, the DHS team representing the French program for the third time participated in the French speaking contest similar to the Forensics tournament which was held at Elmhurst College. This year, our students competed against Chicago and suburban schools such as Chicago Arts Academy, Amos Alonzo Stagg High School, Niles West HS, Carl Sandburg High School, Marist High School, Loyola Academy, Joliet West HS, LaSalle Language Academy, Batavia High School, and Richmond-Burton Community High School.

 

Last year 11 students from DHS competed in 4 categories (poetry, impromptu conversation, persuasive speech, and skit); this year 19 students performed in all five categories adding one more category such as readers’ theater.

 

French 2 students performed a 15 minute play in French based on the favorite French children's book Le Petit Nicolas by Sempé-Goscinny;  French 3 and 4 students presented a short play La Pose by contemporary French play writer Carole Fréchette; in the category Readers’ theater, French 2 students interpreted the short story L’Hôte by Albert Camus. 

 

I am very happy to inform you that from a total of 21 events our students received First rating and a blue ribbon (the highest possible rating out of three ratings) in all 21 events.
