4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Subject
A. Recognition - Enharmonic Fusion
Meeting
Mar 1, 2016 - CUSD #428 Workshop School Board Meeting
Category
4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

The DeKalb High School A Cappella ensemble “Enharmonic Fusion” recently competed in the International Championships of High School A Cappella's Midwest Quarterfinal which was held at DeKalb High School on February 12th, 2016.  Enharmonic Fusion placed first in the event and qualified to continue on to the Midwest Semifinal round.  The Midwest Semifinal takes place on March 19th, 2016 and will be held at DeKalb High School.  
 

This is the first year that the ICHSA tournament has been expanded to multiple rounds in the Midwest region.  The Midwest Semifinal event will include each of the top two high school ensembles from Illinois, Michigan, Missouri, Ohio, and Wisconsin and will feature national powerhouse groups such as Limited Edition of Port Washington, WI and Forte of Centerville, OH. One group from the five states will be selected to advance to the ICHSA National Finals, while the second and third place groups can submit for a wildcard spot in the Finals.
 

Formed in 2010, DeKalb High School's Enharmonic Fusion has advanced to the ICHSA National Finals three times in the past five years. The DHS Boys A Cappella group also competed in the ICHSA National Finals in 2007 and 2008.  
 

Enharmonic Fusion performs for many organizations and events in the community.   Students in the group are also involved in many other musical groups, athletics, and activities at DeKalb High School including: Marching Band, Varsity Choir, Concert Choir, Madrigal Singers, Steel Band, Jazz Ensemble, Orchestra, Student Council, Black Student Union, Football, Tennis, Softball, Cross Country, Track, Golf, Boys Basketball, Boys Soccer, Cheerleading, Key Club, Fall Musical, Spring Play, and Color Guard. 
 

The members of the Enharmonic Fusion and other DHS choirs have already earned many music accolades this year. 

Eleven students were selected to the Illinois Music Educators Association All-State Choirs and Bands, including: Sarah Duffin, Josiah Fagerstrom, Destinee Feliciano, Katie Hagaman, Rachel Hunter-Rosene, Ashley Kelly, Grace Klonoski, Emili Maag, Phebe Palmer, Andrea Ruiz and Ben Vance.
 

Enharmonic Fusion students were also selected to the American Choral Directors Association Central Division All-Midwest Choir - representing the states of Illinois, Indiana, Michigan, and Ohio. These students were selected through a rigorous blind audition process to work with Dr. Joe Miller of the Westminster Choir College and present a concert at the Central Division ACDA Conference in Chicago.   Of the 108 students selected from four states for this honor, 13 are from DeKalb High School.  They are: Jackie Blanchard, Matt Callahan, Shannon Cowan, Josiah Fagerstrom, Nate Harrison, Rachel Hunter-Rosene, Hope Jesmer, Mark Jesmer, Ashley Kelly, Grace Klonoski, Ian Panke, Ben Vance, Elizabeth Warner. Junior Grace Klonoski was selected as Outstanding soloist at the ICHSA Midwest Quarterfinal.  She was also selected at ILMEA as one of the most outstanding performers of the National Anthem, and was invited to sing for the IHSA State Basketball Tournaments.
 

This year’s DHS Enharmonic Fusion Members include Matthew Callahan, Emma Carpenter, Shannon Cowan, Daniel DeLap, Josiah Fagerstrom, Destinee Feliciano, Katie Hagaman, Nate Harrison, Rebecca Helmeid, Rachel Hunter-Rosene, Hope Jesmer, Mark Jesmer, Ashley Kelly, Grace Klonoski, Olivia Klonoski, Emili Maag, Sofia Mattson, Devonte Merrick, Nailah Mobley, Phebe Palmer, Ian Panke, Isaiah Panke, AJ Petersen, Michael Pollack, Andrew Olson, Andrea Ruiz, Joey Sauser, Jim Small, AJ Smith, Richie Spahn, Nyari Turner, Ben Vance, Grace Verbic, Lizzy Warner, Khadijah Williams, Michael Williams
 

Enharmonic Fusion would like to thank the DeKalb High School staff and students, the District #428 Administration and Custodial Staff, the District #428 Music Staff, and community for all their tremendous support. They extend their heartfelt gratitude and are indebted to all who made their participation in all these endeavors possible.  They would especially like to thank sponsors such as the Irving Family, the Proesel Family, DeKalb Music Boosters, DeKalb Athletic Boosters, Kish Health Services, Hayes Body Shop, the DeKalb Elks Lodge, and many, many others.
