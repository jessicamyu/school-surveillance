The Board of Education would like to congratulate members of the Huntley Middle School girls and boys track teams.  The teams competed at the 2017 IESA State Track Meet in East Peoria. They brought home the most medals for a Huntley Middle School Track program since 1996.

 

● Sariyah Watson was the 7th grade girls 400 Meter Dash State Champion.

● Isabelle Dlabal placed 6th in the 7th grade girls 400 Meter Dash.

● The relay of Lilly Nailor, Paige Walk, Alexis Hart, Adriana Figueroa and Sariyah Watson placed 8th in the 7th grade girls 4x200 Meter Relay.

● Kallie Siebrasse was the 8th grade 100 Meter Hurdles State Champion & placed 3rd in the 8th grade girls Long Jump.

● The relay of Victoria Tuszynski, Beyonce Pitts, Katelyn Kolkebeck, Amanda Dubeau and Kallie Siebrasse placed 8th in the 8th grade girls 4x100 Meter Relay.

● The relay of Treyton Butler, Trenton McCollum, Billy Adams, Trenton Kyler and Johnny Ice placed 5th in the 8th grade boys 4 x 100 Meter Relay. 

 

Additional congratulations goes out to the 7th grade boys team as they capped their season off with a 2nd place finish as a team.

● They were led by Toriano Tate who finished 2nd in the 7th grade boys Long Jump & 4th place in the7th grade 200 Meter Dash.

● The relay of Jaleel Wallace, William Zaver, Toriano Tate, Michael Robinson and Cameron Grays were the 7th grade boys 4x100 Meter Relay State Champions.

 

The teams were coached by Todd Hallaron, Matt McIntyre, Jennifer Harrison, Ryan Corcoran, and Levy McElroy.

Girls Track & Field 

The Barbs finished second overall in the conference bringing home 10 all-conference athletes. They followed that performance up the next week with a second place finish in the IHSA Sectional sending seven Barbs to the state finals.  Those seven athletes competed in 9 of 18 events. Two relay teams placed 5th at the state meet and 5 athletes received all-state honors. The 4x100-meter relay team included Brimonet Buggs, Kmaris Kirkwood, Miriea Martinez and Kyra Ozier. 
 

The 4x200-meter relay team included Sierra Long, Brimonet Buggs, Miriea Martinez and Kmaris Kirkwood. Overall 23 school records were broken this year and 6 new conference records were broken by the Barbs. The Barbs finished with a 24th overall ranking in the state in girls track and field.

 

State Qualifiers:

Kyra Ozier – 400-meter Dash and 4x100m and 4x400m relays

Brianna Barwick - 4x400-meter relay and 300 low hurdles 

Brimonet Buggs – 200-meter dash, 4x100 and 4x 200-meter relays 

Sierra Long - 4x400-meter relay and 4x200-meter relay

Kmaris Kirkwood - 4x100 and 4x200-meter relays 

Jasmine Kemp - high jump 

Katherine Olsen – 800-meter and 1600-meter run 

Miriea Martinez - 4x100, 4x200 and 4x400-meter relays 


Badminton

Alyse Mommer & Helene Collin qualified for the IHSA State Tournament as a doubles team and finished in the top four teams at the IHSA Sectional.  Alyse and Helene finished the season with a record of 23-14. 

Hui Zhu & Holly Trapp qualified for the IHSA State Tournament for the second straight year.  Hui and Holly went 3-2 at the IHSA State Finals and were one win away from advancing to the second day of the tournament.  Zhu and Trapp finished their season with a record of 30-10. 

 

Boys Track & Field

The 4x100 relay team made up of Keonte McKinnie, Tyrese Dyson, Tristan Bujarski and Zuerek Day placed 2nd at the IHSA Sectional Meet which qualified them for the IHSA State Meet in Charleston.  At the state meet, the team did not qualify for the finals but ran their best time of the year.  

Zuerek Day placed 2nd at the IHSA Sectional in the 300 hurdles to qualify for the IHSA State Meet.  At the state meet, Zuerek Day brought home a 3rd place medal and a new school record in the 300 hurdles with a time of 38.15!  He also earned all-state honors.

 

Bass Fishing

Many hours of research and practice paid off as our bass fishing team took second at the Shabbona Lake Sectional with a 15.5 lb. limit of 5 fish which earned them 2nd place in the IHSA Sectional.   The team of Chandler Larson, Cameron Kriese, Lauren Collin, Anthony Frazier, and Justin Pederson will be representing us at the state finals on Lake Carlyle, June 23rd and 24th.

 

Baseball

The 19 young men that proudly wore the DeKalb High School baseball uniform were able to overcome a less than stellar regular season to win the 4A Regional Championship.  The road to winning the regional championship was far from easy though.  After being down 4 – 3 going into the bottom of the 6th inning, the team was able to rally, scoring 7 runs in their quarterfinal win vs Jefferson High School.  In the semifinal game vs Rockford East, a (2-1 victory that went 9 innings), and the championship game vs Hampshire (3-2 victory), the Barbs were led by the arms of Bryce Wheeler and Derek Kyler, in addition to some timely hitting from John Brei, and Noah Valin.  Their run to the state championship came up short with a sectional loss to Crystal Lake South.      


Individually, three Barbs received Northern IL Big 12 All-Conference Honors:
Derek Kyler and Bryce Wheeler

 

Honorable Mention

Matt Searls
