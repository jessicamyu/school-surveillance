A. Recognition - Connor Diaz, Clinton Rosette Middle School Student
Meeting
Jun 7, 2016 - CUSD #428 Workshop School Board Meeting
Category
4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

Connor Diaz, 8th grade student at Clinton Rosette, was awarded the Bronze Award (third place) in Aerospace at the Illinois Junior Academy of Science State Science Fair on May 14, 2016 held at Millikin University, in Decatur, Illinois.  His project, titled "Contacting the International Space Station Using Amateur Radio" also awarded him a $1000 scholarship to Millikin University.

 

The Illinois Junior Academy of Science exists for the benefit of students in the state of Illinois. The process students follow for the IJAS gives these students some insight into the problems and methods of thinking that are particular to the scientist. This process encourages these students to find information concerning new investigations and discoveries in science. It gives them a chance to use, and gain an understanding of, scientific equipment. It gives a reason for students with a special interest in science to go beyond the classroom curriculum and develop their own investigation(s). It encourages students in their progress towards a career in science.  ijas.org

At the Six Flags Music in the Parks competition on Saturday, May 14th, Clinton Rosette's 7th/8th Grade Orchestra received Best Orchestra Overall and a Superior rating in their group of 5 Orchestras. The 6th Grade Orchestra received a 1st Place and a Superior rating.  Rosette's 7th/8th Grade Choir received Best Choir Overall and a Superior rating in their group of 5 Choirs. The 7th/8th Band received a Superior rating and 2nd Place in their Band division and the Clinton Rosette Middle School Jazz Band received an Excellent rating.  

 

At the competition, Huntley Middle School's 7th/8th Grade Choir received a 1st Place and a Superior rating in their group of 5 Choirs.
