Recommended Action
"the Board of Education will accept with gratitude grants from the DeKalb County Community Foundation & the Farny R. Wurlitzer Foundation in the amount of $19,500 and a donation from Music Boosters in the amount of $3,000 for a grand concert harp and the renovation of two Steinway upright pianos at DeKalb High School, the donation of $500 from Kelley Williamson's EXXON Mobil Station at 2400 Sycamore Road, DeKalb, Illinois, to DeKalb High School & the donation of $2,000 each to Clinton Rosette & Huntley Middle Schools from General Mills and the National Dairy Council to purchase food service equipment."

Background information for the Board of Education and public:

 

Anita Zurbrugg from the DeKalb County Community Foundation and Jane Ovigz from the Farny R. Wurlitzer Foundation will be in attendance.  Representatives from EXXON will also be in attendance.

 

 

Thanks to General Mills and the National Dairy Council, the "Fuel Up" grant program was offered in July of 2014.  Carol Warren applied for 4 grants, and on October 8, the announcements were made to the winners. Both Huntley Middle School and Clinton Rosette were chosen as grant recipients. Both receiving a $2000.00 per school for purchasing of equipment through General Mills.  The driving force behind these grants were to give school districts the ability to increase breakfast participation.  
 
Equipment that was offered to purchase with breakfast grant funding were portable insulated carts for juice, milk - menu boards to display breakfast menus,cooler barrels for milk and juice, insulated bags to assist with keeping hot breakfast hot as needed, and a cart for the transport of these bags.
 
We used these merchandising items to enhance a portable breakfast that was located in the front entrance of Huntley Middle School.  Our goal was to capture students standing outside visiting with fellow students, with high hopes of enticing them to eat breakfast outside while visiting with fellow students prior to the first bell ringing! Offering an additional outside cart would complement the inside breakfast offering and service line.
