"that the Board of Education accept with gratitude the donation from the Estate of Dean C. Burgemeister in the amount of $43,812.38."

The District has been gifted $43,812.38 by the Estate of Dean C. Burgemeister.  Mr. Burgemeister and his wife Betty were frequent donors to many institutions throughout the country, and locally, including Kish Health Systems, Kishwaukee College where they formed the Dean and Betty Burgemeister Scholarship, and Kishwaukee College Foundation where they formed the Dean and Betty Burgemeister Endowment.

B. Reintegration Differential Plan Proposal (I) and Remote Differential Plan Approvals (A)
Meeting
Aug 18, 2020 - DeKalb CUSD #428 Regular School Board Meeting
Category
6. SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORT / PRESENTATIONS
Type
Action, Information
Recommended Action
"that the Board of Education approve the employment of differential staff as presented."

Because of COVID and remote learning, Differential offerings will change.  As a result, we are recommending that Differential teachers be given an option to offer programs in new ways that still make programs available to students.  For staff who opt to develop revised program offerings, we are asking them to re-submit a plan that would provide adjusted compensation at 0%, 50% or 100%.  Details provided below:

 

The Reintegration Remote Differential Plan Proposal is to be used to submit a plan and a rationale of how and why to run the differential. The submission will be reviewed at the building level and based on the request and recommendation, if approved, will be compensated at 100%, 50% or 0%. 

 

The second form, the Remote Differential Employee Compensation Declaration form is for each coach/advisor/director of that activity/club/sport to complete to determine the stipend pay for each. 

 

Once both forms are completed and submitted to the responsible building administrator/supervisor it should be included with the PARF (for new differentials and elementary) or on the spreadsheet that is submitted to HR and Finance.

D. Student Fees (I)
Meeting
Aug 18, 2020 - DeKalb CUSD #428 Regular School Board Meeting
Category
6. SUPERINTENDENT'S REPORT / PRESENTATIONS
Type
Discussion, Information

Due to COVID-19 and the financial stress that many families are experiencing, the Interim Superintendents would like the Board of Education’s input regarding the potential to reduce or waive student fees for the 20-21 school year. Based on this input, the administration will develop a plan that will be presented to the Board for their consideration at the next board meeting.

Below are the base registration fees for the school year by grade level:

K-5   $140.00

6-8   $160.00

9-12 $175.00

Those students that qualify for free meals automatically have this fee waived. Those that qualify for reduced priced meals automatically have their fees cut in half.

The District also has course fees that vary (primarily at the high school) based on specific courses that are in addition to the above fee. The high school gets a corresponding expenditure budget based on the projected enrollments in these courses, so they can purchase additional supports to help run these classes. The budget they receive is based on all students so includes those that qualify for free/reduced meals. The waiver of all or part of these fees are the same as the base registration fee process; based on free/reduced status.

Driver’s education has a fee of $300.00.

Sports/activity fees are also charged for those that participate at the middle school ($75 per sport/activity-max charged $150) and high school level ($100 per sport/activity-max charged $200).

Both driver’s education and sports/activity fees are waived based on free/reduced status.

For the last school year, here is a breakdown of what was collected:

Base Registration Fees $452,000

Course Fees $63,000 (does not include drivers education fees)

Sports Fees $53,000
