Subject
A. Recognition - DeKalb High School Interact Club
Meeting
Jun 4, 2013 - Workshop Meeting/DeKalb CUSD Board of Education
Category
4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

The Board of Education will recognize the students of the DHS Interact Club for the funds they raised to purchase a shelter box.  This club is sponsored by the Rotary Club and their advisor is Sara Jennings.

 

Additional information:

 

Interact Club, which is sponsored by the DeKalb Rotary Club, has been revived this year after a seven-year hiatus.  In addition to smaller service projects that the students have completed, including ringing the bells for the Salvation Army, participating in Feed My Starving Children, and serving at the district Literacy banquet, the students organized a sleep out to raise funds for Shelter Box USA.  Shelter Box is an organization that provides tent housing and provisions for those whose homes have been destroyed as a result of a natural disaster.  Each box costs $1000, and I am happy to report that our group was able to raise enough money to purchase one.  An exciting aspect of Shelter Box is that once the box is purchased, we will receive a unique ID number that will allow us to track the box when it is delivered to an affected area.  Our sleep out was truly a team effort.  Students advertised the event, developed games and activities, sought the donation of water bottles from HyVee, set up their own tents, and even assisted in constructing and deconstructing an official Shelter Box tent and a giant teepee.  Several teachers and administrators, parents, and the DeKalb Rotary Club also assisted with the event.

 

Sara Jennings

Reading Specialist

AP Spanish V Teacher

Literacy Teacher

Interact Club Sponsor

Subject
C. Recognition - Hanna Bingham and Genevieve Smelser
Meeting
Jun 4, 2013 - Workshop Meeting/DeKalb CUSD Board of Education
Category
4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

The Board of Education will recognize Hanna Bingham and Genevieve Smelser, for being invited to participate in a national chamber music competition at Notre Dame in South Bend, Indiana.  

 Both girls are in two different string quartets through a Saturday music school called Midwest Young Artists in Highwood, IL.  The Fischoff Competition is like the Olympics of chamber music.  Just to be invited you need to send a recording of 15 minutes of your most polished work.  Both groups were selected from over 70 entries.  Twenty-four groups were invited to perform in the quarter finals for an elite panel of judges from the top music colleges in the nation.  Hanna's quartet made it to the semi finals (top 12 groups) and Genevieve's quartet made it into the finals (top 3 groups) and brought home the bronze medal and $1,300 scholarship money.  Both quartets performed for schools in the South Bend area as an outreach to the community. Hanna and Genna have been members of DHS and HMS orchestras every year.
