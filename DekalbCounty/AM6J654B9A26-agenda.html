Subject
B. Recognition of Students from the DeKalb High School French Program
Meeting
May 16, 2017 - CUSD #428 Regular School Board Meeting
Category
4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

The Board of Education recognizes the outstanding achievement of the talented group of students who represented the French program at DHS during the First Francophone Youth Theatre Festival in Chicago on April 22-23, 2017. In September 2016, the group got together for the first time to discuss the plans. Seven months later, after several hours of reading and translating French text, working on French pronunciation, rehearsing the play, and creating set and props, the DHS team presented the adaptation of Candide by Voltaire in French along with students from DePaul University, Marie Curie High school, Lincoln Park High school, Latin School of Chicago, Whitney M.Young High School, Francis Parker High school and Round Lake High School. The presentation by DeKalb High School, directed by the French teacher Nataliya Zimmerman, earned the main award of the festival for Best Play. Congratulations to our talented actors: Katrina Baum, Colette LeBlanc, Madeline Hoth, Genco Akacik, Ming Lin, Phebe Palmer, Katherine Sharp,  Paul Anderson, Lorena Robles, Franziska Plagwitz, Stephanie Vazquez and Clay Ward. Congratulations also go to Anna Arnold, Emma Conway and Shaelyn West who did an excellent job of creating set, props and managing the music for the play. A special thank you goes to Madeline Hoth and her family for providing costumes for the play.

Subject
C. Recognition of DHS A Cappella Group Enharmonic Fusion
Meeting
May 16, 2017 - CUSD #428 Regular School Board Meeting
Category
4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

DeKalb High School's A Cappella ensemble Enharmonic Fusion competed in the International Championships of High School A Cappella (ICHSA) on April 20th, 2017 at The Town Hall Auditorium in New York City, New York. Enharmonic Fusion finished in second place overall and was additionally recognized for Most Outstanding Choreography. Senior Grace Klonoski was recognized as Most Outstanding Soloist for the second year in a row. Junior Mark Jesmer was selected as top vocal percussionist from the nine final groups to perform for the combined group performance. 

 
The group would like to sincerely thank the community for their tremendous support in helping them compete and travel this season. They would also like to thank the Wurlitzer Foundation and the DeKalb Education Foundation for their grant support towards a portable microphone system for the group.
 
Group members are:  Emma Carpenter, Daniel DeLap, Destinee Feliciano, Katie Hagaman, Hope Jesmer, Mark Jesmer, Ashley Kelly, Grace Klonoski, Olivia Klonoski, Devonte Merrick, Andrew Olson, Ian Panke, Aaron Pope, Ben Rodriguez, Richie Spahn, Ben Vance, Grace Verbic, Lizzy Warner. They are directed by Travis Erikson.
