Subject
E. Recognition - Jen Montavon, Kelli Hamilton, Dave Rohlman, and Kelly Wuchte
Meeting
Feb 19, 2013 - February 19, 2013
Category
4. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

Jen Montavon has been an integral part of an extensive collaboration between CRMS and KNPE at NIU. After nearly a year of planning in which Jen assisted with a needs assessment, inventory, grant writing, and content development, the project began in Fall 2012. With district approval, Jenny Parker and Jim Ressler from KNPE co-taught a 9th hour 7th grade PE class to focus on new instructional models for delivering content. The semester began with Adventure Based Learning, continued with Sport Education (Softball and Soccer) and concluded with fitness education. Each day for two quarters Jenny and Jim co-taught the class and then met with Jen afterwards to reflect and discuss changes to further enhance student learning. Jen then re-taught the lesson to all of her classes the following day (with modifications according to grade level that still maintain the integrity of the instructional model) and the cycle continues. In effect, this process was daily professional development for all involved in the collaboration.

This project has resulted in two successful grant applications to supplement equipment for CRMS, one state presentation, one national presentation, and a co-taught class in Sport Education at NIU.

The project is continuing for the last two quarters of the academic year and now Jen has taken a leadership role in developing and delivering the lessons, while Jenny and Jim are present to support as needed.

This professional development initiative would not have been possible without Jen's hard work and commitment to quality daily physical education.

 

 

This year, Northern and the DeKalb schools have committed to strengthening their commitment to excellence by expanding the Professional Development School located within the two middle schools.  Several DeKalb teachers have been instrumental in the success of the partnership.  These individuals have helped to educate colleagues on the mission of the PDS, as well as to develop goals and strategies for effective implementation of the PDS model.  They have volunteered many hours to ensure that the collaborative relationship between NIU and District 428 is maximized.  We would like to recognize Kelli Hamilton, Dave Rohlman, and Kelly Wuchte for their dedication and commitment to the ongoing partnership.
