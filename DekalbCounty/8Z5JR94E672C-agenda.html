2. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Subject
A. Recognition of Huntley Middle School Cross Country Team
Meeting
Oct 23, 2012 - October 23, 2012, 7:00 p.m.
Category
2. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

By taking second place at the Island Lake sectional meet on Saturday, October 6, the Huntley Middle School  boys' cross country team earned a spot at the IESA state meet in Normal, IL  on  October 13.

 

It was the first trip to state for the Huntley team, in their seventh year competing in the sport.

 

The state-qualifying runners included sixth graders Zach Gratschmayr, Riley Marks, Sam Naftzger, and Jonathan Snow; seventh graders Christopher Allen, Henry Barnes, Lincoln Draper, and Nathaniel Harrison; and eighth graders Jacob Alvarez and Zach Gould.

 

With 90 schools competing in sectional meets to try for a spot at the state meet, only 21 made it in, and the J-Barbs finished in 15th place.  The team is coached by Kevin Beverly. 

 

Congratulations to the boys' team on a solid finish to a fine season.

Subject
B. Recognition of Amanda Anderson, Jefferson Reading Specialist
Meeting
Oct 23, 2012 - October 23, 2012, 7:00 p.m.
Category
2. PUBLIC PARTICIPATION/RECOGNITION
Type
Recognition

Amanda Anderson, Jefferson's Reading Specialist, was honored by Office Max as a treasured teacher at Jefferson Elementary.  She was presented with a certificate, flowers and $1,000.00 worth of various office supplies for her to use with her students.  She was selected because she dedicates so much of her energies to serve Jefferson's diverse population of students and she provides support to all of the Jefferson staff through her work on the BLT and the PBIS Green Team.  She was instrumental in creating the Parent Resource Room at her school, which has increased Jefferson's positive, parent involvement and she also facilitates Jefferson's Reading is Fundamental Program and additional Title One events. 

 

In addition to the supplies given to Mrs. Anderson, Office Max provided Jefferson with $700.00 worth of various supplies to use with students, including several digital cameras!

 

This is part of Office Max's campaign called, "A Day Made Better."  They made these presentations to 1,000 teachers all over the United States.
